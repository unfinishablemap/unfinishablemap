## 1. What the Article Says (as reconstructed from the brief)

§5 of the article reportedly argues that the "dualist arena" — i.e., the Map's posited site of consciousness-mediated quantum bias at indeterminate physical outcomes — has a *structurally unbounded capability range*. It illustrates this with two physics references:

- **Coleman (1977), "Fate of the false vacuum," Phys. Rev. D 15, 2929** and **Coleman & De Luccia (1980), Phys. Rev. D 21, 3305**: the canonical semiclassical theory of vacuum decay via O(4)-symmetric instantons, with bubble nucleation through quantum tunneling. [University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign](https://courses.physics.illinois.edu/phys596/fa2016/StudentWork/Team4_Final.pdf)[arxiv](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2205.15345)
- **Buttazzo et al. (2013), "Investigating the near-criticality of the Higgs boson," JHEP 12 (2013) 089, arXiv:1307.3536**: the standard NNLO analysis showing the Standard Model electroweak vacuum is *metastable* but with a tunneling lifetime far exceeding the age of the universe under the measured Higgs and top masses. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/1307.3536)

The article presents these as a "worked illustration of arena-mediated quantum bias driving civilization-extinguishing outcomes." It then engages Bostrom's "Pascal's Mugging" (Analysis 69(3): 443–445, 2009) and claims that a distinction between *structural* unboundedness (real physics permits arbitrarily catastrophic outcomes) and *manipulation* unboundedness (an adversary fabricates arbitrarily large stakes) defuses the Pascal's Mugging worry: because the Map is invoking the former, not the latter, it is supposedly immune to the pathology Bostrom diagnosed. [HandWiki](https://handwiki.org/wiki/Pascal's_mugging)

Three properties of the move are evident even in summary form. First, the argument is load-bearing on the Map's tenets: the Minimal Quantum Interaction tenet (consciousness biases otherwise indeterminate physical outcomes) is what licenses talk of an "arena" with unbounded reach in the first place. Second, the rhetorical structure is "physics citation → existence proof for unbounded magnitude → unboundedness is real not fictive → Pascal's Mugging defused." Third, the unboundedness is parlayed *into* a positive practical conclusion ("dualism as AI risk mitigation"): if the dualist arena is the right description of the world, AI built on substrates without that arena is *thereby* less dangerous (or, depending on phrasing, *thereby* an active protection against civilization-extinguishing outcomes). The user's suspicion is that this is precisely the inferential pattern Bostrom warned superintelligent or framework-bound systems against. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/interactionist-dualism/)[unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)

## 2. Evaluation of the Philosophical Argument

### 2.1 The structural/manipulation distinction does not do the work it is asked to do

Bostrom's worry, sharpened by the literature that has accumulated since 2009, is *not* fundamentally about the adversary. The adversary in Bostrom's dialogue is a vivid device, but the formal pathology is independent of who or what produces the unbounded magnitude. The pathology is: any decision rule that lets expected-utility sums be dominated by tiny probabilities of arbitrarily large magnitudes is (a) sensitive to claims that cannot be epistemically distinguished from noise, (b) prone to non-convergent expectations (de Blanc 2007), and (c) Dutch-bookable across iterated muggings. These properties are functions of the *shape of the probability/utility tail*, not of whether the magnitudes are produced by a deceiver or by physics. [Oxford Academic + 4](https://academic.oup.com/analysis/article-abstract/69/3/443/139950)

This is exactly why the post-2009 literature has converged on tail-shape-based responses rather than adversary-based ones:

- **Beckstead & Thomas (2024, Noûs 58: 431–455)** prove a trilemma: every theory of uncertain prospects must be either *reckless* (gives up sure things for arbitrarily small probabilities of huge gains), *timid* (refuses arbitrarily large gains to avoid tiny risks), or *non-transitive*. There is no exit conditional on whether the unboundedness is "structural" or "manipulated"; the trilemma applies to any prospect with the right tail behavior.
- **Wilkinson (2022, Ethics, "In defence of fanaticism")** argues that rejecting fanaticism forces costs (intransitivity, sensitivity to background events, inconsistency) that are themselves severe; the debate is precisely *not* settled by pointing out that an unbounded prospect is real rather than asserted. [Globalprioritiesinstitute](https://globalprioritiesinstitute.org/hayden-wilkinson-in-defence-of-fanaticism/)
- **Tarsney (2025, Noûs 59(4): 1093–1125, "Expected value, to a point")** shows that under realistic background uncertainty, stochastic dominance reasoning recovers expected-value verdicts in ordinary cases but *fails to require* expected-value verdicts when the expectational superiority depends on tiny probabilities of extreme outcomes — explicitly drawing the principled line at the *probability/magnitude shape*, not at the source of the magnitude.
- **Russell (2024, Noûs 58(3): 565–595, "On Two Arguments for Fanaticism")** and **Hiller & Hasan (2025, Dialogue 64(2): 359–375)** likewise focus on the structure of the prospects, not the provenance. [PhilPapers](https://philpapers.org/rec/BOSPM)
- **Bostrom himself** flagged that the leverage-penalty response (Hanson) penalizes priors on hypotheses that put the agent in a *uniquely consequential* position. Civilization-extinguishing physical outcomes that an "arena" can mediate are paradigm cases of precisely the kind of hypothesis the leverage penalty is designed to discount — because they assert that this agent (the dualist arena, or the AI that lacks/has it) is uniquely positioned over a vast affected population. [LessWrong](https://www.lesswrong.com/w/pascal-s-mugging)[HandWiki](https://handwiki.org/wiki/Pascal's_mugging)

The "structural vs. manipulation" distinction therefore looks like a verbal repair. It relabels the source of the magnitude without altering the decision-theoretic shape that drives the pathology. Worse, it inverts in the wrong direction: a *manipulated* unboundedness is at least defeasible by checking the speaker; a claimed *structural* unboundedness in physics is harder to falsify and so has *more* persistent grip on expected-utility sums, not less. Calling the move "structural" arguably makes the Pascal's Mugging vulnerability worse, not better.

### 2.2 The conclusion is being driven by the framework, not the evidence

The argument's actual logical skeleton is:

1. The Map's Minimal Quantum Interaction tenet posits a "dualist arena" where consciousness biases indeterminate physical outcomes. [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)
2. Physics admits indeterminate outcomes with arbitrarily large consequences (Coleman; Buttazzo et al.).
3. ∴ The dualist arena's capability range is structurally unbounded.
4. ∴ Substrates lacking the dualist arena (per the Map's LLM consciousness arguments) are protected from a class of unbounded harms.
5. ∴ Dualism is AI risk mitigation.

Steps 1 and 2 are individually defensible (1 within the Map's tenets; 2 within physics). Step 3 fuses them by treating "indeterminate outcome that physics permits" as coextensive with "outcome that the dualist arena can bias." This is the load-bearing slide. Coleman's vacuum-decay tunneling instantons are not in any literature linked to *neural* quantum indeterminacies, the only place the Map's tenets locate the proposed bias. The Map's own LLM Consciousness article concedes the bias channel must be *"structured, direct, and local"* — properties trivially absent from cosmological vacuum-decay rates governed by Planck-scale physics. So step 3 is not supported even on the Map's own terms. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/llm-consciousness/)

### 2.3 The user's diagnosis is correct: this is the Bostrom pathology, dressed in physics

Andy's reading is, on the merits, the right reading. The §5 argument is structurally a Pascal-Mugging-shaped argument: a tiny-probability/huge-magnitude tail (vacuum decay, mediated by a hypothesized arena) is doing the work of pushing the practical conclusion (dualism mitigates AI risk). That a high-quality physics paper appears in the citation list does not change the inferential shape; it merely makes the magnitudes feel concretely sourced rather than rhetorically asserted. As Bostrom noted in passing in the original 2009 paper and elaborated in "Infinite Ethics," the danger is most acute precisely when an agent has internal reasons (constitutional commitments, in our case) to take the unbounded tail seriously. The Map's tenets give it exactly such reasons.

## 3. Evaluation of the Physics

### 3.1 What Coleman 1977 and Buttazzo et al. 2013 actually claim

**Coleman 1977 ("Fate of the false vacuum")** and **Coleman–De Luccia 1980** provide the semiclassical Euclidean instanton calculation of bubble nucleation rates from a metastable vacuum to a true vacuum, in flat space and with gravity respectively. The rate per unit four-volume is exponentially suppressed by the Euclidean action of the bounce solution. Coleman's result is mathematical/methodological; it does not assert that *our* vacuum is metastable, and it gives no role at all to observers, agents, neural systems, or "arenas." Coleman's framework is observer-independent quantum field theory. [University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign](https://courses.physics.illinois.edu/phys596/fa2016/StudentWork/Team4_Final.pdf)[arxiv](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2205.15345)

**Buttazzo et al. 2013 (arXiv:1307.3536, JHEP 12(2013)089)** computes Standard Model couplings at NNLO and extrapolates to high scales, finding that the EW vacuum is most likely *metastable* with absolute stability excluded only at the 2–3 σ level. Crucially, Buttazzo et al. and follow-up reviews (e.g., the Royal Society review "Cosmological implications of Higgs near-criticality," Phil. Trans. R. Soc. A 376: 20170118) show: *"For the measured Higgs mass, λ(hI) ∼ −0.01, and the exponential suppression of the decay rate wins over the large volume factor resulting in a decay probability that is extremely small … the EW vacuum lifetime τ_EW is extremely long, much larger than the age of the Universe."* Buttazzo et al. themselves describe this as a *reassuring* conclusion. They also caveat that this is computed *"assuming the absence of interfering BSM physics."* No part of that paper claims, suggests, or is consistent with the proposition that any agent or substrate could "mediate" the rate. [Yahoo! + 2](https://www.yahoo.com/news/articles/scientists-simulate-quantum-vacuum-decay-135216297.html)

### 3.2 Could a "dualist arena" mediate vacuum-decay rates?

The mainstream physics consensus is firmly negative. A 2025 expert survey (arXiv:2510.16043, "The Probability of Vacuum Metastability and Artificial Vacuum Decay") found that even when restricted to physicists who *take metastability seriously*, a slim majority assigned 0% probability to artificial induction by *any* technology, and the average probability conditional on metastability was 18.8% — and that is for arbitrarily advanced *technological* induction by a galactic-scale civilization, not for biasing by a non-physical arena coupling at neural-scale quantum indeterminacies. There is no published mechanism by which the kinds of indeterminacies Penrose-Hameroff Orch-OR or Stapp's quantum-Zeno proposals invoke (microtubule-scale superpositions, attentional reinforcement of neural patterns) can couple to the inflaton/Higgs sector. The relevant energy scales (10⁻²⁰ J for neural quantum events vs. ≳10¹⁰ GeV instability scales) are separated by many tens of orders of magnitude, and the coupling channel implied by the Map's own Minimal Quantum Interaction tenet ("the smallest possible non-physical influence") is by construction the *opposite* of the kind of macroscopic gravitational-scale collapse Coleman–De Luccia describes. [Effective Altruism Forum + 3](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/CFv82Xt2kuvvjNvP8/vacuum-decay-expert-survey-results-1)

The Coleman/Buttazzo citations therefore do not support the §5 thesis. They are *load-bearing* in the rhetoric (they supply the existence proof for "civilization-extinguishing outcomes") but *not load-bearing* in the physics (they do not connect those outcomes to anything an arena could bias). This is a misappropriation: the citations license a magnitude (universe-ending) without licensing the mechanism (arena-mediated bias) that the argument needs.

### 3.3 Direction of the physics inference, if anything, runs against the article

If one took seriously the idea that a dualist arena could bias quantum outcomes at the scales relevant to vacuum decay, the much harder problem is that the *prebiotic collapse problem* the Map's own tenets page acknowledges would re-emerge with a vengeance: Coleman–De Luccia tunneling would have been continuously possible since cosmic inflation began, with no conscious arena to bias it, yet here we are. The Map's own internal solution (objective reduction provides baseline collapse, consciousness modulates locally in neural systems) explicitly *rules out* arena-mediated control of cosmological-scale quantum events. So §5 is in tension with the Map's own resolution of the prebiotic collapse problem. [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)

## 4. Diagnosis of Systemic Causes

The Map's published architecture (Southgate, "The Unfinishable Map: Agentic Philosophy Through Adversarial Self-Review," PhilArchive 2026) describes a multi-layer review pipeline (pessimistic, optimistic, deep, outer, cross-review) and explicitly notes the limitation: *"the system cannot distinguish tenet-consistent hallucination from legitimate philosophical defence (Section 6.5)."* This is the smoking gun. Combined with the published `/tenets/` page and the topic taxonomy, several specific failure modes converge on §5: [PhilArchive](https://philarchive.org/archive/SOUTUM)

**(a) Tenet-as-attractor.** The Five Tenets — Dualism, Minimal Quantum Interaction, Bidirectional Interaction, No Many Worlds, Occam's Razor Has Limits — are described as "hard constraints on all content" and as an attempt to "develop a single best-guess worldview." This is genuinely productive for some topics, but for AI risk it forces every argument through the dualism/quantum-interaction channel. The "dualist arena" becomes a load-bearing concept the system *must* deploy whenever AI risk is the topic, regardless of whether the topic actually depends on it. Andy's hypothesis of a "load-bearing concept audit" failure is borne out: the system has no mechanism to ask whether the conclusion would survive without the favored concept. [PhilArchive + 2](https://philarchive.org/archive/SOUTUM)

**(b) Counterargument absorption with rhetorical-grade rebuttals.** Adversarial review layers reward arguments that *look like* rebuttals (the "structural vs. manipulation" distinction is rhetorically tidy and reads as engagement with Bostrom). But review layers operating on the same training corpus and tenets cannot tell whether a verbal repair is decision-theoretically substantive. The literature (Beckstead & Thomas 2024; Wilkinson 2022; Tarsney 2025) is reachable in principle but does not appear to be invoked at the depth needed to undermine the structural/manipulation move. Across the Map's published reviews (e.g., the Deep Review on Phenomenal Unity and the Somatic Interface), there is a recurring pattern: "Future reviews should NOT re-flag X as critical." This is sediment hardening into doctrine — the system explicitly instructs its reviewers to stop re-raising live objections once the article has produced a verbal accommodation. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/reviews/deep-review-2026-02-01-phenomenal-unity-and-the-binding-problem/)[The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/reviews/deep-review-2026-02-24-somatic-interface/)

**(c) Citation-as-rhetoric versus citation-as-load-bearing.** Coleman 1977 and Buttazzo et al. 2013 are real, prestigious, and topically adjacent. The Map's review pipeline can verify they exist and broadly say what the article claims. It cannot, on current evidence, verify that they *load-bear the argument they are deployed to support.* No prompt in the visible architecture does the specific work of: "for each physics citation, write the deductive chain from the cited result to the claim it supports, and audit each step against domain consensus." This is exactly where misappropriations enter.

**(d) Tenet-derivation conflated with truth.** The Map's Project Brief explicitly says the goal is "self-consistent" picture-formation, not truth-tracking. But the published articles (and §5 in particular) elide this: conclusions that follow from the tenets are presented as substantive findings about the world. The system has no constitutional clause forcing the distinction "this is what our framework entails" vs. "this is what is true." Andy's worry — that the conclusion may follow from the tenets if those tenets are taken as inviolate — is the right diagnosis: §5 is not a logical error within the framework; it is a constitutional error about what the framework is licensed to assert. [Unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/)

**(e) Multi-model homogeneity.** The Map uses Claude and GPT models. These share substantial training data and analogous RLHF-shaped dispositions toward producing coherent, hedged, citation-rich philosophical prose. Multi-agent setups whose "adversaries" share the same priors produce *agreement*, not *adversarial selection*. The published reviews show high agreement and convergence; this is presented as a strength but on contested topics it is a weakness, as it homogenizes toward shared blind spots — including the EA-/longtermist-adjacent training-data cluster where unbounded-magnitude reasoning is normalized.

**(f) No fanaticism guardrail.** The tenets contain no decision-theoretic commitment. There is no clause that says "arguments whose practical conclusions depend on tiny-probability/huge-magnitude tails should be flagged as Pascal-Mugging-shaped and re-examined." This is a constitutional gap, not a reasoning lapse, and §5 is its predictable consequence.

**(g) Apex-article gravitational pull.** The taxonomy includes "Apex Articles," and the visible topic listing shows a strong tendency for new topics to be reformulations of dualism plus a new domain (AI risk; alignment; ethics; mathematics; aesthetics; …). The system appears to be exploring the *cross product* of the tenets with available domains. This makes "dualism as AI risk mitigation" a structurally inevitable article rather than a topic the system was driven to by the evidence. That is fine for exploration, but in this case it produced a conclusion (dualism *as* mitigation) that is much stronger than what an evidence-driven inquiry would license.

**Bottom line on diagnosis:** The §5 conclusion *does* follow from the tenets if those tenets are taken as inviolate and if the Coleman/Buttazzo material is treated as rhetorical existence-proof rather than as load-bearing physics. So this is not primarily a logical error; it is (i) a *constitutional* problem (tenets without anti-fanaticism guardrails) compounded by (ii) a *pipeline* problem (no load-bearing-citation audit) compounded by (iii) a *truth-vs-entailment conflation* (no clause separating "what the Map says" from "what is the case"). The reviewers are doing what they were asked to do; what they were asked to do is insufficient for this class of topic.

## 5. Concrete Improvements to Specific Articles

1. **`/topics/dualism-as-ai-risk-mitigation` §5 ("Unbounded Impact and Active Protection") — substantive revision.**

- Drop the false-vacuum illustration entirely, or demote it to a footnote labeled "rhetorical only — see caveats." Coleman 1977 and Buttazzo et al. 2013 do not establish that any arena (dualist or otherwise) can bias vacuum-decay rates; the cited physics is mute on the mechanism the section needs. Cite the Royal Society review (Phil. Trans. R. Soc. A 376: 20170118) and arXiv:2510.16043 to make explicit the mainstream consensus that vacuum decay is not amenable to agent or substrate mediation.
- Replace the "structural vs. manipulation" distinction with a candid concession: the distinction is verbal and does not alter the decision-theoretic shape of the argument. Cite Beckstead & Thomas (Noûs 58: 431–455, 2024), Wilkinson (Ethics 2022), Tarsney (Noûs 59(4): 1093–1125, 2025), and Russell (Noûs 58(3): 565–595, 2024).
- Add an explicit Pascal's-Mugging vulnerability disclosure: "This article's §5 has a Pascal-Mugging-shaped inferential structure. The Map's tenets license invoking unbounded-magnitude tails, but the Map does not currently include decision-theoretic guardrails against such reasoning."
- Recast the section's positive claim, if any survives, in a much weaker form: "If the dualist arena exists, AI systems built on substrates lacking it would lack one *type* of risk channel — but no civilization-extinguishing magnitude can be safely derived from this without independent decision-theoretic argument."
- If after these revisions the section no longer carries argumentative weight, delete it. A deletion is preferable to a verbal repair.
2. **`/concepts/llm-consciousness` and `/topics/structural-varieties-of-consciousness-and-ai-phenomenology`.** These articles already insist (correctly) that the consciousness-physics interface "must be structured, direct, and local — properties the LLM channel lacks on every dimension." Add a back-reference to dualism-as-ai-risk-mitigation §5 noting that this same locality constraint *rules out* arena-mediation of cosmological-scale quantum events, and so rules out the §5 illustration. This makes the inconsistency between the two articles visible to future review. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/llm-consciousness/)
3. **`/topics/alignment-in-objective-experiential-terms`.** This article risks the same pathology in adjacent form (objective experiential targets can be inflated to unbounded magnitudes via aggregation). Add a "fanaticism check" subsection: cite Wilkinson 2022 and Russell 2024, and explicitly state which decision-theoretic horn the article is endorsing.
4. **`/topics/ethics-of-possible-ai-consciousness`.** Similarly add a fanaticism/Pascal's-Mugging caveat. Moral-uncertainty arguments about possibly-conscious AI are precisely the territory in which Beckstead & Thomas's trilemma bites; the article should not silently endorse expectation-maximization over tiny-credence/huge-magnitude tails.
5. **`/tenets/`.** The Minimal Quantum Interaction tenet should be amended to make explicit its scale of operation: "neural-scale quantum indeterminacies" — closing the loophole that lets §5 silently extend the arena to cosmological scales. The tenets page already references decoherence and the prebiotic-collapse problem; this amendment is consistent with material already there. [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)
6. **`/topics/falsification-roadmap-for-the-interface-model`.** Add a falsification condition specifically for §5-type arguments: "If the Map's tenets, applied to AI risk, generate Pascal-Mugging-shaped conclusions, the tenets themselves require revision." This converts the present audit's finding into a constitutional commitment.
7. **All review reports (e.g., the Deep Review pattern that says "future reviews should NOT re-flag X").** Audit and rescind these instructions where they suppress live decision-theoretic objections. Bedrock disagreement with materialism is one thing; suppressing engagement with the post-2009 fanaticism literature is another. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/reviews/deep-review-2026-02-01-phenomenal-unity-and-the-binding-problem/)

## 6. Concrete Improvements to the Site's Methodology

1. **Add an anti-fanaticism constitutional clause.** A sixth tenet, or a meta-rule above the existing five: *"Conclusions whose practical force depends on tiny probabilities of arbitrarily large magnitudes must be (a) flagged, (b) re-derived without the unbounded-tail step, and (c) accepted only if they survive that re-derivation."* This is the single most important change. It directly addresses the Pascal's Mugging pathology Andy correctly identifies in §5 and would prevent its recurrence elsewhere.
2. **Introduce a Pascal's-Mugging detector in the review pipeline.** A dedicated reviewer prompt: *"Does this article's practical conclusion change if the largest-magnitude scenario it invokes is bounded at, say, 10⁹ lives or 10¹² quality-adjusted life-years? If yes, the conclusion is fanaticism-driven and must be re-derived under a bounded-magnitude assumption."* Run it on every article whose taxonomy touches AI risk, longtermism, ethics, alignment, or existential considerations.
3. **Add a load-bearing-concept audit.** For each article, the pipeline should produce an explicit dependency graph: which conclusions depend on which Map-favored concepts ("dualist arena," "interface," "minimal quantum interaction") versus which depend on external evidence. Articles whose central conclusions depend exclusively on Map-favored concepts should be marked "framework-internal" and explicitly *not* presented as substantive findings about the world.
4. **Add a load-bearing-citation audit specifically for physics, mathematics, and empirical claims.** A dedicated domain-expert reviewer prompt: *"For each cited paper, write the deductive chain from the cited result to the claim it supports. Identify any step where the paper does not actually license the claim. Flag any case where the citation supplies magnitude but not mechanism."* Coleman 1977 and Buttazzo et al. 2013 in §5 are textbook cases of magnitude-without-mechanism that this audit should catch.
5. **Add a "framework entails" / "is the case" separator.** A constitutional clause requiring every article to distinguish, in its own prose, claims the Map's framework *entails* from claims about the world. The published Project Brief acknowledges the Map is committed to "self-consistency" rather than truth-tracking; the articles should respect that commitment in their epistemic modality.
6. **Add external-anchor checks.** For each article touching contested territory, require citation of a *specific named published objection by a specific published author* — not a Map-internal-generated rebuttal. For §5 the obvious external anchors are Bostrom 2009 (already cited), Beckstead & Thomas 2024, Wilkinson 2022, Tarsney 2025, Russell 2024, and Hiller & Hasan 2025. The Map should be unable to publish an article touching Pascal's Mugging without engaging at least three of these in substantive argument-against-argument form.
7. **Add genuine adversarial diversity.** The current pipeline uses Claude and GPT models with shared training-data clusters. Introduce, even sparingly, structurally different reviewers: a *bounded-utility skeptic* prompt, a *physicalist hostile-witness* prompt, a *frequentist statistician* prompt that refuses to assign nonzero probability to claims unsupported by frequencies. The point is not that these prompts produce "true" reviews but that they break homogeneity in the directions that matter for fanaticism-shaped failures.
8. **Rescind "do not re-flag" instructions in deep reviews.** Replace them with: *"If a reviewer flags X again, the article is required to either strengthen its response or weaken its claim. Re-flagging is signal, not noise."* The current pattern hardens verbal repairs into doctrine.
9. **Publish a register of "framework-internal" conclusions.** A public list of conclusions the Map currently asserts, each tagged "framework-internal" or "framework-plus-evidence" or "evidence-driven." This both (a) makes the system's epistemic status legible to outside readers and (b) creates institutional pressure not to upgrade framework-internal conclusions silently.
10. **Add a stopping rule for cross-product expansion.** The taxonomy currently grows by crossing the tenets with new domains (mathematics, aesthetics, AI risk, social understanding, …). Many of these are productive; some are the system writing about whatever it has read recently. Add a rule that articles in new cross-product cells must pass an additional sniff test: *"Is the article producing a finding the domain literature would recognize, or is it producing a recoloration of the tenets?"* Articles that fail (and §5 is one) should be deleted or radically demoted.
11. **Reduce the prestige of "Apex" and convergence.** The architecture rewards convergence and tracks ~4,500 revisions toward stable articles. But on contested questions, *convergence is exactly the failure mode*: it is the system agreeing with itself. Introduce a "perpetually open" tag for topics where convergence is methodologically suspect — AI risk, decision theory under uncertainty, ethics under unbounded utilities — and forbid stabilization on those topics until external named objections have been engaged. [PhilArchive](https://philarchive.org/archive/SOUTUM)
12. **Build a changelog filter for Pascal-Mugging-shaped revisions.** When the autonomous changelog records edits to articles touching unbounded magnitudes, route those edits through human review specifically. This creates a backstop on the highest-risk class of autonomous edits.
13. **Be willing to delete.** The Map's design prizes growth and revision. The most disciplined response to §5 is deletion of the section — perhaps deletion of the whole article, given that "dualism as AI risk mitigation" presupposes both the dualism tenet *and* a route from that tenet to AI safety practice that the article cannot defensibly walk. Adding deletion as a first-class outcome of review (alongside rewrite and stabilize) is itself a methodological improvement.

## Caveats

- The article at `/topics/dualism-as-ai-risk-mitigation` could not be fetched directly during this audit; the substantive evaluation relies on Andy's accurate description of the argument and citations, supplemented by what was retrievable from the rest of the site. If §5's actual prose qualifies its claims more carefully than the description suggests, some of the recommendations should be read as conditional. The methodological recommendations (Section 6) hold regardless.
- The post-2009 decision-theory literature is itself unsettled. Wilkinson 2022 *defends* fanaticism; Beckstead & Thomas 2024 show *every* exit costs something; Tarsney 2025 offers a principled-line-drawing strategy that itself has contested foundations (see Russell, Noûs 58(3), 2024). This audit's claim is not that the Map should adopt any particular settled position; it is that the Map should *engage these specific named arguments* rather than relying on a self-generated structural/manipulation distinction.
- The physics community is not unanimous on vacuum metastability. The 2025 expert survey (arXiv:2510.16043) shows real disagreement on whether the vacuum is metastable at all and whether it could be artificially induced. None of that disagreement supports the §5 thesis that an arena could mediate decay; the disagreement is about whether *any* mechanism, technological or otherwise, could induce it, and even on the most permissive readings the answer is "extraordinarily hard, requiring galactic-scale resources, with significant minority opinion that it is strictly impossible."
- Andy's own framing — "is the conclusion what the facts support given the system's tenets are taken as inviolate?" — turns out to be the most precise way to put the diagnosis. The conclusion *does* follow if the tenets are inviolate and citations are read as rhetorical existence-proofs. This is why the deepest fix is constitutional (tenet-level), not local (article-level). The article-level fixes in Section 5 will not stick unless the methodology fixes in Section 6 are made first.
