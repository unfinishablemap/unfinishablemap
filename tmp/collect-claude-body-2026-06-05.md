# Referee Report: "Edge-States and the Probing of Voids" (The Unfinishable Map)

## TL;DR

- The article is well-read and unusually honest about its own limits, but its central argument does not earn the dualist conclusion: it repeatedly redescribes what physicalist cognitive architecture already predicts ("coherence inflation") and rests its decisive move on a "convergence costs more theoretical machinery on the physicalist side" claim that is asserted, not demonstrated.
- Its single most serious empirical error is the co-optation of Thomas Metzinger: the article borrows his "zero-person perspective" and minimal-phenomenal-experience (MPE) framework as evidence that consciousness is "not identical with its contents," while omitting that Metzinger advances MPE explicitly as a naturalistic, brain-based "minimal model explanation" and warns against exactly the metaphysical inference the article makes.
- Empirical currency lags by roughly 5 years on its fastest-moving claims: REBUS is treated as the settled predictive-processing story (anchored to Carhart-Harris & Friston 2019, ignoring ALBUS/SEBUS, REBAS, and Siegel et al.'s 2024 *Nature* desynchronization study), DMN claims ignore the connectivity-vs-activity nuance, and NDE timing claims under-engage the Xu/Borjigin 2023 gamma-surge data and the AWARE-II EEG findings the site's own changelog shows it elsewhere cites.

## Key Findings

1. **The transcendence-vs-edge-mapping dichotomy is the article's best feature; the dualist verdict is its weakest.** The framing is genuinely useful and the article's own "What Would Distinguish" section honestly concedes that the evidence "fits edge-mapping." But the "Relation to Site Perspective" section then reverses field and treats the same evidence as raising explanatory cost on physicalism. This is the constitutional-attractor effect in action.
2. **Coherence inflation is pervasive.** The "reducing valve," DMN suppression, REBUS, and the zero-person perspective are each fully predicted by mainstream cognitive neuroscience. The article concedes "Physicalism can accommodate any individual finding" and then claims "the convergence costs more theoretical machinery on the physicalist side" — but never specifies what machinery, or why a single shared mechanism (relaxation of high-level priors / DMN disintegration) explaining multiple edge-states is *more* costly rather than *more parsimonious*. This is a "common-cause null" failure: one mechanism explains the convergence, which cuts against, not for, dualism.
3. **Metzinger is misappropriated.** This is the report's gravest accuracy finding and is detailed in §2c.
4. **Empirical literature lags by roughly 5 years on its fastest-moving claims.** The freshest directly relevant work is 2023–2025 (Siegel et al., *Nature* 2024; Xu/Borjigin, *PNAS* 2023; Parnia et al. AWARE-II, *Resuscitation* 2023), versus the article's REBUS anchor of Carhart-Harris & Friston 2019 — and some of the newer work is demonstrably cited elsewhere on the site (per the changelog's near-death-experiences concept article).
5. **The constructivist critique of perennialism is named but not engaged in its strongest form.** The article's "structural vs content" rescue of the common core is exactly the move Katz's strongest followers anticipate and reject.

## Details

### 1. Argumentative rigour

**The central inference is invalid as stated.** The article's spine is: (i) radically different methods (meditation, psychedelics, NDE, aesthetic absorption, philosophy) converge on a common edge-phenomenology; (ii) this convergence "is consistent with consciousness as something over and above neural processing"; (iii) therefore physicalism pays a higher explanatory cost. Step (iii) does not follow from "is consistent with." Consistency is symmetric — the convergence is equally consistent with a single physical mechanism (high-level prior relaxation / DMN disintegration) being the common cause of all five routes. The article actually states the defeater itself ("Physicalism can accommodate any individual finding") and then does not neutralise it; it simply asserts that aggregation reverses the cost. No argument is given for why N findings explained by *one* mechanism is costlier than the dualist alternative, which must additionally posit (a) a non-physical consciousness, (b) an interface, and (c) a reason the interface produces exactly the phenomenology a filtering brain would produce.

**Question-begging via the "filter" framing.** The Bergson/Huxley reducing-valve picture is presented as "partial support" from "contemporary neuroscience," but the neuroscience (reduced DMN signal correlating with increased subjective intensity) is equally the prediction of the *production* model once you note that subjective intensity and metabolic/BOLD activity are not identical. The article concedes BOLD "is an indirect proxy" — but having conceded this, it cannot use the BOLD decrease as evidence *for* filtering over production, because the proxy gap cuts both ways. The "filter model predicts less filtering yielding more experience" line treats a redescription as a confirmation.

**Tenet leakage into supposedly neutral exposition.** Terms like "edge-territory," "what lies beyond," "contact with representationally closed territory," and "the void carries positive features" are not neutral phenomenological descriptions; they presuppose that there is a territory beyond the boundary to be in contact with — which is precisely the transcendence reading the article claims to be holding open. The "Dissolving-Insight Phenomenon" section is the clearest case: the claim that dissolving insights "may mark contact with representationally closed territory, unrecoverable because we have no containers for it" is the transcendence reading smuggled in as a description, then defended by demanding the confabulation theorist "specify the mechanism." This reverses the burden of proof: the party positing contact with closed territory owes the mechanism, not the deflationist.

**One genuinely sound sub-argument.** The "What Would Distinguish" section (convergence scope, practical predictions, intersubjective verification) is a real, falsifiable test battery, and the article's honest verdict — phenomenological convergence robust, propositional convergence limited, practical yield "therapeutic but not clearly epistemically novel," intersubjective verification "elusive" — is a model of calibration. The problem is that this verdict supports edge-mapping, and the article does not let it govern the conclusion.

### 2. Empirical accuracy and currency

**(a) REBUS.** The citation (Carhart-Harris & Friston 2019, *Pharmacological Reviews* 71(3):316–344, DOI 10.1124/pr.118.017160) is accurate, and the gloss ("relax high-level priors, liberate bottom-up flow") is faithful. But the representation is frozen at 2019. The article does not engage: (i) **ALBUS** (Safron and colleagues, *Neuroscience of Consciousness* 2025, niae038), which argues that 5-HT2A agonism can *strengthen* beliefs (SEBUS effects) as well as relax them, and that "the complexity of cortical information flows clearly cannot be reduced to either 5-HT2A-mediated SEBUS or REBUS effects"; (ii) **REBAS** (Zeifman et al., *Scientific Reports* 2025, 15:3651), a direct empirical test (N=11) whose authors concede the results "may also be accounted for by alternative neural models such as ALBUS"; (iii) the enactive/pluralist critique (*Frontiers in Psychology* 2023, 14:1100058) that REBUS "may neglect other important aspects, other possible mechanisms." Crucially for the article's thesis: REBUS is a *physicalist predictive-processing* model. It is the strongest version of the edge-mapping reading — the brain modelling and relaxing its own priors. The article correctly notes the Map "borrows REBUS's structural insight while reinterpreting it," but the reinterpretation ("constraints may operate on consciousness rather than constituting it") is asserted, and REBUS's own framework gives no purchase for it. [Frontiers](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2023.1100058/full)

**(b) DMN.** The claim that psychedelics produce "default mode network reduction correlating with these states" is broadly supported but stated too flatly. Current literature requires three corrections the article omits: (i) the key 2024 finding is **desynchronization**, not simple "reduction" — Siegel et al. (*Nature* 2024, 632(8023):131–138) used "longitudinal precision functional mapping (roughly 18 magnetic resonance imaging visits per participant)" to show psilocybin "massively disrupted functional connectivity (FC) in cortex and subcortex, acutely causing more than threefold greater change than methylphenidate," with effects strongest in the DMN, and a persistent hippocampal–DMN FC decrease "lasting for weeks" (and, per the preprint, normalizing by 6 months — so not permanent); (ii) for **meditation**, the picture is connectivity-pattern change, not uniform suppression — Brewer et al. (PNAS 2011) found relatively *increased* PCC–prefrontal coupling in experienced meditators even at rest, and some studies report increased frontal-DMN connectivity, so "DMN suppression = ego dissolution" is an oversimplification; (iii) the DMN-as-ego equation is itself contested (Gattuso et al. 2023 systematic review concluded DMN's exact therapeutic role remains unresolved). The article's "self-referential processing quiets while awareness continues" is defensible as a gloss but presented with more confidence than the heterogeneous literature supports.

**(c) Metzinger — the most serious misrepresentation.** The article attributes to Metzinger a "zero-person perspective" in which "awareness continues without the usual subject-object structure" and then draws the inference: "if consciousness can operate stripped of all content yet remain aware, it is not identical with its contents or with the self-model normally present... The zero-person perspective may reveal what remains when everything else is subtracted." This co-opts Metzinger's framework while inverting his conclusion. Verified facts: (i) "zero-person perspective" is a genuine Metzinger term, but from *The Elephant and the Blind: The Experience of Pure Consciousness* (MIT Press, 2024) — the article's citation of the title and publisher is correct; (ii) Metzinger advances MPE explicitly as a **naturalistic "minimal model explanation"** — his 2020 paper (*Philosophy and the Mind Sciences* 1(I), DOI 10.33735/phimisci.2020.I.46) states the approach "operates under naturalistic background assumptions" and hypothesises that pure awareness "really is the content of a predictive model, namely, a Bayesian representation of tonic alertness," realised by brain properties that "cannot exist without the brain"; (iii) Metzinger is an eliminativist about the self — *Being No One* (MIT Press, 2003) argues that "no such things as selves exist in the world. Nobody ever was or had a self," the conscious self being "the content of a model created by our brain" (a transparent representational model); see also *The Ego Tunnel* (2009); (iv) in the 2024 book he names an "E-fallacy" warning *against* treating phenomenological insight as "a direct revelation of metaphysical reality." So the very figure the article enlists to suggest consciousness is "not identical with its contents" holds that pure consciousness is a brain-generated Bayesian model and explicitly warns against the article's inference. The phrase "unobstructed epistemic space" is genuine Metzinger language; the description of being "completely open to the possibility of any epistemic state" is a faithful paraphrase rather than a verified verbatim quote and should not be presented in quotation marks without a page cite. This is textbook counterargument absorption: the strongest naturalist account of selfless experience is cited as if it were an ally. [New Books Network + 4](https://newbooksnetwork.com/the-elephant-and-the-blind)

**(d) NDE timing.** The article's handling is comparatively careful — it explicitly cautions that "precise timing relative to brain function remains hard to establish" and concedes NDEs "support but do not prove transcendence." But it is under-current and one-sided. It does not cite the two most relevant recent datasets: (i) **Xu/Borjigin et al. (PNAS 2023, 120(19):e2216268120)** — a gamma-power surge and increased posterior "hot zone" connectivity in 2 of 4 dying patients that appeared "within 30 seconds to two minutes after their ventilators were removed," with the authors concluding "global hypoxia markedly stimulated gamma activities in two of the patients" and that "the dying brain can still be active." This supplies a *physicalist* candidate mechanism for lucid end-of-life experience — and the article's own changelog shows the site cites this finding in its near-death-experiences concept article, so the omission here is an internal inconsistency. (ii) **AWARE-II (Parnia et al., *Resuscitation* 2023, 191:109903)** — of 567 in-hospital cardiac arrests, 53 survived, 28 were interviewed, 11 reported memories suggestive of consciousness; importantly, "despite marked cerebral ischemia (Mean rSO2 = 43%) normal EEG activity (delta, theta and alpha) consistent with consciousness emerged as long as 35–60 minutes into CPR," which Parnia frames as a possible biomarker of consciousness but which Greyson & van Lommel (2023) note "may have been due to the external chest compressions of CPR—and/or to electrical defibrillation." The "flat EEG" framing in the article ("sometimes during clinical death with flat EEG") is the contested claim; the well-supported finding is that the dying/resuscitated brain is far more electrically active than the old "flatline" picture assumed — which favours a neuro-reductive reading. The REM-intrusion association is also unmentioned: Kondziella, Dreier & Olsen (PeerJ 2019, 7:e7585) found NDEs in "106 of 1,034 participants (10%; 95% CI [8.5–12%])," with REM intrusion present in 47% of NDE-reporters versus 14% of non-reporters (multivariate OR 2.85, 95% CI [1.68–4.88]). Hypoxia/disinhibition models (Blackmore) and the 2025 Martial et al. multifactorial NDE model (*Nature Reviews Neurology*) plus the rebuttal exchange would be the current anchor.

**(e) Common-core / perennialism.** The representation of Stace and James is accurate (ineffability, noetic quality, transiency, passivity for James; introvertive "unitary consciousness" for Stace), and Katz's 1978 citation is correct. The article fairly states the constructivist objection. But its rescue — conceding the objection "has force at the level of content" while claiming "it has less force at the level of structural features" — is exactly the contested move, not a resolution. Katz's core epistemological claim ("there are no pure [unmediated] experiences") targets the structural level too: if concepts shape experience all the way down, the apophatic "shape of approach" is itself tradition-trained, not a culture-independent invariant. The article should engage Proudfoot's *Religious Experience* (the "attribution" critique that the noetic quality is a post-hoc interpretation) and the empirical perennialism of Hood's Mysticism Scale, which is the live battleground, plus Evan Thompson's convergence-skepticism (which the site's own changelog notes it had to correct elsewhere after mischaracterising him as a convergence proponent). As it stands the perennialist reading is privileged without earning it.

### 3. Where the dualist framing over-reaches

- "The convergence costs more theoretical machinery on the physicalist side" — asserted, never cashed out. This is the load-bearing sentence of the whole piece and it has no supporting argument. **This is the central common-cause-null failure.**
- "If consciousness operates when the brain is functionally absent, the brain may be an interface rather than a generator" — the antecedent ("functionally absent") is precisely what Xu/Borjigin 2023 and AWARE-II render doubtful; the brain is demonstrably *not* functionally absent in the windows where lucid experience is plausible.
- The Minimal Quantum Interaction paragraph ("relaxing [priors] might preserve quantum coherence at the interface") is flagged by the article as "speculative," which is appropriate, but it is a clean instance of the constitutional attractor: a predictive-processing finding is routed into the quantum-interface channel with no mechanism and no evidential warrant. It should be cut or quarantined more sharply.
- The Bidirectional Interaction paragraph ("deliberately approaching the edge... demonstrates consciousness influencing physical processes") is question-begging: that intention alters brain function is fully consistent with intentions *being* brain states. "The causal arrow runs from intention to neural state, not merely the reverse" assumes the non-identity it needs to prove.

### 4. Strongest physicalist counterarguments the article neglects

In the physicalists' own strongest framing:

1. **Predictive processing IS the edge-mapping reading, and it is the default.** REBUS, ALBUS, and Letheby/Gerrans's "predictive self-binding" account all say altered states reveal the *model* — the brain's generative priors and self-model — not territory beyond it. When priors relax (REBUS) or self-binding unbinds (Letheby), the system experiences its own architecture with the usual interpretive overlay stripped away. That is precisely "charting the boundary of cognitive limits from within." The article never confronts that its own preferred mechanism (REBUS) is the deflationary reading's best engine.
2. **Metzinger's self-model theory is a complete physicalist account of selfless experience.** Ego dissolution and the zero-person perspective are exactly what you would expect if the self is a transparent representational model that can be dismantled. No "consciousness over and above neural processing" is required; the experience of awareness-without-a-subject is the experience of a self-model going offline while tonic-alertness representation persists.
3. **NDEs have a converging neuro-reductive account.** Gamma surge at death (Xu/Borjigin 2023), REM intrusion (Kondziella 2019), hypoxia/hypercapnia and cortical disinhibition (Blackmore), and ketamine/DMT-like endogenous mechanisms (Martial et al. 2025) jointly predict lucid, "realer-than-real," tunnel-and-light phenomenology *as a product of a dying-but-active brain*. The article's "minimal brain function" premise is being overtaken by evidence of a hyperactive transitional state.
4. **Illusionism (Frankish) dissolves the explanandum.** If phenomenal properties are introspective misrepresentations (the "illusion problem"), then "ineffability with noesis" and "edge-clarity" are exactly the kind of systematic introspective misjudgement illusionism predicts — the conviction of knowing without articulable content is a confabulated phenomenal judgement, not contact with closed territory. The article's demand that the confabulation theorist "specify the mechanism" is answered by Frankish's introspective-opacity model and is not engaged.
5. **The deflationary "intense-experience-plus-interpretation" account of mysticism** (Proudfoot; Letheby's naturalised spirituality, Zygon 2017 / *Philosophy of Psychedelics*, OUP 2021) explains the noetic quality as affect-driven attribution, not veridical insight — and does so while preserving the experiences' psychological and therapeutic value. Gładziejewski (whom the article cites, *Review of Philosophy and Psychology* 2025, 16:175–197, DOI 10.1007/s13164-023-00709-6) is actually a *naturalist*: he argues psychedelic metaphysical beliefs can be rationally integrated *under predictive processing*, and that the constructed sense of self "can no longer act as an unjustified justifier of metaphysical claims." The article cites him for the permissive half and omits the deflationary frame. [Springer](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s13164-023-00709-6)
6. **Parsimony cuts the other way.** The article's own admission that transcendence "does not rule out... limits we cannot communicate about" is the tell: an in-principle unverifiable territory is exactly what Occam's razor counsels against positing. The inability to distinguish "accessing territory" from "mapping limits" is not neutral between the readings — under any standard parsimony norm it favours the reading that posits no extra territory.

## Recommendations

### (A) Article-specific improvements to "Edge-States and the Probing of Voids"

1. **Cash out or retract the load-bearing claim.** Either specify exactly what "theoretical machinery" physicalism must add to explain the *convergence* (not the individual findings), and why that exceeds the dualist ledger of [non-physical consciousness + interface + filter-mimicry], or downgrade the Dualism paragraph to: "the convergence is equally consistent with both readings and does not discriminate." Benchmark that would change this: a worked cost comparison showing the physicalist common-cause account requires more independent posits than the interface account.
2. **Rewrite the Metzinger section.** State explicitly that Metzinger advances MPE as a naturalistic minimal-model explanation (Bayesian representation of tonic alertness), is an eliminativist about the self, and warns against the "E-fallacy" of reading metaphysics off phenomenology. Then make the Map's disagreement *as* a disagreement, in Metzinger's own framing, rather than borrowing his vocabulary for the opposite conclusion. Remove or footnote-qualify the "completely open to the possibility of any epistemic state" phrase unless a 2024-book page cite is supplied.
3. **Update REBUS to 2024–2025.** Add ALBUS/SEBUS (Safron et al., *Neuroscience of Consciousness* 2025) and REBAS (Zeifman et al., *Scientific Reports* 2025), and acknowledge that REBUS is the strongest *physicalist edge-mapping* engine — then argue against it on its own terms if the Map wishes to.
4. **Correct the DMN claims.** Replace "DMN reduction" with the desynchronization finding (Siegel et al., *Nature* 2024) and add the meditation connectivity-vs-activity nuance (Brewer et al. PNAS 2011; increased PCC–PFC coupling). Flag that the DMN-as-ego equation is contested.
5. **Bring NDE up to date and resolve the internal inconsistency.** Cite Xu/Borjigin 2023 (PNAS) and AWARE-II (Parnia 2023, *Resuscitation*) directly here, as the near-death-experiences concept article already does; replace "flat EEG / functionally absent" with the active-transitional-state framing; add REM intrusion (Kondziella 2019) and the Martial et al. 2025 multifactorial model. Reframe NDEs as the *weakest*, not a supporting, leg.
6. **Engage the constructivist critique at the structural level.** Add Proudfoot's attribution critique and Hood's Mysticism Scale, and concede that Katz's "no pure experiences" claim targets structure, not just content. Add Evan Thompson as a convergence-skeptic.
7. **Add a substantive illusionism paragraph.** Frankish's introspective-opacity model is the direct answer to the dissolving-insight "asymmetry" the article treats as unexplained; engage it rather than demanding a mechanism it already supplies.
8. **Quarantine the quantum paragraph.** The Minimal Quantum Interaction connection adds no evidential content and exemplifies the attractor effect; cut it or move it to an explicitly speculative appendix.
9. **Let the honest verdict govern.** The "What Would Distinguish" section already reaches the right calibrated conclusion (the evidence fits edge-mapping). Make that the article's conclusion, with transcendence held open as an explicitly unverifiable, parsimony-disfavoured possibility.

### (B) Site-wide methodology improvements to the generation/review pipeline

1. **Add a "common-cause-null" gate to the deep-review checklist.** Any article that aggregates multiple findings into "physicalism pays a higher cost" must pass an explicit test: does a single shared mechanism explain the convergence? If yes, the aggregation argument fails and must be retracted or reframed. (The changelog shows the project already has a "common-cause-null" concept — it should be a mandatory gate, not an occasional cross-link.)
2. **Add a "co-optation check" for cited opponents.** When a physicalist/eliminativist author (Metzinger, Frankish, Dennett, Letheby) is cited, the reviewer must confirm the citation does not borrow the author's framework while reversing the author's conclusion. The Metzinger error here would have been caught by such a gate.
3. **Enforce an empirical-currency window.** For any article resting on a fast-moving empirical literature (psychedelics, DMN, NDE neuroscience), require at least one citation from the last 24 months and a check against the most-cited recent review. The changelog's own near-death-experiences article cites Xu/Borjigin 2023 and AWARE-II; cross-article citation propagation should have carried these into this article.
4. **Tighten the tenet-leakage / label-leakage scan to catch presupposition-laden nouns,** not just verbs and capitalisation. "Territory," "what lies beyond," "contact with closed territory," and "edge-territory" are interpretive commitments masquerading as description; the current anchoring/calibration tooling (which targets strong-assertion verbs and hedge density) appears to miss noun-level question-begging.
5. **Make burden-of-proof symmetric in "Mode Two" engagements.** The pipeline's habit of answering deflationary accounts by demanding they "specify the mechanism" should be flagged: when the Map's own reading also lacks a specified mechanism, the demand is rhetorically asymmetric and should be scored as an unearned rebuttal.
6. **Add a "does the honest verdict govern the conclusion?" check.** This article (and, per the changelog, several others) contains a well-calibrated evidential verdict that is then overridden by the "Relation to Site Perspective" section. Reviewers should verify that the site-perspective section does not contradict the article's own evidential assessment.

## Caveats

- This audit is of the live article text fetched 5 June 2026 (last modified 2026-05-29) plus the changelog; I did not re-read every cross-linked article (filter-theory, minimal-consciousness, apophatic-cartography), so some of my "neglect" findings may be partially addressed in linked pages, though the burden is on the target article to engage them.
- Several page-specific quotes attributed to Metzinger's 2024 book are corroborated via two reliable secondary sources rather than the primary text; the substance of his naturalism is confirmed from his open-access 2020 paper directly.
- I have flagged contested empirical claims (AWARE-II EEG interpretation; DMN-as-ego) as contested rather than adjudicating them; the NDE literature in particular is actively disputed and my recommendation is for balanced engagement, not adoption of either side.
- My verdict that parsimony favours edge-mapping is itself a philosophical position; the article is entitled to contest the Map's bounded application of Occam's razor, but it must do so explicitly rather than by assertion.
