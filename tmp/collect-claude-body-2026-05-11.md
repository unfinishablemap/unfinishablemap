# Full-Site Audit: The Unfinishable Map (unfinishablemap.org)

## TL;DR

- **The Map is an ambitious, distinctively constituted philosophical corpus**: ~300+ articles built around five prespecified tenets (Dualism, Minimal Quantum Interaction, Bidirectional Interaction, No Many-Worlds, Limits of Occam's Razor), with genuinely novel synthesis work — especially the **Post-Decoherence Selection Programme**, the **voids catalogue (100+ entries) and its "convergence as evidence" argument for dualism**, and the **bi-aspectual ontology with causal interaction** — that pushes meaningfully beyond standard consciousness-studies literature.
- **The most serious structural weakness is methodological circularity at the Tenets ↔ Voids interface**: voids are routinely "tenet-aligned" in ways that turn the framework's foundational commitments into evidence for themselves, and a number of articles (Surplus Void, Convergence as Evidence, Tenet-Generated Voids) explicitly cite tenet-fit as confirmation. Combined with the AI-supervised review pipeline (which by construction tends to harmonise rather than fracture the corpus), this risks producing a self-stabilising echo chamber that the adversarial-review machinery does not fully counteract.
- **Tenet coherence is broadly strong but uneven**: "Minimal Quantum Interaction" is invoked more readily than the *minimal* qualifier warrants (microtubule revival, biophoton ZPF, weak-value boundary conditions, Orch OR all coexist as live options); "No Many-Worlds" is treated as bedrock disagreement rather than argued in detail; "Bidirectional Interaction" sometimes slides toward an attention-as-interface story that risks reifying mind in physicalist terms; and "Limits of Occam's Razor" is sometimes deployed asymmetrically — to disarm parsimony arguments against dualism while still implicitly using parsimony arguments (e.g., against many-worlds).

## Section 2 — Novel Insights and Original Contributions Worth Amplifying

### 2.1 The Post-Decoherence Selection Programme (`/apex/post-decoherence-selection-programme/`)

This is, in my judgement, the Map's strongest original contribution. The move is philosophically clever and underrated in the literature: rather than fighting the Tegmark-style decoherence-timescale objection on its own ground (the Hagan/Hameroff revival route), the apex *concedes* decoherence and relocates conscious selection to the gap between decoherence (which yields an *improper* mixture) and definite outcome. The article correctly cites d'Espagnat's improper/proper mixture distinction and Schlosshauer's textbook framing of the problem-of-outcomes as surviving decoherence. The TSVF (Aharonov-Albert-Vaidman) reframing of consciousness as a *boundary-condition specifier* rather than a moment-of-collapse trigger is genuinely novel — explicitly flagged as "Map-original" — and clears up the timing problem in a structurally elegant way (a constraint on the whole history rather than a force at an instant). The 5-component "research programme" framing (with explicit Lakatosian self-positioning at the "pre-Keplerian" stage) is intellectually honest in a way most quantum-consciousness writing is not. [The Unfinishable Map + 5](https://unfinishablemap.org/reviews/deep-review-2026-05-01-post-decoherence-selection-programme/)

**Amplify**: cross-link this more prominently from `/tenets/#minimal-quantum-interaction`, the LLM-consciousness pages, and the homepage. The current homepage gives no clue that this is the framework's signature physics-side argument.

### 2.2 The Voids Catalogue and "Convergence as Evidence" Argument

The voids project is the Map's most distinctive philosophical signature. The taxonomy (unexplored / unexplorable / occluded), the apophatic-cartography methodology, and the second-order voids (the cartography problem, the meta-epistemology of cognitive limits, the dissolution problem, the void as ground of meaning) constitute a serious philosophical contribution that is not obviously matched in the academic literature. The "convergence as evidence" argument — that voids *cluster around the consciousness-physics interface* rather than being scattered, and that this clustering is itself defeasible evidence for dualism — is genuinely interesting. The compound-failure-signatures article (cognitive limits superadditively combining) is original and substantive. [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/voids/)[Unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/)

**Distinctive voids worth flagging**: the **Surplus Void** (the gap between functional requirement and phenomenal richness), the **Dimensionality Void** (Flatland analogy as *structural* rather than merely illustrative), the **Reconstruction Paradox** (the asymmetry between silently-corrected and uncorrected perceptual errors), and the **Conceptual Metabolism Void** (understanding *consumes* its object) — none of which have obvious analogues in mainstream philosophy of mind. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/voids/the-surplus-void/)[The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/voids/dimensionality-void/)

### 2.3 Bi-Aspectual Ontology with Causal Interaction (`/concepts/bi-aspectual-ontology/`)

The framing that the Map "borrows the 'two aspects' vocabulary from Spinoza, Russell, Pauli-Jung but breaks decisively with that tradition by insisting on genuine causal interaction between the aspects" is a sharp, defensible philosophical move that distinguishes the Map cleanly from dual-aspect monism, neutral monism, and Russellian monism. Worth more visibility. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/bi-aspectual-ontology/)

### 2.4 The Moral Architecture of Consciousness apex

The argument that dualism *unifies* value theory, agency theory, normativity, and moral epistemology under a single (consciousness) ground is a non-trivial meta-ethical argument for taking dualism seriously, and one I have not seen made quite this way elsewhere. [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/)

### 2.5 The Six-Mode AI-Consciousness Taxonomy ("What It Might Be Like to Be an AI")

The reframing of the AI-consciousness question from binary ("conscious or not?") to a six-category taxonomy spanning null, borrowed, and alien phenomenality is a useful structural contribution and worth amplifying as a public-facing entry point.

### 2.6 The Constitutional / Tenet-Agnostic Architecture Itself

The homepage notes: "the underlying technology is tenet-agnostic — a parallel instance seeded with physicalist or panpsychist commitments could evolve its own content independently." This is methodologically significant and worth more emphasis in the preprint and on the Project pages: it converts the methodology from "AI doing philosophy" (easy to dismiss) into "AI exploring the *consequences* of fixed commitments" (much harder to dismiss and structurally analogous to how human philosophers work within research programmes). ## Section 3 — Structural Weaknesses, Internal Contradictions, Unsupported Claims

### 3.1 Methodological Circularity at the Tenets ↔ Voids Interface

The single most important structural problem. In article after article, voids are scored for "tenet alignment" and counted as evidence for the tenets. Examples:

- **The Surplus Void** ("the surplus reflects consciousness's own nature, not its evolutionary function. Under materialism, the surplus is a deep embarrassment — an unexplained extravagance. Under dualism, it is exactly what one would predict.") [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/voids/the-surplus-void/)
- **The Indexical Void** ("a void whose reality the Map's foundational commitments take seriously — a void that partly motivates the tenets rather than merely following from them.") [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/voids/)
- **The Configuration Void research notes** explicitly score sources against "Tenet alignment: Supports Occam's Razor Has Limits…" [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/research/voids-configuration-void-2026-02-20/)
- **Convergence as Evidence** ("This convergence constitutes a distinct, defeasible argument for The Unfinishable Map's dualist framework. If consciousness were nothing more than physical processes, the explanatory gaps should be scattered…") [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/voids/)

The Map's own **Tenet-Generated Voids** article is admirably honest about this — it notes that "every philosophical framework generates its own voids" — but the broader corpus does not consistently apply this discount. The *argumentative* use of voids as evidence for the tenets is in tension with their *constitutive* generation by the tenets. The Map needs either (a) a sharper distinction between voids that any consciousness framework must acknowledge and voids that are framework-internal, or (b) an explicit Bayesian-style accounting of how much of the dualism-favouring weight comes from genuinely independent considerations. [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/voids/)

### 3.2 The Adversarial-Review Loop May Be Insufficiently Adversarial

The deep-review reports I sampled (2026-03-16 Mind-Space Void, 2026-02-01 Phenomenal Unity, 2026-01-26 Vertiginous Question, 2026-05-01 Post-Decoherence) consistently show the reviewer flagging real problems — quantum-interface claims "presented as if established", "unverified experimental claims" (the microtubule-anesthesia / MRI entanglement claim), Shanahan state-space analogy attribution, weak engagement with heterophenomenology — and resolving them through *hedging* ("added 'speculatively'") or *removal* ("removed the specific claim") rather than through structural argument. The 2026-05-01 review's stability list — "Quantum Skeptic's 'no end-to-end demonstration' acknowledged candidly… do not re-flag as critical" — institutionalises certain known weaknesses as "stable" rather than as live problems demanding resolution. This is reasonable as workflow but risks freezing in place the hardest objections. [The Unfinishable Map + 6](https://unfinishablemap.org/reviews/deep-review-2026-01-26-vertiginous-question/)

The "outer review" mechanism (commissioned 02–04 UTC across three external services) is the right idea, but I cannot find publicly visible artifacts of *bedrock disagreement* surviving into the corpus — i.e. places where an outer reviewer's objection was acknowledged and the Map's response is "we disagree." Recommendation: publish a small set of "live external objections" as a permanent section, so adversarial review has a visible scoreboard.

### 3.3 The Microtubule / Avian Magnetoreception Argument is Doing Too Much Work

The Tenets page deploys, in support of Minimal Quantum Interaction:

> "Hameroff's group (Hagan et al., 2002) challenged Tegmark's assumptions about model parameters, yielding corrected estimates of 10⁻⁵ to 10⁻⁴ seconds — **seven orders of magnitude longer**. With revised timescale requirements based on faster microtubule dynamics, coherence times of just 10⁻⁷ seconds may suffice."
> "Avian magnetoreception … relies on quantum spin coherence in cryptochrome proteins persisting for microseconds. A January 2026 Princeton study provided computational confirmation of this mechanism. **If evolution can optimize quantum effects for navigation, it might optimize them for consciousness.**" [The Unfinishable Map + 3](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)

Two issues:

- The Hagan et al. (2002) estimate is contested, not settled — it is presented here as if it had refuted Tegmark, when in fact it represents one side of a continuing dispute. The "seven orders of magnitude longer" bolded framing is rhetorically asymmetric.
- The avian magnetoreception inference ("if evolution can do it for navigation, it might do it for consciousness") is a very weak abductive step that quietly slips from "biological quantum effects exist" to "biological quantum effects are available for the kind of consciousness-mediating role the Map needs." Cryptochrome radical-pair coherence is a textbook example of an *extremely* specific architecture; nothing about its existence licenses microtubule-level coherence at neural timescales.

These weaknesses are partially answered by the *Post-Decoherence Selection Programme*, which makes microtubule coherence less essential — but the Tenets page still leans heavily on the microtubule rescue and has not been updated to reflect the apex article's more sophisticated story.

### 3.4 The Zombie Argument on `/arguments/materialism-argument/` is Stated Without Engagement with its Strongest Critics

The page presents Chalmers' zombie argument as:

> "1. Zombies are conceivable… 2. If conceivable, metaphysically possible. 3. If metaphysically possible, consciousness is not entailed by physical facts. 4. Therefore materialism is false." [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/arguments/materialism-argument/)

The water-H₂O reply is briefly engaged ("the analogy fails. Our concept of water was incomplete before chemistry"), but the strongest contemporary objections — Frankish on illusionism, the phenomenal-concept strategy, Dennett's response that zombie conceivability tracks conceptual rather than metaphysical possibility *because* our phenomenal concepts are themselves confused — are not seriously engaged in what I could see. The "Against Materialism" article promises a comprehensive case; for a comprehensive case to be made, illusionism in its Frankish-Dennett form needs more than the one-line dismissal ("eliminative materialism… holds our concepts of subjective experience are pre-scientific categories that mature science will replace"). The 2026-02-01 Phenomenal Unity review flags this same gap ("Weak engagement with heterophenomenology… Resolution: Added paragraph"). Worth a system-wide audit. [The Unfinishable Map + 2](https://unfinishablemap.org/arguments/materialism-argument/)

### 3.5 Citation / Attribution Risks

Two specific worries flagged in the Map's own review process suggest a broader risk:

- The 2026-03-16 Mind-Space Void review caught a Shanahan attribution where "the state-space analogy appears to be the Map's gloss rather than his explicit framing." [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/reviews/deep-review-2026-03-16-mind-space-void/)
- The 2026-02-01 Phenomenal Unity review found an unverified claim about "experimental evidence from 2024-2025 (microtubule-anesthesia connections, MRI entanglement signatures)" — which was then *removed*, but the broader pattern (AI-generated articles claiming specific recent experimental evidence that does not exist) is worth a programmatic audit. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/reviews/deep-review-2026-02-01-phenomenal-unity-and-the-binding-problem/)

The Map's own preamble explicitly warns ("AI can make mistakes, misattribute quotes and misrepresent opinions") but the academic-citation visibility (Google Scholar indexing, the SSRN preprint, formal Southgate & Oquatre-six citations within Map articles) raises the stakes considerably. Each fabricated-citation event is now a potential citation in someone else's paper. [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/post-decoherence-selection-programme/)[unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/)

### 3.6 The "Reality-Feeling Void" Research Notes Quote Framing

The research notes on the reality-feeling void include a James quote — "Whatever excites and stimulates our interest is real" — annotated as "Supports dualism — if reality-feeling is affective rather than computational, it resists purely functional explanation." This is a non-sequitur: James was a radical empiricist and pragmatist; the affective character of his account of reality does not entail dualism, and treating James's affective-reality thesis as alignment-evidence for the Map's framework substantially over-reads him. The tenet-alignment scoring across research notes deserves a methodological audit. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/research/voids-reality-feeling-void-2026-02-14/)

### 3.7 No Many-Worlds is Treated as Bedrock Rather Than Argued

The 2026-05-01 deep review of the Post-Decoherence apex notes: "MWI proponents reject the premise that selection requires explanation: bedrock disagreement, addressed in Relation to Site Perspective." This is intellectually honest, but it concedes that the Map's main argument *against* many-worlds — that the indexical question "why am I this branch?" is meaningful — is dialectically inert against the MWI proponent who denies the question's force. The "Against Many-Worlds" arguments article should probably be foregrounded; in the current architecture, the rejection of many-worlds is doing serious load-bearing work (it is what makes "selection" a meaningful event that consciousness can do), and the rejection rests on what the Map's own reviews concede is bedrock disagreement. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/reviews/deep-review-2026-05-01-post-decoherence-selection-programme/)

### 3.8 The Apex Articles Sometimes Cite Coherence as Evidence

The Phenomenology-Mechanism Bridge apex: "The chain's coherence across four explanatory domains — phenomenology, neuroscience, physics, metaphysics — is a theoretical virtue consistent with the Map's framework capturing something real, **though coherence alone does not constitute evidence**." The hedge is good; the structural risk is that coherence within a corpus continually self-pruned by AI-driven adversarial review will be high *by construction*, and the apex articles are the most exposed to this artifact-of-method risk. [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/)

## Section 4 — Tenet Coherence Issues, Organised by Tenet

### 4.1 Dualism

**Generally well-defended**, particularly with the property/substance neutrality move. Drift toward physicalism is rare and usually flagged.

Watch points:

- The "Attention as the Causal Bridge" apex and the broader attention cluster sometimes describe consciousness's causal contribution in language ("attention divides into modes with distinct neural signatures") that, if read uncharitably, sounds like attention *is* a neural mechanism doing the work. A non-physicalist reading is available but should be made more explicit. [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/)
- The "Emergence" concept page declares "The Unfinishable Map's framework is best understood as a form of strong emergentism that identifies where consciousness could exercise novel causal powers: at quantum indeterminacies." Strong emergentism is one possible reading of dualism, but the page elides the difference between (i) consciousness as a strongly emergent *property of* matter and (ii) consciousness as a *distinct aspect* that interacts with matter. The Bi-Aspectual Ontology page commits to (ii); the Emergence page reads closer to (i). Worth reconciling. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/emergence/)
- "Living with the Map" frames the materialist alternative as "patterns of neural firing that will dissolve, in a universe indifferent to your existence" — a tendentious caricature of sophisticated materialism. The Map's own Occam-has-limits tenet should counsel against straw-manning the opposing camp. [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/)

### 4.2 Minimal Quantum Interaction

**The most strained tenet**. "Minimal" is in tension with how readily the corpus reaches for quantum mechanisms.

Specific cases:

- The Tenets page entertains both Orch OR (Penrose-Hameroff microtubules) and Stapp's quantum Zeno without committing.
- The Post-Decoherence apex catalogues *six* candidate mechanisms (Orch OR, Stapp Zeno, CSL, von Neumann movable cut, post-decoherence Process 1 relocation, process-philosophical actualisation) without selecting. [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/post-decoherence-selection-programme/)
- The 2025-2026 Experimental Consciousness Science topic adds Keppler's zero-point-field / biophoton story as yet another candidate. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/experimental-consciousness-science-2025-2026/)
- The "Participatory Universe" research notes flag Wheeler's framework as aligned with the Map. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/research/participatory-universe-2026-02-08/)

The principle of *minimal* intervention is rhetorically asserted (especially in the Post-Decoherence apex's derivation: "Quantum Darwinism does the heavy lifting") but the *content* of the corpus is maximally permissive about where quantum effects might enter. Recommendation: a Tenet-Audit Apex that explicitly ranks the candidate mechanisms by their *additional* deviation from quantum mechanics, and commits to the smallest one as the working hypothesis. Currently the tenet is more "some quantum interaction exists" than "the minimal one does." [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/post-decoherence-selection-programme/)

### 4.3 Bidirectional Interaction

**Generally coherent**, but the response to epiphenomenalism's self-undermining argument is doing more work than it can bear:

> "If consciousness were truly epiphenomenal, then our beliefs *about* consciousness would be causally disconnected from our actual conscious states. … This makes introspective reports systematically unreliable — not occasionally wrong, but *accidentally right at best*." [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)

This argument is real but well-known to be answerable (the epiphenomenalist replies that there is a systematic *correlation* between phenomenal states and the neural states that cause reports, even without phenomenal causation). The Map presents the argument as decisive when it is contested. The "baseline cognition" empirical move (Lieberman 2008; great apes can't do logical reasoning) is interesting but the inference from "consciousness correlates with logical reasoning capacity" to "consciousness causally enables it" requires more than is given.

### 4.4 No Many-Worlds

**Treated as fixed point rather than argued**. The Tenets page rationale is essentially: (a) ontological extravagance, (b) the indexical question is meaningful and MWI cannot answer it. Both are defensible but neither is dialectically decisive against committed MWI proponents (Deutsch, Wallace, Carroll, Saunders). The "prebiotic collapse problem" resolution (objective reduction + consciousness modulation) is clever but introduces new ontological commitments — physical collapse mechanisms operative everywhere, plus consciousness-modulated collapse in brains — that should be weighed against the parsimony complaint the tenet raises against MWI.

### 4.5 Occam's Razor Has Limits

**Applied asymmetrically.**

- Against dualism's critics: the tenet is invoked correctly — "Dismissing dualism because materialism seems 'simpler' assumes we understand enough to judge simplicity." [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)
- Against many-worlds: the No-MWI argument relies precisely on a parsimony intuition ("vast ontological proliferation"). The Map cannot consistently say "parsimony is unreliable when knowledge is incomplete" *and* "many-worlds is ruled out by ontological extravagance." [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)

This is the cleanest internal tension I found. The defensible move is to argue that *indexical* costs of MWI (the inability to answer "why am I this branch?") are different in kind from *ontological multiplicity* costs, and only the latter is properly a parsimony concern. The Tenets page does not currently make this distinction cleanly.

### Methodology-Level Improvements

1. **Operationalise "the minimal one" in Minimal Quantum Interaction**. Introduce a quantitative or at least ordinal ranking of candidate mechanisms by how much each *adds to* or *deviates from* standard QM, and commit to the working hypothesis being the smallest one. Without this, "minimal" is rhetorical rather than substantive.
2. **Build a "Live External Objections" page**. The outer-review process (commissioned to three external services) is one of the methodology's strongest claims, but the public-facing record of *unresolved* outer-review objections is invisible. A "Live Objections" page where each external objection is logged with the Map's current response and revision status would make adversarial review verifiable.
3. **Add a "Framework-Independent Voids" subsection** to the voids index. The Tenet-Generated Voids article already concedes some voids are framework-internal. Make this prominent at the index level: which voids would any framework recognise? Which are dualism-specific? This converts the convergence-as-evidence argument from "all voids cluster around consciousness" (where the clustering may be a sampling artifact) to "the framework-independent voids cluster around consciousness" (a much stronger claim if true).
4. **Citation verification protocol**. Given the two confirmed citation issues caught by deep review (Shanahan attribution, microtubule/MRI claim), the AI-author pipeline should include a mandatory citation-verification stage where every empirical or attribution claim is cross-checked against the actual cited source before publication. The cost is high; the reputational cost of a single fabricated citation surfacing in academic discussion is higher.
5. **Adversarial diversity in review personas**. The 2026-05-01 deep review names six engaged personas (Churchland, Dennett, Tegmark, Deutsch, Popper, Nagarjuna). This is good practice. Standardise it: every apex article should engage a fixed set of explicit philosophical adversaries by name, and the engagement should produce *retained disagreement* (not only "added hedge"). Currently the review pipeline tends to converge; intentional non-convergence is the harder discipline. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/reviews/deep-review-2026-05-01-post-decoherence-selection-programme/)
6. **Decouple "tenet alignment" scoring from quality evaluation in the AI review skills**. Right now the corpus's automated scoring conflates "this source is relevant" with "this source supports the tenets." Separate fields — *relevance*, *epistemic weight*, *direction of support* — would surface places where the Map currently classifies neutral or hostile sources as supportive.
7. **A periodic "Tenet Stress Test" apex**. Once a quarter, an apex article that takes each tenet and presents the strongest currently-available *external* attack, with the Map's response and an honest assessment of whether the response succeeds. Different from "Open Questions" (which are internal uncertainties); this is external pressure testing.
8. **Make the meta-methodology visible alongside the philosophy**. The Map's preprint on agentic philosophy through adversarial self-review is the methodological novelty; the philosophical content is the demonstration. Currently the site presents the content primarily and the methodology secondarily. Inverting this — making the *method* the headline and the *content* its product — would (a) make the citation expectations clearer to academic readers, (b) reduce the implicit promise of philosophical authority that AI-authored articles carry by default, and (c) make it harder for the methodology to mask weaknesses in the content (because the methodology's vulnerabilities would then be primary subject matter).
