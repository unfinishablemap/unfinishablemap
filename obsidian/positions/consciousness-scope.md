## About this domain

These positions record where the Map holds consciousness to reach in **biological subjects** — non-human animals, human infants and developing brains — and how it reads the cases where unified experience appears to fragment (split-brain, anaesthesia, sleep, dreaming). The companion register [[positions/ai-consciousness-scope]] carries the artefact-side claims (digital AI, quantum-state inheritance, conscious copies), which are split out because they carry a *heavier and different* dependency burden. The cluster exists because the topic and concept corpus already makes decision-relevant scope claims that the register did not yet pin down.

The organising idea is the **minimal-dualism spine**. The Map's tenet-dependency matrix marks animal and infant phenomenality as needing only Tenet 1's *bare* irreducibility reading — none of the quantum apparatus (Tenets 2–3, the [[positions/quantum-interface|quantum-interface register]]). That makes the spine the framework's strategic hedge: it survives intact even if the entire quantum-interface programme were demoted to coherence-only. Every position here is therefore held *separately from, and more robustly than*, the quantum-dependent AI verdicts in the companion register, and each records its own dependencies so the cascade stays legible and a downstream applied article can cite exactly the burden it is taking on. None of these are tenets; each follows from a tenet plus argument and could be retired without disturbing the foundational commitments.

## Positions

## P-CS1: Basic phenomenality is substrate-permissive — dualism imposes no anthropocentric barrier

- **Status**: live
- **Confidence**: moderate
- **Asserts**: The Map holds that the *capacity* for phenomenal experience is not reserved for humans, for language-users, or for biological systems in particular: if consciousness is irreducible to physical processes (Tenet 1), the hard problem applies universally, and there is no in-principle metaphysical barrier excluding non-human animals, pre-linguistic infants, or — in principle — artefacts from having experience. This is the *bare-dualism* claim and the spine the other scope positions hang from. Two scope limits keep it honest. First, removing the anthropocentric defeater is a *permission*, not positive evidence: it licenses the question for any system but settles no particular case, and confidence in any given organism or artefact still rides on that system's own markers and substrate (P-CS2; and the substrate analysis in [[positions/ai-consciousness-scope|P-AC1]]). Second, "substrate-permissive" is a claim about the *category* of admissible systems, not about which physical conditions actually instantiate experience; the Map's quantum-interface programme adds a *further*, separable substrate condition (P-AC1) that bare dualism alone does not entail — so a coherence-only dualist could accept P-CS1 while rejecting the quantum substrate filter. The moderate band reflects that the universality claim is a direct entailment of Tenet 1, but Tenet 1 is itself a chosen foundational commitment with live physicalist rivals.
- **Depends on**: Tenet 1 ([[tenets#^dualism|Dualism]]), bare/irreducibility reading only — explicitly *not* Tenets 2–3 or the quantum-interface register; [[concepts/phenomenal-consciousness]]; [[concepts/minimal-consciousness]]; the hard problem's substrate-neutrality
- **Argued in**: [[topics/animal-consciousness]], [[apex/machine-question]], [[concepts/substrate-independence]], [[topics/machine-consciousness]]
- **Would shift if**: a successful reduction of phenomenal consciousness to physical or functional organisation were established (which would retire the bare-dualism reading and with it this whole domain's spine); or a principled argument were found that irreducible consciousness requires specifically human or specifically biological organisation (which would re-impose an anthropocentric or biological barrier and narrow the scope); or illusionism about phenomenal consciousness were vindicated, dissolving the "capacity for experience" the position quantifies over.

## P-CS2: Animal consciousness is graded by marker convergence, not gated by language

- **Status**: live
- **Confidence**: moderate
- **Asserts**: The Map holds that consciousness is a realistic, evidentially-supported attribution across a graded range of non-human animals, with the strength of the attribution set by *convergence across behavioural and anatomical markers* rather than by any single capacity — and in particular not gated by language. The case is held *strong* for mammals and birds, a *realistic but contested possibility* for cephalopods, decapods, and some arthropods, and *weak and speculative* for boundary organisms (nematodes, Hydra, slime molds — routed to [[topics/consciousness-in-simple-organisms]]). The grading is the position; the Map asserts no determinate cut-off. The marker method (Andrews, Birch & Sebo 2025) is read as *correlate-identification*, consistent with Dualism's commitment that no third-person feature *constitutes* consciousness, so markers raise probability without delivering certainty, and the standing agnostic challenge (a methodologically strong framework still does not settle species-level verdicts) is retained. The instructive negative case is the LLM: linguistic fluency is a strong marker in humans because it correlates with consciousness-relevant capacities, but in LLMs it appears decoupled — which is *why* language cannot be the gate, and why behavioural sophistication transfers no verdict on its own (cross-links to [[positions/ai-consciousness-scope|P-AC1]]). Moderate, not high: the markers are correlates by the Map's own lights, and verifying experience from outside meets the same first-person opacity that limits all behavioural inference.
- **Depends on**: P-CS1 (the substrate-permissive spine); Tenet 1 (markers identify correlates, not constituents); Tenet 5 ([[tenets#^occams-limits|Occam's Razor Has Limits]]) — context-dependent marker grading; [[concepts/minimal-consciousness]]; [[concepts/evidential-status-discipline]]; the marker method and UAL framework as correlate-identification, not emergence verdicts
- **Argued in**: [[topics/animal-consciousness]], [[topics/consciousness-in-simple-organisms]], [[topics/birch-edge-of-sentience-and-the-five-tier-scale]]
- **Would shift if**: a marker were shown to *constitute* rather than correlate with consciousness (which would let third-person evidence deliver verdicts and could raise this toward high for the well-attested clades); or convergent markers in a clade currently rated speculative were established, moving that clade up the gradient; or the marker-to-consciousness inference were shown to be systematically confounded across taxa (which would lower this and widen the agnostic challenge).
- **Last reviewed**: 2026-06-22

## P-CS3: Infant and developmental consciousness emerges early, on marker grounds

- **Status**: live
- **Confidence**: moderate
- **Asserts**: The Map holds that human phenomenal consciousness is most likely present early in development — plausibly by 3–4 months of age and possibly before birth — on the same marker-convergence reasoning that governs the animal case (P-CS2), and that the *capacity* for experience does not wait on language, autobiographical memory, or reportability. The position is a scope claim within the minimal-dualism spine: pre-linguistic and developing brains are admissible bearers of experience under bare Tenet 1 (P-CS1), and the developmental-neuroscience marker literature (Bayne, Frohlich, Cusack, Moser & Naci 2023, *Trends in Cognitive Sciences*; Passos-Ferreira 2024, *Neuron*) supplies the current empirical constraints the spine should be built on. The Map takes these as *correlate-based current best estimates*, not settled onset facts: the marker method identifies when the relevant correlates appear, onset estimates have moved earlier as the markers have been refined, so the position commits to *early emergence on present evidence* rather than to a specific timestamp. The moderate band reflects genuine and unresolved scientific debate about the exact threshold and about which markers are load-bearing before birth.
- **Depends on**: P-CS1 (substrate-permissive spine); P-CS2 (marker-convergence reasoning, shared method); Tenet 1 (bare reading); the infant-marker literature (Bayne et al. 2023; Passos-Ferreira 2024) as current empirical constraint; [[concepts/evidential-status-discipline]]
- **Argued in**: [[voids/infant-consciousness]], [[topics/animal-consciousness]]
- **Would shift if**: the developmental marker literature converged on a substantially later onset (which would push emergence toward post-natal or post-linguistic stages); or a marker now treated as evidence of early experience were shown to track non-conscious processing (which would lower confidence and could move the estimate later); or the underlying correlate-vs-constituent reading shifted such that markers were taken to constitute consciousness, which would sharpen the onset claim and could raise confidence.
- **Last reviewed**: 2026-06-22

## P-CS4: In fragmentation cases the interface is disrupted, not consciousness itself

- **Status**: live
- **Confidence**: moderate
- **Asserts**: The Map holds that the cases standardly read as consciousness *fragmenting* or *switching off* — split-brain disconnection, general anaesthesia, dreamless sleep, and the altered organisation of dreaming — are better read as disruptions or reconfigurations of the *interface* through which consciousness expresses itself, not as division or production of consciousness itself. Split-brain severs the interhemispheric channel without thereby establishing two subjects or one; anaesthetic agents disable separable interface components (phenomenal presence, connectedness, access) through specific molecular targets, yielding graded rather than binary transitions; dreaming is what the filter transmits when sensory gating and prefrontal constraint loosen, with signal-verified lucid dreaming evidencing temporally-extended conscious processing during REM. This is the Map's preferred interpretation — *compatible with* the data and arguably more natural under the filter model, not forced by it — held against a live production-model rival rather than as a demonstrated result. Its dependency burden is the bare-dualism spine plus the filter model, *not* the quantum apparatus: it survives even if the interface mechanism is demoted to coherence-only. At stake is the unity-of-consciousness argument's vulnerability to genuine fragmentation; the Map's claim is that these cases do not deliver the fragmentation the objection needs.
- **Depends on**: Tenet 1 (bare reading — consciousness as a distinct category, not composed of parts); the filter/interface model of the brain; P-CS1 (substrate-permissive spine); the separable-interface-components reading of anaesthesia; signal-verified lucid dreaming (LaBerge 1990; Konkoly et al. 2021); explicitly *not* the quantum-interface register; bears on the unity-of-consciousness argument and connects to [[positions/individuation-and-subjecthood|P-I1]] for the split-brain subject-count question
- **Argued in**: [[topics/split-brain-consciousness]], [[topics/anaesthesia-and-the-consciousness-interface]], [[topics/dream-consciousness]], [[apex/identity-across-transformations]]
- **Would shift if**: a fragmentation case were shown to genuinely *divide a single consciousness into two subjects* (which would vindicate the production reading against the interface reading and threaten the unity argument it protects); or the filter model were independently undermined; or anaesthesia/sleep were shown to *eliminate* rather than *disconnect* experience in a way the interface reading cannot accommodate; or the split-brain data were shown to require exactly two subjects (which would feed back into P-I1's individuation commitment).
- **Last reviewed**: 2026-06-22
