## About this domain

These positions cover the **value-blind / value-sensitive fork**: given that consciousness selects among physically underdetermined neural outcomes (Tenet 3, Bidirectional Interaction) and that value resides in phenomenal experience ([[consciousness-value-connection]]), does felt valence *do* the selecting, or is the selection mechanism value-blind with valence along for the ride? The fork is forced by the Map's own commitments and is, on present evidence, underdetermined: a mechanism-sufficiency rival explains the relevant findings without an experiential term, and the Map concedes that rival is the one to beat rather than one already beaten. This domain records what the Map *holds* on this fork (P-VS1) and collates, in one auditable place, the battery of evidence that would move the fork in either direction (P-VS2) — empirical hooks otherwise distributed across [[valence-and-conscious-selection]], [[wanting-liking-and-the-value-in-mechanism-fork]], and [[marginal-organism-scope-of-value-sensitive-selection]]. The cluster sits downstream of Tenets 3 and 5 (Occam's Razor Has Limits) and connects to Tenet 1 (Dualism); none of these are tenets.

**Mechanism debt inherited, not discharged.** Any claim here that felt value *does selective work* inherits the open mechanism debt logged in the quantum-interface register: no worked toy model of how a mental variable couples to a neural quantum state, and the bias-without-deviation dilemma sitting close to epiphenomenalism until a positive account lands. The value-sensitive horn additionally needs a *second* coupling (valence-to-physics) beyond the attentional one, which has no worked physical implementation — so it carries more debt than the attention-only default, not less. These positions must not read more confident about the metaphysics of causal work than the upstream register does. Full statement of the crux: [[positions/quantum-interface#^mechanism-debt|the quantum-interface register's mechanism-debt anchor]].

## Positions

## P-VS1: The Map holds value-sensitive selection on prior commitments, conceding mechanism-sufficiency is the rival to beat

- **Status**: live
- **Calibration** ([[methodology-and-calibration#^calibration-schema|multi-axis]]): credence low · external-evidence grade D (held on prior commitments — consciousness-value-connection — with no worked valence-to-physics coupling and the mechanism-sufficiency rival undefeated) · structural centrality low · model maturity programme · empirical discriminability indirect · framework-internal only: yes
- **Asserts**: The Map leans toward *value-sensitive* selection — that felt valence is the currency in which conscious selection among underdetermined outcomes is denominated, so that outcomes in conscious brains are selected partly *because of how they would feel* — but holds this as an aspiration grounded in prior commitments ([[consciousness-value-connection]], [[topics/phenomenal-value-realism]]), not as a settled result. The honest standing is conservative. The value-blind horn is the *more minimal* dualism (one mental-to-physical coupling, attention, with a worked implementation in Stapp's observation-rate model; value-sensitivity needs a second, unspecified valence-to-physics coupling), so by Tenet 2 (Minimal Quantum Interaction) it is the horn to beat on the Map's own standards. The external mechanism-sufficiency thesis — reward-prediction-error learning, unconscious "liking," affordance competition, valence-as-free-energy, illusionism — is a serious, multi-disciplinary research programme the Map has *not* defeated; it is the rival to beat, not a position already beaten. The Map declines it on the prior, unsettled hard-problem-and-illusionism question rather than on any datum the rival owns. "Leaning value-sensitive without a mechanism is an aspiration, not a position": the confidence is *low* accordingly. The most defensible current stance is the graduated middle path (valence modulates attention, attention drives selection), and even that leaves the fundamental selection law value-blind unless a direct valence coupling is specified.
- **Depends on**: Tenets 1, 3, 5; [[consciousness-value-connection]]; [[topics/phenomenal-value-realism]]; the quantum-interface mechanism debt ([[positions/quantum-interface#^mechanism-debt]]); P-VS2 (the test battery that would move it)
- **Argued in**: [[topics/valence-and-conscious-selection]], [[topics/wanting-liking-and-the-value-in-mechanism-fork]], [[topics/the-steelman-for-value-blind-selection]], [[apex/moral-architecture-of-consciousness]]
- **Would shift if**: the discriminating battery in P-VS2 returned value-sensitive signatures (a valence selection-advantage; liking-tracks-selection under wanting/liking conflict; degraded selection efficacy in pain asymbolia) — which would *raise* this toward moderate; or the battery returned uniformly value-blind results, or illusionism about felt value were vindicated, which would retire the horn in favour of the mechanism-sufficiency reading; or a worked valence-to-physics coupling were specified and survived review, removing the "aspiration not position" caveat.

## P-VS2: The value-blind / value-sensitive fork is empirically falsifiable, and its discriminating battery points in specified directions

- **Status**: live
- **Calibration** ([[methodology-and-calibration#^calibration-schema|multi-axis]]): credence moderate · external-evidence grade C (real experimental paradigms populate the discriminating battery, but none has yet been run) · structural centrality moderate · model maturity developed · empirical discriminability direct · framework-internal only: no
- **Asserts**: The Map holds that the fork, though currently underdetermined, is *not* purely metaphysical: it carries a collated battery of discriminating tests, each with a stated direction, so the interface claim stays falsifiable rather than retreating into an unfalsifiable add-on. The battery, drawn together from the cluster's three source articles, has three tiers. **(1) Direct selection tests** (from [[topics/valence-and-conscious-selection]]): a valence *selection-advantage* assay — valenced options selected more readily than equally-salient neutral options supports value-sensitive selection, parity supports value-blind; uniform ~10-bit bandwidth across valenced and neutral content, and neural selection signatures correlating with attentional effort rather than with the valence of the selected outcome, support value-blind. **(2) Dissociation tests** (from [[topics/wanting-liking-and-the-value-in-mechanism-fork]]): when wanting and liking conflict, liking (felt quality) tracking which outcome is selected supports value-sensitive, while choices fully predicted by the incentive-salience signal with liking adding nothing disconfirms it; disrupting the conscious hedonic signal while leaving wanting circuitry intact should *change selection* (not merely reported feeling) if value does selective work; pain-asymbolia patients should show degraded selection *efficacy*, not merely reduced motivation; and a complete mechanistic model of choice needing no experiential term would leave the value-in-experience reading explanatorily idle. **(3) Scope credence-shifters** (from [[topics/marginal-organism-scope-of-value-sensitive-selection]]): an anticipatory-evaluation finding in a minimal organism raises value-sensitivity at that rung; further learning/avoidance in non-neural systems (the *Physarum* disjunction) strengthens the value-blind reading of valence-like behaviour everywhere. The moderate (not high) confidence is scoped: the *battery* is real and auditable, but two limits are in-principle — verifying the *absence* of valence-denominated selection requires settling the explanatory gap from outside, and value-sensitive and value-blind selection can be behaviourally indistinguishable at the margin — so the fork is sharpenable and movable, not guaranteed decidable.
- **Depends on**: P-VS1; the four discriminating tests in [[topics/valence-and-conscious-selection]]; the three falsification conditions in [[topics/wanting-liking-and-the-value-in-mechanism-fork]]; the credence-shifter assays in [[topics/marginal-organism-scope-of-value-sensitive-selection]]; [[evidential-status-discipline]]
- **Argued in**: [[topics/valence-and-conscious-selection]], [[topics/wanting-liking-and-the-value-in-mechanism-fork]], [[topics/marginal-organism-scope-of-value-sensitive-selection]], [[apex/moral-architecture-of-consciousness]]
- **Would shift if**: any test in the battery were run and returned a clear result (which would update P-VS1 in the corresponding direction); or one of the named tests were shown to be confounded such that it does not discriminate the horns after all (which would shrink the battery and lower this confidence); or the in-principle limits were shown to be instrumental rather than structural, which would *raise* this toward high by making the fork guaranteed-decidable.
