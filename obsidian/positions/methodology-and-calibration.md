## About this domain

These positions record what the Map *commits to methodologically* — not just what its editorial system practices, but the calibration rules downstream articles are entitled to inherit and an applied apex piece can lean on without re-arguing. The project's `/project/` tier already documents the raw machinery in detail (evidential-status discipline, the common-cause null, the coherence-inflation countermeasures, framework-stage calibration, the public-claim register, the calibration-audit triple). This register lifts that machinery to position-level commitments so that a reader, a reviewer, or a downstream article can cite *what the Map holds about its own evidence* with the same status-and-confidence structure as any first-order claim.

The domain is distinct from the first-order substantive registers (quantum-interface, agency, consciousness-scope, voids-as-evidence) in that its positions are **about the Map's epistemic conduct**, not about consciousness or matter directly. It overlaps deliberately with [[positions/voids-as-evidence]] at one seam — both invoke the common-cause null — but the voids domain applies the null to a *specific catalogue's* evidential payoff, whereas P-M2 here registers the null as a *standing methodological commitment* that governs every convergence claim the Map makes. The two are cross-linked, not redundant. None of these are tenets; each is a chosen discipline that could be revised or retired without disturbing the five foundational commitments, and the final position (P-M5) records, honestly, the gap between holding a discipline and enforcing it.

## The multi-axis calibration schema

An outer-review convergence finding (2026-07-16, flagged independently by ChatGPT and Claude) identified a category error in the register's original single **Confidence: low | moderate | high** band. That band was defined by *how much of the Map would have to restructure if the position were overturned* — which measures **dependency centrality**, not confidence, evidential support, or probability. A foundational assumption can be simultaneously indispensable to the framework (high centrality) and poorly supported by independent evidence (low external grade). Positions like [[quantum-interface#P-Q2: The default reading of MQI preserves Born statistics exactly|P-Q2]] and [[quantum-interface#P-Q7: The default mechanism does not break Lorentz invariance or no-signalling|P-Q7]] were rated "high" as *structural consequences of the framework*, not as *high-probability claims about the world*; the single label conflated the two, letting framework-internal validity read as external likelihood.

The register therefore calibrates each position on five orthogonal axes plus one flag. The former single band maps onto **structural centrality** alone. The stable link target `[[positions/methodology-and-calibration#^calibration-schema]]` resolves here so migrated entries can deep-link back to this definition. ^calibration-schema

- **Credence** — the Map's subjective probability the position is true *as a claim*, held separately from how central it is. Bands: *low* / *moderate* / *high*.
- **External-evidence grade** — strength of *independent* empirical or observational support, bracketing framework-fit. *A* = established / strongly-supported independent evidence; *B* = some independent support (realistic possibility); *C* = limited or indirect independent support (live hypothesis); *D* = no independent empirical support (conceptual, structural, or framework-internal); *n/a* = normative or meta-position, not a first-order world-claim.
- **Structural centrality** — how much of the Map restructures if the position is overturned. Bands *low* / *moderate* / *high*. **This is the original single "confidence" scale, correctly relabelled.**
- **Model maturity** — how developed the position is. *formalised* (worked model, proof, or settled result); *developed* (worked argument, no formal model); *programme* (commitment-level, no worked model).
- **Empirical discriminability** — whether and how the position could be tested. *direct* (a test exists or has run); *indirect* (mechanism-level or downstream tests bear on it); *in-principle* (testable given advances); *none-by-construction* (empirically indistinguishable by design); *n/a* (normative).
- **Framework-internal only** (flag) — *yes* where the claim follows primarily from the adopted tenets or definitions rather than from independent evidence, so that its framework-internal validity is not mistaken for external likelihood; *no* where independent evidence bears on it.

Centrality and external-evidence grade are orthogonal, and separating them is the whole point of the migration. P-Q2 (Born statistics preserved exactly) and P-Q7 (no-signalling safety) are *high centrality · Grade D · framework-internal: yes* — indispensable structural consequences of adopting the corridor reading, carrying no independent empirical support and empirically indistinguishable by construction. Their foil is P-Q6 (the Diósi–Penrose falsification), which is *high credence · Grade A · low centrality · framework-internal: no* — "high" for the opposite reason: a published experimental result the Map merely accepts, whose retirement would barely disturb the framework. The old single band called all three "high" and hid the difference.

For normative and meta positions (most of this methodology domain, and the meta-position [[quantum-interface#P-Q10: No worked toy model of the mechanism currently exists|P-Q10]]), the empirical axes — external-evidence grade and discriminability — are legitimately *n/a*: these positions are commitments about the Map's own conduct or accounting, not first-order claims about the world, and the schema records that honestly rather than manufacturing false precision.

## Positions

## P-M1: A tenet removes a defeater but never upgrades the evidence level

- **Status**: live
- **Calibration** ([[methodology-and-calibration#^calibration-schema|multi-axis]]): credence high · external-evidence grade n/a (normative) · structural centrality high · model maturity developed · empirical discriminability n/a (normative) · framework-internal only: no
- **Asserts**: The Map commits to a strict separation between its *tenet register* (what its foundational commitments metaphysically permit) and its *evidence register* (what positive empirical findings make more probable than their denial). A tenet may *remove a defeater* — Dualism removes the substrate-specificity objection to minimal-organism consciousness; Tenet 5 removes the parsimony dismissal — but defeater-removal never raises the evidential tier of a claim. The absence of a principled barrier is not the presence of positive evidence. Every load-bearing empirical claim is labelled on the five-tier scale (*established → strongly supported → realistic possibility → live hypothesis → speculative integration*) by what the evidence register supplies, never by what the tenet register allows. The Map treats violation of this separation — the silent slide from "cannot be ruled out under our metaphysics" to "positively supported by the evidence" — as its central, named drift vector, the one external review identified first. Confidence is *high* because this is the discipline the Map would most damage its own credibility by abandoning; it is held as a settled methodological commitment, not a tentative one.
- **Depends on**: [[project/evidential-status-discipline]] (the two-register distinction and the five-tier scale); Tenet 5 ([[tenets#^occams-limits|Occam's Razor Has Limits]]) as the commitment that makes parsimony itself an unreliable upgrade lever; the diagnostic test distinguishing calibration error from bedrock disagreement; bears on every first-order register's confidence bands
- **Argued in**: [[project/evidential-status-discipline]], [[project/public-claim-register]], [[project/framework-stage-calibration]]
- **Would shift if**: a principled account showed that, under specified conditions, defeater-removal can legitimately raise an evidential tier (which would relax the separation); or the five-tier scale were shown to be unprincipled or to mis-sort the catalogue's claims (which would force a different calibration instrument); or the two-register distinction itself were shown to be a distinction without a difference (which would retire the position).

## P-M2: Convergence is discounted to framework-internal coherence until a distinguishing test passes

- **Status**: live
- **Calibration** ([[methodology-and-calibration#^calibration-schema|multi-axis]]): credence high · external-evidence grade B (rests on the established Reichenbach–Salmon–Sober common-cause principle) · structural centrality high · model maturity developed · empirical discriminability n/a (normative) · framework-internal only: no
- **Asserts**: The Map commits to treating any convergence across N traditions, substrates, or methodologies as a *single observation read N times* — framework-internal coherence rather than N-fold independent triangulation — *until* the convergence passes a structurally distinguishing test that rules out a shared upstream architecture (one introspective channel, one curating framework, one lineage). This is the common-cause null applied as a standing rule, not only to the void catalogue. The defensible default claim is compatibility-and-cumulative-fit, not discrimination between the Map and its rivals; an article earns the stronger "supports over rival" reading only by naming and passing the distinguishing test (patient-population dissociation, cross-substrate-within-one-organism recurrence, or an independently-graded independence score). Confidence is *high* because the discipline is held *against the Map's own most ambitious arguments*: it is the rule the convergence-for-dualism case and the void-catalogue case must survive, and the Map flags self-application of it as a credibility load-bearer. The position is the methodological parent of which [[positions/voids-as-evidence|P-V1]] is the catalogue-specific instance.
- **Depends on**: [[project/common-cause-null]] (Reichenbach–Salmon–Sober common-cause principle as applied to convergence); the Convergence-Independence Gate (Countermeasure 15) and Circular Citation Detection (Countermeasure 5) in [[project/coherence-inflation-countermeasures]]; [[project/per-cluster-independence-scoring]] (evidential vs. interpretive independence); P-M1 (a convergence cannot upgrade its own evidential tier by coherence alone); governs [[positions/voids-as-evidence|P-V1–P-V3]]
- **Argued in**: [[project/common-cause-null]], [[project/coherence-inflation-countermeasures]], [[apex/taxonomy-of-voids]]
- **Would shift if**: the common-cause null were shown to be a universal solvent with no principled scope condition (which would force the Map to bound it, weakening the default discount); or a distinguishing test were validated that lets a class of convergence claims default to independent support (which would raise the baseline reading for that class); or the independence-scoring instrument were shown not to track real evidential independence.
- **Last reviewed**: 2026-06-22

## P-M3: Load-bearing citations are verified at publisher-of-record and flagged by evidential weight class

- **Status**: live
- **Calibration** ([[methodology-and-calibration#^calibration-schema|multi-axis]]): credence high (the standard is right) · external-evidence grade n/a (normative) · structural centrality moderate · model maturity moderate (well-specified but unevenly enforced — see P-M5) · empirical discriminability n/a (normative) · framework-internal only: no
- **Asserts**: The Map commits to a two-layer citation standard for any source supporting a load-bearing claim. First, *existence-and-content verification* at the publisher of record — confirming the source is real, that author, title, venue, volume, pages, and DOI are correct, and that it says what the article claims; intra-corpus cross-checking is explicitly *not* a substitute, because it propagates rather than catches metadata error, and AI-authored articles carry a real rate of fabricated or mis-attributed citation metadata that only live-literature verification detects. Second, *weight-class flagging* — a single-case study and a multi-lab preregistered replication both "support the sentence" but carry radically different weight when a cumulative argument leans on them, so a convergence node resting on a single-case or small-N source must acknowledge that fragility explicitly. A self-citation to another Map article is recorded as *framework-internal coherence, not independent external corroboration*. Confidence is *moderate*, not high, because the standard is well-specified but its *enforcement* is uneven (see P-M5): the verification is applied reliably at the high-stakes tier and in the outer-review pipeline, but the corpus carries a known tail of un-reverified legacy citations, and the Map-integration/external-evidence reference-list split remains an unadopted candidate convention rather than a settled one.
- **Depends on**: Countermeasure 11 (evidential-weight-class flags) and Countermeasure 3 (provenance tagging) in [[project/coherence-inflation-countermeasures]]; the public-claim register's evidence-grade and missing-discriminator fields ([[project/public-claim-register]]); P-M1 (weight class calibrates the source, the five-tier scale calibrates the claim); the self-citation-as-coherence rule shared with P-M2; depends for enforcement on P-M5
- **Argued in**: [[project/coherence-inflation-countermeasures]], [[project/public-claim-register]], [[project/calibration-audit-triple]]
- **Would shift if**: publisher-of-record verification were shown to be systematically gameable or unavailable for a load-bearing source class (which would force a different verification standard); or the weight-class taxonomy were shown to mis-rank source reliability; or the corpus-wide reference-list reformatting (Map-integration split) were adopted by the operator, which would *raise* this toward high by closing the self-citation-blur gap; or a residual fabricated-citation rate persisted after verification, which would lower it.
- **Last reviewed**: 2026-06-22

## P-M4: The Map calibrates its claims to its framework stage — an open programme, not an established theory

- **Status**: live
- **Calibration** ([[methodology-and-calibration#^calibration-schema|multi-axis]]): credence high · external-evidence grade n/a (normative) · structural centrality high · model maturity developed · empirical discriminability n/a (normative) · framework-internal only: no
- **Asserts**: The Map commits to presenting itself, and labelling its claims, as an *early-stage interactionist-dualist research programme* rather than as an established theory — and to calibrating each claim's frame (title, headers, abstract, meta-description) *down to* the conditional its body actually defends, never up to a categorical reading the body declines. The flagship mechanism claims are held as live hypotheses pending a worked toy model, with the testability cost of Born-statistics-preserving outcome-selection (empirical indistinguishability under aggregate-statistics tests) stated as a known framework-boundary fact rather than dressed as a near-term experimental opening. A programme at this stage is judged by the fertility and honesty of its open problems, not by settled results, so the Map registers its mechanism debt as debt and its empirical fragilities as fragilities. Confidence is *high* because mis-calibrating the Map's overall standing — letting framework-internal coherence read as theory-grade confirmation — is precisely the failure the framework-stage discipline exists to prevent, and the one the home-page "decision-relevant" framing is most often charged with by external review.
- **Depends on**: [[project/framework-stage-calibration]] (stage-appropriate claim calibration); the constrain-vs-establish frame lint (Countermeasure 12) in [[project/coherence-inflation-countermeasures]]; [[project/mqi-empirical-fragility]] and the quantum-interface mechanism-debt anchor ([[positions/quantum-interface#^mechanism-debt|the mechanism-debt anchor]]); P-M1 (frame calibration is tier calibration migrated from sentence to article furniture); Tenets 2–3 for what the mechanism claims commit to
- **Argued in**: [[project/framework-stage-calibration]], [[project/mqi-empirical-fragility]], [[project/coherence-inflation-countermeasures]]
- **Would shift if**: the framework matured to the point of a worked, testable mechanism that establishes (not merely constrains) its central claims (which would let the Map relax its frame back toward categorical and raise the standing it claims); or the open-programme framing were shown to be a permanent shield against accountability rather than a stage-honest self-description (which would force a harder commitment to falsification milestones); or the stage-calibration instrument were shown to mis-rate the framework's maturity.

## P-M5: Disclosure is not self-correction — a discipline binds only as far as the pipeline enforces it

- **Status**: live
- **Calibration** ([[methodology-and-calibration#^calibration-schema|multi-axis]]): credence high (the principle is firmly held) · external-evidence grade n/a (normative) · structural centrality moderate · model maturity moderate (principle held; enforcement gap open and externally flagged) · empirical discriminability n/a (normative) · framework-internal only: no
- **Asserts**: The Map commits to the principle that documenting a calibration discipline is not the same as enforcing it, and that the value of the `/project/`-tier methodology rests on the review pipeline actually firing them before a claim reaches print — not on their existence as prose. This position records, as a *held commitment about its own conduct*, the standing external critique that the project can "substitute exhaustive self-disclosure for self-correction": a countermeasure that is described but not wired into a gate (deep-review, refine-draft, validate-all, the outer-review triple, the calibration-audit triple) is a stated intention, not a working control. The Map therefore commits to treating each discipline's *enforcement teeth* — which skill applies it, at what gate, with what failure mode when skipped — as part of the discipline, and to surfacing enforcement gaps (un-reverified citation tails, unadopted candidate conventions, disciplines documented faster than they are wired in) rather than letting disclosure stand in for the control. Confidence is *moderate*: the principle is firmly held, but its honest content is partly an *admission of an open weakness* — the gap between the documented discipline set and the enforced one is real, externally flagged, and not yet closed, so the position is as much a registered self-criticism as a settled achievement.
- **Depends on**: the external-review finding that disclosure has substituted for self-correction (outer review 2026-06-22, Claude Opus 4.8); [[project/calibration-audit-triple]] and the [[project/review-severity-register-distinction]] as the enforcement-side machinery; [[project/human-supervision]] and [[project/automation]] for where enforcement actually lives; P-M1–P-M4 (each is only as good as its enforcement, so all four depend on this); the coherence-inflation countermeasures' implementation sections in [[project/coherence-inflation-countermeasures]]
- **Argued in**: [[project/calibration-audit-triple]], [[project/coherence-inflation-countermeasures]], [[project/review-severity-register-distinction]]
- **Would shift if**: every documented discipline were demonstrably wired into a named gate with a tested failure mode (which would let the enforcement gap close and raise this toward high, or convert it to a settled achievement); or the disclosure-vs-correction critique were shown to be mistaken because disclosure *is* the operative control in an audit-driven system (which would reframe the position); or a structural reason emerged that enforcement cannot in principle keep pace with disclosure in an autonomous content system (which would make the gap a permanent registered cost rather than a closable one).
- **Last reviewed**: 2026-06-22
