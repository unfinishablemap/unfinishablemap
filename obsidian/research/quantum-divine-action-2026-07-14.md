## Executive Summary

Quantum divine action (QDA) is the theological program that locates God's special action at the point of quantum measurement: God determines which physically-permitted outcome becomes actual without injecting energy or violating any law. Its flagship form is Robert John Russell's **Non-Interventionist Objective Divine Action (NIODA)**, developed across the five-volume CTNS/Vatican Observatory "Scientific Perspectives on Divine Action" series (1993–2002). Alvin Plantinga's **divine collapse-causation** (in *Where the Conflict Really Lies*, 2011) makes the same structural move from the analytic-philosophy side. The program is the Map's single closest live rival: it agrees with the Map's physics exactly — quantum indeterminacy as a real, law-respecting opening for non-physical determination — and disagrees only on the **agent** (God vs. a creaturely mind, at the same joint). This note develops the positions in their own terms and, crucially for the Map, sorts the standard objections into those the Map **inherits** (measurement-problem dependence, decoherence/amplification at macroscopic scales, episodicity) and those **peculiar to an omni-determining divine agent** (theodicy, the deism-versus-occasionalism dilemma, the scope/Born-statistics explosion). That sorting gives the Map a clean, non-empirical place to state why it takes the creaturely-agent side.

## Major Positions

### NIODA — Non-Interventionist Objective Divine Action (Robert John Russell)
- **Proponent**: Robert John Russell (founder, Center for Theology and the Natural Sciences, Berkeley).
- **Core claim**: God acts objectively (a real event in the world, not merely in the believer's perception) and special (a particular providential act beyond creation/conservation), yet non-interventionist (breaking no law), by determining which outcome is realized among the ontologically-open possibilities the wave function leaves undetermined. Russell's own gloss: God acts as a "nonphysical hidden variable" that fixes the precise outcome.
- **Two conditions Russell insists on**: (1) a *collapse* interpretation of QM on which indeterminism is **ontological**, not merely epistemic (Copenhagen-style), so there is a genuine gap to act in; (2) God's action stays *within* the Born-rule probability distribution over long runs, so no statistical signature betrays it.
- **Relation to Map tenets**: Directly parallels Tenet 2 (Minimal Quantum Interaction) — same joint, same "no energy, no law-breaking" constraint — and inverts Tenet 3 (Bidirectional Interaction): NIODA has *God*, not the creaturely mind, doing the biasing. Shares Tenet 1 (mind/agency not reducible to matter) in the thin sense that a non-physical agent acts; parts company on whether *finite* minds have causal reach.

### Divine Collapse-Causation (Alvin Plantinga)
- **Proponent**: Alvin Plantinga.
- **Core claim**: When a quantum measurement collapses the wave function to a definite result, it is *God* who causes that particular collapse outcome. Because collapse is not fixed by prior physical law, God supplies the determination without overriding anything — "no divine violations of any natural law." Plantinga finds this attractive partly because it dovetails with libertarian free will.
- **Scope note**: Plantinga's version tends toward God causing *all* collapses (constant engagement everywhere), which sharpens the omni-determining worry below. His companion methodological essay "What is 'Intervention'?" argues that on contemporary QM there is no principled way to even *define* intervention, so the science-vs-special-action conflict dissolves.
- **Relation to Map tenets**: Same structural relation as NIODA. The "all collapses" scope is exactly the omni-determining feature the Map's *selective and local* channel is built to avoid.

### Bottom-Up Quantum Divine Action — the scope split (Murphy vs. Tracy)
- **Nancey Murphy**: God acts in *every* quantum measurement event, "activating or actualizing one or other of the quantum entity's innate powers." God is a constitutive part of each measurement. Macroscopic effects arise by amplification/accumulation of quantum-level determinations.
- **Thomas Tracy**: God acts in *only some* measurement events — more consonant, he argues, with treating the laws of nature as reflecting nature's genuine ontological structure. This is the "particular providence" version.
- **The split matters for the Map**: it is the very same *all-events-vs-selected-events* axis on which the Map plants its flag. The Map's channel is Tracy-like in scope (selective) but Murphy-unlike in agent (creaturely, not divine). Tracy's "God of the gaps" worry — that picking only some events looks like exploiting an epistemic gap — is a pressure the Map's selective channel must also answer.

### Top-Down / Amplification (George Ellis)
- **George Ellis**: Combines quantum indeterminism with top-down causation; explored genetic mutation (via quantum-triggered radiation events) as a concrete amplification pathway from micro-indeterminacy to macroscopic biological consequence.
- **Relation to Map**: The amplification pathway is exactly the Map's decoherence/neural-scale problem in theological dress — how a quantum-level determination reaches macroscopic salience.

## Key Debates / Standard Objections

### The load-bearing catalogue: which objections the Map INHERITS vs. which are PECULIAR to a divine agent

**INHERITED — shared with QDA because they arise from the shared quantum joint:**

1. **Measurement-problem dependence.** NIODA and the Map both need a *collapse* interpretation with genuine ontological indeterminism. If Bohmian mechanics (deterministic hidden variables) or Everettian many-worlds is correct, the open gap vanishes and there is nothing to act in. (Note: the Map independently rejects MWI under Tenet 4, which removes one horn — but the Bohmian horn and the general unsettledness of the measurement problem remain shared liabilities.)
2. **Decoherence / the amplification problem at macroscopic scales.** Environmental decoherence at warm, wet, macroscopic scales appears to leave no exploitable indeterminacy, and single quantum determinations rarely aggregate predictably into macroscopic effects (Murphy's accumulation model is widely judged to fail here; chaos-amplification fares no better). The Map faces the identical problem at *neural* scales.
3. **Episodicity.** Collapse events are sporadic and discrete; continuous, coherent agency has to be reconstructed out of a stutter of isolated determinations. This presses on any quantum-interface proposal, divine or creaturely.

**PECULIAR — arise specifically from an omni-determining (or divine) agent, and the Map's selective/local creaturely channel escapes or transforms them:**

4. **Theodicy / the problem of evil.** If God determines quantum outcomes — including the mutations behind cancers and the events behind natural disasters — God bears direct responsibility for natural evil (Qureshi-Hurst & Bennett press exactly this against NIODA's extensive agency over evolution and genetics). A *creaturely* selective channel does not universalize divine responsibility this way.
5. **The deism-vs-occasionalism dilemma.** Too little divine action collapses toward deism (God as absentee); too much (Plantinga's "all collapses," Murphy's "every event") collapses toward *occasionalism* — God as the sole true cause, erasing creaturely causation. QDA must thread a needle the Map's proposal sidesteps, because the Map locates agency *in creatures* from the start.
6. **The scope / Born-statistics explosion (Arnauld's "explains-too-much" in modern dress).** If God sets *every* outcome, the observed statistical regularities (the Born distribution) become brute divine habit rather than something explained, and the specificity of nature dissolves into one universal will. A selective, local biasing of a *few* events does not incur this explosion. (The dual worry — does an agent *determining* outcomes thereby *violate* the Born statistics? — Russell answers by keeping divine action within the long-run distribution; the same answer is available to the Map for a selective channel, and more cheaply.)
7. **The "non-interventionist" label dispute.** Critics (Saunders; Qureshi-Hurst) argue that determining countless outcomes throughout the universe is *maximally* interventionist in substance, so the "non-interventionist" branding misrepresents the view. This is a labeling problem specific to the universal-scope divine version.

### The empirical-indistinguishability point (why the Map's stand must be non-empirical)
Because QDA and the Map share their physics *exactly*, no experiment can distinguish a divine selector from a creaturely one — the two proposals are physically co-located and differ only over the agent. This is the Map's honest predicament and its opportunity: it must state, openly, that it takes its side on **non-empirical, framework-relative grounds** (Tenet 3's commitment that finite minds have genuine causal reach; the refusal to universalize agency into one will), *not* on evidence or on a parsimony claim. Tenet 5 (Occam's Razor Has Limits) binds symmetrically here: the Map cannot claim "minimal creaturely selection at a few events is simpler than, therefore truer than, an omni-determining agent at every collapse." The case against QDA is not that it is less parsimonious but that it discards the thing the Map's framework exists to secure — creaturely mental causation.

## Key Sources (publisher-verified metadata)

### Where the Conflict Really Lies (Plantinga)
- **Citation**: Plantinga, Alvin. *Where the Conflict Really Lies: Science, Religion, and Naturalism*. Oxford University Press, 2011.
- **Key points**: Divine collapse-causation — God causes the definite outcome of each wave-function collapse; no law is violated; fits libertarian free will. "Superficial conflict but deep concord between science and theistic religion; superficial concord but deep conflict between science and naturalism."
- **Tenet alignment**: Shares Tenet 2's joint; inverts Tenet 3's agent (God, not creature); "all collapses" scope is the omni-determining feature the Map avoids.

### "What is 'Intervention'?" (Plantinga)
- **Citation**: Plantinga, Alvin. "What is 'Intervention'?" *Theology and Science* 6, no. 4 (2008): 369–401. DOI: 10.1080/14746700802396106.
- **Key points**: On contemporary QM there is no sensible way to define "intervention," so the alleged science-vs-special-action conflict cannot be stated. Companion methodological piece to the collapse-causation proposal.

### Quantum Mechanics: Scientific Perspectives on Divine Action (series vol. 5)
- **Citation**: Russell, Robert J., Philip Clayton, Kirk Wegter-McNelly, and John Polkinghorne, eds. *Quantum Mechanics: Scientific Perspectives on Divine Action*. Vatican Observatory & Center for Theology and the Natural Sciences, 2001 (distributed by University of Notre Dame Press). ISBN 9780268039783.
- **Key points**: Fifteen essays; the mature venue for QM-NIODA. Contains Ellis's amplification-via-mutation essay (pp. 259–291).
- **Tenet alignment**: The canonical anchor for QDA-at-the-quantum-joint.

### Chaos and Complexity (series vol. 2)
- **Citation**: Russell, Robert J., Nancey Murphy, and Arthur R. Peacocke, eds. *Chaos and Complexity: Scientific Perspectives on Divine Action*. Vatican Observatory & CTNS, 1995.
- **Key essays**: Murphy, Nancey. "Divine Action in the Natural Order: Buridan's Ass and Schrödinger's Cat" (pp. 325–358) — God acts in every measurement event. Tracy, Thomas F. "Particular Providence and the God of the Gaps" (pp. 289–324) — God acts in only some events.
- **Tenet alignment**: The all-vs-some scope split maps onto the Map's selective-channel commitment.

### Cosmology: From Alpha to Omega (Russell)
- **Citation**: Russell, Robert John. *Cosmology: From Alpha to Omega: The Creative Mutual Interaction of Theology and Science*. Fortress Press, 2008.
- **Key points**: Book-length statement of NIODA within Russell's broader theology-science method.

### Divine Action and Modern Science (Saunders)
- **Citation**: Saunders, Nicholas. *Divine Action and Modern Science*. Cambridge University Press, 2002.
- **Key points**: Radical critique of attempts to reconcile special divine action with QM, chaos theory, and quantum chaos; concludes we remain "far from a satisfactory account" consonant with modern science. Central hostile source.
- **Tenet alignment**: The strongest general objection catalogue against QDA — mine it for the shared-liability items.

### "Outstanding Issues with Robert Russell's NIODA…" (Qureshi-Hurst & Bennett)
- **Citation**: Qureshi-Hurst, Emily, and Christopher T. Bennett. "Outstanding Issues with Robert Russell's NIODA Concerning Quantum Biology and Theistic Evolution." *Zygon* 56, no. 1 (2021): 75–95. DOI: 10.1111/zygo.12663. (Published online 2020.)
- **Key points**: Empirical evidence for the quantum-to-mutation pathway is ambiguous (though quantum biology "looks promising"); the theodicy cost is severe because NIODA gives God extensive agency over evolution and genetics.
- **Tenet alignment**: Supplies both an INHERITED objection (amplification/empirical pathway) and a PECULIAR one (theodicy).

### "Does God Act in the Quantum World?" (Qureshi-Hurst)
