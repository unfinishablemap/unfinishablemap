# Research: The Born Rule — Derivation Attempts and Their Limits (Follow-Up)

**Date**: 2026-03-16
**Search queries used**: Bohmian mechanics quantum equilibrium hypothesis Born rule typicality; GRW CSL spontaneous collapse Born rule stochastic dynamics; transactional interpretation Born rule retrocausal Kastner Cramer; categorical quantum mechanics Born rule Abramsky Coecke; Penrose objective reduction Born rule gravitational self-energy Diosi; Born rule derivation 2025 2026 new recent approaches

## Executive Summary

This follow-up addresses gaps identified in the [[born-rule-derivation-attempts-2026-03-14|prior research]]. Five additional derivation strategies are examined: (1) Bohmian mechanics derives Born-rule statistics from typicality of initial conditions, but cannot explain why the universe began in quantum equilibrium; (2) GRW/CSL collapse models build Born probabilities into their stochastic dynamics by construction, relocating rather than deriving the postulate; (3) the transactional interpretation claims the Born rule emerges from the offer-confirmation handshake between retarded and advanced waves; (4) categorical quantum mechanics shows Born-rule structure arising from abstract algebraic axioms; (5) the Diosi-Penrose model connects collapse timescales to gravitational self-energy without addressing outcome probabilities directly. Two new 2025-2026 papers are also examined: Agrawal & Wilson's process-theoretic derivation and Torres Alegre's causal-consistency argument. The pattern from prior research holds: every approach either relocates the Born rule to another assumption or proves a structural constraint that presupposes measurement and definite outcomes.

## Key Sources

### "On the Explanation of Born-Rule Statistics in the de Broglie-Bohm Pilot-Wave Theory" (Norsen, 2020)
- **URL**: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7512940/
- **Type**: Journal article (PMC / Entropy)
- **Key points**:
  - In Bohmian mechanics, the Born rule is a theorem rather than a postulate, derived from the quantum equilibrium hypothesis (QEH)
  - The QEH states that particle positions are distributed according to |ψ|² — but this is itself an assumption about initial conditions
  - Typicality arguments (Durr, Goldstein, Zanghi) show that for "most" initial configurations, Born-rule statistics emerge — but "most" is measured by the |ψ|² distribution, introducing circularity
  - Valentini's H-theorem approach shows relaxation toward |ψ|² equilibrium, analogous to Boltzmann's approach to thermodynamic equilibrium
- **Tenet alignment**: Partially interesting — Bohmian mechanics is deterministic with hidden variables, which conflicts with Minimal Quantum Interaction (no genuine indeterminacy to bias). However, the typicality circularity supports the Map's broader claim that Born-rule probabilities resist elimination.
- **Quote**: "The physical significance of quantum equilibrium is as a measure of typicality"

### "Collapse Theories" (Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy)
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/qm-collapse/
- **Type**: Encyclopedia entry
- **Key points**:
  - GRW (1986) modifies the Schrodinger equation with random, spontaneous localisations: collapses are more likely where the wavefunction is larger
  - CSL (Ghirardi, Rimini, Pearle 1990) provides a continuous-time version with stochastic differential equations
  - The Born rule is built into the collapse dynamics by design: the stochastic noise terms are calibrated so that the density matrix evolves according to a GKSL (Lindblad) equation, guaranteeing norm conservation, Born-rule statistics, and no superluminal signalling simultaneously
  - This means GRW/CSL do not derive the Born rule — they engineer their dynamics to reproduce it
- **Tenet alignment**: Supports Dualism and Minimal Quantum Interaction indirectly. GRW/CSL provide the "baseline collapse" mechanism the Map invokes for prebiotic scenarios. The fact that these theories must build in Born probabilities by hand leaves room for the Map's claim that consciousness modulates collapse probabilities in neural systems.

### "The Transactional Interpretation of Quantum Mechanics" (Kastner, 2012)
- **URL**: https://www.cambridge.org/core/books/transactional-interpretation-of-quantum-mechanics/D2396E44BDA8812213A50272112E7BB8
- **Type**: Book (Cambridge University Press)
- **Key points**:
  - Cramer's original transactional interpretation (1986): quantum events emerge from "transactions" — handshakes between retarded offer waves (forward in time) and advanced confirmation waves (backward in time)
  - The Born rule emerges as the product of the offer wave ψ and confirmation wave ψ*, giving |ψ|²
  - Kastner's possibilist transactional interpretation (PTI, 2012) treats offer and confirmation waves as real but sub-empirical possibilities, with transactions actualising one possibility
  - Claims a "natural derivation of the Born Rule that emerges from the physics alone"
- **Tenet alignment**: Highly interesting for the Map. The transactional interpretation makes the observer/absorber ontologically necessary for completing transactions, aligning with Bidirectional Interaction. However, the "derivation" works because the advanced wave is defined as ψ* — the complex conjugate — making the product ψ·ψ* = |ψ|² essentially definitional. The Born rule is relocated to the structure of the transaction rather than derived from independent principles.

### "Categorical Quantum Mechanics" (Abramsky & Coecke, 2004-2008)
- **URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/0808.1023
- **Type**: Research programme (multiple papers)
- **Key points**:
  - Reformulates quantum mechanics using monoidal category theory — compact closed categories with biproducts
  - From the combination of compact closure and biproducts, "scalars" and a "Born rule" emerge naturally from the abstract algebraic structure
  - The framework is interpretation-neutral: it captures the structural skeleton of quantum theory without committing to physical ontology
  - Subsequent work has explored how Born-rule structure arises from categorical axioms
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral — categorical approaches prove structural results about the mathematical framework. Like Gleason's theorem, they constrain the form of probability without explaining why measurements yield definite outcomes. The observer remains implicit in the notion of "effects" (the categorical dual of states).

### "Diosi-Penrose Model" (Diosi 1987, Penrose 1996)
- **URL**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Di%C3%B3si%E2%80%93Penrose_model
- **Type**: Theoretical framework (encyclopedic summary)
- **Key points**:
  - Gravity causes wave function collapse: superpositions of distinct mass distributions create superpositions of spacetime geometries, which are gravitationally unstable
  - Collapse timescale τ ≈ ℏ/E_G, where E_G is the gravitational self-energy of the superposition
  - If energy increases divided by ℏ are interpreted as decay rates, one obtains collapse timescales matching the Diosi-Penrose criterion and reduction probabilities matching the Born rule
  - A 2024 review explored gravitationally-induced collapse times for molecules, finding sub-femtosecond timescales for large systems
- **Tenet alignment**: Directly relevant to the Map's position on objective reduction. Penrose's OR provides the "baseline collapse" mechanism invoked in the prebiotic collapse resolution. The Born rule emerges from the gravitational self-energy structure, but the connection requires interpreting energy differences as transition rates — an assumption that smuggles in the probabilistic framework. The model does not explain why *this particular* outcome occurs, only the timescale of collapse and the probability distribution over outcomes.

### "Deriving the Generalised Born Rule from First Principles" (Agrawal & Wilson, 2025/2026)
- **URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.21355
- **Type**: Research paper (arXiv, November 2025, revised February 2026)
- **Key points**:
  - Process-theoretic framework using unitaries, states, effects, and a probability function with minimal compatibility axioms
  - Central result: any process theory equipped with such structure is equivalent to one in which the generalised Born rule holds
  - Adding noise strengthens the connection from homomorphisms to semiring isomorphisms
  - 44-page paper with extensive diagrammatic notation
- **Tenet alignment**: Another structural result. Shows the Born rule is implicit in the process-theoretic framework — but that framework already assumes states, effects (measurements), and a probability function. The derivation relocates the Born rule to the compatibility axioms rather than eliminating it.
- **Quote**: "Any process theory equipped with such structure is equivalent to an alternative process theory in which the generalised Born rule holds"

### "Causal Consistency Selects the Born Rule" (Torres Alegre, 2025/2026)
- **URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.12636
- **Type**: Research paper (arXiv, December 2025, revised February 2026)
- **Key points**:
  - Within generalised probabilistic theories satisfying purification, the only functional relationship between geometric and predictive probabilities consistent with no-signalling is the identity
  - Nonlinear deviations from the Born rule would enable superluminal communication via steering
  - Combined with standard reconstruction results, yields |⟨φ|ψ⟩|² as the unique causally consistent probability assignment
  - Identifies steering as "the mechanism enforcing the Born rule"
- **Tenet alignment**: Philosophically significant. Shows the Born rule is the *only* probability assignment compatible with relativistic causality. This is a strong constraint but does not explain why there are probabilities at all — it assumes probabilistic structure and derives its form. For the Map: if consciousness-mediated collapse must respect no-signalling (as it must to remain empirically invisible), the Born rule is the only option. This *supports* rather than undermines the interface-specification reading — the Born rule is what causal consistency *requires* of any consciousness-physics interface.

## Major Positions (Supplementing Prior Research)

### 8. Bohmian Typicality (Hidden Variables)
- **Proponents**: Durr, Goldstein, Zanghi (1992); Valentini (1991, 2010)
- **Core claim**: Born-rule statistics are typical — they hold for the overwhelming majority of possible initial configurations of the universe
- **What it achieves**: Makes the Born rule a theorem within Bohmian mechanics, conditional on quantum equilibrium
- **What it assumes**: The quantum equilibrium hypothesis — that initial particle positions are |ψ|²-distributed. Typicality is measured using the |ψ|² measure itself, which is circular.
- **The deeper problem**: Two competing approaches exist. Durr et al.'s typicality argument shows Born-rule statistics hold for "most" initial conditions, but "most" is defined by |ψ|² — the very distribution being justified. Valentini's dynamical relaxation shows systems evolve toward quantum equilibrium, but anomalous initial conditions violating the Born rule are logically possible. Neither approach explains why the actual universe satisfies quantum equilibrium.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Bohmian mechanics is deterministic — all outcomes are predetermined by hidden variables. This conflicts with Minimal Quantum Interaction (which requires genuine indeterminacy for consciousness to bias) and Bidirectional Interaction (consciousness cannot select outcomes if they are already determined). The Born rule is explained but consciousness has no role. However, the circularity of the typicality argument is noteworthy: even in the most successful "derivation" of the Born rule, the rule appears in its own justification.

### 9. GRW/CSL Spontaneous Collapse (Objective Collapse)
- **Proponents**: Ghirardi, Rimini, Weber (1986); Pearle (1989); Bassi, Ghirardi (2003)
- **Core claim**: The Born rule is built into the stochastic collapse dynamics, which modify the Schrodinger equation with noise terms calibrated to reproduce Born-rule statistics
- **What it achieves**: Provides a physical mechanism for collapse that recovers the Born rule and definite outcomes
- **What it assumes**: Specific stochastic dynamics — the noise terms are chosen precisely to yield Born-rule probabilities. The GKSL (Lindblad) master equation simultaneously guarantees norm conservation, Born-rule statistics, and no superluminal signalling.
- **The deeper problem**: GRW/CSL do not derive the Born rule from more fundamental principles. They relocate it from a postulate about measurement to a constraint on stochastic dynamics. The Born rule is engineered into the theory rather than emerging from it. As one review notes: "a relation between model parameters is required to obtain Born's rule."
- **Relation to site tenets**: GRW/CSL are compatible with the Map's framework as the "baseline collapse" mechanism for prebiotic scenarios. Consciousness need not be involved in every collapse — GRW/CSL handle non-biological physics. The Map's position is that consciousness *modulates* collapse in neural systems, not that it causes all collapse. The fact that GRW/CSL must build Born probabilities in by hand is consistent with the claim that probabilities at the consciousness interface encode something beyond physics.

### 10. Transactional/Retrocausal Derivation
- **Proponents**: John Cramer (1986); Ruth Kastner (2012, possibilist version)
- **Core claim**: The Born rule emerges naturally from the transaction mechanism — the product of offer and confirmation waves gives |ψ|²
- **What it achieves**: Makes the Born rule a consequence of the transactional structure rather than a separate postulate
- **What it assumes**: Advanced waves (travelling backward in time); that transactions between offer and confirmation waves produce definite outcomes; the confirmation wave is the complex conjugate ψ*
- **The deeper problem**: The "derivation" works because the confirmation wave is defined as ψ* — the complex conjugate of the offer wave. The product ψ·ψ* = |ψ|² is then arithmetically trivial. The physical content is in the claim that advanced waves exist and that transactions produce definite outcomes — both of which are substantial assumptions not derivable from more basic principles. The Born rule is relocated to the definition of the confirmation wave.
- **Relation to site tenets**: The transactional interpretation makes the absorber (observer) ontologically necessary — no transaction occurs without a confirmation wave from the future. This resonates with Bidirectional Interaction. Kastner's possibilist version avoids literal retrocausation by treating offer/confirmation waves as sub-empirical possibilities, which parallels the Map's view that consciousness selects among possibilities. However, TI does not inherently require consciousness — any absorber will do — so the connection to dualism is indirect.

### 11. Categorical/Process-Theoretic Derivation
- **Proponents**: Abramsky & Coecke (2004-2008); Agrawal & Wilson (2025)
- **Core claim**: Born-rule structure emerges from abstract algebraic axioms (compact closure, biproducts, compatibility conditions)
- **What it achieves**: Shows the Born rule is implicit in the categorical structure of quantum theory — it's not an independent axiom within this framework
- **What it assumes**: The categorical axioms themselves, which encode the structural skeleton of quantum mechanics including states, effects (measurements), and composition
- **The deeper problem**: Like Gleason's theorem and the Masanes-Müller reconstruction, categorical derivations prove that the Born rule is the *unique* probability rule consistent with the framework. But the framework already contains measurement (effects) and probability structure. The result is a mathematical consistency theorem, not a physical explanation. It does not explain why measurements yield definite outcomes.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Neutral. Categorical approaches are interpretation-independent — they describe the mathematical structure without committing to physical ontology. The observer is implicit in the notion of "effects" but not analysed.

### 12. Diosi-Penrose Gravitational Collapse
- **Proponents**: Lajos Diosi (1987); Roger Penrose (1996, 2014)
- **Core claim**: Gravitational self-energy of superpositions determines collapse timescales; Born-rule probabilities emerge from interpreting energy gaps as decay rates
- **What it achieves**: Provides a physical mechanism for collapse tied to gravity; connects collapse timescales to observable quantities (mass, spatial separation)
- **What it assumes**: That gravity does not tolerate superpositions of distinct spacetime geometries; that the Heisenberg time-energy uncertainty relation applies to the collapse process; that energy differences map to transition rates
- **The deeper problem**: The model explains *when* collapse occurs (the timescale) but the derivation of Born-rule *probabilities* requires interpreting gravitational self-energy differences as decay rates — an assumption that imports the exponential-decay framework from quantum mechanics, which already contains Born-rule-like structure. The model is more successful at explaining definite outcomes (gravitational instability forces collapse) than at explaining *which* outcome occurs with which probability.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Directly relevant. Penrose's OR is one of the Map's candidate mechanisms for baseline collapse. The model supports No Many Worlds (collapse is real, not all branches persist) and provides the physical substrate for Minimal Quantum Interaction (consciousness could modulate gravitational collapse in neural microtubules, per Orch OR). The incomplete derivation of Born-rule probabilities from gravity alone leaves room for consciousness to contribute the probabilistic component.

## Key Debates (Supplementing Prior Research)

### Typicality vs. Dynamical Relaxation in Bohmian Mechanics
- **Sides**: Durr-Goldstein-Zanghi (typicality is sufficient) vs. Valentini (dynamical relaxation needed) vs. critics (both are circular)
- **Core disagreement**: Whether measuring typicality by the |ψ|² distribution begs the question; whether quantum non-equilibrium (violations of the Born rule) is physically possible
- **Current state**: Ongoing. Valentini has explored cosmological signatures of primordial quantum non-equilibrium — deviations from Born-rule statistics in the early universe. If found, this would be empirically revolutionary. No such signatures have been observed.

### Engineering vs. Deriving the Born Rule in Collapse Models
- **Sides**: GRW/CSL proponents (the dynamics are natural and the Born rule follows) vs. critics (the dynamics are chosen to produce the Born rule)
- **Core disagreement**: Whether calibrating stochastic noise to reproduce Born statistics constitutes a derivation or merely a relocation
- **Current state**: Most physicists working on collapse models acknowledge that the Born rule is an input constraint on the dynamics, not an output. The physics question has shifted to whether collapse models can be experimentally distinguished from standard quantum mechanics.

### Causal Consistency as a New Constraint
- **Sides**: Torres Alegre (2025/2026: Born rule is uniquely selected by no-signalling + steering) vs. those who argue this merely constrains the form without explaining the existence of probability
- **Core disagreement**: Whether showing the Born rule is the only causally consistent probability assignment constitutes an explanation
- **Current state**: Very recent work. The result is mathematically strong — nonlinear deviations from the Born rule would enable superluminal communication. But it assumes the existence of probabilistic predictions and derives their form, not their origin.

## Potential Article Angles

The prior research suggested two article angles: "The Born Rule as Interface Specification" and "What Every Born Rule Derivation Assumes About the Observer." The follow-up research strengthens both and suggests refinements:

1. **Strengthened "Interface Specification" angle**: The causal consistency result (Torres Alegre 2025/2026) adds a powerful constraint. If consciousness modulates collapse, it *must* do so according to the Born rule — any deviation would enable superluminal signalling. This means the Born rule is not just the observed probability law; it is the *only* probability law compatible with relativistic causality at the consciousness-physics interface. The rule specifies how consciousness *must* encounter quantum indeterminacy if it encounters it at all.

2. **New angle: "The Born Rule Across Interpretations"** — Every interpretation handles the Born rule differently, and none eliminates it:
   - Copenhagen: postulates it
   - Many-worlds: derives it from decision theory or self-locating uncertainty (contested)
   - Bohmian: derives it from typicality (circular — measured by |ψ|²)
   - GRW/CSL: builds it into stochastic dynamics (engineering, not derivation)
   - Transactional: defines confirmation wave as ψ* (definitional, not derived)
   - QBism: takes it as normative coherence constraint (avoids rather than solves)
   - Categorical: shows it follows from algebraic structure (structural, not explanatory)

   The Born rule is the one feature all interpretations share and none can explain from more basic principles. This interpretation-invariance suggests it encodes something deeper than any particular physical ontology — consistent with the Map's claim that it encodes the consciousness-physics interface.

## Gaps in Research

- **Consistent histories** (Griffiths, Gell-Mann, Hartle): How does the Born rule arise in the decoherent histories framework? Not investigated.
- **Relational quantum mechanics** (Rovelli): Born-rule probabilities are relative to an observer-system pair. How does this relate to the Map's indexical concerns?
- **Stochastic mechanics** (Nelson): Derives quantum mechanics from classical stochastic processes. What role does the Born rule play?
- **Quantum reference frames**: Recent work on perspective-dependent physics may bear on the observer-dependence of the Born rule.
- **Experimental tests**: Karolyhazy, Diosi, and Penrose models make testable predictions about collapse rates. Current experimental status (e.g., LISA Pathfinder, underground experiments) was not fully investigated.
