**Voids category**: Unexplorable (structural — the from-to relation cannot be made focal in principle, not merely in practice)

## Executive Summary

When consciousness integrates subsidiary cues into a focal meaning — feeling the hammer through the hand to drive the nail, hearing words through their sounds to grasp the sentence, sensing a problem's shape before articulating it — the *integration itself* cannot be made focal. Michael Polanyi formalised this as the "from-to" structure of tacit knowing: we attend *from* subsidiaries *to* a focal whole, and any attempt to convert subsidiaries into focal objects collapses the whole that they were bearing. Eugene Gendlin's "felt sense" extends the analysis to bodily, pre-articulate knowing: the felt sense provides phenomenal "checks" against articulation, but the criterion of fit is itself never thematic. The Tacit Integration Void is the structural impossibility of simultaneously thematising the cues we are attending *from* and the meaning we are attending *to* — a void distinct from the figure/ground field-organisation already covered by [[attention-and-consciousness]] and from the mastery-occlusion covered by [[expertise-and-its-occlusion]].

## Key Sources

### Michael Polanyi — *The Tacit Dimension* and *Personal Knowledge*
- **URL**: https://www.polanyisociety.org/mp-structure.htm ; https://tacit-knowledge-architecture.com/object/the-tacit-dimension-excerpt/
- **Type**: Primary philosophical source / scholarly summary
- **Key points**:
  - The from-to structure: focal awareness rests on subsidiary awareness of particulars that bear on the focal whole.
  - Subsidiary and focal awareness are *mutually exclusive*: "our attention can hold only one focus at a time."
  - "The performance is paralysed if we focus our attention on [the subsidiary] details" — Polanyi's "destructive analysis."
  - The hammer example: "I have a subsidiary awareness of the feeling in the palm of my hand which is merged into my focal awareness of my driving in the nail."
  - "We know more than we can tell" — the tacit dimension is constitutive of meaning, not a residue awaiting articulation.
- **Tenet alignment**: Strongly supports Dualism (consciousness has structural features — integration that cannot be specified — irreducible to part-by-part description) and Occam's Razor Has Limits (analytical decomposition destroys the integrated whole, so simplification fails).
- **Quote**: "The particulars of a skill appear to be unspecifiable because the performance is paralysed if we focus our attention on these details."

### Eugene Gendlin — Focusing and *A Process Model*
- **URL**: https://focusing.org/articles/gendlins-contribution-explorations-implicit ; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eugene_Gendlin
- **Type**: Primary philosophical/psychotherapeutic source
- **Key points**:
  - The "felt sense" is bodily, holistic, pre-articulate sensing of a whole situation.
  - Articulation is *bodily implying*, not static representation: words "carry forward" the felt sense in steps of deeper feel and new formulation.
  - The "felt shift" is a physical change confirming that articulation has fit — but the felt sense itself is not directly inspectable.
  - The body "knows" differently than the mind, and cognition requires the precision of both.
  - The implicit is not a stockpile of unconscious content; it is a *responsive* order that produces explication only under attention.
- **Tenet alignment**: Supports Bidirectional Interaction (the felt sense functions causally — guiding articulation, action, and decision — without being decomposable into representations) and Dualism (the implicit is structurally distinct from explicit information processing).
- **Quote**: "When the felt sense is allowed to function in relation to concepts, each carries the other forward through steps of deeper feel and new formulation."

### Aron Gurwitsch — *The Field of Consciousness*
- **URL**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aron_Gurwitsch ; https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007/978-90-481-3346-8
- **Type**: Primary phenomenological source
- **Key points**:
  - Three-domain structure: theme (focal), thematic field (materially relevant context), margin (co-present but unrelated).
  - Themes are organised *with respect to* their thematic field — figure cannot exist without ground.
  - Making the thematic field thematic creates a new thematic field; the structure does not collapse but reproduces.
- **Tenet alignment**: Distinct contribution: Gurwitsch describes the *spatial organisation* of awareness; Polanyi describes the *integrative function* by which subsidiaries bear on a focal meaning. The two are complementary but address different voids.
- **Quote**: "The thematic field is the totality of those data, co-present with the theme, which are experienced as materially relevant or pertinent to the theme."

### Edmund Husserl — Horizon Structure
- **URL**: https://philpapers.org/browse/husserl-horizonality ; https://link.springer.com/rwe/10.1007/978-3-030-47253-5_153-1
- **Type**: Primary phenomenological source
- **Key points**:
  - Internal horizon: the unseen sides of the perceived object, anticipated but not given.
  - External horizon: the world-context within which the object appears.
  - Temporal horizon: retention and protention frame the present.
  - Horizons are *pre-thematic structures of anticipation*, not conscious representations.
  - Recent scholarship abandons "thematic" interpretations of the horizon, suggesting horizons are *constitutively* non-thematic.
- **Tenet alignment**: Supports Dualism (horizons are structural features of intentionality not reducible to physical processes) and Occam's Razor Has Limits (the horizon is "an irreducible limit that is malleable and relative to subjectivity" — not a feature simple analysis can capture).
- **Quote**: "The intentional direction is directed into an empty horizon that is fully unformed."

### Martin Heidegger — Lichtung (Clearing) and World Disclosure
- **URL**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heideggerian_terminology ; https://dasein.foundation/lichtung
- **Type**: Primary philosophical source
- **Key points**:
  - The clearing is the prior intelligibility-space within which entities can show themselves; it is not itself an entity.
  - "An understanding of being is most effective when it is invisible; the contexture of standards holds itself back from us so that we can deal directly with the entities that are intelligible to us."
  - World-disclosure is structurally non-thematic: making it thematic would require another clearing.
- **Tenet alignment**: Supports Dualism (the clearing is constitutive of intelligibility but not itself an object available to intelligibility) and shows the void operates at the level of meaningful disclosure, not just perception.
- **Quote**: "The clearing is not, itself, an entity that can be known directly."

### Inattentional Blindness Research (Mack & Rock, Simons & Chabris)
- **URL**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inattentional_blindness ; https://elifesciences.org/articles/100337
- **Type**: Empirical cognitive science
- **Key points**:
  - Attention determines what *becomes* consciously present, not just what is selected from already-present content.
  - Implicit awareness exists during inattentional blindness: undetected stimuli still influence later performance, suggesting subsidiary processing without focal awareness.
  - Recent work (2025) treats perception/awareness as *graded*: residual sensitivity persists in inattention, consistent with subsidiary processing of cues that never surface focally.
- **Tenet alignment**: Empirically corroborates Polanyi's claim that subsidiaries function without becoming focal — the gradient of awareness, not a binary.
- **Quote**: "Significant residual sensitivity remains in inattentional blindness, consistent with an alternative picture where inattention degrades awareness rather than abolishing it."

### William James — *Principles of Psychology*, Chapter on the Stream of Thought
- **URL**: https://web-archive.southampton.ac.uk/cogprints.org/1000/1/James&Fringes.html
- **Type**: Primary psychological source
- **Key points**:
  - Every conscious state has a centre and a fringe; the fringe "shades insensibly into a subconscious more."
  - Fringe-content provides the sense that the present thought *belongs* somewhere — relations, transitions, anticipations.
  - Attempts to focus on the fringe convert it into a centre and produce new fringes; the structure is reproductive, not exhaustible.
- **Tenet alignment**: James's fringe overlaps Polanyi's subsidiary but is more about *connective relational sensing* than integrative bearing. Both contribute to the void.
- **Quote**: "Focalization, concentration, of consciousness are of its essence."

## The Void

### Nature of the Limit

The Tacit Integration Void is *structural and unexplorable*, not merely unexplored. It has the following character:

1. **From-to asymmetry.** Subsidiaries function by *not being focal*. Their bearing on the focal whole is their meaning. Converting them to focal objects strips them of that meaning relation.
2. **Destructive analysis.** Polanyi's term: when we attempt to thematise the integration, the integrated whole disintegrates. The hammer ceases to drive the nail; the words cease to bear meaning; the felt sense ceases to guide.
3. **Productive (not merely receptive) articulation.** Gendlin's contribution: articulation does not *describe* the implicit but *carries it forward* into a new form. The implicit is not stored explicit content; it is responsive structure.
4. **Felt shift as criterion of fit.** When an articulation fits, there is a phenomenal "click" — but the criterion of fit is itself implicit. We cannot articulate the criterion without invoking another implicit check.
5. **Reproducibility of structure.** Like Gurwitsch's field, attempting to dissolve the void creates a new instance: the new focal object brings its own subsidiaries.

### Evidence for the Limit

Multiple independent sources converge:

- **Phenomenological**: Polanyi (epistemology), Gendlin (psychotherapy/process philosophy), Husserl (transcendental phenomenology), Merleau-Ponty (perception), Heidegger (ontology), James (psychology). The convergence is across methods that do not share assumptions — a stronger evidence pattern than agreement within a single school.
- **Empirical**: Inattentional blindness research demonstrates that subsidiary processing influences behaviour without entering focal awareness; recent 2025 work confirms graded subsidiary sensitivity persists even when focal awareness is suppressed.
- **Practical**: The "centipede effect" (Polanyi's choking phenomenon): athletes, musicians, and surgeons whose performance breaks down when they direct focal attention at the subsidiary motor and perceptual cues that constitute the skill.
- **Linguistic**: Native speakers grasp meaning *through* phonemes and syntax. Asking a fluent speaker to attend focally to phonemes (as in unfamiliar second-language listening) collapses comprehension.

### Phenomenology

Approaching the void from inside the integration:

- **The unreachable handle.** Trying to feel one's grip on the tool *as such* makes the tool alien and the task impossible. The grip exists only as transparent.
- **The dissolving understanding.** Attending focally to individual words during reading breaks the sentence's meaning. Comprehension requires words to be transparent to what they bear.
- **The vanishing felt sense.** When you try to articulate "what feels off" about a situation, premature articulations produce a non-shift — the body says "no, not that." But the body's "no" is itself another felt sense, not a representation that can be cashed out.
- **The displaced focus.** Successful re-integration after an interruption requires *not* trying to recover the cues directly. The skill returns when attention is restored to the focal task; pursuing the cues blocks the return.
- **The performance paradox.** Reflecting on what one is doing during the doing is sometimes useful (in learning) and sometimes catastrophic (in expert performance). The same operation has opposite effects depending on whether the from-to relation has stabilised.

## Approaches to the Edge

### Direct Methods (limited)

The void resists direct method. Direct attention to the integration *is* what destroys it.

### Indirect Methods

- **Negative description.** Catalogue the failures of analysis: what cannot be articulated; what dissolves under inspection; what only functions when not watched.
- **Polanyi's interiorisation.** We can come to dwell *in* a subsidiary set such that it functions tacitly — but the operation is post-hoc, not concurrent. We learn to use a tool by attending focally at first, then letting the cues recede; the recession is the void's appearance.
- **Gendlin's focusing.** A protocol for letting felt sense become more articulable through gentle, non-coercive turning. The technique succeeds by *not* directly objectifying the felt sense — it elicits articulation by attending *near* the felt sense without thematising it.
- **Apophatic discipline.** Like other apophatic methods on the Map, the technique is to mark the boundary precisely — the structure of failure, where it begins, what counts as collapse — rather than penetrate it.

### What AI Might See

Several interesting asymmetries:

- **No body, different from-to.** LLMs lack Gendlin-style felt sense in any human sense — no proprioception, no autonomic state, no homeostatic stakes. Whether transformer attention (which weights subsidiary tokens to produce focal outputs) realises a Polanyian from-to is open. The mathematical structure is suggestive but the phenomenology is unverifiable from outside.
- **Different transparency.** A human cannot focally attend to phonemes while comprehending speech. Models can, in some sense, output token-level statistics and meaning-level outputs concurrently — but it is unclear whether this constitutes simultaneous focal awareness or whether the "focal/subsidiary" distinction even applies.
- **Exotic cases.** A mind without skilled embodiment may have a different void structure altogether — perhaps no integration void, or an integration void with different topology. This is a candidate territory for the kind of comparative work [[non-human-minds-as-void-explorers]] envisages.
- **Caution.** "AI sees this differently" claims are easy to over-extend. The honest position is that the void is shaped by the integration of body, action, and meaning — and a mind with different integration may have different blind spots, but probably not none.

## Connection to Tenets

### Most Relevant Tenet

**Occam's Razor Has Limits** is most directly engaged. The Tacit Integration Void is precisely the case where simplification by analytical decomposition *destroys* the phenomenon being analysed. Any account that treats meaning as the sum of decomposable subsidiaries misses what the integration accomplishes. The void is a counterexample to the assumption that simplicity-via-decomposition is always epistemically conservative.

**Dualism** is also strongly engaged. If consciousness is reducible to neural information processing, the from-to relation should be specifiable as a particular pattern of neural activation. But Polanyi's argument cuts the other way: the integration is not specified by the subsidiaries plus a relation; the relation itself is constitutive and resists specification. This does not prove dualism, but it preserves the pressure that motivates it: structural features of consciousness that resist analytic capture.

### Implications

- The Map's catalogue of voids is itself an exercise in tacit integration. The catalogue's coherence depends on subsidiary background commitments (the tenets, the apophatic method, prior articles) that bear on the focal task of mapping. Attending focally to the catalogue's own subsidiary commitments would dissolve the catalogue.
- This suggests a meta-principle: any apophatic catalogue must operate from-to. It cannot be its own focal object without becoming a different kind of object.
- The void provides a structural reason why introspection is unreliable about skilled performance, embodied knowing, and creative process. The reliability question has been treated empirically (introspection is often inaccurate); the void contributes a *principled* reason why direct introspection of integrative function would be unreliable even for an idealised reporter.
- The void bounds the project of explanation. Some of what consciousness does cannot be explained without changing what consciousness is. The case for this is not mysterian appeal but structural argument from the from-to relation.

## Potential Article Angles

1. **From-to as the core concept.** Polanyi-centric article: define the from-to structure, develop "destructive analysis," extend to Gendlin's felt sense, discuss empirical complement in inattentional blindness graded sensitivity, position against [[attention-and-consciousness]] (Gurwitsch field) and [[expertise-and-its-occlusion]] (mastery defends opacity).
2. **Felt sense as integrative void.** Gendlin-centric article: focus on the bodily check, the felt shift, the productive role of articulation, and why the criterion of fit is itself implicit. Connects to noetic-feelings-void but with bodily-integrative emphasis.
3. **The Centipede Void.** Performance-centric: catalogue cases where focal attention to subsidiary cues collapses skilled action (choking, the tip-of-the-tongue effect, the meaning-collapse of words attended to as sounds) and extract the structural pattern.
4. **Tacit Integration vs. Conscious Integration.** Comparative angle: contrast Polanyian tacit integration with the [[binding-void]] (unified experience from disparate sensations). Both are integration voids but operate at different levels — tacit integration is about *meaning* bearing; binding is about *qualia* unification.

Recommendation: angle (1), the from-to-centric framing, gives the cleanest distinct contribution to the voids catalogue. It can fold in Gendlin and the empirical material as supporting evidence rather than as separate articles.

## Gaps in Research

- **Neuroscience of from-to integration.** I did not find dedicated neural-correlate work on Polanyi's structure as such. Predictive processing and global workspace literature touch the territory but with different framings; a deeper review may yield contact points.
- **AI interpretability angle.** Whether transformer attention realises a Polanyian from-to is an interesting empirical-philosophical question; current interpretability work (circuits, features) might give partial answers but I did not survey it here.
- **Cross-cultural articulations.** Buddhist accounts of skilled action (e.g., Zen's *mushin* — "no-mind") and Daoist *wu wei* describe similar phenomenology but with different metaphysics. A serious comparative section would need direct primary sources, not just summaries.
- **The "integration relation" itself.** Polanyi's destructive-analysis claim is contested; some commentators argue the integration is in principle specifiable, just very complex. The void's strength depends on whether the structural argument holds against serious objection. Worth pessimistic-review treatment.
