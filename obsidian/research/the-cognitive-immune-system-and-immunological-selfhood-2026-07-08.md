## Executive Summary

The immune system discriminates self from non-self, remembers antigens it has met (immunological memory), adapts its repertoire over a lifetime, and makes distributed context-sensitive decisions — all with zero neurons and no plausible experiential interface. Irun R. Cohen's "cognitive immune system" reframes immunity as an information-processing, self-representing, *learning* system, complete with an "immunological homunculus" (the immune system's internal image of the body's own molecules). This makes immunity the strongest non-neural learning-and-memory case the Map does not yet own — a fourth sibling to plant cognition, bioelectric/basal cognition, and the enteric nervous system. The crucial move for the Map's competency-without-experience cluster is that the "immunological self" is a **functional/informational self, not a phenomenal self**: it names a body's ability to track and defend its own molecular identity, not a felt point of view. Alfred Tauber is the strongest philosophical voice arguing the "immune self" is a metaphorically loaded concept imported from Western assumptions about autonomous identity — a caution to engage, not merely assert. The whole area sits squarely inside Tenet-3 interface-localisation: there is no neural substrate here, so on Tenet-2 minimality there is no interface to posit.

## Key Sources

### Cohen, "The cognitive paradigm and the immunological homunculus"
- **URL**: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/1463581/
- **Type**: Journal paper (Immunology Today 13(12):490–494, 1992; DOI 10.1016/0167-5699(92)90024-2; PMID 1463581) — VERIFIED at PubMed / ScienceDirect / Weizmann repository
- **Key points**:
  - Argues the clonal-selection paradigm is inadequate; proposes a *cognitive paradigm* in which "preformed internal images guide and restrict the process of clonal activation."
  - Names the immune system's internal image of the body's own molecules the "immunological homunculus" — a self-representation the system carries and computes against.
  - Immune cognition = comparison of a perceived antigenic signal against an internal, learned picture of the world, followed by choice of one response from a large repertoire.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral-to-illustrative for Tenet 3 (a competency with no neural substrate → no interface on Tenet 2). The "cognition" here is functional/computational, not phenomenal.
- **Note**: The homunculus idea has an antecedent in Whittingham and Mackay (1971); Cohen reframed and generalised it. Cite Cohen for the cognitive-paradigm formulation.

### Cohen, *Tending Adam's Garden: Evolving the Cognitive Immune Self*
- **URL**: https://philpapers.org/rec/COHTAG-2
- **Type**: Book (Academic Press / Elsevier, 2000; ISBN 9780121783563) — VERIFIED via PhilPapers, Elsevier, Google Books
- **Key points**:
  - Presents immune function as a dynamic, self-organising system "devoted to cognition."
  - Ranges beyond immunology into causality, information, energy, evolution, cognition, and individuality.
  - Frames the immune system as maintaining a self-image and choosing among responses — learning without a nervous system.
- **Tenet alignment**: Illustrative of competency-without-experience; the "cognitive immune self" is explicitly a *functional* self.
- **Companion paper**: Cohen, "The cognitive principle challenges clonal selection," Immunology Today, 1992 (PMID 1476598) — VERIFIED at PubMed.

### Tauber, *The Immune Self: Theory or Metaphor?*
- **URL**: https://philpapers.org/rec/TAUTIS-3 ; PubMed companion article https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/8172646/
- **Type**: Book (Cambridge University Press, Cambridge Studies in Philosophy and Biology, 1994; ISBN 9780521574433) + companion article (Immunology Today 15(3):134–136, 1994; DOI 10.1016/0167-5699(94)90157-0; PMID 8172646) — BOTH VERIFIED
- **Key points**:
  - First extended *philosophical* critique of immunology; analyses the function of the term "self" underpinning immune theory.
  - Traces the "self" concept from Metchnikoff's view of immunity as "the active pursuit of identity"; argues immunology's "self" reflects Western cultural preoccupations with autonomy and insularity.
  - Critiques the substantialist view that immunity defends an unchanging metaphysical core; argues for a dynamic, relational, ecological reconception of identity.
  - Later developed in Tauber, *Immunity: The Evolution of an Idea* (Oxford University Press, 2017).
- **Tenet alignment**: Methodologically vital caution. Tauber is the reason the Map should treat "immune self" as *metaphorically loaded* — the strongest argument that immunological "selfhood" is a theory-laden term, not a discovered entity. Engage, do not merely assert.
- **Author**: Professor emeritus of Philosophy, Boston University; hematologist/biochemist by training — a rare immunologist-philosopher.

### Matzinger, "The Danger Model: A Renewed Sense of Self"
- **URL**: https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2002Sci...296..301M
- **Type**: Journal paper (Science 296(5566):301–305, 2002) — VERIFIED via NASA ADS bibcode 2002Sci...296..301M; original danger theory in Matzinger, "Tolerance, danger, and the extended family," Annu. Rev. Immunol. 1994
- **Key points**:
  - Immune activation depends on stress-/damage-associated "danger" signals, not on foreignness per se; a self-antigen presented without danger is tolerated, the same antigen amid tissue injury provokes response.
  - Directly challenges the ~60-year dominance of self/non-self discrimination — the categorical self/nonself boundary is dissolved into contextual interpretation.
  - Predicted endogenous danger molecules (HMGB1, uric acid, heat-shock proteins, etc.), several since found.
- **Tenet alignment**: Reinforces that "the immunological self" is not a fixed inner essence but a context-dependent functional distinction — helpful for keeping the self here firmly *functional*, not phenomenal.

### Burnet, clonal selection theory (1957)
- **URL**: https://www.wehi.edu.au/about/history/clonal-selection-theory/ ; https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19408101/
- **Type**: Historical theory / review sources — VERIFIED (WEHI history; PubMed review PMID 19408101)
- **Key points**:
  - Each lymphocyte clone carries a unique antigen receptor; the matching antigen selects and expands that clone.
  - Self-reactive clones are deleted/inactivated during development → self-tolerance; establishes self/non-self discrimination as the organising dogma.
  - Selected clones form memory cells → immunological memory: a faster, stronger response on re-exposure.
- **Tenet alignment**: This is the mechanism that makes immunity a genuine *learning* system — selection-plus-memory, structurally Darwinian, with no neurons. Prime example of the cluster's competency/experience decoupling.

### SEP, "Philosophy of Immunology"
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/immunology/
- **Type**: Encyclopedia — VERIFIED via WebFetch
- **Key points**:
  - Situates all four frameworks: Burnet (self/nonself + tolerance-through-development), Cohen (immunity as information-processing network analogous to the nervous system, distributed computation without central control), Matzinger (danger over foreignness), Tauber (self as culturally-laden concept; relational/ecological identity).
  - Notes Cohen positions cognition as extending across bodily systems beyond the brain.

## Major Positions

### Self/Non-self Discrimination (Burnet)
- **Proponents**: Frank Macfarlane Burnet; the immunological mainstream ~1957 onward.
- **Core claim**: The immune system defends a molecularly-defined "self" by deleting self-reactive clones in development and responding to "non-self" antigens; memory makes it a learning system.
- **Relation to site tenets**: The canonical non-neural learning-and-memory case. Functional selfhood only — no claim of experience. Tenet 2/3: no neural substrate → no interface to posit.

### The Cognitive Immune System / Immunological Homunculus (Cohen)
- **Proponents**: Irun R. Cohen.
- **Core claim**: Immunity is a self-organising, information-processing, *cognitive* system that carries an internal self-image (the homunculus) and chooses responses by comparing signals against learned internal pictures.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Strongest non-neural cognition-language case. The Map should adopt Cohen's own care: "cognition" here is computational/functional. It is precisely a competency without a plausible experiential interface — a clean instance of the cluster's decoupling.

### The Danger Model (Matzinger)
- **Proponents**: Polly Matzinger.
- **Core claim**: The system responds to damage/danger context, not to foreignness; the self/non-self boundary is contextual, not a fixed inner essence.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Keeps "the immunological self" from hardening into a metaphysical inner core — useful pressure toward the functional reading.

### Philosophical Critique of the "Immune Self" (Tauber)
- **Proponents**: Alfred I. Tauber.
- **Core claim**: "Self" in immunology is a metaphor imported from Western ideals of autonomous identity, not a discovered entity; identity is dynamic, relational, ecological.
- **Relation to site tenets**: The essential counterweight. Tauber warns that calling the immune system a "self" (or "cognitive") risks smuggling in exactly the phenomenal connotations the Map must strip out. Engage him directly — he strengthens the functional/phenomenal distinction rather than threatening it.

## Key Debates

### Is "cognition"/"self" in immunology literal or metaphorical?
- **Sides**: Cohen (cognitive paradigm is a genuine, fruitful characterisation) vs. Tauber (self/cognition are theory-laden metaphors reflecting cultural assumptions).
- **Core disagreement**: Whether describing immunity as cognitive/self-representing tracks real system properties or projects folk-psychological framing onto molecular processes.
- **Current state**: Ongoing; productive tension. For the Map, both can be true at the functional level — the dispute is exactly about whether functional language leaks phenomenal content.

### Self/non-self vs. danger
- **Sides**: Burnet/clonal-selection orthodoxy vs. Matzinger's danger model (and later "discontinuity"/context theories).
- **Core disagreement**: What the immune system is fundamentally tracking — molecular foreignness or damage context.
- **Current state**: Unresolved; hybrid/contextual views now common (cf. recent reviews questioning the self/nonself dogma).
