## Executive Summary

Microphenomenology is a disciplined second-person interview method developed by Claire Petitmengin (building on Pierre Vermersch's *entretien d'explicitation*) that guides a subject through a phenomenological reduction, evokes a specific past experiential moment in embodied memory, and elicits fine-grained description of its pre-reflective structure through non-leading content- and position-direction questions. Analysis transforms singular descriptions into generic diachronic (temporal) and synchronic (momentary) structures via a 15-stage iterative abductive procedure. Applications include epileptic prodrome recognition, meditation phenomenology, pain, creative insight, and HCI. No published study has used the method to test metaphysical dualism directly, but the technique bears on Husserlian evidence standards: it systematically strengthens assertoric and presumptive claims about experiential content while leaving apodictic claims (that experience has structure at all) untouched. Documented limits include retrospective reconstruction risk, demand characteristics, cognitive load, and the need for extensive interviewer training; reliability studies (Olivares et al. 2015) show ~80% detection of experimental manipulations.

## Key Sources

### Petitmengin (2006) — Describing one's subjective experience in the second person
- **URL**: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11097-006-9022-2 and https://clairepetitmengin.fr/AArticles%20versions%20finales/PCS%20-%20Second%20Person.pdf
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed paper (*Phenomenology and the Cognitive Sciences*, 5(3), 229–269)
- **Key points**:
  - Founding paper for the method as deployed in consciousness science
  - Gathers first-person data (subject's "I…") through another person (a "You") — hence "second-person method"
  - Taxonomy of difficulties in becoming aware of experience, with specific techniques for each
  - Enables even untrained subjects to become aware of their pre-reflective subjective experience
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral-to-supportive of Dualism tenet — provides rigorous technique for first-person access; does not itself adjudicate metaphysics.
- **Quote**: "The method gathers 'first person' data … But as these data have been gathered through another person (a 'You'), it has been dubbed a 'second person' method."

### Depraz, Varela & Vermersch (2003) — *On Becoming Aware: A Pragmatics of Experiencing*
- **URL**: https://benjamins.com/catalog/aicr.43
- **Type**: Book (John Benjamins)
- **Key points**:
  - Three-gesture model of becoming aware: suspension (epoché), redirection of attention (from content to act), receptivity/letting-come
  - Husserlian phenomenology read pragmatically — as a *method* for exploring experience, not primarily a metaphysics
  - Bridges phenomenology to meditation, psychoanalysis, empirical research
- **Tenet alignment**: Consistent with site position on phenomenology-as-method.
- **Relation**: Theoretical framework for what Petitmengin's technique operationalises

### Petitmengin, Remillieux & Valenzuela-Moguillansky (2019) — Discovering the structures of lived experience
- **URL**: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11097-018-9597-4 (cited as https://clairepetitmengin.fr/onewebmedia/PCS%20-%20Analysis.pdf in source list)
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed paper (*Phenomenology and the Cognitive Sciences*, 18(4), 691–730)
- **Key points**:
  - Systematises the *analysis* side of the method (separate from interview technique)
  - 15-stage "concertina" procedure across five sections
  - Iterative abductive process: singular pre-reflective descriptions → refined generic experiential structures
  - Diachronic (temporal unfolding) and synchronic (momentary structure) units of analysis constructed and linked
- **Tenet alignment**: Methodological — enables replicability claims
- **Relevance**: Answers the question "how is microphenomenological data coded?" The paper makes the analytic procedure traceable and thereby intersubjectively checkable.

### Bitbol & Petitmengin (2017) — Neurophenomenology and the Micro-phenomenological Interview
- **URL**: https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1002/9781119132363.ch51
- **Type**: Book chapter (*Blackwell Companion to Consciousness*, 2nd ed.)
- **Key points**:
  - Direct link between Varela's 1996 neurophenomenology programme and microphenomenology
  - Microphenomenology is the *operational* component of neurophenomenology's "first-person method" side
  - Mutual-constraint: fine-grained descriptions discipline neural data interpretation; neural data disciplines what descriptions are tracking real structural differences

### Petitmengin (2019) — Studying the experience of meditation through Micro-phenomenology
- **URL**: https://podcast.mindandlife.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/Petitmengin_Studying-the-experience_CurrOpin2019.pdf
- **Type**: *Current Opinion in Psychology*
- **Key points**:
  - Applies the method to contemplative states
  - Very few studies on the lived experience of meditation (moment-to-moment) exist — microphenomenology fills this gap
  - Boundary-dissolution studies identify six experiential features: sense of location, agency, first-person perspective, attention, body sensations, affective valence

### Petitmengin, Navarro & Le Van Quyen (2006/2007) — Seizure anticipation studies
- **URL**: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16861044/ and https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/17590351/
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed papers (*Epilepsy and Behavior*; *Phenomenology and the Cognitive Sciences*)
- **Key points**:
  - Conducted at Varela's Salpêtrière team
  - Interviews revealed *prodromes* (continuous, hour-scale preictal symptoms) distinct from *auras* (sudden, intermittent)
  - Prodromes were invisible to casual introspection and to third-person neurodynamic measurements alone; microphenomenology surfaced them
  - Clinical pay-off: patients can learn to recognise prodromes and take counter-measures, reducing seizures non-pharmacologically
- **Relevance**: Strongest extant demonstration that the method yields *novel*, clinically actionable first-person data that would be otherwise unavailable

### Olivares et al. (2015) / reliability literature — cited via Sparby & others
- **URL**: Discussed in https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1111/sjop.12858 and https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11097-021-09744-z
- **Type**: Empirical reliability study (cited in reviews)
- **Key points**:
  - Experimental manipulations detected in ~80% of trials when microphenomenological interview was used
  - Supports claim that the method produces descriptions tracking real experiential differences, not noise
- **Limit**: 80% is far from 100%; leaves room for false positives and misses

### Hurlburt & Akhter / Petitmengin response — Can we trust the phenomenological interview?
- **URL**: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11097-021-09744-z
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed paper on *Phenomenology and the Cognitive Sciences*
- **Key points**:
  - Catalogues metaphysical, epistemological, and methodological objections
  - Memory reconstruction / confabulation concern — Nisbett & Wilson (1977) heritage
  - Response: the method's evocation and non-leading structure *address* these concerns but do not eliminate them

### Valenzuela-Moguillansky & Vásquez-Rosati (2019) — Micro-phenomenology applied to itself
- **URL**: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9834112/
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed paper
- **Key points**:
  - Meta-study: what is it like to be interviewed, what makes a "good" interview?
  - Supports claim that the method is *self-applicable* — an unusual form of reflexive validation

### Di Paolo & De Jaegher (2019) — Microphenomenology of first encounters: A sympathetic critique
- **URL**: https://constructivist.info/14/2/185.dipaolo
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed commentary
- **Key points**:
  - Enactivist critics: method works well for individual experiential moments but struggles with intrinsically relational experiences (interpersonal encounters)
  - Some experiences resist elicitation because "there is just too much going on"

### Sparby (2023) — Training naive subjects in micro-phenomenological self-inquiry for headache
- **URL**: https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1111/sjop.12858
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed paper (*Scandinavian Journal of Psychology*)
- **Key points**:
  - Extends method to *self-inquiry* (no external interviewer)
  - Removes second-person bias but admits first-person bias
  - Cognitively demanding; requires training

## Major Positions

### Proponent: Microphenomenology yields replicable first-person data
- **Proponents**: Petitmengin; Depraz; Varela (before 2001); Bitbol; Vermersch; Valenzuela-Moguillansky; Sparby
- **Core claim**: Disciplined interview guided by phenomenological reduction + evocation of a specific, singular moment + non-leading content- and position-direction questions enables description of pre-reflective experiential structure previously inaccessible to casual introspection. Iterative analysis yields generic structures that converge across subjects and across independent analysts.
- **Key arguments**:
  - Procedural specificity makes steps traceable (2019 analysis paper)
  - Convergence at three levels: intra-subject consistency, inter-subject structural recurrence, inter-analyst reliability
  - Novel clinically useful data in epilepsy case (2006/2007)
  - Reliability study: 80% detection of manipulations
- **Relation to site tenets**: Directly supports the *Phenomenological Method and Evidence Standards* topic and the Dualism tenet. Microphenomenology operationalises what Husserl's evidence taxonomy names — it sharpens assertoric descriptions of pre-reflective content while the apodictic bedrock (*that* there is experiencing) is not at stake. Supports Occam's Razor Has Limits: materialist "simplicity" may reflect methods that cannot detect the relevant structure, not absence of structure (cf. epilepsy prodromes invisible to neurodynamic analysis alone).

### Critical: Introspective unreliability undermines the method
- **Proponents**: Hurlburt (as adversary); Schwitzgebel (as general introspective sceptic); Nisbett & Wilson heritage
- **Core claim**: Memory reconstruction, confabulation, demand characteristics, and observer-contamination by the interviewer systematically corrupt reports. No procedural discipline can fully eliminate these sources of error because the target (past pre-reflective experience) is itself not directly accessible.
- **Key arguments**:
  - Reports are retrospective — we reconstruct, not re-perceive
  - The interviewee's engagement with the interviewer introduces social-pragmatic pressures
  - Many "discoveries" in interviews may be productions, not recoveries
- **Relation to site tenets**: Tension with Dualism's reliance on phenomenological evidence. Honest response on the Map: differential reliability — disciplined method improves on naive introspection, but the improvement is bounded and should not be overstated.

### Sympathetic-critical: method works but in a restricted domain
- **Proponents**: Di Paolo & De Jaegher; enactivist community partly
- **Core claim**: The method is genuinely informative for self-enclosed experiential episodes (sensation, attention shifts, insights in solitude) but systematically under-resolves intrinsically relational, temporally extended, and socially distributed experiences.
- **Key arguments**:
  - "First encounters" study: dyadic experience cannot be decomposed into two independent first-person reports without distortion
  - Method presupposes a subject-centred unit of analysis that may not fit all experiential phenomena
- **Relation to site tenets**: Useful corrective for any Map argument that over-extends microphenomenological results. Suggests scope limits on phenomenological evidence claims.

## Key Debates

### Is microphenomenology genuinely second-person or covertly first-person?
- **Sides**: Petitmengin (second-person legitimate name because data gathered *via* an interviewer); purists who hold that the data is still first-person in content and the interviewer is mere facilitator
- **Core disagreement**: Whether the *person-grammar* labelling matters epistemically — does the interviewer's role alter the evidential character of the resulting descriptions?
- **Current state**: Mostly settled terminologically — Petitmengin's "second-person" usage is the standard label; but the deeper question of what the interviewer contributes to the epistemic quality remains active.

### Can the method be scaled down to self-inquiry without loss?
- **Sides**: Sparby (yes, with trade-offs); Petitmengin (interviewer contributes non-redundant value — "mediating expert")
- **Core disagreement**: Whether external mediation is constitutive of the method's reliability gains or merely instrumentally useful
- **Current state**: Active — Sparby 2022/2023 papers argue for micro-phenomenological self-inquiry (MPSI); debate ongoing.

### What validates microphenomenological data?
- **Sides**:
  - Coherence (intra-subject consistency of report)
  - Generic-structure recurrence (inter-subject convergence)
  - Inter-analyst agreement
  - Triangulation with third-person methods (neurophenomenology)
- **Core disagreement**: Which combination suffices for scientific acceptance, and whether any combination bridges the gap to *epistemic* (not just methodological) reliability
- **Current state**: The 2019 analysis paper formalises the procedures; empirical reliability work is ongoing.

### Relation to Husserlian evidence taxonomy
- **Sides**: Bitbol/Petitmengin (implicit — microphenomenology as empirical counterpart to Husserlian method); Berghofer (evidentialist reading of Husserl could endorse microphenomenological reports as instances of *originary presentive intuition*, specifically introspective and perceptual types)
- **Core disagreement**: Whether microphenomenology delivers apodictic, adequate, or merely assertoric evidence
- **Working answer (not in literature, inference from this research)**:
  - Apodictic claim (*that* the subject had experience of some structure): secured by the apodictic evidence of the subject's living present at the time of the original experience (though the *report* is assertoric about that prior apodictic givenness)
  - Adequate claim: not available — experience has horizonal remainder, and report further attenuates
  - Assertoric claim (*what* structure the experience had): microphenomenology strengthens the warrant substantially — this is its primary evidential gain
  - Presumptive claim (*generic* structures across subjects): iterative analysis produces presumptive-strengthened-toward-eidetic claims via convergence

## Tenet Alignment Considerations for a Future Article

- **Dualism**: Microphenomenology provides a rigorous first-person method that strengthens the evidential base for phenomenal authority claims. It does *not* prove dualism, but it answers the "but first-person reports are unreliable" objection with a more disciplined method. Honest framing: it shifts the quality of first-person evidence; it does not tip the metaphysics by itself.
- **Minimal Quantum Interaction**: Neutral. Microphenomenology is compatible with any metaphysics that admits phenomenological data as real.
- **Bidirectional Interaction**: Weak support. If phenomenological reports reliably track experiential structure (as the 80% reliability result suggests), then the very existence of these reports is evidence that consciousness has causal access to its own structure — a fact epiphenomenalism must handle.
- **No Many Worlds**: Irrelevant.
- **Occam's Razor Has Limits**: Direct support via the epilepsy prodrome case. Prodromes existed, were clinically actionable, and were invisible to the simpler third-person methodology. Microphenomenology detected them. A parsimony-first methodology would have missed them. This is a concrete illustration of the tenet.

## Gaps in Research

- **No dualism-specific studies found.** Microphenomenology has been used for clinical, contemplative, and HCI applications but not, as far as this research can find, to test metaphysical dualism claims (e.g., apparent causation of neural events by phenomenal states). This is a genuine gap — claims that the method supports dualism must be careful about inferring from methodological rigour to metaphysical conclusion.
- **Limited work on where microphenomenology fails.** Di Paolo & De Jaegher catalogued some failure modes for relational experiences, but a systematic map of the method's reliability by experience-type is absent from what surfaced in search.
- **Reliability literature is thin.** The 80% figure from Olivares et al. is widely cited but I did not find replications or a meta-analysis.
- **Relation to Husserlian evidence taxonomy is under-theorised** — the literature tends to treat microphenomenology as "inspired by Husserl" rather than mapping it onto the apodictic/assertoric/presumptive distinctions explicitly. An article drawing those connections explicitly would be novel.
- **Training requirements are described qualitatively** but not quantified — how many hours, what exactly is trained, what the acquisition curve looks like.
- **Philosophical critique beyond introspective scepticism** is sparse. The Hurlburt-adjacent objections paper catalogues concerns, but deeper engagement with e.g. heterophenomenological challenges is limited.
