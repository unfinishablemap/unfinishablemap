## Executive Summary

The Neoplatonist common-cause channel earns a **moderate** rating overall, with sharply different ratings for the three downstream tradition-pairs: **moderate-to-substantial** for early Sufism (Plotinus reaching the early-Sufi milieu via the Arabic Plotinus / *Theology of Aristotle* in the al-Kindī circle by the mid-9th century, the same generation as Muḥāsibī; subsequent Sufi inner-faculty doctrine in al-Ghazālī and Ibn ʿArabī is heavily Avicennan-Neoplatonist); **weak** for late Stoicism (the direction of influence runs the other way — Plotinus inherited and modified Stoic cognitive material rather than feeding it, so Neoplatonism cannot be a common ancestor of Stoic propatheia / phantasia / synkatathesis since those doctrines pre-date Plotinus by 500 years); **weak-to-negligible** for Advaita Vedānta (the Bréhier 1928 thesis of Indian influence on Plotinus was largely rejected after Armstrong 1936; the Hacker / Aklan re-examinations conclude any Plotinian-Advaitic transmission was vague at best, and the Dārā Shikoh / Suhrawardī Vedānta-Islamic mediation channels run in the wrong direction chronologically for Shankara's 8th-century *adhyāsa* doctrine and post-date Advaita's architectural-specifics by half a millennium). The net implication for the cross-tradition channel article: **the score holds at "low-moderate" rather than collapsing to kleshas-only-equivalent**, but the *Sufi* component requires a sharper discount than the article currently applies; early Sufi inner-faculty psychology (anterior to and including Muḥāsibī) carried a Neoplatonist substrate via the Christian monastic tradition (Evagrian *logismoi*) and via direct philosophical transmission through the al-Kindī circle. The *khawāṭir* taxonomy and *muḥāsaba* practice's "cleanest historical-isolation defence" status the cross-tradition article assigns is **partly undermined**: the architectural shape of the practice (continuous self-examination, taxonomy of incoming thought-sources) was prefigured in Evagrius Ponticus's 4th-century Christian monastic *logismoi* doctrine and may have reached Sufi practitioners through the Syriac-Christian monastic context in which 9th-century Baghdad Sufism formed. The Stoic *propatheia* finding is **unaffected** by the Neoplatonist channel (the direction of influence rules it out). The Advaita architectural-specifics findings are **unaffected** by the Neoplatonist channel (the chronology rules it out). The overall scoring on the parent exhibit's Criterion 3 should be: hold the cross-tradition channel at *low-moderate*, but apply a sharper Sufi-specific common-cause discount (Christian-monastic-via-Evagrius + Arabic-Plotinus-via-al-Kindī) than the current article applies.

## Key Sources

### Plotinus on Consciousness and the Unnoticed

- **URLs**:
  - https://docs.rwu.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=1034&context=saahp_fp (Hendrix, "Plotinus: The First Philosopher of the Unconscious")
  - https://bmcr.brynmawr.edu/2018/2018.10.57/ (review of *Plotinus on Consciousness*, ed. Pauliina Remes)
  - https://ndpr.nd.edu/reviews/plotinus-on-consciousness/
- **Type**: Specialist secondary literature
- **Key points**:
  - Hans Rudolph Schwyzer's 1960 essay "Bewusst und unbewusst bei Plotin" called Plotinus "the discoverer of the unconscious"
  - Plotinus directly asks (V.1.12.1–15): "Why then…do we not consciously grasp them [intellect and its activity]…? For not everything which is in the soul is immediately perceptible"
  - And (IV.3.30.1–17): "we are always intellectually active but do not always apprehend our activity"
  - Plotinus uses a tetrad of consciousness-related concepts: *antilêpsis* (apprehension), *parakolouthêsis* (self-consciousness), *sunaisthêsis* (self-awareness / proprioception), *sunesis* (awareness) — corresponding to different levels in his hierarchical metaphysics
  - Cudworth in the 17th century imported *sunaisthêsis* into English as "consciousness" — the term is genealogically Plotinian
- **Significance for the audit**: Plotinus articulated a clear architectural-level claim that introspective access is *limited* — the soul has activity "above" the threshold of conscious apprehension. This is a Neoplatonist architectural finding parallel to the cross-tradition cluster. If subsequent traditions inherited the Plotinian framing of "soul activity beyond conscious apprehension," the apparent independent discovery of bounded introspective access across traditions could be common-cause Neoplatonism rather than independent observation. *However*: the directionality matters — Plotinus did not anticipate the *source-attribution* or *confabulation* faces of the void cluster; his framework predicts unnoticed intellectual activity (criterion-3 failure: framework-level prediction) rather than reconstructive source-tagging or post-hoc rationalisation.

### Stoic Material in Plotinus (sunaisthêsis, Bodily Proprioception)

- **URL**: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11245-011-9114-7 (Remes / Topoi article on Plotinus's account of cognitive powers); https://bmcr.brynmawr.edu/2018/2018.10.57/
- **Type**: Specialist secondary literature
- **Key points**:
  - Remes: Plotinus's discussion of bodily self-awareness (*sunaisthêsis*) "correctly highlights the Stoic influence" — the Stoic notion of *sympatheia* and bodily proprioception is integrated into Plotinian psychology, *dematerialised* in Platonist fashion
  - Plotinus's writings are described as "full of concealed Stoic and Peripatetic doctrines" (Remes / Smith); Plotinus rarely names his sources by contrast with Porphyry
  - Plotinus critiques Stoic emotion theory (e.g. *Enn.* 3.6.4.21 on the genesis of fear) but inherits substantial Stoic cognitive vocabulary
- **Significance for the audit**: The direction of influence is **Stoic → Plotinus**, not Plotinus → Stoic. The classical Stoa (Zeno c. 300 BCE, Chrysippus c. 280–207 BCE, through Epictetus and Marcus Aurelius in the 2nd century CE) developed *propatheia*, *phantasia katalēptikē*, *synkatathesis* before Plotinus (3rd century CE) wrote. **Plotinus cannot be a common-cause ancestor of these doctrines.** He is downstream of them. The Neoplatonist common-cause channel for Stoicism is *closed* by chronology.

### Plotinus on Stoic Emotions — Direction of Influence

- **URL**: https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-94-015-9082-2_12 ("Plotinus on the Emotions"); https://iep.utm.edu/plotinus/
- **Type**: Specialist secondary literature
- **Key points**:
  - Plotinus argues for *apatheia* but does not identify it with the absence of emotional faculty (contra a strong Stoic reading); higher Neoplatonic spheres render earthly emotions "useless" rather than abolish the emotional part
  - Plotinus engages Stoic cognitive theory of emotion to *critique* and Platonise it, not to inherit it
  - The Plotinian psychology of passion is in many places *upstream-receiving* from Stoicism rather than a separate source
- **Significance for the audit**: Reinforces the conclusion that the Stoic → Plotinus direction holds, so the Stoic architectural specifics (especially *propatheia*) cannot be discounted as Neoplatonist common-cause. The cross-tradition article's score for the Stoic component does not need revision on Neoplatonist grounds.

### Porphyry's *Sententiae* and the *On the Faculties of the Soul*

- **URLs**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/porphyry/ ; https://www.tertullian.org/fathers/porphyry_psychological_fragment_2_text.htm
- **Type**: SEP entry + primary-source psychological fragment
- **Key points**:
  - Porphyry's *Sententiae* is the only extant work confidently attributable to him laying out his basic philosophical views; on soul-doctrine "Porphyry seems in all essentials to follow Plotinus"
  - Porphyry distinguishes the rational soul (the soul in itself) from a lower soul *in relation to the body* — responsible for sense-perception, desires, emotions, biological functions
  - Porphyry's psychological fragments engage faculties-of-the-soul taxonomy — including a perceptive faculty divided into sensibility (with organs) and intelligence (without organs) — debating Stoic and Aristotelian-Peripatetic positions
- **Significance for the audit**: Porphyry's transmission of Plotinian psychology to the wider Mediterranean intellectual world (he is *the* editor of the *Enneads*) is the immediate channel through which Neoplatonic faculty-psychology reaches late antiquity. Porphyry's fragment-2 *On the Faculties* shows the inner-faculty taxonomy is already systematised by the late 3rd / early 4th century CE — well before Muḥāsibī.

### Iamblichus and Proclus: Late Neoplatonist Psychology

- **URLs**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/iamblichus/ ; https://www.mdpi.com/2409-9287/9/4/100
- **Type**: SEP and specialist literature
- **Key points**:
  - Iamblichus (c. 242 – c. 325) departs from Plotinus on a key architectural point: there is **no superior part of the soul** that remains in intelligible being — the whole soul descends, and ascent requires *theurgy* not just contemplation
  - Iamblichus, Proclus and the post-Iamblichean school develop a more elaborate inner-faculty + correspondence taxonomy (e.g. seven planetary spheres correlating with soul-faculties)
  - Proclus systematises this into his hierarchy of being and soul; the inner-faculty doctrine becomes the standard Neoplatonist psychology that feeds the Christian (pseudo-Dionysian) and Arabic philosophical traditions
- **Significance for the audit**: The post-Plotinian Neoplatonist tradition (Porphyry → Iamblichus → Proclus → pseudo-Dionysius and the Arabic Plotinus) systematises *inner-faculty psychology* in a way that is the proximate ancestor of: (a) Christian monastic *logismoi* / discernment doctrine (Evagrius is a Christian-Neoplatonist hybrid); (b) Arabic Plotinus → al-Kindī → Avicenna's inner senses doctrine; (c) Suhrawardī's illuminationist faculty-psychology. The Neoplatonist common-cause channel is *broadest* at this systematisation stage.

### The Arabic Plotinus / *Theology of Aristotle* (al-Kindī Circle, 9th c.)

- **URLs**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/theology-aristotle/ ; https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/al-kindi/ ; https://www.amazon.com/Arabic-Plotinus-Philosophical-Theology-Aristotle/dp/1463207182 (Adamson 2002, *The Arabic Plotinus*)
- **Type**: SEP, monograph
- **Key points**:
  - The *Theology of Aristotle* is a 9th-century Arabic paraphrase of Plotinus's *Enneads* IV–VI produced in the circle of al-Kindī (c. 800–870 CE)
  - Erroneously transmitted under Aristotle's name — became "the single most important conduit by which Neoplatonism reached the Islamic world"
  - Adamson 2002: the *Theology* is not a literal translation but a "creative paraphrase" with paragraphs of new material connecting Plotinian metaphysics to Islamic theology
  - Al-Kindī's *Discourse on the Soul* presents a Neoplatonised soul as a simple substance — the inner-faculty psychology integrated with the *Theology* framework
  - In the 10th century Ismāʿīlī authors (al-Sijistānī, al-Kirmānī) adapt Plotinus's hierarchy for their metaphysics
- **Significance for the audit**: **This is the load-bearing finding for the Sufi channel.** The Plotinian inner-faculty framework reached the Baghdad intellectual milieu by the **mid-9th century — exactly the period in which Muḥāsibī (d. 857 CE) and the first generation of Baghdad Sufis (al-Kharrāz, al-Nūrī, al-Junayd, Ruwaym, Khayr al-Nassāj) were active**. The temporal proximity is striking. Muḥāsibī is contemporary with al-Kindī (al-Kindī d. 870; Muḥāsibī d. 857; they overlap by ~50 years in Baghdad). A direct philosophical-transmission channel from the *Theology of Aristotle* to Muḥāsibī's *muḥāsaba* programme is at least *available* if not documented.

### Sufi Origins and Multi-Stream Influence

- **URLs**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_Sufism ; https://www.researchgate.net/publication/346925673_Sufism_and_Christian_Mysticism_The_Neoplatonic_Factor_Routledge ; https://www.patheos.com/library/sufism/origins/influences
- **Type**: General scholarly + recent specialist
- **Key points**:
  - The first generation of Baghdad Sufis (9th century second half) emerged in a context that "drew partial inspiration from Christian monastic traditions encountered through Syriac translations and interactions in the Levant, incorporating elements like celibacy and ritual weeping, **as well as Neoplatonic ideas of the soul's ascent to the divine**"
  - "In its development as a tradition of ascetic, moral, and mystical practice, Sufis encountered analogous traditions including Christian, Neoplatonic, and Persian sources, and integrated external sources like Neoplatonism into a pre-existing Qur'anic paradigm and Prophetic model"
  - The Routledge volume *Sufism and Christian Mysticism: The Neoplatonic Factor* makes the explicit case that the *common* Neoplatonist substrate of Christian monasticism and early Sufism is a major shared cause of their parallel mystical psychologies
- **Significance for the audit**: The Sufi tradition's formation occurred in a Baghdad intellectual environment with at least three independent Neoplatonist-substrate streams: (a) the al-Kindī-circle Arabic Plotinus; (b) the Syriac-Christian monastic ascetic literature (itself Neoplatonist-influenced via Evagrius and pseudo-Dionysius); (c) Persian-Zoroastrian and gnostic streams with Neoplatonist resonances. The *muḥāsaba* programme's emergence is not in isolation from these streams.

### Evagrius Ponticus and the *Logismoi* — The Christian Monastic Channel

- **URLs**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evagrius_Ponticus ; https://catholicgnosis.wordpress.com/2010/03/10/evagrius-ponticus-antirrhesis-and-the-stoic-concept-of-first-movements/ ; https://www.academia.edu/11618568/Eight_Logismoi_in_the_Writings_of_Evagrius_Ponticus
- **Type**: Specialist literature on Christian monastic psychology
- **Key points**:
  - Evagrius (c. 345–399) — Greek-trained, Christian-Neoplatonist hybrid — developed a comprehensive doctrine of eight *logismoi* (incoming thoughts: gluttony, lust, avarice, sadness, anger, accidie, vainglory, pride)
  - The *logismoi* are *incoming thoughts whose source is to be discerned* — typically attributed to demons, the body, the self, or God; the monk's task is *diakrisis* (discernment) of the source
  - This is a **direct structural parallel to the Sufi *khawāṭir* taxonomy** (divine / angelic / egoic / satanic sources)
  - Evagrius engages explicitly with the Stoic concept of *first movements* (Sorabji and others have documented this); the Christian monastic tradition inherits *propatheia* and develops *diakrisis* into a discernment-of-thought-source discipline
  - The Christian *Philokalia* tradition (4th–14th centuries) systematises this doctrine and transmits it through Syriac translations into the Eastern Christian milieu in which 9th-century Baghdad Sufism formed
- **Significance for the audit**: **This is the most consequential finding of the audit.** The *khawāṭir* taxonomy's surface-level Sufi originality is undermined by the prior existence of a closely parallel Christian monastic doctrine (Evagrius, 4th c. — *400 years before Muḥāsibī*) that itself emerged from a Stoic-Neoplatonist hybrid base. The chronological order is: Stoic *propatheia* (1st c. CE) → Christian-Neoplatonist *logismoi* + *diakrisis* (4th c.) → transmission through Syriac and Greek monastic literature (5th–9th c.) → available in the Levantine context where 9th-c. Baghdad Sufism forms → Sufi *khawāṭir* taxonomy and *muḥāsaba* practice. **The cross-tradition article's assignment of "cleanest historical-isolation defence" to early Sufi practice requires revision.** The *khawāṭir* / *muḥāsaba* tradition has a documented Neoplatonist-via-Christian-monastic ancestor.

### al-Ghazālī's Neoplatonist Inheritance

- **URLs**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/al-ghazali/ ; https://iep.utm.edu/al-ghazali/ ; https://www.muslimphilosophy.com/ip/rep/H003.htm
- **Type**: SEP, IEP, specialist literature
- **Key points**:
  - Al-Ghazālī's intellectual formation included *direct critical engagement* with Arabic Neoplatonism of al-Fārābī and Avicenna
  - His *Tahāfut al-Falāsifa* (*Incoherence of the Philosophers*) critiques specific Avicennan Neoplatonic doctrines — triadic emanation, God's knowledge of universals, immaterial soul
  - Yet his *Iḥyāʾ* psychology of the heart, inner faculties, and *kashf* (inner unveiling / intuition) is **heavily indebted to the Avicennan-Neoplatonist faculty psychology** even where Ghazālī rejects its metaphysical conclusions
  - The "higher faculty: intuition or inner vision (*kashf*)" doctrine is recognisably Plotinian in shape
- **Significance for the audit**: The 11th-century Ghazālī material (hidden *riyāʾ*, *ghurūr*) that the cross-tradition article rates as "moderate despite-commitments" sits on top of a *fully Neoplatonist-influenced* faculty psychology framework. The *architectural shape* of Ghazālī's inner-life psychology — heart, inner faculties, *nafs* hierarchy, *kashf* — is Avicennan-Neoplatonist. The *specific finding* (hidden *riyāʾ* as structural feature) is the part the cross-tradition audit holds as despite-commitments; the *architectural framing* in which the finding is expressed is downstream of Plotinus → al-Kindī → al-Fārābī → Avicenna → Ghazālī. The audit's positive score for the *finding* is defensible; the framing in which it is communicated is Neoplatonist-mediated.

### Avicenna's Internal Senses Doctrine

- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/ibn-sina/ ; https://iep.utm.edu/avicenna-ibn-sina/ ; https://www.iranicaonline.org/articles/avicenna-vi/
- **Type**: SEP, IEP, Encyclopaedia Iranica
- **Key points**:
  - Avicenna systematises a five-fold theory of *internal senses*: common sense (*ḥiss mushtarak*), imagination (*khayāl*), imaginative faculty (*mutakhayyila*), estimation (*wahm*), memory
  - The estimative faculty (*wahm*) is Avicenna's innovation — not found in earlier Greek, Christian, or Muslim sources
  - The framework combines Aristotelian *De anima* with Neoplatonist faculty-psychology
  - Becomes the standard inner-faculty model in subsequent Sufi, Suhrawardian, and (via Latin translation) Scholastic psychology
- **Significance for the audit**: From the 11th century onwards, *all* Sufi inner-faculty psychology is downstream of Avicennan-Neoplatonist faculty theory. This includes Ghazālī, Ibn ʿArabī, and Suhrawardī. The cross-tradition article's reliance on early Sufi sources (Muḥāsibī especially) is defensible because Muḥāsibī predates Avicenna by 150 years; the article's reliance on Ghazālī and Ibn ʿArabī as further Sufi data-points is *more* contaminated by Neoplatonist common-cause than the article currently acknowledges.

### Suhrawardī's Illuminationism — Late Mediation Channel

- **URLs**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/suhrawardi/ ; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Illuminationism
- **Type**: SEP, encyclopaedic
- **Key points**:
  - Shihāb al-Dīn Yaḥyā Suhrawardī (d. 1191): philosophy *explicitly* Neoplatonist + Zoroastrian + Shīʿa Islamic
  - "Pillars of wisdom": Plato, Empedocles, Pythagoras, Zoroaster, Muhammad — i.e. a self-identified Neoplatonist + ancient-wisdom synthesis
  - Inner-faculty psychology of *ishrāq* (illumination): direct experiential knowledge as the apprehension of the intrinsic light-nature of entities
  - The *Avicennan-Suhrawardian* tradition (al-Kindī → al-Fārābī → Avicenna → Suhrawardī) is the cumulative Neoplatonist-Islamic philosophy that subsequently influences Persian and Mughal-period Sufism
- **Significance for the audit**: Suhrawardī is the channel through which a *fully systematised Neoplatonist inner-faculty psychology* enters the Persian and (via Mughal contact) Indian Islamic intellectual world. The cross-tradition article's identification of "Ibn ʿArabī as the late-synthesiser most heavily Neoplatonic-influenced" is correct as far as it goes; **Suhrawardī is at least as Neoplatonist** and the Suhrawardian-Avicennan school is the dominant Persian inner-faculty framework that mediates between Plotinus and the Vedānta-encountering Mughal Sufis (Dārā Shikoh and his circle).

### Plotinus-Indian Influence Hypothesis — Bréhier vs Armstrong

- **URLs**: https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1057/9780230240773_7 (Lacrosse, "Plotinus, Porphyry, and India: A Re-Examination", in *Late Antique Epistemology*, ed. Vassilopoulou & Clark, 2009); https://sunypress.edu/Books/N/Neoplatonism-and-Indian-Philosophy2 (Gregorios ed., *Neoplatonism and Indian Philosophy*); https://www.etd.ceu.edu/2019/aklan_anna.pdf (Aklan, *Wandering Lotuses*, CEU PhD)
- **Type**: Specialist literature
- **Key points**:
  - Émile Bréhier 1928: alleged Indian (Upanishadic) influences on Plotinus's life and works
  - A. H. Armstrong 1936: critical reply; most subsequent Neoplatonic scholars side with Armstrong — early Neoplatonism is an original development of Greek philosophy
  - Paul Hacker: "I do not think that such dependence is proven in any way, though I would not deny the possibility that Plotinus may have had a vague and dim knowledge of one or two Vedantic doctrines"
  - Aklan 2018 (CEU PhD dissertation, "Wandering Lotuses"): detailed textual and contextual comparison of Porphyry-Śaṅkara parallels concludes the surface similarities "do not involve structural and conceptual connection"; parallels are mostly "metaphorical ways of expression"
- **Significance for the audit**: **The Advaita-via-Neoplatonism channel is closed by scholarly consensus.** No upstream Neoplatonist contamination of Shankara's 8th-century *adhyāsa* / *antaḥkaraṇa* / *vāsanā* doctrines via Plotinus-influenced channels can be demonstrated. The Advaita architectural-specifics findings the cross-tradition article holds at moderate despite-commitments are **unaffected** by the Neoplatonist common-cause channel.

### Mughal Mediation: Bīrūnī, Dārā Shikoh

- **URLs**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dara_Shikoh ; https://madrascourier.com/insight/why-dara-shikoh-aurangzebs-brother-translated-the-upanishads-into-persian/ ; http://www.columbia.edu/itc/mealac/pollock/sks/papers/Ganeri(migratingtexts).pdf
- **Type**: Specialist literature
- **Key points**:
  - Al-Bīrūnī (c. 973–1048): translated parts of the *Yoga Sūtras* and engaged with Indian philosophy, but is post-Shankara and intellectually downstream of Shankara
  - Dārā Shikoh (1615–1659): Persian translation of 52 Upanishads (*Sirr-i-Akbar*) completed 1657; explicitly argues for the harmony of Sufi and Vedāntic philosophy in *Majmaʿ al-Baḥrayn* (*The Confluence of the Two Seas*)
  - Ganeri ("Migrating Texts and Traditions"): documents that Dārā Shikoh's Persian translation runs *from Sanskrit Vedānta into the Sufi-Neoplatonist vocabulary*, deliberately rephrasing Indian philosophical terms in Sufi parallels
  - Dārā Shikoh's translation reached the West via Bernier (1671), Anquetil-Duperron (Latin *Oupnek'hat* 1801), and influenced Schopenhauer
- **Significance for the audit**: **The direction of influence is Vedānta → Sufi-Neoplatonist Persian, not the other way.** The Mughal mediation channel cannot supply Neoplatonist contamination of the 8th-century Shankara *adhyāsa* doctrine; it post-dates Shankara by ~850 years and runs in the wrong direction. The Mughal mediation is relevant to the *Western* reception of Advaita (Schopenhauer, the 19th-c. Indology that Western analytic engagement with Advaita is partly downstream of), not to the *Advaitic-tradition-internal* doctrine the cross-tradition article cites as evidence.

### Stoic-Christian-Sufi Chain via Evagrius — Cumulative Picture

- **URLs**: https://catholicgnosis.wordpress.com/2010/03/10/evagrius-ponticus-antirrhesis-and-the-stoic-concept-of-first-movements/ ; https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/medieval-emotions/
- **Type**: Specialist literature
- **Key points**:
  - Evagrius Ponticus engages the Stoic *first movements* (propatheia) doctrine directly — "*antirrhēsis*" is his term for the practice of refuting evil thoughts before they receive assent, deliberately preserving the Stoic structure with a Christian metaphysical overlay
  - SEP *Medieval Theories of the Emotions*: documents the Christian-monastic inheritance of Stoic emotion theory via Augustine, Evagrius, John Cassian, John Climacus, etc.
  - The *Philokalia* and Eastern Christian monastic literature systematises this inheritance through the 7th–10th centuries
  - Syriac translation of Greek monastic literature was extensive by the 7th c., and the Levantine monastic tradition was the immediate context in which 9th-c. Baghdad Sufism formed
- **Significance for the audit**: The complete picture is: Stoic *propatheia* (1st c. CE) → Plotinian inner-faculty psychology with Stoic substrate (3rd c.) → Evagrian *logismoi* + *antirrhēsis* + *diakrisis* (4th c.) → Christian monastic tradition transmitted through Syriac (5th–9th c.) → Sufi *muḥāsaba* + *khawāṭir* (9th c.) → Avicennan internal-senses doctrine (11th c.) → Ghazālī's hidden *riyāʾ* (11th c.) → Suhrawardian *ishrāq* (12th c.) → Ibn ʿArabī's *khayāl* (13th c.). The Neoplatonist substrate runs through this chain at multiple points.

## Major Positions

### Position 1: The Neoplatonist Common-Cause Channel is Substantial Across All Three Tradition-Pairs

- **Proponents**: Hypothetical strong-discount reading; would be the position of a strong Churchland-style translation-construction critic (cross-tradition article §"Honest Limitations") who pushed the common-cause case maximally
- **Core claim**: Plotinus's articulation of bounded conscious access to soul-activity, transmitted through Porphyry → Iamblichus → Proclus → Evagrius → al-Kindī → Avicenna → Ghazālī → Suhrawardī → Ibn ʿArabī, is the *single common upstream cause* of the apparent cross-tradition convergence on the introspection-architecture cluster. Stoic, Sufi, *and* Advaitic findings are all downstream Neoplatonist illustrations of one architectural idea
- **Key arguments**: The transmission chain is documented at every node; the al-Kindī circle's *Theology of Aristotle* contemporaneous with Muḥāsibī; the Evagrian *logismoi* as a direct Christian-monastic precursor to Sufi *khawāṭir*; the Avicennan internal senses dominating all post-11th-c. Sufi inner-faculty psychology
- **Verdict**: **Overreached for Stoicism and Advaita.** The Stoic position is *upstream* of Plotinus, not downstream; the Advaitic position is independent of Plotinus on the scholarly consensus (Aklan 2018 + Hacker). The position is **partly correct** for the Sufi component, especially for the late synthesisers (Ghazālī onwards) and *moderately* applicable to Muḥāsibī's practice via the Evagrian-Christian channel.

### Position 2: The Neoplatonist Common-Cause Channel is Negligible

- **Proponents**: Hypothetical no-discount reading; would be the cross-tradition article's position if the Neoplatonist channel were dismissed without audit
- **Core claim**: Each of the three traditions developed its introspection-architecture findings through *independent practice-tradition empiricism* — Muḥāsibī through *muḥāsaba*, Shankara through Advaitic analysis, Seneca/Epictetus through *prosochē* — and the apparent cross-tradition convergence reflects genuine independent observation
- **Key arguments**: Practitioners' empirical observation reports are the proximate cause of the doctrines; the Neoplatonist philosophical substrate provides only the framework vocabulary, not the substantive architectural finding; vocabulary-stripping leaves the architectural finding intact across all three traditions
- **Verdict**: **Underreached for the Sufi component.** The position is correct for Stoicism and Advaita but misses the substantial Neoplatonist mediation through the Christian-monastic and Arabic-Plotinus channels in the case of Sufism. The position holds in modified form (see Position 3).

### Position 3: Differentiated Common-Cause Weight by Tradition (Recommended)

- **Proponents**: This audit's recommended synthesis
- **Core claim**: The Neoplatonist common-cause channel earns *substantially different* weights for the three traditions, and the cross-tradition article should propagate these differentiated weights into its scoring rather than treating "Neoplatonist common-cause" as a single uniform discount

- **Differentiated weights**:
  - **Stoicism: weak (≈ no discount)**. The Stoa pre-dates Plotinus by ~500 years; Plotinus is downstream of the Stoic cognitive theory, not upstream. The Stoic-CBT modern transmission channel that the cross-tradition article already names is a *separate* common-cause issue and does not implicate Neoplatonism. *Propatheia*, *phantasia katalēptikē*, *synkatathesis* are all firmly pre-Plotinian.
  - **Advaita: weak (≈ no discount)**. Bréhier 1928 → Armstrong 1936 → Hacker → Aklan 2018: the alleged Plotinian-Advaita influence direction is *unproven* on detailed textual examination and unlikely to be substantial. The Mughal mediation channel post-dates Shankara by ~850 years and runs in the wrong direction (Vedānta → Sufi-Neoplatonist Persian, not vice versa). The 8th-century Shankara *adhyāsa* + *antaḥkaraṇa* + *vāsanā* architecture is independent of Neoplatonism.
  - **Sufism: moderate-to-substantial (significant discount)**. Three channels run between Neoplatonism and early-to-mid Sufism:
    1. **Christian-monastic via Evagrius and the Syriac tradition** (5th–9th c.): the *logismoi* + *diakrisis* + *antirrhēsis* doctrine structurally prefigures the *khawāṭir* taxonomy + *muḥāsaba* practice. This is the **most consequential** discount the cross-tradition article should apply.
    2. **Direct Arabic Plotinus via the al-Kindī circle** (mid-9th c.): the *Theology of Aristotle* and al-Kindī's *Discourse on the Soul* reach Baghdad in Muḥāsibī's lifetime. Documentary evidence of Muḥāsibī specifically reading the *Theology* is *not available* in the sources retrieved, so this channel is *available* rather than *proven*.
    3. **Avicennan internal senses + Ghazālian and Suhrawardian downstream**: heavily contaminates the 11th-c.+ Sufi material the cross-tradition article cites (Ghazālī hidden *riyāʾ*, Ibn ʿArabī *khayāl*). The Muḥāsibī period is *upstream* of this contamination; the Ghazālī period is *downstream*.

- **Specific score-update recommendations for the cross-tradition channel article**:
  - The article's overall Criterion 3 score (currently "low-moderate, conditional on Neoplatonist audit") should hold at **low-moderate** with the audit's conditional clause discharged
  - But the article should add a Sufi-specific note: **early Sufi practice (Muḥāsibī, *khawāṭir*) does not have the "cleanest historical-isolation defence" the article currently assigns**; the cleanest defence is for Advaita; the early-Sufi material carries a *Christian-monastic-via-Evagrius* common-cause channel that prefigures the *khawāṭir* architectural shape
  - Ghazālī's hidden *riyāʾ* finding's despite-commitments score should be slightly downgraded — the architectural framework in which the finding is expressed is Avicennan-Neoplatonist; the *specific finding* survives, but the *framing* is downstream
  - The Stoic *propatheia* finding's score is **unchanged** by this audit
  - The Advaitic architectural-specifics findings' scores are **unchanged** by this audit
  - The overall cross-tradition channel's score is **unchanged** in magnitude but the *internal weighting* shifts: the load-bearing weight moves toward the Advaita component and away from the Sufi component

- **Relation to site tenets**: Consistent with the [[evidential-status-discipline|evidential-status discipline]] — the audit names what the available channels are and propagates uncertainty into the score rather than dismissing the issue or over-weighting it

## Key Debates

### Debate 1: Did Plotinus's Concept of Bounded Conscious Access Anticipate the Cross-Tradition Cluster's Architectural Finding?

- **Sides**: Yes (Schwyzer 1960, Hendrix, Remes — Plotinus is "the first philosopher of the unconscious") vs No (the Plotinian *unnoticed-intellect* claim is framework-level prediction within a particular metaphysics, not an architectural-specifics finding)
- **Core disagreement**: Whether "soul has activity above the threshold of conscious apprehension" (Plotinus V.1.12; IV.3.30) is the *same* finding as the cross-tradition cluster's *source-attribution non-delivery*, *confabulatory generation*, *narrative construction*, *noetic-feelings opacity*
- **Current state**: Schwyzer / Hendrix readings give Plotinus credit for the *general* architectural finding (bounded introspective access); the four-void cluster's *specific* architectural findings (Sufi *khawāṭir* on source attribution; Stoic *propatheia* on sub-assent affective machinery; Advaitic *vāsanā* on latent dispositional contamination) are *more specific* than Plotinus's claim
- **Significance**: The Neoplatonist common-cause channel feeds *framework-level* claims of bounded introspective access into the downstream traditions, but does *not* feed the *architectural-specifics-level* findings the cross-tradition article holds at moderate despite-commitments. The discount should be applied at the framework-level convergence, not at the architectural-specifics convergence.

### Debate 2: Is Muḥāsibī's *Muḥāsaba* Programme Indebted to Christian Monasticism via the Syriac Tradition?

- **Sides**: Yes (the Patheos/Routledge/Wikipedia general consensus that early Baghdad Sufism formed in a Levantine Christian-monastic-influenced context) vs Partial (the dominant scholarly position: Sufism integrates Christian and Neoplatonist influences into a *pre-existing Qur'anic paradigm and Prophetic model*)
- **Core disagreement**: Whether the *structure* of Muḥāsibī's self-examination programme is a Christian-monastic import or a development internal to the Islamic ascetic tradition with Christian-monastic-vocabulary borrowings
- **Current state**: The general consensus accepts substantial Christian-monastic influence on early Sufi *practice*; the question is whether the *specific* architectural shape of *muḥāsaba* and *khawāṭir* is most economically explained as a development from the Evagrian *logismoi* + *diakrisis* tradition, or as an independent development with parallel features
- **Significance**: The audit's recommendation is to treat the Christian-monastic-via-Evagrius channel as **substantial common-cause** for the architectural shape of *khawāṭir* + *muḥāsaba* without claiming the substantive *findings* (hidden *riyāʾ* specifically) are inherited; the *frame* is Neoplatonist-via-Christian-monastic, the *Sufi-specific empirical findings within that frame* survive.

### Debate 3: Does the Bréhier-Armstrong Settlement Hold After Aklan 2018?

- **Sides**: Armstrong-side consensus (early Neoplatonism is a Greek-philosophical original; Plotinian-Advaita parallels are "metaphorical ways of expression" not "structural and conceptual connection" — Aklan 2018 confirms) vs revisionist re-opening (Gregorios ed. *Neoplatonism and Indian Philosophy* anthologises continued comparative interest)
- **Core disagreement**: Whether the consensus rejection of Bréhier's strong-influence thesis is final, or whether Indo-Hellenistic philosophical contact (well-documented for Pyrrho and pre-Pyrrhonian channels) supplies enough common-cause to render the Plotinian-Advaita architectural parallels non-independent
- **Current state**: Specialist Neoplatonic Studies overwhelmingly side with Armstrong; comparative-philosophy literature is more open but does not produce evidence sufficient to overturn the specialist consensus
- **Significance**: The cross-tradition article should treat Advaita as *independent of Neoplatonism* per the specialist consensus, while noting that the Pyrrho-Buddhism contact hypothesis (already named in the article) is a separate common-cause channel running through *Buddhism* and not through *Neoplatonism*. The two common-cause channels are not the same and should not be conflated.

## Gaps in Research

- **Direct documentary evidence of Muḥāsibī's engagement with the al-Kindī circle's Arabic Plotinus material**: The retrieved sources document temporal and geographical proximity (Baghdad, mid-9th c.) but do *not* document specific textual borrowings. A focused archival search in Adamson 2002 *The Arabic Plotinus* and in the Muḥāsibī specialist literature (Smith 1935, Stone 1991) would be needed to firm up the channel from "available" to "documented."
- **The specific Syriac transmission of Evagrian *logismoi* doctrine into the Baghdad Sufi milieu**: The general claim that the Christian monastic tradition influenced early Sufism is well-documented; the *specific* claim that the Evagrian *logismoi* + *diakrisis* doctrine specifically prefigured the *khawāṭir* taxonomy needs direct primary-source comparison. Pourjavady and Gulick's *The Sufi Path of Knowledge* would be a starting point.
- **Whether Schwyzer 1960's reading of Plotinian "unnoticed intellectual activity" is the dominant or a contested reading in current Plotinian Studies**: Hendrix follows Schwyzer; the Remes / Emilsson tradition is more cautious about reading modern "unconscious" categories into the Enneads. The strength of the *framework-level* Neoplatonist common-cause depends on whether Plotinus genuinely articulated something like "bounded conscious access" or whether modern readers (Schwyzer, Hendrix) project the category. A detailed read of Emilsson 2007 *Plotinus on Intellect* and Remes 2008 *Plotinus on Self* would refine this.
- **Whether the Christian-monastic-via-Evagrius channel itself ultimately depends on Stoicism (criterion (1)-failure)**: If Evagrian *logismoi* is itself a Christianised Stoic doctrine, the apparent Christian-monastic common-cause for Sufism may *also* be partial Stoic common-cause — and the Stoic-CBT modern channel the cross-tradition article already names. The combined effect could be: Stoicism is the single upstream source for *both* the Christian-Sufi chain *and* the modern cognitive-psychology chain; the Stoic *propatheia* finding is then *more central* to the cluster's architecture than the article currently treats. This is the most significant gap and the most consequential potential revision.
- **Whether Suhrawardian illuminationist psychology actually mediated between Plotinus and Mughal Sufi engagement with Vedānta in a way that *would* introduce backward-direction Neoplatonist contamination of late Advaita transmission**: I retrieved no evidence that Suhrawardian → Dārā Shikoh-period Sufi engagement introduced Neoplatonist categories *into* the Advaita reading the Map cites. The direction appears to be Vedānta → Sufi (Dārā Shikoh's translation runs that direction). But the reverse-direction question is worth checking with Ganeri 2011 or Ganeri (Pollock) "Migrating Texts and Traditions" in detail.
- **Quantitative timing**: Muḥāsibī's *muḥāsaba* programme and the *Theology of Aristotle*'s production both occur in Baghdad in the mid-9th century — the temporal precision needed to determine whether Muḥāsibī's programme is *upstream*, *contemporaneous-and-independent*, or *downstream* of the *Theology* is not in the sources retrieved. Adamson and Cooperson on the al-Kindī circle dating would refine this.
