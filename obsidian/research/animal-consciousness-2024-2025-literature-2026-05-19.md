## Key Sources

### Andrews, Birch & Sebo (2025) — "Evaluating Animal Consciousness"
- **URL**: https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.adp4990
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed paper, *Science* 387, 822–824 (Feb 2025)
- **Key points**:
  - Specifies a *marker method* — identify a dimension of consciousness (pain, perception, valence), then look for behavioural and anatomical features that correlate with that dimension in humans
  - Each marker is graded by context-dependent confidence; markers are *not* individually conclusive
  - Linguistic behaviour is a marker in humans but explicitly *not* in LLMs, which is the canonical example the paper uses to keep marker-based reasoning from over-extending to AI
  - This is the operationalised follow-up to the 2024 NY Declaration — the declaration named the phenomenon, this paper specifies the methodology
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral on metaphysics. Compatible with Dualism — markers identify correlates, not constitution. Strongly aligns with **Occam's Razor Has Limits** (Tenet 5) by treating each marker as context-dependent rather than definitive.
- **Quote**: "Markers are observable features of organisms that are thought to correlate with consciousness... The degree to which a particular marker can increase or decrease confidence in particular dimensions of animal consciousness depends on context."
- **Why missing matters**: The article cites the NY Declaration (2024) but not the 2025 methodology paper that specifies how the declaration's "realistic possibility" language gets applied case-by-case. Without the marker method, the article's appeal to convergent evidence is harder to defend against the agnostic position.

### Chittka, Wilson, Solvi et al. (2025) — "The Exploration of Consciousness in Insects"
- **URL**: https://royalsocietypublishing.org/rstb/article/380/1939/rstb.2024.0302/235190 ; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12612707/
- **Type**: *Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society B* 380(1939), special-issue article, 2025
- **Key points**:
  - Documents specific 2024–2025 evidence the existing article gestures at but does not list: bumblebee object-engagement play patterns (young engage more than older), fruit-fly play, predator-attack persistent affect lasting days
  - Frames insects as *especially useful* organisms for consciousness research because their cognition has been studied for over a century and modern tools (calcium imaging, optogenetics) are revealing brain detail
  - Explicitly engages the agnostic critique by specifying which inferences the evidence supports and which it does not
- **Tenet alignment**: Compatible with Dualism — no substrate-specific barrier on consciousness. Compatible with **Occam's Razor Has Limits** (Tenet 5) by refusing the simpler "insects are just reflex machines" verdict where the evidence does not warrant it.
- **Quote**: "Young bees engaged more frequently with objects than older ones in play-like behavior, similar to vertebrate play, and such behavior has also been discovered in fruit flies."
- **Why missing matters**: The current article cites the 2022 *Mind of a Bee* and refers to "Chittka 2025" in passing but does not engage the 2025 paper's specific evidence catalogue. The literature-drift audit's worked-example pattern is exactly this: the surface citation is present but the 2025 evidence is not.

### Wandrey & Halina (2025) — "The Evolution of Animal Consciousness"
- **URL**: https://compass.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1111/phc3.70069 ; https://compass.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/pdfdirect/10.1111/phc3.70069
- **Type**: *Philosophy Compass* 2025 review article (open access)
- **Key points**:
  - Maps the early-evolution vs. latecomer debate explicitly: early-evolution traces consciousness to the Cambrian (~540 mya); latecomer theories restrict to mammals or humans
  - Argues the divergence stems from unresolved debates about *how to identify consciousness in extant animals* — the identification methodology determines the evolutionary reconstruction
  - This is the canonical 2025 philosophy-of-science framing for the methodological circularity the existing article only gestures at via Gutfreund's agnostic challenge
- **Tenet alignment**: Compatible with Dualism — does not commit to physicalist substrate. Strongly compatible with **Occam's Razor Has Limits** (Tenet 5) — explicit that simpler verdicts are unreliable given identification-methodology underdetermination.
- **Quote**: "Which behavioral and neural properties we regard as evidence of consciousness directly impacts how we reconstruct its evolutionary history."
- **Why missing matters**: The article currently cites Ginsburg & Jablonka's UAL framework and Godfrey-Smith's NDS but does not engage the 2025 *Philosophy Compass* review that maps both into a single methodological landscape. This is the most directly tenet-coherent missing citation.

### COGITATE Consortium (2025) — Adversarial Testing of GNWT and IIT
- **URLs**:
  - *Nature* 2025 publication: COGITATE consortium paper directly comparing IIT and GNWT predictions (n=256 participants, fMRI/MEG/iEEG)
  - https://academic.oup.com/nc/article/2025/1/niaf037/8280147 (*Neuroscience of Consciousness* 2025 GNW framework response)
  - https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12137136/ (full adversarial-testing PMC record)
- **Type**: International theory-neutral adversarial collaboration, multi-paper output, 2025
- **Key points**:
  - Pre-registered adversarial collaboration: theory proponents and consortium co-developed predictions and outcomes
  - Results *challenged core assumptions of both* IIT and GNWT — neither theory was vindicated
  - Direct implication for animal-consciousness inference: any species-attribution that routes through "this animal has a global workspace, therefore conscious" or "this animal has high Φ, therefore conscious" inherits the weakness the adversarial test exposed
- **Tenet alignment**: Compatible with Dualism — the inconclusive result is what the Dualism tenet predicts (no neural pattern *constitutes* consciousness, only *correlates with* it). Strongly compatible with **Occam's Razor Has Limits** (Tenet 5) — the major theories of consciousness do not converge even on humans.
- **Quote** (from PMC summary): "Human participants (n = 256) viewed suprathreshold stimuli for variable durations while neural activity was measured with functional magnetic resonance imaging, magnetoencephalography and intracranial electroencephalography... results challenge core assumptions of both models."
- **Why missing matters**: The current article treats higher-order theory (HOT) but does not engage that the *other two* leading theories now have weakened empirical support. Any reader asking "why doesn't the Map's animal-consciousness verdict follow from IIT or GNWT?" needs the 2025 adversarial-test outcome as part of the answer.

- **Why missing matters**: The current article cites the 2024 *Edge of Sentience* book and references the five-tier scale ([[birch-edge-of-sentience-and-the-five-tier-scale]]) but does not engage the 2025 précis or the April 2025 refinement paper. ## Major Positions (2024–2025 Updates)

### Marker Method (Andrews, Birch, Sebo 2025)
- **Proponents**: Kristin Andrews, Jonathan Birch, Jeff Sebo
- **Core claim**: Consciousness in non-humans is assessed by identifying behavioural/anatomical markers that correlate with consciousness in humans, then looking for analogues — but each marker is context-dependent and not individually conclusive
- **Key arguments**:
  - Linguistic behaviour is a marker in humans but the LLM example shows markers can fail to extend across substrate boundaries
  - Multiple markers across multiple dimensions provide convergent (but defeasible) evidence
  - This is the operational answer to "what method does the NY Declaration's 'realistic possibility' language refer to?"
- **Relation to site tenets**: Compatible with all five tenets. Particularly aligned with **Occam's Razor Has Limits** — refuses the simpler "marker present, therefore conscious" inference. The existing article's [[#the-standing-agnostic-challenge|Standing Agnostic Challenge]] from Gutfreund (2024) and the marker method are sister positions: both acknowledge that science can identify candidates but not constitute consciousness.

### Adversarial-Collaboration Outcome (COGITATE 2025)
- **Proponents**: Cogitate Consortium (theory-neutral, includes IIT and GNWT proponents)
- **Core claim**: Both IIT and GNWT have weakened empirical support after pre-registered head-to-head testing
- **Key arguments**:
  - Pre-registration eliminated post-hoc theory-protection
  - Multimodal neural measurement (fMRI + MEG + iEEG) provided convergent data
  - Neither theory's core predictions were robustly confirmed
- **Relation to site tenets**: Strongly aligned with **Occam's Razor Has Limits** — the two leading reductive theories of consciousness do not converge even on humans, which is exactly the pattern Tenet 5 predicts when the underlying phenomenon is more than the available frameworks can capture. Compatible with **Dualism** — the inconclusive result is what dualism predicts about reductive theories.

### Evolutionary-Methodology Mapping (Wandrey & Halina 2025)
- **Proponents**: Mona-Marie Wandrey, Marta Halina
- **Core claim**: The early-evolution vs. latecomer debate is downstream of the identification-methodology debate — fix the identification method and the evolutionary reconstruction follows
- **Key arguments**:
  - Cambrian-origin theorists use one set of identification markers, latecomer theorists use another
  - The disagreement is methodological before it is empirical
  - Anthropocentric assumptions in identification methods bias evolutionary reconstruction toward latecomer verdicts
- **Relation to site tenets**: Compatible with **Dualism** — the methodological circularity is what Dualism predicts when third-person methods are asked to identify first-person phenomena. Compatible with **Occam's Razor Has Limits** — simpler latecomer verdicts are unreliable when the identification methodology is contested.

### NDS Follow-Up (Godfrey-Smith 2024, with 2025 development)
- **Proponents**: Peter Godfrey-Smith
- **Core claim**: Consciousness arises from large-scale dynamic neural patterns (oscillations across cell membranes), not from specific anatomical structures or computational implementations
- **Key arguments**:
  - Multiple independent origins (vertebrate, cephalopod, arthropod) share dynamic-pattern features but not anatomical features
  - "Studies on animal minds suggest consciousness is not computation" (Godfrey-Smith 2025 IAI TV interview)
  - This positions NDS as a non-computational alternative to both IIT and GNWT
- **Relation to site tenets**: Compatible with **Dualism** — Godfrey-Smith's anti-computationalism is a *limit* on physicalist reduction even within the broadly naturalist framing. Compatible with **Bidirectional Interaction** — large-scale dynamic patterns are the right scale for the kind of consciousness-matter coupling the Map's framework requires.

## Key Debates (2024–2025 State)

### Early-Origin vs. Latecomer Evolution of Consciousness
- **Sides**:
  - Early-origin (Feinberg & Mallatt, Ginsburg & Jablonka, Godfrey-Smith): consciousness emerged in the Cambrian (~540 mya), present in all vertebrates and many invertebrates
  - Latecomer (some HOT theorists, some functionalists): consciousness restricted to mammals or humans
- **Core disagreement**: Which markers / behaviours indicate consciousness, and which are functionally explicable without it
- **Current state (2025)**: Wandrey & Halina (2025) argue this debate is downstream of identification methodology; cannot be resolved without first resolving how consciousness is identified

### Adversarial Collaboration as Methodology
- **Sides**:
  - Pro-adversarial-collaboration (Koch, Tononi, Dehaene, COGITATE consortium): pre-registered head-to-head testing is how to break the impasse
  - Skeptical (some theory proponents, post-hoc): adversarial design does not catch everything theories predict
- **Core disagreement**: Whether the COGITATE 2025 results genuinely weaken IIT and GNWT or reveal limits of the experimental paradigm
- **Current state (2025)**: Results published, both theories have responded, the methodological precedent is established

### Sentience Candidate vs. Realistic Possibility
- **Sides**:
  - Three-tier (Birch 2025 précis): candidate / investigation priority / neither — operationalises precaution
  - Two-tier (NY Declaration 2024): strong support / realistic possibility — simpler but less actionable
- **Core disagreement**: Whether the three-tier reframe adds operational value or just renames the two-tier categories
- **Current state (2025)**: Three-tier becoming dominant in welfare policy contexts; two-tier still common in scientific declarations

### Insect Consciousness: The Specific-Evidence Question
- **Sides**:
  - Affirmative (Chittka et al. 2025): bumblebee play patterns, persistent affect after predator attack, fruit-fly play meet enough markers
  - Skeptical (subset of behavioural-ecology community): functional explanations cover the data without consciousness
- **Core disagreement**: Whether the 2024–2025 specific behaviours pass the bar that pre-2020 behaviours did not
- **Current state (2025)**: Chittka 2025 is the strongest pro-affirmative paper; the agnostic position (Gutfreund 2024) remains the principled hold-out
