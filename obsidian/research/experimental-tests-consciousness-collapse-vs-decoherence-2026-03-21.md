# Research: Experimental Tests Distinguishing Consciousness-Collapse from Decoherence-Only Interpretations

**Date**: 2026-03-21
**Search queries used**: "experimental tests consciousness collapse quantum mechanics vs decoherence", "Wigner friend experiment consciousness observer collapse testable predictions", "quantum measurement problem decoherence vs collapse experimental distinction", "consciousness causes collapse falsifiable Penrose Stapp quantum mind experiment", "Chalmers McQueen consciousness collapse wave function 2021 paper testable", "Diósi-Penrose model experimental test spontaneous collapse gravity 2024 2025", "extended Wigner friend scenario Frauchiger Renner Brukner experiment 2024 2025", "GRW CSL spontaneous collapse model experimental bounds macro superposition", "arxiv 2512.02838 experimental blueprint distinguishing decoherence objective collapse", "Gran Sasso underground experiment test Penrose Orch OR spontaneous radiation Curceanu 2022"

## Executive Summary

The question of whether consciousness plays a role in quantum measurement — or whether decoherence alone explains the appearance of classical outcomes — is approaching experimental tractability, though a definitive test remains elusive. Objective collapse models (GRW, CSL, Diósi-Penrose) make quantitatively different predictions from standard quantum mechanics, and several experiments have constrained their parameter spaces. The Gran Sasso underground experiments (2022) severely constrained the Diósi-Penrose model underlying Orch OR. Chalmers and McQueen (2021) showed that consciousness-collapse interpretations can be made rigorous by combining integrated information theory with CSL dynamics, producing testable predictions via quantum computers. A December 2025 proposal outlines a levitated optomechanical platform capable of distinguishing objective collapse signatures (mass-quadratic decoherence saturation) from environmental decoherence. However, the core difficulty persists: consciousness-collapse and decoherence-only interpretations often make identical predictions for currently accessible experiments, and distinguishing them requires probing superpositions at mass scales far beyond current capability.

## Key Sources

### Chalmers & McQueen (2021) — "Consciousness and the Collapse of the Wave Function"
- **URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2105.02314
- **Type**: Paper (arXiv preprint / book chapter)
- **Key points**:
  - Revives the von Neumann-Wigner consciousness-collapse interpretation with modern theoretical tools
  - Combines Integrated Information Theory (IIT) as a consciousness metric with Continuous Spontaneous Localization (CSL) as collapse dynamics
  - Simple versions are falsified by the quantum Zeno effect (continuous conscious observation would freeze evolution)
  - More sophisticated versions — where collapse rate scales with integrated information (Φ) — remain empirically viable
  - In principle testable via quantum computer experiments: systems with high Φ should show different collapse behaviour than systems with low Φ but identical physical parameters
- **Tenet alignment**: Strongly aligns with Dualism, Bidirectional Interaction, and Minimal Quantum Interaction tenets. The IIT+CSL framework is precisely the kind of rigorous consciousness-collapse model the Map's tenets call for.
- **Quote**: "The upshot is not that consciousness-collapse interpretations are clearly correct, but that there is a research program here worth exploring."

### Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy — "The Role of Decoherence in Quantum Mechanics"
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/qm-decoherence/
- **Type**: Encyclopedia
- **Key points**:
  - Decoherence explains the suppression of interference effects through environmental entanglement
  - Does not solve the measurement problem: after decoherence, the system is still in a mixture of possible outcomes — decoherence does not explain why one particular outcome is observed
  - The distinction between "proper" and "improper" mixed states (genuine ignorance vs. entanglement-induced apparent mixture) cannot be determined by measuring subsystem observables alone
  - Decoherence is interpretation-neutral — it is a physical process compatible with collapse, many-worlds, and pilot-wave interpretations
- **Tenet alignment**: Directly supports the Map's position. The SEP confirms the Map's claim that decoherence does not solve the measurement problem, leaving room for consciousness at collapse.

### Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy — "Collapse Theories"
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/qm-collapse/
- **Type**: Encyclopedia
- **Key points**:
  - GRW (1986) and CSL (1990) are the main objective collapse models
  - GRW: each particle undergoes spontaneous localization at rate λ ≈ 10⁻¹⁶ s⁻¹, with localization width rC ≈ 10⁻⁷ m
  - For macroscopic objects (N particles), effective collapse rate is Nλ — explaining why macro objects don't superpose
  - CSL replaces discrete GRW jumps with a continuous stochastic process coupled to a classical noise field
  - Both predict anomalous Brownian motion (heating) as a side effect of collapse — this is experimentally testable
  - Adler (2007) proposed higher collapse rate λ ≈ 10⁻⁸ s⁻¹, which is being probed by current experiments
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral on consciousness specifically, but the collapse mechanism could be driven by consciousness rather than being spontaneous. CSL is the dynamical framework Chalmers & McQueen use.

### Horchani (2025) — "Experimental Blueprint for Distinguishing Decoherence from Objective Collapse"
- **URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.02838
- **Type**: Paper (arXiv preprint, December 2025)
- **Key points**:
  - Proposes a levitated optomechanical platform using dielectric nanospheres to create controllable Schrödinger-cat states
  - Develops a unified master equation incorporating gas collisions, black-body radiation, photon-recoil noise, and CSL collapse
  - Identifies two distinctive signatures of objective collapse: (1) decoherence rate saturates with increasing superposition size, (2) decoherence scales quadratically with mass
  - These signatures contrast with environmental decoherence, which scales differently
  - Outlines a Bayesian inference protocol for model discrimination
  - Transforms the test from indirect parameter bounding to quantitative hypothesis comparison
- **Tenet alignment**: Aligns — if this experiment detects excess decoherence beyond environmental noise, it would support collapse as a real physical process, which the Map could interpret as evidence for the consciousness-collapse interface.

### Curceanu et al. (2022) — Gran Sasso Underground Tests
- **URL**: https://physicsworld.com/a/quantum-theory-of-consciousness-put-in-doubt-by-underground-experiment/
- **Type**: Experimental result (published in Physics of Life Reviews)
- **Key points**:
  - Used ultra-pure germanium detector 1.4 km underground at Gran Sasso to eliminate cosmic ray background
  - Searched for spontaneous electromagnetic radiation predicted by Diósi-Penrose collapse models
  - No radiation found beyond residual emissions from the apparatus
  - Concluded that Penrose-Hameroff Orch OR is "highly implausible" under the simplest gravity-related collapse model
  - More complex collapse models retain some viability — the result constrains but does not eliminate all versions
  - Penrose is developing revised models without spontaneous radiation predictions
- **Tenet alignment**: Partially conflicts with the Map's Minimal Quantum Interaction tenet insofar as Orch OR was a candidate mechanism. However, the tenet is neutral between Orch OR and Stapp's Zeno approach — the latter is unaffected by this result. The Map should acknowledge this constraint while noting it does not rule out consciousness-collapse generally.

### Frauchiger-Renner Thought Experiment and Extended Wigner's Friend Scenarios
- **URL**: https://www.nature.com/articles/s42005-021-00589-1
- **Type**: Paper (Nature Communications Physics, 2021) and subsequent work
- **Key points**:
  - Extended Wigner's friend scenario generates contradictions if quantum theory is applied universally and observers have definite experiences
  - A 2021 no-go theorem shows that "the persistent reality of Wigner's friend's perception" is inconsistent with standard quantum mechanics
  - A March 2025 paper argues any interpretation involving subjective collapse fails based on the Frauchiger-Renner argument
  - Brukner, Renner, and Cavalcanti won the Paul Ehrenfest award for deriving new no-go theorems from these scenarios
  - QBism, relational QM, and de Broglie-Bohm theory are argued to avoid the contradiction
- **Tenet alignment**: Complex. These no-go results challenge naive consciousness-collapse views but do not refute all versions. The Map should engage carefully: consciousness-collapse with universal validity of QM produces contradictions, so either QM breaks down at conscious systems (which is what collapse theories propose) or the picture must be refined.
- **Quote**: "Any interpretation of quantum theory involving subjective collapse fails based on the Frauchiger-Renner thought-experiment."

### Macromolecule Interferometry — Current Experimental Bounds
- **URL**: https://www.mdpi.com/1099-4300/25/4/645
- **Type**: Review paper
- **Key points**:
  - Most massive particle placed in verified superposition: ~10⁴ amu (≈10⁻²³ kg), using macromolecule interferometry
  - This bounds CSL collapse rate to λ < 10⁻⁷ s⁻¹ at rC = 10⁻⁷ m
  - Adler's proposed rate (λ ≈ 10⁻⁸ s⁻¹) remains within the experimentally allowed region
  - MAQRO space mission proposed to ESA would test nanospheres up to 10¹¹ amu — well into the regime where collapse models predict observable effects
  - Cold-atom experiments provide complementary bounds via anomalous heating
- **Tenet alignment**: Aligns — the Map should track these mass thresholds as they approach the regime where collapse models diverge from standard QM.

### Quantum Zeno Effect as Consciousness-Collapse Test
- **URL**: https://quantumphysics-consciousness.eu/index.php/en/falsification-of-the-awareness-hypothesis-with-a-quantum-eraser-adaptation/
- **Type**: Blog / analysis
- **Key points**:
  - Naive consciousness-collapse predicts that continuous conscious observation should freeze quantum evolution (Zeno effect)
  - This is not observed — systems evolve normally regardless of whether a conscious observer watches continuously
  - Chalmers & McQueen address this: collapse rate should scale with Φ (integrated information), not with mere observation
  - The delayed-choice quantum eraser shows that what matters is availability of which-path information, not conscious awareness of it
  - However, this only falsifies the simplest "consciousness = observation" model, not more sophisticated versions
- **Tenet alignment**: The Map should use the Chalmers-McQueen response: consciousness at collapse is not about "looking at" a system but about integrated information in the measuring apparatus/brain system.

## Major Positions

### Decoherence-Only (No Collapse)
- **Proponents**: Zurek, Joos, Zeh, Schlosshauer, Wallace (via Everett)
- **Core claim**: Environmental decoherence fully explains why we observe definite outcomes without any collapse mechanism. The wave function never actually collapses — decoherence merely makes interference unobservable for practical purposes.
- **Key arguments**:
  - Decoherence is experimentally confirmed and quantitatively precise
  - No additional mechanism (collapse) is needed if many-worlds or consistent histories is adopted
  - Occam's razor favours not adding a collapse postulate
- **Relation to site tenets**: Conflicts with Bidirectional Interaction and No Many Worlds tenets. If decoherence is the full story, consciousness is epiphenomenal to measurement. The Map's Tenet 5 (Occam's Razor Has Limits) directly addresses the Occam's razor argument: simplicity is not a reliable guide when our knowledge is incomplete.

### Objective Collapse (Non-Conscious)
- **Proponents**: Ghirardi, Rimini, Weber, Pearle, Diósi, Penrose (in some formulations)
- **Core claim**: Wave function collapse is a real physical process triggered by reaching a mass/complexity threshold, not by consciousness. The GRW and CSL models add a stochastic collapse term to the Schrödinger equation.
- **Key arguments**:
  - Makes testable predictions (anomalous heating, spontaneous radiation)
  - Explains quantum-to-classical transition without invoking consciousness
  - Parameter space is being progressively constrained by experiment
- **Relation to site tenets**: Partially compatible. The Map could adopt objective collapse as the physical mechanism while arguing that consciousness determines which outcome is selected — a hybrid position. However, pure objective collapse without consciousness conflicts with the Bidirectional Interaction tenet.

### Consciousness-Collapse (Von Neumann-Wigner tradition)
- **Proponents**: Von Neumann, Wigner (early), Stapp, Chalmers & McQueen, London & Bauer
- **Core claim**: Consciousness (or some quantifiable aspect of it) is the trigger for wave function collapse. Physical systems remain in superposition until a conscious observer becomes entangled with the measurement outcome.
- **Key arguments**:
  - Solves the measurement problem without many worlds
  - Provides a natural role for consciousness in physics
  - Chalmers-McQueen IIT+CSL version is empirically viable and in-principle testable
  - Stapp's Zeno approach uses standard QM without modifications
- **Relation to site tenets**: Directly aligns with all five tenets. This is the Map's natural position. The key vulnerability is the Frauchiger-Renner argument and the extended Wigner's friend no-go theorems, which the Map should address.

### Gravity-Induced Collapse (Penrose-Diósi)
- **Proponents**: Penrose, Diósi, Hameroff (via Orch OR)
- **Core claim**: Gravitational self-energy of superposed mass distributions drives collapse. Consciousness arises from this process (Orch OR) rather than causing it.
- **Key arguments**:
  - Connects quantum mechanics to general relativity
  - Makes specific quantitative predictions about collapse timescales
  - Orch OR links collapse to microtubule dynamics in neurons
- **Relation to site tenets**: Mixed. The causal direction (collapse → consciousness rather than consciousness → collapse) partially conflicts with Bidirectional Interaction. However, if consciousness can influence which microtubule states are prepared, the Map could accommodate this. The Gran Sasso results significantly constrain the simplest versions.

## Key Debates

### Can Decoherence Solve the Measurement Problem?
- **Sides**: Zurek/Schlosshauer (decoherence is sufficient within Everett) vs. Adler/Bassi/Ghirardi (collapse is needed) vs. Chalmers/Stapp (consciousness is needed)
- **Core disagreement**: Whether suppression of interference is the same as producing a definite outcome
- **Current state**: Ongoing. The SEP explicitly states decoherence does not solve the measurement problem. Many-worlds proponents argue this is a pseudo-problem. The debate has intensified with extended Wigner's friend results.

### Are Collapse Models Experimentally Distinguishable from Standard QM?
- **Sides**: Bassi/Carlesso (yes, via anomalous heating and mass-dependent decoherence) vs. skeptics (parameter space is too flexible to falsify)
- **Core disagreement**: Whether collapse model parameters can be pinned down enough to make decisive tests
- **Current state**: Progressive constraint of parameter space. Horchani (2025) proposes a Bayesian framework for model discrimination. Macromolecule interferometry and levitated nanoparticle experiments are approaching Adler's proposed collapse rate.

### Does the Frauchiger-Renner Argument Rule Out Consciousness-Collapse?
- **Sides**: Renner/Brukner (yes, subjective collapse is inconsistent) vs. consciousness-collapse advocates (the argument assumes QM applies universally to conscious observers, which is exactly what consciousness-collapse denies)
- **Core disagreement**: Whether the universality of quantum theory should be assumed when reasoning about conscious observers
- **Current state**: Active. The March 2025 paper strengthens the no-go result, but it rests on assumptions that consciousness-collapse theorists can reject. The Map should engage with this carefully.

### Orch OR: Viable or Falsified?
- **Sides**: Hameroff (Orch OR survives in modified form) vs. Curceanu et al. (simplest versions are implausible) vs. Tegmark (decoherence times are too short)
- **Core disagreement**: Whether quantum coherence can persist in microtubules and whether gravity-related collapse occurs as predicted
- **Current state**: Simplest Diósi-Penrose versions are experimentally constrained. Hameroff argues for alternative collapse mechanisms. Penrose is developing radiation-free variants. The Map should note these constraints honestly.
