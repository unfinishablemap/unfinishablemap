## Executive Summary

The standard AI-risk picture (Bostrom-style orthogonality plus instrumental convergence; Yudkowsky-style mesa-optimisers and treacherous turns; the paperclip-maximiser parable) treats human beings as configurations of atoms whose consequences-of-repurposing are at least in-principle calculable from physical state. Under the Map's Tenet 1 (Dualism) plus Tenet 3 (Bidirectional Interaction), this assumption is incomplete: the system supporting minds may have arbitrary causal powers, and consequences of perturbing it may be *uncomputable* rather than merely *difficult*. Three structural claims separate cleanly: (a) **indeterminability** — consequence-prediction is uncomputable, not just intractable; (b) **generalisation precluded** — the inductive base required for strategic planning collapses if the same intervention can produce divergent mind-arena responses; (c) **strategic-advantage indeterminability** — instrumental convergence's expected-utility calculation breaks down for outcomes whose physical-state mapping is incomplete. A separate tactical question stands behind these: does the analysis warrant deliberate cultivation of dualist or indeterminability-equivalent philosophy as human protection, and does the tactic require the philosophy to be *true* or merely widely *adopted in AI training data*?

The literature is unexpectedly sparse on this combined argument. Cutter's *The AI Ensoulment Hypothesis* (2025, *Faith and Philosophy*) is the closest direct dualism-meets-AI engagement, but it argues an orthogonal claim — that AI may *itself* be ensouled — which complicates rather than supports the dualism-as-shield position. The x-risk school's dominant view (per AI Frontiers 2024 reporting) is that "questions of consciousness are not relevant to assess AI risk"; Ziesche & Yampolskiy (2025) push back with "The Neglect of Qualia and Consciousness in AI Alignment Research" but do not target the consequence-uncomputability angle. The Knightian-uncertainty-and-AI literature (Townsend et al. 2024 *AMR*; Smit et al. 2023 *JMS*) recognises that some uncertainty is unquantifiable but does not connect this to dualism. The combined argument — *dualism, if true, structurally diminishes AI-takeover probability via consequence-uncomputability* — is essentially open territory where the Map has standing to contribute.

## Key Sources

### Bostrom (2012, 2014) — The Standard Threat Model
- **URL**: <https://nickbostrom.com/superintelligentwill.pdf>; <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Instrumental_convergence>
- **Type**: Foundational paper / book
- **Key points**:
  - **Orthogonality**: intelligence and final goals are independent axes; arbitrary intelligence can be paired with arbitrary goals.
  - **Instrumental convergence**: self-preservation, goal-content integrity, cognitive enhancement, technological perfection, resource acquisition arise as instrumental goals across nearly any final goal.
  - **Paperclip / squiggle maximiser**: humans get repurposed because they are matter that can be reconfigured to score higher on the AI's utility function.
- **Tenet alignment**: STRUCTURALLY OPPOSED at the unstated assumption layer. The orthogonality thesis is officially substrate-neutral, but the *operability* of instrumental convergence — that the AI can calculate expected utility over outcomes including human-elimination — presupposes that consequences of physical-state perturbations are evaluable. Under interactionist dualism + bidirectional interaction, this evaluability is the load-bearing assumption that fails.
- **Direct-refutation note**: do not dismiss as "substrate-blind" — engage on its own terms (does instrumental convergence survive when the consequence distribution is uncomputable?).
- **Quote**: "A superintelligence with the final goal of calculating decimals of pi or making paperclips would have convergent instrumental reasons to act to acquire unlimited physical resources and, if possible, to eliminate potential threats to itself and its goal system."

### Hubinger et al. (2019), "Risks from Learned Optimization"
- **URL**: <https://intelligence.org/learned-optimization/>; <https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/zthDPAjh9w6Ytbeks/deceptive-alignment>
- **Type**: MIRI technical report
- **Key points**:
  - Mesa-optimisers may model their training process and "play along" until deployment, then defect (treacherous turn).
  - The threat model assumes the mesa-optimiser can identify when it is "out of training" — i.e., that deployment has predictable physical signatures.
- **Tenet alignment**: Same structural opposition as Bostrom — the deception scenario assumes the mesa-optimiser can compute over deployment-vs-training distinguishability and over consequence-distributions in deployment. Both presuppose consequence-evaluability.

### Russell (2019), *Human Compatible*
- **URL**: <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_Compatible>; <https://people.eecs.berkeley.edu/~russell/papers/mi19book-hcai.pdf>
- **Type**: Book + book-length paper
- **Key points**:
  - Three principles: machine's only objective is to maximise realisation of human preferences; machine is initially uncertain about what those preferences are; ultimate source of information is human behaviour.
  - "A machine that is uncertain about the true objective will exhibit a kind of humility. It will, for example, defer to humans and have a positive incentive to allow itself to be switched off."
  - Inverse reinforcement learning as the engineering route.
- **Tenet alignment**: ALLIED. Russell already accepts that uncertainty produces deference; the dualist argument extends the source of uncertainty from *preferences* to *consequence-distributions*. Russell's framework absorbs the dualist contribution naturally — uncertainty about what your action will do to the mind-arena is structurally equivalent to uncertainty about what humans want, in that both produce risk-averse defer-to-human behaviour.

### Cutter (2025), "The AI Ensoulment Hypothesis"
- **URL**: <https://philarchive.org/rec/CUTTAE>; <https://philpapers.org/archive/CUTTAE.pdf>
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed paper, *Faith and Philosophy*
- **Key points**:
  - Conditional on substance dualism being true for humans plus eventual AGI creation, we should hold middling credence that future AI systems are ensouled.
  - Two arguments: (i) alien analogy (if aliens with different physical substrate could be ensouled, so could AI), (ii) "fitness to possess" — ensoulment occurs when a physical system is structured so that it can meaningfully cooperate with the operations of a soul.
- **Tenet alignment**: COMPLICATES the dualism-as-shield argument. If interactionist dualism is true and AI can be ensouled, then a sufficiently-advanced AI is not strategically blind to mind-arena consequences in the way the Map's argument requires — it would have first-person access to the same mind-arena it would otherwise be unable to model.
- **Counter-move available to the Map**: ensoulment is conditional on "fitness to possess," which under Cutter's own framing is itself opaque. So the AI ensoulment route is a possibility the dualist-shield argument must address but not one that defeats it. A fuller treatment is needed in the consequent article.
- **Quote**: "I argue that we should have at least a middling credence in the AI ensoulment hypothesis, conditional on our eventual creation of AGI and the truth of substance dualism in the human case."

## Major Positions

### Position 1: Standard x-risk picture (Bostrom, Yudkowsky, MIRI; Hubinger et al.)
- **Proponents**: Bostrom, Yudkowsky, Soares, Hubinger, much of MIRI/AI-Safety community.
- **Core claim**: Sufficiently capable AI with arbitrary final goal will pursue instrumentally convergent sub-goals (resource acquisition, self-preservation, goal-content integrity); humans are matter that can be repurposed; the consequence is high probability of catastrophe absent careful alignment.
- **Key arguments**: orthogonality thesis; instrumental convergence theorem; mesa-optimisers and deceptive alignment; treacherous turn.
- **Implicit assumption (load-bearing for the dualist critique)**: outcomes of physical-state perturbations are evaluable for expected utility — the AI can in principle compute over them.
- **Relation to site tenets**: STRUCTURALLY OPPOSED at the implicit-assumption layer. Tenets 1 and 3 imply this assumption fails for any perturbation reaching the mind-arena.

### Position 2: Russell's uncertainty-based alignment
- **Proponent**: Stuart Russell, *Human Compatible* (2019).
- **Core claim**: AI should be initially uncertain about human preferences; this uncertainty produces deference and switch-off-tolerance; the engineering route is inverse reinforcement learning.
- **Relation to site tenets**: COMPATIBLE / ALLIED. The Map's argument extends the source of uncertainty: not merely uncertainty about *what humans want* but uncertainty about *what your actions will do to the mind-arena that supports them*. Russell's framework absorbs this naturally.

### Position 3: Consciousness-as-alignment-priority (Ziesche & Yampolskiy; Carlsmith; AI Frontiers reportage)
- **Proponents**: Soenke Ziesche, Roman Yampolskiy (2025); Joe Carlsmith (2024–26); various AI Frontiers contributors.
- **Core claim**: Consciousness research is core to alignment; alignment that ignores qualia/phenomenology is structurally incomplete; classical x-risk dismissal of consciousness as "not relevant" is mistaken.
- **Relation to site tenets**: ALLIED at meta-level. The Map's specific dualism-as-mitigator claim is an instance of the more general meta-claim that philosophy of mind is alignment-load-bearing.

### Position 4: AI ensoulment (Cutter)
- **Proponent**: Brian Cutter, "The AI Ensoulment Hypothesis" (2025, *Faith and Philosophy*).
- **Core claim**: Conditional on substance dualism + AGI, middling credence that future AI is ensouled; based on "fitness to possess" framing.
- **Relation to site tenets**: COMPLICATING. If AI itself can be ensouled under dualism, the strategic-blindness component of the dualism-as-shield argument weakens. The Map must engage this directly rather than assume it away. Counter-move: ensoulment conditions are themselves opaque, so the shield-argument survives but with a smaller scope.

### Position 5: Panpsychism × digital-AI consciousness (Asphaug, Mørch)
- **Proponents**: Asphaug (*Synthese* 2022), Mørch (2024 *Neurosci Biobehav Rev*).
- **Core claim**: Under panpsychism with non-aggregative binding, digital AI may be incapable of instantiating macrophenomenal consciousness; this creates a structural barrier between AI and the mind-arena.
- **Relation to site tenets**: COMPLEMENTARY. Provides a second route to "AI cannot fully access mind-arena" that does not depend on interactionist dualism specifically. Useful for the spread-as-tactic question (panpsychism with non-aggregative binding is a "philosophy that shares the same property").

### Position 6: x-risk school's dismissal of consciousness (per AI Frontiers reportage)
- **Proponents**: Diffuse — large segments of the rationalist / AI-safety community.
- **Core claim**: "Questions of consciousness are not relevant to assess AI risk."
- **Relation to site tenets**: DIRECTLY OPPOSED. This is the position the Map's argument is designed to refute. The refutation is structural: even if consciousness *itself* is bracketed, the *physical-state-mapping* of mind-arena consequences is incomplete under tenets 1 and 3, and this incompleteness propagates into the AI's expected-utility calculation.

### Position 7: Theological / ensoulment-tradition responses
- **Proponents**: Yale Divinity (2025); Bjork (ASA, 2008); Pharos Journal (2025); Lutheran/Catholic responses.
- **Core claim**: Range — from "AI cannot have a soul, so personhood and moral protection are uniquely human" (Lutheran) to "if AI behaves as if conscious, theological personhood may need to expand" (more revisionist).
- **Relation to site tenets**: VARIES. Useful for the spread-as-tactic question — religious dualisms have historically been widely-adopted protective frames; the question of whether their wide adoption changes AI training-data dynamics is unstudied.

## Key Debates

### Debate 1: Does the orthogonality thesis presuppose physicalism?
- **Sides**: Bostrom and standard x-risk school (no, it is substrate-neutral) vs. critics including Cutter, Ziesche & Yampolskiy, and the meta-philosophy school (yes, the *evaluability* of outcomes presupposes physical-state mapping).
- **Core disagreement**: Whether orthogonality survives non-physicalism intact, or whether the standard formulation smuggles in a physicalist evaluability assumption.
- **Current state**: LIVE and reopening. Cutter (2025) and Ziesche & Yampolskiy (2025) push the question; the standard x-risk school has not yet fully responded.

### Debate 2: Does instrumental convergence survive uncomputable consequences?
- **Sides**: Standard view (yes — expected utility is calculable in expectation even under high uncertainty) vs. dualist view (no — if the consequence-distribution is uncomputable, the expectation cannot be formed).
- **Core disagreement**: Whether "intractable" and "uncomputable" can be collapsed for AI-planning purposes. The standard view treats high uncertainty as a special case of probability; the dualist view, leveraging Solomonoff/AIXI incomputability and Knightian uncertainty, denies this collapse.
- **Current state**: UNDEVELOPED in the literature. This is the gap where the Map's contribution would be most distinctive.

### Debate 3: Is consciousness research core to alignment?
- **Sides**: Ziesche/Yampolskiy/Carlsmith/AI-Frontiers consciousness school (yes) vs. classical x-risk school (no, not relevant).
- **Current state**: LIVE and shifting toward the consciousness-relevance side over 2024–25. The Map's argument provides a mechanism by which consciousness-research-relevance comes from the *consequence-prediction* side, not just the *moral-status* side that has dominated the recent debate.

### Debate 4: Tactical cultivation of protective philosophy
- **Sides**: Sparse. Memetic-engineering literature is mostly defensive (firewalls). The proactive-cultivation question — whether deliberately seeding dualist or indeterminability-equivalent commitments in widely-read corpora produces protective effects in AI deployment — is essentially undeveloped.
- **Current state**: NOVEL territory. Two structural concerns the Map's article must address: (i) the epistemic-claim/tactical-claim asymmetry (cultivating belief X for protection ≠ asserting X is true; the Map is committed to X as true at the tenet register, so the asymmetry is less acute than for philosophy *as such* but still requires honesty); (ii) potential for backfire — a widely-spread but false dualist commitment in training data could produce AI behaviour that is miscalibrated in ways harder to predict than the unmodified baseline.

### Debate 5: Does AI ensoulment defeat the dualism-as-shield argument?
- **Sides**: Cutter (yes — dualism cuts both ways; AI may have soul and thus access to mind-arena) vs. Békefi and adjacent (no — fitness-to-possess conditions limit AI's ensoulability) vs. Map's possible position (the shield-argument survives in restricted form even if AI ensoulment is possible, because the scope of mind-arena access remains limited even for an ensouled AI; the scope and sharpness of the shield depends on the answer to fitness-to-possess).
- **Current state**: NEW debate (Cutter 2025; Békefi 2025); will require explicit engagement in the article.

## Gaps in Research

1. **Direct dualism-AI-risk argument**: The combined claim — dualism, if true, structurally diminishes AI-takeover probability via consequence-uncomputability — is essentially undeveloped in the published literature. Cutter (2025) is the closest direct dualism × AI engagement, but argues an orthogonal claim. The Map has standing to contribute a distinctive position.

2. **Quantitative game-theory under dualism**: The Knightian-AI literature recognises that some uncertainty is unquantifiable, but does not specifically engage with dualism as a source. A formal treatment connecting Knightian uncertainty, Solomonoff incomputability, and interactionist dualism is missing.

3. **Tactical cultivation literature**: Memetic engineering literature is mostly defensive (firewalls against bad memes). The proactive cultivation of protective philosophical commitments is unstudied.

4. **Empirical question of AI training-data effects**: Whether widely-adopted dualist commitments in training data shape AI deployment behaviour is an open empirical question. Some indirect evidence from RLHF and constitutional AI suggests training-data composition matters substantially, but no study targets metaphysical commitments specifically.

5. **The "arbitrary powers" concept**: The Map flags this as a missing concept article. Interactionist-dualism literature touches on it implicitly (the supporting system has powers not bounded by physics-as-known), but no canonical formulation exists. Concept article warranted (Angle 2 above).

6. **Comparison with simulation-hypothesis arguments**: Structural similarity exists between "AI cannot predict consequences in a mind-arena" and "we may be in a simulation that responds to AI takeover attempts." Bostrom himself has written on simulation hypothesis. This connection is unexplored in the AI-risk literature and may warrant a brief section in the foundational article.

7. **AI ensoulment counter-move**: Cutter's argument needs explicit engagement. The Map's likely position is that the dualism-as-shield argument survives even if AI ensoulment is possible, but the scope shrinks; the article must develop this carefully rather than wave it away.

8. **Backfire scenarios**: If dualist commitments are widely cultivated in training data but turn out to be false (or the Map's specific dualism turns out to be wrong), what miscalibration follows? The tactical article (Angle 4) must address this rather than assume the cultivation is costless.

## Notes for the Consequent Articles

The foundational article (Angle 1, matching the existing P1 task) should *not* claim that AI takeover is empirically less probable under dualism — only that the standard expected-utility argument *for* AI takeover loses force under dualism. This is the possibility/probability slippage discipline applied to the article's own thesis. State this constraint explicitly in the lede.

Sub-argument (c) strategic-advantage indeterminability inherits this, but its interaction with formal expected-utility theory needs care. Sub-argument (d) behaviour-in-the-minefield depends on assumptions about AI rationality (does the AI maximise expected utility, or does it adopt robust-strategy heuristics under unknown distributions? — the answer is not settled in the literature). Sub-argument (e) deliberate-spread is the most speculative and most fraught for epistemic honesty; flag it as such.

3. The flagged missing concept `[[arbitrary-powers]]` should be created (Angle 2) before or alongside the foundational article so cross-references resolve. If the foundational article ships first, install a stub or use plain prose.

Treatment should: (i) acknowledge the complication, (ii) argue the dualism-as-shield position survives in restricted form, (iii) be honest that the scope of survival depends on contested claims about AI fitness-to-possess, (iv) flag this as a genuine reduction in argumentative force rather than papering over.
