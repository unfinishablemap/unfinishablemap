## Executive Summary

Memory is not the only cognitive capacity that resists single-domain explanation. Proprioception, spatial reasoning, and imagination each exhibit features that straddle the physical and non-physical divide in ways instructive for interactionist dualism. Proprioception combines mechanical receptor data with a phenomenal body schema that survives amputation (phantom limbs) and structures self-awareness. Spatial reasoning—especially mental rotation—operates through analog imagery whose qualitative character is orthogonal to the computational task, yet the phenomenal experience appears functionally integrated with performance. Imagination is perhaps the strongest case: it generates novel phenomenal content without sensory input, guides physical action (mental practice effects), and varies dramatically across individuals (aphantasia to hyperphantasia) in ways that dissociate conscious experience from underlying functional capacity. Under the Map's framework, each capability suggests a dual-domain architecture: physical mechanisms provide substrates and constraints, while consciousness contributes phenomenal character, selectional guidance, and creative generation.

### Body Schema vs. Body Image (Neuroscience)

- **Tenet alignment**: Non-conscious schema (physical) vs. conscious image (phenomenal) maps directly onto dual-domain architecture. Consciousness constitutes the body image while the schema constrains it

## Major Positions and Debates

### Embodied Cognition (Anti-Dualist)
- **Proponents**: Merleau-Ponty, Thompson, Noë, Clark
- **Core claim**: Cognition is constitutively embodied; proprioception, action, and perception cannot be decomposed into mental and physical
- **Relation to site tenets**: Conflicts with substance dualism but compatible with interactionism. Tight body-mind coupling is exactly what interactionism predicts. Body schema persistence beyond physical structure points toward irreducible non-physical dimension

### Analog Imagery vs. Propositional Representation
- **Proponents**: Kosslyn, Shepard (analog) vs. Pylyshyn (propositional)
- **Core claim**: Mental images preserve spatial structure and mirror physical perception. Largely resolved toward hybrid view by neuroscience (overlapping brain regions)
- **Relation to site tenets**: If images are genuinely spatial-structural, their qualitative character does functional work—phenomenal properties appear causally efficacious, supporting Bidirectional Interaction

### Imagination as Mental Simulation
- **Proponents**: Goldman, Currie, Ravenscroft
- **Core claim**: Imagination is offline activation of perceptual/motor systems; mental practice improves physical performance
- **Relation to site tenets**: Generates phenomenal content from internal causes. The hard problem applies: why would simulation be accompanied by experience? Supports dual-domain: physical neural simulation + non-physical experiential dimension

### Non-Sensory Phenomenology (Aphantasia)
- **Proponents**: Bayne, Schwitzgebel
- **Core claim**: Conscious thought includes non-sensory phenomenal properties; aphantasics think without imagery yet remain phenomenally conscious
- **Relation to site tenets**: Strongly supportive. Non-sensory phenomenology is even harder to reduce to physical processes than sensory qualia—consciousness has dimensions orthogonal to sensory processing
