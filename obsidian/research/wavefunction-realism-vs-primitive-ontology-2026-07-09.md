## Executive Summary

This is a debate inside philosophy of physics about what quantum mechanics is *fundamentally about* — its basic ontology — quite apart from which interpretation (Bohm, GRW, Everett) is true. Two camps divide over the status of the quantum wavefunction and the number of dimensions in fundamental reality. **Wavefunction realism** (Albert, Ney, Loewer, North) holds that the wavefunction is a real physical field living in a high-dimensional `3N`-dimensional configuration space, and that ordinary three-dimensional space and its objects are derivative or emergent. **Primitive ontology** / local-beables theorists (Dürr, Goldstein, Zanghì, Allori, Tumulka; grounded in Bell's "local beables") hold the opposite: the fundamental furniture is entities *in* ordinary 3D/4D spacetime (particles, "flashes", or a matter-density field), while the wavefunction is not a physical object at all but a *nomological* (law-like) entity that governs how the primitive ontology moves — analogous to a Hamiltonian. The core dispute is whether 3D space is emergent from a genuinely `3N`-dimensional reality, or whether `3N`-dimensional configuration space is a mathematical bookkeeping device and 3D spacetime is fundamental. The sharpest pressure point on wavefunction realism is the **macro-object / "marvelous point" recovery problem**: explaining how the familiar three-dimensional world of tables and observers is recovered from a field on a very high-dimensional space. Both camps are realist, both take quantum theory seriously, and the disagreement is live and unresolved.

## Major Positions

### Wavefunction Realism (`3N`-Fundamentalism)
- **Proponents**: David Albert, Alyssa Ney, Barry Loewer, Jill North.
- **Core claim**: The universal wavefunction `Ψ` is a real physical field, and it lives in — is a field *on* — a `3N`-dimensional configuration space. That high-dimensional space is the fundamental physical arena. The three-dimensional world of ordinary experience is derivative/emergent.
- **Key arguments**:
  - *Separability / locality restoration* (Loewer, Albert): what looks like non-separable, spookily entangled behaviour in 3D is a manifestation of a perfectly separable, local metaphysics in the higher-dimensional space. This rescues something like Humean supervenience (Loewer's link to Lewis's programme).
  - *Reading the formalism literally*: the wavefunction is the mathematically complete object the theory works with; realism about it is realism about what the theory quantifies over.
  - *Recovery via dynamics* (Albert, Ney): the Hamiltonian imposes structure on the `3N` coordinates (grouping into triplets) so that emergent structures with the functional roles of 3D objects appear — a functionalist recovery of the manifest image.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Shared-ground philosophy of physics, not dualism evidence. Congenial to Everett/MWI (a single universal wavefunction evolving unitarily) — hence in tension with Tenet 4 (no many-worlds), though wavefunction realism is separable from MWI and can be paired with collapse or hidden variables. For a consciousness-selection interface (Tenets 2 and 3), this camp implies that what an interface would act on / select among is a location or amplitude structure in `3N`-dimensional configuration space — the "marvelous point" picture — not an object in 3D.

### Primitive Ontology / Local Beables
- **Proponents**: Detlef Dürr, Sheldon Goldstein, Nino Zanghì (the programme's founders); Valia Allori; Roderich Tumulka; rooted in John Bell's "local beables"; Tim Maudlin sympathetic.
- **Core claim**: The fundamental furniture is entities in ordinary 3D/4D spacetime. Which entities depends on the theory:
  - *Bohmian mechanics*: point particles with definite trajectories.
  - *GRW-flash (GRWf, Bell/Tumulka)*: a sparse set of discrete spacetime "flashes" — the primitive ontology is a spacetime point-pattern.
  - *GRW-matter-density (GRWm, Ghirardi)*: a continuous matter-density field `m(x,t)` on ordinary 3D space.
  - In all cases the wavefunction is **nomological** — a law-like entity (compared to a Hamiltonian, or better to the Hamilton–Jacobi action) that *governs* the primitive ontology rather than *being* physical stuff. `3N`-dimensional configuration space is a mathematical device, not a place.
- **Key arguments**:
  - *Empirical contact is in 3D*: measurements, pointers, records, and observers are all arrangements of matter in ordinary space; a theory must have local beables in 3D to make contact with evidence (the "primitive ontology is where the theory meets the world" thesis).
  - *Common-structure unification* (Allori et al. 2008): Bohm and GRW look like rival interpretations but share one architecture — matter in spacetime governed by `Ψ` — which is evidence the architecture is the right level of description.
  - *Parsimony of arena*: no need to posit a vast high-dimensional physical space; keep spacetime and demote `Ψ` to lawhood.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Shared-ground; congenial to single-world realism (both Bohm and GRW are single-world, no branching) — aligns with Tenet 4. For a consciousness-selection interface (Tenets 2 and 3), this camp implies that what an interface would select among is a *local beable* in 3D — which particle configuration is actual, or where/whether a flash occurs (the GRW collapse events are an especially natural hook for a "minimal quantum interaction" story, since GRW already posits stochastic localisation events).

## Key Debates

### Is 3D space emergent, or is `3N`-space a mathematical device?
- **Sides**: Wavefunction realists (3D emergent from a real `3N`-dimensional field) vs. primitive-ontology theorists (`3N`-space is bookkeeping; 3D/4D spacetime is fundamental).
- **Core disagreement**: The ontological status of configuration space itself — a physical arena vs. an abstract space of possible 3D configurations.
- **Current state**: Ongoing; no resolution. Monton's "Against 3N-Dimensional Space" (in Ney & Albert 2013) is a representative counter to the wavefunction-realist arena.

### The macro-object / "marvelous point" recovery problem
- **Sides**: Critics (including primitive-ontology theorists and 3D-space defenders) press wavefunction realists to explain how tables, pointers, and experimenters — things with locations and extension in 3D — arise from a field on a very high-dimensional space. The label "marvelous point" comes from Albert's earlier Bohmian picture, where the entire physical content of the universe is carried by a single point moving through `3N`-space.
- **Wavefunction-realist response**: functionalist/constitution recovery (Albert's triplet-grouping via the Hamiltonian; Ney's constitution account in *The World in the Wave Function*).
- **Core disagreement**: whether the functionalist recovery genuinely *yields* three-dimensional objects or merely *simulates* their behaviour without delivering real 3D entities.
- **Current state**: The central live objection to wavefunction realism; actively debated (e.g. recent "macro-object problem" and "hylomorphism" papers 2017–2026).

### Is the nomological view of `Ψ` coherent?
- **Sides**: Primitive-ontology theorists (yes — `Ψ` is like a law) vs. critics.
- **Core disagreement**: A classical Hamiltonian is fixed by the laws and does not vary contingently, whereas the wavefunction depends on contingent initial/boundary conditions and evolves in time — so how can it be a *law*?
- **Nomological-camp responses**: (a) compare `Ψ` to the Hamilton–Jacobi *action* rather than the Hamiltonian, since the action too depends on initial conditions and lives in configuration space; (b) appeal to a unique *wavefunction of the universe* (from quantum gravity / Wheeler–DeWitt) to remove the contingency, so the universal `Ψ` is effectively fixed and law-like.
- **Current state**: Ongoing; a recognised soft spot the programme actively defends.
