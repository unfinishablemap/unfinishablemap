## Executive Summary

Psychophysical parallelism is the general doctrine that mental and physical events run in exact correlation with **no** causal interaction between them. It is a genus with three species relevant here: Leibniz's pre-established harmony (correlation grounded in God's front-loaded design), Spinoza's dual-aspect reading (one substance under two attributes, correlated because identical under two descriptions), and the de-theologised 19th-century empirical parallelism of Fechner and Wundt (correlation as a working posit for scientific psychology). The Spinozist version is the sharpest foil for the Map because it borrows the "two aspects of one reality" vocabulary while **denying** inter-aspect causation — the exact inverse of Tenet 3. Research confirms the doctrine is a canonical, load-bearing foil, but also confirms the Map-relevant disambiguation is **already written** in `bi-aspectual-ontology.md` (the "Spinozist Parallelism" subsection) and `occasionalism.md` (the sibling-solutions paragraph). The concrete deliverable of this session is publisher-verified metadata (Spinoza IIp7 and 3P2, Curley; Fechner 1860; Wundt's adaptation) plus a factual correction owed to occasionalism.md.

## Key Sources (publisher-verified this session)

### Spinoza's Theory of Attributes — Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/spinoza-attributes/
- **Type**: Encyclopedia (verified verbatim via WebFetch 2026-07-14)
- **IIp7 verbatim (Curley translation)**: "The order and connection of ideas is the same as the order and connection of things." Latin given: *"ordo, & connexio idearum idem est, ac ordo & connexio rerum."* This is the parallelism doctrine's locus classicus.
- **The denial of inter-attribute causation (the sharp contrast with the Map)**: SEP renders **Ethics 3P2**: "The body cannot determine the mind to thinking, and the mind cannot determine the body to motion." There is "a conceptual barrier" between Thought and Extension — each "may be conceived without the aid of the other" — yielding "explanatory independence": physical changes are explained through other physical items, ideas through other ideas.
- **Why this matters for the Map**: Spinoza's parallelism is correlation *because of identity under two descriptions*, with an explicit causal/explanatory barrier. The Map's bi-aspectual ontology borrows the "two aspects of one reality" phrasing but **breaks** exactly here: actuality causally selects among structural possibilities. Same vocabulary, opposite verdict on interaction.
- **Translation used by SEP**: Edwin Curley, *The Collected Works of Spinoza*, vol. 1 (Princeton University Press, 1985) — the standard scholarly English text.
- **Tenet alignment**: Directly conflicts with Tenet 3 (Bidirectional Interaction — Spinoza's barrier forbids exactly the mind→body selection Tenet 3 requires); in tension with Tenet 2 (the parallelism denies the very causal channel Minimal Quantum Interaction locates). Where mind is causally idle w.r.t. body, parallelism slides toward epiphenomenalism — the shared vulnerability the Map treats in the epiphenomenalism article.

### Psychophysical parallelism in scientific psychology — Fechner and Wundt
- **Fechner, Gustav Theodor. *Elemente der Psychophysik* (Elements of Psychophysics), Leipzig, 1860.** Fechner defined psychophysics as the exact science of the functional/dependency relations between the bodily and the mental. Crucially, his parallelism was **not** a bare posit: he grounded it in a **monistic double-aspect / identity view** — bodily facts and conscious facts are "not reducible one to the other" but are "different sides of one reality." He formalised the parallel as an empirical postulate of functional dependence (perfect correlation, no causal interaction) sitting atop that identity metaphysics.
- **Wundt, Wilhelm.** From 1862 onward Wundt made Fechner's work central and **adapted parallelism as a methodological/empirical postulate** for experimental psychology: psychology may study the mental order while physiology studies the physical, with neither reduced to the other and no causal traffic assumed between them. The "bare empirical posit" framing the corpus currently attaches to Fechner belongs more properly to this Wundtian methodological stance.
- **Type**: History of science (search-corroborated; not opened against a print primary this session — see Gaps).
- **Correction this yields**: `concepts/occasionalism.md`'s sibling paragraph attributes "bare empirical posit" to Fechner. That is inaccurate to Fechner's own double-aspect identity monism; the methodological/postulate reading is Wundt's. See recommendation above.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral-to-conflicting. The 19th-century usage is largely a methodological stance (bracket the metaphysics, correlate the two series) rather than a metaphysical rival, but insofar as it denies interaction it inherits parallelism's conflict with Tenet 3.

## Major Position

### Psychophysical Parallelism (the genus)
- **Core claim**: For every mental event there is a correlated physical event; the two orders are strictly coordinated but do **not** causally interact. Apparent mind-body causation is correlation without causation.
- **Three species (by what grounds the correlation)**:
  1. **Leibnizian / theistic** — grounded in God's pre-established design (pre-established harmony). Correlation is engineered once, at creation.
  2. **Spinozist / dual-aspect** — grounded in identity: one substance (God/Nature) under two attributes (Thought, Extension); the two series match *because* they are one order conceived two ways (IIp7). Explicit inter-attribute barrier (3P2). This is the vocabulary-collision species.
  3. **Fechnerian empirical / Wundtian methodological** — de-theologised: correlation taken as an empirical postulate (Fechner atop a double-aspect identity monism; Wundt as a working methodological principle for lab psychology).
- **Relation to Tenets**: Conflicts with **Tenet 3** (the whole genus denies inter-series/inter-aspect causation — the purest structural rival). In tension with **Tenet 2** (denies the causal channel Minimal Quantum Interaction locates). Engages **Tenet 5** (Occam cuts both ways): the theistic species trades a local interaction problem for a global design problem; the identity species buys correlation cheaply but at the price of forbidding the very interaction the Map's evidence (measurement problem + reportability) argues for. Shares with epiphenomenalism the causal-idleness-of-mind vulnerability (Tenet 3 territory, owned by the epiphenomenalism article).

## Key Debates

### Why the correlation? (the genus's defining problem)
- **Sides**: Occasionalism answers "God, at each occasion"; PEH answers "God, once at creation"; Spinozist parallelism answers "because they are one substance under two attributes — no external coordinator needed"; Fechner/Wundt answer "take it as a brute empirical/methodological correlation and get on with the science."
- **Core disagreement**: what does the coordinating work, and whether any coordinator is needed at all. The Spinozist answer is the most economical (identity dissolves the question) — which is exactly why it is the most seductive near-neighbour to the Map's own "two aspects of one reality" phrasing, and why the disambiguation must be explicit.
- **State**: historically settled *against* all parallelist options by the rise of interactionist and physicalist frameworks; philosophically live only as a foil.

### Parallelism vs. epiphenomenalism
- **Sides**: critics say parallelism just *is* epiphenomenalism with the mental series added; defenders say the mind is not inert (it has real intra-series/final-causal life), only causally idle w.r.t. the body.
- **State**: the "idle-w.r.t.-body already suffices for the epiphenomenalist verdict" question is the shared vulnerability; the Map develops it in the epiphenomenalism article, not here.
