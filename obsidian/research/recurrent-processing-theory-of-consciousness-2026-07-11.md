## Executive Summary

Recurrent Processing Theory (RPT), developed by Dutch neuroscientist Victor Lamme, holds that **localized recurrent (feedback / re-entrant) processing in sensory cortex is the neural signature of phenomenal consciousness**, dissociable from the global broadcast and cognitive access emphasized by rival theories. Its central mechanistic claim distinguishes a fast **feedforward sweep** (unconscious feature extraction) from slower **recurrent processing** (feedback loops that render content conscious). RPT is the "local / first-order" member of the neural-correlates-of-consciousness "big four" — the alternative to global-workspace-theory's global broadcast, integrated-information-theory's system-level integration, and higher-order-theories' meta-representation. Its signature and most contested prediction is **phenomenal overflow**: experience is richer than what gets globally accessed or reported, so consciousness can occur without access. This overflow claim converges (partially) with Ned Block's overflow argument. For the Map, RPT is a physicalist NCC theory to engage, not an ally — it identifies experience with a physical mechanism and so denies Tenet 1 on its own terms, yet its overflow thesis is congenial to the Map's view that experience exceeds report, and recurrent loops are a plausible physical-side locus for the consciousness interface.

### The distinct modes of vision offered by feedforward and recurrent processing

- **Full citation**: Lamme, V. A. F., & Roelfsema, P. R. (2000). "The distinct modes of vision offered by feedforward and recurrent processing." *Trends in Neurosciences*, 23(11), 571–579. PMID 11074267.
- **Type**: Review paper (Cell/Elsevier)
- **Publisher verification**: CONFIRMED at PubMed — authors V. A. Lamme & P. R. Roelfsema, journal *Trends in Neurosciences*, 2000, 23(11):571–9.
- **Key points**:
  - Introduces the foundational feedforward-sweep vs recurrent-processing distinction.
  - The feedforward sweep rapidly routes activity across visual areas using hardwired feature groupings but is "probably incapable of yielding visual awareness."
  - Horizontal connections within areas plus feedback from higher areas produce recurrent processing, "necessary before the features of an object are attentively grouped and the stimulus can enter consciousness."
  - Frames the distinction as pivotal for attentive vs pre-attentive AND conscious vs unconscious vision.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral-to-conflicting. Identifies a physical processing mode as the gateway to awareness (would deny Tenet 1 if read as identity); but as a correlate claim it names a candidate physical-side coupling site.

### Adversarial testing of global neuronal workspace and integrated information theories of consciousness
- **URL**: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-025-08888-1
- **Full citation**: Cogitate Consortium et al. (2025). "Adversarial testing of global neuronal workspace and integrated information theories of consciousness." *Nature*, 642(8066), 133–142. Published 2025-06-05. PMID 40307561. DOI 10.1038/s41586-025-08888-1.

- **Publisher verification**: CONFIRMED at Nature/PubMed — *Nature* Vol. 642, No. 8066, pp. 133–142, 5 June 2025.
- **Key points**:
  - n = 256; suprathreshold stimuli of variable duration measured with fMRI, MEG, and iEEG.
  - Results corroborate IIT's claim that **posterior cortical areas ("hot zone") are sufficient** for conscious content; neither PFC involvement nor global broadcasting was necessary.

But its posterior-sufficiency / PFC-not-necessary finding is broadly consonant with RPT's local, first-order, sensory-cortex emphasis and against strong prefrontal-broadcast requirements. Present as supporting context, not as a test of RPT.

## Major Positions

### Recurrent Processing Theory (Lamme)
- **Proponents**: Victor Lamme; Pieter Roelfsema (early co-author on the mechanism).
- **Core claim**: Localized recurrent processing in sensory (esp. visual) cortex is necessary and sufficient for **phenomenal** consciousness, independent of global access.
- **The four-stage model**:
  1. **Superficial feedforward** — local processing within early visual areas. Unconscious.
  2. **Deep feedforward** — signals travel forward through the hierarchy, can drive action. Unconscious.
  3. **Superficial / localized recurrent** — feedback returns into earlier areas; local re-entrant loops. **Phenomenally conscious but not necessarily reportable.**
  4. **Widespread recurrent** — recurrent processing reaches widespread areas including motor/frontal, enabling report / access.
  - Signature reading: Stage 3 is sufficient for phenomenality; the Stage 3 → Stage 4 step adds **access / reportability**, not experience itself. Hence phenomenality can "overflow" access.
- **Key arguments**: (a) neural continuity — Stage 3 and Stage 4 share the same recurrent mechanism, so drawing the consciousness line at report is arbitrary; (b) behavioral — partial-report / no-report paradigms show subjects experience more than they access.
- **Relation to site tenets**: On its own terms RPT is reductive (recurrence *is* experience) and denies Tenet 1. But (i) its phenomenal-overflow claim is congenial to the Map's view that experience exceeds report/access, and (ii) recurrent loops are a natural candidate for *where* a non-physical coupling would engage the physical brain (Tenets 2–3), without conceding that the recurrence itself constitutes the felt quality.

### Global Workspace Theory (contrast — first-order rival)
- **Proponents**: Baars; Dehaene; Changeux (global *neuronal* workspace).
- **Core claim**: Consciousness requires global broadcast ("ignition") of information to a workspace spanning fronto-parietal cortex; access is constitutive.
- **Contrast with RPT**: GWT locates consciousness at Lamme's Stage 4 (widespread broadcast); RPT locates phenomenality already at Stage 3 (local recurrence). GWT ties consciousness to access; RPT dissociates them. This is the sharpest RPT-vs-rival fault line.

### Higher-Order Theories (contrast — meta-representation rival)
- **Proponents**: Rosenthal; Lau; Brown.
- **Core claim**: A mental state is conscious in virtue of a suitable higher-order representation *of* that state (often prefrontal).
- **Contrast with RPT**: RPT is **first-order** — recurrent processing of the sensory content itself suffices; no meta-representation required. HOT typically implicates prefrontal cortex, which RPT and the COGITATE posterior-sufficiency finding both downplay.

### Integrated Information Theory (contrast — system-level rival)
- **Proponents**: Tononi; Koch.
- **Core claim**: Consciousness is identical to a system's maximally irreducible integrated information (Φ), intrinsic and substrate-dependent, centered on a posterior "hot zone."
- **Contrast with RPT**: Both de-emphasize prefrontal broadcast and favor posterior cortex, but IIT is a system-level identity claim (Φ) whereas RPT is a specific dynamical-mechanism claim (re-entrant loops). RPT is mechanistically concrete; IIT is mathematically global.

## Key Debates

### Does phenomenal consciousness overflow cognitive access?
- **Sides**: Overflow / rich view (Block, Lamme) vs sparse / no-overflow view (Cohen, Dennett, Phillips, and probabilistic-hierarchical critics such as Gross & Flombaum).
- **Core disagreement**: Whether the Sperling partial-report phenomenon shows genuine rich phenomenal experience beyond access, or merely a "generic" / gist representation plus post-hoc reconstruction.
- **Current state**: Ongoing and unresolved. Empirically hard to adjudicate because any measurement of "what was experienced" routes through some form of access/report. The no-report paradigm is the main methodological attempt to break the impasse.

### Is access necessary for consciousness? (RPT vs GWT/HOT)
- **Sides**: RPT (no — Stage 3 local recurrence suffices) vs GWT/HOT (yes — global access or meta-representation is constitutive).
- **Core disagreement**: Whether Stage-3 recurrent activity that never reaches report is genuine phenomenal experience or merely an unconscious precursor.
- **Current state**: Open. GWT theorists concede the Stage-3 correlation but deny it is *sufficient*; testing "access-free consciousness" is the crux and is methodologically fraught (any report reintroduces access).

### The explanatory-gap challenge to any NCC identity
- **Sides**: Reductive NCC theorists (RPT, GWT, IIT, HOT all posit some physical identity or sufficiency) vs anti-reductionists (Chalmers-style hard-problem, and the Map).
- **Core disagreement**: Even granting recurrent processing as the reliable correlate, why does re-entrant signalling *feel like* anything? RPT names a mechanism but does not close the gap between the mechanism and the phenomenal character.
- **Current state**: Foundational and open — this is the Map's principal point of engagement.
