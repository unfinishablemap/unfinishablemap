## Executive Summary

The capability-division template (brain-side feature extraction + action-guidance vs. mind-side flexible deployment + ownership) is asserted within five Map articles spanning vision, audition, chemosensation, touch, and proprioception, but the *cross-modal recurrence* of the division has never been argued as a claim in its own right. The cross-modal literature supplies the missing evidence: Sanchez et al. (2020, *PNAS*) find a **common supramodal neural signature of conscious perception** across vision, audition, and touch — a late (>200 ms) "ignition" that decodes across modalities and even appears in sensory regions irrelevant to the task. This converges with the Global Neuronal Workspace claim (Mashour et al. 2020) that conscious access is a modality-general broadcast. The structural reading: the *mind-side boundary* recurs across modalities (the conscious/unconscious transition has a common signature), while the *brain-side* machinery is modality-specific (V1 feature maps, cochlear frequency analysis, combinatorial olfactory codes — no shared substrate). This asymmetry — modality-specific brain side, supramodal mind side — is the genuinely new structural finding, and it is mildly *consonant* with the interface reading without settling it (the physicalist reads the supramodal signature as the global-workspace broadcast itself). The disanalogies matter at least as much as the convergence: amodal/global perceptual completion requires awareness only in *some* modalities, ownership is vivid in touch/proprioception but thin in smell, and "flexible deployment" is hard to operationalise uniformly across senses.

## Key Sources

### Decoding across sensory modalities reveals common supramodal signatures of conscious perception
- **URL**: https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.1912584117 (PubMed: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32184331/)
- **Type**: Paper (PNAS, 2020)
- **Authors / cite**: Sanchez, G., Hartmann, T., Fuscà, M., Demarchi, G. & Weisz, N. (2020). Decoding across sensory modalities reveals common supramodal signatures of conscious perception. *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences*, 117(13), 7437–7446.
- **Key points**:
  - MEG; near-threshold tactile, visual, AND auditory stimuli; cross-modal decoding in the post-stimulus window.
  - Supramodal spatiotemporal patterns predict conscious perception across all three modalities — train on one modality, decode conscious/unconscious in another.
  - The supramodal pattern includes late activity in *primary sensory regions of OTHER modalities* (e.g. visual cortex activity predicts conscious perception of an auditory near-threshold stimulus) → consistent with global broadcast.
- **Tenet alignment**: NEUTRAL-to-CONSONANT. Directly supports the *recurrence* half of the structural claim (the mind-side conscious/unconscious boundary has a common signature across senses). Does NOT support dualism — the authors read it as the global-workspace broadcast; it is a brain-side, physicalist finding the Map must accommodate, not enlist.
- **Quote (paraphrase of abstract)**: "supramodal spatiotemporal neural activity patterns predicting conscious perception of the feeble stimulation … common neural mechanisms for conscious perception across different sensory modalities."

### Conscious Processing and the Global Neuronal Workspace Hypothesis
- **URL**: https://www.cell.com/neuron/fulltext/S0896-6273(20)30052-0
- **Type**: Review (Neuron, 2020)
- **Cite**: Mashour, G. A., Roelfsema, P., Changeux, J.-P. & Dehaene, S. (2020). Conscious Processing and the Global Neuronal Workspace Hypothesis. *Neuron*, 105(5), 776–798.
- **Key points**:
  - "Ignition" — a sudden, late, non-linear amplification with frontoparietal involvement — is the proposed common signature of conscious access regardless of input modality.
  - Conscious access = global *broadcast* making content available to many cognitive modules (report, memory, reasoning).
  - This is, in the Map's vocabulary, a brain-side account of the very "flexible deployment" the capability-division template assigns to the mind side.
- **Tenet alignment**: RIVAL. GNW is the strongest physicalist competitor to the interface reading of the cross-modal division: it explains modality-general flexible deployment without consciousness as a non-physical contributor. Must be engaged as the rival the structural claim has to out-accommodate (parallels how the per-modality articles treat enactivism/predictive processing).

### The Complex Interplay Between Multisensory Integration and Perceptual Awareness
- **URL**: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27795942/
- **Type**: Review (Deroy, Chen, Spence — Phil. Trans. R. Soc. B, 2014; PubMed record)
- **Key points**:
  - Multisensory integration and perceptual awareness are *doubly dissociable*: some cross-modal integration occurs without awareness; awareness can occur without integration.
  - Cautions against assuming integration is a sufficient marker of consciousness — relevant to tempering any "binding = mind-side everywhere" claim.
- **Tenet alignment**: NEUTRAL — a disanalogy source. Useful for the honest "where the division does NOT cleanly recur" section.

### The function of consciousness in multisensory integration
- **URL**: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0010027712001837
- **Type**: Paper (Cognition, 2012)
- **Cite**: Palmer, T. D. & Ramsey, A. K. (2012). The function of consciousness in multisensory integration. *Cognition*, 125(3), 353–364.
- **Key points**:
  - Awareness of a cue is necessary for certain multisensory bindings to influence behaviour (e.g. some sound-induced visual effects require conscious access to the binding cue).
  - Supports the mind-side-does-flexible-cross-modal-work reading, but partially — the dependence is task-specific.
- **Tenet alignment**: NEUTRAL-to-CONSONANT, with caveats.

### Perceptual organization and visual awareness: the case of amodal completion
- **URL**: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2023.1201681/full (PMC: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10470034/)
- **Type**: Paper (Frontiers in Psychology, 2023)
- **Key points**:
  - *Local* completion (contour continuation) can proceed without visual awareness; *global* completion (gestalt symmetry/regularity) appears to require awareness.
  - This is the vision-side echo of the Map's own perceptual-organisation threshold (Ludwig 2023 in capability-division-in-vision) — but it shows the threshold is internally graded, not a clean line, which complicates a uniform cross-modal recurrence claim.
- **Tenet alignment**: NEUTRAL — disanalogy/nuance source. Shows the conscious/unconscious boundary is graded *within* a modality, so "the same division recurs" needs the qualifier "the same graded transition, not a uniform cut."

## Major Positions

### Global Neuronal Workspace (GNW) — the modality-general broadcast account
- **Proponents**: Dehaene, Changeux, Mashour, Roelfsema; supported empirically by Sanchez et al.'s supramodal decoding.
- **Core claim**: Conscious access is a single, modality-general mechanism — late ignition + frontoparietal broadcast — that operates the same way whatever the input sense. Modality-specific cortex does the feature extraction; the workspace does the (modality-general) making-available.
- **Key arguments**: cross-modal decoding of conscious perception; ignition signatures common to vision/audition/touch; broadcast explains report, working-memory maintenance, and flexible use.
- **Relation to site tenets**: This is the **physicalist twin** of the Map's capability division. GNW assigns exactly the same labour split (modality-specific extraction vs. modality-general flexible availability) but reads the second term as a neural broadcast, not a mind-side contribution. The structural claim's whole burden is that this twin exists; the Map's value-add is the *ownership* term GNW under-describes (broadcast explains availability-to-the-system, not availability-*to-someone*).

### Interface / capability-division reading (the Map)
- **Proponents**: The Unfinishable Map (interactionist dualism).
- **Core claim**: Brain-side computation (modality-specific) delivers organised candidate content; the mind-side contributes phenomenal unification, ownership, and flexible deployment. If this division recurs across modalities, it is structural rather than an artefact of vision.
- **Key arguments**: the five per-modality articles; the recurrence of "experienced unity = mind-side" across audition (scene analysis), touch (feeling one object), chemosensation (flavour binding).
- **Relation to site tenets**: Tenet 1 (division is ontological, not merely epistemic); Tenet 3 (mind side is causally non-empty); Tenet 2 (why the boundary resists precise location — below current neuroscience resolution). The supramodal-signature finding is *consonant but not probative*: a common conscious/unconscious boundary across senses is what the interface reading would expect, but GNW predicts the same datum.

### Multisensory-integration-as-marker view (and its critics)
- **Proponents**: integration-information theorists (integration as hallmark of consciousness); critics Deroy/Chen/Spence, Palmer/Ramsey.
- **Core claim (and rebuttal)**: integration ≈ consciousness — rebutted by the double dissociation (integration without awareness; awareness without integration).
- **Relation to site tenets**: cautionary. The Map should NOT claim "binding is the mind-side contribution in every modality" without the dissociation caveat; otherwise the structural claim over-reaches exactly where the literature is most equivocal.

## Key Debates

### Is the conscious/unconscious boundary one mechanism or many?
- **Sides**: GNW/supramodal-signature (one modality-general mechanism) vs. modality-specific / local-recurrence theories (e.g. recurrent processing within sensory cortex, where the boundary is partly modality-bound).
- **Core disagreement**: whether the "mind-side" transition is a single supramodal event (Sanchez/Mashour) or a family of modality-local transitions that merely look alike.
- **Current state**: Ongoing. Sanchez 2020 is strong evidence for a supramodal *signature*, but a shared decodable signature is compatible with a common downstream broadcast *of* modality-specific events — it does not by itself prove a single upstream mechanism.

### Does binding require consciousness across all modalities?
- **Sides**: integration-as-marker vs. the double-dissociation camp (Deroy et al.).
- **Core disagreement**: whether the "experienced unity = mind-side" move the Map makes for audition/touch/flavour generalises, given unconscious cross-modal integration.
- **Current state**: dissociable — so the recurrence claim must be stated as "phenomenal unity tracks awareness" not "all integration requires awareness."

## Gaps in Research

- Could not (in this pass) find a paper that frames the cross-modal conscious/unconscious split using the explicit "brain-side capability vs. mind-side capability" vocabulary — that framing is the Map's own. The literature speaks of "supramodal signatures" and "global workspace," which the synthesis must translate into capability-division terms (and flag the translation honestly).
- The proprioceptive/interoceptive case is under-represented in the *cross-modal-decoding* literature (Sanchez uses vision/audition/touch). Whether the supramodal signature extends to proprioception/interoception is an open empirical question — the apex should not assert it.
- The *ownership* term (vs. mere access) is exactly where GNW is weakest and the Map is strongest, but I did not find direct cross-modal-ownership experiments; this is a genuine gap and an opportunity, not a settled result.
