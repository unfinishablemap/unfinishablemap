## Executive Summary

Wilfrid Sellars (1912–1989) framed the central task of philosophy as fusing two rival total conceptions of "man-in-the-world" into a single synoptic view: the **manifest image** (the framework of persons, perceptible objects, and irreducible normativity in which man first came to be aware of himself as man) and the **scientific image** (the framework of postulated theoretical entities — the austere particulate ontology of physics). His term for the successful fusion is a **stereoscopic vision**. Sellars was a scientific realist: his *scientia mensura* thesis privileges science as the measure of what exists in the dimension of describing and explaining, yet he held that the manifest image's normative and personal dimensions (the "space of reasons") cannot simply be replaced. His own **grain problem** — that the smooth, ultimately homogeneous pink of a pink ice cube resists identification with any grainy, particulate physical structure — is a proto-hard-problem that pushed his late metaphysics toward *sensa* / pure processes. For the Map, the manifest image's irreducible conscious person and the grain problem are congenial, but Sellars' ultimate scientific-realist endpoint (naturalizing the manifest image, letting science measure being) is exactly what the Map's dualism and its limits-of-Occam tenet resist. Sellars is best presented as a load-bearing meta-framework the corpus assumes but never defines — not as a Map ally.

## Major Positions

### The Manifest Image
- **Core claim**: The refined framework in which man first encountered himself *as* man — the framework of persons, perceptible objects, and irreducible normativity. It is a *correlational and categorial refinement* of a prehistoric "original image," disciplined by correlational induction. Critically, it is NOT naive folk belief and NOT unscientific: Sellars stresses it is "in an appropriate sense itself a scientific image," critical and methodical, just restricted to observable entities and properties.
- **Relation to site tenets**: The manifest image houses the conscious *person* — the locus of experience and reasons the Map's dualism (Tenet 1) treats as non-reducible. This is the strongest point of contact.

### The Scientific Image
- **Core claim**: The framework of *postulated / theoretical* entities — the austere ontology of physics (particles, fields, quantum transitions). It is not a mere abstraction from the manifest image; it is a distinct, methodologically autonomous construction that claims to describe what there really is.
- **Relation to site tenets**: When taken as the final measure of being (below), it embodies the physicalist endpoint Tenets 1 and 5 resist.

### Scientia Mensura (Sellars' scientific realism)
- **Proponents**: Sellars; kin to Quine's naturalism.
- **Core claim**: In the dimension of describing and explaining the world, science is the measure of what exists and what does not. The manifest image, though practically and normatively indispensable, does not have the last word on *being*.
- **Key qualifier Sellars insists on**: The thesis is restricted to *description and explanation*; it does not license eliminating the normative/prescriptive dimension (the space of reasons, community intentions).
- **Relation to site tenets**: Direct conflict. This is the load-bearing reason Sellars must be presented framework-relative, not as an ally. He grants the manifest person its due only to *naturalize* it; the Map takes the irreducibility as ontological.

### Sensa / Pure Processes (late Sellars)
- **Core claim**: To honor the grain problem without eliminating sensory qualities, Sellars posits *sensa* — causally efficacious space-time items that are "physical-1" (real, in the world) but "not physical-2" (not governed by the mechanistic laws adequate to non-living matter). His latest metaphysics pushes toward a pure-process ontology in which sensory qualities are primitive.
- **Relation to site tenets**: A near-miss ally. Sellars stretches the scientific image to *accommodate* homogeneous quality rather than granting a second substance — a monist naturalization, not dualism. The Map can enlist the *diagnosis* (grain problem) while declining the *cure* (sensa within an expanded physics).

## Key Debates

### Can the two images be fused — and on whose terms?
- **Sides**: Reductive/eliminativist readings (the scientific image simply supersedes the manifest) vs. Sellars' own "stereoscopic" synthesis (science measures being; the manifest supplies the framework of persons and norms that cannot be dropped) vs. left-Sellarsian readings (Brandom, McDowell) emphasizing the autonomy of the space of reasons.
- **Core disagreement**: Whether normativity and the person survive the fusion as *irreducible* or as *naturalized-but-retained*.
- **Current state**: Ongoing and generative; the "right vs. left" Sellarsians divide over exactly this.

### The grain problem as a live objection to physicalism
- **Sides**: Sellars/Lockwood (homogeneous quality is not identical to particulate structure) vs. reductive physicalists (grain dissolves under the right relocation story, e.g. Rosenthal's quality-space account).
- **Core disagreement**: Whether ultimate homogeneity is a genuine metaphysical constraint or an artifact of manifest-image description.
- **Current state**: Unresolved; a recognized node in the phenomenal-quality / hard-problem literature.
