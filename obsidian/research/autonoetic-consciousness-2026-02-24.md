## Executive Summary

Autonoetic consciousness — the self-knowing awareness that enables mental time travel and the felt sense that a remembered event belongs to one's personal past — is one of the most philosophically rich constructs in memory science. Proposed by Endel Tulving in 1985, it sits atop his tripartite hierarchy (anoetic → noetic → autonoetic) and is tightly linked to episodic memory, personal identity, and the phenomenology of temporal experience. Current philosophical debate centers on whether autonoesis is *constitutive* of episodic memory or merely *contingent* upon retrieval processes. The concept bears directly on several site tenets: its irreducibility to functional description supports dualism, its role in mental causation (shaping future behavior through temporal self-projection) supports bidirectional interaction, and the unresolved question of whether it is uniquely human provides evidence that consciousness is not epiphenomenal.

## Key Sources

### Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy — Memory
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/memory/
- **Type**: Encyclopedia
- **Key points**:
  - Autonoesis is "consciousness of the self in subjective time — which can be roughly described as a feeling of mentally travelling through time to reexperience an event" (Tulving 1985b)
  - Dokic (2014) proposes a related "episodic feeling of knowing" — the sense that one's memory originates in one's experience
  - Klein (2015) argues compellingly for treating autonoesis as a criterion distinguishing episodic memory
  - Some evidence of intact episodic memory despite impaired phenomenology challenges the necessity claim
- **Tenet alignment**: Supports Dualism — autonoetic phenomenology resists functional reduction
- **Quote**: "Memory is indeed intimately linked to the self"

### Sant'Anna, Michaelian & Andonovski (2024) — Autonoesis and Episodicity
- **URL**: https://wires.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/wcs.1665
- **Type**: Review article (WIREs Cognitive Science)
- **Key points**:
  - Distinguishes three philosophical approaches to autonoesis: representational, metacognitive, and epistemic
  - Representational accounts ground autonoesis in the nature of episodic memory representations
  - Metacognitive accounts explain it via episodic feeling of knowing or feeling of pastness
  - Epistemic accounts treat episodic memory as a retained epistemic state
  - Representational and epistemic accounts explain immediacy but struggle with source; metacognitive accounts explain source but may miss immediacy
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral — framework-level analysis rather than ontological commitment
- **Quote**: Representational and epistemic accounts "seem to explain the immediacy of autonoesis but struggle to explain its source"

### De Brigard (2024) — Episodic Memory Without Autonoetic Consciousness
- **URL**: https://royalsocietypublishing.org/doi/10.1098/rstb.2023.0410
- **Type**: Journal article (Phil Trans R Soc B)
- **Key points**:
  - Argues autonoetic consciousness as a construct lacks validity
  - Claims even if valid, it would not be necessary for episodic memory
  - Proposes functional/computational characterization where phenomenology is contingent
  - Suggests strategies for evaluating within- and between-individual variability in conscious experience of memories
- **Tenet alignment**: Conflicts with Dualism — attempts to deflate phenomenological significance of autonoesis. The site perspective would argue De Brigard's "functional" replacement is precisely the kind of reductive move that loses what matters about memory experience.

### Klein (2016) — Autonoetic Consciousness: Reconsidering Episodic Memory in Future-Oriented Self-Projection
- **URL**: https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1080/17470218.2015.1007150
- **Type**: Journal article
- **Key points**:
  - The autonoetic component (not episodic memory per se) is the causally determinative factor enabling future self-projection
  - Memory entails a direct, non-inferential feeling of re-acquaintance with one's past
  - Evolutionary perspective: memory exists not to relive the past but to anticipate and plan for future contingencies
  - Even without episodic memory, semantic self-referential memory can support some future-oriented mental time travel
  - Distinguishes sharply between content (what is remembered) and manner of experiencing (autonoetic awareness during retrieval)
- **Tenet alignment**: Supports Bidirectional Interaction — autonoesis enables causal influence on future behavior through temporal self-projection
- **Quote**: "Memory has been designed by natural selection not to relive the past, but rather to anticipate and plan for future contingencies"

### Tulving (1985) — Memory and Consciousness
- **URL**: (original paper; referenced across sources)
- **Type**: Foundational paper
- **Key points**:
  - Three memory systems correspond to three types of consciousness: procedural/anoetic, semantic/noetic, episodic/autonoetic
  - Anoetic: bound to current situation, perceptual registration
  - Noetic: awareness of relationships, symbolic representations from the past
  - Autonoetic: uniquely involves re-experiencing, mental time travel, and self-referential temporal awareness
  - Autonoetic consciousness is "strongly dependent on episodic long-term memory"
- **Tenet alignment**: Supports Dualism — the qualitative hierarchy (anoetic → noetic → autonoetic) is a gradient of phenomenal richness that resists reduction to information processing
- **Quote**: Autonoetic consciousness is the "unique ability to not only know that an event happened, but also to mentally travel back in time to re-experience that event in one's mind"

### Suddendorf & Corballis — Mental Time Travel Hypothesis
- **URL**: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/279609985_Episodic_Memory_and_Autonoesis_Uniquely_Human
- **Type**: Multiple papers (2007 and following)
- **Key points**:
  - The Bischof-Kohler hypothesis: animals lack a concept of time as a continuum and a continuous self through time
  - Capacity for mental time travel and explicit false-belief understanding likely unique to humans
  - Great apes may possess noetic consciousness but not autonoetic awareness
  - Debate remains unresolved after 15+ years — some researchers now accept mental time travel may precede human evolution
- **Tenet alignment**: Supports Bidirectional Interaction — if autonoetic consciousness is uniquely human (or nearly so), and it enables the unique cognitive capacities humans possess (logical reasoning, counterfactual thought, cumulative culture), this parallels the baseline cognition argument already on the site

### Neuroscience of Autonoetic Consciousness (Functional Neuroanatomy)
- **URL**: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0028393220301482
- **Type**: Meta-analysis (Neuropsychologia, 2020)
- **Key points**:
  - Key brain regions: medial prefrontal cortex, precuneus, retrosplenial cortex, temporoparietal junction, medial temporal lobe
  - Strong overlap with default mode network activations
  - Parietal cortex critically involved; damage can alter personality
  - Past-directed and future-directed mental time travel share common neural substrates
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral — identifies neural correlates without resolving whether they are sufficient for the phenomenology

### Panksepp & Northoff (2009) — The Flow of Anoetic to Noetic and Autonoetic Consciousness
- **URL**: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19713132/
- **Type**: Journal article
- **Key points**:
  - Consciousness as a continuum from anoetic → noetic → autonoetic
  - Primary-process anoetic consciousness provides the raw "felt" dimension
  - The representational contents of anoetic and noetic states determine the phenomenal character of autonoetic awareness
  - Gradual distinction from rudimentary "unknowing consciousness" toward self-aware autonoetic levels
- **Tenet alignment**: Supports Dualism — a phenomenological continuum is more naturally explained by dualist frameworks than by physicalism, which must explain why each level "feels" qualitatively different

## Major Positions

### Autonoesis as Constitutive of Episodic Memory (Orthodox View)
- **Proponents**: Tulving (1985, 2002), Klein (2015, 2016)
- **Core claim**: Autonoetic consciousness is not merely an accompaniment of episodic memory but its defining phenomenological feature. Without the felt sense of re-experiencing, a memory is semantic, not episodic.
- **Key arguments**: The first-person, temporally extended self-awareness that characterizes episodic recall is what makes it episodic; strip it away and you have factual knowledge about the past, not genuine remembering.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Strongly supports Dualism. If autonoesis is constitutive rather than contingent, then episodic memory necessarily involves irreducible phenomenology — functional equivalents that lack the felt quality would not count as episodic memory.

### Autonoesis as Contingent (Deflationary View)
- **Proponents**: De Brigard (2024), Michaelian (to some degree)
- **Core claim**: Autonoetic consciousness is not a valid construct, or at minimum is not necessary for episodic memory. Episodic memory should be characterized functionally/computationally, with phenomenology as a contingent feature of retrieval.
- **Key arguments**: Evidence of intact episodic memory with impaired phenomenology; within-individual variability in autonoetic experience; difficulty validating autonoesis as a distinct construct.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Conflicts with Dualism. This deflationary move parallels illusionism about qualia — it tries to explain away the phenomenological dimension rather than engaging with its irreducibility. The site would argue this is precisely the mistake materialism makes with consciousness generally.

### Three Philosophical Frameworks for Autonoesis (Sant'Anna et al. 2024)
- **Proponents**: Sant'Anna, Michaelian, Andonovski
- **Core claim**: Autonoesis can be understood through three philosophical lenses — representational (content of representations), metacognitive (monitoring states during retrieval), or epistemic (retained knowledge states).
- **Key arguments**: Each framework captures some features of autonoesis but fails on others: representational and epistemic accounts explain immediacy but not source; metacognitive accounts explain source but not immediacy.
- **Relation to site tenets**: The fact that no purely functional/representational framework fully captures autonoesis supports the Map's position that phenomenal consciousness resists reduction.

### Autonoesis as Uniquely Human (Suddendorf-Corballis)
- **Proponents**: Suddendorf, Corballis, Tulving
- **Core claim**: Mental time travel and autonoetic consciousness are unique to humans (or very nearly so). Great apes may have noetic consciousness but lack autonoetic temporal self-projection.
- **Key arguments**: Bischof-Kohler hypothesis — animals lack a continuous temporal self-concept; human-unique capacities (logical reasoning, counterfactual thinking, cumulative culture) correlate with autonoetic consciousness.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Strongly supports Bidirectional Interaction — if autonoetic consciousness enables unique cognitive capacities that unconscious processing cannot achieve, consciousness is causally efficacious, not epiphenomenal. Directly parallels the baseline cognition argument.

## Key Debates

### Is Autonoetic Consciousness Necessary for Episodic Memory?
- **Sides**: Tulving/Klein (yes, constitutive) vs. De Brigard/Michaelian (no, contingent)
- **Core disagreement**: Whether the felt quality of re-experiencing defines episodic memory or is merely a typical but dispensable accompaniment
- **Current state**: Actively debated. De Brigard's 2024 paper in Phil Trans R Soc B is a major recent challenge. No consensus.

### Is Autonoetic Consciousness Uniquely Human?
- **Sides**: Suddendorf/Corballis/Tulving (yes) vs. various comparative cognition researchers (no, or at least not clearly)
- **Core disagreement**: Whether animal behaviors (scrub-jay caching, great ape planning) demonstrate genuine mental time travel or simpler associative processes
- **Current state**: Unresolved after 15+ years. Some researchers now accept mental time travel may precede human evolution; Suddendorf maintains dissenting position.

### What Is the Source of Autonoetic Phenomenology?
- **Sides**: Representational accounts (content-based) vs. metacognitive accounts (monitoring-based) vs. epistemic accounts (knowledge-based)
- **Core disagreement**: Whether autonoetic experience derives from the nature of memory representations, from metacognitive monitoring during retrieval, or from an epistemic relation to past events
- **Current state**: Active philosophical research. Each framework captures something but none is complete.

### Autonoesis and the Hard Problem
- **Sides**: Those who see autonoesis as raising a specific instance of the hard problem vs. those who see it as tractable via cognitive/functional analysis
- **Core disagreement**: Whether the felt quality of mental time travel — the experiential "thereness" of the past — is reducible to information processing
- **Current state**: Implicitly connected to the hard problem but rarely discussed directly in these terms. An opportunity for the Map.
