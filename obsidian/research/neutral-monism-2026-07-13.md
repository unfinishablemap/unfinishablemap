## Executive Summary

Neutral monism holds that ultimate reality consists of entities of one fundamental kind that are intrinsically *neither mental nor physical*; minds and material objects are alike constructions out of (or groupings of) these neutral entities. Its classical trio is Ernst Mach (elements/sensations, 1886), William James (pure experience, 1904/1912), and Bertrand Russell (sensations and images, adopted ~1918, canonical statement in *The Analysis of Mind*, 1921). It is the third monist rival to the Map's dualism — neither physicalism (matter fundamental) nor idealism (mind fundamental) — and the one the Map's Tenet 1 most owes a demarcation against, because it claims to dissolve the interaction problem rather than solve it. The strongest current objections are the neutrality-specification problem (five incompatible readings of "neutral"), the mentalism suspicion (the classical "neutral" entities look paradigmatically mental), and Chalmers's quality/awareness gap (no structure of neutral qualities necessitates awareness of them). The view has revived as an active research programme via Banks (2014), Nagel (2012), Chalmers's panprotopsychism/panqualityism, and Coleman's panqualityism.

## Key Sources

### Stubenberg & Wishon, "Neutral Monism" (SEP)
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/neutral-monism/
- **Type**: Encyclopedia (first published 2005-02-03; substantive revision 2023-01-31; authors Leopold Stubenberg and Donovan Wishon)
- **Key points**:
  - Five competing readings of "neutral": Neither View (intrinsically neither mental nor physical), Actual Constituent View, Possible Constituent View, Law View (both mental and physical laws apply), Both View. Classical neutral monists favoured the Neither View.
  - Mach's neutral entities are "elements" (colours, sounds, pressures): the same element counted in one functional context is physical, in another psychological. "A color is a physical object... When we consider its dependence upon the retina... it is a psychological object, a sensation" (Mach 1886: 17–18).
  - Russell adopted neutral monism in 1918 after earlier rejecting it, and stayed with it for life. In *The Analysis of Mind* (1921) the known neutral entities are sensations and images; he abandons the act–object analysis of experience: "the patch and our sensation are identical" (Russell 1921: 142–3, paraphrased in SEP).
  - By *The Analysis of Matter* (1927) events replace sensations, physics gives only the "causal skeleton of the world," and the epistemic-structuralist strand emerges that later feeds Russellian monism.
  - Treats contemporary Russellian monism as a *development from* neutral monism, not identical: Russellian monism has physicalist, panpsychist, idealist, AND neutral variants; only the neutral variants (panprotopsychism, panqualityism) inherit the Neither View.
- **Tenet alignment**: Conflicts with Tenet 1 (Dualism) — it is the principal position claiming the mental/physical divide is a construction. Useful to the Map on Tenet 5 (Occam's limits): its own history shows "one neutral stuff" parsimony generating unresolved construction problems.
- **Quote**: "We know certain logical characteristics of [the world's] structure, but nothing of its intrinsic character" (Russell 1927, *An Outline of Philosophy* material quoted in SEP).

### James, "Does 'Consciousness' Exist?" (1904)
- **URL**: https://philpapers.org/rec/JAMDCE ; full text at http://fair-use.org/william-james/essays-in-radical-empiricism/does-consciousness-exist
- **Type**: Journal article — *The Journal of Philosophy, Psychology and Scientific Methods* 1(18): 477–491 (issue of 1 September 1904). Reprinted posthumously in *Essays in Radical Empiricism* (1912). Publisher-verified.
- **Key points**:
  - Consciousness is not an entity but a *function* (knowing); James denies the Cartesian "thinking substance."
  - The world's primal stuff is "pure experience" — the instant field of the present, "plain, unqualified actuality" — which counts as mental or physical only by the relational context it enters: the same experience taken with one group of associates is a thought, with another is a thing.
  - In perception, perceiver and perceived are one undivided bit of pure experience counted twice.
- **Tenet alignment**: Conflicts with Tenet 1. Note internal corpus tension already flagged in topics/pragmatist-quantum-foundations-and-the-agent.md: the Map leans on the earlier pluralist James while late James drifts to neutral monism.
- **Quote**: James answers his title question "in the negative" — consciousness as entity "is the name of a nonentity"; it stands for a function, not a stuff.

### Russell, *The Analysis of Mind* (1921)
- **URL**: https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/The_Analysis_of_Mind ; https://www.gutenberg.org/files/2529/2529-h/2529-h.htm
- **Type**: Book — George Allen & Unwin (London) / The Macmillan Company (New York), 1921. Publisher-verified (contemporary IJP review lists both imprints, 310 pp.).
- **Key points**:
  - Canonical statement of Russell's early neutral monism: mind and matter are both logical constructions out of transient particulars (sensations neutral; images classed on the mental side — Russell's neutrality was never pure, a standard criticism).
  - The difference between psychology and physics is one of *causal laws*, not of stuff: the same sensation obeys physical laws as a member of one series and psychological laws (mnemic causation) as a member of another.
  - Explicitly credits Mach's *Analysis of Sensations* as fundamentally important, and follows James's rejection of consciousness-as-entity.
- **Tenet alignment**: Conflicts with Tenets 1 and 3 — no irreducible mental substance, and nothing for consciousness to *do* causally beyond what the neutral causal lines already do.

### Mach, *The Analysis of Sensations* (1886)
- **URL**: via SEP bibliography; German original *Die Analyse der Empfindungen* (1886, expanded editions to 1905)
- **Type**: Book
- **Key points**: World-elements doctrine; the ego and material objects are both "provisional fictions of the same kind" (Mach 1905 preface material, quoted in SEP); anti-metaphysical, economy-of-thought motivation from physics.
- **Tenet alignment**: Conflicts with Tenet 1; historically instructive for Tenet 5 — Mach's parsimony drive is the clearest case of Occam-style reasoning producing the neutral base.

### Banks, *The Realistic Empiricism of Mach, James, and Russell: Neutral Monism Reconceived* (2014)
- **URL**: https://philpapers.org/rec/BANREI ; NDPR review https://ndpr.nd.edu/reviews/the-realistic-empiricism-of-mach-james-and-russell-neutral-monism-reconceived/
- **Type**: Book — Cambridge University Press, 2014. Subtitle publisher-verified as "Reconceived" (one search snippet mis-rendered it "Reconsidered" — corrected against CUP/PhilPapers/NDPR).
- **Key points**:
  - First unified reconstruction of the trio as one movement ("realistic empiricism"); event-based neutral monism inside an "enhanced physicalism": events manifest intrinsic natural powers, and the same neural power manifests as both a neuron firing and a blue sensation — "different manifestations of the same natural powers" (Banks 2014: 164, via SEP).
- **Tenet alignment**: Conflicts with Tenets 1–3; closest contemporary neutral monism to physicalism, so a useful contrast case for demarcation.

### Stubenberg & Wishon SEP objections section + Mihálik (2022)
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/neutral-monism/ ; https://doi.org/10.1007/s10670-020-00256-x
- **Type**: Encyclopedia section + journal article — Mihálik, J. (2022). "Panqualityism, Awareness and the Explanatory Gap." *Erkenntnis* 87: 1423–1445. Publisher-verified (Springer).
- **Key points** (objection inventory):
  - **Neutrality specification problem**: the five readings disagree; readings 2–4 threaten collapse into materialism or idealism.
  - **Mentalism suspicion** (most frequent historical objection): sensations, pure experience, and percepts look paradigmatically mental — the view as rebranded phenomenalism/panpsychism.
  - **Materialism suspicion** (contemporary): late Russell arguably treats all events as physical; "neutrality" becomes merely epistemic.
  - **Quality/awareness gap** (Chalmers): "no instantiations of qualities ever necessitate awareness of qualities" — the neutral base can supply qualities but not the awareness *of* them. Coleman's higher-order-quotational panqualityism is the main reply; Mihálik argues the reply still faces an explanatory gap for "strong awareness."
  - **Dualism suspicion**: physics gives extrinsic relations, intrinsic qualities show up only in experience — two irreducible aspects, i.e., property dualism disguised as monism. (Direct analogue of Cutter's instability argument against Russellian monism already in the corpus.)
- **Tenet alignment**: The dualism-suspicion objection is the Map's natural wedge — it argues neutral monism is *unstable* and resolves into the dualism the Map holds explicitly.

## Major Positions

### Classical neutral monism (Mach / James / early-1920s Russell)
- **Proponents**: Ernst Mach, William James, Bertrand Russell (from ~1918); precursors and fellow-travellers per SEP include aspects of Hume and the American New Realists (Perry, Holt)
- **Core claim**: One kind of basic entity, intrinsically neither mental nor physical; "mental" and "physical" mark two ways of grouping the same entities under different causal/functional laws
- **Key arguments**: Parsimony (one stuff, no interaction problem); epistemological directness (we start from what is given, prior to the mind/matter sorting); dissolution rather than solution of the mind–body problem
- **Relation to site tenets**: Denies Tenet 1 head-on. Undercuts Tenets 2–3 by leaving consciousness nothing to do — there is no mental term to exert influence at quantum outcomes; causation runs entirely along neutral lines. Tenet 5 cuts against it: its parsimony is bought with unresolved construction problems (how neutral entities compose minds), mirroring the combination problem the corpus already presses on panpsychism.

### Contemporary neutral monism (Neo-Russellian neutral variants)
- **Proponents**: Kenneth Sayre (information as the neutral base, 1976); Thomas Nagel ("transphysical and transmental" constituents, *Mind and Cosmos* 2012); Erik Banks (2014); David Chalmers (panprotopsychism as an option, 2013/2015); Sam Coleman (panqualityism)
- **Core claim**: The neutral base is respecified — information (Sayre), powers/events (Banks), unexperienced qualities (Coleman's panqualityism), protophenomenal properties (Chalmers) — to evade the mentalism suspicion
- **Key arguments**: Keeps the anti-physicalist insight (physics is structural) without panpsychism's micro-subjects; panqualityism dodges the combination problem for *subjects* by denying micro-awareness
- **Relation to site tenets**: Sayre's information-monism is exactly the collapse risk concepts/metaphysics-of-information-under-dualism.md guards against (its lines 93 and 133) — the Map holds information is a *relation between* two irreducible domains, not a neutral *substance beneath* them. Chalmers's quality/awareness gap is the Map-friendly rebuttal: qualities without awareness never add up to experience.

### The demarcation: neutral monism vs Russellian monism vs the Map
- Neutral monism proper: base is *neither* mental nor physical (Neither View); mind and matter are constructions. Predates and feeds Russellian monism.
- Russellian monism (existing corpus page): quiddities ground consciousness AND serve as categorical bases of physical dispositions; most variants make the base (proto)phenomenal — i.e., NOT neutral. Only its panprotopsychist/panqualityist wing remains genuinely neutral.
- The Map: two irreducible aspects that causally interact at quantum indeterminacies. Against neutral monism it should press: (a) the neutrality of the base is either unstable (dualism suspicion) or covert mentalism/physicalism; (b) a neutral base gives consciousness no causal role, whereas Tenets 2–3 require one; (c) like Russellian monism, neutral monism is quantum-interpretation-neutral (nothing must happen at measurement), so Tenet 4's many-worlds rejection marks a real divergence, not a notational one.

## Key Debates

### Is anything genuinely neutral?
- **Sides**: Classical trio (yes, the given is pre-categorial) vs critics from both directions — idealists/phenomenalism-diagnosers (the base is mental) and physicalists (late Russell's events are just physical)
- **Core disagreement**: Whether "neither mental nor physical" can be given positive content without collapsing into one side
- **Current state**: Ongoing; SEP's five-views taxonomy is the standard framing; no consensus resolution

### Can qualities yield awareness? (quality/awareness gap)
- **Sides**: Chalmers (no structure of qualities necessitates awareness) vs Coleman (quotational higher-order account of awareness) vs Mihálik 2022 (Coleman's reply leaves a residual gap for strong awareness)
- **Core disagreement**: Whether awareness is reducible to arrangements of unexperienced qualities
- **Current state**: Active 2020s literature (Mihálik 2022; first-order representationalist panqualityism, *Erkenntnis* 2023; Shani's critique of panqualityism)

### Construction vs emergence
- **Sides**: Constructionists (minds are logical constructions from neutral entities) vs Strawson-style critics (radical emergence of experience from the non-experiential is unintelligible)
- **Core disagreement**: Whether the mind-from-neutral-base step is intelligible without either smuggling in mentality or invoking brute emergence
- **Current state**: Ongoing; structurally parallel to the combination problem the corpus already covers for panpsychism
