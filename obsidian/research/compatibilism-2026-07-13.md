# Research: Compatibilism as a Positive Theory of Free Will

**Date**: 2026-07-13
**Search queries used**: Frankfurt 1971 Journal of Philosophy citation; Fischer & Ravizza 1998 guidance control; Strawson 1962 Freedom and Resentment Proceedings; Ayer 1954 Freedom and Necessity Philosophical Essays; Vihvelin 2013 Causes Laws and Free Will dispositions; Christian List 2019 Why Free Will Is Real Harvard; Dennett Elbow Room / Freedom Evolves; PhilPapers 2020 survey compatibilism percentage.

## Executive Summary

Compatibilism holds that free will and moral responsibility are compatible with the truth of causal determinism. It is not a single doctrine but a family: classical conditional-analysis compatibilism (Hobbes, Hume, Ayer), hierarchical/mesh theories (Frankfurt), reasons-responsiveness and semicompatibilism (Fischer & Ravizza), the new dispositionalism (Vihvelin, Fara), neo-compatibilist emergentism (Dennett, List), and the Strawsonian grounding of responsibility in the reactive attitudes. Its unifying move is to reinterpret "could have done otherwise" so that it does not require the libertarian's categorical, same-past, same-laws alternative — most classically as a conditional ("would have, had one chosen otherwise"), or to bypass alternatives entirely and locate freedom in the actual-sequence structure of the will. In the 2020 PhilPapers survey it is by a wide margin the majority position among professional philosophers (~59% accept or lean toward it). For the Map this is the sophisticated rival its whole libertarian-interactionist agency claim is defined against: compatibilism largely shares the Map's moral conclusions (responsibility is real, blame is warranted) while denying the metaphysics — categorical leeway — that the Map's Tenet 3 quantum interface is built to make real.

## Key Sources

### Frankfurt, "Freedom of the Will and the Concept of a Person" (1971)
- **URL**: https://www.pdcnet.org/jphil/content/jphil_1971_0068_0001_0005_0020 ; PhilPapers https://philpapers.org/rec/FRAFOT
- **Type**: Journal article
- **Citation (VERIFIED)**: *The Journal of Philosophy* 68(1), Jan. 14 1971, pp. 5-20.
- **Key points**:
  - Free will as a *hierarchical* structure of desire: first-order desires (to do X) plus second-order volitions (to want a given first-order desire to be one's effective will).
  - A "person" (vs a "wanton") has second-order volitions and *identifies* with some first-order desires; free will is having the will one wants to have.
  - Deliberately sidesteps the ability to do otherwise: the analysis is about the structure of the actual will, not access to alternatives.
- **Tenet alignment**: Conflicts with Tenet 3 as a *complete* account — it locates freedom in mesh, not in categorical selection among quantum-open alternatives. Compatible with the Map's *phenomenology* of identification/wholeheartedness.

### Fischer & Ravizza, "Responsibility and Control" (1998)
- **URL**: https://philpapers.org/rec/FISRAC-3 ; Cambridge Core review https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/canadian-journal-of-philosophy/article/abs/john-martin-fischer-and-mark-ravizza-responsibility-and-control-a-theory-of-moral-responsibility-cambridge-cambridge-university-press-1998-pp-viii-277/F37898F15F61E278D52E8B944307F392
- **Type**: Monograph
- **Citation (VERIFIED)**: *Responsibility and Control: A Theory of Moral Responsibility*, Cambridge University Press, 1998 (Cambridge Studies in Philosophy and Law; ISBN 0521480558).
- **Key points**:
  - Moral responsibility rests on *guidance control*, not *regulative control* (which would require access to alternatives).
  - Guidance control = (a) the action issues from the agent's *own* mechanism (a "taking responsibility" ownership history) and (b) that mechanism is *moderately reasons-responsive*.
  - *Semicompatibilism*: responsibility is compatible with determinism *even if* the freedom-to-do-otherwise is not — decoupling responsibility from leeway. This is the corpus's existing Fischer subsection material.
- **Tenet alignment**: The sharpest rival framing for the Map: it concedes the Map may be right that determinism kills categorical leeway, yet denies that this touches responsibility. The Map must argue leeway is real *and* matters, not merely that responsibility survives.

### Strawson, "Freedom and Resentment" (1962)
- **URL**: British Academy https://www.thebritishacademy.ac.uk/publishing/proceedings-british-academy/proceedings-volumes-1-111/48/strawson/ ; PhilArchive intro https://philarchive.org/archive/RUSFWA
- **Type**: Lecture / journal article
- **Citation (VERIFIED, with pagination caveat)**: *Proceedings of the British Academy* vol. 48 (1962). Page range is cited two ways in the literature: pp. 1-25 (standalone/offprint pagination, widely reproduced) and pp. 187-211 (pagination within the bound Proceedings volume — the Academy's own PDF is filed as `48p187.pdf`). Read 9 May 1962. **Downstream article should cite the volume and pick one pagination consistently, noting the offprint/volume divergence rather than asserting a single range as canonical.**
- **Key points**:
  - Shifts the ground from metaphysics to the *reactive attitudes* (resentment, gratitude, indignation, guilt) that are constitutive of interpersonal life.
  - We excuse or exempt in specific cases, but a *global* suspension of the reactive attitudes on the grounds of determinism is not something we could do or have reason to do.
  - Responsibility is grounded in the practice, not read off a prior metaphysics — "optimist" vs "pessimist" reframed.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral-to-orthogonal on the metaphysics; the Map's existing `reactive-attitudes-and-strawsonian-responsibility` page covers this — cross-ref, do not duplicate.

### Ayer, "Freedom and Necessity" (1954)
- **URL**: PhilPapers https://philpapers.org/rec/AYEFAN-2 ; text https://www.finophd.eu/wp-content/uploads/2018/01/Ayer-freedom-necessity.pdf
- **Type**: Essay in collection
- **Citation (VERIFIED)**: in A. J. Ayer, *Philosophical Essays*, London: Macmillan, 1954 (the essay reprints an earlier 1946 piece). Page range varies by edition/printing (commonly cited pp. 271-284 in the Macmillan volume; the US St. Martin's issue paginates differently). **Cite the essay + volume; avoid over-committing to a single page range.**
- **Key points**:
  - Freedom is opposed to *constraint*, not to causation: an act is free if it is not compelled (external force, coercion, kleptomaniac compulsion), even if fully caused.
  - Classic statement of the *conditional analysis*: "could have done otherwise" means I *would* have done otherwise *had I chosen* to.
- **Tenet alignment**: The canonical target of the corpus's Consequence-Argument page — the conditional analysis is standardly held to collapse because the antecedent choice is itself determined. Cross-ref, do not re-derive.

### Vihvelin, "Causes, Laws, and Free Will" (2013)
- **URL**: OUP https://global.oup.com/academic/product/causes-laws-and-free-will-9780199795185 ; NDPR review https://ndpr.nd.edu/reviews/causes-laws-and-free-will-why-determinism-doesn-t-matter/
- **Type**: Monograph
- **Citation (VERIFIED)**: *Causes, Laws, and Free Will: Why Determinism Doesn't Matter*, Oxford University Press, 2013.
- **Key points**:
  - The *new dispositionalism*: free will is a *bundle of dispositions* (abilities) to respond to reasons, plus an environment with no obstacles (masks/finks) to their manifestation.
  - Revives the conditional analysis by grounding "ability to do otherwise" in dispositions, using a dispositions analysis engineered to dodge the mask/fink counterexamples that sank the old conditional analysis.
  - The book's subtitle stakes the position: determinism does not remove the relevant dispositions.
- **Tenet alignment**: Directly contests the Map's leeway claim — it argues the ability-to-do-otherwise *survives* determinism, so no quantum interface is needed to secure it. Michael Fara ("Masked Abilities and Compatibilism", 2008) is the co-canonical dispositionalist source (verify Fara details at the downstream expand step if quoted).

### Dennett, "Elbow Room" (1984) & "Freedom Evolves" (2003)
- **URL**: MIT Press https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262540421/elbow-room/ ; Freedom Evolves (Wikipedia summary) https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freedom_Evolves
- **Type**: Monographs
- **Citation (VERIFIED)**: *Elbow Room: The Varieties of Free Will Worth Wanting*, MIT Press / Bradford Books, 1984 (based on the 1983 John Locke Lectures); *Freedom Evolves*, Viking, 2003.
- **Key points**:
  - Naturalist/engineering compatibilism: the free will "worth wanting" is self-control and meaningful deliberation, not a metaphysical exemption from causation.
  - Determinism does not imply inevitability for agents who are good evasive/anticipatory systems ("avoiders can avoid" even in a deterministic world).
  - Freedom is an evolved, gradually acquired capacity, not an all-or-nothing metaphysical gift.
- **Tenet alignment**: Deflationary rival — reframes the Map's question as a confusion. The Map's reply: the "worth wanting" move quietly *redefines* the explanandum (categorical authorship) rather than delivering it.

### List, "Why Free Will Is Real" (2019)
- **URL**: Harvard UP https://www.hup.harvard.edu/books/9780674979581 ; NDPR review https://ndpr.nd.edu/reviews/why-free-will-is-real/
- **Type**: Monograph
- **Citation (VERIFIED)**: *Why Free Will Is Real*, Harvard University Press, 2019.
- **Key points**:
  - Free will as a genuine *higher-level* (psychological) phenomenon, emergent from but autonomous relative to physics — like an ecosystem or an economy.
  - Distinctively, List *retains* alternative possibilities and intentional agency as requirements, arguing they hold at the agential level even if physics is deterministic (agential-level indeterminism can coexist with physical-level determinism).
  - A bridge case: compatibilist in metaphysics of physics, yet insistent that leeway is real at the level that matters.
- **Tenet alignment**: The most interesting near-neighbor to the Map — List agrees leeway must be *real*, not merely conditional, but locates it in higher-level emergence rather than in a quantum interface. The Map should mark the shared premise (real alternatives) and the divergence (emergent autonomy vs Tenet-3 physical influence on quantum outcomes).

### PhilPapers 2020 Survey (Bourget & Chalmers)
- **URL**: Daily Nous report https://dailynous.com/2021/11/01/what-philosophers-believe-results-from-the-2020-philpapers-survey/ ; paper "Philosophers on Philosophy" (Philosophers' Imprint 23:11, 2023) https://philarchive.org/archive/BOUPOP-3v2

## Major Positions (variants of compatibilism)

### Classical / conditional-analysis compatibilism
- **Proponents**: Hobbes; Hume (freedom = the power to act according to the determinations of the will, absent external constraint — paraphrase of the *Enquiry* Sec. VIII, not a verbatim quote); Ayer 1954.
- **Core claim**: Freedom is the absence of external constraint/compulsion; "could have done otherwise" = "would have done otherwise had one chosen/willed otherwise."
- **Key weakness**: The conditional analysis is standardly taken to fail because the antecedent choice is itself determined (Consequence-Argument-style critique). *Cross-ref the corpus's consequence-argument page; do not re-derive.*

### Hierarchical / mesh (identification) theories
- **Proponents**: Frankfurt 1971; Watson's valuational variant is the standard foil (verify at expand step if cited).
- **Core claim**: Freedom is a structural harmony (mesh) between orders of desire / between what one wants and what one wants to want; the free agent *identifies* with the will that moves them.
- **Relation to corpus**: `frankfurt-hierarchical-mesh-theory-of-the-will` + `frankfurt-cases-and-the-principle-of-alternate-possibilities` already exist — this variant is a *cross-ref anchor*, not new derivation.

### Reasons-responsiveness / semicompatibilism
- **Proponents**: Fischer & Ravizza 1998.
- **Core claim**: Responsibility needs *guidance control* — an agent-owned, moderately reasons-responsive mechanism — not the ability to do otherwise. Responsibility is compatible with determinism *even granting* that leeway is not.
- **Relation to corpus**: `reasons-responsiveness` exists; the semicompatibilism decoupling is the key structural claim the Map's leeway realism must answer.

### New dispositionalism
- **Proponents**: Vihvelin 2013; Fara 2008.
- **Core claim**: Abilities are dispositions; the ability to do otherwise is a bundle of dispositions that determinism leaves intact — reviving the conditional analysis with a fink/mask-proof dispositions theory.
- **Relation to corpus**: No dedicated page; strongest *live* compatibilist challenge to the Map's "leeway must be metaphysically categorical" premise. Candidate for its own subsection or future concept page.

### Neo-compatibilist emergentism
- **Proponents**: Dennett 1984/2003; List 2019.
- **Core claim**: Free will is a real, higher-level (evolved or emergent) *capacity* — self-control, evitability, agential-level alternatives — not a fundamental-physics property.
- **Relation to corpus**: No dedicated page; List in particular is the closest rival because he too insists leeway is *real* (not conditional), differing only on its *locus* (emergence vs quantum interface).

### Strawsonian (reactive-attitudes) compatibilism
- **Proponents**: P. F. Strawson 1962; R. Jay Wallace as a normative-competence descendant (verify at expand step if cited).
- **Core claim**: Responsibility is constituted by the reactive attitudes embedded in interpersonal practice, insulated from the theoretical question of determinism.
- **Relation to corpus**: `reactive-attitudes-and-strawsonian-responsibility` already exists — cross-ref, do not duplicate.

## Key Debates (objections — situate, do not re-derive)

### The Consequence Argument (van Inwagen)
- If determinism is true, our acts are consequences of laws + the remote past, over neither of which we have any control; so we lack control over our acts. The premier *leeway-incompatibilist* threat to the conditional analysis. *Corpus page exists: `the-consequence-argument-for-incompatibilism`.*

### The Manipulation Argument (Pereboom four-case)
- A determined agent is relevantly like a covertly manipulated one; if manipulation defeats responsibility, so should ordinary determinism (a *source-incompatibilist* argument). *Corpus page exists: `the-manipulation-argument-and-hard-incompatibilism`.*

### The compatibilist-symmetry-challenge (the Map's own)
