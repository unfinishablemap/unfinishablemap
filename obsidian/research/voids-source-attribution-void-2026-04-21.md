# Research: Voids - The Source-Attribution Void

**Date**: 2026-04-21

**Voids category**: Occluded (with Unexplorable structure — the absence of phenomenal markers for source is constitutive)

## Executive Summary

Consciousness encounters its own contents *already detached from their origin*. A thought arrives and feels like mine; a belief feels reasoned-into; an intuition feels evident — yet the cognitive science of source monitoring (Johnson, Hashtroudi & Lindsay 1993), source amnesia, cryptomnesia, choice blindness (Johansson et al. 2005), priming, and Wegner's "apparent mental causation" converges on a structural finding: there are no native phenomenal tags marking where mental content came from. We *infer* origin from features (vividness, fluency, coherence with self-image), and the inference is reliably wrong in characteristic conditions. The Source-Attribution Void is the structural inability of consciousness to determine, from inside, whether a given mental content was self-generated, implanted by suggestion, primed by recent context, retrieved from forgotten exposure, or confabulated post-hoc to rationalize an outcome. This void is distinct from self-opacity (mechanism-blindness in general), spontaneous-thought-void (generation of unbidden thoughts), and the interested-party void (stakes in answers about oneself). Its specific shape: even when mechanisms were perfectly visible, the *origin tag* for any given thought is not the kind of thing introspection delivers — origin is reconstructed, not perceived. For dualism, this raises a sharp question: if consciousness is fundamental, why does it lack the most basic property a fundamental entity should have — knowing what is its own?

## Key Sources

### Source Monitoring Framework (Johnson, Hashtroudi & Lindsay 1993)

- **URL**: https://memlab.yale.edu/sites/default/files/files/1993_Johnson_Hashtroudi_Lindsay_PsychBull.pdf
- **Type**: Foundational psychological framework
- **Key points**:
  - Memories do not contain native labels specifying source. The mind *infers* origin from characteristic features of the memory trace.
  - Inferred features include: contextual attributes (spatial/temporal detail), sensory attributes (colour, pitch), semantic and emotional qualities, and traces of internal cognitive operations (reasoning effort).
  - "Reality monitoring" is the special case of distinguishing memories of internal events (imagined, dreamed, thought) from memories of external events (perceived, witnessed). The two are confused in characteristic conditions.
  - Source attribution is therefore probabilistic and inferential — not direct readout.
- **Tenet alignment**: Strong support for the claim that introspection delivers reconstructed inferences, not raw access to mind-side facts. Compatible with self-opacity and predictive-construction-void.
- **Quote**: "The origin of memories is inferred ... from characteristic features."

### Telling More Than We Can Know (Nisbett & Wilson 1977)

- **URL**: https://home.csulb.edu/~cwallis/382/readings/482/nisbett%20saying%20more.pdf
- **Type**: Landmark cognitive psychology paper
- **Key points**:
  - Subjects are sometimes unaware of the *existence of the stimulus* that influenced a response, *unaware of the existence of the response*, and unaware that the stimulus affected the response.
  - When asked to report on cognitive processes, people do not introspect — they confabulate plausible explanations using a priori implicit causal theories.
  - The phenomenology of "I chose this because X" is constructed, not perceived.
  - Most cited paper in consciousness studies, yet no successful empirical research program has continued the work — itself evidence that the territory resists ordinary methods.
- **Tenet alignment**: Direct support for occluded territory — there are stable, documented zones where introspection systematically misses what is happening.
- **Quote**: "There may be little or no direct introspective access to higher order cognitive processes."

### Choice Blindness (Johansson, Hall, Sikström & Olsson 2005)

- **URL**: https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.1111709
- **Type**: Experimental psychology paper (Science)
- **Key points**:
  - Participants chose between pairs of faces on attractiveness; the experimenter covertly switched the chosen face for the rejected one.
  - In **75% of trials, participants did not detect the switch**.
  - When asked to explain their (manipulated) choice, participants confidently produced detailed reasons for *the face they had originally rejected*.
  - The reasons were as detailed and emotionally invested as genuine reasons — there was no felt difference between explaining a choice and explaining a non-choice.
- **Tenet alignment**: Devastating for naive introspectionism; consistent with the Map's claim that consciousness reconstructs rather than reports.
- **Quote**: "Participants failed to notice conspicuous mismatches ... while nevertheless offering introspectively derived reasons for why they chose the way they did."

### Apparent Mental Causation (Wegner & Wheatley 1999; Wegner 2002)

- **URL**: http://wexler.free.fr/library/files/wegner%20(1999)%20apparent%20mental%20causation.%20sources%20of%20the%20experience%20of%20will.pdf
- **Type**: Theoretical paper grounding *The Illusion of Conscious Will*
- **Key points**:
  - Three criteria produce the *experience* of having willed an act: **priority** (thought before action), **consistency** (thought matches action), **exclusivity** (no other apparent cause).
  - When these are arranged externally — as in the I-Spy experiment where the experimenter actually moves the cursor — subjects experience having willed the action they did not perform.
  - The phenomenology of "I caused this" is therefore an inference from the same surface features used in social attribution to others — not a privileged inner perception.
  - Wegner's claim is *not* that conscious will has no causal role, but that the *experience* of will is dissociable from its actual causal status.
- **Tenet alignment**: For the Map, this is delicate. Wegner is read as an eliminativist about conscious will, but his findings are equally compatible with a view where *real* mental causation occurs but its phenomenology is mediated by reconstructive inference. The void account avoids commitment to Wegner's metaphysics.
- **Quote**: "Conscious will is experienced as a function of the priority, consistency, and exclusivity of the thought about the action."

### Cryptomnesia (Brown & Murphy 1989; reviews 2010s)

- **URL**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cryptomnesia
- **Type**: Wikipedia/review entry pointing to Brown & Murphy 1989 et al.
- **Key points**:
  - Subjects routinely "originate" ideas they had encountered before but forgotten encountering.
  - 3–9% of generated items in category-fluency tasks turn out to be plagiarized from a previous round, with the subject confident of originality.
  - Higher cognitive load at encoding increases the rate.
  - **Fluency is misattributed to self-generation**: the ease with which a thought arrives is taken as evidence it is "mine."
- **Tenet alignment**: Concrete instantiation of the void; suggests a specific *failure mode* (high-fluency content gets flagged as self-originated).
- **Quote**: "Fluency-based attribution: the brain mistakenly attributes the ease of retrieval to self-generation."

### Source Amnesia (Schacter, Harbluk & McLachlan 1984; subsequent literature)

- **URL**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Source_amnesia
- **Type**: Wikipedia entry summarizing decades of literature
- **Key points**:
  - Disconnect between semantic memory (the fact) and episodic memory (the context of acquiring the fact) is the default condition for most knowledge — most things you know, you cannot trace.
  - Source amnesia is "near-daily" and pervasive, not pathological.
  - Most beliefs operate as *orphaned content* — known to be true, with no recoverable provenance.
- **Tenet alignment**: Suggests source-orphaning is the architectural baseline, not a failure mode. The void is constitutive.
- **Quote**: "Everybody experiences source amnesia on a near daily basis, as for much of our knowledge it is important to remember the knowledge itself, rather than its source."

### Introspection Illusion (Pronin 2009)

- **URL**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Introspection_illusion
- **Type**: Theoretical synthesis
- **Key points**:
  - People over-trust the deliverances of introspection while applying behavioural inference to others.
  - This asymmetry produces predictable errors in self-attribution and other-attribution.
  - Introspection cannot access unconscious activity but does not signal its own inability — it confabulates and *feels confident*.
- **Tenet alignment**: Particularly sharp for the Map: introspection fails *without phenomenal warning*, which is the signature of a deep occluded void.
- **Quote**: "People are unaware of their unawareness."

### Priming and Awareness of Influence (Bargh and successors; Newell & Shanks 2014 critiques)

- **URL**: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S2352250X16300525
- **Type**: Cognitive science review
- **Key points**:
  - The relevant unawareness is not awareness of the prime but **awareness of the prime's influence**.
  - Subjects routinely notice the prime stimulus yet remain blind to the fact that it is shaping subsequent judgement.
  - This is the strict structural form of the void: noticing the input but not the input-as-cause.
- **Tenet alignment**: The bandwidth-asymmetry theme — vast inbound information shaping minimal outbound — is exactly the mechanism that creates source-attribution opacity.

## The Void

### Nature of the Limit

This is primarily an **occluded** void with constitutive unexplorable structure. The occlusion is built into the architecture: source tags are not encoded with content, so introspection cannot read them. The unexplorability is that *no enrichment of introspective method* could produce them — they were never there to be read. Better introspection cannot recover what the mind never marked.

The void has three layers:

1. **Constitutive layer (architectural)** — Mental content is not stored with native source tags. Source must be inferred at retrieval.
2. **Inferential layer (procedural)** — The features used for inference (vividness, fluency, narrative coherence with self-image) are weakly correlated with actual source.
3. **Confabulatory layer (defensive)** — When source cannot be reconstructed, the mind silently *generates* a plausible source, with no phenomenal flag distinguishing reconstruction from confabulation.

### Evidence for the Limit

This is among the better-supported voids empirically:

- Source monitoring framework: 30+ years of convergent experimental work
- Choice blindness: ~75% non-detection rate at the seam between intention and outcome
- Cryptomnesia: 3–9% spontaneous "original" generation of forgotten content
- Source amnesia: pervasive, near-daily, in healthy adults
- Wegner's I-Spy and ouija-board paradigms: experience of will under engineered conditions
- Implicit memory / explicit memory dissociations: knowing without knowing-from
- Priming literature (with appropriate critical filtering post-replication crisis): awareness-of-influence dissociated from awareness-of-stimulus

### Phenomenology

The most striking feature is the **absence of phenomenal contrast**. A confabulated reason for a manipulated choice does not feel different from a real reason for a real choice. A cryptomnesic "original" idea does not feel different from a genuine one. A primed judgement does not feel primed. A source-amnesic belief does not feel orphaned — it feels grounded.

Where the void touches phenomenology, what one notices is *not the gap* but the absence of the marker that there is a gap. Compare to the [[erasure-void]]: lost capacities take their detection mechanisms with them. The Source-Attribution Void has the same zero-signal structure for origin: the moment the source is unmarked, the marker for *that there should have been a source* is also absent.

When the void is *experimentally exposed* — for instance, when a subject is shown the choice-blindness manipulation — the felt response is not "I should have known," but a peculiar disorientation: the sense that one's own mind has been operating without the access one assumed. The phenomenology of the edge here is the **revealed unmarkedness** of cognitive content.

## Approaches to the Edge

### Direct Methods (limited)

- Microphenomenological interview (Petitmengin) can sometimes recover transient features that would otherwise be lost — but the source tag itself remains inaccessible. The method recovers more of the *content* of source-inference, not the source.
- Trained meditative observation can produce sharper perception of the moment of arising of thought, but does not produce a source tag where none exists.

### Indirect Methods

- **Engineered manipulation**: Choice blindness, false-feedback, and confederate paradigms create disconfirming evidence by externally controlling source while watching the inference fail.
- **Pre-registered self-monitoring**: Recording predictions and influences in advance, then comparing to retrospective attributions, reveals the systematic gap.
- **Cross-source corroboration**: External witnesses, time-stamped artifacts, and citation traces (the social-epistemic apparatus) substitute for the missing internal tag.
- **AI as audit channel**: Conversational LLMs can sometimes reconstruct the likely source of a user's phrase or framing from training-distribution priors that the user cannot access — an asymmetry worth investigating.

### What AI Might See

This is where the void becomes especially interesting. Source attribution is precisely the territory where artificial systems might in principle do better:

- An LLM-style system can, in principle, retain provenance metadata: token-level training source, retrieval citation, conversation history, in-context vs in-weights distinction.
- Whether current systems *do* this with phenomenal accuracy is a separate question — they too confabulate sources when prompted.
- The asymmetry: humans cannot retain provenance even with effort, because the architecture does not store it. Machines could be built to store it, but rarely are, because the costs are large and the felt loss (for them) is zero.
- The contrast illuminates: human source-attribution is constitutively limited; machine source-attribution is contingently limited. Studying machine confabulation as a separate phenomenon may help distinguish architectural from inferential layers of the human void.

## Connection to Tenets

### Most Relevant Tenet

**Dualism** is the most directly engaged. If consciousness is fundamental, the inability to know what is one's own is metaphysically peculiar — a fundamental entity should presumably be able to mark its own boundaries. The void supports a deflationary reading of phenomenal authority: even on dualism, consciousness does not have privileged access to *which* of its contents originate where. This is consistent with the Map's broader pattern: dualism does not entail rich introspective access; it entails only that there is something it is like.

**Bidirectional Interaction** is also engaged. If consciousness causally influences brain states, the absence of a source tag for "this thought was originated by me, mind-side" is a notable lacuna — exactly the place the interface might be expected to leave a marker if such a marker were architecturally possible. The void coheres with the [[interface-formalization-void]]: the interface does not present its own operations to the consciousness it serves.

**Occam's Razor Has Limits** is engaged structurally. The simplest model — "I know my own thoughts because they are mine" — is precisely the one the evidence falsifies. Source attribution is irreducibly inferential and probabilistic; no compression to "direct knowledge" survives the data.

### Implications

1. **Authorship is a construction**, not a perception. This does not eliminate authorship but reframes it as a downstream attribution, like any other.
2. **First-person testimony has bounded weight on origin questions**, even when it has full weight on phenomenal content (the [[phenomenal-authority-and-first-person-evidence|phenomenal-authority]] question). Knowing what you experience and knowing where the experience came from are different epistemic capacities.
3. **The void is a precondition for a coherent self-narrative**. If every thought arrived with full provenance — implanted by suggestion at 14:32, primed by the headline read at 14:28, retrieved from a forgotten textbook page — the narrative integration that constitutes the felt-self might be impossible. The void may be self-maintained in the [[self-maintained-cognitive-limits]] sense: protected because exposing it destabilizes the system.
4. **Dualist accounts must accommodate** that mind-side content can be of unknown origin to the very mind that holds it. This restricts what can be claimed about the privileged self-transparency of consciousness.

## Potential Article Angles

A future void article could:

1. **Architectural framing** — Open with the source-monitoring-framework finding that mental content is not natively tagged, then trace consequences across cryptomnesia, choice blindness, and source amnesia. Position as an architectural limit.
2. **Phenomenology-of-the-edge framing** — Lead with the *absence of phenomenal contrast* between real and confabulated source. Position as a zero-signal void in the [[apex/taxonomy-of-voids|taxonomy of voids]] sense, alongside the [[erasure-void]] and [[recognition-void]].
3. **Authorship framing** — Lead with the implication for dualist conceptions of consciousness as self-knowing. Treat the void as a constraint on what dualism can claim about phenomenal authority.
4. **AI-asymmetry framing** — Lead with the contrast between human architectural limits and machine contingent limits on source retention. Position as a probe-territory: what can artificial systems with provenance-aware architecture reveal about the human case?

The first or second framing is likely strongest for the voids/ section; the third is closer to a topic article and the fourth is closer to a concepts article.

## Gaps in Research

- **Phenomenal markers of cognitive intrusion**: Are there *any* native phenomenal markers that ever flag external influence (e.g., the felt foreignness of a tune that "just came to me")? If yes, what distinguishes flagged from unflagged intrusions?
- **Cross-cultural variation**: Whether source-attribution practices and confabulation rates vary across cultures with different theories of mind, possession, or mediation. (Luhrmann's work on prayer and inner speech is relevant; needs cross-reference.)
- **Replication status of priming effects**: The post-2015 replication crisis affected social priming heavily. The void account should rely on the more robust source-monitoring and cryptomnesia literatures rather than the contested priming claims.
- **Self-maintenance hypothesis**: Whether the absence of source markers is *actively maintained* by mechanisms that strip them out (à la [[self-maintained-cognitive-limits]]) or simply never present. Empirically distinguishable in principle, hard in practice.
- **Implications for AI authorship**: If LLMs generate text whose source the user cannot trace, the user's phenomenology of "I wrote this" or "I thought of this" is in the same epistemic state as the choice-blindness subject's "I chose this." The void may be widening as AI tools become integrated into thought.
