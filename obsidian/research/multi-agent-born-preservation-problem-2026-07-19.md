## Executive Summary

The **Multi-Agent Born-Preservation Problem** is the question of whether the Map's default *corridor dualism* — on which a conscious agent selects *which* Born-weighted branch actualises on a single measurement while the ensemble average stays exactly |⟨φ|ψ⟩|² — survives generalisation from one agent to *many*. A single agent's ensemble-invisibility is already the Map's working hypothesis ([[born-rule-and-the-consciousness-interface]]). The novel problem is that real measurement scenarios involve multiple conscious selectors acting on the *same* system, on *entangled* systems (Bell/GHZ), or in *nested* sequence (Wigner's friend). Born statistics are not only single-agent *marginals*; they are also *joint correlations* and a *no-signalling* structure that spans agents — including spacelike-separated ones. Preserving all three at once, while keeping each agent's selection genuinely free and the interaction minimal, appears to require either (a) a global coordinating constraint across agents that risks superdeterminism/fine-tuning, (b) a deepening of [[ensemble-level-epiphenomenalism]] to the joint level, or (c) a relational/perspectival dissolution (RQM, QBism, many-minds) that costs the Map elsewhere (indexical identity, observer-independent facts). This note maps the problem, the literature bearing on it, and the tenet stakes. It does **not** appear to be a named problem in the external literature — the *components* (no-signalling selection, intersubjective agreement, Wigner's-friend consistency, qualia fine-tuning) are well studied; the *synthesis as a preservation constraint scaling with agent number* is a Map-internal framing.

## Key Sources

### Causal Consistency Selects the Born Rule (Torres Alegre 2025)
- **URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.12636 (arXiv preprint, not yet peer-reviewed)
- **Type**: Paper
- **Key points**:
  - Within generalised probabilistic theories satisfying purification, the Born rule may be the *unique* assignment consistent with no-signalling; nonlinear deviations would enable superluminal signalling via quantum steering.
  - Fixes the rule's *form*, not its *existence* (like Gleason, from a different premise).
- **Tenet alignment**: Central to Minimal Quantum Interaction. Already load-bearing in [[causal-consistency-constraint]]. For the multi-agent case it is the linchpin: no-signalling is an inherently *multi-party* constraint, so it is exactly the axiom a plurality of independent selectors could break even while each preserves its own marginal.
- **Why it matters here**: If two agents each preserve their single-agent Born marginal but their *joint* selections break no-signalling, the effect is both detectable *and* a relativistic-causality violation — so corridor dualism, to stay consistent, must preserve *joint* Born, not just marginals. That is the crux constraint.

### When will Two Agents Agree on a Quantum Measurement Outcome? Intersubjective Agreement in QBism (DeBrota, Fuchs, Schlosshauer, Stacey 2024)
- **URL**: https://arxiv.org/html/2312.07728 · https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10773-024-05790-w (Int. J. Theor. Phys.)
- **Type**: Paper
- **Key points**:
  - In QBism there is *never a necessity* for two agents to agree on outcomes; agreement is a *goal* an agent can create conditions for, not a formal consequence.
  - Alice reconstructs her own personal Born-coherent credences about what Bob will report by examining his apparatus and checking his assignments satisfy quantum coherence.
  - Objectivity is relocated: "invariance under the Born rule is the mark of reality."
- **Tenet alignment**: QBism is a named rival that *dissolves* the multi-agent problem by denying there is a single joint distribution to preserve — each agent has personal probabilities. Conflicts with the Map only on ontology (QBism withholds the dualist substrate). Aligns operationally: the Map's corridor could be read as the physical under-story of QBist intersubjective agreement ([[pragmatist-quantum-foundations-and-the-agent]]).
- **Quote**: "there is never a necessity for two agents to agree on their respective measurement outcomes."

### Quantum mechanical rules for observed observers and the consistency of quantum theory (Ollivier / others 2024)
- **URL**: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-024-47170-2 · https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11271638/
- **Type**: Paper (Nature Communications)
- **Key points**:
  - Observers who will *themselves* be measured in a superposition of macroscopic states cannot reliably apply the standard Born rule to predict results after such a measurement.
  - Presented as lifting the Frauchiger-Renner inconsistency: the Born deduction rules must be supplemented with the condition that the observer is *not* subjected to "cat" measurements.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral/informative. Shows the *unrestricted* multi-agent use of the Born rule is already known to be inconsistent inside orthodox QM — the Map's multi-agent problem inherits, rather than creates, this hazard.

### Single-world interpretations of quantum theory cannot be self-consistent → "Quantum theory cannot consistently describe the use of itself" (Frauchiger & Renner 2018)
- **URL**: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-018-05739-8 (Nat. Commun.) — via https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1355219819300619
- **Type**: Paper / no-go theorem
- **Key points**:
  - Three assumptions — **Q** (quantum mechanics, incl. macroscopic), **S** (single-world / single outcome), **C** (consistency across agents reasoning about each other) — cannot jointly hold.
  - A nested Wigner's-friend setup where agents apply QM to *each other* yields a contradiction.
- **Tenet alignment**: Direct pressure on **No Many Worlds** (which keeps S) *and* on multi-agent corridor consistency (which needs C). The Map keeps S (single actual outcome, indexical identity) and must therefore locate its escape in Q or C — the same fork every single-world reading faces. This is the sharpest formal statement of the multi-agent consistency sub-problem.

### Many-minds interpretation (Albert & Loewer 1988; Donald critique)
- **URL**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Many-minds_interpretation · https://philsci-archive.pitt.edu/209/1/ommipitt.pdf
- **Type**: Interpretation / encyclopedia + critique
- **Key points**:
  - Each observer has a continuous infinity of minds; the physical state evolves unitarily and deterministically, each mind evolves *randomly* with Born-weighted probabilities.
  - Crucially: "There is no connection or correlation between Alice's and Bob's minds initially… only when observers meet can we talk of matching up subsets of Alice's minds with subsets of Bob's minds."
- **Tenet alignment**: Rival. Solves multi-agent correlation-preservation by making correlations *emerge only on interaction* — but at the cost of a mind-multiplicity the Map rejects (No Many Worlds, indexical identity). Instructive as the interpretation that took the multi-agent correlation problem most seriously and shows what machinery a solution seems to demand.

### On the causal efficacy of qualia: Philosophical zombies are fine-tuned (arXiv 2502.07865, 2025)
- **URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.07865
- **Type**: Paper (preprint)
- **Key points**:
  - Argues qualia can be causally efficacious via de Broglie-Bohm beables / the collapse process rather than at the wavefunction level.
  - **The Born rule must be modified** if collapse is to generate the correct dynamical histories; a zombie world (no qualia efficacy) requires *fine-tuned* initial conditions and is thus statistically unlikely.
  - Proposes fine-tuning as a *test* for consciousness.
- **Tenet alignment**: Mixed. Supports Bidirectional Interaction and the anti-epiphenomenalist thrust, but takes the *minimum-outside-the-corridor* route (Born modification), which the Map treats as a costlier branch. **Directly relevant to multi-agent**: any fine-tuning required to keep qualia-efficacy Born-consistent must hold across *all* agents at once — so the "conspiracy" scales with agent number, sharpening the Map's honest cost.

### Examining psychokinesis: a meta-analysis (Bösch, Steinkamp & Boller 2006); Maier & Dechamps (2018)
- **URL**: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16822162/ · https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2018.00379/full
- **Type**: Meta-analysis / preregistered Bayesian study
- **Key points**:
  - Bösch et al.: 380 intention-to-RNG studies; very small, extremely heterogeneous effect with publication-bias signatures.
  - Maier & Dechamps: strong preregistered *Bayesian evidence for the null* (against micro-PK).
- **Tenet alignment**: Empirical constraint. Relevant because micro-PK is the *multi-agent aggregate* case par excellence (many subjects, many trials). Persistent nulls are what corridor dualism predicts (no ensemble signature) and what gross-violation multi-agent bias forbids. Already handled in [[born-rule-violation-brain-interface-empirical-status-2026-04-23]] and [[brain-internal-born-rule-testing]].

## The Problem Stated Precisely

Corridor dualism for **one** agent: on measurement of state |ψ⟩ in basis {|φᵢ⟩}, the agent's selection realises outcome *i* on a given trial; over the agent's own long run, P(i) → |⟨φᵢ|ψ⟩|². **Ensemble-invisible by construction.**

Generalising to **N** agents raises three preservation demands that are *not* reducible to the single-agent one:

1. **Marginal preservation** (solved case). Each agent's own long-run statistics stay Born. Corridor dualism already delivers this.

2. **Joint / correlation preservation** (the new load). For an entangled multi-party state (Bell pair, GHZ triple), Born prescribes specific *joint* statistics and *correlations* (CHSH ≈ 2√2, GHZ perfect correlations). If Alice freely selects her outcome and Bob freely selects his, their *joint* distribution must still equal |ψ|² on the composite. Two independent selectors are not *guaranteed* to reproduce the correct correlations — and getting them wrong is empirically detectable.

3. **No-signalling preservation** (the causality load). The marginals of an entangled state must be *independent of the far party's measurement choice*. If agents' selections are jointly constrained to reproduce correlations, that constraint must not become a channel by which Alice's *choice* shifts Bob's *marginal* — or the corridor becomes a signalling mechanism, breaching relativistic causality and Minimal Quantum Interaction (Torres Alegre: Born is the unique no-signalling assignment).

The **tension**: demands (2) and (3) are *joint, cross-agent* constraints — sometimes across spacelike separation. Satisfying them while each agent selects *independently and freely* looks like it needs one of:

- **(a) Global harmonisation.** Each agent's "free" selection is pre-coordinated so joint statistics come out Born. This smells of superdeterminism / measurement-dependence / *fine-tuning* — and the coordination, if it acts across spacelike separation, is exactly the non-minimal, spooky interaction the tenets forbid.
- **(b) Joint-level idleness.** The selections wash out not only marginally but jointly, so the multi-agent channel does no distinctive work — deepening [[ensemble-level-epiphenomenalism]] from the single-agent to the joint level, and widening the epiphenomenalist threat.
- **(c) Relational dissolution.** There is *no* single agent-independent joint distribution to preserve (RQM: facts are relative to observers; QBism: probabilities are personal; many-minds: no correlation until minds meet). This dissolves the problem but costs the Map indexical/observer-independent structure it wants to keep (No Many Worlds; a single actual world).

A fourth, Map-internal move worth flagging: **(d) single global experiencer.** If the "many agents" are facets of *one* unified experiencer that selects the *joint* outcome at once ([[composition-and-consciousness|phenomenal non-compositionality]]), correlations and no-signalling are trivially preserved — but this arguably *dissolves the plurality* that generated the problem, and sits uneasily with the everyday plurality of distinct conscious persons. The interesting philosophical work is whether genuine agent-plurality can be reconciled with joint Born preservation *without* collapsing into (d).

## Major Positions

### Corridor dualism, multi-agent extension (Map's working hypothesis)
- **Core claim**: Independent agents each select single-trial outcomes; joint statistics remain Born by whatever constraint keeps them so.
- **Open burden**: name the constraint in (2)/(3) without paying (a)'s fine-tuning or (b)'s idleness. Currently *unspecified* — this is the article's central honest gap.

### QBist relational reading (DeBrota, Fuchs et al.)
- **Core claim**: No joint agent-independent distribution exists; agreement is a goal, Born-invariance is "the mark of reality."
- **Relation to tenets**: Dissolves (2)/(3) but withholds the dualist substrate; the Map can read QBist agreement as the *surface* of a corridor under-story ([[pragmatist-quantum-foundations-and-the-agent]]).

### Relational Quantum Mechanics (Rovelli)
- **Core claim**: Outcomes are facts *relative to* an observer; no observer-independent joint fact. Cross-perspective consistency is guaranteed by the formalism's structure, not by a shared world.
- **Relation to tenets**: Powerful dissolution of the problem; the cost is observer-relative facts, in tension with the Map's single actual world / indexical identity ([[relational-quantum-mechanics]] already in corpus).

### Many-minds (Albert-Loewer)
- **Core claim**: Correlations emerge only when observers interact; unitary physical state + Born-random minds.
- **Relation to tenets**: Rejected (mind-multiplicity vs No Many Worlds) but the clearest worked demonstration of what correlation-preservation machinery costs.

### Minimum-outside-the-corridor / qualia fine-tuning (arXiv 2502.07865)
- **Core claim**: Preserving qualia-efficacy Born-consistently requires fine-tuned initial conditions; Born may need modification.
- **Relation to tenets**: The multi-agent case *multiplies* the fine-tuning burden — the Map should own this as a scaling cost, not hide it.

## Key Debates

### Does no-signalling *over-constrain* multi-agent corridor freedom?
- **Sides**: Torres-Alegre-style rigidity (Born is the unique no-signalling assignment → joint selections are tightly boxed) vs corridor advocates (single-trial freedom lives *inside* the fixed measure).
- **Core disagreement**: Whether "freedom to select which branch" at the joint level is compatible with a measure rigid enough to forbid signalling across all agent pairs.
- **State**: Open. This is the load-bearing unresolved question.

### Is multi-agent preservation a fine-tuning "conspiracy"?
- **Sides**: Fine-tuning critics (2502.07865; general superdeterminism-conspiracy objections) vs corridor defenders (the constraint is a lawlike psychophysical regularity, not a coincidence).
- **Core disagreement**: Whether a law that makes N independent selectors jointly Born-consistent is explanatory or merely stipulated.
- **State**: Open; connects to [[psychophysical-laws]] and the [[epiphenomenalist-threat]].

### Whose selection is authoritative under nesting (Wigner's friend)?
- **Sides**: Frauchiger-Renner (Q+S+C inconsistent) vs restricted-Born responses (Ollivier et al. 2024: observers about to be "cat-measured" can't use standard Born) vs relational/QBist (no cross-agent fact to be inconsistent about).
- **State**: Active; the Map keeps S, so must locate its escape in a restriction on Q or C.

## Gaps in Research

- **No external paper names this exact problem.** The synthesis (Born preservation as a constraint *scaling with agent number*) is Map-internal. The literature supplies the *pieces* (no-signalling selection, QBist agreement, F-R consistency, many-minds correlation, qualia fine-tuning) but not the assembled problem. Worth stating plainly in the article to avoid implying a settled external debate.
- **No formal demonstration** either way of whether two independent corridor-selectors *can* reproduce CHSH/GHZ correlations without a joint constraint. A toy model (two agents, one Bell pair) would be high-value future work — likely showing that independent marginal-preserving selection generically *fails* joint correlation preservation unless coordinated, which is the whole point.
- **Whether option (d)** (single global experiencer) is forced, and whether it is compatible with everyday personal plurality, is unresolved and connects to [[quantum-holism-and-phenomenal-unity]] and [[composition-and-consciousness]].
- **Local Friendliness / Bong et al. (2020)** experimental results were not fetched in detail; a follow-up should read the Local Friendliness inequality literature directly for the multi-observer empirical state.
