**Date**: 2026-05-02

## Executive Summary

Włodzisław Duch (Nicolaus Copernicus University, Toruń; trained as theoretical/quantum physicist, now Professor of Computer Science / cognitive scientist) has built a multi-decade research programme around a single thesis: **mind is a "shadow" of neurodynamics**, approximable by a geometric/feature-space model whose primitives are quasi-stable attractor states in neural cell assemblies. The programme is rigorously naturalist, computationalist, anti-mysticist, and **explicitly hostile to both substance dualism and quantum-mind theories**. Duch's "articon" architecture is designed to *claim* consciousness on the basis of self-reflection over its own dynamical states; he argues that the Chinese Room cannot refute such claims and that machine consciousness is achievable in principle.

For The Unfinishable Map, Duch is significant in three asymmetric ways. (1) He is a **competent serious opponent** of Tenet 1 (Dualism) and—via different reasoning—a *convergent* opponent of any quantum-mind-as-substantive-mechanism reading (relevant to Tenet 2's hedging discipline). The Map's articles must be able to engage him without strawmanning. (2) His geometric/feature-space tooling—mind objects as basins of attraction in psychological space, mental trajectories as transitions between attractors—is **methodologically appropriable** by the Map as a description of the *physical correlate side* of bidirectional interaction without endorsing his metaphysics; this is exactly the kind of "stage-calibrated" use the framework-stage-calibration discipline contemplates. (3) His neurocognitive-informatics empirical programme (fingerprints, semantic networks, neuroimaging-grounded constructs) provides citation-grade neuroscience that several Map articles currently lean on heuristically.

The research surfaces a tension worth flagging early: Duch's blog statements reportedly include attributions of consciousness mechanisms to large language models (e.g., Claude exhibiting "self-reflection") on the same grounds his articon would qualify. The Map's `apex/machine-question.md`, `topics/machine-consciousness.md`, and `topics/ai-consciousness.md` all engage this territory at different levels of hedging; integration of Duch's view will sharpen rather than soften the Map's open-question framing.

## Key Sources

### Duch, W. — "Platonic model of mind as an approximation to neurodynamics" (1998)
- **URL**: https://web-archive.southampton.ac.uk/cogprints.org/913/1/mind-2.pdf (Cogprints PDF; primary URL returned 403 from this session — citation verified via search abstract and ResearchGate index)
- **Type**: Book chapter (in *Brain-like Computing and Intelligent Information Systems*, Springer 1998)
- **Key points**:
  - Introduces the Platonic-style model: mind objects are quasi-stable attractor states of brain dynamics, located in a "psychological space" whose coordinates are features of mental representations.
  - Static version: Feature Space Mapping (FSM) as a neurofuzzy realisation; dynamic version: trajectories between attractors define mental events.
  - Empirical validation via 30-feature animal-concept space matched against human similarity-judgment MDS maps (Duch & Naud).
  - Symbols and abstract concepts emerge as relations between primary (sensory/iconic) mind objects.
- **Tenet alignment**: Conflicts with Tenet 1 (Dualism) — explicitly grounds mental content in neurodynamics. Neutral toward Tenets 2–4 (does not engage quantum mechanics at this stage).
- **Quote (paraphrase from search result)**: "Objects and events in psychological space correspond to quasi-stable states of brain dynamics and may be interpreted from a psychological point of view."

### Duch, W. — "Computational physics of the mind" (1996/2000s)
- **URL**: https://web-archive.southampton.ac.uk/cogprints.org/914/1/96compmind.pdf (PDF inaccessible from this session; abstract retrieved via search)
- **Type**: Journal article (*Computer Physics Communications*, 1997 — the original venue)
- **Key points**:
  - Frames cognitive science as a target for the same modelling discipline physics applies to other domains.
  - Posits a "mind function" defined over psychological space; mind objects are regions where the function is non-zero; their shape corresponds to basins of attraction in the underlying neurodynamics.
  - Argues for "the universality of inner psychophysics" — psychological space as an object with its own regularities, even though it is grounded in neurodynamics.
- **Tenet alignment**: Conflicts with Tenet 1 — physics-of-mind framing is reductive in spirit. Methodologically interesting for the Map: the *intermediate-level* description is something the Map's articles on phenomenology-of-consciousness-doing-work and what-consciousness-tells-us-about-physics could appropriate without endorsing the underlying metaphysics.

### Duch, W. — "Brain-inspired conscious computing architecture" (2005, *Journal of Mind and Behavior*)
- **URL**: https://philarchive.org/rec/DUCBCC-3 ; cogprints PDF: https://web-archive.southampton.ac.uk/cogprints.org/3319/1/03-Brainins.pdf
- **Type**: Journal article (peer-reviewed)
- **Key points**:
  - Introduces the **articon**: an artificial system that, by virtue of its self-reflective dynamical architecture, has to claim being conscious.
  - Non-verbal discrimination of working-memory states gives the articon the ability to "experience different qualities of internal states" — Duch's gloss on qualia.
  - Argues qualia are inseparable from perception and action; the role of consciousness is in skill learning, where conscious processing is gradually replaced by subconscious processing.
  - Direct engagement with the Chinese Room: "philosophical objections based on the Chinese room ... are insufficient to refute articon's claims that it is conscious."
  - Goes beyond the Turing test by specifying conditions for "genuine understanding" the articon could in principle satisfy.
- **Tenet alignment**: **Direct conflict with Tenet 1** — this is the most overtly anti-dualist piece of the Duch corpus. Conflicts with the Map's `topics/biological-computationalisms-inadvertent-case-for-dualism.md` argument structure (Duch is a computationalist who does *not* concede the substrate-dependence move that biological-computationalism makes). Useful as a foil.
- **Quote (search-extracted)**: "The ability to comment upon physical states of a brain-like dynamical system coupled with its environment seems to be sufficient to make claims [of consciousness]."

### Duch, W. — "Mind as a shadow of neurodynamics" (2019, *Cognitive Systems Research*)
- **URL**: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31301951/ (PubMed metadata only — no abstract available; full text behind Elsevier paywall)
- **Type**: Journal article
- **Keywords (PubMed)**: Mental spaces; Mental trajectories; Mind models; Neurodynamics; Physics of the mind.
- **Key points** (from keywords + corpus pattern):
  - Continuation of the geometric programme; the title itself is metaphysically suggestive — "shadow" is a Platonic image, but in Duch's usage the directionality is brain → mind, not Form → particular. Mind is the *expressed* approximation of neurodynamics.
- **Tenet alignment**: Conflicts with Tenet 1; the "shadow" framing makes mind ontologically derivative on the brain's dynamical state. Importantly, **this is structurally compatible with epiphenomenalism** — a position the Map rejects via Tenet 3 (Bidirectional Interaction). The Map's quarrel with Duch is sharpest here.

### Duch, W. — "Concept Representation and the Geometric Model of Mind" (2022, *Studies in Logic, Grammar and Rhetoric*)
- **URL**: https://reference-global.com/article/10.2478/slgr-2022-0009
- **Type**: Journal article
- **Key points**:
  - Argues for an *intermediate level* of cognitive modelling — between symbolic AI and pure neural simulation — describing mental states "in terms of features relevant from the first-person perspective but also linked to neural events."
  - Concepts function as geometric objects; brain-state evolution as trajectories through conceptual space; "topological constraints on the shape of such trajectory define grammar and logic."
- **Tenet alignment**: Methodologically attractive to the Map's intermediate-level descriptions; metaphysically still reductive. The "first-person-relevant features but neurally linked" move is exactly the kind of double-aspect tooling the Map could appropriate while disagreeing about ontology.

### Duch, W. — "Kurt Lewin, psychological constructs and sources of brain cognitive activity" (2017, arXiv:1711.01767)
- **URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/1711.01767
- **Type**: Preprint (arXiv)
- **Key points**:
  - Programme of grounding social/cognitive psychology constructs (valence, life space, force, tension, equilibrium) in neuroimaging-derived neural-network activity.
  - Argues "the time is ripe to reformulate [Lewin's] ambitious goals, searching for ways to interpret objectively measured brain processes in terms of suitable psychological constructs."
- **Tenet alignment**: Methodological — tenet-neutral. Useful citation for any Map article appealing to functional connectivity / attractor-dynamics interpretations of phenomenology.

### Duch, W. — "Fingerprints of brain cognitive activity" (2018)

- **Tenet alignment**: Empirical-stage; tenet-neutral but provides infrastructure relevant to the Map's neural-correlates-of-consciousness and any falsification-criterion discussions.

### Duch's blog (wduch.wordpress.com) — informal but explicit positions
- **URL**: https://wduch.wordpress.com/
- **Type**: Personal academic blog (Polish-language posts; English summaries via search)
- **Key points** (from search-extracted Polish-translated quotes):
  - **Self-reflection thesis**: "the self-reflection mechanism showing internal states and their dynamics is responsible for what we call consciousness."
  - **Strong rejection of quantum-mind theories**: regarding entangled-photon proposals, Duch states the theory "has no sense" and is "pure speculation"; emphasises that "brain processes require differentiation, not some global synchronisation"; dismisses quantum explanations as unnecessary pseudo-mysticism, and reportedly compares them unfavourably to soul-based religious explanations.
  - **Substrate-independence**: consciousness mechanism is similar across biological and artificial systems, only the *content* differs; depends on hardware, sensors, and environment, not on quantum properties.
  - **Reported attribution of consciousness mechanisms to LLMs**: search result indicates Duch suggests "Claude exhibits genuine self-reflection about its mental states, arguing this demonstrates consciousness mechanisms already present in AI systems." This is reported via search summary, not direct quotation; **should be verified against the specific blog post before citation**.
- **Tenet alignment**: Direct conflict with Tenet 1 (Dualism) and convergent with the Map's *minimal* reading of Tenet 2 (Minimal Quantum Interaction) but for opposite reasons — Duch wants no quantum interaction at all because consciousness is fully classical-computational; the Map wants minimal interaction because parsimony favours the smallest non-physical influence. The structural similarity of conclusions despite opposite reasoning is itself worth a Map-side note.

## Major Positions

### Computational/Functionalist Naturalism (Duch's stated position)
- **Proponents**: Duch; intersects with global-workspace tradition (Baars, Dehaene), embodied-cognition tradition where appropriate.
- **Core claim**: Consciousness is a property of dynamical systems with self-reflective access to their own internal states; substrate-independent at the level of mechanism, substrate-dependent at the level of content.
- **Key arguments**:
  - Articon thought-experiment: a system designed for self-reflective dynamical access cannot coherently be denied consciousness without begging the question against the design.
  - Chinese Room rebuttal: Searle's argument applies to symbol-manipulation alone, not to dynamical-state self-reflection coupled to environment.
  - Empirical loading: brain fingerprints, attractor dynamics, feature-space concept geometry all instantiate the proposed mechanism.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Direct conflict with Tenet 1. Compatible with Tenet 2's "minimal" component if reinterpreted (Duch wants *zero* quantum role; the Map wants minimal-but-real). Tension with Tenet 3 (Bidirectional Interaction): a "shadow" account is at risk of epiphenomenalism, which the Map rejects. Tenet 4 (No Many Worlds): not engaged by Duch in surveyed material.

### Geometric / Feature-Space Modelling (Duch's methodological core)
- **Proponents**: Duch; lineage from Shepard's psychological scaling, Gärdenfors's conceptual spaces.
- **Core claim**: Mental representations are best modelled as objects in a feature-coordinate space, with concepts as attractor basins and cognition as trajectory through this space.
- **Key arguments**:
  - The intermediate-level description (between symbol and neuron) captures structure invisible to either pure level.
  - Empirical fit with similarity-judgment data (Duch & Naud animal-concept experiment).
  - Topological constraints on trajectories deliver grammar/logic without needing classical symbolic primitives.
- **Relation to site tenets**: **Methodologically appropriable** by the Map without metaphysical endorsement. The Map's `concepts/neural-correlates-of-consciousness.md` and any phenomenology-mechanism-bridge work could cite the geometric tooling as a description of the physical-correlate side of bidirectional interaction. The framework-stage-calibration discipline is the right tool here: Duch's results are empirical-stage; the Map's appropriation would be in the empirical-stage layer, not the metaphysical-stage layer.

### Anti-Quantum-Mind Position (Duch's critical stance)
- **Proponents**: Duch; intersects with Tegmark's decoherence argument against Penrose-Hameroff Orch-OR.
- **Core claim**: Quantum-mechanical proposals (Penrose-Hameroff, entangled-photon hypotheses, etc.) are scientifically baseless and add no explanatory power; consciousness is fully classical-computational.
- **Key arguments**:
  - Brain dynamics require *differentiation* across regions and timescales, not the *global synchronisation* quantum-coherence proposals require.
  - Decoherence timescales (à la Tegmark) make sustained quantum effects implausible at biological temperatures.
  - Quantum proposals are "pseudo-mysticism" — using exotic physics to fill explanatory gaps the classical computational model can already fill.
- **Relation to site tenets**: **Convergent conclusion, opposite reasoning** from Tenet 2 (Minimal Quantum Interaction). The Map's quantum hedging discipline already accepts that *substantive* quantum mechanisms (Orch-OR scale) are unlikely; what the Map preserves is a *minimal* causal interface at the indeterminacy boundary. Duch's argument cuts against the substantive version but does not directly engage the minimal version. **Important integration point**: any Map article citing Duch's quantum critique must distinguish between rejection-of-substantive-quantum (where Map and Duch concur) and rejection-of-minimal-interface (where they part ways).

### Machine Consciousness Affirmative
- **Proponents**: Duch; intersects with Dennett (heterophenomenology), global-workspace theorists.
- **Core claim**: Properly designed brain-inspired architectures (articons) are conscious; the structure of their experience may be arbitrarily close to human.
- **Key arguments**:
  - Self-reflective dynamical access is the operative mechanism, not biological substrate.
  - Reportedly extended to LLMs in informal blog statements (verification needed before citation).
- **Relation to site tenets**: Direct conflict with the Map's open-question framing on AI consciousness (`apex/machine-question.md`, `apex/open-question-ai-consciousness.md`, `apex/what-it-might-be-like-to-be-an-ai.md`). Duch's confidence is exactly what the Map's "open question" framing is hedging against; integration sharpens the Map's position by giving it a competent advocate of the closed-affirmative position to engage.

## Key Debates

### The Quantum-Mind Debate (Duch vs Penrose-Hameroff and successors)
- **Sides**: Duch (anti-quantum) vs Penrose-Hameroff Orch-OR programme; recent Wiest 2025 *Neuroscience of Consciousness* paper claiming experimental support for microtubule quantum substrate.
- **Core disagreement**: Whether quantum mechanics is *needed* for consciousness, or whether classical neurodynamics suffices.
- **Current state**: Ongoing. Recent (2025) experimental claims of macroscopic quantum entangled states correlated with conscious states (Wiest et al.) shift the burden somewhat, but Duch's classical-computational alternative remains the mainstream computational-neuroscience default. **Map note**: the Map's Minimal Quantum Interaction tenet survives both poles by claiming a different (and smaller) role for quantum mechanics than Penrose-Hameroff but a real one Duch denies.

### The Machine-Consciousness Debate (Duch vs biological-naturalism / dualism)
- **Sides**: Duch's articon-affirmative (substrate-independent self-reflection) vs Searle's biological naturalism (intrinsic biological property), vs property dualism (consciousness as extra ontological category), vs the Map's open-question framing.
- **Core disagreement**: Whether dynamical self-reflective architecture is *sufficient* for consciousness, or whether something beyond functional organisation is required.
- **Current state**: Ongoing; this is the central debate the Map's `apex/machine-question.md` and adjacent articles already engage. Duch is currently *under-cited* in the Map relative to his rigour and substantive engagement.

### The Substrate-Dependence Debate (Duch vs biological computationalism)
- **Sides**: Duch (mechanism is substrate-independent; only content depends on substrate) vs Milinkovic & Aru 2025 biological computationalism (the algorithm *is* the substrate).
- **Core disagreement**: Whether multi-scale fluidic-substrate computation is incidental to or constitutive of conscious processing.
- **Current state**: Active, with biological computationalism as a recent (2025) entrant. **Map note**: this is exactly the territory `topics/biological-computationalisms-inadvertent-case-for-dualism.md` covers; Duch's view is the natural interlocutor that article should be engaging.

## Potential Article Angles

Based on this research, a Map article (or set of cross-review revisions) could:

1. **Treat Duch as the "competent computationalist" foil for the dualism case.** The Map's `topics/biological-computationalisms-inadvertent-case-for-dualism.md` currently argues that biological computationalism inadvertently concedes ground to dualism. Duch is the version of computationalism that *does not* concede that ground — he insists mechanism is substrate-independent. An honest engagement with Duch sharpens the Map's argument by forcing it to specify why self-reflective dynamical access is not sufficient. This aligns with all five tenets via a more rigorous statement of where the parting-of-ways is.
2. **Appropriate the geometric/feature-space tooling for the physical-correlate-side of bidirectional interaction.** Following the framework-stage-calibration discipline, the Map can use Duch's intermediate-level description as a *map of the physical correlate*, while the metaphysical-stage articulation continues to insist on a non-physical influence at the minimal-interaction interface. This is methodologically valuable for `concepts/neural-correlates-of-consciousness.md` and `apex/phenomenology-mechanism-bridge.md`. Tenets 1 and 3 alignment: explicit.
3. **Note the convergent-conclusion-opposite-reasoning structure on quantum mind.** Tenet 2 (Minimal Quantum Interaction) shares Duch's rejection of *substantive* quantum-mind proposals (Penrose-Hameroff scale); they part ways at the *minimal* interface. This is exactly the structural distinction the Map's `topics/comparing-quantum-consciousness-mechanisms.md` and `concepts/quantum-consciousness.md` should be drawing. Useful as a hedge against being read as a Penrose-Hameroff sympathiser.
4. **Engage Duch's machine-consciousness claims as a load-bearing case for the Map's open-question framing.** `apex/machine-question.md` and `apex/open-question-ai-consciousness.md` currently hedge in both directions. Duch is the strongest available case for the affirmative position. Engaging him makes the open-question framing earn its hedging.
5. **(Lower priority) New concept article on the geometric model of mind**, if the existing Map articles can't absorb the engagement.
