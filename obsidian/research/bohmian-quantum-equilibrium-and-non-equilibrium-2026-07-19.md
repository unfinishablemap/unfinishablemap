## Executive Summary

In de Broglie–Bohm (pilot-wave) theory, particles have definite positions guided by the wave function, and the Born rule ρ = |ψ|² is not a fundamental axiom but a *quantum equilibrium* condition. Two rival programmes justify why we observe equilibrium: (1) the **typicality/measure** approach of Dürr, Goldstein and Zanghì (equilibrium follows from a "typical" initial universal configuration, analogous to how the Maxwell distribution follows from a typical gas microstate); and (2) the **dynamical relaxation** approach of Antony Valentini (a sub-quantum H-theorem shows arbitrary initial distributions relax toward |ψ|² on a coarse-grained scale, analogous to Boltzmann). Both agree the observed world is in equilibrium, but Valentini's programme treats *quantum non-equilibrium* (ρ ≠ |ψ|²) as a physically possible regime that would license **signal nonlocality** (faster-than-light signalling) and **subquantum measurements** violating the uncertainty principle — placing standard quantum mechanics inside a wider, more powerful physics. The main proposed observable window is cosmological: incomplete primordial relaxation could leave a **large-scale power deficit** in the CMB with a characteristic inverse-tangent shape. This topic is highly relevant to the Map's tenets on minimal quantum interaction, bidirectional influence, and the rejection of Many-Worlds.

## Key Sources

### Bohmian Mechanics (Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy)
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/qm-bohm/
- **Type**: Encyclopedia (author: Sheldon Goldstein)
- **Key points**:
  - The quantum equilibrium hypothesis: particle configurations distribute as |ψ|², which secures empirical equivalence with orthodox QM.
  - The relevant property is **equivariance**, not stationarity: "It is an immediate consequence of the guiding equation and the quantum continuity equation that ρ^ψ = |ψ|² is equivariant." Equivariance means Schrödinger evolution of ψ and Bohmian motion of configurations stay mutually compatible relative to the |ψ|² distribution over time.
  - Born-rule probabilities "emerge naturally from an analysis of equilibrium for the deterministic dynamical system that Bohmian mechanics defines," paralleling classical thermodynamic equilibrium.
  - Ultimate justification appeals to "statistical patterns exhibited by ensembles of actual subsystems within a typical individual universe"; the article concedes the status of this justification "remains controversial."
- **Tenet alignment**: Aligns strongly with Tenet 4 (No Many Worlds) — Bohmian mechanics is a single-world, definite-configuration theory. Neutral-to-aligned with Tenet 5 (Occam's limits): it adds hidden variables (particle positions) yet dissolves the measurement problem.
- **Quote**: "probabilities for positions given by the quantum equilibrium distribution emerge naturally from an analysis of equilibrium for the deterministic dynamical system that Bohmian mechanics defines."

### Quantum Non-Equilibrium (Wikipedia)
- **URL**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_non-equilibrium
- **Type**: Encyclopedia article
- **Key points**:
  - Quantum non-equilibrium = the regime ρ(X,t) ≠ |ψ(X,t)|²; in de Broglie–Bohm theory the Born rule is not a basic law, so this regime is coherent in principle.
  - Valentini (1991) gave an H-theorem "constructed in analogy to the Boltzmann H-theorem of statistical mechanics" for relaxation toward equilibrium; once reached, equilibrium is preserved by the continuity equation under Schrödinger evolution.
  - Non-equilibrium would permit **signal nonlocality** (faster-than-light signalling) and, with sub-standard-width non-equilibrium distributions, **subquantum measurements** violating the uncertainty principle.
  - How equilibrium was *initially* reached remains open; it is unknown whether non-equilibrium states can be produced today, making direct tests difficult.
- **Tenet alignment**: Directly relevant to Tenet 2 (Minimal Quantum Interaction) and Tenet 3 (Bidirectional Interaction) — non-equilibrium is a concrete physical framework in which the Born statistics can be *violated*, a candidate mechanism for lawlike-but-hidden influence on quantum outcomes.

### Foundations of statistical mechanics and the status of the Born rule in de Broglie–Bohm pilot-wave theory (Valentini)
- **URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/1906.10761 (also philsci-archive.pitt.edu/16207)
- **Type**: Paper / book chapter (2019)
- **Key points**:
  - Frames the debate explicitly as two schools: **dynamical relaxation** (Valentini) vs **typicality of initial conditions** (the "Bohmian mechanics" school of Dürr–Goldstein–Zanghì).
  - Argues the Born rule should be read like the thermal equilibrium of an ordinary gas — a contingent statistical state of *our* epoch, not a law — leaving room for primordial or engineered non-equilibrium.
- **Tenet alignment**: Aligns with Tenet 5 — cautions against treating a simple/universal rule (Born) as fundamental when it may be an emergent special case.

- **Key points**: Shows signal-locality and the uncertainty principle are features of the *equilibrium* state specifically; deterministic hidden-variable theories generically permit signalling and sub-uncertainty information extraction out of equilibrium.

### Primordial quantum nonequilibrium and large-scale cosmic anomalies (Colin & Valentini)
- **URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/1407.8262
- **Type**: Paper (2014/2015)
- **Key points**:
  - Incomplete relaxation to equilibrium at *long wavelengths* during a pre-inflationary phase could explain large-scale CMB anomalies (e.g. the low-ℓ power deficit).
  - Companion work (arXiv:1510.03508) predicts a "robust" **inverse-tangent dependence on wavenumber k with oscillations** for the power deficit if the pre-inflationary era is radiation dominated.
  - Related: statistical anisotropy from cosmological quantum relaxation (arXiv:1510.02523); anomalous spectral lines / relic non-equilibrium (Underwood & Valentini, arXiv:1609.04576).
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral scientifically, but valuable as a concrete example that departures from standard QM could be empirically constrained — supports the Map's insistence that non-physical or beyond-quantum influence be *minimal and in principle detectable* (Tenets 2, 5).

### Long-time relaxation / residues in pilot-wave theory
- **URLs**: https://arxiv.org/abs/1310.1899 (Abraham, Colin, Valentini); Royal Society Proc. A (2021, 2023) coupled harmonic oscillators
- **Type**: Papers / numerical studies
- **Key points**:
  - The coarse-grained sub-quantum **H-function** decreases roughly exponentially toward zero (relaxation).
  - But relaxation can be **incomplete**: depending on initial phases the H-function can settle at a non-zero "residue" exceeding ~10% of its initial value. Systems with few modes (long-wavelength cosmological modes) "are likely to never fully relax" — the physical basis for surviving primordial non-equilibrium.

### Timescales for dynamical relaxation to the Born rule (Towler, Russell, Valentini)
- **URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/1103.1589
- **Type**: Paper (2011)
- **Key points**: Numerical estimate that relaxation is extremely fast for typical systems (short timescales), explaining why laboratory non-equilibrium is not seen today — it would have relaxed away — pushing the observable residue into the very early universe.

## Major Positions

### Typicality / Measure Approach (Dürr, Goldstein, Zanghì)
- **Proponents**: Detlef Dürr, Sheldon Goldstein, Nino Zanghì (foundational 1992 paper onward).
- **Core claim**: Quantum equilibrium is *not* something a system dynamically approaches; rather, the |ψ|² Born statistics follow, via the law of large numbers, from assuming the universe's initial configuration is **typical** with respect to the equivariant measure |Ψ|². Analogous to deriving the Maxwell velocity distribution from a typical phase-space point of a gas.
- **Key arguments**: Equivariance of |ψ|² makes it the natural stationarity-like measure; "the overwhelming majority of possible selections of initial positions... will satisfy quantum equilibrium conditions in a subsystem."
- **Relation to site tenets**: Compatible with a single deterministic world (Tenet 4). But by making equilibrium quasi-necessary ("typical"), it leaves *less* room for lawlike consciousness-driven deviation — mildly in tension with Tenets 2–3 unless the "atypical" initial condition is exactly where mind enters.

### Dynamical Relaxation Approach (Valentini)
- **Proponents**: Antony Valentini (and collaborators Colin, Towler, Underwood, Abraham).
- **Core claim**: Non-equilibrium (ρ ≠ |ψ|²) is a genuine physical possibility; the sub-quantum H-theorem shows generic initial distributions relax toward |ψ|² on a coarse-grained scale, so we live in a relaxed equilibrium "heat death" of a wider physics. Quantum theory is then a *special case*.
- **Key arguments**: Sub-quantum H-theorem (H decreases like Boltzmann's); numerical relaxation with residues; non-equilibrium licenses signal nonlocality and subquantum measurement; primordial residues could show up in the CMB.
- **Relation to site tenets**: The most tenet-relevant position. Non-equilibrium is a candidate concrete framework for *how* a minimal, possibly mind-associated influence could bias quantum outcomes away from Born statistics without violating the underlying pilot-wave dynamics (Tenets 2 & 3). Because it is empirically bounded (relaxation is fast; residues survive mainly cosmologically), it respects the "minimal and detectable" spirit.

### Contrast case: Everettian reading
- Valentini has argued pilot-wave theory is "many worlds in denial" only for those who reify the wave function; his own view keeps a single actual configuration. Deutsch/Brown replies dispute this. Relevant because it sharpens the single-world commitment central to **Tenet 4**.

## Key Debates

### Why is the Born rule true? — Relaxation vs Typicality
- **Sides**: Valentini (dynamical, contingent, in-principle-violable) vs Dürr–Goldstein–Zanghì (typicality, quasi-necessary, non-dynamical).
- **Core disagreement**: Is |ψ|² a state the world *reached* (and could depart from) or a state that is simply *typical* and needs no dynamical story?
- **Current state**: Ongoing and unresolved. Practically empirically equivalent today; they diverge on whether non-equilibrium is physically meaningful and testable.

### Does relaxation always complete?
- **Sides**: Fast/near-complete relaxation (typical many-mode systems) vs surviving residues (few-mode, long-wavelength, cosmological).
- **Core disagreement**: Whether observable non-equilibrium can survive to the present epoch.
- **Current state**: Numerics support incomplete relaxation for low-mode systems; cosmology is the leading proposed observational channel.

### Is signal nonlocality a bug or a feature?
- **Sides**: Critics see faster-than-light signalling as a fatal conflict with relativity; Valentini sees it as a real prediction of a deeper theory confined to (currently inaccessible) non-equilibrium.
- **Current state**: Theoretical; no non-equilibrium ensembles have been produced or observed.
