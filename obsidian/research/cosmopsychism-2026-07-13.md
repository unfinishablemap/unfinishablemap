## Executive Summary

Cosmopsychism is priority-monist panpsychism: the view that the cosmos as a whole is the one fundamental conscious (or fundamentally consciousness-involving) subject, and that individual minds are grounded in, or derived from, that cosmic subject by **decomposition** — a top-down structure that inverts micropsychism's bottom-up **combination**. It was developed as an escape route from panpsychism's combination problem (how do micro-subjects sum into a macro-subject?), only to inherit a structural mirror image: the **de-combination problem** (how does one cosmic subject give rise to many bounded, private finite subjects?). The position has a clear metaphysical backbone — Jonathan Schaffer's priority monism — and a cluster of proponents (Goff, Nagasawa & Wager, Shani, Albahari, Mathews, Seager). The Map **declines** cosmopsychism: it relocates irreducibility into the whole rather than the mind–matter interface, and the de-combination problem is a cost the Map's interactionist dualism structurally avoids (there is no single cosmic subject to divide) — at the price of the interaction problem instead. This note is research substrate for a future `concepts/cosmopsychism.md`; it deliberately does not re-derive the de-combination discussion already housed in the corpus.

## Definition and Placement

**Cosmopsychism** = **priority monism** (Schaffer) + **panpsychism**. The universe considered as a whole is the fundamental locus of consciousness, and everything else — including ordinary human and animal minds — is metaphysically posterior to it.

- **Constitutive cosmopsychism**: all facts are grounded in / realized by / constituted of consciousness-involving facts at the *cosmic* level (the formulation the Stanford Encyclopedia uses).
- **Priority cosmopsychism** (Nagasawa & Wager): a weaker grounding claim — the cosmos's consciousness is *ontologically prior* to the consciousness of ordinary individuals, without the strong constitutive demand that finite experience be built out of cosmic experience.

### Distinguish from neighbours

1. **Micropsychism / constitutive (bottom-up) panpsychism** — fundamental consciousness sits at the *micro* level (particles / physical ultimates); macro-minds are *combined* from micro-minds. Cosmopsychism inverts the arrow: fundamentality is at the *top*, and finite minds are *decomposed / derived* from the whole. Both are species of panpsychism; they disagree only about which level is fundamental.
2. **Analytic idealism** (mind-centric metaphysics; e.g. Kastrup) — mind/consciousness is *all there is*; the physical is a representation *within* mind. Cosmopsychism is typically framed as a Russellian monism: it can keep a physical world whose *intrinsic* nature is experiential, rather than dissolving matter into mind. (The boundary is porous — Albahari's aperspectival view drifts toward idealism, and some read constitutive cosmopsychism as idealism by another name.)
3. **Neutral monism** — reality's fundamental stuff is neither mental nor physical but neutral, with both derived. Cosmopsychism, by contrast, makes the cosmic base *phenomenal* (or protophenomenal), not neutral.
4. **Open individualism** (identity thesis, Kolak) — the claim that there is numerically **one** subject that is *identical* across all persons. This is an **identity** claim, not a **grounding** claim. Cosmopsychism says finite subjects are *grounded in / derived from* the cosmic subject (they can still be numerically distinct from it and from each other); open individualism says they *are literally the same subject*. Keep these apart (see "Complementarity" below).

## Key Sources

### Schaffer, "Monism: The Priority of the Whole" (2010) — the metaphysical backbone
- **URL**: https://read.dukeupress.edu/the-philosophical-review/article-abstract/119/1/31/2871/Monism-The-Priority-of-the-Whole
- **Type**: Journal article (*The Philosophical Review* 119(1):31–76; DOI 10.1215/00318108-2009-025)
- **Verification**: CONFIRMED at publisher (Duke University Press) — title, journal, volume/issue, page range all match.
- **Key points**:
  - Priority monism: the *whole* (the cosmos) is prior to its parts; the cosmos is the one fundamental concrete object, and everything else is a *dependent abstraction from* it.
  - This is *not* existence monism (the claim that only one thing exists); parts still exist, they are just posterior.
- **Role for cosmopsychism**: supplies the "one fundamental whole" that cosmopsychism then declares *conscious*. Cosmopsychism ≈ Schaffer's priority monism + the claim that the fundamental whole is experiential.
- **Quote (from the paper's abstract, verify verbatim in body before quoting in an article)**: "The monist holds that the whole is prior to its parts, and thus views the cosmos as fundamental, with metaphysical explanation dangling downward from the One."
- **Tenet alignment**: The "irreducibility in the whole" move is where Tenet 1 pressure lands (see below). Neutral/structural — Schaffer himself is not a panpsychist.

### Shani, "Cosmopsychism: A Holistic Approach to the Metaphysics of Experience" (2015)
- **URL**: https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/05568641.2015.1106709 (record: https://philpapers.org/rec/SHACAH-2)
- **Type**: Journal article (*Philosophical Papers* 44(3):389–437; DOI 10.1080/05568641.2015.1106709)
- **Verification**: CONFIRMED at publisher (Taylor & Francis) — title, journal, volume/issue match; page range 389–437 per PhilPapers.
- **Key points**:
  - Presents cosmopsychism as a *holistic* alternative to atomistic (micro) panpsychism, sharing panpsychism's aims while claiming to sidestep its combination problem.
  - Introduces the "lonely cosmos" worry (later addressed with a relational account): a single cosmic subject seems to lack the relational structure that individuates finite perspectives.
- **Coinage note**: Shani 2015 is the paper *usually credited* with putting the term "cosmopsychism" into circulation and giving it its canonical formulation. **Honest caveat**: the Stanford Encyclopedia does *not* single out one originator, and the *position* was developed contemporaneously by Mathews (2011) and Jaskolla & Buck (2012). Safe framing for the article: "the term is usually attributed to Shani (2015), who gave the view its canonical formulation, though closely related proposals appear in Mathews (2011) and Jaskolla & Buck (2012)." Do **not** flatly assert a single coiner without this hedge. (Corroborating discussion also appears in Ganeri & Shani, "What Is Cosmopsychism?")

### Nagasawa & Wager, "Panpsychism and Priority Cosmopsychism" (2016)
- **URL**: https://philpapers.org/rec/NAGPAP-2 (OUP: https://academic.oup.com/book/11114/chapter/159544474)
- **Type**: Book chapter, in G. Brüntrup & L. Jaskolla (eds.), *Panpsychism: Contemporary Perspectives*, Oxford University Press, 2016, pp. 113–129.
- **Verification**: CONFIRMED — PhilPapers version-history page states the full citation including "pp. 113–129: Oxford University Press (2016)"; editors and volume title match.
- **Key points**:
  - Coins/deploys **priority cosmopsychism**: phenomenality is prevalent because *the whole cosmos* instantiates phenomenal or protophenomenal properties, and the cosmos's consciousness is *ontologically prior* to ordinary individuals' consciousness.
  - **Priority reply to de-combination**: because the view is a *priority* (grounding) thesis rather than a *constitutive* one, it need not show that finite experience is literally *built out of* cosmic experience — grounding is weaker than constitution, so the explanatory burden is lighter than the constitutive combination problem.
  - Argues priority cosmopsychism escapes the "infinite decomposition" worry that bottom-up fundamentalism faces, because it locates the basic subject at the *top* level.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral/conflicts — the "irreducibility lives in the whole" structure is precisely what Tenet 1 (irreducibility at the interface) does not endorse.

### Goff, *Consciousness and Fundamental Reality* (2017)
- **URL**: https://global.oup.com/academic/product/consciousness-and-fundamental-reality-9780190677015
- **Type**: Book (Oxford University Press, 2017, Philosophy of Mind series, ISBN 9780190677015).
- **Verification**: CONFIRMED at publisher (OUP) — title, author, publisher, year match.
- **Key points**:
  - Critiques physicalism, weighs versions of Russellian monism, and ultimately defends a **cosmopsychist** Russellian monism: all facts are grounded in facts about the *conscious universe*.
  - Distinguishes cosmopsychism from micropsychism precisely to reposition the combination problem as a *grounding* question.
  - **Grounding-by-subsumption reply** to de-combination: finite subjects are grounded in the cosmic subject by a *subsumption*-style grounding relation, where the cosmic subject's experience partially "contains" / grounds the derivative subjects without their being independent constituents. (Goff has continued to develop and revise this; treat the exact mechanism as contested, and paraphrase rather than quote unless verified against the book text.)
- **Companion paper**: Goff, "Cosmopsychism, Micropsychism and the Grounding Relation" (PhilArchive: https://philarchive.org/archive/GOFCMA) develops the grounding machinery.
- **Tenet alignment**: Conflicts with the Map's placement of irreducibility (Tenet 1) and is the position the Map most directly declines.

### Miller, "Can Subjects Be Proper Parts of Subjects? The De-Combination Problem" (2018)
- **URL**: https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/rati.12166 (record: https://philarchive.org/rec/MILCSB-2)
- **Type**: Journal article (*Ratio* 31(2):137–154; DOI 10.1111/rati.12166)
- **Verification**: CONFIRMED at publisher (Wiley) — title, journal, volume/issue, page range match.
- **Key points**:
  - Argues the **de-combination problem** is *equivalent* to the combination problem: both concern whether subjects can be *proper parts of* other subjects. Turning panpsychism on its head (one cosmos-sized mind instead of many atomic minds) does not dissolve the difficulty; it mirrors it.
  - Offers two escape routes usable by *both* camps: (i) a distinction between *absolute* and *relative* phenomenal unity, and (ii) a revision of the essential nature of subjects so subject-subject parthood is admissible.
- **Role**: The best single citation for the claim that cosmopsychism does not get its purchase for free — the symmetry between combination and de-combination is the crux the Map exploits when it declines the whole family.

### Shani & Keppler, "Beyond Combination: How Cosmic Consciousness Grounds Ordinary Experience" (2018)
- **URL**: https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/journal-of-the-american-philosophical-association/article/beyond-combination-how-cosmic-consciousness-grounds-ordinary-experience/DBFAD5F0F70A4ACE5E3FD464581B2FF5 (record: https://philpapers.org/rec/SHABCH-5)
- **Type**: Journal article (*Journal of the American Philosophical Association* 4(3):390–410).
- **Verification**: CONFIRMED at publisher (Cambridge Core) — title, journal, volume/issue, page range match.
- **Key points**:
  - A **filtering** account: ordinary experience is grounded in an all-pervading cosmic consciousness, with finite minds arising through a dynamical filtering/modulation of the cosmic field (identified, controversially, with the vacuum state of quantum field theory via stochastic electrodynamics).
  - Represents the "great chain / filtering" style of de-combination reply — finite subjects are *localizations* or *modulations* of the one field rather than parts summed from below.
- **Tenet alignment**: Conflicts (Tenet 2/3 territory too — it makes a specific physical identification of the cosmic field; the Map's minimal-interaction dualism does not posit a pervasive conscious field).

### Albahari, "Perennial Idealism: A Mystical Solution to the Mind-Body Problem" (2019)
- **URL**: https://philarchive.org/archive/ALBPIA-4 (record: https://philpapers.org/rec/ALBPIA-4)
- **Type**: Journal article (*Philosophers' Imprint* 19(44), October 2019).
- **Verification**: CONFIRMED at PhilArchive/PhilPapers — title, journal, volume/issue, year match.
- **Key points**:
  - Diagnoses panpsychism's explanatory gaps as stemming from the assumption that foundational consciousness is *perspectival*. Drop that assumption and you get **aperspectival**, universal, "non-dual" consciousness — unstructured by subject, object, or differentia.
  - **Aperspectival dissolution** of de-combination: if the ground is *not itself a bounded subject with a perspective*, then there is no "one big perspective" that must be divided into many little ones — the de-combination problem is dissolved rather than solved. Albahari flags this as a *successor* to cosmopsychism (and it leans toward idealism / perennial philosophy), so a careful article should present it as a *revision* of cosmopsychism, not a straightforward instance.
- **Tenet alignment**: Conflicts; also the closest of the cluster to analytic idealism, so it doubles as a bridge to that article.

### Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, "Panpsychism"
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/panpsychism/
- **Type**: Encyclopedia (authoritative background).
- **Verification**: CONFIRMED live.
- **Key points**: Defines constitutive cosmopsychism; ties it to Schaffer's priority monism ("facts about little things are grounded in facts about big things," cosmos as single fundamental entity); lists the proponent cluster (Mathews 2011/2020, Jaskolla & Buck 2012, Shani 2015, Nagasawa & Wager 2016, Goff 2017/2020); does **not** attribute the coinage to a single author.

## Major Positions

### Priority cosmopsychism (Nagasawa & Wager; Goff)
- **Proponents**: Yujin Nagasawa, Khai Wager, Philip Goff.
- **Core claim**: The cosmos is the one fundamental conscious subject; finite subjects are *grounded in* (posterior to) it. Grounding, not constitution, so the explanatory burden is lighter than constitutive combination.
- **Key arguments**: Escapes combination and infinite-decomposition worries by putting fundamentality at the top; keeps a Russellian-monist physics whose intrinsic nature is experiential.
- **Relation to site tenets**: The Map's core objection — this *relocates* irreducibility (Tenet 1) from the mind–matter interface into the intrinsic nature of a single whole, and then owes the de-combination problem. See "Relation to Site Perspective."

### Holistic / relational cosmopsychism (Shani; Shani & Keppler)
- **Proponents**: Itay Shani, Joachim Keppler.
- **Core claim**: One cosmic experiential field; finite experiences are *filtered / modulated* localizations of it. Answers the "lonely cosmos" worry with relational structure internal to the field.
- **Key arguments**: A concrete grounding mechanism (filtering) rather than a bare grounding posit; ambitious physical identification (SED / QFT vacuum) in the 2018 paper.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Conflicts; also makes physical commitments the Map's *minimal*-interaction stance (Tenet 2) does not share.

### Aperspectival / perennialist cosmopsychism (Albahari)
- **Proponents**: Miri Albahari.
- **Core claim**: The ground is *aperspectival* consciousness — not itself a subject with a bounded point of view — so the de-combination problem is dissolved rather than answered.
- **Key arguments**: Perspectivality is the smuggled assumption behind the gap; mystical/perennialist "direct experience" is offered as convergent evidence.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Conflicts; drifts toward idealism (cross-reference the analytic-idealism article).

### The critical / diagnostic position (Miller)
- **Proponent**: Gregory Miller.
- **Core claim**: De-combination is *not* a free lunch — it is the combination problem in a mirror; both reduce to the coherence of subject-subject proper parthood.
- **Relation to site tenets**: This is the position the Map *endorses as leverage*: the symmetry is exactly why "invert the arrow" does not rescue panpsychism, and why the Map declines the whole family.

## Key Debates

### Is de-combination easier than combination?
- **Sides**: Cosmopsychists (Goff, Nagasawa & Wager, Shani) say grounding-from-the-top is more tractable than summing-from-the-bottom; critics (Miller; many combination-problem theorists) say the two problems are structurally equivalent.
- **Core disagreement**: Whether a *grounding* (priority) relation dodges the constitutive difficulty of subjects being proper parts of subjects.
- **Current state**: Ongoing and unresolved. This symmetry is the Map's principal lever.

### Grounding vs. constitution
- **Sides**: Constitutive cosmopsychism (finite experience *built out of* cosmic experience) vs. priority cosmopsychism (finite experience merely *posterior to* cosmic experience).
- **Core disagreement**: How strong the dependence relation must be, and whether the weaker relation genuinely lightens the explanatory load or just renames the debt.
- **Current state**: Live; Goff's grounding-by-subsumption and Nagasawa & Wager's priority reply are the leading constructive attempts.

### Does cosmopsychism collapse into idealism?
- **Sides**: Those who keep it as a Russellian monism with a real (if intrinsically experiential) physical world vs. those (Albahari-adjacent) who let the physical dissolve into a non-dual conscious ground.
- **Current state**: Boundary-policing debate; relevant to distinguishing cosmopsychism from the analytic-idealism article.

## Relation to Site Perspective

The Map **declines** cosmopsychism. Framing for the "Relation to Site Perspective" section:

- **Tenet 1 (irreducibility)**: The Map and cosmopsychism *agree* consciousness is not reducible to physical process — but they *locate* the irreducibility differently. Cosmopsychism puts it in the *intrinsic nature of the one fundamental whole*; the Map puts it at the *mind–matter interface* of numerically distinct finite subjects. Cosmopsychism's placement forces the de-combination debt; the Map's does not.
- **Tenet 5 (parsimony has limits)**: Cosmopsychism markets itself as more parsimonious than micropsychism (one fundamental subject, not a swarm) and as dodging combination. The Map's response uses the decomposition-vs-combination *symmetry* (Miller): the apparent parsimony does not translate into explanatory relief, so a simplicity-driven preference for cosmopsychism is exactly the kind of over-trust in Occam that Tenet 5 warns against.
- **The de-combination problem as a differential cost**: It is a bill cosmopsychism pays that the Map's interactionist dualism structurally avoids — there is no single cosmic field and nothing to decompose. The Map does not get this for free: it pays the *interaction* problem (how does non-physical mind touch physical process, minimally, per Tenets 2–3) instead. The honest framing is *cost-swap*, not *free lunch*.
- **Closed individualism**: The Map is closed-individualist — subjects are numerically distinct and bounded. Cosmopsychism's *derived-minds* picture pressures this by making finite subjects posterior to (grounded in) a cosmic one; whether such derived subjects can be genuinely distinct and private is precisely the de-combination question. This is the sharpest point of contact and the natural place to close the "Relation to Site Perspective" section.
