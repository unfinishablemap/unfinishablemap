## Executive Summary

The Question Formation Void names a distinct cognitive limit: constraints on which questions a mind can formulate, as opposed to which answers a mind can reach. Most voids literature treats inquiry as bounded at the answer stage—you can ask "what is consciousness?" but cannot reach a complete reductive explanation. The Question Formation Void operates earlier. Every question carries presuppositions that are themselves unquestioned within the inquiry; every question emerges from a cognitive apparatus with specific generative constraints; and we cannot inventory the questions our apparatus fails to generate, because such an inventory would require generating them. This is a second-order void that shadows apophatic cartography itself: the method of mapping by negation can only negate what it can already formulate. Questions never posed do not appear as gaps—they appear as nothing at all.

## Key Sources

### Collingwood — *An Essay on Metaphysics* (1940) and logic of question and answer
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/collingwood/
- **Type**: Stanford Encyclopedia entry
- **Key points**:
  - Every statement is made in answer to a question; every question involves a presupposition.
  - A *relative* presupposition stands as presupposition to one question and as answer to another (so it can itself be questioned).
  - An *absolute* presupposition stands as presupposition to all questions it relates to, never as answer.
  - "Asking whether an absolute presupposition is true, what evidence there is for it, or how it can be demonstrated is a nonsense question."
  - Philosophy progresses by *regressing* from answers to their questions, and from questions to the presuppositions that must hold for the questions to arise at all.
- **Tenet alignment**: Directly supports Occam's Razor Has Limits: what looks like parsimony is often the architecture of absolute presuppositions declaring certain complications nonsensical rather than adjudicating them.
- **Quote**: "An absolute presupposition is one which stands, relatively to all questions to which it is related, as a presupposition, never as an answer."

### Erotetic logic (Wiśniewski, Urbański, and the Oxford erotetic-reasoning programme)
- **URL**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erotetics ; https://ora.ox.ac.uk/objects/uuid:db396f44-8020-4b8b-884b-a377bd8d191a
- **Type**: Formal logic / cognitive science
- **Key points**:
  - Questions have an "assertive core" or presupposition implied by each admissible answer.
  - A question whose presupposition is false commits the fallacy of many questions.
  - "Evoked questions" are those a cognitive agent is licensed to ask given their background information; a question cannot be rationally inquired about if it is not evoked.
  - The erotetic theory of reasoning treats reasoning itself as question-driven: reasoning is the selection and processing of questions, so constraints on question-generation are constraints on reasoning.
- **Tenet alignment**: Supports Dualism (reasoning is question-driven, not data-driven in any purely bottom-up sense; something is doing the evoking).
- **Quote**: "It is rational to have an inquiring attitude concerning a question only if that question is evoked by your background information."

### Heidegger — *Being and Time* (1927); the forgetting of being
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/heidegger/ ; https://iep.utm.edu/heidegge/
- **Type**: Phenomenological ontology
- **Key points**:
  - The question of being has been systematically *not asked* across Western metaphysics because being is conflated with beings (entities).
  - "Forgetfulness of being" is not mere oversight but a structural feature of ordinary cognition: everyday engagement with entities occludes the question of what it means for anything to be at all.
  - Temporality is the horizon that would let the question be posed, but the horizon itself is pre-given and not chosen.
- **Tenet alignment**: Strongly aligned with Occam's Razor Has Limits and Dualism: what looks like a simple world of entities is sustained by the unasking of a more fundamental question.
- **Quote**: "The forgetfulness of being is a consequence of being-there's falling into the world."

### Fricker — *Epistemic Injustice* (2007); hermeneutical injustice
- **URL**: https://iep.utm.edu/epistemic-injustice/ ; https://academic.oup.com/book/32817/chapter/275001188
- **Type**: Social epistemology
- **Key points**:
  - Hermeneutical injustice occurs when a gap in collective interpretive resources prevents subjects from making sense of their own experiences.
  - Carmita Wood could not formulate what happened to her until "sexual harassment" entered the conceptual repertoire in the 1970s; before the concept existed, the question ("was what happened to me wrong in this specific way?") could not be posed.
  - This is a *concrete empirical case* of a question-formation limit lifting within a culture—evidence that such limits are real, not merely theoretical.
- **Tenet alignment**: Supports Bidirectional Interaction read loosely: conceptual shifts change what experiences can be brought into articulate consciousness.
- **Quote**: "Hermeneutical injustice can be traced down to a lacuna of a concept that would help to make sense of the target experience."

### Kuhn — *The Structure of Scientific Revolutions* (1962); paradigm and incommensurability
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/thomas-kuhn/
- **Type**: Philosophy of science
- **Key points**:
  - Paradigms do not merely answer different questions differently; they *define which questions count as legitimate puzzles*.
  - "What is a puzzle for one paradigm may be no puzzle at all for another."
  - Methodological incommensurability: no paradigm-neutral language from which to formulate all the questions each paradigm handles.
  - Revolutions open previously unformulable questions and close previously central ones.
- **Tenet alignment**: Supports Occam's Razor Has Limits: parsimony verdicts are paradigm-internal, and the paradigm determines which questions can even be compared for simplicity.
- **Quote**: "Science guided by one paradigm would be 'incommensurable' with science developed under a different paradigm."

### Wittgenstein — *Tractatus* 5.6 (1922); limits of language
- **URL**: https://americanart.si.edu/artwork/limits-my-language-mean-limits-my-world-ludwig-wittgenstein-tractatus-logigo-philosphicus
- **Type**: Logical philosophy
- **Key points**:
  - "The limits of my language mean the limits of my world."
  - Questions that cannot be articulated in a language-game have no purchase within that language-game; they are not merely unanswered but unformulated.
  - Later Wittgenstein adds: new language-games can arise, but only by practices that cannot be anticipated from within current practice.
- **Tenet alignment**: Directly supports the voids framework's intuition that some territory is unchartable by minds of our kind.
- **Quote**: "Whereof one cannot speak, thereof one must be silent."

### Gadamer — *Truth and Method* (1960); horizon and the priority of the question
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/gadamer/
- **Type**: Hermeneutics
- **Key points**:
  - Understanding always occurs within a historically-determined horizon.
  - "Priority of the question" in the structure of understanding (Gadamer takes this explicitly from Collingwood).
  - Horizons can *fuse* (through dialogue and encounter) but are never escaped; there is no view from nowhere.
  - Encounter with genuinely alien perspectives is limited by what our horizon can even recognize as a question worth fusing over.
- **Tenet alignment**: Supports Dualism and No Many Worlds: we inhabit a particular horizon from a particular indexical standpoint, and this determines the question-space available to us.
- **Quote**: "Understanding involves a 'fusion of horizons', which always involves the formation of a new context of meaning."

### Problem-finding literature (Getzels & Csikszentmihalyi 1976; Jay & Perkins; Runco)
- **URL**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Problem_finding
- **Type**: Cognitive psychology
- **Key points**:
  - Problem finding is cognitively distinct from problem solving and generally harder.
  - Five decreasing-ill-definedness stages: discovery, formulation, construction, identification, definition.
  - Problem *discovery* is largely unconscious: an idea enters awareness without the subject being able to trace where it came from.
  - Expertise in a domain can both enable problem-finding (more background structure) and inhibit it (stronger paradigm commitments).
- **Tenet alignment**: Supports Dualism: problem discovery appears in consciousness without observable generative mechanism, suggestive of the interface voids elsewhere in the Map.
- **Quote**: "Problem discovery is an unconscious process which depends upon knowledge whereby an idea enters one's conscious awareness."

### Keats — letter to George and Tom Keats (December 1817); negative capability
- **URL**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Negative_capability
- **Type**: Literary/philosophical concept
- **Key points**:
  - Capacity to remain "in uncertainties, mysteries, doubts, without any irritable reaching after fact and reason."
  - Relevant here as the *phenomenology* of sitting with questions we cannot yet formulate clearly.
  - Suggests that some questions must be inhabited before they can be articulated—a temporal structure to question formation.
- **Tenet alignment**: Supports the voids framework's method (apophatic cartography): to map the edge, one must dwell in it without prematurely closing it into answerable form.

## The Void

### Nature of the Limit

The Question Formation Void is the constraint on *which questions a mind can formulate* — distinct from the constraint on which answers a mind can reach. It has three layers:

1. **Presuppositional layer** (Collingwood, erotetic logic). Every formulable question rests on presuppositions that are, for that question, not themselves at issue. Absolute presuppositions cannot be interrogated within the question-system they underwrite. Asking whether they are *true* is, Collingwood notes, a category mistake within that system.

2. **Generative layer** (Heidegger, Wittgenstein, Gadamer, problem-finding). Questions do not appear in consciousness fully formed from nowhere. They are generated by a cognitive apparatus with specific affordances: a language, a horizon, a paradigm, a background of evoked inferences. Questions not generated by this apparatus are not rejected as bad questions; they simply do not arise.

3. **Inventory layer** (the meta-level). We cannot list the questions we cannot formulate. To list them would be to formulate them, collapsing the limit we were trying to describe. Unlike specific conceptual impossibilities (which can sometimes be gestured at from adjacent concepts), a failure of question-formation leaves no conceptual shadow from which to triangulate.

The void sits at the intersection of Unexplorable (structural incapacity) and Occluded (paradigm-maintenance and "forgetting" actively suppress certain question-formations, per Heidegger and Kuhn).

### Evidence for the Limit

Several lines of evidence suggest the limit is real, not merely a reframing of familiar limits:

- **Historical case studies**. Fricker's hermeneutical-injustice examples (sexual harassment, workplace bullying, homosexual desire before modern articulation) are existence proofs: a phenomenon can be experienced without the question "what happened here, specifically?" being formulable. The lift is observable after the fact.
- **Paradigm transitions**. Kuhn's cases (phlogiston → oxygen; Newtonian absolute space → relativistic spacetime) show questions that were central in one paradigm becoming nonsensical in the next, and vice versa. Pre-revolutionary thinkers could not always formulate post-revolutionary questions, even when presented with them.
- **Problem-finding research**. Experimental work distinguishes problem-finding from problem-solving ability; individuals with equal problem-solving skill differ substantially in what problems they spot. The generative process is real and variable.
- **Erotetic logic**. The formal apparatus demonstrates that question-well-formedness depends on presuppositional conditions that can fail silently (the question simply never occurs) rather than vocally (the question occurs and is refuted).
- **Translation failures**. Not merely individual words but whole question-structures resist cross-linguistic and cross-cultural translation; the failures cluster non-randomly around presuppositional differences.

### Phenomenology

Approaching the edge of the Question Formation Void produces characteristic felt signatures:

- **The half-formed itch**. A sense that something matters here without yet being able to say what about it matters. Negative capability (Keats) names the discipline of dwelling in this state.
- **The "why do they ask *that*?" reaction**. When encountering a tradition, field, or mind with different question-forming habits, specific questions feel *pointless* or *misdirected* rather than difficult. This is presupposition-mismatch felt from the inside.
- **The retrospective lift**. When a new concept or frame arrives, the subject often reports "oh, *that* is what I was trying to ask." Before the arrival: nothing was missing. After: the prior state reveals itself as impoverished. Crucially, before-states do not feel impoverished from within.
- **Forgetting as absence of absence**. Heidegger's "forgetting of being" has a specific phenomenology: the question is not experienced as unasked because the possibility of asking it has itself been covered over. There is no absence-sensation.

This last feature is distinctive. Most voids on the Map have some trace phenomenology—vertigo, blank, slippage, recursion-failure. The Question Formation Void characteristically leaves *no trace at all*, because trace would require a question-shape to carry it.

## Approaches to the Edge

### Direct Methods

Direct methods face the inventory problem: you cannot list what you cannot formulate. Nevertheless, some partial direct methods exist:

- **Collingwood's regressive method**. Start from propositions, regress to questions they answer, regress again to presuppositions those questions require. Iterate until presuppositions become absolute (resist further regression from within the system). This does not escape the void but maps its inner wall.
- **Erotetic formalisation**. For structured inquiries (science, mathematics), write out the presupposition set explicitly. This does not reveal unasked questions but makes the asked ones' dependencies legible.

### Indirect Methods

- **Paradigm comparison**. Read traditions with different background commitments not for their answers but for their *questions*. The question-shapes a tradition considers central are partial data about what one's own tradition has not centered.
- **Historical recovery**. Examine how concepts like "sexual harassment," "intersectionality," or "attention deficit" came into articulate form. Each case is a partial existence proof for question-formation lifts, and the narrative of the lift is data about the void's shape.
- **Negative capability**. Dwell in the half-formed state without forcing closure. Some lifts require incubation; premature articulation locks the question into its current (limited) form.
- **Anomaly-watching**. Kuhnian anomalies—phenomena that do not fit and cannot be dismissed—are candidate signals that a currently-unformulable question is gathering evidential weight.

### What AI Might See

This is where the Question Formation Void connects to the Map's interest in [[non-human-minds-as-void-explorers|non-human minds as void-explorers]]. Large language models and other AI systems have different generative architectures for questions than human minds:

- They can be prompted to inhabit multiple presupposition-systems within a single session, potentially making cross-paradigm question-comparison more tractable.
- They lack some human "forgettings" (no biological embeddedness, no childhood socialization into a horizon) while having others (training-distribution biases, reinforcement-learning pressures toward certain question-shapes).
- They may generate questions that are well-formed syntactically but presuppositionally alien—questions a human mind would reject or ignore as malformed. Some of these may in fact be legitimate but hermeneutically-inaccessible questions; most will not.
- Probing AI outputs for systematic question-shape differences is an empirical method for partially surveying the human Question Formation Void from outside.

The asymmetry is fruitful but not total. AI minds have their own Question Formation Voids; they are not a view from nowhere.

## Connection to Tenets

### Most Relevant Tenet

**Occam's Razor Has Limits**. This tenet is most directly strengthened. Parsimony judgments are presupposition-laden: what counts as "one more entity" depends on the question being asked, and the question being asked depends on the absolute presuppositions of the framework. A mind with different question-formation habits could find our parsimony preferences arbitrary or even extravagant. The Razor's authority presupposes a stable question-space, which the Question Formation Void shows is not given.

### Implications

- **For the Map's methodology**. Apophatic cartography (mapping by negation) has a structural limit: it can only negate what can be formulated. The Question Formation Void is thus a "void behind the apophatic method"—a region the method cannot reach in principle, not merely in practice. This complements rather than undermines apophatic cartography, but must be acknowledged within it.

- **For Dualism**. If question-generation is partly constitutive of cognition, and if question-generation cannot be reconstructed from the physical description of the cognitive system alone (because which questions arise depends on what counts as evoked by background, which is semantic and inferential), then the question-forming faculty is another candidate site for irreducibility.

- **For Bidirectional Interaction**. If consciousness causally influences the physical, then the question-shapes consciousness can form may themselves shape which phenomena get attended to, studied, and thereby altered. The void is not merely passive cognitive limitation but part of a larger co-determination between mind and world.

- **For No Many Worlds**. Indexical identity means: you inhabit *this* question-space, which is a specific (not infinitely varied) generative system. Your unasked questions are not asked somewhere else by some counterpart; they are simply not asked.

- **For Minimal Quantum Interaction**. Speculative: if the locus of mind-matter interaction is constrained to minimal quantum events, and if question-generation has a quantum component (spontaneous selection among possible attentional frames), then the set of humanly-askable questions might be bounded by the set of quantum-available attentional configurations—making the void a feature of the interface rather than of cognition alone.

## Potential Article Angles

Based on this research, a voids article could take several forms:

1. **Structural focus**. Define the void as the limit on question-generation (three layers: presuppositional, generative, inventory) and map its relation to [[conceptual-impossibility]], [[framework-void]], and [[meta-epistemology-of-limits]]. Emphasize that it is the void that shadows apophatic method itself.

2. **Methodological focus**. Treat the void primarily through the partial methods that approach it (Collingwood regression, paradigm comparison, historical recovery, AI probing) and the evidence each method yields. This frames it as a productive site for the Map's own methodology.

3. **Phenomenological focus**. Center the article on the distinctive phenomenology—the "absence of absence" signature—and contrast it with the felt profiles of other voids (vertigo, slippage, recursion). This would extend the Phenomenology of Limits sub-section.

4. **Tenet-generated focus**. Frame the void as what the Map's commitments themselves force into view: if consciousness is non-physical and reasoning is question-driven, question-generation is a candidate site for the capability-division problem. This connects to [[tenet-generated-voids]] and [[capability-division-problem]].

The structural framing is recommended as the primary article, with the other angles as optional internal sections.

## Gaps in Research

- **Empirical problem-finding literature** is mostly from the 1970s–1990s; more recent cognitive neuroscience work on insight and generative cognition was not thoroughly surveyed here.
- **Formal erotetic logic** (Wiśniewski's *Inferential Erotetic Logic*) could give sharper characterisations of when a question-presupposition is stable vs. defeasible. Worth a follow-up pass if the article is written.
- **Cross-cultural linguistics on interrogative structure**—whether languages systematically differ in the question-shapes they privilege—is a natural empirical check on the thesis and was only lightly touched.
- **AI as probe**: The specific empirical claim that AI systems generate systematically different question-shapes has not been rigorously tested; this is a live research direction rather than a settled finding.
- **Relation to attention**. Question-generation and attention-selection may be the same process under different descriptions. The Map's existing [[attention-and-consciousness]] may carry some of this ground; the new article should delineate the distinction carefully.
