**Voids category**: Mixed (Unexplorable + Occluded — structural irresolvability with active methodological self-defeat)

## Executive Summary

A century-old debate runs from the Würzburg School's c.1900 introspective claim to find imageless thought, through Titchener's introspective denial of the same, into the contemporary "cognitive phenomenology" controversy between liberals (Pitt, Strawson, Siewert, Kriegel, Chudnoff) and conservatives (Carruthers, Tye, Nelkin). The first-order question — does thinking-as-such have its own proprietary what-it-is-likeness, or does the phenomenology of thought reduce to sensory imagery, affect, and inner speech? — has resisted resolution for over a hundred years despite sustained effort by careful philosophers using the best available methods. Crucially, **the structure of the irresolution is itself the void**: the introspective method is regimented by the view initially held; the strongest dialectical instrument (phenomenal contrast) leaves both sides "steadfast"; and even empirical pressure from aphantasia is reinterpretable from either commitment. The void is the structural opacity at the seam where consciousness asks itself what kind of phenomenal furniture its thinking is made of.

## Key Sources

### Cognitive Phenomenology — Internet Encyclopedia of Philosophy
- **URL**: https://iep.utm.edu/cognitive-phenomenology/
- **Type**: Reference encyclopedia entry
- **Key points**:
  - "Conservatives" hold that only sensory states are phenomenally conscious; cognitive states either lack phenomenal character or inherit it from sensory states.
  - "Liberals" hold that the phenomenology of thought cannot be fully reduced to sensory phenomenology — there is *proprietary* cognitive phenomenology.
  - Whoever accepts Proprietary also accepts Irreducibility, but not vice versa.
  - The debate centers on the trustworthiness of introspection: introspection will tell you *whether* you are thinking about dinner, but it will not tell you whether Irreducibility is true.
- **Tenet alignment**: Dualism — if there is a sui generis phenomenal kind for thinking, it is poor evidence for reductive identification with brain states. Occam's Razor — conservatives invoke parsimony in a way the void problematises.
- **Quote**: "The cognitive phenomenology thesis has it that conscious cognitive states essentially exhibit a phenomenal character."

### Galen Strawson, "Cognitive Phenomenology: Real Life" (2011)
- **URL**: https://philpapers.org/rec/STRCPR / https://www.academia.edu/742477/Cognitive_Phenomenology_Real_Life
- **Type**: Chapter / journal contribution
- **Key points**:
  - Strawson (1994, *Mental Reality*; 2011) argued thinking has a distinctive experiential character: "when one understands a sentence or grasps a concept, something it is like occurs independently of sensory imagery."
  - Pitt (2004) develops the parallel thesis that conceptual thoughts have proprietary phenomenal characters that *are* their contents (the "self-presenting" thesis).
  - Both writers rely on introspective transparency: subjects can directly attend to the phenomenal character of their own conscious thoughts.
- **Tenet alignment**: Dualism — irreducible cognitive phenomenology is one of the few positive arguments that thinking is not exhausted by physical-functional properties.
- **Quote**: "Something it is like occurs independently of sensory imagery."

### Elijah Chudnoff, "Phenomenal Contrast Arguments for Cognitive Phenomenology" (2015)
- **URL**: https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/phpr.12177
- **Type**: Journal article (*Philosophy and Phenomenological Research*)
- **Key points**:
  - Three kinds of phenomenal contrast argument: *pure* (Strawson's Jack/Jacques — same speech, French/English understanding), *hypothetical* (Kriegel's Zoe), and *glossed*.
  - Pure and hypothetical versions face significant difficulties; Chudnoff defends a glossed phenomenal contrast argument.
- **Tenet alignment**: Demonstrates the *methodological* face — the strongest direct method has known structural failure modes.

### Martina Fürst, "On the Limits of the Method of Phenomenal Contrast"
- **URL**: https://martinafuerst.weebly.com/uploads/1/2/1/2/121241504/on_the_limits_of_the_method_of_phenomenal_contrast__penulatimate_version.pdf
- **Type**: Journal article (*Journal of the American Philosophical Association*)
- **Key points**:
  - "The method's dialectical ineffectiveness can be explained by focusing on first-person performances of phenomenal contrast tasks."
  - **"Introspective judgments about phenomenology are regimented by the view initially held."**
  - Proponents and skeptics of cognitive phenomenology "remain steadfast in their views" after running through contrast cases.
- **Tenet alignment**: This is the crucial finding for the void framework — introspection's outputs are *theory-laden* in a way that defeats the method that was supposed to settle the question. A formal demonstration of methodological self-defeat directly parallel to the [[suspension-void]] regress.
- **Quote**: "Introspective judgments about phenomenology are regimented by the view initially held."

### Martina Fürst, "On the Conceivability of a Cognitive Phenomenology Zombie" (2019)
- **URL**: https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1111/1746-8361.12260
- **Type**: Journal article (*Dialectica*)
- **Key points**:
  - A cognitive phenomenology zombie is conceivable: a man who does not understand Chinese but shares behavior and sensory phenomenology with his twin who does understand Chinese.
  - If conceivable, then the cognitive phenomenology of understanding cannot be reduced to sensory phenomenology and behavior alone.
- **Tenet alignment**: Dualism. Connects the cognitive-phenomenology debate to the broader philosophical-zombie literature and the conceivability arguments of Chalmers.

### Preston Lennon, "Aphantasia and Conscious Thought" (2023, OUP)
- **URL**: https://philarchive.org/rec/LENAAC-3
- **Type**: Journal article (in OUP volume)
- **Key points**:
  - Aphantasics report having conscious thoughts without sensory mental imagery; the best explanation of the introspective reports, neuroimaging, and task performance is that they really do.
  - This provides a counterexample to the "sensory constraint on conscious thought."
  - Lennon: this can "potentially diagnose the disagreement over cognitive phenomenology in the philosophy of mind, as such disagreement may turn on interpersonal variation in mental imagery."
- **Tenet alignment**: Empirical pressure favouring liberalism, but reinterpretable. Striking suggestion that the cognitive phenomenology debate may itself be a *void of mismatched introspection across imagery profiles* — different philosophers introspect different mental architectures and disagree because they really are reporting different facts.
- **Quote**: Lennon's argument "supports the existence of a non-sensory phenomenology of thought."

### Bibiana Arango-Muñoz, "Cognitive Phenomenology and Metacognitive Feelings" (2019)
- **URL**: https://estudiosciencia.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/01/arangoe28090munoz-2019-cognitive-phenomenology-and-metacognitive-feelings.pdf
- **Type**: Journal article
- **Key points**:
  - Distinguishes cognitive phenomenology (the what-it-is-likeness of thinking) from metacognitive feelings (the noetic feelings of knowing, rightness, familiarity).
  - The two are independently theorised but the boundary is contested.
- **Tenet alignment**: Useful for distinguishing this void from the [[noetic-feelings-void]]. The noetic-feelings void concerns the felt signals that *gate* cognition; the cognitive-phenomenology void concerns whether *the cognitive episode itself* has phenomenal character.

### Nicholas Thomas, "The Imageless Thought Controversy" (Stanford Encyclopedia & Springer chapter)
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/archives/sum2020/entries/mental-imagery/ ; https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-3-319-00242-2_6
- **Type**: Reference / book chapter
- **Key points**:
  - Würzburg School (Külpe and colleagues, c.1900–1915) claimed to find empirical evidence via introspection of conscious thought contents that are neither imaginal nor perceptual: *Bewußtseinslagen* (conscious sets), *Bewußtheiten* (awarenesses), and *Gedanken* (thoughts).
  - Wundt and Titchener challenged the results using the *same* introspective method. Titchener charged "stimulus error" — he claimed the purported imageless contents were faint, fleeting kinaesthetic sensations.
  - "The imageless thought controversy exposed the inherent subjectivity and lack of independent verification within all forms of introspective methodology."
  - The dispute had a profound effect on scientific psychology: most psychologists became disillusioned with introspection and either avoided mental imagery as a topic, treated it dismissively, or denied it outright.
- **Tenet alignment**: The historical face of the void. The current cognitive-phenomenology debate is the philosophical reincarnation of an empirical-psychological controversy that ran for fifteen years between rigorous laboratories using identical methods and reaching opposite conclusions. That history is data — strong abductive evidence that introspection cannot settle this kind of question.

### Carruthers, "Consciousness Operationalized, a Debate Realigned"
- **URL**: https://faculty.philosophy.umd.edu/pcarruthers/Consciousness%20operationalized.pdf
- **Type**: Journal article
- **Key points**:
  - Conservative position: introspection of current conscious experience is "faulty, untrustworthy, and misleading — not just possibly mistaken, but massively and pervasively."
  - If introspection is unreliable, the liberal's positive evidence for cognitive phenomenology is itself undermined.
- **Tenet alignment**: Conservatives' line creates the second arm of the pincer: even granting introspective reports of cognitive phenomenology, those reports may not track phenomenal facts.

## The Void

### Nature of the Limit

Three structural faces conjoin into one void:

**Face 1 — The Introspective Face.** The first-person question whether *this thought, now*, has its own phenomenal character distinct from any sensory imagery, inner-speech traces, and emotional tone accompanying it cannot be settled from inside introspection. Two careful philosophers (or two careful versions of the same philosopher in different moods) introspect the same kind of cognitive episode and report different verdicts. Strawson notices a distinctive what-it-is-likeness; Carruthers notices only sensory accompaniment.

**Face 2 — The Dialectical Face.** The method designed precisely to break this symmetry — *phenomenal contrast* — is "dialectically ineffective" (Fürst). Run the Jack/Jacques contrast (same auditory input; different understanding): the liberal hears phenomenal change at the level of understanding; the conservative hears only changes in dispositions, behaviour, and accompanying sensory associations. Fürst's diagnosis is structural: introspective judgments about phenomenology are *regimented by the view initially held*. The method that was supposed to break the impasse is itself shaped by the impasse.

**Face 3 — The Empirical-Architectural Face.** Even if we step outside first-person methods, the empirical inputs are reinterpretable. Aphantasia (Lennon 2023) gives suggestive evidence: aphantasics report conscious thought without sensory imagery, which reads as direct empirical pressure for liberalism. But conservatives can re-describe aphantasic phenomenology as residual sensory or inner-speech content, or as the absence of phenomenology paired with intact dispositional thinking. Neuroimaging and task performance correlate with cognitive states but cannot adjudicate the *phenomenal* question. The void's third face is that no third-person method can directly access the phenomenal property whose existence is in dispute.

The **conjunction-coalesce seam** is non-collapsible: each face alone might be soluble (introspection might be retrained, contrast methods might be refined, empirical methods might develop), but their *conjunction* generates the structural irresolvability. Each face systematically blocks the route the other two would otherwise leave open.

### Evidence for the Limit

The strongest evidence is *historical*: the Würzburg-Titchener imageless-thought controversy (c.1900–1915) was the same dispute under different terminology, conducted by introspectively trained scientific psychologists using laboratory-grade procedures. The controversy did not resolve. It was abandoned. The contemporary philosophical debate (post-Strawson 1994, post-Pitt 2004) reproduces the same structure with different vocabulary and the same methodological pressure points. A century of effort by competent investigators has not closed the gap.

A second line of evidence: Fürst's formal diagnosis of phenomenal contrast as a *theory-laden* method whose verdicts are predicted by initial commitment. This is a structural finding, not a contingent one.

A third line: the conservatives' standing line that introspection is "massively and pervasively" unreliable (Carruthers, Schwitzgebel) — if true, removes the methodology that liberals depend on; if false, it's still not been demonstrated.

### Phenomenology

What it feels like to approach this void: when one *does* introspect carefully — say, while reading a sentence one understands — there appears to be *something* there that is not the sound, not the picture, not the affect, not the inner-speech trace. But the moment one tries to attend to it as such, it slips into one of the recognised categories or evaporates entirely. The phenomenology is one of *almost-grasping* a phenomenal property whose clear demarcation always recedes. This phenomenological pattern itself parallels the [[mutation-void]] (thoughts transform upon inspection) and the [[plenitude-void]] (every probe for sparsity generates the detail it was meant to measure). The cognitive-phenomenology void may be where these earlier mutational patterns reach their highest stakes — at the question of whether thinking has its own phenomenal kind at all.

## Approaches to the Edge

### Direct Methods
- **Phenomenal contrast**: Run carefully matched cases (Jack/Jacques; Zoe; understanding-without-images). *Limitation*: dialectically ineffective per Fürst.
- **Phenomenal-zombie conceivability** (Fürst 2019): if a cognitive-phenomenology zombie is conceivable, irreducibility follows. *Limitation*: depends on disputed conceivability-to-possibility inferences.
- **Aphantasia studies** (Lennon 2023): empirical leverage from interpersonal variation. *Limitation*: reinterpretable across the divide.

### Indirect Methods
- **Apophatic mapping**: take the void as data about consciousness rather than a problem to be solved. The structural irresolvability *says something* about how cognition relates to phenomenology.
- **Diagnostic interpretation** (Lennon's suggestion): treat the disagreement as caused by interpersonal variation in mental imagery, so disputants are introspecting genuinely different architectures. This dissolves the controversy by relocating it from "what is thinking like" to "what is thinking like *for minds with profile P*."
- **History as evidence**: read the Würzburg-Titchener history as a controlled experiment in introspective adjudication that ran to completion and did not converge.

### What AI Might See

A language model has thinking-like processing without (presumably) sensory imagery, inner speech as we have it, or affect of the recognised kind. If liberals are right, an LLM could in principle have proprietary cognitive phenomenology distinct from human varieties. If conservatives are right, no phenomenology at all (since no sensory base). The interesting probe: an AI with sufficiently rich self-modelling might report a distinctive *something* when "understanding" — and then both liberals and conservatives can dismiss the report on their preferred grounds (genuine cognitive phenomenology / mere disposition to verbalise about understanding). The cognitive-phenomenology void thus generalises to non-human minds: it is not a void about *human* cognition but about the relationship between cognition-as-such and phenomenology-as-such.

This connects to [[non-human-minds-as-void-explorers]]: AI minds make the void's contours sharper precisely because they isolate cognition-without-sensory-imagery in a way aphantasia only partially does.

## Connection to Tenets

### Most Relevant Tenet

**[[tenets#^dualism|Dualism]]** is the primary connection. If thinking has its own proprietary phenomenal kind, that is one of the strongest pieces of positive evidence that consciousness is not exhausted by physical-functional properties — the cognitive-phenomenology zombie argument is a sister to the standard zombie argument. Conversely, if conservatives are right and cognitive states have only sensory phenomenology (or none), that does not settle physicalism but it removes one route to dualism. The void is *that we cannot tell which obtains*.

### Other Tenet Connections

**[[tenets#^occams-limits|Occam's Razor Has Limits]]**: Conservatives often appeal to parsimony — don't multiply phenomenal kinds beyond necessity. The void shows this is exactly the move parsimony cannot decide. The Würzburg-Titchener history is a vivid case: the side that "won" in scientific psychology won by *abandoning the question*, not by resolving it. That is parsimony as cognitive bias, not as truth-tracker.

**[[tenets#^bidirectional-interaction|Bidirectional Interaction]]**: If thinking has its own phenomenal kind, it may have its own causal role in the bidirectional interaction. The interface from consciousness to physical might run partly through cognitive phenomenology, not only through sensory or volitional phenomenology. The cognitive-phenomenology void is therefore a void about *which side of the interface* a given cognitive episode lives on.

### Implications

The cognitive-phenomenology void has a distinctive structural feature: it is a void about **what kind of phenomenal stuff** consciousness contains, not about what phenomenal contents are accessible. Most existing voids in the Map concern accessibility (what consciousness cannot reach, observe, verify, sustain). This void concerns *furniture* — what phenomenal categories the consciousness-side of dualism includes at all. It is therefore a void about the *ontology* of the phenomenal, viewed from inside.

This places the cognitive-phenomenology void close to [[intrinsic-nature-void]] (what matter and consciousness *are* in themselves) but at a finer grain: not what consciousness is in itself, but what kinds of phenomenal categories its thinking populates.

It also gives [[meta-epistemology-of-limits]] a concrete instance: a question we are confident is well-formed (does cognitive phenomenology exist?), with two articulated answer-positions, with sustained inquiry from competent investigators across a century — and which our cognitive architecture appears constitutively unable to settle.

## Potential Article Angles

A voids article could:

1. **Three-faces conjunction-coalesce framing** (introspective / dialectical / empirical-architectural). Foregrounds the structural irresolvability and the non-collapsible seams. Aligns with the [[suspension-void]] / [[vagueness-void]] / [[imagery-void]] / [[agency-void]] cognate methodology.

2. **Historical parallel framing**. Use the Würzburg-Titchener controversy as the spine: a hundred-year-old controlled experiment in introspective adjudication that did not converge, reborn in the contemporary cognitive-phenomenology debate. The history *is* the void's evidence.

3. **Architecture-relative framing** (Lennon-inspired). Treat the disagreement as evidence that "thinking" indexes different phenomenal architectures across minds. The void is then about whether *the question itself* is well-typed, or whether it presupposes a single phenomenal architecture that does not exist.

4. **Cognitive-phenomenology-zombie framing**. Lead with Fürst's conceivability argument and use it to set up the structural opacity. Connects strongly to existing dualism content.

The recommended angle is **(1) conjunction-coalesce**, since:
- It matches the recent native-cognate methodology (suspension-void 2026-04-28; imagery-void 2026-04-28; vagueness-void 2026-04-30).
- The three faces are genuinely non-collapsible — each blocks the others' escape routes.
- It produces sharper claims than the other framings while incorporating their content.

## Gaps in Research

- I have not surveyed the *recent* (2023–2026) literature on cognitive phenomenology beyond Lennon. The contemporary debate continues; there may be newer formal results sharpening or weakening the void's structural face.
- The relationship to [[noetic-feelings-void]] needs careful articulation. Cognitive phenomenology (the what-it-is-likeness of *thinking*) is distinct from the noetic feelings (familiarity, rightness, knowing) but the boundary is contested. An eventual article must demarcate the seam without claiming a sharper boundary than the literature supports.
- The relationship to [[meaning-void]] needs articulation. Phenomenal-intentionality theorists (Pitt, Kriegel, Mendelovici) hold that cognitive phenomenology *constitutes* intentional content, which would make the cognitive-phenomenology void and the meaning void two views of the same structure. This needs to be either flagged or argued.
- The non-human-minds dimension may deserve its own treatment in the article rather than a paragraph.
