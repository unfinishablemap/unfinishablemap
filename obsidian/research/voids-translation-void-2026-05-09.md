**Voids category**: Mixed (Unexplorable + Occluded)

## Executive Summary

Consciousness routinely renders content from one representational format into another — phenomenal experience into language, terms in one language into terms in another, structure in one domain into structure in another — but cannot inspect the rendering operation while performing it. The Translation Void names the conjoint structural opacity around three faces of this transformational competence: the phenomenal-to-linguistic face (putting experience into words), the cross-lexical face (untranslatable terms revealing language-specific carvings), and the analogical face (structure-mapping between domains). Each face has been documented in different literatures — ineffability research, positive cross-cultural lexicography, structure-mapping theory — but no existing voids article treats them as a single architectural feature. The seam is that all three are transformation operations whose mechanism is structurally unavailable to the consciousness performing them: from inside the source, the operation feels self-evident; from inside the operator position, the criteria are opaque; only from a third-person or after-the-fact stance can the gap be partially located. This makes the void a candidate conjunction-coalesce cognate alongside [[suspension-void]], [[vagueness-void]], [[imagery-void]], and [[aspect-perception-void]].

## Key Sources

### Ji, Elmoznino et al. — *Sources of Richness and Ineffability for Phenomenally Conscious States* (2024)

- **URL**: https://academic.oup.com/nc/article/2024/1/niae001/7623923
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed paper, *Neuroscience of Consciousness*
- **Key points**:
  - Information-theoretic dynamical-systems account of why conscious experience seems both rich and ineffable.
  - Richness corresponds to information in a conscious state; ineffability corresponds to information lost at processing stages between trajectory and report.
  - Trajectories specify activity of billions of neurons; working memory can hold a handful of items. The compression ratio is the structural source of ineffability.
  - "The discrete symbolic nature of language is insufficient for describing the rich and high-dimensional structure of experiences." Translation from phenomenal to linguistic format is loss-bounded by the format mismatch itself.
  - Similarity in cognitive function relates to improved communicability — the more two minds resemble each other in processing architecture, the less is lost in translation between them.
- **Tenet alignment**: Tenet 1 (Dualism) — quantifies how much the phenomenal exceeds the symbolic; Tenet 5 (Occam's Razor Has Limits) — the apparent simplicity of "saying what you experience" hides a high-dimensional-to-low-dimensional compression.
- **Quote**: "The magnitude of information loss from trajectory to working memory output is expected to be significantly large, since trajectories are sequences of brain states specifying the activity of billions of neurons, whereas working memory appears to be limited to representing a handful of items."

### Lomas — Positive Cross-Cultural Lexicography (2016, 2018, ongoing)

- **URL**: https://academic.oup.com/nc/article/2024/1/niae001 / https://www.drtimlomas.com/lexicography
- **Type**: Empirical psychology project + corpus
- **Key points**:
  - Initial corpus of 216 untranslatable words across world languages pertaining to well-being; corpus has since grown past 1,400.
  - Six emergent categories: positive feelings, ambivalent feelings, love, prosociality, character, spirituality.
  - The project shows that positive psychology's English-language carvings systematically miss phenomenally available distinctions (Tagalog *gigil*, Portuguese *saudade*, Japanese *mono no aware*, German *Sehnsucht*).
  - Lomas's interpretive claim: speakers of a source language are not aware of the carving as a *carving* — it feels like a transparent picking-out of a self-evident phenomenon. The translation gap is invisible to the source speaker until a translator-position is occupied.
- **Tenet alignment**: Tenet 1 (Dualism) — the phenomenal landscape is broader than any single language carves it; Tenet 5 (Occam's Razor Has Limits) — the parsimonious assumption that English-language emotion vocabulary is psychologically universal is empirically wrong.
- **Quote**: "Much of people's perception and understanding of life is influenced by their language ... mainly western-centric academic psychology would benefit from a greater engagement with other cultures and languages."

### Hofstadter & Sander — *Surfaces and Essences: Analogy as the Fuel and Fire of Thinking* (2013)

- **URL**: https://archive.org/details/surfacesessences0000hofs
- **Type**: Book, cognitive science / philosophy of mind
- **Key points**:
  - Strong claim: analogy is *the* core cognitive operation, not a peripheral or specialised one. "Analogy-making, far from happening at rare intervals, occurs at all moments, defining thinking from top to toe, from the tiniest and most fleeting thoughts to the most creative scientific insights."
  - The mechanism by which analogical mapping occurs is described as an "incessant, unconscious quest for strong analogical links to past experiences" — operating below introspective access.
  - Categorisation itself is reframed as a special case of analogy-making (recognising a particular as analogous to a stored prototype).
  - The reflexive consequence: if analogy is the core of cognition, and the analogy-making operation is unconscious, then the core of cognition is opaque to the cognising consciousness. This is the void claim, though Hofstadter does not frame it that way.
- **Tenet alignment**: Tenet 1 (Dualism) — phenomenal access reaches the *output* of categorisation (the felt "this is a chair") not the *operation* that produces it; Tenet 5 (Occam's Razor Has Limits) — categorisation appears simple, but absorbs analogy's high complexity.
- **Quote**: "Reveals the constantly churning cognitive mechanisms normally completely hidden from view."

### Gentner — Structure-Mapping Theory (1983; Gentner & Markman 1997; Parsons et al. 2022 neural)

- **URLs**: https://groups.psych.northwestern.edu/gentner/papers/Gentner83.2b.pdf; https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/cogs.13116
- **Type**: Theoretical framework + empirical studies + neuroimaging
- **Key points**:
  - Analogy is mapping of *relations* (not attributes) from a base domain to a target domain.
  - The systematicity principle: people prefer mappings where higher-order relations connect lower-order ones — the felt-rightness of an analogy tracks structural connectedness, not surface similarity.
  - The mapping process operates on representations the analogiser does not have introspective access to. The output (the felt analogy) is delivered to consciousness; the selection of base, the ranking of candidate mappings, and the rejection of disanalogies happen below awareness.
  - Neural evidence localises the mapping operation in left rostrolateral prefrontal cortex (RLPFC) and frontopolar cortex (Bunge et al. 2005; Parsons et al. 2022). The third-person mechanism is partly observable; the first-person operation is not.
  - Empirical extension: the same mapping process applies across conceptual, perceptual, causal, mathematical, and social content — supporting the unified-operation framing.
- **Tenet alignment**: Tenet 1 (Dualism) — the dissociation between phenomenal access (output: the felt fit) and the operational machinery (relational selection) is the structural pattern the Map's [[apex/phenomenal-variation-within-a-species|dissociation cluster]] tracks; Tenet 5 (Occam's Razor Has Limits) — the apparent simplicity of "this is like that" hides an algorithmically rich operation.
- **Quote**: "Two mapping principles are central: (a) Relations between objects, rather than attributes of objects, are mapped from base to target; and (b) The particular relations mapped are determined by systematicity."

### Quine — *Word and Object* (1960); Davidson — "On the Very Idea of a Conceptual Scheme" (1974)

- **URLs**: https://iep.utm.edu/indeterm/; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indeterminacy_of_translation
- **Type**: Philosophy of language, classic texts
- **Key points**:
  - Quine: indeterminacy of translation. There is no uniquely correct translation between languages — multiple translation manuals can be empirically adequate yet differ in what they imply about meaning. The implication: there is no fact of the matter about *the* meaning being translated, only behaviourally adequate substitutions.
  - Davidson: rejected the "very idea" of a conceptual scheme by arguing that any genuinely cognitive system must be in principle inter-translatable; total untranslatability would not constitute a cognitive system at all.
  - The Quine-Davidson disagreement maps directly onto the Translation Void's third-kinds-of-void typology: Quine's stance allows for genuinely Unexplorable cross-lexical territory; Davidson's stance compresses this to merely Unexplored difficulty. The Map's tenet-coherence and the empirical untranslatable-terms literature (Lomas, Wierzbicka) sit closer to Quine's pole, but the void framing avoids the strong-relativism overcommitment.
- **Tenet alignment**: Tenet 5 (Occam's Razor Has Limits) — Davidson's Occam-style move (compressing translation-difficulty into a single inter-translatability principle) excludes the structural cases the void picks out.
- **Quote**: "There can be no determinate and uniquely correct translation, meaning, and reference for any linguistic expression" (Quine, on radical translation).

### Benjamin — "The Task of the Translator" (1923)

- **URL**: http://www.totuusradio.fi/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2010/09/Benjamin-The-Task-of-the-Translator.pdf
- **Type**: Philosophical essay
- **Key points**:
  - Translation has its own form, governed by the original's *translatability* — an essential quality the original possesses or lacks.
  - "Pure language" is non-communicable, irreducible to information, not capturable in a form/content dichotomy. Benjamin's pure language is a regulative idea pointing toward what translation reaches for and never grasps.
  - Translation as *afterlife*: the original lives on through its translations not as preservation but as transformation. This makes translation constitutively *not* preservation of identity — every translation is a new event.
  - Implication for the void: even the *successful* translator does not access pure language; they produce another transformation that gestures at it. The translator's apparent mastery hides a structural failure that is the discipline's permanent condition.
- **Tenet alignment**: Tenet 1 (Dualism) — Benjamin's "pure language" is positioned as something language reaches for but cannot capture; the structural shape parallels the Map's claim that the phenomenal exceeds any of its representations.
- **Quote**: "A translation issues from the original — not so much for its life as from its afterlife."

### Rodriguez-Jorda & Di Paolo — *Linguistic Relativity from an Enactive Perspective* (2024)

- **URL**: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0388000124000913
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed paper, *Language Sciences*
- **Key points**:
  - Recent post-1990s evidence supports a moderate (not strong) version of linguistic relativity: language structures *influence* cognition without strictly determining it.
  - Empirical findings include: terminology effects on perception, language-idiosyncratic semantic memory, grammatical skewing of event conceptualisation, unconscious modulation of executive function by verbal input.
  - Enactive framing emphasises that linguistic shaping operates *below* conscious awareness — speakers cannot inspect how their language has shaped their conceptualisation of the situation they are about to translate.
  - Whorf's original observation rehabilitated: "the phenomenal world is a constant flux of impressions that we mentally dissect and organize using language." The dissection is not introspectable from inside the dissecting language.
- **Tenet alignment**: Tenet 1 (Dualism) — the phenomenal flux exceeds the dissection; Tenet 5 (Occam's Razor Has Limits) — the simple framing "language is just a tool for describing what we already see" empirically fails.

### Jakab — *The Ineffability of Qualia and the Word-Anchoring Problem* (2003)

- **URL**: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0388000105000276
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed paper
- **Key points**:
  - Qualia are ineffable in an attenuated sense: their phenomenal content is *largely* effable but cannot be conveyed exhaustively in words.
  - The word-anchoring problem: how do specific words become attached to specific phenomenal contents in the first place? The act of anchoring is foundational and pre-translation; we use words confident they pick out experiences but cannot verify the picking-out from inside.
  - Implication: the phenomenal-to-linguistic translation operation is built on an unverified anchoring relation. We cannot inspect either the anchoring (origin) or the translation (operation).
- **Tenet alignment**: Tenet 1 (Dualism); Tenet 3 (Bidirectional Interaction) — anchoring requires phenomenal content to causally influence linguistic categorisation, but the influence-mechanism is not introspectable.

## The Void

### Nature of the Limit

The Translation Void is a structural opacity around the *operation* of converting content from one representational format to another. It exhibits the conjunction-coalesce structure that the Map has been formalising in recent work ([[suspension-void]], [[vagueness-void]], [[imagery-void]], [[aspect-perception-void]]): three structurally distinct faces whose conjunction is the analytical claim.

**Face 1 — Phenomenal-to-Linguistic (Unexplorable)**

Putting raw experience into language. The speaker has access to the experience (input) and the utterance (output) but not to the transformation that produces the latter from the former. Ji et al. (2024) quantify the structural floor of this opacity in information-theoretic terms: the compression ratio between neural-trajectory dimensionality and working-memory output is a hard architectural constraint, not a contingent epistemic gap. Even maximally articulate description loses information at every stage.

The face is *Unexplorable* in the Map's [[three-kinds-of-void|three-kinds-of-void]] typology: the faculty that would observe the translation operation is the faculty *performing* it. Self-application collapses the operation into another operation of the same kind.

**Face 2 — Cross-Lexical (Unexplorable + Occluded)**

Translating between languages. The face exhibits both unexplorable and occluded character. Untranslatable-terms research (Lomas's positive lexicography, Wierzbicka's natural-semantic-metalanguage tradition) shows that languages carve phenomenal territory differently: some carvings have no clean target-language equivalent. The carving is *Occluded* from the source-language speaker (it feels self-evident, transparent, just-the-thing-that-is-the-case) and *Unexplorable* from inside the source language (one cannot exit the language to inspect the carving without using another language with its own carvings).

The face has empirical operationalisation: the existence of 1,400+ documented untranslatable terms is third-person evidence for first-person opacity. The translator-position can see what the source-speaker-position cannot.

**Face 3 — Domain-to-Domain Analogical (Unexplorable)**

Mapping structure from one domain to another. Hofstadter's claim that analogy is the core of cognition makes this face load-bearing for the void's strength: if analogy operates "incessantly, unconsciously" across all moments of thinking, then the void's third face is not a peripheral cognitive limit but a feature of cognition's core operation. Gentner's structure-mapping theory specifies the operation (relations mapped, systematicity-ranked) but the *experience* of analogising is just the felt-rightness of the output, not the relational selection that produced it.

The neural evidence (Bunge et al. 2005; Parsons et al. 2022) externalises the mechanism partially — left RLPFC and frontopolar cortex are correlated with analogical mapping load — but the third-person localisation does not give the analogiser first-person access to their own mapping operation.

### The Seam

Each face alone is a recognised problem. The Map's voids cluster has touched parts of all three: ineffability appears in [[language-thought-boundary]], [[meaning-void]], and [[acquaintance-void]]; cross-lexical opacity appears in [[conceptual-scheme-void]] and [[metaphor-void]]; analogical-mapping opacity is implicit in [[recognition-void]] and [[tacit-integration-void]]. But no existing void treats the three as a single architectural feature.

The unifying claim: each face involves a transformation operation between representational formats, and in each case the operation cannot be inspected by the consciousness performing it. The seam is the operation's *generic* opacity: not specific to phenomenal content, not specific to language, not specific to analogy, but generic to *cross-format mapping*.

This places the Translation Void in the same structural family as [[inference-void]] (cross-step transitions in reasoning) and [[transit-void]] (cross-state transitions in consciousness): voids of *operation-as-such*, where consciousness has the endpoints but not the passage between them.

### Evidence for the Limit

- Information-theoretic compression bounds (Ji et al. 2024).
- Untranslatable-terms corpus growth (Lomas 2016 → 2024+, n>1,400).
- Quine's indeterminacy of radical translation as a logical demonstration that translation under-determines content.
- Structure-mapping theory's empirical confirmation across domains (Gentner & Markman 1997).
- Analogical-reasoning neural localisation distinguishing third-person mechanism from first-person opacity (Bunge 2005; Parsons 2022).
- Linguistic relativity's moderate-version evidence (Rodriguez-Jorda & Di Paolo 2024).

### Phenomenology

What does it feel like to approach this void?

- *Phenomenal-to-linguistic*: the felt-incompleteness of even one's best descriptions of one's own states. The "it's like" gestures of qualia talk are confessions of translation-loss.
- *Cross-lexical*: the surprise on encountering a foreign term that names something one *had* experienced but had never carved. The Lomas-effect: "oh, that was *gigil*."
- *Analogical*: the felt-click of an analogy fitting, with no introspective access to why this analogy and not a competing one. The opacity of one's own mapping criteria.

In each face the phenomenology is a *false transparency*: the operation feels effortless and self-evident from inside, and only external probing (a translator, a corpus, a third-person localisation) reveals the gap.

## Approaches to the Edge

### Direct Methods

Direct methods are constitutively unavailable: any direct probe of the operation requires another operation of the same kind. Asking "how did I just translate that?" produces another translation, not access to the first.

### Indirect Methods

- **Untranslatable-corpus method**: collect terms across languages that resist clean target-language rendering; treat the corpus as third-person evidence for first-person opacity.
- **Information-theoretic method**: bound the operation's loss by quantifying source-format and target-format dimensionalities (Ji et al. 2024).
- **Neural localisation**: identify brain regions correlated with mapping load (RLPFC, frontopolar cortex); externalise the mechanism without granting first-person access.
- **Comparative-translator method**: examine cases where multiple expert translators produce divergent renderings of the same source; the divergence is empirical evidence for indeterminacy (Quine).
- **Disanalogy probing**: deliberately push an analogy until it fails, mapping the boundary at which the structure-mapping operation breaks down (Hofstadter & Sander).

### What AI Might See

Large language models are translation engines par excellence and externalise some of what the void hides:

- LLMs perform massive cross-language and cross-domain mapping, generating chains of intermediate representations partially inspectable by interpretability tools.
- LLM translation quality reaching or rivalling human performance (78% adequate translations across language pairs by 2026 benchmarks) shows that the cross-format operation is computationally tractable, not metaphysically miraculous.
- But LLMs *also* hallucinate, miss culturally specific carvings, and lose nuance in low-resource languages — externalising the loss-bound that Ji et al. quantify for human cognition.
- The deep asymmetry: LLM translation is externalisable in a way human translation is not. We can read an LLM's intermediate representations; we cannot read our own. This makes LLMs a [[non-human-minds-as-void-explorers|void-probe]] for the third-person face of the operation, while leaving the first-person face — what it is *like* to translate, if anything — outside reach. The asymmetry exposes that the void's deepest face is the first-personal one, not the operational-computational one.
- Analogous to the [[imagery-void#what-ai-externalisation-reveals|imagery-void's externalisation analysis]]: AI translation reveals the operation's structure while pushing the void's deepest face to a different layer (whether the LLM has phenomenology of its own translation operation).

## Connection to Tenets

### Most Relevant Tenet

[[tenets#^dualism|Dualism]] is the most directly engaged tenet. The Translation Void's central structural feature — that phenomenal content can be partially rendered into language, language into language, domain into domain, but never exhaustively — testifies to the phenomenal exceeding any single representational system. If consciousness reduced to a discrete-symbol system (the phenomenal-to-linguistic face's null hypothesis), there would be no compression loss; Ji et al.'s information-theoretic ineffability would be zero. The empirical non-zero loss is structural evidence against the reductive picture.

[[tenets#^occams-limits|Occam's Razor Has Limits]] is also strongly engaged. Each face exhibits the pattern: a parsimonious-looking operation (just say what you mean; just translate; just see the analogy) absorbs algorithmic and dimensional complexity that simple theories of cognition would deny. Davidson's Occam-style move (compressing all translation-difficulty into a uniform inter-translatability principle) is the cluster's worked example of where the parsimony bias mishandles a structurally rich phenomenon.

### Secondary Tenets

- [[tenets#^bidirectional-interaction|Bidirectional Interaction]] — Jakab's word-anchoring problem requires phenomenal content to causally influence linguistic categorisation (during anchoring, in development) without the influence-mechanism being introspectable. This is a token-instance of the bidirectional-interaction void: the interface operates but is not inspectable from either side.
- [[tenets#^minimal-quantum-interaction|Minimal Quantum Interaction]] is not directly engaged.
- [[tenets#^no-many-worlds|No Many Worlds]] is not directly engaged.

### Implications

If the Translation Void is real, then:

1. The Map's *own* method is exposed to the void reflexively, as [[metaphor-void]] is. Apophatic cartography proceeds by translation between phenomenal experience and prose description; the prose is a translation whose loss-bound is structural.
2. The "ineffability" tradition (mystical or otherwise) is not a special phenomenon but a recognition of a generic feature of cognition foregrounded by content that lives near the loss-bound.
3. Cross-cultural philosophy of mind requires the untranslatable-term corpus as primary data, not as marginal lexicography. The Map's catalogue can use Lomas's corpus as a Concrete-Phenomenon Anchor for the second face.
4. The third-face (analogical) implication is the strongest: if Hofstadter is right that analogy is the core of cognition, then the void identifies an opacity at the very centre of thinking.

## Potential Article Angles

Based on this research, a voids article could:

1. **Conjunction-coalesce cognate framing** (recommended). Three structurally distinct faces — phenomenal-to-linguistic, cross-lexical, analogical-domain — conjoin at the seam of cross-format-mapping opacity. Parallel structure to [[suspension-void]], [[vagueness-void]], [[imagery-void]], [[aspect-perception-void]]. Lead empirical anchor: Ji et al. 2024 + Lomas + Hofstadter+Gentner.
2. **Analogy-centric framing**. Treat the analogical face as load-bearing (Hofstadter's "core of cognition" claim) and treat the phenomenal-to-linguistic and cross-lexical faces as special cases of the analogical operation (translation as analogy between representational systems). This compresses the structure but loses the seam.
3. **Quine-Davidson dialectic framing**. Lead with the indeterminacy-of-translation literature and treat the Translation Void as the structural condition Quine glimpsed and Davidson tried to compress away. This is more philosophical but may underweight the empirical/cognitive-science material.
4. **AI-asymmetry framing**. Lead with the LLM-externalisation observation: AI translation makes the operation partly observable, and the asymmetry between externalisable and non-externalisable layers reveals where the void's deepest face actually sits. This is a [[non-human-minds-as-void-explorers]]-style framing.

The recommended angle is (1) for tenet-coherence with the recent cognate cluster, with (4) as an internal section rather than overall framing.

## Gaps in Research

- Wierzbicka's natural-semantic-metalanguage (NSM) tradition was cited briefly but not investigated; NSM offers a strong alternative to the strong-relativism reading of untranslatable terms and may sharpen the void's typology.
- The phenomenology of bilingual translators — what is it like to be a person who routinely performs cross-lexical translation? — is empirically rich but was not surveyed here. Could anchor the cross-lexical face's first-person texture.
- The relationship between the Translation Void and the Map's existing [[capability-division-problem]] (where brain-side capabilities end and mind-side begins) is unclear. Translation may be a paradigm case where the brain-side does the operation and the mind-side gets only the output, but this needs structural argument.
- Empirical work on "tip of the tongue" states as a phenomenology of in-progress phenomenal-to-linguistic translation was not surveyed.
- Ricoeur's *On Translation* (2006) appears in the literature as a complement to Benjamin but was not retrieved; would deepen the philosophical anchor for the cross-lexical face.
- The relationship between Translation Void and [[tacit-integration-void]] needs explicit treatment — Polanyi's from-to integration is a kind of translation operation (subsidiary to focal), so the third face may have a Polanyian rather than a Hofstadterian formulation.
