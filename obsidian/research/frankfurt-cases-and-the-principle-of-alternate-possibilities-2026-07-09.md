## Executive Summary

Harry Frankfurt's 1969 paper "Alternate Possibilities and Moral Responsibility" mounted the most influential 20th-century challenge to the intuitive **Principle of Alternate Possibilities (PAP)** — that a person is morally responsible for what she has done only if she could have done otherwise. Frankfurt's counterexamples deploy a **counterfactual intervener** (the neurosurgeon "Black") who stands ready to force Jones's choice but never actually has to, because Jones does the deed on his own. Jones seems responsible despite having no alternative possibility, so (Frankfurt concludes) PAP is false. The two central replies are the **flicker of freedom** (Jones retains *some* residual alternative — e.g., the involuntary sign of an incipient contrary choice) and the **Widerker–Kane dilemma defense** (the intervener needs a prior sign of Jones's choice: if that sign is deterministically connected to the choice, the case begs the question against the libertarian; if it is not, a robust alternative survives). John Martin Fischer's **semicompatibilism** grants Frankfurt's conclusion and relocates responsibility to the *actual sequence* (guidance control / reasons-responsiveness) rather than to alternative possibilities. **Calibration for downstream expand-topic: Frankfurt's challenge is forceful and the actual-sequence response is a live, respectable position — the Map's route (the dilemma defense) is ONE contested reply, not a refutation of Frankfurt.** The debate also drives the modern **source vs. leeway incompatibilism** split.

## Key Sources

### Frankfurt, "Alternate Possibilities and Moral Responsibility" (1969) — the canonical source
- **Citation**: Frankfurt, H.G. (1969). "Alternate Possibilities and Moral Responsibility." *Journal of Philosophy* 66(23): 829–839.
- **Verification**: VERIFIED at publisher of record. Philosophy Documentation Center record `jphil_1969_0066_0023_0829_0839` confirms *Journal of Philosophy*, vol. 66, no. 23, pp. 829–839 (issue dated Dec. 4, 1969). SEP entry independently gives the same 66(23): 829–839. (One aggregator, scirp.org, mis-transcribes the volume as "86" — ignore; pdcnet is authoritative.)
- **DOI**: NOT independently verified in this pass — quarantine for re-verify-at-expand; do NOT print a DOI unless confirmed at the publisher/JSTOR record.
- **Key points**:
  - States PAP: "a person is morally responsible for what he has done only if he could have done otherwise."
  - Constructs counterexamples where an agent cannot do otherwise yet the factor guaranteeing this plays *no role* in what the agent actually does.
  - Concludes PAP is false as stated; proposes a revised principle keyed to *why* the agent acted (a person is not responsible if he acted *only because* he could not have done otherwise).
- **Tenet alignment**: CONFLICTS (as read by many) with the Map's leeway-libertarian motivation — see Philosophical Payload below.

### Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy — "Moral Responsibility and the Principle of Alternative Possibilities"
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/alternative-possibilities/
- **Type**: Encyclopedia (authoritative survey)
- **Key points**:
  - Lays out the Black/Jones structure; distinguishes flicker replies (general-ability vs. all-in alternatives; robust vs. non-robust flickers).
  - Presents the Widerker–Kane dilemma defense and the post-1995 shift to explicitly *indeterministic* Frankfurt-style cases (FSCs) built to answer it.
  - Distinguishes leeway vs. source incompatibilism (Stump, Klein, Pereboom, Zagzebski on the source side).
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral (survey). Anchors the article's structure and citations.

### John Martin Fischer — semicompatibilism / actual-sequence response
- **Citations**: Fischer, J.M. (1994). *The Metaphysics of Free Will*. Oxford: Blackwell. Fischer, J.M. & Ravizza, M. (1998). *Responsibility and Control: A Theory of Moral Responsibility*. Cambridge University Press.
- **Verification**: VERIFIED via SEP citation list (author/title/publisher). Page-level cites (Fischer 1994: 140–7 on flicker non-robustness; Fischer 1999: 113 on the dilemma) reported by SEP but NOT independently page-checked — quarantine page numbers for re-verify-at-expand if quoted.
- **Key points**:
  - Distinguishes **regulative control** (having genuinely open alternatives) from **guidance control** (the actual-sequence mechanism being the agent's own and appropriately reasons-responsive).
  - Responsibility requires only guidance control; Frankfurt cases show alternatives (regulative control) are inessential.
  - **Semicompatibilism**: even if free will (in the alternatives sense) is incompatible with determinism, *moral responsibility* is compatible with it — because responsibility tracks the actual sequence, not leeway.
- **Tenet alignment**: CONFLICTS with the Map's leeway-libertarian route but is a respectable rival the article must grant.

### Widerker (and Kane) — the dilemma defense
- **Citation**: Widerker, D. (1995). "Libertarianism and Frankfurt's Attack on the Principle of Alternative Possibilities." *Philosophical Review* 104(2): 247–261.
- **Verification**: VERIFIED (journal/volume/issue/pages) via SEP citation list, corroborated by web search. **DOI NOT verified** — quarantine (do not invent).
- **Companion citation**: Kane, R. (1985). *Free Will and Values*. Albany: SUNY Press (dilemma-defense roots, esp. p. 51); also Kane (1996), *The Significance of Free Will*, Oxford UP. Ginet (1996) is a further proponent. VERIFIED at book level via SEP; specific pages quarantined.
- **Key points**:
  - The intervener must read a **prior sign** of which way the agent is heading. Two horns:
    - **Determinism horn**: if the prior sign deterministically necessitates the choice, then assuming the agent is nonetheless responsible *begs the question* against the incompatibilist/libertarian (who denies responsibility under determination).
    - **Indeterminism horn**: if the sign does not necessitate the choice, then up to the moment of decision the agent *could* still have gone the other way — a **robust alternative** survives, so PAP is not actually refuted. (Relatedly, the *epistemic* Kane–Widerker point: in an indeterministic world Black's device cannot know in advance what the agent will freely do.)
  - Prompted a wave of "indeterministic FSCs" (e.g., blockage/buffer cases by Mele–Robb, Pereboom, Hunt, Stump) designed to close the flicker while conceding indeterminism.
- **Tenet alignment**: ALIGNS with the Map's leeway-libertarian route — but the Map should present it as ONE contested reply (Fischer, Widerker & Goetz debate whether the defense ultimately prevails).

### Source vs. leeway incompatibilism (framing sources)
- **Citations**: Timpe, K., "Source Incompatibilism and Its Alternatives" (philpapers rec TIMSIA); Pereboom, D. (1995, 2001); Stump, E. (1990); Zagzebski, L. (2000).
- **Verification**: framing-level only (positions, not exact page cites). Pereboom 1995/2001 already appear (verified) in `the-manipulation-argument-and-hard-incompatibilism.md`; reuse those verified entries at expand time.
- **Key point**: If Frankfurt cases succeed, incompatibilists can retreat from **leeway** (alternatives required) to **source** incompatibilism (what matters is that the agent be the ultimate *source*; determinism defeats responsibility by defeating sourcehood, not by removing alternatives). Timpe further splits *narrow* source incompatibilism (no alternatives needed at all) from *wide* (sourcehood fundamental, but alternatives still required).

## Major Positions

### PAP-affirming leeway incompatibilism (Frankfurt's target; the Map's default motivation)
- **Proponents**: traditional libertarians; van Inwagen (leeway strand); Widerker, Kane, Ginet (defending PAP against Frankfurt).
- **Core claim**: Responsibility requires that alternatives were genuinely open — "could have done otherwise" is a necessary condition.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Closest to the Map. Tenet 2/3 interactionism has consciousness *selecting among genuinely-open quantum alternatives*; Tenet 4 (No Many Worlds) insists rejected alternatives are genuinely excluded, which is the counterfactual-exclusion structure PAP encodes. If PAP is false, part of this motivation weakens (though source-style routes remain).

### Frankfurt / semicompatibilist actual-sequence view (the forceful rival)
- **Proponents**: Frankfurt; Fischer; Fischer & Ravizza.
- **Core claim**: Responsibility supervenes on the actual sequence (reasons-responsive guidance control); alternative possibilities are inessential. Hence responsibility can survive even determinism.
- **Relation to site tenets**: A live challenge. The Map cannot claim this fails; it must argue that *its* dualist-interactionist grounds independently favor the leeway route, while granting the actual-sequence view is coherent and well-defended.

### Source incompatibilism (the fallback if Frankfurt succeeds)
- **Proponents**: Pereboom, Stump, Zagzebski, Klein.
- **Core claim**: Determinism threatens responsibility via *sourcehood*, not alternatives; Frankfurt cases (even if sound) leave this untouched.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Partially compatible. The Map already grants that its `moral-responsibility.md` treats sourcehood as a co-equal condition; source incompatibilism is a hedge that preserves an incompatibilist conclusion even if PAP is abandoned.

## Key Debates

### Does the flicker of freedom rescue PAP?
- **Sides**: Flicker defenders (there is always *some* residual alternative — the involuntary sign, the "on his own" difference) vs. Fischer (such flickers are not *robust* — not a "sufficiently full-blooded" alternative to ground responsibility, since the agent has no voluntary control over the flicker).
- **Current state**: Ongoing; recent work (e.g., "robust flickers of freedom," "extinguishing the flickers," self-inflicted FSCs, 2021–2023) keeps the robustness question live.

### Does the dilemma defense defeat Frankfurt?
- **Sides**: Widerker/Kane/Ginet (yes — no FSC both closes the alternative *and* avoids begging the question) vs. Frankfurt-friendly theorists building indeterministic buffer/blockage cases (Mele–Robb, Pereboom, Hunt, Stump) to answer the indeterminism horn.
- **Current state**: Unresolved; Widerker & Goetz ("Fischer against the dilemma defence: the defence prevails") argue the defense survives Fischer's rebuttal — direct evidence this is genuinely contested, which the Map should cite to keep its calibration honest.

### Leeway or source? What does responsibility ultimately require?
- **Sides**: Leeway incompatibilists (alternatives) vs. source incompatibilists (ultimate origination). Frankfurt cases are the fulcrum: their success pushes incompatibilists toward the source model.
- **Current state**: The dominant modern framing; both models have serious defenders.

- **Note the source-vs-leeway connection**: even if Frankfurt succeeds against *leeway* PAP, incompatibilism can retreat to a *source* model — so "PAP is false" does not by itself vindicate compatibilism about responsibility.
