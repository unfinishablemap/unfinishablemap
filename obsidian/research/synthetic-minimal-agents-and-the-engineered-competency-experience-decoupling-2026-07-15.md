## Scope note: what this adds over existing coverage

The Map already treats the competency-experience decoupling extensively through the **evolved-organism** and **Levin-basal-cognition** lens: see the topic article *Basal and Bioelectric Cognition* (`topics/basal-and-bioelectric-cognition`), the apex synthesis *Competency Without Felt Experience* (`apex/competency-without-felt-experience`), and the 2026-07-08 research note on Levin's morphogenetic agency and xenobots. Xenobots, TAME, and Levin's sentience-bracketing are captured there in depth.

What is **not** yet a dedicated node — and what the harvester correctly flagged — is the *engineering* angle as a distinct epistemic instrument: the class of **synthetic, designed, minimal agents** (xenobots, anthrobots, minimal synthetic cells, self-modelling robots) considered specifically as *deliberately constructed* decoupling tests, where the provenance of the competency is a design process we partly specify rather than an evolutionary history we reconstruct. The interesting question this note isolates is whether **building** a competent agent yourself gives a stronger — or merely differently-shaped — epistemic handle on the competency-vs-experience question than observing an evolved one. This is a genuine and separable angle; the note is worth writing.

## Executive Summary

A new experimental genre has emerged in which competent, goal-directed, self-organising agents are *built* rather than found: Levin and Bongard's xenobots (frog-cell reconfigurable organisms, 2020–2021), Gumuskaya and Levin's anthrobots (motile biobots self-constructed from adult human airway cells, 2023–2025), the J. Craig Venter Institute's minimal synthetic cells (JCVI-syn3.0/syn3A, 2016 onward), and, at the fully non-biological edge, Bongard and Lipson's self-modelling robots (2006). Each realises some cognitive-grade competency — locomotion, navigation, self-repair, kinematic self-replication, minimal autonomous metabolism, damage-recovery via a self-model — in a substrate whose construction contained no obvious phenomenal ingredient. The distinctive philosophical feature of the *engineered* case is a partial **designer's-eye view**: because we specify (much of) what goes into the system, we can be relatively confident about which competencies were installed and by what mechanism. The Map's usable point is that this confidence attaches to *function*, not to *experience* — design lets us know what a system can do, but does not by itself let us read experience either in or out. That asymmetry, made vivid by engineered agents, sharpens the same decoupling the evolved cases establish, while a rival "multiple realizability of sentience" camp presses in the opposite direction, arguing that synthetic substrate cannot rule sentience out any more than in.

## Key Sources

### Motile Living Biobots Self-Construct from Adult Human Somatic Progenitor Seed Cells (anthrobots)
- **URL**: https://advanced.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/advs.202303575
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed paper
- **Citation**: Gumuskaya, G., Levin, M., et al. (2024). "Motile Living Biobots Self-Construct from Adult Human Somatic Progenitor Seed Cells." *Advanced Science*, 11(4), 2303575. DOI 10.1002/advs.202303575. (Published online Nov 2023.)
- **Key points**:
  - "Anthrobots": multicellular, motile, self-constructing biobots grown from **adult human tracheal/airway epithelial cells**, with *no genetic modification* — a purely morphological/environmental reprogramming.
  - They self-assemble, are ciliary-driven and motile, range from ~human-hair to pencil-tip scale, and — placed on a scratched sheet of cultured human neurons — promote neuronal regrowth across the gap.
  - Extends the xenobot result off *Xenopus* embryonic cells and onto **adult human somatic cells**, showing the competency is not a frog-embryo quirk.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral-to-tension, same shape as xenobots. Human-cell provenance moves the case rhetorically closer to "us," but the cells are non-neural airway epithelium; nothing here asserts felt experience. Its usefulness is that it makes the decoupling harder to wave away as "mere frog cells."

### The Morphological, Behavioral, and Transcriptomic Life Cycle of Anthrobots (2025)
- **URL**: https://advanced.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/advs.202409330
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed paper (follow-up)
- **Citation**: Gumuskaya, G., et al. (2025). "The Morphological, Behavioral, and Transcriptomic Life Cycle of Anthrobots." *Advanced Science*. DOI 10.1002/advs.202409330.
- **Key points**: Characterises anthrobot development as a reproducible *life cycle* with distinct morphological/behavioural/transcriptomic stages — reinforcing that a designed cell collective can run a stable developmental programme it was never evolved to run.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral. A cleaner "designed developmental trajectory" case for the functional-competency-without-asserted-experience file.

### Design and synthesis of a minimal bacterial genome (JCVI-syn3.0)
- **URL**: https://www.jcvi.org/research/first-minimal-synthetic-bacterial-cell
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed paper / institute report
- **Citation**: Hutchison, C. A. III, et al. (2016). "Design and synthesis of a minimal bacterial genome." *Science*, 351(6280), aad6253. (JCVI-syn3.0; later refined to JCVI-syn3A.)
- **Key points**:
  - A designed-and-chemically-synthesised genome of **473 genes / ~531 kbp** transplanted into a recipient cell — the smallest genome of any self-replicating cell grown in lab media.
  - **149 of the 473 essential genes are of unknown biological function** — i.e. we built a viable autonomous agent whose own operating requirements we do not fully understand.
  - syn3A adds back ~19 genes (including division proteins FtsZ, SepF) to restore normal morphology and division.
- **Tenet alignment**: Strongly supportive of the decoupling *from the minimal end*. Here is autonomous self-maintaining, self-dividing agency at the theoretical floor of genomic complexity — a designed minimal metabolism/replicator with no plausible candidate for experience. The 149-unknown-gene fact is a useful humility marker: engineered agency does not entail engineer's transparency.

### An evaluation of the xenobotic cognitive project: Towards Stage 1 of xenobotic cognition
- **URL**: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0160932724000164
- **Type**: Philosophy / cognitive-systems paper (abstract only; full text paywalled, HTTP 403 on fetch)
- **Citation**: (2024). "An evaluation of the xenobotic cognitive project: Towards Stage 1 of xenobotic cognition." *Cognitive Systems Research* (Elsevier). Author name not captured — verify at publisher before citing in an article.
- **Key points** (from abstract/secondary summaries — treat as provisional):
  - Argues xenobots are best treated as **"neomachines"**: synthetically constructed systems whose agency-relevant properties depend on self-organising measurement-control relations rather than fixed syntactic programming.
  - Presses the point that xenobot competencies **cannot be explained by a long selection history for those traits** — because the morphologies are novel — which is exactly what makes them a clean designed-vs-evolved contrast.
  - Keeps *cognition* (functionally definable) apart from *sentience* (not functionally definable) — matching Levin's own bracketing.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral-to-supportive as a framing source. The "neomachine / not-selection-explained" framing is the sharpest available articulation of why the *engineered* case is epistemically distinct.

### The Multiple Realizability of Sentience in Living Systems and Beyond
- **URL**: https://www.eneuro.org/content/10/11/ENEURO.0375-23.2023
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed paper (the *rival* framing)
- **Citation**: (2023). "The Multiple Realizability of Sentience in Living Systems and Beyond." *eNeuro*, 10(11), ENEURO.0375-23.2023. Author names not captured — verify before citing.
- **Key points**:
  - Argues sentience is *multiply realizable*: the same function can be realised by different substrates, so sentience need not be confined to canonical neural tissue.
  - Discusses biobots, hybrots (hybrid biological-robotic systems), and engineered neural circuits as candidate testbeds, and floats attribution criteria such as a substrate-independent **"Volitional Agent Criterion"** — testing for unpredictable, non-programmable adaptive responses to distinguish agents from automatons.
- **Tenet alignment**: **Direct conflict** with the Map, and valuable precisely as the strongest opposing voice. This camp takes engineered competency as *reason to widen* the sentience circle, not narrow it. The Map must engage it head-on rather than treat the decoupling as uncontested.

### Resilient Machines Through Continuous Self-Modeling (fully non-biological edge)
- **URL**: https://www.science.org/doi/abs/10.1126/science.1133687
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed paper
- **Citation**: Bongard, J., Zykov, V., & Lipson, H. (2006). "Resilient Machines Through Continuous Self-Modeling." *Science*, 314(5802), 1118–1121. DOI 10.1126/science.1133687.
- **Key points**: A four-legged robot infers its own body structure from actuation-sensation relationships, detects damage (e.g. a removed leg segment) as a self-model/observation mismatch, and synthesises a compensatory gait — an engineered **self-model** and damage-recovery competency.
- **Tenet alignment**: Supportive at the non-biological extreme. A machine builds and updates a model *of itself* and adapts — "self-modelling" in a functional sense — with no temptation to phenomenal attribution. Marks the outer end of the engineered spectrum (silicon/metal) that the biological biobots sit inside.

## Major Positions

### Engineered decoupling (the Map-usable reading)
- **Proponents (as framing, not as endorsers of the Map)**: Levin, Bongard, Kriegman, Blackiston (functional competency); Gumuskaya (anthrobots); JCVI/Hutchison/Venter (minimal cell); the "neomachine" framing.
- **Core claim**: One can *deliberately construct* agents that display cognitive-grade competency (self-organisation, navigation, self-repair, self-replication, self-modelling, autonomous metabolism) in substrates whose design contained no phenomenal ingredient. Because the build is partly specified by the designers, we have a **partial designer's-eye view** of what competencies were installed and how.
- **Relation to site tenets**: The competencies are granted as genuine *function*. The Map's addition is the epistemic gloss: the designer's-eye view certifies *function*, not the presence or absence of *experience*. So engineered agents supply competency-without-warrant-for-experience at will — a decoupling that can be produced on demand, which is stronger than merely finding it in nature.

### Multiple-realizability / substrate-independent sentience (the rival)
- **Proponents**: the eNeuro 2023 multiple-realizability line; Volitional Agent Criterion proponents; panpsychist-adjacent readers of Levin.
- **Core claim**: Because sentience is multiply realizable and defined (if at all) functionally, engineered agents that pass volition-type tests are candidate *sentients*; synthetic substrate does not exclude experience.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Conflicts with Tenets 1–3. The Map's reply: multiple realizability of *function* is granted; multiple realizability of *phenomenal experience* is exactly the disputed claim, and the decoupling shows behavioural/functional criteria cannot settle it in either direction. A volition-based behavioural test detects a *functional* signature, which the engineered cases show is present where no one need be home.

### Levin's own "cognition all the way down" (deliberately agnostic)
- **Core claim**: Competency (goals, memory, anticipation, error-correction) is scale-free and substrate-independent; provenance (designed vs evolved) is not fundamental — hence "neomachines." Sentience is bracketed, not asserted.
- **Relation to site tenets**: The bracketing is the working scientist's restraint the Map already treats as data (see the apex synthesis). The designed/evolved indifference is a mild tension the Map can absorb: the Map need not claim provenance changes the *competency*, only that the *engineered* case gives us better epistemic access to the *absence of an installed experiencer*.

## Key Debates

### Does building an agent tell you more about its inner life than finding one?
- **Sides**: (a) Designer's-privilege view — if you specified the parts and the assembly, and no step introduced a phenomenal ingredient, you have positive reason to withhold experience-attribution. (b) Multiple-realizability view — you specified the *functional organisation*, and if experience supervenes on functional organisation, you may have unknowingly built an experiencer; the 149 unknown-function genes in syn3.0 show designers do not fully understand even what they built.
- **Core disagreement**: Whether the phenomenal question is a fact *about the functional organisation* (then design is relevant to it) or a further fact *not fixed by the functional organisation* (then design certifies only function).
- **Current state**: Open; this is the crux the engineered case brings into focus more sharply than evolved organisms do.

### Designed vs evolved: is the distinction load-bearing?
- **Sides**: Levin/"neomachine" — no, competency is intrinsic to the cellular collective regardless of provenance. Critics/Map-adjacent — the *epistemic* value differs even if the *metaphysics* does not: novel designed morphologies have no selection history to be read as "evidence of felt stakes," removing one common inflationary move.
- **Current state**: Contested; usefully so.
