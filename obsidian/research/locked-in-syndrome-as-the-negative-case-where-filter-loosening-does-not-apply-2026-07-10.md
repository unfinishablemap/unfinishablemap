## Executive Summary

Locked-in syndrome (LIS) is caused by a lesion of the ventral pons that severs the descending corticospinal and corticobulbar (motor) tracts while sparing the ascending sensory pathways and the reticular activating system. The result is quadriplegia and anarthria with fully preserved consciousness, cognition, and (in the classic form) vertical eye movement and blinking. The philosophically load-bearing point for the Map: LIS is a case where the deficit is *purely output*. The consciousness-brain interface is intact on its ascending/input side and consciousness itself is undamaged; only the physical effector arm is cut. This makes LIS the honest **negative case** for filter theory. The filter-theory idea that damage can *paradoxically release* more consciousness (the "filter-loosening" reading applied to psychedelics, near-death experiences, or certain dementias) simply does not apply here — there is no filter to loosen, because the receptive interface is undisturbed. LIS therefore *bounds* the filter model rather than confirming it, and it does so in a way that supports Tenet 3 (Bidirectional Interaction) by exhibiting a clean dissociation between the interface's two arms: the descending/output channel is severed while the ascending/input channel and consciousness persist. This is categorically distinct from covert consciousness / cognitive-motor dissociation (CMD), which is a *detection* problem in apparently-unconscious patients — the opposite situation, and one the Map has already covered.

### Bauer G, Gerstenbrand F, Rumpl E. "Varieties of the locked-in syndrome." *J Neurol* 1979 Aug;221(2):77-91
- **URL**: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/92545/
- **Type**: Paper (foundational taxonomy)
- **Key points**:
  - Three-category classification, graded by how much motor output is preserved:
    - **Classic LIS**: total immobility except vertical eye movement and blinking.
    - **Incomplete LIS**: as classic but with some additional voluntary movement preserved.
    - **Total LIS**: total immobility including all eye movement, with EEG evidence of undisturbed cortical function (i.e., consciousness present but *no* output channel at all).
  - The classification axis is explicitly the amount of surviving motor output — a taxonomy of *output* loss, not of consciousness loss. This is exactly the point that makes LIS a pure-output case.
  - Anatomical basis for total LIS: bilateral lesions of the cerebral peduncles interrupting pyramidal and corticobulbar tracts plus supranuclear and postnuclear oculomotor fibres.
- **Tenet alignment**: Aligns with Tenet 3 (Bidirectional Interaction) as evidence — the taxonomy grades *severed output* while consciousness is stipulated intact, isolating the descending arm.
- **Quote (paraphrase from abstract/secondary; verify exact wording at PubMed before quoting)**: classification "based on the amount of motor outputs which are preserved."

### Smith E, Delargy M. "Locked-in syndrome." *BMJ* 2005;330(7488):406-409
- **URL**: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC549115/ (also https://doi.org/10.1136/bmj.330.7488.406)
- **Type**: Clinical review (BMJ)
- **Key points**:
  - LIS results from an insult to the ventral pons — most commonly infarct, haemorrhage, or trauma (basilar artery territory).
  - Cardinal features: quadriplegia and anarthria with preservation of consciousness; vertical eye movement and blinking preserved and used for communication (eye-coded language boards).
  - Diagnosis is frequently delayed; the review stresses distinguishing LIS from coma and vegetative state.
  - Prognosis: with good care, ten-year survival rates as high as ~80% have been reported; recovery of function is limited but possible in incomplete forms.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral clinical source; supplies the anatomy and the diagnostic-distinction backbone.

### Bruno M-A, Bernheim JL, Ledoux D, Pellas F, Demertzi A, Laureys S. "A survey on self-assessed well-being in a cohort of chronic locked-in syndrome patients: happy majority, miserable minority." *BMJ Open* 2011;1(1):e000039
- **URL**: https://orbi.uliege.be/bitstream/2268/94343/1/Bruno_BMJ_2011.pdf (also https://doi.org/10.1136/bmjopen-2010-000039)
- **Type**: Survey study (French ALIS association cohort; 168 members invited)
- **Key points**:
  - Self-assessed global well-being via the Anamnestic Comparative Self-Assessment (ACSA) scale.
  - A "happy majority" reported satisfactory well-being; a "miserable minority" did not. Many did not wish to die.
  - The "happiness paradox": able-bodied observers systematically underestimate LIS patients' quality of life, with implications for end-of-life decisions and for the ethics of judging conscious-but-immobile lives from the outside.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral/supportive. Useful for the article's ethical coda and for the point that intact consciousness (not behavioural output) is what carries the value of the life — a first-person/inner-life emphasis congenial to the Map's dualism, though the article should not over-read the survey as metaphysical evidence.

### Contemporary BCI / speech-neuroprosthesis literature (2021-2026)
- **URLs**:
  - Brain-Computer Interfaces for Communication: Preferences of Individuals With Locked-in Syndrome — https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33530868/ (2021)
  - Speech neuroprosthesis review — https://www.nature.com/articles/s41583-024-00819-9 (Nat Rev Neurosci 2024)
  - Intracortical speech BCI in long-standing anarthria/LIS — https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2211124726002408 (Cell Reports 2026)
- **Type**: Empirical / review
- **Key points**:
  - BCIs restore a communication *output* channel by decoding attempted speech or movement directly from cortex, bypassing the severed descending tracts.
  - This is conceptually important: the technology confirms that in LIS the *intent-to-act signal is present and decodable* — the loss is downstream of intention, in the effector pathway, not in consciousness or volition.
  - Reported milestones include high-accuracy, large-vocabulary decoding and use by completely locked-in patients.
- **Tenet alignment**: Aligns with Tenet 3. The fact that a decodable motor-intention signal survives while the natural output pathway is cut is exactly the dissociation between the interface's two arms that the article makes load-bearing.

## Major Positions

### Filter / transmission theory (James, Bergson, Huxley; the Map's working model)
- **Core claim**: The brain does not produce consciousness but constrains/transmits it; brain damage disrupts the *interface*, not consciousness itself. A sub-claim — "filter-loosening" — holds that certain disruptions can *reduce* filtering and thereby *release* a wider or more intense consciousness (invoked for psychedelics, NDEs, some dementias, meditation).
- **Relation to LIS**: The general filter reading is compatible with LIS (consciousness persists intact behind a broken output channel). But the specific **filter-loosening** move does **not** apply: LIS involves no loosening and no paradoxical release. The receptive interface is undisturbed and consciousness is neither expanded nor degraded — only disconnected from motor expression. LIS is thus the *negative/bounding* case that marks where the filter-loosening argument stops.

### Production / materialist reading
- **Core claim**: Consciousness is generated by neural activity; behavioural unresponsiveness usually indexes reduced or absent consciousness.
- **Relation to LIS**: The production theorist accommodates LIS easily — the generative substrate (cortex, thalamus, ascending arousal) is intact, only the motor output is destroyed; so consciousness is expected to persist. LIS therefore does **not** by itself favour the interface reading over production. The article must be honest that LIS is not pro-filter evidence; its value is as a *scope-limiting* case that keeps the Map from over-claiming filter-loosening.

### The bidirectional-interface reading (Map-specific)
- **Core claim**: The mind-brain interface has two dissociable arms — an ascending/input arm (world → consciousness) and a descending/output arm (consciousness → world / body). LIS is the clean exhibit of the descending arm severed with the ascending arm and consciousness intact.
- **Relation to tenets**: Directly supports Tenet 3 (Bidirectional Interaction) at an anatomical grain, and complements the existing `lived-objectified-body-distinction` framing (severed coupling between the lived body's "I can" and the objectified body's execution).

## Key Debates

### Is locked-in syndrome interface *evidence*?
- **Sides**: (a) It is a dissociation that any theory of consciousness must handle; (b) it is not *discriminating* evidence between filter and production because both accommodate it trivially.
- **Core disagreement**: Whether a pure-output deficit can adjudicate the production/transmission question.
- **Current state**: The honest answer for the Map is (b) — LIS is *not* filter-loosening evidence. Its contribution is negative: it bounds the filter model's scope. This honesty is the article's distinctive value.

### The happiness paradox and the ethics of judging conscious-but-immobile lives
- **Sides**: External observers vs. patients' self-report (Bruno et al. 2011).
- **Core disagreement**: Whether quality of life in LIS justifies pessimistic prognostic/end-of-life judgements.
- **Current state**: Empirically, self-reported well-being is higher than observers predict; ethically live and ongoing.

### Distinguishing LIS from covert consciousness / CMD
- **Sides**: LIS = fully conscious patient who *looks* awake (eyes open, vertical gaze) but cannot move → a *communication* problem. CMD / covert consciousness = apparently-unconscious patient (vegetative/unresponsive) who is *covertly* aware, detectable only by neuroimaging (Owen tennis-imagery paradigm; Bodien et al. 2024 NEJM) → a *detection* problem.
- **Core disagreement**: These are opposite epistemic situations. LIS: consciousness is evident, output is gone. CMD: output and behaviour are gone *and* consciousness is hidden/uncertain.
- **Current state**: Clinically well-established distinction. **This is the key dedup**: the Map's clinical-review pass explicitly declined covert consciousness / CMD as already covered (Bodien 2024, Owen). The new article must NOT re-tell the CMD/DoC catalogue; its contribution is the pure-output negative case.
