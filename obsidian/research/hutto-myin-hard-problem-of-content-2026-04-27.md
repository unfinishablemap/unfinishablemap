## Executive Summary

Hutto and Myin's "Hard Problem of Content" (HPC), developed across *Radicalizing Enactivism* (2013) and *Evolving Enactivism* (2017), is the claim that no naturalistic theory can explain how neurological states acquire genuine semantic content—truth-conditional, intensional, referential properties. Their target is the family of naturalistic semantics that runs from Dretske's informational covariation to Millikan's teleosemantics: covariation between an internal state and a worldly state, however reliable and however biologically functional, does not constitute content. Their positive proposal is Radical Enactive (or Embodied) Cognition (REC): basic minds are *contentless*, engaging the world through embodied skill rather than representation; only linguistically sophisticated cognition that participates in public claim-making practices acquires genuine content. The HPC is structurally parallel to Chalmers's hard problem of consciousness—both identify failures of naturalisation—but Hutto and Myin remain naturalists who reject dualism, attempting to dissolve rather than vindicate non-physicalism. For the Map, REC is the strongest contemporary anti-representationalist alternative to physicalist semantics, and the HPC is a usable structural ally to dualism's explanatory-gap arguments even though the two positions disagree metaphysically.

## Key Sources

### Hutto & Myin, *Radicalizing Enactivism* (2013)
- **URL**: https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262534642/radicalizing-enactivism/
- **Type**: Book (foundational text)
- **Key points**:
  - Coins the "Hard Problem of Content" (HPC): natural-scientific theories cannot accommodate truth-bearing, referential, intensional features of contentful states
  - Core argument: "covariance does not constitute content"—a reliable causal correspondence relation reveals only when an internal state will be tokened, not what it "says" or "means"
  - Targets Dretske's informational semantics and Millikan's teleosemantics as the strongest available naturalistic theories of content
  - Distinguishes "basic minds" (contentless, embodied, dynamically coupled) from content-involving cognition (linguistic, sociocultural)
  - REC is anti-representationalist *within* naturalism—rejects "the manipulation of contents" but does not reject scientific explanation
- **Tenet alignment**: Conflicts with Tenet 1 (REC is naturalist, denies non-physical mind) but the *negative* HPC argument is ally-shaped: a structural failure of physicalist semantics that parallels the Map's hard problem of consciousness arguments
- **Quote**: "If covariance is the only scientifically respectable notion of information that can do the work required by explanatory naturalists, it follows that informational content does not exist in nature"

### Hutto & Myin, *Evolving Enactivism* (2017)
- **URL**: https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262036115/evolving-enactivism/
- **Type**: Book
- **Key points**:
  - Develops a *positive* programme rather than purely critical: an account of how contentless basic cognition can interface with content-involving cognition
  - Two-step developmental sequence: (i) contentless social cognition where individuals "target each other...without representing them under descriptions with correctness conditions"; (ii) sociocultural scaffolding via "claim-making practices" and public symbol systems generates correctness-conditional content
  - "RECtification" strategy: re-read competing theories (predictive processing, ecological dynamics) as compatible with REC
  - Distinguishes "contentless embodied expectations" from "contentful predictions"—the central dispute with Andy Clark
  - Imagination and memory have basic (contentless) and developed (content-involving) forms
- **Tenet alignment**: Same structural status as 2013 book; the sociocultural-scaffolding move is broadly compatible with the Map's view that high-content propositional thought emerges from a substrate distinct from raw experience
- **Quote**: Content-involving social cognition emerges only "when special sorts of sociocultural norms are in place," dependent on "public symbol systems through which biologically inherited cognitive capacities can be scaffolded"

### Daly, "Radicalizing Enactivism" review (NDPR, 2013, by Anthony Roberts)
- **URL**: https://ndpr.nd.edu/reviews/radicalizing-enactivism-basic-minds-without-content/
- **Type**: Critical review (Notre Dame Philosophical Reviews)
- **Key points**:
  - Identifies the central HPC argument: "a relation of correspondence or indication, however reliable, explains nothing more than the conditions under which an internal state will be tokened; it reveals nothing about what the state 'says' or 'means'"
  - Teleosemantic dilemma: emphasising covariation inherits covariation's problems; emphasising consumer functions struggles to explain intensionality—"biology lacks the resources for specifying under which guise such states might represent" their targets
  - Objections: (i) non-predicative perception inadequately explains object-tracking under sensory flux; (ii) without content, distinguishing veridical from illusory experience is intractable; (iii) the scaffolding story for how language *generates* content is underspecified; (iv) the negative arguments are stronger than the positive alternative
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral—useful for catalogueing legitimate worries about REC's coherence

### Thompson, review of *Evolving Enactivism* (NDPR, 2018)
- **URL**: https://ndpr.nd.edu/reviews/evolving-enactivism-basic-minds-meet-content/
- **Type**: Critical review by Evan Thompson (autopoietic enactivist)
- **Key points**:
  - Calling basic cognition "contentless" rather than "nonrepresentational" misaligns with phenomenological traditions (Husserl, Merleau-Ponty) that distinguish intentional content from representation
  - Hutto and Myin lack a positive theory of intentionality—how objects appear *as* something to agents
  - Mischaracterises ecological dynamics, autopoietic enactivism, and teleosemantics as competitors
  - Ignores empirical literature on sociocultural scaffolding (Vygotsky, Donald, Hutchins) that would do the explanatory work they need
  - Concludes Hutto and Myin are "good gadflies against representationalism" but fail to deliver a coherent positive alternative
- **Tenet alignment**: Useful witness that even sympathetic enactivists find the HPC's positive programme underdeveloped—reduces the cost of the Map's partial endorsement of the *negative* argument while declining the *positive* programme

### Mann & Pain, "Teleosemantics and the Hard Problem of Content" (*Philosophical Psychology*, 2021)
- **URL**: https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/09515089.2021.1942814 (paywalled; abstract via https://philarchive.org/rec/MANTAT-16)
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed paper
- **Key points**:
  - The HPC objection requires teleosemantics to satisfy two criteria: (i) truth-evaluability (the state can be true or false) and (ii) intensionality (content can differ while reference holds fixed)
  - Argue teleosemantics *does* meet the truth-evaluability criterion through the consumer-function machinery
  - Concede teleosemantics does not meet the intensionality criterion—but argue intensionality is not a requirement for a viable theory of content for sub-personal cognitive states
  - Conclude Hutto and Myin's HPC objection fails by a narrowing-of-criteria move
- **Tenet alignment**: Reduces the dialectical strength of the HPC for naturalists who are happy with non-intensional content; the Map can note that this "rescue" comes at the cost of admitting that naturalism cannot deliver the full conceptual content folk psychology and dualism both rely on

### Shani, "Befuddling the Mind" (*Phenomenology and the Cognitive Sciences*, 2020)
- **URL**: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11097-020-09656-4
- **Type**: Critical paper
- **Key points**:
  - REC's attempt to discard the hard problem of consciousness is unsuccessful
  - The hard problem is best understood as a *transcendental* challenge that no phenomeno-physical identity theory eliminates
  - Hutto and Myin adopt a "theoretically unstable stance" by blending strong deflationism with implicit metaphysical revisionism
- **Tenet alignment**: Aligns with Tenet 1 indirectly—if REC cannot dissolve the hard problem of consciousness even on its own anti-representationalist terms, the explanatory gap survives the deflation

### Kirchhoff & Hutto, "Never Mind the Gap" (*Constructivist Foundations*, 2016)
- **URL**: https://constructivist.info/11/2/346
- **Type**: Paper (Hutto-side reply on consciousness)
- **Key points**:
  - Hutto and Myin argue the hard problem can be dissolved by denying the metaphysical distinction between phenomenal and physical that gives the problem life
  - Neurophenomenology (Thompson) cannot close the gap because there is "no such gap to close" on REC's view
  - Critique standard framings of phenomenal properties as confused: the relata (qualia and brain states) are not what enactivists posit
- **Tenet alignment**: Conflicts with Tenet 1—this is REC's deflationary move on consciousness, which Shani argues fails

### Ash & Welshon, "Dynamicism, radical enactivism, and the case of subitization" (*Philosophical Psychology*, 2020)
- **URL**: https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/09515089.2020.1775798
- **Type**: Critical paper (counterexample-driven)
- **Key points**:
  - Subitization—rapid numerosity perception of small sets (≤4 items)—appears to involve representational cognitive processing in basic cognition
  - If so, REC's claim that *all* representational processes require sociocultural scaffolding is false at the basic-mind level
  - Provides a concrete empirical pressure point against REC's positive thesis
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral; useful for showing REC's empirical vulnerabilities

### Pantsar, "On Radical Enactivist Accounts of Arithmetical Cognition" (*Ergo*, n.d.)
- **URL**: https://journals.publishing.umich.edu/ergo/article/id/3120/
- **Type**: Critical paper
- **Key points**:
  - REC's account of how basic minds acquire content through linguistic scaffolding is the main pressure point
  - Numerical cognition shows representational structure even in pre-linguistic infants and non-human animals
  - The bridge from contentless basic cognition to content-involving sophisticated cognition is REC's weakest joint
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral; useful for catalogueing positive-programme objections

### Roberts, NDPR review of *Radicalizing Enactivism* (already cited above as the principal review)

### Kiverstein & Rietveld, "The Primacy of Skilled Intentionality" (*Philosophia*, 2015)
- **URL**: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11406-015-9645-z
- **Type**: Sympathetic critical paper
- **Key points**:
  - Develops "skilled intentionality" as alternative non-representational account: an organism's selective openness and responsiveness to a landscape of affordances
  - Refines Hutto and Satne's "ur-intentionality" framework
  - Frog fly-snapping mechanism: intentionality without internal representation
- **Tenet alignment**: Conflicts with Tenet 1 (locates intentionality in embodied skill rather than mental substance) but the underlying point that content does not bottom-out in covariance is structurally compatible with the Map

### "Enactivism" entry, Internet Encyclopedia of Philosophy
- **URL**: https://iep.utm.edu/enactivism/
- **Type**: Encyclopedia entry
- **Key points**:
  - Three strands: autopoietic, sensorimotor, radical
  - Radical enactivism (Hutto, Myin, Hutto & Satne) is the most anti-representationalist
  - REC remains within naturalism—it is *physicalist anti-representationalism*, not non-physicalism
- **Tenet alignment**: Useful framing—conflicts with Tenet 1, but only within the physicalist family of options

### Boghossian connection (via Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy on Eliminative Materialism)
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/materialism-eliminative/
- **Type**: Encyclopedia entry
- **Key points**:
  - Boghossian's self-refutation argument and Hutto-Myin's HPC are independent attacks on physicalist semantics
  - Boghossian: irrealism about content generalises to the eliminativist's *own assertions*
  - Hutto-Myin: irrealism about content is true *down at the basic-mind level* but content reappears at the sociocultural-linguistic level
  - Both arguments converge on the difficulty of naturalising content; they diverge on what to do about it (Boghossian: don't go irrealist; Hutto-Myin: contain content within sociocultural practices)
- **Tenet alignment**: The two arguments together form a pincer against physicalist treatments of content—useful for the Map's eliminativism engagement

## Major Positions

### Hutto-Myin Radical Enactive Cognition (REC)
- **Proponents**: Daniel Hutto, Erik Myin, Glenda Satne, Michael Kirchhoff
- **Core claim**: Basic cognition is contentless; only linguistically sophisticated cognition involves representational content. Naturalistic theories of content fail (HPC). REC is the correct positive replacement: cognition as embodied, dynamically coupled, world-engaging activity, with content emerging only through public claim-making practices.
- **Key arguments**:
  - The HPC: covariance does not constitute content; teleosemantics inherits covariance's problems and cannot deliver intensionality
  - Representation-hunger arguments (Clark) overestimate what cognition needs—much "representation-hungry" work is actually doable through skilled coupling
  - Frog fly-snapping is mechanistic, not representational
  - The hard problem of consciousness is dissolved by denying the phenomenal/physical distinction
- **Relation to site tenets**: REC is naturalist and *anti-dualist* in self-presentation. But the HPC is a usable structural ally: it identifies a real failure of physicalist semantics. The Map can endorse the negative argument (naturalistic content theories fail) while rejecting REC's positive alternative (eliminate content from basic minds) in favour of the dualist alternative (content has non-physical grounding for conscious creatures).

### Conservative Representationalism (the target)
- **Proponents**: Fred Dretske, Ruth Millikan, Jerry Fodor, Andy Clark (in his representationalist mode)
- **Core claim**: Mental states have intentional content in virtue of natural relations—causal covariation, evolutionary proper function, predictive role. Naturalism plus representationalism is consistent.
- **Key arguments**:
  - Information-theoretic semantics (Dretske 1981, 1995): representation is reliable indication
  - Teleosemantics (Millikan 1984, 1993): representation is what evolution selected the consumer system to use as a sign
  - Predictive processing (Clark 2013, 2016): brain represents through generative-model parameters that minimise prediction error
- **Relation to site tenets**: All conflict with Tenet 1 by attempting to naturalise content. The Map's quarrel with these views is the same as Hutto-Myin's at this stage; the disagreement opens later, on what follows from the failure.

### Sympathetic Enactivists (Thompson, Di Paolo, Gallagher)
- **Proponents**: Evan Thompson, Ezequiel Di Paolo, Shaun Gallagher, Hanne De Jaegher
- **Core claim**: Cognition is enactive but not necessarily contentless; intentionality is constituted by embodied engagement with affordances rather than by representation
- **Key arguments**:
  - Phenomenological tradition distinguishes intentional content from representation; "contentless" is the wrong frame
  - Sociocultural scaffolding stories require empirical grounding REC does not provide
  - Autopoietic and sensorimotor enactivism preserve more of mainstream cognitive science than REC
- **Relation to site tenets**: Conflicts with Tenet 1 but in a different way—locates irreducibility in biological organisation rather than non-physical mind. See `enactivism-challenge-interactionist-dualism-2026-03-21.md` for fuller treatment.

### Teleosemantic Defence (Mann & Pain 2021)
- **Proponents**: Stephen Francis Mann, Ross Pain
- **Core claim**: Teleosemantics meets the truth-evaluability criterion REC demands; intensionality is not required for sub-personal content
- **Key arguments**:
  - Consumer functions plus environmental conditions yield truth conditions for cognitive states
  - The intensionality requirement is a personal-level desideratum, not a sub-personal one
- **Relation to site tenets**: This is the strongest naturalist reply available. The Map can engage by noting that the rescue *concedes* what dualism needs—naturalism cannot deliver intensional, fully conceptual content.

## Key Debates

### Does the HPC Succeed Against Teleosemantics?
- **Sides**: Hutto and Myin (no naturalistic theory of content works) vs. teleosemantics defenders (Mann & Pain 2021; Millikan-school)
- **Core disagreement**: Whether biological proper function plus environmental covariation can deliver truth-evaluable, intensional, semantic content
- **Current state**: Genuinely open. Mann and Pain show the HPC objection succeeds only against a strong reading of what teleosemantics is required to deliver. If naturalists weaken the requirement to truth-evaluability without intensionality, teleosemantics survives—but at the cost of admitting it cannot deliver folk-psychological content.

### Are Basic Minds Genuinely Contentless?
- **Sides**: Hutto and Myin (yes, basic cognition involves no content) vs. critics (Shapiro, Pantsar, Ash & Welshon)
- **Core disagreement**: Whether subitization, numerical cognition in pre-linguistic infants and animals, and tracking of object identity require representation
- **Current state**: The empirical evidence is contested. Subitization is the strongest counter-case in the recent literature; REC's reply is that these are still skill-based capacities mischaracterised in representational terms. The dispute often turns on what counts as "representation."

### How Does the Sociocultural Bridge Work?
- **Sides**: Hutto and Myin (claim-making practices generate content) vs. critics (Thompson, Roberts)
- **Core disagreement**: Whether the developmental story from contentless basic cognition to content-involving sophisticated cognition is genuinely explanatory or merely asserted
- **Current state**: Even sympathetic critics find the bridge underspecified. *Evolving Enactivism* (2017) attempts to address this but reviewers (Thompson 2018, Roberts 2013) judge the account incomplete.

### Does REC Dissolve or Inherit the Hard Problem of Consciousness?
- **Sides**: Hutto and Kirchhoff ("never mind the gap"—it dissolves) vs. Shani 2020 and others (it merely relocates)
- **Core disagreement**: Whether denying the phenomenal/physical distinction eliminates the explanatory gap or whether the gap is transcendental and survives any deflation
- **Current state**: The Shani critique is forceful and not yet answered. REC's strongest move on consciousness (deflationary identity) faces the same problems as standard physicalist identity theories.

### REC's Relation to Eliminativism
- **Sides**: REC partisans claim REC is not eliminativist about *all* content; eliminativism critics (and Boghossian-style arguments) push REC to choose
- **Core disagreement**: Whether containing content within sociocultural practices is a tenable middle ground between full content realism and full content eliminativism
- **Current state**: The Map's `concepts/status-of-content.md` already treats the Boghossian-Hutto-Myin pincer; this research strengthens that treatment.

## Potential Article Angles

The task notes ask whether a `topics/` article on radical enactivism or a `concepts/` entry on the Hard Problem of Content is the better target. Recommendation: a `concepts/` entry on the **Hard Problem of Content** is the better primary target, with optional later expansion to a `topics/` article on radical enactivism.

Reasoning:
- The HPC is referenced in three existing files (`topics/eliminative-materialism.md`, `concepts/status-of-content.md`, `topics/enactivism-challenge-to-interactionist-dualism.md`) but never gets its own treatment. A concept page would consolidate the references.
- Radical enactivism *as a whole* is already partially treated in `topics/enactivism-challenge-to-interactionist-dualism.md`; a separate radical-enactivism topic would significantly overlap.
- The HPC has independent dialectical work to do for the Map (parallel to hard problem of consciousness, ally against eliminativism) that justifies a dedicated entry.
- `concepts/` has capacity (~226/250); `topics/` is at ~230/250.

Possible article angles for a `concepts/hard-problem-of-content.md`:

1. **The structural ally (recommended)** — Present the HPC as a structural parallel to the hard problem of consciousness: both identify failures of naturalisation in the same explanatory direction. The Map endorses Hutto-Myin's negative argument (naturalistic content theories fail) without endorsing their positive REC alternative. Develop the parallel: where Chalmers identifies an unbridgeable gap between physical processes and *experience*, Hutto and Myin identify an unbridgeable gap between physical processes and *aboutness*. The Map reads both gaps as evidence that physicalism keeps encountering the same boundary from different directions—and that the boundary is real, not an artefact of conceptual confusion. Cite Mann & Pain 2021 honestly: the teleosemantic rescue narrows criteria, conceding that naturalism cannot deliver folk-psychological content.

2. **The pincer with Boghossian** — Develop the Hutto-Myin/Boghossian pincer against eliminativism more fully. The structure: Boghossian shows that content-irrealism cannot consistently be asserted; Hutto-Myin show that content-realism cannot be naturalised. Together these arguments force the eliminativist to choose between coherent self-stultification and embracing non-naturalist semantics. The Map's position: the dualist resolution accepts non-physical grounding for content without being self-stultifying. Cross-link `concepts/status-of-content.md` and `concepts/self-stultification.md`.

3. **The contested empirical core** — Focus on the subitization and numerical-cognition disputes (Ash & Welshon 2020, Pantsar) as the empirical pressure points where REC's positive thesis is most exposed. Argue that these disputes do not weaken the *negative* HPC argument—they only show that REC's attempt to manage without representation in basic cognition is empirically optimistic. The Map can keep the negative argument while declining the positive elimination of content.

4. **The naturalism boundary** — Use the HPC to argue that "naturalism" is doing different work in REC and in the Map. Hutto-Myin's naturalism is a methodological commitment to scientifically respectable explanation; the Map's resistance to naturalism is metaphysical. The HPC clarifies that even *within* the methodological-naturalist project, content cannot be reduced—which is exactly the conclusion the Map draws metaphysically.

When writing the article, follow `obsidian/project/writing-style.md` for:
- Named-anchor summary technique for forward references (especially "explained below" patterns for the parallel-to-hard-problem-of-consciousness move)
- Background vs. novelty: omit general enactivism background (already in `topics/enactivism-challenge-to-interactionist-dualism.md`); focus on the HPC argument structure and the Map's specific stance
- Tenet alignment: explicit "Relation to Site Perspective" section connecting to Tenet 1 (the structural-ally argument) and to the methodological commitment to engage the strongest contemporary anti-representationalist position
- LLM optimization: front-load the HPC statement and the Map's stance; readers (human and LLM) should know within the first 200 words what the HPC is and how the Map relates to it
- Avoid the "this is not X, it is Y" cliché when contrasting REC with dualism; phrase positively

Honest limitations to flag in the article:
- The HPC is contested—Mann & Pain 2021 is a serious teleosemantic reply, and the article should not present the HPC as a knockdown
- REC's positive alternative is *not* something the Map endorses; the article must distinguish negative argument from positive programme
- The empirical disputes (subitization etc.) cut against REC, not against the negative HPC argument
