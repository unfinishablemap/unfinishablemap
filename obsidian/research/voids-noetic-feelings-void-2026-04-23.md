## Executive Summary

Noetic feelings—also called epistemic feelings or metacognitive feelings—are the family of affective experiences that gate cognition from the inside: the feeling of knowing (FOK), feeling of rightness (FOR), feeling of familiarity, feeling of confidence, tip-of-the-tongue (TOT), feeling of understanding, feeling of coherence. These feelings are what tell the system when to stop inquiry, when to trust a recalled answer, when to engage analytic deliberation, when to accept an explanation as sufficient. Philosophical and cognitive-scientific consensus converges on a striking fact: these feelings are produced by heuristic processes (cue familiarity, retrieval fluency, accessibility) that are *systematically inaccessible* to the very consciousness they inform. The mechanism is opaque; the output feels authoritative. The resulting void is structural: consciousness can access the *that* of its noetic feelings (I feel I know, I feel this is right) but never the *how* of their production. Because these feelings decide what counts as settled, and because introspection itself runs on the same kind of feelings, the void is recursive—we cannot evaluate the reliability of our evaluators without using them.

## Key Sources

### Slawa Loev, "Epistemic Feelings are Affective Experiences" (2022, *Emotion Review*)
- **URL**: https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/17540739221104464
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed paper
- **Key points**:
  - Epistemic feelings are mild affective experiences whose affective character tends to *elude introspection* precisely because of their mildness.
  - Introspection lacks a proprietary neural mechanism; it inherits errors from sensory, conceptual, and linguistic subsystems.
  - The feelings have phenomenal valence and felt arousal—they are experiences, not mere functional states.
- **Tenet alignment**: Strongly supports *Occam's Razor Has Limits* (our monitoring of our own cognition uses the same flawed instruments it is trying to monitor) and *Dualism* (the gap between phenomenal feel and mechanism).
- **Quote**: "Epistemic feelings are mild affective experiences, so their affective nature tends to elude us introspectively."

### Asher Koriat, "The feeling of knowing: Some metatheoretical implications for consciousness and control" (2000)
- **URL**: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/10924234/
- **Type**: Paper in *Consciousness and Cognition*
- **Key points**:
  - Noetic feelings are *not* direct readouts of memory traces; they are produced by inferential heuristics (cue familiarity, accessibility of partial information) operating *implicitly and unintentionally*.
  - Once produced, they enter consciousness as feelings and then influence controlled action—the heuristic machinery is one-way opaque from above.
  - Two heuristics interact: cue-familiarity (fast, early) and accessibility of partial information (slower, conditional on high cue-familiarity).
- **Tenet alignment**: *Bidirectional Interaction*—the occlusion of the mechanism from conscious inspection is exactly the kind of structural hiddenness the Map predicts around interface operations.
- **Quote**: "Rather than reflecting direct access to memory traces, noetic feelings are based on inferential heuristics that operate implicitly and unintentionally."

### Valerie Thompson et al., "Intuition, reason, and metacognition" (2011)
- **URL**: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0010028511000454
- **Type**: Cognitive-psychology paper
- **Key points**:
  - The Feeling of Rightness (FOR) is the metacognitive experience that accompanies intuitive answers and determines whether analytic (Type 2) processing is engaged at all.
  - FOR is predicted by *generation fluency*—the speed and ease with which the initial answer came to mind, not by the answer's actual correctness.
  - Low FOR triggers rethinking; high FOR terminates inquiry. Consciousness does not see this gate operate—it only experiences the downstream effect.
- **Tenet alignment**: Occam's Razor Has Limits—the very mechanism that tells us when to stop explaining is a fluency-heuristic we cannot inspect. What feels like "enough" is a felt signal, not a verified judgment.
- **Quote**: "Fluently produced answers give rise to a strong feeling of rightness… Low FOR was associated with longer rethinking times and an increased probability of answer change."

### Joëlle Proust, *The Philosophy of Metacognition: Mental Agency and Self-Awareness* (2013, OUP)
- **URL**: https://global.oup.com/academic/product/the-philosophy-of-metacognition-9780199602162
- **Type**: Monograph / philosophical framework
- **Key points**:
  - Metacognition operates via *self-probing* ("can I do this?") and *post-evaluating* ("did I succeed?"), both of which generate noetic feelings without requiring conceptual articulation.
  - Distinct families of metacognitive feelings correspond to distinct cognitive domains: metaperception, metamemory, metareasoning, metacomprehension. Each domain has its own monitoring "feel."
  - Proust's "self-evaluative" account rejects the metarepresentational view—metacognition is *procedural*, not descriptive, and operates beneath the level of attributed mental states.
- **Tenet alignment**: Dualism—metacognition as a form of agency that need not be reducible to self-directed theory of mind; its procedural opacity resists third-person capture.
- **Quote**: "Neither question need be articulated conceptually for a feeling of knowing or of being right to be generated, or to drive epistemic control."

### "Epistemic feelings, metacognition, and the Lima problem" (*Synthese*, 2021)
- **URL**: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11229-021-03094-8
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed paper
- **Key points**:
  - Formalizes the "Lima problem": epistemic feelings *inform* us about content we cannot independently access, so we have no way to cross-check their accuracy except by more feelings.
  - Three competing accounts: doxastic (feelings as implicit beliefs), mental-scanner (feelings as readouts), and heuristic-mechanism (feelings as outputs of opaque inferential processes); the heuristic account best fits the evidence.
  - Evaluativism: epistemic feelings are outputs of a *distinct primitive metacognitive mechanism* operating on heuristics that correlate with inaccessible content.
- **Tenet alignment**: This paper articulates the void directly. We have epistemic access to the feel, not the content it tracks; and the feel is produced by a mechanism we cannot inspect.
- **Quote**: "The information is supplied by a feeling."

### Déjà vu and Jamais vu research (multiple, e.g., Colorado State University, Psychological Science)
- **URL**: https://natsci.source.colostate.edu/deja-vu-feelings-prediction-theyre-just-feelings/
- **Type**: Popular and peer-reviewed empirical research
- **Key points**:
  - Déjà vu: familiarity feeling detached from any corresponding memory—the feeling fires without its normal cause.
  - Jamais vu: recognition fails for genuinely familiar material—the feeling is *withheld* where it should appear.
  - Déjà vu generates an *illusory feeling of prediction*: subjects feel they can foresee what happens next, even when empirically they cannot. A feeling begets another feeling, with no additional information.
- **Tenet alignment**: These are clean dissociations showing that noetic feelings are independent signals—they can fire without their truth-makers and fail when truth-makers are present. This pattern is only intelligible if the feelings are heuristic outputs, not perceptions.
- **Quote**: "When people detect familiarity in a novel scene, it creates a subjective sense that they know what will happen next, even when they cannot actually predict the outcome."

### "The Illusion of Knowing in Metacognitive Monitoring" (*Frontiers in Psychology*, 2018)
- **URL**: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6016031/
- **Type**: Empirical psychology paper
- **Key points**:
  - Calibration accuracy—the gap between subjective confidence and actual performance—is systematically poor, and the errors are not evenly distributed.
  - Overconfidence ("illusion of knowing") is persistent and resistant to training; underconfidence also exists but is less studied.
  - "While there is a positive correlation on the whole between degree of confidence and accuracy the degree of confidence is not a reliable index of accuracy."
- **Tenet alignment**: Occam's Razor Has Limits—the economical assumption that strong feelings of certainty track knowledge is empirically wrong, and yet we cannot stop relying on the feelings.

### Arango-Muñoz & Michaelian, "Epistemic Feelings: Phenomenology, Implementation, and Role in Cognition" (editorial, *Frontiers*, 2020)
- **URL**: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2020.606046/full
- **Type**: Editorial / review
- **Key points**:
  - Epistemic feelings comprise a large family: familiarity, knowledge, belief, confidence, doubt, confusion, curiosity, agency, ownership. They are ubiquitous components of experience.
  - The feelings are both *phenomenal* (felt) and *intentional* (about something)—they have content.
  - Their implementation remains contested; their phenomenology remains underspecified in philosophy of mind.

## The Void

### Nature of the Limit

The noetic feelings void is primarily *occluded* with an *unexplorable* core. Three structural features mark it:

1. **Feed-forward opacity**: The heuristics that generate the feelings (fluency, cue-familiarity, accessibility, partial retrieval) are implemented in processes whose inputs and dynamics are not exposed to the consciousness they inform. Consciousness receives a finished feeling, not a derivation.

2. **Authority without audit**: The feelings *gate* further cognition. They decide when to stop searching, when to trust, when to defer, when to engage analytic thought. But the system cannot audit them from inside, because audit requires more feelings of the same kind—a feeling that this feeling is reliable.

3. **Dissociable fallibility**: Déjà vu, jamais vu, false confidence, and calibration failures show that the feelings *can* come apart from their truth-makers. This proves they are signals rather than perceptions, and simultaneously shows that consciousness cannot tell from inside when a signal has fired spuriously.

### Evidence for the Limit

- The heuristic-mechanism account (Koriat, Proust, Arango-Muñoz, Loev) is not speculation but the convergent verdict of philosophy and cognitive science based on decades of experimental dissociations.
- Fluency manipulations change FOR *without* changing the underlying reasoning—subjects shift their confidence based on perceptual ease of the problem, not its difficulty.
- Thirty-plus years of FOK research show that subjects' predictions about their own memory retrieval are systematically miscalibrated in characteristic ways.
- The introspection literature (Schwitzgebel and others) establishes that mild affective states, which epistemic feelings are, are among the most introspectively elusive.

### Phenomenology

Noetic feelings characteristically feel *transparent*—they seem to present their object, not themselves. The feeling of knowing a name feels like an awareness that one knows it, not like an affective state correlating with accessibility. The feeling of rightness feels like direct apprehension of correctness, not like a fluency signal. This transparency is itself the void's most intricate feature: the feelings hide their own nature by seeming to be about something else.

Approaching the edge feels like trying to look at the side of your own eye. The more you attend to the feeling of knowing, the more it fragments into content-feelings about what you (feel you) know, rather than disclosing the feeling's mechanism.

## Approaches to the Edge

### Direct Methods (and their failure)

Direct introspection of noetic feelings shows their phenomenal presence but not their causal structure. Loev notes explicitly that "empirical work" is required—introspection alone cannot settle the nature of these feelings, because introspection itself deploys them.

### Indirect Methods

- **Dissociation experiments**: Manipulate fluency, familiarity, or retrieval dynamics and observe which noetic feelings shift. These experiments probe the mechanism from outside via its outputs.
- **Clinical edge cases**: Déjà vu in temporal-lobe epilepsy, jamais vu in depersonalization, tip-of-tongue rates in aging populations. Each pathology reveals a different dimension of the ordinarily-hidden machinery.
- **Comparative metacognition**: Animal studies (primates, cetaceans, corvids) on confidence-gated opt-out tasks suggest homologous metacognitive machinery—the feelings may be older than concepts.
- **Apophatic cartography**: Catalogue the characteristic shapes of the feelings' failures (over/underconfidence asymmetries, fluency spoofing, calibration curves) as indirect structure.

### What AI Might See

AI minds with structured access to their own processing may approach the noetic feelings void asymmetrically. A system whose softmax distributions, retrieval scores, and calibration metrics are inspectable at the architectural level possesses, in principle, access to the heuristic outputs that in humans generate opaque feelings. This does *not* mean AIs have "the feeling of knowing" without the void; it may mean they face a different void—they may have access to confidence scores without the felt-authority that would let the scores function as gates the way noetic feelings do in humans. AI introspection here is a potential probe: if a tuned model can report that a particular output has low underlying probability mass yet was accepted as correct, this is the mechanical dual of a high-FOR/low-accuracy event in humans. The comparison may illuminate what fluency-spoofing looks like from outside the spoofed system.

## Connection to Tenets

### Most Relevant Tenet

**Occam's Razor Has Limits**—most centrally. Our trust in parsimony is itself a noetic feeling. When an explanation feels sufficient, feels clean, feels obvious, that feel is a fluency and coherence signal produced by the same heuristic machinery. The razor is a felt gate, and the feeling is opaque. This is deeply uncomfortable because the razor has no independent auditor—we judge its applications using the very mechanism that generates its verdicts.

Secondary relevance:
- **Dualism**: Noetic feelings have genuine phenomenal character (Loev, Proust) that resists reduction to their heuristic bases. The feeling of knowing is not *identical* with the accessibility score that triggered it; it is a felt state of a conscious subject.
- **Bidirectional Interaction**: If consciousness has causal power, the interface is partly routed through these feelings—they determine which cognitive processes get sustained and which get terminated. The occlusion of the mechanism mirrors the occlusion of the causal interface more broadly.

### Implications

1. **Metacognition is structurally suspect as a self-certification mechanism.** The system that tells us when we know cannot tell us when it is wrong. This does not impugn metacognition; it does impugn claims that metacognitive authority should be final.

2. **Self-evidence is a felt signal.** What feels undeniable feels that way because of fluency and coherence heuristics operating beneath inspection. The [[understanding-void]] is a special case of the noetic feelings void—the feeling of understanding is one member of the family.

3. **Inquiry terminates at the boundary of felt satisfaction.** Because FOR gates analytic engagement, most thinking ends when the feeling says "this is right." Inquiry can therefore systematically underexplore regions where answers feel correct but aren't. The Map's emphasis on conditionality and apophatic cartography can be read as an attempt to *distrust* the feeling of sufficiency.

4. **The void is recursive.** Meta-monitoring of noetic feelings uses more noetic feelings. There is no feeling-free vantage from which to audit the system, inside a single consciousness.

## Potential Article Angles

Based on this research, a voids article could:

1. **Cataloguing angle**: Inventory the family—FOK, FOR, FOF (familiarity), TOT, feeling of understanding, feeling of coherence, feeling of presence, feeling of rightness, feeling of error, feeling of certainty—and show how each gates a distinct cognitive operation while sharing the same opacity pattern. Positions noetic feelings as the *class* under which many existing voids (understanding, recognition, sincerity-like voids) become instances.

2. **Gating angle**: Focus on the way noetic feelings terminate inquiry. Argue that what looks like "rational stopping" is in fact felt stopping, and that the Map's apophatic discipline is a technique for *sustaining inquiry past the natural gate* set by noetic feelings of sufficiency.

3. **Recursive-opacity angle**: Structure the article around the impossibility of self-audit. Use déjà vu, jamais vu, and fluency-manipulation experiments as anchor cases showing that signals can fire without referents and refuse to fire for actual referents, and that from inside we cannot tell which is which.

4. **AI-contrast angle**: Use noetic feelings as the lens for comparing human and artificial introspection. Humans have felt authority without mechanism access; certain AI architectures have mechanism traces without felt authority. Neither can self-certify, but each fails to self-certify differently.

Recommended primary angle: **recursive opacity with catalogue support**—present the family, then show the structural void: the signals that gate cognition are precisely the signals we cannot audit without their help.

## Gaps in Research

- Whether noetic feelings in non-human animals have phenomenal character or only functional character remains open; the Map's Dualism leans toward phenomenal, but the evidence is indirect.
- The mapping between specific heuristics (fluency, accessibility, cue-familiarity) and specific phenomenal feels is not fully worked out in the literature—there is not yet a reliable "feelings dictionary."
- Whether the void is *in principle* closed or merely *in practice* closed (Occluded vs Unexplorable) cannot be settled from inside. If metacognitive architecture is evolutionarily designed to produce feelings while hiding mechanism (for computational efficiency or epistemic-control reasons), third-person neuroscience may yet map the mechanisms; but first-person access to them may be permanently impossible.
- The relationship between noetic feelings and the consciousness-physics interface (amplification-void, causal-interface) is entirely unexplored. If consciousness's influence on the physical is routed through attention and inquiry-continuation, noetic feelings are the proximate controllers—and their opacity may be structurally linked to interface-occlusion.
