## Executive Summary

This note maps the **prokaryotic floor** of the biological-competency ladder — goal-directed, memory-bearing, adaptive behaviour in a *single cell with no neurons and no multicellularity*. The empirical anchor is *E. coli* chemotaxis: a molecularly characterised sense→decide→act→adapt loop (receptor MCP → CheA/CheW → CheY → flagellar motor), whose **methylation-based adaptation** (CheR/CheB modifying the chemoreceptor) implements a genuine short-term "memory" of recent chemical concentration. Pamela Lyon's **biogenic cognition / cognitive biology** programme reads this as the *minimal case* of cognition, treating cognition as continuous with life itself and running "all the way down" to bacteria. A stronger claim — Reber & Baluška's **Cellular Basis of Consciousness** — holds that the same cellular machinery is *sentient*, that feeling is coterminous with life. This is the sharpest **competency-without-experience** case in the sub-personal-competence cluster: it exhibits sensing, memory, valence, and adaptive decision in one cell, and it is precisely where the Map's framework locates *no neural substrate to host a consciousness interface*. The note is designed to fill the prokaryotic gap **below** the eukaryotic simple organisms (slime moulds, C. elegans, Hydra) covered by [[consciousness-in-simple-organisms]], and below the neuron-less *animals* of [[neuron-less-animals-sponges-placozoans-and-the-lower-bound-of-cognition]].

## The Mechanism (verified empirical floor)

The *E. coli* chemotaxis loop, component by component (verified):

1. **Sense** — Methyl-accepting chemotaxis proteins (**MCPs**), membrane-bound chemoreceptors, detect attractants (sugars, amino acids) and repellents.
2. **Transduce/decide** — The receptor–**CheW**–**CheA** complex is the signalling core. Ligand binding changes CheA autophosphorylation; CheA transfers phosphate to the response regulator **CheY**.
3. **Act** — CheY-P binds the flagellar switch, biasing rotation clockwise → **tumble**; default CCW rotation → **run**. Suppressing tumbles when conditions improve produces a biased random walk up-gradient.
4. **Adapt / remember** — **CheR** methylates the receptor (adds methyl groups; each MCP has ~four methylation sites), **CheB** (activated by CheA phosphorylation) demethylates. This methylation/demethylation feedback resets receptor sensitivity to the *current baseline*, giving near-perfect adaptation and encoding a **short-term memory of recent concentration** — the molecular substrate for the temporal comparison Berg and Koshland identified.

**Two distinct "memories" — keep separate:**
- *Chemotactic short-term memory* (~seconds): receptor-methylation gain-control / temporal-comparison mechanism above. This is the one that matters for "memory in a single cell."
- *Epigenetic long-term memory*: Lyon separately notes **DNA** methylation and chromatin remodeling as a longer-term learning channel. Different molecule, different timescale — do not conflate the two in the article.

## Major Positions

### Deflationary functionalism (mechanism-only)
- **Proponents**: mainstream molecular microbiology (Berg, Koshland lineage); the EMBO dissent.
- **Core claim**: Chemotaxis is a fully specified biochemical control system; "memory," "decision," and "choice" are useful shorthand for feedback dynamics, not evidence of mind or feeling.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Compatible with the Map. Supplies the "competency-as-function" reading — a *defeasible marker*, decisive nowhere alone.

### Biogenic cognition / cognitive biology (Lyon)
- **Proponents**: Pamela Lyon; Baluška; the cognitive-biology programme.
- **Core claim**: Cognition is continuous with life; bacteria are the minimal genuine case of sensing–valuing–remembering–acting. Cognition ≠ consciousness, but cognition goes all the way down.
- **Relation to site tenets**: The Map can grant the *functional* continuity. The article's job is to hold the line: **functional cognition does not entail phenomenal experience.** Engage Lyon as the strongest "cognition all the way down" position to *answer*, not to assert past.

### Cellular Basis of Consciousness (Reber & Baluška)
- **Proponents**: Arthur Reber; František Baluška; William Miller.
- **Core claim**: Sentience is coterminous with life; the excitable membrane of a single cell already *feels*. Bacteria are minimally conscious.
- **Relation to site tenets**: **Conflicts** with Tenet-2 (minimality) and the Map's interface-localisation. On the Map's framework there is **no neural substrate to host a consciousness interface** at the bacterial rung; CBC posits experience precisely where the Map's commitments license its absence. Frame framework-relatively (see below).

## Key Debates

### Does "cognition" imply "experience"?
- **Sides**: Biogenic cognition (Lyon) keeps them separable; CBC (Reber/Baluška) fuses them; deflationists deny both above the metaphorical.
- **Core disagreement**: Whether a molecularly specified adaptive feedback loop is *evidence of felt experience* or merely of function.
- **Current state**: Ongoing and unresolved. The Map sides with keeping function and phenomenality distinct, and treats bacteria as the cleanest demonstration that competency can be fully present with no plausible substrate for feeling.

### Is bacterial "decision" real choice or stochastic noise?
- **Sides**: CBC (real proto-choice) vs. molecular skeptics (stochastic protein-level variation).
- **Core disagreement**: The reality and locus of agency in a single cell.
- **Current state**: Empirically live; the EMBO dissent captures the skeptical pole.
