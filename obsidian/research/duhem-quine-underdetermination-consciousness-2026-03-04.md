## Executive Summary

The Duhem-Quine thesis—that no hypothesis can be tested in isolation because empirical tests always presuppose auxiliary hypotheses—has profound implications for consciousness science, where rival theories (IIT, Global Workspace, Higher-Order, dualist frameworks) face an extreme form of underdetermination. The 2025 adversarial collaboration published in Nature tested IIT against GNWT and found mixed results supporting neither theory decisively, illustrating the underdetermination problem in action. Recent scholarship (Lenharo, 2024) has proposed Lakatos's methodology of scientific research programmes as a framework for interpreting such results, distinguishing core commitments from auxiliary hypotheses and asking whether programmes are progressive or degenerating. For the Map's dualist position, underdetermination cuts both ways: it undermines physicalist claims to have "settled" the consciousness question, but it also means dualist proposals face the same evidential challenges. The Map's Occam's Razor tenet is directly relevant—underdetermination shows why simplicity cannot adjudicate between consciousness theories.

## Key Sources

### Underdetermination of Scientific Theory (Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy)
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/scientific-underdetermination/
- **Type**: Encyclopedia
- **Key points**:
  - Distinguishes holist underdetermination (which belief to revise when predictions fail) from contrastive underdetermination (whether evidence can confirm one theory against alternatives)
  - Duhem's original formulation applied only to physics; Quine extended it to all knowledge including logic and mathematics
  - Stanford's "unconceived alternatives" argument: historically, scientists have repeatedly failed to exhaust theoretically distinct possibilities, suggesting present theories are likely underdetermined by future alternatives
  - Laudan's critique distinguishes logically possible revisions (weak) from rationally defensible ones (strong), but on Quine's holism even ampliative principles are revision-candidates
  - Bayesian and virtue epistemology responses attempt to constrain theory choice beyond pure evidence
- **Tenet alignment**: Strongly aligns with **Occam's Razor Has Limits**—underdetermination shows that simplicity arguments cannot resolve fundamental disputes. Also supports **Dualism** by showing physicalism's evidential advantages are less decisive than commonly assumed.
- **Quote**: "Because no hypothesis is ever tested in isolation, no experiment ever tells us precisely which belief it is that we must revise or give up as mistaken."

### (Dis)confirming Theories of Consciousness: Towards a Lakatosian Consciousness Science (Lenharo, 2024)
- **URL**: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10944285/
- **Type**: Paper (Neuroscience of Consciousness, Oxford Academic)
- **Key points**:
  - Proposes Lakatos's methodology as superior to "experimental eliminativism" for consciousness science
  - Distinguishes core claims from auxiliary hypotheses in IIT (phenomenological axioms, integrated information identity) and GNWT (global broadcast, workspace availability)
  - Core claims are protected by "belts" of auxiliary hypotheses; falsified predictions target the conjunction of core and auxiliaries
  - Three criteria for theory assessment: prediction vs accommodation, structural relevance of predictions, boldness of predictions
  - Bold predictions—peculiar to one theory and unlikely if the theory were false—carry more confirmatory power
  - After the Cogitate results, both IIT and GNWT remain progressive because theorists respond by revising auxiliaries and generating new testable predictions
  - "For Lakatos, a degenerating research programme can ultimately be discarded only by another research programme, not by an experiment"
- **Tenet alignment**: Strongly supports **Occam's Razor Has Limits** and **Dualism**. The Lakatosian framework implies that physicalist theories of consciousness cannot be confirmed merely by being "simpler"—they must be empirically progressive. It also implies that dualist research programmes cannot be dismissed by single experiments; they must be outperformed by rival programmes over time.

### Adversarial Testing of GNW and IIT (Nature, 2025)
- **URL**: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-025-08888-1
- **Type**: Paper (Nature)
- **Key points**:
  - 256 participants viewed suprathreshold stimuli; neural activity measured with fMRI, MEG, and intracranial EEG
  - Results partially supported both theories: IIT better predicted face orientation decoding; GNWT better predicted object category decoding
  - IIT challenged by lack of sustained synchronisation in posterior cortex
  - GNWT challenged by lack of ignition at stimulus offset and limited prefrontal representation
  - No single experiment could decisively refute either theory—"the theories are just too different in their assumptions and explanatory goals"
  - Demonstrates underdetermination in action: same data admits interpretation under both frameworks
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral to positive for the Map. The inconclusive results illustrate that physicalist theories of consciousness face severe underdetermination, undermining claims that neuroscience has or will "solve" consciousness through empirical methods alone. Supports the Map's contention that the hard problem is not merely an empirical puzzle.

### How Can We Construct a Science of Consciousness? (Chalmers)
- **URL**: https://consc.net/papers/scicon.html
- **Type**: Paper
- **Key points**:
  - The science of consciousness relates third-person data (brain processes, behaviour) to first-person data (conscious experience)
  - First-person data are irreducible to third-person data and vice versa; both are needed
  - Subjects' introspective reports are not infallible, and training risks corrupting observations with theory
  - The introspectionist programme of the 19th century collapsed when schools could not agree on introspective data—a historical precedent for theory-ladenness problems
  - Functional, dynamical, and structural explanations of consciousness are incomplete: "even after we have explained the functional, dynamical, and structural properties of the conscious mind, we can still meaningfully ask the question, Why is it conscious?"
- **Tenet alignment**: Strongly aligns with **Dualism** and **Bidirectional Interaction**. Chalmers' argument that third-person methods cannot capture first-person data parallels the Map's commitment to consciousness as irreducible. The theory-ladenness of first-person reports adds a further layer of underdetermination beyond the Duhem-Quine thesis as standardly formulated.

### Pierre Duhem (Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy)
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/duhem/
- **Type**: Encyclopedia
- **Key points**:

  - For Duhem, good sense (bon sens) guided theory choice where logic could not

- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral. Duhem's "good sense" is a philosophical ancestor of Kuhn's values, which the Map could invoke to argue that theory choice in consciousness science involves legitimate extra-empirical considerations.

### Kuhn on Values in Theory Choice
- **URL**: https://cseweb.ucsd.edu/~goguen/courses/275f00/kuhn.html
- **Type**: Paper summary
- **Key points**:
  - Kuhn argued that accuracy alone is insufficient for theory choice; consistency, scope, simplicity, and fruitfulness also matter
  - These values function as criteria but do not determine unique choices—scientists can weight them differently
  - Theory choice involves judgment, not algorithm
- **Tenet alignment**: Aligns with **Occam's Razor Has Limits**. If theory choice involves values beyond accuracy and simplicity, then dismissing dualism on parsimony grounds is methodologically naive.

## Major Positions

### Holist Underdetermination (Duhem-Quine)
- **Proponents**: Pierre Duhem, W. V. O. Quine
- **Core claim**: No hypothesis is tested in isolation; failed predictions indict the conjunction of hypothesis plus auxiliaries, and logic alone cannot determine which to revise
- **Key arguments**: Foucault's experiment refuting emission theory still required auxiliary assumptions; Newton's theory survived anomalous Uranus orbit by postulating Neptune (auxiliary revision)
- **Relation to site tenets**: Directly supports **Occam's Razor Has Limits**. If we cannot isolate hypotheses for testing, parsimony arguments carry even less force than commonly assumed. The underdetermination problem is amplified for consciousness because the auxiliary hypotheses include contested assumptions about the relationship between brain states and experience.

### Contrastive Underdetermination
- **Proponents**: Van Fraassen, Stanford
- **Core claim**: Multiple theories can be empirically equivalent—supported by identical evidence—making evidence alone insufficient for theory choice
- **Key arguments**: Stanford's "unconceived alternatives" argument shows that historically, the scientific community has repeatedly been unable to conceive of all theory options, suggesting current underdetermination by future alternatives
- **Relation to site tenets**: Strongly supports the Map's framework. If contrastive underdetermination is genuine, then physicalist consciousness theories cannot claim evidential superiority over dualist alternatives. The evidence is compatible with both.

### Lakatosian Research Programmes Applied to Consciousness
- **Proponents**: Lenharo (2024), extending Lakatos
- **Core claim**: Consciousness theories should be evaluated as research programmes with core commitments protected by auxiliary hypotheses; what matters is whether they are progressive (generating novel predictions) or degenerating (merely accommodating evidence)
- **Key arguments**: The Cogitate adversarial collaboration results do not "eliminate" either IIT or GNWT; instead, both respond by revising auxiliaries and generating new predictions, remaining progressive. Only a rival programme—not a single experiment—can rationally displace a theory.
- **Relation to site tenets**: The Lakatosian framework legitimises dualist research programmes provided they make and confirm novel predictions. Dualism cannot be dismissed by a single "killer argument" (contra eliminativists); it must be outperformed as a research programme over time. This aligns with **Occam's Razor Has Limits** and supports the Map's commitment to treating dualism as a live research programme rather than a dead philosophical position.

### Theory-Ladenness of First-Person Data
- **Proponents**: Chalmers, historical introspectionism debate
- **Core claim**: First-person reports about conscious experience are themselves theory-laden, adding a unique layer of underdetermination beyond what Duhem-Quine describes for natural science
- **Key arguments**: The collapse of introspectionist psychology when rival schools could not agree on data; subjects trained in introspection may have their observations "corrupted by theory"; first-person data are irreducible to third-person data
- **Relation to site tenets**: Aligns with **Dualism** and **Bidirectional Interaction**. If first-person data are genuinely irreducible, this supports the Map's claim that consciousness cannot be captured by third-person physical descriptions alone. The theory-ladenness of introspection means that physicalist interpretations of consciousness data are not more "objective" than dualist ones—both involve theoretical commitments in interpreting first-person reports.

## Key Debates

### Can Adversarial Collaborations Resolve Consciousness Theory Disputes?
- **Sides**: Eliminativists (experiments can refute theories) vs Lakatosians (experiments constrain but cannot eliminate theories)
- **Core disagreement**: Whether a single experiment or series of experiments can decisively falsify a consciousness theory, or whether theory revision (modifying auxiliaries) is always a rational response
- **Current state**: Ongoing. The 2025 Nature Cogitate results favoured neither IIT nor GNWT decisively. Lenharo (2024) argues the Lakatosian interpretation is both descriptively accurate and normatively sound. The adversarial collaboration framework is expanding to test more theories (Higher-Order, Recurrent Processing).

### Is Consciousness Science Uniquely Underdetermined?
- **Sides**: Those who see consciousness as posing special methodological problems (Chalmers, Nagel) vs those who see it as an ordinary scientific problem with standard underdetermination (Dennett, Churchland)
- **Core disagreement**: Whether the irreducibility of first-person data creates a form of underdetermination qualitatively different from that in physics or biology
- **Current state**: Ongoing. The hard problem remains central. Chalmers argues that even complete functional/structural explanation leaves "Why is it conscious?" unanswered. Physicalists respond that this gap will close with further empirical progress.

### What Role Do Extra-Empirical Values Play in Consciousness Theory Choice?
- **Sides**: Kuhnian pluralists (values like simplicity, scope, fruitfulness legitimately influence theory choice) vs strict empiricists (evidence should be sufficient)
- **Core disagreement**: Whether invoking extra-empirical considerations (including metaphysical commitments like dualism) in theory choice is legitimate science or ideology
- **Current state**: Ongoing. The proliferation of 325+ consciousness theories (Kuhn's Landscape) suggests that empirical evidence alone cannot adjudicate. The Map's tenets represent explicit extra-empirical commitments, which the Kuhnian position validates as a legitimate component of theory choice.
