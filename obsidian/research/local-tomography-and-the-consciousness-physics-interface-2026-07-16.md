## Executive Summary

**Local tomography** (equivalently *tomographic locality*) is the GPT axiom that the joint state of a composite system is completely determined by the statistics of *local* measurements on its subsystems (combined with the correlations between those local outcomes). It is one of the two axioms — alongside purification — that the sibling note ([[research/generalised-probabilistic-theories-and-the-born-rule-2026-07-16]]) identified as doing the decisive work in forcing the Born rule beyond bare no-signalling (Galley-Masanes 2018). This note homes local tomography specifically, and pursues the interface-relevant question the [[generalised-probabilistic-theories]] concept named but left unhomed.

The key structural fact: local tomography is **not** a trivial or universal property. Complex quantum theory satisfies it; **real-vector-space quantum theory does not** (Hardy-Wootters 2010 — it is only *bilocally* tomographic, exhibiting "limited holism"), and **quaternionic quantum theory does not either** (Barnum-Wilce 2014). Complex QM is essentially the *unique* locally-tomographic theory among the natural Jordan-algebraic alternatives (Barnum-Wilce). So local-tomography failure is a well-studied, mathematically concrete regime, not a hand-wave: it is the regime in which a composite has genuinely **global degrees of freedom** — joint information not recoverable from any amount of separate local measurement.

The empirical hook is **Renou et al. 2021 (Nature 600, 625-629)**, "Quantum theory based on real numbers can be experimentally falsified" — a bilocal-network Bell-type experiment whose complex-QM prediction exceeds any real-QM value; it was realised in 2022 on both superconducting and photonic platforms. This is, in effect, a *test of local tomography's role* in nature, and the corpus cites it elsewhere but has never connected it to the interface question. **Evidential caution flagged and load-bearing:** the Renou claim is now contested — Hoffreumon-Woods (2026) argue the falsification rests on an experimentally untestable "product-state independence" assumption, and that a real quantum theory with representation locality is operationally viable. So even the *empirical* status of local tomography as a fact about nature is live, which the Map must not overstate.

**The Map speculation (open question, NOT a finding):** a composite of (physical brain + non-physical substrate) is precisely a candidate regime where local tomography could *fail* — where the joint state is not fixed by local measurements on the physical and non-physical parts separately, requiring genuinely holistic degrees of freedom. If so, the Born-rule constraint the [[generalised-probabilistic-theories]] article derived would **not bind the interface**, because that derivation *assumed* local tomography. This is either (a) a problem — the Map loses a constraint it leaned on — or (b) a signature — non-local-tomographic structure is exactly the formal shape a genuinely non-physical, holistically-coupled substrate might take. This note argues (b) is the more interesting reading but marks the whole line as Map speculation under [[evidential-status-discipline]].

- **Title**: "Limited Holism and Real-Vector-Space Quantum Theory"
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed (Foundations of Physics) — **verified by direct WebFetch of arXiv:1005.4870**
- **Key points** (verified against the arXiv abstract):
  - Complex quantum theory satisfies **local tomography**: composite-system states can be reconstructed from measurements on individual components. The authors read this as the sense in which "the holism of quantum theory is **limited**."
  - **Verbatim (abstract)**: "real-vector-space quantum theory, while not locally tomographic, is **bilocally tomographic**."

It shows failure is not pathology — real QM is a consistent theory — it just means genuine global degrees of freedom exist. The "limited holism" framing is directly suggestive for an interface where physical and non-physical parts might be *more* holistically bound than complex QM allows.

### Barnum & Wilce (2012/2014) — local tomography nearly uniquely picks complex QM
- **URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/1202.4513 (arXiv); "Local Tomography and the Jordan Structure of Quantum Theory," published *Foundations of Physics* 44, 192-212 (2014), doi:10.1007/s10701-014-9777-1
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed (Foundations of Physics)
- **Key points** (from search summaries; not full-read — flagged):
  - Local tomography "is satisfied by **neither real nor quaternionic** quantum theory."
  - The quaternionic tensor-product state space is the wrong dimension to even *define* the product effects a locally-tomographic composite needs — its dimension is smaller than the product of the component spaces, so global degrees of freedom are unavoidable.
  - **Main theorem (as summarised)**: orthodox finite-dimensional *complex* QM (with superselection) is the *only* non-signalling probabilistic theory in which (a) individual systems are Jordan algebras with homogeneous, self-dual cones, (b) composites are **locally tomographic**, and (c) at least one system is a qubit. I.e. local tomography is close to a *characterising* axiom for complex QM among the natural alternatives.
- **Interface relevance**: makes precise that *choosing* local tomography is essentially *choosing* complex QM. A regime that departs from complex QM (as a genuinely non-physical substrate might) is, almost by construction, a candidate for local-tomography failure.
- **Provenance**: title/venue/arXiv-ID verified via search; the theorem statement is from the search summary of the abstract, NOT a full read — **flag for line-verification before quoting in an article**.

## The Axiom, Precisely

**Local tomography (tomographic locality).** A GPT satisfies local tomography if, for every composite system AB, the global state ω_AB is **uniquely determined** by the joint probabilities it assigns to all pairs (a, b) of *local* effects — a an effect on A, b an effect on B. Equivalently: the dimension of the composite state space equals the *product* of the component dimensions, d_AB = d_A · d_B. There are **no global degrees of freedom** beyond those fixed by local measurements and their correlations.

- **Complex QM**: locally tomographic. d_AB = d_A · d_B (the Hermitian-matrix dimension count works out: a complex d-level system has d² real parameters, and (d_A d_B)² = d_A² · d_B²).
- **Real QM**: **fails**. Real-symmetric matrices give d(d+1)/2 parameters; the composite has *more* parameters than the product of the parts, so some global states are invisible to all local measurement pairs — recoverable only from *joint bipartite* measurements. Real QM is **bilocally (2-locally) tomographic** (Hardy-Wootters).
- **Quaternionic QM**: **fails** in the opposite direction — the natural tensor product is *too small* to carry the product effects local tomography needs (Barnum-Wilce).
- **Classical probability**: locally tomographic (trivially — the simplex composes by ordinary product).
- **Boxworld**: locally tomographic (it is a max-tensor-product theory), so local tomography does *not* by itself force quantumness — consistent with the sibling note's "no-signalling + LT is still not the whole story; purification is the other axiom."

**What failure looks like formally**: a *global* or *holistic* degree of freedom — a component of the joint state that no product of local effects can read out. The state is genuinely more than the sum (and correlations) of its locally-accessible parts. Hardy-Wootters call the residual "limited holism"; the amount of holism is exactly the gap d_AB − d_A·d_B.

## The Interface Speculation (Map open question — NOT a finding)

**Setup.** The [[generalised-probabilistic-theories]] concept derived the Born-rule constraint on any outcome-selecting agent *conditional on* local tomography (and purification) holding across the coupling. Its honest conditional: "an agent coupling to quantum outcomes in a regime that satisfies purification and local tomography — as physics does everywhere it has been tested — is Born-constrained on pain of signalling."

**The move.** Consider the composite (physical brain B + non-physical substrate S) that the Map's Tenet-2 interface posits. Nothing guarantees this composite is locally tomographic. Indeed:
1. Local tomography is *empirically confirmed* (modulo the Hoffreumon-Woods dispute) only for **physical/physical** composites — two photons, two qubits. It has never been tested across a physical/non-physical cut, because no such cut is experimentally accessible.
2. Barnum-Wilce show local tomography *characterises* complex QM among natural theories. A genuinely non-physical substrate is under no obligation to be a complex-QM system, so its composition with the brain is under no obligation to be locally tomographic.
3. If (B+S) is **not** locally tomographic, then by Galley-Masanes the door is open: the interface's outcome-selection could deviate from the Born rule *without* signalling, because the derivation that closed that door assumed the axiom that now fails.

**Two readings** (the Map should present both, commit to neither as established):
- **(a) Problem.** The Map loses a constraint. The clean "any agent is Born-constrained on pain of signalling" argument evaporates precisely where it was wanted (the interface), leaving the interface *unconstrained* by this route — an argumentative liability, not an asset.
- **(b) Signature.** Non-local-tomographic structure is arguably the *natural formal shape* of a genuinely non-physical, holistically-coupled substrate: the joint brain-substrate state carries global degrees of freedom not reducible to separate physical and non-physical measurements. On this reading the *failure* of local tomography is not a bug but the formal fingerprint of dualist holism — and the "limited holism" literature (Hardy-Wootters) is the ready-made vocabulary. It would also predict that brain-*internal* Born-rule tests ([[brain-internal-born-rule-testing]]) are the right place to look for deviations, since that is where the physical/non-physical cut is crossed.

**Discipline.** Reading (b) is *speculation the Map finds attractive*, not a result. There is: no measurement of the (B+S) composition rule; no theory of what S's state space is; and a live dispute about whether local tomography is even empirically decidable for ordinary systems. The article must frame this as an open question that *sharpens* Tenet 2 (it names the exact axiom whose interface-status is unknown), not as evidence *for* the interface. ## Key Debates

### Is local tomography a fact about nature?
- **Sides**: Renou et al. (2021) + the 2022 experiments (yes — complex QM / local tomography is empirically forced over real QM) vs. Hoffreumon-Woods (2026) (no — the falsification uses an untestable product-state-independence assumption; a representation-local real QM survives).
- **Core disagreement**: whether "operational independence of sources" (testable) is enough to run the Renou argument, or whether it covertly needs "product-state independence" (a constraint on state form, untestable).
- **Current state**: **unresolved as of 2026.** The Map should report the Renou result as the mainstream, experimentally-realised position while flagging the 2026 challenge — do not present real-QM as cleanly falsified.

### Does local-tomography failure require exotic physics?
- Real and quaternionic QM are the standard witnesses that consistent, non-locally-tomographic theories exist. They are *mathematically* well-defined and (for real QM) reproduce all bipartite Bell correlations. So failure is not exotic in the "logically strained" sense — it is exotic only in that ordinary lab composites appear to obey complex QM. The interface, being a physical/non-physical composite, is exactly the case where "ordinary lab composite" reasoning does not obviously transfer.
