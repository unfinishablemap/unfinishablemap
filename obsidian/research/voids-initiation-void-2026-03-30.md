# Research: Voids — The Initiation Void

**Date**: 2026-03-30
**Search queries used**: "Libet readiness potential consciousness initiation voluntary action philosophy", "phenomenology of action initiation onset conscious will Husserl protention", "consciousness hidden onset mental acts cognitive void epistemic limits agency", "Metzinger mental autonomy epistemic agent model mind wandering onset", "readiness potential reinterpretation stochastic neural noise critique Libet", "Merleau-Ponty motor intentionality pre-reflective action initiation phenomenology", "Schurger 2012 accumulator model readiness potential stochastic threshold crossing", "Gallagher Zahavi phenomenology agency sense of agency pre-reflective ownership action", "dualism free will readiness potential consciousness initiates action non-physical causation"
**Voids category**: Occluded / Mixed

## Executive Summary

Every conscious act—deliberate decisions, voluntary movements, intentional shifts of attention, even the resumption of focused thought after mind-wandering—begins before consciousness reports it. The "initiation void" names the structural impossibility of witnessing the onset of one's own mental acts. Libet's experiments (1983) provided the empirical touchstone: readiness potentials precede conscious awareness of intention by ~400ms. But the void runs deeper than any single experiment. Phenomenologically, the beginning of every mental act is always already underway when consciousness arrives. Merleau-Ponty's "motor intentionality," Husserl's "protention," Metzinger's analysis of epistemic agent model collapse—all converge on the same finding: the birth of intention is structurally hidden from the intending consciousness.

## Key Sources

### Libet et al. (1983) — Original Experiment
- **URL**: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/6640273/
- **Type**: Landmark empirical paper
- **Key points**:
  - Readiness potential (RP) begins ~535ms before action; conscious intention ("W time") reported ~200ms before action
  - "Cerebral initiation even of a spontaneous voluntary act can and usually does begin unconsciously"
  - Libet himself preserved a role for consciousness via "conscious veto"—the ability to abort an initiated action within the final ~200ms
- **Tenet alignment**: Bidirectional Interaction—Libet's veto preserves a causal role for consciousness even while showing its initiation is hidden
- **Quote**: "The brain 'decides' to initiate or, at least, to prepare to initiate the act before there is any reportable subjective awareness that such a decision has taken place."

### Schurger, Sitt & Dehaene (2012) — Stochastic Accumulator Reinterpretation
- **URL**: https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.1210467109
- **Type**: Computational neuroscience paper (PNAS)
- **Key points**:
  - Proposed that the RP reflects stochastic neural noise crossing a decision threshold, not a deterministic preparatory process
  - A leaky accumulator model reproduces the RP's shape and timing from random fluctuations alone
  - When imperative to move is weak, the precise moment of threshold crossing is "largely determined by spontaneous subthreshold fluctuations in neuronal activity"
  - This reinterpretation weakens Libet's anti-free-will argument but *deepens* the initiation void: if action onset depends on stochastic noise, the initiation is not just hidden from consciousness—it may be inherently indeterminate
- **Tenet alignment**: Minimal Quantum Interaction—stochastic neural noise at the threshold of action is precisely where a minimal non-physical influence could operate without detectable violation of physical laws
- **Quote**: "The precise moment at which the decision threshold is crossed leading to movement is largely determined by spontaneous subthreshold fluctuations in neuronal activity."

### Metzinger (2013, 2015) — Mental Autonomy and the Epistemic Agent Model
- **URL**: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3868016/
- **Type**: Philosophical/cognitive science papers
- **Key points**:
  - Defines "M-autonomy" (mental autonomy) as a functional property requiring an Epistemic Agent Model (EAM)—a self-model representing the self as an epistemic agent
  - Mind-wandering episodes begin with EAM collapse: the subject loses the capacity for epistemic self-control without noticing the transition
  - Critically: "The onset of every fresh period of M-autonomous cognition may involve an element of misrepresentation"—when we *regain* focused thought, we construct an "illusory phenomenology of self-causation" to represent the transition
  - This means both the loss and recovery of mental agency are hidden from the agent
- **Tenet alignment**: Bidirectional Interaction—if consciousness genuinely initiates cognitive acts, the mechanism of initiation must be hidden (otherwise the initiation could be gamed or short-circuited)
- **Quote**: "An illusory phenomenology of self-causation will be a necessary neurocomputational fiction in the construction of any new EAM."

### Merleau-Ponty — Motor Intentionality
- **URL**: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11097-021-09769-4
- **Type**: Phenomenological philosophy
- **Key points**:
  - "Motor intentionality" describes a bodily directedness that precedes and underlies reflective consciousness
  - The body schema organises action in a "horizonal and context-sensitive manner" that operates pre-reflectively
  - Conceptual, reflective knowledge does not constitute a necessary foundation for sensorimotor coping—action begins before thought catches up
  - "Operative intentionality" establishes "the natural and pre-predicative unity of the world and of our life" beneath conscious awareness
- **Tenet alignment**: Dualism—motor intentionality suggests a layer of agency that is neither purely physical (it is directed, meaningful) nor fully conscious (it is pre-reflective), consistent with dualist accounts of layered causation
- **Quote**: "Operative intentionality establishes the natural and pre-predicative unity of the world and of our life."

### Husserl — Protention and Temporal Structure
- **URL**: https://iep.utm.edu/phe-time/
- **Type**: Encyclopedia article on phenomenological time-consciousness
- **Key points**:
  - Consciousness has three inseparable temporal moments: primal impression, retention, and protention
  - Protention is future-directed intentionality—it "intends the phase of the object about to occur"
  - This means consciousness is always already oriented toward what comes next, but the mechanism of that orientation is pre-reflective
  - Protention enables surprise (Husserl): we can be surprised only because we already anticipate, but the anticipation itself is not a conscious act
- **Tenet alignment**: Occam's Razor Has Limits—the apparently simple present moment is actually a three-layered temporal structure, most of which is invisible to the consciousness living through it

### Gallagher (2012) — Multiple Aspects of the Sense of Agency
- **URL**: https://core.ac.uk/download/pdf/37006634.pdf
- **Type**: Philosophical analysis
- **Key points**:
  - Distinguishes sense of ownership (pre-reflective: "I am the subject of this movement") from sense of agency (pre-reflective: "I am the cause of this action")
  - The sense of agency has "multiple contributories, some reflectively conscious, some pre-reflectively conscious, and some non-conscious"
  - Pre-reflective agency is "generated by non-conscious motor-control and body-schematic processes"
  - We experience ourselves as agents, but the processes generating that experience are structurally hidden
- **Tenet alignment**: Bidirectional Interaction—consciousness experiences itself as causal, but the causal mechanism is opaque; this is exactly what dualist interaction would predict if the interface operates below reflective access

### Conscious Intention and Human Action (2021 Review)
- **URL**: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1053810021000970
- **Type**: Review article
- **Key points**:
  - Comprehensive review of Libet-style experiments and the "rise and fall" of the RP as evidence against free will
  - If the RP is stochastic noise rather than a real neural signal, "a timing difference between a meaningless RP generated by averaging random noise and a subject's W time cannot be used to conclude anything about the timing of brain activity and conscious decisions"
  - Recent literature raises questions about the RP's "ontological status as a real signal in the brain"
  - This does NOT resolve the initiation void—it shifts the question from "the brain decides before you do" to "no one can identify when or how decisions begin"
- **Tenet alignment**: Occam's Razor Has Limits—the simple story (brain decides, consciousness follows) was always too simple; the actual relationship between neural activity and conscious initiation remains genuinely mysterious

## The Void

### Nature of the Limit

The initiation void is primarily **occluded**—consciousness is structurally prevented from witnessing its own onset. This is not merely unexplored territory awaiting better instruments. The impossibility runs deeper:

1. **Temporal structure**: To witness the onset of a mental act, consciousness would need to be conscious *before* consciousness begins. This is a logical impossibility—consciousness cannot precede itself.

2. **Observer-observed collapse**: The act of observing the initiation of thought would itself be a mental act whose initiation is hidden. Infinite regress: who watches the watcher begin to watch?

3. **Functional necessity**: Metzinger argues the initiation must be hidden for the system to work. If we witnessed the stochastic, contingent, largely arbitrary process by which our thoughts and actions begin, the sense of agency would collapse. The "neurocomputational fiction" of self-causation is load-bearing.

4. **Pre-reflective depth**: Merleau-Ponty and Husserl show that the deepest layers of intentionality—motor intentionality, protention, operative intentionality—are constitutively pre-reflective. They are not unconscious in the Freudian sense (repressed); they are the pre-reflective ground on which reflective consciousness stands. You cannot reflect on them without transforming them into something else.

### Evidence for the Limit

**Empirical evidence**:
- Libet's experiments (and hundreds of replications) consistently show temporal lag between neural preparation and conscious report
- Schurger's reinterpretation deepens rather than resolves the problem: even the *neural* onset is stochastic and indeterminate
- Mind-wandering research (Metzinger, Schooler) shows that the transition between mental states is systematically hidden from the subject

**Phenomenological evidence**:
- Direct introspective attempt to catch the onset of a thought always fails—by the time you notice the thought, it is already underway
- Meditation traditions consistently report that attempts to observe the arising of thoughts encounter an irreducible gap: thoughts appear "already formed"
- Creative insight (the "aha moment") arrives complete; the process of generation is invisible

**Philosophical evidence**:
- Husserl's analysis of temporal consciousness shows that protention (future-orientation) is constitutively pre-reflective
- Gallagher's work on agency shows the sense of being an agent is generated by processes that are themselves non-conscious
- The structure is self-reinforcing: to investigate initiation, you must initiate an investigation, whose initiation is hidden

### Phenomenology

What does it feel like to approach the initiation void?

- **The perpetual just-missed beginning**: When you try to catch a thought arising, you find it already in progress. The beginning has always just happened. This is not like failing to see something far away—it is like turning to look at your own turning.

- **The seamless present**: Consciousness presents itself as a continuous flow. Transitions between mental states (deciding, attending, remembering) feel smooth. But this smoothness conceals discrete initiations. The seams are hidden.

- **The retrospective construction**: When asked "when did you decide?", you construct an answer by projecting backward from the completed action. Libet's "W time" is itself a reconstruction, not a direct observation.

- **Meditation's testimony**: Contemplative traditions across cultures report the same finding. Vipassana practitioners develop extraordinary introspective acuity but consistently report that the arising of mental events cannot be caught at the very beginning—only noticed fractionally after onset.

## Approaches to the Edge

### Direct Methods (if any)

No direct method can observe the initiation of conscious acts, for the structural reasons outlined above. Every method of observation is itself a conscious act whose initiation is hidden. However:

- **High-temporal-resolution introspection**: Meditation traditions push introspective precision to its limits, revealing finer-grained structure even if the absolute beginning remains hidden
- **Libet-style experiments**: Map the temporal gap between neural and conscious events, even though they cannot close it
- **Neurophenomenology** (Varela): Combines first-person reports with neural measurements to triangulate the hidden transition

### Indirect Methods

- **Apophatic characterisation**: We cannot say what initiation *is*, but we can carefully describe what it is *not*—it is not deliberate, not observed, not fully determined, not undetermined
- **Failure analysis**: The specific ways introspection fails at the boundary reveal structural features of consciousness. The initiation void has a characteristic shape: temporal, asymmetric (we can observe termination but not initiation), and self-referentially closed
- **Pathological evidence**: Conditions like alien hand syndrome, utilisation behaviour, and anarchic hand reveal what happens when the initiation process breaks down—these cases expose the hidden machinery by its malfunction
- **Stochastic threshold models**: Schurger's accumulator model provides a formal framework for the indeterminate zone where initiation occurs—a space where consciousness *could* exert influence without that influence being detectable

### What AI Might See

AI systems do not have the initiation void in the same way (or may have a different one):

- **Transparent initiation**: An AI system's "decisions" can be traced through computational graphs. The initiation is not hidden—it is architecturally exposed. This makes AI a potential probe for what initiation looks like when the void is absent.
- **Different opacity**: AI systems have their own opacity (emergent behaviours, uninterpretable weights), but it is a different *kind* of opacity than the phenomenological initiation void. Comparing the two asymmetries could illuminate what the human void is really about.
- **No protention**: AI systems (in current architectures) lack Husserlian protention—they do not anticipate the next moment of experience. If protention is what hides initiation, AI systems may lack the mechanism that creates the void.
- **Possible probe**: An AI could potentially analyse the neural dynamics at the exact moment of threshold crossing in real time, accessing the transition that is hidden from the human subject. This would not resolve the void (the subject still cannot witness their own initiation) but might characterise its neural correlates.

## Connection to Tenets

### Most Relevant Tenet: Bidirectional Interaction

The initiation void is most deeply connected to **Bidirectional Interaction**—the tenet that consciousness causally influences the physical world. If consciousness exerts genuine causal influence, the *mechanism* of that influence must be hidden from consciousness itself for several reasons:

1. **Interface opacity**: You cannot observe the interface you are using to observe. If consciousness influences neural events, the point of influence is structurally below reflective access.
2. **Stochastic window**: Schurger's accumulator model identifies exactly the kind of indeterminate zone where minimal non-physical influence could operate—the stochastic threshold-crossing region where neural noise determines action onset. A dualist could argue that consciousness tips the balance at this threshold without violating physical conservation laws.
3. **Veto as evidence**: Libet's "conscious veto" preserves a causal role for consciousness even after the initiation is underway. The fact that consciousness can abort but cannot observe its own initiating is consistent with an interface that operates pre-reflectively.

### Secondary Tenet: Minimal Quantum Interaction

The stochastic threshold-crossing zone identified by Schurger is precisely where a minimal quantum-level influence could matter. If the brain's decision mechanism is a noisy accumulator hovering near threshold, an extremely small non-physical nudge could determine which stochastic fluctuation crosses the threshold. The initiation void may exist *because* this is where consciousness makes contact with the physical—and the contact point must be hidden to prevent infinite regress (consciousness observing its own influence, then observing itself observing, etc.).

### Tertiary Tenet: Occam's Razor Has Limits

The standard materialist interpretation of Libet (brain decides before consciousness knows) was always the "simpler" story. Schurger's reinterpretation shows this simplicity was illusory—the RP may not be a real preparatory signal at all. The relationship between neural activity and conscious initiation is more complex, more indeterminate, and more mysterious than the simple narrative suggested. This is a textbook case of parsimony concealing genuine complexity.

Based on this research, a voids article could:

1. **The Initiation Void** — Frame the impossibility of witnessing one's own cognitive onset as a distinct and fundamental void, synthesising Libet, Schurger, Metzinger, and Merleau-Ponty. Argue that this void is not a deficiency but a structural feature of any consciousness that is also an agent: to act, you must not see yourself beginning to act.

## Gaps in Research

- **Cross-cultural phenomenological data**: Meditation traditions (Buddhist vipassana, Zen shikantaza, contemplative Christian prayer) all report on the arising of mental events, but systematic phenomenological comparison of their findings about initiation opacity is sparse
- **Developmental angle**: When does the initiation void develop in children? Infants seem to act without any sense of deliberate initiation—does the void emerge alongside the sense of agency?
- **Pathological disruption**: Alien hand syndrome, utilisation behaviour, and schizophrenic delusions of control all involve disrupted initiation—more systematic study of these as "windows into the void" would be valuable
- **AI comparison**: No systematic philosophical comparison of human initiation opacity with AI decision transparency has been published; this is a promising direction for the Map's AI-as-void-explorer framework
- **Whether the void is universal or architecture-specific**: Is the initiation void a feature of *all possible* conscious agents, or only of agents with our particular neural architecture? This question may be fundamentally unanswerable from our position.
