## Executive Summary

Yogacara (also Vijnanavada, "doctrine of consciousness"; Cittamatra, "mind-only"), founded by the half-brothers Asanga and Vasubandhu (4th-5th c. CE), replaced orthodox Buddhism's six-consciousness psychology with an **eight-consciousness model** (*asta-vijnana*): the five sense consciousnesses, the sixth mental consciousness (*mano-vijnana*), the seventh afflicted mind (*klista-manas*), and the eighth **storehouse consciousness** (*alaya-vijnana*). The alaya is a subliminal, individual, ever-changing stream that stores **karmic seeds** (*bija*) deposited by experience through **perfuming** (*vasana*); seeds ripen into new experience, which perfumes fresh seeds. This solves the Buddhist problem of **continuity-without-self**: it carries personal and rebirth continuity across moments and lives **without** a permanent *atman*, because the storehouse itself is impermanent and conditioned — a process, not a soul. For the Map, alaya-vijnana is a basin-**edge** case: a fully worked-out **consciousness-first** substrate-continuity theory that is a **rival** (closer to idealist monism) rather than an ally to interactionist dualism, though whether it is metaphysical idealism or epistemic/phenomenological remains scholarly-contested and should be held unadjudicated.

## Key Sources

### Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy — "Yogacara"
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/yogacara/ (Spring 2026 edition archived at /archives/spr2026/entries/yogacara/)
- **Type**: Encyclopedia (peer-reviewed)
- **Key points**:
  - Yogacara's signature doctrine attributes priority to mind/cognition; the sensible world "depends for its nature and existence on being cognised by a mind."
  - The storehouse consciousness holds karmic seeds as the source of all forms of consciousness; they "arise only when their seeds are ready to ripen."
  - **Whether Yogacara is "idealism" in the Western sense is one of the most-debated questions in modern scholarship** — some read it as realist about non-conceptual content, some as phenomenology, not metaphysical idealism. (Load-bearing for the unadjudicated framing.)
- **Tenet alignment**: Conflicts with Tenet 1 (Dualism) on its face — mind-first monism, not dualism — but the idealism-vs-phenomenology dispute keeps the conflict framework-relative.
- **Quote**: "only... mind or cognition."

### SEP — "Vasubandhu"
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/vasubandhu/
- **Type**: Encyclopedia
- **Key points**: Vasubandhu's *Vimsatika* (Twenty Verses) argues *vijnaptimatra* ("appearance/cognition only") — objects have "no real existence outside of perception"; his *Trimsika* (Thirty Verses) lays out the storehouse, the afflicted mind, and the three-natures doctrine. The storehouse is also defended in his *Karmasiddhiprakarana* ("Establishment of Action").
- **Tenet alignment**: Conflicts with Tenet 1; relevant to Tenet 4 (indexical identity) since each alaya is individual.

### Lambert Schmithausen, *Alayavijnana: On the Origin and the Early Development of a Central Concept of Yogacara Philosophy* (1987)
- **URL**: https://ndlsearch.ndl.go.jp/en/books/R000000004-I2926704 ; reprint (2007, Part II) at archive.org/details/schmithausen-alayavijnana-part-ii-reprint-2007
- **Type**: Monograph (2 vols., Studia Philologica Buddhica Monograph Series 4, International Institute for Buddhist Studies, Tokyo; reprinted 2007 with addenda)
- **Key points**: The foundational philological study of the concept's origin. Schmithausen locates the earliest occurrences in the "Basic Section" of the **Yogacarabhumi**, and argues the alaya was introduced to solve a *dogmatic* problem — how consciousness persists through the meditative cessation-attainment (*nirodhasamapatti*), when the six active consciousnesses lapse — rather than arising directly from meditative experience.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral (historical-philological); grounds the claim that the storehouse is a **continuity-preserving posit**, which is the load-bearing philosophy-of-mind point.

### William S. Waldron, *The Buddhist Unconscious: The Alaya-vijnana in the Context of Indian Buddhist Thought* (2003)
- **URL**: https://www.routledge.com/.../Waldron/p/book/9780415406079 (RoutledgeCurzon / Routledge Critical Studies in Buddhism; hbk 9780415298094)
- **Type**: Monograph
- **Key points**: Where orthodox Buddhist psychology has six consciousnesses, Yogacara posits an eighth "to serve as the substratum of the seeds engendered by karma." Waldron shows the posit grew from problems inherited from pre-Yogacara Abhidharma (continuity of latent karma, of the mind-stream through cessation). His framing of alaya as a "**Buddhist unconscious**" is the natural bridge to modern depth-psychology and personal-continuity debates.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral-to-rival; the substratum/subliminal-continuity reading is exactly the "substrate-continuity theory" angle.

### Dan Lusthaus, *Buddhist Phenomenology: A Philosophical Investigation of Yogacara Buddhism and the Ch'eng Wei-shih Lun* (2002)
- **URL**: https://philpapers.org/rec/LUSBPA (RoutledgeCurzon, 2002, xii+611 pp.; Routledge Critical Studies in Buddhism)
- **Type**: Monograph (built around Vasubandhu's *Trimsika* and Xuanzang's *Cheng Weishi Lun*)
- **Key points**: Argues Yogacara is best read as a **phenomenology / epistemological idealism**, not a metaphysical idealism that denies matter's existence — a corrective to the naive "mind-only = only minds exist" reading. Central to holding the Map's rival/ally verdict unadjudicated.
- **Tenet alignment**: Softens the Tenet-1 conflict (phenomenological reading is less directly anti-realist), but does not make Yogacara dualist.

### Asanga, *Mahayanasamgraha* ("Compendium of the Great Vehicle")
- **URL**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mah%C4%81y%C4%81nasa%E1%B9%83graha ; modern translation Karl Brunnholzl, *A Compendium of the Mahayana* (Snow Lion/Shambhala, 2018, ISBN 9781559394659)
- **Type**: Primary treatise (with modern critical translation + 20 appendices on the alaya and its seeds)
- **Key points**: Chapter 1 gives the most extensive early analysis of the storehouse: a subliminal consciousness in which **impressions (*vasana*) from past experience are stored as seeds (*bija*) of future experience**; the active consciousness (*pravrtti-vijnana*) of present experience grows from those seeds. Asanga presents the alaya as "the foundation of what is to be known."
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral primary source; defines the seed/perfuming continuity mechanism.

### Cheng Weishi Lun / *Vijnaptimatratasiddhi-sastra* (Xuanzang, c. 659 CE)
- **URL**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cheng_Weishi_Lun ; IEP "Xuanzang" at https://iep.utm.edu/xuanzang/
- **Type**: Primary compendium
- **Key points**: A commentary/synthesis on Vasubandhu's *Trimsika* by the Tang monk Xuanzang (602-664), uniquely composed as a **selective, evaluative edit of ten Indian commentaries** rather than a straight translation. Systematizes the eight consciousnesses and the *tathagatagarbha* into the definitive East Asian statement of Consciousness-only.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral primary source; the canonical systematization.

## Major Positions

### The Eight-Consciousness Model (asta-vijnana)
- **Proponents**: Asanga, Vasubandhu, Xuanzang.
- **Core claim**: Mind decomposes into eight streams: (1-5) the five sense consciousnesses; (6) *mano-vijnana*, discriminating mental consciousness; (7) *klista-manas*, the afflicted mind; (8) *alaya-vijnana*, the storehouse.
- **Key arguments**: The extra two layers explain phenomena the six-consciousness model cannot — subliminal karmic continuity (alaya) and the persistent, pre-reflective sense of "I" (manas). The **seventh consciousness (klista-manas)** continuously **mis-takes the storehouse for a permanent self**, generating the four self-afflictions (self-view, self-pride, self-love, self-ignorance). This is a striking philosophy-of-mind result: the *illusion* of an enduring self is itself given a mechanism, distinct from the storehouse that actually carries continuity.
- **Relation to site tenets**: The manas/alaya split is a non-Western foil for Tenet 4 (indexical identity): continuity is real and individual (each being has its own alaya) even though the felt permanent self is a construction — continuity-without-a-substantial-self.

### Alaya-vijnana as Substrate-Continuity (the load-bearing contribution)
- **Proponents**: Asanga (systematizer), Vasubandhu; Schmithausen and Waldron (modern reconstruction).
- **Core claim**: Personal experience and rebirth continuity are carried by an impermanent, conditioned, ever-changing **substrate stream** that stores and ripens karmic seeds — solving continuity-without-*atman*. Seeds are deposited by perfuming (*vasana*) and ripen into experience, which deposits new seeds: a self-sustaining feedback loop, not a static warehouse.
- **Key arguments**: Solves the Abhidharma continuity problems (latent karma across time, mind-stream through *nirodhasamapatti*/dreamless-like cessation, rebirth linkage) without violating *anatman*. "Patterns persist while their substrate remains impermanent."
- **Relation to site tenets**: A **rival substrate account.** The Map posits a non-physical consciousness interacting with the physical (Tenets 1, 3); Yogacara posits a consciousness-only substrate with no independent physical to interact *with* — continuity is intra-mental. It vindicates a **non-physical continuity-substrate** (superficially ally-ish to Tenet 1's anti-physicalism) while **rejecting the mind/matter duality** the Map's Tenets 1 and 3 require. Hold unadjudicated: do NOT conscript Yogacara as dualist.

### Consciousness-Only (vijnapti-matra) as Idealism — contested
- **Proponents**: standard reading (idealism); Lusthaus and some SEP contributors dispute (phenomenology / epistemic idealism).
- **Core claim (standard)**: The external world is a projection/construction from seeds in the alaya; "nothing but cognition."
- **Relation to site tenets**: On the strong idealist reading, direct **rival to Tenet 1 (Dualism) and Tenet 3 (Bidirectional Interaction)** — there is no mind-independent physical for consciousness to push against, so the interaction problem "dissolves" by denying one relatum. On the phenomenological reading, the conflict softens to a difference of emphasis. **This dispute is exactly why the verdict stays framework-relative.**

## Key Debates

### Is Yogacara metaphysical idealism or phenomenology?
- **Sides**: Traditional/analytic reading (metaphysical idealism, only-minds-exist) vs Lusthaus-style phenomenological reading (a disciplined analysis of experience / epistemic idealism that brackets rather than denies matter).
- **Core disagreement**: Whether *vijnaptimatra* denies the existence of matter or only denies our access to it as anything but cognition.
- **Current state**: Ongoing; unresolved. **The Map must not pick a side to score a tenet point.**

### Did the alaya originate in meditative experience or dogmatic problem-solving?
- **Sides**: Experiential-origin readings vs Schmithausen's philological thesis (dogmatic speculation on a *nirodhasamapatti* sutra passage).
- **Core disagreement**: Whether the storehouse is a report of contemplative phenomenology or a theoretical posit to plug a continuity gap.
- **Current state**: Schmithausen's account is dominant among philologists; relevant because it frames the alaya primarily as a **continuity-explaining posit** (which is the Map-relevant angle).
