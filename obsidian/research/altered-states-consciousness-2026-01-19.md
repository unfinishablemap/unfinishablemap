## Executive Summary

Altered states of consciousness (ASCs) constitute a diverse family of experiences—psychedelics, anesthesia, meditation, hypnosis, flow states, sleep, near-death experiences—unified by qualitative deviation from baseline waking consciousness. Charles Tart's foundational definition identifies ASCs as "profound changes in the overall pattern of subjective experiences." Recent neuroscience (2024-2025) reveals that different altered states produce distinct neural signatures: psychedelics increase between-network connectivity while decreasing within-network connectivity (especially in the default mode network); anesthesia disrupts corticothalamocortical circuits and prefrontal-posterior connectivity; flow states involve "transient hypofrontality" with reduced executive control; hypnosis alters executive control network dynamics. For dualism, ASCs present a complex picture: the tight correlation between brain states and conscious states seems to support materialism, yet several findings align better with the filter/transmission theory—particularly that radically different brain states (propofol vs. ketamine) can produce identical behavioral outputs with divergent conscious experiences, that psychedelics may *expand* rather than merely *alter* consciousness (consistent with Huxley's "reducing valve" being loosened), and that the Penrose-Hameroff Orch OR theory proposes psychedelics send microtubules into quantum coherence. Most importantly, ASCs reveal that consciousness operates in multiple modes—suggesting the brain-consciousness interface has configurable settings rather than being a simple on/off producer.

## Major Positions

### Materialist/Physicalist View
- **Proponents**: Dehaene, Baars, Tononi, Dennett
- **Core claim**: ASCs are brain states; altered consciousness = altered brain activity
- **Key arguments**:
  - Tight correlation between neural changes and subjective changes
  - Pharmacological manipulation predictably alters consciousness
  - Brain damage produces specific consciousness deficits
- **Relation to site tenets**: Conflicts with Dualism. Materialist view treats correlation as evidence of production.

### Filter/Transmission Theory
- **Proponents**: William James, Henri Bergson, Aldous Huxley, Kelly & Kelly
- **Core claim**: Brain filters/transmits consciousness rather than producing it; ASCs reveal altered filtering
- **Key arguments**:
  - Psychedelics *expand* rather than merely *distort* consciousness (loosened filter)
  - Different anesthetics produce identical behavioral output with different conscious states
  - NDEs and meditation cessation suggest consciousness can persist with minimal brain activity
  - Correlation proves interface, not production
- **Relation to site tenets**: Directly aligns with Dualism and Bidirectional Interaction tenets. This is the site's interpretive framework.

### Quantum Consciousness View
- **Proponents**: Penrose, Hameroff, Stapp
- **Core claim**: Consciousness involves quantum processes; ASCs alter quantum dynamics
- **Key arguments**:
  - Orch OR: consciousness arises from quantum collapse in microtubules
  - Psychedelics may induce microtubule quantum coherence
  - Anesthetics may dampen quantum oscillations
  - Biological quantum effects (avian magnetoreception) prove evolution can harness coherence
- **Relation to site tenets**: Directly supports Minimal Quantum Interaction tenet. Provides mechanism for how consciousness might interact with brain.

### Predictive Processing View
- **Proponents**: Clark, Friston, Carhart-Harris
- **Core claim**: ASCs involve altered prediction/inference; psychedelics relax priors
- **Key arguments**:
  - REBUS/ALBUS models: psychedelics relax high-level predictive constraints
  - Meditation trains different predictive models
  - Flow involves releasing predictive control
- **Relation to site tenets**: Neutral—compatible with either materialism or dualism depending on interpretation of what "priors" fundamentally are.

## Key Debates

### Production vs. Transmission
- **Sides**: Materialists (brain produces consciousness) vs. Filter theorists (brain transmits/filters consciousness)
- **Core disagreement**: What does brain-consciousness correlation prove?
- **Current state**: Ongoing. Most neuroscientists assume production; philosophers of mind more divided.

### Reliability of ASC Content
- **Sides**: Skeptics (ASCs produce misrepresentations) vs. Advocates (ASCs reveal valid aspects of consciousness)
- **Core disagreement**: Are psychedelic insights, mystical experiences, NDE reports *true* or merely *felt as true*?
- **Current state**: Empirical claims (veridical NDEs) can be tested; metaphysical claims remain contested.

### Mechanism of Psychedelic Effects
- **Sides**: 5-HT2a signaling model vs. Quantum coherence model vs. Predictive processing model
- **Core disagreement**: What level of description explains psychedelic consciousness?
- **Current state**: Ongoing. Models not necessarily exclusive—could describe different levels.

## Gaps in Research

- **Anesthesia-meditation comparison**: Both produce reduced consciousness but via opposite paths (pharmacological suppression vs. volitional withdrawal). Direct comparison would illuminate voluntary vs. involuntary aspects of the interface.
- **Cross-cultural ASC taxonomy**: Most research Western-centric. Systematic comparison of how different cultures categorize and induce ASCs would be valuable.
- **Long-term ASC effects on baseline consciousness**: Do repeated ASCs permanently alter the filtering mechanism? Some meditation research suggests yes.
- **Quantum mechanism specifics**: Hameroff's psychedelic-microtubule hypothesis needs more direct testing.
