## Executive Summary

Thomas Metzinger's Self-Model Theory of Subjectivity (SMT) is the most fully developed naturalist "no-self" account in contemporary philosophy of mind, and its author is the single most-cited philosopher who currently has no dedicated page on the Map. SMT's central claim is deflationary: no such thing as a *self* exists — "nobody ever had or was a self." What exists is a **phenomenal self-model (PSM)**, a representational structure the brain continuously generates to model the organism-as-a-whole. We take ourselves to *be* selves rather than to *have* self-models because the PSM is **phenomenally transparent**: introspection cannot detect its representational character, so its content is experienced as immediately given reality rather than as a model. SMT matters to the Map for a specific reason: it is a direct, sophisticated challenge to Tenet 4 (indexical identity / a determinate experiencing subject). If the subject is a virtual construct, what anchors "this experience is *mine*, now"? The Map's engagement (framework-relative, not a refutation) is that SMT superbly explains the *structure* of self-representation while the transparency mechanism *relocates* rather than *closes* the hard problem, and that SMT is the naturalist cousin of the Buddhist/Yogācāra *anattā* traditions the Map already engages.

## Key Sources

### Metzinger, *Being No One: The Self-Model Theory of Subjectivity* (2003)
- **URL**: https://direct.mit.edu/books/monograph/1991/Being-No-OneThe-Self-Model-Theory-of-Subjectivity
- **Type**: Book (MIT Press / A Bradford Book), xiv + 699 pp. ISBN 9780262633086.
- **Publisher-verified**: Yes — MIT Press book gateway; Cambridge *Philosophy of Science* review confirms 2003, xiv+699.
- **Key points**:
  - Foundational statement of SMT. Representationalist and functional analysis of the consciously experienced first-person perspective.
  - Central thesis: no selves exist in the world; only phenomenal selves exist, as contents of a *transparent self-model*. The self is a *process*, not a *thing*.
  - Two core theoretical posits: the **PSM** (phenomenal self-model) and the **PMIR** (phenomenal model of the intentionality relation).
  - Method: multilevel constraints on the concept of consciousness; case studies of anomalous states (agnosia, neglect, blindsight, hallucination, phantom limbs, OBEs).
- **Tenet alignment**: Directly *conflicts* with Tenet 4 (no determinate self to anchor indexicality) and with Tenet 1 (dualism) — SMT is reductive/representationalist. Engaged as a principal rival.

### Metzinger, *The Ego Tunnel: The Science of the Mind and the Myth of the Self* (2009)
- **URL**: https://www.basicbooks.com / Amazon ISBN 9780465020690
- **Type**: Book (Basic Books, 2009). Trade/popular exposition of SMT.
- **Publisher-verified**: Yes — Basic Books 2009; multiple catalog confirmations.
- **Key points**:
  - Popular restatement: the conscious self is the content of a model the brain creates — "an internal image, but one we cannot experience *as* an image." The "Ego Tunnel" is the transparent phenomenal world-model with the self at its center.
  - Extends SMT to ethical, cultural, and social consequences of consciousness science (the "consciousness ethics" agenda).
- **Quote**: "The conscious self is the content of a model created by our brain — an internal image, but one we cannot experience as an image."

### Blanke, O. & Metzinger, T. (2009), "Full-body illusions and minimal phenomenal selfhood"
- **URL**: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1364661308002507
- **Type**: Review article, *Trends in Cognitive Sciences* 13(1):7-13, 2009.
- **Publisher-verified**: Yes — ScienceDirect / TiCS vol. 13, pp. 7-13.
- **Key points**:
  - Introduces **minimal phenomenal selfhood (MPS)**: "the experience of being a distinct, holistic entity capable of global self-control and attention, possessing a body and a location in space and time."
  - Argues the constituting (necessary and sufficient) features of MPS are: (1) global identification with the body as a whole, (2) self-location in space, and (3) a first-person (weak, geometrical) visuospatial perspective.
  - Empirical anchor: full-body illusions and autoscopic phenomena (rubber-hand illusion generalized to the whole body; out-of-body experiences induced experimentally) show self-location and self-identification are *manipulable* — evidence the sense of being an embodied self is a constructed model.
- **Tenet alignment**: Supplies the empirical backbone for SMT's anti-realism about the self. Neutral-to-conflicting for the Map: the illusion data are real and must be accommodated, but the *inference* from manipulability to "no self" is where the Map contests.

### Metzinger, T. (2020), "Minimal phenomenal experience: Meditation, tonic alertness, and the phenomenology of 'pure' consciousness"
- **URL**: https://philosophymindscience.org/index.php/phimisci/article/view/8960 (DOI 10.33735/phimisci.2020.I.46)
- **Type**: Article, *Philosophy and the Mind Sciences* 1(I):1-44, 2020.
- **Publisher-verified**: Yes — Philosophy and the Mind Sciences; DOI resolves.
- **Key points**:
  - Develops **minimal phenomenal experience (MPE)** — "pure awareness" / contentless consciousness in meditation — as a candidate for the simplest form of consciousness, via six semantic constraints and sixteen phenomenological case studies.
  - Hypothesizes the prototype of "pure awareness" is the content of a predictive model: a Bayesian representation of *tonic alertness*.
  - Relevance here: MPE is where SMT reaches toward the very "witness"/contentless-consciousness phenomenology that the contemplative traditions (and the Map's [[witness-consciousness]] page) treat as evidence of an irreducible subject. Metzinger reads it as *still* representational (a model of alertness), keeping the no-self line intact even at the phenomenological limit.

## Major Positions

### Self-Model Theory of Subjectivity (SMT) — Metzinger's own view
- **Proponents**: Thomas Metzinger; empirically, Olaf Blanke (self-location / MPS); broadly aligned with predictive-processing and representationalist theories of mind.
- **Core claim**: The self is not an entity but the *transparent content of a self-model*. Subjectivity = a system running a PSM plus a PMIR (a model of the relation between the modeled self and its intentional objects). Because the model is transparent, the system is "naively realistic" about its own selfhood — it cannot experience the self *as* a representation.
- **Key mechanisms**:
  1. **PSM (phenomenal self-model)** — the brain's integrated, ongoing representation of the organism-as-a-whole (body, affect, cognition), the content of which is the experienced self.
  2. **PMIR (phenomenal model of the intentionality relation)** — a conscious model of the *arrow of directedness* linking the self to an object/goal ("I am now attending to / reaching for X"). The first-person perspective is the PMIR in action.
  3. **Phenomenal transparency** — we see *through* the self-model to its content, not the model itself; earlier processing stages are introspectively unavailable. Transparency is *why* it feels like *being* a self rather than *having* one.
  4. **No-self / Ego Tunnel** — the self is virtual/simulated, a construct, not a substance; "no one" is home behind the model.
  5. **Minimal phenomenal selfhood (MPS)** — the empirically isolable floor of embodied selfhood (identification + self-location + perspective), shown manipulable by full-body illusions.
- **Relation to site tenets**: A principal *rival*. Reductive representationalism denies Tenet 1 (dualism) and, most sharply, dissolves the determinate subject Tenet 4 requires.

### Representationalism / Functionalism about the self (the broader family)
- **Proponents**: Metzinger; adjacent to Dennett's narrative-self deflationism and predictive-processing self-models (Hohwy, Seth, Friston-adjacent).
- **Core claim**: Selfhood is exhausted by representational/functional facts; no further phenomenal residue needs a non-physical carrier.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Conflicts with Tenets 1 and 4; the Map's standing reply is that the *phenomenal character* of the modeling is left unexplained (see below).

### Realist / substantival self views (the foils SMT opposes)
- **Proponents**: Cartesian and neo-Cartesian subject-realists; some phenomenological (Zahavi's minimal/experiential self) and Indian traditions (Advaita *ātman*, the *sākṣin* witness).
- **Core claim**: There is an experiencing subject or minimal self that is not reducible to a representational construct.
- **Relation to site tenets**: *Aligns* structurally with the Map's Tenet-4 commitment to a determinate subject — though the Map grounds this in dualist coupling, not in a substantival Cartesian ego.

## Key Debates

### Does transparency *explain* or merely *relocate* subjectivity?
- **Sides**: Metzinger (transparency reductively explains why selfhood feels non-representational) vs. hard-problem critics (transparency explains the *sense of immediacy* but not *why there is phenomenal character at all*).
- **Core disagreement**: Grant that the self-model is transparent — this predicts we would *represent* ourselves as simple, present, real. It does not obviously entail there is *something it is like* to run such a model. The explanatory target (why any modeling *feels*) survives.
- **Map's stake**: This is the Map's central lever. SMT brilliantly accounts for the *structure* of self-representation, but "why does a transparent self-model feel like being someone at all?" is the hard problem re-sited inside the self, not dissolved. Present as dialectic, not refutation.
- **Current state**: Ongoing; live in predictive-processing and illusionism debates.

### Is the "no-self" conclusion licensed by the illusion data?
- **Sides**: Blanke/Metzinger (manipulability of self-location/identification ⇒ the self is a constructed model) vs. minimal-self realists (Zahavi et al.: the illusions manipulate *bodily* self-representation but presuppose an underlying experiential first-person givenness that is not itself manipulated away).
- **Core disagreement**: Whether "the felt locus of the self can be moved" entails "there is no subject," or only "the subject's *bodily model* is plastic."
- **Current state**: Unresolved; central to the minimal-self literature.

### SMT vs. contemplative "pure awareness"
- **Sides**: Metzinger (MPE / pure awareness is *still* a predictive model of tonic alertness — no-self holds even here) vs. contemplative-realist readings (contentless witness-consciousness evidences a subject prior to all representation).
- **Core disagreement**: Whether the phenomenological limit case of contentless awareness is a *model* or a *witness*.
- **Current state**: Actively debated (2020 MPE program).

## Map-Relative Framing and the Tenet-4 Special Relation

SMT is a *representationalist/functionalist no-self account* — a rival the Map engages, with a special bearing on **Tenet 4 (indexical identity; a determinate experiencing subject; rejection of self-diffusing readings)**. Three framing moves, all framework-relative (the Map has a *stake* in resisting no-self, not a proof against it):

1. **SMT as a direct challenge to Tenet 4.** If there is no self — only a transparent self-model — then what anchors the indexical "*this* experience is *mine*, *now*"? SMT dissolves the very determinate subject Tenet 4 requires; the "owner" of experience becomes a represented feature of a model rather than a metaphysical fact. This parallels the phenomenal-sorites threat (cross-link **phenomenal-sorites-problem**): where the sorites attacks the *determinacy* of *whether/how much* experience is present, SMT attacks the determinacy of *whose* experience it is. Both press on Tenet 4's demand for a sharp, indexed subject, and the Map must locate its resistance rather than assume it. The Map's resistance is not a denial of the PSM's reality but a claim that the *ownership/indexicality* relation is a genuine metaphysical fact underdetermined by the representational story — plausibly grounded in the dualist coupling Tenet 3 posits, not in the model's transparency.

2. **Transparency relocates, does not close, the hard problem.** SMT's real achievement is explaining the *structure* of self-representation: transparency predicts the naive-realist sense of *being* rather than *having*. But it leaves the residual question — *why is there phenomenal character to the modeling at all?* Why does running a transparent self-model *feel like* being someone, rather than proceeding in the dark? Transparency is a claim about the *availability* of representational vehicles to introspection; it does not manufacture experience from non-experience. On the Map's dualist reading this is exactly where a non-physical contribution is doing work that the functional story cannot cover. Present as dialectic: SMT is not thereby refuted; the hard problem is re-sited inside the self-model.

3. **SMT as the naturalist cousin of *anattā*.** SMT's no-self converges strikingly with the Buddhist/Yogācāra no-self traditions the Map already engages (cross-links **yogacara-alaya-vijnana-storehouse-consciousness**, **witness-consciousness**, **self-and-self-consciousness**). Both deny a substantival ego; both treat the felt self as a construction. The divergence is instructive: the Yogācāra *ālaya-vijñāna* is a *conditioned mental continuum*, and the contemplative *witness* (*sākṣin*) is sometimes read as an irreducible experiential locus — whereas SMT's self is a *virtual* representational construct with "no one" behind it. The Map's dualism reads the transparency/witness structure differently from SMT: the witness is a candidate for the *non-physical coupling point* (Tenets 1, 3) rather than a further layer of representation. So the Map can accept much of SMT's *deconstruction* of the substantival ego while resisting its *elimination* of the subject — precisely the move contemplative-realist readers make against Metzinger's MPE reading of pure awareness.
