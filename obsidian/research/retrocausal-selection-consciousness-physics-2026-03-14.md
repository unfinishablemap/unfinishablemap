# Research: Retrocausal Selection Model for Consciousness-Physics Interaction

**Date**: 2026-03-14

## Executive Summary

The retrocausal selection model proposes that consciousness selects which quantum transaction is actualized, operating atemporally rather than at a single forward-in-time moment. This research surveys the major retrocausal frameworks in quantum mechanics—the Transactional Interpretation (Cramer), Possibilist TI (Kastner), Two-State Vector Formalism (Aharonov), all-at-once Lagrangian models (Wharton), Causally Symmetric Bohm model (Sutherland), and Costa de Beauregard's zigzag causation—evaluating each as a candidate mechanism for the Map's consciousness-physics interaction tenets. The retrocausal approach is particularly promising because it resolves the Libet timing puzzle (consciousness need not precede neural activity if selection is atemporal), provides a Lorentz-invariant account of Bell correlations, and offers a natural home for consciousness-as-selector within the quantum formalism. Key tensions include the bilking objection, fine-tuning concerns, and the relationship between retrocausality and the growing-block time model already developed on the Map.

## Key Sources

### Retrocausal Frameworks in Physics

**SEP — Retrocausality in QM** (https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/qm-retrocausality/)
Core motivation: retrocausality circumvents Bell's theorem by violating measurement independence. The bilking argument fails because QM prevents independent epistemic access to past states (Price 1996). Fine-tuning objection (Wood & Spekkens 2015) answered by Evans (2018): "internal cancelling paths" are physically stable, not conspiratorial. No discussion of consciousness—retrocausality treated as purely interpretive.

**Cramer — Transactional Interpretation** (1986, updated 2022)
Quantum events are "transactions"—handshakes between offer waves ψ (forward) and confirmation waves ψ* (backward). Collapse is atemporal: "does not happen at any specific point in time, but occurs along the whole transaction." Born rule emerges naturally. Cramer does not invoke consciousness, but the mechanism could be adapted for consciousness-as-selector.

**Kastner — Possibilist TI** (Cambridge UP, 2012/2022)
Extends Cramer: offer and confirmation waves exist in a "pre-spacetime" (PST) realm of possibilities. Actualized transactions establish spacetime events—spacetime itself emerges from transactions. Most promising framework for the Map: consciousness could operate in the PST realm, selecting which transactions become actual without injecting energy.

**Aharonov et al. — Two-State Vector Formalism** (1964+)
Quantum systems determined by both past (pre-selected) and future (post-selected) boundary conditions. Weak measurements reveal "weak values" outside the eigenvalue spectrum. Time-symmetric, empirically equivalent to standard QM. Aharonov, Cohen et al. (2015) show retrocausality is compatible with free will—quantum indeterminism masks future states from present access.

**Wharton & Price — All-at-Once / Time-Symmetry Models** (2010+)
Wharton: measurements are constraints on Hamilton's action integral; solutions emerge "en bloc." Consciousness could function as a constraint, not a force—the lightest possible intervention. Price: if fundamental laws are CPT-invariant, time-asymmetric causation requires special justification. Retrocausality provides a "third option" beyond nonlocality and many-worlds. Price distinguishes retrocausality from retro-signaling: no information travels backward.

**Sutherland — Causally Symmetric Bohm Model** (2008)
Extends Bohmian mechanics with final boundary condition ψ_f alongside initial ψ_i. Lorentz invariant at hidden level. Consciousness could function as selector of final boundary conditions.

**Costa de Beauregard — Zigzag Causation** (1947+)
EPR correlations result from "zigzag" causal influence—backward from Alice's measurement to shared origin, then forward to Bob. Causal influences follow timelike trajectories, avoiding spacelike action-at-a-distance. Late in career, connected retrocausality to consciousness, though these claims remain speculative.

### Consciousness-Collapse Models (Comparison)

**SEP — Quantum Approaches to Consciousness** (https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/qt-consciousness/)
Von Neumann/Wigner lineage: consciousness completes measurement. Stapp: mental effort protracts neural assembly lifetimes via quantum Zeno effect—implicitly retrocausal (selecting trajectories the system would not follow under free evolution). Primas distinguishes "tensed (mental) time" from "tenseless (physical) time." Tegmark decoherence objection disputed by Hagan et al.

**Chalmers & McQueen** (2021)
Rigorous consciousness-collapse framework. Identifies "super-resistance" problem: if conscious states cannot superpose, Zeno effect prevents transitions. Proposes gradual collapse dynamics. Forward-in-time model, but retrocausal alternative may dissolve super-resistance since temporal ordering would not apply to atemporal selection. Covered in Map's existing research (2026-03-07).

**Schulman — Special States** (1997+)
Future boundary conditions cryptically constrain initial states, avoiding macroscopic superpositions without collapse. No consciousness role—conflicts with Tenet 3, but the mechanism could be adapted if consciousness provides future boundary conditions in neural systems specifically (prebiotic collapse resolution).

## Major Positions

### Position 1: Transaction Selection (Cramer/Kastner) — Most Promising
Consciousness selects which offer-confirmation wave handshake is actualized, operating in Kastner's pre-spacetime realm. Satisfies all five tenets: dualism (consciousness in PST realm), minimal interaction (selection among possibilities, no energy injection), bidirectional interaction (causal selection), no many worlds (single transaction actualized), Occam's limits (forward-only picture may be wrong). Resolves Libet timing since transactions are atemporal.

### Position 2: Two-State Post-Selection (Aharonov/TSVF)
Consciousness functions as the post-selection condition in neural systems. Time-symmetric treatment, explicitly compatible with free will (Aharonov et al. 2015). Strong on Tenet 3. The question of what provides post-selection in non-conscious systems connects to the prebiotic collapse discussion.

### Position 3: Constraint Selection (Wharton/Price)
Consciousness functions as a constraint on the Lagrangian—the lightest possible intervention. Very strong on Tenet 2. Potential tension with growing-block time since Wharton's framework suggests block universe.

### Position 4: Forward-Only Selection (Stapp/Chalmers-McQueen)
Already well-represented on the Map. Does not resolve Libet timing as cleanly. Covered in existing research note (2026-03-07).

### Position 5: No Consciousness Role (Schulman/mainstream)
Future boundary conditions are physical, not mental. Conflicts with Tenet 3. The Map can acknowledge this while maintaining consciousness provides boundary conditions in neural systems specifically.

## Key Debates

### Libet Timing Problem
If consciousness selects atemporally, the sequence "neural activity → conscious awareness → action" is compatible with genuine conscious causation. The retrocausal framework dissolves rather than solves the Libet problem. Already developed in `time-symmetric-selection-mechanism.md`.

### Wheeler's Delayed-Choice
Retrocausal theorists see delayed-choice experiments as evidence of backward influence; others argue no definite properties exist before measurement. Wheeler himself rejected retrocausal readings. Everettian interpretations (Carroll et al.) explain delayed choice without retrocausality. The argument is contested.

### Time Ontology Tension
Does atemporal transaction selection require the future to already exist (block universe)? Kastner's PTI may help: transactions occur in a pre-spacetime realm, so growing-block could describe actualized spacetime while PST remains timeless. The Map's growing-block apex article should engage with this.

### Retrocausality and Free Will
Largely resolved. Aharonov et al. (2015): quantum indeterminism masks the future from present access, preserving libertarian free will. Compatible with the Map's position.

## Gaps in Research

- **Kastner on consciousness**: PTI does not explicitly address consciousness as transaction selector. The Map would construct this as original synthesis.
- **Decoherence interaction**: If selection is atemporal, does decoherence apply? Wharton suggests decoherence is "ignorance about future constraints."
- **Neural implementation**: No retrocausal framework specifies neural-level mechanisms. Existing Stapp/Orch OR material provides scaffolding but integration is underdeveloped.
- **Empirical distinguishability**: Can retrocausal consciousness-selection make distinct predictions? Weak value experiments offer some hope.
- **Growing-block compatibility**: Tension between retrocausal models (suggesting block universe) and the Map's growing-block position needs further development.
