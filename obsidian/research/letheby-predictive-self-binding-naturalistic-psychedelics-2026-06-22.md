# Research: Letheby's Predictive Self-Binding and the Naturalistic Account of Psychedelic Experience

**Date**: 2026-06-22
**Search queries used**: "Letheby predictive self-binding psychedelic experience naturalistic account", "Letheby Gerrans 'Self Unbound' psychedelics ego dissolution predictive processing", "Letheby 'Philosophy of Psychedelics' book argument epistemic benefits naturalism", "predictive self-binding self-model REBUS Carhart-Harris relaxed beliefs psychedelics prior", "Letheby naturalism psychedelics objections criticism Sjöstedt-Hughes limits of naturalism panpsychism dualism", "Letheby self-binding mechanism predictive processing salience network self-model hierarchical inference 2020", "minimal self witness consciousness survives ego dissolution Millière fictionalism"

## Executive Summary

Chris Letheby (with Philip Gerrans) offers the most developed *naturalistic* philosophical theory of psychedelic experience, built on two pillars: the predictive-processing model of brain function and a "self-binding" theory of self-representation. On this **predictive self-binding** account, the felt self is a high-precision Bayesian prior — a hierarchical model the brain maintains because positing a stable, persisting entity efficiently integrates and predicts salient, egocentric, autobiographical information. Psychedelics relax the precision of these high-level self-priors (the REBUS mechanism), "unbinding" mental contents from the self-model; the model becomes phenomenally *opaque* (experienced *as* a model rather than seen-through), which is ego dissolution. Letheby's most consequential metaphysical conclusion is **self-fictionalism**: the unified, substantial self is a "useful Cartesian fiction" with no referent. His *Philosophy of Psychedelics* (Oxford 2021) extends this into an argument that psychedelic therapy yields genuine **epistemic benefits** compatible with naturalism — the cure works by phenomenological insight into the constructed nature of the self, not by instilling non-naturalistic metaphysical beliefs — defeating the "Comforting Delusion Objection." This is the strongest contemporary rival to the Map's filter-theoretic and dualist reading of the same neuroimaging data. Critically, the two accounts are *empirically equivalent on altered-state data alone*; they diverge on the hard problem, on whether a minimal/witnessing self survives dissolution, and on whether psychedelic insights are veridical or merely therapeutic. The corpus already treats Letheby in `topics/psychedelics-and-the-filter-model.md`; this note deepens the mechanism and the self-fictionalism tension for a possible standalone topic.

## Key Sources

### Letheby & Gerrans — "Self unbound: ego dissolution in psychedelic experience" (2017)
- **URL**: https://academic.oup.com/nc/article/2017/1/nix016/3916730
- **Type**: Paper (*Neuroscience of Consciousness*)
- **Key points**:
  - The self-model is a hierarchical predictive-coding structure that predicts the *salience* of information for the organism over time, incorporating its history and goals at multiple scales.
  - "Self-binding": self-related information is processed faster and bound into a representation of a persisting object that unifies and predicts ongoing patterns of salient, egocentric, autobiographical experience. They name the mechanism **predictive self-binding**.
  - Ego dissolution = breakdown of the integrated functioning of these hierarchical models that posit a stable, unchanging entity to which representations are bound.
  - Metaphysical upshot: self-representation is a "useful Cartesian fiction" — a false representation of a simple enduring substance that nonetheless serves to integrate cognition across levels and domains. This supports **fictionalism about the self**.
- **Tenet alignment**: Conflicts directly with the Map. Self-fictionalism (no substantial subject) cuts against the Map's commitment to indexical identity (Tenet 4) and a consciousness that is not reducible to physical modelling (Tenet 1).
- **Quote**: "an ultimately false representation of a simple and enduring substance to which attributes are bound which serves to integrate and unify cognitive processing across levels and domains."

### Letheby — *Philosophy of Psychedelics* (Oxford, 2021)
- **URL**: https://global.oup.com/academic/product/philosophy-of-psychedelics-9780198843122 ; précis: https://philosophymindscience.org/index.php/phimisci/article/view/9627
- **Type**: Book (monograph)
- **Key points**:
  - Central problem: the apparent conflict between psychedelics' clinical use and a naturalistic worldview. The **Comforting Delusion Objection (CDO)**: if psychedelics heal by inducing mystical beliefs that naturalism deems false, prescribing them is prescribing comforting delusions.
  - Letheby's response (a novel "fourth option"): psychedelics work through **transformative phenomenological insight**, not the inducing of false metaphysical beliefs. The therapeutic mechanism is a felt insight into the *constructed, non-substantial nature of the self* — change in the *sense* of self and in how one relates to one's mind, not in beliefs about ultimate reality.
  - Therefore the epistemic *risks* under naturalism are surprisingly small and the epistemic *benefits* surprisingly large: new psychological insight, existential reflection, knowledge-by-acquaintance of altered states, ability to disidentify from maladaptive self-beliefs ("just thoughts").
  - **Naturalistic spirituality**: experiences legitimately called "spiritual" (unity, connectedness, ego transcendence) need not involve belief in anything beyond the natural world.
- **Tenet alignment**: Conflicts (naturalist framing, self-fictionalism) but is methodologically valuable — it is the disciplined physicalist baseline the Map must clear. Its CDO is precisely the objection the Map's *non*-naturalist reading invites.

### Carhart-Harris & Friston — "REBUS and the Anarchic Brain" (2019)
- **URL**: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6588209/
- **Type**: Paper (*Pharmacological Reviews*)
- **Key points**:
  - **REBUS** = "Relaxed Beliefs Under Psychedelics." Combines the free-energy principle with Carhart-Harris's entropic-brain hypothesis.
  - Psychedelics relax the *precision weighting* of high-level priors, so they are held with less confidence; bottom-up prediction errors are released to revise them.
  - A paradigm high-level prior is "the belief that one has a particular personality / set of views" — i.e., the narrative self / ego. This is the physiological substrate Letheby's self-binding account rides on.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral mechanism, physicalist framing. The Map reads the *same* precision-relaxation as filter-loosening (see `psychedelics-and-the-filter-model`).
- **Note**: REBAS ("Revised Beliefs After Psychedelics") extends REBUS to the post-acute therapeutic window; psilocybin reduced confidence in negative self-beliefs, with entropy correlating with the decrease.

### Deane — "Consciousness in active inference: Deep self-models, other minds, and the challenge of psychedelic-induced ego-dissolution" (2021)
- **URL**: https://academic.oup.com/nc/article/2021/2/niab024/6360857
- **Type**: Paper (*Neuroscience of Consciousness*)
- **Key points**:
  - Phenomenal consciousness = "subjective valuation," a system's inference about how well it is self-evidencing (controlling outcomes through action) across nested timescales — the **deep self-model**.
  - Adopts REBUS for ego dissolution: relaxed high-level priors flood the system with interoceptive/sensory prediction error; the system "ceases to posit itself as an endogenous controller of sensation," and the structured self collapses.
  - **Selfless states remain conscious**: affective valence is the most fundamental layer of experience and persists even when structured self-consciousness vanishes. This is a different bet from Letheby's and bears on the minimal-self debate below.
- **Tenet alignment**: Physicalist (active inference) but its claim that bare affective consciousness survives ego dissolution is *structurally* friendly to the Map's "for-me-ness persists" intuition — a useful internal disagreement to exploit.

### Sjöstedt-Hughes — "Psychedelics and the Limits of Naturalism" (review of Letheby, 2022)
- **URL**: https://www.thephilosopher1923.org/post/psychedelics-and-the-limits-of-naturalism
- **Type**: Review essay
- **Key points** (the strongest *non*-naturalist counter to Letheby):
  - **Hard-problem leverage**: because the hard problem is unsolved, Letheby cannot establish that consciousness requires only neural substrates; invoking an unexplained "consciousness-making property" is itself a kind of mysticism, which undercuts dismissing psychedelic experiences as delusions on "scientific" grounds.
  - **Internal tension**: predictive processing casts experience as "controlled hallucination," which sits awkwardly with Letheby's emphasis on genuine *connectedness* — if all experience is internally constructed simulation, how is connection real rather than illusory?
  - **Narrowed options**: naturalism vs. non-naturalism is a false binary; panpsychism, idealism, Spinozism and some dualisms are live and empirically compatible. Under some of these, psychedelic insights could be **veridical**, not delusional — flipping the CDO.
- **Tenet alignment**: Aligns with the Map's anti-reductionism (Tenets 1 and 5). This is the ready-made philosophical wedge for the Map's standalone treatment.

### Millière — "Looking for the Self" (2017) and the minimal-self debate
- **URL**: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5441112/
- **Type**: Paper (*Frontiers in Human Neuroscience*)
- **Key points**:
  - Drug-induced ego dissolution disrupts the subpersonal processes underlying the *minimal/embodied* self (multimodal self-related integration), consistent with Bayesian models of phenomenal selfhood.
  - Ego dissolution challenges the thesis that consciousness *always* involves self-awareness — yet ordinary experience may involve a minimal self-awareness rooted in multisensory processing that fades in DIED.
  - **Live counter-current**: later critics argue Letheby and Millière work with *overinflated* notions of "self," so that more minimal forms of self/for-me-ness (a witnessing pole) arguably persist even in "totally selfless" 5-MeO-DMT reports. This is exactly the seam the Map's filter reading exploits.
- **Tenet alignment**: The persistence-of-minimal-self critique aligns with the Map's prediction of a continuing for-me-ness under dissolution.

## Major Positions

### Predictive Self-Binding (Letheby & Gerrans)
- **Proponents**: Chris Letheby, Philip Gerrans; building on Metzinger's self-model theory and Friston/Hohwy/Clark predictive processing.
- **Core claim**: The phenomenal self is a hierarchical Bayesian prior structure that binds self-relevant, salient, autobiographical information into a representation of a persisting entity. It is a useful but referent-less *fiction*. Ego dissolution = relaxation of these priors → model becomes opaque → "self-unbinding."
- **Key arguments**: (1) Self-prioritisation / salience-tracking data motivate a self-model; (2) predictive processing supplies the binding mechanism; (3) psychedelic ego dissolution + its neuroimaging signature (DMN/precision relaxation) is the natural experiment; (4) the "more real than real" quality is explained as opacity revealing the model, not revealing anything beyond it.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Direct rival. Self-fictionalism denies a substantial subject (tension with Tenet 4 indexical identity); the account is avowedly physicalist (tension with Tenet 1). Crucially it *helps itself* to the unpaid assumption that a structured Bayesian model is phenomenally felt — the hard problem in predictive-processing register (Tenet 1 / Tenet 5 leverage).

### Naturalistic Epistemic-Benefit Account (Letheby, 2021)
- **Proponents**: Letheby.
- **Core claim**: Psychedelic therapy delivers real epistemic and "spiritual" benefits compatible with naturalism; it heals by phenomenological insight into the self's constructed nature, not by instilling non-naturalistic beliefs. The CDO fails.
- **Key arguments**: mechanism is sense-of-self change, not metaphysical-belief change; metaphysical belief shifts are a *side-effect*, not the active ingredient; "naturalistic spirituality" is coherent.
- **Relation to site tenets**: The Map can accept much of the *therapeutic* story while rejecting the *metaphysical* deflation. The Map's reading reverses the CDO: if filter/dualism is true, the unity/noetic insight may be *veridical* (Sjöstedt-Hughes's move), so the "delusion" framing presupposes the very naturalism in dispute.

### Filter / Transmission Reading (The Map)
- **Proponents**: The Unfinishable Map; ancestry in Huxley, Bergson, James.
- **Core claim**: The same precision-relaxation that Letheby reads as prior-relaxation, the Map reads as *loosening of a constraint on consciousness the brain transmits rather than produces*. A minimal for-me-ness should persist under dissolution; "more real than real" is taken closer to face value.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Aligned (Tenets 1, 3, 5). Honest concession (already in the corpus): on altered-state data alone the filter account does *not* exceed Letheby's — they predict the same signatures. The discriminating work is done by the broader convergence corpus and foundational stance, not by psychedelic neuroimaging.

## Key Debates

### Does any self survive ego dissolution? (the minimal-self / witness question)
- **Sides**: Letheby/Millière (totally selfless states are possible; "self" includes a thick narrative self that genuinely vanishes) vs. critics (overinflated notion of self; a minimal witnessing for-me-ness persists even in 5-MeO-DMT) vs. Deane (structured self collapses but affective consciousness/valuation remains).
- **Core disagreement**: Whether "for-me-ness" is itself a model component that can decohere, or an irreducible feature of any conscious state.
- **Current state**: Ongoing; first-person reports underdetermine it. This is the single most important seam for the Map — the Map predicts persistent for-me-ness; Deane's "affective valence survives" gives an *internal-to-physicalism* ally.

### Is naturalism itself a contestable metaphysics? (the CDO reversal)
- **Sides**: Letheby (naturalism is the responsible default; non-naturalist beliefs are epistemic risks) vs. Sjöstedt-Hughes / Goff / Segall (naturalism is one contentious metaphysics among live rivals; under panpsychism/idealism/dualism the insights may be veridical).
- **Core disagreement**: Whether labelling mystical insight a "comforting delusion" begs the metaphysical question.
- **Current state**: Active; Letheby's 2024 metaphysics work continues to defend the naturalist baseline while granting that metaphysical-belief change is a common (if non-essential) side-effect.

### Does predictive processing explain *phenomenal* selfhood, or only its functional signature?
- **Sides**: Letheby/Gerrans (the self-model account explains phenomenal self-awareness) vs. symposium critics — Hipólito (worries it conflates binding self-relevant info with phenomenal self-representation, raises homunculus/causal-efficacy concerns), Michael (why a *Cartesian* self rather than a Humean bundle? self-prioritisation is graded, not sharp), Millière ("self-binding" equivocates between *self-related* and *self-specific* processing).
- **Core disagreement**: Whether the formalism reaches phenomenality at all (the hard problem) or only behavioural/functional self-prioritisation.
- **Current state**: Unresolved — and it is the Map's strongest point of entry (Tenet 1).
