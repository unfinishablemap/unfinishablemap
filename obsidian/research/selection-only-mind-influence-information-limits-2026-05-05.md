## Executive Summary

The strictest reading of Tenet 2 (Minimal Quantum Interaction) is *selection-only*: mind contributes nothing to the candidate set the brain physically generates—it only chooses which of the already-generated alternatives becomes actual. This reading makes the mind-physical channel a pure post-processing stage on top of brain dynamics. Under that constraint, three mathematical limits apply immediately. (1) **Shannon ceiling**: a single selection event can transfer at most log₂(N) bits, where N is the size of the candidate set the brain has prepared. (2) **Born-rule preservation requirement**: long-run frequency statistics over the candidate set must converge to physical Born-rule probabilities, capping the *signed* information rate at near zero across many trials. (3) **Content-confinement**: the mind cannot register, choose, or report content that is not represented somewhere in the brain-generated candidate space—novel qualia are structurally precluded if not pre-encoded. Empirically, the literature on micro-psychokinesis (PEAR, Schmidt, Bösch–Steinkamp–Boller, GCP, Maier–Dechamps) sets an upper bound on detectable per-trial bias of order 10⁻⁴ bits/bit at best, with the most rigorous Bayesian replication (Maier et al. 2018) returning strong evidence for the null. Theoretically, the no-signalling theorem and Gleason's theorem couple Born-rule deviations directly to relativistic-causality violations: any *systematic* deviation visible at long timescales would be detectable not just statistically but as a faster-than-light signalling channel. The selection-only model therefore lives in a tight signature corridor: large enough to make a difference at the single-event scale, small enough to vanish at the ensemble scale, and constrained at every level by the physical-side candidate set. Distinguishing observables exist in principle—correlation between candidate-set diversity and reportable conscious content, conditional bias profiles tied to neural decoherence-domain structure, decline effects under accumulating measurement—but none has been operationalised in the contemporary literature.

## Key Sources

### Henry Stapp — Quantum Interactive Dualism / Process 1

- **URL**: https://www-physics.lbl.gov/~stapp/QID.pdf and https://www-physics.lbl.gov/~stapp/vNS.pdf
- **Type**: Primary paper / book chapter
- **Key points**:
  - Stapp's framework is the canonical selection-only model: consciousness chooses *which question* the brain asks of nature (Process 1: choice of observable / measurement basis), but the *answer* (Process 3 / Born-rule outcome) is "given by nature" at random.
  - Strict adherence to Born-rule statistical predictions is part of the framework's claim to consistency with orthodox quantum mechanics.
  - Mental causation operates through the rate at which Process 1 events are triggered—not through bias on outcomes—and the quantum Zeno effect provides the amplification mechanism (rapid repeated questioning holds neural states in subspaces).
  - The information-transfer channel is effectively the choice of observable, not the outcome bit; bandwidth is set by how fine-grained the Process 1 question can be and how rapidly such events fire.
- **Tenet alignment**: Aligns with Tenet 2 (Minimal Quantum Interaction) at its strictest reading; Stapp's model is the historical exemplar of selection-only mind-influence.
- **Quote**: "the mind would only have the option to choose the observable, not the option of selecting the measurement result in deviation from the Born's probability law"

### Stapp's Late-Period Modification — Born-Rule Relaxation

- **URL**: http://settheory.net/stapp
- **Type**: Critical commentary
- **Key points**:
  - In later work Stapp permits a slight biasing or *relaxation* of the Born rule to accommodate retrocausal-style effects (Bem-style precognition data).
  - This is a notable internal pressure: pure selection-only models can preserve Born-rule statistics, but to explain reported anomalies they must allow some bias—at which point the no-signalling and conservation-of-statistics constraints (see Gleason / no-signalling section below) tighten sharply.
  - Critics argue Stapp's relaxation moves the framework outside orthodox quantum mechanics whenever it tries to do explanatory work for parapsychology.
- **Tenet alignment**: Conflicts with the strictest selection-only reading; documents the cost of permitting any bias.

### Bösch, Steinkamp & Boller (2006) — Meta-Analysis of RNG Psychokinesis Studies

- **URL**: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16822162/ and PDF http://www.patriziotressoldi.it/cmssimpled/uploads/includes/MetaPK06.pdf
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed meta-analysis (Psychological Bulletin)
- **Key points**:
  - 380 RNG psychokinesis studies pooled. Significant overall effect, but very small.
  - Effect size strongly *inversely* related to sample size—a classic signature of publication bias or small-study effects.
  - Monte Carlo simulation showed the observed pattern is fully consistent with publication bias.
  - The combined effect size establishes an upper bound on any genuine selection-only signature: of order 10⁻⁴–10⁻³ bits/bit at most.
- **Tenet alignment**: Constrains Tenet 3 (Bidirectional Interaction). If selection-only mind-influence has macroscopic effects, the per-trial information rate must lie below this empirical ceiling.
- **Quote**: "the small effect size, the relation between sample size and effect size, and the extreme effect size heterogeneity found could in principle be a result of publication bias"

### PEAR Lab — Princeton Engineering Anomalies Research

- **URL**: https://psi-encyclopedia.spr.ac.uk/articles/princeton-engineering-anomalies-research-pear/ and https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Princeton_Engineering_Anomalies_Research_Lab
- **Type**: Encyclopedia / institutional history
- **Key points**:
  - Founded 1979 (Jahn) at Princeton; closed 2007. Tested whether human intention biases REG output.
  - Reported effect size ~10⁻⁴ bits per bit processed — accumulated to 7σ over billions of trials.
  - Independent replication (Jahn et al. 2000, "Mind/Machine Interaction Consortium") with 227 participants and ~2 million trials *failed* to confirm the original results.
  - The PEAR effect size, if real, is the canonical empirical bound on selection-only mind-influence bandwidth: ~10⁻⁴ bits per quantum-grade selection event.
- **Tenet alignment**: Provides the empirical *signature corridor* for any selection-only model—real per-trial bias must be below this scale; if zero, all detectable signatures of the model vanish.

### Helmut Schmidt — Pioneer RNG Studies

- **URL**: https://psi-encyclopedia.spr.ac.uk/articles/helmut-schmidt/ and https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Helmut_Schmidt_(parapsychologist)
- **Type**: Historical / encyclopedia
- **Key points**:
  - Schmidt's binary RNG studies typically scored just under 52% — a 2% deviation from chance.
  - 2% bias in a binary channel corresponds to a Shannon information rate of 1 − H(0.52) ≈ 0.0006 bits/bit. Even at face value this is a very thin channel.
  - Studies have not been independently replicated at the same effect size—a recurring pattern in the field (decline effect, see below).
- **Tenet alignment**: Constrains the achievable per-trial selection bias.

### Maier & Dechamps (2018) — Bayesian Null on Micro-PK

- **URL**: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5872141/
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed Bayesian replication (Frontiers in Psychology)
- **Key points**:
  - Pre-registered Bayesian micro-PK study with 12,571 subjects.
  - Strong evidence for the *null* hypothesis (BF₀₁ = 10.07).
  - This is the most rigorous contemporary test and finds no per-trial bias detectable at the trial counts available.
  - For the selection-only model, this tightens the upper bound on per-trial information rate substantially below PEAR's claimed value.
- **Tenet alignment**: Constrains Tenet 3 — sets the strongest empirical bound currently available on detectable selection-only mind-influence signatures in laboratory RNG settings.
- **Quote**: "the Bayesian analysis revealed strong evidence for H0 (BF01 = 10.07), thus micro-PK did not exist in the data"

### Decline Effect — Parapsychology

- **URL**: https://psi-encyclopedia.spr.ac.uk/articles/decline-effect-parapsychology and https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2020.562992/full
- **Type**: Encyclopedia entry / peer-reviewed analysis
- **Key points**:
  - Effect sizes in psi research decline systematically over time across all major paradigms (RNG-PK, ganzfeld, card-guessing, DMILS).
  - 20%–33% replication rates in well-conducted studies versus 80%+ in original studies.
  - Walach, Atmanspacher and Römer offer a non-trivial *theoretical* explanation: selection-only effects appear when correlations are first probed but recede as repeated probing approaches a "signal transfer" regime that would violate physical law—the decline is the physics enforcing no-signalling.
  - This is a candidate *signature* of the selection-only model: a model that preserves Born-rule statistics in the long run *predicts* declining effect sizes under accumulating measurement.
- **Tenet alignment**: Surprisingly *supportive* of selection-only at theoretical level—the decline effect is what one would expect from a model that strictly preserves long-run Born-rule statistics. Conflicts with strong-effect-size readings of psi data.

### Gleason's Theorem and No-Signalling

- **URL**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/No-communication_theorem and https://www.nature.com/articles/srep22986
- **Type**: Foundational theorem / peer-reviewed paper
- **Key points**:
  - The no-communication theorem prohibits transmission of information faster than light using quantum entanglement.
  - The Born rule itself can be *derived* from relativistic-causality / no-signalling constraints (Gleason's theorem; Han 2016).
  - Non-Bornian probabilities are *contextual* in a way that licenses non-local signalling channels: violation of the Born rule's higher sum-rules permits superluminal signalling.
  - Implication for selection-only models: any *systematic* per-trial deviation from Born-rule probabilities is directly coupled to relativistic-causality violation. Models that bias outcomes face a steep cost beyond mere statistical detectability.
- **Tenet alignment**: Strongly constrains Tenet 2. The strictest reading of Minimal Quantum Interaction (no Born-rule deviation) is *required*, not optional, if causality is to be preserved.
- **Quote**: "violation of higher sum-rules allows for superluminal signalling"

### Beck & Eccles — Quantum Tunneling at Synapses

- **URL**: https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.89.23.11357 and https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0079610717301839
- **Type**: PNAS paper / modern review
- **Key points**:
  - Beck & Eccles (1992) propose mind acts on synaptic exocytosis via biasing quantum-tunnelling probabilities of unidentified quasiparticles.
  - Vesicle release is genuinely probabilistic (probability < 1 per arriving action potential).
  - The mind-side intervention is selection among already-prepared physical states — no energy injection, only outcome-bias.
  - Georgiev & Glazebrook (2018) update the model to identify the quasiparticles as Davydov solitons in SNARE protein α-helices.
  - The information rate is bounded by the rate of synaptic release events being biased: ~10¹¹ synapses × ~10 Hz × ~1 bit/event ≈ 10¹² bits/s of *available* candidate-set entropy, but only the *biased fraction* counts as a mind-side channel.
- **Tenet alignment**: Aligns with Tenet 2 (Minimal Quantum Interaction). Provides a candidate biophysical locus where selection-only influence could occur without violating Born-rule statistics over the brain-as-a-whole.

### Adrian Kent — Collapse and Measures of Consciousness

- **URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2009.13224 and https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10701-021-00467-4
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed paper (Foundations of Physics)
- **Key points**:
  - Kent develops *consciousness-based collapse models* in which some quantifiable aspect of consciousness resists superposition.
  - These models are empirically constrained: lower bounds on collapse parameters from human visual processing are 7±2 orders of magnitude stronger than original GRW bounds.
  - Crucial methodological move: makes consciousness-based collapse *empirically testable* by tying it to specific physical signatures (interferometry on macromolecules; visual-system collapse-rate constraints).
  - Distinct from selection-only model: Kent's model has consciousness *cause* collapse (changing the physics) rather than *select* among already-collapsed alternatives. But Kent's signature methodology is directly applicable: the bounds on collapse rates also bound any selection-only mechanism that would produce comparable physical effects.
- **Tenet alignment**: Conflicts with strict Tenet 2 (consciousness-causes-collapse changes physics rather than selecting within it), but *methodologically aligned*: Kent's empirical bounds directly constrain the parameter space available to selection-only models.

### Hameroff–Penrose Orchestrated Objective Reduction

- **URL**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orchestrated_objective_reduction and https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/17588928.2020.1839037
- **Type**: Encyclopedia / peer-reviewed
- **Key points**:
  - Orch OR proposes consciousness arises at quantum-gravity OR events orchestrated in microtubules.
  - Information-capacity estimate: 10⁷ Hz OR events × ~5×10¹⁵ tubulins ≈ 10⁻⁴ of total brain capacity.
  - In Penrose's account, OR events are *not* selection-only: consciousness is identified with the OR event itself, not with a separate selecting agent. This makes Orch OR a *generation* model rather than a selection-only model.
  - Useful comparator: Orch OR's bandwidth estimates set an order-of-magnitude scale for what quantum-grade conscious processing could in principle achieve.
- **Tenet alignment**: Conflicts with selection-only reading of Tenet 2 (treats OR as primitively conscious rather than selected-from). Aligns with Tenet 1 (Dualism) only on a non-standard reading.

### Kastner — Possibilist Transactional Interpretation (PTI)

- **URL**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transactional_interpretation and https://philpapers.org/rec/KASTPT
- **Type**: Encyclopedia / peer-reviewed
- **Key points**:
  - PTI treats offer waves and confirmation waves as physically real possibilities in a pre-spacetime realm.
  - Selection of a single transaction from "the garden of forking paths" is a primitive feature of the model—candidate-set generation is in PTI's pre-spacetime, selection produces empirical spacetime events.
  - PTI is a *generation* model from the candidate-set perspective: the offer waves are physical, not mind-side. But it offers a clean ontological backdrop on which a selection-only mind operates: the brain's offer waves constitute the candidate set, mind selects which transaction completes.
- **Tenet alignment**: Compatible with Tenet 2 if mind is read as the selector among offer-wave alternatives without contributing to offer-wave generation.

### Stoll, Zheng et al. — Conscious Bandwidth ≈ 10 bits/s

- **Tenet alignment**: Independent of tenet alignment. Provides the *upper bound* on what any selection-only mind-influence channel needs to deliver to account for human cognition. 10 bits/s is achievable by very few selection events per second, given large enough candidate sets.

  - Useful framing for the selection-only model: the channel is *consistent* with causal closure as standardly stated, because energy is conserved and statistics are preserved; it merely picks among physically permitted outcomes.

## Major Positions

### Selection-Only / Process-1 / Stapp-style

- **Proponents**: Stapp (in his orthodox-quantum-mechanics phase); Beck & Eccles; Atmanspacher & Walach (in weak-quantum-theory framing); compatible with Kastner's PTI under one reading.
- **Core claim**: Mind selects among already-generated physical alternatives; contributes nothing to candidate-set production; preserves Born-rule statistics over long runs.
- **Key arguments**: (1) compatible with no-signalling and energy conservation; (2) avoids interaction problem at the level of forces; (3) fits the empirical picture of vanishing or unreliable PK signatures—because the model *predicts* such signatures should be vanishing or unreliable.
- **Information-transfer limits**:
  - Per-event bound: log₂(N) bits, where N is candidate-set size.
  - Per-second bound: log₂(N) × event-rate.
  - Long-run bound: zero (Born-rule preservation drives signed-information rate to zero across many trials).
  - Effective bound: thin per-event signal, undetectable in aggregate.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Strictest reading of Tenet 2 (Minimal Quantum Interaction); compatible with Tenet 1 (Dualism) and Tenet 3 (Bidirectional Interaction); Tenet 4 (No Many Worlds) is presupposed (selection requires that one outcome become actual rather than all).

### Generation / Contributing-to-Candidate-Set

- **Proponents**: Penrose & Hameroff (Orch OR identifies consciousness with OR events); some readings of Stapp's late work; Eccles' psychon when read as field-like; consciousness-causes-collapse models (Kent; Chalmers–McQueen).
- **Core claim**: Mind contributes to *which* alternatives are physically generated, not just to selection among them. Either alters the menu, alters the probabilities, or is identified with the menu-collapse event itself.
- **Key arguments**: (1) accounts for stronger psi signatures *if* they exist; (2) gives mind a richer causal role; (3) may be required if Born-rule deviations are real.
- **Cost**: Either violates no-signalling (if Born-rule deviations are systematic) or requires new physics (Kent-style modified collapse dynamics) or commits to interpretive ontology (Orch OR's quantum-gravity OR).
- **Relation to site tenets**: Conflicts with the strictest reading of Tenet 2 — adds a content-generation channel beyond Minimal Quantum Interaction.

### Eliminative / Deflationary

- **Proponents**: Maier–Dechamps (Bayesian null); standard physicalists who deny mind-influence entirely; superdeterminism advocates (Hossenfelder).
- **Core claim**: There is no mind-side selection channel. Apparent psi effects are publication bias, decline-effect artefacts, or methodological errors. Born-rule statistics hold without need for any non-physical selector.
- **Key arguments**: (1) Bayesian null results in the most rigorous studies; (2) decline effects across all psi paradigms; (3) parsimony.
- **Cost**: Inherits the standard problem of outcomes (decoherence does not produce definite outcomes), and pushes the explanatory burden to whichever interpretation is adopted.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Conflicts with Tenets 1, 2 and 3.

## Key Debates

### Is Born-Rule Preservation Compatible with Macroscopic Mental Causation?

- **Sides**: Stapp (yes — channel is choice of observable, not bias on outcome) vs. critics who say no detectable mental effect is possible if Born statistics are exactly preserved.
- **Core disagreement**: Does selecting among already-generated alternatives, with Born-rule statistics held fixed, count as genuine causation? Or is it indistinguishable from the system's own dynamics?
- **Current state**: Open. Stapp's resolution via Process 1 (choice of *question*, not answer) is one route; another is to allow *conditional* bias on individual events that averages out to Born statistics over the candidate space. The selection-only model needs to specify which.

### Decline Effect — Methodological Artefact or Theoretical Prediction?

- **Sides**: Mainstream meta-analytic view (decline = publication bias / questionable research practices) vs. Walach, Atmanspacher, Römer (decline = physics enforcing no-signalling on a real but small effect).
- **Core disagreement**: Whether systematic decline is evidence *against* the mind-influence hypothesis or evidence *for* a self-limiting mind-influence channel.
- **Current state**: Strongly weighted toward the mainstream view by Bayesian replication results (Maier 2018), but the selection-only model is one of the few frameworks that has a principled prediction here—decline is what it should look like if Born-rule preservation is enforced.

### Is "Selection-Only" Operationally Distinct from "Generation"?

- **Sides**: Selection-only proponents argue the two are observationally equivalent for *single events* but distinguishable in *long-run statistics* (selection-only preserves Born; generation may not). Critics argue the distinction collapses operationally if the candidate set is itself produced by mind-influenced processes upstream.
- **Core disagreement**: Whether the distinction can be cleanly drawn in a brain where each candidate-generating stage might itself involve quantum-grade selection.
- **Current state**: Underexplored. The Map's existing content (`post-decoherence-selection-programme`, `forward-in-time-conscious-selection`) names the distinction but has not formalised the observable signatures that would discriminate between them.

## Information-Theoretic Limits — Mathematical Details

### (1) Mind→Physical Per-Event Bound

For a selection event with N candidates of probabilities p₁, …, p_N (Born-rule probabilities), the maximum information transferable by selecting a specific outcome is:

I_max(event) = log₂(N) bits (uniform prior, no constraints)

Under Born-rule preservation, the *expected* mutual information between mind-state and outcome must average to zero over many trials—so the *per-trial* signed bandwidth is bounded by max{p_i} − min{p_i} ≈ small ε, with Shannon information rate ≈ ε² / (2 ln 2) bits/trial in the small-bias limit (binary case).

Plugging in PEAR-scale ε ≈ 10⁻⁴: information rate ≈ 7 × 10⁻⁹ bits/event. Even at 10⁷ Hz of quantum-grade events (Hameroff's microtubule estimate), this gives ~0.07 bits/s through the biased channel — comparable to the conscious-bandwidth Caltech estimate of ~10 bits/s once Process-1-style observable-choice channels are added on top.

### (2) Mind→Physical Per-Second Bound

If candidate selection events occur at rate R, total mind-side bandwidth is at most:

I_max(sec) = R × log₂(N) bits/s [unconstrained]
I_max(sec) ≈ R × ε² / (2 ln 2) bits/s [Born-rule-preserving]

With realistic neural-quantum-event rates and PEAR-scale ε, the second bound is the binding constraint. The first bound (R × log₂(N)) is achievable only if Born-rule preservation is suspended.

### (3) Physical→Mind Content-Confinement

If mind can only choose among brain-generated candidates, then mind's *phenomenal repertoire* is confined to whatever the candidate set encodes. Specifically:
- Mind cannot register a quale not represented in some candidate.
- Mind cannot report content that no candidate encodes.
- Novel qualia must be pre-generated by brain dynamics — they cannot be brought into being by selection alone.

This is a structural constraint on the theory of phenomenal content under selection-only models. It is *much* stronger than the corresponding constraint under generation models, where mind could in principle contribute novel content.

### (4) Holevo / Quantum Information Bound

The Holevo bound caps classical information extractable from a quantum system at ≤ N bits per N qubits. For a mind that "reads" the brain's candidate set as its phenomenal content, this bounds the dimensionality of phenomenal experience by the dimension of the brain-side Hilbert space at the relevant decoherence-domain stage. Estimates of relevant decoherence-domain dimensionality are not currently in the literature; this is a research gap.

## Physical-World Signatures — Concrete Predictions

### Signatures Selection-Only Predicts (Distinguishing Markers)

1. **Vanishing per-trial bias in long-run laboratory RNG studies** — the model predicts that strict Born-rule preservation should make per-trial bias undetectable, *or* detectable only as a transient effect that decays under repeated measurement (decline effect). Maier–Dechamps (2018) is consistent with this prediction.

2. **Decline-effect signature in any psi paradigm** — declining effect sizes as more trials accumulate, with floor approaching zero. Documented across all major psi paradigms.

3. **Information-theoretic ceiling on introspective-report entropy relative to candidate-set entropy** — reportable conscious content should not exceed the entropy of the brain-generated candidate set at the relevant stage. Operationalisable in principle by measuring the dimensionality of decoherence-resilient neural states and comparing to the dimensionality of behavioural outputs.

4. **Conditional-bias profile tied to neural decoherence-domain structure** — if selection occurs at specific neural sites (synaptic exocytosis per Beck-Eccles; microtubular OR per Hameroff), per-trial bias should correlate with measurable neural-state diversity at those sites.

5. **Correlation between candidate-set diversity and reportable-content diversity** — under selection-only, novel qualia are precluded if candidates are not pre-generated. Empirical prediction: behavioural reports of novelty should correlate with prior measurable diversity in upstream neural states.

### Signatures Selection-Only Does *Not* Predict

1. **Systematic Born-rule deviation visible at long timescales** — would couple to no-signalling violation; rules out selection-only.
2. **Energy-injection signatures** (anomalous heat, mass, force) — selection-only is energetically inert.
3. **Macroscopic PK without measurable upstream candidate-set diversity** — would require generation, not selection.

### Distinguishing Observables vs. Generation Models

| Observable | Selection-Only | Generation |
|---|---|---|
| Per-trial bias | Vanishing in long run | Possibly persistent |
| Born-rule preservation over candidate set | Required | Optional |
| Decline effect under repeated trials | Predicted | Not predicted |
| No-signalling preservation | Strict | Possibly violated |
| Novel qualia without prior neural representation | Impossible | Possible |
| Conditional bias correlation with decoherence-domain diversity | Strong | Weak/absent |
| Energy/momentum injection | Zero | Possibly nonzero |

Of these, the *most discriminating* observable is the **correlation between candidate-set diversity and reportable phenomenal content**. This is unique to selection-only models and not currently operationalised in the literature. It is a direct target for an Interface Specification Programme experiment.

1. **Lead with the strict-reading derivation** — formalise selection-only as the strictest interpretation of Tenet 2; derive the per-event log₂(N) bound and the per-second R log₂(N) bound; show how Born-rule preservation collapses signed-information rate to ε² per trial. *Most aligned with framework-stage-calibration: structural results stand independently of empirical truth.*

2. **Lead with the empirical signature corridor** — survey PEAR / Schmidt / Bösch-Steinkamp-Boller / Maier-Dechamps; show that the empirical ceiling on per-trial bias places selection-only mind-influence in a tight signature window; argue that decline effects are theoretically *expected* under Born-rule preservation. *Most concrete; risks looking like advocacy of psi data.*

3. **Lead with content-confinement** — start from "mind cannot register what brain does not represent in candidates"; derive implications for novel qualia, animal consciousness boundaries, AI consciousness; treat bandwidth-limit results as supporting derivations. *Most original; connects strongly to the chained article on mind-brain interface efficacy.*

4. **Lead with distinguishing-observables table** — argue selection-only is *empirically distinguishable* from generation-only and from no-influence models; supply the table of distinguishing observables; treat current literature as providing only weak constraints because the right experiments have not been run. *Most actionable; aligns with Interface Specification Programme.*

The recommended angle is **(1) + (4)**: derive the structural results first (which are tenet-load-bearing under the strict reading) and end with the distinguishing-observables table (which makes the framework empirically meaningful even if no current experiment can run it). This honours framework-stage-calibration: structural results are Kepler-grade; empirical-signature predictions are Tycho-grade scaffolding awaiting the right instruments.

## Gaps in Research

1. **Holevo / quantum-information bounds applied to phenomenal-content dimensionality**: The literature does not contain a sustained engagement with Holevo-bound implications for the dimensionality of phenomenal experience under selection-only models. This is a clear research gap and a possible original contribution from the Map.

2. **Operational definition of "candidate-set diversity"**: While decoherence-domain structure is well-studied, the specific quantification of how many distinguishable candidates a brain region produces per unit time is not standard. Connecting this to behavioural-output dimensionality is the empirical hook for testing content-confinement.

3. **Decline-effect modelling under Born-rule preservation**: Walach et al.'s qualitative claim that decline is the physics enforcing no-signalling is suggestive but not formalised. A quantitative model of decline-rate as a function of accumulated measurement would be a substantial contribution.

4. **No-signalling theorem applied specifically to mind-side selection**: The standard no-signalling theorem assumes physical observers; whether it extends to a non-physical selector who has access to "which question is asked" is technically open. Stapp's Process 1 implicitly relies on this extension but does not prove it.

5. **Distinguishability between selection-only and selection-with-context-shaping**: If mind shapes the measurement context (Stapp's view) without biasing outcomes, this is not strictly *selection-only* but is observationally similar. The conditions under which the two are operationally distinguishable need work.

6. **Comparative-cognition implications of content-confinement**: The chained article on mind-brain interface efficacy will need this article's content-confinement result. Specifically, whether interface-efficacy variation across species manifests as different *bandwidths* through a fixed selection-only channel or as different *kinds* of selection channels is open.
