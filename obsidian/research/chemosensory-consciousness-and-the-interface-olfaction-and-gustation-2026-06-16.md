## Executive Summary

The chemical senses — olfaction and gustation, integrated in flavor — are the most vision-unlike of the modalities, and contemporary (2020s) philosophy of smell (Barwich) and odor-coding neuroscience make them an unusually sharp case for the Map's interface thesis. Three features stand out: (1) **the percept is not read off the stimulus** — mirror-image molecules (carvone) smell categorically different while structurally unrelated molecules can smell identical, because perceived odor is a brain-side combinatorial construction, not a molecular property; (2) **odor quality space is non-dimensional / non-Euclidean** (recent work models it as hyperbolic), so there is no clean ordering to "read off," unlike the color wheel or pitch height; and (3) **olfaction has a privileged, thalamus-bypassing route to emotion and autobiographical memory** (the Proust phenomenon, "LOVER": Limbic, Old, Vivid, Emotional, Rare), making smell qualia constitutively affect-laden. Flavor adds a binding case — taste, retronasal smell, and somatosensation fuse into a unified percept (a "Barolo") that lives in no single channel. As with the auditory article, none of this *settles* dualism (the physicalist has combinatorial-coding and multisensory-integration accounts of each); what it does is press the explanatory gap in a register vision cannot, and show the interface framework extends cleanly to the chemical senses.

## Key Sources

### Barwich, *Smellosophy: What the Nose Tells the Mind* (2020) and "From Molecules to Perception" (2022)
- **URLs**: https://www.hup.harvard.edu/books/9780674278721 ; https://compass.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/phc3.12883
- **Type**: Book (Harvard UP) + survey article (Philosophy Compass)
- **Key points**:
  - Central thesis: any account of *what the object of odor perception is* must center the **sensory system that processes the stimulus, not the chemical stimulus itself**. The percept is a brain achievement, not a molecular readout.
  - Direct call to stop importing the **vision paradigm**: scents are "fickle and boundless" compared with visual images and "do not line up with well-defined neural regions." (Same move the Map's auditory article makes against the visual snapshot.)
  - The brain acts "not only like a map but also as a measuring device" for odors — a processual, comparative account rather than a representational-correspondence one.
  - Philosophy of smell "probes the limits of theories built on the dominant sense of vision."
- **Tenet alignment**: Strongly usable for **Dualism** / explanatory gap (the percept outruns the stimulus); Barwich herself is **not a dualist** — her view is naturalist/processual — so cite as the empirical-philosophical baseline the Map then reads through the interface lens, exactly as the auditory article does with Bregman and O'Callaghan.
- **Quote (search abstract, paraphrase-safe)**: any account of the object of odor "must center its analysis on the sensory system that processes the chemical stimulus, not the chemical stimulus itself."

### "Smell" — Open Encyclopedia of Cognitive Science (MIT)
- **URL**: https://oecs.mit.edu/pub/1ba2q8ay/release/1 (HTTP 403 on fetch; metadata + search abstract only)
- **Type**: Encyclopedia entry
- **Key points**:
  - The live debate is whether smell perceives **odor objects** at all: some hold odor molecules carry no spatial information and olfaction is object-independent; others hold smells are complex perceptual objects with perceptible properties (concentration, intensity, valence).
  - The **same volatile stimulus elicits markedly different experiences** across people and occasions (pleasantness, intensity, quality vary) — the stimulus-percept gap as a first-class datum.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral framing of the objecthood debate; the variability point supports Dualism/explanatory-gap.

### Frontiers in Psychology — "A Critique of Olfactory Objects" (2019)
- **URL**: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2019.01337/full
- **Type**: Paper
- **Key points**: Skepticism about whether smells represent discrete, **publicly shareable** objects the way visual percepts do, or are closer to **affective/interoceptive states** carrying information about bodily state. This is the philosophical hinge that links olfaction to `evaluative-phenomenal-character`.
- **Tenet alignment**: Supports the Map's reading that olfactory qualia are constitutively valenced (affect is not added downstream).

### Combinatorial Receptor Codes for Odors — Malnic, Hirono, Sato & Buck (1999), *Cell*
- **URL**: https://www.cell.com/fulltext/S0092-8674(00)80581-4
- **Type**: Foundational neuroscience paper (Buck shared the 2004 Nobel for olfactory receptor work)
- **Key points**: ~400 human olfactory receptors encode thousands of odors **combinatorially** — one receptor recognizes many odorants, one odorant activates a group of receptors. A **slight molecular change, or a concentration change, can shift the code and thereby the perceived quality**. Perceived odor is the *pattern of activation interpreted by the brain*, not the molecule.
- **Tenet alignment**: The empirical core of the stimulus-percept gap (Dualism / explanatory gap). The combinatorial code is the chemical-sense analog of audition's *missing fundamental*: the perceived quality corresponds to no single property in the stimulus.

### Hyperbolic geometry / structure of olfactory space (2018–2023)
- **URLs**: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6114987/ (hyperbolic geometry of olfactory space) ; https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3704697/ (structure of human olfactory space) ; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10726579/ (2023, high-precision mapping of odor coding in the human brain)
- **Type**: Neuroscience / psychophysics
- **Key points**:
  - Natural odors and human odor descriptions are well represented in **three-dimensional hyperbolic** (non-Euclidean) space.
  - Olfactory space is **high-dimensional, not smoothly occupied** — odor dimensions apply categorically, clustered and discrete rather than continuous.
  - (2023) Encoded perceptual-space dimensionality *increases* from piriform cortex to orbitofrontal cortex.
- **Tenet alignment**: This is the **"non-dimensional quality space"** the context flags. It contrasts cleanly with color (a smooth ~3-D Euclidean-ish space, the hue circle) and pitch (a near-linear ordering). Smell qualities resist the dimensional ordering that lets the physicalist say "the quality just *is* its place in stimulus space." Strong Dualism/explanatory-gap material.

### Proust phenomenon / odor-evoked autobiographical memory
- **URLs**: https://www.bps.org.uk/psychologist/smell-and-memory-proust-phenomenon ; https://link.springer.com/article/10.3758/s13423-018-1545-3 (review) ; https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0028393203002161 (amygdala correlation)
- **Type**: Psychology review + neuroimaging
- **Key points**:
  - Odor-evoked autobiographical memories are **more emotional and more vivid** than those cued by other modalities — summarized as **LOVER**: Limbic, Old, Vivid, Emotional, Rare.
  - Mechanism: olfaction is the **only sense that bypasses the thalamic relay**, with primary/direct access to the amygdala (emotion), hippocampus (long-term memory), and orbitofrontal cortex (evaluation).
  - Subjective emotional potency of odor memory correlates with amygdala activation at recall.
- **Tenet alignment**: Distinctive Dualism/`evaluative-phenomenal-character` material — chemical-sense qualia come pre-bound to affect and memory, which neither vision nor audition matches. Note honestly: the limbic-bypass anatomy is *itself a physicalist mechanism*; the Map's point is the felt character, not the wiring.

### Flavor as multimodal perception (philosophy of taste)
- **URLs**: https://philarchive.org/archive/SKRMDA (Skrzypulec, "Multimodal Diversity and Unity of Flavour Perception") ; https://philarchive.org/archive/SKRCIF-2 ("Constitutivity in Flavour Perception") ; https://www.jneurosci.org/content/44/20/e0071242024 (2024, odor-taste mixtures in gustatory cortex)
- **Type**: Philosophy of perception + neuroscience
- **Key points**:
  - "Taste" colloquially is really **flavor**: the multisensory integration of gustation, **retronasal olfaction**, and oral somatosensation (touch, temperature, chemesthesis/pungency).
  - Open philosophical question: which channels are **constitutive** of flavor vs merely causal contributors. The unified flavor percept (a "Barolo") corresponds to no single channel.
  - (2024) Gustatory cortex represents odor-taste mixtures as **distinct** integrated objects, not the sum of parts.
- **Tenet alignment**: A **binding case** without spatial scaffolding — directly parallels the auditory article's use of auditory scene analysis. Flavor binding generalizes `the-binding-problem` / `capability-division-in-vision` beyond vision and hearing.

### Taste coding debates: tongue-map myth, labeled-line vs across-fiber, gustotopic map
- **URLs**: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC8956797/ (tongue map and spatial modulation, 2022) ; https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0960982220318236 ("Taste: A Scattered Affair", 2020)
- **Type**: Review / neuroscience
- **Key points**:
  - The **tongue map is a myth** (mistranslation of Hänig 1901 by Boring 1942); all five basic tastes are detectable across the tongue, with only small regional sensitivity differences.
  - Five basic tastes (sweet, sour, salty, bitter, **umami** — Ikeda); a possible sixth (fat) debated.
  - Coding debate: **labeled-line** (receptor cells tuned to one quality) vs **across-fiber / population** coding (most central taste-responsive neurons are broadly tuned). A "gustotopic map" in cortex has been reported but remains contested. Taste is a "scattered affair."
- **Tenet alignment**: The coding debate shows even the "simplest" chemical sense resists a clean stimulus→quality mapping; useful for tempering the modality (taste is *more* tractable than smell, so the article should be honest that gustation's gap is narrower than olfaction's).

## Major Positions

### Eliminative / deflationary about olfactory objects
- **Proponents**: associated with "no odor objects" readings (see Frontiers critique); some treat smell as sensational/affective rather than world-directed.
- **Core claim**: Smell may not represent discrete, publicly shareable external objects at all; it is closer to an affective/interoceptive signal.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Cuts both ways. If smell is *not* object-directed, that *strengthens* the Map's claim that the perceived quality is constituted on the experiencing side (interface) rather than read off an external property — but the Map should resist the deflationary slide to "smell is merely sensation," since the Map holds olfactory experience has genuine determinate qualitative character.

### Naturalist / processual realism about smell (Barwich)
- **Proponents**: Ann-Sophie Barwich.
- **Core claim**: Odor perception is real and world-involving, but the object of perception must be defined through the **processing system**, not the molecule; the brain is a "measuring device." Stop importing vision.
- **Relation to site tenets**: The empirical-philosophical baseline. Barwich is a **physicalist/naturalist**, not a dualist — her stimulus-percept gap is fully brain-side. The Map borrows her *data and her anti-vision-paradigm argument*, then reads the residual felt character through the interface, exactly as the auditory article borrows Bregman/O'Callaghan. **Do not overclaim Barwich as supporting dualism.**

### Multisensory / constitutivity view of flavor
- **Proponents**: Skrzypulec and the flavor-perception literature.
- **Core claim**: Flavor is irreducibly multimodal; the unity of the flavor percept is a genuine perceptual achievement, and which channels are constitutive is an open question.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Provides the binding case. The unified flavor object that lives in no single channel is the chemical-sense parallel to auditory scene analysis — phenomenal unity not parasitic on spatial co-location.

### Physicalist coding accounts (combinatorial code, population coding)
- **Proponents**: Buck/Axel lineage (Malnic et al. 1999); across-fiber taste-coding theorists.
- **Core claim**: Perceived quality is the brain's interpretation of a combinatorial/population activation pattern; there is no quality-in-the-molecule, but no dualism is needed — it is computation all the way.
- **Relation to site tenets**: The live, well-supported rival the article must take seriously. The Map's honest line: the combinatorial code *explains the discrimination* but not *the felt character* of the smell — the explanatory gap, not a mechanism gap.

## Key Debates

### Does olfaction have objects?
- **Sides**: odor-object realists vs "no objects / affective state" skeptics; Barwich's processual middle (objects defined via the system).
- **Core disagreement**: whether smell is world-directed and what its objects are (the source? the molecule? the perceived quality?).
- **Current state**: Active and unresolved (2019–2024).

### Is the stimulus-percept gap evidence for anything beyond computation?
- **Sides**: physicalist combinatorial-coding accounts vs gap-pressing readings (the Map's interface reading).
- **Core disagreement**: whether "the molecule doesn't fix the quality" is just *interesting neural coding* or a pressure point on physicalism.
- **Current state**: The coding facts are settled (carvone, combinatorial code); their philosophical weight is contested — the same dialectic as the missing fundamental in audition.

### Labeled-line vs across-fiber coding for taste
- **Sides**: labeled-line (receptor-level specificity) vs population/across-fiber coding; gustotopic-map proponents vs "scattered affair" skeptics.
- **Core disagreement**: how taste qualities are represented centrally.
- **Current state**: Ongoing; cortical gustotopy reported but contested.
