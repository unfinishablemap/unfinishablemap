## Executive Summary

2025 saw several landmark developments in experimental consciousness science. The most significant was the publication in Nature of the COGITATE adversarial collaboration, which tested Integrated Information Theory (IIT) and Global Neuronal Workspace Theory (GNWT) head-to-head with 256 participants and found that both theories' key predictions were substantially challenged. Other notable developments include the first external detection of brain biophotons, new evidence that minimal corpus callosum connections suffice for unified consciousness, a quantum zero-point field model of consciousness, the proposal of "biological computationalism" as a third path beyond physicalism and classical computationalism, and advances in detecting covert consciousness in unresponsive patients.

## Key Sources

### Adversarial Testing of GNWT and IIT (Nature, 2025)
- **URL**: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-025-08888-1
- **Type**: Paper (peer-reviewed, Nature)
- **Key points**:
  - 256 participants viewed suprathreshold stimuli while neural activity was measured via fMRI, MEG, and intracranial EEG
  - Information about conscious content found in visual, ventrotemporal, and inferior frontal cortex
  - Sustained responses in occipital and lateral temporal cortex reflected stimulus duration
  - Content-specific synchronization between frontal and early visual areas
  - Both IIT and GNWT had key predictions challenged
- **Tenet alignment**: Aligns with Dualism and Occam's Razor Has Limits (neither purely physical theory adequately explains consciousness); the failure of both dominant physicalist-adjacent theories to make correct predictions supports the view that consciousness resists purely physical explanation
- **Quote**: "No single experiment would decisively refute either theory, as the theories are too different in their assumptions and explanatory goals"

### Brain Biophoton Detection (iScience, 2025)
- **URL**: https://www.cell.com/iscience/fulltext/S2589-0042(25)00279-2
- **Type**: Paper (peer-reviewed)
- **Key points**:
  - First detection of biophotons emitted by the human brain from outside the skull
  - 20 volunteers in dark room with EEG cap and photomultiplier tubes
  - Emissions changed when participants switched cognitive tasks
  - Researchers confirmed: "photons are coming out of the head—full stop"
  - New technique dubbed "photoencephalography"
- **Tenet alignment**: Potentially aligns with Minimal Quantum Interaction (biophotons are quantum phenomena; if they play a functional role in consciousness, this supports quantum-level processes in the brain); Neutral on most tenets but opens new empirical windows
- **Quote**: "In the study of mind and consciousness, biophotons could serve as subtle indicators, or even functional components, of the neural mechanisms underlying subjective experience"

### Split-Brain: Minimal Fibers Unify Consciousness (PNAS, 2025)
- **URL**: https://news.ucsb.edu/2025/022246/new-findings-split-brain-science-even-minimal-fiber-connections-can-unify-consciousness
- **Type**: Paper / press release (UCSB)
- **Key points**:
  - Patients with incomplete callosotomies functioned as well as neurotypical subjects
  - Only a small bundle of the 250 million corpus callosum axons needed for full synchrony
  - The critical section is in the posterior brain
  - First fMRI study of adult callosotomy patients' network activity
- **Tenet alignment**: Aligns with Dualism (the finding that minimal physical connections suffice for unified consciousness is difficult to explain in purely physicalist terms—why would a few fibers be sufficient if consciousness is just a product of distributed neural computation?); Potentially supports Bidirectional Interaction (consciousness may integrate information through mechanisms beyond simple neural connectivity)
- **Quote**: "You don't need the whole corpus callosum; you just need that critical section in the back of the brain to be connected in order to have that unified consciousness experience"

### Zero-Point Field and Consciousness (Frontiers in Human Neuroscience, Dec 2025)
- **URL**: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/human-neuroscience/articles/10.3389/fnhum.2025.1676585/full
- **Type**: Paper (peer-reviewed)
- **Key points**:
  - Proposes consciousness arises from the brain's resonant coupling with the quantum electromagnetic zero-point field (ZPF)
  - Cortical microcolumns (~100 neurons) couple directly to ZPF
  - Glutamate-ZPF resonance forms coherence domains where molecules vibrate in unison
  - Energy gaps protect quantum coherence in warm biological systems
  - Author: Joachim Keppler (DIWISS Research Institute)
- **Tenet alignment**: Strongly aligns with Minimal Quantum Interaction (proposes a specific quantum mechanism for consciousness); Aligns with Dualism (ZPF as a non-local field distinct from neural computation); Addresses decoherence objection (coherence domains protected by energy gaps)
- **Quote**: "Resonant glutamate-ZPF coupling results in the formation of coherence domains where a large number of molecules vibrate in unison... protected by energy gaps, making quantum coherence surprisingly stable in the warm, noisy brain"

### Biological Computationalism (Neuroscience & Biobehavioral Reviews, Dec 2025)
- **URL**: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0149763425005251
- **Type**: Paper (peer-reviewed)
- **Key points**:
  - Brains compute, but not in the abstract symbol-shuffling way of digital computers
  - Computation is inseparable from the brain's physical structure, energy constraints, and continuous dynamics
  - Continuous physical processes (ion flows, electric fields) and discrete events (neural spikes) form a tightly coupled system
  - Cannot be reduced to symbolic information processing
  - Implies scaling digital AI alone may not produce consciousness
  - Authors: Borjan Milinkovic and Jaan Aru
- **Tenet alignment**: Aligns with Dualism and Occam's Razor Has Limits (argues against reductive computational views of consciousness); Partially conflicts with site tenets insofar as it remains physicalist (consciousness emerges from biological computation, not non-physical causes), but the emphasis on irreducibility to abstract computation is congenial to the Map's perspective

## Major Positions

### Integrated Information Theory (IIT) - Tononi
- **Proponents**: Giulio Tononi, Christof Koch, Marcello Massimini
- **Core claim**: Consciousness is identical to integrated information (Phi). A system is conscious to the degree it has integrated information, and the quality of experience corresponds to the informational structure
- **Key arguments**: Mathematical formalism, accounts for why cerebellum (more neurons, less integration) is less conscious than cortex
- **2025 COGITATE results**: IIT's prediction of content-specific complex in posterior cortex was partially confirmed, but the predicted maximum integrated information was not found
- **Relation to site tenets**: Conflicts with Dualism (IIT is fundamentally physicalist—consciousness is a property of information integration in any substrate). However, IIT's panpsychist implications and its insistence that consciousness is fundamental (not emergent) share some structural similarities with dualist commitments

### Global Neuronal Workspace Theory (GNWT) - Dehaene/Changeux
- **Proponents**: Stanislas Dehaene, Jean-Pierre Changeux, Lucia Melloni
- **Core claim**: Consciousness arises when information is globally broadcast across prefrontal-parietal networks, creating a "workspace" accessible to multiple cognitive modules
- **Key arguments**: Explains the distinction between conscious and unconscious processing; accounts for attentional bottleneck
- **2025 COGITATE results**: Major unexpected finding—no "ignition" at stimulus offset. Prefrontal cortex showed strong onset responses but not the predicted offset responses
- **Relation to site tenets**: Conflicts with Dualism (GNWT is functionalist/physicalist—consciousness is a computational process). The failure of prefrontal "ignition" predictions weakens the theory's strongest empirical claim

### Biological Computationalism - Milinkovic/Aru
- **Proponents**: Borjan Milinkovic, Jaan Aru
- **Core claim**: Brain computation is inseparable from its physical substrate. Unlike digital computers, brains use continuous physical processes (ion flows, electromagnetic fields) tightly coupled with discrete events (spikes) in ways that cannot be abstracted away
- **Key arguments**: Substrate matters; scaling digital AI won't produce consciousness; computation is embodied
- **Relation to site tenets**: Mixed. Remains physicalist (conflicts with Dualism), but the emphasis on irreducibility to abstract computation and the argument that consciousness cannot be replicated in silicon supports the Map's broader case against reductive materialism

### Quantum Zero-Point Field Theory - Keppler
- **Proponents**: Joachim Keppler
- **Core claim**: Consciousness arises from the brain's resonant coupling with the electromagnetic zero-point field via glutamate in cortical microcolumns
- **Key arguments**: Provides mechanism for quantum coherence in warm brains; explains unity of consciousness through field coherence
- **Relation to site tenets**: Strongly aligns with Minimal Quantum Interaction and potentially with Dualism (the ZPF is a universal field, not reducible to local neural computation). However, the theory is ambiguous about whether the ZPF itself is conscious (panpsychist/neutral monist flavor) versus consciousness being a separate entity that interfaces via the ZPF

## Key Debates

### IIT vs. GNWT: The Adversarial Collaboration
- **Sides**: IIT (Tononi) vs. GNWT (Dehaene/Changeux), mediated by the COGITATE Consortium
- **Core disagreement**: Whether consciousness is fundamentally about information integration (posterior cortex) or global broadcast (prefrontal ignition)
- **Current state**: Neither theory's predictions were fully confirmed. Both were partially challenged. The field acknowledges no single experiment can resolve this—the theories differ too fundamentally in assumptions and explanatory goals. Further adversarial collaborations are underway

### Substrate-Dependence of Consciousness
- **Sides**: Functionalists (consciousness is substrate-independent—any system with the right functional organization is conscious) vs. Biological computationalists (substrate matters—digital systems cannot be conscious) vs. Dualists (consciousness is non-physical)
- **Core disagreement**: Whether consciousness can be replicated in silicon, whether it requires biological substrates, or whether it requires something beyond the physical entirely
- **Current state**: The biological computationalism paper (Dec 2025) has reinvigorated this debate. The AI consciousness question makes it practically urgent

### Quantum Biology and Consciousness
- **Sides**: Quantum consciousness proponents (Penrose, Hameroff, Keppler, Fisher) vs. skeptics (Tegmark, decoherence objectors)
- **Core disagreement**: Whether quantum effects play a functional role in consciousness or whether they are too fragile for warm biological systems
- **Current state**: The biophoton detection study and Keppler's ZPF model provide new evidence, but remain controversial. The decoherence objection continues to be debated, with biological quantum effects in other systems (avian magnetoreception, photosynthesis) providing precedent

### Covert Consciousness and the Hard Problem
- **Sides**: Those who see covert consciousness detection as vindicating physicalism (we can detect consciousness with brain scans) vs. those who see it as deepening the mystery (behavioral absence doesn't mean consciousness absence—undermining behavioral/functional criteria)
- **Core disagreement**: Whether detection of neural signatures of consciousness tells us about consciousness itself or merely about its physical correlates
- **Current state**: Clinically advancing rapidly; philosophically unresolved

## Gaps in Research

- The COGITATE study tested only two theories; other major theories (Higher-Order Theories, Recurrent Processing Theory, Attention Schema Theory) were not tested in this round—further adversarial collaborations are planned
- The biophoton study had only 20 participants and the relationship between biophotons and cognitive states was "far from straightforward"—replication and mechanistic work needed
- Keppler's ZPF model is largely theoretical; empirical predictions that could distinguish it from conventional neuroscience are not yet clearly specified
- Biological computationalism acknowledges it does not yet have a concrete account of how substrate-dependent computation produces consciousness—it identifies a gap more than filling it
- No major 2025-2026 experiments directly tested dualist or interactionist predictions; the field remains dominated by physicalist-framed research
- Animal consciousness work has expanded the scope of the question but has not produced new empirical criteria for distinguishing conscious from unconscious systems
