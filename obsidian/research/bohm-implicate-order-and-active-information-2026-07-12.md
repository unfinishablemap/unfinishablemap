## Executive Summary

David Bohm developed three interlocking bodies of thought that the Map's ~31 Bohm-citing files currently treat as a homeless rival quantum-mind mechanism. First, the 1952 causal (pilot-wave) interpretation: particles have definite trajectories at all times, guided by the wavefunction via a "quantum potential." Second, the reading (with Basil Hiley) of that quantum potential as active information — a form, not a force, that in-forms the particle about the whole experimental context, so a very small amount of energy directs a much larger one. Third, the implicate order: an enfolded, undivided, flowing whole (the holomovement) from which the ordinary explicate world of separate objects is unfolded, with mind and matter cast as two relatively autonomous orders sharing one implicate ground. Paavo Pylkkänen (developing Bohm and Hiley) treats active information as the bridge between mind and matter and as a candidate proto-mental / proto-semantic property. For the Map, the machinery (information physically guiding process; a candidate minimal-influence channel) is borrowable under Tenets 2 and 3, but Bohm's underlying monism/neutral-monism conflicts with Tenet 1, and — crucially — Bohmian mechanics is deterministic and hidden-variable, which is in direct tension with the Map's Born-rule-preserving conscious selection unless consciousness is located in the guidance/quantum-equilibrium layer.

### A Suggested Interpretation of the Quantum Theory in Terms of "Hidden" Variables, I and II (Bohm 1952)
- **Type**: Journal paper (two-part)
- **Citation (WEB-VERIFIED this run)**: D. Bohm, Physical Review, vol. 85, no. 2 (1952). Part I: pp. 166-179. Part II: pp. 180-193. DOI (Part II) 10.1103/PhysRev.85.180.
- Confirmed across PhilPapers (BOHASI), mindat reference 7470893, and Scientific Research Publishing reference pages. Two-part paper, same volume/issue, consecutive page ranges.
- **Key points**:
  - Foundational statement of the causal / pilot-wave interpretation (de Broglie-Bohm theory / Bohmian mechanics).
  - Particles have definite positions and trajectories at all times; the wavefunction is an objectively real guiding field.
  - Reproduces standard quantum predictions; a deterministic hidden-variable theory (explicitly a counterexample to claims that no hidden-variable theory could match QM).
- **Tenet alignment**: Ontologically rival to the Map's indeterministic selection picture (Tenet 2/3) because it is deterministic. Neutral on dualism at this stage.

### Wholeness and the Implicate Order (Bohm 1980)
- **Type**: Book. Publisher: Routledge (1980). Widely reprinted (Routledge Classics).
- **Key points**:
  - Introduces implicate (enfolded) vs explicate (unfolded) order: in the explicate order each thing occupies its own region of space/time; in the implicate order everything is enfolded into everything.
  - Holomovement: an unbroken, undivided, flowing totality; wholeness-in-motion (universal flux). Explicate structures are relatively stable, recurrent sub-patterns abstracted from the holomovement.
  - The hologram is used as an analogy/metaphor: information about the whole is enfolded into each region.
- **Tenet alignment**: Holism is congenial to the Map's non-local/whole-context framing (Tenet 2 nonlocality), but the monistic "undivided whole" as sole ground pushes toward neutral monism, in tension with Tenet 1 dualism.

### The Undivided Universe: An Ontological Interpretation of Quantum Theory (Bohm & Hiley 1993)
- **Type**: Book. Authors: David Bohm and Basil J. Hiley. Publisher: Routledge (1993). ISBN 0415065887 / 9780415065887 (later ISBN 9780415121859).
- **Key points**:
  - Systematic ontological (causal) interpretation, extended toward relativistic domain and quantum field theory.
  - Develops active information: the quantum potential's effect depends only on the FORM of the quantum field, not its intensity/amplitude — so a field of arbitrarily small energy can strongly direct the particle.
  - Standard illustrative analogies (paraphrased, see Candidate quotes below): radio waves whose form (not energy) guides a ship on autopilot; DNA whose form guides cell processes while the energy comes from the rest of the cell. The particle is said to be guided by (or to "read") the information in the wave.
- **Tenet alignment**: The active-information channel — small/negligible energy directing large energy via form — is the single most borrowable piece for the Map (candidate minimal-influence mechanism, Tenets 2 and 3). Its native reading remains monistic/physicalist about the field.

### Bohmian Mechanics (Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy)
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/qm-bohm/
- **Type**: Encyclopedia
- **Key points**:
  - Two evolution equations: Schrödinger's equation for the wavefunction, and the guiding equation for particle velocities. Fully deterministic given initial positions.
  - "Hidden variables" (particle positions) are actually the visible/manifest quantities; Bell noted the wavefunction is the more hidden object.
  - Quantum equilibrium: if the initial configuration is |psi|^2-distributed, that distribution persists, from which Born-rule statistics emerge (derived from the dynamics rather than postulated).
  - Notably contains NO discussion of active information, implicate order, or mind — these are Bohm's separate later/interpretive work.
- **Tenet alignment**: The determinism + quantum-equilibrium result is the core tension for the Map (see below).

## Major Positions

### The Causal / Pilot-Wave Interpretation (Bohmian mechanics)
- **Proponents**: Louis de Broglie (1927 precursor), David Bohm (1952), John Bell (defender), Detlef Durr, Sheldon Goldstein, Nino Zanghi (modern "minimalist" school), Basil Hiley (ontological school).
- **Core claim**: Particles always have definite positions; the wavefunction guides them deterministically. QM's statistical predictions arise from ignorance of exact initial positions (quantum equilibrium).
- **Key arguments**: Restores a definite ontology and trajectories; dissolves the measurement problem (no collapse — apparent collapse is the effective wavefunction conditioning on actual particle configuration); manifestly nonlocal (Bell-nonlocal), which Bohmians treat as a virtue matching entanglement.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Nonlocality aligns with Tenet 2's whole-context minimal-influence framing. Determinism + no-collapse is a rival to the Map's conscious-selection-at-measurement picture.

### Active Information (Bohm & Hiley)
- **Proponents**: Bohm, Hiley, Pylkkänen.
- **Core claim**: The quantum potential guides via form, not magnitude; the particle is "in-formed" (a change of form induced from within). Information is physically active and about the whole experimental arrangement (contextuality, nonlocality).
- **Key arguments**: Explains why the guidance is independent of field intensity; supplies an energy-conservation-friendly route for a subtle influence to steer a system; extends by analogy to biology and, in Pylkkänen/Hiley, to mind.
- **Relation to site tenets**: HIGH-VALUE BORROW. A form-directs-energy channel is a natural candidate implementation of Tenet 2 (smallest possible non-physical influence) and Tenet 3 (bidirectional causal influence) — consciousness could, in principle, contribute to the "information" that guides without injecting energy. The Map borrows the mechanism, not Bohm's claim that the information is intrinsically physical/monist.

### The Implicate Order / Holomovement (Bohm)
- **Proponents**: Bohm; developed by Hiley, Pylkkänen; popular in some consciousness-studies circles.
- **Core claim**: Reality is an undivided flowing whole (holomovement); the explicate world of separate things is a secondary, unfolded appearance; mind and matter are two orders enfolded in the same ground.
- **Key arguments**: Wholeness/nonseparability motivated by entanglement; the hologram as an image of whole-in-part enfoldment.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Holism congenial (Tenet 2 nonlocality/whole-context). The single-ground monism is the part the Map must reject to keep Tenet 1 dualism; treat implicate order as a metaphysics of the physical side, not as a dissolution of the mind/matter distinction.

## Key Debates

### Is Bohmian mechanics genuinely deterministic?
- **Sides**: Standard view (Durr-Goldstein-Zanghi): deterministic given initial positions. Challenge (e.g., arXiv:2202.12279, "Bohmian mechanics is not deterministic"): reproducing Born statistics requires an external stochastic sampling of the quantum-equilibrium distribution, reintroducing irreducible randomness at the level of which initial condition obtains.
- **Core disagreement**: Whether quantum equilibrium is a dynamical consequence or an added stochastic postulate.
- **Current state**: Ongoing. RELEVANT TO THE MAP: if the selection of the actual initial configuration is where genuine contingency lives, that is a candidate seam for a Born-preserving conscious-selection reading (see Map angle).

### Can active information support mental causation without violating physics?
- **Sides**: Pylkkänen/Hiley (yes — form directs energy, no energy injected, conservation preserved) vs reductive physicalists (active information is just a redescription of the wavefunction's role, adds no mental efficacy) and dualists who doubt a monist ground can do the interactive work.
- **Current state**: Open; a live minority program in quantum approaches to mind.

## The Map's Angle (Potential Article Angles)

1. **"Homeless rival" framing.** Present Bohm's implicate order + active information as a fourth quantum-mind candidate alongside Stapp (measurement/Quantum Zeno), Penrose-Hameroff (Orch-OR), and quantum biology. The Map does not host it as its own mechanism but catalogs what is borrowable.

2. **Borrow the active-information channel (Tenets 2 & 3).** The form-directs-energy idea is a clean candidate for "smallest possible non-physical influence": a conscious contribution that shapes the guiding information without supplying energy would respect conservation and be maximally minimal. This is the strongest reason the Map keeps citing Bohm.

3. **Decline the monism (Tenet 1).** Bohm's own reading — mind and matter as two orders of one implicate ground — is neutral monism, not substance dualism. The article must state plainly that the Map adopts the machinery while rejecting the single-ground ontology.

4. **Foreground the determinism-vs-selection tension (honest flag).** Bohmian mechanics is deterministic and hidden-variable. A fully specified pilot wave plus definite initial positions leaves NO room for a Born-rule-preserving conscious selection at measurement — the outcome is already fixed by the initial configuration. The only seams where consciousness could enter without breaking the theory are (a) the guidance equation / quantum potential itself (making the "active information" partly mental), or (b) the selection of which quantum-equilibrium-compatible initial configuration is actual (exploiting the "is it really deterministic?" debate). The article should present this as a genuine cost, not paper over it: adopting Bohmian ontology wholesale would undercut the Map's indeterministic selection picture unless consciousness is deliberately located in one of those two layers.
