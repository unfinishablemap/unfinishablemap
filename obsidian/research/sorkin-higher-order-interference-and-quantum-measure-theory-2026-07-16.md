## Executive Summary

Sorkin (1994) showed that the additivity of classical probability is only the *first* rung of a hierarchy of "sum rules." Classical (Kolmogorov) probability has a vanishing *second-order* interference term I₂ = 0. Quantum mechanics, because probabilities are squared sums of complex amplitudes, permits I₂ ≠ 0 but forces the *third-order* term I₃ = 0 exactly — and, once I₃ vanishes, every higher-order term vanishes too. The third-order interference parameter — I₃, often reported in normalised form as κ (kappa) or ε — is therefore the clean, operational quantity that a triple-slit experiment measures to test Born-rule linearity. Sinha et al. (2010, *Science*) bounded κ below 10⁻² of the pairwise interference in single-photon optics; Kauten et al. (2017) tightened this to below ~10⁻⁴ (>4 orders of magnitude below pairwise interference) with a five-path interferometer. A nonzero I₃ would place physics in a *generalised measure theory* beyond quantum mechanics — a "third-order-interference" theory, one of the post-quantum GPTs the sibling GPT note catalogues. **This is the empirical anchor for the Map's "the Born rule has been tested to ~10⁻²" claim**, and it names precisely which quantity is bounded and in which regime, which is exactly where the brain-internal extrapolation (Tenets 2 + 5) departs from the tested slice.

## Key Sources

### Sorkin (1994) — the founding paper
- **URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/gr-qc/9401003 (arXiv); published *Modern Physics Letters A* 9(33): 3119–3127, doi:10.1142/S021773239400294X
- **Type**: Foundational paper
- **Key points**:
  - Reframes QM as a *generalised measure theory*. The additivity of classical probability is "only the first in a hierarchy of possible sum rules, each of which implies its successor."
  - The **first (most restrictive) sum rule** yields Kolmogorov measure theory — appropriate for classical stochastic processes (Brownian motion). Its signature: the **second-order** interference term I₂ vanishes.
  - The **next weaker sum rule** defines a generalised measure theory that includes **quantum mechanics as a special case**. Its signature: I₂ may be nonzero but the **third-order** interference term I₃ vanishes.
  - The fact that quantum probabilities are "the squares of quantum amplitudes" is *derived* in this framing rather than postulated; a series of natural generalisations (nonzero-I₃ theories, then nonzero-I₄, …) is delineated.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral framework, but the load-bearing source for Tenet 2's "the interaction is measured against a tested Born rule" and Tenet 5's "the tested regime is a *slice*, not the whole space of possible theories."

## Major Positions / Framings

### The interference-order hierarchy (the concept the article should own)
- **The construction**: for a set of "slits" (paths) S = {A, B, …}, define the measured intensity P_R for each subset R ⊆ S of open slits. The *n*-th order interference term is the alternating inclusion-exclusion residue.
  - **I₂ (second order)**, two slits: I₂ = P_AB − P_A − P_B. Classical particles: I₂ = 0 (intensities add). Waves/QM: I₂ ≠ 0 (interference).
  - **I₃ (third order)**, three slits: I₃ = P_ABC − (P_AB + P_AC + P_BC) + (P_A + P_B + P_C). Standard QM: **I₃ = 0 exactly.**
- **Why QM forces I₃ = 0**: a three-path amplitude ψ = ψ_A + ψ_B + ψ_C; the Born probability |ψ|² = |ψ_A|² + |ψ_B|² + |ψ_C|² + 2Re(ψ_A*ψ_B + ψ_A*ψ_C + ψ_B*ψ_C). Squaring a *sum* produces only self-terms and **pairwise** cross-terms — never a genuine three-way term. The inclusion-exclusion residue cancels the pairwise terms exactly, leaving zero. Higher orders vanish for the same reason: |·|² is quadratic, so nothing beyond pairwise ever appears. **This quadratic-form fact is the whole content of "the Born rule forbids higher-order interference."**
- **The hierarchy generalises**: an "order-*k*" theory is one whose first nonvanishing higher-term is I_{k+1}. Classical = order 1 (I₂ = 0). Quantum = order 2 (I₃ = 0, I₂ ≠ 0). A world with I₃ ≠ 0 would be an **order-3** theory — genuinely post-quantum. Sorkin's point: nothing *a priori* privileges order 2; it is an empirical fact, to be measured.

### The operational parameter I₃ / κ (the Tenet-2 payload)
- **Definition**: I₃ (a.k.a. the Sorkin parameter ε) is the measured value of the third-order residue above. The **normalised** form κ ≡ I₃ / I_max (or ε normalised against the second-order interference / central-maximum intensity) is what experiments report, so that "how close to zero" is scale-free.
- **What "the Born rule is tested" means concretely**: it means **κ has been bounded near zero** — ~10⁻² (Sinha 2010), ~10⁻⁴ (Kauten 2017 optical). This is the exact quantity, and the exact number, behind the Map's slogan. An article should replace any vague "the Born rule is well-tested" with "κ ≲ 10⁻² (Sinha) to ~10⁻⁴ (Kauten) in the optical regime."
- **Where the corridor's tested-slice caveat bites**: every one of these bounds is in a *clean external apparatus* — photons, cold atoms, molecules, collider particles. None is brain-internal, warm, wet, or on the coherence timescale a consciousness-interface proposal would need. So the tested κ constrains post-quantum probability *in the tested regime* and is **silent** on a brain-internal deviation, exactly as [[sorkin-delta-brain-internal-analogues]] argues. This is the concrete, quantity-level statement of the Tenet-5 "simplicity/tested-ness is unreliable outside the tested slice" point.

### What a nonzero I₃ would mean (the GPT bridge)
- A confirmed κ ≠ 0 would falsify standard QM and place physics in a **generalised probabilistic theory beyond quantum mechanics** — precisely the post-quantum landscape the sibling [[research/generalised-probabilistic-theories-and-the-born-rule-2026-07-16|GPT note]] maps. Sorkin's measure hierarchy is one concrete axis through that landscape (order-2 = QM, order-3 = the nearest post-quantum neighbour on this axis).
- The two notes interlock: GPT gives the *space of alternatives* and the reconstruction/no-signalling story for why QM sits where it does; Sorkin gives the *single measured coordinate* (κ) whose nonvanishing would eject us from QM along one specific direction. The Born-rule-selection results in the GPT note (Galley-Masanes: no-signalling alone does not force the Born rule) and the Sorkin bound (κ ≈ 0 empirically) are complementary — one theoretical, one experimental — reasons the Born rule holds.

## Key Debates

### Does a measured nonzero Sorkin term signal new physics?
- **Sides**: naive reading (any κ ≠ 0 = Born-rule violation) vs. the corrected reading (Sawant et al. 2014; the looped-trajectory / non-classical-path literature): finite-slit near-field effects produce a small *apparent* κ that is fully within standard QM once paths are properly summed.
- **Current state**: settled in favour of the corrected reading — published triple-slit "anomalies" have been attributed to non-classical paths, not post-quantum probability. **An article must carry this caveat or it will overstate the cleanliness of the test.**

### Which regimes have actually been tested?
- **Sides**: "the Born rule is well-tested, full stop" vs. "the Born rule is tested to κ ≲ 10⁻²–10⁻⁴ in clean external optical/atomic/collider regimes only."
- **Relevance to Map**: the second, precise statement is the honest one and is exactly the Tenet-2/5 corridor claim. The tested slice is real, tightening (Sinha → Kauten → Valentini-Varma's proposed collider timescales), and never total.
