## Executive Summary

The standard reading of human cognitive uniqueness treats the human-other-animal gap as a *brain-side* phenomenon — more cortical neurons (Herculano-Houzel 2009), elaborated prefrontal connectivity (Buckner & Krienen 2013), expanded white-matter pathways, distinctive von Economo neurons, and the resulting ratcheted social cognition (Tomasello 2010), mental time travel (Suddendorf & Corballis 2007), relational reasoning (Penn, Holyoak & Povinelli 2008), and shared intentionality (Tomasello/Call/Gómez). Under interactionist dualism (Eccles–Popper, Beck & Eccles, Stapp, Hodgson) the *same* observed differences admit a structurally different reading: rather than (or as well as) the brain becoming a better computer, the *mind-brain interface* itself may have evolved toward higher efficacy — wider selection bandwidth, higher fidelity from intent to physical outcome, finer-grained selection per event, and access to a broader range of neural-pattern types as candidates for selection. Under this reading, consciousness may be present at full grade across many species (consistent with the New York Declaration 2024) while *effective reach* into behavioural outcomes varies along the same phylogenetic gradient that produced the cognitive gap. The hypothesis is *speculative integration* tier (Map five-tier scale) — tenet-coherent under Tenet 1 (Dualism) and Tenet 3 (Bidirectional Interaction), but not empirically forced. Gutfreund's (2024) Standing Agnostic Challenge applies symmetrically: behavioural difference cannot adjudicate between brain-side and interface-side explanations using current methods, so the framework's value is heuristic-explanatory rather than prediction-generating. Distinguishing observables in principle — Born-rule deviation patterns scaling with phylogenetic intelligence rank, volitional/reflexive task contrasts in neural-coherence-domain signatures, candidate-set-diversity correlates of reportable content — exist but are not currently operationalised. The note surveys the literature these claims rest on and identifies the structural moves an interface-efficacy reframe would have to make.

## Key Sources

### Suzana Herculano-Houzel — "The Human Brain in Numbers" (2009)

- **URL**: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC2776484/
- **Type**: Primary review (Frontiers in Human Neuroscience)
- **Key points**:
  - Isotropic-fractionator counts give the human brain ~86 billion neurons (~16B cortical), not the textbook 100B.
  - The human brain is *not* exceptional in cellular composition for its size — it is a linearly scaled-up primate brain.
  - The "absolute number of cortical neurons" view treats raw cortical-neuron count as the dominant predictor of cognitive ability across primates.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral — the data are interpretable both as a brain-side computation thesis (Herculano-Houzel's reading) *and* as a substrate-for-interface thesis (more cortical neurons = larger candidate-set substrate the interface has to bias).
- **Quote**: "If absolute brain size is the best predictor of cognitive abilities in a primate, and absolute brain size is proportional to number of neurons across primates, human superior cognitive abilities might be accounted for simply by the total number of neurons in the human brain."
- **Interface-efficacy reframe**: "More neurons" raises an interface-side question the original framing does not ask — *more neurons available as candidates for what?* If selection bandwidth scales sub-linearly with candidate-set size, more neurons may yield diminishing returns until interface-side capacity also scales.

### Roth & Dicke — "Evolution of the Brain and Intelligence" (2005)

- **URL**: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1364661305000823 (Trends in Cognitive Sciences 9, 250–257)
- **Type**: Primary review
- **Key points**:
  - Number of cortical neurons and conduction velocity are the best brain-side correlates of intelligence — better than absolute brain size.
  - Humans have only marginally more cortical neurons than whales and elephants — yet the cognitive gap to those species is sometimes claimed to be larger than the gap from humans to monkeys.
  - "Outstanding intelligence of humans appears to result from a combination and enhancement of properties found in non-human primates" — theory of mind, imitation, language, prolonged developmental window — rather than from unique properties.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral. The combination-and-enhancement reading is compatible with both incremental brain-side scaling *and* incremental interface-side scaling.
- **Interface-efficacy reframe**: The "marginal-cortical-neuron-difference, large-cognitive-difference" gap with cetaceans is exactly the puzzle interface-efficacy scaling could absorb without remainder — if cetacean interfaces are configured for different selection profiles (e.g., high-bandwidth coupling to acoustic/social-tracking patterns, low-bandwidth coupling to recursive-symbolic patterns), then equal cortical neuron counts can support unequal cognitive *reach* without the mind-side differing in kind.

### Buckner & Krienen — "The Evolution of Distributed Association Networks in the Human Brain" (2013)

- **URL**: https://www.cell.com/trends/cognitive-sciences/fulltext/S1364-6613(13)00221-0
- **Type**: Primary review (Trends in Cognitive Sciences 17(12), 648–665)
- **Key points**:
  - The "tethering hypothesis": rapid cortical-mantle expansion in human evolution untethered large association regions from the molecular-gradient and early-activity-cascade constraints that structure sensory hierarchies in shorter-cortex species.
  - The freed regions develop densely interconnected cross-cortical networks that mature late and span the cortex, forming the substrate of distributed association.
  - Mechanism: late-forming neurons distant from instructive patterning centres become free to develop new organisational principles.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral. The thesis is structural — what changed — not interpretive — what the change is *for*.
- **Interface-efficacy reframe**: An "untethered" association cortex is precisely the kind of substrate whose value depends on *what selects within it*. If the neural-pattern candidates are not pre-shaped by sensory-hierarchical priors, then high-bandwidth, finely-grained interface selection becomes the rate-limiting factor for cognitive output. Under this reading, tethering-release is necessary but not sufficient for human cognition: the sufficient condition is that the interface evolved efficacy to use the unstructured substrate.

### Sakai et al. & Schoenemann et al. — Differential Prefrontal White Matter Development

- **URL**: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21835623/ and https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.1818512116
- **Type**: Primary studies
- **Key points**:
  - Human prefrontal white matter is nearly two-fold greater than chimpanzee prefrontal white matter; PFC cortical proportion is up to 86% larger than in macaques and 24% larger than in chimpanzees.
  - The arcuate fasciculus shows greater curvature, volume, and temporal extension in humans — absent in chimpanzees, consistent with language-pathway role.
  - Network analyses reveal higher centrality of multimodal-association connections and more pronounced modular topology in the human connectome relative to chimpanzees.
- **Interface-efficacy reframe**: Increased white-matter mass and modularity raise candidate-set integration speed — the rate at which neural-pattern candidates can be assembled for selection. Under selection-only or selection-plus-bias readings of mind-influence, this is structurally *interface-bandwidth-relevant* even when described in pure conduction-and-routing terms.

### von Economo Neuron Comparative Distribution

- **URL**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Von_Economo_neuron and https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3953677/
- **Type**: Synthesis / primary distribution studies
- **Key points**:
  - VENs are large spindle-shaped projection neurons concentrated in anterior cingulate cortex (ACC) and fronto-insular cortex.
  - Convergently appeared in cetacean ancestors (~35 MYA), hominid ancestors (~16 MYA), and elephantids (~8 MYA) — exactly the lineages standardly identified as high-grade cognition lineages.
  - Distribution gradient within hominids: humans > gracile great apes > robust great apes.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral. The convergent appearance in elite-cognition lineages is consistent with both a "fast long-range broadcast" brain-side reading and an "interface-relevant projection neuron" interface-side reading.
- **Interface-efficacy reframe**: VENs are striking candidates for *interface-relevant* projection neurons under any framework where conscious selection couples preferentially to subsets of the cortical population. Their distribution gradient tracks the cognitive gradient more cleanly than raw cortical-neuron count — which is exactly what interface-efficacy scaling would predict if the interface couples preferentially to specific projection-neuron classes.

### Tomasello (2010, 2014) — Shared Intentionality and the Cumulative-Culture Ratchet

- **URL**: https://dornsife.usc.edu/henrike-moll/wp-content/uploads/sites/404/2023/11/Tomasello_Moll_2010.pdf
- **Type**: Book chapter / primary theoretical work
- **Key points**:
  - Cumulative cultural evolution requires not just creative invention but *faithful social transmission* operating as a ratchet — modifications preserved until further improvement arrives.
  - Chimpanzee culture: behavioural biases generated by founder effects and product-oriented copying within the existing cognitive repertoire.
  - Human culture: process-oriented social learning, active teaching, normative conformity sanctions, distinctive shared-intentionality cluster (joint attention, intention-reading, declarative pointing).
  - The cognitive root: "uniquely human cognitive capacities that emerge early in human ontogeny" centred on shared-intentionality machinery.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral on the framing question — Tomasello's account is mechanism-agnostic between brain-side and interface-side stories.
- **Interface-efficacy reframe**: Process-oriented imitation and shared intentionality are structurally *high-fidelity selection problems* — selecting another agent's *intended* selection rather than copying surface motor output. If the interface couples to abstract-intentional-content candidates rather than only to motor-output candidates, the ratchet becomes a story about which *types* of candidates the interface can reach in addition to a story about social-cognitive content.

### Suddendorf & Corballis (2007) — "The Evolution of Foresight"

- **URL**: https://www.prospectivepsych.org/sites/default/files/pictures/Suddendorf-and-Corballis_2007.pdf
- **Type**: Primary review (Behavioral and Brain Sciences)
- **Key points**:
  - Mental time travel (MTT) — the flexible capacity to project the self into imagined past and future scenarios — argued to be uniquely or near-uniquely human.
  - In their review of comparative studies "there is as yet no convincing evidence for mental time travel in nonhuman animals" beyond limited future-oriented behaviour patterns.
  - Episodic memory is part of a more general MTT faculty supporting flexible foresight, planning, and counterfactual reasoning.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral. The capacity is operationalised behaviourally; the underlying architecture is left open.
- **Interface-efficacy reframe**: MTT requires sustained selection-and-amplification of *self-projected, non-currently-instantiated* neural-pattern candidates — patterns about states of affairs that are neither currently present nor stably stored. This is a stringent interface-efficacy demand: bandwidth high enough to maintain candidate-set turnover within an imagined-temporal frame, fidelity high enough to keep selection coherent across multi-step projection. Suddendorf-Corballis's cross-species absence pattern aligns *better* with an interface-efficacy ceiling than with raw computational ceiling, because the underlying neural-pattern types appear to be present (great apes can prospect short-term) but the sustained selection regime is not.

### Penn, Holyoak & Povinelli (2008) — "Darwin's Mistake"

- **URL**: https://reasoninglab.psych.ucla.edu/wp-content/uploads/sites/273/2021/04/Penn-Holyoak-Povinelli.2008.pdf
- **Type**: Primary target article (Behavioral and Brain Sciences 31(2), 109–130)
- **Key points**:
  - Strong continuity claims about human-nonhuman cognition mask a profound *discontinuity* in higher-order, systematic, relational reasoning capacity.
  - The discontinuity centres on "the system enabling higher-order, abstract, role-governed, relational reasoning" grafted onto an inherited primate cognitive architecture.
  - Innate analogical ability — reinterpreting sensory and cognitive data in abstract relational terms — is what the system delivers and what no nonhuman animal demonstrates fully.
- **Tenet alignment**: Mixed — the "discontinuity" framing rules out pure continuity views but does not rule out either brain-side or interface-side scaling stories.
- **Interface-efficacy reframe**: Higher-order relational reasoning requires the conscious system to bias *abstract-relational* candidates over *concrete-perceptual* candidates within the same cortical substrate. An interface-efficacy reframe asks: did the human interface evolve coupling to relational-pattern candidates that other primates' interfaces do not couple to (despite the patterns being computable in their substrate)? This is testable in principle by neural-decoding studies that compare candidate-set diversity in PFC across species during analogous tasks.

### Gómez (2007) and Tomasello/Call/Carpenter — Pointing and Shared Intentionality

- **URL**: https://royalsocietypublishing.org/doi/10.1098/rstb.2021.0093 and surrounding literature
- **Type**: Primary research synthesis
- **Key points**:
  - Apes can point imperatively (request-based) but not declaratively (sharing-based) without extensive training.
  - Apes coordinate attention with humans but do not appear to coordinate *recursively* — they do not represent the joint-attention state itself as a shared object.
  - Shared-intentionality machinery is hypothesised to be the human-uniqueness substrate underlying joint attention, declarative communication, imitative learning, and teaching.
- **Interface-efficacy reframe**: Recursive joint attention requires the interface to couple to *meta-representational* candidates — patterns *about* joint-attentional patterns. Imperative-but-not-declarative pointing is the behavioural signature of an interface that reaches first-order intentional candidates but not the meta-representational candidates needed for shared intentionality.

### Gutfreund (2024) — "Neuroscience of Animal Consciousness: Still Agnostic After All"

- **URL**: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2024.1456403/full
- **Type**: Primary review (Frontiers in Psychology)
- **Key points**:
  - Consciousness in other animals cannot be directly observed, only inferred — the inference is bounded by epistemic limits intrinsic to first-person phenomena.
  - Behavioural responses are perceptual decisions, not reports of subjective experience; neural correlates of those decisions do not establish consciousness.
  - The agnostic stance: the question of animal-consciousness presence and grade may be unresolvable through scientific methods alone.
  - Counter-position: the New York Declaration on Animal Consciousness (2024) asserts robust scientific evidence for consciousness in mammals, birds, and "realistic possibility" of consciousness in other vertebrates and many invertebrates.
- **Tenet alignment**: Aligns with the Map's evidential-status-discipline and framework-stage-calibration commitments. Gutfreund's epistemic ceiling applies symmetrically to interface-efficacy claims — they too rely on behavioural inference and so cannot be empirically forced.
- **Quote**: "Animals do not report their subjective experiences; rather, they make a behavioral decision."
- **Critical importance**: This source defines the *Standing Agnostic Challenge* the interface-efficacy hypothesis must engage. Any reading of behavioural-cognition data as evidence for *any* specific mind-side claim — including interface-efficacy — faces the same epistemic ceiling as readings supporting any other mind-side claim. The interface-efficacy reframe is therefore not in better evidential standing than the brain-side framing it competes with; both face the agnostic ceiling.

### Eccles, Beck, Hodgson, Stapp — Interactionist-Dualist Interface Tradition

- **URLs**:
  - Eccles biography & psychon model: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interactionism_(philosophy_of_mind)
  - Beck-Eccles quantum-tunnelling exocytosis: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/275584999_Quantum_interactive_dualism_From_Beck_and_Eccles_tunneling_model_of_exocytosis_to_molecular_biology_of_SNARE_zipping
  - Stapp QID: https://www-physics.lbl.gov/~stapp/QID.pdf
- **Type**: Primary theoretical works
- **Key points**:
  - Eccles posits 40 million dendrons each linked to a "psychon" — unitary-conscious-experience unit operating on synapses by quantum processes; willed action raises firing probability of selected neurons.
  - Beck-Eccles model: mind-brain action via quantum tunnelling at the synaptic exocytosis step, energy-conservation-preserving.
  - Stapp: consciousness chooses the *observable* (Process 1) and the quantum Zeno effect amplifies selected neural patterns by rapid repeated probing.
  - Hodgson: quantum indeterminacy manifests at high level when acts of observation collapse the wave function.
- **Interface-efficacy gap in the tradition**: None of the canonical interactionist-dualist authors developed an *evolutionary scaling* account of interface efficacy across species. Eccles's "Evolution of consciousness" argues special anatomical adaptations of mammalian cortex gave *rise* to consciousness — closer to a threshold model than a scaling model. The interface-efficacy hypothesis fills a gap in the tradition: an explicitly scalar variable, varying continuously across species, predicting the cognitive gradient.

### Dehaene Global Neuronal Workspace — Selection and Broadcasting

- **URL**: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0896627320300520
- **Type**: Primary theoretical synthesis
- **Key points**:
  - Local cortical processors are linked by highly-interconnected hub regions that *select*, *amplify*, and *broadcast* a piece of information across the cortex, rendering it consciously accessible.
  - Both humans and monkeys exhibit GNW-style signatures, but content-level differences (e.g., humans' nested compositional representations) suggest the workspace itself differs in operational repertoire.
  - Working-memory bottleneck: incoming stimuli compete for conscious access; broadcast is single-stream.
- **Interface-efficacy reframe**: The GNW selection-and-broadcast architecture is *exactly* the brain-side substrate an interface-efficacy account would predict to be the variable target of evolutionary tuning. Whether the workspace differences across species are best read as brain-side architectural differences (Dehaene's framing) or as interface-side coupling differences operating on similar architectures (an interface-efficacy framing) is a question that current data do not adjudicate.

### DeWall, Baumeister & Masicampo (2008) — Consciousness-Specific Cognitive Load Effects

- **URL**: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1053810007001766
- **Type**: Primary experimental study
- **Key points**:
  - Cognitive load that occupies *conscious* processing causes substantial decrements in logical reasoning.
  - Cognitive load that occupies *unconscious* processing (suppressed-thought paradigm, unconscious priming) does not cause comparable decrements.
  - Tests Lieberman et al.'s (2002) C-system / X-system distinction: only the reflective system supports full logical reasoning.
- **Tenet alignment**: Aligns with the Map's [[consciousness-independent-baseline-cognition|baseline cognition hypothesis]] — consciousness causally contributes to functions the unconscious baseline cannot achieve.
- **Interface-efficacy reframe**: If conscious processing is interface-mediated and unconscious processing is not, then load-on-conscious effects are direct evidence that interface bandwidth is finite and bottlenecked. The cross-species version of this experiment — comparing whether cognitive functions in great apes are similarly bottlenecked when their candidate "conscious bandwidth" is loaded — is not currently feasible but would be a distinguishing observable.

### Nieder and Colleagues — Convergent Avian Cognitive Architecture

- **URL**: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S2352154616302303 and https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.abb1447
- **Type**: Primary research syntheses
- **Key points**:
  - Corvid nidopallium caudolaterale (NCL) is functionally equivalent to mammalian PFC despite developing from different (ventral vs dorsal) embryonic pallium.
  - Four convergently evolved features of smart birds and mammals: many associative pallial neurons, pallial PFC-equivalent area, dense dopaminergic innervation of associative pallium, flexible neural fundaments of working memory.
  - Carrion crows show neural correlates of sensory consciousness in NCL — abstract-rule neurons in endbrain support intelligent behaviour.
- **Interface-efficacy reframe**: Corvid convergent intelligence with radically different pallial architecture is a critical test case. If interface efficacy scales with cognitive grade rather than with specific cortical architecture, then corvid interface-side configuration should resemble primate configuration in *functional* terms (selection bandwidth, candidate-set diversity, fidelity) despite differing in *structural* implementation. The Map's [[interface-heterogeneity]] concept is relevant here; this hypothesis adds a quantitative-scaling layer on top of structural heterogeneity.

## Major Positions

### Position 1 — Brain-as-Better-Computer (Standard Reading)

- **Proponents**: Mainstream comparative neuroscience, including the strict reading of Herculano-Houzel (cortical-neuron count), Roth & Dicke (neuron count + conduction velocity), Buckner & Krienen (untethered association cortex), Dehaene (GNW operational repertoire).
- **Core claim**: Human-other-animal cognitive differences are explained by quantitative and qualitative differences in *brain-side* computational capacity — more neurons, better connectivity, freer association cortex, more flexible workspace.
- **Key arguments**: Cortical-neuron-count correlations across primates; PFC expansion in hominids; tethering hypothesis; arcuate fasciculus extension; GNW signatures; convergent appearance of cognition-supporting features in elite-cognition lineages.
- **Relation to Map tenets**: Compatible with all five tenets — does not require denial of dualism, just relegates the interface to a non-explanatory role for the gap. The Map need not reject this position to maintain its tenets.

### Position 2 — Better Interface (The Hypothesis)

- **Proponents**: Implicit in but never developed by the interactionist-dualist tradition (Eccles, Beck, Hodgson, Stapp); explicitly named by The Unfinishable Map (this research note seeds the formal article).
- **Core claim**: Human-other-animal cognitive differences arise primarily from evolved efficacy of the *mind-brain interface itself* — the mind side's effective reach into behavioural outcomes through the brain — rather than from greater brain-computational capacity. Consciousness may be present at full grade across many species while *effective reach* varies.
- **Sub-claims**:
  1. **Wider selection bandwidth** — more candidate patterns the interface can select among per unit time.
  2. **Higher fidelity** — less decoherence loss between mind-side intent and physical actualisation.
  3. **Finer-grained selection** — smaller probability adjustments per event, supporting cumulative effects.
  4. **Broader candidate-type coupling** — interface couples to motor, planning, abstract-reasoning, and meta-representational candidates rather than to motor-only candidates.
- **Key arguments**: The cortical-neuron-count gradient does not match the cognitive-grade gradient cleanly (cetaceans have ≈human counts with arguably smaller cumulative-cultural ratcheting); convergent intelligence in radically different neural architectures (corvids) suggests a functional rather than structural variable scales; the comparative-cognition gaps (cumulative culture, MTT, relational reasoning, declarative pointing) admit interface-efficacy reframings that subsume them under a single mechanism.
- **Relation to Map tenets**: Tenet-compatible under Tenet 1 (Dualism, presupposed) and Tenet 3 (Bidirectional Interaction, scaled). Methodologically requires Tenet 5 (Occam's Razor Has Limits) — the hypothesis is not parsimonious by standard lights but the standard lights are precisely what the tenet asks the Map to defeat as a *decisive* argument.

### Position 3 — Hybrid (Brain-and-Interface Co-Evolution)

- **Proponents**: Implicit in much of the contemporary interactionist literature where authors do not commit fully to interface-only or brain-only readings.
- **Core claim**: The cognitive gradient reflects co-evolution of brain-side substrate and interface-side coupling. Better substrate is necessary but not sufficient for better cognition; interface-efficacy scaling is necessary on top.
- **Relation to Map tenets**: Most aligned with the Map's general stance — neither brain alone nor interface alone is the full story.

### Position 4 — Eliminative / Behavioural-Decision Reading (Gutfreund's Stance)

- **Proponents**: Gutfreund (2024); strict behaviourists; some materialist comparative psychologists.
- **Core claim**: We cannot adjudicate between mind-side and brain-side scaling readings using behavioural data; the question may be unresolvable. Behavioural responses are perceptual decisions, not reports of subjective experience.
- **Relation to Map tenets**: This is the *epistemic* position the Map's interface-efficacy hypothesis must respect — it sets the evidential ceiling, not the metaphysical conclusion. The Map's evidential-status-discipline absorbs Gutfreund's challenge by placing the interface-efficacy claim at *speculative integration* tier, not at any tier requiring empirical force.

## Key Debates

### Debate 1 — Does the Cortical-Neuron-Count Gradient Match the Cognitive-Grade Gradient?

- **Sides**: Herculano-Houzel (yes, within primates) vs Roth & Dicke (yes, but only marginally for cetaceans/elephants relative to humans) vs interface-efficacy reframe (no — the gap with cetaceans is too large for raw count to explain).
- **Core disagreement**: Whether the correlation between cortical neurons and cognition holds tightly enough to function as a brain-side full explanation.
- **Current state**: Open. Cetacean and elephant cognition data remain contested; the 2024 New York Declaration affirms consciousness in those lineages without committing on cognitive grade.

### Debate 2 — Is Cumulative Culture a Brain-Side or Interface-Side Phenomenon?

- **Sides**: Tomasello (brain-side: shared-intentionality cognitive cluster); interface-efficacy reframe (interface-side: meta-representational candidate coupling); hybrid views.
- **Core disagreement**: Whether the underlying neural-pattern candidates are absent in great apes (brain-side) or present-but-unselectable (interface-side).
- **Current state**: Open. The candidate-set-diversity question is not currently operationalised in comparative neuroimaging.

### Debate 3 — Standing Agnostic Challenge — Can Comparative Behavioural Data Adjudicate Mind-Side Claims at All?

- **Sides**: Gutfreund 2024 (no — epistemic ceiling) vs New York Declaration 2024 (yes — robust evidence for consciousness presence) vs Map's evidential-status-discipline (yes, with calibration — claims register at appropriate tiers but do not exit the agnostic ceiling).
- **Core disagreement**: Whether the inference from behaviour to mind admits any empirical purchase or remains permanently underdetermined.
- **Current state**: Active. The Map's interface-efficacy hypothesis lives at the *speculative integration* tier precisely because of this debate.

2. **Convergent-intelligence-as-test-case angle**: Lead with corvid intelligence as the puzzle that the brain-side reading struggles to absorb (radically different cortex, similar functional cognition); argue interface-efficacy scaling explains this more naturally; treat the human-other-animal gap as one instance of a broader pattern.

4. **Phylogenetic-scaling angle**: Lead with the human > great apes > monkeys > other primates gradient and ask: what single variable scales monotonically with this gradient and predicts cognitive grade? Argue interface-efficacy is a candidate; brain-side variables fit imperfectly; interface-efficacy fits the gradient *and* the cetacean/corvid outliers.

## Gaps in Research

- **No quantitative cross-species interface-efficacy operationalisation**: The four axes (bandwidth, fidelity, granularity, candidate-type coupling) are conceptually distinct but no measurement procedure currently distinguishes them across species.
- **No Born-rule deviation comparative data**: The Map's [[born-rule-violation-brain-interface-empirical-status]] note already documents that Born-rule deviation evidence is currently null for humans; comparative cross-species data does not exist at all.
- **No cross-species GNW selection-bandwidth measurement**: Dehaene's framework assumes GNW signatures across primates but does not measure bandwidth or selection-rate quantitatively.
- **No cetacean cognitive-grade settled estimate**: The cetacean-vs-human cortical-neuron-count argument cannot be resolved without a settled comparative cognitive-grade measure, which Gutfreund's challenge says may be permanently elusive.
- **No interface-side reading of corvid convergent intelligence**: The convergent-evolution literature on corvid NCL is brain-side only; the interface-side reading proposed here is novel and untested.
- **No formal scaling theory connecting interface-efficacy to cognitive-grade rank**: The hypothesis lacks the mathematical scaffolding the Map's selection-only-mind-influence article will provide for the strict Tenet-2 reading; an analogous formalism for the efficacy-scaling reading is open work.
