Integration as activity is the discipline of treating the integration of conscious experience as something consciousness *does* rather than as a property consciousness *has* or *is*. The distinction sounds slight; it does most of the philosophical work in the Map's critique of [[integrated-information-theory|Integrated Information Theory (IIT)]]. IIT identifies consciousness with integrated information—a structural property of a physical system at an instant. The Map holds that integration is better understood as the active synthesis a conscious subject performs on what the brain presents. Recurrent processing theory, global workspace theory, predictive processing, and Whitehead's process philosophy each, in different vocabulary, treat integration as activity rather than property. The convergence is what makes the distinction load-bearing rather than idiosyncratic.

This page states the identity/activity distinction at its sharpest, catalogues the four convergent alternatives, and names the limit of the discipline: it does not require panexperientialism, and it does not by itself prove [[interactionist-dualism]].

## The Identity Thesis at Its Strongest

IIT's core commitment is an identity. Tononi writes that consciousness "is one and the same" as a maximally irreducible cause-effect structure—the phi-structure (Tononi 2008; Albantakis et al. 2023). This is not the weaker claim that integration correlates with consciousness, or that integration is necessary for consciousness, or that consciousness supervenes on integration. It is the metaphysical claim that the integrated structure and the experience are *the same thing*, viewed from different angles.

The thesis is attractive. It avoids any explanatory gap between physical structure and experience, because there is no gap to bridge: integration just *is* what experience is, from the inside. It explains why experience feels unified—the structure is unified—and why it has the particular qualitative character it does—the structure has that character. The theory is mathematically precise, empirically engageable, and refuses the easy retreat into mystery.

But the thesis has a consequence the Map finds fatal: if consciousness *is* a physical structure, then consciousness has no causal powers over and above that structure's physical causal powers. The integrated information does what it does because of the physical laws governing its substrate. Adding "and it is conscious" does not change which subsequent state the system enters. Consciousness, on the identity thesis, is something the universe *contains* rather than something a subject *does*. This is [[epiphenomenalism]] in identity-theoretic dress. (For the full argument, see [[consciousness-and-integrated-information]].)

## What "Activity" Adds

The activity framing reverses the order of explanation. Experience is unified not because some structure has the right shape, but because a subject actively binds available information into a coherent whole. The brain presents possibilities; consciousness selects and integrates among them. The integrated experience is the *output* of an act, not the *constitution* of a structure.

Three features distinguish activity from property:

**Activity is temporally extended.** A property holds at an instant; an activity unfolds across time. Listening to a melody is integration *across* moments, not integration *within* a moment. (See [[temporal-consciousness]] for the structural argument.) IIT computes phi for a system at time *t*; the experienced flow of the melody has no natural home in that snapshot.

**Activity is selective.** A property is exhaustively determined by the system's state; an activity chooses among options the state makes available. When attention shifts from the violin line to the rhythmic structure of a piece, the integrated experience changes without the brain's physical state changing in any way IIT's measure would register at the right grain. The selection is part of the experience, not separable from it.

**Activity admits a subject.** A property has no "for whom"—it just obtains. An activity is performed by something. The grammar of "integration *by* consciousness" presupposes that consciousness is the kind of thing that performs acts. On the identity thesis, this grammar is misleading: there is no agent of integration, only the structure that *is* the integration. The activity framing takes the grammar seriously.

These features do not by themselves refute IIT. They mark where the two framings diverge and where the Map's commitments tilt toward activity. The argument that the divergence favours activity is developed in [[consciousness-and-integrated-information]]; the present page is concerned with the distinction itself.

## Four Convergent Alternatives

The activity framing is not the Map's invention. Four well-developed theories of consciousness, each starting from different premises, converge on treating integration as something that happens rather than something that is. Their convergence is part of what gives the distinction its weight: it is not a philosophical preference imposed on a recalcitrant theory but a pattern that emerges when one looks at what makes integration *integrative*.

### Recurrent Processing

Victor Lamme's recurrent processing theory (Lamme 2006) identifies consciousness with recurrent neural processing—signals that loop back from higher cortical areas to lower ones. Feed-forward processing, however integrated, is unconscious; consciousness begins when recurrence creates loops. This is integration as *process*: recurrence is something the brain does over time, not a static measure of its state. The temporal dimension is built in; there is no "phi at an instant" because recurrence cannot exist at an instant.

The Map registers the convergence with activity framing while noting RPT's limit: it identifies which neural states are conscious without explaining *why* any neural state is conscious at all. The [[hard-problem-of-consciousness|hard problem]] persists. RPT shows that the activity framing is available in cognitive neuroscience; it does not by itself supply the Map's metaphysics.

### Global Workspace Broadcasting

Bernard Baars's global workspace theory (Baars 1988) and its neural implementation by Stanislas Dehaene (Dehaene and Changeux 2011) characterise integration differently from IIT. Where IIT measures internal binding, [[global-workspace-theory|GWT]] measures *availability*: information becomes conscious when it is broadcast from specialised processors to a workspace accessible to memory, planning, language, and motor control. Integration is *reach*, not internal structure. Broadcasting is something a system does, not a property it possesses at a moment.

The 2025 COGITATE adversarial collaboration (COGITATE Consortium 2025) tested GWT against IIT directly; neither was clearly vindicated. What survives the result, regardless of which empirical signatures hold up, is the GWT framing of integration as a *process* of making information widely available. That framing is structurally activity-shaped.

### Predictive Integration

[[predictive-processing|Predictive processing]] characterises the brain as a hierarchical generative model that constantly predicts its sensory inputs. Integration happens when predictions from multiple levels and modalities are reconciled into a coherent best explanation of what is happening (Friston 2010). This reconciliation is inherently active: hypotheses compete, evidence is weighed, the model updates. There is no snapshot at which the integration simply *is*; integration is the ongoing settlement of a competition.

The predictive-processing community largely treats the activity as computational rather than experiential. The Map's reading—that some such activity is the *machinery* through which consciousness exercises selection at decision points—is a Map-specific interpretation, not a claim the predictive-processing literature endorses (see [[predictive-processing-and-dualism]] for the integration). What the framework supplies, regardless of metaphysics, is a vocabulary in which integration is constitutively dynamic.

### Whiteheadian Prehension

Alfred North Whitehead's process philosophy (Whitehead 1929) treats every moment of reality as an act of integration. An "actual occasion" *prehends* its predecessors—grasps the just-past, the sensory data, the relevant context—and synthesises them into a new unity. (See [[prehension]] for the concept and [[process-philosophy]] for the wider framework.) Integration is not a property of an occasion; it is what an occasion *does* in coming into being. This is the activity framing taken to its metaphysical limit: integration is ontologically basic, not derivative on substance.

**The Whitehead limit.** Whitehead's framework comes attached to [[panpsychism|panexperientialism]]—the view that every actual occasion has some experiential aspect, not just brains. The Map does not commit to this. The discipline of treating integration as activity does *not* require panexperientialism. It is consistent to hold (a) that conscious integration is an activity performed by conscious subjects, and (b) that not every physical process is a conscious subject performing an activity. Whitehead's metaphysics is one consistent home for the activity framing; it is not the only one. The Map borrows the activity structure without inheriting the universal-experience commitment.

## What the Discipline Does Not Settle

Treating integration as activity is a framing, not an argument for [[interactionist-dualism]] specifically. Several positions remain compatible with the discipline:

- A property dualist could hold that the activity is performed by a physical subject whose physical states have non-physical experiential aspects, without any non-physical causation. The activity is real; it just does not require an interactionist tenet.
- An emergentist could hold that the activity is a high-level pattern that genuinely does causal work via downward causation, without being non-physical.
- A neutral monist could hold that the activity is fundamental at a level that is neither mental nor physical, with both aspects derivative.

The Map takes the activity discipline to be most naturally at home in an interactionist framework, where the subject performing the integration has causal powers not exhausted by the substrate's physical laws (see [[mental-causation-and-downward-causation]] and [[type-token-causation]] for the operational mechanism). That naturalness is an argument from fit, not a proof. The discipline's main work is to deny the identity thesis, not to specify the positive metaphysics that replaces it.

## Relation to Site Perspective

The activity framing is the operationalisation of two tenets.

**[[tenets#^dualism|Dualism]].** If integration is a physical property and consciousness is identical to that property, consciousness reduces to a physical structure. The activity framing keeps consciousness over and above the substrate: the substrate provides the possibilities; consciousness selects and binds among them. This is the structural commitment dualism requires without specifying any particular metaphysics of the mental.

**[[tenets#^bidirectional-interaction|Bidirectional Interaction]].** An identity leaves no room for influence—the structure simply *is* what it is. An activity naturally accommodates influence: the act of integration shapes which neural pattern is realised. Whether this influence operates at quantum indeterminacies (the Map's [[tenets#^minimal-quantum-interaction|Minimal Quantum Interaction]] specification) or through some other mechanism is a downstream question. The framing makes room for influence; the tenet specifies its register.
