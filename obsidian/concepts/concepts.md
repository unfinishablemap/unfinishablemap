Concepts are the Map's building blocks—the terms, frameworks, and arguments that recur across the [[topics]] deep dives and the [[apex/apex-articles|apex syntheses]]. If a topic article uses a piece of vocabulary as though it were already defined, this is where the definition lives. Read concepts to learn what the Map means by a word; read topics to see the word doing work. The fastest orientation is the five foundational concepts below, then the cluster matching the thread you are following: **dualism and interaction** (the metaphysical foundation), the **quantum interface** (measurement, decoherence, selection), **agency and free will** (agent causation and its objections), or **phenomenal consciousness** (qualia, acquaintance, the hard problem's raw material).

Each page is a working definition—continuously reviewed and refined—framed for the Map's consciousness-first perspective and connected back to the [[tenets]], rather than a neutral encyclopedia gloss. Where a concept has standard textbook content an LLM already knows, the Map states only what is distinctive to its own dualist framing.

## Five Foundational Concepts

- [[dualism|Dualism]] — consciousness is not reducible to physical processes. The root commitment everything else builds on.
- [[interactionist-dualism|Interactionist Dualism]] — the specific form the Map defends: mind and matter causally influence one another.
- [[phenomenal-consciousness|Phenomenal Consciousness]] — what it is like to undergo experience, the explanandum the hard problem names.
- [[agent-causation|Agent Causation]] — the conscious subject as an originating cause, not merely a link in a physical chain.
- [[mind-matter-interface|The Mind-Matter Interface]] — where and how mind meets matter, unifying filter theory and quantum selection.

**If you are following dualism and interaction,** begin with [[dualism|Dualism]], then [[interactionist-dualism|Interactionist Dualism]], [[bidirectional-interaction|Bidirectional Interaction]], [[objections-to-interactionism|Objections to Interactionist Dualism]], and the contrast positions ([[epiphenomenalism|Epiphenomenalism]], [[panpsychism|Panpsychism]], [[disguised-property-dualism|Disguised Property Dualism]]).

**If you are following the quantum interface,** begin with [[measurement-problem|The Measurement Problem]], then [[decoherence|Decoherence and the Quantum Consciousness Objection]], [[post-decoherence-selection|Post-Decoherence Selection]], [[contextual-selection-in-quantum-foundations|Contextual Selection in Quantum Foundations]], [[locality|Locality and Mind-Matter Interaction]], and [[qbism|QBism]].

**If you are following agency and free will,** begin with [[agent-causation|Agent Causation]], then [[luck-objection|The Luck Objection to Libertarian Free Will]], and the interface concepts that give agency its causal opening ([[mind-matter-interface|The Mind-Matter Interface]], [[bidirectional-interaction|Bidirectional Interaction]]).

**If you are following phenomenal consciousness,** begin with [[phenomenal-consciousness|Phenomenal Consciousness]], then [[phenomenal-acquaintance|Phenomenal Acquaintance]], [[intentionality|Intentionality]], [[inverted-qualia|Inverted Qualia]], [[phenomenal-transparency-opacity-spectrum|The Phenomenal Transparency-Opacity Spectrum]], and [[phenomenal-contrast-method|the Phenomenal Contrast Method]] for how the Map argues from felt experience.
