Retrocausal selection is the proposal that consciousness selects which quantum outcome becomes actual by operating outside temporal sequence — not at a specific moment, but atemporally across the entire transaction that constitutes a quantum event. Where [[consciousness-selecting-neural-patterns|non-retrocausal selection]] requires consciousness to act on a superposition at the moment of collapse, retrocausal selection dissolves the question of timing altogether. The Unfinishable Map develops this as one of two pathways for the [[tenets#^bidirectional-interaction|Bidirectional Interaction tenet]]: consciousness genuinely influences physical outcomes even when neural activity precedes conscious awareness in the linear temporal sequence.

The concept is distinct from [[retrocausality]] as a general principle in physics. Retrocausality concerns whether backward-in-time causation is physically coherent; retrocausal selection concerns whether consciousness specifically uses this structure to influence quantum outcomes in neural systems.

## The Core Mechanism

Three frameworks provide physics for retrocausal selection, each with a different account of how consciousness interfaces with the quantum formalism.

### Transaction Selection (Cramer/Kastner)

On Cramer's transactional interpretation, quantum events are "handshakes" between offer waves propagating forward in time and confirmation waves propagating backward. Collapse is not instantaneous but atemporal — it "does not happen at any specific point in time, but occurs along the whole transaction" (Cramer, 1986). Kastner's possibilist extension relocates these waves to a pre-spacetime (PST) realm of possibilities, with actualized transactions establishing spacetime events themselves.

Retrocausal selection adapts this framework: consciousness operates in Kastner's PST realm, selecting which offer-confirmation handshake becomes actual. Selection requires no energy injection — it is a determination among pre-existing possibilities, not a force applied to physical systems. This is the Map's most developed retrocausal pathway, because it naturally satisfies the [[tenets#^minimal-quantum-interaction|Minimal Quantum Interaction tenet]]: consciousness participates in a process the interpretation says already exists.

Kastner herself does not invoke consciousness as the transaction selector. The Map constructs this as original synthesis, extending PTI's ontology to accommodate [[interactionist-dualism]].

### Post-Selection (Aharonov/TSVF)

The two-state vector formalism describes quantum systems as determined by both past boundary conditions (pre-selected state) and future boundary conditions (post-selected state). Weak measurements reveal "weak values" that depend on both temporal directions simultaneously. Retrocausal selection casts consciousness as the source of post-selection conditions in neural systems — determining which future boundary condition applies, and thereby retroactively constraining which outcomes were possible.

Aharonov, Cohen, and Elitzur (2015) show this framework is explicitly compatible with free will: quantum indeterminism masks future states from present access, so backward influence does not entail fatalism.

### Constraint Selection (Wharton/Price)

Wharton's all-at-once models treat measurements as constraints on Hamilton's action integral, with solutions emerging "en bloc" rather than evolving forward in time. Price argues that if fundamental laws are CPT-invariant, time-asymmetric causation requires special justification. On this framework, consciousness functions as a constraint — the lightest possible intervention, compatible with the minimality tenet.

Adlam (2022) argues that retrocausality follows naturally from accepting nonlocality without a preferred reference frame. This structural retrocausality — constraint satisfaction across spacetime rather than signals traveling backward — is more coherent than dynamical backward causation and more compatible with the Map's [[philosophy-of-time|growing-block time]] position.

## Why Retrocausal Selection Matters

### Dissolving the Libet Problem

[[libet-experiments|Libet's experiments]] show neural readiness potentials appearing hundreds of milliseconds before conscious awareness of deciding. If consciousness must precede the neural events it causes, this is devastating for mental causation. Retrocausal selection dissolves the problem rather than solving it: if the selection is atemporal, the sequence "neural activity → conscious awareness → action" is fully compatible with genuine conscious causation. The readiness potential is part of the selected package, not a prior cause that renders consciousness epiphenomenal.

Non-retrocausal models must address the Libet timing through other means — arguing the readiness potential is neural noise (Schurger et al., 2012), reinterpreting what counts as the decision moment, or accepting that consciousness acts on present superpositions regardless of what prepared them. Retrocausal selection bypasses all of this.

### Lorentz Invariance

Standard consciousness-collapse models face a relativistic problem: if consciousness collapses a spatially extended wavefunction, this requires a preferred reference frame — simultaneous collapse across space. Retrocausal models, particularly the transactional interpretation, are naturally Lorentz invariant. The handshake between offer and confirmation waves respects relativity without requiring simultaneity. For a dualist framework that must interface with physics, this is a significant advantage.

### All-or-Nothing Selection

A 2025 experimental result in *Nature Communications* demonstrated that if retrocausal influences operate, they must operate fully — partial retrocausal bias is excluded. For the Map, this strengthens the selection framing: consciousness fully determines which outcome actualizes among available options rather than slightly biasing probabilities. This connects to the phenomenology of choice — decisions present as definite selections, not probability adjustments.

## Comparison with Non-Retrocausal Selection

The Map develops both [[non-retrocausal-conscious-selection-models|non-retrocausal]] and retrocausal pathways for conscious selection without requiring a final choice between them.

| Feature | Non-Retrocausal | Retrocausal |
|---------|----------------|-------------|
| Temporal direction | Forward — acts on present superposition | Atemporal — selects entire transaction |
| Libet problem | Must address through other means | Dissolved |
| Relativistic compatibility | Requires preferred frame or careful treatment | Naturally Lorentz invariant |
| Minimality | One intervention at one moment | Constraint on entire history |
| Macroscopic superposition required? | Yes — critical dependency | No — operates at transaction level |
| Physics status | Uses established frameworks (GRW, CSL, OR) | Depends on minority interpretations (TI, TSVF) |

Non-retrocausal selection has the advantage of using better-established physics and requiring no backward-in-time influence. Retrocausal selection has the advantage of dissolving the Libet problem cleanly and avoiding the macroscopic superposition requirement — the question of whether neural superpositions survive decoherence long enough for consciousness to act on them does not arise if selection is atemporal.

## Open Questions

**Time ontology tension.** The transactional interpretation seems to require the future to already exist (block universe) for confirmation waves to propagate backward. Kastner's PTI partially addresses this — transactions occur in a pre-spacetime realm, so growing-block could describe actualized spacetime while the PST realm remains timeless. Whether this resolution is fully coherent remains under development.

**Neural implementation.** No retrocausal framework specifies how consciousness interfaces with quantum transactions in neural tissue. The transactional interpretation describes physics; where and how consciousness participates in neural transactions is unspecified. This gap is analogous to the implementation gaps in non-retrocausal models.

**Emergent collapse.** The QTSI (Tlalpan) interpretation proposes that collapse emerges from time-symmetric microscopic physics at an amplification threshold. If collapse is emergent rather than fundamental, consciousness could modulate where the threshold is crossed in neural systems — biasing which amplification events occur. This connects retrocausal selection to testable physics but remains speculative.

## Relation to Site Perspective

Retrocausal selection connects to all five tenets.

The [[tenets#^dualism|Dualism tenet]] gains coherence: if consciousness operates in Kastner's pre-spacetime realm or provides Aharonov's post-selection conditions, its non-physical character is built into the framework rather than awkwardly appended to physicalist physics.

The [[tenets#^minimal-quantum-interaction|Minimal Quantum Interaction tenet]] is satisfied differently from non-retrocausal models. Constraint selection (Wharton/Price) may represent the lightest possible intervention — consciousness as a boundary condition rather than a force. The 2025 all-or-nothing result means "minimal" refers to the type of interaction (selection among pre-existing possibilities), not its scope.

The [[tenets#^bidirectional-interaction|Bidirectional Interaction tenet]] receives its strongest support from retrocausal selection. The Libet timing objection — the most pointed empirical challenge to bidirectional interaction — dissolves entirely under atemporal selection. Consciousness can genuinely cause physical outcomes even when awareness follows neural preparation in linear time.

The [[tenets#^no-many-worlds|No Many Worlds tenet]] is directly supported. The transactional interpretation produces one actual history through one handshake; retrocausal selection determines which history becomes real rather than generating parallel branches.

The [[tenets#^occams-limits|Occam's Razor Has Limits tenet]] applies straightforwardly. Retrocausality seems exotic only from a classical perspective. Quantum mechanics' fundamental equations are time-symmetric; the appearance that causes must precede effects may reflect cognitive habit rather than physical structure.
