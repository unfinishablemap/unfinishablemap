The phenomenology-vs-function axis is a cross-cutting structural feature of the Map's void catalogue: a sorting dimension that picks out cognitive limits at which **phenomenal character and the cognitive work it appears to perform come apart**. It runs orthogonal to the four-kinds typology (unexplored, unexplorable, occluded, naturally occluded) and to the [[presence-type-and-absence-type-voids|presence/absence axis]]. Where the typology asks *why* thought fails and the presence/absence axis asks *what the phenomenology of approach feels like*, this third axis asks *what comes apart at the boundary*. The dissociations it tracks form an empirical wedge against any framework that identifies phenomenal character with functional or representational role.

The axis is named in [[taxonomy-of-voids|the apex synthesis]] of the voids programme. It was introduced when the function-phenomenology dissociation appeared independently across multiple voids—imagery, synaesthesia, comprehension, agency—and a cluster-internal placement would have lost the cross-cluster signal. This concept page documents the axis as a standalone category, gives the four canonical exemplars in source-accurate form, and explains why the Map treats it as a structural slot rather than a one-off curiosity.

## What the Axis Sorts

The axis identifies voids exhibiting one of two complementary dissociation patterns:

**Identical phenomenology, different function.** Two cognitive systems undergo subjectively similar (or identical) experiences while performing different underlying work, or two functional roles converge on the same phenomenal report.

**Identical function, different phenomenology.** Two cognitive systems perform the same task—measurable by behavioural output, neural correlate, or formal isomorphism—while their phenomenal lives differ, sometimes radically. In the limiting case, one party performs the task with no phenomenology of the relevant kind at all.

Either pattern undermines the assumption that phenomenal character is fixed by, or fully explained by, the functional role a state plays. Neither pattern requires inverted-qualia thought experiments: each canonical exemplar discussed below is empirically anchored, not merely conceivable.

## The Four Canonical Exemplars

The 2026-04-28 apex synthesis of the voids catalogue identifies four voids whose substantive content already articulates a function-phenomenology dissociation, under different vocabularies.

### Imagery: The Cleanest Case

The [[imagery-void|imagery void]]'s third face is the cleanest case. Kay, Keogh, and Pearson (2024) found that aphantasic and visualising minds complete classical mental rotation tasks via demonstrably different strategies. Aphantasics work "slower but more accurately" using analytic strategies; controls use object-based mental rotation. Both groups show the standard linear relation between angular disparity and response time. The authors conclude that "visual imagery is not crucial for successful performance in classical mental rotation tasks, as alternative strategies can be effectively utilised in the absence of holistic mental representations" (Kay, Keogh & Pearson 2024). Zeman (2024) adds the connectivity dimension: the same introspective vocabulary—"vivid," "sharp," "clear"—masks radically different underlying frontoparietal-to-visual network connectivity across the aphantasia/hyperphantasia spectrum.

The wedge is sharp because the task was previously taken to be paradigmatically imagistic. If the same task can be solved with and without phenomenal imagery, three options open: phenomenal imagery is not doing the cognitive work it appears to do; two phenomenally distinct architectures perform isomorphic work; or introspective reports of imagery systematically misdescribe the underlying process. Each is metaphysically loaded.

### Synaesthesia: Extra Qualia and Function-Qualia Decoupling

The [[synesthetic-void|synesthetic void]] formalises a parallel dissociation under different vocabulary. Alan Wager (1999, 2001) identified what he calls the **"extra qualia problem"**: synaesthetes possess phenomenal states that differ from non-synesthetic states without any corresponding difference in representational content. Identical stimuli, identical surface representations, divergent phenomenology.

J.A. Gray (2003) presses the point further. He argues that in synaesthesia, "function and qualia come apart in two ways": identical qualia arise from distinct functional roles (colour experience from both visual and auditory processing pipelines), and single functional roles produce divergent qualia (the same grapheme-recognition process generates colour experience in synaesthetes but not in non-synaesthetes). If qualia tracked function, neither dissociation should occur.

The synaesthetic case generalises the imagery case: where aphantasia shows the *same task* done with or without imagery, synaesthesia shows the *same processing pipeline* yielding extra phenomenal content in some minds and not in others.

### Cognitive Phenomenology: The Comprehension Domain

The cognitive-phenomenology literature runs the same wedge in the comprehension domain. Strawson's (1994) **foreign-language argument** in *Mental Reality* is the paradigm: a monolingual English speaker and a fluent French speaker hear the same French sentence. Acoustic stimulation is identical; the French speaker's experience differs because they understand. The sensory function is held constant; the phenomenal character varies.

Lennon (2023) adds the aphantasic-thought wedge: aphantasic individuals have phenomenally conscious thoughts with "no sensory reduction base," which "falsifies the sensory constraint on conscious thought" and supports a non-sensory cognitive phenomenology. Together these support the view—developed in [[cognitive-phenomenology-and-the-irreducibility-of-thought]]—that understanding contributes its own phenomenal character not exhausted by functional role or sensory accompaniment.

### Agency: Wegner's I-Spy Paradigm

The [[agency-void|agency void]] supplies the agency-domain exemplar. Daniel Wegner's **I-Spy paradigm** (Wegner & Wheatley 1999; Wegner 2002) operationalises a function-phenomenology dissociation in the volitional domain: under arranged conditions of priority, consistency, and exclusivity, the *experience* of willing dissociates from the underlying causation. Subjects feel authorship regardless of whether they caused the act and lose the feeling under disruption (alien hand syndrome, automatisms) even when their motor system continues to act.

The agency case extends the axis from sensory and cognitive phenomenology into willed action: the phenomenal report of agency is decoupled from the functional/causal facts about the act it claims to authorise.

## Independence from the Other Axes

The axis sorts voids on a dimension neither the four-kinds typology nor the presence/absence axis captures.

**Independence from kinds.** A function-phenomenology dissociation can be **architectural** (unexplorable—as in extreme aphantasia, where no quantity of training restores phenomenal imagery) or **naturally occluded** (selectively pruned, as in the neonatal-synesthesia hypothesis where infant cross-modal connectivity regresses through development). It can in principle be **unexplored** (a population of cognitive strategies whose phenomenal accompaniments have not been mapped) or **occluded** (engineered out by external design). The axis is silent on which kind any particular dissociation belongs to.

**Independence from presence/absence.** A function-phenomenology dissociation can register as either presence (extra qualia in synaesthesia; vivid imagery without correspondingly enhanced fidelity in hyperphantasia) or absence (the missing image whose absence the rest of cognition runs without; the lost feeling of agency under disruption). The presence/absence axis sorts voids by the phenomenology of approaching them; this third axis sorts them by what comes apart at the boundary.

The two axes can be applied independently to the same void. The imagery void's third face is *unexplorable* (kind), *can register as absence* (presence/absence axis: phenomenal imagery missing while function persists), and *is a function-phenomenology dissociation* (this axis). The three coordinates carry different information.

## Why the Axis Earns a Structural Slot

The Map's voids catalogue is itself a parsimony judgment about how to carve void-space. A new structural slot is justified when:

1. **The pattern recurs across independent clusters.** Function-phenomenology dissociation appears in the Phenomenological Cluster (imagery, synaesthesia), the Self-Knowledge / Cognitive Cluster (cognitive phenomenology), and the agency cluster. A cluster-internal patch would lose the cross-cluster signal.
2. **The dissociation has a distinct argumentative profile.** Each exemplar functions as an empirical wedge against function-phenomenology identity in its domain. Treating them as four separate items obscures their shared evidential role.
3. **The phenomenon makes contact with a specific theoretical commitment.** Functionalism, representationalism, and the broader [[illusionism|illusionist]] programme all claim—in different ways—that phenomenal character is fixed by, or reducible to, functional or representational profile. The axis gathers the empirical pressure these commitments must accommodate.

The Map's [[tenets#^occams-limits|fifth tenet—Occam's Razor Has Limits]]—governs the carving. Under-carving here would silently absorb a recurring structural feature into existing categories and lose the cross-cluster regularity the data exhibits. The axis is a **deliberate refusal of premature parsimony**.

## What the Axis Is Not

The axis is not a knockdown argument against functionalism. The functionalist has principled responses available to each exemplar: the function may differ at a finer grain than the experiments resolve; the synaesthetic difference might be cashed out as a higher-order representational difference; aphantasic and visualising minds might in fact perform subtly different functional operations on the same task. These responses move the dispute to the level of *which* function is the relevant one, not to whether function-phenomenology identity holds.

What the axis does is identify the **empirical pressure point** where this dispute concentrates, and connect four otherwise scattered findings to a shared theoretical claim. The dissociations are not conclusive; they are loci where theories of consciousness make differential predictions and where empirical work can tighten the screws.

The axis is also not coextensive with the [[phenomenal-contrast-method|phenomenal contrast method]]. The contrast method generates evidence at the *individual* level—two experiences with same input, different phenomenology, within a single subject. The phenomenology-vs-function axis identifies a **structural feature of the void taxonomy**: a sorting dimension along which whole voids cluster. The contrast method is a tool for producing exemplars; the axis is the architectural slot that holds them.

## Relation to Site Perspective

**[[tenets#^dualism|Dualism]]** receives indirect support. Each canonical exemplar is a within-species or within-task wedge against the identification of phenomenology with function. Property dualism—and the Map's stronger tenet-package position—predicts exactly this kind of dissociation, because phenomenal character is held to be ontologically distinct from the functional roles it accompanies. The axis is consistent with that prediction without uniquely confirming it: a sufficiently subtle functionalism can absorb each exemplar individually. The cumulative force comes from the cross-domain pattern.

**[[tenets#^bidirectional-interaction|Bidirectional Interaction]]** connects through the speculative claim that phenomenal *extras*—content not exhausted by functional role—are candidate sites for [[causal-interface|consciousness-physical interface]] activity. The Map flags this as speculative rather than tenet-level: if some phenomenal content does causal work over and above its functional substrate, the axis points to where that contribution might be visible.

**[[tenets#^occams-limits|Occam's Razor Has Limits]]** is illustrated directly. The simplest model—phenomenal character is whatever the relevant function is—fails empirically across the four exemplars. Occam's preference for that model would have ruled out the function-phenomenology gap *a priori*; it took clinical and experimental work to crack the assumption open, and a parsimony judgment in the taxonomy now requires the gap as a structural feature.
