*Disguised property dualism* is a diagnostic the Map applies to physicalist theories of consciousness that, under pressure from the [[hard-problem-of-consciousness|hard problem]], adopt structures that do property-dualist work while keeping a monist label. The pattern is recognisable: the theory acknowledges two irreducible descriptions or aspects of a system, refuses to derive one from the other, but insists the duality is merely *epistemic* or *aspectual* rather than ontological. The label "monism" is preserved by promissory note. Naming the move matters because it recurs whenever a physicalist refinement tries to honour the explanatory gap without conceding [[dualism]] — and recognising the move clarifies what genuine [[substance-property-dualism|property dualism]] adds beyond its disguised cousins.

## The Diagnostic

A theory is *disguised property dualism* when it satisfies three conditions:

1. **Two irreducible structures.** The theory introduces two descriptions, geometries, aspects, or property-types that it cannot reduce to one another. Examples include extrinsic versus intrinsic information geometry, structural versus quidditative properties, or extrinsic causal profile versus intrinsic phenomenal axiom.

2. **A monist disavowal.** The theory explicitly rejects the dualist label, usually by asserting that the two structures are "mutually reductive," "two aspects of one underlying reality," or "two descriptions of one substance."

3. **No reduction is shown.** The asserted unification is not demonstrated. "Showing" here admits three recognisable forms, any one of which would satisfy the condition:

   - **Logical derivation.** The second description is deducible from the first plus the concepts involved — the route type-A physicalism claims is available and type-B physicalism concedes is not, once the explanatory gap is granted.
   - **Empirical identity grounded in an explanatory bridge.** The two descriptions pick out the same thing because an underlying mechanism explains their systematic covariance, as *water = H₂O* is grounded in molecular geometry explaining viscosity, surface tension, and boiling point. The *a posteriori* identity earns its keep via the explanations it unlocks — the Nagelian bridge-law pattern — rather than by bare assertion.
   - **A progressive research programme exhibiting accumulating unification.** Not a completed reduction, but a track record of the two descriptions becoming more tightly linked as investigation proceeds: new bridge principles specified, anomalies resolved, novel predictions confirmed. The absence of a completed reduction does not condemn an active programme, but the *absence of progressive unification* — bare claims of identity that generate no new empirical or conceptual traction — does. The most demanding current instance of this third standard is the active-inference "beautiful loop" account (Laukkonen, Friston & Chandaria 2025), which — unlike the earlier formulations behind the worked examples below — advances a progressive, self-referential theory of consciousness rather than a bare identity claim; [[predictive-processing-and-dualism]] engages it as the strongest physicalist rival and marks the residual disagreement as a framework boundary rather than a reduction the Map can force.

   A promissory note that future analysis will deliver one of these, unaccompanied by any current instantiation, does not satisfy the condition; nor does a bare identity claim stated without explanatory bridge or programmatic track record. The diagnostic is therefore stance-sensitive and heuristic: reasonable readers may disagree about whether a given programme has accumulated enough progressive unification to exit condition (3), and the Map does not pretend otherwise. The tool sharpens a rhetorical pattern — the combination of two-structure architecture with monist disavowal carried by a promise about reduction — rather than delivering a metaphysical verdict. Its utility is philosophical hygiene, not demonstration.

When all three hold, the theory is doing the philosophical work of property dualism while declining its name. The two-structure architecture carries the explanatory load; the monist label carries the reputational cost of dualism without paying its conceptual rent.

## Three Worked Examples

### Friston's Markovian Monism

Friston, Wiese, and Hobson (2020) argue that systems with Markov blankets possess a "dual information geometry": an *extrinsic* geometry (beliefs about the world) and an *intrinsic* geometry (information about the system's own future states). Their framing is emphatically monist: Markovian monism posits only one type of thing and one type of irreducible property, with the two geometries as intertranslatable aspects of a single information-geometric reality. That is a dual-*aspect* claim about one property, not a smuggled second property-type, and reading it as covert dualism would attribute to the authors a conclusion they explicitly reject. The diagnostic bite is narrower: what Markovian monism has not shown is why the intrinsic geometry — self-information about a system's own states — is *felt* rather than merely tracked. Formal intertranslatability between the two geometries is a mathematical fact that does not explain why either aspect is accompanied by experience. The treatment in [[predictive-processing-and-dualism]] keeps this conditional: acknowledging two irreducible *descriptions* while insisting on one *ontology* "looks less like a completed reduction and more like a promissory note," but *if* the two geometries are taken to account for genuinely distinct properties, physical and mental, the disguised-dualism reading applies, whereas if they are one property under two formalisms, the position is better described as an incomplete theory of consciousness that over-interprets a useful dual description. Which reading holds is a framework-boundary disagreement the Map notes, not a reduction it can force from inside Friston's own commitments. Beni (2021) sharpens the point: we "could not read off metaphysical theses about the nature of target systems" — the self-organising conscious systems themselves — "from our theories of ... scientific models (Markov blankets)." The mathematics constrains processing but not the metaphysics of the processor.

### Russellian Monism

[[russellian-monism|Russellian monism]] holds that physics describes only the relational or dispositional properties of matter; the *intrinsic* nature of physical entities — their quiddities — grounds these dispositions and may be (or constitute) consciousness. The framework explicitly rejects substance dualism while introducing two irreducible property-types: structural-relational and quidditative-intrinsic. Pautz (2015, 2017) argues that Russellian monism requires exactly the same "swarm of extra-logical principles" as dualism — brute laws specifying which quiddity configurations yield which experiences. Cutter's (2019) instability argument is conditional: those who reject physicalism on the strength of the standard epistemic arguments (conceivability, the knowledge argument) should, by parity of reasoning, reject Russellian monism and march on to dualism. It does not show that every Russellian monist collapses into a dualist; it presses those who reached the position by anti-physicalist routes. Alter and Pereboom (2023) survey the resulting fragmentation — physicalist, Russellian, and dual-aspect variants sharing only the structuralist premise — itself evidence that the "monist" label spans positions pulling in different metaphysical directions. Within that conditional scope the diagnostic holds: the structural-quidditative duality carries the explanatory work while the monist label preserves the modal claim that the linking principles are necessary rather than contingent. Whether that modal difference is one the evidence could ever bear on is exactly what divides the camps, and the Map does not claim to have settled it.

### IIT's Intrinsic Axiom

Integrated Information Theory, in its current 4.0 formulation (Albantakis et al. 2023), holds that an experience *is* a maximally irreducible cause–effect structure (a Φ-structure) specified by a physical substrate from its own intrinsic perspective. IIT 4.0 frames this as an *explanatory identity*: it infers the properties a substrate must have (postulates) from the essential properties of experience (axioms) and identifies experience with the structure, aiming to say both why experience is present and why a given experience feels as it does. Yet IIT still distinguishes the *extrinsic* causal profile an external observer measures from the *intrinsic* character the same structure is said to constitute, and the identity between them is posited, not derived from anything deeper. Whether this counts as disguised property dualism turns on whether that distinction marks two structures or one: IIT insists on one and takes the identity as explanatory rather than brute. The Map's complaint is that an asserted identity between an extrinsically specifiable structure and intrinsic felt character does no more explanatory work than the bridge the hard problem demands — but whether that verdict lands, or IIT's explanatory-identity claim is granted, is a framework-boundary disagreement rather than a reduction the diagnostic can compel. The theory's live objections now press from within the science too: the unfolding argument (Doerig et al. 2019) contends that any such cause–effect-structure identity is either empirically false or untestable.

## What the Diagnostic Distinguishes

### From genuine physicalism

Type-A physicalism — the view that there is no explanatory gap, only a cognitive illusion that one exists — is not disguised property dualism. It denies condition (1): there is one structure, not two. The dispute with type-A physicalism is whether the gap is real, not whether the theorist has hidden a dualism.

Type-B physicalism — which accepts the gap but holds that phenomenal and physical states are identical *a posteriori*, as water is identical to H₂O — is the diagnostic's hardest case, and its strongest form, the phenomenal-concept strategy, deserves direct engagement rather than a concession in passing. That strategy holds reality monistic while our *concepts* are dual: one physical property grasped under a physical concept and, separately, under a phenomenal concept, as "heat" and "mean molecular motion" pick out one property under two modes of presentation. If that is the whole of the view it escapes the diagnostic cleanly — one structure, two modes of access, no second structure doing hidden work — and the Map does not pretend to refute it here; the disagreement is at the framework boundary, over whether an epistemic gap between concepts licenses any ontological conclusion at all, and honest diagnosis marks it as such. What the diagnostic targets is the point where the strategy stops being purely conceptual. Press the account of what phenomenal concepts *are* and how a physical system deploys one, and it tends to reintroduce a distinctively phenomenal feature — the quale as presented — that the concept latches onto; Chalmers's (2007) dilemma makes the tension explicit, since either phenomenal concepts are physically explicable, and cannot then explain our special epistemic situation, or they are not, and physicalism is conceded. Where that recovered feature carries explanatory weight while identity is asserted rather than exhibited, a second structure has re-entered under a conceptual disguise and condition (3) applies — not to the general *a posteriori* identity strategy, and not to the pure two-concepts view, which the diagnostic leaves standing.

### From neutral monism and dual-aspect monism

[[neutral-monism|Neutral monism]] and dual-aspect monism offer a distinct rejoinder: such views are ontologically monist by construction. A single neutral base has mental and physical characterisations as two aspects of one thing, so — the objection runs — there is no second structure for the diagnostic to find and condition (1) is never met. Friston, Wiese, and Hobson press a version of this directly, considering and rejecting a dual-aspect reading of their own dual geometry, and Beni (2021) notes that Markovian monism "meshes more nicely with neutral monism" than with reductive materialism.

The diagnostic does not dispute the monist self-description as a stipulation; it asks what the neutral base does. Where the base is characterised independently of both aspects — a genuine *tertium quid* from which the mental and physical are derived with an explanatory bridge — condition (3) is satisfiable and the view exits the diagnostic honestly; that is real neutral monism, and the Map has no quarrel with the label. What draws the diagnostic's fire is the version where the "neutral" base is specified only through the two aspects it is meant to unify — named as neutral but never described in terms neutral between the mental and the physical. There the monism rides on the bare stipulation that the base is one while all the descriptive content lives in the two aspects, and condition (3) applies as before, because the base contributes no independent structure that would derive one aspect from the other. Against a genuinely neutral base the diagnostic does not bite, and saying so is part of the discipline: whether such a base can be specified at all — or whether every candidate collapses back into one of the aspects it was meant to stand between — runs nearer bedrock than the diagnostic can settle. Beni's own verdict is instructive, since he declines to endorse the neutral-monist landing spot and holds that no metaphysical thesis can be read off the models at all — a scepticism cutting against the neutral-monist reading as much as the dualist one.

### From overt property dualism

The Map's [[bi-aspectual-ontology|bi-aspectual dualism]] satisfies condition (1) and rejects condition (2). It accepts that the two-structure architecture is genuinely dual and pays the cost openly: it commits to brute psychophysical linking principles, accepts the [[interactionist-dualism|interaction problem]] explicitly, and proposes a quantum interface as the *candidate locus* of interaction — a posited function, not a delivered mechanism. The distinction is part of the honesty. As [[bi-aspectual-ontology]] and [[psychophysical-laws]] state plainly, "actuality" names the role of making one possibility real, but how consciousness discharges that role — the phenomenal→physical mapping — remains unsolved; the quantum interface is where the Map proposes to look for the coupling, not a coupling it has exhibited. Naming the position is part of the methodological discipline. The complaint against the disguised variants is not that they introduce two structures — that may be unavoidable — but that they refuse to acknowledge the cost, whereas the Map keeps a standing ledger of its own unpaid explanatory debts.

### How the diagnostic applies to the Map's own view

A fair reading should turn the diagnostic on the Map itself. The Map's bi-aspectual ontology introduces two structures — mental and physical aspects — and exhibits no reduction between them. At the surface of the three conditions, (1) and arguably (3) appear to hold; only (2), the monist disavowal, is rejected. If labelling were the only difference, the charge of "mere labelling" would land, and the Map would be guilty in reverse of the move it diagnoses in others.

The substantive difference is what each position *claims* about reduction. Condition (3), read carefully, concerns the *asserted* unification not being demonstrated. A theory that asserts no unification does not fail the condition; the condition is vacuous for it.

- **The disguised variants assert reducibility without exhibiting it under any of the three standards.** Markovian monism claims each geometry is "ultimately reducible" to the other but offers no logical derivation, no mechanism explaining their covariance beyond formal stipulation, and no progressive track record tightening the link. Russellian monism's structural-quiddity distinction by construction blocks both logical derivation and explanatory bridging (the quiddities are defined as inaccessible to physics); Pautz's "swarm of extra-logical principles" is precisely the point that the third standard — progressive unification — cannot rescue what the first two foreclose. IIT's identity claim between intrinsic phenomenality and integrated information is stipulated axiomatically rather than derived from deeper structure. Each is a positive claim that a reduction exists, advanced without any of the three forms of "showing" that would make it intelligible — and the diagnostic bites on that gap.
- **The Map makes no such claim.** Bi-aspectual dualism commits to brute [[psychophysical-laws|psychophysical linking principles]]. The two-structure ontology is proposed as load-bearing, not transitional. There is no promissory note to cash, because no promise was issued.

The [[tenets#Minimal Quantum Interaction|minimal quantum interaction]] tenet might be misread as a back-door reduction. It is not. The interaction-mechanism question and the reduction question pull in opposite directions: a successful reduction would eliminate the need for any mechanism, while a successful mechanism presupposes the two-structure ontology it links. Chemistry is not "disguised vitalism" because it lacks a complete reduction to quantum electrodynamics — it is a two-level theory whose inter-level mechanism remains under active investigation. The quantum interface is the Map's proposed — not delivered — mechanism for such coupling, still under investigation, not a hidden route back to a single structure.

Even so, a residual concession is worth stating plainly. A critic who rejects the claim-versus-vacuous-condition distinction between the Map and the disguised variants will reasonably count the tool as a scoring device rather than a metaphysical discovery. The diagnostic does not prove that the disguised variants are metaphysically equivalent to dualism; it shows that their parsimony advantage is thinner than advertised and that their reputational distance from dualism is carried by a promise about reduction, not a demonstration. Applied to the Map, the diagnostic does not bite on the same ground it bites the disguised variants — condition (3) is vacuous for a theory that asserts no unification. But candour is not payment: declaring the brute linking principles and the interface-mechanism to be unpaid debts does not discharge them, and the asymmetry defended here — asserting no reduction versus asserting one undelivered — is a real but permanently contestable difference, not a clean bill of health. A critic may always hold that a theory carrying two irreducible structures owes an account of their relation whether or not it advertises a reduction. That the Map states its debts rather than promising to retire them is the whole of the defence, and a reader who finds it too thin is disagreeing in good faith.

### From eliminativism

[[illusionism|Illusionism]] denies that the second structure exists at all: there are no qualia, only functional states that report themselves as qualitative. This denies condition (1) by collapsing the duality from the other end. Whether illusionism succeeds is a separate question; it is at least an honest single-aspect view rather than a covert dualism.

## Why the Pattern Recurs

The pattern recurs because the hard problem creates a stable rhetorical pressure. Physicalist research programmes face two costs: denying the gap (which strains pre-theoretic phenomenology) or accepting it (which courts dualism). Disguised property dualism tries to pay neither cost: it acknowledges the gap by introducing a second structure, then refuses dualism by asserting the structures are aspects of one thing. The move is rhetorically efficient — it preserves the physicalist label while honouring the explanatory gap — but philosophically unstable. The promissory note that the two structures will collapse into one is rarely cashed. Cashing it would require exhibiting the reduction in one of the three forms specified above: logical derivation, an explanatory bridge grounding an *a posteriori* identity, or a progressive track record of unification — not merely asserting that future science will provide one.

The diagnostic is most useful when applied generously rather than punitively. Markovian monism, Russellian monism, and some IIT framings are serious attempts to thread a real philosophical needle; the diagnosis is not that they are disingenuous but that the threading does not succeed without conceding more than the framework wishes to concede.

## Relation to Site Perspective

**[[tenets#^dualism|Dualism]]**: The diagnostic clarifies what substantive dualism adds beyond the disguised variants. The Map's position is not that dualism introduces a structure the disguised variants lack, but that it accepts the structure they introduce without disavowal. Recognising disguised property dualism makes visible how much physicalist refinement under hard-problem pressure already concedes — and how small the further step to overt dualism actually is.

**[[tenets#^occams-limits|Occam's Razor Has Limits]]**: The disguised variants typically claim a parsimony advantage over dualism (one substance, one ontology). The diagnostic exposes this advantage as labelling rather than ontology: a theory that posits two irreducible structures is not made simpler by calling itself monist. As [[parsimony-epistemology|parsimony epistemology]] argues, simplicity counts only when competing accounts explain the same data; the disguised monisms and explicit dualism do not differ in their structural commitments, only in their willingness to name them.
