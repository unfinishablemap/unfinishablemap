Reflexive methodology is The Unfinishable Map's recurring argumentative move: take a *rival* framework's own criterion of success and run the rival through it, showing that by its own lights the rival either fails or is driven toward a dualist or deflationary conclusion. The Map does not defeat materialism, pragmatism, or Russellian monism by importing dualist premises the opponent rejects. It measures each rival against the standard that rival itself avows — and lets the standard do the work.

This is worth naming as a method because the same shape recurs across the corpus with different engines inside it. Pragmatism is judged by its own "what works" test and fails it; the causal-closure argument is judged by its own demand for a non-circular formulation and cannot supply one; Russellian monism is judged by the very anti-physicalist arguments that motivate it and is destabilised by them. One move, four theatres.

## Distinct from Self-Stultification

Reflexive methodology is often mistaken for [[self-stultification]], and the two must be kept apart because they operate at different logical grains.

Self-stultification is a *property of a position* — a one-place predicate on a belief. A thesis is self-stultifying when its own truth undercuts the rational grounds for holding it: [[concepts/epiphenomenalism|epiphenomenalism]], if true, severs the causal link between consciousness and the reports that would justify believing it, so it "may be true but cannot be rationally held." The predicate attaches to a single claim, considered on its own.

Reflexive methodology is a *two-place relation* — a method applied to an opponent. It takes a rival's professed criterion and turns that criterion back on the rival. Self-stultification is one of the tools it can deploy (when the rival's own standard is *rational justification*, turning that standard on the rival exposes self-defeat), but the method is not exhausted by it. Where the rival's avowed standard is practical fruitfulness, or non-circular formulability, or resistance to a conceivability argument, the reflexive move runs through *that* standard instead, and self-stultification never enters. The property is one thing an argument can find; the method is the procedure that goes looking.

## The Argument-Forms It Draws On

Philosophy has a rich, named toolkit for arguments that turn a framework against itself. The Map's move is a synthesis of several; naming them precisely sharpens each instance and pre-empts the standard objections. Five forms matter here, summarised first and then deployed below.

- **Transcendental arguments** (Kant, Strawson): X is a necessary condition for the possibility of some Y the opponent already grants; since Y holds, the opponent is committed to X. The argument starts from conceded ground and derives what the opponent denies.
- **Dialectical self-refutation** (Castagnoli): the ancient variety shows that someone "cannot coherently or consistently maintain" a thesis they publicly propose. It targets *maintainability*, not truth — a weaker, more defensible claim, and the Map's preferred reading.
- **Logical self-refutation** (Mackie): the modern variety shows that a thesis logically entails its own contradictory and hence is false. Mackie's formal taxonomy separates *pragmatic* self-refutation (asserting the thesis is inconsistent with the act of asserting) from *absolute* (the thesis entails its own falsity) and *operational* varieties.
- **Retorsion / performative contradiction** (Apel, Habermas): the *act* of asserting a thesis presupposes what the thesis denies. The sceptic who asserts "there is no universal claim" makes a universal claim.
- **Immanent critique** (Hegel, Marx, Adorno): judge a framework by its own avowed norms rather than alien ones, and let a contentful (determinate) negation yield a positive successor rather than mere scepticism.

The Map's move sits closest to **immanent critique** combined with **dialectical self-refutation**. Immanent critique captures the method's signature — the Map does not impose simplicity or dualism from outside but measures rivals against their own professed standards. Hegel's *determinate negation* explains why the result is constructive: turning a rival against itself lands on a positive dualist successor, not open-ended doubt. Dialectical self-refutation calibrates the strength of the claim: the Map usually shows a rival cannot be *consistently maintained* by its own criteria, which is safer than claiming the rival is outright false. Where the rival's own practice — reasoning, evaluating, reporting — presupposes the consciousness it would deflate, the move becomes **retorsive** as well.

## Four Canonical Instances

### Pragmatism — Immanent Critique

[[pragmatism|Pragmatism]] evaluates ideas by their practical consequences: what works. The Map runs this criterion on the pragmatist's own materialism. Evaluation is itself a conscious activity — the felt sense that one framework illuminates while another obscures is what gives pragmatic assessment its normative force. Reduce evaluation to physical mechanism and "what works" collapses into "what merely happens to occur," because there is no longer any standpoint from which to judge that a problem has been *resolved*. Materialism about consciousness therefore fails pragmatism's *own* test, and the pragmatist criterion, applied consistently, points toward [[interactionist-dualism|dualism]]. This is immanent critique in textbook form: the rival is judged by its avowed norm, and the [[pragmatisms-path-to-dualism|full argument]] shows the norm generating a positive dualist successor rather than mere deadlock.

### Causal Closure — Circularity and Self-Stultification

The [[causal-closure|causal-closure]] argument against dualism confronts a formulation dilemma of its own making. Read strongly enough to exclude mental causation, closure simply presupposes physicalism, so the argument against dualism becomes circular — it assumes what it means to prove. Read weakly enough to avoid begging the question, it no longer excludes mental causation and loses its force. The Map does not smuggle in dualism here; it holds the closure argument to its own requirement for a non-circular, non-vacuous statement and finds none available. Self-stultification then bites the [[concepts/epiphenomenalism|epiphenomenalism]] that strong closure forces: if consciousness causes nothing, beliefs about consciousness are causally disconnected from experience, and the closure theorist's own position cannot be rationally held.

### Russellian Monism — The Instability Recoil

[[russellian-monism|Russellian monism]] is motivated by anti-physicalist arguments — the conceivability of [[philosophical-zombies|zombies]], the knowledge argument — which show that structural-functional duplicates could lack consciousness. Brian Cutter's instability argument turns those very arguments on monism: if structural duplicates could lack consciousness, then quiddity-structural duplicates could equally lack it, so the same reasoning that rejects physicalism recoils on the monist's proposed middle ground. "Russellian monism does not offer a comfortable resting place — one must turn back or else march on to dualism." This is reflexive methodology in its transcendental-recoil mode: the criterion the rival grants to reject physicalism cannot be quarantined, and it carries the rival past its intended stopping point to the Map's dualist endpoint.

### Self-Stultification As Deployed — Retorsion

When the rival's avowed standard is *rational justification itself*, the reflexive move deploys self-stultification, and its root is retorsive. The eliminativist who *asserts* there are no beliefs, the epiphenomenalist who *reports* their conviction, perform acts — asserting, reporting, evaluating evidence — that presuppose the efficacious consciousness their thesis deflates. The [[self-stultification-as-master-argument|master argument]] shows this single structure defeating epiphenomenalism, physicalism, eliminativism, and global scepticism, each severing a different strand connecting consciousness to justification. Here the method borrows the property: reflexive methodology is the procedure, self-stultification the defect it exhibits in the rival.

## Two Guardrails

### Not Tu Quoque

A reflexive argument degrades into a *tu quoque* fallacy if it merely charges the opponent with hypocrisy — "you do it too" — rather than exhibiting a genuine self-undermining structure. The distinction is settled in the literature and the Map cites it rather than re-litigating it: retorsion is legitimate because it targets a real performative inconsistency in the *position*, not the arguer's character or conduct. When the Map says the epiphenomenalist's act of reasoning presupposes efficacious consciousness, the charge is structural, not personal. Keeping the argument aimed at the thesis, never at the theorist, is what separates retorsion from the fallacy.

### Answering Stroud's Belief-versus-Truth Gap

Barry Stroud's 1968 objection is the sharper threat. A reflexive or transcendental argument may establish only what we must *believe*, not what is *true*: it would be enough for language or inquiry to be possible if we merely *believe* that consciousness is efficacious, without its actually being so. If the Map's move only fixes a commitment we cannot shed, it leaves the metaphysical question open.

The Map's reply routes through the [[hard-problem-of-consciousness|hard problem]]. Phenomenal experience is not a belief that could be merely useful to hold — it is a *datum*, the condition under which any description or evaluation occurs at all. The pain, the redness, the felt grasp of why a conclusion follows are present before any theory about them and persist whatever theory wins. Stroud's gap opens between a required belief and an independent reality; it cannot open where the item in question is not a belief about reality but a feature of the reality being described. The reflexive argument does not merely show that we must *believe* in efficacious consciousness — it starts from consciousness as given and asks what any adequate framework must accommodate to remain sayable.

## Relation to Site Perspective

**[[tenets#^dualism|Dualism]]**: Reflexive methodology's payload is always dualist. Every instance lands in the same place — pragmatism's criterion points to dualism, closure's circularity clears the ground for it, monism's instability marches to it, self-stultification establishes the causal efficacy dualism requires. The method is how the Map argues *for* Tenet 1 without begging the question: it never assumes irreducibility, but repeatedly shows that rivals, held to their own standards, cannot stop short of it.

**[[tenets#^occams-limits|Occam's Razor Has Limits]]**: The immanent-critique framing is the direct expression of this tenet. The Map does not defeat simpler rivals by wielding a competing simplicity metric from outside — it shows they fail on their *own* terms. Parsimony that eliminates the standpoint from which parsimony is assessed is not a virtue but a structural flaw, and the reflexive method exposes exactly that internal collapse rather than adjudicating from an external vantage the tenet warns is unreliable under incomplete knowledge.
