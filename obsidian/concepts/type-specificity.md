**Type-specificity** is the structural feature an explanandum has when its character is determinate type-by-type rather than schematic. A reductive account of a type-specific explanandum must deliver *that* character — the particular shape of *this* type — rather than substitute a generic mechanism. Where the explanandum is type-specific and the proposed reduction is not, the appeal to a generic mechanism does not discharge the explanatory demand; it changes the subject.

The concept is methodological, not metaphysical. It does not by itself entail that any particular reduction must fail. It supplies a discipline for evaluating reductive proposals: ask whether the explanans matches the *form* of the explanandum, not merely its magnitude or its functional signature. Where the forms diverge, the proposal owes either a derivation of the form from the explanans or an honest acknowledgement that the generic move does not address the type-specific demand. The Map deploys type-specificity at three grains — metaphysical, phenomenal, and functional — each as an independent exhibit of the same structural pattern. A later section ([explained below](#independence-scoring)) scores the three grains on the four-criterion independence framework borrowed from [[apex/taxonomy-of-voids|the taxonomy of voids]] worked-exhibits programme, testing whether the parallelism is genuine cross-grain independence or framework-internal coherence.

## The Structural Feature {#structural-feature}

A type-specific explanandum has *internal structure that individuates it from other members of its family*. Redness is one phenomenal type, painfulness is another, the taste of coffee is a third. Each is determinate in its character: the redness of red is not the painfulness of pain merely shifted along some intensity dimension. A reductive account that locates redness in some neural firing pattern but is silent on why *that* pattern realises *that* phenomenal type — rather than another type, or no type at all — has not explained redness. It has identified a correlate, leaving the [[explanatory-gap]] intact at the type level.

The contrast is with explananda whose specification is *schematic* — that is, where the explanandum names a role or a placeholder without committing to particular determinate features that the explanation must match. "Vital force" was such a placeholder. It did not pick out any particular feature a reduction had to deliver in any particular form. Any mechanism for any part of the target counted as success: Wöhler's urea synthesis (1828) reduced one part, Buchner's cell-free fermentation (1897) reduced another, the DNA structure (1953) reduced a third. Each reduction shrank the placeholder, because the placeholder had no determinate form a reduction was required to preserve.

Type-specificity is what the placeholder *lacks*. Where the explanandum is "this particular phenomenal type, in its determinate character," not "whatever it is that makes the organism alive," a reduction by generic substitution is structurally unavailable.

## The Vitalism Disanalogy {#vitalism}

The original deployment is in [[the-convergence-argument-for-dualism|the convergence argument for dualism]]: type-specificity is what distinguishes the consciousness case from past reductive successes, including the case of vitalism.

A vitalist in 1820 could have observed accurately that the chemistry-life gap had not yet narrowed. The temporal observation — *no reduction has yet been found* — is symmetric across consciousness and vitalism. The disanalogy is not temporal. It is structural, and it lies in the shape of the explanandum:

> "'Vital force' was a placeholder for unspecified mechanism, picking out no particular feature a reduction had to deliver in any particular form... Phenomenal qualities have a different shape: redness, painfulness, and the taste of coffee are each determinate phenomenal types. A reductive account must deliver *that* character type-by-type, and a complete reduction of one type would not extend automatically to others." ([[the-convergence-argument-for-dualism]], §What Convergence Does Not Prove)

This is the metaphysical-grain deployment. The disanalogy does not claim that phenomenal qualities are *unreducible* — that would be the convergence argument's conclusion, and using it here would be circular. The disanalogy claims something weaker and earlier: that the *form of the demand* placed on any reduction is type-specific, in a way the vitalist's demand was not. The historical pattern of reductive successes — fermentation, heredity, metabolism — does not extend automatically across this structural divide.

The disanalogy is not unlimited. Type-specific reductive demands could in principle be met. A future neuroscience that delivered "this is what redness *is* in physical terms" analogous to "this is what fermentation *is*" would discharge the demand at one type, and the discipline of [[reductionism-and-consciousness|reductionism-and-consciousness]] would have to consider whether the discharge extends. Type-specificity names a structural feature of the explanandum, not a verdict on whether the explanandum can be addressed.

## Type-Specificity at the Unity Grain {#unity-grain}

The phenomenal-grain deployment is in [[the-binding-problem|the binding problem]]. The article catalogues five varieties of binding — intra-modal, cross-modal, temporal, cognitive, subject — and observes that each presents the same shared structure: physical processes are distributed, neural mechanisms coordinate them, experience is unified, and the gap between coordination and unity resists explanation.

The structural argument is type-specific. Each variety presents *its own* unity-type: the unity of a perceived scene is not the unity of a remembered episode shifted in modality, and neither is the unity of a self that owns both. Intra-modal feature binding (redness with roundness with motion), cross-modal binding (incommensurable codes resolving into the unity of place), temporal binding (the specious present, the heard melody as arc), cognitive binding (the "click" when a complex argument coheres), and subject binding (the perspective from which any of the above is experienced) are five determinate unity-types whose character a reduction must match.

The article makes the type-specific demand explicit:

> "Any successful explanation must work across all five varieties: a theory that explains perceptual binding but cannot address temporal or subject binding has explained BP1, not BP2." ([[the-binding-problem]], §The Shared Structure)

The persistence of the gap across all five varieties is the load-bearing observation. The persistence is what would not be expected if a generic mechanism were available, because a generic mechanism — synchrony, integration, broadcast — would discharge all five demands at once by addressing whatever they had in common. The varieties do not have a common type-shape; they have a common *structural pattern* (coordination-to-unity) at five distinct grains. A generic mechanism for the pattern does not address any of the five types as such.

This is the same discipline as the vitalism disanalogy, applied at the phenomenal grain. The shape of the demand — type-by-type across five varieties — is what places the constraint on what would count as a solution, derivable from the form of the explanandum rather than from any verdict about whether the explanandum is irreducible.

## Type-Specificity at the Capacity Grain {#capacity-grain}

The functional-grain deployment is in [[baseline-cognition|baseline cognition]]. The article catalogues consciousness-dependent functional capacities — durable working-memory maintenance, novel combinations of operations, declarative metacognition, counterfactual thinking, recursive mindreading, cumulative culture — and argues that the human-ape gap systematically tracks these specific capacities rather than tracking cognition generically.

The argument is type-specific in the same structural sense. Durable maintenance is a different capacity-type from novel combinations, which is a different capacity-type from declarative metacognition. Each is individuated by its determinate character — what it *is* to hold an item across an interval, what it *is* to chain procedures novelly, what it *is* to represent a knowledge state as a knowledge state. A theory that explained the human-ape gap as a generic increase in *cognitive resource* — more memory, more computation, more associations — would be a generic-mechanism appeal at the capacity grain. It would not address why the gap tracks *these* capacities and not others equally resource-intensive.

The baseline-cognition framework's central inference is structurally type-specific: the systematic correspondence between consciousness-dependent capacities and the human-ape gap is itself the explanandum. A generic resource-expansion account cannot deliver the correspondence because it does not predict the type-by-type pattern; it predicts a generalised performance shift. The pattern is what would be expected if consciousness amplifies cognition through *specific* operations (durable maintenance, novel combinations, spontaneous intentional action) rather than through a generic boost.

The discipline operates here in the same way as at the other two grains: the form of the explanandum (capacity-by-capacity correspondence) constrains what would count as an explanans (a capacity-by-capacity mechanism), and a generic-mechanism appeal at the wrong grain does not discharge the demand.

## Independence Scoring of the Three Grains {#independence-scoring}

The three preceding sections present the three deployments of type-specificity as *parallel applications* of the same discipline. The parallelism is the article's central methodological claim: that one structural feature shows up at metaphysical, phenomenal-unity, and capacity grains, and that the disciplined response is the same in each case. The temptation that lives next door to this claim is to count the three deployments as three independent confirmations of the discipline's load-bearing force — the sort of cluster-coherence move [[evidential-status-discipline|the evidential-status discipline]] names as a standing temptation across the catalogue and the [[common-cause-null|common-cause null]] discipline names at the structural level. If the three grains share enough framework-resources, the three-fold count overstates the discipline's evidential weight, and the parallelism is framework-internal coherence rather than framework-independent triangulation.

The scoring discipline that [[apex/taxonomy-of-voids#two-worked-exhibits-in-independence-scoring|the taxonomy of voids]] applies to its two worked exhibits — the [[the-surplus-void|surplus void]] retracted and the [[topics/introspection-architecture-independence-scoring|introspection-architecture sub-cluster]] partially retained — is portable to the meta-argument level. Where the taxonomy scores *void-clusters* on signature specificity, structured persistence, cross-observer convergence, and framework independence (the [[apophatic-cartography-four-criteria|four criteria]]), the same instrument can score *meta-argument deployments* on whether each deployment is genuinely independent of the others. This section applies the framework to the three grains as a third worked exhibit for the catalogue's methodology programme — the first exhibit at the meta-argument register, where the taxonomy's exhibits are at the void-cluster register. The contrast tests whether the four-criterion framework is portable across argument-types or only handles void-clusters.

### Scoring the Vitalism Disanalogy

The metaphysical-grain deployment in [[the-convergence-argument-for-dualism|the convergence argument for dualism]] is the discipline's original site. The scoring asks: is the vitalism disanalogy genuinely independent of the other two grain-deployments, or does it borrow framework-resources from them?

*Signature specificity* is strong. The disanalogy has a distinct shape that the other two deployments do not share: it operates on the *historical pattern* of reductive successes (chemistry of life, atomic theory, molecular genetics) and identifies one structural feature — the schematic-versus-determinate shape of the explanandum — that distinguishes consciousness from those cases. The shape is recognised independently of any view about what the binding problem or capacity-correspondence demand. The disanalogy's positive content is the *form-of-demand* claim, and it would survive even if the binding problem dissolved tomorrow under some unforeseen neural-coordination finding, or if the baseline-cognition framework turned out to be empirically wrong about the human-ape gap.

*Structured persistence* is strong on the long view. The basic structure of the disanalogy — that "vital force" was a placeholder and that phenomenal types are not — has held since the post-Wöhler debates clarified what placeholder reduction had accomplished. Modern articulations of the disanalogy (Chalmers's running distinction between *reductive explanation of structure and function* and *reductive explanation of consciousness*, Levine's explanatory-gap formulation, McGinn's structural-versus-contingent distinction) reach the same shape from different starting points. The shape survives translation across analytic-philosophy-of-mind, philosophy-of-science, and history-of-science vocabulary.

*Cross-observer convergence* is moderate. The disanalogy is most articulated within Western analytic philosophy of mind, and its observers (Chalmers, Levine, McGinn, the convergence argument's own treatment) share methodological commitments. Buddhist Abhidharma's *vijñāna* taxonomy and Sankhya's *guṇa*-individuation of phenomenal types deploy what may be a comparable type-specificity discipline, but the cross-cultural-philosophy channel for the *vitalism disanalogy specifically* is mostly unbuilt — these traditions did not have a vitalism debate of the relevant kind, so the disanalogy's structural feature reaches them only by translation. The historical-tradition channel (pre-1990s philosophy-of-science) is partly built: the schematic-versus-determinate distinction is implicit in late-19th-century debates about reduction in the special sciences, but it was not named at the resolution the contemporary disanalogy requires.

*Framework independence* is mixed. The structural feature the disanalogy identifies (the form-of-demand a reductive account must satisfy) is derivable from the explanandum's shape alone, independently of any verdict on whether the explanandum can be reduced — this is what the *Scope* section of the article calls the non-circularity bound. The structural observation is therefore framework-independent at the *identification* level. The deployment's *evidential force* for the convergence argument is lower on this criterion: that the disanalogy *matters* — that the form-of-demand difference is the load-bearing distinction between vitalism and consciousness, rather than one of many structural differences — is a judgement the convergence argument makes, and the judgement is part of the convergence argument's framework. A reader who rejected the convergence argument could grant the disanalogy as a structural feature while denying that the feature carries the evidential weight the convergence argument places on it.

*Independence-of-deployment.* The vitalism disanalogy does not depend on the binding-problem grain or the capacity grain for its formulation. The form-of-demand claim is statable purely in terms of phenomenal types (redness, painfulness, the taste of coffee) and the schematic-versus-determinate distinction; binding-of-features and capacity-by-capacity correspondence are not required premises. The disanalogy could have been articulated had Revonsuo never catalogued the binding varieties and had the baseline-cognition framework never been formulated. The *converse* dependency — whether the other two deployments depend on the vitalism disanalogy — is examined in the next two scorings.

### Scoring the Binding-Problem Unity Grain

The phenomenal-unity grain deployment in [[the-binding-problem|the binding problem]] is the catalogue's second site. The scoring asks: is the binding-problem unity-grain genuinely third-person-tractable in the way the convergence argument's Cluster 3 requires, or does it inherit phenomenal-intuition Cluster 1 dependencies?

*Signature specificity* is strong. The unity-grain has a distinct shape that neither of the other two deployments shares: it operates on the *five-variety taxonomy* (intra-modal, cross-modal, temporal, cognitive, subject binding) and identifies one structural feature — the coordination-to-unity gap repeating at each variety — that any candidate explanation must address across all five. The positive content of the unity-grain is the *five-fold persistence* claim, and it survives even if the vitalism disanalogy turned out to be misleading at the metaphysical grain, or if the baseline-cognition framework dissolved.

*Structured persistence* is strong on the empirical signature and weaker on the unity-as-explanandum framing. The empirical persistence of the binding question — Treisman's feature-integration theory (1980), Crick and Koch's 40-Hz synchrony proposal (1990), Singer's binding-by-synchrony programme (1995–2010), Tononi's IIT formalisation (2004–present), Tegmark's perceptronium framing (2015) — has held across competing programmes for over forty years. The binding question's empirical content survives translation across cognitive-psychology, neuroscience, mathematical-physics, and integrated-information vocabulary. The framing of unity *as the load-bearing demand* — rather than as one feature among many that any neural-coordination story will eventually account for — is less robustly persistent: it requires the prior framing-decision that phenomenal unity is the explanandum, which Cluster 1 supplies.

*Cross-observer convergence* is mixed. Within cognitive psychology and neuroscience, the binding question converges strongly across competing programmes. The contemplative-tradition channel is partially built: Buddhist Abhidharma's analysis of *manas* as the unifying mental faculty, Yogācāra's account of *ālaya-vijñāna* as the ground for ownership of mental events, and the Theravāda commentarial treatment of *cetasika* coordination all converge on a unity-grain phenomenon at roughly the same grain as the contemporary binding problem. The cross-species channel is weakly built but not absent — recent work on perceptual unity in birds, octopuses, and bees (Mather and Dickel 2017; Klein et al. 2024) treats binding-of-features as a substrate-independent question. The framework-independence channel within philosophy of mind is mixed: functionalists, identity theorists, illusionists, and panpsychists all engage the binding question, but they engage it under different theoretical pressures — for the functionalist, binding is a coordination question; for the illusionist, the unity-explanandum is itself partly illusory; for the panpsychist, the combination problem is binding's deep structure.

*Framework independence* is the criterion the original article most explicitly addresses, and the answer is partial. The convergence argument's Cluster 3 treatment names the binding problem as the prong of the structural-features cluster most robustly *third-person-tractable*: binocular rivalry, attentional blink, and feature-binding deficits can be measured experimentally without taking phenomenal reports as ontological data. That is the binding-question's framework-independent empirical face. The framing of unity *as the load-bearing explanandum* — the move from "feature-binding deficits are measurable" to "phenomenal unity is the demand any theory must address" — is less framework-independent: it depends on prior acceptance that phenomenal unity is what needs explaining, which is a Cluster 1 commitment. Dennett's heterophenomenologist would grant the empirical binding-question and contest the unity-as-explanandum framing. The article's own observation — that Cluster 3's independence from Cluster 1 *needs defence in vocabulary the heterophenomenologist accepts*, and that *the binding-problem prong resists this reduction* — is the load-bearing claim of partial framework-independence: the empirical face is independent; the explanandum-framing face inherits Cluster 1 dependencies it does not fully discharge.

*Independence-of-deployment.* The binding-problem unity-grain does not depend on the vitalism disanalogy for its formulation. The five-variety taxonomy and the coordination-to-unity gap are stated entirely within phenomenology and the neural-coordination literature; the metaphysical-grain claim about schematic-versus-determinate explananda is not a premise the binding-grain requires. The dependency runs in a different direction: the unity-grain *exemplifies* type-specificity at the phenomenal grain, but the type-specificity discipline could be articulated using any of the unity-grain, the capacity-grain, or other phenomenal-type cases (redness, painfulness, taste). The unity-grain is one instance, not the source of the discipline. The capacity-grain is independent from the unity-grain in the same sense.

### Scoring the Baseline-Cognition Capacity Grain

The functional-grain deployment in [[baseline-cognition|baseline cognition]] is the catalogue's third site. The scoring asks: is the baseline-cognition capacity-grain genuinely architecturally distinct from the other two grains, or does it reduce to one of them under sufficiently fine-grained reduction-pressure?

*Signature specificity* is strong. The capacity-grain has a distinct shape neither other deployment shares: it operates on the *human-ape cognitive gap* and identifies one structural feature — the systematic correspondence between consciousness-dependent capacities (durable working-memory maintenance, novel combinations, declarative metacognition, counterfactual thinking, recursive mindreading, cumulative culture) and the gap's location — that any candidate explanation must deliver type-by-type rather than as a generic resource-expansion. The positive content of the capacity-grain is the *capacity-by-capacity correspondence* claim, and it survives even if the vitalism disanalogy and the binding-problem unity-grain dissolved.

*Structured persistence* is strong on the empirical signature for the capacities individually and moderate on the *correspondence framing*. The capacities the framework names have been studied across comparative cognition, developmental psychology, neuroscience, and primatology for forty-plus years (Suddendorf and Corballis on mental time travel, Penn et al. on relational reinterpretation, Tomasello on shared intentionality, Henrich on cumulative culture, Carruthers on dual-process architectures). The capacities are each empirically persistent. The *correspondence framing* — that the gap *tracks these specific capacities and not others equally resource-intensive* — is more recently articulated and depends on the structural shape of the comparative-cognition literature being read in one particular way: that the gap is *real* and *systematic* rather than artefactual, that the consciousness-dependent capacities form a *natural family* rather than a list, and that *resource-expansion* accounts genuinely fail to predict the type-by-type pattern. Each of these is contested within the literature.

*Cross-observer convergence* is mixed. Within Western comparative cognition and developmental psychology, the consciousness-dependent capacities converge moderately across programmes — Suddendorf, Penn, Tomasello, Henrich, and Carruthers do not work in identical frameworks but reach overlapping capacity-lists. The cross-cultural-philosophy channel is partially built: the Yogācāra treatment of *manovijñāna*'s role in novel combinatoriality and the Theravāda commentarial treatment of *paññā*'s role in declarative metacognition converge on a subset of the capacity-list at high resolution. The cross-species channel is the criterion's most controversial face: the framework's central inference (the gap tracks consciousness-dependent capacities) requires that some species lack the capacities in their consciousness-dependent form, and the line between *lacks* and *has a different version of* is contested for great apes, corvids, and cephalopods. The cross-species observers (de Waal on chimpanzee mindreading, Heyes on cultural learning, Carruthers on architecture-substitution) frequently dispute whether a given capacity is genuinely absent or merely differently realised.

*Framework independence* is mixed. The empirical signature (capacities of the types named, with measurable performance gaps between humans and other primates) is framework-independent: it is recorded by researchers across competing theoretical commitments. The *correspondence-as-explanandum* framing — the move from "humans and apes differ on these capacities" to "the differences track *consciousness-dependent* capacities specifically" — is less framework-independent. The Map's framework prioritises consciousness-dependence as the gating criterion; alternative framings (Carruthers's massive modularity, Heyes's cognitive gadgets, Tomasello's shared intentionality without consciousness-gating) reach the same empirical pattern via different theoretical routes and do not need the consciousness-dependence framing to organise the data. The capacity-grain's evidential force for type-specificity therefore depends partly on the prior acceptance that consciousness-dependence is the gating dimension, which is a framework-internal commitment.

*Independence-of-deployment.* The capacity-grain does not depend on the vitalism disanalogy or the binding-problem unity-grain for its formulation. The systematic-correspondence claim is statable entirely within comparative cognition and the consciousness-amplification literature; neither metaphysical-grain placeholder-versus-determinate distinctions nor phenomenal-unity grain coordination-to-unity gaps are required premises. The capacity-grain could have been articulated had the vitalism disanalogy never been formulated and had Revonsuo never catalogued the binding varieties. The reverse holds: neither of the other two grains depends on the capacity-grain.

### What the Scoring Shows

The three grains score visibly differently on the four criteria, which is what makes the scoring instrument non-flat — an instrument that scored all three the same would, in the [[apex/taxonomy-of-voids|taxonomy-of-voids]] worked-exhibits idiom, be *doing no work*. The vitalism disanalogy scores strong-strong-moderate-mixed; the binding-problem unity-grain scores strong-strong-mixed-partial; the baseline-cognition capacity-grain scores strong-strong/moderate-mixed-mixed. The shape of the divergence: signature specificity is uniformly strong across the three grains, which is the discipline's most robust face (each grain has a genuinely distinct structural shape, not merely a relabelled version of the others); structured persistence is uniformly strong on the empirical signatures and weaker on the explanandum-framings, which tracks the empirical-versus-framing distinction the [[evidential-status-discipline|evidential-status discipline]] names; cross-observer convergence and framework independence are the discipline's weakest faces across all three grains, with each grain inheriting partial framework-dependence from the philosophical commitments that motivate it.

The *independence-of-deployment* finding is the scoring's load-bearing positive result. The three grains are genuinely structurally distinct: each is statable without the others as premises, each survives counterfactual dissolution of the others, and the discipline's articulation could in principle have started from any of them. The parallel-applications claim the article's introduction makes is therefore *not* an artefact of the three grains borrowing framework-resources from each other at the level of formulation. The discipline does have three genuine sites.

The *framework-independence* finding is the scoring's load-bearing constraint. None of the three grains has built-out cross-cultural-philosophy or cross-species channels at the resolution the [[apophatic-cartography-four-criteria|four criteria]] would require for a *strong* score on framework independence. Each grain's articulation is most developed within Western analytic philosophy of mind (the vitalism disanalogy, the binding problem's explanandum-framing) or within Western comparative cognition (the capacity-grain's correspondence framing). What the discipline has at this stage is *parallel deployments within a shared philosophical context*, not deployments tested across independent traditions. The cluster's cumulative evidential weight calibrates downward to what genuinely independent observers can supply — at most, *moderate* convergence at the methodological-discipline level rather than the *strong* convergence the three-fold parallel might initially suggest.

Following [[evidential-status-discipline|the evidential-status discipline]]'s core constraint — *the framework may identify a meta-argument's structural significance but must not upgrade its independence score beyond what its sources supply* — the discipline's calibrated status is: type-specificity as a methodological discipline is *strongly supported* at the level of recognising the structural feature (signature specificity strong across three grains, structured persistence strong on the empirical face); type-specificity's *evidential force against generic-mechanism reductions* is at *realistic possibility, contested* tier (the three deployments are coherent but framework-internally so; the cross-cultural and cross-species channels would need to be built out to upgrade further); type-specificity's role *as a load-bearing component of the convergence argument's verdict* remains a *live hypothesis* within the convergence argument's overall calibration, which the convergence argument itself holds at *strongly supported on the architectural finding, realistic possibility on the dualist conclusion*.

### What This Worked Exhibit Tests for the Methodology

The two worked exhibits at the [[apex/taxonomy-of-voids|taxonomy of voids]] — the surplus void retracted, the introspection-architecture sub-cluster partially retained — score *void-clusters* on the four criteria. The present exhibit scores a *meta-argument's three deployments* on the same criteria. The cross-argument-type portability of the scoring instrument is itself what the exhibit tests: if the four criteria handle meta-argument-level independence questions with the same non-flatness they showed at the void-cluster level, the framework demonstrates generality across argument-types and the catalogue's calibration architecture earns broader scope. If the four criteria collapsed at the meta-argument level — scored every meta-argument deployment the same, or produced uninterpretable results on the new register — that would constrain the framework's scope to void-clusters specifically.

The exhibit produces visibly different scores across its three sub-cases (the three grains diverge on cross-observer convergence and framework independence while converging on signature specificity and structured persistence), and the scoring procedure is recognisably the same procedure the taxonomy-of-voids applies. The framework therefore appears to handle meta-argument independence with the non-flatness it shows at void-clusters. With N=3 worked exhibits across two argument-types (two void-clusters, one meta-argument), the generality claim is provisional rather than vindicated. A clean unfavourable test case at the meta-argument level — a deployment that *should* score weakly on independence but the framework instead scores strongly — remains the methodology's open burden.

The exhibit also tests whether type-specificity itself can carry the [[concession-convergence|concession-convergence]] discipline's parallel audit: *non-Map* deployments of type-specificity (in philosophy of mathematics, philosophy of biology, philosophy of language) could in principle exhibit the same three-grain pattern, which would strengthen the discipline's framework-independence face by showing that the structural feature is recognised outside the Map's philosophical context. The audit is left as an open programme. If such non-Map deployments converge structurally with the Map's three grains, the type-specificity discipline earns an upgrade on framework independence; if they do not, the discipline's force remains constrained to the philosophical context that motivated it.

## What Type-Specificity Does and Does Not Do {#scope}

Type-specificity is a methodological discipline, not a metaphysical conclusion. It does the following work:

- **Identifies the form-of-demand a reductive account must satisfy.** Where the explanandum is type-specific, a reduction must address the determinate character of each type rather than substituting a generic mechanism. This is a constraint derivable from the explanandum's structure, independently of any verdict on whether the constraint can be met.

- **Distinguishes consciousness from past reductive successes structurally.** Vitalism's "vital force" was schematic; phenomenal qualities are type-specific. The temporal observation that no reduction has yet been found is symmetric across the two cases; the structural observation about the shape of the demand is not.

- **Sets a non-circular constraint on what would count as a solution.** The constraint is "the explanans must match the form of the explanandum." This is derivable from the explanandum alone and does not presuppose the irreducibility conclusion the constraint might be invoked to support.

It does *not* do the following work:

- **Prove irreducibility.** Type-specific reductive demands could in principle be met. A neuroscience that delivered the determinate character of redness in physical terms — an account from which *redness specifically*, not "some phenomenal type or other," could be derived as the output of the proposed physical structure — would discharge the demand at redness; the discipline would then have to consider whether the discharge extends type-by-type or whether the first reduction reveals a generic strategy applicable to the family.

- **Defeat physicalism on its own.** Type-specificity is the structural feature of the explanandum, not an argument that physicalism cannot address the explanandum. It identifies what a successful physicalist account would have to deliver, on the assumption that phenomenal types are the explananda. A physicalist strategy that contests the explananda themselves — illusionism, heterophenomenology, or eliminative reduction of folk-psychological categories — is a separate strategy that bypasses the type-specific demand rather than meeting it; whether such strategies succeed is taken up in [[reductionism-and-consciousness]] and runs counter to the Map's foundational commitments in ways honestly noted there. Whether a phenomenal-type-granting physicalist account is forthcoming is a separate question that [[the-convergence-argument-for-dualism]] addresses through the pattern of evidence rather than through type-specificity alone.

- **Stand on irreducibility.** The discipline must not be defended by appeal to "phenomenal qualities are irreducible, therefore the reductive demand is unmeetable." That would be circular: type-specificity is invoked in support of the irreducibility verdict, so it cannot draw its anti-generic-mechanism force from the verdict it helps support. The force comes from the structural correspondence between explanandum-shape and explanans-shape, recognised independently of any reduction's success or failure.

## Relation to Site Perspective {#relation-to-site-perspective}

Type-specificity supports the Map's [[tenets#^dualism|Dualism tenet]] in a structurally constrained way. The discipline does not by itself establish that consciousness is irreducible. It does establish that the historical pattern of reductive successes — chemistry of life, atomic theory, molecular genetics — does not extend automatically to consciousness, because the explananda in those cases were schematic in ways phenomenal qualities are not. The convergence argument earns its irreducibility conclusion from the broader pattern of evidence (the hard problem, the explanatory gap, philosophical-zombie conceivability, contemplative convergence, and others); type-specificity is one structural feature that places the convergence's load-bearing observations in a coherent frame.

The discipline aligns with the [[tenets#^occams-limits|Occam's Razor Has Limits tenet]]. A parsimony-based preference for physicalism rests partly on the assumption that one kind of stuff is simpler than two. Type-specificity reframes the parsimony comparison: where the explanandum is type-specific and the proposed unified explanans does not deliver the type-specific demand, the apparent parsimony is purchased at the cost of an unaddressed explanatory gap. Parsimony as a tiebreaker requires equal explanatory power; type-specificity is one way the explanatory power may not in fact be equal.

The discipline observes the Map's standing constraint against [[concession-convergence|concession convergence]]: when physicalist theories retreat toward positions that respect type-specificity (selectively reducing some phenomenal types while remaining silent on others, or treating type-specific phenomenal facts as primitive within a physicalist framework), the retreat is itself evidence that the type-specificity demand is being recognised, even where the verdict is not granted. The structural feature is doing work whether or not the metaphysical conclusion is drawn.
