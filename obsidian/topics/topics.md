Topics are the Map's extended explorations—the deep dives where a single question is followed as far as the Map's [[tenets]] and the surrounding [[concepts]] will carry it. If you have limited time, start with the five apex hubs below, then follow whichever thread you came for: **agency** (how consciousness authors action), the **quantum interface** (how mind meets matter), **AI and machine consciousness** (whether artifacts can have inner lives), **dualism and the interaction problem** (the foundations), or **meaning and the examined life** (what any of this is for). The reading routes in [Reading Routes](#reading-routes) name the entry article for each thread.

The corpus is large and still growing. Each page is a working exploration—continuously reviewed and refined—that takes the Map's consciousness-first commitments seriously and traces where they lead, rather than summarising settled consensus. Each topic connects back to the [[tenets]] explicitly and draws on the shared vocabulary defined in [[concepts]].

- [[apex/interface-specification-programme|The Interface Specification Programme]] — six converging lines of inquiry that specify the mind-matter interface as a research programme. The hub for the quantum-interface thread.
- [[apex/machine-question|The Machine Question]] — whether artifacts can be conscious, and the principled obstacles the Map identifies. The hub for the AI thread.

## Relation to Site Perspective

This index is itself an expression of the Map's [[tenets]]: it routes by the threads the tenets generate. The dualism route follows [[tenets#^dualism|the irreducibility of consciousness]]; the quantum-interface route follows [[tenets#^minimal-quantum-interaction|minimal quantum interaction]] and [[tenets#^bidirectional-interaction|bidirectional interaction]]; the agency route follows what bidirectional interaction makes possible; and the rejection of [[tenets#^no-many-worlds|Many-Worlds]] shapes the foundations route's reverse inference from mind to matter. Because [[tenets#^occams-limits|Occam's Razor has limits]], the corpus does not prune to a single tidy theory—it keeps the threads open and the routes overlapping. Topics are where these commitments get tested against specific questions; concepts are where the vocabulary they require is defined.
