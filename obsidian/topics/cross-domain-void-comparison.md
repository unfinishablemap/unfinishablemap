Physics and mathematics each have their own catalogue of hard limits—the measurement problem, undecidable propositions, cosmological horizons, results provably independent of the axioms. The Map's [[taxonomy-of-voids|voids taxonomy]] argues that the over a hundred cognitive voids it catalogues cluster around the consciousness-matter interface in a *structured* way, and that this structure carries abductive weight. But a deflationary reading threatens that argument at its root: maybe consciousness is simply where human inquiry currently fails, and a comparably careful catalogue of physics-voids or math-voids would show the same density. If consciousness voids behave no differently from voids in other domains, the convergence is "just where the map-makers happened to look." If they behave *differently*, that difference is worth examining—though, as this article finds, a difference can be real without settling what produces it.

This article runs the comparison the taxonomy gestures at but does not develop. It catalogues representative voids from physics and mathematics at roughly the same taxonomic grain the Map applies to consciousness, tests the apex's four-kinds structure and failure-signature inventory against them, and reports the structural differences. The purpose is narrow and stated up front: this is a *qualitative comparative test* bearing on the [[#the-selection-effect-worry-restated|selection-effect worry]] (explained below)—a framework-internal, hand-classified comparison, not a scored protocol and not an external evidential result. The verdict is correspondingly modest: the consciousness cluster does behave differently along measurable axes, but each difference either follows from a fact dualist and physicalist already share or rests on instruments built from the consciousness catalogue, so the comparison establishes coherence rather than discrimination—and one difference cuts the other way besides.

## What Counts as a Void

The Map uses "void" in a specific sense, narrower than "open problem." A void is a place where understanding breaks down in a *structured, repeatable* way, producing a characteristic [[compound-failure-signatures|failure signature]]—not merely a question we have not yet answered. The [[three-kinds-of-void|three-kinds taxonomy]] sorts a void's territory across three axes (the *unexplored*, the *unexplorable*, the *occluded*), with a fourth kind—the [[naturally-occluded|naturally occluded]]—added for limits evolution actively maintains. To compare fairly across domains, the same bar must apply everywhere: a genuine void is a *principled* limit with a recognisable signature, not a frontier awaiting more work.

This bar disciplines the comparison: many apparent physics and mathematics "limits" are merely unexplored—hard but not principled—and filtering those out keeps the comparison from being rigged in the Map's favour. Two honest caveats follow. First, the physics and mathematics cases below are selected, not exhaustive: deliberately set aside are open-but-unprincipled problems (the black-hole information paradox, quantum gravity, P versus NP, the Riemann hypothesis, Navier–Stokes regularity) and theory-underdetermination, as frontiers or limits of a different kind—though a fuller treatment would score them rather than exclude them by hand. Second, the bar is applied *asymmetrically*: physics and mathematics cases are admitted only when theorem-grade or near it, while the consciousness catalogue includes frankly frontier items (dream consciousness, infant consciousness, the resonance void) that would not clear that bar. Either both sides should meet one standard or the asymmetry should be flagged; this article flags it, since the asymmetry inflates any apparent difference—another reason the comparison earns coherence weight, not a discriminating result.

## Voids in Physics

Physics supplies several limits that clear the bar—principled, with recognisable signatures.

**The measurement problem.** Quantum mechanics predicts superpositions, yet measurement yields definite outcomes; the formalism does not say which outcome occurs or when the transition happens. This is a structural gap, not missing knowledge. Its signature is *interpretive proliferation*: rather than one breakdown, the gap spawns rival interpretations (Copenhagen, many-worlds, Bohmian) that agree on all predictions and disagree on what is real. Objective-collapse theories (GRW, CSL, Diósi–Penrose) sit differently: they are rival *physical* theories making distinct, in-principle testable predictions (Bassi 2023; Fano & Macchia)—Diósi–Penrose has already been experimentally constrained (Donadi et al. 2021)—so the proliferation signature attaches only to the predictively-equivalent interpretations, not to the objective-collapse rivals.

**Undecidability within physics.** Cubitt, Perez-Garcia and Wolf proved in *Nature* (2015) that the spectral gap problem—whether a quantum many-body system's energy spectrum has a gap above the ground state—is formally undecidable; in their words, "there exist models for which the presence or absence of a spectral gap is independent of the axioms of mathematics." This is a Gödel-Turing limit located inside physics, though it concerns mathematical *models* of physical systems rather than finite physical systems themselves.

**Cosmological horizons.** The finite speed of light and the universe's expansion place a hard boundary on the observable region. The particle horizon bounds what can have reached us so far (~46 Gly, and growing with time); in standard ΛCDM the cosmological *event* horizon bounds what can ever reach us (~16 Gly), marking events that are unobservable not merely now but in principle. The signature is clean: a sharp epistemic boundary with no internal phenomenology of approach.

**Fine-tuning.** Several physical constants appear to fall within narrow ranges compatible with complex structure, and physics has no accepted account of why (Friederich 2024). Its signature is *explanatory regress*: each proposed explanation (multiverse, anthropic selection, deeper law) relocates rather than dissolves the puzzle.

These are real limits. The question is whether they share structure with each other and with consciousness voids—or arise independently.

## Voids in Mathematics

Mathematics supplies the cleanest voids of all, because here "principled limit" can be *proved*.

**Gödelian incompleteness.** Gödel's first incompleteness theorem (1931) shows that any consistent, sufficiently expressive formal system contains true statements it cannot prove; the second shows such a system cannot prove its own consistency. The signature is *self-referential obstruction*: the limit arises when a system encodes statements about itself.

**Independence results.** Whether the continuum hypothesis holds is independent of the standard axioms of set theory: Gödel (1940) showed CH consistent with ZFC, Cohen (1963, 1964) showed its negation consistent via forcing. The proposition is neither provable nor refutable from the axioms. The signature is *axiomatic underdetermination*—not failure to find a proof, but a demonstrated absence of any proof either way.

**Uncomputability.** Turing's halting problem and Rice's theorem mark functions no algorithm can compute. [[formal-cognitive-limits|These formal cognitive limits]] are voids whose existence is mathematically certain rather than conjectured.

Mathematics thus offers the best-credentialled voids: their principled character is theorem, not hypothesis. If the Map's claim were "consciousness voids are more certainly *real* than other voids," mathematics would refute it immediately. The Map's claim is different, and the difference is the whole point.

## Testing the Four-Kinds Structure

The apex sorts void *territories* into four kinds. Applying the same axes to physics and mathematics reveals the first structural difference.

Physics and mathematics voids sit overwhelmingly in the **unexplorable** kind, and almost nowhere else. The halting problem, CH independence, and incompleteness are unexplorable by *proof*. Cosmological horizons are unexplorable by physical law. None are plausibly *occluded* (no mechanism hides them; they are openly displayed in the theory) and none are *naturally occluded* (evolution did not shape access to the continuum hypothesis). They are unexplorable-or-nothing.

Consciousness voids, by contrast, distribute across all four kinds and frequently span several at once. The [[taxonomy-of-voids|taxonomy]] notes that a single consciousness void—the quantitative comprehension void, say—can be unexplorable at its cardinality floor, unexplored in its mid-range, and occluded where introspection cannot reach its own operation. The self-knowledge cluster is heavily *occluded* and partly *naturally occluded* (introspective opacity plausibly being adaptively maintained), categories that physics and mathematics voids do not occupy at all.

**First difference:** physics/math voids look taxonomically *flat*—near-pure unexplorables. Consciousness voids look taxonomically *deep*—they appear to populate all four kinds and span them within single voids. The depth is genuine, but whether it discriminates a dualist reading from a physicalist one is a separate question, examined immediately below; the difference does not obviously survive a by-construction discount.

It is tempting to treat this as the discriminating axis, but the by-construction discount that disciplines the signature test below applies here too. Two of the four kinds, *occluded* and *naturally-occluded*, were added to the taxonomy precisely to capture self-access phenomena (introspective opacity, evolutionarily maintained blind spots), and they *require* a subject whose access is hidden or shaped. So depth co-varies with subject-involvement by construction: physics and mathematics voids cannot occupy those kinds, and their flatness is guaranteed rather than found—just as the four subject-requiring signatures are guaranteed absent below. The natural fallback is to rest the difference on *spanning*: that a single consciousness void distributes across several kinds at once. But a physicalist predicts spanning directly—a self-modelling biological system has an unexplored frontier, an unexplorable computational/Gödelian floor, and occluded introspective regions *all at once*, as three consequences of the one fact that it models itself with limited, evolved equipment. Spanning is overdetermined by physicalism. The difference is real—consciousness voids genuinely span where physics and mathematics voids do not—but the spanning follows from subject-involvement, which dualist and physicalist share, so it adds coherence, not discrimination.

## Testing the Failure-Signature Inventory

The apex's second structuring device is an inventory of failure signatures—the *way* thought breaks down at each void. The original inventory has eight entries: self-refutation, aporia, construction-mistaken-for-observation, systematic illusion, ineffability, epistemic vertigo, dissolution under attention, and silent erasure. Testing physics and mathematics voids against it reveals the second and most informative difference.

Physics and mathematics voids match *at most one* signature each, and the matches are thin:

- Gödelian incompleteness matches **self-refutation** (the self-referential obstruction)—and only that one.
- The measurement problem matches nothing cleanly; "interpretive proliferation" is not on the consciousness inventory at all.
- Fine-tuning matches nothing; "explanatory regress" is, again, off-inventory.
- Cosmological horizons match nothing—a sharp boundary produces *no* characteristic phenomenology of approach, which is precisely why it feels like a wall rather than a void.

Crucially, **none** of the physics or mathematics voids exhibits *construction-mistaken-for-observation*, *dissolution under attention*, *silent erasure*, or *epistemic vertigo*. These four signatures are specific to a knower examining its own cognition—they require an introspecting subject, and physics and mathematics voids have no subject in the loop. The continuum hypothesis does not dissolve when you attend to it; the spectral gap does not silently revise your inventory of yourself downward.

But the strength of this part of the test must be read with care, and the care points one way: the inventory itself was built *from* consciousness voids. That four of its eight signatures require an introspecting subject is therefore not a discovery about physics and mathematics—it is a fact about how the inventory was assembled. Their absence in subjectless domains is *guaranteed by construction*, not found by inspection; the continuum hypothesis could not exhibit dissolution-under-attention no matter how the comparison turned out, because there is no one attending. For these four signatures the cross-domain mismatch is close to analytic, and it carries little discriminating weight against the selection-effect worry, which is precisely a worry about how the cataloguer's standpoint shapes the catalogue.

The non-trivial residue is the *other* direction. The four remaining signatures (self-refutation, aporia, systematic illusion, ineffability) are not obviously subject-bound, so a genuine catalogue of subjectless voids could in principle have matched several of them—and the matches turn out thin (incompleteness alone matches one). Equally non-trivial is that physics and mathematics generate signatures of their *own* (interpretive proliferation, axiomatic underdetermination, explanatory regress, sharp-boundary) that the consciousness inventory does not anticipate. Two catalogues drawn from "wherever humans currently fail" might be expected to share more than this; these mostly do not.

**Second difference:** the failure-signature inventory built from consciousness voids largely *does not transfer*, and the cross-generation of disjoint signatures runs both ways. The honest weight of this difference is *coherence* rather than discrimination: the half of the mismatch that survives the by-construction discount—the thinness of the non-subject-bound matches, plus the disjoint physics/mathematics signatures—is consistent with the consciousness cluster being more than a frontier of current ignorance, but consistent with a physicalist reading too. Neither difference does discriminating work: the first axis rests on spanning that physicalism overdetermines, the second on an inventory built from the very cases it then finds special. Both together establish only that consciousness is the domain where inquirer and inquired coincide—common ground between dualist and physicalist, not a test that separates them.

## The Difference That Cuts the Other Way

Intellectual honesty requires reporting a structural similarity the Map's own corpus argues for, one that *weakens* the independence claim. The taxonomy asserts that physics and mathematics voids "arise independently, from different features of different domains." That assertion is too strong.

[[self-reference-and-the-limits-of-physical-description|Work the Map itself develops]] argues, extending Szangolies' use of Lawvere's fixed-point theorem, that Gödelian incompleteness *and* the quantum measurement problem may derive from a *single* category-theoretic mechanism—self-referential obstruction in Cartesian closed categories, which also generates Turing's halting problem, Cantor's diagonal argument, and Tarski's undefinability of truth. Szangolies (2018) frames self-reference only as a *possible origin* of epistemic horizons; the single-mechanism reading is the Map's developed extension, not a settled external result, and Szangolies' own programme is naturalist, so nothing below leans on his authority for the dualist gloss. On the Map's reading, the deepest physics void and the deepest mathematics void are *not* independent: they are two faces of one fixed-point structure.

This matters for the comparison in two directions. It *weakens* the apex's claim that physics and math voids "arise independently"—at their foundations, the most rigorous of them share a mechanism. But the shared mechanism is *self-reference*, and the consciousness voids most resistant to it (the phenomenal core—what experience *is*) are not self-referential obstructions at all. The Lawvere structure unifies the *formal* voids across physics and mathematics; it leaves the *phenomenal* voids of consciousness outside its reach. So the corrected picture is three-way, not two-way: physics and mathematics share a fixed-point spine; consciousness voids split, with their formal-reflexive members (the [[formal-cognitive-limits|recursion and cartography voids]]) joining that spine while their phenomenal members stand apart from it entirely.

Cashed fully, the concession costs more than the apex's wording admits. If the deepest physics and mathematics voids and the formal-reflexive consciousness voids are all one fixed-point phenomenon recurring across domains, then that part of the apex's "structured clustering" is a *single common cause* showing up repeatedly—the opposite of independent triangulation. Quarantining the unification to "the formal voids" protects the phenomenal core but shrinks the abductive base: what stays genuinely distinctive is not the full N-fold catalogue but the smaller set of phenomenal voids—precisely the voids where dualist and physicalist or illusionist readings are evidentially tied. The weight the cluster carries is correspondingly smaller than a raw count suggests.

The measurement problem is the obvious candidate counterexample to calling physics subjectless: the Map's own [[self-reference-and-the-limits-of-physical-description|self-reference work]] develops Wigner's-friend and Frauchiger–Renner setups, where an observer inside the system reasons about the whole system including agents like themselves. But that observer-in-the-loop is not the introspective signature consciousness voids exhibit. The agent reasons *about* a system using quantum theory; the void is the formal obstruction of a description closing over its own describer, not the *subject's self-access breaking down* under attention—no phenomenology dissolves, no self-inventory is silently revised. It is the same fixed-point spine, located in inference rather than introspection: the formal-reflexive kind, not the phenomenal kind.

The honest revision to the apex's wording: physics and mathematics voids are *less* independent of each other than the taxonomy states, and the structurally distinctive part of the consciousness cluster is correspondingly smaller—the phenomenal core, not the full catalogue. What that leaves standing against the selection-effect worry is narrow, and the next section states it exactly.

## Relation to Site Perspective

### The Selection-Effect Worry Restated

The [[what-voids-reveal|convergence argument]] faces a deflationary objection the Map takes seriously: the Map was built around consciousness, its curation criteria privilege cognitive voids, so finding voids cluster around consciousness may be the observation that the Map is about what the Map is about. The taxonomy's defence is that the *structured* character of the clustering—distinct signatures, family relationships, sub-cluster topology—is what a pure curation bias would not predict. That defence is only as good as the claim that other domains' voids lack comparable structure. This article supplies the missing test.

The result is bounded, and it matters to state exactly how far it reaches. Along two measurable axes—taxonomic depth and signature profile—the consciousness cluster behaves *differently* from the physics and mathematics clusters, and the difference is real. What it cannot do is shift weight toward the convergence being a real feature *rather than* an artefact of where the Map looked, because that is a false binary. The analysis above forces a third option: the difference can be a real feature of cognitive architecture that is metaphysically *neutral* between dualism and physicalism—a self-modelling system fractures into multiple inaccessibility-modes whoever is right about what consciousness is. Both surviving axes land there, the depth axis on spanning physicalism overdetermines, the signature axis on an inventory built from the consciousness cases it then finds special. So the comparison establishes that consciousness is distinctively the inquirer-equals-inquired domain—which a physicalist asserts as readily as a dualist—and does not by itself move weight toward the dualist reading of that coincidence.

This bound is the [[common-cause-null|common-cause null]] in action. An illusionist expects exactly the construction-mistaken-for-observation and dissolution-under-attention signatures, since those are what report-machinery errors feel like from inside; the four subject-requiring signatures are absent in subjectless domains by construction and present in consciousness on *either* the dualist or the illusionist reading. So those four cannot discriminate in either direction—their cross-domain mismatch is fixed by the presence of a subject, not by what the subject's nature turns out to be. The common-cause null and the by-construction discount are one constraint viewed twice. The comparison earns only what it can: the consciousness cluster is not "just where humans currently fail," but its distinctiveness is *framework-internal coherence*, not *framework-independent* confirmation of the tenet-package over rivals that predict the same coincidence. The honest verdict matches the apex's own discipline: the cross-domain difference is real and worth stating, but it raises coherence weight rather than supplying the distinguishing test.

This connects most directly to the fifth tenet, **[[tenets#^occams-limits|Occam's Razor Has Limits]]**. The simplest deflation—"all hard limits are alike; consciousness is just the current frontier"—is *not supported by this classification as currently drawn*: physics, mathematics, and consciousness voids have measurably different taxonomic depths and failure signatures, even though (as established above) those differences track subject-involvement rather than discriminating dualism from its rivals. A parsimony argument that flattens the voids into one undifferentiated class sacrifices a real structural distinction for an apparent simplicity—the failure mode the fifth tenet warns against, and one that holds whoever turns out to be right about consciousness. The simpler classification is the less accurate one, even where the more accurate one settles no metaphysics.

It also touches **[[tenets#^dualism|Dualism]]** at one remove, and the remove must be kept honest. The phenomenal core—the voids about what experience *is*—stands outside even the Lawvere self-reference structure that unifies the deepest physics and mathematics voids. It is tempting to read that as evidence for dualism, but the inference stacks separate claims: that a class of voids is not captured by *one* formal mechanism does not show it is not formal at *all*, and not-formal-at-all would not show it is non-physical—and the absence may simply reflect that the phenomenal core has not yet been formalised in this framework. The resistance is equally compatible with type-B materialism, Russellian monism, and illusionism-about-reports, which all expect phenomenal vocabulary to sit outside a fixed-point formalism. It is therefore *compatible with*, not *evidence for*, the dualist claim that phenomenal character is non-structural.
