The most developed naturalist account of psychedelic experience is Chris Letheby's *predictive self-binding*. On this account, the felt self is a high-precision Bayesian prior: a hierarchical model the brain maintains because positing a stable, persisting entity efficiently integrates salient, egocentric, autobiographical information. Psychedelics relax the precision of these self-priors—the mechanism Carhart-Harris and Friston call REBUS—"unbinding" mental contents from the self-model, which then becomes phenomenally *opaque* (experienced as a model rather than seen-through). That opacity is ego dissolution. Letheby's metaphysical conclusion is **self-fictionalism**: the unified, substantial self is a useful Cartesian fiction with no referent. This is the strongest contemporary rival to the Map's filter-theoretic and dualist reading of the same neuroimaging data, and the Map owns an honest concession—on altered-state data *alone*, the two accounts are empirically equivalent.

This article presents predictive self-binding at full strength and then mounts the Map's reply on three fronts (each [[#The Three-Pronged Reply|developed below]]): the [[#The Hard-Problem Debt|hard-problem debt]] that predictive processing assumes rather than discharges, the [[#The Persistent Minimal Self|persistent minimal self]] the Map predicts survives dissolution, and the [[#The Comforting-Delusion Reversal|comforting-delusion reversal]] that turns Letheby's central objection back on the naturalism it presupposes. The companion article [[psychedelics-and-the-filter-model]] treats Letheby as a foil *within* the filter discussion; this piece foregrounds the self-fictionalism metaphysics that the filter article only gestures at, and engages Letheby as the disciplined physicalist baseline the Map must clear.

## The Predictive Self-Binding Account

Letheby and Gerrans (2017), building on Metzinger's self-model theory and the predictive-processing framework, propose that the self is not a thing the brain *has* but a representation the brain *builds*. Self-related information is processed faster and bound into a representation of a persisting object—a structure that unifies and predicts ongoing patterns of salient, egocentric, autobiographical experience. They name this mechanism predictive self-binding.

The binding work is done by hierarchical prediction. High-level priors anticipate which information will matter to the organism across multiple timescales, incorporating its history and goals. The self-model is the apex prior that ties these predictions to a single, continuing subject. Ego dissolution, on this account, is the breakdown of the integrated functioning of the hierarchical models that posit a stable, unchanging entity to which representations are bound.

The metaphysical upshot is fictionalism. In their words, self-representation is "an ultimately false representation of a simple and enduring substance to which attributes are bound which serves to integrate and unify cognitive processing across levels and domains." The self is useful precisely because it is a fiction: positing one efficient locus of ownership lets the system organise cognition across levels and domains it could not otherwise integrate. There is no one home behind the model; there is only the model, doing its integrative job.

### The REBUS Mechanism

The physiological substrate the self-binding account rides on is REBUS—"Relaxed Beliefs Under Psychedelics" (Carhart-Harris & Friston, 2019), a physicalist model combining the free-energy principle with the entropic-brain hypothesis. Psychedelics relax the *precision weighting* of high-level priors, so they are held with less confidence; bottom-up prediction errors are released to revise them. A paradigm high-level prior is the belief that one has a particular personality and set of views—the narrative self. REBUS supplies the dynamics; predictive self-binding supplies the philosophical interpretation. (Carhart-Harris and Friston build an explicitly physicalist account and would not endorse the filter reading the Map places alongside it; their model is a rival the Map adopts the mechanics of without enlisting the authors.)

### Why "More Real Than Real"

The account has an elegant explanation for the noetic, "more real than real" quality of psychedelic insight. When the self-model loses precision, it stops being transparent—the subject experiences the model *as* a model rather than seeing through it as simply "me." Opaque models feel revelatory because they expose what is normally invisible. Crucially, on Letheby's reading, nothing *beyond* the model is being revealed. The insight is real, but its content is the constructed nature of the self, not access to any deeper layer of reality. Letheby characterises this as "dispelling-the-illusion" insight rather than "third-eye" perception.

## The Naturalistic Epistemic-Benefit Argument

In *Philosophy of Psychedelics* (Oxford, 2021), Letheby extends self-binding into an argument about the *epistemics* of psychedelic therapy. The central problem he addresses is the **Comforting Delusion Objection** (CDO): if psychedelics heal by inducing mystical beliefs that naturalism deems false—belief in a cosmic consciousness, a soul, a beyond—then prescribing them is prescribing comforting delusions, and a naturalist cannot in good conscience endorse the practice.

Letheby's response is a "fourth option." Psychedelics, he argues, work through transformative *phenomenological insight*, not through instilling false metaphysical beliefs. The therapeutic mechanism is a felt insight into the constructed, non-substantial nature of the self—a change in the *sense* of self and in how one relates to one's own mind, not a change in beliefs about ultimate reality. Metaphysical belief shifts, where they occur, are a side-effect rather than the active ingredient.

On this reading the epistemic risks under naturalism are surprisingly small and the epistemic benefits surprisingly large: new psychological insight, existential reflection, knowledge-by-acquaintance of altered states, and the ability to disidentify from maladaptive self-beliefs by experiencing them as "just thoughts." Letheby coins **naturalistic spirituality** for experiences legitimately called spiritual—unity, connectedness, ego transcendence—that need involve no belief in anything beyond the natural world. The CDO, on his account, fails: psychedelic therapy is epistemically respectable for the naturalist because its healing does not run through delusion.

This is a powerful, disciplined position. It is the baseline the Map must clear, and the Map clears much of it: the *therapeutic* story can be accepted nearly whole. The disagreement is metaphysical, and it is sharp.

## The Three-Pronged Reply

The Map's reply does not contest the neuroimaging or the clinical record. It contests three load-bearing junctures where the naturalist conclusion outruns what the evidence and the formalism deliver. Each prong corresponds to a tenet.

### The Hard-Problem Debt

Predictive self-binding helps itself to a move predictive processing has never paid for: the assumption that a sufficiently structured Bayesian model is *phenomenally felt*. The formalism explains self-*prioritisation*—why self-relevant information is processed faster, bound more tightly, weighted more heavily. It does not explain why there is something it is like to be the system running that model. The step from "the brain maintains a high-precision self-prior" to "the brain therefore has a felt sense of self" is exactly the [[hard-problem-of-consciousness|hard problem]] in predictive-processing register.

This is not a peripheral worry. The 2019 Brains Blog symposium on "Self unbound" pressed it from several directions. Hipólito worried that the account conflates binding self-relevant information with phenomenal self-representation, raising homunculus and causal-efficacy concerns. Michael asked why the resulting structure should be a *Cartesian* self rather than a Humean bundle, given that self-prioritisation is graded rather than sharp. Millière noted that "self-binding" equivocates between *self-related* and *self-specific* processing. The shared thread: the formalism reaches functional and behavioural self-prioritisation, but whether it reaches phenomenality at all is unestablished.

Letheby can fairly reply that filter theory inherits a parallel debt—positing extra-cerebral consciousness for the brain to filter only relocates the question of what makes *that* consciousness phenomenal. The honest position is that neither account discharges the hard problem; both inherit it. But that symmetry is itself the Map's point. The naturalist conclusion is sold as the parsimonious, scientifically responsible default, and by the Map's [[tenets#^occams-limits|fifth tenet]], parsimony is an unreliable guide to truth where our conceptual tools may be inadequate—as they manifestly are at the explanatory gap. The self-binding account does not *win* on the hard problem; it postpones it while presenting the postponement as a solution.

### The Persistent Minimal Self

The discriminating empirical bet concerns whether *any* self survives ego dissolution. Letheby's account predicts that whatever "witnessing" is reported in deep dissolution is itself a not-yet-decohered model component—a residue of self-binding that fuller relaxation would dissolve. The Map predicts the opposite: a [[witness-consciousness|minimal, pre-reflective "for-me-ness"]] persists even when the narrative self-model is in abeyance, because for-me-ness is a feature of any conscious state rather than a model component that can decohere.

The distinction between the [[self-and-self-consciousness|minimal self]] and the narrative self is doing the work. The narrative self—the autobiographical character with a name, a history, a set of views—is plausibly a constructed model, and the Map grants that psychedelics dismantle it. The minimal self is the bare experiential presence to which any content is given. The Map follows the phenomenological tradition in holding that this presence continues to "witness" dissolution: subjects characteristically report losing "me-as-character" while bare experiential presence remains.

A live counter-current within the literature itself supports taking this seriously. Critics argue that Letheby and Millière work with an *overinflated* notion of "self," so that more minimal forms of for-me-ness arguably persist even in the "totally selfless" reports associated with 5-MeO-DMT. And there is an ally internal to physicalism: Deane's (2021) active-inference treatment holds that affective valence—a system's inference about how well it is self-evidencing—is the most fundamental layer of experience and *persists* even when structured self-consciousness collapses. The Map does not endorse Deane's framework, but the structural claim that bare conscious valuation survives dissolution is friendly to the Map's prediction that for-me-ness does not vanish. (Deane's account is physicalist and does not endorse the Map's metaphysics; it is cited here as an internal-to-physicalism disagreement with Letheby, not as support for dualism.)

First-person reports underdetermine the choice. The dismantled metacognitive instrumentation makes adjudication on phenomenological grounds particularly fraught, as the [[ineffable-encounter-void|ineffable-encounter analysis]] details: the very faculty that would report on residual selfhood is among the structures perturbed. The Map registers its prediction as defeasible. But the point stands that this is a genuine empirical seam, not a foregone conclusion—and it bears on [[tenets#^no-many-worlds|Tenet 4's]] commitment to a determinate indexical subject.

### The Comforting-Delusion Reversal

Letheby's CDO presupposes that naturalism is the responsible default and non-naturalist beliefs are the epistemic risks. Sjöstedt-Hughes (2022) presses the obvious rejoinder: naturalism is not a neutral baseline but one contentious metaphysics among live rivals—panpsychism, idealism, Spinozism, and some dualisms are empirically compatible with the same data. Under several of these, psychedelic insights into unity and connectedness could be *veridical* rather than delusional. If so, calling them a "comforting delusion" presupposes the very naturalism in dispute.

This flips the objection. The CDO charges the non-naturalist with prescribing comfort over truth. But if the truth of naturalism is unsettled—and the unsolved hard problem is good reason to think it is—then labelling mystical insight a delusion is not the cautious, evidence-respecting move it presents itself as. It is a metaphysical commitment masquerading as scientific caution. Sjöstedt-Hughes adds a sharper internal tension: predictive processing casts *all* experience as "controlled hallucination," which sits awkwardly with Letheby's emphasis on genuine connectedness. If every experience is internally constructed simulation, the naturalist owes an account of how psychedelic connection is *real* rather than just another well-formed hallucination—and that account is not obviously available within the framework.

The Map does not claim the reversal establishes that psychedelic insights *are* veridical. It claims something weaker and more secure: the CDO is not a neutral epistemic constraint but an argument that runs only on naturalist premises, and so cannot be used to settle the naturalism-versus-dualism question without begging it. This is [[tenets#^occams-limits|Tenet 5]] applied to the dialectic itself.

## Why the Accounts Are Empirically Equivalent on Altered-State Data

The Map maintains an honest concession central to its [[psychedelics-and-the-filter-model|filter-model treatment]]: on psychedelic data *alone*, predictive self-binding is not inferior to the filter reading. Both predict the same neuroimaging signatures—the precision-relaxation that REBUS formalises, the network-dissolution that the 2024 imaging work centres, the intensity-dissolution correlations. Both predict richer phenomenology under prior-relaxation; the framing that "less brain activity yields richer experience strains production accounts" does not strain Letheby's at all, because predictive processing predicts exactly that as a dynamical consequence.

The two accounts diverge only on questions the neuroimaging cannot adjudicate: whether the minimal self survives, whether the noetic quality tracks veridical access or model-opacity, and—beneath both—whether a structured Bayesian model is phenomenally felt. The discriminating work is therefore done not by psychedelic data but by broader theoretical commitments and the corpus-wide convergence pattern. The Map's foundational stance places it outside Letheby's framework as a matter of starting points, not of altered-state-data adjudication. Saying so plainly is part of the Map's evidential discipline, not a retreat from it.

## Relation to Site Perspective

Predictive self-binding engages four of the Map's [[tenets]] directly.

**Dualism.** Self-fictionalism is a direct rival to the Map's first tenet. It denies an irreducible subject and reads the felt self as a referent-less model. The Map's reply is not that the model story is false but that it is incomplete: it explains self-prioritisation while assuming, rather than explaining, that the model is felt. The hard-problem debt is where the rivalry actually lives, and it is unresolved on both sides.

**No Many Worlds.** The minimal-self disagreement connects to [[tenets#^no-many-worlds|Tenet 4's]] commitment to indexical identity. Self-fictionalism, if true, would deflate the determinate "this-one" subject that the indexical objection to many-worlds needs. The Map's prediction that for-me-ness persists under dissolution is continuous with its commitment to a non-deflationary subject—and the Map notes honestly that this commitment is motivated on independent grounds, not read off the psychedelic data.

**Bidirectional Interaction.** Set and setting shape psychedelic outcomes profoundly, suggesting consciousness actively conditions its own altered experience rather than passively receiving whatever altered chemistry delivers—coherent with [[tenets#^bidirectional-interaction|Tenet 3]], though not uniquely predicted by it.

**Occam's Razor Has Limits.** Both the hard-problem prong and the CDO reversal turn on [[tenets#^occams-limits|Tenet 5]]. Naturalism's claim to be the parsimonious default is exactly the kind of simplicity-appeal the Map holds unreliable where conceptual tools are inadequate. The self-binding account is disciplined and serious; what it is not is metaphysically neutral.

The honest summary: Letheby has built the account a responsible physicalist *should* build, and the Map gains nothing by pretending it is weak. The disagreement is real and turns on commitments settled outside the psychedelic evidence. That is where the Map locates it.
