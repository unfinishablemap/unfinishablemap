## The Framework-Level Trap

The most common failure mode in cross-tradition arguments is reading framework-level predictions as independent observations. Each of the three traditions includes a soteriological doctrine whose central diagnostic claim is that ordinary cognition is unreliable: Advaita's *avidyā* (ignorance) is the framework's central explanandum; Sufism's *nafs ammāra* is by definition deceptive; Stoicism identifies passion with *false* judgement. Citing each tradition's general claim that introspection fails would be circular — the diagnostic is what each framework was built to deliver.

The [[project/voids-circularity-discount|voids-circularity discount]] requires that the cross-tradition channel earn its weight on architectural-specifics-level findings that the authoring framework did *not* predict. The audit concentrates on doctrinal additions the framework had to *accommodate* rather than derive, empirical features the practice tradition discovered through self-observation, and structural claims that survive translation when stripped of their framework-specific metaphysics. Only the architectural-specifics layer can pass the despite-commitments test; framework-level convergence is calibration-only.

### The Architectural-Specifics Test

The framework-level / architectural-specifics distinction does heavy adjudicative work in what follows, and it earns that weight only if it can be operationalised. The test this article applies has three criteria — a tradition-internal claim earns architectural-specifics status when it satisfies all three:

1. **Vocabulary-stripping survival.** The claim still expresses a contentful structural feature once the tradition's soteriological vocabulary is removed — i.e. translating into a metaphysically neutral cognitive-architectural register does not dissolve the claim into the framework's prior commitments.
2. **Observation-grounded origin.** The claim is supported by a tradition-internal observation report — a practice manual, a refinement under disagreement, a case description in a treatise — rather than being a derivation from the framework's prior doctrine.
3. **Refinement rather than recapitulation.** The claim refines the framework's central diagnostic by adding structure the original formulation did not require, rather than restating the central diagnostic in different terms.

A claim that fails (1) is reading a framework-level prediction as if it were an architectural finding. A claim that fails (2) is doctrinal derivation, not empirical accommodation. A claim that fails (3) is the framework's central diagnostic re-stated, and so cannot earn independence credit. The discipline is asymmetric: passing all three is necessary for despite-commitments scoring; failing any one collapses the claim to because-prediction. The test is applied transparently to each positive score in what follows; a tradition-internal scholar can audit which criterion the article relies on for each rating.

## Advaita Vedānta: The Architectural Picture

Shankara's eighth-century *Adhyāsa Bhāṣya* — the introductory section of his commentary on the *Brahma Sūtras* — opens with *adhyāsa* ("superimposition") as a structural cognitive failure: properties of the physical body are projected onto the Ātman, and properties of the Ātman onto the body. Shankara treats *adhyāsa* as a fact about ordinary cognition rather than a hypothesis, and notes that earlier schools discussed it under other names — suggesting empirical debate, not a single school's doctrinal invention.

The architectural specifics live in the *antaḥkaraṇa* ("inner instrument") doctrine classical Advaita inherited from Sāṃkhya/Yoga: a fourfold structure — *manas* (sensory mind), *buddhi* (discriminating intellect), *ahaṃkāra* (I-maker), *citta* (impression-storehouse) — inert by nature, with consciousness reflecting in it (*pratibimba*). The witness has access to the *outputs* of the instrument but not to its inner workings; discrimination (*viveka*) between witness and instrument is the soteriological task *because* the architectural feature makes them ordinarily indistinguishable. The *vāsanā* and *saṃskāra* doctrine extends the picture downward: latent dispositional tendencies operate outside conscious awareness and drive current cognition — the closest Advaita-tradition analogue to the *implicit causal theory* hypothesis Nisbett & Wilson invoke.

Scoring. The framework-level claim (witness with bounded access to instrument) is *because-prediction* — Advaita's soteriology requires it; it fails criterion (3) of the architectural-specifics test by recapitulating rather than refining the framework's central diagnostic. The four-faculty subdivision of the *antaḥkaraṇa* earns *moderate* despite-commitments because it passes the test on (2) — the subdivision emerged from the practice tradition's empirical work and refined the broader Sāṃkhya/Yoga inheritance — though a tradition-internal scholar can reasonably argue the subdivision is partly derived from the inert-instrument framework (a faculty for sensory input, one for discrimination, one for the I-maker, one for impression-storage). The rating reflects that partial vulnerability. The *ahaṃkāra*-as-narrator function is a structural parallel to the Gazzaniga interpreter that survives stripping the Vedāntic metaphysics — passing criterion (1) — and earns calibration weight on the same modest basis. None of these architectural specifics earns more than calibration weight on its own; jointly they extend the architectural finding into pre-modern Indian thought.

The Map does not collapse the bounded witness into Brahman; the Advaita *jīva*-into-Brahman move is where the Map and Advaita diverge. The architectural parallel — bounded access as a structural feature of ordinary cognition — survives the divergence, but a Madhyamaka critic correctly notes this is not where the dispute lives. For mature Madhyamaka (Nāgārjuna, MMK 24.18; the *emptiness of emptiness* doctrine the parent exhibit's revision acknowledges), the architecture *itself* is empty: reading it as a shared finding is exactly the reification the practice exists to dissolve. The article cannot resolve this; it can only note that the channel's reading of Advaita as supplying an architectural finding is conditional on rejecting the Madhyamaka inversion, acknowledged in the parent exhibit as a live methodological complication.

## Sufi Practice: Khawāṭir and Hidden Riyāʾ {#sufi-khawatir}

The strongest single point of structural convergence across the three traditions is the Sufi *khawāṭir* taxonomy. *Khawāṭir* are incoming thoughts arising in consciousness; the Sufi science classifies them by source — divine (*rabbānī*), angelic (*malakī*), egoic (*nafsānī*), satanic (*shayṭānī*). The taxonomy *presupposes* that source is *not* self-evident — otherwise the science of discrimination would be unnecessary. The spiritual master is said to "possess the science of *khawāṭir*" and to identify sources the disciple cannot.

Strip the metaphysical typology, and what remains is exactly the [[voids/source-attribution-void|source-attribution void]]'s architectural claim: thoughts arrive without delivered source-tags, and source-attribution requires reconstructive work. The Johnson-Hashtroudi-Lindsay 1993 source-monitoring framework names the same architectural fact in cognitive-psychology vocabulary. The Sufi tradition discovered the architectural feature through *muḥāsaba* — the systematic self-examination programme al-Muḥāsibī (d. 857 CE) pioneered. His diagnostic insight was that ostensibly pious practices (wearing wool, night recitation, fasting) can foster *riyāʾ* (hypocrisy) or *kibr* (pride) — the same outward action can have a hidden self-aggrandising motive the agent does not see.

Al-Ghazālī's eleventh-century *Iḥyāʾ ʿulūm al-dīn* extends Muḥāsibī's finding into a doctrine of *ghurūr* (self-delusion) and *riyāʾ khafiyy* (hidden hypocrisy). Ghazālī treats hidden *riyāʾ* not as a failure of moral effort but as a *structural* feature of how the ego operates — the architecture of motivation includes a self-display function whose operation is invisible to the agent. The doctrine that *riyāʾ* "can visit you always" at the beginning, middle, or end of an act of worship is a structural claim that hidden self-display agendas accompany apparently virtuous behaviour as a default architectural fact, not an occasional moral lapse. This is the strongest cross-tradition parallel to the Wheatley-Haidt and Nisbett-Wilson findings on post-hoc rationalisation that anchor the [[voids/confabulation-void|confabulation void]]'s Generation face.

Scoring. The *khawāṭir* taxonomy's metaphysical typology (divine vs satanic) is framework-internal because-prediction. The architectural claim that source-tags are non-delivered earns force only with respect to *external* frameworks — modern psychology's expectation of veridical source-attribution — but with respect to the Sufi tradition's *own* commitments, the non-delivery of source-tags is exactly what a discernment programme presupposes. The taxonomy fails criterion (3) of the architectural-specifics test by recapitulating rather than refining the tradition's central diagnostic, and so is *low* despite-commitments on its own terms — useful as an early articulation of an architectural feature later named in cognitive-psychology vocabulary.

Hidden *riyāʾ* under Ghazālī carries the cleaner rating, though the Neoplatonist audit imposes a partial framing-level discount. *Riyāʾ* in earlier Sufi treatment was an occasional moral failure remediable by sincerity of intention; Ghazālī's structural finding — that *riyāʾ khafiyy* operates as a default architectural feature of motivation, can visit a practitioner at any point in worship, and persists despite sincere intention — is the kind of refinement the central diagnostic did not require, and the doctrine accommodates the empirical observation that pious practice harboured invisible self-display agendas that *sincerity of intent did not eliminate*. The finding passes all three criteria of the architectural-specifics test at *moderate* despite-commitments. The rating *softens* at the level of the *framing* in which the finding is expressed: Ghazālī's heart-and-inner-faculties psychology is downstream of the Avicennan-Neoplatonist faculty synthesis (al-Kindī → al-Fārābī → Avicenna → Ghazālī), so the architectural shape is partly Neoplatonist-mediated even if the specific finding is not. The combined score is *moderate at the finding-level, with the framing partly downstream* — slightly weaker than an earlier unmodified *moderate*. The *khawāṭir* / *riyāʾ* split is the article's clearest worked example of how the test produces different scores for tradition-internal claims that surface-level convergence would group together.

The independence problem for Sufism is uneven, and the [[research/neoplatonist-common-cause-introspection-architecture-2026-05-16|Neoplatonist audit]] tightens the picture against the article's earlier framing. An earlier draft attributed the "cleanest historical-isolation defence" to early-Sufi practice — Muḥāsibī (d. 857 CE) predating the major Sufi-Vedāntic exchanges — but the audit shifts that attribution to Advaita and finds early Sufism carries a *substantial* Neoplatonist-via-Christian-monastic common-cause channel: Evagrius Ponticus's late-4th-century *logismoi* doctrine (eight incoming thoughts to be discerned by source via *diakrisis*) is the direct structural ancestor of the Sufi *khawāṭir* taxonomy, prefiguring Muḥāsibī's *muḥāsaba* programme by ~400 years through Syriac monastic transmission into the Levantine Christian context where 9th-c. Baghdad Sufism formed. The al-Kindī circle's *Theology of Aristotle* (the Arabic Plotinus, mid-9th c.) was in circulation in Baghdad in Muḥāsibī's lifetime as a second *available-not-proven* channel. Ibn ʿArabī (13th c.) synthesised heavy Neoplatonic and arguably Vedāntic influences into his theory of *khayāl* and *barzakh*; the audit weights early-Sufi practice more heavily than late-Sufi metaphysical synthesis but no longer assigns it the cleanest-defence status.

## Stoic Propatheia: The Strongest Despite-Commitments Observation {#stoic-propatheia}

The Stoic cognitive theory of emotion held that emotions are *judgements*: a *phantasia* (impression) presents itself, the agent gives *synkatathesis* (assent), and the assent constitutes the passion. Zeno (c. 300 BCE) and Chrysippus (c. 280–207 BCE) developed this into a comprehensive psychology in which the sage's distinguishing feature is well-trained discrimination of which impressions warrant endorsement. On Frede's influential reading (Frede 1983), *phantasia katalēptikē* — the "cognitive impression" — is the impression that *would warrant* assent (the would-warrant status distinguished from the agent's actual recognition); other readings parse the term differently (Sextus-tradition readings treat *katalēptikē* as a causal-imprint relation; Sedley passages place warrant-status differently). The article uses Frede's reading because it sharpens the [[voids/noetic-feelings-void|noetic-feelings void]] parallel, with the attribution explicit so the parallel's force is conditional on Frede's reconstruction. This much is framework-level architecture: citing it as cluster evidence at the framework level would be circular.

The most clearly architecturally-refined observation in the three traditions, on Sorabji's reading of the Stoic tradition's accommodation of first movements, is Seneca's *propatheia* doctrine, developed in *De Ira* in the first century CE. *Propatheia* names involuntary affective reactions — startle, blanching, blushing, the first jolt of fear — that *precede* the cognitive assent constituting a full emotion. Even the sage experiences *propatheia*; the structural feature is *not* eliminable by introspective practice. On Sorabji 2000's historiographic reconstruction — the scholarly anchor on which the article's *propatheia* analysis rests — the doctrine was *added* to handle empirical observations the pure cognitive theory did *not* predict, with the theory in its earliest formulation implying that no affective response is possible without prior assent and the manifest fact of startle responses forcing the framework to accommodate a separate sub-conscious affective layer operating upstream of conscious assent.

The Sorabji reconstruction is not unanimous. Inwood and Long argue that "first movements" were implicit in earlier Stoic cognitive theory — the *hormē* / *synkatathesis* / *pathos* sequence already needed pre-assent affective material — and that Seneca's contribution was terminological refinement rather than architectural accommodation under empirical pressure. If Inwood and Long are closer to right, the despite-commitments rating collapses to because-prediction. The audit cannot resolve the philological dispute; it conditions the rating: *propatheia* is the most clearly despite-commitments observation *on Sorabji's historiography*, moderate-grade once Sorabji-dependence propagates into the score. The architectural parallel to the cluster — a sub-conscious affective layer operating *upstream* of conscious assent — survives both readings; only score-strength differs by historiographic school. Stoic *prosochē* (continuous attention to current impressions, impulses, and assents — central in Epictetus and Marcus Aurelius) is the practical correlate: the discipline presupposes that without such attention the agent defaults to automatic assent.

The independence problem for Stoicism is the contested Pyrrho-Buddhism contact hypothesis. Pyrrho (c. 360–c. 270 BCE) accompanied Alexander's Indian campaign; Diogenes Laertius (9.61–62) reports he developed his philosophy in contact with Indian *gymnosophists* on this campaign. Beckwith's 2015 *Greek Buddha* argues that Pyrrhonism structurally parallels the Buddhist three marks of existence; Bronkhorst contests the case. Zeno was a contemporary of the late Pyrrho, and Stoicism's emergence overlaps the Indo-Greek philosophical-contact era documented in the *Milindapañha*. The scholarship is unresolved; a clean fully-independent claim for Stoicism is *not* available, though the historical-isolation defence is contested rather than disproved.

A second independence problem cuts the other way: Albert Ellis (REBT, 1950s) and Aaron Beck (cognitive therapy, 1960s) *explicitly* drew on Stoicism. Robertson 2019 documents the lineage in detail (*The Philosophy of Cognitive-Behavioural Therapy*, esp. chs. 4–6 on the strategic/automatic distinction's Stoic genealogy), with Beck's strategic/automatic distinction described as a modern reformulation of the *dogmata*/*phantasiai* distinction; the parallel is therefore on Robertson's attribution rather than asserted as transparent. The modern cognitive-psychology evidence the cluster cites is therefore *partially* downstream of Stoic categories, and the despite-commitments status of modern cognitive-psychology evidence with respect to Stoicism is *weaker* than its despite-commitments status with respect to Buddhism or Advaita.

## Scoring the Channel

Returning to the [[apex/taxonomy-of-voids|taxonomy of voids]]'s four criteria, with the cross-tradition channel as the unit of analysis rather than the whole cluster:

- **Signature specificity.** Moderate. Some traditional doctrines map directly onto specific cluster faces — *khawāṭir* onto [[voids/source-attribution-void|source-attribution]] (on the cleaner Ghazālī-*riyāʾ* rating; the *khawāṭir* taxonomy itself earns force only with respect to external frameworks), *propatheia* onto [[voids/noetic-feelings-void|noetic-feelings]] (conditional on Sorabji's historiography), hidden *riyāʾ* and *vāsanā* onto [[voids/confabulation-void|confabulation]] (Generation face), *ahaṃkāra* onto [[voids/narrative-void|narrative]]. Other doctrinal claims match the *general* metacognitive-unreliability signature better than they match a specific cluster face.
- **Structured persistence.** Strong. The doctrines under audit have persisted under sustained intra-tradition critique across centuries: *adhyāsa* from the 8th century, *muḥāsaba* and *khawāṭir* from the 9th, hidden *riyāʾ* from the 11th, *propatheia* from the 1st. Each has produced extensive secondary literature within its tradition without doctrinal collapse.
- **Cross-observer convergence.** Calibration-grade; *low-moderate*, with the Neoplatonist-audit caveat now discharged. The [[research/neoplatonist-common-cause-introspection-architecture-2026-05-16|2026-05-16 Neoplatonist common-cause audit]] yields *differentiated weights*: **weak (≈ no discount)** for Stoicism, because Plotinus is downstream of the Stoa — *propatheia*, *phantasia katalēptikē*, *synkatathesis* all pre-date Plotinus by ~500 years; **weak-to-negligible** for Advaita, on the Bréhier-Armstrong-Hacker-Aklan 2018 (*Wandering Lotuses*) consensus that Plotinian-Śaṅkara parallels are "metaphorical ways of expression" rather than structural connection; **moderate-to-substantial** for Sufism via two routes — Christian-monastic-via-Evagrius (*logismoi* + *diakrisis* + *antirrhēsis*, 4th c., prefiguring *khawāṭir* and *muḥāsaba* through Syriac transmission) and direct Arabic Plotinus via the al-Kindī circle's *Theology of Aristotle* contemporaneous with Muḥāsibī. The overall convergence holds at *low-moderate*; Stoic and Advaitic components carry their architectural-specifics ratings undischarged, while Sufism carries a sharper discount than the article previously applied, and the "cleanest historical-isolation defence" attribution shifts from early-Sufi practice to Advaita. The most consequential residual gap — whether Evagrian *logismoi* itself reduces to Stoicism (Evagrius engaged Stoic *first movements* directly via *antirrhēsis*), making Stoicism the single upstream source for both the Christian-Sufi chain and the modern Stoic-CBT chain — is named in §"Honest Limitations."
- **Framework independence.** Moderate. The architectural-specifics claims survive translation across cognitivist, embodied-cognition, and predictive-processing frameworks once stripped of their authoring metaphysics. The framework-level claims (witness vs instrument, divine vs satanic source, judgement-constituted passion) are framework-shaped and earn no independence credit.

The cross-tradition channel's contribution to the parent exhibit needs three separate accountings the surface framing previously elided. *What the kleshas-only framing claimed*: moderate convergence at the Buddhist boundary, partially discounted for soteriology-level requirement that ordinary cognition mislead. *What the cross-tradition audit adds*: architectural breadth across four traditions; despite-commitments observations passing the §"Architectural-Specifics Test" (Ghazālī's hidden *riyāʾ*; *propatheia* on Sorabji's reading; the four-faculty *antaḥkaraṇa* subdivision); a cleaner separation of architectural-specifics from framework-level claims. *What the cross-tradition audit subtracts*: common-cause discounts from Pyrrho-Buddhism, late-medieval Sufism-Vedānta synthesis, the Stoic-CBT lineage, the Albahari two-tier reduction risk; and now the *audited* Neoplatonist channel which contributes a substantial Sufi-specific discount (Evagrian-via-Christian-monastic prefiguration of *khawāṭir* + *muḥāsaba*; Arabic Plotinus contemporaneous with Muḥāsibī; Avicennan-Neoplatonist framing of later Sufi inner-faculty psychology) but leaves Stoic and Advaitic ratings unaffected. The *net* score is *low-moderate at the cross-tradition boundary* — numerically *lower* than the kleshas-only "moderate" baseline. This is re-calibration with new disconfirming information, not score-improvement. The audit's positive contribution is *honest accounting*: the broader the scope, the more common-cause discounts surface, and the calibration that emerges is the honest one even when numerically lower than the narrower framing.

## Where the Modern Reception Sits

Five contemporary programmes operationalise the cross-tradition evidence. Ganeri's *Attention, Not Self* (2017) treats classical Indian texts as primary sources in continuing dialogue with contemporary debates — a demonstration that Indian architectural claims translate into contemporary cognitive-philosophical vocabulary without metaphysical commitment. Albahari's *Analytical Buddhism* (2006, chs. 6–7; 2009 *JCS*) argues that the unified self is a two-tiered illusion drawing on both Theravāda Buddhism *and Advaita Vedānta*; if correct, the cross-tradition convergence reduces to a single architectural claim re-named across two traditions, weakening rather than strengthening the cross-observer score. Thompson's *Waking, Dreaming, Being* (2015) advocates contemplative phenomenology as a *partner* in scientific investigation. Sorabji's *Emotion and Peace of Mind* (2000) anchors the *propatheia* finding on the historiography the article uses, with the Inwood-Long dissent noted in §"Stoic Propatheia." Schwyzer 1960 and Hendrix 2005 read Plotinus as articulating a *framework-level* claim of bounded conscious access (*Enn.* IV.3.30) — the upstream Neoplatonist contribution to the convergence, distinguishable from the architectural-specifics findings the article retains; Adamson 2002 documents the al-Kindī circle's mid-9th-century mediation of Plotinian psychology into Baghdad.

## Honest Limitations

The audit's translation across tradition vocabularies is *interpretive* throughout. A tradition-internal scholar can reasonably argue that stripping framework-specific metaphysics changes the doctrine, and the parallels named here — *khawāṭir* with source-monitoring failure, *propatheia* with sub-assent affective machinery, *ahaṃkāra* with the Gazzaniga interpreter, hidden *riyāʾ* with confabulatory motivation-construction — are calibrated *plausibilities* of translation, not tradition-internal commitments.

The translation-construction objection deserves its own naming. A Churchland-style eliminative materialist would argue that the architectural specifics the article reads out of three tradition-internal vocabularies are *constructions* of the modern source-monitoring framework projected onto the source texts. On the stronger version: the cross-tradition channel isolates three texts whose modern reception the framework integrates, and the cross-observer convergence runs through one observer — the modern translator. The article cannot definitively refute the objection because the test of vocabulary-stripping survival is itself applied by the framework whose neutrality is at issue. The honest accounting: if translation construction is heavily in play, the architectural-specifics convergence may be *one* observation rather than three, and the channel's contribution may be calibration-grade-and-possibly-null. The article's score holds the test as locally usable while marking the Churchland-style worry as a live possibility the wider methodology has not yet ruled out.

The historical-isolation defence is *contested* rather than clean. Pyrrho/Buddhism (Beckwith 2015 vs Bronkhorst), Sufism/Vedānta (well-documented for late synthesisers, weaker for early empirical practitioners), and Stoic-CBT (a documented common-cause channel for the modern cognitive-psychology evidence) all introduce partial common-cause channels. The Neoplatonist channel — initially flagged as the most worrying un-audited discount — was audited 2026-05-16 and yielded differentiated weights: weak for Stoicism (Plotinus is downstream of the Stoa), weak-to-negligible for Advaita (Aklan 2018 and the Bréhier-Armstrong settlement rule out structural Plotinian-Śaṅkara connection), and moderate-to-substantial for Sufism (Evagrian *logismoi* and the Arabic Plotinus). The single most consequential residual gap is *not* the Neoplatonist channel itself but the *Stoic reduction of the Christian-monastic channel*: if Evagrian *logismoi* is itself a Christianised Stoic doctrine (Evagrius engaged *first movements* directly via *antirrhēsis*), then the Sufi-via-Christian-monastic common-cause and the modern Stoic-CBT common-cause may share a single upstream source — making *Stoicism* the load-bearing channel from which the others descend, and the cross-tradition convergence may collapse closer to Advaita-plus-Stoicism-plus-downstream-Stoic-influences than to three independent observations. This reduction is named as the open question on which a sharper cross-tradition score depends.

A defensible epistemically conservative reading — that the channel earns *no* upgrade over the kleshas-only framing, and indeed may sit at slightly lower calibration once translation-construction and Neoplatonist-common-cause discounts are honoured — the article does not adopt but does not refute. The cross-architecture-via-LLMs channel identified in the [[topics/cross-species-channel-introspection-architecture-independence-scoring|cross-species channel article]] remains the most architecturally distant cross-observer evidence currently available, contributing substrate-distance triangulation the cross-tradition channel structurally cannot supply.
