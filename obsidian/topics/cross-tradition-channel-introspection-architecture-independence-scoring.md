The cross-tradition channel is one of the four structural defences against the [[project/common-cause-null|common-cause null]] catalogued in the [[introspection-architecture-independence-scoring|introspection-architecture independence scoring exhibit]] — alongside patient-population dissociation, healthy-adult experimentation, and cross-species substrate evidence. For the four-void cluster (narrative, source-attribution, noetic-feelings, confabulation), the parent exhibit calibrated the Buddhist *kleshas* parallel at *moderate* and explicitly deferred the three-tradition audit across Advaita Vedānta, Sufi practice, and Stoicism. This article runs that audit. The result is *calibration-grade rather than load-bearing* convergence, concentrated in two architectural-specifics-level parallels that survive translation across frameworks: the Sufi *khawāṭir* taxonomy as a direct structural analogue of the [[voids/source-attribution-void|source-attribution void]] (explained in [[#sufi-khawatir|the *khawāṭir* section below]]), and Seneca's *propatheia* doctrine as the single clearest despite-commitments observation across the three traditions (explained in [[#stoic-propatheia|the *propatheia* section below]]). The framework-level prediction — that each tradition's soteriological commitments predict introspective unreliability and therefore earn no independence credit — is partially correct but misses the architectural refinements each tradition added under empirical pressure.

This article is the cross-tradition half of the introspection-architecture cluster's per-channel audit, paired with the [[topics/cross-species-channel-introspection-architecture-independence-scoring|cross-species channel article]]. It walks the three traditions, scores each by despite-commitments / because-prediction at the *architectural-specifics* level, names the historical-isolation problems the [[project/common-cause-null|common-cause null discipline]]'s independence test requires, and updates the parent exhibit's Criterion 3 (cross-observer convergence) score with calibrated caveats.

## The Framework-Level Trap

The most common failure mode in cross-tradition arguments is reading framework-level predictions as independent observations. Each of the three traditions includes a soteriological doctrine whose central diagnostic claim is that ordinary cognition is unreliable: Advaita's *avidyā* (ignorance) is the framework's central explanandum; Sufism's *nafs ammāra* is by definition deceptive; Stoicism identifies passion with *false* judgement. Citing each tradition's general claim that introspection fails would be circular — the diagnostic is what each framework was built to deliver.

The [[project/voids-circularity-discount|voids-circularity discount]] requires that the cross-tradition channel earn its weight on architectural-specifics-level findings that the authoring framework did *not* predict. The audit concentrates on doctrinal additions the framework had to *accommodate* rather than derive, empirical features the practice tradition discovered through self-observation, and structural claims that survive translation when stripped of their framework-specific metaphysics. Only the architectural-specifics layer can pass the despite-commitments test; framework-level convergence is calibration-only.

## Advaita Vedānta: The Architectural Picture

Shankara's eighth-century *Adhyāsa Bhāṣya* — the introductory section of his commentary on the *Brahma Sūtras* — opens with *adhyāsa* ("superimposition") as a structural cognitive failure: properties of the physical body are projected onto the Ātman, and properties of the Ātman onto the body. Shankara treats *adhyāsa* as a fact about ordinary cognition rather than a hypothesis, and notes that earlier schools discussed it under other names — suggesting empirical debate, not a single school's doctrinal invention.

The architectural specifics live in the *antaḥkaraṇa* ("inner instrument") doctrine classical Advaita inherited from Sāṃkhya/Yoga: a fourfold structure — *manas* (sensory mind), *buddhi* (discriminating intellect), *ahaṃkāra* (I-maker), *citta* (impression-storehouse) — inert by nature, with consciousness reflecting in it (*pratibimba*). The witness has access to the *outputs* of the instrument but not to its inner workings; discrimination (*viveka*) between witness and instrument is the soteriological task *because* the architectural feature makes them ordinarily indistinguishable. The *vāsanā* and *saṃskāra* doctrine extends the picture downward: latent dispositional tendencies operate outside conscious awareness and drive current cognition — the closest Advaita-tradition analogue to the *implicit causal theory* hypothesis Nisbett & Wilson invoke.

Scoring. The framework-level claim (witness with bounded access to instrument) is *because-prediction* — Advaita's soteriology requires it. The four-faculty subdivision of the *antaḥkaraṇa* is *moderate* despite-commitments — empirically motivated within the practice tradition rather than mechanically derived from the soteriology. The *ahaṃkāra*-as-narrator function is a structural parallel to the Gazzaniga interpreter that survives stripping the Vedāntic metaphysics. None of these architectural specifics earns more than calibration weight on its own; jointly they extend the architectural finding into pre-modern Indian thought.

The Map does not collapse the bounded witness into Brahman, and the Advaita *jīva*-into-Brahman move is where the Map and Advaita diverge. The architectural-specifics parallel survives the divergence because the cluster's signature is *bounded access*, not the metaphysical identity of the bound entity.

## Sufi Practice: Khawāṭir and Hidden Riyāʾ {#sufi-khawatir}

The strongest single point of structural convergence across the three traditions is the Sufi *khawāṭir* taxonomy. *Khawāṭir* are incoming thoughts arising in consciousness; the Sufi science classifies them by source — divine (*rabbānī*), angelic (*malakī*), egoic (*nafsānī*), satanic (*shayṭānī*). The taxonomy *presupposes* that source is *not* self-evident — otherwise the science of discrimination would be unnecessary. The spiritual master is said to "possess the science of *khawāṭir*" and to identify sources the disciple cannot.

Strip the metaphysical typology, and what remains is exactly the [[voids/source-attribution-void|source-attribution void]]'s architectural claim: thoughts arrive without delivered source-tags, and source-attribution requires reconstructive work. The Johnson-Hashtroudi-Lindsay 1993 source-monitoring framework names the same architectural fact in cognitive-psychology vocabulary. The Sufi tradition discovered the architectural feature through *muḥāsaba* — the systematic self-examination programme al-Muḥāsibī (d. 857 CE) pioneered. His diagnostic insight was that ostensibly pious practices (wearing wool, night recitation, fasting) can foster *riyāʾ* (hypocrisy) or *kibr* (pride) — the same outward action can have a hidden self-aggrandising motive the agent does not see.

Al-Ghazālī's eleventh-century *Iḥyāʾ ʿulūm al-dīn* extends Muḥāsibī's finding into a doctrine of *ghurūr* (self-delusion) and *riyāʾ khafiyy* (hidden hypocrisy). Ghazālī treats hidden *riyāʾ* not as a failure of moral effort but as a *structural* feature of how the ego operates — the architecture of motivation includes a self-display function whose operation is invisible to the agent. The doctrine that *riyāʾ* "can visit you always" at the beginning, middle, or end of an act of worship is a structural claim that hidden self-display agendas accompany apparently virtuous behaviour as a default architectural fact, not an occasional moral lapse. This is the strongest cross-tradition parallel to the Wheatley-Haidt and Nisbett-Wilson findings on post-hoc rationalisation that anchor the [[voids/confabulation-void|confabulation void]]'s Generation face.

Scoring. The *khawāṭir* taxonomy's *metaphysical* typology (divine vs satanic) is framework-internal because-prediction. The *architectural* claim that source-tags are non-delivered is *moderate-to-high* despite-commitments: practitioners discovered the non-delivery through *muḥāsaba*, the taxonomy handled empirical observations of source-ambiguity in practice, and the structural feature survives stripping the metaphysical typology. Hidden *riyāʾ* is *moderate-to-high* despite-commitments for the same reason: Ghazālī's framing has the structure of empirical observation forcing diagnostic refinement rather than soteriological deduction.

The independence problem for Sufism is uneven. Muḥāsibī (9th century) predates the major Sufi-Vedāntic exchanges and is the most independently-Islamic source cited here; the *khawāṭir* taxonomy and *muḥāsaba* practice carry the cleanest historical-isolation defence. Ibn ʿArabī (13th century) by contrast synthesised heavy Neoplatonic and arguably Vedāntic influences into his theory of *khayāl* (imagination) and *barzakh* (isthmus), and is *less* useful for the cross-tradition channel because the Neoplatonic common-cause channel is substantial. The audit weights early-Sufi practice more heavily than late-Sufi metaphysical synthesis.

## Stoic Propatheia: The Strongest Despite-Commitments Observation {#stoic-propatheia}

The Stoic cognitive theory of emotion held that emotions are *judgements*: a *phantasia* (impression) presents itself, the agent gives *synkatathesis* (assent), and the assent constitutes the passion. Zeno (c. 300 BCE) and Chrysippus (c. 280–207 BCE) developed this into a comprehensive psychology in which the sage's distinguishing feature is well-trained discrimination of which impressions warrant endorsement. *Phantasia katalēptikē* — the "cognitive impression" — is the impression that *would warrant* assent, but the Stoics carefully distinguished the would-warrant status from the agent's actual recognition of it. This much is framework-level architecture: the distinction between appearance and warrant is *the* Stoic technical apparatus, and citing it as evidence for the cluster's [[voids/noetic-feelings-void|noetic-feelings void]] at the framework level would be circular.

The single strongest despite-commitments observation in the three traditions is Seneca's *propatheia* doctrine, developed in *De Ira* in the first century CE. *Propatheia* names involuntary affective reactions — startle, blanching, blushing, the first jolt of fear — that *precede* the cognitive assent constituting a full emotion. Even the sage experiences *propatheia*; the structural feature is *not* eliminable by introspective practice. Critically, the doctrine was *added* to handle empirical observations the pure cognitive theory did *not* predict. The theory in its earliest formulation implied that no affective response is possible without prior assent; the manifest fact that startle responses occur prior to and independent of assent forced the framework to accommodate a *separate* sub-conscious affective layer operating upstream of conscious assent.

This is architectural refinement under empirical pressure. The cognitive theory predicted X; the empirical observation was not-X at the level of involuntary first movements; the framework absorbed not-X by positing a separate architectural feature the original theory did not require. The structural parallel to the cluster is direct: a sub-conscious affective layer operates *upstream* of conscious assent, the agent cannot prevent its operation, only train assent-withholding. Seneca, working without modern psychology, identified the architectural fact within his framework's terms. The Stoic introspective practice *prosochē* (continuous attention to one's current impressions, impulses, and assents — central in Epictetus and Marcus Aurelius) supplies the practical correlate: the discipline *presupposes* that without such attention the agent defaults to automatic assent.

The independence problem for Stoicism is the contested Pyrrho-Buddhism contact hypothesis. Pyrrho (c. 360–c. 270 BCE) accompanied Alexander's Indian campaign; Diogenes Laertius reports he developed his philosophy *in India*. Beckwith's 2015 *Greek Buddha* argues that Pyrrhonism structurally parallels the Buddhist three marks of existence; Bronkhorst contests the case. Zeno was a contemporary of the late Pyrrho, and Stoicism's emergence overlaps the Indo-Greek philosophical-contact era documented in the *Milindapañha*. The scholarship is unresolved; a clean fully-independent claim for Stoicism is *not* available, though the historical-isolation defence is contested rather than disproved.

A second independence problem cuts the other way: Albert Ellis (REBT, 1950s) and Aaron Beck (cognitive therapy, 1960s) *explicitly* drew on Stoicism. Beck's strategic/automatic distinction directly parallels the Stoic *dogmata*/*phantasiai* distinction. The modern cognitive-psychology evidence the cluster cites is therefore *partially* downstream of Stoic categories, and the despite-commitments status of modern cognitive-psychology evidence with respect to Stoicism is *weaker* than its despite-commitments status with respect to Buddhism or Advaita.

## Scoring the Channel

Returning to the [[apex/taxonomy-of-voids|taxonomy of voids]]'s four criteria, with the cross-tradition channel as the unit of analysis rather than the whole cluster:

- **Signature specificity.** Moderate. Some traditional doctrines map directly onto specific cluster faces — *khawāṭir* onto [[voids/source-attribution-void|source-attribution]], *propatheia* onto [[voids/noetic-feelings-void|noetic-feelings]], hidden *riyāʾ* and *vāsanā* onto [[voids/confabulation-void|confabulation]] (Generation face), *ahaṃkāra* onto [[voids/narrative-void|narrative]]. Other doctrinal claims match the *general* metacognitive-unreliability signature better than they match a specific cluster face.
- **Structured persistence.** Strong. The doctrines under audit have persisted under sustained intra-tradition critique across centuries: *adhyāsa* from the 8th century, *muḥāsaba* and *khawāṭir* from the 9th, hidden *riyāʾ* from the 11th, *propatheia* from the 1st. Each has produced extensive secondary literature within its tradition without doctrinal collapse.
- **Cross-observer convergence.** Calibration-grade. The despite-commitments observations across three traditions point at a small set of architectural specifics: source-tag non-delivery, sub-assent affective machinery, latent dispositional contamination of report, a narrator/I-maker function. The convergence is real at the architectural-specifics level and partial at the framework level. Historical-isolation problems (Pyrrho/Buddhism, Sufism/Vedānta, Stoicism/CBT) discount the convergence weight to *low-moderate* rather than full strength.
- **Framework independence.** Moderate. The architectural-specifics claims survive translation across cognitivist, embodied-cognition, and predictive-processing frameworks once stripped of their authoring metaphysics. The framework-level claims (witness vs instrument, divine vs satanic source, judgement-constituted passion) are framework-shaped and earn no independence credit.

The cross-tradition channel's contribution to the parent exhibit is *calibration-grade architectural-feature triangulation*. The parent's Criterion 3 score moves from "moderate at the contemplative-tradition boundary (Buddhist *kleshas*)" to *low-moderate at the cross-tradition boundary including Buddhism, Advaita, Sufism, and Stoicism*. This is a small upgrade over the *kleshas*-only framing, not the strong upgrade the surface-level convergence would suggest.

## Where the Modern Reception Sits

Four contemporary programmes operationalise the cross-tradition evidence. Ganeri's *Attention, Not Self* (2017) treats classical Indian texts as primary sources in continuing dialogue with contemporary debates; the witness/instrument distinction is one of the architectural features Ganeri and his critics discuss in non-Vedāntic terms — a demonstration that Indian architectural claims translate into contemporary cognitive-philosophical vocabulary without metaphysical commitment. Albahari's *Analytical Buddhism* (2006) argues the unified self is a two-tiered illusion drawing on both Theravāda Buddhism *and Advaita Vedānta*; the framework *predicts* the introspection-architecture signature, so if Albahari's identification is correct the cross-tradition convergence reduces to a single architectural claim re-named across two traditions, weakening rather than strengthening the cross-observer score. Thompson's *Waking, Dreaming, Being* (2015) advocates contemplative phenomenology as a *partner* in scientific investigation, providing methodological scaffolding for treating trained-introspector evidence as cognitive-science input. Sorabji's *Emotion and Peace of Mind* (2000) is the authoritative scholarly anchor for the *propatheia* finding, documenting its *despite-commitments* status across the eight centuries from Plato to Augustine.

## Relation to Site Perspective

The exhibit honours the [[project/voids-circularity-discount|voids-circularity discount]] at the channel level. The three traditions' architectural-specifics claims are compatible with both dualism and physicalism: the bounded-witness reading is one available reading of Advaita's antaḥkaraṇa, of the Sufi *khawāṭir* taxonomy, and of Stoic *propatheia*, but each tradition is also compatible with substantively non-dualist readings. The Map's framework supplies the *significance* of the architectural feature for [[tenets|Tenet 1 (Dualism)]] and [[tenets|Tenet 3 (Bidirectional Interaction)]]; the cross-tradition evidence supplies the architectural-feature's empirical extension only.

The honest framing: the cross-tradition channel partially fills the gap the parent exhibit named as an open programme, does so through despite-commitments observations of architectural specifics that survive translation across frameworks, and earns *low-moderate* rather than load-bearing weight against the strongest common-cause null. The cluster's empirical anchoring remains concentrated in the patient-population and healthy-adult despite-commitments observations the [[introspection-architecture-independence-scoring|parent exhibit]] catalogues; the cross-tradition channel contributes calibration-grade architectural breadth, neither closing the four-void cluster's case nor abandoning the structural defence the contemplative literatures can supply.

## Honest Limitations

The audit's translation across tradition vocabularies is *interpretive* throughout. A tradition-internal scholar can reasonably argue that stripping framework-specific metaphysics changes the doctrine, and the parallels named here — *khawāṭir* with source-monitoring failure, *propatheia* with sub-assent affective machinery, *ahaṃkāra* with the Gazzaniga interpreter, hidden *riyāʾ* with confabulatory motivation-construction — are calibrated *plausibilities* of translation, not tradition-internal commitments.

The historical-isolation defence is *contested* rather than clean. Pyrrho/Buddhism (Beckwith 2015 vs Bronkhorst), Sufism/Vedānta (well-documented for late synthesisers, weaker for early empirical practitioners), and Stoic-CBT (a documented common-cause channel for the modern cognitive-psychology evidence) all introduce partial common-cause channels. The Neoplatonist contemplative tradition is a candidate single common ancestor influencing late Stoicism, early Sufism, and Vedāntic-Islamic interpreters; the audit has not retrieved a focused scholarly assessment of the Neoplatonic common-cause weight, and this is the largest residual gap in the channel's independence calibration.

A defensible epistemically conservative reading — that the channel earns *no* upgrade over the kleshas-only framing — the article does not adopt but does not refute. The article's position is that architectural-specifics-level despite-commitments observations earn *a small upgrade*; a reasonable critic can hold that the upgrade is smaller than claimed here or absent altogether. The cross-architecture-via-LLMs channel identified in the [[topics/cross-species-channel-introspection-architecture-independence-scoring|cross-species channel article]] remains the most architecturally distant cross-observer evidence currently available, and contributes substrate-distance triangulation the cross-tradition channel structurally cannot supply.
