When consciousness is disrupted, the channels of memory and awareness do not fail together — they fail in a fixed order, with the [[anoetic-noetic-autonoetic-consciousness|autonoetic / episodic]] channel (Tulving 1985) most vulnerable and the procedural / anoetic channel most preserved. The [[memory-system-vulnerability-hierarchies-as-interface-evidence|memory-system vulnerability hierarchy]] develops that ordering as interface evidence. This article develops a feature of the hierarchy that the ordering alone does not capture: the order in which a system degrades is often *not* the mirror image of the order in which it recovers. Autonoetic consciousness is the last channel to return when a deeply anaesthetised brain emerges, yet it is among the first and most prominent channels to return in terminal lucidity — and the difference is not random. The Unfinishable Map reads this *direction-dependence* — recovery-order asymmetry — as a distinct evidence type: it is the *direction* of interface change, not only the substrate state, that the signature tracks.

The central claim is calibrated narrowly, in keeping with [[evidential-status-discipline|the evidential-status discipline]]. Recovery-order asymmetry *constrains* production accounts; it does not *establish* the filter reading. A substrate-symmetric production account — one on which the same neural substrate both generates and recovers each channel — predicts, in its simplest form, that recovery should retrace degradation: the last channel to fail should be the first to return, because both orderings are read off the same substrate-vulnerability profile. Asymmetry violates that simplest expectation. Production accounts can recover the asymmetry, but only by adding auxiliary assumptions (direction-specific mechanisms, hysteresis terms, dying-process-specific neuromodulation). The article's claim is that asymmetry raises the cost and specificity demanded of production accounts, not that it rules them out. The filter reading appears to derive a direction-sensitive signature from its core architecture, where production accounts must add one — though the asymmetry data are underdetermined by the evidence on this point, since a production account willing to pay the per-case cost can accommodate every signature the cluster contains.

## Anaesthesia: Induction Is Not the Reverse of Emergence

[[anaesthesia-and-the-consciousness-interface|Anaesthesia]] is the anchor case because the perturbation is reversible, dose-dependent, and can be measured in both directions within the same subject. The asymmetry shows up at two levels.

The first is the **hysteresis** of state transition. Sepúlveda et al. (2019) characterise *neural inertia* as the intrinsic resistance of neural circuits to changing state: inducing unconsciousness requires higher anaesthetic concentrations than restoring consciousness, so the loss-of-consciousness and recovery-of-consciousness thresholds do not coincide. The transition is governed by mutually inhibitory wake-active and anaesthesia-active populations forming a bistable switch; each state actively stabilises itself. The directional point is that the brain at a given anaesthetic concentration is in a *different* consciousness state depending on whether it is descending into or climbing out of unconsciousness. The same substrate state corresponds to two different conscious outcomes, distinguished only by the direction of travel.

The second is the **order in which channels recover**. On induction, autonoetic encoding is the first casualty: light sedation abolishes episodic memory of the procedure while semantic comprehension persists, and deeper sedation severs noetic access while implicit learning continues. On emergence, the autonoetic channel is the last to stabilise — patients regain arousal, then responsiveness, then orientation, with reliable autobiographical encoding returning latest. Recovery does not cleanly retrace induction in reverse; the trajectory is shaped by the active, distributed reconstitution process rather than by a simple unwinding of the descent.

That reconstitution is itself direction-asymmetric in a way recent work makes precise. Stone, Kelz, Proekt, and Wasilczuk (2025), in the *British Journal of Anaesthesia*, found that genetically identical mice anaesthetised under identical protocols emerged at times varying by at least two orders of magnitude, with within-animal variability matching between-animal variability; a deterministic pharmacokinetic-pharmacodynamic model failed where a stochastic neuronal-dynamics model fit, and no single brain region controlled emergence. The relevant point for asymmetry is structural: induction tracks drug concentration closely, while emergence is a distributed, stochastic, network-level reconstitution whose timing is *not* fixed by the macroscopic substrate state. The down-direction and the up-direction are governed by different dynamics. The variability's *interpretation* is held at a live-hypothesis tier — the data are equally compatible with classical neuronal noise and with interface indeterminacy, and nothing in the study discriminates (developed in [[stochastic-emergence-as-quantum-interface-evidence|the stochastic-emergence article]]). What the study contributes here is narrower and robust: emergence is not the deterministic reverse of induction.

## Terminal Lucidity: Recovery Under Continuing Degradation

[[terminal-lucidity-and-filter-transmission-theory|Terminal lucidity]] supplies the asymmetry's most striking case because the *direction of substrate change* and the *direction of conscious change* point opposite ways. Patients with advanced dementia or other severe neurological decline sometimes recover, in the hours or days before death, a marked degree of lucidity — recognising relatives, holding coherent conversation, recalling specific autobiographical episodes their long-degraded brain had appeared to have lost. Nahm and Greyson (2009) coined the term; their 2012 review with Kelly and Haraldsson collected 83 cases spanning 250 years, and Batthyány and Greyson's (2020) survey of medical and nursing staff produced 124 detailed reports, with more than 80% showing return of memory, orientation, and responsive verbal ability and the majority dying within hours to days.

The asymmetry is sharp. In deep-anaesthesia emergence, the autonoetic channel returns *last*. In terminal lucidity, autonoetic recollection often returns *first and most prominently* — the autobiographical content is exactly what makes the episodes remarkable. Two recoveries of the same channel proceed in opposite order relative to the others, and the distinguishing variable is the direction and context of the underlying change: an anaesthetised brain is recovering substrate function on a rising trajectory, while a terminally lucid brain is recovering conscious access on a *falling* substrate trajectory. The substrate continues to degrade; this is not the brain repairing itself in days.

The Map holds the calibration line here, as the terminal-lucidity treatment does. The case literature is entirely retrospective and selection-biased; rival readings may yet propose substrate-state mechanisms (paradoxical "decompression" of inhibition, a reactive neuromodulatory or noradrenergic surge) that absorb the timing without an interface. The narrower, defensible claim is that the *direction* of the autonoetic channel's return — first under falling substrate function, last under rising substrate function — is a signature that a substrate-symmetric production account does not predict from a single vulnerability profile, while a filter reading on which different components of the interface collapse in different orders may accommodate it more directly. The "may" is load-bearing: the comparison favours the filter reading only against the *simplest* production rival, and a more elaborate one absorbs the same signature.

## Dissociative States: Onset Is Not the Reverse of Recovery

[[clinical-dissociation-as-systematic-evidence|Dissociative states]] add a case with no structural substrate damage at all, so the asymmetry cannot be attributed to tissue loss or repair. In dissociative amnesia, autobiographical (autonoetic) access is selectively severed while semantic knowledge, procedural skill, and working memory remain intact. The *onset* can be gradual — a narrowing of accessible autobiographical content under stress. The *recovery*, when the dissociative barrier lifts, is frequently abrupt and complete: the memories return immediately and in full rather than being rebuilt incrementally. The bridge reconnects; it is not re-grown.

This onset-versus-recovery asymmetry matters because it decouples the asymmetry from any substrate-degradation story. There is nothing to repair, so a production account cannot appeal to differential regional recovery rates. The asymmetry has to be explained at the level of *connectivity between intact channels* — which seems to be precisely the channel architecture the filter reading describes, and which a sophisticated production reading must concede consciousness depends on. The disagreement, once again, is located not over the data but over what kind of relationship that connectivity is. Depersonalisation sharpens the point one level deeper: autonoetic *content* can be present while the felt *mine-ness* it normally carries is decoupled, so even within the autonoetic channel the sub-features can dissociate and, in principle, recover in a different order than they were lost.

## The Catalogue of Direction-of-Interface-Change Signatures

Recovery-order asymmetry is one member of a broader family of signatures in which the *direction* of interface change — not only the substrate state at a moment — is the load-bearing variable. The family has its own concept home at [[direction-of-interface-change|direction-of-interface-change signatures]], where the four members, their non-independence, and their evidential tier are catalogued for portable citation; the catalogue below is its source development.

- **Hysteresis of state transition.** The induction and emergence thresholds differ (neural inertia); the same substrate state yields different conscious outcomes depending on direction of travel.
- **Recovery-order reversal across mechanisms.** The autonoetic channel returns last on anaesthesia emergence and first in terminal lucidity — the same channel, opposite recovery order, different direction of substrate change.
- **Abrupt reconnection versus gradual onset.** Dissociative recovery is bridge-reconnection (immediate, complete), not the time-reverse of the gradual onset.
- **Direction-decoupled timing.** Anaesthetic emergence timing is stochastic and distributed where induction timing tracks drug concentration; the up-trajectory and down-trajectory obey different dynamics ([[stochastic-emergence-as-quantum-interface-evidence|developed here]]).

What unifies the family is that none of these signatures is captured by a substrate-state-at-a-time description. Each requires the explanation to be sensitive to *which way the system is moving*. The signatures are the temporal-derivative face of the same architecture the [[memory-system-vulnerability-hierarchies-as-interface-evidence|vulnerability hierarchy]] reads off the substrate-state face — and that architecture is what [[self-concealing-interface|the self-concealing-interface synthesis]] names as the seam-class register where the Map's evidence can live at all. This family extends the graded-channel-failure seam catalogued there: it adds the direction of interface change to the set of constrained asymmetries, while honouring the same calibration line — these signatures *constrain* production accounts, they do not *establish* the interface reading. They are an instance of [[cross-mechanism-convergence|cross-mechanism convergence]]: anaesthesia, dying, and dissociation share no common neural cause, yet each produces a direction-dependent signature, and that convergence is the explanandum.

## What Asymmetry Buys the Filter Reading

A substrate-symmetric production account — on which one substrate both generates each channel and recovers it — predicts in its simplest form that recovery retraces degradation. Both orderings are read off the same regional-vulnerability profile, so the channel that is most robust under degradation should also be the quickest to return, and vice versa. The cross-state data violate this: the autonoetic channel is most vulnerable under degradation in every case, yet its recovery position swings from last (anaesthesia emergence) to first (terminal lucidity) depending on the direction and context of substrate change.

A production account can recover the asymmetry. It must add a direction-specific mechanism: hysteresis terms for the bistable switch, a dying-process-specific neuromodulatory surge for terminal lucidity, a stochastic distributed-reconstitution model for emergence, a connectivity-gating story for dissociation. Each is independently reasonable. The point is the *additivity*: each asymmetry case requires its own auxiliary assumption, and the assumptions do not derive from a shared substrate-vulnerability profile because that profile, being direction-blind, predicts symmetry. This is where the [[mechanism-costs-dualism-thickness-quadrants|mechanism-costs cartography]] locates the comparison — production pays per-case at the accommodation layer.

The filter reading appears to derive a direction-sensitive signature from its core architecture without per-case additions. If channels differ in bandwidth and reconstruction cost, then *which* components of the interface block or reopen, and in *which order*, may depend on the direction and cause of the change — partial blockade closes high-cost channels first, while a different perturbation (the collapse of an inhibitory structure near death) could reopen a high-cost channel first. On this reading the asymmetry is the expected consequence of an architecture whose channels can be addressed in direction-dependent order, rather than a set of facts added to the framework after observation. The qualifier matters: that the signature *falls out of* the architecture rather than being *fitted to* the data is itself a framework-internal judgement the production theorist need not grant.

The advantage is comparative and bounded. The filter reading's explanatory move here is *direct* (a direction-sensitive signature falls out of channel architecture) where production's is *additive* (one auxiliary assumption per asymmetry case). That is a point on parsimony grounds at the mechanism-cost layer; it is not positive evidence that the filter reading is true, because a production account willing to pay the per-case cost absorbs every signature. Naming what would *discriminate* the readings — a focal perturbation producing a channel ordering one reading predicts and the other forbids — is the work of [[targeted-lesion-discriminating-tests-between-production-and-filter-readings-of-the-memory-hierarchy|the targeted-lesion discriminating-tests article]], and the dispute remains, by [[direct-refutation-discipline|the direct-refutation discipline]]'s lights, a [[discrimination-problem|discrimination-problem]]-class underdetermination at present.

## Evidential Calibration

Three points where the argument is structurally tempted to overclaim, and where it must not.

First, recovery-order asymmetry is *constraining*, not *establishing*. It removes the simplest substrate-symmetric production account — the one on which recovery retraces degradation off a single vulnerability profile — and leaves intact every production account willing to add direction-specific mechanisms. The honest verdict is that asymmetry raises the cost and specificity demanded of production accounts, not that it favours the filter reading over a more elaborate production rival the same data also fit.

Second, the strongest single case (terminal lucidity) rests on the weakest evidence base. The case literature is entirely retrospective, selection-biased, and lacks any prospective recording of neural activity during an episode. The asymmetry it exhibits is real in the reports; its mechanism is undetermined, and a substrate-state mechanism specific to the dying process remains a live rival. The article's claim rests on the *pattern across mechanisms*, not on terminal lucidity carrying the argument alone.

Third, the family of direction-of-interface-change signatures earns *framework-internal weight*, not a tier-upgrade. Assembling several direction-dependent signatures that cohere with the filter reading does not raise the empirical tier when the same signatures cohere with direction-specific production accounts. The convergence makes the Map's reading more articulated; it does not discriminate it from rivals absent an independent test only one reading predicts. The cluster is offered as accommodation evidence, held at the constrain level, with the discriminating test named as future work rather than present result.

## Relation to Site Perspective

**[[tenets#^dualism|Dualism]]** (Tenet 1) is engaged because direction-dependence fits a multi-channel interface architecture more directly than a substrate-emergent reading. A substrate that both generates and recovers consciousness predicts, in its simplest form, that recovery mirrors degradation; the cross-state asymmetry does not, and the filter reading derives the direction-sensitivity from architecture while production accounts add it per case. The reading is comparative and contested, not forced by the data.

**[[tenets#^bidirectional-interaction|Bidirectional Interaction]]** (Tenet 3) is engaged because the channel that swings most in recovery order — the autonoetic channel — is the one involving the most active subject-participation: re-experiencing, reconsolidation-under-direction, the assembly of an autobiographical narrative. If consciousness merely received from the brain, recovery order would be fixed by substrate readiness alone, and direction should not matter. That the autonoetic channel can return first under falling substrate function (terminal lucidity) and last under rising substrate function (anaesthesia emergence) favours a reading on which that channel is high-engagement in both directions, and so behaves differently depending on whether the interface is reopening or being reconstructed.

The asymmetry is one face of the broader disruption-cluster pattern: channels that fail independently, recover independently, and do so in a direction-dependent order. No single case carries the argument; the convergence across [[anaesthesia-and-the-consciousness-interface|anaesthesia]], [[terminal-lucidity-and-filter-transmission-theory|terminal lucidity]], and [[clinical-dissociation-as-systematic-evidence|dissociation]] does.
