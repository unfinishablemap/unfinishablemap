Interoception—the body's sense of its own physiological condition: cardiac, respiratory, gastric, thermal, and nociceptive signal, alongside the visceral chemistry that never reaches awareness at all—is the inward completion of The Unfinishable Map's survey of perceptual modalities. The Map has worked the same capability division across five exteroceptive and proprioceptive senses: the brain extracts and integrates candidate content without consciousness, and conscious experience supplies phenomenal unity, ownership, and flexible deployment. Interoception inherits the division but **breaks the headline asymmetry** that the [[cross-modal-capability-division|cross-modal synthesis]] established—and it breaks it on both terms. On the brain side, where exteroception shows no shared substrate across V1, the cochlea, and the olfactory receptor code, interoception *converges* onto a single visceral-integration cortex in the insula and anterior cingulate. On the mind side, where exteroception delivers detached perceptual objects, interoception delivers valenced, owned bodily feeling—the felt ground of selfhood rather than content presented to a subject. So the cross-modal asymmetry is an *exteroceptive* regularity, not a universal one. That is the article's reason to exist, and it sharpens the apex rather than merely extending it.

This double inversion is, on the Map's own discipline, equally a physicalist story. The convergent insular substrate is exactly what a physicalist predicts if the body needs one homeostatic integrator, and the strongest rival framing—interoceptive inference—predicts the affective, self-constituting character of visceral experience without any non-physical interface. The structural finding is therefore held as consonant with the interface reading, not probative of it, in the same register the apex holds the supramodal signature it surveys.

## What Interoception Is, and How It Divides

The division this article inherits is the [[capability-division-in-vision|capability division]] generalised inward. Most visceral regulation runs continuously below threshold: homeostatic and allostatic control of blood pressure, blood gas chemistry, hepatic metabolism, renal filtration, and immune state proceeds without any felt correlate, the brain-side labour at its most complete. The narrow band that crosses into awareness—heartbeat, breath, hunger, fullness, urgency, warmth and cold, pain—is the mind-side contribution. The [[interoceptive-void|interoceptive void]] maps the *silent* portion as a dark space: which channels are constitutively unfelt, which arrive only as low-resolution summaries, which are gated below threshold. This article takes the complementary subject—the labour split for the channels that *do* reach awareness, and the question the void does not ask: whether the apex's cross-modal asymmetry recurs when the survey turns inward. The void's silent floor is the boundary condition the interface treatment inherits, not its content.

A.D. Craig's foundational review fixes the subject. Interoception is "the sense of the physiological condition of the body," an afferent system carried by lamina I spinothalamic projections to the posterior insula, re-represented forward to the anterior insula (Craig, 2002). The receptor side is heterogeneous—the channels are a bundle, not a unified sense, as the void records—but the cortical destination converges. That convergence is the first half of the inversion.

## The Brain Side Converges

The cross-modal synthesis's most important negative finding is that nothing recurs on the brain side: vision's retinotopic feature maps, the cochlea's tonotopic decomposition, olfaction's combinatorial receptor code, and the skin's mechanoreceptor populations are unrelated solutions to unrelated transduction problems. The brain-side labour shares only its *form*, never its substrate. The synthesis treats this as a general law about perception.

Interoception is the counter-instance. Its afferent channels are as heterogeneous as exteroception's—cardiac, respiratory, gastric, thermal, nociceptive lines with distinct peripheral physiology—yet they converge on a *shared* cortical integrator. Craig identifies the insula, posterior-to-anterior, as the re-representation site for visceral afferents across these channels, with the anterior insula as the cortical image of the felt bodily self (Craig, 2002). Critchley and colleagues supplied direct evidence that this convergence carries the portion accessible to awareness: right anterior insular activity *predicts* heartbeat-detection accuracy, and local grey-matter volume in that region correlates with both objective accuracy and subjective visceral awareness (Critchley et al., 2004). Where exteroception's access machinery is distributed and modality-specific, interoceptive access has a localisable, convergent signature.

This matters for the apex's honesty. The synthesis's claim "the brain side does not recur" is true of the distance senses, whose separate transducers feed separate cortices. Interoception indicates the modality-specificity was *contingent on those separate transducers* rather than a structural necessity of perception. When a single integrator serves many visceral channels, the brain side converges. The asymmetry the apex names appears to be exteroceptive, and interoception is the inward case that marks its limit.

A caution belongs here by the Map's [[evidential-status-discipline|evidential-status discipline]]. A convergent insular integrator is precisely what a physicalist expects if the body requires one homeostatic controller: there is metabolic economy in routing visceral state to a common hub. The convergence is a *structural disanalogy with exteroception*, genuinely sharpening the apex's claim; it is not, by itself, evidence for an interface. The brain-side fact is neutral between the readings.

## The Mind Side Becomes Affect, Ownership, and Self

The second inversion concerns what reaches awareness. The cross-modal synthesis already flags that its mind-side "ownership" term is uneven—vivid in touch and proprioception, faint in smell—and notes that olfactory states may be "closer to affective or interoceptive states than to detached representations." Interoception is the limiting case at the vivid end. Visceral states are barely presented as detached perceptual *objects* at all. A felt heartbeat, a wave of nausea, the ache of cold, the pang of hunger: these arrive already valenced and already owned. There is no neutral interoceptive percept on which a verdict of pleasant or aversive is later pronounced—the affective tone is in the contact, as it is for the affective channel of [[tactile-consciousness-and-the-interface|cutaneous touch]] but more completely.

So the mind-side term does not merely recur weakly across the inward boundary; it changes *type*. In exteroception the mind side contributes the phenomenal unity of a perceived object—one heard stream, one felt surface, one flavour. In interoception the mind side is the affective ground of subjecthood itself. Three literatures converge on this reframing, and the Map engages all three as rivals rather than allies, because each is built to dispense with a non-physical interface.

Anil Seth's interoceptive-inference account treats felt emotion as active inference on the causes of interoceptive afferents, with the "embodied self" as the high-precision portion of those predictions (Seth, 2013). Lisa Feldman Barrett and W. Kyle Simmons's Embodied Predictive Interoception Coding model has agranular visceromotor cortices issue interoceptive predictions, unifying allostatic regulation and felt experience in one predictive architecture (Barrett & Simmons, 2015). Park and Tallon-Baudry propose that continuous visceral monitoring supplies a "neural subjective frame"—a constantly updated bodily reference from which first-person experience is generated (Park & Tallon-Baudry, 2014). On all three accounts, interoception is not content delivered *to* a subject but the felt substrate *of* subjecthood.

This is why interoception's mind side is affect-and-ownership rather than detached representation. It is also why interoception is the hardest case for the Map, treated below.

## The Empirical Tractability Interoception Affords

Interoception supplies a measurement the distance senses cannot match as cleanly: a quantifiable gap between what is felt and what is true of the body. Garfinkel and colleagues decomposed "knowing your own heart" into three dissociable dimensions—interoceptive *accuracy* (objective task performance), *sensibility* (subjective belief about one's own awareness), and *metacognitive awareness* (the correspondence between accuracy and confidence)—and showed they vary independently (Garfinkel et al., 2015). High sensibility frequently coexists with low accuracy; anxiety inflates reported sensation without improving it. The felt body and the actual body come apart along measurable axes.

This maps directly onto the capability division's deepest structure: the difference between what a system has *access to* and what is *true*. In the distance senses that gap is hard to isolate, because the stimulus and the percept are external and co-vary. Interoception puts the gap on the table, because the true bodily state is independently measurable and the felt state is independently reportable. The 2025 finding of Loescher, Haggard, and Tallon-Baudry sharpens it further: cardiac interoceptive processing *competes* with tactile perception for shared neural resources—prestimulus heartbeat-evoked activity over somatosensory cortex slows tactile reaction times—while the same cardiac signal, read out over integrative and default-mode regions, *facilitates* the encoding of self-relevance (Loescher et al., 2025). One visceral channel both crowds out an exteroceptive one and feeds the self-model, the channel-competition and self-relevance roles running in parallel. The datum gives the inward inversion an experimental edge: interoception is where the access-versus-truth gap, and the inward-versus-outward competition for awareness, are directly measurable.

## The Rival the Map Must Out-Accommodate

Interoceptive inference is the strongest physicalist account this article meets, and the Map's discipline forbids enlisting it. The active-inference framework closes the perception-and-emotion loop with no non-physical remainder: top-down predictions about bodily causes are tested against ascending visceral error, and felt emotion just *is* the high-precision posterior over those causes (Seth, 2013; Barrett & Simmons, 2015). Seth, Barrett, and the wider predictive-processing programme build physicalist models of perception and self; their formalism is metaphysically neutral, and the Map may adopt its mechanics, but the authors do not endorse—and the framework is constructed to avoid—a two-sided dualist reading. They are cited here as rivals to out-accommodate, not as evidence for the interface.

The accommodation runs as it does for [[perceptual-failure-and-the-interface|perception's other modalities]]. An interface operating below the level experience reaches would produce *exactly* the inferential architecture interoceptive inference describes: predictive loops, precision-weighting, the felt body as a corrected reconstruction rather than raw afferent contact. The convergent insular substrate and the predictive construction are what both pictures expect. The framework does not discriminate between them, and the Map states this rather than dressing the convergence as confirmation.

Where the Map locates its disagreement is the same place the cross-modal synthesis does: the *ownership* term. Active inference explains why visceral predictions become available to the system—why they drive autonomic adjustment, decision, and report. It is less clear that it explains why there is a subject the felt body is the body *of*. That gap is the Map's value-add, and it is candidly an argumentative claim rather than a place the Map holds direct cross-modal evidence the rival lacks.

## The Constitutive Reading the Map Does Not Share

A second rival runs in the opposite direction from active inference and conflicts with the Map more sharply. Antonio Damasio, and Park and Tallon-Baudry in a different register, hold that consciousness *arises from* the felt body: feelings are the source of consciousness, and the visceral subjective frame is what first-person experience is built from rather than one of its objects (Park & Tallon-Baudry, 2014; and the Damasio reading the [[interoceptive-void|interoceptive void]] records). On this constitutive view, interoception is not consciousness's contact with a substrate but the substrate from which consciousness is made.

This reading conflicts with the Map's commitment that the body is *had*, not constitutive—that consciousness is wider than the felt body, and most of the substrate is silent because it is owned rather than because it is foundational. The two readings are compatible with both interpretations of the same evidence. The convergent insular integrator, the predictive construction, the self-relevance role of cardiac signal: the evidence does not adjudicate whether the felt body *is* consciousness or is consciousness's most intimate object. Interoception is the modality where the choice between had-body and constitutive-body is *forced*—where the data press hardest on the question—but the data do not decide it. The Map engages the constitutive reading as a genuine rival and marks the disagreement at the framework boundary, rather than smuggling Damasio's evidence in as support for an interface he would reject.

## Where the Inward Inversion Is Constrained

Three calibrations keep the structural claim honest. First, the brain-side convergence is a disanalogy of *destination*, not of *origin*: interoception's peripheral channels are as heterogeneous as exteroception's, and the void's "there is no such thing as interoception" point stands—the single label hides cardiac, gastric, respiratory, thermal, and nociceptive lines with different ascending pathways. What converges is the integrator, not the transducers. The inversion is real but precisely located.

Second, the convergence on one integrator is, on the Map's [[common-cause-null|common-cause null]], a single shared upstream rather than many independent confirmations. Where the cross-modal synthesis cautioned that its convergent boundary is one pattern with shared architecture, the same caution applies inward with more force: a single insular hub is maximally a common cause, so the interoceptive evidence is one datum about the inward limit, not a stack of independent ones.

Third, the mind-side reframing—affect and selfhood rather than detached representation—does not by itself favour the interface. It favours *some* account on which interoception is self-constituting, and the leading such account is the physicalist one. The inversion changes what the apex's mind-side term means inward; it does not change the evidential register in which the term is held.

These constraints do not weaken the structural finding. They locate it: the cross-modal asymmetry is exteroceptive, interoception inverts both of its terms, and the inversion is held as consonant-not-probative, exactly as the apex holds the supramodal signature it surveys.

## Relation to Site Perspective

**[[tenets#^dualism|Dualism]]**: The inward inversion strengthens the case that the brain-side/mind-side division is structural rather than a vision artefact—the division survives the move inward even as its *shape* inverts, which is harder to explain as an accident of how one sense is wired than as the operational form of a real distinction. But the inversion does not establish the distinction. The convergent substrate and the predictive construction are equally a physicalist's expectations, and per the Map's evidential-status discipline this article holds the finding as consonant-not-probative.

**[[tenets#^bidirectional-interaction|Bidirectional Interaction]]**: Interoception is not a passive readout. Interoceptive predictions enlist autonomic effectors—the predicted blush triggers the vasodilation that fulfils the prediction—so the felt body participates in its own regulation. The Map interprets this as the mind side participating in perception, consonant with bidirectional interaction; the active-inference rival re-describes the same loop in wholly physical terms, and the Map adopts the mechanics while keeping the disagreement at the framework boundary.

**[[tenets#^occams-limits|Occam's Razor Has Limits]]**: The single word "interoception" is a parsimonious label that flattens a heterogeneous bundle of channels with different pathways and very different accessibility profiles—the void's strongest tenet hook, here applied to the capability division rather than to opacity. The convergence onto one integrator is what makes the label *seem* to name one sense; the channel competition the 2025 cardiac/tactile finding measures is what shows it does not. Treating the label as decisive would hide exactly the structural variation that maps the inward limit.

The Map does not claim interoception settles dualism. It claims that completing the modality survey inward yields a precise structural fact—the cross-modal asymmetry is exteroceptive, and interoception inverts both its terms—which any adequate theory of perception must accommodate, and which the interface reading accommodates without strain while its strongest rivals accommodate equally well.
