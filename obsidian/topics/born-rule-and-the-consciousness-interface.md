The Born rule — quantum mechanics' prescription that measurement probabilities equal |ψ|² — is the one feature every quantum interpretation shares and none can eliminate. Every major attempt to derive it either smuggles in probabilistic assumptions or presupposes an observer. A March 2026 analysis by Zhang demonstrates that all five leading derivation strategies depend irreducibly on an additivity assumption that cannot itself be derived from non-probabilistic postulates. This failure persists across the full landscape of quantum interpretations: hidden-variable theories, objective-collapse models, retrocausal frameworks, categorical approaches, and gravitational-collapse proposals. Combined with Torres Alegre's 2025 proof that the Born rule is the *unique* probability assignment compatible with relativistic causality, this interpretation-invariance suggests the rule encodes the consciousness-physics interface — a constraint on how experience encounters indeterminacy that no purely physical framework can generate from within.

The Unfinishable Map reads this persistent failure as abductive evidence that the Born rule marks a real boundary of physical description. The Map develops this primarily as an *interface reading* — the rule governs how consciousness encounters quantum indeterminacy — and admits a parallel [[trumping-preemption|trumping reading]] ([[delegatory-causation]]) on which consciousness is authoritative over trajectories the formalism already describes, leaving the Born rule outside the gap analysis. On either reading, underivability is what we should expect.

A separate empirical question runs alongside the theoretical one: **no published experiment directly probes the Born rule inside living neural tissue during a conscious act of selection** — which is precisely the regime that consciousness-physics-interface programmes target. The corridor-vs-outside-the-corridor distinction (taxonomy below) is explicitly held as a live branch rather than a settled preference. Anyone claiming the question is already answered — by pointing to the Born rule's success in photon experiments or to weak positive signals in legacy mind-matter studies — is overreading the available evidence.

## The Brain-Internal Gap in Born-Rule Testing

Every precision test of the Born rule to date has run in a regime very unlike brain tissue. Sinha et al. (2010) bounded Sorkin's third-order interference parameter at less than 10⁻² of the expected second-order contribution using single photons through a three-slit aperture. Later optical, atomic, and high-energy-collision work has tightened this bound further (Phys. Rev. D 2025). The GPT-steering derivation of the Born rule by causal consistency (arXiv:2512.12636, 2025) complements these experiments by showing the rule is uniquely picked out among probability assignments *conditional* on purification, steering, and exact no-signalling. The Masanes-Galley-Müller (2019) operational redundancy result similarly derives Born's prescription from Hilbert-space structure, unitary evolution, tensor-product composition, and a finite-parameter estimation postulate.

The quoted language matters. Masanes, Galley, and Müller state that "the mathematical structure of quantum measurements […] can be deduced from the other quantum postulates […] and the assumption that ensembles on finite-dimensional Hilbert spaces are characterized by finitely many parameters." This is a conditional theorem: if the premises hold, the Born rule follows. It is not a demonstration that the premises *must* hold in every physical system. The brain-internal regime during a conscious act of selection is an untested extrapolation from the tested systems. Any one of the MGM postulates — in particular measurement uniqueness and tensor-product subsystem independence — could in principle fail where conscious selection operates, and the available data cannot adjudicate.

This is what "the empirical status of Born-rule-violating tests at the brain interface" reduces to: the regime of interest has not been probed, the closest experimental programmes measure adjacent but non-equivalent quantities, and the most direct legacy tests (micro-psychokinesis on random number generators) trend toward the null when analysed with preregistered Bayesian methodology.

## A Century of Circular Derivations

Born introduced the probability interpretation in 1926 as a pragmatic guess — what Araújo (2021) calls "wire and bubble gum." Landsman's 2025 centenary review concludes that "all derivations found in the literature are either circular or unrelated to measurement."

Zhang (2026) identified the structural bottleneck. Analysing five major strategies — Gleason's theorem, Busch's POVM extension, Deutsch-Wallace decision theory, Zurek's envariance, and Finkelstein-Hartle frequentism — he showed every one depends on additivity: the assumption that probabilities of orthogonal outcomes sum. But additivity is itself a probabilistic postulate, underivable from non-contextuality and normalisation alone. Every derivation assumes probability to derive probability.

## The Observer in Every Standard Derivation

Beyond additivity, each derivation presupposes an observer. Decoherence selects a preferred basis without selecting a single definite outcome. The transition from "probabilities over outcomes" to "this outcome actually occurred" is the step each derivation presupposes and none explains. What they require is something for which one outcome is *actual* and the others are *excluded*. Whether this actuality requires phenomenal consciousness is a further question the Map answers affirmatively; the structural point holds independently.

**Gleason's theorem** (1957) proves that any non-contextual probability measure on Hilbert-space projections must take the Born-rule form — but says nothing about why measurements yield definite outcomes.

**Deutsch-Wallace decision theory** (1999, 2010) shows rational agents in a branching Everettian universe must assign Born-rule credences. But the rationality axioms — measurement neutrality, branching indifference, diachronic consistency — are substantive philosophical claims, and critics argue they smuggle in the conclusion. Carroll and Sebens's (2014) self-locating uncertainty route requires the Epistemic Separability Principle — itself a substantive assumption.

**Zurek's envariance** (2003, 2005) appeals to symmetries of entangled states within his broader [[quantum-darwinism-and-consciousness|quantum Darwinism]] programme. Schlosshauer and Fine (2005) showed the auxiliary assumption extending from equal to unequal amplitudes is strong enough to yield the Born rule directly; Máttyus (2023) showed envariance does not necessitate Born's rule for all measurements.

**QBism** reframes the Born rule as a normative coherence constraint on an agent's credences (DeBrota, Fuchs & Schack 2021), making the observer central by design. It avoids rather than solves the measurement problem. The [[pragmatist-quantum-foundations-under-dualism|pragmatist family]] reading treats this normativity as the structural surface of a dualist interface that QBism declines to posit.

**Masanes-Galley-Müller** (2019) derives the Born rule from quantum postulates plus finite-dimensionality. But the operational axioms presuppose definite outcomes, relocating observer-dependence without eliminating it.

The pattern is uniform: every derivation presupposes that measurements yield definite, actual results — the step neither unitary evolution nor decoherence provides. The specific actualising element differs, but the smuggle is universal.

## Beyond Standard Derivations: The Interpretation-Invariant Pattern

If alternative frameworks — approaching quantum mechanics from entirely different ontological starting points — could eliminate the Born rule, the failure would be technical. The failure persists across all frameworks, making it structural.

### Hidden Variables: Bohmian Typicality

In Bohmian mechanics, particles have definite positions guided by a pilot wave. The Born rule becomes a theorem — conditional on the quantum equilibrium hypothesis, which states positions are distributed according to |ψ|². Dürr, Goldstein, and Zanghì (1992) argue from typicality, but typicality is measured using |ψ|² itself. Valentini's (1991) dynamical relaxation avoids this circularity but cannot explain why the universe began near equilibrium, and no cosmological signatures of primordial non-equilibrium have been observed.

### Objective Collapse: Engineering, Not Derivation

GRW/CSL models modify the Schrödinger equation with spontaneous localisations. The stochastic noise terms are calibrated to a GKSL master equation, simultaneously guaranteeing norm conservation, Born-rule statistics, and no superluminal signalling. The noise parameters are chosen *because* they reproduce the Born rule — design, not derivation.

### Retrocausal Frameworks: Definitional Derivation

The transactional interpretation (Cramer 1986; Kastner 2012) derives |ψ|² from "handshakes" between offer and confirmation waves. But the derivation is arithmetically trivial once the confirmation wave is defined as ψ* — the Born rule is relocated to a definition.

### Categorical and Gravitational Approaches

Categorical quantum mechanics (Abramsky and Coecke, 2004; Agrawal and Wilson, 2025) proves the Born rule is the *unique* probability assignment consistent with framework axioms — but the framework already contains measurement and probability structure. The Diósi-Penrose gravitational collapse model explains *when* collapse occurs but not *which* outcome with *which* probability; the Born rule governs outcome selection and gravity alone does not address this.

### The Summary

| Framework | Born Rule Status | What It Assumes |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Copenhagen | Postulate | Measurement axiom |
| Many-worlds | Decision-theoretic "derivation" | Rationality axioms, branching indifference |
| Bohmian | Theorem (conditional) | Quantum equilibrium (measured by |ψ|²) |
| GRW/CSL | Built into dynamics | Noise calibrated to Born statistics |
| Transactional | Product of offer × confirmation | Confirmation wave defined as ψ* |
| Categorical | Structural consequence | Framework includes effects and probability |
| Diósi-Penrose | Partial (timescales only) | Decay-rate interpretation of energy gaps |

Every framework either postulates the Born rule, assumes it in disguised form, or relocates it. None generates it from non-probabilistic, observer-independent premises.

## Absorbing the Urgleichung: What Survives Bayesian Personalism

DeBrota, Fuchs, and Schack (2021) reformulate the Born rule as the "urgleichung" — a relation among probabilities expressed on a basis of symmetric informationally complete positive operator-valued measures (SIC-POVMs):

> Q(D_j) = (d+1) Σ_i P(H_i) P(D_j | H_i) − 1/d

where d is the Hilbert-space dimension, {H_i} are SIC outcomes, and {D_j} outcomes of any target measurement. QBism reads P and Q as personal credences and the equation as a normative coherence constraint on a Bayesian agent. A realist critic replies that, stripped of Bayesian personalism, the urgleichung is a theorem of probability theory on a particular operator structure — leaving "the interface" no independent work to do.

**What formal shape survives separation.** The urgleichung says that probabilities for an actually-performed measurement cannot be recovered as the classical marginal of a joint distribution over a counterfactual SIC measurement: classical total probability requires coefficient 1 and offset 0, whereas the quantum rule requires (d+1) and −1/d. The deformation is the structural residue, with magnitude set by dimension d and form set by SIC geometry. These features hold whether probabilities are read as credences, propensities, or frequencies.

**Why this shape evidences the interface reading.** Two features resist a purely mathematical-realist redescription. First, the deformation is *irreducibly counterfactual*: the unperformed reference SIC functions as the informational ground against which actual outcomes are specified — the structural signature of a gap being crossed rather than a distribution being updated. Second, the (d+1)/−1/d form falls out of SIC geometry, but the realist explains this only by deferral. The interface reading offers an independent motivation — the deformation measures the non-classicality of outcome actualisation where consciousness encounters d-dimensional indeterminacy, with (d+1) playing a coupling-like role. Combined with Kochen-Specker contextuality, the urgleichung supplies the *positive* relation replacing classical total probability, and its shape is what the interface reading predicts.

**The honest fallback.** A Hilbert-space primitivist can accept the theorem and refuse the meaning, resting on the geometry alone. On that reading, the Map's absorption of the urgleichung is heuristic rather than derivational: its formal shape is consistent with and suggestive of the interface reading the Map defends on independent grounds, not forced by the formalism.

## The Causal Consistency Constraint

Torres Alegre (2025) showed that within generalised probabilistic theories satisfying purification, the Born rule is the *only* probability assignment consistent with no-signalling. Nonlinear deviations would enable superluminal communication via quantum steering. The result assumes probabilistic structure and derives the rule's *form*, not its existence.

For the consciousness-interface interpretation, this is illuminating rather than threatening. If consciousness modulates quantum outcomes, it *must* follow the Born rule — any deviation would enable superluminal signalling, inconsistent with [[tenets#^minimal-quantum-interaction|minimal interaction]]. The Born rule is what relativistic causality *requires* of any agent — physical or non-physical — that participates in measurement outcomes.

The rule's form is determined by causal consistency; its existence — the fact that one outcome actualises from among possibilities — is what physics cannot generate. This is the kind of partial progress [[the-interface-problem#What Would Count as Progress?|the interface specification problem]] identifies as tractable short of a full mapping: physics-side constraints narrow the space of viable corridor-family specifications even while the mental-side variable remains unsettled.

Two qualifications temper this picture. Born-uniqueness and causal-consistency have been tested in photon, atom, qubit, triple-slit, and high-energy regimes — not in brain-internal tissue during conscious selection — so the corridor binds wherever the standard postulates hold rather than universally. And it binds only *interface* theories; the [[trumping-preemption|trumping alternative]] ([[delegatory-causation]]) leaves the formalism untouched, so the Born rule is neither a constraint on nor a signature of the interface under that reading.

## Which Consciousness-Physics Proposals Require Born Violation {#corridor-taxonomy}

Not every proposal that posits a consciousness-physics interaction entails Born-rule violation. Three families of positions emerge:

**Corridor dualism (Born-rule-preserving).** Consciousness influences which branch is actualised on a single measurement event, but the influence averages to exactly |⟨φ|ψ⟩|² over the ensemble. No statistical signature appears in long runs. This is the Map's current working hypothesis for minimum-detectable interaction, compatible with every tested Born-rule regime. Sympathetic readings of Stapp (without the Zeno variant), various "interface" authors, and the corridor-reading of the pragmatist-family positions (QBism, Healey) sit here.

**Minimum-outside-the-corridor dualism (Born-rule-bending).** The corridor is insufficient to produce the observed correlation between conscious intention and physical outcome, so the Minimal Quantum Interaction tenet — properly read as "smallest interaction actually sufficient" rather than "smallest interaction that preserves ensemble statistics" — requires deviation. Several distinct proposals fall here:

- *Stapp's quantum Zeno picture.* Rapid conscious attention holds a particular eigenstate, shifting selection probabilities away from what a non-conscious measurement would produce. The modification depends on observation frequency and is explicitly Born-rule-bending.
- *Chalmers-McQueen Φ-dependent collapse.* Integrated-information-theoretic Φ gates collapse dynamics; "Φ resists superposition, and superpositions of distinct Φ values trigger collapse" (Chalmers & McQueen 2022). At the ensemble level, with Φ-dependent stochasticity added, the Born rule is generally not preserved — collapse probabilities depend on a consciousness-related variable that standard QM does not include. Chalmers and McQueen themselves note that no single scalar Φ measure suffices, sharpening rather than softening the Born-modifying implication.
- *Penrose-Hameroff Orch-OR.* Orchestrated objective reduction is explicitly a modification of QM, not selection within it. The theory lives or dies on deviations from standard unitary plus Born dynamics.
- *Arana (2025) time-extended weighting.* A time-integrated functional perturbs Born probabilities by an exponentially suppressed factor λ, recovering standard Born as λ → 0. This is a worked example of a "Born-violating-but-arbitrarily-close" proposal — a falsifiable phenomenological model.
- *Kauffman-Radin-Stapp acausal actualisation.* Quantum measurement as an acausal conversion of possibles into actuals by mind; naturally entails departures from a measurement-independent probability distribution.

**Trumping dualism (orthogonal to Born).** The [[trumping-preemption|trumping-preemption reading]] developed in the Map treats consciousness as authoritative over trajectories the formalism already fully describes, leaving the Born rule outside the gap analysis entirely. Trumping sits on a different explanatory axis: the Born rule and the consciousness-physics question belong to separate axes under this reading, so trumping counts as neither a corridor-preserving nor a Born-modifying position. See [[concepts/trumping-preemption]] and the [[delegatory-causation]] articulation for the full treatment.

A fourth family — **no consciousness-physics interaction at all** (Everettian, Bohmian, orthodox pragmatism-without-interface) — rejects the premise and lies outside the Map's tenet commitments.

## Active Experimental Programmes and What They Could Falsify

Three experimental strands are alive in the 2025-2026 landscape. None isolates Born-rule violation as the measured variable; each bears on the broader question indirectly.

### Brain-Internal Quantum Coherence Witnesses

Kerskens and Pérez (2022) applied an MRI protocol to proton spins in brain water using entanglement-witness logic adapted from quantum-gravity experiments: if two known quantum systems entangle via an unknown mediator, the mediator must itself be quantum. They detected MRI signals resembling heartbeat-evoked potentials that standard MRI should not resolve, with signal strength correlated to short-term memory performance and sleep-wake transitions. Follow-up work through Lopez Perez et al. (2023) and the Frontiers 2025 review continues this programme.

Babcock, Hameroff, and colleagues (2025, *Neuroscience of Consciousness*) review room-temperature quantum effects in microtubules, microtubule resonances controlling membrane spiking, volatile anaesthetic targeting of microtubules, and what they describe as "direct biophysical evidence of a macroscopic entangled state in the living human brain." Hagan, Hameroff, and Tuszyński (2002) earlier estimated microtubule decoherence times up to ~10⁻⁵ seconds — seven orders of magnitude longer than Tegmark's (2000) estimate — under Debye-layer screening and ordered water.

Keppler (2025) proposes that cortical microcolumns couple to the electromagnetic zero-point field via glutamate-mediated resonance, supplying a field-theoretic candidate interface.

What these could falsify: a *negative* outcome — demonstrating that brain-internal coherence times are too short or coherence scales too small for any macroscopic quantum effect to matter — would foreclose the Penrose-Hameroff and Keppler programmes and weaken (but not kill) other Born-violating proposals that require a quantum substrate rich enough for conscious selection to operate.

What they could not falsify: these witnesses detect coherence or entanglement, not Born-rule deviation. Even a positive outcome establishes only the *possibility space* in which Born-violating dynamics could live. The Map notes that some of these programmes (microtubule-centred Orch-OR and Keppler's zero-point field resonance) should be presented as specific proposals rather than as established mechanisms; the Map's core position survives whether or not any particular mechanism holds.

### Brain-to-Quantum-System Coupling Devices

The Nirvanic "Spark of Life" experiment (Gildert 2025, roadmap 2025-2027) couples a robot's sensory-action feedback loop to a quantum computer's qubits, using wave-function collapse to "choose" actions. The test is whether a quantum-controlled agent behaves differently — statistically — from a classical one. The framing is panpsychist ("QCAT"), not dualist, but the experimental design is the closest operational analogue currently on the roadmap to a "does consciousness bias quantum outcomes?" test. The CEO forecasts one to two years to an interesting signal.

What this could falsify: a *positive* signal — a quantum-coupled agent systematically outperforming its classical counterpart in a way that admits no classical explanation — would provide the first direct operational evidence for some form of mind-quantum interaction. A convincingly negative signal would be less decisive, since the device couples an artificial apparatus to a synthetic qubit system and the absence of a biological brain is a genuine disanalogy from the Map's target regime.

### Legacy Micro-Psychokinesis and RNG Studies

The Bösch, Steinkamp, and Boller (2006) meta-analysis of 380 studies on intention-to-random-number-generator effects found a small but statistically significant positive effect size, a strong inverse correlation between sample size and effect size, extreme heterogeneity, and Monte Carlo simulations compatible with publication bias. Maier and Dechamps (2018), using preregistered Bayesian methodology, reported strong evidence for the null hypothesis — no detectable micro-PK effect on quantum RNGs. The 227-participant multi-lab replication of the PEAR programme (~2 million trials) failed to reproduce the original composite 7σ signal. The Global Consciousness Project (Nelson, 1998-present) remains ongoing but faces critiques of post-hoc event selection, XOR bit-stream artefacts, and the absence of a proposed mechanism.

What this has already partially foreclosed: gross, unaided, intention-driven Born-rule violation of the PEAR variety is on very thin empirical ground. Maier-Dechamps's preregistered evidence for the null is the strongest single result in this debate as of 2026, and a Map that bet heavily on large macroscopic micro-PK signals would be bet against the current data.

What it has not foreclosed: smaller, targeted, brain-internal deviations of the Arana-λ kind, where the modification is exponentially suppressed outside the immediate neural-selection regime. Nor does it rule out corridor dualism, which predicts no statistical signature of the PEAR kind by construction.

### Collapse-Theory Parameter Bounds

[[spontaneous-collapse-theories|Spontaneous collapse models]] (GRW, CSL, Diósi-Penrose) add nonlinear stochastic collapse terms to Schrödinger evolution. Diósi-Penrose in its simplest form is now ruled out by germanium-detector data (2021). GRW and CSL have "survived by a whisker" at their original parameter values after LISA Pathfinder bounds, X-ray detector experiments, and intergalactic-medium heating constraints (SEP 2024). If a Chalmers-McQueen-style model couples Φ to the collapse dynamics, the parameter space shifts into less-constrained brain-specific regions — an advantage for the theorist, but also a warning about unfalsifiability drift.

## Why Underivability Matters for Consciousness-Collapse

If the Born rule could be derived from physics alone — with no reference to observers, agents, or outcomes — there would be no conceptual space for consciousness at the measurement interface. That it *cannot* be so derived, despite a century of effort across every framework, is abductive evidence that it encodes something the formalism does not contain. On the Map's primary [[quantum-probability-consciousness|interface reading]], the Born rule describes how consciousness actualises one possibility among many — [[conservation-laws-and-mental-causation|without injecting energy]]. The [[consciousness-physics-interface-formalism]] establishes Born rule preservation as the first of five non-negotiable constraints on any *corridor-compliant* consciousness-physics coupling, and the [[delegation-meets-quantum-selection|delegation-selection bridge]] identifies the Born-rule distribution with Saad's "default causal profile" in [[delegatory-causation|causal delegation]]. The parallel [[trumping-preemption|trumping reading]] interprets the same underivability differently: what the formalism cannot generate is not a selection mechanism but consciousness's *authority* over trajectories the formalism already describes in full.

The underivability becomes more striking combined with uniqueness. Masanes, Galley, and Müller (2019) showed the Born rule is simultaneously unique and underivable — precisely what a consciousness-interface interpretation predicts: mathematical structure determines the rule's *form*, while observer-dependence reflects its *meaning*.

Tonetto's analysis locates the meeting point: quantum mechanics provides "statistical closure with outcome-level openness". The [[completeness-in-physics-under-dualism|completeness analysis]] sharpens this — physics is structurally complete yet ontologically partial, and that partiality is exactly the Born rule gap. The [[mathematical-structure-of-the-consciousness-physics-interface|mathematical corridor]] tightly constrains what corridor-dwelling consciousness can do at this gap; the gap remains. Trumping-based readings do not locate consciousness at the gap and the corridor analysis does not bind them.

This reinterpretation explains several puzzling features:

**Why additivity is irreducible.** It constrains how consciousness encounters mutually exclusive possibilities — one outcome experienced, others excluded. [[phenomenal-non-compositionality|Phenomenal non-compositionality]] reinforces this: consciousness acts as a unified selector rather than an aggregate of micro-selections.

**Why every derivation presupposes definite outcomes.** An interface specification necessarily involves both sides. No description of a lock alone specifies the key's shape.

**Why the rule works so precisely.** Any deviation would break relativistic causality — an interface specification can be exact.

## What the Map's Minimal Quantum Interaction Commits To

The Minimal Quantum Interaction tenet, strictly read, does not privilege the corridor. It privileges whichever minimum is *actually sufficient* to produce the observed correlation between conscious intention and physical outcome. The corridor is the Map's working hypothesis because it is the most conservative scenario, not because it is definitionally required.

The Map holds three attitudes in tension here, each defensible:

1. **If corridor dualism is sufficient**, the Map's programme is maximally compatible with existing Born-rule tests and the 2025 GPT-steering derivation, at the cost of making the single-event mental-to-physical channel ensemble-invisible — which risks collapsing into a subtle epiphenomenalism-at-the-ensemble-level unless the [[trumping-preemption|trumping reading]] is adopted.

2. **If minimum-outside-the-corridor dualism is required**, the Map becomes empirically distinguishable from physicalism in principle — an Arana-λ, a Stapp-Zeno rate, a Chalmers-McQueen Φ-collapse strength, a Penrose OR timescale — and loses nothing tenet-critical, since the Minimal Quantum Interaction tenet reads "minimal" as "smallest sufficient," not "corridor-respecting."

3. **If trumping dualism is the correct structural move**, the Born rule and the consciousness question are orthogonal. The Born rule governs the physical formalism's probabilistic prescription; consciousness is authoritative on a different axis, so Born-rule tests — positive or negative — neither support nor threaten the Map's dualism.

All three remain live. The empirical state of Born-rule testing cannot adjudicate among them because it cannot yet probe the brain-internal regime at the required precision.

## What This Does Not Establish

Three caveats apply to the theoretical argument.

First, absence of a derivation is not proof that no derivation exists. Future work might identify non-probabilistic postulates from which additivity follows. The argument is abductive — the best current explanation of the pattern — not deductive.

Second, consciousness-collapse is not uniquely supported by the underivability pattern alone. Bohmian mechanics, GRW/CSL, and retrocausal interpretations each relocate the explanatory burden rather than eliminating it.

Third, the Map's position does not predict specific deviations from the Born rule. It reinterprets what the rule means without changing what it predicts — a limitation shared by all quantum interpretations. The causal consistency result constrains the rule's form but not its existence: why there are definite outcomes with any probability at all remains open.

## What Negative Results Would and Would Not Foreclose

A hypothetical clean null on all three experimental strands — no brain-internal quantum coherence, no quantum-agent signal from Nirvanic-style coupling, and continued preregistered nulls on micro-PK — would foreclose:

- Orch-OR and any theory requiring macroscopic biological quantum coherence
- PEAR-variant large-signal micro-PK hypotheses (already mostly foreclosed)
- Keppler's zero-point-field resonance interface (insofar as it predicts detectable coherence)
- Any "minimum-outside-the-corridor" model that predicts detectable effects at current experimental sensitivity

It would not foreclose:

- Corridor dualism, which predicts no statistical signature at any sensitivity
- Arana-style exponentially-suppressed Born modifications with λ below the experimental resolution
- Trumping dualism, which is agnostic to Born-rule violation
- Chalmers-McQueen Φ-collapse if Φ-coupling parameters are tuned below detectability

This asymmetry matters. The Map can survive a clean sweep of negative experimental results by retreating to corridor or trumping readings. What it cannot do — without dishonesty — is pretend that existing Born-rule tests have already validated the corridor over the minimum-outside-corridor alternative. They have not. They could not, given their regime.

## Relation to Site Perspective

**[[tenets#^dualism|Dualism]]**: That no physical framework can generate the Born rule without presupposing observers or probability supports the claim that consciousness contributes something ontologically irreducible to measurement. The rule marks the [[completeness-in-physics-under-dualism|structural-ontological gap]] where physics is complete about structure yet silent on actuality. The existence of a physical regime that is genuinely unprobed — the brain-internal conscious-selection regime — is what opens the space for a non-reducible mental channel. If the Born rule were tested inside conscious brains and held to the same 10⁻² precision as in triple-slit experiments, one degree of freedom for dualism would close, though trumping and corridor variants would still survive.

**[[tenets#^minimal-quantum-interaction|Minimal Quantum Interaction]]**: On the Map's default corridor reading, consciousness's influence must follow the Born rule exactly — the unique law compatible with no-signalling — wherever that regime binds. [[phenomenal-non-compositionality|Phenomenal non-compositionality]] further constrains this: consciousness operates at a unified locus rather than distributed micro-interactions. Trumping-based readings realise MQI without any trajectory-level interaction — arguably an even smaller footprint at the cost of empirical invisibility. The Map's internal taxonomy of what "minimal" can mean — corridor-preserving, minimum-outside-the-corridor, or trumping-orthogonal — is a live structural question rather than a settled commitment.

**[[tenets#^bidirectional-interaction|Bidirectional Interaction]]**: On the interface reading, obtaining an outcome is itself an act — consciousness selecting among possibilities — so observer-dependence reflects genuine bidirectional causation; consciousness shapes which outcome actualises without altering the governing distribution. The [[trilemma-of-selection]] supports this: at any point of genuine indeterminacy, determinism, randomness, and mental causation exhaust the options, and only the third preserves authorship. The [[trumping-preemption|trumping route]] realises bidirectional causation at the authority layer — consciousness is the cause by being authoritative over the sufficient trajectory. A Born-rule-preserving corridor position has to explain how single-event selection adds up to a genuine mental-to-physical causal channel without leaving an ensemble signature; this is a known tension, not a dissolved one — arguably the Map's sharpest open question, and the reason minimum-outside-the-corridor readings remain attractive despite their empirical riskiness.

**[[tenets#^no-many-worlds|No Many Worlds]]**: MWI must explain not only the Born rule's form but what probability *means* when all outcomes occur. The [[probability-problem-in-many-worlds|probability problem in many worlds]] is the Born rule gap at its widest, and the [[many-worlds-argument|cumulative case against MWI]] combines it with indexical identity and consciousness objections.

**[[tenets#^occams-limits|Occam's Razor Has Limits]]**: The interpretation-invariance of the Born rule gap is evidence that the consciousness-physics boundary is a genuine feature of reality. A century of failure across every interpretive framework suggests counselling patience for a purely physical derivation may be the simpler but wrong assumption. The temptation to treat "the Born rule has held in every test so far" as evidence of universal validity is a case where simplicity is unreliable with incomplete knowledge: the tested regimes are a narrow slice of physical configurations, and treating the extrapolation to the brain-internal regime as parsimony disguises an underlying assumption.

The Map's working position is that the empirical question is genuinely open, that the present generation of experiments cannot adjudicate it, and that intellectual honesty requires holding the corridor-vs-minimum-outside-corridor distinction as a live fork rather than a solved problem.
