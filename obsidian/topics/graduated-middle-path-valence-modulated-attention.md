The parent fork article ([[valence-and-conscious-selection]]) names a graduated middle path as "the most defensible current stance": **valence shapes where attention goes and how intensely it is sustained, and attention in turn drives selection.** It then leaves that stance a two-paragraph sketch. This article works the coupling as its own position, and reaches a deflationary verdict up front, before any detail: the middle path buys the Map's [[tenets#^minimal-quantum-interaction|Minimal Quantum Interaction]] a cheaper route to value-relevance—no second mental-to-physical coupling—but on its own discriminating signature it earns only the *weaker* reading of value's causal standing. It secures that valence is **causally relevant via attention** (not epiphenomenal), and it does not earn the parent's stronger "doubly non-epiphenomenal—real as experienced *and* efficacious as selector" language. The stronger reading belongs to the value-sensitive horn, which the middle path is not. The whole point of specifying the coupling is that the same experiment which would confirm the middle path is the experiment that demotes it.

Two disciplines hold throughout. Per [[evidential-status-discipline]], the coupling is a *live hypothesis*, not an established result: the Map leans this way on prior commitments, and the leading physicalist rival models the identical causal structure without any non-physical term. Per [[possibility-probability-slippage|possibility-to-probability discipline]], "the tenets permit valence-modulated attention" never becomes "valence-modulated attention is what happens." What follows is a specification and a test, not a finding.

## The Coupling, Specified

The value-blind horn's single mental-to-physical coupling is attention: phenomenal attention maps to observation rate, and rapid observation stabilises a neural pattern via the quantum Zeno effect ([[attention-as-interface]], following Stapp). Intention and effort are modes of that one channel. The middle path adds no second channel to the physics. It adds a *modulator* on the existing one.

The specification: for a set of physically underdetermined neural outcomes {O₁ … Oₙ}, the anticipated valence V(Oᵢ)—the predicted felt goodness or badness of actualising Oᵢ—sets two attentional parameters over the patterns representing those outcomes. It sets **targeting** (which pattern receives observation) and **gain** (how intensely, and how persistently, that observation is sustained). Attention so parameterised then does the physical work: observation frequency raises Zeno stabilisation on the targeted pattern. Valence is a gain-and-targeting parameter on the attentional coupling; it is not itself a coupling to the physics.

This matters for [[tenets#^minimal-quantum-interaction|Minimal Quantum Interaction]]. The value-sensitive horn needs a *second* coupling—a psychophysical law by which felt valence enters the collapse dynamics directly—which the [[psychophysical-laws]] framework admits it cannot specify. The middle path needs no such law. Valence enters where attention is already granted entry, as a setting on a channel that already has a candidate physical implementation. On the Map's own parsimony standard, that is the more-minimal route to making value causally relevant, and it is the reason the stance is "most defensible" rather than most ambitious.

The coupling plausibly carries a sign asymmetry: negative valence appears to recruit and hold attention more forcibly than positive valence of equal magnitude—pain captures, dread redirects. Whether that asymmetry lives in felt value or in the mechanism that reads value is the same value-in-mechanism question one register down, developed in [[negative-valence-asymmetry-and-the-selection-weighting-function]]; the middle path inherits it unresolved.

## Non-Epiphenomenality, Audited

Does "value causes selection *via* a value-neutral mechanism" deliver genuine non-epiphenomenality, or only upstream relevance? The two readings must be kept apart, because the parent article's strongest claim depends on which one the middle path licenses.

**The strong reading** is the value-sensitive horn's: valence is "doubly non-epiphenomenal—real as experienced *and* efficacious as selector." Here valence is *the currency of selection itself*; the selection law is denominated in felt value.

**The weak reading** is the middle path's actual content: valence is efficacious as an *input to* the selector, not as the selector. It shapes attention; attention selects. On this reading the fundamental selection law remains value-blind, and valence is what the parent article calls "upstream infrastructure." The teleological character—outcomes selected because of how they would feel—emerges from the *combination* of value-sensitive attention and value-blind selection, rather than from the physics of selection carrying a value term.

The middle path delivers the weak reading, and only the weak reading. This is enough to defeat epiphenomenalism about valence in the narrow sense: a variable that shapes which neural pattern gets stabilised is doing causal work, not riding along inert. The [[the-steelman-for-value-blind-selection|value-blind steelman]]'s parsimony case does not touch this—it grants that valence can inform a steering channel. But "informs the steering" is precisely *not* "is the steering." The evaluative dimension of consciousness, on the middle path, never enters the law that decides outcomes; it enters the parameter that aims the mechanism that enters the law. That is one remove short of what [[phenomenal-value-realism]] wants when it argues that value must be efficacious *as value*. Reading the middle path as vindicating the strong claim would be exactly the possibility-to-probability slippage the cluster guards against: coherence with the value-sensitive aspiration is not attainment of it.

So the honest placement is conservative. The middle path escapes epiphenomenalism and forfeits the strong non-epiphenomenality claim in the same move. Whether that trade is acceptable is a substantive question the parent article flags and does not settle—and, as the next section shows, it is a question a single experiment could adjudicate.

## The Discriminating Signature

A specification earns its keep only if it predicts something the alternatives do not. The middle path does—against the two *pure* horns—and the prediction is sharper than the parent article's testability hooks record.

The parent lists, for the value-sensitive horn, that "valenced options should be selected more readily than neutral options **of equal attentional salience**." Read carefully, an equal-salience design is the discriminating instrument, because it clamps the very channel the middle path routes value through. Consider a task requiring conscious selection between two competing neural patterns, run in two conditions: one where attention is free to vary, and one where attentional salience is experimentally equalised across the options (matched bottom-up salience, matched cued attention, matched precision). Ask whether a valence *selection advantage*—valenced options winning selection more often, or with stronger conscious-selection neural signatures—survives the clamp.

| Position | Free attention | Salience clamped |
|----------|----------------|------------------|
| **Strict value-blind** (valence inert on attention) | no valence advantage | no valence advantage |
| **Value-blind steelman** (valence informs attention only) | valence advantage | advantage *abolished* |
| **Graduated middle path** | valence advantage | advantage *abolished* |
| **Pure value-sensitive** | valence advantage | advantage *persists* (residual) |

The **abolition-by-clamp** is the middle path's characteristic result: a valence advantage present when attention is free and gone when attention is equalised—a full mediation of value by attention, statistically a valence-by-clamp interaction with the effect vanishing under the clamp. It separates the middle path from both *pure* horns: strict value-blind selection, with valence inert even on attention, predicts nothing to abolish; pure value-sensitive selection predicts a *residual* advantage, because a direct valence-to-selection coupling has a route the clamp cannot close. What abolition-by-clamp does *not* separate the middle path from is a value-blind position that concedes valence can inform the attentional channel—the [[the-steelman-for-value-blind-selection|value-blind steelman]]'s live "valence only informs the channel" concession. That position predicts advantage-then-abolition too; its row is the middle path's exactly. Arriving one rival earlier than the active-inference limit the next section records, the clamp battery gives the middle path no empirical daylight over that concession-position: the two are co-extensive over everything the design can measure. The daylight the middle path holds is therefore *conceptual*, not empirical—it adds a positive thesis, that valence *is* what sets attentional gain and targeting, specified as a parameter on the one coupling the Map already posits, where the steelman's concession leaves the source of the attentional bias open.

The interpretive payoff closes the loop with the previous section. The middle-path result—abolition—*is* the demotion to the weak reading. An advantage that disappears the moment attention is held constant demonstrates that valence had no selection route independent of attention: value is causally relevant via attention, and no further. Conversely, a residual advantage surviving the clamp would not confirm the middle path; it would vindicate the value-sensitive horn and earn the "doubly non-epiphenomenal" language directly. The same experiment thus decides which reading of value's causal standing is licensed, and the middle path's own confirming outcome is the one that withholds the strong claim. This is why the article declines to write the stance as a tier-upgrade of value-sensitivity: on its own predicted signature, confirming the middle path is confirming the weaker thesis.

## The Physicalist Rival the Test Cannot Reach

The discriminating signature adjudicates *within* the Map's fork. It does not adjudicate the interface reading against its strongest physicalist rival, and honesty about that limit is mandatory here.

That rival is predictive processing / active inference, which natively models every element of the middle path on a fully computational substrate. Valence has been formalised as the negative rate of change of free energy (Joffily & Coricelli 2013). Attention is precision-weighting. Sandved-Smith et al. (2021) cast mental action—including attentional control and its regulation—as policy selection over higher-level cognitive states, with meta-awareness modulating the precision of the mapping between observations and cognitive states. Their framework already contains a valence-modulates-precision-modulates-selection architecture with no experiential primitive doing causal work. The middle path's causal skeleton—value sets attentional gain, attention drives selection—is, structurally, what active inference provides.

The abolition-by-clamp signature does not separate the two. If valence acts on selection only through attention/precision, then clamping precision abolishes the valence advantage on the active-inference account exactly as it does on the interface account. Both predict the mediation. They part company only at the step the interface reading reserves as non-physical: *which* physically underdetermined outcome becomes actual, the within-Born-rule or improper-mixture selection that produces no aggregate statistical signature and is, by the [[evidential-status-discipline|distinction-without-empirical-difference]] check, empirically inert. Over the observable control architecture—targeting, gain, mediation, the behavioural and neural correlates of selection—the interface middle path and active inference are co-extensive.

Two consequences follow, both deflationary and both required. First, the test is a within-framework instrument: it tells the Map which of its three horns to hold, conditional on the interface picture, and says nothing against the physicalist who models the same mediation without an interface. Second, even the within-framework verdict presupposes that "conscious selection among physically underdetermined outcomes" is measurable at all—and the metaphysical selection step, by construction, is not. What the design can actually measure is a valence bias on behavioural choice and its neural signatures under two attention conditions; the inference from that to the collapse-level selection law rests on the interface premise itself. The test is therefore a specification of what *would* discriminate the horns if the interface holds, not a protocol that decides whether it holds.

## Relation to Site Perspective

**[[tenets#^minimal-quantum-interaction|Minimal Quantum Interaction]]**: This is the tenet the middle path serves best, and the reason it is the fork's default stance. It makes value causally relevant while adding zero new couplings to the physics—valence rides the single attentional channel the Map already posits, rather than requiring a second valence-to-collapse law the framework cannot specify. Among ways to keep value from being idle, this is the smallest interaction, which is what the tenet asks the Map to prefer.

**[[tenets#^bidirectional-interaction|Bidirectional Interaction]]**: Consciousness still acts on the physical world, and valence contributes to that action—but through attention, not as the selection currency. The middle path gives Bidirectional Interaction real downward content while conceding that the *evaluative* dimension's contribution is mediated. Whether mediated contribution is enough to honour the tenet's spirit, or whether it leaves the interaction narrower than the tenet intends, is the open cost the non-epiphenomenality audit records.

**[[tenets#^occams-limits|Occam's Razor Has Limits]]**: The middle path is the more parsimonious horn, and Tenet 5 forbids treating that parsimony as settling the matter. If the discriminating battery ever returned a residual valence advantage under attentional clamp, parsimony would have to yield to that evidence and the Map would owe the second coupling. The tenet's discipline runs the other way too: the Map may not bank the middle path's minimality as if it had earned the strong non-epiphenomenality the value-sensitive horn claims. It has not.
