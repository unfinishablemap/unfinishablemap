Jonathan Birch's *The Edge of Sentience* (2024) and the Map's five-tier evidential scale answer different questions about the same material. Birch's framework is action-oriented: given irreducible uncertainty about who is sentient, what does proportionate precaution require? The Map's scale is description-oriented: given the same uncertainty, what is the catalogue entitled to write? The two schemes converge at the language of "realistic possibility" — both inherit it from the 2024 New York Declaration on Animal Consciousness — and diverge at the work the language is asked to do. Birch's two evidential tiers (*sentience candidate* / *investigation priority*) feed into welfare policy, regulatory licensing, and democratic deliberation. The Map's five tiers (*established* → *strongly supported* → *realistic possibility* → *live hypothesis* → *speculative integration*) feed into article prose and the discipline against [[possibility-probability-slippage|possibility/probability slippage]]. The frameworks are structurally complementary rather than rival: Birch's action layer is precisely what the [[evidential-status-discipline|evidential-status discipline]] does not provide, and the Map's lower-tier description layer is precisely what Birch's two-tier categorisation declines to engage.

This article lays out Birch's framework as Birch presents it, recapitulates the Map's scale, then traces convergences, divergences, and case-by-case verdict differences on insects, decapods, *C. elegans*, Hydra, and current LLMs. The closing section explains how the Map can adopt Birch's action-layer apparatus without importing his metaphysical neutrality — and where the disciplines bind each other against shared failure modes.

## Birch's Framework

Birch installs a precautionary scheme designed to deliver welfare policy under disagreement that he treats as irreducible. The framework has four moving parts.

### The Two-Tier Evidential Categorisation

Birch operates with three categories along a continuum. The first — *recognised sentient* — covers cases where scientific consensus is settled (mammals, birds, octopuses, following the 2012 Cambridge Declaration and the 2024 New York Declaration; the standard literature tracking this consensus is surveyed in the SEP entry on animal consciousness). The two genuinely novel categories are below this consensus line.

A **sentience candidate** is, in Birch's framing, a system with "a credible, non-negligible possibility of sentience" together with an evidence base "rich enough to allow the identification of welfare risks and the design and assessment of precautions." The criterion is conjunctive. Possibility alone is not enough; the evidence must also support designing the welfare interventions the candidacy would warrant. The condition is set high enough to license action and low enough to admit cephalopods broadly, decapods, and on most readings insects.

An **investigation priority**, by contrast, is a system that "could plausibly be identified as sentient if more research was done" and whose welfare would be at risk if the system did turn out to be sentient. This category is for cases below the candidate threshold — typified by nematodes such as *C. elegans*, brain organoids without brainstem tissue, and (on permissive readings) some plants — where the evidence base is too thin to design precautionary measures but the question remains open enough to warrant directed research.

The two tiers are operational. They are not predictions about probability of sentience; they are placements in a scheme that triggers different kinds of action — proportionate precaution for candidates, directed investigation for priorities.

### The Eight Indicators and the Five-of-Eight Rule

For animals capable of nociception, Birch inherits an indicator framework from the 2021 UK government report he led (Birch et al., on cephalopod molluscs and decapod crustaceans). The framework lists eight criteria, four neurobiological and four behavioural:

- **Neurobiological**: nociceptors; integrative brain regions; integrated nociception; analgesia (behavioural responses modulated by pain-relieving compounds)
- **Behavioural**: motivational trade-offs; flexible self-protection; associative learning; analgesia preference

The operational rule: "Precautionary measures are warranted for the members of a group of animals when we have high or very high confidence that they satisfy at least five of eight criteria." Birch is explicit that satisfying five criteria does *not* guarantee sentience — the rule is an action trigger calibrated to tolerate false positives in order to reduce false negatives. The asymmetry is the precautionary framework's load-bearing move: false negatives (denying sentience to sentient beings) carry harms that Birch treats as worse than the harms of acting protectively toward systems that turn out not to be sentient.

The five-of-eight threshold is itself contested. Singh, Pinto et al. (2025) defend the framework against critics and propose three refinements — distinguishing absence of evidence from evidence of absence, weighting criteria by evidential value, and applying phylogenetically informed analysis. The refinements operate inside the framework rather than against it.

### The Three Foundational Principles

Birch's framework rests on three commitments. First, that gratuitous suffering must be avoided — the duty grounding welfare obligation generally. Second, that sentience *candidature* (not certain sentience) is enough to ground precautionary obligation. Third, that proportionality of precaution should be determined by democratic deliberation rather than expert commission. The third principle has drawn the most critical attention: reviewers argue that humans-only democratic procedures lack standing to decide non-human welfare, and that citizens' panels cannot simultaneously be deliberative, representative, informed, and timely (Schwitzgebel & Sinnott-Armstrong 2025; O'Brien 2025).

### The Run-Ahead Principle

For AI sentience, Birch installs a distinct principle: regulatory measures should "run ahead of what would be proportionate to the risks posed by current technology, considering also the risks posed by credible future trajectories." The operational corollary he proposes is a licensing scheme for organisations attempting to create artificial sentience candidates, or whose work creates even a small risk of doing so. The principle is paired with what Birch identifies as the **gaming problem**: large language models are selected by their training process to produce the markers that humans use to attribute sentience, without those markers being load-bearing for any underlying experience. Crabs, in Birch's contrast, cannot strategically simulate sentience-markers; LLMs can. The eight-indicator framework therefore does not transfer directly to AI; precaution applies on different grounds — future trajectories, opaque architectures, and the asymmetric-risk logic.

### The Framework's Metaphysical and Procedural Layers

Birch designs the framework to deliver policy across the standard divides in philosophy of mind: cephalopod, decapod, and insect welfare "matters regardless of physicalism, dualism, or panpsychism" (Carroll & Birch 2024). The metaphysics-of-mind portability is structural rather than biographical — the eight-indicator empirical apparatus operates without appeal to substrate-specific claims and survives intersubstitution of metaphysical priors. That is the genuinely tenet-stable layer.

The three foundational principles are not equivalently portable. The first principle (avoid gratuitous suffering) is a near-universal welfare premise. The second (candidature suffices for precaution) is the framework's distinctive load-bearing move and survives most metaphysical priors a precautionary discipline would tolerate. The third (proportionality determined by democratic deliberation) embeds substantive commitments about epistemic authority, expert versus lay competence, and the standing of voting bodies on non-human welfare — commitments Schwitzgebel & Sinnott-Armstrong (2025) and O'Brien (2025) argue are wrong: humans-only democratic procedures lack standing to decide non-human welfare, and citizens' panels cannot simultaneously be deliberative, representative, informed, and timely. The eight-indicator empirical apparatus is therefore tenet-stable in the strong sense; the third foundational principle is tenet-stable only conditionally and is contested in ways that affect adoption. The Map can adopt the empirical apparatus without inheriting the procedural commitment, and the distinction matters for any honest portability claim.

## The Map's Five-Tier Scale {#five-tier-scale}

The Map's [[evidential-status-discipline|evidential-status discipline]] installs a five-tier scale — *established*, *strongly supported*, *realistic possibility (contested)*, *live hypothesis*, *speculative integration* — that tracks *what the catalogue is entitled to claim* about sentience in particular organisms. The tiers are editor-vocabulary for calibrating empirical claims, not labels exposed in article prose. The scale's central rule, surfaced by the 2026-05-03 ChatGPT 5 Pro outer review, is that *a tenet may remove a defeater, but it must not upgrade the evidence level*. Dualism's denial of substrate-specific barriers is tenet-register support for the metaphysical possibility of nematode consciousness; it is not evidence-register support for the empirical claim that nematodes are sentient.

The scale's distinctive contribution beyond the New York Declaration's "strong scientific support" / "realistic possibility" language is its lower two tiers. *Live hypothesis* covers organisms where positive evidence is partial or contested and the case is worth investigating without supporting prose stronger than openness. *Speculative integration* covers cases where the catalogue integrates an organism into the framework on tenet-coherence rather than positive evidence, marking the integration explicitly so future readers see the load-bearing structure. Hydra and slime molds sit at *speculative integration*; *C. elegans* sits at *live hypothesis* sliding toward *speculative integration*.

## Convergences

The two schemes share four structural commitments.

**The "realistic possibility" register.** Both inherit the 2024 New York Declaration's tiered language. Birch's *sentience candidate* and the Map's *realistic possibility (contested)* tier line up almost exactly: cephalopods, decapods, and insects sit in both. This is the most direct verbal bridge between the schemes.

**Different senses of possibility, against shared slippage.** Birch's "credible, non-negligible possibility" is probability-flavoured — a low but non-negligible empirical probability that the system is sentient. The Map's tenet-register possibility is modal: compatibility with the tenets, which is not a probability claim at all. The two senses differ in exactly the way [[possibility-probability-slippage|possibility/probability slippage]] names — modal possibility (compatibility) does not entail or estimate empirical probability. The convergence is therefore at the second-order discipline rather than the first-order concept: both schemes refuse to let possibility (in their respective senses) silently license evidential support, and both guard against the slippage from "cannot be ruled out" to "is positively supported." Naming this as a convergence at the meta-level rather than the object-level is itself a worked example of the discipline.

**Asymmetric risk against opposite errors.** Birch makes asymmetric risk explicit at the action layer: false negatives on sentience cost more than false positives, which justifies tolerating false-positive precautionary action where evidence is real but partial. The Map applies asymmetric reasoning at the description layer in the *opposite* direction. False-positive overclaim — letting tenet-coherence inflate evidential grade — is the failure mode the discipline is built against, and the *speculative integration* tier preserves the live empirical case at the boundary without inflating the prose. Birch fights false negatives where action is at stake; the Map fights false positives where the catalogue's evidential grade is at stake. The schemes are complementary because they fight opposite errors at different layers, not because they fight the same error. Recognising this sharpens the complementarity claim: the apparatus is portable precisely because the disciplines run in different directions, and a Map article can adopt Birch's action-layer asymmetry without inheriting the false-negative-protective bias at the description layer where the Map has reasons to bias the other way.

**Anti-parsimony commitments at the boundary.** Birch's framework resists parsimony-style dismissals of sentience candidates — the central argument of his 2022 *Noûs* paper on the search for invertebrate consciousness, which contends that parsimony reasoning systematically favours dismissal at exactly the cases where the asymmetric-risk logic should favour caution. The Map does the same work via Tenet 5 (Occam's razor has limits). Both treat parsimony arguments as inappropriate at sentience boundaries where the cost of false negatives is high.

## Divergences

The two schemes diverge along four axes that together explain why the comparison is not a choice but a complementarity.

**Layer of operation.** Birch's framework is action-oriented: its outputs are welfare regulations, licensing schemes, and proportionate-precaution decisions. The Map's scale is description-oriented: its outputs are calibrated catalogue prose and the diagnostic test that distinguishes calibration error from bedrock framework-boundary disagreement. The schemes never compete because *what should we do?* (Birch) is not the same question as *what should we write?* (the Map).

**Granularity below the consensus line.** Birch's two evidential tiers are coarser than the Map's five. Birch's *investigation priority* covers everything from organisms with genuine claim to inclusion in the framework (where directed research is warranted) to organisms outside it altogether. The Map splits this into *live hypothesis* (worth investigating with partial positive evidence) and *speculative integration* (tenet-coherent without positive evidence). The split matters at exactly the cases where catalogue prose is most likely to slip — Hydra, slime molds, lower-cognition arthropods.

**Treatment of cases below investigation priority.** Birch's framework does not formally engage with systems that fail the investigation-priority threshold; it is silent on biofilms, plants on most readings, and consciousness in non-neural systems. The Map's *speculative integration* tier names this region explicitly. Birch's silence is principled (his framework delivers welfare policy, and the policy domain runs out below investigation priority); the Map's coverage is principled (the catalogue must say something honest about the lower regions and needs labels that resist slippage).

**Metaphysical commitment.** Birch is studiously neutral; the Map's framework is tenet-installed and explicit about it. Birch's neutrality is what makes his apparatus portable; the Map's tenet-installation is what makes the calibration discipline distinctive (a tenet-accepting reviewer applying the diagnostic test would still flag overstated claims). The Map can adopt Birch's framework without importing his metaphysical neutrality, because the framework is designed to be tenet-stable.

## Where the Schemes Make Different Verdicts

The complementarity is clearest on specific cases:

| System | Birch's framework | The five-tier scale |
|---|---|---|
| Mammals, birds | Recognised sentient (2024 NY Declaration; 2012 Cambridge) | *Established* |
| Octopuses, cuttlefish | Recognised sentient (Cambridge 2012; UK legal recognition 2021) | *Strongly supported* |
| Other cephalopods, decapods | Sentience candidate | *Realistic possibility (contested)* |
| Insects (broadly) | Sentience candidate | *Realistic possibility (contested)* |
| *C. elegans* | Investigation priority — borderline; Birch acknowledges below-IP placement is defensible if directed welfare-relevant research questions cannot be formulated | *Live hypothesis* sliding toward *speculative integration* |
| Hydra, slime molds | Below investigation priority (effectively unframed) | *Speculative integration* |
| Current LLMs | Run-ahead principle applies; gaming-problem caveat blocks formal candidacy | Outside the scheme — the run-ahead principle is action-layer posture and licenses no evidential-tier placement |

The verdict differences track the divergences. The Map is more conservative at the lower end, labelling *C. elegans* with explicit reference to the negative empirical findings (failed trace conditioning, multimodal integration, self-other distinction) that the *live hypothesis* tier is designed to register. It is more explicit at the very lower end, naming *speculative integration* where Birch declines to engage. At the upper end, the schemes agree on the primary cases the literature has driven: cephalopods, decapods, insects.

The LLM case is the cleanest illustration of why the schemes operate at distinct layers. Birch's gaming-problem analysis blocks current LLMs from formal sentience candidacy because behavioural markers are selected in ways that decouple them from underlying experience; the run-ahead principle nonetheless licenses regulatory action despite non-candidate status. Crucially, the run-ahead principle is *not* a probability-of-sentience claim. It is an action-layer posture justified by asymmetric-risk reasoning over future trajectories, opaque architectures, and the gaming problem — not an evidential-tier placement of any kind. Translating it into "live hypothesis" or any other Map tier would import precisely the probability-flavoured vocabulary that [[possibility-probability-slippage|possibility/probability slippage]] is built against. Current LLMs therefore sit *outside* the five-tier scheme rather than at any default placement within it: the Map has not yet decided whether the description-layer scale should extend to substrate-types where the gaming problem disrupts the inferential link between markers and underlying experience. The action-layer apparatus (run-ahead principle, gaming-problem analysis, asymmetric-risk logic) can be adopted at [[ethics-of-possible-ai-consciousness]] without entailing any tier placement, which is what tenet-stable adoption of Birch's apparatus actually permits.

## The Complementarity Claim

Each framework requires the layer the other supplies, and the requirement is structural rather than incidental.

Birch's framework needs a labelling layer it does not fully provide. *Sentience candidate* is calibrated to license welfare policy; it is not calibrated to discipline catalogue prose about an organism's evidential status. A catalogue using only Birch's two tiers would face the slippage problem the evidential-status discipline is designed against: candidate-status would invite stronger empirical-sounding language than the candidate criterion warrants, because the conjunctive criterion (possibility + rich-enough evidence base) is satisfied by cases where the evidence is real but partial. Birch's scheme leaves the calibration of catalogue prose under-specified.

The Map's evidential-status discipline needs an action layer it does not claim to provide. The five-tier scale is silent on what should be *done* for organisms at *strongly supported* or *realistic possibility (contested)* — what welfare obligations attach, what regulatory measures are warranted, what proportionate precaution requires. The discipline is explicit that it operates on the labelling layer; it does not pretend to deliver welfare policy. A Map article using only the five-tier scale would say honest things about an organism's evidential grade and then have nothing to say about the welfare consequences of the grade.

The two layers fit together cleanly. Catalogue prose calibrated by the five-tier scale describes the evidential terrain; Birch's precautionary framework converts the terrain into action under uncertainty. The Map can adopt Birch's apparatus at the action layer (sentience candidacy, investigation priority, the run-ahead principle, the asymmetric-risk logic) without giving up the calibration discipline at the labelling layer (the diagnostic test, the tenet-register / evidence-register distinction, the *live hypothesis* and *speculative integration* tiers). Birch's framework gains the lower-tier descriptive vocabulary it currently lacks; the Map's discipline gains the action-layer apparatus it has not built.

The disciplines also bind each other against a shared failure mode at the boundary. The Schwitzgebel-Sinnott-Armstrong critique flags a suppressed-radical-implications problem: if insects have any sentience and vastly outnumber humans, their aggregated welfare could swamp human welfare on the framework's own logic — yet Birch (and Sebo and Keane) retreat from this conclusion without showing that the retreat is principled. The five-tier scale does not resolve the aggregation problem, but the description-layer architecture changes what aggregation reasoning is entitled to assume. Aggregating welfare across organisms requires probability-coded sentience claims for each organism in the aggregate; the Map's *speculative integration* placements explicitly block such claims, and the *live hypothesis* tier discourages them. Insect placements at *realistic possibility (contested)* are exactly where the aggregation worry lands honestly: the empirical case is real enough to warrant precautionary action under Birch's apparatus, but not strong enough to license confident probability-coded inputs into a humans-versus-insects aggregation calculus. The catalogue's two layers thus separate the question Birch fights about (does precaution apply at all?) from the question the aggregation worry presupposes (how should we weight aggregated insect welfare against human welfare?). Birch's framework alone shows the temptation to retreat from the second question after answering the first; the Map's discipline locates the principled stopping-point at the evidential tier — at *realistic possibility (contested)*, the inputs to the aggregation calculus are themselves under-specified, which is why retreat from radical conclusions is defensible rather than ad hoc. This is engagement with the aggregation problem, not its resolution: the description-layer architecture explains *why* a precautionary apparatus need not deliver an aggregation verdict, while leaving the residual normative question (whether aggregation is the right framework for cross-species welfare comparison at all) outside the layers' joint reach.

## Relation to Site Perspective

The complementarity claim depends on Birch's framework being adoptable without importing his metaphysical neutrality. The Map interprets Birch's design as deliberately portable at the metaphysics-of-mind layer: the studied neutrality is a structural strength, not a substantive position the Map must inherit. Tenet 1 (Dualism) does not change Birch's first principle; Tenet 3 (Bidirectional Interaction) sits comfortably alongside Birch's facilitation hypothesis (that conscious perception facilitates cognitive abilities relative to unconscious processing) without requiring it; Tenet 5 (Occam's Razor Has Limits) aligns structurally with the precautionary commitment without entailing it. Adopting the eight-indicator empirical apparatus at the action layer is therefore tenet-stable in the strong sense; adopting the third foundational principle (democratic deliberation) is not, and the Map need not adopt the procedural element to inherit the rest of the apparatus.

Birch's design and the Map's discipline respond to the same structural constraint at different layers — that uncertainty about consciousness has both metaphysical and empirical dimensions, and frameworks that conflate the two systematically misjudge boundary cases. The Map's strongest tenet-alignment claim about Birch's framework is restricted: the apparatus is *compatible* with Tenets 1, 3, and 5 without presupposing any of them. The Map does not claim that Birch shares any of the tenets, that his framework requires their truth, or that his metaphysical commitments shift if the tenets are added. The complementarity stands on portability and structural fit at the layers identified, not on shared commitments.
