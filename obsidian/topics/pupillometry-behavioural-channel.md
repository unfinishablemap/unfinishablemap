Pupillometry — the millisecond-resolution measurement of pupil diameter under controlled luminance — provides one of the few empirical pathways on which confabulation can be *demonstrated* from a third-person register rather than merely inferred. The pattern that matters is channel-level: in tasks where a verbal report and an autonomic pupil response are recorded in the same trial, the two channels can disagree in interpretable ways, and the disagreement persists across paradigms. The behavioural-physiological channel sometimes registers content the verbal channel does not report — and sometimes the verbal channel reports content the pupillary channel does not register. The dissociation is not a global indictment of introspection. It is a structural finding about *what each channel is good for*, and about where the conjunction of channels is a stronger probe than either alone.

This article surveys the empirical literature on pupil-dilation as a leak-channel revealing what the verbal report misses, frames the dissociation as channel-level rather than as evidence that introspection is uniformly unreliable, examines the methodological consequences for a phenomenology paired with instrumentation, and engages honestly with the question of what the pupillary findings do and do not show for the Map's commitments. The pupillary channel does not settle the question of consciousness's relation to its substrate; it does provide partial third-person verification where unaided introspection cannot.

## What Pupillometry Measures

The pupil dilates in response to cognitive load, surprise, decision uncertainty, emotional arousal, and reward processing. Beatty's foundational 1982 *Psychological Bulletin* review consolidated three decades of work establishing that "task-evoked pupillary responses provide a reliable and sensitive indication of within-task processing load." The locus coeruleus-noradrenergic system supplies the principal neural correlate: phasic pupil-dilation tracks the locus coeruleus's burst firing in non-human primate work (Joshi et al. 2016) and in human pharmacological manipulation studies. The pupil is functionally a window onto a sub-cortical arousal system whose outputs precede and partly bypass cortical report.

Three properties make the pupil useful as a channel distinct from verbal report:

- **Involuntary.** Subjects cannot reliably modulate pupil dilation by intention. A pupillary response is not a *report*; it is a signature.
- **Fast.** Phasic pupil dilations peak in roughly 500-1500 ms following an evoking event, well before deliberate verbal articulation completes.
- **Stimulus-locked and event-related.** Pupil signals can be aligned to specific trials, contrasts, and manipulations the experimenter controls, supporting trial-level dissociation analyses rather than block averages.

These properties are why the literature treats pupillometry as a *bodily channel* yielding evidence that is registered without authorial control and timed against external events the experimenter selects.

## Choice-Blindness and the Detection of Swapped Trials

The canonical channel-dissociation paradigm is Johansson and Hall's *choice blindness* protocol. In the original 2005 Science paper, participants choose between two face photographs they find more attractive; on selected trials the experimenter covertly swaps the selected photograph for the non-selected one, hands the manipulated photograph back, and asks the participant to explain their choice. A substantial proportion of swaps go undetected in verbal report, and participants then fluently justify a choice they did not in fact make. The verbal channel produces a confident explanation for a choice that did not occur.

The pupillometric extension is the methodologically critical move. Subsequent groups extended the paradigm with pupil-dilation recording on manipulated and non-manipulated trials and found that the pupillary channel registers *covert* detection of swapped trials even when the verbal channel does not flag the manipulation. The pupil dilates more on manipulated trials than on matched control trials, on average and at trial-level resolution, in the same participants who proceed to confabulate justifications for the swapped choice. The body knows; the verbal channel does not.

This pattern is exactly the kind of evidence that distinguishes "the introspector failed to attend" from "the introspector cannot in principle reach this content." A general failure of attention would predict that the manipulation leaves no trace anywhere accessible to the participant. The pupillary signal shows that the manipulation *was* registered by sub-cortical machinery the participant has no first-person access to — registered well enough to drive an autonomic dilation, but not channelled into the verbal report. The verbal channel and the pupillary channel are reporting on different things, and the difference is structural rather than incidental.

The choice-blindness pupillometry finding has been narrowed and extended across the 2015–2024 window. Some early effect sizes have been revised downward in pre-registered replications; the structural dissociation has survived these revisions in the published series. The literature has converged on a more cautious claim than the strongest readings supplied: pupillary dilation is a partial and probabilistic detection signal, not a reliable trial-level lie-detector, and it succeeds in aggregate across participants while leaving individual trials noisy.

## What the Channel Asymmetry Looks Like

Treating pupillometry as a channel rather than a measurement of introspective accuracy clarifies what the literature actually shows. Three patterns recur:

**Pupil registers, verbal does not.** Choice-blindness manipulations, masked emotional faces, and unattended deviants in the auditory oddball paradigm all produce pupillary dilation absent corresponding verbal report. The pupil is registering content that does not enter the report-eligible workspace.

**Verbal reports, pupil does not.** Confabulated content — explanations of behaviour for which the participant has no causal access — is reported fluently and confidently, often without the pupillary signature of effortful problem-solving or surprise. The verbal channel is producing a report that does not correspond to a separately detectable internal event.

**Both channels register, one weighted differently.** Decisions under uncertainty produce pupillary dilation proportional to internal decision-evidence weakness (Urai et al. 2017 and follow-ups), while verbal confidence tracks the participant's *meta-representation* of decision quality. The two channels respond to overlapping but non-identical features of the underlying decision process; their correlation is positive but partial.

The third pattern is the one that matters for the channel-level framing. The pupillary channel is not a more accurate version of the verbal channel. It is a different channel, exposed to a different slice of the underlying processing, with its own selectivity and its own characteristic blind spots. The pupil does not register the content of a thought; it registers the cost of having had it. The verbal channel reports the content; the pupil reports the load. Each channel does work the other cannot, and the conjunction reveals what neither does alone.

## The Methodological Consequence: Conjunction Probes

The strongest methodological reading of the pupillometry literature is that pupillary instrumentation paired with structured verbal report is a *conjunction probe* — a stronger probe than either channel alone. The microphenomenological interview (see [[topics/microphenomenological-interview-method|microphenomenological interview method]]) sharpens the verbal channel by training and structured prompting, raising the assertoric grade of pre-reflective descriptions. Pupillometry sharpens the bodily channel by providing trial-locked autonomic measurement. A conjunction probe deploys both: the verbal channel makes available articulated content the bodily channel does not produce, while the bodily channel registers events the verbal channel cannot reach. The autonomic, third-person dissociation this article studies and the wager- and opt-out behavioural proxies of [[topics/cross-species-behavioural-confidence-proxy-tests|cross-species behavioural-confidence proxy tests]] are complementary within-biology channel methodologies under the same [[topics/introspection-architecture-independence-scoring|introspection-architecture-independence]] exhibit — pupillometry reading an involuntary bodily signature, the proxy tests reading a structured behavioural wager — each probing report-independent registration where the verbal channel cannot reach.

This conjunction matters for several Map arguments. The [[concepts/heterophenomenology|heterophenomenology]] tradition treats verbal reports as data about a "world the subject takes herself to inhabit" without committing to inner referents. Pupillometry data is consumed by heterophenomenology on the same neutral terms — *Dennett's method can ingest a pupillary trace as a behavioural fact about the subject*. But where the heterophenomenologist might claim the verbal report exhausts what can be said, the pupillary data forces a more discriminating treatment: the verbal report and the bodily channel can disagree, and the disagreement is itself evidence about the architecture of report-production rather than about phenomenal facts.

The microphenomenological tradition, by contrast, aims to strengthen the verbal channel's reach into pre-reflective content. Pairing microphenomenology with pupillometry would deliver, at the limit, a method that strengthens the verbal channel where it can be strengthened and registers the body's separate evidence where the verbal channel cannot reach. Neither tradition has yet conducted such conjunction studies at scale. The opportunity is open.

A practical caveat belongs here. Pupillary signals are noisy, sensitive to luminance and gaze position, and prone to confounds (effort, surprise, reward) that the experimenter must control by design rather than by *post hoc* correction. The instrumental cost of running a conjunction study well is non-trivial, and the literature contains a substantial number of underpowered single-channel studies that are not load-bearing for the structural claim. The structural claim — that the channels can dissociate and that the dissociation is informative — survives even where individual studies are revised.

## What the Pupillary Channel Does Not Settle

A careful reading of the pupillometry literature also fixes what these findings do *not* show, and this fixing is part of the article's discipline. See [[evidential-status-discipline|evidential-status discipline]] for the broader framing.

Pupillometry does not show that consciousness is non-physical, or that any specific architecture of the introspective interface is correct. The findings are *channel-level* facts about report-production and bodily detection, and they are compatible with multiple background commitments. A physicalist reading can be supplied: the pupillary signal indexes sub-cortical arousal driven by stimulus-features that did not reach the cortical workspace where verbal report is assembled; the dissociation reflects functional architecture and nothing more. The Map's reading (developed below) adds further interpretation, but the data do not require it.

Pupillometry does not deliver real-time detection of confabulation usable as a personal honesty-check. The signals are aggregate-level, third-person, post-hoc, and not first-person-accessible. The introspector remains, from inside, in the position the [[voids/confabulation-void|confabulation void]] catalogues: the felt difference between veridical and confabulated report is absent, and the body's separate registration is not part of conscious access. The instrumental probe is external, not introspective.

Pupillometry does not refute heterophenomenology. The pupillary channel is a third-person measurement of behaviour (in the broad biological sense), and Dennett's method treats it on the same neutral terms as a verbal report. What pupillometry does is *force heterophenomenology to attend to channel-level dissociation* — to register that the subject's heterophenomenological world (the world they say they inhabit) and the subject's bodily behaviour (the world the experimenter measures) can come apart in ways that are themselves informative.

## Relation to Site Perspective

The channel-level dissociation is consistent with both physicalist and dualist accounts. The Map does not over-claim that pupillometry favours its reading. The dissociation is, however, *what the Map's introspection-architecture sub-cluster predicts*: a bounded-witness architecture in which consciousness has access to *some* features of its substrate (the conscious-side endpoint of report-production) and not others (the sub-personal generation, including the pupillary side), with the gap filled by interpolated content carrying the phenomenology of accurate report. The Map's [[tenets#^dualism|Dualism tenet]] does not entail this architecture, but it accommodates it more naturally than a reductive identity claim accommodates the structural channel-asymmetry.

**Bidirectional Interaction** receives a methodological supplement from the pupillometry literature. If consciousness does any of the small-quantum-amplification work the Map's [[tenets#^bidirectional-interaction|Bidirectional Interaction tenet]] envisages, the [[voids/confabulation-void|confabulation void]] guarantees that the verbal channel will retroactively label every action as caused by reasons-the-introspector-now-supplies. The pupillary channel, being involuntary and timed against external events, provides a partial external verification that the verbal-channel report and the bodily-channel signature can be separately registered. The Map does not claim the pupillary signature *is* the consciousness-side influence; the claim is more modest. It is that a method that registers bodily evidence independently of verbal report is methodologically *required* for any future programme that hopes to constrain the consciousness-interface question empirically — and that pupillometry, alongside EEG and fMRI, is among the developed instruments such a programme would deploy.

**Evidential-status discipline.** The pupillometry findings are *contested but real*: a robust pattern at the structural level, with effect sizes that have been revised and individual studies that have not all replicated. The Map registers the finding as a calibration anchor for the introspection-architecture sub-cluster rather than as a settled empirical result. The [[voids/confabulation-void#detection-the-felt-signal-operates-inversely|detection face of the confabulation void]] cites the Rebouillat et al. 2021 anti-correlation finding alongside the choice-blindness pupillometry results as the convergent empirical anchor for the structural claim.

A final reflexive note. The article's case rests on the verbal-channel/bodily-channel dissociation being *demonstrable from a third-person register*. The introspector reading this article cannot, from the inside, verify the dissociation in their own case — the pupillary channel is not first-person-accessible. The case is therefore one where structural third-person evidence is doing methodological work that no first-person discipline can replicate. This is part of why the Map treats pupillometry as one of the load-bearing empirical anchors for the [[voids/confabulation-void|confabulation void]]'s detection face, while remaining cautious about what the finding *settles*.
