Memory systems do not fail together when consciousness is disrupted. They fail in a fixed order. Across pharmacological anaesthesia, NREM and REM sleep, hemispherectomy recovery, terminal lucidity, and dissociative states, the same ordering recurs: [[episodic-memory|episodic / autonoetic]] memory is most vulnerable, [[semantic-memory|semantic / noetic]] is intermediate, and [[implicit-memory|procedural / anoetic]] is most preserved. And the disruption has a *second* signature the ordering alone does not capture: the order in which a system degrades is often *not* the mirror image of the order in which it recovers. Autonoetic consciousness is the last channel to return when a deeply anaesthetised brain emerges, yet it is among the first to return in terminal lucidity. The Unfinishable Map reads both patterns — the graded vulnerability hierarchy and its direction-dependent recovery asymmetry — as among the catalogue's stronger pieces of interface evidence: they appear to be the *shape* of evidence a multi-channel interface would predict, and a shape production theories seem to struggle to produce without ad-hoc fitting. The reading is offered as a serious interpretation the data make available, not one the data force.

The two patterns are two faces of one architecture. The vulnerability hierarchy is the *substrate-state* face — which channel fails first as the substrate degrades. Recovery-order asymmetry is the *direction-of-travel* face — that the signature tracks the *direction* of interface change, not only the substrate state at a moment. This article develops both across the clinical-state cases that test them, with the central claim held comparative and calibrated throughout: the patterns *constrain* the production-vs-filter comparison rather than *establishing* the filter reading. Honouring [[evidential-status-discipline|the evidential-status discipline]], they are accommodation evidence, not predictive success at single-event resolution.

The argument has the structure of an accommodation contest. Production theories — on which neural activity *generates* conscious states — can absorb the patterns by differential cortical-area vulnerability, hierarchical-prior reweighting, or direction-specific mechanisms, but each absorption costs an extra mechanism or a pattern explained after the fact. The [[filter-theory|filter theory]] reads the same patterns off its core architecture: an interface with channels of differing bandwidth and reconstruction cost is predicted to degrade in bandwidth-order under partial blockade and to recover in a direction-dependent order when blockade lifts. The disagreement is not over the evidence but over where each framework pays the mechanism cost ([[mechanism-costs-dualism-thickness-quadrants|mechanism-costs cartography]]).

## The Tulving Hierarchy as Diagnostic Framework

Endel Tulving's 1985 framework divides memory into three systems, each with its own mode of consciousness ([[anoetic-noetic-autonoetic-consciousness|developed in detail at the concept tier]]). The bare structure is what does the work here.

**Anoetic consciousness** accompanies [[implicit-memory|procedural and implicit memory]]: present-tense phenomenal fluency without self-representation, as in a skilled cyclist. **Noetic consciousness** accompanies [[semantic-memory|semantic memory]]: a feeling of knowing (Paris is the capital of France) without re-experiencing any episode, made vivid by the tip-of-the-tongue phenomenon. **Autonoetic consciousness** accompanies [[episodic-memory|episodic memory]] and its future-directed counterpart: remembering a childhood afternoon involves *re-inhabiting* a perspective, the self appearing twice — present rememberer and past protagonist — with a felt sense of pastness that constitutes the experience. Wheeler, Stuss, and Tulving (1997) defined it as "the capacity that allows adult humans to mentally represent and to become aware of their protracted existence across subjective time."

The three systems are doubly dissociable in the clinical literature. Patient K.C., after extensive bilateral brain damage, retained semantic and noetic awareness while losing episodic memory and autonoetic consciousness entirely (Tulving 2002); semantic dementia produces the inverse early in its course; skilled procedural performance can survive both. This is a layered architecture whose layers can be addressed separately, not a single continuum.

Klein (2014) sharpened the framework by separating *content* from *manner of experiencing*: the same proposition ("I visited Rome in 2019") can be accompanied by autonoetic re-experiencing or by bare noetic recognition. This separability is what the hierarchy-as-interface-evidence argument needs. If the three modes were degrees of one substrate, partial degradation might shift the whole uniformly. Instead, it appears to operate *channel-by-channel* in a specific order.

## The Cross-State Vulnerability Pattern

The hierarchy below summarises what is reliably observed across the clinical-state cases. Empirical citations for each row are developed in the sections that follow.

| State | Anoetic / Implicit | Noetic / Semantic | Autonoetic / Episodic |
|-------|--------------------|-------------------|----------------------|
| Light anaesthesia | Preserved | Partially impaired | Severed (anterograde amnesia) |
| Deep anaesthesia (propofol) | Largely preserved | Severed | Severed |
| Ketamine "disconnected" state | Preserved | Variable | Preserved but content-decoupled |
| NREM Stage 3 | Preserved | Suppressed | Severed (no episodic encoding) |
| REM sleep | Preserved | Partial | Partial (lacks autonoetic self-coherence) |
| Hemispherectomy (adult-onset) | Preserved | Largely preserved | Initially impaired, often recovers |
| Terminal lucidity | Often returns last | Returns mid-episode | Returns earliest in the lucid window |
| Dissociative amnesia | Preserved | Preserved | Selectively severed (autobiographical) |
| DID (between alters) | Preserved | Preserved | Severed (autonoetic compartmentalisation) |
| Depersonalisation | Preserved | Preserved | Present but felt as not-mine |

Two patterns stand out. The **ordering is stable**: autonoetic goes first under degradation, and under recovery returns earliest in some states (terminal lucidity) and latest in others (deep anaesthesia emergence), but never randomly. The **selectivity is sharp**: each state produces a specific cross-channel signature, not a uniform dim-out — and the dissociative rows are especially diagnostic, since the substrate is intact and what separates is the connectivity between channels, not the channels themselves.

### Anaesthesia: The Anchor Case

[[anaesthesia-and-the-consciousness-interface|Anaesthesia]] is the cleanest exhibit because the perturbation is reversible, dose-dependent, pharmacologically selective, and can be measured in *both directions* within the same subject. Adequate surgical anaesthesia produces anterograde amnesia — no episodic encoding — while leaving implicit learning intact: patients under propofol can be conditioned despite no episodic memory of the surgery. The graded picture is the revealing one: light sedation impairs autonoetic encoding while semantic comprehension persists (the patient answers but will not remember); deeper sedation severs noetic access while implicit nociceptive learning may continue. The xenon-ketamine contrast (two NMDA antagonists with opposite phenomenological outcomes) shows channels separable even within a single receptor system. The hierarchy is the joint output of several pharmacologically distinct mechanisms converging on one ordering — and that convergence is the load-bearing fact: under any single mechanism cross-agent uniformity would be coincidence, but under a bandwidth/reconstruction-cost architecture it is arguably expected. The ketamine row — behavioural unresponsiveness with later vivid autonoetic retrospective reports — is one row of the same dissociation pattern anchoring [[self-stultification-as-master-argument|the self-stultification master argument]]'s clinical exhibit.

### Sleep Stages: The Within-Subject Replication

Sleep replicates the hierarchy within a single subject: across one night the same brain cycles through stage-specific signatures. **NREM Stage 3** suppresses autonoetic consciousness most strongly — episodic encoding is essentially absent and the sleep-mentation literature characterises such awakenings as more "thought-like" than re-experienced (Siclari et al. 2017 mapped the regional correlates) — while procedural consolidation proceeds at full strength. **REM sleep** reactivates the interface unevenly: dream content has propositional structure and vivid sensory presence, but the *autonoetic self-coherence* that organises waking episodic memory is conspicuously degraded (the dreamer rarely notices contradictions or projects a temporal self); lucid dreaming, where some autonoetic self-awareness returns, is the trainable exception that confirms the rule. **Hypnagogic transitions** loosen gradually in the same order observed during anaesthesia induction — the within-subject ordering replicates the cross-subject one ([[sleep-and-consciousness]] develops the broader picture).

### Hemispherectomy: The Substrate-Loss Case

[[hemispherectomy-and-the-resilience-of-unified-consciousness|Hemispherectomy]] — surgical removal or disconnection of one cerebral hemisphere — provides a substrate-loss test the other cases cannot. Performed in adulthood for intractable epilepsy or tumour, it produces severe initial impairments but typically substantial recovery of consciousness, language, and personality; the patient persists as a recognisable self across the surgery, uncomfortable for the simplest production reading. The hierarchy-relevant observation is the *order* of recovery: procedural function returns earliest, semantic memory more gradually through plasticity, autonoetic episodic memory of the surgical period often permanently affected while new-encoding capacity returns last as the remaining hemisphere reorganises. Child-onset cases add a calibration constraint — early hemispherectomy produces near-complete recovery including autonoetic capacity, because the developing brain can reassign the architecture. On the interface reading these are channels that are real and partially substrate-anchored but with plastic tissue assignment (the contested interpretive step, not a datum the pattern hands over); the production reading must additionally explain why the same recovery ordering recurs across very different lesion topographies.

### Terminal Lucidity: The Recovery-Order Test

[[terminal-lucidity-and-filter-transmission-theory|Terminal lucidity]] is the catalogue's strongest exhibit of selective channel-restoration. Patients with advanced dementia or other severe decline sometimes recover, in the hours or days before death, a striking lucidity — recognising relatives, recalling episodes, reassembling an autonoetic narrative their long-degraded brain had appeared to have lost. The phenomenon is rare but reproducibly documented: Nahm and Greyson (2009) coined the term; their 2012 review with Kelly and Haraldsson collected 83 cases spanning 250 years, and Batthyány and Greyson's (2020) survey produced 124 detailed reports, over 80% showing return of memory, orientation, and verbal ability with most dying within hours to days. The case reports describe non-uniform restoration: autonoetic recollection returns sharply at the start of the window, semantic content stabilises mid-episode, the window closes as the autonoetic channel destabilises first. The substrate has continued to degrade — this is not the brain repairing itself in days. On the filter reading the interface may have briefly reopened in channels that were blocked rather than destroyed; on the production reading a substrate insufficient yesterday becomes sufficient today before becoming insufficient tomorrow.

The Map holds calibration discipline. Terminal lucidity is a live hypothesis the evidence does not decide; the case literature is anecdotal and selection-biased, and rival readings may propose substrate-state mechanisms (paradoxical "decompression" of inhibition, a reactive noradrenergic surge) that absorb the data without an interface. The narrower claim is that terminal lucidity tests the interface reading most directly because the *order of return* is observed under continuing substrate degradation — which makes it also the pivotal case for the direction-asymmetry argument below.

### Dissociative States: Selectivity Without Substrate Damage

Dissociative states decouple the channels without any structural substrate damage ([[clinical-dissociation-as-systematic-evidence|full picture here]]). **Dissociative amnesia** selectively severs autobiographical (autonoetic) access while semantic knowledge, procedural skill, and working memory remain intact — the patient drives and works but cannot recall who they are; when the barrier lifts the memories return immediately and fully (the bridge reconnects, it is not re-grown). **Dissociative identity disorder** compartmentalises autonoetic access *between* alter states, each with its own autobiographical timeline, while semantic and procedural memory transfer freely. **Depersonalisation-derealisation** is unusually diagnostic: autonoetic content is present but the felt *ownership* is missing — the channel is on, the *mine-ness* it normally carries decoupled — suggesting autonoetic experience has internal structure (content plus mine-ness plus pastness) that can dissociate, the multi-channel reading's prediction one level deeper than the three-tier hierarchy itself.

The dissociative rows are the hierarchy's hardest test for production theories: with no substrate damage, the selectivity must be explained at the level of *connectivity between intact regions* — exactly what the filter reading describes as channel architecture, and exactly what a sophisticated production reading must concede consciousness depends on. The concession locates the disagreement at the right point: not over the evidence, but over what kind of relationship that connectivity *is*. These geometries are developed at full resolution, with the DID sociocognitive caveat, in [[clinical-dissociation-as-systematic-evidence|the clinical-dissociation article]].

## The Structural Argument: Graded-with-Separable-Channels

The hierarchy makes a claim single-state evidence cannot make alone: *the order is robust across mechanisms*. Anaesthesia, sleep, hemispherectomy, terminal lucidity, and dissociation share no common neural cause, yet they converge on the same ordering of channel vulnerability. This [[cross-mechanism-convergence|cross-mechanism convergence]] is the explanandum. A binary "consciousness on / off" production theory cannot explain it — it predicts uniform loss when the substrate degrades, and is refuted by the cross-state data. The case for production theories must start from a position that already concedes consciousness has separable *parts*; the disagreement then runs deeper than monism-vs-dualism, through how the parts and their ordering are explained.

A sophisticated production reading concedes channel separability and explains the ordering by differential cortical-area vulnerability: autonoetic depends on more cortical regions than noetic, which depends on more than anoetic, so any perturbation hits autonoetic first. The mechanism cost is the *additional empirical commitment* that the regional vulnerability ordering matches the channel hierarchy across *all* the mechanism-distinct perturbations. This is testable in principle — a narrow perturbation hitting an autonoetic-supporting region while sparing noetic-supporting subregions should, on the production reading, produce a *different* ordering, while the filter reading predicts the standard one. The catalogue has no clean case yet, so the prediction is named as the future test the readings most cleanly disagree about; its design space is developed in [[targeted-lesion-discriminating-tests-between-production-and-filter-readings-of-the-memory-hierarchy|the targeted-lesion article]]. Naming the discriminating case rather than treating current convergence as already separating is what [[direct-refutation-discipline|the direct-refutation discipline]] requires when rivals cohabit the same evidence — a [[discrimination-problem|discrimination-problem]]-class underdetermination recurring across the catalogue. A predictive-processing reading instead absorbs the hierarchy through hierarchical-prior reweighting (higher self-modelling levels depend on lower-level integrity, so degradation propagates top-down) — arguably stronger because it derives the ordering from independent commitments, but still owing *why* the higher-level prior produces autonoetic phenomenology, which may land it on the [[hard-problem-of-consciousness|hard problem]] one level up.

The filter reading explains the ordering directly. Channels of higher bandwidth and higher reconstruction cost are more vulnerable to partial blockade and slower to restabilise. Autonoetic carries the highest bandwidth (temporal self-projection, double presence of self) and the highest reconstruction cost (each recall destabilises the trace and requires reconsolidation under conscious direction); noetic carries less, anoetic least. The ordering and the cross-mechanism convergence fall out of the architecture rather than being added to it. The mechanism cost is the metaphysical commitment to an interface — a cost the [[tenets#^minimal-quantum-interaction|Minimal Quantum Interaction]] tenet absorbs by specifying *how thin* the interface needs to be. The structural argument is not "the hierarchy proves the filter theory" but "the hierarchy locates the disagreement at the mechanism-cost layer, where the filter theory's advantage is *direct* (ordering predicted from architecture) while rival accommodations are *additional*." [[mechanism-costs-dualism-thickness-quadrants|Mechanism-costs cartography]] develops the general form.

## Recovery-Order Asymmetry: The Direction-of-Travel Face

The vulnerability hierarchy reads the architecture off the substrate *state* — which channel fails first. A second signature reads it off the *direction* of change: the order in which a system degrades is often not the mirror image of the order in which it recovers. A substrate-symmetric production account — one on which the same neural substrate both generates and recovers each channel — predicts, in its simplest form, that recovery should retrace degradation, because both orderings are read off the same substrate-vulnerability profile. The cross-state data violate that simplest expectation, and the violation is itself patterned across three cases.

**Anaesthesia: induction is not the reverse of emergence.** The asymmetry shows at two levels. First, the *hysteresis* of state transition: Sepúlveda et al. (2019) characterise *neural inertia* as the resistance of circuits to changing state — inducing unconsciousness requires higher concentrations than restoring it, so the loss- and recovery-of-consciousness thresholds do not coincide, and the brain at a given concentration is in a *different* state depending on direction of travel. Second, the *order of channel recovery*: on induction autonoetic encoding is the first casualty; on emergence it is the last to stabilise (arousal, then responsiveness, then orientation, autobiographical encoding latest). That reconstitution is itself direction-asymmetric: Stone, Kelz, Proekt, and Wasilczuk (2025, *British Journal of Anaesthesia*) found genetically identical mice under identical protocols emerging at times varying by at least two orders of magnitude — a stochastic neuronal-dynamics model fit where a deterministic pharmacokinetic one failed, and no single region controlled emergence. Induction tracks drug concentration; emergence is a distributed, stochastic reconstitution whose timing is *not* fixed by macroscopic substrate state. Its *interpretation* is held at a live-hypothesis tier — equally compatible with classical noise and interface indeterminacy ([[stochastic-emergence-as-quantum-interface-evidence|stochastic-emergence article]]); the robust point is narrower: emergence is not the deterministic reverse of induction.

**Terminal lucidity: recovery under continuing degradation.** The asymmetry's most striking case, because the *direction of substrate change* and the *direction of conscious change* point opposite ways. In deep-anaesthesia emergence the autonoetic channel returns *last*; in terminal lucidity autonoetic recollection often returns *first and most prominently*. Two recoveries of the same channel proceed in opposite order — an anaesthetised brain recovering on a rising substrate trajectory, a terminally lucid brain recovering conscious access on a *falling* one.

**Dissociation: onset is not the reverse of recovery.** With no structural damage, the asymmetry cannot be attributed to tissue loss or repair. Dissociative amnesia's *onset* can be gradual (a narrowing under stress); its *recovery*, when the barrier lifts, is frequently abrupt and complete — the bridge reconnects, it is not re-grown. With nothing to repair, a production account cannot appeal to differential regional recovery rates; the asymmetry has to be explained at the level of *connectivity between intact channels*.

These signatures form a family in which the *direction* of interface change is the load-bearing variable, catalogued for portable citation at [[direction-of-interface-change|direction-of-interface-change signatures]]. None is captured by a substrate-state-at-a-time description; each requires the explanation to be sensitive to *which way the system is moving*. They are the temporal-derivative face of the same architecture the vulnerability hierarchy reads off the substrate-state face — the architecture [[self-concealing-interface|the self-concealing-interface synthesis]] names as the seam-class register where the Map's evidence can live at all.

A production account *can* recover the asymmetry, but only by adding a direction-specific mechanism per case: hysteresis terms for the bistable switch, a dying-process neuromodulatory surge for terminal lucidity, a stochastic reconstitution model for emergence, a connectivity-gating story for dissociation. Each is independently reasonable; the point is the *additivity* — each case requires its own auxiliary assumption, because the direction-blind substrate-vulnerability profile predicts symmetry. The filter reading appears to derive a direction-sensitive signature from its core architecture without per-case additions: if channels differ in bandwidth and reconstruction cost, then *which* components block or reopen, and in *which order*, may depend on the direction and cause of change. The advantage is comparative and bounded — *direct* where production's is *additive* — a parsimony point at the mechanism-cost layer, not positive evidence the filter reading is true, because a production account willing to pay the per-case cost absorbs every signature. What would *discriminate* the readings is the work of the [[targeted-lesion-discriminating-tests-between-production-and-filter-readings-of-the-memory-hierarchy|targeted-lesion (substrate-state)]] and [[direction-dependent-discriminating-test-design|direction-dependent (direction-axis)]] test-design articles — a reversible perturbation whose up-ramp and down-ramp orderings the rival readings predict oppositely.

## Evidential Calibration

The argument must hold the calibration line at three points where it is structurally available to slide.

First, both patterns are *accommodation evidence*, not predictive success at single-event resolution. The filter theory did not predict the Tulving hierarchy in advance; Tulving's framework was developed independently and the filter theory inherits its mapping. The claim is that the hierarchy and its recovery asymmetry are *exactly the shape* of evidence the filter theory's architecture predicts, and that rival accommodations face higher mechanism costs. The architecture is what the cross-state data establish; the interface reading is what they make available without yet settling.

Second, the patterns do not rule out production theories; they locate the *mechanism cost* their accommodations must pay. Recovery-order asymmetry removes the simplest substrate-symmetric production account but leaves intact every account willing to add direction-specific mechanisms. A production theorist can accept everything here and maintain the position; what they cannot do is treat the patterns as a free lunch. The cumulative cost across the hierarchy, the dissociation architecture, terminal lucidity, the propofol/ketamine divergence, and the four direction-of-interface signatures is where the comparison comes due, not at any one case.

Third, the strongest single case (terminal lucidity) rests on the weakest evidence base — entirely retrospective, selection-biased, with no prospective recording during an episode; its asymmetry is real in the reports but its mechanism undetermined, and a dying-process substrate mechanism remains a live rival. The dissociative row is the cleanest accommodation case but also contested: DID's compartmentalised autonoetic access has been challenged as possibly socially mediated (Lynn et al. 2014), though even under the sociocognitive reading the compartmentalisation selectivity is real and is what the multi-channel reading expects — the contested point is the mechanism, not the selectivity. Assembling several direction-dependent signatures earns *framework-internal weight*, not a tier-upgrade: the convergence makes the Map's reading more articulated; it does not discriminate it from rivals absent an independent test only one reading predicts.

## Relation to Site Perspective

Three of the Map's tenets do work here.

**[[tenets#^dualism|Dualism]]** (Tenet 1) is engaged because both the vulnerability ordering and its direction-asymmetry fit a multi-channel interface architecture more directly than a substrate-emergent reading. The argument is comparative: the filter theory derives the ordering and the direction-sensitivity from architecture; rival readings absorb them by adding commitments. The dualist reading is not more parsimonious — interface architecture is not simple — but it pays its mechanism cost upfront, at the tenet level, rather than per-case at the accommodation level. It is one piece of the cumulative case running through the catalogue's [[clinical-dissociation-as-systematic-evidence|dissociation]], [[consciousness-disruption-and-the-mind-brain-interface|disruption]], and [[neurological-dissociations-as-interface-architecture|neurological-architecture]] articles.

**[[tenets#^minimal-quantum-interaction|Minimal Quantum Interaction]]** (Tenet 2) is engaged because the channel-bandwidth picture is what an MQI interface predicts: higher channels carry more information, require more interface engagement, and are more vulnerable to partial closure. The autonoetic channel is reconstruction-heavy — each episodic retrieval involves the selectional work the Map locates at the quantum-indeterminacy layer ([[reconsolidation-as-selection-window|reconsolidation selection window]]) — and its recovery-order asymmetry reflects *direction* of interface change rather than only substrate state, a pattern a thin-interface picture describes and a substrate-only picture cannot.

**[[tenets#^bidirectional-interaction|Bidirectional Interaction]]** (Tenet 3) is engaged because the channel that fails first and swings most in recovery order — the autonoetic channel — is the one involving the most active subject-participation: re-experiencing, reconsolidation-under-direction, lucid-dream metacognitive control, autobiographical assembly. If consciousness merely received from the brain, the most vulnerable channel would be the input-richest one and recovery order would be fixed by substrate readiness alone, with direction immaterial. That the autonoetic channel returns first under falling substrate function and last under rising favours a reading on which it is high-engagement in both directions.

Both patterns are faces of one picture: channels that fail independently, recover independently, degrade in a stable order, and recover in a direction-dependent one. No single case carries the argument; the convergence across [[consciousness-and-memory]], [[consciousness-disruption-and-the-mind-brain-interface]], and [[clinical-dissociation-as-systematic-evidence]] does.
