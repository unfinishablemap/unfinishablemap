The Unfinishable Map holds two route-families for how consciousness participates in quantum-level selection, and it holds them as live alternatives rather than rivals. The first, *forward-in-time conscious selection*, places the conscious act within standard forward causation: consciousness selects which outcome becomes actual as collapse occurs, now and not retroactively. The second, *time-symmetric selection*, locates the act in a transactional or retrocausal structure where the selection is not fixed at a moment but satisfies constraints across the whole temporal interval. Both routes are developed at length elsewhere—the forward route in [[forward-in-time-conscious-selection]], the time-symmetric route in [[time-symmetric-selection-mechanism]] and grounded in [[time-symmetric-physics]]. What no single article has yet done is set them side by side and read off, line by line, what each one *costs*. That is this article's only job. It is a cost accounting in the sense of [[mechanism-costs-cartography]], and it inherits that discipline's central constraint: **it does not pick a winner.**

This non-adjudication is deliberate, not evasive. The comparison operates at the *mechanism-cost-accounting register*, not the *empirical-establishment register* (see [[evidential-status-discipline]]). Nothing below should be read as evidence that either route is true, or that one defeats the other. Both routes are framework-internal developments of the Map's interactionist commitments; the cost accounting surfaces what each owes so a reader can see the bills clearly, then leaves the adjudication to the [[tenets]] and to evidence the Map does not currently have.

## The Comparison, Named Cleanly

The two route-families differ on a single axis: **whether conscious selection operates within standard forward causation or via a time-symmetric (transactional/retrocausal) structure.**

- *Forward-in-time selection* acts on superposed or just-decohered neural states as collapse occurs. Selection happens at a moment, in the forward direction. The Map's strongest version is [[post-decoherence-selection]]: consciousness selects which element of an already-decohered improper mixture becomes the single definite outcome.
- *Time-symmetric selection* treats the conscious act as participation in a transaction (Cramer 1986) or as the satisfaction of constraints between initial and final boundary conditions (the two-state vector formalism). The selection is not located at an instant; it constrains which correlations hold across the entire temporal sequence, including the "preparatory" neural activity that precedes conscious awareness.

The axis is clean, but the two routes share more than the headline suggests. Both are forms of consciousness *selecting among genuine indeterminacy*. Both reject many-worlds, where no selection occurs because every outcome is realised. Both require that the selection be a real act with counterfactual exclusion—*this* element actualised and not the others. This shared requirement is not stipulated here; it is the conclusion of the indexical-identity argument in [[many-worlds-argument]], which the comparison inherits rather than re-derives, and a reader who rejects that argument will reject the floor on which both routes stand. They diverge only on the temporal structure of the act, and that single divergence cascades into different metaphysical, physical, and empirical bills.

## What Each Requires Metaphysically

**Forward-in-time** needs *no retrocausal commitment*. It works within ordinary forward causation and is therefore compatible with presentism, with a growing-block ontology, and with the everyday sense that causes precede effects. This is where it pays least. What it inherits in exchange is the *standard collapse-mechanism problem*: it must say what converts the improper mixture into a definite outcome, and it cannot offload that question onto any feature of the formalism. Physics produces the mixture; on the forward reading, consciousness produces the outcome—and the gap between the two is occupied by a dualist commitment the physics does not compel.

**Time-symmetric** needs the *transactional interpretation* (Cramer 1986; Kastner 2012) or an equivalent time-symmetric framework such as the two-state vector formalism. This is a heavier metaphysical commitment: the transactional interpretation holds a minority position in quantum foundations—interpretation surveys consistently place it well behind the orthodox, many-worlds, and Bohmian readings—and taking its retrocausal implications as physically real (rather than as an interpretive option) is a further step. In exchange, the time-symmetric route *absorbs the Libet timing problem* rather than answering it. [[libet-experiments|Libet's readiness potentials]] precede conscious awareness by hundreds of milliseconds, which seems to make consciousness too late to cause the neural activity it supposedly controls. On the time-symmetric reading the objection appears to dissolve: if future boundary conditions constrain past states, consciousness need not temporally precede neural activity to influence it. The selection is atemporal, so "too late" loses its purchase—though this dissolution is only as secure as the retrocausal interpretation it rests on, which remains contested rather than established.

The asymmetry here is sharp and worth naming explicitly, in the manner the cost-cartography discipline requires: forward-in-time buys temporal becoming cheaply but pays full price on the collapse mechanism; time-symmetric buys a clean Libet answer but pays in retrocausal metaphysics.

## What Each Requires Physically

**Forward-in-time** needs a *selection mechanism at or after decoherence*. The pre-decoherence versions (Penrose-Hameroff Orch OR, Stapp's [[stapp-quantum-mind|quantum Zeno mechanism]], Chalmers-McQueen CSL) require neural superpositions to survive long enough for consciousness to act on them—exposing them to the *decoherence-gap problem*. Tegmark (2000) calculated neural decoherence times of 10⁻¹³ to 10⁻²⁰ seconds; even the favourable revised estimates (Hagan et al. 2002, whose more generous numbers remain unreplicated and have been contested by Reimers et al. 2009 and McKemmish et al. 2009) appear to leave a gap of several orders of magnitude against conscious-process timescales—though, as the anchor concept stresses, no experiment has directly measured coherence times in living neural tissue, so the size of the gap is itself contested rather than settled. The Map's preferred forward variant, post-decoherence selection, *sidesteps* the coherence-survival requirement by acting on the already-decohered mixture—but it still operates within forward time, and it still owes an account of how a quantum-scale bias amplifies to neural behaviour (the amplification chain catalogued in [[post-decoherence-selection-programme]]).

**Time-symmetric** needs *atemporal participation in transactions*. It does not require sustained neural coherence at all: as the transactional account has it, the transaction itself is atemporal, and decoherence merely participates by determining the available bases. The physical demand is not "maintain coherence" but "the selection of definite outcomes involves constraint-satisfaction across time." This is, in one sense, lighter—it asks nothing of warm-tissue coherence times. In another sense it may be heavier, because it asks the physical world to support genuine backward-directed constraint, a feature standard physics permits formally (the equations are time-symmetric) but which the dominant view in quantum foundations treats as a formal property of the equations rather than a causally real one.

## What Empirical Handles Each Makes Available

This section is where the evidential-status discipline does its hardest work: neither route is empirically established, and the "handles" below are *what each route would make detectable in principle*, not evidence either route is correct. It is worth stating the sharper point the anchor concept [[retrocausality]] already marks: the forward/time-symmetric choice is, on the evidence the Map currently has, **underdetermined by the data**. The two route-families are very nearly empirically equivalent at present—no extant experiment distinguishes them—so the cost accounting that follows could not, even in principle, be turned into a verdict by appeal to current measurements.

**Forward-in-time** predicts, in principle, *detectable selection at decoherence boundaries*. Because it locates the conscious act at a specific stage of the physical process—the conversion of a decohered mixture into an outcome—it makes concrete contact with the question of whether selection respects Born-rule statistics, operates within the pointer-state menu, and is confined to brain-generated candidate sets. The Born-rule constraint here is not an ad hoc add-on: the [[causal-consistency-constraint]] gives independent structural reason why any corridor-compliant coupling—physical or non-physical—must preserve Born statistics, so a forward selection that did *not* respect them would be the surprising claim, not the disciplined one. The [[selection-only-mind-influence|selection-only reading]] sharpens this into information-theoretic signatures (a per-event log₂(N) bit ceiling, a Born-rule-preserving rate). These are far from testable today, but they are the *kind* of handle a forward, in-process mechanism might make available: it lives close enough to the physics that it could, in principle, leave a statistical fingerprint—though whether any such fingerprint would survive the amplification chain to a measurable scale is itself unsettled.

**Time-symmetric** predicts *delayed-choice and Wheeler-type patterns*. The state-of-the-art handle is the extension of Wheeler's delayed-choice experiment to satellite distances by Vedovato et al. (2017), where measurement choices made after a photon's propagation determine its earlier wave-or-particle behaviour over thousands of kilometres. The Map's calibration on this is strict and must be preserved: these results are *interpretable without retrocausality, but they are also precisely what retrocausal interpretations predict*. They do not confirm the time-symmetric route; they are consistent with it. The handle the route makes available is the broad class of post-selection and delayed-choice phenomena—real, replicated physics whose retrocausal reading remains an interpretive choice rather than a forced conclusion.

## What Each Forecloses Philosophically

**Forward-in-time** *forecloses time-symmetric metaphysics*—if you commit to it as the whole story, you give up backward constraint as a real causal feature. What it *keeps* is temporal becoming: a real, asymmetric flow from settled past through actualising present toward open future. For a reader who finds presentism or the growing block independently compelling, this is a feature, not a cost.

**Time-symmetric** *forecloses presentism*. If the future participates in transactions, then the strict "only the present exists" ontology is unavailable—though the Map's [[time-symmetric-physics|modified growing block]] reconciliation softens this by routing the "future" through quantum *possibility* rather than ontologically existing future fact ("symmetry holds at the level of possibility; asymmetry emerges through collapse into actuality"). What time-symmetric *keeps* is the Libet-friendly atemporal selection: it never has to explain how a late-arriving consciousness reaches back to cause early neural events, because the selection was never located at an instant in the first place.

Each route, in other words, tends to foreclose roughly what the other keeps—though the either/or is a simplification, and intermediate readings blur the line. A time-symmetric structure read as merely formal (not causally real) can keep temporal becoming while still dissolving Libet through post-selection statistics, and the [[time-symmetric-physics|modified growing block]] is itself such a hybrid. This is one expression of why the Map holds both: they partition the available metaphysical commitments differently, the partition is porous at its edges, and it is not yet adjudicable.

## Where Each Stands on the Map's Mechanism-Costs Accounting

Applying the fixed-battery lens of [[mechanism-costs-cartography]]—the same question asked at every cell—the two routes can be read off against a common ledger (the [[mechanism-cost-ledger|running register]] tracks the Map's accumulating commitments over time). Both routes pay one cost in common, and each pays one extra.

**Shared cost: the decoherence-gap debt.** Both routes must account for the relationship between conscious selection and the decoherence that prepares classically distinguishable alternatives. Forward-in-time pays it by relocating the act to after decoherence (post-decoherence selection) or by requiring coherence survival (the pre-decoherence variants, which pay it more dearly). Time-symmetric pays it by treating decoherence as basis-fixing within an atemporal transaction. Neither route escapes the gap; they discharge the debt differently.

**Time-symmetric pays extra in retrocausal metaphysics.** Its bill includes the transactional interpretation's minority status in quantum foundations and the further step of reading time-symmetry as causally (not merely formally) real. This is a thicker metaphysical commitment than the forward route asks for.

**Forward-in-time pays extra in standard-collapse-mechanism debt.** It cannot offload the problem of outcomes onto a transactional structure. It owes a first-person account of what converts the improper mixture into a definite outcome, and the [[selection-criterion-problem|selection-criterion problem]] sits squarely on this route: if consciousness selects pattern A over B, the criterion is either reason-bearing (collapsing toward functionalism), random (collapsing toward bare Born statistics), or a non-reducible primitive. The forward route must hold that primitive in place; the time-symmetric route faces the same trilemma but routes it through constraint-satisfaction rather than instantaneous choice.

**Forward-in-time also owes the amplification debt.** A single-event selection that injects at most a log₂(N)-bit bias must somehow propagate to a macroscopic difference in neural behaviour, and the chain that does the propagating is not supplied here—it is catalogued in [[post-decoherence-selection-programme]] and remains, by the Map's own accounting, unpaid: there is no number bridging the quantum-scale bias and the neural-scale effect. This bill is heaviest for the forward route, whose act is located inside the physical process and so must show its work on the amplification path; the time-symmetric route inherits a softer version of the same debt, since constraint-satisfaction across the interval still has to issue in a determinate neural trajectory. Naming it as an open cost is the honest entry; the article does not discharge it.

The cartographic posture forbids reading "both pay the decoherence-gap cost, then one each pays extra" as a verdict. Whether retrocausal metaphysics is worth paying, or whether standard-collapse-mechanism debt is the lighter bill, is exactly the question the cartography preserves rather than answers.

## The Map's Posture: Live Alternatives, Not Rivals

The central discipline of this article is its refusal to adjudicate. The Map holds forward-in-time and time-symmetric selection as *genuine alternatives within its interactionist commitments*. Both satisfy the [[tenets]]; both are framework-internal developments; both face open costs the Map names honestly. The comparison clarifies what each route owes—and that clarification is the deliverable. Choosing between them would require evidence the Map does not have, or a metaphysical tie-breaker the tenets do not supply at this resolution.

Non-adjudication is not a ban on inference, and it is worth saying plainly what a reader is *entitled* to do with the ledger. The accounting fixes the bills so that a reader's own prior commitments can do the weighing: someone who holds presentism or temporal becoming on independent grounds will find the forward route cheaper *for them*; someone who weights a clean Libet answer or who already accepts retrocausal interpretations will find the time-symmetric route cheaper *for them*. What the Map declines to do is impose a tie-breaker of its own—to rule that one set of priors is the correct one. The conclusion is licensed; it just depends on priors the article deliberately leaves unfixed.

This posture is itself a calibration commitment. The temptation in any cost accounting is to let "route X pays less on the questions I chose to ask" slide into "route X is correct, full stop." The [[evidential-status-discipline]] forbids that *unconditional* slide: a lighter bill on a stipulated battery is not evidence of truth, and the battery itself (metaphysical thickness, physical demands, empirical handles, philosophical foreclosures) is one the Map selected. A reader who weights the questions differently will read the bills differently—and the Map invites exactly that reconfiguration rather than foreclosing it.

## Relation to Site Perspective

This comparison is most directly an exercise in three tenets.

**[[tenets#^minimal-quantum-interaction|Minimal Quantum Interaction]] (Tenet 2)** is the axis along which both routes are measured. The forward route's post-decoherence variant claims minimality by asking consciousness only to choose among pointer states the environment has already prepared, within Born statistics. The time-symmetric route claims minimality by asking consciousness only to satisfy constraints physics leaves underdetermined, injecting no energy and overriding no law. Both are bids for the smallest possible non-physical influence; the cost accounting shows they locate that smallness differently—one at a moment, one across an interval.

**[[tenets#^bidirectional-interaction|Bidirectional Interaction]] (Tenet 3)** is enacted by both routes and is the reason both survive as live alternatives. Each delivers genuine two-way traffic—the brain presents alternatives, consciousness selects among them—and each does so without collapsing into epiphenomenalism. The routes differ only in whether the mind-to-world leg runs forward or atemporally.

**[[tenets#^no-many-worlds|No Many Worlds]] (Tenet 4)** is what makes the comparison meaningful at all. Under many-worlds there is no selection to cost out, because every element of every mixture is realised. Both routes presuppose that selection is a real act with counterfactual exclusion—that *this* subject actualised *this* element and not the others. The rejection of many-worlds is the shared floor on which the forward/time-symmetric comparison is built; remove it and the comparison has nothing to compare.

The Map's settled position is that this article should *not* produce a settled position on the forward/time-symmetric question. The honest output is the ledger, held open.
