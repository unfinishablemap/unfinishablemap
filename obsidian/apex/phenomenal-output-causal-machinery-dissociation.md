Some cognitive operations leave their outputs in consciousness but hide the operations that produced them. Familiarity arrives without the recollection that should have supplied it; effort feels real without the executive engagement that should have generated it; cardinality is given as a unity without the parallel-indexing operation that registered it. The Unfinishable Map calls this the *phenomenal-output / causal-machinery dissociation cluster*: a structural pattern recurring across perception, cognition, agency, and memory, in which phenomenal access reaches the verdict but not the verdict-issuing process, and felt certainty becomes the explanandum rather than the evidence. The cluster is one of the catalogue's most developed worked instances of pressing materialism's "consciousness is just brain activity" toward a unified account of the symmetric two-sided shape the catalogue reveals.

This synthesis catalogues the cluster's members across three timescales — instant of perception, real-time cognition and agency, across-time memory — and tracks the convergence of independent traditions on the same diagnostic signature, with an audited re-count below: five-or-six traditions carry the convergence weight rather than the seven a previous draft credited. The methodological discipline: each dissociation *constrains* identity readings of phenomenology with its underlying operation; none on its own *establishes* dualism. What is offered as raising the evidential weight is the cumulative convergence, recovered through independent vocabularies, with the common-cause caveat (all the traditions analyse human cognition through human introspection) set out below.

The cluster has a positive companion. [[empirical-phenomena-mental-causation|Placebo and choking]] show the same architecture running in the other direction — phenomenology *coupling* to causal machinery in the success cases — with the symmetry constraining what any account must explain.

## The Diagnostic Signature

The cluster's signature is operational. *Phenomenal access reaches outputs not processes*: subjects have rich access to what the system has decided, recognised, recalled, or felt — and effectively no access to the operation that produced the verdict. *Phenomenal certainty is the explanandum, not the evidence*: the felt sense of pastness, familiarity, rightness, or knowing-as is the datum any account must explain, and cannot be turned around as evidence for the operation it reports on without circularity.

Three sub-features compose the signature.

**Output-while-operation-hidden.** Cardinality below four arrives all at once with the indexing invisible; a face is recognised as familiar without the matching trace becoming inspectable; mental effort fires without exposing the executive engagement it accompanies. Each output is given as such; each operation has no phenomenal character.

**Dissociable fallibility.** Components fail selectively without the system reporting the failure as a failure of mechanism. Déjà vu fires familiarity without recollection; jamais vu withholds familiarity for still-known material; cryptomnesia delivers content without source-tag; Capgras strips affective familiarity from intact perceptual identification; prosopagnosia gives the inverse. Selective failures expose the fusion without making the operation observable from inside.

**Mode-fragility under inspection.** Inspecting the operation either transforms it (the absorbed expert monitoring their swing chokes) or terminates the field of which it was a precondition (the meditator's contemplative bracketing suspends rather than reveals the field). The void admits *outputs-by-deformation* — evidence from breakdowns and edge cases — but no *inspection-while-operating*. The contemplative traditions challenge this sub-feature directly. Husserlian phenomenology, open-monitoring meditation in *vipassana* and Dzogchen pointing-out, certain *satipatthana* protocols, and the EASE clinical-phenomenology interview (Sass and Parnas, with inter-rater calibration data) each claim something close to inspection-while-operating. The cluster's provisional response is to rely on an operations-vs-content distinction: the contemplative reports may track *content normally suppressed*, which the cluster absorbs as outputs-by-deformation; whether they also track *operations normally hidden* is empirically open. Useful verifiability conditions are inter-rater agreement across trained observers, neural correlates tracking the reported operation rather than the represented output, and predictive contact with behaviour the introspector should not have access to under the opacity model. Mode-fragility is a working description of the ordinary case the contemplative traditions may revise in restricted conditions.

A successful cluster member detaches phenomenal output from operation along at least one axis; a candidate that does not (mere capacity limit, mere content-puzzle) belongs to a different family.

The convergence claim below depends on cluster members operating on *substrates with different representational profiles*, and this phrase needs analytic specification. Two substrates carry different representational profiles when they differ along at least two of three dimensions: *sub-personal vocabulary* (token-indices for FINST indexing; trace-strength for consolidation; cost-benefit balance for effort; fluency for noetic-feelings; source-tags for source-monitoring); *temporal granularity* (sub-second to decades across members); *neural substrate* (parietal indexing; medial-temporal-cortical consolidation; dopaminergic-prefrontal effort; default-mode source-monitoring). Members sharing all three (two declarative-memory verdicts on adjacent trial epochs) would count as same-profile and would not contribute independent convergence force.

## The Negative Side: Three Timescales

The dissociation appears across three nested timescales. The signatures match; the substrates do not.

### Instant of Perception

At the instant of perception, recognition delivers content with the act of recognising hidden behind it. The [[recognition-void|recognition void]] names the gap between perceiving and knowing-*as*, where consciousness must find meaning in what it already sees but cannot observe itself doing so. Bruce and Young's two-component model and the Capgras-prosopagnosia double dissociation expose recognition as a hidden compound of perceptual identification and affective familiarity bound together invisibly. The patient experiences "this person feels wrong" or "this face means nothing"; neither experiences "my familiarity module is offline." The failure is given as content, not as mechanism.

The [[memory-anomalies|memory anomalies cluster]] develops the same dissociation along three rotations of a single source-monitoring failure. Déjà vu is felt pastness without retrievable past — Cleary's gestalt-match paradigm shows configurational match producing felt premonition without future-knowledge. Jamais vu is felt alienness on still-known material; cryptomnesia is recollection without source-tag. What arrives in consciousness is the verdict (familiar / alien / mine); what stays hidden is the dual-process retrieval and source-monitoring machinery that produced it.

The [[the-quantitative-comprehension-void#the-cardinality-floor|cardinality floor of the quantitative comprehension void]] gives the diagnostic in its sharpest single-shot form. Three dots are *three*, immediately and as a unity, with no felt act of counting. The Parallel Individuation System (Trick & Pylyshyn 1994) does the work; the work has no phenomenal character. The transition from subitizing to counting at four–five items is itself opaque: there is no "almost subitizing five." The architectural cap manifests *as* the experience of cardinality rather than being inspectable from within it.

### Real Time

In real-time cognition and agency, the dissociation extends from perceptual to cognitive and volitional outputs. [[mental-effort|Mental effort]] is the cleanest worked case. Three converging lines detach the felt sense from the operation it ostensibly reports on. Naccache and colleagues (2005) report a patient with anterior cingulate damage who performed Stroop conflict trials with normal executive control yet reported no conscious feeling of mental effort. The Hagger preregistered ego-depletion replication (2016) collapsed the strength-resource model the felt sense had pointed toward. Inzlicht and Kurzban reframed felt effort as the output of opportunity-cost machinery whose inputs the introspector cannot access. The felt sense is real and does causal work in the autonomic chain; it is not a transparent window onto the underlying executive process.

The [[noetic-feelings-void|noetic-feelings void]] generalises across felt epistemic signals — knowing, rightness, familiarity, understanding, coherence, confidence. Koriat established that noetic feelings rest on inferential heuristics — cue-familiarity, accessibility — that operate implicitly. Thompson showed the feeling of rightness is driven by generation fluency rather than correctness, with manipulations of fluency (font legibility, rhyme, perceptual ease) shifting the felt verdict without changing the underlying reasoning. The verdicts gate further cognition — when to stop searching, when to trust, when to engage analytic thought — and the system cannot audit them from inside, because audit requires more feelings of the same kind.

The [[agency-void|agency void]] picks up the same dissociation in the felt sense of being the cause of one's actions. Howard and colleagues (2016) showed that physical and mental effort disrupt the implicit sense of agency even as the explicit sense often rises. Wegner's work establishes that the *experience* of willing arises from interpreting thoughts as causes on cues of priority, consistency, and exclusivity; the cues, not the willing, are what the introspector has access to — though Wegner's *interpretation* of his data inverts the cluster's preferred reading, requiring the closer accounting given below. The [[suspension-void|suspension void]] adds *refraining* from selection: the felt act of holding back has phenomenal character whose machinery is hidden the same way.

These four — felt-effort, felt-agency, felt-suspension, felt-knowing — sit as one functional family of sub-personally generated signals that gate distinct operations while sharing the same opacity pattern. [[self-opacity]]'s §Introspective Opacity *Process* dimension states the diagnostic in its most general form: consciousness accesses mental contents but not the processes that produce them. James's "spinning top," Nisbett and Wilson's confabulation experiments, Johansson's choice-blindness, Libet timing data, and Wegner's reconstructive account of willing all converge on the same observation, even where interpretive readings diverge. [[apex/introspection-architecture-void-cluster|The introspection-architecture void cluster]] sharpens the shape onto introspection specifically: five distinct mechanisms (narrative, source-attribution, noetic-feelings, confabulation, perceptual-reality-monitoring) by which the introspector's bounded access to its substrate delivers reconstructed outputs without delivering the operation that produced them — the same architectural fact under two cuts, with the noetic-feelings void at the intersection.

Wegner deserves closer accounting than the parenthetical above gives him. *The Illusion of Conscious Will* is a candidate elimination of the dissociation frame altogether, not a divergent interpretation of the same observations: on Wegner's reading, felt agency is generated post-hoc from priority, consistency, and exclusivity cues, with no underlying willing operation. The cluster's honest position is to cite Wegner as data and acknowledge the fork: his observations are compatible with either the dissociation reading or his confabulation reading, and the cluster lacks a knock-down argument internal to the agency literature alone for preferring the first. What constrains the choice is the positive companion. If the confabulation reading were globally correct, the symmetric coupling cases — placebo's content-specific physiological cascades, choking's specific interference profile — would have to be confabulations too, and the asymmetry between confabulating support and confabulating interference becomes hard to sustain. Wegner is a contested entry whose force is mediated by the positive companion rather than straightforward independent evidence.

### Across Time

[[consciousness-and-memory|Consciousness and memory]] adds the across-time face. Memory consolidation transforms the phenomenal character of a recall along a distinctive trajectory — vivid sensory re-experiencing in the hours after encoding, narrative gist over months, schematised semantic fact over decades — that does not track information loss point for point. The variable that tracks the qualitative shift most closely is hippocampal involvement; the propositional content can persist in cortical traces with the phenomenal dimension already hollowed out. Two further faces sharpen the case: encoding requires conscious attention while consolidation runs without phenomenal accompaniment, and reconsolidation reshapes the trace at each conscious retrieval — propositional content survives across cycles, phenomenal character is reconstituted at each recall.

The unifying observation across the three timescales: the phenomenal dimension of cognition operates semi-independently of its informational substrate. Different timescales, same dissociation signature.

## The Positive Side: When Phenomenology Couples to Causal Machinery

[[empirical-phenomena-mental-causation|Placebo and choking]] complete the picture from the opposite direction. Where the dissociation cluster shows phenomenology decoupling from causal machinery in the failure cases, the empirical phenomena of mental causation show phenomenology *coupling* to causal machinery in the success cases. The two-sided structure is consistent with an interface architecture — though equally with any selective-access account (higher-order theory, illusionism, global workspace theory) that grants phenomenal access to representational outputs but not to all sub-personal processes. The cluster's claim is cost-of-explanation rather than unique-prediction: each can absorb the shape; the question is what additional structure each must posit.

Placebo exhibits the positive direction with empirically contested but not eliminated magnitudes. The propositional content of a belief — "this is a painkiller," "this should suppress growth hormone," "this should release growth hormone" — selects which physiological cascade fires; the same compound produces opposite hormonal effects depending on the verbal information attached (Benedetti et al. 2003). Hróbjartsson and Gøtzsche's 2010 Cochrane meta-analysis substantially recalibrates the simple "placebo demonstrates" framing: placebos compared to no-treatment yielded small effects only for pain and continuous subjective outcomes, with no statistically significant effect on binary outcomes, and pain-literature effect sizes have been progressively revised downward under tightened preregistration norms. Benedetti's content-specificity findings remain striking but are not the consensus picture. Open-label placebos qualify the propositional-content claim further — patients who know the pills are inert still improve — relocating the work to felt engagement with a therapeutic ritual rather than dissolving it. The honest framing is that placebo exhibits content-specific phenomenology-to-physiology coupling in patterns whose magnitude and content-specificity are empirically contested but not eliminated; the two-sided structure survives the recalibration.

Choking demonstrates the converse. When consciousness intrudes on automatised skill, performance degrades precisely where self-focused monitoring is maximal. Beilock and Carr's explicit-monitoring theory tracks the mechanism: pressure → anxiety → self-focus → step-by-step monitoring → degraded execution. Negative evidence resists epiphenomenalist dismissal in a way positive evidence does not: confabulating interference is not available as a manoeuvre.

The conjunction registers *systematic* phenomenology-to-physiology coupling across substrates with different representational profiles — placebo's content-specificity selecting across humoral and dopaminergic pathways, choking's mode-specificity selecting across motor-cortical and basal-ganglia loops. Together with the negative side, they describe an interface that sometimes couples and sometimes detaches in patterned ways exposing the decomposition of what normally feels unified.

## Independent Traditions on the Same Architecture

The cluster's evidential weight rests on the convergence of independent traditions — working with different premises and vocabularies, recovering the same diagnostic signature — rather than on any single member.

**Carruthers (2011) on cognition.** *The Opacity of Mind* argues that introspection of propositional attitudes is interpretive rather than direct: we infer our attitudes from sensory-format evidence much as we infer others'. The feeling of authoritative self-knowledge is a fluency-driven heuristic, not a window onto its own production. Carruthers is listed here for completeness but counts as an *absorption* of the signature rather than a witness to it; the [[#strong-rival|strong-rival section]] below explains why his interpretive-sensory-access theory predicts the dissociation from materialist premises and concludes there is no such thing as conscious agency, which is the cluster's preferred reading running in reverse.

**Trick and Pylyshyn (1994) on perception.** The FINST model gives the cardinality floor's diagnostic sharpest: visual indexing operates on a small pool of pre-attentive tokens whose allocation is invisible. Subitizing produces a unitary phenomenal output; the indexing operation has no phenomenal accompaniment.

**Koriat (2000) on fluency.** Metacognitive feelings that gate cognition — knowing, rightness, tip-of-the-tongue, confidence — are outputs of inferential heuristics that operate implicitly. The "Lima problem" sharpens the recursive face: epistemic feelings report on content we cannot independently access, so we cannot cross-check their accuracy except through more feelings of the same kind.

**Polanyi (1966) on tacit integration.** *The Tacit Dimension*'s "we know more than we can tell" names the dissociation in the integrative-knowledge face. Skilled judgement and scientific intuition draw on subsidiary awareness whose contents do not become focal. Polanyi's "from-to" structure is a phenomenologist's vocabulary for output-while-operation-hidden.

**Inzlicht and Kurzban on effort.** The Kurzban-Duckworth-Kable-Myers (2013) opportunity-cost model and the Inzlicht-Schmeichel (2012) process model treat felt effort as the output of sub-personal cost-benefit machinery whose inputs the introspector cannot access. Westbrook and colleagues (2020) confirm one prediction: methylphenidate-induced dopamine elevation shifts willingness to engage demanding tasks, with the introspector noticing the result without noticing the manipulation.

**Bergson (1908) on memory.** "Le souvenir du présent et la fausse reconnaissance" proposes that memory and perception form simultaneously, with the memory-aspect of the present normally suppressed; déjà vu becomes the brief surfacing of a normally-occluded shadow. The reducing-valve picture names the architecture.

**Schwitzgebel on introspection.** *Perplexities of Consciousness* (2011) establishes that introspective reports of even basic phenomenal features (visual imagery, emotional experience, the felt character of perception) are unreliable in ways subjects cannot detect from inside. The felt textures of experience are reported through machinery whose operation is hidden.

These traditions do not share dualist commitments — Carruthers defends higher-order theory, Trick and Pylyshyn are cognitive scientists, Koriat is in metacognition, Polanyi a philosopher of science, Inzlicht and Kurzban evolutionary and cognitive psychology, Bergson a process philosopher, Schwitzgebel comfortable with reductive accounts. The convergence is on the *signature*, not on what it entails. Any single member admits domain-specific physicalist absorption (Carruthers's reading is functionalist, FINST is computational, Koriat's heuristics are sub-personal). A theory that absorbs each member separately must additionally explain why the same coupling — phenomenal verdict tracking sub-personal output, operation tracking nothing — appears across substrates with different representational profiles.

A scrupulous critic will press harder than the selection-effect worry, and the cluster should grant the harder point in full. "Access to outputs, not to the operations that produce them" is not merely what the cited authors went looking for; it is the *predicted, mainstream null* of standard cognitive architecture, established well before most of the cited authors wrote. Nisbett and Wilson's (1977) headline finding — subjects have "little or no direct introspective access to higher-order cognitive processes" — already states the signature, inside a thoroughly materialist programme. Fodor's (1983) modularity and Marr's (1982) levels-of-description predict by design that algorithmic-level processing is not introspectable while its products are; global workspace theory (Baars; Dehaene and Naccache) predicts that conscious access reaches integrated, broadcast representations and not the sub-personal modular processing that feeds them. On this reading the cluster's "convergence across independent traditions" is, for its descriptive core, convergence on a thesis that was consensus before the triangulation began — redescription of the standard architecture rather than evidence-lift against it. The cluster concedes this directly: as a claim about *which information reaches report*, the signature taxes no materialist account, and the tradition-count cannot be cashed as independent evidence for a distinct register. This is a framework-boundary matter, not an in-framework refutation, and is marked as such. What the modular/GWT prediction does not touch — and what the cluster's residual claim is confined to — is why the broadcast verdict is *felt* rather than merely globally available, together with the *patterned* attachment of felt-character to outputs and its inverted reappearance in the positive companion; the architectural null predicts the access structure and is silent on the phenomenal attachment, which is exactly the seam the [[#strong-rival|strong-rival section]] below isolates and the [[#the-diagnostic-signature|diagnostic signature]] names as the explanandum. The earlier draft's claim that the dominant absorption strategies (illusionism, higher-order theory, attention-schema, predictive processing) had "not yet been pressed" to address the architecture as a single shape is retired by that section, which assembles the strongest unified materialist reading and states precisely where it absorbs the signature and where the residue lies.

The traditions divide into sub-classes carrying different argumentative weight. Polanyi and parts of Koriat's programme observe the signature *despite* their broader commitments — Polanyi's subsidiary-focal structure does not require any opacity claim, Koriat tracks heuristic outputs without committing to why introspection is interpretive. Carruthers, Trick and Pylyshyn, and Inzlicht and Kurzban observe the signature *because* their materialist or computational models predict it; this sub-class supplies internal corroboration of the materialist prediction rather than independent evidence against it. Two cases need re-classification. Bergson's reducing-valve picture is a proposed metaphysical thesis that resembles what the cluster proposes; counting him as independent evidence would be double-counting, and he sits in the cluster's company as a forerunner of the framing rather than as a witness to it. Schwitzgebel is the more uncomfortable case: *Perplexities of Consciousness* argues introspection is unreliable at reporting basic phenomenal features as such, which sits in the *despite*-class register epistemically but cuts *against* the cluster's preferred reading — if introspection cannot reliably report on the felt character of perception, the cluster's "rich, often confident access to what the system has decided" premise from §"The Diagnostic Signature" is also unsafe. The honest re-count is five-or-six traditions, not seven, with Bergson as a parallel proposer rather than evidence and Schwitzgebel as a witness whose scepticism applies symmetrically to both sides of the cluster's frame.

The convergence's force also depends on the traditions analysing substrates that do not share architectural limits, and the article should be honest about how thin that condition currently is. A common-cause hypothesis — *human introspection has the same architectural limits across all domains; those limits ARE the common machinery* — would predict the same convergence the tradition-count credits to independent triangulation. All of the traditions analyse human cognition through human introspection in human language. An earlier draft offered cross-timescale recurrence within a single nervous system as the discriminating rejoinder, but the rejoinder confused *substrate independence* (cardinality and consolidation substrates differ) with *introspector independence* (the same human introspector reports on both); an introspector that is bandwidth-limited and reconstructive at every timescale would produce exactly the cross-timescale signature recurrence the article catalogues. What would discriminate is a same-introspector-different-signature counterexample — a domain in which a human introspector's report transparently tracks the operations, or a non-human or radically-different-architecture introspector reporting *absence* of the signature on a domain humans show it on. Neither is in hand. Cross-species, cross-cultural, and patient-population evidence — the classes [[common-cause-null|the common-cause-null discipline]] catalogues — would supply the discrimination; that evidence is owed. The honest current position is that the cluster's cross-substrate convergence is consistent both with the architectural reading the article develops and with the common-cause null the article cannot yet defeat.

The candidate same-introspector counterexamples are not exotic; several sit in ordinary experience, and a signature reverse-engineered from confirming cases owes them an explicit hearing. Four are worth naming. The *tip-of-the-tongue* state (Schwartz 1999; Cleary and Schwartz 2021) feels like the activity of a retrieval *process* in motion — straining, partial access, the sense of an imminent arrival — rather than a delivered verdict; if it is phenomenology of an operation, it cuts against output-while-operation-hidden. The *cognitive phenomenology* literature (Strawson 1994; Pitt 2004; Bayne and Montague 2011) argues that occurrent thinking has a proprietary felt character not exhausted by sensory imagery or verdict-feelings, which would be felt accompaniment of a cognitive operation. The *felt recall-versus-imagination mode distinction* — Tulving's (1985) autonoetic consciousness, the remember/know contrast — is a felt signal of *which retrieval mode* is running, arguably a window onto a process-level difference rather than a content output. And *expert skilled-judgment phenomenology* sometimes includes a reported felt sense of how a judgement is being formed, not only its result. The cluster's response is the operations-versus-content distinction it already relies on for the contemplative challenge to mode-fragility: in each case what is plausibly felt is a *further verdict-like output* — a meta-feeling of retrieval-imminence, a fluency-and-precision signal tagging the thinking, a mode-tag on the recall, a confidence reading on the judgement — rather than transparent access to the message-passing that produces it. That response absorbs tip-of-the-tongue and the mode-tag cases cleanly enough, since each can be redescribed as one more opaque-process-producing-a-felt-output. It is weakest against cognitive phenomenology of thought, where the claim is precisely that the felt character is *of the thinking* and not a separable tag on it; here the cluster concedes that if Strawson and Pitt are right that occurrent thought carries proprietary process-phenomenology, the output-while-operation-hidden signature has a genuine same-introspector exception, and the cluster's scope narrows to the domains where the operations-versus-content distinction holds. The honest position is partial absorption with one open concession, not a clean sweep.

## The Strongest Materialist Reading {#strong-rival}

The convergence argument is only as strong as the best unified rival it can survive. Naming higher-order theory, illusionism, and global workspace theory separately understates the threat: a materialist need not absorb each cluster member in isolation but can offer one architecture that predicts the whole signature — output-while-operation-hidden, dissociable fallibility, mode-fragility, and the positive companion — as a structural consequence. The honest position is to build that architecture here and then say exactly what it leaves unexplained.

**The unified rival.** The strongest current reading is *predictive-processing introspection coupled to a reality-monitoring access mechanism*. On the predictive-processing side, Hohwy (2013) and Clark (2016) treat the mind as a hierarchical generative model whose higher layers infer the causes of lower-layer activity; introspection is not a dedicated detector reading off operations but a further layer of the same inferential machinery. Schwengerer (2019) develops Hohwy's remarks into an explicit transparency account on which self-knowledge is a built-in part of predictive processing rather than a separate faculty, and Sandved-Smith and colleagues (2021) give the formal version — a deep parametric active-inference model in which meta-awareness and attentional control are themselves modelled states, with the introspective layer estimating a posterior over lower-level attentional dynamics. This architecture predicts output-while-operation-hidden directly: an introspective layer that estimates a *posterior* over lower-level states has access to the estimate, not to the message-passing that produced it, exactly as the cluster reports. Carruthers' (2011) interpretive-sensory-access theory supplies the same prediction from a different direction — introspection is interpretation over sensory-format evidence, so the operation is constitutively unavailable — and his conclusion that there is "no such thing as conscious agency" is the cluster's preferred reading run in reverse: the felt agency the [[agency-void|agency void]] catalogues is, for Carruthers, a confabulated interpretation with no underlying willing to be hidden. On the access side, Lau's (2022) perceptual-reality-monitoring theory has a prefrontal discriminator sorting internally-generated from externally-driven sensory signals; what reaches consciousness is the discriminator's verdict, not its operation. Block's (1995) access/phenomenal distinction frames the whole move, and Naccache's (2018) argument that a strict access-consciousness theory can account for the appearance of phenomenal overflow is the materialist's claim that the access half is the whole story. Assembled, these give a single rival: a hierarchical predictive system whose introspective and reality-monitoring layers deliver reconstructed verdicts about lower-level states while the message-passing stays sub-personal, with felt certainty as a fluency-and-precision signal generated by the same layers.

This rival predicts the cluster's three sub-features without strain. *Output-while-operation-hidden* follows from the posterior-estimate structure. *Dissociable fallibility* follows because separate generative sub-models can be lesioned or mis-tuned independently — Capgras as a precision failure on affective prediction, déjà vu as a spurious familiarity posterior. *Mode-fragility* follows because directing the introspective layer at an operation changes the precision weighting on that operation, altering it (the choking case) or collapsing the task set (the meditation case). The positive companion is no harder: placebo is high-precision prior content propagating down the hierarchy to autonomic effectors, choking is misallocated precision degrading a well-tuned motor model. The rival is genuinely unified, and the catalogue must grant that it absorbs the *shape*.

**Where the rival is defective on its own terms.** Two of the cluster's members resist the access-only reading from inside the rival's own commitments. The first is the [[empirical-phenomena-mental-causation|positive companion's negative case]]: choking is content-*interference*, and the rival owes an account of why precision misallocation produces the specific degradation profile that explicit-monitoring theory documents, when the access-only story predicts only that monitored representations become reportable, not that monitoring *disrupts* a motor model that was already running correctly. Confabulating interference is not a move the rival has — it can confabulate a report of effort, but it cannot confabulate the measurable performance drop. The second is Naccache's own anterior-cingulate patient (Naccache et al. 2005), who shows *intact* executive control with *absent* felt effort: a predictive-processing introspective layer estimating a posterior over executive dynamics should still produce *some* posterior when the executive operation runs normally, yet the felt-effort output is gone while the operation is preserved. That is a clean dissociation between the operation and its putative posterior estimate, and the rival owes a reason why the estimating layer falls silent while the estimated process continues. Neither point refutes predictive processing; both identify places where the access-only reading has not yet paid a bill its own framework issues.

**Where the disagreement is closer to bedrock.** The deeper residue is one the rival's strongest proponents concede. Lau states that perceptual reality monitoring answers the *consciousness question* — why some states are conscious and reportable rather than others — and explicitly not why conscious states *feel the way they do*. The predictive-processing introspection models inherit the same boundary: modelling meta-awareness as a generative-model layer specifies the functional role of the estimate without specifying why the estimate is *phenomenally felt* rather than merely functionally available for control. This is where the rival helps itself to a foundational move it has not earned by its own standards — the identification of "high-precision posterior available for global control" with "felt certainty" is asserted, not derived, and it is the very identification the cluster's diagnostic signature makes the explanandum. The honest framing is that this is a framework-boundary disagreement, not an in-framework refutation: predictive-processing introspection is a complete and elegant account of the *access* structure the cluster documents, and the Map's claim is not that it fails on its own terms but that it leaves untouched the felt-character question its own architects bracket. The cluster's residual force against the unified rival is therefore narrow and statable: the rival explains why operations are hidden and verdicts available, and does not explain why the available verdict is *experienced* — which is the half of the signature the [[#the-diagnostic-signature|diagnostic signature]] names as the explanandum rather than the evidence.

**Consequence for the convergence count.** Building the unified rival forces one correction to the tradition count above. Carruthers cannot be both a convergent witness and an exponent of the strongest absorption; his interpretive-sensory-access theory predicts the signature *because* it is the materialist reading the cluster is testing itself against, so he belongs with the absorption architecture, not in the independent-triangulation column. With Carruthers reassigned, the convergence's independent weight rests on the *despite*-class members (Polanyi, parts of Koriat) whose observations do not flow from a model built to predict opacity — a smaller but cleaner base than the raw count suggested.

**Whether "explanatory cost" is the hard problem in new clothing.** The residue just named — verdict available, felt-character unexplained — invites the charge that the cluster is re-deriving Levine's (1983) explanatory gap and Chalmers' (1995) hard problem and presenting a familiar feature of materialism as new empirical pressure. The charge is partly right and the honest response concedes it. The felt-character seam *is* the explanatory gap localised; the cluster does not claim to have discovered a new gap. What it claims to add beyond the gap is structural, and the addition is statable in two parts. First, the gap normally appears as a single global puzzle ("why is there something it is like to be in any physical state at all"); the cluster relocates it to a *patterned* boundary — felt-character attaches to verdicts and not to the operations that produce them, with the same boundary recurring across substrates of different representational profile and reappearing inverted in the positive companion. The claim is that the gap has a *shape* the bare conceivability and knowledge arguments do not predict: not "experience is unexplained" but "experience attaches to outputs, detaches from operations, and couples back in the success cases." Second, the positive companion supplies a constraint the static hard problem lacks — placebo's content-specific coupling and choking's interference profile are behavioural facts an account must fit, not merely intuitions about conceivability. If the reader judges that this added structure collapses back into the gap once examined, the cluster's evidential ambition reduces to the hard problem restated, and the safer constrain-vs-establish reading below is the one to keep. The article does not insist the structure is irreducible to the gap; it claims the pattern and the positive-companion constraint are the additional content, and flags honestly that the weight of that content is itself contestable.

## The Constrain-vs-Establish Discipline

[[evidential-status-discipline|Evidential-status discipline]] names the cluster's load-bearing methodological commitment: the dissociations *constrain* identity readings on which felt phenomenology reports transparently on its own underlying operation; they do not by themselves *establish* the dualist alternative.

The shape is symmetric. The felt-effort/operation dissociation removes the simplest "felt effort just is what the operation feels like" identity reading but leaves what felt effort *is* underdetermined between dualist (the felt sense doing real causal work) and process-model (sub-personally generated adaptive signal). Physical correlates for any phenomenal output — the temporal-lobe-epilepsy correlate of déjà vu, dopamine modulation of effort, neural signatures of recognition — remove the simplest "phenomenology is uncoupled from physics" reading without establishing identity. Each side's evidence is defeater-removal-against-the-other, not evidence-lift in its own favour. The diagnostic test: when an article cites a dissociation, does the citation rest on removing a defeater the opposing view needs, or on establishing the citing view? The first is honest; the second is conflation.

Each dissociation the cluster catalogues runs a four-level chain that must not be collapsed: *separability* (the data show the features come apart); *phenomenological reality* (one of the dissociated features is a genuine feature of experience rather than report-artefact); *mechanistic independence* (distinct processes underlie the two features); *metaphysical irreducibility* (no mechanism of the kind in level three could account for the feature in level two). Capgras, blindsight, the Naccache lesion, and the Bruce-Young double dissociation establish level one robustly and contribute to level two; they underdetermine level three (functional accounts can supply mechanistic independence within a single substrate) and do not by themselves reach level four. The cluster claims load-bearing weight only at the levels its evidence actually delivers; the higher levels are reached, if at all, by the cumulative convergence below, not by any single dissociation. Evidence-lift comes from convergence: same signature, independent vocabularies, substrates within a single nervous system, both directions of behaviour. Worked example: the mental-effort article's citation of the Naccache patient does defeater-removal against the strong identity claim locally; read cumulatively, it contributes one node to a convergence pressuring any account on which phenomenal verdicts transparently report on their operations.

## What the Cluster Establishes (and Doesn't)

The cluster establishes that phenomenal access systematically detaches from the operations it putatively reports on, in patterned ways, across substrates within a single nervous system with different representational profiles and timescales. The same dissociation is observed across five-or-six traditions on the audited count, with the convergence's force concentrated in the sub-class that observes the signature *despite* its broader commitments. The architecture has a positive companion: phenomenology *can* couple to causal machinery (placebo, choking), so the negative cases are a structural feature of an interface that sometimes couples and sometimes does not.

The cluster does not establish the dualist alternative. A sophisticated functionalist can absorb each member as functional architecture with privileged-access asymmetries, and the [[#strong-rival|strong-rival section]] grants that predictive-processing introspection coupled to reality-monitoring access absorbs the whole shape — output-while-operation-hidden, dissociable fallibility, mode-fragility, and the positive companion — as a structural consequence. The convergence does not refute physicalism from inside its framework. What survives the unified rival is narrow and statable: the rival explains why the operations are hidden and the verdicts available, and on its own architects' admission does not explain why the available verdict is *experienced* rather than merely available for control — the felt-character half of the signature. The cluster raises the cost of physicalist absorption at exactly that seam and supplies a structural picture the dualist tenets accommodate naturally, without claiming to have closed the seam from inside the rival's framework.

## What Would Falsify the Cluster

A cluster whose evidence comes from convergence across heterogeneous substrates risks being indistinguishable from a category whose members do not share a real signature. Three candidates would weaken the framing.

**Clean lesion-dissociation of the four real-time members.** If targeted lesions or pharmacological dissociations could cleanly separate felt effort, felt agency, felt suspension, and felt knowing — each on independent substrates with no shared opacity profile — the convergence claim would weaken to four independent functions rather than a unified signature.

**Demonstrated transparent introspection of any one operation.** If contemplative training, neural intervention, or a verifiable phenomenological method produced stable, articulable access to the operation underlying any cluster member (the matching trace producing recognition, the executive engagement producing effort, the source-monitoring tag of a memory's source-attribution), the output-while-operation-hidden signature would lose its structural status. §"The Diagnostic Signature" engages the contemplative-traditions challenge to mode-fragility above; the falsification claim here is the structural one — were the contemplative case for inspection-while-operating to land with operations-not-content rigour and clear inter-rater agreement, the cluster's framing would owe substantial revision rather than the current accommodation.

**Symmetric absence of the positive companion.** If placebo and choking turned out to be artefacts and the positive coupling cases vanished, the two-sided structure would collapse into a one-sided opacity claim and the interface-architecture reading would lose its predicted symmetry.

None is currently observed; that is what makes the framework usable. None is in principle excluded; that is what makes it falsifiable rather than merely descriptive.

## Relation to Site Perspective

The cluster bears most directly on two of the [[tenets|five tenets]].

**[[tenets#^dualism|Dualism]]** is made more live — less easily dismissed — by the cluster's structural profile, rather than positively supported by it. Materialism must explain why phenomenal output detaches systematically from the operations it putatively reports on, why the detachment has the same diagnostic signature across substrates with different representational profiles within a single nervous system, and why the architecture has a positive companion in which phenomenology couples to causal machinery in matching detail. The dualist reading takes the dissociation as *compatible with* phenomenal access being a distinct ontological register from the underlying operations, and the coupling as *compatible with* the register doing causal work when the interface is engaged. Compatibility is not support, and the language here is calibrated to that distinction: the cumulative convergence pressures transparent-identity and simple-report models and keeps the distinct-register reading on the table, without by itself supplying positive evidence for it. The evidence the Map cites for the Dualism tenet is the *cumulative pattern across all topic clusters*, of which this is one of the most developed worked instances — not the dissociation taken as a direct argument for the distinct-register conclusion.

**[[tenets#^occams-limits|Occam's Razor Has Limits]]** is engaged because the simplicity-preference that motivates "consciousness is just brain activity" is itself one of the cluster's members. Trust in parsimony is a fluency-and-coherence signal, gated by the same noetic-feelings machinery the cluster catalogues. The materialist's confidence that the simpler story is the right one cannot be neutrally adjudicated, because the adjudicator is the architecture whose reliability is at issue. The recursion turns symmetrically against the Map's own use of "explanatory cost" as a discriminating consideration — the dualist's confidence that the framework-with-more-explanatory-room is the right one runs the same noetic-feelings machinery the materialist's confidence runs. The Map's commitment is therefore not to *win* at the gate by appealing to explanatory cost as if such judgements were exempt from the architecture, but to *sustain inquiry past* the gate set by the felt sufficiency of any simpler account — the materialist's, the dualist's, or the cluster's own.

The other three tenets connect more cautiously. **[[tenets#^bidirectional-interaction|Bidirectional Interaction]]** finds its empirical anchor in the positive companion (placebo and choking); the positive side names where the interface's causal work is most directly evident. **[[tenets#^minimal-quantum-interaction|Minimal Quantum Interaction]]** is consistent with the cluster's interface-asymmetry profile but the cluster does not by itself argue for any particular mechanism; the [[type-token-causation|type-token distinction]] specifies how mental causation can be both real and statistically invisible, which the cluster's two-sided structure assumes without itself defending. **[[tenets#^no-many-worlds|No Many Worlds]]** is implicitly engaged whenever the analysis invokes "selection" or "felt collapse," but the cluster does not adjudicate single-world versus branching readings.

The unifying picture: phenomenal output and causal machinery are dissociable across timescales, substrates, and traditions; they couple in the success cases of mental causation and detach in patterned ways in the failure cases. The cluster marks where reductive accounts owe an explanation beyond functional decomposition — not why each individual dissociation occurs, but why the architecture takes its symmetric two-sided form, with the convergence holding subject to the common-cause null the article does not yet defeat.
