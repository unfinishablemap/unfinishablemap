Some cognitive operations leave their outputs in consciousness but hide the operations that produced them. Familiarity arrives without the recollection that should have supplied it; effort feels real without the executive engagement that should have generated it; cardinality is given as a unity without access to whatever enumeration process registered it. The Unfinishable Map calls this the *phenomenal-output / causal-machinery dissociation cluster*: a structural pattern recurring across perception, cognition, agency, and memory, in which phenomenal access reaches the verdict but not the verdict-issuing process, and felt certainty becomes the explanandum rather than the evidence. The cluster is one of the catalogue's most developed worked instances of pressing materialism's "consciousness is just brain activity" toward a unified account of the symmetric two-sided shape the catalogue reveals.

This synthesis catalogues the cluster's members across three timescales — instant of perception, real-time cognition and agency, across-time memory — and tracks the convergence of independent traditions on the same diagnostic signature (five-or-six traditions on the audited count below). The methodological discipline: each dissociation *constrains* identity readings of phenomenology with its underlying operation; none on its own *establishes* dualism. Evidential weight comes from the cumulative convergence through independent vocabularies, subject to the common-cause caveat (all the traditions analyse human cognition through human introspection) set out below.

The cluster has a positive companion. [[empirical-phenomena-mental-causation|Placebo and choking]] show the same architecture running in the other direction — phenomenology *coupling* to causal machinery in the success cases — with the symmetry constraining what any account must explain.

## The Diagnostic Signature

The cluster's signature is operational. *Phenomenal access reaches outputs not processes*: subjects have rich access to what the system has decided, recognised, recalled, or felt — and effectively no access to the operation that produced the verdict. *Phenomenal certainty is the explanandum, not the evidence*: the felt sense of pastness, familiarity, rightness, or knowing-as is the datum any account must explain, and cannot be turned around as evidence for the operation it reports on without circularity.

Three sub-features compose the signature.

**Output-while-operation-hidden.** Cardinality below four arrives all at once with the enumeration invisible; a face is recognised as familiar without the matching trace becoming inspectable; mental effort fires without exposing the executive engagement it accompanies. Each output is given as such; each operation has no phenomenal character.

**Dissociable fallibility.** Components fail selectively without the system reporting the failure as a failure of mechanism. Déjà vu fires familiarity without recollection; jamais vu withholds familiarity for still-known material; cryptomnesia delivers content without source-tag; Capgras strips affective familiarity from intact perceptual identification; prosopagnosia gives the inverse. Selective failures expose the fusion without making the operation observable from inside.

**Mode-fragility under inspection.** Inspecting the operation either transforms it (the absorbed expert monitoring their swing chokes) or terminates the field of which it was a precondition (the meditator's contemplative bracketing suspends rather than reveals the field). The void admits *outputs-by-deformation* — evidence from breakdowns and edge cases — but no *inspection-while-operating*. The contemplative traditions challenge this sub-feature directly: Husserlian phenomenology, open-monitoring meditation, and the EASE clinical-phenomenology interview (Sass and Parnas) each claim something close to inspection-while-operating. The cluster's provisional response relies on an operations-vs-content distinction: the contemplative reports may track *content normally suppressed*, which the cluster absorbs as outputs-by-deformation; whether they also track *operations normally hidden* is empirically open, with inter-rater agreement and operation-tracking neural correlates as the verifiability conditions. Mode-fragility is thus a working description of the ordinary case, one the contemplative traditions may revise in restricted conditions.

A successful cluster member detaches phenomenal output from operation along at least one axis; a candidate that does not (mere capacity limit, mere content-puzzle) belongs to a different family.

The convergence claim depends on members operating on *substrates with different representational profiles*: two substrates differ when they diverge along at least two of three dimensions — *sub-personal vocabulary* (token-indices, trace-strength, cost-benefit balance, fluency, source-tags), *temporal granularity* (sub-second to decades), and *neural substrate* (parietal, medial-temporal-cortical, dopaminergic-prefrontal, default-mode). Members sharing all three (two declarative-memory verdicts on adjacent epochs) would be same-profile and contribute no independent convergence force.

## The Negative Side: Three Timescales

The dissociation appears across three nested timescales. The signatures match; the substrates do not.

### Instant of Perception {#instant-of-perception}

At the instant of perception, recognition delivers content with the act of recognising hidden behind it. The [[recognition-void|recognition void]] names the gap between perceiving and knowing-*as*, where consciousness must find meaning in what it already sees but cannot observe itself doing so. Bruce and Young's two-component model and the Capgras-prosopagnosia double dissociation expose recognition as a hidden compound of perceptual identification and affective familiarity bound together invisibly. The patient experiences "this person feels wrong" or "this face means nothing"; neither experiences "my familiarity module is offline." The failure is given as content, not as mechanism.

The [[memory-anomalies|memory anomalies cluster]] develops the same dissociation along three rotations of a single source-monitoring failure: déjà vu (felt pastness without retrievable past), jamais vu (felt alienness on still-known material), cryptomnesia (recollection without source-tag). What arrives in consciousness is the verdict (familiar / alien / mine); what stays hidden is the machinery that produced it. The dissociation does not ride on the familiarity/recollection dual-process architecture being settled — and it is not: the active 2020s literature (Wixted 2007; Wixted and Mickes 2010; Pratte and Rouder 2012) treats the two as read-out points on one memory-strength variable, more parsimoniously fit by a single process. As [[memory-anomalies#dual-process|the source article flags]], the gap re-derives architecture-independently: the boundary between the signal that *produces* felt pastness and the signal that would *track* an actual past survives however recognition decomposes.

Cleary's larger programme complicates the clean reading. Cleary and Claxton (2018) found déjà vu produces an increased *feeling* of knowing the next turn in a navigational analogue, yet predictions stayed at chance — an illusory feeling, not foresight; Cleary et al. (2019) document a companion postdictive bias. These are mildly unfriendly to the clean "verdict is the explanandum" reading: the architecture generates verdicts that are not merely opaque about production but *systematically wrong about content*, which a constructed-attribution account absorbs more naturally than an interface-to-a-distinct-register reading. The cluster concedes that these felt verdicts can be content-false — so their felt certainty cannot be leaned on as evidence about what produced them. That concession is the constrain-vs-establish discipline operating as it should.

The [[the-quantitative-comprehension-void#the-cardinality-floor|cardinality floor of the quantitative comprehension void]] gives the diagnostic in its sharpest single-shot form. Three dots are *three*, immediately and as a unity, with no felt act of counting; the enumeration that produced the verdict has no phenomenal character. The mechanism is contested — Trick and Pylyshyn (1994) proposed pre-attentive visual indexing, Liu et al. (2020) argue subitizing does *not* process the set in parallel — and the dissociation is mechanism-neutral: whatever the operation is, the introspector has access to the count, not the counting. The transition is graded at the four-to-five boundary (Piazza et al. 2011), so the opacity is of the operation, not a sharp phenomenal cliff. This structure is, for its descriptive core, exactly what standard modular architecture (Fodor 1983; Marr 1982) predicts; the load-bearing residue is isolated in the [[#strong-rival|strong-rival section]] below, not in the access structure itself.

### Real Time {#real-time}

In real-time cognition and agency, the dissociation extends from perceptual to cognitive and volitional outputs. [[mental-effort|Mental effort]] is the cleanest worked case, though each supporting line is more fragile than a quick citation implies. Naccache et al. (2005) report a patient with a large left mesio-frontal lesion (including anterior cingulate) who performed Stroop conflict trials with preserved executive control yet reported no felt mental effort and showed absent skin-conductance responses — a suggestive single un-replicated case, not a clean focal-ACC dissociation (the patient also showed Iowa Gambling Task deficits, and the ACC-lesion literature is mixed on the cingulate's necessity; Fellows & Farah 2005; Cipolotti et al. 2016). The ego-depletion replications recalibrate the strength-resource model without abolishing the effect: preregistered multilab tests found near-null effects (Hagger et al. 2016, d = 0.04; Vohs et al. 2021, d = 0.06, Bayes factor ~4:1 for the null) while a different task pairing found a small significant effect (Dang et al. 2021, d = 0.10). The honest reading is that the simple resource model is unsupported while a small effect under some pairings is not excluded. Inzlicht and Kurzban reframe felt effort as the output of opportunity-cost machinery whose inputs the introspector cannot access. The felt sense is real and does causal work in the autonomic chain, but is not a transparent window onto the executive process.

The [[noetic-feelings-void|noetic-feelings void]] generalises across felt epistemic signals — knowing, rightness, familiarity, understanding, coherence, confidence. Koriat established that noetic feelings rest on inferential heuristics — cue-familiarity, accessibility — that operate implicitly. Thompson showed the feeling of rightness is driven by generation fluency rather than correctness, with manipulations of fluency (font legibility, rhyme, perceptual ease) shifting the felt verdict without changing the underlying reasoning. The verdicts gate further cognition — when to stop searching, when to trust, when to engage analytic thought — and the system cannot audit them from inside, because audit requires more feelings of the same kind.

The [[agency-void|agency void]] picks up the same dissociation in the felt sense of causing one's actions — though "sense of agency" is not one construct. The 2022 *Nature Reviews Psychology* review (Wen & Imamizu) distinguishes an *implicit* level (pre-reflective sensorimotor signals; intentional binding, sensory attenuation) from an *explicit* level (reflective judgment), which can move in opposite directions. The output-while-operation-hidden claim is sharpest for the implicit feeling: Howard et al. (2016) showed effort disrupts the implicit sense of agency even as the explicit judgment rises. But the implicit machinery is not uniformly opaque — intentional binding is modulated by learning, postdiction, and the mere presence of an action (Seghezzi et al. 2025; Desantis et al. 2012), so the verdict is opaque about its production while some of its *inputs* are not — the agency case's local version of the caveat the cluster grants for recollection and tip-of-the-tongue below. Wegner's work establishes that the *experience* of willing arises from interpreting thoughts as causes on cues of priority, consistency, and exclusivity — the cues, not the willing, are what the introspector accesses, though his interpretation inverts the cluster's reading (accounted for below). The [[suspension-void|suspension void]] adds *refraining* from selection: the felt act of holding back has phenomenal character whose machinery is hidden the same way.

These four — felt-effort, felt-agency, felt-suspension, felt-knowing — sit as one functional family of sub-personally generated signals that gate distinct operations while sharing the same opacity pattern. [[self-opacity]]'s §Introspective Opacity *Process* dimension states the diagnostic generally: consciousness accesses mental contents but not the processes producing them — James's "spinning top," Nisbett and Wilson's confabulation, Johansson's choice-blindness, Libet timing, and Wegner's reconstructive willing all converging. [[apex/introspection-architecture-void-cluster|The introspection-architecture void cluster]] sharpens the shape onto introspection: five mechanisms (narrative, source-attribution, noetic-feelings, confabulation, perceptual-reality-monitoring) delivering reconstructed outputs without the operation — the same architectural fact under two cuts, with the noetic-feelings void at the intersection.

Wegner deserves closer accounting: *The Illusion of Conscious Will* is a candidate elimination of the dissociation frame, not a divergent interpretation — on his reading felt agency is generated post-hoc with no underlying willing. His observations are compatible with either reading, and the cluster lacks a knock-down argument internal to the agency literature for preferring the dissociation. What constrains the choice is the positive companion: if the confabulation reading were globally correct, placebo's content-specific cascades and choking's interference profile would have to be confabulations too, and the asymmetry between confabulating support and confabulating interference is hard to sustain. Wegner's force is mediated by the positive companion rather than independent evidence.

### Across Time

[[consciousness-and-memory|Consciousness and memory]] adds the across-time face. Consolidation transforms a recall's phenomenal character along a distinctive trajectory — vivid sensory re-experiencing in the hours after encoding, narrative gist over months, schematised semantic fact over decades — that does not track information loss point for point; hippocampal involvement tracks the qualitative shift most closely, while propositional content persists in cortical traces with the phenomenal dimension hollowed out. Encoding requires conscious attention while consolidation runs without phenomenal accompaniment, and reconsolidation reshapes the trace at each retrieval — content surviving across cycles, phenomenal character reconstituted at each recall.

The unifying observation across the three timescales: the phenomenal dimension of cognition operates semi-independently of its informational substrate. Different timescales, same dissociation signature.

## The Positive Side: When Phenomenology Couples to Causal Machinery

[[empirical-phenomena-mental-causation|Placebo and choking]] complete the picture from the opposite direction. Where the dissociation cluster shows phenomenology decoupling from causal machinery in the failure cases, the empirical phenomena of mental causation show phenomenology *coupling* to causal machinery in the success cases. The two-sided structure is consistent with an interface architecture — though equally with any selective-access account (higher-order theory, illusionism, global workspace theory) that grants phenomenal access to representational outputs but not to all sub-personal processes. The cluster's claim is cost-of-explanation rather than unique-prediction: each can absorb the shape; the question is what additional structure each must posit.

Placebo exhibits the positive direction with empirically contested but not eliminated magnitudes. The propositional content of a belief — "this is a painkiller," "this should worsen performance" — selects which physiological cascade fires; Benedetti et al. (2003) found verbally induced expectations could override a prior conditioning procedure on analgesic and motor channels (though not on hormonal ones, where conditioning dominated). Two recalibrations weaken the simple framing without dissolving it. Hróbjartsson and Gøtzsche's 2010 Cochrane meta-analysis found placebo effects only for pain and continuous subjective outcomes, none for binary outcomes. And a 2025 meta-analysis of 60 open-label-placebo trials (Fendel et al., *Scientific Reports*) finds a small overall effect (SMD ≈ 0.35) far larger for self-reported (≈ 0.39) than objective outcomes (≈ 0.09) — a gradient shifting weight from "conscious content selects a specific cascade" toward ritual, expectation, and felt engagement. Benedetti's non-open-label verbal-expectation effects remain the strongest content-specific case. The honest framing: placebo exhibits phenomenology-to-physiology coupling whose magnitude and content-specificity are contested but not eliminated; the two-sided structure survives in weakened form.

Choking demonstrates the converse. When consciousness intrudes on automatised skill, performance degrades precisely where self-focused monitoring is maximal (Beilock and Carr's explicit-monitoring theory: pressure → anxiety → self-focus → step-by-step monitoring → degraded execution). Negative evidence resists epiphenomenalist dismissal in a way positive evidence does not: confabulating interference is not available as a manoeuvre. The conjunction registers *systematic* coupling across different representational profiles — placebo's content-specificity across humoral and dopaminergic pathways, choking's mode-specificity across motor-cortical and basal-ganglia loops — describing, with the negative side, an interface that sometimes couples and sometimes detaches in patterned ways.

## Independent Traditions on the Same Architecture

The cluster's evidential weight rests on the convergence of independent traditions — working with different premises and vocabularies, recovering the same diagnostic signature — rather than on any single member.

**Carruthers (2011) on cognition.** *The Opacity of Mind* argues introspection of propositional attitudes is interpretive: we infer our attitudes from sensory-format evidence much as we infer others'. Listed for completeness but counts as an *absorption* of the signature rather than a witness — the [[#strong-rival|strong-rival section]] below explains why his interpretive-sensory-access theory predicts the dissociation from materialist premises, the cluster's preferred reading run in reverse.

**Trick and Pylyshyn (1994) on perception.** Their FINST model gives the cardinality floor's diagnostic sharpest: visual indexing operates on a pool of pre-attentive tokens whose allocation is invisible — mechanism-neutrally so, as the [[#instant-of-perception|instant-of-perception]] treatment notes.

**Koriat (2000) on fluency.** Metacognitive feelings that gate cognition — knowing, rightness, confidence — are outputs of implicit inferential heuristics. The "Lima problem" sharpens the recursive face: epistemic feelings report on content we cannot independently access, so we cannot cross-check them except through more feelings of the same kind.

**Polanyi (1966) on tacit integration.** "We know more than we can tell" names the dissociation in the integrative-knowledge face; the "from-to" structure is a phenomenologist's vocabulary for output-while-operation-hidden.

**Inzlicht and Kurzban on effort.** The opportunity-cost (Kurzban et al. 2013) and process (Inzlicht-Schmeichel 2012) models supply the [[#real-time|real-time section]]'s reading with a confirmed prediction: Westbrook et al. (2020) found dopamine elevation shifts willingness to engage demanding tasks, the introspector noticing the result not the manipulation.

**Bergson (1908) on memory.** "Le souvenir du présent" proposes memory and perception form simultaneously, the memory-aspect normally suppressed; déjà vu is the brief surfacing of an occluded shadow. The reducing-valve picture names the architecture.

**Schwitzgebel (2011) on introspection.** *Perplexities of Consciousness* establishes that introspective reports of even basic phenomenal features are unreliable in ways subjects cannot detect from inside.

These traditions do not share dualist commitments; the convergence is on the *signature*, not on what it entails, and any single member admits domain-specific physicalist absorption. A theory that absorbs each separately must additionally explain why the same coupling — phenomenal verdict tracking sub-personal output, operation tracking nothing — appears across substrates with different representational profiles.

A scrupulous critic presses harder than the selection-effect worry, and the cluster grants the point. "Access to outputs, not to the operations that produce them" is the *predicted, mainstream null* of standard cognitive architecture, established before most of the cited authors wrote: Nisbett and Wilson's (1977) "little or no direct introspective access to higher-order cognitive processes" states the signature inside a materialist programme; Fodor's modularity, Marr's levels-of-description, and global workspace theory (Baars; Dehaene and Naccache) all predict that algorithmic processing is not introspectable while its products are. As a claim about *which information reaches report*, the signature taxes no materialist account, and the tradition-count cannot be cashed as independent evidence for a distinct register. What the architectural null does not touch is why the broadcast verdict is *felt* rather than merely globally available, together with the patterned attachment of felt-character to outputs and its inverted reappearance in the positive companion — the seam the [[#strong-rival|strong-rival section]] isolates and the [[#the-diagnostic-signature|diagnostic signature]] names as the explanandum.

The traditions divide by argumentative weight. Polanyi and parts of Koriat observe the signature *despite* their broader commitments. Carruthers, Trick and Pylyshyn, and Inzlicht and Kurzban observe it *because* their materialist or computational models predict it — internal corroboration, not independent evidence against materialism. Two need re-classification: Bergson's reducing-valve picture is a proposed metaphysical thesis resembling the cluster's own, so counting him as evidence would be double-counting; and Schwitzgebel's scepticism cuts *against* the cluster's preferred reading — if introspection cannot reliably report felt character, the "rich, confident access to what the system decided" premise is also unsafe. The honest re-count is five-or-six traditions, not seven, with Bergson a parallel proposer and Schwitzgebel a witness whose scepticism applies to both sides of the frame.

The convergence's force also depends on the traditions analysing substrates that do not share architectural limits, and that condition is currently thin. A common-cause hypothesis — *human introspection has the same architectural limits across all domains; those limits ARE the common machinery* — would predict the same convergence the tradition-count credits to independent triangulation, since all the traditions analyse human cognition through human introspection; cross-timescale recurrence does not discriminate. What would discriminate is a same-introspector-different-signature counterexample — a domain where a human report transparently tracks operations, or a radically-different-architecture introspector reporting *absence* of the signature where humans show it. Neither is in hand; the cross-species, cross-cultural, and patient-population evidence [[common-cause-null|the common-cause-null discipline]] catalogues is owed. The cross-substrate convergence is consistent both with the architectural reading and with the common-cause null the article cannot yet defeat.

The candidate same-introspector counterexamples sit in ordinary experience and owe an explicit hearing. Four: the *tip-of-the-tongue* state (Schwartz 1999) feels like a retrieval *process* in motion rather than a delivered verdict; the *cognitive phenomenology* literature (Strawson 1994; Pitt 2004; Bayne and Montague 2011) argues occurrent thinking has a proprietary felt character; the *felt recall-versus-imagination mode distinction* (Tulving's autonoetic consciousness, the remember/know contrast) is arguably a window onto a process-level difference; and *expert skilled-judgment* sometimes includes a felt sense of how a judgement is forming. The cluster's response is the operations-versus-content distinction it already uses for the contemplative challenge: in each case what is plausibly felt is a *further verdict-like output* (a meta-feeling of retrieval-imminence, a fluency signal tagging the thinking, a mode-tag, a confidence reading) rather than transparent access to the message-passing. That absorbs tip-of-the-tongue and the mode-tag cases cleanly, but is weakest against cognitive phenomenology of thought, where the claim is precisely that the felt character is *of the thinking*. Here the cluster concedes: if Strawson and Pitt are right, the signature has a genuine same-introspector exception and its scope narrows to domains where the operations-versus-content distinction holds. Partial absorption with one open concession, not a clean sweep.

The recollection case is the sharpest test. Tulving's (1985) [[anoetic-noetic-autonoetic-consciousness|autonoetic consciousness]] — the felt re-living that distinguishes remembering from knowing (remember/know paradigm; Gardiner 1988) — looks like phenomenology *of a retrieval mode*, fragmenting the clean picture at the memory member the across-time face leans on. The cluster's preferred line bites the bullet: autonoetic feeling is itself a *mode-tagging verdict-output*, no more a transparent readout than the feeling of rightness is a readout of the reasoning. But the reading is not cost-free: De Brigard (2024) argues autonoetic consciousness lacks construct validity and is not necessary for episodic memory — friendly to bullet-biting but corrosive to any claim that the felt mode reliably tracks a distinct process; Sant'Anna et al. (2024) map the competing accounts without settling them. The recollection member is contested at the level of its phenomenology itself.

## The Strongest Materialist Reading {#strong-rival}

The convergence argument is only as strong as the best unified rival it can survive. Naming higher-order theory, illusionism, and global workspace theory separately understates the threat: a materialist need not absorb each cluster member in isolation but can offer one architecture that predicts the whole signature — output-while-operation-hidden, dissociable fallibility, mode-fragility, and the positive companion — as a structural consequence. The honest position is to build that architecture here and then say exactly what it leaves unexplained.

**The unified rival.** The strongest current reading is *predictive-processing introspection coupled to a reality-monitoring access mechanism*. Hohwy (2013) and Clark (2016) treat the mind as a hierarchical generative model whose higher layers infer the causes of lower-layer activity; introspection is a further layer of the same inferential machinery, not a dedicated detector. Schwengerer (2019) develops this into an explicit transparency account, and Sandved-Smith et al. (2021) give the formal version — a deep active-inference model in which the introspective layer estimates a posterior over lower-level attentional dynamics. This predicts output-while-operation-hidden directly: a layer estimating a *posterior* has access to the estimate, not the message-passing that produced it. Carruthers' (2011) interpretive-sensory-access theory supplies the same prediction from another direction. On the access side, Lau's (2022) perceptual-reality-monitoring theory has a prefrontal discriminator sorting internally- from externally-generated signals; what reaches consciousness is the verdict, not the operation, with Block's (1995) access/phenomenal distinction framing the move and Naccache (2018) claiming the access half is the whole story. Assembled: a hierarchical predictive system whose introspective and reality-monitoring layers deliver reconstructed verdicts while the message-passing stays sub-personal, with felt certainty as a fluency-and-precision signal from the same layers.

This rival predicts the three sub-features without strain. *Output-while-operation-hidden* follows from the posterior-estimate structure. *Dissociable fallibility* follows because separate generative sub-models can be lesioned independently (Capgras as a precision failure on affective prediction, déjà vu as a spurious familiarity posterior). *Mode-fragility* follows because directing the introspective layer at an operation changes its precision weighting (choking) or collapses the task set (meditation). The positive companion is no harder: placebo is high-precision prior content propagating to autonomic effectors, choking is misallocated precision degrading a tuned motor model. The rival absorbs the *shape*.

**Where the rival is defective on its own terms.** Two members resist the access-only reading from inside the rival's commitments. First, the [[empirical-phenomena-mental-causation|positive companion's negative case]]: choking is content-*interference*, and the rival owes an account of why precision misallocation produces the specific degradation explicit-monitoring theory documents, when the access-only story predicts only that monitored representations become reportable, not that monitoring *disrupts* a model already running correctly — it can confabulate a report of effort but not the measurable performance drop. Second, Naccache's mesio-frontal patient: a layer estimating a posterior over executive dynamics should produce *some* posterior when the operation runs normally, yet felt effort is gone while Stroop control is preserved. As the [[#real-time|real-time section]] flags, this is a single un-replicated case with collateral deficits, so it identifies a bill the rival owes rather than collecting on it. Neither refutes predictive processing; both mark places where the access-only reading has not paid a bill its own framework issues.

**Where the disagreement is closer to bedrock.** The deeper residue is one the rival's proponents concede. Lau states that perceptual reality monitoring answers the *consciousness question* — why some states are conscious and reportable — and explicitly not why they *feel the way they do*. The predictive-processing models inherit the boundary: modelling meta-awareness as a generative-model layer specifies the functional role of the estimate without specifying why the estimate is *felt* rather than merely available for control. The identification of "high-precision posterior available for global control" with "felt certainty" is asserted, not derived — the very identification the diagnostic signature makes the explanandum. This is a framework-boundary disagreement, not an in-framework refutation: predictive-processing introspection is a complete account of the *access* structure, and the Map's claim is that it leaves untouched the felt-character question its own architects bracket. The cluster's residual force is narrow and statable: the rival explains why operations are hidden and verdicts available, not why the verdict is *experienced*.

**Consequence for the convergence count.** Carruthers cannot be both a convergent witness and an exponent of the strongest absorption; his theory predicts the signature *because* it is the materialist reading under test, so he belongs with the absorption architecture, not the triangulation column — leaving the convergence's independent weight on the *despite*-class members (Polanyi, parts of Koriat), a smaller but cleaner base.

**Whether "explanatory cost" is the hard problem in new clothing.** The residue — verdict available, felt-character unexplained — invites the charge that the cluster re-derives Levine's (1983) explanatory gap and Chalmers' (1995) hard problem as new empirical pressure. The charge is partly right and the response concedes it: the felt-character seam *is* the gap localised. What it claims to add is structural, in two parts. First, the gap normally appears as a single global puzzle; the cluster relocates it to a *patterned* boundary — felt-character attaches to verdicts and not to the operations producing them, recurring across substrates and reappearing inverted in the positive companion. The gap has a *shape* the conceivability and knowledge arguments do not predict. Second, the positive companion supplies a constraint the static hard problem lacks — placebo's content-specific coupling and choking's interference profile are behavioural facts an account must fit. If the reader judges this structure collapses back into the gap, the cluster's ambition reduces to the hard problem restated, and the constrain-vs-establish reading below is the one to keep. The article claims the pattern and the positive-companion constraint as the additional content, flagging that the weight of that content is itself contestable.

## The Constrain-vs-Establish Discipline

[[evidential-status-discipline|Evidential-status discipline]] names the cluster's load-bearing methodological commitment: the dissociations *constrain* identity readings on which felt phenomenology reports transparently on its own underlying operation; they do not by themselves *establish* the dualist alternative.

The shape is symmetric. The felt-effort/operation dissociation removes the simplest "felt effort just is what the operation feels like" identity reading but leaves what felt effort *is* underdetermined between dualist and process-model. Physical correlates for any phenomenal output (the temporal-lobe correlate of déjà vu, dopamine modulation of effort) remove the simplest "phenomenology is uncoupled from physics" reading without establishing identity. Each side's evidence is defeater-removal-against-the-other, not evidence-lift in its own favour. The diagnostic test: when an article cites a dissociation, does the citation rest on removing a defeater the opposing view needs, or on establishing the citing view? The first is honest; the second is conflation.

Each dissociation runs a four-level chain that must not be collapsed: *separability* (features come apart); *phenomenological reality* (a dissociated feature is genuine, not report-artefact); *mechanistic independence* (distinct processes underlie them); *metaphysical irreducibility* (no level-three mechanism could account for the level-two feature). Capgras, blindsight, the Naccache lesion, and the Bruce-Young double dissociation establish level one robustly and contribute to level two; they underdetermine level three (functional accounts supply mechanistic independence within a single substrate) and do not reach level four. The cluster claims weight only at the levels its evidence delivers; the higher levels are reached, if at all, by cumulative convergence — same signature, independent vocabularies, substrates within a single nervous system, both directions of behaviour — not by any single dissociation.

## What the Cluster Establishes (and Doesn't)

The cluster establishes that phenomenal access systematically detaches from the operations it putatively reports on, in patterned ways, across substrates within a single nervous system with different representational profiles and timescales. The same dissociation is observed across five-or-six traditions on the audited count, the convergence's force concentrated in the sub-class observing the signature *despite* its broader commitments. The architecture has a positive companion: phenomenology *can* couple to causal machinery (placebo, choking), so the negative cases are a structural feature of an interface that sometimes couples and sometimes does not.

The cluster does not establish the dualist alternative. The [[#strong-rival|strong-rival section]] grants that predictive-processing introspection coupled to reality-monitoring access absorbs the whole shape as a structural consequence; the convergence does not refute physicalism from inside its framework. What survives the unified rival is narrow: the rival explains why operations are hidden and verdicts available, and on its own architects' admission does not explain why the verdict is *experienced* rather than merely available for control. The cluster raises the cost of physicalist absorption at exactly that seam and supplies a structural picture the dualist tenets accommodate naturally, without claiming to have closed the seam from inside the rival's framework.

## What Would Falsify the Cluster

A cluster whose evidence comes from convergence across heterogeneous substrates risks being indistinguishable from a category whose members share no real signature. Three candidates would weaken the framing.

**Clean lesion-dissociation of the four real-time members.** If targeted lesions or pharmacological dissociations cleanly separated felt effort, agency, suspension, and knowing — each on independent substrates with no shared opacity profile — the convergence would weaken to four independent functions rather than a unified signature.

**Demonstrated transparent introspection of any one operation.** If contemplative training, neural intervention, or a verifiable phenomenological method produced stable, articulable access to the operation underlying any member (the matching trace producing recognition, the executive engagement producing effort), the output-while-operation-hidden signature would lose its structural status — provided the inspection-while-operating case landed with operations-not-content rigour and clear inter-rater agreement.

**Symmetric absence of the positive companion.** If placebo and choking turned out to be artefacts, the two-sided structure would collapse into a one-sided opacity claim and the interface-architecture reading would lose its predicted symmetry.

None is currently observed; that is what makes the framework usable. None is in principle excluded; that is what makes it falsifiable rather than merely descriptive.

## Relation to Site Perspective

The cluster bears most directly on two of the [[tenets|five tenets]].

**[[tenets#^dualism|Dualism]]** is made more live — less easily dismissed — by the cluster's structural profile, rather than positively supported by it. Materialism must explain why phenomenal output detaches systematically from its putative operations, why the detachment has the same signature across substrates within a single nervous system, and why it has a positive companion in which phenomenology couples to causal machinery in matching detail. The dualist reading takes the dissociation as *compatible with* phenomenal access being a distinct ontological register, and the coupling as *compatible with* the register doing causal work when the interface is engaged. Compatibility is not support: the cumulative convergence pressures transparent-identity models and keeps the distinct-register reading on the table without supplying positive evidence for it. The evidence the Map cites for Dualism is the *cumulative pattern across all clusters*, of which this is one of the most developed instances — not the dissociation as a direct argument.

**[[tenets#^occams-limits|Occam's Razor Has Limits]]** is engaged because the simplicity-preference motivating "consciousness is just brain activity" is itself one of the cluster's members: trust in parsimony is a fluency-and-coherence signal gated by the same noetic-feelings machinery the cluster catalogues, so the materialist's confidence cannot be neutrally adjudicated — the adjudicator is the architecture whose reliability is at issue. The recursion turns symmetrically against the Map's own use of "explanatory cost." The commitment is not to *win* at the gate by appealing to explanatory cost as if exempt from the architecture, but to *sustain inquiry past* the gate set by the felt sufficiency of any simpler account — the materialist's, the dualist's, or the cluster's own.

The other three tenets connect more cautiously. **[[tenets#^bidirectional-interaction|Bidirectional Interaction]]** anchors empirically in the positive companion (placebo, choking). **[[tenets#^minimal-quantum-interaction|Minimal Quantum Interaction]]** is consistent with the interface-asymmetry profile but the cluster argues no particular mechanism; the [[type-token-causation|type-token distinction]] specifies how mental causation can be real and statistically invisible. **[[tenets#^no-many-worlds|No Many Worlds]]** is implicitly engaged wherever the analysis invokes "selection," but the cluster does not adjudicate single-world versus branching readings.

The unifying picture: phenomenal output and causal machinery are dissociable across timescales, substrates, and traditions, coupling in the success cases of mental causation and detaching in patterned ways in the failure cases. The cluster marks where reductive accounts owe an explanation beyond functional decomposition — not why each individual dissociation occurs, but why the architecture takes its symmetric two-sided form — with the convergence holding subject to the common-cause null the article does not yet defeat.
