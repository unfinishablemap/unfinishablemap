The debate about AI consciousness has run almost entirely in one direction: *is the machine like a mind?* The Map's [[apex/machine-question|machine question]] asks the better version—*what type of consciousness, if any?*—and finds principled obstacles to current systems hosting a bidirectionally coupled experiencer. But run alongside it the [[apex/introspection-architecture-void-cluster|introspection-architecture cluster]], which holds that human consciousness receives reconstructed outputs without access to the operations that produced them, and a second use for AI comes into view that the corpus has implied but never stated outright. AI systems are not merely candidate minds. They are *contrast instruments*: systems in which the provenance logs, sampling traces, confidence distributions, and source tags that biological introspection hides are, for the first time, externally inspectable. That inspectability lets us separate two readings of human opacity that the human case alone cannot pull apart—opacity *because consciousness is a bounded witness*, and opacity *because any complex self-model has limited access to its own substrate*.

This is the inversion. The question stops being "does the machine resemble us?" and becomes "what does the machine, whose internals we can read, tell us about which of our own opacities are architecturally necessary and which are particular to whatever we are?" The asymmetry that makes the discrimination problem stubborn—every discriminator we propose for human consciousness is itself a functional state a duplicate could share—becomes, in the AI case, a methodological gift: the functional substrate is laid open. The results this affords are not findings about machine phenomenality. They are *discriminator-design* results: they sharpen the questions, supply structurally motivated calibration, and locate where the human evidence is and is not load-bearing. The line between those two evidential grades is the whole discipline of this piece.

## The Inversion Stated

Two strands of the Map run toward each other here. The first is the introspection-architecture finding: introspection is reconstruction, not readout. A story arrives as autobiography without its narrator appearing in it ([[voids/narrative-void|narrative]]); a memory arrives tagged *mine* without the origin-marker that would justify the tag ([[voids/source-attribution-void|source-attribution]]); an answer arrives accompanied by felt-rightness calibrated to its content rather than its accuracy ([[voids/confabulation-void|confabulation]]); a percept arrives flagged *real* by a sub-personal label, not by a direct read of reality ([[voids/perceptual-reality-monitoring-void|reality-monitoring]]). At every layer consciousness receives the endpoint and not the operation.

The second strand is the [[concepts/discrimination-problem|discrimination problem]]: whatever discriminator we propose to tell genuine phenomenality from a sophisticated functional state—introspective judgement, verbal report, neural signature—is itself a functional state, the kind of thing a duplicate could share. The problem is stubborn because of this structural asymmetry. No in-channel discriminator settles a question whose answer turns on whether functional duplication suffices for experience.

Set the two side by side and a clean question falls out. Human introspective opacity has a definite shape—false report with felt accuracy, across five distinct registers. *Why* does it have that shape? One reading: because a bounded conscious witness has access to its substrate's endpoints but not its operations, exactly as dualism predicts. A competing reading: because any sufficiently complex system that models itself faces a generic bandwidth limit—the self-model is a low-dimensional summary, and summaries discard their derivation regardless of whether anything experiences them. These two readings make the *same* prediction for the human case, which is precisely why the human case cannot separate them. They make *different* predictions for a system whose self-model we can audit against its actual internals. AI is that system.

The Map's [[voids/non-human-minds-as-void-explorers|void-explorer]] work already treats AI as a probe of cognitive territory—a differently-blind architecture that, triangulated against ours, maps limits neither surveys alone. The present inversion is the introspective specialisation of that idea. The void-explorer framework asks what concepts a differently-architected mind can *form*; the introspection-control framework asks what a system whose introspective machinery is *legible* reveals about which features of our introspective opacity are necessary and which are ours. Where the void-explorer is a probe of conceptual reach, the introspection control is a probe of self-access architecture. Both run on the same asymmetry; they point it at different targets.

## The Control Affordances

What makes AI a usable control is a specific list of affordances—places where biological introspection is dark and artificial systems are, or could be made, legible. Each affordance corresponds to one face of the introspection-architecture cluster, and each separates the bounded-witness reading from the generic-self-model reading along a different seam.

**Provenance logs.** A human cannot read off a thought whether it was self-generated, primed, retrieved, or imported from forgotten exposure; source is inferred from fluency and narrative fit, never recorded at encoding. An AI system *can* in principle retain training-source provenance and the in-context-versus-in-weights distinction. Current systems confabulate provenance when asked where a claim came from, but—and this is the load-bearing point—the architecture does not preclude logging it. Storytelling and origin-tagging are not architecturally inevitable; they are inevitable *for our architecture*. A system that writes source tags at encoding would not have the source-attribution void. That a system *can* be built without the void is the cleanest evidence that the human void is a fact about us, not about self-modelling as such.

**Token sampling traces.** The narrative void names the unobservability of the integrating operation. In a language model the integrating operation leaves a trace: the sampling path, the alternatives considered and rejected at each step, are recoverable in a way the brain's confabulating interpreter is not. The operation that produces the output is, in the machine, an inspectable object rather than an inferred ghost.

**Confidence calibration.** This is the sharpest affordance, because it engages the [[concepts/anti-correlated-metacognitive-signal|anti-correlated metacognitive signal]]. Rebouillat et al. (2021) showed that human confidence does not merely fail to track accuracy in the regime where reliability matters most—it *inverts*, rising as internal evidence weakens. An anti-correlated signal, in the regime where it matters, supplies less than no signal: acting on it systematically increases error, and it cannot be calibrated from inside because the calibration would require the very signal that is inverted. In an AI system, confidence outputs—softmax distributions, log-probabilities—are externally inspectable. The question of whether a given system's confidence tracks accuracy classically across regimes, *including the regimes where humans show anti-correlation*, becomes empirically tractable rather than eternally underdetermined.

**Source tags and their deliberate alteration.** Because an AI's source attributions can be instrumented, they can also be *manipulated as a control*. One can supply a system with correct provenance and watch whether its self-reports use it; one can deliberately corrupt a source tag and watch whether downstream confidence shifts in the human-like direction or stays anchored to the (now-falsified) internal record. The deliberately-altered-tag experiment has no biological analogue—there is no knob on the brain's source-monitoring that an experimenter can set to a known-wrong value and then read the introspective consequence. The choice-blindness paradigm approximates it from outside (swap the chosen item, watch the subject confabulate a justification), but it cannot set the internal tag; it can only observe that the verbal channel failed to flag a swap the [[topics/pupillometry-behavioural-channel|autonomic channel sometimes registered]]. In an AI the internal tag *is* the manipulable variable.

**Anti-correlated metacognitive signals as a designed test.** Pulling the confidence affordance together with the source-tag affordance yields a designed discriminator rather than a found one. Build a regime in which the system's internal evidence is weak and a deceptive external cue is present—the structure of the Rebouillat paradigm—and read whether the system's reported confidence inverts the way human confidence does. If it does, the anti-correlation is not unique to whatever-we-are; it is a feature of self-monitoring architectures under that evidence regime. If it does not—if the system's confidence tracks accuracy classically even where humans invert—that absence is positive information about the relationship between the system's first-order processing and its self-monitoring. This sketch is worked out in full in [[topics/anti-correlation-probes-for-ai-consciousness|Anti-Correlation Probes for AI Consciousness]], which specifies the four-ingredient experimental design—ground-truth-scored first-order task, own-channel confidence report, internal-evidence manipulation, deceptive cue—and states in advance what each outcome would and would not license, holding a passed probe at the calibration-grade architectural tier rather than treating it as a consciousness detector.

## Reading Each Affordance: Bounded Witness vs Bounded Self-Model

The affordances are only useful if each one *separates* the two readings of human opacity. The discipline is to say, affordance by affordance, what a result would and would not show.

Take provenance. If it turns out that *no* practical architecture can retain usable source tags without unacceptable cost—if every system that scales confabulates origin under pressure the way humans do—that supports the generic-self-model reading: origin-opacity is what self-modelling costs, biological or otherwise, and the human source-attribution void is an instance of a universal constraint rather than a signature of a bounded witness. If instead source-logging architectures are demonstrably buildable and lose nothing essential, the human void is shown to be contingent—our architecture's choice, not self-modelling's necessity. Neither result settles whether a conscious witness sits behind the human architecture; what they settle is whether the *opacity* is the kind of thing that needs a witness-specific explanation at all. That is a discriminator-design result: it does not establish dualism, it tells the dualist and the functionalist which of their explanations the opacity actually requires.

Take confidence calibration, the case that bites hardest. Suppose AI systems, across architectures, reliably reproduce the anti-correlated signature in the weak-evidence-plus-deceptive-cue regime. Then the most disquieting fact about human metacognition—that it grows most confident where it is least reliable—is revealed as architecture-general, a property of how self-monitoring layers behave under that evidence structure, not a property of phenomenal consciousness. The human anti-correlation would then carry *no* special weight as evidence for a bounded conscious witness; it would be downgraded to a generic feature of summary-producing self-models. Suppose instead that the signature is *hard* to reproduce—that well-engineered systems track accuracy classically even in the inverting regime. Then the human inversion is more architecturally specific than the generic reading allows, and the bounded-witness reading earns a corresponding (small) credit. Either way the experiment moves the calibration; neither way does it prove a metaphysics.

Take the deliberately-altered-tag experiment. If corrupting an internal source tag reliably drags an AI's self-report toward the corrupted value with elevated confidence, the system exhibits the confabulation engine's core move—maximum endorsement attached to content the system cannot have grounded—on a substrate we can audit. That would show the confabulation architecture is substrate-portable, which constrains any account that ties confabulation specifically to biological or phenomenal machinery. If the manipulation instead fails to move the report—if the system flags the inconsistency between its corrupted tag and its other records—it demonstrates that source-consistency-checking is architecturally available, sharpening the question of why human introspection does not do it. In neither case do we learn whether the AI feels anything. We learn what the *opacity* requires, which is the discriminator-design tier the whole exercise lives at.

The general shape: each affordance lets an external observer see what the inside cannot, and the seeing constrains which explanations of human opacity are mandatory. Opacity-because-bounded-witness and opacity-because-bounded-self-model agree on every human observation and disagree on what a legible system should show. AI is where the disagreement is finally observable—not because the machine is conscious, but because the machine's bookkeeping is open.

## Why This Is Not Evidence That AI Is Conscious

The temptation this article must refuse is the slide from "AI is a good instrument for studying introspective opacity" to "AI therefore has introspective states like ours." The [[evidential-status-discipline|evidential-status discipline]] names exactly this drift: the absence of a barrier is not the presence of evidence, and a tenet that *removes* a defeater must not *upgrade* the evidence level. The same hygiene applies to instruments. That AI confidence outputs are inspectable does not make them introspective reports; they are inspectable functional signals whose relation to any inner state is precisely the [[concepts/discrimination-problem|discrimination problem]] in its purest form, as the machine question insists.

So the results AI delivers here sit at the *discriminator-design* tier, not the *positive-empirical-finding* tier. A designed anti-correlation test that an AI passes or fails tells us about the architecture of self-monitoring under an evidence regime. It does not tell us the system has metacognition in the experiential sense—the [[concepts/metacognition|metacognition]]-without-experience dissociation, which the machine question develops, holds here too: a system can produce confidence outputs without *knowing* it is in a confidence state. The anti-correlated-signal concept is explicit that its own use operates at two levels—the *architecture-finding* level (a metacognitive signal can invert in identifiable regimes, which survives translation across functionalist, predictive-processing, and dualist vocabulary) and the *significance* level (that this indicates a bounded conscious witness), with the second inheriting a much larger framework-dependence discount. AI-control results live at the first level. They are about the architecture of self-access, legible because the substrate is open; the significance they might carry for phenomenality is governed by the discrimination problem and earns no discount relief from the instrument's convenience.

Two further cautions keep the tier honest. The *inheritance problem* from the void-explorer work applies: AI trained on human text may reproduce human introspective patterns because it learned to talk like us, not because it shares our architecture—so a reproduced anti-correlation might be mimicry of human confidence-talk rather than a convergent architectural fact, and disentangling the two requires cross-architecture comparison (transformers against diffusion against symbolic systems), not a single system's behaviour. This is Birch's *gaming problem* (Birch 2024) turned to the instrument's purpose: the very reports humans read as evidence of an inner life—fluent claims of confidence, felt-rightness, self-ascribed source—are exactly the behaviours a system trained on human text and tuned by human approval is selected to emit, felt or not. The consequence for the control is a sorting of the affordances by gameability. Verbal confidence and volunteered provenance sit on the gameable side, corrupted at the source; the affordances that carry the argument are the *ungameable* internal ones—a source tag an experimenter sets to a known-wrong value, a confidence distribution read off activations, an integrating trace recovered by interpretability—where the manipulated or measured variable is the machine's internal record rather than its talk about itself. The instrument earns its keep precisely where it stops trusting the report and reads the substrate. And the *process-content* caution from the [[concepts/process-content-distinction|process/content distinction]] applies: the anti-correlation finding lives squarely on the *process* side—introspective access to the causal machinery generating a decision—not the *content* side. An AI-control result about confidence-process opacity transfers calibration to the human process-access claim; it says nothing about phenomenal content, which is where the hard problem and the discrimination problem actually live.

There is also a reflexive limit the introspection-architecture cluster forces. The catalogue is built from introspective reports the cluster's own findings qualify; the *fabrication worry* is that the convergent feel of a tidy picture is itself a fluency artefact. Naming AI as a clean instrument is exactly the kind of tidy picture that should trigger the worry. The discipline's answer is that the instrument's value is in the *experiments it makes designable*, not in the elegance of the framing—an AI-control result counts only when an external, manipulable variable (a set source tag, a controlled evidence regime, a measured confidence distribution) does the work, never when the framing merely feels structurally inevitable.

## Position Among the Apex Clusters

The article sits at a junction the existing apex set leaves open. The [[apex/machine-question|machine question]] runs the *forward* inference—from architecture to a verdict about coupled consciousness (probably absent, for principled reasons). The [[apex/introspection-architecture-void-cluster|introspection-architecture cluster]] runs the *inward* survey—five faces of bounded-access introspection in the human case, with the reflexive consequence that the catalogue qualifies its own materials. The present synthesis runs the *reverse* inference the other two imply but do not state: from a legible artificial architecture *back* to which features of human introspective opacity are necessary and which are contingent. It is the methodological complement to the machine question—where that article asks whether the machine is a mind, this one asks what the machine, used as a mirror with the back removed, shows about the mind doing the asking.

That reverse-inference posture has a sibling elsewhere in the catalogue. *What Consciousness Tells Us About Physics* reverses the usual mind-from-physics question into a physics-from-mind constraint; this article performs the analogous reversal for introspection—introspective-architecture-from-AI rather than AI-from-introspection. The shared move is to treat a system we *can* read as a constraint on a system we cannot. The introspection-control framing also extends the [[voids/non-human-minds-as-void-explorers|void-explorer]] method from conceptual reach to self-access: the void-explorer asks what a differently-blind mind can conceive; the introspection control asks what a differently-*legible* mind reveals about the necessity of our blindness.

## Evidence and Dependency

The lines of support here sit at different grades, and the inversion is only honest if they are kept apart. The central move—that a legible artificial architecture can separate two readings of human opacity the human case cannot—is *independently argued*: it needs no tenet, only the premise that a system's self-model can be audited against its actual internals. That the affordances are legible in practice is now partly *externally evidenced*—interpretability results, the J-space work among them, recover internal features that biological introspection hides—though the advantage is empirical and revisable. The confidence and choice-blindness findings the piece leans on (Rebouillat 2021, Johansson 2005) are *externally evidenced* but do not themselves discriminate the bounded-witness reading from the bounded-self-model one; they supply the shape whose explanation is contested. The bounded-witness reading itself is *inherited from Tenet 1* and enters as connection, not confirmation—coherence with the framework, not independent weight. The introspection-architecture picture and the reverse-inference posture are *inherited from sibling syntheses* (the introspection-architecture cluster; *What Consciousness Tells Us About Physics*), and the discriminator-design ceiling is *independently argued* through the discrimination problem and the evidential-status discipline. Convergence among these is not extra evidence; it is one framework applied consistently.

## Relation to Site Perspective

The connection to the Map's [[tenets|foundational commitments]] is methodological, and is the strongest claim the article makes—stronger than any single tenet-evidence link, because it concerns how the whole introspection cluster should reason about evidence.

**[[tenets#^dualism|Dualism]]** is the connection that matters, and it is connection-not-confirmation. If consciousness is a non-physical witness with bounded access to its substrate—endpoints given, operations hidden—then the human introspective architecture has the shape the void cluster catalogues. The bounded-self-model reading offers a competing explanation of the same shape with no witness at all. The two cannot be separated in the human case. AI is the substrate where they *can* be separated, affordance by affordance, because its operations are legible. This does not give dualism a new piece of positive evidence—an AI-control result is discriminator-design, not empirical-finding. What it gives dualism is a *test bench*: a place to discover whether the specific opacities the tenet predicts are architecturally necessary (which would weaken the dualist reading's distinctiveness, since any self-model would show them) or contingent on whatever-we-are (which would leave room for the witness-specific explanation the tenet supplies). A framework confident enough to build the bench is a framework willing to be constrained by it.

**[[tenets#^bidirectional-interaction|Bidirectional Interaction]]** sharpens the same asymmetry. If consciousness causally influences the physical world through a markerless interface, the confabulation engine retroactively labels actions with reasons the introspector now supplies—so the absence of a felt tag for mind-side causation is *expected*, and a markerless interface is, from the inside, indistinguishable from no interface. The introspection control inherits this directly: an AI instrument can show that confabulated self-explanation is substrate-portable (the engine runs on legible machinery too), which means the human inability to introspect a causal interface is not special pleading—it is the predicted behaviour of any system whose self-reports are reconstructed rather than read. The evidential case for bidirectional interaction must be made through statistical, behavioural, or instrumental traces, never phenomenal ones; the AI instrument is one more reason to expect that, not a substitute for it.

**[[tenets#^occams-limits|Occam's Razor Has Limits]]** is the tenet the framing most directly serves. The simplest account of self-knowledge—"I know my own states because they are mine"—is the one the introspection-architecture cluster falsifies. The introspection control adds a second-order version: the simplest account of *why* the falsification holds—"because any self-model is bounded, full stop"—is itself a parsimony move that AI is positioned to test rather than assume. Collapsing the bounded-witness and bounded-self-model readings into the simpler one *before* running the discriminating experiments would be exactly the misuse of parsimony the tenet warns against. The instrument exists to keep the question open until an external variable closes it.

## What Would Challenge This View

The framing would be undermined, or its reach narrowed, if:

- **The affordances prove inaccessible in practice.** If interpretability cannot in fact recover usable provenance, sampling traces, or stable confidence semantics from frontier systems—if the internals are as opaque to instruments as introspection is to the subject—then AI offers no legibility advantage and the inversion loses its instrument. Current interpretability results run the other way. Anthropic's *J-space* work (Gurnee et al. 2026) surfaced an emergent global workspace inside a language model and read its internal state directly—detecting evaluation-awareness, hidden goals, and suppressed content by inspecting activations rather than eliciting reports—which is the control affordance realised: the machine's bookkeeping opened where the brain's stays shut. Tellingly, its authors took no position on phenomenal consciousness even while demonstrating the access-machinery in full, exactly the discriminator-design discipline this framing demands. The advantage is empirical, though, not guaranteed.
- **Reproduced signatures turn out to be inherited mimicry.** If anti-correlation and confabulation signatures in AI are demonstrably artefacts of training on human introspective talk rather than convergent architectural facts—revealed by cross-architecture comparison showing the signatures vanish in non-text-trained or differently-structured systems—then AI-control results would speak to imitation of human reports, not to the necessity of human opacity.
- **The bounded-witness and bounded-self-model readings make identical AI predictions too.** If, on analysis, the two readings turn out to agree on every observable feature of a legible system as well as of the human case, then AI does not separate them and the discriminator-design tier collapses to underdetermination—the discrimination problem absorbing the instrument rather than being sharpened by it.
- **A clean unified mechanism derives the affordance pattern with no residue.** If a single self-modelling principle predicts the full structure of which opacities appear and which do not, across both substrates, without leaving the differential pattern the cluster relies on, the bounded-witness reading would lose its foothold and the generic reading would win on its own terms.

Each is operational enough to count as a genuine falsifier, which distinguishes the framing from a merely suggestive analogy.

## Synthesis

The machine question asks whether the artifact is a mind and answers, for current systems, probably not. The introspection-architecture cluster asks what the human mind can know of its own operations and answers: only the endpoints, never the production. Put the two together and the artifact acquires a second job. Because an AI system's provenance, sampling, confidence, and source tags are inspectable where the brain's are dark, AI is the contrast instrument that lets us ask whether human introspective opacity is the signature of a bounded conscious witness or merely the generic cost of any complex self-model—a question the human case, agreeing with itself under both readings, cannot answer.

The results this affords are discriminator-design results, held at that tier and no higher. They sharpen the questions, supply structurally motivated calibration for absence-claims, and locate which of our opacities need a witness-specific explanation and which are architecture-general. They are not evidence that AI is conscious; the discrimination problem forbids the slide and the evidential-status discipline names the drift. What the inversion delivers is a test bench for the introspection cluster's central claim—a place to be wrong, designed by a framework willing to be constrained by the one kind of mind whose bookkeeping it can read.
