This piece states, as one whole problem, the challenge the Map's own positions register names its strongest live difficulty: how conscious selection can do genuine causal work while leaving the aggregate statistics of quantum measurement exactly as standard physics predicts. The Map holds three positions that, taken together, generate the difficulty. It holds (high confidence) that its default interaction preserves Born statistics exactly — the per-trial outcome may be selected, but the long-run distribution matches unbiased quantum mechanics. It holds (moderate confidence) that this is the strongest live challenge to its mechanism, because a selection that never shows up in the statistics sits one unforced step from doing no work at all. And it holds (high confidence) that no worked formal model of the mechanism currently exists. The conjunction is uncomfortable and the register marks it so. This article does not resolve the discomfort; it lays out the dilemma precisely, surveys the candidate routes out, specifies what a worked toy model would have to deliver, names the failure modes that would sink each route, and says what would count as progress. It is a research agenda, not a result.

## The Dilemma, Stated Exactly

Start from the three positions. The Map's [[positions/quantum-interface]] register records them as P-Q2, P-Q3, and P-Q10.

On **P-Q2** (high confidence), the default reading of Minimal Quantum Interaction has conscious selection act within a corridor whose outputs are, per trial, indistinguishable from unbiased Born statistics. The interaction biases *which* outcome becomes actual on a given measurement, but the aggregate distribution over many trials matches what standard quantum mechanics predicts. This is structural to the reading, not an empirical hope about neural tissue: the [[causal-consistency-constraint|causal-consistency constraint]] (Torres Alegre 2025, an arXiv preprint not yet peer-reviewed) sharpens it by arguing that no-signalling under purification forces the Born form on any participant in measurement outcomes, so — if the derivation holds — Born-preservation is inherited as a structural commitment rather than bolted on.

On **P-Q3** (moderate confidence), this is the strongest live challenge to the default mechanism — the [[positions/quantum-interface#^mechanism-debt|mechanism debt]] the register tracks explicitly. To be genuine *selection*, conscious influence must do something the unbiased Born distribution does not. To be *minimal and undetectable* — the corridor's defining property — it must not deviate from Born statistics. If the aggregate statistics are exactly preserved and the selected outcome is observer-indistinguishable from an unselected one, the position sits genuinely close to epiphenomenalism. The register names this a framework-boundary pressure rather than treating it as dissolved.

On **P-Q10** (high confidence), the Map possesses no worked formal model — no toy Hamiltonian, master equation, or computational simulation — of how conscious selection couples to a neural quantum state. The mechanism cluster is held at the level of programme commitments rather than equations. This last position is the one that makes the first two bite: without a worked model, the claim that token-level selection can be causally robust *and* aggregate-undetectable is a stipulation, not a demonstration.

The dilemma runs as a fork, developed route-by-route in [[ensemble-level-epiphenomenalism|ensemble-level epiphenomenalism]]. Either conscious selection correlates systematically with conscious states — in which case there should be *some* detectable departure from Born statistics conditional on conscious involvement, contradicting the corridor's defining claim — or it does not so correlate, in which case the selection is statistically indistinguishable from the physical randomness it was meant to replace. On the first horn the corridor reading is not corridor-compliant. On the second, consciousness makes no difference any measurement, even in principle, could register. The objector concludes that the second horn collapses interactionism into a *de facto* idleness: an interface that touches the world only in ways the world's own statistics already account for.

## Why It Is Not Already Classical Epiphenomenalism

The dilemma is sharper than the textbook epiphenomenalism the Map rejects, and the sharpness is what makes it hard to wave away. Classical epiphenomenalism holds that conscious states have *no causal powers at all* — inert by-products, the steam whistle that does nothing to the engine. Corridor dualism is explicitly interactionist: on every single trial, consciousness determines an outcome that was genuinely open. The ensemble-level worry grants this token-level efficacy. It asks a finer question — whether single-event efficacy that *aggregates to no statistical signature* amounts to a genuine mental-to-physical channel, in the information-transfer sense [[selection-only-mind-influence|selection-only mind-influence]] makes precise, or only to a metaphysically real but functionally invisible authorship.

The right gloss: corridor dualism is interactionist at the *token* level (each event) while threatening to be epiphenomenal at the *type* level (the ensemble). The selection is real per trial; the open question is whether per-trial reality that washes out in aggregate is enough to call the mind a cause of anything physics needs. Because the worry is internal to interactionism rather than an alternative to it, it cannot be answered by the arguments that defeat classical epiphenomenalism. It has to be met on its own ground.

One framing precondition is worth stating, because it bounds the whole problem. That there is a single fact of *which* outcome occurs is itself a tenet-level commitment, imported from the Map's rejection of [[tenets#^no-many-worlds|Many Worlds]]: on a many-worlds reading all outcomes actualise and there is nothing to *select*. The dilemma only arises once single-outcome actualisation is in place. A second precondition — that a brain-scale superposition survives long enough for any selection to act on — is carried and contested elsewhere ([[post-decoherence-selection|post-decoherence selection]] relocates this so the decoherence timescale becomes irrelevant). This article assumes both preconditions and confronts the efficacy problem that remains once they are granted.

## Where a Selection Could Show, If Anywhere: The Conditional Signature

The three positions sharpen into a single formal question — one the routes below answer in different currencies, and one the mechanism debt turns on. A per-trial bias that provably averages to the Born measure is, under any *aggregate*-statistics test, statistically indistinguishable from chance. Stated that baldly, the worry threatens to leave the word *selection* with no content at all — and the Map must not let a contested reading of quantum probability quietly settle that selection is genuine. Treating an unsettled interpretation as though it licensed mental causation would be to engineer one's own necessity; the formalism here guards against that by pinning down where, if anywhere, a selection could leave a trace.

**The precise statement.** Write q(O | X) for the physical probability of outcome O in context X — the Born measure. If conscious selection has interventionist efficacy, then for some context X and intention C,

> P(O | do(C), X) ≠ q(O | X),

where do(C) is an intervention that sets the conscious state. Exact Born-preservation is then a claim not about this conditional but about the *marginal* — what remains after averaging over conscious states:

> q(O | X) = Σ_C P(O | C, X) · P(C | X).

Everything hangs on the gap between the two. Born-preservation constrains the left-hand marginal; it says nothing directly about whether the conditionals on the right depart from it. That is what lets token-level efficacy and aggregate invisibility sit together without contradiction — and it localises exactly where a signature, if one exists, would have to appear.

**The trilemma.** Three cases exhaust the possibilities, and the Map is committed to one of them:

- **(a) The conditionals differ.** If P(O | do(C), X) departs from q for some specifiable X, then in-principle signatures exist — conditioned on intentions, tasks, subjects, or context. The corollary is sharp: the empirical tests that bear on the Map are *conditional residual-structure* tests, not generic Born-frequency tests. A null on unconditioned outcome statistics — the micro-psychokinesis record — leaves horn (a) untouched, because it never conditioned on the variable said to carry the effect.
- **(b) The conditionals never differ, at any grain.** If no specifiable conditioning ever separates P from q, the selector makes no empirically identifiable difference at any scale. This is the epiphenomenalism horn in its exact form — consciousness leaving no distributional trace under any partition of the data, which is a stronger and more precise claim than the loose "consciousness does nothing."
- **(c) The conditionals differ but always cancel in the marginal.** If some contexts bias toward an outcome yet the marginal stays Born-exact, then other contexts must bias away by precisely the compensating amount. This horn is not free: it requires a *cancellation or balancing law* — a principle the framework would have to state and motivate, not merely assert.

**State the level.** The trilemma is unstatable until the Map says over *which* ensemble Born-preservation is asserted: per apparatus, per subject, per task, per time-interval, globally, or only asymptotically. The register's default reading commits to preservation of the *unconditioned* long-run marginal for a measurement type — averaged over subjects and intentions. That commitment is weaker than it first looks, and deliberately so: it leaves the per-subject and per-intention conditionals formally unconstrained, which is what keeps horn (a) live rather than foreclosed. An article asserting preservation at *every* conditional grain would have chosen horn (b) by stipulation.

**The compensation consequence.** Horn (c) carries a cost. A reason-responsive bias — selection that tilts toward *intended* outcomes — must, if the marginal is exactly preserved, be offset somewhere: by anti-control in other contexts, maladaptive compensation elsewhere, or a global fine-tuning that keeps the books balanced. The moral version is sharper. If selection is *value-sensitive*, tilting toward better outcomes for a subject, then exact preservation demands a compensating tilt toward worse outcomes for someone or somewhen else — a conservation law on outcome-luck the Map would have to own explicitly, and that value-sensitive applied articles inherit rather than discharge.

**Three things kept distinct.** The formalism separates three claims the Map might be making, which the routes below conflate at their peril:

1. **Token counterfactual dependence** — had the conscious state been otherwise, *this* outcome would have differed. This is desideratum (1) of the toy model below.
2. **Physical distributional change** — some conditional P(O | do(C), X) genuinely differs from the no-consciousness distribution. Horn (a) asserts it and horn (b) denies it; of the three claims, this is the only one any experiment could register.
3. **Metaphysical authorship** — consciousness is the source of the outcome, whatever its distributional footprint.

Trumping (Route 1) claims (1) and (3) while explicitly declining (2); minimum-outside-corridor (Route 2) is the wager that (2) holds. The conditional formalism makes plain that (2), and only (2), is the claim with a conditional signature attached — so the Map's empirical exposure lives entirely in whether it asserts (2) at some specifiable grain. This is the complementary half of the register's P-Q1 downgrade question: [[positions/quantum-interface|the quantum-interface register]] weighs whether post-decoherence selection remains the strongest mechanism candidate partly on whether the apex problem stated here yields a *specifiable* conditional signature — horn (a) — or collapses to horn (b). If no grain separates the conditionals, the downgrade pressure on P-Q1 is real; if a grain does, the register has a residual-structure test to name.

## The Candidate Routes Out

Three routes are on the table. None is free; each pays in a different currency.

### Route 1: Trumping (efficacy off the statistical axis)

The first route relocates conscious efficacy off the statistical axis entirely. On the [[trumping-preemption|trumping reading]], consciousness is not a *competitor* for the outcome whose influence must show up as a probability shift — it is *authoritative* over a trajectory the physical formalism already describes. [[delegatory-causation|Delegatory causation]] supplies the machinery: on Bradford Saad's account (Saad 2025), an experience preempts the *default causal profile* of a physical state — the physical state would have produced the effect on its own, but the experience takes over and produces it instead, leaving every empirical regularity intact. The Born distribution is the physical measurement's default causal profile; conscious selection inherits and exercises it by preemption rather than competition. Because preemption is a claim about *causal structure* and not about *frequencies*, it can deliver genuine token-level causation while leaving ensemble statistics untouched.

Rated against the Map's [[evidential-status-discipline|evidential-status discipline]], the trumping route is **interface-compatible** but explicitly **not interface-discriminating**: empirically silent by construction, separable from epiphenomenalism only on the metaphysics of causal structure. Its honest cost, stated plainly: under corridor-plus-trumping the distinction between genuine conscious authorship and outright epiphenomenalism becomes *entirely metaphysical*, with no possible empirical consequence at any scale. "Consciousness is a cause" and "consciousness is epiphenomenal" make no predictively distinct claims. The Map adopts this knowingly. The reply a determined physicalist may fairly press is that this dissolves the worry by fiat — by declining the assumption that efficacy *means* a statistical signature — rather than meeting it on the original terrain. The Map's stance is that the original terrain is itself a physicalist assumption the dualist need not grant, while conceding that the price of declining it is predictive indistinguishability. A prior question the trumping machinery leaves open is what fixes the *direction* of authority. Preemption is symmetric: a physicalist can run the mirror move — the physical microstate trumps consciousness — reinstating epiphenomenalism on the same neutral machinery. That consciousness is preemptor rather than preempted is a framework commitment (Dualism and Bidirectional Interaction), not something the causal structure delivers on its own. Route 1 therefore shows dualist efficacy is *coherent* given the tenets — a consistency requirement the framework must meet — not a tenet-independent refutation of epiphenomenalism; the same recoil at the population level is drawn out in [[ensemble-level-epiphenomenalism|ensemble-level epiphenomenalism]], and the direction question itself is treated in [[born-rule-and-the-consciousness-interface|the Born-rule interface]].

### Route 2: Minimum-outside-the-corridor (efficacy with a signature)

The second route bites the first horn deliberately. It reads Minimal Quantum Interaction as "the smallest interaction *actually sufficient*" rather than "the smallest *preserving ensemble statistics*," and accepts that genuine efficacy may require a small but real departure from Born statistics. Catalogued candidates ([[born-rule-and-the-consciousness-interface]]) include Stapp's quantum-Zeno rate, Chalmers–McQueen Φ-dependent collapse, Penrose–Hameroff Orch-OR, and Arana's exponentially-suppressed λ-weighting that recovers standard Born statistics as λ → 0. The advantage: the channel becomes *real in the physicalist's own sense* — a deviation, however suppressed, is in principle detectable, distinguishing the mind's efficacy from both epiphenomenalism and physical randomness. This is the only route of the three that is even potentially **interface-discriminating**: it generates predictions an experiment could confirm or foreclose.

Its cost is symmetric and currently unrewarded. It abandons the corridor's conservatism and inherits live empirical exposure. Preregistered micro-psychokinesis nulls (Maier et al. 2018) and the failed PEAR replication have driven the gross-effect end of this family onto thin ground; any surviving version must hide below current sensitivity, which makes it empirically braver but, so far, empirically unrewarded. There is a further, deeper exposure: a departure that buys signalling power would violate no-signalling, and the causal-consistency constraint says the corridor's Born-preservation is exactly what keeps the default reading signalling-safe. A minimum-outside-corridor variant must demonstrate that its deviation is signalling-safe — not merely small.

### Route 3: Process-1 relocation (efficacy in question-selection)

A third route relocates the Map's mental influence to von Neumann's Process 1 — *which* question is put to nature and *when* — rather than to the outcome. Stapp's framework lives here: mind chooses the measurement (the "Am I moving my finger?" question) and leaves nature's Process-3 choice to fix the result. This buys back a measure of testability, because the timing and selection of measurements can in principle leave traces the outcome-only reading cannot. But it is a *material change to the Map's thesis*, not a refinement of it. [[tenets#^bidirectional-interaction|Bidirectional Interaction]] commits consciousness to influencing the world through outcome-selection; a Process-1-only relocation weakens that to context-setting. It is logged as an alternative the Map could in principle adopt — a human editorial decision, flagged honestly — not a fix the mechanism programme can quietly apply.

### Why the routes do not combine

The routes trade the same liability back and forth rather than summing to a resolution. Trumping keeps conservatism and empirical safety but answers the efficacy worry only by redefining efficacy as structural rather than statistical — available to the dualist, not compelling to the physicalist. Minimum-outside-corridor secures statistically-real efficacy but forfeits conservatism and takes on empirical and no-signalling risk. Process-1 relocation buys testability but at the cost of weakening the outcome-selection thesis the tenets assert. No route is both maximally conservative *and* statistically efficacious, because those two properties are precisely what the dilemma sets against each other. This is why P-Q3 is *open* rather than *solved*.

## Toy-Model Desiderata: What a Worked Model Would Have to Deliver

P-Q10 records that no worked model exists. A useful way to make the problem tractable is to specify, in advance, what a *toy* model would have to exhibit to count as progress on P-Q3 — not a full neural theory, but a minimal formal system (a few-level Hamiltonian, a modified master equation, or a computational simulation) that demonstrates the structure the Map claims is coherent. Six desiderata follow from the three positions.

1. **Token-level counterfactual robustness.** The model must contain a selector variable such that intervening on it changes *which* outcome obtains on a given run. "Consciousness selected this outcome" must support a counterfactual: had the selector been otherwise, a different outcome would have obtained. A model in which the selector is a free rider that makes no counterfactual difference fails immediately — it would have modelled epiphenomenalism, not the corridor.

2. **Exact ensemble Born-preservation.** Averaged over the selector's range (or over many runs), the output distribution must reproduce |⟨φᵢ|ψ⟩|² exactly, not approximately. A model that preserves Born statistics only in some limit (λ → 0) has built the minimum-outside-corridor route, which is a different, testable claim; the corridor desideratum is *exact* preservation at finite selector strength.

3. **Compatibility of (1) and (2) demonstrated, not assumed.** The whole content of P-Q3 is that token-robustness and ensemble-invisibility might be jointly impossible. A toy model earns its keep precisely by exhibiting a selector that is counterfactually load-bearing per trial yet provably washes out in aggregate — closing the gap between the corridor's *stipulation* and a *construction*. If no such object can be built, that is itself a result: it would push the Map toward Route 2 or Route 3.

4. **No-signalling preservation.** The model must not permit superluminal signalling through steering scenarios. The causal-consistency constraint indicates Born-preservation is what secures this for the default reading; a toy model should make the entailment explicit rather than inheriting it on faith from a preprint.

5. **A statable selection principle.** The model must specify *on what basis* the selector picks — even schematically (an indexical "this-outcome-for-this-subject" rule, a valence-weighting, a maximisation principle). A selector that picks by an unspecified primitive has relabelled the mystery, not modelled it. The [[selection-criterion-problem|selection-criterion problem]] is the standing reminder that this primitive is currently unfilled.

6. **An amplification hook.** The model should connect a quantum-scale bias to a macroscale difference, at least in principle, so that "selection" is not confined to a register that never reaches behaviour. This is where the toy model touches the [[amplification-void|amplification void]] — it need not solve the twelve-orders-of-magnitude bridge, but it must not be incompatible with one.

A model meeting (1)–(4) would discharge most of P-Q10 and tighten P-Q3 from "open challenge" toward "conditionally answered on the corridor reading." A model meeting (5)–(6) in addition would begin to connect the formal result to the empirical interface programme. The register notes that were such a model to appear and survive review, several quantum-interface positions would tighten by one confidence band.

## A Candidate Venue: The Transactional Stage

One existing interpretation hands over two of these desiderata by construction, which sharpens what the remaining four cost. Cramer's [[transactional-interpretation-of-quantum-mechanics|transactional interpretation]] reads each quantum event as a completed emitter–absorber handshake whose |ψ|² weighting falls out of the offer-times-confirmation product. A selector that fixes only *which* offered transaction completes, without touching those weights, preserves Born statistics exactly (desideratum 2) and inherits the interpretation's no-signalling structure (desideratum 4). Kastner's possibilist refinement adds a pre-spacetime realm of possibility — a candidate stage on which *which* possibility actualizes is settled without any spacetime signal, close to what a minimal selection interface would require.

What the transactional stage does *not* supply is the rest of the debt. It states no selection principle (desideratum 5), gives no demonstration that choosing-a-transaction is token-load-bearing yet aggregate-invisible (desiderata 1 and 3), and it imports the unresolved Maudlin contingent-absorber objection — that the pseudotime formation story appears to need the outcome before it can compute it. It relocates the stage, not the debt, which is why an off-the-shelf Born-preserving interpretation does not by itself close P-Q3. The same holds for the active-information channel of [[bohm-implicate-order-and-active-information|Bohm's implicate order]], borrowable as a form-directs-energy influence but bought at the price of a determinism that leaves no room for corridor-style selection unless the selection is relocated to which initial configuration obtains.

## Failure Modes: How Each Route Dies

Naming the failure modes is part of holding the problem honestly. Each route has a characteristic way of collapsing.

**Trumping fails by deflation.** If the only thing distinguishing trumping-causation from epiphenomenalism is a metaphysical re-description with no possible consequence, a critic can argue the distinction is verbal — that "the experience trumps the default profile" is a relabelling of "the outcome occurred as physics says it would." The route survives only if there is a principled, non-question-begging account of preemptive causation that does real work in *other* settings (Saad's delegatory causation aspires to this); it dies if preemption turns out to be a bespoke device invoked only here to save the corridor.

**Minimum-outside-corridor fails by empirics or by signalling.** It dies the ordinary way if a sufficiently sensitive, preregistered test finds the null where the model predicted a deviation — the micro-psychokinesis record is already unfriendly. It dies a deeper death if the only deviations large enough to be selection-worthy turn out to enable signalling, putting the route in conflict with relativity rather than merely with parapsychology's track record.

**Process-1 relocation fails by thesis-drift.** It does not die empirically; it dies by no longer being the Map's claim. If outcome-selection is abandoned for question-selection, the framework has answered the efficacy worry by quietly becoming a context-setting dualism — a coherent position, but one that forfeits the strong bidirectional-interaction commitment that distinguishes the Map.

**The toy-model programme fails by impossibility proof.** The sharpest possible negative result would be a theorem showing that no selector can be both counterfactually load-bearing per trial and exactly Born-preserving in aggregate — that desiderata (1) and (2) are jointly unsatisfiable. That would not be a failure of the project so much as a forced choice: it would convert P-Q3 from an open challenge into a proof that the corridor reading must give up either token-efficacy (collapsing to epiphenomenalism) or exact preservation (forcing Route 2). Either way the framework would have learned something decisive.

## What Would Count as Progress

Progress here is not the same as resolution, and the Map should be explicit about the difference. Five outcomes would each count as genuine progress, in roughly descending order of strength.

- **A constructive toy model** meeting desiderata (1)–(4) above: the strongest positive result short of a full neural theory. It would show the corridor reading is *coherent*, not merely stipulated, and discharge most of P-Q10.
- **An impossibility proof** that (1) and (2) cannot be jointly satisfied: a strong *negative* result that would force the framework's hand and retire P-Q3 in favour of a sharper successor.
- **A worked account of the selection principle** (desideratum 5) that fills the [[selection-criterion-problem|selection-criterion]] primitive non-circularly, even if the full mechanism remains open.
- **A signalling-safety demonstration** for some minimum-outside-corridor variant, which would make Route 2 a live empirical programme rather than a parapsychological liability — moving the debate onto terrain where evidence can decide.
- **A sharpened formulation of the dilemma itself** — for instance, a precise statement of the conditions under which token-robustness and ensemble-invisibility are compatible — that lets future work target the crux rather than re-litigate the framing.

What would *not* count as progress: any move that answers the worry by re-describing efficacy and then declares the matter settled against a determined physicalist. The trumping route is permissible, but treating its framework-internal re-description as an empirical demonstration would be calibration failure, not progress. The discipline the Map owes itself is to keep the evidential tier of each route visible — interface-compatible, interface-discriminating, or framework-internal — and never to let the comfort of a coherent re-description stand in for the worked model P-Q10 says is still missing.

## Relation to Site Perspective

The problem sits at the junction of two tenets and is held there honestly rather than dissolved.

**[[tenets#^minimal-quantum-interaction|Minimal Quantum Interaction]]** *is* the source of the dilemma. Read "minimal" as "smallest preserving ensemble statistics" and you get the corridor and its invisibility; read it as "smallest actually sufficient" and you get the outside-corridor route and its empirical exposure. The Map holds this as a live structural fork, not a settled commitment — which is exactly the posture P-Q2 and P-Q3 jointly record.

**[[tenets#^bidirectional-interaction|Bidirectional Interaction]]** asserts that consciousness causally influences the physical world. The Born-preserving efficacy problem is the sharpest internal challenge to that assertion, because it grants single-event causation yet questions whether aggregate-invisible causation is the kind of influence the tenet needs. The [[trilemma-of-selection|trilemma of selection]] secures that *something* must select at a point of genuine indeterminacy — determinism, randomness, and mental causation exhaust the options — but it secures only that selection occurs, not that conscious selection constitutes a channel a physicalist would recognise. That further step is what this problem contests.

The remaining three tenets bound the problem rather than generate it. **[[tenets#^no-many-worlds|No Many Worlds]]** is the precondition that makes selection meaningful at all: only because the Map insists one outcome obtains and the others do not is there anything for consciousness to select. **[[tenets#^occams-limits|Occam's Razor Has Limits]]** is why the Map does not simply take the cheaper exit — denying the outcome gap, embracing branching — to make the dilemma disappear: parsimony is unreliable when knowledge is incomplete, and the question of why *this* outcome, for *this* subject, is one that dissolution does not answer. And **[[tenets#^dualism|Dualism]]** is what keeps the problem from being merely a puzzle in physics: it is because consciousness is held to be irreducible that its causal contribution must be located somewhere the physics does not already account for — and the Born-preserving corridor is the narrowest somewhere there is.

That this challenge is recorded in the register at all, with its confidence bands and its explicit mechanism debt, is the practice the [[evidential-status-discipline|evidential-status discipline]] demands. The honest summary: the Map's interface programme has a worked-out *catalogue* of routes and a precise statement of the crux, and does not yet have the worked *model* that would tell it which route the world takes. Naming that gap as the deepest one is not a weakness in the framework's self-presentation; it is the framework declining to spend confidence it has not earned.
