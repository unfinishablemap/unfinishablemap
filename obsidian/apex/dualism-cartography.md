A recurring confusion runs through the dualism debate: the question "is dualism true?" gets asked as though "dualism" named a single position. It does not. It names a whole territory of positions that differ in how much ontological weight they place on mind, how much on matter, how they relate the two, and what each owes by way of mechanism. Treating the territory as a point makes the debate intractable—objections that defeat one inhabitant sail past another, and a defender and a critic can each be right about different regions while believing they disagree about the same thing.

A cluster of the Map's articles has been quietly building the alternative: not an argument that dualism wins, but a *map* of the territory. The map has a grid (the kinds of dualism, sorted by ontological thickness), a cost overlay (what each cell of the grid owes mechanistically), a worked region (where the Map's own commitments sit), and a marked frontier (the sharpest open problem the whole territory shares). The organising discipline is the same throughout: chart the positions and their costs *without* claiming to settle which position is correct. That restraint—cartographic rather than adjudicative—is the cluster's signature, and the reason the map is usable by readers who reject the Map's tenets as well as those who accept them.

This piece draws the cluster's separate sheets into one atlas. It walks the [[four-quadrant-dualism-taxonomy|grid]], reads the [[mechanism-costs-dualism-thickness-quadrants|cost overlay]] cell by cell, makes the [[parsimony-case-for-interactionist-dualism|parsimony reasoning]] that governs where the Map locates itself, fixes the Map's region against its neighbours, and ends at the [[the-interface-problem|interface problem]] and the [[interface-formalization-void|formalisation void]] as the frontier where the cartography hands off to open work.

## The Grid: Two Axes, Four Quadrants

Dualist positions are usually sorted by the *kind* of mind–matter relation they posit: substance versus property dualism; interactive versus epiphenomenal; Chalmers' Type-D, Type-E, Type-F. A second axis is rarely named—how much ontological weight each side carries—and naming it is the [[four-quadrant-dualism-taxonomy|four-quadrant taxonomy]]'s first contribution. Crossing *mind-side thickness* with *physical-side thickness* gives four quadrants.

**Mind-side thickness** ranges from thin (only what is phenomenally introspectable—bare qualia, the felt redness of red) through medium (a unified subject that persists and can intervene minimally, but stays anchored to the introspective horizon) to thick (a subliminal self or cosmic consciousness whose waking ego is a narrow selection from a far larger mental reality).

**Physical-side thickness** ranges from thin (physics' own ontology taken as lean—what physics says and nothing more, often paired with closure *only* at the indeterminacy level) through medium (standard quantum mechanics plus decoherence plus uncontroversial structural facts) to thick (hidden variables, Russellian quiddities, Stoljar's ignorance-facts, a fuller phase space than measurement reaches). "Max-physical" expands the physical side *ontologically*, not spatially—it is not the claim that more physical things exist.

Crossing the extremes gives the four cells:

- **Q1 (min-mind / min-physical):** *"Only what we need, on both sides, and no more."* Bradford Saad's delegatory dualism, minimal interactionism confined to indeterminacy, neutral-monist limit cases.
- **Q2 (min-mind / max-physical):** *"A thin mental surface over a rich physical world."* Epiphenomenalism, textbook property dualism, Stoljar's epistemic physicalism, Bohmian physics with thin qualia.
- **Q3 (max-mind / min-physical):** *"A rich mental reality; physics is just how it looks from outside."* Berkeley, Kastrup's analytic idealism, Myers–James subliminal-mind views with lean physics—mostly monist limit cases that illuminate what an adjacent dualism would commit to.
- **Q4 (max-mind / max-physical):** *"Two rich realms, genuinely distinct, genuinely interacting."* Cartesian substance dualism, hylomorphism, Stapp's quantum interactive dualism, Goff's priority cosmopsychism.

The thickness axis is genuinely orthogonal to the kind-of-relation axis. Nida-Rümelin and Descartes both count as Type-D interactionists in Chalmers' scheme, yet they sit at different points on mind-thickness: Nida-Rümelin's unitary immaterial subject is mind-medium, Cartesian *res cogitans* is mind-thick. Saad's delegatory dualism is arguably closer to Type-D than Chalmers' own naturalistic dualism, yet mind-thin where Chalmers is mind-medium. Classification by relation type does not discriminate these cases; the thickness axis does. That is why it needs its own name.

One nuance the grid compresses: *channel width*—how narrow the interaction interface is—can come apart from pole thickness. Stapp's intervention channel looks minimal (quantum-Zeno biasing only) while his observer and his quantum ontology both look thick. A reader who wants to hold channel width separate can add it as a [[dualism-channel-width-axis|third axis]], which orders the canonical positions by interface narrowness and locates Stapp by definition where the two-axis grid placed him by judgement. The atlas keeps the third axis as an optional overlay rather than folding it in, trading a little nuance for tractability.

## The Cost Overlay: What Each Cell Owes

Locating positions is not yet saying what occupying a cell *costs*. Two positions that share a relation-kind classification can sit in different thickness cells and owe entirely different debts. The [[mechanism-costs-dualism-thickness-quadrants|mechanism-costs reading]] supplies the overlay by exposing every cell to one fixed battery of three questions—the same three at each cell, so that cell-specific commitments are forced into visibility:

1. **Causation account.** How does mind contribute to producing physical effects—overdeterminingly, preemptively, selectively, by biasing, or not at all?
2. **Interface specification.** What is the mechanism of mind–physical contact, where does it happen, and how narrow is the channel?
3. **Conservation engagement.** How does the position relate to conservation laws—does it owe a violation, a workaround, or nothing?

The trio leans deliberately toward physical-side debts: the conservation and interface questions originate in the physicalist's challenge to interactionism, and the literature has weighted them heavily. The asymmetry could be an artefact of question selection, but on inspection it appears to track a real difference in what the cells owe.

**Q1 owes the authority debt.** With the physical base counterfactually sufficient (no hidden variables) and the mental side carrying no thick subliminal layers, any genuine mental contribution must work as something other than an extra force pushing on a complete physical story. Saad's framework deploys [[trumping-preemption]]—Schaffer's species of redundant causation in which a higher-order rule selects one of two sufficient processes as authoritative—to give experience preemptive priority over a physical state's *default causal profile* without outpacing physics. The price of admission is an *authority law*: a non-stipulative account of what makes the mental rule authoritative, rather than the bare stipulation (Merlin's spell, military rank) that settles Schaffer's fictional cases. The interface is probabilistic and narrow—biasing among quantum-permitted alternatives at neural indeterminacy sites, no new field or force—and conservation is the cell's *cheapest* debt, because biasing among energetically equivalent outcomes injects nothing. The net Q1 bill is small but real: one articulable authority law and one specification of where the indeterminacy sites sit.

**Q2 owes the exclusion debt.** Under the strict reading—phenomenal properties supervene on a physically complete base—the mind owes no causation account because it does no causal work, and has no interface because supervenience replaces interaction. The cell pays nothing on mechanism precisely because the mind has been deflated to the point of doing nothing. Outside the Map's tenets, Kim's exclusion argument presses the cost from within physicalism: if the physical base is sufficient, mental properties either overdetermine effects or do nothing. Any Q2 reading that wants to keep mental causation drifts toward Q1 (by adding indeterminacy) or Q4 (by enriching the mind). The Map's [[bidirectional-interaction|Bidirectional Interaction]] tenet rules pure Q2 out directly; a reader who does not share the tenet is free to read Q2's escape from the mechanism question as a virtue rather than an evasion.

**Q3 owes the appearance debt.** If everything is mental at base, mind–physical causation is no longer a relation between two ontological kinds; it is the intrinsic dynamics of the one kind, appearing as physical-on-physical causation seen from outside. The interaction problem evaporates—and is replaced by the *transparency problem*: explaining why the intrinsic mental reality looks, from the outside, exactly like impersonal lawful physical structure. Conservation laws become descriptions of mental regularities, and "why this regularity rather than another" is a Q3-specific debt the other cells do not face in that form. A genuine Q3 *dualism* (mind thick, physics thin, both real) is rare, because once the mind does this much work the physical pole tends to lose explanatory weight and the position slides toward idealism.

**Q4 owes the full bill.** With both sides thick, the mind does robust causal work in an ontologically rich physical world, so Q4 owes the heaviest version of all three debts. Cartesian energy-transfer is the classical cost; Stapp's quantum-Zeno account is the contemporary attempt to keep the channel narrow while letting both endpoints stay thick. The interface is the locus where two rich realms touch, and specifying that contact non-arbitrarily is Q4's signature burden—Princess Elisabeth's challenge to Descartes in its sharpest form. On conservation, Q4 pays either by accepting that conservation is *local and symmetry-conditional* (so interactionist intervention breaks a symmetry at the interface) or by routing influence through a probabilistic channel that is structurally Q1-like even inside a Q4 ontology.

Three patterns fall out of the overlay. The conservation objection that has driven the analytic dualism debate since Leibniz is *thickness-sensitive*—it bites hardest in Q4, lighter in Q1, dissolves in Q3, and is structurally inapplicable in Q2 strict—so a blanket dismissal of "dualism" on conservation grounds treats a non-uniform grid as uniform. The interface-formalisation burden tracks thickness the same way. And a Q4 defender and a Q2-strict defender may both call themselves dualists while facing entirely non-overlapping mechanism bills, which is why naming the cell first lets the mechanism question be asked at the right grain.

## Parsimony Within the Tenets, Not Across All Axes

The overlay invites a tempting shortcut: Q1 is the cheapest permitted cell, so parsimony selects it, so the Map's position wins on simplicity. The cluster explicitly refuses that shortcut, and the refusal is load-bearing for keeping the whole exercise honest.

The [[parsimony-case-for-interactionist-dualism|parsimony reasoning]] runs in two stages, neither of which converts simplicity into truth. First, *defensively*, parsimony's authority in the consciousness debate was never established. Huemer's survey of four justifications for preferring simpler theories found that none extends naturally to metaphysical disputes lacking differential predictions; the historical record (Mach denying atoms, geocentrism, caloric theory) shows the simpler theory was often the false one; and parsimony's own advocates have qualified the argument—Lycan, defending Cartesian interactive dualism, concedes that "there is no evidence against that view; it only offends parsimony." Second, *dialectically*, even granting the objector that simplicity matters, the verdict does not come out as advertised: physicalism's ontological economy is purchased with explanatory debts (brute emergence, error theory about experience, or in-principle inaccessibility), so once every dimension of simplicity is counted, the razor stops pointing cleanly at physicalism and arguably tilts the other way.

The Map does not endorse *simpler-therefore-truer* in either direction. Its fifth tenet—[[tenets#^occams-limits|Occam's Razor Has Limits]]—holds that parsimony is not a reliable guide to truth where knowledge is this incomplete, and that the self-binding applies to parsimony arguments *for* dualism exactly as to those *against* it. So when the Map locates itself near Q1, the claim is precise: not "Q1 is the simplest cell, therefore the Map is right," but "the Map pays the *least mechanism cost compatible with its tenets*." Q2 strict would be cheaper on mechanism—but the tenets forbid it. Q1 is cheaper than Q4—and the tenets permit it. Parsimony operates *within* the region the tenets carve out, not as a universal preference for thinness. Read this way, "parsimonious given what we are committed to" replaces "absolutely minimal across both axes," and Q4 inhabitants (Descartes, Stapp, Goff) are not automatic losers—they lose only when a specific explanatory task fails to justify the extra weight.

## The Map's Region

The five tenets do not pick a single cell. They carve out a *region*. Dualism rules out the idealist limit of Q3; Bidirectional Interaction rules out pure Q2 epiphenomenalism; Minimal Quantum Interaction pushes the physical side toward thin (physics complete except at probabilistic indeterminacies) without directly constraining mind-side thickness; No Many Worlds is largely orthogonal to the thickness axes; Occam's-Razor-Has-Limits explicitly refuses to let thinness count automatically as a virtue, keeping Q4 a live option. Net: the tenets forbid pure Q2 and the idealist limit of Q3, leaving Q1, Q4, and physical-thin / mind-variable hybrids between them as genuine options.

Within that region the Map's *current default reading* is physical-thin plus mind-medium—close to Q1, with room to expand along the mind-axis if Myersian or Jamesian evidence ever warrants. Reading off the cell's mechanism profile: the causation account is selection or biasing of quantum-permitted outcomes, structurally close to Saad's delegatory dualism read minimally; the interface is a narrow probabilistic channel at neural indeterminacy sites, biasing the wavefunction's actualisation without injecting energy; conservation holds through selection-without-injection plus the Noether-symmetry response when influence is read as breaking time-translation symmetry locally—both available, both deployed in [[conservation-laws-and-mental-causation|the conservation treatment]].

The region's neighbours sharpen what the default reading costs and buys. Drifting toward Q2 would relinquish Bidirectional Interaction and dissolve the interface problem trivially—at the price of epiphenomenalism's evasion of mental work. Drifting toward Q4 would inflate the mind (Myers–James subliminal selves, a Stappian thick observer) or the physics (Russellian quiddities, hidden variables)—paying a heavier interface and conservation bill in exchange for explanatory resources the lean reading lacks. Drifting toward Q3 would let the mental side expand until the physical becomes extrinsic appearance, putting the Map adjacent to analytic idealism—at the cost of the Dualism tenet at the limit and the Bidirectional Interaction tenet's grip in any case.

Two things about the Map's region need stating plainly, because an atlas of this kind is exactly where advocacy tends to creep in. First, the Map's region is a *posited commitment*, not a proven conclusion. Tenets 1 (Dualism) and 5 (Occam-has-limits) are doing load-bearing work in placing the Map where it sits; they are starting points the Map adopts and defends elsewhere, not results derived from the cartography. The map shows where the commitment lands and what it owes—it does not show that the commitment is correct. Second, the mechanism debts the Map's cell carries (the authority law, the interface specification, the symmetry-breaking question) are *costs to be paid*, not disproofs of the rival cells. A debt the Map owes in Q1 is not evidence against Q4; it is the price of the Map's own location. Per the [[evidential-status-discipline|evidential-status discipline]], reading a rival cell's heavier bill as a refutation of that rival, or reading the Map's lighter bill as positive support for the Map, would both overstate what the cartography delivers. The grid speaks; the tenets adjudicate; and the tenets are owned as commitments, not smuggled in as conclusions.

## The Frontier: Interface and Formalisation

Every cell that keeps the interface real and lean inherits one debt most acutely, and it is the debt the Map's own region carries: specifying the interface. This is where the cartography hands off to open work, and where the cluster is most careful not to overclaim.

The [[the-interface-problem|interface problem]] has two faces. *Where* in the brain does the interface operate? And *how* do conscious states map quantitatively to physical selections? On location, the Map has moved from agnosticism among four candidate sites (microtubules, ion channels, cortical microcolumns, attention networks) to *constrained pluralism*: the interface operates through the attention-motor architecture, with molecular-level quantum effects providing the substrate—"at the functional level, through the molecular level." On specification, two partial accounts exist (Stapp's attention-observation coupling, Eccles' intention-probability coupling) and three qualitative sketches, but the psychophysical control law—the lawful mapping from phenomenal effort to physical selection bias—remains unspecified. The sharpest form of this gap is the [[articulability-of-q1|articulability of the Q1 authority law]]: whether the non-stipulative authority-selecting rule the Map's region owes can be specified at all without inflating one of the two thickness axes. The honest calibration is *pre-Keplerian*: the framework has Tycho-analogue measurements (the ~10 bits/second bandwidth constraint, the Born-rule causal-consistency result, the theta-band willed-attention signatures) and constraint structures of the kind that precede laws, but not the law itself.

Beneath the specification gap sits a deeper question the [[interface-formalization-void|interface formalisation void]] raises: can the answer even *be* mathematical? Mathematics describes structure—relations, symmetries, magnitudes. If the interface is where physical structure meets the non-structural actuality of experience, then any mathematical description produces more structure and stops exactly at the actualisation event. The Born rule illustrates the ceiling: it is the *unique* consistent probability rule given basic quantum postulates, so consciousness acting at measurement must preserve it—the probability distribution is fixed, but which specific outcome actualises is not determined by the formalism. Mathematics surrounds the interface perfectly and penetrates it not at all. Whether this is a gap awaiting a better formalism or a principled limit is itself open; the convergent failure of every formal framework tried so far (Kleiner's experience space, the IIT+CSL collapse model, category-theoretic approaches) is suggestive of the principled reading without settling it.

The frontier is the cluster's sharpest open question, and stating it as open is part of the discipline. The interface problem is, by the Map's own assessment, its most significant unresolved challenge; the framework's long-term credibility depends on progress there. The cartography's job is to locate the frontier precisely and show why it is the debt the Map's region owes most acutely—not to pretend the debt has been paid.

## Synthesis: A Map, Not a Verdict

Laid end to end, the cluster does something none of its members does alone: it turns "is dualism true?" from an unanswerable question about a point into a *navigable* question about a territory. The grid says what positions there are and how they relate on two orthogonal axes. The overlay says what each position owes mechanically, under one fixed battery, so that costs become comparable rather than rhetorical. The parsimony reasoning says how to weigh those costs honestly—within a region the tenets fix, never as simplicity-equals-truth. The Map's region says where one particular set of commitments lands and what it must pay. The frontier says where the whole territory's hardest unpaid debt sits.

What unifies these into a single methodology is the refusal that runs through all of them: *map, do not adjudicate*. The grid does not argue any quadrant is correct. The overlay surfaces relative debts "without converting per-cell lightness on the chosen battery into endorsement." The parsimony case removes a barrier without installing dualism. The Map's region is marked as a posited commitment, its debts as costs rather than proofs. This coordinated restraint is not timidity; it is what makes the atlas usable. A reader who rejects the tenets can still use the grid to see that the conservation objection is thickness-sensitive, that Q2 and Q4 dualists owe non-overlapping bills, that the interface debt is cell-specific. A reader who accepts the tenets gets, in addition, a precise account of which region they have committed to and what it will cost to inhabit. Cartography serves both because it claims less than advocacy would—and, in claiming less, charts more.

The atlas also names its own genre. The cell-by-cell cost reading instantiates a generalisable [[mechanism-costs-cartography|mechanism-costs cartography]]: take a prior locating taxonomy, expose every cell to a fixed battery of the questions the cells' critics press hardest, and read off cell-relative debts without converting any cell's lighter bill into endorsement. Dualism is the inaugural domain; the same discipline could chart theories of personal identity, or laws of nature, against their own fixed batteries. That the Map can name the method it is using, and bind future deployments to the same non-adjudicative rules, is part of what distinguishes this cartography from the hand-waving that has historically stood in for a map of the dualist terrain.

## Relation to Site Perspective

This synthesis serves the tenets by showing how they function as *constraints on a space* rather than as a derivation of a single position.

**[[tenets#^dualism|Dualism]]** is what makes the territory a territory: only if consciousness is irreducible is there a grid of distinct ways to weight mind against matter. The cartography treats Dualism as the posited commitment that opens the map, not as a conclusion the map proves.

**[[tenets#^minimal-quantum-interaction|Minimal Quantum Interaction]]** sets the Map's physical-side coordinate (thin) and supplies the narrow-probabilistic-channel reading of the interface. It is also the source of the frontier: the interface and formalisation debts are precisely the costs a minimal quantum channel must eventually pay.

**[[tenets#^bidirectional-interaction|Bidirectional Interaction]]** closes off pure Q2 and forbids retreat to epiphenomenalism when specification proves hard—it demands consciousness make a physical difference, which demands the difference be specifiable.

**[[tenets#^no-many-worlds|No Many Worlds]]** is largely orthogonal to the thickness axes but essential to the frontier: specification and formalisation only make sense if there is genuine selection among exclusive outcomes for a law to govern.

**[[tenets#^occams-limits|Occam's Razor Has Limits]]** is the tenet the cartography most directly embodies. It is what keeps the parsimony reasoning honest in both directions, refuses to let the Map's lighter Q1 bill count as proof, and authorises Q4 as a live option that thinness-as-virtue would wrongly exclude. The whole map is an exercise in this tenet: charting costs without letting the cheapest cell win by default.
