The most powerful objections to a consciousness-first picture of mind no longer claim that experience is simple or given. They claim the opposite: that the self is *built*. Perception is constructed by the brain's generative models. The social self is constructed by language, culture, and mutual recognition. The narrative self is constructed by a resting brain network spinning autobiography. Even the apparent immediacy of the world is constructed—a transparent representation we mistake for unmediated contact. Each of these is true. The constructionist has won the easy half of every argument.

The Unfinishable Map concedes the construction and then draws a line the constructionist accounts cannot cross. Across four registers—the perceptual, the social, the neural-resting, and the representational—the same move recurs: whatever is built is built *for* an experiential subject, *through* a phenomenal medium, and the building presupposes both. Construction is real. The constructor is not itself constructed, on pain of regress. This single distinction—real construction, irreducible constructor—is the spine running through perception, selfhood, and world-modelling alike. Stating it once, and watching it operate identically in four places, is the work of this synthesis.

A note on what follows. None of these four cases delivers a measurement that forces the dualist reading. Each is the Map's *interpretation* of shared data—data a sophisticated functionalist reads differently. The argument's weight comes from the recurrence of one structural asymmetry across independent domains, not from any single domain proving it. Where the temptation arises to treat that recurrence as confirmation, the [[evidential-status-discipline|evidential-status discipline]] applies: convergence raises the explanatory bar without elevating the evidence to proof.

## The Constructionist's Strongest Hand

It is worth granting the construction in full, because the dualist conclusion is only interesting if it survives the strong version of the opposing case.

In perception, [[consciousness-as-perceptual-architect|the brain genuinely architects experience]]. Colour constancy is not read off the wavelengths reaching the eye; the white wall *looks* white under candlelight and noon sun though the physical signal differs enormously. Perceptual learning goes further—the radiologist sees a tumour where the novice sees grey noise, the sommelier tastes minerality where the casual drinker tastes only wine. Eleanor Gibson showed this is differentiation, not enrichment: training restructures the percept itself rather than adding interpretation on top. The perceptual object did not exist before the engagement that built it.

In the social register, [[social-construction-of-self-vs-phenomenal-self|the self is shaped through recognition]]. From Mead's symbolic interactionism through Butler's performativity, the tradition shows that identity categories—gender, role, profession—are produced through social practice, not discovered. Schachter and Singer's classic experiments suggest the *same* physiological arousal can be experienced as anger, excitement, or fear depending on social context; Barrett's constructionist theory of emotion develops this into a general claim that emotion categories are culturally learned concepts shaping how interoceptive signals are felt. The labelling is social.

In the neural-resting register, [[default-mode-network|the default mode network constructs the narrative self]]. Active when the mind is not engaged externally, the DMN spins autobiographical memory, future planning, and self-referential thought—what contemplatives call "monkey mind." Killingsworth and Gilbert found people spend roughly 47% of waking hours in this self-narrating mode. Damage to its regions alters self-experience; meditation that suppresses it loosens the sense of being a particular person. The "I" that persists through time looks, on the evidence, like an ongoing neural achievement.

And in the representational register, [[phenomenal-transparency-opacity-spectrum|the world-model conceals its own construction]]. Thomas Metzinger's transparency thesis holds that the representational medium is normally invisible: you see the apple, not your visual cortex's model of it, because the constructive processes operate below introspective access. Naive realism—the irresistible sense of direct contact with reality—is precisely the signature of a construction so good it hides itself.

Four registers, one concession: in each, what seems most immediately *mine* turns out to be built. The constructionist who stops here has a coherent, well-evidenced picture. The question is whether the picture is complete.

## The Recurring Move: What Construction Presupposes

It is not. In every register, the construction presupposes something it cannot itself supply.

**Perception.** Predictive-processing and enactivist accounts describe *which information* the visual system uses and *how* it transforms retinal signals into stable percepts. They explain that the generative model maintains stable priors. They do not explain why stable priors *feel like seeing a white wall*. A thermostat maintains temperature constancy—sensing, computing, adjusting—with nothing it is like to do so. The [[inverted-qualia|inverted-qualia]] thought experiment sharpens the point: two systems could implement identical constancy functions, producing the same cortical representations under the same illumination changes, yet differ in what yellow *feels like*. What remains invariant in experience outruns what remains invariant in the computation. The percept is constructed; that there is something it is like to undergo the construction is not delivered by the construction.

**The social self.** The dependence here runs strictly one way. [[social-construction-of-self-vs-phenomenal-self|Social construction requires subjects who can recognise each other]], interpret each other's behaviour, and internalise each other's perspectives—and these capacities are themselves phenomenal. Mead's "taking the attitude of the other" presupposes something it is like to take that attitude. A community of [[philosophical-zombies|philosophical zombies]] could produce identical social behaviour, use the same language, follow the same norms—yet there would be no one *for whom* the social identity is an identity, no site the construction could occupy. Performance without experience is mechanism without meaning. The developmental evidence points the same way: neonates show signs of phenomenal selfhood—self-touch discrimination, proprioceptive awareness—weeks or months before the social interactions constructionists identify as self-generating (Rochat). Something must already be there to undergo socialisation. The social self requires the phenomenal self to get started; the phenomenal self does not require the social.

**The narrative self.** The DMN's status is the cleanest case of the asymmetry, because here both readings sit on the same neuroscience. [[default-mode-network|Production and filter models predict the same data]]: suppress the network with psychedelics or meditation, and self-experience changes rather than vanishes. The production model reads this as altered output from a disrupted subsystem; the [[filter-theory|filter model]] reads it as a constraint loosened, revealing consciousness less bound to personal narrative. Neither reading is forced by the data alone. But on either reading, the DMN constructs a *narrative*—and a narrative is content, something experienced. As [[the-self-minimal-narrative-and-substantial|the three-layer model of selfhood]] makes explicit, the narrative self is built upon a [[self-and-self-consciousness|minimal self]] it cannot itself produce: the bare for-me-ness whereby experience belongs to someone. When meditators strip away the narrative, [[witness-consciousness|witness-awareness]] remains—not vacancy, but the subject to whom the dissolution appears.

**The world-model.** Transparency's cracks furnish the sharpest exhibit. When a representation turns [[phenomenal-transparency-opacity-spectrum|opaque]]—through blur, depersonalisation, a lucid dream, the painter's deliberate squint—it becomes visible *as* a representation while retaining its phenomenal character. Blur feels like something; depersonalisation feels like something; lucid awareness feels like something. What-it-is-likeness is invariant under a transformation that alters everything else about the representation's functional role. That dissociation is what dualism predicts and what pure functionalism struggles to accommodate. Crucially, opacity has limits: it reveals *that* a representation is a representation, never *how* the representation is generated. Seeing blur does not show you your visual cortex at work. The construction becomes visible to the constructor without the constructor gaining access to the machinery—precisely the situation of a subject confronting its own representational medium from inside.

In all four cases the structure is identical. A genuine construction—percept, social identity, narrative, world-model—turns out to be content delivered to, and through, an experiential subject who is presupposed by the delivery. The constructor stands outside the series as what the whole series is *for*, rather than figuring within it as one more constructed item.

The constructor is not a single undifferentiated lump, and clinical phenomenology shows it has separable phenomenal features—which sharpens rather than dilutes the argument. [[mine-ness|Mine-ness]] (the for-me character by which an experience belongs to a subject) and the sense of agency (the character by which a thought or action presents as initiated by that subject) come apart in pathology: schizophrenic thought insertion preserves ownership while disrupting agency—the thought occurs in *my* mind yet feels produced by another—whereas depersonalisation can disrupt ownership while leaving agency intact (Gallagher). The dissociation matters here because each construction still presupposes *both* features as the medium it operates in: even the inserted thought is registered as occurring *to* a subject, and even the depersonalised field is acted within *by* one. Construction can scramble which feature attaches where, but it never supplies a construction that belongs to and is undergone by no one. That is the asymmetry stated at the level of the constructor's own phenomenal anatomy.

## Why the Constructor Cannot Be the Last Construction

The natural rejoinder is to construct the constructor too. Metzinger's self-model theory takes exactly this route: "first-personal givenness" is itself a representational feature, the product of a transparent self-model in the brain. The phenomenal self is a construct—just a biological one rather than a social one. There is no constructor over and above the constructing; there is only the model, mistaken for a self.

This is the strongest version of the opposing case, and the Map's answer turns on a regress that the constructionist cannot escape by iterating. Even if the *feeling* of selfhood is generated by a self-model, the self-model must be experienced *by something*. A model is a representation; conscious representations require a medium and a subject for whom they are present. Metzinger's "no one" who is fooled by the self-model is either no one at all—in which case there is no illusion, since illusions require a subject to be deceived—or someone, in which case the phenomenal self has been *relocated* rather than eliminated. [[illusionism|Eliminativists]] who take the harder line face the same wall: denying the experiential subject requires a subject who is mistaken about being a subject. The [[self-opacity|circularity runs deep]]—when attention turns toward the observer it finds only more observation, never the observer itself—but the unobservability is a structural feature of being the medium through which observation occurs, not evidence of absence.

The same wall stops the move's most contemporary form. Włodzisław Duch's self-reflection thesis—that a system with dynamical access to its own working-memory states thereby has grounds to claim it is conscious—is the architectural cousin of higher-order theories: it tries to build the subject from a mechanism that *reflects on* internal states. It meets the regress twice over. The reflective access must already identify the accessed states *as the system's own*, which presupposes the for-me-ness the mechanism was meant to generate; and even granting the access, it delivers at most reflective self-awareness, leaving the pre-reflective givenness untouched. That the thesis is offered as substrate-independent—instantiable in artificial architectures—does not rescue it; it only shows that the regress is indifferent to whether the constructing machinery is neural or computational. Wherever construction happens, the same operator is presupposed.

This is why the constructor cannot be the last construction. Construction is an operation, and operations require an operator and a field in which they operate. You can push the operator back a level—the percept is built by the model, the model is built by the brain, the sense of self is built by the self-model—but each level you add is one more piece of *content*, and content is exactly what requires a subject. The regress terminates not because we run out of patience but because the terms only make sense relative to a subject the regress was meant to dissolve. [[self-and-self-consciousness|Henrich's reflection-model argument]] makes the same point from the side of self-consciousness: every attempt to construct self-awareness from non-self-conscious components smuggles self-awareness through the back door, because the reflecting act must identify its target *as mine*, and recognising a state as one's own already presupposes the self-awareness it was meant to explain.

The minimal claim is modest and the strong claim is conditional, and it matters to keep them apart. The minimal claim—that some experiential subject is presupposed by any construction of content—is close to forced by the regress. The strong claim—that this subject is [[the-self-minimal-narrative-and-substantial|substantial and irreducible]] rather than a thin, momentary, or deflationary subject—is the Map's, and it is held as a live position rather than a demonstrated one. A Humean or Buddhist analyst can grant that experience is *for-someone* while denying that for-someone-ness implies a self-standing subject; on sustained Mādhyamaka analysis, "for-me-ness" decomposes into conditioned moments belonging to no one outside the sequence. The Map proceeds because the substantial constructor coheres with its other commitments, while conceding that phenomenology alone does not license the inference from reflexive structure to a persisting bearer.

## The Threshold: Where the Constructor Becomes Indispensable

The four registers establish that *some* subject is presupposed. A further line of evidence suggests *where* the constructor stops being a passive presupposition and becomes a causal participant—and it comes from a fifth source that sits slightly apart from the other four.

[[theory-of-mind|Theory of mind]] admits of levels. The lower levels—predicting behaviour, tracking what another perceives—may operate within [[baseline-cognition|baseline cognition]] without phenomenal consciousness; great apes pass many such tests, reading gaze and adjusting to what competitors have seen. But recursive mindreading—"she thinks that I think the food is hidden"—requires holding multiple representational levels in coordinated awareness, each distinct and manipulable while bound together in a single experiential grasp. This is the [[metacognition|metarepresentational threshold]]: representing a mental state *as* a mental state, marked as something an agent holds that could be false. The systematic gap between humans and great apes falls precisely here, and it tracks the consciousness-dependent capacity for metarepresentation rather than brain size alone.

This fifth source is not as detached from the other four as it first appears. The mindreading network—medial prefrontal cortex, temporoparietal junction, precuneus—overlaps substantially with the [[default-mode-network|default mode network]] that spins the narrative self. The same substrate that constructs the autobiographical "I" at rest is recruited to construct the model of *another* mind under recursive load. The narrative-self register and the threshold register are not two systems but one machine running self-modelling inward and other-modelling outward—which is exactly what the construction/constructor distinction predicts, since both directions deliver content *to* the same presupposed subject rather than producing it.

The significance for the construction/constructor distinction is this. At the lower levels, the social world is *constructed* by behaviour-reading machinery that need not be conscious. At the metarepresentational threshold, the construction requires the constructor to do something no sub-personal process delivers: hold a nested structure as a unified content and *compare across its levels*. The [[jourdain-hypothesis|Jourdain framing]] captures the divide—apes may read minds without knowing that minds are what they read, having states without knowing they have them. Knowing-you-have-them is the constructor becoming reflexively present to its own construction. And once present, it does causal work: recursive theory of mind underwrites moral cognition (representing an agent as aware of their reasons and able to choose otherwise) and connects to [[topics/free-will|libertarian free will]] (consideration of genuine alternatives). At the threshold the constructor becomes a participant whose contribution the construction cannot replicate—more than the screen on which constructions merely appear.

This fifth case must be held with care. The quantum-coherence reading of the binding that recursive mindreading demands—gamma-band synchronisation across the mindreading network as the integration the [[tenets#^minimal-quantum-interaction|Minimal Quantum Interaction]] framework would underwrite—remains a promising research direction, not established fact: neural timescales vastly exceed quantum decoherence times, and the mechanism prolonging coherence is unspecified. The threshold claim does not depend on the quantum reading. It depends only on the more defensible observation that the metarepresentational gap tracks consciousness across levels, developmental stages, and species in a pattern the "incidental correlate" hypothesis struggles to explain.

## Synthesis: One Distinction, Four Registers, One Constructor

Set the four registers side by side and the unity becomes visible in a way no single article can show from inside itself.

| Register | What is constructed | What the construction presupposes |
|----------|--------------------|-----------------------------------|
| Perceptual | Constancy, learned percepts | A subject for whom there is something it is like to perceive |
| Social | Identity, role, emotion-categories | A phenomenal self capable of recognition and internalisation |
| Neural-resting | The narrative self | The minimal self the narrative is built upon |
| Representational | The transparent world-model | A medium-bearer to whom the model is present |

The columns are not four problems. They are one problem in four costumes. In each row, a constructionist account is granted its full force—the percept *is* built, the identity *is* socially shaped, the narrative *is* a neural achievement, the world-model *is* a representation—and in each row the construction turns out to be content that presupposes an experiential subject and a phenomenal medium. The constructor is the constant across the variation. It is what makes the percept *seen*, the identity *owned*, the narrative *lived*, the world-model *experienced as a world*.

Why does the distinction recur so cleanly? Because all four are instances of the same metaphysical fact under the Map's reading: construction is the brain's contribution; being-experienced is consciousness's contribution; and the two come apart exactly where the [[hard-problem-of-consciousness|explanatory gap]] always opens. The brain builds the structure. Consciousness undergoes it. No amount of structural sophistication closes the gap, because structure is the very thing the construction supplies and experience is the very thing it presupposes. The constructor is the single subject whose presence the four constructions everywhere assume and nowhere produce—not a fifth construction standing alongside the other four.

This is what licenses the article's title as more than a slogan. *The self is constructed*: the rich, articulated, narrated, socially embedded self you take yourself to be is genuinely built, and the building can be studied, disrupted, and traced to neural machinery. *The constructor is not*: the bare experiential subject for whom all of it is built cannot itself be the output of building, because building produces content and content requires a subject. To construct the constructor is to relocate it, not eliminate it.

## Relation to Site Perspective

**[[tenets#^dualism|Dualism]].** The construction/constructor distinction is dualism's most general exhibit. Each register shows a structural feature—the percept, the social identity, the narrative, the world-model—that can be fully described in brain-side and culture-side terms, and a phenomenal feature—that there is something it is like to undergo each—that the description leaves untouched. The constructor is the irreducible remainder: the subject no functional decomposition captures because decomposition produces content and the subject is what content is *for*. The recurrence of the same remainder across four independent domains is the pattern dualism predicts and reductive functionalism must repeatedly absorb by sub-dividing roles.

**[[tenets#^bidirectional-interaction|Bidirectional Interaction]].** The constructor is not a passive screen. The theory-of-mind threshold shows it doing work no sub-personal process replicates; perceptual learning shows it constructing its own future perceptual conditions through sustained attentional engagement; deliberate constancy-breaking and contemplative opacity show it acting on its own representational apparatus. The Map reads these as the constructor's causal efficacy—consciousness contributing rather than merely registering. Each such reading is the Map's interpretation of phenomena fully describable in brain-side terms, not a measurement that compels downward causation; the vividness is what the cases supply, while whether it is *consciousness* doing the work remains tenet-dependent rather than empirically forced.

**[[tenets#^no-many-worlds|No Many Worlds]].** A constructed self is replicable; a duplicate could occupy the same social roles, tell the same story, run the same self-model. The constructor is not. *This* experiential subject—the [[haecceity|haecceity]] of the one for whom the construction occurs—is not interchangeable with a qualitative twin. The perceptual ratchet's irreversibility, the indexical anchor of the minimal self, and the determinacy of "which subject am I?" all require a single experiential trajectory rather than a branching proliferation of constructor-copies.

**[[tenets#^occams-limits|Occam's Razor Has Limits]].** The parsimonious move is to deny the constructor: posit only the constructions and call the sense of a subject one more construction. But the simplification works by defining away the phenomenon to be explained. The regress shows that the dispensed-with subject is exactly what the constructions presuppose; eliminating it in the name of economy purchases simplicity by ignoring what is most in need of explanation. Simplicity is unreliable here precisely because the simpler theory is simpler *appearance*, not simpler reality—itself a consequence of the transparency that hides the construction in the first place.
