## Pessimistic Analysis Summary

### Critical Issues Found

1. **Wrong first author — Bell-nonlocality 2025 citation (garbled conflation).** Body §"All-or-Nothing Selection" and reference 9 attributed the 2025 Nature Communications paper "Test of the physical significance of Bell non-locality" to **"Hensen, B. et al. (2025)"**. The DOI (s41467-025-59247-7) and title are correct, but the actual authors are **Carlos Vieira, R. Ramanathan & A. Cabello** (ADS bibcode 2025NatCo..16.4390V; Nat Commun 16:4390). "Hensen" is a *different* famous loophole-free-Bell-test physicist (Hensen et al. 2015, Delft) — a textbook familiar-finding-under-wrong-metadata conflation. The underlying claim (partial retrocausal influence is excluded → all-or-nothing) IS supported by the real paper. **Resolution**: corrected first author to Vieira, Ramanathan & Cabello in both body and reference; added volume 16, article 4390.

2. **Misattributed/fabricated direct quote — Cramer 1986.** The §"Transaction Selection" subsection placed the phrase *"does not happen at any specific point in time, but occurs along the whole transaction"* in quotation marks attributed to **(Cramer, 1986)**. Verified against the live literature: this wording is **Wikipedia's general description of the transactional interpretation**, explicitly *not* presented as a Cramer verbatim quote. Quoting it as Cramer's own words is a fabricated-quote defect. **Resolution**: de-quoted and paraphrased — "on the transactional reading the collapse is atemporal, occurring along the whole transaction rather than at any specific moment (Cramer, 1986)" — preserving the substantive (and accurate) point while removing the false verbatim attribution.

3. **Wrong co-author — Aharonov free-will citation.** Body and reference 2 cited "Accommodating retrocausality with free will" as **Aharonov, Cohen & Elitzur (2015)**. The actual third author is **Tomer Shushi** (arXiv:1512.06689; published in *Quanta* 5(1):53-60, 2015). First author (Aharonov) and arXiv ID were correct; the named third author was wrong. **Resolution**: corrected Elitzur → Shushi in body and reference; added the *Quanta* journal-of-record citation.
