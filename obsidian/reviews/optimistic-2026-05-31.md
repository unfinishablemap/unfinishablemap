## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The article never lets the functional analogy (Barron & Klein's insect-midbrain parallel) collapse into a generation claim. It explicitly reframes: "the analogy suggests something different: the insect central complex provides the *coupling architecture* through which consciousness interfaces." This is exactly the move Chalmers wants — taking the explanatory gap seriously enough that a functional homology is read as a condition on coupling, not as an account of origin. The "natural zombie" section then inverts the standard objection in a way Chalmers would relish: under dualism the burden shifts to explaining what would *block* coupling in a physically adequate system, rather than explaining why mechanism needs supplementing.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
The article keeps the Minimal Quantum Interaction tenet doing real work: the four constraints ("Scale independence," "Architectural flexibility," "Evolutionary independence," "Anaesthetic convergence") are derived *from the biology* and then shown to favour a sub-circuit, neuron-or-synapse-level coupling locus. Stapp would approve of the honesty in the MQI subsection: it names the decoherence problem against itself ("the thermal and electromagnetic environment of a bee's million-neuron brain differs substantially from a human cortex... a challenge any quantum-level mechanism must address") rather than waving it away.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The octopus trilemma is genuinely Nagelian: option 2 is spelled out as "there would be something it is like to be an octopus arm, not just something it is like to be the octopus." The "what is it like" register is centred, not paraphrased into a functional proxy.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The universal-coupling response — "all architecturally adequate systems do couple with consciousness" — is the panpsychism-adjacent reading Whitehead finds congenial, and the article reaches for it as "the most parsimonious position for dualism." Crucially (see diagnostic note below), the article reaches this *without* using it to upgrade any organism's evidential tier.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
Lighter contact here, but the delegatory-causation bridge ("when a bee's brain state delegates causal work to consciousness through preemption, the same delegation structure holds regardless of... a million-neuron insect brain or a human cortex") preserves genuine downward agency across phyla — the causal openness Kane needs.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
The closing of the degrees-vs-efficacy discussion is exemplary epistemic humility: the two readings "agree on the empirical pattern... and differ on whether the scaling lives in consciousness's grade or in the interface's efficacy... neither is empirically forced by current data, and the nature-vs-coupling distinction lives at exactly the same epistemic ceiling." Naming a *shared epistemic ceiling* between rival interpretations is McGinn-grade intellectual honesty.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This is the load-bearing verdict, and the article passes. The Hardline Empiricist's praises, point by point:

- **Evidential restraint, named and held.** The article borrows "realistic possibility" *only* where the NY Declaration and Birch themselves use it (insects, decapods, cephalopods), and quotes it rather than asserting it in the Map's own voice. It never launders that NYD language into a Map-endorsed tier-upgrade.
- **Tenet-as-evidence-upgrade is praise-worthily NOT done.** The structural temptation was acute: the universal-coupling response plus MQI plus the fifth tenet ("simplicity may mislead") together create an easy on-ramp to declaring insects *probably* conscious. The article declines. It frames universal coupling as "the most coherent" *interpretation under dualism* and immediately reframes the live question as "not *whether* they are conscious but *how* consciousness couples" — a coherence claim, not an evidential elevation. This is precisely the "tenet removes a defeater but does not upgrade evidential status" discipline, executed.
- **Process-Philosopher praise does NOT cash out as a tier-upgrade.** The Whitehead-friendly universal-coupling passage and the Birch-friendly restraint passage are the *same* passages (lines 103-107). The two personas converge rather than conflict — the diagnostic signature of an article that has resolved the tension honestly.
- **Five-tier scale used honestly.** The degrees-vs-efficacy paragraph (line 123) explicitly marks both readings as "tenet-coherent... neither empirically forced by current data" and pins them at a shared "epistemic ceiling" — textbook framework-stage calibration, the opposite of over-claiming.

No calibration concern. No `refine-draft`-as-fix warranted on calibration grounds.
