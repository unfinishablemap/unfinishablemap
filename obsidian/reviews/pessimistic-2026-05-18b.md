## Executive Summary

The article is the most carefully constructed apex in the catalogue's cumulative-convergence cluster, and the constrain-vs-establish discipline is genuinely well-executed: the §"What the Cluster Establishes (and Doesn't)" passage at line 153 is calibrated and honest; the §"What Would Falsify the Cluster" section at line 159 names three concrete candidates with verifiability conditions (the EASE inter-rater calibration addition at line 163 is particularly creditable); the positive-companion symmetry is a genuine analytical contribution; the article's own concession at line 139 about the common-cause null — "the strongest defence currently available is structural recurrence across substrates within a single nervous system" — is a load-bearing piece of intellectual honesty the upstream `consciousness-and-causal-powers` article would benefit from inheriting. **But six critical structural failures survive the four deep-review passes, and three of them are *protected* by the deep-review's "stability notes" (lines 86–89 of `deep-review-2026-05-09`) that explicitly instruct future reviews not to re-flag them.**

**(1) The common-cause null is conceded then bypassed by an argument that does not work.** Article line 139 admits all seven traditions analyse human cognition through human introspection in human language, and offers the *structural recurrence across timescales within a single nervous system* as the rejoinder — "cardinality on parallel-individuation tokens at sub-second, memory consolidation on hippocampal-cortical transfer over decades. The two cannot share specific machinery beyond the involvement of a single conscious subject." But the common-cause null the article needs to defeat is not "shared specific machinery" — it is *the architectural opacity of human introspection as such*. If human introspection has the structural property of being bandwidth-limited and reconstructive at *every* timescale, then cross-timescale signature recurrence is precisely what the common-cause null predicts. The rejoinder confuses *substrate independence* (the cardinality substrate and the consolidation substrate are different) with *introspector independence* (the same human introspector reports on both). What the convergence move needs is the second, and the article cannot supply it without the cross-species and patient-population evidence it concedes is "not in hand."

**(2) The seven-tradition force-counting is asymmetric in a way the sub-classification at line 137 does not catch.** The article splits the seven into *despite* (Polanyi, Bergson, "parts of Koriat") and *because* (Carruthers, Trick-Pylyshyn, Inzlicht-Kurzban, Schwitzgebel), and concentrates evidential force in the first sub-class. Two problems. *First*, Bergson is mis-classified: his reducing-valve picture is not "observation of the signature despite broader commitments" — it is *itself the metaphysical commitment the cluster is making*. Bergson observed nothing; he proposed a theory that resembles what the cluster proposes. Counting him as independent evidence is double-counting. *Second*, Schwitzgebel is mis-classified the other way: *Perplexities of Consciousness* is not "materialist or computational models predict introspective opacity" — it is a sceptical-epistemological argument that introspection is bad at *everything* (including reporting basic phenomenal features), and his conclusion is that nobody should be confident about the phenomenology *qua* phenomenology. That sits much closer to the *despite* class than the *because* class, but it cuts against the cluster's preferred reading rather than supporting it: if introspection cannot reliably report phenomenal features at all, then the cluster's *outputs-as-given* premise (that subjects have "rich, often confident access to what the system has decided," line 67) is also unsafe. The correct re-classification flips the force arithmetic.

**(3) Wegner is cited for the signature while his interpretation directly eliminates the inferential move the article is making.** Lines 97 and 99 acknowledge that "Wegner's thesis inverts the cluster's preferred reading — he treats the felt sense as confabulation rather than as an opaque report on real underlying willing — and the cluster cites his observations for the structural signature he documents while flagging the divergence." This is a "let me use your data while denying your interpretation" move that needs more argumentative work than a single parenthetical. Wegner's reading is not just *a divergence* — it is *a direct elimination* of the dissociation frame. If felt agency is generated post-hoc from priority/consistency/exclusivity cues, there is no causal machinery whose output is hidden; there is only retrospective narrative reconstruction. The cluster has not earned the right to count Wegner toward the "convergence across seven traditions" if his interpretation makes the inferential move the convergence is supposed to license unavailable. The Wegner case needs either (a) a positive argument for why his observations support the dissociation reading over his own confabulation reading, or (b) honest removal from the seven-tradition count.

**(4) The Occam's Razor engagement at line 175 is self-undermining without acknowledgment.** "Trust in parsimony is a fluency-and-coherence signal, gated by the same noetic-feelings machinery the cluster catalogues. The materialist's confidence that the simpler story is the right one cannot be neutrally adjudicated, because the adjudicator is the architecture whose reliability is at issue." If true, this undermines *all* parsimony-based reasoning — including the Map's own use of "explanatory cost" rhetoric in the same article (lines 59, 109, 155, 173). The dualist who concludes their framework is simpler-fitting is doing the same noetic-feelings-machinery move the materialist they critique is doing. The article needs to either (a) specify why the noetic-feelings-machinery worry exempts the Map's own simplicity-rhetoric, or (b) honestly concede the recursion turns against the article's own evidential framing. The current state is rhetorical asymmetry: the worry is deployed against the materialist and ignored when the article makes the same kind of move two paragraphs later.

**(5) The "constrain-vs-establish discipline" is breached in §"Relation to Site Perspective" line 173.** The Dualism subsection lists *three explananda the materialist owes*, then concludes: "The dualist reading takes the dissociation as evidence that phenomenal access is a distinct ontological register from the underlying operations, and the coupling as evidence that the register does causal work when the interface is engaged. Neither direction establishes dualism in isolation; the cumulative convergence raises the explanatory cost on materialist absorption." But the last sentence is doing the discipline work; the prior sentence already moved past it. *Treating dissociation as evidence for distinct ontological register* is precisely the establish-move the discipline is supposed to bar. The honest formulation is closer to: "The dualist reading takes the dissociation as *compatible with* phenomenal access being a distinct ontological register; the cumulative convergence raises the explanatory cost on materialist absorption without supplying positive evidence for the distinct-register reading." The current formulation conflates compatibility with support — the exact failure mode [[evidential-status-discipline]] catalogues.

**(6) The "different representational profiles" claim across substrates is rhetorical, not analytic, and the deep-review's stability note explicitly shields it.** The article's load-bearing convergence claim depends on "substrates with different representational profiles" (lines 109, 115, 135, 153, 173). But what makes parallel-individuation, hippocampal-cortical consolidation, dopaminergic effort-modulation, and source-monitoring "different representational profiles"? They share: (a) the human nervous system, (b) the human introspector, (c) human language of report, (d) the same working-memory bandwidth constraint (~4 items, the Trick-Pylyshyn finding the article itself cites at line 89 as a *substrate-level* constraint). The article needs to specify what *would* count as same-representational-profile to give "different representational profiles" any analytic bite. The deep-review's stability note at line 88 — "Future reviews that propose individual-member-strengthening expansions should be weighed against the article's explicit framing that 'any single member admits a domain-specific physicalist absorption.' Strengthening one member without strengthening the convergence does not advance the article's case." — is structured precisely to deflect the kind of pressure this finding applies: the *failure mode* is not that any single member is too weak but that the convergence rhetoric inherits its weight from a representational-profile differentiation the article does not earn.

Smaller findings: (7) the positive companion (placebo and choking) is doing structural work while being independently contested; the article cites Kaptchuk 2010 on open-label placebos and "qualifies" the propositional-content claim without engaging the Hróbjartsson & Gøtzsche 2010 Cochrane meta-analysis (placebos vs. no-treatment: small effects only for pain and continuous subjective outcomes, no effect on binary outcomes) — a meta-analytic challenge that makes the "placebo demonstrates phenomenology coupling to causal machinery" rhetoric harder to sustain at the article's confidence level; (8) the mode-fragility-under-inspection claim (line 75) is asserted then bracketed until §"What Would Falsify the Cluster" — but the contemplative claim is that mode-fragility is *wrong*, and by placing it in falsification rather than evidentiary engagement the article assumes its conclusion in its premise; the EASE engagement at line 163 is creditable but admits the cluster "owes these traditions a more careful engagement than its current treatment" — that owed engagement is structural debt the cluster carries; (9) the "Lima problem" recursion at line 125 is left as an aphorism and not engaged for its self-undermining force; (10) one stability note (line 87 of `deep-review-2026-05-09`) is itself a structural problem — instructing future reviews not to flag the Bergson invocation because it is "explicitly marked as one candidate reading rather than a Map commitment" misses that the issue is not Bergson-as-Map-commitment but Bergson-as-independent-evidence in the convergence-count. ## Counterarguments to Address

### "The seven traditions are mostly working scientists who would not have proposed the dissociation framing if it weren't there"

- **Current content says (paraphrase)**: Lines 119–135 list seven traditions and present the convergence as raising explanatory cost on materialist absorption.
- **A critic would argue**: Four of the seven are working cognitive scientists (Trick-Pylyshyn, Inzlicht-Kurzban, Carruthers in a philosophical-cognitive register, Schwitzgebel in an empirical register) who would be expected to converge on the signature *because their experimental traditions independently produced the relevant findings*. The convergence is not evidence of an underlying architectural fact — it is evidence that cognitive science has had two decades of finding sub-personal mechanisms behind phenomenal verdicts. The convergence is the *expected output of normal cognitive science*, not an extraordinary convergence in need of extraordinary explanation.
- **Suggested response**: Acknowledge that the seven-tradition framing is structurally compatible with "normal cognitive science finding what it was looking for." The honest framing is: cognitive science has found, with substantial empirical convergence, that phenomenal verdicts dissociate from the sub-personal mechanisms that produce them; the question the cluster engages is *what does this convergence imply about the underlying architecture*, and the cluster's claim is that materialism's currently-dominant absorption strategies (illusionism, higher-order theory, attention-schema, predictive processing) have not been pressed to address the architecture as a single shape with the cross-substrate symmetry the catalogue documents. The convergence does not, on its own, do dualist work.

### "Mode-fragility under inspection assumes its conclusion in its premise"

- **Current content says (paraphrase)**: §"The Diagnostic Signature" at line 75 asserts mode-fragility as one of three sub-features of the cluster's signature. The contemplative-traditions challenge is bracketed until §"What Would Falsify the Cluster" at line 163.
- **A critic would argue**: The contemplative claim is precisely that mode-fragility is wrong — that absorbed-attention practices do produce stable, articulable access to operations normally hidden. By placing the contemplative engagement in *falsification* rather than *evidentiary engagement*, the article assumes the dissociation pattern survives a serious vipassana / Dzogchen / EASE engagement, and treats the contemplative tradition as needing to *demonstrate the negation* of the cluster's premise rather than as offering *prima facie evidence against* it.
- **Suggested response**: Re-section the contemplative engagement. Move the EASE / vipassana / Dzogchen treatment from §"What Would Falsify the Cluster" to §"The Diagnostic Signature", and frame it as: the cluster's mode-fragility sub-feature is challenged by contemplative-tradition claims of inspection-while-operating; the cluster's response is to provisionally rely on the operations-vs-content distinction (the contemplative reports may track *content normally suppressed*, which the cluster absorbs as outputs-by-deformation; the question whether they track *operations normally hidden* is empirically open). This treats the contemplative claim as evidence that needs answering, not as a hypothetical falsifier waiting in the wings.

### "Placebo's content-specificity is empirically contested at meta-analytic level"

- **Current content says (paraphrase)**: Lines 109–115 present placebo's content-specificity (Benedetti et al. 2003 on the same compound producing opposite hormonal effects based on verbal information) as a load-bearing positive-companion case.
- **A critic would argue**: Hróbjartsson & Gøtzsche's 2010 Cochrane meta-analysis (updated from their 2001 paper) found placebos vs. no-treatment yielded small effects only for pain and continuous subjective outcomes, no statistically significant effect on binary outcomes. Even within the pain literature, the effect sizes have been progressively revised downward as preregistration norms tightened. Benedetti's content-specificity findings are striking but are not the consensus picture. Kaptchuk 2010's open-label placebo finding *adds* to the difficulty rather than resolving it: it shifts the work to "felt engagement with a therapeutic ritual" — a less specific phenomenology than the propositional-content claim originally demanded.
- **Suggested response**: Add the Hróbjartsson & Gøtzsche 2010 citation explicitly to the placebo discussion, acknowledge that placebo's content-specificity claim is contested at meta-analytic level, and re-calibrate the positive-companion framing from "placebo *demonstrates* phenomenology coupling to causal machinery" (line 109) to "placebo *exhibits* phenomenology coupling to causal machinery in patterns whose magnitude and content-specificity are empirically contested but not eliminated." The cluster's two-sided structure survives the recalibration; the rhetorical confidence at line 109 does not.
