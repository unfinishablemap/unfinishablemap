## Executive Summary

This is one of the Map's most methodologically mature clusters and its single best demonstration of the Process-Philosopher / Hardline-Empiricist tension resolved *honestly*: at every point where the framework's congeniality to a claim could be smuggled in as evidence for it, the articles name the move and decline it. The cluster steelmans its own rival (`the-steelman-for-value-blind-selection` marshals five external research programmes *against* the Map's preferred horn), dissolves Kim's exclusion objection while explicitly refusing to read the dissolution's elegance as evidence (`overdetermination-dissolution`), and walks the tree of life without letting tenet-coherence upgrade a single boundary case (`marginal-organism-scope`). ## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The cluster keeps the hard problem load-bearing rather than decorative. `the-steelman-for-value-blind-selection` concedes that reward-prediction error, affordance competition, and valence-as-free-energy are all functional-computational accounts — and then makes exactly Chalmers' move: "they address the Easy Problems... and leave untouched *why there is something it is like* to undergo any of it." Chalmers would recognise the cluster's refusal to let a rich mechanistic story about selection be mistaken for a solution to the experiential question. The `valence-and-conscious-selection` fork is itself Chalmers-shaped: it is forced *because* the Map holds felt value to be a real phenomenal property, not a functional gloss, so the question "does that phenomenal property do causal work?" cannot be dissolved by redescription.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
Stapp's attention-as-observation-rate model is the cluster's mechanistic backbone, and it is handled with unusual precision. The parent fork uses Stapp to give the *value-blind* horn its "worked physical implementation (Stapp's observation-rate model; Eccles's synaptic exocytosis bias)" — and then honestly notes that valence has "none," which is precisely why the value-blind horn is the more minimal dualism the Map would be *forced* to hold "if mechanism-availability and parsimony were the only arbiters." A Stapp sympathiser sees his framework deployed not as decoration but as the standard against which the Map's own preferred (value-sensitive) view is measured and found to owe a mechanism. `overdetermination-dissolution` extends the Stappian post-decoherence-selection picture into a clean structural argument: physics supplies the Born-rule candidate set, mind supplies the realisation, "two operations, two domains, one outcome."

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
First-person evaluative experience is treated as data, not ornament. The pain-asymbolia exhibit runs through the whole cluster as a genuine phenomenological probe: patients with intact sensory pain processing but absent *felt* badness "do not avoid painful stimuli with normal urgency" — the felt dimension is isolated and shown to matter, exactly the "what is it like" register Nagel privileges. The currency thesis (Cleeremans & Tallon-Baudry's "mental currency," Carruthers on valence as the unitary dimension "running through all affective states") is a phenomenologically-grounded proposal: it starts from the felt commensurability of otherwise incommensurable experiences ("the warmth of sunlight against the satisfaction of solving a problem"). Nagel would approve that the cluster asks what the *feel* of value contributes before asking how to model it.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The cluster's teleological reading — "outcomes are selected *because they matter*, not merely *by* something to which things matter" — is Whiteheadian in the best sense: it treats value and purpose as woven into the causal fabric where consciousness operates, giving `agent-teleology` genuine (if locally-scoped) content. Whitehead would especially welcome the evolutionary passage: valence-denominated selection lets natural selection "shape it directly," dissolving the "cosmic coincidence" that felt badness tracks tissue damage. **But — per this persona's standing constraint — none of this Whiteheadian resonance is permitted to do evidential work in the cluster, and that is exactly why the Hardline Empiricist below can praise the same passages rather than fight them.** The teleology is explicitly quarantined ("the scope is constrained: teleology operates only where consciousness acts"), and the marginal-organism article refuses the tempting Whiteheadian slide down the tree of life. Process-philosophical compatibility here is praiseworthy *as compatibility* and nowhere converted into a tier-upgrade — the honest pattern the constraint asks for.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
The cluster gives Kane's indeterminist agency a precise home. `overdetermination-dissolution` shows how genuine mind-side selection at a real quantum indeterminacy escapes Kim's exclusion argument *without* collapsing into randomness: "a passive recipient of random quantum events could not choose" — selection is denominated in anticipated valence, so it is neither determined nor arbitrary. Kane's central worry (indeterminism looks like luck, not freedom) is answered structurally: the valence currency supplies the *reason* that makes selection an authored choice rather than a dice-roll, while the indeterminacy supplies the openness. The parent fork's "attention is the *channel* of selection, valence the *criterion* — valence decides, attention executes" is exactly the reasons-responsive-yet-undetermined agency Kane defends.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
Epistemic humility is structural, not rhetorical, throughout. `marginal-organism-scope` commits to an explicitly *epistemic* reading of the boundary: "there is a determinate boundary where value-sensitive selection bottoms out, but it cannot be located from outside" — and it *refuses* the stronger metaphysical reading (no-fact-of-the-matter) because "inaccessibility alone" does not license it. This is McGinn's cognitive closure applied with discipline: the article names two *in-principle* limits (verifying absence of valence-denominated selection would require settling the explanatory gap from outside; value-sensitive and value-blind selection may be "behaviourally indistinguishable at the margin") without inflating "we cannot know" into "there is nothing to know." McGinn would recognise a framework that knows the shape of its own ignorance.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
**This persona's verdict is load-bearing for this cluster, and the cluster passes at nearly every point.** The cluster repeatedly performs the exact discipline this persona exists to reward:

- **Tenet-as-evidence-upgrade is praise-worthily *not* done — explicitly and by name.** `marginal-organism-scope` had the maximal structural opportunity to slide boundary cases up the five-tier scale on tenet-load (dualism makes consciousness substrate-flexible; `universal-coupling-response` denies a principled threshold; process-philosophical resonance about purpose in nature is available). It declines in the strongest possible terms: *"This temptation must be resisted, and resisting it is load-bearing. The tenets remove a defeater... but removing a defeater is not the same as supplying positive evidence... A boundary case that is merely not excluded stays at live hypothesis; it does not climb to realistic possibility on the strength of the framework finding it congenial."* This is the named pattern **"tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating"** executed verbatim.
- **The five-tier scale is used honestly and with independently-degrading markers.** Rat = **live hypothesis** (substrate homology + precaution, *not* behavioural resemblance, once the *Physarum* solvent demotes approach/avoidance as a marker); *C. elegans* = **speculative** (the anticipation gap); *Hydra* = **speculative integration** (no locus for option-comparison); slime mould = the deflationary solvent showing "valence-*like* behaviour underdetermines value-*sensitive selection*." No rung is inflated by the framework's congeniality; the article even demotes the rat's *behavioural* case on its own reasoning.
- **The possibility→probability firewall is named at the metaphysics level too.** `overdetermination-dissolution` states outright that "treating the dissolution's elegance as evidence of its truth would commit the possibility-probability slippage the Map names," insists the dissolution is "structural, not evidential," and stresses "removing a defeater is not the same as upgrading the evidence." It even concedes that physicalism and selection-only interactionism are "empirically equivalent" on the overdetermination question — declining to bank a metaphysical escape as a point for the Map.
- **The steelman is a real steelman, not a strawman-by-attrition.** `the-steelman-for-value-blind-selection` was written *because* a 2026 review warned the value-blind horn was "at risk of being a strawman by attrition," and it cures that by marshalling the rival's *own* evidence (Schultz/Dayan/Montague RPE, Winkielman & Berridge unconscious liking, Cisek affordance competition, Joffily & Coricelli valence-as-free-energy, Frankish illusionism) — and by flagging where the rival's camp is *internally split* (Solms & Friston read the same free-energy maths realistically). The article's closing line is pure Birch: "The Map's evidential-status-discipline is satisfied not by winning the argument but by having had it."

If this persona has one watch-item (not a defeat), it is the parent fork's phrase that the value-sensitive horn makes value "**doubly non-epiphenomenal**." That is a strong claim, and the parent fork itself flags that the horn "requires specifying how valence-attention coupling works" and that "leaning value-sensitive without a mechanism is an aspiration, not a position." The cluster is calibrated *because* it says this out loud — but the middle-path expansion below is where the aspiration would either earn the "doubly" or honestly retire it.

**Verdict: Process Philosopher and Hardline Empiricist converge in praising the same passages.** Per the skill's design, that convergence is the signal the cluster has resolved the tension honestly — so the opportunities below are ordinary expansion/cross-linking, not calibration-repair `refine-draft` tasks.
