# Adversarial Peer-Review Audit: "Disguised Property Dualism" (The Unfinishable Map)

## TL;DR

"Disguised property dualism" is a genuinely defensible diagnostic — the underlying pattern it names (a nominally-monist theory doing property-dualist work while disavowing the label) is a real, decades-old debate, not a site invention, and the article is unusually self-aware about its own limits. But it earns a **REVISE-HARD** verdict: condition (1) equivocates between "descriptions/geometries/aspects" and "property-types," which is precisely the epistemic-vs-ontological distinction physicalists insist on and which the diagnostic must not simply help itself to; the article co-opts Friston, Wiese & Hobson toward a reading their paper explicitly forecloses ("the dual information geometry itself does not entail property dualism"); the load-bearing companion page misattributes a blogger's paraphrase ("mutually reductive") to Friston as a verbatim quote; and a quote-boundary inflation ("exactly the same swarm of extra-logical principles") puts the article's own intensifier inside Pautz's quotation marks. Metadata is clean across the board and no citation is fabricated.

## FIVE-DIMENSION ANALYSIS

### 1. Logical soundness / question-begging

The three-condition diagnostic is better engineered than most constitutional-attractor artifacts, but it has one load-bearing flaw and one honestly-confessed structural asymmetry.

**The load-bearing flaw is tenet leakage inside condition (1).** Condition (1) is triggered by a theory introducing "two descriptions, geometries, aspects, or property-types that it cannot reduce to one another." That disjunction silently fuses two categories the physicalist keeps rigidly apart: *descriptions/perspectives* (epistemic) and *property-types* (ontological). The entire physicalist reply to the disguised-dualism charge is that positing two irreducible descriptions is not positing two irreducible properties. By writing the epistemic and ontological readings into a single condition, the diagnostic pre-loads the conclusion it is supposed to earn — this is a textbook epistemic-to-metaphysical slide, embedded in the criterion itself rather than committed in the inference. The article never isolates or defends the move from "two descriptions" to "two structures doing ontological work."

**The confessed asymmetry is the self-application defence.** To its credit, the article turns the diagnostic on the Map's own bi-aspectual ontology and concedes that conditions (1) and (3) appear to hold for it too, escaping only via condition (2). Its defence — that condition (3) targets *asserted* unification, so a theory (like the Map's) that asserts no reduction faces a vacuous condition — is a real distinction, but a thin one, and the article says so: "a critic who rejects the claim-versus-vacuous-condition distinction ... will reasonably count the tool as a scoring device rather than a metaphysical discovery." This is confession-without-full-correction: the asymmetry is disclosed but the diagnostic's reputational payload (that the disguised variants' "parsimony advantage is thinner than advertised") continues to ride on it.

**On the constrain-vs-establish gate the article is mostly clean.** It explicitly disclaims the establishing reading: "The tool sharpens a rhetorical pattern ... rather than delivering a metaphysical verdict. Its utility is philosophical hygiene, not demonstration," and "The diagnostic does not prove that the disguised variants are metaphysically equivalent to dualism." This is the correct register. The problem is that the companion page, predictive-processing-and-dualism, abandons that discipline — "once unpacked, Markovian monism turns out to be covertly dualist" and "the position is the Map's paradigm case of disguised property dualism" are establishing-register claims that the concept page's own hedges disown.

**Falsifiability** is partially secured by the third "showing" standard (a progressive research programme exhibiting accumulating unification lets a theory exit condition (3)). This is a real improvement over an unfalsifiable criterion. But the exit bar — "progressive unification" — is admittedly stance-sensitive ("reasonable readers may disagree"), so the diagnostic is heuristic, not a sharp test. It does not automatically convict every rich physicalism, but the analyst's discretion over "enough progressive unification" is where any residual question-begging would re-enter.

**The property-vs-substance equivocation** flagged in the brief is real but subtle. The article treats property dualism as a physicalist embarrassment and its own view as overt "bi-aspectual dualism," and it does keep the two conceptually distinct in the "overt property dualism" section. The equivocation is not in conflating property and substance dualism directly; it is in using "disguised *property* dualism" as the charge while the Map's own alternative is an aspect/property dualism plus a substance-dualist interface — so the rhetorical force of "you're secretly a property dualist" is doing work for a site whose own commitment is to the very thing being used as the embarrassing terminus. Note that the canonical historical exemplar the article names — Searle's biological naturalism — is precisely a case where a naturalist denies being a property dualist while conceding consciousness is "ontologically irreducible" (Searle, J.R. (2002), "Why I Am Not a Property Dualist," *Journal of Consciousness Studies* 9(12):57–64, which characterizes property dualism as holding that "any conscious animal, such as a human being, will have two sorts of properties, mental properties and physical properties"). The disguised-dualism debate is thus genuinely pre-existing and does not originate with the Map.

### 2. Citation & attribution accuracy

**Metadata: clean.** All load-bearing references verify at publisher of record:

- Friston, K.J., Wiese, W., & Hobson, J.A. (2020), "Sentience and the Origins of Consciousness: From Cartesian Duality to Markovian Monism," *Entropy* 22(5):516, DOI 10.3390/e22050516 — author order, title, journal, volume, article number all correct.
- Beni, M.D. (2021), "A critical analysis of Markovian monism," *Synthese* 199:6407–6427, DOI 10.1007/s11229-021-03075-x — correct.
- Cutter, B. (2019), "Against the middle ground: Why Russellian monism is unstable," *Analytic Philosophy* 60(2):109–129, DOI 10.1111/phib.12149 — correct.
- Pautz (2015) in Alter & Nagasawa (eds.), *Consciousness in the Physical World* (OUP); Pautz (2017) reply-to-Roelofs manuscript — correctly described, including the "unpublished manuscript" status.
- Tononi & Koch (2015), *Phil. Trans. R. Soc. B* 370(1668) — correct.

**Verbatim fidelity: mostly sound, three defects.**

- "ultimately reducible" (attributed to Friston in the concept page) — **CONFIRMED verbatim** against the paper (§5, after Eq. 8): "the extrinsic information geometry is ultimately reducible to the intrinsic information geometry (and the other way around), in the sense that there is a necessary link between them." Good. [ucl](https://discovery-pp.ucl.ac.uk/10102186/1/entropy-22-00516-v2.pdf)
- "mutually reductive" (used on the load-bearing companion page predictive-processing-and-dualism, attributed to Friston: "he insists both geometries are 'mutually reductive'") — **NOT in the paper.** The exact phrase does not appear anywhere in Friston, Wiese & Hobson 2020; it originates in a secondary blog paraphrase (Consciousness Realist). The authors' own words are "reducible to one another." The authors actually use the near-opposite phrase "mutually irreducible" once — and only to describe a dual-aspect criterion they are sceptical of. This is a genuine citation-fidelity defect: a paraphrase laundered into a scare-quoted authorial phrase. (I distinguish this from fabrication — the sense is arguably defensible — but a quotation mark around words the author did not write is exactly the documented systemic risk.)
- Pautz "exactly the same swarm of extra-logical principles" — quote-boundary inflation. Pautz's verbatim is "a swarm of extra-logical principles"; "exactly the same" is the article's intensifier placed inside the quotation marks. The substance (RM and dualism both require the swarm; differ only in modal status) is faithful, but the quotation boundary is inaccurate. [concepts/russellian-monism](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/russellian-monism/)
- Cutter "Russellian monism does not offer a comfortable resting place — one must turn back or else march on to dualism" — the original is two sentences ("...comfortable resting place. One must turn back or else march on to dualism. The middle ground is unstable ground."). The article silently fuses them with an em-dash. Light, non-distorting, but presented as verbatim. [Academia.edu](https://www.academia.edu/36437213/Against_the_Middle_Ground_-_Why_Russellian_Monism_is_Unstable_Analytic_Philosophy_)
- Beni "could not read off metaphysical theses about the nature of target systems ... from our theories of ... scientific models (Markov blankets)" — faithful splice of Beni's abstract sentence; the interpolated gloss ("the self-organising conscious systems themselves") matches Beni's own parenthetical. Acceptable.

**Author-stance / co-optation firewall: the central problem.**

- **Friston, Wiese & Hobson are naturalists cited toward a conclusion they explicitly reject.** Their paper favours "reductive materialism" (the whole of §13 is titled "Markovian Monism as Reductive Materialism"; they write "This physicalist interpretation of Markovian monism is the one we favour"), commits to "only one type of thing and only one type of irreducible property," and states outright, under a section headed "Why do we rule out dualistic interpretations of the dual information geometry?", that "the dual information geometry itself does not entail property dualism" and "we therefore have to reject dualism." The article's diagnosis (they are "really" disguised property dualists) is the exact reading the authors foreclose. The concept page partly earns this by framing the whole exercise as an adversarial "hygiene" diagnosis and by openly acknowledging the monist disavowal — so it is a *licensed* hostile reading, not a covert misattribution. But two things push it over the line into a co-optation problem: (i) the companion page's establishing-register "covertly dualist"; and (ii) the article's gloss that "Friston proposes that the extrinsic geometry potentially accounts for 'mental' properties or qualia" subtly reverses the paper's deflationary thrust — Friston says "'qualia' ... are just Bayesian beliefs that imply an extrinsic information geometry," i.e., a reductive claim, not the positing of an irreducible mental locus. [nih +2 + 2](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC7517007/)
- **Beni** is used correctly and narrowly — to constrain the inference from formalism to metaphysics ("we could not read off metaphysical theses ... from our theories of ... scientific models"). The article does not claim Beni endorses dualism, and this is a faithful, firewall-respecting use. One nuance worth surfacing (and stating precisely): Beni observes in his text that Markovian monism "could hardly be reconciled with any standard form of reductive materialism, and it meshes more nicely with neutral monism" — but he then *declines to endorse* that landing spot, concluding in his abstract that "none of these interpretations contributes to the defence of a metaphysical stance (either in terms of neutral monism or reductive physicalism)." So Beni's actual position is a global scepticism about reading metaphysics off the models — which cuts against *both* the physicalist self-description and the dualist-adjacent reading. The article surfaces only the anti-physicalist half. [Semantic Scholar](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/A-critical-analysis-of-Markovian-monism-Beni/c1369e9088bf631119290085775fad2a1a01c247)
- **Cutter and Pautz** are used faithfully. Both genuinely argue Russellian monism collapses toward, or gains no advantage over, dualism; Cutter is himself an anti-physicalist, Pautz a critic. No firewall breach. [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/331941585_Against_the_Middle_Ground_Why_Russellian_Monism_is_Unstable)[Academia.edu](https://www.academia.edu/36437213/Against_the_Middle_Ground_-_Why_Russellian_Monism_is_Unstable_Analytic_Philosophy_)
- **Tononi & Koch 2015** is the sole IIT source, carrying claims about "IIT's exposition consistently" distinguishing intrinsic/extrinsic. The article's hedge (the diagnosis "depends on how seriously the identity claim is meant; some IIT exponents ... accept the dualist implications openly") is defensible and even accurate — IIT's authors make critical remarks about all metaphysical labels, and IIT 4.0 (Albantakis, L., Barbosa, L., Findlay, G., Grasso, M., et al. & Tononi, G. (2023), "Integrated information theory (IIT) 4.0: Formulating the properties of phenomenal existence in physical terms," *PLoS Computational Biology* 19(10):e1011465, DOI 10.1371/journal.pcbi.1011465) has been read by some commentators as dualist (e.g., the *Journal of NeuroPhilosophy* paper "Integrated Information Theory 4.0 is both Weakly Panpsychist and Strongly Dualist, but many Theories of Consciousness are also Prone to It"; and Cea, I., Negro, N., & Signorelli, C.M. (2023), "The Fundamental Tension in Integrated Information Theory 4.0's Realist Idealism," *Entropy* 25:1453). But a single 2015 citation is thin support for a claim about IIT's consistent exposition, and there is no engagement with IIT 4.0.

No performative-inoculation citation pattern is evident: the critics cited (Beni, Cutter, Pautz) are engaged substantively, not name-checked to appear responsive.

### 3. Engagement with physicalist counterarguments

This is where the concept page is weakest, though the site as a whole is stronger than the page alone.

- **The descriptive/epistemic-vs-ontological reply (a)** — the single strongest physicalist rebuttal, made explicitly by Friston (dual geometry "does not entail property dualism") and standard among predictive-processing theorists — is *not engaged in the concept page's own text*. It is pre-empted by being folded into condition (1) rather than answered. This is the audit's central engagement finding: the diagnostic's architecture absorbs the objection instead of meeting it.
- **The Russellian realiser reply (b)** — that quiddities are the categorical realisers of physical structure, not "extra" non-physical properties, so the "two properties" framing misdescribes the view — is engaged only indirectly. The article asserts the structural-quiddity distinction "by construction blocks both logical derivation and explanatory bridging," and leans on Pautz's swarm argument. That is a legitimate in-literature response (Pautz and Cutter are exactly the philosophers who press it), so the engagement is real but routed through cited critics rather than confronted head-on; a Russellian would say the article still misdescribes a monist categorical-basis view as a "second structure doing explanatory work."
- **The neutral-monism / dual-aspect monism reply (c)** — that such views are ontologically monist by construction — is *not engaged at all*, which is a notable gap given that (i) Friston's paper explicitly considers and rejects a dual-aspect reading ("mutually irreducible ... we are sceptical"), and (ii) Beni, the article's own witness, notes the view "meshes more nicely with neutral monism." The article omits both. [ucl](https://discovery-pp.ucl.ac.uk/10102186/1/entropy-22-00516-v2.pdf)
- **Predictive processing / active inference as the strongest unaddressed physicalist rival** — here the verdict must be split. On the *concept page*, PP is essentially name-checked. But the *companion topic page* engages the Laukkonen, R.E., Friston, K.J., & Chandaria, S. (2025) "beautiful loop" active-inference theory of consciousness ("A beautiful loop: An active inference theory of consciousness," *Neuroscience & Biobehavioral Reviews* 176:106296, DOI 10.1016/j.neubiorev.2025.106296, which offers three conditions purporting to be jointly sufficient for phenomenal experience) at length, states it "in its own terms before replying," runs the meta-problem objection, and concedes it is "a live and serious rival, defeated neither by the tenets nor by decisive evidence." That is substantive, good-faith engagement, not co-optation. So the site does confront the strongest rival — but the concept page under audit does not carry that weight and should not be read in isolation as though it does.

**Constitutional-attractor assessment:** The diagnostic is *partly* an independently-discovered tool and *partly* an artifact. In its favour: the pattern is real and pre-dates the site (Searle 2002 and the large literature arguing biological naturalism is property dualism in disguise; the article itself names biological naturalism as "the canonical target of the disguised-dualism charge"). Against it: the exemption carved out for the Map's own aspect dualism (via the "asserts no unification → vacuous condition (3)" move, propped up by the "chemistry is not disguised vitalism" analogy) is exactly the shape one would design to reclassify every gap-honouring physicalism as secretly dualist while immunising the house view. The chemistry analogy is doing heavy, arguably question-begging work: it presupposes the two-level ontology is a legitimate mechanism-in-progress for the Map but an unpaid promissory note for others — the very asymmetry in dispute.

Summarising the site-methodology-vocabulary findings:

- **Tenet leakage:** Present, in condition (1)'s fusion of epistemic descriptions with ontological property-types. This is the most consequential leak — it imports the site's assumption that the explanatory gap marks a real ontological seam into a criterion that is supposed to be neutral about that.
- **Constrain-vs-establish gate:** Passed on the concept page (explicit "hygiene, not demonstration" register); **failed on the companion page** ("covertly dualist").
- **Epistemic-to-metaphysical slide:** Flagged and disowned at the level of the stated verdict, but built into the diagnostic's condition (1) and re-committed on the companion page.
- **Co-optation firewall:** Breached for Friston et al. in the softened sense described above; the deflationary "'qualia' are just Bayesian beliefs" thrust of the source is not surfaced.
- **Citation-fabrication pattern:** No fabrication. One misattributed paraphrase ("mutually reductive"), one quote-boundary inflation (Pautz "exactly the same"), one silent sentence-fusion (Cutter). These are fidelity defects, not inventions.
- **Confession-without-correction:** The self-application section is a paradigm case — the fatal-looking objection is named ("guilty in reverse of the move it diagnoses") and then absorbed rather than resolved, with the reputational payload preserved.
- **Evidential-independence failure:** The tool's discriminating power between "Map = honest dualism" and "rivals = disguised dualism" reduces to a single distinction (asserted vs. not-asserted reduction) that no evidence adjudicates — the article concedes as much.

### 5. Overall methodological integrity

High by the standards of an AI-assisted partisan project, and notably higher than the co-optation risk would predict. The page is transparent about authorship and provenance, hedges its central claim to the correct ("hygiene") register, runs the hardest objection against itself, and cites real critics substantively. Metadata discipline is excellent. The integrity failures are specific and fixable rather than systemic: an equivocation in one condition, a co-optation that is licensed on the concept page but not on its companion, and three quotation-fidelity slips of decreasing severity. The changelog shows the disguised-property-dualism page has not been touched since 2026-07-16 and was not implicated in any recent outer-review finding, so these defects are standing, not artifacts of a mid-edit state.

## BOTTOM-LINE VERDICT

**REVISE-HARD** (concept page). The diagnostic is legitimate and should be retained, but three things must change before it is sound: (1) condition (1) must separate epistemic descriptions from ontological property-types and the diagnostic must *argue* the bridge rather than build it in; (2) the co-optation of Friston et al. must be corrected to surface that their paper explicitly rules out the dualist reading and favours reductive materialism; (3) the neutral-monist/dual-aspect reply must be engaged.

**DEMOTE-TO-COHERENCE-ONLY** the specific claim that Markovian monism (and the other exemplars) are metaphysically equivalent to, or "covertly," dualism. On the concept page this is already correctly demoted to "hygiene, not demonstration"; on the companion predictive-processing-and-dualism page it is stated in establishing register and should be brought into line.

**FLAG AS PERPETUALLY CONTESTED** the self-application defence (the claim-vs-vacuous-condition asymmetry). The article already half-concedes this; the concession should be elevated from a closing aside to a standing status marker.

Not DELETE: the pattern is real, independently attested (Searle 2002 and the biological-naturalism-as-property-dualism literature), and usefully named.

## ARTICLE-SPECIFIC FIXES

1. **Split condition (1).** Distinguish "two irreducible *descriptions/perspectives*" (epistemic) from "two irreducible *property-types*" (ontological). State explicitly that only the latter triggers the dualist diagnosis, and that moving a theory from the first category to the second requires an argument the diagnostic must supply — not a disjunction that treats them as interchangeable.
2. **Correct the Friston co-optation.** Add, in the Markovian-monism worked example, that Friston, Wiese & Hobson explicitly favour reductive materialism and explicitly state "the dual information geometry itself does not entail property dualism," ruling out dualistic interpretations by name. The diagnosis can still be run as an adversarial reading, but the reader must see that it is contested by the authors at the source. [ucl](https://discovery-pp.ucl.ac.uk/10102186/1/entropy-22-00516-v2.pdf)
3. **Fix the "'qualia'" gloss.** Replace "Friston proposes that the extrinsic geometry potentially accounts for 'mental' properties or qualia" with the source's actual deflationary claim that on Friston's view "'qualia' ... are just Bayesian beliefs that imply an extrinsic information geometry." This reverses a subtle stance inversion.
4. **Repair the "mutually reductive" quotation on the companion page.** On predictive-processing-and-dualism, the scare-quoted "mutually reductive" attributed to Friston must be replaced with the authors' verbatim "reducible to one another" (or "ultimately reducible"), since "mutually reductive" is a third-party blog paraphrase, not the authors' words — and the authors actually use "mutually irreducible" only for a dual-aspect criterion they reject.
5. **Fix the Pautz quote boundary.** Change "exactly the same swarm of extra-logical principles" to the article's own words plus Pautz's verbatim: e.g., *the same "swarm of extra-logical principles."* Remove "exactly the same" from inside the quotation marks.
6. **Regularise the Cutter quote.** Either quote the two sentences as written ("...comfortable resting place. One must turn back or else march on to dualism.") or mark the compression; do not present a fused em-dash version as verbatim.
7. **Engage the neutral-monism reply.** Add a paragraph addressing the dual-aspect / neutral-monist rejoinder that such views are monist by construction, and disclose that Beni — the article's own witness — notes Markovian monism "meshes more nicely with neutral monism" (while declining to endorse any metaphysical stance), and that Friston's paper considers and rejects the dual-aspect reading. Explain why the diagnostic still bites (or concede that against a genuinely neutral base it may not). [PubMed Central](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7885977/)
8. **Bolster or hedge the IIT case.** Either add IIT 4.0 sourcing (Albantakis et al. 2023, *PLoS Comput. Biol.* 19(10):e1011465) and the literature reading it as dualist/panpsychist (the *Journal of NeuroPhilosophy* "Weakly Panpsychist and Strongly Dualist" paper; Cea, Negro & Signorelli 2023, *Entropy* 25:1453), or soften "IIT's exposition consistently distinguishes" to reflect that it rests on a single 2015 citation.
9. **Elevate the self-application concession** from a closing aside to an explicit standing status line ("this diagnostic's application to the Map's own view is perpetually contested; its discriminating power rests on a distinction no evidence adjudicates").
10. **Reconsider the chemistry/vitalism analogy.** Acknowledge that "chemistry is not disguised vitalism" presupposes the legitimacy of the two-level ontology it defends, and that a physicalist will say the same defence is available to Markovian monism — so the analogy illustrates the Map's position rather than adjudicating between it and its rivals.

## SITE-WIDE METHODOLOGY IMPROVEMENTS

1. **Add a descriptions-vs-properties firewall to the concept-creation checklist.** Any diagnostic or argument that turns on "two irreducible X" must explicitly type X as epistemic (descriptions, perspectives, models, concepts) or ontological (properties, substances, structures), and may not silently disjoin them. This is the specific mechanism by which epistemic-to-metaphysical slides enter the corpus at the level of definitions rather than inferences.
2. **Extend author-stance verification to include a "conclusion-reversal" check.** The existing author-stance ledger records debate role and one-sentence thesis; add a required field flagging when a cited author's *stated conclusion contradicts the use the article makes of them* (as with Friston's explicit "does not entail property dualism"). Adversarial readings of a naturalist are legitimate but must be labelled as contested-at-source, not narrated as the author's own trajectory.
3. **Propagate register discipline from concept pages to their companion topic pages.** The "hygiene, not demonstration" hedge on the concept page and the establishing-register "covertly dualist" on the companion page is a semantic-version-skew failure of the type the changelog repeatedly catches elsewhere. Add companion pages to the scope sweep whenever a concept page's central claim is calibrated, so the strongest phrasing does not survive at the point of first application.
4. **Require secondary-source quotation provenance tagging.** The "mutually reductive" defect arose from a blog paraphrase being scare-quoted as authorial. Institute a rule that any scare-quoted phrase attributed to a primary author must be verified against the primary text, not a review, blog, or encyclopedia gloss, before publication — and that quote boundaries may not include the article's own intensifiers (the Pautz "exactly the same" defect).
5. **Add a "monist rejoinder" gate for every disguised-dualism deployment.** Whenever the diagnostic is applied, the target's strongest available *monist* self-description (neutral monism, dual-aspect monism, categorical-realiser Russellianism) must be stated and answered in the same article, not deferred. The tool's credibility depends on not being an attractor that reclassifies every gap-respecting physicalism as covert dualism by construction.
