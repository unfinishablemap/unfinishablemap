## Executive Summary

This is the Map's strongest structural cluster: three articles that answer *where* and *how* a non-physical experience could do causal work, each anchored to a real, correctly-cited literature (Saad 2025, Schaffer 2000, Cramer/Kastner/Aharonov) and each disciplined about the line between "removes an objection" and "supplies a positive mechanism." The standout virtue is calibration honesty — every load-bearing claim is explicitly marked framework-internal rather than empirically demonstrable, and the recent Vaassen re-framing (author-corrected) is now clean: trumping-preemption situates Vaassen 2024 as a *rival* interventionist co-causation route, not an instance of preemption. One genuinely-uncovered, article-sized subject sits one inch outside the cluster: a dedicated home for the interventionist / counterfactual-dualist ("lawful co-causation") rival family, which trumping-preemption can only name in a clause.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The cluster is built on Chalmers' distinctness commitment and refuses every collapse into it. Delegatory causation states the point exactly: "If experience just *is* the brain state under a different description, there is no default profile to preempt — the distinction collapses. Constitution models eliminate the gap; delegation requires it." Chalmers would approve that psychophysical laws (his own framework, cited via `psychophysical-laws`) do real work here — Saad's Subset Law\* is presented as a candidate *psychophysical* law, not a reduction.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
Atemporal causation is where Stapp's instincts are honoured most carefully: consciousness "operates in Kastner's pre-spacetime realm, selecting which offer-confirmation handshake becomes actual," with "no energy injection" — von-Neumann-process-1-style selection given a specific interpretive home (TI/TSVF/all-at-once). Crucially the article does *not* oversell it: it flags decoherence as "the most contested step" and lists the physics frameworks as "all minority positions." Stapp gets a physically-specifiable locus without the hand-waving he is often accused of.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The cluster keeps the first-person fact as explanandum rather than dissolving it. Atemporal causation's appeal to the phenomenology of choice — "Deliberation feels like weighing genuine alternatives... Resolution feels like crystallisation" — treats lived decision-experience as data the metaphysics must fit, and the all-or-nothing selection result (Vieira et al. 2025) is read *through* that phenomenology ("decisions present as definite selections, not probability adjustments"). This is Nagel's methodological priority in practice.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
Whitehead is cited exactly where he belongs and nowhere he doesn't. Atemporal causation's "Philosophical Precedents" section places concrescence precisely: "The actual occasion doesn't exist and then become definite; its becoming definite is what constitutes it as existing" — atemporal constitution, not sequential causation. And it flags the *partiality* of the precedent honestly (Plotinus's emanation is "necessary rather than selective, while the Map's atemporal causation centrally involves selection"). Process resonance is present and praised on its own axis, used to remove a defeater (causation need not be temporally ordered), not to inflate any empirical claim.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
Two things Kane wants are here and, importantly, *one over-reach has been correctly resisted*. The trilemma-of-selection framing preserves authorship at indeterminacies (delegatory causation: "only mental causation preserves authorship"). And trumping-preemption declines to overclaim the agent-causal payoff: it explicitly refuses to call the template "the structure agent-causal libertarianism has been after," noting O'Connor–Clarke libertarians "want the agent to make a difference among genuinely open alternatives, not to author an outcome the physical process already suffices to produce." That is the honest thing to say, and it is said.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
The cluster's spine is a mysterian-grade humility about the *how*. Atemporal causation's "Removing an Objection Is Not Providing a Mechanism" section is model epistemic hygiene: "Saying consciousness 'participates in determining which transaction completes' identifies a role without explaining how that role is filled... This is a genuine explanatory gap, and the Map does not claim to have closed it." McGinn's cognitive-closure worry is met not with defeatism but with a precise inventory of what is and isn't delivered.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This cluster does not touch minimal-organism consciousness, so the tier-upgrade tension is not the load-bearing axis — but the persona's *core discipline* (keep the registers separate; do not let a defeater-removal masquerade as evidence) is honoured throughout, and that restraint is the cluster's defining strength:

- **Objection-removal is never sold as mechanism.** Atemporal causation states it in a section heading and repeats it in the tenet mapping: the Libet timing objection "dissolves entirely," but "the positive mechanism of non-physical participation in quantum transactions remains an open problem." The two registers are kept apart explicitly.
- **Framework-internal efficacy is labelled as such.** Delegatory causation rates the trumping route's efficacy "honestly there as framework-internal rather than empirically demonstrable," and concedes the tu quoque directly: appealing to theoretical virtue under empirical equivalence "is a move the Map elsewhere criticises physicalists for making... it looks suspect when dualists make it too." Delegation's advantage over epiphenomenalism is then argued to be a *different kind* of constraint (epistemic self-defeat via `self-stultification`), not a prettier-theory preference — a genuinely sharper move than parsimony-appeal.
- **Candidate laws are not derivations.** Trumping-preemption: Saad's laws "are candidate laws, not derivations, and any workable account needs both the laws and a story about why *these* rather than others obtain." The `articulability-of-q1` open problem is named rather than papered over.
- **Rivals are marked live, not strawed.** Trumping-preemption concedes that if Kroedel/Bennett/List-Stoljar/interventionist dissolutions succeed "the exclusion pressure trumping is built to answer may not be live, and the template would be answering a question already closed. The Map does not claim to refute these dissolutions inside their own frameworks."

No calibration flag is warranted. The Process Philosopher and the Hardline Empiricist praise the *same* passages here (defeater-removal on one axis, refusal-to-cash-it-out on the other), which is the signature of an honestly-resolved cluster.

## Content Strengths

### trumping-preemption.md
- **Strongest point**: The Vaassen re-framing is now exactly right and is a model of citation-framing accuracy. The article distinguishes Vaassen's interventionist co-causation ("both the experience and its physical base are genuine causes... no preemption at all") from the preemption template and concludes "Vaassen's proposal is a rival to the trumping template rather than an instance of it." This resolves the author-flagged misframing (Vaassen emailed 2026-07-11 that his AJP 2024 is *both-are-causes interventionist*, not preemption) — and it lands the paper in the correct family (lawful co-causation, Mills 1996 / Lowe 2003).
- **Notable quote**: "The sentence 'the brain is sufficient' is true and uncontroversial; what trumping adds is that sufficiency is not the same as authority, and the step from the one to the other is where dualist analysis productively concentrates."
- **Why it works**: It isolates the one conceptual move (decouple authority from sufficiency) and then spends the article stress-testing whether nature can supply a non-stipulative authority law — the honest pressure point — rather than assuming it. The disanalogy flag ("Schaffer's cases are fictional or conventional... Nature is not in the business of stipulating such rules") is the kind of self-imposed objection that builds reader trust.

### delegatory-causation.md
- **Strongest point**: The epiphenomenalism-parallel section confronts the sharpest self-directed worry head-on — that an observationally-closed theory "is doing causal work or only riding the distribution physics already fixes" — and answers it with a *bounded* claim: the self-undermining argument "eliminates epiphenomenalism but does not distinguish delegation from physicalism." Naming what an argument does *not* buy you is rarer and more valuable than the argument itself.
- **Notable quote**: "A theory that severs the link between its subject matter and the evidence for believing in it is self-undermining: it could still be true, but it cannot be rationally held."
- **Why it works**: The Born-rule identification is clearly fenced as "a Map-specific integration, not a claim Saad makes," and the tension between the quantum-selection channel (difference-making, gives up closure) and the trumping channel (difference-making-free, preserves closure) is held open as "distinct and potentially competing mechanisms" rather than falsely synthesised. That is the correct, hard-won distinction.

### atemporal-causation.md
- **Strongest point**: The "What Atemporal Causation Is Not" four-way fence (not timelessness / not backward time travel / not acausality / not law-violation) pre-empts the four most common misreadings in one tight block — front-loaded, LLM-friendly, and each disjunct does real work.
- **Notable quote**: "The readiness potential that Libet detected isn't prior to the cause — it's *part of* what gets caused."
- **Why it works**: The comparison table (non-retrocausal vs atemporal) is genuinely decision-useful: it makes the tradeoff legible (established physics vs explanatory reach) and refuses to declare a winner, which matches the cluster's "hold rival channels open" discipline. The "What Would Challenge This View?" section lists five concrete defeaters including a *classical* Libet dissolution (Schurger) — a dualist article naming the classical off-ramp is a credibility signal.
