## Executive Summary

This is the Map's calibration exemplar. Across six articles it builds a cumulative abductive case that the consciousness-physical interface is tied to specific neural machinery rather than to functional sophistication — and it polices its own inference at every rung, naming the framework-generated share of its convergence and subtracting it out. The two personas designed to collide here — the Process Philosopher (who welcomes agency/experience-friendly, "cognition all the way down" framings) and the Hardline Empiricist (who polices possibility/probability slippage) — **converge in praise rather than conflict**, which is the skill's own signal that the tension is resolved honestly. There are no calibration errors to flag as concerns; the cluster is instead the model the rest of the corpus should be measured against. ## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The cluster is built on the explanatory gap and says so in Chalmers' own terms: "there are no non-behavioural, third-person markers of consciousness to fall back on — that very absence *is* the explanatory gap" (`basal-and-bioelectric-cognition`, echoed verbatim in the apex). The apex's deepest move — that the phenomenal question is *invariant* under the two third-person facts that most tempt a verdict (behavioural richness and neural architecture) — is Chalmers' "structure and function leave something out" generalised into a two-directional discipline. Chalmers would recognise the gap being taken seriously not as a slogan but as a working constraint that changes what the articles are willing to conclude.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
Peripheral to this cluster and handled without overreach. The enteric article's reading (b) — a *genuinely distributed interface* at enteric quantum junctures — is exactly the kind of minimal-selection question Stapp cares about, and it is raised rather than assumed, flagged "least-supported" on minimality grounds. `consciousness-in-simple-organisms` correctly scopes the Penrose-Hameroff dependency (slime moulds have microtubules but not the orchestrated neuronal architecture the hypothesis treats as load-bearing). Stapp would approve both the seriousness and the refusal to over-claim a mechanism the evidence does not yet reach.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
"What is it like" is the pivot of every rung. The immune article's "functional self, not felt self" section is the Nagelian core: a body "has an immunological self in the functional sense... and no immunological self in the phenomenal sense, no locus of experience attaching to the tracking." Nagel's insistence that the first-person fact is not captured by any third-person description is precisely what the cluster operationalises — and never inflates, since each article concedes it has not shown nothing is felt, only that the competence gives no purchase either way.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
This is the cluster's home turf — Levin/Dennett's "cognition all the way down," Bechtel and Bich's "eating is cognition," Reber and Baluška's Cellular Basis of Consciousness, Pamela Lyon's biogenic cognition are all engaged at full strength — and the Process Philosopher gets everything he can legitimately want: experience-friendly, substance-avoiding, continuity-of-life framings taken seriously rather than caricatured. Crucially, **none of it is cashed out as a tier-upgrade.** Levin's own bracketing of xenobot sentience and Cohen's expressly computational immune "self" are admitted as *framework-resonant restraint the Map treats as data*, never as evidence elevating any organism's status. This is the axis-discipline the skill demands: process-compatibility praised, tier-upgrade withheld.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
Peripheral here. The cluster is about the competence/experience decoupling, not agency-as-freedom; where agency-language appears (xenobot goal-pursuit, ENS reflex control, immune "decisions") it is explicitly the functional/intentional-stance sense, deliberately not the libertarian one. No overreach for Kane to welcome or object to — the restraint itself is the point.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
Well-served. The `single-cell-proto-agency-and-the-evidence-problem` companion (cited by the apex) argues the prokaryotic-floor question may be *not empirically tractable* — a McGinn-flavoured cognitive-closure claim, held honestly as a limit rather than smuggled in as a win. The apex's "three calibrations set the ceiling" section is sustained epistemic humility: the convergence is "partly framework-generated," each marker "defeasible and substrate-sensitive," the verdict "framework-relative, not a proof of absence." McGinn would recognise a framework that knows the shape of what it cannot settle.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
**This persona's verdict is load-bearing for this cluster, and it is strongly positive.** The cluster does, repeatedly and by name, the thing that is praise-worthy to *not* do: it declines to slide boundary cases up the five-tier scale on tenet-load.

- **The named pattern is present verbatim.** `consciousness-in-simple-organisms` runs an explicit "Evidential Register: Tenet vs. Evidence" section: "Removing a defeater keeps a hypothesis live; it does not, on its own, supply positive evidence... The dualist framing defeats dismissive *a priori* arguments; it does not elevate the evidence." This is the *"tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating"* discipline stated as policy, not left implicit.
- **The boundary cases are calibrated exactly where restraint requires.** *C. elegans* sits at *live hypothesis* (sliding toward speculative integration); *Hydra* and *Physarum* at *speculative integration* with cognition-without-consciousness named the more parsimonious reading. Nowhere is a nematode, Hydra, or slime mould labelled "realistic possibility" on tenet-load — the precise slippage the persona exists to catch is absent.
- **The New York Declaration's "realistic possibility" language is quarantined**, applied to the vertebrate/invertebrate cases the declaration actually covers and endorsed as *precaution*, not imported as an evidential upgrade for the neuron-less rungs.
- **The apex subtracts the framework-generated convergence** ("their agreement is in part the framework consulting itself... the abductive support is real but discounted") and states what survives at its "honest height and not one inch above it" — that behavioural/functional markers *do not settle* the phenomenal question, a limits-of-evidence claim "even a committed materialist can grant." This is Birch's evidence-quality discipline enacted at the synthesis level.
- **The discipline is bidirectional.** The apex's "The Discipline Cuts Both Ways" section refuses to convert missing fish cortex into a proof of phenomenal absence with the *same* rule that refuses to read experience into a bacterium — a symmetry that proves the restraint is principled rather than a one-directional deflationary reflex.

The Process Philosopher and the Hardline Empiricist praise *different aspects of the same passages* without conflict. Per the skill, that convergence is the signal the cluster has resolved the possibility/probability tension honestly. No `refine-draft` is warranted on calibration grounds.

## Content Strengths

### apex/competency-without-felt-experience.md
- **Strongest point**: The two-axis geometry (descend the tree stripping substrate; hold two edge cases — organoids as "substrate without world," fish as "substrate with the wrong architecture") turns nine loosely-related articles into a single argument no rung makes alone.
- **Notable quote**: "the phenomenal question is invariant under the two third-person facts that most tempt a verdict — behavioural richness and neural architecture... That selective invariance is the deepest thing the cluster shows."
- **Why it works**: It earns the cumulative conclusion and then immediately caps it, distinguishing what the framework-neutral subtraction leaves ("consistent with" neural specificity) from what only the internal commitment yields ("points toward"). Textbook calibration.

### topics/neuron-less-animals-sponges-placozoans...md
- **Strongest point**: "The Parts-List Precedes the Machine" — the demosponge post-synaptic scaffold in a synapse-less animal, used to make the discipline "genomic toolkit presence is not functional presence, and functional presence is not experience" concrete rather than asserted.
- **Notable quote**: "maximal behavioural interest, zero neural substrate — is what makes neuron-less animals the decisive stress-test they are."
- **Why it works**: It states the expansive rival (Bechtel and Bich's "eating is cognition") at full strength before answering, and grants the competence while withholding only the experience — the wedge "goes in most cleanly" precisely because nothing is caricatured.

### topics/the-cognitive-immune-system-and-immunological-selfhood.md
- **Strongest point**: Turning Tauber's "self is a metaphor" critique from a threat into support — the critique targets the same smuggling of a quasi-experiential self that the Map refuses, so it *sharpens* the functional/phenomenal distinction rather than dissolving it.
- **Why it works**: It picks the word most loaded with phenomenal connotation ("self"), engages the field's own sharpest internal critic, and comes out with the distinction strengthened.

### topics/the-enteric-nervous-system...md
- **Strongest point**: The three-reading structure (a: brain-only, b: distributed-interface, c: sub-personal-competence) that refuses to over-resolve, with (c) supplying (a) the "principled reason it needs" to avoid ad-hoc brain-chauvinism.
- **Why it works**: It converts an apparent challenge to the interface picture into an occasion to make the interface *criterion* (anatomical vs functional vs phenomenological) explicit — registering a boundary of the framework honestly.
