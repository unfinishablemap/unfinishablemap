- **Tenet tension**: Bidirectional Interaction (Tenet 3)
- **Issue**: The "Open Possibilities" section entertains one-way (epiphenomenal) consciousness in uploads as a genuine possibility: "But what if consciousness can exist without causal efficacy? If one-way consciousness is possible — experience occurring without the ability to influence physical processes — then an upload might preserve subjective experience even in a deterministic classical substrate."
- **Mitigating context**: The article explicitly acknowledges this "would undermine the Bidirectional Interaction tenet's relevance to uploading" and the Relation to Site Perspective section correctly affirms bidirectional interaction. This is honest intellectual engagement with a speculative scenario rather than endorsement.
- **Recommendation**: No change strictly needed. The tension is acknowledged transparently.

### buddhism-and-dualism.md

- **Tenet tension**: Dualism (Tenet 1) / Bidirectional Interaction (Tenet 3)
- **Issue**: The article's compatibility thesis between Buddhism and the Map leans heavily on property dualism and impermanence, stating "the Map does not require substance dualism," "the Map does not require a permanent soul," and "the Map does not require a unified self." Discussion of Samkhya's passive *purusha* (witness rather than causal agent) is noted as diverging from the Map's position but treated sympathetically.
- **Mitigating context**: The article explicitly sides with Nyaya over Samkhya on bidirectional causation, and the Map's dualism tenet is indeed compatible with property dualism. These are carefully qualified claims about minimal requirements.
- **Recommendation**: No change needed. The qualifications are accurate and the article correctly distinguishes property dualism from substance dualism.

### samkhya-three-way-distinction.md

- **Tenet tension**: Bidirectional Interaction (Tenet 3)
- **Issue**: One passage states Samkhya "preserves causal closure for the physical domain" as though this is a positive feature, when the Map explicitly rejects causal closure as a constraint. The word "valuable" is applied to a reframing that renders consciousness causally inert.
- **Mitigating context**: The Relation to Site Perspective section explicitly notes the Map "parts ways with Samkhya here" and rejects the passive-witness position.
- **Recommendation**: Consider adding a brief qualifier to the "preserves causal closure" sentence (e.g., "preserves causal closure — a feature the Map does not regard as desirable") to eliminate ambiguity.

### types-of-consciousness.md

- **Mitigating context**: The article elsewhere identifies the Map's position as active selection and explicitly mentions the Bidirectional Interaction tenet.
- **Recommendation**: Consider adding a brief parenthetical (e.g., "rejected by the Map for human consciousness") to sharpen alignment.
