## Critiques by Philosopher

### The Eliminative Materialist (Churchland)
Her own Luminous Room parody is already the article's centerpiece, and the article fairly concedes the third premise "rests on an intuition its critics reasonably distrust." A residual push: the article's phrase "one cannot read the truth about a complex, emergent, scale-dependent property off a first-person impression" concedes more than it may intend — if understanding is genuinely scale-and-emergence dependent, the Map's own confidence that "LLMs do not close the gap" is a first-person impression of a slow, small instance too. The article notes this tension but does not fully neutralize it.

### The Hard-Nosed Physicalist (Dennett)
The intuition-pump charge gets its own section and is not strawmanned. Dennett would still press that the article's "original intentionality" is precisely the inflated intuition at issue — treating "your thought of Paris is intrinsically about Paris" as a datum rather than a folk-theoretic posit. The article asserts the original/derived distinction as settled machinery; a Dennettian denies there is any original intentionality to be the source of. This is load-bearing and stated without defense (the defense is out-sourced to [[intentionality]]), which is legitimate division of labor but worth naming as an assumption the article leans on.

### The Quantum Skeptic (Tegmark) / The Many-Worlds Defender (Deutsch)
Little purchase here — the article makes no quantum or branching claims. Honestly noted rather than forced.

### The Empiricist (Popper's ghost)
The article is careful to disclaim deriving the dualist verdict from the room, so the "unfalsifiable metaphysics" charge lands on the tenets, not on this page. Fair. The one place a Popperian bites: "Whether learned distributed representations differ in kind from a lookup rulebook … is the unresolved question." The article states the Map "holds that they do not close the gap" without offering any observable that would distinguish "thin derived grounding" from "genuine grounding" — the claim is marked contested, which is the honest move, but no discriminating test is even gestured at.

### The Buddhist Philosopher (Nagarjuna)
The whole apparatus of *original/intrinsic* intentionality reifies exactly the self-standing aboutness Madhyamaka would deconstruct as empty and dependently originated. The article's dualism treats intrinsic intentionality as the bedrock computation lacks; Nagarjuna would say that bedrock is itself a construction. Not a defect in the article's own terms, but the deepest unaddressed opponent.

## Critical Issues

### Issue 1: Hofstadter over-attributed as "co-originator" of "intuition pump"
- **File**: `obsidian/concepts/chinese-room-argument.md`
- **Location**: "The Intuition-Pump Charge" section — "Douglas Hofstadter, co-originator of the 'intuition pump' label, presses the scale objection…"
- **Problem**: The term "intuition pump" is Dennett's coinage. SEP (Cole 2023) and general sources credit Dennett with the term, coined in his 1980 BBS response to Searle, in the course of discussion with Hofstadter — not as a joint coinage. "Co-originator of the … label" overstates Hofstadter's role. (Hofstadter's genuine associated contribution is the "turn all the knobs" method for testing intuition pumps, developed with Dennett in *The Mind's I*, 1981 — which is a method, not the label.)
- **Severity**: Low
- **Recommendation**: Replace "co-originator of the 'intuition pump' label" with something accurate, e.g. "who with Dennett developed the practice of 'turning the knobs' on such intuition pumps" or simply "Douglas Hofstadter presses the scale objection…". The scale-objection attribution to Hofstadter is itself fine.

Learned distributed representations that carry predictive world-structure are the candidate for the non-lookup grounding the virtual-mind reply needs.
- **Suggested response**: The article already marks the claim contested and routes the substance to [[symbol-grounding-problem]]/[[llm-consciousness]]; no change required, but the tension with the Luminous Room concession could be acknowledged in one clause rather than left implicit.
