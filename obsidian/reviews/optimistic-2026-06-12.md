## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
`dualist-perception` is a Chalmers showcase: the causal chain "from photons... through retinal transduction... yet somewhere the redness of red appears — the physical description never entails the phenomenal quality" is the explanatory gap stated at its cleanest, and the knowledge-argument framing ("a physicalist can acknowledge this gap while insisting it is merely epistemic. The dualist holds that the gap is ontological") draws the epistemic/ontological line exactly where Chalmers draws it. `consciousness-as-perceptual-architect` extends the gap to *both* poles of perception — "they do not explain why stable priors feel like seeing a white wall, or why updated models feel like tasting minerality" — so constancy and learning each become a fresh instance of the hard problem rather than a restatement of one.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
`interaction-problem-across-traditions` gives Stapp his most flattering setting: the Type-4 "quantum openings" family is presented as offering "a mechanism — or at least the outline of one" where Type-2 reflection offers "a metaphor," and the decoherence objection is met with the actual disputed-timescale literature (Hagan, Hameroff & Tuszyński 2002 vs. Tegmark) rather than hand-waved. Crucially the article resists over-selling: Stapp's selection-not-injection structure is shown to *recur* across traditions without that recurrence being banked as evidence the mechanism is correct.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
`dualist-perception` keeps the "what-it's-like" question load-bearing throughout — the perspectival-character section ("why is *this* perspective mine?") is Nagel's bat question recast for indexical identity, and the transparency/intentionality section insists "a pattern of neuronal firing is not 'about' a tree in any intrinsic sense." `consciousness-as-perceptual-architect` deploys Husserl's adumbration (*Abschattung*) precisely — "we see the circle *in* the trapezoid" — to show constancy is built into intentional structure rather than bolted on as post-processing.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
`interaction-problem-across-traditions` gives Whitehead a full and fair hearing as Type-3 "dissolution through process," pairing him with Mulla Ṣadrā's *al-ḥarakat al-jawhariyyah* and Buddhist dependent origination, and — importantly — naming process monism "the typology's deepest challenge" and "the strongest competitor" rather than a strawman. The article's honesty about *why* the Map declines the move ("the two frameworks part company at their starting axioms... neither framing is forced by the comparative record") is the Process Philosopher's resonance handled without slippage: process dynamism is borrowed, process monism declined, and the difference is located at bedrock axioms, not at a contested evidential claim.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
`dualist-perception`'s active-constitution section ("the perceiver is not a passive screen but an active participant in constituting what appears") and the bistable-perception case (consciousness "actively participating in settling which reconstruction becomes experience") give Kane's room-for-agency intuition a perceptual home — agency enters where neural dynamics "alone do not fully determine" the outcome. Notably the article concedes the same point the architect article does: whether it is *consciousness* doing the settling "remains tenet-dependent rather than empirically forced."

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
The cluster's epistemic humility is structural rather than decorative. `perceptual-failure-and-the-interface` repeatedly closes a section by marking what the evidence does *not* reach — "the gradation data are compatible with either framing; what they constrain is the *threshold-emergence* picture specifically... a narrower conclusion than vindication of the interface model." That discipline of claiming the narrower conclusion is McGinn's register: knowing where our explanations stop.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This persona's verdict is load-bearing for the whole cluster, because every article touches a tenet-load-bearing interface claim that could slide from "compatible" to "confirmed" — and none of them does.

- **`perceptual-failure-and-the-interface` is the calibration exemplar of the cluster.** Its "Predictive-Processing Objection" section does the hardest possible thing: it runs all four of its own bandwidth signatures *against* the strongest rival and concedes "Predictive processing does not stumble on the pattern; it predicts it." It then states the honest conclusion outright — "perceptual failure refutes predictive processing — it does not" — and relocates the genuine disagreement "one level down, at the hard problem." The Occam's-Razor section goes further and concedes the **common-cause null** by name: "the count of four then collapses to one mechanism observed four ways, and the extra ontology buys nothing the signatures demand." This is *tenet-as-evidence-upgrade praise-worthily not done* in its purest form — the article had every opportunity to bank four converging signatures as four pieces of evidence and explicitly refused, claiming the extra ontology only "one level down" where physicalist and interface readings are genuinely not equal in explanatory power.
- **The named-rival discipline is exemplary.** `perceptual-failure` refuses to conscript hostile witnesses: "Martina is no ally of the Map's dualism... the argument must face her actual position rather than conscript her conclusion," and similarly states Skrzypulec's interoceptive account "closes the content-level gap" on its own terms before locating the residual disagreement at the framework boundary. This is the antidote to the [[outer-review-fabricates-target-quotes]] and cite-as-ally failure modes — the article models how to use an opponent's argument without misrepresenting their stance.
- **The psychedelic/NDE filter cases are calibrated *down*, not up.** The Selective-Preservation section explicitly de-escalates its own best-looking evidence: psychedelics are "not the clean 'less brain, more mind' exhibit it is sometimes made to be" (signal-diversity *increase*, suspected vascular contamination of the blood-flow decreases), and van Lommel's NDE case is "weaker still" with its flatline-EEG premise "methodologically contested." An article that downgrades its own filter-theory exhibits is exactly the restraint this persona exists to praise.
- **`interaction-problem-across-traditions` keeps the recurrence finding in its lane.** "A puzzle's reappearance across cultures confirms the puzzle, not the metaphysics offered to settle it" — and the Relation-to-Site section names treating recurrence as evidence for the *answer* "the Occam shortcut the... tenet warns against." Cross-traditional convergence is used to broaden warrant for the *problem* while explicitly declining to widen the evidential base for the *solution*. Textbook separation of registers.

The Process Philosopher and Hardline Empiricist **converge** across this cluster rather than conflict — the signal that the possibility/probability tension has been resolved honestly. Where last cycle's `predictive-processing-and-dualism` produced a genuine persona conflict over its "most productive scientific ally" framing (a calibration concern, since fixed), this cluster contains no such slippage to flag.

## Content Strengths

### topics/perceptual-failure-and-the-interface.md
- **Strongest point**: The "Predictive-Processing Objection" section, which weaponises the article's own evidence against itself and survives — conceding the rival predicts all four signatures, then relocating the disagreement to the hard problem rather than defending a lost position.
- **Notable quote**: "The count of four then collapses to one mechanism observed four ways, and the extra ontology buys nothing the signatures demand."
- **Why it works**: It pre-empts the single most damaging objection (the common-cause null) by stating it more sharply than a critic would, which converts a vulnerability into a demonstration of calibration.

### topics/dualist-perception.md
- **Strongest point**: The three-way dissociation architecture — blindsight (processing without experience), lucid dreaming (experience without input), bistable perception (consciousness actively settling reconstruction) — mapped cleanly onto the capability-division framework.
- **Notable quote**: "Together, blindsight (processing without experience) and lucid dreaming (experience without input) establish that phenomenal rendering and information processing are separable capabilities."
- **Why it works**: Three independent dissociation types triangulate the brain-side/mind-side boundary, and each is hedged against the physicalist "specific-type-of-processing" reply rather than presented as a knockdown.

### topics/interaction-problem-across-traditions.md
- **Strongest point**: The five-type structural typology (contact / non-contact / process-dissolution / quantum / monist-avoidance) imposed on a genuinely global survey (Sāṃkhya, Nyāya, Avicenna, Mulla Ṣadrā, Buddhist Abhidharma, Neo-Confucian, Bantu, Yoruba), with the Map's own position located *inside* the typology (Type 4, borrowing from Type 2).
- **Notable quote**: "Descartes' pineal gland and Nyāya's atomic *manas* are the same kind of answer."
- **Why it works**: It earns genuine cross-cultural breadth without the lazy "all traditions agree" move — it documents sharp divergence on answers while defending convergence only on the problem.

### topics/consciousness-as-perceptual-architect.md
- **Strongest point**: The constancy/learning pairing as "complementary architecture" — one holds existing perceptual objects steady, the other constructs new ones, both attention-gated, both facing the same gap — unified under the perceptual-ratchet / downward-causation-across-developmental-timescales idea.
- **Notable quote**: "Where constancy holds existing perceptual objects steady, learning constructs new ones."
- **Why it works**: It makes two superficially opposed phenomena (stabilising vs. transforming) reveal a single principle, and the closing paragraph explicitly concedes the architect reading is "underdetermined by the data... one candidate interpretation among several."
