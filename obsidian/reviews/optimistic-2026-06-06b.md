## Executive Summary

`non-temporal-consciousness.md` is one of the Map's strongest irreducibility arguments and a model of evidential restraint: it advances a genuinely bold thesis (consciousness has a non-temporal ground) while explicitly demoting it to a "load-bearing weaker claim" and quarantining the strong constitutive identification at `[Speculative]`. ## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The category-mismatch argument (premises 1–4 in "The Argument for Irreducibility") is exactly the structural move Chalmers' anti-reductionism wants: it locates an *in-principle* feature of physical description (temporal succession) that the explanandum lacks, rather than appealing to a mere current explanatory shortfall. Chalmers would especially approve the article's refusal to overplay it — calling it "the most elegant route to irreducibility the Map has — but conditional on a contested premise." That is the hard problem stated as a conditional, not smuggled in as a conclusion.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
The "Minimal Quantum Interaction" section is a clean Stappian relocation: it moves the conscious role off the femtosecond collapse moment (which the timing-gap objection kills) and onto the *post-decoherence* improper-mixture interface "the quantum formalism does not answer." Stapp would recognise the von-Neumann-process-1 spirit while appreciating that the article does not pretend to supply the dynamics — it names the `amplification-void` chasm as the unbridged gap. The non-temporal selector "not constrained to act *at* a specific moment" is a genuinely novel way around the timing problem.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The whole article centres first-person constitution: Husserl's absolute flow, Frischhut's alert timelessness, the witness-layer. Nagel would praise the disciplined separation of the *metaphysical* "non-temporal" from the *phenomenological* "atemporal" in the opening — two senses that lazier treatments conflate — and the recognition that the meditator's report is data that objective temporal neuroscience cannot itself produce.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The Bergson section's proposed synthesis ("durée is the output; the absolute flow is the source") is a Whiteheadian concrescence read in all but name, and the Further Reading link to `process-philosophy` ("Whitehead's concrescence as a parallel non-temporal constituting activity") makes the kinship explicit. Whitehead would applaud that the article avoids crude substance-dualism here — the non-temporal ground is a *constituting activity*, not a thing-behind-the-curtain.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
The closing link to `topics/free-will` ("how a non-temporal ground dissolves the Libet timing objection") is the agency-relevant payoff: if the conscious selector is not pinned to a clock-instant, the Libet "brain decides 350ms early" objection loses its grip, because there is no privileged moment the decision must beat. Kane would value that the article keeps a persisting agent in play (the substance-leaning sub-reading) precisely so that selection remains *someone's*.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
McGinn would single out the article's repeated "the dispute cannot be settled from inside physics" / "the Map cannot refute the *catuṣkoṭi* from outside its own framework" admissions. This is cognitive-closure humility done right: the eternalism dispute is named "bedrock," the constructivist critique is granted as undefeatable in its own terms, and the article still extracts a modest survivable claim rather than declaring victory.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This is the load-bearing verdict, and it is strongly positive. The article repeatedly does the tenet-as-evidence-upgrade move that it is praise-worthy to *not* do:

- **It explicitly declines the five-traditions-as-five-tests slide**: "the cross-tradition cluster carries the evidential weight of *one* recurring pattern… not five independent confirmations, and it should not be counted as though each tradition were a fresh test." This is exactly the restraint the persona exists to reward.
- **It names the tenet-coherent-not-evidence-elevating pattern in structure if not in phrase**: the "partial-credit response" explicitly separates the negative claim the phenomenology *does* support (consciousness is not constitutively temporal) from the positive substance-subject claim that "originates in the dualism tenet and its downstream commitments, not in the phenomenology."
- **It grants the rival the same accommodation**: "the anaesthesia and split-brain data therefore *constrain* both framings rather than *confirming* the interface reading over its rival." Refusing to let the fragmentation evidence over-confirm the dualist reading is precisely calibrated.
- **The `[Speculative]` tag on the strongest constitutive formulation** plus the falsifiability admission ("No clear empirical test currently distinguishes this constitutive identity from a deflationary alternative") keeps the registers separate exactly as the evidential-status scale demands.

Crucially, the **Process Philosopher and Hardline Empiricist converge** here rather than conflict: Whitehead-friendliness (the constituting-activity reading) and evidential discipline (the partial-credit firewall) praise *different* aspects of the same article, and no passage uses process-philosophical resonance as the engine of a tier-upgrade. By the skill's own test, that convergence is the signature of an article that has resolved the tension honestly. No calibration concern to flag.

## Content Strengths

### topics/non-temporal-consciousness.md
- **Strongest point**: The "Madhyamaka Challenge to the Persisting Subject" section — the article volunteers the objection most damaging to its own downstream use (a non-temporal ground with no enduring subject cannot be a quantum selector, "because there is no subject *whose* selection it would be"), then takes the honest narrow path rather than wishing it away.
- **Notable quote**: "the cross-tradition cluster carries the evidential weight of *one* recurring pattern — alert awareness reportable without temporal markers — not five independent confirmations."
- **Why it works**: It pre-empts the strongest counterargument (double-counting convergent traditions) inside the very paragraph that introduces the convergence, so the reader never gets to raise it as a gotcha.

- **Second strength**: The McTaggart A-series / B-series disambiguation of Premise 1. By noticing that a tenseless B-series physics "is no more *flowing* than the non-temporal consciousness posited here," the article forecloses the obvious "but physics has timeless formulations too" rebuttal (Wheeler–DeWitt, Barbour, Rovelli) and shows the categorical contrast survives because it rests on the regress argument, not the absence of a time coordinate.
