## Pessimistic Analysis Summary

### Critical Issues Found

- **Misattribution of "third identity" to Avicenna**: The article attributed the term "third identity" to Avicenna's *al-Shifā'*, but this term comes from Jamali & Golshani (2019), a modern interpretation paper by Iranian physicists. Mainstream Avicenna scholarship (SEP, IEP) does not use this term. **Resolution**: Rewrote to describe Avicenna's actual account of holistic soul-body governance through the practical intellect, with the modern quantum resonance attributed to Jamali & Golshani.

- **Anachronistic quote attributed to Avicenna**: The passage "cannot be at a specific space-time point, but must affect the whole dynamics and the motion of matter from the initial point to the final point" uses modern physics language ("space-time point") impossible for an 11th-century philosopher. This was from Jamali & Golshani's quantum-mechanical reinterpretation, presented in quotation marks as if Avicenna's own words. **Resolution**: Removed the false quotation and replaced with accurate characterization of Avicenna's position, with the modern interpretive framework properly attributed.

### Medium Issues Found

- **Wang Yangming characterization overstated**: "knowing and acting are identical" was a common simplification. Wang's *zhīxíng héyī* is better characterized as "inseparable" rather than "identical"—the doctrine denies akrasia rather than asserting metaphysical identity. **Resolution**: Rephrased to "genuine knowing and acting are inseparable" with the Chinese term added.

- **Sāṃkhyakārikā dating imprecise**: "c. 350 CE" was the lower end of a scholarly range; standard dating is c. 350–450 CE, with recent work pushing as late as 447–490 CE. **Resolution**: Updated to "c. 350–450 CE" in both body and references.

- **Missing citation for Jamali & Golshani**: The Avicenna section drew on this modern source but didn't cite it. **Resolution**: Added full citation to References section.

- **Missing cross-links**: The [[pairing-problem]] concept was in the frontmatter but not linked in the body where the same problem is discussed (Nyāya section). [[dualism]] was similarly unlinked. **Resolution**: Added both cross-links at natural points in the text.
