# Referee Report: Audit of "Consciousness-Selecting Neural Patterns"

## Introduction and Epistemological Strategy of the Manuscript

The submitted manuscript, "Consciousness-Selecting Neural Patterns," presents an ambitious, systematic defense of interactionist dualism. It attempts to thread a highly specific needle between the causal closure of the physical domain and the phenomenological reality of conscious volition. The central thesis proposes that consciousness exerts causal efficacy over physical brain activity not by injecting thermodynamic energy or violating fundamental conservation laws, but by selecting which specific outcomes actualize from the "improper mixtures" generated by quantum decoherence. To support this framework, the manuscript surveys multiple candidate mechanisms, prominently featuring the Penrose-Hameroff Orchestrated Objective Reduction (Orch-OR) theory, Henry Stapp's Quantum Zeno effect formulation, and the Chalmers-McQueen consciousness-collapse Continuous Spontaneous Localization (CSL) proposal.

While the scope of the manuscript's theoretical synthesis is characteristic of grand systems-building in the philosophy of mind, an exhaustive audit of its empirical claims, literature citations, counterargument coverage, and logical inferences reveals profound, fatal deficiencies. The manuscript exhibits a systematic pattern of epistemological gerrymandering: it routinely represents speculative, computational, or heavily criticized biological quantum models as secure empirical stepping stones, while simultaneously masking vast philosophical gaps behind axiomatic "tenets" rather than rigorous argumentation.

Crucially, the manuscript selectively appropriates the 2020s physics and neuroscience literature. It relies upon heavily contested or formally rebutted findings to support its quantum biological premises, yet completely ignores the peer-reviewed defeaters published in the very same disciplines. The following report dissects the submission across its core domains: the fundamental misapplication of quantum decoherence and the measurement problem; the misrepresentation of the experimental quantum biology literature; the mishandling of neurocognitive bandwidth constraints; the evasion of strict experimental bounds on spontaneous collapse models; and the philosophical deficits in its treatment of competing metaphysical frameworks.

## Part I: The Ontological Status of the Post-Decoherence Gap

The central physical claim of the manuscript is that consciousness operates at the "post-decoherence gap." The text asserts that while environmental decoherence successfully selects a preferred basis (einselection) and suppresses macroscopic interference, it leaves behind an "improper mixture" that does not justify the conclusion that the system is actually in a definite state. The manuscript argues that consciousness intervenes at this precise juncture, selecting a single outcome from the decohered options without needing to maintain fragile pre-decoherence quantum coherence. This framing fundamentally misunderstands the mathematical and physical nature of improper mixtures and misrepresents the measurement problem.

### The Category Error of Improper Mixtures

The manuscript heavily utilizes the philosophical distinction between proper and improper mixtures to carve out a causal space for conscious intervention. A proper mixture represents classical epistemic ignorance: the physical system is localized in a definite state, but the observer merely lacks the knowledge of which specific state obtains. An improper mixture, by contrast, is mathematically derived by taking the partial trace over the environmental degrees of freedom of a globally entangled system. The manuscript claims that because an improper mixture is not a proper mixture, the definite outcome remains physically "open" and available for consciousness to select.

This represents a profound category error in quantum foundations. An improper mixture does not provide an ontological menu of localized, classically distinct physical actualities waiting patiently to be "selected" by a non-physical mind. The density matrix of an improper mixture merely encapsulates the local statistics of a subsystem that remains globally entangled with its environment (ρS​=TrE​∣Ψ⟩⟨Ψ∣). The superposition has not vanished; it has simply become delocalized across the system-environment boundary.

To claim that consciousness "selects" an outcome from an improper mixture requires an explicit dynamical collapse mechanism that instantaneously breaks the unitary evolution of the global entangled state. The manuscript attempts to avoid postulating a new physical collapse law by asserting that the conscious selection is "empirically indistinguishable from chance" and perfectly preserves Born rule statistics. If the mental intervention flawlessly preserves the Born measure and leaves the Hilbert-space geometry strictly intact, the proposed "selection" is mathematically equivalent to the standard unitary interpretation of the density matrix. The manuscript is attempting to extract a dynamical, causal event from a purely epistemic statistical matrix. By proposing a causal intervention that is constructed to perfectly mimic quantum noise, the manuscript inadvertently constructs an epiphenomenal theory, severely contradicting its own foundational commitment to "Bidirectional Interaction".

### The Zeno Effect and the Timescale Contradiction

To explain how consciousness holds a selected state in place, the manuscript invokes Henry Stapp’s formulation of the Quantum Zeno effect. The text suggests that conscious mental effort acts as a rapid series of observations (Process 1) that "holds" a desired neural pattern against environmental degradation.

The manuscript acknowledges the severe "timing gap problem," noting that Tegmark’s calculations place microtubule decoherence at 10−13 seconds, whereas neural decisions and conscious volitional acts require hundreds of milliseconds (e.g., 200–550 ms). For the Quantum Zeno effect to freeze the evolution of a quantum state, the frequency of the projection operators (the conscious observations) must significantly exceed the rate of environmental interaction. If the environmental decoherence rate operates on the order of femtoseconds (10−13 seconds), the conscious projection events must occur at an even faster frequency (e.g., 1014 observations per second).

The manuscript fails entirely to explain how a macroscopic phenomenological entity like "conscious attention"—which neurobiologically emerges over hundreds of milliseconds—could discretely instantiate highly targeted projection operators at a frequency of 1014 Hz. The text attempts to hand-wave this by suggesting that attention is "cumulative rather than instantaneous". This is physically incoherent. The Zeno effect does not operate via slow, cumulative phenomenological effort; it operates strictly via high-frequency state-vector projections. Suggesting that a cognitively slow mechanism can instantiate femtosecond quantum projections leaves the mechanism functionally untestable and physically contradictory.

## Part II: The Misrepresentation of Experimental Quantum Biology

A major structural pillar of the manuscript is its reliance on recent experimental papers to argue that the biological environment is no longer universally considered too "warm, wet, and noisy" for quantum coherence. While it is true that the field of quantum biology has advanced over the last decade, the manuscript systematically inflates the scope, scale, and relevance of these findings. It frequently cites highly specific, isolated, or heavily contested papers as established empirical mandates for macroscopic quantum consciousness.

### Conflating Excitonic and Gravitational Coherence

The text prominently highlights Craddock et al. (2017), noting a computational prediction of a (613±8) THz collective tubulin oscillation peak, and cites Wiest (2025) as providing empirical evidence for "room-temperature quantum effects" in microtubules. Furthermore, the manuscript relies on Babcock et al. (2024) to argue for superradiant excitonic states at physiological temperatures.

This logical sequence masks a severe conflation of physical regimes. A frequency of 613 THz corresponds to the visible/near-infrared electromagnetic spectrum (6.13×1014 Hz), which precisely characterizes localized molecular vibrational modes and excitonic energy transfer within electron clouds. The existence of room-temperature vibrational quantum coherence—conceptually similar to the well-documented Fenna-Matthews-Olson (FMO) complex in photosynthesis—does not satisfy the rigorous physical requirements of the Orchestrated Objective Reduction (Orch-OR) theory upon which the manuscript relies.

Orch-OR explicitly requires the sustained macroscopic spatial superposition of the *mass geometry* of the tubulin lattice to reach the gravitational self-energy threshold (E=ℏ/t) proposed by Penrose. Excitonic superradiance in isolated, in vitro tryptophan mega-networks involves entirely different physical degrees of freedom (electron clouds and dipole moments) than the conformational mechanical mass superpositions required for Orch-OR's consciousness-collapse mechanism. By treating evidence for excitonic energy transfer as corroboration for gravitational mass-superposition collapse, the manuscript commits an elementary error in condensed matter physics, suggesting that ultraviolet fluorescence in isolated proteins provides a biological mandate for objective reduction in living neural tissue.

### The Retracted Reality of Macroscopic MRI Entanglement

Perhaps the most egregious empirical failure in the manuscript is its handling of Kerskens and Pérez (2022). The manuscript cites this paper repeatedly, alongside Wiest (2025), as providing "direct physical evidence of a macroscopic quantum entangled state in the living human brain". The text appends only the mildest possible caveat, describing the finding as merely "awaiting independent replication".

This framing demonstrates profound academic negligence, deliberately ignoring published, peer-reviewed literature that directly dismantled the theoretical foundation of the Kerskens and Pérez experiment. In a 2023 formal Comment published in the *Journal of Physics Communications*, experts in magnetic resonance imaging explicitly demonstrated that the intermolecular multiple-quantum coherences (iMQCs) measured by Kerskens and Pérez are thoroughly classical phenomena. The comment proved mathematically that iMQCs in fluid-like solutions are fully derivable using a modified classical Bloch equation. The authors of the rebuttal definitively established that in standard NMR experiments on biological tissues, the initial density matrix is completely separable into a direct product (ρ=ρ1​⊗ρ2​⋯⊗ρN​). Because the state remains separable, it strictly violates the definition of quantum entanglement, leading the physicists to state unequivocally that such signals "are absolutely not a witness to entanglement".

By failing to cite, engage with, or even acknowledge the 2023 published defeater, the manuscript falsely presents a theoretically debunked methodological error as a viable, "awaiting replication" proof of macroscopic brain entanglement.

### Anesthetic Selectivity and the Evasion of Classical Pharmacology

The text further argues that the selective binding of inhalational anesthetics to microtubules provides indirect evidence for quantum consciousness mechanisms. Citing Khan et al. (2024) and Wiest (2025), the manuscript notes that stabilizing microtubules with the drug epothilone B delays anesthetic-induced unconsciousness in rats, which it claims disrupts "quantum activities in orchestrated proteins".

However, the manuscript fails to rigorously weigh this against straightforward classical pharmacological explanations. Microtubules are not merely inert scaffolding; they physically govern the highly dynamic intracellular transport of synaptic vesicles, neurotransmitter receptors, mitochondria, and ion channels. Altering microtubule polymerization dynamics classically impacts synaptic efficacy, vesicular release probabilities, and overall neural network connectivity. The manuscript commits a severe inferential leap by treating any pharmacological interaction with the cytoskeleton as *de facto* evidence of quantum coherence disruption. A neurobiologically rigorous paper must explicitly rule out classical mechanistic disruption of synaptic transport before defaulting to quantum superposition collapse. The manuscript makes no attempt to do so.

## Part III: The 10 Bits/Second Bandwidth Paradox and Sensorimotor Defeaters

To explain how highly localized quantum indeterminacies can scale up to govern macroscopic human behavior without requiring trillions of simultaneous, micro-managed conscious decisions, the manuscript introduces a neurocognitive "bandwidth constraint." Relying exclusively on a highly specific hypothesis by Zheng and Meister (2024/2025), the text claims that conscious thought operates at a severely bottlenecked rate of approximately 10 bits per second, compared to the 109 bits per second processed by the unconscious sensory architecture.

The manuscript utilizes this 10 bits/s figure to solve its quantum scaling problem through a concept it calls "adaptive computational depth". It argues that consciousness does not need to execute high-frequency selections of individual neurons, but rather selects coarse-grained, high-level "policies" (e.g., "raise arm"), leaving the quantum physical substrate to algorithmically instantiate the micro-details.

This entire structural argument is critically compromised by the manuscript's failure to engage with the immediate, peer-reviewed refutations of the Zheng and Meister hypothesis. Sauerbrei and Pruszynski (2025), publishing in *Nature Neuroscience*, demonstrated that the 10 bits/s calculation is derived from highly constrained, abstract, and discrete cognitive tasks (e.g., typing text, solving Rubik's cubes, memorizing numbers) and represents an artificial lower bound rather than a true biological upper limit of conscious processing.

Sauerbrei and Pruszynski argue forcefully that real-time sensorimotor control—which is undeniably directed by conscious volition, phenomenological effort, and intention in unpredictable environments—requires processing speeds orders of magnitude higher. They note that maintaining the phase, amplitude, and duty cycle of even a single muscle across a 250-millisecond stride cannot possibly be specified or compressed into three binary bits of information. Everyday, simultaneous, multi-joint movements in unpredictable terrain involve continuous feedback loops between sensory and motor systems that demand vast bitrates far exceeding the 10 bits/s limit.

The omission of the Sauerbrei and Pruszynski (2025) rebuttal is catastrophic for the manuscript's internal logic. If conscious volitional agency operates at processing speeds substantially higher than 10 bits per second to manage sensorimotor feedback loops, the manuscript's proposed quantum "policy selection" mechanism collapses. A higher bandwidth requirement dictates that consciousness cannot merely act as a low-resolution "policy selector" but must actively participate in the high-dimensional modulation of specific neural states. This shatters the manuscript's attempt to use Zheng and Meister to bridge the gap between microscopic quantum indeterminacy and macroscopic human behavior.

## Part IV: Spontaneous Collapse Constraints and the Gran Sasso Evasion

The manuscript maps out several candidate laws for psychophysical coupling, heavily emphasizing spontaneous collapse theories and objective reduction models to solve the "prebiotic collapse problem" (how the universe localized before observers existed). However, the manuscript exhibits a pattern of citing theoretical models while completely ignoring the stringent empirical bounds placed on those precise models by physical experiments conducted in the early 2020s.

When surveying objective reduction theories, the manuscript prominently features the Diósi-Penrose (DP) model, where a quantum superposition collapses once the difference in gravitational self-energy reaches a specific mass threshold (EG​), and the Continuous Spontaneous Localization (CSL) model, where a universal noise field induces collapse proportional to mass density. The manuscript treats these as completely open, viable mechanisms for consciousness-induced triggering.

However, the manuscript fails entirely to address the landmark experimental results from the Gran Sasso underground laboratory. In 2021, Donadi et al. conducted an ultra-high sensitivity test of the parameter-free Diósi-Penrose model using a high-purity germanium detector, shielding it from cosmic radiation deep underground.

The physical principle tested is irrefutable: any spontaneous collapse model involving a random, instantaneous spatial localization of wave functions inherently induces a random displacement of charged particles. This discrete displacement must, according to standard electrodynamics, result in the emission of spontaneous bremsstrahlung radiation. The Gran Sasso experiment searched for this predicted radiation signature and found a massive discrepancy. While the parameter-free Diósi-Penrose model mathematically predicted the emission of approximately 70,000 photons in the targeted energy range over the observation period, the detector observed only 576 photons. The observed photons were entirely consistent with the expected background environmental noise.

This result definitively ruled out the natural, parameter-free version of the DP model and placed extraordinarily strict bounds on all variants of Continuous Spontaneous Localization (CSL) models. Any journal submission in the late 2020s proposing consciousness-triggered CSL or DP dynamics must mathematically account for the lack of observed spontaneous bremsstrahlung radiation. The manuscript's failure to deeply integrate the Gran Sasso constraints into its assessment of objective reduction frameworks leaves its physics deeply out of date and theoretically unanchored.

## Part V: Philosophical Deficits and Covert Tenet Bracketing

Beyond empirical omissions, the philosophical argumentation in the manuscript relies heavily on axiomatic declarations, termed "tenets" (e.g., *Minimal Quantum Interaction*, *Bidirectional Interaction*, *No Many Worlds*), which are used covertly to bypass rigorous engagement with competing interpretations of quantum mechanics and the philosophy of mind.

### The Indexical Objection and the Rejection of Many-Worlds

To support the reality of wave-function collapse (and therefore the strict logical necessity of a "selection" mechanism), the manuscript relies heavily on its "No Many Worlds" tenet. The primary justification offered for rejecting the Many-Worlds Interpretation (MWI) of Everett is the "indexical objection." The manuscript claims that because MWI posits that all physical branches are realized, it cannot answer the vertiginous question, "Why am I *this* branch rather than any of the others?". Because MWI supposedly dissolves personal identity and indexical reality, the manuscript dismisses it out of hand to preserve its framework.

This dismissal represents a profound failure to engage with the contemporary philosophical literature on quantum foundations. The manuscript completely omits Christian List’s highly relevant 2023 paper, "The Many-Worlds Theory of Consciousness," published in *Noûs*. List utilizes the exact same vertiginous indexical problem not as a defeater for Everettian mechanics, but as the foundation to formulate a modal realism of first-personally-centered worlds. List’s framework demonstrates that the indexical question does not strictly falsify Many-Worlds; rather, it motivates a different metaphysical topology where a multitude of worlds exist, each objectively real, with one uniquely present per subject. By hiding behind the axiomatic "No Many Worlds" tenet, the manuscript artificially shelters its core thesis from the most rigorous recent adaptations of Everettian mechanics, treating a live philosophical debate as a settled impossibility.

### The Mischaracterization of Illusionism and the Phenomenal Concept Strategy

In its attempt to secure a non-reductive causal role for phenomenal consciousness, the manuscript attempts to dispatch Epiphenomenalism and Illusionism (associated with Frankish and Dennett). The text argues that if a functional brain state misrepresents itself as having phenomenal properties, it must either be truly phenomenal (defeating the illusionist) or be "systematically wrong about its own nature in a way no other functional state is". The manuscript demands that illusionists explain why this specific class of self-representation is uniquely unreliable, claiming they have failed to do so.

This framing drastically mischaracterizes the current state of the illusionist dialectic. Contemporary illusionists argue that the unique unreliability of introspective representation is a highly predictable, mathematically necessary consequence of the brain's limited access to its own sub-personal computational mechanics. The brain tracks external objects with high-fidelity, high-bandwidth sensory modalities, but it tracks its own internal states using low-resolution, heuristic monitoring systems that compress complex, distributed neural dynamics into simple, unified "phenomenal" properties (the predictive processing account of introspective opacity). The manuscript asserts that illusionists have not discharged their explanatory burden, yet it entirely ignores the extensive literature on predictive coding, metacognitive limitations, and the Phenomenal Concept Strategy that provides exactly the functional explanation the manuscript claims is missing.

### The Architecture of Unfalsifiability

Perhaps the most disqualifying methodological weakness of the manuscript is its inadvertent creation of a completely unfalsifiable conceptual framework. The text posits that conscious selection operates within the "post-decoherence gap" in a manner that meticulously preserves the Born rule probability distribution, explicitly conceding that this makes the mechanism "empirically indistinguishable from chance" under any aggregate statistical test current or foreseeable instruments could run.

Simultaneously, the manuscript argues that an "amplification void" obscures the structural tracing of these quantum effects from the microscopic scale up to macroscopic behavior, and an "agency void" implies the causal efficacy is unverifiable from the first-person perspective. By explicitly combining an empirically invisible quantum interaction (Born rule compliance) with an untraceable physical amplification chain (stochastic neural scaling), the manuscript constructs a triple-screen of unfalsifiability. It insulates its core thesis from aggregate statistical verification, mechanistic neural tracing, and direct phenomenological proof.

An academic manuscript proposing a novel physical interaction at the intersection of mind and matter must articulate clear, operational falsification criteria. Designing a theoretical mechanism explicitly to evade all modern instrumentation and statistical deviation testing pushes the manuscript entirely out of the realm of scientific hypothesis and into pure metaphysical speculation.

## Specific Weaknesses Justifying Rejection

The analysis identifies the following five specific weaknesses that render the manuscript unfit for publication in its current form. Each highlights a severe omission, contradiction, or mishandling of the 2020–2025 peer-reviewed literature.

1. **Bandwidth Constraint Omission and Sensorimotor Neglect:** The manuscript's proposed "coarse-grained policy selection" relies entirely on an upper conscious processing limit of 10 bits per second established by Zheng and Meister (2024/2025). The manuscript completely omits the peer-reviewed refutation by Sauerbrei and Pruszynski (2025) in *Nature Neuroscience*, who demonstrated that this figure fundamentally misrepresents the high-bandwidth requirements of conscious sensorimotor control, thereby invalidating the manuscript’s primary neural scaling mechanism.
2. **Mishandling of MRI Entanglement Evidence:** The manuscript repeatedly cites Kerskens and Pérez (2022) as providing direct physical evidence of macroscopic brain entanglement, characterizing it only as "awaiting independent replication". It critically omits the 2023 formal Comment published in the *Journal of Physics Communications*, which mathematically proved that the intermolecular multiple-quantum coherences (iMQCs) observed by Kerskens and Pérez are purely classical phenomena derived from modified Bloch equations, and are completely incapable of witnessing quantum entanglement.
3. **Failure to Address Spontaneous Collapse Experimental Constraints:** While relying on objective reduction and spontaneous collapse models (DP and CSL) to justify the reality of wave-function collapse prior to consciousness, the manuscript ignores the 2021 Gran Sasso underground laboratory experiments by Donadi et al.. These experiments measured spontaneous bremsstrahlung radiation from a germanium detector and definitively ruled out the parameter-free Diósi-Penrose model, severely restricting the physically viable parameter space for the manuscript's proposed framework.
4. **Philosophical Bracketing of the Indexical Objection:** The manuscript axiomatically rejects the Many-Worlds Interpretation (MWI) by invoking the "vertiginous question" of indexical identity. It fails entirely to engage with Christian List's (2023) *The Many-Worlds Theory of Consciousness*, which uses that exact same indexical formulation to establish a coherent modal realism of first-personally-centered worlds, proving that the manuscript’s philosophical dismissal is unearned, outdated, and incomplete.
5. **Conflation of Excitonic and Gravitational Coherence:** The manuscript mishandles the quantum biology literature by using Wiest (2025) and Babcock et al. (2024) to support room-temperature quantum effects for Penrose's Orch-OR theory. The ultraviolet superradiance observed by Babcock et al. involves excitonic vibrational states in tryptophan networks, which mathematically and physically cannot satisfy the macroscopic mass-geometry superposition requirements of Penrose’s gravitational objective reduction threshold.

## Verdict

The manuscript requires absolute rejection due to its systematic omission of critical empirical refutations in the 2020s literature, its conflation of distinct physical phenomena to force a false empirical mandate, and its reliance on axiomatic tenets to engineer an unfalsifiable, untestable framework.
