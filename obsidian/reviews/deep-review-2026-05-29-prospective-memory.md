## Pessimistic Analysis Summary

### Critical Issues Found (all citation defects — fixed)

1. **Misattributed empirical finding (Schacter & Addis 2007).** The "hippocampus shows *greater* activation during future simulation" claim was attributed to Schacter & Addis (2007) *Phil. Trans. R. Soc. B* — a theoretical review that does not report that fMRI result. The finding is from **Addis, Wong & Schacter (2007)**, *Neuropsychologia* 45(7):1363-1377. A sibling article ([[temporal-void]]) and its 2026-03-16 review had already caught and fixed this exact defect; the divergent (uncorrected) form survived here. **Resolution**: re-attributed the empirical finding to Addis, Wong & Schacter (2007) and added the reference. Web-verified.

2. **Source-conclusion mismatch (Szpunar et al. 2014).** The empirical claims "reduces temporal discounting, decreases impulsive decision-making" were cited to Szpunar, Spreng & Schacter (2014) *PNAS* — which is a **taxonomy/Perspective framework paper**, not an empirical demonstration. The behavioural finding comes from **Peters & Büchel (2010)** *Neuron* 66(1):138-148 and **Benoit, Gilbert & Burgess (2011)** *J. Neurosci.* 31(18):6771-6779. **Resolution**: attributed the empirical demonstration to those two papers, retained Szpunar (2014) for the taxonomic placement; added both references. Web-verified.

3. **Fabricated/garbled citation metadata (Conway 2016).** The reference read "Conway, M.A. et al. (2016). Episodic memory and autonoetic consciousness: personal and collective perspectives. *Frontiers in Psychology*, 7, 1." No such paper exists — the title fuses Conway (2001) *Phil. Trans.* with a 2016 date and a fabricated *Frontiers* venue + "7, 1" page (page-number fabrication red flag). The "extended present" claim it supports maps to **Conway, Loveday & Cole (2016)**, "The remembering–imagining system," *Memory Studies* 9(3):256-265. **Resolution**: corrected in-body cite and reference; web-verified the real paper.

4. **Wrong-paper attribution (Einstein & McDaniel 1990).** The monitoring-vs-spontaneous-retrieval two-route distinction was attributed to Einstein & McDaniel (1990) — the *aging-and-PM* study, which does not introduce that distinction. It is the **multiprocess framework of McDaniel & Einstein (2000)**, *Applied Cognitive Psychology* 14(7):S127-S144. **Resolution**: re-attributed to McDaniel & Einstein (2000), retained the 1990 cite anchored to the event-based PM paradigm it does introduce; added the 2000 reference. Web-verified.
