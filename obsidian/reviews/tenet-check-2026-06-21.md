## Notes

### Parsimony-located quantum action (corpus-wide pattern, already self-flagged)

- **Tenet in tension**: 5 (Occam's Razor Has Limits)
- **Issue**: Several articles motivate *where* consciousness acts (the quantum-measurement locus) partly by a least-disruption / simplest-interface consideration. Read carelessly this is the very move Tenet 5 warns against — leaning on simplicity in a domain of incomplete knowledge.
- **Why this is a NOTE, not a finding**: in each instance located, the corpus already names the tension explicitly and downgrades the move to a defeasible heuristic about where to look rather than evidence that consciousness acts there. Representative, well-handled case — `concepts/measurement-problem.md`: "Quantum measurement is the *least disruptive* locus for the causation consciousness requires — but that appeal to least disruption is a simplicity consideration, and the Map's own fifth tenet holds simplicity unreliable under incomplete knowledge. The move is therefore a defeasible heuristic for *where* to look, not evidence that consciousness acts there, and it stands in acknowledged tension with the tenet that warns against trusting it."
- **Recommendation**: No action required. Future checks should confirm new or condensed quantum-interface articles preserve this self-flagging rather than letting the least-disruption move read as positive evidence.
