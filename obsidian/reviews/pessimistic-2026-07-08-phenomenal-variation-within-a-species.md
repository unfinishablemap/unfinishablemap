# Pessimistic Review

**Date**: 2026-07-08
**Content reviewed**: `apex/phenomenal-variation-within-a-species.md`
**Lens**: adversarial-logical (does the newly-added 5th class, alexithymia, earn its place?)

## Executive Summary

The apex was refreshed this session by promoting alexithymia from a caveat to a full fifth
divergence class ("Emotional Access"). The addition is elegantly written but the integration is
incomplete and, on the logical merits, it *weakens* the article's load-bearing convergence
argument rather than strengthening it. Three genuine defects: (1) the article's own "What the
Wedge Does Not Do" section enumerates only **four** absorption strategies — one of which already
covers two classes and none of which is alexithymia's — directly contradicting the "five different
grain-shifts" claim the thesis rests on; (2) alexithymia's status as an *access-side* wedge is a
precondition the article declines to establish, not a caveat, and under the reading it leaves open
the case supports functionalist covariance instead; (3) the "feeds the common-cause null" claim is
stated indicatively but rests on an explicitly unconfirmed prediction, and pulls in the opposite
evidential direction from the wedge. Residual numeric staleness ("of the four", "the four classes
share") confirms the four→five upgrade was applied to the framing but not to the body.

## Critical Issues

### Issue 1: The addition of alexithymia undercuts the convergence-parsimony argument it was meant to strengthen

- **File**: `apex/phenomenal-variation-within-a-species.md`
- **Location**: §"What the Wedge Does" (line 136) vs §"What the Wedge Does Not Do" (lines 140–144) vs §"Why the Joint Package Matters" (lines 148–150); and lines 114, 130.
- **Problem**: The apex's entire thesis is a **parsimony-corrosion** argument: absorbing all classes "require[s] five different grain-shifts" (line 148), and "no single grain-shift covers the five domains" (line 150). But the article's own detailed enumeration in "What the Wedge Does Not Do" lists only **"Four named absorption strategies"** (line 140), and one of them — *metacognitive re-description* — is stated to absorb **two** classes at once ("absorbs source-attribution and cognitive-phenomenology reports", line 142). Alexithymia's nominal absorption (the "constructed-emotion reply", offered at line 136) is **absent entirely** from that enumeration. Worse, the prose twice classifies alexithymia as *"the same access-side failure"* as source-attribution's option-3 reading (line 114) and *"the emotional analogue of the source-attribution class's option-3 reading"* (line 130). Since the option-3/access reading is exactly what *metacognitive re-description* absorbs, a functionalist gets source-attribution + cognitive-phenomenology + alexithymia via **one** move. So the honest count of distinct absorption families is at most three (multi-realizability, representational sub-individuation, metacognitive/access re-description), the last covering three of five classes — the opposite of the "five distinct grain-shifts" the thesis needs. Adding alexithymia as the *third* class falling to the single access-side move arguably strengthens the functionalist's parsimony **defense**, not the wedge's parsimony **attack**.
- **Severity**: High
- **Recommendation**: Reconcile the two sections. Either (a) show why the constructed-emotion absorption of alexithymia is genuinely distinct from the metacognitive re-description that already absorbs source-attribution and cognitive-phenomenology (and add it as a fifth named strategy in "What the Wedge Does Not Do"), or (b) concede that the access-side classes share an absorption family and re-state the convergence claim in terms of *distinct absorption families* (≈3) rather than "five different grain-shifts." As written the article asserts a five-way count its own catalogue of live absorptions refutes.

### Issue 2: Alexithymia's "access-side" classification is a precondition treated as a caveat; the unsettled reading is functionalist-friendly

- **File**: `apex/phenomenal-variation-within-a-species.md`
- **Location**: §"Emotional Access: Alexithymia", lines 128 and 130.
- **Problem**: Line 130 asserts alexithymia "is the access-layer case — ... where the function-phenomenology identity fails from the access side rather than the phenomenal side." That classification is only earned if the felt affect is **present-but-inaccessible** (a genuine phenomenal state the machinery fails to report). But line 128 explicitly declines to settle this: "Whether the felt affect is present-but-inaccessible or genuinely attenuated is a mixed literature the Map does not try to settle." Under the *genuinely-attenuated* branch the felt affect is diminished **and** its functional/behavioural emotion signature is diminished — that is covariance, the pattern functionalism predicts, **not** a wedge against it. So the unsettled question is not a peripheral caveat about alexithymia's nature; it is the precondition on which alexithymia's membership in the anti-functionalist catalogue depends. This is a textbook epistemic/metaphysical equivocation (see `evidential-status-discipline`): the *epistemic* reading (a determinate felt state we cannot access/report) is recruited to carry the wedge, while the evidence the article itself cites is "mixed" between that and the *constitutive/attenuation* reading.
- **Severity**: Medium-High
- **Recommendation**: Make the conditional explicit — state that alexithymia contributes to the wedge *only under* the present-but-inaccessible reading, that the attenuation reading would instead be a covariance data-point compatible with functionalism, and that the case is therefore held provisionally pending resolution. Do not present "access-layer case" as settled when the article declines to settle the very fact that would make it one.

## Counterarguments to Address

### The common-cause-null "feed" is asserted indicatively but is an unconfirmed promissory note, and points the wrong way

- **Current content says** (line 130): "It also feeds the [[common-cause-null|common-cause null]]: if a population whose emotional-reporting architecture differs still tracks the same phenomenology, cross-tradition agreement about the irreducibility of consciousness cannot be a mere artefact of shared reporting machinery." Two sentences later: "no located study yet asks whether high-alexithymia individuals report the irreducibility signature itself. The exhibit is predicted, not confirmed."
- **A critic would argue**: (a) The "feed" is stated in the indicative ("It also feeds") though its antecedent ("*if* a population ... *still tracks the same phenomenology*") is exactly the thing the article admits is unconfirmed — this is asserting a contribution the evidence does not yet license. (b) There is an unflagged directional tension: the *wedge* requires alexithymics to show phenomenal **divergence** (different access, phenomenology comes apart from function), whereas the *common-cause null* requires alexithymics to **converge** on the same irreducibility-reporting despite different reporting architecture. The same population is being asked to diverge (for the wedge) and converge (for the null). (c) Since alexithymia is *difficulty reporting inner states*, the natural prior is that a group with degraded emotional self-report might *under*-report the irreducibility signature — the opposite of what the null needs — so the assumption that emotional-reporting difference leaves irreducibility-reporting untouched is itself substantive and unargued.
- **Suggested response**: Downgrade "It also feeds" to a conditional prediction ("*would* feed the common-cause null, were the patient-population test run and to come back convergent"), and add one clause distinguishing the divergence the wedge needs from the convergence the null needs, so the two roles alexithymia is asked to play are not silently in tension.

## Strengths (Brief)

- The article is admirably honest about alexithymia's two caveats (graded trait not lesion population; exhibit predicted not confirmed) — the raw materials for the fixes above are already on the page; the defects are ones of *reconciliation*, not of missing candour.
- The core four-class wedge (imagery, synaesthesia, source-attribution, cognitive phenomenology) remains well-sourced and the within-species framing (no zombie, no Nagelian leap) is a genuine methodological strength worth preserving through any revision.
- The single-structural-form framing (line 82) and the "locally absorbable, jointly recalcitrant" thesis are sound; the fixes should tighten the *count* the thesis rests on, not abandon it.
