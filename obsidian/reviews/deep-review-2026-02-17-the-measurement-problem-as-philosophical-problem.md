## Pessimistic Analysis Summary

### Critical Issues Found
- **Bell's theorem overstated**: Article claimed Bell's theorem "rules out pre-existing values for all observables" — this is incorrect. Bell's theorem rules out *local* hidden variables. The article itself discusses Bohmian mechanics (a non-local hidden variable theory), creating an internal contradiction. **Resolution**: Added "local" qualifier.
- **Bell misquotation**: Article attributed "fundamentally shifty" to Bell as his phrase. Bell's actual phrase is "shifty split" (from "Against 'Measurement'"). **Resolution**: Corrected to "shifty split."
- **Tomaz citation incomplete**: Listed only one author when the paper has three (Tomaz, Mattos, & Barbatti). **Resolution**: Added all co-authors.

### Medium Issues Found
- **Missing decoherence engagement**: The article listed decoherence as a concept link but never engaged with it in the body text. Decoherence is the mainstream physicalist response to the measurement problem, and its absence weakened the article's comprehensiveness. **Resolution**: Added "The Decoherence Response and Its Limits" section explaining what decoherence achieves (dissolving the practical measurement problem) and what it does not (resolving the philosophical measurement problem).
- **Missing causal-closure cross-link in body**: The concept was in the frontmatter but never linked in the text. **Resolution**: Added inline link in the agential dimension section.
