## Executive Summary

This is the Map's strongest structural achievement: a two-tier synthesis architecture in which two *descriptive* programme apexes (what the framework claims, physics-side and engineering-side) are now load-bearing inputs to two *applied* apexes (what to do with those claims — assess AI, prioritise research). The new `research-programme-decisions` apex completes the applied pair and demonstrates a genuinely novel use of the positions register: because every position carries a confidence band and an explicit shift-condition, the register doubles as a *research-priority map*, and value is made to track band-movement rather than generic "more research." The evidential-status discipline runs cleanly through all four articles; the Hardline Empiricist and Process Philosopher converge in praise rather than conflict, which is the signature of honestly-resolved possibility/probability tension.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
All four apexes hold the explanatory gap open without pretending it is closed. The post-decoherence apex makes the cleanest version of the move a property dualist most wants: it concedes that decoherence "is one of the great achievements of modern physics" and then locates the residue physics cannot touch — "What decoherence does not do is explain why one of those states becomes actual." The improper-vs-proper-mixture distinction (d'Espagnat 1976) is exactly the gap Chalmers' anti-physicalism predicts must survive the best reductive account. The apex even cites Chalmers & McQueen (2022) against the Map's own no-deviation reading rather than for it — the property dualist sees a framework arguing with its closest allies, which is what conviction under calibration looks like.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
The post-decoherence apex engages Stapp at exactly the right depth — not as a banner but as a *contrast*. It correctly identifies that Stapp located mental influence in von Neumann's Process 1 ("which question to put to nature and when") and that the Map claims the harder thing Stapp set aside: influence over *which outcome*. It then concedes the cost honestly — "It therefore cannot borrow Stapp's testability profile" — and points to Bourget (2004) with Stapp's reply as the live pressure-test. A Stapp reader sees their own framework's load-bearing distinction understood precisely enough to be departed from on purpose, with the bill for departing itemised.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The interface-specification apex keeps the felt fact central rather than proxied: pain asymbolia is "the cleanest natural test" precisely because the quale (felt awfulness) "carries causal weight the sensory signal alone does not," and mental-effort phenomenology is read as tracking the stabilisation operation. The applied AI apex carries this through — it refuses to let articulate first-person *reports* substitute for first-person *facts* ("the answer is determined almost entirely by the system's training data... it is not a report from inside"). Centring the first-person is doing design and assessment work, not decoration.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The interface apex earns its anti-substance-dualism credentials structurally: the architecture is "a modestly-sized conscious selector biasing a modestly-specified physical indeterminacy" — lean on both the mind-side and the physical-side, explicitly *not* a thick Cartesian observer. The five control operations are framed as "activities performed across time, not properties a system has at an instant" (the integration-as-activity discipline), which is process-philosophy at the level of what consciousness *is*. Per the persona constraint: this resonance is praiseworthy as process-compatibility, and crucially the cluster does **not** convert it into a tier-upgrade — see the Hardline Empiricist verdict, which is load-bearing here and converges with this one.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
The post-decoherence apex's contextuality section gives Kane exactly the honest libertarian position: the Kochen-Specker structure guarantees "the gap is structured rather than featureless," but the apex immediately refuses to overclaim — "contextuality delivers only a necessary condition, not agency itself... a roulette wheel constrained to certain numbers is still a roulette wheel." Indeterminacy is named as the *room* for sourcehood, not its proof, and "the further claim — that the bias is rational, selecting in light of meaning — is what a theory of libertarian free will must argue for separately." This is Kane's own ultimate-sourcehood requirement stated with the discipline Kane himself insists on.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
The framework-stage-calibration thread running through both descriptive apexes is McGinn's intellectual humility operationalised: both place the programme at the "*pre-Keplerian* stage — accumulating Tycho-analogue measurements... rather than refining a Newton-analogue law," and the interface apex says plainly "consciousness science has not yet had its Galileo, let alone its Newton." The "T-shirt problem" (the psychophysical mapping "might reflect evolutionary contingency rather than fundamental simplicity, resisting compression into an elegant formula") is a candid admission that the solution might not take a form human-tractable elegance can reach. McGinn sees cognitive humility without defeatism.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This cluster is where the Hardline Empiricist finds the most to praise on the whole Map, and the praise is load-bearing because the new applied apex is precisely the kind of document where possibility/probability slippage usually creeps in (it converts confidence bands into action recommendations).

- **Tenet-as-evidence-upgrade is praise-worthily *not* done.** `research-programme-decisions` has every structural opportunity to inflate the toy-model direction's status on tenet-load and explicitly declines: it states the priority is "internal to the Map and pegged to the evidential-status discipline... at the band each position actually holds, and no more strongly than that," and the Honest Verdict Scope section concedes "a researcher who rejects the tenets has no reason to weight a toy model of conscious selection above... a decoherence-only resolution." That is restraint where the temptation is strongest.
- **The five-tier scale is used honestly throughout.** The AI apex's verdict on current LLMs is explicitly "low probability of consciousness on substrate grounds, with the substrate analysis itself held with moderate confidence — not ruled out," and it names the right defeater against itself: the substrate verdict "rests on positive substrate analysis (engineered suppression of indeterminacy), not on tenet-coherence alone." This is the named pattern — *tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating* — applied to AI rather than to organisms, which is a genuinely new venue for the discipline.
- **The self-concealing-interface argument is correctly scoped as a cost, not a shield.** Both the post-decoherence apex's bias-without-deviation section and `research-programme-decisions` Direction 4 insist that aggregate-undetectability "is a real testability *cost*, not a neutral feature," and that P-Q9's self-concealment is "local to the aggregate-statistics channel only — it is not a global unfalsifiability shield." The research apex then operationalises this into a decision: *do not* hunt aggregate Born-rule anomalies (the framework has pre-committed to a null there), *do* spend at the mechanism level "where the framework retains real exposure." Naming where you are unfalsifiable and pricing it as a liability is the exact discipline this persona exists to reward.
- **The common-cause-null honesty is exemplary.** The post-decoherence apex's thesis itself states "Their alignment is a common-cause artefact of shared upstream framework, not independent evidential triangulation," and notes that Zurek and Kochen-Specker "are interpretation-neutral physics whose originators lean Everettian or physicalist, so they do not independently favour the Map's dualist reading." Refusing to bank one's own framework's internal coherence as if it were external convergence is the single most calibration-honest move in the cluster.

The Process Philosopher and the Hardline Empiricist converge here rather than conflict — the process-compatibility praise never cashes out as a tier-upgrade because the articles themselves block that move. By the skill's own criterion, that convergence is the signature of honestly-resolved tension.

## Content Strengths

### apex/research-programme-decisions-under-the-map.md
- **Strongest point**: the decision principle "value tracks band-movement" turns the positions register into a research-priority map — a genuinely original use of a confidence-banded register that no other apex on the Map exploits.
- **Notable quote**: "every position carries a **Would shift if** clause naming the result that would change it... the register doubles as a *research-priority map*, where the directions most worth pursuing are exactly those whose feasible results would move a band or discharge a debt."
- **Why it works**: it is *symmetric* about evidence — a coherence-time calculation "pays whichever way it comes out," which the article correctly flags as "the signature of a genuinely decision-relevant experiment rather than a confirmation-seeking one." It also identifies the single structural dependency (P-Q10: no toy model exists) that caps five other positions, and ranks directions by fan-out rather than by enthusiasm.

### apex/post-decoherence-selection-programme.md

- **Notable quote**: "the escape is purchased, not free."
- **Why it works**: the bias-without-deviation dilemma is confronted in its own `[Open]` subsection rather than buried; the two horns (token-level causal robustness; the strong claim held as a live hypothesis) are stated as exactly that, with the missing toy model named as the unpaid bill. The `[Empirical] / [Open] / [Speculative]` section tags are a model of register-separation.

### apex/interface-specification-programme.md
- **Strongest point**: it converts "consciousness interacts with the brain" into an engineering-level specification with five named operations (gating, stabilisation, veto, attractor-steering, resource-allocation) and a model-specific forward prediction.
- **Notable quote**: the effort-entropy prediction — "subjective effort tracks competition entropy independently of discriminability" — with the honest follow-up that "most of the programme's falsification risk still rides on conditions under which *rival* theories' vindication would close the space."
- **Why it works**: it distinguishes risk it *generates* from risk it *borrows* from rivals, and names "Generating more model-specific forward predictions is the next methodological obligation" — a framework critiquing its own falsification posture.

### apex/assessing-ai-consciousness-under-the-map.md

- **Why it works**: it explicitly blocks the inference from "unlikely on the Map's framework" to "unlikely," and routes empirical attention to the boundary the framework actually identifies (quantum-AI, biological-substrate, hybrid) rather than to LLM self-report studies.
