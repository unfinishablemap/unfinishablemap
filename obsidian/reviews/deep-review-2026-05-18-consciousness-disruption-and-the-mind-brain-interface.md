## Pessimistic Analysis Summary

### Critical Issues Found
- **"Order of magnitude" quantitative overstatement**: The claim "Brain complexity under propofol decreases by an order of magnitude (Mashour, 2024)" attributes a specific factor-of-10 reduction to Mashour 2024. Different complexity measures yield different reduction factors—PCI typically drops from ~0.45 to ~0.20 (factor of ~2), while Lempel-Ziv and integrated-information measures show larger drops but not reliably 10x. The sister article ([[anaesthesia-and-the-consciousness-interface]]) uses "graded changes" and "continuous." Resolution: Replaced with "Measures of neural complexity—perturbational complexity index, Lempel-Ziv complexity, integrated information—drop substantially under propofol (Mashour, 2024; Sarasso et al., 2015)." This preserves the empirical point without an unsupported quantitative claim. This is a calibration-error category (dropped/inverted qualifier) caught by the §2.5 attribution-accuracy check.

### Medium Issues Found

The argumentative weight is preserved; the rhetorical overreach is removed.
