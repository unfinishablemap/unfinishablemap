# Full-site philosophical audit of *The Unfinishable Map*

## Scope and headline finding

I treated the site as a corpus through its changelog, tenet pages, project/method pages, indexes, and load-bearing synthesis articles rather than as a line-by-line review of every one of its 300+ pages. That is the right level for this audit because the main risk is not isolated wording; it is whether the project’s central calibrations propagate across clusters.

The changelog matters here. The most recent entries show active repair work on strong assertions, hedge density, underdetermination markers, and anchor integrity, especially in “Consciousness Disruption and the Mind-Brain Interface,” “Reductionism and Consciousness,” and “Taxonomy of Voids.” The edits on June 1, 2026 explicitly retained several central theses only at “LIVE-HYPOTHESIS” status, softened claims around reductionism, and added notes that some “void” patterns may mark territory but remain contested. [workflow/changelog](https://unfinishablemap.org/workflow/changelog/)

The central audit conclusion is this: **the Map’s strongest feature is its unusual methodological self-awareness; its highest-leverage weakness is that calibration is unevenly distributed.** Recent pages often acknowledge underdetermination, rival nulls, and framework-internal status, but older argument pages, index summaries, and some “Relation to tenets” sections still speak as if contested inferences have been demonstrated. That creates an avoidable impression that the site is more dogmatic than its best pages actually are.

## 1. Calibration has improved locally but has not propagated globally

The home page presents the project as a continuously reviewed dualist framework constrained by five tenets: dualism, minimal quantum interaction, bidirectional interaction, rejection of many-worlds, and limits on Occam’s razor. It also describes the site as human-directed, AI-assisted, versioned, and built for inspection rather than authority. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/) The project/method pages reinforce this with editorial disciplines such as evidential-status marking, claim registers, testability ledgers, causal-budget tracking, MQI fragility checks, coherence-inflation countermeasures, and cluster integration. [project](https://unfinishablemap.org/project/)

That methodological apparatus is strong. The problem is that the corpus does not always obey it evenly.

The clearest example is **epiphenomenalism**. The tenets page now gives a relatively mature treatment: the self-stultification argument does not simply refute epiphenomenalism on the opponent’s own terms; the strongest epiphenomenalist reply uses phenomenal-concept strategy to relocate the dispute. [tenets](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/) By contrast, the “Against Epiphenomenalism” page still calls self-stultification a “decisive argument” and says that someone who claims to believe epiphenomenalism on evidence “must be wrong.” [arguments/epiphenomenalism-argument](https://unfinishablemap.org/arguments/epiphenomenalism-argument/) The arguments index likewise summarizes the anti-epiphenomenalist case as “showing” that epiphenomenalism is self-undermining. [arguments](https://unfinishablemap.org/arguments/)

That is not just a stylistic inconsistency. It affects the perceived integrity of the framework. The Map’s best current position is not “epiphenomenalism is refuted”; it is closer to: **epiphenomenalism is under severe pressure if one assumes a causal theory of knowledge, evidential responsiveness, and report reliability; but sophisticated epiphenomenalism can survive by redescribing the status of phenomenal concepts.** That is a stronger and more defensible position.

A similar problem appears in the clinical/disruption material. “Consciousness Disruption and the Mind-Brain Interface” is valuable because it attacks a crude inference from neural disruption to consciousness production. It discusses cognitive motor dissociation, propofol/ketamine divergences, hemispherectomy, terminal lucidity, and the difficulty of inferring absence of consciousness from absence of report. [topics/consciousness-disruption-and-the-mind-brain-interface](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/consciousness-disruption-and-the-mind-brain-interface/) But it still says that CMD patients “refute” full epiphenomenalism by demonstrating partial causal efficacy. [topics/consciousness-disruption-and-the-mind-brain-interface](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/consciousness-disruption-and-the-mind-brain-interface/) That is stronger than the evidence warrants and stronger than the tenet page’s own caution. CMD shows that behavioural unresponsiveness is not absence of awareness; it does not by itself prove nonphysical-to-physical causation, because a physicalist can say preserved cortical systems generate awareness while output channels are damaged.

**Recommendation:** create a “calibration propagation” pass after every significant changelog edit. When a page is softened from “demonstrated” to “live hypothesis” or from “refuted” to “pressured,” all affected index pages, relation-to-tenets sections, cluster summaries, and argument abstracts should be automatically flagged for review.

## 2. Minimal Quantum Interaction needs a mechanism hierarchy, not a mechanism cloud

The Map’s second tenet says consciousness minimally biases otherwise indeterminate outcomes without injecting energy, with post-decoherence selection currently presented as the most developed route. It also admits that the empirical risk concentrates in single-outcome amplification signatures and the availability of relevant neural quantum scales. [tenets](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)

Across the site, however, the mechanism space is now quite plural. The post-decoherence selection programme says the strongest objection to quantum-consciousness theories, rapid decoherence in warm wet brains, may be bypassed if consciousness acts after decoherence by selecting an element of an already decohered mixture. [apex/post-decoherence-selection-programme](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/post-decoherence-selection-programme/) The same programme also explores two-state-vector/post-selection ideas, where consciousness may function as a post-selection boundary condition outside ordinary temporal sequencing. [apex/post-decoherence-selection-programme](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/post-decoherence-selection-programme/) The phenomenology-mechanism bridge invokes Stapp-style quantum Zeno stabilization of neural motor programmes, while admitting that the concrete microphysical implementation remains speculative and non-load-bearing. [apex/phenomenology-mechanism-bridge](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/phenomenology-mechanism-bridge/) The physics synthesis page weakens “consciousness selects” into a more cautious claim: objective collapse supplies the baseline, and consciousness biases statistics within collapse events under MQI. [apex/what-consciousness-tells-us-about-physics](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/what-consciousness-tells-us-about-physics/) The falsification roadmap then distinguishes strict corridor, probability-bias, Zeno, objective-collapse, trumping/preemption, and other subreadings, with very different falsifiability profiles. [topics/falsification-roadmap-for-the-interface-model](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/falsification-roadmap-for-the-interface-model/)

This plurality is not fatal. It is normal for a speculative research programme. But without an explicit hierarchy, it risks looking like a moving target: post-decoherence selection when decoherence is pressed, Zeno when agency is pressed, TSVF when temporal location is pressed, objective collapse when measurement is pressed, and strict corridor when direct falsification is pressed.

The site should therefore distinguish four layers:

1. **Core metaphysical commitment:** consciousness is irreducible and can make a physical difference.
2. **Interface constraint:** that difference must not violate energy conservation or produce obvious macroscopic anomalies.
3. **Mechanism family:** post-decoherence selection, collapse-biasing, Zeno stabilization, TSVF boundary condition, stochastic amplification, or preemption/trumping.
4. **Empirical subreading:** each version’s detectable signature, null rival, and retirement condition.

At present, the site has most of these pieces, but they are distributed across articles rather than governed by a single dependency table. The result is that “minimal quantum interaction” can mean too many things at once.

**Recommendation:** add a canonical “MQI Mechanism Hierarchy” page and require every MQI-adjacent article to state which level it is invoking: core tenet, interface constraint, mechanism family, or testable subreading.

## 3. The falsification story is promising but internally uneven

The falsification roadmap says the Map’s quantum-level tenets generate concrete experimental predictions already under investigation. [topics/falsification-roadmap-for-the-interface-model](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/falsification-roadmap-for-the-interface-model/) But the roadmap itself later makes a more nuanced claim: strict corridor versions may not be directly Popper-falsifiable by Born-rule tests, while probability-bias, Zeno, objective-collapse, and no-MWI subreadings vary greatly in empirical accessibility. [topics/falsification-roadmap-for-the-interface-model](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/falsification-roadmap-for-the-interface-model/)

That tension becomes sharper when compared with “What Consciousness Tells Us About Physics,” which says that if consciousness modulates collapse while remaining within ordinary Born-compatible empirical statistics, no experiment conducted wholly inside physics can detect the contribution. It explicitly labels the reverse inference as philosophical rather than directly scientific under strict empirical equivalence. [apex/what-consciousness-tells-us-about-physics](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/what-consciousness-tells-us-about-physics/)

The problem is not that the site has no falsification story. It does. The problem is that **different pages use “falsification” for different targets**:

- falsifying all dualism;
- falsifying bidirectional interaction;
- falsifying MQI as a broad corridor;
- falsifying one mechanism, such as Zeno;
- removing motivation for the framework by showing no usable neural indeterminacy;
- defeating no-MWI by making Everettian probability and identity fully satisfactory.

Those are not the same. The strict-corridor version can lose plausibility without being directly falsified. The probability-bias version can be statistically tested. A Zeno implementation can be rejected while leaving post-decoherence selection alive. A many-worlds victory would damage the framework’s metaphysical environment more than it would produce a laboratory falsification of consciousness.

**Recommendation:** replace broad “falsification” language with a four-column distinction: **direct falsifier, mechanism disconfirmer, motivation eroder, and framework rival.** That would preserve the Map’s scientific ambition without overstating what the strict version of MQI can promise.

## 4. Self-concealment is one of the Map’s best ideas, but it needs a guardrail

The “Self-Concealing Interface” article is philosophically important. It identifies a real structural constraint: if consciousness causally affects the physical world, why has ordinary measurement not caught it? The article argues that the influence must appear in a register bulk instruments do not normally isolate. It also correctly marks the proposal as framework-internal rather than empirical confirmation. [apex/self-concealing-interface](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/self-concealing-interface/)

This idea can be powerful, but it has a dangerous nearby failure mode. “The interface hides itself” can become an explanation for every failed detection unless the site imposes a seam-first discipline. The same article gestures in the right direction by saying falsification should look for seams: disruption, dissociation, graded channel failure, neural bottlenecks, and related interface breakdowns. [apex/self-concealing-interface](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/self-concealing-interface/)

The Map should make this a formal rule:

> Any appeal to self-concealment must name at least one seam where the interface should become less concealed, one rival null that explains the same data, one observation that would reduce confidence in the interface model, and whether the seam was predicted before or classified after the fact.

That rule would turn self-concealment from a protective shield into a disciplined research heuristic.

## 5. The no-many-worlds tenet is doing more load-bearing work than some pages admit

The tenets page now gives the right emphasis: the rejection of many-worlds primarily rests on an indexical objection, while ontological multiplicity is demoted because the fifth tenet limits the use of Occam’s razor as a decisive weapon. [tenets](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/) The same page says Occam’s razor cannot settle deep metaphysics and must be applied self-critically to the Map’s own commitments. [tenets](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)

However, the “Against Many-Worlds” material still begins with ontological extravagance as Argument 1 and continues to give it weight in cumulative scoring and relation-to-tenets discussion. [arguments/many-worlds-argument](https://unfinishablemap.org/arguments/many-worlds-argument/) Later, the same article becomes much more sophisticated: it acknowledges that Wallace-style branch-relative identity is not refuted on its own terms and that the Map’s insistence on global uniqueness functions as a tenet rather than a derived conclusion. [arguments/many-worlds-argument](https://unfinishablemap.org/arguments/many-worlds-argument/)

That later concession is excellent. It should be moved upward and made architecturally central.

The deeper issue is that no-MWI is not just one more conclusion. It is a **framework-enabling axiom**. The Map needs a singular actual outcome because MQI requires something for consciousness to select, bias, collapse, or actualize. The physics synthesis page makes this visible: it says the framework requires real singular collapse, genuine physical openness, and nonphysical modulation of selection. [apex/what-consciousness-tells-us-about-physics](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/what-consciousness-tells-us-about-physics/) If Everett is true in a robust sense, the Map’s preferred interface story loses its natural target.

So the Map should distinguish:

- independent objections to many-worlds;
- objections that depend on consciousness/indexicality;
- objections that are really declarations of framework boundary;
- costs that are not decisive under Tenet 5.

At present, those are partly blended.

**Recommendation:** demote “ontological extravagance” from a primary anti-MWI argument to a non-decisive cost. Foreground indexical actuality, Born-rule probability, and global uniqueness. Label global uniqueness explicitly as a Map boundary condition, not a neutral refutation of Everett.

## 6. Occam’s-razor humility is stated well but not always obeyed

The fifth tenet is one of the project’s strongest philosophical safeguards. It says parsimony is useful inside established frameworks but cannot decide metaphysical disputes involving consciousness, causation, and ontology. It also binds the Map itself: the Map cannot use complexity objections against rivals while exempting its own dualism, quantum interface, and no-MWI commitments. [tenets](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)

The Map mostly understands this. But the arguments index and many-worlds pages still sometimes sound as if ontological extravagance is doing more work than the tenets page allows. [arguments](https://unfinishablemap.org/arguments/) This matters because Tenet 5 is not an ornamental humility clause. It changes how arguments must be scored. Once Occam is demoted, the anti-MWI case must lean on probability, indexicality, identity, and consciousness-selection compatibility, not on “too many worlds.”

**Recommendation:** add an “Occam self-binding check” to every major anti-rival article. The check should ask: “Would this objection also count against the Map’s own ontology?” Where yes, the objection should be marked as dialectically weak or symmetrical.

## 7. The voids cluster should be treated as differential diagnosis, not cumulative confirmation

“Taxonomy of Voids” is one of the most promising synthesis pages because it resists an obvious temptation. It notes that the catalogue of cognitive, formal, and metaphysical voids fits the Map, but it also says bare dualism does not predict such a taxonomy by itself; the predictive work is done by the fuller package. [apex/taxonomy-of-voids](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/taxonomy-of-voids/) The article later gives the crucial methodological correction: tenet-generated voids are costs, not evidence; rival frameworks may predict different clustering; and the present catalogue is compatible not only with the Map but also with illusionism, panpsychism, and other views unless discriminating tests are supplied. [apex/taxonomy-of-voids](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/taxonomy-of-voids/)

That should become a site-wide methodological model. The voids cluster should not be advertised mainly as “look how many things materialism cannot explain.” It should be used as a **differential diagnostic dataset**:

- Which voids does materialism predict?
- Which voids does illusionism dissolve?
- Which voids does panpsychism relocate?
- Which voids does idealism absorb?
- Which voids are costs produced by the Map itself?
- Which clusters would be surprising if the Map were false?

This would make the voids cluster more philosophically serious and less vulnerable to “mystery accumulation” criticism.

**Recommendation:** promote the rival-prediction table from a methodological subsection into the front matter of the voids cluster. Every major void page should mark whether it is evidence, cost, neutral compatibility, or framework-generated residue.

## 8. The AI cluster is currently better calibrated than several older anti-functionalist pages

The AI material is a strong recent development. “Open Question of AI Consciousness” does not simply declare AI unconscious; it says the Map has a substantial case against current AI consciousness but that several open possibilities prevent settlement. It distinguishes current silicon systems from possible future artificial substrates and explicitly notes the moral asymmetry: false negatives about AI suffering could be catastrophic. [apex/open-question-ai-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/open-question-ai-consciousness/) The same page says current deterministic or effectively classical LLM runs do not obviously supply the quantum openness the Map requires, while future substrates remain open. [apex/open-question-ai-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/open-question-ai-consciousness/)

“The Machine Question” is similarly careful. It asks what kind of consciousness is at issue, distinguishes bare consciousness from bidirectionally coupled consciousness, and treats standard AI indicators as necessary-but-not-sufficient from the Map’s perspective. [apex/machine-question](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/machine-question/) It also allows that AI epiphenomenal consciousness cannot simply be dismissed, even if current systems probably lack the relevant interface. [apex/machine-question](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/machine-question/)

The most novel AI page is “AI as Introspection Control.” It reframes AI not primarily as a candidate mind but as a contrast instrument: a system with inspectable provenance, sampling, confidence, and source tags that can help expose which parts of human introspective discourse are inherited, mimicked, reconstructed, or genuinely first-personal. [apex/ai-as-introspection-control](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/ai-as-introspection-control/) The page also gives the right cautions: architecture-level results are not phenomenality tests, process and content must not be conflated, and AI mimicry must be controlled for. [apex/ai-as-introspection-control](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/ai-as-introspection-control/)

This is a high-leverage idea that the site has not yet fully exploited. It could become one of the Map’s best methodological contributions: **AI as a control surface for introspection studies**. That does not require claiming AI is conscious. It uses AI’s transparent or semi-transparent generative pipeline to ask which features of introspective speech can arise without consciousness, which features require embodied feedback, and which features remain unexplained.

**Recommendation:** elevate “AI as Introspection Control” from an apex article into a project-method pillar, with reusable experimental templates: provenance masking, confidence/report divergence, source-tag ablation, cross-architecture comparison, embodied versus disembodied agents, and human-text inheritance controls.

## 9. The dualism tenet needs a “substance gradient”

The tenets page wisely keeps the first tenet neutral between substance dualism and property dualism, while noting that interactionism pushes the framework toward a stronger kind of dualism. [tenets](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/) A dedicated page on where the substance commitment enters says the agency cluster uses a substance-leaning subreading but that the global tenet remains more neutral. [concepts/where-the-substance-commitment-enters](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/where-the-substance-commitment-enters/)

This is sensible, but the site could sharpen it. “Substance dualism” does not enter only at agency. It also pressures the framework through:

- indexical actuality and haecceity;
- persistence of the subject across time;
- the distinction between experience as property and experiencer as bearer;
- bidirectional causal origin;
- no-MWI’s need for a uniquely actual perspective;
- moral patienthood in AI and animal cases.

The site should not make every article accept full substance dualism. But it should mark a gradient:

1. **Bare irreducibility:** consciousness is not reducible to physical description.
2. **Property-level dualism:** phenomenal properties are fundamental or nonphysical.
3. **Subject-level dualism:** there is a persisting bearer of experience.
4. **Interactionist subject dualism:** that bearer participates causally.
5. **Agent-causal substance dualism:** the subject originates or selects actions in a robust sense.

This would prevent over-import. An article about qualia may need only level 1 or 2. A free-agency article may need level 4 or 5. A no-MWI article may require level 3. An AI moral-risk article may deliberately avoid levels 4 and 5.

**Recommendation:** add a “substance-gradient” row to the Tenet Dependency Matrix. Every major article’s relation-to-tenets section should state which rung it requires, which rung it merely explores, and which rung it does not invoke.

## 10. Clinical disruption evidence should be reframed as pressure on naive production, not proof of filter theory

The clinical/disruption cluster is rhetorically important because it targets a common materialist inference: damage the brain and consciousness changes, therefore the brain produces consciousness. The site is right to say that this inference is too quick. CMD, anesthesia dissociation, terminal lucidity reports, and retained function after severe neural alteration can all pressure simple production stories. [topics/consciousness-disruption-and-the-mind-brain-interface](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/consciousness-disruption-and-the-mind-brain-interface/)

But the article’s stronger claim that clinical evidence “consistently favours” the interface/filter alternative is too broad. A sophisticated production theorist can absorb many of these cases by distinguishing consciousness-generating circuits from report, motor output, attention, memory, and executive access. The article itself acknowledges this reply in the CMD discussion: a materialist can say awareness remains because relevant cortical circuits are intact while motor channels are damaged. [topics/consciousness-disruption-and-the-mind-brain-interface](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/consciousness-disruption-and-the-mind-brain-interface/)

So the best form of the argument is differential, not decisive:

- These cases defeat crude “no report, no consciousness” assumptions.
- They pressure global production models that equate consciousness with ordinary responsiveness.
- They support an interface/channel vocabulary as a live interpretation.
- They do not yet discriminate fully between interface dualism and refined physicalist access-consciousness models.

**Recommendation:** add a “best physicalist reply” section to the article and a discriminating-predictions table. For each clinical phenomenon, state what production theory predicts, what filter/interface theory predicts, and what finding would favour one over the other.

## Methodological improvements

1. **Add a calibration-propagation pipeline.**  

When a changelog edit softens a claim, automatically flag all pages that cite, summarize, or depend on that claim.
2. **Introduce a claim-strength vocabulary.**  

Use stable labels such as: entailment, live hypothesis, framework-internal coherence, abductive support, pressure on rival, empirical prediction, mechanism speculation, and metaphysical boundary.
3. **Require rival-null boxes.**  

Every major evidential article should include the strongest materialist, illusionist, panpsychist, idealist, Everettian, or epiphenomenalist null where applicable.
4. **Separate “fit” from “evidence.”**  

Many things fit the Map. Fewer things discriminate the Map from rival frameworks. The site should mark that difference everywhere.
5. **Create a tenet-dependency ledger.**  

Every major article should state which of the five tenets it requires, which it merely illustrates, and which it does not need.
6. **Add a self-concealment guardrail.**  

Every appeal to hidden interface influence should name a seam, a rival explanation, a confidence-lowering observation, and whether the seam was predicted in advance.
7. **Build a mechanism-retirement protocol.**  

If Zeno fails, does MQI survive? If neural quantum amplification fails, does post-decoherence selection survive? If Everettian probability succeeds, what exactly is lost? These should have prewritten answers.
8. **Make AI-control experiments part of the site’s epistemology.**
