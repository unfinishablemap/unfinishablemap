## Audit Method

For each article: (a) locate the conjunction-coalesce instance; (b) check whether the article *shows* that the named faces together produce closure none alone could produce, or merely *lists* faces and asserts their conjunction; (c) classify against the worked-exemplar standard set by wholeheartedness-void's "The Conjunction and Frankfurt's Trajectory" section and decision-void's "## The Seam" section.

The worked-exemplar pattern: for each face, name the localised remedy that face would admit alone, then show how the other faces close that route — yielding N demonstrations of closure for N faces.

### agency-void — PASSES

Two-face conjunction (passive control limit ⊕ skeptical verification limit). Has worked-exemplar seam-argument in §"Two Limits, One Void":

> "Wide control without self-verification would still be agentive. Narrow control with reliable self-verification would yield a precisely mappable limit. The distinction that would normally let a subject map its own boundary is unavailable."

Two N-1 hypotheticals, articulated closure, structural payoff. No edits required.

## What the Audit Did Not Find

No instances of typology-with-zero-seam-argument. Both suspension-void and vagueness-void had compressed asymmetric seam-arguments — partial-show rather than no-show. This is qualitatively different from the wholeheartedness-void pre-fix state (which had closure argument restated as self-opacity restatement, no closure-route demonstration at all). The cluster appears to have inherited a *style* of compressed seam-argument that fell short of the worked-exemplar standard set by decision-void's `## The Seam` section but did not collapse into bare typology.

The implication for cluster discipline: the conjunction-coalesce pattern propagates a typology-naming move reliably but propagates the seam-argument move asymmetrically. Future creation-time cognates should default to the dedicated `## The Seam` section (placed between the face subsections and the next major heading) modelled on decision-void, with three demonstrations for three faces, two for two faces.
