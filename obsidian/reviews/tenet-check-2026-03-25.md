### topics/analytic-idealism-and-mind-centric-metaphysics.md
- **Tenet**: Occam's Razor Has Limits (#5)
- **Issue**: The sentence "Parsimony favours one ontological category over two" is stated while explaining what analytic idealism gains, without an immediate inline caveat that the Map rejects this reasoning. A reader skimming could misread it as the article's own endorsement.
- **Quote**: "What idealism gains: The hard problem dissolves. If consciousness is all there is, there's no gap between physical and phenomenal because there's nothing non-mental to bridge from. Parsimony favours one ontological category over two."
- **Recommendation**: The surrounding text does clarify this is idealism's claimed advantage, and the article later defends dualism. Adding a brief inline note (e.g., "though the Map contests whether ontological category count is the right parsimony metric") would prevent any ambiguity.

### topics/language-recursion-and-consciousness.md
- **Tenet**: Dualism (#1) — coherence issue
- **Issue**: The article opens with "The Unfinishable Map proposes that recursive linguistic structure depends on phenomenal consciousness" followed by an inline editorial comment from the human author expressing skepticism about the AI-generated thesis. The claim itself favors dualism, but the editorial uncertainty is left unresolved in the published text.
- **Recommendation**: Either strengthen the justification and remove the editorial note, or soften the thesis to "The Map explores whether recursive linguistic structure depends on phenomenal consciousness" to match the uncertainty. Inline editorial comments should not appear in published content.

### topics/it-from-bit-and-participatory-ontology.md
- **Tenet**: Minimal Quantum Interaction (#2)
- **Issue**: The Relation to Site Perspective section describes Wheeler's participatory framework and states "consciousness shapes reality through informational acts." The constraint ("The Map constrains this to neural systems") follows immediately, but the attribution is slightly ambiguous — a reader could take the unconstrained sentence as the Map's own position.
- **Quote**: "Wheeler's participatory framework goes further: if measurement constitutes physical facts, and consciousness participates in measurement, then consciousness shapes reality through informational acts. The Map constrains this to neural systems (not universal observer-participancy) and to the minimal interaction required."
- **Recommendation**: Restructure to lead with the constraint: "The Map accepts only a restricted version of Wheeler's participatory claim — consciousness shapes physical outcomes through informational acts, but only at neural quantum indeterminacies, not as a universal observer-constituting-reality thesis."
