## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
Chalmers would admire how the article relocates the explanatory gap into a register the rich senses obscure. Because the vestibular sense has "scarcely any quale to attach the question to," the article can pose the gap in its purest form: "why is there a felt first-person perspective, oriented within a spatial frame, at all?" — "The frame is felt as *had by a viewpoint*, and the viewpoint is what the functional story does not deliver." This is the hard problem with the easy problems' distractions removed, and the article keeps the gap *open* against its own preferred reading: "The physicalist's inference-and-integration reply stands; the residue the reply does not address is the experienced viewpoint."

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
Restraint Stapp would respect. Minimal Quantum Interaction and No Many Worlds "enter only obliquely," and the article states the dependency direction explicitly: "No vestibular datum establishes the quantum-interface mechanism; the dependence runs from the framework to the reading of the data, not from the data to the mechanism." Physics is never conscripted as load-bearing support — the indexical-determinacy point (which located body is experienced as mine) is offered as a presupposition the framework secures, not as evidence the vestibular data supply.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The article is Nagelian in the best way: it centres the felt point of view as the thing the third-person story cannot reach. "Neither states why any of it is *experienced from a point of view*—why there is a someone for whom there is an up and a heading." The treatment of the silent sense — a modality that "contributes the *frame* of conscious spatial experience while barely showing up *as* an experienced object" — is precisely the "what it is like to occupy a viewpoint" question carried to the one sense where the viewpoint, not the object, is the whole phenomenal contribution.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The article treats mind and matter as making *distinguishable contributions* to a single capability — "the brain extracts and integrates spatial content without consciousness, and the mind side contributes the felt point of view from which that content is experienced" — rather than as warring substances, which is the two-aspect spirit Whitehead would endorse. Crucially, it does *not* convert that congeniality into an evidential upgrade: the frame-providing modality is described as "an accommodation, not a proof," and the article explicitly marks the open question (spatial frame as *condition of* experience vs. *component of* the subject) at the framework boundary rather than resolving it with process-philosophical resonance. That is exactly the discipline the persona's constraint demands.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
Under Bidirectional Interaction the article grounds genuine participation in perception: "Self-motion is estimated with the help of motor commands—the system predicts the sensory consequences of intended movement... so that active and passive motion are estimated differently. This is consonant with the Map's reading, on which perceiving involves the perceiver's participation rather than mere registration." Kane would value that the agency is woven into perception itself — and that the article weights it correctly: "The alignment is interpretive, not probative: the active-inference theorist re-describes the very same motor-prediction in wholly physical terms."

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
The article keeps the wall where the evidence puts it. It declines to adjudicate whether the silence reflects "a genuine lack of proprietary phenomenology, or merely a faint phenomenology usually below the threshold of notice" — "a live dispute the evidence does not close" — and turns that very indeterminacy into a feature: "A modality whose phenomenology is genuinely disputed is, for that reason, a clean exhibit of phenomenal structure." McGinn would recognise the refusal to manufacture a verdict the evidence cannot support.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch) — load-bearing verdict
This is where the article earns its highest marks, and the verdict is load-bearing because the piece makes a *structural* claim (the brain-side extreme of an asymmetry) of exactly the kind most prone to being dressed up as confirmation of the interface. It is not dressed up. The article names the move at every turn:

- **Tenet-as-evidence-upgrade is praise-worthily *not* done.** The frame-providing modality fits the capability division "at one extreme," and the article immediately disowns the inference: "That framing is an accommodation, not a proof. The same facts are equally the output of frame-coding multisensory inference, and the Map claims no more than that the interface reading represents the silent frame without strain." The structural opportunity to slide a "mostly brain-side" sense into evidence *for* the division was present and explicitly declined.
- **The rival is engaged at full strength, not strawmanned.** A dedicated section ("The Brain as a Gravity-Tuned Inference Engine") concedes the Bayesian estimator "predicts the silence, the gravity prior, the otolith illusions, the depersonalization, and the OBE without any non-physical remainder," and states the honest stalemate: "the framework does not discriminate between the readings on the strength of this evidence."
- **Naturalist authors are not conscripted.** "de Vignemont is a naturalist; Pfeiffer, Serino, Blanke, Lopez, Lenggenhager, Laurens, and Droulez are building physicalist models and draw no dualist conclusion. None of them is an ally of the interface reading, and none is enlisted as one here."
- **The Process Philosopher's congeniality cashes out as restraint, not a tier-upgrade.** The depersonalization/OBE data — the article's most tempting upgrade material, since it shows the felt self has a vestibular dependency — is held open: "they do not show whether that dependency is on a condition of experience or on a component of the subject. The Map holds this open at the framework boundary."

The two personas converge here rather than conflict: the Process Philosopher's two-aspect praise and the Hardline Empiricist's evidential-discipline praise land on the *same* passages because the article keeps congeniality and evidence on separate axes. That convergence is the signature of an article that has resolved the tension honestly. A point worth recording for system memory: the supporting apex (`cross-modal-capability-division`) goes one step further and applies the [[common-cause-null]] to this case, arguing it "bites hardest here" because the vestibular signal *is* multisensory integration with no independent channel — "not a seventh converging line but a deeper sounding of the architecture the other lines already tap." The article and the apex are mutually calibrated: the new datum extends what the asymmetry *means* without inflating how strongly it is confirmed.

## Content Strengths

### topics/vestibular-consciousness-and-the-interface.md
- **Strongest point**: The two-axis reframing. The article (and its apex companion) shows the survey's mind-side "ownership is uneven" gradient is not a single scale from smell to touch but a *family* of distinct contributions — "object-unity (touch, hearing), owned affect (interoception), and the situatedness of the viewpoint (vestibular)." Naming the viewpoint-frame as a member the object-centred senses never isolate "turns the apex's 'differently-weighted family' from a hedge into a structure." This is genuine conceptual payload, not a seventh restatement.
- **Notable quote**: "Where touch added a perceiver who is also perceived, and interoception added affect and owned selfhood, the vestibular sense adds the limiting case the whole series quietly assumed away: a modality that supplies the spatial frame of experience while barely appearing within it."
- **Why it works**: It front-loads the structural inversion in the opening paragraph (LLM-truncation-resilient), carries every empirical datum (silent sense, no unisensory cortex, gravity prior, depersonalization, OBE, rubber-hand modulation) with a publisher-verified citation, and frames each as a phenomenon the interface reading *accommodates* while the physicalist redescription is adopted as mechanism. The "Relation to Site Perspective" section connects to all four tenets with correctly-weighted (interpretive vs. probative) claims. Cited de Vignemont 2024, Pfeiffer/Serino/Blanke 2014, Blanke & Arzy 2005, Lopez/Lenggenhager/Blanke 2010, Laurens & Droulez 2007, Blanke 2012, Cento et al. 2026 — all reused from the apex's already-publisher-verified set per the 2026-06-21 apex-evolve changelog entry.
