## Executive Summary

This is one of the most coherent freshly-strengthened regions on the Map. Four new/updated articles plus two anchors now speak with a single, disciplined voice on one thesis: **competency/agency and phenomenal experience are separate axes, and behavioural markers of value-sensitive selection are defeasible, substrate-sensitive, and decisive nowhere on their own.** The cluster's standout virtue is calibration — it repeatedly has the *structural opportunity* to slide a boundary case up the evidential scale on tenet-load and explicitly declines. The Process Philosopher and the Hardline Empiricist, who are designed to be in tension, here converge in praise rather than conflict, which is the signature of an honestly-resolved region. The genuine expansion frontier lies one rung further out: neuron-less animals, organoid tissue, non-neural biological cognition (immune, bacterial, fungal), all currently absent as dedicated subjects.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The cluster takes the explanatory gap as its organising principle rather than an afterthought. The fish article's central move — that "structure determines *function*" is unobjectionable engineering while "structure determines *feeling*" is a metaphysical commitment smuggled in disguised as a bioengineering principle (Key 2016) — is exactly the reductive-identity diagnosis Chalmers presses against physicalist confidence. The plant article's insistence that "the moment functional description is settled, the question of experience remains completely open" is the hard problem stated at the non-neural extreme. Chalmers would recognise the gap being honoured, not narrated away.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
The ENS article is where this persona finds most to like: it takes the interface-location question seriously as a *criterial* question (anatomical vs. functional-quantum-juncture vs. phenomenological) rather than assuming a skull-bound default. Reading (b) — that if the interface is defined by quantum-juncture selection rather than anatomy, enteric neurons "present junctures at which conscious selection *could*, in principle, apply" — is precisely the kind of physics-consciousness interface question Stapp would want raised, and the article's honesty in flagging it as raised-but-least-supported (rather than dismissed) is a model of engaging the physics without over-claiming.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The "what-it's-like" question is kept central and is used as the load-bearing discriminator throughout — most sharply in the fish article ("whether there is something it is like to be the trout with acetic acid on its lips") and in the interoceptive-void's mapping of the body's silent interior. The somatic-interface anchor's insistence that phenomenal character is *shaped* by which channel carries it (emotion feels different from proprioception feels different from pain) is first-person structure taken seriously as data.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The cluster is deeply congenial to process-philosophical intuitions: Levin-and-Dennett's "cognition all the way down," goals-of-varying-scale, and the refusal of the "real goal vs as-if goal" dichotomy all resonate with experience-adjacent, gradualist metaphysics. **Crucially, the basal-cognition article earns this persona's praise without letting it cash out as a tier-upgrade** — it explicitly takes "the decoupling, not the panpsychist extrapolation," noting that panpsychist readings "would extend *experience* along with competency" and "would conflict with the Map's dualism." That is the exact firewall the persona-constraint requires: process-philosophical compatibility is embraced as defeater-removal, never as evidence that a xenobot or flatworm *feels*.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
Less central to this cluster (agency here is sub-personal competency, not libertarian choice), but Kane would approve the through-line that value-sensitive *selection* — where felt value does causal work — is held apart from value-blind mechanism, and that the cluster refuses to collapse genuine evaluative agency into reflex on the one side or to inflate reflex into agency on the other.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
Amply satisfied. The fish article closes on "the case is open and, at its core, resistant to closure from outside"; the ENS article registers "a boundary of its own framework honestly, not a solved problem"; the interoceptive-void treats the body's interior as partly constitutively unfelt ("the void's hard floor"). This is intellectual humility as method, not decoration.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch) — load-bearing for this cluster
This is the persona whose verdict matters most here, because every article touches minimal-organism or boundary-case consciousness, and the cluster **passes cleanly**. The discipline is explicit and repeated:

- **Tenet-as-evidence-upgrade is praise-worthily *not* done.** The fish article states the rule outright: "Dualism blocks the argument for 'no'; it does not supply an argument for 'yes'," and "removing a defeater is not the same as supplying positive evidence." The basal-cognition article: the decoupling "cannot by itself be *positive evidence* for consciousness." The plant article splits the verdict surgically — "plants very probably have no phenomenal experience" on the empirical question, while rejecting the *reason* (neural-necessity) the skeptics give. Each had the structural opening to upgrade a boundary case on tenet-coherence and refused.
- **The named pattern is used.** The marginal-organism parent article states it in as many words: "Tenet-coherence removes a defeater against marginal-organism consciousness; it supplies no positive evidence for it. Every boundary case below stays at the status of *live hypothesis* or *speculative integration*—never 'realistic possibility' or higher." This is "tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating" operationalised against the five-tier scale.
- **Behavioural markers are graded, not discarded.** The cluster avoids the opposite error too: it does not throw out behavioural evidence, noting there are "no non-behavioural, third-person markers of consciousness to fall back on — that very absence *is* the explanatory gap," so markers stay "suggestive but defeasible." That is correct calibration in both directions.

**Convergence, not conflict.** Where the persona design anticipates the Process Philosopher's praise cashing out as slippage the Hardline Empiricist must flag, this cluster instead lets both personas praise different aspects of the *same* passages without conflict — because the articles do the separating work themselves (competency embraced all the way down; experience withheld at every rung). No calibration concern is raised by this review. That is a strong result for a region this dense with upgrade-tempting material.

## Content Strengths

### the-enteric-nervous-system-and-the-gut-brain-distributed-interface-question.md
- **Strongest point**: Turns a potential embarrassment for a skull-bound interface ("a second nervous system this functionally complete runs without the brain — so where does the interface sit?") into a sharpening of the framework, by making explicit that interface-location rests on a *criterion choice* (anatomical / functional / phenomenological) rather than an anatomical given.
- **Notable quote**: "enteric autonomy does not multiply interfaces because competency is not experience, and only experience needs an interface."
- **Why it works**: The three-reading structure (a)/(b)/(c) with named anchors and an up-front forward-reference is textbook LLM-first navigation, and reading (c) is explicitly wired back to the two sibling articles, making the "one voice" claim structurally real rather than asserted.

### fish-sentience-and-the-teleost-pain-debate.md
- **Strongest point**: The "orthogonal rung" insight — fish are not *further down* the capacity ladder but vary neural *architecture* while holding nociception fixed, isolating the single premise "felt experience requires a particular structure." This is a genuinely clarifying reframe of a well-worn debate.
- **Notable quote**: "the slide from 'structure determines function' to 'structure determines feeling' is where a metaphysical commitment enters disguised as a bioengineering principle."
- **Why it works**: It separates the identity route from the functional route and gives them *different* rebuttals (one dissolves, the other survives as an open empirical question), which is more honest and more precise than a single blanket move.

### plant-cognition-and-the-plant-neurobiology-debate.md
- **Strongest point**: The split verdict — landing near the deflationists empirically ("very probably no experience") while rejecting their neural-necessity *reason* — models exactly how the Map can agree with a conclusion without accepting a physicalist argument for it.
- **Notable quote**: "Taiz et al.'s conclusion may well be right; their argument for it rests on a foundation the Map does not accept."
- **Why it works**: The Gagliano learning disputes are used as a live worked example of the Map's own calibration discipline (habituation contested, associative conditioning unreplicated), turning a content section into a self-demonstration of method.

### basal-and-bioelectric-cognition.md
- **Strongest point**: Uses Levin's *own* restraint about phenomenal sentience as the lever — the decoupling is "Levin's and Dennett's framing, not the Map's imposition" — which makes the argument far more robust than if the Map had to impose the competency/experience split from outside.
- **Notable quote**: "Uncontested agency sits alongside unasserted experience."

### High Priority

#### Neuron-less animals: sponges and placozoans (Trichoplax)
- **Builds on**: the marginal-organism ladder, which currently jumps from plants/*Physarum* (non-neural, non-animal) straight to *Hydra*'s diffuse nerve net.
- **Would address**: the sharpest possible in-kingdom test — *animals* (Metazoa) that coordinate behaviour with **zero neurons**. Sponges execute whole-body contractile "sneeze" behaviours and Trichoplax performs coordinated feeding and chemotaxis with no nervous system and no synapses, using peptidergic/paracrine signalling. This is the cleanest "are neurons necessary *within* animals" rung and a direct structural sibling to the plant and bioelectric articles.
- **Coverage check**: **Confirmed absent.** "sponge" returns only 2 oblique passing mentions; "placozoa" and "Trichoplax" return 0 across topics/concepts/voids and 0 research notes. No dedicated article.
- **Estimated scope**: Medium. **Tenet alignment**: Tenet 1/5 — extends the "processing/agency without neurons" decoupling to the animal kingdom's floor.

#### Brain organoids and organoid intelligence: the interface and moral-status question
- **Builds on**: the ENS interface-location question (a)/(b)/(c) and `concepts/ai-hardware-substrate-taxonomy` (which discusses DishBrain/CL1 wetware as an *AI substrate*, not organoids-as-consciousness-candidates).
- **Would address**: does lab-grown neural tissue (Cortical Labs' DishBrain playing Pong; the CL1 "Wetware-as-a-Service" product; Johns Hopkins "organoid intelligence") present a genuine interface site or a sub-personal competency? This is the ENS distributed-interface question transplanted to engineered tissue, and it is acutely topical (2022–2025). Birch's own scale already lists "brain organoids without brainstem tissue" as an investigation priority — the Map should have a dedicated home for it.
- **Coverage check**: **Confirmed absent as a dedicated subject.** ~5 passing mentions (ethics-under-dualism, testing-consciousness-collapse, ai-hardware-substrate-taxonomy) but no article treats organoids as a consciousness/interface candidate in their own right. 0 research notes.
- **Estimated scope**: Medium. **Tenet alignment**: Tenet 2/3 — a fresh interface-location stress-test with real moral stakes.

### Medium Priority

#### The cognitive immune system and the immunological self
- **Builds on**: `biological-teleology-and-the-interface-framework` (which currently uses the immune system only as a throwaway "mechanism without mind" example) and the cluster's competency/experience decoupling.
- **Would address**: the immune system exhibits *learning* (immunological memory), *self/non-self discrimination*, distributed decision-making, and adaptive anticipation — Irun Cohen's "cognitive immune system" — all with no neurons and no plausible experience. It is the strongest non-neural *learning-and-memory* case the Map does not yet own, a natural fourth sibling to plants, bioelectric agency, and the ENS.
- **Coverage check**: **Confirmed absent as a subject.** "immune" appears only as illustrative asides in ~6 files; no dedicated treatment, 0 research notes on immune cognition.
- **Estimated scope**: Medium. **Tenet alignment**: Tenet 1/5 — memory/learning/self-modelling without experience.

#### Bacterial chemotaxis and biogenic (minimal) cognition
- **Builds on**: the ladder's non-neural floor; currently *Physarum* (a single-celled *eukaryote*) is the bottom rung.
- **Would address**: bacterial chemotaxis, quorum sensing, and Pamela Lyon's "biogenic approach" push minimal-cognition vocabulary to the *prokaryotic, molecular* floor — sensing, memory (methylation-based adaptation), and value-relevant approach/avoidance with no cytoskeleton-based information integration at all. It is the sharpest version of the ladder's own disjunction: either value-*like* behaviour runs on wholly value-blind mechanism, or conscious selection reaches a bacterium.
- **Coverage check**: **Confirmed absent.** "bacterial chemotaxis" and "quorum-sensing" return 0 across the corpus; no research notes.
- **Estimated scope**: Medium. **Tenet alignment**: Tenet 5 — the parsimony/calibration knife at its finest grain.
