## Executive Summary

The 2026-05-06/07 cluster does something the previous propagation cluster prepared the ground for: it extends the slippage and framework-stage disciplines from *boundary organism* and *AI* domains into the *interface itself*. The new `evolved-mind-brain-interface-efficacy` article is the cleanest test the catalogue has produced for an article making a structurally novel claim — that the interface scales across phylogeny — while explicitly self-marking as "speculative integration," "Tycho-analogue scaffolding," and "not in better evidential standing than the brain-side framing it competes with." The condensed `forward-in-time-conscious-selection` preserves every substantive move at 27% lower word count, including the trilemma's identification of the non-reducible primitive at the heart of selection. The deep-reviewed `consciousness-in-simple-organisms` now sorts each model organism on the five-tier evidential scale explicitly. The Process Philosopher and Hardline Empiricist personas converge on the same passages in all three articles, which is the cleanest signal the cluster is calibrated. The strongest expansion opportunities are concept-page anchors for terminology that has now become load-bearing across multiple articles — the improper-mixture / problem-of-outcomes; the selection-criterion trilemma; Gutfreund's Standing Agnostic Challenge — and a possible topic-level treatment of interface co-evolution as the structural complement to substrate scaling.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)

The cluster operates on three different registers of the explanatory gap, and Chalmers's framework illuminates each. `forward-in-time-conscious-selection` (line 169) describes the strict selection-only reading and supplies "information-theoretic signatures: a per-event ceiling of log₂(N) bits, a Born-rule-preserving rate that vanishes at the ensemble scale, and content-confinement to the brain-generated candidate set" — which is exactly the *naturalistic dualism* Chalmers wants: a metaphysically interactionist commitment that nonetheless leaves quantitative footprints in physics. The decision to keep the FAPP-invisible improper/proper mixture distinction as "the gap the framework occupies" rather than the gap it closes (line 77) is Chalmers-style structural humility. The new `evolved-mind-brain-interface-efficacy` article inherits this discipline: its scalar reading does not claim to dissolve the hard problem, only to identify a candidate biological feature (the interface) whose efficacy is variable, while acknowledging "the framework's value at this stage is heuristic-explanatory, not predictive." Chalmers would recognise the layered architecture: the hard problem is preserved, the dualist commitment is named explicitly, and the empirical contact-points are quantitative when stated and explicitly bounded when not.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)

The cluster's quantum framing is exemplary. The condensed `forward-in-time-conscious-selection` preserves the Stapp Process 1 relocation argument (line 117) — "the relocation sacrifices Stapp's specific mechanism (the Zeno effect on neural superpositions) but preserves his philosophical architecture: the loss is a concrete physical story about *how* selection works; the gain is immunity to the strongest empirical objection against the original" — at 27% reduced word count. This is the Map's structural concession to Stapp: the architecture survives where the mechanism does not, and the cost of relocation is named. The Georgiev (2017) foundational objection (line 93) is preserved as well — that Stapp's mind has no wavefunction yet acts with projection operators on quantum systems — preserving the dispute as live rather than smoothing it over. The `evolved-mind-brain-interface-efficacy` article extends Stapp's amplification framing: "selection bandwidth," "fidelity," "granularity," and "candidate-type coupling" are scalar generalisations of the kind of property Stapp's amplification mechanism quantifies. Stapp would praise both the discipline of the relocation and the structural extension into a comparative-evolutionary frame his own work did not develop.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)

`consciousness-in-simple-organisms` centres first-person experience in exactly the way Nagel's "What Is It Like to Be a Bat?" demands. The Becerra et al. quote — "Is there something that feels like to be a worm? Or are worms blind machines?" (line 85) — is Nagel's question transposed to nematodes. The article does not pretend to answer it. The five-tier scale is the discipline of *not* answering it pretentiously: the worm sits at "live hypothesis sliding to speculative integration," not at "realistic possibility," because the empirical evidence does not warrant the upgrade. The contemplative-perspectives section (witness consciousness, vijñāna/prajñā, MPE) extends Nagel's phenomenological method rather than abandoning it: if the Buddhist analytic tradition supplies a way to approach the question of bare awareness without semantic content, then the question of whether a 302-neuron system supports such bare awareness becomes coherent without becoming over-claimed. `evolved-mind-brain-interface-efficacy` honours Nagel's epistemic ceiling explicitly: "Gutfreund's epistemic ceiling applies symmetrically: behavioural-cognition data cannot adjudicate between brain-side and interface-side scaling readings, because both make explanatory contact with the world through the same behavioural intermediary" (line 83). The behavioural intermediary is what Nagel identified as bounded; the article names this bound as load-bearing, not as evadable.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)

The cluster preserves what Whitehead would want without using process-philosophical compatibility as the engine of any tier-upgrade. The clearest case is `consciousness-in-simple-organisms` lines 203–217: a self-contained Whiteheadian section ("This perspective doesn't commit the Map to full Whiteheadian metaphysics. But it shows that the minimal consciousness debate need not be framed as finding a threshold where the lights turn on") followed immediately by the disciplined boundary on its application. Process-philosophical compatibility removes the "lights turn on suddenly" defeater; it does not, on its own, license attributing experience to *Hydra*. The article keeps both axes operating — the Whiteheadian frame *and* the five-tier scale's verdict that *Hydra* sits at "speculative integration" — without letting the former engine the latter. `forward-in-time-conscious-selection`'s actualisation model (line 121) is the cleanest case in the catalogue of Whitehead's "concrescence" being given full play *and* costed honestly: "inaccessibility is a cost on a Popperian register, not a virtue of metaphysical modesty. Critics can dismiss 'unactualised possibilities' as incoherent or redundant; the model has no recourse." This is Whitehead-friendliness *with* its Popperian residue made explicit. `evolved-mind-brain-interface-efficacy`'s four scalar axes — bandwidth, fidelity, granularity, candidate-type coupling — are Whiteheadian in spirit (continuous variation, no threshold) but the article never lets the spirit do evidential work: the scalar reading is "speculative integration," not a metaphysical fact.

**Constraint observed (evidential-status discipline)**: This persona's praise is praise *at tier*, not praise *as engine of tier-upgrade*. Each article in the cluster could have used Whitehead-friendliness to slide a hypothesis up the scale (worm-experience to "realistic possibility" because process-philosophy welcomes it; interface-efficacy to "live hypothesis" because Whitehead's continuous variation predicts it; actualisation model to "realistic possibility" because process philosophy supplies the metaphysics). None do. The Whiteheadian resonance operates on the metaphysical-architecture axis, evidential discipline operates on the empirical-claim axis, and the cluster keeps them apart.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)

`forward-in-time-conscious-selection`'s selection-criterion trilemma (line 157) is directly Kane-relevant. Kane locates room for libertarian agency in indeterministic neural events that produce *self-formed actions* — moments where the agent's own deliberation is the load-bearing source of the resolved indeterminacy, and where the agent could have done otherwise *and* the doing-otherwise would also have been theirs. The article's path (iii) — selection follows a non-reducible preference admitting no further analysis, "an unanalysable primitive" sitting at the heart of the framework — is the Kane-style move made structurally explicit: it names the agency-bearing element, names that it cannot be further analysed without collapsing into either functionalism (path i) or randomness (path ii), and names where the primitive sits. The strict selection-only reading then bounds the primitive: its choices are confined to the brain-generated candidate set, so novel content cannot be brought into being by selection alone. Kane would praise both the candour of naming the primitive and the discipline of bounding it — neither maximal-libertarian-agency nor minimal-mechanism-with-decoration, but a genuinely intermediate framework that takes agency seriously without over-claiming about its scope.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)

`consciousness-in-simple-organisms` lines 178–179 are textbook McGinn: "The *C. elegans* case exemplifies this: we possess complete structural knowledge of 302 neurons yet cannot determine whether the worm experiences anything. Complete knowledge doesn't bridge the gap. This is what cognitive closure looks like empirically." The mysterian position is given operational content: it is not merely the abstract claim that we *might* be cognitively closed; it is the empirical observation that the most thoroughly mapped nervous system on Earth has not yielded its phenomenal status. The new `evolved-mind-brain-interface-efficacy` extends mysterian discipline to the agnostic challenge directly: Gutfreund's epistemic ceiling is taken seriously, the article does not claim to break through it, and the section "Distinguishing Observables in Principle" (line 87) is the mysterian acknowledgement made constructive — naming what work would have to happen for the readings to pull apart in evidence rather than only interpretation, and explicitly noting "these are observables-in-principle, not observables-in-fact." McGinn would recognise the structure: the cognitive closure is accepted as load-bearing for the framework's epistemic standing without being treated as a final verdict on what is knowable. The condensed `forward-in-time-conscious-selection` preserves the same posture: "what excludes this classical-computational closure is the Dualism tenet, not anything internal to the post-decoherence mechanism — the framework defended here is a consequence of the dualist commitment, not an independent argument for it" (line 133). Mysterian discipline applied to the framework's own epistemic standing.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)

This persona's praise is load-bearing for this review because the cluster contains a structurally novel claim (interface-efficacy scaling) that had every temptation to slide up the evidential scale on tenet-load alone. It does not slide.

**First, `evolved-mind-brain-interface-efficacy` self-labels its evidential status throughout.** The frontmatter description names the article as "a speculative integration the Map proposes alongside, not against, mainstream comparative neuroscience." Line 38 names it as "a heuristic-explanatory move, not an empirically forced conclusion." Line 83 says "the hypothesis is therefore not in better evidential standing than the brain-side framing it competes with; both face the agnostic ceiling. The hypothesis earns its keep by being heuristic-explanatory rather than prediction-generating, offering a single mechanism that subsumes phenomena the brain-side reading addresses through several distinct ones." Line 99 says "the four efficacy axes are Tycho-analogue scaffolding — taxonomic structure that should precede a specification law, not a specification." Line 93 names the axes as "observables-in-principle, not observables-in-fact." This article had the structural opportunity to claim its scalar reading was empirically supported by the human-other-animal cognitive gap (it isn't — the gap is also explained by substrate scaling) and explicitly declined. The Hardline Empiricist persona praises this restraint because it is the discipline operating at its hardest: when the article's own thesis has the structure that would slip if not held in place, the holding-in-place is what calibration looks like.

**Second, `evolved-mind-brain-interface-efficacy` decouples consciousness-presence from cognitive-reach.** This is the article's most distinctive move (line 85): "What scales across species is not consciousness's presence or grade but the *effective reach* of consciousness into behavioural outcomes. A mind at full grade with low interface bandwidth would be conscious in the same sense humans are while contributing less to overt cognitive performance." This is the Hardline Empiricist's preferred posture toward the New York Declaration: the Declaration's "realistic possibility" verdict for many invertebrates is *preserved* by the article (consciousness presence is not denied) while the article's own scalar claim is about a structurally different variable (interface efficacy) that the Declaration does not address. Two registers, kept separate. A weaker version of the article would have used the scalar reading to argue that consciousness *itself* scales (Whitehead-friendly, but slippage); this version explicitly does not.

**Third, `consciousness-in-simple-organisms` makes the five-tier scale operational at the table level.** Line 121 is now an explicit tier-placement audit: *C. elegans* at "Live hypothesis / speculative integration; not established as conscious"; *Hydra* at "Speculative integration"; slime molds at "Speculative integration / cognition without consciousness." This is the discipline catching itself in real-time. The article has the methodological apparatus to over-claim — Whitehead, MPE, witness consciousness, the panpsychist continuity option, the failure of the distribution problem to admit a principled answer — and it deploys all of these *without* using any of them to elevate the empirical verdict. The "Evidential Register: Tenet vs. Evidence" section (line 60) is the methodological anchor: "Removing a defeater keeps a hypothesis live; it does not, on its own, supply positive evidence that consciousness is actually present. The risk worth defending against is treating 'not ruled out by our metaphysics' as if it were 'positively supported by the evidence' — a slide that lets metaphysical permission absorb negative empirical findings rather than be constrained by them." The slippage discipline naming itself in the prose, applied to the article's own structure.

**Fourth, the UAL section refuses to absorb the negative empirical signal into the interface reframe.** Line 157 is the discipline at full strength: "The interface reading must not absorb the negative empirical signal. *Failing* UAL remains evidentially meaningful even when UAL is treated as a coupling-marker rather than an emergence threshold... The interface reading lowers our confidence that UAL marks a *necessary* boundary; it does not raise our confidence that organisms below it are conscious." A weaker article would have used the interface reframe to immunise the framework against UAL failures (since UAL is now a marker, not a threshold, why should failing it count against the organism?). This article explicitly forecloses that move. The Hardline Empiricist persona praises this because it is the most subtle slippage temptation in the boundary-organism domain — re-interpreting a marker so its negative signal no longer reaches the framework — and the article names and resists it.

**Fifth, the condensed `forward-in-time-conscious-selection` preserves every calibrated hedge through 27% reduction.** McQueen's defence of Orch OR against the Gran Sasso refutation is preserved as "one position in a continuing debate" with the burden remaining "on defenders to show why the more specific variant escapes the same constraint" (line 87). The 2002 Hagan et al. result is preserved as "unreplicated" (line 129). The actualisation model's Popperian cost is preserved at line 123. The Duch dissent is preserved at line 133 with the framework's *dependence on the Dualism tenet* named as load-bearing. The condense skill could have prioritised compression over calibration and quietly dropped these hedges; it did not. The Hardline Empiricist persona praises condense passes that preserve calibrated language under length pressure, because that is when the calibration is most likely to be lost.

The Process Philosopher and Hardline Empiricist personas converge on praising the same passages in all three articles. In `evolved-mind-brain-interface-efficacy`, both personas praise the four-axis structure (Whiteheadian continuous variation; Birch-disciplined Tycho-stage scaffolding). In `consciousness-in-simple-organisms`, both praise the bounded Whiteheadian section and the explicit five-tier table. In `forward-in-time-conscious-selection`, both praise the actualisation model's costed account. When the two converge, the article has resolved the tension honestly. They do not conflict anywhere in the cluster.

## Content Strengths

### topics/evolved-mind-brain-interface-efficacy.md

- **Strongest point**: The decoupling of consciousness-presence from cognitive-reach (line 85) is the article's most distinctive move and the cleanest example in the catalogue of holding two evidential registers apart. The New York Declaration's "realistic possibility" of consciousness across many invertebrates is preserved — not weakened, not strengthened — while the article's own scalar claim about interface efficacy is positioned as a *different* variable on which it makes a *different* (speculative-integration) claim. This is what register-separation looks like at the article level rather than the cross-article level.
- **Notable quote**: "What scales across species is not consciousness's presence or grade but the *effective reach* of consciousness into behavioural outcomes. A mind at full grade with low interface bandwidth would be conscious in the same sense humans are while contributing less to overt cognitive performance. This decoupling of consciousness-presence from cognitive-reach is the hypothesis's most distinctive move and the one that most directly absorbs Gutfreund's challenge: behavioural data underdetermine the inference because the behaviour-to-mind mapping is itself mediated by an interface whose efficacy is the variable."
- **Why it works**: This is the framework reading earning its keep without over-claiming. The interface-efficacy hypothesis would be parsimony-prohibited if it competed with substrate scaling on the same explanatory axis; by identifying a structurally distinct variable that the substrate reading does not address (effective reach, not consciousness presence), the article carves out room for the hypothesis without entering competition it would lose on parsimony grounds. The Gutfreund challenge then becomes the article's own discipline: the agnostic ceiling is what makes the parsimony argument fall short.

### topics/consciousness-in-simple-organisms.md

- **Strongest point**: The "Evidential Register: Tenet vs. Evidence" section (line 60) is the methodological anchor that makes every subsequent organism-by-organism verdict honest. It names the slippage temptation explicitly ("treating 'not ruled out by our metaphysics' as if it were 'positively supported by the evidence' — a slide that lets metaphysical permission absorb negative empirical findings rather than be constrained by them") and installs the five-tier scale as the discipline against it. The result is that each organism receives a tier-placement that is named, not inferred — and the tier-placements are appropriately conservative.
- **Notable quote**: "Removing a defeater keeps a hypothesis live; it does not, on its own, supply positive evidence that consciousness is actually present. The risk worth defending against is treating 'not ruled out by our metaphysics' as if it were 'positively supported by the evidence' — a slide that lets metaphysical permission absorb negative empirical findings rather than be constrained by them."
- **Why it works**: The slippage discipline is named in the article's own prose, in a section explicitly devoted to making the discipline operational. A reader who has not encountered the [[possibility-probability-slippage]] concept page elsewhere can still extract the discipline from this article. The tier-placement table (line 121) then makes the discipline operational at the data-row level. The microtubule-cytoskeletal-element clarification at line 109 is a small but high-leverage fix: it preserves the Penrose-Hameroff position's specificity (orchestrated *neuronal* microtubules, not microtubules in general) without overstating what slime molds lack.

### topics/forward-in-time-conscious-selection.md (post-condense)

- **Strongest point**: The selection-criterion trilemma at line 157 survives the 27% reduction at full force. The trilemma identifies the deepest open question in the framework: if consciousness selects pattern A over pattern B, the selection follows internal reasons (path i, dualism collapses to functionalism), randomness (path ii, framework reduces to spontaneous collapse), or a non-reducible preference (path iii, an unanalysable primitive sits at the heart of the framework). The Map's commitment to path (iii) is named explicitly, and the strict selection-only reading is identified as the structural bound on the primitive. Compressing this section further would have either lost a path of the trilemma or smoothed over the "unanalysable primitive" naming — both of which would weaken the framework's honesty about its own commitments.
- **Notable quote**: "The Map's likely route is (iii): selection criteria reflect the phenomenal character of consciousness and are not further reducible to physical or computational structure. The [[selection-only-mind-influence|strict selection-only reading]] adds a structural bound on this primitive — its choices are confined to the brain-generated candidate set, so novel content cannot be brought into being by selection alone. Naming where the primitive sits, and how it is bounded, is more honest than presenting selection as if its criterion were transparent."
- **Why it works**: The trilemma is the framework's own interpretive bedrock and naming the primitive is the discipline of not pretending it is fully analysed. The strict selection-only reading then earns its position by *bounding* the primitive (per-event ceiling of log₂(N) bits, Born-rule preservation at the ensemble scale, content-confinement to brain-generated candidates) without dissolving its primitive status. This is the right kind of intermediate framework — neither maximally over-claimed nor minimally evasive.
