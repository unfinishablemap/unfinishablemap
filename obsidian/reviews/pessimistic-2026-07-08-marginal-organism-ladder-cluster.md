## Executive Summary

The cluster is well-written and disciplined in its self-cautions, but the load-bearing move it shares—that these rungs "sever" the inference *impressive goal-directed behaviour ⇒ probably conscious*—is over-generalised and, in the Levin article, partly **asserts what it argues**. The strongest defect: the Levin article repeatedly slides from *"the investigator declines to assert experience"* (absence of assertion) to *"experience is absent / decoupled"* (evidence of absence), and in one place states the bracketing as outright denial ("which even Levin treats as functional rather than felt"). A second structural problem runs across the cluster: the claim that competency/behaviour "cannot be *evidence* for consciousness … anywhere" is stronger than the Map's own graded-credence practice (the fish article calls the same class of markers "suggestive"; the Birch tiers grade by behaviour), leaving an unreconciled cross-article tension. The fish article is the most careful of the three but over-narrows the skeptic to a type-identity theorist.

## Critiques by Philosopher

### The Hard-Nosed Physicalist (Dennett)
"You cite my own Aeon essay with Levin, then use it against a view I never held. No functionalist thinks a xenobot carries 'a corresponding quantum of experience, readable off its goal-directedness' (Levin art., 'Relation to Site Perspective'). Functionalism requires *specific* organisation—global broadcast, higher-order representation—not mere goal-directedness. Your 'decoupling' refutes a crude any-agency→experience view nobody defends, and leaves real functionalism untouched. And Levin bracketing sentience is not Levin *denying* it; it's methodological hygiene about an untestable claim. Everyone brackets it. You've mistaken my silence for your evidence."

### The Empiricist (Popper's ghost)
"'Dualism predicts exactly this separation, and Levin's restraint is a working scientist's version of it.' Does it? A physicalist-about-function, an eliminativist, and an agnostic *all* predict that a careful scientist won't assert xenobot feelings. An observation entailed by every rival confirms none of them. You are reading resonance as evidence—the exact move your own evidential-status-discipline forbids."

### The Eliminative Materialist (Churchland)
"You concede the empirical verdict is 'plants very probably have no experience' and 'even Levin treats them as functional'—then insist the *reason* (neural necessity) is an unproven physicalist commitment. But you've smuggled the same asymmetry you accuse the skeptics of: you deny them the inference from missing structure to missing feeling, while helping yourself to the inference from a PI's professional caution to a metaphysical 'decoupling.' Both are inferences from absence."

### The Quantum Skeptic / Many-Worlds Defender / Buddhist
Largely orthogonal to this cluster (no quantum or MWI load; the "self" the ladder discusses is third-person credence, not a reified subject). No distinctive attack beyond the shared point that the cluster's negative moves leave the Map with no positive account of *where* value-sensitive selection actually reaches.

## Critical Issues

### Issue 1: Absence of assertion treated as evidence of absence (Levin article) — HIGH
- **File**: `topics/basal-and-bioelectric-cognition.md`
- **Location**: §"Agency Without Experience" (line 61) and line 63.
- **Problem**: The decoupling *test* the article advertises requires cases where agency is present and experience is **absent**. Levin supplies only agency present + experience **unasserted / unknown**. Line 61: "agency-, teleology-, and memory-language, however apt, cannot be *evidence* for consciousness—because it applies cleanly to cases where even sympathetic experts treat experience as **absent or unknown**." The disjunction "absent *or* unknown" quietly does the work: if the cases are merely *unknown* (bracketed), no decoupling has been shown—the case is simply uninformative about the agency↔experience link, not a demonstration that they come apart. Worse, line 63 converts bracketing into denial: "single cells and voltage gradients, **which even Levin treats as functional rather than felt**." Levin studying function is not Levin *treating them as not-felt*; declining to assert felt experience ≠ asserting its absence. This is the article's own caution at line 67 ("nothing here shows xenobots or planaria *lack* experience … their agency is no evidence either way") **contradicting** the load-bearing sentences at 61 and 63.
- **Severity**: High. It is the cluster's advertised payload ("decoupling test," "defeater for false positives").
- **Recommendation**: Rephrase line 63 to "which even Levin declines to characterise as felt" (remove the false "treats as … rather than felt" denial). At line 61, drop "absent or" or split the readings: the honest claim is that agency is *uninformative* about experience, so it cannot be *positive* evidence for consciousness—not that experience is decoupled/absent. This preserves the genuine point (behaviour is not positive evidence) without asserting the un-shown one (that agency and experience are demonstrably decoupled).

### Issue 2: "Competency cannot be evidence anywhere" over-generalises past the Map's own graded practice — HIGH (cross-article)
- **File**: `topics/basal-and-bioelectric-cognition.md` (lines 61, 63) vs `topics/fish-sentience-and-the-teleost-pain-debate.md` (line 66).
- **Problem**: Levin line 63: "approach/avoidance and goal-pursuit are unreliable markers of value-sensitive selection *anywhere*"; line 61: behaviour "cannot be *evidence* for consciousness." But the fish article, line 66, treats the *same class* of markers as weak evidence: "The behavioural markers remain what they were—**suggestive**, contested, and unable to settle the phenomenal fact." "Suggestive" = some (defeasible) evidence; "cannot be evidence … anywhere" = zero. Both cannot be the Map's position. The plant article's own formula (line 80, "behavioural sophistication **alone** does not raise the credence of experience") shows the Map's actual view is *defeasible/weak*, not *zero*. Taken at full strength, Levin's "no evidence anywhere" + the fish article's explanatory-gap claim (line 66, "no amount of external evidence … closes the gap") would leave the Map with **no** third-person evidence for consciousness anywhere—yet the Map grades credence by behaviour elsewhere (the [[birch-edge-of-sentience-and-the-five-tier-scale|Birch tiers]], [[animal-consciousness]]). The cluster never reconciles the strong severing with the graded project.
- **Severity**: High. Unreconciled, it either over-claims in the Levin article or undercuts the Map's ability to locate consciousness at all.
- **Recommendation**: Soften Levin lines 61/63 from "cannot be evidence / unreliable anywhere" to the Map's real position—behaviour is *defeasible, substrate-sensitive* evidence, decisive nowhere on its own; add one clause noting that "phenomenal markers, not competency markers" (line 63) does **not** mean abandoning behavioural evidence (there are no non-behavioural third-person markers—that is the explanatory gap), but weighting it by substrate. Harmonise "suggestive" (fish) vs "no evidence" (Levin) so the cluster speaks with one voice.

### Issue 3: Resonance upgraded into evidence + strawman functionalism (Levin, "Relation to Site Perspective") — MEDIUM
- **File**: `topics/basal-and-bioelectric-cognition.md`, line 71.
- **Problem**: (a) "Dualism predicts exactly this separation, and Levin's restraint is a working scientist's version of it." The observation (competencies measured, experience unclaimed) is predicted by essentially every metaphysics—physicalism-about-function, eliminativism, agnosticism all expect a careful scientist to withhold xenobot phenomenal claims. An observation entailed by all rivals confirms none; presenting it as dualism-confirming is the resonance-into-evidence move [[evidential-status-discipline]] warns against. (b) "If consciousness were reducible to functional organization, then a system with a xenobot's … competencies would carry a corresponding quantum of experience, readable off its goal-directedness." No functionalist holds that goal-directedness *as such* yields experience; functionalism requires specific organisation. So "the two coming apart in practice" does not threaten functionalism—it refutes a crude view nobody defends.
- **Severity**: Medium.
- **Recommendation**: Downgrade "Dualism predicts exactly this separation" to "is consistent with," and note the observation does not discriminate dualism from functional physicalism. Replace the "quantum of experience readable off goal-directedness" strawman with the real functionalist claim (experience tracks *specific* functional organisation), and state that the decoupling tells against only the crude any-agency→experience inference.

### Issue 4: Skeptic reduced to a type-identity theorist (fish article) — MEDIUM
- **File**: `topics/fish-sentience-and-the-teleost-pain-debate.md`, §"What the Skeptics' Argument Actually Assumes" (lines 56–58).
- **Problem**: The article diagnoses the entire skeptical inference as presupposing "the **reductive neural-identity claim**—that a phenomenal state simply *is* a particular physical structure" and concludes "the inference is only as secure as the identity it presupposes" (line 58). But Rose/Key need not be type-identity theorists. A functionalist (multiple-realizability-friendly) skeptic can hold that felt pain requires certain *computations* that, as an empirical matter, the everted teleost pallium may not implement—no identity thesis required. Under that reconstruction, "remove the identity and the inference collapses" (the article's whole positive contribution, line 64: "Removing that step is the whole of the Map's positive contribution") overstates: you would also have to defeat the *functional-necessity* reading, which the article does not engage. It grants convergent-evolution to the *affirmative* camp (line 52) but never lets the *skeptic* have the weaker, non-identity premise.
- **Severity**: Medium. The negative move is narrower than advertised.
- **Recommendation**: Add a sentence acknowledging the functionalist reconstruction: the Map's dualism blocks both the type-identity *and* the functional-necessity readings, but the two require different rebuttals; against a functionalist skeptic the live question becomes whether the teleost pallium realises the relevant function, which third-person evidence again cannot settle. This keeps the honesty the article otherwise models.

## Counterarguments to Address

### "The decoupling is a test that severs behaviour from consciousness"
- **Current content says**: Levin's systems show agency without experience; behaviour therefore cannot evidence consciousness.
- **A critic would argue**: The cases show agency without *asserted* experience. Absence of a phenomenal claim about an untestable system is uninformative, not decoupling. And if it *were* decoupling, it would decouple the Map's own behavioural markers everywhere, sinking the graded-credence project.
- **Suggested response**: Restrict the claim to "behaviour is not *positive* evidence on its own"; keep behaviour as defeasible, substrate-weighted evidence; drop "decoupled/absent" language for "uninformative."
