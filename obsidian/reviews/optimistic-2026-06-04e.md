## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The page locates dualism precisely where Chalmers would want it: "the Map's dualism *lives at this bridge* — it is where reducibility is contested in concrete cases, not in the abstract." It refuses to let the hard problem dissolve into a verbal dispute by specifying the single register (sub-personal role → felt quality) where identity-under-description and genuine surplus come apart.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
Less central here, but the causal-work reading is the precondition for any interface story: if the felt quality were merely the computational role redescribed, there would be nothing for a quantum-selection interface to *do*. The page protects the explanandum the interface program needs.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The "felt awfulness" that survives nociceptive detection in asymbolia is exactly the first-person residue Nagel insists objective specification leaves out — "an account that specifies only the role has left out a working part." The page centres that residue without inflating it.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The causal-work reading — feeling as a distinct causal increment, not an inert redescription of a role — is congenial to a process view in which experience contributes rather than supervenes. Crucially, the page does *not* let this congeniality cash out as a tier-upgrade: it keeps the causal-work reading a "live hypothesis, not an established result."

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
The surplus the causal-work reading defends is the same surplus agency needs: a feeling that does work of its own rather than being shadowed by a sub-personal policy-selector.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
Exemplary humility: "Defining a battlefield is not the same as declaring a winner." The page explicitly declines to adjudicate and names the temptation it resists.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This is the load-bearing verdict, and it is strongly positive. The article is a *worked instance* of evidential-status discipline, not a citation of it. Three calibration moves stand out, each a praise-worthy thing *not* done:
- It refuses to present a **shared explanandum as confirmation**: "When the Map concedes 'the dissociation is compatible with both readings,' it has not conceded the dispute — it has *relocated* it." Pain asymbolia is correctly handled as compatible with *both* a PP valence-prior account and the Map's, so compatibility is not laundered into evidence.
- It names and forbids **establish-by-relocation** by name: "That would be a possibility-to-probability slippage of exactly the kind [[evidential-status-discipline]] is built to catch." This is the rare article that pre-empts its own most tempting abuse.
- It is honest about grade: the tenets "*motivate* the causal-work reading and *remove* the standard physicalist reason to default to constitution — but they do not, by themselves, supply positive evidence that the surplus is real." This is the canonical *tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating* pattern, executed cleanly. The Process Philosopher's praise and the Hardline Empiricist's converge on the same passages — the diagnostic that the tension is resolved honestly.
