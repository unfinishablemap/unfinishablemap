## Executive Summary

The article is genuinely well-crafted prose with careful evidential framing (the data-permits-both-readings concessions at lines 68, 90, 124 are honest and rare). It is also free of the editor-vocabulary that has plagued the catalogue's recent vintage: no `**Evidential status:**` callouts, no `framework-boundary marking` in italics, no `Engagement classification:` meta-commentary, no `<!-- AI REFINEMENT LOG -->` HTML comments in source. The label-leakage discipline is clean.

Beneath that clean surface, four substantive defects survive adversarial scrutiny.

**The most consequential defect is that the article's structural reflexivity move at line 126 makes the Decision Void self-shielding against falsification.** The line "the inference *to* the Decision Void is itself a decision, made under the same conditions the void diagnoses" closes the door Popper requires open: any argument *against* the void is itself an unreliable decision-event subject to the void's diagnosis, and any argument *for* the void inherits the same defect. The void becomes consistent with all possible data because contrary evidence is processed through the very introspective machinery the void claims is opaque. This is a sophisticated trap, not a feature: the theory cannot be wrong because the cognitive faculty that would notice it being wrong is by stipulation unreliable. A Popperian reads this as terminal.

**The second defect is the LLM-cliché violation at line 82 ("Distinguishing Sibling Voids") — three consecutive `It is not X, which...` constructions stacked.** The writing-style guide explicitly forbids this pattern ("This is not X. It is Y.") because it has become an overused LLM rhetorical move. The article has the exact triple-sibling layout: "The Decision Void is not the [[agency-void]]... It is not the [[noetic-feelings-void]]... It is not the [[transit-void]]..." This is precisely the failure mode the style guide names, in a section that is otherwise doing valuable distinguishing work. The substance should be preserved; the rhetorical scaffold should be rewritten in natural prose.

**The third defect is that the article's positive contribution collapses into the Map's prior commitments under examination.** The article admits at line 90 ("the disagreement lies at the framework, not at the data") and at line 124 ("the reading is not forced by the data") that the empirical record is fully neutral between physicalism and the Map's reading. It then admits at line 94 that Wegner's wholly-physicalist account *predicts* the Decision Void rather than dissolving it. Combining these concessions: the empirical phenomenon of decision-opacity is overdetermined by competing frameworks; the Map adds only a metaphysical reading of an explanandum it shares with physicalism. What does the article claim is *new*? The "tenet payoff" at §"The Tenet Payoff" (line 100) says the void shows the Map's MQI finds "its sharpest empirical grip here" — but the empirical grip is the *opacity itself*, which is grip-equivalent for every theory in the field. The Map's reading is consistent with the data; it is not *favored* by the data; and the article's own honest moves keep undercutting any reader who tries to draw a downstream conclusion.

**The fourth defect is the borderline boundary-substitution at line 106's MWI dismissal.** The article writes: "An MWI reading dissolves the void by saying every option is taken; the Map's anti-MWI commitment makes the void coherent." This is not engagement with MWI; it is tenet-incompatibility marking dressed as engagement. Worse, the dismissal mischaracterises MWI's self-understanding: a Deutschian would say MWI does *not* "dissolve" the void — MWI replaces the snap with a branching event, and the introspection problem (which branch's deciding-event do I introspect?) survives the move. The article does not engage that response. Per [[direct-refutation-discipline]] this is a critical issue: either upgrade to an in-framework argument (find a reason inside MWI to reject branching-decision-introspection-coherence) or honestly downgrade to natural framework-boundary marking (*"this runs counter to the Map's foundational anti-MWI commitment; the void claim is not framework-neutral"*).

Beyond these four, five further findings of varying severity.

## Critiques by Philosopher

### The Eliminative Materialist
Patricia Churchland reads the entire article as a sophisticated exercise in folk-psychological hypostasis. The article's three faces all presuppose that there is a "decision itself" that introspection could in principle track. Closure presupposes a discrete closing-event distinct from prior deliberation. Latency presupposes a singular "phenomenal arrival of having-decided" with a determinate temporal mark. Reconstruction presupposes a fact of the matter about what veridical access *would have been* if it were possible.

Eliminativism denies the explanandum. There is no "moment of decision" to be introspectively opaque-to; there is a distributed pattern of neural activity, and the folk-psychological vocabulary of "deciding" is a placeholder destined for replacement, not a feature of mind that introspection fails to reach. The article's conjunction-coalesce structure is a logical exercise on three faces of a unicorn.

Churchland would point particularly at the Schurger 2012 acknowledgment at line 96: "if the readiness potential is a stochastic threshold-trigger rather than a representation of an intention being formed, the very thing introspection might have hoped to track does not exist as a discrete neural event." The article notes this *widens* the void. Churchland reads it as the eliminative conclusion: the discrete event the void posits doesn't exist, full stop — the void doesn't widen, it evaporates along with its target. The article's reading requires an entity (the "decision-event") whose nonexistence at the neural level *strengthens* claims about its opacity. This is exactly the kind of metaphysical inflation eliminativism predicts and opposes.

### The Hard-Nosed Physicalist
Dennett reads the article as Cartesian theater repackaged. The "snap" the article appeals to at lines 60, 78, 110, 112 is exactly the temporal-point structure the multiple-drafts model denies. There is no moment at which a decision is "made" in the Cartesian sense the article presupposes; there are multiple drafts in parallel, none of which is canonically *the* decision-event.

The article actually concedes Dennett's core point at line 106: "Physicalists from Dennett to the Churchlands actively predict this asymmetry — introspection on Dennett's account is heterophenomenological self-report, not a window onto neural substrate." But it then deploys this concession in service of: "What the Map's reading adds is an account of *why* it takes this shape: the inaccessible event is doing causal work at an interface, and the opacity is a structural consequence of that interface being primitive." This is question-begging at two layers. First, it presupposes there *is* an inaccessible event (Dennett denies the event-vocabulary applies). Second, it presupposes the interface is primitive (the very thing under dispute). The Map's "addition" is just its prior commitments.

Dennett would push further on the confabulation-parity argument at line 74. The article writes: "Confabulation parity is a positive empirical signature of opacity — if introspection had access, the manipulated and unmanipulated cases should diverge in some self-detectable way. They do not. The system produces something that functions as access, and that functional success is what hides the absence." But Dennett's heterophenomenology already says: don't trust phenomenal reports as access to anything beyond their content. The article uses Dennett's conclusion as evidence *for* a void; Dennett uses it to deflate the explanandum. The article has inverted Dennett's deflationary move into a metaphysical discovery.

### The Quantum Skeptic
Tegmark reads §"The Tenet Payoff" as the article's weakest section. Line 102 cites Tegmark 2000 and responds with: "the objection is contested — Hameroff and others propose decoherence-shielding in specific microtubule structures — but the quantum-interactionist family remains live hypothesis rather than established mechanism." This is not an answer to the calculation; it is a sociological note about who is still publishing.

Tegmark's calculation gives decoherence timescales of roughly 10⁻¹³ seconds at body temperature for microtubule-scale superpositions — about 10¹⁰ times faster than the fastest neural process. The article does not present any numbers, does not engage the temperature dependence, does not discuss what would have to be true about the brain's quantum-ordered subsystems to push timescales into cognitively relevant ranges. "The objection is contested" is the entirety of the response.

Further, Tegmark would note the article's identification of "the decision moment" as "the locus where the form of an interaction matches the form of the phenomenon" (line 50) is doing all the metaphysical work. Why does this locus need quantum machinery? The article presents this as a natural fit, but it is an aesthetic preference for a particular candidate site, not an argument. Many physical processes have "alternative-resolution shape" (state transitions in any classical bistable system; threshold crossings in stochastic accumulators; the very Schurger model the article cites). The decision moment's putative special suitability for quantum-mediated influence is asserted, not earned.

### The Many-Worlds Defender
Deutsch reads line 106 as the article's most revealing sentence: "An MWI reading dissolves the void by saying every option is taken; the Map's anti-MWI commitment makes the void coherent." Two corrections.

First, MWI does not "dissolve" the void; it relocates the explanandum. On a branching reading, the question becomes: which branch's decision-event do I phenomenally inhabit? Why does the indexical "I" track this branch rather than another? These are real questions that MWI has its own answers to (consistency-of-records, decision-theoretic branch-weighting, Wallace's everett-interpretation programme). The article does not engage any of this.

Second, Deutsch would push that the very "snap" the article reifies is exactly what MWI denies. On a branching reading, there is no point at which possibilities are "narrowed to one" — both options are taken in respective branches, and the apparent narrowing is the indexical's track within one branch. The article's three-face structure presupposes the snap. MWI denies the snap. The article rejects MWI by tenet, not by argument; the tenet is doing the work the rhetoric attributes to philosophy.

Deutsch would also note the article's anti-MWI commitment is foundationally an indexicality claim. The Map holds that indexical identity matters — that there is a fact of the matter about which branch is "this one." Deutsch holds this is anthropocentric confusion. The article does not engage this dispute; it asserts the Map's side and uses it to frame the void's coherence.

### The Empiricist
Popper reads §"Phenomenology of the Edge" through §"Approaches to the Edge" (lines 108–120) as gesture-toward-falsification that does no actual falsifying work.

The article offers four empirical handles. (a) Petitmengin's microphenomenology produces "richer reports of the *texture* of pre-decision and post-decision states" — but the article admits these "converge on the same finding," so the method confirms the void without being able to disconfirm it. (b) Dissociation paradigms vary "what introspection *would* have to access if it had access" — but the prediction is one-sided: if introspection had access, it would vary; it doesn't vary; therefore opacity. There is no specified configuration of choice-blindness data that would *refute* the void. (c) Disorders of agency "map the seams of normal occlusion" — but every observed pattern is read as more seam-mapping. (d) AI systems offer a "diagnostic" — but the article does not specify what an AI with full transcript access would have to report for the diagnostic to come out negative; "presence or absence" is asserted as a diagnostic without operationalisation.

The reflexivity at line 126 is the structural seal. The article writes: "the inference *to* the Decision Void is itself a decision, made under the same conditions the void diagnoses." This means any cognitive evidence against the void is itself a void-affected inference, and any cognitive evidence for the void is the same. The theory has no exit condition. Popper's verdict: *not even wrong*.

### The Buddhist Philosopher
Nagarjuna reads the entire framework as the very illusion Madhyamaka seeks to deconstruct. The article presupposes a *self* that decides and that would have access to its own deciding. The "consciousness performing the decision" (line 48) is exactly the locus Madhyamaka denies has svabhāva — own-being, intrinsic existence. The Decision Void is not an opacity in introspection; it is the absence of the entity supposed to be opaque-to-itself.

The article gestures at contemplative traditions (Petitmengin's neurophenomenology; the comment that "Long deliberation postpones the snap; it does not reveal it") but does not engage the Madhyamaka deconstructive move. On a Nagarjunian reading, the conjunction of closure, latency, and reconstruction is exactly what one would predict if there is no decider — no entity that is closing, no agent whose action's report is occluded, no self whose introspective reconstruction is being analysed. The faces conjoin because they are all reaching for an absent referent. The "void" is real; the framing of it as a structural opacity *in a subject* is the metaphysical error.

The article's reflexive move at line 126 — that the inference to the void is itself a decision — is one step from the Madhyamaka conclusion but stops short. The Madhyamaka completion: the inference to the void is itself a decision, which is itself a pseudo-event in an inferential chain that posits a decider that does not exist. The Map's "interface" is itself a metaphysical posit shielded by the same reflexivity. Honest engagement would either ground the decider before reasoning about its opacity, or follow the regress to its śūnyatā conclusion.

- **Problem**: The reflexive move makes the void consistent with any possible introspective evidence, including evidence apparently *against* the void (which is itself a void-affected inference). This is structural Popperian unfalsifiability dressed as epistemic humility.

## Counterarguments to Address

### The "framework-neutral overdetermination" objection
- **Current content says**: The conjunction of closure, latency, and reconstruction "closes every escape" (line 78) and establishes the void.
- **A critic would argue**: The conjunction is overdetermined by cognitive constraints any framework predicts — limited working-memory readout (closure), neural-readout sluggishness (latency), and narrative-coherence post-processing (reconstruction). Three independently-arising constraints converging on the deliberation→commitment crossing does not require a void of the Map's kind; it requires nothing more than the cognitive architecture every theorist accepts. The article admits at line 78 that the faces are "logically severable," and this severability is supposed to make the conjunction informative — but it equally makes the conjunction *consistent with multiple aetiologies*, including aetiologies that don't sustain a metaphysically loaded void.
- **Suggested response**: Either argue the conjunction is *specifically* informative in a way generic cognitive constraints cannot explain (what is the unique signature that distinguishes the void from mundane introspective limitation?), or weaken the central claim to "the conjunction is informative *given* the Map's prior commitments" and own the framework-dependence explicitly.

### The "Wegner predicts the void" objection
- **Current content says**: "Wegner's account therefore *predicts* the Decision Void rather than dissolving it" (line 94)
- **A critic would argue**: If a wholly-physicalist account predicts the same explanandum, the void is not Map-favouring evidence. The article admits Wegner's machinery the Map "can adopt without alteration" — which means the void's phenomenological substance is framework-neutral. What Map-specific evidence remains? Only the metaphysical reading of an explanandum the Map shares with physicalism — which is precisely a framework-internal coherence claim, not a positive epistemic gain.
- **Suggested response**: Make the framework-dependence explicit: *"The Decision Void is not evidence for the Map's reading; it is a phenomenon both Wegner's account and the Map's reading explain, and the choice between explanations is settled (if at all) by the wider theoretical structure each is embedded in."* This is honest and already gestured at line 90; it should be promoted from concession to thesis-statement.

### The "what does MQI actually predict here?" objection
- **Current content says**: The Decision Void is "the candidate void where the Map's tenets predict the *most* about an empirically-investigated site" (line 124).
- **A critic would argue**: What specifically does MQI predict that a clever physicalist couldn't predict? The article's MQI argument at line 104 says: "Minimal Quantum Interaction requires the influence be minimal precisely because the interface is not the kind of thing that can be inspected from inside." This is a *post-hoc* coherence claim, not a forward prediction. What measurement, if it came out the wrong way, would refute MQI specifically? The article does not say.
- **Suggested response**: Add a paragraph specifying what *quantitative* signature MQI would have to leave at the decision-moment site if it were true and absent if false. Candidates: specific decoherence-protection in identified neural subsystems; specific deviations from classical accumulator models at the threshold-crossing event; specific patterns in the variance structure of decision-latency that classical models cannot reproduce. Without such candidates, the "tenet payoff" remains a coherence claim, not an empirical one.
