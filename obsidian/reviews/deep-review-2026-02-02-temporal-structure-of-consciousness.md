## Stability Notes

The following represent fundamental philosophical disagreements rather than fixable problems:

- **Eliminative materialist objection**: The article takes temporal phenomenology as genuine explanandum; eliminativists deny there is such a thing. This is a bedrock disagreement at the level of the Map's tenets.

- **Functionalist objection to "what it's like"**: The article follows Chalmers in taking the further question (what it's like beyond functional description) seriously. Dennett-style functionalists deny there is such a question. The article now explicitly engages this but does not attempt to refute it—this too is a foundational disagreement.

- **Contemplative evidence falsifiability**: Reports from meditation practitioners about cessation states and witness consciousness lack clear falsifiability criteria. The article presents them as "consistent with the framework, though not proof"—this is appropriate epistemic humility, not a flaw to fix.

- **Buddhist empty-witness concern**: Whether the "witness" of witness consciousness has substantial existence or is itself empty is a deep metaphysical question the article does not resolve. Future engagement with emptiness teachings could be valuable but would require substantial expansion.
