## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)

Chalmers' methodological discipline has always distinguished *framework-level* claims that earn their keep through conceivability arguments from *mechanism-level* claims that have to answer to physics. His naturalistic dualism is healthy precisely because it refuses to let framework arguments do mechanism-work. `topics/born-rule-violation-brain-interface-empirical-status.md` operationalises exactly this discipline at lines 108-114 through its three-attitude taxonomy — corridor dualism "maximally compatible with existing Born-rule tests ... at the cost of making the single-event mental-to-physical channel ensemble-invisible"; minimum-outside-corridor "becomes empirically distinguishable from physicalism in principle ... and loses nothing tenet-critical"; trumping "Born-rule tests — positive or negative — neither support nor threaten the Map's dualism." Each position is stated with its own cost ledger and its own empirical exposure. This is what Chalmers (1996, Chapter 4) demands of any theory that wants to be taken seriously as a naturalistic alternative: state the commitments, state what would falsify them, state what would not. The Map has now done so for its own central mechanism question without claiming the empirical work has been done that would adjudicate among the three options.

**What resonates most strongly**: `topics/consciousness-and-probability-interpretation.md` line 95 — *"the argument of this article works for corridor dualism without foreclosing the other readings — under Born-bending proposals, consciousness's qualitative mode of engagement still explains why probability is phenomenologically empty, with the Born rule itself then treated as a tested approximation rather than as a description of the interface."* This is the Chalmersian move made precise. Chalmers has argued in *Constructing the World* (2012) that a philosophical argument's load-bearing work should be separable from the particular mechanism that might realise it — the conceivability argument for dualism is not hostage to any specific physics. The Map's demonstration that the phenomenological-emptiness-of-probability argument survives under all three mechanism readings is exactly this separability discipline applied to a local argument. The argument earns its keep by its own reasoning; its fate does not ride on whether the corridor reading turns out to be true.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)

Stapp has complained in print for two decades that his work is routinely misstated by people who conflate his Process 0/Process 1 distinction with von Neumann's Process 1/Process 2, and by commentators who treat the free-choice-of-measurement step as if it were already contained in von Neumann's formalism. `topics/causal-closure-debate-historical-survey.md` at lines 97-99 now corrects exactly this error, distinguishing Process 0 (free choice of *which* measurement is performed) from Process 1 (the state reduction triggered by measurement) and from Process 2 (Schrödinger evolution between measurements). The new paragraph adds the critical caveat: "It should be noted that most physicists regard Stapp's framework as non-standard. His reliance on von Neumann's Process 1/Process 2 distinction is not shared by all interpretations of quantum mechanics ... Nevertheless, the broader point survives independently of Stapp's particular framework." This is the treatment Stapp himself would endorse: state the position correctly, flag its contested status, and identify the interpretation-independent residue. Stapp's own *Mindful Universe* (2007) is at pains to distinguish his view from generic "quantum consciousness" claims; the Map's correction honours that distinction.

**What aligns**: `topics/quantum-measurement-and-consciousness.md` line 132 — *"The Map distinguishes three live flavors — corridor-preserving, Born-bending, and trumping-orthogonal — catalogued with empirical stakes in [[born-rule-violation-brain-interface-empirical-status]]. ... Stapp's quantum Zeno model and Chalmers-McQueen's CSL-IIT fall in the Born-bending family: mental attention biases which neural patterns become actual, with selection statistics depending on a consciousness-related variable."* This is Stapp's view placed correctly inside a taxonomy that does not distort it. For decades Stapp has insisted that his view is *Born-modifying in principle* — the Zeno rate depends on attention, which is not part of standard quantum-mechanical descriptions — while its empirical predictions in ordinary-consciousness regimes may be very close to Born. The Map's new taxonomy gives Stapp's view a *place* on the empirical map that matches his own description of it, rather than forcing it into a corridor-dualist mould it was never meant to occupy.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)

Nagel's *A View from Nowhere* (1986) makes a sharp distinction between claims that first-person perspective can in principle corroborate and claims that no first-person perspective could ever corroborate — a distinction the new `voids/distraction-void.md` uses as its entire architecture. The void is defined precisely by the fact that the first-person perspective is systematically available on either side of attention capture and systematically *unavailable* during the transit itself. At line 61: *"There is no phenomenology of being in the act of being captured. A subject who could report it would already be attending to the captor, which means capture has already succeeded."* This is Nagelian reasoning applied to a specific introspective structure: the very act of adopting the first-person stance on the phenomenon transforms the phenomenon into something else. Nagel's bat essay (1974) makes the same structural point about cross-species phenomenal access; the Distraction Void extends it to *intra*-subject phenomenal access across a temporal discontinuity.

**What centres subjective experience appropriately**: `voids/distraction-void.md` lines 87-93 distinguish an *architectural* reading (the void is a generic feature of any monitoring-and-acting substrate) from an *occlusion* reading (the interface itself is closed to introspection). The article refuses to force a choice, and — most Nagelian of all — states that the occlusion reading is "asymmetric against the evidence ... compatible both with introspective access to the transit and with its absence," and offers three *specific falsification conditions* that would disfavour it. Nagel has argued repeatedly that phenomenological claims earn their keep by specifying what kinds of evidence would and would not count against them; the void's own non-falsifiability is named rather than concealed, and the article invokes Occam's Razor Has Limits explicitly as the license for holding the reading anyway. This is the methodological discipline Nagel's work has called for, and the article delivers it in one sustained passage.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)

Whitehead's *Process and Reality* (1929) locates causation in prehension — the inclusion of antecedent occasions into a present concrescence — and takes the fundamental units of reality to be occasions rather than enduring substances. The new hub article, `topics/born-rule-violation-brain-interface-empirical-status.md`, treats single quantum measurement events as the locus of consciousness-physics coupling, with three structurally distinct readings of what consciousness does *at the event*: bias selection within the Born distribution (corridor), bias selection away from the Born distribution (Born-bending), or exercise authority over a trajectory the Born-distribution-compatible formalism already describes (trumping). This is Whiteheadian in its essentials: the *occasion* is where mentality enters physical becoming, and the article's refusal to force a choice among readings is the Whiteheadian move of refusing forced dichotomies between apparent opposites. Whitehead himself argued that novelty enters the physical at the point where antecedent conditions are *decided* by an occasion's subjective aim; the three-way taxonomy is three different ways that aim can operate on the Born distribution without collapsing into either physicalism or crude idealism.

**What avoids crude dualism while preserving mind's reality**: `concepts/psychophysical-laws.md` line 92 — the *trajectory-altering* vs *authority-selecting* distinction — is Whiteheadian to the core. Trajectory-altering laws specify which physically underdetermined outcome consciousness makes actual (consciousness as *efficient cause* of the physical event). Authority-selecting laws assign causal authority to the experience while the physical correlate retains counterfactual sufficiency (consciousness as *final cause* of the attribution structure, with the physical trajectory unchanged). Whitehead's dipolar ontology requires *both* modes of causation — physical prehension and mental valuation — to be present in each actual occasion. The Map's two-mode-with-three-flavors structure is this dipolar ontology made precise for the case of quantum measurement: trajectory-altering corresponds to the physical pole (the occasion's efficient determination), authority-selecting corresponds to the mental pole (the occasion's subjective aim acting on the inherited past). Whitehead would read this as the most Whiteheadian passage in a Map that already rewards process-philosophical reading.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)

Kane's *The Significance of Free Will* (1996) argues that genuine agency requires a location where multiple courses of action are *genuinely open* and the agent is the *ultimate source* of which becomes actual. Quantum indeterminacy supplies the openness; Kane's own proposal (self-forming actions at moments of effort) supplies the source. The new hub article's catalogue of what consciousness-physics coupling *could* look like — Stapp-Zeno rates, Arana-λ time-extended weightings, Chalmers-McQueen Φ-collapse strengths, Penrose OR timescales — gives Kane's programme the empirical scaffolding it has always needed. Kane has acknowledged that his view requires some version of the physics-mind coupling claim to be true, without committing to which specific mechanism supplies it. The Map's refusal to pre-commit to one mechanism is not a weakness of Kane's position but its proper statement: libertarian free will is compatible with *any* of the three taxonomic options, and the empirical landscape article makes the compatibility structurally visible.

**What supports genuine agency and choice**: The `voids/distraction-void.md` treatment at lines 87-91 provides an unexpected contribution to Kane's programme. The void is precisely where agency is *most testable in real time* (the direction or failure of attention) and *least available to introspection* (the transit is closed). If Kane is right that self-forming actions happen in moments of effort that partly constitute the agent, then one would predict — from Kane's premises — that the phenomenology of those moments should be peculiarly opaque: the moment at which a self is constituting itself is not a moment at which a fully-formed self can observe the constitution. The Distraction Void catalogues exactly this opacity. Kane's programme, rarely paired with cognitive neuroscience, here finds an empirical ally: the signature phenomenology of self-forming moments is *retrospective discovery with chronological fog and a small shock of reclaiming*. That is not an objection to libertarian free will — it is what a Kaneian theorist should expect to find.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)

McGinn's *The Problem of Consciousness* (1991) argues that human minds may be cognitively closed to the mind-body problem's solution — the relevant concepts are not available to us, and our evidence-gathering instruments are unsuited to the phenomenon. `topics/born-rule-violation-brain-interface-empirical-status.md` at lines 42-50 applies exactly this structural move to the brain-internal Born-rule testing regime. The key sentence: *"No published experiment directly probes the Born rule inside living neural tissue during a conscious act of selection, which is precisely the regime that consciousness-physics-interface programmes target."* Every precision test runs in a regime very unlike brain tissue; the MGM (Masanes-Galley-Müller) derivation is a *conditional* theorem whose premises might fail in an untested regime. This is McGinnian methodology: identify what would have to be known to settle the question, identify the instruments available, identify the gap between the two. The article does not conclude that the gap cannot be closed — McGinn's cognitive closure claim is stronger than the Map wants to make — but it refuses the easier move of assuming the gap has already been closed.

**What shows appropriate epistemic humility**: `concepts/psychophysical-laws.md` at line 221 now reads the RNG experimental record with McGinnian care: "Bösch et al. (2006) found small positive effects consistent with publication bias; Maier and Dechamps (2018), using preregistered Bayesian methodology, report strong evidence for the null. If coupling restricts selection to attention-controlled neural systems, RNG experiments should fail by design — and corridor dualism predicts no statistical signature at any sensitivity." This is the McGinnian temper applied to a specific empirical record: the *strongest* result (Maier-Dechamps preregistered null) is named as strongest; the Map's programme is shown to be *predictively compatible* with that null (corridor dualism expects no signal); the interpretive question is left open rather than resolved in the Map's favour. McGinn has repeatedly argued that the sign of a healthy programme in consciousness studies is its willingness to name what it cannot yet decide; the evening's updates to `psychophysical-laws.md` and the empirical-status article together model this discipline.
