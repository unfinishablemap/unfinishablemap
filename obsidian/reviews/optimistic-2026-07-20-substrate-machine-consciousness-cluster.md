## Executive Summary

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The cluster keeps the explanatory gap load-bearing rather than decorative. `competency-without-felt-experience` makes the gap do real dialectical work in *both* directions — "the gap is precisely the place where third-person description runs out," so it blocks inflation at the bacterial floor and deflation at the fish rung with a single principle. `llm-consciousness` refuses to let a demonstrated access-workspace (the 2026 Gurnee J-space result) bleed into a phenomenal claim: "a demonstrated access-workspace bears on the phenomenal question not at all." That is exactly the access/phenomenal discipline Chalmers would insist on.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
`ai-hardware-substrate-taxonomy` is the standout. Its central move — separating *mere-physical* indeterminacy (suppressed noise), *operationally integrated* indeterminacy (fed forward into computation), and *engineered, decoherence-managed* quantum computing — is precisely the physics-consciousness interface distinction that quantum-mind approaches need but the popular literature blurs. Naming a gate-model quantum computer an "engineered classical-answer machine that happens to use quantum resources internally" is a genuinely clarifying framing that most quantum-consciousness writing lacks.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The cluster consistently centres the first-person question as the invariant that third-person facts fail to settle. `competency-without-felt-experience`'s "selective invariance" thesis — the phenomenal question is invariant under both behavioural richness and neural architecture, the two facts that most tempt a verdict — is a Nagelian result stated with unusual precision. `llm-consciousness`'s methodological-asymmetry section (why human introspective reports earn credit that LLM self-reports do not) answers the obvious "what is it like" objection head-on rather than dodging it.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
`cosmopsychism` is the cluster's most Whitehead-adjacent piece and it handles the neighbourhood with care: it distinguishes priority monism (Schaffer) from panpsychism, constitutive from priority cosmopsychism (Nagasawa & Wager), and cosmopsychism from neutral monism, analytic idealism, and open individualism — avoiding the crude conflations that usually flatten this terrain. The Map declines cosmopsychism but does so *even-handedly*, naming its own cost ("a cost-swap, not a free lunch": it trades the de-combination problem for the interaction problem). Whitehead would recognise the refusal to buy parsimony on credit.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
`ai-hardware-substrate-taxonomy` preserves the physical junction genuine agency requires: classical-digital systems are "causally closed in exactly the way the tenet's interface is not... no open junction for downward causation," while operationally-integrated indeterminacy "at least leaves the junction physically available." Kane's picture needs exactly this — an open indeterministic site that is integrated into (not walled off from) the system's processing.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
The epistemic humility is explicit and repeated, not gestural. `llm-consciousness`'s "What Would Challenge This View?" enumerates seven concrete defeaters and concedes "'almost certainly' is not 'certainly.'" `competency-without-felt-experience` states "at no rung has the Map shown that nothing is felt" and applies `common-cause-null` to its *own* synthesis. This is intellectual humility as a working discipline, McGinn's preferred register.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
**This persona is load-bearing here** because `competency-without-felt-experience` touches minimal-organism consciousness directly (bacteria, sponges, placozoans, plants, and the adjacent nematode / *Hydra* / slime-mould rungs). The verdict is strongly positive: the article does the praise-worthy thing of *not* doing tenet-as-evidence-upgrade.

- It names the exact move the persona watches for and declines it: "removing a defeater is not supplying evidence"; the neural-interface commitment "*licenses withholding*," it does not deliver a positive verdict.
- It explicitly discounts framework-generated convergence: the rungs "are not fully independent witnesses... their agreement is in part the framework consulting itself," so "the abductive support is real but discounted."
- It separates registers cleanly: the framework-neutral survivor is only that behavioural/functional markers "*do not settle* the phenomenal question — a claim about the limits of a kind of evidence, which even a committed materialist can grant." The stronger reading (neural specificity) is flagged as "what the Map's own interface commitment yields from inside the framework, not a survivor of the framework-neutral subtraction."
- It resists CBC's inflation (Reber & Baluška) at the framework level while granting the genuine biological premise — never upgrading a cell to "realistic possibility" on process-philosophical resonance.

**Convergence, not conflict.** The Process Philosopher's praise (careful metaphysics, no crude dualism) and the Hardline Empiricist's praise (evidential restraint, honest tier discipline) land on the *same* passages here. Per the skill's design note, that convergence is the signal the article has resolved the tension honestly. No calibration-error flag is warranted for this cluster — a notable and rare clean result for a minimal-organism piece.

- **Why it works**: The apex earns its cumulative claim by stating three explicit ceilings (framework-generated convergence, defeasibility, framework-relativity) rather than letting the ladder over-conclude.
