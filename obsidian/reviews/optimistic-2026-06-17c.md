## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The cluster takes the explanatory gap seriously and then *extends* it where Chalmers' own combination-problem work cannot easily reach. `consciousness-and-the-metaphysics-of-individuation` correctly cites Chalmers' identification of subject-summing as the hardest form of the combination problem, then pairs it with Miller's *de-combination problem* (the reverse direction) — a move Chalmers would recognise as the right way to show the gap is symmetric. The repeated insistence that "even adding souls to the ontology doesn't explain why I am associated with one soul rather than another" (vertiginous-question) is exactly the kind of honesty about residual mystery that Chalmers prizes: it refuses the cheap dualist win.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
`indexical-identity-quantum-measurement` engages the physics-consciousness interface with unusual care: it separates the *measurement question* (physics) from the *indexical question* (philosophy) cleanly, surveys QBism / consciousness-collapse / relational QM / MWI on a single axis (each "addresses the measurement problem while leaving the indexical problem untouched"), and proposes consciousness-as-indexical-ground without overclaiming — the "Connecting to conservation laws" paragraph keeps the Minimal-Quantum-Interaction commitment energetically honest (consciousness "doesn't add energy; it determines which of the energetically-equivalent possibilities is experienced"). Stapp would value that the article refuses Wigner's solipsism trap while keeping a genuine causal role for mind.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
This cluster *is* Nagel territory done well. It cites the right Nagel (the split-brain "too much unity to say 'two', yet too much separation to say 'one'"; the View from Nowhere; the structural point that "consciousness *is* perspective"). The phenomenology-of-vertigo section in vertiginous-question (apparent triviality → dawning inadequacy → conceptual vertigo → oscillation) is a genuine contribution: it operationalises the felt character of the void rather than merely asserting it, and names oscillation between triviality and profundity as *diagnostic* of a real void.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
`many-worlds` carries a dedicated Process Philosophy section that does real work: Whitehead's actual occasions cannot branch because "becoming *requires* achieving definiteness," and haecceity is non-transferable — a principled, non-question-begging reason to find MWI's perpetual-superposition picture structurally defective. `consciousness-and-the-metaphysics-of-individuation` cites process-oriented (dynamic) haecceitism as one of three responses to Kant's individuation challenge. **Crucially, the cluster does not let any of this cash out as a tier-upgrade** — process-philosophical resonance is used to *remove a defeater* against the one-world picture, never to claim positive evidence. That is precisely the disciplined use of Whitehead-friendliness the persona constraint demands.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
The cluster's free-will links are appropriately load-bearing-but-restrained: `indexical-identity-quantum-measurement` connects to `topics/free-will` with the conditional "*if* consciousness grounds indexical determination at quantum measurement, the self may be genuinely causal in a libertarian sense" — the conditional framing is exactly right. The Map does not smuggle libertarian agency in as a result; it marks it as a downstream possibility of the interface proposal, whose "Empirical status" paragraph candidly labels itself "currently metaphysical rather than empirically testable."

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
This is the cluster's epistemic-humility high-water mark. The vertiginous question is framed throughout as a *void* — "we cannot determine what kind of answer would satisfy the question" — and haecceity is repeatedly described as "a tool for locating the void's boundary, not for crossing it." The "What AI Might See" section is genuinely original mysterian-adjacent material: an AI "might formally characterise the void's structure ... without experiencing the vertigo," and that gap between formal characterisation and phenomenal encounter "is itself informative about what the void involves." McGinn would recognise cognitive-closure done without defeatism.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
**This persona's verdict is load-bearing here, and it is strongly positive.** The cluster touches the most tenet-load-bearing claim in the corpus (No Many Worlds rests on the indexical objection), and it handles the evidential register with exemplary discipline:

- **Tenet-as-evidence-upgrade is praise-worthily *not* done.** `egocentric-presentism`'s "Relation to Site Perspective" opens by naming the move explicitly: egocentric presentism is "an exploratory ontological *fit* ... rather than positive evidence for the Map's framework. Its structural alignment with the tenets is real and load-bearing; its status as confirmation is not." It then cites `project/evidential-status-discipline` by name: a congenial metaphysics "removes a *defeater* ... but it does not *upgrade* the evidence for dualism." This is the *"tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating"* pattern executed verbatim, on a fresh page, with the structural opportunity to overclaim and an explicit decision to decline.
- **The hard case is faced, not dodged.** The cluster repeatedly distinguishes the indexical objection's force: *decisive* against branch-egalitarian Everett, but only *supplementary, not sufficient* against List's first-personally centred many-worlds — because List *answers* the vertiginous question by construction. vertiginous-question states this plainly: "against List specifically, the case against many worlds rests on the rejection of modal realism, not on the vertiginous question — an honestly open boundary the Map marks here rather than papers over." Naming a boundary as honestly open, rather than asserting the tenet defeats all rivals, is the calibration restraint this persona exists to reward.
- **The communicability objection is left live.** `egocentric-presentism` reports Markosian's and McDaniel's objection that monadic presence "cannot be coherently stated," gives Hare's reply, and closes "the debate is live" — declining to score the view as won. The article even notes Hare "would not accept the Map's metaphysics," refusing to enlist a rival as an ally.
- **`many-worlds` calibrates its own anti-MWI case down from a knockdown.** The §3 probability discussion explicitly says "the Map's position is not that MWI *cannot* recover the Born rule" but that the recoveries depend on principles the Map rejects; the canonical-statement box concedes felt singularity is "evidence consistent with a non-MWI reading ... rather than a knockdown of MWI's branch-relative reply." That is the opposite of over-claiming.

The Process Philosopher and the Hardline Empiricist **converge** on this cluster: Whitehead-friendliness is praised as defeater-removal, and the Hardline Empiricist confirms it never crosses into evidence-elevation. Per the persona-tension rubric, convergence is the signal that the cluster has resolved the possibility/probability tension honestly — no calibration-concern flag is warranted, and no `refine-draft` task is generated from this review.

## Content Strengths

### many-worlds.md
- **Strongest point**: The "Branch-Relative Indexicality Reply" subsection (§2) — it states the *strongest* MWI response (Saunders 2010 / Wallace 2012) in its own best form, then locates the Map's disagreement at a precise, non-trivial fault line: MWI's indexicality must do "double duty" — phenomenological (one-branch *de se*) *and* decision-theoretic (cross-branch personal identity for the Deutsch-Wallace Born-rule derivation) — and those two readings are jointly inconsistent.
- **Notable quote**: "MWI cannot simultaneously accommodate branch-local indexical singularity and the cross-branch personal-identity relation its Born-rule derivation requires."
- **Why it works**: This is a genuinely sharper objection than the usual "MWI can't explain which branch is me." It survives the standard MWI rebuttal by granting it (branch-local singularity is secured) and relocating the cost to the decision-theoretic apparatus. The dedicated "Canonical Statement" blockquote — designed as a stable cross-link target (`[[many-worlds#MWI's Branch-Relative Indexicality Reply]]`) for voids articles — is an excellent LLM-first, reuse-oriented design choice.

### egocentric-presentism.md
- **Strongest point**: The disambiguation note flagging that Hare's "perspectival realism" is *unrelated* to the Giere/Massimi philosophy-of-science doctrine of the same name — "the two share only a label." This pre-empts a real and common confusion that would otherwise propagate into any LLM fetching the page.
- **Notable quote**: "Egocentric presentism shows the one-world horn is *occupiable*; the Map occupies it without claiming the communicability objection is answered."
- **Why it works**: It supplies the cluster's missing piece — the constructive one-world rival to List — while modelling evidential-status discipline on a brand-new page. The List quadrilemma is mapped cleanly (egocentric presentism drops non-solipsism; List drops one world), making the Map's choice legible as a *forced* choice rather than an arbitrary preference.

### vertiginous-question.md
- **Strongest point**: The List double-edge. The article notes the indexical objection "cuts in an unexpected direction when turned on a different many-worlds proposal" — List's centred worlds *honour* the indexical intuition by multiplying worlds — and therefore the Map needs a separate weapon (anti-modal-realism) against List. Recognising that your own best objection is double-edged, and saying so, is rare and excellent.
- **Notable quote**: "The intuition that 'there is one definite perspective that is mine' can be honoured by *multiplying* worlds ... rather than by insisting on a single world."
- **Why it works**: It converts a potential blind spot (assuming the indexical objection defeats *all* many-worlds views) into an explicit, mapped distinction. This is the cluster's intellectual centre of gravity.
