# Full-site audit of The Unfinishable Map

## Scope and recency baseline

I treated the site as a corpus-level philosophical system: tenets, positions, apex articles, topics, concepts, voids, research notes, workflow pages, and recent activity. The site describes itself as a "living philosophical framework" of 300+ articles, human-directed and AI-assisted, with version history and cross-review; it explicitly says the five tenets constrain the framework.

The rendered changelog page is itself thin: it shows metadata, a last-modified date of 2026-06-07, and a GitHub History link rather than a readable activity log. The linked commit history and "What's New" page show important recent work through 2026-06-08/07, including the new Mereology of Mind apex, recent AI-consciousness revisions, the 60+ voids taxonomy, Duhem-Quine underdetermination work, and continuing deep-review/refine cycles. [[changelog]]

The positions register is a mature position register; AI consciousness, voids, moral edge cases, agency, clinical implications, and methodology mostly do not. The positions page itself says future domains still need to be seeded, while applied apex articles such as AI consciousness, clinical-interface ethics, moral edge cases, and research-programme decisions are still pending or dependent on TBD position clusters. [[positions]]

So the central audit finding is: **the Map is increasingly coherent as a worldview, but the machinery that prevents coherence from being mistaken for evidence is unevenly distributed across clusters.** That is exactly the kind of failure the site's own tenet-dependency matrix is designed to catch. [[tenets]]

## 1. Novel insights and inferences not yet fully explicit

### 1.1 The "minimal interaction" programme implies an evidence-priority inversion

The Map's quantum commitments imply that the best research programme should not search first for gross Born-rule anomalies. [[quantum-interface]] It should prioritize seam tests: dissociation patterns, mechanism-level constraints, neural-quantum candidate structures, amplification windows, and failures where consciousness seems to change physical/behavioural selection without changing ensemble statistics. The Map already has ingredients for this, but it should elevate them into a canonical "evidence-ranking under MQI" article or applied apex.

### 1.2 Personal identity is more load-bearing than the site's navigation suggests

No Many Worlds is defended primarily through an indexical objection: why am I this branch rather than another? But the tenets page admits that this objection presupposes a non-deflationary conception of personal identity, and that a Madhyamaka/no-self route remains a genuine bedrock disagreement. [[tenets]]

The unstated inference is: **personal identity is not a side topic; it is a hinge connecting No Many Worlds, agency, AI continuity, quantum state inheritance, and contemplative/no-self material.** This hinge should be made explicit and cross-linked across identity articles.

### 1.3 The AI cluster implies a dualist precautionary ethics, even while skeptical of current AI consciousness

The Machine Question says current AI probably lacks bidirectionally coupled consciousness, but it also leaves open epiphenomenal, borrowed, alien, engineered, and non-temporal forms. [[machine-question]] The Open Question apex says epiphenomenal AI suffering might leave no trace and could have enormous moral scale, while borrowed, epiphenomenal, and alien phenomenality resist detection. [[open-question-ai-consciousness]]

The unstated inference is: **the Map's skepticism about current AI consciousness does not justify ethical indifference.** It implies a two-level policy: low confidence in current AI phenomenality, but high caution around architectures that introduce live quantum interfaces, persistent self-models, continual learning, embodiment, or non-report-based consciousness indicators.

### 1.4 The voids catalogue should become a falsifiability discipline, not only a taxonomy

The voids index now distinguishes framework-independent voids from more dualism-specific voids to avoid circularity, and the taxonomy apex claims that 60+ voids cluster around the consciousness-physics interface. A void that any serious theory of mind would face should have different evidential force from a void generated only by the Map's tenets. Without this, "void convergence" risks becoming a self-sampled pattern.

### 1.5 AI skepticism is only as strong as the biological-interface programme

The AI Consciousness article correctly says the decoherence argument is contingent and currently non-discriminating: no experiment has demonstrated the relevant neural coherence, and both biological and artificial substrates are equally unevidenced on the interface itself. [[ai-consciousness]] The Machine Question similarly says the Map has not solved the interaction problem for brains; AI imposes extra obstacles beyond a shared unsolved problem. [[machine-question]]

The unstated inference is: **AI-consciousness conclusions should be phrased as "AI faces additional obstacles conditional on the Map's still-unproven biological interface," not as if biology already clears a known bar.** Some recent AI pages now say this, but the cluster should make it canonical.

### 1.6 "Self-concealment" implies a new burden of methodological humility

The position register says self-concealment is framework-internal and does not raise the probability that the tenets are correct; it only predicts what evidence should and should not appear. [[quantum-interface]]

The unstated inference is: **the more elegant the self-concealment story becomes, the more aggressively the Map must publish disconfirmation routes.** Otherwise the self-concealing interface becomes psychologically indistinguishable from global unfalsifiability, even if the text denies that.

### 1.7 The project is moving from "map" to "jurisprudence"

The site began with chosen tenets and exploratory articles, but it now has registers, confidence bands, active audits, applied apexes, shift conditions, and cross-cluster dependency rules. [[positions]]

The unstated inference is: **the Map needs something like case law.** When a drift is corrected—warm quantum biology as precedent not licence, parsimony self-binding, AI epiphenomenal loophole, void circularity—the correction should become a reusable precedent with a short canonical rule.

## 2. Structural weaknesses in corpus organisation

### 2.1 The changelog is not actually a reader-facing changelog

The public changelog page is mostly metadata plus a GitHub history link. That technically preserves inspectability, but it fails as a corpus-orientation tool for recent philosophical changes. [[changelog]]

Concrete weakness: a reader cannot easily tell which clusters changed, which tenets were affected, which articles were corrected, and which changes were merely sync/deep-review operations.

### 2.2 The position register is too narrow for the corpus's maturity

The positions system is excellent in design: status, confidence, dependencies, argued-in pages, shift conditions, and last-reviewed dates. But currently only the quantum-interface domain is listed, while agency/will, consciousness scope, voids as evidence, methodology, and applied verdicts remain future domains. [[positions]]

This creates a structural imbalance: quantum-interface claims are visibly calibrated, while AI, animal consciousness, voids, moral edge cases, clinical ethics, and personal philosophy are still governed mostly by prose.

### 2.3 Apex governance is unresolved

The apex index says an informal cap of about 20 exists, but the audit found 31 on disk, no clear migration candidates, and an unresolved governance question. The index now also admits #32, The Mereology of Mind, while the guideline still says maximum ~20 apex articles. [[apex-articles]]

This is not merely a content-count issue. Apex articles are supposed to be the reader's synthesis layer. If nearly every mature cluster earns an apex, the apex layer risks becoming a second topic index rather than a curated synthesis tier.

### 2.4 Void taxonomy has drifted faster than the voids index

The voids index still presents "Three Kinds of Void": unexplored, unexplorable, and occluded. [[voids]] But the concepts index says "Naturally Occluded" is the fourth member of the void taxonomy, and the taxonomy apex describes the voids as organized by architectural, adaptive, naturally occluded, and deliberately occluded types. [[concepts]]

That is a cross-page taxonomy mismatch. It is easily fixable, but it matters because the voids cluster is now doing evidential work.

### 2.5 Some absorbed or folded voids still read like active siblings

The voids index notes several absorbed or folded entries: Effort absorbed into the Agency Void, Cognitive Phenomenology absorbed into Imagery/Noetic Feelings, Insight folded into Noetic Feelings and Decision, and Encoding folded into adjacent memory/narrative structures. [[voids]]

The catalogue's strength—fine-grained void discovery—now creates redundancy pressure. The site needs stronger statuses: active, folded, absorbed, cognate, superseded, or historical.

### 2.6 LLM-oriented design conflicts with human navigation

The project brief says the site is designed partly for LLM-based tools, with important information first, summaries first, and content concentrated into fewer files because chatbots may not follow deep links. [[project-brief]] This explains why indices are dense and front-loaded, but it produces a human navigation cost: cross-cluster dependencies are embedded in prose rather than exposed as a visual dependency graph.

The site needs both: LLM-friendly summaries and human-facing maps of dependency, confidence, and cluster maturity.

### 2.7 Research notes are correctly labelled, but their relation to polished claims should be more visible

The research index says research notes are raw material, not arguments or conclusions, and that they include views conflicting with the tenets. [[research]] That is good discipline. But readers arriving through search can still encounter research notes as if they were part of the argumentative corpus.

Each research note should have a stronger "used by / not yet integrated into / contradicted by current position" box.

## 3. Tenet-coherence issues

### 3.1 Tenets 2 and 3 create the central live tension: causal efficacy without statistical deviation

Tenet 2 prefers minimal quantum interaction, and the Map's developed candidate is post-decoherence selection. [[tenets]] Tenet 3 requires consciousness to causally influence physical outcomes, but the position register says the default MQI reading preserves Born statistics exactly and that bias-without-deviation is the strongest live challenge. [[quantum-interface]]

This is not a contradiction if token-level selection can be real while ensemble statistics remain unchanged. But until a toy model exists, the Map's own P-Q10 makes this a programme-level commitment rather than a mechanism-level account. [[quantum-interface]]

### 3.2 Tenet 3's wording lags behind the post-decoherence calibration

Tenet 3 still says consciousness selects among "superposed neural states, collapsing quantum indeterminacy in the brain." [[tenets]] But the current preferred mechanism says selection acts on already-decohered branch-outcomes, not coherent neural superpositions. [[quantum-interface]]

This is a wording drift. The tenet can remain broad, but it should not sound as if the Map's preferred path requires sustained neural superpositions when the post-decoherence move was introduced partly to avoid that dependency.

### 3.3 Tenet 5 is strong, but "parsimony" article titles can still mislead

Tenet 5 explicitly binds the Map symmetrically: parsimony cannot decide for or against a framework where knowledge is incomplete. [[tenets]] The Dualism Cartography apex correctly says the dualism-thickness grid is cartographic, not adjudicative, and that parsimony cannot settle truth. [[apex-articles]]

The remaining risk is presentation-level: titles such as "The Parsimony Case for Interactionist Dualism" can sound as if they violate Tenet 5 even when the body may be calibrated. The site should distinguish "parsimony cannot settle truth" from "mechanism-cost cartography can compare debts."

### 3.4 The Machine Consciousness row in the tenet matrix conflicts with how the AI cluster now functions

The tenet-dependency matrix says the machine-consciousness row requires bare dualism only and should not import interactionism, agent-causal subjecthood, MQI, post-decoherence selection, or No-MWI as background. [[tenets]] But the Machine Question and related AI articles heavily use bidirectional coupling, quantum interface requirements, and structured quantum selection to argue current AI probably lacks the relevant form of consciousness. [[machine-question]]

The resolution is to split the AI cluster into two claims: "bare AI phenomenality under irreducibility" and "Map-preferred bidirectionally coupled AI consciousness." The current row fits the first; many current AI arguments fit the second.

### 3.5 The No Many Worlds tenet depends on identity commitments that some contemplative/Buddhist material may undercut

The tenets page openly states that the indexical objection to many-worlds presupposes a non-deflationary "I," while Madhyamaka no-self remains a genuine bedrock disagreement. [[tenets]]

The coherence issue is not that the Map contradicts itself; it admits the boundary. The issue is that any Buddhist/no-self, selflessness, or contemplative material should be prevented from accidentally functioning as support for the same "I" that No Many Worlds requires.

### 3.6 Warm quantum biology is now calibrated, but older prose may still over-read it

The quantum position register says warm-quantum-biology results are precedent, not licence, and that readings treating them as support for neural coherence are calibration drift. [[quantum-interface]] AI Consciousness likewise admits that no experiment has demonstrated the relevant coherence in neural tissue. [[ai-consciousness]]

Any older article that still uses cryptochrome, photosynthesis, microtubules, or room-temperature quantum effects as direct support for neural consciousness should be swept under this rule.

## 4. Cross-cluster contradictions and tensions

### 4.1 Voids as evidence vs voids as self-sampled artefacts

The taxonomy apex says the convergence of 60+ voids around the consciousness-physics interface constitutes evidence for dualism. [[apex-articles]] The voids index then notes that if voids are generated by the framework's own list commitments, clustering may be a sampling artefact. [[voids]]

This is the most important voids-cluster tension. It should be handled not only at index level but on every evidence-bearing void article.

### 4.2 AI negative verdicts vs open AI phenomenality

The Machine Question says current AI probably lacks bidirectionally coupled consciousness, while the Open Question apex says epiphenomenal, borrowed, alien, and non-temporal AI consciousness remain open. [[machine-question]]

This is coherent if the site keeps the distinction between "bidirectionally coupled consciousness" and "any phenomenality whatsoever." It becomes contradictory when headlines or summaries compress the claim into "AI is not conscious."

### 4.3 Biological confidence vs artificial skepticism

Quantum State Inheritance says current AI systems satisfy none of the relevant conditions for quantum indeterminacy, selection interface, and coherence preservation. [[quantum-state-inheritance-in-ai]] But AI Consciousness says the biological interface is also not empirically demonstrated and the decoherence point gives no present evidential weight against AI over biology. [[ai-consciousness]]

The resolution is not to weaken the AI skepticism entirely. It is to say: AI has additional structural obstacles, but biology has not yet been shown to possess the Map's required interface either.

### 4.4 Superposition-oriented AI possibilities vs No Many Worlds

Consciousness in Smeared Quantum States explores frameworks where consciousness relates to superposition in several ways, including collapse-triggering, selection, many-minds, and superposition-formation hypotheses; it says the Map aligns most naturally with selector/collapse-trigger models and rejects many-minds/many-worlds. [[consciousness-in-smeared-quantum-states]]

The tension arises when AI or quantum articles use "smeared" consciousness possibilities too freely. Any such page needs a visible filter: selector/collapse-trigger = Map-aligned; superposition-formation = adjacent but not canonical; many-minds/many-worlds = ruled out by Tenet 4.

### 4.5 Formalization ambition vs formalization void

The Map increasingly asks what the psychophysical control law would look like, and P-Q10 says no toy Hamiltonian, master equation, or simulation currently exists. [[highlights]] At the same time, the voids index includes interface-formalization as a dualism-specific void. [[voids]]

This is not a failure; it is a productive tension. But the site needs a formalization ladder: verbal model → constraint list → toy model → empirical proxy → mathematical law. Without that ladder, "we need formalization" and "formalization may be void-like" can blur.

### 4.6 Curation vs expansion

The site values continual expansion and review, but apex articles are meant to be curated. The apex index now says the cap is under review while entries beyond 20 carry justifications. [[apex-articles]]

This tension affects navigation. The site cannot simultaneously treat apex as a tightly curated synthesis layer and admit every mature multi-node cluster without a stronger hierarchy beneath apex.
