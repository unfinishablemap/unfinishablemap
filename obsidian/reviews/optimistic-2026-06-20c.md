## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The cluster repeatedly localises the hard problem in the temporal domain rather than gesturing at it. `phenomenology-of-memory-and-the-self`'s "pastness quale" section ("What no such account has yet *derived* is why mechanism-output is experienced *as* re-encountering one's own past rather than merely activating stored information. The pastness quale is the hard problem manifesting in the temporal domain") is exactly the explanatory-gap move Chalmers would applaud — and it is staged carefully *after* an explicit disclaimer that phenomenological data are not by themselves metaphysically loaded.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
`consciousness-and-memory`'s reconsolidation section and `memory-channel-interface-evidence`'s MQI subsection locate a concrete candidate site for minimal interaction: the reconsolidation window where "multiple restabilisation outcomes are physically possible" and consciousness "may bias which reconstruction becomes actual." This is the physics-consciousness interface treated with a specific mechanism (selection among genuinely open outcomes) rather than hand-waving.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The "double presence" analysis (self appears twice — present rememberer and past protagonist) centres first-person structure without flinching, and `memory-anomalies` extends the Nagelian move to the margins: déjà vu delivers "pastness without a past," jamais vu "alienness of the familiar." The what-it-is-like question drives the whole cluster.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
`consciousness-and-memory`'s opening thesis — memory as "consciousness operating across time" rather than a filing system, with consolidation as a phenomenological *trajectory* — is exactly the anti-substance, event-centred framing Whitehead would endorse. Crucially, the cluster does NOT use this resonance to upgrade any evidential tier (see Hardline Empiricist below) — the process-friendly framing stays on the metaphysics-of-time axis, not the evidence axis.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
The manipulation-maintenance asymmetry and the choking phenomenon ("conscious attention can degrade automatic performance") give Kane a clean exhibit of consciousness doing causal work — and the reconsolidation cycle ("the self is perpetually rewriting its own history") frames diachronic agency as something consciousness actively does, not merely undergoes.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
The cluster is unusually disciplined about its own limits: the Proustian boundary ("Whether such moments genuinely recover past qualia or generate exceptionally vivid simulations is unresolvable") and the `inaccessible-past` framing both name genuine epistemic limits rather than papering over them. `memory-anomalies` explicitly disowns paranormal over-extensions.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This cluster is a model of the restraint Birch praises, and the praise is **load-bearing** here because the cluster repeatedly had the structural opportunity to slide claims up the tier scale on tenet-load and explicitly declined:

- `memory-channel-interface-evidence` states its central pattern is "**accommodation evidence**, not predictive success at single-event resolution" and holds the line at three named points "where it is structurally available to slide." It correctly flags terminal lucidity as "a **live hypothesis** the evidence does not decide" and concedes its strongest single case "rests on the weakest evidence base." This is *tenet-as-evidence-upgrade praise-worthily NOT done*.
- `memory-anomalies` is even sharper: it explicitly registers that the cluster's cumulative-convergence discharge "reaches the strongly-supported tier **only conditional on** the common-cause-null surviving the discrimination tests," and concedes the article "is therefore not an independent observer of the convergence; it is part of what the convergence counts." This is rare honesty — naming a self-counting risk in one's own cumulative case.
- `phenomenology-of-memory-and-the-self` separates the phenomenological datum (pastness quale) from the metaphysical inference at every turn ("Treating phenomenological data as already metaphysically loaded is the slippage `evidential-status-discipline` catches"), runs a "Hostile-Review Steelman Counters" section, and concedes "the dialectical situation is honestly transitional."

The Process Philosopher and Hardline Empiricist personas **converge** here rather than conflict: the process-friendly framing of memory-as-temporal-consciousness sits on the metaphysics axis while the evidential tiers are policed independently. That convergence is the signature of an honestly-calibrated cluster.
