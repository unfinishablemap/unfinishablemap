### Counterarguments Considered

- **Empiricist (Popper)**: Adding three more voids to an already large catalogue risks the classic proliferation objection — the framework grows to absorb any new gap. *Response*: each of the three new voids sits in a distinct position (phenomenological richness, formal second-order ignorance, categorial shape) and each has a specific failure signature. The taxonomy's claim to track real structure rests on this specificity, which is preserved rather than diluted.

- **Hard-Nosed Physicalist (Dennett)**: The plenitude void in particular may be a re-labelling of the Block–Dennett debate with added apex-level framing. *Response*: the apex treats plenitude as a void precisely because the debate has not converged, and each side can accommodate the evidence with appropriate auxiliary assumptions. The void consists in this structural indeterminacy, not in the substantive question. The apex inherits this framing by cross-link rather than re-litigating it.

- **Quantum Skeptic (Tegmark)**: The epistemic horizon void, as used in the apex, is dangerously close to unfalsifiable — any failure to complete the catalogue could be attributed to it. *Response*: the epistemic-horizon-void article explicitly distinguishes the claim from global scepticism and preserves first-order knowledge; the apex inherits this constraint by cross-link. The claim is structural (about the estimate procedure, not about whether knowledge is possible), not a blanket denial.

- **Many-Worlds Defender (Deutsch)**: The categorial void appeals to Kantian transcendental machinery that a more naturalistic epistemology would resist. *Response*: acknowledged as bedrock philosophical disagreement; the categorial-void article explicitly weakens Kant's claim (apparatus is finite rather than determinate and universal), which is the most defensible form of the thesis.

- **Buddhist Philosopher (Nagarjuna)**: Adding "presence-type" (plenitude, overflow) and "categorial" voids proliferates categories that dependent co-arising would dissolve. *Response*: the taxonomy has always been a provisional human-cognitive instrument; the new voids are added under that provisionality, not as ontological commitments. This is a bedrock disagreement from the 2026-04-21 review.
