# Pessimistic Review

**Date**: 2026-07-11
**Content reviewed**: `concepts/phenomenal-sorites-problem.md` (freshly created this session), read against `concepts/composition-and-consciousness.md` and `concepts/interface-threshold.md`

Per the review brief, this pass deliberately avoids duplicating the cross-review already queued for the sorites article (citation checks, standalone argument audit) and instead targets the *cross-article* seams a single-document review cannot see: the way the sorites article borrows sharpness from, and lends its resolution to, two neighbouring articles whose own doctrines do not underwrite the loan.

## Executive Summary

The phenomenal-sorites article is careful, well-hedged, and self-aware about its own load-bearing step. Its weaknesses are relational, not internal. Its constructive move points the reader at `[[interface-threshold]]` as "the simple, non-graded coupling the Map uses to secure a sharp subject-fact" — but the interface-threshold article is explicitly about a *different* threshold (selection-grade efficacy), one below which it says phenomenal consciousness is present, and one it frames as a phase transition inside a graded evolutionary continuum. That is close to the opposite of what the sorites article recruits it for. A second, subtler tension: the sorites article's own Antony-conditional resolution (sharpness cannot be grounded in a *complex/graded* property) actively undercuts the integrated-information (Φ) and organicist criteria that `composition-and-consciousness` presents as the convergence evidence for dualism — yet the sorites article names the "inverse move" without discharging that debt.

## Critical Issues

### Issue 1: The sorites article recruits `interface-threshold` for a job that article explicitly declines
- **Files**: `concepts/phenomenal-sorites-problem.md` (para at line 71 + Further Reading line 77); `concepts/interface-threshold.md` (lines 50, 68, 70)
- **Problem**: The sorites article's positive proposal is to "locate [the sharp on/off subject-fact] in a *simple*, non-graded interface-threshold relation, so that crossing the coupling is not itself a sorites series," and its Further Reading glosses `[[interface-threshold]]` as "the simple, non-graded coupling the Map uses to secure a sharp subject-fact." But the interface-threshold article is unambiguous that its threshold is **not** the subject-existence boundary. It says (line 68) that below the threshold "phenomenal consciousness may well be present — possibly across many species," and (line 70) that "the question of *whether* a system is conscious is separable from the question of *what conscious access to that system can do*." Its threshold is the transition from a *receptive* coupling to a *selection-grade* one — an efficacy/effectiveness boundary with consciousness on **both** sides. It is therefore constitutionally unable to fix whether a subject exists, which is exactly the fact the sorites article asks it to secure. Worse, interface-threshold describes itself (line 50) as "a region in cognitive evolution" and a "phase transition" — i.e. a location within a graded evolutionary series — which is the very sorites-susceptible structure the sorites article claims the coupling escapes.
- **Severity**: High
- **Note on self-inconsistency inside the sorites article**: The slippage is visible without even opening interface-threshold. Line 27 correctly sets interface-threshold *aside* as "a *mechanism* claim about where the consciousness–physical coupling engages; the sorites question is metaphysical, not mechanistic." Line 71 then re-imports that same mechanistic construct to do the metaphysical work of fixing the on/off fact. The article both distances itself from and leans on the identical construct.
- **Aggravating factor (session-introduced)**: This is not a pre-existing mismatch the new article merely inherited. The interface-threshold article was edited this same session (`ai_modified: 2026-07-11T00:50:00+00:00`) to add a reciprocal Further Reading line — "[[phenomenal-sorites-problem]] — Why the on/off consciousness-fact needs a simple, non-graded coupling to resist ontic vagueness" (line 128). That new back-link hard-wires the conflation into both articles: it asserts the interface threshold *is* the on/off-consciousness-fact coupling, contradicting its own body text three paragraphs later.
- **Recommendation (for human triage / a future `refine-draft`, not created here)**: Either (a) disambiguate — introduce/point at a distinct construct for "the coupling *engaging at all*" (subject-existence) as opposed to "the coupling becoming *selection-grade*" (efficacy), and stop borrowing the `interface-threshold` name for the former; or (b) if the Map genuinely intends one construct to do both jobs, that identity claim needs to be argued in one place, because as written the two articles assert incompatible things about what lies below the threshold (no subject vs. a present-but-ineffective subject). The reciprocal back-link added this session should be corrected in whichever direction the disambiguation goes.

### Issue 2: The Antony-conditional resolution strands the composition criterion it is supposed to protect
- **Files**: `concepts/phenomenal-sorites-problem.md` (lines 69–71); `concepts/composition-and-consciousness.md` (lines 55, 59, 61)
- **Problem**: The sorites article correctly reads `composition-and-consciousness` as running "the *inverse* move, using consciousness as a non-arbitrary criterion that answers the vagueness of mereological composition" (line 27), and it invokes Antony's conditional to constrain how the Map may buy sharpness: "Because sharpness-plus-correctness sinks *complex-property* theories — complex-property dualism included — the Map cannot ground the on/off fact in a graded interface" (line 70). But `composition-and-consciousness` grounds its headline convergence in exactly such complex, graded properties: McQueen & Tsuchiya's criterion is that "composition occur[s] where integrated information (Φ) is maximised" (line 61) — Φ is a continuous magnitude — and Van Inwagen's organicism turns on "the activity of simples constitut[ing] a life," itself a paradigm sorites predicate. So the sorites article's own resolution implies that the composition article's leading criteria (Φ, organic life) **cannot** be where the sharp consciousness-fact lives. The non-arbitrariness that `composition-and-consciousness` sells as consciousness's advantage over contact/fastening/cohesion (all rejected there precisely for "fac[ing] a sorites problem," line 55) is therefore not delivered by the graded proxies that article actually cites — and `composition-and-consciousness` further concedes McQueen & Tsuchiya "explicitly decouple that composition criterion from consciousness itself" (line 61), severing the last link between the sharp on/off fact and the graded composition criterion.
- **Severity**: Medium-High
- **Why a cross-review misses it**: The sorites article *names* the inverse relationship and moves on, presenting it as a tidy division of labour ("this article stays on the one question the others leave open"). It never notices that its preferred fix (sharpness from a *simple non-graded* coupling, never from a complex property) is in direct tension with the composition article's evidential base (convergence of *complex-property* theories). Reading either article alone, the tension is invisible; it only appears when the Antony-conditional in one is held against the Φ/organicism criteria in the other.
- **Recommendation (for human triage)**: A `refine-draft` on `composition-and-consciousness` (or a short bridging paragraph in the sorites article) should address whether the "non-arbitrary boundary" consciousness supplies is the *sharp on/off subject-fact* (secured, per the sorites article, by a non-physical coupling) or the *graded Φ/organic-life* proxy — and, if the latter, whether the composition argument still escapes the sorites problem it uses to reject contact/fastening.

## Counterarguments to Address

### The coupling's immunity to sorites is asserted, not argued (self-flagged, noted for completeness)
- **Current content says**: "it denies that continuity in the physical substrate transmits to the non-physical fact about whether a subject is present" (line 71).
- **A critic would argue**: If the non-physical coupling engages *in response to* some physical condition, and every physical condition is graded, the on/off of the coupling inherits the sorites via its trigger. Schwitzgebel's own charge against option (c) — that any sharp line across a smooth physical continuum is "metaphysically arbitrary" — re-applies to the coupling's switch-on point. The bare denial of boundary-supervenience is the whole load-bearing step.
- **Assessment**: The article does hedge this honestly ("Whether that move fully succeeds remains open," line 71), so this is not overclaiming. It is flagged only because it is the single point on which the entire Tenet-4 defence rests, and the article gives it one sentence. A future expansion could say *why* a non-physical coupling's engagement condition would be non-graded where physical ones are not — otherwise the resolution relocates the sorites rather than dissolving it.

### The no-self dissolution is never engaged (Nagarjuna persona)
- **Current content says**: The article takes for granted throughout that there is a determinate "I" whose presence might or might not be vague, and defends its sharpness via Tenet 4.
- **A critic would argue**: The Madhyamaka reply is that the "sharp subject-fact" the Map is straining to protect is itself the reification error — the self is conventionally real and ultimately empty, so the question "is there a determinate fact whether a subject exists?" is malformed, and the sorites dissolves rather than being answered. The article engages the *ontic/semantic/epistemic* trilemma exhaustively but never the *no-such-relatum* option.
- **Suggested response**: A sentence acknowledging the eliminativist-about-self / emptiness option as a fourth stance orthogonal to the three loci would close the gap. It need not be conceded — only named, since the Map's Tenet-4 framing presupposes exactly what that tradition denies.
