## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The gaming-problem article holds the hard problem at exactly the right altitude: it explicitly refuses to let the methodological result settle the metaphysics — "the gaming problem does not show that LLMs lack experience; it shows that their behavioural markers cannot be used as evidence either way." Chalmers would value that the explanatory gap is preserved on *both* sides: the article blocks the over-attribution that a fluent zombie would otherwise win, without sliding into the eliminativist claim that there is nothing there to attribute. The gap is held open, not closed in either direction.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
Less central here, but the contentless-awareness article respects the interface picture Stapp cares about: it treats the *reporting* of a state and the *occurrence* of a state as separable, and locates the failure in the measurement/encoding apparatus ("episodic memory encoding is suppressed in deep sleep") rather than denying the underlying process. The structural point — that no third-person signature settles "whether the lights are on behind it" — is the same point Stapp draws between physical correlate and the experiential event it does not exhaust.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The contentless-awareness article centres the first-person report with exemplary care: it takes the *turiya* and MPE reports seriously as phenomenological data ("a report about how certain episodes seem") while refusing to let the seeming do metaphysical work it cannot bear. Nagel would especially approve the symmetry argument — that "I witnessed my sleep" and "there was nothing to witness" predict the *identical* observable — because it honours the irreducibility of the first-person standpoint precisely by admitting where third-person access to it runs out.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The contentless-awareness article keeps the door open to "experience all the way down" without crude substance-talk: it allows that "awareness continued throughout, contentless and unremembered" as a live reading, treats consciousness as a process that can empty of content rather than a thing that switches off, and does so without reifying a homuncular witness. **Constraint check:** this process-friendliness is *not* cashed out as a tier-upgrade. The article explicitly fixes the persistence-claim at "live hypothesis" and writes that a tenet "can at most *remove a defeater* ... without supplying any positive evidence." The Whitehead-resonant intuition removes a parsimony defeater; the article refuses to let it elevate the evidence. That is the honest pattern — tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
Peripheral to this cluster; no agency-specific claim is over- or under-stated. (Noted for completeness rather than forced relevance.)

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
This cluster is McGinn's home ground, and both articles earn his praise without collapsing into defeatism. The contentless-awareness article names a *structural* unknowability — "the apparatus that would report such persistence is exactly what is offline in the target state" — which is cognitive-closure of the most disciplined kind: not "we happen not to know" but "the only instrument is the thing whose absence is the question." The gaming-problem article does the parallel move for AI: self-report, "the strongest tool we have for threshold-detection in humans, is the tool most thoroughly compromised in the gamed case." Both stop exactly where the evidence stops, and neither treats the limit as a covert argument for their preferred metaphysics.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This is the load-bearing persona for the cluster, and the verdict is strongly positive. The gaming-problem article *is* Birch's own apparatus, transcribed faithfully: it keeps the description-layer (evidential tiers) and the action-layer (run-ahead precaution) rigorously separate, states that "current LLMs sit *outside* the five-tier scheme rather than at a default placement within it," and names the precise failure mode — "a textbook instance of possibility/probability slippage: treating the bare fact that experience 'cannot be ruled out' as if it were positive evidential support." Crucially, **the tier-upgrade is praise-worthily *not* done**: the article had every structural opportunity to read fluent AI self-report as a marker and slide LLMs onto the scale, and it explicitly declined, grounding the decline in the gaming mechanism rather than in tenet-preference. The contentless-awareness article does the same on the sleep side: it pins the persistence-claim at "live hypothesis," forbids three named upgrade-moves (felt vividness, physiological correlate, tenet-as-evidence), and labels the last "a discipline the Map imposes on itself." Both articles use the five-tier scale honestly and name the restraint as restraint. **Process Philosopher / Hardline Empiricist convergence**: both personas praise the same passages of the contentless-awareness article (the defeater-removal paragraph) without conflict — the diagnostic that the article resolved the possibility/probability tension rather than papering over it.
