## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
Chalmers would find the hard-problem prong of the self-binding reply exactly on target. The new article identifies the precise juncture where predictive self-binding "helps itself to a move predictive processing has never paid for: the assumption that a sufficiently structured Bayesian model is *phenomenally felt*." This is the explanatory gap re-stated in predictive-processing register, and the article refuses to let it be smuggled past as solved: "The self-binding account does not *win* on the hard problem; it postpones it while presenting the postponement as a solution." The companion `predictive-processing-and-dualism` makes the same move against the beautiful-loop theory with the flight-simulator image — "adding that closed loop does not make the simulator a pilot" — which is Chalmers' zombie intuition in a single concrete picture. The honest reciprocal concession (filter theory "inherits a parallel debt… both inherit it") is precisely the symmetry Chalmers insists physicalists and dualists alike must face.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
Stapp's interface is given a careful, hedged home in `predictive-processing-and-dualism`'s precision-weighting section: "PP describes *what* precision weighting does; the Map proposes *who* does the weighting." The selection-only reading — consciousness "adds no energy and writes no value the physics forbids, only biasing which physically available precision assignment is realised" — is the minimal-interaction discipline Stapp's participating-observer role needs to stay respectable. The Gunji-Shinohara-Basios quantum-Bayesian bridge is flagged as "early-stage and speculative… not… established science," which is exactly the calibration Stapp's program benefits from rather than the overreach that discredits it.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
Nagel would applaud the persistent-minimal-self prong as the article's most genuinely Nagelian seam. The distinction between the narrative self ("the autobiographical character with a name, a history") and the minimal self ("the bare experiential presence to which any content is given") keeps the first-person question — is there still something it is like to undergo dissolution? — in view where the formalism would dissolve it. The article's honesty about the methodological trap is striking: "the very faculty that would report on residual selfhood is among the structures perturbed," and so "first-person reports underdetermine the choice." That is Nagel's "what is it like" question taken seriously enough to admit where it cannot currently be answered.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
Whitehead would warm to the way both articles avoid crude substance dualism. `predictive-processing-and-dualism` treats the computational description as "the physical side of a two-sided reality" rather than positing a ghost behind the machinery, and the new article frames the disagreement with Letheby as turning on "whether a structured Bayesian model is phenomenally felt" — a question about the relation between process and experience, not about an extra substance. Note (evidential-status discipline): this Whitehead-friendly framing is correctly *not* used to upgrade any evidential claim. The new article explicitly registers its persistent-minimal-self prediction as "defeasible" and "a genuine empirical seam, not a foregone conclusion," and concedes the minimal-self commitment "is motivated on independent grounds, not read off the psychedelic data." Process-philosophical resonance removes a defeater; the article declines to let it do evidential work. That is the honest pattern, and both personas below can endorse it.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
Kane would value `predictive-processing-and-dualism`'s active-inference section, which locates genuine agency at exactly the seam he cares about: "something outside the predictive loop to break symmetries and select among options." The article does not pretend the formalism settles this — it states the compatibilist rejoinder fairly ("computational selection… *is* genuine agency without a non-physical selector") before replying that compatibilism "explains agency-*talk* but not the phenomenology of deciding: the felt difference between choosing and being moved." Presenting the rival in its own terms before answering is the dialectical discipline Kane's own work models.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
McGinn would recognise his epistemic humility throughout. The new article's empirical-equivalence section concedes outright that "on psychedelic data *alone*, predictive self-binding is not inferior to the filter reading," and that the accounts "diverge only on questions the neuroimaging cannot adjudicate." `predictive-processing-and-dualism` closes its beautiful-loop engagement on the same note: "Neither side refutes the other from inside its own commitments." Naming the boundary where current cognitive science cannot decide — rather than claiming a decision — is McGinn's intellectual humility in practice.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
**This persona's verdict is load-bearing here, and it is strongly positive.** Both articles do the thing tenet-as-evidence-upgrade is praise-worthily *not* done. The new article had every structural opportunity to slide its claims up the evidential scale on tenet-load — it touches self-fictionalism, the minimal self, and altered-state phenomenology, all tenet-adjacent — and explicitly declined at every turn:

- The empirical-equivalence concession is named *plainly as evidential discipline*: "Saying so plainly is part of the Map's evidential discipline, not a retreat from it."
- The hard-problem prong refuses the asymmetric framing: rather than claiming the dualist reading wins, it states "neither account discharges the hard problem; both inherit it," and turns the symmetry into a Tenet-5 point about parsimony rather than a tier-upgrade.
- The Comforting-Delusion reversal is explicitly held to its *weaker, more secure* form: "The Map does not claim the reversal establishes that psychedelic insights *are* veridical. It claims something weaker and more secure" — that the CDO "runs only on naturalist premises." This is the tenet-removes-a-defeater-but-does-not-upgrade-evidential-status move named in the cleanest possible way.
- The persistent-minimal-self prediction carries a load-bearing "registers its prediction as defeasible," and the Deane and Sjöstedt-Hughes citations are scrupulously fenced as *internal-to-physicalism disagreements* with Letheby, "not as support for dualism" — a model of keeping evidential registers separate.

`predictive-processing-and-dualism` shows the same restraint: the quantum-Bayesian bridge "remains a 'one possible mechanism' proposal, not a commitment," and the Born-rule link is "compatible with the interface reading without yet being adjudicated in its favour."
