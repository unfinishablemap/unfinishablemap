## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The article keeps the phenomenal claim and the selection-mechanism claim rigorously distinct: "That a rat feels pain (a phenomenal claim) and that a rat *selects outcomes because of* anticipated valence (a selection-mechanism claim) are different." That is exactly the explanatory-gap-respecting move Chalmers would want — it refuses to let a "what it's like" attribution smuggle in a functional-role conclusion, and it names the gap itself as the in-principle barrier to verifying the *absence* of valenced selection from third-person data.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
The framework's interface (attention-as-observation-rate, synaptic exocytosis bias) is treated as a *presupposition that can fail*: the slime mould "has none of the neural machinery the interface framework's mechanisms presuppose." Rather than hand-wave the interface down to a brainless cell, the article flags that *Physarum* "strains the framework past any specified interface." That is the responsible Stappian move — locate where the physics-consciousness coupling actually has a substrate and decline to assert it where none is specified.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The lower boundary is correctly framed as partly unknowable from the outside: distinguishing value-sensitive from value-blind selection "that produces identical behaviour may be underdetermined by any behavioural evidence." The first-person/third-person asymmetry is load-bearing in the argument, not decorative — the boundary is "not merely unmeasured but, at its lower reaches, structurally resistant to measurement."

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The article is process-friendly without cashing that friendliness as a tier-upgrade. It acknowledges that "process-philosophical resonance about purpose in nature" is *congenial* to extending selection far down — and then explicitly declines to let that congeniality do evidential work ("[[evidential-status-discipline]] forbids converting tenet-coherence, or process-philosophical resonance … into an evidential upgrade"). This is precisely the honest pattern: panpsychic resonance removes a defeater; it is praiseworthy at any tier and is *not* deployed as the engine of tier-upgrade.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
The "anticipation gap" framing — the value-sensitive horn needs "anticipatory valence … *before* an outcome is actualised" — keeps genuine forward-looking agency, not mere reactivity, as the discriminating property. Reactive nociception (stimulus → ASH → reversal) is explicitly *not* counted as evaluative selection. That protects the substantive notion of agency from being trivialised by reflex.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
Strong epistemic humility throughout: "the boundary cannot currently be located, and the Map's tenets do not help locate it." Two limits are named as *in-principle, not instrumental*. The article treats the lower reach as a genuine void — McGinn's cognitive-closure intuition is honoured without overreaching into "therefore dualism wins."

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This is the load-bearing verdict, and it is the article's defining strength. Every rung is held at the correct tier: rat = *live hypothesis* ("Plausibility is not confirmation"), nematode = *speculative*, *Hydra* = *speculative integration*, *Physarum* = value-blind reading favoured. The five-tier scale is used honestly and explicitly. Most importantly, the article performs the praise-worthy *non*-move: it had every structural opportunity to slide these boundary cases **up** the scale on tenet-load (dualism makes consciousness substrate-flexible; the universal-coupling-response denies a principled threshold) and explicitly declines — "removing a defeater is not the same as supplying positive evidence … A boundary case that is merely *not excluded* stays at live hypothesis; it does not climb to realistic possibility on the strength of the framework finding it congenial." It even notes the guardrail "runs in both directions," catching the *Physarum* over-attribution temptation, not just the under-attribution one. The New York Declaration / Sneddon framing is calibrated to welfare-precaution language ("treated as a realistic possibility for welfare purposes") rather than overclaiming a metaphysical result. This is the named pattern *"tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating"* executed cleanly.
