### Critical Issue Found and Fixed — source-fidelity (§2.5 attribution accuracy)

**Mischaracterization of the authors' defined term "epistemic depth"** (the precise risk the 2026-06-22 outer review flagged: confirm the reply "does not put words in the authors' mouths"). The refine glossed epistemic depth as **higher-order self-modelling** — "*epistemic depth*: the system models its own modelling — its inference includes a representation that it is itself the inferring system" — and built the reply on that reading ("The authors move from 'the system models itself as experiencing' to 'the system experiences'"). But Laukkonen, Friston & Chandaria define epistemic depth as **"the recurrent sharing of the Bayesian beliefs throughout the system,"** such that "the world model contains the knowledge that it exists," and they are explicit that this **"is distinct from self-consciousness"** and that "rudimentary forms of epistemic depth involve minimal meta-inference... absent any richer conceptual or introspective dimension" — i.e. consciousness "precedes introspection or complex metacognition." Casting it as higher-order self-representation overstates the authors' claim. Verified against the abstract and the authors' own descriptions (ScienceDirect abstract; Asterisk Magazine interview-piece; multiple consistent secondary sources — the paywalled PDFs returned 403, but the abstract + author descriptions are unambiguous and mutually corroborating).
