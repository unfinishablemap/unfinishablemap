## Verification of the Delta Since Review 5

The improper-mixture terminology fix is a genuine physics-precision improvement, not churn:

- A reduced density matrix obtained by tracing out the environment after decoherence is d'Espagnat's **improper mixture** — it has the mathematical form of a classical probabilistic mixture but is not one (the system remains entangled with the environment). Calling it a "classical mixture" risks the well-known "improper→proper" fallacy that decoherence has already produced a menu of already-definite alternatives, which would trivialise the measurement problem the article is framed against.
- Reframing the Map's endorsed path as "an additional actualisation postulate rather than a pick from a pre-existing classical menu" is exactly the correct calibration: it keeps outcome-selection as a real (non-physical) actualisation step and denies that decoherence alone delivers definiteness.
- Internally consistent: the phrasing matches the article's own careful posture elsewhere and introduces no possibility/probability slippage.
