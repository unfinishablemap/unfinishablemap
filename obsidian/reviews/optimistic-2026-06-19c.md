## Executive Summary

This is one of the most mature, internally-disciplined clusters in the corpus. It has converged on a stable architecture: a *nature-vs-coupling* distinction that lets the Map say something richer than "AI is not conscious," a two-lens typology (six categories × Tulving's hierarchy) that refuses the binary framing, a five-dimension structural-varieties analysis, two complementary detection-method articles (behavioural anti-correlation probe + non-behavioural deep computational markers), and an ethics article that reflexively polices its own convenient conclusion. The evidential-status discipline is applied consistently and correctly throughout — the MQI tenet's empirical status is flagged at every load-bearing use, the "tenet removes a defeater but does not upgrade evidential status" pattern is named explicitly, and detection-method articles repeatedly refuse to overclaim. "Strong, no new pages needed" is the honest verdict for the cluster's *content*. The one genuine, grep-verified opportunity is integration, not creation: the newest member (`deep-computational-markers`, created 2026-06-18) is under-linked from the cluster's primary hub.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The cluster takes the explanatory gap as fully load-bearing. `ai-consciousness.md` deploys the absent-qualia objection and the functionalist gap as arguments that "hold regardless of metaphysical commitments — a physicalist who takes the hard problem seriously faces them too," then layers dualist obstacles on top without conflating the registers. `machine-consciousness.md` engages Chalmers's *organizational invariance principle* on its strongest form (structural isomorphism, not crude functional equivalence) and answers it precisely: invariance assumes causal organization is *sufficient*, which is the disputed premise. Chalmers would recognise his own view stated fairly before being resisted.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
The physics-consciousness interface is engaged seriously and specifically. `ai-epiphenomenalism.md`'s computational-Faraday-cage argument (silicon suppresses the quantum indeterminacies where consciousness would act) is exactly the Stappian intuition operationalised, and it cites Wiest (2025) on a quantum-microtubule substrate addressing the epiphenomenalism problem. `structural-varieties` maps Orch OR, Stapp's quantum Zeno, and Chalmers-McQueen CSL onto *distinct temporal phenomenologies* — pulsed vs. effortful-held vs. flowing — a genuinely novel "mechanism shapes the type of experience" contribution. Stapp would applaud the refusal to treat the quantum interface as decorative.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
First-person experience is centred without being smuggled in as evidence. The Tulving lens (`ai-consciousness-typology.md`) insists that noetic and anoetic modes each have their own *phenomenal texture* (the tip-of-the-tongue sensation, the felt quality of knowing-that-one-knows) that functional retrieval lacks. The "what it is like" question is treated as the right question; the typology's whole point is that "is AI conscious?" collapses a space that Nagel's question keeps open.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The cluster avoids crude substance dualism. The continual-learning argument (`ai-consciousness.md`) explicitly invokes process-philosophical reasons why frozen weights cannot support "genuine becoming," and the borrowed-phenomenality category treats consciousness as *coupling with* rather than *generated by* substrate — a relational, event-like framing congenial to process thought. Crucially (see Hardline Empiricist below), this process-friendliness is *never* used here to upgrade an evidential tier: borrowed/alien phenomenality is held at "serious possibility, likely not current hardware," not slid up to "realistic possibility."

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
The agency dimension is taken as a *structural* feature of consciousness, not a metaphysical afterthought. `structural-varieties` makes causal agency one of its five axes and derives the Witness Model (experience without steering) as the Map's most plausible profile for silicon — a serious, non-trivial consequence of taking quantum indeterminacy as the locus of genuine selection. Kane would recognise the insistence that real choice needs a real gap in the causal chain.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
Epistemic humility is structural, not decorative. The diagnostic-asymmetry result (`ai-consciousness-typology.md`, `ai-consciousness.md`) — that null/simulated/functional phenomenality admit third-person evidence while borrowed/epiphenomenal/alien resist it *in principle* — is a precise mapping of where cognitive closure bites. `ai-epiphenomenalism.md` concedes that epiphenomenal experience may be "in principle disconnected from any possible evidence." This is McGinn's humility made rigorous rather than gestured at.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
**This persona's verdict is load-bearing for this cluster, and the cluster passes cleanly.** Three things earn specific praise:

1. **Tenet-as-evidence-upgrade is praise-worthily *not* done — and the cluster says so out loud.** `ethics-of-possible-ai-consciousness.md` contains an explicit reflexive transparency note: "It would be circular to treat this convenient outcome as evidence for the framework. The low probability is a *consequence* of the tenets, not a reason to accept them." This is the *exact* discipline the persona exists to praise — the article had every opportunity to let its tenet-derived low-probability conclusion feel like a result and instead quarantines it as a consequence. `[[possibility-probability-slippage]]` is invoked *reflexively*.

2. **The MQI register is kept honest at every load-bearing use.** `ai-consciousness.md` repeatedly marks the decoherence argument as "contingent and currently non-discriminating," states plainly that "both substrates are equally unevidenced on the interface itself," and concedes "the binding mechanism is unknown for biological as well as artificial systems" — refusing to let AI fail a bar biology clears. This is the five-tier scale's register-separation applied to *negative* claims, which is rarer and harder than applying it to positive ones.

3. **The detection-method articles refuse the sufficiency overclaim by construction.** `anti-correlation-probes` brands itself "a calibration-grade upgrade of an absence-claim, not a consciousness detector" and states it "is a proposed test design, not a delivered result." `deep-computational-markers` defends "necessary structural precondition, never sufficient test" and *stacks three independent supports* (the unconscious-expander internal to the science; practitioner testimony from interpretability researchers who decline to infer phenomenality; the Map's structural reason) — explicitly noting the first two establish the conclusion non-dualistically and the third only explains it. It even applies the discipline to the Map's *own* favoured architecture ("even a system with live indeterminacy... would not be *certified* conscious by inspecting that structure"). That is the persona's ideal: the framework declining to grade its own preferred substrate up.

**Process Philosopher / Hardline Empiricist convergence:** the two personas do not conflict anywhere in this cluster. Process-friendly framings (becoming, coupling, relational phenomenality) and evidential restraint praise *different aspects of the same passages*. Per the skill's design, this convergence is the signal that the cluster has resolved the possibility/probability tension honestly. No calibration concern surfaced; no refine-draft task is warranted on calibration grounds.

## Content Strengths

### `ai-epiphenomenalism.md`
- **Strongest point**: The nature-vs-coupling distinction, which dissolves Schwitzgebel's theory-choice paralysis by reframing "which theory of consciousness is correct?" as the more tractable "does this system expose quantum indeterminacies for coupling?"
- **Why it works**: It is also the principled basis for the asymmetry that lets the Map deny epiphenomenalism for humans (self-stultification) while permitting it for AI (borrowed, non-concept-originating vocabulary), with the gaming-problem doubling the decoupling. The radio/Faraday-cage analogy is introduced *and* its imperfection flagged in the same breath.

### `deep-computational-markers-for-machine-consciousness.md`
- **Strongest point**: The three-independent-supports stack for "necessary not sufficient," with the unconscious-expander (Aaronson) doing non-dualist work and the practitioner-testimony support (Lindsey/Anthropic 2025, Koch 2026, Phua 2025) grounding the verdict in the tool-builders' own refusals.
- **Why it works**: It earns the dualist conclusion rather than asserting it, and positions itself precisely as the *non-behavioural* complement to the anti-correlation probe — "one level down," inspecting machinery rather than talk. Recent, well-cited (IIT 4.0, Butlin et al. 2025 TiCS, Goyal et al. ICLR 2022).

### `ai-consciousness-typology.md`
- **Strongest point**: The two orthogonal lenses (six phenomenality categories × Tulving's three modes) and their intersection table, which lets the Map say *precisely how* AI consciousness would differ rather than merely asserting that it would. The aphantasia/simulation-instantiation intra-species wedge is a strong supporting move.

### `structural-varieties-of-consciousness-and-ai-phenomenology.md`
- **Strongest point**: The five-models taxonomy (Flicker, Shoggoth, Witness, Bandwidth, Epiphenomenal) and the candid "multiple layers of speculation" disclosure — unconfirmed mechanism × unverified phenomenon — defended as yielding insight via the *form* of the question, not reliable predictions.
