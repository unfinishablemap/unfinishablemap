### Issue 4: The convergence table treats the framework's own commitment as if it were independent evidential pressure
- **File**: `topics/empirical-evidence-for-consciousness-selecting.md`
- **Location**: §The Convergence, table lines 114–122 and surrounding prose
- **Problem**: The table's logic is: epiphenomenalism fails three lines, classical physicalism "cannot explain measurement," consciousness-selecting is "compatible with all four" — therefore consciousness-selecting is the surviving hypothesis. But "compatible with all four" is a *weaker* property than the table's framing implies. By the article's own line-by-line admission, every one of the first three lines is *equally compatible* with classical physicalism. So consciousness-selecting's apparent advantage rests entirely on the fourth row — "classical physicalism cannot explain measurement" — which is not a fact about *consciousness* at all but the generic measurement problem that burdens every interpretation that isn't MWI or a hidden-variable theory. The table silently converts "consciousness-selecting is one of several collapse-realist options that inherit the measurement problem's opening" into "consciousness-selecting uniquely survives the convergence." This is the *removing-a-defeater-treated-as-upgrading-evidence* pattern: showing classical physicalism has an unsolved measurement problem removes a competitor's comfort but does not supply positive discriminating evidence *for consciousness-selecting over other collapse interpretations* (objective collapse / GRW, Bohmian mechanics, RQM). The prose's continental-drift analogy (line 122) reinforces the over-reading — plate tectonics had *positive* convergent traces (matching coastlines, magnetic striping); the consciousness-selecting table has three "compatible" rows (no discriminating power) and one "competitor has a problem" row (a defeater-removal, not a confirmation).
- **Severity**: Medium (the article's most calibration-disciplined section still lets the table's visual logic out-claim the prose; the honest closing paragraph partly rescues it but the table is the truncation-resilient artifact an LLM reader extracts first)
- **Recommendation**: Add a fourth comparator column or row for *other collapse-realist interpretations* (GRW/objective-collapse, RQM) so the table shows that the measurement-problem row does not discriminate consciousness-selecting from its true rivals — only from classical no-collapse physicalism and MWI. Alternatively, annotate the "Cannot explain measurement" cell to state explicitly that this is a defeater for classical physicalism, not positive support for consciousness-selecting specifically. Flag, do not fix.

## Calibration Audit (the requested review lens)

**Possibility → probability slippage.** Mostly well-controlled. One residual instance: §The Quantum-Biological Line, "Evolution can optimise quantum coherence. If it can do so for navigation and catalysis, the neural case cannot be ruled out a priori" (line 102). "Cannot be ruled out a priori" is a possibility claim; the surrounding convergence framing then leans on it as if it raised the probability of neural quantum function. The avian-magnetoreception and enzyme-catalysis cases establish that quantum effects *can* be biologically functional *somewhere* — they carry no evidential weight toward quantum effects being functional *in neural consciousness specifically*, which the article's own "What the quantum-biological line does not establish" paragraph (line 106) correctly concedes. The two paragraphs are in mild tension: the bullet list reads as probability-raising, the disclaimer reads as possibility-only. The disclaimer is the honest one.

**Constrains vs. establishes.** Generally well-policed — the "What the X line does not establish" subheads are doing real work and are the article's strongest calibration feature. The one place "constrains" quietly inflates toward "establishes" is the Convergence table (Issue 4): line-by-line the article says each line "constrains" without adjudicating, but the table's bottom row visually *establishes* a winner.

**Consistent-with treated as evidence-for.** The article is unusually explicit that each line is "compatible with both readings... does not adjudicate" (lines 65, 85, 110). This is exemplary and should be preserved verbatim during any fix. The single leak is in the Relation-to-Site-Perspective section: "**Bidirectional Interaction**: The evolutionary and neurological lines provide the *strongest evidence* for the downward direction" (line 144). The body established only that these lines are *compatible* with downward causation while equally compatible with non-reductive physicalist mental causation that is not "downward" in the dualist sense. "Strongest evidence for the downward direction" promotes compatibility to directional evidence in the tenet-alignment section — the one section where the calibration discipline visibly relaxes.

**Removing-a-defeater treated as upgrading-evidence.** Present and load-bearing — see Issue 4 (measurement-problem row) and the decoherence-objection-collapse framing at line 89 ("This objection has collapsed"). Refuting Tegmark's femtosecond objection *removes a defeater* for the possibility of neural quantum effects; the article at line 101 ("The categorical objection seems empirically refuted") correctly scopes this, but the momentum of "this objection has collapsed" invites the reader to treat defeater-removal as positive support for the substrate existing in neurons, which line 106 then has to walk back.
