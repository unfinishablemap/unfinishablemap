## Critiques by Philosopher

### The Eliminative Materialist (Churchland)
"Convergent behavioural and neurological evidence grounds the inference" — grounds it in *folk-psychological* vocabulary you have already decided to keep. Every marker the article cites (pain, valence, perception, metacognition) is a folk category that mature neuroscience will redescribe or retire; calibrating markers on "the human case where consciousness and the functional profile travel together" simply imports the folk confound and ships it to the bee. The article's own deflationary-functionalist section concedes exactly this, then declines to follow it to the eliminativist conclusion — it stops at "the disagreement is closer to bedrock" rather than asking whether "phenomenal experience accompanying the functional profile" names anything over and above the profile. That refusal is the unargued residue.

### The Hard-Nosed Physicalist (Dennett)
The article is at its weakest where it most wants to be strong: the section "Why the Inference Is Stronger for Animals Than for AI." Homology gives you a *causal-historical* story for why animals and humans share mechanisms; it does nothing to bridge the mechanism to felt quality that the hard problem allegedly leaves open. If neural homology licensed the consciousness inference for mammals, it would license it for any sufficiently homologous functional system — and "different mechanism" is doing all the work excluding AI, which is precisely the kind of substrate-chauvinism the article elsewhere disavows ("no substrate-specific barrier"). There is an unflagged tension between "no anthropocentric/substrate barrier" (Dualism section) and "homology supplies stronger inference grounds" (anti-AI section): the article wants substrate-neutrality for metaphysics and substrate-privilege for epistemology, and never reconciles them.

### The Quantum Skeptic (Tegmark)
The "Minimal Quantum Interaction" subsection is the article's thinnest moment. It name-checks the microtubule-decoherence dispute (Tegmark 2000 vs Hagan 2002, Reimers/McKemmish 2009) and then evacuates: "The broader animal-consciousness case routes through the evolutionary argument... rather than any specific interface scale." This is a graceful dodge, but it means the quantum tenet contributes *nothing* to the animal case while still being asserted as relevant — if the interface mechanism is bracketed for every organism, the section is decorative. Worse, citing the decoherence dispute without noting that the post-2009 consensus runs *against* biologically relevant microtubule coherence leaves the impression of a live 50/50 controversy that the physics community does not grant.

### The Many-Worlds Defender (Deutsch)
The "No Many Worlds" paragraph asserts "Each animal subject has *this* experience... The haecceity of animal experience is a fact MWI cannot accommodate." This is a bare assertion dressed as a "fact." An Everettian holds the bat's experience is perfectly determinate *within a branch*; "which branch" is no more mysterious for a bat than for a thermometer reading. Calling indexical haecceity "a fact MWI cannot accommodate" smuggles the article's own metaphysics into the premise. This is boundary-marking presented as refutation — it should be honestly downgraded to "this runs counter to the Map's commitments; MWI denies indexical haecceity is a brute fact, and the Map does not refute that denial within MWI's framework."

### The Empiricist (Popper's ghost)
Credit where due: the "What Would Challenge This View?" section is genuinely Popperian in form and even concedes "(3)-(5) are inaccessible by the framework's own commitments... The framework's net falsifiability is genuinely modest." That honesty is exemplary. But it sharpens a problem rather than dissolving it: if three of five potential defeaters are in-principle inaccessible and the two in-practice ones ("deterministic explanation of bee play"; "apes reaching human cognition without consciousness expansion") depend on operational definitions "the field does not share," then the positive thesis is doing no predictive work the deflationary rival could not match. The article admits the disagreement "turns on a discriminator no current method supplies" — which is the definition of underdetermination, and underdetermined theories should not lean on phrases like "Convergent evidence makes animal consciousness more plausible than denial" (closing line) that read as if the evidence asymmetrically favours one side.

### The Buddhist Philosopher (Nagarjuna)
The article invokes me twice — "Buddhist commitment to animal consciousness" and the contemplative-cessation note — as *support*. But it reifies precisely what should be deconstructed: it treats "the bat's phenomenal world," "this creature has subjective experience," and "haecceity of animal experience" as substantial, self-identical things that either are or are not present. The commensurability-void gesture is closer to my view, yet the article's whole evidential apparatus presupposes a determinate fact-of-the-matter about whether experience is "present" in an organism — the same substantialist framing the standing-agnostic challenge keeps tripping over. The Map borrows my conclusion (animals are conscious) while rejecting the analysis (no abiding experiencer) that would dissolve the very verification problem it cannot solve.

## Critical Issues

### Issue 1: New York Declaration signatory count is inflated by ~6x
- **File**: `topics/animal-consciousness.md`
- **Location**: "Growing Scientific Agreement" — "The **New York Declaration (2024)**, signed by over 500 scientists and philosophers..."
- **Problem**: Public sources (NYU declaration site; Brooks Institute Animal Law Digest; Kimmela Center) report the declaration launched April 2024 with ~40 signatories and grew to "more than 80." No source supports "over 500." The figure appears to conflate the declaration's signatories with conference attendance or general field endorsement.
- **Severity**: Medium (factual error in a load-bearing "growing agreement" sentence; cheap to fix, embarrassing if cited).
- **Recommendation**: Change to "signed by more than 80 scientists and philosophers" (or "an initial group of researchers, since grown to over 80"). Verify against the live NYU declaration page before publishing the corrected figure.

### Issue 2: "Stronger for animals than AI" relies on unreconciled substrate-asymmetry
- **File**: `topics/animal-consciousness.md`
- **Location**: "Why the Inference Is Stronger for Animals Than for AI" + "Dualism and Animal Minds: No Anthropocentric Barrier"
- **Problem**: The metaphysics asserts no substrate barrier (mind "interfaces with suitably organised matter wherever that organisation exists"); the epistemology privileges biological homology as inference-grounds against AI. The article never explains why homology should be epistemically privileged once substrate is metaphysically irrelevant — the load is carried by "different mechanism," which is substrate-talk. The two sections are several paragraphs apart and the tension is never named.
- **Severity**: Medium.
- **Recommendation**: Add one or two sentences explicitly reconciling them: homology privileges the *inference* (it supplies a shared-cause explanation unavailable for AI) without privileging the *metaphysics* (organisation, not biology, is what matters; AI is harder to assess, not barred). The article gestures at this with "harder to assess, not barred" elsewhere — make it explicit at the seam.

### Issue 3: "No Many Worlds" paragraph is boundary-marking dressed as refutation
- **File**: `topics/animal-consciousness.md`
- **Location**: "Relation to the Map's Perspective" → "No Many Worlds": "The haecceity of animal experience is a fact MWI cannot accommodate."
- **Problem**: Per the direct-refutation discipline, this is a boundary-substitution in miniature: it states a Map commitment (indexical haecceity is brute) as a "fact" and presents MWI as unable to accommodate it, when MWI simply *denies the premise*. The disagreement is at the level of competing foundational commitments, not an in-framework refutation of MWI.
- **Severity**: Low-Medium (single sentence; tenet-section register, not body prose, so lower stakes — but it is exactly the failure mode the discipline targets).
- **Recommendation**: Downgrade to honest framework-boundary marking: "Each animal subject has *this* experience; the Map treats that indexical haecceity as a brute fact, which runs counter to MWI's denial that there is any fact of the matter about 'which branch.' This is a clash of foundational commitments, not a refutation of MWI on its own terms."
