# Pre-Publication Audit: "Phantom Limb Phenomena"

**Article under review:** *Phantom Limb Phenomena*, The Unfinishable Map (live URL [https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/phantom-limb-phenomena/](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/phantom-limb-phenomena/), last modified 2026-05-09).
**Auditor brief:** Pre-publication review for academic submission; emphasis on intellectual honesty, methodological weakness, and concrete revision targets.

**Overall verdict (executive summary):** The article is well-written, generally honest about its commitments, and shows visible tenet-bracketing discipline at the right moments. However, on the empirical side it is roughly a decade out of date on the single most important debate in the field — the Makin–Krakauer challenge to the cortical-remapping story — and it omits the one physicalist theory (Melzack's body-self neuromatrix) that *already* accounts for almost every "philosophically loaded" feature the article points to. The mirror-therapy literature is presented in its most flattering form rather than its current contested form. The dualism-supporting inferences therefore lean on an empirical base that is shakier than the prose suggests. The methodological recommendations at the end propose concrete fixes that would, in this auditor's judgment, *strengthen* rather than weaken the Map's broader programme by making the dialectic genuinely live.

## 1. Summary of the article's claims and argumentative structure

The article argues that phantom limb phenomena, while not refuting materialism, **sharpen what an adequate theory of embodied consciousness must explain** and are interpreted by the Map as **"evidence for filter-mediated embodiment, not against dualism."** The argumentative spine is a three-pillar inference: [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/phantom-limb-phenomena/)

1. **The explanatory-gap pillar.** Phantom qualia are vivid yet have no peripheral source, so the "look-to-the-periphery" deflection that materialists allegedly use elsewhere is unavailable; the gap reasserts itself.
2. **The top-down causation pillar.** Mirror therapy and related interventions modulate phantom pain via purely visual/imaginative routes, providing "one of the cleanest available demonstrations that conscious perceptual content can shape pain processing without any intermediate bodily intervention." [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/phantom-limb-phenomena/)
3. **The innate body-schema pillar.** Congenital phantoms (Brugger et al. 2000) show that the felt body is not built up entirely from sensorimotor experience, undercutting strong empiricist body-schema theories and supporting "structural commitments not exhausted by the body's actual configuration." [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/phantom-limb-phenomena/)

A "Relation to Site Perspective" section maps these onto three tenets (Dualism, Bidirectional Interaction, Occam's Razor Has Limits), and a "What Would Challenge This View?" section offers four named defeaters. The article explicitly disavows the strong dualist claim: "the phantom is not a free-floating limb-soul... The Map's dualism is not the claim that consciousness operates without the brain; it is the claim that consciousness is not reducible to the brain." This calibration is welcome and rare in dualist polemics. [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/phantom-limb-phenomena/)

The article cites 8 primary sources spanning 1992–2014 (median year 2000), plus two internal Map references. **There are no citations from 2015 onward.** This is the single most important fact for what follows.

## 2. Empirical claims — section-by-section audit

### 2.1 What is accurately characterized

- **Prevalence figures (60–80%)** for phantom sensations and phantom limb pain are within the standard range reported in current reviews.
- **Phenomenology** (telescoping, frozen postures, referred sensation from face stimulation, impossible postures, pain memory) is faithfully reported and uncontroversial.
- **Brugger et al. 2000** is cited correctly. The paper's headline result — vivid phantoms of all four limbs in a woman with congenital aplasia, with premotor and parietal activation during phantom movement — is reported accurately. One small but informative detail the article *elides*: the Brugger fMRI showed **no activation of primary sensorimotor areas** during phantom movement; activation was confined to premotor and parietal cortex. This is actually relevant to the article's argument and could be added (it strengthens the "structural-default" reading and weakens the "ordinary motor command" reading). [PNAS](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.100510697)
- **Ramachandran & Rogers-Ramachandran 1996** and the original face-mapping work (1992) are correctly characterized at the level of detail given.

### 2.2 Where the empirical base is materially out of date or one-sided

#### 2.2.1 The Makin challenge to cortical remapping — substantively absent

The article presents the Flor et al. (1995) MEG correlation between cortical remapping and phantom pain intensity as the canonical neuroscientific story, qualifies it only as "the dominant neuroscientific account," and gestures vaguely at "multilevel reorganisation" via Flor 2006. **The 12+ year programme by Tamar Makin and colleagues directly contesting this picture is not cited or engaged.** Key papers the article omits: [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/phantom-limb-phenomena/)

- **Makin et al. 2013, *Nature Communications* 4:1570** — "Phantom pain is associated with preserved structure and function in the former hand area." The headline finding directly contradicts the maladaptive-plasticity reading: amputees with *more* phantom pain show *stronger*, not weaker, cortical representations of the missing hand, and *no* evidence of face-into-hand remapping in primary sensorimotor cortex. [Nature + 2](https://www.nature.com/articles/ncomms2571)
- **Makin & Bensmaia 2017**, *Trends in Cognitive Sciences* — synthesis arguing for stability of body maps.
- **Wesselink et al. 2019, *eLife*** — hand "map" maintained in amputees with and without phantom sensations, even decades after amputation.
- **Makin & Krakauer 2023, "Against cortical reorganisation," *eLife*** — the senior review-article articulation of the case.
- **Schone, Maimon-Mor, Kollamkulam, Makin et al., "Stable cortical body maps before and after arm amputation," *Nature Neuroscience* 28 (October 2025), 2015–2021** — the methodologically strongest paper in the literature: longitudinal pre/post fMRI in three participants undergoing planned amputation, followed up to 5 years post-amputation. Direct quantification shows amputation does not trigger large-scale cortical reorganization; hand and lip representations are stable. [bioRxiv + 2](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2023.12.13.571314v2.full)
- **Amoruso, Terhune, Kromm, Kirker, Muret & Makin 2021/2022** (Reassessing referred sensations following peripheral deafferentation) — questions whether referred sensations require remapping at all. [medRxiv](https://www.medrxiv.org/content/10.1101/2021.12.08.21267128v1.full)
- **Root et al. 2022, *eLife* 11:e76158** — finds "complex pattern of facial remapping in somatosensory cortex following **congenital but not acquired** hand loss," with direct relevance to the article's congenital-phantoms section. [PubMed Central](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10760201/)

This is not a minor omission. The article's first pillar leans on Flor 1995 to ground the cortical-remapping mechanism, then in Section 4 the article writes:

> "The Map accepts this picture as the correct mechanistic story for the *production conditions* of phantom phenomena." [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/phantom-limb-phenomena/)

The Map cannot honestly *accept* a picture that has been the subject of a high-profile, ongoing scientific revision for over a decade without flagging the revision. At minimum the article needs a paragraph acknowledging that the Flor 1995 correlation has been challenged by Makin's group with longitudinal evidence; that "persistent representation" rather than "maladaptive remapping" is the leading alternative; and that the 2025 *Nature Neuroscience* longitudinal study by Schone et al. provides the strongest evidence yet that adult cortical body maps remain stable through amputation. Crucially, **the Makin programme is not bad news for the article's Map-side reading.** It is arguably better news: stable cortical body maps in the absence of the limb are stronger evidence for an innate/persistent body model than dynamic remapping would be. The article's dialectic should be rewritten with this updated empirical base.

#### 2.2.2 Mirror therapy efficacy — overstated in current literature

The article describes mirror therapy as "mixed but real" and cites Chan et al. 2007 (a one-page *NEJM* report, n=18) and Foell et al. 2014. Both are cherry-picked toward the positive end. The current literature is more skeptical:

- **Guémann, Olié, Raquin, Courtet & Risch 2023, *European Journal of Pain* 27(1):3–13** — systematic review restricted to *randomised placebo-controlled* trials concludes: "the present systematic review that included only studies with the best level of evidence did not find any evidence about its effectiveness." This is a strong, recent, methodologically targeted null. [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36094758/)
- **Scholl, Schmidt & Alfuth 2023/2024, *Z Orthop Unfall*** — for lower-limb amputation, finds mirror therapy effective only with high frequency/duration; "superiority of mirror therapy to other interventions cannot be concluded, as the evidence was of low quality." [Thieme](https://www.thieme-connect.com/products/ejournals/pdf/10.1055/a-2188-3565.pdf)
- **Rajendram et al. 2022, *BMJ Mil Health*** — mirror therapy and VR are equivalent (both work, neither better than the other; small samples). [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35042760/)
- **Earlier favourable meta-analyses** (Wu et al., Barbin et al.) report short-term but not long-term benefit and considerable heterogeneity.

The article's argument that mirror therapy is "one of the cleanest available demonstrations that conscious perceptual content can shape pain processing without any intermediate bodily intervention" is doing structural philosophical work and therefore needs to survive the current evidence base, not the 2007–2014 enthusiasm. A revised treatment should:

- cite Guémann 2023 explicitly as the strongest recent skeptical RCT-only review;
- distinguish *short-term subjective pain reduction* (where evidence is moderate but heterogeneous) from *durable, long-term, placebo-controlled benefit* (where evidence is weak);
- note that even where mirror therapy works, *graded motor imagery* (Moseley) and *virtual reality* are equivalent or better, which complicates the specific story that "visual feedback of the phantom" is the operative variable — it may be motor imagery, attention, expectation, or some combination.

Note that the article's own falsifier #2 ("Failure of mirror therapy under controlled conditions: If well-powered randomised trials returned null results...") is *arguably already met* by Guémann 2023 — yet the article does not flag this. That's a calibration failure relative to the article's own stated standards. [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/phantom-limb-phenomena/)

#### 2.2.3 Peripheral mechanisms — under-engaged

The article briefly mentions "stump neuromas, ectopic firing in dorsal root ganglia, and spinal-level changes" before dismissing them as not "dominating the conscious phenomenology." But Marshall Devor's three decades of work on ectopic firing in DRGs and peripheral neuromas (Devor 2013, Devor 2020 reviews) provides a substantial mechanism for at least early and possibly persistent phantom pain that does not require the cortical-remapping story *or* the dualist interpretation. The article should engage at least one of Devor's reviews. The case for top-down causation as load-bearing weakens if early phantom pain is substantially peripheral and only late/chronic phantom pain is centrally generated. [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/phantom-limb-phenomena/)

#### 2.2.4 Congenital phantoms — contested status under-flagged

The article uses Brugger et al. 2000 as if it were a settled paradigm case. The actual literature is more divided:

- **Price 2006, *Consciousness and Cognition*** — critical review showing that *only a minority* of people with limb aplasia experience phantoms, that prosthesis-fitting before age 7 predicts phantom occurrence, and proposing a developmental/mirror-neuron alternative in which the "innate" body schema is built up from external (visual) experience of other bodies during a sensitive period. [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/7580777_A_critical_review_of_congenital_phantom_limb_cases_and_a_developmental_theory_for_the_basis_of_body_image)[ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0010945264800034)
- **Crawford 2014** has explicitly called this "one of the longest running and most acrimonious debates within the phantom literature." [Taylor & Francis Online](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/15240657.2020.1842075)
- **Funk, Shiffrar & Brugger 2005** themselves note developmental factors.
- **Melzack et al. 1997** — original case series finding phantoms in roughly 20% of congenital limb-deficient children, lower than the article's framing implies.

The article's section on congenital phantoms states they "exist robustly enough to undermine the strong claim that the body schema is built up entirely from sensorimotor experience with an actual body." This is defensible if "robust" means "real, documented, and not eliminable by simple explanations" — but it elides that (a) the majority of aplasics do *not* report phantoms, (b) developmental/visual-imitation accounts remain live, and (c) Root et al. 2022 found that the cortical signature differs between congenital and acquired limb absence in ways that complicate a single "innate body schema" reading. [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/phantom-limb-phenomena/)[PubMed Central](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10760201/)

Note also that the falsifier the article offers (#4: "If congenital phantom reports proved systematically attributable to confabulation, suggestion, or the influence of seeing other people's bodies, the structural-default reading would weaken substantially") is essentially Price's account — already published, already debated. The article should explicitly engage Price 2006 rather than name the position only as a hypothetical defeater. [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/phantom-limb-phenomena/)

### 2.3 Minor empirical points

- The "pain memory" claim ("the phenomenal content of pain can be stored and re-instantiated by neural mechanisms in the absence of any current peripheral source") is presented with more confidence than the literature supports. Katz & Melzack's original 1990 case series is suggestive; subsequent attempts to confirm "veridical" pain memory as opposed to general pre-amputation pain influencing post-amputation pain are mixed (Nikolajsen et al. 1997, 2001). [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/phantom-limb-phenomena/)
- "Cortical territory... does not go silent" — the Schone et al. 2025 longitudinal data shows this more cleanly than the older deafferentation literature did, and could be cited.
- The brief reference to "magnetoencephalography studies showing that the magnitude of remapping correlates with the intensity of phantom pain (Flor et al., 1995)" presents Flor 1995 as a robust finding without flagging the multiple subsequent failed replications and methodological critiques (sample size, MEG localization precision). [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/phantom-limb-phenomena/)

## 3. Counterarguments from physicalist neuroscience and pain science — what the article inadequately addresses

### 3.1 The most consequential omission: Melzack's body-self neuromatrix theory

**The article never cites Ronald Melzack's neuromatrix theory of pain.** This is striking because Melzack's theory (Melzack 1989, 1990 *Trends Neurosci.*, 1999, 2001) was developed *specifically* to account for phantom limb phenomena and *already* posits, within physicalism, every feature the article presents as philosophically loaded:

> "Neural networks within the brain generate all the qualities of experience that are felt to originate in the body; inputs from the body may trigger or modulate the output of the networks but are not essential for any of the qualities of the experience." (Melzack 1990) [PAINLOSOPHY](https://painlosophy.wordpress.com/2024/02/18/the-neuromatrix-theory-of-pain-a-hermeneutic-device-that-has-been-superseded/)

The body-self neuromatrix is:

- a **genetically determined**, partly hard-wired distributed neural network spanning somatosensory, thalamocortical, and limbic regions; [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/0304395996030461)
- the substrate of a "neurosignature" that produces the felt body **with or without peripheral input**;
- explicitly designed to explain **congenital phantoms** (Melzack proposed the hard-wired component precisely because phantoms in aplasics exist); [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/0304395996030461)
- responsive to top-down modulation from cognitive-evaluative and affective neuromodules.

In other words, Melzack's theory *already* says: (i) the felt body is brain-constructed not peripherally read off, (ii) it has innate structural components that survive in the absence of any peripheral history, and (iii) it is modulable by cognitive and affective inputs. All three of the article's "philosophically loaded" features are predictions of, and explanatory targets for, an explicit physicalist theory. The article writes:

> "Whatever pain is, it is not simply the conscious read-out of nociceptive transduction at a tissue site." [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/phantom-limb-phenomena/)

This is a strawman of pain science. No serious pain theorist since the 1990s holds the "conscious read-out of nociceptive transduction" view. Melzack's neuromatrix is the canonical alternative *within physicalism*. The biopsychosocial model (Engel, then Gatchel, Turk), the predictive-processing accounts (below), and even Quintner & Cohen's 2024 critique of the neuromatrix all proceed from the assumption that pain is centrally generated.

This omission has a direct dialectical consequence. The article's argument is that phantoms "raise the explanatory gap in a setting where the standard materialist resort — pointing to peripheral signals as the cause of the felt content — is not available." But that is **not** the standard materialist resort about the explanatory gap. The standard materialist response to the gap is functional/representational identity, type-type identity, higher-order theory, illusionism, or predictive processing. None of these depend on peripheral signaling. So phantoms do not differentially embarrass materialism; they embarrass only a strawman version of it. [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/phantom-limb-phenomena/)

**Recommendation:** Add a substantive engagement with Melzack 1990 and Melzack 2001 ("Pain and the Neuromatrix in the Brain," *J Dent Educ*) including: (i) acknowledgment that Melzack's theory is the most direct physicalist competitor to the Map's reading; (ii) honest statement of what neuromatrix theory does and does not explain; (iii) Map-side articulation of what additional explanatory work the Map's reading does that neuromatrix theory does not. Without this, the article's "phantoms sharpen the gap" argument is in the position of pushing on an open door.

### 3.2 Predictive processing / Bayesian brain accounts — under-engaged

The article mentions predictive processing once in passing ("This is consistent with the general framework of [predictive processing](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/predictive-processing/) accounts"), as if it were a friendly framework that absorbs the Map's reading rather than a serious physicalist competitor. The opposite is closer to the truth: predictive-processing accounts are perhaps the strongest contemporary materialist account of phantoms that the article does not engage. Key references: [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/phantom-limb-phenomena/)

- **De Ridder, Vanneste & Freeman 2014**, *Neurosci Biobehav Rev* — "The Bayesian brain: Phantom percepts resolve sensory uncertainty." Argues that phantom pain emerges from the free-energy principle: deafferentation produces topographically restricted uncertainty (high-precision prediction errors), and phantom percepts are the brain's least-bad inference about a body that should be there. [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0149763412000607)
- **Kiverstein, Kirchhoff & Thacker 2022**, *Review of Philosophy and Psychology* 13(4):973–998 — "An embodied predictive processing theory of pain experience." Specifically an *embodied* PP account that grounds phantom pain in failures of active inference over the body model. [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37421540/)
- **Lersch, Frickmann, Urman & Luedi 2023**, *Current Pain and Headache Reports* — "Analgesia for the Bayesian Brain: How Predictive Coding Offers Insights Into the Subjectivity of Pain." [Springer](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11916-023-01122-5)
- **Sanders, Casey & Saalmann 2021**, *British Journal of Anaesthesia* — predictive coding model of sensory disconnection relevant to phantoms. [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37421540/)
- **Andy Clark** and **Anil Seth** — though they do not write specifically on phantoms, their general PP/predictive-self frameworks have been applied by others (Limanowski, Friston) to body-ownership phenomena including phantoms and the rubber-hand illusion.

These accounts provide *physicalist* explanations for every feature the article lists:

- *Felt position without proprioceptive input* — high-precision prior on body configuration in the absence of contradicting prediction error.
- *Felt pain without nociceptive transduction* — pain as inference over interoceptive expectations; deafferentation generates pain *because* the brain is reducing uncertainty about a body part it expects to be present.
- *Mirror therapy effects* — visual prediction errors update the body model, downweighting the painful posture prior.
- *Congenital phantoms* — innate priors on body configuration (the "default human body" generative model) instantiate phantoms when sensorimotor evidence is sparse.

The article's failure to engage these accounts seriously is the single largest counterargument gap. Worth noting: Makin's own lab explicitly frames its programme as a *predictive coding* test (UCL Brain Stability & Phantom Limb Pain page: "we use a predictive coding framework to investigate whether phantom limb pain relates to sensorimotor prediction error"). The contemporary frontline of phantom-limb research *is* a physicalist programme; the article presents it as if no such programme exists. [UCL](https://www.ucl.ac.uk/brain-sciences/brain-stability-phantom-limb-pain)

### 3.3 Embodied/enactive cognition

The article's gloss "the felt body is constructed by the brain rather than transmitted from the periphery" is closer to Gallagher's *body schema vs body image* distinction, Thompson's enactive embodiment, and Noë's sensorimotor enactivism than the article acknowledges. None of these frameworks is dualist. None is engaged. At minimum, Gallagher's *How the Body Shapes the Mind* (2005) and Thompson's *Mind in Life* (2007) should be considered, and the article should explain why the Map's "filter-mediated embodiment" is preferable to or distinct from enactive embodiment. [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/phantom-limb-phenomena/)

### 3.4 Central sensitization

A modern peripheralist-plus-central account of phantom pain often emphasizes central sensitization in dorsal horn and brainstem nuclei (Woolf, Latremoliere). The article mentions "spinal-level changes" but does not engage central sensitization as a candidate explanation. This is a smaller gap but worth noting.

### 3.5 Top-down modulation does not entail mental causation in the Map's sense

The article writes:

> "Mirror therapy is not, on its own, decisive against materialism — neuronal accounts of how visual input modulates somatosensory and pain pathways are well-developed. What it shows is that the relevant causal pathway runs through what the patient *sees and attends to*, which is precisely the kind of pathway the Map's [interactionist reading](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/interactionist-dualism/) expects to find." [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/phantom-limb-phenomena/)

This is calibrated honestly, but the inference is weaker than it sounds. Visual cortex → premotor/parietal → S1/S2 → cingulate/insular nociceptive modulation is a standard physicalist account that explains exactly the same phenomenon. "The pathway runs through what the patient sees and attends to" describes the *phenomenology* of the intervention; the *causal pathway* runs through neurons. The Map's interactionism is "expected to find" mirror therapy effects, but so is every contemporary materialist theory of pain. This is therefore not differential evidence. The article should either drop the implicit dialectical weight on mirror therapy or argue that the Map's prediction is *quantitatively* different from materialism's (which the article does not do).

## 4. Tenet bracketing — section-by-section

### 4.1 What is done well

The article does several things that are admirably above the standard of philosophical writing on these topics:

- The opening is explicit that the Map's reading is *interpretive*: "phantoms decisively refute the naive picture that bodily experience is read off from peripheral signals... This in itself is compatible with materialism. What makes phantoms philosophically loaded for the Map is..." [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/phantom-limb-phenomena/)
- The closing of Section 2 distinguishes mechanism-explanation from gap-explanation cleanly: "The remapping account specifies which neurons fire under which conditions; it does not bridge from those firings to the felt burning of a hand that does not exist." [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/phantom-limb-phenomena/)
- The "Relation to Site Perspective" section calls out the three tenets by name with bold headers, so a reader can see exactly where the Map's commitments are loaded.
- The phrase **"None of this proves dualism"** appears in the lede, and the article explicitly notes that phantoms "do not support any claim of consciousness existing wholly independently of the brain." [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/phantom-limb-phenomena/)
- The "What Would Challenge This View?" section is genuine intellectual hygiene and rare in this genre.

### 4.2 Where tenet bleed occurs

Despite the discipline, several places leak Map-side commitments into supposedly neutral descriptive passages:

**Tenet leak 1: "Somatic interface" smuggled in as descriptive vocabulary.**

> "...it has to be one in which the brain — or the brain-mediated [somatic interface](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/somatic-interface/) — generates the felt body..." [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/phantom-limb-phenomena/)

The em-dash gloss "or the brain-mediated somatic interface" presents the Map's interface-theoretic framework as an *alternative phrasing* for "brain," as if the two are interchangeable descriptions. They are not. "Somatic interface" carries the Map's filter-theoretic commitment; "brain" does not. This is the most concrete instance of tenet leakage in the article. Recommend either dropping the em-dash gloss or rephrasing as "the brain (which on the Map's reading is the substrate of a *somatic interface* — see linked concept)."

**Tenet leak 2: "Filter-theoretic accounts can absorb" as gentle persuasion.**

> "This does not entail anything mystical about the phantom — the brain is still doing the work — but it shifts the locus of the felt body from peripheral history to neural architecture in a way that materialist accounts must accommodate and that filter-theoretic accounts can absorb." [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/phantom-limb-phenomena/)

The asymmetric verbs — materialism must "accommodate," filter theory can "absorb" — do rhetorical work. Both accounts accommodate or absorb the data; the asymmetric phrasing presupposes filter theory's superior fit. Recommend symmetrizing: "...that materialist accounts must accommodate and that the Map's filter-theoretic reading naturally houses."

**Tenet leak 3: "The felt body is constructed by the brain" presented as phantom-discovered.**
The opening claims "phantoms... support the broader claim that the felt body is constructed by the brain rather than transmitted from the periphery." This is *also* a core neuromatrix-theory claim, a core PP claim, a core enactivist claim. It is not unique evidence for the Map's reading. Recommend rephrasing to acknowledge that this is a now-shared finding across multiple physicalist and non-physicalist frameworks, and locate the Map's distinctive interpretation more precisely. [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/phantom-limb-phenomena/)

**Tenet leak 4: "What pain is" treated as settled.**

> "Whatever pain is, it is not simply the conscious read-out of nociceptive transduction at a tissue site. It is a phenomenal state with its own conditions of existence..." [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/phantom-limb-phenomena/)

The slip from "not a transparent indicator of peripheral state" (uncontroversial) to "a phenomenal state with its own conditions of existence" (already half-way to the Map's irreducibility thesis) is rapid. A neutral phrasing would be: "it is a centrally generated experience whose conditions of existence include but are not exhausted by peripheral nociceptive input."

**Tenet leak 5: The metadata.**
The article's `meta-description` and `meta-citation_abstract` read: *"The Map reads them as evidence for filter-mediated embodiment, not against dualism."* This is fine for the abstract; it is the right level of tenet-transparency. Internally consistent with the article body. [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/phantom-limb-phenomena/)

### 4.3 Methodology check against the site's own discipline

Examining the changelog reveals an active methodology including:

- *evidential-status-discipline* (no "solves"/"answers" language)
- *direct-refutation-discipline* (boundary-substitution avoidance)
- *common-cause-null* (audit shared upstream mechanisms before claiming independent convergence)
- *possibility/probability-slippage* checks
- *engagement-mode classification* (One: internal critique; Two: foundational-move identification; Three: framework-boundary marking; Four: bedrock)

By these standards, the article shows:

- **Strong evidential-status discipline** — no "solves" claims; the phrase "sharpens what an adequate theory must explain" is exactly the right register.
- **Good framework-boundary marking** in the closing — "the Map's dualism is not the claim that consciousness operates without the brain" is a Mode-Three boundary-marking move that prevents over-claiming. [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/phantom-limb-phenomena/)
- **Weak direct-refutation discipline against materialism's actual contemporary form** — the article engages a strawman of "peripheralist" materialism. A Mode-One internal critique of neuromatrix theory or predictive processing is owed.
- **No common-cause-null audit** of the three pillars. The three "philosophically loaded" features (vivid qualia, top-down modulation, congenital phantoms) are not independent observations — they all draw on the same finding that the brain centrally constructs the felt body. The article presents them as three independent lines of evidence; a common-cause-null audit would say they are one structural observation read three ways.
- **Modest possibility/probability slippage** at "this is one of the cleanest available demonstrations that conscious perceptual content can shape pain processing without any intermediate bodily intervention" — *cleanest* is doing work and is contestable. [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/phantom-limb-phenomena/)

## 5. Logical structure — section-by-section

### 5.1 The "no peripheral signal" argument

> "phantoms decisively refute the naive picture that bodily experience is read off from peripheral signals — there are no peripheral signals, yet there is bodily experience" [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/phantom-limb-phenomena/)

Sound but rhetorically over-strong. No 21st-century materialist account holds the "read off from peripheral signals" picture. The argument refutes a position no one defends; it does not differentially favour the Map's reading.

### 5.2 The qualia/explanatory-gap argument

> "phantoms make the [explanatory gap](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/explanatory-gap/) vivid in a setting where the standard materialist resort — pointing to peripheral signals as the cause of the felt content — is not available." [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/phantom-limb-phenomena/)

**Logical problem:** This is non-sequitur unless "pointing to peripheral signals" is "the standard materialist resort" to the explanatory gap. It is not. Materialists do not deflect the gap by pointing to peripheral signals; they deflect it (when they do) by functional/representational identity, by predictive-processing reduction, by illusionism, or by mysterianism. Phantoms therefore do not "sharpen" the gap. They re-instance it in a clinically vivid setting — which has rhetorical force but not differential evidential force.

### 5.3 The top-down causation argument

> "A purely visual stimulus — a mirror image — modulates the phenomenology and intensity of a pain that has no peripheral generator at the felt site. The intervention does not target the periphery. It targets the patient's *visual experience* of their own body." [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/phantom-limb-phenomena/)

**Logical problem 1 (equivocation):** "Top-down causation" is used in two distinct senses across the article: (a) cortical-on-subcortical neural modulation (uncontroversial physicalism, present in every account of pain since gate-control theory), and (b) mind-on-brain causation (the dualist commitment). The article slides from (a) to (b) without flagging the move. Recommend explicit disambiguation.

**Logical problem 2 (empirical condition unmet):** The argument depends on mirror therapy *working*. The current literature is mixed-to-skeptical (see §2.2.2). If Guémann 2023 is right that placebo-controlled RCT evidence is null, the argument's empirical base evaporates.

**Logical problem 3 (alternative explanations equally available):** Even granting that mirror therapy works, the modulation pathway "patient's visual experience of their own body → pain" is exactly what neuromatrix theory and predictive processing predict, with no dualist remainder.

### 5.4 The congenital-phantoms argument

> "Congenital phantoms... show that the bodily self-model is not built up entirely from sensorimotor experience." [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/phantom-limb-phenomena/)

**Logical problem 1 (selection effect):** Most aplasics do not report phantoms. The article infers from the minority who do to a "predisposition" of the brain. This inference is defensible but should engage the developmental/visual-modeling alternatives (Price 2006, Funk 2005).

**Logical problem 2 (over-strong universalisation):** "The brain appears to be predisposed to generate a body schema with a default human form." This claim needs heavy qualification — the evidence supports a *capacity* of some brains under some developmental conditions to instantiate such a schema, not a robust universal predisposition. [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/phantom-limb-phenomena/)

### 5.5 The "no fully reductive account" defeater

> "If a neuroscientific theory provided a non-trivial bridge from the cortically remapped activity to the specific phenomenal content of phantom experience — not just correlating activity with reports, but explaining why *this* activity produces *this* felt quality — the [explanatory gap](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/explanatory-gap/) argument from phantoms would lose force." [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/phantom-limb-phenomena/)

This is a **god-of-the-gaps formulation** of the defeater. The criterion sets the bar at "explaining why *this* activity produces *this* felt quality" — a bar that, by hypothesis (cf. Chalmers 1995), no third-person empirical theory can ever meet because the gap is structural, not contingent. The defeater is therefore unfalsifiable by design. Recommend reformulation: the defeater should specify what kind of *evidence* (functional, predictive, neural-correlate-of-content) would shift the balance, not require an in-principle impossible bridging law.

### 5.6 Overall argumentative structure

The article's three pillars are *individually* honest about not proving dualism, but *jointly* present a cumulative case that leans harder than the underlying empirical base supports. The cumulative case would be more honest if:

- pillar 1 (qualia gap) were de-emphasised as not specific to phantoms;
- pillar 2 (top-down causation) were qualified by recent skeptical mirror-therapy meta-analyses;
- pillar 3 (congenital phantoms) were qualified by Price 2006 and the minority-of-aplasics fact;
- *and* a fourth move were added: the **stable-body-maps** finding from Makin/Schone, which is the strongest currently-available evidence for an "interface-side" reading and which the article does not currently use because it does not cite the relevant literature.

The Map's reading would be *stronger*, not weaker, after these revisions.

### 6(b) Site-wide methodology improvements

These are derived from patterns visible across the article, the tenets page, and the changelog.

**1. Citation-recency audit as a standing review pass.**
The article is methodologically sophisticated in its tenet-discipline but cites no source after 2014. Across the corpus this pattern likely repeats wherever a topic is rooted in older paradigm-setting work (Ramachandran, Libet, Sperry, Penfield, etc.). Recommend a periodic "recency audit" review pass — analogous to the existing *common-cause-null* and *direct-refutation-discipline* passes — that:

- flags articles citing no source after a given recency window (e.g., 5 or 10 years);
- requires the next deep-review pass to either add a current-decade citation or explicitly justify the citation profile;
- in topics with active scientific debate, requires engagement with at least one paper from the last 3 years on each load-bearing empirical claim.

**2. Explicit "contemporary materialist competitor" pass.**
The article's most consequential omission (Melzack neuromatrix) suggests a corpus-wide risk: dualism-supporting articles may engage stylised or older materialist positions rather than the specific, contemporary, mature physicalist theory of the relevant domain. Recommend a methodology rule: every Map article that draws empirical-philosophical inferences in domain D must engage the *strongest contemporary physicalist account* in D by name. For phantoms: neuromatrix + predictive processing. For consciousness generally: global workspace, IIT, illusionism, predictive self. For free will: compatibilism in Frankfurt/Fischer form. For mental causation: Yablo/proportionality, Bennett-style autonomy. The current methodology check (`check-tenets`) appears to be a tenets-consistency check; an analogous `check-physicalist-engagement` pass would close this gap.

**3. Standardised falsifier-quality criterion.**
The article's falsifiers are honest but variable in quality — falsifier #3 (the "fully reductive account") is structurally unfalsifiable as written. Recommend a methodology criterion: each falsifier must specify *the kind of evidence* that would shift the balance, not a metaphysical achievement that cannot in principle be reached. Falsifiers of the form "if a complete reductive account existed" should be reformulated as "if evidence E1, E2, E3 obtained."

**4. Common-cause-null audit applied to multi-pillar arguments.**
The site's methodology already includes a *common-cause-null* discipline (per the changelog's `epistemology-of-convergence-arguments` work). This article makes a three-pillar cumulative argument without applying common-cause-null to its own pillars. Recommend that every Map article with a cumulative ≥2-pillar case explicitly run common-cause-null on the pillars in the article body (not only in editor-internal review).

**5. "Mode classification" leakage prevention vs. accessibility.**
The changelog shows extensive use of editor-internal engagement-mode classification (One/Two/Three/Four) that is correctly excluded from article bodies. This is the right discipline. *However*, a reader of the published article cannot see how rigorously the dialectic has been processed. Consider exposing a *summary* of engagement-mode classifications in a small footer or appendix on each article ("This article engages neuromatrix theory in Mode One on phenomenology, Mode Two on the explanatory-gap question, and Mode Three on the substance-question"). This gives the academic reader the rigorous machinery without leaking editor-jargon into prose.

**6. Tenet-bleed lint pass.**
The article has at least one clear tenet-bleed instance ("the brain — or the brain-mediated somatic interface — generates the felt body"), where Map-side vocabulary is treated as a synonym for descriptively-neutral vocabulary. Recommend a corpus-wide lint pass that flags em-dash glosses on neutral terms with Map-specific terms ("brain — or somatic interface," "neural mechanism — or filter side," "mental causation — or downward causation," etc.) and requires the next refine-draft to either remove the gloss or explicitly tenet-mark it.

**7. Engagement with currently-active research programmes by name.**
The changelog reveals deep engagement with Stapp, Penrose-Hameroff, Schwartz, Duch, Tononi-style approaches at the quantum-consciousness end. The empirical-cognitive-neuroscience end (Makin lab, Friston/Clark predictive processing, Damasio interoception, Seth controlled hallucination) appears comparatively under-engaged. Recommend a deliberate balance-of-engagement target: for each major Map domain, ensure at least one named contemporary research programme on the physicalist/empiricist side is engaged with comparable depth to the named programmes on the dualist/interactionist side.

**8. Adversarial pre-publication review by domain experts.**
The site's existing optimistic/pessimistic-review cycle is internal. For academic submission specifically, consider an external pre-publication review from a working phantom-limb researcher (the Makin lab is one obvious choice; Lorimer Moseley's group is another), even if only informally. The current article would have benefited from a 30-minute conversation with anyone whose primary research is current phantom-limb neuroscience.

**9. Calibration around "evidence for" vs "compatible with."**
The article often uses "supports," "evidence for," and "is supportive of" where the strict relation is "is compatible with." Compatibility is weaker than support. A site-wide pass tightening *evidence-for* language to *compatibility/no-violation* language where appropriate would improve calibration without weakening the substantive case. This article's strongest phrasing — "phantoms exemplify the second without supporting the first" — shows the discipline is already available; it should be applied consistently. [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/phantom-limb-phenomena/)

**10. Explicit "absorbed by competing frameworks" note per pillar.**
For each empirical observation a Map article invokes, consider a brief explicit note of which competing frameworks already absorb it (neuromatrix absorbs brain-constructed body; PP absorbs top-down modulation; enactivism absorbs embodied phenomenology). This is a Mode-Three discipline at the level of individual evidence-pieces and would prevent the cumulative inflation that comes from presenting widely-absorbable observations as distinctive support.

## 7. Closing assessment

The article is the work of a careful, philosophically literate author with good tenet-discipline and an honest disavowal of over-strong dualist claims. Its weaknesses are concentrated in empirical recency and the under-engagement of contemporary physicalist competitors. The single most consequential revision is to engage the Makin programme (2013–2025) on cortical body-map stability: doing so will require revising the Flor-1995 framing, but will *strengthen* rather than weaken the Map's claim that phantoms point to a persistent, partly innate body model rather than to dynamic remapping. The second most consequential revision is to engage Melzack's neuromatrix theory and the predictive-processing literature as the contemporary materialist competitors the article currently elides. The third is to qualify the mirror-therapy claims to match the contested current evidence base. With these revisions, the article would be substantially more defensible for academic submission and would, in this auditor's judgment, retain or strengthen its philosophical force rather than lose it.
