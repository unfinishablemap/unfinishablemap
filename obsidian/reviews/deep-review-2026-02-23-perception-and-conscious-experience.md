### Medium Issues Found

1. **Dissociation argument overstated its conclusion**: The original claim "If consciousness were identical to the processing, these dissociations would be incoherent" was logically imprecise. Physicalists identify consciousness with *specific types* of processing (global broadcasting, higher-order representation), not all processing—so dissociations are coherent on their view. **Resolution**: Rewrote the passage to engage with the physicalist response directly, then explain why the explanatory gap persists even when the correct neural correlate is identified. Added cross-link to [[conscious-vs-unconscious-processing]].

2. **Transparency argument used a weak premise**: "If perceptual experience were a neural state, we might expect introspection to reveal neural properties" is not what physicalists claim. Representational physicalists expect introspection to reveal worldly content, which is exactly what it does. **Resolution**: Restructured the transparency section to acknowledge the representational physicalist response, then explain why even that response concedes something important (the identity claim becomes purely theoretical, never first-personally confirmed). Shifted to the stronger argument about the intrinsic nature of intentionality vs. causal relations.

3. **Block's phenomenal overflow presented without noting contestation**: The debate about whether phenomenal consciousness can outrun access consciousness is significant and ongoing. **Resolution**: Added brief acknowledgment that critics contest the interpretation while noting Block's empirical support.
