### Counterarguments Considered
- **Eliminative Materialist (Churchland)**: Anosognosia is just neural damage; the "void" framing adds nothing. Response: the apex doesn't claim supernatural explanation — it treats anosognosia as a category anchor joining a convergent pattern of self-knowledge limits. The neural-damage case sharpens rather than undermines the structural point.
- **Hard-Nosed Physicalist (Dennett)**: Anosognosia is well-studied in clinical neuropsychology; it needs no apex-level framing. Response: the apex function is precisely to reveal that ordinary clinical phenomena fit into a larger taxonomy of cognitive limits converging on the consciousness-matter interface. Integration into the taxonomy is the contribution.
- **Empiricist (Popper)**: Adding a "zero-signal" failure signature risks unfalsifiability — any unnoticed failure could be attributed to erasure. Response: the signature is constrained by two falsifiability anchors — external audit (longitudinal testing, AI comparison) can reveal what introspection misses, and the signature is defined structurally (monitoring system shares fate with capacity), not phenomenologically.
- **Buddhist Philosopher (Nagarjuna)**: The self whose completeness is allegedly eroding is empty to begin with. Response: acknowledged as bedrock disagreement — the taxonomy assumes the operational utility of the self-model it examines, while noting that the erasure void can itself be read as evidence for the emptiness thesis.
