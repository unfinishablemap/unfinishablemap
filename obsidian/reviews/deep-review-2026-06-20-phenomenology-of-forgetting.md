## Pessimistic Analysis Summary

### Critical Issues Found

- **Ricoeur position-strength / attribution error** (FIXED): The article presented Ricoeur (2004, *Memory, History, Forgetting*) as affirmatively treating "a 'happy forgetting' that parallels happy memory." Publisher-of-record verification shows Ricoeur *questions* the parallel rather than asserting it — his text reads "there cannot be a happy forgetting in the same way as one can dream of a happy memory," and he is wary of an *ars oblivionis* that licenses complacency before "the wearing away of time." The genuinely Ricoeurian notion is *forgetting held in reserve* (oubli de réserve) and forgetting as the condition of remembering. This is the classic fresh-create "explores → affirms" position-strength slippage (§2.5): an exploratory/skeptical treatment was presented as a commitment. Resolution: rewrote the passage to attribute the *forgetting-held-in-reserve* notion, present the "happy forgetting" idea as a question Ricoeur poses (not affirms), and note his wariness of *ars oblivionis* — keeping the experiential "releasing" texture the article names as the reserve's felt side, not a claim that forgetting is straightforwardly happy.
