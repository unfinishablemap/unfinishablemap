## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
`predictive-processing-and-dualism` is a Chalmers-pleasing piece. Its central move — "the more precisely PP describes the brain's operations, the sharper the hard problem becomes" — is exactly the explanatory-gap-takes-it-seriously stance Chalmers defends. The treatment of the meta-problem (Clark, Friston & Wilkinson 2019) as "clever but question-begging — explaining consciousness-*talk*, not consciousness itself" is the right Chalmersian reply and is stated cleanly. `consciousness-in-simple-organisms` earns the same praise at every scale: "the explanatory gap exists at every complexity level."

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
`brain-specialness-boundary` is the strongest engagement of the three with the physics-consciousness interface. It takes Stapp's Process 1 and the quantum Zeno mechanism seriously as the *source* of the brain-locality prediction rather than as decoration, and it correctly notes external systems "face even more severe decoherence challenges than neural tissue." The article engages the parapsychology record (PEAR, Global Consciousness Project, Ganzfeld) head-on rather than dodging it — Stapp's own concern that interactionism not collapse into PK is answered mechanistically.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
`predictive-processing-and-dualism` centers first-person experience well: the Austin "moderate-sized specimens of dry goods" line and the insistence that "the felt experience of deliberate attending... is not captured by describing signal gain adjustment" keep the what-it's-like question load-bearing against an otherwise third-person formalism. `consciousness-in-simple-organisms` extends the Nagelian question downward responsibly — "the 302-neuron connectome tells us nothing about whether that processing is witnessed."

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
`consciousness-in-simple-organisms` gives Whitehead a full, fair hearing (the "Process Philosophy Perspective" section: actual occasions, prehension, degrees of integration rather than presence/absence). Crucially, it does this *without* letting the Whiteheadian "experience all the way down" intuition do load-bearing tier-upgrade work — the article explicitly states process philosophy "raises the metaphysical question without supplying positive evidence." This is the honest pattern: process-philosophical resonance is praised at every tier, never used as the engine of a tier-upgrade. The Process Philosopher and Hardline Empiricist personas *converge* here, which is the signal that the tension has been resolved honestly.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
`predictive-processing-and-dualism` makes Kane's room-for-agency argument precise: PP "describes a brain that generates probability distributions and resolves them through precision weighting. The question is what ultimately does the resolving." The active-inference section — "genuine agency requires something outside the predictive loop to break symmetries" — is Kane's ultimate-sourcehood intuition recast in PP vocabulary, and it honestly registers the compatibilist objection rather than ignoring it.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
`consciousness-in-simple-organisms` contains the cleanest single McGinn passage in the recent corpus: "we possess complete structural knowledge of 302 neurons yet cannot determine whether the worm experiences anything... This is what cognitive closure looks like empirically." Cognitive closure is presented as epistemic humility that neither wins nor loses the dualism debate — exactly McGinn's register.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This persona's verdict is load-bearing for two of the three articles (both touch minimal-organism consciousness and tenet-load-bearing claims), and the news is good.

- **`consciousness-in-simple-organisms` is a calibration exemplar.** The dedicated "Evidential Register: Tenet vs. Evidence" section names the exact discipline this persona exists to enforce: "Removing a defeater keeps a hypothesis live; it does not, on its own, supply positive evidence." Every model organism is held at *live hypothesis* or *speculative integration* — none is slid up to *realistic possibility* on tenet-load. The UAL section explicitly refuses to let the interface reading absorb the negative empirical signal ("Failing UAL remains evidentially meaningful... it should flow through to the evidential-status verdict"). This is **tenet-as-evidence-upgrade praise-worthily *not* done**: the article had the structural opportunity to upgrade nematodes/Hydra/slime molds on tenet-coherence and explicitly declined. The named pattern "tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating" is used verbatim.
- **`brain-specialness-boundary` applies the same discipline to a different domain.** "This is a coherence claim, not an evidential upgrade. The mechanism's brain-locality makes the null result *unsurprising*; it does not turn the null result into positive confirmation of the mechanism." The article repeatedly marks its argument as conditional on the mechanism ("only as secure as that mechanism") and concedes the embodiment expectation is "shaped by the framework, not an independent check on it." Textbook register-separation.
- **`predictive-processing-and-dualism` is the exception — see Calibration Concern below.** Its quantum-Bayesian section is well-hedged ("early-stage and speculative... The Map does not claim it as established science"), but the framing claim "PP is dualism's most productive scientific ally" is precisely the cite-as-ally slippage this persona flags. The Process Philosopher would happily praise the PP-compatibility passage; the Hardline Empiricist flags that the *ally* framing recruits committed physicalists (Friston, Clark, Hohwy, Seth) toward a conclusion their own programmes were built to avoid. The two personas conflict here — the diagnostic the skill specifies for possibility/probability slippage. This is a calibration concern to fix, not a strength to praise (and it is already queued; see below).
