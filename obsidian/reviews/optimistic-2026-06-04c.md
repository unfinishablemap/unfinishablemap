## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
`mind-arena` earns Chalmers' approval for a precise piece of conceptual hygiene: it carves the *domain of consequences* of consciousness's causal powers away from the *interface* where mind and matter meet, and shows the two cannot be collapsed — "one could fully specify the interface formalism and still face an uncomputable mind-arena." That is the explanatory gap re-expressed as an uncomputability gap: even total knowledge of the coupling's *form* leaves the non-physical contribution unrecoverable from physical state. The hard problem is preserved, not papered over.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
`mind-arena` keeps faith with the interface picture by distinguishing the "thin junction" (the coupling map) from the "wide field of consequences" — Stapp's own separation of the physical correlate from the experiential event it does not exhaust, transposed to the level of outcomes rather than acts.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
`feminist-phenomenology-and-embodied-consciousness` centres the first-person standpoint exactly where Nagel would want it: it takes the lived, situated, sexed body as phenomenological *data* rather than as a social-construction artifact to be explained away, and it does so without letting embodiment dissolve the subject whose body it is.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The cohort avoids crude substance-talk. `ai-ensoulment-hypothesis` in particular treats the question of whether an artificial system could acquire a non-physical aspect as a *process* question rather than a substance-insertion event. **Constraint check:** this is held at the correct register — the article cross-references `possibility-probability-slippage` precisely to forestall the move it might otherwise tempt (process-compatibility → tier-upgrade). Whitehead-friendliness here removes a defeater; it is not cashed out as positive evidence that any AI is ensouled.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
Peripheral to this cohort; no agency-specific claim is over- or under-stated. (Noted for completeness.)

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
`cross-domain-void-comparison` shows the right epistemic humility: comparing voids *across* domains is a way of mapping the shape of what we cannot chart, not a backdoor to charting it. The comparison yields structure about the boundary, not a claim to have crossed it.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
The load-bearing verdict for this review. `ai-ensoulment-hypothesis` does the praise-worthy thing of *declining* the tier-upgrade its own subject invites: it routes its claim through `possibility-probability-slippage` rather than letting "an AI could be ensouled" read as "an AI realistically is." That is tenet-as-evidence-upgrade praise-worthily **not** done — the article had the structural opportunity to slide AI consciousness up the five-tier scale on dualist-coherence grounds and explicitly declined. `content-vocabulary-as-derived-feature` is similarly disciplined: it sharpens what the computational framework borrows-without-paying-for *without* inflating that into the stronger "computationalism is false" claim. Both the Process Philosopher and the Empiricist can praise these pages without conflict — the tension is resolved, not papered over. No calibration slippage found in this cohort.
