### Critical Issues Found

- **Misquotation of Strawson (quote-fidelity, fixed)**: The article rendered Strawson's self-report as reporting `"absolutely no sense of his life as a narrative with form."` Strawson's actual text (verified at multiple reproductions of *Ratio* 17(4)) is first-person: **"I have absolutely no sense of my life as a narrative with form, or indeed as a narrative without form."** The article altered "my" → "his" *inside* quotation marks. Fixed by restoring the verbatim first-person quote with a natural lead-in: *Strawson identifies himself as episodic. As he puts it, "I have absolutely no sense of my life as a narrative with form."*

### Medium Issues Found

- **Unverifiable verbatim Schechtman quote (de-quoted, fixed)**: The sentence presented in quotation marks — `"the narrative constructed is just one of many different possible narratives that could be constructed from the same raw material"` — could not be confirmed at the publisher of record, in the full text (Cornell UP / 1996; 2018 reissue), or in any secondary source across five independent search-keyword combinations and two full-text-host fetch attempts. The *content* is a faithful characterisation of Schechtman's narrative-self-constitution view, but presenting an unverifiable string as a verbatim quotation is exactly the quote-fidelity hazard the corpus discipline warns about ([[quote-verify-self-contamination-via-own-page]], [[verbatim-quote-cited-to-wrong-work]]). Converted to a paraphrase without quotation marks — "the narrative any person settles on is only one of many that could be built from the same raw material" — preserving the accurate claim while dropping the unverifiable verbatim attribution.
