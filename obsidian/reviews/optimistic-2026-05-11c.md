## Executive Summary

This cluster is the catalogue's most ambitious *cartographic* deployment to date — three content tiers (topics / concepts / voids) and an apex-adjacent set of methodological commitments wired together by a single articulating spine (the mind-side × physical-side thickness grid). What makes the cluster distinctive is not the new mechanism-costs article in isolation but the *load-bearing role* the grid now plays across five previously-independent articles: each of the five was written before the grid existed, each is now legible *as a cell-specific argument*, and none required substantive body changes to absorb the new framing — the cross-tier integration pass at 22:50 UTC was reciprocal-link-only across all five. That is a strong signal of *retrospective coherence*: the catalogue had already been doing four-quadrant work without having named the axes, and the new article makes that work visible. The most striking structural achievement is that the mechanism-costs article *explicitly declines* the temptation to argue any cell is correct — section "What the Mechanism Lens Reveals" closes "Nothing in this article argues that any cell is *wrong*. The strongest downstream opportunity is an apex-tier synthesis tying the four-quadrant taxonomy + mechanism-costs + parsimony case + interface-problem into a single navigable structure under the rubric *dualism cartography*, which is currently distributed across four topic-tier articles without an apex anchor.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)

Substantial purchase across the cluster, with the mechanism-costs article supplying the catalogue's sharpest deployment of Chalmers's "category-of-fact" discipline applied to *thickness-as-explanatory-cost*. The article's Q2 reading at lines 78–86 — that under the strict reading "the mind owes *no* causation account at all, because it does no causal work," paired with the observation that "any Q2 reading that wants to preserve mental causation drifts toward Q1 by adding indeterminacy or toward Q4 by enriching the mental side" — is Chalmers's Type-E classification turned into a *structural* exclusion rather than a labelling exercise: Q2 strict is not a stable cell for anyone who wants causal efficacy. Chalmers's own programme has gestured toward this without consolidating it; the mechanism-costs article makes the move structural by tying causation, interface, and conservation debts to a single thickness coordinate.

The parsimony article's "Three Unavoidable Debts" section (lines 70–80) extends Chalmers's hard-problem discipline at the meta-architectural register. The trio — brute emergence, error theory, conceptual gap — corresponds precisely to Chalmers's own Type-B / Illusionist / Mysterian taxonomy, and the article's framing that "interactionist dualism avoids all three" by adding "psychophysical laws connecting mental and physical domains" reads Chalmers's psychophysical-laws proposal as the *parsimony purchase* rather than the parsimony cost. The follow-up paragraph at line 78 — that dualism's psychophysical laws "fan out once made specific" into a substructure of sub-questions (domain, functional form, determinism, conservation, pairing problem, ground) — is the modesty-argument discipline at its sharpest and Chalmers's own framework needs this granularity.

The interface-problem article's pre-Keplerian framing at lines 130–132 ("Newton had precise measurements and a mathematical law; the specification problem has neither. The honest comparison is pre-Keplerian — a research programme awaiting its founding measurements rather than a mature science awaiting refinement") is Chalmers-discipline at its most disciplined: the article does not claim Chalmers's "fundamental laws of consciousness" exist, only that the *scaffolding from which a Kepler's law might one day be inferred* is being assembled. Chalmers would recognise this as the proper calibration of an unfinished programme.

The conservation-laws article supplies Chalmers's framework with the precise statistical-structure constraint at lines 92–94: consciousness biases which outcome actualises while preserving total energy because biasing among equal-energy alternatives adds no energy. The article's positioning of this as *the fifth of five mathematical constraints* (conservation law compatibility) reads the constraint at the right register — not as a hostage payment to physicalism but as a structural feature any psychophysical law must respect.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)

Load-bearing purchase across the cluster, with mechanism-costs supplying the catalogue's sharpest *Stapp-as-Q4-with-narrow-channel* reading and the conservation-laws article supplying the calibrated quantum-Zeno engagement.

Mechanism-costs at line 104: "Stapp's framework places a thick observer — a full agent with selection power — and a thick quantum ontology — full state-vector realism with potentialities treated as real — on either side of a narrow selection channel; thickness on both ends, parsimony on the contact." This is the cleanest framework-level reading of Stapp's programme the catalogue has produced. Stapp's own writing emphasises the narrowness of the agent's "process-1 choice of a probing action" without always making explicit that the *narrow channel* is compatible with *thick endpoints* — the article's framing separates those two dimensions and shows that Stapp's programme operates them independently. Stapp would recognise the reading as his own programme correctly classified.

The conservation-laws article's quantum-Zeno passage at lines 99–106 is Stapp-discipline at the right calibration. The article installs Stapp's mechanism, names the experimental confirmation of the Zeno effect ("requires no speculative new physics"), and then in the same paragraph names Georgiev's Monte Carlo timescale objections without inflating Stapp's response. The decoherence-objection passage at lines 113–121 then *separates* Stapp's specific mechanism from the broader selection framework: "the broader selection framework does not stand or fall with Stapp's specific mechanism. The framework's core claim is more general: consciousness biases *which outcome* occurs when superposition resolves." This is the discipline Stapp's programme is sometimes criticised for not always practising — distinguishing the strongest version of the framework from its mechanism-specific elaboration.

The interface-problem article's hierarchical model at lines 96–98 ("constrained pluralism adds direction: attention-motor architecture all the way down, molecular level as substrate. Consciousness acts 'at the functional level, through the molecular level'") gives Stapp's quantum-mechanism work an explicit place within a wider architectural framework. The four-candidate enumeration treats ion channels (Stapp/Schwartz) as "the most physically specific account" while honestly naming "the timescale objection persists" — neither overselling nor dismissing.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)

Substantial purchase on three articles, with parsimony supplying the cluster's most disciplined first-person-as-datum argument.

The parsimony article's restructured "Interactionism Over Epiphenomenalism" section (lines 95–106) — distinguishing parallelist epiphenomenalism (where the coincidence objection bites) from supervenience epiphenomenalism (where the coincidence objection loses its grip) — is Nagel-discipline at its most calibrated. The article does not let the standard parsimony argument do its work uniformly across epiphenomenalist variants. Against supervenience variants the case is properly first-personal: "phenomenal properties [it preserves] are explanatorily idle… This conflicts with first-person evidence of mental efficacy: the sense that thirst contributes to reaching for water, not merely accompanies it. Interactionism accommodates that evidence at face value; supervenience epiphenomenalism must treat it as an artefact of the subvenient physical process." This is Nagel's discipline at the right register — the first-personal phenomenon is not adduced as evidence the physicalist must accept but as a datum the framework that takes it at face value can preserve while the alternative explains it away.

The conservation-laws article at lines 156: "the positive case for consciousness affecting brain states comes from philosophical arguments — the self-stultification of epiphenomenalism, the explanatory gap, the cognitive differences between conscious and unconscious processing — not from physics" is Nagel's separation of *first-person datum* from *third-person observation*. The framework's evidential weight is correctly located at the philosophical-argument level, not at the physics-experiment level.

The mechanism-costs article at line 144 preserves Nagel's distinction at the cartographic register: "A reader who does not share the Map's tenets is free to read Q2 strict's escape from the mechanism question as a virtue rather than as an evasion; a reader who shares the tenets sees the same evasion as foreclosed." The framing — the taxonomy speaks, the tenets adjudicate — preserves Nagel's insight that what counts as a *problem to be solved* depends on what counts as a *phenomenon to be respected*.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)

Substantial purchase across the cluster, with the mechanism-costs article supplying the cleanest *non-substance-dualist* reading of dualism the catalogue has produced. The article's grid does not commit to res-cogitans–res-extensa substance dualism in any cell — Q1 inhabitants are "delegatory" or "minimal interactionist," Q3 is "max-mind / min-physical" without naming a substance, Q4 includes both Cartesian substance dualism *and* Aristotelian–Thomistic hylomorphism *and* Stapp's process-friendly approach. The framework is by construction agnostic between substance and process readings of the mind side.

The four-quadrant taxonomy's "Limits of the Thickness Metaphor" subsection (lines 70–72) is Whitehead-discipline at the methodological grain: "Thickness is a family of related but distinguishable measures: structural richness… causal work… counterfactual role… channel width. The taxonomy tracks structural richness above the minimum each side's proponents are forced to concede." This is the process-philosophical instinct to *not collapse multiple metaphysical dimensions onto a single axis* operating at the taxonomy's foundations.

The conservation-laws article's "selection without injection" framework (lines 79–98) is Whitehead-friendly: consciousness operates *through* an event-structure (measurement / actualisation) rather than as a force pushing on a substance. Whitehead would recognise the actualisation event as ontologically primary — exactly the discipline the article installs.

**Persona-constraint observation**: this cluster is one of the few in the catalogue where Whitehead-friendliness could have been used to argue for a tier-upgrade — the grid invites it (the Q3/process-monism boundary), the parsimony article invites it (the Maxwell analogy could be deployed as "process-philosophical expansion deserves the same charity"), the conservation article invites it (selection-without-injection is process-friendly). The cluster *does not* deploy Whitehead-friendliness as warrant for any specific cell's elevation. The mechanism-costs article's section "What the Mechanism Lens Reveals" closes "Nothing in this article argues that any cell is wrong" — a pure cartographic verdict that the Hardline Empiricist persona will praise from a different axis.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)

Substantial purchase across three articles, with the conservation-laws article supplying the cluster's most disciplined libertarian-friendly argument.

The conservation-laws article at lines 89–95 — "consciousness selects among quantum-permitted outcomes without adding energy. All possible outcomes are already present in the wavefunction with their associated energies. When consciousness biases which outcome actualises, it doesn't inject anything new — it determines which of the pre-existing possibilities becomes real" — is Kane's libertarianism made precise. Kane's worry is that quantum indeterminacy alone gives randomness, not agency; the selection framework gives agency *through* indeterminacy without requiring agency to violate physics. The article's pairing of this with Tenet 4 (No Many Worlds) at line 95 ("under the Many-Worlds interpretation, every outcome occurs in a parallel branch and there is nothing to select") preserves Kane's *indexical realism* about which choice is actually made.

The interface-problem article's "Bidirectional Interaction" passage at lines 152: "the dissociation in dopamine-deficient mice (motor capacity intact, goal-directed initiative absent) is a cognitive-functional finding consistent with the framework's distinction between neural computation and conscious selection, without itself establishing that distinction" is Kane-discipline at its calibrated register — the empirical correlate is named as *consistent with* rather than as *establishing* the libertarian commitment. Kane's programme has sometimes overclaimed from neural-correlate findings; the article shows how the framework can name the correlate without that overclaim.

The mechanism-costs article's Q1 framing at lines 64–74 gives Kane's libertarianism a precise home: a cell that pays an authority debt and an interface debt but not a conservation debt is exactly the structural space Kane's programme requires. The article's framing of Saad's delegatory dualism — "experience adds only causal authority, not subliminal content" — is Kane's libertarian agency *without* substance-inflation.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)

Load-bearing purchase across the cluster, with the interface-formalization-void article supplying the catalogue's strongest McGinn-discipline piece to date.

The interface-formalization-void's central argument at lines 48–54 — "Mathematics describes structure (relations, symmetries, quantities). The consciousness-physics interface involves something non-structural (actuality, phenomenal experience). Therefore, mathematics cannot fully describe the interface" — is McGinn's cognitive-closure argument deployed at exactly the right scope. McGinn argued *human minds* are cognitively closed to the solution; the void article restricts the claim more carefully to *mathematical formalism*, which is in some ways stronger (it applies to any cognition that uses mathematical structure) and in other ways more modest (it does not foreclose non-formal understanding). The article at line 105: "this does not mean we cannot understand it at all — but it means that whatever understanding we reach will not take the form of equations" preserves McGinn's humility while not committing to McGinn's complete closure thesis. McGinn would recognise this as a calibrated improvement on his framework.

The Born rule passage at lines 56–64 — "the Born rule thus marks a structural ceiling… mathematics describes the statistical envelope perfectly. But the actualisation event… is exactly where the formalism falls silent" — is McGinn's framework operating at its sharpest grain. The void is not the empty space *outside* mathematics; it is the *event* mathematics surrounds without entering.

The mechanism-costs article's cartographic-rather-than-adjudicative framing (line 48) is McGinn-discipline at the methodological register: the article maps what each cell owes without claiming to know which cell is correct. The Q3 passage at line 96 ("conservation laws become *descriptions* of mental regularities… why this regularity rather than another is a Q3-specific debt") makes explicit that even when one quadrant *dissolves* a debt, it incurs a different one at a different register. McGinn would recognise this as the right humility about *which problem is the real problem*.

The interface-problem article's "Why Specification Is Hard" section at lines 120–122 supplies three structural difficulties — the concept gap, the access problem, underdetermination — each of which McGinn's cognitive-closure argument predicts. The catalogue's framing is more disciplined than McGinn's: the access problem is named as *currently structural*, not as *permanently structural*, awaiting "detection-threshold improvements rather than reflecting a structural feature of the framework." That refinement of McGinn's closure thesis is the calibration McGinn's own framework needs.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)

This cluster is the catalogue's strongest exhibit of *tenet-coherent restraint* — the cluster identifies what tenets allow and what they exclude *without* converting those allowances into evidential upgrades. The Hardline Empiricist persona's praise is load-bearing for this review because the cluster touches three of the persona's signature concerns: parsimony rhetoric (where tier-upgrade is the standard temptation), epistemic-status framing (where pre-Keplerian self-assessment is the discipline), and the Q3/process-monism boundary (where Whitehead-friendliness invites tier-elevation).

**Praise: the cluster does not upgrade any cell on the evidential-status scale on tenet-load alone**. Mechanism-costs at line 144: "Nothing in this article argues that any cell is *wrong*. The cells differ in what they owe, not in whether they are defensible." Four-quadrant taxonomy at lines 138–140: "The Map is not committed to a specific quadrant; it is committed to a *region* of the taxonomy that excludes epiphenomenalist and idealist extremes." This is *tenet-coherent without being evidence-elevating*: the tenets remove defeaters for certain cells (Q2 strict, Q3-idealist-limit are foreclosed) without supplying positive evidence that any specific remaining cell is correct.

**Praise: the parsimony article's modesty-argument concession** (line 117): "the honest modesty comparison is therefore closer to a draw than a clean win for either side — already a significant result, since the standard parsimony argument needs modesty to deliver a clean win for physicalism. The weaker claim the Map defends here is structural… That is a modest advantage, not a decisive one. It suffices to block the modesty argument from rescuing the razor; it does not, on its own, install dualism as the obvious winner." This is the discipline of *naming exactly what the argument achieves and what it does not* — the parsimony case is a *defeater-removal* not a *demonstration*. The Hardline Empiricist's signature pattern.

**Praise: the interface-problem article's pre-Keplerian framing** at lines 130–132 is framework-stage calibration in its most explicit form. The article does not claim Newton-stage results from pre-Keplerian work. The Tycho's-measurements analogue listing — the bandwidth constraint, the Born-rule causal-consistency result, the theta-band willed-attention signatures — is precisely what *scaffolding without laws* looks like, named as such.

**Praise: the conservation-laws article's symmetry concession** at line 67: "The dialectical situation is symmetric. The conservation objection assumes from the start that no external influences exist — precisely what it is supposed to prove. But equally, the dualist who invokes 'external influence' assumes that consciousness *is* non-physical and causally efficacious — precisely what the physicalist disputes." This is the cluster's strongest *Mode Two engagement* (defective on its own terms, both sides) — the article does not allow the dualist response to congeal into a victory. Both sides beg each other's question; conservation cannot adjudicate. That is the right register for a defeater-removal argument and the Hardline Empiricist will recognise it.

**Praise: the interface-problem article's "currently unfalsifiable in practice" framing** at line 122 *as a concession*, not as a virtue. The article does not present unfalsifiability as evidence of profundity; it names it as a real cost awaiting instrumentation. Coupled with the falsification-roadmap cross-link at line 140, the framing turns the unfalsifiability concession into a *research programme* rather than a *philosophical hedge*.

**Praise: the four-quadrant taxonomy's articulability framing** at line 145 ("the question is about the availability of an argument, not dynamical-system forces on a position — the 'stability' framing imports connotations that can mislead") is the catalogue's most explicit *non-dynamical-systems metaphor discipline*. The article was previously framed as "Q1 stability" — a dynamical-systems metaphor that could be misread as making empirical claims about real systems. The rewrite to "Q1 articulability" preserves the structural concern (can the position be specified non-stipulatively?) without inheriting the unwarranted physical-systems connotations.

**Praise: tenet-as-evidence-upgrade is a praise-worthy thing the cluster does *not* do**. The cluster had multiple structural opportunities to argue that the Map's region is *correct* rather than merely *coherent*: parsimony could have argued Q1 is uniquely cheap; mechanism-costs could have argued Q1 is uniquely articulable; the interface-problem could have argued the constrained-pluralism architecture is uniquely supported. Each article declines the opportunity. The mechanism-costs article's closing — "the taxonomy speaks; the tenets adjudicate" — is the discipline in its most compact form.

**Convergence with the Process Philosopher**: this cluster is one of the rare exhibits in the catalogue where both personas can praise the same passages for different reasons without conflict. The Process Philosopher praises Q3's preservation as a *possible coherent cell* (the mind-side can be thick without crude substance dualism); the Hardline Empiricist praises the cluster's refusal to upgrade Q3 from "possible coherent cell" to "established" or "supported" on tenet-load alone. Both personas converge on the same framing: tenet-coherent at the metaphysical-possibility register, not evidence-elevating at the empirical register.
