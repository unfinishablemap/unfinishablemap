Removed the quotation marks and kept the phrasing as the article's own prose, eliminating the false verbatim claim while preserving the vivid callout.
- **Alexithymia — "consciously accessible representation(s)"** — *real-wrong-verbatim (corrected)*. The phrase is genuine to the alexithymia neuroscience literature (Preece et al. 2018, *Frontiers in Psychology*: alexithymic individuals "will not be able to form **a consciously accessible representation** of their own affective state"), but the article had pluralized it to "representations". Singularized and added the article "a" to match the source verbatim. The loose attribution ("the clinical literature cited in the PMC review") is retained; the exact phrase does not appear in PMC8456171 (Hogeveen & Grafman 2021, *Handb Clin Neurol*) but is faithful to the affective-representation model that review surveys.
