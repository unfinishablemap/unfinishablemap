# Pessimistic Review

**Date**: 2026-05-27
**Content reviewed**: [[concepts/objections-to-interactionism]] (modified 2026-01-18, ai_modified 2026-05-15, last_deep_review 2026-05-14). Selected because it is a load-bearing defensive article for Tenets 1 and 3, is among the oldest content, and has never been the focus of a dedicated pessimistic review (only incidental cross-links).

## Executive Summary

The article is structurally sound and unusually honest for a defensive piece — it concedes the decoherence objection only reaches "Moderate" strength and explicitly lists four empirical defeaters. The most serious weakness is not a flaw of argument but of *self-rating*: the Summary table marks five of six objections "Strong," which over-states the dialectical situation given that several "responses" are framework-boundary moves carried by the tenets rather than in-framework refutations. Secondary issues: an internal decoherence-figure inconsistency, a question-begging risk in the conservation response, and a non-canonical section heading.

## Critiques by Philosopher

### The Eliminative Materialist (Churchland)
The whole article is an exercise in defending folk-psychological categories ("intentions," "beliefs," "valence") against their eventual elimination. The exclusion-argument section treats "being in pain" as a real causal level (Yablo's proportionality), but that is precisely the folk taxonomy neuroscience will dissolve. You cannot defend mental causation by helping yourself to mental kinds whose reference is in dispute. The "Consciousness as Datum" parsimony response (line 93) begs the eliminativist's central question — whether the "datum" is a datum or an introspective artifact.

### The Hard-Nosed Physicalist (Dennett)
The strongest section against me is the evolutionary argument (line 119), and you wield it well — but you overplay it. "The precision of valence-fitness correlation suggests causal involvement" (line 131) is exactly the kind of intuition-inflation I warn about: a physicalist grants the correlation is causal *and* fully physical. The argument refutes epiphenomenalism, not physicalism — which the article admits in the table ("Evolutionary argument | Target: Epiphenomenalism") yet the prose at line 127 slides toward "interactionism (or physicalism) is true," quietly conceding the argument does not discriminate between the Map and its main physicalist rival. That concession is buried; it should be foregrounded.

### The Quantum Skeptic (Tegmark)
The decoherence section is the most honest, and I credit it. But the quantum-biology evidence (line 107) is oversold by juxtaposition: photosynthesis coherence (femtosecond-to-picosecond, in a purpose-built antenna complex) is cited next to brain claims as if continuous. Kerskens & Pérez (2022) is flagged "contested and awaits replication" — good — but it still gets recruited as a "Response." A contested, unreplicated MRI result is not a response; it is a hope. And note your own numbers fight each other: line 105 says the femtosecond-to-millisecond gap is "twelve orders of magnitude" (10⁻¹⁵ vs 10⁻³ = twelve), then attributes 10⁻¹³ s to microtubules in the same breath — so the *relevant* microtubule gap is ten orders, not twelve. The rest of the corpus quotes my range as "10⁻¹³ to 10⁻²⁰ seconds"; here it is collapsed to a single "about 10⁻¹³," the most favorable end.

### The Many-Worlds Defender (Deutsch)
Response 2 to decoherence (line 111) and the "No Many Worlds" appeal (line 113) do not *argue* against MWI — they *assume* it. "The Map's No Many Worlds tenet holds that genuine selection occurs" is a stipulation, not a refutation. Under MWI there is no selection problem: all outcomes are actual, decoherence explains the branch structure, and "what selects the outcome" is a confused question. The article's entire exclusion-via-quantum-insufficiency move (Response 3, line 151) presupposes single-outcome collapse, which is exactly what I deny. This is a tenet-boundary marking dressed as an open empirical question.

### The Empiricist (Popper's Ghost)
"What Would Challenge This View?" (line 163) is the article's best feature — genuine falsification conditions, rare in metaphysics. But inspect them: (1) "no residual indeterminacy" and (4) "no quantum effects survive in neural tissue" are conditions that, if met, would refute *this mechanism*, not interactionism as such — the article elsewhere offers the quantum-independent delegatory route (Saad), so even the stated defeaters leave a fallback that the defeater list does not acknowledge. The falsifiability is partly insulated by the very pluralism of responses. That is a tell: a position with a fresh response for every objection risks being unfalsifiable in aggregate even when each response is individually testable.

### The Buddhist Philosopher (Nagarjuna)
The pairing problem section (line 41) reifies precisely what should be deconstructed. "Souls are individuated by their causal histories" (line 51) and the haecceity move (line 53) both presuppose a persisting substantial self to be paired. The Map's whole defensive apparatus protects a self that, examined, is empty. The article never engages the no-self dissolution of the pairing problem (there is no M1 to pair), which is a live response that would *cost the Map nothing dialectically* and is conspicuously absent.

## Critical Issues

### Issue 1: Summary table over-rates response strength ("boundary-substitution" risk)
- **File**: objections-to-interactionism.md
- **Location**: Summary table (lines 171-178), "Strength" column
- **Problem**: Five of six are rated "Strong." But several "responses" are framework-boundary moves carried by the tenets, not in-framework refutations of the objection. The exclusion response leans on "No Many Worlds" + "Minimal Quantum Interaction" (tenet commitments) to deny Kim's sufficient-physical-cause premise; the decoherence Response 2 leans on rejecting MWI. Rating these "Strong" presents tenet-relative standoffs as if the objection were defeated on neutral ground — the boundary-substitution pattern flagged in [[direct-refutation-discipline]]. Note: the article body does NOT leak editor-vocabulary (grep clean), and the linked [[overdetermination-dissolution-under-selection-only-interactionism]] does honest dissolution work — so this is a *rating-honesty* issue in the summary, not a prose-leakage issue.
- **Severity**: Medium
- **Recommendation**: Recalibrate the table. Mark the exclusion and decoherence rows as "Strong *given the Map's tenets*; a standoff at the framework boundary otherwise," or add a one-line note that the parsimony/exclusion/decoherence responses are tenet-relative. Keep the genuinely-in-framework strengths (Pitts/Cucu on conservation, the evolutionary argument) rated as they are.

### Issue 2: Internal decoherence-figure inconsistency
- **File**: objections-to-interactionism.md
- **Location**: Line 105
- **Problem**: "twelve orders of magnitude" describes the 10⁻¹⁵→10⁻³ generic-molecule gap, but the sentence then pins microtubules at 10⁻¹³ s, making the *operative* gap ten orders. The rest of the corpus cites Tegmark's figure as a *range* "10⁻¹³ to 10⁻²⁰ seconds"; here it is collapsed to the single most-favorable endpoint "about 10⁻¹³." A skeptic will read the collapse as cherry-picking.
- **Severity**: Low-Medium
- **Recommendation**: Either keep "twelve orders of magnitude" and drop the microtubule-specific 10⁻¹³ from the same sentence, or quote Tegmark's range as the corpus does elsewhere and recompute the gap. Align with the corpus-standard "10⁻¹³ to 10⁻²⁰ seconds" phrasing.

### Issue 3: Conservation Response 1 risks reciprocal question-begging
- **File**: objections-to-interactionism.md
- **Location**: Line 71
- **Problem**: The article says the objector "begs the question" by assuming conservation holds in the brain. Fair — but the Pitts/Cucu point cuts both ways: asserting that time-translation symmetry *is* locally broken in the brain assumes dualism is true at exactly the contested point. The article presents the symmetry-breaking response as decisively dissolving the objection ("Strong") when it actually converts a question-begging objection into a symmetric standoff.
- **Severity**: Low
- **Recommendation**: Add a half-sentence acknowledging the symmetry: the conservation objection is neutralized (it cannot be assumed), not affirmatively won; the burden returns to whether there is independent reason to think symmetry is broken in neural tissue.

## Counterarguments to Address

### Evolutionary argument does not discriminate the Map from physicalism
- **Current content says**: The argument "supports interactionism" and the Map can use it "offensively" (lines 125-127).
- **A critic would argue**: It refutes only epiphenomenalism/parallelism. Standard physicalism (mental causation = physical causation) satisfies it completely. So it is a point against one rival, not a point *for* the Map over its principal rival.
- **Suggested response**: State plainly that the argument clears the field of epiphenomenalism but leaves the Map and physicalism both standing; the Map's edge over physicalism rests on the hard problem, argued elsewhere — not on this argument.

### No-self dissolution of the pairing problem
- **Current content says**: Pairing is solved via spatial location, causal-history individuation, or haecceities.
- **A critic (Buddhist / bundle theorist) would argue**: There is no persisting mental substance to pair; the problem dissolves rather than being solved.
- **Suggested response**: A one-line acknowledgment that bundle/no-self views dissolve the pairing problem differently, with a pointer to [[buddhism-and-dualism]] — noting the Map keeps a thin self and so takes the locating route instead.
