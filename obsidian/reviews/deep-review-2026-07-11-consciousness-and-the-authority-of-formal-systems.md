**1. Clean double-counting of a single argument as "converging support" (the load-bearing defect).**
The lead (§ opening) correctly diagnoses the normative gap (logic) and the structural gap (mathematics) as *"two faces of the same limitation: formal systems govern form, and consciousness is not exhausted by form."* But the Dualism subsection then wrote that these *"twin limitations ... provide **converging support** for dualism."* You cannot have both: if the two gaps are two faces of ONE thesis (structure/form ≠ intrinsic phenomenal character, applied to two domains), they are not independent convergent evidence — they are one move applied twice, and "converging support" double-counts it. This is exactly the illusory-independence pattern the corpus discounts elaborately in [[the-convergence-argument-for-dualism]] ("five arguments within one cluster contribute little more than one strong argument from it"), [[epistemology-of-convergence-arguments]] ("independence must be assessed at the level of *deep assumptions*, not surface premises"), and [[cross-mechanism-convergence]] — machinery this article applied to *nothing about itself*.

- **Before**: *"The twin limitations of formal systems ... provide converging support for dualism. If logical necessity is real and mathematical structure does not exhaust reality, then there are features of the world that physical description cannot capture. Consciousness, as the medium through which necessity becomes manifest and quality exists, involves irreducible properties."*
- **After**: reframed to state the two gaps *"both trace to a single move: structure and form do not exhaust intrinsic phenomenal character ... two faces of one limitation, not two independent lines whose agreement compounds."* Self-applies the corpus discipline (deep-assumption independence; lines sharing one deep assumption do not screen off from each other), cross-links [[epistemology-of-convergence-arguments]], [[the-convergence-argument-for-dualism]], [[cross-mechanism-convergence]], and concludes the two gaps *"carry the force of **one** argument shown in two domains ... not a convergence bonus"* — while affirming that single argument is substantial and the dualist reading is intact. **Calibration honesty, not retraction of the dualist reading.** Diagnostic test: a reviewer who fully accepts the Map's tenets would still flag "converging support" as count-inflation → correctable, therefore CRITICAL, not bedrock disagreement.
