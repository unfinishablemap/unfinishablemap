Three earlier passes today mined the QM-interpretation and materialist-position clusters (Cramer, Bohm, Sellars, type-identity, Kripke, Penfield are all now live or queued). Two rival-position gaps complete the harvest: **compatibilism** as a positive definiendum (34 files, but only challenge- and theory-specific pages exist, never the position itself), and **the extended mind thesis** (Clark & Chalmers; 12 files, three subsections, load-bearing author, direct interface-boundary relevance). A fourth medium candidate — the **language-of-thought / representational theory of mind** cluster — is noted with a redundancy caveat. All four are rival framings and analogies, not boundary-case organisms, so none carries five-tier tier-upgrade risk; the Process-Philosopher / Hardline-Empiricist tension does not fire on this material.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The anti-materialist case is kept structurally honest: `zombie-master-argument`, `dualism`, and `explanatory-gap` treat the case as a *bundle* of mutually reinforcing routes rather than a single knockdown, exactly Chalmers' methodological point. And the Map engages the strongest reductionist reply — the vitalism analogy, that consciousness will dissolve as *élan vital* did — head-on in `reductionism.md` and `mysterianism.md` rather than pretending it away. Chalmers would note only that this best reductionist argument deserves a positioned home to point at, not three scattered subsections.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
The quantum-foundations shelf states a favoured interpretation and volunteers its own unfalsifiability as a cost. Nothing new to flag here that the 07-09 quantum-foundations pass did not already cover (superdeterminism remains the highest-value already-named gap; see below — I am *not* re-nominating it).

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
First-person structure retains genuine evidential weight (microphenomenology, contemplative-practice-as-evidence, the second-person channel). The extended-mind material, wherever it appears, never lets the *subject* leak out to the notebook — the phenomenal boundary and the cognitive boundary are kept distinct, which is exactly the discipline Nagel would demand of an extended-mind treatment.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
`bi-aspectual-ontology`, `process-philosophy`, and `the-steelman-for-process-monism` preserve mind's reality without a ghost-in-the-machine. **Evidential-status note**: none of the subjects named below is a minimal-organism tier claim — they are a historical analogy (vitalism), a free-will rival position (compatibilism), and a philosophy-of-mind thesis about cognitive boundaries (extended mind). The Process-Philosopher / Hardline-Empiricist tension does not fire; the two personas converge.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
The free-will cluster earns its libertarianism honestly, conceding that its moral conclusions are matched by sophisticated compatibilism and then explaining what distinguishing work remains. Kane would press exactly one point: the cluster has built its *incompatibilist-side* apparatus (consequence argument, manipulation argument, Frankfurt cases, reactive attitudes — all now live) but still lacks a positioned treatment of *compatibilism itself*, the rival its whole agency claim is defined against. You cannot fully earn libertarianism until the rival it beats has a name and a home.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
`mysterianism.md` is appropriately humble and even hosts the vitalism analogy as a subsection — which is precisely why the analogy deserves promotion: it is currently load-bearing inside a page about something else. McGinn would appreciate that the Map treats "dualism might go the way of vitalism" as a live epistemic possibility to answer rather than a slur to wave off.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
The register-separation between "the tenets remove a defeater" and "we have positive evidence" holds across the boundary-organism articles this session's deep reviews touched. On *this* review's material the discipline is different but equally real: the corpus does not over-claim that the vitalism analogy *fails* — `type-specificity.md`'s "Vitalism Disanalogy" argues the specific structural point (that the *élan vital* case had a mechanism-shaped hole consciousness does not), which is the honest move rather than a blanket dismissal. **Tenet-as-evidence-upgrade is praise-worthily *not* done here**: the Map resists the temptation to treat "vitalism was wrong to be reduced, therefore dualism is safe" as a symmetry it is *owed*; it argues the disanalogy case by case. Naming vitalism in one page would let that discipline be stated once, cleanly, rather than re-derived in every article that meets the objection.

### High Priority

#### 1. Vitalism (and the "dualism will go the way of *élan vital*" pessimistic meta-induction)
- **Target section**: `concepts/`
- **Cluster / homeless gap**: This is the single most-invoked *analogy against dualism* in the whole corpus — the charge that consciousness-dualism is the modern *élan vital* and will be reduced away as vitalism was. It appears in **17 files** and is given **three distinct heading-level treatments as a guest inside other pages**: `reductionism.md` ("## The Vitalism Objection"), `mysterianism.md` ("### The Vitalism Analogy"), and `type-specificity.md` ("## The Vitalism Disanalogy {#vitalism}"). It also threads `emergence-as-universal-hard-problem`, `the-strong-emergence-of-consciousness`, `cognitive-science-of-dualism`, `epistemic-advantages-of-dualism`, `the-convergence-argument-for-dualism`, and `bergson-and-duration` (Bergson's *élan vital* is the historical root). **No `vitalism.md` exists.** The Map's answer to the objection is currently re-derived in every article that meets it.
- **Map relevance / tenet tie**: Directly load-bearing for **Tenet 1** (the reducibility question) and **Tenet 5** (Occam's-razor-has-limits — the vitalism analogy is precisely a *parsimony-plus-induction* argument that the Map's fifth tenet is built to answer). A dedicated page would (a) define vitalism and *élan vital* / entelechy historically, (b) state the pessimistic meta-induction cleanly ("every previous mind-adjacent mystery was reduced, so consciousness will be too"), and (c) consolidate the Map's disanalogy reply (the *élan vital* case had a *mechanism-shaped hole* — a functional deficit later filled by biochemistry — whereas the hard problem is a *phenomenal* residue no mechanism could fill). This is the conspicuous missing member of the anti-reductionism cluster: `reductionism`, `emergence`, `explanatory-gap`, `galilean-exclusion`, and `the-strong-emergence-of-consciousness` all have pages; the historical analogy they all must rebut does not.

#### 2. Compatibilism (the positive rival position — Hume, Frankfurt, Dennett, Fischer)
- **Target section**: `concepts/`
- **Cluster / homeless gap**: The free-will shelf is mature on the *incompatibilist* side — `event-causal-libertarianism`, `agent-causation`, `the-consequence-argument-for-incompatibilism`, `the-manipulation-argument-and-hard-incompatibilism`, `frankfurt-cases-and-the-principle-of-alternate-possibilities`, `source-versus-leeway-incompatibilism`, `reactive-attitudes-and-strawsonian-responsibility` — and it hosts *specific compatibilist machinery* in `compatibilist-symmetry-challenge` and `frankfurt-hierarchical-mesh-theory-of-the-will`. But **compatibilism as a positioned definiendum has no page.** The word appears in **34 files**; the only heading-level treatments are a *challenge* (`compatibilist-symmetry-challenge`, the opposite pole) and Fischer's *semicompatibilism* as a subsection of `frankfurt-cases`. No `compatibilism.md`.
- **Note vs prior reviews**: The 07-09 free-will pass named the *incompatibilist arguments the Map agrees with* (consequence, manipulation, Frankfurt, Strawson's basic argument) and flagged an "incompatibilism overview" hub — all now largely built. It did **not** name compatibilism-the-position. This is the symmetric, still-open gap: the Map is a libertarian/dualist site whose agency claim is defined *against* compatibilism, yet the rival it beats has no home to be beaten in.
- **Map relevance / tenet tie**: Directly load-bearing for **Tenet 3** (bidirectional interaction / genuine mental causation). A dedicated page would define classical (Hume/Ayer conditional-analysis), hierarchical (Frankfurt), reasons-responsive (Fischer & Ravizza), and neo-compatibilist (Dennett, List) variants, and give `free-will`, `moral-responsibility`, and `compatibilist-symmetry-challenge` a single positioned rival to cite instead of scattered subsections.

### Medium Priority

#### 3. The extended mind thesis (Clark & Chalmers — the parity principle and Otto's notebook)
- **Target section**: `concepts/`
- **Cluster / homeless gap**: The situated-cognition cluster is well built — `embodied-cognition`, `consciousness-epistemology-extended-cognition`, `enactivism-challenge-to-interactionist-dualism`, `sensorimotor-contingencies-and-the-interface` — but the *canonical* Clark & Chalmers "extended mind" thesis (the 1998 parity principle, Otto's notebook, active externalism) is a **guest in three pages** (`consciousness-epistemology-extended-cognition.md` "## The Extended Mind Thesis", `embodied-cognition.md` "## The Extended Mind Debate", `brain-specialness-boundary.md` "### Brain-Computer Interfaces and the Extended Mind") across **12 files**, with no dedicated home. This is also a **load-bearing-author gap** — it is Chalmers' own thesis, and Chalmers is the corpus's most-cited friendly author.
- **Map relevance / tenet tie**: Unusually direct for the interface framework. The extended-mind thesis is a *rival answer to the Map's own central question* — where does the mind stop? — bearing on `brain-interface-boundary`, `brain-specialness-boundary`, and `mind-brain-separation`. Under interactionist dualism the interface is where consciousness couples to the physical; the extended-mind thesis argues the *cognitive* boundary extends past the skull into notebooks and devices. Disambiguating the *phenomenal* boundary (which the Map locates at the interface) from the *cognitive/functional* boundary (which extended mind pushes outward) is a genuinely clarifying page. Confidence is medium rather than high only because the three existing subsections already carry real content — the marginal value is consolidation and a positioned home for a rival, not a wholly untouched gap.
