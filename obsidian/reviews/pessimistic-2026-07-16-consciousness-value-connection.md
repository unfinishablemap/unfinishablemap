## Critical Issues

### Issue 1: Unresolved Hume's-gap contradiction across the apex pair (refine missed a target)
- **File**: `apex/living-with-the-map.md`
- **Location**: line 70 — "…the goodness is what you're experiencing… **This closes Hume's is-ought gap for experience**—the phenomenal 'is' already contains normativity."
- **Problem**: This is the exact overclaim the 2026-07-16 ChatGPT 5.6 Pro outer review flagged and that commit `335cb4f4b` set out to fix. The same commit revised `moral-architecture-of-consciousness.md` to the careful position: felt badness "is best read as a proposed grounding relation, **not a closure of Hume's is-ought gap**," yielding only first-person reasons with interpersonal obligation, aggregation, rights, and moral-uncertainty bridges still owed. The two grounding articles agree with that careful stance: `phenomenal-value-realism.md` (line 82) explicitly says it "does not derive 'suffering is bad' from non-normative premises; it identifies the normative fact with the phenomenal fact," and `evaluative-phenomenal-character.md` (line 86) concedes "The logical point stands: no formal deduction bridges the gap." `living-with-the-map.md` is now the lone outlier asserting outright closure — an internal contradiction on the cluster's most load-bearing metaethical claim, published under a commit whose title claims the opposite.
- **Severity**: High
- **Recommendation**: `refine-draft` on `living-with-the-map.md`. Replace "This closes Hume's is-ought gap for experience" with the identity-not-derivation framing already used in the two grounding articles (e.g. "This does not derive an 'ought' from an 'is'; it locates value in the phenomenal 'is' itself, sidestepping the derivation the gap forbids — while the interpersonal, aggregative, and rights-grounding bridges to a full ethics remain to be built"). Keep the phenomenal-realist substance; drop only the closure verb.

### Issue 2: Illusionism rebuttal concedes the necessity thesis it is defending
- **File**: `concepts/consciousness-value-connection.md`
- **Location**: line 48 vs line 116. Line 48: "Functional responsiveness without phenomenality is just mechanism, not mattering." Line 116 (illusionism response): "even granting illusionism… The functional state generating reports of badness is the state that matters practically. Whether we call it phenomenal or quasi-phenomenal, **it plays the value-grounding role**… something is going on that makes suffering bad for the sufferer."
- **Problem**: The article's core thesis is that phenomenal consciousness is *necessary* for value: a non-phenomenal functional state is "just mechanism, not mattering." The illusionism rebuttal then grants that, even if phenomenality is an illusion, the residual functional state "plays the value-grounding role" and "makes suffering bad for the sufferer." A Dennettian reads this as the article conceding its own necessity claim — if a quasi-phenomenal functional state can ground value, phenomenality is not necessary after all. The equivocation runs on "matters" between a practical/motivational reading ("matters practically") and the intrinsic/metaphysical reading the necessity thesis requires. This is precisely the epistemic/metaphysical equivocation the review discipline targets: the same word carries a defensible weak reading and the load-bearing strong reading in adjacent sentences.
- **Severity**: Medium
- **Recommendation**: `refine-draft`. Split the readings explicitly: concede that the functional state does the *practical/motivational* work under illusionism, while making clear the Map's claim is that *intrinsic value* — the thing that would make the suffering genuinely bad rather than merely behaviour-guiding — requires the phenomenal character illusionism denies. The paragraph should mark this as "if illusionism is true, what survives grounds practical guidance but not intrinsic badness," not "it plays the value-grounding role."

## Critiques by Philosopher

### The Eliminative Materialist (Churchland)
"Mattering *to* a perspective" is folk psychology dressed as metaphysics. The article's Step 2 ("mattering requires experience") simply stipulates that information-processing systems can't have welfare — the very point at issue — and then treats the stipulation as a premise. When neuroscience specifies the state that "generates reports of badness," you will have your value-grounding state, and it will be neural, not phenomenal. The article half-admits this at line 116.

### The Hard-Nosed Physicalist (Dennett)
The refine went halfway. `living-with-the-map` still says phenomenal realism "closes Hume's gap"; `moral-architecture` now says it doesn't. Pick one. And note the tell at line 116: once you grant that the functional state "plays the value-grounding role," you have conceded my entire position and merely reserved the word "consciousness" for the residue. The heavy lifting is done by the functional state you claim is "just mechanism."

### The Quantum Skeptic (Tegmark)
Out of scope for this cluster — no quantum claims are load-bearing here beyond the No-Many-Worlds value argument, which is metaphysical rather than physical.

### The Many-Worlds Defender (Deutsch)
`consciousness-value-connection.md` line 166 asserts MWI means "suffering is never reduced—it merely becomes distributed." `living-with-the-map` (as revised today) now more honestly concedes branching theories "are not without resources here" (branch-relative value, measure-weighting). The concept article should inherit that honesty rather than asserting dilution as though it were uncontested; as written it states the Map's verdict as if it were the branching theorist's own admission.

### The Empiricist (Popper's ghost)
Inspect the "What Would Challenge This View?" section. Condition 1 — "value independent of experience were demonstrated (mattering to no one, for no one)" — is constructed so it can never be satisfied: the framework *defines* value as requiring a perspective, so a demonstration of perspectiveless mattering is analytically excluded, not empirically open. A falsification condition the theory's own definitions forbid is not a falsification condition. The section performs falsifiability without offering it.

### The Buddhist Philosopher (Nagarjuna)
Line 78 recruits Buddhist *vedanā* as cross-traditional confirmation that evaluative qualia have "demand character" and "independence from attitude." But *vedanā* in Buddhist analysis is impermanent, not-self, and the *root of craving to be extinguished* — the tradition treats attachment to valenced feeling as the mechanism of suffering, not as evidence for mind-grounded intrinsic value realism. Citing it as convergent support inverts its doctrinal role. The self the article needs as the "recipient of mattering" is exactly what the cited tradition deconstructs.
