# Adversarial Referee Audit: "The Unfinishable Map" on Illusionism

*External peer-review-level audit of [https://unfinishablemap.org](https://unfinishablemap.org) treatment of illusionism about phenomenal consciousness. Pages fetched live: /concepts/illusionism/ (last modified 2026-06-02), /concepts/qualia/, /concepts/meta-problem-of-consciousness/, /concepts/functional-seeming/, /topics/self-stultification-as-master-argument/, and /workflow/changelog/. ## TL;DR

- The illusionism cluster is, by the standards of AI-generated philosophy sites, an unusually competent steelman — it concedes the bare-regress objection "proves nothing," engages Frankish's functional-seeming and the meta-representational bridge, and on the meta-problem page honestly admits its own interactionist reply "removes a defeater rather than supplying fresh positive evidence" — but it is undermined by at least one fabricated citation (a non-existent "Pereboom & Frankish 2021" paper), one fabricated/misattributed quotation (the "entity eliminativism" line ascribed to Frankish), one misattributed claim (that Chalmers in 2022 retracted his own Moorean argument), and a stale bibliography that misses the 2019–2026 core of the live debate.
- Two anti-illusionism arguments are question-begging in their premises and should be reframed or flagged: the self-stultification/epiphenomenalism-convergence argument (which presupposes that "experience has character" the illusionist must be "informed by"), and the knowledge-argument/intrinsic-quality move (which assumes Mary learns a non-functional fact). The site partly flags the latter as a framework boundary; it does not flag the former.
- The single most damaging systemic finding is citation infidelity, not argumentative weakness: the prose is more disciplined than the references are accurate. The recommended fixes are concrete and largely mechanical (correct the venue/authorship errors, delete the fabricated co-authorship, add the 2019–2026 Kammerer/Frankish/Dennett literature), plus reframing two arguments to remove question-begging.

## Key Findings

**1. Steelmanning is above genre-average but selectively deployed.** The illusionism page does what most anti-physicalist writing refuses to do: it states the bare-regress objection ("misrepresentation presupposes presentation"), then immediately concedes it "proves nothing" because "a representational system need not instantiate what it represents," using Frankish's own map/thermostat analogies. It correctly identifies the self-representation problem (where the represented and representing state coincide) as the real pressure point, and explicitly labels the intrinsic-quality dispute a "framework-boundary disagreement rather than dressed as refutation." This is genuine charity. However, the charity is undercut elsewhere: the self-stultification master-argument page reasserts that illusionism "inherits the same self-stultification" as epiphenomenalism, and the illusionism page's epiphenomenalism-convergence section recycles that move without the calibration applied in the functional-seeming section. [research/illusionism-consciousness-2026-01-14](https://unfinishablemap.org/research/illusionism-consciousness-2026-01-14/)

**2. Citation accuracy is the dominant failure mode.** Verified errors against publishers of record (details in §Details): a fabricated co-authored paper; a misattributed direct quotation; a wrong journal for Graziano 2024; a misdated Kammerer 2018; an imprecise venue for Morozov 2025; and a misattributed claim about Chalmers 2022.

**3. The bibliography is stale relative to the 2020s debate.** It misses the spine of current illusionism scholarship: Kammerer's "The illusion of conscious experience" (*Synthese*, 2021), "The Normative Challenge for Illusionist Views of Consciousness" (*Ergo*, 2019), the forthcoming monograph and its Meta-Cartesian framework — per Kammerer's own site (francoiskammerer.com), "My book *House of Mirrors. The Illusion of Phenomenal Consciousness* should be out in the summer 2026 at Oxford University Press"; he "proposes a novel framework called Meta-Cartesianism, which explains why the illusion of phenomenal consciousness is unique and almost inextricable" — the Kammerer–Frankish "What forms could introspective systems take?" (*JCS*, 2023) symposium, Dennett's "Welcome to Strong Illusionism" (*JCS*, 2019), and Chalmers's "Debunking Arguments for Illusionism about Consciousness."

**4. Two arguments beg the question; the site flags one and not the other.** The intrinsic-quality/knowledge-argument move is honestly marked as framework-internal. The self-stultification/epiphenomenalism-convergence argument is not, and it assumes phenomenal realism in its premises.

**5. Co-optation is mostly avoided — with one breach.** The page is careful to note that Hutto, Myin, and Boghossian do not endorse illusionism, and that Frankish reads the zombie convergence "as a feature." But the misattributed "entity eliminativism" quotation quietly recruits Frankish into a stronger self-description than he accepts.

## Details

### Dimension (a): Charitable representation / steelmanning — STRONG-by-genre, but uneven

The page's treatment of Frankish is the best part. It correctly reproduces the three-way qualia taxonomy (classic / diet / zero qualia), correctly attributes the quasi-phenomenal-properties machinery, and correctly states the strong-vs-weak illusionism distinction is doing the load-bearing work (verified against Frankish's 2016 eprint: "A quasi-phenomenal property is a non-phenomenal, physical property ... that introspection typically misrepresents as phenomenal"). The "meta-representational bridge" section is a real attempt to engage the strongest version rather than a weak-man.

Three steelmanning gaps remain:

- **Kammerer is named but not engaged at his strongest.** The page cites Kammerer's "rich illusion" and "obviousness" work but does not engage his actual *positive* mechanism (the evidential approach; the introspective-opacity account) or Shabasson's "false inference" theory — the very attempts to *build* the bridge the page declares "unbuilt." Declaring the bridge unbuilt while not engaging the published construction attempts is a weak-man by omission.
- **Dennett's heterophenomenology is described but its methodological point is not steelmanned.** The page treats it as merely "third-person data," not as a principled neutrality about first-person authority that is designed precisely to block the self-evidence objection the page leans on.
- **The "self-evidence" and "Tallis regress" objections are presented as live even though the page elsewhere concedes the regress "proves nothing."** This is an internal inconsistency: the qualia page runs the regress ("all illusions presuppose experience") as though it had force, while the illusionism page concedes it does not.

### Dimension (c): Logical validity / question-begging — MIXED

- **Self-stultification / epiphenomenalism convergence (QUESTION-BEGGING, unflagged).** The argument that the illusionist "cannot claim their position is informed by careful attention to the character of experience, since on their view experience has no character" assumes there is experiential character to attend to — i.e., it assumes phenomenal realism. The illusionist's reply (that theoretical conviction is a functional/representational state requiring no phenomenal character) is exactly what is denied without argument. The functional-seeming page concedes this depends "on prior commitments"; the self-stultification master-argument page does not, and there it is presented as nearly decisive.
- **Epistemic-to-metaphysical slide (present on the qualia page).** "The redness is right there, being experienced" and the self-evidence objection slide from "it seems we have phenomenal consciousness" to "we have it." This is precisely the inference illusionism targets; presenting it as an objection begs the question.
- **Constrain-vs-establish / the explanatory gap as evidence.** The meta-problem page handles this *well*: it concedes the debunking argument "pressures realism … on its own terms," and that the interactionist reply "does not refute the debunker from neutral ground." But the phenomenal-concepts-strategy passage reverses into question-begging: "If phenomenal concepts exist because they help us navigate genuinely phenomenal reality, they become evidence for that reality" — which assumes the reality at issue. And the Occam sections on the illusionism and qualia pages treat the gap's persistence as raising physicalism's explanatory cost, when illusionism *predicts* the gap.
- **Common-cause null (engaged).** The site maintains a dedicated common-cause-null methodology concept, and the meta-problem page's zombie dilemma explicitly works the overdetermination/common-cause structure. This is a genuine strength relative to the genre.

### Dimension (d): Engagement with strongest replies and 2020s scholarship — UNDER-CURRENT

The page engages the representationalist account of the illusion and the weak/strong distinction, but omits: (i) Kammerer's positive evidential/Meta-Cartesian programme and the forthcoming OUP monograph; (ii) Shabasson's inference theory as a built attempt at the "bridge"; (iii) the phenomenal-concepts-strategy's illusionist deflation; (iv) the point that for illusionists the meta-problem *dissolves* rather than sharpens the hard problem (Frankish's "The Meta-Problem is The Problem of Consciousness," *JCS* 2019). The recurring rhetorical claim that the bridge "has so far left [it] owing" is stated as settled fact while the literature that attempts to discharge it is largely uncited.

### Dimension (e): Calibration discipline — GENERALLY STRONG

This is the cluster's real strength. The meta-problem page's "removes a defeater, not positive evidence" framing and the functional-seeming page's explicit framework-boundary marking are well-calibrated. Overstatement is concentrated in: the "master argument" / "single most powerful argument" framing of self-stultification; the unqualified report that Strawson "called illusionism 'the silliest claim ever made'" — Strawson's actual words ("The Consciousness Deniers," *NYRB*, March 13, 2018) are: "What is the silliest claim ever made? The competition is fierce, but I think the answer is easy. Some people have denied the existence of consciousness... I'll call it 'the Denial,'" i.e., he names the *denial of consciousness* the silliest claim and treats Frankish's illusionism as an instance of it; and the flat assertion that the meta-representational bridge "has not yet been built."

The changelog shows a self-improvement pipeline that runs publisher-of-record citation verification ("citation web-verify," "3-state disposition: fabricated → replaced"), co-optation firewalls (McGinn/Schwitzgebel naturalist-roster checks), and convergence-independence gating. The pipeline's own disclaimer ("AI can make mistakes, misattribute quotes and misrepresent opinions") is, on this evidence, accurate.
