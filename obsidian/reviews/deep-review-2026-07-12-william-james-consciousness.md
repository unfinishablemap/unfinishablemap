## Pessimistic Analysis Summary

### Critical Issues Found (fixed this pass)

**"Voluntary life / chain forged" quote — verbatim error + bare-quote (fixed).** L97 rendered James's famous free-will passage as "...forged **innumerably** ages ago." The correct word is the adjective **"innumerable"** ("forged innumerable ages ago" = forged countless ages ago); "innumerably ages ago" is ungrammatical (an adverb cannot modify "ages ago") and cannot be James's text. The only web attestation of "innumerably" is unfinishablemap.org itself (self-contamination) — no independent source renders it that way. The quote is **genuine James**, from *The Principles of Psychology* (1890), Chapter XI "Attention," in the free-will discussion that concludes that chapter (Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy's James entry, Summer-2005 archived edition, quotes "the whole sting and excitement of our voluntary life… things are really being decided" and cites it to *Principles*, PP 429 — placing it in the Attention chapter, not the Will chapter). The passage was also a **bare quote** with no work/chapter reference. Fix applied: corrected "innumerably" → "innumerable" (restoring James's actual word) and added a lead-in pinning it to *The Principles of Psychology* (1890), the free-will close of the attention chapter — citation-completion for a demonstrable target.

*Note on the wording fix:* every full-text host (York psychclassics prin11, Gutenberg 57628, the archive.org 1890 scan djvu, Global Grey) truncates before Chapter XI's ending, so a direct primary *render* of the exact ending word could not be captured by the fetch tool. The correction rests on three convergent grounds: (a) SEP confirms the quote is genuine James/Principles/Attention; (b) "innumerably ages ago" is grammatically impossible in a way an unusual-but-valid preposition choice is not; (c) sole-Map attestation of "innumerably." This is a verbatim-fidelity restoration, not a re-authoring — cohort held.

### "Specious present" coinage — correctly NOT attributed to James
L57 says the specious present is "the experienced duration of the present moment, **which James helped develop**." This is accurate: James did not coin "specious present" — he credited E. R. Clay for the term (in *Principles* Ch. XV). The article does not claim James coined it, so there is no misattribution. The Clay attribution lives in [[temporal-consciousness]]. No change — flagging as verified-correct.

### "Stream of consciousness" vs "stream of thought"
The section heading and L154 ("This phrase entered general usage from James") use "stream of consciousness." James titled the *Principles* Ch. IX "The Stream of Thought" and the 1892 *Briefer Course* chapter "The Stream of Consciousness"; both phrases are his, and he popularized "stream of consciousness." The article does not misname the *Principles* chapter. No defect.
