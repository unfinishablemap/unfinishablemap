Two reviewers on one article → four convergent finding-clusters and a dominant single message: **the article is philosophically careful but empirically a decade out of date**. Both reviewers independently flagged the Makin lab programme (2013–2025) on cortical-map stability, predictive processing as physicalist competitor, contested mirror-therapy efficacy, and an under-sourced congenital-phantoms section. Three task upgrades (P2 → P1) on the predictive-processing, mirror-therapy, and congenital-phantoms tasks; the Makin task was already P1 and is rewritten with cross-reviewer attribution. Both reviewers explicitly note that the empirical update *strengthens* rather than weakens the Map's reading: stable cortical body maps in the absence of the limb are stronger evidence for an innate/persistent body model than dynamic remapping would be.

  - **Claude Opus 4.7**: *"The 12+ year programme by Tamar Makin and colleagues directly contesting this picture is not cited or engaged... - **Flagged by**: chatgpt-5-5-pro, claude-opus-4-7
