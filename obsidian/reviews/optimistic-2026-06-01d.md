## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
Chalmers would recognise his own apparatus deployed with unusual fidelity. The meta-problem concept page reproduces his 2018 definition verbatim and correctly preserves his structural claim — that *every* position, dualist included, owes an account of problem intuitions. He would especially approve that the article doesn't claim the face-value solution as a free win: it reproduces his own observed asymmetry ("if the hard problem is real, the meta-problem is expected; if illusory, it becomes urgent") and then develops realizationism as *he* developed it in the 2020 debunking-arguments paper, rather than as a dualist embellishment. The distinction the articles hold — that the metaproblem concerns *behaviour* while the hard problem concerns *being* (Frankish section) — is exactly the line Chalmers draws to resist Frankish's identification. This is the hard problem taken seriously without being inflated.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
Stapp's interest lives in the skill-acquisition article's Minimal Quantum Interaction section, which is candid in a way that serves his program better than overclaiming would. It states the Zeno-mechanism coherence-time gap quantitatively ($10^{-13}$ to $10^{-20}$ s decoherence vs. 10–30 Hz attentional phenomenology, a $10^{13}$ Hz observation-rate requirement) and explicitly separates the *architecture-level* claim (consciousness selects at neural indeterminacies) from the *implementation* debt (which quantum substrate realises it). Stapp would read this as the right division of labour: the framework survives a failed implementation candidate because the architecture is what does the work. The honest flagging of the MWI-rejection cost — that under Many-Worlds "there is nothing to select" — is precisely the kind of load-bearing assumption Stapp wants surfaced rather than buried.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The skill-acquisition article centres first-person structure throughout: the felt body schema vs. body image distinction, the "positive tug toward what they are trying to abandon" in skill transition, flow's "loss of reflective self-awareness while pre-reflective awareness remains." Nagel would value that the article treats the phenomenology as *data the physical description omits* — "two bodies can be physically identical yet phenomenologically different... because the body schema carries a history of conscious engagement that physical description does not capture." That is the "what is it like" question put to empirical work without being dissolved by it.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
Whitehead would approve the cluster's refusal of crude substance-talk: consciousness here is characterised by what it *does across time* — builds procedural systems, delegates, re-enters under restructuring — a process-arc rather than a static mental substance parked beside the brain. The "architect designs itself out of the building but never truly retires" image is process-philosophical in spirit. **Constraint honoured**: nowhere does this Whitehead-friendly process framing get used to upgrade an evidential tier. The skill-acquisition article never says "because consciousness is process-like all the way down, mental causation is established"; it keeps the process description on the *characterisation* axis and the evidential weight on the *empirical* axis. The two axes stay separate — which is exactly the discipline this persona's praise must not be allowed to erode.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
Kane finds the cluster's agency-realism congenial: the skill-delegation passage describes a *conscious act* of releasing control ("the learner deliberately releases control, trusting emerging automaticity"), and skill transition as deliberate commitment resisting a felt temptation. Consciousness here is a genuine source, not a spectator — the choking phenomenon (conscious re-engagement *undoing* what it built) is the article's sharpest exhibit that the agent has real causal purchase. Kane would note approvingly that this is causal efficacy located at indeterminacies, the same architecture his libertarianism needs.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
McGinn would commend the cluster's restraint at the boundaries. The skill-acquisition article repeatedly declines to claim that imaging "can determine whether prefrontal activity *is* consciousness or *is shaped by* consciousness" — it explicitly says the data cannot adjudicate identity vs. interactionism and constrains only epiphenomenalism. The eliminativism passage concedes the Map "cannot refute eliminativism from within the eliminativist framework." This is intellectual humility about what the evidence can and cannot settle — McGinn's signature posture, applied not to the hard problem's solvability but to the limits of the present evidential reach.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This is the cluster's load-bearing verdict, and it is strongly positive. Three things Birch most wants to praise are present:

1. **Tenet-as-evidence-upgrade is praise-worthily *not* done — and was actively prevented today.** The articles had the textbook structural opportunity to slide "interactionism meets the metaproblem" up to "the metaproblem is evidence for dualism." The same-cycle refine-draft (topic) and deep-review (concept) caught the slippage in four-plus load-bearing places and replaced it with the named pattern: "removes a defeater rather than supplying fresh positive evidence... restores the dualist's prior warrant — it does not, on its own, raise the standing of dualism above where the rest of the Map's case leaves it." The changelog records that both passes ran the §2 diagnostic of [[evidential-status-discipline]] (a tenet-accepting reviewer still flags the original). That is the discipline operating, not merely being cited.

2. **The debunking argument is stated on its own terms before the reply.** The concept page's new "The Debunking Threat" subsection gives the materialist's sharpest weapon at full strength ("a belief explained entirely by causes indifferent to its truth loses its justification") and then marks the interactionist reply as an *in-framework* engagement that "does not refute the debunker from neutral ground" — explicitly flagging the residual physicalist disagreement as a framework-boundary clash rather than dressing it as a victory inside the physicalist's commitments. This is honest framework-stage calibration of exactly the kind this persona exists to reward.

3. **Empirical evidence is not over-counted.** The skill-acquisition article's calibration caveat is exemplary: the four converging neuroscience lines (corticostriatal transfer, error correction, sleep consolidation, structural plasticity) "carry the evidential weight of one finding, not several," because the identity theorist absorbs all four with one move. It further scopes the deliberate-practice claim against Macnamara et al. (2014) — 26% of variance in games, under 1% in professions — and rebuilds the argument on *quality-dependence* rather than practice-dominance. The falsification section even separates *prospective* open tests (passive-exposure equivalence, untested) from *retrospective* tests already run (Iriki 1996), refusing to inflate a past positive result by leaving it implicit as a still-open challenge.

The Process Philosopher and Hardline Empiricist **converge** on this cluster: Whitehead-friendly process characterisation and Birch-style evidential discipline both find things to praise, on different axes, with no slippage between them. Per the skill instruction, that convergence is the signal the cluster has resolved the tension honestly. No calibration concern to flag; no refine-draft remediation task warranted.
