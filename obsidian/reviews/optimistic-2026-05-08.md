## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)

The cluster's relationship to the explanatory gap is exemplary across all three articles. `aphantasia` lines 79–85 lay out the trichotomy as an unflinching exhibit of where the gap sits empirically: option 1 makes phenomenal imagery "a candidate epiphenomenon at the task level"; option 2 saves functionalism but at the cost of fine-grained individuation that has to be *specified*; option 3 invokes Schwitzgebel-style introspective unreliability and dissolves the wedge from a different angle. None of the options is presented as the answer. The article's Chalmersian discipline is in naming each option's metaphysical loading without smoothing toward the one the Map prefers. `synaesthesia` line 88 anchors Wager's *extra qualia problem* — "the synaesthete and the non-synaesthete shown the same letter on the same page have indistinguishable representational input at the surface stimulus level, but the synaesthete experiences additional phenomenal content" — at the formal point Chalmers' representationalism critique requires it, then immediately preserves the van Leeuwen pipeline-divergence caveat (line 88) as the rejoinder a representationalist would deploy. This is Chalmers' "naturalistic dualism" applied editorially: the gap is named, the rejoinder is named, the article doesn't claim to settle either side. `out-of-body-experiences` line 91 names the precise inferential bound: "What stimulation work *does* refute is the strong version of embodied consciousness which holds that the bodily self-model is constitutive of conscious experience." That is a Chalmersian *modus tollens* operating at exactly the structural register he favours — the gap survives the experimental result, but the result rules out one specific physicalist-friendly closure of it. Chalmers would recognise the layered architecture: the hard problem is preserved in all three articles, the dualist-friendly framing is named explicitly where it appears, and the empirical contact-points are kept honest.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)

The cluster does not foreground quantum mechanism explicitly — these are concrete-phenomenon articles in clinical and cognitive-neuroscience registers, not interpretation articles. But Stapp's broader architecture of *consciousness contributing to the resolution of the otherwise-unresolved* is honoured indirectly across all three. `synaesthesia` line 104's reading of synaesthesia as a "load-bearing concrete-phenomenon case for the **interface-vs-substrate distinction**" is the kind of structural extension Stapp would welcome: humans with the same biological substrate present measurably different phenomenal interface configurations, with extra channels open in one subset of minds and closed in another. The article is meticulous in flagging this as "speculative-integration tier rather than empirically forced" (line 104), which is the discipline Stapp's own programme has often been criticised for lacking — naming the metaphysical reach honestly rather than presenting it as flowing from the data. `out-of-body-experiences` line 99 makes the filter-theoretic reading available — "filter theory does not require literal disembodiment to explain ordinary OBEs; it predicts that the bodily self-model is part of what gets filtered" — without pretending it is empirically forced; it is the reading that *coheres* with the dualist commitment, not the reading the OBE data alone establish. Stapp would praise the cluster's restraint: an interactionist-friendly reading is articulated wherever it earns its place but is never used to inflate the data's evidential weight.

This persona has substantial purchase across the cluster. `aphantasia` lines 41–47 take the lived report seriously in exactly the Nagelian register: "Aphantasic adults, asked the standard imagery questions ('Picture your kitchen — how vivid is it on a scale of 1 to 5?'), often answer that there is nothing to rate: the request returns no visual content at all." Nagel's "What Is It Like" framing is then extended explicitly: hyperphantasics "describe imagery 'as vivid as real seeing'" — and the article immediately raises the deeper Nagelian question (line 47): "Whether the phenomenology these reports pick out is comparable across people is itself a deep question." The article does not claim to answer it. `synaesthesia` line 74 (the "simultaneous-occupancy puzzle") is Nagel's bat-question made concrete within a single species: "Standard adult perception affords no analogue, and the conceptual vocabulary non-synaesthetes have for colour does not extend to this configuration." ### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)

This persona's praise is load-bearing for this review because the cluster contains three articles whose claims had every structural temptation to slide up the evidential scale on metaphysical-coherence grounds. None do.

**First, `synaesthesia` explicitly self-labels its interface reading as speculative-integration tier rather than empirically forced.** Line 104 is the discipline operating at full strength: "the data alone are compatible with cross-activation under physicalism (Ramachandran & Hubbard's preferred reading), and the interface reading earns its place from cluster-level convergence rather than from synaesthesia in isolation." This article had structural opportunity to claim the interface reading was empirically supported by the synaesthesia data themselves (the cross-activation is real, the genetic linkage is real, the simultaneous-occupancy is real — why not claim the interface reading is forced?). The article explicitly declines on the grounds that the same data also support a physicalist-friendly cross-activation reading; the interface reading earns its keep from converging across multiple cases (synaesthesia + aphantasia + reality-monitoring), not from its fit with this case alone. This is the cluster-level evidential discipline operating — a single article does not vindicate the framework; the framework's plausibility comes from the joint fit, and a single article that cannot vindicate the framework alone should not pretend it can.

**Second, `out-of-body-experiences` refuses the absorption move at the load-bearing claim.** Lines 81–89 are the cleanest example in the cluster of refusing-to-absorb-the-negative-signal: "The strongest dualist claim — that during NDE-OBEs consciousness occupies a perspective from which it can perceive information unavailable to the body — is testable, has been tested, and has so far not been confirmed. That non-result is real evidence against the literal-exit interpretation, even when the sample is small and the situations are difficult to control." The article had every structural opportunity to absorb AWARE I/II's zero-hit results into the framework via a "small sample / difficult conditions / future replication might shift this" hedge so heavy it functionally cancelled the empirical signal. It does not. The hedge is named honestly (sample small, conditions difficult), but the article unambiguously preserves AWARE's negative result as *evidence against* the literal-exit interpretation. Compare the parallel discipline in `topics/consciousness-in-simple-organisms` (line 157, the UAL discussion) — the same anti-absorption move applied to a different boundary case. The Map is now installing this discipline systematically: when an empirical signal would falsify or weaken the framework's preferred reading, the framework absorbs the discomfort rather than diluting the signal.

**Third, `aphantasia`'s option 3 is the most subtle Hardline-Empiricist move in the cluster.** Lines 83–85 introduce introspective unreliability not as an *escape* from the wedge but as a *way the wedge could fail* that the article must acknowledge. "Taken to its limit, option 3 dissolves the wedge from a different angle than options 1 and 2: if neither cohort's reports are reliable, then we do not know that the phenomenologies diverge in the way the cohort labels suggest, and the within-species comparison loses its evidential grip. The argument from aphantasia therefore depends on giving introspective reports *some* evidential weight — enough to register the contrast, even if not enough to settle which option is correct." This is a standard Birchian move applied to the article's *own* central evidence: the wedge depends on a methodological assumption (introspective reports are *some* evidential weight) that is itself contested, and the article names this dependency as load-bearing rather than presenting the wedge as if it sat above methodological dispute. The Hardline Empiricist persona praises this restraint highly — it is the kind of self-undermining-conditional that articles attached to their conclusions rarely include voluntarily. (The article preserves the conclusion, which is correct: the within-species cluster does provide cumulative pressure on functionalism, and option 3 does not by itself dissolve the cluster's joint-pressure result. But it preserves the conclusion *with* the methodological dependency named, which is the discipline's signature.)

**Fourth, `synaesthesia` preserves the Wager hedge under van Leeuwen's pressure.** Line 88: "Wager's hedge survives: van Leeuwen's semantic-mediation finding shows synaesthete and non-synaesthete pipelines are not identical at deeper grain, so the 'identical input' claim is a coarse-grain claim about surface representation rather than an end-to-end claim about processing." The article had structural opportunity to deploy Wager's "extra qualia problem" at full strength — same input, different phenomenology, therefore representationalism falsified — and declined to. The deeper-grain pipeline divergence is acknowledged, the claim is bounded as a *surface* representational claim, and the article's argument is calibrated to that bound rather than to a stronger version that the data would not support. This is the same discipline Birch applies to invertebrate-consciousness markers: the marker's evidential reach is bounded by what the marker can actually distinguish, and the inference is calibrated to that bound.

**Fifth, the cluster makes joint-pressure rather than single-case pressure the engine of the within-species-divergence argument.** Both `aphantasia` line 93 and `synaesthesia` line 100 explicitly argue that the wedge is *cumulative*: "aphantasia alone is locally absorbable by a sufficiently fine-grained functionalism, but absorbing aphantasia *and* synaesthesia *and* reality-monitoring divergence with the same move puts strain on the absorption strategy." This is the empirical-discipline structural complement to the Whiteheadian framing — the cluster's plausibility comes from convergence across multiple cases, no single case is presented as decisive, and the *joint constraint* on functionalist absorption is what makes the wedge non-trivial. The Hardline Empiricist persona praises this framing because it exhibits the appropriate evidential humility about each individual case while preserving the cumulative pressure-result that the cluster as a whole earns.

The Process Philosopher and Hardline Empiricist personas converge on the same passages in all three articles. In `synaesthesia`, both praise the speculative-integration self-label at line 104 (Whiteheadian continuous-variation framing earning its place; Birch-disciplined evidential calibration). In `aphantasia`, both praise the trichotomy and the Schwitzgebel option-3 anchor (Whitehead-friendly graded continuum *and* Birch-disciplined acknowledgement of the wedge's methodological dependencies). In `out-of-body-experiences`, both praise lines 91–95 (the strong-embodiment-thesis refutation is Whitehead-friendly without being inflated; the dislocation-vs-disembodiment distinction is Birch-disciplined evidential separation). When the two converge, the article has resolved the tension honestly. They do not conflict anywhere in the cluster.

- **Strongest point**: The trichotomy at lines 79–85, with the Schwitzgebel option-3 self-undermining-conditional preserved. The article's central wedge is that two minds performing the same task with phenomenally divergent inner content presents three options, each metaphysically loaded, none empirically forced. Option 3 is the methodological self-check: the wedge depends on introspective reports having *some* evidential weight, and if they have *none*, the wedge dissolves. The article names this dependency as load-bearing. This is not merely "naming the limitation"; it is the limitation reaching all the way back to the article's own central argument and being preserved as load-bearing rather than being framed away.
- **Notable quote**: "Taken to its limit, option 3 dissolves the wedge from a different angle than options 1 and 2: if neither cohort's reports are reliable, then we do not know that the phenomenologies diverge in the way the cohort labels suggest, and the within-species comparison loses its evidential grip. The argument from aphantasia therefore depends on giving introspective reports *some* evidential weight — enough to register the contrast, even if not enough to settle which option is correct."
- **Why it works**: A weaker article would have used option 3 as either a defeater for the materialist (introspection is unreliable, therefore introspectively-grounded materialism is also unreliable) or a defeater for the dualist (introspection is unreliable, therefore the divergence might be illusory). This article uses option 3 as a methodological check on its own argument — the wedge survives only because we grant introspection *some* evidential weight — and preserves the argument's force *with* that dependency named, rather than presenting the argument as if it sat above methodological dispute. Schwitzgebel's installation as the anchor (line 84) gives the dependency a citable shape rather than leaving it vague. The Würzburg-aphantasia recurrence note (line 57) is a small but high-leverage addition: the same observation surfaced twice across a century under different vocabularies and survived being declared unsound the first time, which is structural evidence that the territory keeps producing this shape rather than being a methodological artefact.

#### Concept page: The Argument from Mechanism (Sufficiency-Necessity Non-Sequitur)

- **Builds on**: `topics/out-of-body-experiences` (lines 83–91, where the inferential pattern is named and refused), `concepts/possibility-probability-slippage`, `concepts/evidential-status-discipline`, `concepts/direct-refutation-discipline`, `topics/lucid-dreaming-and-dualist-rendering` (parallel structural use of the same discipline), `topics/psychedelics-and-the-filter-model`, `topics/clinical-phenomenology-and-altered-experience`
- **Would address**: The OBE article identifies a recurrent inferential pattern in consciousness-debate literature — the move from "phenomenon X can be produced by mechanism M" to "all instances of X are produced by M" — and refuses it as a non-sequitur ("That a phenomenon can be produced by mechanism M does not show that all instances of the phenomenon are produced by M. Visual experience can be induced by direct cortical stimulation; that does not make ordinary vision hallucinatory"). The pattern is *portable*: it surfaces in psychedelic-experience debates (drug-induced mystical experience produces serotonergic cascade ↛ all mystical experience reduces to serotonergic cascade), in lucid-dreaming debates, in NDE debates, in dissociative-disorder debates, in religious-experience debates, and in many places where dualist-friendly phenomenology has a reductive sufficient-mechanism explanation available. A standalone concept page would (a) name the inferential pattern formally as a *sufficiency-vs-necessity* fallacy in the Map's named-discipline catalogue alongside `possibility-probability-slippage` and `evidential-status-discipline`; (b) catalogue the catalogue's existing instances (OBE, lucid dreaming, psychedelics, hypnagogic phenomenology) as worked examples; (c) develop the symmetric discipline — the materialist's argument from mechanism is rejected, but so is the dualist's argument from "no mechanism fully explains X therefore mind is involved", which is the parallel non-sequitur in the opposite direction; (d) install the article as a citable handle for dependent articles facing this inference-pattern. The Map already has the discipline operational across multiple articles; what is missing is the *named anchor* for it.
- **Estimated scope**: Short article (1500–2000 words)
- **Tenet alignment**: Methodological. The Relation to Site Perspective section can name how the discipline interacts with Tenet 1 (the dualism-friendly inferences depend on the symmetric version of the discipline), Tenet 5 (Occam's Razor Has Limits — premature parsimony is what the materialist's argument-from-mechanism move attempts), and methodological tenets generally.
