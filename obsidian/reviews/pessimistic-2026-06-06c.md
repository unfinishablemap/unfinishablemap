## Critiques by Philosopher

### The Eliminative Materialist (Churchland)
"Pastness quale," "mineness," "double presence" are folk-phenomenological posits dressed as data. The article concedes source-monitoring theory identifies the mechanism, then declares the mechanism doesn't explain "why output is experienced at all" — but that is the explanatory-gap promissory note restated, not a finding. The KC and Klein-Nichols dissociations it cites are exactly the kind of evidence a maturing neuroscience uses to *decompose* the folk category, not to vindicate it. (The article largely anticipates this: it explicitly relocates the hard problem "one tier up" rather than claiming a refutation. The honest move is present.)

### The Hard-Nosed Physicalist (Dennett)
The whole edifice rests on introspective reports ("subjects report a robust felt difference") being trustworthy guides to phenomenal facts. Heterophenomenology says treat them as data about what subjects *believe*, not about irreducible qualia. The article cites `[[phenomenal-authority-and-first-person-evidence]]` to license introspective evidence — but that is the contested premise doing the work, not an independent support. Fairly flagged in the text as an auxiliary commitment.

### The Quantum Skeptic (Tegmark)
Not directly applicable — no quantum-mechanical claims load-bear here. The only physics-adjacent move is the No-Many-Worlds inference, handled below.

### The Many-Worlds Defender (Deutsch)
The article's strongest opponent-handling. It correctly refuses to claim memory phenomenology *refutes* MWI; it routes the disagreement through the `[[haecceity]]` auxiliary and marks it as a framework-boundary disagreement ("branch-local determinacy is epistemic, not ontological"). This is honest framework-boundary marking, not boundary-substitution dressed as refutation. No issue.

### The Empiricist (Popper)
"Pastness quale resists reduction to computational description" — what observation would falsify this? The article does supply defeasibility conditions ("a successful computational account of mental time travel, a smooth-gradient finding... would weaken the reading here"), which is more than most. Acceptable.

### The Buddhist Philosopher (Nagarjuna)
The article reifies a "substantial self" that "must persist through the revision" (line 84) and grounds it in `[[haecceity]]` — a "deep further fact." This is precisely the permanent-self clinging Madhyamaka deconstructs. The article does not engage anatta / emptiness anywhere, despite linking `[[buddhism-and-dualism]]` elsewhere in the corpus. A genuine gap: the self-constitution thesis invites the Buddhist objection and does not answer it.
