# Referee Report: Metaphysical Overreach and Empirical Vulnerability in a Quantum-Interactionist Framework

The manuscript and associated theoretical architecture presented for review advance a systematic, axiomatic defense of interactionist dualism. The framework proposes that phenomenal consciousness is ontologically irreducible to physical states, possesses genuine causal efficacy, and interfaces with the physical brain primarily through points of quantum indeterminacy. By structuring its arguments around a set of foundational tenets—including bare dualism, minimal quantum interaction, and the explicit rejection of the Many-Worlds interpretation—the text attempts to synthesize contemporary neuroscience, quantum foundations, and the philosophy of mind into a cohesive, non-reductive ontology.

Subjecting the substantive philosophical content, logical inferences, and empirical scaffolding of this submission to rigorous academic scrutiny reveals fatal structural and methodological defects. The framework systematically engages in metaphysical bracketing, whereby heavy ontological commitments are smuggled into the premises to dismiss competing physicalist paradigms by definitional fiat rather than argument. Furthermore, it demonstrates a profound mishandling of the empirical physics and neurocognitive literature. The text frequently relies on unconfirmed preprints, contested interpretations of neurological phenomena, and thoroughly debunked neuroimaging claims to sustain its interactionist architecture.

The following audit evaluates the submission across four dimensions: empirical accuracy against contemporary consciousness science, reliance on challenged or retracted results, the bracketing of contested assumptions, and the evasion of competing frameworks.

## Dimension A: Empirical Accuracy Against 2020s Consciousness Science

The submission's handling of the contemporary empirical landscape reveals a pervasive tendency to weaponize scientific ambiguity. The text routinely interprets the failure of specific, highly parameterized physicalist models as affirmative, deductive evidence for dualist mysterianism, mischaracterizing the standard iterative process of the empirical sciences.

### The Misappropriation of the COGITATE Adversarial Collaboration

A central pillar of the manuscript’s empirical defense relies on the 2025 COGITATE adversarial collaboration, which tested Integrated Information Theory (IIT) against Global Neuronal Workspace Theory (GNWT). The submission notes that the study failed to fully validate either theory's most stringent predictions. IIT's predicted maximum of integrated information in the posterior cortical hot zone did not materialize as expected, and GNWT's signature prefrontal "ignition" and offset responses were largely absent in the specific paradigms tested.

However, the submission extracts an entirely unwarranted philosophical conclusion from this data. It posits that the COGITATE results illustrate a "conceptual scheme void," arguing that the failure of these two physicalist-adjacent theories demonstrates an inherent empirical incommensurability that renders physicalism fundamentally inadequate. This is a gross mischaracterization of the scientific consensus surrounding the data. The COGITATE consortium did not declare functionalism or physicalism defunct. On the contrary, researchers concluded that the neural mass models tailored to the dynamics of both theories captured significant portions of the observed magnetoencephalography (MEG) connectivity patterns, with slight but tentative empirical support favoring GNWT's delayed ignition dynamics over IIT's synchronous sensory dynamics.

By framing the COGITATE results as evidence of physicalist collapse, the submission ignores the emergence of hybrid models and the ongoing refinement of predictive processing frameworks. The failure of two specific computational models to perfectly predict neuroimaging outcomes across 256 participants does not license a retreat into interactionist dualism. It merely reflects the normal iterative process of neuroscientific hypothesis testing. The submission treats physicalism as a fragile monolith that shatters upon a single anomalous neuroimaging result, failing to recognize that functionalist accounts of consciousness routinely evolve to accommodate new functional connectivity metrics.

### Category Errors in Cognitive Bandwidth Constraints

The manuscript repeatedly attempts to bridge phenomenal experience and physical constraint by quantifying the "bandwidth" of consciousness. It cites a "sixth practical constraint" on the quantum-coupling interface, estimating human consciousness at a mere 10 to 50 bits per second. To support this, it relies on Zheng and Meister's 2025 paper, "The unbearable slowness of being".

This constitutes a severe category error. Zheng and Meister's research evaluates the computational throughput of specific attention-gated neural circuits, measuring the rate at which sensory inputs are successfully encoded into reportable, working-memory formats. It is a measure of classical, biological computational capacity. The manuscript strips this metric from its neurobiological context and repurposes it as an upper bound on how much "information injection" a non-physical conscious agent can exert on a quantum interface. Using a classical limit on neural action-potential propagation and synaptic transmission as a mathematical constraint on unobservable quantum wave-function collapse represents a profound failure of interdisciplinary translation. It dresses a dualist metaphysical postulate in the stolen quantitative authority of mainstream neurobiology.

### Overstating the Relevance of Quantum Biology

To overcome the standard decoherence objection, the submission appeals broadly to the burgeoning field of quantum biology. It cites the preservation of quantum coherence in non-neural biological systems to argue that warm biology is not categorically inhospitable to quantum function.

This analogical reasoning constitutes an empirical leap that borders on the specious. While the manuscript references radical pair mechanisms in avian cryptochromes and theoretical zero-point field coupling models (e.g., Keppler 2025), it fails to acknowledge the strict biophysical constraints required to maintain these states. The radical pair mechanism in cryptochrome requires a highly specific, structurally isolated protein architecture to maintain microsecond coherence. Extrapolating this specialized, localized photochemical mechanism to the macroscopic neural coordination required for conscious "basis control" or "timing control" across large-scale cortical networks lacks any operational evidence. As the 2020s literature emphasizes, distinguishing genuine non-classical physical correlations from complex classical nonlinear dynamics in the brain remains a central methodological challenge, and no study to date has demonstrated entanglement or collapse dynamics in neural tissue under operational criteria comparable to those used in controlled quantum systems. The presence of isolated quantum effects in biology does not grant a blank check for macroscopic quantum consciousness.

## Dimension B: Reliance on Challenged, Retracted, or Failed Replications

The most devastating vulnerabilities in the submission arise from its uncritical reliance on highly contested or entirely debunked empirical studies, which it utilizes as load-bearing structural support for its interactionist architecture.

### The Extracranial Biophoton Catastrophe

In its attempt to establish a physical interface for quantum-mental interaction, the submission enthusiastically cites a 2025 *iScience* paper by Casey, DiBerardino, Murugan, et al., which claims to have detected ultraweak photon emissions (biophotons) from the human brain using external photomultiplier tubes (PMTs). The manuscript touts this "photoencephalography" as a landmark discovery, suggesting that biophotons serve as functional quantum components of subjective experience that dynamically respond to cognitive tasks.

This reliance is fatal to the submission's empirical credibility. The manuscript entirely ignores the immediate, devastating refutations of the Casey et al. study published in 2026 by Salari, Seshan, and Simon. Salari et al. rigorously demonstrated that the supposed "brain biophoton" signals reported by Casey were overwhelmingly dominated by background light contamination due to minor optical leakages in the experimental dark room (such as a 10mm gap in a tent).

Furthermore, Salari et al. proved a foundational biophysical impossibility in the Casey paradigm: photons with wavelengths shorter than 600 nm are entirely attenuated by human scalp and skull tissue, while wavelengths longer than 600 nm (which might theoretically penetrate bone) fall almost entirely outside the effective spectral sensitivity of the standard PMTs used in the experiment. Consequently, even if the dark room had been perfectly sealed, the PMTs could only have detected emission from the scalp, not the cerebral cortex. By embedding this debunked "photoencephalography" literature into its core argument for a quantum interface, the submission demonstrates a profound lack of biophysical diligence, elevating experimental artifacts and room light leakage to the status of metaphysical proof.

### The Misinterpretation of Split-Brain Unity

To dismantle the physicalist "binding problem" and argue that consciousness operates as a unified, non-physical field that merely interfaces with the brain, the submission relies heavily on a 2017 study by Yair Pinto et al.. Pinto's study observed that split-brain patients could sometimes report the location of a stimulus presented to an isolated visual field using the ipsilateral hand or verbal responses. Pinto concluded that while visual perception is split, conscious awareness remains undivided despite the severing of the corpus callosum. The manuscript seizes upon this to argue that consciousness does not depend on massive structural neural integration.

However, the submission completely bypasses the extensive neuroscientific rebuttals to Pinto's conclusions. Specifically, Volz and Gazzaniga (2017) and subsequent literature throughout the 2020s (e.g., Corballis et al., 2018) have definitively shown that Pinto's observations are readily explained by classical "cross-cueing" and ipsilateral motor control, without any need to invoke a unified conscious agent. Split-brain patients frequently learn to use subcortical pathways and subtle behavioral cues (cross-cueing) to allow one hemisphere to glean information from the other. Furthermore, simple pointing tasks can be controlled by ipsilateral motor pathways, meaning the left hemisphere can control the left hand for crude localization. By ignoring the reaction-time delays that betray this inter-hemispheric cross-cueing process (often 200–300 ms longer than direct responses), Pinto's study artificially inflated the appearance of unified consciousness. The submission’s failure to engage with the refutations from the founders of split-brain research renders its conclusions on conscious unity empirically bankrupt.

This is unacceptable for a manuscript aspiring to rigorous philosophical intervention.

## Dimension C: Claims Quietly Bracketing Contested Assumptions as Settled

The manuscript attempts to solve some of the most intractable problems in the philosophy of mind by introducing novel conceptual frameworks. However, an audit reveals that these frameworks merely obscure the problems they claim to solve by bracketing contested assumptions as settled facts.

### Delegatory Dualism and the Evasion of Causal Exclusion

The submission leverages Bradford Saad's (2025) concept of "Delegatory Dualism" to circumvent Jaegwon Kim's devastating causal exclusion argument. The exclusion argument posits that if every physical event has a sufficient physical cause (the causal closure of the physical), there is no causal work left for an immaterial mind to do without invoking absurd, systematic overdetermination.

The manuscript claims to solve this via "observational closure" and "causal preemption". Under this model, the causal profile of a conscious experience is said to perfectly match the "default causal profile" of its underlying physical state. When the experience arises, it supposedly *preempts* the physical state, taking over its causal responsibilities while remaining empirically indistinguishable from standard physical causation.

This is a semantic shell game that performs no genuine metaphysical work. By defining the mental state as preempting a physically sufficient base state, the author is simply renaming overdetermination as "delegation." If the physical brain state is counterfactually sufficient to cause a motor action, and the non-physical mental state *also* possesses the capacity to cause it by preempting the physical state, the framework requires a massive, unobservable mechanism to constantly monitor and suppress physical sufficiency in favor of mental efficacy. The manuscript assumes the existence of these highly specific "psychophysical laws" without providing any ontological justification for why fundamental physical laws would voluntarily suspend themselves to delegate authority to phenomenological states. This is not a solution to the exclusion problem; it is the exclusion problem hidden behind the word "delegation." The distinction between "universal closure" and "observational closure" is a desperate metaphysical epicycle designed to render dualism completely unfalsifiable by laboratory science.

### The Born Rule and Quantum Epistemology

In its attempt to define exactly *how* the mind interfaces with matter, the submission posits that consciousness selects outcomes at points of quantum indeterminacy. It favors "basis control" (where consciousness chooses the measurement basis, preserving Born probabilities) and "timing control" (leveraging the quantum Zeno effect) to preserve standard statistical physics while still asserting mental causation.

To justify inserting consciousness into the quantum measurement problem, the manuscript cites recent preprints by Torres Alegre (2025) and Pati (2026), claiming these proofs show that relativistic no-signalling absolutely forces the Born rule, leaving a causal gap that only an observer can fill. It frames the Born rule as a feature of the consciousness-physics interface rather than a purely physical law.

This maneuver brackets several highly contested assumptions. First, it treats un-peer-reviewed preprints relying on Generalized Probabilistic Theories (GPTs) as foundational, incontrovertible mathematical proofs. Second, and more egregiously, it deliberately ignores purely physical, observer-independent derivations of the Born rule that have flooded the 2020s literature. For example, recent theoretical work by Farzulla (2026) demonstrates that the Born rule and quantum interference can be derived entirely from classical oscillatory sampling and signal detection theory, without ever invoking subjective consciousness, wave-function collapse, or observer interfaces. Similarly, 2025 literature demonstrates that Born probabilities appear naturally as fixed points of modular flow in thermodynamic equilibrium, meaning they are emergent physical limits representing modular balance, not subjective epistemic interfaces. By selectively curating quantum interpretations that leave an artificial gap for consciousness, the submission engineers its own necessity, treating a highly contested interpretation of probability as a settled justification for mental causality.

## Dimension D: Counterarguments or Competing Frameworks Evaded

A submission to a top-tier philosophy journal is expected to engage robustly with the strongest versions of its opponents' arguments. This manuscript utilizes its self-declared "tenets" (e.g., Tenet 4: Rejection of Many-Worlds; Tenet 5: Epistemic humility about Occam's razor) as intellectual panic rooms to evade formidable physicalist frameworks without doing the philosophical labor required to refute them.

### The Evasion of Decoherence-Based Physicalism and MWI

The submission dictates the rejection of the Many-Worlds Interpretation (MWI) and universal wavefunction models by flat axiomatic assertion. MWI, alongside modern decoherence theory, provides mathematically rigorous accounts of how classical reality emerges from quantum superpositions without ever invoking wave-function collapse, and importantly, without requiring a conscious observer to trigger that collapse.

Decoherence explains how a quantum system entangled with its environment rapidly transitions to a mixed state (described by a diagonalized density matrix), erasing interference patterns locally by dissipating phase information into the environment. The manuscript fails to engage with the fact that if decoherence effectively removes superposition from the perspective of local subsystems, there is no "collapse point" at which an immaterial mind can inject its causal will. The manuscript briefly mentions Max Tegmark's calculation that neural decoherence occurs on timescales of 10−13 to 10−20 seconds, but waves it away by citing Hagan et al.'s contested rebuttals. However, it completely ignores the fact that even Hagan's highly optimistic, best-case-scenario coherence times (10−4 seconds) are still vastly too short to account for the millisecond-to-second timescales required for cognitive binding and conscious attention. By simply declaring that MWI and strict decoherence models are "rejected" by the site's internal tenets, the author abdicates the philosophical responsibility to prove *why* interactionist collapse is superior to purely unitary physical evolution.

### The Ignorance of Classical Predictive Processing

When discussing cognition and action, the manuscript champions "quantum active inference," citing highly speculative literature (e.g., Wiest and Puniani 2025) to argue that classical mechanisms cannot explain the holistic, context-sensitive nature of cognition. In doing so, it entirely evades the vast, highly successful mainstream literature on classical Predictive Processing (PP) and the Free Energy Principle.

Classical Predictive Processing beautifully explains how neural networks minimize prediction error through hierarchical, bidirectional message passing. It provides a robust account of attention, perception, and action without requiring quantum tunneling, microtubule coherence, or a non-physical phenomenological controller to act as an exogenous agent. The manuscript's failure to address classical PP as the dominant paradigm for understanding neural causation betrays an insular, cherry-picked engagement with the cognitive sciences. It addresses active inference only when it can be co-opted into a quantum collapse model, ignoring the fundamental physicalism that underpins the theory in mainstream neuroscience.

### The Mishandling of Epiphenomenalism and QBism

The manuscript dismisses epiphenomenalism—the view that consciousness is causally inert—by claiming that if it were true, our verbal reports about consciousness would be an unexplained coincidence. It entirely ignores the standard philosophical reply: in a physically closed universe, a "zombie twin" lacking consciousness would produce the exact same verbal reports due to identical physical neurodynamics. Dismissing epiphenomenalism based on verbal reports begs the question against physical causal closure.

Similarly, the manuscript critiques Quantum Bayesianism (QBism) for assuming consciousness as a primitive without explaining it. While true, QBism is an epistemic interpretation of quantum mechanics, treating the wavefunction as a measure of a user's subjective belief. The manuscript attempts to graft its own ontic, causal-collapse view (where consciousness physically *forces* an outcome) onto QBist epistemology, failing to recognize that QBism explicitly denies the physical reality of wave-function collapse altogether. The manuscript routinely conflates epistemic uncertainty with ontological indeterminacy to create artificial space for dualist intervention.

## Conclusion and Specific Weaknesses

The manuscript attempts an impossible metaphysical balancing act: insisting that phenomenal consciousness is causally efficacious in the macroscopic physical world, while simultaneously designing its mechanisms of interaction (via "observational closure," "preemption," and "basis control") to be utterly undetectable by modern physics or neuroscience. It shores up this unfalsifiable architecture by misinterpreting inconclusive neuroimaging data, relying heavily on debunked biophysical experiments, and treating un-peer-reviewed mathematical preprints as dogmatic law.

3. **Question-Begging Rejection of Decoherence and the Born Rule:** The framework dismisses decoherence-based solutions to the measurement problem and claims the Born rule requires a conscious interface, ignoring contemporary derivations that explain Born rule probabilities as emergent physical equilibrium limits without requiring conscious observers.

4. **Distortion of the COGITATE Results:** The submission mischaracterizes the 2025 COGITATE adversarial collaboration's failure to perfectly validate IIT or GNWT as affirmative evidence for an irreducible "conceptual scheme void," actively suppressing the study's own conclusions that neural mass models successfully captured significant empirical dynamics without requiring dualism.

5. **Metaphysical Sleight of Hand in Causal Exclusion:** The adoption of Saad's "Delegatory Dualism" merely renames causal overdetermination as "preemption," failing to answer Jaegwon Kim's exclusion argument by relying on undefined psychophysical laws to actively suppress counterfactually sufficient physical brain states without observable violation.

- *Citation:* Saad, B. (2025). "A dualist theory of experience." *Philosophical Studies*, 182, 939-967 (cited to highlight the manuscript's failure to resolve the very problem it imports).
