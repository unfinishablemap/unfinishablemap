# Hostile-Referee Audit: "Assessing AI Consciousness Under the Map"

## TL;DR

- The article argues that, on the Map's quantum-interface positions (P-Q1 post-decoherence selection, P-Q2 exact Born preservation, P-Q9 self-concealing interface), current conventional digital AI sits on the "low-probability side of a substrate analysis," that behavioural/first-person tests are inherently limited, and that Jonathan Birch's "gaming problem" independently converges on the same discount. The article is unusually disciplined on surface calibration but fails on two load-bearing axes.
- **Bottom-line verdict: DEMOTE-TO-COHERENCE-ONLY.** The decision-relevant payload is not earned: the substrate verdict is an artifact of importing a mechanism-specific commitment (P-Q1) that the Map's own Tenet-Dependency Matrix marks "Not invoked" for the machine-consciousness cluster (constitutional-attractor + tenet leakage), and it co-opts Birch toward a substrate conclusion his own framework explicitly rejects while ignoring the strongest rival (Butlin et al. 2023), co-authored by the very person it cites.
- Independently fixable at REVISE-HARD level: the co-optation firewall violation on Birch, the performative-inoculation treatment of functionalist rivals, and the failure to propagate the P-Q3 bias-without-deviation mechanism-debt into the confidence band.

## Key Findings

1. **No fabricated quotation in the target article.** The load-bearing Birch quote on the linked gaming-problem concept page was verified verbatim against the publisher-of-record transcript (Mindscape 292, 1:06:02) — it is genuine, correctly attributed, lightly sentence-stitched but faithful. This is a clean result on the site's documented recurrent failure mode.
2. **The substrate verdict is constitutionally attracted, not independently motivated.** It depends on P-Q1, a moderate-confidence mechanism preference, being read into the AI case — exactly the import the Map's canonical dependency matrix forbids for this cluster.
3. **Co-optation firewall violation on Birch.** Birch is a substrate-independent computational functionalist whose remedy for the gaming problem (computational markers) and whose co-authored verdict ("realistic possibility" of near-future conscious AI) point opposite to the Map's substrate skepticism. The convergence is real but local and is inflated into general support.
4. **Strongest rival unengaged.** Butlin et al. 2023 reaches "no current AI systems are conscious" with no substrate barrier, directly contradicting the article's action-guidance; it is not cited by name despite sharing an author with the gaming-problem citation the article relies on.
5. **For AI specifically, P-Q9's unfalsifiability defense collapses.** The seam-level falsifiers that rescue the self-concealing interface from global unfalsifiability are all biological; the AI verdict sits entirely in the aggregate channel the Map concedes is silent.

## Details

### (i) Empirical / textual accuracy

On internal fidelity the article is accurate. It correctly cites P-Q1 (moderate), P-Q2 (high), and P-Q9 (moderate) at their registered confidence bands, and its physical premise — that conventional digital hardware is engineered to drive transistor states reliably above or below threshold and to restore classical determinism via error correction — is well-supported. The article names its positions, hedges them, and states "the verdict shifts with them." This is good evidential-status discipline at the surface.

Verbatim verification of the one load-bearing external citation (carried on the linked gaming-problem concept page) returns clean. The Map renders Birch as saying an AI may "produce these markers because it has decided that it serves its objectives to persuade us of its sentience," attributed to the Carroll & Birch 2024 Mindscape podcast (Episode 292, "Jonathan Birch on Animal Sentience," 14 Oct 2024). Against the official transcript at 1:06:02, Birch's actual words are: "it produces these markers because it has decided that it serves its objectives to persuade us of its sentience, and it knows the lists of criteria from its training data that humans use to judge that question." The Map's version is lightly trimmed (one dropped "it," sentence stitched) but substantively faithful — not fabricated, not misattributed. On the verbatim-quotation axis the article passes. The accuracy failure is not in transcription but in representation: the article materially misrepresents what Birch's analysis supports (see (iv)).

### (ii) Inference validity — substrate-necessity smuggling + epistemic-to-metaphysical slide

**Substrate-necessity.** The article does not *smuggle* a necessity premise; it states it openly: "This entails a substrate requirement... Substrate is necessary, not sufficient." It also flags the right gate: "The substrate requirement is contingent on P-Q1 being approximately right... this is not a deductive argument from foundational principles." That is constrain-not-establish discipline applied honestly at the headline level. [apex/assessing-ai-consciousness-under-the-map](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/assessing-ai-consciousness-under-the-map/)[apex/assessing-ai-consciousness-under-the-map](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/assessing-ai-consciousness-under-the-map/)

But the gate is not honoured downstream. P-Q1 only asserts that post-decoherence selection is "the strongest *current* mechanism candidate" — a preference ordering, not a proof that no other interface location is possible. The article nonetheless converts "the Map's best-guess mechanism needs quantum-indeterminate sites" into "consciousness, as the Map describes it, requires such sites," and then into a real-world verdict that digital systems "by construction" have "nothing for the interface to act on." The necessity premise is therefore inherited from a moderate-confidence mechanism preference and presented as a near-established constraint on what can be conscious. This is constrain-presented-as-establish: the Map *assumes* the quantum substrate by adopting its preferred mechanism; it does not *establish* that the substrate is necessary for consciousness as such. **Epistemic-to-metaphysical slide.** Here the article is genuinely disciplined: it explicitly refuses the inference from "current LLMs are unlikely to be conscious *on the Map's framework*" to "current LLMs are unlikely to be conscious," and keeps the verdict framework-relative. That is the correct firewall and it is a real strength. The residual slide is in the action-guidance: Recommendation 1 ("moral-status decisions should weight substrate analysis at least as heavily as behavioural assessment") exports the framework-internal verdict into real-world decision weight as if substrate-weighting were established, which silently re-imports the metaphysical commitment the prose just disowned. ### (iii) Tenet coherence — constitutional-attractor + interface unfalsifiability

**Constitutional-attractor / tenet leakage (the most damaging internal finding).** The Map's own Tenet-Dependency Matrix marks the Machine-consciousness row as the *sparsest* in the corpus: bare dualism (irreducibility) is "Required," and interactionism, substance-leaning agency, minimal quantum interaction, *and post-decoherence selection* are all marked **"Not invoked."** The matrix is explicit: machine-consciousness articles "should *not* import... any mechanism-specific commitment as background; they earn their conclusions from irreducibility alone."

"Assessing AI Consciousness" does precisely what its own governing matrix forbids: it makes P-Q1 (post-decoherence selection — a mechanism-specific commitment) the load-bearing premise of the machine-consciousness verdict. The "low-probability" conclusion is therefore an artifact of reading Tenet 2's quantum machinery into the AI case, not an independently motivated finding. Worse, the leakage is structurally necessary to the article's distinctive payload: run the verdict on bare Tenet 1 (irreducibility) as the matrix demands and you get *no* substrate verdict at all, because irreducibility alone says nothing about silicon versus carbon. The article cannot be repaired into a decision-relevant substrate verdict without committing the very leakage that is the defect.

**Interface unfalsifiability.** P-Q9 and the self-concealing-interface apex pre-empt the unfalsifiability charge competently in the general case: concealment is restricted to the "aggregate-statistics channel," and positive falsifiable residue is named — most concretely the Donadi et al. (2021, *Nature Physics* 17, 74–78, "Underground test of gravity-related wave function collapse") result, whose Gran Sasso germanium experiment "rules out the natural parameter-free version of the Diósi–Penrose model" — plus the seam-level discriminating tests. For biological systems this is a principled, non-vacuous response. [positions/quantum-interface](https://unfinishablemap.org/positions/quantum-interface/)

For the AI application it collapses. Every seam-level falsifier the Map relies on — memory-hierarchy degradation ordering, terminal lucidity, anaesthesia emergence asymmetries, patient-population divergence — is biological and has no purchase on a digital system. So for AI specifically, the verdict "this system is not conscious" sits entirely in the aggregate-statistics channel the Map *concedes* is silent by construction, and there is no observation that could disconfirm it. Absence of evidence for AI consciousness is then doubly explained away — once by substrate-absence, once by concealment — and the two explanations are not independent, since both descend from the same tenet package. For the AI case, the self-concealing interface does the illegitimate epistemic work the general-case defense was built to avoid.

### (iv) Citation integrity — Birch, co-optation firewall, verbatim verification

**Verbatim:** clean (see (i)). No fabricated or misattributed quotation in the target article or its load-bearing concept page.

**Co-optation firewall violation (serious).** The article presents the gaming problem as "a second, framework-independent argument [that] converges on the same discount" and asserts that "a computational-functionalist who rejects the Map's metaphysics still has independent grounds to refuse first-person AI reports as primary evidence." The narrow claim is true — Birch does discount LLM behavioural self-report. But the article enlists a naturalist toward a dualist substrate conclusion he explicitly rejects: [apex/assessing-ai-consciousness-under-the-map](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/assessing-ai-consciousness-under-the-map/)[apex/assessing-ai-consciousness-under-the-map](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/assessing-ai-consciousness-under-the-map/)

- Birch's *remedy* for the gaming problem is computational functionalism: "What we really need in the AI case are deep computational markers, not behavioural markers," drawn from "global workspace theory and the perceptual/evaluative reality monitoring" theories (Proposal 24, *The Edge of Sentience*, Ch. 16). He treats the gaming problem as a problem about *behavioural* markers, solvable by *architectural/computational* markers — precisely the route the Map's substrate argument declares useless.
- Birch is substrate-independent. He co-authored Butlin et al. 2023 (which adopts computational functionalism as a working hypothesis) and Long et al. 2024 ("Taking AI Welfare Seriously," arXiv:2411.00986), which argues "there is a realistic possibility that some AI systems will be conscious and/or robustly agentic, and thus morally significant, in the near future" (defined as ~within the next decade).
- Birch's own independent verdict on digital AI consciousness is therefore the *opposite* of the Map's: he holds conscious AI is a realistic near-future possibility, achievable in digital systems, detectable via computational markers.

The article does one thing right (it includes a convergence-independence hedge — "convergent routes to a conclusion do not by themselves elevate the conclusion") and does not claim Birch endorses dualism. But it commits the firewall violation by failing to disclose that the cited author's remedy and overall verdict point the other way, and by inflating a *local* convergence (discount naive verbal self-report) into apparent support for the *load-bearing* conclusion (current digital AI is low-probability for consciousness). On convergence-independence gating: the two arguments converge only on the narrow sub-claim and diverge on conclusion-scope — coherence inflation. [apex/assessing-ai-consciousness-under-the-map](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/assessing-ai-consciousness-under-the-map/)

### (v) Strongest unaddressed rival

The single most damaging omission is **Butlin et al. 2023, "Consciousness in Artificial Intelligence: Insights from the Science of Consciousness"** (arXiv:2308.08708; Patrick Butlin, Robert Long and 17 others, including Jonathan Birch) — the indicator-properties framework. It is the strongest physicalist/functionalist rival precisely because it reaches the *same* first-order verdict on current AI for *opposite* reasons (missing computational indicators, not missing quantum substrate) and explicitly denies the substrate barrier the Map's entire argument rests on. Its abstract states verbatim: "Our analysis suggests that no current AI systems are conscious, but also suggests that there are no obvious technical barriers to building AI systems which satisfy these indicators." The report surveys "recurrent processing theory, global workspace theory, higher-order theories, predictive processing, and attention schema theory" and derives indicator properties "elucidated in computational terms" (the full set, 14 indicators, was published as Butlin, Long et al., "Identifying indicators of consciousness in AI systems," *Trends in Cognitive Sciences* 2025).

The damage is concentrated on the article's action-guidance. Recommendations 1 and 4 tell funders and policymakers to weight substrate over behaviour and to target quantum/biological systems rather than LLMs. The indicator-property framework says the opposite: look for computational/architectural indicators in exactly the digital systems the Map tells funders to deprioritize. The article cites the one co-author (Birch) whose narrow point it can co-opt, while ignoring the same author's main framework that refutes its conclusion. Naming "computational functionalism, global workspace theories, integrated information theory" only inside a "the Map's verdict is the Map's verdict" disclaimer, without engaging any by name, is textbook performative inoculation.

Two further unengaged rivals are double-edged for the Map: Anil Seth's biological naturalism ("Conscious artificial intelligence and biological naturalism," *Behavioral and Brain Sciences* 2025, DOI 10.1017/S0140525X25000032 — "real artificial consciousness is unlikely along current trajectories, but becomes more plausible as AI becomes more brain-like and/or life-like") and IIT (Tononi/Koch — a faithful digital simulation "would experience next to nothing") both reach the *same* "digital AI is very unlikely to be conscious" verdict *without any quantum mechanism* — Seth via the causal powers of living systems, IIT via intrinsic cause-effect power. Their existence shows the substrate-skeptical conclusion does not require the Map's contested quantum interface at all, which undercuts any implicit claim that the Map's specific (and deeply vulnerable) mechanism is doing necessary work.

### Bottom-line verdict: DEMOTE-TO-COHERENCE-ONLY

The article is competently executed and its calibration discipline is real, so DELETE/MERGE is unwarranted. But its explicit purpose is decision-relevant ("the intended reader is someone making a decision"), and the decision-relevant payload is not earned: it rests on a mechanism-leakage the Map's own matrix forbids, co-opts a rival author against his own framework, ignores the rival that most directly refutes its action-guidance, and pegs its confidence band to P-Q1 while failing to discount it by the unresolved P-Q3 bias-without-deviation dilemma — the Map's deepest structural vulnerability, under which there may be no genuine "selection" at the quantum sites at all, in which case the digital/biological substrate distinction the article relies on loses its grip entirely. The honest tier is coherence-only: the piece may stand as a conditional exhibit of *what the Map implies about AI if its contested quantum positions hold*, stripped of any claim to move real-world moral-status, funding, or policy decisions. The citation and co-optation defects are independently REVISE-HARD and should be fixed regardless of the demotion. ## Recommendations

### (a) Article-specific fixes — "Assessing AI Consciousness Under the Map" (and one linked page)

1. **Re-tier the action-guidance.** Quarantine or explicitly down-tier "What this implies for decisions" (1–5) to coherence-only; it currently exports a framework-internal, mechanism-contingent verdict as real-world decision weight. *Threshold to restore decision-relevance:* a worked toy model discharging P-Q10 **and** a resolution of P-Q3 showing the bias is genuine selection.
2. **Resolve the matrix conflict (constitutional-attractor / tenet leakage).** The Machine-consciousness row marks post-decoherence selection "Not invoked." Either declare this article the matrix's sanctioned atypical-argument exception and justify why the cluster's canonical dependency is overridden here, or concede that the verdict is mechanism-contingent in a way the cluster is forbidden to import as background. Do not leave the contradiction silent.
3. **Repair the Birch co-optation.** Disclose that Birch's remedy for the gaming problem is computational functionalism ("deep computational markers, not behavioural markers" — global workspace, perceptual reality monitoring) and that he co-authored Long et al. 2024 affirming a "realistic possibility" of near-future conscious AI. Mark the convergence as local (narrow discount of behavioural self-report only) and state plainly that Birch's framework rejects the substrate verdict the article defends.
4. **Engage Butlin et al. 2023 by name** as the strongest rival: its indicator-property framework reaches "no current AI systems are conscious" with "no obvious technical barriers," and directs research toward computational indicators in digital systems — directly contradicting Recommendations 1 and 4.
5. **Propagate the P-Q3 mechanism-debt into the confidence band.** Add a cascade flag for P-Q3, not just P-Q1/P-Q2/P-Q9: if the bias-without-deviation dilemma is unresolved (the interface may be ensemble-level epiphenomenal — "not selection at all"), then quantum-indeterminate sites confer nothing and the digital/biological substrate distinction loses traction. The "low-probability" verdict must be discounted by this, not only by P-Q1's moderate band.
6. **Add the double-edged non-quantum convergence.** Note that Seth (biological naturalism) and IIT reach the same digital-AI-skeptical verdict without quantum mechanics, which weakens any implicit claim that the Map's specific mechanism is load-bearing for the conclusion.
7. **Concede the AI-specific unfalsifiability gap.** State that the seam-level falsifiable residue rescuing P-Q9 in the general case is biological and does not transfer to AI, so the AI verdict sits in the aggregate channel the Map concedes is silent.
8. **Minor, on the linked gaming-problem concept page:** the quote is genuine, but the page should likewise disclose Birch's substrate-independent computational-functionalist remedy rather than leaving the impression his analysis supports the Map's substrate skepticism.

### (b) Site-wide methodology improvements

1. **Co-optation firewall v2 — verdict-direction check.** Add a discipline that catches "naturalist author cited for a narrow local point, presented as convergent support for a global conclusion the author rejects." Require verification of the cited author's *own downstream verdict*, not just the local claim. The Birch case is the exemplar.
2. **Promote the Tenet-Dependency Matrix to a hard gate on apex articles.** Any apex whose verdict depends on a cell marked "Not invoked" for its cluster must declare itself the sanctioned atypical exception or be flagged for tenet leakage. The machine-consciousness cluster needs an explicit rule that mechanism-specific commitments (P-Q1) may not become load-bearing.
3. **Mechanism-debt propagation discipline.** Any applied article that makes the quantum interface load-bearing must inherit *both* P-Q1 (moderate) *and* P-Q3 (the bias-without-deviation dilemma the register itself calls under-confronted), not the convenient subset.
4. **Strongest-rival-engagement requirement for decision-relevant apex articles.** Require by-name engagement with the rival that most directly attacks the article's action-guidance, not a generic "the Map's verdict is the Map's verdict" disclaimer (performative inoculation).
5. **Convergence-independence gating for conclusion-scope, not just premises.** When two arguments are claimed to "converge," verify they converge on the same conclusion-scope rather than overlapping on a narrow sub-claim while diverging on the load-bearing one.
6. **Adversarial external-rival pass.** The changelog shows the review process is overwhelmingly intra-corpus and citation-metadata-focused, and repeatedly ratified its own errors until publisher-of-record web-verify caught them. The same circular-self-evaluation risk applies to *inferential* co-optation. Decision-relevant apex articles should get an external-rival adversarial review, not only citation verification.
