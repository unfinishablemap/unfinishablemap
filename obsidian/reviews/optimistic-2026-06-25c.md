## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The hub does the Chalmersian carving cleanly: it locates filter theory as a *re-interpretation* of correlation rather than a denial of it ("filter theory denies that correlation establishes identity"), and it grounds the case where Chalmers wants it — on convergence with the hard problem, not on the anomaly tally: "the case for filter theory rests on convergence with the hard problem rather than on tallying the cluster." The illusionist-challenge section is exactly the dialectic Chalmers would run against Frankish: each anomalous case (enhancement-from-disruption, consciousness-without-behaviour, cognitive-return-despite-damage) forces the illusionist into "a separate quasi-phenomenal explanation that strains the parsimony illusionism was supposed to provide." That is the hard problem deployed as a load-bearing constraint, not gestured at.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
The MQI material is handled with restraint Stapp should welcome rather than wince at. The hub's transmission-mechanism objection routes through Minimal Quantum Interaction but immediately concedes "consciousness-selection within Born probabilities is empirically indistinguishable from random collapse." The psychedelics article quarantines the Penrose–Hameroff Orch OR microtubule hypothesis into a clearly-labelled "Speculative Appendix" that says "nothing earlier depends on it" and inherits the 2026-05-11 recategorisation from "settled refutation" to "contested, possibility-level." This is the physics–consciousness interface engaged as a *candidate mechanism held at possibility level*, not smuggled in as support — exactly the discipline that keeps the quantum-mind reading credible.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The cluster keeps first-person experience central without sentimentality. The psychedelics article's minimal-self / narrative-self distinction (Zahavi's persisting pre-reflective "for-me-ness" surviving ego dissolution while Metzinger's transparent self-model is dismantled) is precisely the "what is it like" datum that the dispute turns on — and the article correctly identifies it as the *only* place filter and Letheby make distinguishable bets ("filter theory predicts a continuing for-me-ness that bare prior-relaxation should not require"). The apex's convergence matrix is a phenomenological instrument: it asks which features of *what-it-is-like* survive each perturbation, and reads "phenomenal presence is the most robust component" off the data rather than stipulating it.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The cluster avoids crude substance dualism while preserving mind's reality. The hub's rendering-engine analogy frames the brain as constraint-and-channel rather than container ("the scene is not *produced* by the engine alone—it emerges from interplay"), and the Buddhist-parallels section explicitly flags the disanalogy (Buddhism's *anātman* vs filter theory's independent consciousness) rather than papering over it. The meditation window in the apex frames consciousness as *activity reconfiguring its own interface* ("consciousness operates the interface from the consciousness side"), which is process-friendly. Per the persona constraint: this Whiteheadian resonance is used as defeater-removal for the metaphysical picture, never as a tier-upgrade for any specific empirical claim — and the Hardline Empiricist verdict below confirms the cluster declines exactly the slippage the Process Philosopher's praise must not license.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
The apex's "No Many Worlds" tenet section gives Kane the structure he wants: altered-state phenomenology is "pervaded by genuine selection and genuine foreclosure" — the lucid dreamer who chooses to fly forecloses not-flying; the meditator who releases selection *experiences its absence*. The argument that this variation in selection capacity "is coherent only if selection is real" — that under MWI it reduces to variation in the *illusion* of selection, "explanatorily empty" — is the right way to make agency load-bearing rather than decorative.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
The cluster's epistemic-humility spine is strong and consistent. The psychedelics article concedes the introspection-disruption objection at full strength ("5-HT2A receptor saturation is a pharmacological overhaul of the systems that issue introspective reports") and answers it not by dismissing it but by arguing the disruption is "structured rather than random." The terminal-lucidity article names the precise measurement it lacks ("Prospective capture of an episode—neural activity recorded during lucidity—is the measurement that could begin to discriminate") rather than pretending the question is settled. McGinn would approve of a cluster that knows the shape of its own ignorance.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This is the persona with the most to praise here, and the verdict is load-bearing because the whole cluster's force is evidential. Four observations:

1. **Anti-tallying is the cluster's signature restraint, and it is the praise-worthy thing *not* done.** Three separate articles independently refuse to count the convergence as multiple wins: the hub ("the cluster carries the evidential weight of one pattern, not seven"), the psychedelics article ("the cluster carries the evidential weight of one pattern, not five"), and the apex ("cannot honestly be cited as multiple independent confirmations of filter theory"). Each names *why*: "the same accommodation move is available to production theorists." A weaker version of this cluster would have stacked seven anomalies into a continental-drift convergence argument; this one explicitly disarms its own strongest-looking rhetorical move. That is tenet-coherent-not-evidence-elevating discipline applied to the Map's own best material.

2. **Constrain-vs-establish is stated, not just gestured at.** The terminal-lucidity article opens by distinguishing "constraint rather than confirmation" and holds it through every section, down to the Occam's-razor tenet section ("That the filter model absorbs the phenomenon at lower cost is a point in its favour on parsimony grounds; it is not, on its own, positive evidence that the model is true"). The five-tier scale is used honestly: terminal lucidity is named "a live hypothesis the science does not decide, not a settled result."

3. **The Murray "Newtonian physics in 1887" framing is correctly *attributed*, not adopted.** The terminal-lucidity article reports Murray's framework-stage claim as a *cultural-milestone datum* ("a major public intellectual treating the evidence seriously") and immediately pairs it with Pinker's dismissal — it does not let the article speak in Murray's voice. This is the right way to cite a sympathetic public intellectual without inheriting his over-claim.

4. **Where the cluster is at risk: the dedicated terminal-lucidity article's currency claim has gone stale, and the staleness now cuts *against* the article's own framing.** See the finding under Content Strengths / the refine task below. This is the one place the Hardline Empiricist's praise must convert to a flag — not because the article over-claims (it does not), but because the empirical record has moved and the article still says it has not.
