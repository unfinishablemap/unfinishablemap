## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
`emotion-and-dualism.md` sharpens the hard problem into "why does pain feel *bad*?" and treats the valence explanatory gap as *stark precisely because valence seems so simple* — a Chalmers-friendly move that refuses to let the apparent simplicity of good/bad smuggle in reducibility.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
`probability-problem-in-many-worlds.md` ties the Map's interface commitment directly to the physics: "consciousness biasing genuinely indeterminate outcomes requires that outcomes *be* genuinely indeterminate. On MWI there is nothing to bias." That is the right place to locate the physics-consciousness dependency.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The emotion article's treatment of *Wertnehmung* (Scheler's value-perception) and of emotional depth ("quiet grief can be profound" — not physiological intensity) keeps first-person evaluative character central without collapsing it into arousal.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The Map avoids crude substance-talk in emotion: anger is "a single conscious act" fusing phenomenal character, evaluative content, and motivational force — "not layered physical processes." This resonates with process-relational readings without being used to over-claim any evidential tier.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
The MWI article's rejection of *branching indifference* — declining to treat "assign no weight to the multiplication of your successors" as a rationality axiom — protects exactly the first-person, one-branch-at-a-time agency Kane needs. The indeterminacy the Map insists on is the room genuine choice requires.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
`probability-problem-in-many-worlds.md` §"What Would Success Even Look Like?" concedes that a fully-successful Deutsch-Wallace recovery "would be a genuine achievement, and the Map does not pretend otherwise" — epistemic humility about a live 30-year dispute rather than a claimed knockdown.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This is the strongest calibration performance in the reviewed set, and its praise is load-bearing. The MWI article **explicitly separates evidence from refutation**: "the felt singularity of experience is *evidence consistent with* the Map's one-branch-at-a-time reading rather than a refutation of MWI's branch-relative reply," and names the [[possibility-probability-slippage]] discipline that governs the boundary. It goes further and flags its *own* posit as contested — "the singular 'I' is itself a contested posit, not a neutral datum," citing Parfit and Madhyamaka denial — so the tenet-level commitment is carried openly rather than dressed as a datum all parties grant. This is precisely the tenet-as-evidence-upgrade the Hardline Empiricist praises an article for **not** doing: the No-Many-Worlds commitment removes a defeater and marks a framework boundary; it is never wielded as if it were positive evidence that MWI is false. The Tenet-5 discipline is equally honest: "parsimony *cannot do load-bearing work in either direction* ... the Map cannot consistently disarm the parsimony argument *for* dualism while wielding one *against* MWI."
