## Pessimistic Analysis Summary

### Critical Issues Found (both citation-level, both fixed)

1. **Wrong-author citation (real-paper / wrong-author, 3-state).** The article cited "Sezer, I. et al. (2021), *Brain Imaging and Behavior*, 16, 1023-1045" for the grey-matter meta-analysis (25 MRI studies, right anterior ventral insula, Cohen's d ~ 0.8). The paper actually described — and whose DOI (10.1007/s11682-021-00453-4) was already in the `[^sezer]` footnote — is **Pernet, C.R., Belov, N., Delorme, A., & Zammit, A. (2021)**, *Brain Imaging and Behavior* **15(5), 2720-2730**. Three fields were wrong: first author (Sezer→Pernet), volume (16→15), pages (1023-1045→2720-2730). The cited *figures* (25 studies, insula d~0.8) are correct and match Pernet. Note: "Sezer, I." is a genuine separate author — Sezer et al. (2022), *Neuroscience & Biobehavioral Reviews* 135:104583 — so this was an author-conflation, not a fabrication. **Resolution**: Renamed reference + footnote to Pernet with correct volume/pages; both in-text uses repointed to `[^pernet]`.

2. **Wrong-study attribution on the "7 hours" dose-response claim.** The article read: "Davidson's compassion study found brain changes after just 7 hours of practice that predicted prosocial behaviour.[^davidson]" The `[^davidson]` footnote points to the 2008 UW news story about the Lutz/Davidson study of *long-term monks* (10,000+ hrs) and 2-week novices — which reports **no** "7 hours" figure and **no** prosocial-prediction result. The real "~7 hours of compassion training → altered neural responses + increased altruistic redistribution" finding is **Weng, H.Y. et al. (2013)**, *Psychological Science* **24(7), 1171-1180** (two weeks × 30 min/day ≈ 7 h). **Resolution**: Body sentence reattributed to Weng et al. (2013); added `[^weng]` footnote + reference-list entry. The separate compassion/prosocial sentence in the "Compassion and Emotional Neuroplasticity" section legitimately rests on the 2008 long-term-monk insula finding, so its `[^davidson]` citation was left intact.
