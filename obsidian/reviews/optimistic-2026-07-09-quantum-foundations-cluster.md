This is the **quantum-foundations / interpretations cluster** — the Map's account of *which reading of quantum mechanics* its interactionism rides on, and how it defends the No-Many-Worlds and Minimal-Quantum-Interaction tenets at the level of physics rather than at the level of the mind. It has not had a dedicated optimistic pass.

## Executive Summary

This cluster is one of the Map's strongest and most intellectually honest. It does the rare thing of stating a favoured interpretation (time-symmetric collapse plus objective-collapse-with-modulation), defending it against the sharpest live physics objections, and then *volunteering its own unfalsifiability as a genuine cost* rather than hiding it. The main expansion headroom is not in the core — which is mature and well-defended — but in a ring of adjacent physics-foundations topics the cluster leans on as one-line premises without ever giving them dedicated treatment: **superdeterminism**, the **consistent (decoherent) histories interpretation**, the **PBR theorem / ψ-ontology debate**, **wavefunction realism vs primitive ontology**, and **modal interpretations**. Each is load-bearing somewhere in the Map yet appears only as a citation or a dismissal.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The cluster takes the explanatory gap seriously *at the physics end* as well as the mind end. `measurement-problem.md`'s "case for structural coherence" is exactly the move Chalmers would applaud: it declines the cheap unification ("both involve first-person facts") and instead anchors the parallel on the *specific* shared feature — **determinacy from indeterminacy** — "measurement yields one definite outcome from superposition, and conscious experience is determinate where the brain states realizing it should be superposed." The Albert–Loewer determinate-experience framing gives the hard problem a foothold *inside* physics without collapsing one problem into the other. Chalmers would also note the article's care to mark the disclosure reading as "an inference to the better reading, not something the result forces" — naturalistic dualism's own epistemic register.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
This cluster is Stapp's natural home, and it treats his lineage with unusual precision. `von-neumann-wigner-interpretation.md` gets the history exactly right — von Neumann's movable cut, London–Bauer 1939, Wigner's 1961 thesis and *significant* retreat — and then makes a genuinely sophisticated distinction Stapp himself would recognise: that his Process-1 "Heisenberg choice" placement "leaves the Schrödinger dynamics and the Born rule intact, a genuine virtue for theoretical conservativeness even if not strictly for minimality." Stapp would appreciate that the Map does not merely borrow his name but *works out where it diverges from him* — endorsing post-decoherence outcome-selection over Process-1 context-selection because Bidirectional Interaction commits it to influence over *which outcome*, not merely *which question*. That is engagement at the level Stapp always demanded: within the von Neumann formalism, not gesturing at it.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The "indexical dimension" running through `measurement-problem.md` is the Nagelian core: even granting definite outcomes, "why does *this* consciousness experience *this* particular result?" The cluster consistently refuses to let the third-person formalism swallow the first-person fact — and, crucially, it states the functionalist reply fairly ("the functionalist who denies the extra fact denies the explanandum honestly") rather than strawmanning it. Nagel would recognise the vertiginous-question framing as the right name for the boundary where objective description runs out.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
`quantum-interpretations.md`'s "Process Philosophy Perspective" section is textbook Whitehead done well: actual occasions with physical and mental poles mapped onto quantum events, "the wave function encodes possibilities, collapse represents selection," with the discriminating criterion made explicit — "whether the mental pole does explanatory work." MWI eliminates it, Bohm makes it epiphenomenal, time-symmetric interpretations make it efficacious. This is process-philosophical *compatibility* deployed correctly: as a defeater-removal for the metaphysical possibility of efficacious experience, not as evidence that the mechanism obtains. The article keeps that boundary.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
The cluster supplies the physics substrate Kane's event-causal libertarianism needs without over-claiming it: genuine (not merely epistemic) indeterminacy at measurement, an energy-neutral selection channel ("selecting among equi-energetic outcomes requires no added energy, provided any bias preserves Born statistics over ensembles"), and an explicit statement that hidden-variables and superdeterminism would *close* the opening. Kane would want exactly this honesty about what has to be true of physics for libertarian freedom to have room.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
The Gödelian-parallel section in `measurement-problem.md` is McGinn-friendly at its best: it reaches for genuine formal results (Cubitt et al. 2015 spectral-gap undecidability; Landsman 2020 on Chaitin) *and* cites Franzén's caution against category error, endorsing only "the structural level." The willingness to say the unification "generates no new predictions, and critics are right that it creates a larger mystery" is precisely the intellectual humility the Mysterian prizes — the Map marks the boundary of what it can show rather than papering over it.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This cluster is where the Map's evidential discipline is at its most exemplary, and it earns strong praise on Birch's own terms. Three things it **praise-worthily does *not* do**:

1. It does not let the measurement-problem/hard-problem parallel do more work than its specificity licenses — it explicitly guards against the "looser version [that] would equally link the hard problem to why there is something rather than nothing."
2. It does not upgrade the causal-closure *loophole* into evidence: "The loophole shows where non-physical causes *could* operate without conflicting with physical law — not that consciousness does," and it lists brute stochasticity and non-conscious selection as live alternatives on the same footing.
3. Most impressively, `measurement-problem.md` flags its own strongest heuristic as *self-undermining*: the "least disruptive locus" appeal "is a simplicity consideration, and the Map's own fifth tenet holds simplicity unreliable under incomplete knowledge ... a defeasible heuristic for *where* to look, not evidence that consciousness acts there, and it stands in acknowledged tension with the tenet that warns against trusting it." That is a model of register-separation.

And the unfalsifiability disclosures are exactly the "name the cost" discipline Birch wants: `measurement-problem.md`'s "**Honest limitation**: This framework predicts exactly what standard quantum mechanics predicts ... This unfalsifiability is a genuine cost," and `von-neumann-wigner-interpretation.md`'s "the modulation thesis is *empirically indistinguishable from chance under any aggregate-statistics test* current or foreseeable instruments could run." The cluster keeps possibility and evidence on separate axes throughout. The Process Philosopher and the Hardline Empiricist **converge** here rather than conflict — the Whiteheadian compatibility claim and the evidential-restraint claim are both present and neither is doing the other's job. That convergence is the signature of an honestly-calibrated cluster.

## Content Strengths

### concepts/von-neumann-wigner-interpretation.md
- **Strongest point**: The "ancestor not final form" framing, executed with real precision. It front-loads *two* clarifications before inheriting anything — "The Map's actual thesis is weaker than V-N-W's headline" and "What the Map keeps from V-N-W is the problem space, not the mechanism" — pre-empting the single most common misreading of the Map (that it holds "consciousness causes collapse").
- **Notable quote**: "The Map's lineage claim is therefore not 'Wigner was right' but 'Wigner identified the right problem space.'"
- **Why it works**: It converts a historical association that could be a liability (Wigner's discredited strong thesis, solipsism worries) into an asset by explicitly banking the problem-identification and discarding the mechanism. The Frauchiger–Renner handling is exemplary — it concedes F–R "puts pressure on V-N-W rather than supporting it" rather than spinning a no-go theorem as friendly.

### concepts/measurement-problem.md
- **Strongest point**: The three-way partition of the loophole (brute stochasticity / hidden variables / non-conscious selection) followed by an explicit "positive case" section that grades its *own* arguments as non-decisive. The article never lets the reader mistake permissibility for evidence.
- **Notable quote**: "physics fixes the *distribution* of outcomes but leaves the *selection* of individual outcomes unexplained."
- **Why it works**: The structural/ontological completeness distinction (physics is "*structurally* complete ... yet *ontologically* partial") is the cleanest one-line statement of the Map's whole physics stance anywhere in the corpus, and it is stated as a *result* the Map borrows, not a claim it needs to defend.

### concepts/quantum-interpretations.md
- **Strongest point**: The comparison table plus per-interpretation "Site compatibility" ratings give an LLM-fetchable, at-a-glance map of the whole landscape, and the "What Would Challenge This View?" section names four concrete defeaters including the live Tegmark/Hagan/Reimers decoherence dispute — presented as "live, not settled either way" rather than resolved in the Map's favour.
- **Notable quote**: "the question [is] not whether physics rules out consciousness-participation (it doesn't) but which interpretation best explains both quantum phenomena and consciousness."
- **Why it works**: The MWI rebuttal engages the *strongest* modern defence (Wallace's decision-theoretic self-locating-uncertainty account) and concedes it is "not a defeater of MWI, but ... the indexical argument ... has been relocated, not answered." Steelmanning the rival before rejecting it is exactly the register that makes the rejection credible.

## Expansion Opportunities

### High Priority

#### Superdeterminism and the measurement-independence loophole
- **Builds on**: `measurement-problem.md` (lists superdeterminism as a defeater of the causal-closure opening), `apex/what-consciousness-tells-us-about-physics.md` (rules it out in two places), and the free-will cluster (superdeterminism is equally a defeater of libertarian freedom via the Bell-test free-choice assumption).
- **Would address**: The Map currently *dismisses* superdeterminism in a single clause everywhere it appears ("superdeterminism eliminates the indeterminacy that makes consciousness relevant") but never gives it a dedicated steelman-and-response. This is the single most important defeater of BOTH Tenet 2's quantum opening and libertarian free will, and it deserves the same serious engagement the Map gives MWI. The measurement-independence (statistical-independence) assumption in Bell's theorem, 't Hooft's cellular-automaton interpretation, and Hossenfelder & Palmer's (2020) "Rethinking Superdeterminism" are the obvious anchors.
- **Estimated scope**: Medium-Long article.
- **Tenet alignment**: Directly load-bearing for Tenets 2 and 3 — the whole causal-closure loophole vanishes if superdeterminism holds. A dedicated treatment lets the Map own its dependence on genuine indeterminacy.

#### The consistent (decoherent) histories interpretation
- **Builds on**: `comparing-quantum-consciousness-mechanisms.md` bundles it as "family 5: history selection," but the main `quantum-interpretations.md` survey **omits it entirely**.
- **Would address**: A genuine hole in a survey that presents itself as comprehensive. Consistent histories (Griffiths, Omnès, Gell-Mann & Hartle) is a major single-world, no-collapse, realist interpretation that reframes the measurement problem via "frameworks" rather than solving it — and its framework-dependence / preferred-set problem is precisely the kind of thing the Map's disclosure reading could engage. Its absence is conspicuous next to the article's careful treatment of Bohm, QBism, RQM, and TI/TSVF.
- **Estimated scope**: Medium article.
- **Tenet alignment**: A consciousness-free single-world rival; engaging it strengthens the No-Many-Worlds tenet's flank (shows the Map has considered single-world realist alternatives to MWI, not just MWI).

### Medium Priority

#### The PBR theorem and the ψ-ontic / ψ-epistemic debate
- **Builds on**: `quantum-completeness.md`, `physical-completeness.md`, `quantum-probability-consciousness.md` all cite PBR (2012) as a one-sentence premise grounding the wavefunction's reality and the rejection of QBism.
- **Would address**: PBR is load-bearing for the Map's realism (it is *why* the Map can reject QBism's anti-realism) yet never gets a dedicated treatment of the Harrigan–Spekkens ψ-ontology framework or the debated auxiliary preparation-independence assumption (Emerson, Serbin, Sutherland & Veitch 2013, already cited once in `quantum-completeness.md`). A dedicated article would consolidate a premise the Map repeatedly leans on. *Self-check*: this is threaded, not absent — the harvester may read it as covered by `quantum-completeness.md`'s no-go-theorems section. Named here as a *deepening* candidate rather than a pure gap.
- **Estimated scope**: Medium article.
- **Tenet alignment**: Underwrites Tenet 1's realism (the wavefunction is real, so there is something physical for consciousness to modulate).

#### Wavefunction realism vs primitive ontology
- **Builds on**: Nothing yet — a **zero-mention gap** (grep across concepts/topics/apex returns no content hits for "wavefunction realism," "primitive ontology," or "local beables").
- **Would address**: The question "*what* does consciousness select among, ontologically?" The Albert/Ney configuration-space-realism camp vs the Maudlin/Allori/Goldstein primitive-ontology (local-beables) camp bears directly on what the Map's modulation acts *on*. The Map talks constantly about consciousness biasing "which outcome becomes actual" without ever fixing the ontology of the thing the outcomes are outcomes *of*.
- **Estimated scope**: Medium article.
- **Tenet alignment**: Sharpens Tenets 1–3 by specifying the ontological target of interface action.
