## The Finding (calibration honesty, not retraction)

The article wields Kim's exclusion argument against non-reductive physicalism ("once content supervenes on a physical base that already has all the credentials to serve as a sufficient cause, the supervenient description does no additional causal work") and demands as its positive requirement that "the content and validity of a thought makes a causal difference to which belief forms," invoking the quantum interface as the HOW.

But the Map's *preferred* reading of that interface is **corridor dualism**, whose defining feature is that conscious selection averages exactly to the Born measure and makes no aggregate difference — so the physical Born distribution "already has all the credentials" to fix which-belief-forms statistics, and token-level selection adds nothing at the ensemble level. That is the SAME exclusion shape the article presses against Kim's target, now recurring against the Map's OWN mechanism — the ensemble-level-epiphenomenalism question the [[born-rule-and-the-consciousness-interface]] article and its apex treatment already flag as the Map's sharpest open question. The article presented the interface as clean, established-enough causal efficacy without flagging that whether it delivers content-efficacious causation AT ALL is the Map's own open question.

> Honesty requires adding that the Map's *preferred* reading of that interface—corridor dualism, on which conscious selection averages exactly to the Born measure—faces a *symmetric* version of the very exclusion pressure this article presses against non-reductive physicalism: if the physical Born distribution already has all the credentials to fix which-belief-forms statistics, token-level selection adds nothing the ensemble registers, and the mechanism looks ensemble-level epiphenomenal at exactly the point rational efficacy is needed. Whether the corridor interface delivers content-efficacious causation *at all* is [[ensemble-level-epiphenomenalism|the Map's own sharpest open question]], logged as such in [[born-rule-and-the-consciousness-interface]]; the argument from reason's conclusion—that consciousness must be causally efficacious—therefore stands here as a requirement the framework must *meet*, not as one the Map's default mechanism can yet be said to have met.

The argument-from-reason CONCLUSION (consciousness must be causally efficacious) stands as a requirement the framework must meet; what no longer stands is the impression that the Map's own mechanism ALREADY meets it, when the corpus elsewhere holds that open.
