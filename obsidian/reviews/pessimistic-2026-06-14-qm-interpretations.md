## Critiques by Philosopher

### The Eliminative Materialist
Churchland would say the whole survey presupposes that "consciousness" names a real explanatory target rather than folk-psychological residue awaiting elimination. The article's framing — "which interpretations leave room for consciousness as a causal participant" — smuggles in the very reification she rejects. *Article's standing defence*: the piece is explicit (Honest limitation box, "compatibility is not support") that leaving-room ≠ support, and treats consciousness-collapse as a live minority option, not established science. The eliminativist's deeper charge — that the target itself is illusory — is a framework-boundary disagreement the article does not pretend to refute in-framework. Adequately handled.

### The Hard-Nosed Physicalist
Dennett would press that the article inflates the "measurement problem ↔ hard problem identical logical structure" parallel (line 54) into metaphysics. Both "gaps," he'd argue, are artifacts of insisting on a first-person fact over and above the complete third-person description — exactly the intuition-pump he distrusts. The article asserts the structural identity but leans on a linked page (`quantum-measurement-and-consciousness`) to argue it; within *this* article the parallel is stated more than earned. Not a defect (it is sign-posted to the dedicated treatment), but the load-bearing claim is imported, not demonstrated here.

### The Quantum Skeptic
Tegmark's decoherence objection is the one the article most owes, and it engages it squarely in the Decoherence Challenge section: it states Tegmark's 10⁻¹³ s figure, concedes the objection is "serious but not decisive," and — crucially — gives the *classical* reading of the Khan 2024 epothilone-B result equal footing ("the data fits both readings"). This is honest. A residual Tegmark rejoinder: even granting the microtubule caveat, the article never quantifies what coherence time *would* suffice for the proposed selection role, so "not decisive" rests on the absence of a calculation on both sides. The article admits the mechanism is unspecified, so this is acknowledged rather than hidden.

### The Many-Worlds Defender
Deutsch would say the rejection of MWI is anthropocentric intuition overriding the Schrödinger equation's elegance. The article's response is its strongest disciplinary moment: the "Engaging MWI's strongest responses" passage steelmans Saunders (2010) and Sebens-Carroll (2018) branch-relative indexicality, concedes the reply is "internally available to MWI… not incoherent on Everettian terms," and frames the Map's reservation explicitly as framework-boundary, *not* in-framework refutation. This is exactly what the direct-refutation discipline requires. No boundary-substitution failure here.

### The Empiricist
The Popperian charge bites hardest: the favoured interpretations (TI/TSVF, Φ-modulated CSL) are conceded to be empirically equivalent to standard QM ("reproduces quantum predictions exactly," "permits but doesn't require"). What experiment could discriminate the Map's preferred reading from its rivals? The article does not name one. *Mitigation*: it never claims falsifiability — the comparative-assessment box explicitly lists "empirical risk" as an axis on which its favoured rows score low, and the Honest-limitation framing concedes this is "philosophical space, not scientific consensus." The empiricist's complaint is registered by the article against itself. Honest, but the falsifiability gap remains the framework's deepest vulnerability and is structural, not fixable by editing.

### The Buddhist Philosopher
Nagarjuna would target the indexical-identity argument (the Map's core anti-MWI move): the "real further fact about which successor *I* am" reifies precisely the permanent self Madhyamaka deconstructs. If the self is empty, "why am I this branch?" is a malformed question — which is closer to the MWI dissolution the article rejects than to its own position. The article does not engage the emptiness critique at all; its indexical realism is asserted as a tenet ("carried in openly as a tenet"). This is a genuine un-engaged opponent, though defensibly out of scope for a QM-interpretations survey (the self/no-self question lives in the personal-identity cluster).
