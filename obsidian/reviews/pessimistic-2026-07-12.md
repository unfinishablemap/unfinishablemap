## Critical Issues

### Issue 1: The "narrator" rebuttal asserts past Dennett rather than engaging his on-record answer
- **File**: `obsidian/concepts/multiple-drafts-model.md`
- **Location**: "Relation to Site Perspective" → "The deeper structural point…" paragraph (line 55): *"Even a purely retrospective, narratively-imposed unity requires a narrator — a subject for whom the draft is edited into a story… MDM thus moves the hard question from 'where does it all come together?' to 'for whom is the narrative assembled?' — a relocation, not a resolution."*
- **Problem**: The passage presents the "for whom?" question as one MDM has *failed to resolve* ("a relocation, not a resolution"). But Dennett's cerebral-celebrity / fame-in-the-brain account — which this same article describes at line 41 ("conscious not by reaching the stage but by winning a competition for lasting, system-wide effect") — *is* his answer: there is no "for whom," only downstream influence with no audience. The rebuttal's premise ("narrative requires a narrator / a subject for whom") is precisely the subject-requiring commitment Dennett denies and which he pre-empts as the smuggled theater (the article records this pre-emption at line 25). As written, the rebuttal therefore either (a) begs the question against MDM by assuming the audience-structure MDM rejects, or (b) marks a Map commitment while dressing it as an in-framework relocation that shows Dennett incomplete. Under the direct-refutation discipline this reads as the *in-framework-argument-that-actually-appeals-to-Map-commitments* failure: the disagreement is not earned inside Dennett's framework because the load-bearing step depends on the Map's own subject-realism, and Dennett's explicit counter is not engaged at the crux.
- **Severity**: Medium. It is the "deeper structural point" of the parting-company section and it overclaims (asserting Dennett has *no* resolution), but the philosophical substance — that the Map judges the subject-for-whom a genuine unmet burden — is legitimate and worth keeping.
- **Fixability**: Additive conditionalization, no deletion. The fix is to (1) surface Dennett's fame-in-the-brain answer *at this point* ("Dennett's reply is that there is no 'for whom' — cerebral celebrity is influence without an audience"), then (2) reframe the narrator point from a *demonstrated* relocation into a *requirement the Map holds MDM has not met*: the Map contends that a narrative edited "into a story" presupposes an interpreter and that the no-audience competition-for-influence picture does not honestly discharge that, but this is the Map pressing a burden Dennett rejects, not a refutation on his terrain. That converts an assert-past into the honest standoff the three sibling articles already model (cf. type-identity line 64's explicit "the Map does not pretend to refute the identity theory from within the identity theorist's framework — the incompatibility is a commitment, honestly marked as such"). The MDM article should inherit that same honesty at line 55.
