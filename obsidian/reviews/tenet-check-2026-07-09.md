No article claims consciousness is reducible, illusory, or epiphenomenal.
- **Tenet 2 (Minimal Quantum Interaction)** — ALIGNED. The free-will articles locate the Map's positive machinery at the post-decoherence-selection interface and correctly flag its empirical indistinguishability-from-chance cost (manipulation article: "empirically hard to distinguish from chance"). No quantum-woo, no energy injection, no detectable-PK claim.
- **Tenet 3 (Bidirectional Interaction)** — ALIGNED. The consciousness-as-genuine-selection (not randomiser) framing is preserved throughout the free-will trio. The aesthetics articles invoke Tenet 3 only "thematically" (aesthetic value moves us to reshape/protect the world) and explicitly refuse to overstate the thematic fit into shared metaphysics. No epiphenomenalist drift.
- **Tenet 4 (No Many Worlds)** — ALIGNED. The Consequence and Frankfurt articles actively deploy No-MWI: the indeterminism the Map needs "must involve genuine collapse to a single actual outcome, not Everettian branching," and sourcehood "presupposes that *this* agent actualized *this* outcome and not a counterpart's." No branch is treated as equally real; indexical identity is upheld.
- **Tenet 5 (Occam's Razor Has Limits)** — ALIGNED, and applied symmetrically. Every article that leans on Tenet 5 against a deflationary/parsimony rival also concedes the move buys no positive support for the Map's reading ("parsimony is a consideration, not a verdict"; the manipulation article: blocks the objection "from being a refutation; it does not convert the Map's bet into a demonstration").
