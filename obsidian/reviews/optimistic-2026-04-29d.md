### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)

The numerosity-void's restructure into "dialectical, not knockdown" framing is exemplary property-dualist epistemic posture. *"On the phenomenal reading the Map adopts—dialectical, not knockdown—this dissociation suggests cardinality-perception is a distinct conscious operation rather than a downstream function of attentional bandwidth. The behavioural data alone is consistent with a deflationary alternative on which the dissociation marks two cognitive routines with different latencies and error profiles, with no upgrade to 'distinct conscious operation' required."* Chalmers's distinction between phenomenal and access-consciousness requires precisely this honesty about what the empirical data alone warrants vs. what the phenomenal reading adds; the article makes the interpretive layer visible as interpretive. This is the move Chalmers makes in *The Conscious Mind* when separating coverage of "structure and dynamics" from coverage of phenomenal character — and the article executes it with empirical anchoring rather than from-the-armchair assertion.

The strong-emergence article's three-test signature for non-reducibility — *the explanatory gap is structural not informational; functional definability is absent; third-person accessibility fails* — is one of the catalogue's cleanest property-dualist case statements. The signature converts the inductive challenge ("everything else got reduced") into a *type-level* response: "every property *of the type amenable to reduction* was reduced." The corrective is sharp: extending the inductive argument to consciousness "is like extending the success of veterinary medicine to treating diseases in rocks — it mistakes a domain-specific pattern for a universal law." This is Chalmers-grade type-discrimination applied to the reductive-track-record argument, and it does the dialectical work the article needs without overclaiming.
