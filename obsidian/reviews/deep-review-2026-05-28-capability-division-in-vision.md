## Pessimistic Analysis Summary

### Critical Issues Found (citation-currency pass)

- **Misattribution: niac018 is Dylan Ludwig, not "Fahrenfort et al."** The reference "Fahrenfort, J. J. et al. (2023). 'The functions of consciousness in visual processing.' *Neuroscience of Consciousness*, 2023(1), niac018" is a **single-authored paper by Dylan Ludwig** (York University). Verified against PubMed (PMID 36628118) and the Oxford Academic/PMC records. The article cited the phantom "Fahrenfort et al. 2023" in three in-text places plus the reference list. **Resolution**: reference corrected to `Ludwig, D. (2023)`; in-text citations at the feature-extraction and perceptual-organisation claims repointed to `(Ludwig, 2023)`, which the paper genuinely reviews.
  - *Note*: The sibling article `voids/capability-division-problem.md` had this **identical** misattribution caught and fixed on 2026-03-15 (replaced with Fahrenfort et al. 2017 PNAS). The fix was never propagated to the vision article, and the 2026-04-17 deep review then *re-used* the phantom citation to support a brand-new claim — compounding the defect.

- **Unsupported claim attached to a source that does not make it.** The 2026-04-17 review added "ventral-pathway activity is substantially higher for consciously perceived stimuli than for matched unconscious stimuli (Fahrenfort et al., 2023)." The real niac018 (Ludwig) makes **no** regional ventral-stream activation comparison; it is a functional-pluralism review, not an fMRI activation study. **Resolution**: the unsupported quantitative magnitude claim was removed and the passage reframed to the well-supported two-streams dependence claim (Goodale & Milner, 1992) plus Ludwig's functional clustering, with no fabricated activation figure.

- **Author-order error: Goodale & Milner, not "Milner & Goodale" (1992).** The canonical 1992 *Trends in Neurosciences* paper "Separate visual pathways for perception and action" is bylined **Goodale, M. A. & Milner, A. D.** (Goodale first) — confirmed via the publisher PDF byline, Semantic Scholar, and PhilPapers. The article reversed it in two in-text citations and the reference (likely confusion with the 1995 *Milner & Goodale* book *The Visual Brain in Action*). **Resolution**: all three occurrences corrected to "Goodale & Milner."
  - *Note*: the same reversal lives in `voids/capability-division-problem.md` and `research/capability-division-vision-2026-03-08.md`, and the 2026-05-20 deep review of the voids article wrongly asserted "Milner & Goodale 1992 — correctly attributed." See follow-up task.

### Medium Issues Found

- **Phillips (2021) gloss omitted the load-bearing mechanism.** The article described Phillips's view as "residual phenomenal experience — faint, fragmented, difficult to report" but omitted that the *reason* it appears unconscious, on Phillips's account, is a **conservative response bias** (high decision threshold). **Resolution**: gloss tightened to name the response-bias mechanism, improving source fidelity. Citation metadata (Psych Review 128(3), 558-584) verified correct.

- **Perceptual-organisation threshold over-stated as a clean transition.** Reframed to "candidate transition" and added the Fahrenfort et al. 2017 (PNAS) complication — that some perceptual integration proceeds *without* conscious access with an indistinguishable neural signature — so the boundary now reads as "where integration reliably tracks awareness" rather than an on/off switch. This matches the hedged framing the sibling void article already uses.
