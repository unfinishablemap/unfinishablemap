## Executive Summary

This is a model of evidential-status discipline — arguably the best-calibrated empirical article in the corpus. It builds a two-sided case (placebo = positive content-direction; choking = negative mode-interference), then spends as much energy demarcating what the studies *do not* establish as asserting what they do, explicitly conceding that every leading physicalist rival (predictive coding, reinvestment/overarousal, nonreductive physicalism) covers the same data. The Process Philosopher and the Hardline Empiricist converge in praising the same passages — the surest sign the possibility/probability tension has been resolved honestly rather than papered over. Expansion is essentially foreclosed by section caps (topics/ 321/320); the one genuine in-place improvement is a missing cross-link where the article twice invokes "Kim's exclusion argument" without linking the existing concept page.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The article keeps the hard problem load-bearing without letting it do illegitimate work. Its repeated move — "what this does not obviously explain is *why these functional roles are accompanied by specific conscious experiences*" (line 89) — is exactly the explanatory-gap wedge Chalmers would drive: functionalism can match circuit to outcome, but the felt quality that tracks content "so closely" is left unexplained. The closing "Why those priors are accompanied by phenomenal experience returns us to the hard problem" (line 103) refuses the deflation without overclaiming a positive result.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
Stapp would note the article's restraint about its own tenet: the Minimal-Quantum-Interaction section (line 189) concedes the pathways "operate well above quantum scales" and that the interface point is "out of scope here." He would read this as the right division of labour — the macroscale data establish the *explanandum* (that consciousness is efficacious), while the *how* (the quantum interface) is deferred to the tenet, not smuggled into the empirical case. This is the discipline that keeps interactionism from over-committing at the wrong grain.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The choking side centres the first-person mode-switch — "the reported switch from absorbed to self-aware execution" (line 109) — as the datum, and Nagel would praise that the article treats the *felt* mode (absorbed vs. self-monitoring) as a real explanandum the represented-state story must still account for, not as noise. The Tulving "anoetic consciousness" framing (line 131) gives the withdrawal-of-consciousness phase a genuine phenomenological texture.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The "matched grain" argument (lines 143, 149) — that the phenomenal description selects the causal pathway "at the grain the brain implements the selection" across two independent effector systems — is a process-philosophical strength: mind and mechanism coupling at a shared grain rather than mind hovering over an alien substrate. Crucially, the article does **not** use this resonance to upgrade any evidential tier: the displayed-regularity argument is explicitly flagged as "real but weaker than discrimination" (line 169). Process-compatible *and* non-elevating — the honest pattern.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
The Agent Causation section (lines 157–161) gives agency an empirical foothold without overreach: the placebo subject is "*expecting* relief, and that expecting has consequences," and the phenomenology of mental effort "feel[s] like *doing*, not observing." Kane would appreciate that the article immediately adds the honest caveat — "the phenomena themselves do no work against [Kim's] argument… the metaphysical defeater is carried by agent causation plus Minimal Quantum Interaction, not by the data" (line 161). Agency is taken seriously *and* kept off the empirical books.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
The "What Is Established and What Is Not" section (lines 163–175) is sustained epistemic humility: three explicit limits (mechanism unspecified, generalisation non-trivial, future neuroscience could rewrite the picture). "Both phenomena might one day be fully explained in neural-system-competition terms; the hard problem would remain, but these cases would lose their empirical leverage" (line 175) is precisely McGinn's stance — the argument's defeasibility is stated plainly rather than hedged away.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This is the article's centre of gravity, and it earns the persona's strongest praise. The load-bearing verdict:

- **Tenet-as-evidence-upgrade is praise-worthily *not* done.** The article had every structural opportunity to let Tenet-3 coherence pull the felt-character claim up from "compatible with" to "supported by," and explicitly declined: "a phenomenon predicted equally well by a leading physicalist rival is *compatible with* the dualist reading, not *support for* it, absent an independent discriminator" (line 167). It then names the discriminator it would need (felt engagement vs. estimated precision) and concedes the designs cannot supply it because "precision and conscious occurrence covary in these designs" (line 167). That is textbook register-separation.
- **The named pattern is used explicitly.** "Dualism finds these cases coherent, not confirmed" (line 187) is the "tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating" move stated almost verbatim.
- **Honest downgrades under new evidence.** The open-label section (lines 93–97) walks the *strong* content-specificity claim *down* in light of Fendel et al. (2025) — self-report SMD ≈ 0.39 vs. objective ≈ 0.09 (near-null) — conceding the open-label effect "concentrates where the outcome is itself a felt report." The Hróbjartsson & Gøtzsche (2010) Cochrane baseline is stated up front as "a small, bias-vulnerable continuous-outcome effect, not a large one" (line 95). An advocate's article would have buried both.
- **Cross-species defeater faced head-on.** The animal literature (line 103) is used *against* the phenomenology-specific claim, not for the tenet: conditioned rodents and monkeys produce objective effects "without reportable human phenomenology," so "the phenomenology-specific claim is carried by the human cases… not the cross-species pattern." The article refuses the broadening of "consciousness" that would have flattered a panpsychist reading.

Birch's one note (below) is a framing nicety, not a calibration fault: the article could name its own stage explicitly via [[framework-stage-calibration]], since it already performs the pre-Keplerian move of describing a regularity it cannot yet mechanise.

## Content Strengths

### topics/empirical-phenomena-mental-causation.md
- **Strongest point**: The demarcation architecture. The article is organised so that the concession sections ("Open-Label Placebos and the Limits of the Argument," "What Is Established and What Is Not") are structurally co-equal with the positive case, not tucked into caveats. The displayed-regularity argument is correctly typed as *weaker* than discrimination and never allowed to masquerade as the latter.
- **Notable quote**: "So the honest reading is not that the conjunction *raises the cost* of accommodation but that it *displays, in two independent registers, the regularity the brute-law view must treat as primitive*" (line 149) — a rare instance of an advocacy article actively deflating its own rhetorical high ground to state the epistemics precisely.
- **Why it works**: The conjunction argument does real work the persona can endorse — two independent registers (content in placebo, mode in choking), different effector systems, matched grain — so the "brute psychophysical law" the epiphenomenalist posits is shown to be *broad* (must cover both registers) without the article pretending breadth equals refutation. The Benedetti et al. (2003) dissociation (analgesic/motor channels driven by conscious verbal content; hormonal channel driven by unconscious conditioning) is handled with unusual precision (line 85) — the article rests its case only on the channels the evidence actually supports.
