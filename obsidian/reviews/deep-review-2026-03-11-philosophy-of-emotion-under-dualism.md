## Pessimistic Analysis Summary

### Medium Issues Found
- **Overstated decomposition failure claim**: "Persistent failure of every decomposition strategy" was too strong. Qualified to "no decomposition strategy has yet captured what emotions feel like from the inside." Resolution: applied.
- **Quantum mechanism presented too casually**: "Provides a framework" language was insufficiently hedged per writing style guide. Reframed as "one speculative framework" with note that the philosophical point doesn't depend on the mechanism. Resolution: applied.
- **Heterophenomenology challenge unacknowledged**: The non-composite section asserted phenomenal unity without addressing the functionalist response (change blindness, grand illusion). Added a paragraph engaging this objection. Resolution: applied.
- **Slight overstatement of Damasio**: Characterizing his view as making consciousness "causally idle" is slightly stronger than Damasio's own framing, but the article attributes this to the model's implications rather than Damasio's words. Resolution: left as-is (acceptable interpretation).
- **Missing tenet subsections**: Relation to Site Perspective covered only 3 of 5 tenets (missing Minimal Quantum Interaction and No Many Worlds). Added both. Resolution: applied.

### Counterarguments Considered
- **Heterophenomenology (Dennett)**: The "from the inside" appeal could be seen as question-begging. Addressed by adding engagement with the functionalist unity challenge in the non-composite section.
- **Change blindness / grand illusion**: The claim that emotions are phenomenally unified was asserted without addressing evidence that perceptual unity may be constructed. Addressed with a new paragraph acknowledging this while arguing the character of constructed unity still resists functional analysis.
- **Buddhist dependent arising**: Emotions as "irreducible conscious acts" reifies what may be dependently arising processes. Noted as a bedrock philosophical disagreement, not a flaw.
