### Medium Issues Found
- **Functionalism critique too thin**: The article dismissed functionalism by asserting that functional descriptions specify causal rather than justificatory relations, without engaging the functionalist's actual claim that justificatory relations *are* certain causal patterns. **Resolution**: Added a sentence acknowledging and responding to this functionalist move — that collapsing justification into causation eliminates the normative force the account needs to preserve.

Article implicitly addresses this via the self-stultification structure (any argument against normativity invokes normativity), but does not name eliminativism explicitly. Deferred — article is already well-focused.
- **Dennett-style functionalism**: Justificatory relations as causal patterns at the right level of abstraction. Addressed via the sharpened functionalism paragraph.
- **Logical realism assumption**: Article assumes logical norms exist independently of thinkers (Platonic realism) without explicitly committing to or defending this position. Low priority — the article's purpose is to argue normativity resists physical reduction, not to settle the metaphysics of logic.
