### Empirical-Record Currency Sweep (post-2023 aphantasia literature)

**Material update applied at line 129 (the aphantasia-prediction paragraph).** The prior text framed the divergence as untested: "this has not been confirmed with controlled studies measuring both neural activation and performance outcomes." A 2024 controlled study — **Dupont, Papaxanthis, Madden-Lombardi & Lebon (2024), *Brain Communications* 6(2), fcae072** — now bears directly on it: 14 aphantasics vs 14 controls, TMS/MEP measurement, finding corticospinal excitability rose during kinesthetic imagery and action observation in controls but **did not increase in any condition for aphantasics** (a genuine neurophysiological deficit, explicit and implicit).

Calibration significance: this evidence cuts toward the **identity-theory branch**, not the dualist one. The article's divergence (lines 127-129) was: identity theory predicts reduced aphantasic benefit *only if* neural imagery circuits are correspondingly impaired; dualism predicts reduced benefit *even when circuits are intact*. Dupont shows the neural simulation circuits ARE impaired in aphantasia — the identity-theory-consistent condition. The paragraph was rewritten to (a) cite Dupont, (b) note it currently favours the identity-theory reading, (c) preserve that isolating a distinct phenomenal contribution still needs the harder result (reduced benefit despite *intact* circuits, with longitudinal performance + neural measures in the same subjects — which no study has yet done), and (d) keep the honest verdict that the prediction "cannot bear argumentative weight for the dualist position; if anything, the newest evidence points the other way."
