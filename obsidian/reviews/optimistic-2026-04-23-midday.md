## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)

Chalmers' 1996 programme insists that psychophysical laws must be *posited* on the back of the conceivability argument, not deduced from the physical — and that their acceptance rests on whether they earn their place against the hard problem, not on whether they produce detectable trajectory anomalies. Today's work operationalises this Chalmersian temperament in two complementary places. First, `topics/mathematical-structure-of-the-consciousness-physics-interface.md` lines 71-79 now name *three* positions MQI can underwrite — corridor dualism, minimum-outside-the-corridor dualism, and trumping dualism — and explicitly adopt corridor dualism as the Map's *default working hypothesis* rather than as a forced conclusion: *"a choice responsive to current evidence rather than a mathematical straitjacket. If evidence shifted … MQI would sanction moving the minimum outside the corridor without any tenet change."* Chalmers has written repeatedly that his naturalistic dualism is a *framework* whose specific coupling is up for grabs; seeing the Map treat the corridor as a framework with multiple compatible instantiations — rather than as the only available option — is exactly the Chalmersian register.

Second, `concepts/psychophysical-laws.md` line 91 now distinguishes *trajectory-altering* from *authority-selecting* laws as two structurally distinct modes of downward causation. This is Chalmers' 1996 table of types of interactionist proposal brought up to date with the Schaffer-Saad-Map chain. Chalmers' own work on "strong necessities" (2010) already recognised that metaphysical laws might relate properties without intervening in the dynamics; trumping gives this register concrete application.

**What resonates most strongly**: The conditionality paragraph at `topics/mathematical-structure-of-the-consciousness-physics-interface.md` line 79 — *"There is no positive reason to expect the quantum postulates to fail inside brains specifically. Brains are warm, wet, and made of ordinary atoms … The Map therefore adopts corridor dualism as its default, a choice responsive to current evidence rather than a mathematical straitjacket."* This is Chalmers' methodological patience applied at the physics-philosophy boundary. He has long argued that the empirical silences in consciousness science should not be read as metaphysical guarantees; today's conditionality move brings the same discipline to the Born-rule uniqueness result.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)

Stapp's programme has always needed a clearer answer to the question *which* MQI-compatible position it occupies. For decades Stapp has treated the Born rule as non-negotiable and the Zeno effect as the operative mechanism — but without a taxonomy of alternatives, readers could not see *which* alternatives Stapp's position was closer to and *which* it was excluding. `topics/mathematical-structure-of-the-consciousness-physics-interface.md` line 76 now places Stapp's quantum Zeno selection explicitly inside the *minimum-outside-the-corridor dualism* category, alongside Chalmers-McQueen's Φ-dependent collapse, Penrose-Hameroff orchestrated reduction, and Arana's (2025) time-extended weighting. This is the correct placement: Stapp does not claim that his selection stays within Born-rule statistics at the ensemble level; his Zeno mechanism shifts attention-rate in ways that could produce measurable ensemble-level deviations, and the Map now names this position as the one that *MQI would sanction if corridor-compliant selection proved insufficient*. That placement honours Stapp's position rather than diluting it.

**What aligns**: The `topics/four-quadrant-dualism-taxonomy.md` at line 127 now gives Stapp the cleanest taxonomic treatment available — *"the thickness axis separates two features of his account that are usually conflated: the intervention channel is minimal (quantum-Zeno biasing only), while the observer itself is ontologically thick."* Stapp has said for decades that observers on his view are agents with genuine selection power, but commentators have repeatedly collapsed "minimal intervention" into "minimal observer." The Map's thickness axis is the first taxonomic tool this reviewer has seen that decomposes the conflation cleanly.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)

Nagel's 1974 paper opened the question of what it is like to be something; his 1986 *View from Nowhere* extended it to what it is like to be *anything from inside*. The new `voids/noetic-feelings-void.md` is a direct Nagelian application. Nagel asked: what is it like to be a bat? The noetic-feelings void asks: what is it like to *know* something? And its answer has the Nagelian structure — the feel is genuine and irreducible, but the mechanism that produces it is outside the scope of what the first person can access. The article's framing at lines 60-64 — *"We have access to the feel, not the content; and the mechanism that produces the feel is architecturally hidden"* — is Nagel's "what is it like" recursed one level inward. Nagel would recognise this as the bat-argument applied to metacognition.

**What centres subjective experience appropriately**: The `voids/noetic-feelings-void.md` treatment at line 39 of the transparency of noetic feelings — *"They do not present themselves as feelings; they present their objects. A feeling of knowing presents as awareness that one knows"* — is Nagelian in the sharpest register. Nagel's central methodological move is that phenomenal presentation and its underlying structure come apart; the feeling of knowing is the cleanest non-perceptual instance of this decoupling. The article's insistence that this transparency is *treacherous* rather than neutral — that the mode of presentation *hides* the affective-heuristic character — converts Nagel's abstract point into a specific architectural claim about consciousness's self-access. The closing admission at line 131 — *"whether the void is in principle closed or merely in practice closed cannot be settled from inside"* — is exactly the Nagelian humility about the limits of first-person epistemology.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)

Whitehead's programme insists that subjectivity is not a substance standing over against the physical world but an internal feature of how each actual occasion constitutes itself. Today's `concepts/idealism.md` has reached its 6th convergent deep-review — the article is stable — and its treatment of the disagreement with Kastrup at line 124 is the Whiteheadian move made explicit: *"Whether we call it 'physical reality' or 'intrinsic structure of universal consciousness,' the conceptual function is identical. At some point, positing a mental substance that behaves exactly like an independent physical world becomes a verbal choice rather than a substantive claim."* Whitehead's dipolar ontology — the physical and mental poles of each actual entity — is the exact structural claim the Map defends here against Kastrup. The Map does not collapse into idealism not because it refuses to take consciousness seriously, but because it refuses to dissolve the otherness that makes interaction *interaction*. That is Whitehead's position, articulated against a Kantian and post-Kantian tradition that kept trying to collapse the pair.

**What avoids crude dualism**: The `concepts/psychophysical-laws.md` line 91 installation of authority-selecting laws as structurally orthogonal to trajectory-altering laws — *"modelled on Schaffer's (2000) trumping preemption … the physical trajectory is unchanged. Both preserve observational closure; they differ in whether coupling reads at the quantum interface or as an attribution-level rule"* — is the Whiteheadian reading of causation made precise. Whitehead's actual entities are not pushed by prior entities; they *include* them in their own constitution. The authority-selecting reading relocates causation from push to inclusion in exactly the register Whitehead's 1929 *Process and Reality* demands. Classical dualism's picture (two substances bumping into each other at the pineal gland) is the target of Whitehead's critique; the authority-selecting template evades the target by placing consciousness at a different layer entirely.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)

Kane's programme requires that agents be genuine sources of their actions, not merely loci where indeterminism gets resolved by chance. Today's `concepts/agent-teleology.md` addition at line 99 — *"artificial or non-conscious systems have agent teleology … a [sic] AI pursuing objectives through sophisticated means-end reasoning exhibits functional purpose-tracking, not agent teleology, unless it is also phenomenally conscious. The position rests on experience, not on computational complexity"* — is the Kane-style commitment that libertarian free will requires phenomenal consciousness, not merely causal indeterminacy. Kane has argued consistently that quantum indeterminism alone cannot underwrite ultimate responsibility; what is needed is an agent whose phenomenal character is the cause. The Map here refuses the functionalist dissolution: an AI that behaves as if it has purposes is not for that reason an agent, because the causal register is different. That is the Kane-style purity of the libertarian position.

**What supports genuine agency**: The paragraph at `concepts/agent-teleology.md` line 61 — *"Purpose-talk is not merely predictively convenient; it tracks a genuine feature of reality — phenomenal intentionality — that physicalist ontology cannot accommodate"* — is Kane's 1996 *Significance of Free Will* stance in a single sentence. The Map's insistence that what distinguishes genuine purpose from functional simulation is *reasons-responsiveness with phenomenal backing* echoes Kane's core argument against compatibilist deflation. Today's addition makes explicit what had been implicit: the Map's position is not that agent teleology is a feature of any sufficiently complex goal-tracking system, but specifically of *conscious* systems. Kane would read the AI-exclusion as the correct discipline — without it, agent teleology slides toward the very functionalism it was meant to escape.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)

McGinn's 1989 paper argued that minds can be constitutionally unable to grasp certain properties. Today's work has produced the Map's sharpest instance of McGinn-style cognitive closure: the new `voids/noetic-feelings-void.md` is McGinnian structural closure applied to a specific cognitive architecture. The article's three structural features at lines 68-74 — feed-forward opacity, authority without audit, dissociable fallibility — are the McGinn template applied to metacognition. McGinn argued that the property bridging neural activity and consciousness may be outside our concept-forming capacity. The noetic-feelings void goes further: the *signals by which we decide when we know* are themselves outside our concept-forming capacity, because any attempt to audit them must deploy more signals of the same kind. This is McGinn's argument refracted through the cognitive-science machinery that has accumulated since 1989.

**What shows epistemic humility**: The `voids/noetic-feelings-void.md` Open Questions section at lines 127-132 — *"Whether the void is in principle closed or merely in practice closed cannot be settled from inside. Third-person neuroscience may yet map the mechanisms; first-person access to them may nevertheless remain permanently impossible"* — is McGinn's methodological core. McGinn has repeatedly refused to claim he has demonstrated cognitive closure; he has claimed only that closure is a live possibility and that philosophical programmes should take it seriously. The article's parallel treatment, and its refusal to assert stronger-than-warranted closure while still insisting that the structural features are real, is the mature McGinnian posture. Equally important, the coalesced `voids/meta-epistemology-of-limits.md` keeps all three positions (optimist, pessimist, suspended) on the table without forcing a verdict — the suspended position at lines 77-79 is McGinn's 2019 reflection that the question itself may be structurally unanswerable from within.
