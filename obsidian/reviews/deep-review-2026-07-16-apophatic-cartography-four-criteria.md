The article stated "The Pseudo-Dionysian corpus shaped Christian, Jewish, and Islamic negative theology" and, in Honest Bounds bound (2), "The Pseudo-Dionysian corpus shaped negative theology across Christianity, Judaism, and Islam." **This is historically inaccurate.** Pseudo-Dionysius (c. 5th–6th C.) shaped *Christian* negative theology strongly, but Jewish negative theology (Maimonides, 12th C.) derives from the **Islamic Neoplatonic tradition** (al-Farabi, Avicenna), and Islamic negative theology developed from **Neoplatonism (Plotinus, Proclus)** largely independently of Dionysius. The genuine shared upstream cause across all three traditions is Neoplatonism — of which Pseudo-Dionysius is himself the *Christian* downstream branch.
