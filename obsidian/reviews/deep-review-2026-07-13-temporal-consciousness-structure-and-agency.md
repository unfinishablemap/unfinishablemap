## Pessimistic Analysis Summary

### Critical Issues Found (all FIXED)

Three publisher-verified quote/attribution defects, each invisible to intra-corpus review because the surrounding prose was internally consistent:

1. **James quote-fidelity — one-preposition divergence (L108).** Article read James's stream flowing rather than `"chopped up into bits."` Verbatim source: *"Consciousness, then, does not appear to itself chopped up in bits. ... It flows."* — William James, *The Principles of Psychology* (1890), ch. IX "The Stream of Thought." The canonical wording is **"in bits"**, not "into bits." **Fixed** → `"chopped up in bits."` Verified at psychclassics.yorku.ca/James/Principles/prin9.htm (standard scholarly online edition).

2. **Specious-present coinage misattributed to James (L84).** Article read *"William James identified the specious present."* James does NOT claim the coinage; he explicitly credits E. R. Clay: *"The only fact of our immediate experience is what Mr. E. R. Clay has well called 'the specious present.'"* — James 1890 ch. XV "The Perception of Time." (E. R. Clay = pseudonym of E. Robert Kelly, *The Alternative*, 1882.) **Fixed** → *"William James, crediting the phrase to E. R. Clay, described the specious present..."* — a grep-verified, named defect. Verified at psychclassics.yorku.ca/James/Principles/prin15.htm.

3. **Broad misclassified as a Retentionalist (L90).** Article read *"**Retentionalists** (Husserl, Broad)."* The article's OWN cited reference #15 — the SEP "Temporal Consciousness" entry (Dainton) — classes Broad as an **Extensional** theorist (extended-content / "partial extensionalism," committed with Foster to the Immediacy Thesis), and names **Husserl and Brentano** as the paradigm Retentional figures. Listing Broad under Retentionalists directly contradicts the cited source. **Fixed** → `**Retentionalists** (Husserl, Brentano)` — Brentano is the unambiguous paradigm retentionalist, aligning the pairing with the article's own SEP citation and sidestepping Broad's cross-period ambiguity (1923 Scientific Thought = extensional; 1938 = retentional-leaning). Verified at plato.stanford.edu/entries/consciousness-temporal/.
