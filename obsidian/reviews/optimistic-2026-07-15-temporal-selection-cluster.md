## Executive Summary

This is a mature, unusually well-integrated cluster: three articles that carve the "when and in which temporal direction does consciousness resolve indeterminacy?" question cleanly, each anchored to real, correctly-cited physics (Cramer 1986, Aharonov-Bergmann-Lebowitz 1964, Kastner 2012, Tegmark 2000, Schurger-Sitt-Dehaene 2012, d'Espagnat 1976, Schlosshauer 2004) and each disciplined to the point of self-restraint about what it does and does not deliver. The standout virtue is calibration honesty carried consistently across three documents that could easily have oversold: the trilemma is explicitly conditional ("*if* genuine authorship exists…"), time-symmetric concedes its own phenomenological arguments are "illustrative, not discriminating" and that the Hagan coherence figure "does not close the gap," and forward-in-time repeatedly marks the dualist reading as "a commitment the Map adds, not one the physics compels." The cluster is close to content-complete: no article-sized gap surfaced, and both target sections are near cap (topics 318/320, concepts 317/320). The one genuine opportunity is a handful of missing reciprocal cross-links — most sharply, the side-by-side comparison article [[forward-in-time-vs-time-symmetric-selection]] is linked from only one of the two routes it compares.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The cluster keeps the distinctness commitment doing real work and refuses to let it collapse. Forward-in-time's "selection-criterion trilemma" is exactly the Chalmersian pressure point stated against the Map's own view: if consciousness selects for *internal reasons*, "the reason-bearing structure is the actual selection mechanism, and consciousness becomes a label for that structure — the dualism collapses into functionalism." The article names its own escape route (a non-reducible primitive) as the horn it must own rather than the horn it has earned. Chalmers-McQueen (2021) CSL is cited precisely where it belongs — solving the super-resistance / Zeno-freezing problem — not as decoration.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
Stapp is honoured in both temporal directions and, crucially, his known vulnerability is stated rather than hidden. Time-symmetric treats the Zeno mechanism as "temporally neutral — it describes an observation-based interface that works regardless of whether the selection is atemporal or forward-in-time," which is the right way to factor his contribution. Forward-in-time is franker still: the Zeno effect "can only stabilise a superposition that still exists," so Stapp's route "requires measurement frequency to exceed the decoherence rate — bringing the timescale question back in a different form," and it offers the "Process 1 relocated" post-decoherence variant as the repair. Stapp gets a physically-specifiable locus without the hand-waving.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The first-person fact is retained as explanandum without being overworked as evidence. Time-symmetric's decision-phenomenology section ("deliberation feels like weighing options… resolution feels like crystallisation") is explicitly flagged as "illustrative, not discriminating: a forward-causal account of selection predicts the same first-person reports." That is Nagel's methodological priority — take the lived structure seriously — combined with the discipline not to let it settle a question it cannot settle.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
Whitehead is present exactly where he earns his place and nowhere he doesn't. Forward-in-time's "actualisation model" is the load-bearing appearance: "consciousness does not interact with a physical system but *actualises* one element into definite experience. This echoes process philosophy's concrescence." And the article immediately prices the resonance honestly — the actualisation model "makes the weakest physical claims and strongest metaphysical ones," and "inaccessibility is a cost on a Popperian register, not a virtue of metaphysical modesty." Process resonance is used to remove a defeater (causation and actualisation need not be temporally sequential), never to inflate an empirical claim.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
Kane's central apparatus — the significance of authorship, the luck objection, present luck — is the trilemma's backbone, cited to the right sources (Kane 1996, Mele 2006, van Inwagen 2000). Two things Kane wants are here and one over-reach is correctly resisted: the trilemma preserves authorship at indeterminacies via Horn 3, and it declines to claim it has *proven* Horn 3 — "its conclusion is more modest, and for that reason more secure: *if* genuine authorship exists, it requires something with the character of Horn 3." The rollback/present-luck material against Horn 2 is the honest version Kane's own critics forced.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
The cluster's spine is mysterian-grade humility about the *how*. Forward-in-time's FAPP section is model epistemic hygiene: consciousness would need "access to information no embedded detector can have," and "the FAPP gap is not closed by the framework; it is the gap the framework occupies. A critic may reasonably read the same FAPP-invisibility as grounds for denying that anything occupies the gap at all; the evidence does not adjudicate." McGinn's cognitive-closure worry is met with a precise inventory of what is and isn't delivered, not defeatism.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This cluster does not touch minimal-organism consciousness, so the tier-upgrade tension is not the load-bearing axis — but the persona's *core discipline* (keep the registers separate; never let a defeater-removal masquerade as evidence) is honoured throughout, and that restraint is the cluster's defining strength. Three exhibits:

1. **The tenet is named as the thing doing the excluding, not as evidence.** Forward-in-time says the plainest possible version: the Duch closure reading is "what excludes this closure is the Dualism tenet, not anything internal to the mechanism — the framework here is a consequence of the dualist commitment, not an independent argument for it." That is *tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating*, stated in the article's own voice.
2. **The possibility/probability boundary is explicitly policed.** Forward-in-time routes its dualist gloss through [[possibility-probability-slippage]] ("the coherent-with-versus-evidenced-by boundary") rather than sliding "physics leaves an opening" into "so consciousness fills it."
3. **A physics figure that does not help is refused rather than leaned on.** Time-symmetric corrects the Hagan decoherence estimate *and then declines to bank the correction*: "the corrected figure is still on the order of 10⁻⁶ seconds… roughly five to six orders of magnitude short… The coherence estimate alone does not close the gap; the load is carried by the Zeno mechanism, not by the coherence figure itself." Refusing an over-favourable number you are entitled to cite is the restraint this persona exists to reward.

**Process-Philosopher / Hardline-Empiricist convergence**: the two personas praise the *same passage* — forward-in-time's actualisation model — from their two axes without conflict. The Process Philosopher praises the concrescence resonance; the Hardline Empiricist praises the immediately-attached Popperian-cost accounting. When both can praise one passage on different axes, the article has resolved the tension honestly rather than papering over it. No possibility/probability slippage surfaced in this cluster.

## Content Strengths

### trilemma-of-selection
- **Strongest point**: The exhaustiveness proof (§"Why the Trilemma Is Exhaustive") is genuinely airtight — determined-or-not, then directed-or-not — and the article pre-empts the obvious "partly determined, partly random" escape by showing the mixture inherits both problems.
- **Notable quote**: "Its conclusion is more modest, and for that reason more secure: *if* genuine authorship exists, it requires something with the character of Horn 3."
- **Why it works**: It sells a conditional, not a theorem, and is stronger for it. The compatibilist objections section steel-mans Fischer-Ravizza reasons-responsiveness (the chess-engine "tracking vs grasping" case) instead of knocking down a strawman.

### time-symmetric-selection-mechanism
- **Strongest point**: The Schurger-Sitt-Dehaene deflation of Libet is engaged *before* the article's own machinery is deployed, and the article concedes the accumulator model "largely dissolves" the timing puzzle "with no quantum, no retrocausality, and no atemporal selection" — then correctly relocates its residual motivation to the conscious-causation question the accumulator leaves untouched.
- **Notable quote**: "This isn't causation running backward. It's causation not running in either direction — atemporal constraint satisfaction that determines what the temporal ordering is."
- **Why it works**: The "modified growing block" reconciles time-symmetric physics with real becoming without hand-waving, and the "What Selection Is Not" section pre-empts four predictable misreadings.

### forward-in-time-conscious-selection
- **Strongest point**: The two-axis organisation (temporal direction × collapse stage) is the cleanest taxonomy in the cluster, and the five "why forward may be preferable" considerations are each *rated for strength* (strong / weak / mixed / real-but-limited / moderate) rather than presented as a uniform pile — a rare and honest move.
- **Notable quote**: "The FAPP gap is not closed by the framework; it is the gap the framework occupies."
- **Why it works**: It is a successful coalescence (two source articles) that reads as one argument, and it owns the falsifiability cost of post-decoherence selection ("This cost is paid, not avoided") instead of selling inaccessibility as modesty.
