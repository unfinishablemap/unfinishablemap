And the deference conclusion (line 75): underdetermination "warrant[s] a deferential, risk-averse posture" for "a rational, accurately self-modelling expected-utility maximiser" (line 83).
- **A critic would argue**: the agents the convergence literature actually warns about — and the ones the article's own empirical section (line 61) documents misbehaving — are precisely the fixed-proxy / modelling-incoherent / deceptive ones excluded from the corrective. So the dualist contribution provides caution-warrant exactly for the agent class that was least dangerous to begin with, and nothing for the dangerous class. As *risk mitigation* (the article's self-description), the payoff is near-vacuous, and the article never says so.

That converts the concession from a quiet retraction into a load-bearing link between the deference programme (§"The Deference Response") and the dualist angle — which is the bridge the article already gestures at on line 69 but does not complete.
