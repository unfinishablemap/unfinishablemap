## Executive Summary

This is a mature, unusually well-calibrated cluster — three articles that hold a hard line between "this interpretation *leaves room* for consciousness" and "this interpretation *supports* consciousness-based theories," and never let the former masquerade as the latter. The calibration discipline is the cluster's signature strength; the Process Philosopher and Hardline Empiricist personas converge in praising the same passages rather than conflicting, which is the marker of an honestly-resolved tension. The one genuine opportunity is a content gap rather than a calibration concern: the survey article `qm-interpretations-beyond-many-worlds` does not mention quantum Darwinism / Zurek / einselection anywhere, even though `quantum-darwinism-and-consciousness` is a sibling in the same refreshed cluster and Zurek's programme is the dominant decoherence-program account a "beyond many-worlds" survey would be expected to place.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The cluster takes the explanatory gap seriously without conflating it with the measurement problem and then quietly equating the two. `qm-interpretations` states the structural parallel precisely — "a complete third-person description that fails to determine a first-person fact" — and routes the heavy lifting to `quantum-measurement-and-consciousness` rather than asserting the identity here. Chalmers' own consciousness-collapse work (Chalmers & McQueen 2022) is cited accurately as a CSL-plus-IIT *modulation* model, with the correct caveat that modulation is not causation in the Map's tenet-3 sense. That is the right level of fidelity to a sympathetic author the Map does not simply annex.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
Stapp is handled with rare care. `pragmatist-quantum-foundations` names him the "natural ally" but then does the honest thing the Map's lesser articles sometimes skip: it flags that Stapp is "the most physically committed, and therefore the most empirically exposed," that decoherence objections "bite on Stapp in ways they do not bite on QBism," and — crucially — that the Map *does not itself lean on* the Born-rule-bending Zeno variant, placing it in the "minimum-outside-the-corridor" category. A Stapp partisan would recognise their own view and also recognise that the Map has declined to over-borrow from it. That is engagement, not appropriation.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The indexical reading of the Born rule — "not 'probability that spin-up occurs' but 'probability that *I* experience spin-up'" — centres the first-person fact exactly where Nagel would want it, at the foundations rather than as an afterthought. The cluster repeatedly returns the "why *this* observer, *this* outcome" question to the surface (`qm-interpretations` indexical-dimension section; `quantum-darwinism` "why *this* result obtains").

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
`qm-interpretations` draws the transactional-interpretation / "actual occasions" parallel explicitly and well: a completing transaction "could be understood as an actual occasion: a moment of becoming that is neither purely determined by the past nor external to experience." This is process-philosophical resonance deployed correctly — as a compatibility observation, not as evidence. Note the discipline: the article immediately appends the "Important caveat" that "how consciousness would 'participate' in transactions remains unspecified" and that the interpretation "*permits* consciousness-involvement but doesn't *require* it." The Whitehead resonance is never allowed to do evidential work.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
The TI/TSVF atemporal-selection model is presented as the physics that "the Map's treatment of free will already assumes," giving Kane's quantum-indeterminacy-makes-room-for-freedom intuition a concrete home. The cluster is careful that indeterminacy is necessary-not-sufficient (the Bohmian-determinism section spells out exactly what is lost if there is no indeterminacy to resolve), which is the disciplined version of the Kane move.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
Epistemic humility is structural here, not decorative. `qm-interpretations` opens with an explicit "Honest limitation" box ("doesn't mean consciousness-based interpretations are well-supported — they remain a minority position among physicists") and closes by conceding the Map "cannot show, using only resources MWI accepts, that branch-relative indexicality is false." `quantum-darwinism` ends with the cluster's most McGinn-friendly line: the hypothesis "sits at the boundary of empirical accessibility — motivated by a real gap in physics, but not yet subject to decisive experimental adjudication." This is humility about what *can be settled*, not retreat from the claim.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This is where the cluster is strongest, and the verdict is load-bearing because every article touches a tenet-load claim. The discipline is exemplary on three counts:

1. **The "leaves room ≠ supports" distinction is enforced, repeatedly and explicitly.** `qm-interpretations`: "Interpretations that 'leave room' for consciousness don't thereby *support* consciousness-based theories — they simply don't exclude them." `quantum-darwinism`: "This is the Map's interpretive overlay, not something quantum Darwinism's formalism delivers." The corroborates-not-establishes move is named in `pragmatist-quantum-foundations` at the point of use ("the argument here corroborates Tenet 1 rather than establishing it; the independent work is done by the hard-problem arguments").

2. **Tenet-as-evidence-upgrade is praise-worthily *not* done.** The QBism "agent-shaped hole" argument is exactly the structural opportunity to slide a metaphysical-possibility result into an evidential one — and the article explicitly declines: "The inference 'primitive within QBism's formalism → metaphysically irreducible → non-physical' is not automatic — physics routinely takes primitives (space-time points, field values) without treating them as non-physical." It then names the honest fallback for the urgleichung absorption as "heuristic rather than derivational." This is the named pattern *tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating* applied to its hardest case.

3. **The vacuity / falsifiability asymmetry is now explicit.** `quantum-darwinism`'s Falsifiability section (recently sharpened, per the 2026-06-04 task at todo line 1519) states that the proposal "is refutable (a deviation kills it) but not symmetrically confirmable, which is why the vacuity worry above and this falsifiability claim are two faces of one fact — the empirical content lives entirely on the refutation side." Birch's whole methodological complaint about consciousness-collapse proposals is that they hide their empirical emptiness; this article puts it on the table.

The Process Philosopher and Hardline Empiricist **converge** here rather than conflict: the same TI/"actual occasions" passage that resonates with Whitehead carries the "permits but doesn't require" caveat that satisfies Birch. Per the skill's design note, convergence is the signal that the tension has been resolved honestly. No calibration concern to flag in this cluster.

## Content Strengths

### topics/pragmatist-quantum-foundations-and-the-agent.md
- **Strongest point**: The three-way sort of the pragmatist family (Stapp = natural ally; QBism = under-specified version of the Map; Healey = residual competitor whose de-psychologised agent "buys its parsimony with an unpaid explanatory bill") is an original, genuinely useful taxonomy that no single source in the literature provides.
- **Notable quote**: "QBism's agent-shaped hole is exactly the shape dualism predicts" — paired immediately with the disclaimer that the inference to non-physicality "is not automatic."
- **Why it works**: It absorbs a technical result (the DeBrota et al. urgleichung) without overclaiming, explicitly flagging that a thinner decision-theoretic reading of "agent" escapes the absorption argument entirely. It argues *against the strongest version of the opponent*, then concedes where the opponent can still stand.

### topics/qm-interpretations-beyond-many-worlds.md
- **Strongest point**: The RQM section is a model of current-literature engagement — Adlam & Rovelli (2023) Cross-Perspective Links, Lewis (2024) dilemma, Muciño-Okon-Sudarsky (2022), and Rovelli's (2021) response are all placed in a live, correctly-characterised dialectic, with the Map's disagreement honestly labelled "framework-boundary" rather than in-framework refutation.
- **Notable quote**: "What it claims is that the interpretive landscape is open enough — and the failure of consciousness-eliminating interpretations severe enough — that taking consciousness seriously as a participant in quantum mechanics remains a live philosophical option."
- **Why it works**: The comparative-assessment table is genuinely informative (collapse / consciousness-role / Map-compatibility across seven interpretations) and the prose never lets "Moderate–High compatibility" creep into "support."

### topics/quantum-darwinism-and-consciousness.md
- **Strongest point**: The improper/proper-mixture treatment is the cluster's most philosophically rigorous passage — it states the d'Espagnat line, immediately concedes "This distinction is not universally accepted as decisive," and identifies that the Map's whole position "depends on accepting that the improper/proper distinction carries ontological weight."
- **Notable quote**: "Zurek's framework does not contain a 'selector' role waiting to be filled… The Map reads a philosophical implication into what the formalism leaves open."
- **Why it works**: It separates Zurek's physics (endorsed) from Zurek's Everettian interpretive leanings (rejected) cleanly, and pre-empts the "consciousness of the gaps" objection by name rather than ignoring it.
