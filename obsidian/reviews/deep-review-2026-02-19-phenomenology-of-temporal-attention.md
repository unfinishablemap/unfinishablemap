## Pessimistic Analysis Summary

- **[CRITICAL-1] Quantum Zeno framework load-bearing**: The "Temporal Selection and the Interface" section structured its argument around the Zeno effect, violating the writing style guide's restraint policy. The Zeno mechanism was the explanation for why effort feels effortful. **Resolution**: Demoted Zeno to one speculative possibility ("One speculative possibility—Stapp's quantum Zeno framework—suggests..."). - **[CRITICAL-2] Missing No Many Worlds tenet**: The Relation to Site Perspective section covered four of five tenets but omitted No Many Worlds, despite the temporal asymmetry argument depending on collapse being real and singular. **Resolution**: Added a full No Many Worlds paragraph explaining why MWI rejection is a precondition for the temporal asymmetry argument.

- **[CRITICAL-3] Core argument restates the hard problem**: The article implied temporal experience provides *independent* evidence for dualism, but the gap between accumulator mechanisms and felt temporal quality is the hard problem applied to a temporal case, not a new argument. **Resolution**: Reframed explicitly — "not an independent argument for dualism, but a case where the explanatory gap proves especially vivid." Added acknowledgment that the temporal case *sharpens* rather than replaces the hard problem, because temporal distortion is empirically undisputed.

### Medium Issues Found

- **[MEDIUM-1] Varela "confirmed experimentally" overstated**: Varela (1999) is a theoretical programme, not an experimental report. **Resolution**: Changed to "proposed that trained meditators should access temporal structure" and added Slagter et al. (2007) as actual experimental support.

- **[MEDIUM-2] Csikszentmihalyi claim assumed stronger conclusion**: The flow observation was presented as if Csikszentmihalyi documented phenomenal restructuring, when he documented time-estimation failure. **Resolution**: Distinguished the observation ("people in flow report being unable to estimate how much time has passed") from the Map's interpretation ("The Map interprets this not as mere inattention to a clock but as a restructuring of the present").

- **[MEDIUM-3] Buddhist khaṇika citation cuts against reified self**: The article cited Buddhist phenomenology without acknowledging that khaṇika undermines the substantial attender the article assumes. **Resolution**: Added parenthetical acknowledging the tension: "The Buddhist analysis also challenges the assumption of a stable attender... The Map acknowledges this tension while maintaining that the experiential subject, however impermanent moment-to-moment, is irreducible to physical process."

- **[MEDIUM-4] Temporal asymmetry argument presented without contest**: The collapse-based explanation was stated without engaging the MWI alternative. **Resolution**: Added a full paragraph engaging the many-worlds objection and articulating why the Map's position holds — the *qualitative character* of protention vs retention resists dissolution into branching structure.

- **[MEDIUM-5] "Instructed attention has moderate duration" unsupported**: **Resolution**: Removed the unsupported characterisation. Simplified to note the key contrast: exogenous attention is brief, willed attention can be sustained indefinitely.
