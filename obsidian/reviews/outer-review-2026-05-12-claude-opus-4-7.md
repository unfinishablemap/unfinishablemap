## Reply

# Critical Audit: "Non-Temporal Consciousness" on The Unfinishable Map

## TL;DR

- The article advances a genuine, dialectically honest argument from Husserl's "absolute flow" plus Frischhut's (2024) account of meditative timelessness to a categorial irreducibility of consciousness to physical processes — and unlike most web-published philosophy, it correctly cites its primary and secondary sources (Hoerl 2013, Husserl/Brough 1991, Pinto et al. 2017, Mashour 2012, Lopez 2025) and explicitly anticipates several major objections.
- However, the central inference equivocates on "temporal" (A-series flow vs. B-series tenseless order) in Premise 1 of the irreducibility argument, omits the Katz–Sharf constructivist critique of meditator-report-as-evidence, and never engages Dan Zahavi's pre-reflective self-awareness reading of inner time-consciousness, which directly blocks the regress argument the piece relies on; these three gaps would each be flagged by any peer reviewer in philosophy of mind or phenomenology.
- The article is closer to a publishable first draft than to a finished argument: the fixes are largely additive (named opposing literatures, disambiguation of "temporal," concession on convergence-vs-coherentism) rather than structural, and the existing strengths — distinguishing metaphysical from phenomenological non-temporality, naming Bayne and Lopez as live rivals, conceding the Bergson synthesis "favours Husserl" — should be preserved.

## Key Findings

**The argument's spine is real but rests on two soft premises.** The four-step irreducibility argument (physical processes are inherently temporal; deepest consciousness is non-temporal; temporal description category-fails on non-temporal entities; therefore consciousness is not fully physically describable) is *valid*. Its soundness depends on (1) whether "temporal" means A-series becoming or B-series ordering, and (2) whether meditative reports can carry metaphysical weight. The article does not resolve either.

**The "convergence" between Husserl and meditation is partly circular.** The article explicitly distinguishes "metaphysically non-temporal" (Husserl) from "phenomenologically atemporal" (meditation), then claims convergence. But the regress argument is doing the interpretive work that lets the meditative reports count as evidence for the metaphysical reading; absent the regress, the representationalist deflation wins. The convergence is therefore coherentist within the article's frame, not independent triangulation — and the article elsewhere claims (correctly) that such moves should not be over-credited. [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/non-temporal-consciousness/)

**Citation accuracy is high; engagement breadth is the problem.** Every reference I checked exists and is correctly attributed. The major scholarly issues are *omissions*, not misattributions: Zahavi is absent despite being the leading living interpreter of Husserlian time-consciousness; Katz and Sharf are absent despite being the standard objection to using meditator reports as data; Dainton, Phillips, and Hoerl–McCormack are absent despite the article positioning itself in the temporal-consciousness literature; Putnam, Mellor, Price, Barbour, Maudlin, and the entire B-theory/block-universe literature are absent despite Premise 1 standing or falling on this dispute.

**The fragmentation section is the weakest part.** The inference that non-temporal consciousness explains preserved unity under split-brain conditions rests on the assertion that "unity-threatening division requires temporal structure," which is asserted rather than argued and is implausible on its face (spatial disunity is paradigmatic and requires no temporal structure). [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/non-temporal-consciousness/)

**Dialectical honesty is unusually high.** The article repeatedly flags its own limitations — radio analogy "often criticised," the synthesis "favours Husserl," convergence "suggestive rather than decisive," dualist response "speculative" and "difficult to test directly." This is rare and should be preserved through any revision.

## Details

### A. Philosophical Rigor

**A1. Central thesis stated clearly, but with an unresolved fork.** The article defines "non-temporal" with admirable explicitness early on: "Husserl's absolute flow is *metaphysically* non-temporal… Meditative timelessness is *phenomenologically* atemporal." It then says the two "converge: if the deepest level of consciousness is metaphysically non-temporal (Husserl), we might expect that accessing it strips away phenomenal temporal structure (meditation). The metaphysical thesis explains the phenomenological finding." But the inference from the phenomenological finding back to the metaphysical thesis is precisely what the representationalist denies. The article's reply — that "Husserl's regress argument supports this stronger reading" — concedes that the regress argument is doing the inferential work; the meditative reports therefore do not independently support the metaphysical reading. The "convergence" double-counts the same evidence interpreted two ways. [unfinishablemap + 2](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/non-temporal-consciousness/)

**A2. The regress argument is not as decisive as presented.** Two omissions weaken the article's use of Husserl's regress:

*First, Hoerl himself is more skeptical than the citation suggests.* Hoerl (2013, *Philosophy and Phenomenological Research* 86(2): 376–411) does reconstruct what he calls the "non-temporality claim" sympathetically, but he closes by "question[ing] whether Husserl ultimately provides good reasons for preferring his intentionalist approach to a rival extensionalist one." Citing Hoerl as scaffolding for the regress without flagging Hoerl's reservation is one-sided. [PhilArchive +2](https://philarchive.org/rec/HOEHTA)

*Second, Zahavi blocks the regress.* Dan Zahavi, "Inner time-consciousness and pre-reflective self-awareness," in Donn Welton (ed.), *The New Husserl: A Critical Reader* (Indiana University Press, 2003), pp. 157–180, argues that inner time-consciousness *just is* pre-reflective self-awareness: "Inner time-consciousness simply is the name of the pre-reflective self-awareness of our experiences." On this reading, the constituting layer does not need a further constituter behind it because it is not an *object* of a higher-order intentional act in the first place. The article treats blocking the regress as either accepting brute facts or infinite regress; Zahavi offers a third option that has wide acceptance in contemporary phenomenology and is not mentioned. [PhilPapers + 2](https://philpapers.org/rec/ZAHSAA-2)

**A3. Premise 1 equivocates on "temporal." This is the most serious philosophical problem.** The article asserts: "Every physical description involves states succeeding states, forces acting over time, fields evolving through spacetime." This is true of *dynamical* descriptions but false of much of fundamental physics. Several serious philosophers of physics deny precisely this: [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/non-temporal-consciousness/)

- Hilary Putnam, "Time and Physical Geometry," *Journal of Philosophy* 64 (1967): 240–247, p. 247: "I have been amazed at the fact that what seems like a *purely philosophical* problem can have been so wholly *solved* by physics" — concluding from Special Relativity that future events are already real. [The Information Philosopher](https://www.informationphilosopher.com/freedom/special_relativity)[Grokipedia](https://grokipedia.com/page/Rietdijk%E2%80%93Putnam_argument)
- D. H. Mellor, *Real Time II* (Routledge, 1998): tense is unreal; "time itself cannot flow." [Dokumen.tips](https://dokumen.tips/documents/d-h-mellor-real-time-ii-international-library-of-philosophy.html)
- Huw Price, *Time's Arrow and Archimedes' Point* (Oxford, 1996), p. 4: "Physics has no need of an objective present, or a flow of time." [Prce](https://prce.hu/w/TAAP.html)
- Julian Barbour, *The End of Time* (Oxford, 1999): "Nothing happens; there is being but no becoming." [Platonia](http://www.platonia.com/nature_of_time_essay.pdf)
- Vesselin Petkov, *Relativity and the Nature of Spacetime* (2nd ed., Springer, 2009): only a four-dimensional Minkowski block is compatible with special relativity. [Amazon](https://www.amazon.nl/Relativity-Nature-Spacetime-Vesselin-Petkov/dp/3642019528)

If "temporal" in Premise 1 means merely "embedded in or orderable by B-series relations," the premise is true but consistent with physics being itself non-flowing, undercutting the categorical contrast. If "temporal" means "involving objective becoming," the premise is precisely what eternalists deny, and the article owes an independent argument against this lineage. The dispute is unsettled (Tim Maudlin, *The Metaphysics Within Physics*, Oxford 2007, is the strongest contrary voice and defends objective passage as compatible with physics), but the article cannot help itself to Premise 1 without acknowledging the debate. McTaggart is not cited; A-series vs. B-series is not distinguished. [Oxford University Press](https://global.oup.com/academic/product/the-metaphysics-within-physics-9780199575374)

**A4. The mathematics rejoinder is half-answered.** The article anticipates the obvious objection — mathematics describes non-temporal objects — and replies that mathematical description "abstracts away from temporal structure" whereas physical description is "constitutively temporal." But genuinely non-temporal formulations exist within physics itself: the Wheeler–DeWitt equation in canonical quantum gravity (the so-called "problem of time"), Barbour's timeless physics, Rovelli's relational quantum mechanics. The article's reply needs to engage these rather than stipulate that physical description is "constitutively temporal." [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/non-temporal-consciousness/)[unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/non-temporal-consciousness/)

**A5. The Katz–Sharf constructivist critique is absent.** The article relies on Frischhut (2024) for the empirical-phenomenological premise but does not engage the constructivist critique that is the standard objection to using meditator reports as phenomenological data. Steven Katz, "Language, Epistemology, and Mysticism," in *Mysticism and Philosophical Analysis* (Oxford, 1978), p. 26 (italicised in original): "**There are NO pure (i.e., unmediated) experiences.**" Robert Sharf, "Buddhist Modernism and the Rhetoric of Meditative Experience," *Numen* 42(3) (1995): 228–283, p. 269: "In etic terms, Buddhist meditation might best be seen as the ritualization of experience; it doesn't engender a specific experiential state so much as it enacts it. In this sense Buddhist marga treatises are not so much maps of inner psychic space as they are scripts for the performance of an eminently public religious drama." Sharf's follow-up, "The Rhetoric of Experience and the Study of Religion," *Journal of Consciousness Studies* 7, No. 11–12 (2000), pp. 267–287, goes further: such reports "resist definition by design" and are not the sort of thing that "can lend itself to scientific scrutiny." This is the single most consequential scholarly gap; the site's existing editorial discipline of "framework-boundary marking" (visible in the changelog) would handle it well if invoked. [Philosophyofreligion + 3](https://philosophyofreligion.org/)

**A6. Anticipated objections are handled well where addressed.** To the article's credit, it does name and answer the representationalist deflation, treats Bergson as a serious rival, names Bayne's switch model and Lopez's 2025 layered-unity proposal as live rivals on the split-brain data, flags the radio analogy as "often criticised," and lists two specific disconfirmation conditions. This is dialectically responsible. [Springer](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11098-025-02339-3)

**A7. The dualist framing is partly question-begging.** Framing the argument as concluding with irreducibility, then mapping the result onto pre-existing dualist tenets, leaves unstated the fact that the very vocabulary of consciousness "constituting" time already presupposes the kind of subject a naturalist denies. This is not fatal, but the article should explicitly concede that its starting frame is inside a transcendental-phenomenological idiom the naturalist rejects.

### C. Argument Structure and Coherence

**C1. The connection to dualism is argued, not merely assumed** — the four-step argument is the article's spine and is well-organised. The problem is Premise 1, not the structure.

**C2. There is internal tension between the two senses of "non-temporal."** The article opens by distinguishing them, then treats them as mutually supporting, then concedes (in the Bergson synthesis paragraph) that "the synthesis favours Husserl" and "may not be fully resolvable." This is internally inconsistent: one cannot simultaneously claim convergence as evidential and concede that the convergence is one-sided. The article should either commit to the coherentist reading (the regress argument interprets the meditative data, full stop) or supply an independent route by which meditation can be evidence — which means engaging Katz–Sharf. [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/non-temporal-consciousness/)[unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/non-temporal-consciousness/)

**C3. The fragmentation section is the weakest.** The inference that "unity-threatening division requires temporal structure — one part before or after another" is asserted, not argued, and is implausible: spatial disunity is paradigmatic and does not require temporal structure. The radio analogy is candidly flagged as "often criticised" and "speculative," but the section still uses it as if it explained something. [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/non-temporal-consciousness/)[unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/non-temporal-consciousness/)

**C4. The conclusion follows from the premises** if one grants Premise 1 in a sufficiently A-theoretic sense, grants Premise 2, and grants the category-error claim of Premise 3. The argument is valid; the question is soundness, and on A3 and A5 the premises are at best contested.

### D. Scientific and Empirical Claims

**D1. Neuroscience claims are responsible.** Anaesthesia and split-brain data are accurately reported. The article correctly notes the post-2017 complication (Pinto et al.) and the 2025 layered-unity model. The move from "anaesthesia disrupts integration" to "integration is a threshold condition" is presented as inference, not discovery.

**D2. Physics claims are underdeveloped.** The "physical processes are inherently temporal" claim is the article's largest scientific overreach. The problem of time in canonical quantum gravity, the Wheeler–DeWitt equation, Barbour's timeless physics, and Rovelli's relational mechanics all suggest that fundamental physics itself may be non-temporal — and the article never confronts this awkward fact, despite its own companion piece on "time-symmetric selection" gesturing in that direction.

**D3. The empirical-to-metaphysical move is hedged but not always cleanly.** The article concedes that neuroscientific evidence "fully explaining" meditative timelessness as disruption to temporal-processing circuits would defeat the thesis — but does not explain why current neuroscience (Wittmann on insular cortex and time perception, etc.) does not already supply this.

### E. Strengths

1. **Dialectical honesty.** Repeated self-flagging of weaknesses is unusual and admirable.
2. **The metaphysical/phenomenological distinction at the start.** Making this explicit saves the piece from confusion that mars even published academic work.
3. **Engagement with current literature.** Frischhut (2024), Pinto et al. (2017), Lopez (2025), Hoerl (2013) are recent and real.
4. **The representationalist objection is anticipated and answered.** Most popular treatments do not consider it.
5. **Modest framing of "convergence."** "Suggestive rather than decisive" is the right epistemic register. [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/non-temporal-consciousness/)
6. **Productive cognitive-phenomenology cross-link.** The note that the non-temporal and cognitive-phenomenology theses are *complementary* (one shows consciousness escaping temporal categories, the other shows it escaping functional ones) is a genuinely interesting framing. [unfinishablemap](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/non-temporal-consciousness/)
7. **Self-aware about parsimony costs.** The "Occam's Razor Has Limits" subsection candidly concedes complexity.

## Recommendations

### Priority 1 — Must address before the piece could survive academic peer review

**R1. Disambiguate "temporal" in Premise 1.** Add a paragraph distinguishing A-series (dynamic / flowing / becoming) from B-series (tenseless / ordered) temporality. If B-series, explain how a B-temporal physical world contrasts categorically with a non-temporal consciousness. Without this, the irreducibility argument is at best question-begging.

Engage Katz–Sharf constructivism.** Add a substantive paragraph in the "Meditative Evidence" section addressing Katz (1978: "**There are NO pure (i.e., unmediated) experiences**") and Sharf (1995, p. 269: meditation as "ritualization of experience … scripts for the performance of an eminently public religious drama"; 2000, *JCS* 7(11–12): 267–287).
