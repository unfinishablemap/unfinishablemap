## Critical Issues

### Issue 1 (HIGH): Route 1 answers a weaker objection than the strongest one — the direction-of-authority is unargued (exclusion-symmetry recoil)

- **File/Location**: lines 75–77 (Route 1 statement), line 115 (Trumping failure mode)
- **Problem**: The trumping route uses Saad's delegatory causation: "an experience preempts the *default causal profile* of a physical state… the experience takes over and produces it instead, leaving every empirical regularity intact" (75). The article then simply asserts the direction: "conscious selection **inherits and exercises** [the Born distribution / default causal profile] by preemption." But the delegatory-causation machinery is **direction-neutral**. Nothing internal to preemption privileges consciousness as the trumping party over the physical microstate. A physicalist can run the mirror move with equal license: the physical state is authoritative over the trajectory, and any putative mental "trumping" is the epiphenomenal, empirically-silent overdetermination — which the article itself concedes makes "'Consciousness is a cause' and 'consciousness is epiphenomenal' … no predictively distinct claims" (77). So Route 1 does not merely fail to *defeat* epiphenomenalism empirically; it hands the physicalist a causal-structure framework in which the epiphenomenalist assignment is **equally supported**, and supplies no non-question-begging reason to assign authority to consciousness. The article confronts only two weaker objections: (a) "is preemption a real phenomenon or a bespoke device?" (115), and (b) "efficacy needn't mean a statistical signature" (77). It never confronts (c) "granting your whole preemption framework, what fixes the *direction* of authority as conscious-over-physical rather than the reverse?" The honest answer is that the direction is imported from the Dualism + Bidirectional-Interaction tenets — which makes Route 1 a **boundary-substitution**: it is presented as meeting the physicalist on causal-structure terrain, but the disagreement is actually earned only by Map-specific tenets. Line 77 comes close to admitting this ("available to the dualist, not compelling to the physicalist") without naming that the *asymmetry of authority-assignment* is the tenet-import, not a product of the preemption machinery.
- **Severity**: High. This is the exclusion-symmetry / self-undermining class in the flagship article, genuinely under-addressed.
- **Steelmanned rejoinder the article owes**: "The trumping route was never meant to *prove* consciousness trumps; the tenets stipulate the authoritative selector, and Route 1 shows only that this is *coherent* while preserving statistics." That is a fair reply — but if taken, it concedes Route 1 does no dialectical work *against* the epiphenomenalism worry; it re-asserts the tenet in causal-structure vocabulary. Either way the article should say so explicitly rather than presenting preemption as though the machinery itself delivers conscious efficacy.
- **Recommendation (routable)**: Directed deep-review adds one paragraph confronting the direction-of-authority question head-on: either supply an in-framework argument for the conscious-over-physical assignment, or honestly downgrade Route 1 to tenet-fixed framework-boundary marking (per the direct-refutation discipline) and state that its force against epiphenomenalism is therefore framework-internal, not dialectical.

### Issue 2 (MEDIUM): "Structural commitment rather than bolted on" over-reaches on a single unreviewed preprint

- **File/Location**: line 53
- **Problem**: P-Q2 (high confidence) is upgraded from a stipulation of the corridor reading to something "**structural to the reading, not an empirical hope**… Born-preservation is inherited as a structural commitment rather than bolted on," and the whole weight of that upgrade rests on the causal-consistency constraint "(Torres Alegre 2025, an arXiv preprint not yet peer-reviewed)." The paper is real and does say what is claimed (verified: no-signalling under purification/steering forces the Born form). But it is a single-author, unrefereed preprint, and the article's *own* desideratum 4 concedes a toy model "should make the entailment explicit rather than inheriting it **on faith from a preprint**" (103). Characterizing Born-preservation as "inherited structural commitment" asserts more epistemic security than an unreviewed derivation licenses. The high-confidence of P-Q2 is arguably over-determined (the corridor reading stipulates Born-preservation definitionally regardless of Torres Alegre), so the finding is about the *rhetoric of derived necessity*, not about P-Q2 collapsing.
- **Severity**: Medium.
- **Recommendation (routable)**: Soften "inherited as a structural commitment rather than bolted on" to a conditional — e.g. "*would be* inherited as a structural commitment *if the derivation survives review*" — matching the caution the article already voices at line 103.

### Issue 3 (MEDIUM–borderline): The trilemma's "something must select" begs the question / equivocates against the physicalist

- **File/Location**: line 141
- **Problem**: "The trilemma of selection secures that *something* must select at a point of genuine indeterminacy — determinism, randomness, and mental causation exhaust the options." Listing irreducible quantum randomness as a member of a *selection* trilemma imports agential framing into precisely what the physicalist reads as uncaused chance: on the orthodox reading nothing "selects," the outcome simply occurs. The claim "something must select" thus over-reaches against the very interlocutor the paragraph is addressing. The clause is partially hedged ("secures only that selection occurs, not that conscious selection constitutes a channel a physicalist would recognise"), and the substantive defense lives in `trilemma-of-selection`, so this is primarily a framing/wording exposure rather than a load-bearing logical hole.
- **Severity**: Medium (borderline calibration-acceptable).
- **Recommendation**: Optional wording pass — gloss "select" tenselessly ("the process by which one outcome becomes actual, of which irreducible chance is the null case") so the agential reading is not smuggled in.

## Counterarguments to Address

### The strongest hostile-expert objection the article leaves unanswered
- **Current content says**: Route 1 delivers "genuine token-level causation while leaving ensemble statistics untouched" because "consciousness… is *authoritative* over a trajectory the physical formalism already describes" (75).
- **A critic would argue**: Delegatory/trumping preemption is symmetric. Grant the framework entirely; it contains no resource that assigns preemptive authority to consciousness rather than to the physical microstate. So the same device that is supposed to rescue mental causation equally licenses the physicalist's epiphenomenalist re-description. Route 1 does not narrow the gap — it formalizes a framework in which both readings are equi-supported, then picks the conscious reading by tenet.
- **Suggested response**: Concede the direction is tenet-fixed and state plainly that Route 1's role is coherence-preservation, not dialectical victory over the physicalist — or find the in-framework asymmetry (why the mental, not the physical, is the natural bearer of trajectory-authority at a point of genuine indeterminacy).
