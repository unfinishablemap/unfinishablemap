## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)

The cluster is strong on Chalmers' core commitment that phenomenal character is a *separable* explanandum from functional/computational structure. `consciousness-and-memory` lines 138 (Phenomenological Trajectory) explicitly separates the information-trajectory of consolidation (CLS-tractable) from the qualitative-trajectory ("vivid reliving → bare knowing"), and refuses to let either functionalism or predictive processing dissolve the gap: "predictive processing accounts can frame schematisation as increasing predictive compression, but they still face the gap between describing an information-theoretic transformation and explaining why compression should feel like anything at all." This is the explanatory-gap discipline applied to a specific empirical structure rather than asserted abstractly. `memory-anomalies` line 67 makes the parallel move on the TLE evidence: "the localisation result tells us *that* a brain substrate produces the signal, not *why* the signal feels the way it does. The hard-problem residue is the same residue the Map flags everywhere: a neural correlate is a correlate, not an explanation of phenomenal character." Chalmers would recognise the *modus operandi*: name the empirical correlate honestly, name the residual phenomenal question that the correlate does not close, and refuse to treat the correlate's existence as a closure of the question. `mental-effort` line 108 closes the loop: "how do sub-personal cost-benefit outputs become *felt* as anything at all — a version of the [hard-problem-of-consciousness|hard problem] localised to effort." The hard problem is *localised* rather than abstracted — applied to the specific phenomenology under discussion, with its specific residual question stated. This is Chalmersian discipline operating at article-grain rather than chapter-grain.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)

Substantial purchase across all three articles. `memory-anomalies` is the cleanest case: the lived report — the felt certainty of pastness without recoverable past, the felt alienness of a known word, the felt ownership of an unowned line — is preserved as the explanandum throughout. Line 37 frames the entire article around the Nagelian datum: "in each case, the felt-quality survives the failure intact." This is Nagel's "what is it like" applied editorially: the felt-quality is not what gets explained away, it is what the cognitive architecture has to be made consistent with. `consciousness-and-memory` lines 130–136 (Phenomenological Trajectory) is Nagel-friendly in a different register: each consolidation stage is described from the inside ("Hours after encoding: the memory is vivid and emotionally intense... Decades: schematisation is nearly complete... Proustian moments... can temporarily restore vivid detail"), and the article makes the qualitative trajectory load-bearing as a *separate object of explanation* from the information trajectory. `mental-effort` line 56 names the modal-case discipline that protects the article's first-person datum from the standard Nagel-skeptic move: "this phenomenology is widely shared — the modal case, with rare clinical exceptions discussed below." A weaker article would have universalised the report and so made itself vulnerable to a single counterexample (the Naccache lesion patient). This article anticipates the move, names the rare exception, and uses the exception as evidence rather than as defeater (the lesion patient's autonomic decoupling shows the felt sense is wired into the autonomic chain in normals — Nagel's lived-report register doing real work without being inflated to universality). The cliché-removal in the same section ("not because consciousness stopped working, but because the interface has been trained" → positive claim) is Nagelian discipline at the prose level: the claim is made directly rather than being constructed in opposition to a strawman.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)

This persona has the heaviest purchase on `mental-effort`, where the willed-instructed distinction (lines 64–68) is the article's closest approach to Kane's own framework: "When you *freely choose* where to attend, different neural processes engage — frontal theta oscillations (~500ms post-decision), bidirectional frontoparietal coherence, and additional frontal recruitment. Kane's central claim — that genuine self-formation requires a phenomenologically distinctive *generative* act, not merely the implementation of an externally provided goal — is given a neural-empirical correlate without being inflated into proof. The article's discipline at line 100 is the Kane-relevant restraint: the willed-instructed distinction *constrains* the dualist reading but does not establish it. `consciousness-and-memory` line 88 (the encoding-consolidation dissociation) is Kane-relevant in a structurally parallel way: "consciousness participates at the point of selection — choosing what matters, what to attend to, what to encode — and withdraws once the selection is made. The brain handles storage; consciousness handles significance." The agency-relevant claim is not "consciousness causes everything"; it is "consciousness is required where selectional significance is established, and not required where storage and parallel-replay are the operations." This is Kane's selective-engagement framing applied to memory: agency operates where the genuinely-open question is, and withdraws where the operation is mechanical. `memory-anomalies` line 81's framing of cryptomnesia is Kane-relevant on a different axis: the recombinant work creative output requires depends on a degree of *source-tag slack* — "a mind that perfectly tagged every source would be unable to forget a borrowing well enough to repurpose it." Genuine creative agency requires an architectural opacity that the perfectly-monitoring mind would lack. This is the Kane discipline operating as an *enabling* condition rather than as a defeater: agency is preserved by the very source-monitoring slack that produces cryptomnesia.
