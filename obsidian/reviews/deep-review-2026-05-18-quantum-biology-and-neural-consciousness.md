## Pessimistic Analysis Summary

### Critical Issues Found (and Resolved)

**Issue A (topic article, line 192 Further Reading; concept article, line 191 Further Reading)**: Both articles cross-linked to [[evolutionary-case-for-quantum-neural-effects]] with the description "Why evolutionary logic makes quantum neural effects **probable**." This is the *exact* possibility/probability slippage pessimistic-2026-05-17b flagged as the central defect of the source article (Issue 1, lede line 40). Propagating that slippage into the cross-link description in two other articles amplifies the drift catalogue-wide. Per the calibration error rule in the deep-review skill: a tenet-accepting reviewer would still flag "probable" as overstated relative to the evidential-status scale, because the load-bearing support is "precedent + tenet removes defeater," not positive evidence that neural quantum effects are more likely than not.

*Resolution*: Both Further Reading entries rewritten to "Why evolutionary logic establishes quantum neural effects as a realistic possibility under selective pressure." This matches the discipline-honest framing pessimistic-2026-05-17b proposed and aligns with the [[evidential-status-discipline]] five-tier scale.

**Issue B (concept article, lines 85-90)**: The decoherence-debate paragraph and table presented Hagan et al. (2002) as a settled "corrected" coherence-time figure — "Corrected coherence times extend to 10⁻⁵ to 10⁻⁴ seconds—eight to nine orders of magnitude longer." This is the *acute* form of pessimistic-2026-05-17b Issue 2 ("Hagan 2002 figure load-bears without the warning it deserves"). The topic article's prior 2026-05-11 refinement softened this language ("one estimate in a contested calculation"), but the concept article retained the unhedged version, presenting a 24-year-old counter-calculation by Hameroff collaborators (interested parties in defending Orch OR) as a definitive correction. A tenet-accepting reviewer would still flag the framing because the issue is empirical-calibration, not framework-boundary disagreement.

*Resolution*: Both article passages now specify that Hagan et al. are Hameroff collaborators with vested interest in defending Orch OR, that their counter-calculation has not been independently replicated in the 24 years since publication, that the underlying assumptions remain contested by decoherence theorists, and that the dispute is unresolved rather than a settled correction. The coherence-time table now also includes a "Status" column making this explicit.

**Issue C (topic article, line 45)**: The article framed Tegmark 2000's calculation as targeting "neural quantum states" generically — "Max Tegmark's influential 2000 calculation estimated that decoherence... would destroy neural quantum states in femtoseconds." But the calculation was specifically aimed at ion superpositions in open neural membranes and at microtubule conformational superpositions (the structures Penrose-Hameroff Orch OR requires) — not at all neural quantum hypotheses. Line 149 of the same article correctly scoped it ("Tegmark's femtosecond estimate targeted ion superpositions in open neural membranes—the least protected environment"), yielding an internal scope inconsistency. This is pessimistic-2026-05-17b Issue 4 (Tegmark 2000 misattribution).

*Resolution*: Line 45 rewritten to specify Tegmark's calculation targeted "neural superpositions of the specific kind orchestrated-objective-reduction (Orch OR) requires (microtubule conformational states, ion superpositions in open neural membranes)" and to note explicitly that the calculation does not address discrete-event mechanisms (ion-channel tunnelling, synaptic SNARE tunnelling, Posner-molecule nuclear spins, Stapp-style quantum Zeno events), which face different empirical objections. A corresponding note has been added to the concept article's decoherence section.

### Medium Issues Found (and Resolved)

**Issue D (topic article line 53; concept article lines 73, 77)**: Teleological language about evolution as a discovering, engineering, optimising agent. Topic article line 53: "We know evolution **optimises** quantum effects for biological advantage... natural selection **discovered** quantum solutions." Concept article: "**Natural selection produced** a quantum compass... evolution can **optimise** biological systems for quantum-coherent function" (line 73); "**Evolution exploited** quantum tunneling to accelerate reactions" (line 77). This is pessimistic-2026-05-17b Issue 6 — teleological framing imports an aim that does not exist in the causal account, and the aimedness is doing inferential work the discipline does not license.

*Resolution*: Replaced teleological framings with descriptive evolutionary language. "Selection has propagated biochemical variants whose dynamics involve quantum effects." "Selection has propagated cryptochrome variants that exploit radical-pair quantum coherence." "Selection has propagated active-site geometries that exploit tunneling." The convergence-table entry "Evolution exploits quantum effects in warm biology" rewritten to "Selection has propagated quantum-exploiting variants in warm biology (cryptochromes, enzymes, photosystems)." The "Established" bullet "Evolution optimises quantum phenomena for biological function" rewritten to specify the propagation of specific molecular architectures rather than a teleological aim.

**Issue E (topic article line 53; previous lede)**: Possibility/probability slippage in the cross-link sentence: "if natural selection discovered quantum solutions for navigation and chemistry, the computational payoffs in neural systems **would dwarf** those of a compass or a faster enzyme." "Would dwarf" is the same unsupported quantitative claim pessimistic-2026-05-17b flagged in the source article's lede (line 38). The sentence implied an inferential leap from precedent-class to target-class that the article had not earned.

*Resolution*: Rewritten to "variants supplying neural quantum capacity could face selective pressure if neural problems demand quantum solutions classical mechanisms cannot deliver — but that is an evolutionary argument from realistic possibility, not a demonstration of actuality, and the bridging premise (that neural problems genuinely demand quantum solutions) is itself contested." This makes the conditional explicit and the bridging premise honest about its contested status.

**Issue F (concept article line 146)**: "Experimental Convergence" claimed: "The quantum hypothesis made specific predictions—microtubule involvement in anaesthesia (confirmed, Cohen's d = 1.9), measurable entanglement signatures during awareness (Kerskens & Pérez 2022, disputed by Warren 2023), viable coherence times—and **those predictions held up**." This is the unfalsifiable-prediction-list pattern pessimistic-2026-05-17b Issue 3 flagged in the sister article. Each "prediction" is consistent with classical accommodation; "predictions held up" overstates the discipline-honest reading.

*Resolution*: Rewritten to "the discipline of 'predictions held up' deserves care," with each line of evidence now framed in "consistent with" rather than "confirmed/held up" language. The closing sentence now explicitly notes that "each line accommodates classical explanations."
