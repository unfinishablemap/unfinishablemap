## Why this cluster is diverse

This session's prior optimistic passes covered free-will/incompatibilism, quantum-foundations/interpretations, indexical-identity, and AI/machine-consciousness. This cluster is a genuinely distinct vein: it is the Map's *empirical* register — third-person clinical and neurological data (dissociation syndromes, neuroplasticity trials, lesion/disconnection anatomy, disorders of consciousness, blindsight) assessed for what it can and cannot show about consciousness. None of the prior four passes touched it. It is also the cluster where the Hardline Empiricist persona does the heaviest lifting, so it is the right domain to mine for calibration-honest strengths.

## Executive Summary

This is arguably the Map's most methodologically mature cluster. Its defining strength is that every article down-counts its own evidence in-body: convergence is named and then discounted, "removes a defeater" is held apart from "upgrades the evidence," and the retraction record is tracked. The expansion opportunities are specific clinical windows the cluster gestures at but has not given dedicated treatment — Capgras delusion, Charles Bonnet syndrome, locked-in syndrome, and anosognosia — each a clean interface-architecture case in the same idiom as the existing blindsight and phantom-limb articles.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
Blindsight is treated exactly as a property dualist would want: the article isolates the residual-V1 deflation, the Phillips (2021) "qualitatively degraded conscious vision" challenge, and the metacognitive-access reading, and states plainly that "whether the dissociation proves consciousness is non-physical depends on interpreting what's absent." The double dissociation with blind insight (Scott et al. 2014) — and the honest note that it spans different domains, which "limits the inferential force somewhat" — is the kind of three-systems argument (processing / phenomenal / metacognitive) that takes the explanatory gap seriously without overselling it.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
The neuroplasticity article engages the Schwartz–Stapp–Beauregard (2005) quantum-Zeno model directly and quotes it, while flagging the decoherence-timescale challenge — serious engagement with the physics-consciousness interface rather than a hand-wave. Blindsight's closing links the V1 attentional interface to Stapp's attention-as-observation proposal without pretending there is direct evidence for it ("this remains highly speculative").

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The clinical-evidence-quality article's "First-Person Data Problem" section is a highlight: it argues first-person reports are "evidence of a distinct kind, requiring its own quality standards rather than awaiting reduction to third-person measurements," invoking Husserl's evidence taxonomy (Berghofer 2019) and Varela's neurophenomenology. Depersonalisation is read as "a phenomenological experiment" — centring what the loss of felt mineness is *like*, distinct from any content-retrieval failure (the Cambridge Depersonalisation Scale's "Anomalous Subjective Recall" factor).

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
This cluster is empirical rather than panpsychist, so there is little for the process reading to grab — and, importantly, the cluster never reaches for process-philosophical resonance to inflate an evidential claim. That restraint is itself the correct posture here (see the Hardline Empiricist verdict). Where process-friendliness does appear — the filter/transmission lineage (Bergson's *Matter and Memory* "selective valve", James's "permissive or transmissive" brain) — it is presented as an interpretive framework whose cost is openly booked, not as evidence.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
Conversion disorder / FND is the cluster's cleanest agency exhibit, and the article's honesty about it is a strength: it corrects the tempting "severed descending channel" reading to the contemporary "overactive prior actively generates the symptom" model (Voon et al. 2010; Edwards et al. 2012), and concedes this makes FND "the weakest of the four windows for the bidirectional reading." An agency theorist gets a real datum — volitional motor output abolished with the substrate intact — without being sold more than the evidence bears.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
The clinical-evidence-quality article is structurally mysterian in the best sense: the "missing comparator" argument (there is no "consciousness placebo") and the closing standard — "a finding can clear every methodological bar and still leave the question of consciousness's nature exactly where it was" — locate a principled limit on what third-person clinical data can ever settle, without collapsing into defeatism.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This cluster is a showcase for the persona, and its verdict is load-bearing here. Named strengths:

- **Tenet-as-evidence-upgrade is praise-worthily *not* done, repeatedly and explicitly.** The neuroplasticity article: "Clinical neuroplasticity therefore removes a defeater — the epiphenomenalist claim that consciousness can make no measurable difference — without thereby establishing dualist causation over its physicalist rival. Removing a defeater is not the same as upgrading the evidence." The clinical-evidence-quality article generalises this into a stated standard (#5 Honest Calibration) and applies it to the Map's *own* quantum-interaction claims, conceding they "score poorly" on replication and control.
- **Convergence named and discounted in-body.** Clinical-dissociation runs its own common-cause null: three of the four windows may reduce to one "precision-weighting" family under active inference, so "the convergence reads one upstream architecture several times." It then gives the *honest partial* reply (the label is a loose family, DID resists it) rather than either abandoning or overclaiming the compound signature. Neuroplasticity does the same, down-counting "three superficially independent streams to one preferential signature."
- **The five-tier register kept honest, with self-correction visible in the git record.** Terminal-lucidity was revised to absorb the 2025 Tollock prospective study and now states the early prospective evidence "points away from, not toward, the filter interpretation" — a case where new data *lowered* the article's confidence and the article says so. It distinguishes "constrain" from "establish" throughout.
- **Retraction- and replication-tracking as first-class content.** Neuroplasticity and clinical-evidence-quality both cite the retracted Siew & Yu (2025) mindfulness meta-analysis *as* a retraction (40% of null participants omitted), track the Kral et al. (2022) replication failure that dropped meditation as a "fourth pillar," and grade Schwartz's n=18 OCD PET as "suggestive." Disconnection-neuroscience formalises the discipline into a three-rule citation protocol ("do not let layer 1 lend its grade to layer 2").
- Where the Process Philosopher's praise would normally risk cashing out as a tier-upgrade, this cluster never gives it the chance — the filter lineage is booked as an interpretive framework with an openly-stated unfalsifiability cost, not as evidence. The two personas converge: the cluster has resolved the tension honestly.

## Content Strengths

### clinical-dissociation-as-systematic-evidence.md
- **Strongest point**: it states the strongest physicalist rival "at full strength before the interface reading is pressed" — the developed functionalist/predictive-processing self-model account for which selective dissociation is "the theory's home move, not a retreat" — and then locates the disagreement precisely at the intact-substrate structural argument. This is steelmanning as method.
- **Why it works**: the DPDR section shows a rare intellectual move — it *retires* an older argument (the "precision objection" pressing functionalism) because the Ciaunica et al. 2022 active-inference account "absorbs the objection," conceding the interface reading now "competes with a fully specified physicalist rival rather than filling a gap."

### terminal-lucidity-and-filter-transmission-theory.md
- **Strongest point**: the "constrain vs establish" framing, made concrete by the monotonicity argument (production models predict substrate-cognition monotonicity; terminal lucidity strains it) paired with the candid admission that filter theory absorbing the phenomenon "is not, on its own, positive evidence that the model is true."
- **Why it works**: the Selectivity Problem section names filter theory's own hardest objection (most brain damage *diminishes* rather than *loosens*) and concedes the interface/filter distinction "introduces flexibility that risks unfalsifiability."

### clinical-evidence-quality-standards-consciousness-research.md
- **Strongest point**: a reusable five-standard framework (replication, control adequacy, interpretive range, convergence, honest calibration) that the Map then turns on itself, including its Tenet-2 quantum claims — self-consistency as a demonstrated practice, not a promise.
- **Notable quote**: "Raising evidence quality removes defeaters and narrows the space of tenable readings; it does not by itself convert a metaphysically neutral result into support for one framework over another."

## Expansion Opportunities

Each candidate below was grep-self-checked against the live corpus. The cluster's granularity (dedicated articles already exist for blindsight, phantom-limb, split-brain, pain-asymbolia) means single clinical windows are article-sized here.

### High Priority

#### Capgras delusion and the affective-recognition channel
- **Target section**: topics/ (clinical window; could also sit in concepts/)
- **Builds on**: disconnection-neuroscience (within-recognition disconnection), blindsight (processing/phenomenology dissociation), clinical-dissociation (selective channel severance).
- **Would address**: Capgras — the delusion that a familiar person has been replaced by an impostor — is the textbook *affective-familiarity-without-perceptual-deficit* case (Ellis & Young 1990 two-route model; Ramachandran & Hirstein): face *recognition* is intact while the affective-familiarity response is severed, the mirror-image of prosopagnosia. It is a clean interface-architecture exhibit (a within-recognition channel disconnection producing a specific belief), and it carries a built-in Hardline-Empiricist caveat the Map will handle well: the two-factor model (anomalous data *plus* a belief-evaluation deficit) blocks any naive "severed channel therefore dualism" reading.
- **Self-check verdict**: **GENUINELY UNCOVERED as a dedicated treatment.** Capgras appears only as a passing *example* inside the recognition-void (a void, at cap), inventory-blindness, introspection, and empirical-phenomena-mental-causation — always illustrating hidden-component opacity, never treated as a clinical window in its own right with the Ellis/Young + two-factor apparatus. Near-miss risk: low; the existing mentions are all the "recognition = perceptual ID + affective familiarity" slogan, not the modality-specificity (photo vs voice), the delusional-elaboration second factor, or the reduplicative-paramnesia family.
- **Tenet alignment**: primarily Tenet 1 (interface/channel reading — is affective familiarity a separable ascending channel?); secondary Tenet 5 (the two-factor caveat is exactly where parsimony gets contested).

#### Charles Bonnet syndrome as release phenomenon / the converse of blindsight
- **Target section**: topics/
- **Builds on**: blindsight (dissociation), filter-theory, perceptual-failure-and-the-interface (compensatory generation), dualist-perception.
- **Would address**: CBS — vivid, formed visual hallucinations in the visually impaired with intact insight — is the deafferentation/release case: as the transmission channel narrows, internally-generated content is unmasked. It is the clean *converse of blindsight* (blindsight = processing without phenomenology; CBS = phenomenology without input), and it is a natural filter-theory exhibit while also being the case where the strongest physicalist rival (cortical disinhibition / unchecked predictive-processing priors) is most compelling. A dedicated article can run that contest at full resolution the way blindsight runs its three interpretations.
- **Self-check verdict**: **GENUINELY UNCOVERED as a dedicated treatment, but flag overlap.** CBS appears as a one-line example in at least six articles (perceptual-failure-and-the-interface, dualist-perception, lucid-dreaming-and-dualist-rendering, hypnagogic-phenomenology, naturalist-relationalism) — always as "compensatory generation." Overlap risk is real: perceptual-failure already absorbs CBS *and* runs the predictive-processing "unchecked priors" rival. A dedicated article must earn its place by going deeper than that one paragraph — the Ffytche et al. functional-imaging work (hallucination content tracks the specialised cortical area active), the intact-insight feature that distinguishes CBS from psychotic hallucination, and the deafferentation gradient. Recommend as High only if scoped to *exceed* the perceptual-failure paragraph; otherwise Medium.
- **Tenet alignment**: Tenet 1 (filter/interface reading) + Tenet 3 (interface actively generating a phenomenal field when input drops — the bidirectional "miniature" perceptual-failure already flags).

### Medium Priority

#### Locked-in syndrome: intact ascending channels, severed descending output
- **Target section**: topics/
- **Builds on**: consciousness-disruption-and-the-mind-brain-interface (DoC catalogue), neurological-dissociations, brain-computer-interfaces-and-the-interface-boundary.
- **Would address**: LIS is the paradigm of *fully intact consciousness with near-total motor-output severance* (classically ventral-pontine lesion), where communication is recovered only through the residual eye-movement channel or BCI. Philosophically it is the cleanest "mind intact, descending channel cut, ascending channels intact" case — and, crucially, the honest case where filter theory's "damage loosens the filter" prediction plainly does *not* apply (pure interface/output degradation, no enhancement). That makes it a good discipline exhibit: it shows the Map distinguishing output-channel severance from filter-loosening rather than reading every deficit as filter evidence. First-person testimony (Bauby) and Bauer's classical/incomplete/total taxonomy give it phenomenological texture.
- **Self-check verdict**: **UNCOVERED as a dedicated treatment; moderate overlap risk.** LIS is named in the DoC catalogue of consciousness-disruption and listed in neurological-dissociations' "further dissociations," but never given dedicated space. Distinct from covert consciousness / CMD (see declined candidate below): in LIS awareness is behaviourally *detectable*; in CMD it is not. Overlap risk with the BCI article and consciousness-disruption is the main caveat — the article should focus on the ascending-intact/descending-severed signature and the filter-loosening-does-not-apply point, not re-run DoC diagnostics.
- **Tenet alignment**: Tenet 3 (bidirectional interaction — the descending/output channel isolated) + Tenet 1.

#### Anosognosia and the self-monitoring channel
- **Target section**: topics/ or concepts/
- **Builds on**: introspection, inventory-blindness, metacognition, consciousness-and-testimony, perceptual-failure.
- **Would address**: anosognosia (denial of deficit — classically denial of hemiplegia after right-hemisphere damage) is the clinical window on the *self-monitoring / metacognition* interface: the striking datum is the caloric-vestibular stimulation finding (temporary restoration of deficit-awareness), a near-literal on/off switch for self-knowledge, plus the right-hemisphere lateralisation. It bears directly on the Map's introspection-architecture programme and the occluded-void idea (a knowledge limit hidden by the mechanism producing it).
- **Self-check verdict**: **UNCOVERED as a dedicated treatment, lower confidence — flag near-miss.** Anosognosia is referenced across curated-mind, introspection-architecture-independence-scoring, consciousness-and-testimony, perceptual-failure, inventory-blindness, and a confabulation-void research note. The concept is heavily pre-referenced as an *example* of self-monitoring failure, so a dedicated article risks being a re-packaging of inventory-blindness + introspection unless it adds the distinctive clinical material (caloric remission, Ramachandran's denial studies, the somatoparaphrenia boundary) and a focused interface reading. Recommend the harvester treat this as the *lowest-confidence* of the four; verify against inventory-blindness depth before minting.
- **Tenet alignment**: Tenet 1 + the introspection/metacognition-limit theme (self-model channel).
