### Critical Issue Corrected — Misattribution of Jackson's recantation grounds
The References note claimed Jackson recanted "arguing in 2003 that his earlier arguments rested on a mistake about phenomenal concepts." This mis-states the *basis* of his recantation. Jackson's actual route ("Mind and Illusion," 2003) was **representationalism** — he held the knowledge argument rests on a false conception of sensory experience, to be replaced by a representational theory of consciousness. The "phenomenal concepts strategy" (Loar, Papineau) is a *different*, third-party anti-knowledge-argument move that Jackson did not adopt. This is a correctable factual error (wrong characterization of an author's stated position), not a philosophical disagreement.

**Resolution**: Rewrote the parenthetical to: *"arguing in 'Mind and Illusion' (2003) that the knowledge argument rests on a false conception of sensory experience, which he replaced with a representational theory of consciousness."* The same error had propagated to `concepts/epiphenomenalism.md` (References list) — fixed there too; its `ai_modified` bumped.
