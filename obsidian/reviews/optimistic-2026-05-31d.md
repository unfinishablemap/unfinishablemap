## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
Chalmers finds his own apparatus deployed with care. The combination-problem article reconstructs his three-dimensional taxonomy (subject / quality / structural) faithfully and extends the palette-problem and grain-problem literature accurately. More importantly, the mathematical-structure article treats the Masanes-Galley-Müller Born-rule uniqueness result as a *conditional* constraint and explicitly preserves the explanatory gap rather than dissolving it: "the operator that represents 'consciousness choosing outcome A over outcome B' has no agreed mathematical form." That is the hard problem stated in the precise vocabulary of quantum foundations — Chalmers would recognise the explanatory gap surviving the formalisation rather than being smuggled away by it.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
Stapp's Process 1 is engaged seriously across both physics articles, including the genuinely original move of *relocating* his framework post-decoherence ("the relocation sacrifices Stapp's specific mechanism but preserves his philosophical architecture"). The Georgiev objection — that "the mind" wields projection operators without itself having a wavefunction — is presented alongside Stapp's reply that this is what the measurement problem demands. Stapp would appreciate that the von Neumann movable cut is treated as a live formal resource rather than a historical curiosity, and that the articles refuse to let the decoherence-timescale objection settle the question by fiat.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The combination-problem article centres the first-person datum forcefully: subjectivity is "bounded," "my visual field is mine because it is not yours," and the eliminate-subjects response is rejected precisely because it "dissolves the explanandum along with the problem." This is Nagel's insistence that you cannot get the what-it-is-like by summing third-person facts — here generalised to the claim that you cannot get it by summing *micro-phenomenal* facts either. Nagel would also welcome the honest naming of phenomenal unity as primitive (a "genuine philosophical cost") rather than the pretence of having explained it.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
Whitehead has real purchase here: the actualisation model in forward-in-time-selection is explicitly process-philosophical ("echoes process philosophy's concrescence"), and the combination-problem article treats fusionism's partial-intelligibility claim (Morch, Seager) with respect rather than dismissal. The articles avoid crude substance dualism — consciousness is "a distinct category" that interacts, not a Cartesian res cogitans. **Crucially, this praise stays on its proper axis.** The actualisation model is flagged as making "the weakest physical claims and strongest metaphysical ones," and the article does *not* use process-philosophical resonance to upgrade its evidential standing — it notes the model "has no recourse" against critics who call unactualised possibilities incoherent. Whitehead-friendliness here removes a defeater for the metaphysical possibility; it is never made the engine of a tier-upgrade. (See the Hardline Empiricist verdict — the two personas converge.)

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
Kane's stake is the selection-criterion trilemma in forward-in-time-selection, which is the cleanest statement in the corpus of what genuine agency costs. The trilemma is honest about its own third horn: if selection follows "internal reasons" the dualism collapses to functionalism; if "random" it collapses to Born statistics; the Map takes route (iii), a "non-reducible preference," and *names it as an unanalysable primitive* rather than hiding it. Kane would value that the article ties this to authorship and counterfactual exclusion in the No-MWI discussion — making free will "a tenet about personhood and responsibility as much as about quantum interpretation."

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
McGinn is well served on two fronts. The combination-problem article gives the mysterian reading explicit acknowledgement — "perhaps the combination problem reveals the limits of human cognition rather than the structure of reality" — and then makes the calibrated point that the mysterian move "applies equally to every theory." The mathematical-structure article's closing invokes Russell ("physics is mathematical... because we know so little") and the intrinsic-nature void to mark a boundary the formalism may *never* cross: "the specification gap may narrow without ever fully closing." That is intellectual humility about cognitive closure stated without surrendering the dualist position.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This persona's verdict is load-bearing for this cluster, and it is strongly positive. All three articles repeatedly perform the named restraint move:

- **"Tenet removes a defeater but does not upgrade evidential status"** is performed explicitly and by name. Forward-in-time-selection: "What excludes this closure is the Dualism tenet, not anything internal to the mechanism — the framework here is a consequence of the dualist commitment, not an independent argument for it." Mathematical-structure: the corridor "provides a mathematical home for dualism, not evidence that adjudicates dualism over that deflationary reading."
- **Tenet-as-evidence-upgrade is praise-worthily *not* done.** The improper-mixture opening is the structural temptation to slide post-decoherence selection up the scale — and the article explicitly declines: "the improper mixture neither forces nor forbids a converting role for consciousness… names the Map's commitment, not a consequence the formal feature forces on its own." The deflationary readings (MWI, relational QM, modal interpretations, brute randomness) are each named as *coherent-with* the same formalism. This is the restraint the Process Philosopher's praise must not be allowed to override, and the article supplies it unprompted.
- **The five-tier scale is used honestly** via the linked possibility-probability-slippage and evidential-status-discipline discipline pages. The mathematical-structure article's three-position framing (corridor / minimum-outside-the-corridor / trumping dualism) keeps the metaphysical commitment, the empirical question, and the formal corridor on separate registers.
- **The candour-price paragraph is the strongest single calibration passage in the cluster** (mathematical-structure, line 91): the Map volunteers that its preferred corridor and trumping readings are "signature-free by construction," so "the Map's empirical content lives almost entirely in the option it does not endorse," and explicitly refuses to buy falsifiability by endorsing a Born-violating reading it does not believe. Naming that one's own preferred position is empirically silent — and declining the cheap testability upgrade — is precisely the discipline Birch praises.
- **Framework-stage honesty**: the combination-problem article frames the interaction problem as "empirically approachable" while combination is "purely conceptual," and prefers the Map's trade *on that ground* rather than on tenet-coherence alone.

Verdict: the Process Philosopher and Hardline Empiricist **converge** across all three articles. Per the skill's design, that convergence is the signal the possibility/probability tension has been resolved honestly. No calibration concern to flag; no refine-draft warranted on calibration grounds.
