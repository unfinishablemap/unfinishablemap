## Executive Summary

The cluster is unusually well-calibrated: the P.F./Galen Strawson disambiguation holds robustly across every article, the three-argument classification (Consequence = leeway, Manipulation = source, Frankfurt-cases = pivot) is internally consistent, the "mesh brackets sourcehood" claim coheres with the manipulation argument's bite, and the discipline against reading compatibilist gaps as dualism-evidence is applied scrupulously and repeatedly. ## Critical Issues

### Issue 1: Cross-article contradiction on the Map's source-family (wide vs narrow)
- **Files**: concepts/source-versus-leeway-incompatibilism.md (Relation to Site Perspective, lines 80–82); concepts/moral-responsibility.md (line 43); topics/frankfurt-cases-and-the-principle-of-alternate-possibilities.md (Relation to Site Perspective, lines 79–81)
- **Problem**: The hub *defines* wide source incompatibilism as the view that "ultimate sourcehood *itself requires* morally relevant alternative possibilities—that one cannot be the genuine origin of an action without there having been more than one thing one could originate," and then explicitly locates the Map there: "The Map wants both conditions, which places its closest mainstream neighbour at Timpe's **wide** source incompatibilism." moral-responsibility agrees the Map "leans hardest on [source] while wanting both." On the wide view, *no alternatives entails no sourcehood*. But frankfurt-cases makes its "load-bearing move" the claim that "even granting that the counterexamples succeed against leeway PAP... the Map can concede the point and still hold that responsibility requires ultimate sourcehood." That concession — alternatives may not be required, keep pure sourcehood — is precisely a *narrow* source move, and it is incoherent for a wide-source theorist, whose sourcehood cannot survive the loss of alternatives. The retreat-to-source fallback therefore silently relocates the Map from wide to narrow source, contradicting the position the other two articles assert.
- **Severity**: Medium (high salience: this is the cluster's central self-location claim, the calibration centerpiece the whole wave is built around).
- **Recommendation**: A refine pass to reconcile. Either (a) frankfurt-cases should state that the fallback *relocates* the Map from wide to narrow source — a substantive further concession, not the costless "the tenets survive the translation" it currently claims; or (b) the hub should mark the Map's wide-source location as *conditional on its leeway commitment holding*, with narrow source as the acknowledged fallback taxon if Frankfurt succeeds. Note that frankfurt-cases' existing "honesty note" ("the retreat to source is a *retreat*... it costs the Map the clean leeway picture") under-describes the cost: it names the loss of the leeway *picture* but not the loss of the wide-source *location*.

## Counterarguments to Address

### Move-one leeway preservation is contested by the article's own cited cases
- **File**: topics/frankfurt-cases-and-the-principle-of-alternate-possibilities.md (lines 77, 45–57)
- **Current content says**: The Map "reads the prior sign in a Frankfurt case as non-necessitating and lands on the indeterminism horn, where a robust alternative survives" — presented as the first reason its leeway commitment is "not straightforwardly refuted."
- **A critic would argue**: Landing on the indeterminism horn does *not* by itself preserve a robust alternative. The article itself, two paragraphs earlier, records the wave of "*indeterministic* Frankfurt-style cases — blockage and buffer constructions by Mele and Robb, Pereboom, Hunt, and Stump — each engineered to answer the indeterminism horn by closing the alternative while conceding that the prior link is not deterministic." Those cases exist precisely to defeat the Map's move-one escape. So move-one depends on the buffer/blockage cases *failing*, not merely on the sign being non-necessitating.
- **Severity**: Medium.
- **Suggested response**: Move-one should explicitly condition its success on the buffer/blockage cases failing (an unresolved question the article already flags as "unresolved"), rather than presenting "indeterminism horn ⇒ robust alternative survives" as automatic. The hedges are present nearby ("not a proof that Frankfurt is wrong") but the specific engagement with the buffer cases that target this exact move is missing.

### The cluster's defense reduces to one repeated, purely-defensive Tenet-5 move
- **Files**: source-versus-leeway (line 82), frankfurt-hierarchical-mesh (line 89), the-manipulation-argument (line 71), reactive-attitudes (line 83), frankfurt-cases (line 81)
- **Current content says**: Each article's "Relation to Site Perspective" resolves the pressure with a near-verbatim Tenet-5 move — the Map "declines to treat [X] as decisive... while conceding, symmetrically, that this buys the Map no positive support."
- **A critic would argue** (the Empiricist/Popperian persona): Against five structurally distinct opponents (van Inwagen's leeway argument, Pereboom's source argument, Frankfurt's mesh, Frankfurt's cases, Strawson's dissolution), the cluster deploys a *single* escape, and by its own repeated admission that escape yields "no positive support" — only a block on refutation. The cumulative honest picture is a free-will program that everywhere achieves a draw and nowhere gains positive ground. A Popperian presses that a position defended solely by "parsimony is an unreliable guide here" against every challenger looks engineered to be unfalsifiable-by-argument; the uniformity is itself a warning sign, not a virtue.
- **Severity**: Medium (this is honest, not a defect — but the cluster nowhere *names* that the aggregate effect of five identical Tenet-5 stand-offs is a purely defensive program, and the near-verbatim repetition is also a prose-monotony concern across articles that will often be read together).
- **Suggested response**: Consider one place in the cluster (the hub, or free-will) that steps back and acknowledges the pattern: the Map's libertarian free-will program, taken across these opponents, secures coherence-with-tenets and refutation-resistance, not positive argumentative support — and vary the Tenet-5 phrasing so the five articles do not read as a copied refrain.
