## Critical / Medium Issues

### Issue 1: Relation-to-Site-Perspective reverts to a claim the body retired
- **File**: `obsidian/topics/clinical-neuroplasticity-evidence-for-bidirectional-causation.md`
- **Location**: line 122 (Relation to Site Perspective) vs line 64 (§Route versus Direction)
- **Problem**: Line 122 asserts: *"This interpretation strengthens the case against causal closure: if the physical domain were causally closed, there would be no reason for top-down and bottom-up routes to diverge systematically."* But line 64 — the body's settled position after four paragraphs of calibration — explicitly **concedes** the opposite: *"different physical inputs naturally produce different physical pathways ... so route divergence is expected,"* and *"the physicalist can in principle absorb even a robust directional tendency into a sufficiently detailed account of cortical-versus-subcortical entry points,"* closing with *"the input-modality deflation is here **declined rather than refuted** on the physicalist's own terms."* The RtSP "there would be no reason for routes to diverge" is precisely a reason a causal-closure physicalist **can** give (input anatomy). The conclusion section re-inflates an anti-closure inference the body deliberately walked back.
- **Severity**: Medium (internal contradiction / calibration drift; the article holds itself to the higher standard set in its own body)
- **Why this is NOT a by-design framework move**: argued-dualism and tenet-perimeter reasoning are deliberate, but here the mismatch is internal — the body sets the calibrated bar and the RtSP section violates it. The fix is to bring line 122 into line with line 64, not to weaken the Map's commitment.
- **Recommendation**: Soften line 122 to "compatible with / suggestive of" register and drop (or hedge) the "no reason for routes to diverge" conditional, e.g. "the systematic preferential top-down signature is *compatible with* and *suggestive of* a failure of causal closure, though as §Route versus Direction notes the physicalist can absorb route-divergence via input anatomy; the Map declines that deflation rather than claiming to refute it."
