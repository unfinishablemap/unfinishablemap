## Pessimistic Analysis Summary

### Critical Issues Found
- **Factual error: Brouwer's fixed-point theorem (FIXED)**. The intuitionism section claimed "Brouwer's fixed-point theorem and the fan theorem depend on properties of choice sequences." This inverts the history. Brouwer's fixed-point theorem (1911) is a *classical, non-constructive* topological result; Brouwer himself later rejected it as intuitionistically invalid precisely because its proof is non-constructive. It does not depend on the choice-sequence apparatus. The genuine intuitionistic examples are the **fan theorem** and **bar theorem**, which do depend on choice sequences / bar induction. **Resolution**: replaced with "The fan theorem and bar theorem—the load-bearing results of intuitionistic analysis—depend on properties of choice sequences..." and added a parenthetical noting Brouwer disowned his own fixed-point theorem on constructive grounds. The fix strengthens the article's point: Brouwer rejecting a famous result that bears his name is a vivid illustration of the involuntariness/strictness the article argues for.
