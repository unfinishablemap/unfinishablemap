### Medium Issues Found
- **"First causal evidence" overstated Konkoly 2026**: The study (N=20, pre-selected for lucid dreaming, unreplicated) was described as "first causal evidence." Changed to "preliminary causal evidence" and added note about sample pre-selection. [Resolved]
- **Epiphenomenalism argument had an addressable gap**: The argument that phenomenal mode predicts cognitive contribution type is strong, but didn't address the physicalist objection that different neural modes trivially produce both the phenomenal character and the functional output. Added a paragraph addressing this objection—conceding the tracking observation while noting the unexplained coincidence of systematic alignment across four consciousness states. [Resolved]
- **No Many Worlds tenet section was argumentatively weak**: The original tried to use dream phenomenology (felt singularity, choosing forecloses alternatives) as evidence for collapse interpretations—a stretch. Rewrote to frame the connection as "suggestive rather than evidential" and as a phenomenological analogue rather than an argument. [Resolved]
- **Dennett "cassette theory" slightly oversimplified**: Changed from "instantaneous memory insertions" to "confabulated or instantaneously composed at awakening," and reattributed as "associated with" Dennett rather than directly stated by him. [Resolved]

### Low Issues
- Sebastián (2014) cited for DLPFC deactivation—this is a philosophical paper that discusses the finding rather than a primary neuroscience source. Retained because it's being used as a review/argument source, not a primary discovery claim. [Deferred—acceptable as-is]

### Counterarguments Considered
- **Eliminative materialist**: Different neural configurations producing different information processing doesn't require a non-physical consciousness. The article appropriately frames its claims as "creating difficulty for identity theories" rather than proving dualism.
- **Functionalist/Dennettian**: Experience during dreams is what neural processing does, not evidence of an independent agent. The article's "What Dreams Cannot Yet Prove" section addresses this honestly.
- **Empiricist**: Small sample sizes, selected populations, and unreplicated findings limit the strength of causal claims. Addressed by softening language around Konkoly 2026.
- **Buddhist**: "Persistence" of consciousness across states may be narrative construction by waking cognition. Noted as philosophical framework difference, not an article flaw.
