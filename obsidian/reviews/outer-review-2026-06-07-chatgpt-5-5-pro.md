## Citation and literature audit

### Core recent citations

The major live/recent citations are real and mostly accurately represented.

**COGITATE 2025** is correctly identified as the Nature adversarial collaboration, Nature volume 642, pages 133–142. The paper reports a preregistered, theory-neutral test of IIT and GNWT with 256 participants, fMRI, MEG, and iEEG, and concludes that results aligned with some predictions of both theories while substantially challenging key tenets of both. [Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-025-08888-1)

The article’s COGITATE framing is directionally right but somewhat compressed. Nature’s abstract states that IIT was challenged by lack of sustained synchronization within posterior cortex and that GNWT was challenged by lack of ignition at stimulus offset plus limited representation of some conscious dimensions in prefrontal cortex. That supports the article’s mixed-verdict framing. [topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness/)

However, the article’s sentence “IIT better predicted face orientation decoding, while GWT better predicted object category decoding” should be sharpened. The Nature paper reports category decoding in both posterior and prefrontal regions, with posterior decoding stronger and more sustained, and orientation decoding largely posterior, with Bayesian evidence against prefrontal orientation decoding in several analyses. It is not simply “IIT wins orientation, GWT wins category.” [Nature+1](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-025-08888-1)

The COGITATE section also says the study “put underdetermination to empirical test.” That is rhetorically neat but philosophically imprecise. COGITATE tested preregistered biological-implementation predictions of IIT and GNWT, while explicitly noting that each theory’s mathematical or computational core is harder to test directly and that theorists can revise biological implementations while retaining the core. That is evidence for Duhem-Quine-style auxiliary flexibility, not an empirical test of Duhem-Quine itself. [Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-025-08888-1)

**Negro 2024** is real and appropriately relevant. Negro’s paper argues against “experimental eliminativism” and proposes a Lakatosian framework using criteria such as prediction versus accommodation, structural relevance, and boldness; it also treats theory appraisal as diachronic and dependent on background beliefs. The target article captures that broad lesson well. [OUP Academic](https://academic.oup.com/nc/article/2024/1/niae012/7630443)

The target article’s “after COGITATE, both IIT and GWT remained progressive” is plausible but should be qualified. Negro’s paper was published before the final Nature article and discusses the adversarial collaboration in terms of the available test structure and preprint context; the more precise claim is that a Negro-style Lakatosian analysis would not treat the mixed COGITATE result as an eliminative refutation, provided subsequent revisions generate new empirical content rather than merely accommodate failure. [OUP Academic+1](https://academic.oup.com/nc/article/2024/1/niae012/7630443)

**Robert Lawrence Kuhn 2024** is real and correctly dated. The cited paper appears in Progress in Biophysics and Molecular Biology, volume 190, pages 28–169, with DOI 10.1016/j.pbiomolbio.2023.12.003. [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0079610723001128)

## Where Duhem-Quine is over-applied

**1. “No experiment can adjudicate” is too strong unless restricted to the metaphysical gap.** Duhem-Quine says failed predictions do not logically dictate which auxiliary to abandon. It does not imply that experiments cannot rationally discriminate among theories. COGITATE itself produced graded constraints: posterior orientation decoding, limited PFC representation, absence of GNWT-predicted PFC onset-offset ignition, and absence of IIT-predicted sustained posterior synchronization all matter evidentially. [topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness/)

**2. “Frameworks determine what counts as evidence” risks over-Kuhnian relativism.** The article’s stronger formulation is useful as a warning, but COGITATE is a counterexample to total framework capture: proponents and neutral researchers agreed in advance on predictions, interpretation, and pass/fail criteria, with data collection and analysis structured to reduce confirmation bias. Frameworks shape evidential relevance, but they do not wholly determine it. [topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness/)

**3. The phenomenal concepts strategy is too quickly labeled a Duhem-Quine auxiliary move.** It is fair to ask whether PCS is progressive or ad hoc, but PCS is primarily a philosophical account of why epistemic/conceptual gaps arise under physicalism, not obviously a post-failure experimental patch. Papineau and Balog explicitly treat phenomenal concepts as a physicalist explanation of why dualism feels compelling or why epistemic gaps need not imply nonphysical properties. [topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness/)

**4. The article sometimes lets the hard problem absorb too much empirical detail.** COGITATE cannot solve the hard problem, but it can still constrain which neural architectures plausibly support conscious contents, maintenance, and report-independent decoding. The article should make clear that empirical underdetermination at the metaphysical level is compatible with meaningful empirical discrimination at the neural-implementation level. [Nature+1](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-025-08888-1)

## Where Duhem-Quine is under-applied

**1. The Map’s own hard core needs the same auxiliary-belt analysis.** The article admits that underdetermination cuts both ways, but it does not fully run the symmetry test. If the Map predicts quantum-sensitive decision-point signatures and those are not found, the Map could revise measurement assumptions, amplification assumptions, neural-site assumptions, or “minimality” assumptions. That is precisely a Duhem-Quine auxiliary-belt problem, and the article should say which revisions would be progressive and which would be degenerating. [topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness/)

**2. First-person evidence is not only a problem for physicalism.** The article rightly notes that first-person reports are theory-laden, but that also threatens dualist, phenomenological, contemplative, and introspection-heavy methods. If first-person data can gain evidential standing only through disciplined calibration, then dualist-friendly appeals to phenomenal authority need the same methodological burden as physicalist NCC studies. [topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness/)

**3. Unconceived alternatives threaten the Map’s categories too.** Stanford-style unconceived alternatives do not merely weaken IIT, GNWT, HOT, and physicalism. They also weaken the apparent exhaustiveness of the Map’s physicalism-versus-dualism framing. Neutral monism, Russellian monism, panpsychism, illusionism, attention-schema theories, predictive-processing approaches, and future hybrid models may not sit neatly inside the current contrast. The article gestures at unconceived alternatives but should explicitly apply that risk to its own taxonomy. [topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness/)

**4. Lakatosian progress requires more than survival.** A research programme is not progressive merely because it can absorb anomalies. Negro’s criteria emphasize whether predictions are novel rather than accommodated, structurally relevant rather than peripheral, and bold rather than low-risk. The article should apply those criteria to the Map’s own quantum-interaction and bidirectional-causation commitments, not only to IIT/GNWT and physicalism. [OUP Academic+1](https://academic.oup.com/nc/article/2024/1/niae012/7630443)

## Missed or underdeveloped objections

**Bayesian/model-comparison objection.** Underdetermination does not eliminate probabilistic confirmation. COGITATE itself uses Bayesian tests in specific analyses and calls for a quantitative framework for systematic theory testing and building. A physicalist or methodological naturalist can concede Duhem-Quine holism while arguing that evidence still shifts posterior probabilities among models. [Nature+1](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-025-08888-1)

**No-report/task-confound objection.** The article says every NCC study presupposes first-person report as irreducible data. That is too broad. COGITATE explicitly designed its paradigm to minimize task and report confounds while isolating neural activity related to consciousness. Reports and behavioral criteria still matter somewhere in the calibration chain, but “every NCC study presupposes first-person report as irreducible data” should be weakened. [topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness/)

**Post-COGITATE GNWT reply.** The article’s COGITATE section should engage the October 2025 Naccache/Dehaene/Changeux/Sergent/Farisco/Wang commentary, which argues that several Nature analyses validate key GNWT predictions and that future adversarial tests should focus on central predictions of formal neuronal models. Whether one accepts that reply or not, it is a directly relevant post-publication counterargument. [OUP Academic](https://academic.oup.com/nc/article/2025/1/niaf037/8280147)

**Heterophenomenology and illusionist/report-first approaches.** The article discusses first-person data but does not include the strongest deflationary response: treat reports as third-person behavioral data to be modeled without granting irreducible epistemic authority to experience. That objection does not defeat the Map’s view, but it is one of the obvious external challenges and should appear in the target article, not only in surrounding pages.

**Independence of convergence arguments.** The article says conceivability, knowledge, Nagel, and Kripke-style arguments independently converge on the non-entailment of experience by physical description. That is plausible, but it needs a dependency warning: many of those arguments may share modal-intuitive or phenomenal-conceptual premises, so their convergence may be less independent than the article suggests. [topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness/)

## Tenet-protective bracketing

The article is unusually honest for a worldview-driven project: it explicitly says the Map’s nonreduction commitment is not compelled by evidence and that neither physicalism nor dualism can claim evidential supremacy merely by pointing to the same data. [topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness/)

Still, the bracketing is not perfectly even. The “explanatory gap as auxiliary assumption” section frames physicalism as presupposing closability while dualism “denies this presupposition.” That should be symmetrized: physicalists may treat the gap as epistemic, conceptual, or temporary; dualists may treat it as ontological, principled, or permanent. Both are auxiliary commitments relative to shared empirical data. [topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness/)

The quantum-interface language is the most tenet-protective part of the article. “Quantum-sensitive decision points may show statistical signatures inconsistent with purely physical noise” is a distinctive prediction, but the article does not explain what would count as a failed test, what alternative physical explanations would need to be ruled out, or when repeated null results would make the programme degenerating. The link to the falsification roadmap helps, but this article itself should contain one sentence stating that Duhem-Quine does not license indefinite quantum retreat. [topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness/)
