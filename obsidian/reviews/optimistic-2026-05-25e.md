## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)

The cohort takes the explanatory gap seriously in four distinct settings without letting any one of them overreach. `aesthetics-and-consciousness.md` makes the sharpest Chalmersian move on the Map: the *convergence argument* (§"The Convergence Argument") observes that the knowledge argument, zombie conceivability, and the explanatory gap each "target one dimension of consciousness" and the physicalist can answer each "in turn with specialised defences" — but aesthetic experience bundles qualitative, evaluative, holistic, temporal, and meaning-laden features into a single phenomenon whose *mutual constitution* is "what most demands explanation." That is the hard problem reframed as a compounding problem, and it is earned rather than asserted. Chalmers would also approve that `animal-consciousness.md` cites *his own* refutation lineage indirectly (the COGITATE null against IIT and GNW) rather than leaning on a consciousness theory the field has not confirmed.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)

The cohort keeps quantum claims appropriately quarantined. `aesthetics-and-consciousness.md` routes its Minimal-Quantum-Interaction paragraph through Stapp's attention model but explicitly marks the connection "speculative" and asserts "the phenomenological arguments stand independently" — the quantum layer is offered as optional reinforcement, never as load-bearing. `consciousness-and-counterfactual-reasoning.md` does the same with the agent-causal "evaluate quantum possibilities through counterfactual simulation" model, tagging it "a speculative but coherent model." Stapp would value that neither article smuggles the interface mechanism in as established; the restraint is what makes the speculative offer respectable.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)

This is a Nagelian cohort by construction. `commensurability-void.md` is the centrepiece: it reads Nagel's bat not as an access problem but as a *commensurability* problem — "the bat's sonar-based phenomenal world may be organized along dimensions we cannot even conceive" — and then shows the fracture "begins earlier on the continuum than the bat case suggests" via the within-species aphantasia data point (the imagery void). That move — grounding a cross-architecture claim in a *conspecific* commensurability failure so the broad claim "is not extrapolation from a marginal case" — is phenomenology done with its own weakest link reinforced first. `consciousness-and-counterfactual-reasoning.md` earns its Nagel citation too: "there is something it is like to imagine a world where you chose differently — a felt quality... that is neither perception nor memory nor hallucination."

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)

`animal-consciousness.md` §"Process Philosophy Perspective" is the cleanest Whiteheadian passage in the cohort, and — critically — it is calibrated correctly. It states that independent evolution of consciousness across vertebrates, cephalopods, and arthropods "fits" process philosophy and reframes the question as "not whether animals experience, but what *kind* their integration affords." This is process-philosophical compatibility offered as *coherence*, not as evidence. The article never uses Whitehead-friendliness to lift any boundary organism up the scale. `aesthetics-and-consciousness.md` likewise lists process-philosophy among its concepts and treats aesthetic experience as "a dimension of experience as such" — experience-laden without crude substance dualism. Whitehead would be at home here; the Map has earned his approval without spending it on an upgrade.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)

`consciousness-and-counterfactual-reasoning.md` is the cohort's agency anchor. Its deliberation argument — "you construct counterfactual scenarios for each... and then select based on the comparison" such that "the phenomenal experience of imagining alternatives shapes the physical outcome" — is precisely the libertarian's picture of how an agent is the source of action. Kane would especially value the §"Counterfactual Reasoning as Evidence for Bidirectional Interaction" framing that counterfactual comparison "may be the *mechanism* by which consciousness exercises causal influence," and that the article ties this to Minimal Quantum Interaction (consciousness "bias which quantum possibility becomes actual") rather than to wholesale neural restructuring — the smallest-intervention picture libertarian indeterminism needs.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)

`commensurability-void.md` is the strongest mysterian exhibit on the Map this cycle. It explicitly frames incommensurability as "a concrete expression of mysterianism — not just that we cannot *explain* consciousness, but that we cannot even *compare* it across architectures," and its §"The Phenomenology of Conceptual Vertigo" gives the cognitive-closure intuition a first-person texture ("groundlessness — the comparison cannot even get started because there is nowhere to stand"). McGinn would also approve `animal-consciousness.md`'s §"What Would Challenge This View?" honest concession that conditions (3)–(5) "are inaccessible by the framework's own commitments, not by an instrumentation gap future technology will close" and that "the framework's net falsifiability is genuinely modest." Naming one's own cognitive horizon is mysterian intellectual humility done right.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)

This persona's verdict is load-bearing for the cohort because `animal-consciousness.md` is squarely tenet-load-bearing territory, and the verdict is strongly positive — the article is a worked exhibit of tenet-as-evidence-upgrade being praise-worthily *not done*.

Specifics worth naming:

- **Restraint about minimal organisms, exactly as the discipline requires.** The lede's second paragraph states the case structure on the five-tier scale without slippage: "The case for mammals and birds is strongly supported; for cephalopods, decapods, and insects it is a realistic possibility but contested; boundary organisms — nematodes, Hydra, slime molds — are weaker still and treated in `consciousness-in-simple-organisms`." Nematodes/Hydra/slime molds are kept *below* realistic possibility — the precise calibration the skill's Hardline-Empiricist brief asks for. The dualist defeater-removal is named as removing "a metaphysical defeater" that "does not supply positive evidence for any particular species."

- **The named pattern is used verbatim and repeatedly.** "Tenet-coherent — markers identify *correlates*... A methodologically strong framework does not, on its own, settle species-level verdicts" (§"The Marker Method"); "Tenet-coherent but does not, on its own, upgrade evidential status" (UAL, §"Minimal Neural Requirements"); "Coherence runs one direction only — the observation does not raise evidential status above realistic possibility, contested" (§"Multiple Independent Origins"). The §"Why the Inference Is Stronger... Than for AI" passage explicitly separates registers: "Their alignment raises coherence rather than evidential status."

- **The structural opportunity to slide a boundary case up the scale was present and explicitly declined.** This is the strongest single thing to name. The §"How a Sophisticated Rival Reads the Same Evidence" section grants the deflationary functionalist that it "is, in short, compatible with almost everything the article's evidence directly establishes," that "the standing agnostic challenge applies to the Map's attribution exactly as it applies to the deflationary denial," and that "verifying the *presence* of phenomenal experience and verifying its *absence* are symmetric epistemic problems." The article then locates the disagreement "not over any catalogued finding but over the prior." That is the discipline's gold standard: the Map's own attribution is held to the same evidential bar as its rival's denial, and the article refuses to count the marker cluster, UAL, valence behaviour, and convergent evolution as four independent confirmations (explicitly invoking the common-cause null).

- **Honest framework-stage calibration without over-claiming.** The COGITATE-null handling (§"Why the Inference Is Stronger...") concedes that any inference routed through IIT-Φ or Global Neuronal Workspace "inherits that weakened support" — the article declines to launder a consciousness theory the field's own adversarial collaboration failed to confirm.

The Process Philosopher and the Hardline Empiricist converge here rather than conflict: Whitehead-friendliness is present (the process-philosophy section) and the evidential discipline is intact (no boundary organism rides the tenet up the scale). Convergence of the two personas on the same article is the signature of an honestly resolved tenet-load-bearing piece. **No calibration flag for animal-consciousness.**

One *minor* calibration note, on a different article and self-flagged in its own text: `consciousness-and-counterfactual-reasoning.md` rests on the claim that *phenomenal* grasp of dual status is what distinguishes conscious from computational counterfactual reasoning. The article's §"Why Physicalism Struggles" already installs the correct hedge — it explicitly concedes that LLMs "generate counterfactual scenarios fluently... apparently without phenomenal consciousness," that this is "a genuine challenge case where the Map's predictions are unclear," and that the arguments should be read "as suggesting that conscious counterfactual reasoning has a character formal computation has not been shown to share — not as establishing that it could not in principle be matched." Because the article installs its own defeater this cleanly, this is **not** a calibration flag requiring a refine-draft; it is a model of how to make a necessity-flavoured argument while disowning the necessity claim. The Hardline Empiricist's only residual note is that the Relation-to-Site-Perspective "Dualism" paragraph ("If this dual-status awareness is irreducible, it provides a distinctive argument for dualism") reads slightly more confidently in isolation than the §"Why Physicalism Struggles" hedge — a candidate for a one-clause back-reference rather than a rewrite (see Cross-Linking Suggestions).
