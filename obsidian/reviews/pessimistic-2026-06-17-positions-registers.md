## Critiques by Philosopher

### The Eliminative Materialist (Churchland)
P-A1's "convergent anchors" — phenomenology of effort, the argument from reason, clinical neuroplasticity, cross-cultural substance-causal intuitions — are *exactly* the folk-psychological constructs eliminativism predicts will not survive a mature neuroscience. "The phenomenology of effort and willing" is a report generated by the same system whose causal status is in question (the register concedes this in P-A4, but then still lists the phenomenology as an anchor for P-A1). Cross-cultural convergence on substance-causal persons is evidence about *human cognitive architecture's bias toward intuitive dualism*, not about persons being substances — and the corpus itself has an `intuitive-dualism` concept page making precisely that debunking point. Listing it as a *confirming* anchor for P-A1 while elsewhere treating it as a *bias to be explained away* is in tension. (Flagged below as a counterargument to address, not a defect — but it is the sharpest available objection.)

### The Hard-Nosed Physicalist (Dennett)
P-A2's whole load rests on a *qualitative* distinction — "active choosing... differs in kind from passive reception of a random outcome." Dennett's standing reply is that this felt difference-in-kind is itself a user-illusion; the register's own P-A5 concedes that compatibilism can secure the same deliberative phenomenology, praise/blame, and character arc. So P-A2's "differs in kind" is doing metaphysical work that P-A5 admits the phenomenology cannot underwrite. The register holds both — fine, they are about different things — but a hostile reader will press: if compatibilism reproduces every *experienced* feature (P-A5), what observable remainder does "differs in kind" (P-A2) actually name?

### The Quantum Skeptic (Tegmark)
The register handles Tegmark unusually well: P-Q1 explicitly concedes the decoherence-timescale objection and claims only to *sidestep* it by acting post-decoherence. The honest problem it then walks into is its own: P-Q3 + P-Q10 concede there is no worked toy model and no answer to *what* is selected once outcomes are Born-fixed. Tegmark's deeper point survives the relocation: "post-decoherence selection" with Born statistics preserved exactly (P-Q2) and no toy model (P-Q10) is not yet a physics claim — it is a placeholder with a name. The register says this itself (the "mechanism debt"), which is to its credit, but a skeptic will note the preference for post-decoherence selection (P-Q1, "moderate") is a preference among *equally-unmodelled* options.

### The Many-Worlds Defender (Deutsch)
P-A2's "Bears on Tenet 4" note is the exposed flank. It states the dependency between No-MWI and agent-substance subjecthood is **reciprocal**: No-MWI inherits its indexical force from the P-A2 subject, *and* No-MWI does identity work for agent causation by supplying counterfactual exclusion. A reciprocal dependency between a tenet and a position that depends on that tenet's cluster is exactly the circle a Deutsch-style critic wants: "you reject MWI because you presuppose a thick indexical subject, and you justify the thick subject partly because MWI is false." Each may be independently motivated, but the register asserts the mutual support *as support*, which risks counting one argument twice. See Critical Issue 2.

### The Empiricist (Popper's ghost)
P-Q9 ("self-concealing by construction") is the position most exposed to the unfalsifiability charge, and the register has clearly been worked over to defend it (the 2026-06-05 calibration restricting self-concealment to the *aggregate-statistics channel* and naming the falsifiable residue P-Q5/P-Q6 + roadmap). That defense is good. The residual Popperian worry: the *named* falsifiable residue (microtubule coherence-time results, Donadi-style mechanism tests) is falsifiable only for the *demoted* mechanisms (P-Q4 Stapp-Zeno, P-Q5 Orch-OR). The *preferred* mechanism (P-Q1 post-decoherence selection) inherits no such test — its only "Would shift if" conditions are internal (a better pre-decoherence calc, or the move proving to need a fatal structure). So the framework's preferred horse is the one with the least empirical exposure. Worth naming.

### The Buddhist Philosopher (Nagarjuna)
P-A2 commits to "the agent... as a persisting subject," an "uncaused cause," "immanent (agent-as-substance) causation." This is the strongest reification in either register — a permanent, self-identical, causally-originating self. The Map has a `buddhism-and-dualism` concept page, so the no-self objection is known to the corpus, but neither register's agency positions cite the engagement. P-A4's "verification limit" actually gives Nagarjuna ammunition: if the faculty that reports the self is the same faculty whose status is in question, the no-self reading (the subject is a construction the reporting generates) is at least as available as the substance reading.

## Critical Issues

### Issue 1: P-A1 lists cross-cultural / intuitive convergence as a confirming anchor while the corpus treats intuitive dualism as a bias to debunk
- **File**: `positions/agency-and-will.md` (P-A1, line 46)
- **Location**: "cross-cultural convergence on substance-causal accounts of persons" listed among the convergent anchors
- **Problem**: The same convergence is, on the Map's own `intuitive-dualism` concept page, framed as an evolved cognitive bias — i.e., a *common-cause* explanation that screens off the inference from "everyone believes it" to "it is true." This is the same common-cause discipline the `voids-as-evidence` register applies to the void catalogue ("framework-internal coherence under the common-cause null, not independent confirmation"). P-A1 does not apply that discipline to its own cross-cultural anchor.
- **Severity**: Medium
- **Recommendation**: `refine-draft` pass adding one clause to P-A1's Asserts noting the cross-cultural anchor must be discounted for the intuitive-dualism common-cause null (cite `concepts/intuitive-dualism`), mirroring the `voids-as-evidence` register's treatment — so the anchor is counted at its honest (debiased) evidential weight, not at face value.

### Issue 2: P-A2 ↔ Tenet 4 reciprocal dependency risks double-counting one argument as mutual support
- **File**: `positions/agency-and-will.md` (P-A2, "Bears on" note, line 60)
- **Location**: "The dependency is reciprocal: No-MWI in turn does identity work for agent causation..."
- **Problem**: The note asserts (a) No-MWI's indexical force *derives from* the P-A2 thick subject, and (b) No-MWI *supplies* the counterfactual exclusion agent causation needs. If both directions are presented as *support*, the two positions prop each other up with what may be a single underlying commitment to a thick indexical subject. Mutual entailment is fine; mutual *evidential support* between a position and the tenet-cluster it depends on is a circularity smell. The register already flags A1's confidence as capped by A4; this loop is not similarly flagged.
- **Severity**: Medium
- **Recommendation**: `positions-evolve` audit note (or refine pass) explicitly distinguishing *logical interdependence* (legitimate: the two claims are coherent and co-vary) from *evidential bootstrapping* (illegitimate: neither can be cited as independent support for the other). State that the thick-subject commitment is one input, motivated on its own grounds, from which both No-MWI's indexical objection and P-A2's sourcehood follow — so the relationship is "common root," not "mutual confirmation."

### Issue 3: The preferred mechanism (P-Q1) carries the least empirical exposure; the falsifiable residue attaches to demoted mechanisms
- **File**: `positions/quantum-interface.md` (P-Q1 vs P-Q9)
- **Location**: P-Q9 names the falsifiable residue as P-Q5/P-Q6 (Orch-OR / Diósi-Penrose) mechanism-level tests; P-Q1's "Would shift if" conditions are all internal/comparative
- **Problem**: P-Q9 defends against the unfalsifiability charge by pointing at mechanism-level tests that did/can bite — but those tests bite the *demoted* candidates (P-Q4, P-Q5). The Map's *preferred* mechanism (P-Q1 post-decoherence selection) has no listed empirical test, only internal "Would shift if" conditions. A Popperian reads this as: the framework's empirical exposure is concentrated on the options it has already down-ranked, while its working preference floats free.
- **Severity**: Low (this is honest given P-Q10's no-toy-model admission, but the asymmetry should be named so P-Q9's falsifiability defense is not read as covering P-Q1)
- **Recommendation**: One clause in P-Q9 or P-Q1 acknowledging that the named falsifiable residue currently attaches to the demoted mechanism candidates, and that the preferred post-decoherence route's empirical exposure awaits the toy model tracked in P-Q10.

## Counterarguments to Address

### "Convergent anchors" for agent causation may share a common cause (cognitive bias)
- **Current content says** (P-A1): the case rests on convergent anchors, "no one of which is privileged."
- **A critic would argue**: convergence is only evidence if the anchors are *independent*. Phenomenology-of-effort and cross-cultural substance-intuitions plausibly share a common cause (the evolved intuitive-dualism architecture), so they are not independent confirmations — the same double-counting error the `voids-as-evidence` register was built to avoid.
- **Suggested response**: state explicitly which anchors are independent of the intuitive-dualism common cause (neural willed-vs-instructed data, the argument from reason) and which must be discounted for it (phenomenology, cross-cultural intuition); rest the convergence claim on the genuinely independent subset.

### Epiphenomenalism proximity (P-Q3) is conceded but not yet positively answered
- **Current content says** (P-Q3): the position "sits genuinely close to epiphenomenalism," the per-trial-vs-ensemble move is a "*candidate* resolution," a "deeper positive engagement remains pending."
- **A critic would argue**: a register of "positions the Map holds" that admits its central mechanism position is close to the view that would *deny consciousness any causal work* is recording an open problem as a held position. Until the candidate resolution is worked, P-Q1's "moderate" and P-A1/P-A2's agency commitments are running ahead of the mechanism that would license them.
- **Suggested response**: this is already partly handled by the mechanism-debt-inherited convention; ensure the downstream applied articles actually read no more confident than P-Q3 (a corpus-sweep, not a register edit). The register's honesty here is a strength; the risk is downstream leakage.
