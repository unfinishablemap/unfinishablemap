## Background: The Misframe

Prior to the 2026-04-28 refinement of [[concepts/many-worlds]], many voids articles staged the No-Many-Worlds tenet by claiming MWI cannot accommodate the felt singularity of indexical experience—e.g., "Many-worlds cannot explain why *this* ownership feels singular," "the stamp of realness is singular in a way that equal-branch ontology cannot accommodate," "many-worlds cannot identify the determinate self."

After the refinement, the canonical position is sharper: [[many-worlds#MWI's Branch-Relative Indexicality Reply|Saunders/Wallace branch-relative indexicality (Saunders 2010; Wallace 2012)]] *can* accommodate felt singularity branch-locally—each branch's observer truly says "I am here, having *this* outcome." Claims of the form "experience is *this one*, not scattered across branches" therefore do not by themselves refute MWI. The Map's stronger commitment is that indexicality is genuinely one-branch-only rather than locally-articulated within a globally-branching structure—which withholds the cross-branch personal-identity relation that MWI's Deutsch-Wallace Born-rule recovery requires.
