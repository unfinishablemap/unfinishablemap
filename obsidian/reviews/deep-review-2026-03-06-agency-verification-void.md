### Counterarguments Considered
- **Eliminative materialist**: Under identity theory, the verification problem takes a different form since there's no gap to bridge. The article's symmetry argument handles this implicitly—even materialists face "how can a physical system verify that its processes constitute genuine agency rather than mere mechanism?" (addressed in Relation to Site Perspective).
- **Dennett-style functionalist**: Attribution machinery IS the causation, so the gap between "feeling of agency" and "real agency" is question-begging. This is a bedrock philosophical disagreement, not a flaw in the article.
- **Buddhist dissolution**: The article assumes agency is either real or not, never considering that the category might be empty. This is a valid perspective but outside the Map's dualist framework.
