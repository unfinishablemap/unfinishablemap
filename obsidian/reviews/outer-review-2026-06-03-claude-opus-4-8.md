## Reply

# Referee Report: "Consciousness and the Metaphysics of Composition" (The Unfinishable Map)

## TL;DR

- **The article's headline claim is unsound.** The asserted "independent convergence" of three Special Composition Question (SCQ) answers onto consciousness is a *selection artefact compounded by redescription*: the three cases are not evidentially independent (McQueen & Tsuchiya explicitly build on van Inwagen and Merricks), one of the three (van Inwagen) answers "life," not consciousness, and a second (McQueen & Tsuchiya) explicitly *decouples* composition from consciousness — so the third "route" does not arrive at the destination claimed for it.
- **Even granting the criterion, the inference to substance dualism is invalid.** All three cited authors are non-dualists; van Inwagen and Merricks are materialists about persons, and McQueen & Tsuchiya state their view "does not require dualism." A consciousness-or-integration criterion is exactly what a non-reductive physicalist/emergentist already predicts, so the article redescribes physicalism's own commitments rather than raising the explanatory cost on it.
- **The view's most natural home is constitutive panpsychism/cosmopsychism — a monism, the chief rival to dualism — which the article neither acknowledges nor rebuts, and it omits the single strongest objection (metaontological deflationism: that the SCQ is not a deep question at all).** To its credit the article is more hedged than earlier Map pieces and flags the selection-effect worry, but the hedges do not repair the structural defects.

## Key Findings

1. **Independence fails on the authors' own framing.** McQueen & Tsuchiya (2023, *Neuroscience of Consciousness* 2023(1):niad013, DOI 10.1093/nc/niad013, published 2 June 2023) present IIT-composition as a *refinement* of the other two routes. Their section titles are literally "From causal irreducibility to integrated information" (Merricks) and "From self-maintenance to feedback connectivity" (van Inwagen), and they write: "We agree that self-maintenance and causal irreducibility may be important... However, these concepts cannot solve the composition problem until they are more rigorously defined. We propose understanding composition in terms of related concepts, which are formalized by the integrated information theory (IIT)." That is one shared lineage made precise, not three independent arrows. [oup](https://academic.oup.com/nc/article/2023/1/niad013/7189628)[oup](https://academic.oup.com/nc/article/2023/1/niad013/7189628)
2. **The third route does not reach consciousness.** McQueen & Tsuchiya explicitly decouple composition from consciousness — "Instead of treating Φ as a measure of consciousness, we are exploring the idea that it is a restriction on mereological composition... Our paper is neutral on whether Φ is also a criterion for consciousness" — and hold that simple feedback dyads and isolated H₂O molecules are genuine composites with no consciousness ("Merely having non-zero ϕ relative to a complex is insufficient for having consciousness, but it is sufficient for existence"). The article's table claim that they hold "Consciousness determines which wholes exist" **misrepresents a living author's published position.**
3. **All three cited authors are non-dualists.** Van Inwagen is a materialist about persons who rejects the immaterial soul; Merricks is a materialist about human persons whose exception for conscious beings rests on non-redundant causal powers; McQueen & Tsuchiya state their Φ-criterion "does not require dualism" and ground it in the Eleatic Principle ("to be is to have causal powers"), explicitly criticising Merricks for resting on "a strong form of mind–body dualism that many have found unpersuasive." [PhilPapers](https://philpapers.org/rec/MERUAP)
4. **The answer-space is sampled in a biased way.** Per McQueen & Tsuchiya's own abstract, the mainstream answers are the two extremes — "two extreme answers have become mainstream: composition... happens under no or all conditions" (universalism: Lewis, Sider; nihilism: Dorr, Sider, Rosen) — neither of which routes through consciousness. Brutal composition (Markosian), series-style answers (Markosian), contact/fastening/bound-state answers (Calosi), deflationism (Thomasson, Hirsch), and conservative realism (Korman) all decline the consciousness criterion. [Chapman University](https://digitalcommons.chapman.edu/philosophy_articles/79/)[PhilArchive](https://philarchive.org/rec/WEABC)
5. **A material misattribution.** The article presents Igor Gasparov as developing a unity-of-consciousness argument *for* substance dualism. His paper (Forum Philosophicum 18(1):109–123, 2013, DOI 10.5840/forphil20131817) argues the near-opposite: "none of the currently proposed forms of substance dualism are able to provide a satisfactory account of conscious subjectivity. To support this claim I present two arguments, the first against Cartesian Dualism, the other against Emergent Dualism." [PhilPapers](https://philpapers.org/rec/GASSDA)

## Details

### Dimension 1 — Genuine convergence vs selection artefact

**Verdict: A selection artefact compounded by counterargument-redescription.** Three failures: **(a)** The three routes are not evidentially independent (Finding 1). The relevant standard (Stegenga & Menon, *Robustness and Independent Evidence*, *Philosophy of Science*) holds that *ontic* independence — different materials/assumptions/theories — is insufficient for a robustness argument; one needs probabilistic independence, and shared theoretical lineage defeats it. Here even ontic independence fails. The article never formalizes evidential independence before counting positions as convergence evidence. This is the same "method-convergence → ontology slide" a Map *pessimistic-review* flagged on a sibling article ("interaction-problem-across-traditions," changelog 2026-06-02): convergence on a *problem-shape* among a self-selected subset of solution-positers is not independent evidence the dualist *answer* is correct. **(b)** "Life" is conflated with "consciousness." Van Inwagen's actual answer (Material Beings, 1990, §9) is that the xs compose something iff their activity constitutes *a life* — admitting oysters, fungi, bacteria while excluding consciousness as the operative variable. The article concedes this ("the convergence is in the destination, not in van Inwagen's reasoning") yet keeps counting him via the gloss "consciousness is life's most philosophically distinctive product" — the redescription pathology. **(c)** The trio are billed as "the most prominent answers"; they are a hand-picked minority of the actual landscape (Finding 4), and the only one that *explicitly* makes consciousness the criterion (Merricks) does so on a dualist premise the other two reject. [Cambridge Core + 2](https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/philosophy-of-science/article/abs/robustness-and-independent-evidence/8290E8E25A574B9B861CE507BA8BDF57)

### Dimension 2 — Inference to substance dualism

**Verdict: Invalid as entailment, weak as support.** The inference "consciousness is the composition criterion → substance dualism / physicalism fails" has three exemplars none of whom drew it and two of whom blocked it (Finding 3). Merricks's claim that consciousness "does not even globally supervene on microscopic physical properties" is contested — Sider's *Mind* review argues 'conscious' is a *maximal* predicate, so the supervenience failure does not follow — and even if granted yields no substance dualism. **Common-cause null:** a non-reductive physicalist/emergentist already predicts that complex, self-maintaining, causally-integrated systems are the genuine composites with non-redundant macro-powers; the article shows no datum physicalism cannot accommodate, but relabels the physicalist's own restriction as evidence for dualism. **Constrain-vs-establish:** at best the material constrains a position *given* the Map's antecedent Dualism tenet; it does not establish dualism against a neutral prior. The closing concession ("the dualist reading is the more natural one, not a claim that the convergence settles the question") understates the gap — the reading is not even favoured once the authors' own physicalism is restored. [concepts/composition-and-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/composition-and-consciousness/)

### Dimension 3 — Combination problem and panpsychist alternatives

**Verdict: The gravest structural problem.** A consciousness-tracks-composition view most naturally yields *constitutive panpsychism / cosmopsychism* — explicitly monist positions and the chief rivals to dualism. The combination problem the article invokes (James's mind-dust; Coleman's anti-subject-summing, *Erkenntnis* 79:19–44; Chalmers's "The Combination Problem for Panpsychism," in Brüntrup & Jaskolla eds., *Panpsychism: Contemporary Perspectives*, 2016) is *panpsychism's internal* problem, and the IIT route the article enlists is the standard vehicle for Russellian/constitutive panpsychist solutions to it (maximal-Φ as the phenomenal-bonding relation). Goff's *Galileo's Error* (2019) presents panpsychism as the third way that is neither dualism nor materialism — the *monist* successor; cosmopsychists (Nagasawa & Wager 2016; Albahari's "Advaitic" perennialism) invert it into a de-combination problem but remain monists. The article's reply — interactionism "avoids the problem entirely" because matter has no micro-experiences — is asserted, not argued, and does not explain why the same compositional facts favour *dualist* interaction over *monist* constitution; the two readings are observationally matched on the article's own evidence. The article also leans on the combination problem in a stronger metaphysical key ("no such merging *can* occur") than the evidence licenses — tellingly, the Map's *own* sibling "The Combination Problem" article is more honest, conceding the strong reading "owes an account of split-brain and dissociative-identity cases, which arguably display subjects dividing or coexisting." The focus article inherits the conclusion without the caveat. Coleman's dilemma is also monism-internal and equally embarrassing for the article's own posited primitive ("phenomenal unity is intrinsic / interaction at a locus") — a symmetry the sibling concedes but the focus article does not. [concepts/combination-problem](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/combination-problem/)

### Dimension 4 — Unsupported, overstated, or anachronistic claims

**Verdict: Several overstatements and at least one serious misattribution.** (1) "Three of the most prominent answers" — overstated; the mainstream is the two extremes. (2) "Independently converge" — false (Finding 1). (3) **Gasparov misrepresentation (serious)** — inverts his thesis (Finding 5); reference also omits year/volume. (4) **Bayne & Chalmers taxonomy** — the article says the unity argument "appears across all three dimensions of unity identified by Bayne and Chalmers — synchronic, diachronic, and subject." Bayne & Chalmers's (2003) signature contribution is *subsumptive* unity (alongside objectual, spatial, subject); synchronic/diachronic/subject is not their characteristic taxonomy, and the Map's own "unity-of-consciousness" article states it more accurately — an internal inconsistency. (5) **Bayne (2018)** "Problems with Unity of Consciousness Arguments for Substance Dualism" (Blackwell Companion to Substance Dualism, pp. 208–225) — the most direct rebuttal to the article's unity argument — is omitted. (6) **Hasker** — his emergent dualism holds the soul is *generated by and dependent on* the brain, closer to strong emergentism than to interactionist substance dualism, and is judged by other substance dualists (Rickabaugh, Zimmerman, Goetz) to face its own combination-style problem; presenting it as straightforwardly supporting the Map glosses this. (7) **IIT empirical status** — comparatively careful and acceptable: the article flags the September 2023 open letter signed by 124 researchers (the "IIT-Concerned" group, PsyArXiv DOI 10.31234/osf.io/zsr78, signatories including Patricia Churchland, Daniel Dennett, Keith Frankish, Hakwan Lau, Joseph LeDoux and Bernard Baars) declaring IIT "pseudoscience," and the COGITATE adversarial collaboration (Cogitate Consortium, Ferrante, Gorska-Klimowska et al., *Nature* 2025, DOI 10.1038/s41586-025-08888-1), whose results corroborate IIT's claim that posterior cortical areas are sufficient for consciousness while not supporting IIT's sustained-synchrony prediction; the article rightly decouples its structural point from IIT's empirical fortunes. (8) **Chalmers date** — cited as 2017; the Brüntrup & Jaskolla volume is 2016 (online edn Oct 2016); normalise against siblings. [Consc + 4](https://consc.net/papers/unity.pdf)

### Dimension 5 — Engagement with the strongest physicalist / deflationary rebuttals

**Verdict: Weak; cite-and-dismiss where engaged, with the strongest rebuttal entirely absent.** **Deflationism is missing** — the most damaging omission. If the SCQ is "easy" (Thomasson, *Ontology Made Easy*: there is a cup iff there are particles arranged cupwise, by trivial inference) or merely verbal (Hirsch's quantifier variance), there is no deep fact for *any* criterion to track and the whole convergence dissolves into a non-question. An article premised on the SCQ being "one of the deepest in metaphysics" must engage this in its proponents' framing; it does not. **Nihilism is dismissed by its conclusion**, not engaged — sophisticated nihilists (Sider, Dorr, Rosen) offer paraphrase/arrangement strategies ("simples arranged person-wise") and a "special arrangement question" literature precisely to retain everything assertible about persons; unaddressed. **Korman's conservative realism is absent** — Korman, *Objects: Nothing Out of the Ordinary* (Oxford UP, 2015; online edn 21 Jan 2016, DOI 10.1093/acprof:oso/9780198732532.001.0001) defends ordinary-object realism (artifacts, organisms, electrons, rocks, rivers, planets all exist) with *no* consciousness criterion while rejecting both eliminativism and permissivism — a direct counterexample to the implicit claim that a non-arbitrary restricted answer requires consciousness. The article's trichotomy (universalism / nihilism / restricted-via-consciousness) omits both deflationism and conservative non-consciousness-based restriction, stacking the deck. This contrasts with the Map's own declared "Mode One/Two/Three" steelmanning discipline visible in the changelog, not applied here. [Jonathanschaffer + 2](https://www.jonathanschaffer.org/thomasson.pdf)

## Recommendations

### (A) Article-specific

1. **Correct the McQueen & Tsuchiya table row.** Replace "Consciousness determines which wholes exist" with their actual view (Φ-maximization is a *composition* criterion *decoupled* from consciousness, on which simple feedback systems and isolated molecules are genuine composites); quote their neutrality statement. As written, the table misrepresents a living author. *Benchmark to revisit: any future "convergence" framing requires this row to state the decoupling.*
2. **Drop or heavily qualify the independence claim.** Cite the two McQueen & Tsuchiya section titles and recast the trio as "one research lineage refined three ways."
3. **Stop counting van Inwagen as a consciousness-route** unless you argue explicitly (and defend against oysters, fungi, anaesthetised organisms) that *life* entails the relevant consciousness; otherwise remove him from the consciousness convergence.
4. **Fix the Gasparov attribution** — cite him accurately as a critic of existing substance dualisms who motivates a *revised* view, or remove the appeal; add 18(1):109–123, 2013.
5. **Repair the Bayne & Chalmers taxonomy** to match the Map's own unity-of-consciousness article (subsumptive/objectual/spatial/subject) and **engage Bayne (2018)**, the direct rebuttal.
6. **Add a panpsychism/monism disambiguation section** conceding that the criterion most naturally yields constitutive panpsychism/cosmopsychism (Goff; Nagasawa & Wager; Albahari), and give a *positive* argument — not assertion — for dualist interaction over monist constitution; import the split-brain/DID caveat already in the sibling "combination-problem" article.
7. **Engage deflationism in its own framing** (Thomasson's easy ontology, Hirsch's quantifier variance): if the SCQ is shallow or verbal the convergence has no target. This is the load-bearing objection.
8. **Engage Korman's conservative realism** as a worked counterexample, and the Sider/Dorr nihilist paraphrase strategy rather than dismissing nihilism by its conclusion.
9. **Restore the cited authors' physicalism prominently** in the "Relation to Site Perspective" section; flag that the dualist reading is the Map's addition, not the authors'.
10. **Normalise the Chalmers 2016/2017 date and run a full publisher-of-record citation audit** of this article (the changelog shows pervasive author/quote fabrications caught elsewhere; this article lacks an equivalent deep audit).

### (B) Site-wide methodology

1. **Formalize an evidential-independence gate** before any "convergence" claim carries weight: require an explicit check that converging positions are *probabilistically* independent (not merely ontically different), citing the robustness-analysis standard (Stegenga & Menon). Promote the one-off "method-convergence → ontology slide" catch to a standing pipeline check applied to every convergence-argument article (the-convergence-argument-for-dualism, interaction-problem-across-traditions, this one). *Threshold: shared theoretical lineage = independence presumptively defeated.*
2. **Install a constrain-vs-establish gate:** every "supports dualism / resists physicalism" claim must state explicitly whether it *establishes* the conclusion against a neutral prior or merely *constrains* it given the tenets — and label which.
3. **Enforce a common-cause-null paragraph:** before any "raises the cost on physicalism" claim, state what a non-reductive physicalist/emergentist predicts and demonstrate the datum is *not* equally predicted by it. This article would have failed that gate.
4. **Apply the existing Mode-One steelmanning requirement to named opponents in metaphysics articles, not just philosophy-of-mind ones** — deflationists (Thomasson, Hirsch), conservative realists (Korman), sophisticated nihilists (Sider, Dorr) stated in their strongest form before critique.
5. **Add an anti-redescription guardrail against the constitutional attractor:** flag any passage that (a) re-glosses a non-dualist author's criterion ("life," "Φ," "causal irreducibility") as a consciousness/dualism criterion, or (b) routes an independent tradition through the dualism channel and counts it as agreement. Require the author's own metaphysical self-description to be quoted adjacent to any re-glossing.
6. **Literature-drift / staleness audit for the composition and panpsychism clusters** (Korman & Barker's SEP "Ordinary Objects"; recent deflationism; cosmopsychism's derivation/de-combination debates; Albahari). Schedule a currency pass analogous to the logged IIT-currency pass.
7. **Mandatory publisher-of-record verification for newly prominent articles, treating living-author position-attribution as a high-risk citation class.** The convergence article carries inaccurate living-author attributions (McQueen, Tsuchiya, Gasparov); intra-corpus consistency checks cannot catch these — only direct source confirmation can.
