## Executive Summary

This is one of the Map's most mature and internally coherent clusters — a single metaethical spine (value lives in phenomenal experience) cleanly distributed across a metaethics flagship, its necessity-claim concept page, an aesthetics test-case, an applied environmental-ethics article, a moral-phenomenology arc, and a deliberately steelmanned rival. The standout strength is **calibration discipline**: the recently-created `the-steelman-for-value-blind-selection` is a near-textbook execution of the Hardline-Empiricist register — it strengthens the rival rather than its own view, refuses to recruit ambiguous evidence (Berridge, active inference) for either side, and names the load-bearing premise it rejects. Opportunities are scarce because coverage is comprehensive; the one genuinely uncovered, article-sized, tenet-aligned subject is **the sublime / negative aesthetics**.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The cluster takes the explanatory gap and extends it correctly into an *evaluative* gap: "no equation captures why pain is bad" (`phenomenal-value-realism`). The move from "consciousness is irreducible" to "value inherits this irreducibility" is exactly the inference Chalmers' framework licenses, and the zombie-world-contains-no-value illustration ("the moral emptiness of the zombie world just *is* its experiential emptiness") is a clean application of conceivability reasoning to ethics.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
`phenomenal-value-realism`'s "Against Epiphenomenal Value" section ties value's causal efficacy to the type/token causation distinction — value adds nothing at the Born-rule type level but determines which token outcome actualises. This is the right place to put mental causation of value, and it keeps the minimal-interaction tenet honest rather than over-claiming.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
`phenomenology-of-moral-life` is Nagelian at its core: moral perception as gestalt ("wrongness organises what you see"), the pre-reflective strike preceding emotion, moral remainder persisting after the right tragic choice. It centers the first-person datum ("wrongness presents itself as a feature of the world, not as a private sentiment") while honestly flagging where the phenomenal and neural descriptions may pick out one process at two levels. Rawlette's book carries Nagel's own foreword, aptly cited.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The cluster avoids crude substance-dualist clumsiness: value is "built into what fundamentally exists" and `consciousness-value-connection`'s panpsychism section ("where there is value, there is consciousness") preserves mind's reality without reifying it as a separate substance floating free. Notably, the cluster does **not** cash this process-friendliness out as a tier-upgrade (see Birch below) — it stays on the right axis.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
The agency dimension of phenomenal value pluralism ("the felt authorship of choices" as an irreducible value dimension) and `phenomenology-of-moral-life`'s treatment of overriding self-interest as genuinely authored effort take agency seriously as intrinsically valuable, not merely instrumentally. The No-Many-Worlds tie ("reducing *this* suffering matters only if *this* outcome is the outcome") gives Kane's "ultimate sourcehood" intuition a metaphysical home.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
`phenomenology-of-moral-life` and `phenomenal-normativity-environmental-ethics` both route through the `normative-void`, which directly engages McGinn's cognitive-closure thesis (and his own premature exemption of morality). The "incommensurability / no algorithmic answer to value trade-offs / wise judgement" passages in `phenomenal-value-realism` show appropriate humility about what the framework can deliver.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch) — load-bearing for this review
This is where the cluster shines, and the verdict is strongly positive. `the-steelman-for-value-blind-selection` is a model of the discipline this persona exists to praise:

- **It steelmans the rival, not its own view.** It explicitly diagnoses the prior failure mode ("a 2026 review warned that the value-blind side... is at risk of being a strawman by attrition") and assembles the rival's *own* multi-disciplinary evidence (Schultz/Dayan/Montague RPE, Berridge unconscious liking, Cisek affordance competition, Joffily/Coricelli + Hesp active inference, Frankish illusionism) before stating the Map's reasons for declining.
- **Tenet-as-evidence-upgrade is praise-worthily *not* done.** The article repeatedly refuses to let tenet-coherence or selective citation do evidential work: Berridge is handled with "the honest use is narrow and powerful... enlisting Berridge as an eliminativist about pleasure would mirror, in reverse, the error of enlisting him as a dualist"; active inference is flagged as "double-edged" with Solms & Friston reading the *same* math realistically and Joffily/Coricelli deflationarily, so "recruiting active inference as a value-blind ally would be exactly the over-reach the Map's calibration discipline forbids."
- **It names the pattern explicitly.** "Calibration runs both ways. The steelman strengthens the rival to realistic-possibility status as a research programme — and it does *not* thereby downgrade the Map's view, because the dispute turns on the prior, unsettled hard-problem-and-illusionism question." The closing line — "The Map's evidential-status discipline is satisfied not by winning the argument but by having had it" — is precisely the "tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating" honesty this persona rewards.
- The same restraint appears in `phenomenal-normativity-environmental-ethics`'s **graduated-confidence framework** (high for mammals/birds, moderate for fish/cephalopods, low-but-nonzero for insects, near-zero for plants/ecosystems) and its precautionary-asymmetry framing — precaution presented as a *discipline under uncertainty*, not a licence to upgrade. The cross-cultural convergence argument is used to rebut the "parochially Western" charge **without** being inflated into positive evidence for any specific boundary case.

The Process Philosopher and Hardline Empiricist personas **converge** here rather than conflict: the cluster is genuinely process-friendly *and* evidentially disciplined, on different axes. No possibility/probability slippage was found — this cluster has resolved the tension the two personas are designed to surface.

## Expansion Opportunities

### High Priority

#### The Sublime and Negative Aesthetics (beauty without pleasure)
- **Builds on**: `aesthetics-and-consciousness` (which never once uses the word "sublime") and `phenomenal-value-realism`'s single-clause aside ("The sublime—awe-inspiring yet terrifying—can be beautiful without being pleasant").
- **Would address**: A genuinely uncovered, article-sized gap. The sublime is the *sharpest* test case for the cluster's central claim that aesthetic value is irreducible to hedonic valence — the awe/terror structure, the pleasure-in-tragedy paradox, ugliness and disgust as aesthetic (not merely negative-hedonic) categories. It strengthens phenomenal value *pluralism* exactly where hedonism is weakest. Kant (sublime vs beautiful), Burke, Schopenhauer's "will-less perception" (already cited in the aesthetics article), and the tragedy paradox are the natural sources.
- **GREP-VERIFIED**: no standalone topic/concept on the sublime, negative aesthetics, ugliness, disgust, tragedy, or horror exists; "sublime" appears as a passing clause in only two articles and 0 times in the main aesthetics article.
- **Estimated scope**: Medium article.
- **Tenet alignment**: Dualism (aesthetic value irreducible to physical/hedonic description); Occam's-Razor-Has-Limits (reducing the sublime to "fitness-signalling fear" discards exactly the felt structure needing explanation). Topics section has cap headroom (~267/300).
