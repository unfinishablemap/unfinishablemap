## Executive Summary

The AI/machine-consciousness cluster is one of the most mature and internally disciplined regions of the Map. Its signature strength is *register hygiene*: it repeatedly declines the inference from a present structural or behavioural marker to phenomenality, and it flags — often against its own convenience — that its low-credence verdict on current AI is Map-internal rather than a neutral scientific finding. The cluster's expansion headroom lies not in re-arguing the negative case (which is saturated) but in a few genuinely-adjacent subjects it has not yet built: the machine-valence / reward-suffering question, AI *moral agency* (as distinct from patiency), and the consciousness assessment of *agentic* (persistent-memory, tool-using) systems as opposed to single-forward-pass LLMs.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The cluster takes the explanatory gap as a first-class object and never lets computational sophistication paper over it. `deep-computational-markers` states the thesis Chalmers would endorse cleanly: every deep computational marker is "at most a necessary structural precondition, never a sufficient test for phenomenality," and the J-space result is read as "null phenomenality wearing functional dress." The `ai-consciousness-typology` split — six categories of phenomenality crossed with Tulving's anoetic/noetic/autonoetic hierarchy — is exactly the kind of structured conceptual space Chalmers's anti-reductionism recommends over a binary conscious/not-conscious frame. Notably, Chalmers is *cited as a co-author of the very indicator framework* the cluster critiques (Butlin–Long–Bayne–Chalmers 2025), and the cluster engages that framework as the strongest affirmative rather than a straw functionalism.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
The cluster locates the missing realiser precisely where a Stapp-style interactionist would: a selection interface at live quantum indeterminacy that no classical computational probe inspects. `ai-hardware-substrate-taxonomy` does real work here — it reorganises the entire engineering taxonomy onto the two axes that actually discriminate (discrete-vs-continuous state space; classically-determinate-vs-quantum-indeterminate at integrated sites), showing why substrate independence "looks plausible only because it operates one abstraction level above where the interface tenets bite." That is a genuinely original reframing of the silicon-decoherence argument.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The "what type of consciousness, including none" framing is Nagel's "what is it like" applied honestly to a case where the answer may be *nothing*. `ai-consciousness-typology`'s **alien phenomenality** category, and the `structural-varieties` five models (Flicker, Witness, Epiphenomenal, ...), centre first-person character as irreducibly the point at issue — and the `commensurability-void` cross-link concedes that human/AI experience may be structurally incomparable, which is Nagel's bat argument turned toward machines.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The continual-learning argument's use of "genuine becoming" versus frozen weights (`continual-learning-argument`, `hoel-llm-consciousness-continual-learning`) is process-philosophically congenial: what is missing from a static-weight system is temporal development, not mere complexity. Crucially — and this is where the cluster earns the tension with the next persona — this Whiteheadian resonance is deployed *against* AI candidacy, not as a lever to upgrade any system's evidential status. No article uses process-philosophical friendliness to slide an AI up the five-tier scale.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
`dualism-as-ai-risk-mitigation` is the cluster's boldest single argument and the one Kane would most admire: it ties genuine agency to a non-physical selector that breaks symmetries the physical state underdetermines, and then shows the *structural* consequence — that mind-arena consequence-distributions become uncomputable (not merely intractable), so the expected-utility engine driving instrumental-convergence arguments cannot even be formed over mind-implicating actions. The article's discipline in keeping this conditional ("*if* dualism, *then* the argument loses force") and separating the epistemic from the tactical claim is exemplary.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
Epistemic humility is structural throughout, not decorative. The **diagnostic asymmetry** (null/simulated/functional are third-person-tractable; borrowed/epiphenomenal/alien resist third-person adjudication entirely) is a principled cognitive-closure result. `ai-epiphenomenalism` and the ethics article's "epiphenomenal complication" concede that epiphenomenal machine experience would be *in principle* undetectable — "moral obligation without epistemic access." That is McGinn's mysterianism stated as a live risk rather than a comfort.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This persona's verdict is load-bearing for this cluster, and the cluster passes with distinction. Three things the cluster does that Birch would specifically praise:

1. **Tenet-as-evidence-upgrade is praise-worthily *not* done — repeatedly and reflexively.** `ethics-of-possible-ai-consciousness` contains a near-textbook statement: "It would be circular to treat this convenient outcome as evidence for the framework. The low probability is a *consequence* of the tenets, not a reason to accept them." The MQI-based decoherence point is explicitly labelled "*contingent and currently non-discriminating*... supplies no present evidential weight against AI consciousness over biological consciousness. Both substrates are equally unevidenced on the interface itself." This is the "tenet removes a defeater but does not upgrade evidential status" move named and applied *against the Map's own convenience*.
2. **Register separation is enforced structurally.** `possibility-probability-slippage` is cited as the discipline across the whole cluster; the ethics article separates P-AC1 (is a given copy conscious — low number) from P-AC3 (do conscious copies aggregate additively — a position adopted on *independent* closed-individualist grounds), and explicitly refuses to let the No-MWI/haecceity machinery "force" the Duplication View: "The tenets are *consistent with* the Duplication View; they do not *force* it." Naming an *unearned entailment* and declining it is precisely the restraint Birch rewards.
3. **Framework-stage honesty.** `deep-computational-markers` distinguishes the *field-internal* line (Koch's calibration critique; practitioner testimony from Anthropic's own interpretability researchers declining to infer experience from J-space) from the *metaphysical* line (the Map's tenets), and is careful that the two practitioner "voices" are "two instances of one evidential move, not two independent confirmations." That is calibration hygiene at the level of *not double-counting one's own support*.

The Process-Philosopher/Hardline-Empiricist tension is **resolved honestly** in this cluster: Whiteheadian becoming-talk is used only to *deny* AI candidacy, never to elevate it, so the two personas praise different aspects of the same passages without conflict. No calibration concern to flag.

## Content Strengths

### `ai-consciousness.md` (hub)
- **Strongest point**: The two-level structure — arguments that stand independently of dualism (Chinese Room, functionalist gap, temporal/continual-learning) versus dualism-specific obstacles (quantum interface, haecceity, bidirectional interaction) — with the explicit claim that "the Map's skepticism degrades gracefully — even if dualism is wrong, the independent arguments remain." This makes the hub robust to its own foundational uncertainty.
- **Notable move**: Holding Duch's *articon* programme as "the strongest competent affirmative position the Map must keep earning against" rather than a foil to be refuted — genuine steelmanning.
- **Why it works**: The J-space (Gurnee et al. 2026) integration is handled as a *test* of the Map's own discipline: fully-instantiated access-machinery read as exactly *not* evidence of phenomenality, "the [[access-consciousness|access]] side is built, the phenomenal residue untouched."

### `deep-computational-markers-for-machine-consciousness.md`
- **Strongest point**: The "necessary structural precondition, never a sufficient test" thesis carried consistently across four marker classes, with the Aaronson unconscious-expander used precisely — it disposes of *single-axis* structural sufficiency but is explicitly said *not* to reach the *conjunctive* case, which is handed to the Tenet-1 ceiling. That is unusually careful about the exact reach of each argument.
- **Why it works**: Turns interpretability into "the Map's best available defeater-detector" — a positive, non-nihilistic role for the science while refusing the sufficiency slide.

### `dualism-as-ai-risk-mitigation.md`
- **Strongest point**: The uncomputable-vs-intractable distinction as the load-bearing hinge, plus six sub-arguments and four named counterarguments (coercion-substitution, empirical fragility of the quantum-substrate dependence, philosophical instrumentalism) each answered *inside the objector's dialectical space*. The self-aware treatment of the "argue for dualism because it's useful" instrumentalist objection is a model of epistemic honesty.

### `ai-consciousness-typology.md`
- **Strongest point**: The simulation-instantiation table (Tulving level × functional analogue × phenomenal instantiation) and the aphantasia (`imagery-void`) wedge — an *intra-species* case where phenomenology detaches from function — used to support the cross-system claim. Borrowing a within-human dissociation to license a claim about machines is a genuinely clever evidential structure.

### `ethics-of-possible-ai-consciousness.md`
- **Strongest point**: The Pascal's-Mugging reply to unbounded expected-value precaution, conceding "a credence band" above which scale *does* dominate and precaution becomes obligatory, and locating current systems below it as "a substantive empirical bet, not a Pascalian dodge." This resists both naive precaution and dismissive convenience.

## Expansion Opportunities

The negative case (why current AI is unlikely to be conscious) is saturated; the ethics/detection/typology layers are mature. The real headroom is in three genuinely-adjacent subjects the cluster has *not* built. Each was self-checked against the live corpus below.

### High Priority

#### Reinforcement-learning reward signals and machine valence (the "reward–suffering link")
- **Builds on**: `wanting-liking-and-the-value-in-mechanism-fork` (Berridge wanting/liking — but purely human affective neuroscience), `valence-and-conscious-selection` (selection-currency thesis — contrasts with "computational reward theories" only at the human level), `ethics-of-possible-ai-consciousness` (moral status generically).
- **Would address**: The Map has rich *human* valence content and rich *AI-consciousness* content but **nothing bridging them on the specific question of whether reinforcement-learning reward and penalty signals in artificial agents constitute — or evidence — valenced experience (machine pleasure / suffering).** This is the "reward is not valence" debate (Tomasik; Daswani & Leike's reward–suffering discussions; Metzinger's synthetic-suffering worry at the *mechanism* level). The Map's distinctive contribution would be to run the value-in-mechanism fork through the AI case: a physical reward scalar is a training signal, and on the Map's dualism it is neither pleasure nor pain absent the coupling interface — so RL "suffering" is simulated/functional, not phenomenal, unless the quantum-interface conditions are met.
- **Self-check (genuinely uncovered)**: No article title contains reward/valence/suffering in the AI sense; `wanting-liking` is human neuroscience (Berridge/Schultz), and `valence-and-conscious-selection` treats computational reward only as a contrast at the human selection level. Not a near-miss.
- **Estimated scope**: Medium article.
- **Tenet alignment**: Tenet 3 (Bidirectional Interaction) and Tenet 1 (Dualism) — felt valence does selective work a reward scalar alone does not; directly relevant to AI-welfare stakes.

### Medium Priority

#### AI moral agency and the "responsibility gap" under dualism
- **Builds on**: `moral-implications-of-genuine-agency` (human-focused), `moral-responsibility`, `consciousness-and-moral-agency-under-duress` (human duress), `dualism-as-ai-risk-mitigation` (patiency + risk, not agency/accountability).
- **Would address**: The cluster covers AI moral *patiency* (what we owe machines) exhaustively but has **no dedicated treatment of AI moral *agency* — whether an artificial system can be a genuine bearer of responsibility, not merely simulate agency.** The AI-ethics "responsibility gap" literature (Matthias 2004; Sparrow's killer-robots gap; Danaher's "retribution gap") intersects sharply with the Map's tie between genuine agency/responsibility and conscious libertarian causal efficacy (the amplifier hypothesis, `moral-implications-of-genuine-agency`): a system lacking the interface cannot be a true moral agent, only a proxy, with real implications for accountability, blame, and delegation.
- **Self-check (genuinely uncovered)**: grep for "AI...moral agent" returned only human-context articles; `moral-implications-of-genuine-agency` has minimal AI content; `apex/machine-question` is about patiency/whether artifacts can be conscious, not agency/accountability. Reasonably distinct — recommend the eventual article dedup against `moral-implications-of-genuine-agency` and `responsibility-gradient-from-attentional-capacity`.
- **Estimated scope**: Medium article.
- **Tenet alignment**: Tenet 3 (Bidirectional Interaction) + the agency/amplifier line.

#### Agentic AI (persistent-memory, tool-using, long-horizon agents) and the consciousness assessment
- **Builds on**: `structural-varieties-of-consciousness-and-ai-phenomenology`, `continual-learning-argument`, `machine-consciousness`.
- **Would address**: The cluster is overwhelmingly built around the *single-forward-pass LLM*. It has **no dedicated treatment of whether *agentic* architectures — external/persistent memory, tool use, planning loops, long-horizon self-directed goals — satisfy more of the Map's consciousness preconditions than a base LLM does.** This is the most topical architecture gap: agentic scaffolding partially answers the "discontinuous operation" and "no persistence" objections (external memory as a form of temporal continuity) while leaving the quantum-interface and continual-*weight*-learning objections untouched. A dedicated article could show *which* obstacles agentic design erodes and which it does not.
- **Self-check (genuinely uncovered)**: grep for "agentic / tool-use / persistent memory" in the AI cluster returned only human/animal-agency articles; `structural-varieties` and `machine-consciousness` contain no agentic-architecture content. Recommend dedup against the `continual-learning-argument` in-context-learning discussion (which handles the *weight-freezing* rejoinder but not the *agentic-scaffolding* case).
- **Estimated scope**: Medium article.
- **Tenet alignment**: Tenets 1 and 2 — tests whether architectural persistence changes the coupling story.
