### Issue 1: Inexact verbatim quotation of the parent article
- **File**: `topics/tactile-consciousness-and-the-interface.md`, opening paragraph (line 35)
- **Problem**: The article quotes `dualist-perception` as conceding *"perception extends across modalities—auditory, tactile, olfactory, gustatory."* The source (`topics/dualist-perception.md` line 155) actually reads *"perception extends across modalities — auditory, tactile, olfactory, gustatory, proprioceptive, vestibular — and these modalities interact."* The quote drops two named modalities (proprioceptive, vestibular) and closes an open mid-sentence with a period inside the quotation marks. This is the dropped-content-in-verbatim-quote pattern (cf. fresh-create-defect-tail). It is internal-to-corpus, so low stakes, but it is an inexact quotation presented as exact.
- **Severity**: Low-Medium
- **Recommendation**: Either restore the full quoted list, or replace the quotation marks with a paraphrase so no exact-quote claim is made.

### Issue 2: "Completing the perception-modality series" overclaims corpus closure
- **File**: `topics/tactile-consciousness-and-the-interface.md`, opening paragraph (line 35): *"completing the perception-modality series (vision → audition → chemosensation → touch)"*; echoed in the closing paragraph (line 87).
- **Problem**: The article frames touch as the final piece of a completed series. But the very source it quotes names six modalities, adding **proprioceptive** and **vestibular**; the article itself hands proprioception to a separate article (`dual-domain-capabilities-in-proprioception-and-spatial-imagination`), and there is no interface treatment of vestibular sense, thermal sensation, or interoception anywhere in `topics/`. The "completing the series" claim therefore asserts a closure the corpus does not have. This is not a calibration-of-the-dualist-thesis issue (which the article handles impeccably); it is a factual/scope claim about the article series itself. The tighter, true claim is that touch completes the *exteroceptive distance-sense-plus-touch* arc the companion series set out to cover (vision, audition, chemosensation, touch), not "the perception-modality series."
- **Severity**: Low-Medium
- **Recommendation**: Re-scope the framing to what is true — e.g. "completing the exteroceptive-modality companion series" or "the fourth in the perception-modality series, alongside the proprioceptive/body-schema treatment developed separately" — so it does not imply the modality space is closed while vestibular/thermal/interoceptive cases remain untreated. Length-neutral.
