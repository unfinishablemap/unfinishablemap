## Pessimistic Analysis Summary

### Critical Issues Found (all fixed this pass)

The §2.4 web-verify pass caught three citation/quote defects that survived all four prior "verified" reviews — a textbook case of intra-corpus consistency *ratifying* an error rather than catching it (the 2026-06-22 ledger carried the Friston entry as "real-correct; entry unchanged").

- **Friston et al. 2020 — wrong title (real-wrong-metadata, fixed).** References gave the title as "Markovian monism." *Entropy* 22(5):516 (DOI 10.3390/e22050516) is actually titled **"Sentience and the Origins of Consciousness: From Cartesian Duality to Markovian Monism."** "Markovian monism" is the concept the paper coins, not its title. Corrected to the full title. Now matches the canonical form already used in sibling `predictive-processing-and-dualism.md` (line 176).
- **"mutually reductive" — paraphrase-as-quote (fixed, ×2).** The article twice put "mutually reductive" in quotation marks attributed to Friston et al. The paper never uses that phrase; it says the extrinsic geometry "is ultimately reducible to the intrinsic information geometry (and the other way around)." Replaced with the verbatim phrase "ultimately reducible ... to the other." A third instance at condition (2) is a generic illustrative locution (not source-attributed) and was left.
- **Beni 2021 — altered verbatim quote (fixed).** Article quoted: "we cannot read off metaphysical theses about the nature of target systems from our theories." Original (PMC7885977, abstract): "we **could not** read off metaphysical theses about the nature of target systems (self-organising conscious systems...) from our theories **of nature of scientific models (Markov blankets)**." The article had (a) changed "could not"→"cannot" inside quotes and (b) truncated away the target-systems-vs-scientific-models contrast that *is* Beni's point. Re-rendered with verbatim fragments + ellipsis, restoring the contrast. Now consistent with the sibling's rendering.
