## Pessimistic Analysis Summary

### Critical Issues Found
- **Stapp/von Neumann misattribution**: The three-process decomposition (Process 1/2/3) was attributed to "Von Neumann's decomposition" but is actually Stapp's refinement. Von Neumann (1932) distinguished only two processes (I: measurement/collapse, II: unitary evolution). Stapp split Process I into Process 1 (choice of measurement) and Process 3 (Born rule collapse). **Resolution**: Reworded to correctly attribute the three-process framework to Stapp's refinement of von Neumann.

### Medium Issues Found
- **Unreferenced citation (Adan et al. 2023)**: Listed in References but never cited in article text. **Resolution**: Removed. The citation is properly used in the quantum-completeness concept article where it belongs.
- **No engagement with decoherence objection**: A major counterargument was entirely absent. **Resolution**: Added a paragraph explaining that decoherence narrows options but does not make the choice—it explains why macroscopic superpositions vanish but not why one outcome becomes actual.
- **Thin unfalsifiability defense**: The article called unfalsifiability "a feature" without adequate justification. **Resolution**: Expanded to explain why statistical invisibility follows from the nature of the gap itself and that the hypothesis should be evaluated by explanatory reach rather than falsifiability at individual-outcome level.
- **Cursory MWI dismissal**: Did not engage with sophisticated decision-theoretic Born rule derivations (Deutsch, Wallace). **Resolution**: Added mention of these approaches and noted that even if they succeed, they leave the indexical question unanswered.

### Counterarguments Considered
- **Eliminative materialist**: "Ontological completeness" is a philosopher's invention—if physics captures all structure and structure is all there is, there is no gap. Response: The article argues that structure being "all physics describes" differs from structure being "all there is"; this is a bedrock disagreement.
- **Hard-nosed physicalist**: Decoherence dissolves the measurement problem for all practical purposes. Response: Now addressed in article—decoherence narrows options but doesn't select outcomes.
- **Quantum skeptic**: Decoherence timescales in the brain are far too short for consciousness to act at quantum indeterminacies. Response: This is addressed elsewhere on the Map (conservation-laws-and-mental-causation, born-rule-and-the-consciousness-interface); not appropriate to replicate here.
- **MWI defender**: Self-locating uncertainty and decision-theoretic Born rule derivations answer the indexical question. Response: Now briefly engaged—whether these derivations succeed remains contested.
- **Empiricist**: Unfalsifiability is fatal, not a feature. Response: Now addressed—statistical invisibility follows from the gap's nature; evaluate by explanatory reach.
- **Buddhist philosopher**: The article assumes a unified selector without examining what it is. Response: Valid expansion opportunity for future work but outside this article's scope.
