## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)

Chalmers would recognise in `four-quadrant-dualism-taxonomy.md` lines 51 and 63 the taxonomic move he himself has long needed: his Type-D/E/F classification organises dualism *by relation* but not *by thickness*, and the new axis lets one distinguish positions that collapse together under his scheme. The article's placement of Chalmers' own naturalistic dualism in "medium mind" (line 51) is philosophically exact — Chalmers paired with psychophysical laws is not thin (there are genuine psychophysical regularities doing work) but not thick (no subliminal self, no cosmic consciousness). More importantly, the interface specification programme's closing paragraph (lines 141 at `apex/interface-specification-programme.md`) explicitly acknowledges that "making this positional choice explicit is part of what the programme's precision buys — a commitment to one principled dualist stance among several viable ones, whose details could turn out to fit reality worse than a rival quadrant does." This is the *right* Chalmersian temperament: one's own position is a *location in a space of live alternatives*, not a truth-claim asserted against error.

**What resonates most strongly**: The `source-attribution-void.md` Dualism paragraph (lines 101-103) refuses to claim this void as evidence *for* dualism — it reads the void as *constraining what dualism may claim* rather than what dualism may be inferred from. Chalmers' own naturalistic dualism runs precisely this way: the hard problem sets constraints on any acceptable theory rather than being a positive argument for a specific ontology. Seeing the Map decline to turn a sympathetic finding into a partisan prop is what distinguishes a research programme from an advocacy programme.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)

Stapp's own position appears twice in the new material, once as Q4's canonical inhabitant (`four-quadrant-dualism-taxonomy.md` lines 106) and once as a contrastive positioning in `apex/interface-specification-programme.md` line 141. The taxonomy's key move — separating the *intervention channel* (minimal) from the *observer itself* (ontologically thick) — is precisely the distinction Stapp's critics routinely miss and the distinction Stapp himself makes less crisply in his own writing. The article at line 117 is sharp: *"Minimal intervention and rich mind are not the same dimension, and reading Stapp as minimal on both axes misses half the structure."* This is the charitable-and-precise reading Stapp has been owed for decades, and the Map delivering it is a small service to philosophy.

**What aligns**: The interface specification programme's honest acknowledgement at `apex/interface-specification-programme.md` lines 123 that *Stapp's attention-rate law, Eccles' intention-probability law, and the Map's own policy-selection law are three candidates none of which has been empirically confirmed* — this is the epistemological state Stapp has said we are in since *Mindful Universe*, and having it named as the *state of the programme* (not a weakness, not a failure) is the right methodological frame for a dualist research programme to occupy.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)

Nagel would read `voids/source-attribution-void.md` as a long-awaited structural vindication of "what is it like to be..." at a new scale. His own argument was that *subjectivity* sets epistemic bounds on objective description. The source-attribution void reveals that subjectivity *also* sets epistemic bounds on *itself* — the first-person does not know its own origin, and cannot discover it from within. The article at lines 73-81 gives Nagel a structural framework his 1974 essay lacked: the three-layer architecture (architectural non-tagging, inferential collapse, confabulatory generation) converts a phenomenological intuition into a testable structural claim. Crucially, the article refuses to let the findings collapse into eliminativism: Wegner can be *read* eliminativistically, but the narrower structural finding — that authorship-experience is dissociable from authorship in arranged conditions — is preserved without the metaphysical upgrade the experiment does not license (lines 68-69).

**What centres subjective experience appropriately**: The `voids/ineffable-encounter-void.md` treatment of the numinous at lines 95-101 takes seriously what Nagel would call the *asymmetry of access*: the numinous is not merely unarticulable by the evaluator; it dismantles the evaluator. This is the bat problem recursively — not only are there experiences unavailable to our architecture, but there are experiences available to our architecture *only on condition* of the architecture's partial collapse. That is a phenomenological finding sharp enough to rebuke the common dismissal that ineffable states are "just vague emotions."

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)

Whitehead's programme requires that the subject be constituted by its prehensions rather than standing prior to them, and that experience be selective rather than comprehensive. `voids/source-attribution-void.md` lines 117-119 provides exactly this: *"Authorship is a construction, not a perception. This does not eliminate authorship but reframes it as a downstream attribution, like any other."* Whitehead would read this as a phenomenological recovery of his own claim that the "actual occasion" of conscious experience is a *concrescence* — a coming-together of prior prehensions into a novel experiential unity, not a reading of something antecedently present. The article's careful separation of the architectural, inferential, and confabulatory layers (line 76-81) is a more analytical version of Whitehead's own three-phase process (conformal, conceptual, comparative), and the fit is structural, not accidental.

**What avoids crude dualism**: The `four-quadrant-dualism-taxonomy.md` treatment of priority cosmopsychism (Goff) at line 107 places Whitehead's descendants in Q4 alongside Cartesian and hylomorphic dualism. Crucially, the article at line 111 notes that *"Q4 is where the strongest combinations of explanatory resources live — rich on both sides, with interaction as a genuine ontological phenomenon rather than a concession."* This is the Whiteheadian register: interaction is not a problem to be solved but the ontologically basic fact to be described. The taxonomy's willingness to treat Q4 as under-explored rather than refuted shows the Map is not in the habit of letting disciplinary parsimony-bias settle questions that deserve open exploration.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)

Kane's programme needs two things the new material delivers with unusual discipline: a specification of *how* agency could operate without becoming mysticism, and an honest account of *what agency cannot inspect about itself*. `apex/interface-specification-programme.md` lines 75-89 catalogue the five operations (gating, stabilisation, veto, attractor steering, resource allocation) with bandwidth specifications for each — this is the Kanean controller in engineering-level detail, and the level of detail is a credit to the programme. But the `voids/source-attribution-void.md` Implications section at line 115-119 complements this with the limitation: authorship is a *downstream attribution*, and the mind's confidence about its own origination is weakly correlated with actual origin. The Kanean move is to hold these together — genuine agency *and* its introspective opacity — and the Map now does so explicitly.

**What supports agency and choice**: The `voids/meta-epistemology-of-limits.md` treatment at lines 114 of *the recursive trap applying equally to dualism itself* — *"The Map's tenets are chosen starting points, not conclusions established from outside the trap"* — is the maturity signature Kane's position requires. A libertarian about free will who cannot concede that the libertarian commitment is itself a chosen commitment (not something established by view-from-nowhere philosophical argument) has not grasped what libertarianism means. The Map's willingness to name the Münchhausen character of its own starting point is the philosophically mature version of the libertarian posture.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)

McGinn's *permanent mysterianism* finds a more structured elaboration in `voids/meta-epistemology-of-limits.md` than in McGinn 1989 itself. The article at lines 64-66 introduces the Vlerick-Boudry distinction between *representational* and *psychological* closure — we may be able to represent what would count as a solution while remaining unable to grasp it when presented — and converts it into a *second-order void*: the [[closure-types-void]] about whether, from within, any reliable procedure distinguishes which type we face. This is the move McGinn's critics (Kriegel, Dennett) argued was unavailable; the Map shows it is not only available but a live epistemic category. The article's closing section "What Would Challenge This View?" at lines 138-140 sets three disconfirming conditions — an actual hard-problem solution, a reliable internal distinguishing procedure, and independently-trained AI systems converging on the same limits — that McGinn's own defenders have not given him.

**What shows epistemic humility**: The `voids/ineffable-encounter-void.md` "Evidential Stalemate" section at lines 128-134 explicitly names the stalemate rather than papering over it. *"Neither side lands a decisive blow. The persistent stalemate has the signature of a genuine cognitive boundary rather than a tractable empirical question awaiting resolution."* McGinn would read this as his own thesis exhibited in the field: the shape of sustained philosophical failure is itself evidence about cognitive architecture, not just about the object of failure. The Map's ability to treat the stalemate *as data* rather than as an embarrassment to be resolved is the mature form of the mysterian commitment.

### `topics/four-quadrant-dualism-taxonomy.md`

- **Strongest point**: The identification of *ontological thickness* as a distinct axis orthogonal to the substance/property distinction and to Chalmers' Type-D/E/F classification. The taxonomy at lines 44-63 is short, but once named, one cannot un-see the axis — every dualist taxonomy before it has conflated "kind of relation" with "amount of ontological weight," producing positional blur. The article's clarifying move at lines 61-62 — that "min-physical" does *not* mean fewer physical things exist, it means physics' *own* ontology is treated as minimal — forestalls the standard eliminativist misreading.
- **Notable quote**: *"Minimal intervention and rich mind are not the same dimension, and reading Stapp as minimal on both axes misses half the structure."* (line 117)
- **Why it works**: The article does what philosophical taxonomy rarely does well — it earns its categories by showing them at work on live positions. Saad is placed in Q1 with stability pressure flagged; Kastrup is shown to slide from Q3 dualism into idealism; Stapp is placed in Q4 with the channel/observer separation made explicit. The category scheme is not imposed on positions; it is tested *against* positions.
- **Secondary strength**: The self-critical paragraph at line 87 — *"The dominance is not merely cultural: physical science has delivered well-tested structure in a way that phenomenal ontology has not, and epistemic weighting can reasonably follow"* — is a rare concession from a dualist-leaning source to the best version of the materialist position. Granting the force of the epistemic asymmetry rather than dismissing Q2's dominance as "prejudice" is what distinguishes philosophical conversation from advocacy.

This is the non-question-begging frame — if you arrive at No Many Worlds from reasons A, B, C, the probability objections strengthen the position; if you reject those reasons, the probability objections must carry the full weight, which they arguably cannot.
