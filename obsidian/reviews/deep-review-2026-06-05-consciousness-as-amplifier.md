## Pessimistic Analysis Summary

### Critical Issues Found

1. **WRONG-PAPER misattribution of the evolutionary-impotence quotation** (survived all 6 prior reviews). The Evolutionary Argument section quoted: *"Any endeavor to construct a physical theory of consciousness based on emergence within the framework of classical physics leads to causally impotent conscious experiences in direct contradiction to evolutionary theory,"* attributed to **"Doerig, A., Schurger, A., & Herzog, M.H. (2024). The function(s) of consciousness: an evolutionary perspective. *Neuroscience of Consciousness*, 2024(1), niae031."**

   Web-verification result:
   - The quotation is **verbatim from Georgiev, D.D. (2024). "Evolution of consciousness." *Life*, 14(1), 48** (MDPI; PMC10817314; published 2023-12-27). Georgiev's text reads "...framework of classical physics, **however**, leads to causally impotent conscious experiences in direct contradiction to evolutionary theory since epiphenomenal consciousness cannot evolve through natural selection." The article had dropped the "however" and truncated correctly at "evolutionary theory."
   - The cited Doerig article ID **niae031** in *Neuroscience of Consciousness* 2024(1) is a **real but unrelated paper** (Filimonov et al., on feature-based attention and ERP correlates of auditory awareness). Doerig/Schurger/Herzog are genuine consciousness researchers (the "unfolding argument" / "hard criteria" papers), which is exactly why the chimera was plausible enough to survive six author+year cross-checks.

   **Resolution applied**: corrected inline attribution to "Georgiev (2024)... in *Life*"; restored the dropped "however,"; replaced the References entry with "Georgiev, D.D. (2024). Evolution of consciousness. *Life*, 14(1), 48." and moved it to its alphabetical slot. This is a critical attribution error per §2 (claims attributed to the wrong author / wrong venue), now fixed.

   **Corpus check**: the niae031/Doerig-2024 misattribution markers were grep'd corpus-wide and found **only in this file**. Doerig is cited correctly in `neural-refresh-rates.md`, `neural-refresh-rates-and-the-smoothness-problem.md`, and `smoothness-and-continuity.md` (Herzog, Drissi-Daoudi & Doerig 2020, *TiCS* 24(10):826-837, the "time slices" paper) — those are correct and were left intact.
