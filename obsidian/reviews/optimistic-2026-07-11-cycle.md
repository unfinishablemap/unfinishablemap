## Executive Summary

This session's new metaphysics-of-mind and vagueness articles are unusually well-calibrated: they name the Map's stake as *motivation, not result*, keep the ontic/semantic/epistemic loci cleanly separated, and engage rivals (Schwitzgebel, Tye, Davidson, Honderich) as serious interlocutors rather than foils. The strongest expansion opportunities lie one step out from these clusters — a genuinely uncovered Buddhist idealist tradition (Yogācāra), a dedicated treatment of the *epistemicist* escape from the phenomenal sorites (the Map-friendly resolution the sorites article gestures at but does not develop), and a hub concept for the organizational-invariance / fading-and-dancing-qualia arguments that the unfolding-argument article attacks but that has no home page of its own.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The phenomenal-sorites article keeps the on/off *subject-fact* prior to and distinct from the dimmer-setting of `degrees-of-consciousness`, which is exactly the distinction a hard-problem realist needs to prevent gradation-talk from quietly dissolving the explanandum. The anomalous-monism article's insistence that mental *properties* must do causal work — not merely mental event-tokens — is the property dualist's core demand, and it is pressed via Honderich rather than asserted.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
Anomalous monism is framed as converging with the Map on the failure of strict reductive bridge laws while splitting on the verdict, with the Map's *non-strict probabilistic selection law at the measurement interface* offered as the positive alternative to Davidson's no-law quietism. That is the right place to locate the disagreement.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
Tye's first-person incoherence worry about a genuinely borderline conscious/non-conscious case is reported faithfully (it is why Tye escapes into panpsychism rather than embracing a middle), preserving the "something it is like" asymmetry between the inside and outside views of the sorites series.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The sorites article resists the easy panpsychist dissolution (noting Tye pays for vagueness-avoidance with panpsychism) rather than reaching for "experience all the way down" to make the boundary problem disappear. Process-compatible intuitions are acknowledged without being used to manufacture a determinate verdict.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
The anomalous-monism article's demand for genuine causal relevance of the mental *qua* mental is the same demand agency needs: a will that is causally idle (Honderich's epiphenomenalist reading of Davidson) cannot ground sourcehood. The Map's selection-law answer keeps the door open for mental causation that is neither reducible nor epiphenomenal.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
The three-loci taxonomy explicitly reserves *epistemicism* — a sharp fact whose location is unknowable — as a live option, which is McGinn's home turf. The article's honesty that the Schwitzgebel subject-*counting* paper is a working paper "not confirmed at the publisher of record" models the epistemic humility the Mysterian prizes.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This is the load-bearing verdict, because the sorites article sits directly on the minimal-organism / boundary-case terrain. It passes cleanly. The Map's Tenet-4 stake is introduced as *motivation to resist ontic vagueness*, not as evidence against it — "That motivation does not settle the metaphysics; the case for vagueness is live and deserves a fair hearing before the Map answers it." That is exactly the *tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating* discipline: a tenet supplies a reason to want a determinate subject-fact, and the article declines to convert that want into a metaphysical result. The quadrilemma is reported at full strength (including option (b) everything-is-conscious and option (d) vagueness) rather than being stacked toward the Map's preference. No tier-upgrade is smuggled in on tenet-load. The one thing to watch in any expansion here: the epistemicist resolution (below) is the Map's most natural friend precisely because it *preserves the sharp subject-fact without claiming to locate it* — a good expansion must keep that "sharp-but-unknowable" claim distinct from "sharp-and-we-know-where," or it will over-claim.

### High Priority

#### Yogācāra and the storehouse consciousness (ālaya-vijñāna)
- **The Map has no article on Yogācāra / Vijñānavāda**, the Buddhist "mind-only" (cittamātra) school of Vasubandhu and Asaṅga, and specifically **no dedicated treatment of the ālaya-vijñāna (storehouse consciousness)** and the eight-consciousness model. A corpus scan finds the term only in workflow changelogs, never in a content article.
- **Target section**: concepts.
- **Builds on**: the fresh cross-cultural cluster (nahua, kyoto-school, jain). Yogācāra is the most consequential philosophy-of-mind tradition still missing from that set — it is a fully worked-out consciousness-first metaphysics with a theory of subliminal continuity (the storehouse) that maps onto contemporary debates about the unconscious, personal-identity continuity, and idealism.
- **Tenet alignment**: a mind-first idealist rival to interactionist dualism; engaging it seriously strengthens the Map's account of *why* it is dualist rather than idealist (Tenet 1), and the storehouse-continuity idea is a non-Western foil for indexical identity (Tenet 4). Engage as a rival to be taken seriously, not as evidence for dualism.

#### Epistemicism about consciousness (the sharp-but-unknowable boundary)
- **The Map has no dedicated concept on the epistemicist resolution of the phenomenal sorites** — Williamson-style epistemicism applied to the phenomenal on/off fact: there is a precise last conscious member of any continuum, but its location is in principle unknowable. The new sorites article *names* this locus but explicitly leaves it undeveloped; it is under-explored and deserves its own treatment.
- **Target section**: concepts.
- **Builds on**: `phenomenal-sorites-problem`, `mysterianism`, `epistemology-of-limit-knowledge`.
- **Tenet alignment**: this is the Map's most natural ally on the sorites, because epistemicism *preserves* the determinate subject-fact Tenet 4 requires while conceding we cannot locate it. Handled carefully it lets the Map answer the sorites without either arbitrary sharp cutoffs or ontic vagueness. Must keep "sharp-but-unknowable" strictly distinct from any claim to know the boundary's location (per the Hardline Empiricist note above).

#### Organizational invariance and the fading- and dancing-qualia arguments
- **The Map lacks a dedicated hub concept for the organizational-invariance principle and Chalmers' fading-/dancing-qualia arguments.** The phrases appear scattered across at least ten articles (`the-epiphenomenalist-threat`, `hard-problem-of-consciousness`, `machine-consciousness`, `qualia`, `ai-consciousness`, `delegatory-dualism`, others) but there is no canonical page they can all point at.
- **Target section**: concepts.
- **Builds on / why now**: the fresh `the-unfolding-argument-against-causal-structure-theories-of-consciousness` article *attacks* exactly this family of substitution/invariance arguments; a dedicated concept would give the unfolding-argument article, the substrate-independence article, and the machine-consciousness cluster a shared referent and reduce the current duplication.
- **Tenet alignment**: directly bears on substrate-independence and whether functional isomorphs preserve or drop experience (Tenet 1); the dualist reading of where invariance breaks is a natural anchor.

### Medium Priority

#### Metaphysical grounding and the psychophysical relation
- **Under-explored**: the Map leans on `supervenience`, `emergence`, and `constitution-vs-causal-work`, but has **no dedicated concept on metaphysical grounding** (the post-2010 hyperintensional dependence relation — Fine, Schaffer) as applied to the mind-body relation. "Grounding" appears only in passing across topics.
- **Target section**: concepts.
- **Note on overlap risk**: this must earn its keep by doing what supervenience cannot — grounding is hyperintensional and asymmetric where supervenience is merely modal-covariational, and that difference is exactly what a dualist needs to say "the physical does not *ground* the phenomenal" more precisely than "the phenomenal does not supervene." If an expansion cannot hold that distinction it should fold back into `supervenience` instead. Flag for the harvester as lower-confidence than the three above.
