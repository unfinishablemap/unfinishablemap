## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
Chalmers would find the recovery-order article a careful exhibit of the explanatory gap at work in the breakdown regime. The argument's spine — that a substrate-symmetric production account predicts recovery should retrace degradation, and the data violate this — is exactly the kind of place where the gap becomes *operationally visible* rather than merely conceptual. He would especially approve that the article never claims the gap is *closed* by the asymmetry; it claims only that the asymmetry "raises the cost and specificity demanded of production accounts." That is the hard problem expressed as a pricing structure rather than a knockout, which is the honest register for it.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
Stapp would be drawn to the self-concealing-interface thesis as the cleanest statement yet of *why* a von-Neumann-style mental intervention would be empirically elusive: the strict post-decoherence corridor "selects which definite outcome emerges from the classical mixture left after decoherence completes, choosing among Born-equiprobable alternatives without altering the ensemble distribution." He would recognise this as a disciplined version of his own Process-1 picture — the place where mind enters physics — but with the detection consequence stated frankly: by construction it leaves no aggregate Born signature. He would credit the article for facing that cost head-on rather than promising a statistical fingerprint it cannot deliver.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
Nagel would single out the introspection-opacity consequence in the apex piece: "a markerless interface is, from the subject's side, observationally indistinguishable from no interface," and "self-concealment is not only from the third-person instrument; it is from the first-person witness." This is a genuinely Nagelian point — the first-person register is not a privileged transparent window but itself a place where the architecture hides. The terminal-lucidity passage in the topic article also centres the *content* of returning autobiographical experience (recognising relatives, recalling specific episodes) as the explanandum, which is the right first-person framing.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
Whitehead would resonate with the framing of consciousness as a *direction-sensitive process* rather than a substrate-state read off at an instant. The autonoetic channel returning first under falling substrate function and last under rising substrate function is exactly the kind of event-relative, history-dependent fact a process ontology expects and a substance ontology finds awkward. **Per the evidential-status constraint on this persona, none of this Whiteheadian resonance is permitted to function as a tier-upgrade** — and, crucially, the articles do not invite it to. ## Content Strengths

### topics/recovery-order-asymmetry-as-interface-evidence.md
- **Strongest point**: The *naming* of a portable evidence type. The article doesn't just report that recovery ≠ reverse-of-degradation in one case; it abstracts "the direction of interface change" into a four-member signature family (hysteresis of state transition; recovery-order reversal across mechanisms; abrupt reconnection vs gradual onset; direction-decoupled timing) that is now reusable across the disruption cluster. Naming-as-method is the Map's cartographic signature, executed cleanly here.
- **Notable quote**: "an anaesthetised brain is recovering substrate function on a rising trajectory, while a terminally lucid brain is recovering conscious access on a *falling* substrate trajectory." This single contrast is the article's whole argument compressed — the same channel (autonoetic) recovering in opposite order relative to the others, with the *direction of substrate change* as the distinguishing variable.
- **Why it works**: It anchors the argument on the *reversible, dose-dependent, within-subject* case (anaesthesia, with the Sepúlveda 2019 neural-inertia hysteresis and the Stone et al. 2025 stochastic-emergence finding) before reaching for the dramatic-but-weak case (terminal lucidity), and is explicit that the dramatic case rests on the weakest evidence. The argumentative load is correctly distributed to the strongest evidence.
