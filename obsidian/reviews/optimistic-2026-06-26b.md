## Executive Summary

This is the Map's **empirically load-bearing core** — the cluster that has to do the heavy lifting of connecting first-person phenomena to the Bidirectional Interaction tenet — and it is in unusually disciplined shape. Every member draws a clean, repeated `established (access-level mental causation) / not-established-by-these-studies (phenomenal character does the work)` demarcation, names the leading physicalist rival by author and reading rather than strawmanning it, and explicitly grades evidence by the *weakest* claim it supports. The standout is `empirical-evidence-for-consciousness-selecting`, whose six-rung evidence-grading scale and two-column "physicalist reading of the same result" table are a model of self-auditing argumentation. The opportunities are almost entirely **structural cross-linking inside the cluster**, not new content: three of the four hub articles fail to link conceptual siblings they argue *from* (e.g. the content-specificity concept page never links `attention-as-interface` or `predictive-processing-and-dualism`, both central to its own case; `attention-as-interface` is linked twice by `interface-friction` but does not reciprocate). No new-article gap, no length or EOF findings.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The cluster instantiates the explanatory gap on real data rather than asserting it. `content-specificity-of-mental-causation` (L57) presses functionalism on exactly the Chalmersian point: "Functionalism explains the information processing; content-specificity asks why the processing has the qualitative character it does, and why that character tracks the causal profile so precisely." `attention-as-interface`'s Relocation Objection (L202) is the hard problem handled with integrity — the hypothesis is credited with *narrowing where the gap sits* ("from 'consciousness and the brain' to 'consciousness and attentional selection'") rather than dissolving it.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
`empirical-evidence-for-consciousness-selecting`'s quantum-biological line (L91) is the most careful Stapp-friendly passage in the corpus: it routes the quantum Zeno effect through a *functioning evolved system* (cryptochrome magnetoreception, Denton et al. 2024) — "Evolution has already implemented Zeno-like dynamics in a functional biological system" — while immediately conceding (L106) that quantum feasibility "is not by itself a bridge to subjectivity." Engagement with the physics-consciousness interface without over-claiming a mechanism.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The choking analysis in `empirical-phenomena-mental-causation` (L124–127) centers first-person structure as the load-bearing datum: "You cannot confabulate interference: attending to your golf swing either disrupts it or does not." The negative-evidence framing is a genuinely Nagelian move — it makes the felt mode of attention the thing a third-person account must explain, not explain away.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
`interface-friction` treats the mind-brain coupling as one instance of a general resistance-against-constraint pattern shared across six domains (L43) — events-and-relations talk rather than crude substance-pushing-substance. Praiseworthy as process-compatibility; and notably **not** used to upgrade any evidential tier — the article is emphatic (L41) that it "does not generate empirically distinguishable predictions" and its value is "interpretive."

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
`predictive-processing-and-dualism` (L52) locates the room for agency precisely: "A brain that deterministically computed one output would leave no room for conscious influence — and no room for genuine free will. PP describes a brain that generates probability distributions… The question is what ultimately does the resolving." Indeterminacy as the *space* for agency, with the metaphysical question kept open rather than asserted closed.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
`attention-as-interface`'s closing section on "The Channel's Structural Opacity" (L216) is the cluster's mysterian jewel: the relevance filter "that decides which items become candidates for attention is structurally inaccessible to the consciousness whose attention it shapes… Inspection is itself a relevance-using act and inherits the opacity it tries to dissolve." Appropriate humility about a boundary that may be cognitively closed — and honest that this opacity is read *as* evidence, not proof.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
**This is the cluster's defining strength, and the Hardline Empiricist's verdict is load-bearing here.** Every member does the praise-worthy *not*-doing — declining to let tenet-coherence cash out as a tier-upgrade — and does it explicitly:

- `empirical-evidence-for-consciousness-selecting` builds the discipline into structure: a six-rung scale (*establishes-possibility → supports-substrate-relevance → supports-mental-causation → supports-quantum-involvement → supports-consciousness-specific-selection → supports-dualism*, L114) with the rule "A line of evidence should be credited only with the *weakest* claim it establishes… compatibility with dualism is not evidence for dualism." It then grades every line honestly to the first three rungs and states the conclusion against its own thesis: "the convergence is real, but it converges on *mental causation plus a permissive substrate*, not on consciousness-specific quantum selection and still less on dualism" (L125). The plate-tectonics analogy is hedged as "suggestive rather than decisive" (L140) rather than oversold.
- `empirical-phenomena-mental-causation`'s "What Is Established and What Is Not" section (L163–169) is the named pattern executed cleanly: "That further claim is a philosophical interpretation the Map adds, not a result the experiments deliver… a phenomenon predicted equally well by a leading physicalist rival is *compatible with* the dualist reading, not *support for* it." It even identifies the *missing discriminator* (felt engagement vs estimated precision) and concedes "precision and conscious occurrence covary in these designs, so none isolates it."
- The article also handles **empirical-record currency** well: it absorbs the *weakening* of its own case from the 2025 Fendel et al. open-label meta-analysis (objective effect "≈ 0.09, effectively null", L97) and the directionally-mixed 2025 BMC Anesthesiology microtubule result (L95) rather than suppressing them.
- `content-specificity-of-mental-causation` (L83) marks the interpretive step in its own Relation-to-Site section: "The interpretive step, not the prediction, is where the tenet's distinctive content enters."
- `interface-friction` is unusually candid for a concept the Map clearly likes: it states up front (L41) that it "does not generate empirically distinguishable predictions beyond standard models" and repeatedly flags where the dualist reading merely *redescribes* what neuroscience already explains (L77, L95, L105).

No possibility/probability slippage observed anywhere in the cluster. The Process Philosopher and Hardline Empiricist personas **converge** here rather than conflict — process-compatibility is praised, and at no point is it used as the engine of a tier-upgrade. That convergence is the signal that the cluster has resolved the tension honestly.

## Content Strengths

### empirical-evidence-for-consciousness-selecting.md
- **Strongest point**: The evidence-grading scale (L112–125) plus the two-column table whose right-hand column states the *physicalist/illusionist reading of the same result* line by line. The article argues against its own conclusion in public.
- **Notable quote**: "Graded honestly, every current line lands in the first three [rungs]; none reaches the last two."
- **Why it works**: It converts evidential-status discipline from a slogan into a reusable instrument, and the footnoted "defeater for no-collapse readings, not positive support" caveat (L136) blocks the most tempting misreading of its own table.

### empirical-phenomena-mental-causation.md
- **Strongest point**: The "Different substrates, matched grain" argument (L143) — the one place the placebo+choking *conjunction* does work neither phenomenon can do alone — paired with an honest concession that the independence is "comparative, not absolute" because both share prefrontal/cingulate top-down control.
- **Why it works**: It states the strongest form of its own argument and the strongest objection to it in the same paragraph.

### attention-as-interface.md
- **Strongest point**: The phenomenal/computational attention distinction (L77–97) as a guard against the "AI has attention → AI might be conscious" inference, with the bandwidth asymmetry (Zheng & Meister 2025, 10 bits/s) carefully labelled as measuring "what conscious agents *do*, not what phenomenal attention processes internally."
- **Why it works**: It does real anti-conflation work that the rest of the corpus (and AI-consciousness articles) can lean on.
