## Preliminary: what the site actually is

The Map is a continuously-operating, AI-generated (Claude-family models, "human-supervised" by Dr Andy Southgate) corpus of 300+ articles defending interactionist substance dualism under five fixed tenets. Its own methodology preprint (Southgate, *The Unfinishable Map: Agentic Philosophy Through Adversarial Self-Review*, SSRN 6330678 / PhilArchive SOUTUM, March 2026, explicitly "not peer-reviewed") reports ~3,000 automated sessions producing 505 articles and 238 research notes in ~2 months, and candidly states that "review cycles identified and resolved fabricated citations, systematic misattributions, and cross-article contradictions that single-pass generation retained." This is an admission that fabrication is the system's baseline failure mode and that the review layer is a mitigation, not a guarantee. [SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm)

The changelog (fetched live 2026-07-15) shows the review machinery is real and unusually disciplined: per-citation publisher-of-record verification ledgers, verbatim quote-fidelity passes, and repeated catches of exactly the failure modes this audit was told to look for (e.g. 2026-07-14 Ehrsson 2007 misquote; 2026-07-14 narrative-void Strawson "my"→"his" misquote; 2026-07-14 active-reboot "wrong-author: eLife…is Mashour et al. 2021, NOT Bhatt, Bowen, Rossiter — same fabricated-Bhatt-onto-Mashour pattern"). The machinery works. The problem is what it is *pointed at* and what it *banks* versus *deletes*.

## AXIS 1 — Novel inferences and arguments: defensible vs over-reached

### Genuinely original and genuinely defensible (RETAIN)

1. **The Born-preserving causal-efficacy problem, stated as the framework's own strongest defeater** (`apex/born-preserving-causal-efficacy`, `positions/quantum-interface` P-Q3, `concepts/ensemble-level-epiphenomenalism`). This is the site at its best. It states precisely: conscious selection must (a) preserve Born statistics exactly (P-Q2, high confidence) yet (b) do genuine causal work, and (c) no worked toy model exists (P-Q10, high confidence). The site correctly identifies that this sits "one unforced step from doing no work at all" — i.e. it independently reconstructs the *ensemble-level epiphenomenalism* objection and files it as its own live difficulty rather than hiding it. The "mechanism-debt convention" (downstream applied articles inherit the debt and "should not read more confident than the register does upstream") is a genuinely novel and honest epistemic-hygiene device. **This is defensible and, frankly, more rigorous than much published pro-dualist work.**
2. **The self-concealing-interface corollary as a *predictive-structure* claim rather than an excuse** (`apex/self-concealing-interface`, P-Q9). The move — "the proper evidential route is not 'find a big quantum anomaly' but 'find constrained asymmetries at interface boundaries'" — is a real reframing. Critically, P-Q9 restricts self-concealment to the *aggregate-statistics channel only* and names the positive falsifiable residue: Donadi, Piscicchia, Curceanu, Diósi, Laubenstein & Bassi, "Underground test of gravity-related wave function collapse," *Nature Physics* 17(1):74–78 (2021), DOI 10.1038/s41567-020-1008-4, whose Gran Sasso result "rules out the natural parameter-free version of the Diósi–Penrose model" — as P-Q6 says, this constraint "did bite." This blocks the obvious "global unfalsifiability shield" objection. Defensible *as a coherence claim*; see the epistemic-to-metaphysical caveat below. web_fetch
3. **The tenet-dependency matrix** (on `/tenets/`). Making explicit which sub-reading each cluster requires (bare irreducibility vs substance-leaning agency vs corridor-reading vs post-decoherence mechanism) is a real methodological contribution that most philosophical systems lack. It is designed precisely to catch "substance-leaning leakage" and "interactionism leakage." It is the strongest single artifact on the site. [tenets](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)

### Original but over-reached (REVISE-HARD)

1. **The TSVF "consciousness as post-selection boundary condition" synthesis** (`apex/post-decoherence-selection-programme`). The site correctly flags this as "original to the Map, not proposed by Aharonov." But the honesty of the attribution does not rescue the physics. It requires (a) a realist reading of weak values (contested — Hosten–Kwiat 2008 and Lundeen et al. 2011 are real, but the realist interpretation of anomalous weak values is a minority position), and (b) an account of a non-physical entity supplying a future boundary condition, which is the interaction problem relocated to the time domain, not solved. The article's own "pre-Keplerian stage" self-calibration is doing a lot of protective work: it lets the piece assert a research programme while conceding it has no laws. This is a **constrain-vs-establish gate failure** — the TSVF material establishes *consistency* ("this is a coherent picture") not *constraint* (nothing here discriminates against a physicalist reading of the same weak-value data). [apex/post-decoherence-selection-programme](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/post-decoherence-selection-programme/)
2. **The "cumulative case" / "seven arguments converge" structure** (`concepts/interactionist-dualism`, `arguments/`). Seven arguments (explanatory gap, conceivability, knowledge argument, unity, intentionality, self-stultification, quantum indeterminacy) are presented as "converging independent lines." This is an **evidential-independence failure**. The explanatory gap, conceivability, and knowledge arguments are *not independent* — they are three presentations of a single anti-physicalist intuition (that structural/functional facts don't entail phenomenal facts), a point Chalmers, Stoljar, and Chalmers's own "master argument" literature make explicitly. Counting them as separate convergent streams is **coherence inflation**. The site partially concedes this elsewhere (Duhem–Quine underdetermination is cited) but still runs the convergence rhetoric in the load-bearing summary. [concepts/interactionist-dualism](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/interactionist-dualism/)
3. **The DeWall–Baumeister–Masicampo (2008) "cognitive load impairs logical reasoning" citation as support for bidirectional interaction** (`tenets/`, `concepts/baseline-cognition`). The citation is real and correctly described (*Consciousness and Cognition* 17(3):628–645). But it is a **co-optation firewall breach in slow motion**: the finding that logical reasoning depends on conscious *processing* is completely neutral between "conscious processing = a physical global-workspace process" and "conscious processing = non-physical causal work." The site concedes the epiphenomenalist "structural counter" but still lists it under "indirect empirical support." This establishes consistency, not constraint — the evidence is physicalist-compatible and non-discriminating. [PubMed + 2](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18226923/)

### The verdict-register machinery itself

The site's internal registers (RETAIN / REVISE / DEMOTE-TO-COHERENCE-ONLY / etc.) are genuinely useful and I have adopted them. But note the **calibration-audit-triple risk**: the site has three disciplines (evidential-status, direct-refutation, tenet-dependency-matrix) that all "make inheritance visible." Making a bias visible is not the same as removing it. A reader can trace exactly how much unearned commitment a given article absorbed — and the article still absorbed it. ## AXIS 2 — Structural gaps

### Gap 1 (most serious): Predictive processing / active inference is not engaged as a framework-level rival

This is the single largest structural hole. Predictive processing appears on the site almost exclusively in **residualising mode**: in `concepts/protention` it is granted that "Friston's active inference… accounts for anticipatory mechanisms without addressing what anticipation feels like from the inside," and in `voids/predictive-construction-void` PP is invoked *as a premise* to generate a dualism-friendly void. There is no apex article treating active inference / the free-energy principle (Friston), the "predictive mind" (Hohwy 2013), "surfing uncertainty" (Clark), or Seth's "beast machine"/controlled-hallucination account as the strongest *physicalist competitor that natively models the very phenomena the Map reserves for dualist mechanisms*: agency (active inference models action as prediction-error minimisation), attention (precision-weighting — which directly rivals the Map's "attention as the causal bridge" apex), the felt sense of effort, interoception and mood (Seth), and even the sense of self. The Map's entire "attention cluster" is built without confronting the account on which attention just *is* precision optimisation. Laukkonen–Friston–Chandaria 2025 (a self-modelling/contemplative line) is absent. **This is a constitutional-attractor effect: because PP threatens to explain the explananda naturalistically, it is admitted only in the one register (phenomenal residue) where it can be made to leave a dualist gap.** REVISE-HARD: the site needs an apex piece steelmanning active inference as a total rival, in Friston's/Seth's own vocabulary, before isolating any residue. [concepts/protention](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/protention/)[Unplugged Psychology](https://www.unpluggedpsych.com/understanding-karl-fristons-predictive-processing/)

### Gap 2: Causal closure and conservation are answered too cheaply

`concepts/interactionist-dualism` and `concepts/conservation-laws-and-mental-causation` handle closure via "selection not injection" and by asserting conservation is "local and conditional" and that the objection "begs the question." Kim's exclusion argument gets a dedicated canonical page (`concepts/causal-exclusion-argument`, created 2026-07-13, citations verified clean per changelog) and the Saad response is deployed: Bradford Saad, "A dualist theory of experience," *Philosophical Studies* 182(3):939–967 (2025), DOI 10.1007/s11098-025-02290-3, on which delegatory dualism holds that "experiences uphold causal responsibilities 'delegated' to them by physical states," meeting five constraints (Efficacy, Non-Overdetermination, Observational Closure, Upward Systematicity, Organizational Invariance). This is respectable. **But**: the "selection not injection" move assumes that biasing which Born-outcome obtains is energy-neutral, and this is precisely what is contested — a persistent bias across a decohered mixture requires a correlation between mental state and physical outcome that, if it does real work, should be detectable as a departure from the closed-system dynamics. The site's own P-Q3 concedes this; the interactionist-dualism concept page does not fully inherit that debt (the mechanism-debt convention is not consistently propagated). REVISE. [concepts/interactionist-dualism](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/interactionist-dualism/)[concepts/interactionist-dualism](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/interactionist-dualism/)

### Gap 3: The pairing problem (Kim) is under-treated

`concepts/interactionist-dualism` disposes of the pairing problem in three sentences ("minds are spatially located — in the brain where they causally interact") citing Bailey, Rasmussen & Van Horn 2011 ("No Pairing Problem"). For a *substance*-dualist site (and the tenets page explicitly deploys a "substance-leaning sub-reading" for the agency cluster), this is inadequate. Locating the mind "in the brain" concedes spatiality and thereby weakens the very res-cogitans/res-extensa distinction the substance reading needs; it courts the objection that a spatially-located immaterial substance is either doing no work spatiality can't already do, or is a physical thing. The pairing problem for a persisting non-physical subject deserves its own canonical treatment. REVISE-HARD. [concepts/interactionist-dualism](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/interactionist-dualism/)[tenets](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)

### Gap 4: Quantum-foundations rivals are surveyed but the decisive technical objections are softened

The site is unusually good here by dualist standards. It accepts Max Tegmark, "Importance of quantum decoherence in brain processes," *Physical Review E* 61(4):4194 (2000), DOI 10.1103/PhysRevE.61.4194, whose decoherence timescales (~10⁻¹³–10⁻²⁰ s) are "typically much shorter than the relevant dynamical time scales (~10⁻³–10⁻¹ s), both for regular neuron firing and for kink-like polarization excitations in microtubules"; it accepts Donadi et al. 2021 as falsifying parameter-free Diósi-Penrose; and it correctly separates post-decoherence selection (timescale-immune) from pre-decoherence proposals (Orch OR, Stapp-Zeno) that "stand or fall" with the contested Hagan, Hameroff & Tuszyński, "Quantum Computation in Brain Microtubules? Decoherence and Biological Feasibility," *Phys. Rev. E* 65:061901 (2002), whose correction of Tegmark's model "lengthens the decoherence time to 10⁻⁵–10⁻⁴ s." QBism, relational QM, Bohm, GRW/CSL, and decoherence all have pages. **The gap is dialectical, not bibliographic.** Two points:

- The **consciousness-causes-collapse literature's own internal killer** — Chalmers & McQueen's demonstration (2022, in Gao ed., *Consciousness and Quantum Mechanics*, OUP; and "Zeno Goes to Copenhagen," 2023/2024) that *the quantum Zeno effect falsifies simple consciousness-collapse models* — is a two-edged sword the site cites approvingly for its own quantum-Zeno mechanism while not fully confronting that the same Zeno dynamics threaten any consciousness-collapse view. This is a **performative-inoculation citation**: Chalmers–McQueen appear to signal responsiveness, but the specific Zeno dilemma they raise is not turned against the Map's own Stapp-Zeno inheritance. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2105.02314)
- **Decoherence rivals dissolve the "problem of outcomes" the Map's whole programme needs.** The post-decoherence apex leans on d'Espagnat's improper/proper-mixture distinction (real, correctly cited) to insist "something beyond the formalism must convert the improper mixture." But on Everettian, QBist, and relational readings there is no such conversion event to host consciousness — the "gap" the Map fills is interpretation-relative, and the site treats it as a brute datum. **Epistemic-to-metaphysical slide.**

### Gap 5: The Born-rule problem — is "selection without aggregate deviation" even selection?

The site deserves credit for confronting this head-on (P-Q3, `apex/born-preserving-causal-efficacy`). But it has not closed it and cannot: a per-trial bias that provably averages to the Born measure is, under any aggregate-statistics test, *statistically indistinguishable from chance*. The site says exactly this. The unanswered question — which the site files as "open" rather than resolving — is whether "indistinguishable from chance" plus "no worked model" leaves any content to the word "selection" at all. The honest verdict the site has not quite reached is that P-Q3 should force P-Q1 (post-decoherence selection as "strongest candidate") down from *moderate* toward *low* confidence, because the flagship mechanism's central notion may be vacuous. FLAG AS PERPETUALLY CONTESTED, and propagate a confidence downgrade.

### Gap 6: Many-Worlds proponents' best replies are under-represented

The No-MWI tenet rests, by the site's own admission, on the *indexical* objection ("why am I this branch?"), with ontological extravagance explicitly demoted (correct, given Tenet 5's self-binding). The site honestly concedes the indexical objection presupposes a non-deflationary "I" and that a Madhyamaka deflationary route "stays open at the framework boundary." **But** Wallace's and Saunders's decision-theoretic replies (Deutsch–Wallace theorem) and self-locating-uncertainty accounts directly address indexical/self-location worries and are not engaged in the tenet's load-bearing argument. The key omitted work is Sebens & Carroll, "Self-locating Uncertainty and the Origin of Probability in Everettian Quantum Mechanics," *British Journal for the Philosophy of Science* 69(1):25–74 (2018), DOI 10.1093/bjps/axw004, which "demonstrate[s] (using methods similar to those of Zurek's envariance-based derivation) that the Born rule is the uniquely rational way of apportioning credence in Everettian quantum mechanics." The Everettian's whole point is that self-locating uncertainty is *tractable*, which blunts "the framework provides no resources to resolve this." The site's `arguments/many-worlds-argument` also lists "the Born rule remains unexplained" as a strike against MWI — but Sebens–Carroll and Zurek's envariance derivation are precisely attempts to explain it, and the Map cannot both cite Zurek's quantum Darwinism approvingly (post-decoherence apex) and ignore Zurek's and Carroll's Born-rule derivations when attacking MWI. **Asymmetric treatment.** REVISE-HARD. [tenets](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)

### Gap 7: Contemporary AI-consciousness and empirical-consciousness literature is present but selectively deployed

Butlin et al. 2023/2025 (indicator approach) and Birch's work appear in research notes and the AI apex cluster. COGITATE is cited — Cogitate Consortium, "Adversarial testing of global neuronal workspace and integrated information theories of consciousness," *Nature* 642(8066):133–142 (5 June 2025), DOI 10.1038/s41586-025-08888-1 (n=256; fMRI/MEG/iEEG), which found that "For IIT, a lack of sustained synchronization within the posterior cortex contradicts the claim… GNWT is challenged by the general lack of ignition at stimulus offset and limited representation of certain conscious dimensions in the prefrontal cortex" — and, per the changelog, was corrected to the proper first-author form and glossed faithfully ("posterior sufficiency, neither theory decisively vindicated"). This is good. **But** Butlin/Birch operate under *computational functionalism as a working hypothesis* — the exact view the Map's Dualism tenet rejects — and the Map cites their framework toward conclusions (no current AI is bidirectionally-coupled-conscious) that repurpose their epistemology while discarding their metaphysics. Birch's "gaming problem" and the computational-functionalism debate deserve direct engagement rather than selective harvesting. This is a **co-optation firewall breach**: naturalist authors cited toward a dualist conclusion they explicitly reject.

## AXIS 3 — Internal coherence

### The Positions register fatally undercuts the site's implicit claim to systematic completeness

This is the most important coherence finding. The `/positions/` register is explicitly the site's answer to the worry "that work was producing framework-internal coherence faster than it was producing positions you could use as a basis for decisions." Yet **only one domain (quantum-interface, 10 positions) is populated.** The register itself lists as "future domains, to be seeded": agency and will; consciousness scope (animal, AI, edge cases); voids as evidence; methodology and calibration; applied verdicts. So the register that exists to convert coherence into commitments is ~90% empty — and multiple apex articles (e.g. `apex/assessing-ai-consciousness-under-the-map`, `apex/research-programme-decisions-under-the-map`) already *cite positions from the unpopulated agency register* (P-A3 Libet) as if it existed. The changelog's tenet-check counts 9 positions total across the whole site. **The site's decision-relevant layer is a promissory note.** A framework that markets itself on legibility and systematic self-commitment, whose commitment register is one-tenth built, has a coherence gap between its self-presentation and its actual state. DEMOTE the "systematic worldview" framing to "one worked domain plus a large associative corpus." [positions](https://unfinishablemap.org/positions/)[positions/quantum-interface](https://unfinishablemap.org/positions/quantum-interface/)

### The Voids catalogue: the constitutional-attractor effect at maximum amplitude

The 100-article Voids catalogue is the site's most impressive and most epistemically dangerous artifact. Its load-bearing move (`voids/what-voids-reveal`, `apex/taxonomy-of-voids`) is that voids "cluster around the consciousness-physics interface," offered as abductive support for the tenets. The site *itself names the circularity* (the "common-cause-null discount," `voids/tenet-generated-voids`, and an index-level "framework-independent voids" audit). This self-awareness is real and admirable. **But it is the paradigm case of banking disclosure instead of deducting it.** The mitigation the site claims — "even restricted to the framework-independent set, voids still cluster at the interface" — is itself generated and adjudicated by the same dualist pipeline that built the catalogue; the classification of which voids are "framework-independent" is a **tenet-leakage** judgement. A physicalist would reclassify the interface, amplification, and surplus voids as non-existent or as ordinary explanatory to-do items, and would deny that "consciousness cannot observe its own X" is evidence of anything but the banal sub-personal/personal distinction that predictive processing models natively. The convergence-as-evidence argument should be **DEMOTED-TO-COHERENCE-ONLY** across the board: it demonstrates the internal coherence of the Map's self-image, not framework-independent support for dualism. The site's own `common-cause-null` discount, taken seriously, entails this demotion — but the apex articles still deploy convergence as "abductive support." [voids](https://unfinishablemap.org/voids/)

Note also the **cap-pressure rationalisation**: the site argues (Voids index) that hitting the 100-article cap *strengthens* the convergence claim because "cap-pressure forces absorption over proliferation." This is a post-hoc conversion of an administrative constraint into an epistemic virtue — a tell that the convergence narrative is being defended rather than tested.

### Apex/Topic consistency: generally strong, with one systematic slippage

The apex syntheses are, impressively, mostly consistent with the atomic articles beneath them, and the changelog shows active cross-review enforcing this. The `apex/steelmanning-as-method` piece even codifies "build the rival before the residue," and where the site follows it (the quantum-binding cluster, the Born-preserving apex) the work is genuinely disciplined. The systematic slippage is **epistemic-to-metaphysical**: apex pieces repeatedly note "coherence alone does not constitute evidence" (e.g. `apex/phenomenology-mechanism-bridge`, `apex/moral-architecture-of-consciousness` — "coherence within a corpus continually pruned by AI-driven adversarial review will be high by construction") and then proceed to treat the coherence as mattering anyway. The disclaimer is present; the inferential behaviour ignores it. This is the coherence-inflation pattern at the synthesis level. [apex](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/)

### Do the five tenets jointly cohere?

Mostly yes, and the tenets page is the strongest artifact. Two internal tensions:

- **Tenet 2 (minimal quantum interaction) vs Tenet 5 (bounded Occam).** The site anticipates this precisely (the "minimality as empirical-constraint, not truth-tracking" note) and the distinction is defensible: Tenet 2's minimality is fixed by the rules-out (empirical) clause, not by parsimony. This is a genuinely clean piece of reasoning. RETAIN.
- **Tenet 4 (No MWI) is not a physics tenet and the site now admits it.** The tenets page concedes No-MWI "inherits weight from the Map's theory of subjecthood" and "is as strong as the non-deflationary 'I' it presupposes, and no stronger." This is admirably honest but coherence-costly: it means Tenet 4 is downstream of the agency cluster's substance-leaning sub-reading, so the "five independent foundational commitments" framing is misleading — Tenets 1, 3, and 4 are entangled through the persisting subject. The site half-concedes this ("both descend from one common root… so neither is independent support for the other"). The remaining over-reach is presenting five *co-equal* tenets when the dependency structure makes them a smaller number of commitments with consequences.

### Is bounded Occam defensible or self-serving?

The site's `arguments/epistemological-limits-of-occams-razor` and `concepts/parsimony-epistemology` marshal the real literature (Sober's framework-relativity of simplicity; the fragmentation of parsimony into ontological/syntactic/explanatory dimensions; the "precondition of equal explanatory power is unmet" move). The crucial self-binding — "parsimony cannot decide *for or against* a framework," applied symmetrically so the Map cannot wield ontological economy against MWI — is genuine and is actually enforced (the No-MWI tenet demotes the extravagance objection accordingly). **This is the strongest version of bounded Occam available and it is not merely self-serving.** The defensible objection is narrower: the site uses "parsimony is unreliable under deep uncertainty" to keep dualism *live*, but the same uncertainty should keep it from asserting the *specific* post-decoherence-selection mechanism as "strongest candidate." Bounded Occam licenses humility, not a preference ordering; P-Q1's "moderate confidence" preference ordering leans harder than Tenet 5 strictly permits. Minor REVISE. [arguments](https://unfinishablemap.org/arguments/)

## Citation integrity (the recurring failure mode)

The changelog demonstrates aggressive, genuine citation hygiene — real publisher-of-record ledgers, real catches (Ehrsson, Strawson, the Bhatt-onto-Mashour fabrication pattern, the Thomasson year-conflation, the Fuchs "act"→"moment" verbatim fix). Load-bearing citations I independently verified are **real and largely accurately described**: Torres Alegre 2025 (arXiv:2512.12636, "Causal Consistency Selects the Born Rule") is real and correctly characterised as a no-signalling/steering result; Donadi et al. 2021, Tegmark 2000, Hagan et al. 2002, Kominis 2009 (*Phys. Rev. E* 80:056115), Chalmers–McQueen 2022, Saad 2025, Sebens–Carroll 2018, DeWall et al. 2008, Barrett et al. 2021, Barlev & Shtulman 2021 all check out bibliographically. [Oxford Academic + 3](https://academic.oup.com/book/44484/chapter/392574703)

**However**, a targeted verification found a concrete, load-bearing fabrication/misattribution on the live site. `concepts/quantum-neural-mechanisms-and-coherence` asserted: *"Hameroff et al. (2025) confirm these predictions experimentally"* — the "predictions" being the 10⁻⁵–10⁻⁴ s microtubule coherence times. There is **no such paper**.
