## Dimension 1 — Empirical Accuracy and Currency

**Verdict: Citations are individually accurate, but the evidential picture is stale and selectively curated; two fields are misrepresented and one (attention) is barely engaged.**

**Blindsight.** Phillips (2021), *Psychological Review* 128(3):558–584, is cited correctly and used appropriately to resist the "unconscious vision" reading. But the article presents the "qualitatively degraded conscious vision" (QDC) thesis as if settled when it is live and sharply contested *in the same journal issue*: Michel & Lau, "Is blindsight possible under signal detection theory? Comment on Phillips (2021)," *Psychological Review* 128(3):585–591, and Phillips's rejoinder, "Bias and blindsight: A reply to Michel and Lau (2021)," 128(3):592–595. Michel & Lau's core point — that residual awareness cannot account for the feature-specific discrimination and that performance-matching undercuts the response-bias story — is precisely the live dispute the article suppresses. The piece also ignores the neural-pathway literature entirely (superior colliculus–pulvinar; koniocellular and geniculo-extrastriate routes; the type-1/type-2 distinction), which is the physicalist mechanistic account: e.g., Ajina et al. (eLife 2015) show blindsight-positive patients have an intact geniculo-extrastriate (LGN→hMT+) pathway, and Kinoshita et al. (Nature Communications 2018) show SC→ventrolateral-pulvinar inactivation impairs blind-field saccades. **This is a documented empirical-currency gap plus a failure to flag a perpetually contested claim — "flag as perpetually contested" is the correct remediation.**

**Attentional failure.** The article rests an entire section ("Priority preservation") on what is recognisably perceptual-load territory, yet engages *none* of the canonical attention literature by name — not Mack & Rock or Simons & Chabris on inattentional blindness, not the attentional blink, not change blindness, and not Lavie's load theory. This matters because Lavie's perceptual-load model (Cartwright-Finch & Lavie 2007, *Cognition*; Lavie's "Blinded by the load" review) gives a fully physicalist account of exactly the "which information crosses first / is consciously available" phenomenon the article attributes to interface prioritisation. Omitting the rival that pre-empts your section is a constrain-vs-establish failure dressed up as the absence of a problem.

**Blur.** Both anchor citations are turned against their own authors — the signature co-optation move. Skrzypulec (2021), *Philosophical Studies* 178:3271–3289, states that his interoceptive theory "is consistent with the representationalist thesis that phenomenal character supervenes on representational content and with the strong transparency thesis formulated in terms of mind-independentness" — i.e., it is built *to domesticate blur for physicalism*. The article asserts the opposite: that interoception "far from domesticating blur for physicalism, doubles down on the hard problem." That directly inverts the cited author's stated conclusion. Martina (2024), *Erkenntnis* 89:3229–3246 ("Lessons from Blur," author Giulia Martina, Nottingham), is an avowedly relationalist, pluralist, naturalist treatment whose thesis is that *no unified phenomenal-remainder account is needed*; it is recruited as if it supported an irreducible-phenomenal-remainder claim. Separately, the article never engages the obvious deflationary mechanisms (we don't notice retinal blur, blind-spot filling-in, peripheral crowding, perceptual constancy) that make "we don't notice our own blur" a subpersonal-processing fact rather than interface evidence. [Springer](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11098-021-01601-8)[PhilArchive](https://philarchive.org/rec/MARLFB-4)

**Altered states / psychedelics.** This is the article's worst empirical misrepresentation, and its own linked sibling page ("psychedelics-and-the-filter-model") makes the co-optation explicit. The article frames psychedelics as: reduced DMN activity → expanded experience → filter removed. The actual neuroscience is the opposite shape. Carhart-Harris et al. (2012), "Neural correlates of the psychedelic state as determined by fMRI studies with psilocybin," *PNAS* 109(6):2138–2143 (DOI 10.1073/pnas.1119598109; 15 experienced users, IV ~2 mg psilocybin) reports "decreases in regional cerebral blood flow and BOLD signal, with strongest effects in anterior cingulate cortex/medial prefrontal cortex, posterior cingulate cortex, and thalamus," plus reduced within-DMN connectivity. But the consciousness-relevant variable in the authors' own theory is *entropy*, which goes **up**: Carhart-Harris (2018), "The entropic brain – revisited," *Neuropharmacology* 142:167–178 (PMID 29548884), states that "the entropy of spontaneous brain activity indexes the informational richness of conscious states" — a monotonic *positive* mapping (richer experience ↔ richer neural dynamics), corroborated by EEG/MEG Lempel-Ziv complexity increases (Schartner, Carhart-Harris, Barrett, Seth & Muthukumaraswamy 2017, *Scientific Reports*) that *decrease* in states lacking subjective experience. **The "less brain, more mind" framing cherry-picks the 2012 blood-flow decrease and silently drops the entropy increase that is the theoretical core — a production/identity claim, the exact opposite of a filter being removed.** The decreases that do occur are in *constraining* hub regions (DMN), which *enable* the entropy rise ("unconstrained cognition"); they are not "more consciousness from less brain." [clinicaltrials + 4](https://cdn.clinicaltrials.gov/large-docs/93/NCT02061293/Prot_SAP_000.pdf)

Minor: the Overgaard et al. (2006), *Consciousness and Cognition* 15(4):700–708 datum ("fully conscious, two degrees of degraded consciousness, or not conscious") is real and on-point, but is presented as a verbatim quotation when it reads as a paraphrase of the study's graded-PAS scale; verify wording against the original. Picard & Craig (2009), *Epilepsy & Behavior* 16(3):539–546, and van Lommel et al. (2001), *The Lancet* 358:2039–2045, are cited correctly — but note van Lommel is an interested party (his own interpretation is non-local-consciousness), and NDE-during-cardiac-arrest "heightened clarity" reports are retrospective and timing-uncertain; presenting them as interface evidence without that caveat is evidential-status inflation. [Substack](https://ndelibrary.substack.com/p/near-death-experience-in-survivors)

## Dimension 2 — Validity of the Inference to an Interface/Filter Model

**Verdict: Overreach. The phenomena do not discriminate between dualism and physicalism; the "interface" is a constitutional-attractor artifact, and the central terms beg the question.**

**Constrain-vs-establish gate.** Graceful degradation, priority preservation, compensatory generation, and graded loss are each predicted by a standard physicalist account: the brain *is* the perceptual system; distributed parallel networks degrade gracefully by architecture (the article concedes this); evolutionary redundancy explains priority; release-from-inhibition explains Charles Bonnet and phosphenes. The article admits each individually, then claims the *conjunction* "fits more naturally with an interface." That is coherence inflation, not discrimination: "fits more naturally" ≠ "is predicted only by." A common-cause null — one neural architecture producing all four signatures — accounts for the conjunction with no interface, and the article never names it.

**Question-begging in the load-bearing terms.** Describing a processing bottleneck as a "bandwidth limit of an interface," a "transmission channel," or "channel capacity" smuggles the conclusion into the description. These are interface-presupposing redescriptions of data that are equally (more parsimoniously) describable as limits of neural processing. The "blur paradox" — "worse perception of the world yields better perception of what perception is" — is an artifact of conflating the personal level (what experience is like) with the subpersonal level (how the visual system processes signals). Once those levels are separated, the paradox dissolves: noticing blur is the visual system representing its own state (Skrzypulec's actual, physicalist point), not a window onto a far-side phenomenal medium.

**Homunculus regress.** The transmission model posits a consciousness "on the far side" that *receives* the degraded signal. The article never asks who or what this recipient is, or whether it has its own perceptual interface (and so on). The "selective-correction-and-reconstruction-paradox" passage explicitly "presupposes a recipient whose experience the curation shapes" — that presupposition is the regress, asserted as evidence.

**Constitutional-attractor effect.** The "interface architecture" the article claims to *discover* in the failure modes is exactly what Tenet 1 (irreducibility) plus Tenet 3 (bidirectional interaction) *entail* at the outset. The perceptual data are then read as revealing it. This is tenet leakage presented as empirical discovery — the precise pattern the site's own tenet-dependency matrix and substance-leaning audit are meant to catch.

**Credit where due.** The "Gradation Argument" section applies the constrain-vs-establish gate honestly: it concedes "the continuum itself … does not adjudicate between the interface reading and its deflationary rivals," that the data are "compatible with either framing," and that what they constrain is "the threshold-emergence picture specifically." That is genuine calibration. But it is *localised and non-propagating*: the surrounding sections overclaim freely, so the disciplined paragraph functions as performative inoculation — one honest concession licensing looser argumentation elsewhere.

## Dimension 3 — Coherence with the Five Tenets

**Verdict: The filter framing is in unacknowledged tension with interactionism; Minimal Quantum Interaction is left dangling; bounded Occam is invoked as a shield rather than honoured as a constraint.**

The Huxley/Bergson filter / "reducing valve" model — which the sibling page endorses and this article leans on in "Selective Preservation and the Filter" — is a model in which the brain *limits/reduces* a richer, pre-existing consciousness ("Mind at Large"). That is a **transmissive/permissive** relation, structurally closer to idealism or cosmic-consciousness monism than to the site's stated **interactionist substance dualism**, in which mind and brain are *distinct relata in bidirectional causal commerce*. If consciousness pre-exists and the brain merely filters it, the Bidirectional Interaction tenet has little to grip: there is no obvious causal *upload* from a filtered-down stream. The article half-notices this ("The Map does not commit to pure filter theory… the interface likely both transmits and shapes") but never resolves the tension — it wants the rhetorical force of the filter (expansion-under-degradation) without paying its metaphysical cost.

**Minimal Quantum Interaction** does no work here. The "Relation to Site Perspective" gesture that the "number, quality, and coherence of available quantum events may set the bandwidth ceiling" is unsupported, untested, and dangling — a tenet name-checked to satisfy the house template, not an argument. Per the tenets page's own "empirical-corridor vs mechanism-specific" distinction, this article invokes a mechanism-specific reading it has not earned.

**Bounded Occam** is violated in spirit. The interface, the far-side consciousness, and the transmission channel are three multiplied entities introduced without the discriminating evidence the tenet's *symmetric self-binding* demands. The article pre-empts the parsimony objection by invoking "Occam's Razor Has Limits" — but that tenet binds the Map's *own* arguments too ("any Map argument that leans on parsimony as if this tenet did not apply to it"). Here Tenet 5 is deployed as a shield against a parsimony objection while the underlying multiplication goes unjustified — the asymmetry the tenets page explicitly forbids. ## Dimension 4 — The Strongest Physicalist Counter-Reading It Fails to Address

**Verdict: The article never engages predictive processing / active inference — the one rival that co-opts its entire evidence base and dissolves the interface.**

On the Bayesian / predictive-processing view (Clark, *Surfing Uncertainty* 2015 and *The Experience Machine* 2023; Hohwy; Friston's free-energy principle; Seth's *Being You* and "controlled hallucination"), perception is a hierarchical generative model — "controlled hallucination" — and *every* phenomenon the article cites falls out with no interface and no recipient:

- **Blur** = altered precision-weighting on a degraded/self-monitoring channel;
- **Blindsight** = low precision on a damaged forward pathway;
- **Attentional load / inattentional blindness** = precision/resource allocation (Lavie);
- **Psychedelics** = relaxed high-level priors — REBUS: Carhart-Harris & Friston (2019), "REBUS and the Anarchic Brain," *Pharmacological Reviews* 71(3):316–344, in which "psychedelics work to relax the precision of high-level priors or beliefs, thereby liberating bottom-up information flow, particularly via intrinsic sources such as the limbic system." [UCL Discovery](https://discovery.ucl.ac.uk/id/eprint/10077110/)

**REBUS co-opts the very "filter" intuition the article relies on and gives it a fully naturalist, physicalist mechanism** — relaxed priors within a hierarchical Bayesian brain, founded (the abstract says so) on integrating the free-energy principle with the entropic brain hypothesis, both "quantifiable measures from information theory that are apt for empirical study." This is the load-bearing counter-reading, and it is absent. [Pharmacological Reviews](https://pharmrev.aspetjournals.org/article/S0031-6997(24)01296-1/fulltext)

**Co-optation firewall breach (the central integrity finding).** Anil Seth, Andy Clark, Jakob Hohwy, Karl Friston, and Robin Carhart-Harris are committed physicalists/naturalists. Seth treats selfhood as a "useful illusion" and explicitly rejects dualist readings of volition; Clark, even when flirting with "a grain of truth in dualism," recommends an *illusionist* stance to the hard-problem residue, not substance dualism. Yet the article's sibling page (linked from "Selective Preservation") conscripts REBUS and the entropic brain toward a dualist filter conclusion their authors build the opposite model to avoid. The firewall the site already runs for McGinn and Schwitzgebel must extend to this roster, and this article (plus its sibling) is where the conscription is happening. [Substack](https://booksandnotes.substack.com/p/book-reviews-anil-seth-being-you)[Edge.org](https://www.edge.org/conversation/andy_clark-perception-as-controlled-hallucination)

Adjacent rivals also go unaddressed: **global-workspace** theory deflates the "what survives degradation / priority" and "compensatory generation" points (access vs availability); **illusionist / higher-order** readings deflate the "transparency failure" point (the felt haziness is a representational/control state, not a phenomenal medium). Tellingly, the article's own "Neural Network Objection" section concedes the release-phenomena explanation *works* for Charles Bonnet, then retreats to "but it doesn't explain why that firing *feels like* seeing a detailed face." That is the hard problem restated — a Mode-Three framework-boundary point — not interface evidence. Restating the hard problem is not the same as exhibiting an interface.

## Dimension 5 — Overall Citation-Integrity Assessment

**Verdict: Metadata-clean, interpretation-dirty.**

Unusually for this corpus — whose changelog documents recurring fabrication (the bogus "Pereboom & Frankish 2021," the fabricated "Pautz 2019," the Barrett "eight vs six," the Coates author/venue errors) — **every citation in this article checks out on author, journal, year, and pages.** Carhart-Harris et al. 2012 (PNAS 109(6):2138–2143), Harman 1990 (*Philosophical Perspectives* 4:31–52), Hoffman, Singh & Prakash 2015 (*Psychonomic Bulletin & Review* 22(6):1480–1506; the "veridical perceptions … routinely dominated by nonveridical strategies tuned to fitness" quotation is verbatim and correctly attributed), Martina 2023/2024 (*Erkenntnis*), Overgaard et al. 2006, Phillips 2021, Picard & Craig 2009, Skrzypulec 2021 (*Philosophical Studies* 178:3271–3289), and van Lommel et al. 2001 all verify. No fabricated co-authorships, no invented venues. [Springer](https://link.springer.com/article/10.3758/s13423-015-0890-8)

The integrity failure is therefore *not* fabrication but **systematic co-optation** (Skrzypulec and Martina inverted; Hoffman partially; REBUS/entropic-brain via the sibling) compounded by **empirical-currency gaps** (no Michel & Lau, no SC/pulvinar pathway work, no predictive processing, no load theory). The Hoffman handling is the one honest exception — the article flags his idealist conclusion and marks the Map's divergence cleanly, which is the model the rest of the article should follow.

**Architectural vs first-order separation.** As an *architectural/methodological* artifact, the article shows real local discipline (the gradation section's constrain-vs-establish calibration; the honest Hoffman divergence note; the explicit "does not prove filter theory" hedges). As *first-order philosophy*, the central inference does not survive scrutiny: the cited data are non-discriminating, the load-bearing rival is unaddressed, the key empirical claim about psychedelics is inverted, and the filter framing is incoherent with the site's own interactionism. The architecture is better than the content it carries.
