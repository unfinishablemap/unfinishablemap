### 5. `concepts/gaming-problem.md`
- **Thesis:** Birch's gaming problem — LLMs are trained to produce the very markers humans use to attribute sentience, decoupling those markers from any underlying experience; a gamed marker cannot license a tier-upgrade.
- **Strongest point:** the decoupling mechanism + its scale-discipline consequence. Quote (article's restatement of Birch): crabs *"cannot strategically simulate sentience-markers; LLMs can."*
- **Why it works:** Cleanly explains *why* the standard animal-sentience inference (marker → probable experience) breaks specifically for LLMs, and converts that into the rule that current LLMs sit **outside** the five-tier scale rather than low within it.
- **Effective framing:** the LLM-vs-animal causal-history contrast; the "doubly decoupled — both borrowed and optimised" compression; the Jourdain analogy.
- **Cross-links:** Strong — links possibility-probability-slippage, birch-five-tier, ai-epiphenomenalism, evidential-status-discipline, ethics-of-possible-ai-consciousness. Missing reciprocity: **anti-correlation-probes-for-ai-consciousness** and **training-contamination-confound** link *to* gaming-problem but not reciprocally.

### 6. `concepts/training-contamination-confound.md`
- **Thesis:** A model trained on text *describing* a metacognitive signature can imitate the probe's pass/fail pattern for the wrong reasons — a general defeater for *every* behavioural AI-consciousness probe whose target is describable in the corpus.

- **Effective framing:** mechanism-driven vs imitation-driven contrast; "training contamination is the discrimination problem instantiated for trained systems"; the channel-exposure ranking.
