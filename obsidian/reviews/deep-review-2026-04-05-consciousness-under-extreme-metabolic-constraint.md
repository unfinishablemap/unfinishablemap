### Medium Issues Found
- **Xu et al. sample size omitted**: The article cited gamma surges in dying brains without noting N=4, only 2/4 showed surges. Resolution: Added explicit sample size caveat and noted the other two patients showed no such activity.
- **42% threshold overstated**: Presented Stender et al.'s diagnostic classifier as a sharp biological threshold. Resolution: Clarified it is a statistical classifier from clinical data, while noting the underlying pattern is still threshold-like rather than gradual.
- **Production model strawman**: Claimed production models predict linear proportional decline ("half metabolic rate → half consciousness"). Sophisticated network models naturally predict threshold effects. Resolution: Acknowledged graceful degradation and threshold effects in network models; reframed the anomaly as paradoxical *intensification*, which no production model predicts.
- **Missing asymmetry problem**: Article didn't address why most brain damage reduces consciousness while acute metabolic collapse sometimes enhances it. Resolution: Added "Chronic Versus Acute Metabolic Failure" section distinguishing filter dissolution (acute) from interface degradation (chronic), with cross-link to consciousness-and-neurodegenerative-disease.

### Counterarguments Considered
- **Network criticality**: Production models can explain thresholds via network criticality theory. Addressed by acknowledging this in the production model section and reframing the core anomaly as intensification, not just threshold behavior.
- **Excitotoxic interpretation of gamma surges**: Gamma surges could represent glutamate-driven excitotoxic cascades rather than organised consciousness. Not directly addressed in article (acknowledged as a limitation), but the cross-frequency coupling and interhemispheric connectivity findings are harder to dismiss as mere excitotoxicity.
- **Small sample sizes throughout**: N=4 for Xu et al., self-selected NDE samples. Addressed by adding caveats where most critical.
