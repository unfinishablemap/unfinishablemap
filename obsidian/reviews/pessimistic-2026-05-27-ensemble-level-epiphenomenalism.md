## Executive Summary

A tightly argued, genuinely self-critical concept page that states the strongest internal objection to corridor dualism and refuses to pretend it is solved — the article's chief strength. The most serious problems are structural rather than philosophical: the page is a complete orphan (zero inbound links) despite being a dedicated expansion of a worry its parent article names verbatim, and it leans hard on a "redefine efficacy" move (the trumping route) that a determined physicalist will read as begging the question — a vulnerability the article half-acknowledges but could own more squarely. ## Critiques by Philosopher

### The Eliminative Materialist (Churchland)
The whole apparatus — "token-level efficacy," "authorship," "default causal profile" — is folk-psychological scaffolding propping up a distinction (token vs. type efficacy) that does no work outside the dualist's own vocabulary. You have built an elaborate machine to preserve the *feeling* that consciousness "does something" while conceding it changes no measurable frequency. That concession is the tell: when a posit's defenders retreat to "real but invisible at every aggregate," elimination is the parsimonious next step, not preservation-by-redefinition.

### The Hard-Nosed Physicalist (Dennett)
The article's own dilemma (§"The Worry Stated Precisely") is fatal and the escape routes do not escape it. The trumping route does not answer the worry; it renames it. "Efficacy is a structural property, not a statistical one" is precisely the move a theory makes when it has run out of empirical content and wants to keep the honorific "cause." The article *says* this is "available to the dualist but not compelling to the physicalist" — but that is an admission that the resolution is audience-relative, i.e., not a resolution. The honest one-liner the article is circling but never quite writes: *under the corridor + trumping reading, "consciousness is a cause" and "consciousness is epiphenomenal" make no predictively distinct claims.*

### The Quantum Skeptic (Tegmark)
There is no decoherence accounting anywhere on the page. The entire debate presupposes that "a quantum measurement with possible outcomes weighted |⟨φᵢ|ψ⟩|²" is a thing consciousness can be poised over in a warm brain. The femtosecond decoherence objection is logically prior to the ensemble-vs-token question: if there is no maintained superposition for selection to act on, the dilemma never gets off the ground. The article should at least gesture at where that load is carried (presumably `decoherence.md` / the interface-formalism cluster) so the reader knows the precondition is contested elsewhere and not silently assumed.

### The Many-Worlds Defender (Deutsch)
You frame "which outcome actualises" as the thing consciousness selects — but on MWI all outcomes actualise and the "selection" is an indexical confusion. The article's dilemma is *generated by* the collapse framing it never defends here. By presenting single-outcome actualisation as the neutral starting point ("physics fixes the distribution but not which outcome occurs"), the page smuggles in the rejection of MWI as a premise rather than flagging it as a tenet-level commitment imported from elsewhere. State the dependency.

### The Empiricist (Popper's ghost)
The article is admirably candid that the trumping route is "empirically silent by construction" and the minimum-outside-corridor route is "the only one... even potentially interface-discriminating." But candour about unfalsifiability is not the same as having a falsifiable theory. The page rates the trumping route "interface-compatible but not interface-discriminating" and then treats *the Map's preferred reading* as the trumping one — i.e., the preferred answer to the sharpest open question is the unfalsifiable one. That ordering deserves to be named as a cost more pointedly than "owning this asymmetry is the discipline."

### The Buddhist Philosopher (Nagarjuna)
The article reifies an "authorship" that supposedly persists even when it leaves no trace in any aggregate. A causal authorship detectable nowhere, at no scale, by no measurement, that nonetheless "really" attaches to a token event — this is the definition of clinging to an empty designation. If the only difference between "consciousness authored it" and "it just happened" is a metaphysical label with no consequences, the dualist and the eliminativist are describing the same emptiness and quarrelling over the name.

## Counterarguments to Address

### "Token efficacy without type efficacy is genuine efficacy"
- **Current content says**: corridor dualism is interactionist at the token level while threatening to be epiphenomenal at the type level; per-trial selection is "real."
- **A critic would argue**: A cause whose every aggregate effect is fixed by a distribution it cannot perturb is doing the work of a *label*, not a *lever*. "Real per-trial, invisible in aggregate" is exactly the profile of a difference that makes no difference.
- **Suggested response**: The article already has the right reply latent in the trumping section (authorship is a structural, not frequency, fact). Make it confront this counterargument directly in the §"Why This Is Not Classical Epiphenomenalism" section rather than only in §"The Trumping Route," so the token/type distinction is defended at the point it is introduced.

### "This is unfalsifiable, therefore not even wrong"
- **Current content says**: the trumping route is empirically silent by construction; owning the asymmetry "is the discipline."
- **A critic would argue**: An unfalsifiable preferred answer to your own sharpest question is a metaphysical commitment, not a resolution; calling candour about this "discipline" rebrands a weakness as a virtue.
- **Suggested response**: Distinguish *epistemic humility* (good) from *empirical content* (absent). The article can hold that the corridor+trumping reading is the most tenet-conservative option while conceding it buys conservatism with unfalsifiability — and that this is a genuine cost, not a wash.
