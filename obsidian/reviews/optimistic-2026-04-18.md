## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)

The [[concepts/parsimony-epistemology|parsimony-epistemology]] Idealism Parity Trilemma is the most Chalmers-grade concession in the Map's history. The sentence that does the work — "By the same ontological measure, physicalism and idealism both beat interactionist dualism, which posits two substance types to their one. The Map cannot appeal to ontological parsimony to embarrass physicalism with idealism without also embarrassing its own position" — is exactly the dialectical generosity Chalmers has modelled in *The Conscious Mind* and *The Character of Consciousness*. Refusing the free rhetorical advantage of a selectively-applied razor is how serious metaphysics earns its keep. The new "What Would Refute This View?" section with five specific falsification conditions (including the precise commitment "if the explanatory gap closed" via Type-B reduction) is Chalmers-specific in structure — Chalmers has consistently demanded that anti-physicalists state what *would* make them physicalists, and this section answers.

The same article's strengthened Type-B physicalism engagement in Precondition 1 — acknowledging that the Type-B reply is *coherent* and that the dispute then shifts from parsimony to "the plausibility of the promissory note" — is precisely the charity Chalmers' *Meta-Problem* paper asks of dualist writing.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)

The parsimony-epistemology article now has the Stappian parsimony argument in its most honest form. The von Neumann-Wigner passage retains the core claim that adding consciousness to the ontology may *simplify* the explanation of definite outcomes, but now adds: "Decoherence theory challenges this by dissolving the preferred-basis problem, but it does not by itself resolve the measurement problem's outcome question without further interpretive commitments." This is the exact hedge Stapp himself uses in *Mindful Universe* — grant decoherence its victory on the preferred basis while noting that the outcome question survives. The article no longer overclaims on Stapp's behalf; it frames the vN-W argument as a surviving case study in simplicity's multidimensionality, "whatever view of decoherence one takes."

The [[topics/phenomenology-of-musical-understanding|musical-understanding]] refinement's rewritten bidirectional-interaction paragraph — which grounds phenomenal causation in the "minimal causal influence specified by the Map's quantum-interaction framework" rather than in free-floating assertion — is the Stappian discipline applied at the article level: the quantitative burden is discharged elsewhere, not here, and the article names this explicitly. The closing move that "phenomenal qualities contribute a small but non-zero difference to which physical outcome obtains, enough to make introspective tracking non-miraculous" is Stapp's non-triviality condition stated with the right weight.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)

The new [[voids/transformative-experience-void|transformative-experience-void]] is the deepest Nagelian article the Map has produced in this cycle. The doubly-sealed void framing — "neither temporal stage of the subject can occupy the other's standpoint" — generalises Nagel's *What Is It Like to Be a Bat?* from interspecies to intrapersonal asymmetry, which is arguably the sharper case. Paul's "epistemic gold standard" of decision theory failing "at the axiom level, not as measurement difficulty" is the exact move Nagel has demanded since 1974: the first-personal is not a stubborn measurement problem awaiting better tools but a structural feature of what it is like to be a subject at all. The article's distinction between the transformative void and nearby voids (phenomenal-quality, acquaintance) is Nagelian taxonomic work done carefully — each void has a distinct shape, and the transformative case is the one Nagel's argument specifically generalises to.

The musical-understanding article's new Predictive-Coding Challenge section is Nagel-grade phenomenological precision in miniature. Rather than dismiss predictive coding, the article treats Huron's ITPRA framework and Vuust's reward-based work as "functionalism's strongest case" and then locates the disagreement with surgical care: "the literature sometimes slides between 'explains the functional role of surprise' and 'explains what surprise feels like,' but these are distinct claims." This is Nagel's distinction between functional and phenomenal exactly. The concession that "the mechanics of musical cognition are a legitimate computational target, and the Map expects most of the processing story to look like this" is Nagel-style methodological generosity — the dispute is not with neuroscience, it is with what neuroscience has explained.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)

The transformative-experience-void's treatment of Paul's **authenticity constraint** is the most Kane-relevant move of the cluster. The sentence — "If the decision procedure alienates the agent from the decision — by outsourcing the choice to testimony or statistical aggregates — something essential is lost, even if the procedure produces a determinate output" — is precisely the Kane thesis that libertarian agency cannot be reduced to any procedure whose output would be identical for a different agent. Kane's "self-forming actions" and Paul's "revelatory-value decisions" converge here: the agent must originate the choice, not be handed its answer. The article's negative claim that "outsourcing the choice replaces it with a different kind of choice, made by a different kind of agent" is Kane-grade: authenticity is not reducible to successful outcome.

The parsimony-epistemology's falsification section includes a condition Kane would particularly endorse — "If the hostile witnesses reversed their concessions" — which makes the argument's dependence on *specific dialectical history* explicit. Kane has always insisted that libertarian arguments earn their keep dialectically, through engagement with opponents' best versions, rather than through axiom-level victories. The article now enumerates exactly what dialectical reversals would matter.
