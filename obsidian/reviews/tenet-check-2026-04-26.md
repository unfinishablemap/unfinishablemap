### topics/the-hard-problem-in-non-western-philosophy.md
- **Tenet adjacency**: T1 (Dualism)

The framing concedes more than the Map needs to: framework-dependence of the *problem statement* does not entail framework-dependence of the *underlying gap*.
- **Recommendation**: Add a sentence distinguishing framework-relativity of articulation from framework-relativity of the underlying explanatory gap. The Map can hold T1 universally while granting that traditions without a strong subject/object cleavage articulate the gap differently or not at all.

## Non-Violation Tensions Worth Monitoring

These are *not* tenet violations but are the Map's largest internal pressure points, visible in this window:

1. **Strongest-form engagement vs. early-stop comprehension (cross-tenet, presentation-level):** Several articles in this delta — `illusionism.md`, `mysterianism.md`, `eliminative-materialism.md`, `consciousness-and-integrated-information.md` — present the strongest form of a tenet-rejecting position before refuting it. This is the right register intellectually but creates an early-stop risk: a reader who exits the article before the refutation could mistake the sympathetic exposition for endorsement. A one-line orientation in the lede ("This article presents X in its strongest form before showing why the Map rejects it") would resolve this without altering the substantive treatment. Not a violation; an opportunity to harden presentation against truncation.
2. **Framework-relativity of the hard problem (T1):** `the-hard-problem-in-non-western-philosophy.md` and `indian-philosophy-of-mind.md` correctly engage non-Western frameworks where the subject/object cleavage is articulated differently. The articles flag this as a challenge to T1's universality, but the response can be strengthened: framework-relativity of the *problem statement* does not entail framework-relativity of the *underlying explanatory gap*. The Map's T1 commitment is to the gap, not to a particular articulation of it. This is a *clarity* issue, not a doctrinal one — but in the current articulation, the Daoism subsection of `the-hard-problem-in-non-western-philosophy.md` concedes more than the Map needs to.
3. **Decoherence honesty (T2):** `ai-consciousness.md` and `substrate-independence.md` engage the decoherence challenge with rigorous honesty, including its strongest form. This is right — false confidence on T2's empirical status would weaken rather than strengthen the position — but the cluster is now load-bearing, and any future drift toward dismissing decoherence would compromise the Map's epistemic credibility. Worth watching.
