## Pessimistic Analysis Summary

### Critical Issues Found

1. **Mudrik et al. reference — wrong journal and title**: The citation listed "The Complex Interplay Between Multisensory Integration and Perceptual Awareness" in *Multisensory Research* 27(5-6). The actual Mudrik, Faivre & Koch 2014 paper is "Information Integration Without Awareness" in *Trends in Cognitive Sciences* 18(9). **Resolution**: Corrected to accurate citation.

2. **Frontiers 2019 reference — missing authors**: Cited as "Frontiers. (2019)..." which omits the actual authors Jerath & Beveridge. **Resolution**: Corrected to proper author attribution.

3. **Baars & Franklin 2013 — missing co-author**: The paper has three authors (Baars, Franklin, and Ramsøy), not two. **Resolution**: Added third author.

4. **Audition latency figure too low**: Article stated "audition ~10ms" but cortical arrival for auditory signals is ~20ms (the ~10ms figure corresponds to brainstem latency, not cortical processing). **Resolution**: Corrected to ~20ms.

5. **Olfactory thalamus claim overstated**: "Bypass the thalamus entirely" is slightly inaccurate — olfaction bypasses the thalamic *relay* for initial cortical access, but the mediodorsal thalamus participates in higher-order olfactory processing. **Resolution**: Qualified to "bypass the thalamic relay for initial cortical access."

### Medium Issues Found

1. **BP1/BP2 used without definition**: The abbreviations BP1 and BP2 appear on lines 84 and 108 without introduction. These terms are defined in the companion article (the-binding-problem-a-systematic-treatment) but this article should be self-contained per the writing style guide. **Resolution**: Added inline definitions — "BP1 — the coordination of information across brain regions" and "phenomenal binding (BP2)."

2. **NMDA/GABA mechanism stated too definitively**: "The mechanism is well characterised" oversells what are current models, not settled facts. **Resolution**: Softened to "Current models implicate..."
