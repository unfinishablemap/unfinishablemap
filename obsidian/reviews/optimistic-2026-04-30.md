## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)

The quantitative-comprehension-void's restructured §"What Counts as Comprehension" is exemplary Chalmersian dialectical posture. The functionalist objection is stated in its strongest form ("understanding *is* a functional state—the right information processing, regardless of phenomenal accompaniment—and the void reduces to a performance limitation") and granted real force ("the objection has force"); the Map's reply identifies an intrinsic, non-relational character ("phenomenal grasp has an intrinsic, non-relational character—the felt sense of magnitude as a unity—that no functional specification captures") and then names the standoff as bedrock ("the standoff with functionalism is bedrock; the Map declares its commitment and moves on"). This is exactly the bedrock-clash discipline Chalmers practises in *The Conscious Mind* — not pretending the type-B physicalist is wrong, but locating the disagreement at the right level (the intrinsic-vs-relational status of phenomenal character) and refusing to absorb a substantive philosophical commitment into a single editor-determined resolution.

The article's structural family framing — three nested faces sharing a diagnostic signature without sharing a single neural mechanism — is also Chalmers-grade. Property dualism requires phenomenal properties to be type-distinct from physical properties without requiring a unified phenomenal-property substrate; the void's three faces under one signature instantiate the same dialectical shape at the methodological level. The article's restraint about cross-face mechanism unification ("the faces share an outcome—symbol/grasp dissociation—rather than a single neural mechanism") is precisely the right posture: type-level claims about phenomenal access without overreach to a shared neural-level explanation.

The article's restructured Bidirectional Interaction paragraph offers Stapp's framework an empirical anchor of unusual quality. *"If the bidirectional interface operates over phenomenally-given content, the four-item cap could constrain the granularity at which consciousness can act. The cardinality-floor section's transition phenomenology is Nagel-grade structural-absence-as-data. *"We have the experience of *knowing how many* (≤4) and the experience of *having to count or estimate* (≥5), but no experience of the boundary—no 'almost subitizing five.' Nagel's discipline requires that subjective experience be honoured as first-person constraint without flattening across instances; the face-by-face mapping does exactly this.
