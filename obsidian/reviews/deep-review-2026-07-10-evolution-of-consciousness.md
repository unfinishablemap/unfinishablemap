## Critical Issues Found

**1. New York Declaration tier misquote (possibility/probability slippage) — FIXED.**

The article read: *"The New York Declaration (2024) extended this to 'strong scientific support' for consciousness in all vertebrates and 'realistic possibility' for many invertebrates..."*

The declaration's actual tiering (verified at sites.google.com/nyu.edu/nydeclaration/declaration):
- **"strong scientific support"** applies to **mammals and birds** only.
- **"at least a realistic possibility"** applies to **all other vertebrates (reptiles, amphibians, fishes)** AND many invertebrates (cephalopod mollusks, decapod crustaceans, insects).

The article had upgraded the "realistic possibility" tier for non-mammal/bird vertebrates to "strong scientific support for all vertebrates" — inflating the source's evidential calibration by one tier for reptiles, amphibians, and fishes. This is a factual attribution error AND a possibility/probability slippage (an evidential-status upgrade unsupported by the source). A tenet-accepting reviewer would still flag it, so it is correctable, not bedrock — CRITICAL.

Fixed to: *"affirmed 'strong scientific support' for consciousness in mammals and birds, and 'a realistic possibility' of it in all other vertebrates (reptiles, amphibians, fishes) and many invertebrates including cephalopods, crustaceans, and insects."* Length-neutral (+~3 words).

No corpus propagation search needed for a metadata author-list defect (this was a body paraphrase, not a citation-metadata error), but the same declaration is quoted in `animal-consciousness` and related articles — a spot-grep is recommended as a follow-up if the same "all vertebrates / strong support" phrasing recurs (not observed in this article's related set during this pass).
