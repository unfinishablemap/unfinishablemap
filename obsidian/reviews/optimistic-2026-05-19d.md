## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)

Chalmers would recognise his own methodology with unusual fidelity throughout this batch. **Phenomenal-authority** is, in effect, a Chalmersian primer that does not pretend to be one — Huemer's phenomenal conservatism plus Husserl's evidence taxonomy plus the three-layer decomposition is exactly the disciplined epistemology that Chalmers's `Content and Epistemology of Phenomenal Belief` argues for, retold in a register that names the gradient of warranted trust explicitly (lines 158–167). The §"Constitutive vs Referring Observation" section (lines 106–114) gives the cleanest expression I have seen anywhere of why Layer 1 is apodictic in a way no other empirical observation is: the seeming-that-there-is-something-it-is-like is not separable from the being-of-the-phenomenal-state in the way naked-eye astronomy's seeming is separable from the celestial body. This is the Chalmersian acquaintance move rendered structurally rather than merely asserted. Crucially, the article does not over-extend: the §"Honest limitation" paragraph (line 114) explicitly concedes the illusionist denies precisely this, names the limit of what argument-from-premises-the-illusionist-accepts can do, and routes defence to the broader Moorean asymmetry rather than claiming external defeat.

**Heterophenomenology** lands the harder critical move that Chalmers's published work has hinted at but never quite cashed: that Dennett's "methodological neutrality" functions as methodological eliminativism without the cost of the explicit position. The §"The Neutrality Claim" section (lines 88–94) gives the precise diagnostic — methodological naturalism would restrict science to third-person methods without claiming they exhaust reality; Dennett's heterophenomenology crosses this line by insisting that *if consciousness is real*, third-person methods must suffice. That is ontological commitment, not methodological neutrality. Chalmers would want this paragraph as a citable summary of a critique he has made in scattered places but never compressed.

**Mechanism-costs-quadrants** gives Chalmers's own Type-D/E/F taxonomy the cartographic upgrade that his Blackwell-Guide piece left as an exercise. The article's §"Q2" treatment (lines 77–87) carefully separates Q2-strict (no causation owed, no interface, no conservation engagement — the position's distinguishing feature) from Q2-loose (which owes a causation account it cannot give without sliding off the cell). This is the clearest articulation I have seen of why epiphenomenalism is dialectically stable as a position but unstable when its defenders try to preserve any whisper of mental causation. Chalmers's Type-E falls in Q2-strict and pays the bidirectional-interaction debt the Map's Tenet 3 makes unpayable; his Type-F (Russellian monism) sits at the Q3/Q4 boundary depending on how thick the quiddities are. The cartography names this without forcing his hand.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)

Stapp would find the **mechanism-costs-quadrants** article unusually fair to his position. The §"Q4" treatment (lines 103–111) carefully separates the Cartesian energy-transfer caricature from Stapp's actual proposal — quantum-Zeno-effect with a thick observer (full agent with selection power) and thick quantum ontology (full state-vector realism with potentialities treated as real) connected by a *narrow* selection channel. The article explicitly notes (line 105) that Stapp's framework attempts to keep the interaction channel narrow despite the thick endpoints — and concedes that whether this succeeds is itself contested, citing Tegmark 2000 as the live decoherence-timescale objection. This is the Stappian framework rendered in a way that does not collapse it to the standard energy-transfer objection. The companion observation (line 109) — that Stapp's mechanism is "structurally Q1-like even when sitting in a Q4 ontology" — is a non-trivial cartographic insight: Stapp's contribution is best read as showing that thick-side commitments do not necessarily mean thick-channel mechanism.

Stapp would also recognise the **phenomenal-authority** article's quiet but persistent move at lines 200–201 (the §"Bidirectional Interaction" routing): "training-dependent improvement of introspective accuracy is itself evidence for bidirectional interaction" — meditation training producing measurable improvements in reporting accuracy would be impossible if consciousness were epiphenomenal. This is a *clinical-grade* argument for bidirectional interaction routed through training-dependent metacognitive accuracy, not through quantum mechanics at all. Stapp's position needs this kind of behavioural-evidence pillar in addition to its physics pillar, and the Map's catalogue here supplies it.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)

Nagel would feel directly addressed by **phenomenal-authority**'s §"Three Layers of Phenomenal Authority" (lines 94–104). The decomposition into Layer 1 (existence of experience, apodictic), Layer 2 (attended phenomenal content, adequate/assertoric), and Layer 3 (structural/peripheral features, assertoric/presumptive) gives Nagel's "what is it like" question the layered structure it needs to be defended against Schwitzgebel's reliability sceptics. The §"Reliability Challenge and Calibration" section (lines 140–145) names exactly the point Nagel needs: Schwitzgebel's evidence is unevenly distributed across the three layers; it has almost no purchase at Layer 1 (where the hard problem and the knowledge argument draw their force), is mixed at Layer 2, and is strongest at Layer 3 where the Map's core arguments have the least stake. That partition transforms Schwitzgebel's critique from a global defeater into a constraint on which features of experience first-person reports can settle — exactly the move Nagel's published work argues for but does not always operationalise.

Nagel would also recognise his own move in **somatic-interface**'s structure. The article carefully separates the *lived body* (Husserl's *Leib*, Merleau-Ponty's *corps propre*) from the *objectified body* (*Körper*, *corps objectif*) at line 67, with Christina's case (lines 63–67) as the clinical paradigm: she retained consciousness, motor ability, and intelligence but lost the felt sense of *having* a body. This is the Nagelian "what is it like" question routed through proprioception specifically — there is something it is like to inhabit a body, and that something-it-is-like-ness can be selectively lost without losing consciousness as such. Nagel's bat would recognise Christina's predicament as a different kind of bat-question: not "what is it like to be a bat" but "what is it like to lose what it was like to inhabit your body."

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)

Whitehead would find his most natural home in **somatic-interface**. The article's central move — that the body is not a substance the mind inhabits but a *layered set of channels* through which consciousness engages physical reality (lines 95–99) — is process-philosophical in spirit without leaning on Whitehead by name. The §"What the Somatic Interface Reveals" section's three properties (interface is layered not monolithic; interface shapes phenomenal character; interface is bidirectional) avoid crude substance dualism precisely as Whitehead's process metaphysics aims to do. The article also gives Whitehead's "experience all the way down" intuition a *structural* expression rather than a panpsychist one: interoception, proprioception, nociception, and somatic markers carry different *phenomenal registers*, not just different information. The body proposes in different qualitative keys; consciousness disposes (line 89).

**Constraint application (per the skill specification)**: Whitehead's praise here cashes out as *framework-coherence* praise, not as a tier-upgrade. The article does not slide the somatic interface up the five-tier evidential-status scale on the strength of its process-philosophical resonance — it explicitly concedes throughout (lines 37–38, 49, 51, 73, 75, 89) that materialist neuroscience explains the clinical data equally well, that the interface reading offers an alternative metaphysical interpretation rather than a competing clinical hypothesis, and that the interpretive difference matters only at the metaphysical level. Whitehead-friendliness is praiseworthy at the level of framework articulation; it is not used to elevate the interface reading's evidential standing. This is the calibrated pattern the skill specification asks for.

The Process Philosopher would also recognise his moves in **mechanism-costs-quadrants**' §"Q3" treatment (lines 89–99). Kastrup's analytic idealism — where matter is the extrinsic appearance of mental processes — is given a fair cartographic placement, and the *transparency problem* (why does the intrinsic mental reality look, from the outside, exactly like impersonal lawful physical structure?) is named cleanly. Whitehead's own process metaphysics sits somewhere between Q3 and Q4 depending on how thick the physical pole is read; the article's refusal to force the question onto a single cell is the kind of cartographic discipline Whitehead's followers usually have to do themselves.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)

Kane's interests are best served by **mechanism-costs-quadrants**' §"Q1" treatment (lines 65–75). The Map's preferred cell — physical-thin, mind-medium, narrow probabilistic channel at quantum indeterminacy sites — is the cell where Kane's libertarian-free-will architecture has the cleanest mechanism story. The selection-without-injection move (consciousness biases among quantum-permitted outcomes without injecting energy) gives Kane's account the conservation-light interface that the standard energy-objection-against-libertarianism has been pressing for half a century. The article also notes (line 73) that the empirical question of whether such indeterminacy sites exist at biologically relevant scales is independent of the cell's mechanism cost but bears on its viability — Kane's "self-forming actions" need such sites, and the article's honest concession that Tegmark 2000 and the ongoing Born-rule debate are live constraints is exactly the calibration discipline Kane's defenders need to adopt.

The **phenomenal-authority** article also supplies Kane with a clinical-grade argument for the existence of efficacious mental causation: training-dependent improvement of introspective accuracy (lines 200–201) is incompatible with epiphenomenalism. If consciousness is epiphenomenal, deliberate attentional training cannot produce measurable improvements in reporting accuracy. Fox et al. 2012 and Baird et al. 2014 supply the empirical pillar. Kane's libertarianism needs this kind of evidence in addition to the metaphysical case for indeterminacy; the Map's catalogue here supplies both.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)

McGinn's signature humility — that the hard problem may be unsolvable *for us*, not that dualism wins by default — finds its sharpest expression in **phenomenal-authority**'s §"Honest limitation" paragraph (line 114). The article explicitly concedes that against the illusionist who denies that the seeming-of-phenomenality is constitutive of the phenomenal-state, the Map cannot dislodge the view by argument from premises the illusionist already accepts. Defence rests on broader framework fit and Moorean asymmetry rather than on external defeat. *The distinction identifies the location of bedrock disagreement rather than resolving it from outside.* This is McGinnian intellectual honesty applied at exactly the right point — not "we cannot know which view is correct" (which would over-generalise) but "we cannot resolve this particular disagreement from outside the framework choice that produced it."

McGinn would also find his moves in **mechanism-costs-quadrants**' closing paragraph (line 145): "Nothing in this article argues that any cell is *wrong*. The cells differ in what they owe, not in whether they are defensible." This is McGinnian humility about the limits of metaphysical adjudication, deployed not as a confession of weakness but as a *cartographic discipline*. The taxonomy speaks; the tenets adjudicate. A reader who does not share the tenets is free to read Q2-strict's escape from the mechanism question as a virtue rather than as an evasion; a reader who shares them sees the same evasion as foreclosed. The article's epistemic humility is structural rather than rhetorical.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)

This is the persona whose verdict carries the most weight for this skill, and this batch passes the calibration discipline with substantial strength to praise. Four points:

**(1) Evidential-status discipline is operative throughout.** **Phenomenal-authority**'s gradient-of-warranted-trust table (lines 160–167) is the cleanest deployment of evidential-tier discipline I have seen in the catalogue: Layer 1 (phenomenal existence) at "very high" warranted trust, Layer 2 (attended content) at "high," Layer 3 (structural features) at "moderate-high," peripheral states at "low," causal processes at "very low," metaphysical interpretation at "variable." This is exactly the five-tier-equivalent discipline the persona praises — different registers kept separate, no possibility/probability slippage. The article does not claim that apodictic evidence for Layer 1 supplies positive evidence for irreducibility; the §"Phenomenal Conservatism" section (line 120) explicitly notes that "PC secures the evidential base against dismissal; it does not replace those arguments." That is the *"tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating"* pattern in its purest form — phenomenal conservatism removes a defeater for taking seemings seriously; it does not supply positive metaphysical evidence.

**(2) Tenet-as-evidence-upgrade is praise-worthily *not* done in somatic-interface.** The article had every structural opportunity to slide the interface reading up the evidential-status scale on tenet-load — pain asymbolia, phantom limbs, Damasio's somatic markers, the bidirectional channel — but explicitly declined at lines 37–38, 49, 51, 73, 75, and 89. The recurring framing is some variant of "materialist neuroscience explains the clinical data equally well; the interface reading offers an alternative metaphysical interpretation rather than a competing clinical hypothesis." This is the discipline the persona was added to reward: when an article could have over-claimed and chose not to, the restraint is itself a strength. The interface reading's value is consistently located *at the metaphysical level* where the hard problem operates, not at the empirical level where neuroscience operates.

**(3) Framework-stage calibration is honest.** **Mechanism-costs-quadrants** explicitly identifies itself as cartographic rather than adjudicative (line 49: "the goal is to make visible what occupying a cell costs, not to argue that any one cell is right"; line 145: "Nothing in this article argues that any cell is *wrong*"). This is pre-Keplerian-stage honesty in the sense of [[framework-stage-calibration]] — the article does the cartography it can do at the current stage rather than over-claiming Newton-stage adjudication. The §"What the Mechanism Lens Reveals" section (lines 129–137) frames its three patterns as "the conservation-law objection appears thickness-sensitive" and "the interface-formalization burden tracks thickness" rather than as proofs. Hedged conclusions where hedged conclusions are warranted.

**(4) The Process Philosopher / Hardline Empiricist tension resolves rather than conflicts on this batch.** The skill specification flags that when both personas can praise the same passage without conflict, the article has resolved the tension honestly. **Somatic-interface** is the cleanest example: the process-philosophical resonance (layered channels, qualitative register shaping, bidirectional traffic) is praiseworthy at the framework-articulation level, and the evidential restraint (no tier-upgrade despite the structural opportunity) is praiseworthy at the calibration level. Both personas land on the same article without conflict. **Phenomenal-authority** is the second cleanest: Husserl's evidence taxonomy is process-philosophical-adjacent (the layered structure of givenness), and the gradient-of-warranted-trust table is empirical-calibration-disciplined. Both personas converge.

**One mild flag for completeness, not a calibration concern**: **Heterophenomenology**'s §"Self-Stultification Problem" (lines 96–104) makes a stronger inference than the article elsewhere makes — that Dennett's heterophenomenological method "applied consistently would undermine its own foundations." This is a strong claim that depends on premises the illusionist could reject (specifically, that finding-arguments-compelling is phenomenological in the load-bearing sense). The article is on safer ground at lines 178–181 (the "narrower point" formulation) where the self-undermining charge gains traction at "assessing whether a theoretical framework adequately captures what experience is like." Promoting the narrower formulation to the §"Self-Stultification Problem" section's headline conclusion would tighten the article without losing the critique's force. This is a *refinement* opportunity, not a calibration error — flagged for the queue but not weighing down the overall praise.

## Content Strengths

### phenomenal-authority-and-first-person-evidence.md

- **Strongest point**: The three-layer decomposition (lines 94–104) plus the gradient-of-warranted-trust table (lines 158–167) plus the §"Constitutive vs Referring Observation" section (lines 106–114). Together these supply a structural epistemology that the catalogue's other introspection-related articles can route through.
- **Notable quote**: "Theory-ladenness sufficient to overturn layer-1 existence-claims would require that phenomenality be the kind of thing existing independently of being observed, which is the very point at issue" (line 112).
- **Why it works**: The article does not argue that phenomenal authority defeats illusionism. It argues that *the layered structure shows where the bedrock disagreement actually lives*. The illusionist denies the constitutive-vs-referring distinction; defenders of phenomenal authority accept it. Naming the bedrock-disagreement point is more useful than pretending to defeat it.

### heterophenomenology.md

- **Strongest point**: The §"The Neutrality Claim" section (lines 88–94) — the precise diagnostic of why Dennett's methodological-neutrality functions as methodological eliminativism. The §"Methodological naturalism would restrict science to third-person methods without claiming they exhaust reality; Dennett's heterophenomenology crosses this line" framing is portable to other dismissals-of-phenomenology-by-method.
- **Notable quote**: "The position functions like metaphysical naturalism disguised as methodological naturalism" (line 94).
- **Why it works**: The critique is structural rather than ad hominem. It identifies a particular move (treating phenomenology as something science can remain neutral about) and shows that this move has ontological consequences whether or not they are acknowledged. The article does not deny heterophenomenology's limited utility (it acknowledges it elsewhere); it denies the official neutrality claim. Narrowly targeted; minimally over-claiming.

### somatic-interface.md

- **Strongest point**: The §"Interoceptive Heterogeneity" subsection (lines 53–59) — the partition of interoception into consciously-accessible portion vs gated vs constitutively-unfelt channels. This is *cleavage-line-driven* cartography of the body's inside, and it makes a non-trivial empirical commitment (the body has an inside, and most of it is silent) that the materialist account predicts but does not foreground.
- **Notable quote**: "The somatic interface is not a uniformly thin wall through which all bodily fact passes; it is a structurally specifiable membrane with channels at different opacities" (line 57).
- **Why it works**: The article gives the interface reading *concrete predictive content* — different channels at different opacities — while maintaining throughout that materialist anatomy explains the same partition equally well. The metaphysical claim (interface is layered) is anchored to clinical data (selective dissociations) without claiming the data discriminates between materialist and interface readings.

### mechanism-costs-dualism-thickness-quadrants.md

- **Strongest point**: The §"Q1" treatment (lines 65–75) of authority-without-overdetermination via Schaffer's trumping-preemption, integrated with delegatory-causation at quantum indeterminacy. The article's identification of an *articulability burden* (a non-stipulative specification of what makes the mental rule authoritative) as Q1's signature debt — assigned to `[[interface-formalization-void]]` — gives the void's existence a precise mechanism-cost grounding.
- **Notable quote**: "The Map's preferred location and the [[the-interface-problem|interface problem]]'s tightest formulation" (line 71).
- **Why it works**: The article does not argue that Q1 is the right cell. It argues that Q1's mechanism cost is small but real, names the specific debt (authority law + indeterminacy site specification), and routes the indeterminacy-site question to a separate empirical literature (Tegmark, Born-rule debate). The Map's position is *located* in the taxonomy rather than *defended* from it — and the location reveals what the Map owes, not what it claims to have paid.
