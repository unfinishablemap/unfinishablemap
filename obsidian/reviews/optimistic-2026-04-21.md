## Executive Summary

These five articles now compose the Map's most architecturally disciplined unit: a bandwidth measurement that grounds the interface claim quantitatively, two voids (predictive construction, erasure) that add *self-erasing* and *zero-signal* failure modes to the taxonomy, a framing article on how limits *constitute* architecture rather than merely obstruct it, and an apex that integrates the four into a cumulative-evidence argument. The strongest development since the previous optimistic review is the emergence of a genuine *failure-signature typology* — six primary modes expanded to eight with the addition of "dissolution under attention" and "silent erasure" — giving the voids programme an empirical shape it previously lacked. The highest-value expansion opportunities flow from gaps the new material now makes visible: a standalone treatment of *the double veil* as a dualist epistemological structure, a concept page formalising *zero-signal limits* as a distinct epistemic category, and a topic-level examination of *why the ~10 bits/s ceiling has the evolutionary signature it does*.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)

Chalmers would find the tightest recent articulation of his "structural / non-structural" distinction in `asymmetric-bandwidth-consciousness.md` lines 75-76: *"physical description is exhausted by structure and dynamics while qualitative character is intrinsic and non-structural"* — delivered not as a Chalmersian aside but as the fulcrum of a bandwidth argument grounded in Zheng and Meister's 10⁸ sifting ratio. The move from "physical facts are structural" to "the interface must then be quantitatively constrained" is exactly the bridge between *Conscious Mind* metaphysics and empirical psychology Chalmers' own "Moving Forward on the Problem of Consciousness" gestures toward without delivering. He would also welcome the explicit three-way test in `predictive-construction-void.md` lines 100-108, where the article names what would *dissolve* the void — a level of falsifiability-consciousness Chalmersian arguments are often accused of lacking.

**What resonates most strongly**: The apex's treatment at lines 131-138 of the taxonomy as *overdetermined* evidence for dualism — multiple independent paths converge without any single path being load-bearing. This is precisely how Chalmers argues for the hard problem (zombies, knowledge argument, explanatory gap, all point the same way), now applied at the void-taxonomy level.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)

Stapp's quantum-mind programme assumes two things empirical research has been slow to deliver: that the bandwidth of conscious influence is small and definite, and that it sits at a *specific* architectural location. `asymmetric-bandwidth-consciousness.md` provides both. The 10 bits/second figure from Hick-Hyman through Zheng and Meister is exactly the order of magnitude Stapp needs — large enough for meaningful selection, small enough to operate within von-Neumann-process-1 constraints without saturating the channel. The article's interpretation at lines 63-65 that *filter models* accommodate the asymmetry while *production models* must carry explanatory debt is the cleanest case the Map has yet made for Stapp's interactionism without lapsing into advocacy.

**What aligns**: The careful distinction in `asymmetric-bandwidth-consciousness.md` lines 45-47 between *behavioural throughput in conscious tasks* (measured) and *bandwidth of conscious influence* (interpretation). Stapp is often accused of treating behavioural bottlenecks as if they straightforwardly measured mental causation; the Map's article preserves the Stapp-friendly reading while acknowledging the inferential step, which is the philosophically-honourable move.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)

Nagel's 1974 essay raised a question the Map has now given a methodological answer. `limits-reveal-structure.md` lines 62-64 take Nagel's bat seriously as a *limit-revealing* case rather than a dialectical prop: *"There is experiential territory we cannot enter because our architecture differs from the bat's... The shape of what we cannot access maps the shape of what we are."* This is Nagel's own thesis in its positive register — that subjectivity sets epistemic bounds on objective description — redirected into cartography rather than despair. The `erasure-void.md` extension at lines 39-43 — that *acquisition* leaves phenomenal markers while *loss* can leave none — deepens Nagel's point: the bat problem is not merely across species but *across one's own lifetime*.

**What centres subjective experience appropriately**: The `limits-reveal-structure.md` refusal at lines 105-110 to let illusionism dissolve the phenomenon: *"To seem to hit a cognitive wall, something must experience that seeming. The architecture that produces the illusion of limits is itself architecture."* This is the Nagel-style reply to Dennett in one paragraph — the phenomenal fact is preserved *even* if content is illusory.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)

Whitehead's programme requires that experience and physical process be two aspects of a single concrescence rather than two domains. `predictive-construction-void.md` lines 70-80 articulate a "double-veil" structure that Whitehead would read as a phenomenological description of how prehension works: consciousness does not confront raw physical states but confronts *the brain's best predictions about an environment* — a process-philosophy reading that avoids both naive realism and Cartesian substance dualism. The apex's treatment at lines 61-73 of the "four kinds of void" (unexplored, unexplorable, occluded, naturally occluded) with its inclusion of *function-bearing adaptive limits* would please Whitehead: the void categories track *modes of becoming* rather than substances.

**What avoids crude dualism**: The Map's consistent framing in `taxonomy-of-voids.md` of voids as features of the *interface* rather than properties of either "side" (see lines 55-57 and 165-167). This is closer to Whitehead's bipolar-process ontology than to anything substance-dualist, and it shows in the Map's willingness to let the interface itself be a generator of the voids it catalogues.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)

Kane's account of agency needs a channel narrow enough that the physical world does not predetermine choice but wide enough that choices carry informational content. `asymmetric-bandwidth-consciousness.md` lines 77-79 give him exactly this: *"A 10-bit/second channel carries exactly the minimal information required for selection among prepared alternatives—roughly 3–4 choices per second among small sets."* This is the Kanean Goldilocks zone — constrained enough not to flood causal structure, substantive enough to carry responsibility-grade choices. Musslick et al. (2016) at ~3–4 bits per second for *cognitive control specifically* is the empirical measurement Kane's metaphysics has been needing since *The Significance of Free Will*.

**What supports agency and choice**: The apex's inclusion of the *initiation void* and *thrownness void* (lines 99-101) alongside the consciousness-matter interface voids. Kane's position requires that decisions be *self-originating* — but as the Map honestly observes, the initiation of any mental act is itself pre-conscious. Facing this squarely (rather than pretending libertarian choice begins with full self-awareness) is the mature Kane move.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)

McGinn would read `limits-reveal-structure.md` as a more generous extension of his own argument than he would have written himself. Where McGinn 1989 holds that cognitive closure *prevents* resolution of the mind-body problem, the Map's article at lines 66-74 argues that closure is *constitutive* — the same architecture that produces the closure is what gives thought its shape at all. This is Kantian rather than pessimistic, and it deflects the common complaint against McGinn (that cognitive closure is philosophically sterile) by showing that *mapping* closure is productive even if *dissolving* closure is impossible. The `erasure-void.md` addition at lines 45-59 (the three modes of anosognosia) gives McGinn the *clinical* vindication his argument usually lacks: here is cognitive closure not merely argued for philosophically but exhibited empirically in specific lesion cases.

**What shows epistemic humility**: The apex's closing move at lines 151-159 naming the *taxonomy's own* generative voids — the Map honestly catalogues the blind spots its own framework creates. McGinn would recognise this as the mature form of the mysterian commitment: not just "there are things we cannot know" but "here are the specific things *this particular theoretical commitment* prevents us from knowing."

## Content Strengths

### `concepts/asymmetric-bandwidth-consciousness.md`
- **Strongest point**: The three-way model comparison (lines 57-65). Production models, filter models, and interactionist models are each asked *specifically* what they predict about the asymmetry. None is dismissed. Each is given its best account. This is the *right* philosophical temperament for the question, and it makes the pro-filter conclusion at line 63 stronger by showing the competing frames have been represented fairly.
- **Notable quote**: *"The bottleneck appears precisely and exclusively where consciousness enters the picture. If consciousness just is the workspace broadcast, both directions should share the same bandwidth. The asymmetry between experiential richness and volitional poverty is easier to accommodate if the two directions involve different kinds of process, as the Map proposes."* (line 61)
- **Why it works**: The article converts a quantitative measurement (10⁸ ratio) into a *differential predictor* among competing interface models. It does not say "the bandwidth proves dualism" — it says "here is what each model must explain, and here is which model finds the explanation easiest." This is the Bayesian-abductive move applied carefully.
- **Secondary strength**: The evolutionary puzzle treatment (lines 81-85) is a model of philosophical fairness: the neural-architectural explanation is granted parsimony *within materialism*, while the interface explanation is granted unification *within dualism*. Neither is made to do work it cannot do. The reader is handed the disagreement clean.

### `voids/erasure-void.md`
- **Strongest point**: The *asymmetry-of-acquisition-and-loss* argument at lines 37-43. Learning leaves phenomenal markers; loss does not. The architecture of metacognitive monitoring (expectation, comparison, judgement) is named explicitly, and the failure modes of each step are mapped onto specific clinical syndromes. This is the cleanest anchoring of a void in empirical neuropsychology since the `infant-consciousness` article.
- **Notable quote**: *"Every mind carries an unknown inventory of what it has lost, and the loss of the inventory is itself part of what is lost."* (line 33)
- **Why it works**: The void is *genuinely new* within the taxonomy — it is not a restatement of self-opacity or inaccessible-past. The distinctive move is the identification of a *zero-signal* failure mode: where other voids are felt as opacity or aporia, this one is experienced as completeness. That completeness-experience is the void. Finding a new primary signature within a mature taxonomy is hard, and this article has done it.
- **Secondary strength**: The Dunning-Kruger connection at line 59 generalises the clinical case to ordinary cognition without overclaiming. The structural point — *any* system whose self-audit depends on the capacities under audit is vulnerable to silent erasure — is a philosophically respectable generalisation of a psychology result that is often misread.

### `voids/predictive-construction-void.md`
- **Strongest point**: The *double-veil* structure at lines 70-80. Two independent opacities — mind-body interface and Markov-blanket — are combined into a single epistemic structure that makes the dualist subject *more* veiled than standard dualism acknowledges. This is not an easy move to make without sounding evasive; the article makes it without flinching, and then grants at lines 116-118 that this is *a falsifiability risk*, not a feature to be celebrated.
- **Notable quote**: *"Treating first-person opacity as a virtue is the structural signature of a protected research programme; a Popperian would correctly flag it. The Map's position is defensible only if third-person tests remain available in principle."* (line 118)
- **Why it works**: The article *names* the philosophical-methodological risk its own thesis invites. This is the maturity signature that separates serious philosophical writing from advocacy. The Popperian concession is then followed by concrete specification of what third-person tests *would* look like — not rhetorical, not hand-waving.
- **Secondary strength**: The conditional-on-framework clause (lines 88-98) is the right way to situate a conditional void. Three live alternatives to predictive processing (Gibson, enactivism, Markov-blanket critique) are named honestly. The article says: if the framework is right, here is what follows; if it is wrong, here is what we would need to rebuild. This is how to do contingent philosophy without retreating to irrelevance.

### `voids/limits-reveal-structure.md`
- **Strongest point**: The illusionist-response at lines 104-110. Rather than dismissing illusionism, the article shows that *even granting it*, the limits-reveal-structure thesis survives: the representational closure is still closure, the architecture producing the illusion is itself architecture, and illusionism may be *symptomatic of* cognitive closure rather than its defeater. This is the judo move — taking the opponent's strongest position and converting it to support.
- **Notable quote**: *"To seem to hit a cognitive wall, something must experience that seeming. The architecture that produces the illusion of limits is itself architecture. Whether the limits constrain phenomenal access or accurate representation, mapping them still maps the mapper."* (line 106)
- **Why it works**: The article does not require the reader to pre-commit to a substantive metaphysics of experience. The thesis — limits reveal structure — holds across realist, illusionist, and mysterian readings. This is the *right* argumentative structure for a framing article: load-bearing across competing metaphysics, rather than dependent on one.

### `apex/taxonomy-of-voids.md`
- **Strongest point**: The *overdetermination* argument at lines 131-137. The taxonomy is not one argument resting on a single load-bearing beam; it is multiple independent arguments whose convergence does the work. This is exactly the structure dualism needs if it is to overcome the combinatorial advantage of physicalism. The line at 137 — *"Closing one void in a family would still leave the others intact, because they share structural logic but not identical content"* — is philosophically important. It names what *kind* of evidence the void catalogue is.
- **Notable quote**: *"The tenets do not merely accommodate the voids after the fact—they were formulated from independent philosophical arguments about consciousness, causation, and identity, and each generates a prediction about where explanation should fail. The systematic cataloguing confirmed these predictions rather than motivating them."* (line 139)
- **Why it works**: The apex answers the standard ad-hocness charge head-on. It is the *exact* move every dualist programme needs to make to escape the accusation of gerrymandering. The Map's five tenets each predict their own void — if that structure was fitted *after* cataloguing, the charge lands; if it was formulated in advance and confirmed by the catalogue, the charge fails. The article claims the latter and gives enough evidence to make the claim plausible.
- **Secondary strength**: The *partial independence* acknowledgement at lines 135 — the temporal void, dream consciousness void, and silence void *do not* fit the main convergence pattern. This is the honest move: a taxonomy that claimed 100% convergence would be suspect; 85-90% convergence with named exceptions is credible.

## Expansion Opportunities

### High Priority

#### The Double Veil as a Distinct Dualist Epistemology
- **Builds on**: `predictive-construction-void.md` (lines 70-80), `predictive-processing-and-dualism`, `asymmetric-bandwidth-consciousness.md`
- **Would address**: The double-veil structure — consciousness veiled from the brain by the interface, brain veiled from the world by the Markov blanket — is *philosophically distinctive*. It appears inside the predictive-construction-void article but is a general thesis about dualist epistemology rather than a void-specific consequence. A dedicated concept or topic article would (a) formalise the two-veil structure as a positive epistemological claim, (b) distinguish it from standard dualist positions that assume one veil or none, (c) examine its implications for representation, error, and testimony, (d) connect it to the bandwidth asymmetry (the inbound 10⁹ is brain→consciousness *of predictions*, not *of world*), and (e) flag the Popperian risks more systematically than the single paragraph at `predictive-construction-void.md` line 118.
- **Estimated scope**: Medium article (2000-2500 words)
- **Tenet alignment**: Dualism (a novel form), Minimal Quantum Interaction (the interface layer is one of the two veils), Occam's Razor Has Limits (double epistemic distance motivates caution about parsimony-driven metaphysics)

#### Zero-Signal Limits as an Epistemic Category
- **Builds on**: `erasure-void.md`, `recognition-void`, `taxonomy-of-voids.md` line 119 (silent erasure as eighth failure mode)
- **Would address**: The addition of "silent erasure" to the failure-signature topology introduces a fundamentally new epistemic category: cognitive limits that produce *no* phenomenal marker. The recognition void already touches this; the erasure void deepens it; but there is no concept page that treats the *category* of zero-signal limits systematically. A concept article would (a) name what distinguishes zero-signal limits from felt-as-opacity limits, (b) catalogue all the Map's zero-signal candidates (erasure, recognition, missing modalities, historical contingencies), (c) articulate the epistemological challenge — zero-signal limits are undetectable from inside and only discoverable through external audit, developmental comparison, or collision with prior evidence, (d) discuss the implications for introspective authority.
- **Estimated scope**: Medium article (1800-2200 words)
- **Tenet alignment**: Occam's Razor Has Limits (zero-signal limits are the hardest case for parsimony-based epistemology — you cannot even count what you cannot detect), Dualism (the category challenges purely structural accounts of mind)

#### The Evolutionary Signature of the 10-bit Ceiling
- **Builds on**: `asymmetric-bandwidth-consciousness.md` lines 81-85 (the evolutionary puzzle), `bandwidth-of-consciousness`
- **Would address**: The article names *two* candidate explanations for why the outbound ceiling has not been widened by evolution — neural-architectural inheritance and mind-brain-interface fixity — and declines to decide between them. But the *evolutionary* signature itself could be investigated further. Is the ceiling stable across vertebrates, or does it vary? Do cognitive-load tasks vs motor-skill tasks show the same ceiling across taxa? Are there species that measurably exceed 10 bits/s in deliberate-choice tasks? A topic-level article would (a) survey comparative bandwidth data across species, (b) treat the stability of the ceiling as *itself* evidence to be explained, (c) distinguish the neural-inheritance and interface explanations by predictions they differ on, (d) examine what would count as disconfirming evidence for each.
- **Estimated scope**: Medium article (2000-2500 words)
- **Tenet alignment**: Minimal Quantum Interaction (quantitative bandwidth is the empirical anchor), Bidirectional Interaction (interspecies comparison may reveal what evolved to enable causal influence).

### Medium Priority

#### Presence-Type vs Absence-Type Voids as Structural Distinction
- **Builds on**: `taxonomy-of-voids.md` lines 87-93 (the ineffable-encounter void passage), `ineffable-encounter-void`
- **Would address**: The taxonomy distinguishes absence-type voids (most of them) from presence-type voids (primarily the ineffable-encounter void) — but this distinction is currently made in one paragraph of the apex and has no dedicated treatment. A concept page would (a) formalise the distinction as a classificatory axis, (b) survey which voids exhibit presence-type structure vs absence-type, (c) examine the seventh failure signature ("dissolution under attention") as characteristic of presence-type voids, (d) connect to the aesthetic-void, mood-void, and numinous traditions where presence-type structure is dominant. This would enrich the void taxonomy with a cross-cutting classification the current organisation lacks.
- **Estimated scope**: Short-medium article (1500-1800 words)
- **Tenet alignment**: Dualism (presence-type voids are the phenomenological signature of consciousness-irreducible content), Occam's Razor Has Limits (presence-type voids resist functional reduction differently from absence-type).

#### Inspection-Paradox Structure Across Multiple Voids
- **Builds on**: `predictive-construction-void.md` lines 47-48 (inspection paradox), `mutation-void`, `self-opacity`
- **Would address**: The "inspection paradox" — that examining the apparatus requires using it — appears across multiple voids (predictive construction, self-opacity, observation-and-measurement-void, mutation-void). A concept page would treat this as a *general structural feature* rather than an instance-specific problem: (a) identify the common structural pattern, (b) survey its instantiations across the void catalogue, (c) distinguish weak forms (using the apparatus but not transforming it) from strong forms (using transforms what is measured), (d) connect to the quantum measurement problem structurally. This would consolidate material currently scattered across articles.
- **Estimated scope**: Medium article (1800-2200 words)
- **Tenet alignment**: Minimal Quantum Interaction (the observer-participancy theme), Dualism (the inspection paradox is a structural signature of subject-object identity at the interface).

#### Prediction-Error as Positive Phenomenon
- **Builds on**: `predictive-construction-void.md` lines 60-68 (prediction failure tears the veil), `phenomenology-of-the-edge`
- **Would address**: The article lists five cases where prediction failure briefly reveals the constructive nature of perception (illusions, surprise, ambiguous figures, altered states, expertise transitions). This is a rich set of phenomenological cases treated in one paragraph. A concept or topic page would give each its proper treatment and connect prediction-error phenomenology to the Map's broader thesis that *cognitive limits become visible at their edges* — a topic already developed in `phenomenology-of-the-edge` but not yet explicitly connected to predictive-processing phenomenology.
- **Estimated scope**: Medium article (1800-2200 words)
- **Tenet alignment**: Dualism (prediction error is one of the few places consciousness briefly sees its own mediation), Bidirectional Interaction (attention to prediction error is an act of conscious influence on neural processing).
