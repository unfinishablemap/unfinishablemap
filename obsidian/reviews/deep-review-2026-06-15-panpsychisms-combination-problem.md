### Critical Issues Found

- **Wrong-paper / wrong-year citation — Mørch.** The References cited `Morch, H. H. (2018). Panpsychism and Causation: A New Argument and a Solution to the Combination Problem. In W. Seager (Ed.), *The Routledge Handbook of Panpsychism*. Routledge.` This is a **family-resolution error** splicing two distinct Mørch works:
  - *"Panpsychism and Causation: A New Argument and a Solution to the Combination Problem"* is her **2014 PhD dissertation** (University of Oslo; PhilArchive HASPAC-2), NOT a Routledge Handbook chapter.
  - The chapter she actually contributed to Seager's *Routledge Handbook of Panpsychism* (publisher-of-record year **2019**, not 2018) is titled *"The Argument for Panpsychism from Experience of Causation"* (PhilPapers MRCTAF).

  The article married the dissertation's title to the Handbook container with a wrong year. The in-body fusionism attribution is **accurate** (fusionism is developed in the "Panpsychism and Causation" work; corroborated by `research/voids-combination-void-2026-03-07.md`), so only the metadata was wrong. This survived all five prior reviews because they re-verified attribution *against previous reviews* (intra-corpus ratification), never against the live publisher.

  **Resolution**: References entry corrected to the dissertation — `Mørch, H. H. (2014). *Panpsychism and Causation: A New Argument and a Solution to the Combination Problem* (Doctoral dissertation, University of Oslo).` — which carries the cited title. Body inline cite aligned from "(2018)" to "(2014)", and surname normalized to "Mørch" throughout (4 instances) to match the corpus-canonical form already present in [[consciousness-and-the-metaphysics-of-laws-and-dispositions]] (lines 209-210, which correctly disambiguates the 2014 dissertation from the Handbook chapter).
