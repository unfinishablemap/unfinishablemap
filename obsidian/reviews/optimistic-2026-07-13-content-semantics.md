## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
`the-naturalisation-failure-for-content.md` and `hard-problem-of-content.md` do for *content* what the hard-problem articles do for *phenomenal character*: they locate a distinct explanatory gap (intensionality, the "under which guise" problem) that survives every naturalising resource, and they run it as a structural ally of the phenomenal explanatory gap rather than conflating the two. Chalmers would recognise the Hutto-Myin dilemma as a genuine second front and appreciate that the Map keeps the two gaps analytically separate.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
Praise already banked by the 07-09 quantum-foundations pass and not re-litigated. Worth reaffirming only that `contextual-selection-in-quantum-foundations.md` gives the Kochen-Specker theorem full heading-level treatment (Spekkens preparation-contextuality, Peres proofs) and is careful that contextuality supplies *a necessary structured space* for selection, not the rational-agency claim itself — exactly the "within the formalism, not gesturing at it" discipline Stapp demanded.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The content articles center the first-person "what it is to mean *that p* under a guise" and refuse to let sub-personal covariation stand in for it. That is the semantic analogue of Nagel's insistence that the objective story omits the point of view.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The naturalisation-failure case avoids crude substance-talk: it does not claim content is a special substance, only that it resists reduction to covariance-plus-function. Compatibility-friendly without being asked to carry evidential weight it cannot bear.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
`contextual-selection` explicitly declines to let contextuality *deliver* rational agency, flagging that "consciousness fills this space with rational agency rather than functioning as a sophisticated random number generator requires its own defence." That is the honest placement of the libertarian's remaining burden.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
`hard-problem-of-content` concedes the Mann-Pain (2022) reply is a live escape (narrow what content must deliver) and grants the argument "is not a knock-down." Appropriate humility about the reach of the Map's own favoured argument.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
No minimal-organism or boundary-case tier-upgrade risk in this cycle's material — the one nomination below is a rival *theory of content*, not an organism, and carries no possibility/probability slippage. Birch's load-bearing praise here is for the naturalisation-failure article's explicit **"Honest Limitations"** section, which names the Mann-Pain narrowing as a place where "readers who accept the narrowing will find the Map's argument less compelling" — the register-keeping discipline of admitting exactly where the anti-reduction case is dialectically reasonable rather than decisive. The Process Philosopher and the Hardline Empiricist **converge** on this article: its anti-reduction intuition and its evidential restraint are both present and neither is doing the other's job.

## Expansion Opportunities

### High Priority — Nominated Uncovered Subject

#### Conceptual (Inferential) Role Semantics
- **Article-title-shaped noun phrase**: "Conceptual Role Semantics and the Naturalisation of Content" (or "Inferential-Role Semantics") — **section: `concepts/`**.
- **Why a gap (evidence)**: The Map's Tenet-1 anti-reduction case surveys exactly three naturalising families and no more. `the-naturalisation-failure-for-content.md` (§ "The Naturalising Project") names **Dretske informational semantics, Millikan teleosemantics, and Clark predictive processing**. `hard-problem-of-content.md` adds only **"The Functionalist Reply"** — and that section engages the *deflationary Dennettian/heterophenomenological* version ("saying-that is a functional posit... a useful pattern of interpretation"), which the Map rebuts as "sliding from explaining content to explaining it away." The **constitutive** conceptual/inferential-role tradition — Block's narrow content via inferential role, Harman's conceptual role, Sellars→Brandom's inferentialism — is a *different and stronger* claim: content is *constituted by* a state's inferential/functional role among other states, **not** read off world-covariance. It therefore steps neatly out from under Hutto & Myin's "covariance does not constitute content" dilemma, which is the entire engine of the Map's argument. Conceptual-role semantics appears in **zero** content files (only `voids/inference-void.md`), so the strongest live rival to the Map's naturalisation-failure thesis is currently unengaged.
- **Cluster siblings that already have homes**: `teleosemantics` (live), `content-externalism` (research-topic queued this session), Dretske indicator-semantics (deferred to harvester), plus `intentionality`, `status-of-content`, `hard-problem-of-content`, `symbol-grounding-problem`. Conceptual/inferential-role semantics is the conspicuous missing member of the four-way naturalisation-of-content map. It is also the direct Sellarsian descendant of the just-created `sellars-manifest-and-scientific-images`, giving a live internal thread.
- **Would address**: The unengaged-rival hole in the Tenet-1 argument — if content can be naturalised by inferential role rather than covariance, "content resists physicalist reduction" needs a dedicated response (the Map's likely line: inferential role either presupposes already-contentful inferential norms — regress — or collapses back into the same interpretationist deflation, reproducing the covariance dilemma one level up; but that argument is not currently made anywhere).
- **Estimated scope**: Medium (concepts ceiling).
- **Tenet alignment**: Tenet 1 (irreducibility of the mental). Mirrors the `consciousness-and-the-normativity-of-reason` / `rational-normativity` structure — content-normativity as a second irreducible register alongside phenomenal character.
- **Confirmed NOT on the exclude list**: not among the created/queued/deferred items (teleosemantics, Dretske, content-externalism, LOT/RTM, etc.), and not named in any 07-08→07-12 optimistic/harvest review (grep-verified).
