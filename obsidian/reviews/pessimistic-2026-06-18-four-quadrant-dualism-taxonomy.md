## Critiques by Philosopher

### The Eliminative Materialist (Churchland)
The whole exercise is folk-psychological cartography. You have built a two-by-two grid whose *axes are not operationalised* — "ontological weight," "structural richness above the minimum proponents are forced to concede" — and then sorted historical figures into cells by interpretive feel. None of these quadrants makes a prediction a neuroscientist could test. Q1's "biasing agent at indeterminacy" and Q4's "rich observer with selection power" are not rival empirical hypotheses; they are rival ways of decorating the same ignorance. When the explanatory gap closes, every cell on this grid evaporates together. The article's own admission that placements are "by judgement rather than definition" is the tell: a taxonomy whose membership criterion is connoisseurship is not carving nature, it is curating intuitions.

### The Hard-Nosed Physicalist (Dennett)
The article quietly inflates an intuition into a coordinate axis. "How much ontological work does the mind do?" presupposes that there is a determinate fact of the matter about mental ontology to be weighed — exactly what is in dispute. Worse, the Q2 section concedes that Q2 is "the dialectical default" and "feels virtuous in a materialist-leaning discipline," then frames the Map's preferred region as the underexplored alternative. That is a sociological diagnosis doing argumentative work: "philosophers avoid Q4 because of training conventions" lets the article treat the emptiness of Q4 as bias rather than as evidence that the cell is uninhabitable for good reasons. The honest reading of an empty cell is that nobody could make it work, not that everybody was too timid to try.

### The Quantum Skeptic (Tegmark)
The article leans on a "min-physical" reading where "physics specifies probabilities but leaves outcomes undetermined," and on Stapp's "quantum-Zeno biasing." It at least cites Tegmark (2000) and the decoherence dispute via the Born-rule status article. But it never confronts the consequence for its own grid: if decoherence is as fast as I calculate, then the entire Q1 "biasing-at-indeterminacy-without-thick-physics" cell is *empty for physical reasons*, not merely articulation-pressured. The article treats Q1's emptiness as an open articulability question; I would say the timing objection already decides it, and the taxonomy should say so rather than deferring to a sibling article.

### The Many-Worlds Defender (Deutsch)
Your grid has no cell for me, and that is not an oversight you should be comfortable with. MWI is min-mind *and* min-physical in the only sense that matters — it adds nothing to the Schrödinger equation. By your own axes it should be the deepest Q1 inhabitant, yet it appears nowhere, because your taxonomy is built to range over "non-reductive positions on mind–matter" and silently excludes the interpretation that dissolves the mind–matter interaction problem entirely. A taxonomy that defines its domain so as to exclude the strongest rival is question-begging at the level of scope.

### The Empiricist (Popper's ghost)
What observation would move a position from one quadrant to an adjacent one? The article gives "candidate empirical anchors" for thick mind (dissociation, hypnagogia, dream mentation) but immediately says whether any "warrant inflating the mind-side... is a question the Map has not yet settled." So the axis is currently unanchored on the mind side and, by the article's own indistinguishability admissions elsewhere in the corpus, unanchored on the physical side too. A two-axis grid neither of whose axes can be empirically located is a conceptual diagram, not a finding — which is fine, provided the article never claims the axis "tracks something" the rivals miss. It does make that claim. That is the overreach.

### The Buddhist Philosopher (Nagarjuna)
The mind-thickness axis reifies precisely what should be deconstructed. "How much mental substance is there?" assumes a self-standing mental side that could carry more or less weight. The Madhyamaka reply — already conceded at the *tenets* page's No-Many-Worlds note — is that the question is ill-formed: there is no mind-stuff to be thick or thin, only dependent arising. Q3's "thick subliminal self" and Q4's "res cogitans" are the same clinging error at different magnitudes. The article gestures at neutral monism as a Q1 edge case but never lets the no-self reading touch the *axis itself*, only the cells.

## Medium Issues

### Issue 1: The novelty claim outruns what "judgement, not definition" placements can support
- **File**: four-quadrant-dualism-taxonomy.md
- **Location**: §"Limits of the Thickness Metaphor" vs. §"Why the Thickness Axis Matters"
- **Problem**: The article's central pay-off claim is that the thickness axis "discriminates these cases" where "Chalmers' classification by relation type does not" (Nida-Rümelin vs. Descartes; Saad vs. Chalmers). But the limits section concedes thickness is *four* distinguishable measures (structural richness, causal work, counterfactual role, channel width) compressed into one, and that the grid places Stapp — the article's flagship illustration — "by judgement rather than definition." If the discriminating power is what sells the axis, and the discriminations are admitted to be judgement calls on a conflated measure, then the strongest worry (raised inside the article as "a skeptic might suspect the axis is merely a re-parameterisation of Type-F vs Type-E") is answered by example rather than by criterion. The reply "the axes seem to pull apart on concrete cases" is an existence claim resting on the very placements whose definitional status the article disclaims.
- **Severity**: Medium
- **Recommendation**: A `refine-draft` pass could state explicitly that the axis's job is *heuristic discrimination* (it surfaces a question the relation-type taxonomies hide) rather than *definitional discrimination* (sorting by a sharp criterion), and scope the "discriminates these cases" sentence accordingly. The substance is fine; the calibration of the claim is one notch too strong given the conceded conflation.

### Issue 2: "Sociological emptiness" of Q4 does argumentative work it has not earned
- **File**: four-quadrant-dualism-taxonomy.md
- **Location**: §"Quadrant 4" and §"Why the Thickness Axis Matters"
- **Problem**: The article asserts Q4's under-discussion "is *sociological* — the cell is coherent and inhabited, merely disfavoured," contrasting it with the channel-width axis's structurally-impossible vacant cell. It does add the honest hedge that "the parsimony pressure is not merely sociological" and that maximum-commitment-on-both-sides carries "real explanatory costs." But the framing still front-loads the sociological diagnosis ("often dismissed as pre-modern," "training conventions") before the cost concession, leaving the rhetorical weight on "the discipline is biased" rather than "the cell is expensive." An eliminativist or physicalist reader will read this as the article explaining away contrary evidence (an empty cell) by impugning the motives of those who left it empty.
- **Severity**: Medium
- **Recommendation**: Reorder so the genuine explanatory-cost reason leads and the sociological observation follows as a secondary contributor, not the headline. This is a calibration/ordering fix, not a content change — the cost concession is already present, just downstream of the bias claim.

## Counterarguments to Address

### The Q1/Q2 boundary may be unstable in a way that threatens the Map's stated region
- **Current content says**: The Bidirectional Interaction tenet "rules out *pure* Q2 epiphenomenalism... Q2 is then only open to 'weak causal efficacy' readings that collapse toward Q1," and the Map's region "excludes only the epiphenomenalist and idealist extremes," sitting "close to Q1."
- **A critic would argue**: If every causally-efficacious Q2 reading "collapses toward Q1," and Q1's own articulability is the article's central open question (it "may be a degenerate limit between Q2 and Q4"), then the Map's preferred region is pinned to a quadrant the article itself flags as possibly degenerate. The reassuring "region not a single cell" framing could be masking the possibility that the surviving region is *thin* — bounded below by an unworkable Q1 and above by a parsimony-costly Q4, with the middle being exactly the "physical-thin / mind-medium" point the Map already occupies. The taxonomy may show less room to manoeuvre than "a region, not a cell" implies.
- **Suggested response**: The article need not resolve this, but a sentence acknowledging that the surviving region's *interior* depends on Q1 turning out to be a genuine quadrant (per the `articulability-of-q1` open question) would make the dependency honest rather than letting "a region" suggest comfortable latitude.

### Excluding MWI by domain definition
- **Current content says**: The taxonomy "ranges over non-reductive positions on mind–matter."
- **A critic would argue**: This scope decision quietly removes the one interpretation (MWI) on which the mind–matter interaction problem does not arise, which is precisely the rival the Map's No-Many-Worlds tenet must defeat on independent grounds. A reader could see the grid as structurally pre-committed to the interaction framing.
- **Suggested response**: One scope-note sentence noting that MWI is out of frame because the grid presupposes a mind–matter relation MWI declines, with a pointer to `many-worlds-argument`, would pre-empt the question-begging charge.
