## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The article keeps the explanatory gap load-bearing: it parallels the *normative* gap ("a belief that fails to match the world *ought* to change") to the phenomenal explanatory gap, arguing that describing error-correction circuitry no more entails a state's "ought-to-match" than describing neural correlates entails experience. That is the hard problem applied to normativity — a clean extension Chalmers would endorse.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
Less central here, but the double-direction-of-fit case (intention-in-action both representing and causing the arm's rising) is exactly the place a selection/interaction account locates mind entering the physical — the article frames it as "consciousness both registers physical states and selects among possible physical outcomes," which maps cleanly onto the Map's interface picture.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The article centres the first-person: the *felt* sense that a belief is wrong, the *experienced* pull of a desire toward its object. It explicitly says the phenomenal dimension of normativity "is not obviously captured" by the mechanism — the right Nagelian instinct that structure-talk leaves the subjective remainder untouched.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The article avoids crude substance-talk: direction of fit is treated as a *relation* between representation and world, and intention-in-action as a simultaneously bidirectional process rather than a static mental object. Process-philosophical compatibility is present without being asked to do evidential work.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
The "Direction of Fit and Agent Causation" section is the strongest passage for Kane: world-to-mind fit is read as the agent holding an intention whose content specifies a not-yet-actual state and bringing it about — "precisely what substances exercising causal powers looks like from the intentional perspective." Agency is taken at face value, with the event-causal alternative shown to make "direction" merely metaphorical.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
Appropriate humility is on display in "What Would Challenge This View?" — three concrete defeaters (normative naturalism succeeding, functional fit proving sufficient, AI exhibiting genuine world-to-mind fit). The article does not claim direction of fit *settles* anything; it claims the question is open and shifts the burden. That is the calibrated McGinn register.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This article touches no minimal-organism / animal-boundary claim and makes **no tenet-as-evidence-upgrade move** — there is no five-tier evidential slippage to flag. The praise-worthy restraint to *name*: the placebo paragraph explicitly concedes "a physicalist can attribute this content-specificity to neural patterns rather than irreducible mental content" before sharpening the puzzle, and the functionalist/biological objections are stated at full strength with the Map's reply kept modest ("evolutionary function may explain *why* these structures exist without explaining *what they are*"). The thermostat counter-example is allowed to bite. This is tenet-coherent argument presented as a *burden-shift*, not as a metaphysical-possibility result smuggled up to "realistic possibility." The Process Philosopher and the Hardline Empiricist converge here: process-compatibility is present and the article declines to cash it out as a tier-upgrade. Calibration is honest.
