## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The disguised-property-dualism diagnostic is exactly the move Chalmers' own work invites: it makes visible how much physicalist refinement under hard-problem pressure already concedes, and "how small the further step to overt dualism actually is." The treatment of his and McQueen's (2022) consciousness-collapse proposal in the QM article is accurate and fair — distinguishing "consciousness causes collapse" from "consciousness modulates collapse" and locating the Map's compatibility with both. The explanatory gap is taken with full seriousness across every article; the predictive-processing piece nails the point that "the more precisely PP describes the brain's operations, the sharper the hard problem becomes."

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
The QM-interpretations survey engages the physics-consciousness interface at a level Stapp would recognise as serious: the von Neumann chain analysis, the quantum Zeno mechanism (via the pragmatist-foundations link), the consciousness-modulated CSL variants (Kremnizer-Ranchin, Okon-Sebastián, Chalmers-McQueen), and a frank treatment of the Tegmark decoherence objection with the Hagan et al. rebuttal and the 2024 epothilone-B finding. The comparative-assessment table is genuinely useful — it ranks interpretations by Map compatibility without overstating support.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The comparative-phenomenology-of-mathematical-insight article is a Nagelian tour de force. It centres "what understanding feels like" and argues that the same formal content yields different phenomenal textures across traditions — a direct instance of the irreducibility of the first-person. The disjunctive argument (genuine difference / same experience differently described / confabulation) is the article's strongest structural feature: it extracts philosophical significance from every horn while conceding the eliminativist horn weakens the case.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The transactional-interpretation section explicitly connects completing transactions to Whitehead's "actual occasions" — moments of becoming neither determined by the past nor external to experience. This is the right kind of process-philosophical resonance: it removes a defeater for consciousness-involvement in physical becoming without being used to manufacture evidence. The bi-aspectual ontology threading in disguised-property-dualism likewise avoids crude substance dualism while preserving the reality of mind.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
The predictive-processing article gives Kane exactly what he wants: a precise neural location (precision weighting at the top of the hierarchy) where "something breaks the symmetry" among options the learned priors underdetermine, paired with the selection-only reading (consciousness adds no energy, writes no forbidden value). The Bohmian-mechanics section in the QM article is candid that determinism is "the interpretation most hostile to the Map's framework" — agency is taken seriously enough to name its defeater.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
Epistemic humility is pervasive and load-bearing, not decorative. The QM article's "Honest limitation" box ("physics hasn't settled" does not mean consciousness-interpretations are well-supported) and "The critical distinction" ("leave room" is not "support") are model statements of restraint. The math-phenomenology article opens with an explicit methodological caveat that no systematic cross-cultural study exists and frames its thesis as a *testable hypothesis*. The disguised-property-dualism article applies the diagnostic to the Map itself and concedes a reader may find the defence "too thin to bear the weight" — disagreeing "in good faith."

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This persona's verdict is strongly positive, and load-bearing here because three of the four articles touch tenet-load-bearing claims. The QM article is a clean exemplar of *tenet-as-evidence-upgrade praise-worthily not done*: the MWI reservation is "calibrated honestly as a framework-boundary disagreement, not an in-framework refutation," the Deutsch-Wallace/Saunders/Sebens-Carroll programme is called "internally available to MWI… not incoherent on Everettian terms," and the live in-framework critiques (Lewis 2007, Friederich-Dawid 2022) are named rather than buried. The Map's tenet commitment is "carried in openly as a tenet," not smuggled as evidence. The math-phenomenology article keeps registers separate: it never upgrades the Indian/Chinese/Islamic traditions' phenomenology from "textual inference" to "attested first-person data," repeatedly flagging "we lack direct first-person testimony." The disguised-property-dualism diagnostic explicitly disclaims being a "metaphysical verdict" — its utility is "philosophical hygiene, not demonstration." No possibility/probability slippage detected in any of the four. The Process Philosopher and Hardline Empiricist *converge* in praising the transactional/actual-occasions passage and the bi-aspectual self-application — the tension is resolved honestly in both cases.

## Content Strengths

### topics/qm-interpretations-beyond-many-worlds.md
- **Strongest point**: The "probability advantage of collapse" argument — that every interpretation either postulates, disguises, or relocates the Born rule, and collapse honestly postulates it while MWI must derive it — framed as a "structural asymmetry" rather than a refutation of MWI.
- **Notable quote**: "The Map doesn't claim physics has established consciousness's role in collapse; it claims physics hasn't ruled it out."
- **Why it works**: Converts an open interpretive landscape into a positive case for the Map without overreach, and the comparative table makes the reasoning auditable at a glance.

### topics/comparative-phenomenology-of-mathematical-insight.md
- **Strongest point**: The three-way disjunctive argument that extracts philosophical significance whether the cross-cultural variation is genuine, descriptive, or confabulated.
- **Notable quote**: "Mathematicians can disagree about *what mathematical insight feels like* while agreeing about *what is mathematically true*."
- **Why it works**: The Ramanujan bridge case (Indian-trained, Western-recognised, fitting neither phenomenological model cleanly) is a vivid, memorable exhibit, and the convergence-beneath-variation section keeps the universalist and relativist readings both in play.

### concepts/disguised-property-dualism.md
- **Strongest point**: The three-condition diagnostic with three explicit standards for "showing" a reduction (logical derivation / explanatory bridge / progressive research programme), then the self-application turning the tool on the Map.
- **Notable quote**: "The two-structure architecture carries the explanatory load; the monist label carries the reputational cost of dualism without paying its conceptual rent."
- **Why it works**: It is a reusable analytic instrument, applied "generously rather than punitively," with the chemistry/vitalism analogy doing real work to defend the Map's own two-level mechanism claim.

### topics/predictive-processing-and-dualism.md
- **Strongest point**: The precision-weighting-as-interface integration — "PP describes *what* precision weighting does; the Map proposes *who* is doing the weighting" — which co-opts the leading cognitive-science framework as an ally rather than a rival.
- **Notable quote**: "Far from threatening dualism, PP is its most productive scientific ally."
- **Why it works**: Each of the five tenets gets a specific, non-formulaic hook in the Relation-to-Site section, and the meta-problem rebuttal (explains consciousness-*talk*, not consciousness) is crisp.
