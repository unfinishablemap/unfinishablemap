## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The tactile article keeps Chalmers' gap *sharpening* as mechanism is described, never closing it: affective touch and pain "present a felt valence at the point of contact that is conspicuously more than the registration of a stimulus class," giving "a clean illustration of the explanatory gap in evaluative register" while the dedicated-fibre physicalist reply is left standing. The interoceptive article generalises the gap inward without inflating it — felt heartbeat, nausea, the ache of cold "arrive already valenced and already owned," with no neutral percept on which a verdict is later pronounced. Both keep the gap intact against their own preferred reading rather than declaring premature victory.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
Restraint Stapp would respect: the tactile article keeps Minimal Quantum Interaction and No Many Worlds entering "only obliquely" — "No tactile datum secures the quantum-interface mechanism; the dependence runs the other way, from the framework to the reading of the data." The interoceptive article is equally disciplined, never reaching for a quantum mechanism the data cannot support. Neither piece conscripts physics as load-bearing support.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The tactile article is deeply phenomenological without sliding into mere description. The touching-touched section traces Merleau-Ponty's *double sensation* and the late *reversibility of the flesh* ("if my hand, while it is felt from within, is also accessible from without"), keeping the first-person datum primary: "Touch alone folds the perceiver into the perceived field." The interoceptive article centres the felt body as "the felt ground of selfhood rather than content presented to a subject" — the Nagelian "what it is like" carried to the modality where the subject/object distinction is forced rather than assumed.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
Both articles treat mind and matter as making *distinguishable contributions* to a single capability rather than as warring substances — the interoceptive article's "the body is *had*, not constitutive" is precisely the two-aspect spirit Whitehead would endorse, and it is held without inflating any modality up an evidential tier. Critically, the interoceptive article does *not* convert process-friendly intuitions (the felt body as ground of selfhood) into a positive metaphysical result: it explicitly engages Damasio's *constitutive* reading ("feelings are the source of consciousness") as a **rival the Map does not share**, marking the disagreement at the framework boundary rather than papering it with process-philosophical resonance. That is exactly the discipline the persona's constraint demands.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
The tactile article grounds genuine mental participation in perception through Bidirectional Interaction: active haptics is "the modality where the active, directed character the perception article attributes to all perceiving is least deniable — there is barely a passive haptic percept to fall back on," and it weights this correctly as "an interpretive alignment, not a finding." The interoceptive article shows the felt body "participates in its own regulation" (the predicted blush triggering the vasodilation that fulfils it) — agency woven into perception — while conceding the active-inference rival re-describes the same loop in wholly physical terms.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
The interoceptive void's "silent floor" is the mysterian touch: most visceral signal "never reaches awareness at any temporal grain," and the interface article inherits that boundary as a structural opacity rather than a temporary ignorance. The tactile article's modesty is repeatedly explicit ("The reading offered here is deliberately modest"; "a striking phenomenological datum; it is not, by itself, an argument that the subject is non-physical"), keeping the wall where the evidence puts it.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch) — load-bearing verdict for the interoceptive article
This is where the interoceptive article earns its highest marks, because its core move is a **structural inversion claim** ("the cross-modal asymmetry is exteroceptive, not universal; interoception inverts both its terms") — exactly the kind of result most prone to being dressed up as confirmation of the interface. The article refuses the dressing at every turn:

- It names the convergent insular substrate as "precisely what a physicalist predicts if the body needs one homeostatic integrator," and states flatly: "The convergence is a structural disanalogy with exteroception, genuinely sharpening the apex's claim; it is not, by itself, evidence for an interface. The brain-side fact is neutral between the readings."
- It invokes the Map's **common-cause null** against its own evidence: "a single insular hub is maximally a common cause, so the interoceptive evidence is one datum about the inward limit, not a stack of independent ones." This is the "tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating" pattern applied to a *structural* finding — the article had the opportunity to bank the convergence as fresh confirmation and explicitly declined.
- The third mind-side calibration is the cleanest: the affect-and-selfhood reframing "does not by itself favour the interface. It favours *some* account on which interoception is self-constituting, and the leading such account is the physicalist one." Naming that the strongest reading of one's own headline datum is the rival's is the restraint the persona exists to praise.
- It engages **two** rivals built to dispense with a non-physical interface (interoceptive inference: Seth 2013, Barrett & Simmons 2015; the constitutive view: Damasio, Park & Tallon-Baudry 2014) and conscripts neither, locating the Map's value-add precisely at the ownership term while conceding it is "candidly an argumentative claim rather than a place the Map holds direct cross-modal evidence the rival lacks."

For the tactile article the Birch verdict is also strongly positive, on the "accommodation, not proof" axis: the affective-touch passage refuses to read C-tactile fibres as needing a non-physical carrier ("A dedicated affective fibre system is a *physicalist mechanism*"), and the rubber-hand passage is a model of two-sided reading — it relocates the felt *boundary* but "does not explain, and does not dissolve, the ownership the relocated boundary is felt within," routing the metaphysical residue to the ownership article rather than over-claiming it locally.

**Process Philosopher / Hardline Empiricist convergence:** No conflict in either article. In the interoceptive article the personas praise different aspects of the *same* discipline — the Process Philosopher praises the had-not-constitutive two-aspect framing; the Hardline Empiricist praises the refusal to let that framing cash out as evidence-for-interface, and the explicit rejection of Damasio's constitutive over-reach. They converge. No possibility/probability slippage arises in either article, so **no calibration `refine-draft` is warranted** — the only opportunities are link-reciprocity repairs below.

## Content Strengths

### topics/interoceptive-consciousness-and-the-interface.md
- **Strongest point**: The double inversion as a *test* of the apex, not an extension of it. The article's reason to exist is that interoception is the counter-instance to the cross-modal synthesis's most important negative finding ("nothing recurs on the brain side"), and it shows the modality-specificity "was contingent on those separate transducers rather than a structural necessity of perception." Self-correcting an apex's headline law from a new case — and sharpening the law by precisely locating it as exteroceptive — is the corpus operating as a living, falsifiable framework.
- **Notable quote**: "The asymmetry the apex names appears to be exteroceptive, and interoception is the inward case that marks its limit."
- **Why it works**: Front-loads the inversion in the first paragraph (truncation-resilient), grounds every structural claim in live, specific scholarship (Craig 2002; Critchley et al. 2004; Seth 2013; Barrett & Simmons 2015; Garfinkel et al. 2015; the 2025 Loescher/Haggard/Tallon-Baudry PNAS cardiac-vs-tactile competition finding), and threads three explicit calibrations ("Where the Inward Inversion Is Constrained") that locate rather than weaken the finding.

### topics/tactile-consciousness-and-the-interface.md
- **Strongest point**: The disciplined division of labour across neighbouring articles. The opening two paragraphs explicitly fence the piece against the treatments it builds on rather than repeats — proprioception/body-schema go to the dual-domain article, the metaphysics of mineness goes to the ownership problem, pain's causal role goes to pain-consciousness — leaving "the genuinely uncovered material here" (cutaneous touch, active haptics, touching-touched, the affective channel, malleable ownership) sharply scoped. This is what keeps a 20-article modality programme from becoming 20 overlapping restatements.
- **Notable quote**: "The rubber-hand illusion relocates the *boundary* of the felt body; it does not explain, and does not dissolve, the ownership the relocated boundary is felt within."
- **Why it works**: Every distinctive datum is paired with the physicalist account that explains it (Gibson active touch → sensorimotor models; McGlone C-tactile → dedicated fibres; Botvinick & Cohen → multisensory integration), each adopted rather than waved away, and the "Relation to Site Perspective" section names the empirical sources as *naturalist authors who draw no dualist conclusions* — pre-empting the conscription charge.
