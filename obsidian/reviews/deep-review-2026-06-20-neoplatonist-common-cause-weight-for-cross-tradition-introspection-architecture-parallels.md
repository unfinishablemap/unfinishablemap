### Critical Issues Found (CITATION DEFECTS — fixed)

Two intertwined citation defects, both traced to a single conflation in the source research note:

1. **Reference #3 — wrong author + wrong year + wrong venue (real paper).** The article (and the research note, and the parent exhibit) attributed the chapter *"Plotinus, Porphyry, and India: A Re-Examination"* to **"Aklan (2013), in *Knowledge and the Sacred*, Palgrave Macmillan, ch. 7."** Publisher-of-record verification (Springer DOI 10.1057/9780230240773_7) shows the chapter is by **Joachim Lacrosse**, in **"Late Antique Epistemology: Other Ways to Truth"** ed. **P. Vassilopoulou & S. R. L. Clark**, Palgrave Macmillan, **2009**, ch. 7, pp. 103–116 — NOT Aklan, NOT 2013, NOT *Knowledge and the Sacred*. State: **real-wrong-metadata** (real paper, wrong author/year/venue). Corrected in place to the canonical Lacrosse 2009 citation. The chapter is genuinely relevant (it re-examines the Plotinus-Porphyry-India question and reaches the same anti-influence verdict), so it was corrected, not removed.

2. **Reference #2 — wrong title (real dissertation).** The article gave Aklan's CEU dissertation the title *"Wandering Lotuses: Plotinus, Porphyry, and India: A Re-Examination."* The CEU etd record (https://www.etd.ceu.edu/2019/aklan_anna.htm, .pdf) gives the actual title as **"Wandering Lotuses: Parallel Philosophical Illustrations in Late Antique Greek and in Indian Philosophies"** (defended 2018, DOI 10.14754/CEU.2018.07). The "Plotinus, Porphyry, and India: A Re-Examination" subtitle had been **borrowed from the Lacrosse chapter** — the two errors share one root cause (the research note conflated the Lacrosse Springer chapter with the Aklan CEU dissertation, both of which concern the same Plotinus-Porphyry-India question and reach the same verdict). State: **real-wrong-metadata** (wrong title). Corrected in place; URL/author/year unchanged. The **content** attributed to Aklan 2018 inline (parallels "do not involve structural and conceptual connection"; "metaphorical ways of expression") is **faithful** to the dissertation abstract — only the title was wrong.

3. **Inline ↔ References alignment — "Remes's Topoi article" → "Remes's *Plotinus on Self*."** The body (§Stoicism) attributed the Stoic-influence-on-Plotinus proprioception material to "Remes's Topoi article," but the cited Reference (#14, Remes 2008 *Plotinus on Self*, CUP) is the work that actually carries that content (verified: BMCR review + publisher confirm *Plotinus on Self* "highlights and properly justifies the Stoic influence in Plotinus' conception of bodily proprioception").
