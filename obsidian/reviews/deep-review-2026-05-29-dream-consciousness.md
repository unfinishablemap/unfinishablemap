## Pessimistic Analysis Summary

### Critical Issues Found (all citation defects; all fixed)

1. **Konkoly et al. 2026 statistics wrong.** The article reported the incorporated-vs-non-incorporated puzzle-solving rates as "42% versus 17%". The primary source (*Neuroscience of Consciousness*, 2026(1), niaf067; Konkoly, Morris, Hurka, Martinez, Sanders & Paller — verified via Oxford Academic) reports **39% (9 of 23 incorporated puzzles solved) versus 16%**. The "more than double" framing survives; the specific numbers were wrong. Corrected in all 4 occurrences in the live article (prose, phenomenal-mode-tracking table, epiphenomenalism specificity-problem paragraph, and caveat sentence). The metadata (venue, issue, article number, N=20) verified correct.

2. **Haar Horowitz et al. 2023 "43%" fabricated.** The article claimed targeted dream incubation "increased creative performance by 43%." The primary source (*Scientific Reports*, 13, 7319 — verified via PMC) reports a z-score Creativity Index composite and **no percentage improvement at all**. The 43% is a synthesis-time invention. Replaced with the qualitative, source-faithful claim ("significantly increased creative performance across three tasks relative to control conditions").

3. **Hohwy & Metzinger (2025), *Consciousness and Cognition*, 99, 103305 — fabricated reference.** No such paper exists; volume 99 of that journal is ~2022, and the article number does not resolve. In the live article it was an orphaned reference (never cited in body) — removed. The same phantom reference appears in the archive copy [[lucid-dreaming-and-consciousness]], where it is cited in-body attached to a **real direct quotation**. The quotation actually belongs to **Sinn, R. & Borner, M. (2025), "Dreaming as fascinated predictions: bridging Sartre's phenomenology and predictive processing," *Phenomenology and the Cognitive Sciences*** (DOI 10.1007/s11097-025-10104-4). This is a textbook author-conflation / synthesis-time misattribution: real quote, fabricated attribution. Re-attributed in the archive copy.
