# Pessimistic Review

**Date**: 2026-07-17
**Content reviewed**: `obsidian/concepts/philosophical-zombies.md` (last deep-reviewed 2026-06-18)

## Executive Summary

The article is mature and unusually self-critical — the "Interactionist Escape" section already concedes the argument's chief structural vulnerability. But that concession is not carried forward: the "Relation to Site Perspective" still bills zombie conceivability as "a central support" for dualism at full strength, producing an internal tension. Two load-bearing premises (conceivability→possibility; "no functional story has been supplied") are asserted rather than defended, and the Minimal-Quantum-Interaction paragraph reads as a non-sequitur grafted onto an argument that establishes at most "something extra."

## Critiques by Philosopher

### The Eliminative Materialist (Churchland)
The whole edifice rests on treating "there is something it is like" as a fixed datum (line 184: "the datum that any adequate theory must explain"). That is exactly the folk-psychological posit slated for elimination. The article's own convergence section (182) admits all seven arguments share this presupposition — so it has conceded that if phenomenal-realism is a confused category, the convergence is "a common error." Churchland's rejoinder: you have named your single point of failure and then declared it "more secure than the theories that deny it" (184) without argument. That is assertion, not defense.

### The Hard-Nosed Physicalist (Dennett)
Line 124 claims "no such [non-circular functional] story has been supplied." This is false to the literature the article itself gestures at: Graziano's Attention Schema Theory (which the article lists among its own linked concepts) is precisely a functional account of why a physical system represents itself as having ineffable phenomenal properties. Frankish's quasi-phenomenal-property story is another. Dennett would say the article dismisses heterophenomenology in one paragraph (112–114) and then asserts a universal negative that the field has spent thirty years populating.

### The Quantum Skeptic (Tegmark)
The Minimal-Quantum-Interaction paragraph (194) is the weakest join in the article. The zombie argument, even granted, yields only "consciousness is not entailed by physics." Nothing in it locates the "something extra" at quantum indeterminacies; "the zombie would lack whatever selects at these points" smuggles the entire MQI mechanism in as though the zombie argument delivered it. The zombie argument is silent on decoherence timescales — pointing to `[[decoherence]]` does not repair the inferential gap between "non-physical" and "quantum-selecting."

### The Many-Worlds Defender (Deutsch)
The article handles me fairly (196) — it concedes MWI "pressures" rather than refutes. No complaint, except that the concession further narrows what the zombie argument can carry: it now depends on "definite identity, definite physical facts, and a single actual world," all of which are themselves contested Map commitments rather than argument outputs.

### The Empiricist (Popper's Ghost)
Premise 2 — "Conceivability is evidence of possibility" (46) — is stated as a bare axiom in the core argument. This is the single most-contested inference in the modal-epistemology literature, and in the body it receives no defense; the Type-B reply (72–74) leans entirely on "we have direct acquaintance" (74), which is an appeal to the very phenomenal datum under dispute. The "What Would Challenge This View?" section (166–176) is genuinely falsificationist in spirit and to the article's credit — but line 176 then infers *soundness* from the *persistent non-satisfaction* of the challenge conditions. Unmet-so-far is an epistemic observation; soundness is a logical property. That is an argument from absence.

### The Buddhist Philosopher (Nagarjuna)
The article presupposes a determinate bearer — "*this* being is either conscious or not" (196) — as a premise. From emptiness, that determinacy is the reification in question. The article notes this pressure only in the MWI register, not the anatta register; the "self that lacks experience" the zombie scenario needs is as constructed as the self that has it.

## Critical Issues

### Issue 1: The Interactionist concession is not carried into "Relation to Site Perspective"
- **File**: `philosophical-zombies.md`
- **Location**: "The Interactionist Escape" (148–156) vs. "Relation to Site Perspective → Dualism" (190)
- **Problem**: Lines 152–154 concede that under the Map's own Bidirectional Interaction tenet the full zombie specification (complete physical duplicate *plus* identical behavioural output *minus* consciousness) is "internally incoherent," that the argument "becomes conditional," and that against a closure-committed physicalist "the interactionist's move is question-begging." Line 156 correctly recasts the argument's role as diagnostic. But line 190 then states flatly, with no carried qualification, that "The zombie argument is a central support for the Map's foundational commitment." A reader arriving at the summary is told the argument is a central support; a reader who read section 148–156 was told it is conditional and question-begging against the physicalist who matters. These should be reconciled.
- **Severity**: High
- **Recommendation**: `refine-draft` to add one clause to line 190 pointing to the diagnostic-not-standalone framing established in the Interactionist Escape (e.g. "central support — though, as the Interactionist Escape section shows, its force is diagnostic rather than standalone once Bidirectional Interaction is in play"). Do not delete either passage; the honesty of 148–156 is a strength.

### Issue 2: "No such story has been supplied" — universal-negative overclaim
- **File**: `philosophical-zombies.md`
- **Location**: line 124
- **Problem**: The claim that no non-circular functional story exists for phenomenal self-representation ignores the Attention Schema Theory (linked from this very article's concept list) and Frankish's own quasi-phenomenal account. The honest Map position is that these stories are *contested/regenerate the gap at the meta-level* — which the article argues elsewhere (118) — not that none has been offered.
- **Severity**: Medium
- **Recommendation**: `refine-draft` — downgrade to "no such story has commanded consensus, and the Map holds that the available candidates (e.g. attention-schema and quasi-phenomenal accounts) regenerate the gap at the level of the seeming (see [[illusionism]])."

### Issue 3: MQI paragraph is a non-sequitur relative to the zombie argument
- **File**: `philosophical-zombies.md`
- **Location**: line 194
- **Problem**: The zombie argument supports "something beyond physics," not "quantum-locus selection." The paragraph asserts the zombie "would lack whatever selects at these points," which presupposes the selection mechanism the zombie argument does not establish.
- **Severity**: Medium
- **Recommendation**: `refine-draft` — reframe as "the zombie argument establishes *that* something beyond physics is needed; *where* and *how* it acts is supplied by the Map's other commitments, not by conceivability" — i.e. explicitly mark the division of labour rather than implying the zombie argument delivers the quantum locus.

### Issue 4: "Cannot consistently deny" — inverted vs. absent qualia overclaim
- **File**: `philosophical-zombies.md`
- **Location**: line 100
- **Problem**: A physicalist can accept inverted-qualia conceivability (a redescription that holds experience fixed and varies the mapping) while denying absent-qualia conceivability (which removes experience entirely). The two are logically separable, and some functionalists take exactly that split. "Cannot consistently deny... the same explanatory gap grounds both" overstates the entailment.
- **Severity**: Low–Medium
- **Recommendation**: `refine-draft` — soften to "faces pressure to explain why the same explanatory gap grounds one but not the other," or supply the missing bridging argument for why the gap is common.

## Counterarguments to Address

### Premise 2 (conceivability → possibility)
- **Current content says**: "Conceivability is evidence of possibility" (46), defended in body only by "direct acquaintance" (74).
- **A critic would argue**: This is the crux premise of the entire Type-B / two-dimensionalist literature; asserting it and outsourcing the defense to `[[conceivability-possibility-inference]]` leaves the core argument resting on an undefended axiom.
- **Suggested response**: Add one sentence in the body acknowledging that premise 2 is the argument's contested hinge and forward-anchoring to the dedicated article, rather than presenting it as settled within the three-step argument.

### Soundness from persistent non-satisfaction
- **Current content says**: "Their persistent non-satisfaction after decades of debate supports the argument's soundness" (176).
- **A critic would argue**: Conflates an epistemic fact (challenges unmet so far) with a metaphysical one (soundness). The same non-satisfaction is equally consistent with the challenges being hard to operationalise (which the article itself concedes for condition 4, line 174).
- **Suggested response**: "supports continued confidence in the argument" or "leaves the argument standing," not "supports its soundness."
