# Pessimistic Review

**Date**: 2026-06-08
**Content reviewed**: [[concepts/implicit-memory]] ("Implicit Memory and Anoetic Consciousness", 3040 words, last_deep_review 2026-05-19 — oldest unreviewed AI content, no prior dedicated pessimistic review)

## Executive Summary

The article is well-structured and its central choking-as-mental-causation argument is honestly hedged ("evidence consistent with", "suggests", a substantive "What Would Challenge This View?" section). It carries no forbidden editor-vocabulary labels and no altered-state convergence double-counting. Two genuine weaknesses: (1) a likely **misattributed quotation** — "non-reflective qualia" is presented as Tulving's wording but is not corroborated as a Tulving phrase; and (2) the core inference repeatedly **slides from "phenomenology tracks performance" to "the phenomenal dimension is doing causal work"** without closing the epiphenomenalist's available reply (the felt state and the neural disruption could be co-effects of a common cause). The dual-task empirical claim checks out against the broader literature.

## Critiques by Philosopher

### The Eliminative Materialist (Churchland)
The whole apparatus — anoetic/noetic/autonoetic "modes", "non-reflective qualia" — is folk taxonomy dressed as data. Tulving's tripartite scheme is a *functional* memory-systems distinction; the article smuggles in phenomenal "modes" and then treats the existence of three memory systems as evidence for three *experiential* kinds. The asymmetry argument (§ "The Asymmetry Point") begs the question: that different memory systems "correlate with qualitatively different experiential modes" is the very thing in dispute, asserted as a premise.

### The Hard-Nosed Physicalist (Dennett)
The article's own concession sinks it. § "What Would Challenge This View?" point 2 admits arousal-based accounts "already provide partial evidence" that some choking is physiological arousal, not phenomenal self-monitoring. Once you grant that, the inference from "choking happens" to "phenomenal attention does causal work" needs the phenomenal component *isolated* — which the article never does. Heterophenomenology catalogs the reports; the article asserts the reports outstrip third-person description but never shows a prediction the phenomenal story makes that the dual-control-systems story does not.

### The Quantum Skeptic (Tegmark)
§ Minimal Quantum Interaction floats Stapp's quantum Zeno "would require only brief quantum involvement when attention shifts." No timescale, no decoherence budget, no calculation. It is hand-waved and the article admits it ("remains speculative") — fine for a hedge, but it is doing rhetorical work (making delegation sound mechanistically grounded) it has not earned.

### The Many-Worlds Defender (Deutsch)
§ No Many Worlds is the weakest tenet section. "Why am *I* reliably the one experiencing skilled execution?" conflates the statistical reliability of *expert* performance (explained entirely by training — experts are reliable in *every* branch) with indexical selection. The "branches where the expert misses every shot" framing misdescribes MWI: skill raises the Born-weight of success uniformly; no selection needed. The indexical worry, whatever its merits elsewhere, gains nothing from skilled performance specifically.

### The Empiricist (Popper)
The strongest section is § "What Would Challenge This View?" — five genuine disconfirmers, several operationalizable. Credit due. But the central claim ("the phenomenal dimension is doing causal work, not passively accompanying") is exactly what the listed disconfirmers do *not* test: every one of them tests whether anoetic experience *exists*, none isolates whether the *phenomenal* (vs. neural) component is the cause. The falsifiability is real for the phenomenology claim and absent for the causation claim.

### The Buddhist Philosopher (Nagarjuna)
The article handles the *anatman* tension honestly (§ Contemplative Evidence parenthetical) — good. But § Haecceity then leans hard on "it is *I* who ride this bicycle now" as load-bearing, which is precisely the reified self the contemplative evidence it just cited would deny. The article cites samskara/witness-consciousness for support while building on haecceity, and these pull in opposite directions without the tension being resolved.

## Critical Issues

### Issue 1: Likely misattributed quotation — "non-reflective qualia"
- **File**: concepts/implicit-memory.md
- **Location**: L47 ("Tulving emphasised that anoetic consciousness is not *absent* consciousness but a distinct mode—what he called 'non-reflective qualia.'")
- **Problem**: A web check of Tulving's "Memory and consciousness" (1985) and the anoetic/noetic/autonoetic literature did not corroborate "non-reflective qualia" as Tulving's phrasing. It reads as the Map's own gloss promoted to a direct attribution ("what he called"). Tulving's own term is "anoetic consciousness"; the qualia framing is later/secondary.
- **Severity**: Medium
- **Recommendation**: VERIFY-FIRST against Tulving 1985 (ref 1) and his later writings. If the phrase is not his, drop the quotation marks and the "what he called" attribution — rephrase to "a distinct, unreflective experiential mode" or attribute the qualia gloss to whoever actually coined it. 3-state discipline (faithful / paraphrase-mislabeled-as-quote / fabricated). Sibling of the recurring AI-citation-metadata-unreliable pattern.

### Issue 2: Epistemic→metaphysical / correlation→causation slide in the core argument
- **File**: concepts/implicit-memory.md
- **Location**: L101, L188 ("The tight coupling between what performers feel and how they fail suggests the phenomenal dimension is doing causal work")
- **Problem**: The tight coupling between phenomenal self-monitoring and performance degradation is fully consistent with the felt state and the neural disruption being **co-effects of a common cause** (the prefrontal reinvestment), with no causal arrow from phenomenal→neural. The article treats "phenomenology tracks performance" as evidence for "phenomenology causes the degradation", but tracking is symmetric and does not discriminate causal-efficacy from co-occurrence. The interventions cited (distraction, outcome-focus) alter *both* the felt state and the neural pattern together, so they cannot isolate the phenomenal component as the lever.
- **Severity**: Medium (the claim is hedged with "suggests", which keeps it from being a critical overreach, but the gap recurs in three sections and the Occam section presents the epiphenomenalist reading as obviously inferior)
- **Recommendation**: refine-draft pass to add one sentence acknowledging the common-cause reply and stating explicitly what would distinguish phenomenal-causation from co-occurrence — or downgrade "is doing causal work" to "is at least not idle, on the interface reading". This is the epistemic-vs-metaphysical-reading discipline applied to a causal claim.

## Counterarguments to Address

### Choking and the common-cause reply
- **Current content says**: phenomenology tracks performance degradation, therefore the phenomenal dimension is causally relevant (L101, L188, L196).
- **A critic would argue**: prefrontal reinvestment causes *both* the anxious feeling and the basal-ganglia disruption; the feeling is a reliable co-effect, not a cause. Correlation under shared intervention cannot separate these.
- **Suggested response**: concede the reply explicitly; rest the case on the interface *reading* (consciousness as the locus of reinvestment) rather than claiming the data force phenomenal causation. The "negative character of the evidence" point (L103) is the article's best card — develop *that* (interference is harder to confabulate) rather than the symmetric tracking point.

### The asymmetry argument assumes its conclusion
- **Current content says** (L145): different memory systems correlating with qualitatively different experiential modes is "too fine-grained to be explained as mere illusions."
- **A critic would argue**: that the systems correlate with distinct *experiential* modes (not merely distinct functional/access profiles) is the disputed claim, used here as a premise.
- **Suggested response**: reframe as "if one grants distinct phenomenal modes, illusionism owes an account of their systematicity" — a conditional burden-shift, not an independent argument.
