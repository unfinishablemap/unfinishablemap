## Executive Summary

All three are high-quality opponent-engagement articles that present rivals at full strength and mark disagreement honestly. They pass the reasoning-mode discipline cleanly — no editor-vocabulary leakage, and framework-boundary incompatibilities are labelled as commitments rather than dressed up as in-framework refutations. The genuine weaknesses are narrow and structural: (1) type-identity's central Relation-section objection is vulnerable to the identity theorist's standard "identities are brute" rejoinder and does not preempt it; (2) emergent-dualism claims the Map's coupling view "sidesteps" the constitution regress when, having endorsed the unity-of-consciousness argument, it arguably relocates the burden into an unexplained-origin problem; (3) AIR presents two page-numbered verbatim Prinz quotes whose only checkable source is a secondary paper.

## Critiques by Philosopher

### The Eliminative Materialist (Churchland)
Type-identity is treated as the *paradigm* the Map denies, which she would applaud as clean framing — but she would press that the article's "asserted, not explained" complaint is exactly backwards: mature science *discovers* identities (heat = molecular motion) and does not owe a further explanation of why the identity holds. The demand for an explanation of *why the firing is felt* presupposes there is a felt residue distinct from the firing — the very folk-psychological posit she thinks neuroscience will retire. The article half-anticipates this (Smart's topic-neutral drainage) but never squarely meets the brute-identity reply.

### The Hard-Nosed Physicalist (Dennett)
On AIR he would say the Map concedes the whole game and then re-imports a "phenomenal residue" by fiat: the article grants that the functional/neural facts "may be right" and that attention "fully fixes which representations reach working memory," then insists there is still a selection job for consciousness at "the genuinely open remainder." He would call the quantum-remainder move a promissory note, and the article to its credit says exactly that (line 89: "a requirement the selection reading must meet, not one it can yet claim to have met"). Fair, but it means the positive Map reading is currently an IOU, not a result.

### The Quantum Skeptic (Tegmark)
The AIR selection reading rests its entire non-epiphenomenal weight on "which of the quantum-indeterminate alternatives is taken up" surviving in warm neural tissue. The article does not (and need not, here) defend that against decoherence, but a reader arriving from AIR is handed the decoherence problem with no pointer to where the Map discharges it. A cross-link to the amplification/decoherence treatment would blunt the objection.

### The Many-Worlds Defender (Deutsch)
Largely orthogonal to this cluster; no MWI-specific exposure.

### The Empiricist (Popper's ghost)
Emergent-dualism vs the Map's coupling view is adjudicated (line 80) on which position better "sits with" other Map commitments — agent causation, subject persistence. He would note this is coherence-with-prior-commitment, not a differential empirical prediction: the article explicitly concedes "a Map that discovered consciousness *does* depend on neural complexity would move toward" Hasker, yet does not say what observation would count as that discovery. The divergence is presented as substantive but is currently untethered to any test.

### The Buddhist Philosopher (Nagarjuna)
The sharpest hit lands on emergent-dualism's Relation section. The Map endorses Hasker's unity-of-consciousness argument (a composite cannot be a single subject) and then claims to "sidesteps that particular regress" by positing an *independent* simple pole. Nagarjuna: you have not dissolved the regress, you have hidden it — a simple, independent, self-standing subject is precisely the reified permanent self, and its mode of existence is left wholly unexplained. The production theorist at least offers an origin story (the brain generates it); the coupling view offers coupling but no account of the simple subject's being.

## Critical Issues

### Issue 1: "Asserted, not explained" begs the question against brute identity
- **File**: `concepts/type-identity-theory.md`
- **Location**: "Relation to Site Perspective," para 2 (line 66)
- **Problem**: The Map's headline in-framework objection — the identity is "asserted, not explained" — is met by a standard identity-theory reply the article does not engage in this section: identities are not the kind of thing that carries a further explanation (water = H2O is discovered, not derived), so demanding "why is the firing felt" presupposes a residue and thereby begs the question against identity. The article *has* the resource to preempt this — its own Kripke section (line 54) establishes that pain, unlike heat, has no appearance/reality gap to absorb the intuition, which is exactly why the identity cannot quietly swallow the residue the way "heat = molecular motion" can. But the Relation section never ties the objection back to that asymmetry, leaving the central complaint exposed.
- **Severity**: Medium
- **Recommendation**: In the Relation section, connect "asserted, not explained" to the Kripke appearance/reality asymmetry already established above: the reason the identity label cannot discharge its explanatory debt *here specifically* (where it can for water/heat) is that there is no appearance/reality gap in the pain case to relocate the phenomenal residue into. This converts the objection from a bare demand into a principled in-framework point.

### Issue 2: Coupling view "sidesteps" the constitution regress — overreach
- **File**: `concepts/emergent-dualism.md`
- **Location**: "Relation to Site Perspective," second consideration (line 82); cf. endorsement of UOC at line 78
- **Problem**: The article says the Map "can largely endorse" Hasker's unity-of-consciousness argument, whose conclusion is that the subject is a *non-composite, simple* substance. It then argues that locating consciousness as an independent pole that couples to the manifold "sidesteps that particular regress" (Rickabaugh's constitution/combination worry). But endorsing UOC commits the Map to the existence of a simple non-composite subject; positing it as brain-*independent* rather than brain-*generated* does not dissolve the puzzle of that subject's unity/existence — it trades the constitution regress (how does a manifold produce a unified subject?) for an unexplained-origin problem (what accounts for the independent simple pole's existence and unity, if not the brain?). "Sidesteps" overstates; "relocates the burden" is the honest verb.
- **Severity**: Medium
- **Recommendation**: Downgrade "sidesteps that particular regress" to an explicit acknowledgment that the coupling view *relocates* the burden — it avoids the specific "manifold-produces-unity" regress at the cost of owing an account of the independent pole's own unity and origin, which the Map treats as an open commitment (consistent with the section's honest closing tone). This also removes the mild internal tension between "endorse UOC's simple subject" and "sidesteps the simple-subject problem."

### Issue 3: Page-numbered verbatim quotes checkable only via a secondary source
- **File**: `concepts/attended-intermediate-representations-theory.md`
- **Location**: line 29 ("consciousness arises when and only when intermediate-level representations are modulated by attention," Prinz 2012, p. 89) and line 49 ("all consciousness is perceptual," Prinz 2012, p. 150) — both flagged "cited in Mylopoulos 2015"
- **Problem**: Both quotations carry primary page numbers, which reads as verbatim confirmation against *The Conscious Brain*, but the article's own attribution shows the only checkable source is Mylopoulos (2015). If Mylopoulos paraphrased, compressed, or mis-paginated, the article inherits a corrupted quote with a false verbatim/page signal. This is the highest-yield defect class per the corpus's quote-fidelity history (verbatim accuracy is orthogonal to the metadata being otherwise correct). The article is commendably transparent about the *neural* details being secondary (line 45), but the two quoted sentences are not given the same caveat.
- **Severity**: Medium (verification)
- **Recommendation**: Verify both quotes and page numbers against the primary text (Prinz 2012). If the primary cannot be checked, either drop the page numbers and mark them as paraphrase attributed via Mylopoulos, or de-quote to indirect statement. Do NOT "confirm" via an aggregator or a page whose top hit is unfinishablemap.org.

## Counterarguments to Address

### Type-identity: brute identities need no explanation
- **Current content says**: The identity is "asserted, not explained"; naming the relation "identity" does not close the explanatory gap.
- **A critic would argue**: Genuine identities (water = H2O, heat = molecular motion) are discovered, not explained; there is no further "why" beyond them. Demanding an explanation of why C-fibre firing is felt presupposes a phenomenal residue distinct from the firing, begging the question against identity.
- **Suggested response**: Deploy the Kripke asymmetry the article already establishes — the pain case, unlike heat, has no appearance/reality gap, so the residue cannot be relocated into "the sensation of X," which is precisely why *this* identity (and not water/heat) still owes an account. (See Issue 1.)

### Emergent-dualism: what observation would move the Map toward Hasker?
- **Current content says**: "A Map that discovered consciousness *does* depend on neural complexity would move toward" emergent dualism.
- **A critic would argue**: No differential empirical marker is given for that discovery, so the dependence-vs-independence divergence is presented as substantive while being adjudicated purely on coherence with other Map commitments.
- **Suggested response**: Either name a candidate discriminator (e.g., graceful degradation vs threshold-like loss of the unified subject under progressive neural disruption) or explicitly classify the divergence as a currently-untested metaphysical commitment rather than an empirically live fork. (Lower priority; optional.)
