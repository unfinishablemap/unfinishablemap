## Content fix (argument calibration) — applied

**Section: "Relation to Site Perspective → Dualism (Tenet 1)".** The draft claimed the Acquaintance Principle and the knowledge argument are "two independent routes" to "some knowledge is essentially experiential," and that this is "a stronger evidential situation than either alone." This over-states the corroboration: the two arguments are not independent at their core — both trade on the same first-person/third-person intuition, so a critic who deflates one (a physicalist reading of Mary; a naturalistic reading of aesthetic resistance) will press the same deflation against the other.

The fix rescopes "independent" → "partly independent" and adds the qualification explicitly: *"The corroboration is real to the degree the two resist a common debunking; it is not automatic from their merely being two."* This is a rescope, not a deletion — the claim survives at its honest strength.
