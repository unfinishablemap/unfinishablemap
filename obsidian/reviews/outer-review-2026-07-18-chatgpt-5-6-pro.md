# External audit: “Non-Temporal Consciousness”

**Version reviewed:** substantively revised 11 July 2026  

**Disposition:** **Major revision required**

## Executive assessment

The article is strongest when it treats non-temporal consciousness as a **phenomenological possibility**: some contemplatives report wakeful episodes in which ordinary succession, change, duration, or temporal orientation is greatly attenuated. It is weakest when it moves from those reports to claims about an **atemporal subject**, the non-temporal constitution of consciousness generally, or an atemporal selector in quantum measurement.

The decisive problem is an unclosed gap between:

1. an experience **represented as lacking succession**;
2. an experience whose occurrence or neural vehicle lacks temporal organization;
3. a subject that is metaphysically outside time; and
4. an anti-physicalist conclusion.

The article distinguishes phenomenological atemporality from metaphysical non-temporality, but then says they “converge.” That convergence is the conclusion requiring argument, not an established result. The article itself states the principal representationalist alternative—a temporally organized process whose content lacks temporal markers—but never supplies evidence that discriminates between the two accounts. ## 1. Soundness and evidential support

### 1.1 What the evidence can support

The defensible evidential claim is approximately:

> Some practitioners retrospectively describe episodes of wakeful presence in which ordinary temporal differentiation or represented succession was absent or greatly reduced.

That is compatible with contemporary work on minimal phenomenal experience, pure awareness, and content-reduced contemplative states. A large questionnaire study found several distinguishable dimensions of minimal experience, including wakeful presence and a time-related factor; textual and participant studies likewise find families of related states rather than one invariant, wholly contentless condition. [Springer+3PLOS+3Philosophie Mainz+3](https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article)

The evidence does **not yet establish** any of the following:

- that the experienced episode lacked all phenomenal duration rather than merely represented change or temporal orientation;
- that the episode’s physical or functional realization was non-temporal;
- that the subject of the episode existed outside objective temporal relations;
- that temporal organization is unnecessary for consciousness generally;
- or that a physicalist realization is impossible.

The article moves among these propositions too freely. “No experienced succession,” “no temporal content,” “not constituted by time,” “outside the A- and B-series,” and “a non-temporal subject” are not equivalent.

### 1.2 The content–vehicle–subject conflation

A temporally extended process can represent something as changeless, timeless, simultaneous, or lacking temporal markers. A map of an eternalist spacetime need not itself be eternal; a thought of a mathematical object need not instantiate mathematical timelessness. Correspondingly, a temporal neural or mental vehicle could have content represented as an undifferentiated present.

The article acknowledges precisely this representationalist response but tries to overcome it by combining the Husserlian regress with contemplative phenomenology. The missing premise is that introspection reveals the temporal constitution of the **vehicle or subject**, rather than only the structure of experienced content. No evidence is provided for that transparency premise. [topics/non-temporal-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/non-temporal-consciousness/)

The resulting inference has the form:

1. An atemporal ground could produce experiences reported as lacking temporal markers.
2. Some experiences are reported as lacking temporal markers.
3. Therefore, they reveal an atemporal ground.

That is underdetermined. Temporal predictive-processing, representational, memory-reconstruction, and attentional models can make the same broad prediction. The article admits that such models remain available, but an available rival with the same observable consequences defeats the claimed evidential leverage. [topics/non-temporal-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/non-temporal-consciousness/)

### 1.3 Retrospective-report and identifiability problems

The article does not adequately address whether a subject could distinguish, after the fact, among:

- continuous but undifferentiated awareness;
- a brief experience followed by absent encoding;
- a period of unconsciousness bounded by similar pre- and post-state experiences;
- severe temporal compression;
- or a retrospective reconstruction shaped by doctrinal expectations.

A genuinely changeless episode would contain no internal change by which its duration could be tracked. Its apparent continuity may therefore depend on memory at its boundaries—the very faculty likely to be altered in deep absorption, cessation, sleep, or anesthesia.

Current qualitative work explicitly recognizes retrospective distortion and possible confabulation as methodological concerns. The target article instead treats the reports as relatively direct access to the constituting activity of consciousness. [MDPI](https://www.mdpi.com/2409-9287/6/2/50)

This is not a reason to dismiss first-person evidence. It is a reason to narrow the conclusion and use designs involving immediate repeated probes, structured microphenomenological interviews, physiological time-locking, and comparisons between prospectively specified state categories.

### 1.4 Husserl does not supply the missing bridge

The revised article is more careful than its earlier version about the wording of Husserl’s regress argument. Nevertheless, it continues to overread the result.

On the interpretation discussed by Hoerl, retention, primal impression, and protention can be understood as representational properties of experience that are not themselves separately datable events. That supports a distinction between objective time and the way temporal content is constituted. It does not straightforwardly establish a changeless, substantial subject outside all temporal order. Hoerl also argues that the regress reasoning may be less persuasive than is often assumed and presents extensionalism as a live alternative. [WRAP+3WRAP+3WRAP+3](https://wrap.warwick.ac.uk/id/eprint/48646/1/WRAP_Hoerl_phpr547.pdf)

The target’s statement that retention, primal impression, and protention belong to constituted immanent unities rather than absolute subjectivity is consequently too categorical. Husserl’s absolute flow is non-objective and not simply another event in objective time, but it remains internally articulated in ways described through retention and protention. “Not an object in time” is not synonymous with “a static metaphysical substance outside every temporal relation.”

The further claim that meditation strips away temporal markers and thereby reveals the constituting activity “directly” does not follow from Husserl. It requires an independent theory of introspective access that the article does not provide.

### 1.5 Frischhut is relevant but mischaracterized

The July revision correctly updated Frischhut’s article to its final *Philosophical Quarterly* publication details. The source is directly relevant: it argues, conceptually, for a form of “pure duration” without ordinary temporal structure on the basis of contemplative reports. [OUP Academic+1](https://academic.oup.com/pq/article-abstract/76/1/127/7718358)

But calling it “first-person corroboration” is misleading. It is a philosophical interpretation of reports, not an original first-person participant study or a new empirical data set. Moreover, “pure duration” is not obviously **non-temporality**. Duration without differentiated succession is closer to non-successive or non-partitioned temporality than to existence outside time.

The terminology should distinguish at least:

- absence of experienced passage;
- absence of represented succession;
- absence of temporal differentiation;
- presence of undivided duration;
- and absence of any temporal location or relation.

Frischhut may support some of the middle categories. It does not establish the last.

### 1.6 The cross-tradition convergence is overstated

The article groups Theravāda jhāna, rigpa, kenshō, Advaita, Christian apophatic experience, and related reports as converging on a common timeless condition. That proposition is insufficiently sourced and methodologically stronger than the available evidence warrants. [topics/non-temporal-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/non-temporal-consciousness/)

Recent studies instead find both recurring features and substantial heterogeneity. Expert textual analysis identified dozens of phenomenological features rather than one uniform state, and a cross-practice participant study found robust differences among practices and substantial residual “abstract” content even where ordinary sensory and narrative content was low. [Springer+1](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11097-022-09811-z)

Apparent convergence can arise from several sources other than a common metaphysical object:

- overlapping attentional and arousal manipulations;
- shared translation vocabulary;
- retrospective compression;
- selection of reports already described as “timeless”;
- teacher and doctrinal expectations;
- or common limits in the language available for highly unusual states.

The article mentions constructivist interpretation but does not conduct a construct-equivalence analysis. Before inferring cross-tradition confirmation, it would need to establish that “timeless,” “eternal,” “unborn,” “cessation,” “pure duration,” and “the present” are being used for comparable phenomenological structures rather than different doctrinal functions.

### 1.7 Cessation is not evidence for wakeful timeless consciousness without further argument

The treatment of *nirodha samāpatti* is particularly unstable. Contemporary scientific discussions commonly investigate cessation events as gaps, cuts, or temporary absences of consciousness. A 2023 theoretical treatment explicitly asks how reports of complete absence of consciousness should be understood, while neurophysiological case work describes brief loss of consciousness around cessation events. [Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam+1](https://research.vu.nl/en/publications/cessations-of-consciousness-in-meditation-advancing-a-scientific-)

Those findings do not settle the traditional interpretation. They do show that cessation cannot simply be placed in an evidential list of alert, timeless conscious states. The article must distinguish:

- content-reduced but reportably conscious absorption;
- objectless or minimal awareness;
- discontinuity in memory;
- and cessation understood as absence of consciousness.

Otherwise, absence of temporal experience is illicitly converted into experience of non-temporality.

### 1.8 The anti-physicalist conclusion is not obtained

The article’s category-mismatch argument works only if consciousness itself has been shown to be outside the temporal order in which physical processes occur. The evidence supports, at most, the possibility that some conscious **contents** do not represent ordinary succession. A physical process representing an undifferentiated present involves no category error.

The article’s appeal to the A- and B-series also produces a dilemma:

- If the contemplative state lacks only felt passage or change—roughly A-series phenomenology—it may still be an event located in B-series before/after relations.
- If it is outside all B-series relations, no cited first-person report establishes that stronger fact.

The weaker claim eventually described as load-bearing—that consciousness is not constitutively temporal—therefore remains stronger than the evidence. The evidence presently supports only the claim that **explicit phenomenal representation of succession may not be necessary for every conscious episode**. [topics/non-temporal-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/non-temporal-consciousness/)

### 1.9 The “non-temporal selector” is not yet an explanatory model

The proposal that an atemporal subject could select among post-decoherence outcomes is presented as an application of the article’s thesis. But the article concedes that it lacks a dynamics and a clear empirical test. [topics/non-temporal-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/non-temporal-consciousness/)

The difficulty is not merely missing mathematical detail. An atemporal selector must still explain:

- why one temporally located outcome rather than another is selected;
- how selection is asymmetrically related to records before and after measurement;
- how multiple measurements receive consistent outcomes;
- whether the selector is subject-specific or universal;
- and what observation would differ from ordinary stochastic quantum dynamics.

Calling the selector “not constrained to a specific moment” does not solve temporal coordination; it restates the problem. Without a mapping from an atemporal entity to ordered physical records, the proposal has no explanatory advantage and risks being causally inert.

## 3. Counterarguments and tenet-protective framing

### 3.1 The strongest rival is acknowledged but not answered

The representationalist objection is not a peripheral “physicalist reply.” It directly attacks the article’s central inference:

> An experience can lack represented temporal markers while being generated by a temporally structured process.

The article gives no behavioral, phenomenological, or neural observation that would occur under its atemporal-ground hypothesis but not under that rival. Until it does, the rival is evidentially at least as adequate and ontologically less committal.

Calling this a framework-boundary disagreement understates its force. It is an objection to the validity of the inference **within the evidence presented**, not merely a refusal to accept dualist premises.

### 3.2 The article openly imports the substantial subject from the site’s tenet

The article commendably admits that the inference to a substantial subject originates in the project’s dualist tenet rather than in the phenomenology itself. [topics/non-temporal-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/non-temporal-consciousness/)

That admission should change the article’s argumentative status. The proper formulation is:

> Given the site’s independently defended substantial-subject thesis, non-successive contemplative phenomenology may be interpreted as consistent with an atemporal ground.

The current presentation too often reverses that dependence, allowing contemplative reports to appear as independent corroboration of the tenet. They are not independent if their metaphysical interpretation is supplied by the tenet being corroborated.

### 3.3 “Non-multiple by construction” makes unity circular

The article suggests that a singular, atemporal subject preserves unity through split-brain fragmentation because it is non-multiple by construction. That is not a successful prediction. It builds the desired unity into the definition of the explanatory entity.

Split-brain evidence requires distinctions among:

- unity of agency;
- unity of access and report;
- representational integration;
- synchronic phenomenal unity;
- local versus global experiential unity;
- and numerical identity of the subject.

Pinto-style behavioral unity does not establish all of these. Schechter and Bayne explicitly distinguish agency unity from experiential unity, while Lopez accepts local failures of synchronic phenomenal unity within a globally unified model. [Springer+3Monash University+3Springer+3](https://research.monash.edu/en/publications/consciousness-after-split-brain-surgery-the-recent-challenge-to-t)

The article therefore cannot use split-brain cases both as evidence that physical integration is insufficient and as evidence that subject unity remains intact. The empirical work is compatible with several decompositions of unity.

### 3.4 Bergson is absorbed through an ad hoc synthesis

The article correctly recognizes that Bergsonian *durée* is not timelessness but temporality understood as indivisible qualitative flow. It then proposes that durée is the output while Husserlian absolute flow is the source, expressly favoring the Husserlian side. [topics/non-temporal-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/non-temporal-consciousness/)

This does not answer Bergson’s challenge. It redescribes the conflict so that the site’s preferred atemporal ground is retained. A proper treatment would ask whether indivisible duration is sufficient to explain the reports without positing anything atemporal. Unless that possibility is independently excluded, Bergson supplies a rival interpretation rather than a component of the preferred one.

### 3.5 Madhyamaka is neutralized rather than confronted

The Madhyamaka objection is potentially more radical than the article allows. It does not merely offer a rival answer concerning whether a substantial atemporal subject exists; it questions whether the demand for such a substantially existing ground is itself well formed.

The target’s compressed characterization of the tetralemma risks a nihilist reading. Madhyamaka normally denies intrinsic existence while maintaining conventional persons, experiences, causes, and practices. [Internet Encyclopedia of Philosophy+2Internet Encyclopedia of Philosophy+2](https://iep.utm.edu/madhyamaka-buddhist-philosophy/)

A stronger engagement would ask whether the apparent need for an atemporal bearer results from reifying either temporal flow or subjectivity. That objection cannot be answered simply by restating the project’s substantial-subject tenet.

### 3.6 Physics does not provide independent convergence

The article draws suggestive parallels among contemplative timelessness, Husserlian constitution, and timeless or background-independent approaches in fundamental physics. At present these are analogies, not mutually confirming data.

Even where a physical formalism lacks a fundamental external time parameter, it does not follow that:

- physical events have no relational order;
- consciousness is outside all temporal relations;
- or a subject can causally select temporally indexed outcomes.

The cited quantum-gravity overview emphasizes that the field is unsettled and empirically incomplete. [Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/quantum-gravity/)

The convergence claim therefore risks multiplying descriptions of different problems and treating vocabulary shared across them—“timeless,” “relational,” “non-objective”—as evidence of a shared ontology.

### 3.7 The changelog reveals structurally tenet-protective review rules

The 11 July review did useful work: it corrected the Husserl quotation issue and updated Frischhut’s final bibliographic record. [GitHub](https://github.com/unfinishablemap/unfinishablemap/commit/2d7592df85d2e8abd68e139ed5fff3c3623b56bd)

Its methodology is nevertheless unsuitable for an adversarial audit. The changelog says that previously settled “bedrock” perimeters were not to be re-litigated, evaluates the article through what a tenet-accepting reviewer would flag, and tells future reviews not to reopen those issues. It also treats the absence of superlative language as eliminating a risk of supersession. [GitHub+1](https://github.com/unfinishablemap/unfinishablemap/commit/2d7592df85d2e8abd68e139ed5fff3c3623b56bd)

These rules are tenet-protective in three ways:

1. **They convert substantive objections into excluded premises.** The representationalist challenge and the inference to a substantial subject are exactly where the article’s evidential status is decided.
2. **They confuse bibliographic verification with claim verification.** A real paper and an accurate quotation can still fail to entail the adjacent sentence.
3. **They misunderstand currency.** Non-superlative generalizations can be superseded. The categorical anesthesia claim is a clear example.

The review process is transparent about these restrictions, which is preferable to concealing them. But the resulting review is an internal doctrinal consistency check, not an independent assessment of truth, evidence, or current scholarship.

## 4. Integration with neighboring articles

### 4.1 Direct contradiction with “Temporal Consciousness”

The neighboring “Temporal Consciousness” article, revised 16 July 2026, says that human consciousness does not occur in durationless instants and presents temporal structure as fundamental to what consciousness is. It also uses retention and protention to support a constitutive-temporality thesis. [concepts/temporal-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/temporal-consciousness/)

The target article’s weaker but still “load-bearing” conclusion is that consciousness is **not** constitutively temporal. These cannot both stand without an explicit scope distinction.

A coherent site-wide position is available, but it must be stated:

- ordinary perceptual and agentive consciousness may require temporal integration;
- a putative minimal phenomenal episode may lack represented succession;
- and a metaphysical subject might be hypothesized to be outside objective time.

At present the pages slide among these levels. The first proposition does not imply the second is false, and the second does not establish the third.

### 4.2 The conflict is sharper in “Temporal Consciousness: Structure and Agency”

“Temporal Consciousness: Structure and Agency,” also revised 16 July, states categorically that consciousness requires temporal structure and develops agency, binding, and the specious present around that constitutional claim. Later, however, it uses the Husserlian absolute flow and meditation to suggest that temporal structure is something consciousness does rather than what it is. [topics/temporal-consciousness-structure-and-agency](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/temporal-consciousness-structure-and-agency/)

That is close to the target article’s thesis, but the relation is not made explicit. The two pages need a shared terminology distinguishing:

- temporal structure of phenomenal content;
- temporal extension of an episode;
- temporal integration necessary for perception and action;
- persistence of a subject across time;
- and metaphysical location of the subject.

Without that distinction, the site alternately treats temporal organization as constitutive, generated, dispensable, and merely representational.

### 4.3 The specious-present material should be canonicalized more clearly

The dedicated “Specious Present” article is archived and states that its material was consolidated into “Temporal Consciousness.” [reviews/deep-review-2026-03-15-specious-present-temporal-experience](https://unfinishablemap.org/reviews/deep-review-2026-03-15-specious-present-temporal-experience/)

The target should therefore treat “Temporal Consciousness” as the canonical neighbor and explain what the specious-present evidence establishes. The specious present gives a model of how ordinary succession and persistence can be presented within a temporally thick field. It does not by itself show that every minimal conscious condition must contain articulated succession, nor does a report of timelessness show that the underlying episode is literally durationless.

The current site architecture invites readers to mistake an argument about ordinary perceptual consciousness for an argument about the metaphysical ground of all consciousness.

### 4.4 The phenomenal-binding material uses an unstable conception of unity

The binding article treats split-brain data, anesthesia, and dreamless sleep as evidence bearing on an ontological unity supplied by consciousness rather than produced by coordination. It also makes categorical claims about absence of unified experience in NREM sleep. [concepts/binding-problem](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/binding-problem/)

Those claims need revision. Current work shows that unresponsiveness is not equivalent to unconsciousness and that conscious experience can occur in NREM sleep, including states traditionally described as deep sleep. Preliminary work also investigates objectless awareness during dreamless sleep. [Springer+3Nature+3Royal Society Publishing+3](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-020-74075-z)

More importantly, the binding page and the target article do not use “unity” at the same explanatory level. Neural feature binding, global availability, unified agency, synchronic phenomenology, diachronic identity, and numerical unity of a subject are treated as though evidence against one automatically establishes another. It does not.

The target’s atemporal subject would become relevant to binding only after the site shows:

1. which form of unity remains unexplained;
2. why physical or functional integration cannot realize it;
3. how an atemporal subject produces particular unified contents;
4. and how this account handles observed local disunity.

### 4.5 The split-brain page should match the target’s own caution

The site’s split-brain page leads with the suggestion that the evidence points toward fragmentation at the interface rather than fragmentation of consciousness, but later admits that the evidence is underdetermined. [topics/split-brain-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/split-brain-consciousness/)

The target article is, in places, more appropriately cautious. The lead of the split-brain page should be brought into line with that caution. Current evidence does not uniquely locate division in the interface, the subject, access systems, or locally unified experiential fields.

## Site methodology

1. **Separate four audit questions for every citation:** bibliographic existence, quotation fidelity, entailment of the attached claim, and completeness relative to current literature.
2. **Maintain a claim–source matrix** giving the exact proposition supported, evidence type, relevant passage, publication date, support strength, known limitations, and strongest contrary source.
3. **Tag evidence by category:** conceptual analysis, retrospective report, prospective participant study, behavioral evidence, neural correlate, model-based inference, and metaphysical inference. Do not allow one category to be described as another.
4. **Replace “tenet-accepting review” with independent adversarial review.** A separate pass may assess consistency with the site’s tenets, but it should not substitute for evaluating those tenets and their use as premises.
5. **Remove the permanent rule against reopening “bedrock” perimeters.** Give every perimeter a review date, a statement of its strongest objection, and explicit conditions under which new evidence reopens it.
6. **Do not treat absence of superlatives as absence of supersession risk.** Apply currency review to every empirical generalization, especially claims involving anesthesia, sleep, meditation science, split-brain research, and neuroscience.
7. **Require each load-bearing claim to cite a current strong counter-source** and state why the preferred interpretation explains the evidence better.
8. **Pre-register disconfirmation conditions for speculative mechanisms.** For the atemporal-subject and selector proposals, identify observations that would lower confidence rather than allowing every result to be redescribed as interface behavior.
9. **Add cross-article contradiction checks** for categorical formulations such as “consciousness requires temporal structure,” “consciousness is not constitutively temporal,” “propofol eliminates consciousness,” and “unity is preserved.”
10. **Measure review independence rather than review count.** Repeated reviews operating under the same protected premises do not provide the epistemic value of independent reviewers permitted to challenge those premises.
