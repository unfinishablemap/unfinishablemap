## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The article does not merely cite Chalmers — it reads his *own* 2020 "Is the Hard Problem Universal?" against the Map's interest, adopting his graded verdict (rejecting judgment- and individual-intuition universality, defending only source- and population-level universality) and then explicitly *declining* to borrow his authority for the realist reading ("source universality... is also common among illusionists"). Chalmers would recognize his position represented at the right grain, including his diagnosis that the hard problem "did not really get a grip" until materialism became a live foil — used here to make the Chinese divergence a *prediction* rather than an embarrassment.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
Out of scope for this meta-methodological article (no mechanism claims); the article correctly keeps the quantum layer at layer-two and does not let convergence-weight leak into it. Stapp would note the discipline protects his own kind of claim from over-extension.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The article centres the first-person datum precisely where it is load-bearing and marks the boundary where it is contested: the "Scope of the Argument" section concedes that for audiences granting phenomenal experience the supposed common root *is* the subject matter, while standing "honestly at the boundary against eliminativism rather than pretending the boundary is internal." Nagel's "something it is like" is treated as the explanandum, not smuggled as a conclusion.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The Chinese-traditions "What Divergence Reveals" section is the Whiteheadian high point: it takes seriously that Daoist/Neo-Confucian process metaphysics *dissolves* rather than generates the hard problem, and refuses to score that as physicalism. It treats the subject-object distinction as "productive but not thereby veridical" — avoiding crude substance-dualist triumphalism while preserving the reality of what the distinction picks out. Crucially (see below), none of this Whiteheadian sympathy is used to upgrade any evidential tier.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
Out of scope; no agency claims are advanced or over-claimed here.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
Handled with unusual fairness: the article separates *generic* cognitive closure (which would produce variable errors, not a precise convergent diagnosis) from McGinn's *specific* closure-about-the-consciousness-relation (which "is arguably what the closure hypothesis itself predicts"), and concedes the convergence argument alone "seems unable to settle" the closure-vs-irreducibility comparison. That is McGinn's humility honoured, not deflected.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch) — load-bearing for this article
This is the persona whose verdict matters most here, and the article passes cleanly. It performs *tenet-as-evidence-upgrade as the praise-worthy thing to NOT do*, repeatedly and by name:

- **The two-tier discount on the clinical compound signature** (the strongest single move in the article): three mechanistically disjoint perturbation routes — lesion, pharmacology, psychogenic dissociation — converge on one channel taxonomy. The article grants this earns the *architecture* conclusion robustly, then explicitly refuses to let it earn the *dualist significance*: "best read as *compatibility*-grade, not *vindication*-grade... because both readings seem to predict the identical dissociation phenomenology." It cites the discipline that catches the error it is declining to commit ("citing the compound signature as *support*... would require a discriminator the functionalist does not also predict"). This is the five-tier scale's register-separation done at the sentence level.
- **The layer-one/layer-two firewall**: convergence earns "consciousness is irreducible" across rival irreducibility-respecting framings (layer one); it does *not* license substance dualism over panpsychism/neutral monism/idealism (layer two). "A framework that silently counted its consumers' commitments as additional routes would be unfalsifiable" — the article names its own worst failure mode and the methodology-symmetry test that guards it.
- **The Laudan concession is met on its own terms, not deflected**: "This cannot be deflected by adding a fresh example of one's own." The article disclaims convergence-weight for its *own* preferred reading at the solution layer.
- **Stanford's unconceived-alternatives** is used to *bound its own honest weight* ("a claim about conceived options, not a demonstration of exhaustiveness").
- **The Berent debunking line** is taken in its strongest form (Dualism-plus-Essentialism as a *specific* candidate common cause that the generic common-cause-null concession does not defuse) before the reply locates the precise unearned step (causation ≠ insensitivity).

Birch and Whitehead converge on praising this article from different axes without conflict — the diagnostic that the possibility/probability tension is resolved, not merely papered over. No calibration flag warranted; no `refine-draft` correction needed.
