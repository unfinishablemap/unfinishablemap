## Executive Summary

This is one of the Map's strongest and most calibration-disciplined clusters. It runs the hardest rival programme to interactionist dualism — constitutive panpsychism — at full strength (James, Chalmers, Coleman, Goff, Mørch, Seager, Roelofs), draws the Map's advantage *by elimination* rather than by refutation, and marks every tenet-driven move as tenet-driven rather than data-forced. The Process Philosopher and the Hardline Empiricist both find much to praise here, and — the decisive test for this cluster — they praise the *same* passages, which means the possibility/probability tension has been resolved honestly rather than papered over. The main opportunity is a single genuinely uncovered adjacent topic (the ethics of experience-sharing / overlapping-minds aggregation), plus a handful of reciprocal cross-links.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The cluster takes the explanatory gap seriously at *two* levels, and says so explicitly: "The hard problem asks how experience arises from non-experience. The combination problem asks how experience arises from *experience*—and still has no answer" (`panpsychisms-combination-problem` L49). The zombie-argument parallelism — physicalism cannot close physical→phenomenal, panpsychism cannot close micro-phenomenal→macro-phenomenal, and the explanatory gap simply relocates (L61) — is exactly the structural symmetry Chalmers systematised in his 2017 "Combination Problem" essay, and the article cites and uses all three of his dimensions (subject/quality/structure) faithfully.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
`quantum-holism-and-phenomenal-unity` engages the physics-consciousness interface seriously without over-claiming: entanglement as the one physical structure exhibiting genuine ontological holism, collapse as the locus where conscious selection enters, and the explicit "division of labour" between classical BP1 (feature segregation) and a candidate quantum BP2 (ontological unity). Stapp's own von-Neumann-collapse instinct is honoured in the closing move: "the same event that is physically described as entangled state collapse is phenomenologically described as unified experience" (L122).

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The cluster centres first-person givenness throughout — "there is something it is like to have *all of them together* irreducible to what it is like to have each separately" (`unity-of-consciousness` L56), the wine-tasting conjoint-phenomenology example (L74), and Nagel's own 1971 split-brain verdict ("too much unity to say two minds, too much separation to say one") deployed as live evidence for dimensional dissociation (L90). The subject as "shut in its own skin, windowless" (James, via Nagel's lineage) anchors the subject-summing discussion.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
This cluster is a model of how to invoke Whitehead *without* letting his praise cash out as an evidential upgrade. `quantum-holism` L164-168 reads quantum collapse as Whiteheadian "actual occasions" achieving internal unity through concrescence — and immediately labels it "a suggestive alignment ... an illustrative parallel rather than an argued identification." That is precisely the discipline the persona constraint demands: process-philosophical resonance is used to remove a *defeater* (the intuition that unity must be assembled), never to supply positive evidence that any particular state is conscious. Fusionism's "consciousness-from-consciousness is more intelligible than consciousness-from-non-consciousness" (Mørch, refining Strawson) is credited as *partial* intelligibility, not as a solution (`panpsychisms-combination-problem` L91).

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
Subject unity is correctly framed as a *precondition for agency*: "the subject who selects among possibilities must be one subject having one unified experience of the options" (`quantum-holism` L188), and "if consciousness were fragmented ... there would be no integrated subject to select among quantum possibilities" (`unity-of-consciousness` L144). Quantum selection at collapse gives Kane's indeterministic "room" a concrete locus while preserving downward efficacy "without injecting energy or violating conservation laws."

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
Intellectual humility is not merely gestured at — it is calibrated. `panpsychisms-combination-problem` L125 grants the mysterian reading real force ("perhaps the combination problem reveals the limits of human cognition rather than the structure of reality") while noting the honest asymmetry: the mysterian move "applies equally to every theory," so a framework facing an *empirically approachable* mystery (interaction) is preferable to one facing a *purely conceptual* mystery (combination). The `emergence-void` cross-link generalises this to a "general cognitive limit at level transitions."

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This is where the cluster most impresses, and the persona's verdict is load-bearing because the cluster repeatedly stands at the edge of possibility/probability slippage and repeatedly declines to slip.

- **Tenet-as-evidence-upgrade is praise-worthily *not* done, in the exact words the persona asks for.** `quantum-holism` states the quantum-holism reading is "posited on tenet grounds (Minimal Quantum Interaction) rather than forced by the data" (L104), "a claim the Map advances on tenet grounds, not one forced by the data" (L48), and "the step from formalism to 'one thing' is an interpretive commitment, not a datum of physics" (L98). Neven's "only true binding agent" conjecture is flagged as "reflect[ing] this interpretive commitment, not the formalism alone."
- **Honest register-keeping on contested empirics.** The decoherence section presents Tegmark → Hagan et al. → Reimers/McKemmish as a *live, unclosed* dispute and explicitly warns "citing Hagan as a settled rebuttal would be selective citation" (L128). The epothilone / Wiest result is read "better as an open finding than as a point in the quantum column" (L142). The Kerskens-López Pérez MRI signature is flagged unreplicated with Warren's artefact objection stated fairly (L148).
- **Restraint on minimal-organism consciousness.** `combination-problem` L159 handles *C. elegans*/*Hydra* exactly as the persona wants: "302-neuron nematodes show integrated information (positive Phi) yet fail behavioral markers of unified consciousness" — no tier-upgrade on tenet-load; the evidence is called "ambiguous."
- **The named honest pattern is used.** `manyism` L66 states the discipline outright: manyism's unmet intelligibility burden "must not be misread as positive evidence for dualism ... that the burden is unmet is a cost the monist owes, not a point scored *for* the Map's ontology." And `combination-problem` L187 refuses the win: "whether 'empirical and unspecified' represents genuine progress over 'conceptual and unresolved' is a question the Map acknowledges rather than presumes to have answered."
- **Framework-stage calibration.** The epistemic-vs-metaphysical reading of non-compositionality is explicitly separated: the introspective evidence "earns the epistemic reading" (we cannot conceive the merging), while "the stronger, metaphysical reading—that no such merging *can* occur—is a further bet, and one that owes an account of split-brain and dissociative-identity cases" (`combination-problem` L126). Naming its own debt is Newton-vs-pre-Kepler honesty.

**Convergence verdict:** Process Philosopher and Hardline Empiricist praise the *same* Whitehead passage (L164-168) — one for its process resonance, the other for its "illustrative parallel, not argued identification" disclaimer. Per the skill's tension rule, convergence on the same passage means the article has resolved the possibility/probability tension honestly. No calibration concern is flagged in this cluster.

## Content Strengths

### panpsychisms-combination-problem.md
- **Strongest point**: The "advantage by elimination, not refutation" architecture. Every panpsychist solution is given its strongest form (fusionism called "the most promising panpsychist response," L93) before its cost is named, and the Map's own primitiveness is admitted as "a genuine philosophical cost—it declines to explain something that seems to demand explanation" (L139).
- **Notable quote**: "Panpsychism's motivating insight—that brute emergence is unintelligible—cuts against its own proposed solutions" (L85).
- **Why it works**: It turns the combination problem into a *positive structural thesis* (subjectivity is inherently non-compositional) rather than a mere scoring point, and links that thesis forward to the interface's "irreducible seam" (L121), unifying the negative and positive halves of the Map's programme.

### combination-problem.md
- **Strongest point**: The comparison table (L142-148) pairing panpsychism's "conceptual" combination problem against interactionism's "empirical" interaction problem — and then honestly conceding in the very next section that "at the level of detailed mechanism, interactionism's quantum selection is as unspecified as panpsychism's phenomenal bonding" (L149).
- **Notable quote**: "Interactionism's problems may be empirical rather than conceptual—but whether 'empirical and unspecified' represents genuine progress over 'conceptual and unresolved' is a question the Map acknowledges rather than presumes to have answered" (L187).
- **Why it works**: The Special-Composition-Question bridge (van Inwagen / Merricks / McQueen & Tsuchiya converging on consciousness-as-criterion, L126) and the compression-criterion contrast give the non-compositionality claim independent scaffolding beyond intuition-pumping.

### unity-of-consciousness.md
- **Strongest point**: The dissociation evidence section (L84-104) — split-brain, dreamless sleep, DID, and the octopus — empirically motivates the thesis that "unity of consciousness may be several related problems, not one." The 2025 Santander PNAS finding (a centimetre of posterior callosal fibre sustaining full integration) is fresh and well-deployed.
- **Notable quote**: "A system could have access unity without phenomenal unity ... as when a distributed AI aggregates sensor data into a global model" (L82) — a clean, LLM-legible statement of the phenomenal/access distinction.
- **Why it works**: The subsumptive-unity vs subject-summing disambiguation (L78) pre-empts the most common objection (that phenomenal part-whole structure smuggles in composition) with genuine philosophical care.

### manyism.md
- **Strongest point**: Treats Roelofs' manyism as "the most credible monist rival ... engaged here as a rival to be taken seriously, not as evidence for dualism" (L30), then keeps three distinct multiplications rigorously apart — manyism's overlapping subjects, No-Many-Worlds branches, and the Many-Minds Interpretation — noting the indexical objection bites MMI but *not* manyism (L68). This is unusually precise register-keeping.
- **Notable quote**: "The Map does not refute manyism, and manyism does not refute the Map" (L66).

### quantum-holism-and-phenomenal-unity.md
- **Strongest point**: The Vicente et al. (2008) correction on zero-lag synchrony (L68) — the article actively *disarms* an over-strong argument in its own favour (Baum's Quantum Binding Argument), conceding that dynamical relaying reproduces zero-lag synchrony classically, and relocates the genuinely non-classical residue to phenomenal unity alone. Self-correcting against a friendly-but-flawed argument is the mark of a mature article.
- **Why it works**: The Leibniz-mill "mismatch of categories" argument (L90-92) is used to bound *its own* quantum proposal — entanglement is "analogical and locational, not constitutive" (L120) — so the article never claims the physics dissolves the hard problem.
