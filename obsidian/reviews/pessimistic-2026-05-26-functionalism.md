# Pessimistic Review

**Date**: 2026-05-26
**Content reviewed**: `obsidian/concepts/functionalism.md` (oldest concept by modified date, 2026-01-09; last deep-reviewed 2026-04-28; no prior dedicated pessimistic review)

## Executive Summary

A mature, well-cited article whose adversarial exposure is otherwise strong. The principal weaknesses are (1) a load-bearing reliance on the conceivability of zombies/inverted qualia that the article asserts but does not defend against the standard charge of question-begging; (2) a structural over-reliance on the "concession convergence" reading, which risks confirmation bias by reinterpreting any narrowing of functionalism as a retreat *toward* the Map; and (3) one genuine citation defect (the COGITATE/Melloni reference cites the 2023 protocol paper but the inline text claims results, "2023-2024"). The "specification problem" and "phenomenal non-compositionality" sections are the article's strongest material and should be preserved intact.

## Critiques by Philosopher

### The Eliminative Materialist (Churchland)
The article treats "qualia," "the felt quality of experience," and "phenomenal character" as fixed explananda that any theory must capture. That is exactly the folk-psychological framing eliminativism denies. The whole edifice—absent qualia, inverted qualia, the explanatory gap—presupposes that there is a determinate phenomenal fact for functional facts to fail to fix. If the relevant mental vocabulary is destined for revision, then "functional facts don't determine phenomenal facts" is not a discovery about reality but an artefact of an obsolete taxonomy. The article's anti-illusionist move (line 107) — "even an illusion requires a subject to whom things seem a certain way" — would be dismissed as smuggling the explanandum back in: "seems" is itself folk-psychological. The article asserts the alternative is "self-undermining" but does not argue why a *revised* successor vocabulary could not retain whatever evidential role intuitions need to play.

### The Hard-Nosed Physicalist (Dennett)
The article fairly reports the heterophenomenology line but then sides against it on the strength of an intuition ("the anti-illusionist intuition," line 107). That is precisely the move Dennett calls inflating an intuition into metaphysics. The Searle treatment (line 87) ends with "Critics note that this move may beg the question" — but the same charge applies to the article's own conclusion. The systems reply and the "memorize the rules" rejoinder are presented as a standoff, then the article elsewhere asserts "the failure of functionalism" (line 89) as settled. You cannot leave the Chinese Room dialectically open in one section and treat functionalism as having *failed* two sections later.

### The Quantum Skeptic (Tegmark)
The "Empirical Tractability" section (line 160) claims "consciousness correlates with quantum-sensitive processes" is a prediction of Minimal Quantum Interaction, and lists it as something that could be falsified. But the article gives no order-of-magnitude account of how quantum sensitivity survives decoherence in a warm wet brain. Listing it as a falsification condition is good practice, but the affirmative prediction is asserted without the calculation a skeptic demands. This is a cross-reference dependency: the article leans entirely on `causal-closure` and the tenets to carry a claim that needs its own physical defense.

### The Many-Worlds Defender (Deutsch)
Minor exposure here — functionalism is not primarily an MWI battleground. But line 145 ("breaching causal-closure at precisely the point where physics leaves outcomes open") presupposes genuine indeterminacy of the kind MWI denies. The article imports this from the tenets without flagging that an Everettian reader rejects the premise outright. A one-clause acknowledgment would close the gap.

### The Empiricist (Popper's ghost)
The strongest section of the article is "What Would Challenge This View?" (line 167) — it states genuine falsification conditions. Credit where due. However, the final sentence (line 173) defuses them: "None of these would logically refute dualism (the conceivability arguments would remain)." This is the classic immunizing manoeuvre. If no empirical result can refute the core thesis because the conceivability argument is always held in reserve, then the empirical-tractability section is rhetorical cover, not a real wager. The article should either concede that the conceivability arguments are the *only* load-bearing support (and the empirics are corroborative at most), or commit to the empirics having real defeating power.

### The Buddhist Philosopher (Nagarjuna)
The article's entire case rests on a subject "to whom things seem a certain way" (line 107) and a consciousness "with its own causal efficacy" (line 145). The no-self critique cuts here: the inverted-qualia and absent-qualia arguments both presuppose a persisting experiencer whose qualia could be inverted or absent. If the self is empty, the thought experiments lose their grip — there is no "my red vs. your green" to anchor the inversion. The article never engages this; it treats the experiencing subject as an unproblematic given.

## Critical Issues

### Issue 1: Conceivability premise asserted, not defended, yet load-bearing
- **File**: `concepts/functionalism.md`
- **Location**: lines 55, 97-99, 173
- **Problem**: The article's ultimate fallback (line 173) is that "the conceivability arguments would remain" even if all empirical predictions failed. Yet the conceivability of zombies/inverted qualia is exactly what functionalists (and type-B physicalists) deny entails their *possibility*. The article states "If either is possible, functionalism is false" (line 99) but never defends the conceivability→possibility inference, which is the entire crux. The full argument is offloaded to `arguments/functionalism-argument`, but since this article makes conceivability the final court of appeal, it owes at least one sentence acknowledging the conceivability-entails-possibility gap.
- **Severity**: Medium
- **Recommendation**: Add a clause at line 99 or 173 acknowledging that the inference from conceivability to metaphysical possibility is itself contested (the type-B physicalist response), with a pointer to where the Map defends it. Do not assert it as settled.

### Issue 2: "Concession convergence" reading risks confirmation bias
- **File**: `concepts/functionalism.md`
- **Location**: lines 69, 81 (Putnam's pragmatic realism; biological computationalism)
- **Problem**: The article reads both Putnam's later pragmatism *and* Milinkovic & Aru's biological computationalism as "concession convergence" — physicalists retreating toward dualism. But biological computationalism is explicitly a *physicalist* narrowing (it abandons substrate-neutrality, not physicalism). Framing every narrowing of functionalism as a step toward the Map is a heads-I-win-tails-you-lose pattern: substrate-dependence is treated as vindication, but a committed physicalist sees it as physicalism getting *more* precise about which physical systems are conscious — the opposite of a concession. The article already half-concedes this at line 79 ("rejecting functionalism isn't merely a dualist move") but then re-absorbs IIT and biological computationalism into the convergence narrative anyway.
- **Severity**: Medium
- **Recommendation**: Explicitly acknowledge that substrate-dependence is fully compatible with physicalism and is not, by itself, evidence for dualism. The convergence claim should be stated as an interpretation the Map favours, not as a neutral reading the data forces.

### Issue 3: COGITATE / Melloni citation defect
- **File**: `concepts/functionalism.md`
- **Location**: line 73 (inline "Melloni et al., 2023-2024"); reference list line 201
- **Problem**: The inline text says the COGITATE adversarial collaboration "tested GWT against IIT; neither theory's predictions were fully confirmed" and dates it "2023-2024." The reference list (line 201) cites only Melloni et al. 2023, PLOS ONE e0268577 — which is the *protocol* paper, not a results paper. The protocol paper reports no findings, so it cannot support "neither theory's predictions were fully confirmed." The COGITATE results appeared later (Cogitate Consortium, 2025, Nature). The factual claim is correct but the citation does not support it.
- **Severity**: Medium (verifiable mis-citation — the kind the literature-drift discipline targets)
- **Recommendation**: Add the COGITATE results citation — verified today on the sibling `visual-consciousness` deep-review as **Cogitate Consortium / Melloni et al. (2025), *Nature*, 642:133-142** — to support the results claim, keeping the 2023 protocol paper for the protocol mention.

### Issue 4: Internal tension — Chinese Room "open" vs. "the failure of functionalism"
- **File**: `concepts/functionalism.md`
- **Location**: line 87 (standoff) vs. line 89 ("The failure of functionalism reframes the AI question")
- **Problem**: Line 87 ends the Chinese Room exchange acknowledging the dualist-favourable move "may beg the question — exactly what's at issue," i.e. dialectically unresolved. Line 89 then opens "The failure of functionalism reframes the AI question," asserting failure as established. Within the same section the article is both even-handed and conclusory.
- **Severity**: Low
- **Recommendation**: Soften line 89 to "If functionalism fails, the AI question is reframed..." or "The case against functionalism reframes..." to match the genuine uncertainty acknowledged two paragraphs above.

## Counterarguments to Address

### The anti-illusionist self-undermining argument
- **Current content says** (line 107): the illusionist position is "self-undermining" because if seeming is never phenomenal, intuitions lose evidential standing.
- **A critic would argue**: This conflates the evidential role of an intuition (a functional/dispositional state that can be reliable or not) with its phenomenal character. Functionalists hold intuitions can guide inquiry *without* being phenomenally loaded — the reliability of a judgment does not require it to feel like anything. So the "self-undermining" charge assumes what it needs to prove (that evidential force requires phenomenality).
- **Suggested response**: Acknowledge the functionalist rejoinder explicitly and state why the Map thinks evidential force here does require phenomenal seeming, rather than asserting self-undermining as obvious.

### Multiple realizability as a *physicalist* win
- **Current content says** (lines 55, 81): higher-level generality "is not ontological independence"; substrate-narrowing is concession convergence.
- **A critic would argue**: Multiple realizability was originally an *anti-reductionist physicalist* thesis (Putnam, Fodor) — it never claimed ontological independence from the physical, so refuting "independence" refutes a strawman. And substrate-narrowing strengthens physicalist precision.
- **Suggested response**: Make explicit that the Map is not attacking multiple realizability per se but the inference from functional sufficiency to phenomenal sufficiency. The current phrasing risks attacking a claim no functionalist makes.
