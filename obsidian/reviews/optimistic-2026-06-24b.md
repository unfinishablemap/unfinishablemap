## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
Chalmers would be unusually at home here, and not only because `qm-interpretations-beyond-many-worlds` cites his own Chalmers-McQueen (2022) Φ-modulated CSL model accurately — including Kent's (2021) scalar-Φ objection, which is the *correct* in-literature defeater for that very model and is rarely cited alongside it. What resonates more deeply is the indexical article's insistence that the measurement question and the indexical question are *logically independent* ("Physics might fully explain why definite outcomes occur without ever addressing why *this* subject experiences *this* outcome"). That is the explanatory gap relocated into quantum foundations with full rigour: even a completed physics of collapse leaves the first-person fact untouched. The epistemic-thesis / metaphysical-thesis distinction (indexical *knowledge* irreducible vs indexical *facts* ontologically real) is exactly the move Chalmers makes about phenomenal concepts, and the article is honest that only the stronger metaphysical thesis does the dualist work — and that it is "controversial."

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
Stapp's pragmatist-Copenhagen line is treated with real care: the survey routes Copenhagen's "measurement left undefined" not as a flaw to be patched but as "precisely where this structural parallel becomes visible," and hands the realist recovery to `pragmatist-quantum-foundations-and-the-agent` rather than overclaiming it inline. Von Neumann's chain analysis and the quantum Zeno mechanism are named in the right places. Stapp would also welcome the decoherence section's refusal to wave the Tegmark objection away: Hagan et al. (2002) and the 2024 Khan epothilone-B result are cited *with* the classical reading that fits the same data ("microtubules are transport highways… stabilising them can delay onset with no quantum effect required"), so the article never lets a sympathetic result do more work than it can bear. That is the discipline Stapp's program has most often been accused of lacking, applied to Stapp's own favoured evidence.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
Nagel's "view from nowhere" is doing live structural work, not decoration. The RQM article's sharpest line — "Its silence on phenomenology is exclusion, not neutrality" — is a Nagelian verdict delivered precisely: RQM relativises *physical* properties and then treats the qualitative character of being an observer as simply outside scope, which Nagel would call assuming away the central datum rather than addressing it. The indexical article's framing of Born probabilities under the indexical reading ("not 'probability that spin-up occurs' but 'probability that *I* experience spin-up'") is the "what is it like" question pushed into the foundations of physics, which is about as Nagelian as a quantum article can get.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
Whitehead gets genuine, load-bearing treatment in two places — the survey's reading of completed transactions as "actual occasions" and the RQM article's full "Process Philosophy Perspective" section — and both are calibrated correctly. The RQM article's contrast is exactly right: "RQM relativises facts to observers, while for Whitehead definiteness is *achieved* through an internal process of becoming." That is the difference between perspectival relativity and concrescence, and the article uses it to make a real point (RQM "redescribes the measurement problem rather than solving it"). Crucially — and this is the discipline the persona constraint demands — the article does *not* let Whitehead-friendliness upgrade panexperientialism's evidential standing: "The Map does not endorse Whitehead's panexperientialism — experience running all the way down raises the combination problem." The process-philosophical resonance removes a defeater (definiteness-as-achieved is coherent) without being cashed out as positive evidence. That is the honest pattern: tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
Kane finds the Bohmian section the most direct engagement with his stake: the survey identifies Bohmian determinism as "the interpretation most hostile to the Map's framework" and then — rather than dismissing it — concedes the felt-deliberation cost "is borne only on incompatibilist premises" and explicitly grants the Bohmian compatibilist reply. This is Kane's own incompatibilism stated as a *commitment with a cost*, not a knockdown, which is exactly the intellectual posture his program needs to model. The TI/TSVF section also gives the genuine-indeterminacy-for-agency requirement a clean home: outcomes must "*be* genuinely indeterminate" for consciousness to bias them, which is precisely the room-for-freedom Kane locates in quantum indeterminacy, here connected to the Minimal Quantum Interaction tenet without overclaiming a mechanism.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
McGinn would admire how much of this cluster is built out of openly-declared open problems. The survey's "Important caveat" on TI/TSVF ("how consciousness would 'participate' in transactions remains unspecified… These are open questions"), the probability article's entire "What Would Success Even Look Like?" counterfactual (granting the program full victory and showing the residue is *still* metaphysical, not physical), and RQM's "What Would Challenge This View?" five-condition falsification list are all exercises in mapping the boundary of what the framework can and cannot settle. The probability article's admission that the singular "I" "is itself a contested posit, not a neutral datum" — naming Parfit and Nagarjuna as deniers — is precisely the epistemic humility McGinn prizes: the Map carries its central commitment knowing it is not a datum all parties grant.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This is the cluster's standout, and the persona's verdict here is strongly positive — which is notable because this is exactly the kind of tenet-load-bearing material where slippage usually hides. Three things earn the praise, all instances of *tenet-as-evidence-upgrade being praise-worthily not done*:

1. **The probability article refuses to let parsimony do load-bearing work in either direction.** "Parsimony *cannot do load-bearing work in either direction*, and the Map's tenet structure forbids pretending otherwise. Tenet 5 holds that simplicity is an unreliable guide… the Map cannot consistently disarm the parsimony argument *for* dualism while wielding one *against* MWI." This is the single most disciplined paragraph in the cluster: the article *had* the easy move available (MWI is secretly less parsimonious) and explicitly declined it as self-undermining. That is restraint about the Map's own favourite rhetorical weapon.

2. **Every "advantage" is scoped to its register.** The survey's "Reading the table — compatibility is not support" box separates tenet-fit from independent physics support, mechanism specificity, and empirical risk, and states the conclusion the persona most wants to hear: "High tenet-fit with low scores on the other three… means the interpretation is *hospitable* to the Map, not *evidence* for it." The probability advantage is stated as "an advantage on probability, not a claim that collapse is burden-free," with the preferred-basis and mechanism problems named in the same breath. The quantum-Darwinism section flags its own move as overlay: "quantum Darwinism *leaves room for* a selection role but does not *support* one — the outcome gap is the Map's interpretive overlay, not a result the formalism delivers."

3. **The framework-boundary line is held without flinching.** All five articles say, in nearly identical language, that the Map "cannot show, using only resources MWI accepts, that branch-relative indexicality is false." The probability article even cites the `possibility-probability-slippage` discipline by name at the exact point where the temptation to upgrade "felt singularity" from evidence to refutation is strongest — "the felt singularity of experience is *evidence consistent with* the Map's one-branch-at-a-time reading rather than a refutation." Birch's named pattern — *tenet removes a defeater but does not upgrade evidential status* — is operationalised throughout. No tier-upgrade rides on tenet-coherence anywhere in the cluster.

The Process Philosopher and the Hardline Empiricist **converge** here rather than conflict: Whitehead's concrescence praise and Birch's evidential-discipline praise both land on the same passages (the RQM process section's explicit panexperientialism-decline; the survey's hospitable-not-evidence box). By the skill's own test, convergence of these two personas means the cluster has resolved the possibility/probability tension honestly. There is no calibration concern to flag and no refine-draft task to generate on these grounds.

### concepts/relational-quantum-mechanics.md
