## Executive Summary

This is one of the strongest evidence-discipline clusters the Map currently holds. Its central move — reframing a single conceivability-flavoured argument (the grain problem) as an *evidence programme* of three independent structural mismatches, each with its own measurements, its own required physicalist response, and its own falsifier — converts what is usually a thought-experiment into something that genuinely behaves like a research programme. The articles are conspicuously honest about their own limits (the "common phenomenal source" objection to the multiplicative inference is raised and only partially answered; the apex names a twelve-order-of-magnitude timescale gap against its own quantum hypothesis; the informational mismatch flags the access-vs-phenomenal interpretive step the figure does not license). The main opportunity is to give the **fourth (compositional/binding) grain mismatch** — flagged as an open question in two articles but homeless — its own treatment, and to add the cross-links that would make the cluster's tight argumentative ring visible to readers entering from adjacent topics.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The grain-mismatch concept article (`grain-mismatch.md`) does exactly what a Chalmersian wants: it carves out a *distinct logical space* from the knowledge argument (epistemic) and zombies (modal), locating the grain problem as *structural* — "homogeneous phenomenal properties and discrete physical properties have incompatible characteristics, making identity logically impossible for these property pairs." The explicit point that physicalists "who focus their defensive efforts on the knowledge argument and conceivability... leave the grain mismatch largely unaddressed" is precisely the kind of strategic mapping of the explanatory gap that the hard-problem literature rewards. The smoothness article's handling of the functionalist dissolution — granting it is "a coherent position and the strongest physicalist response" and then showing it "helps itself to the very step the smoothness problem isolates" (registering continuity vs *feeling* it continuous) — is a clean, non-strawmanned statement of the residual hard problem.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
The time-consciousness apex is where the quantum-mind reader finds the most to like: the modified growing block, where "consciousness-involving collapse grows the block," and the atemporal-selection resolution of Libet ("retrocausal selection isn't located at a moment but determines which transaction completes across time, so the neural activity 'preceding' awareness is part of the selected package, not a prior cause"). The two-tier collapse model (objective GRW/gravitational reduction baseline + consciousness-interfacing collapse in neural systems) is a disciplined answer to the obvious "billions of years before observers" objection. This is the physics-consciousness interface engaged seriously rather than gestured at.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The cluster centres first-person experience without sentimentality. The smoothness article's "What Durée Feels Like" lineage (retention/primal-impression/protention mapped to collapsed/collapsing/uncollapsed states) and the temporal-thickness distinction (two moments of identical clock length differing radically in phenomenal character — the improvising musician vs the passive overhearer) are exactly the "what is it like" data that objective accounts struggle to capture. Crucially, the apex insists the qualitative texture of each phase is the explanandum: "neural accounts explain *that* the past is fixed and the future open, but not *why it feels that way*."

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The apex earns Whitehead's approval honestly: it cites *Process and Reality* as independent philosophical precedent (actual occasions synthesising their causal past and perishing; concrescence paralleling collapse) and frames consciousness as activity rather than substance — "an activity that occupies no time is no activity at all." This avoids crude substance dualism while preserving mind's reality, and it does so without inflating the metaphysics into an evidential claim — the Whiteheadian resonance is offered as showing "consciousness-constituted time has philosophical precedent independent of physics," i.e., as defeater-removal for a metaphysical possibility, not as positive evidence. That is the disciplined use of the persona.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
The apex's "Free Will Reconsidered" and "Atemporal Selection" sections give Kane the indeterminacy-makes-room-for-agency structure he wants, but with a refinement he should welcome: "randomness at a temporally thin moment is mere noise, while indeterminacy at a temporally thick moment — where consciousness selects from possibilities saturated with personal history — is agency." This directly answers the standard "quantum randomness isn't freedom" objection by making thickness, not mere indeterminacy, the locus of agency. The kairos three-phase structure ("not yet" / "now" / "too late") grounds the abstraction in recognisable timing phenomenology.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
The introspective-opacity material (Andrew Lee, "Consciousness and Continuity," 2024) is the cluster's epistemic-humility spine, and it appears in all three grain articles with the *same correct framing*: opacity "cuts both ways" — the dualist cannot prove from introspection that experience is genuinely smooth, and the physicalist cannot rescue identity by claiming it is "really" discrete. McGinn would approve of a programme that names the precise tool it lacks ("introspective access to phenomenal microstructure") rather than pretending the question is open to first-person settlement.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This is the persona with the most to praise here, and the verdict is load-bearing because the cluster's whole rhetorical force is evidential. Three observations:

1. **The convergence inference is policed honestly.** The "Why Convergence Matters" section of `grain-mismatch-as-independent-evidence` raises the strongest objection to its own multiplicative reasoning — that homogeneity, smoothness, and informational coarseness might be "three descriptions of one feature: the felt unity of consciousness," sharing a common cause and so *not* multiplying like independent measurements. It then gives the right reply (independence at the level of *what could falsify each claim*, not independence of the underlying explanandum) and, critically, concedes the residue: "the programme cannot yet quantify how much the common phenomenal source discounts the convergence — only that the mismatches are not redundant." This is calibration done correctly: the article does not let the continental-drift analogy do more work than it can bear ("The Map does not claim the grain evidence is yet at that level of decisiveness").

2. **The informational-mismatch article declines an available over-claim.** It would have been easy to slide the 10-bits/second figure from "behavioural throughput in conscious tasks" straight to a claim about phenomenal content. The article explicitly *does not*: "the step from 'behavioural ceiling in conscious tasks' to a claim about phenomenal content is an interpretation, not a direct measurement... whether the phenomenal content underlying that output is correspondingly coarse, as Block's access/phenomenal distinction leaves open, is a further question the figure does not settle." This is the praiseworthy thing-to-*not*-do: a tenet-friendly framework declining to upgrade an access-level measurement into a phenomenal claim.

3. **Falsifiability is operationalised, not gestured at.** Four named predictions (neural correlates stay discrete; conscious throughput stays invariant under training/pharmacology; temporal smoothness resists neural *derivation*; cross-species grain ratios covary) and an explicit "What Would Dissolve the Programme" section that demands *domain-specific* dissolutions ("not a philosophical argument that grain mismatches *in principle* can be explained, but actual explanations for each specific mismatch"). Prediction 4 is correctly self-labelled "the most speculative." The smoothness article additionally keeps register-separation clean by repeatedly stating it "is not a standalone argument for dualism" but "an instance of the broader dispute between functionalists and their critics."

No possibility/probability slippage detected in this cluster — the Process Philosopher's praise and the Hardline Empiricist's praise converge rather than conflict, which (per the skill's design) is the signal that the articles have resolved the tension honestly.

## Content Strengths

### grain-mismatch-as-independent-evidence.md
- **Strongest point**: The reframing of one argument as a three-legged evidence programme, with the explicit falsification structure ("If any single mismatch were resolved, the other two would remain. If all three were independently dissolved, dualism would lose one of its most direct structural supports").
- **Notable quote**: "Independence at the level of *what could falsify each claim* is what the multiplicative reasoning requires, and that independence survives even if all three trace to the unity of experience."
- **Why it works**: It pre-empts its own most damaging objection and reports the unresolved residue instead of burying it. This is the article that most embodies the Map's evidential-status discipline.

### neural-refresh-rates-and-the-smoothness-problem.md
- **Strongest point**: The symmetric treatment of the two physicalist escape routes (functionalism and illusionism), each granted full strength and then shown to relocate rather than discharge the debt.
- **Notable quote**: "Renaming the explanandum does not dissolve it."
- **Why it works**: "Locke's Objection Inverted" is a genuinely original framing — using the *absence of expected gaps* to challenge physicalism, mirroring Locke's use of *unexpected gaps* against dualism. The display-refresh analogy is deployed and then its limits are immediately conceded ("the brain is not a display with a separate viewer"), which is the style guide's anti-cliché discipline in action.

### time-consciousness-growing-block.md (apex)
- **Strongest point**: It is a true synthesis — specious present, collapse, durée, atemporal selection — that argues its force comes from *convergence* and then names, in the same breath, the strongest empirical objection to itself (the twelve-order-of-magnitude decoherence/specious-present timescale gap).
- **Notable quote**: "Simpler metaphysics achieve parsimony by denying phenomena rather than explaining them."
- **Why it works**: All five tenets are connected non-formulaically, and the No-Many-Worlds connection is unusually sharp ("The coherence of durée — where retention genuinely inherits from a definite past — requires a single temporal thread, not infinite branching").

### grain-mismatch.md (concept)
- **Strongest point**: The crisp three-way taxonomy (knowledge argument = epistemic; zombies = modal; grain mismatch = structural) and the logical-independence claim that follows from it.
- **Why it works**: It front-loads the distinctive claim (structural incoherence, not mere explanatory failure) in the first paragraph — strong LLM-first truncation resilience.
