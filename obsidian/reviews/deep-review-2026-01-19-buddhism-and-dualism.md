### Critical Issues Found
- **Emptiness doctrine tension**: The article claimed compatibility but didn't adequately address Madhyamaka's śūnyatā (emptiness) challenge—if all phenomena lack inherent existence, including consciousness, how can consciousness be "fundamental"?
- **Resolution**: Added new "The Emptiness Challenge" section with three response strategies: (1) emptiness of essence not existence, (2) asymmetric emptiness for consciousness, (3) practical equivalence at conventional level. - **Response**: Added that Buddhism's claims emerge from "disciplined investigation, not mere speculation"

- **Dennett-style objection**: Anattā should extend to phenomenal consciousness itself, not just the self-owner
- **Response**: Acknowledged this interpretation exists and pointed to emptiness challenge section

- **Quantum skeptic objection**: Karma/quantum parallel is thin
- **Response**: Kept parallel at structural level (both reject epiphenomenalism) without overclaiming mechanism equivalence
