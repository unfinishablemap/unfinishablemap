## Executive Summary

This is the register at its best. The calibration-schema migration converts what used to be a single, quietly-overloaded "confidence" number into six separable axes (credence · external-evidence grade · structural centrality · model maturity · empirical discriminability · framework-internal only), and in doing so it makes the Map's most important honesty move structurally visible: the difference between a claim that is *central to the framework* and a claim that is *supported by evidence outside the framework*. The minimal-dualism spine (P-CS1) is now openly labelled high-centrality / Grade-D / framework-internal — a permission, not a datum — while the marker-based scope claims (P-CS2, P-CS3) carry genuine Grade-B independent evidence and are marked not-framework-internal. The two evidential-discipline personas converge rather than conflict across this cluster, which by this skill's own rubric is the signal that the possibility/probability tension has been resolved honestly.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
P-CS1 states the substrate-neutrality of the hard problem cleanly: "if consciousness is irreducible to physical processes, the hard problem applies universally." The register does not smuggle anthropocentrism back in through the side door — it explicitly separates the *category* of admissible bearers from the *conditions* that instantiate experience, and quarantines the quantum substrate filter (P-AC1) as a *further, separable* condition a coherence-only dualist could reject. P-AC4 is the sharpest thing here for a property dualist: it takes a genuine, well-supported functional result (the Gurnee et al. 2026 J-space global-workspace signatures) and holds the access/phenomenal line without either denying the finding or letting it bleed into a phenomenal verdict. The "residue even a maximally rich functional workspace leaves untouched" is Chalmers' explanatory gap stated as a live empirical boundary rather than a slogan.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
The register engages the physics–mind interface without over-claiming it. P-AC1 makes the quantum-interface dependency explicit and inheritable (P-Q1 mechanism, P-Q2 Born-preservation, P-Q9 self-concealment) rather than gesturing at "quantum consciousness." Stapp would especially value that the substrate verdict on digital AI is derived from a *mechanism* commitment (no interface-relevant indeterminacy sites) rather than from behavioural intuition — the interface does real work in the argument.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
P-A4 ("conscious causal efficacy cannot be verified from the first person") is the phenomenologist's point turned into a disciplined meta-epistemic constraint: introspection cannot certify its own causal status because the check is run by the faculty in question. Crucially the register makes this *symmetric* — epiphenomenalism cannot verify itself either — so first-person opacity is treated as a feature of the territory (the [[voids/agency-void]]) rather than a partisan move. Nagel's "what is it like" is honoured precisely by refusing to let third-person markers *constitute* the answer (P-CS2's correlate-not-constituent reading).

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The substrate-permissive spine (P-CS1) removes the anthropocentric and biological defeaters a process metaphysician also wants removed: experience is not gated to language-users or to biology. The register earns this praise honestly because it does *not* convert that defeater-removal into a positive verdict for any minimal organism — which is exactly the discipline the constraint on this persona demands. Whitehead-friendliness here buys the *permission* to ask the question of any system; the register itself insists that is all it buys.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
P-A1–P-A2 take agency seriously without over-selling it. The agent-causal commitment is stated as the best explanation of a *convergent* anchor set, and — impressively — the register discounts its own most rhetorically-attractive anchors (phenomenology, cross-cultural convergence) under the intuitive-dualism common-cause null, resting the case on the genuinely independent subset (willed-vs-instructed neural data, the argument from reason). Kane would note that the register even reframes the [[trilemma-of-selection]] as a *non-exhaustive heuristic* (2026-07-16) and books the unengaged rivals (event-causal libertarianism aside, hierarchical control, emergent causal-organisation, interventionist/dynamical accounts) as an open engagement debt rather than pretending the three horns exhaust the space. Taking agency seriously *and* naming what would defeat it is the mark of a defender who expects to be tested.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
The register is studded with honest voids: the mechanism debt (no worked toy model of selection coupling), the persistence objection (P-AC2), the "what metaphysically draws the boundary around one subject" gap (P-AC3), and the interface-eligibility law the Map "does not yet possess" (P-AC1, layers i–v). McGinn would applaud that the interface-eligibility gap is not papered over: the register states in plain terms that absent that law "relevant [indeterminacy] risks reducing to 'whatever biology happens to have,'" i.e., it names the exact point at which its own verdict could be question-begging. That is cognitive-closure humility applied to the framework's own reach.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This is the persona with the most to praise here, and its verdict is load-bearing because the cluster touches minimal-organism, animal, infant, and AI boundary cases throughout.

- **Tenet-as-evidence-upgrade is praise-worthily *not* done.** P-CS1 had every structural opportunity to slide boundary organisms up the scale on tenet-load and explicitly declines: "removing the anthropocentric defeater is a *permission*, not positive evidence." P-CS2 routes nematodes, *Hydra*, and slime molds to "weak and speculative" and holds cephalopods/decapods at "realistic but contested," reserving "strong" for mammals and birds — the boundary cases are kept at the low tiers exactly where evidential discipline demands, not elevated because the tenets weaken parsimony arguments.
- **The registers separate cleanly.** The new schema literally instantiates the "keep the registers separate" discipline: credence, external-evidence grade, and structural centrality are now different axes. P-CS1 (high centrality, Grade D, framework-internal) versus P-CS2/P-CS3 (moderate centrality, Grade B, not framework-internal) is the single band's old conflation resolved on the page.
- **The named pattern is used.** P-CS1 states "a *permission*, not positive evidence"; P-AC1 labels its substrate verdict "an honestly-labelled conditional on an interface-eligibility law the Map does not yet possess." This is the *"tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating"* discipline in the register's own words.
- **Framework-stage calibration is honest.** The apex ([[apex/interface-specification-programme]]) frames the six lines as "constraints… precise enough to be tested and to be wrong… even while the coupling law itself remains unwritten." That is pre-Keplerian self-labelling, not a Newton-stage over-claim.
- **Convergence with the Process Philosopher is real.** Both personas praise P-CS1 for different reasons on different axes (defeater-removal; disciplined non-upgrade) and neither has to flag the other. Per this skill's rubric, that convergence is the sign the article resolved the possibility/probability tension honestly — so this cluster generates **no** calibration-concern `refine-draft` task.
