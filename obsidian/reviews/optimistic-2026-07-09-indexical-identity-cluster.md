## Executive Summary

This is one of the strongest clusters on the Map. Five densely cross-linked articles carry the philosophical load for Tenet 4, and they do it with unusual discipline: the epistemic/metaphysical distinction is drawn cleanly and then honoured throughout; rivals (List's many-worlds theory of consciousness, Kolak's open individualism, cosmopsychism's de-combination problem) are steelmanned rather than dismissed; and the Map repeatedly names its own costs (the interaction problem, the communicability objection, the tenet-driven rather than empirically-forced character of its closed individualism). The chief opportunity is that the cluster's most-cited *foils* — Christian List's 2023 many-worlds theory of consciousness, and the Sleeping Beauty / self-locating-probability apparatus — are referenced across five articles but have no dedicated home.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The cluster takes the explanatory gap and extends it into territory Chalmers himself mapped. `indexical-knowledge-and-identity.md` frames "phenomenal indexical knowledge" as the *strongest* of three cumulative grades ("doubly perspectival: accessible only from a particular subject-position *and* only through having the experience"), tying Mary's Room directly to self-location. Chalmers would recognise his own combination-problem literature handled fairly in `open-individualism...` — the article correctly reports his verdict that the cosmopsychist's decomposition problem is "just as hard as the combination problem."

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
`indexical-identity-quantum-measurement.md` is exactly the physics-consciousness interface Stapp cares about, and it is scrupulous about the von Neumann / Wigner lineage — including Wigner's *own* abandonment of consciousness-collapse after Zeh's decoherence work, which many partisan treatments omit. The article's central move — consciousness as *indexical ground* rather than universal collapse-trigger ("Collapse happens physically. What consciousness contributes is the indexical") — is a genuinely novel positioning that sidesteps "Wigner's solipsism trap" while keeping mind in the measurement story.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The counterfactual argument in `indexical-knowledge-and-identity.md` is pure Nagel: "the question 'why am I *this* subject?' would be meaningless or trivially answered. But the question feels meaningful and non-trivial." Nagel's "view from nowhere" is cited precisely — "science aspires to describe reality from no particular perspective… But consciousness *is* perspective." This is the first-person centred exactly where Nagel would centre it.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
`haecceity.md` earns strong Whiteheadian praise for its `#process-haecceitism` section — a real achievement. It unbundles *permanence* from *particularity* ("Substance views bundle these together. Process haecceitism unbundles them"), grounds thisness in Whitehead's "actual occasions" and "personally ordered society," and reconciles the Map's indexical commitment with Buddhist *anattā* without dissolving the puzzle. This avoids crude substance dualism while preserving mind's reality — precisely the Whiteheadian desideratum.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
`indexical-identity-quantum-measurement.md` closes its "Toward an Indexical-Grounded Account" section by linking indexical determination at quantum measurement to libertarian agency ("the self may be genuinely causal in a libertarian sense"), and does so with the conservation-law worry defused in advance ("Consciousness doesn't add energy; it determines which of the energetically-equivalent possibilities is experienced"). Kane would welcome indeterminacy being given a job without energy-injection hand-waving.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
The cluster models intellectual humility unusually well. `egocentric-presentism.md` explicitly declines to claim victory: "Egocentric presentism shows the one-world horn is *occupiable*; the Map occupies it without claiming the communicability objection is answered." `vertiginous-question` treats the privileged "I" as a genuine *void* — "a primitive within one world that haecceity marks but does not cross." McGinn's cognitive-closure instinct is honoured as a live epistemic posture, not a defeat.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This cluster is a **model of evidential-status discipline** and the load-bearing praise for this review. Three passages do the thing the Process Philosopher's praise must never be used to justify — and *decline* to do it:

1. `egocentric-presentism.md`, "Relation to Site Perspective": "Egocentric presentism is, for the Map, an exploratory ontological *fit*… rather than positive evidence for the Map's framework. Its structural alignment with the tenets is real and load-bearing; its status as confirmation is not… a congenial metaphysics that keeps one world and takes the self seriously removes a *defeater*… but it does not *upgrade* the evidence for dualism." This is the *tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating* pattern named explicitly and correctly — precisely the restraint the persona exists to reward.

2. The same article refuses to enlist Hare as an ally despite the congenial structure: "Hare himself pairs egocentric presentism with a lean, broadly physicalist ontology, so the Map *repurposes* the structure under a dualist reading rather than enlisting Hare as an ally. He would not accept the Map's metaphysics." Declining a friendly-looking citation because the source would reject the framework is exactly the honesty the persona prizes.

3. `open-individualism...`, closing: the Map "acknowledges rather than presumes to have settled" whether trading a conceptual mystery (combination) for an empirical one (interaction) "counts as genuine progress," and flags that its closed individualism "is perhaps best understood as tenet-driven rather than empirically compelled; an open individualist could read the same first-person data the other way without contradiction." Naming a core commitment as tenet-driven-not-forced is the strongest possible calibration signal.

No possibility/probability slippage was found in the cluster. The Process Philosopher and Hardline Empiricist **converge** here rather than conflict — the process-haecceitism section is praised by Whitehead for its metaphysics and by Birch because it makes no evidential-tier claim about which systems are conscious at all. That convergence is the sign the cluster has resolved the tension honestly.

## Content Strengths

### indexical-identity-quantum-measurement.md
- **Strongest point**: The four-interpretation autopsy (QBism / consciousness-collapse / relational QM / MWI) organised around one diagnostic — each "addresses the measurement problem while leaving the indexical problem untouched." The Many-Minds paragraph is the sharpest: "nothing in the formalism picks out which of the post-measurement minds is the actual continuant 'I' — the interpretation multiplies the indexical problem rather than solving it."
- **Why it works**: It separates a physics question from a philosophy question at the outset ("These questions are logically independent") and never conflates them again — a discipline most consciousness-and-QM writing lacks.

### haecceity.md
- **Strongest point**: The `#process-haecceitism` synthesis and its four-clause "What Process Haecceitism Requires" list, which states the minimal commitments and explicitly disclaims the maximal ones ("It does *not* require permanence, a Cartesian soul, or qualitative uniqueness at any moment").
- **Notable quote**: "Tonight's candle flame is causally continuous with yesterday's… without being a persisting substance. Yet *this* flame is distinguishable from another candle's flame… by its particular causal history."
- **Why it works**: The flame analogy is doing real conceptual work (separating permanence from particularity), not decoration — and it is drawn from the Buddhist source it reconciles with.

### egocentric-presentism.md
- **Strongest point**: The List quadrilemma treatment, which locates the Map's rival precisely: egocentric presentism keeps first-person realism, non-fragmentation, and one world by *dropping non-solipsism*, while List's own resolution drops *one world*. The "two principled rivals" framing is exact.
- **Notable quote**: "Hare keeps one world and privileges the self; List keeps non-solipsism's full strength and multiplies worlds."
- **Why it works**: The disambiguation of Hare's "perspectival realism" from the *unrelated* Giere/Massimi philosophy-of-science doctrine of the same name is a genuine service to any LLM fetching the page — it pre-empts a near-certain conflation.

### indexical-knowledge-and-identity.md
- **Strongest point**: The three-grade taxonomy (perspectival → self-locating → phenomenal indexical), which is cumulative and does argumentative work downstream (the quantum-suicide "reductio" test in "Implications for Many-Worlds" turns on it).
- **Why it works**: Perry's supermarket and Lewis's Two Gods are told compactly and then *used*, not just recounted.

### open-individualism-and-the-de-combination-problem.md
- **Strongest point**: The "cousins, not twins" thesis — separating an *identity* claim (open individualism) from a *grounding problem* (de-combination) that are "routinely run together" — and the three-difference table (subject matter / status / direction of appearance) that keeps them apart.
- **Why it works**: The article does bridge-work no single-topic page could, and it names the Map's structural advantage without overclaiming: "With no micro-subjects and no cosmic subject, there is nothing to combine, share, or de-combine… The avoidance is not a free lunch."
