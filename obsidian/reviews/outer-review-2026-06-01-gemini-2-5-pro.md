## 5. The Interactionist Evasion of Noether's First Theorem

A persistent, historically devastating vulnerability for any theory of interactionist dualism is the objection from energy conservation, which stipulates that a non-physical mind interacting causally with a physical brain would inevitably violate the thermodynamic closure of the universe. This argument relies on a profound, superficial misunderstanding of Hamiltonian mechanics and Noether's first theorem (Dimension C). The corpus deliberately frames the lack of *macroscopic energy injection at a singular, instantaneous moment of quantum collapse* as wholly sufficient to satisfy the laws of thermodynamics. In doing so, it entirely ignores the geometric and temporal roots of conservation physics.

As meticulously delineated in the contemporary philosophy of physics literature, notably by Cucu and Pitts (2019), the principle of energy conservation is not merely an empirical accounting trick or a biological bank account of joules; it is a profound mathematical derivation via Emmy Noether's first theorem. Noether mathematically proved that the conservation of energy is strictly bi-conditional upon time-translation symmetry. If a physical system's dynamics and underlying Lagrangian remain invariant under continuous shifts in time, energy is strictly conserved. If the symmetry is broken, energy cannot be conserved.

If the interactionist dualism proposed by the framework is true—if an irreducible, non-physical consciousness actively influences the physical trajectory of neural events by choosing a basis, determining the precise timing of a collapse via the Zeno effect, or biasing probabilities—then the temporal evolution of the physical brain is inextricably dependent on a non-physical, time-varying conscious variable. Consequently, the Hamiltonian describing the physical brain is inherently time-dependent (it changes depending on exactly *when* the non-physical mind decides to focus its attention or exert its agency).

A time-dependent Hamiltonian explicitly breaks time-translation symmetry. By breaking time-translation symmetry, the neural system mathematically *cannot* conserve energy. The framework's central, comforting claim that it successfully preserves physical causal closure and energy conservation simply by hiding the dualist interaction inside the "quantum opening" is a mathematical fiction.

Cucu and Pitts argue forcefully that interactionist dualists should abandon the hopeless attempt to pacify energy conservation through quantum loopholes. Instead, they should adopt the rigorous "conditionality response"—openly acknowledging that since mind-brain interaction inherently breaks continuous mathematical symmetries, energy conservation is simply false at the highly localized locus of the neural interaction. The audited framework completely omits this mathematically rigorous reality (Dimension D), falsely comforting the reader with the unsupported assertion that selecting a quantum outcome requires zero energy exchange, thereby bracketing the underlying continuous symmetries that actually dictate conservation physics.
