## Cross-Review Findings

### Against [[taxonomy-of-voids]]

- **Convergent commitment**: Taxonomy-of-voids argues void structure is data about consciousness's architecture (organised by *type*, *domain*, *failure signature*). The conjunction-coalesce identifies a *fourth* axis the taxonomy doesn't capture: a *relation* between voids (the conjoint two-face structure). This was already gestured at in the article's §Synthesis but is now sharper after the apex-placement section makes the dependency explicit.
- **Distinct work**: Taxonomy-of-voids is *organisational* (where voids fit in a catalogue); conjunction-coalesce is *editorial* (how to express conjoint structure in article form). The two pieces are complementary, not redundant.
- **Reciprocal load-bearing**: The conjunction-coalesce's dialectical justification for apex placement *depends* on taxonomy-of-voids' claim that void structure is informative. If taxonomy-of-voids' central claim weakened, conjunction-coalesce's apex-placement case would weaken with it. This is now stated explicitly.

### Against [[apex-articles]] (the index)

- The apex-articles index admits the conjunction-coalesce as the 21st entry with the explicit guideline: "future additions should require similar dialectical justification to prevent quiet drift past the curated set."
- The article's new §"A Note on Apex Placement" reads this guideline as live policy and operates within it: it states the dialectical justification, acknowledges the counter-case, and accepts ongoing scrutiny.
- This is healthier than letting the article quietly inherit apex status without engaging the question.

### Against other methodological apex articles

The Map currently has a small number of apex pieces with methodological emphasis ([[interface-specification-programme]], [[post-decoherence-selection-programme]]) but these synthesise *philosophical research programmes* in physics and consciousness. They are not methodology-about-the-Map; they are research programmes the Map endorses. Conjunction-coalesce is genuinely different in kind: it is a piece on the Map's editorial practice. The cross-review confirms the pessimistic review's category-drift concern is real, while also confirming that the dialectical justification offered in §"A Note on Apex Placement" is the appropriate response (rather than relocation to `project/`).
