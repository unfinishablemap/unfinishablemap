## Pessimistic Analysis Summary

### Critical Issues Found

- **Attribution error — Derrida "polished to invisibility"**: The phrase "metaphors polished to the point of invisibility" was presented as a direct quote from Derrida's "White Mythology" (1971), enclosed in quotation marks. The coin-worn-to-illegibility image is actually drawn by Derrida from Anatole France's *Le Jardin d'Épicure* (1895). Presenting it as verbatim Derrida is a misattribution. **Resolution**: Rewrote the passage to credit Anatole France's image (which Derrida cites) and paraphrase the argument rather than quote it. Removed the associated unverifiable direct quotes ("Metaphysics and metaphor are locked in reciprocal antagonism..." and "There will always be at least one metaphor...") and rendered them as paraphrase.
- **Dangling reference**: Wittgenstein's *Tractatus* appeared in the References list but was never cited in the body. **Resolution**: Removed the Wittgenstein reference; integration would have added length and the body's argument does not currently require him.

### Medium Issues Found

- **Style guide violation — "This is not X. It is Y." construct**: Line 89 used the exact forbidden pattern ("This is not a reason to abandon the project. It is a reason to hold it..."). **Resolution**: Rephrased to integrate the contrast positively: "The consequence for the Map is humility rather than collapse... The Map should therefore hold its findings with appropriate epistemic reservation..."
- **Softer instance of same pattern**: "This is not a difficulty that can be overcome by effort or ingenuity" — rephrased to the more direct "No amount of effort or ingenuity dissolves this difficulty."
- **Missing cross-link**: Lakoff/Johnson's "embodied domains" was undertstruck. **Resolution**: Added [[embodied-cognition]] wikilink at the natural anchor.

### Counterarguments Considered

- **Eliminative Materialist**: Would reject the entire frame — "voids" and "metaphor" as tracked here presuppose phenomenal content whose existence is disputed. Response: The article acknowledges this implicitly by framing the metaphor void as *mixed* (partly unexplorable, partly occluded) and by connecting to Dualism explicitly as a site-perspective commitment. The bedrock disagreement with eliminativism remains.
- **Empiricist (Popper's Ghost)**: The claim that "metaphor constitutively distorts the description of cognitive limits" is itself unfalsifiable — every counterexample can be reframed as still-metaphorical. Response: The article's honesty about this ("none of them escapes from metaphor") functions as an acknowledgment rather than evasion.
- **Derridean pedant**: Could object that the rewritten Derrida passage now underquotes rather than misquotes — that Derrida's actual vocabulary ("usure," "concept," "metaphysics of presence") is load-bearing and paraphrase loses it. Response: The tradeoff favors accuracy over precision here; presenting invented direct quotes as verbatim Derrida was the larger error.
