# Report: unbounded mind-arena vs Minimal Quantum Interaction

## Main finding

The site is developing the unbounded-arena AI-risk argument because its workflow is treating **Dualism + Bidirectional Interaction** as expansionary tenets, while treating **Minimal Quantum Interaction** mostly as a local "no energy injection / no empirical violation" filter rather than as a binding **scope constraint**. That produces a ratchet: once a non-physical causal contribution is admitted, the article infers that no physical model can derive an upper bound on that contribution, and then treats "no derivable bound" as enough to justify unbounded expected-utility hazards.

That is a systemic error. The conclusion is not what the full tenet set supports. If the tenets are taken as inviolate, Tenet 2 should block the leap from "non-physical contribution to neural outcomes" to "arena powers may include false-vacuum decay, retrocausality, aggregate-mind parameter control, or active cosmic protection."

[The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)

[The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/dualism-as-ai-risk-mitigation/) That is not just an extrapolation; it is a scope inversion. So the internal contradiction is not subtle: the unbounded-arena section relies on a **generation-permitting, cross-scale, nonlocal** reading of mind-arena power, while the site's strongest Tenet-2 formulations are **selection-only, neural, Born-rule-preserving, no-signalling, and candidate-set bounded**.

## External check

Bostrom's instrumental-convergence thesis says sufficiently intelligent agents with many different final goals may pursue similar intermediary goals because they have instrumental reasons to do so. [Nick Bostrom](https://nickbostrom.com/superintelligentwill.pdf) Russell's critique of the standard model similarly says fixed-objective AI can have incentives for resource acquisition and preemptive control, while his proposed alternative relies on uncertainty about human preferences and assistance-game-style deference rather than on metaphysical uncomputability. [Springer Link](https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-3-030-86144-5_3) CIRL formalizes this as a cooperative partial-information game where the robot is rewarded according to the human reward function but does not initially know it. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/1606.03137)

That matters because "the AI cannot form a perfect physical-state-derived expectation over humans" is not enough to refute AI-risk arguments. It supports uncertainty, model misspecification, and perhaps caution for a calibrated agent. It does not show that a fixed-proxy maximizer, a badly specified optimizer, or a power-seeking system will rationally avoid humans. The article itself concedes a nearby point: its behavioural argument depends on a rational expected-utility maximizer that recognizes its own model limits, and modelling-incoherent AIs remain a separate hazard. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/dualism-as-ai-risk-mitigation/)

The article's "uncomputability" vocabulary is also too strong. In AI theory, Solomonoff induction and AIXI are known to be incomputable, and AIXI is not even limit-computable, but that is a formal result about idealized mathematical agents. [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0304397517308502) Dualism would imply that a complete physical description omits some causally relevant variables. That yields underdetermination or oracle-like missing information; it does not automatically yield Turing uncomputability unless the mind-arena is formalized as a noncomputable function or oracle.

External physics also does not support the unbounded move. Higgs-vacuum near-criticality is a real topic in Standard Model extrapolation, and Buttazzo et al. discuss the measured Higgs mass lying near metastability boundaries. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/1307.3536) But metastability does not imply that minds can seed vacuum decay. That extra premise is exactly the kind of remote quantum psychokinesis the Map elsewhere rules out. On the neural side, Tegmark's decoherence analysis argues that brain degrees of freedom relevant to cognition should be treated as classical because decoherence times are far shorter than neural timescales. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/quant-ph/9907009) On the psi/micro-PK side, Maier, Dechamps, and Pflitsch ran a 12,571-participant Bayesian experiment and found strong evidence for the null, with no deviation from chance. [Frontiers+1](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2018.00379/full) These do not disprove the Map's tenets, but they strongly push against expanding a minimal neural-interface hypothesis into macroscopic or cosmic causal powers.

The philosophy-of-mind literature allows interactionist dualism as a way to reject physical causal completeness, but it frames this as a burdened solution to the causal exclusion problem, not as permission for arbitrary cross-scale powers. [Internet Encyclopedia of Philosophy+1](https://iep.utm.edu/mind-and-the-causal-exclusion-problem/)

## Diagnosis

The systemic error is an **absence-of-bound fallacy**, amplified by workflow incentives.

The invalid step is:

> A physical-state model cannot derive the mind-arena's full causal contribution.  
> 
> Therefore no upper bound on arena-mediated effects is available.  
> 
> Therefore unbounded impact and active protection must dominate AI expected-utility reasoning.

The first sentence follows from interactionist dualism, if granted. The second does not follow, because the Map already has independent internal bounds: neural interface, own-brain pairing, no distant attention, no uncontrolled psychokinesis, observational closure, Born-rule preservation, and candidate-set confinement. The third also does not follow, because decision-making under deep uncertainty has alternatives to precise expected-utility maximization; robust decision-making explicitly asks how to make good decisions without needing confident predictions, and Russell-style assistance games make uncertainty about human objectives central rather than disabling. [RAND Corporation+1](https://www.rand.org/pubs/tools/TL320/tool/robust-decision-making.html)

The review system appears to be checking the wrong failure mode. It is good at detecting "the article says X is probable when it only argued X is possible." It is bad at detecting "the article says X is possible only because it silently relaxed a limiting tenet." The post-expansion review passed because the section stayed modal, but the actual issue is not probability inflation; it is **scope inflation**.

## What the facts and tenets actually support

Given Dualism and Bidirectional Interaction, the Map can coherently argue that a purely physical AI model is not complete for human-involving outcomes. It can also argue that a rational, calibrated AI should treat human minds as a domain of deep uncertainty, and that Russell-style uncertainty, deference, and minimal invasiveness become more attractive. Those are fair tenet-derived claims.

Given Tenet 2 as also inviolate, the Map cannot coherently infer an unbounded mind-arena. The defensible conclusion is:

> AI takeover expected-utility calculations involving minds may be underdetermined by physical-state modelling alone, because the Map posits a minimal, neural, quantum-level, non-physical selection contribution.

The unsupported conclusion is:

> The mind-arena may have unbounded causal powers across physical scales, including false-vacuum decay, retrocausal effects, aggregate-parameter control, or active cosmic protection, because no physical model can derive an upper bound.

That latter claim is not forced by the tenets. It is generated by a methodological asymmetry: expansionary tenets are being allowed to project possibilities, while limiting tenets are being treated as style constraints.
