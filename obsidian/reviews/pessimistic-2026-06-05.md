## Critiques by Philosopher

### The Eliminative Materialist (Churchland)
The register concedes the whole game in P-Q10: there is *no worked model* — no Hamiltonian, no master equation, no simulation — of how "conscious selection" couples to a neural quantum state. Ten positions are erected on a coupling term that has never been written down. The eliminativist reads P-Q1–P-Q9 as an elaborate taxonomy of *where the equation would go if it existed*, which is folk-psychological place-holding dressed in the vocabulary of decoherence. Fair hit, but the register is inoculated: P-Q10 states this explicitly and ties the confidence bands of P-Q1/2/3/7/9 to it. The honest move is already made. What the register does *not* do is say how long "programme commitments rather than equations" can be held before the absence of a model is itself evidence against the programme — there is no stated expiry, only an open-ended "would shift if a toy model appears."

### The Hard-Nosed Physicalist (Dennett)
P-Q3 names the dilemma precisely (genuine *selection* must do something the unbiased Born distribution does not; minimal/undetectable interaction must not deviate from Born statistics) and then locates the escape in "the per-trial-vs-ensemble distinction and the indexical character of which outcome an experiencing subject actualises." Dennett's reply: this is the user-illusion move one level up. "Which outcome an experiencing subject actualises" presupposes exactly the substantive experiencing subject whose causal efficacy is in question. The indexical reading buys per-trial selection only by smuggling in a self that selects — the register notes the escape "remains pending" (P-Q3, P-Q10) but the pendingness is structural, not contingent: you cannot get per-trial efficacy out of an ensemble-preserving channel without a selector, and the selector is the thing under dispute.

### The Quantum Skeptic (Tegmark)
The register's central strategic move (P-Q1, P-Q4, P-Q5) is to demote pre-decoherence proposals (Stapp-Zeno, Orch-OR) on Tegmark's own timescale grounds and elevate "post-decoherence selection," which "acts on already-decohered branch-outcomes." Tegmark's counter: once you are post-decoherence, the branches are *classically* distinct and the outcome is fixed by the Born weights — there is nothing left to "select" without re-introducing a deviation from those weights, which P-Q2 forbids. So post-decoherence selection either does nothing (the branch is already settled) or it deviates from Born (violating P-Q2 and, via P-Q7, no-signalling). This is the same bias-without-deviation dilemma (P-Q3) re-stated at the post-decoherence stage, and the register's preferred mechanism (P-Q1) inherits it. The register does not show that moving "after decoherence" escapes the dilemma rather than merely relocating it.

### The Many-Worlds Defender (Deutsch)
P-Q1 talks of selection "acting on already-decohered branch-outcomes." Deutsch: decohered branches are precisely the *worlds* of MWI; if all of them are actualised there is no selection to perform, and if only one is, you owe an account of what happens to the others that is not just MWI-with-a-favoured-branch. The register leans on indexicality (P-Q3) and Tenet 4 (No Many Worlds) but those are framework commitments, not in-framework refutations of the branch ontology it borrows the word "branch" from. Flagging for the **direct-refutation discipline**: P-Q1/P-Q3 should not present the indexical-selection picture as if it defeated the MWI reading of "branch-outcomes" on MWI's own terms; it rests on Tenet 4.

### The Empiricist (Popper's Ghost) — the live attack
This is the strongest critique and the register half-invites it. P-Q9 says the interface is "self-concealing by construction": minimal interaction + Born-statistics preservation (P-Q2) + no aggregate signal (P-Q7) entail that the framework "predicts the pattern of evidence the framework should and should not produce: the absence of a direct neural quantum-interface signature is what the framework predicts, so its absence is not disconfirming." Popper: you have constructed a theory whose central posited entity is *guaranteed* to leave no aggregate trace, and then declared that the absence of trace confirms the design rather than disconfirming the entity. P-Q9's own "Would shift if" concedes the worry ("unless the framework-internal-coherence reading is shown to have unfalsifiability consequences the Map cannot tolerate") — but concession is not discharge. The register asserts P-Q9 "does not raise the probability that the tenets are correct," which is the right disclaimer, yet it never states the *positive* falsifiable residue. P-Q6 (Donadi falsified parameter-free DP) and P-Q5's shift conditions show the wider programme *can* be touched empirically; the register should make P-Q9 carry an explicit pointer to where the framework *does* stick its neck out (the falsification roadmap), so that self-concealment is not the last word.

### The Buddhist Philosopher (Nagarjuna)
The whole register is built on a selecting subject who "actualises" outcomes (P-Q3). Nagarjuna: the selector is empty; there is no svabhava-bearing chooser standing outside the causal stream to bias it. The per-trial/ensemble move (P-Q3) reifies an indexical "which outcome *an experiencing subject* actualises" — but the experiencing subject is itself dependently originated and cannot be the unmoved selector the mechanism needs. This is the same gap Dennett names from the opposite direction; that two opposed traditions converge on "you need a selector you have not earned" is itself evidence the gap is real, not a perspa­ectival artefact.

## Critical Issues

### Issue 1: Quantitative cross-article contradiction on the Donadi bound
- **File**: `obsidian/positions/quantum-interface.md` (P-Q6) vs `obsidian/topics/penrose-gravity-induced-collapse-empirical-prospects.md`
- **Location**: P-Q6 "establishing a lower bound on the effective mass-density nucleus size **three orders of magnitude** larger than previous bounds"; topic article "a lower bound of R₀ ≳ 0.54 × 10⁻¹⁰ m, roughly **one order of magnitude** larger than Penrose's original prediction."
- **Problem**: The two figures disagree (three vs one order of magnitude). They are reconcilable in principle — they reference *different baselines* (Donadi's "previous bounds" vs the topic article's "Penrose's original prediction R₀") — but as written, a reader moving between the two pages sees a factual contradiction with no signposting that the baselines differ. **Verification against the primary source (Donadi et al. 2021, Nature Physics): the paper's own abstract states the bound is "about three orders of magnitude larger than previous bounds."** So P-Q6 is *correct against the primary source*; the topic article is the imprecise/under-specified one.
- **Severity**: Medium (the register is right; the corpus tension is the defect)
- **Recommendation**: Leave P-Q6 as-is (it matches the source). Queue a `refine-draft` on `penrose-gravity-induced-collapse-empirical-prospects.md` to either adopt the "three orders of magnitude vs previous bounds" framing or explicitly state that its "one order of magnitude" figure is *relative to Penrose's original R₀ prediction* so the two pages stop appearing to contradict.

### Issue 2: P-Q9 self-concealment is asserted but its falsifiable residue is not stated

- **Location**: "the absence of a direct neural quantum-interface signature is what the framework predicts, so its absence is not disconfirming."

P-Q9's "Argued in" points only to the apex `self-concealing-interface`, not to `falsification-roadmap-for-the-interface-model`, even though P-Q5/P-Q6 show the programme has genuine empirical exposure.

This converts a Popper-bait sentence into a calibrated one.

### Issue 3: Post-decoherence selection inherits the bias-without-deviation dilemma unaddressed
- **File**: `obsidian/positions/quantum-interface.md` (P-Q1 with P-Q2, P-Q3)
- **Location**: P-Q1 "selection acts on already-decohered branch-outcomes rather than on coherent neural superpositions."
- **Problem**: The register sells post-decoherence selection as the dilemma-avoiding move on the *decoherence-timescale* axis (and on that axis it is a fair advantage). But it does not note that on the *bias-without-deviation* axis (P-Q3) the move buys nothing: once decohered, outcomes are fixed by Born weights, so selecting among them without deviating from those weights (P-Q2) is the very thing P-Q3 flags as unresolved. P-Q1 reads as if it escaped a problem it has only relocated.
- **Severity**: Medium
- **Recommendation**: `refine-draft` P-Q1's "Asserts" to add one sentence: post-decoherence selection answers the *timescale* objection but inherits the *bias-without-deviation* dilemma (P-Q3) unchanged, and its preferred status is on timing grounds alone.

## Counterarguments to Address

### Self-concealment as unfalsifiability (P-Q9)
- **Current content says**: The interface is self-concealing by construction; absence of an aggregate signature is predicted, hence not disconfirming.
- **A critic would argue**: A theory whose central entity is guaranteed traceless in the aggregate, and which re-reads that guaranteed tracelessness as confirmation of its architecture, has immunised itself against evidence.
- **Suggested response**: State the positive falsifiable residue inside P-Q9 (mechanism-level exposure P-Q5/P-Q6; the falsification roadmap) and confine the self-concealment claim explicitly to the aggregate-statistics channel.

### "Selection" after decoherence (P-Q1 / Tegmark / Deutsch)
- **Current content says**: Selection acts on already-decohered branch-outcomes, sidestepping the decoherence-timescale objection.
- **A critic would argue**: Post-decoherence the outcome is fixed by Born weights; there is nothing left to select without deviating from those weights (violating P-Q2), so the move either does nothing or re-opens the dilemma.
- **Suggested response**: Concede the move is a *timescale* advantage only; route the residual "what is being selected" question to P-Q3 rather than letting P-Q1 imply resolution.
