## Executive Summary

This is the Map's "delegation" cluster operating at a high level of calibration maturity.
The four articles share one elegant structural idea — that the mind-body interface can hand
off control in *both* directions (physical→experience in Saad's causal delegation;
consciousness→procedure in skill delegation) — and each cashes it out while scrupulously
refusing to let the coherence of the picture stand in for evidence. The signature move,
repeated across all four, is to concede that the identity theorist accommodates the data and
that only epiphenomenalism is genuinely constrained; the Process Philosopher and Hardline
Empiricist personas therefore **converge** rather than conflict. The new
`philosophy-of-habit-under-dualism.md` is a clean addition that fills real white space
(habit-as-such: cue-triggering, outcome-insensitivity, second nature) without duplicating the
skill cluster it sits beside. Opportunities are integration-flavoured: the cluster is dense
and near-complete, so the marginal value is a deep review of the brand-new habit article and a
few reciprocal links, not new pages.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
`consciousness-and-skill-acquisition.md` takes the explanatory gap exactly where Chalmers
would want it: it presses on *why* "neural reorganisation is accompanied by a transformation
in the felt character of the body" and why "the phenomenal quality of engagement correlates
with outcomes" — questions a functional redescription relabels rather than dissolves. It does
so without weaponising the gap: every claim is paired with the identity theorist's
accommodation, so the hard problem is honoured, not brandished.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
The flow-at-the-interface reading in `consciousness-and-skill-acquisition.md` engages the
physics honestly to a degree Stapp would respect *and* would have to concede: it invokes the
Zeno mechanism but then states the quantitative gap in the open — decoherence times of
$10^{-13}$–$10^{-20}$ s against 10–30 Hz attentional phenomenology, "twelve to sixteen orders
of magnitude" — and explicitly refuses to treat "Zeno mechanism" as if the coherence-time
requirement had been met. The architecture-level claim is separated from the
implementation debt it "owes." This is engaging the physics-consciousness interface with
its own bill attached.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
`philosophy-of-habit-under-dualism.md` centres the first-person with real texture: Ravaisson's
"double law" (receptivity diminishes as facility grows), Merleau-Ponty's "to have a habit is
to have a world" and "I can" over "I think", Bergson's habit-memory that "acts the past rather
than representing it." `consciousness-and-skill-acquisition.md`'s body-schema-vs-body-image
distinction ("A surgeon's hands have not changed shape, but their operative sense is
fundamentally different") is precisely the what-it-is-like datum that physical description
does not carry.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
`philosophy-of-habit-under-dualism.md` is the standout here, and it avoids crude substance
dualism in the Whiteheadian way: via Dewey's *transactional* habit ("the cooperation of
organism and environment") and Malafouris's Material Engagement Theory, it locates habit at
the **brain-body-thing** boundary — "habit's traces are not only neural but *material*," the
worn tool and grooved path as physical residues of past conscious engagement. This makes the
mental continuous with an ongoing material process rather than a spooky add-on. And it
handles the arch-process ally correctly: Peirce's cosmic-habit / tychism is *cited for its
form and explicitly bounded* ("The Map cites the shape of habit-taking and explicitly declines
the metaphysics"), refusing the panpsychist monism.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
`skill-delegation.md` gives agency real machinery through its Phase 3 "Retained Override
Capacity": the delegator remains a genuine source who "can slow down and consciously select
keys," demonstrated "every time an expert consciously intervenes." The habit article extends
this into moral territory via the `responsibility-gradient-from-attentional-capacity` link —
"culpability tracks whether re-engagement was available, not merely whether an action was
voluntary in the moment" — a Kane-friendly account of ownership that survives automaticity.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
`delegatory-dualism.md` shows model epistemic humility about its own load-bearing primitive:
it states plainly that the "default causal profile" is "in principle never directly observed
wherever the theory applies," that its only access route (the zombie functional-duplicate)
"depends on a contested metaphysical possibility," and that the Subset Law* is therefore "best
read as a *postulate* of the framework rather than an independently-motivated law." Naming the
primitive you cannot see, rather than smuggling it, is the disciplined form of mysterianism.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This cluster is a showcase of evidential restraint, and this persona's verdict is emphatic.

**`consciousness-and-skill-acquisition.md` refuses to inflate a supportive-evidence count.**
Its four converging neuroscience lines (corticostriatal transfer, error correction, sleep
consolidation, structural plasticity) would be tempting to bank as four confirmations; the
article instead states the discount explicitly: "the four-fold profile is one pattern
instantiated across timescales and tissue types, not four independent confirmations. The
supportive cluster carries the evidential weight of one finding, not several." It further
scopes deliberate-practice's reach honestly (Macnamara et al. 2014: 26% of variance in games,
under 1% in professions) rather than riding the Ericsson headline, and it separates
*prospective* from *retrospective* falsification conditions so that an already-passed test
(Iriki 1996) is not double-counted as still-open evidence *and* pressure on the claim.

**`delegatory-dualism.md` does the tenet-as-evidence-upgrade that it is praise-worthy to
*not* do.** It nets out that delegation's advantages are "primarily *intra-dualist*" and
"presuppose rather than support the choice of dualism… give no one a reason to adopt dualism
in the first place" — the *"tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating"* pattern executed
cleanly. It even turns its own falsifier over honestly: because Observational Closure is a
*constitutive* constraint, "a condition the theory's own defining constraint guarantees will
never be met is not a live empirical test of *this* theory" — declaring its own empirical
silence against its nearest competitors rather than glossing it.

**`skill-delegation.md`** keeps the register clean at the level of a single datum: choking is
labelled "*compatible* with a purely physical account, so it constrains and illustrates the
delegation picture rather than demonstrating dualism on its own; its evidential weight rests
on the abductive argument… not on the disruption as a brute fact."

**Process Philosopher / Hardline Empiricist convergence.** There is no minimal-organism
tier-upgrade in this batch, so the two personas' tension plays out on the adjacent axis —
mental-causation evidence vs the identity-theory reading. The articles resolve it the honest
way: the Whitehead-friendly transactional/material framing of habit is embraced *and* barred
from doing evidential work, because every article concedes the identity theorist accommodates
the findings and only epiphenomenalism is constrained. Both personas can praise, and they
praise different aspects of the same passages. No calibration concern is flagged.

## Content Strengths

### philosophy-of-habit-under-dualism.md
- **Strongest point**: The boundary-drawing section "Habit Is Not Skill." Rather than let a
  new article blur into the existing skill cluster, it states the distinction crisply — skill
  answers "how well can you?", habit answers "what will you do, unprompted, here?" — and names
  the marks unique to habit (cue-triggering, outcome-insensitivity, second nature's ethical
  ambivalence, Ravaisson's double law) that "are absent from the skill literature."
- **Notable quote**: "Habit, in short, is delegatory dualism observed at its most mundane and
  most material: the clearest everyday case of consciousness leaving a physical mark that goes
  on working when it has stepped away."
- **Why it works**: It earns its place in a dense neighbourhood by defining its own white
  space first, then anchors an abstract thesis (bidirectional interaction) in the most ordinary
  phenomenon available — driving a familiar route, reaching for a relocated light switch.

### consciousness-and-skill-acquisition.md
- **Strongest point**: The one-finding-not-four evidential discount, stated in the body *and*
  restated in the "Relation to Site Perspective" so an LLM reader truncating early still gets
  the calibration.
- **Notable quote**: "The supportive cluster carries the evidential weight of one finding, not
  several."
- **Why it works**: Its confidence is signalled by candour — it leads with the confounds
  (embodiment, the Macnamara variance scoping, the decoherence timescale gap) rather than
  burying them.

### delegatory-dualism.md
- **Strongest point**: The honest netting-out that delegation's report-tracking and
  function-tracking advantages "hold *only* against epiphenomenalism, not against physicalism."
- **Notable quote**: "It earns its keep by the coherence it buys, not by independent
  observation of the entity it quantifies over."
- **Why it works**: It presents a friendly theory (Saad's) at full strength and then states
  precisely what it does and does not buy the Map — a model of using an ally without
  over-drawing on them.

### skill-delegation.md
- **Strongest point**: The delegation-runs-both-ways table (causal delegation vs skill
  delegation), which makes the structural parallel legible at a glance and flags the crucial
  asymmetry — causal delegation's retained capacity is "Theoretical (preemption structure)"
  while skill delegation's is "Empirical (choking, override)."
- **Why it works**: It converts an elegant symmetry into a testable-vs-postulated contrast
  rather than letting the symmetry itself do rhetorical work.
