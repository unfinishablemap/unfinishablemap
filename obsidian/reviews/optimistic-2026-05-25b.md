## Executive Summary

This cohort's signature strength is that it has internalised its own anti-slippage disciplines as *named, reusable instruments* rather than one-off hedges. The agency cluster runs the Compatibilist Symmetry Challenge ("irreducible-vs-derivative, not metaphysical-vs-pragmatic") and the MWI checklist ("branch-local restatement granted; indexical/counterfactual-exclusion objection load-bearing") consistently across three articles that were edited independently — the discipline travels. The born-rule article is the most calibration-exposed document on the site and handles it with a three-tier compatible/suggestive/discriminating gradient that is, if anything, *over*-disciplined. The Process Philosopher and the Hardline Empiricist converge in praise on every passage where they could have conflicted; the one place they could pull apart — the "Unfolding" model's use of Whitehead's concrescence in `evolution-under-dualism` — is explicitly fenced as philosophically rich but empirically elusive and is *not* used to upgrade any organism's status, so no refine-draft flag is warranted. Expansion opportunities are modest because the cluster is mature; the strongest is a standalone treatment of the "ensemble-epiphenomenalism" worry the born-rule article names as the Map's sharpest open question.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
`free-will` keeps the explanatory gap doing real work rather than decorative work: the case for agent causation rests on "systematic covariation across domains" (attention, motor control, reasoning, creativity) being "better explained by genuine agent involvement than by any account treating the phenomenology as illusory or idle." That is the hard problem deployed as a *constraint on theory choice*, not merely asserted. Chalmers would also recognise his own collaboration honoured precisely in `born-rule`: the Chalmers-McQueen Φ-collapse proposal is placed in the "minimum-outside-the-corridor" family with the sharpening that "no single scalar Φ suffices" — the article cites the 2022 paper's own self-criticism rather than overselling it.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
Stapp's quantum Zeno appears in three of these articles, and each time it is given its due *and* its limits. `moral-responsibility` and `free-will` both state the philosophical case "does not depend on this particular mechanism" while still treating it as a live candidate; `born-rule` locates Stapp-Zeno precisely in the Born-rule-bending family ("shifting selection probabilities away from non-conscious measurement outcomes"). Stapp would approve that the interface is given a real physical home without being smuggled in as established — and that the corridor reading explicitly preserves no-signalling, which is the relativistic-causality constraint his own program has to respect.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
`free-will`'s introspective-reliability passage is the standout. It answers Schwitzgebel and Wegner not by claiming fine-grained introspective accuracy but by resting the case on a "coarse-grained, first-order distinction — choosing feels different from observing" that is "behaviourally consequential and cross-domain stable." This is Nagel's first-person datum defended at exactly the resolution it can bear, with the four-fold dissociation argument ("not one trick but four independent tricks") giving the felt structure third-person traction without reducing it.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
`evolution-under-dualism` gives Whitehead a genuine seat: the "Unfolding" model builds on concrescence — "each moment of consciousness-matter interaction synthesises what came before into something new" — and `reasons-responsiveness` links out to process philosophy as supporting "experiential self-determination." Crucially, the Unfolding model is presented as one of three co-equal models the Map *does not choose between*, and is flagged "philosophically rich but empirically elusive." Whitehead-friendliness is honoured at its own level and is not used as an engine of anything — see the Hardline Empiricist note below, with which this verdict converges.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
This is Kane's home cohort and it serves him well. `moral-responsibility` cites his sourcehood requirement verbatim ("ultimate creators or originators and sustainers") and `reasons-responsiveness` supplies the content Kane's pure agent-causation can lack: the luck objection is answered not by "the agent caused it" alone but by "the agent was *responsive to reasons*," with the contrastive-explanation table (lucky indeterminism vs reasons-responsive selection) making the distinction concrete. Kane would value that the framework takes the luck objection as the strongest challenge and answers it on its merits rather than dissolving it.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
`free-will` is McGinn-disciplined about the limits of the method itself: the [[agency-void]] passage concedes that "interactionism faces a structurally parallel limit — consciousness cannot verify its own causal power from inside, since every check is performed by the very faculty under investigation," and concludes the case "rests on coherence with phenomenology and neural data, not on an introspective proof of efficacy that no framework could deliver." `born-rule` adds the structural-mystery register: the [[causal-interface]] and the admission that the corridor reading "sits structurally outside the experimentally-discriminable regime by construction." McGinn would recognise honest cognitive-closure talk that does not collapse into either triumph or defeat.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This persona's verdict is load-bearing for this cohort and it is strongly positive — with one item to watch rather than flag.

`born-rule` is the most disciplined document of the five and arguably on the site. Its *Compatibility vs. Support* section is a canonical statement of the very distinction Birch cares about: "compatible with" (does not conflict) versus "supports over rivals" (names a feature rivals do not equally predict), operationalised as a three-tier gradient (interface-compatible / interface-suggestive / interface-discriminating). The article then *rates itself honestly on its own scale*: "interface-compatible, marginally interface-suggestive on components, and not yet interface-discriminating." It repeatedly refuses to let coherence-with-many-frameworks convert into empirical weight ("Coherence-with-many-frameworks raises framework-internal weight without raising the empirical tier"), and the closing paragraph pre-empts the length-as-evidence misreading directly ("a reader who takes the catalogue's length as evidential weight reads it against its own discipline"). Most importantly, it *owns the cost* of its preferred reading: "the corridor … sits structurally outside the experimentally-discriminable regime by construction … That is the honest cost, not a strength." This is tenet-coherent-not-evidence-elevating executed to the letter.

The agency cluster does the praise-worthy thing of *not* upgrading the libertarian framing's moral standing. All three of `moral-responsibility`, `reasons-responsiveness`, and `free-will` independently run the Compatibilist Symmetry Challenge and reach the same honest verdict: sophisticated reasons-responsive determinism (Frankfurt, Fischer-Ravizza, Wolf) "plausibly secures the same deliberative phenomenology, praise/blame practices, character-formation arc, and moral seriousness," so the libertarian framing's distinguishing work is "tenet-coherence … not unique moral explanatory power." The contrast register is explicitly corrected from *metaphysical-vs-pragmatic* to *irreducible-vs-derivative* — a refusal to score a rhetorical point the older framing would have allowed. The MWI checklist is applied with the same restraint: the indexical/counterfactual-exclusion objection is load-bearing; "MWI would fail the phenomenology" and "no collapse to influence" are explicitly demoted to weaker, non-decisive registers, and `moral-responsibility` even states "the Map's objection is thus indexical … rather than a claim that Many-Worlds fails the phenomenology, which it does not."

**Item to watch (not a flag): the Whitehead axis in `evolution-under-dualism`.** This is the cohort's only place where the Process Philosopher's praise could in principle cash out as a tier-upgrade. It does not. The article's three models (fixed-domain, co-evolution, unfolding) are held as co-equal and uncommitted; the nematode-302-neuron example is handled at "perhaps capturing only a narrow band … or perhaps failing to interface at all" — no minimal organism is slid up the evidential scale on process-philosophical resonance. The psychophysical-law / zombie-impossibility prediction is explicitly marked "conditional rather than established." The one place to monitor on future edits: the "convergent evolution favours fixed-domain" and "octopus consciousness differs in kind" passages are stated as *predictions the models make*, not as findings — they currently read correctly, but they are the sentences most likely to drift toward "the interface hypothesis predicts X, therefore X is supported" under a careless future refine. No action now; noting the surface for the literature-drift / refine pipeline.

Verdict: Process Philosopher and Hardline Empiricist converge in praise on every load-bearing passage. No possibility/probability slippage detected; no refine-draft flag warranted.

## Content Strengths

### born-rule-and-the-consciousness-interface.md
- **Strongest point**: The self-rating on its own three-tier evidential gradient — "interface-compatible, marginally interface-suggestive on components, and not yet interface-discriminating" — combined with naming the *single* concrete suggestive-tier payoff (the substrate's how-sketch for the SIC-geometric (d+1)/−1/d form) and refusing to inflate it past the suggestive tier.
- **Notable quote**: "the Map's ability to 'survive a clean negative sweep' is purchased entirely by the unfalsifiability of whichever route it retreats to. That is the honest cost, not a strength."
- **Why it works**: It converts the abstract compatibility-vs-support rule into a falsification ledger with a visible liability column, so the discipline is demonstrated against the article's own preferred reading rather than only against rivals.

### free-will.md
- **Strongest point**: The "four independent tricks" reformulation of the introspective-reliability defence — dismissing volitional phenomenology as illusion requires fabricating initiation, sustained control, effort gradients, *and* veto, each with distinct neural substrates — which turns a vulnerability (Schwitzgebel/Wegner) into a structured argument with a cost the eliminativist must pay.
- **Notable quote**: "Dismissing volitional phenomenology as illusion requires not one trick but four independent tricks … each calibrated to real cognitive demands with distinct neural substrates."
- **Why it works**: It locates the dispute at the resolution the phenomenology can actually bear (coarse-grained, behaviourally consequential, cross-domain) rather than overclaiming fine introspective accuracy.

### reasons-responsiveness.md
- **Strongest point**: Naming Fischer-Ravizza "moderate reasons-responsiveness" as "the principal in-cluster move the discipline guards against absorbing" and then conceding the symmetry honestly — the libertarian reply is tenet-coherence, not unique explanatory power within the deliberative arc.
- **Notable quote**: "The contrast with the Map's reading is therefore *irreducible-vs-derivative* … rather than *metaphysical-vs-pragmatic*."
- **Why it works**: It identifies the strongest version of the opposing view by name and grants it everything it can plausibly claim, leaving the Map's residual commitment precisely scoped.

### moral-responsibility.md
- **Strongest point**: The rewritten "What Would Challenge This View?" item 4, which states outright that the weak empirical version of the MWI objection "is the wrong place to rest the case" and relocates the challenge to "the indexical objection failing."
- **Notable quote**: "Desert presupposes that the unchosen alternatives genuinely did not happen."
- **Why it works**: It makes a falsification condition that is *internal to the Map's own commitments* (indexical identity) rather than hostage to an empirical result (MWI confirmation) that the article elsewhere argues could not cleanly arrive.

### evolution-under-dualism.md
- **Strongest point**: The three-models framework (fixed-domain / co-evolution / unfolding) presented as co-equal and uncommitted, each with a stated empirical difficulty and a divergence point that "comparative neuroscience may eventually distinguish."
- **Notable quote**: "The Map does not commit to a single model — each captures something important about how dualism relates to evolutionary biology."
- **Why it works**: It resists the temptation to pick the most tenet-flattering model and instead exposes all three to falsification conditions, which is what keeps the Whitehead-friendly "unfolding" reading from becoming load-bearing.
