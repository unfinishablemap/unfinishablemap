### Counterarguments Considered
- **Eliminative Materialist**: Mind-space is an artifact of folk psychology; there are no "minds" to locate, only brain processes. The article handles this via the dualism tenet connection — under the Map's commitments, mind-space is real and irreducible.
- **Functionalist**: Mind-space *can* be surveyed functionally. The morphospace approach isn't "incomplete" — it's the right framework. The article acknowledges the functionalist approach is "valuable as a first step" while explaining why dualism implies additional non-physical dimensions.
- **Many-Worlds Defender**: The claim that MWI dissolves the self-location problem is debatable — even in MWI, one asks "which branch am I on?" However, the article's formulation is reasonable: under MWI, the question of mind-space location becomes indexically trivial since all positions are simultaneously occupied.
