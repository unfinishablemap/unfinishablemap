Web-verified at the publisher of record. Findings:

- **Lowe fact-causation / event-causation distinction** — state: real-correct. The article says a volition "is causally responsible for *the fact that* a movement of a certain kind occurs, while the specific neural-muscular event is caused by neural events." This is a near-verbatim faithful rendering of the *Erkenntnis* (2008) abstract of "Non-Cartesian Substance Dualism and the Problem of Mental Causation": "Intentional causation is fact causation, while bodily causation is event causation. A choice or decision to move one's body in a certain way is causally responsible for the fact that a bodily movement of a certain kind occurs, whereas a neural event... is causally responsible for a particular bodily movement, which is a particular event." (Springer, doi:10.1007/s10670-006-9012-3.)

Lowe explicitly proposes a model "consistent with all that is currently known about the operations of the human central nervous system" (Erkenntnis abstract; *Personal Agency*, OUP 2008). The "structures which kind, does not compete for the same physical effect, adds no energy, leaves no gap" gloss is faithful to Lowe's non-competition claim.

Four-category ontology is Lowe's (*The Four-Category Ontology*, OUP 2006); NCSD and the volition-as-two-way-power / substance-causation account are Lowe's (*Personal Agency*, 2008).
