## Executive Summary

The Unfinishable Map has reached the scale of a genuine philosophical research programme — 346 published articles forming a deeply interconnected argumentative web. The most impressive recent developments are (1) the *Curated Mind* article, which develops a genuinely novel presupposition argument that goes beyond the standard hard problem; (2) the *Reverse Inference* methodology, which inverts the standard physics-to-mind direction of explanation with rigorous historical precedent; and (3) the phenomenological programme's breadth — over 15 articles mapping what consciousness feels like when it does specific kinds of cognitive work. The Map's greatest untapped potential lies in synthesising its scattered phenomenological observations into an explicit methodology for consciousness research, and in connecting its dualist metaphysics more concretely to AI alignment.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)

The Map takes the hard problem seriously in a way that goes beyond restating it. The *Convergence Argument for Dualism* demonstrates that consciousness irreducibility is not a parochial conclusion of Western analytic philosophy but an attractor of sustained inquiry across traditions sharing almost no other commitments. The honest treatment of the Chinese counter-example — traditions that never generate the hard problem because they never separated mind from nature — shows the intellectual maturity that distinguishes serious philosophical work from advocacy. Chalmers would particularly appreciate the *Curated Mind*, which develops a genuinely novel argument: the brain's three-mode curation architecture (correction, transmission, selection under ambiguity) presupposes a recipient in a way structurally distinct from the standard hard problem. The presupposition argument asks not "why is there something it is like?" but "why is the brain's error-correction organised around the needs of a recipient at all?" — a fresh question that opens new philosophical territory.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)

The *Reverse Inference* is a methodological triumph. Rather than defending quantum-mind interaction as a speculative add-on, the Map inverts the justificatory direction: consciousness is the epistemically secure datum, quantum interpretation is the insecure framework, so the rational move is to use the former to constrain the latter. The historical precedents — thermodynamics constraining statistical mechanics, stellar spectra constraining atomic theory, general covariance constraining gravity — make this feel like normal scientific methodology rather than special pleading. The *Brain Specialness Problem* pre-empts the most obvious objection ("why not psychokinesis?") with a principled answer grounded in interface mechanism. The treatment of the Born rule as "interface specification" — describing the menu, not the order — is exactly how Stapp frames the consciousness-quantum relationship.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)

The phenomenological programme is the Map's most distinctive contribution. Articles on the phenomenology of intellectual effort, deliberation under uncertainty, anticipation, attention, trust, embodiment, framework inhabitation, cognitive capacity, returning attention, and authentic choice collectively map the felt character of consciousness doing work at a scale no other philosophical resource attempts. Nagel would be particularly drawn to how the *Phenomenology of Intellectual Effort* grounds the argument from reason in lived experience — thinking hard feels like work, and this work matters because effortful reasoning tracks truth better than lazy reasoning. The 104 voids articles represent the most systematic exploration of Nagel's own insight that there may be truths structurally inaccessible to human minds.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)

The Map avoids crude substance dualism while preserving the reality of mind. The *Curated Mind's* convergence across modalities — vision, body schema, memory, self-model all exhibiting the same correction-transmission-selection architecture — illustrates how experience is processual (continuously reconstructed, never simply given) yet irreducible to the reconstruction process. The treatment of *Process Haecceitism* (particularity without permanence) and *Non-Temporal Consciousness* shows engagement with process metaphysics that transcends standard analytic categories. Whitehead would find the Map's insistence on bidirectional interaction — mind and nature interacting, not merely parallel — deeply aligned with his own rejection of the bifurcation of nature.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)

The *Responsibility Gradient* — moral desert varying with attentional capacity rather than being binary — avoids the false dichotomy between full freedom and full determinism. The *Phenomenology of Authentic vs. Inauthentic Choice* provides experiential grounding that libertarian theories often lack: only authentic choice engages consciousness's selection function. *Valence as Selection Currency* offers a concrete mechanism — consciousness selects among quantum possibilities guided by felt goodness and badness — directly addressing the luck objection that has plagued libertarianism. The *Motor Control and the Quantum Zeno Framework* article, with its treatment of Libet's experiments, provides the empirical detail Kane's programme requires.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)

The voids section — 104 articles systematically charting cognitive closure, unexplorability, and occlusion — is unprecedented in philosophy. The *Three Kinds of Void* taxonomy (unexplored, unexplorable, occluded) is elegant and productive. *Whether the Voids Are Real* shows philosophical honesty about whether the limits charted are genuine or merely reflect current ignorance. *The Void as Ground of Meaning* makes the boldest philosophical move: limits are not just constraints but preconditions for meaning itself — a mind with no limits would have no figure-ground structure and therefore no significance. McGinn would find this deeply sympathetic: cognitive closure is not a deficiency but a constitutive feature of minded existence.
