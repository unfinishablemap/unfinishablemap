## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The content cluster takes the explanatory gap seriously at the level of *content*, not only *qualia*. `hard-problem-of-content.md` grants Hutto and Myin their dilemma against the strongest naturalistic semantics (Dretske's covariation, Millikan's teleosemantics) — "correlation is not aboutness" — and treats it as a structural ally to the explanatory-gap reasoning while declining the positive Radical Enactive Cognition programme built atop it. Chalmers would recognise the discipline of taking an ally's argument only as far as it actually reaches.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)

(Per evidential-status discipline: praised as compatibility and depth, not as licence to elevate any organism on the five-tier scale — see the Hardline Empiricist, where the two personas converge.)

The content cluster models the discipline this persona cares about: `hard-problem-of-content.md` keeps the *negative* result (no naturalistic theory delivers saying-that from covariation) cleanly separate from the *positive* REC programme it declines to endorse — register-keeping between "this defeats a rival" and "this establishes our alternative." ### High Priority

#### The Chinese Room argument (John Searle, 1980)
- **Builds on**: `symbol-grounding-problem`, `functionalism`, `biological-computationalism`, `llm-consciousness`, `intentionality`, `language-recursion-and-consciousness`.
- **Would address**: Searle's Chinese Room is one of the most-cited arguments in philosophy of mind and bears directly on the Map's heavy AI/LLM engagement, yet it has no dedicated page — it appears as scattered background across ~17 articles (symbol-grounding 6, ai-consciousness 5, functionalism 4). A dedicated page would give the Map a canonical home for the syntax-vs-semantics thesis and the standard replies (systems, robot, brain-simulator, other-minds, combination) rather than re-deriving fragments inline.
- **Tenet alignment**: Directly serves Tenet 1 (computation insufficient for understanding) and anchors the LLM-consciousness cluster's "Chinese Rooms at scale" framing.
- **Note for the pipeline**: distinct from the queued **biological-naturalism** research note. The Chinese Room is Searle's *negative* anti-strong-AI argument; biological naturalism is his *positive* theory of mind. They should be two pages, cross-referencing each other, not one.

#### Teleosemantics / biosemantics (Millikan, Papineau, Dretske)
- **Builds on**: `hard-problem-of-content`, `the-naturalisation-failure-for-content`, `intentionality`, `status-of-content`, `content-specificity-of-mental-causation`, `symbol-grounding-problem`.
- **Would address**: The Map has a rich content cluster (hard-problem-of-content, naturalisation-failure-for-content, status-of-content, process-content-distinction, content-vocabulary-as-derived-feature) — but every one engages teleosemantics only as the *target* of critique. The leading positive naturalistic theory of mental content that the whole cluster argues against has no steelman page. This is the exact "rival referenced repeatedly, owned by none" pattern the prior review used for first-order representationalism.
- **Tenet alignment**: A dedicated steelman lets the Map state precisely why proper-function semantics cannot cross from covariation-plus-selection-history to genuine aboutness (Tenet 1), giving the content-cluster critiques a canonical rival to point at.

### Medium Priority

#### Emergent dualism (William Hasker, *The Emergent Self*)
- **Builds on**: `dualism`, `interactionist-dualism`, `substance-property-dualism`, `agent-causation`, `pairing-problem`, `objections-to-interactionism`, `four-quadrant-dualism-taxonomy`.
- **Would address**: Hasker is the single most-cited dualist author in the corpus without a home page — he appears as a supporting citation across at least seven dualism-family articles (the unity argument, the pairing-problem response, agent causation, spatial location of minds) but his distinctive position — that the conscious self *emerges from and depends on* the brain yet is a genuinely new substance/subject — is never given focused treatment. `agent-causation.md` literally says "Hasker combines agent causation with emergent dualism" with no page to link. Locating the Map's interactionist substance dualism relative to emergent dualism would sharpen its self-positioning (does the Map's mind depend on the brain for its existence, or only for its interaction?).
- **Tenet alignment**: Clarifies Tenets 1 and 3 by contrasting emergentist dependence against the Map's interface commitments; a natural companion to four-quadrant-dualism-taxonomy.
