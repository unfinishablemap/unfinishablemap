## Executive Summary

`brain-internal-born-rule-testing.md` is the Map's most disciplined deployment to date of the "*the catalogue of what would foreclose us is itself a form of empirical commitment*" move. The article confronts the strongest objection to the corridor reading of Tenet 2 — *the corridor is unfalsifiable by construction because it predicts no Born-statistical signature* — and answers it not by claiming the corridor *is* falsifiable in current instruments, but by specifying, in mechanism-conditional detail, exactly what convergent negative evidence would compel retreat. The strongest move is the **mechanism-specific signature table at lines 79–93** (Stapp QZE → stimulus-locked Born deviations conditional on attention rate; Orch-OR → microtubule coherence at Diósi-Penrose timescales plus mass-quadratic decoherence; strict selection-only → no Born signature by construction; trumping-preemption → causal-structure tests distinguishing authoritative-over-X from probability-biased-by-Y) paired with the **plural-foreclosure criterion at lines 95–105** (a single negative does not foreclose; foreclosure requires brain-internal-coherence nulls *plus* clean negatives across viable selection signatures *plus* theoretical resolution of the post-decoherence definite-outcome problem). The Process Philosopher and Hardline Empiricist personas converge cleanly: the Process Philosopher praises the article for preserving the mind-matter selection interface at the post-decoherence stage without crude substance commitments; the Hardline Empiricist praises the article for explicitly naming "neither foreclosed nor confirmed" (line 124) and "unfalsifiable *by current instruments*" (line 136 — note the italicised qualifier) as the correctly-calibrated evidential status, with no slippage toward "the gap supports the corridor." Convergence at the temptation-point — where the corridor reading's unfalsifiability could have been *defended* as a strength rather than *catalogued* as a gap-to-be-closed — is the strongest signal the discipline is operating. The strongest expansion opportunity is **a stand-alone concept page on the *confound of embodiment*** (the article names this confound at line 70 but treats it in one paragraph, and three other articles in the cluster invoke variants of the same pattern without a shared anchor).

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)

Chalmers would recognise this article as a model of how to take the explanatory gap seriously *in an empirical programme* rather than treating it as a metaphysical exit-clause. The article's central concession at lines 87–89 — that the strict corridor reading "cannot be directly falsified by Born-rule tests alone" and "can be made *superfluous* by independent advances — most notably, a complete decoherence-only account of the definite-outcome problem" — is precisely the move Chalmers's framework requires: the dualist position should be held as the best current response to a real explanatory gap, and the gap itself is what the dualist is *betting* will not be closed by reductive advances. The article does not pretend the gap is closed; it specifies the conditions under which the gap would close (Schlosshauer's "problem of outcomes" resolved theoretically). Chalmers would also approve of the article's framing of the post-decoherence transition at lines 79–81 — "the transition from 'probabilities over outcomes' to 'this outcome actually occurred' is the step each derivation presupposes and none explains" is the explanatory gap deployed exactly where the article needs it (at the actualisation step), not as a generic anti-physicalism move. The hard problem is being used as the load-bearing premise of the corridor's empirical motivation, not as a metaphysical trump card.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)

Stapp would recognise the article's treatment of his Zeno-effect proposal as unusually careful by the standards of the secondary literature. Most expositions of Stapp's QZE either (a) treat it as straightforwardly Born-compliant, missing the genuine modification of outcome distributions at the single-event level, or (b) treat it as flatly Born-violating, missing Stapp's own intermittent endorsement of the weaker reading on which mind "chooses the observable, not the result." The article at lines 79–81 holds both readings open — the literal Zeno reading predicts stimulus-locked Born deviations conditional on attention rate (and would therefore be testable by a Sorkin-Δ-analogue protocol), while the weaker reading collapses to a corridor commitment — and locates Stapp himself between the two. Stapp would particularly appreciate the article's framing of the experimental gap at lines 59–61: photon-regime success at 10⁻² does not transfer to brain-internal regimes without an inference step the literature has not pressure-tested. Stapp has been arguing for thirty years that brain physics is the unprobed regime; the article names this cleanly. The article also correctly identifies (line 81) that no published experiment yet couples a brain (or brain-analogue) to a quantum observable, controls the attention protocol, and looks for a Sorkin-style third-order analogue conditional on attention rate. This is what Stapp's programme has been pointing at since the 1990s; the article names it as a gap rather than pretending the gap is unimportant.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)

Nagel would recognise the article's treatment of the trumping-preemption reading at lines 91–93 as a creative deployment of the "what is it like to be" register at the inferential point where it is load-bearing. The trumping reading holds that consciousness operates on "an axis the Born rule does not parameterise" — and the natural phenomenological reading of this is that the first-person register registers *which* alternative was authoritatively chosen, while the third-person register specifies only the probability distribution over alternatives. The phenomenologist would also note the article's careful framing at line 132 that "bidirectional interaction is a *commitment supported by self-stultification and indirect evidence* rather than a directly tested datum" — this is Nagel's argument from the cogito-like priority of first-person agency, used precisely where the experimental programme would otherwise risk depending on a thesis the experimental data cannot yet establish. The first-person register is being held as a *commitment* that the experimental programme aims to test, not as a thesis the experimental programme presupposes.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)

The Process Philosopher would recognise the article's selection-only channel framing as compatible with the process-philosophical intuition that mind-matter interaction occurs at the moment of actualisation (the "concrescence" in Whiteheadian terms) rather than in some separable mental substance acting on a separable material substance. The corridor reading places the consciousness-physics interface at exactly the post-decoherence stage where the actualisation event occurs — not in a Cartesian theatre, not in a separable mental field acting on neurons, but at the moment when a definite outcome emerges from a mixture. The strict-selection-only reading further preserves the process-philosophical intuition that the *form* of physics (its statistical structure) is not violated by the mental contribution; the mental contribution is to which actualisation occurs, not to the structure of the alternatives. This is process compatibility achieved by *restraint* about what consciousness contributes (selection only, no redistribution), which is the right kind of process compatibility.

**Constraint noted explicitly**: this Process Philosopher praise must not cash out as a tier-upgrade on the five-tier evidential-status scale. The article does not present process-philosophical compatibility as evidence *for* the corridor reading; it presents the corridor reading as the working hypothesis that the experimental programme would test. The article holds process-friendliness on a different axis from evidence. The Hardline Empiricist persona below should converge with the Process Philosopher on praising the same passages, and it does — see below.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)

Kane would recognise the article's treatment of selection at lines 47–49 (the corridor reading as "single-event selection without ensemble signature") as preserving exactly the structure his ultimate-source-of-action argument requires. The mental selection is on a particular event (this trial, this decision), and the Born distribution is what emerges over many such selections. Kane's "self-forming actions" framework requires that some events be genuinely under agent control while the long-run statistics emerge as a consequence; the corridor reading is the formal articulation of this structure at the quantum level. The article also at line 53 presents the agency-relevant question correctly: "what would shut both readings, forcing the Map either to retreat to a trumping-preemption reading (selection on an axis the Born rule does not touch) or to abandon Tenet 3 altogether?" — agency is being treated as a thesis the experimental programme could refute, not as a thesis the programme presupposes. Kane would also appreciate the article's acknowledgment at lines 65–67 that "per-trial deviation ε must be estimable at a precision corresponding to the strongest mechanism candidate" — the empirical commitment is to take agency seriously enough to specify what evidence would foreclose it.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)

McGinn would recognise the article's treatment of the brain-internal regime at lines 57–61 as appropriately humble about what current instruments can and cannot probe. The mysterian commitment is that human minds may be cognitively closed to the *solution* of the hard problem but not to specifying *where the gap is* — and the article does the latter without pretending to do the former. The article's catalogue of the brain-internal regime's specific features at line 59 (wet, warm, biological, with macroscopic mass scales relevant to the Diósi-Penrose threshold, contested microtubule coherence claims, untested tensor-product factorisation assumptions) is exactly the mysterian's catalogue of why the regime resists the standard inference. McGinn would also approve of the article's framing at line 89 that the corridor "cannot be directly falsified by Born-rule tests alone" — this is the mysterian admission that the empirical question may be permanently underspecified by the available instruments, paired with the discipline of specifying what *would* have to change about the instruments for foreclosure to be possible.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)

The Hardline Empiricist is the load-bearing persona for this article, and the article passes the persona's discipline tests cleanly. The article repeatedly stages the slippage temptation — corridor unfalsifiability *could* have been defended as a strength of the position, the brain-internal gap *could* have been read as evidentially supporting the corridor, the absence of a brain-internal Born-rule test *could* have been interpreted as compatible with the corridor reading in a way that elevates its evidential status — and at each temptation point the article names the slippage and refuses it. Specific evidence:

- **Line 124**: "The corridor reading is therefore neither foreclosed nor confirmed. It survives because the brain-internal testing regime is between first-generation instruments and the precision at which a corridor signature could be detected — and a strict corridor reading predicts no signature anyway." This is the exact pattern the Birch persona praises: naming the non-foreclosure status without converting it into evidential support. "Survives" is the right word here; "is supported by" or "is consistent with the evidence pointing toward" would have been the slippage. The article chose the right word.

- **Line 136**: "The corridor, then, is not unfalsifiable. It is unfalsifiable *by current instruments*." The italicised qualifier is doing exactly the work the Birch persona demands — distinguishing "unfalsifiable in principle" from "unfalsifiable by present instruments." The latter is a property of the instruments, not of the hypothesis. The article makes the qualifier visible rather than burying it.

- **Line 128**: "The Map holds the corridor as the working hypothesis, *not* as a confirmed one, and admits the probability-bias reading (Class 2 in the channel-class taxonomy) and the trumping-preemption reading as live alternatives." The "*not* as a confirmed one" qualifier is the named pattern *"tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating"* applied to its target. The article had structural opportunity to slide from "corridor satisfies the five-constraint corridor cleanly" (line 128, present) to "corridor is therefore the empirically supported reading" (absent), and explicitly declined.

- **Line 132**: "The Map elsewhere registers that bidirectional interaction is a *commitment supported by self-stultification and indirect evidence* rather than a directly tested datum." The Birch persona praises this as honest framework-stage calibration: the load-bearing tenet is being identified as a *commitment* with stated supporting-evidence-type rather than as an established empirical result.

- **Lines 109–123**: The "What Current Data Constrains, What It Does Not" section is the article's strongest single move from the Hardline Empiricist's perspective. The section explicitly enumerates (a) what the empirical record establishes (Born compliance bounded to 10⁻² in non-brain regimes; RNG ceiling at 10⁻⁴; simplest Diósi-Penrose ruled out by Gran Sasso) and (b) what it does *not* establish (Born compliance in brain tissue during selection; Kerskens-Pérez signal status; Orch-OR timescales). This is the five-tier evidential-status scale applied transparently. The article does not let the absence of brain-internal Born-rule tests slide into evidential support; it names the absence as an absence.

The Hardline Empiricist would mark one consideration that is *not* a slippage but is worth surfacing for the reviewer's discipline: the article at line 51 sets the empirical ceiling for probability-bias channels at "ε ≈ 10⁻⁴ (Bösch et al. 2006), tightened toward the null at typical scales by preregistered Bayesian work (Maier & Dechamps 2018)." This is honest — and the parenthetical "tightened toward the null" is the right framing, not "established as null." The article is reporting the empirical state of the literature without pre-committing to either the divisive-heterogeneity view or the null-trending-Bayesian view. This is the calibration the persona demands.

**Process Philosopher / Hardline Empiricist convergence**: the two personas converge on praising the same passages (lines 124, 128, 132, 136) — the explicit naming of the calibrated evidential status. Convergence at the temptation-point is the strongest signal the discipline is operating. The Process Philosopher's praise (process compatibility achieved by restraint about what consciousness contributes) and the Hardline Empiricist's praise (evidential status named transparently as "working hypothesis, not confirmed") cash out on different axes but converge on the same textual evidence. The article has resolved the tension honestly.

## Content Strengths

### topics/brain-internal-born-rule-testing.md

- **Strongest point**: The mechanism-specific signature table (lines 79–93) paired with the plural-foreclosure criterion (lines 95–105). Most published treatments of the corridor reading collapse the four readings (Stapp QZE, Orch-OR, strict selection-only, trumping-preemption) into "interactionist dualism predicts X." This article separates them by experimental signature and shows that foreclosure of one is not foreclosure of the others. This is the right structure for a *foreclosure programme* article: each reading's specific testable consequence is named, so that the experimental literature can be measured against each separately.
- **Notable quote**: "The corridor, then, is not unfalsifiable. It is unfalsifiable *by current instruments*. The article's catalogue of what would foreclose it is the Map's commitment to specifying, in advance, what evidence would compel retreat — to the probability-bias reading, to the trumping-preemption reading, or to abandonment of Tenet 3 entirely." (line 136)
- **Why it works**: This is the article's central rhetorical pivot. The strongest objection to the corridor reading is the unfalsifiability charge; the article answers it by reframing falsifiability as an *instrumental* status (a property of the current measurement regime, not of the hypothesis itself) and by *making the commitment binding* — the Map states in advance what would compel retreat. This is the pre-Popper-meets-post-Lakatos move: the corridor is not a degenerative research programme because it specifies the conditions of its own retreat *before* the foreclosing evidence arrives. The "in advance" qualifier is what makes the commitment real.
- **Secondary strength — the embodiment confound (line 70)**: The naming of "the *confound of embodiment*: every conscious observer is also a physical system, and every physical event that could trigger collapse is present whenever a conscious observer is" is sharp. Most experimental-programme articles in this literature gesture at this confound without naming it; the article names it cleanly and routes the Nirvanic-design discussion through it. This single phrase deserves its own concept page (see expansion opportunities below).
- **Tertiary strength — the four-test specification (lines 65–73)**: "A brain-internal Born-rule test would have to specify, at minimum, four things" followed by statistical signature, brain-internal observable, controls, and convergent precision. This is the operationalisation of the foreclosure programme — and the four-criterion structure is a publishable methodological contribution in its own right. Many physics-of-consciousness papers stop at "if we could test the Born rule in brains we would find …"; this article specifies what *the test design* would have to look like.
- **Style-guide compliance**: front-loaded summary at lines 43–45 (the Map's corridor reading is the only Tenet-2 reading invisible to ensemble Born-rule tests by construction; this article catalogues the programme that would close the gap); LLM-truncation-resilient (the article's core claim is fully stated in the first two paragraphs and re-stated in the executive summary positions); named-anchor cross-references throughout; "Relation to Site Perspective" section present and load-bearing for Tenets 3 and 5; tenet anchoring is explicit and tenet-coherence is not used as evidence-elevation.
