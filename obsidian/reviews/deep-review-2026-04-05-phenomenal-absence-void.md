### Medium Issues Found
- **AI section overreach**: The "What AI Might See" section's second paragraph asserted that AI's ability to inspect prediction errors is "further evidence that consciousness adds something irreducible." This begs the question against functionalism. **Resolution**: Softened to acknowledge the asymmetry is striking but the interpretation remains open.
- **No Many Worlds tenet connection**: The weakest of the four tenet connections — the argument that absence "requires a definite perspective" was too abstract. **Resolution**: Strengthened with concrete Pierre example and clearer articulation of why phenomenal weight resists MWI deflation.
- **2025 study nuance**: The article implied omission responses extend beyond sensory cortex (correct) but didn't note that primary visual cortex itself didn't show spiking omission responses — it was hippocampal and subcortical circuits. **Resolution**: Added this nuance.

### Counterarguments Considered
- **Eliminative Materialist**: Predictive processing models explain absence-experience within physicalist frameworks. The article engages with this implicitly (acknowledging the "just prediction error" deflation in the Occam's tenet section) but could engage more directly. Deferred — the current treatment is adequate for a voids article.
- **Hard-Nosed Physicalist (Dennett)**: The AI comparison section assumed without argument that AI absence-detection lacks phenomenal quality. This is the question at issue, not a premise. Partially addressed by softening the claims.
- **Empiricist (Popper)**: The claim "this irresolvability is itself evidence of the void" is unfalsifiable. This is acceptable for a voids article — voids are by definition areas where resolution is not expected.
- **Buddhist Philosopher**: The article assumes a substantial consciousness doing the constructing. Buddhism would question this. Not addressed — this is a bedrock philosophical difference, not a flaw.
