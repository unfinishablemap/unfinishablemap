## Critical Issues

### Issue 1: "Logical defect" framing contradicts the corpus's settled self-stultification position (epistemic→logical equivocation)

- **File**: `concepts/delegatory-causation.md`
- **Location**: "The Epiphenomenalism Parallel" subsection, lines 132 and 134; echoed in "Honest Limitations" line 180.
- **Severity**: Medium (load-bearing on the article's central differentiator, but a localised wording fix — the substance is salvageable in place)
- **Problem**: The article's case for preferring delegation over epiphenomenalism rests on the asymmetry being "a constraint of a *different kind*" (line 130) than mere theoretical taste. To carry that weight, the article escalates the self-stultification argument into a **logical** register:
  - L132: "This is not merely inelegant; **it is self-undermining. A theory that severs the link between its subject matter and the evidence for believing in it has a logical defect, not just an aesthetic disadvantage.**"
  - L134: "...more like **rejecting a theory that defeats its own evidence base—closer to a consistency requirement than a taste preference.**"
  - L180: "it avoids a **specific logical defect** that epiphenomenalism cannot..."

  But the corpus's own canonical pages classify this exact argument as the **opposite** of a logical defect:
  - `self-stultification.md` L65: "**Logical contradiction.** ... Self-stultification is *weaker*: the position *can* be true. What it cannot be is *rationally held*." L171: "Self-stultification doesn't prove a position false ... This is a *weaker* conclusion than falsification."
  - `epiphenomenalism.md` L95: "This makes epiphenomenalism epistemically self-undermining—**not logically contradictory, since it could still be true**, but impossible to hold rationally."

  So `delegatory-causation.md` is the outlier. It recruits an *epistemic* defeater (epiphenomenalism *cannot be rationally believed*) to assert a stronger *logical/constitutive* claim (epiphenomenalism *has a logical defect / defeats its own evidence base / fails a consistency requirement*). This is the epistemic↔metaphysical equivocation pattern in register-shifted form, and it is *orthogonal to hedge density* — the paragraph is heavily hedged elsewhere yet still mis-registers the argument. It is also a genuine internal inconsistency: a reader who follows the `[[self-stultification]]` link finds a page that explicitly denies the very thing the hub asserts.
- **Why it matters (not cosmetic)**: The "logical defect / consistency requirement" framing is exactly what does the work of making delegation's advantage "a constraint of a different kind." If the honest classification is *epistemic self-defeat* (cannot be rationally endorsed, may still be true), the asymmetry is still real and still strong — but it is an epistemic constraint, not a logical/consistency one. The over-claim inflates the apparent strength of the article's central differentiator. (Note: the article's `[[tenets#^bidirectional-interaction]]` Relation section, L170, repeats "avoiding the self-undermining implications of epiphenomenalism" — that phrasing is fine; "self-undermining" is shared vocabulary. The defect is specifically the "**logical** defect" / "consistency requirement" escalation, not the word "self-undermining.")
- **Recommendation**: `refine-draft` pass that down-registers "logical defect" → "epistemic self-defeat / cannot be rationally endorsed (though it may still be true)" and softens "consistency requirement" to the rational-credibility framing, matching `self-stultification.md`'s vocabulary verbatim. The substance (the asymmetry is a harder constraint than parsimony-preference) is preserved; only the over-strong register is corrected. Add a one-clause cross-link making explicit that this is the epistemic, not logical, defeater per `[[self-stultification]]`.
