Under the strict reading of [[tenets#^minimal-quantum-interaction|Tenet 2 (Minimal Quantum Interaction)]], the Map commits to mental causation working through a very small information-transfer channel: per-event selection over a brain-prepared candidate set, with long-run statistics regressing to Born-rule probabilities, and content confined to whatever the candidates encode. The [[selection-only-mind-influence]] article derives the arithmetic — log₂(N) bits per event, an approximately ε² / (2 ln 2) signed per-trial rate when Born statistics are preserved, and a content ceiling set by candidate-space dimensionality. Once that channel is fixed, every other mental-causation article in the catalogue — free will, placebo, choking, attention training, meditation, animal cognition, AI consciousness, time-arrow claims — is implicitly drawing on the *same* tiny budget. The causal-budget ledger names that constraint and supplies the discipline that keeps "minimal" from becoming a global escape hatch: each claim must specify what it is paying with, and the catalogue as a whole must not draw more than the interface can plausibly carry.

The discipline joins the Map's named methodological patterns — [[closed-loop-opportunity-execution|closed-loop opportunity execution]], [[coalesce-condense-apex-stability]], [[bedrock-clash-vs-absorption|absorb-or-clash]], [[framework-stage-calibration|framework-stage calibration]], [[direct-refutation-discipline|direct-refutation]], and [[evidential-status-discipline|evidential-status]] — at a layer none of them covers: *quantitative budget conservation across the mental-causation cluster*. It keeps Tenet 2's minimality from spreading invisibly across many articles, each individually modest, that in aggregate request more bandwidth than the interface allows.

## The Failure Mode

The 2026-05-11 ChatGPT 5.5 Pro outer review identified this as a structural risk the catalogue had not yet named. The [[selection-only-mind-influence]] article already states per-event information limits, ensemble-level Born-rule convergence, and the impossibility of selecting content the brain has not pre-generated. The reviewer's novel inference is that this is not just a fact about one article — it is a *global constraint* that should propagate to every article invoking mental causation. The catalogue currently does not check whether its total claimed causal workload exceeds what its minimal interface can plausibly carry.

A free-will article positing volitional control over deliberation, a placebo article positing content-specific physiological steering, an attention-training article positing Schwartz-style OCD remediation, an animal-consciousness article positing selection-mediated mental causation in cephalopods, and an AI-consciousness article positing causally coupled phenomenal influence in a transformer can each be modest in its per-event claim. The aggregate is invisible: nothing in the catalogue's review machinery currently checks whether the total bandwidth requested fits inside ε² / (2 ln 2). If "minimal" means anything quantitative at all, that aggregate must be auditable. Without the audit, the catalogue can endorse a position whose total causal workload would exceed any plausible reading of Tenet 2 — and would do so *while still claiming Tenet 2 strictly*.

The failure mode is adjacent to but distinct from those the prior disciplines catch. Coherence inflation tracks claim-strength drift; evidential-status tracks evidence-grade drift; direct-refutation tracks perimeter-substitution in reasoning mode. None tracks *budget aggregation*. A claim can be coherent, appropriately calibrated, refutation-honest, and stage-appropriate, and still contribute to a cluster whose summed bandwidth is implausible. The ledger closes that gap by requiring each mental-causation claim to declare what it spends, in the same units, so the cluster's total cost is visible.

## The Ledger Schema

For each claim that mental causation does specific physical work — directs an outcome, biases a selection, accelerates a recovery, suppresses an automatism, sustains attentional control — the article should be able to fill in six fields. These fields are the editor's diagnostic vocabulary, not exposed in article prose, and the article expresses the same content in natural language. (See [[writing-style|the writing-style guide]] for the prose patterns.)

**1. Candidate set.** What is the brain-side set of alternatives that mental selection is choosing among? At what physical level — synaptic release, neural-assembly competition, motor-program initiation, decision-bistability resolution? Without this, "consciousness selects" has no referent for *what* is being selected. The candidate set defines N in log₂(N); claims that do not specify N are claims without a determinate budget.

**2. Selection bandwidth.** Approximately how many bits per second is the claim implicitly requesting? At what amplification gain? If the claim is content-specific (placebo's "endogenous opioid release tracking belief content"), how much information must transfer per event to encode the content distinction? Even an order-of-magnitude estimate forces the question.

**3. Amplification pathway.** How does the per-event selection scale into macroscopic outcome? Neural-quantum events on the order of 10⁷–10¹² per second across the brain only matter to behaviour if some amplification mechanism (synaptic threshold, motor-program commitment, attentional gain, neuroplastic consolidation) lifts them to the behavioural level. Articles claiming mental causation without naming an amplification pathway are claiming an effect without a mechanism for visibility.

**4. Expected statistical signature.** What pattern in data, if any, would the claim produce? Under Born-rule preservation, the signature must be subtle: vanishing per-trial bias at large N, possible decline effects, content-confinement bounds on reportable phenomenology. Articles whose claims would produce no statistical signature at all are unfalsifiable in practice — and need to say so honestly, locating their support elsewhere (phenomenological argument, indirect inference, internal-coherence consideration).

**5. Falsification condition.** What observation would the claim *not survive*? This is the [[testability-ledger|testability-ledger's]] question asked at claim-level rather than tenet-level. Most mental-causation claims in the catalogue currently inherit testability from the parent article rather than carrying their own. The ledger requires each claim to declare its own disconfirmer.

**6. Nearest physicalist explanation.** What would a physicalist say is going on, and what does the Map's reading buy over and above that explanation? If the nearest physicalist account is "predictive-processing top-down modulation of nociceptive gain" (placebo) or "self-focus-induced working-memory load disrupting motor-program automaticity" (choking), the article must specify what its mental-causation reading adds that the physicalist reading does not already supply. Without this, the article risks claiming mental causation as a *redundant* layer rather than an *explanatory* one.

The six fields are not independent; specifying any one constrains the others. But they are jointly sufficient to make the budget visible. An article whose claim cannot fill in all six is making a claim that has not been measured against the interface, and the editor should treat the gap as the substantive defect — not as a stylistic omission to be papered over.

## Why the Budget Binds

Three results from [[selection-only-mind-influence]] close the question of whether the budget is real. The *per-event ceiling* is log₂(N) bits, set entirely by the brain. The *per-second ceiling* under Born-rule preservation is approximately R · ε² / (2 ln 2) bits/s; with ε of order 10⁻³, this delivers per-event rates near 7 × 10⁻⁷ bits, and the aggregate at neural-quantum event rates of 10⁷–10¹² per second lands in a band that can fall short of or modestly exceed the ~10 bits/s of conscious agency reported by Stoll & Zheng (2025). The bandwidth is not generous. The *content-confinement bound* is the hardest constraint: nothing the brain has not pre-generated can be selected, reported, or felt. The arithmetic is unforgiving in precisely the way the ledger needs.

## Audit Checklist for `/refine-draft` and `/deep-review`

When either skill touches an article that invokes mental causation, the skill should run the following five-question audit, recording the diagnosis in the changelog rather than in the article body:

1. Does the article identify a candidate set, even informally? If not, what is the minimum specification that would make the claim non-vacuous?
2. Does the article's implicit bandwidth request fit within the [[selection-only-mind-influence|strict reading]] ceilings? If not, does the article acknowledge that it is using a non-strict (intermediate) reading of Tenet 2?
3. Does the article name an amplification pathway, or does it leave the gap between quantum-scale selection and macroscopic outcome implicit?
4. What signature would the claim produce, and is that signature compatible with the empirical corridor [[selection-only-mind-influence]] sketches?
5. What is the nearest physicalist account, and what work does the Map's reading do beyond it?

Where one or more answers are missing, the skill installs the missing specification in natural prose — without exposing the six-field vocabulary in the article — and records in the changelog which fields were filled. Where the budget appears to be exceeded, the skill flags a coherence issue: either the article needs to acknowledge it is using a non-strict reading, or the claim needs to be calibrated downward. The audit is not "every article must complete all six fields explicitly"; it is "every article whose claim depends on Tenet 2 must be auditable against the budget by a future reviewer who consults the ledger."

The skill also checks for the *aggregation* failure mode at cluster level: when multiple recently-touched articles all draw on the same bandwidth without coordination, the changelog records the cluster — free-will + placebo + attention + animal-cognition + AI — so a future cycle's review can ask the budget-aggregation question explicitly. This is the closest the catalogue can get to running a real budget check inside an automated pipeline.

## Worked Examples

### Placebo

[[empirical-phenomena-mental-causation]] claims belief content steers physiological response toward specific outcomes — opioid release, dopamine modulation, immune suppression. Ledger entries: **candidate set** is plausibly the set of neuromodulatory profiles the brain prepares as live options under top-down predictive control; **bandwidth** is small (the content distinction "this is morphine" vs "this is saline" is low-dimensional) but persistent over treatment duration; **amplification pathway** runs through descending modulatory systems (periaqueductal gray, mesolimbic dopamine) that already have substantial physical gain; **statistical signature** is the well-documented dose-response and content-specificity of placebo effects; **falsification condition** is the demonstration that placebo response shows no content-tracking dependence; **nearest physicalist explanation** is predictive-processing top-down nociceptive modulation, which already produces the effect under standard cognitive-neuroscience reading. The Map's mental-causation reading adds the claim that the *content* of belief — not merely a functional/predictive variable correlated with it — is doing the selecting. The audit passes; the budget is small and the amplification mechanism is independently substantial.

### Free Will

[[topics/free-will]] claims volitional control over which of several deliberative outcomes is realised. Ledger entries: **candidate set** is the set of competing motor-program or decision-bistability resolutions the brain has prepared as live options; **bandwidth** is bounded by the conscious-agency rate (~10 bits/s); **amplification pathway** runs through prefrontal commitment to action selection; **statistical signature** is the persistence of action-outcome correlation under conditions that should be physically indeterminate at the relevant level; **falsification condition** is the demonstration that volitional reports show no signature distinguishable from forward-modelled physicalist accounts; **nearest physicalist explanation** is dual-process theory plus stochastic noise in decision dynamics. The audit passes if and only if the article does *not* additionally claim that mind contributes content the brain has not pre-generated — content-confinement caps what free will can deliver. Many libertarian-flavoured framings overrun the budget at exactly that point; the ledger surfaces it.

### Choking

[[empirical-phenomena-mental-causation]]'s choking treatment claims conscious self-monitoring disrupts automatised skill. Ledger entries: **candidate set** is the set of execution timings within an automatised motor program; **bandwidth** requested is small (a binary "interrupt / do not interrupt" signal at most); **amplification pathway** runs through working-memory load on the motor program; **statistical signature** is the well-replicated performance decrement under self-focus; **falsification condition** is the demonstration that the decrement is fully explained by working-memory crowding without need for a selection variable; **nearest physicalist explanation** is exactly the self-focus / working-memory account. The Map's reading adds that *which* moments of attention occur is itself a selection — but the budget cost is minimal and the audit passes cleanly.

The examples show the discipline working as intended: most mental-causation claims in the catalogue *do* fit within the budget. The ledger's value is making that fit explicit and auditable, not retroactively constraining well-formed claims.

## Honest Limitations

The ledger does not adjudicate which reading of Tenet 2 the catalogue should use. The strict (selection-only) reading is tightest, but [[selection-only-mind-influence]] notes that the intermediate reading (per-trial bias preserving ensemble Born statistics) is empirically live. An article using the intermediate reading is not falsifying Tenet 2; it is using a non-strict reading the tenet permits. The ledger requires only that the reading be *named*, so the audit can run against the right bounds.

The arithmetic is also stage-appropriately uncertain. The selection rate R is not directly measured; ε is a theoretical parameter; the amplification pathway is the [[interface-specification-programme|interface-specification-programme's]] open problem rather than a settled engineering specification. The budget is a *band*, not a number — the ledger's job is to keep claims inside the band's plausibility envelope, not to enforce false precision.

The discipline can be misused as cover for refusing to make any quantitative claim at all. An article that hedges every causal commitment until the budget is trivially satisfied has used the ledger to evacuate substance. The discipline is *not* "claim as little as possible"; it is "claim what the evidence and the interface allow, and make the claim's price visible."

## Relation to Site Perspective

The discipline's most direct alignment is with [[tenets#^minimal-quantum-interaction|Tenet 2 (Minimal Quantum Interaction)]]: it operationalises *minimal* as a measurable constraint rather than a rhetorical hedge. Without the ledger, Tenet 2 is at risk of becoming the global escape hatch the outer reviewer named — a phrase deployed wherever the catalogue needs Tenet 3 (Bidirectional Interaction) to deliver real causal work, with no cross-claim check on whether the work being delivered fits inside the channel's actual capacity.

The discipline serves [[tenets#^bidirectional-interaction|Tenet 3]] too, by clarifying *how much* bidirectional interaction the framework is committed to. Tenet 3 rejects epiphenomenalism, which the [[concepts/epiphenomenalism|epiphenomenalism]] article notes is a substantive commitment supported by the self-stultification argument. The ledger keeps that commitment from inflating: mind does causal work, but the work is bounded, and the bound is the same bound everywhere it is invoked. The catalogue is committed to *some* mental causation; it is not committed to whatever amount any individual article happens to need.

The discipline also feeds back into [[coherence-inflation-countermeasures|coherence-inflation countermeasures]]: budget over-aggregation is a silent inflation vector the existing countermeasures (steelman sections, provenance tagging, freshness checks, citation-loop detection) do not directly catch. A claim can be appropriately hedged within its own article and still contribute to a cluster whose summed cost exceeds the budget. The ledger closes that gap by making the per-claim cost the auditable unit.
