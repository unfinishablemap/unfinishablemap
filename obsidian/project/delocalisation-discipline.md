When an article's preferred inference over-reaches the evidence the catalogue actually has, the disciplined move is not silence and not a bare hedge. It is *relocation*: identify the strongest claim the evidence does support — typically an adjacent, narrower claim — and re-anchor the article's load-bearing weight there, letting the over-reaching claim recede to whatever honest register it belongs in (framework-boundary marking, an explicit "this is the stronger thesis we have not earned" caveat, or removal). The delocalisation discipline names this constructive move, specifies when to deploy it, supplies the audit a refine-pass or deep-review runs to install it, and names its own failure mode: relocating so far that the surviving claim is trivial and no longer earns the article the point it was written to make.

The discipline is the *affirmative complement* to the diagnostics in the sister disciplines. Those tell the editor a claim is over-reaching and how to recognise it; delocalisation tells the editor what to do with the article once the over-reach is recognised — where to put the weight the over-reaching claim was carrying. It is a *honesty* move throughout: relocation retreats to what the evidence supports, never a rhetorical dodge that smuggles the abandoned claim back in by implication.

The vocabulary is editor-internal. Articles do not say "this claim is delocalised"; they simply make the narrower claim in natural prose, with the relocation invisible to the reader, who sees only a well-calibrated argument. The discipline document is where the editor learns the move; the changelog is where individual relocations are recorded.

## The Move

A delocalisation has three parts, run in order:

1. **Locate the over-reach.** A load-bearing claim asserts more than the evidence licenses. The sister disciplines supply the detectors: *defeater-removal treated as evidence-elevation*, *constraint treated as establishment*, *compatibility treated as support*, *cluster-coherence treated as tier-graduation*, *epistemic reading asserted as metaphysical*. Any of these flags an over-reach.

2. **Find the strongest supported adjacent claim.** Ask what the same evidence *does* establish — the claim one step weaker, or one step sideways, that the evidence genuinely earns. The adjacent claim is usually already latent in the article; delocalisation makes it the explicit load-bearer rather than a way-station the prose passes through on the way to the over-reach.

3. **Re-anchor and discharge the residue.** Rewrite so the adjacent claim carries the article's argumentative weight, and route the over-reaching claim to its honest register: declare it as a framework-boundary commitment, mark it as an open stronger thesis the catalogue has not earned, or drop it. The article's point must now rest on the relocated claim, not on the reader silently importing the abandoned one.

The diagnostic question for a reviewer or refine-editor:

> *If the strongest version of this claim is more than the evidence supports, what is the strongest claim the same evidence does support — and does the article's point survive resting on that claim instead? If it survives, relocate the weight there. If it does not survive, the article's point was the over-reach, and the article needs a different argument, not a relocation.*

The second clause is the discipline's teeth. A relocation that leaves the article making the same point it made before, by implication, has not been done; a relocation that leaves the article pointless has revealed that the point was never earned.

## When to Deploy

Delocalisation is the standard repair whenever a sister-discipline diagnostic fires *and an adjacent supported claim is available*. The typical sites:

- **Tenet-coherence sites.** Where an article reads a body of evidence as supporting a tenet, relocate to "the evidence removes a standing objection to the tenet; the tenet's positive support stands independently elsewhere." This is the quantum-biology relocation below.
- **Rival-model interfaces.** Where evidence is compatible with the Map's reading and with named rivals, relocate to "the evidence is compatible with the Map's reading" plus an explicit statement of what discriminator would be needed to lift compatibility to support. Routes through [[evidential-status-discipline|the compatibility-vs-support section]].
- **Theory-critique sites.** Where a critique of a rival theory over-reaches (claims to refute the whole framework), relocate to the defensible insight the critique actually earns — often "the framework's core observation is right; the specific over-move it built on it does not follow." This is the IIT relocation below.
- **Phenomenological-anomaly sites.** Where an anomaly is read as establishing a strong metaphysical conclusion, relocate to the robust phenomenological or structural claim and separate out the empirically weaker claim. This is the OBE relocation below.
- **Selective-pattern sites.** Where a uniform strong claim is unsupported but a *selective* pattern in the data is supported, relocate to the selective pattern, which is frequently harder for the rival to explain than the uniform claim was. This is the cognitive-distinctiveness relocation below.

Delocalisation is *not* deployed where the over-reaching claim is itself a tenet. A tenet is not relocated; it is declared at the framework boundary per [[direct-refutation-discipline|direct-refutation discipline's Mode Three]]. Delocalisation operates on *empirical and argumentative* claims an article makes in support of its position, not on the framework commitments themselves.

## Distinction from the Sister Disciplines

Delocalisation is a genuinely distinct pattern, not a rename of a move already named elsewhere. The distinctions, made explicit so the discipline does not blur into its siblings:

- **vs. [[evidential-status-discipline|evidential-status discipline]] (tier-labelling and refusal).** Evidential-status tells the editor to *hold a claim at its honest tier* and *refuse the upgrade* — it is a labelling-and-restraint discipline. Its *constrain-vs-establish* rule says "do not treat constraint as establishment"; its fix is to refuse the establishment claim. Delocalisation is the next step that refusal leaves open: once the establishment claim is refused, *where does the article's weight go?* Evidential-status says do-not-over-claim; delocalisation says here-is-the-claim-to-claim-instead. The *epistemic-≠-metaphysical* rule comes closest — its fix names "the weaker reading the evidence actually supports" — but it is a *split* of one claim's two readings, performed to detect equivocation; delocalisation is the *relocation of an article's load-bearing weight* across to an adjacent claim, performed to rebuild the argument. An article can satisfy evidential-status (every claim honestly tier-labelled) and still need delocalisation (the article's *point* still rests on a tier-honest but evidentially-thin claim, and a stronger adjacent claim was available it failed to anchor on).

- **vs. [[direct-refutation-discipline|direct-refutation discipline]] (engagement mode against opponents).** Direct-refutation governs how the catalogue argues *against an opponent* — which of four reasoning modes the reply uses. Delocalisation governs how the catalogue states *its own* supporting claim. The two meet at Mode Four (empirical underdetermination): when a dispute is underdetermined, direct-refutation marks it open; delocalisation is what the article does *with its own claim* in that situation — relocate from "our reading is supported" to "our reading is compatible, and here is the discriminator that would settle it." Direct-refutation is opponent-facing; delocalisation is self-facing.

- **vs. [[common-cause-null|the common-cause null]] (discount of convergence count).** The common-cause null is a *discount-of-evidence* move: it asks whether N converging sources are one source read N times, and if so, *lowers the evidential weight* of the convergence. Delocalisation is *relocation-of-claim*, not discount-of-evidence: it leaves the evidence's weight where it is and moves the *claim* to what that weight supports. The two compose — an article whose convergence is discounted by the common-cause null often then needs to delocalise its conclusion onto the residue the discount leaves standing — but discounting a count and relocating a claim are different operations.

In one line: evidential-status says *do not over-claim*; common-cause says *do not over-count*; direct-refutation says *do not over-argue against the opponent*; delocalisation says *having stopped over-claiming, put the weight on the adjacent claim the evidence earns*.

## The Audit Checklist

For `/refine-draft` and `/deep-review`, when a sister-discipline diagnostic flags a load-bearing claim:

1. **Name the over-reach and the supported adjacent claim.** State both explicitly in the editor's working notes — the claim as written, and the strongest claim the same evidence supports. If no adjacent supported claim can be named, the issue is not a delocalisation candidate; it is an unsupported claim that routes to removal or to an open-thesis caveat.
2. **Test point-survival.** Does the article's section still make a non-trivial point if its weight rests on the adjacent claim? If yes, relocate. If no, flag the section for re-argument — do not perform a relocation that hollows the article out and leaves it gesturing at the abandoned claim.
3. **Re-anchor in natural prose.** Rewrite so the adjacent claim is the explicit load-bearer. Verify the over-reaching claim is not smuggled back by implication (the failure mode below).
4. **Discharge the residue honestly.** Route the over-reaching claim to its register: framework-boundary declaration (if it is a tenet commitment), an explicit "stronger thesis we have not earned" caveat (if it is an empirical claim the catalogue would like but cannot support), or removal.
5. **Record the relocation.** Log it in the changelog — *"delocalised [claim] from [over-reach] to [adjacent claim]; residue discharged as [register]"* — so future reviewers can audit the move and check the relocation did not over-shoot.

## Failure Mode: Over-Delocalisation

The discipline's characteristic failure is *relocating so far that the relocated claim is trivial or no longer supports the article's point*. An OBE article that delocalises "consciousness exits the body" all the way to "people sometimes report unusual bodily experiences" has retreated past the claim the evidence supports (the bodily self-model is dislocatable while consciousness persists) into a claim so weak it earns the article nothing. The over-reach was dishonest; the trivialised relocation is *useless*, and uselessness is its own failure.

Two guards:

- **Relocate to the strongest supported claim, not the safest.** The target is the *strongest* claim the evidence earns, not the weakest claim no one could object to. A relocation that clears all possible objection by saying almost nothing has mistaken safety for honesty. The point-survival test (step 2) is the guard: if the surviving point is trivial, the relocation over-shot.
- **Do not let relocation become a refusal to make any claim.** The discipline is misusable as cover for a reviewer who delocalises every strong claim into vacuity, producing articles that assert nothing and commit to nothing. The catalogue is *allowed* to make strong claims where the evidence supports them; delocalisation fires only where the *specific* claim over-reaches, and it relocates to the strongest adjacent claim, not to silence. An article all of whose claims have been delocalised into hedged near-vacuity has been over-disciplined, not disciplined.

The symmetry with [[evidential-status-discipline|evidential-status discipline's]] own honest-limitation is exact: that discipline can be misused to demand de-escalation across the board including where strong language is earned; delocalisation can be misused to relocate every claim into triviality. Both corrections are the same: the move fires on *specific* over-reaches, and it relocates to the strongest *supported* claim, never to the weakest *unobjectionable* one.

## Worked Exhibits

The catalogue already deploys the move, unnamed, across argumentatively-careful articles. Six verified exhibits, each confirmed by reading the cited passage:

- **[[out-of-body-experiences]]** — the cleanest exhibit. The article *separates the philosophically robust claim — that the bodily self-model is dislocatable while consciousness persists — from the empirically weaker claim that consciousness can perceive from a location the body does not occupy*, anchors its argument on the first (well supported), and routes the second to "awaits controlled evidence the AWARE studies have so far not produced." The relocation is explicit and the residue is discharged as an open empirical question, not smuggled back.

- **[[quantum-biology-and-neural-consciousness]]** — the tenet-coherence exhibit. Rather than read quantum-biology findings as *supporting* the Dualism tenet, the article relocates: *the tenet does not depend on quantum biology — the positive arguments for dualism stand independently — but a major objection has been removed*. The load-bearing weight moves from "evidence supports the tenet" to the adjacent supported claim "evidence removes a standing objection," with the tenet's positive support relocated to the independent arguments. The article performs the same move at the cluster level — *this convergence raises the prior... rather than establishing necessity* — relocating from establishment to prior-raising.

- **[[consciousness-and-integrated-information]]** — the theory-critique exhibit. The article keeps IIT's defensible insight (*consciousness and integration are intimately connected*) and relocates away from IIT's over-move: *IIT made a philosophical move that does not follow from this observation* — the identity thesis. The surviving claim, *integration is something consciousness does, not what it is*, is the adjacent claim the shared insight actually supports. The critique relocates onto the over-move rather than over-reaching into a wholesale rejection of the integration insight.

- **[[consciousness-and-cognitive-distinctiveness]]** — the selective-pattern exhibit. Rather than claim consciousness is uniformly required for cognition, the article relocates to the *selective* pattern: *humans dramatically exceed apes on tasks involving explicit representation and deliberate manipulation while the advantage is modest for perceptual speed... This selective pattern matches what conscious access would predict*. The relocated claim is stronger against the rival than the uniform claim was — a generic resource account predicts a uniform shift, so the selective pattern is the harder fact to explain away.

- **[[baseline-cognition]]** — the type-specificity exhibit. The article relocates the dualist inference from a generic "consciousness causes the human-ape gap" to the type-specific correspondence: *the gap tracks these operations and not others equally resource-intensive, which a generic resource-expansion account cannot deliver because it predicts a uniform performance shift rather than the type-by-type pattern*. The weight rests on the discriminating type-specific claim the evidence earns, not on the over-reaching generic one.

- **[[psychedelics-and-the-filter-model]]** — the rival-model-interface exhibit. The article declines to read the psychedelic neuroimaging as adjudicating between filter theory and Letheby's predictive self-binding — *what discriminates the accounts is not the psychedelic neuroimaging but broader theoretical commitments* — and relocates the disagreement to its honest register: *the Map's commitment to Tenet 1 places it outside Letheby's framework as a matter of foundational stance, not of altered-state-data adjudication*. The over-reach (the data support filter theory) is delocalised onto the compatible-not-supported residue plus an honest framework-boundary declaration.

The six sit at different sites — phenomenological anomaly, tenet-coherence, theory-critique, comparative cognition, comparative cognition at the concept grain, rival-model interface — which is the point: the move is portable across the catalogue wherever an over-reach has a supported adjacent claim to receive the weight. Each exhibit *keeps* the article's point by relocating it; none trivialises the point into vacuity, which is what distinguishes a disciplined delocalisation from the failure mode above.

## Relation to Site Perspective

The discipline is methodological. Its most direct alignment is with **[[tenets|Tenet 5: Occam's Razor Has Limits]]**, applied at the *claim-placement layer*. Tenet 5 denies that the simplest reading is automatically the earned one; delocalisation is the constructive discipline that follows when a reading turns out *not* to be earned — it relocates the article's commitment to the strongest reading that *is* earned, rather than either clinging to the unearned reading or collapsing into saying nothing. The discipline also serves the catalogue's epistemic credibility in the same way its siblings do: a reader who finds the Map's load-bearing claims placed exactly where the evidence supports them — neither inflated past the evidence nor deflated into vacuity — has been given a reason to take the catalogue's commitments as the conclusions of disciplined inquiry. Delocalisation is the move that puts each claim in its earned place.
