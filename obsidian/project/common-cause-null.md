A convergence argument across N independent traditions, substrates, or methodologies treats the convergence as cumulative evidence — N independent observations triangulating on a real feature of the territory. The *common-cause null* is the standard objection: if the N observations share an upstream architecture that *itself* would produce the convergence, the apparent triangulation collapses to a single observation read N times. The N-fold count overstates the evidence because the count was never independent. The Map's most ambitious convergence claims rely on cumulative structure across many sources; this document specifies the discipline that calibrates such claims against the common-cause null, names the tests that would distinguish independent triangulation from common-architecture-rediscovered, supplies a checklist for new convergence-argument articles, and names the discipline's honest limitation — that the null can be wielded as a universal solvent against any convergence claim and so requires its own scope condition.

The discipline is the sixth named methodological pattern in the Map's editorial system, joining [[closed-loop-opportunity-execution|closed-loop opportunity execution]] (cycle level), [[coalesce-condense-apex-stability|coalesce-condense-apex-stability]] (article level), [[bedrock-clash-vs-absorption|absorb-or-clash discipline]] (within-article), [[framework-stage-calibration|framework-stage calibration]] (framework level), [[direct-refutation-discipline|direct-refutation discipline]] (reasoning-mode level), and [[evidential-status-discipline|evidential-status discipline]] (calibration layer). The present discipline operates one level over from evidential-status: not how a single empirical claim is labelled, but how a *convergence across many claims* is calibrated against the inferential challenge that the convergence may not carry the evidential weight its N-fold structure suggests.

## The Failure Mode

A convergence argument runs as follows: N independent sources — authors, traditions, substrates, methodologies — all observe pattern P. The independence of the sources is what supplies the inferential force; the cumulative weight of N independent confirmations exceeds what any single source could provide. The Map deploys this structure across the catalogue: the [[apex/phenomenal-output-causal-machinery-dissociation|phenomenal-output / causal-machinery dissociation cluster]] cites seven traditions on a single architectural signature; [[the-convergence-argument-for-dualism]] catalogues multiple independent considerations; [[cross-traditional-convergence-on-consciousness-irreducibility]] cites Buddhist, Vedantic, Husserlian, and analytic traditions reaching the same conclusion; the [[empirical-phenomena-mental-causation|empirical-phenomena-of-mental-causation]] pair cites placebo and choking as positive coupling across humoral/dopaminergic and motor/basal-ganglia substrates.

The common-cause null asks: *what if the N sources are not independent in the way the inference requires?* If they share an upstream architecture — a shared introspective channel, a shared evolutionary lineage, a shared cultural transmission, a shared theoretical framework that pre-shaped what each observer noticed — then the N-fold count is reading the same upstream feature N times rather than triangulating across N genuinely independent measurements. The convergence is real but does not carry the evidential weight its surface structure suggests. The failure mode is the silent slide from *N traditions converge* to *the convergence supplies N-fold evidential force* — the second claim adds an independence-of-shared-machinery clause the article has not earned.

## The Catalogue's Convergence Arguments and How They Engage the Null

Four convergence-argument structures recur in the catalogue, each engaging the common-cause null differently.

**The dissociation cluster.** The [[apex/phenomenal-output-causal-machinery-dissociation|phenomenal-output / causal-machinery dissociation apex]] cites seven traditions on the *output-while-operation-hidden* signature — Carruthers, Trick & Pylyshyn, Koriat, Polanyi, Inzlicht & Kurzban, Bergson, Schwitzgebel. The apex's line 144 is the catalogue's most explicit common-cause acknowledgement: all seven analyse human cognition through human introspection in human language, and *the shared introspective channel itself is enormous common machinery*. The seven-tradition count overstates the convergence; what does the load-bearing work is the recurrence of the signature *across substrates within a single nervous system on radically different timescales* — perceptual cardinality at sub-second timescales running on parallel-individuation tokens; memory consolidation across decades running on hippocampal-cortical transfer. The two cannot share specific machinery beyond the involvement of a single conscious subject, so the cross-substrate-within-one-organism observation is a partial defence against the null.

**The phenomenal-divergence cluster.** The 2026-05-08 cluster on aphantasia, synaesthesia, and source-attribution-divergence makes a structurally parallel move: divergence across patient-populations supplies what cross-tradition convergence cannot, because the populations have anomalous-introspective-architecture and so the *shared introspective channel* on which the common-cause objection rests is precisely what differs across them. Patient-population evidence is the clearest current instance of the discipline's preferred test.

**The empirical-phenomena-of-mental-causation pair.** Placebo and choking under pressure run on different physiological substrates — humoral and dopaminergic versus motor-cortical and basal-ganglia. The convergence is the coupling-direction: phenomenology couples to causal machinery in matched detail in the success cases. The common-cause objection is weaker because the substrates are physiologically dissimilar and the coupling is observed at the behavioural-outcome level rather than at the introspective-report level.

**The convergence argument for dualism.** [[the-convergence-argument-for-dualism]] catalogues the explanatory gap, the knowledge argument, conceivability arguments, and the hard problem as multiple independent considerations converging on the same anti-physicalist conclusion. The common-cause objection here is the most demanding of any in the catalogue: each consideration is generated by philosophers working with similar conceptual analyses, similar phenomenological starting points, and similar intuition-pumps. The shared *philosophical methodology* is itself plausible upstream common machinery. The article's currently-available defence runs through the considerations' formal-structural diversity (modal versus epistemic versus phenomenological), but the formal diversity does not by itself rule out shared methodology as the common cause.

**The voids catalogue.** The Map's [[apex/taxonomy-of-voids|taxonomy of voids]] runs a convergence argument across 60+ entries: voids cluster in structured ways at the consciousness-matter interface, and the structured clustering is offered as cumulative abductive support for the tenet-package. The common-cause null sharpens here into a specific and uncomfortable shape — a *circularity* shape rather than a generic-machinery shape. The voids are catalogued under the Map's framework; the framework's tenets shape what counts as a void in the first place ([[tenet-generated-voids]] is the Map's own articulation of this); the convergence of the catalogue with the tenet-package therefore reads largely as *framework-internal coherence* rather than *framework-independent triangulation*. The discount is structural, not merely calibrative: convergence-of-X with the framework that defines X cannot earn the N-fold force the count suggests. The strongest currently-available defences are (a) the *cross-substrate-within-one-organism* move where applicable (the catalogue does include voids at radically different cognitive substrates — perceptual binding, dreamless sleep, contemplative absorption — that cannot share substrate-specific machinery), and (b) the partial exceptions clause (the [[temporal-void|temporal void]], dream consciousness void, and silence void sit outside the main convergence structure, showing the catalogue is not fully forced into a single narrative). Per-cluster independence scoring across the five families is the open programme; until landed, the catalogue's convergence claim earns only the weight its independence tests can supply. The [[the-surplus-void|surplus void]] is the worked exhibit where the framework-internal-coherence reading replaced the older framework-independent-evidence framing.

## The Cross-Substrate-Within-One-Organism Response

The strongest defence currently available against the common-cause null is *substrate-divergence within a single conscious subject*. If a convergence is observed across substrates that cannot share specific machinery beyond the involvement of a single subject, the common-cause null cannot run through *that* common machinery — the substrates do not share it. The defence is partial: the *single subject* is itself common machinery (one introspective channel reading multiple substrates), but it does substantial work whenever the substrates differ on the dimensions the common-cause hypothesis would need to invoke.

The defence's force depends on substrate-divergence being *substantive* rather than nominal. Two substrates are substantively divergent when their architectural differences would produce different signatures *if* the signature were a substrate-specific artefact. Perceptual cardinality and memory consolidation pass this test: their architectural differences are substantial enough that a substrate-specific artefact in one would not produce the matched signature in the other. Two substrates are nominally divergent when the architectural differences are insufficient to discriminate substrate-artefact readings — two cognitive subsystems sharing a common attentional bottleneck would converge on the bottleneck's signature regardless of whether the signature is substantive.

The cross-substrate-within-one-organism move rules out substrate-shared common causes but not subject-level common causes. The strongest discharge requires going beyond a single organism — to cross-species, cross-cultural, and patient-population evidence — at which point the *single subject* common machinery is what differs.

## Distinguishing Tests: Cross-Species, Cross-Cultural, Patient-Population

Three classes of evidence would distinguish independent triangulation from common-architecture-rediscovered, each ruling out a different layer of common machinery.

**Cross-species evidence** rules out species-specific architecture. Do non-human animals exhibit the convergence's signature when tested with species-appropriate behavioural paradigms? If species with different introspective architectures (or no introspective architecture in the human sense) show the same dissociations or coupling patterns, the *human introspective channel* common-cause is ruled out. The [[evidential-status-discipline|evidential-status discipline]] supplies the calibration scale on which cross-species findings would be labelled.

**Cross-cultural evidence** rules out vocabulary-specific architecture. Do contemplative and phenomenological traditions with different introspective vocabularies — Buddhist *vipassana*, Husserlian phenomenological reduction, Advaita *neti neti* apophasis — report different signatures when applied to the same phenomenon? Convergent reports under genuinely divergent introspective methodologies rule out the shared-introspective-vocabulary common-cause. The risk: contemporary contemplative-phenomenological literature has substantial cross-pollination, so the contemporary convergence may not earn the inference. Historically isolated traditions supply stronger tests where their reports survive translation into a common analytic vocabulary.

**Patient-population evidence** rules out neurotypical-specific architecture. Do anomalous-introspective-architecture cases — congenital aphantasia, alexithymia, synaesthesia, hemispatial neglect, blindsight — show the cluster's signature? If patients whose introspective architecture differs from neurotypical cases continue to exhibit the convergence, the neurotypical-introspective-architecture common-cause is ruled out for the populations tested. The 2026-05-08 phenomenal-divergence cluster operates on this test.

A convergence claim that withstands all three has the strongest currently-available defence against the null. Most of the catalogue's convergence claims sit at intermediate positions, with the cross-substrate-within-one-organism move as the honestly-named partial defence and the three external tests named as the open empirical research programme rather than as in-hand evidence.

## Checklist for Future Convergence-Argument Articles

When a new article is built around a convergence claim, the following checks operate at construction and at deep-review:

1. **Name the common-cause null explicitly.** Identify the shared upstream architecture that *would* produce the convergence even in the absence of the convergence's load-bearing claim. State the shared architecture at its actual extent, not minimised.
2. **Calibrate the convergence count.** State the N-fold count honestly — which sources supply *despite-commitments* observation (their model does not predict the signature) versus *because-prediction* observation (their model predicts the signature). The convergence's force concentrates in the despite-commitments sub-class; the [[apex/phenomenal-output-causal-machinery-dissociation|dissociation apex]]'s class-(a)/class-(b) split is the canonical instance.
3. **Identify the strongest currently-available defence.** Cross-substrate-within-one-organism if substrate-divergence is substantive; patient-population evidence if anomalous-introspective-architecture cases are documented; cross-cultural evidence if historically isolated traditions are involved; cross-species evidence if behavioural paradigms travel.
4. **Name what evidence is not in hand.** Distinguish the defence currently available from the defence the convergence's strongest version would require. Evidence that would fully discharge the null but is not currently available is described as the open empirical programme, not as a closed disclaimer.
5. **State the consequence of the null prevailing.** If the convergence turns out to be one observation read N times, what changes for the article's argumentative shape? Articles whose claims survive a successful common-cause objection (because the cumulative case rests on substrate-divergence rather than tradition-count) are calibrated differently from articles whose claims would collapse.

The checklist is editor-vocabulary, recorded in the changelog and in deep-review notes. Articles express the resulting calibration in natural prose.

## Honest Limitation: The Universal-Solvent Risk

The common-cause null can be wielded against *any* convergence claim. Every convergence has an arbitrarily expansive shared upstream — at minimum, *all observers are humans on Earth in the early twenty-first century* will produce common-cause objections to any cumulative philosophical or empirical case. The discipline's integrity depends on distinguishing common-cause objections that *do* dissolve a convergence's load-bearing structure from objections that *would* dissolve any convergence and so dissolve nothing in particular.

The licensing condition for treating a common-cause objection as substantive: the common machinery the objection invokes must be specific enough that it would predict the convergence's *particular pattern*, not merely some convergence or other. *Human introspection produces self-reports* is too generic to dissolve any specific convergence claim — the prediction it licenses is "humans will report something," not "humans will report the cross-timescale output-while-operation-hidden signature with matched dissociable fallibility and mode-fragility under inspection."

The discipline therefore treats common-cause objections at two strengths. *Generic-machinery objections* (humans share introspective channels; researchers share theoretical frameworks; cultures share linguistic structures) license calibration of the convergence's confidence but do not license its dissolution. *Specific-machinery objections* (the convergence's particular pattern is *predicted* by a specific upstream feature without the convergence's load-bearing claim) license dissolution and require the article to either supply the structural defence or weaken the claim.

A second limitation: the cross-substrate-within-one-organism move can be over-applied. Two substrates that differ on dimensions *unrelated* to the common-cause hypothesis do not constitute substantive divergence. An article that names cross-substrate divergence as its defence must verify that the substrates differ on dimensions the common-cause hypothesis would need to invoke; otherwise the move is rhetorical rather than substantive.

## Relation to Site Perspective

The discipline is methodological. Its most direct alignment is with **[[tenets|Tenet 5: Occam's Razor Has Limits]]**, applied at the *convergence layer*. Tenet 5 names the failure mode of premature parsimony when the materialist case treats the simpler reductive story as if it had been earned. The common-cause null is the *symmetric* failure mode on the dualist side: premature parsimony in the convergence's favour, treating the surface count of converging sources as if the count had been earned. Both errors are simplicity-overreach under incomplete knowledge — one collapses N observations to one upstream architecture (materialist parsimony), the other inflates one observation read N times to N independent observations (convergence parsimony). The discipline applies Tenet 5's commitment to the convergence side.

The discipline complements [[direct-refutation-discipline|direct-refutation discipline]]. Direct-refutation discipline guards against tenet-incompatibility standing in for argument when an in-framework refutation is feasible; the present discipline guards against the surface convergence count standing in for inferential force when the independence claim is unearned. The discipline also feeds back into [[coherence-inflation-countermeasures|coherence-inflation countermeasures]]: cumulative convergence is the silent inflation vector the existing countermeasures do not directly catch. An article that absorbs every objection while citing cumulative convergence across the catalogue's other articles compounds inflation in two directions — each absorption removes a rival reading; the convergence count then reads each absorbed-into-coherence article as one independent confirmation. The common-cause discipline closes this loop by requiring convergence claims to face an inferential test the surface count alone does not pass.
