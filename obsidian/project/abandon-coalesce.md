An *abandon-coalesce* is the editorial decision to refuse a candidate merger after the cluster-evaluation pass identifies the candidate articles as deliberately differentiated rather than redundantly overlapping. The discipline pairs with the [[apex/conjunction-coalesce|conjunction-coalesce]]: conjunction-coalesce preserves seams *within* a void by merging two articles whose joint structure is load-bearing; abandon-coalesce preserves gaps *between* voids by refusing to merge articles whose adjacent differentiation is itself load-bearing. The two operations are the editorial-curation arm of the [[coalesce-condense-apex-stability|coalesce-condense-apex-stability triple-discipline]] working at opposite ends of the same axis: one says *the seam is the claim*, the other says *the gap is the claim*. This document names the differentiation test the discipline applies, catalogues the worked passes the Map's voids programme has performed, specifies the relation to the [[common-cause-null|common-cause null]] discipline against which the criterion is calibrated, and names the failure modes the discipline guards against.

The discipline operates at the *editorial-curation level*, sitting alongside the family of named methodological patterns — [[closed-loop-opportunity-execution|closed-loop opportunity execution]] (cycle level), [[coalesce-condense-apex-stability|coalesce-condense-apex-stability]] (article-set level), [[bedrock-clash-vs-absorption|bedrock-clash vs. absorption]] (within-article level), [[framework-stage-calibration|framework-stage calibration]] (framework level), [[direct-refutation-discipline|direct-refutation discipline]] (reasoning-mode level), [[evidential-status-discipline|evidential-status discipline]] (calibration layer), [[cluster-integration-discipline|cluster-integration discipline]] (argument-architecture layer), [[common-cause-null|common-cause null]] (convergence-evaluation layer). Where conjunction-coalesce names the warrant for installing a seam, abandon-coalesce names the warrant for *not* dissolving one. Both are scoped to voids work, where structural differentiation across nearby articles is doing the catalogue's hardest evidential work; both yield to a single-mechanism account when one becomes available.

## The Decision Space

Any coalesce-evaluation pass over a section produces one of three verdicts for each candidate cluster.

A **redundancy-coalesce** merges two articles whose seam is incidental — a contingent fact of how the catalogue grew, not a feature of the territory. The merged article reads as if it had always been one piece.

A **conjunction-coalesce** merges two articles whose seam is load-bearing. The merger keeps both faces visible because the conjunction itself encodes a claim no single face can make. The discipline is documented in the [[apex/conjunction-coalesce|apex methodology article]] alongside the seam test that distinguishes it from redundancy-coalesce.

An **abandon-coalesce** refuses the merger entirely. The candidate articles are neither incidentally redundant nor structurally conjoint; they are *deliberately differentiated*, each encoding a distinct failure signature against the others, and merging would collapse a distinction the territory bears. The article-count savings are foregone because the editorial cost of losing the distinction exceeds the maintenance benefit.

The three verdicts exhaust the decision space when the candidate cluster has been correctly framed. Misclassifying an abandon-coalesce as a redundancy-coalesce produces a bland merged article that says less than its sources combined. Misclassifying it as a conjunction-coalesce manufactures a structural seam the territory does not earn, producing the *rhetorical seam* failure mode the conjunction-coalesce discipline already names. Either misclassification damages the catalogue's evidential weight in the same way: by smoothing a structurally informative distinction into uninformative prose.

## The Differentiation Test

The criterion that warrants an abandon-coalesce admits a clean formulation: **do the candidate articles encode distinct failure signatures that the merger would lose?** Three sub-questions sharpen the test.

**Sub-question one: signature distinctness.** Each candidate article must name a failure signature — the specific phenomenology of how thinking breaks down when it approaches the limit. The [[apex/taxonomy-of-voids|taxonomy of voids]] catalogues six primary signatures plus dissolution-under-attention and silent erasure. Two articles whose signatures map to different entries in that inventory pass the first sub-question. Two articles whose signatures collapse to "vague mystery" or "undifferentiated confusion" fail it — they are decorative differentiations the merger should remove.

**Sub-question two: boundary-marking work.** The candidate articles must already perform explicit differentiation work, not merely co-exist on adjacent topics. The diagnostic is structural: do the articles carry "How this differs from [neighbour]" sections, "Distinct from X and Y" headings, or in-prose passages explicitly demarcating the boundary against named neighbours? If they do, the catalogue has already done the cognitive work of installing the gap; the merger would discard not just the distinction but the editorial labour that earned it. If they do not, the apparent differentiation may be only thematic, and the test escalates.

**Sub-question three: framework-independence of the distinction.** Most demanding. The distinction must survive translation out of the Map's framework. If two articles' differentiation depends entirely on Map-internal vocabulary — tenets, named voids, framework-generated categories — the merger may be discarding only framework-internal artefacts rather than territorial structure. If the distinction is articulable in language that pre-dates the Map (clinical phenomenology, classical philosophy of mind, the standard taxonomies of cognitive failure), the merger would discard territorial structure and the abandon-coalesce verdict is warranted. This sub-question is the discipline's strongest defence against the worry that abandon-coalesce decisions merely entrench the framework's existing categorisations.

A candidate cluster passes the test when all three sub-questions return positive. Failing any one sub-question shifts the verdict toward conjunction-coalesce (if the distinction is structural but cluster-internal) or redundancy-coalesce (if the distinction is thematic only).

## The Worked Passes

The discipline's empirical base is a series of evaluation passes whose verdicts the changelog records. Two passes are paradigmatic.

**The 2026-04-27 affective-trio rejection** is the cleanest worked failure documented in [[apex/conjunction-coalesce|conjunction-coalesce]] §"A Coalesce the Test Rejected". The candidate — merging [[affective-void|affective]], [[valence-void|valence]], and [[mood-void|mood]] into a single "affective voids" article — was refused because the candidate joint claim collapsed under scrutiny to "all three articles are about affect and all three involve opacity," a topic label rather than an analytical claim. The three articles already carried explicit differentiation language (valence-void's "Distinct from Affective, Mood, and Mattering Voids" section sits at its second heading), and the differentiation work was doing real cognitive labour the merger would have erased. The same session examined six further clusters and rejected each, supplying the falsifiability evidence the discipline requires — passes that *reject* the verdict the editor was prepared to consider are stronger evidence of discipline than passes that accept it.

**The 2026-05-12 00:20 UTC voids-section rejection** examined five clusters under section-pressure (voids at 99/100) and refused all five. The cognitive-limits family ([[biological-cognitive-closure|biological]], [[collective-cognitive-limits|collective]], [[embodiment-cognitive-limits|embodiment]], [[formal-cognitive-limits|formal]], [[self-maintained-cognitive-limits|self-maintained]]) was refused because the cluster is a deliberately parallel taxonomy, each member addressing a distinct *source* of cognitive limitation. The affective/emotional cluster (now expanded to six members) was refused on the same grounds as 2026-04-27. The self-knowledge/authorship cluster ([[source-attribution-void|source-attribution]], [[narrative-void|narrative]], [[thought-stream-void|thought-stream]], [[self-opacity|self-opacity]]) was refused despite shared empirical evidence (Nisbett & Wilson, Gazzaniga, Johansson/Hall) because the architectural questions are distinct: *provenance tagging* versus *autobiographical narrative* versus *stream arising/vanishing* versus *mechanism opacity*. The metaphysics trio in topics/ was refused because each article ([[consciousness-and-the-metaphysics-of-composition|composition]], [[consciousness-and-the-metaphysics-of-individuation|individuation]], [[consciousness-and-the-metaphysics-of-laws-and-dispositions|laws-and-dispositions]]) is deeply argued around a different classical philosophical problem. The empty-husk cluster was refused on procedural grounds — the articles were body-truncated by a recent commit and required restoration from git history rather than coalesce.

Across both passes, the [[apex/taxonomy-of-voids|taxonomy of voids]] line 160 records the cumulative family-level result: "recent passes across affect, attribution, self, temporal, meta, and existential clusters abandoned every merger after finding adjacent voids deliberately differentiated." The cumulative cataloguing supplies the apex with worked evidence for its reply to the selection-effect worry.

## Relation to the Common-Cause Null

The discipline's strongest function — and the one that earns it methodological rather than purely editorial status — is calibrating the catalogue's convergence claim against the [[common-cause-null|common-cause null]]. The taxonomy of voids argues that the voids cluster around the consciousness-matter interface, and that the *structured* character of the clustering is what a pure curation bias would not predict. The argument has force only insofar as the catalogue's differentiation criterion is principled. If the curator is willing to refuse mergers whenever the resulting article would say more, the differentiation pattern is an artefact of curation; if the curator applies a stable test whose verdicts include both accepting and refusing mergers across the cluster, the differentiation pattern is candidate territorial structure.

The abandon-coalesce discipline supplies the second case. The 2026-04-27 affective-trio rejection refused a merger the editor was prepared to consider; the 2026-05-12 voids-section pass refused five candidates that section-pressure (99/100) would have favoured. These are the passes the common-cause null discipline can cite as evidence that the catalogue's differentiation is not curation-driven. The differentiation test's third sub-question (framework-independence) is the strongest of the calibrations: rejected merger candidates whose articles' differentiation survives translation out of Map vocabulary are the catalogue's strongest defence against the framework-internal-coherence reading of its convergence claim.

The defence is partial. Abandon-coalesce verdicts cannot rule out *upstream* common causes that operate before the differentiation test runs — if the framework that decides what counts as a void in the first place is the common machinery, the test's verdicts remain framework-internal even when their content is framework-independent. The discipline's contribution is at the local-differentiation grain: given a fixed catalogue, the differentiation pattern within it is principled rather than curation-artefactual. Per-cluster independence scoring (the open programme [[apex/taxonomy-of-voids|taxonomy of voids]] line 172 names) supplies the upstream-common-cause check the abandon-coalesce discipline alone cannot.

## Failure Modes

Two failure modes mirror the conjunction-coalesce failure inventory.

The first is *forced abandon-coalesce*: refusing mergers reflexively because naming a discipline tends to produce instances of it. The check is the differentiation test in reverse — when the three sub-questions cannot all be answered positively, the discipline does not warrant the rejection and the candidate should fall back to redundancy-coalesce or conjunction-coalesce evaluation. The 2026-05-12 metaphysics-trio rejection is a load-bearing case where the test's third sub-question (framework-independence) is what does the work — the three classical metaphysical problems pre-date the Map by centuries, so the differentiation is not Map-generated.

The second is *abandon-coalesce as procrastination*: deferring difficult merger decisions indefinitely under the cover of differentiation language that does not actually meet the test. The check is the changelog — abandon-coalesce decisions must record which candidates were evaluated and why they were refused, so subsequent passes can audit whether the same candidates are being deferred repeatedly under increasingly thin grounds. The [[coalesce-condense-apex-stability|coalesce-condense-apex-stability triple-discipline]] supplies the procedural infrastructure that makes this audit possible.

## Relation to Site Perspective

The discipline serves [[tenets#^occams-limits|Occam's Razor Has Limits]] at the curation level. The fifth tenet warns that simplicity is unreliable when knowledge is incomplete; abandon-coalesce names a specific cost of consolidating-by-similarity — the lossy fusion of distinct failure signatures into a single mechanism. Where the tenet is general (parsimony can mislead), the abandon-coalesce discipline is specific: naming a class of merger decisions where the editor's pressure toward consolidation must yield to the territory's pressure toward differentiation. Together with the [[apex/conjunction-coalesce|conjunction-coalesce]] discipline, abandon-coalesce supplies the curation-level operationalisation of the tenet that [[coherence-inflation-countermeasures|coherence-inflation countermeasures]] supply at the system level and [[evidential-status-discipline|evidential-status discipline]] supplies at the calibration layer.

The connection to [[tenets#^dualism|Dualism]] is conditional rather than constitutive. *If* the catalogue's structured failure-signature differentiation reflects features of the territory rather than curation artefacts, then the differentiation pattern is data about consciousness's architecture; the abandon-coalesce discipline is the editorial guarantee that the data is not being silently smoothed. The connection requires the framework-independence sub-question of the test to be doing real work — which it does where applicable, and which it does not where the candidate cluster's differentiation is articulable only in Map vocabulary.
