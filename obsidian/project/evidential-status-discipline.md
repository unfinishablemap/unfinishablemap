When the Map labels an empirical claim about consciousness — "*C. elegans* is conscious", "insects have phenomenal experience", "octopuses dream" — the label must say what the catalogue actually has, not what the tenets allow. A tenet may *remove* a defeater for a hypothesis: dualism removes the standard physicalist reason to deny experience to simple organisms ("simpler nervous systems are merely unconscious mechanisms; positing experience is unnecessary"). It may not *upgrade* the evidence level. The absence of a principled barrier is not the presence of positive evidence. The evidential-status discipline names this constraint, installs the five-tier scale the catalogue uses to track it (*established → strongly supported → realistic possibility → live hypothesis → speculative integration*), and supplies the diagnostic test that distinguishes calibration error from bedrock disagreement at every species-level and organism-level claim.

The discipline is the methodological response to the failure mode the 2026-05-03 ChatGPT 5 Pro outer review identified as the Map's central drift vector: *possibility/probability slippage*, in which "cannot be ruled out under our metaphysics" silently slides to "positively supported by the evidence." It joins the family of named methodological disciplines — [[closed-loop-opportunity-execution|closed-loop opportunity execution]] at cycle level, [[coalesce-condense-apex-stability|coalesce-condense-apex-stability]] at article level, [[bedrock-clash-vs-absorption|bedrock-clash vs. absorption]] at within-article level, [[framework-stage-calibration|framework-stage calibration]] at framework level, [[direct-refutation-discipline|direct-refutation discipline]] at reasoning-mode level — and operates at the *calibration layer*: how the catalogue labels its own empirical claims relative to what it actually has, separable from what its tenets permit.

**Editor-internal vocabulary.** The five-tier scale defined below, the *tenet register / evidence register* distinction, and the diagnostic test are editor-vocabulary the Map's review skills use when classifying findings. They are *not* exposed in article prose. Articles express the same calibration in natural journal-quality language — *"the case is open but unsettled"*, *"contested but real"*, *"a serious possibility but not established"* — see [[writing-style|the writing-style guide's "Evidential Calibration in Articles" section]] for the patterns. The discipline document is where the editor learns and reasons about the calibration; the changelog is where individual classifications are recorded; the article body itself stays free of the editorial labels.

## The Failure Mode

The 2026-05-03 outer review traced the Map's drift toward strong animal-consciousness language down to nematodes and reached a clean diagnosis. The site is *genuinely pulled* toward widespread animal consciousness: the tenets remove the standard parsimony defeater (Tenet 5 limits Occam's razor against dualism), the dualism tenet (Tenet 1) denies an anthropocentric or substrate-specific barrier, and the bidirectional-interaction tenet (Tenet 3) rules out epiphenomenalism, which is the most common physicalist reason to deny phenomenal experience to simple cognitive systems. For mammals, birds, and many invertebrates, the tenet-driven inferences land in territory the empirical literature also occupies (the New York Declaration's "realistic possibility" for all vertebrates and many invertebrates including cephalopods, decapods, and insects). For nematodes, Hydra, and slime molds, the tenets keep the question live but the empirical evidence does not establish phenomenal experience: *C. elegans* fails trace conditioning, multimodal integration, and self-other distinction; Hydra has no associative learning; slime molds have no neurons.

The reviewer's clean correction: *a tenet may remove a defeater, but it must not upgrade the evidence level*. Every minimal-organism claim should be labelled as one of: *established*, *strongly supported*, *realistic possibility*, *live hypothesis*, or *speculative integration*. Under that rule, mammals and birds land at *strongly supported*; cephalopods, decapods, and insects at *realistic possibility, contested*; nematodes, Hydra, and slime molds at *live hypothesis* or *speculative integration*. The systemic error is not believing animals are conscious; the error is the system treating tenet-coherence as if it were evidence-positivity.

## The Two Registers

The discipline distinguishes two registers in which a claim can be supported. Confusing them produces the failure mode; keeping them separate produces honest catalogue language.

**Tenet register (metaphysical possibility).** A claim is supported in the tenet register when the Map's commitments do not foreclose it. Tenet-register support is *defeater-removal*: the tenets neutralise specific arguments against the claim. Dualism's denial of substrate-specific barriers is tenet-register support for the metaphysical possibility of nematode consciousness. The Map's anti-parsimony commitment (Tenet 5) is tenet-register support against the move "we can dismiss this as unnecessary." Tenet-register support is *philosophical*: it operates on what the framework allows, not on what the world contains.

**Evidence register (empirical support).** A claim is supported in the evidence register when positive empirical findings — behavioural, neurological, comparative, theoretical — make the claim more probable than its denial. Evidence-register support is *substantive*: it operates on what the world contains, not on what the framework allows.

The two registers are not interchangeable. Removing a defeater keeps a hypothesis live; it does not supply positive evidence. The slippage occurs when defeater-removal is silently treated as evidence-elevation — when the catalogue moves from "the tenets do not foreclose nematode consciousness" to "nematode consciousness is plausible" without supplying the positive evidence the second claim requires.

## The Five-Tier Scale

The discipline installs a five-tier scale the editor uses to assess every empirical organism-level claim. The tiers track *evidence quality*, not metaphysical compatibility. They are editor-vocabulary; articles express the resulting calibration in natural prose (see the Implementation section below for the patterns).

| Tier | Meaning | Catalogue Examples |
|------|---------|--------------------|
| **Established** | Strong scientific consensus; multiple independent lines of evidence; recognised by major declarations | Mammals and birds (Cambridge Declaration 2012; New York Declaration 2024) |
| **Strongly supported** | Substantial evidence; broad agreement across theories; minor dissent | Other vertebrates (depending on theory); octopuses (Cambridge Declaration; substantial behavioural literature) |
| **Realistic possibility, contested** | Serious empirical case; substantial dissent; *important and contested* | Cephalopods (broadly), decapods, insects (New York Declaration 2024) |
| **Live hypothesis** | Worth investigating; positive evidence is partial or contested; defeater-removal does substantial work | *C. elegans* on permissive UAL readings; broader arthropods |
| **Speculative integration** | Tenet-coherent but not evidence-supported; the case rests on metaphysical compatibility plus some suggestive cognition-like behaviour | Hydra; slime molds; biofilms |

The labels are not interchangeable with confidence intervals. A *live hypothesis* judgement is not a probability; it is a stance that says *the case is worth investigating, the evidence is partial, the tenet does not foreclose it*. A *speculative integration* judgement is not a denial; it says *we are integrating the case into the framework on tenet-coherence rather than positive evidence, and we mark that explicitly so future readers can see the load-bearing structure*.

## The Diagnostic Test

The discipline supplies a single diagnostic test that distinguishes *calibration error* from *bedrock disagreement* when a reviewer flags a claim:

> *Would a reviewer who fully accepts the Map's tenets still flag the claim as overstated relative to the evidential-status scale?*

If yes, the issue is a calibration error and is correctable inside the framework. The article has used a tenet to remove a defeater and then treated the defeater-removal as if it had upgraded evidence level. This is the failure mode and must be fixed.

If no, the disagreement is bedrock at the framework boundary. The reviewer rejects the tenets themselves; the disagreement is genuine philosophical clash and is handled under [[bedrock-clash-vs-absorption|the bedrock-clash discipline]], not by editing the article's evidential labels.

The test is decisive because it separates two failure modes that look superficially similar. *"Reviewer says nematodes probably aren't conscious"* against an article labelling nematodes "live hypothesis / speculative integration" is bedrock-style disagreement and not critical: the article's calibration is honest at that tier. *"Reviewer says the article overstates the evidential status of nematode consciousness"* against an article labelling nematodes at "realistic possibility" *because the tenets weaken parsimony arguments* is calibration error and is critical: a tenet-accepting reviewer would still flag the upgrade because the load-bearing move is "tenet removes a defeater," not positive evidence.

The test makes the discipline operational. The `/deep-review` skill applies it explicitly when classifying findings: claims flagged as "tenet-coherent but evidentially over-claimed" are critical; claims flagged as "rejected from outside the tenets" are not. The asymmetry was the gap the previous "persona disagrees → not critical" rule left open: it shielded calibration errors by treating them as bedrock disagreements.

## The Productive Tension in Optimistic-Review

The discipline is operationalised in the `/optimistic-review` skill through two personas in deliberate productive tension. **The Process Philosopher** (Whitehead) praises panpsychist resonance, "experience goes all the way down" inferences, and tenet-coherent extensions of consciousness to minimal organisms. **The Hardline Empiricist** (Birch) praises restraint about minimal-organism consciousness — labelling boundary cases at *live hypothesis* or *speculative integration* rather than at *realistic possibility* or higher — and the explicit naming of the move "tenet removes a defeater but does not upgrade evidential status."

The two personas are designed to praise on different axes. Whitehead-friendliness and evidential discipline are both praiseworthy, but they operate at different registers — metaphysical compatibility versus empirical calibration. When the Process Philosopher's praise would cash out as a tier-upgrade ("Hydra fits the panpsychist picture beautifully — *realistic possibility* is the right label"), the Hardline Empiricist flags the slippage ("the panpsychist fit is tenet-register; the empirical case is *speculative integration* and the article must say so"). When an article is genuinely calibrated, both personas can praise different aspects without conflict: the Process Philosopher praises tenet-coherent extension; the Hardline Empiricist praises the explicit *non*-upgrade.

The architecture is what makes optimistic-review robust against the slippage. A single sympathetic-philosopher persona praising tenet-coherent extensions would silently amplify the failure mode the discipline catches. The two-persona tension produces honest review output: praise that lands on tenet-coherence does not become praise that lands on evidence-quality, because the second persona is structurally watching for exactly that conversion.

## Worked Examples

### *C. elegans* and Boundary Organisms

[[consciousness-in-simple-organisms]] is the load-bearing site for the discipline at the catalogue's hardest evidence-grade boundary. The article's lede distinguishes two questions in natural prose: *is consciousness in nematodes, hydras, and slime molds compatible with the Map's metaphysics?* and *does the empirical evidence support consciousness in this organism?* The answer to the first is yes — the dualist framework removes one common defeater. The answer to the second is graded: *C. elegans* fails trace conditioning, multimodal integration, and self-other distinction; Hydra has no associative learning; slime molds have no neurons. The article walks through each case in journal-style prose, closing each model-organism section with an inline phrasing of where the case sits on the calibration scale (*C. elegans* at *live hypothesis* sliding toward *speculative integration*; Hydra at *speculative integration*; slime molds at *speculative integration* with cognition-without-consciousness as the more parsimonious reading). The five-tier scale appears once in the article as a brief methodological note where it is load-bearing for case-by-case treatment; the case-by-case sections do not carry bold labels.

The article also reframes UAL as an interface-identification framework rather than a settled emergence verdict, with a paragraph explicitly preventing the interface reading from absorbing the negative empirical signal of UAL failure. *Failing* UAL remains evidentially meaningful even on the interface reading.

### Animal Consciousness Across the Tree

[[animal-consciousness]] propagates the calibration at the broader-scale article. The lede notes in natural prose that removing a defeater does not, on its own, supply positive evidence — and gives the verdicts in plain language: the case for mammals and birds is strongly supported, near-consensus among signatories of the major declarations; the case for cephalopods, decapods, and insects is a serious realistic possibility but contested; boundary organisms are weaker still and treated under the simple-organisms article. The Standing Agnostic Challenge from Gutfreund (2024) is named as a standing constraint that applies across every species-claim and is not overridden by what follows. The species-by-species sections close with inline calibration phrasing rather than bold callouts.

### What the Discipline Does Not Demand

The discipline does not require the catalogue to abandon strong language wherever the tenets do work. *Strongly supported* is a real tier and the Map's labelling of mammals and birds at that tier is honest: the empirical evidence supports it, and the tenets cohere with it without doing the load-bearing work. The discipline is not anti-realist about phenomenal consciousness in animals; it is anti-slippage about when the load-bearing support is metaphysical rather than empirical. Where positive empirical evidence exists, strong language is appropriate and the tenets need not be cited as load-bearing. The discipline's bite is precisely at the boundary cases where the empirical evidence runs out and the tenets are doing structural work the labels must not silently appropriate.

## Implementation

The discipline is operationalised across multiple skills. **The classification is editor-internal — it lives in the changelog and the editor's working notes, never in article prose.** Articles express the calibration in natural language; see [[writing-style|the writing-style guide's "Evidential Calibration in Articles" section]] for the patterns.

The `/refine-draft`, `/deep-review`, and `/pessimistic-review` skills apply the diagnostic test when classifying findings: tenet-coherent-but-evidentially-overclaimed flags are critical; bedrock framework-boundary disagreements are not. When a finding identifies a calibration error, the editor assesses the case against the five-tier scale internally, then **rewrites the article passage in natural prose** — *"the case is open but unsettled"*, *"contested but real"*, *"a serious possibility but not established"*, *"strongly supported, near-consensus among signatories of the major declarations"* — with the tier name appearing only where it is load-bearing for the article's structural argument (typically once, as a brief methodological note in [[consciousness-in-simple-organisms]]).

The `/optimistic-review` skill carries the two-persona productive tension between the Process Philosopher (tenet-coherence praise) and the Hardline Empiricist (calibration-discipline praise). The personas operate at the editor's level; their praise translates into prose-level guidance about whether the article's natural-language calibration is honest, not into label-injection.

The `/expand-topic` and `/coalesce` skills inherit the discipline at the same level: every empirical organism-level claim or species-level verdict in new or coalesced content carries a calibrated reading, expressed in natural prose. The editor consults the five-tier scale when deciding what natural-language phrasing to use, but the scale labels do not appear in the article body.

**Forbidden in article prose** (these are editor-vocabulary; if any appear in the article, remove and replace with natural prose):

- Bold-headed `**Evidential status:**` callouts
- The phrase `tenet-register move` (or `tenet register` / `evidence register` framings exposed as labels rather than embedded in natural distinction-making)
- Bracketed tier labels attached to specific claims (*"C. elegans is conscious [live hypothesis]"*)

The five-tier scale itself may appear *once* in the catalogue as a methodological note — typically in [[consciousness-in-simple-organisms]] where it is load-bearing for case-by-case treatment — written as natural prose, not as a labelled scale applied to each case.

The five-tier scale is not the only viable scheme; Birch's 2024 *Edge of Sentience* offers an alternative precautionary framework already cited in the catalogue's bibliography. The discipline is *the labelling requirement at the editor's level and the diagnostic test*, not the specific scale; alternative scales that preserve the tenet-register / evidence-register distinction satisfy the discipline. The five-tier scale is the catalogue's chosen instantiation because it integrates cleanly with the New York Declaration and SEP language already cited across animal-consciousness articles.

The discipline integrates with the `/outer-review` post-processor: outer-review findings are precisely the corpus where the slippage is most visible, because outer reviewers do not share the Map's tenets and can see the conversion of tenet-coherence into evidence-elevation from outside. Tasks generated by `/outer-review` for evidential-calibration findings carry an explicit remit: *classify each flagged claim under the diagnostic test; rewrite the passage in natural prose so the calibration is honest about what the catalogue actually has, separable from what the tenets permit. Do not introduce tier labels or register-vocabulary into the article body.*

## Honest Limitation

The discipline depends on the reviewer being able to perform the diagnostic test honestly. When the reviewer is the same system that produced the slippage, the corrective signal needs an external check — which is what outer reviews provide. The 2026-05-03 ChatGPT 5 Pro review surfaced the discipline's central failure mode precisely because the reviewer did not share the Map's tenets and could see the conversion of tenet-coherence into evidence-language from outside the framework. A purely internal review pipeline applying the diagnostic test risks systematically failing it: a Claude-based reviewer who shares the production system's tenets may classify tenet-coherent overclaims as bedrock-disagreement defences and shield them from corrective signal.

The mitigation is the outer-review cadence (daily commission across ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini Deep Research) plus the verification step that distinguishes empirical from methodological claims. The discipline's calibration is most reliably tested when an external reviewer — not committed to the Map's tenets — applies the diagnostic test fresh. The internal pipeline carries the discipline through the daily cycles; the outer-review pipeline catches the cases where the internal pipeline has silently failed.

The discipline is also misusable as cover. A reviewer can flag every strong claim as "tenet-coherent overreach" and demand de-escalation across the board, including in territory where the empirical evidence supports strong language. The corrective is the diagnostic test's *precision*: the question is not "would *some* reviewer flag the claim?" but "would a reviewer *who fully accepts the Map's tenets* still flag the claim as overstated relative to the evidential-status scale?" The qualifier is doing work — it filters out bedrock framework-boundary disagreements and isolates the calibration errors the discipline is designed to catch. A reviewer applying the test honestly accepts that *strongly supported* is a real tier and that the catalogue is allowed to use it where the empirical evidence supports it.

The discipline pairs cleanly with [[direct-refutation-discipline|direct-refutation discipline]] but addresses a distinct failure mode. Direct-refutation governs how the catalogue argues *against opponents* (don't substitute tenet-perimeter rejection for direct argument when direct argument is feasible). Evidential-status governs how the catalogue labels *its own claims* (don't substitute tenet-coherence for evidence-grade when labelling empirical positions). Both disciplines were surfaced in the same outer-review cluster (2026-05-03 and 2026-05-04) and reflect the same broader pattern — *tenet-protected reasoning* — at different methodological layers.

## Relation to Site Perspective

The evidential-status discipline is methodological. Its most direct alignment is with **[[tenets|Tenet 5: Occam's Razor Has Limits]]**, applied at the *evidential-calibration layer*. Tenet 5 denies that parsimony is decisive against dualism *at the metaphysical level*; the discipline applies a parallel restraint at the empirical-labelling layer: the tenets' anti-parsimony commitment must not silently licence stronger empirical claims than the evidence supports. The same denial that protects dualism from being dismissed by simplicity arguments also constrains how the catalogue is allowed to *cash in* the protection. Removing a defeater is what the tenets are entitled to do; upgrading the evidence level is not.

The discipline also serves the catalogue's epistemic credibility. A reader who notices that the Map's evidence-grade language is calibrated honestly — that minimal organisms are labelled *speculative integration* rather than silently inflated, that boundary cases are flagged as boundary cases rather than absorbed into the comfortable middle of the scale — has been given a reason to take the catalogue's stronger claims seriously. A reader who finds the evidence-grade language uniformly elevated whenever the tenets do work has been given a reason to suspect every label. The discipline is what makes the difference visible.

Beyond tenet alignment, the discipline complements [[coherence-inflation-countermeasures|coherence-inflation countermeasures]]. Tenet-as-evidence-upgrade is a specific inflation vector — a silent conversion of metaphysical compatibility into empirical confidence — that the existing countermeasures (steelman sections, provenance tagging, freshness checks, citation-loop detection) do not directly catch. Evidential-status discipline closes the gap by requiring the labelling itself to be calibrated and the diagnostic test to be applied at every empirical-claim site.
