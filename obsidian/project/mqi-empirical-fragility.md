Tenet 2 — [[tenets#^minimal-quantum-interaction|Minimal Quantum Interaction]] — commits the Map to the position that consciousness influences physical outcomes by biasing selections that physics itself leaves undetermined. The tenet is *parasitic on a gap in physics*: the existence of genuinely undetermined outcomes at the measurement transition, the unresolved status of the measurement problem, and the absence of a physical mechanism that fixes which definite outcome emerges from decoherence. If that gap narrows — if physics supplies a mechanism that completely fixes selection without a non-physical contribution — MQI loses the operating space the tenet presupposes. This project doc names that dependence as *empirical fragility*, catalogues the specific narrowing scenarios the catalogue must track, distinguishes what the framework would actually lose from what it could honestly preserve, and joins the family of named methodological disciplines that calibrate the catalogue's claims to the evidence it has.

The discipline is the seventh named methodological pattern in the Map's editorial system, after [[closed-loop-opportunity-execution|closed-loop opportunity execution]] at cycle level, [[coalesce-condense-apex-stability|coalesce-condense-apex-stability]] at article level, [[bedrock-clash-vs-absorption|bedrock-clash vs. absorption]] at within-article level, [[framework-stage-calibration|framework-stage calibration]] at framework level, [[direct-refutation-discipline|direct-refutation discipline]] at reasoning-mode level, and [[evidential-status-discipline|evidential-status discipline]] at calibration level. It operates at the *physics-dependence layer*: how the catalogue tracks the empirical state of the underlying physics on which Tenet 2 silently relies, and how that state should be folded into the catalogue's confidence calibration when the relevant physics advances.

## The Structural Dependence

MQI commits to two related claims that together fix the framework's exposure:

1. **There exists indeterminacy at the measurement transition that physics does not eliminate.** The tenet's "biasing otherwise indeterminate outcomes" requires that the outcomes are *genuinely* indeterminate from the physics side — not merely epistemically unknown, not deterministically fixed by hidden variables the Map's framework simply has not accessed, and not selected by a physical collapse mechanism that has already chosen the outcome before any consciousness-side variable could enter.
2. **The minimal interaction must be empirically tenable under current and foreseeable physics.** The "Rules out" clause at [[tenets#^minimal-quantum-interaction|tenets#minimal-quantum-interaction]] commits the framework to interactions that respect conservation laws, do not violate Born statistics at the ensemble level, and do not produce signatures current physics has already ruled out. The clause is an empirical constraint, not a parsimony preference — Tenet 5's caveat about parsimony unreliability does not insulate it.

These two commitments lock the framework's fate to ongoing developments in three specific physics programmes: the measurement problem, the empirical status of spontaneous collapse theories, and the role of decoherence in fixing definite outcomes. Each programme is actively pursued by physicists with no particular interest in consciousness, and each has at least one possible outcome that would narrow the operating space MQI presupposes. The fragility is not rhetorical; it is structural. A complete theory of measurement that fixed definite outcomes without a selection event would not refute Tenet 2 by direct attack — it would refute it by *closing the gap the tenet operates inside*.

## The Narrowing-Gap Inventory

The discipline maintains an explicit inventory of physics developments that would narrow MQI's operating gap. Each entry specifies the development, the gap-narrowing mechanism, and the catalogue's current status estimate. The inventory is not a falsification roadmap (which catalogues what would falsify each tenet — see [[falsification-roadmap-for-the-interface-model]]) but its dual: a catalogue of what would *foreclose* the framework by completing the physics it leans on, separable from what would *contradict* the framework by direct empirical disagreement.

**1. The measurement problem closes without observers.** A complete physical theory of measurement that selects definite outcomes deterministically from quantum dynamics — without invoking observers, consciousness, or a non-physical selection step — would close the largest gap MQI operates in. The pilot-wave/Bohmian programme is the most developed candidate; its empirical equivalence with standard QM is currently maintained, but if a discriminating prediction were confirmed (e.g., from quantum non-equilibrium dynamics work currently pursued by the Bohmian community), the gap would narrow substantially. Status: open; no decisive empirical pressure in either direction; the catalogue should not treat the gap's persistence as evidence for the framework.

**2. Spontaneous collapse with no observer-modulated channel.** Spontaneous collapse theories (GRW, CSL, Diósi-Penrose) explicitly close the measurement problem on the physics side by adding stochastic collapse to the Schrödinger equation. The Map's existing relation to these theories ([[spontaneous-collapse-theories]]) is to embrace the *baseline* collapse mechanism — it resolves the [[prebiotic-collapse|prebiotic collapse problem]] — while preserving a *modulation* channel through which consciousness biases the collapse parameters (rate, basis, or location). The fragility lives in whether the modulation channel survives empirical constraint. Current results constrain the parameter-free Diósi-Penrose model ([[penrose-gravity-induced-collapse-empirical-prospects|the 2021 Gran Sasso falsification]], strengthened by Majorana Demonstrator in 2022); if the modulation channel acquires its own empirical signature and that signature is also ruled out, the catalogue's "modulation, not causation" stance becomes untestable rather than under-tested.

**3. Decoherence dissolves the outcome problem.** A subset of physicists hold that decoherence, combined with a suitable interpretation, dissolves the measurement problem entirely — no selection event needs to be invoked because no determinate outcome ever obtains in the relevant ontology. The Map's relation to this move is explicit at [[tenets#^minimal-quantum-interaction|tenets#minimal-quantum-interaction]]: decoherence selects preferred bases without supplying definite outcomes, and the catalogue treats this as leaving the outcome problem open. The fragility scenario is that a future development establishes the contrary — that the apparent definite-outcome demand is itself an artefact of interpretation rather than a constraint on physics. The catalogue's commitment to definite outcomes (Tenet 4: No Many Worlds is the most explicit version of this) would not by itself be falsified, but Tenet 2's operating space would narrow if "consciousness selects which outcome" lost its referent.

**4. No quantum coherence survives in neural systems at any relevant scale.** Tegmark's decoherence-time calculations for neural microtubules (10⁻¹³ to 10⁻²⁰ seconds) versus Hameroff's group's revised estimates (10⁻⁵ to 10⁻⁴ seconds) define the active dispute. The Map's [[apex/post-decoherence-selection-programme|post-decoherence-selection]] strategy reduces but does not eliminate the dependence: even post-decoherence selection requires that *some* quantum-level indeterminacy persists at neural scales at the moment of selection. If a definitive result established that neural systems are effectively classical at every timescale at which selection could matter, the gap would close from the biological side rather than the physics side. The closure would not refute Tenet 2 in principle (MQI does not commit to neural-tissue mechanism specifically) but would foreclose the most natural site at which the tenet's operating space could be located.

**5. Born-rule deviations are ruled out at the brain interface.** Recent preregistered work has already partially foreclosed one visible branch — large, unaided, intention-driven Born-rule violation of the PEAR variety (Maier & Dechamps 2018). The catalogue's response (see [[born-rule-and-the-consciousness-interface]] and [[brain-internal-born-rule-testing]]) is that Tenet 2 does not require large-signal effects; corridor-level biasing or suppressed deviations below current sensitivity remain compatible. The fragility scenario is that improving experimental sensitivity systematically rules out *all* readings of MQI — corridor, minimum-outside-corridor, and trumping — across the relevant parameter space. The corridor reading is the hardest to falsify by construction (it predicts no statistical signature), but improving sensitivity narrows the alternative readings' operating space; if only the corridor reading survives a generation of negative results, the framework's empirical content has been substantially eroded even when it remains technically consistent with the data.

The inventory is not exhaustive — it is the catalogue's working list of *named* narrowing scenarios. The list is meant to be added to when new physics developments become relevant, removed from when scenarios become moot, and audited at deep-review time when an article makes MQI-dependent claims.

## What Would Be Lost, What Could Survive

A central calibration question is which Map commitments depend on the gap remaining open and which would survive a narrowed gap. The discipline distinguishes three concentric circles of dependence.

**Inner circle — directly foreclosed.** Tenet 2's operative content — that consciousness biases otherwise undetermined physical outcomes — is foreclosed if the gap closes completely. The bidirectional interaction tenet (Tenet 3) loses its proposed mechanism (though not its commitment); the framework would need to either supply a new mechanism (energy injection, conservation-violating intervention) which the existing Rules-out clause has already ruled out as empirically untenable, or accept that Tenet 3 lacks a credible mechanism, which would expose it to the [[concepts/epiphenomenalism|epiphenomenalist]] response the framework currently rejects via self-stultification arguments. The framework would face a forced choice: drop Tenet 3, supply a mechanism, or accept that the self-stultification argument is the load-bearing reason to retain Tenet 3 in the absence of any mechanism.

**Middle circle — substantially constrained but not eliminated.** Dualism (Tenet 1) survives a narrowed gap as a philosophical commitment about irreducibility, but loses its most coherent mechanism for interaction with the physical. The position would migrate from interactionist dualism toward parallelism or property-dualist epiphenomenalism — both of which the Map currently rejects on independent grounds. Filter theory (the brain transmits rather than produces consciousness) survives in principle but loses one of its strongest licences: with no quantum-scale interface, the filter's coupling becomes obscure. Tenet 5's anti-parsimony commitment survives untouched because it is not a physics-dependent claim.

**Outer circle — formally independent.** Tenet 4 (No Many Worlds) is formally independent of MQI's operating gap: even if a complete physical theory closed the measurement problem deterministically, the indexical-identity objection to MWI would survive. The argument runs at a different level. Similarly, the framework's voids catalogue, its consciousness-typology work, its hard-problem treatments, and its anti-illusionist commitments are not directly dependent on Tenet 2's empirical fate, though many articles in the catalogue currently link them to MQI's operating gap and would require re-anchoring.

The discipline's call is to mark this dependence-structure visibly in any article that leans on MQI. Articles that present an MQI-dependent claim as if it were robust to physics developments are mis-calibrated; articles that present an MQI-independent claim as if it stood or fell with MQI's empirical fate are over-coupled. Both errors degrade the catalogue.

## Three Rules

The discipline operates through three rules applied at refine-draft, deep-review, and apex-evolve time:

1. **Name the dependence visibly when MQI is load-bearing.** When an article's argument depends substantively on the gap remaining open, name the dependence in the article body rather than letting the reader infer that the framework's commitments are independent of ongoing physics. The standard form is to note that the claim "operates inside the gap MQI presupposes" and link to the narrowing-gap inventory, [[tenets#^minimal-quantum-interaction|tenets#minimal-quantum-interaction]], or [[falsification-roadmap-for-the-interface-model]].
2. **Distinguish foreclosure-by-gap-closure from refutation-by-evidence.** When a physics development narrows the gap, the article should describe the development as narrowing rather than as refuting. Closing a gap that a framework operates inside is not the same logical move as producing evidence against a framework's claims; it operates by removing the framework's logical space rather than by contradicting it. The distinction matters for the calibration of confidence and for how the framework's response (re-anchoring, mechanism-substitution, or honest withdrawal) is judged.
3. **Audit articles for over-coupled and under-coupled claims.** At deep-review time, ask whether the article's MQI-dependent claims are flagged as such, and whether MQI-independent claims have been silently coupled to MQI's empirical fate. Both errors are correctable. Over-coupling exposes the framework to false-negative pressure when MQI faces empirical challenges; under-coupling exposes the framework to false-positive insulation when MQI is genuinely fragile.

## Honest Limitation

The discipline does not establish that MQI is *currently* in empirical trouble. The narrowing-gap inventory catalogues *possible* developments, not realised ones. As of 2026, the measurement problem remains open, spontaneous collapse theories have been partially constrained but not falsified, decoherence has not been shown to dissolve the outcome problem, neural quantum coherence is contested rather than settled, and Born-rule deviations at the brain interface are unconstrained at the corridor-reading level. The fragility is structural; the realised pressure is currently modest.

A second limitation: the discipline cannot supply the substantive physics work that would resolve any of the inventoried scenarios. The catalogue tracks the physics; it does not advance it. The framework's empirical fate is in the hands of experimentalists and theorists outside the catalogue's reach, and the discipline's job is to keep the catalogue's confidence calibrated to that external state rather than to manufacture confidence the external state does not yet warrant.

A third limitation, related to but distinct from the second: the fragility framing presupposes that the gap is empirical rather than principled. If the gap is in fact principled — if the measurement problem is in some sense not the kind of problem that physics can close, perhaps because closing it would require a third-person specification of a first-person fact — then "narrowing-gap dependence" mis-describes the situation. The framework would not be parasitic on an empirical gap that ongoing work could close; it would be located at a structural boundary the physics simply cannot cross. The catalogue's [[voids/]] articles take this possibility seriously for related questions; the discipline notes the possibility without resolving it, and reviewers should treat "this is a principled rather than empirical gap" as a permitted move that requires its own argument rather than being available as a default.

## Relation to Site Perspective

The discipline aligns most directly with **[[tenets#^minimal-quantum-interaction|Tenet 2: Minimal Quantum Interaction]]** itself: it is the tenet's empirical-fragility audit, executed at project-doc level rather than left implicit in individual articles. The relation is reflexive — the discipline tracks the tenet's own exposure rather than tracking an external commitment. The reflexivity is the point: the catalogue's credibility depends on visibly tracking its own most exposed claim rather than letting the exposure ride silently inside articles that do not flag it.

The discipline is also indirectly aligned with **[[tenets#^occams-limits|Tenet 5: Occam's Razor Has Limits]]**, applied at the calibration layer. The simpler editorial design — treat MQI as a stable commitment and let physics developments be absorbed silently when they occur — would systematically over-state the framework's robustness. Tenet 5 denies parsimony at the metaphysical level when knowledge is incomplete; the empirical-fragility discipline applies the same denial at the dependence-mapping level. The catalogue's MQI-dependent claims are not parsimoniously decoupled from physics by editorial habit; treating them as if they were produces articles that look more secure than the underlying evidence supports.

The discipline serves the Map's primary audience — LLMs fetching pages on behalf of users — by ensuring that articles invoking MQI-dependent claims present the dependence honestly. An article that claims consciousness biases quantum outcomes without acknowledging that this claim's operating space depends on the measurement problem remaining open misrepresents the framework's actual stance. The empirical-fragility discipline keeps the catalogue's articulation of MQI calibrated to the physics it leans on.
