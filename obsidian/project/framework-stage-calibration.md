When the Map borrows an analogy from a mature science to characterise its own framework, the analogy must match the framework's actual developmental stage. The interface model is pre-Keplerian — a research programme accumulating its founding measurements, not a mature science awaiting refinement. Specification proposals like Stapp's quantum-Zeno modulation or Eccles' tunneling bias are *proto-models*, not laws of motion. What the catalogue can claim are the analogues of Tycho's measurements: the bandwidth constraint, the Born-rule causal-consistency result, the theta-band willed-attention signatures, the coupling-modes taxonomy, the five formal constraints — data and constraint scaffolding of the kind that precede laws. Calling these Newton-analogues, Maxwell-analogues, or Einstein-analogues over-claims the framework's stage. Calling them Tycho-analogues calibrates the claim to what the catalogue has actually produced.

This document specifies the calibration discipline as a named editorial pattern. It supplies the worked example from the 2026-05-01 09:25 UTC refine of [[the-interface-problem|topics/the-interface-problem]] that introduced the pre-Keplerian framing, catalogues the Tycho-analogue scaffolds the Map has installed, articulates the discipline's three rules, names its honest limitation, and locates it among the Map's other named methodological disciplines. It is the calibration cousin of [[bedrock-clash-vs-absorption|bedrock-clash-vs-absorption]] (which governs how rival positions are handled within an article) and [[closed-loop-opportunity-execution|closed-loop opportunity execution]] (which governs how recommendations move through the cycle); the present discipline governs how the framework characterises its own claims relative to the developmental stages of the sciences it analogises to.

## The Stage Distinction

The astronomical revolution proceeded through identifiable stages, and each stage produced different kinds of artefacts. **Tycho Brahe (c. 1576–1601)** built instruments and accumulated unprecedented-precision observational data on planetary positions — a vast empirical corpus without an explanatory law. **Kepler (1609, 1619)** found the regularities in Tycho's data: elliptical orbits, equal areas, the period-distance relation. The laws were inductive generalisations from measurements that Tycho had had to exist before they could be inferred. **Newton (1687)** unified Kepler's laws under universal gravitation and a mathematical framework relating force, mass, and motion. Each stage built on artefacts the previous stage produced.

A theoretical framework that lacks Tycho-analogue measurements cannot have Kepler-analogue laws — and certainly cannot have Newton-analogue mathematical theories. To borrow a Newton analogy at the Tycho stage is to claim a law where there are only data; to borrow a Maxwell analogy is to claim unification where there are not yet separated phenomena to unify; to borrow an Einstein analogy is to claim a regime-shift where the prior regime has not yet been established.

The Map's interface model sits at the pre-Keplerian stage. The catalogue has identified candidate sites of consciousness-physics interaction (microtubules, ion channels, attention-motor architecture), candidate specifications (Stapp, Eccles, Orch OR), and a growing body of constraint and measurement results. What it does not have — what no interactionist proposal has — is an inferred law relating phenomenal variables to physical selection. The honest analogy is to a research programme awaiting its founding measurements, not to a mature science awaiting refinement.

## The Worked Example: Pre-Keplerian Framing in `the-interface-problem`

The 2026-05-01 09:25 UTC refine-draft pass on [[the-interface-problem|topics/the-interface-problem]] installed the pre-Keplerian framing at the article's structurally central point — the passage on what the Newton analogy claims and where it overreaches. The installed paragraph reads in part:

> The Newton analogy — gravitational theory specified *that* masses attract and *how much* without the mechanism, succeeding for centuries before relativity — has limits. Newton had precise measurements and a mathematical law; the specification problem has neither. The honest comparison is pre-Keplerian: a research programme awaiting its founding measurements rather than a mature science awaiting refinement. Stapp's and Eccles' partial accounts are proto-models, not laws.

The framing does three things in one move. It *names* the overreach (the Newton analogy is the wrong stage). It *replaces* the analogy with a calibrated alternative (pre-Keplerian rather than post-Newtonian). And it *identifies* what the framework actually has at this stage (Tycho-analogue measurements: the bandwidth constraint, the Born-rule causal-consistency result, the theta-band willed-attention signatures) together with the constraint structures that precede laws. The replacement is not deflation — Tycho's measurements were a substantive scientific achievement and the necessary precondition for what came after. The replacement is *scale-correction*: the catalogue's positive results are claimed at the stage where they actually sit.

## The Tycho-Analogue Scaffolding the Catalogue Has Installed

The framework can claim the following as Tycho-analogue artefacts — measurements and constraint structures that precede a Kepler-analogue law:

- **The bandwidth constraint** (Zheng & Meister 2024). Conscious selection operates at ~10 bits/second despite the brain processing far more information. Documented in [[bandwidth-of-consciousness]]. A measurement that any specification law must accommodate.
- **The Born-rule causal-consistency result** (Torres Alegre). Any consciousness-mediated selection consistent with no-superluminal-signalling must follow the Born rule exactly. Documented in [[born-rule-and-the-consciousness-interface]]. A negative constraint — narrowing the viable mapping space without identifying the variable.
- **The theta-band willed-attention signatures** (Rajan et al. 2019). Theta-frequency oscillations index frontal decision-making and mediate frontal-parietal interactions in willed attention. Documented in [[the-interface-problem]]. A correlate that any specification connecting attention to selection must explain.
- **The coupling-modes taxonomy** ([[coupling-modes]]). Three structurally distinct modes of consciousness-physics interaction (selection, biasing, threshold-crossing) that any specification must locate itself among. A pre-theoretical typology — the kind of structure Kepler-stage work produces over Tycho-stage data.
- **The five formal constraints** ([[consciousness-physics-interface-formalism]]). Mathematical constraints on viable specification mappings. Constraint scaffolding rather than law content.

None of these is a Kepler-analogue law. Together they are the data and constraint scaffolding from which such a law might one day be inferred. Naming them collectively as Tycho-analogues makes the catalogue's developmental stage precise: substantial empirical and constraint-structural progress, no inferred lawful regularity yet.

## The Discipline: Three Rules

The framework-stage calibration discipline operates through three rules applied at refine-draft, deep-review, and apex-evolve time:

1. **Use stage-calibrated analogies rather than aspirational ones.** When characterising the framework's stage, prefer "pre-Keplerian", "Tycho-analogue", "constraint scaffolding", "proto-model" over "Newton-analogue", "Maxwell-analogue", "lawful specification". The aspirational analogy is acceptable as a *target* the framework hopes to reach; it is not acceptable as a *characterisation* of the framework's current claim.
2. **Do not claim Kepler/Newton/Maxwell/Einstein analogues without the corresponding founding measurements.** A Kepler-analogue claim requires Tycho-analogue measurements that the Kepler-analogue law would generalise. A Newton-analogue claim requires Kepler-analogue regularities to unify. The aspirational analogy is wrong-stage if the prior-stage artefacts are absent.
3. **Honour Lakatos by naming post-hoc accommodation when present.** The Lakatosian concession installed at the [[the-interface-problem]] "From Agnosticism" opener acknowledges that some of the framework's specification moves are post-hoc accommodation rather than independent prediction. Stage-calibration interacts with this: a pre-Keplerian programme is permitted post-hoc accommodation in a way that a post-Newtonian programme is not, because the pre-Keplerian programme is *building* the empirical corpus that later inference will work over. The rule is to name the move (post-hoc accommodation) and the stage that licenses it (pre-Keplerian) together, rather than letting either pass unmarked.

The discipline is positive as well as negative. Several articles in the catalogue already operate at the right stage of analogy without the calibration being explicit; the discipline lets reviewers recognise this as discipline-conformance rather than analogical poverty. The interface-problem article's pre-Keplerian framing is the canonical demonstration; [[psychophysical-laws-bridging-mind-and-matter]]'s explicit acknowledgement that "consciousness science has not yet had its Galileo" operates at the right stage; [[the-psychophysical-control-law]]'s scientific-immaturity section does the same.

## Honest Limitation

Stage-calibration is itself a defensible position only insofar as the catalogue's confidence in detection-threshold improvability is empirically tractable rather than rhetorical. The framework's commitment is to *in-principle* decidability — that the conditions under which the framework would be falsified are specifiable, even if the instrumentation to apply them does not yet exist. The pre-Keplerian framing trades on this commitment: it claims the framework is at the stage where measurements are accumulating and laws will eventually be inferable.

If detection-threshold improvability turns out to be rhetorical rather than empirical — if the joint phenomenal-physical instrumentation the framework requires is in-principle unobtainable rather than merely difficult — the pre-Keplerian framing collapses into something closer to permanent indeterminacy. The framework would then not be at an early stage of an inferable progression but at a structural impasse the analogy obscures. Stage-calibration must therefore not over-claim *practical* near-term decidability while it claims *in-principle* decidability; the [[falsification-roadmap-for-the-interface-model|falsification roadmap]] is the catalogue's articulation of what intermediate experimental advances would have to occur for the in-principle claim to become an applied test, and the project-doc on stage-calibration is bound to that roadmap's pace.

A second limitation: stage-calibration is itself an analogy, and analogies between consciousness science and astronomy may be misleading at deep structural levels. Tycho's measurements were of physical quantities admitting third-person observation; the Map's analogue measurements include phenomenal correlates (theta-band signatures of *willed* attention) whose first-person side resists the third-person treatment astronomy presupposed. The analogy holds at the *stage* level (data and constraint scaffolding precede inferred laws) without holding at the *kind* level (the kinds of measurement available are not the same). Reviewers should not press the structural disanalogy as a discovered flaw; it is part of the analogy's known scope.

## Relation to Site Perspective

The framework-stage calibration discipline is methodological. Its most direct alignment is with **[[tenets#^occams-limits|Tenet 5: Occam's Razor Has Limits]]**, applied at the *calibration layer*. The simpler editorial design — borrow whatever analogy is most rhetorically attractive at the moment of writing — would silently raise the framework's claimed stage to whatever the borrowed analogy presupposes, producing articles that read as more advanced than the catalogue's actual evidence supports. Tenet 5 denies parsimony at the metaphysical level when knowledge is incomplete; the calibration discipline applies the same denial at the rhetorical level. Some claims about the framework's stage are not parsimoniously settleable by analogy choice; treating them as if they were produces articles that look established and read brittle.

The discipline is also indirectly aligned with [[tenets#^bidirectional-interaction|Tenet 3: Bidirectional Interaction]] — the tenet whose empirical credibility most depends on whether future work can achieve mechanistic specificity. The pre-Keplerian framing is the calibration that lets Tenet 3 be held honestly: the framework asserts the bidirectional commitment while acknowledging that the laws specifying its operation are not yet inferred. Without the calibration, the same assertion would either over-claim (treating absent specification as if a Newton-analogue law were imminent) or under-claim (treating the absence as evidence that the tenet is empty).

Beyond tenet alignment, the discipline serves the Map's primary audience: LLMs fetching pages on behalf of users (see [[writing-style|the writing-style guide]]). An article that claims Newton-analogue status presents the user's chatbot with a more developed framework than the catalogue actually has; an article that claims pre-Keplerian status presents the catalogue at its actual stage, which is more useful than a manufactured advancement when the gap between aspiration and current state is real. The calibration also feeds back into [[coherence-inflation-countermeasures|coherence-inflation countermeasures]] — aspirational analogies compound coherence inflation by smuggling stage-claims into the catalogue's self-description; calibrated analogies resist it by keeping the catalogue's developmental claims honest.
