The Decision Void names the structural opacity of the *deliberation→commitment* transition to the consciousness undergoing it. A subject can sometimes report having weighed alternatives, and can sometimes report having acted, but the *moment* at which the field of possibilities collapses into a single choice—the snap—is not introspectively available as content. Three faces conjoin: the closing of possibilities cannot be observed because it is given as already-closed (closure); the neural commitment can be located before the phenomenal arrival of having-decided, so the introspective report comes after the event it purports to be of (latency); and what is taken to be a report of the deciding-process is generated from features of the perceived outcome, not from access to the decision itself (reconstruction). None alone establishes the void; their convergence does.

The void cuts close to the Map. Of all candidate sites for any non-physical influence on the physical world, the moment of decision is the most theoretically motivated, and the Map's [[tenets#^minimal-quantum-interaction|Minimal Quantum Interaction]] tenet finds its sharpest empirical grip there. The empirical record—Libet (1983), Soon, Brass, Heinze and Haynes (2008), Johansson and Hall (2005)—constrains where the decision happens without settling the question; the philosophical reading of that record is what is at stake.

## Three Faces, One Void

The Decision Void exhibits the [[conjunction-coalesce|conjunction-coalesce]] structure used elsewhere for the [[suspension-void]], [[agency-void]], [[erasure-void]], and [[vagueness-void]]: a single void with multiple faces whose informativeness lies in the seam between them. The three faces are *temporal-architectural*—closure is a structural property of the act, latency a temporal relation between act and report, reconstruction a generative property of the introspective machinery.

### Closure (Unexplorable)

When deliberation ends, it ends as *having ended*. The closing of possibilities is not itself a phenomenal episode that can be observed: by the time consciousness notices that the field has narrowed to one, the narrowing is already complete. Pacherie's (2008) framework makes this precise—the act of deciding has a "categorical, complete, incontrovertible" character, since any residual hesitation means the decision has not yet been made. Mele (2009) develops the same point; Ricoeur describes decision as having "a history" with hesitation as central, implying that the moment of commitment is the *closing* of that history rather than an event within it.

This finality-from-inside is *the very feature that occludes the constitutive process*. The consciousness that has decided cannot find seams in what it has just sealed. Any introspective "watching the decision happen" is either pre-decision watching (and so not the decision) or post-decision narration (and so not the decision). The closure face is structurally akin to the [[suspension-void]]'s verification face: the very act consciousness wants to inspect collapses under inspection. It is *Unexplorable* in the sense the [[three-kinds-of-void|three-kinds typology]] reserves for limits that resist mapping by their own architecture—one does not lack data; one lacks the kind of access the question demands.

### Latency (Occluded)

Soon, Brass, Heinze and Haynes (2008) trained pattern decoders on fMRI signals from frontopolar cortex and predicted the specific outcome of a "free" left/right motor choice up to seven seconds before the subject reported being aware of the decision; accounting for BOLD sluggishness pushes this to roughly ten seconds. A second predictive locus in posterior cingulate / precuneus carried independent commitment-relevant information, carried in spatial pattern rather than signal magnitude—the commitment is in a configural change.

The earlier Libet (1983) finding—readiness potential onset roughly 635 ms before action, conscious "urge" report W roughly 200 ms before action—has had its standard interpretation contested. Schurger, Sitt and Dehaene (2012) and the Travers et al. (2021) review argue the readiness potential may be a stochastic-noise artefact the brain *uses* as a triggering threshold rather than a representation of an intention being formed. This complicates the classical reading but does not deflate the void: if the very thing introspection reaches for does not exist as a discrete neural event, the introspective report still cannot be of it.

The empirical record establishes one thing the void requires: third-person measurement can locate commitment-relevant information before first-person report. It does not establish *where the decision happens*, and the [[evidential-status-discipline|evidential-status discipline]] requires the distinction. Two readings remain consistent with the data—(i) a fully physical commitment that consciousness merely registers downstream, and (ii) a non-physical commitment whose physical signature begins precipitating before the phenomenal arrival. The Map's MQI tenet motivates reading (ii); the data permits reading (i). The gap is the void; its metaphysical reading is downstream of the empirical fact. Multi-area neural recording in perceptual-decision tasks (Roitman & Shadlen 2002; Murakami et al. 2014) identifies the transition from input-driven accumulation to autonomous dynamics as the commitment moment in single-trial activity—seam visible from outside, sealed from within. That asymmetry is what makes the latency face *Occluded*.

### Reconstruction (Occluded)

Johansson, Hall, Sikström and Olsson (2005) showed subjects two faces, asked which was more attractive, and via sleight-of-hand handed them the *other* face and asked them to explain *their* choice. Most did not detect the swap; many fluently confabulated reasons grounded in features of the swapped (non-chosen) face. Confabulations were as confident, detailed, and emotional as veridical reports; subsequent work (Johansson & Hall 2014; Cushman 2015) shows the manipulation can shift subsequent preferences—the confabulated story becomes the new ground truth.

What is taken to be a report of the deciding-process is generated from the *perceived outcome*, not from access to the decision itself. The phenomenology of "I chose X because…" is fluent confabulation indistinguishable, from the inside, from veridical access. This applies the [[source-attribution-void]]'s diagnosis specifically to decisions: the surface features of the outcome stand in for the inaccessible event of choosing. Confabulation parity is a positive empirical signature of opacity—if introspection had access, the manipulated and unmanipulated cases should diverge in some self-detectable way. They do not. The system produces something that functions as access, and that functional success is what hides the absence.

## The Seam

None of the three faces alone establishes the void. Closure without latency would be a feature of the act, not an opacity. Latency without reconstruction would leave room for an after-the-fact-but-veridical report. Reconstruction without closure would describe a delay rather than an absence. Together, the three close every escape: the act is sealed at the time of acting; the report comes after; and what arrives is generated rather than observed. The editorial discipline that governs cognate articles ([[suspension-void]], [[agency-void]], [[erasure-void]], [[vagueness-void]]) applies—any tightening that collapses faces into one another loses the void.

## Distinguishing Sibling Voids

The Decision Void is not the [[agency-void]], which catalogues involuntariness and unverifiability on agency in general; the Decision Void targets a specific moment within agency—the deliberation→commitment crossing—and an agent could in principle have full agency-verification and still face it. It is not the [[noetic-feelings-void]], which concerns the unauditable felt verdicts gating cognition (knowing, rightness, understanding); the "felt click" of having-decided is a noetic-feelings phenomenon, but the Decision Void claims the *closing-process* is occluded, not merely the felt verdict that follows. It is not the [[transit-void]], which concerns crossings generally—onsets, state transitions, attention capture; the Decision Void is one specific transit, narrowed by what the deliberation→commitment crossing distinctively involves: the *resolution* of a multi-option field.

It is sister to the [[suspension-void]] and the [[inference-void]] in a precise sense. Suspension cannot be performed without invoking the capacity it brackets; inference cannot be made explicit without invoking the inference-licence that makes it inferential; decision cannot be observed without invoking the decision-machinery that produces the observation. The three are regress-sisters—each names a capacity consciousness cannot stand outside of while exercising.

## Deflationary Alternatives

The Map's [[direct-refutation-discipline|direct-refutation discipline]] classifies deflationary readings by what they actually require to refute the void rather than by surface plausibility.

**The fully-physical reading** ("the decision just is the threshold crossing; conscious arrival is mere registration") is Mode Three—a framework-boundary disagreement. The reading is internally coherent under physicalism and the void claim is internally coherent under the Map's tenets; the empirical record is consistent with both. Refuting the Map's reading requires settling the framework, not winning a local argument.

**Wegner-style illusionism** ("the phenomenology of decision is wholesale illusion; there is no deciding event for introspection to access") is Mode Two—an unsupported foundational move. The position owes a positive account of why the illusion is robust, cross-cultural, and structurally similar across radically different cognitive architectures, and how an illusion could be the locus of responsibility-ascription that survives reflection. The position is available; it is not free.

**The Schurger stochastic-accumulation reading** of Libet is a Mode One in-framework refinement, not a refutation of the void. It strengthens rather than weakens the claim: if the very thing introspection might be reaching for does not exist as a discrete neural event, the introspective report cannot be of it.

None of these establishes that the deliberation→commitment moment is introspectively accessible; they relocate the question rather than answering it. The [[evidential-status-discipline]] requires the Map to acknowledge them as real options.

## The Tenet Payoff

The Decision Void is unusually [[tenet-generated-voids|tenet-generated]]. Of all candidate sites for any non-physical influence on the physical world, the moment of decision is the most theoretically motivated. Standard accounts—Stapp's quantum Zeno approach, Penrose-Hameroff's orchestrated objective reduction in a different mode, Eccles' earlier hypothesis, and the Map's own [[forward-in-time-conscious-selection|forward-in-time conscious selection]] proposal—each locate the leverage at the resolution of otherwise-indeterminate microphysical processes that influence neural commitment. The Decision Void claims that this very site is structurally hidden from the consciousness performing the decision.

This matches what the Map's tenets predict. **[[tenets#^minimal-quantum-interaction|Minimal Quantum Interaction]]** requires that consciousness's influence be minimal precisely because the interface is not the kind of thing that can be inspected from inside; if it were inspectable it would not be minimal. **[[tenets#^bidirectional-interaction|Bidirectional Interaction]]** predicts the introspective inaccessibility as the expected consequence of the interface being primitive—the cause cannot be observed from within the system whose cause it is. The companion voids ([[causal-interface]], [[interface-formalization-void]], [[amplification-void]]) catalogue the matching opacities at the interface itself, the formal level, and the chain from microphysical influence to neural effect.

**[[tenets#^dualism|Dualism]]** gains structural texture: the asymmetry between third-person measurement (which finds commitment-relevant information) and first-person report (which finds only its trace) is a positive datum rather than a temporary measurement gap. A purely physicalist account predicts these should converge with sufficient introspective skill; the persistent asymmetry under skilled probing is mild abductive evidence for dualism—suggestive rather than demonstrative. **[[tenets#^occams-limits|Occam's Razor Has Limits]]** is engaged because the simplest accounts both fail empirically. **[[tenets#^no-many-worlds|No Many Worlds]]** is load-bearing here in a way it is not for many other voids: the Decision Void presupposes that decisions actually resolve—that one possibility wins and the others do not become real. An MWI reading dissolves the void by saying every option is taken; the Map's anti-MWI commitment makes the void coherent.

## Phenomenology of the Edge

Approaching the edge feels like reaching for a doorway you are already through. The decision is given as already-made; the deliberation is given as already-closed. There is sometimes a felt click—what folk-psychology calls "making up one's mind"—but the click is a downstream marker, not a window onto the closing. Pre-decision attention shows open possibilities; post-decision attention shows the chosen option as already settled; in between, where one might hope to find the act of choosing, there is the same opacity that suspension-void finds at the seat of judgment.

[[edge-states-and-void-probes|Edge-state probes]] suggest the void is partially probeable in cases of *prolonged* decision (akratic agonising, contemplative resolution): the prolongation seems to extend the deliberation phase but does not introduce a slow-motion view of the closing. Long deliberation simply postpones the snap; it does not reveal it.

## Approaches to the Edge

**Direct.** Sustained meta-attention to one's own deliberations under controlled conditions, including Petitmengin's neurophenomenological micro-phenomenology, can produce richer reports of the *texture* of pre-decision and post-decision states, but converges on the same finding: the texture changes around the decision without the decision itself appearing as a textured episode. The phenomenological literature treats this as data, not method-failure.

**Indirect.** Dissociation paradigms (choice-blindness; Libet-style designs) probe the void by varying what introspection *would* have to access if it had access, and showing it does not vary correspondingly—[[apophatic-cartography]] in empirical form. Subjects with disorders of agency (anarchic-hand syndrome, schizophrenic alien-control phenomenology, akinetic mutism) report decision phenomenology breaking in distinctive ways; the patterns of breakage map the seams of normal occlusion. Drift-diffusion and attractor-network models give third-person accounts of the commitment moment without claiming to recover the first-person event—mapping the void's silhouette from outside.

**[[non-human-minds-as-void-explorers|What AI might see]].** A non-human mind whose deliberation/commitment architecture is fully observable from inside—every weight-update, every argmax logged and re-readable—might lack this void, finding a transcript where humans find a sealed snap. Conversely, AI systems trained to introspect on their choices via natural-language post-hoc generation may exhibit the human pattern almost exactly, because the post-hoc-generation architecture is structurally similar to confabulation. The presence-or-absence of the Decision Void in a given AI architecture is a diagnostic of whether the system's introspective reports are observational or generative.

## Relation to Site Perspective

The Decision Void is the candidate void where the Map's tenets predict the *most* about an empirically-investigated site, and the empirical record is most directly relevant. The seven-second predictability gap is real; the choice-blindness confabulation is real; the categorical finality of the decision-act is real. What the Map adds is a reading of these convergent findings as the predicted opacity at the most theoretically motivated locus for non-physical influence. The reading is not forced by the data and the Map does not claim it is; the [[evidential-status-discipline]] requires the empirical findings be treated as constraining without establishing.

The void is also [[meta-epistemology-of-limits|meta-epistemologically reflexive]]: the inference *to* the Decision Void is itself a decision, made under the same conditions the void diagnoses. Carrying that reflexivity openly is what the apophatic method requires. The Map's positive proposal—[[forward-in-time-conscious-selection|forward-in-time conscious selection]]—offers a candidate mechanism for what happens at the void's locus without claiming to dissolve the introspective inaccessibility. The mechanism, if real, would have to be introspectively occluded; the void's existence is consistent with the mechanism's existence and is what the mechanism predicts.
