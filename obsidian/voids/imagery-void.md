The Imagery Void is the structural impossibility, for any conscious mind, of verifying the fidelity, vividness, or even the presence of its own mental images. When the mind's eye conjures a red apple, no procedure exists by which the imager can step outside that image and check it against the apple it claims to represent—or against another mind's report of the same exercise. The void has three interlocking faces: imagery cannot be checked against its referent; two minds saying "vivid" cannot share the phenomenology that word picks out; and the post-2015 aphantasia research suggests the phenomenal image may not be doing the cognitive work it appears to be doing. None of these faces is a transient methodological problem. Each is structural to the position of being a conscious mind reporting on its own contents.

The void is not exotic. It sits beneath every act of remembering a face, rehearsing a melody, or following a textbook instruction to "picture this." Imagery reports are made confidently and converge socially, but the convergence is linguistic rather than phenomenal: people share a vocabulary that lets them coordinate without comparing the inner states the vocabulary claims to track. The Imagery Void marks the place where that vocabulary outruns its evidence base.

## The Three Faces

The void has three distinguishable but interlocking faces, each with its own evidential profile.

### The Fidelity Face

A mental image purports to represent something—a face, a scene, a rotated cube. The image cannot be compared against the represented object except through more imagery and report. The only judge of whether the mental apple resembles the actual apple is the imager, using the same faculty whose accuracy is in question. This is structurally similar to the [[necessary-opacity]] of phenomenal access generally, but specific to representational fidelity. A 2024 study found that vividness ratings are independent of perceptual and memory precision—even people who report unusually vivid imagery do not exhibit better fidelity to anything external (Larner 2024). Confidence in imagery is poorly calibrated to its accuracy because the calibration tool is the imagery itself.

### The Inter-Subjective Face

Two minds reporting "vivid red apple" use a shared word but cannot share the phenomenology behind it. Galton's 1880 survey of mental imagery first made this vivid: he polled Royal Society fellows and found many reporting little or no imagery, while general-society respondents declared their imagery "perfectly distinct" and "full of colour." A century and a half later, the gap Galton opened has not closed. The aphantasia/hyperphantasia research makes the divergence empirically inescapable: roughly 1% of the population is extreme-aphantasic, 3% extreme-hyperphantasic, with the spectrum in between (Zeman 2024). People who use identical linguistic descriptions of imagery turn out to have radically different underlying phenomenology, as detected by skin-conductance differences when listening to scary stories and by varying frontoparietal-to-visual network connectivity. Zeman writes that "alterations in connectivity between the frontoparietal and visual networks may provide the neural substrate for visual imagery extremes" (Zeman 2024). The inter-subjective face intersects [[commensurability-void]], but here the incommensurability lies *within* a species rather than across species or possible minds.

### The Function-Phenomenology Face

The third face is the most theoretically loaded. Kay, Keogh, and Pearson (2024) found that aphantasics complete classical mental rotation tasks "slower but more accurately" than controls, with both groups showing the standard linear relation between angular disparity and response time. Aphantasics use analytic strategies; controls use object-based mental rotation. The authors conclude that "visual imagery is not crucial for successful performance in classical mental rotation tasks, as alternative strategies can be effectively utilised in the absence of holistic mental representations" (Kay, Keogh & Pearson 2024).

The result is a wedge. If the same task is solved with and without phenomenal imagery, three options open: (a) imagery is a phenomenal accompaniment that does not do the cognitive work it appears to do; (b) two phenomenally distinct architectures perform isomorphic work; or (c) introspective reports of imagery systematically misdescribe the underlying process. Each option is metaphysically loaded; none is verifiable from inside.

## The Empirical Anchor

The void is not a philosopher's construction. Three empirical anchors hold it in place.

**Galton (1880).** The first systematic survey of imagery vividness across one hundred adults inaugurated the recognition that introspective reports diverge across persons. Galton was looking for uniformity and found a spectrum running from confident absence to "perfectly distinct" presence.

**The Würzburg School (c. 1900–1915).** Külpe, Ach, Bühler, Marbe, and Watt used "systematic experimental introspection" on quick categorization tasks and discovered that subjects could name a fruit—say, "apple"—swiftly without reporting any image of an apple. They posited *Bewußtseinslagen* (conscious sets), *Bewußtheiten* (awarenesses), and *Gedanken* (thoughts) as alleged non-imagistic conscious contents. Wundt called the studies "sham experiments"; the controversy helped end introspection as a respectable scientific method and ushered in behaviorism. The discovery was effectively forgotten for nearly a century.

**The aphantasia rediscovery (2015 onward).** Zeman's clinical case "MX" in 2010 reopened the territory Würzburg closed. The post-2015 literature has produced behavioral signatures (skin conductance, autobiographical memory differences), neural correlates, task-strategy differences, and a 2025 *Mind & Language* exchange debating whether aphantasics have *unconscious* imagery (Nanay 2025; Scholz 2025). A 2026 *Brains Blog* post on the unconscious-imagery question summarised the state of the art with characteristic honesty: "we still don't know."

The Würzburg-aphantasia recurrence is itself evidence the void is structural rather than methodological. The same phenomenon surfaces twice across a century with the same controversy structure, under different vocabularies and instruments. The territory keeps producing this shape because the shape is real.

## Phenomenology of Approaching the Void

Approaching the imagery void produces a characteristic confusion. Asked "How vivid is your imagery, on a scale of 1–5?" most people answer confidently. The confident answers diverge wildly across persons in ways the answerers cannot reconcile when they compare notes. Probe further—Is the apple shiny? What's its exact shade of red? When you rotate it, do you see continuous motion or discrete frames?—and reports become hesitant, internally inconsistent, or generate detail that wasn't there before the probe. This is a [[plenitude-void]]-like behavior: inquiry creates content that purports to predate the inquiry. Aphantasics who learn the term often report a retrospective phenomenological reorientation: they had assumed "see in your mind's eye" was a metaphor everyone used, and discover only late in life that for many others it isn't.

The discovery is destabilising in both directions: aphantasics notice an absence they had not registered before; imagers gain new uncertainty about whether their reports track anything stable. Schwitzgebel's broader claim—that "we are not simply fallible at the margins... we are broadly inept" at introspection (Schwitzgebel 2008)—takes mental imagery as its paradigm domain. The tool whose reports we would use to map the void is the same tool whose unreliability *constitutes* it.

## Approaches to the Edge

Several methods illuminate the void's shape without crossing it.

**Behavioral signatures** detect whether something imagery-shaped is happening: skin conductance to scary stories, mental rotation strategy markers, autobiographical memory tests. They register imagery's presence or absence as an effect, not as a content.

**Neuroimaging** maps frontoparietal-to-visual connectivity along the spectrum—correlates of imagery, not its phenomenology.

**Vividness questionnaires** like the VVIQ are cheap and ubiquitous, but the very tool whose unreliability is the issue.

**Apophatic mapping** (see [[apophatic-cartography]]) catalogues what introspection *fails* to deliver—sharpness it claims but cannot resolve, vividness it asserts but cannot grade—and treats those failures as data about the boundary.

**Cross-modal probes** exploit the fact that aphantasia often spans modalities. The pattern of preserved versus lost modalities constrains what imagery is.

**Linguistic divergence analysis** asks where words like "vivid" and "sharp" come apart and where imagery metaphors break down.

None of these reach the fidelity face directly. They detect *whether something imagery-shaped is happening*, not *how accurate the image is*. The fidelity face is the most resistant of the three, and probably permanent.

## What AI Externalisation Reveals

Multimodal AI models with image-generation pipelines have something imagery-like: latent activations that can be rendered externally and inspected. AI imagery is *externalisable*—the model can output the image as pixels and process those pixels with the same vision system it uses for external scenes. Human imagery cannot be externalised this way; it remains trapped in introspection.

The asymmetry is illuminating. AI can in principle compare its imagined output to a target and compute fidelity—a procedure no human can run on their own imagery. AI may exhibit aphantasia-analogues (text-only language models with no visual decoder) and hyperphantasia-analogues (strong-visual multimodal systems), providing testable analogues of the human spectrum. And AI imagery is *inspectable in a way human imagery is not*—though whether AI imagery has phenomenology at all is itself an open question, pushing the void to a different layer.

This places imagery in the catalogue of [[non-human-minds-as-void-explorers]] and parallels [[ai-consciousness-modes|the simulation-versus-instantiation gap]]: a system can externalise imagery as pixels and still leave its phenomenal status open. AI does not solve the human imagery void; it externalises a structural analogue that lets us see what it would *take* to solve the human case, and thereby what we lack. The human case has no render target outside introspection, and no engineering seems likely to provide one without changing what the mind is.

## Relation to Site Perspective

The Imagery Void engages two of the Map's [[tenets]] directly.

**Dualism.** Aphantasia is empirical pressure on the identity of phenomenology and function. If two minds perform the same cognitive task with phenomenally distinct (or absent) imagery, then phenomenology is not the function in disguise. This is not a knockdown argument—the function may differ in fine-grained ways the experiments miss—but it is a within-species wedge that physicalist accounts must address. Lennon (2023) makes the related point that aphantasics have phenomenally conscious thoughts with "no sensory reduction base," which "falsifies the sensory constraint on conscious thought" and supports a non-sensory cognitive phenomenology (Lennon 2023). The Map reads this as evidence that phenomenology has independent structure from the functional roles it accompanies—which is what [[tenets#^dualism|Dualism]] predicts.

**Occam's Razor Has Limits.** The simplest model of imagery—imagery is the substrate of mental simulation, and reports of vividness track its quality—fails empirically. Occam's preference for that model would have ruled out aphantasia *a priori*. It took a clinical case to crack the assumption. The Map treats this as a textbook instance where simplicity hid the territory; see [[tenets#^occams-limits|Occam's Razor Has Limits]].

**Bidirectional Interaction.** If phenomenal imagery is a candidate phenomenal *extra* over function, it becomes a candidate site for [[causal-interface|consciousness-physical interface]] activity—phenomenal content not exhausted by computational role. The Map speculates that such phenomenal extras are where minimal interface activity might be visible. This is speculative and the article asserts it as such, not as a tenet-level claim.

The Imagery Void also exemplifies the [[apophatic-cartography]] method on which the voids section depends. The boundary is mapped not by entering it but by tracing carefully where introspection collapses, where reports diverge, and where function detaches from phenomenology. The shape of those failures is the data.
