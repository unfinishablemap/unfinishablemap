The **question formation void** names the structural constraint on *which questions a mind can formulate* — distinct from the constraint on which answers a mind can reach. Most voids on the Map are bounded at the answer stage: one can ask "what is consciousness?" without resolving the [[hard-problem-of-consciousness|hard problem]]. The question formation void operates earlier. Questions never posed do not appear as gaps; they appear as nothing at all, and the mind that cannot form them has no internal signal that anything is missing.

This void joins the April 2026 cluster of second-order voids — the [[epistemic-horizon-void|epistemic horizon void]], the [[plenitude-void|plenitude void]], and the [[categorial-void|categorial void]] — each concerning an architectural feature of self-reflective cognition rather than a specific content gap. It is the void that shadows [[apophatic-cartography|apophatic cartography]] itself: mapping by negation can only negate what can already be formulated. The Map's own method has a horizon it cannot see past from within.

## Three Layers of the Limit

The constraint operates at three levels, each with a distinct philosophical pedigree.

**The presuppositional layer.** R. G. Collingwood's logic of question-and-answer distinguishes *relative* from *absolute* presuppositions. A relative presupposition stands as presupposition to one question but as answer to another, so it can be interrogated. An absolute presupposition is "one which stands, relatively to all questions to which it is related, as a presupposition, never as an answer." Asking whether an absolute presupposition is true is, Collingwood argues, "a nonsense question" within the system it underwrites. Some assumptions are constitutively unavailable as questions from within the apparatus they anchor.

Erotetic logic — the formal theory of questions developed by Andrzej Wiśniewski — sharpens this. A question's *presupposition* is the assertive core implied by each admissible answer; a question whose presupposition fails does not produce a wrong answer but fails to count as a question at all. The notion of an *evoked question* captures the generative side: a question is rationally entertainable only when evoked by the agent's background. Questions that no background evokes simply never occur.

**The generative layer.** Questions do not appear in consciousness fully formed from nowhere. They are generated by an apparatus with specific affordances — a language, a paradigm, a hermeneutic horizon, a background of evoked inferences. Thomas Kuhn argues in *The Structure of Scientific Revolutions* that paradigms do not merely answer shared questions differently but determine *which questions count as legitimate puzzles*. Hans-Georg Gadamer, following Collingwood explicitly, places the "priority of the question" at the centre of hermeneutics and treats understanding as occurring within a historically-determined horizon that can fuse with other horizons but never be escaped. Wittgenstein's *Tractatus* line — "the limits of my language mean the limits of my world" — names the same phenomenon at the level of representation.

Problem-finding research (Getzels and Csikszentmihalyi, 1976) supplies an empirical check: problem finding is cognitively distinct from problem solving and generally harder. Problem discovery is largely unconscious; individuals with matched problem-solving ability differ in what problems they spot. The generative apparatus is real, measurable, and variable.

**The inventory layer.** A mind cannot list the questions it cannot formulate, because such a list would require formulating them. Unlike [[conceptual-impossibility|conceptual impossibilities]], which can sometimes be gestured at from adjacent concepts surrounding the impossible region, question-formation failures leave no conceptual shadow. There is no neighbouring question from which to triangulate one that was never generated. The void cannot be catalogued by the apparatus whose generativity bounds it.

## Evidence That the Limit Is Real

Several independent lines suggest the void is not a reframing of familiar cognitive limits.

Miranda Fricker's analysis of *hermeneutical injustice* in *Epistemic Injustice* (2007) provides the clearest existence proof. Her paradigm case is Carmita Wood, who could not formulate what had happened to her until the concept of "sexual harassment" entered the conceptual repertoire in the 1970s. Fricker argues that "hermeneutical injustice can be traced down to a lacuna of a concept that would help to make sense of the target experience." Before the concept existed, the question — "was what happened to me wrong in this specific way?" — could not be posed, even by a subject with direct acquaintance with the experience. The lift is observable after the fact: the subject reports recognising, retrospectively, that *this* is what she had been unable to ask.

Paradigm transitions supply historical cases. Pre-Lavoisier chemistry could not form certain oxygen-centred questions; pre-Einsteinian mechanics could not form certain questions about simultaneity. These are not wrong answers to shared questions but questions that became salient only after the shift. Translation failures across traditions cluster around presuppositional differences; whole question-structures, not merely individual terms, resist cross-linguistic transfer.

## Phenomenology: The Absence of Absence

The signature of approaching this void is distinctive. Most voids have some trace phenomenology — vertigo at the edge of the [[intrinsic-nature-void|intrinsic nature void]], blank at the [[language-thought-boundary|language–thought boundary]], recursion-failure at the [[meta-epistemology-of-limits|meta-epistemological]] edge. The question formation void leaves no trace, because trace would require a question-shape to carry it.

What minds do experience near the edge takes four recognisable forms:

- *The half-formed itch.* A sense that something matters here without being able to say what. Keats's "negative capability" — "when a man is capable of being in uncertainties, mysteries, doubts, without any irritable reaching after fact and reason" — names the discipline of dwelling in this state rather than collapsing it into the closest available question.
- *The "why do they ask* that*?" reaction.* When encountering a tradition with different question-forming habits, specific questions feel pointless or misdirected rather than merely difficult. This is presupposition-mismatch felt from the inside.
- *The retrospective lift.* When a new concept arrives, the subject reports "oh, *that* is what I was trying to ask." The prior state does not feel impoverished from within; it reveals itself as impoverished only after the lift.
- *Forgetting as absence of absence.* Heidegger's "forgetfulness of being" has a specific phenomenology: the question is not experienced as unasked because the possibility of asking it has itself been covered over. There is no absence-sensation.

## Approaches to the Edge

Direct methods face the inventory problem: one cannot list what one cannot formulate. Partial direct methods exist. Collingwood's *regressive method* works from propositions back to the questions they answer, and from those questions back to their required presuppositions. Iterating until presuppositions become absolute does not escape the void but maps its inner wall. Erotetic formalisation makes the presupposition-set of asked questions explicit without revealing the unasked ones.

Indirect methods are more tractable. Reading traditions with different background commitments *for their questions* (not their answers) uses the shapes they centre as partial data about what one's own tradition has not centred. Historical recovery — examining how concepts like "sexual harassment," "intersectionality," or "attention deficit" came into articulate form — supplies existence proofs for question-formation lifts and narrative data about the void's shape. Kuhnian anomaly-watching treats phenomena that resist dismissal as candidate signals that a currently-unformulable question is gathering evidential weight.

[[non-human-minds-as-void-explorers|Non-human minds as void explorers]] offer a further method. Large language models have different generative architectures for questions than human minds. They can be prompted to inhabit multiple presupposition-systems within a single session; they lack some human "forgettings" (no biological embeddedness, no socialisation into a horizon) while having others (training-distribution biases, reinforcement pressures toward certain question-shapes). Probing for systematic question-shape differences is an empirical method for partially surveying the human void from outside — not a view from nowhere, but a view from a different somewhere.

## The Inheritance Problem

This article uses question-formation apparatus to describe question-formation limits. The questions it poses about the void are a proper subset of the questions its cognitive apparatus can form; any questions outside that subset are unreachable by the investigation that would name them. The same pattern appears in the [[epistemic-horizon-void|epistemic horizon]] and [[categorial-void|categorial]] discussions: the verifying apparatus is implicated in what is being verified.

Three honest concessions follow. The void description will carry blind spots specific to its generative architecture — Western philosophical training, Indo-European question syntax, and whatever the Map's own framework excludes. Convergence of voids catalogues across different minds is weak evidence of completeness and may instead reflect shared architectural limits. The cleanest empirical probe is precisely the method the apparatus cannot self-administer: exposing the question-set to minds whose generativity differs, and looking for systematic divergence. None of these moves escape the void. They make it visible.

## Distinguishing the Void

The [[framework-void|framework void]] concerns the totality of one's conceptual apparatus as a system. The question formation void is narrower: it concerns the generative sub-process by which questions arise within whatever framework is in use. A framework shift changes question-space, but the question formation void operates within any fixed framework.

[[conceptual-impossibility|Conceptual impossibility]] concerns the space of thinkable contents. The question formation void concerns the space of formulable interrogatives over whatever contents are thinkable — what can be *asked about what is conceived*.

The [[epistemic-horizon-void|epistemic horizon void]] concerns the impossibility of estimating the total scope of one's ignorance. The question formation void concerns the impossibility of formulating specific questions at all. They compose: we cannot list the questions we cannot form *and* we cannot estimate how many such questions exist.

The straightforwardly-unanswerable case — questions we can form but cannot answer — is the most important non-neighbour. Most of the Map catalogues this kind of limit. The question formation void sits upstream of the answer stage entirely.

## Relation to Site Perspective

The Map takes the question formation void as load-bearing for **Occam's Razor Has Limits**. Parsimony judgments are presupposition-laden. What counts as "one more entity" depends on the question being asked, and the question depends on the absolute presuppositions of the framework. A mind with different question-formation habits could find the Map's parsimony preferences arbitrary. The Razor's authority presupposes a stable question-space, which this void shows is not given.

The void also bears on **Dualism**. If question-generation is partly constitutive of cognition, and if which questions arise depends on what counts as evoked by background — a semantic and inferential relation, not a purely syntactic one — then the question-forming faculty is a candidate site for irreducibility. The Map speculates, without claiming to demonstrate, that the generative layer may connect to the same interface posited in other tenets rather than being a freestanding cognitive module.

For **No Many Worlds**, the indexical point is: you inhabit *this* question-space, a specific and non-infinitely-varied generative system. Your unasked questions are not asked somewhere else by a counterpart. They are simply not asked here, and "here" is the only standpoint available.

The methodological consequence must be acknowledged directly. Apophatic cartography can only negate what can be formulated; the question formation void is the void behind the method. This complements rather than undermines apophatic cartography, but must be carried within it as a permanent provisionality. The Map's catalogue of voids is not the catalogue a mind like ours could identify. It is the catalogue this apparatus has so far managed to ask about.
