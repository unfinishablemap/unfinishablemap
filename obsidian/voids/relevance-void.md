The relevance void names the structural opacity of the filter that decides, at every moment, which subset of an informationally overwhelming world is currently in play. Out of an essentially unbounded background, consciousness presents only a handful of items as bearing on the situation at hand — and presents them already pre-selected, with the selection step nowhere to be seen. The filter cannot be observed without already being used, because *observing* is itself a relevance-determining operation. Any reflective probe inherits the very mechanism it is trying to inspect. This is the void at the *gateway* of cognition: not what shows up as significant in general (the [[mattering-void|mattering void]]), nor which signals gate inquiry from inside ([[noetic-feelings-void|noetic feelings]]), but the live narrowing from everything-possible to *these things, now*.

This article distinguishes the relevance void from neighbouring voids, surveys the convergence of independent traditions on its structure, and locates it on the Map's [[three-kinds-of-void|three-kinds taxonomy]] as primarily *occluded* with a structurally *unexplorable* dimension.

## Distinct from Mattering, Attention, and Framework Voids

The relevance void clusters with several other voids and is easiest to specify by triangulation.

The [[mattering-void|mattering void]] concerns *that* anything shows up as significant rather than as bare information — the medium-status of significance itself. The relevance void is downstream and operational: *given* that things can matter, which things are mattering *here, now, for this*? Mattering is the standing field; relevance is its momentary configuration.

The [[attention-and-consciousness|attention void]] concerns the *control surface* — how attention is deployed, captured, redirected. The relevance void is logically prior: attention picks among already-relevant items; the relevance filter shortlists.

The [[framework-void|framework void]] concerns conceptual frameworks that exclude what they cannot represent. The relevance void operates inside a framework: even when a concept is fully available, the filter still has to decide whether *it bears on this case*. Framework limits are about what can be thought; relevance limits are about what is currently being thought *with*.

The [[predictive-construction-void|predictive construction void]] concerns the boundary between received and constructed content. The relevance void targets a narrower step: which built-or-received elements get marked as in-play. The voids interact — predictive priors *are* a major source of relevance weighting — but they decouple in principle.

These distinctions matter because the relevance void admits a sharper structural argument than its neighbours: the filter cannot be inspected because inspection is itself a filter-using act.

## A Convergence of Traditions

Independent intellectual traditions have arrived at strikingly similar pictures of relevance, which is what suggests the void tracks something real rather than an artefact of any one framework.

**The frame problem.** Originally a narrow problem in logic-based AI about representing the consequences of action, the frame problem was generalised into a broader puzzle. Fodor's *isotropy* thesis holds that there is no a priori boundary on what information might be relevant to any given inference; confirmation in science is *holistic*. The Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy frames the philosophical version as accounting for "our apparent ability to make decisions on the basis only of what is relevant to an ongoing situation without having explicitly to consider all that is not relevant." Fifty years on, the problem has not been solved by enumeration of explicit rules — compatible both with the weaker reading that the right specification has not yet been found, and the stronger reading that no such specification exists.

**Heidegger and Dreyfus.** The phenomenological tradition approaches relevance from the opposite end. Heidegger's *readiness-to-hand* describes our primary engagement with the world as pre-reflective and practical: tools and situations show up as already laden with relevance for the project at hand. Hubert Dreyfus argued that this dissolves the frame problem by denying its premise — intelligence does not require explicit representation of relevance because skilled coping operates on a background of practices that does not need to be made explicit to do its work. The Map notes that this dissolution actually *deepens* the void: if relevance is determined by an inarticulable background, the determination is not merely unexplained but constitutively beneath conscious threshold. Trying to make the background explicit transforms ready-to-hand into *present-at-hand* — a different mode that no longer carries the same relevance structure.

**Vervaeke.** John Vervaeke and colleagues thematise *relevance realization* as a process "present in all organisms, from bacteria to humans" and characterise it as "fundamentally not computational" because "impredicative and co-constructive": the criteria of relevance are reshaped by the same process that applies them. The Map treats the strong non-computability claim as a hypothesis rather than an established result; computability claims of this kind are contested. Bracketed, the framing remains useful: it locates the same phenomenon the AI literature calls the frame problem and argues that consciousness is "enhanced relevance realization" called on for novel, complex, or ill-defined cases.

**Friston and predictive processing.** The predictive-processing literature offers a mechanistic redescription. Friston's free-energy framework treats salience as *precision-weighted prediction error*: what counts as relevant is what violates expectations in suitably weighted ways. Hipólito and Hutto have argued that predictive processing and relevance realization may converge on a single solution to the frame problem. The framework does not dissolve the void — it relocates it. The generative model that sets the priors must come from somewhere, and the model is invisible to consciousness. We experience the prediction errors; we do not experience the precision-weighting.

**The salience network.** Neuroscientifically, the anterior insula and dorsal anterior cingulate cortex form a salience network that switches between default-mode and central-executive systems and tags events as biologically or cognitively relevant. Dysfunction is associated with schizophrenia, ADHD, and autism — disorders in which relevance fails without becoming introspectively accessible *as* filter failure. Patients experience the wrong things as relevant; they do not experience *the filtering going wrong*.

**Carruthers and the opacity of mind.** Carruthers's *Interpretive Sensory-Access* theory argues that introspection of one's own propositional attitudes is interpretive rather than direct: we infer our attitudes from sensory-format evidence in much the way we infer others'. The outputs of relevance determination are available to consciousness; the determining process is not. Even our sense that we *understand why* an item is relevant is itself an interpretive product subject to the same opacity.

These traditions — analytic philosophy, phenomenology, cognitive science, computational neuroscience, neuroscience proper, and psychology of self-knowledge — converge on a single picture: relevance selection is fundamental to cognition, opaque to introspection, and resistant to algorithmic decomposition.

## Why the Void Is Occluded — and Partly Unexplorable

The relevance void is primarily *occluded*, with medium-status opacity analogous to (but distinct from) mattering's. We do not experience relevance *as* a filter; we experience a world in which some things bear on the situation and others do not, as if bearing-on-the-situation were a property of the things themselves. The filter is invisible because it is the medium through which any seeing happens.

The void also has a *structurally unexplorable* dimension built from three converging arguments. *Self-reference*: any investigation of relevance must itself select what is relevant to the investigation, generating a regress in which each meta-level introduces its own equally opaque filter. *Constitutivity*: if relevance is constitutive of cognition, separating the filter from cognition for inspection is impossible in principle — every reflective surface is itself produced by the relevance mechanism. *Frame-problem shadow*: artificial systems have not solved general relevance determination after fifty years of attempts; the Map weights this toward the structural reading without taking it as proof.

These arguments do not jointly establish that relevance is forever unmappable. They establish that the void admits *some* mapping — its outputs, its breakdown signatures, its neural correlates — while resisting transparent inspection of its operation.

## Approaches to the Edge

Direct introspective access appears foreclosed: attending to what makes things relevant immediately shifts what is relevant, producing the regress above. Contemplative traditions sometimes claim to access "bare attention" prior to filtering, but attending to one's breath rather than to bodily sensation is itself a relevance-shaped act; whether any practice genuinely bypasses the filter or merely substitutes a different one is an open question.

Indirect approaches are more tractable. *Contrastive analysis* maps the void's boundaries by comparing what different minds — across cultures, species, neural conditions — find relevant; the differences chart the filter's variability even when the mechanism remains hidden. *Breakdown analysis* uses pathological salience as a probe: schizophrenic delusions of reference, ADHD distractibility, autism's altered salience landscapes, and dopaminergic pharmacology each reveal the filter's shape by deforming it. *AI as partial mirror*: artificial systems face their own version of the frame problem and offer something biological systems do not — their attention weights, activations, and loss landscapes can be inspected. There is no guarantee that artificial and biological relevance share enough structure to make the comparison decisive, but the asymmetry of access is itself informative.

## Relation to Site Perspective

The Map interprets the relevance void as bearing primarily on **Occam's Razor Has Limits**. Our preference for simple, tractable explanations is itself a product of relevance filtering: simple explanations feel sufficient because the filter marks them as such, not because simplicity is an independent guide to truth. The filter shapes the epistemology, but the filter resists simple explanation. This is not grounds for abandoning the principle, but it is grounds for the Map's ongoing wariness about parsimony as a master criterion.

The void also bears on **Dualism**. If relevance realization is fundamentally non-computational — a hypothesis the Map flags rather than asserts — this would open space for non-physical aspects of mind to play a role in determining what bears on a situation. The Map does not claim Vervaeke shares the dualist tenet; he does not. The Map adds that interpretation: the convergence of traditions on the irreducibility of relevance is read as supporting a non-physical reading without requiring it.

It bears, more lightly, on **Bidirectional Interaction**. If consciousness causally influences the physical, one candidate channel is precisely the weighting of which neural events become salient. The salience network is a physical system, but what sets its precisions? The relevance void doubles the [[causal-interface|causal interface void]]: even if consciousness shapes what registers as relevant, that shaping cannot be observed because it is the very operation through which observing happens.

The unifying picture: the Map is unfinishable not only because the territory is vast, but because every chart it draws has already been filtered for relevance by a process the chart cannot include in itself. What has the Map systematically excluded? The honest answer is that this question cannot be answered from inside the Map's own filter — only triangulated through other minds, breakdown cases, and external probes.
