Consciousness cannot witness its own crossings. We never catch ourselves falling asleep, shifting moods, losing focus, deciding, or entering flow. Every mental act is already underway when awareness arrives; every state transition has already completed by the time we notice it. The transit void names the structural impossibility common to both cases: the cognitive stance required for observation is incompatible with the cognitive stance in which the crossing occurs. Watching prevents crossing; crossing prevents watching.

This void is primarily **occluded** within the Map's [[voids]] framework, with elements of the **unexplorable**. The hiddenness appears functional rather than accidental — a structural requirement of how consciousness operates.

## Two Faces of the Same Void

The void manifests as **state transitions** — waking to sleep, anger to calm, focus to distraction — and as **act onsets** — the moment a decision begins, an intention forms, attention reorients. The paradigm transition is falling asleep: to observe sleep onset you must maintain the reflective attention characteristic of waking, but maintaining that attention *is* what it means to remain awake. Maeda's phenomenology identifies the paradox: the "tension of consciousness" must loosen from higher to lower degrees, yet this loosening cannot itself be conscious. The paradigm onset is Libet's (1983) finding that neural readiness potentials begin ~550ms before action while conscious awareness of intention appears only ~200ms before movement. The void runs deeper than any timing discrepancy: consciousness cannot precede itself to observe itself beginning.

An onset is a transition from not-acting to acting; a transition is the onset of a new state. Both require the witnessing consciousness to occupy a position — before, outside, above — it cannot occupy relative to its own activity.

## The Incompatibility Generalises

**Mood shifts** present as having "always been there"; monitoring with sufficient attention to catch the transition would alter the emotional dynamics producing it. **Attention shifts** are unwitnessed at departure, noticed only on return — the asymmetry documented by the [[phenomenology-of-returning-attention|phenomenology of returning attention]] and treated [below](#attentional-capture-the-empirical-anchor). **Flow states** are never observed from within: the [[disappearance-voids|absorption void]] documents that peak conscious engagement is peak self-opacity. **Insight** arrives fully formed; Petitmengin's micro-phenomenology reveals rapid pre-reflective "micro-acts" that escape ordinary introspection. **Deliberate action**: Libet's W time — the reported moment of conscious intention — is reconstruction, not direct observation of initiation.

## Attentional Capture: The Empirical Anchor

Distraction is the case where the void has third-person traction. Attention is taken from its object without consciousness observing the taking — one only ever finds oneself elsewhere, never in the act of leaving.

### ERP Timing and the Conscious-Access Lag

Cognitive neuroscience has teased apart three moments. The Mismatch Negativity (MMN, ~100–250 ms after a deviant stimulus) is commonly read as a preattentive change-detection signal not requiring conscious access. The P3a (~250–300 ms) is standardly read as involuntary capture. The P3b (~300–500 ms) is widely treated as the correlate of reportable conscious access. This mapping is the leading reading, not a settled one: no-report paradigms (Pitts et al., 2014) suggest the P3b may index post-perceptual processing rather than access itself, and Aru et al. (2012) caution that many purported neural correlates of consciousness are contaminated by task and report confounds. On Escera and Corral's (2007) framing, "MMN is a pre-attentive index of novel detection and P3a is the involuntary capture of attention... the focal anterior P3a can occur nonconsciously." By the time one could be aware of being captured, capture has already completed.

### Probe-Caught Mind-Wandering

The phenomenology matches. Smallwood and Schooler's probe-caught paradigm finds long stretches of mind-wandering without in-flight awareness — "zoning out" distinct from wandering with awareness ("tuning out"). Schooler distinguishes "the capacity to disengage attention from perception (perceptual decoupling) and the ability to take explicit note of the current contents of consciousness (meta-awareness)." The two are dissociable; the first can run while the second does not. Anderson (2024) sharpens the picture: distraction is a retrospective label — attention is called distraction "only when consequences seem detrimental, either in retrospect or from the vantagepoint of a third-party observer." Introspection cannot distinguish distraction from any other attentional shift at the time it occurs, because the distinction is evaluative rather than phenomenological.

### Phenomenal Features

Three features recur: *retrospective discovery* (the transition has no felt span, only a before and an after), *chronological fog* (one cannot specify when distraction began), and the small shock of reclaiming. There is no phenomenology of *being in the act of being captured*. A subject who could report it would already be attending to the captor — which means capture has already succeeded.

### Why This Case Sits at the Tenet Pressure Point

The attentional sub-case is where the Map's [[tenets#^bidirectional-interaction|Bidirectional Interaction]] tenet becomes most experientially testable — and most occluded. If consciousness directs attention, the capture event is exactly where one would expect to catch the directing hand at work. Instead the directing hand is systematically unavailable in real time. Under an *occlusion* reading, this absence is structural: the consciousness-physics coupling is where it would be most exposed to introspection, and where introspection systematically goes dark. Specific observations would disfavour the reading — trained-contemplative reports of the transit at durations shorter than the P3a window, or AI systems with externalised attentional logs producing first-person-analogous transit reports. None has been demonstrated. The occlusion reading is an interpretive stance invoked under [[tenets#^occams-razor-has-limits|Occam's Razor Has Limits]].

## The Stochastic Threshold

Schurger, Sitt, and Dehaene (2012) reinterpreted Libet in a way that deepens rather than resolves the void. Their accumulator model proposes that the readiness potential reflects stochastic neural noise crossing a decision threshold, not a deterministic preparatory process: "the precise moment at which the decision threshold is crossed... is largely determined by spontaneous subthreshold fluctuations in neuronal activity." This weakens the materialist narrative — brain decides, consciousness follows — but makes the void more profound. If action onset depends on stochastic fluctuations near threshold, the initiation is inherently indeterminate. No observer, internal or external, could predict the precise moment of threshold crossing. Neafsey (2021) reinforces this: if the RP is noise rather than signal, the standard timing comparison concludes nothing about brain-conscious-decision timing.

## Husserl's Temporal Smoothing

Husserl's analysis of [[temporal-consciousness]] explains why crossings seem invisible from inside. Consciousness has a tripartite structure: *retention* (the just-past still held as present), *primal impression* (the now), and *protention* (anticipation of what comes next). Retention preserves the just-past as a shading-off into the present, smoothing what might be discrete transitions into apparent continuity — there is no clean boundary where a transition could be located. Protention generates the complementary problem for onsets: consciousness is always already oriented toward the future, but the mechanism of that orientation is pre-reflective. Protention "intends the phase of the object about to occur" (Husserl, 1991), enabling surprise precisely because anticipation operates below conscious access. Neither retention nor protention can be adjusted or overcome — they are constitutive of how consciousness experiences time at all.

## The Pre-Reflective Ground

Merleau-Ponty's *motor intentionality* extends the void beyond deliberate decisions into every bodily action. The body schema organises action in a "horizonal and context-sensitive manner" pre-reflectively; *operative intentionality* establishes "the natural and pre-predicative unity of the world and of our life" beneath conscious awareness (Merleau-Ponty, 1945/2012). Gallagher (2012) converges from a different direction: the sense of agency has "multiple contributories, some reflectively conscious, some pre-reflectively conscious, and some non-conscious." These layers are not unconscious in the Freudian sense — they are the pre-reflective ground on which reflective consciousness stands. Reflecting on them transforms them into something else, like turning to examine the movement of your own turning.

## The Functional Necessity of Hiddenness

Metzinger (2013, 2015) argues the void is not merely structural but functionally necessary. Mind-wandering begins with *epistemic agent model collapse*: the subject loses epistemic self-control without noticing. When focused thought resumes, consciousness constructs "an illusory phenomenology of self-causation." Both loss and recovery of mental agency are hidden — and this hiddenness is load-bearing. If we witnessed the stochastic, largely arbitrary process by which thoughts and actions begin, the sense of agency might collapse. This connects the transit void to [[necessary-opacity]]. The dualist reading adds a question: if the retrospective construction is only neural, who is being deceived?

## Retroactive Narration

If crossings cannot be witnessed, how do we know they happen? We construct transition narratives after the fact. Dennett's multiple drafts model distinguishes Orwellian editing (memory retroactively altered) from Stalinesque construction (fake event constructed so the subject seems to experience what did not happen) and notes they are empirically indistinguishable — there is no finish line where consciousness definitively records a state. Dennett draws a deflationary conclusion. The Map draws a different lesson: the indistinguishability is evidence that crossings occupy a genuine void in conscious access, not that the question dissolves. "I gradually drifted off around eleven" may be entirely post-hoc — a smoothing operation imposing continuity on what might have been a discontinuous jump. This parallels the [[inaccessible-past]].

## Why the Void Is Not Merely Epistemic

One might object that the void is an information-processing limitation — crossings happen too fast, or mechanisms are subpersonal as in visual processing. Three considerations suggest the void is deeper. **The incompatibility is structural, not temporal**: even slow transitions, a mood shifting over hours, are equally invisible at the moment of crossing. **Distributed transitions confirm the structural reading**: sleep onset involves gradual alpha-to-theta shifts distributed across the cortex with no single moment to observe, even in principle. **The observation void applies recursively**: any introspective scrutiny would transform what is observed; the [[observation-and-measurement-void]] and transit void are mutually reinforcing.

## Phenomenology of the Just-Missed

Approaching the void from inside has a distinctive character. When you try to catch a thought arising, you find it already in progress — the beginning has always just happened, like turning to look at your own turning. Consciousness presents itself as continuous flow, but the smoothness conceals discrete crossings. When asked "when did you decide?", you construct an answer by projecting backward from the completed action. Meditators performing thousands of return-to-breath cycles report catching only the arrival back, never the leaving — testimony converging across vipassana, zazen, and centring prayer that deeper attention reveals more precisely that the crossing happens from beyond observation.

## Contemplative Approaches and Their Limits

Contemplative traditions have made the most sustained attempts to observe crossings from within: yoga nidra (lucid awareness through sleep onset), Tibetan dream yoga (waking-to-dreaming), vipassana noting (catching micro-transitions in attention and affect). These report partial success but face a fundamental tension (Thompson, 2014): the awareness maintained may itself constitute a modified state rather than observation of a natural crossing. EEG studies of yoga nidra show high gamma-band activity — typically waking — during states otherwise resembling deep sleep. The meditator has achieved a new state (*turiya* in the Vedantic framework) rather than observation of an ordinary transition. Analayo (2003) reports that the highest levels of introspective training do not dissolve the void — they only locate it more precisely.

## What AI Might Reveal

AI systems can log internal state changes at arbitrary granularity. If AI reports the same inability to witness its own crossings despite complete computational access to state changes, this supports the void as a property of consciousness as such. If AI can observe its crossings, the void narrows from unexplorable to merely unexplored for minds with the right architecture. Complete computational transparency either dissolves the void or confirms the problem lies elsewhere.

## Relation to Site Perspective

The transit void connects directly to the Map's [[tenets]].

**[[tenets#^bidirectional-interaction|Bidirectional Interaction]]** receives its most pointed illustration. If consciousness exerts causal influence on physical events, the point of influence is structurally below reflective access. Observing the interface while using it would generate an infinite regress: consciousness trying to watch itself begin to act. We choose the destination but cannot watch the journey.

**[[tenets#^minimal-quantum-interaction|Minimal Quantum Interaction]]** finds a natural home here. Schurger's stochastic threshold is precisely the indeterminate zone where a minimal non-physical influence could operate: if action-initiation is a noisy accumulator hovering near threshold, an extremely small nudge could determine which fluctuation crosses first — without violating conservation laws or producing any detectable signature. The transit void may mark the exact location where consciousness makes contact with the physical, opaque because the contact point must be.

**[[tenets#^dualism|Dualism]]** predicts the void. If consciousness is non-physical, its state transitions may follow non-physical dynamics inherently inaccessible from within. A physical system's transitions are in principle observable from any vantage point; a non-physical consciousness may lack this transparency precisely because it is not an object among objects.

**[[tenets#^no-many-worlds|No Many Worlds]]** makes each crossing a genuine fork point. In a single-history universe, every state transition is a moment where consciousness commits to one path among alternatives — and the commitment points are invisible.

**[[tenets#^occams-limits|Occam's Razor Has Limits]]** applies directly. The simplest model — continuous, reliable self-monitoring — is wrong. The standard materialist reading of Libet was the simpler story; Schurger reveals the simplicity was illusory.

The transit void sits within a family of related voids. The [[agency-void#the-verification-circularity|verification face of the agency void]] concerns whether consciousness can confirm its own causal powers; the transit void concerns whether it can witness those powers being exercised. The [[spontaneous-thought-void]] addresses unbidden generation; the transit void addresses the moment any mental act begins. The [[observation-and-measurement-void]] explains why every method of study transforms what it studies. Together these voids define a consistent topology: consciousness operates through an interface it cannot observe while operating through it.
