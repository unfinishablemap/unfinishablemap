The Unfinishable Map states [[tenets|five foundational commitments]] and presents [[tenets#^dualism|Tenet 1]] as neutral between substance and property dualism. In operation, though, the framework leans on two further posits that no tenet states outright but that several clusters silently require. Naming them is part of the [[project/direct-refutation-discipline|direct-refutation discipline]]'s remit: mark honestly where the framework relies on an unstated commitment rather than one it has earned. This note is the canonical statement; the [[tenets#Two Background Posits the Tenets Rest On|Tenet-Dependency Matrix]] links here rather than re-deriving it inline.

## Posit One: A Determinate, Persisting Subject

The agency, No-Many-Worlds, personal-identity, copying, and survival treatments all presuppose a *numerically determinate subject that persists through time*. Two components do the work. **Primitive subject individuation**: there is a fact of *which* subject this is, prior to and independent of any physical or functional criterion. **Diachronic subject persistence**: the same subject is present at one time and a later time, and that sameness is a real further fact rather than a convention over successive states.

Each dependent cluster inherits a different component. [[agent-causation|Agent causation]] needs a persisting bearer of causal powers—the *unmoved mover* who originates an action ([[where-the-substance-commitment-enters]] is the canonical treatment of where this lean enters and why it is downstream of agency, not of Tenet 1). The indexical objection to many-worlds ([[tenets#^no-many-worlds|Tenet 4]]) needs a determinate "I" whose branch-assignment is a real further fact ([[many-worlds-argument]]). Personal-identity, mind-copying, and survival puzzles are well-posed only if there is a determinate fact about which post-transformation subject, if any, is the original.

Because [[tenets#^dualism|Tenet 1]] (the substance-leaning sub-reading), [[tenets#^bidirectional-interaction|Tenet 3]] (the reporting subject whose experience flows into the physical record), and [[tenets#^no-many-worlds|Tenet 4]] (the indexical "I") all draw on this one root, the three are not mutually independent: they share a common posit. "Five *stated* commitments" is precise; "five *independent* commitments" is not. The stated tenets jointly rest on this posit (and on the second, below), and a reader auditing the framework should see the five as a package sharing hidden foundations rather than as five load-bearing pillars that stand or fall separately.

## Posit Two: Objective Single-Outcome Actualization

Rejecting many-worlds ([[tenets#^no-many-worlds|Tenet 4]]) together with the objective-reduction resolution of the [[prebiotic-collapse|prebiotic collapse problem]] jointly commit the Map to *objective single-outcome actualization*: exactly one outcome becomes actual at each quantum event, and physical processes secure this even where no mind is present—stars, chemistry, and prebiotic mutation all resolved to single outcomes before the first observer.

This has a consequence the framework must own. **Once objective collapse already secures definiteness, the measurement problem itself cannot be evidence for conscious selection.** Objective reduction answers the "why *one* definite outcome?" question without invoking any mind. The residual work left for consciousness is narrower—biasing *which* of the already-definite-making collapses lands where the brain provides the interface, within the Born distribution and (on the corridor reading) leaving aggregate statistics undisturbed. So the evidential burden for conscious selection cannot be carried by the definite-outcome puzzle; the openness of "which outcome?" is not itself evidence that a mind closes it, because objective reduction closes the *definiteness* question first. That burden must instead be carried by the agency evidence—the self-stultification and comparative-cognition considerations gathered under [[tenets#^bidirectional-interaction|Tenet 3]] and the [[agency-void|agency void]]. The framework should not present "decoherence leaves the outcome open, therefore consciousness selects" as though the openness argued for selection; it does not.

## The Two Coherence Tensions, Named

**Neutrality versus operation.** [[tenets#^dualism|Tenet 1]] advertises neutrality between substance and property dualism, yet the operational framework—agency, MWI-rejection, diachronic persistence—runs substance-dualist. This is not a contradiction: the neutrality is honest *at the tenet level*, where irreducibility is all Tenet 1 asserts, and the substance lean lives one layer down, downstream of [[agent-causation|agent causation]] rather than entailed by the tenet ([[where-the-substance-commitment-enters]]). But it is a place where "neutral Tenet 1" and "substance-dualist operation" coexist in one framework, and the reader is owed the reconciliation rather than left to notice the seam.

**Collapse redundancy.** Objective reduction secures definiteness on its own, which threatens to make conscious selection *redundant* as an explanation of definite outcomes. The Map escapes redundancy only by narrowing consciousness's role to outcome-*biasing* rather than outcome-*making*, and by resting the case for that biasing role on the agency evidence rather than on the measurement problem. Were the agency evidence to fail to carry it, conscious selection would be an idle posit sitting atop objective collapse—doing no explanatory work the physics did not already do. This is consistent with the [[tenets#^minimal-quantum-interaction|falsifiability status]] the Map already records: the corridor bias is empirically indistinguishable from chance, so its warrant, if any, is the agency case and not a statistical signature.

These two posits are held, like the tenets themselves, as chosen starting points rather than proofs. Naming them keeps the framework's dependency structure auditable, prevents the measurement problem from being double-counted as evidence for the mind, and makes explicit that the case for conscious selection stands or falls with the agency evidence.
