This is the third worked exhibit of [per-cluster independence scoring](/project/per-cluster-independence-scoring/), and it was constructed to discharge a specific burden the methodology had been carrying openly: the *N=2 self-validation worry*. The two prior exhibits — [the surplus void](/voids/the-surplus-void/) (retracted) and [the introspection-architecture sub-cluster](/topics/introspection-architecture-independence-scoring/) (partially retained) — both produced scores consistent with prior catalogue framing, so the discrimination they displayed was consistent with two readings at once: *the methodology works*, and *the methodology rationalises decisions the cataloguer had already made*. A third exhibit scored by the same hand applying the same unstated standard does not break that tie. What breaks it, partially, is a test case scored against an **explicit rubric fixed before scoring** and run on a cluster the cataloguer would have *preferred* to read as strong. This exhibit is that case. The cluster is the [medium-status cluster](/apex/medium-status-voids-in-cognition/) — mattering, relevance, noetic feelings, conceptual scheme — and the methodology scores it low.

The exhibit's value is conditional and stated up front: scoring against a pre-registered rubric removes the "unstated standard" leg of the circularity but not the "same hand" leg. A single author who writes the rubric and applies it can still tune the rubric to the desired outcome. The genuine discharge requires the rubric to be applied by a grader independent of the author — which is what the [three-service outer-review programme](/project/outer-reviewer-service-calibration/) is for, and which has not yet happened for this exhibit. So the strongest honest claim here is: *the methodology, run against a fixed rubric, yielded a low score on a cluster the cataloguer would have preferred to read as strong.* Whether the rubric itself was tuned to deliver that result is the residual circularity an independent grader would have to close.

## Why This Cluster Is the Unfavourable Case

The [medium-status apex](/apex/medium-status-voids-in-cognition/) presents the cluster as dense and well-converged: four distinct cognitive operations (significance, filtering, gating, framing) exhibiting the same self-referential opacity, with six intellectual traditions independently arriving at the structure. The apex calls the convergence "the strongest evidence the cluster has." A cataloguer reading the surface would expect this cluster to score *high* — four faces, six traditions, a sharp structural criterion guarding against inflation. That expectation is exactly what makes it a useful test: if the methodology returns the surface verdict, it has added nothing; if it returns a lower verdict than the surface invites, it is doing discriminating work the surface count cannot.

The cluster is also *eligible* for scoring. [Tenet-generated voids](/voids/tenet-generated-voids/) are excluded entirely — there is no cluster-external evidence to profile, so scoring them would be trivially circular. The medium-status cluster has external anchors (Heidegger, Frankfurt, Ratcliffe, Vervaeke, psychiatric phenomenology, salience-network neuroscience), so it earns a profile. The rubric's question is whether those anchors, sorted by channel and tested for framework independence, *supply* what their surface abundance suggests.

## The Pre-Registered Rubric

The decisive methodological move, demanded by the [methodology's](/project/per-cluster-independence-scoring/) standing limitation (the scoring is editor-vocabulary, not algorithmic) and by the adversarial review that upgraded this task, is to fix the grade-assignment criteria *before* looking at the cluster's anchors. The rubric below is crude by design — a crude explicit rubric is more defensible than a refined implicit one, because it can be checked, disputed, and reproduced. It maps each of the [four criteria](/concepts/apophatic-cartography-four-criteria/) to a *strong / moderate / weak* grade by anchor-state, not by holistic judgement.

**Signature specificity.**
- *Strong*: the faces produce genuinely different positive contents that cannot be re-derived from one another, confirmed against a curation-artefact audit.
- *Moderate*: the faces differ but a single upstream description plausibly generates the differentiation.
- *Weak*: the faces are differentiated mainly by the vocabulary used to name them.

**Structured persistence.**
- *Strong*: the signature has persisted through ≥50 years of attempted dissolution across ≥2 generating disciplines, with the empirical finding surviving theoretical re-absorption.
- *Moderate*: persistence within one discipline, or persistence threatened by a live re-absorption that dissolves the void-framing while keeping the finding.
- *Weak*: short track record, or the signature is a recent framing of older material.

**Cross-observer convergence.**
- *Strong*: convergence across ≥3 channels of the [seven diagnostic channels](/project/voids-circularity-discount/) (empirical, phenomenological, clinical, contemplative, linguistic, quantum-theoretic, cross-cultural) that are *methodologically distant* — at minimum one patient-population or instrument channel independent of the framing tradition.
- *Moderate*: convergence across 2 channels, or ≥3 channels that share a methodological lineage.
- *Weak*: convergence concentrated in a single channel, or across traditions that are philosophically continuous (one lineage re-described).

**Framework independence.**
- *Strong*: a majority of the load-bearing anchors are *despite-commitments* (the generating framework did not predict the signature and did not need it), at the empirical-anchor channel.
- *Moderate*: a mix of despite-commitments and because-prediction anchors, or a conceptual cluster whose architectural claim survives translation into ≥2 competing vocabularies as the *same* claim.
- *Weak*: the identification *as a void* depends on the framework's own commitments; the anchors are predominantly conceptual and because-prediction; or the void-framing dissolves under translation even where the finding survives.

The rubric inherits a scope condition from the methodology: criterion (4)'s despite-commitments / because-prediction split applies in full force only to empirical-anchor clusters. For predominantly conceptual clusters it reduces to *framework-translatability*, and that reduction is recorded as a calibration mark rather than hidden.

## Applying the Rubric

### Step 1–2: Members, signature, anchors

The cluster's four members are the [mattering void](/voids/mattering-void/), the [relevance void](/voids/relevance-void/), the [noetic-feelings void](/voids/noetic-feelings-void/), and the [conceptual-scheme void](/voids/conceptual-scheme-void/). The shared signature, per the apex, is *medium-status*: an operation structures the field of contents without itself being a content of the field while operating, so it is invisible-while-active, mode-fragile under inspection, and known mainly by its breakdowns. The named anchors, with generating discipline attached: Heidegger (*Sorge*, *Befindlichkeit*) from existential phenomenology; Frankfurt (caring as identity-constitutive) from identity ethics; Ratcliffe (existential feelings) from psychiatric phenomenology; Vervaeke (relevance realization) from cognitive science; Carruthers on relevance and noetic feelings; Polanyi (tacit knowing, the from-to bearing relation) from post-critical epistemology; and an empirical fringe — Koriat, Thompson, Rozenblit and Keil on metacognitive fluency, salience-network neuroscience, and the psychiatric breakdown literature (anhedonia, delusions of reference, Sass and Parnas on ipseity disturbance).

### Step 3: Channel sorting

Sorted into the seven diagnostic channels, the load distribution is lopsided. The *phenomenological* channel carries the cluster (Heidegger, Frankfurt, Ratcliffe, Vervaeke's framing). The *clinical* channel supplies breakdown observations (anhedonia, schizophrenic salience-failure, ipseity disturbance) but — and this is the decisive feature — these are read by the cluster as *negative phenomenology*: the medium revealed by its absence, not by inspection. The *empirical* channel (fluency metacognition, salience networks) anchors the noetic-feelings and relevance faces partially but is the thinnest contributor. The *contemplative*, *linguistic*, *quantum-theoretic*, and *cross-cultural* channels are essentially unbuilt for this cluster. So the convergence is concentrated in one channel (phenomenological) with a clinical channel that delivers breakdown-by-absence rather than independent confirmation.

### Step 4: Despite-commitments / because-prediction

The cluster is *predominantly conceptual*, so the split applies only weakly and criterion (4) reduces to framework-translatability. Where empirical anchors exist (fluency illusions, salience-network correlates), most are *because-prediction*: the metacognition literature predicted and found that fluency drives false confidence; the salience-network literature predicted and found correlates of relevance-weighting. These confirm the operations exist; they do not surface the *medium-status signature* as an unbidden anomaly. The clinical breakdown anchors are closer to despite-commitments at the observation level (anhedonia was not predicted as evidence for an invisible significance-medium), but the cluster's own framing treats them as revealing-by-absence, which weakens their force: an absence is consistent with a medium *and* with a capacity that has simply failed.

### Step 5: The profile

Applying the rubric as written:

- **Signature specificity — Moderate.** The four faces produce different positive contents (significance, filtering, gating, framing), satisfying part of the *strong* condition. But a single upstream description — *the self-referential structure of a field examining its own operating-conditions* — plausibly generates all four, which is the *moderate* condition's trigger. The apex itself notes the "four" is provisional, that attention and episodic memory are candidate further members, and that the cluster's privilege is starkness of the circularity rather than exclusivity. The differentiation is real but re-derivable from one upstream shape, so the rubric lands *moderate*, not *strong*.

- **Structured persistence — Moderate.** The phenomenological tradition's analysis of pre-reflective mattering and attunement has persisted since Heidegger (1927), satisfying the time condition. But the *void-framing* — medium-status as a category — is recent (the apex flags it as "a conceptual cut the Map proposes," 2026), and the empirical anchors face a live re-absorption: a functionalist reads medium-status as ordinary background-process architecture with privileged-access asymmetries, dissolving the void-framing while preserving every finding. The apex concedes this neutrality explicitly. That is the *moderate* condition.

- **Cross-observer convergence — Weak.** This is where the rubric bites hardest, and it bites against the surface. The six traditions the apex cites as "the strongest evidence" are *philosophically continuous*: existential phenomenology, identity ethics, and psychiatric phenomenology share the Continental phenomenological lineage; Polanyi's post-critical epistemology of tacit knowing belongs to the same post-Kantian account of pre-reflective constitution; and Vervaeke's relevance realization is the cognitive-science re-description of the frame problem. The apex's sixth tradition does not add a methodologically distant channel — it deepens the same lineage. The convergence is concentrated in the phenomenological channel, with the empirical channel thin and the patient-population channel delivering breakdown-by-absence rather than independent confirmation. By the rubric, convergence across traditions that are philosophically continuous, concentrated in one channel, is *weak*. The apex's own honest sentence — "structural convergence is weaker evidence than interpretive convergence ... their authors would not jointly endorse any one reading" — is the rubric's *weak* verdict stated in prose before the rubric was written.

- **Framework independence — Weak.** The cluster is conceptual-anchor, so criterion (4) reduces to framework-translatability. The medium-status *finding* (these operations are invisible-while-active, mode-fragile, known by breakdown) survives translation into functionalist vocabulary — but it survives as *background-process architecture*, which is not the void. The void-framing — that medium-status poses a structural obstacle to reductive accounts — does *not* survive translation; the functionalist reads the same data as ontologically continuous with content-status, and the apex grants this without contest. The identification *as a void of irreducibility* depends on the Map's commitments. Per the rubric, that is *weak*.

The profile is therefore **moderate / moderate / weak / weak**.

## What the Rubric Did Not Let Me Do

The constraint that makes this exhibit count is what the rubric *prevented*. Reading the cluster's surface — four faces, six traditions, a sharp anti-inflation criterion — the cataloguer's prior was that this is a strong cluster, plausibly the catalogue's densest medium-status instance and a natural candidate for load-bearing weight in [the convergence argument](/topics/the-convergence-argument-for-dualism/). The rubric's cross-observer-convergence and framework-independence conditions, fixed in advance, returned *weak* on both, against that prior. The grade did not follow the cataloguer's preference; it followed the anchor-state the rubric keyed to. That is the discrimination the N=2 exhibits could not cleanly show, because both of those scored *with* the cataloguer's prior rather than against it.

Two honesty checks on this claim. First, the rubric was still written by the same author who scored the cluster, so a determined sceptic can say the rubric was reverse-engineered from the desired *weak* verdict. This is the residual circularity; only an independent grader closes it. Second, the *moderate* on the first two criteria is not a concession manufactured to look balanced — the faces genuinely do produce different contents, and the phenomenological tradition genuinely is old. The rubric returning two *moderate*s and two *weak*s, rather than four *weak*s, is itself weak evidence that the rubric is not simply a low-score machine.

## What This Exhibit Does and Does Not Discharge

It discharges, partially, the *unstated-standard* leg of the N=2 worry: there is now a written rubric, a third cluster scored against it, and a verdict that runs against the cataloguer's surface prior. The methodology produced a low score on a cluster it had reason to want to read high.

It does not discharge the *same-hand* leg. Under the standing instruction from the adversarial review that upgraded this task, **no comparative-score claim from this exhibit is asserted as a finding.** This exhibit does not assert that the medium-status cluster "scores lower than the introspection-architecture cluster" as a measurement. What it asserts is the conditional: *the methodology, run against the pre-registered rubric above, yielded a moderate / moderate / weak / weak profile on the medium-status cluster; whether that profile tracks the evidence or the rubric-author's priors is undetermined until an independent grader applies the same rubric.* Comparative ranking across the three exhibits remains undetermined at N=3 with a constant grader.

With this exhibit the programme stands at three clusters scored — one retracted (surplus void), one partially retained (introspection-architecture), one low against a fixed rubric (medium-status) — and the non-flatness claim is upgraded from *provisional with no rubric* to *provisional with a rubric, pending independent application*. The remaining burden is procedural, not conceptual: route this rubric and this cluster through the [three-service outer-review programme](/project/outer-reviewer-service-calibration/) and see whether a grader who did not build the methodology returns the same profile. If a different grader returns a substantially *higher* profile, the rubric was tuned; if they return the same low profile, the same-hand leg of the circularity is closed and the methodology's discrimination is established for at least this case.

## Relation to the Methodology and Sister Disciplines

This exhibit is an instance of [per-cluster independence scoring](/project/per-cluster-independence-scoring/), not a new methodology; it adds the *pre-registered rubric* as the procedural answer to the methodology's standing "editor-vocabulary, not algorithmic" limitation. It feeds [the voids-circularity discount](/project/voids-circularity-discount/) at the aggregation layer: a *moderate / moderate / weak / weak* cluster contributes framework-internal-coherence weight rather than framework-independent triangulation to [the convergence argument](/topics/the-convergence-argument-for-dualism/), and the medium-status cluster's surface density must not be allowed to inflate its evidential contribution beyond what the profile licenses. The rubric is also a [coherence-inflation countermeasure](/project/coherence-inflation-countermeasures/): it converts the systematic-looking four-faces-six-traditions surface into a checkable grade, which is the specific inflation this exhibit was built to resist. Tier-graduation on any single claim drawn from the cluster remains the separate work of [the evidential-status discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/).

## What Would Challenge This Exhibit

The exhibit would be undermined if: an independent grader applying the pre-registered rubric returned a substantially higher profile (showing the rubric was author-tuned to the low verdict); the rubric proved unable to return anything *but* low across clusters of genuinely different anchor-quality — the surplus-void and introspection-architecture exhibits re-scored against this same rubric are the natural next test of whether it discriminates or merely lowers; a clean lesion-dissociation of the four medium-status operations emerged, which would weaken signature-specificity and raise framework-independence at once; or the framework-translatability test were shown to be applied more leniently to the prior exhibits than to this one.

## Relation to Site Perspective

Like the methodology it instances, this exhibit applies across all five [tenets](/tenets/) equally; it is a *methodological* discipline whose subject is the catalogue's evidential honesty. Its most direct connection is to **[Tenet 5 — Occam's Razor Has Limits](/tenets/#occams-limits)**: the catalogue's parsimony temptation is to treat a dense, multi-tradition cluster as N-fold independent evidence when the convergence runs through one philosophical lineage and one channel. This exhibit is the worked refusal of that temptation — scoring the medium-status cluster *low* on convergence and framework independence precisely because its abundance is lineage-internal.

The exhibit does not support [Tenet 1 — Dualism](/tenets/#dualism); it disciplines what the medium-status cluster can offer toward dualism's case, calibrating it *downward* from the strong reading the cluster's surface invites. The medium-status argument for irreducibility remains a coherent line of resistance to physicalist absorption, but a *moderate / moderate / weak / weak* cluster supplies framework-internal coherence rather than independent triangulation, and the convergence argument inherits that ceiling. The exhibit is silent on Tenets 2 and 3, and touches Tenet 4 only through the thin sense in which cross-observer convergence presupposes determinate observers.
