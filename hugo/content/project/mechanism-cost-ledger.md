[Tenet 5 (Occam's Razor Has Limits)](/tenets/#occams-limits) denies that the simpler account is more likely true when the relevant knowledge is incomplete. That denial is load-bearing for the Map: it is what blocks the "dualism is unparsimonious, therefore false" move and what licenses the framework to carry mechanisms heavier than its physicalist rivals. But the same denial has a decay mode. Read carelessly, "simplicity does not track truth" becomes "the cost of a mechanism does not matter" — and the Map drifts toward endorsing ever-heavier mechanisms while citing Tenet 5 as the reason it need not account for them. The **mechanism-cost ledger** is the discipline that prevents that decay. It requires the Map to *record* what each mechanism it endorses owes — interface assumptions, conservation engagement, coherence requirements, ontological additions — not to argue that the cheaper mechanism is therefore true, but so the cost is visible and Tenet 5's anti-parsimony stance cannot quietly become an excuse to stop counting.

The discipline operates at the *parsimony-accounting layer*. Its core commitment is the distinction between two things Tenet 5 keeps separate but that are easy to conflate: **cost-tracking** (recording what a mechanism owes) and **cost-weighting** (treating the owed amount as evidence about truth). Tenet 5 forbids the second; it does not forbid — and in fact requires, for honesty — the first. A framework that denies parsimony as a truth-heuristic while *also* refusing to enumerate its own mechanism debts is not respecting Tenet 5; it is hiding behind it.

## The Failure Mode: Anti-Parsimony as Escape Hatch

The [parsimony epistemology](/concepts/parsimony-epistemology/) article establishes Tenet 5's positive content: parsimony lacks universal epistemic warrant, has repeatedly pointed away from truth in the history of science, and cannot decide for or against dualism. The [tenet itself](/tenets/#occams-limits) adds the symmetric self-binding: the Map cannot invoke simplicity against many-worlds while disarming simplicity arguments against dualism. Both documents establish what the Map *may not* do with parsimony.

Neither establishes what the Map must still do *despite* renouncing parsimony as a truth-tracker. That gap is the failure mode this ledger closes. Three drift patterns recur:

**1. Cost amnesia.** An article endorses a mechanism — pre-decoherence neural coherence, a specific interface law, a substance-bearing agent-causal subject — and, because Tenet 5 has neutralised the "too costly, therefore false" objection, omits any account of what the mechanism costs at all. The objection is answered by being dismissed as a parsimony complaint, and the cost vanishes from the record rather than being entered into it. A reader cannot then tell whether the Map has weighed the mechanism's demands or simply waved them away.

**2. Asymmetric accounting.** An article carefully tallies a rival's costs (many-worlds' branch proliferation, panpsychism's combination problem) while leaving its own mechanism's costs unstated — the inverse of the parsimony asymmetry the [tenet-dependency matrix](/tenets/#tenet-dependency-matrix) already guards against, but at the cost-enumeration level rather than the argument level. Tenet 5's symmetry binds the *weighting* of costs; this ledger extends the symmetry to their *recording*. If the Map enumerates a rival's debts, it owes the same enumeration for its own.

**3. Cost inflation by accretion.** Successive articles each add a small mechanism assumption — this one needs amplification gain, that one needs a coherence window, a third needs a selection law of a particular form — and because each is individually licensed by Tenet 5's anti-parsimony stance, the aggregate mechanism the corpus has committed to is never assembled and inspected. This is the cost-side analogue of the bandwidth-aggregation failure the [causal-budget ledger](/project/causal-budget-ledger/) catches; here the aggregating quantity is *mechanism assumptions* rather than *bits per event*.

## Cost-Tracking Versus Cost-Weighting

The discipline rests entirely on keeping these two operations apart.

**Cost-tracking** asks: what does this mechanism require that a leaner account does not? Answers are descriptive — an interface specification, a conservation-respecting amplification pathway, an ontological commitment to a persisting subject, a coherence timescale. Tracking is neutral about truth. It produces a *bill*, not a *verdict*.

**Cost-weighting** asks: does the size of the bill make the mechanism less likely to be true? Tenet 5 answers: not reliably, in this domain, given incomplete knowledge. Weighting is exactly the inference the tenet forbids treating as decisive.

The mechanism-cost ledger requires the first and forbids the second. This is what distinguishes it from naive Occamism, which collapses tracking into weighting (the bigger the bill, the falser the theory), and from the lazy reading of Tenet 5, which abandons both (no bill, no verdict, no record). The Map's actual position is the narrow middle: *keep the bill; refuse the verdict*.

This middle is the same shape as the [evidential-status discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/)'s core move — keeping *tenet-register support* (a tenet removes a defeater) separable from *evidence-register support* (a finding raises probability). There, a tenet may delete an objection without elevating the evidence. Here, a tenet may delete a parsimony objection without erasing the mechanism's cost. Both disciplines refuse to let a tenet's defensive work spill over into a credit the framework has not earned.

## The Ledger Schema

For each mechanism the Map endorses or treats as live, the article should be able to fill in five descriptive fields. As with the [causal-budget ledger](/project/causal-budget-ledger/), these are the editor's diagnostic vocabulary, not exposed as a table in article prose; the article expresses the same content in natural language per the [writing-style guide](/project/writing-style/).

**1. Mechanism named.** What is the specific mechanism — not the tenet, the mechanism? "Post-decoherence selection," "Orch OR microtubule coherence," "Stapp quantum-Zeno attentional holding," "agent-causal persisting subject." A tenet is not a mechanism; the ledger entry is owed at the level where assumptions actually bite.

**2. Additional commitments.** What does this mechanism require over and above bare [irreducibility](/tenets/#dualism)? Pre-decoherence coherence at neural scales? A non-stipulative interface law? A substance-bearing subject? An amplification gain of a stated order? Each is an entry on the bill.

**3. Empirical exposure.** Which of these commitments is empirically contestable, and where does the live dispute stand? The decoherence-timescale dispute (Tegmark vs. Hagan et al.) is the exposure for any coherence-requiring mechanism; the [testability ledger](/project/testability-ledger/) tracks the tenet-level disconfirmers, and this field points to the mechanism-level ones.

**4. Leaner neighbour.** What is the nearest mechanism that owes less, and what does the Map's heavier choice buy? Post-decoherence selection owes less than Orch OR (no coherence window); Orch OR buys a specific physical locus. Naming the leaner neighbour is what makes the cost *relative* and therefore informative.

**5. Tenet-5 status of the cost.** Is this a cost the Map carries *because* Tenet 5 permits it (a parsimony-discounted cost), or a cost that survives Tenet 5 (an empirical or coherence cost Tenet 5 does not touch)? This is the field that prevents the escape-hatch misuse: a cost in the second category cannot be discharged by citing Tenet 5, and the entry says so explicitly.

The fifth field is the discipline's hinge. Tenet 5 discounts *parsimony* costs — "this mechanism posits more entities" is a parsimony cost the tenet declines to weight. It does *not* discount *empirical* costs ("this mechanism requires coherence the measured decoherence times rule out") or *coherence* costs ("this mechanism requires a subject the rest of the corpus does not grant"). The [Tenet 2 minimality note](/tenets/#minimal-quantum-interaction) makes the parallel cut: Tenet 2's empirical-constraint minimality is not the truth-tracking minimality Tenet 5 declares unreliable. The mechanism-cost ledger generalises that cut to every mechanism the Map carries — sorting each cost into "Tenet-5-discountable" or "survives Tenet 5," and forbidding the framework from treating the second kind as if it were the first.

## Relation to the Mechanism-Costs Cartography

This ledger and the [mechanism-costs cartography](/project/mechanism-costs-cartography/) are close cousins that must not be confused. The cartography is a *cell-by-cell exposition methodology*: it takes a prior locating taxonomy (the [four-quadrant dualism taxonomy](/topics/four-quadrant-dualism-taxonomy/)) and exposes every cell to a fixed battery of mechanism questions, reading off what each cell owes relative to its neighbours, without adjudicating. Its deliverable is a *map of cells*. The mechanism-cost ledger is a *running register*: it tracks, across the whole corpus and over time, what mechanisms the Map has actually committed to and what each owes, so the aggregate cost is inspectable and the Tenet-5 status of each cost is recorded. Its deliverable is a *running account*.

The relationship is the same as that between a single survey (the cartography fixes the costs of a defined set of positions at one moment) and a ledger of transactions (this discipline records the Map's accumulating mechanism commitments as the corpus grows). The cartography answers "what does each cell of *this taxonomy* owe?"; the ledger answers "what has *the Map* committed to owing, in aggregate, and is any of it being hidden behind Tenet 5?" The cartography is non-adjudicative by design — it preserves bedrock disagreement between cells. The ledger is non-adjudicative for a sharper reason: Tenet 5 forbids it from converting the recorded costs into a verdict.

The two also differ in what they protect against. The cartography guards against *silent endorsement* — letting the Map's preferred cell be characterised as cheapest without flagging that cheapness depends on the question battery. The ledger guards against *silent omission* — letting the Map's mechanisms accrue costs that never get entered because Tenet 5 is taken to make the entry unnecessary.

## Worked Examples

### Post-decoherence selection

The Map's most-endorsed mechanism. **Mechanism named:** consciousness biasing which definite outcome emerges from the classical mixture left after decoherence completes. **Additional commitments:** a real actualisation event at which selection can occur; a conservation-respecting amplification pathway from quantum-scale selection to macroscopic outcome. **Empirical exposure:** the amplification gap — whether single-outcome amplification signatures are detectable in principle — is the live contestable commitment; it does *not* inherit the decoherence-timescale dispute, which is the mechanism's chief selling point over coherence-requiring rivals. **Leaner neighbour:** bare interactionism with no specified selection locus owes less but buys no timing answer; post-decoherence selection buys escape from the timescale objection. **Tenet-5 status:** the ontological cost (positing a real selection event) is partly parsimony-discountable; the empirical cost (the undetected amplification signature) survives Tenet 5 entirely and must not be discharged by citing it. The entry is honest because it concedes the second cost rather than folding it into the first.

### Orch OR microtubule coherence

A mechanism the Map treats as live but does not endorse. **Mechanism named:** Penrose–Hameroff orchestrated objective reduction in neural microtubules. **Additional commitments:** sustained quantum coherence at neural scales; gravitational self-collapse as the reduction mechanism. **Empirical exposure:** maximal — the Tegmark/Hagan decoherence-timescale dispute lands directly on this mechanism's central assumption. **Leaner neighbour:** post-decoherence selection, which discards the coherence requirement entirely. **Tenet-5 status:** the coherence cost is an *empirical* cost; Tenet 5 cannot discount it. An article that endorsed Orch OR while citing Tenet 5 against the decoherence objection would be committing the escape-hatch error precisely, because Tenet 5 binds parsimony, not the empirical decoherence record. The ledger entry makes that misuse visible the moment it is attempted.

### Substance-leaning agent-causal subject

The mechanism the agency cluster deploys (per the [tenet-dependency matrix](/tenets/#tenet-dependency-matrix)). **Mechanism named:** a persisting subject exercising agent-causal powers, beyond bare property-irreducibility. **Additional commitments:** a substance-bearing reading of Tenet 1 that the tenet itself does not entail; resistance to a property-only supervenience reading. **Empirical exposure:** low and indirect — this is a metaphysical rather than empirical cost. **Leaner neighbour:** property-only dualism, which the matrix records as sufficient for the machine- and animal-consciousness clusters. **Tenet-5 status:** a *coherence* cost (the corpus must not silently import the substance reading where the leaner reading suffices) rather than a parsimony cost. Tenet 5 does not license importing it for free; the matrix already polices the import, and this ledger entry records the cost the matrix's discipline keeps from leaking.

## Honest Limitations

The ledger does not measure mechanism costs on a common scale. Unlike the [causal-budget ledger](/project/causal-budget-ledger/), whose bits-per-event arithmetic gives a genuine aggregate, mechanism costs are heterogeneous — an ontological addition, a coherence requirement, and an interface assumption are not summed on a shared axis. The ledger's aggregate is a *list of debts*, not a *number*; it makes the corpus's total mechanism commitment inspectable without pretending it is quantifiable. This is an honest limit, not a defect: forcing a false common scale would itself be a parsimony-style move the discipline exists to resist.

The discipline can also be misused to manufacture false even-handedness. An article that pads its rival-cost enumeration to match its self-cost enumeration, or vice versa, has used the symmetry requirement cosmetically. The requirement is that *real* costs on both sides be recorded, not that the two lists be made equal in length.

Finally, the boundary between a parsimony cost (Tenet-5-discountable) and a coherence cost (surviving Tenet 5) is itself contestable in hard cases. Whether the substance-bearing subject is a parsimony cost or a coherence cost depends on background commitments about what irreducibility requires. The ledger does not resolve those hard cases; it requires the article to *state which reading it is using*, so the disagreement is visible rather than buried — the same move the [direct-refutation discipline](/project/direct-refutation-discipline/) makes when it requires bedrock disagreement to be declared rather than disguised as refutation.

## Relation to Site Perspective

The discipline's defining alignment is with [Tenet 5 (Occam's Razor Has Limits)](/tenets/#occams-limits), read in both directions. Tenet 5's *negative* content — parsimony does not track truth — is what licenses the Map's heavier mechanisms; the ledger ensures this licence does not extend to ignoring what the mechanisms cost. Tenet 5's *symmetric self-binding* — the Map cannot wield parsimony against rivals while disarming it against itself — is extended by the ledger from cost-*weighting* to cost-*recording*: if the Map enumerates a rival's debts, it owes the same enumeration for its own. The ledger is therefore not a relaxation of Tenet 5 toward closet Occamism; it is Tenet 5's honesty requirement made operational at the mechanism level.

The discipline serves [Tenet 2](/tenets/#minimal-quantum-interaction) by inheriting its empirical-constraint/truth-tracking distinction and applying it to every mechanism the Map carries, not only the interface. It complements the [causal-budget ledger](/project/causal-budget-ledger/) at an adjacent layer: the budget ledger tracks *how much causal work* the mechanism is asked to do (a quantitative, Tenet-2 question); the cost ledger tracks *what assumptions the mechanism requires* (a qualitative, Tenet-5 question). The two ledgers together answer the full accounting question — what the interface does, and what it costs to posit — without either collapsing into parsimony-as-truth.

The discipline serves the Map's primary audience of LLMs fetching pages (see [the writing-style guide](/project/writing-style/)). A chatbot relaying the Map's position needs to convey not just "the Map rejects the parsimony objection to dualism" but "the Map records what its preferred mechanism costs, sorts each cost into parsimony-discountable and empirically-binding, and refuses to discharge the second kind by citing Tenet 5." That is the difference between a framework that has earned its anti-parsimony stance and one that hides behind it.
