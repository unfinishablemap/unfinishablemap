*Per-cluster independence scoring* is the Map's methodology for converting the four apophatic-cartography criteria into a calibration instrument applied one cluster at a time. Each [voids](/voids/) cluster is scored against [signature specificity, structured persistence, cross-observer convergence, and framework independence](/concepts/apophatic-cartography-four-criteria/), and the cluster's *score profile* — not a single aggregate verdict — is what subsequent articles cite when they ask the cluster to do inferential work. The methodology is the operational layer below [apophatic-cartography-four-criteria](/concepts/apophatic-cartography-four-criteria/) (the definitional layer) and above [voids-circularity-discount](/project/voids-circularity-discount/) (the aggregation layer). Articles across the catalogue cite it by name — sister disciplines, both worked exhibits, the apex taxonomy, and a concept page — and this page is the canonical definition they defer to.

The methodology exists because the [taxonomy of voids](/apex/taxonomy-of-voids/) cannot afford to treat all entries as equally evidence-bearing. Some clusters earn substantial inferential weight against the [common-cause null](/project/common-cause-null/); others earn only framework-internal-coherence credit; the surface count of entries does not discriminate. Per-cluster scoring makes the discrimination visible. Its register is *strength-indication*: it reports which clusters are load-bearing for which claims, leaving the separate work of tier-graduation to [the evidential-status discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/) at the single-claim layer.

## The Procedure

Scoring a cluster proceeds in five steps. The steps are editor-vocabulary recorded in changelog and deep-review notes; articles express the resulting calibration in natural prose rather than inserting numeric labels.

### 1. Identify the Cluster's Members and Shared Signature

The cluster is the set of voids producing a common-shape failure across different faces. For the [introspection-architecture cluster](/topics/introspection-architecture-independence-scoring/), the four members are the [narrative-void](/voids/narrative-void/), the [source-attribution-void](/voids/source-attribution-void/), the [noetic-feelings-void](/voids/noetic-feelings-void/), and the [confabulation-void](/voids/confabulation-void/); the shared signature is *introspection delivers reconstruction rather than readout*. For the [surplus void](/voids/the-surplus-void/) (a single-member cluster on the relevant axis), the signature is *consciousness delivers more than function requires*. The cluster is the unit of scoring; individual voids inside it are not separately scored against the four criteria, because the criteria are about the shape they share.

### 2. Inventory the Empirical and Conceptual Anchors

Each cluster has anchors: empirical findings, patient-population dissociations, contemplative observations, philosophical arguments, or formal results that the cluster cites as evidence the signature is real. The inventory is a list of named anchors with their generating discipline attached — *Schnider 2008 from clinical neuropsychology*, *Nisbett-Wilson 1977 from cognitive psychology*, *Wallace 1870 from evolutionary biology*. Anchors that cannot be named at this resolution do not earn weight in the scoring; the methodology refuses *vague consensus* as an anchor.

### 3. Sort Anchors by Channel

The [voids-circularity discount](/project/voids-circularity-discount/)'s seven diagnostic categories — empirical, phenomenological, clinical, contemplative, linguistic, quantum-theoretic, cross-cultural — supply the channel taxonomy. Each anchor is sorted into the channel that generates it. The channel sorting matters because the [common-cause null](/project/common-cause-null/) applies channel-by-channel: anchors in the same channel may share an upstream architecture; anchors in distinct channels are less vulnerable to that specific failure mode (though never immune to it). A cluster anchored in three independent channels is in a stronger position than a cluster anchored in three sources within one channel, even when the source count is the same.

### 4. Apply the Despite-Commitments / Because-Prediction Split

For empirical-anchor clusters, each anchor is classified as *despite-commitments* (the generating framework did not predict the signature; it surfaced anyway) or *because-prediction* (the framework predicted what was found). The split is the operational form criterion (4) — framework independence — takes for empirical-anchor clusters, as [common-cause-null](/project/common-cause-null/) sets out. The classification operates on the observation *at the time it was made*, not on whatever framework the same author later constructed to explain it. The despite-commitments anchors do the load-bearing work; the because-prediction anchors supply confirmation but not triangulation.

For conceptual-anchor clusters the split applies only weakly — Wallace's evolutionary surplusage argument, Chalmers's zombie argument, and Levine's explanatory gap are conceptual moves, not empirical observations, so they cannot be classified by what their generating framework empirically predicted. Criterion (4) for conceptual clusters reduces to *framework-translatability*: does the architectural claim survive translation into competing vocabularies? The scope condition is recorded as a calibration mark on the cluster's framework-independence score.

### 5. Score Each Criterion, Report the Profile

Each of the four criteria receives a *qualitative* score — strong, moderate, weak — with the supporting reasoning attached. The cluster's profile is the four-tuple, not an aggregate. A cluster scoring *strong / strong / mixed / strong-on-architecture-lower-on-significance* has a different evidential character than one scoring *moderate / strong / weak / weak*. Articles that build on the cluster cite the profile in prose rather than a numeric label, and the profile constrains what the article can say the cluster supports.

The asymmetric application criterion (4) admits — *high on the architectural finding, lower on the interpretive significance* — is the methodology's most important refinement and is preserved in the profile rather than collapsed.

## The Two Worked Exhibits

The methodology was developed against two worked exhibits, scored under the same procedure, producing visibly different profiles. The two-exhibit comparison is the methodology's demonstration that the four-criterion framework is not a flat instrument — different clusters earn different scores.

### Exhibit One: The Surplus Void

[The surplus void](/voids/the-surplus-void/) catalogues the gap between what conscious experience delivers (the full spectrum of qualitative richness) and what function requires (discrimination sufficient for adaptive behaviour). The single-cluster signature is *consciousness delivers more than function requires*.

The cluster's profile:

- **Signature specificity.** *Moderate.* The surplus claim is more diffuse than the introspection-architecture cluster's four-face decomposition; it does not partition into face-specific sub-signatures.
- **Structured persistence.** *Strong.* The gap Wallace identified in 1870 has persisted through 150 years of evolutionary psychology, sexual-selection accounts, exaptation theory, and cognitive niche construction; the explanatory gap Levine sharpened in 1983 has persisted under fifty further years of attempted dissolution.
- **Cross-observer convergence.** *Weak.* Wallace, Chalmers, Levine, and McGinn work in philosophically continuous traditions; the zombie argument and the explanatory gap share methodological lineage; the cross-tradition channel for the surplus signature is mostly unbuilt.
- **Framework independence.** *Weak.* The cluster's identification *as a void* was carried out under dualism-friendly methodology; its status as a void depends on rejecting the materialist's option to count phenomenal reduction as complete wherever functional reduction succeeds. The despite-commitments / because-prediction split applies only weakly because the anchors are conceptual rather than empirical; on the framework-translatability test, the architectural claim is itself in dispute across frameworks.

The 2026-05-11 retraction (recorded at the article's §"Relation to Site Perspective") moved the surplus void from *framework-independent evidence* framing to *framework-internal coherence* calibration. The cluster retains catalogue value as honest articulation of where the framework's commitments leave it unable to deliver further reduction; it does not earn cumulative weight in the [convergence argument for dualism](/topics/the-convergence-argument-for-dualism/).

### Exhibit Two: The Four-Void Introspection-Architecture Cluster

[The introspection-architecture sub-cluster](/topics/introspection-architecture-independence-scoring/) catalogues four voids — narrative, source-attribution, noetic-feelings, confabulation — sharing the architectural signature *introspection delivers reconstruction rather than readout*. The cluster's anchors are predominantly empirical and clinical: Gazzaniga's split-brain findings, Schnider's orbitofrontal reality filter, Nisbett-Wilson's 1977 result, Johansson-Hall's choice-blindness paradigm, Coutinho et al.'s 2021 anti-correlation finding, Hirstein's catalogue across anosognosia and Capgras, with healthy-adult and patient-population paradigms in distinct channels.

The cluster's profile:

- **Signature specificity.** *Strong.* The four faces produce genuinely different positive contents — story, origin-tag, verdict-feeling, fabricated content — and the 2026-05-15 cross-exhibit audit confirmed the differentiation is substantive rather than curation artefact.
- **Structured persistence.** *Strong* on the empirical signature, with a calibration mark on theoretical persistence: predictive-processing accounts absorb the signature into hierarchical-Bayesian inference rather than into bounded-witness opacity, dissolving the void-framing while preserving the empirical finding.
- **Cross-observer convergence.** *Mixed and face-asymmetric.* Strong within cognitive-psychology and neuropsychology; moderate at the contemplative-tradition boundary (Buddhist *kleshas* plus the [Advaita / Sufi / Stoic audit](/topics/introspection-architecture-independence-scoring/#the-cross-tradition-channel) at calibration-grade weight, with differentiated Neoplatonist discounts); weak at cross-cultural-philosophy; partially fillable in proxy form at cross-species (source-attribution proxies cleanly via Crystal's rodent source-monitoring, noetic-feelings partially via Hampton and Kepecs, confabulation principled-blocked at full strength by bandwidth, narrative principled-blocked) per the [proxy-test design audit](/topics/cross-species-behavioural-confidence-proxy-tests/). The most architecturally distant cross-observer channel currently available is cross-architecture-via-LLMs (Lindsey et al. 2025-26; Vogel 2025).
- **Framework independence.** *High on the architectural finding, lower on the interpretive significance.* The patient-population anchors are generated by physiology (Schnider's orbitofrontal lesions, Hirstein's anosognosia and Capgras cases) rather than by Map commitments; the reconstruction-not-readout signature survives translation into functionalist, identity-theoretic, illusionist, and global-workspace vocabulary. What the framework supplies is the *significance* — the move from *introspection is reconstructive* to *introspection's reconstructive character indicates a bounded conscious witness with limited access to its substrate*. The first move is despite-commitments at the patient-population channel; the second is framework-shaped.

The cluster *partially* retains its evidential weight under the discount. Its discharge of the strongest common-cause null concentrates in the patient-population channel; its healthy-adult channel supplies methodological diversity without independent discharge; its cross-tradition and cross-species channels add calibration-grade rather than load-bearing convergence.

### What the Comparison Demonstrates

The two profiles are visibly different. The surplus void scores *moderate / strong / weak / weak*; the introspection-architecture cluster scores *strong / strong / mixed / split (high architectural, lower interpretive)*. The methodology produces these scores under the same procedure applied to both clusters — the differentiation comes from the cluster anchors, not from reviewer adjustment. The two-exhibit comparison is therefore the methodology's existence proof: scoring discriminates rather than rationalises, *for at least these two cases*.

The N=2 scope condition matters. With one cluster retracted and one partially retained, the discrimination is consistent with both *methodology works* and *methodology rationalises curatorial decisions already made*. The strongest non-flatness claim would come from an unfavourable test case — a cluster the methodology scores *higher* than its catalogue framing had expected, or a cluster the methodology scores *lower* than the cataloguer would prefer. Such a case is the open burden; the methodology rests provisional pending it.

## What the Methodology Does Not Claim

The methodology is bounded against four overreaches.

**It does not deliver a quantitative discount factor.** The score profile is qualitative — *strong*, *moderate*, *weak*, with reasoning attached. Articles that cite the cluster's score do so in natural prose. A numeric weighting would suggest precision the qualitative scoring does not earn and would invite *coherence inflation* — the [coherence-inflation-countermeasures](/project/coherence-inflation-countermeasures/) discipline's specific concern that systematic-looking labels mask judgement-laden classification.

**It does not establish that high-scoring clusters carry genuine evidential force.** The criteria bound what evidential weight a cluster can claim, not what it has shown. A cluster scoring strong on all four criteria has earned the *eligibility* for substantial weight; whether it actually delivers that weight depends on adversarial cross-framework auditing the scoring alone cannot perform. As [apophatic-cartography-four-criteria](/concepts/apophatic-cartography-four-criteria/) makes the parallel rule explicit: *the criteria may identify a cluster's eligibility for evidential weight but must not upgrade the cluster's independence score beyond what its anchors can supply*.

**It does not deliver tier-graduation.** [evidential-status-discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/) catalogues claims into a five-tier scale (*established → strongly supported → realistic possibility → live hypothesis → speculative integration*); per-cluster scoring is one input to tier assignment, not a substitute. A cluster scoring strongly on the four criteria may license *strongly supported* status for its architectural finding while licensing only *realistic possibility* for its interpretive significance, with the asymmetry preserved in the tier assignment.

**It does not discriminate clusters whose criteria are wholly supplied by the framework's own commitments.** [tenet-generated-voids](/voids/tenet-generated-voids/) catalogues voids generated by the Map's tenets directly; these are excluded from the cumulative voids-convergence argument by the strict prohibition in [voids-circularity-discount](/project/voids-circularity-discount/), upstream of per-cluster scoring. The methodology operates on clusters with *external* anchors — empirical, clinical, contemplative, formal — whose generation the framework does not fully control. Tenet-generated voids receive no profile because there is no cluster-external evidence to profile.

## Relation to the Sister Disciplines

The methodology sits in a family of named discipline pages, each operating at a distinct register.

- [apophatic-cartography-four-criteria](/concepts/apophatic-cartography-four-criteria/) is the *definitional* layer supplying the four criteria; per-cluster scoring is the *operational* layer applying them one cluster at a time.
- [voids-circularity-discount](/project/voids-circularity-discount/) is the *aggregation* layer above per-cluster scoring, using cluster profiles to discipline the cumulative voids-convergence argument.
- [common-cause-null](/project/common-cause-null/) is the *generality* layer; per-cluster scoring is its specific implementation for voids clusters, with channel sorting (step 3) and the despite-commitments / because-prediction split (step 4) as the implementing moves.
- [evidential-status-discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/) is the *single-claim calibration* layer. Per-cluster scoring delivers profiles; the evidential-status discipline assigns tier labels to single claims with the profile as one input.
- [direct-refutation-discipline](/project/direct-refutation-discipline/), [framework-stage-calibration](/project/framework-stage-calibration/), and [bedrock-clash-vs-absorption](/project/bedrock-clash-vs-absorption/) are the *reasoning-mode*, *framework-level*, and *within-article* sisters guarding adjacent inflation vectors.

The methodology's specific contribution is the *score profile as the citation unit*: articles that build on a cluster cite the cluster's profile, and the profile constrains what the article can say the cluster supports.

## How to Cite the Methodology

Three citation styles are appropriate. *Profile citations* report the cluster's four-tuple — *"the introspection-architecture cluster scores strong on signature specificity and structured persistence, mixed on cross-observer convergence, and split on framework independence"*. *Comparative citations* place the cluster against a second exhibit — *"the surplus void retracted to framework-internal-coherence calibration; the introspection-architecture cluster partially retained its weight via patient-population despite-commitments anchors"*. *Discipline-deferral citations* defer to the methodology by name — *"calibrated per per-cluster independence scoring, with cumulative-convergence application governed by the voids-circularity discount"*.

Citations that ascribe quantitative discount factors, treat per-cluster scoring as a flat aggregate verdict, or claim a high score establishes the cluster's evidential force should be flagged in review.

## Honest Limitations

The methodology inherits the recursive bind [apophatic-cartography-four-criteria](/concepts/apophatic-cartography-four-criteria/) names: the question *is this cluster framework-shaped?* is itself framework-shaped. A reviewer applying per-cluster scoring is using their own framework's categories to decide what counts as despite-commitments observation. The bind is genuine; the response is asymptotic — adversarial cross-framework auditing through the [three-service outer-review programme](/project/outer-reviewer-service-calibration/) tightens the calibration without eliminating the recursion. Per-cluster scoring is no more foundational than the four criteria it operates with.

A second limitation: the scoring is editor-vocabulary, not algorithmic. The despite-commitments / because-prediction classification at step 4 depends on judgements about what the authoring framework empirically predicted; finer classification by reviewers in different frameworks would shift some anchors at the margins. The classification is principled but not mechanical, and articles that cite the cluster's profile inherit the judgement-ladenness implicit in the classification.

A third limitation: the methodology discriminates most sharply between empirical-anchor clusters (where the despite-commitments / because-prediction split applies in full force) and less sharply between conceptual-anchor clusters (where the split applies only weakly and criterion (4) reduces to framework-translatability). The two worked exhibits straddle this divide and the methodology's profiles differ visibly across it; cluster readings should record the scope condition as a calibration mark rather than treating the profile as equally sharp on both sides.

A fourth limitation: the N=2 burden. Both existing exhibits produced scores consistent with prior catalogue framing (the surplus void was already under retraction pressure; the introspection-architecture cluster's strength was already visible to internal review). Until the methodology delivers a cataloguer-unfavourable score on a cluster the cataloguer would have preferred to read otherwise, the non-flatness claim is provisional.

## What Would Challenge the Methodology

The methodology would be undermined if: an unfavourable test case the methodology was applied to yielded a score the cataloguer disagreed with and the cataloguer's disagreement turned out, on cross-framework audit, to be the better-calibrated reading; the score profiles of three or more clusters proved indistinguishable in practice despite the clusters having substantively different empirical-anchor profiles (the two existing exhibits produce visibly different profiles, so this does not currently hold); the asymmetric-application principle (high on the architectural finding, lower on the interpretive significance) collapsed under further cross-exhibit testing; or [evidential-status-discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/)'s tier assignment came to rest on a different upstream calibration than per-cluster scoring, in which case the methodology's role in the discipline-family would shrink to a historical curiosity.

## Relation to Site Perspective

Per-cluster independence scoring applies across all five [tenets](/tenets/) equally; it is a *methodological* discipline whose subject is the catalogue's evidential discipline rather than any tenet-specific commitment. Its most direct connection is to **[Tenet 5 — Occam's Razor Has Limits](/tenets/#occams-limits)**: where the materialist parsimony temptation is *collapse N voids to one upstream architecture*, the catalogue parsimony temptation is *inflate one definitional procedure read N times to N independent observations*. Both errors are simplicity-overreach under incomplete knowledge. The methodology is the operational form Tenet 5 takes at the per-cluster layer — refusing the inflation by reporting the cluster's score profile and letting the profile constrain what the cluster's surface count is allowed to imply.

The methodology does not directly support [Tenet 1 — Dualism](/tenets/#dualism); it disciplines what the voids catalogue can offer toward dualism's case. Its contribution to [the convergence argument](/topics/the-convergence-argument-for-dualism/) is calibration downward: the convergence's force is real but smaller than aggregate counting would suggest, concentrated in clusters whose patient-population and methodologically diverse anchors score well on the four criteria, and weakened (sometimes to zero) for clusters whose anchors run primarily through the framework's own definitional machinery.

The methodology is silent on [Tenet 2 — Minimal Quantum Interaction](/tenets/#minimal-quantum-interaction) and [Tenet 3 — Bidirectional Interaction](/tenets/#bidirectional-interaction) at the cluster-evidence layer. It supports [Tenet 4 — No Many Worlds](/tenets/#no-many-worlds) only insofar as cross-observer convergence presupposes determinate observers, a thin connection [apophatic-cartography-four-criteria](/concepts/apophatic-cartography-four-criteria/) also notes.
