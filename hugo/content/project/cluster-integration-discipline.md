A *cluster-integration argument* supports a load-bearing inference with a set of independent considerations whose joint force derives from systematic correspondence between the cluster's shape and the explanandum's shape, not from the strength of any single member. The Map's catalogue now carries three converged exhibits of the pattern at different grains — the [convergence argument for dualism](/topics/the-convergence-argument-for-dualism/)'s three argument clusters (metaphysical grain), the [binding problem](/topics/the-binding-problem/)'s five varieties of phenomenal unity (phenomenal grain), and [baseline cognition](/concepts/baseline-cognition/)'s five consciousness-dependent capacities (functional grain). This document names the discipline these articles share, distinguishes it from the convergence-argument-as-such (cluster-integration is the structural form; convergence-argument is one deployment of it), and specifies the editorial rules — calibration register, falsifier-design pattern, independence audit — that future articles invoking the pattern must satisfy.

The discipline operates at the *argument-architecture* layer, separable from the calibration layer that [evidential-status discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/) governs, the editorial layer that [bedrock-clash vs. absorption](/project/bedrock-clash-vs-absorption/) governs, and the framework-stage layer that [framework-stage calibration](/project/framework-stage-calibration/) governs. Where those disciplines control *how the catalogue labels claims* and *how individual articles handle objections*, cluster-integration discipline controls *how a single article structures an inference that no single piece of evidence can carry*. It joins the family of named methodological disciplines as the seventh: alongside [closed-loop opportunity execution](/project/closed-loop-opportunity-execution/) (cycle level), [coalesce-condense-apex-stability](/concepts/coalesce-condense-apex-stability/) (article-set level), [bedrock-clash vs. absorption](/project/bedrock-clash-vs-absorption/) (within-article level), [framework-stage calibration](/project/framework-stage-calibration/) (framework level), [evidential-status discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/) (calibration level), and [direct-refutation discipline](/project/direct-refutation-discipline/) (reasoning-mode level), this discipline operates at the *argument-architecture* level — the structural form a multi-part inferential case takes when no single consideration is decisive.

## The Structural Form

A cluster-integration argument has three parts: a load-bearing inferential move (the explanandum the article exists to address), a cluster of independent considerations (the explanans members), and a correspondence claim that links them (the assertion that the cluster's shape *systematically* tracks the explanandum's shape).

The correspondence claim is the load-bearing feature. The cluster's strength does not come from the number of its members, nor from the strength of the strongest member, nor from the average across members. It comes from the structural fact that the explanandum has features the cluster's distribution-of-members tracks. *Five* binding varieties matter because phenomenal unity has five recognisable subtypes, not because five is a magic number; if subject binding turned out to reduce to temporal binding without remainder, the cluster would have four members, the correspondence would shrink, and the argument would weaken proportionally.

This distinguishes cluster-integration from two near neighbours that are sometimes confused with it. *Cumulative force* arguments work by adding independent supports for a single conclusion — each support is a separable reason that could in principle carry the conclusion alone, just less reliably. *Inference to the best explanation* works by ranking competing explanations on shared criteria — fit, simplicity, scope. Cluster-integration is neither. Its members do not carry the conclusion individually (the binding problem's temporal-binding variety does not on its own establish that phenomenal unity is irreducible to physical coordination); the conclusion is not the *best* explanation of any single member but the explanation that *matches the shape of the cluster*.

## The Calibration Register

The discipline imposes three calibration rules on any cluster-integration deployment.

**Rule 1: Independence audit.** Cluster-internal dependencies reduce effective independence; the audit must be performed explicitly. The convergence-argument article performs this audit at the cluster level — its three clusters (subjective experience / structural features / cross-traditional convergence) are clearly independent; within each cluster the arguments cluster around shared assumptions and contribute less than naive multiplication suggests. The article's honest statement of "two clearly independent clusters and a third partially earned" is the discipline operating: where the audit reveals a cluster member is largely derivative of another, the article must report that and not count it as independent contribution.

**Rule 2: Systematicity claim must be stated.** The correspondence-as-strength move requires saying *what* about the cluster's distribution tracks the explanandum's distribution. The binding-problem article does this by naming the five varieties and observing that the BP1/BP2 gap persists *across all five* — perception, time, cognition, modality, subjecthood — which is the load-bearing structural claim. Baseline-cognition does it by naming the five consciousness-dependent capacities (working memory, novel combinations, spontaneous intentional action, metarepresentation, counterfactual reasoning) and observing that the human-ape gap *systematically* tracks consciousness-dependent capacities rather than tracking capacities at random. The convergence article does it by naming the three argument-clusters and observing that they exploit *different* features of consciousness through *different* logical routes.

**Rule 3: Cluster boundaries must be defensible.** A cluster of five whose members were chosen post-hoc to fit the inference is no stronger than a single argument. The discipline requires that cluster boundaries be drawable from features of the explanandum *prior to* the inference: BP1/BP2 derives from Revonsuo's pre-Map taxonomy of unity-puzzles; the five baseline-cognition capacities derive from Global Workspace Theory's independent identification of consciousness-dependent functions; the three argument-clusters derive from the standard taxonomy of arguments for dualism that pre-dates the convergence framing. Where cluster boundaries are constructed for the inference, the article must say so and discount accordingly.

## The Falsifier-Design Pattern

A cluster-integration argument inherits a specific falsifier-design pattern from its structural form: each cluster member must support a falsifier that would *dissociate it from the inference*, and the cluster as a whole must support a structural falsifier that would dissolve the correspondence claim.

The pattern is visible in the catalogue's two strongest exhibits. The binding-problem article's "What Would Challenge This View?" section lists six conditions — five member-level falsifiers (one per variety: a reductive explanation of any BP2 variety would weaken the structural-persistence argument; a computational model predicting conjoint phenomenology would narrow the BP1/BP2 gap; symmetric temporal recalibration would weaken the consciousness-specificity claim; the identity move achieving adequacy would reclassify the BP2 gap as epistemic; evidence against quantum holism would cut the substrate bridge) plus one cluster-level falsifier (the vitalism template — multiple seemingly irreducible phenomena dissolving under a single framework — that would defeat the structural-persistence argument as such). Baseline-cognition's five falsifiers map the same pattern at functional grain: each falsifier targets a specific consciousness-dependent capacity (great apes achieving human-level cognition; working memory expansion without cognitive gains; unconscious logical reasoning; AI achieving human-level cognition without consciousness; random distribution of the gap), and together they specify what the *systematic tracking* claim requires.

The discipline's editorial rule: a cluster-integration article without per-member falsifiers is under-specified. The cluster does not earn its argumentative weight until each member is shown to be dissociable from the inference under specifiable conditions — otherwise the cluster is decorative rather than load-bearing.

## The Three Catalogue Exhibits at Three Grains

The catalogue's three exhibits demonstrate the discipline at three different grains and serve as the primary referential anchors for future cluster-integration deployments.

**Metaphysical grain: [the convergence argument for dualism](/topics/the-convergence-argument-for-dualism/).** The load-bearing inference is the irreducibility of consciousness to physical processes; the cluster has three members (subjective experience arguments; structural arguments from unity, intentionality, and rational agency; cross-traditional convergence on irreducibility); the correspondence claim is that arguments starting from different premises, using different methods, and targeting different features of consciousness all point the same way. The article's honest assessment — "substantially stronger than any single argument," not "overwhelming" — exemplifies the calibration register.

**Phenomenal grain: [the binding problem](/topics/the-binding-problem/).** The load-bearing inference is that phenomenal unity is irreducible to physical coordination; the cluster has five members (intra-modal, cross-modal, temporal, cognitive, subject binding); the correspondence claim is that the BP1/BP2 gap *persists across all five varieties* rather than dissolving under one. The condense pass that reduced the article from 4,719 to 3,292 words preserved every cluster member because the cluster's shape is the article's load-bearing structure — losing a variety would mean losing a unit of correspondence.

**Functional grain: [baseline cognition](/concepts/baseline-cognition/).** The load-bearing inference is that the human-ape gap is evidence consciousness amplifies cognition rather than being epiphenomenal; the cluster has five members (working memory expansion; novel-combination capacity; spontaneous intentional action; metarepresentation; counterfactual reasoning); the correspondence claim is that the gap *systematically* tracks consciousness-dependent capacities rather than tracking cognitive complexity at random. The five-falsifier scaffold is the catalogue's strongest deployment of the per-member falsifier pattern.

The three grains exhaust the discipline's natural targets: metaphysical claims about what consciousness is, phenomenal claims about what experience structurally requires, and functional claims about what consciousness causally contributes. Future cluster-integration deployments will inherit one of these grains or operate at a fourth not yet exhibited.

## Distinguishing from Convergence-Argument-As-Such

Convergence-argument-as-such — the [epistemological pattern](/topics/epistemology-of-convergence-arguments/) in which multiple independent arguments raise the probability of a shared conclusion — is one deployment of cluster-integration discipline at the metaphysical grain. Cluster-integration is the more general structural form: convergence-argument is the case where the cluster members are *arguments* and the inference is *to a metaphysical conclusion*. The binding problem's five-variety architecture is cluster-integration without being a convergence argument in the standard epistemological sense — its members are phenomenological-structural observations, not separate arguments, and the inference is to a structural claim about phenomenal unity rather than a metaphysical conclusion about dualism.

Conflating the two would lose the discipline's scope. A future article on, for instance, the five formal constraints on consciousness-physics coupling (no-signalling, Born-rule consistency, bandwidth, ordering, locality) would be cluster-integration at a *constraint-design* grain — not a convergence argument, because the cluster members are mathematical constraints rather than philosophical arguments, but governed by the same correspondence-claim discipline.

## What the Pattern Resists

The discipline resists a specific inflation vector the [coherence-inflation countermeasures](/project/coherence-inflation-countermeasures/) do not catch on their own. A multi-member argument is inflation-prone because each member can be weakened by an objection without the overall claim seeming to weaken — the cluster's apparent strength can absorb the loss silently. Cluster-integration discipline forces the absorption to be visible: if a member is lost, the correspondence claim shrinks and the article must say so. Without the discipline, articles drift toward arguments that name many considerations without specifying which the conclusion actually rests on; with the discipline, the structural-correspondence claim is the load-bearing feature, and changes to any cluster member force a visible recalibration. This is the specific contribution cluster-integration discipline makes to the inflation-resistance project that [common-cause-null discipline](/project/common-cause-null/) complements at the convergence-evaluation grain.

## Relation to Site Perspective

Cluster-integration discipline serves the [Occam's Razor Has Limits](/tenets/#occams-limits) tenet at the argument-architecture level. A worldview that takes consciousness seriously as irreducible cannot expect any single argument to be decisive; the catalogue's strongest cases are necessarily multi-part. The discipline ensures that multi-part cases earn their argumentative weight from structural correspondence rather than from sheer accumulation, preserving the falsifiability the [testability ledger](/project/testability-ledger/) tracks.

It also serves the [Dualism](/tenets/#dualism) tenet indirectly. The Map's central commitment is supported by exactly this pattern: not by any single argument for dualism but by the systematic correspondence between the catalogue's argument-clusters, phenomenological-structural clusters, and functional-evidence clusters all pointing the same way. The discipline is the editorial guarantee that this support is genuinely earned — that the catalogue's coherence reflects real structural correspondence rather than the absorption pressure [coherence inflation](/project/coherence-inflation-countermeasures/) applies to multi-part arguments.
