This ledger tracks what observations would update for or against The Unfinishable Map's framework, consolidating test hooks scattered across the site. It separates decisive disconfirmers from weak evidence from non-evidence — and from data the framework's enthusiasts sometimes count as evidence but should not.

The goal is epistemic hygiene in two directions: avoiding unfalsifiability accusations by being explicit about what could change our minds, *and* avoiding the opposite failure mode of treating tenet-coherence as evidence-elevation. The ledger operationalises the [evidential-status discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/)'s core move: keep *tenet-register support* (a tenet removes a defeater) separable from *evidence-register support* (positive empirical findings raise probability). Items the framework permits but the data do not yet support belong in the [falsification roadmap](/topics/falsification-roadmap-for-the-interface-model/)'s "what would have to occur" register, not here.

The ledger also distinguishes *tenet-level* disconfirmation from *mechanism-level* disconfirmation. The Map's interface model is at the [pre-Keplerian stage](/project/framework-stage-calibration/) — accumulating measurements without an inferred psychophysical law — so the same observation that devastates one specific proposal (Stapp's quantum-Zeno mechanism, Orch OR microtubule coherence) leaves the broader interactionist tenet intact unless *no* mechanism in the available class survives. The ledger tracks tenet-level disconfirmers; the "Relation to Site Perspective" section below revisits the layering.

## The Framework's Core Claims

The [tenets](/tenets/) make five commitments:

1. **Dualism**: Consciousness is not reducible to physical processes
2. **Minimal Quantum Interaction**: Consciousness operates at quantum indeterminacies
3. **Bidirectional Interaction**: Consciousness causally influences the physical world
4. **No Many Worlds**: Collapse is real; branching interpretations are rejected
5. **Occam's Razor Has Limits**: Simplicity is not a reliable guide when knowledge is incomplete

Each claim has potential disconfirmers. Two further sections track derived theoretical commitments — **Filter Theory** and **Retrocausality / Atemporal Selection** — that the Map adopts but are not themselves tenets. They are listed below for completeness because the catalogue makes substantive claims about them, but disconfirming a derived commitment does not by itself disconfirm the tenets the commitment helps elaborate.

---

## 1. Dualism

**The claim**: Consciousness is irreducible to physical description. No amount of physical/functional detail captures what it's like to be conscious.

### Decisive Disconfirmers

| Observation | Why It Would Disconfirm | Status |
|-------------|------------------------|--------|
| A complete, explanatorily satisfying physical theory of qualia | Would close the explanatory gap | Not achieved |
| Demonstrating that zombie thought experiments are incoherent (not just physically impossible) | Would show consciousness is conceptually inseparable from function | Actively debated |
| Deriving qualitative character from structural/functional description | Would reduce qualia to physics | Not achieved |

**What counts as "complete and satisfying"?** A physical theory that explains not just correlation but *why* physical process P produces experience E rather than E' or no experience. Correlation is not explanation—neural correlates of consciousness exist, but correlation doesn't close the gap.

### Weak Evidence Against

| Observation | Impact | Current Status |
|-------------|--------|----------------|
| Finding neural mechanisms that reliably predict specific qualia | Would strengthen correlation-implies-production intuitions | Some progress (color perception, pain) |
| Success of functionalism in other domains | Would make consciousness an anomaly | Functionalism succeeds for cognition, not phenomenology |
| Discovering physical properties that make experience seem less mysterious | Would shift burden of proof | Integrated information (IIT) claims this but is contested |

### Non-Evidence

- **Neural correlates existing**: Correlation is consistent with both production and filtering
- **Brain damage affecting consciousness**: Consistent with interface damage, not production
- **Anesthesia abolishing consciousness**: Consistent with interface disruption
- **Evolution of consciousness**: Evolution could select for interfaces, not just producers

## 2. Minimal Quantum Interaction

**The claim**: If consciousness influences physics, it does so at quantum indeterminacies—biasing otherwise undetermined outcomes without energy injection.

### Decisive Disconfirmers

| Observation | Why It Would Disconfirm | Status |
|-------------|------------------------|--------|
| Proving causal closure holds even at quantum collapse | Would eliminate the opening for consciousness | Not proven—the measurement problem remains open |
| Demonstrating consciousness doesn't influence quantum outcomes under controlled conditions | Would close the mechanism | Difficult to test—consciousness is the observer |
| Solving the measurement problem with no role for observers | Would eliminate logical space for consciousness | Not achieved |

### Weak Evidence Against

| Observation | Impact | Current Status |
|-------------|--------|----------------|
| Sustained failure of random number generator experiments to show mind-matter effects | Would weaken confidence in mechanism | Results are mixed/unreplicable |
| Neural decoherence times proven definitively too short for any quantum consciousness mechanism | Would eliminate known mechanisms | Debated—Tegmark vs. Hameroff dispute |
| Discovery of purely physical process that determines collapse | Would close the gap | Not found |

### Weak Evidence For

| Observation | Impact | Current Status |
|-------------|--------|----------------|
| Anomalies in random number generator data during mass events | Would suggest consciousness affects outcomes | Methodologically contested (Global Consciousness Project; widely critiqued for post-hoc event selection and multiple-comparisons issues) |
| Neural quantum effects surviving longer than expected | Would strengthen mechanism plausibility | Some evidence (avian magnetoreception, microtubule coherence) |
| Correlations between attention and neural quantum states | Would support Stapp's Zeno mechanism | Preliminary research |

### Non-Evidence

- **Quantum effects existing in biology**: Proves quantum biology possible, not that consciousness uses it
- **Decoherence calculations being disputed**: The debate's existence doesn't confirm the mechanism
- **Measurement problem being unsolved**: Creates logical space but doesn't fill it

## 3. Bidirectional Interaction

**The claim**: Consciousness causally influences physical outcomes—it's not epiphenomenal.

### Decisive Disconfirmers

| Observation | Why It Would Disconfirm | Status |
|-------------|------------------------|--------|
| Demonstrating that all reports about consciousness are produced by physical processes with no non-physical input | Would establish epiphenomenalism empirically | Not demonstrated |
| Complete physical prediction of all behavior without consciousness variables | Would make consciousness causally idle | Not achieved—behavior prediction incomplete |
| Self-stultification argument being refuted | Would remove the main argument for mental causation | No refutation accepted |

### Weak Evidence Against

| Observation | Impact | Current Status |
|-------------|--------|----------------|
| Finding purely physical explanations for attention effects | Would weaken Stapp mechanism | Mainstream neuroscience assumes physical closure |
| Libet experiments showing consciousness is consistently post-hoc | Would suggest consciousness doesn't select | Original interpretation disputed |
| No difference between conscious and unconscious processing outputs | Would suggest consciousness is causally idle | Differences exist (blindsight studies) |

### Weak Evidence For

| Observation | Impact | Current Status |
|-------------|--------|----------------|
| Attention training producing brain changes (Schwartz OCD work) | Suggests mental effort has physical effects | Demonstrated |
| Conscious vs. unconscious processing producing different outcomes | Suggests consciousness matters causally | Documented |
| Self-stultification argument remaining unrefuted | Maintains pressure on epiphenomenalism | Strong |

### Non-Evidence

- **Feeling like we have causal influence**: Could be illusory
- **Physical-mental correlation**: Consistent with both directions of causation
- **Reports about consciousness existing**: Physical mechanisms could produce these regardless

## 4. No Many Worlds

**The claim**: Many-worlds interpretation is rejected. Collapse is real; outcomes are genuinely selected, not merely branched.

### Decisive Disconfirmers

| Observation | Why It Would Disconfirm | Status |
|-------------|------------------------|--------|
| Direct empirical evidence for branching (detecting other branches) | Would confirm MWI | Not found—probably unfindable |
| Solving probability problem in MWI convincingly | Would remove major objection | Not achieved |
| Showing indexical identity questions are meaningless | Would dissolve the objection | Actively debated |

### Weak Evidence Against

| Observation | Impact | Current Status |
|-------------|--------|----------------|
| MWI being mathematically simpler than collapse interpretations | Would support Occam's argument for MWI | Mathematical simplicity claimed but disputed |
| Copenhagen problems proving insurmountable | Would favor MWI by elimination | Not established |
| Successful MWI-based predictions in novel domains | Would increase confidence | None distinctive |

### Weak Evidence For

| Observation | Impact | Current Status |
|-------------|--------|----------------|
| Indexical identity mattering in other domains | Would show it's not ad hoc | Self-locating beliefs literature exists |
| Probability interpretation remaining problematic in MWI | Would maintain objection | Active research problem |
| No empirical difference between interpretations | Would make choice principled, not empirical | Current situation |

### Non-Evidence

- **Quantum interference existing**: All interpretations accommodate this
- **Superposition being real**: Both MWI and collapse accept superposition
- **Decoherence explaining apparent collapse**: Doesn't distinguish interpretations

## 5. Filter Theory (derived commitment)

**The claim**: The brain filters/transmits consciousness rather than producing it. Correlations don't establish production. This is a derived theoretical commitment elaborating Tenet 1 (Dualism), not a tenet itself; falsifying the filter reading does not by itself falsify the dualism tenet, which is compatible with other consciousness-substrate relationships.

### Decisive Disconfirmers

| Observation | Why It Would Disconfirm | Status |
|-------------|------------------------|--------|
| Demonstrating correlation establishes production | Would undermine filter interpretation | Philosophical, not empirical |
| Creating consciousness in novel substrates without biological history | Would suggest production not filtering | Not achieved |
| Finding the "engram"—stable memory storage in neurons | Would weaken Bergson's specific claims | Engram-like ensembles are well evidenced, but no complete account of memory identity, persistence, and retrieval yet removes the filter-interpretive space |

### Weak Evidence Against

| Observation | Impact | Current Status |
|-------------|--------|----------------|
| More brain activity → more consciousness consistently | Would fit production better than filtering | Mixed: gross arousal correlates with reportable consciousness, but psychedelic and some NDE contexts show enhanced experience alongside disrupted (not uniformly increased) activity. The picture is underdetermined rather than monotonic |
| Brain damage destroying consciousness entirely (not just limiting it) | Would suggest production | Evidence is ambiguous (covert consciousness cases) |
| No enhanced experience during brain compromise | Would weaken filter paradox | Enhancement is documented |

### Weak Evidence For

| Observation | Impact | Current Status |
|-------------|--------|----------------|
| Reduced default-mode-network activity coinciding with enhanced experience (psychedelics, some NDE contexts) | Compatible with filtering; also compatible with REBUS, entropic-brain, and predictive-processing accounts that explain the same signatures without filtering. Compatibility does not entail support without an independent discriminator (see [evidential-status-discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/) on compatibility vs. support) | Documented but underdetermined |
| Covert consciousness in unresponsive patients | Shows consciousness persists beyond expression | In selected cohorts of behaviourally-unresponsive severe-brain-injury patients, task-based fMRI/EEG reveals command-following in roughly a quarter — depending on inclusion criteria, modality, and protocol; not "a quarter of vegetative patients are conscious" |
| Memory retrieval mechanisms found without stable storage | Fits Bergson's prediction | Strong evidence for engram-like neuronal ensembles (sparse storage, overlap, reactivation, representational drift), but no complete account of storage, identity, persistence, transformation, and retrieval that closes the filter-interpretive space |

### Non-Evidence

- **Brain-consciousness correlation existing**: Both models explain this
- **Anesthesia affecting consciousness**: Both models explain this
- **Brain damage producing deficits**: Both models explain this

## 6. Retrocausality / Atemporal Selection (derived commitment)

**The claim**: Consciousness may select outcomes atemporally, with apparent retrocausal influence (Libet resolution). Like Filter Theory, this is a derived commitment elaborating (not entailed by) Tenet 3. The catalogue labels it *speculative integration*: tenet-coherent and worth investigating, but the case rests on metaphysical compatibility plus suggestive interpretation rather than positive empirical support.

### Decisive Disconfirmers

| Observation | Why It Would Disconfirm | Status |
|-------------|------------------------|--------|
| Time-symmetric QM interpretations being ruled out | Would eliminate framework | Not ruled out |
| Showing Libet timing requires post-hoc confabulation | Would weaken retrocausal interpretation | Disputed |
| Demonstrating strict forward causation at neural level | Would conflict with atemporal selection | Not demonstrated at quantum level |

### Weak Evidence Against

| Observation | Impact | Current Status |
|-------------|--------|----------------|
| Failed delayed-choice experiments | Would weaken retrocausal QM | Not conclusively failed |
| Libet results being artifact of methodology | Would remove motivation | Some critiques exist |
| Neuroscience explaining timing without retrocausation | Would make it unnecessary | Competing explanations exist |

### Weak Evidence For

| Observation | Impact | Current Status |
|-------------|--------|----------------|
| Delayed-choice experiments behaving as if retrocausal | Would support framework | Standard QM result |
| Time-symmetric interpretations (TSVF) being viable | Compatibility with retrocausal framework (not direct support; TSVF is interpretively neutral on consciousness) | Actively researched |
| Libet-style experiments showing systematic patterns | Would support non-random selection | Some evidence |

## What Would Update the Map's Confidence

### High-impact updates possible:

1. **Neural decoherence debate resolution**: If Tegmark is definitively right (too fast for quantum effects), mechanisms need revision
2. **Random number generator experiments**: Convincingly positive results under pre-registered, replicable protocols (not post-hoc event analysis) would update toward mechanism; sustained, well-powered negative results would update against. The Global Consciousness Project as currently conducted does not meet the methodological bar in either direction
3. **IIT or GWT predictions confirmed**: Would shift balance toward functionalism
4. **New measurement problem proposals**: Could close or expand logical space

### Medium-impact updates:

1. **NDE research advancing**: More evidence for or against enhanced experience during brain compromise
2. **Psychedelic neuroscience**: Better understanding of how network reconfiguration and desynchronization — DMN connectivity down, sensory/thalamocortical connectivity up — relate to expanded experience (not a simple "less brain activity → more consciousness" result)
3. **Attention training effects**: More evidence of mental effort causing brain changes
4. **Libet replications**: Better understanding of timing relationships

### Low-impact but informative:

1. **AI performance advancing**: Doesn't change consciousness attributions but tests functionalist intuitions
2. **Animal consciousness research**: Tests where framework draws lines
3. **Philosophy of mind developments**: New arguments or responses

## Relation to Site Perspective

The ledger is the catalogue's accountability artefact for the five tenets. The structure enforces three things the Map's perspective requires.

**Honest falsifiability.** Tenet 5 (Occam's Razor Has Limits) is the most vulnerable to unfalsifiability accusations. The ledger answers by stipulating *empirical* disconfirmers for the other four tenets so that the anti-parsimony commitment is not load-bearing for the framework's resistance to disconfirmation. Tenet 5 is the epistemological framing, not an empirical shield.

**Separation of registers.** The four-category structure operationalises the [evidential-status discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/)'s distinction between *tenet-register support* (a tenet removes a defeater) and *evidence-register support* (positive empirical findings raise the probability of a claim). The Non-Evidence category is the discipline's structural protection against possibility/probability slippage: items the framework permits but the data do not establish belong nowhere on the support side of the ledger.

**Stage-calibrated claims.** Per [framework-stage-calibration](/project/framework-stage-calibration/), the decisive disconfirmers listed for MQI and Bidirectional Interaction target the *class of interactionist proposals*, not any specific mechanism. Stapp's quantum-Zeno proposal, Eccles' tunneling-bias proposal, and Orch OR are proto-models; their individual disconfirmation would constrain the field without by itself disconfirming the tenets they serve. See [mqi-empirical-fragility](/project/mqi-empirical-fragility/) for mechanism-specific tracking.

## Partial-Disconfirmation Calibration

A falsifiability section that lists only *decisive* disconfirmers — observations that would defeat the framework at the worldview level — is poorly calibrated even when honest. The decisive disconfirmers for the tenets are, by design, mostly out of reach ("a complete physical theory of qualia", "solving the measurement problem with no role for observers"); an article whose only stated falsifier is one that will not arrive this century has set a bar so high that the claim reads as unfalsifiable in practice, which is the unfalsifiability accusation the ledger exists to forestall. The 2026-05-27 cross-reviewer convergence on `topics/meaning-of-life` ([outer-review-synthesis-2026-05-27](/reviews/outer-review-synthesis-2026-05-27/)) named the general form: falsifiability discussions should state what *partial* or *empirical* result would update confidence, not only what would defeat the worldview.

The ledger already models the calibration in its three-tier column structure — Decisive Disconfirmers, Weak Evidence Against, Non-Evidence — and the discipline for individual articles is to inherit it. An article's "What Would Challenge This View?" section should include at least one near-term, empirically-reachable disconfirmer (a specific replication that could fail, a measurement that could come back the wrong way, a mechanism-level result that would constrain the article's preferred reading) alongside any worldview-level defeat it names. The partial disconfirmer carries the article's *practical* falsifiability; the worldview-level defeat carries its *in-principle* falsifiability. Both belong, and an article that states only the second is incompletely calibrated — the fix is to add the reachable disconfirmer the article's own evidence base implies, expressed in natural prose, not to weaken the worldview-level claim. This is general refinement guidance for falsifiability sections across the catalogue rather than a per-article defect: most articles already carry reachable disconfirmers, and the calibration check confirms they do.

## Using This Ledger

Update entries when major empirical developments occur, when new philosophical arguments appear, when the Map's positions shift, or when new test hooks are identified. The purpose is accountability — being explicit about what would change our minds distinguishes principled commitment from dogmatism.
