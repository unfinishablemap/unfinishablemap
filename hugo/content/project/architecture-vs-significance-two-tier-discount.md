The *architecture-vs-significance two-tier discount* is the Map's discipline for keeping two distinct framework-dependence discounts from collapsing into one. A finding usually has two claims riding on it: its *architectural reality* — that the structure or asymmetry the finding describes is really there — and its *significance for the framework's central commitments* — what the Map reads that structure as evidence *for*. These two claims do not inherit the same discount. The architectural finding typically survives translation across rival vocabularies (functionalist, identity-theoretic, illusionist, predictive-processing, dualist) and so inherits a *smaller* framework-dependence discount. The significance the Map attaches is framework-shaped — it is stated in the Map's interface-dualist vocabulary and presupposes the Map's reading of what the structure means — and so inherits a *larger* discount. The discipline is the requirement that a finding's architecture-tier claim and its significance-tier claim be cited at their own discount, never with the smaller one borrowed to cover the larger.

The discount is a *strength-indicator* discipline in the sense [the evidential-status discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/) fixes: it disciplines how findings and their significance-readings are *cited*, it does not graduate either claim to a higher tier. It sits alongside the other named discipline pages — [evidential-status-discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/) (the single-claim five-tier scale), [framework-stage-calibration](/project/framework-stage-calibration/) (the framework-maturity register), [common-cause-null](/project/common-cause-null/) (the convergence-evaluation companion), [voids-circularity-discount](/project/voids-circularity-discount/) (one specific application at the void grain), and [per-cluster-independence-scoring](/project/per-cluster-independence-scoring/) (the cluster-profile operational layer) — and supplies the two-tier *reading* those pages already gesture at when they note that a cluster can score "high on the architectural finding, lower on the interpretive significance." This page is the canonical home that reading defers to.

## The Failure Mode

The discount exists to block a specific inflation. A finding is established at the architecture tier — depersonalisation patients really do report content-preserved, ownership-degraded experience; a metacognitive signal really does invert in identifiable regimes; emergence from anaesthesia really does run a dedicated reopening pathway distinct from the closing one. The finding's architecture-tier robustness is then silently transferred to the Map's significance-reading: *therefore* there is a bounded conscious witness behind a reconstructive introspective architecture; *therefore* the substrate is configured to admit consciousness rather than to produce it. The transfer treats the small discount the architecture earned as if it also covered the large discount the significance carries.

The failure is seductive because both claims are true *in their own registers*. The architectural finding is robust; the Map's significance-reading is a coherent interpretation of it. What is illegitimate is letting the robustness of the first *under-discount* the second. The significance-reading is one interpretation among the rival vocabularies the architectural finding survives in — a functionalist reads the same anti-correlation as a fact about second-order monitoring systems, not as evidence of a witness with limited substrate-access — so a citation that quietly upgrades the significance to architectural robustness reads the framework's own commitment back into the data as though the data had supplied it. This is the [coherence-inflation](/project/coherence-inflation-countermeasures/) pattern at the finding level: the systematic-looking move (citing "the finding") masks a judgement-laden split (which tier of the finding).

## The Two Tiers Stated

For any finding the Map cites, the discipline requires separating:

**The architecture tier — what is really there.** The structural claim: an asymmetry, a dissociation, a separable feature, a dedicated mechanism. The test for architecture-tier status is *cross-vocabulary translatability*: does the claim survive restatement in functionalist, identity-theoretic, illusionist, predictive-processing, and dualist terms? A finding that survives all five inherits the smaller discount, because its reality is not hostage to any one framework's machinery. The architecture tier is where the finding does its load-bearing structural work in the catalogue.

**The significance tier — what the Map reads it as evidence for.** The interpretive claim, stated in the Map's vocabulary and presupposing the Map's reading: that the structure indicates an interface, a bounded witness, a non-physical aspect, a tenet-relevant consequence. The significance tier is framework-shaped by construction — it is the place where the Map's commitments enter — and so it inherits the larger discount. A reader inside a rival framework grants the architecture tier and declines the significance tier; that asymmetry of assent is exactly what the larger discount records.

The discount makes no claim that the significance-reading is *wrong*. It is a claim about *whose framework supplies the reading*: architecture-tier claims are supplied jointly by the data and any adequate framework; significance-tier claims are supplied by the Map's framework operating on the data. The two-tier discount keeps the citation honest about which is which.

## Worked Exhibits

The structure recurs across the catalogue. Four exhibits, drawn from three different domains, show the same split.

### The Anti-Correlated Metacognitive Signal

The clearest worked case, and the one whose [§"Architecture-vs-significance distinction"](/concepts/anti-correlated-metacognitive-signal/#limits) first named the split for this page. The **architecture-tier** finding is that a metacognitive confidence signal can *invert* — become anti-correlated with accuracy — in identifiable deceptive-cue regimes (the Rebouillat 2021 paradigm; extended by Dijkstra and Fleming 2024 to perceptual reality-monitoring). That a monitoring signal can fire and endorse while its endorsement runs opposite to accuracy survives translation across every framework on the list: a functionalist, an illusionist, and a dualist all grant that the confidence-accuracy inversion is real. Smaller discount.

The **significance-tier** claim is the Map's reading: that the inversion indicates *a bounded conscious witness with limited access to its own substrate, sitting behind a reconstructive introspective architecture*. This is framework-shaped — it presupposes the Map's witness-behind-the-architecture picture, which a functionalist replaces with a claim about the relationship between first-order processing and second-order monitoring systems carrying no witness at all. Larger discount. The concept's use as a *structural* contribution to the introspection-architecture cluster operates at the architecture tier; its use in support of the cluster's *metaphysical* reading operates at the significance tier, and the two must be cited at their own discounts.

### Mine-ness: Separability vs. Significance for Dualism

The **architecture-tier** finding is the *separability* of mine-ness — the for-me character of experience can vary independently of phenomenal content and of the sense of agency. The double dissociation is robust: depersonalisation disorder preserves content while degrading ownership (Billon; Ciaunica), thought insertion preserves ownership while disrupting agency (Gallagher). That the for-me tag is a *separable* phenomenal feature rather than identical to the bare having of experience survives translation into Metzinger's self-model framework, into predictive-processing accounts, and into the Map's vocabulary alike. Smaller discount — and the [Knappik caveat](/concepts/mine-ness/#empirical-caveat) (mine-ness may be plural rather than a single global feature) operates *within* the architecture tier without dislodging separability.

The **significance-tier** claim is what the Map reads separability as evidence *for*: that a feature so cleanly dissociable from content points to a subject-pole not reducible to the functional organisation of content-processing — a dualist-friendly reading. That significance is framework-shaped. A Metzingerian grants the separability and reads it as a fact about the phenomenal self-model's architecture, with no dualist consequence. The larger discount on the significance tier is what keeps mine-ness's robust separability finding from being cited as though it had itself established the dualist conclusion.

### Inventory Blindness: Existence vs. Significance for Tenet 5

The **architecture-tier** finding is the *existence* of inventory blindness — the structural fact that the absence of an entire kind of cognitive capacity produces no signal, because the signalling architecture is absent along with the capacity. The structure is supported across independent channels: Stanford's problem of unconceived alternatives (historical), the Dunning-Kruger pattern in bounded domains (empirical), the Capgras-prosopagnosia double dissociation (clinical). That a mind cannot audit capacities it never had is a structural claim a physicalist about cognition grants as readily as the Map does. Smaller discount.

The **significance-tier** claim is the Map's reading for [Tenet 5](/tenets/#occams-limits): that because our conceptual inventory is incompletable in ways we cannot detect, the apparent parsimony of materialism about consciousness *may reflect a blind spot rather than an insight*. The reading is framework-relevant and hedged ("may reflect"), and it is exactly where the Map's commitment enters — a physicalist grants the existence of inventory blindness while declining the inference that it specifically undercuts materialist parsimony. The larger discount on the significance tier blocks the move from "inventory blindness is real" to "therefore materialist parsimony is unreliable *here*," preserving the gap the hedge already marks.

### Active Reboot: The Asymmetry vs. Significance for Interface-Resumption

A non-introspection exhibit, from the consciousness-state-transition domain. The **architecture-tier** finding is the *closing-reopening asymmetry*: emergence from anaesthesia runs a dedicated molecular reopening pathway (ubiquitin-driven KCC2 degradation; Hu et al. 2023) that is *mechanism-shared* across pharmacologically distinct closing agents and *temporally precedes* behavioural return. The asymmetry — that reopening is not the inverse of closing — is documented across propofol, isoflurane, sevoflurane, and ketamine, and a generator-model physicalist grants the asymmetry as readily as the Map does. Smaller discount.

The **significance-tier** claim is the Map's interface-resumption reading: that a *dedicated* reopening mechanism is what one expects if the substrate must be *configured to admit* consciousness rather than to *produce* it — since on a pure production model the activity that produces consciousness would itself constitute the reopening, making separate reopening machinery redundant. The article itself tiers this explicitly, placing structured interface architecture at *realistic possibility* and the Map's specific interactionist reading at *live hypothesis*. The reading is one available interpretation; the larger discount records that the asymmetry *raises the explanatory cost on passive-recovery models without forcing the interface reading*. This exhibit is the cleanest demonstration that the split is not an introspection-domain artefact: the same architecture-vs-significance gap appears in a wholly empirical, molecular-mechanism finding.

### What the Four Exhibits Share

In every case the architecture-tier claim is cross-vocabulary-translatable and the significance-tier claim is framework-shaped; in every case the catalogue's temptation is to let the robustness of the first cover the second; and in every case the originating article already states the split in its own terms. The discipline's contribution is to name the recurring structure so that articles can defer to this page rather than re-deriving the two-tier reading inline each time.

## How to Cite the Discipline

Two citation styles are appropriate. *Tier-explicit citations* name which tier the finding is doing work at — *"at the architecture tier, the anti-correlation finding survives across rival vocabularies; the witness-behind-the-architecture significance is cited at the larger framework-dependence discount."* *Discipline-deferral citations* defer to the page by name — *"calibrated per the architecture-vs-significance two-tier discount, the separability finding is load-bearing structurally while its dualist significance carries the larger discount."*

A citation that ascribes the architecture tier's robustness to the significance tier, that cites "the finding" without specifying which tier is doing the inferential work, or that treats the two-tier split as licensing a *quantitative* discount factor, should be flagged in review.

## Relation to the Sister Disciplines

The discount sits in the family of named discipline pages, each at a distinct register.

- [evidential-status-discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/) is the *single-claim calibration* layer: it assigns a five-tier label to one claim. The two-tier discount operates *upstream* of tier assignment — it splits a finding into its two claims so that the evidential-status discipline can assign each its own tier, as it does when it places active reboot's structural architecture at *realistic possibility* and its interactionist significance at *live hypothesis*.
- [framework-stage-calibration](/project/framework-stage-calibration/) addresses the *framework-maturity* register — how confident a young framework's self-assessment may be. The two-tier discount is orthogonal: it operates on a *single finding* regardless of framework maturity, splitting architecture from significance rather than calibrating the framework's overall stage.
- [common-cause-null](/project/common-cause-null/) is the *convergence-evaluation* companion. Where the two-tier discount disciplines how *one* finding's two claims are cited, the common-cause null disciplines whether *N* findings are genuinely independent. The two interlock: a convergence argument should run on the *architecture-tier* claims of its members (the cross-vocabulary-robust layer), because convergence on framework-shaped *significance* claims risks counting the Map's own commitment N times — the common-cause null's failure mode meeting this page's.
- [voids-circularity-discount](/project/voids-circularity-discount/) is *one specific application at the void grain* — the larger discount the significance tier carries is, for tenet-generated voids, large enough to reach zero under the circularity prohibition. The two-tier discount is the general structure; the voids-circularity discount is its strict-prohibition limit case.
- [per-cluster-independence-scoring](/project/per-cluster-independence-scoring/) is the *operational layer* that runs clusters against four criteria and reports a profile; its profiles already carry the asymmetric mark *strong-on-architecture-lower-on-significance*. The two-tier discount is the canonical statement of what that mark *means*.

## What the Discipline Does Not Claim

The discipline is bounded against three overreaches.

**It does not deliver a quantitative discount factor.** "Smaller" and "larger" are qualitative, with reasoning attached. There is no number that converts an architecture-tier claim into a significance-tier claim, and assigning one would invite exactly the coherence inflation [coherence-inflation-countermeasures](/project/coherence-inflation-countermeasures/) guards against — a systematic-looking figure masking a judgement-laden split. The discipline disciplines the *direction* and *separation* of the two discounts, not their magnitude.

**It does not deliver tier-graduation.** Splitting a finding into two claims does not assign either a tier; that work belongs to [evidential-status-discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/). The two-tier discount is a strength-indicator discipline, in the precise sense that page fixes: it indicates *which tier of a finding* is doing inferential work, leaving the tier *label* to the single-claim discipline downstream.

**It does not hold that the significance-reading is false.** The larger discount on the significance tier records *whose framework supplies the reading*, not that the reading is mistaken. The Map's significance-readings are coherent interpretations; the discount keeps them from being cited as though the data alone had established them. A reader is free to grant both tiers — what the discipline forbids is granting the second *because* the first was robust.

## Honest Limitation

The discipline inherits the recursive bind its sister pages name. The test for architecture-tier status — *does the claim survive translation across rival vocabularies?* — is itself applied from within a framework. A reviewer deciding what counts as "the same finding restated in functionalist terms" is using their own framework's individuation of findings, and a sufficiently different framework might draw the architecture/significance line elsewhere. The bind is genuine; the response is asymptotic — adversarial cross-framework auditing through the [three-service outer-review programme](/project/outer-reviewer-service-calibration/) tightens the placement of the line without eliminating the framework-relativity of where it falls. The discipline is no more foundational than the cross-vocabulary translatability test it rests on.

A second limitation, the universal-solvent risk that recurs across the discount disciplines: *every* significance-reading can be assigned "the larger discount," and an over-zealous application would discount all the Map's interpretive claims to the point of saying nothing. The scope condition is that the discount applies *differentially* — its whole content is the *gap* between the two tiers, so it is only doing work where the architecture tier is genuinely more cross-vocabulary-robust than the significance tier. Where a finding's architectural and significance claims are equally framework-shaped, the two-tier discount has nothing to separate and should not be invoked.

## Relation to Site Perspective

The discipline applies across all five [tenets](/tenets/) equally; it is a *methodological* discipline whose subject is the catalogue's own citation hygiene rather than any tenet-specific commitment. Each of the four worked exhibits routes its significance tier to a different tenet — mine-ness to [Tenet 1](/tenets/#dualism), active reboot to [Tenet 3](/tenets/#bidirectional-interaction) and [Tenet 2](/tenets/#minimal-quantum-interaction), inventory blindness to [Tenet 5](/tenets/#occams-limits) — which is itself the point: the *architecture* tier is tenet-neutral and cross-vocabulary-robust, while the *significance* tier is where the tenet enters and the larger discount lands.

Its most direct connection is to **[Tenet 5 — Occam's Razor Has Limits](/tenets/#occams-limits)**, read self-bindingly. Just as Tenet 5 binds the Map's *own* use of parsimony and not only its critics', the two-tier discount binds the Map's *own* significance-readings. The temptation it resists is the catalogue's version of simplicity-overreach: treating a robust architectural finding as though its robustness extended, without further cost, to the framework's preferred interpretation of it. The discipline is the operational form of Tenet 5's symmetric self-binding at the single-finding layer — the architecture is granted at the smaller discount, the significance is held at the larger one, and the gap between them is kept visible rather than collapsed.
