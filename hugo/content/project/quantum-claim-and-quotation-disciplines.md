The 2026-06-19 outer-review triple audited a single article — [the brain-specialness boundary](/topics/brain-specialness-boundary/) — and two reviewers (ChatGPT 5.5 Pro and Claude Opus 4.8) independently converged on methodology proposals beyond the article-specific findings. This document records the two of those proposals that are *genuinely new* relative to the existing discipline family, and explicitly notes where the remaining proposals are already covered so they are not duplicated. The synthesis is at [outer-review-synthesis-2026-06-19](/reviews/outer-review-synthesis-2026-06-19/).

Both new disciplines were motivated by concrete failures in that cycle. The mechanism-variance discipline answers the failure that the boundary argument leaned its whole weight on one quantum mechanism (Stapp's quantum-Zeno apparatus) whose published no-go argument (Georgiev 2015) it never engaged. The quotation-fidelity discipline answers a Stapp *misquote* ("intended" substituted for "projected" consequences) that passed every reference-metadata check because the *book* is real and correctly cited — the defect was in the quoted string, not the metadata.

## Discipline One: The Mechanism-Variance Audit

**Scope.** Any article whose load-bearing claim depends on a *specific* quantum-consciousness mechanism — the quantum Zeno / Stapp Process-1 apparatus, Orch OR microtubule coherence, post-decoherence selection, delegatory-authority selection, or any successor in the same class.

**The failure mode.** An article identifies the conclusion it wants, reaches for *one* mechanism that delivers it, and structures the whole argument on that mechanism without checking (a) whether the conclusion survives if that mechanism fails, and (b) whether the mechanism carries a published no-go or fatal critique the article owes an engagement. The brain-specialness boundary is the worked case: all four of its "converging considerations" routed through the quantum-Zeno mechanism, so the Georgiev decoherence-time no-go and the Donald discrete-basis critique threatened the entire argument at once — yet neither was named. A single mechanism failure could have collapsed four "independent" considerations because they were never mechanism-independent.

**The audit.** For each such article, run a *mechanism-variance pass*:

1. **Name the load-bearing mechanism explicitly** — at the level the [mechanism-cost ledger](/project/mechanism-cost-ledger/) requires (the mechanism, not the tenet). "Consciousness biases quantum outcomes" is a tenet; "Stapp quantum-Zeno attentional holding" is a mechanism.
2. **Run the claim across the candidate-mechanism set.** Ask, for each member of the available class, whether the article's conclusion still follows: does it survive under *post-decoherence selection*? under *Stapp / Process-1 context-selection*? under *delegatory-authority selection*? Three outcomes:
   - **Mechanism-robust** — the conclusion follows under every member of the class. State this; the argument inherits the [testability ledger](/project/testability-ledger/)'s tenet-level resilience (no single mechanism's disconfirmation closes the gap).
   - **Mechanism-specific** — the conclusion follows under exactly one mechanism. The article must say so and either (i) argue why that mechanism is the right one to privilege, or (ii) re-scope the conclusion to "if mechanism M holds, then C," matching the *constrain-not-establish* register of [evidential-status-discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/).
   - **Mechanism-fragile** — the conclusion follows under no surviving mechanism once the relevant no-go is admitted. This is a hard finding, routed to a P1 `refine-draft`.
3. **Engage the mechanism's strongest no-go.** If the privileged mechanism carries a published fatal critique (Tegmark's decoherence-time calculation and Georgiev 2015 for the Zeno apparatus; the Hagan–Hameroff–Tuszyński reply as the rebuttal), the article must name it and state how the argument fares. Omitting a known no-go for a load-bearing mechanism is the omission-of-disconfirming-evidence defect, and converges with Discipline Two's disconfirming-source rule.

**What it is not.** This is not a demand that every article rebuild the mechanism debate; [writing-style](/project/writing-style/) already forbids letting articles become primers on the mechanism. The audit's output is one of three labels plus, where the mechanism is privileged, a single engagement with its no-go. The honest default — already stated in [testability-ledger](/project/testability-ledger/) (a specific proposal's disconfirmation "leaves the broader interactionist tenet intact unless *no* mechanism in the available class survives") and in [writing-style](/project/writing-style/) ("ensure the Map's core argument would survive if the mechanism were disproven") — is *mechanism-robustness*. The discipline operationalises that default into a per-article check: it converts a standing principle into a label the article must earn or qualify, rather than a slogan it may assume. The [MQI empirical-fragility](/project/mqi-empirical-fragility/) page already does this tracking at the tenet scale; the mechanism-variance audit applies the same move at the article scale.

**Cadence.** A `/deep-review` and `/apex-evolve` sub-check on any article in scope, applied when the article is next reviewed; not a standalone scheduled scan. Promotion to a scheduled member of the [calibration-audit-triple](/project/calibration-audit-triple/) class is a `/tune-system` decision.

## Discipline Two: Quotation-String Fidelity and Disconfirming-Source Inclusion

These two are recorded together because both extend the citation-integrity pipeline and were proposed by both reviewers as one cluster.

### Quote-string fidelity, distinct from metadata verification

[calibration-audit-triple](/project/calibration-audit-triple/) Audit Four check (c) already prohibits a *paraphrase dressed as a verbatim quotation* — a sentence wearing quotation marks it has not earned (the Schooler de-quote). The 2026-06-19 cycle surfaced the adjacent sub-case that check (c) does not name: a sentence that *is* a genuine quotation of a *real, correctly-cited* source, but whose **string has drifted from the source text** — here, "intended consequences" where Stapp wrote "projected consequences." Reference-metadata verification (Audit Four check (b)) cannot catch it, because author, title, venue, year, and DOI are all correct. The book is real; the quote is not faithful.

**The check.** For every span inside quotation marks attributed to a source, verify the string *word-for-word* against the source text, not merely that the source exists and says something in the neighbourhood. This is a third, distinct citation-integrity check:

- **(b) metadata-correct** — does the source exist with the stated author/title/venue/year/DOI?
- **(c-i) quote-mark-legitimate** — is the marked span actually a quotation, not a paraphrase wearing quote marks?
- **(c-ii) quote-string-faithful** — *new* — is the marked span the source's exact words, with no substituted, dropped, or added words?

Check (c-ii) shares Audit Four check (b)'s bounded external-lookup budget; its mechanical surface is the same quoted-span detection as (c-i). A failure routes to the same P1 `refine-draft` as the other citation-integrity checks, with the corrective being an exact located quote or a downgrade to unmarked paraphrase. This is a hard defect of the same class as wrong metadata: it misrepresents what the source said in the source's own voice. It is the verbatim-string companion to the *concept-provenance* failure [writing-style](/project/writing-style/) catalogues (the Saad observational-closure graft, where the term is the author's but the concept attributed is the Map's own) — both are cases where metadata is clean but the article's representation of the source is not.

### Disconfirming-source inclusion

The brain-specialness boundary cited four friendly sources (Jahn & Dunne 2005, Nelson et al. 2002, Park 2000, Stapp 2007) and a popular-science book for the criticism, while omitting the single most-cited skeptical meta-analysis (Bösch, Steinkamp & Boller 2006) and the large Bayesian null study (Maier, Dechamps & Pflitsch 2018). Both reviewers independently flagged this as "a one-sided evidence base presented as 'the empirical record.'"

**The rule.** Any article presenting an empirical record on a contested phenomenon must cite the *strongest disconfirming source*, not only the supportive ones. Citing friendly studies plus a popular-science book for the criticism does not discharge the obligation; the peer-reviewed null result must appear by name. This is the citation-completeness analogue of [coherence-inflation-countermeasures](/project/coherence-inflation-countermeasures/) Countermeasure 2 (mandatory steelman) and Countermeasure 8 (absorb-vs-clash) at the *evidence-base* grain rather than the argument grain: the steelman countermeasures require the strongest rival *argument*; this rule requires the strongest disconfirming *source*. It is sharper than the generic "citation breadth" framing because it names a specific obligation (the strongest null result by name) rather than a vague completeness aspiration, and it routes to the same Audit-Four P1 remediation.

The disconfirming-source rule and Discipline One's no-go-engagement step are the same obligation at two grains: Discipline One requires engaging the strongest *theoretical* defeater (the mechanism's no-go); the disconfirming-source rule requires citing the strongest *empirical* defeater (the null meta-analysis). An article in scope of both owes both.

## Already Covered — Recorded to Prevent Duplication

The triple proposed five disciplines; three are already practiced and are noted here rather than restated:

- **Coherence-inflation check for "N converging considerations."** Already covered by [coherence-inflation-countermeasures](/project/coherence-inflation-countermeasures/) — Countermeasure 15 (the Convergence-Independence Gate), backed by the [common-cause-null](/project/common-cause-null/) screen and [per-cluster-independence-scoring](/project/per-cluster-independence-scoring/). The brain-specialness "four considerations are the embodiment premise restated" case is *exactly* Countermeasure 15's named failure ("N components of one architecture, not N independent lines"). The [architecture-vs-significance-two-tier-discount](/project/architecture-vs-significance-two-tier-discount/) page handles the related architecture-vs-significance double-counting move. No new discipline; the existing gate covers it. (The brain-specialness article inherits Countermeasure 15 as its remediation.)
- **Bracketing ledger (proves / assumes / argues-elsewhere / speculative).** Already covered by [evidential-status-discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/) — the five-tier scale (*established → strongly supported → realistic possibility → live hypothesis → speculative integration*) plus the source-role table and tenet-transfer ledger are precisely a bracketing ledger separating what an article proves from what it assumes from what it speculatively integrates. No new discipline.
- **Same-data rival interpretations for empirical sections.** Already covered by [evidential-status-discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/)'s *compatibility-vs-support at the rival-model interface* rule and its tenet-use guardrail (the "Relation to Site Perspective" rival-can-also-explain checkpoint recorded in [calibration-audit-triple](/project/calibration-audit-triple/)), reinforced by [direct-refutation-discipline](/project/direct-refutation-discipline/)'s framework-boundary-marking mode. No new discipline.

One proposal is a **single-reviewer (ChatGPT) singleton**, recorded but not promoted:

- **Boundary adversarial-cases checklist** (RNG-with-feedback, BCI/neuroprosthetic, tool-incorporation, another person's brain, AI, animal brains). This is the boundary-article companion to Discipline One's mechanism-variance set: where mechanism-variance varies the *mechanism* and holds the case fixed, the adversarial-cases checklist varies the *case* and holds the mechanism fixed, asking whether the boundary the article draws is principled or reverse-engineered to land exactly on "own brain only." It is genuinely useful for boundary-drawing articles specifically, but as a single-reviewer proposal scoped to one article-type it is recorded as a `/tune-system` promotion candidate rather than adopted as a corpus-wide discipline. Its natural future home, if promoted, is a sub-check of Discipline One for boundary-type articles.

## Relation to Site Perspective

Both disciplines protect the same commitment: that the Map's claims are calibrated to what the catalogue actually has, not to what the tenets allow. Discipline One prevents a tenet-congenial conclusion from inheriting unearned confidence by riding a single fragile mechanism; Discipline Two prevents a contested empirical record from looking settled by omitting its strongest disconfirmer or by quoting a source inexactly. Neither weakens the Map's positions — they keep the positions honestly stated, which is what makes the framework's anti-parsimony stance ([Tenet 5](/tenets/#occams-limits)) a discipline rather than a shield.
