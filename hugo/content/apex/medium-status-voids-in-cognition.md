Some cognitive voids concern neither absent contents nor exceeded capacities. They concern operations that *structure* other contents without themselves appearing as contents of the field. The Unfinishable Map calls these the medium-status voids: regions where the cognitive process being investigated is the very condition under which any investigation occurs. Four such voids cluster densely. The [mattering void](/voids/mattering-void/) names the medium of significance — why anything shows up as bearing on us at all. The [relevance void](/voids/relevance-void/) names the medium of filtering — which of the unbounded possible foci are currently in play. The [noetic-feelings void](/voids/noetic-feelings-void/) names the medium of epistemic gating — the felt signals that decide when to trust, when to search, when to stop. The [conceptual-scheme void](/voids/conceptual-scheme-void/) names the medium of framing — the categorial apparatus through which any thought becomes the kind of thought it is. Their shared structural feature, and the reason they resist neighbouring forms of analysis, is that each is the eye that cannot see itself seeing.

This synthesis articulates what the four articles converge on but cannot fully voice from inside themselves: the *medium-status of cognitive operations* as a category, the recurring self-referential circularity, the convergence of five traditions on that circularity, and the criterion that prevents the framing from inflating into "everything is a medium."

## Content-Status, Capacity-Status, Medium-Status

The Map distinguishes three ways a cognitive feature can become the subject of a void.

**Content-status** treats the feature as something experience contains. Qualia, phenomenal characters, the redness of red, what-it-is-likeness — these are *items in the field*. Voids around them concern access (Mary's room), comparability across minds (the inverted-spectrum thought experiment), or articulation (why words fail to convey what red is like). The classic hard problem of consciousness sits at this layer: an explanatory gap between physical description and felt content.

**Capacity-status** treats the feature as a limit on what minds can do. Computational complexity, [biological cognitive closure](/voids/biological-cognitive-closure/), the [incompleteness phenomena](/voids/formal-cognitive-limits/) — voids of this kind concern the boundary of the doable. The McGinn argument that human minds may be species-relative blind to the consciousness-physics interface is a capacity-status claim: certain solutions are not achievable in principle for cognitive architectures like ours. The contents are not present, but the *reason* is not that something is occluding them; the reason is that the apparatus runs out of room. [The quantitative-comprehension void's](/voids/the-quantitative-comprehension-void/) magnitude-probability face — probability cognition collapsing at extreme scales, "one million" landing midway between "one thousand" and "one billion" on a number line — is a similar case: the cap is a quantitative limit on grasp, not an operation that constitutes the field through which any grasp occurs.

**Medium-status** is a third category the Map proposes — not a discovery awaiting clearer formulation but a conceptual cut that organises cases the content/capacity pair handles awkwardly. A feature has medium-status when it *constitutes the field through which other contents are presented* without itself appearing as a content of the field. Air, for the fish; the lens, for the eye; the framework, for the framework's own user. Medium-status features are not outside the field, but they are not in it either. They are the field's own operating-condition. The voids that surround them are neither gaps in content nor exceedings of capacity but the structural shadow cast by the impossibility of a field examining its own medium without using it.

A reductionist will read medium-status as functional architecture with privileged-access asymmetries — background processes whose outputs feed the conscious workspace but whose own operations are not workspace-content — and therefore as ontologically continuous with content-status. That reading is coherent at the cognitive-science level, and the Map does not contest it there. Medium-status as offered here names a *kind of cognitive role*, not a substrate; the category is neutral between functionalist and dualist readings of how the role is realised. The dualist commitments invoked later are an additional, independent move; they do not derive from the typology.

Distinguishing the three is load-bearing. The hard problem is a *content* puzzle: how any felt quality arises from physical process. Cognitive closure is a *capacity* puzzle: which problems are in principle solvable given our architecture. Medium-status voids are *operational* puzzles: how the operation that makes contents available becomes available to itself. The three kinds compose but are not reducible to each other, and their failure signatures differ.

This typology is orthogonal to the [unexplored / unexplorable / occluded taxonomy](/voids/three-kinds-of-void/) developed elsewhere on the Map. That taxonomy classifies the *epistemic status* of a void — what kind of inaccessibility it has. The present typology classifies the *kind of feature* a void surrounds. A medium-status void will typically present as occluded with structurally unexplorable dimensions, but the framings track different cuts and can be applied jointly.

## The Four Voids and Their Medium-Features

Each void in the cluster identifies a different cognitive operation occupying medium-status, and each picks out a distinct face of the same structural circularity.

**The mattering void.** The medium here is *significance*. Things show up to consciousness as urgent, boring, threatening, inviting — and this charge is so pervasive that it normally vanishes from notice the way air vanishes for a fish. Anhedonia drains the system without revealing its mechanism; anxiety briefly destabilises it without making it inspectable while operational. The medium-feature is *the standing field of significance* against which any item can become an object of attention. As [the mattering-void article](/voids/mattering-void/) argues, even nothing-mattering is a mode of mattering — the system can be diminished or partially collapsed but does not produce the clean view-from-nowhere that would make the mechanism inspectable.

**The relevance void.** The medium here is *filtering*. Out of an unbounded background, consciousness presents only a handful of items as in-play, already pre-selected. Relevance is downstream of mattering: given that things can matter, which are mattering *here, now, for this*. Mattering is the standing field; relevance is its momentary configuration. The filter cannot be observed without already being used, because observing is itself a relevance-determining act. Every reflective surface is itself produced by the relevance mechanism — a structural rather than contingent inaccessibility, as [the relevance-void article](/voids/relevance-void/) develops.

**The noetic-feelings void.** The medium here is *epistemic gating*. The felt signals that decide when to trust, when to search, when to stop — feelings of knowing, rightness, familiarity, obviousness, coherence — are produced by heuristic machinery not exposed to the consciousness it informs. Consciousness receives a finished verdict, not a derivation. Authority is granted without the possibility of audit, because audit requires more signals of the same kind. The medium-feature is *the verdict-issuing layer that terminates inquiry from inside*, and it is the most recursive of the four: the obviousness of an obviousness is itself an obviousness. The [noetic-feelings article](/voids/noetic-feelings-void/) tracks how the void conceals itself by feeling like settled territory.

**The conceptual-scheme void.** The medium here is *categorial framing*. Every mind operates with a finite system of categories — *property*, *substance*, *cause*, *event*, *relation* — that constitutes thought before any specific thought occurs. Kant's first *Critique* gave the canonical formulation: the categories are preconditions for experience rather than generalisations from it. The scheme is not a content among contents; it is the general shape any concept must take to track a kind of feature at all. It is invisible because it constitutes the seeing rather than what is seen. The framework feels like transparent access to truth; the walls of the prison are made of the same material as the air inside, as [the conceptual-scheme-void article](/voids/conceptual-scheme-void/) puts it.

The four scopes differ. Mattering is broadest: any conscious creature for whom anything registers as significant has the medium. Relevance is a sharper cut just below it. Noetic feelings sit at the boundary of metacognition. Conceptual scheme is the most architectural — a precondition for anything having determinate categorial shape. Yet the four share a structural feature their individual articles cannot fully articulate: each is invisible-while-active, each self-references in attempting inspection, and each remains tractable to *outputs* analysis (correlates, breakdowns, behavioural traces) while resisting transparent inspection of its own *operation*.

## The Eye That Cannot See Itself Seeing

The recurring circularity has the same logical shape across all four voids. Call it the *operative-self-reference* shape:

> To inspect operation O from inside a system S that operates by O, the inspection must itself be an instance of O. The inspecting act is therefore not external to its target; the target underwrites the inspection. Whatever the inspection produces about O is filtered, framed, gated, and significance-charged by O.

For mattering: any investigation is itself motivated; nothing-mattering would not generate one. For relevance: any investigation must select what is relevant to the investigation, generating a regress where each meta-level introduces its own filter. For noetic feelings: the question whether the feeling of rightness is reliable must be settled by some feeling — typically of rightness about the rightness-feeling. For conceptual scheme: the audit of the categorial apparatus employs the apparatus being audited.

The circularity is the structural face of medium-status, not a defect better methods could overcome. A field's medium becomes inspectable only by being temporarily replaced or suspended — anaesthesia, anxiety, anhedonia, contemplative bracketing, psychiatric disruption. These edge cases give *outputs-by-deformation*, not *inspection-while-operating*. Each glimpse is real evidence; none dissolves the circularity. The medium remains; the field falls apart; when the field reassembles the medium reasserts itself.

## Five Traditions on the Same Circularity

Independent intellectual traditions, working with different premises and vocabularies, have arrived at strikingly similar pictures of medium-status operations. The convergence is the strongest evidence the cluster has — none of the traditions makes the dualist commitments the Map adds, but each names the same circularity from a different angle.

**Heidegger: readiness-to-hand.** *Being and Time* describes our primary engagement with the world as pre-reflective, practical, and saturated with significance. Tools show up as already in-play; *Befindlichkeit* (attunement) discloses how entities matter before any reflective thought occurs. When practical engagement breaks down, the medium briefly becomes visible as a medium — but only by ceasing to do its medium-work. The present-at-hand mode that emerges from breakdown is the canonical phenomenology of medium-disclosure-by-failure. Hubert Dreyfus generalised the move against AI's frame problem: skilled coping operates on a background that does not need to be made explicit to do its work, and the demand to make it explicit transforms the mode rather than illuminating it. Heidegger himself would resist the cognitive-architecture register: for him the medium is *Dasein*'s mode of being, not a brain-implementable function, and the recasting in terms of cognitive operations is the Map's, not his.

**Frankfurt: caring as identity-constitutive.** *The Importance of What We Care About* argues that caring is not an emotion among emotions but constitutive of who one is — a hierarchical structure of second-order volitions internally related to importance. A being for whom nothing mattered would not be a *self* at all. The Map adds the medium-status reading: caring is not an overlay on a pre-existing self that could be removed without remainder, which means it cannot be cleanly separated from the self for inspection. The framing belongs to Frankfurt; the medium claim is the Map's. Frankfurt's register is normative-evaluative rather than phenomenological, and he would not endorse the introspective-opacity reading that the medium-status framing borrows from elsewhere.

**Ratcliffe: existential feelings.** Matthew Ratcliffe describes bodily feelings that "constitute ways of relating to the world as a whole" — not emotions about specific objects but background orientations structuring all experience. In severe depression, what changes is not that specific items have become sad but that the field of *possibilities-of-mattering* has narrowed or warped. The opacity of existential feelings to ordinary introspection is the phenomenological face of the medium: they are not reportable as moods but the texture of which moods are even possible. Ratcliffe's framework is firmly embodied and naturalistic; he would not endorse a non-physical reading of the medium-character of existential feelings.

**Carruthers: interpretive sensory-access.** *The Opacity of Mind* argues that introspection of one's own propositional attitudes is interpretive rather than direct: we infer our attitudes from sensory-format evidence in much the way we infer others'. The outputs of the relevant operations are available to consciousness; the determining processes are not. Even the sense that one *understands why* an item is relevant is itself an interpretive product subject to the same opacity. Carruthers sharpens the noetic-feelings face especially: the feeling of authoritative self-knowledge is a fluency-driven heuristic, not a window onto its own production. Carruthers is opposed in spirit to Heidegger here: he treats the opacity as the contingent design of a sensory-format introspection mechanism, not as constitutive of selfhood, and would reject any move from the opacity to a non-naturalistic ontology.

**Vervaeke: relevance realization.** John Vervaeke and colleagues argue that the process by which organisms determine what is relevant is "impredicative and co-constructive": the criteria of relevance are reshaped by the same process that applies them. The strong non-computability claim is contested, and the Map flags it as hypothesis rather than established result. Bracketed, the framing remains useful — it locates the same phenomenon the AI literature has been calling the frame problem for fifty years. Vervaeke's project is naturalist: the impredicativity is meant to characterise a real cognitive process within physical organisms, not to license a non-physical interpretation of the medium.

These five framings, working from existential phenomenology, identity-constitutive ethics, psychiatric phenomenology, philosophy of self-knowledge, and cognitive science, converge on a single structural picture: the operations that structure cognition are pre-reflective, constitutive, interpretively self-presenting, and resistant to transparent decomposition. The traditions cover the four voids unevenly. Heidegger touches all four — care for mattering, readiness-to-hand for relevance and conceptual scheme, breakdown phenomenology for noetic feelings. Frankfurt and Ratcliffe focus on the mattering face; Carruthers on relevance and noetic feelings; Vervaeke on relevance with mattering as upstream condition. Other traditions extend the cluster's empirical anchoring without appearing among the central five — Kant, Davidson, and Kuhn on conceptual scheme; Koriat, Thompson, Rozenblit and Keil on noetic feelings; Friston and the salience-network neuroscience on relevance. The convergence claim is structural: independent vocabularies arrive at the same circularity. None of the cited traditions endorses the Map's dualist tenets; the convergence is on the circularity, not on what the circularity entails. The Map adds the interpretation. Structural convergence is also weaker evidence than interpretive convergence: it shows that frameworks built on quite different premises can be re-described as tracking the same shape, not that their authors would jointly endorse any one reading.

## The Inflation Risk and the Medium-Status Criterion

The medium-status framing has an obvious failure mode: if every cognitive feature can be reclassified as a medium, the category collapses into "the cognitive in general" and ceases to do explanatory work. Some discipline is required.

The criterion is structural rather than vague. *A cognitive feature has medium-status only if it structures other contents without itself being a content of the field while operating.* Three sub-conditions follow.

**First, the feature must be operative-while-invisible.** Memory contents are not media: they appear in the field as remembered. Beliefs are not media: they appear as beliefs. The mattering charge does not appear as a charge; it appears as the bearing-on-the-situation of whatever bears on the situation. This is the diagnostic difference between content and medium.

**Second, attempts to make the feature an object must transform what it is.** Heidegger's present-at-hand mode is the model: when the hammer becomes an object of contemplation rather than a tool, it is no longer a tool. When relevance becomes an object of investigation, it ceases to be operative-relevance and becomes a content-about-relevance. Medium-status is mode-fragile.

**Third, breakdowns must reveal the medium *by its absence* rather than by inspection.** Anhedonia does not show how mattering works; it shows that something which was working has stopped. Anxiety shows the texture of significance failing rather than the mechanism of significance succeeding. Schizophrenic salience-failures reveal the relevance filter by going wrong, not by being accurately introspected. This *negative phenomenology* — knowledge of the medium through patterns of its non-operation — is characteristic of medium-status voids and absent for content-status voids.

Working-memory capacity fails the third condition: its breakdowns are quantitative rather than mode-shifting. Logical inference partially passes — there is reason to think the [inference void](/voids/inference-void/) is medium-adjacent — but the criterion's discipline is in being willing to deny medium-status when the case is not made. The criterion also marks the difference between medium-status and capacity-status: a capacity limit is in principle observable from outside; a medium limit is observable from outside only by cessation or deformation, never by direct inspection from inside, even given unlimited resources.

Two harder cases test the criterion. *Attention*, on most contemporary models (Watzl, Wu, Mole), is operative-while-invisible — we see *via* attention more than we see attention — mode-fragile when asked what we are attending to, and known largely through breakdown (neglect, attentional blink, ADHD). Attention therefore passes all three sub-conditions and has a strong medium-status face. The Map treats it as medium-adjacent rather than canonical here because attention is largely downstream of relevance and inherits relevance's circularity rather than adding a distinct one — but a sharper account would either fold it into the relevance face or admit it as a fifth member of the cluster. The "four" is provisional. *Episodic memory* is the second hard case. The bare claim that "memory contents appear in the field as remembered" handles only memory's *output*. The *reconstructive operation* — every recall re-encodes; the reconstruction is invisible from inside; only the re-encoded content is given — has a medium-status profile of its own. The honest reading is that episodic memory has a content-status face (the remembered) and a candidate medium-status face (the reconstructing), and the cluster's privileging of four canonical voids is partly that they exhibit the circularity most starkly, not that they are exclusively medium-bearing. Either the cluster admits more members than four, or the criterion needs a fourth sub-condition that carves attention out by name; the article does not pretend the current cut is final.

## What the Cluster Reveals

The convergence of four medium-status voids on a single structural feature is itself evidence-bearing. Each face — significance, filtering, gating, framing — could be dismissed individually as an artefact of one tradition's vocabulary. The conjunction is harder to dismiss. Five traditions with different premises arrive at the same circularity, and four distinct cognitive operations exhibit the same self-referential opacity. The Map reads this convergence as evidence that the medium-status of cognitive operations is a feature of the territory rather than a coincidence among descriptions.

The cluster does not establish that the medium is non-physical. The four articles individually flag the available alternatives — physicalist functional explanations, deflationary readings of Vervaeke's non-computability claim, eliminative responses to the noetic-feelings claim — and this synthesis inherits those hedges. What the cluster establishes is that medium-status is a coherent category, that it admits a sharp criterion, that four operations satisfy it, and that any reductive account must explain how a medium of significance, filtering, gating, or framing is reducible to features that are themselves contents within the field the medium constitutes. That is a structural challenge rather than a knockdown argument.

## What Would Falsify This Cluster

A category whose evidence comes mostly from breakdowns risks being structurally indistinguishable from a category whose alleged contents do not exist — the Popperian worry already faced by phlogiston. The medium-status framing earns its keep only if it specifies what would tell against it. Three concrete candidates:

**Controlled bypass of the relevance filter.** If a paradigm — attentional-capture, psychedelic, or computational — could demonstrate that the relevance-determining operation can be suspended while the rest of cognition continues unimpaired, the relevance face would lose its medium status. The void was never the claim that filtering is *necessary in principle*; if the filter turns out to be contingent infrastructure that can be removed without ceasing to think, it is more capacity-like than medium-like.

**Lesion-dissociation of the four operations.** If targeted lesions, pharmacological dissociations, or computational ablations could cleanly separate significance, filtering, gating, and framing — each running on independent substrates with no shared circularity — the convergence claim would weaken to four independent functions rather than a unified medium structure. The cluster predicts shared signature; clean dissociation falsifies the prediction.

**Verifiable scheme-articulation while the scheme is operating.** If a meditator, psychedelic subject, or contemplative practitioner could articulate the conceptual scheme while using it — articulating the framework that makes the articulation possible, with the description checkable from outside — the conceptual-scheme face would lose its mode-fragile profile. The criterion stipulates that such inspection transforms what it inspects; a worked-out counter-example would put the stipulation under pressure.

None of the three is currently observed; that is what makes the framework usable. None is in principle excluded; that is what makes it falsifiable rather than merely descriptive. The candidates are not symmetrically weighted — a single clean lesion-dissociation result would do more damage than a contested introspective report — but each is operational enough to count.

## Relation to Site Perspective

The cluster bears on the Map's [tenets](/tenets/) in three primary ways.

**[Dualism](/tenets/#dualism).** The medium-status reading supplies a structurally different argument for irreducibility than the standard hard-problem framing. The hard problem is a content-level argument: a felt quality is not entailed by a physical description. The medium-status argument is operational-level: the operation that makes any felt quality showable-as-a-felt-quality cannot be a content of the field it constitutes, and reductive accounts must therefore explain how a structuring operation reduces to features that are only available *after* being structured. This does not establish dualism. It supplies a new line of resistance to physicalist absorption that is parallel to, and compounding with, the content-level lines.

**[Bidirectional Interaction](/tenets/#bidirectional-interaction).** Three of the four medium-status voids name *candidate* channels for consciousness-physics interaction — candidate in the conditional sense made explicit below. The mattering void identifies *the felt weight of mattering* as a phenomenal contribution that, on the Map's reading, the brain's value-computation substrate may not exhaustively deliver. The relevance void identifies *the precision-weighting of salience* as the candidate causal-interface variable. The noetic-feelings void identifies *the gating that decides when to commit cognitive resources* as a verdict-layer compatible with low-bandwidth interaction. The bridge from medium-status to interface-candidacy is consistency-and-suggestion rather than evidence: the structural profile of medium-status (operative-while-invisible, mode-fragile, breakdown-revealable) is *compatible with* the profile a non-physical interface would be expected to show, but compatibility is not evidence — many physical features (DNS resolution, the speaker's articulatory motor commands during fluent speech, predictive-coding precision-weighting) have the same profile without being non-physical interfaces. The Map's claim is therefore conditional: *if* the dualist tenets are correct on independent grounds, medium-status names where the interface would be expected to sit. The void and the candidate channel share structural features rather than being the same feature viewed from opposite directions; treating them as identical would affirm the consequent. A compounding observation drawn from [the quantitative-comprehension void](/voids/the-quantitative-comprehension-void/): the *variables* such a channel would modulate — quantum-scale probabilities, magnitudes far from the human envelope — are themselves outside phenomenal access. The channel would be invisible from within; the quantities it would bias are not phenomenally graspable; the two opacities compound, and any account of the interface must accept both layers.

**[Occam's Razor Has Limits](/tenets/#occams-limits).** Parsimony at the content level fails when medium-level features structure everything else. A theory simpler in the categories internal to a scheme may be less faithful than a theory more complex in those categories that tracks structure along axes the scheme lacks. The medium-status framing makes this concrete: the simpler explanation often appears simpler because the medium of simplicity-judgement is presupposed. The Map's commitment to limited parsimony under incomplete knowledge is, among other things, a commitment to distrust the *feeling* of sufficiency that medium-status operations supply.

The unifying picture: the four medium-status voids together mark out a region where reductive accounts owe an explanation — how a medium of significance, filtering, gating, or framing reduces to features that are themselves contents within the field the medium constitutes. The cluster does not force the dualist conclusion; the structural profile is consistent with sophisticated functionalism. It does suggest consciousness has an architectural shape that physicalism, in its currently dominant forms, has not been pressed to address head-on. The structuring is invisible because it is the structuring — and that invisibility is consistent with the profile a non-physical operating-condition would generate, while remaining consistent with several physical alternatives as well.
