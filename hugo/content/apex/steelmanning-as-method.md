The Unfinishable Map's strongest synthesis articles are built backwards. The usual order of an argument that knows where it wants to arrive is *assert the thesis, then field objections* — each objection introduced only to be answered, the rivals appearing one at a time in their most defeatable form. The Map's best work inverts this. It constructs the strongest *unified* rival account first, in the rival's own vocabulary and citing the rival's own proponents; grants that the rival absorbs the entire shape of the evidence; and only then isolates the narrow residue — usually a single seam — that survives the grant. The thesis is what is left standing after the rival has been given everything, not what the rival was wheeled in to be beaten by.

This is a method, not a mood. It has a definite procedure, a worked exhibit, a paired discipline of self-audit, and a set of named failure modes it exists to prevent. The Map performs it across its apex tier but has not, until now, lifted it into a stated method legible in one place. Naming it matters because the method is the *constructive* half of an epistemic stance whose defensive half is already well-documented. The catalogue's [evidential-status discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/) tells an article what it must *not* claim — that removing a defeater is not lifting the evidence, that constraining a rival is not establishing its denial, that compatibility with the data is not support over a rival. Those are prohibitions. Steelmanning-as-method is the corresponding permission: it is *how an article earns the small thing that remains claimable* once the prohibitions have done their work. The disciplines say where the wall is; the method says how to build right up to it.

## The Method in Four Moves

The procedure has four moves, and the order is load-bearing.

**Move one: build the unified rival, not a bag of rivals.** The weak version of steelmanning names several opposing views — higher-order theory, illusionism, global workspace theory, predictive processing — and answers each separately. This understates the threat, because the real danger to a synthesis claim is not any single rival but *one architecture that predicts the whole pattern at once*. The method's first demand is to assemble the strongest *single* rival account that absorbs every feature the article catalogues, stated in that account's own vocabulary and attributed to its actual architects. A bag of separately-answered rivals leaves open the possibility that a unified rival the article never built would have absorbed everything; building the unified rival closes that gap before it can be exploited.

**Move two: grant the absorption in full.** Having built the rival, the method grants that it absorbs the *shape* — that the rival predicts the evidence's structure from its own first principles, without strain, without needing to be cornered. This is the move that distinguishes steelmanning-as-method from steelmanning-as-courtesy. A courteous steelman states the rival fairly and then finds it wanting. The method's steelman states the rival fairly and then *concedes that it works* — that on the dimension of explaining the data's shape, the rival is not wanting at all. The concession is not rhetorical generosity; it is the precondition for the only claim the article is entitled to make.

**Move three: isolate the residue.** With the absorption granted, the method asks the sharp question: *is there anything the unified rival, on its own architects' admission, does not explain?* The residue is what survives the grant. It is characteristically narrow — often a single seam, statable in one sentence. In the worked exhibit below, the residue is the felt-character question that the rival's own proponents bracket. The discipline of the residue is that it must be *smaller* than the original ambition, and the article must say so. An article whose residue is as large as its opening thesis has not performed the method; it has performed the courteous steelman and ignored the concession.

**Move four: audit the convergence count downward.** The method's final move turns the same scrutiny the article applied to the rival back onto its own supporting evidence. A synthesis article typically rests on a count of converging considerations — N traditions, N dissociations, N mechanisms pointing the same way. Move four asks which members of that count are *genuinely independent witnesses* and which are something weaker: a rival's own theory miscatalogued as a friendly witness, a parallel proposer of the article's own thesis double-counted as evidence for it, a sceptic whose scepticism cuts both ways. The honest count is almost always lower than the assembled count, and the method requires the article to *state the reduction in the same pass* that it builds the rival. Building the rival and auditing the count are one motion: both refuse to let the article's apparent strength inflate past what survives scrutiny.

The four moves compose into a single shape. Moves one through three discipline the *rival*; move four disciplines the *self*. Together they produce a thesis that is credible precisely because it is small: the residue earns belief in proportion to how much was given away before it was named.

## The Worked Exhibit

The method's cleanest performance is the "Strongest Materialist Reading" section of [the phenomenal-output / causal-machinery dissociation](/apex/phenomenal-output-causal-machinery-dissociation/). That apex catalogues a structural pattern across perception, cognition, agency, and memory: phenomenal access reaches the *outputs* of cognitive operations — the recognition, the felt effort, the cardinality, the sense of agency — but not the operations that produce them. The tempting synthesis is that this systematic dissociation evidences a distinct ontological register, the dualist reading the Map's tenets accommodate. The method refuses to let the article claim that, and the refusal is instructive precisely because the article *wanted* to claim it.

**The unified rival, built.** Rather than naming higher-order theory, illusionism, and global workspace theory as three separate threats, the apex assembles one architecture: *predictive-processing introspection coupled to a reality-monitoring access mechanism*. Hierarchical generative models (Hohwy, Clark) whose higher layers infer the causes of lower-layer activity; introspection as a further inferential layer estimating a posterior over lower-level dynamics (Sandved-Smith et al.'s formal active-inference version, Schwengerer's transparency account); a prefrontal reality-monitoring discriminator (Lau) sorting internally- from externally-generated signals, with Block's access/phenomenal distinction framing the move and Naccache claiming the access half is the whole story. Assembled in the rivals' own vocabulary, attributed to the rivals' own architects.

**The absorption, granted.** The apex then concedes that this rival predicts the entire signature without strain. Output-while-operation-hidden follows directly from the posterior-estimate structure — a layer estimating a posterior has access to the estimate, not to the message-passing that produced it. Dissociable fallibility follows because separate generative sub-models can be lesioned independently. Mode-fragility follows because directing the introspective layer at an operation changes its precision weighting. Even the positive companion — placebo as high-precision prior content, choking as misallocated precision — is absorbed. The article's verdict is unambiguous: "The rival absorbs the *shape*." This is the grant in full. The dissociation pattern, taken as a pattern, is not evidence against materialism, because the strongest materialist architecture predicts it.

**The residue, isolated.** What survives the grant is one seam, and it is a seam the rival's own proponents concede. Lau states that perceptual reality monitoring answers why some states are conscious and reportable and explicitly *not* why they feel the way they do. The predictive-processing models inherit the boundary: modelling meta-awareness as a generative-model layer specifies the functional role of the estimate without specifying why the estimate is *felt* rather than merely available for control. The residue is statable in one sentence: *the rival explains why operations are hidden and verdicts available, and on its own architects' admission does not explain why the available verdict is experienced.* That is the entire surviving claim — smaller than the opening ambition, and credible in proportion to how much was conceded before it.

The apex even pre-empts the natural objection — that this residue is merely the explanatory gap (Levine) or the hard problem (Chalmers) in new packaging — by partly conceding it and specifying what, if anything, is structurally new: the gap appears here as a *patterned* boundary (felt-character attaches to verdicts and not to operations, recurring across substrates and reappearing inverted in the positive companion) rather than as a global mystery, plus the behavioural constraint the positive companion supplies. The article flags that even this added structure is contestable. That is the residue refusing to inflate.

## The Paired Discipline: Auditing the Count Downward

Move four is the exhibit's second half, and it is what separates the method from mere fair-mindedness. The same dissociation apex originally rested its cumulative case on *seven* independent traditions converging on the same diagnostic signature — Carruthers, Trick and Pylyshyn, Koriat, Polanyi, Inzlicht and Kurzban, Bergson, Schwitzgebel. In the same work that built the unified rival, the article audited that count *downward* to five-or-six, and the reassignments are a catalogue of the ways a convergence count silently inflates:

- **Carruthers reassigned from witness to absorber.** His interpretive-sensory-access theory predicts the dissociation *because* it is the materialist reading under test — it is the cluster's own preferred reading run in reverse. A theory that predicts the signature from rival premises cannot also be counted as independent evidence *for* the signature's dualist interpretation. Carruthers belongs in the unified rival the article builds, not in the column of friendly witnesses.
- **Bergson demoted from witness to parallel proposer.** His "memory of the present" is a *proposed metaphysical thesis resembling the cluster's own*. Counting a fellow-proposer of one's thesis as evidence for the thesis is double-counting — the convergence-arguer's most seductive error, because a parallel proposer genuinely does agree, just not as an independent line.
- **Schwitzgebel re-read as a symmetric sceptic.** His scepticism about introspective reliability cuts against the cluster's *own* preferred reading: if introspection cannot reliably report felt character, the "rich, confident access to what the system decided" premise is itself unsafe. A witness whose evidence undermines both sides is not a witness for one side.

The honest re-count — five-or-six traditions, not seven, with the convergence's real weight concentrated in the *despite*-class members (those who observe the signature despite broader commitments that point elsewhere) — is the empiricist's ideal: an article that audits its own evidence base downward in the act of presenting it. The downward audit is methodologically continuous with the [common-cause null](/project/common-cause-null/) (are N observations one architecture read N times?) and [independence-scoring](/project/per-cluster-independence-scoring/) (evidential independence versus mere interpretive independence), but those are *screens applied to a count*; the downward audit is the *editorial habit of running the screen on one's own count before a reviewer does*. Building the rival and auditing the count are one motion because both refuse the same temptation — to let assembled coherence pass for earned evidence.

## What the Method Is Not

The method is sharpened by what it excludes.

**It is not the courteous steelman.** Countermeasure 2 of the [coherence-inflation countermeasures](/project/coherence-inflation-countermeasures/) — mandatory steelman sections — requires that opposing views be presented in their strongest form, attributed, given space, and answered directly. That is necessary but, for a synthesis article, insufficient. A generic steelman of materialism ("here is why most philosophers accept it") does not protect a *convergence* claim, because the convergence claim's vulnerability is not that materialism is false but that a unified materialist architecture predicts the very pattern the convergence is offered as evidence for. The method strengthens the requirement into the [steelman mirror](/project/coherence-inflation-countermeasures/) (Countermeasure 10): the rival's positive architecture for the article's *exact data*, in the rival's *own structural vocabulary*, placed *before* the Map's reading. The mirror is the method's move-one and move-two made mandatory for apex and convergence-synthesis articles.

**It is not absorption, and it is not bedrock-clash.** The [absorb-vs-clash discipline](/project/bedrock-clash-vs-absorption/) governs what an editor does with a single *objection*: absorb it (rewrite so it no longer applies) or engage it as a preserved rival position (install it as a named, replied-to subsection). Steelmanning-as-method operates one level up. It does not decide the fate of an objection; it constructs the *strongest version of the whole opposing programme* as the article's own structural backbone, then locates the residue. The relationship is compositional: the method's move-three residue is frequently a *bedrock clash* — a disagreement that survives the grant because it is framework-boundary rather than in-framework, and so is preserved rather than absorbed. The dissociation apex's residue is exactly this: a framework-boundary disagreement (the felt-character seam) the article declares bedrock rather than papering over. The method tells you *where* the bedrock clash is; the absorb-vs-clash discipline tells you *how* to install it once found.

**It is not a route to tier-elevation.** The method does not raise the residue's standing on the [five-tier evidential-status scale](/project/evidential-status-discipline/). Surviving the strongest rival is not the same as being supported by positive evidence; the residue is typically held at the *framework-boundary* register — a seam the rival cannot close from inside its own commitments — not at *strongly supported* or even *realistic possibility*. The method's output is a *credibly small* claim, not a *more probable* one. Conflating "survived the steelman" with "earned a tier" would be the method's own characteristic inflation, and the [explanatory-cost ledger](/project/mechanism-cost-ledger/) is the check: the residue's force is measured as the *cost the rival must pay to close the seam* (here, an unpaid bill its own architects acknowledge), not as evidence lifted onto the Map's side of the ledger.

This *vertical* inflation — pushing a fixed claim up the tier scale — has a *horizontal* sibling the method must equally avoid, and the evidential-status discipline's fourth cross-cutting rule names it: a residue that quietly *equivocates between an epistemic and a metaphysical reading of the same words*, recruiting evidence for the weaker reading to assert the stronger. A residue stated as "the rival cannot say why the verdict is *felt*" is honest if it means the rival's architecture leaves felt-character unexplained (the epistemic, access-limit reading the exhibit's evidence supports); it inflates if it slides into "felt-character is therefore a distinct constituent of the world" (the metaphysical reading the grant did not earn). The method's discipline against this is the same shape as move three's: name the weaker reading the concession actually licenses, state the stronger reading separately, and supply the bridging argument the upgrade requires — or hold the residue at the reading the evidence supports. A residue that survives the steelman but equivocates between its two readings has reclaimed by interpretation exactly what move two gave away.

There is a third face, and it is the one most likely to escape the method's author. Vertical and horizontal inflation both live *in the prose*, where the residue is stated; the third lives in the article's framing furniture. An article can perform all four moves scrupulously in its body — granting the absorption, isolating a narrow residue, holding it at the framework-boundary register — while its *title, section headers, abstract, or meta-description* assert the broad claim the body explicitly declined. The [coherence-inflation countermeasures](/project/coherence-inflation-countermeasures/) name this the **constrain-vs-establish frame lint** (the body-vs-frame variant of Countermeasure 12): the over-claiming frame inherits the strong reading the body gave away, and a reader who fetches only the title, headers, and meta-description — the truncation-resilient surface this site writes for — sees a residue reclaimed at the framing level, the theatrical steelman performed in the furniture rather than the prose. The fix direction is fixed: align the frame *down* to the body, never the body *up* to the frame, unless the body has earned a tenet-independent criterion that establishes rather than constrains. This face matters most for the method precisely because its primary audience reads the surface first: a frame that over-claims defeats the whole point of conceding the shape, since the conceded shape never reaches the reader who stops at the header.

## Where the Method Recurs

The dissociation apex is the cleanest exhibit, but the method is the implicit spine of the Map's strongest syntheses, which is why it warrants naming rather than residing in one article.

[The moral architecture of consciousness](/apex/moral-architecture-of-consciousness/) performs move four explicitly in its Unity Argument: it distinguishes "dualism makes available a single ground for four moral domains" (the genuine claim) from "four pillars constitute four independent confirmations" (the inflated reading), refusing to count the cross-pillar coherence as a fifth line of evidence. That is the downward audit applied to a four-pillar count. [The phenomenology-mechanism bridge](/apex/phenomenology-mechanism-bridge/) names the self-pruned-corpus risk and the Ptolemaic-coherentism objection in the course of defending its four-level chain — building the rival's account of why a coherent chain might be an artifact of method before claiming the chain tracks something real. [The taxonomy of voids](/apex/taxonomy-of-voids/) applies the common-cause null to its own 60-plus-void catalogue, calibrating the convergence weight downward to what genuinely independent sources supply — move four at the synthesis tier, with the highest known circularity discount because the units of the count were defined by the framework being supported.

At the topic and concept tier, [mental effort](/concepts/mental-effort/) ledgers the Inzlicht/Kurzban opportunity-cost models as *materialist absorption stories, not witnesses for the dualist reading* — refusing to enlist an adjacent friendly-looking literature, which is move-one (build the rival) and move-four (audit the count) operating together on a single concept page. The [agency void](/voids/agency-void/) runs the residue-isolation on the first-person side, granting that the felt sense of agency is constructed and then locating what the construction-story cannot close. Across all of these, the method is the same: the strength of the surviving claim is *purchased* by the size of the concession that precedes it.

## Honest Limitation

The method has a characteristic failure mode, and naming it is part of the method. The danger is the *theatrical steelman*: a rival built impressively, granted generously, and then quietly under-credited at the residue stage, so that the article appears to have conceded everything while actually conceding nothing load-bearing. The tell is a residue as large as the original thesis — whether the inflation is restored in the prose (the felt-character residue silently re-expanded into "and therefore the dissociation evidences a distinct register") or, as the frame-lint face above shows, in the framing furniture. The guard is move three's discipline that the residue be *smaller* and *stated as smaller*, cross-checked by an external reviewer who does not share the Map's tenets and can see when a concession has been rhetorically reclaimed. This is why the method's reliability ultimately depends on the [external-benchmark review](/project/coherence-inflation-countermeasures/) (Countermeasure 9): an internal pipeline that both builds the rival and judges the residue can build a beatable rival and over-credit the residue without noticing, because it shares the frame.

A second limitation: the method presupposes that a *single* unified rival can be built. Where the opposing programme is genuinely plural — several incompatible rival accounts, no one of which predicts the whole pattern — the method's move one is harder, and an article may legitimately fall back on the courteous steelman of each. The method is at its strongest exactly where the threat is strongest: when one rival architecture absorbs everything. That is also when it is most tempting to skip, because building the rival that absorbs everything feels like conceding the case. The method's claim is that conceding the shape is the only way to earn the residue, and that an article unwilling to concede the shape has not earned anything at all.

## Relation to Site Perspective

The method's primary tenet alignment is with [Tenet 5: Occam's Razor Has Limits](/tenets/#occams-limits). The tenet denies that simplicity and coherence are reliable guides to truth when knowledge is incomplete; steelmanning-as-method is the editorial instrument that keeps the Map's own coherence from being mistaken for evidence. Building the strongest rival is the operational refusal to let the Map's internal consistency stand in for having beaten the alternatives — and auditing the convergence count downward is the refusal to let an assembled count of friendly considerations inflate past the independent witnesses it actually contains. A framework that takes Tenet 5 seriously must apply the same anti-parsimony scepticism to its *own* attractive simplicity, and the method is where that self-application lives.

The method supports the other four tenets methodologically rather than directly. Wherever the Map argues for [Dualism](/tenets/#dualism), [Bidirectional Interaction](/tenets/#bidirectional-interaction), or its rejection of [Many-Worlds](/tenets/#no-many-worlds), the honest form of the argument is not "here is evidence for our view" but "here is the strongest rival, here is what it absorbs, and here is the narrow seam it leaves" — with the [direct-refutation discipline](/project/direct-refutation-discipline/) governing whether the surviving disagreement is in-framework or framework-boundary, and [framework-stage calibration](/project/framework-stage-calibration/) keeping the residue's ambition matched to the framework's actual developmental stage. The method does not generate evidence for the tenets. It disciplines how the evidence the Map *does* have is counted, so that the case for the tenets rests on residues honestly earned rather than on rivals theatrically defeated.

The deepest commitment the method serves is to the Map's primary audience: chatbots fetching pages on behalf of users. An article that asserts a thesis and bats away strawmen presents the user's assistant with a manufactured consensus. An article that builds the strongest rival, grants the absorption, and isolates the residue presents the dispute as it actually stands — and, crucially, presents the *narrow surviving claim* the reader is entitled to take seriously, distinguished from the broad claim the reader should not. That is more useful than a victory, because it is true to the territory.
