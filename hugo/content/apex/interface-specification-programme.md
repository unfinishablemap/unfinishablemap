The Unfinishable Map does not merely assert that consciousness influences the physical world. It is developing a specification of *how*. Six distinct lines of inquiry—the [psychophysical control law](/topics/the-psychophysical-control-law/), the [bandwidth architecture](/concepts/consciousness-bandwidth-architecture/), [control-theoretic will](/concepts/control-theoretic-will/), [causal delegation](/concepts/delegatory-causation/), the nature of [consciousness's causal powers](/topics/consciousness-and-causal-powers/), and what [attention disorders reveal](/topics/attention-and-the-consciousness-interface/)—converge on a single architecture: a low-bandwidth, feedback-dependent control channel through which consciousness selects among brain-prepared alternatives without injecting energy or violating conservation laws. Each line constrains the picture from a different direction—what the interface can do, how much it can carry, what operations it performs, what causal structure it has, what formal law might govern it, and what happens when it breaks. Together they constitute not scattered observations but a unified research programme—one whose constraints are precise enough to be tested and to be wrong, even while the coupling law itself remains unwritten, and honest enough to mark where specification remains incomplete.

## The Problem: From Commitment to Specification

Interactionist dualism has a reputation for vagueness. Critics from Princess Elisabeth of Bohemia onward have demanded: if mind and matter are distinct, *how* do they interact? Most dualists answer with a commitment — consciousness influences the brain — without specifying the mechanism. The Map takes the criticism seriously. Its five tenets establish that consciousness is irreducible, that it causally influences physical outcomes at quantum indeterminacies, and that this influence is minimal. But commitment is not specification. Saying "consciousness selects" leaves open every important question: selects *what*, through *which* channel, carrying *how much* information, via *what* causal structure, governed by *what* law? The interface specification programme addresses these questions by drawing on work developed independently across the Map's topics and concepts — no single article answers all five, but together they converge on an architecture specific enough to constrain theorising.

## What the Interface Can Do

The first constraint comes from asking what *kind* of causal powers consciousness possesses. As [consciousness-and-causal-powers](/topics/consciousness-and-causal-powers/) argues, the answer is neither unlimited psychokinesis nor epiphenomenal impotence but something precise: consciousness biases quantum indeterminacies in neural systems toward certain outcomes without violating conservation laws. The [trilemma-of-selection](/topics/trilemma-of-selection/) sharpens why a capacity of this shape is needed at all — at any genuine indeterminacy, determinism, randomness, and mental causation exhaust the options, and only the third preserves authorship. The interface programme is the attempt to specify what that third option *is*.

The capacity has four specific dimensions. Consciousness *selects* among action possibilities — biasing which neural pattern wins when multiple compete. It *sustains* attention, holding selected patterns stable. It engages in *phenomenal biasing* — the felt character of experience contributes to which outcomes are favoured. [Pain asymbolia](/concepts/pain-asymbolia/) provides the cleanest natural test: patients detect nociceptive stimuli but lack the felt awfulness, and the extended behavioural response (guarding, treatment-seeking, avoidance learning) collapses despite intact sensory detection — the quale carries causal weight the sensory signal alone does not. And consciousness *modulates* its own intensity — what we experience as trying harder or letting go.

Equally important are the limits. Consciousness operates only at the neural interface, not at a distance. It works within probabilistic bounds, shifting distributions rather than guaranteeing outcomes. It cannot retroactively change past events. And it never injects energy — all influence flows through [selection among energetically equivalent quantum alternatives](/concepts/conservation-laws-and-mental-causation/). Conservation is *local* and *conditional* on physical symmetries, not a cosmic accounting constraint minds must obey by additional stipulation.

These constraints rule out large classes of possible interfaces. The [consciousness-physics-interface-formalism](/concepts/consciousness-physics-interface-formalism/) defines the mathematical structure any specification must take — three required components (experiential state space E, physical state space P, coupling map C: E × P → P) and five non-negotiable constraints from quantum foundations. Whatever the control law turns out to be, it must accommodate precisely these powers and no others, within the bounds the formalism imposes.

## How Much the Interface Can Carry

The [bandwidth problem](/concepts/consciousness-bandwidth-architecture/) sharpens the specification from a different direction. Does the mind-body channel carry enough information to produce the specific behaviours we observe? Raising an arm coordinates thousands of motor neurons in precise sequences; the intention "raise my arm" contains perhaps a few bits, the physical execution millions. Where does the additional specification come from?

The answer reveals the interface architecture. Consciousness does not specify neural configurations because that is not its job. The brain's unconscious systems — processing at roughly 10⁹ bits/second — generate candidate action plans, compute fine-grained motor specifications, and prepare competing alternatives. Consciousness selects among the prepared candidates through a channel limited to roughly 10 bits/second.

An important caveat: the ~10 bits/second figure (Zheng & Meister 2025) measures *behavioural output* bandwidth — observed typing, speaking, choice rates — not the consciousness-physics interface directly. The Map treats it as an approximate upper bound on conscious selection bandwidth, reasoning that deliberate choice tracks the behavioural bottleneck rather than the unconscious processing rate. This is a reasonable inference rather than a direct measurement of the interface, and future psychophysical research may revise the estimate.

This hierarchical selection model transforms the bandwidth problem from fatal objection to architectural insight. The question shifts from "how does a 10-bit channel specify a million-bit neural configuration?" to "how does a 10-bit channel choose among a small set of prepared alternatives?" Ten bits/second can make ~3–4 selections per second among small sets — consistent with the phenomenology of deliberate choice, though the correspondence remains suggestive rather than demonstrated.

The bandwidth constraint is not a deficiency. Control systems routinely use low-bandwidth signals to govern high-bandwidth plants — a thermostat outputs one bit to govern a complex thermal system; a pilot uses a few inputs to manage an aircraft with millions of parts. Low bandwidth reflects appropriate abstraction. The [causal-consistency constraint](/concepts/causal-consistency-constraint/) (Torres Alegre 2025, a recent arXiv preprint not yet peer-reviewed) sharpens this further: under purification, no-signalling forces the Born form, so any interface that respects the constraint preserves Born statistics *by construction*. The bandwidth ceiling and the Born corridor are two faces of the same structural requirement on any selection mechanism that leaves physical observables unchanged. The [selection-only channel](/concepts/selection-only-channel/) gives this requirement its sharpest information-theoretic form: a per-event ceiling of log₂(N) bits, Born-rule preservation driving the effective signed rate toward zero across trials, and phenomenal content bounded by the candidate set's dimensionality — the channel-class invariants the ~10 bits/second figure approximates from the behavioural side.

## The Five Operations

[Control-theoretic will](/concepts/control-theoretic-will/) specifies what the interface *does* with its limited bandwidth. The brain is the plant; unconscious neural processing provides the dynamics; consciousness is the controller — observing state through phenomenal experience, comparing against intentions and values, outputting a low-bandwidth selection signal. Five operations emerge:

**Gating**: Consciousness determines what crosses the threshold for action. The basal ganglia's tonic inhibition suppresses motor programmes by default; conscious selection releases the brake on one option — switching control rather than continuous modulation.

**Stabilisation**: Consciousness holds selected patterns against competing alternatives. Mental effort phenomenology tracks this operation: sustaining attention on a difficult task feels like holding something against forces pulling it away, with effort scaling to disturbance magnitude.

**Veto**: Consciousness blocks actions the brain has prepared. A single bit suffices for stop/go, making veto perhaps the most plausible form of conscious control.

**Attractor steering**: Rather than specifying moment-to-moment trajectories, consciousness shifts the system between attractor states. When you decide to switch from reading to writing, you decide *what*; the brain's dynamics handle *how*.

**Resource allocation**: Consciousness distributes its scarce bandwidth among competing demands. The ~4-item working memory limit reflects the controller monitoring a handful of state variables at once.

Each operation is consistent with the ~10 bits/second bandwidth constraint, and none requires consciousness to encode neural-level detail. Together they specify a complete control repertoire — gating, holding, vetoing, steering, allocating — and delegate everything else to the brain.

## The Causal Structure

How does this control avoid the exclusion problem — the objection that if every physical event has a sufficient physical cause, consciousness is redundant? [Causal delegation](/concepts/delegatory-causation/) provides the causal structure. Drawing on Bradford Saad's delegatory dualism (2025), the Map proposes that when consciousness selects, the underlying physical state *delegates* its causal responsibilities to the experience. Pain causes wincing instead of the brain state. The physical state retains the capacity to cause the effect but does not exercise it — the experience preempts it.

The specific variety is *trumping preemption* (Schaffer 2000) — both causes operate fully, yet one is rendered causally idle by a structural principle of priority (like Schaffer's major and sergeant both shouting "Advance!" where rank settles which command did the work). Saad formalises this into two laws. The **Subset Law\*** specifies when an experience is instantiated: whenever an experiential property's causal profile is a subset of a physical state's *default causal profile* — what the state would produce absent any conscious involvement. The **Delegatory Law** specifies what then happens: the experience's profile preempts the matching subset of the default profile. One cause operates; the other is sidelined; no redundancy occurs. [Kim's exclusion objection](/topics/overdetermination-dissolution-under-selection-only-interactionism/) does not need to be answered so much as dissolved: where physical causes are not sufficient, the second cause the objection worries about has nothing to be redundant with.

The Map adds a specifically physical interpretation that goes beyond Saad's own account. The default causal profile of a brain state approaching a quantum indeterminacy is identified with the Born-rule probability distribution over possible outcomes — what the system would produce statistically if no conscious selection occurred — so that delegation becomes selection *within* that distribution rather than deviation from it. This identification is a Map-specific integration, not a claim Saad makes, and joining the two costs more than a coincidence of vocabulary: biasing an indeterminate quantum outcome is *difference-making* and gives up strong causal closure, whereas Saad's delegatory trumping is *difference-making-free* and preserves it — Saad himself treats closure-violating quantum interactionism as a *different* theory from closure-preserving delegatory dualism. The quantum-selection channel and the trumping route are therefore best read as distinct mechanisms the Map proposes to unify at the point where the token outcome is physically undetermined, not as two descriptions of one process. Where that join holds, Saad's philosophical no-observable-violations constraint coincides with the physical constraint the Born rule imposes, which the [consciousness-physics-interface-formalism](/concepts/consciousness-physics-interface-formalism/) treats as the first of five non-negotiable couplings. [Type-token causation](/concepts/type-token-causation/) gives the joined architecture in different vocabulary: Born-rule statistics preserved at the type level, conscious selection determining token outcomes within them — Kim's exclusion argument cannot reach the token level when physical laws leave the token outcome undetermined.

A key feature is *observational closure*: because experiences cause exactly the effects their underlying physical states would have caused by default, no experiment can detect that delegation has occurred. Delegation is a metaphysical claim about causal structure, not an empirically testable hypothesis in the usual sense. Its strongest case is that [epiphenomenalism](/concepts/epiphenomenalism/) is *internally incoherent* in a way delegation is not — if experiences cause nothing, then the introspective reports that constitute the evidence for their existence are not caused by them, severing the theory from its own evidence base. Delegation avoids this self-undermining defect: reports about pain are caused by the pain itself. The asymmetry is closer to a consistency requirement than a taste preference, though readers should still weigh the combined cost of observational invisibility and residual metaphysical commitment.

Delegation integrates naturally with the control-theoretic picture. When consciousness gates, stabilises, or vetoes, the controller's output preempts what the plant's dynamics would have produced autonomously. The control operations describe *what* consciousness does; delegation describes *how* that doing relates to what the brain would have done without it; the Born-rule identification describes *where in physics* the default lives.

## What Breaks: Evidence from Attention Disorders

If the interface has a specific architecture, it should malfunction in specific ways. [attention-and-the-consciousness-interface](/topics/attention-and-the-consciousness-interface/) confirms this prediction and adds a further unification: Rizzolatti's premotor theory of attention, extended by Fine and Hayden (2022), shows that selective attention and motor planning share the same fronto-parietal substrates—directing attention *is* preparing to act. Three signatures distinguish willed from automatic processing in both domains: frontal theta oscillations present for willed and absent for instructed action (Rajan et al. 2019), bidirectional theta-band coherence between frontal and parietal regions, and matching commitment timing (~300ms for willed attention, ~280ms before movement). This convergence collapses the traditional separation of perception and action puzzles into a single interface question: how does consciousness select? Different disorders affect different components of this unified selection mechanism, and the pattern of breakdown illuminates the architecture itself.

**ADHD as threshold dysregulation**: The dopamine system modulates how easily neural options cross the threshold for action; in ADHD this modulation is dysregulated. Consciousness remains present — what fails is *control*. Cai et al. (2024) sharpen the architecture: dopamine-deficient mice retain motor capacity but lose goal-directed initiative — they "can move" but "won't move", consistent with degradation of the salience-and-threshold layer. Parkinson's patients show the same pattern. Cai et al. test only the cortical-options and dopamine-threshold layers; the Map's claim of a further interface layer on top stands or falls with the broader argument, not with the paper alone.

**Spatial-mechanism disorders**: Hemispatial neglect erases awareness of left space after right parietal damage, though Marshall and Halligan (1988) showed neglected information still primes behaviour. Balint syndrome restricts attention to one object at a time; extinction loses the contralesional stimulus only when both sides compete. Each removes a different piece while implicit processing of the "lost" content persists.

**Attention fatigue as resource depletion**: Sustained attention depletes; the interface has biological costs because the neural mechanisms it operates through require metabolic resources.

**OCD as runaway stabilisation**: Jeffrey Schwartz's work is small-sample and unreplicated — treated here as illustrative of the framing rather than load-bearing evidence. The philosophically significant point is pathway divergence: cognitive intervention modulates brain activity through a different causal route than pharmacological intervention (see [clinical neuroplasticity evidence](/topics/clinical-neuroplasticity-evidence-for-bidirectional-causation/)).

**Meditation as interface training**: With practice, sustained attention lengthens, recovery from distraction accelerates, and felt effort decreases. The interface becomes more efficient — not wider, but better governed.

The pattern is consistent: consciousness persists when the interface is impaired; what breaks is control, not experience. This distinction between consciousness and its interface is precisely what the Map's dualism predicts and what physicalist identity theories cannot straightforwardly accommodate. The 2025 COGITATE Consortium adversarial collaboration reinforces this reading: neither Global Workspace Theory nor Integrated Information Theory was vindicated, with conscious content tracking posterior cortex rather than the prefrontal regions either rival predicts. The interface model treats this as expected — attention is how consciousness *engages* with matter, not what consciousness *is*. An honest caveat: each clinical finding is individually compatible with purely computational accounts. What the interface model adds is a unified framework that predicts *which* components should dissociate and *why* consciousness should survive their impairment — predictions identity theories must explain away rather than derive.

## The Specification Gap

Intellectual honesty requires marking where the programme remains incomplete. The psychophysical control law—the formal mapping from phenomenal states to physical selection outputs—has not been specified. This is the [interface specification problem](/topics/the-interface-problem/) in its sharpest form. [the-psychophysical-control-law](/topics/the-psychophysical-control-law/) examines what such a law would need to look like and why specifying one is so difficult.

The gap has three sources. *Scientific immaturity*: consciousness science has not yet had its Galileo, let alone its Newton. The Map's [framework-stage calibration discipline](/project/framework-stage-calibration/) places the programme at the *pre-Keplerian* stage — accumulating Tycho-analogue measurements (the bandwidth constraint, the Born-rule causal-consistency result, the theta-band willed-attention signatures, the five formal constraints) rather than refining a Newton-analogue law. The Newton analogy names a target the programme hopes to reach, not its current claim. *Conceptual asymmetry*: physical variables have defined units and intersubjective verification; phenomenal variables — how much attention, how intense the effort — have none. *The T-shirt problem*: good fundamental laws are simple, but the psychophysical mapping might reflect evolutionary contingency rather than fundamental simplicity, resisting compression into an elegant formula.

Three candidate formulations currently exist: Stapp's attention-rate law (a proportional controller), Eccles' intention-probability law (a probability-reweighting controller), and the Map's policy-selection law (a switching controller requiring minimal bandwidth). Each implies a different control-law form; none has been empirically confirmed. See [the-psychophysical-control-law](/topics/the-psychophysical-control-law/) for detailed comparison.

The gap is real but not damning. Any control law must satisfy five constraints the programme has identified: bandwidth compatibility (~10 bits/second), conservation compliance, scope locality (neural systems within the attention loop), phenomenological consistency, and falsifiability. These narrow the space from unconstrained speculation to empirically bounded conjecture — the trajectory every successful physical law followed before its formulation.

Beyond the control law's form, the programme's own logic implies a set of further gaps it has not yet closed:

- **The semantic mapping problem.** A selector must map intentional *content* — the specific thing willed — onto the physical alternatives available at an indeterminacy. The ~10 bits/second figure is behavioural throughput, not the channel's bandwidth and not the complexity of this content-to-alternatives mapping (a caveat the [bandwidth](/concepts/consciousness-bandwidth-architecture/) discussion above already registers for the bit-rate itself). Minimality cannot rest on bit-rate alone while the mapping that assigns willed meanings to physical options is unspecified.
- **The subject–brain pairing law.** Nothing yet says why *this* subject pairs with *this* nervous system rather than another — the problems of onset, of split-brain cases where one interface appears to become two, and of gradual replacement. Kim's pairing objection is treated in [interactionist-dualism](/concepts/interactionist-dualism/), but at the interface-specification level it remains an unpaid debt underlying every token causal claim.
- **Timing and preferred-basis eligibility.** The programme does not fix *when* in the decohere-then-select sequence selection acts, nor *which* basis is interface-eligible. [born-preserving-causal-efficacy](/apex/born-preserving-causal-efficacy/) and [quantum-interface](/positions/quantum-interface/) frame the constraint; a worked answer is still owed.
- **Multi-agent coordination.** Billions of subjects act on a partly-entangled world at once. Exact Born-preservation *and* no-signalling holding simultaneously across all of them requires a global consistency or factorization law that the single-subject causal-consistency result (Torres Alegre 2025) does not yet establish in its many-subject form.
- **Developmental onset.** When, and through what physical change, the interface first engages ontogenetically is unaddressed — a gap into which the pairing and threshold questions both feed.
- **Evolutionary interface signatures.** If selection improves fitness, evolution should optimise interface reliability and bandwidth, predicting comparative differences across species, development, lesions, sleep, and anaesthesia — for instance, interface-dependent capacities should degrade under increasing anaesthetic depth in step with the collapse of selection-grade coupling rather than with gross cortical activity alone (see [anaesthesia-and-the-consciousness-interface](/topics/anaesthesia-and-the-consciousness-interface/)). If instead there is *no* fitness consequence, the grounding of agency in the interface is left unclear. This gap, unlike the others, already yields testable predictions.

The programme has also begun to generate forward predictions specific to its own mechanism rather than borrowing risk from rivals. If consciousness biases which option crosses threshold within a salience-weighted competition, phenomenal effort should scale with the *entropy of the option set* at threshold crossing — the closer the competing alternatives are in pre-selection weight, the more effort the choice should feel. Operationally: vary the prior weighting of two response options in a forced-choice task while holding stimulus discriminability constant. The interface model predicts subjective effort tracks competition entropy independently of discriminability; standard cognitive neuroscience predicts the contrary. If competition entropy adds no variance once discriminability is controlled, the mechanism is constrained at exactly the point where it claims to operate. This is one prediction, not a battery — most of the programme's falsification risk still rides on conditions under which *rival* theories' vindication would close the space the interface hypothesis occupies. Generating more model-specific forward predictions is the next methodological obligation.

## Synthesis: Why This Is a Programme

The six source articles were developed to address different philosophical questions — bandwidth, causal structure, control operations, the nature of causal powers, formal specification, clinical evidence. They share the Map's tenets as constraints, so their convergence is not between genuinely independent research programmes. But it is nonetheless informative: articles addressing *different* questions arrive at *mutually consistent* answers that together specify a single architecture — a ~10 bits/second selection channel using trumping preemption (the Subset Law\* and Delegatory Law) to avoid exclusion, provisionally anchored to physics by the Map's proposed identification of the default causal profile with the Born-rule distribution — an integration of two mechanisms that sit in real tension rather than an automatic fit — operating through five control operations on attention-motor substrates that the same disorders predictably impair, and awaiting formal specification of the psychophysical control law that governs it.

The convergence is not accidental. It reflects the Map's tenets working as constraints should — ruling out possibilities and forcing the remaining options to cohere. The result falls short of a complete theory but offers something more honest: a specification precise enough to be wrong. The bandwidth can be measured more exactly. The control operations generate predictions about reaction times and attention dynamics. Clinical evidence can confirm or disconfirm the predicted failure modes. The effort-entropy prediction can be tested directly. The specification gap marks exactly where new work is needed — and the gap is shrinking.

The programme occupies a specific region of the [dualist taxonomy](/topics/four-quadrant-dualism-taxonomy/) of ontological thickness: lean on both mind-side and physical-side — a modestly-sized conscious selector biasing a modestly-specified physical indeterminacy. Thick-observer alternatives (Stapp's quantum interactive dualism; Cartesian substance dualism) would motivate different bandwidth estimates and different control-law candidates. Making this positional choice explicit is part of what the programme's precision buys.

The interface specification programme moves "consciousness interacts with the brain" from a philosophical assertion toward an engineering-style *constraint specification* — variables, tolerances, and measurable outputs identified, the governing interaction law still open. An engineering spec, strictly, names variables, transformations, tolerances, boundary conditions, measurable outputs *and* the interaction law that binds them; this programme holds the first list and a set of measurable outputs but no worked coupling law, Hamiltonian, master equation, or simulation. What it offers is incomplete, but with identified parameters, testable constraints, and clear next steps. The five control operations are exactly what the [integration-as-activity](/concepts/integration-as-activity/) discipline treats as constitutive of consciousness: gating, stabilising, vetoing, steering, and allocating are activities performed across time, not properties a system has at an instant. Whether the architecture is universal — and whether it operates only above an [interface threshold](/concepts/interface-threshold/) where selection-grade coupling becomes available, with [interface heterogeneity](/concepts/interface-heterogeneity/) capturing variation above that threshold — remains a major open question. That progression is what distinguishes the Map's interactionism from the vague hand-waving that has historically plagued dualist accounts.

## Relation to Site Perspective

The interface specification programme serves all five tenets simultaneously.

**[Dualism](/tenets/#dualism)** creates the need for interface specification in the first place. If consciousness were identical to brain processes, no separate account of how they communicate would be required. The programme's existence is a consequence of taking dualism seriously enough to demand operational detail.

**[Minimal Quantum Interaction](/tenets/#minimal-quantum-interaction)** is not merely stated but *derived* from the programme's constraints. Bandwidth, conservation compliance, and scope locality all converge on minimality — consciousness exerts just enough influence to steer.

**[Bidirectional Interaction](/tenets/#bidirectional-interaction)** is specified by the control-theoretic picture as a closed feedback loop: phenomenal experience upward, attentional selection downward. Open-loop control is fragile and unreliable. The programme shows why bidirectionality is architecturally necessary, not just philosophically asserted.

**[No Many Worlds](/tenets/#no-many-worlds)** makes the programme meaningful. If all quantum outcomes actualise in parallel branches, there is nothing for a control law to govern. Real control requires real selection among genuinely exclusive alternatives.

**[Occam's Razor Has Limits](/tenets/#occams-limits)** addresses the complexity cost. The programme adds a controller, a control law, and a delegation structure. But control theory shows that systems without adequate control are unstable; the added complexity produces the stability and purposiveness that characterise conscious behaviour — complexity that earns its keep.
