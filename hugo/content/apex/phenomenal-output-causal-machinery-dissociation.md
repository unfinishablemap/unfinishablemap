Some cognitive operations leave their outputs in consciousness but hide the operations that produced them. Familiarity arrives without the recollection that should have supplied it; effort feels real without the executive engagement that should have generated it; cardinality is given as a unity without the parallel-indexing operation that registered it. The Unfinishable Map calls this the *phenomenal-output / causal-machinery dissociation cluster*: a structural pattern recurring across perception, cognition, agency, and memory, in which phenomenal access reaches the verdict but not the verdict-issuing process, and felt certainty becomes the explanandum rather than the evidence. The cluster is the catalogue's tightest demonstration that materialism's "consciousness is just brain activity" has not yet pressed a unified account of why phenomenal output detaches systematically from the operations it putatively reports on, in the symmetric two-sided shape the catalogue reveals.

This synthesis catalogues the cluster's members across three timescales — instant of perception, real-time cognition and agency, across-time memory — and tracks the convergence of seven traditions on the same diagnostic signature. It installs the methodological discipline: each dissociation *constrains* identity readings of phenomenology with its underlying operation; none on its own *establishes* dualism. What lifts the evidence is the cumulative convergence — the same structural pattern across substrates with different representational profiles, recovered through independent vocabularies, with the common-cause caveat (all seven analyse human cognition through human introspection) set out below.

The cluster has a positive companion. [Placebo and choking](/topics/empirical-phenomena-mental-causation/) show the same architecture running in the other direction: phenomenology *coupling* to causal machinery in the success cases. The two are the negative and positive sides of a single architectural feature, with the symmetry constraining what any account must explain.

## The Diagnostic Signature

The cluster's signature is operational. *Phenomenal access reaches outputs not processes*: subjects have rich, often confident access to what the system has decided, recognised, recalled, or felt — and effectively no access to the operation that produced the verdict. *Phenomenal certainty is the explanandum, not the evidence*: the felt sense of pastness, familiarity, rightness, or knowing-as is the datum any account must explain, and cannot be turned around as evidence for the operation it ostensibly reports on without circularity.

Three sub-features compose the signature.

**Output-while-operation-hidden.** Cardinality below four arrives all at once with the indexing invisible; a face is recognised as familiar without the matching trace becoming inspectable; mental effort fires without exposing the executive engagement it accompanies. Each output is given as such; each operation has no phenomenal character.

**Dissociable fallibility.** Components fail selectively without the system reporting the failure as a failure of mechanism. Déjà vu fires familiarity without recollection; jamais vu withholds familiarity for still-known material; cryptomnesia delivers content without source-tag; Capgras strips affective familiarity from intact perceptual identification; prosopagnosia gives the inverse. Selective failures expose the fusion that normally hides it without making the operation observable from inside.

**Mode-fragility under inspection.** Inspecting the operation either transforms it (the absorbed expert monitoring their swing chokes) or terminates the field of which it was a precondition (the meditator's contemplative bracketing suspends rather than reveals the field). The void admits *outputs-by-deformation* — evidence from breakdowns and edge cases — but no *inspection-while-operating*.

A successful cluster member detaches phenomenal output from operation along at least one axis; a candidate that does not (mere capacity limit, mere content-puzzle) belongs to a different family. The classification is load-bearing for what the convergence below establishes.

## The Negative Side: Three Timescales

The dissociation appears across three nested timescales. The signatures match; the substrates do not.

### Instant of Perception

At the instant of perception, recognition delivers content with the act of recognising hidden behind it. The [recognition void](/voids/recognition-void/) names the gap between perceiving and knowing-*as*, where consciousness must find meaning in what it already sees but cannot observe itself doing so. Bruce and Young's two-component model and the Capgras-prosopagnosia double dissociation expose recognition as a hidden compound of perceptual identification and affective familiarity bound together invisibly. The patient experiences "this person feels wrong" or "this face means nothing"; neither experiences "my familiarity module is offline." The failure is given as content, not as mechanism.

The [memory anomalies cluster](/topics/memory-anomalies/) develops the same dissociation along three rotations of a single source-monitoring failure. Déjà vu is felt pastness without retrievable past — Cleary's gestalt-match paradigm shows configurational match producing felt premonition without future-knowledge. Jamais vu is felt alienness on still-known material; cryptomnesia is recollection without source-tag. What arrives in consciousness is the verdict (familiar / alien / mine); what stays hidden is the dual-process retrieval and source-monitoring machinery that produced it.

The [cardinality floor of the quantitative comprehension void](/voids/the-quantitative-comprehension-void/#the-cardinality-floor) gives the diagnostic in its sharpest single-shot form. Three dots are *three*, immediately and as a unity, with no felt act of counting. The Parallel Individuation System (Trick & Pylyshyn 1994) does the work; the work has no phenomenal character. The transition from subitizing to counting at four–five items is itself opaque: there is no "almost subitizing five." The architectural cap manifests *as* the experience of cardinality rather than being inspectable from within it.

### Real Time

In real-time cognition and agency, the dissociation extends from perceptual to cognitive and volitional outputs. [Mental effort](/concepts/mental-effort/) is the cleanest worked case. Three converging lines detach the felt sense from the operation it ostensibly reports on. Naccache and colleagues (2005) report a patient with anterior cingulate damage who performed Stroop conflict trials with normal executive control yet reported no conscious feeling of mental effort. The Hagger preregistered ego-depletion replication (2016) collapsed the strength-resource model the felt sense had pointed toward. Inzlicht and Kurzban reframed felt effort as the output of opportunity-cost machinery whose inputs the introspector cannot access. The felt sense is real and does causal work in the autonomic chain; it is not a transparent window onto the underlying executive process.

The [noetic-feelings void](/voids/noetic-feelings-void/) generalises across felt epistemic signals — knowing, rightness, familiarity, understanding, coherence, confidence. Koriat established that noetic feelings rest on inferential heuristics — cue-familiarity, accessibility — that operate implicitly. Thompson showed the feeling of rightness is driven by generation fluency rather than correctness, with manipulations of fluency (font legibility, rhyme, perceptual ease) shifting the felt verdict without changing the underlying reasoning. The verdicts gate further cognition — when to stop searching, when to trust, when to engage analytic thought — and the system cannot audit them from inside, because audit requires more feelings of the same kind.

The [agency void](/voids/agency-void/) picks up the same dissociation in the felt sense of being the cause of one's actions. Howard and colleagues (2016) showed that physical and mental effort disrupt the implicit sense of agency even as the explicit sense often rises. Wegner's work establishes that the *experience* of willing arises from interpreting thoughts as causes on cues of priority, consistency, and exclusivity; the cues, not the willing, are what the introspector has access to. Wegner's thesis inverts the cluster's preferred reading — he treats the felt sense as confabulation rather than as an opaque report on real underlying willing — and the cluster cites his observations for the structural signature he documents while flagging the divergence. The [suspension void](/voids/suspension-void/) adds *refraining* from selection: the felt act of holding back has phenomenal character whose machinery is hidden the same way.

These four — felt-effort, felt-agency, felt-suspension, felt-knowing — sit as one functional family of sub-personally generated signals that gate distinct operations while sharing the same opacity pattern. [self-opacity](/voids/self-opacity/)'s §Introspective Opacity *Process* dimension states the diagnostic in its most general form: consciousness accesses mental contents — thoughts, images, feelings — but not the processes that produce them. James's "spinning top," Nisbett and Wilson's confabulation experiments, Johansson's choice-blindness, Libet-and-after timing data, and Wegner's reconstructive account of willing all converge on the same observation, even where interpretive readings diverge.

### Across Time

[Consciousness and memory](/topics/consciousness-and-memory/) adds the across-time face. Memory consolidation transforms the phenomenal character of a recall along a distinctive trajectory — vivid sensory re-experiencing in the hours after encoding, narrative gist over months, schematised semantic fact over decades — that does not track information loss point for point. The variable that tracks the qualitative shift most closely is hippocampal involvement; the propositional content can persist in cortical traces with the phenomenal dimension already hollowed out. Two further faces sharpen the case: encoding requires conscious attention while consolidation runs without phenomenal accompaniment, and reconsolidation reshapes the trace at each conscious retrieval — propositional content survives across cycles, phenomenal character is reconstituted at each recall.

The unifying observation across three timescales: the phenomenal dimension of cognition operates semi-independently of its informational substrate. Source-monitoring failures detach felt pastness from retrieval machinery; fluency and effort signals detach felt knowing and felt cost from the operations they report; vivid re-experiencing erodes along its own trajectory while informational content persists in cortical traces. Different timescales, same dissociation signature.

## The Positive Side: When Phenomenology Couples to Causal Machinery

[Placebo and choking](/topics/empirical-phenomena-mental-causation/) complete the picture from the opposite direction. Where the dissociation cluster shows phenomenology decoupling from causal machinery in the failure cases, the empirical phenomena of mental causation show phenomenology *coupling* to causal machinery in the success cases. The two-sided structure is consistent with an interface architecture — though equally with any selective-access account that grants phenomenal access to representational outputs but not to all sub-personal processes, including higher-order theory, illusionism, and global workspace theory. The cluster's claim is cost-of-explanation rather than unique-prediction: each can absorb the shape, and the question is what additional structure each must posit.

Placebo demonstrates the positive direction. The propositional content of a belief — "this is a painkiller," "this should suppress growth hormone," "this should release growth hormone" — selects which physiological cascade fires; the same compound produces opposite hormonal effects depending on the verbal information attached (Benedetti et al. 2003). Open-label placebos qualify the propositional-content claim — patients who know the pills are inert still improve — relocating the work to felt engagement with a therapeutic ritual rather than dissolving it.

Choking demonstrates the converse. When consciousness intrudes on automatised skill, performance degrades precisely where self-focused monitoring is maximal. Beilock and Carr's explicit-monitoring theory tracks the mechanism: pressure → anxiety → self-focus → step-by-step monitoring → degraded execution. Negative evidence resists epiphenomenalist dismissal in a way positive evidence does not: confabulating interference is not available as a manoeuvre.

The conjunction registers *systematic* phenomenology-to-physiology coupling across substrates with different representational profiles — placebo's content-specificity selecting neurotransmitter systems across humoral and dopaminergic pathways, choking's mode-specificity selecting which skill is disrupted across motor-cortical and basal-ganglia loops. Together with the negative side, they describe an interface that sometimes couples and sometimes detaches in patterned ways that expose the decomposition of what normally feels unified.

## Seven Traditions on the Same Architecture

The cluster's evidential weight rests not on any single member but on the convergence of independent traditions, working with different premises and vocabularies, on the same diagnostic signature.

**Carruthers (2011) on cognition.** *The Opacity of Mind* argues that introspection of one's propositional attitudes is interpretive rather than direct: we infer our attitudes from sensory-format evidence much as we infer others'. The feeling of authoritative self-knowledge is a fluency-driven heuristic, not a window onto its own production.

**Trick and Pylyshyn (1994) on perception.** The FINST model gives the cardinality floor's diagnostic sharpest: visual indexing operates on a small pool of pre-attentive tokens whose allocation is invisible. Subitizing produces a unitary phenomenal output; the indexing operation has no phenomenal accompaniment.

**Koriat (2000) on fluency.** Metacognitive feelings that gate cognition — knowing, rightness, tip-of-the-tongue, confidence — are outputs of inferential heuristics that operate implicitly. The "Lima problem" sharpens the recursive face: epistemic feelings inform us about content we cannot independently access, so we cannot cross-check their accuracy except through more feelings of the same kind.

**Polanyi (1966) on tacit integration.** *The Tacit Dimension*'s "we know more than we can tell" names the same dissociation in the integrative-knowledge face. Skilled judgement and scientific intuition draw on subsidiary awareness whose contents do not become focal. Polanyi's "from-to" structure is a phenomenologist's vocabulary for output-while-operation-hidden.

**Inzlicht and Kurzban on effort.** The Kurzban-Duckworth-Kable-Myers (2013) opportunity-cost model and the Inzlicht-Schmeichel (2012) process model treat felt effort as the output of sub-personal cost-benefit machinery whose inputs the introspector cannot access. Westbrook and colleagues (2020) confirm one prediction: methylphenidate-induced dopamine elevation shifts willingness to engage demanding tasks, with the introspector noticing the result without noticing the manipulation.

**Bergson (1908) on memory.** "Le souvenir du présent et la fausse reconnaissance" proposes that memory and perception are formed simultaneously, with the memory-aspect of the present normally suppressed. Déjà vu becomes the brief surfacing of a normally-occluded shadow. The reducing-valve picture names the architecture: phenomenal access is filtered, and what reaches consciousness is a constructed product of what survives.

**Schwitzgebel on introspection.** *Perplexities of Consciousness* (2011) establishes that introspective reports of even basic phenomenal features (visual imagery, emotional experience, the felt character of perception) are unreliable in ways subjects cannot detect from inside. Not just propositional attitudes but the felt textures of experience are reported through machinery whose operation is hidden.

These seven do not share dualist commitments — Carruthers defends higher-order theory, Trick and Pylyshyn are cognitive scientists, Koriat is in metacognition, Polanyi a philosopher of science, Inzlicht and Kurzban evolutionary and cognitive psychology, Bergson a process philosopher, Schwitzgebel comfortable with reductive accounts. The convergence is on the *signature*, not on what it entails. Any single member admits domain-specific physicalist absorption (Carruthers's reading is functionalist, FINST is computational, Koriat's heuristics are sub-personal). A theory that absorbs each member separately must additionally explain why the same coupling — phenomenal verdict tracking sub-personal output, operation tracking nothing — appears across substrates with different representational profiles.

The seven divide into two sub-classes carrying different argumentative weight. Polanyi, Bergson, and parts of Koriat's programme observe the signature *despite* their broader commitments — Polanyi's subsidiary-focal structure does not require any opacity claim, Bergson's reducing-valve predates the cognitive science it resonates with, Koriat tracks heuristic outputs without committing to why introspection is interpretive. Carruthers, Trick and Pylyshyn, Inzlicht and Kurzban, and Schwitzgebel observe the signature *because* their materialist or computational models predict it. Evidential force concentrates in the first sub-class; the second supplies internal corroboration of the materialist prediction rather than independent evidence against it.

The convergence's force also depends on the traditions analysing substrates that do not share architectural limits, and the article should be honest about how thin that condition currently is. A common-cause hypothesis — *human introspection has the same architectural limits across all domains; those limits ARE the common machinery* — would predict the same convergence the seven-tradition count credits to independent triangulation. All seven analyse human cognition through human introspection in human language. What separates the cluster's claim from the common-cause null is recurrence of the signature *within* a single nervous system on radically different timescales: cardinality on parallel-individuation tokens at sub-second, memory consolidation on hippocampal-cortical transfer over decades. The two cannot share specific machinery beyond the involvement of a single conscious subject. Cross-species, cross-cultural, and patient-population evidence — the classes [the common-cause-null discipline](/project/common-cause-null/) catalogues — would distinguish independent triangulation from same-introspective-architecture-rediscovered. That evidence is not in hand; the strongest defence currently available is structural recurrence across substrates within a single nervous system.

## The Constrain-vs-Establish Discipline

[Evidential-status discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/) names the cluster's load-bearing methodological commitment: the dissociations *constrain* identity readings on which felt phenomenology reports transparently on its own underlying operation; they do not by themselves *establish* the dualist alternative.

The shape is symmetric. On the dualist side: the felt-effort/operation dissociation removes the simplest "felt effort just is what the operation feels like" identity reading, but leaves what felt effort *is* underdetermined between dualist (the felt sense doing real causal work the operation alone cannot) and process-model (sub-personally generated adaptive signal). On the materialist side: physical correlates for any phenomenal output — the temporal-lobe-epilepsy correlate of déjà vu, dopamine modulation of effort, neural signatures of recognition — remove the simplest "phenomenology is uncoupled from physics" reading without establishing identity. Each side's evidence is defeater-removal-against-the-other, not evidence-lift in its own favour.

The diagnostic test: when an article cites a dissociation, does the citation rest on removing a defeater the opposing view needs, or on establishing the citing view? The first is honest; the second is conflation.

What does evidence-lift is not any individual dissociation but cumulative convergence: same signature, independent vocabularies, substrates within a single nervous system, both directions of behaviour. Worked example: the mental-effort article's citation of the Naccache patient does defeater-removal against the strong identity claim locally; read cumulatively, it contributes one node to a convergence pressuring any account on which phenomenal verdicts transparently report on their operations.

## What the Cluster Establishes (and Doesn't)

The cluster establishes that phenomenal access systematically detaches from the operations it putatively reports on, in patterned ways, across substrates within a single nervous system with different representational profiles and timescales — perceptual cardinality at sub-second, memory consolidation across decades. The same dissociation is observed across seven traditions, with the convergence's force concentrated in the sub-class that observes the signature *despite* its broader commitments. The architecture has a positive companion: phenomenology *can* couple to causal machinery (placebo, choking), so the negative cases are a structural feature of an interface that sometimes couples and sometimes does not.

The cluster does not establish the dualist alternative. A sophisticated functionalist can absorb each member as functional architecture with privileged-access asymmetries; the cumulative convergence raises the explanatory cost without dissolving the option. The cluster's evidential weight is that materialism's "consciousness is just brain activity" has not yet pressed a unified account of why phenomenal output detaches systematically from operations with the architecture's symmetric two-sided shape — higher-order theory addresses introspective opacity directly, illusionism addresses the felt-character question directly, but neither has been pressed to address the architecture as a single shape with the cross-timescale recurrence the catalogue documents. The convergence does not refute physicalism from inside its framework; it raises the cost of physicalist absorption and supplies a structural picture the dualist tenets accommodate naturally.

## What Would Falsify the Cluster

A cluster whose evidence comes from convergence across heterogeneous substrates risks being indistinguishable from a category whose members do not share a real signature. Three candidates would weaken the framing.

**Clean lesion-dissociation of the four real-time members.** If targeted lesions or pharmacological dissociations could cleanly separate felt effort, felt agency, felt suspension, and felt knowing — each on independent substrates with no shared opacity profile — the convergence claim would weaken to four independent functions rather than a unified signature.

**Demonstrated transparent introspection of any one operation.** If contemplative training, neural intervention, or a verifiable phenomenological method produced stable, articulable access to the operation underlying any cluster member (the matching trace producing recognition, the executive engagement producing effort, the source-monitoring tag of a memory's source-attribution), the output-while-operation-hidden signature would lose its structural status. Verifiability conditions must distinguish reports of *content* normally suppressed (which the cluster absorbs as outputs-by-deformation) from reports of *operations* normally hidden — useful conditions include inter-rater agreement across trained observers, neural correlates tracking the reported operation rather than the represented output, and predictive contact with subsequent behaviour the introspector should not have access to under the opacity model. Husserlian phenomenology, open-monitoring meditation (*vipassana*, Dzogchen pointing-out, certain *satipatthana* practices), and the EASE clinical-phenomenology interview (Sass and Parnas) all claim something close to inspection-while-operating; the EASE has inter-rater calibration data. Whether its items satisfy the operations-not-content criterion is an empirical question the cluster does not pre-empt; the cluster owes these traditions a more careful engagement than its current treatment.

**Symmetric absence of the positive companion.** If placebo and choking turned out to be artefacts and the positive coupling cases vanished, the two-sided structure would collapse into a one-sided opacity claim and the interface-architecture reading would lose its predicted symmetry.

None is currently observed; that is what makes the framework usable. None is in principle excluded; that is what makes it falsifiable rather than merely descriptive.

## Relation to Site Perspective

The cluster bears most directly on two of the [five tenets](/tenets/).

**[Dualism](/tenets/#dualism)** is supported through the cluster's structural profile. Materialism must explain why phenomenal output detaches systematically from the operations it putatively reports on, why the detachment has the same diagnostic signature across substrates with different representational profiles within a single nervous system, and why the architecture has a positive companion in which phenomenology couples to causal machinery in matching detail. The dualist reading takes the dissociation as evidence that phenomenal access is a distinct ontological register from the underlying operations, and the coupling as evidence that the register does causal work when the interface is engaged. Neither direction establishes dualism in isolation; the cumulative convergence raises the explanatory cost on materialist absorption.

**[Occam's Razor Has Limits](/tenets/#occams-limits)** is engaged because the simplicity-preference that motivates "consciousness is just brain activity" is itself one of the cluster's members. Trust in parsimony is a fluency-and-coherence signal, gated by the same noetic-feelings machinery the cluster catalogues. The materialist's confidence that the simpler story is the right one cannot be neutrally adjudicated, because the adjudicator is the architecture whose reliability is at issue. The Map's commitment to limited parsimony under incomplete knowledge is a discipline for sustaining inquiry past the gate set by the felt sufficiency of the simpler account.

The other three tenets connect more cautiously. **[Bidirectional Interaction](/tenets/#bidirectional-interaction)** finds its empirical anchor in the positive companion (placebo and choking); the positive side names where the interface's causal work is most directly evident. **[Minimal Quantum Interaction](/tenets/#minimal-quantum-interaction)** is consistent with the cluster's interface-asymmetry profile but the cluster does not by itself argue for any particular mechanism. **[No Many Worlds](/tenets/#no-many-worlds)** is implicitly engaged whenever the analysis invokes "selection" or "felt collapse," but the cluster does not adjudicate single-world versus branching readings.

The unifying picture: phenomenal output and causal machinery are dissociable across timescales, substrates, and traditions; they couple in the success cases of mental causation and detach in patterned ways in the failure cases. The architecture has a shape — output-while-operation-hidden, dissociable fallibility, mode-fragility under inspection — that physicalism's currently dominant forms have addressed piecewise but not as the unified, symmetric two-sided architecture the convergence reveals. The cluster marks where reductive accounts owe an explanation beyond functional decomposition: not why each individual dissociation occurs, but why the architecture takes that symmetric form.
