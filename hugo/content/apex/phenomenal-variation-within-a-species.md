Two minds belong to the same biological species. They share evolutionary history, neural architecture, language community, and—on the standard behavioural measures—task performance. One looks at a page and sees the printed letter alone; the other sees the same letter and, simultaneously, an extra colour that has been there as long as memory reaches. One closes their eyes and conjures a vivid red apple; the other reports nothing at all, then completes the same mental rotation problem by analytic means. One feels their thoughts as marked with their own provenance; the other discovers, when the experiment is run, that they cannot tell self-generated content from primed content from confabulated reconstruction.

Same biological kind, same architecture at the level neuroscience carves, same task performance at the level behavioural science measures, different inner lives. Within-species phenomenal divergence is the empirically anchored shape that several decades of careful work have given to a question philosophers used to have to imagine: are there two minds, otherwise functionally equivalent, whose phenomenology nevertheless differs? The answer, by 2026, is yes. The Unfinishable Map's claim is that the cumulative evidence forms a wedge against the strong identity claim of [functionalism](/concepts/functionalism/) — phenomenal character is not the functional role in disguise — and that the wedge, while not a knockdown, becomes harder to dispatch as the cases multiply.

## The Structural Form

The argument has a single structural form, repeated across four domains:

> Two minds, the same biological species, the same neural architecture at the level the standard literature carves, the same task performance at the level behavioural science measures, with phenomenology that diverges.

Each of the four divergence classes catalogued below instantiates this form. None requires the inverted-qualia thought experiment, the philosophical zombie, or the cross-species Nagelian leap. The phenomenologies that diverge are reported by neighbours; the task performances that match are recorded under the same paradigms; the architectures that share are imaged by the same scanners. The methodological easements that come from inter-species or inter-system comparison are not available — the cases stand or fall on what humans say to other humans about what their inner lives contain.

The hardest functionalist response to any single divergence is to deny that the architectures are really matched at the relevant grain — to argue that the function is being individuated too coarsely and that finer grain restores covariance. The within-species frame makes that response work harder, because the architectures *are* shared at every level coarser than the one where the dispute concentrates. A finer-grained functionalism that absorbs the imagery, synaesthesia, source-attribution, and cognitive-phenomenology cases must say *which* grain does the work in each, and why phenomenology diverges *at any grain compatible with task-level functional identity*. The cluster is what makes the question hard to skip.

## The Four Divergence Classes

### Imagery Vividness: Aphantasia and Hyperphantasia

The first class is what the post-2010 [aphantasia](/topics/aphantasia/) literature has documented. Roughly 1% of the human population is extreme-aphantasic — voluntary phenomenal imagery is absent — and roughly 3% is extreme-hyperphantasic, with the remainder spread across a continuous spectrum of reported vividness ([Zeman 2024](https://www.cell.com/trends/cognitive-sciences/fulltext/S1364-6613\(24\)00034-2)). The naming is recent; the underlying observation is older. Galton's 1880 imagery survey of Royal Society fellows already found the spectrum, and the Würzburg School's "imageless thought" controversy ran the same dispute under different vocabulary between roughly 1900 and 1915 before the rise of behaviourism took the question off the table for a century. The shape recurs because the territory keeps producing it.

The wedge sits in the [function-phenomenology face](/voids/imagery-void/#the-function-phenomenology-face) of the data. Kay, Keogh, and Pearson (2024) found that aphantasic individuals complete classical mental rotation tasks "slower but more accurately" than visualising controls, with both groups exhibiting the standard linear relation between angular disparity and response time. Aphantasics use analytic, feature-based strategies; controls use object-based mental rotation. The authors conclude that "visual imagery is not crucial for successful performance in classical mental rotation tasks, as alternative strategies can be effectively utilised in the absence of holistic mental representations." Same task. Different — sometimes absent — phenomenology. The standard behavioural signature, intact.

This is the imagery class's contribution to the wedge. The phenomenology of imagery is reported by one cohort and denied by another, and the cognitive work the phenomenology is conventionally said to support gets done in both cohorts. Lennon (2023) develops the formal point: aphantasic individuals have phenomenally conscious thoughts with "no sensory reduction base", which "falsifies the sensory constraint on conscious thought." The finer-grain functionalist can reply that aphantasic and visualising minds perform subtly different functional operations on the same task — the strategy difference is precisely a function difference at the level multi-realizability tracks. The reply is principled. It also concedes, by the move it makes, that the *task-level* function is shared while phenomenology diverges. The question moves to grain rather than away from the dissociation.

### Synaesthetic Extra Qualia

The second class runs the wedge in the opposite direction. Where aphantasia removes phenomenal content the cognitive task is conventionally said to require, [synaesthesia](/topics/synaesthesia/) adds phenomenal content the surface representation does not require. Around 4% of the population have at least one form of synaesthesia (Simner et al. 2006); grapheme-colour and chromaesthesia are the most studied. The same letter on the same page produces the printed colour for everyone and, for the synaesthete, an additional involuntary colour at the same spatial location.

Fiona MacPherson's "extraordinary feature" (2007) is the philosophically loaded signature: a grapheme-colour synaesthete sees both the actual ink colour and the synaesthetic colour at the same place, without blending or competition — a configuration ordinary perception affords no analogue for, and ordinary colour vocabulary cannot accommodate. Alan Wager (1999, 2001) formalised the underlying problem as the *extra qualia problem*: synaesthetes possess phenomenal states that differ from non-synaesthetic states without any corresponding difference in surface representational content. J.A. Gray (2003) presses the point further, arguing that in synaesthesia "function and qualia come apart in two ways" — identical qualia arise from distinct functional roles (colour from visual *and* from grapheme-recognition pipelines), and identical functional roles produce divergent qualia (the same grapheme-recognition process generates colour for synaesthetes but not for non-synaesthetes).

The synaesthetic class is the architecturally cleanest exemplar of the [phenomenology-vs-function dissociation pattern](/concepts/phenomenology-vs-function-axis/) in the catalogue, because Gray's two patterns appear at once in the same phenomenon. The honest hedge survives: van Leeuwen, Singer, and Nikolić (2015) find that most synaesthetic experience is mediated through semantic processing, so synaesthete and non-synaesthete pipelines are not identical at the deeper grain that representationalists track. The Wager–Gray dissociation is a coarse-grain, surface-input claim, not an end-to-end pipeline-identity claim. The wedge survives at the surface representational level the within-species frame uses; the pipeline-identity question is exactly where the finer-grain absorption strategy gets pursued.

### Reality-Monitoring Divergence

The third class is less visible because it does not declare itself by colour or vividness. The [source-attribution void](/voids/source-attribution-void/) catalogues a structural finding: human cognitive content is not stored with native source tags. Origin is inferred at retrieval from features (vividness, fluency, narrative fit), and the inference fails in characteristic ways. *Reality monitoring* — the distinction between memories of internal events (imagined, dreamed, thought) and memories of external events (perceived, witnessed) — varies across the population in profile, calibration, and the conditions under which it collapses. Choice-blindness experiments (Johansson, Hall, Sikström & Olsson 2005) find that roughly three-quarters of manipulated trials go undetected across concurrent and retrospective measures combined; cryptomnesia studies (Brown & Murphy 1989) find systematic confusion of own-generation with prior-encounter; source-amnesia work (Schacter, Harbluk & McLachlan 1984) finds that for most of what we know we cannot recover how we came to know it.

The within-species point is that the spread of source-attribution profiles is itself a phenomenal-divergence pattern. Two individuals can complete the same memory or judgement task at matched accuracy while the *phenomenology of certainty* about the source diverges substantially across them and across conditions. One sits inside the experience of authorship for a content fragment; another reports source-amnesia for the same content; a third confabulates a source under a surface texture identical to the unmanipulated case. The phenomenology is graded; the task accuracy can be matched; the introspective report runs along an axis the standard behavioural measures do not separate. The standard finer-grain functionalist response is available — feature-similarity at retrieval is a function difference that *should* track the phenomenology — and works case by case while making the joint pattern harder to absorb.

### Cognitive Phenomenology

The fourth class lives in the comprehension domain. The [cognitive-phenomenology](/concepts/cognitive-phenomenology/) debate asks whether thinking itself has phenomenal character irreducible to sensory accompaniments — whether there is something it is like to grasp a meaning, follow an inference, or recognise a concept, over and above the inner speech, imagery, and emotional tone that often accompany these acts. Strawson's foreign-language case (1994) is the paradigm contrast: an English monolingual and a French speaker hear the same French sentence with identical acoustic input; the French speaker's experience differs because they understand. Tip-of-the-tongue states deliver meaning-without-form, with semantic content phenomenally present in the absence of phonological vehicle.

The within-species variation appears here with a different texture. Some people report rich, individuating cognitive phenomenology; others, with no impairment of cognition itself, report little beyond the sensory accompaniments. The aphantasic-thought case bridges the imagery class and the cognitive-phenomenology class: aphantasic individuals reason and understand without imagery, and report that the cognitive phenomenology is intact. The [comprehension-domain wedge](/topics/cognitive-phenomenology-and-the-irreducibility-of-thought/) takes the same structural form — same task, divergent phenomenology, whether vividly cognitive, sparsely sensory, or imageless-but-still-experiential.

The cognitive-phenomenology case is the most epistemically fragile of the four: the dialectic structurally cannot close from inside introspection-driven methods, and conservatives have a coherent re-description in which the phenomenal contrasts reduce to subtle sensory differences. The Map adopts qualified liberalism on abductive grounds rather than introspective certainty. What this class contributes to the wedge is breadth of domain — the dissociation pattern appears in the very fabric of rational life, where materialist programmes most need cognition to be safely functional.

## What the Wedge Does

The [phenomenal contrast method](/concepts/phenomenal-contrast-method/) is the methodological backbone the four classes share. Hold the input constant, vary the phenomenal report, and infer that something not exhausted by the input is doing work. The four within-species classes generalise the contrast method from the individual subject (where the contrast lives within one mind across two conditions) to the population (where the contrast lives across two minds presented with the same input or matched on the same task). The phenomenal contrast at population scale is itself the data point — different minds, same task, divergent phenomenology — and the *individual* contrast is what each member of the population uses to characterise their own side of the boundary.

The wedge is directed against a specific theoretical commitment: that phenomenal character is fixed by functional or representational role. Functionalism in the contemporary literature is multiply realisable by design (Putnam 1967; Block & Fodor 1972), and the multi-realizability resource is supposed to absorb cases of substrate variation while preserving function-phenomenology covariance. The within-species cluster constrains the absorption strategy. Each individual class is locally absorbable: the imagery case can be absorbed by individuating function at strategy-level grain; the synaesthesia case can be absorbed by appealing to representational difference at deeper pipeline grain; the source-attribution case by appealing to feature-similarity at retrieval; the cognitive-phenomenology case by re-describing reports as functional outputs of metacognitive subsystems. The joint package puts pressure on the parsimony of the absorption strategy: any successful absorption must work *across* the four classes by appealing to grain-shifts whose convergence on phenomenal-divergence-at-task-level-function-identity needs its own explanation.

## What the Wedge Does Not Do

The within-species cluster is an empirical wedge, not a knockdown. It registers as a pressure point against function-phenomenology identity of the kind a sufficiently subtle functionalism can absorb case by case. Four named absorption strategies are live in the literature.

**Multi-realizability at finer grain** absorbs the imagery case by individuating function at strategy-level grain: aphantasic and visualising minds run different strategies, and strategy is itself a functional kind. Pattern (b) of the [axis](/concepts/phenomenology-vs-function-axis/) dissolves at strategy-level grain; the wedge re-emerges when the question is pressed about why phenomenology should track strategy rather than task at the within-species grain. **Representational sub-individuation** absorbs synaesthetic extra qualia as higher-order representational differences in the grapheme-recognition pipeline; the van Leeuwen semantic-mediation finding supports the absorption, at the cost of specifying which level of representation is supposed to track phenomenology. **Metacognitive re-description** absorbs source-attribution and cognitive-phenomenology reports as functional outputs of metacognitive subsystems — "different report" need not entail "different phenomenal fact" — though the pressure is uneven, since the imagery case rests primarily on behavioural data. **Introspective unreliability** (Schwitzgebel 2008) dissolves the wedge from a different angle: if neither cohort's reports are reliable, the phenomenologies do not divergently track in the way the cohort labels suggest. The argument therefore rests on the partial-reliability of introspective report, which is itself a substantive philosophical commitment.

The Map registers all four absorption strategies as live and treats the wedge accordingly. The empirical findings — the spectrum, the mental-rotation result, the extra qualia, the choice-blindness phenomenology — are well established. The interpretive claim that these findings *jointly* press the function-phenomenology identity is a speculative-integration move: the convergence across four classes is what does the work, and the convergence has to be assessed at the joint level rather than dismantled case by case.

## Why the Joint Package Matters

The single-case responses to the within-species cluster are reasonable. Each moves the dispute to grain rather than dismissing the dissociation outright. What the cluster does is force a question the single cases did not have to answer: is there a *single* finer-grained functional individuation that absorbs imagery, synaesthesia, source-attribution, and cognitive-phenomenology together, or does the absorption require four different grain-shifts? If the latter, the absorption strategy is taking on theoretical commitments the simpler functionalism did not have to take on, and the parsimony advantage the original position claimed is corroded by the cumulative work the absorption now does.

This is the structural form of arguments that work by convergence rather than decisive case. The within-species cluster is empirically independent across its four classes — different methods, different literatures, different signature findings — and theoretically aligned, in that all four exhibit the same dissociation pattern between phenomenal report and the cognitive work the report is conventionally said to track. The cumulative-force claim has to absorb the report-versus-behaviour asymmetry across exemplars rather than wave it away. The wedge's load-bearing prediction is that absorption attempts will continue to succeed for individual cases — as multi-realizability plausibly already does for imagery — and continue to fail as a joint package, because no single grain-shift covers the four domains.

## Connection to the AI Question

The within-species frame has a direct bearing on the [machine question](/apex/machine-question/). The standard functionalist argument for machine consciousness runs through substrate-independence: if mental states are fixed by functional role, any system implementing the right roles instantiates the corresponding states, regardless of substrate. The within-species cluster pressures the antecedent. If two human minds sharing biological substrate at every level coarser than strategy can exhibit phenomenal divergence under matched task-level function, the inference from "matches at task-level function" to "matches in phenomenology" loses its force in the human case where it should be strongest. The [AI consciousness typology](/concepts/ai-consciousness-typology/) takes the consequence seriously: the question for an artificial system is not "does it implement the function?" but "what *kind* of consciousness, if any, does it support?" — and the answer cannot be read off task-level behavioural matching. The artificial case adds degrees of freedom to a dissociation pattern already visible within a single biological species.

## Relation to Site Perspective

The within-species cluster engages three of the Map's [foundational commitments](/tenets/) in different ways and at different evidential tiers.

**Dualism.** The cluster is the empirical pressure point closest to where [the Dualism tenet](/tenets/#dualism) predicts pressure should fall. If phenomenology has independent structure from the functional roles it accompanies, the within-species data are exactly the data such a framework predicts: same kind, same architecture, same task, divergent phenomenology. The cluster does not settle dualism — strict-covariance variants of property dualism (Leibnizian parallelism in the limiting case) are compatible with the same data, and a sufficiently subtle finer-grained functionalism can absorb each case individually. What the cluster offers is convergent empirical pressure on the strong identity claim, in a register the more abstract conceivability arguments (zombies, [inverted qualia](/concepts/inverted-qualia/), [Mary's room](/concepts/knowledge-argument/)) reach by imagination rather than by within-species observation. The empirical findings are well established; the interpretive claim that they jointly press function-phenomenology identity is a speculative integration the catalogue carries with its load-bearing structure visible.

**Occam's Razor Has Limits.** The simplest model of human phenomenal experience — that all members of one biological species share roughly one experiential palette, with individual variation only in vividness — is empirically inadequate. It took clinical case-study work, longitudinal-consistency rehabilitation, and the post-2010 imagery literature to crack the assumption open, and the [Occam-Limits tenet](/tenets/#occams-limits) flags exactly this kind of structural variation that parsimony would have ruled out *a priori*. The cluster is also an instance of the tenet's broader point: the absorption strategies that attempt to preserve function-phenomenology identity by sub-dividing functional roles are themselves loss-of-parsimony moves that the original position cannot afford to deny.

**Bidirectional Interaction.** The Map speculates — explicitly as speculation, not tenet-level commitment — that phenomenal *extras* (content not exhausted by functional or representational role) are candidate sites for [consciousness-physical interface](/tenets/#bidirectional-interaction) activity. The within-species cluster does not force this speculation, but it documents the within-species phenomenal-extras pattern that makes the speculation not absurd. The data alone are compatible with cross-activation under physicalism (Ramachandran & Hubbard's preferred reading); the interface reading earns its place from cluster-level convergence rather than from any single phenomenon in isolation.

The phenomenology that varies is real — that is the empirical anchor — and the function that does not vary is doing different work in different minds. The wedge is in the gap between those two claims, and the gap is what the catalogue points to when it asks where the standard identity claim runs out.
