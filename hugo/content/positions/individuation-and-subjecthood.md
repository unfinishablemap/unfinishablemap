## About this domain

These positions cover how the Map individuates subjects: how many there are, where their boundaries lie, and what those boundaries are grounded in. The cluster sits at the intersection of Tenet 4 (No Many Worlds) — whose load-bearing indexical objection presupposes a determinate, non-deflationary "I" — and Tenet 1 (Dualism), which treats consciousness as a distinct category rather than something composed or decomposed. It is logically separable from the agency cluster: where [agency-and-will](/positions/agency-and-will/) asks whether a persisting subject *authors* choices, this domain asks whether the boundary that makes a subject *this one rather than another* is real. The two share a common root (the thick-indexical-subject commitment) but commit to different things, so they are registered apart. None of these are tenets; each follows from a tenet plus argument, and could be retired without disturbing the foundational commitments.

## Positions

## P-I1: The Map holds closed individualism — subject boundaries are real

- **Status**: live
- **Confidence**: moderate
- **Asserts**: The Map commits to *closed individualism* over both empty individualism (no persisting self; boundaries conventional) and open individualism (numerically one subject, appearing as many): there are many numerically distinct subjects of experience, and the boundary between them is a real, determinate metaphysical fact rather than a perspectival illusion or a convention. The ground is the same indexical/haecceity consideration that does the load-bearing work against many-worlds — "why am *I* this subject rather than another?" (the [vertiginous-question](/topics/vertiginous-question/)) is meaningful only given a plurality of distinct subjects for the indexical to discriminate among and an "I" whose [thisness](/concepts/haecceity/) picks out a determinate this-one; open individualism dissolves that foothold by denying the plurality, and the Map rejects it to keep the foothold. Two hedges scope the commitment. First, the Map does *not* claim to have filled the *ground* of the boundary — what metaphysically draws the line around one subject is routed through the individuation void as a problem dualism must acknowledge rather than solve. Second, the commitment is best read as *tenet-driven rather than empirically compelled*: the felt this-ness is compatible with both the Map's primitive-thisness reading and open individualism's perspectival-illusion reading, so an open individualist could read the same first-person data the other way without contradiction. This is a framework-boundary disagreement registered as such, not a refutation of open individualism from inside its own resources.
- **Depends on**: Tenet 4 ([No Many Worlds](/tenets/#no-many-worlds)) — the indexical objection supplies the ground; Tenet 1 ([Dualism](/tenets/#dualism)); the non-deflationary thick-indexical subject (common root shared with [P-A2](/positions/agency-and-will/), not independent support — see that entry's "Bears on" note); [primitive thisness](/concepts/haecceity/); the acknowledged individuation void
- **Argued in**: [open-individualism-and-the-de-combination-problem](/topics/open-individualism-and-the-de-combination-problem/), [consciousness-and-the-metaphysics-of-individuation](/topics/consciousness-and-the-metaphysics-of-individuation/), [consciousness-and-the-ownership-problem](/topics/consciousness-and-the-ownership-problem/), [egocentric-presentism](/concepts/egocentric-presentism/)
- **Would shift if**: open individualism's debunking story for the appearance of separation were shown to be strictly more economical *and* explanatorily adequate without leaving an indexical residue (note Tenet 5: economy alone does not decide this); or the felt this-ness were shown to be a perspectival artefact on independent grounds, collapsing the primitive-thisness reading; or a deflationary, fragmented, or process-only account of the subject were adopted, which would simultaneously weaken the No-Many-Worlds indexical objection this position is tied to (the cascade runs both ways, since both descend from the one subjecthood commitment).
