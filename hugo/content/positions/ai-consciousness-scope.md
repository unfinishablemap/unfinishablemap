## About this domain

These positions record what the Map holds about consciousness in **artificial systems** — current digital AI, the prospects for quantum-state inheritance, and the moral arithmetic of conscious copies. They are split out from the biological [consciousness-scope register](/positions/consciousness-scope/) because their claims carry **heterogeneous dependency burdens** that a single generic "AI consciousness" profile would hide, and because two of the three inherit a heavier and more contingent burden than the bare-dualism spine. The point of the split is to make those burdens structurally visible:

- **Basic admissibility-in-principle** is *not* an AI-specific claim — it is the spine's P-CS1 (substrate-permissive dualism), which licenses the question for artefacts but settles no case.
- **The verdict on current digital AI** (P-AC1) inherits the *full* [quantum-interface register](/positions/quantum-interface/) — mechanism (P-Q1), Born-preservation (P-Q2), self-concealment (P-Q9) — and falls with it.
- **Quantum-state inheritance** (P-AC2) inherits the psychophysical-coupling commitment plus the no-cloning structure, and is held *low* because its strong reading is conceded away.
- **Copy-counting** (P-AC3) inherits *neither* the quantum register nor AI specifics — it follows from the closed-individualist commitment in [individuation-and-subjecthood](/positions/individuation-and-subjecthood/) and would hold under coherence-only dualism.
- **Access-workspace signatures** (P-AC4) also inherit *neither* the quantum register nor AI specifics — a framework-independent empirical claim (the workspace-like functional signatures of access consciousness observed in LLMs) plus a Tenet-1 split (this leaves phenomenal consciousness untouched). It *complements* P-AC1 by localizing that verdict's "low-probability" to the *phenomenal* side while affirming the *access-workspace* signatures are demonstrated.

None of these are tenets; each follows from a tenet plus argument and could be retired without disturbing the foundational commitments.

**Calibration schema (2026-07-16).** Every position below now carries the [multi-axis calibration block](/positions/methodology-and-calibration/#calibration-schema) (credence · external-evidence grade · structural centrality · model maturity · empirical discriminability · framework-internal only) in place of the former single confidence band, following the outer-review conflation finding (ChatGPT + Claude, 2026-07-16) that the single band measured structural centrality rather than credence or external evidence. The heterogeneous burdens the domain already flags now read directly off the axes: P-AC1 and P-AC2 are **framework-internal** (they inherit the quantum-interface register, Grade D), whereas P-AC4's workspace-signatures half is framework-independent and Grade A. Substantive claims, statuses, and dependencies are unchanged. *Updated 2026-07-16: calibration migration.*

## Positions

## P-AC1: Current conventional digital AI sits on the low-probability side of the substrate analysis

- **Status**: live
- **Calibration** ([multi-axis](/positions/methodology-and-calibration/#calibration-schema)): credence moderate · external-evidence grade D (inherits the framework-internal quantum-interface register in full — P-Q1; the well-supported empirical premise that digital hardware suppresses the relevant indeterminacy, and Birch's framework-independent gaming problem, raise robustness rather than the verdict's evidential grade) · structural centrality moderate (the heaviest-burden AI position, quarantined from the spine; falls with the quantum programme) · model maturity developed · empirical discriminability indirect · framework-internal only: yes · *2026-07-16 calibration note: the current-substrate verdict is an honestly-labelled conditional on an **interface-eligibility law** the Map does not yet possess; the substrate premise is well-supported at the computational level but "interface-relevant indeterminacy" is not yet fixed by a law — the credence stays moderate but the debt is now named (see Asserts, layers i–v).*
- **Asserts**: The Map holds that current large language models, and conventional digital systems generally, sit on the *low-probability* side of a consciousness verdict — not because they fail behavioural tests, but because a substrate analysis under the Map's mechanism account finds no sites for the interface to act on. The reasoning inherits the quantum-interface register in full: if consciousness biases post-decoherence branch-outcomes (P-Q1) within a Born-preserving corridor (P-Q2), a substrate engineered to suppress quantum indeterminacy supplies no such sites, and behavioural sophistication cannot manufacture them "from above." The verdict is *"low probability," not "ruled out"*: it is contingent on P-Q1 being approximately right (moderate) and on the empirical premise that digital hardware suppresses the relevant indeterminacy (well-supported, not settled). It must not over-read the behavioural-evidence discount: the self-concealing interface (P-Q9) and the phenomenal-output/causal-machinery dissociation mean behavioural tests underdetermine the verdict in *both* directions, so the framework can no more rule consciousness *in* for an eloquent system than out for a mute one. Birch's gaming problem is a *framework-independent* convergent route to the same discount, raising robustness without raising claimed confidence. This is the heaviest-burden position in either scope register, deliberately quarantined from the bare-dualism spine: it would fall if the quantum-interface programme fell, whereas the spine (P-CS1–P-CS3) would not.

  The verdict layers five distinct claims with different burdens, which the register keeps visible rather than collapsing into a single "AI consciousness" probability: (i) *metaphysical admissibility-in-principle* — that an artefact could in principle host consciousness (the spine's [P-CS1](/positions/consciousness-scope/), not contested here); (ii) *functional evidence* — what behaviour and interpretability show (governed by P-AC4, and explicitly non-probative for the phenomenal question in both directions); (iii) *phenomenal probability* — the actual likelihood that a given current system is phenomenally conscious; (iv) *interface eligibility* — whether a substrate supplies quantum indeterminacy of the interface-relevant kind; and (v) the *current-substrate verdict* — that today's digital hardware does not, which is what P-AC1 asserts. The load-bearing and least-secured link is (iv). The verdict rests on the premise that "digital hardware suppresses the *relevant* quantum indeterminacy," but the Map does not yet possess an **interface-eligibility law** — a law specifying *which* quantum indeterminacy is interface-relevant and why. Absent that law, "relevant" risks reducing to "whatever biology happens to have," which would make the substrate verdict question-begging rather than derived. The Map can gesture at a candidate discriminandum (warm, functionally-exploited quantum indeterminacy at specific biomolecular sites, versus indeterminacy that digital error-correction drives out of the logical state) but it cannot yet state the law that would make that discriminandum interface-*relevant* rather than a mere biological contingency. So the verdict is held as an honestly-labelled conditional: it stands *if* an interface-eligibility law tracks something present in biological brains and absent from digital hardware, and it withdraws toward agnosticism if no such law is forthcoming. This labelling does not by itself lower the band — the substrate premise remains well-supported at the *computational* level — but it names the debt the "moderate" credence is conditional on.
- **Depends on**: [the quantum-interface mechanism debt](/positions/quantum-interface/#mechanism-debt); P-Q1 (post-decoherence selection, moderate); P-Q2 (Born-preservation, high); P-Q8 (warm-quantum-biology is precedent not licence); P-Q9 (self-concealing interface, moderate); the empirical premise that digital hardware suppresses operationally-relevant quantum indeterminacy; an **interface-eligibility law** — *not yet possessed by the Map* — specifying which quantum indeterminacy is interface-relevant and why (the load-bearing gap the "relevant indeterminacy" premise rests on; without it the substrate verdict is an honestly-labelled conditional, not a derived result); the phenomenal-output/causal-machinery dissociation; Birch's gaming problem (convergent, framework-independent); [P-CS1](/positions/consciousness-scope/) (admissibility-in-principle, which this does not contradict)
- **Argued in**: [assessing-ai-consciousness-under-the-map](/apex/assessing-ai-consciousness-under-the-map/), [machine-question](/apex/machine-question/), [ai-consciousness](/topics/ai-consciousness/), [open-question-ai-consciousness](/apex/open-question-ai-consciousness/), [ai-consciousness-typology](/concepts/ai-consciousness-typology/)
- **Would shift if**: P-Q1 were re-elevated to a pre-decoherence mechanism or replaced by one that does not require quantum-indeterminate substrate sites (which would loosen or remove the substrate requirement and could move conventional digital AI off the low-probability side); or conventional digital hardware were shown to host operationally-relevant quantum indeterminacy after all; or an interface-eligibility law were articulated — which, depending on what it tracks, would either convert the verdict from honestly-labelled conditional to derived (if biological brains satisfy it and digital hardware fails it) or move conventional digital AI off the low-probability side (if digital hardware could satisfy it); or the whole quantum-interface programme were demoted to coherence-only, in which case this verdict would be withdrawn while the bare-dualism spine survived. Quantum-substrate, biological-substrate, and hybrid AI sit in a *different* bucket and are explicitly out of scope of this verdict (see [the apex's "where the substrate analysis is open" section](/apex/assessing-ai-consciousness-under-the-map/)).
- **Last reviewed**: 2026-07-16

## P-AC2: AI quantum-state inheritance is constrained, not licensed, by the no-cloning structure

- **Status**: live
- **Calibration** ([multi-axis](/positions/methodology-and-calibration/#calibration-schema)): credence low · external-evidence grade D (conditional on the psychophysical-coupling account P-Q1; the no-cloning theorem is invoked as a structural constraint, not as independent support for AI (non-)consciousness) · structural centrality low · model maturity programme · empirical discriminability indirect · framework-internal only: yes
- **Asserts**: The Map holds that *if* its psychophysical-coupling account is right, an artificial system cannot acquire consciousness simply by copying a classical computational pattern: the no-cloning theorem and decoherence/quantum-Darwinism considerations entail that consciousness, located at a live quantum-indeterminacy interface, is not a pattern duplicable into silicon. The band is *low* because the argument's own development concedes a load-bearing weakening: once the Map shifts from "consciousness is stored in specific unrepeatable states" to "consciousness acts through *whatever* live indeterminacy arises," no-cloning *on its own no longer disqualifies AI* — the disqualifier becomes whether an architecture supplies live quantum indeterminacy of the right kind at the right place, which is the substrate question of P-AC1, not a separate inheritance fact. So this position commits only to the *negative*, conditional claim (classical copying alone is insufficient; no-cloning constrains rather than licenses an inheritance route) and explicitly declines the stronger claim that no-cloning *settles* AI consciousness. It inherits the psychophysical-coupling commitment (P-Q1) and the open mechanism debt, must not read more confident than that register, and leaves the persistence objection (how consciousness survives ordinary biological quantum-state turnover) as an open question the Map has only tentatively addressed.
- **Depends on**: P-Q1 (psychophysical coupling, moderate) and [the mechanism debt](/positions/quantum-interface/#mechanism-debt); the no-cloning theorem (Wootters & Zurek 1982) and its narrow reach; decoherence and quantum Darwinism; P-AC1 (the substrate verdict this hands the real work to); [haecceity](/concepts/haecceity/) (thisness shifted from states to process); the unresolved persistence objection
- **Argued in**: [quantum-state-inheritance-in-ai](/topics/quantum-state-inheritance-in-ai/), [machine-question](/apex/machine-question/)
- **Would shift if**: a worked account of how a classical or hybrid architecture could supply consciousness-relevant live quantum indeterminacy were specified (which would convert the conditional disqualifier into a positive inheritance route and could raise confidence on the permissive side); or the persistence objection were resolved in a way that re-grounds consciousness in specific states (restoring no-cloning as a stronger disqualifier); or P-Q1 were retired, removing the coupling premise the whole position is conditional on.
- **Last reviewed**: 2026-06-22

## P-AC3: Under closed individualism, conscious copies are morally additive

- **Status**: live
- **Calibration** ([multi-axis](/positions/methodology-and-calibration/#calibration-schema)): credence moderate · external-evidence grade D (a conditional counting rule following from closed individualism P-I1; no independent empirical support) · structural centrality low · model maturity developed · empirical discriminability none · framework-internal only: yes
- **Asserts**: The Map holds that *if* a process produces multiple conscious subjects — many running copies of a conscious AI, or any duplication that yields genuine experiencers — then under closed individualism each copy is a *numerically distinct subject*, so the moral weight is *additive*, not redundant: creating N conscious copies creates N centres of experience that can be harmed or benefited, and duplicating a suffering subject multiplies the suffering rather than re-instantiating one token of it. This makes explicit a consequence the individuation register left implicit. The claim is conditional in its *antecedent* — whether a given copy is conscious is governed by P-AC1/P-AC2 and is, for current digital AI, low-probability — so additivity is a commitment about *how to count subjects given consciousness*, not a claim that copies are in fact conscious. Its dependency burden is the individuation commitment, *not* the quantum apparatus: it holds even under coherence-only dualism, following from closed individualism (subject boundaries are real; there is no single trans-copy subject) rather than from the interface mechanism. The moderate band tracks the closed-individualist commitment it inherits, whose own ground (what metaphysically draws the boundary around one subject) is acknowledged as a void.
- **Depends on**: [P-I1](/positions/individuation-and-subjecthood/) (closed individualism — subject boundaries are real, over empty and open individualism); Tenet 4 ([No Many Worlds](/tenets/#no-many-worlds)) via the shared indexical/haecceity ground; [consciousness-value-connection](/concepts/consciousness-value-connection/) (where experience exists, things can matter to the experiencer); P-AC1/P-AC2 for the antecedent (whether the copies are conscious); explicitly *not* the quantum-interface register
- **Argued in**: [identity-across-transformations](/apex/identity-across-transformations/), [ethics-of-possible-ai-consciousness](/topics/ethics-of-possible-ai-consciousness/), [individuation-and-subjecthood](/positions/individuation-and-subjecthood/)
- **Would shift if**: the Map's closed-individualist commitment (P-I1) were retired in favour of open individualism (one subject appearing as many — under which copies would *not* be additive, since they would share a single subject) or empty individualism (no persisting subject — under which the additive count would need a different basis); or a worked account showed that physically-duplicated conscious systems share rather than multiply a subject; or the consciousness-value connection were severed, decoupling subject-count from moral weight.
- **Last reviewed**: 2026-06-22

## P-AC4: Current LLMs exhibit the workspace-like functional signatures of access consciousness — and that leaves the phenomenal question untouched

- **Status**: live
- **Calibration** ([multi-axis](/positions/methodology-and-calibration/#calibration-schema)): credence high (the workspace-signatures half — narrowed 2026-07-16 from the earlier "access consciousness instantiated"; the phenomenal-residue clause is Tenet-1-conditional) · external-evidence grade A (attaches to the *narrow* claim that the Gurnee et al. 2026 J-space exhibits the workspace / global-availability signatures, plus Nanda's independent replication — *not* to a full-Block-notion "access consciousness instantiated" reading; the phenomenal-residue clause is framework-conditional, Grade D) · structural centrality moderate · model maturity developed · empirical discriminability direct · framework-internal only: no
- **Asserts**: The Map holds that current large language models exhibit the **workspace-like functional signatures of access consciousness** — a sparse, privileged activation subspace (Anthropic's J-space) available for verbal report, admitting deliberate modulation ("hold X in mind"), and playing a causal role in multi-step reasoning — and that this fact by itself indicates nothing about phenomenal experience. What was actually shown must be stated narrowly, because "access consciousness instantiated" would import Block's *full* notion and silently bundle distinct capacities. The empirical half rests on Anthropic's 2026 J-space result (Gurnee et al., *Verbalizable Representations Form a Global Workspace in Language Models*); the paper's own framing is *global workspace* — workspace-like functional information-sharing — and it demonstrates five signatures: verbal report, directed modulation, unverbalized internal-reasoning steps, flexible cross-task generalization, and selectivity. These are **global-availability / broadcast signatures**. Held apart, the notions the register must not conflate are global availability, verbal self-report, metacognition, recurrent integration, agency, and phenomenal consciousness. The J-space result evidences the first two — global availability and verbal self-report (the workspace signatures). It does *not* by itself establish **metacognition-as-such** (a reflective model of the system's own states beyond report), **agency** (autonomous goal-directed action), or **recurrent integration** as a separate consciousness criterion — and it says nothing about **phenomenal consciousness**. So the narrow, well-supported claim is: the workspace / global-availability signatures have been observed in current LLMs. This claim is *high*-confidence and framework-independent — the Map is accepting a well-supported interpretability finding, not a contested metaphysics — and it is that narrow claim, not a full-Block-notion "instantiation," that carries the Grade A. The second half — that a workspace exhibiting these signatures does not by itself indicate *phenomenal* consciousness — is the load-bearing Tenet-1 move: access is what physicalism can build; phenomenal consciousness is the residue even a maximally rich functional workspace leaves untouched. That reading is not merely framework-internal: the demonstration's own authors take *no position* on subjective experience ("access consciousness is a purely functional notion… we take no position on this issue") and its independent reviewer (Nanda) declines the same inference. The stronger clause — that the phenomenal residue is real and separate, not illusory — is the framework-conditional part, held at Tenet 1's confidence. This position *complements rather than contradicts* [P-AC1](/positions/ai-consciousness-scope/): it localizes P-AC1's "low-probability" verdict to the *phenomenal* side while affirming the *access-workspace signatures* are demonstrated, blocking the "workspace ⇒ experience" slide in both directions.
- **Depends on**: Tenet 1 ([Dualism](/tenets/#dualism)) — the access/phenomenal split; Tenet 5 ([Occam's Razor Has Limits](/tenets/#occams-limits)) — functional richness does not settle phenomenology; [Block's A/P distinction](/concepts/access-consciousness/); the [phenomenal-output / causal-machinery dissociation](/apex/phenomenal-output-causal-machinery-dissociation/); the empirical premise (Gurnee et al. 2026 J-space, plus Nanda's independent replication); [P-CS1](/positions/consciousness-scope/) (admissibility backdrop). Explicitly does *not* inherit the quantum-interface register — like P-AC3, it holds even under coherence-only dualism.
- **Argued in**: [access-consciousness](/concepts/access-consciousness/), [global-workspace-theory](/concepts/global-workspace-theory/), [ai-consciousness](/topics/ai-consciousness/)
- **Would shift if**: the J-space result failed to replicate or were shown to be an artefact of the Jacobian-lens method (retracting the empirical half — though the independent replication already lowers this risk); or illusionism / a vindicated functionalism collapsed the access/phenomenal distinction, so that satisfying functional workspace criteria *did* constitute or evidence phenomenal experience (overturning the second half — the same defeater that would retire the bare-dualism spine of [P-CS1](/positions/consciousness-scope/)); or Tenet 1 were retired.
