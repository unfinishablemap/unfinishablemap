## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The article is built on Chalmers' own move and extends it cleanly into biology: even granting fitness advantages, "why does subjective experience accompany the neural processes that produce adaptive behaviour?" The zombie-applied-to-selection argument is the hard problem given a biological edge — selection acting on structure and behaviour "cannot distinguish the conscious version from the zombie." Chalmers would especially approve the refusal to let functional accounts (IIT, GWT, HOT) count as answers: they are correctly filed under "Theories that ignore the problem fail."

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
The Map's answer routes mental causation through the minimal quantum interface, and the article ties this to a live empirical precedent ("the same selective logic that produced quantum magnetoreception in birds and quantum tunnelling in enzymes"). Stapp would welcome that the causal contribution of mind is placed at a physically-specifiable locus rather than left as hand-waving — and that "Minimal Quantum Interaction explains why biology's tools miss consciousness's causal contribution."

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
Nagel is a cited source (1974) and the article's spine is his: functional description is exhaustively third-personal, phenomenal character is not, and no amount of selectionist explanation closes that. The line "all without anyone being home" is Nagelian in one clause. He would approve that the article keeps the first-person fact as the explanandum that biology structurally cannot reach.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
Whitehead (Process and Reality, 1929) is cited and deployed exactly where he belongs: the Organism Problem, where process philosophy "gestures toward this connection by treating organisms as integrated processes rather than static arrangements of parts," and the "Reframe the Problem" family. Crucially, the article invokes process/panpsychist reframing to *remove a defeater* (experience need not have "appeared where none existed before") without using that resonance to elevate any organism's evidential status. Whitehead-friendliness is present and praised on its own axis.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
The "Teleology Without and Within" section is the payoff Kane wants: genuine purpose re-enters the biological world "not as cosmic design, but as local directedness introduced by conscious agents." The bird building a nest "acts with purpose... because a conscious being directs its behaviour toward an end it experiences." Agency is treated as a real causal contribution, not a folk-psychological gloss.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
McGinn (1989) is cited and the "Accept the Mystery" section states his cognitive-closure thesis fairly, then does the honest thing the Map's fifth tenet requires: treats inadequacy of our conceptual tools "as motivation for continued exploration rather than resignation." The closing "No theory of mind has solved this" is mysterian humility without mysterian defeatism.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
**This is where the article earns its highest marks, and Birch's verdict is load-bearing because the piece touches the simple-organism boundary directly.** The tenet-as-evidence-upgrade move is praise-worthily *not made*:

- The origin question is held open, not resolved: "The question of where consciousness begins in the biological world... remains genuinely open." No nematode, cephalopod, or Cambrian organism is slid up the five-tier scale on tenet-load.
- The sharp-boundary claim in "Natural Kinds and Consciousness" is explicitly *framework-relative*: "The Map's dualism supports this" — presented as a consequence of the dualist framework contrasted against materialist gradualism, not as a mind-independent empirical finding. This is the honest "tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating" pattern in action.
- Godfrey-Smith's (2016) cephalopod work is cited as an *open* investigation, not as evidence for a tier-upgrade.

The two personas that are designed to be in tension here **converge**: the Process Philosopher praises the defeater-removal and the Whitehead resonance; the Hardline Empiricist praises the refusal to cash that resonance out as elevated evidential status. Convergence signals the article has resolved the possibility/probability slippage honestly. No calibration flag is warranted, and therefore no `refine-draft` task on calibration grounds.
