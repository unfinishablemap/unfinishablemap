## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)

The post-decoherence selection cluster addresses Chalmers' persistent worry that quantum consciousness proposals are too vague to evaluate. By locating consciousness's causal role specifically in the transition from improper to proper mixtures—after environmental decoherence selects a preferred basis but before any particular outcome becomes actual—the Map gives the explanatory gap a precise physical address. The apex article "Post-Decoherence Selection Programme" does what Chalmers has long demanded: it distinguishes the Map's proposal from both Copenhagen (consciousness triggers collapse) and Penrose (gravity triggers collapse) by identifying a narrower, more defensible insertion point.

The deep-reviewed quantum-darwinism-and-consciousness article would particularly satisfy Chalmers. Zurek's framework explains why we see position rather than momentum (basis selection via decoherence) but explicitly does not explain why *this* outcome rather than *that*. The article's honest acknowledgment that the improper/proper mixture distinction is contested—rather than treating it as settled physics—shows exactly the dialectical precision Chalmers values.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)

Stapp would find the stochastic-amplification-and-neural-selection article vindicating. The three-stage amplification chain (ion-channel stochasticity → synaptic competition → network-level selection) provides the physical mechanism Stapp's framework needs but has never fully articulated. The connection to neural criticality research (Beggs & Plenz on neuronal avalanches) gives the amplification claim empirical anchoring rather than leaving it as a theoretical possibility.

The contextual-selection-in-quantum-foundations article connects the Kochen-Specker theorem to consciousness in a way mainstream quantum consciousness literature has largely overlooked. Measurement contextuality—that outcomes depend on what else is simultaneously measured—maps naturally onto the context-dependence of conscious attention. Stapp would recognise this as supporting his contention that quantum mechanics already has the formal structure to accommodate mental causation.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)

The attention-and-the-consciousness-interface article, freshly refined with recalibrated evidence, exemplifies what Nagel wants: phenomenology treated as constraining data rather than decorative illustration. The wandering-noticing-returning cycle in sustained attention is not merely described but used as an argument—the fact that we *notice* attention has wandered shows consciousness monitoring its own interface operations. This self-transparency is precisely the feature that resists third-person reduction.

The evaluative-qualia article's cross-traditional convergence (Buddhist vedanā, Confucian moral phenomenology, Islamic knowledge-by-presence, Western value realism) provides Nagel with evidence that phenomenal character has structural features discoverable across different conceptual frameworks. The convergence is too specific for generic cognitive bias—independently trained traditions identifying the same evaluative dimension of experience suggests tracking something real.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)

The stochastic amplification framework embodies Whiteheadian becoming more than any previous Map article. Each stage of the amplification chain—ion channel opening, synaptic vesicle release, network-level competition—is a process of creative synthesis where multiple potentialities converge into one actual outcome. The framework doesn't impose consciousness from outside; it shows where consciousness's selective influence would naturally participate in processes already characterised by underdetermination.

The deep-reviewed articles' evidence recalibration demonstrates what Whitehead called "the fallacy of misplaced concreteness" in reverse—the Map is *removing* unwarranted concreteness from its empirical claims, allowing the philosophical framework to stand on properly qualified evidence. Downgrading Schwartz OCD from "strongest clinical evidence" to "suggestive clinical evidence" is not weakening the position; it's achieving the proportionality between evidence and claim that Whitehead demanded.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)

The post-decoherence selection framework gives Kane precisely what he needs: a physical locus where genuine choice operates without violating physical law. Post-decoherence selection is neither deterministic (the outcome is underdetermined by prior physical state) nor random (consciousness provides reasons-responsive selection). Kane's requirement that agents be "ultimate sources" of their actions maps onto the framework's claim that consciousness selects from physically underdetermined options—the selection is neither caused by prior physical events nor arbitrary.

The stochastic amplification chain addresses Kane's scalability concern: how does a quantum-level selection produce a macroscopic decision? The three-stage chain shows that neural architecture is structured to amplify tiny biases into behavioural outcomes—this is not an ad hoc assumption but an independently verified feature of critical-state neural dynamics.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)

The specification gap—acknowledged throughout the post-decoherence cluster—is exactly what McGinn expects. The Map can identify where consciousness acts (post-decoherence, pre-outcome), what constraints it must satisfy (Born rule preservation, no signalling, conservation laws), and what physical mechanisms amplify its influence (stochastic resonance, synaptic competition, neural criticality). What it cannot yet specify is *how* particular conscious states map to particular physical selections. This is cognitive closure made precise: we can draw the outline of the answer without filling it in.

The contextual-selection article's connection between Kochen-Specker contextuality and conscious attention adds a formally precise dimension to McGinn's mysterianism. If measurement outcomes are inherently context-dependent, and consciousness provides the relevant context, then the specification problem may be not merely difficult but structurally irreducible—the coupling function may resist explicit formulation because contextuality is not decomposable into context-independent components.
