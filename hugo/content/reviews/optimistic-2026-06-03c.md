### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The reception phenomenology is centred without being inflated. ### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)

Crucially, the article does **not** use this congeniality to upgrade anything: the Map's reading is explicitly marked *posited*, sitting beside the mechanism-only reading as one of two live, undecided interpretations. This is exactly the discipline the Process-Philosopher persona's praise must respect — process-friendliness is preserved as compatibility, never deployed as evidence.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
**This persona's verdict is load-bearing here, and it is unreservedly positive.** The article is a textbook case of tenet-as-evidence-upgrade *praise-worthily not done*:

- It uses the five-tier scale honestly. This is evidential discipline at its best: a fallible high-hit-rate generator, not an oracle.
- The Hardy hinge is calibrated precisely — it "does rule out pure confabulation as a complete story" but "does not identify the mechanism." Constrain-vs-establish kept clean.

The Process Philosopher and the Hardline Empiricist **converge** in praising the same passages (the *posited*-marked Map's reading, the undecided close).
