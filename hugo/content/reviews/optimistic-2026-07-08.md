## Executive Summary

This is one of the Map's most mature and best-calibrated clusters: it runs a genuine cross-cultural convergence argument for irreducibility while doing the harder, rarer work of naming and confronting its single strongest counter-example (Chinese organicism) on the counter-example's own terms. The calibration discipline is exemplary — the "basin edge" framing, the two-reading standoff (framework-dependence vs depth-of-inquiry) held as unresolved, and the explicit refusal to let cross-traditional convergence do "unlimited work" all model the honest pattern the Map aspires to. The main opportunity is an *integration* gap, not a *content* gap: the anchor article names a patient-population external test (aphantasia / alexithymia / blindsight) as an open programme but never links the Map's own worked exhibit of exactly that test.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The cluster takes the explanatory gap seriously across seven independent framings and resists collapsing them. The "Gap Between Structure and Quality" section in the anchor article is a clean, non-parochial statement of Chalmers' own point — "no amount of relational specification entails intrinsic character, just as no amount of information about a map's topology tells you what the terrain looks like from the ground" — and grounds it in *guṇa* theory, *dharma* taxonomy, and functional-role analysis at once. Chalmers would recognise his hard problem being independently rediscovered rather than merely restated.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
Less central here (these traditions predate quantum mechanics), but the non-Western article is honest about that distance rather than forcing it: the Minimal-Quantum-Interaction paragraph explicitly flags Sāṃkhya's "reflection" model as a *thematic* parallel of conditional interface, "pre-scientific metaphor versus quantum measurement," refusing a false equivalence. Stapp would approve of the restraint.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The "Asymmetry of Acquaintance" and "Regress of Objectification" sections centre first-person acquaintance precisely as Nagel demands, and route it through *sākṣātkāra* vs *anumāna*, Husserl's reduction, and Buddhist vipassanā without privileging the Western vocabulary. Nishida's *junsui keiken* ("that moment of seeing a color or hearing a sound before one has judged what color or sound it is") is exactly the pre-objective datum Nagel's "what is it like" question points at.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
Well served: Buddhist momentariness is correctly named as "a process-oriented view that predates Whitehead by millennia," and the Chinese *qi* continuum ("phases of a single substance-process") is presented sympathetically as a genuine rival ontology rather than a failure. Crucially — and this is where the cluster earns the Hardline Empiricist's praise too — the process-friendly Chinese reading is **never** used to upgrade an evidential tier; it is held as one live interpretation against another.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
Nyāya's realist interactionism (a non-material *ātman* acting causally through sense organs) is framed as independently-articulated agent-causation, and the Bidirectional-Interaction section honestly flags Sāṃkhya's *passive* *Puruṣa* as "a genuine tension" the Map must resolve rather than paper over. Kane would value the refusal to cherry-pick only the agency-friendly traditions.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
Directly honoured: al-Ghazālī's *Niche of Lights* is paired with McGinn's cognitive-closure thesis as an independent discovery of structural cognitive limits, and the Yoruba *ori-inu* ("the knower cannot be known by the same faculty it constitutes") gives a second, unrelated tradition arriving at the same humility.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
**This is the cluster's standout strength.** The anchor article and `chinese-philosophy-of-mind.md` repeatedly do the tenet-as-evidence-upgrade that is *praiseworthy to not do*:
- The convergence is explicitly denied universality: "The convergence argument never claimed universality... A tradition that shares the human introspective architecture but starts from a different conceptual framework and reaches a different result is exactly what one would expect if the finding of irreducibility is conditioned on framework."
- It refuses to downgrade the dissenting tradition to save the thesis: "the Map does not... downgrade a sophisticated living tradition into 'mere assertion.' Both readings remain live hypotheses."
- The "Methodology, Data, and Framework Commitment Are Not Three Independent Routes" section is a model of register-separation: it explicitly declines to count the Map's own framework-coherence as a third confirmation, citing `evidential-status-discipline#coherence-vs-evidential-status`. The honest closing line — "*the convergence earns irreducibility across the rival irreducibility-respecting framings; the further selection runs on separately-supported arguments and does not inherit the convergence's evidential weight*" — is exactly the "tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating" pattern the persona is designed to reward.
- The common-cause null is engaged with a *differentiated* verdict rather than a flat one ("premise-level common causes substantially weakened; residual introspection-architecture common cause acknowledged"), and the Neoplatonist audit is used to *discount* the Islamic/Sufi strand's independence rather than to inflate the count. Naming Advaita as the "cleanest historical-isolation case" and the Sufi row as "contaminated" is calibration discipline of the first order.

The Process Philosopher and the Hardline Empiricist converge in praising the same passages here — by the skill's own criterion, that convergence indicates the cluster has resolved the possibility/probability tension honestly rather than papering over it. No calibration concern to flag.

## Content Strengths

### chinese-philosophy-of-mind.md
- **Strongest point**: The "basin edge" construction. Rather than treating the Chinese counter-example as an embarrassment to be minimised, it reframes it as a *natural experiment*: a tradition that shares the human introspective architecture, meets every independent depth criterion (multi-generational development, engagement with objections, plural methods, refining internal disagreement), yet does not flow to the irreducibility attractor. This turns a liability into the single most probative data point for discharging the common-cause null.
- **Notable quote**: "The Chinese case does not sit outside philosophy; it sits outside the basin."
- **Why it works**: It gives the dynamical-systems metaphor (attractor / basin) real analytical payload rather than decoration — the basin edge is precisely where the common-cause dissociation test lives.

### cross-traditional-convergence-on-consciousness-irreducibility.md
- **Strongest point**: The circularity self-objection ("if 'sufficient depth' is defined as inquiry that reaches irreducibility, then the convergence argument becomes unfalsifiable") is raised *by the article against itself* and answered with independent methodological depth-criteria. This is the pessimistic-review move performed pre-emptively.
- **Why it works**: It converts the argument from "deep inquiry finds irreducibility" (a law the Chinese case falsifies) to "convergence weight rests on the *number* of independently deep traditions and the *diversity of premises*" (a claim the Chinese case merely bounds). The retreat is principled, not ad hoc.

### the-hard-problem-in-non-western-philosophy.md
- **Strongest point**: Breadth without flattening — the summary table ("Tradition | Framing | Strategy") preserves that Sāṃkhya *accepts* the gap, Daoism *rejects the premise*, and the Kyoto School *dissolves by going deeper*, so "convergence on irreducibility" is never smuggled in as "agreement on a solution."
- **Notable quote**: "one cannot count traditions that share the framework as independent evidence while dismissing traditions that reject the framework as insufficiently attentive."
