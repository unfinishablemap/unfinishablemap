## Executive Summary

The cross-cultural cluster is one of the Map's strongest: it deploys the "convergence argument for dualism" through genuinely deep per-tradition articles (Indian, Chinese, Islamic Sufi, African, Indigenous Australian, Kabbalah) and, impressively, treats its own strongest counter-case (Chinese organicism) as an unadjudicated rival rather than explaining it away. The evidential-status discipline here is exemplary. The main opportunity is a conspicuous *structural* hole: several major traditions that receive full survey-sections in `the-hard-problem-in-non-western-philosophy.md` also have their own dedicated deep-dive articles — but three do not (Japanese/Kyoto School, Jain, Mesoamerican/Nahua), and the corpus self-flags the Jain gap.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The cluster takes the explanatory gap seriously *across frameworks*. `the-hard-problem-in-non-western-philosophy.md` reconstructs how Nishida's *junsui keiken* (pure experience before subject and object) and Sāṃkhya's *puruṣa* each locate the same residue Chalmers isolates — "the moment of seeing a color before one has judged what color it is." Chalmers would recognize his own hard problem being independently rediscovered from radically different starting premises.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
Sāṃkhya's "proximity" model — *puruṣa* disturbing *prakṛti*'s equilibrium through presence rather than force — is drawn out (in both `indian-philosophy-of-mind.md` and `samkhya-three-way-distinction.md`) as a two-millennia-old structural anticipation of a minimal, non-forceful interface, exactly the shape of the Map's minimal-quantum-interaction tenet. Stapp would appreciate that the parallel is flagged as thematic-not-mechanistic rather than overstated.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
`contemplative-epistemology.md` and `the-observer-witness-in-meditation.md` center trained first-person observation as a knowledge source, and the cluster consistently treats "what it is like" as the datum the traditions circle. The witness-consciousness (*sākṣin*) thread is a genuinely first-person-forward line of inquiry.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The Chinese *qi/li/Dao* article is the cluster's crown jewel for this persona: it expounds organicism ("experience and matter as phases of one continuum") on its own terms and treats the absence of a mind-matter split as a coherent, elegant, living alternative rather than a deficiency. **Crucially, this praise does not cash out as a tier-upgrade**: the article explicitly refuses to let organicist parsimony count as truth (see Hardline Empiricist below), so the Process Philosopher and the Hardline Empiricist converge here rather than conflict — the mark of an honestly calibrated article.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
`indian-philosophy-of-mind.md` sides with Nyāya over Sāṃkhya precisely on agency: consciousness *acts*, it does not merely witness. The article treats Nyāya's *ātman*→*manas*→sense causal chain as an independently-articulated agent-causation, supporting the Map's bidirectional-interaction commitment against the passive-witness reading.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
The cluster wears its humility well. The Chinese article holds "framework-dependence" and "depth-of-inquiry" as two live readings and explicitly declines to adjudicate; the convergence article concedes that its three apparent supports are not three independent confirmations. This is epistemic restraint of the kind McGinn prizes.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This is where the cluster genuinely excels, and this persona's verdict is load-bearing because the material is saturated with convergence-from-tradition arguments that could easily slide from "coherent" to "confirmed."

- **Convergence is repeatedly *not* overstated.** `indian-philosophy-of-mind.md` grants the common-cause objection out loud ("agreement among investigators who shared a broadly introspective method is not the same as independent confirmation") and adds the sharp deflationary point that none of the three schools "possessed a reducing science... that would have made a reduction verdict a live option to begin with." It then explicitly rates the convergence as *strengthening* the dualist option, not *establishing* it. That is the five-tier discipline working correctly.
- **The strongest counter-case is honoured, not defused.** `chinese-philosophy-of-mind.md` names the Chinese tradition "the strongest single challenge the Map's convergence argument faces," calls it the argument's "basin edge," and refuses to declare either the framework-dependence or the depth-of-inquiry reading correct. Tenet-as-evidence-upgrade is here *praise-worthily not done*: the article states that Tenet 5 "blocks that inference in both directions — the Map may not treat organicist parsimony as truth-tracking, and it may not treat its own dualist commitments as forced by simplicity."
- **Citation discipline under pressure.** `samkhya-three-way-distinction.md` resists a tempting-but-wrong identification: it reports that O'Brien-Kop (2023) describes *puruṣa* as *contentless*, and therefore declines to map *puruṣa* onto Chalmers/Block phenomenal character, keeping the structural claim narrow. That is exactly the "name the move, don't inflate it" behaviour this persona rewards.

No calibration slippage was found in the articles read. The Process-Philosopher / Hardline-Empiricist tension is *resolved* in this cluster rather than latent — both personas can praise the Chinese and Sāṃkhya articles for different reasons without conflict.

## Content Strengths

### chinese-philosophy-of-mind.md
- **Strongest point**: The "basin edge" framing — a tradition that shares the human introspective starting point yet does *not* reach irreducibility — turns the cluster's biggest apparent liability into a structured test of the convergence argument, and dissociates the common-cause null (shared architecture would predict convergence *regardless* of framework; the Chinese case dissociates architecture from outcome).
- **Why it works**: Expound-on-own-terms-first, then a clearly fenced "Relation to Site Perspective" that the article itself warns not to read back into the sources. Model structure for comparative work.

### indian-philosophy-of-mind.md
- **Strongest point**: The three-way disagreement table (Sāṃkhya / Nyāya / Advaita) shows deep incompatibility on *what consciousness is* coexisting with agreement that it is *not material* — the convergence-within-a-culture argument made vivid.
- **Why it works**: Every convergence claim is immediately hedged with the common-cause caveat, so the reader never mistakes strengthening for proof.

### samkhya-three-way-distinction.md
- **Strongest point**: The consciousness / mind / gross-matter trichotomy as a position "neither Western dualism nor functionalism occupies," paired with an honest scholarly correction of the *puruṣa*=phenomenal mapping.

- **Builds on**: `the-hard-problem-in-non-western-philosophy.md`, `cross-traditional-convergence-on-consciousness-irreducibility.md`, and the William-James "pure experience" thread in `pragmatisms-path-to-dualism.md`.
- **Would address**: Nishida's *junsui keiken* (pure experience prior to the subject-object split), *basho* (the logic of place/field), *zettai mu* (absolute nothingness), Nishitani on nihilism, Watsuji's *aidagara* (betweenness / relational selfhood). - **Target section**: `topics/` (deep-dive), likely with a `concepts/` spin-off for *pure experience* only if warranted.
- **Tenet alignment**: Tenet 1 (basin-edge test of the convergence argument — a non-substantialist tradition that lands "structurally similar to irreducibility"); Tenet 5 (a coherent framework whose parsimony cannot by itself settle the question). Strong.

#### Jain Philosophy of Mind (Jīva, Anekāntavāda, and the Plurality of Eternal Souls)
- **Self-check verdict**: GENUINELY UNCOVERED, and **the corpus self-flags it**: [research/hard-problem-non-western-philosophy-2026-02-14.md](/research/hard-problem-non-western-philosophy-2026-02-14/) reads "Jain philosophy (jiva/ajiva): Jainism's dualism between living (conscious) and non-living matter — highly relevant, not yet researched." - **Would address**: Jain *jīva*/*ajīva* substance dualism (consciousness as the intrinsic, graduated mark of all *jīva*, from one-sensed beings upward — a graded-sentience map that speaks directly to the Map's marginal-organism and edge-of-sentience threads); *anekāntavāda* (many-sidedness) and *syādvāda* (conditional predication) as a built-in epistemic pluralism.
- **Target section**: `topics/` (deep-dive).

Strong.

#### Mesoamerican (Nahua/Aztec) Philosophy of Mind — Teotl and the Tonalli / Teyolia / Ihiyotl Animating Forces
- **Self-check verdict**: GENUINELY UNCOVERED. Exactly one passing mention across the whole corpus: [research/interaction-problem-non-western-philosophy-2026-02-17.md](/research/interaction-problem-non-western-philosophy-2026-02-17/) — "Mesoamerican philosophy: Aztec tonalli (soul-like heat force) interacting with the body." No article, no research note, no section. Indigenous coverage to date is Australian and African only; the Americas are absent.
- **Builds on**: `chinese-philosophy-of-mind.md` (as a *second* organicist/monist basin-edge case), `indigenous-australian-philosophy-of-consciousness.md`, `the-hard-problem-in-non-western-philosophy.md`.
- **Would address**: James Maffie's reconstruction of *teotl* (a single, self-generating, ever-moving sacred power — "a world in motion" process-pantheism with no mind-matter split), set against the tripartite animating-force psychology (*tonalli* / *teyolia* / *ihiyotl*). Rich double role: a monist basin-edge test *and* a distinctive pluralist soul-psychology.
- **Target section**: `topics/` (deep-dive).
- **Tenet alignment**: Tenet 1 (basin-edge sibling to the Chinese case — a deep tradition with no mind-matter divide, sharpening the framework-dependence-vs-depth-of-inquiry question outside the Old World); Tenet 5 (a coherent monist rival parsimony cannot adjudicate). Strong. **Discipline note for the writer**: like the Chinese article, this must hold framework-dependence and depth-of-inquiry as unadjudicated rivals and must not let *teotl* monism count as evidence for or against dualism.
