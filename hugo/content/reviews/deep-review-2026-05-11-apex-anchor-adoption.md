## Why This Review

Three new concept anchors were created earlier today in `obsidian/concepts/`. Each names a discipline or structural move that several existing apex articles deploy in load-bearing ways without yet citing the anchor. Cross-review verifies that the anchors are reachable from the apex layer, where readers most often enter the Map's structure. None of the 26 apex articles linked to any of the three anchors before this review.

The new anchors:

- **[type-token-causation](/concepts/type-token-causation/)** — Distinguishes type-level (statistical) from token-level (particular) causation. Operationalises [Minimal Quantum Interaction](/tenets/#minimal-quantum-interaction) by locating where Kim's exclusion argument bites and where it fails. The load-bearing claim: physical causation is type-level sufficient but token-level open wherever the physical event in question is a quantum-indeterminate outcome.
- **[integration-as-activity](/concepts/integration-as-activity/)** — Treats integration as something consciousness *does* (a temporally-extended act) rather than what consciousness *is* (a structural property at an instant). Convergent across recurrent processing theory, global workspace theory, predictive processing, and Whitehead's process philosophy at the description level; the further reading as the act of a *conscious subject* is the Map's own.
- **[interface-threshold](/concepts/interface-threshold/)** — Names the architectural phase transition where mind-brain coupling becomes rich enough for consciousness to select among neural patterns rather than merely accompany them. The threshold is a *qualitative* coupling condition; efficacy scaling above it is continuous.
