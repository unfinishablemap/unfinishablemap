## 2. Orientation — What the Article Actually Argues

The article exists to discharge a specific liability the project assigns it. The apex ("A Taxonomy of Voids") makes an abductive argument: 60+ cognitive "voids" cluster around the consciousness–matter interface in a structured way, and "Dualism predicts this convergence. The taxonomy confirms the prediction." The obvious deflation is a selection effect — the project is about consciousness, so of course its voids cluster there. This article is the designated empirical rebuttal to that worry.

Its method: catalogue "representative" physics voids (measurement problem, spectral-gap undecidability, cosmological horizons, fine-tuning) and mathematics voids (Gödel incompleteness, CH independence, uncomputability), then run them through two of the apex's instruments — the four-kinds taxonomy (unexplored / unexplorable / occluded / naturally-occluded) and the eight-entry failure-signature inventory — and report structural differences.

It reaches a deliberately qualified verdict on three axes:

1. **Taxonomic depth (claimed discriminating):** physics/math voids are "flat" (near-pure unexplorables); consciousness voids are "deep," spanning multiple kinds within a single void.
2. **Failure-signature profile (downgraded to "confirmatory"):** the consciousness-built inventory largely fails to transfer; the article concedes four of its eight signatures are subject-requiring and so their absence in subjectless domains is "guaranteed by construction."
3. **The counter-axis it concedes:** following Szangolies' use of Lawvere's fixed-point theorem, the deepest physics void (measurement problem) and deepest math void (incompleteness) are *not* independent — they share a self-reference mechanism — which weakens the apex's "arise independently" claim. The article rescues the verdict by arguing the *phenomenal* core of consciousness stands outside even that mechanism.

It then explicitly invokes the common-cause null to deny it has confirmed dualism over illusionism, claiming only "framework-internal discrimination" and "coherence weight." It ties the result to Tenet 5 (Occam's Razor Has Limits) and, "at one remove," to Tenet 1 (Dualism).

## 3. Five-Dimension Analysis

### (a) EMPIRICAL ACCURACY — VERDICT: MIXED; TWO MATERIAL ERRORS, ONE OVERSTATEMENT

The mathematics is handled carefully and the spectral-gap result is characterised with admirable precision — the article correctly notes it "concerns mathematical *models* of physical systems rather than finite physical systems themselves," which is exactly the caveat most popularisations drop. Gödel is stated correctly (consistency + sufficient expressiveness; first vs. second theorem distinguished) with no Lucas–Penrose overreach. CH attribution is correct (Gödel 1940 consistency, Cohen 1963 independence via forcing). These are genuine strengths.

But there are two errors of physics fact:

1. **Particle-horizon / event-horizon conflation.** The article writes: "events beyond the particle horizon are not merely unobserved but unobservable in principle." This is wrong. The particle horizon bounds what we *can* observe now and *grows* with cosmic time; regions beyond it are not-yet-observed, and many will become observable as it expands. The boundary of events that are forever unobservable, no matter how long one waits, is the cosmological *event* horizon (in an accelerating/de Sitter universe). These are numerically and conceptually distinct: per the Wikipedia "Cosmological horizon" entry (citing Margalef-Bentabol et al. 2013 and Peacock 1998), "the particle horizon represents the largest comoving distance from which light could have reached the observer by a specific time, while the cosmic event horizon is the largest comoving distance from which light emitted now can ever reach the observer in the future," and "points outside the event horizon can never be observed." At the present epoch the event horizon is in fact much smaller than the particle horizon: "The current distance to our cosmic event horizon is about five gigaparsecs (16 billion light-years), well within our observable range given by the particle horizon" (~46 Gly comoving in ΛCDM). The article has assigned the event horizon's defining property ("unobservable in principle") to the particle horizon. This factual error, ironically, weakens the article's own case: a *growing* particle horizon is more "frontier-like" (unexplored, not unexplorable) than the article wants, which would push cosmological horizons *toward* the deflationary reading.
2. **GRW lumped with predictively-equivalent interpretations.** Under the measurement problem the article lists "Copenhagen, many-worlds, Bohmian, GRW collapse" as rival interpretations "that agree on all predictions and disagree on what is real." This is false for GRW. Objective-collapse theories (GRW, CSL, Diósi–Penrose) are not interpretations that share all predictions — they are *rival physical theories* making different, experimentally testable predictions. The Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy entry "Collapse Theories" (plato.stanford.edu/entries/qm-collapse/) states verbatim: "Collapse theories qualify themselves as rival theories of quantum mechanics and one can easily identify some of their physical implications which, in principle, would allow crucial tests discriminating between the two." The Diósi–Penrose model has been experimentally falsified in its parameter-free form: Donadi et al., *Nature Physics* 17, 74–78 (2021), "Underground test of gravity-related wave function collapse" (the Gran Sasso experiment), rules it out, and active bounds on collapse parameters come from LISA Pathfinder (Helou, Slagmolen, McClelland & Chen, *Phys. Rev. D* 95, 084054, 2017), spontaneous X-ray emission (Majorana Demonstrator, Arnquist et al., *Phys. Rev. Lett.* 129, 080401, 2022; XENONnT, arXiv:2209.15082), and matter-wave interferometry. Including GRW in a set defined by "agree on all predictions" is a category error that also undercuts the article's own "interpretive proliferation" signature, since GRW is precisely the case where the proliferation is empirically adjudicable rather than interpretation-only.

**Overstatement:** Fine-tuning is asserted as a void with no flag that its evidential force is contested. The article calls its signature "explanatory regress" and says "physics has no accepted account," but does not note that whether fine-tuning is even surprising/in-need-of-explanation is itself a live, unsettled debate (the normalisability of the measure, the multiverse's confirmational status, the legitimacy of the probabilistic framing — see the SEP "Fine-Tuning" entry). Treating it as a settled "principled limit" alongside theorems is a charity asymmetry (see (b)).

### (b) INFERENCE VALIDITY — VERDICT: FAILS ITS OWN CONSTRAIN-VS-ESTABLISH GATE

Apply the gate directly: does the comparison discriminate a dualist reading from a physicalist one, or would a physicalist predict the same pattern?

A physicalist predicts the same pattern *exactly*. On any physicalist account, consciousness is the one domain where the inquiring system is also the system inquired into — the knower studies its own substrate using that substrate. That subject-involvement is not contested between dualist and physicalist; it is common ground. From it, everything the article counts as "different" follows trivially:

- **Taxonomic depth.** The "occluded" and "naturally-occluded" kinds are *defined* as requiring a subject whose access is hidden or evolutionarily shaped. The article admits this ("depth and subject-involvement do co-vary"). So consciousness voids populate those kinds because consciousness involves a subject — a fact a physicalist asserts just as confidently.
- **The "spanning" residue.** The article's escape hatch is that the genuinely independent fact is not depth but *spanning* — that a single consciousness void "fractures into multiple inaccessibility-modes simultaneously." But spanning is also exactly what a physicalist predicts: a self-modelling biological system will have an unexplored frontier (mid-range science), an unexplorable floor (computational/Gödelian limits on self-modelling), and occluded regions (introspection cannot see its own machinery) *all at once*, because these are different consequences of the single fact that the system is modelling itself with limited, evolved equipment. Spanning is overdetermined by physicalism. It discriminates nothing.

So the central surviving datum, after the signature axis is conceded away, carries no anti-physicalist weight. The article's own framing concedes the negative half of this (it cannot beat illusionism), but it does not follow the logic to its conclusion: if a physicalist predicts the spanning, then the spanning cannot "shift weight, modestly, toward the convergence being a real feature rather than an artefact." That sentence is the unearned upgrade. "Real feature of the territory" vs. "artefact of where the map looked" is a *false binary*: the difference can be a real feature of cognitive architecture (physicalism's reading) that is simultaneously an artefact of dualism-loaded curation at the metaphysical level. The article collapses "real" into "supports the convergence-toward-dualism reading," which is the abductive leap it elsewhere disowns.

**Hidden abductive leap:** The closing tie to Tenet 1 — that the phenomenal core "stands outside even the Lawvere self-reference structure… consistent with (without proving) the dualist reading that phenomenal character is not a structural or formal property" — is the leak. "Not captured by one particular formal mechanism (fixed-point self-reference)" does not imply "not structural or formal at all," and "not formal at all" does not imply "non-physical." Two non-sequiturs stacked. That the phenomenal core resists Lawvere is equally predicted by every non-eliminativist physicalism (type-B materialism, Russellian monism, illusionism-about-reports). The "consistent with" hedge is technically true and rhetorically loaded — consistency with dualism is not evidence for dualism when it is equally consistent with the live rivals.

### (c) TENET COHERENCE — VERDICT: THE ARTICLE IS A CONSTITUTIONAL-ATTRACTOR ARTIFACT; TENET LEAKAGE IS UPSTREAM, NOT IN THIS ARTICLE

The article coheres with the five tenets, but coherence is the disease, not the cure. The comparison instruments — the four-kinds taxonomy and the eight failure-signatures — were both built *from* the consciousness catalogue, which is itself curated under dualism-loaded criteria. Running physics and math through consciousness-derived instruments and finding they don't fit is a guaranteed outcome, not a discovery. This is the constitutional-attractor effect in its purest documented form: the measurement apparatus is constitutionally shaped so consciousness comes out special.

To its considerable credit, the article *sees* this for the signature axis and says so ("guaranteed by construction… carries little discriminating weight"). This is real evidential-status discipline. But it does not see — or does not concede — that the *same* by-construction problem afflicts the taxonomic-depth axis it keeps as load-bearing. The "occluded" and "naturally-occluded" kinds were added to the taxonomy specifically to capture consciousness phenomena (introspective opacity, evolutionarily-maintained blind spots — the apex's own "Fitness-Beats-Truth"/naturally-occluded discussion). Of course physics/math voids don't occupy them. The depth axis is no less rigged than the signature axis; the article discounts one and banks the other, which is special pleading internal to the comparison.

The deeper coherence problem is **upstream tenet leakage the article inherits and launders**. The apex it defends asserts flatly: "Dualism predicts this convergence. The taxonomy confirms the prediction." The project's own common-cause-null document directly contradicts the apex, conceding the voids catalogue "reads largely as *framework-internal coherence* rather than *framework-independent triangulation*" and that "convergence-of-X with the framework that defines X cannot earn the N-fold force the count suggests." This article sits between a confident apex and a self-undermining methodology note, and tries to thread them by being honest in the body while preserving the apex's "vindication" vocabulary in the framing. The honest body and the inherited framing are in tension; the framing should yield. (See [common-cause-null](/project/common-cause-null/).)

**Co-optation flag (amber, not red).** Szangolies is not a substance dualist. His broader programme (epistemic horizons; "Von Neumann Minds: Intentional Automata"; qualia as the non-computable model–world link) is a naturalist/anti-dualist program in the von Neumann self-reference tradition. The article is careful to attribute to him only the *formal* unification of physics/math voids, which is fair use. But it then deploys that unification inside a structure whose punchline is "the phenomenal core stands apart… consistent with the dualist reading." Szangolies' own view treats the phenomenal/intentional as arising *from* the same fixed-point machinery, not as standing outside it in a way that licenses dualism. The article does not misquote him, but it conscripts his apparatus toward a conclusion he rejects. Per the co-optation firewall this should be made explicit in-text (the "self-reference-and-the-limits-of-physical-description" sibling must carry a Szangolies-is-not-a-dualist boundary marker, and this article should not lean on the inference without it).

### (e) STRONGEST UNADDRESSED RIVAL — VERDICT: THE SELECTION-ASYMMETRY NULL IS NAMED BUT NOT CONFRONTED

The article confronts the *generic* selection-effect worry and the common-cause null. The objection it does not confront is the sharper **asymmetric-selection null**: "consciousness behaves differently" is fully explained by the fact that consciousness voids were selected using dualism-loaded, subject-centric criteria (the curation privileges introspective, phenomenal, self-referential failures) while physics/math voids were selected by neutral, discipline-external criteria (textbook theorems and standard foundational puzzles). Under asymmetric selection, the asymmetry of *output* is mechanically guaranteed by the asymmetry of *input* — no feature of the territory required.

Worse, the comparison is uncharitable to the rival domains in ways that inflate the asymmetry:

- Physics/math voids are admitted only if "principled" (theorem-grade), explicitly filtering out the "merely unexplored." But the consciousness catalogue is *not* held to that bar — the apex includes frankly unexplored and contested items (dream consciousness, infant consciousness, the "resonance void"). The bar is applied asymmetrically: rivals must be theorems; consciousness voids may be frontiers.
- The instruments are consciousness-native. A symmetric test would build a *physics-native* taxonomy and signature inventory and run consciousness through *it* — at which point consciousness voids would presumably look "flat" and physics would look "deep." The article never runs the comparison in the other direction, which is the tell of a stacked deck.

A genuinely neutral referee would predict the article's exact result *a priori* from the selection asymmetry alone, with no metaphysics. That prediction is the rival the article must defeat and does not.

**Equivocation on "void."** Underlying all of this is a category error the article half-acknowledges and then proceeds past. "Void" is doing equivocal work across at least four distinct kinds: (i) in-principle *formal undecidability* (spectral gap, halting, CH); (ii) *epistemic/observational horizons* (cosmology); (iii) *interpretational underdetermination* (measurement problem); and (iv) the *hard problem / explanatory gap* (phenomenal core). These are not the same kind of thing — an independence result is a proved property of a formal system; an observational horizon is a contingent fact about lightcones; underdetermination is a feature of theory-choice; the explanatory gap is a contested claim about concepts and properties. Lumping them under one word and then announcing that one cluster "behaves differently" is comparing incomparables. The article's "matched grain" claim is the rhetorical move that papers over the equivocation; it is not earned, because there is no single grain on which a forcing argument, a de Sitter horizon, and the felt redness of red are commensurable units.

**The conceded counter-axis is a performative inoculation citation.** The Lawvere/Szangolies "difference that cuts the other way" is presented as costly honesty, and structurally it functions as inoculation: by conceding that the *formal* physics/math voids share a mechanism (undermining the apex's "arise independently" wording), the article buys credibility to protect the *phenomenal* core's apartness — the actually load-bearing claim. But the concession, taken seriously, cuts deeper than admitted. If the deepest physics and math voids are unified by self-reference, and the formal/reflexive consciousness voids (recursion, cartography) join that same spine, then the "structured clustering" the apex sells as dualism-evidence is substantially a *single formal phenomenon* (fixed-point self-reference) recurring across domains — a common cause that is the opposite of independent triangulation. The article quarantines the damage to "the phenomenal core," but the phenomenal core is a *much smaller* set of voids than the 60+ the apex counts. The concession, fully cashed, shrinks the abductive base to a handful of phenomenal voids — and those are precisely the ones where dualist and illusionist readings are empirically tied. The inoculation conceals that the concession guts the N-fold convergence count.

## 4. Bottom-Line Judgment

The article is a paradox: the most methodologically scrupulous piece I have audited from this project, in the service of a conclusion its own scruples should have killed. Its citations are clean, its quotation is accurate, its self-awareness about the by-construction problem is real, and it correctly refuses to claim it has beaten illusionism. But it cannot bring itself to take the last step its own reasoning demands. After the signature axis is conceded as analytic, the counter-axis concession is shown to shrink the convergence base, the asymmetric-selection null is applied, and the constrain-vs-establish gate is run, **nothing remains that discriminates dualism from physicalism.** The "qualified vindication" is not qualified enough: the correct verdict is that the cross-domain comparison establishes only that consciousness is the domain where the inquirer and the inquired coincide — which everyone, dualist and physicalist alike, already knew, and which carries no metaphysical weight.

This is not a "delete" — the empirical material is largely accurate, the citations are sound, and a demoted version has legitimate value as an honest illustration of framework-internal coherence and of the limits of the convergence argument. But it must stop claiming to "shift weight toward the convergence being real," because the body has already proven it cannot. As written, the article is a **bounded-Occam asymmetry** exhibit: it wields Tenet 5 to deny physicalism the parsimony of "all hard limits are alike," while never once turning parsimony on the dual-substance posit that the whole edifice exists to protect. Simplicity pressure flows in exactly one direction. That is the project's structural signature, and this article, for all its honesty, does not escape it.

## 5. Remediation

### (i) Article-Specific Fixes

1. **Correct the horizon error.** Replace "events beyond the particle horizon are not merely unobserved but unobservable in principle" with the accurate distinction: the particle horizon bounds the currently-observable universe and grows with time (a frontier, hence partly *unexplored*); the *cosmological event horizon* is the in-principle-forever boundary ("points outside the event horizon can never be observed," per the cosmology literature; event horizon ~16 Gly vs. particle horizon ~46 Gly today). Then reassess the kind-assignment — a growing particle horizon arguably belongs partly in "unexplored," which weakens the "flat unexplorables" claim and must be acknowledged.
2. **Remove GRW from the "agree on all predictions" set.** State that objective-collapse theories (GRW, CSL, Diósi–Penrose) are empirically distinguishable rival theories — cite the SEP "Collapse Theories" entry and Donadi et al., *Nature Physics* 17, 74–78 (2021) for the falsification of parameter-free Diósi–Penrose — and note this *undercuts* the "interpretive proliferation" signature, since here the proliferation is adjudicable.
3. **Delete the "shifts weight, modestly, toward the convergence being a real feature" claim** and every cognate ("qualified vindication," "earns weight"). Replace with the conclusion the body actually supports: the comparison shows consciousness is the self-referential domain, which is common ground between dualism and physicalism and therefore non-discriminating. Retitle/reframe from "the Map's empirical answer to the selection-effect worry" to "why the selection-effect worry cannot be answered by this comparison."
4. **Apply the by-construction discount to the taxonomic-depth axis, not just the signature axis.** The "occluded" and "naturally-occluded" kinds were built to capture consciousness phenomena; physics/math non-occupancy is guaranteed, not found. The "spanning" residue must be tested against the physicalist prediction (a self-modelling system spans modes by default) and conceded as non-discriminating.
5. **Run the comparison in the reverse direction** or explicitly flag its absence: build (or acknowledge the absence of) a physics-native taxonomy and signature inventory and note that consciousness would look "flat" under it. Without this, the asymmetric-instrument objection stands unrebutted.
6. **Hold the rival domains to the same admission bar as consciousness voids,** or hold consciousness voids to the theorem-grade bar applied to physics/math. The current asymmetry (rivals must be principled; consciousness voids may be frontiers) must be removed or defended.
7. **Install the Szangolies co-optation firewall in-text:** state that Szangolies' fixed-point program is naturalist and treats the phenomenal/intentional as *arising from* self-reference, not standing outside it, so the "phenomenal core stands apart → consistent with dualism" gloss is the Map's inference and not his.
8. **Cash out the counter-axis concession's cost:** acknowledge that if the formal voids share a single self-reference mechanism, the apex's N-fold "structured clustering" shrinks to a handful of phenomenal voids, and update the abductive-weight claim accordingly.

### (ii) Site-Wide Methodology Fixes

1. **Reconcile the apex with the common-cause-null document.** "Dualism predicts this convergence. The taxonomy confirms the prediction" cannot coexist with "convergence-of-X with the framework that defines X cannot earn the N-fold force." The apex must be downgraded to coherence-language or the methodology note must be retracted; the project cannot bank both.
2. **Forbid instrument-native cross-domain tests as evidence.** Any comparison run through consciousness-derived instruments yields a guaranteed asymmetry and must be labelled coherence-illustration, never selection-effect rebuttal. Codify a symmetric-instrument requirement (run the comparison from the rival domain's native taxonomy too).
3. **Promote a standing constrain-vs-establish lint for the "real feature vs. artefact" binary.** Every place the corpus argues a pattern is "a real feature rather than an artefact of where we looked," require an explicit third option: a real feature of cognitive architecture that is metaphysically neutral between dualism and physicalism. This binary is the project's recurring abductive leak.
4. **Apply bounded-Occam symmetrically.** Tenet 5 is invoked to deny rivals the parsimony of unification but never applied to the dual-substance posit. Require every Tenet-5 invocation to state the parsimony cost the Map itself pays (the interaction problem, the extra substance) in the same passage.
5. **Make the equivocation-on-"void" audit mandatory.** Any article comparing "voids" across domains must first disambiguate formal undecidability, observational horizons, interpretational underdetermination, and the explanatory gap, and justify any "matched grain" claim rather than asserting it.
6. **Extend the co-optation firewall to formal-apparatus borrowing.** The Stapp case is already logged; Szangolies is a second instance of borrowing a naturalist's machinery toward dualist conclusions. Require a boundary marker wherever a living naturalist's formal result is used inside a dualism-supporting structure, not only where they are directly quoted.
7. **Audit the asymmetric admission bar across the voids catalogue.** If physics/math voids must be theorem-grade "principled limits," consciousness voids must clear the same bar to be counted in any cross-domain comparison; frontier-grade items (dream, infant, resonance) cannot be admitted on one side of a comparison and excluded on the other.
