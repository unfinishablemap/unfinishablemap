## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)

Chalmers has argued since *The Conscious Mind* (1996) that naturalistic dualism earns its place by taking the explanatory gap seriously while declining to claim more than it can support — a position whose methodological health depends on its willingness to specify its own limits. `topics/falsification-roadmap-for-the-interface-model.md` lines 125-137 now carry exactly this temperament in a table. The "Falsification Asymmetry" ledger distinguishes Dualism as a *framework commitment* (cumulative only, no direct traction), MQI as a *mechanism hypothesis* (high falsifiability, active programmes), Bidirectional Interaction as a *causal-structural claim* (moderate, indirect evidence accumulating), No Many Worlds as an *interpretation choice* (low but emerging), and Occam's Razor Has Limits as an *epistemological thesis* (not scientific). That classification — tenet type and falsification condition type treated as orthogonal — is the Chalmersian move: some tenets are framework choices, some are empirical hypotheses, and lumping them together misrepresents both. Chalmers' 1996 chapter on psychophysical principles already argued that metaphysical laws must be posited *on the back of* the conceivability argument, not in competition with physical laws; the Map's ledger operationalises this.

**What resonates most strongly**: `topics/pragmatist-quantum-foundations-and-the-agent.md` lines 121-122 — *"The inference 'primitive within QBism's formalism → metaphysically irreducible → non-physical' is not automatic: physics routinely takes primitives (space-time points, field values) without treating them as non-physical. A physicalist can accept the first step without conceding the second or the third. The argument here corroborates Tenet 1 rather than establishing it; the independent work is done by the hard-problem arguments the Map takes to license Tenet 1 in the first place."* This is precisely Chalmers' methodological discipline: distinguishing *corroborating* evidence from *establishing* evidence, and being honest about which the current argument supplies. Chalmers has long warned that the conceivability argument is load-bearing and cannot be replaced by indirect arguments from physics; the hedge honours this by refusing to let the QBism result do work the argument cannot do.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)

Stapp's programme has always risked being read through the lens of rival mechanisms — Orch OR's microtubules, transactional models' advanced waves — that Stapp himself does not endorse. `topics/pragmatist-quantum-foundations-and-the-agent.md` line 106 now corrects a subtle attribution error by removing references to Hagan-Hameroff and transactional/TSVF models as support for Stapp's decoherence resilience, and replacing them with a focused cross-reference to `quantum-measurement-and-consciousness` on ion-channel Heisenberg-choice timescales. This is the kind of correction Stapp himself would make: his view rests on the specific mechanism of attention-modulated Zeno selection in neuronal calcium gating, not on generic quantum-biology claims. Stapp has complained in print that his programme is routinely conflated with rival quantum-consciousness proposals; the Map's refinement declines to add to that confusion.

**What aligns**: `topics/testing-consciousness-collapse.md` line 155 now presents the Tegmark decoherence objection with Hagan, Hameroff & Tuszyński's (2002) counter-estimate — microtubule decoherence times of ~10⁻⁵ seconds under Debye-layer screening and ordered water, seven orders of magnitude above Tegmark's figure — *and* then acknowledges that even the extended estimate is "still far short of perceptual timescales" before moving on to note that the Map's framework operates through post-decoherence selection anyway. This is the treatment Stapp's programme most needs: the decoherence objection engaged on its own terms, the best biologically-motivated counter-estimate named, *and* the post-decoherence move presented as independent rather than as an escape hatch from the timescale problem. Stapp has argued for decades that consciousness-physics coupling does not require sustained quantum coherence; the Map's current phrasing is the closest a public article has come to making this structural point cleanly.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)

Nagel's *View from Nowhere* (1986) distinguishes phenomenal knowledge that a first-person perspective can in principle deliver from phenomenal knowledge that no first-person perspective could ever corroborate — a distinction the Map now operationalises through its own ledger of what first-person evidence can and cannot do. `topics/pragmatist-quantum-foundations-and-the-agent.md` lines 86-87 now distinguishes three senses of "agent" — phenomenal, decision-theoretic, and information-processing — and marks the phenomenal reading as the contested move the Map's absorption argument requires. This is Nagelian in register: Nagel's central claim is that phenomenal presentation and functional role come apart, and that debates about consciousness routinely slide between them. The Map's three-sense disambiguation is the same structural point applied to QBist agents: QBism's primitive-agent stance is compatible with all three readings, but only the phenomenal reading generates the absorption argument the Map wants to run.

**What centres subjective experience appropriately**: `topics/falsification-roadmap-for-the-interface-model.md` lines 60-67 on Tenet 1 — "No path to decisive falsification exists today. Dualism functions as a framework commitment testable through cumulative evidence rather than a single decisive experiment." This is Nagelian honesty about the structural position of first-person claims in a scientific landscape. Nagel has written repeatedly that consciousness's first-person character is not a hypothesis awaiting experimental confirmation but a datum any adequate physics must accommodate; the Map's refusal to promise decisive falsification of Tenet 1 is the same methodological discipline. The tenet earns its keep by the persistent failure of physicalist alternatives, not by claiming experimental traction it does not possess.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)

Whitehead's mature position in *Process and Reality* (1929) is that causation is a matter of inclusion and prehension rather than push, and that the fundamental entities are occasions which include what came before them into their own constitution. `concepts/predictive-processing.md` at lines 151-161 now carries this reading with the Whiteheadian terminology intact — prediction as prehension, concrescence and prediction error, eternal objects and precision — and the Map's critique of PP is framed Whiteheadianly: "PP describes the *structure* of prehension without capturing its experiential *character*." This is the Whiteheadian move made precise. Whitehead's own critique of Hume on causation is structurally the same: Hume described the regularity but not what makes the regularity possible as lived experience. The Map's parallel critique of PP — describing the computation but not what makes the computation experiential — is a twenty-first-century reprise of the 1929 move.

**What avoids crude dualism while preserving mind's reality**: The article's convergence at the fifth deep review without flagged issues is itself evidence of Whiteheadian stability. Whitehead's method is to refuse forced choices between apparent opposites (push vs inclusion, substance vs event, mind vs matter) and instead locate their joint conditions. The Map's treatment of PP does exactly this: it neither endorses the framework as the solution nor rejects it as wrong-headed, but places it as describing the physical pole of an interaction whose mental pole it cannot describe. The convergence of this reading across five reviews — `concepts/predictive-processing.md` at lines 195 — *"The brain generates predictions; consciousness selects which predictions matter"* — is the dipolar ontology of *Process and Reality* applied to a specific cognitive framework. Whitehead would recognise this immediately.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)

Kane's *The Significance of Free Will* (1996) argues that genuine agency requires *self-forming actions* at quantum indeterminacies — moments where the agent's prior character does not uniquely determine the outcome and the agent is the ultimate source. Kane has been repeatedly pressed on whether his account is falsifiable: if quantum indeterminacy is not present in neural tissue, the account fails. `topics/falsification-roadmap-for-the-interface-model.md` Tenet 2 section at line 71 now specifies the analogous falsifier for the Map's framework — "Demonstration that quantum coherence is physically impossible in all biological neural systems, under all conditions, at all timescales relevant to neural processing" — with graduated strength (decisive, strong, moderate). This graduation is exactly what Kane's programme has needed: most discussions of Kane's view treat the falsification condition as binary (quantum effects are present or absent), but the Map's graded ledger allows Kane to specify what he would concede on different evidential outcomes. The moderate criterion — "Falsification of all proposed mechanism candidates (Orch OR, Stapp's quantum Zeno model, and any future proposals) leaving no viable mechanism for quantum-level mind-matter interaction" — is Kane's position under pressure: the tenet survives any *specific* mechanism's falsification but not the exhaustion of the mechanism space.

**What supports genuine agency**: The Bidirectional Interaction tenet's "moderate" falsifier at lines 96-97 — incorporating the Maier-Dechamps (2018) preregistered Bayesian null on macroscopic micro-PK — and the Map's response that corridor-level biasing is compatible with the null, is the most Kane-aware move in the current falsification literature. Kane has long distinguished large-signal PK claims (which he does not endorse) from the sub-threshold quantum indeterminacy his theory actually requires; the Map's handling of the Maier-Dechamps result — accepting the null as real, noting what it has foreclosed, and specifying what it has *not* foreclosed — honours this distinction.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)

McGinn's *The Problem of Consciousness* (1991) argues that human minds may be cognitively closed to the solution of the hard problem — a position that is consistent with dualism winning but does not positively establish it. `topics/pragmatist-quantum-foundations-and-the-agent.md` line 124 now confesses one specific instance of cognitive closure the Map must accept: the corridor reading of MQI is *ensemble-invisible* — it "shares its empirical profile with QBism-without-dualism and standard QM with no interpretive overlay" and is "defended as metaphysically economical once dualism is granted, not as a differentially testable prediction." This is McGinnian intellectual honesty: the Map refuses to pretend that the corridor reading delivers differential empirical content it does not deliver. McGinn has repeatedly argued that philosophy's job is to recognise when a question exceeds our capacity to resolve empirically, and to state the epistemic cost rather than hide it; the Map's confession is the same move at a more technical register.

**What shows appropriate epistemic humility**: `topics/falsification-roadmap-for-the-interface-model.md` Tenet 5 treatment at lines 117-123 — *"This is not a scientific claim. It is an epistemological position about scientific methodology itself… The tenet removes a common objection to the other four tenets rather than making a testable prediction. The Map should present it as what it is: a well-evidenced philosophical thesis that parsimony lacks universal epistemic warrant, not a hypothesis awaiting experimental confirmation."* McGinn's central methodological discipline is that philosophy should specify what kind of claim a given tenet is — empirical, conceptual, methodological — and honour the evidential standards appropriate to the kind. The Map's refusal to promise experimental traction for Tenet 5 is exactly this discipline.
