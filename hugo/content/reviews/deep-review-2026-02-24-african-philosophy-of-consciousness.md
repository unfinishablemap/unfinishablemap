## Pessimistic Analysis Summary

### Critical Issues Found

1. **Wiredu misattribution**: The article presented Wiredu as confirming that Akan thought straightforwardly assumes dualism ("the real philosophical question is not whether mind is reducible to matter — Akan thought never entertained that possibility"). Wiredu is actually known for resisting straightforward dualist readings of Akan thought, suggesting quasi-physical interpretations of sunsum. **Resolution**: Rewrote the Wiredu paragraph to acknowledge his nuanced position — that Akan categories defy easy classification into Western materialist/dualist binaries — while preserving the point that Akan thought operates outside the materialist framework.

2. **Overstated quantum-Akan connection**: The article presented the Map's quantum interactionism as "compatible with the Akan intuition" without clearly flagging this as speculative extension. **Resolution**: Reworded to describe a "structural parallel — though not a direct connection" and noted that the Akan framework "predates and is independent of quantum mechanics."

### Medium Issues Found

3. **Ubuntu metaphysical vs. ethical debate**: The article presented Ubuntu as a metaphysical claim without acknowledging the scholarly debate. **Resolution**: Added note that Thaddeus Metz and others interpret Ubuntu primarily as an ethical framework, with the article explicitly noting the Map favours the ontological reading.

4. **Abimbola attribution too strong**: "shows that" overstated what is primarily an expository work. **Resolution**: Softened to "exposition of the Ifá literary corpus documents how."

5. **Combination problem claim unexplained**: The article claimed African traditions avoid the combination problem but didn't explain how. **Resolution**: Added explanation that African frameworks posit a hierarchy of vital force with qualitative distinctions between levels, rather than treating consciousness as homogeneous and requiring combination.

6. **Missing cross-link**: No link to `cross-cultural-convergence-on-mental-causation`. **Resolution**: Added to frontmatter, body text (in Vital Force section), and Further Reading.
