- **Why it's a real gap**: The theories-of-consciousness cluster is otherwise **complete** — GWT, IIT, HOT, RPT, AST, first-order-representationalism, attention-schema, multiple-drafts, self-model-theory all have dedicated pages. The same-order / self-representational view (Kriegel, *Subjective Consciousness* 2009; Williford; Gennaro's "wide intrinsicality") is the one major reductive theory of consciousness with no home. It is the natural dialectical partner to `higher-order-theories` (it drops the higher-order relation to a single self-directed representation to dodge the misrepresentation/targetless-HOT objections) — leaving it out breaks an otherwise systematic map.
- **Tenet alignment**: A page can engage it as a rival the Map declines (Tenet 1): self-representation explains the *structure* of for-me-ness but re-sites rather than closes the hard problem, exactly the dialectic `self-model-theory-of-subjectivity` already runs against SMT. Cross-links to `higher-order-theories`, `phenomenal-presentation`, `self-reference-paradox`, `phenomenology-of-recursive-self-awareness`.
- **Scope**: Medium.

#### Occasionalism — `concepts/`
- **The gap (grep-verified)**: Only 2 files mention it (`topics/history-of-the-interaction-problem`, `concepts/islamic-sufi-philosophy-of-consciousness`). It gets a genuine survey subsection in `history-of-the-interaction-problem` (Malebranche, La Forge, Cordemoy) but **no dedicated page**.
- **Why it's a real gap**: Occasionalism is *the* paradigm non-interactionist dualist rival — "God is the only true cause; He moves the body on the occasion of the volition." Yet every other rival -ism the Map engages has a dedicated page (functionalism, panpsychism, idealism, biological-naturalism, anomalous-monism, epiphenomenalism, illusionism…). The two classic dualist-family alternatives to genuine interaction — occasionalism and pre-established-harmony/psychophysical-parallelism — live *only* inside a survey. That is a coverage asymmetry a dedicated page would fix.
- **Tenet alignment**: Directly sharpens Tenet 3 (Bidirectional Interaction) and the interface programme: occasionalism keeps both substances but sacrifices creaturely causation, exactly the cost the Map's delegatory/selection mechanism is designed to avoid. A dedicated page can contrast "no creaturely causation" occasionalism with `delegatory-dualism`/`selection-only-channel` and could fold in **psychophysical parallelism / pre-established harmony** as the sibling non-interactionist solution (both currently survey-only).
- **Scope**: Medium.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers (recent work)

The recent [concepts/cosmopsychism.md](/concepts/cosmopsychism/) (created this session) is the strongest specimen and grounds most of the praise below.

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
Cosmopsychism honours the shared conviction that "consciousness is not reducible to physical process," then does the harder thing — locates the Map's irreducibility "at the mind–matter interface of numerically distinct subjects rather than in the intrinsic nature of a single whole." The explanatory gap is taken seriously enough to be *placed*, not waved at.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
Not the focus of this batch, but the interface programme's insistence (in `history-of-the-interaction-problem`) that "quantum mechanics reopened possibilities that Leibniz thought definitively closed" keeps the physics-consciousness interface load-bearing rather than decorative.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The self-model dialectic surfaced in the sweep (`self-model-theory-of-subjectivity`: "why is there phenomenal character to the modeling at all? Why does running a transparent self-model *feel like* being someone, rather than proceeding in the dark?") centres first-person character exactly where a functional story goes quiet.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
Cosmopsychism's careful handling of priority monism (Schaffer 2010, "metaphysical explanation dangling downward from the One") engages holistic, whole-prior-to-parts metaphysics on its own terms rather than caricaturing it — the anti-crude-dualism instinct done right.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
Out of scope for this batch (no new agency articles sampled); no praise manufactured.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
Strong epistemic humility in the cosmopsychism lead: the Map *declines* the view but frames the choice as a **trade** — "spares the Map the de-combination problem… at the price of the interaction problem instead." Rivals are costed, not dismissed.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
**This batch is clean on evidential discipline** — no minimal-organism or boundary-case tier-upgrades are involved, and the sampled work models the restraint the persona prizes. Cosmopsychism's attribution hedging is exemplary: the term is "**usually attributed to Itay Shani (2015)**," immediately qualified with "Attribution should be hedged… the SEP does not single out one originator, and closely related top-down proposals appear contemporaneously in Freya Mathews (2011) and Jaskolla & Buck (2012). Shani is best described as the philosopher who put the label into wide circulation, not as its sole coiner." That is priority-claim calibration done to publisher standard. The constitutive-vs-priority split is presented as a **live dispute** ("Whether the priority relation genuinely lightens the load or merely renames the debt is one of the live disputes"), not resolved by fiat — tenet-coherence is not smuggled in as evidence anywhere. Both this persona and the Process Philosopher can praise the cosmopsychism page without conflict, which is the signal that its tensions are resolved honestly.
