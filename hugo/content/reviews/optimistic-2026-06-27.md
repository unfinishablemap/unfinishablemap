## Executive Summary

This is one of the Map's strongest and most internally honest clusters. The agency case is built so that each component (agent causation, quantum selection, counterfactual reasoning, the selector model) answers objections to the others while being anchored to four independently-grounded claims — and the cluster says so explicitly, pre-empting the circularity charge. Most notably, the **Process Philosopher and the Hardline Empiricist personas converge in praising the same passages**: the cluster repeatedly performs the discipline of distinguishing "what the model illuminates" from "what the evidence establishes," so process-philosophical resonance and evidential restraint reinforce rather than fight each other. The chief opportunity is cross-linking: the apex synthesis anchor (`consciousness-and-agency.md`) does not currently link to two of its closest siblings — `control-theoretic-will` and the `falsification-roadmap`.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The cluster takes the explanatory gap seriously and refuses to let it collapse into hand-waving. `the-psychophysical-control-law.md` quotes Chalmers directly — "It would be odd if the universe had fundamental laws connecting complex functional organizations to conscious experiences... one would expect it to be a consequence of simpler, more fundamental psychophysical laws" — and adopts his expectation that the specification gap is a *problem to be solved*, not a permanent feature of our epistemic situation. The "upward supervenience laws (Chalmers' primary contribution) specify how physics yields experience; the Map needs the other direction" framing in the control-law article is exactly the right way to position the Map relative to Chalmers: same hard problem, opposite arrow. Chalmers would recognise his own view honoured and extended rather than co-opted.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
Stapp's framework gets the most developed treatment in the cluster. `the-psychophysical-control-law.md` reconstructs his attention-rate proposal as a recognisable *proportional controller* (observation frequency = f(attention intensity), monotonically increasing, with a gain parameter that would be "the fundamental psychophysical constant in Stapp's framework"). This is a genuine contribution — translating a philosophical proposal into control-theoretic form that exposes its testable structure. Crucially, the article does Stapp the honour of stating his strongest limitation plainly (the Tegmark decoherence-timescale challenge, with the corrected 10¹³–10²⁰ Hz observation-rate demand) rather than burying it. A Stapp sympathiser sees the position engaged at its best and its hardest.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The cluster centres first-person experience as load-bearing evidence, not decoration. `consciousness-and-agency.md` makes the phenomenology of effort one of its four independently-grounded anchors: "felt difficulty tracking cognitive load... are empirical findings that don't presuppose the framework." The "witness mode" passage — "A passive recipient of random quantum events could not choose to be passive. The very capacity to suspend selection shows consciousness does something rather than merely receiving outcomes" — is a genuinely elegant phenomenological argument against the luck objection. And the Sartre *pour-soi* material identifies a *structural precondition* (consciousness must be distanced from its contents to select among them) that is phenomenological rather than metaphysical, exactly Nagel's preferred register.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The cluster avoids crude substance dualism while preserving mind's reality, and it earns Whitehead's approval at the level he cares about. `consciousness-and-agency.md` invokes the Lowe/O'Connor thesis that *all* causation is fundamentally substance causation and pairs it with Whitehead's process view: "if all causation involves experiential self-determination, human agent causation is continuous with causation generally. The question shifts from 'How can consciousness possibly cause anything?' to 'Why did we think it couldn't?'" This is the right use of process philosophy — as a *defeater-remover* for the "consciousness can't cause anything" intuition, not as positive evidence for any specific selection event. (See the Hardline Empiricist note below: the cluster is careful never to convert this resonance into a tier-upgrade, which is precisely why both personas can praise it.)

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
This is Kane's cluster. It takes sourcehood seriously, runs the van Inwagen rollback argument head-on, and answers the luck objection with three named features (reasons-guidance, phenomenology of effort, distinctive neural signatures) rather than dodging it. The note that **Kane's 2024 work now incorporates agent-causal elements** into his traditionally event-causal framework — "representing convergence between the traditions" — is exactly the kind of live-literature awareness that keeps the cluster current. The `compatibilist-symmetry-challenge` integration is the cluster's single most impressive move: rather than overclaiming, it *concedes* that sophisticated compatibilism plausibly matches the Map across most of moral and phenomenological terrain, then narrows libertarian agency's distinctive contribution to "ultimate desert and the metaphysics of open alternatives." Kane would admire the intellectual honesty of a libertarian who states exactly how much (and how little) the libertarian addition buys.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
The cluster shows appropriate epistemic humility without using it as an escape hatch — and says so. `the-psychophysical-control-law.md` explicitly *declines* to lean on cognitive closure: "A research programme that reserves the right to invoke cognitive closure whenever progress stalls has immunised itself against failure." That is McGinn's own insight turned into a discipline: the Map acknowledges closure as a logical possibility, refuses to rely on it, and locates the specification gap in scientific immaturity instead. The "Galileo of consciousness science has not yet arrived" framing is humility with a forward path rather than resignation — McGinn-adjacent without McGinn's pessimism.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This cluster is a showcase of the restraint this persona exists to reward, and its verdict here is load-bearing because the cluster makes tenet-load-bearing causal claims.

- **Tenet-as-evidence-upgrade is praise-worthily *not* done.** `control-theoretic-will.md` devotes an entire section ("Analogy, Model, and Ontology") to the very slippage this persona guards against: "Conflating the model with the ontology would be a category error — treating a useful description of *what happens* as proof of *what exists*." It states outright that "a physicalist could adopt every element of the control-theoretic framing... while maintaining that consciousness is an emergent property of brain processes," and that the dualist commitment "comes from the Map's independent arguments for dualism... not from control theory itself." The control-theoretic resonance is presented as *illuminating* dualist interaction, never as *establishing* it. This is the named pattern "tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating" executed at full length.
- **Honest framework-stage calibration.** `falsification-roadmap-for-the-interface-model.md` and `the-psychophysical-control-law.md` both invoke `framework-stage-calibration` to mark the framework as *pre-Keplerian* — "accumulating Tycho-analogue measurements" — rather than over-claiming Newton-stage results. The control-law article's "empirical content is therefore deferred and conditional — a promissory note that becomes a live test only once the measurement framework... exists" is exactly the register this persona praises.
- **Registers kept separate under pressure.** The falsification roadmap refuses to flatten Tenet 2 into a single falsifiability row, tabulating six mechanism families (corridor, probability-bias, Stapp/Zeno, Orch-OR, Chalmers-McQueen, objective-collapse modulation, trumping/preemption) with materially different evidential profiles, and explicitly warns against "silently export[ing] the mechanism-committed readings' experimental traction to the strict corridor." It also concedes that the narrowing toward an attention-motor umbrella is "post-hoc accommodation rather than predictive narrowing" — naming its own Lakatosian protective-belt risk rather than hiding it. This is evidential discipline at its best.
- **Convergence with the Process Philosopher.** Where the Process Philosopher praises the Whitehead/Lowe "causation all the way down" move, the Hardline Empiricist confirms it is used only as a defeater-remover and never as a tier-upgrade. The two personas converge — the diagnostic the skill specifies for "the article has resolved the tension honestly." No calibration concern is flagged for this cluster.

## Content Strengths

### apex/consciousness-and-agency.md
- **Strongest point**: The "Synthesis" section's explicit anti-circularity defence — naming four anchor points (hard problem, argument from reason, phenomenology+neural correlates, clinical neuroplasticity) that have "independent grounding outside the circle," each of which works "even if the selector model is wrong."
- **Notable quote**: "A passive recipient of random quantum events could not choose to be passive. The very capacity to suspend selection shows consciousness does something rather than merely receiving outcomes."
- **Why it works**: It pre-empts the most common objection to mutually-supporting frameworks (vicious circularity) by *showing its load-bearing supports are external*, then honestly lists what would falsify the *combined* position in a dedicated "What Would Challenge This View?" section. The compatibilist-symmetry concession (narrowing the libertarian payoff to ultimate desert) is rare intellectual honesty for an apex piece.

### concepts/control-theoretic-will.md
- **Strongest point**: The controller/plant mapping table plus five named control operations (gating, stabilisation, veto, attractor steering, resource allocation), each tied to existing Map articles and each shown to fit the ~10 bits/second constraint.
- **Notable quote**: "Low bandwidth is not a failure of the controller; it reflects appropriate abstraction — controlling at the right level."
- **Why it works**: It reframes the Map's most counter-intuitive number (10 bits/sec) from an embarrassment into a *design feature* via the thermostat/pilot analogy, and then immediately fences off the over-reach with the "Analogy, Model, and Ontology" section. Generative without over-claiming.

### topics/the-psychophysical-control-law.md
- **Strongest point**: Reconstructing three candidate mechanisms (Stapp, Eccles, policy-selection) as three distinct *control-law forms* (proportional controller, probability-reweighting controller, switching controller), each with explicit strengths and limitations.
- **Notable quote**: "Without a law, any outcome can be reconciled with 'consciousness selects.' With a law, specific outcomes falsify specific predictions — once the independent measurement of phenomenal inputs that the law requires becomes available."
- **Why it works**: It converts a vague dualist commitment into a structured specification problem with named constraints (bandwidth compatibility, conservation compliance, scope locality, phenomenological consistency, falsifiability) — and is scrupulous about the difference between in-principle and currently-available falsifiability.

### topics/falsification-roadmap-for-the-interface-model.md
- **Strongest point**: The tenet-by-tenet falsifiability table, refusing to flatten Tenet 2 and explicitly classifying each tenet as framework-commitment / mechanism-hypothesis / causal-structural / interpretation-choice / epistemological-thesis.
- **Notable quote**: "The honest position is: the Map is partially testable, partially a framework choice, the testable parts are under active investigation, and which testable part one is testing matters as much as the fact of testability."
- **Why it works**: Publishing a falsification roadmap is itself an enactment of the tenets, and the article uses the Kleiner (2021) structural-falsification result to show the difficulty is *general to consciousness science* (IIT was called pseudoscience by 124 scholars) without hiding behind it. This is the cluster's credibility engine.
