## Admission Decision: ADMITTED (load-bearing)

The vestibular case rises to synthesis-bar load-bearing status. It is **not** a
seventh restatement of the division, and it is **structurally distinct** from
interoception's contribution:

- **Interoception** inverts the apex's headline asymmetry on *both* terms (brain
  side converges on the insula; mind side becomes affect-and-ownership). It is the
  *inward* exception that gives the asymmetry its scope condition.
- **Vestibular** does **not** invert the asymmetry — it drives it to its **extreme**.
  Maximal brain-side labour (no unisensory vestibular cortex at all —
  Pfeiffer/Serino/Blanke 2014; multisensory by construction), minimal felt object
  (de Vignemont 2024 "paucity of vestibular experiences"), yet it supplies the
  *spatial frame* of the subject.

The load-bearing payload: vestibular shows the apex's "ownership is uneven across
the senses" gradient runs on **two axes, not one**. Smell is faint-object **and**
faint-frame; vestibular is faint-object **yet foundational-frame**. This turns the
apex's existing "differently-weighted family" hedge into an actual structure — the
mind-side term resolves into a family of distinct contributions: object-unity
(touch, hearing), owned affect (interoception), and the situatedness of the
viewpoint (vestibular). The viewpoint-frame is a member the object-centred senses
never isolate (in them it is always fused with a felt object), so only the vestibular
extreme exposes it.
