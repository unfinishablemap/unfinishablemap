## Critiques by Philosopher

### The Eliminative Materialist
The whole apparatus — "trying," "volition," "the agent as substance" — is folk-psychological scaffolding you have promoted to metaphysics. Ward's 2024 dispositional analysis of non-deviance, which you cite approvingly, is precisely the kind of naturalistic story that dissolves your "basic act of trying" into ordinary dispositional causation with no residue for a non-physical subject to occupy. You cite the tool that undercuts you.

### The Hard-Nosed Physicalist
Your case for agent causation rests entirely on the phenomenology of "settling" — the felt sense that *I* originate the act. But that is exactly the introspective report a sophisticated event-causal story predicts we would issue whether or not it is veridical. You concede in the final section that first-person certainty about the causal route is unavailable (the agency-void circularity). Grant that, and the "something more" the disappearing-agent objection supposedly demands is something you have just admitted you cannot detect. The posit does no work introspection could confirm.

### The Quantum Skeptic
"The trying biases which neural outcome becomes actual" — where is the calculation? Decoherence timescales in warm wet neural tissue are femtoseconds. The article asserts a quantum-margin selection mechanism as the contact point but does no physics; it is the site's standing IOU, and this article draws on it without discharging it.

### The Many-Worlds Defender
"Selection among outcomes physics leaves open" presupposes exactly one outcome becomes actual — i.e. collapse. That is a substantive interpretive commitment smuggled in as neutral phrasing. On a no-collapse reading there is nothing to "select"; all outcomes occur. The article's own Tenet 4 owns this, but the action-theory argument here leans on the single-outcome picture without flagging that it is doing so.

### The Empiricist
"Biasing... without any detectable violation of physical law" is unfalsifiability stated as a virtue. If the volitional selection is by construction undetectable, no experiment distinguishes your volitionist-agent-causal hybrid from pure event-causalism. The article's *philosophical* discriminator (answering the disappearing agent) is not an *empirical* one, and the piece never says so plainly.

### The Buddhist Philosopher
The "persisting subject who exercises causal powers" that answers your disappearing-agent objection is the very self Nagarjuna deconstructs. You solve a problem in action theory by reifying precisely what should be seen as empty. The disappearing agent is not a bug to be patched; it is a glimpse of anatta.

- **Severity**: Medium (factual, isolated to this file — corpus grep found no other occurrence)

### Issue 2: Deviance is reintroduced at the psychophysical selection interface
- **File**: [topics/philosophy-of-action-under-dualism.md](/topics/philosophy-of-action-under-dualism/)
- **Location**: Line 96 ("less room for a 'wrong route' to open... the trying is not a distinct earlier event waiting to misfire") in tension with line 92 (the trying "biases which neural outcome becomes actual").
- **Problem**: The article's stated reason volitionism softens deviance is that the trying is not a *distinct earlier event* that must then cause the movement. But selection-only interactionism posits exactly such a downstream step: the trying biases a *separate* neural/quantum outcome. That is a causal route, and a route can go wrong — the biasing could land on a parallel outcome not the intended one. So the deviance gap is not removed, it is relocated to the mind→quantum-selection junction. The article half-sees this (it invokes the agency-void verification circularity at line 96), but it *asserts* "less room" for a wrong route while its own mechanism opens a new one. The two claims are in unacknowledged tension.
- **Severity**: Medium
- **Recommendation**: `refine-draft` — either weaken the "less room for a wrong route" claim, or explicitly state that deviance is *relocated* (mind→selection) rather than *reduced*, and that the agency-void circularity is the honest residue of that relocation.

### Issue 3: The hybrid's regress — does "agent causes a trying" preserve the agent-causal answer?
- **File**: [topics/philosophy-of-action-under-dualism.md](/topics/philosophy-of-action-under-dualism/)
- **Location**: Line 76 / line 94 (the Lowe hybrid: "the agent originates; what she originates is a volition").
- **Problem**: The disappearing-agent objection is answered (line 72, 90) by making the agent a genuine relatum — an originating substance-cause. But the hybrid says what the agent directly causes is *a trying* (an event/volition). Pereboom's objection can be re-pressed one level up: is "the agent causes the trying" itself substance-causal or event-causal? If the trying is an event with its own antecedents, the agent risks disappearing into the causation *of the trying*. Lowe's answer (all causation is ultimately substance causation) is *named* but not *defended* here — the article reports Lowe's move rather than showing why the regress stops. A critic gets a free re-run of the original objection.
- **Severity**: Low–Medium
- **Recommendation**: `refine-draft` — add one or two sentences discharging the regress (why substance-causation of the trying is not just relabeled event-causation), or explicitly mark it as a load the linked [agent-causation](/concepts/agent-causation/) article carries.

## Counterarguments to Address

### Undetectability vs. the disappearing-agent payoff
- **Current content says**: The dualist self supplies the "something more" the disappearing-agent objection finds missing (line 90); separately, the subject cannot verify from the inside that its trying produced the movement (line 96).
- **A critic would argue**: These sit in tension. If the agent's extra contribution is real but in-principle unverifiable first-personally *and* undetectable third-personally (line 92), then it answers a *philosophical* objection with a posit no vantage point can confirm — which is close to the "too thin"/"idle" charge the article says dualism escapes.
- **Suggested response**: Distinguish explicitly: the posit earns its keep as the best *explanation* of control, not as an *observable*; concede the epistemic cost openly rather than presenting undetectability only as a virtue (line 92).
