## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)

The Lakatosian concession at `topics/the-interface-problem` is Chalmers-grade discipline at the level Chalmers' methodology operates: *the framework's claims are calibrated to what the framework can actually deliver, with the structural cost of the position named explicitly*. Chalmers in *The Conscious Mind* repeatedly distinguishes between what dualism *commits one to* (the existence of psychophysical laws relating phenomenal to physical states) and what dualism *currently delivers* (substantively partial specification, not laws of the F=ma form). Chalmers calls this "the t-shirt problem" — a citation the article preserves verbatim at line 54: *"the absence of psychophysical laws compact and precise enough to fit on a t-shirt, as F=ma can."* The refine-draft's framing of Stapp's and Eccles' models as *proto-models, not laws* and the analogues of Tycho's measurements (bandwidth constraint, Born-rule causal-consistency, theta-band willed-attention signatures) as *constraint scaffolding from which such a law might one day be inferred* operates at exactly Chalmers' register. The honesty about position-stage matches the honesty Chalmers' programme requires of any interactionist text.

The "currently unfalsifiable in practice" replacement for "underdetermination, not unfalsifiability" is also Chalmers-grade. Chalmers' methodological commitment is that bedrock philosophical positions must be defensible at their own level rather than verbally re-described. The original phrasing *underdetermination, not unfalsifiability* gestured at a distinction that Popperian critics had reasonably objected to as too clever; the new phrasing *"currently unfalsifiable in practice, though whether this remains so depends on detection-threshold improvements rather than a structural feature of the framework"* concedes the descriptive accuracy of the Popperian charge while preserving the framework's actual commitment (improvable in principle, not architecturally insulated from empirical refutation). This is the kind of concession Chalmers' programme makes routinely — and that less mature dualist texts often refuse.
