- **"This is not X. It is Y." cliché** (M6): Multiple instances of the LLM cliché pattern flagged in the writing style guide. **Resolution**: Revised the most obvious instances—protention definition and surprise description—to use more natural constructions.

- **Passive/active tension** (M4): Protention described as passive openness (following Husserl) in the tripartite structure section, then as active reaching in the agency section, without acknowledging the interpretive departure. **Resolution**: Added explicit acknowledgment that Husserl described protention primarily as passive synthesis, and that the Map's active reading extends beyond the standard Husserlian account.

- **No-self challenge unaddressed** (M5): Article cited Varela and contemplative traditions without engaging with the Buddhist no-self (anattā) doctrine that these traditions are founded on. **Resolution**: Added acknowledgment of the tension between protentional phenomenology and the no-self challenge, noting the Map's position.

- **"Protention Is Not Prediction" vulnerable to functionalist reply** (M3): The section drew the distinction between phenomenological and computational accounts but did not defend it against the strongest functionalist objection (that sufficient complexity just would produce protention). **Resolution**: Added zombie-argument parallel—a functionally identical system could lack felt forward-directedness, suggesting protention names something functional description cannot guarantee.

- **No Many Worlds argument weak** (M7): Claimed protention "presupposes a single anticipated future" without acknowledging that a many-worlds defender can account for singular-seeming phenomenology within a branching framework. **Resolution**: Added acknowledgment of the MWI reply while clarifying the Map's position—the structure of experience is oriented toward actualisation rather than proliferation.
