## Executive Summary

This is a freshly-assembled applied-apex (`embodied-interface`) sitting on top of seven per-modality source articles, and its defining strength is *disciplined under-claim*. The apex builds a three-way distinction — causal / constitutive / phenomenal-presentation consciousness — that the per-modality articles imply but none had stated, and uses it to shrink the Map's interface claim rather than expand it: most of felt embodiment is named as causally passive *presentation*, and that under-claim is argued to be the framework working, not failing. The Process Philosopher and the Hardline Empiricist — the two personas this skill holds in tension — converge in praise here, which by the skill's own criterion signals that the cluster's one genuine slippage-risk (the constitutive category) has been resolved honestly rather than papered over: the constitutive claim is pinned to position **P-VS1 at *low* confidence** with the value-blind rival explicitly named as "the rival to beat, not a position already beaten." ## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)

Chalmers gets his strongest single-article showcase in `pain-consciousness-and-causal-power`, which is the hard problem "made personal": the [knowledge-argument](/concepts/knowledge-argument/) is deployed with unusual force (the suppressed-nociceptor neuroscientist learns "a phenomenal fact about the felt quality of suffering"), and the apex then refuses to let that force inflate the causal claim — the explanatory gap "holds across presentation, constitution, and selection alike," yet "irreducibility does not entail causal activity: an experience can be genuinely phenomenal, genuinely owned, and causally idle." A property dualist reads this as the right separation: the gap is real everywhere, but realness of phenomenal character is not silently converted into causal work. That concession — "what keeps the dualism honest rather than triumphalist" — is exactly the move Chalmers's own naturalistic dualism makes when it concedes epiphenomenalism-proximity rather than declaring victory.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)

The interface here is the *embodiment-level* expression of minimal quantum interaction, and Stapp would approve of how narrowly it is drawn. The apex ties the causal category to selection among "quantum-underdetermined neural outcomes" and immediately books the **mechanism debt**: P-Q3's bias-without-deviation dilemma (held *moderate*) is stated squarely — genuine selection must do something the unbiased Born distribution does not, yet must not deviate from Born statistics to stay minimal, leaving even the clearest case "genuinely close to epiphenomenalism" until a positive account lands, and there is no worked toy model (P-Q10). A serious quantum-mind theorist wants the coupling problem named, not hidden; here it is named as the reason the causal category is "the category the Map most wants and least securely possesses." The "smallest causal footprint compatible with genuine agency, leaving the vast presented body outside the corridor entirely" is minimality taken seriously at the level of lived experience.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)

First-person experience is the explanandum throughout, and the cluster's central achievement is phenomenological: it carves the felt body by *what it is like* rather than by neural plumbing. The vestibular case is the cleanest Nagelian exhibit — a sense with "almost no felt object" that nonetheless "supplies the felt spatial frame of the self," load-bearing yet barely appearing, its role visible only when it fails in depersonalization and the out-of-body experience. Interoception is centred as "the affective ground of subjecthood itself" rather than "a detached object delivered to a subject." This is exactly Nagel's instinct — that the subjective point of view is the thing to be explained — operationalised into a taxonomy, and the apex preserves it by refusing to call the resting frame "causal" merely because the self is located within it.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)

Whitehead would welcome the cluster's avoidance of crude substance-dualism: the felt body is not a passive lump animated by a ghost but a layered, bidirectional field of channels (the `somatic-interface`) within which a narrow searchlight occasionally moves. "Presentation is the condition of selection, not an instance of it" is a process-philosophical sentence — the standing felt field is constitutive of, not separate from, the moments of causal activity that arise within it. The "experience all the way down" intuition is honoured by treating the vast unattended body as genuinely *experienced* (owned, present) rather than as mere mechanism. **Crucially, and in line with this skill's evidential-status discipline, none of this Whitehead-friendliness is used to upgrade anything**: process-philosophical resonance makes the picture coherent, but the apex does not lean on it to push the constitutive category up the evidential scale. That restraint is what lets the next persona praise the same passages.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)

Agency is taken seriously and *calibrated*, which is the harder discipline. The causal category is exactly Kane's territory — "the effortful holding of a difficult intention, the willed continuation of an action against fatigue" — and the [phenomenology of consciousness doing work](/apex/phenomenology-of-consciousness-doing-work/) (effort, selection, holding) marks its felt signature. But the apex holds agent-causal libertarian free will at P-A1's *moderate* confidence ("the best explanation of convergent evidence rather than a proven result") and adds the introspective-defeater honesty Kane's view needs: by P-A4 (*high* confidence) "no agent can verify its own causal efficacy by introspection, because the checking faculty is the one whose status is in question." A libertarian reads this as agency defended without self-congratulation — the felt sense that "my attending made the difference" is correctly flagged as "not self-certifying."

### The Mysterian (McGinn)

Epistemic humility is structural here, not decorative. The whole apex is framed as a *framework-internal map* that "tells the reader how to apply the Map's commitments consistently, not that those commitments are confirmed," and the "Cascade and Scope" section spells out exactly which future results (raising P-VS1, retiring P-A1, resolving P-Q3) would redraw the map. McGinn would appreciate that the deepest claim — whether the constitutive layer is "causally load-bearing or causally idle" — is explicitly left "undecided," with the low confidence on P-VS1 named as "the calibrated mark of that." This is humility about what is currently knowable, embedded in the position-confidence machinery rather than asserted as a closing flourish.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)

This persona is load-bearing for this review, because the cluster's one real slippage-risk is the **constitutive category** built on pain and `evaluative-phenomenal-character`. The temptation was available and the apex declined it: it would have been easy to argue from pain's asymbolia dissociation ("the felt severity, not the magnitude of tissue damage, predicts the sustained response") directly to "felt valence does causal work," upgrading the constitutive layer to a settled causal result. Instead the apex performs the praise-worthy *non-upgrade* — it names the move and refuses it: "That the awfulness is inseparable *in experience* from the urge to withdraw is phenomenologically secure. That the awfulness therefore does causal *work* the underlying neural process does not already do is exactly the value-blind/value-sensitive fork, and the fork is open." It pins this to **P-VS1 at *low* confidence**, quoting the register's own self-discipline that value-sensitive selection is "an aspiration, not a position," and the value-blind rival is "the rival to beat, not a position already beaten." This is textbook *tenet-coherent-not-evidence-elevating*: the phenomenological inseparability is reported at the tier it earns (secure description) and the causal-upgrade is held at the tier the evidence supports (low). The clinical filter in implication #4 is the same discipline — the materialist account "explains the clinical data completely," and the interface reading "offers an alternative metaphysical interpretation of the same data," so asymbolia/phantom-limb/vestibular cases "belong to presentation and to the open constitutive question, not to demonstrated selection." Birch's verdict: this is the rare article whose *headline contribution is a calibrated under-claim*, and where the Process Philosopher's resonance and evidential discipline reinforce rather than fight. The two personas converging on the same passages (§"Constitutive consciousness", §"What This Implies for Decisions" #2 and #5) is the signal the slippage was resolved, not buried.

## Content Strengths

- **Strongest point**: The three-way causal taxonomy (causal / constitutive / phenomenal-presentation) is a genuinely new axis — the per-modality articles tracked *where the brain/mind division recurs*; this asks *what consciousness is doing* in each region and names a distinction "the per-modality articles imply but none states." It does real synthetic work rather than restating sources.
- **Notable quote**: "An interface that selects has to be fed something to select among and from somewhere to select; the presented body is the standing field within which the narrow searchlight of causal consciousness occasionally moves. Presentation is the condition of selection, not an instance of it."
- **Why it works**: It earns its "applied apex" label — five concrete decision-implications (don't infer causal participation from vividness; hold the claim at its positions' confidence; disaggregate before AI-consciousness verdicts; filter clinical claims; treat the under-claim as the framework working), each tied to a cited position. Meets the applied-apex bar (≥3 positions cited: P-VS1, P-Q3, P-CS4, P-A1; explicit "What This Implies for Decisions" section).

### topics/pain-consciousness-and-causal-power.md
- **Strongest point**: Asymbolia is deployed as "the single most discriminating empirical test case in philosophy of mind," and the theory-by-theory section (functionalism, HOT, GWT, IIT, representationalism) shows each theory can *redescribe* the dissociation but none can explain "why the presence or absence of a particular neural connection determines whether nociceptive processing *hurts*." That is the explanatory gap brought to clinical focus.
- **Notable quote**: "When pain hurts, the hurting *is* what drives avoidance."
- **Why it works**: It distinguishes pain from suffering with real phenomenological care (suffering-without-pain via grief/dread/moral anguish; pain-without-suffering via asymbolia), and the deep-review's honesty notes ("the data do not adjudicate between the Map's layered reading and the Buddhist one") keep the strongest article in the cluster from over-reaching.

### concepts/evaluative-phenomenal-character.md
- **Strongest point**: The cross-traditional convergence argument (Buddhist *vedanā*, Neo-Confucian *liángzhī*, Indian *rasa*, Mulla Sadra's knowledge-by-presence) is used carefully — convergence "demands explanation beyond shared cognitive bias" without being claimed to *prove* realism — and the C-tactile touch case supplies a "worked dissociable case" for constitutive valence rather than an assertion.
- **Notable quote**: "Pain is bad not because we judge it bad but because badness is constitutive of what pain feels like."
- **Why it works**: The "What Would Challenge This View" section lists five concrete defeaters (value-experience dissociation, illusionism's empirical vindication, contemplative construction-findings, moral-qualia epiphenomenalism, cross-cultural divergence) — falsifiability conditions stated plainly, which is the calibration `evaluative-phenomenal-character` lends *upward* to the apex's constitutive category.
