## Executive Summary

The perception/interface cluster is among the strongest, most calibration-disciplined material on the Map. Its defining strength is a sustained, explicit distinction between *accommodation* and *demonstration*: every modality piece states that the phenomena "do not settle dualism" and that the physicalist has a credible account, locating the Map's actual leverage in the felt-from-inside residue the rivals relocate but do not dissolve. The clearest expansion opportunity is to complete the modality series — vision, audition, and chemosensation are now developed; tactile/somatosensory (and proprioceptive/vestibular) consciousness is named as a gap by three articles but not written.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The cluster takes the explanatory gap as its load-bearing engine without overplaying it. `chemosensory-consciousness-and-the-interface` is the sharpest instance: carvone enantiomers (one molecule, mirror-flipped, spearmint vs. caraway) and the combinatorial receptor code give a clean case that perceived quality is not read off the stimulus — yet the article explicitly concedes "the residue is not a mechanism gap but the explanatory gap," precisely Chalmers' move of granting the easy problems while preserving the hard one. The inverted-qualia handling in `consciousness-as-perceptual-architect` ("two systems could implement identical constancy functions ... yet differ in what yellow feels like") is textbook hard-problem reasoning.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
`consciousness-selecting-neural-patterns` engages the physics-consciousness interface seriously and *honestly*: it presents Stapp's Zeno mechanism but immediately flags the ten-order-of-magnitude timescale gap (attention ~10⁻¹ s vs. femtosecond decoherence) and reframes the framework as "where consciousness might intervene rather than how." The post-decoherence/improper-mixture (d'Espagnat) move is a genuinely substantive contribution — it sidesteps the Tegmark decoherence objection while openly conceding the trade buys coherence-immunity at the cost of an amplification/specificity burden. Stapp would recognise his program treated as a live research direction with its open problems named, not as settled doctrine.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
Perspectival character and "what it's like" are centred throughout. `auditory-consciousness-and-the-interface` makes a genuinely original observation: Nagel reached for an *auditory* creature (the echolocating bat) precisely because the felt character of a heard quality is most easily isolated from any external object-property — pitch and timbre have "no external home," unlike colour-as-reflectance. This is Nagel's point sharpened, not merely cited.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The cluster avoids crude substance dualism: the Map's stated position is a "hybrid indirect realism" where the interface is "neither private theatre nor unmediated contact, but the experiential face of an interaction across the divide." Perception as active constitution (constancy, learning, the perceptual ratchet) treats mind and world as co-constituting rather than as two inert substances bumping together. Crucially — and per the evidential-status discipline — none of this process-friendly framing is used to *upgrade* any evidential claim; it remains avoidance of crude dualism, not an engine of over-claiming (see Hardline Empiricist below, with whom this praise converges rather than conflicts here).

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
`consciousness-selecting-neural-patterns` takes agency seriously and meets the luck objection head-on: selection is distinguished from randomness via reasons-guidance, the phenomenology of effort, and coherence-over-time, while explicitly conceding "this doesn't fully resolve the luck objection, which remains a live question." Indeterminacy is treated as making room for agency without being mistaken *for* agency — exactly the discipline Kane's own program requires.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
Epistemic humility is pervasive and structural, not decorative. `consciousness-selecting-neural-patterns` carries an explicit "Falsifiability Dilemma" section conceding selection within Born probabilities is undetectable by quantum experiment, and a "What Would Challenge This View?" section listing four concrete defeaters. The closing line — "a metaphysical interpretation ... that becomes attractive if independent arguments for mental causation are found persuasive" — is precisely the bounded, non-triumphalist register McGinn prizes.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This is the cluster's signature strength and the persona's verdict is strongly positive. The articles repeatedly do the praise-worthy thing of *not* converting tenet-coherence into an evidential upgrade:

- `perceptual-failure-and-the-interface` runs all four bandwidth signatures against predictive processing and concludes "predictive processing does not stumble on the pattern; it predicts it" — refusing to claim the signatures as dualist evidence. It then meets the common-cause-null objection by name: "the count of four then collapses to one mechanism observed four ways, and the extra ontology buys nothing the signatures demand."
- The same article applies the Occam's-Razor-Has-Limits tenet *against the Map's own argument*: "The limit binds the Map's own argument too: it licenses the extra ontology only where the physical story leaves something genuinely unexplained ... On the perceptual signatures the data are merely compatible; on phenomenal presence they are not." This is exactly the "tenet removes a defeater but does not upgrade evidential status" discipline, self-applied.
- `auditory-` and `chemosensory-` both carry a "framed throughout as phenomena the dualist/interface reading *accommodates*, not as evidence that settles the dispute" disclaimer, and re-state it per-tenet ("interpretive alignment, not a finding"). The psychedelic/filter case in `perceptual-failure-` is explicitly demoted: "it belongs in the suggestive column, not the decisive one," flagging vascular-confound contamination of the blood-flow data and the *increase* in signal diversity a production theorist can read as more generative activity.
- `naturalist-relationalism` is the model of honest rival-engagement: it concedes relationalism is "thoroughly naturalist," "not a naive direct realism," a "working research programme," even "the simpler theory" — and refuses to win by parsimony ("The case rests on the explanatory residue, not on parsimony in either direction"). It cites a *naturalist-internal* attack (Byrne & Green) rather than importing dualist premises.

No tenet-as-evidence-upgrade slippage was found in this cluster. The Process Philosopher and Hardline Empiricist personas converge here rather than conflict — the process-friendly framing and the evidential discipline are praising different, compatible aspects of genuinely calibrated articles.

## Content Strengths

### perceptual-failure-and-the-interface.md
- **Strongest point**: The "Predictive-Processing Objection" section, which names predictive processing as "the framework that co-opts this article's evidence base most directly," concedes it accommodates all four bandwidth signatures, and locates the disagreement "one level down, at the hard problem." This is how to engage one's strongest rival.
- **Notable quote**: "The conditions under which perception fails to track the external world are exactly the conditions under which it succeeds in revealing the nature of experience itself." (the blur paradox, crisply stated)
- **Why it works**: It earns the interface posit by paying the parsimony bill rather than waving it away with the tenet-5 slogan.

### naturalist-relationalism.md
- **Strongest point**: Framing the position as "dangerous to the Map's inference rather than merely opposed to it" — relationalism is the clean demonstration that transparency/directness data *underdetermine* dualism, which forces the Map to make the narrower, harder claim. Treating a rival as sharpening one's own argument is rare and valuable.
- **Notable quote**: "relationalism has changed the address of the difficulty without paying it off."
- **Why it works**: Cite-as-rival firewall is explicit and disciplined; disjunctivism, the Phenomenal Principle, Brewer's objective looks, and the causal/screening-off arguments are all handled at the level of the actual literature.

### chemosensory-consciousness-and-the-interface.md
- **Strongest point**: It gives the Map's recurring sommelier/Barolo example its long-missing modality home, and presses the explanatory gap "in a register vision shaped no theory to expect" — no spatial scaffolding, no tidy quality-space geometry (hyperbolic odour space), and a thalamus-bypassing route to affect — while conceding each is a physicalist mechanism the Map adopts.
- **Notable quote**: "There is no place where the smell is, no axis along which it sits."
- **Why it works**: It does not over-reach on taste, explicitly noting gustation's gap is "genuine but narrower" and flagging the labeled-line vs. across-fibre dispute as unsettled.
