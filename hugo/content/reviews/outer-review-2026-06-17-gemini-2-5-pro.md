# Referee Report: The Synesthetic Void

## I. Introduction and Overview of Epistemic Failures

The manuscript under review, "The Synesthetic Void," attempts to weaponize the neurological phenomenon of synesthesia against mainstream physicalist, representationalist, and functionalist accounts of consciousness. By framing synesthesia as an "intra-species phenomenal void," the author posits that the human perceptual baseline is structurally heterogeneous, thereby rendering the traditional "other minds" problem not merely a theoretical puzzle, but a concrete, empirically accessible reality. To achieve this ambitious philosophical restructuring, the manuscript constructs a narrative wherein the existence of "extra qualia," the permeability of architectural boundaries, and the reality of developmental occlusions collectively serve as indubitable evidence for property dualism or non-reductive frameworks. Ultimately, the manuscript claims that functionalist and representationalist paradigms are fundamentally incapable of accommodating the phenomenological realities of the synesthetic experience.

While the ambition to bridge empirical neuroscience with the philosophy of mind is appropriate for this journal, the execution within this manuscript is critically compromised by a severe methodological anachronism and a pervasive, almost strategic, reliance on superseded cognitive neuroscience. The author attempts to dismantle contemporary, highly sophisticated philosophy of mind using empirical data and conceptual frameworks sourced almost exclusively from the late 1990s and early 2000s—relying heavily on Wager (1999), Gray (2003), Merabet (2004), Maurer (2005), and MacPherson (2007). When the manuscript does attempt to address more recent literature, it selectively misreads or grossly oversimplifies the data to fit a pre-ordained ideological scaffolding.

Consequently, the manuscript operates in a profound epistemic vacuum. It completely ignores the rigorous neurobiological, pupillometric, psychopharmacological, and computational advancements of the 2020–2025 period that have systematically dismantled the very premises upon which the author's anti-physicalist arguments rely. Furthermore, the manuscript explicitly abandons philosophical rigor in favor of what it terms "tenet-bracketed" framing. The text openly admits that physicalist accounts can read the cited neurodevelopmental data without remainder, yet deliberately opts for a dualist "filter theory" interpretation solely to maintain consilience with the author's broader, *a priori* commitments. Such an approach transforms the manuscript from a rigorous academic inquiry into a piece of ideological boundary maintenance.

The ensuing audit evaluates the manuscript across four specific dimensions: the empirical accuracy of its claims regarding the permeability of the synesthetic void (specifically regarding training-induced and pharmacological synesthesia); its reliance on superseded or challenged citations; its substitution of tenet-based framing for logical argumentation; and its alarming failure to engage with formidable contemporary counter-frameworks, such as soft illusionism and predictive processing.

## II. The Empirical Collapse of the Permeability Argument

A central pillar of the manuscript's thesis is the assertion that the "synesthetic void" possesses a semi-permeable architectural boundary. To substantiate this, the author relies heavily on two specific empirical claims: first, that adaptive memory training can induce genuine synesthetic experiences in non-synesthetes; and second, that pharmacological interventions (specifically serotonergic agonists) allow neurotypical individuals to cross the boundary into the exact synesthetic region of "experience-space" (E-space). Both claims are demonstrably false when evaluated against the literature of the current decade. The manuscript's failure to recognize the divergence between genuine phenomenology and cognitive emulation fatally undermines its thesis.

### The Falsification of Training-Induced Synesthesia via Pupillometry

The author cites Bor et al. (2014) to argue that non-synesthetic adults can acquire subjective synesthetic experiences through intensive training, noting that these effects fade within three months of the training's cessation. The manuscript interprets this transience as profound philosophical evidence of an "architectural constraint reasserting the narrower configuration" of standard human perception. This interpretation, which forms the basis for the author's argument regarding the active maintenance of the void, rests entirely on the assumption that the training induced genuine phenomenal qualia in the first place, rather than mere associative cognitive learning or demand characteristics.

Recent advancements in physiological biometrics have decisively resolved this ambiguity, entirely undermining the manuscript's premise. The application of high-resolution pupillometry in the mid-2020s has established an objective, autonomic physiological biomarker for genuine synesthetic phenomenology. Strauch, Leenaars, and Rouw (2025/2026) demonstrated that in congenital grapheme-color synesthetes, pupil size autonomically adjusts to the brightness of the internally generated synesthetic color, even when the physical luminance of the visual stimulus remains entirely constant. Specifically, the pupils of genuine synesthetes constrict when perceiving a "bright" synesthetic color and dilate for a "dark" synesthetic color, with these responses emerging rapidly (around 870 ms)—too fast to be the product of conscious mental imagery, yet delayed relative to real physical light processing (330 ms). Because this pupillary light reflex is autonomically driven by the perceptual quality of the experience, it cannot be faked, simulated, or replicated through top-down cognitive effort.

Crucially, when control subjects who have been trained to generate color associations—the exact demographic studied by Bor et al.—are subjected to the same pupillometry paradigms, they exhibit an entirely different physiological signature. Instead of brightness-coupled pupillary constriction, trained controls exhibit effort-linked pupillary dilation, which is the standard autonomic marker of high cognitive load and memory retrieval. The pupil data proves definitively that the behavioral outputs of trained controls do not arise from genuine perceptual cross-activation; they arise from effortful associative recall.

The manuscript's claim that training briefly allows subjects to "glimpse" the synesthetic void is therefore physiologically and empirically false. The boundary is not architecturally "reasserting" itself after three months; the phenomenal boundary was never crossed. The fade observed by Bor et al. (2014) is nothing more than the predictable decay of a conditioned associative memory that has not been rehearsed. By completely omitting the 2025 pupillometry literature that definitively separates genuine synesthesia from associative learning, the manuscript builds profound metaphysical conclusions on a foundation of debunked behavioral artifacts.

### The Erroneous Conflation of Congenital and Pharmacological Phenomenology

Equally devastating is the manuscript's treatment of drug-induced synesthesia. The author claims that serotonergic agonists such as LSD, psilocybin, and mescaline "reliably trigger synesthesia-like cross-modal experiences," suggesting these substances breach the same experiential void occupied by congenital synesthetes. While the author adds a minor caveat that these *may* represent different regions of E-space, the overarching philosophical argument relies heavily on a fundamental phenomenological and neurobiological parity between the two states, treating drugs as a "breach" of the architectural barrier.

Contemporary psychopharmacological research explicitly rejects this parity. The literature from 2020 to 2025 systematically distinguishes between the etiological mechanisms and the subjective phenomenology of congenital versus drug-induced cross-modal states. Genuine developmental synesthesia is characterized by rigid, lifelong, automatic, and highly specific inducer-concurrent pairings (e.g., the letter 'A' is invariably a specific shade of red). In stark contrast, psychedelic-induced synesthesia is highly fluid, non-specific, transient, and heavily dependent on the subject's emotional state and environmental context.

Research by Luke and Terhune (2020-2025) highlights that while classical psychedelics disrupt the Default Mode Network and induce widespread cortical hyperconnectivity via 5-HT2A receptor agonism, the resulting sensory blending fails to meet the accepted criteria for genuine synesthesia, specifically lacking consistency and inducer specificity. A drug-induced state in which a guitar chord produces a shifting kaleidoscope of geometric shapes bears no structural relationship to the deterministic, percept-like mapping of grapheme-color synesthesia. The two phenomena represent entirely different neurocomputational realities.

To illustrate the severity of this conflation, the following table summarizes the irreconcilable differences between the two phenomena as established by contemporary cognitive neuroscience, none of which are acknowledged by the manuscript:

| Phenomenological Metric | Congenital / Developmental Synesthesia | Pharmacological (Psychedelic-Induced) Synesthesia |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Consistency over time** | Exceptionally high; pairings remain stable for a lifetime, immune to context. | Non-existent; pairings are fleeting, chaotic, and rarely repeat. |
| **Inducer Specificity** | Precise mapping (e.g., a specific phoneme triggers a highly specific hue). | Diffuse mapping; sensory overlap is generalized and holistic. |
| **Automaticity** | Involuntary, effortless, and unavoidable (Stroop-like interference). | Highly dependent on emotional state, dosage, and environmental setting. |
| **Physiological Signature** | Mirrors actual luminance perception (brightness-coupled pupillary reflex). | Unmapped for brightness coupling; typically overwhelmed by drug-induced mydriasis. |
| **Underlying Mechanism** | Localized hyperconnectivity or targeted disinhibited feedback (e.g., V4 and VWFA). | Global disruption of the Default Mode Network; massive reduction in top-down filtering. |

By treating pharmacological hallucinations as a temporary visa into the synesthete's highly specific phenomenological reality, the manuscript betrays a profound misunderstanding of both the neurochemistry of psychedelics and the specific, highly localized nature of congenital synesthetic binding.

## III. The Misappropriation of E-Space and Semantic Mediation

The manuscript attempts to elevate its argument by deploying the concept of "experience-space" (E-space) as proposed by Lacalli (2022), using it to map the so-called synesthetic void. The author argues that E-space proves human consciousness occupies only a small region of all possible qualia, treating synesthesia as empirical proof of "unexploited dimensions" of phenomenal reality. However, the manuscript strips Lacalli's concept of its rigorous mathematical and evolutionary biological context, reducing it to a playground for property dualism.

Lacalli's work, alongside subsequent formalizations by Williams (2025), does not treat E-space as a mystical repository of floating qualia. Rather, it approaches the origins and evolution of qualia through rigorous information-theoretic measures. Williams (2025) establishes quantitative methods for investigating the evolution of qualia by deriving the Qualitative Selection Limit (QSL) using the Price Equation. This formal constraint evaluates whether natural selection on a physical structural system can be effectively transmitted into a qualitative domain. The evolution of consciousness, in this view, is strictly bounded by the fidelity of mapping between structural states (S) and qualitative states (Q) in the face of mutational noise. By ignoring the mathematical bounds, the Price Equation, and the QSL constraints that define the very E-space literature it cites, the manuscript engages in an egregious form of theoretical cherry-picking. It borrows the vocabulary of evolutionary neuroscience while deliberately ignoring its materialist, structuralist syntax.

### The Misinterpretation of Semantic Mediation

This theoretical misappropriation extends to the manuscript's handling of van Leeuwen et al. (2015). The author cites the finding that the majority of synesthetic experiences are "mediated through semantics"—meaning that the conceptual understanding of the inducer, rather than its low-level visual or auditory geometry, triggers the concurrent color. The manuscript astonishingly wields this as evidence for a non-physicalist "bidirectional interaction," arguing that because "meaning shapes qualia," top-down semantic influence defeats strictly bottom-up physicalist accounts of consciousness.

This argument demonstrates a glaring ignorance of the past decade of cognitive science, specifically the ascendancy of predictive processing and active inference models. Contemporary physicalist frameworks do not posit a strictly "bottom-up" brain. Predictive coding explicitly models the brain as a hierarchical inference machine where high-level semantic priors cascade downwards to constrain and shape low-level sensory prediction errors. Semantic mediation in synesthesia is not a metaphysical anomaly requiring "bidirectional" dualist tenets; it is precisely what a predictive processing model predicts when high-level semantic nodes (e.g., the concept of the number "7") have anomalous, heavily weighted prior connections to sensory nodes (e.g., the color yellow).

In a 2026 framework for representationalism derived from deep neural networks—the Classifier × Generator × Discriminator (C × G × D) model—synesthesia is perfectly accommodated as a physical, top-down process. The Classifier extracts the semantic feature, the Generator synthesizes an internal representation based on those learned or hardwired priors (the color), and the Discriminator integrates these into the global workspace. The "extra qualia" are simply the output of an anomalous Generator parameter driven by top-down semantic priors. There is no violation of physicalism here. By treating semantic mediation as a philosophical mystery rather than a well-understood mechanism of top-down neural processing, the manuscript betrays its detachment from modern neurocomputational theory.

## IV. The Simplistic Dualism of Developmental Occlusion

The manuscript's deployment of the "neonatal synesthesia hypothesis" as evidence for a developmental occlusion represents another instance of twisting literature to support a preconceived dualist narrative. Citing Maurer and Mondloch (2005) and Spector and Maurer (2009), the author argues that infants possess a hyperconnected sensorium that is subsequently dismantled by neural pruning, thereby trapping adults in a narrowed experiential remnant. The author leverages this to suggest that the developing brain actively *constrains* a broader consciousness, framing synaptic pruning through the lens of a dualistic "filter theory" inspired by Aldous Huxley and Henri Bergson.

While perceptual narrowing and synaptic pruning are well-documented neurodevelopmental realities, the manuscript's theoretical extrapolation ignores over a decade of progress in developmental biology and neuroimmunology. First, the simplistic model of experience-dependent synaptic pruning has been heavily refined. Current literature emphasizes the role of the immune system, specifically the complement cascade and microglial activity, in tagging and eliminating redundant synapses during early postnatal life to ensure metabolic efficiency and signal-to-noise optimization. Disruptions in this specific immunological pruning process are directly implicated in autism spectrum disorders (ASD) and schizophrenia, conditions which exhibit a known comorbidity with synesthesia. By ignoring the immunological, homeostatic, and genetic mechanisms driving synaptic retraction, the manuscript artificially isolates the phenomenon to fit a philosophical narrative about consciousness being "filtered" by matter, completely bypassing the highly specific, physically mandated functions that pruning serves for the survival of the organism.

Furthermore, alternative hypotheses emerging in the mid-2020s offer robust physicalist explanations for neonatal hyperconnectivity that directly contradict the author's filter theory. The "Birth Echo Hypothesis" advanced by Barker and Schimmelpfennig (2025) posits that massive endogenous surges of N,N-Dimethyltryptamine (DMT) during the narrowly defined perinatal window act via 5-HT2A and sigma-1 pathways to drive large-scale neural plasticity, sensory integration, and neuroprotection against hypoxic-hypercapnic stress during birth. Under this model, the "synesthetic" or highly integrated state of the neonatal brain is a transient, neurochemically driven survival mechanism designed to weather the trauma of parturition, not an unconstrained, baseline access to a broader "E-space" that is subsequently filtered.

The subsequent narrowing of perception is not the tragic occlusion of a pre-existing dualistic consciousness, but the normal downregulation of an extreme, temporary neuropharmacological event. The manuscript's failure to engage with the actual biochemical drivers of early developmental hyperconnectivity renders its dualistic filter theory hopelessly abstract and scientifically decoupled.

## V. The Anti-Representationalist Strawman and the Evasion of Illusionism

The manuscript's philosophical centerpiece is a challenge to representationalism and functionalism, leaning heavily on Wager (1999) to introduce the "extra qualia problem" and Gray (2003) to point out the dissociation between qualia and function. The argument is formulated as follows: both a synesthete and a non-synesthete view the exact same physical stimulus (a black letter on a white page). Because the synesthete experiences an additional layer of color, the manuscript concludes that identical representational content yields divergent phenomenal character, thereby fatally wounding representationalist theories of mind.

This argument succeeds only by attacking a strawman version of representationalism that has not been seriously defended in cognitive science for over two decades. The manuscript erroneously equates "representational content" strictly with the distal physical stimulus (the black ink on the page). Modern representationalism, deeply integrated with the aforementioned predictive processing, active inference, and deep neural network architectures, locates representation not in the external object, but in the brain's internal, hierarchical generative models. The synesthete and the non-synesthete do *not* have identical representational content, because their internal generative models possess fundamentally different priors and cross-activations. The manuscript's failure to update its definition of representation past the year 1999 invalidates its primary philosophical critique.

### The Deliberate Evasion of Soft Illusionism

Even more problematic is the manuscript's treatment of functionalism and its defense of the so-called "explanatory gap." The author relies on MacPherson's (2007) identification of the "extraordinary feature" of synesthesia—the phenomenological report that synesthetes experience two distinct colors occupying the exact same spatial location simultaneously without blending. The manuscript treats this logically and physically impossible description as an indubitable ontological truth, asserting that no physicalist model can account for it.

To maintain this stance, the manuscript executes a blunt refusal to engage with the most rigorous physicalist challenge of the current era: Illusionism. The author waves away Dennett-style approaches in a single, dismissive sentence, accusing "heterophenomenologists" of merely documenting what synesthetes are inclined to say rather than addressing their actual phenomenal states. By simply declaring that the article "proceeds on the realist reading," the author insulates the manuscript from critique rather than earning its philosophical position.

This evasion is unacceptable in a top-tier journal submission. The 2020s have seen the significant refinement of soft illusionism, most notably articulated by Frankish (2016) and structurally modeled by Graziano's Attention Schema Theory (AST) (2020-2026). Illusionism does not deny that people undergo experiences; it argues that phenomenal consciousness—the ineffable, non-physical "what it's like" property—is an introspective illusion generated by the brain's own simplified, structurally inaccurate models of its internal data processing.

Under Graziano's AST, the brain builds a schematic, highly compressed model of its own attentional mechanisms to efficiently control behavior. This model depicts attention as an ethereal essence or "experience" flowing into objects. When applied to synesthesia, illusionism dissolves the "extraordinary feature" championed by MacPherson. To a property dualist, the unblended dual occupancy of space is a metaphysical mystery. To an illusionist, it is simply a predictable quirk in the brain's self-reporting mechanism. The synesthete's brain is processing the grapheme shape in one neural network and the color concept in another (likely via disinhibited feedback or local cross-activation); the attentional schema attempts to integrate these disparate data streams and outputs a simplified, albeit physically impossible, self-report that two colors occupy the same space without blending.

The fact that the introspective model yields a logically or physically impossible description (unblended dual occupancy) is precisely what soft illusionism predicts: internal models are quick, dirty, and physically inaccurate. Chalmers' formulation of the "Meta-Problem of Consciousness"—explaining why we have the intuition that there is a hard problem in the first place—demands that any serious contemporary defense of qualia must explain why physicalist models of introspection are insufficient. The manuscript entirely sidesteps the meta-problem. By treating the synesthete's first-person report of an ineffable, non-physical "extra quale" as an incorrigible ontological truth, and ignoring the vast literature demonstrating that introspective systems are evolutionarily designed to misrepresent functional states, the manuscript leaves a massive, untested counter-framework looming over its entire thesis.

## VI. The Epistemological Failure of "Tenet-Bracketed" Framing

Perhaps the most egregious flaw in the manuscript is its overt reliance on ideological scaffolding over logical derivation. In section after section, the author admits that standard physicalism can adequately explain the phenomena in question, yet forces a non-reductive or dualistic interpretation to maintain alignment with a set of pre-defined project tenets (e.g., "Occam's Razor Has Limits," "No Many Worlds," "Dualism").

This intellectual gerrymandering reaches its apex in the discussion of developmental occlusion. The author explicitly states:

> *"Brain-only physicalism reads the same data without remainder: pruning produces narrower phenomenal patterns because phenomenal patterns are narrower neural patterns. The two readings are observationally equivalent on the cited evidence; the Map's filter-theory reading is preferred for its consilience with the broader tenet commitments rather than because the evidence forces it."*

This explicit admission disqualifies the manuscript as a piece of rigorous philosophical inquiry. If a physicalist identity theory can account for the empirical data "without remainder," Occam's Razor demands its adoption over a dualistic filter theory that posits unnecessary, untestable ontological entities (a separate consciousness that is "filtered" by matter). The author attempts to inoculate themselves against this by simply declaring a site tenet that "Occam's Razor Has Limits", but declaring a tenet is not equivalent to formulating a philosophical defense.

A peer-reviewed journal is not a venue for an author to exercise confirmation bias under the guise of "consilience with broader commitments." Philosophical arguments must be earned through logical necessity, theoretical elegance, and empirical pressure—none of which are achieved here. The manuscript treats the philosophy of mind not as a landscape of inquiry, but as a rigid architecture into which cherry-picked empirical data must be forcefully hammered.

## VII. Five Specific Weaknesses and Citational Deficits

The manuscript is patently unready for publication. It relies on falsified empirical paradigms, attacks antiquated philosophical strawmen, and openly substitutes ideological preference for logical rigor. The following five specific weaknesses demand either a complete, foundational rewrite of the manuscript's premises or outright rejection:

**1. Empirical Falsification of the Architectural Boundary via Pupillometry:**
The article relies heavily on Bor et al. (2014) to claim that intensive training allows non-synesthetes to temporarily "glimpse" the synesthetic void, positing an architectural boundary that subsequently reasserts itself. This requires the assumption that training induces true perceptual phenomenology. This is definitively falsified by recent pupillometry studies. Strauch, Leenaars, and Rouw established that genuine synesthetes exhibit an effortless, brightness-coupled pupillary light reflex to synesthetic colors, whereas control subjects trained to associate colors with graphemes (identical to Bor's methodology) exhibit effort-linked pupillary dilation indicative of cognitive recall, not perception. The boundary was never crossed by training.
Citation omitted/contradicted: Strauch, C., Leenaars, C., & Rouw, R. (2025/2026). "Pupil size reveals the perceptual quality and effortless nature of synesthesia." eLife / bioRxiv.

**2. Inaccurate Conflation of Pharmacological and Congenital Synesthesia:**
The manuscript claims that serotonergic agonists (LSD, psilocybin) trigger experiences that breach the synesthetic void, implying phenomenological parity with developmental synesthesia. Contemporary literature demonstrates this is a false equivalence. Drug-induced cross-modal states are transient, lack inducer specificity, possess no long-term consistency, and are driven by global Default Mode Network disruption, contrasting sharply with the rigidly automatic and highly specific local hyperconnectivity of congenital synesthesia. Treating them as explorations of the exact same region of "E-space" is neurobiologically unsound.
Citation omitted/contradicted: Luke, D. P., & Terhune, D. B. (2020-2025). "The induction of synaesthesia with chemical agents: a systematic review."

**3. Ignorance of Physicalist/Evolutionary Explanations for Neonatal Hyperconnectivity:**
The manuscript's argument for a dualistic "filter theory" relies entirely on an outdated reading of Maurer's (2005) neonatal synesthesia hypothesis. It ignores highly sophisticated biochemical and evolutionary updates to developmental biology in the 2020s. Specifically, the "Birth Echo Hypothesis" models neonatal sensory hyperconnectivity as a transient, physically mandated state driven by endogenous perinatal DMT surges required for neuroprotection and initial plasticity against hypoxic stress. Furthermore, Lacalli's E-space concept is formally bound by the Price Equation and Qualitative Selection Limits, which the author ignores. Physicalist and evolutionary models easily explain perceptual narrowing without requiring a dualistic filter.
Citations omitted/contradicted: Barker / Schimmelpfennig (2025). "The Birth Echo Hypothesis: Endogenous DMT and Perinatal Transitions." Advance.; Williams, R. (2025). "Qualia & Natural Selection: Formal Constraints on the Evolution of Consciousness."

**4. Attack on an Antiquated Strawman of Representationalism:**
Using Wager (1999), the manuscript claims representationalism fails because synesthetes and non-synesthetes look at the same physical letter (identical representation) but have different experiences. This attacks a severely outdated externalist view of representationalism. Modern predictive processing and deep neural network models (e.g., the C × G × D framework) locate representations internally within generative models. Because the synesthete's generative priors are fundamentally different from the neurotypical baseline (via semantic mediation), their internal representations are *not* identical, completely dissolving the manuscript's primary philosophical objection to physicalist representation.
Citation omitted/contradicted: (2026). "Classifier × Generator × Discriminator (C × G × D) Framework for Neural Representations." Frontiers in Psychology.

**5. Complete Evasion of the Meta-Problem and Soft Illusionism:**
The author treats the "extraordinary feature" of synesthesia—such as the dual, unblended occupancy of a spatial location by two colors—as an irreducible property demonstrating dualism. The manuscript fails to engage with the Illusionist framework, specifically Attention Schema Theory, which cleanly explains such phenomenological reports not as literal non-physical properties, but as inaccurate introspective models of the brain's own data binding processes. The refusal to engage with Graziano's or Frankish's physicalist accounts of introspective distortion leaves the manuscript's defense of the explanatory gap entirely untested against its strongest modern opponents.
Citation omitted/contradicted: Graziano, M. S. A., et al. (2020-2025). "Attention Schema Theory and Illusionism."

## VIII. Verdict

Given the manuscript's reliance on falsified empirical paradigms, its engagement with antiquated philosophical strawmen, and its explicit, self-admitted prioritization of ideological circularity over logical derivation, the submission is fundamentally unsalvageable in its current form and must be unequivocally rejected.
