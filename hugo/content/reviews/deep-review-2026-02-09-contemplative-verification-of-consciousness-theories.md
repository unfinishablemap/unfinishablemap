## Pessimistic Analysis Summary

### Critical Issues Found

1. **Straw characterisation of epiphenomenalism**: The original text claimed epiphenomenalism "predicts no difference between phenomenologically engaged practice and passive neural stimulation producing similar brain states." This misrepresented epiphenomenalism—an epiphenomenalist can grant that different physical brain states produce different results; they deny that the *phenomenal* aspect does causal work. **Resolution**: Rewrote the causal efficacy section to distinguish between the physicalist reinterpretation (different physical states cause different outcomes) and the dualist reading (phenomenological distinctions themselves are causally relevant). ### Counterarguments Considered

- **Physicalist reinterpretation of neuroplasticity**: The physicalist can explain the same data without dualism—different physical states cause different outcomes, with no residual phenomenal contribution. **Resolution**: Acknowledged this explicitly in the causal efficacy section rather than treating the physicalist as equivalent to the epiphenomenalist.

- **Gap narrows vs. sharpens**: Whether better data show irreducibility or progress toward explanation depends on prior commitments. **Resolution**: Presented both readings, stated the Map's position, acknowledged contestability.

- **MWI dissolves the verification argument**: Under many-worlds, the appearance of selection is indexical, not genuine. **Resolution**: Added No Many Worlds section noting that contemplative evidence cannot settle this debate.
