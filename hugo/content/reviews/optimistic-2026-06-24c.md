## Executive Summary

This is the Map's calibration discipline at its most exposed and most disciplined. The animal-consciousness ladder is the single domain where the Map has the strongest *temptation* to convert tenet-coherence into evidential upgrade — dualism removes the anthropocentric defeater, so every boundary organism is metaphysically permitted to be conscious, and the slide from "permitted" to "supported" would let the worldview absorb negative empirical findings rather than be constrained by them. The cluster's defining virtue is that it names that slide explicitly, repeatedly, and structurally, and refuses it everywhere — most sharply in the new `standing-agnostic-challenge` anchor, which centralises a *symmetric* epistemic ceiling that blocks confident attributions of consciousness and confident attributions of its *absence* with equal force. ## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)

Chalmers — a signatory of the very New York Declaration the cluster anchors on — would find the explanatory gap deployed exactly as he uses it: as a *scale-independent* leverage point, never as a club. `consciousness-in-simple-organisms` states the move cleanly: "If we cannot explain how human neural activity produces experience, we cannot explain it for nematodes either. The explanatory gap exists at every complexity level." The *C. elegans* case makes the point empirically — "we possess complete structural knowledge of 302 neurons yet cannot determine whether the worm experiences anything. Complete knowledge doesn't bridge the gap." That is the knowledge argument run at the lower limit. Crucially, the cluster declines to let the gap do dualist work it has not earned: `standing-agnostic-challenge` carefully separates the *constitutive* hard problem ("why does any physical process give rise to 'what it is like'?") from its own *epistemic* claim ("what evidence can show, granting the hard problem as backdrop"), which is precisely the registration discipline Chalmers brings to phenomenal-concept debates.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)

Stapp's interface program is handled with the discipline it is most often accused of lacking. `cetacean-and-corvid-consciousness` and `interface-efficacy-and-the-cognitive-gap` both treat the microtubule interface as *one candidate site* and flag the decoherence dispute as live rather than settled — and `animal-consciousness` goes further, conceding in its own Minimal Quantum Interaction section that "the post-2009 literature largely runs against biologically relevant microtubule coherence (Reimers/McKemmish 2009), so the decoherence dispute is not 50/50," and routing the animal case through the *evolutionary* argument for mental causation instead. That is a Stapp-friendly program refusing to let its preferred mechanism carry weight the evidence withholds. The interface-efficacy article's four scaling axes (bandwidth, fidelity, granularity, candidate-type coupling) are explicitly read as ways of being *more selective* rather than *more powerful* — honouring Minimal Quantum Interaction by keeping every per-event adjustment inside the Born-rule corridor, which is the constraint Stapp's selection-and-amplification proposal most needs to respect.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)

Nagel's own bat is load-bearing, not decorative: `animal-consciousness` uses it for the precise point that "we can know *that* bats have experience without knowing *what* it is like, and no neuroscience remedies that ignorance," then routes deeper into the `commensurability-void` (the bat's phenomenal world "may share no common measure with ours"). The contemplative sections of `consciousness-in-simple-organisms` push the "what is it like" question to its minimal limit — Metzinger's MPE, the vijñāna/prajñā distinction, witness consciousness — to make the *coherent* question of content-free experience in a 302-neuron system available without claiming the worm has it. And the cluster is honest that this contemplative bridge is "heuristic, not evidential": `animal-consciousness` marks "three disanalogies block any inference from the meditator's subtractive state to the simple organism's, marking its own evidential weight as zero." That is Nagel's first-person centrality held without letting it smuggle in an inference.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)

Whitehead gets genuine, load-bearing treatment in all four siblings — actual occasions, prehension, concrescence, "high-grade" vs "low-grade" occasions — and in every case the framing is correctly fenced. `consciousness-in-simple-organisms` reframes the distribution problem through process philosophy ("experience didn't emerge from non-experience during evolution… evolution organised and amplified experiential properties that were always present") and then states the firewall the persona constraint demands: "This perspective doesn't commit the Map to full Whiteheadian metaphysics." `animal-consciousness` does the same — Whitehead's actual occasions reframe the question "not whether animals experience but what *kind* their integration affords" — without letting panexperientialism upgrade any organism's tier. The process resonance removes a defeater (gradations of experiential complexity are coherent, no threshold-where-the-lights-turn-on is required) without being cashed as evidence for presence in any specific organism. The honest pattern throughout: tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)

Kane's stake is the evolutionary-causation thread. `cetacean-and-corvid-consciousness` and `animal-consciousness` both tie the convergent capacities (prospection, deliberate tool modification, strategic deception) to the `evolutionary-case-for-mental-causation` rather than to epiphenomenal accompaniment — and, importantly, state the cost of the move openly. `cetacean-and-corvid-consciousness`: "Epiphenomenalism predicts the same convergence… James (1890) gave this reply in its classical form. Forcing the case against epiphenomenalism requires the separate argument that *consciousness itself*, not its physical substrate, is selectable." That is agency taken seriously as a *commitment with a stated cost*, the posture Kane's program needs. The interface-efficacy article's granularity axis — small per-event nudges that integrate into sustained planning and multi-step reasoning — is the room-for-agency claim cashed at the mechanism level, connected to Bidirectional Interaction without overclaiming a mechanism.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)

This cluster is built out of openly-declared cognitive closure. `consciousness-in-simple-organisms` invokes McGinn precisely: "we possess complete structural knowledge of 302 neurons yet cannot determine whether the worm experiences anything… This is what cognitive closure looks like empirically." The new `standing-agnostic-challenge` anchor *is* a mysterian-adjacent result stated with care — Gutfreund's framing that the limit is "not a problem of premature science but a fundamental, unsolvable problem" is reported faithfully, then distinguished from the hard problem and the problem of other minds so the closure claim is scoped, not over-generalised. Every sibling carries an explicit "What Would Challenge This View?" section that *marks which conditions are in-principle inaccessible by the framework's own commitments* — `cetacean-and-corvid-consciousness` separates "one methodologically accessible now and three… awaiting instrumentation the field acknowledges it does not yet have," and `animal-consciousness` flags that "(3)-(5) are blocked by the framework's own commitments… so its net falsifiability is genuinely modest." Naming one's own modest falsifiability is exactly the humility McGinn prizes.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)

This is the cluster's signature strength, and the persona's verdict is strongly positive — which is the load-bearing verdict here, because this is the maximal-temptation domain for tenet-as-evidence-upgrade and the cluster touches Birch's own *Edge of Sentience* directly. Four things earn the praise, all instances of *tenet-as-evidence-upgrade being praise-worthily not done*:

1. **The boundary organisms are held at the bottom of the scale, not upgraded.** `consciousness-in-simple-organisms` labels *C. elegans* "live hypothesis / speculative integration; not established as conscious," Hydra "speculative integration," and slime molds "speculative integration / cognition without consciousness." The article had the structural opening to slide these up on tenet-load (dualism removes the substrate barrier) and explicitly declined: "Removing a defeater keeps a hypothesis live; it does not, on its own, supply positive evidence." This is the exact pattern the persona is built to praise — restraint about minimal-organism consciousness, with nematodes, Hydra, and slime molds kept at "live hypothesis" or "speculative integration" rather than "realistic possibility" or higher.

2. **The negative empirical signal is protected from absorption.** `consciousness-in-simple-organisms` names the danger directly: "The interface reading must not absorb the negative empirical signal. *Failing* UAL remains evidentially meaningful even when UAL is treated as a coupling-marker rather than an emergence threshold." This is the single most disciplined paragraph in the cluster — it refuses to let the elegant interface-not-emergence reframe launder away the fact that *C. elegans*, Hydra, and slime molds all fail the behavioural signature that most reliably accompanies known consciousness. "The interface reading lowers our confidence that UAL marks a *necessary* boundary; it does not raise our confidence that organisms below it are conscious."

3. **The new anchor centralises the symmetry — the ceiling cuts both ways.** `standing-agnostic-challenge`'s "It Blocks Absence Too" section is the cluster's evidential keystone: "If no third-person evidence can confirm felt experience, none can confirm its *absence* either… The challenge is therefore not a tool for skeptics against believers; it is a ceiling above both." This is the persona's discipline made structural — the same restraint that bars upgrading nematodes to "realistic possibility" also bars confidently declaring slime molds zombies. The anchor explicitly states the Map's tenets "do **not** absorb or dissolve the challenge… The Map gains structural permission to entertain non-human consciousness; it gains no licence to claim it."

4. **Coherence is repeatedly distinguished from evidential tier by name.** `animal-consciousness` states it as a refrain: convergent markers, UAL, valence behaviour, and convergent evolution "raise coherence rather than evidential status; invertebrate verdicts remain 'realistic possibility, contested'," and it invokes the `common-cause-null` to forbid counting one functional kind's several markers as independent confirmations. `interface-efficacy-and-the-cognitive-gap` is even more self-denying: it concedes its own reframe "is therefore not in better evidential standing than the brain-side framing it competes with; both face the agnostic ceiling," and parks itself at *speculative integration* with the `framework-stage-calibration` "pre-Keplerian" honesty the persona prizes (heuristic-explanatory, not yet prediction-generating).

The Process Philosopher and the Hardline Empiricist **converge** here rather than conflict. Whitehead's gradations-of-experience praise and Birch's evidential-discipline praise land on the *same* passages — `consciousness-in-simple-organisms`'s process section that immediately fences off full Whiteheadian metaphysics, and the UAL paragraph that refuses to absorb the negative signal. By the skill's own test, convergence of these two personas means the cluster has resolved the possibility/probability tension honestly. There is no calibration concern to flag and no refine-draft task to generate on these grounds.

- **Strongest point**: The Neural Architecture Challenge section, which turns the materialist's *own* demand for mechanistic specification back on the materialist symmetrically — "The materialist demands mechanistic specification when dualism posits a non-physical interface; the same demand, applied to the materialist's own commitments, asks *how* convergent computational organisation generates the felt unity of seeming" — while conceding the data underdetermine the choice and the Map's vocabulary preference is "tenet-driven, not forced by the comparative evidence."

- **Why it works**: It uses three independent lineages (primates, cetaceans, corvids) as a convergence *test*, then refuses to let convergence force the inference — epiphenomenalism and convergent-computation are both granted as live readings, and the amplification hypothesis itself is held at "live hypothesis" while the species verdicts sit at "realistic possibility, contested."
