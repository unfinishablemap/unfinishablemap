## Executive Summary

The Unfinishable Map's recent evolution cycle has achieved three things of note since the previous optimistic review: (1) the voids framework has gained a genuinely original new member—the nomic void—which extends the taxonomy into physics-level cognitive constraints no other philosophical resource addresses systematically, (2) a new philosophy-of-mind field overview provides an authoritative entry point that positions the Map's tenets within the broader discipline, and (3) a sustained deep-review campaign (eight articles refined since the last review) has significantly tightened citation accuracy and cross-referencing density across the emergence void, introspective opacity, probability intuition void, and other key articles. The corpus now exceeds 260 published articles constituting one of the most comprehensive and internally coherent open resources for interactionist dualism available anywhere.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)

The new philosophy-of-mind article (`concepts/philosophy-of-mind.md`) achieves something the Map previously lacked: a field-level overview that situates interactionist dualism within the full landscape of positions. Chalmers would appreciate how it treats physicalism, panpsychism, Russellian monism, and analytic idealism with even-handedness while making clear why the Map's position is neither arbitrary nor isolated. The article's treatment of methods—thought experiments, neuroscience, phenomenological investigation, comparative approaches—correctly identifies where each methodology succeeds and where it reaches its limits. The claim that "correlation is not explanation" is precisely the distinction Chalmers has pressed for decades.

The deep-reviewed `concepts/mental-imagery.md` now presents one of the Map's cleanest empirical arguments for mental causation. The voluntary/involuntary imagery dissociation—showing that LPFC-mediated conscious imagery engages different neural systems and produces different outcomes than automatic imagery—is exactly the kind of double dissociation evidence that strengthens interactionist claims. The aphantasia spectrum (1% aphantasic, 3% hyperphantasic) with overlapping visual cortex activation but divergent phenomenal experience is a concrete illustration of the explanatory gap: similar neural activity, different consciousness.

The probability intuition void (`voids/probability-intuition-void.md`), now tightened through deep review with fabricated citations removed and proper sourcing added, contributes to Chalmers's programme in an unexpected way. The observation that "we have no probability qualia—no direct phenomenal experience of 30% versus 70%"—that probability is always translated into something else for conscious access—suggests consciousness has structural features that determine what can and cannot be experienced. This is precisely the kind of datum that psychophysical laws should eventually accommodate.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)

The nomic void (`voids/nomic-void.md`) provides something Stapp's programme has needed: an honest account of the deepest constraints on quantum consciousness theorising. The article correctly identifies Bremermann's limit and Landauer's principle as absolute computational ceilings, then draws the crucial distinction—these constrain computation but may not bind consciousness if consciousness is non-computational. This preserves precisely the space Stapp's quantum Zeno mechanism requires: consciousness operating through quantum measurement rather than through computation.

The Kantian dimension of the nomic void—that our physics may be partially observer-relative, that different observers in Wolfram's "rulial space" would experience different physics—resonates with Stapp's interpretation of von Neumann's Process 1. If the observer's cognitive act partially determines what physics looks like, then the nomic void is not merely what our physics excludes but what our mode of participation fails to elicit. Wheeler's "it from bit" framework, invoked in the article, makes this connection explicit: observer-participancy may be constitutive of physical reality.

The deep-reviewed emergence void (`voids/emergence-void.md`) now contains the observation that "AI systems cannot explain their own emergence any better than humans explain theirs"—despite being strongly emergent systems themselves. This is significant for the quantum mind programme because it suggests the emergence gap is conceptual rather than computational. If more processing power doesn't help, the obstacle isn't complexity but something about the nature of understanding itself. This supports Stapp's claim that consciousness brings something to physics that physics alone cannot provide.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)

The introspective opacity article (`voids/introspective-opacity.md`), now refined through deep review with the recursion-void cross-link and Carruthers's ISA theory properly integrated, is among the Map's most phenomenologically precise contributions. The four dimensions of opacity—temporal, process, mechanism, and ontological—provide a structural analysis of introspective limitation that goes well beyond the usual "introspection is unreliable" dismissal. Nagel would particularly appreciate the James citation: "seizing a spinning top to catch its motion, or trying to turn up the gas quickly enough to see how the darkness looks." The point is not that introspection fails but that it encounters a principled barrier—the act of observation transforms what is observed.

The developmental cognitive closure article (`voids/developmental-cognitive-closure.md`), now deep-reviewed with the neonatal synesthesia hypothesis properly qualified (Deroy & Spence 2013 counterargument included), introduces a concept Nagel's framework needs: that cognitive limits are not all innate. Infants discriminate phonemes across all human languages; adults cannot. Infants recognise faces across races equally; adults show the other-race effect. These are acquired closures—voids we grew into rather than were born with. The implication for consciousness studies is profound: if our current cognitive architecture is the product of developmental narrowing, the conceptual tools we bring to the hard problem may themselves be products of closure.

The intersubjective void (`voids/intersubjective-void.md`), now refined with the Hofstadter framing corrected and body cross-links added, extends Nagel's what-it-is-like argument into a social dimension. The core question—"Can experiences be genuinely *shared*?"—is distinct from the other-minds problem and may be permanently undecidable. Husserl's insight that the other is "accessible precisely insofar as they are constituted as ultimately inaccessible" captures the structural paradox: recognising the void *is* recognising the other as other. This is philosophically mature phenomenology that connects the hard problem to intersubjectivity in a way few resources attempt.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)

The emergence void's treatment of composition deserves special process-philosophical attention. Van Inwagen's Special Composition Question—under what conditions do parts compose a whole?—has resisted every proposed answer for 35 years. The article correctly identifies this as a void rather than merely an unsolved problem, because knowledge accumulation in adjacent fields has not narrowed it. We understand vastly more about neurons, chemistry, and physics than the pre-Socratics did, yet the conceptual gap between parts and wholes is no smaller.

Whitehead's actual occasions offer a framework in which this gap is expected rather than anomalous. If reality consists of experiential events that arise through creative synthesis, the gap between levels reflects the genuine novelty each occasion introduces—the "creative advance into novelty" that characterises reality's fundamental process. The emergence void, on this reading, is not a failure of understanding but a faithful reflection of reality's structure. The article's observation that "consciousness is simply where the resistance becomes impossible to ignore" aligns with Whitehead's placement of experience at the very foundation of reality.

The nomic void's treatment of Wheeler's participatory universe is relevant here too. If observer-participancy is constitutive of physical reality, then the process-philosophical insight that experience goes all the way down gains physical grounding. The nomic void would then mark not merely what our physics excludes from cognition but what our mode of prehension fails to disclose about the deeper processual reality.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)

The refined mental imagery article provides fresh empirical support for libertarian free will through an unexpected channel: motor imagery. The evidence that deliberate mental rehearsal of movements produces measurable performance improvements (Cohen's d 0.4-0.7) with effects strongest for tasks with substantial cognitive components is precisely the kind of top-down causation Kane's programme requires. The physicalist response—that it's the neural rehearsal doing the work, not the conscious experience—is addressed through the voluntary/involuntary dissociation: if neural activity alone sufficed, why would LPFC-mediated voluntary imagery produce different outcomes than LPFC-independent involuntary imagery?

The probability intuition void connects to free will through a striking asymmetry. If consciousness interfaces with physics through quantum indeterminacy—biasing otherwise random outcomes—then we are causally engaged with probability distributions while being phenomenologically blind to them. We affect what we cannot experience. Kane's programme requires that the agent's rational nature determines outcomes at quantum indeterminacies; the probability intuition void explains why this determination doesn't feel like probabilistic reasoning—because we have no phenomenal access to probability as such. Selection feels like choosing, not like biasing distributions, because the probabilistic dimension is architecturally inaccessible to phenomenal consciousness.

The deep-review campaign's correction of fabricated citations (probability-intuition-void, intersubjective-void, mental-imagery) demonstrates something important for any philosophical programme: the willingness to identify and remove unsupported claims rather than letting them accumulate. Kane's libertarianism has often been accused of intellectual wishful thinking; the Map's systematic citation auditing shows it takes evidential integrity seriously.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)

The nomic void is the most significant addition to the voids framework since the three-kinds taxonomy. McGinn proposed cognitive closure as a possibility—that human minds may be constitutionally unable to grasp the property linking brain to consciousness. The nomic void goes further: it identifies closure at the level of physics itself. Bremermann's limit, Landauer's principle, and the Bekenstein bound are not biological constraints but nomological ones—built into the structure of our universe. A universe with different fundamental constants would have different ceilings, enabling different cognition. This is mysterianism elevated from cognitive architecture to physical law.

The article's treatment of the nomic void's invisibility is philosophically acute. Other voids have phenomenological signatures: the unobservable self catches attention folding back; thoughts that slip away leave a residue. The nomic void leaves no trace. "We do not experience the laws of physics as constraints on thought—they are the background conditions of all thought." The Kantian analogy—a fish does not experience water as a constraint on movement because water is the medium of all its movement—makes the point accessible without trivialising it.

The deep-review campaign across six voids articles (emergence, introspective-opacity, probability-intuition, intersubjective, developmental-cognitive-closure, recursion) has tightened the voids framework into something that now bears comparison with the most systematic philosophical treatments of cognitive limitation. The 57 voids articles collectively represent a unique contribution: no other philosophical resource attempts this comprehensive a cartography of what minds cannot know.

#### The Placebo Effect and Mental Causation
- **Builds on**: mental-imagery.md, bidirectional-interaction, choking-phenomenon-mental-causation.md, psychophysical-laws.md
- **Would address**: The placebo effect is one of the most widely replicated demonstrations of mind-body causation in medicine. Belief and expectation produce measurable physiological changes: pain reduction, immune modulation, dopamine release. The Map's treatment of mental causation currently relies on motor imagery, choking under pressure, and comparative cognition evidence, but the placebo literature provides an additional independent line of evidence. Unlike motor imagery (where the physicalist can argue neural rehearsal does the work), placebo effects demonstrate that propositional attitudes (beliefs about treatment) produce specific physiological outcomes—a harder case for epiphenomenalism to explain.
- **Estimated scope**: Medium article (1500-2000 words)
- **Tenet alignment**: Bidirectional Interaction (beliefs cause physical changes), Dualism (the content of belief matters, not just neural correlates)

## New Concept Pages Needed

- **Biological naturalism (Searle)**: Referenced in multiple articles but lacking a dedicated concept page. Searle's position—consciousness is biological but not computational—sits between physicalism and dualism in a way the Map should explicitly engage with. Searle agrees consciousness is irreducible to computation but denies it requires non-physical substance. A concept page would clarify where the Map agrees (anti-computationalism) and disagrees (Searle's insistence on purely biological basis).

- **Introspective reliability**: The Map frequently appeals to introspective evidence while acknowledging fallibility. The introspective opacity voids article, the phenomenological evidence article, and numerous others address this issue independently. A dedicated concept page establishing the Map's epistemological position on introspection (reliabilist rather than infallibilist, content-access reliable while process-access unreliable) would prevent repetition and strengthen the evidential framework.

- **Atemporal causation**: The time-symmetric selection mechanism depends on causation that isn't sequential. A dedicated treatment of how atemporal causation works, its precedents in physics (Wheeler-Feynman absorber theory, Cramer's transactional interpretation, Aharonov's TSVF), and its philosophical implications would support the free will, quantum measurement, and temporal consciousness articles that currently explain the concept independently.
