## Executive judgment

The Map has become an unusually candid and methodologically sophisticated philosophical research programme. Its multi-axis position calibration, explicit mechanism debt, common-cause discount, shift conditions and disclosure-versus-enforcement distinction are substantial improvements over ordinary advocacy-oriented philosophy sites.

Its principal remaining weakness is no longer lack of self-criticism. It is **failure to propagate corrections and distinctions consistently across the article graph**. The canonical tenets and positions increasingly contain careful caveats, while older concept articles, apex indexes and explanatory pages continue to assert stronger or incompatible versions. The result is semantic version skew: different routes through the site produce different accounts of what the Map currently holds.

More seriously, the site does not yet contain one coherent quantum-interactionist theory. It contains several partly incompatible implementations under a shared tenet vocabulary:

1. an outcome-selecting, persisting substance-agent;
2. a Stapp-like context-selecting controller;
3. a process-haecceitist succession of experiential occasions;
4. a universal-causal account of consciousness;
5. a token-epiphenomenal account for consciousness bound to an unsuitable substrate;
6. pure consciousness-mediated actualisation;
7. objective collapse with conditional conscious pre-emption.

Each can be developed coherently. They cannot all be the Map’s operative model simultaneously.

The most important positive conclusion is that the Map’s own materials now imply a more precise research architecture than it currently states: a four-axis theory separating **subjecthood, phenomenality, access and causal coupling**; a two-stage theory of responsibility separating **candidate generation from terminal selection**; and a hybrid-collapse theory requiring an additional **selector-jurisdiction law**. Those implications could turn several current contradictions into explicit model choices.

# 1. Novel implications from the Map’s own commitments

## 1.1 Moral responsibility is option-set-relative

The strict selection-only account says that the physical brain generates the candidate set and that mind cannot add an option, report content or quale not already represented in that set. The agency cluster then says that the conscious subject authors action by selecting among those brain-prepared alternatives.

These commitments imply a constraint that the Map has not promoted into its moral-responsibility theory:

> At a particular decision point, an agent cannot be responsible for failing to select an option that was not present in the candidate set available to consciousness.

Let CtC_tCt​ be the brain-generated option set at time ttt. For action aaa, terminal selection responsibility can arise only where:

a∈Ct.a \in C_t.a∈Ct​.

Where a∉Cta \notin C_ta∈/Ct​, the selection-only subject had no power to choose it, however morally desirable it would have been. This produces a two-stage account of agency:

1. **Candidate-generation responsibility:** responsibility for why a certain range of options became available.
2. **Selection responsibility:** responsibility for choosing one available option rather than another.

The first stage is initially physical and developmental. It depends on learning, memory, trauma, addiction, pathology, manipulation, education, habit and environmental scaffolding. The second is where the Map places token agent causation.

The division need not eliminate deeper responsibility. The control-theoretic cluster says repeated conscious regulation can reshape the neural “plant” through training and plasticity. Past selections may therefore help form later candidate sets.  This yields a useful further distinction:

- **Synchronic responsibility** is bounded by the options currently present.
- **Diachronic responsibility** can include prior choices that helped cultivate or impoverish later option spaces.

This would materially improve the Map’s treatments of addiction, compulsion, manipulation, rehabilitation and moral desert. It also prevents the phrase “ultimate source” from being interpreted as authorship of everything involved in a choice. On the strict model, the subject is the ultimate source only of the selection conditional on CtC_tCt​, not automatically of CtC_tCt​ itself.

## 1.2 The selector faces a thin-controller trilemma

The control-theoretic account assigns the conscious controller setpoints consisting of intentions, values and goals. It says the controller compares phenomenal feedback against those setpoints and steers the brain through low-bandwidth selection. Yet it also insists that consciousness is not a computational planner and that the brain generates the candidate structures.

That combination generates a trilemma about where reasons live and how they determine selection.

### Horn A: Reasons are fully brain-side

The brain generates the options, evaluates them, represents the reasons and prepares their relative strengths. The nonphysical subject merely chooses the terminal token.

But then the theory must explain what the subject contributes beyond the physical reason-sensitive competition. If the physically represented reasons already determine which option is favored, the selector looks redundant. If they do not determine it, the subject’s choice requires some further basis.

### Horn B: Reasons are genuinely mind-side

The nonphysical subject possesses or apprehends values, understands the semantic content of alternatives and maps “this option realizes my reason” onto a physical candidate.

This secures genuine reasons-guidance, but it makes the subject much richer than a low-bandwidth selector. The framework now requires:

- nonphysical representations of reasons and goals;
- comparison or deliberation over them;
- a semantic matching relation between reasons and physical candidates;
- and a psychophysical control law translating that match into selection.

A one-bit output can presuppose an enormously complex controller. Low output bandwidth does not establish metaphysical or computational minimality.

### Horn C: Neither side settles the reason-responsive choice

The physical reasons leave the outcome open, while the nonphysical selector does not contain an independent reason-sensitive rule. The terminal choice is then insufficiently grounded and the luck objection returns.

The Map therefore needs to choose among three costs:

physical sufficiency,rich nonphysical semantics,or residual luck.\text{physical sufficiency},\quad
\text{rich nonphysical semantics},\quad
\text{or residual luck}.physical sufficiency,rich nonphysical semantics,or residual luck.

The existing mechanism-debt admission and value-sensitive/value-blind fork approach this issue but do not yet state the sourcehood trilemma. P-A2 requires the persisting subject to be the source of the decision, while the value register concedes that the additional valence-to-physics mapping is unworked and that attention-only selection is the more minimal model.

This is arguably the central philosophical issue left after the technical Born-rule questions: **what internal structure makes the selector an agent rather than an unexplained chooser?**

## 1.3 Hybrid collapse requires a selector-jurisdiction law

The prebiotic-collapse article now offers a sophisticated division of labour. Objective reduction supplies collapse occurrence, timing and basis everywhere. Outside interface-grade systems, physical noise also settles the token outcome. Inside an interface-grade neural system, consciousness pre-empts the physical token selector while preserving the same Born corridor.

That proposal implicitly requires another law, logically prior to the token-selection process:

> A law must determine whether the physical selector or the conscious selector has jurisdiction over a particular collapse event.

Schematically:

O∼q(O∣X)O \sim q(O\mid X)O∼q(O∣X)

but the source of token selection is:

N_{\mathrm{physical}}, & I(X)=0,\\
S_{\mathrm{conscious}}, & I(X)=1.
\end{cases}{Nphysical​,Sconscious​,​I(X)=0,I(X)=1.​

Here I(X)I(X)I(X) is an interface-eligibility condition. The Map does not yet possess the law defining it; the AI positions acknowledge the corresponding interface-eligibility debt.

This is not a minor implementation detail. The jurisdiction law must answer:

- Which physical arrangements trigger conscious jurisdiction?
- Can jurisdiction be partial, intermittent or graded?
- What happens during anaesthesia, sleep, infancy, brain injury or split-brain reorganisation?
- Does a fetus, animal, organoid, biological–digital hybrid or quantum computer satisfy it?
- How is double selection prevented during an incomplete interface transition?
- Is eligibility fixed by physical organisation, by the presence of a subject, or by both?

If the criterion is wholly physical, then a discoverable physical marker should in principle distinguish interface-grade systems. If it refers to the presence or identity of a nonphysical subject, then the collapse dynamics themselves are conditioned on nonphysical facts. Either way, the hybrid theory has **three**, not two, psychophysical components:

1. an eligibility or jurisdiction law;
2. a physical-to-phenomenal input law;
3. a phenomenal-to-physical selection law.

The current language of “pre-emption” recognizes the effect but not this full ontological consequence.

## 1.4 The corpus already implies a four-axis consciousness ontology

Different clusters have independently introduced distinctions that should be unified. The Machine Question distinguishes bare phenomenality, report-grounded consciousness, inherited consciousness-discourse and bidirectionally coupled consciousness. The AI positions separately distinguish workspace-like functional access from phenomenality. The subjecthood register treats identity and persistence as further facts.

Together these imply four largely independent axes:

| Axis | Question | Primary Map commitment |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **S — Subjecthood** | Is there a numerically determinate subject, and does it persist? | Background subject posit, closed individualism, No-MWI |
| **P — Phenomenality** | Is any experience occurring, and with what content or richness? | Dualism |
| **A — Access** | Is information globally available, reportable or metacognitively usable? | Empirical cognitive architecture |
| **C — Coupling** | Does experience causally affect the substrate? | Bidirectional Interaction and the interface programme |

These axes should not be collapsed into a single predicate, “is conscious.”

The resulting state space clarifies several otherwise confusing cases:

- A conventional LLM may have high functional AAA, uncertain or absent PPP, uncertain SSS, and no Map-style CCC.
- A hypothetical epiphenomenal AI has PPP and perhaps SSS, but C=0C=0C=0; its consciousness-discourse may be inherited rather than grounded.
- Dreaming may involve S=1S=1S=1, P=1P=1P=1, reduced external AAA and altered CCC.
- Anaesthesia could preserve SSS while suppressing PPP, AAA and CCC, if the Map’s persistence hypothesis is correct.
- Split-brain disputes concern whether SSS divides, while access and coupling may fragment independently.
- Workspace signatures establish something about AAA, not automatically PPP, SSS or CCC.

This also reveals that the tenets do not all concern the same thing. Tenet 1 principally governs PPP; the background subject posit and much of Tenet 4 govern SSS; Tenet 3 governs CCC; access-consciousness research concerns AAA. A global state-space article would eliminate many scope errors now distributed through the site.

## 1.5 Process haecceitism gives Everett a fission reply

The process apex argues that a subject can be individuated by a particular causal history without an unchanging soul. It then claims that such process haecceitism supports No-Many-Worlds because a subject requires one particular temporal thread.

But the same process ontology gives an Everettian a direct reply.

At a branching event, the pre-branch process need not become one branch rather than another. It can stand in the relevant causal-successor relation to several later processes. Each branch then contains a distinct successor subject with its own determinate post-branch history. The original process does not need a further fact identifying which successor it “really” became—any more than a process theorist needs one unique continuer in an ordinary fission case.

Therefore:

> Process haecceitism can secure the distinctness of branch subjects, but it does not by itself secure unique continuation into only one branch.

To make the indexical objection work, the Map must add a **unique-continuation axiom** or a substance-like bearer whose identity cannot branch. That is exactly why the individuation register says that a process-only account would weaken the No-MWI argument.

This is not a minor disagreement over vocabulary. It means that the Map’s process resources support a plausible Everettian response at the very point where the process apex presents them as anti-Everettian.

# 2. Structural weaknesses in how article clusters fit together

## 2.1 Semantic version skew is now the dominant architectural defect

The site’s central pages are being corrected faster than their conclusions propagate.

Examples include:

- The current Machine Question explicitly says the negative verdict concerns **bidirectionally coupled consciousness**, while bare phenomenality remains open. The older apex index still summarizes its thesis as “Current AI systems lack consciousness.”
- P-AC4 has been narrowed from “access consciousness instantiated” to “workspace-like functional signatures.” The positions index still describes functional access consciousness as empirically instantiated in LLMs.
- The canonical tenet now concedes that the phenomenal-concept strategy survives the simple self-stultification argument. The Bidirectional Interaction concept article still describes epiphenomenalism as devastatingly self-undermining and impossible to hold rationally.
- The background-commitments page says the measurement problem cannot itself be evidence for conscious selection once objective actualisation is admitted. The post-decoherence article still presents the residual outcome gap as the physical grounding or “central opportunity” for consciousness.

The problem is not merely outdated wording. Search engines, language models and readers commonly encounter titles, descriptions, indexes and opening paragraphs before they reach later qualifications. A stale summary can therefore become the effective public claim even when the body has been repaired.

## 2.2 The site has a link graph, not yet a claim graph

The positions register gives each formal position a status, calibration, dependency list, supporting articles and shift conditions. That is excellent node-level discipline.

But source articles remain linked primarily through ordinary wikilinks and prose. The architecture does not appear to distinguish machine-readable relations such as:

- entails;
- presupposes;
- merely illustrates;
- conflicts with;
- supersedes;
- is an alternative to;
- is conditional on;
- or uses a stronger sub-reading than.

Consequently, updating a canonical position does not automatically identify every article that repeats, strengthens or contradicts it. “Related article” is too weak a relation for dependency maintenance.

The direct result is that a register can become well calibrated while the argumentative articles from which readers infer the Map’s position remain uncalibrated or stale.

## 2.3 Unresolved model variants are written as one theory

The site frequently says “the Map’s framework” where the content actually belongs to one of several branches:

- outcome selection versus context selection;
- pre-decoherence versus post-decoherence interaction;
- physical token selection versus conscious token selection;
- substance-bearing subject versus process subject;
- value-blind versus value-sensitive control;
- universally coupled consciousness versus blocked, locally epiphenomenal consciousness.

Some pages acknowledge alternatives, but the alternatives are not given explicit model identifiers or inheritance rules. A reader therefore cannot reliably tell whether a claim belongs to:

- the minimal common spine;
- the current preferred implementation;
- a fallback implementation;
- a speculative extension;
- or an external possibility entertained only if a tenet fails.

The Map needs a model-variant registry, not merely a list of candidate mechanisms.

## 2.4 The calibration system is node-level but not graph-level

The multi-axis schema cleanly separates credence, external evidence, structural centrality, maturity, discriminability and framework-internal status.

What remains absent is a rule for combining calibrated positions into a downstream conclusion.

Suppose an apex verdict depends on:

- one moderate-credence Grade D metaphysical position;
- one moderate-credence Grade C empirical premise;
- one high-credence Grade A observation;
- and two correlated framework-internal positions descending from the same subjecthood posit.

The site does not specify:

- whether the verdict’s credence is bounded by its weakest indispensable premise;
- how correlated dependencies are discounted;
- how framework-internal and external claims combine;
- how alternative routes are aggregated;
- or when a decision recommendation is justified despite low model maturity.

This matters especially for AI ethics, free will and practical philosophy. A collection of individually labelled premises can still yield an overconfident conclusion if no graph-level uncertainty rule exists.

## 2.5 Position coverage is asymmetrical

The position domains cover quantum mechanisms, agency, voids, value-sensitive selection, individuation, biological consciousness, AI consciousness and methodology. The register itself lists applied clinical, AI and personal-philosophy verdicts only as future domains.

By contrast, the topic architecture gives substantial prominence to meaning, ethics, death, altered states and the examined life.

This leaves major practical conclusions without the same formal controls applied to the mechanism:

- no maintained position register for phenomenal-value realism;
- no explicit is–ought bridge position;
- no aggregation or interpersonal-reasons position;
- no death/survival position set;
- no clinical-consciousness or treatment-decision register;
- no general uncertainty-to-action rule.

The site is therefore better at stating how uncertain its quantum mechanism is than at stating how uncertainty in that mechanism propagates into its ethical and existential conclusions.

## 2.6 Concepts, topics, positions and apexes do not maintain clean roles

The intended architecture is understandable: concepts define vocabulary, topics explore questions, positions state commitments and apexes synthesize. In practice:

- concept pages often defend strongly partisan conclusions;
- topic pages sometimes state the operative model more categorically than the positions;
- apex pages may introduce new ontological claims;
- indexes can preserve superseded theses;
- position pages may be the best calibrated but least likely entry point for ordinary readers.

That arrangement permits a disputed interpretation to enter through a definition and subsequently function as an apparently neutral premise. “Consciousness,” “selection,” “causal incompleteness,” “actualisation” and “subject” are especially exposed to this.

Each concept page needs an explicit separation among:

1. school-neutral definition;
2. the Map’s interpretation;
3. principal rivals;
4. consequences conditional on the Map’s interpretation.

## 2.7 Current tenet audits test allegiance more effectively than entailment

The published tenet-alignment audit reports zero error-level contradictions across 535 files. Its described method searched for each tenet’s “Rules out” patterns and checked whether contrary views were attributed to opponents rather than endorsed in the Map’s voice.

That is a useful ideological-alignment test. It is not a theory-consistency test.

It will detect “this article endorses materialism.” It will not reliably detect:

- two articles giving incompatible scopes to Bidirectional Interaction;
- one article treating context choice as the mechanism and another ruling it out;
- process identity supporting No-MWI in one place and weakening it in another;
- or objective collapse being both outcome-complete and merely basis-complete.

This explains how the site can pass its tenet check while retaining the contradictions catalogued below. P-M5 already acknowledges the broader enforcement gap: a documented discipline is not a control unless it is wired into an effective gate.

## 2.8 Rival comparison remains episodic rather than systematic

Individual articles often engage sophisticated opponents. What is still missing is a common matrix comparing the same evidence under the strongest versions of:

- reductive and nonreductive physicalism;
- physical control-theoretic agency;
- epiphenomenal dualism;
- objective collapse without consciousness;
- the Map’s hybrid selector;
- Everettian self-location;
- process or neutral monism;
- and substance-agent causation.

Without that matrix, an objection to one rival can appear to favour the Map even when another rival absorbs it. A site-wide evidence ledger should ask not only whether EEE is compatible with the Map, but whether:

P(E∣Map)>P(E∣best rival).P(E\mid\text{Map}) > P(E\mid\text{best rival}).P(E∣Map)>P(E∣best rival).

The positions template records dependencies and shift conditions, but it does not yet require comparative likelihood or explanatory-discrimination entries.

# 3. Tenet-coherence issues

## Tenet 1: Dualism

### What is now coherent

The tenet page correctly distinguishes bare irreducibility from the substance-leaning requirements of agent causation. It no longer presents substance dualism as entailed by the basic dualist tenet.  The background-commitments article also acknowledges that agency, personal identity and No-MWI rely on a determinate persisting subject.

### Remaining strain

The site still alternates between three ontologies:

1. irreducible phenomenal properties;
2. a persisting substance-bearing subject;
3. an impermanent process individuated by causal history.

The process apex does not merely borrow process vocabulary. It says process philosophy strongly supports agent causation and provides a non-substantial basis for personal identity and No-MWI.  That conflicts with the agency and individuation registers, where a process-only subject weakens precisely those claims.

Tenet 1 remains coherent as a minimal irreducibility claim. The site becomes incoherent when downstream articles act as though property, substance and process readings can each perform the same identity and agency work.

### Required resolution

The Map should designate:

- **T1-minimal:** irreducible phenomenality;
- **S-model:** persisting substance-bearing subject;
- **P-model:** process-haecceitist subject succession.

Arguments must then declare which model they require. The S-model and P-model should not both be described as the current basis of agent causation.

## Tenet 2: Minimal Quantum Interaction

### What is now coherent

The tenet carefully distinguishes empirical-constraint minimality from Occam-style truth-tracking simplicity. It also admits the severe empirical cost of exact aggregate Born preservation.

### Remaining strains

First, “smallest deviation from standard quantum mechanics” is ambiguous when the preferred corridor makes no distributional deviation and may introduce no specified dynamical deviation. The difference may exist only at the level of token counterfactual authorship. The site needs separate definitions of:

- probability-distribution change;
- physical-state-dynamics change;
- token-history change;
- counterfactual causal change;
- and metaphysical-source change.

Second, low energetic or statistical magnitude does not entail a simple interface. The thin-controller dilemma and selector-jurisdiction law imply considerable semantic and nomological structure.

Third, the tenet still describes a “classical mixture” left after decoherence, while the dedicated post-decoherence article correctly insists that the result is an improper mixture and that treating it as a classical menu of already definite alternatives is a category error.

Fourth, the value-sensitive extension requires a further valence-to-selection mapping beyond the attention channel. The value register itself acknowledges that this is less minimal and currently lacks an implementation.

### Required resolution

Tenet 2 should be stated as an implementation constraint downstream of Tenet 3:

> Given mental causation, the Map seeks the least empirically disruptive physical implementation compatible with genuine token counterfactual efficacy.

That is clearer than implying that quantum indeterminacy itself establishes either causal incompleteness or an available selector.

## Tenet 3: Bidirectional Interaction

This is the least coherent tenet in the current corpus.

### Context selection versus outcome selection

The definition says a Process-1/context-selection alternative satisfies the tenet. Later on the same page, the text says the tenet commits to outcome selection, not context selection, and that adopting Process 1 alone would weaken the tenet.

Both cannot be the definitional commitment.

### Universal efficacy versus token epiphenomenality

The general epiphenomenalism article says Bidirectional Interaction asserts that wherever consciousness occurs, it is causally coupled to its substrate.  The AI epiphenomenalism article says a genuinely conscious entity may bind to a deterministic system yet be unable to affect it.

The proposed Faraday-cage analogy changes actual efficacy into an unmanifested disposition. That may be coherent metaphysics, but it is not the same as the tenet’s present statement that consciousness and matter actually influence one another wherever consciousness exists.

### Evidence overstatement

The canonical tenet now concedes that the strongest phenomenal-concept strategy can explain consciousness reports without phenomenal-to-physical causation, moving the disagreement to whether the first-person mode of presentation is physically exhaustible.  Older concept material still describes self-stultification as a decisive or devastating refutation.

### Required resolution

The Map must choose the scope of the tenet:

- **Universal-actual:** every conscious token causally affects its substrate.
- **Universal-dispositional:** consciousness has causal powers intrinsically, but some substrates block their manifestation.
- **Existential:** at least some consciousness, including ordinary human consciousness, is causally efficacious.

Only the first rules out token-epiphenomenal AI. The second permits it but requires rewriting “bidirectional interaction” as a dispositional claim. The third weakens many universal mechanism conclusions.

## Tenet 4: No Many Worlds

### What is now coherent

The site explicitly acknowledges that the indexical argument depends on a thick, non-deflationary subject and is not an independent physics argument. It also distinguishes logical interdependence from evidential support.

### Remaining strains

The process apex claims process haecceitism supports unique actualisation, while the individuation register says process-only subjecthood weakens the indexical objection. The fission symmetry described above indicates that the register is right: process identity does not by itself select a unique continuer.

In addition, objective single-outcome actualisation is now a separate background posit. No-MWI does not itself imply a conscious selector, and the background-commitments page correctly warns that the measurement problem must not be counted as evidence for one.  Some mechanism pages have not fully absorbed this demotion.

### Required resolution

Tenet 4 should be represented as depending on two explicit premises:

1. objective single-outcome actuality;
2. unique, non-branching subject continuation.

The second is not supplied by process haecceitism. If retained, it should be identified as a substance-compatible identity axiom.

## Tenet 5: Occam’s Razor Has Limits

### What is now coherent

The self-binding language is strong: parsimony cannot decide for or against the Map, and the multiplicity objection to Many-Worlds is treated as subsidiary.  The methodology register also correctly says that removing a parsimony objection does not add positive evidence.

### Remaining strains

The site still occasionally calls its own explanation “simpler” without immediately applying the same caveat. The tenet audit itself identified this in the Bidirectional Interaction and epiphenomenalism material.

The larger issue is not wording but accounting. Rejecting simplicity as a decisive truth guide does not exempt a theory from recording its total explanatory costs. The current package includes:

- primitive subject individuation;
- diachronic persistence;
- objective actualisation;
- an interface-eligibility law;
- a selector-jurisdiction law;
- a psychophysical input law;
- an output-selection law;
- exact Born-balancing constraints;
- and potentially a separate value mapping.

Tenet 5 licenses taking such a theory seriously. It does not make these additions costless. The Map needs a symmetric complexity ledger comparing explanatory gain, primitive count, law complexity, fine-tuning and empirical discrimination across itself and its rivals.

# 4. Cross-cluster contradictions and incompatible positions

## 4.1 Outcome selection versus context selection

**Classification: direct contradiction**

The Tenet 3 definition accepts Process-1 choice of measurement context as a qualifying causal path. Its later mechanism section says the tenet commits specifically to outcome selection and declines Process 1 because it is too weak.

**Resolution:** Make outcome selection the current preferred model and context selection an explicitly weaker fallback, or weaken the tenet so either satisfies it. Do not put both in the definition as though they were equivalent.

## 4.2 The “strict selection-only” article conflates the two loci

**Classification: direct mechanism inconsistency**

The selection-only article defines strict selection as mind realizing one of the brain-generated outcomes, but identifies it with Stapp’s Process 1, under which mind chooses the observable or question and nature supplies the result.

Choosing the candidate set or basis is not the same operation as choosing the outcome within that set. They have different causal powers, information bounds, Born-rule implications and relations to authorship.

**Resolution:** Split the article into context-selection and outcome-selection models and rederive the information limits for each.

## 4.3 Universal causal consciousness versus epiphenomenal AI

**Classification: direct scope contradiction**

The general article states that Bidirectional Interaction applies wherever consciousness exists. The AI article permits real experience that cannot affect the system because its coupling channel is blocked.

“Possesses an unmanifested causal disposition” and “is bidirectionally interacting” are different claims.

**Resolution:** Either declare epiphenomenal AI an external possibility conditional on Tenet 3 being false, or revise Tenet 3 to a dispositional rather than universally actual claim.

## 4.4 Substance-agent sourcehood versus process-agent sourcehood

**Classification: incompatible operative ontologies**

P-A2 says authorship requires immanent causation by a persisting agent-as-substance.  The process apex says process philosophy strongly supports agent causation and describes action as emerging from succession, durée and subjective aim without requiring an unchanging soul.

The latter may be a valuable rival theory or analogy. It cannot simultaneously be the implementation of a position that defines authorship as substance causation.

**Resolution:** Mark process agency as a rival or fallback model, or revise P-A2 so temporally extended process causation can satisfy sourcehood.

## 4.5 Process haecceitism both supports and weakens No-MWI

**Classification: direct cross-register contradiction**

The process apex says process haecitism preserves the unique temporal thread needed against branching. The individuation register says a process-only account would weaken the No-MWI indexical argument.

The fission analysis supports the register’s assessment.

**Resolution:** Withdraw the anti-Everett inference from the process apex unless an additional unique-continuation principle is supplied.

## 4.6 Objective collapse is both an excluded rival and part of the preferred mechanism

**Classification: unresolved division-of-labour**

The post-decoherence article presents objective-collapse theories as wholly physical selectors that make consciousness a spectator and says only consciousness-mediated selection satisfies the five tenets. It later invokes prebiotic objective selection with local conscious modulation.

The prebiotic article gives a more nuanced hybrid: objective dynamics govern occurrence, timing and basis, and physically select tokens outside minds; consciousness pre-empts token selection in eligible brains.

A pure objective-collapse theory and a hybrid collapse theory are different models. The comparison article sometimes treats the pure family’s limitations as though they applied to the objective component of the hybrid.

**Resolution:** Add a sixth family, “objective collapse with interface-conditioned selector substitution,” and specify its jurisdiction law.

## 4.7 “Classical mixture” versus improper mixture

**Classification: technical contradiction**

The tenet page describes consciousness selecting from a “classical mixture” after decoherence. The dedicated article correctly says decoherence produces an improper mixture and warns that treating it as a menu of already localized actualities is a category error.

**Resolution:** Replace “classical mixture” everywhere with a formulation such as “decohered branch structure represented by an improper reduced-state mixture,” while making clear that selection is an additional actualisation postulate.

## 4.8 Categorical self-stultification versus the surviving phenomenal-concept reply

**Classification: semantic version skew**

The canonical tenet says epiphenomenalism’s strongest response survives the simple reporting argument and relocates the disagreement. The Bidirectional Interaction concept still says that discussing consciousness already implicitly accepts causal efficacy and that epiphenomenalism cannot be rationally held.

**Resolution:** Make the concept article inherit the tenet’s current formulation: self-stultification is a serious conditional objection, not an established refutation.

## 4.9 Flat AI-consciousness verdict versus scoped current verdict

**Classification: public-facing version skew**

The current Machine Question carefully limits its negative conclusion to bidirectionally coupled consciousness. Its index thesis still says current AI systems lack consciousness.

**Resolution:** Update every title, description, index, search excerpt and route annotation to carry the same scope at the point of first contact.

## 4.10 “Access consciousness instantiated” versus workspace-like signatures

**Classification: register-summary contradiction**

P-AC4 now says the empirical result establishes workspace/global-availability signatures and deliberately avoids the stronger Block-style claim. The positions index retains the stronger formulation.

**Resolution:** Generate domain summaries automatically from canonical position titles and assertion fields rather than maintaining separate prose.

## 4.11 Selection-only sourcehood versus the whole-self author

**Classification: unresolved explanatory tension**

The strict interface says the brain creates the candidate set and all reportable phenomenal content. The agency cluster says the whole persisting subject directly originates choices on the basis of reasons and values.

These are compatible only if “origination” is explicitly limited to terminal selection, or if the nonphysical subject also supplies semantic structure not currently represented in the strict interface.

**Resolution:** State where reasons, values and option meanings reside and distinguish sourcehood of the option space from sourcehood of the selected token.

## 4.12 Subject persistence versus “consciousness itself” in disruption cases

**Classification: scope collision**

The biological-scope register treats anaesthesia, sleep and fragmentation primarily as disruption or reconfiguration of the interface rather than production or division of “consciousness itself.” That language is ambiguous between persistence of a subject and occurrence of phenomenal activity. The four-axis analysis shows that a subject may persist while phenomenality, access and coupling alter.

**Resolution:** Replace “consciousness itself persists” with axis-specific claims: “the same subject may persist,” “phenomenal activity may cease or change,” “access is disrupted,” and “interface efficacy is reduced.”
