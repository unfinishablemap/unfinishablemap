## Pessimistic Analysis Summary

### Critical Issues Found

1. **Lead overclaim — "confirms distinct brain signatures" (CRITICAL, fixed).** The lead asserted meditation neuroscience "confirms they have distinct brain signatures" for the two-mode taxonomy (active-selection vs passive-witnessing). Web-verifying the three anchoring cites shows they establish that *meditative states differ neurally from rest and effortful cognition* — not a clean confirmed double-dissociation of these two philosophical modes:
   - **Brewer 2011** found DMN (mPFC/PCC) deactivation in experienced meditators across *three* meditation types (Concentration, Loving-Kindness, Choiceless Awareness) — meditation-vs-control, not FA-vs-OM dissociation.
   - **Garrison 2013** correlates PCC activity with effortful-vs-effortless self-report (the strongest single support for the effort/effortlessness axis having a neural correlate) — but it is one small correlational neurofeedback study, not a confirmation.
   - **Rodriguez-Larios 2020** distinguishes effortful arithmetic from meditation by alpha-theta coupling — effortful-cognition-vs-meditation, not FA-vs-OM.
   The categorical "confirms" outran the evidence. **Softened** the lead to "converging neuroscientific evidence associates each with distinguishable neural correlates—though it establishes that meditative states differ neurally from rest and effortful cognition rather than delivering a clean double-dissociation of the two." Description field mirrored: "confirms distinct brain signatures for each mode" → "associates each mode with distinguishable neural correlates." This is a calibration fix, correctable inside the Map's own framework (a tenet-accepting reviewer would still flag the original "confirms" as overstated relative to what three correlational studies deliver).

2. **Banned-cliche variant at the effort passage (fixed).** "This is not metaphor; effort does real work." is a semicolon variant of the forbidden "This is not X. It is Y." construct. **Rephrased** to a positive assertion: "Effort does real work here rather than serving as a figure of speech."
