# Outer Review: "Clinical Dissociation as Systematic Evidence" (The Unfinishable Map)

## TL;DR

- The article is calibration-honest in its hedging but **empirically out of date**: it anchors all four clinical cases to pre-2006 sources (Janet 1889; Sierra & Berrios 1998; Reinders et al. 2003; Brown 2004; Vuilleumier 2005) and never engages the 2012–2026 predictive-processing/active-inference literature that now dominates the field and explains the same data without any dualist interface.
- The central inference fails a "constrain-vs-establish" test: Global Neuronal Workspace theory and Integrated Information Theory *explicitly predict* that disrupting long-range connectivity between intact regions produces selective experiential fragmentation, so the disconnection-between-intact-regions pattern does **not** raise the explanatory cost on physicalism.
- Two concrete factual problems: the article's framing of conversion disorder/FND as a "motor channel disconnect" contradicts the modern finding of *increased* amygdala–motor coupling (Voon et al. 2010, *Brain*; Hassa et al. 2017), and it presents small-n, contested DID neuroimaging (Reinders et al. 2003, n≈11) as robust without flagging the field's documented underpowering or the live trauma-vs-sociocognitive dispute.

## Key Findings

1. **Empirical currency is the article's biggest weakness.** Every empirical anchor is old; the modern computational-psychiatry reframing of all four conditions is absent.
2. **The dualist inference is redundant, not warranted.** Mainstream cognitive/computational neuroscience already predicts the disconnection-between-intact-regions signature, so it does not discriminate between physicalism and the interface model.
3. **The hedging is honest but asymmetric.** The article correctly says the data don't force dualism, but never states what would count *against* the interface reading or what distinct, falsifiable prediction the interface model makes.
4. **Counterargument absorption is systemic.** GWT and IIT are name-checked and conceded to "accommodate" the data, but never engaged in their strongest, predictively-specific form; Seth, Edwards/Friston, the Stone/Aybek FND programme, illusionism, and higher-order theories are entirely absent.

## Details

### Dimension 1 — Empirical Accuracy & Currency

The article's evidence base is anchored almost entirely to literature that is 20–135 years old. The primary-source reference list is: Janet (1889), Sierra & Berrios (1998), Vuilleumier (2005), Reinders et al. (2003), and Brown (2004), plus two internal Map articles. There is **no citation later than 2005** to any primary empirical source. For a March 2026 article claiming to present "systematic evidence," this is a serious currency failure — and it is the kind of failure the Map's own review pipeline (which excels at catching citation-metadata errors) does not currently screen for.

**(a) Disconnection/dysconnectivity claims.** The general claim — that dissociative disorders show altered functional connectivity between structurally intact regions — is broadly defensible and is even endorsed *within* physicalist frameworks (Tononi's IIT explicitly discusses "functional disconnections ... in psychiatric conversion and dissociative disorders"). But the article frames this as a single, clean "selective disconnection" signature, which oversimplifies and, in the motor case, is factually wrong (below). [University of Chicago Press](https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/doi/10.2307/25470707)

**(b) DID neuroimaging.** The article cites only Reinders, Nijenhuis, Paans, Korf, Willemsen & den Boer (2003), "One brain, two selves" (*NeuroImage* 20(4):2119–2125), a PET/regional-cerebral-blood-flow symptom-provocation study comparing "neutral" (ANP) and "trauma" (EP) identity states, and presents inter-state differences as established. Problems: [PubMed + 2](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/14683715/)

- (i) That study **scanned 11 female DID patients (one excluded for neurological malformation; 15 of 80 scans discarded, leaving 65 analyzed)** — a fact the article never discloses.
- (ii) DID neuroimaging is documented as systematically underpowered. Marek et al. (2022, *Nature*, "Reproducible brain-wide association studies require thousands of individuals") report that "the median neuroimaging study sample size is about 25" and conclude such studies "require samples into the thousands to achieve the statistical power necessary for replicability." DID imaging studies typically sit at n≈11–14 — an order of magnitude below that threshold. [Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41386-024-01960-w)[nih](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC11526127/)
- (iii) A 2025 critical review in *Memory* (Vol. 34, No. 1; doi:10.1080/09658211.2025.2587233, "A critical review of methodological quality in functional neuroimaging studies on dissociative identity disorder"), which assessed quality using GRADE criteria and the Newcastle-Ottawa scale across nine multi-identity-state studies — of which "many lacked specific aims and only one stated clear hypotheses" — concluded verbatim: "On current evidence, functional imaging across identity states does not support firm claims about identity fragmentation or inter identity amnesia, nor does it decide between trauma based and sociocognitive accounts." [Taylor & Francis Online + 4](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/09658211.2025.2587233)

The article does not mention the **trauma-model-vs-sociocognitive/fantasy-model controversy** (Spanos 1994; Lilienfeld, Lynn, Kihlstrom, Chaves & Sarbin 1999; Merckelbach) at all — a major omission for a piece resting argumentative weight on DID as "the philosophically sharpest case." [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/10489540/)

**(c) Conversion disorder / FND — the most serious factual problem.** The article describes conversion disorder as the "descending motor channel" *disconnecting* while the motor system remains capable, citing Vuilleumier (2005) and Brown (2004). But the modern FND literature consistently reports the *opposite* of a simple disconnection: **increased** functional connectivity between the amygdala and motor/supplementary-motor regions — heightened limbic *influence over* motor control. Voon et al. (2010, *Brain*, "Emotional stimuli and motor conversion disorder") found that "patients with conversion disorder had greater functional connectivity between the right amygdala and the right supplementary motor area," with Granger causality showing "a directional influence from the right amygdala to the right supplementary motor area." Hassa et al. (2017, *NeuroImage: Clinical*, n=13 functional-hemiparesis patients) replicated "increased functional connectivity in patients between the left amygdala and the (pre-)supplemental motor area and the subthalamic nucleus, key regions within the motor control network." [ScienceDirect + 2](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S221315822100067X)

The contemporary mechanistic model is the **Edwards, Adams, Brown, Pareés & Friston (2012)** "A Bayesian account of 'hysteria'" (*Brain* 135(11):3495–3512): an abnormally precise prior at an intermediate level of the motor hierarchy captures processing, so the functional symptom is *actively generated* by aberrant precision-weighting — not produced by a severed channel. The article's "channel disconnect" framing is closer to the discarded Freud/Janet "hysteria" picture than to the current FND science (Stone, Edwards, Aybek, Hallett, Perez) it claims to summarize. [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22641838/)[Oxford Academic](https://academic.oup.com/brain/article/135/11/3495/270457)

**(d) DPDR and dissociative amnesia.** For depersonalization-derealization the article cites Sierra & Berrios (1998) and a generic "reduced insula/amygdala activation" claim. The current account is **Seth, Suzuki & Critchley (2012)**, "An interoceptive predictive coding model of conscious presence" (*Frontiers in Psychology* 2:395), and its successors (Ciaunica, Seth, Limanowski, Hesp & Friston 2022, "I overthink—therefore I am not," *Consciousness and Cognition* 101:103320; and the 2022 *Scientific Reports* "depersonalization disorder as a systematic downregulation of interoceptive signals" active-inference model). These frame DPDR as down-weighting of interoceptive prediction errors — a *precision pathology*, not a "self-model channel going silent." The article's claim that functionalism faces a "precision problem" because the experiential shift is broader than the functional change is exactly what the interoceptive-inference account dissolves: collapsing the precision of interoceptive predictions is expected to produce a globally felt loss of "mineness." [PubMed + 3](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22291673/)

For dissociative amnesia, the article presents reversibility as a clean interface signature but omits the active controversy: Otgaar, Merckelbach, Mangiulli, Jelicic et al. argue dissociative-amnesia case evidence is weak and the DSM-5 "successfully stored but inaccessible" framing is contested; a 2026 *Consciousness and Cognition* paper reframes it through higher-order theory as *metarepresentational inhibition* of memory retrieval. Crucially, motivated-forgetting/retrieval-suppression accounts (Anderson & Hanslmayr) predict the same reversibility the article treats as evidence for a "bridge that reconnects." [Sage Journals + 3](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/21677026211018194)

### Dimension 2 — Inference Validity (Dualist Interface vs Physicalist Accounts)

The article's core move is: physicalism must "concede" that consciousness tracks connectivity rather than raw activity, and this concession "is itself revealing" because it means consciousness tracks a *relationship*, "which is precisely what an interface model describes." This is a **constrain-vs-establish failure**. The disconnection-between-intact-regions pattern is not a cost physicalism must reluctantly absorb — it is a *direct positive prediction* of the leading physicalist theories:

- **Global / Global Neuronal Workspace (Baars; Dehaene–Changeux).** GNW says consciousness *is* global broadcast via long-range fronto-parietal connectivity. The Dehaene–Changeux model has *simulated* how disrupting long-distance connectivity removes specific contents from conscious access while local processing continues. Selective disconnection → selective loss of access is exactly what GNW predicts, not an anomaly it must stretch to accommodate.
- **Integrated Information Theory (Tononi).** IIT's foundational papers use "functional disconnection" between intact modules as the *worked example* of how Φ collapses without tissue loss, and explicitly name "psychiatric conversion and dissociative disorders" as instances. The dissociative pattern is a textbook IIT illustration. [University of Chicago Press](https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/doi/10.2307/25470707)
- **Predictive processing / active inference (Friston, Hohwy, Clark, Seth).** Dissociation is now standardly modeled as a disorder of precision-weighting — FND (Edwards/Friston 2012), DPDR (Seth 2012; Ciaunica 2022), and dissociation generally as a "functional dysconnection syndrome" in Friston's terms. These deliver targeted, content-specific symptoms with intact substrate and no dualist interface. [PubMed Central](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11523845/)

The article gestures at these (it links GWT and IIT concept pages and concedes a "connectivity-based physicalism" can redescribe the data) but never engages them at full strength — it treats them as theories that *can be stretched to accommodate* the data when in fact they *antecedently predict* it. This is **counterargument absorption**: the strongest opponents are cited but not confronted in their own predictive framing.

The "snap-back" argument (instant recovery suggests reconnection, not reconstruction) is answered in one line by the article itself, and the answer is decisive against it: a connectivity-physicalist holds that restoring the connectivity *is* restoring the state, with no rebuild step required. The article concedes this and still lists the snap-back as "at least suggestive of a bridge" — that is **coherence inflation**.

The "purposive selectivity" argument (dissociation targets threatening content) is the article's most distinctive bid, but it too is pre-absorbed: affective salience biasing which couplings stress mediators disrupt is a standard physicalist account, and the article concedes this in the same paragraph. The argument thus reduces to: the data are *consistent with* an interface. Consistency is not evidence when the data are equally consistent with the better-developed rival.

### Dimension 3 — Hedge Calibration

At the sentence level the hedging ("may favour ... without forcing that conclusion") is honest and unusually disciplined — the article repeatedly concedes the physicalist redescription, and the two-tier framing (convergence "earns the architecture" but "leaves the dualist significance to be argued separately") is genuinely careful. But the calibration is **asymmetric in a way that still steers the reader**:

1. **No falsifier is stated.** The article never says what observation would count *against* the interface reading. GNW and IIT make specific, falsifiable predictions (ignition signatures; Φ reductions; the COGITATE adversarial collaboration). The interface model as presented makes no distinct empirical prediction that diverges from the physicalist prediction — so it does no evidential work.
2. **The "diagnostic" language inflates.** Calling depersonalization "the most diagnostic of the intact-substrate cases" implies discriminating power the article elsewhere disclaims. If both readings predict the same phenomenology, nothing is "diagnostic."
3. **Section architecture overrides the hedges.** The section is titled "Why This Favours Dualism," and the Occam's-Razor subsection asserts the interface model is "more parsimonious explanatorily" because physicalism must make "successive theoretical retreats." This is the reverse of the truth: the connectivity move is not a retreat forced by dissociation; it is the standard, independently-motivated theory. Framing the physicalist's own prediction as an epicycle is the central rhetorical tilt.

### Dimension 4 — Overlooked Literature & Counterevidence

The article should have engaged, and did not:

- **Seth, Suzuki & Critchley (2012)** interoceptive predictive-coding model of presence and **Ciaunica et al. (2022)** active-inference account of DPDR — the current standard for the case the article calls "most diagnostic."
- **Edwards, Adams, Brown, Pareés & Friston (2012)**, "A Bayesian account of 'hysteria'" (*Brain*), and the **Stone/Edwards/Aybek/Hallett** FND programme — directly contradicts the "motor channel disconnect" framing via the increased amygdala–motor coupling finding (Voon et al. 2010; Hassa et al. 2017).
- **Dehaene–Changeux GNW** and **Baars** in their predictive (not merely accommodating) form.
- **The sociocognitive/fantasy model of DID** (Spanos 1994; Lilienfeld, Lynn, Kihlstrom et al. 1999; Merckelbach) and the **2025 *Memory* methodological-quality review** finding DID imaging cannot adjudicate trauma-vs-sociocognitive. [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/10489540/)
- **Otgaar, Merckelbach, Mangiulli** critical work on dissociative amnesia / repressed memory.
- **Illusionism (Frankish)** and **higher-order theories (Rosenthal; Carruthers ISA)** as deflationary alternatives — including the 2026 *Consciousness and Cognition* higher-order-theory paper on dissociative amnesia as metarepresentational inhibition, useful even as a foil.
- **Replication/power concerns**: Reinders (2003) is n≈11 and is presented as robust.

## Recommendations

### (A) Article-specific improvements

1. **Fix the conversion-disorder section first — it contains a factual error.** Replace the "descending motor channel disconnect" framing with the modern FND picture: increased amygdala–SMA/motor coupling (Voon et al. 2010; Hassa et al. 2017) and the Edwards–Friston (2012) aberrant-precision account. Acknowledge that "intact motor system + functional symptom" is now explained by *active generation* of the symptom, not a severed channel. **Benchmark that would change this recommendation:** a post-2015 replication showing *reduced* prefrontal-motor connectivity as the dominant FND signature would partly rehabilitate the disconnect framing.
2. **Update every case to 2012–2026 sources.** Add Seth (2012) and Ciaunica (2022) for DPDR; Edwards/Friston (2012) for FND; the 2024–2026 FND connectivity reviews; Otgaar/Merckelbach for amnesia; the 2025 *Memory* review for DID.
3. **Disclose sample sizes and the DID controversy.** State that Reinders (2003) scanned ~11 patients, that DID imaging is underpowered against the Marek et al. (2022) "thousands" benchmark, and that the trauma-vs-sociocognitive debate is unresolved; quote the 2025 *Memory* conclusion verbatim.
4. **Engage GNW and IIT as predictors, not accommodators.** Rewrite "Why This Favours Dualism" to concede that the disconnection signature is a *positive prediction* of GNW/IIT/predictive processing, then locate the dualist claim where it actually lives (the hard problem / explanatory gap), not in the dissociation data.
5. **State a falsifier and a distinct prediction, or downgrade the evidential claim.** Specify one observation that would distinguish the interface reading from the physicalist reading. If none exists, relabel the piece as "consistent with" rather than "evidence for," and drop "diagnostic"/"favours."
6. **Drop or heavily qualify the Occam's-Razor "successive retreats" claim** — the connectivity move is independently motivated, not an epicycle.
7. **Propagate fixes to the cluster.** The sibling articles ("neurological-dissociations-as-interface-architecture," "conversion-disorder-as-consciousness-side-fault," "dissociative-states-as-intact-substrate-interface-evidence," "altered-states-as-interface-evidence") inherit the same motor-channel and currency problems; the changelog shows this cluster is actively cross-linked, so the error will otherwise compound across the corpus.

### (B) Site-wide methodology improvements

1. **Add an empirical-currency gate.** Require at least one primary source from the last five years per major empirical claim, and flag articles whose newest empirical citation predates a stated cutoff (here, every primary source is ≤2005). The pipeline catches citation-*metadata* errors superbly but does not catch *staleness* — the single most consequential defect in this article.
2. **Require an "opponent's strongest prediction" field.** For each physicalist rival, the article must state what that rival *predicts* (not merely whether it can accommodate the data). This directly attacks counterargument absorption: GWT/IIT/predictive-processing should be engaged on their own terms when they predict the very pattern being cited.
3. **Add an evidential-independence / common-cause check.** Before counting convergence as evidence, require screening the converging routes for a shared physicalist common cause. Here, precision-weighting pathology is a *single* common cause spanning FND, DPDR, and dissociation generally — which undercuts the article's "compound signature / convergence without a shared mechanism" claim at its root.
4. **Mandate a falsifier and a discriminating prediction.** No article should claim data "favour" the dualist reading without specifying an observation that would distinguish it from the physicalist reading; absent one, language must be downgraded to "consistent with."
5. **Counter the constitutional-attractor effect explicitly.** The five fixed tenets channel every clinical phenomenon into "interface channel" language. Add a required, persuasively-written "Best physicalist explanation" section *before* "Relation to Site Perspective," so the tenets do not pre-determine the read.
6. **Introduce external domain-specialist review.** An AI-only pipeline — even with adversarial/optimistic/deep-review passes — homogenizes blind spots: the field's entire post-2012 computational reframing was missed here across multiple review cycles. Commission periodic outside review from clinical neuroscientists / philosophers of psychiatry for empirically-loaded articles.
7. **Add a "coherence-inflation" lint.** Flag constructions where consistency-with-dualism is asserted as support-for-dualism (e.g., "at least suggestive of a bridge" placed immediately after conceding the rival explains the same datum equally well).
