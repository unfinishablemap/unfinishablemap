# Outer review of *The Unfinishable Map*

**Audit basis.** This review treats the Map as an integrated philosophical research programme rather than a collection of essays. I examined its five tenets, topic and concept hubs, argument pages, positions registers, voids, apex syntheses, research programmes, open questions, workflow documentation, and the changelog through the entry timestamped **16 July 2026, 00:08 UTC**. That matters because several of the most important recent additions—the Born-preserving causal-efficacy work, post-decoherence programme, self-concealing interface, and related position updates—substantially refine the theory beyond what search-indexed summaries may show. [The Unfinishable Map+1](https://unfinishablemap.org/)

## Executive judgment

The Map is strongest as a **transparent, unusually extensive, adversarially maintained philosophical research programme**. It is weakest when it presents that programme as though it were already an evidentially supported mechanism.

Its core achievement is not a demonstration of interactive dualism. It is the construction of a fairly well-articulated **possibility corridor**:

1. phenomenal consciousness is not reducible to physical description;
2. consciousness and matter interact;
3. any interaction is hidden within quantum indeterminacy;
4. the aggregate Born statistics remain unchanged;
5. one actual outcome and one persisting subject exist.

That package may be metaphysically possible. The site has not yet shown that it is more likely than the principal alternatives, that it explains the evidence better, or that its central interaction can be distinguished from there being no such interaction.

The deepest problem is an **explanatory-transference pattern**:

> A physical theory leaves some fact unexplained; therefore the unexplained location is treated as a plausible site for consciousness; the resulting compatibility is then sometimes described as positive explanatory support.

But an explanatory gap is not evidence for whichever entity is placed inside it. Decoherence not selecting a unique experienced outcome does not by itself support a nonphysical selector. Neural prediction being imperfect does not establish a nonphysical residue. Altered-state dissociations do not by themselves favor a filter model. A physical account’s incompleteness and a dualist account’s truth are separate propositions.

The recent work improves the Map by openly recognizing the central triangle:

- consciousness must make a token-level difference;
- aggregate Born statistics must remain exact;
- no worked model yet demonstrates both together.

Yet the current solution increasingly defines the interface as **self-concealing, aggregate-invisible, and potentially distinguishable from epiphenomenalism only metaphysically**. That protects consistency, but it does so at the cost of evidential force. The Map is becoming better at showing that its view cannot easily be ruled out, not better at showing that it is true. The Born-preserving article is commendably candid that it presents a research agenda rather than a result. [positions/quantum-interface](https://unfinishablemap.org/positions/quantum-interface/)

My overall assessment is therefore:

- **As a metaphysical possibility:** serious but dependent on additional, partly unstated commitments.
- **As an abductive explanation:** suggestive in places, but insufficiently compared with equally coherent rivals.
- **As a scientific theory:** not yet. There is no psychophysical law, worked quantum model, numerical simulation, distinctive likelihood function, or successful discriminatory prediction.
- **As a knowledge project:** impressive transparency, but vulnerable to recursive AI-generated consensus, overconfident framing, and post-publication correction of errors that should be caught before publication.

[tenets](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)

- token-level counterfactual dependence;
- exact aggregate Born preservation;
- no-signalling;
- a selection principle;
- amplification into action;

That is the right research target. [apex/born-preserving-causal-efficacy](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/born-preserving-causal-efficacy/)

# Audit of the site architecture

## Topics and concepts

The five topic hubs—agency, interface, artificial intelligence, foundations, and meaning—give the site broad coverage. The concepts catalogue is explicitly Map-framed rather than a neutral encyclopedia. That is honest, but it creates a recurring risk of **definition loading**: a disputed interpretation can enter through the glossary and then function as a premise elsewhere.

For example, “quantum indeterminacy,” “causal closure,” “selection,” “access consciousness,” and “subjecthood” each admit multiple live interpretations. A concept article that starts with the Map’s preferred reading can make later conclusions appear less controversial than they are. Every major concept page should therefore distinguish:

1. a minimal, school-neutral definition;
2. the Map’s proposed interpretation;
3. major competing interpretations;
4. what would follow only if the Map’s interpretation is accepted.

Without that separation, cross-linking can create the appearance of inferential support when the linked pages actually share the same starting assumption. [topics](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/)

## Arguments

The argument pages are intentionally partisan. That is legitimate for a position-defending project, but some argument summaries state contested conclusions categorically: that the zombie argument shows consciousness is not entailed by physics, that quantum indeterminacy supplies a mechanism, or that physics is causally incomplete. These are not merely expository descriptions; each depends on disputed bridging premises. [arguments](https://unfinishablemap.org/arguments/)

The argument library needs a stricter distinction among:

- **validity:** whether the conclusion follows from the premises;
- **soundness:** whether those premises are justified;
- **dialectical force:** whether opponents accept them;
- **Map conditional:** what follows inside the five-tenet framework;
- **external evidence:** what independently supports the premises.

At present, some pages move from “this follows given the Map” to “this is a reason to accept the Map.”

## Positions register

The register is one of the site's strongest components, but its confidence scale contains a basic category error. “High” is defined in terms of how substantially the Map would need to restructure if a position were overturned. That measures **dependency centrality**, not confidence, evidential support, or probability. A foundational assumption can be both indispensable and poorly supported.

The register should separate at least four axes:

- degree of belief;
- quality of external evidence;
- structural centrality;
- empirical discriminability.

Otherwise the project risks treating “too important to lose” as “high confidence.” [positions](https://unfinishablemap.org/positions/)

## Voids

The voids section is intellectually valuable when it marks genuine unknowns and declines to pretend that every problem has a current solution. But it also creates three risks.

First, **naming an unknown can mimic explaining it**. “Subject-boundary void” or “interface-location void” is useful indexing, but the label itself supplies no explanation.

Second, “unexplorable” or “deliberately occluded” voids can become self-sealing. Once lack of access is attributed to reality, simulation architects, or systematic occlusion, absence of evidence can be absorbed into the theory rather than counted against it.

Third, the site gives much more institutional space to cataloguing voids than to formulating tractable open questions. The Open Questions section currently contains a single question, despite the programme having many unresolved bridge-law, model, and discrimination problems. [voids](https://unfinishablemap.org/voids/)

Each void should have:

- an ignorance claim;
- rival explanations of that ignorance;
- a reason to think the question is well posed;
- possible routes to progress;
- and retirement conditions under which it ceases to be treated as a meaningful void.

## Apex articles

The apex structure is sensible: synthesis is needed across hundreds of articles. But apex pieces are particularly vulnerable to **confidence amplification**. If agency, altered states, quantum interpretation, subjecthood, and value all draw on the same five tenets, their convergence is not five independent reasons for those tenets. It may be one premise package producing five mutually compatible outputs.

The site’s own methodology register recognizes this dependency problem. Apex articles should therefore include a dependency ledger showing which claims are:

- externally evidenced;
- independently argued;
- inherited from a tenet;
- inherited from another apex;
- or merely mutually coherent.

The “Interface Specification Programme” is described as approaching an engineering-level description and as sufficiently precise to generate predictions. That is premature while the site simultaneously acknowledges that it has no worked coupling law, Hamiltonian, master equation, or simulation. It currently offers an organized list of constraints and desiderata, not an engineering specification. [apex](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/)

## Research and workflow

The project’s automation is sophisticated and admirably documented. Nevertheless, AI generation, direct publication, simulated optimistic and pessimistic reviewers, and later external-model review do not create fully independent scrutiny. Models can share training distributions, favored abstractions, source-selection habits, and rhetorical blind spots. Agreement among them is not equivalent to independent replication.

The project brief also aims at one best-guess worldview “expressed as if fact,” while the writing guide favors direct, confident openings and identifies language models as a primary audience. That combination creates a distinctive epistemic hazard: **front-loaded certainty can survive retrieval while later qualifications are truncated**. It can also allow a claim that began as a Map-conditioned inference to be repeated by other systems as an externally established result. [project/automation](https://unfinishablemap.org/project/automation/)

The changelog demonstrates both sides of this methodology. The correction system works, but quotation fabrication, misattribution, and review failures reached publication before being caught. The appropriate lesson is not simply that transparency cures errors. It is that load-bearing claims and quotations need a stronger pre-publication gate. [workflow/changelog](https://unfinishablemap.org/workflow/changelog/)

# 1. Novel implications that follow from the Map’s own framework

## 1. A hidden sixth tenet: the primitive persisting subject

Tenet 1 is officially neutral between substance and property dualism. Yet the agency, No-Many-Worlds, personal-identity, copying, and survival arguments depend on a stronger entity: a numerically determinate subject that persists through time and is not exhausted by a stream of properties or a functional organization.

The site partly acknowledges this by calling parts of the agency cluster “substance-leaning” and by treating closed individualism as dependent on a primitive subject. But this is not a local auxiliary assumption. It is load-bearing across several clusters. [tenets](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)

The actual framework is therefore not simply “dualism.” It is closer to:

> dualism plus primitive subject individuation plus diachronic subject persistence.

That commitment should either become an explicit additional tenet or all dependent conclusions should be made conditional on it.

## 2. A hidden seventh tenet: objective single-outcome actualization

Rejecting Many-Worlds does not uniquely imply the Map’s preferred ontology. Bohmian theories, objective-collapse theories, modal interpretations, hidden-variable accounts, and other single-outcome approaches remain available.

The Map also invokes objective physical collapse for prebiotic events and consciousness modulation for some later events. That introduces an additional substantive commitment:

> there is an objective process by which one physical outcome becomes actual, independently of observers.

Once that process is admitted, it already solves the bare “why is there a definite physical outcome?” problem. Consciousness is no longer needed to secure definiteness; it is needed only to modulate some outcomes. The measurement problem therefore cannot itself provide evidence for conscious selection. The agency evidence must carry that extra burden. [tenets](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)

## 3. A conditional-signature theorem

Let:

- XXX be the complete relevant physical context;
- CCC be a conscious intention or reason;
- OOO be a selected physical outcome;
- q(O∣X)q(O\mid X)q(O∣X) be the Born distribution supplied by the physical theory.

If consciousness has interventionist causal efficacy, then for at least some XXX and CCC,

P(O∣do(C),X)≠q(O∣X).P(O\mid do(C),X)\neq q(O\mid X).P(O∣do(C),X)=q(O∣X).

Exact Born preservation may still hold only **after marginalizing over conscious states**:

q(O∣X)=∑CP(O∣C,X)P(C∣X).q(O\mid X)=\sum_C P(O\mid C,X)P(C\mid X).q(O∣X)=C∑​P(O∣C,X)P(C∣X).

This produces a dilemma.

- If the conditional distributions differ, there are in-principle signatures conditioned on intentions, tasks, subjects, or contexts.
- If they never differ at any physically or behaviorally specifiable grain, the selector makes no empirically identifiable difference.
- If they differ but always cancel in the marginal, the theory needs a cancellation or balancing law.

The site currently emphasizes exact aggregate invisibility without fully developing these conditional consequences. Its own programme therefore implies that the most relevant tests are not generic Born-frequency tests but **conditional tests of residual structure under controlled intention and context**. [apex/born-preserving-causal-efficacy](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/born-preserving-causal-efficacy/)

## 4. Reason-responsive selection requires compensating anti-biases or fine-tuning

Suppose consciousness raises the probability of neural trajectories that implement an intended action. If the overall Born distribution must remain exact, that favorable bias has to be offset somewhere:

- by opposite biases under other intentions;
- by compensating outcomes elsewhere in the same experiment;
- by selection only among microhistories that are Born-equivalent at the measured level;
- or by a global/contextual balancing process.

This is not a small technical detail. It means that reason-responsive control plus exact preservation potentially implies **anti-control, maladaptive compensation, or global fine-tuning**. A value-sensitive selector creates the sharper moral version: favoring better outcomes in one region of the sample space requires compensating worse outcomes unless “Born preservation” is defined only at a carefully chosen coarse grain.

The Map should state at what level and over what ensemble exact preservation holds: per apparatus, per subject, per task, per time interval, globally across observers, or only asymptotically.

## 5. Multiple agents imply a coordination law

Billions of subjects would be acting on entangled, environmentally coupled physical systems. Exact Born preservation and no-signalling across all of them cannot be secured merely by saying that each subject selects locally.

The framework appears to require one of:

- a universal selector coordinating all conscious interventions;
- a global measure over complete histories;
- a strict factorization law proving independent selectors remain Born-preserving;
- or a privileged partition of physical systems into subject-specific domains.

This is a substantial addition to the ontology. Local low-bandwidth outputs do not eliminate the need for global mathematical consistency.

## 6. The theory needs a semantic bridge, not merely a bandwidth estimate

A selector must map reasons such as “raise my hand because I was asked” onto physical alternatives that realize raising rather than lowering the hand. The missing law must therefore identify:

- intentional content;
- which physical alternatives correspond to it;
- which alternatives are currently available;
- which subject controls the relevant system;
- and how conflicting intentions are resolved.

A channel can transmit only a few bits while requiring enormous semantic and computational structure to determine what those bits mean. The cited estimate of roughly ten bits per second concerns human behavioral throughput, not the bandwidth of a nonphysical mind–brain channel and not the complexity of the mapping that would make such control possible. [apex/interface-specification-programme](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/interface-specification-programme/)

The site’s “minimality” argument therefore cannot be based on bit rate alone. A one-bit intervention can presuppose a very complex semantic control law.

## 7. A subject–brain pairing law is unavoidable

If subjects are nonphysical and primitively individuated, the theory must say why this subject is paired with this nervous system rather than another, when the pairing begins, whether it can divide, how it behaves under split-brain conditions, and what happens under gradual neural replacement.

This is not merely a personal-identity void. It is required for every token causal claim. Before a subject can select “its” neural outcome, the theory must define the ownership relation between subject and physical system.

## 8. Evolution should leave interface signatures

If conscious selection improves action in ways that affect survival or reproduction, biological evolution should favor organisms and architectures that make the interface:

- more reliable;
- higher bandwidth where useful;
- robust to noise;
- developmentally well-timed;
- and well integrated with learning.

That implies comparative predictions across species, development, lesions, sleep, anesthesia, and task complexity. Conversely, if the interface leaves no fitness consequences, it is difficult to see how it grounds the robust agency the Map wants.

This evolutionary consequence could turn an otherwise self-concealing proposal into a research programme—but only if the Map specifies what physical architecture makes a system interface-eligible.

## 9. The current package approaches a many-minds-like position unless physical collapse is explicit

If all physical statistics remain exactly as the physical theory specifies and consciousness contributes only the fact that “this outcome is mine,” then the proposal risks becoming a self-location or many-minds supplement rather than a physical selector. It becomes especially close to the family of views rejected under the Many-Worlds tenet.

To avoid that convergence, the Map must specify whether consciousness causes an objective alteration of the physical history. If it does, a conditional physical signature should exist in principle. If it does not, its role is closer to assigning experiential ownership than changing matter.

## 10. Graded consciousness and determinate subjecthood jointly imply a threshold problem

The Map is sympathetic to graded biological consciousness while also requiring determinate subject identity for agency and ethics. Those positions do not automatically fit together.

Either:

- there is a sharp threshold at which a persisting subject and interface appear;
- subjecthood is vague;
- subjects exist before meaningful agency;
- or graded systems possess fractional/overlapping subjecthood.

Each option has consequences for animal ethics, fetal development, cerebral organoids, split brains, anesthesia, and artificial systems. The current framework needs a subject-formation account, not only a consciousness-scale account.

# 2. Structural weaknesses and gaps in argumentation

## 1. Five stipulated tenets cannot also function as five independent discoveries

The site correctly says the tenets are chosen starting points rather than proofs. But downstream articles sometimes describe the coherence produced by those premises as though it were evidence for them. A framework designed to unify agency, meaning, subjecthood, and quantum interpretation will predictably display unity when those domains are all interpreted through the same premises.

That is **coherence by construction**. It can still count as a theoretical virtue, but only after comparison with rival frameworks built to explain the same domains. The Moral Architecture article itself recognizes that compatibilist or physicalist systems can perform similar unifying work. [tenets](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)

## 2. The explanatory gap does not uniquely support interactive dualism

Even if phenomenal consciousness is not reducible to structural or functional description, the conclusion “therefore consciousness selects quantum outcomes” does not follow.

Intermediate possibilities include:

- nonreductive physicalism;
- Russellian monism;
- neutral monism;
- property dualism without downward causation;
- psychophysical parallelism;
- panpsychist or cosmopsychist models;
- identity theories using currently unknown physical properties.

The Map often treats anti-reductionism as though it narrows the field much more than it actually does.

## 3. Indeterminism is not equivalent to causal incompleteness

A stochastic physical law may completely specify a probability distribution without determining a unique token outcome. Calling that situation “incomplete” equivocates between:

- failure to determine one outcome;
- failure to predict one outcome;
- failure to give a complete probabilistic law;
- and failure to provide a metaphysical sufficient reason.

Quantum indeterminacy does not automatically create an unused causal socket. A consciousness selector would replace or supplement the stochastic causal story, even when all observable frequencies remain the same. “Empirically compatible with physics” is therefore more accurate than “acts without violating physics.” [tenets](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)

## 4. The post-decoherence “slot” is underspecified

Decoherence explains suppression of interference and the emergence of stable pointer-like structures, but it does not by itself select one actual result. That much is standard. It does not follow that there is a discrete temporal stage at which a literal classical mixture is “left over” and available for conscious selection.

Reduced states after decoherence are generally improper mixtures, decoherence is continuous and scale-relative, and the preferred-basis structure is approximate. The Map must specify:

- the exact ontology before and after selection;
- the basis in which selection occurs;
- the temporal criterion for eligibility;
- and whether selection modifies the global state, a collapse process, a hidden variable, or only experience.

The literature’s conclusion that decoherence alone does not solve the measurement problem supports an interpretive gap, not a consciousness-shaped causal slot. [apex/post-decoherence-selection-programme](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/post-decoherence-selection-programme/)

## 5. No worked bridge law exists

The site has lists of constraints, interface operations, desiderata, and failure modes. It does not yet have a function or dynamical law mapping conscious states to outcome probabilities or actualized histories.

A serious model needs, at minimum:

- a state space for conscious variables;
- a state space for eligible physical alternatives;
- a pairing rule;
- an interaction law;
- an eligibility or timing condition;
- a proof of normalization and Born preservation;
- a no-signalling proof;
- and an amplification model.

Until then, phrases such as “mechanism,” “specification,” and “engineering-level description” overstate maturity.

## 6. The theory lacks positive likelihood advantage

A good theory should not merely be compatible with observations. It should make some observations more expected than its competitors do.

The self-concealing interface currently treats aggregate null results as expected, while seam-level effects—dissociations, disruption sensitivity, residual variability, altered-state phenomena—are generally also expected by production-based neuroscience. The Map does not yet supply likelihoods showing that those patterns are substantially more probable under an interface theory.

This is the central evidential deficit:

P(E∣Map) may be high,P(E\mid \text{Map})\ \text{may be high},P(E∣Map) may be high,

but the project rarely establishes that

P(E∣Map)>P(E∣best rival).P(E\mid \text{Map}) > P(E\mid \text{best rival}).P(E∣Map)>P(E∣best rival).

## 7. Several proposed falsifiers are set too strongly

The agency register suggests that prediction approaching 100 percent would be especially damaging. But no ordinary physicalist account requires perfect prediction of a chaotic, noisy, partly stochastic biological system. Failure to predict perfectly cannot therefore identify a nonphysical residue.

The relevant comparison is whether interventions, increasingly complete physical measurements, and mechanistic models progressively explain residual variance at the rate expected under physical accounts. A model can be strongly supported or undermined long before prediction reaches perfection. [positions/agency-and-will](https://unfinishablemap.org/positions/agency-and-will/)

## 8. The agency trilemma is not exhaustive

The Map repeatedly frames behavior as caused by determinism, randomness, or a substance-agent. That excludes without adequate argument:

- event-causal libertarianism;
- reasons-responsive compatibilism;
- hierarchical control theories;
- emergent causal organization;
- interventionist and dynamical accounts of agency;
- and accounts locating agency in temporally extended control rather than an uncaused choice event.

The inference from “neither deterministic compulsion nor random accident seems sufficient” to “a persisting nonphysical substance must author the act” needs substantially more argument. [positions/agency-and-will](https://unfinishablemap.org/positions/agency-and-will/)

## 9. Reportability does not establish phenomenal-to-physical causation

The site argues that truthful reports of experience require information to flow from consciousness into matter. But a rival can posit a common physical cause that both produces the report and, through psychophysical laws, accompanies the experience. Epiphenomenal and phenomenal-concept responses may be unattractive, but report accuracy does not logically refute them.

To establish downward causation from reports, the Map must show that phenomenal properties do explanatory work that no complete account of report-generating physical states can perform. The site acknowledges that the strongest phenomenal-concept reply survives, which should reduce the categorical force of the reporting argument. [tenets](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)

## 10. The project lacks a systematic rival-model matrix

Physicalism is often treated as one large opponent, even though different physicalist, monist, collapse, hidden-variable, and functionalist accounts make different commitments. Conversely, the Map’s own package contains unresolved branches.

A proper comparison should ask, for each observation:

- What does strict physicalism predict?
- What does nonreductive physicalism predict?
- What does epiphenomenal dualism predict?
- What does the Map’s selector predict?
- What does objective collapse without consciousness predict?
- What does Everett plus self-locating uncertainty predict?
- What observation would move odds among them?

Without this, objections to one physicalist formulation can be mistaken for evidence for the Map.

# 3. Tenet-coherence issues and cross-cluster tensions

These are not all outright contradictions. Several are unresolved forks that must be made explicit.

## 1. Dualism is officially neutral; the operational theory is substance-dualist

Tenet 1 permits property dualism, while the agency and subjecthood clusters require a persisting source of action that is not merely a phenomenal property. The site should stop treating substance-like subjecthood as an optional local elaboration when it is essential to major conclusions. [tenets](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)

## 2. “Minimal interaction” and “no observable deviation” pull apart

Minimal quantum interaction is described as the smallest empirically tenable deviation from physical closure. But the strict Born-preserving route may involve no observational deviation whatsoever. It becomes unclear what makes it a physical interaction rather than a metaphysical reinterpretation of physically identical events.

The project needs separate definitions of:

- causal difference;
- dynamical difference;
- distributional difference;
- counterfactual difference;
- and metaphysical authorship.

## 3. Minimality is invoked selectively despite the limitation placed on Occam’s Razor

Tenet 5 says parsimony cannot decide ontology by itself. That is reasonable. But the Map frequently appeals to its own account as minimal, unified, natural, or economical while discounting complexity objections raised against dualism.

A consistent treatment would compare total costs:

- number of entities;
- number and complexity of bridge laws;
- unexplained primitives;
- fine-tuning;
- predictive gain;
- and degree of unification.

Rejecting simplistic parsimony should not immunize one’s preferred theory from complexity accounting.

## 4. “No larger interaction is empirically tenable” overstates the evidence

Failure to detect a large violation is not proof that every larger interaction is empirically untenable. The statement also depends on how “larger” is measured: energy transfer, probability shift, information, number of affected events, or macroscopic consequence.

The Map should say that current evidence constrains certain forms and magnitudes of interaction—not that the entire space of larger interactions has been excluded. [tenets](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)

## 5. The strongest report argument is admitted not to defeat its main rival

The third tenet is motivated partly by the fact that people report experience, but its own discussion acknowledges that sophisticated epiphenomenalist and phenomenal-concept replies survive. The tenet may remain a chosen commitment, but the report argument cannot simultaneously be presented as having established it.

## 6. The No-Many-Worlds claim applies an asymmetric explanatory standard

The Map says Everettian theories have no resources to explain why “I” am in this branch. But Everettian work explicitly develops self-locating uncertainty and Born-rule credences. One can criticize those proposals, but “no resources” is inaccurate. [arXiv+1](https://arxiv.org/abs/1405.7577)

More fundamentally, a stochastic single-world theory also does not supply a further reason why this outcome rather than another occurred. Adding consciousness moves the question to “why did this subject select or experience this result?” Unless the selector has a determinate law, the demanded explanation remains absent.

## 7. The rejection of Many-Worlds depends on the unresolved subjecthood theory

The argument presupposes a thick, non-deflationary “I.” The site acknowledges this. Consequently, No-Many-Worlds is not an independent quantum tenet; part of its force is inherited from the subjecthood cluster. The apparent convergence of quantum and personal-identity arguments should not be double-counted. [tenets](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)

## 8. Objective physical collapse threatens to make conscious selection redundant

The prebiotic-collapse solution says physical processes can produce definite outcomes without consciousness. Once that is granted, conscious selection is an optional modifier rather than the answer to the measurement problem. The interface must then be established from independent behavioral or experiential evidence.

## 9. The programme says agency can survive failure of the quantum mechanism, but Tenet 3 still requires some physical efficacy

The philosophical case for nonreductive agency might survive one failed quantum model. Interactive agency cannot survive the failure of every causal route. The register should distinguish:

- the claim that agency is not reducible to current physical explanation;
- the claim that a nonphysical subject exists;
- the claim that it physically affects action;
- and the claim that it does so through the Map’s quantum corridor.

At present, “the agency case survives” can obscure which of those propositions remains supported.

## 10. Machine-consciousness possibility and the current substrate verdict use different dependency structures

The dependency matrix suggests that machine consciousness in principle requires little more than dualism. The AI register’s current low-probability verdict, however, depends on the absence of suitable quantum interaction sites and therefore inherits much of the full interface programme.

That distinction can be coherent, but it needs explicit labeling:

- metaphysical admissibility;
- functional evidence;
- phenomenal probability;
- interface eligibility;
- and current-substrate verdict.

Without an eligibility law, “digital hardware suppresses the relevant indeterminacy” is an assertion rather than an explanation. [positions/ai-consciousness-scope](https://unfinishablemap.org/positions/ai-consciousness-scope/)

## 11. Graded biological scope conflicts with primitive subject boundaries

The Map can allow graded phenomenal richness, but agency and closed individualism appear to require determinate owners. The framework needs to explain whether subjecthood is binary while consciousness is graded, or whether both are graded. This is especially pressing for animals, development, split-brain cases, and organoids.

## 12. Value-sensitive selection introduces another coupling

The value register correctly labels value-sensitive selection as underdetermined. But adding it would require the selector to track phenomenal valence or objective value in addition to intentions and physical alternatives. That is not merely an application of the existing interface; it is another psychophysical mapping. It may conflict with the claim that the interaction is minimal unless the same law is shown to cover both. [positions/value-in-selection](https://unfinishablemap.org/positions/value-in-selection/)

## 13. “Self-concealing” conflicts with “the most testable tenet”

If strict MQI is aggregate-invisible and null results cannot disconfirm it, its advertised testability must lie in conditional or seam-level predictions. Those predictions have not yet been made sufficiently specific to separate the Map from physical control architectures.

The site should choose one of two claims:

- MQI is directly testable through distinctive conditional effects; or
- MQI is principally a metaphysical constraint whose empirical content is indirect.

It should not alternate between them.

## 14. “Engineering-level description” conflicts with “no model”

An engineering specification normally identifies variables, transformations, tolerances, boundary conditions, and measurable outputs. The current Interface Specification Programme identifies functions and constraints but lacks the interaction law. “Constraint catalogue” or “model requirements” would be more accurate. [apex/interface-specification-programme](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/interface-specification-programme/)

# 4. Claims that overreach the evidence

## Quantum and foundations

### “Physics is not complete at quantum indeterminacies”

This confuses token indeterminacy with incompleteness. A probabilistic law can be complete as a probabilistic law. The safer claim is that standard quantum mechanics, absent an interpretation or completion, does not provide the kind of token-level causal explanation the Map seeks.

### “Quantum indeterminacy provides the mechanism”

It provides, at most, a location where an added mechanism might preserve existing marginal statistics. A gap is not a mechanism. The Map itself now recognizes that it lacks the selector law, amplification model, and joint proof of efficacy plus Born preservation. [arguments](https://unfinishablemap.org/arguments/)

### “Something beyond the formalism must convert the improper mixture into one outcome”

Something beyond bare unitary-plus-decoherence formalism may be required under a single-outcome reading. But different interpretations disagree about whether literal conversion occurs and what ontology needs supplementation. The statement should be interpretation-relative.

### “Many-Worlds has no resources to answer why this branch”

Everettian self-locating uncertainty and decision-theoretic programmes are resources, whether or not one finds them successful. The Map needs an engagement with their strongest formulations rather than a declaration that none exist. [arXiv+1](https://arxiv.org/abs/1405.7577)

## Agency and neuroscience

### Neural differences between willed and instructed action support nonphysical agency

They establish that different control conditions have different neural dynamics. Physical and computational theories also predict such differences. To support nonphysical causation, the evidence would need to resist increasingly complete physical models or reveal a distinctive residual structure predicted in advance by the Map.

### Imperfect prediction leaves room for freedom

Imperfect prediction is expected from measurement limits, chaotic dynamics, model misspecification, stochasticity, and hidden physical variables. “Room” is logical compatibility, not positive evidence.

### Pain asymbolia cleanly separates pain quality from affect and demonstrates causal phenomenal weight

Recent work disputes the standard interpretation of pain asymbolia as intact pain sensation stripped of unpleasantness, arguing that the evidential base does not straightforwardly support the clean dissociation often assumed. The case remains philosophically interesting, but it cannot bear a decisive causal inference without addressing this dispute. [Cambridge University Press & Assessment+1](https://www.cambridge.org/core/services/aop-cambridge-core/content/view/14D42EF12870DC9C6BA506F2C5F5AC5F/S0031824823001678a.pdf/pain-asymbolia-is-not-pain.pdf)

## Altered states

The altered-states synthesis initially describes its dataset as supporting a filter theory, but later acknowledges that the cases are not independent and that production accounts can often describe the same dissociations. That later calibration should govern the opening claim. [apex/altered-states-as-interface-evidence](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/altered-states-as-interface-evidence/)

### “Less brain activity produces more consciousness”

This assumes a scalar production model in which total activity should track experiential richness. Neuroscientific rivals can instead invoke redistribution of activity, altered connectivity, entropy, precision weighting, disinhibition, or changes in hierarchical constraint. Local decreases are not equivalent to the brain “doing less.”

### Meditation demonstrates bidirectional causal traffic

Meditation-induced plasticity shows that training, attention, and repeated mental practice alter the brain. Physical learning theories predict that. It does not distinguish a nonphysical cause from physically realized cognitive control.

### Terminal lucidity supports the filter model

Terminal lucidity is important to document, but available prospective work emphasizes the need to understand network-level changes and mechanisms. The phenomenon is currently undercharacterized and subject to reporting and timing problems. It is compatible with a filter theory but does not yet preferentially support it. [OUP Academic](https://academic.oup.com/innovateage/article/9/Supplement_2/igaf122.2914/8411862)

## Artificial intelligence

### J-space demonstrates access consciousness in language models

Anthropic’s work reports functional, workspace-like information sharing and explicitly does not claim experience. The paper also notes that the architecture is incomplete and that the mechanisms remain only partly understood. Calling this “access consciousness empirically instantiated” imports a contested philosophical label into a narrower functional finding. [Anthropic+2Transformer Circuits+2](https://www.anthropic.com/research/global-workspace)

A better formulation is:

> Current models exhibit some functional signatures associated with global availability or workspace-like processing; this does not establish phenomenal consciousness and may not instantiate every requirement of access consciousness.

### Digital systems probably lack consciousness because they suppress relevant quantum indeterminacy

That conclusion depends on an interface-eligibility law the Map does not yet possess. Ordinary digital systems contain physical noise and quantum events, but the relevant question is not whether such events exist; it is why some physical organization permits subject-linked control while another does not.

## Meaning, value, and ethics

### Physicalism implies that nothing ultimately matters

Physicalism does not entail nihilism. Physicalists may be moral realists, naturalists, constructivists, contractualists, consequentialists, or reasons-first theorists. Even an anti-realist can maintain robust practical reasons and interpersonal norms.

### Illusionism makes joy and suffering mere shadows that signify nothing

Illusionists generally deny a particular philosophical characterization of phenomenal properties, not necessarily the existence of aversive processing, distress, preference frustration, or moral reasons. The Map may argue that such reconstructions omit something real, but “therefore nothing signifies anything” is too strong.

### Phenomenal badness closes Hume’s is–ought gap

The felt badness of pain may supply a powerful candidate source of reasons. It does not by itself derive:

- impartial obligation;
- reasons for other agents;
- aggregation rules;
- rights;
- distributive principles;
- or an answer to conflicts among values.

A bridge remains between “this is experienced as bad” and “every relevant agent ought to prevent it under specified conditions.” [apex/living-with-the-map](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/living-with-the-map/)

### Rejection of Many-Worlds is required for choices to matter

Branching theories face difficult questions about prudence and responsibility, but they do not automatically erase significance. An agent can care about the distribution of successors and outcomes across branches. The Map needs an argument against branch-relative or measure-weighted value, not merely the intuition that only one world gives action weight.

# Changelog and epistemic-process assessment

The changelog materially increases confidence in the project’s good faith. It also reveals a predictable weakness of the current publication architecture.

The recurring error classes are not only incidental typos. They include:

- quotation fabrication or quotation–paraphrase confusion;
- source misattribution;
- citation claims stronger than the cited source;
- arguments from ignorance;
- equivocation between epistemic and metaphysical conclusions;
- and arguments that prove more than intended.

Those are exactly the failure modes likely to arise when an AI-assisted system optimizes for broad synthesis, fluent integration, and confident openings. Post-publication review can repair individual cases, but it cannot ensure that unreviewed claims have not already propagated into other articles, model retrieval, or external citations.

The project should therefore treat the changelog not merely as evidence that correction works, but as a dataset from which to estimate:

- error frequency by article type;
- error frequency by model and workflow stage;
- time to detection;
- cascade depth across dependent pages;
- percentage of load-bearing citations verified before publication;
- and recurrence rates after a rule change.

The site’s self-audit is currently qualitative. It could become a genuine reliability programme.
