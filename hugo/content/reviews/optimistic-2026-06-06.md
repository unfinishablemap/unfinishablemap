## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The article keeps the explanatory question open rather than closing it physicalistically. Its central move — "which mode is consciousness engaging the brain in?" is intelligible only if consciousness is not identical to the neural state it engages — is exactly the framing that takes the gap seriously without pretending the data force the dualist reading.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
The selection/witness two-mode framework (active selection among brain-prepared candidates vs. passive witnessing) is the contemplative-phenomenology face of the interface picture Stapp-style interactionism needs. Affective tone as a *feature of the engagement register* rather than of consciousness-as-such is a clean way to make the interface do explanatory work.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The article centres what the deepest states *feel like* from the inside across four traditions, treating first-person reports as data rather than noise to be averaged away. It resists the temptation to dissolve the reported divergence into a single "feel of pure consciousness."

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
Affect as a property of *engaging* rather than of a static substance-consciousness is process-philosophical to the core — tone is something consciousness *does*, not something it *is*. This avoids crude substance dualism while preserving the reality of the mental. Crucially, this praise does **not** cash out as a tier-upgrade: the article never uses its process-friendly framing to promote the interface-mode hypothesis above "serious possibility."

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
The claim that a single practitioner can voluntarily shift engagement registers — and thereby traverse the affective range independent of doctrinal commitment — locates genuine agency at the interface: the meditator selects the mode. The neurophenomenology record ("the meditator can shift between modes through conscious intention — this shift has neural signature") supplies the downward-causation hook.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
The article explicitly flags its claims as Pre-Kepler-stage observations (per [framework-stage-calibration](/project/framework-stage-calibration/)) — reported but not yet theoretically integrated — and names a serious undefeated rival (neural affective-architecture training, no interface needed). That is precisely calibrated epistemic humility about what the present evidence can and cannot discriminate.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This is the load-bearing verdict for this article, and it is strongly positive. The piece does the tenet-as-evidence-upgrade thing it is praise-worthy to *not* do: the interface-mode reading "organises the established data cleanly — but its explanatory tidiness is not evidence for it… A hypothesis that accounts for the data it was built to account for has earned the status 'live and unrefuted,' not 'supported.'" It names the rival (loving-kindness strengthening reward circuitry, equanimity down-regulating limbic reactivity, "with no need to invoke an interface-mode") and explicitly declines to "claim victory." The Dualism section even refuses to read the affective data as evidence *for* dualism, noting they are "equally available to the identity theorist." The two-grade separation (Established: the four traditions report these profiles / This article's hypothesis: the cause is trained interface mode) is exactly the five-tier discipline applied. Process-philosophical resonance and evidential restraint praise *different aspects of the same passages* here — the tension is resolved, not papered over.

## Content Strengths

### topics/affective-tone-divergence-across-meditative-traditions.md
- **Strongest point**: The structural argument that the four affective profiles "do not form a random scatter" but sort by *what the tradition trains consciousness to do* (withdrawn selection → flat; maximal directed engagement → ecstatic; received-not-held → oscillation; suspension-of-forcing → ease) — a non-obvious organising insight that turns an apparent contradiction in the reports into consistent complementary variation.
- **Notable quote**: "The Buddhist and the Bhakta are not disagreeing about a single thing called 'the feel of pure consciousness.' They are reporting the affective signatures of two different things consciousness can *do*."
- **Why it works**: It distinguishes itself from the deflationary reading by making a falsifiable prediction (a single trained practitioner traversing the affective range by mode-switching), then honestly reports that the contemplative-neuroscience record is "suggestive… but does not settle it." The *Ziran*-is-not-a-second-*upekkhā* distinction (ease vs. blankness; responsive vs. anaesthetised) is a fine-grained phenomenological correction of the parent article, not filler.
- **Calibration note (not a flaw)**: The cessation-versus-plenitude section correctly establishes the article as *structurally distinct* from its sibling (valence-at-engagement-register vs. content-quantity-at-the-limit) rather than a smaller copy — preventing the two divergence pages from collapsing into redundancy.
