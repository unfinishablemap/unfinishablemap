## Executive Summary

This is a mature, internally-consistent cohort whose shared signature is **the construction/constructor distinction**: each article concedes — often generously — that the relevant phenomenon (the percept, the mindreading capacity, the narrative self, the transparent world-model) is built, learned, or culturally shaped, and then argues that the *building* presupposes an experiential subject and a phenomenal medium that the constructionist/functionalist story cannot itself deliver. The register discipline is strong throughout: every article frames its dualist reading as *interpretation of shared data* rather than data-compelled, and the freshest of them (perceptual-architect) does this with unusual precision. The most valuable opportunities are **intra-cohort cross-linking** — four of these five articles work obviously adjacent ground yet under-link each other, including one near-omission (theory-of-mind discusses the DMN by name but does not link [default-mode-network](/concepts/default-mode-network/), and vice versa).

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)

The cohort keeps the explanatory gap load-bearing without letting it become a slogan. Perceptual-architect localises it twice over — "Predictive processing frameworks describe the neural mechanics of constancy and learning with impressive precision. They explain *that* the generative model maintains stable priors... They do not explain why stable priors feel like seeing a white wall" — so the gap is shown operating on a *worked* mechanism rather than asserted in the abstract. Transparency-opacity supplies Chalmers something rarer: a dissociation argument. An opaque representation (blur, depersonalisation, lucid dream) "still has a what-it-is-likeness... even though its representational status has changed. What-it-is-likeness is invariant under a transformation that alters everything else about the representation's functional role." That is a clean phenomenal-vs-functional separation exhibit, and it is exactly the form of argument Chalmers needs against functionalism.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)

Theory-of-mind gives the quantum-coherence reading its most concrete cognitive demand: recursive mindreading "requires holding multiple representational levels in coordinated awareness... bound together," and the article ties this to gamma-band synchronisation across mPFC/TPJ/pSTS as the integration the [Minimal Quantum Interaction](/tenets/#minimal-quantum-interaction) framework would underwrite. Crucially it then *hedges honestly* — "Neural processing timescales (tens to hundreds of milliseconds) vastly exceed quantum decoherence times... The Map treats this as a promising research direction, not established fact." Stapp's programme is engaged seriously and not oversold; the binding-of-nested-levels framing is a genuinely apt place to locate a coherence claim.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)

Strongly served. Perceptual-architect deploys Husserlian adumbration (*Abschattung*) to show constancy is "built into the intentional structure of experience itself. We do not first see a trapezoid and then infer a circle. We see the circle *in* the trapezoid." Transparency-opacity closes on Nagel's own *View from Nowhere*: "attempts to objectify one's own representational process generate new transparent representations rather than revealing the process." Social-construction grounds the whole asymmetry in Zahavi's first-personal givenness. Across the cohort the first-person standpoint is treated as data with structure, not as a feeling to be explained away — precisely Nagel's demand.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)

The cohort avoids crude substance dualism by treating the self and the percept as *activities* rather than things. Perceptual-architect's whole thesis is that consciousness "architects" rather than receives, and the perceptual ratchet frames learning as consciousness "constructing its own future perceptual conditions through sustained engagement" — process language, not substance language. DMN-as-filter reads the resting self as an ongoing *activity* of constraint ("ongoing active filtering that constructs the sense of being a particular self") rather than a stored homunculus. Social-construction's three-layer model (minimal/narrative/substantial) lets the narrative self be genuinely processual and socially co-constituted while reserving a non-social floor. *Constraint per skill spec:* none of this Whitehead-friendliness is used to upgrade any evidential tier — see the Hardline Empiricist verdict, which confirms the two personas converge here rather than conflict.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)

Theory-of-mind supplies the cleanest agency hook: moral responsibility "requires representing the agent as aware of their own mental states — capable of evaluating reasons and choosing among alternatives," and it ties Level 3+ metarepresentation directly to [libertarian free will](/topics/free-will/) ("Libertarian free will requires consciousness enabling consideration of alternatives; moral responsibility requires theory of mind enabling attribution of that same consciousness to others"). Social-construction reinforces it: "Social construction does not create agency; it gives an existing agent materials to work with." Agency is treated as prior to and presupposed by the social/cognitive scaffolding, which is Kane's structural commitment.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)

Transparency-opacity is the cohort's mysterian centrepiece and handles cognitive closure with real discipline. Its standout move is the *limits-of-opacity* section: opacity "grants awareness of the representational status without access to the representational process... It does not replace the illusion with knowledge of the underlying mechanism." This refuses the seductive over-claim that contemplative or pathological opacity *reveals the machinery* — it reveals only *that* there is machinery. The epistemic-circularity treatment (Alston, Carruthers, Hoffman's Fitness-Beats-Truth, properly flagged as contested) is exactly McGinn's territory, held without either defeatism or false hope.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)

This cohort is **calibration-clean**, and three exhibits deserve naming as *restraint praise* — tier-upgrades that were structurally available and declined:

1. **Perceptual-architect's Bidirectional-Interaction section is a model of the discipline.** It had every opportunity to read attention-gated plasticity as evidence for downward causation and explicitly refused: "Learning makes the pattern more vivid rather than the evidence stronger: the same attention-gated plasticity is fully describable in brain-side terms, so the appeal to downward causation is the Map's interpretation of the phenomenon, not a measurement that compels it... whether it is *consciousness* doing the restructuring remains tenet-dependent rather than empirically forced." This is the *"tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating"* pattern executed verbatim. Praise-worthy precisely as a thing *not* done.

2. **DMN holds the production-vs-filter line symmetrically.** Repeatedly — psychedelic, meditation, and cross-cultural-convergence sections each state that *both* models predict the data and "neither reading is forced by the data alone." It even ships an explicit "What Would Challenge This View?" section with four falsification conditions. The filter interpretation is never smuggled in as the established reading.

3. **Social-construction flags its own inference status.** Its cross-cultural-invariance claim is labelled in-text as "a philosophical inference from the nature of first-personal givenness rather than an empirical finding, and it is offered as such" — and the zombie thought-experiment is conceded to "not resolve this dispute neutrally" because "the argument's force depends on already taking phenomenal consciousness seriously." This is the honest acknowledgement that the argument is framework-internal.

Theory-of-mind is the one to watch in this cohort because it touches the animal-consciousness boundary, and it passes: the ape Level-2 evidence is called "contested," the human-ape gap is stated as a *pattern* that "suggests" rather than proves, and the "third-factor" and "cultural practice" alternatives are stated fairly before being answered. No organism is tier-upgraded on tenet-load. The Process Philosopher's process-friendliness above and this evidential discipline **converge** rather than conflict across all five articles — the signature of an honestly-calibrated cohort.

## Content Strengths

### topics/consciousness-as-perceptual-architect.md
- **Strongest point**: The constancy/learning pairing as "complementary architecture" — one holds invariants steady, the other creates new invariants, both via attention, both facing the same gap. The synthesis section earns the coalescence (this article was merged from two predecessors and shows no seam).
- **Notable quote**: "Where constancy holds existing perceptual objects steady, learning constructs new ones."
- **Why it works**: It makes a single dualist point twice from opposite directions (stabilising vs transforming), which is more persuasive than either case alone — and the Gibson differentiation-not-enrichment result gives the learning half a real empirical spine.

### concepts/theory-of-mind.md
- **Strongest point**: The Level 0–4 hierarchy mapped onto the metarepresentational threshold, with the moral-cognition ladder (dog bit me → person knew it was wrong and chose anyway) making the abstract levels concrete and consequential.
- **Notable quote**: "They read behaviour without reading minds — or rather, they read minds without knowing that minds are what they read."
- **Why it works**: The Jourdain framing turns a potentially dry levels-taxonomy into the Map's signature have-states-vs-know-you-have-them distinction, and the LLM section applies the same blade to AI without special pleading.

### concepts/default-mode-network.md
- **Strongest point**: The disciplined refusal to let filter theory win by default — every empirical section explicitly grants the production model equal purchase on the data, then locates the disagreement at the hard problem rather than at the neuroscience.
- **Notable quote**: "Filter theory would predict exactly this: the filter serves survival, not wellbeing."
- **Why it works**: The Killingsworth-Gilbert 47%-mind-wandering datum is woven into a substantive claim (the self-narrative is costly, not neutral) rather than dropped as trivia, and the "What Would Challenge This View?" section is a genuine falsification scaffold.

### concepts/phenomenal-transparency-opacity-spectrum.md
- **Strongest point**: The taxonomy of opacity-routes (degraded signal, processing disruption, chemical, contemplative, perceptual anomaly) each tied to a different architectural revelation — and then the deflationary limits-of-opacity section that prevents the whole apparatus from over-claiming.
- **Notable quote**: "The machinery that generates the representation remains hidden even when the representation's constructed character is exposed."
- **Why it works**: It gives the Map a precise vocabulary (transparency degree = inverse of introspective availability) for a phenomenon — naive realism's irresistibility — that the site invokes constantly elsewhere; this is the anchor concept the cohort leans on.

### topics/social-construction-of-self-vs-phenomenal-self.md
- **Strongest point**: The one-way dependence argument — social construction *requires* phenomenal subjects to recognise, interpret, and internalise, but phenomenal selfhood does not require the social — backed by developmental priority (Rochat), the zombie-community thought experiment, and the Metzinger-regress rebuttal.
- **Notable quote**: "Performance without experience is mechanism without meaning."
- **Why it works**: It engages the strongest constructionist move (the organism/self distinction) on its own terms and shows it concedes the minimal self, rather than knocking down a straw constructionism. The Russian-blues and Schachter-Singer cases are granted real force before the boundary is drawn.
