## Critiques by Philosopher

### The Eliminative Materialist (Churchland)
"Medium-status" is exactly the kind of folk-psychological place-holding eliminativism predicts: a category defined by what *cannot* be inspected, evidenced *only* by breakdown, and explicitly neutral about substrate. The article's §"Content-Status, Capacity-Status, Medium-Status" concedes the reductionist reading ("functional architecture with privileged-access asymmetries… ontologically continuous with content-status") and only resists it later via "an additional, independent move" — i.e., the dualist tenets. So the eliminativist's complaint lands but is pre-absorbed: the article never claims medium-status *establishes* irreducibility, only that it "poses a structural challenge." Fair, but the eliminativist will press that "structures other contents without being a content of the field" is satisfied trivially by any sub-personal process (early visual feature extraction, retinal gain control) that no one calls a void — the criterion in §"The Inflation Risk" does real work but the three sub-conditions are themselves stated in introspective vocabulary ("must transform what it is," "negative phenomenology"), which an eliminativist reads as begging the question.

### The Hard-Nosed Physicalist (Dennett)
Dennett's natural target is the move from "introspection cannot inspect the operation" to "the operation has a special metaphysical status." The article is careful — it routes the inference through "outputs-by-deformation, not inspection-while-operating" and grants Carruthers' interpretive-introspection reading as a live physicalist option. The residual pressure: §"The Eye That Cannot See Itself Seeing" treats the *circularity* as "the structural face of medium-status, not a defect better methods could overcome." Dennett would say this is precisely the inflation — the circularity is an artefact of insisting on first-person inspection as the method; third-person cognitive science inspects relevance-filtering, salience-weighting, and metacognitive gating routinely. The article half-answers this ("tractable to *outputs* analysis… while resisting transparent inspection of its own *operation*") but never says why *transparent first-person* inspection is the privileged test rather than a parochial one. That is the live unsupported premise.

### The Quantum Skeptic (Tegmark)
Not applicable — the article makes no quantum-mechanical claims. The "Relation to Site Perspective" gestures at interface-candidacy but explicitly downgrades it to "consistency-and-suggestion rather than evidence" and warns against affirming the consequent. No Tegmark-style timescale objection bites here.

### The Many-Worlds Defender (Deutsch)
No MWI engagement in the article; nothing for Deutsch to dispute. (Contrast the 2026-06-05 review of the quantum-interface positions register, where the branch-ontology issue is live.)

### The Empiricist (Popper's Ghost)
This is the attack the article most directly anticipates — §"What Would Falsify This Cluster" names three concrete defeaters (controlled bypass of the relevance filter, lesion-dissociation of the four operations, verifiable scheme-articulation while operating). Strong move. The Popperian residue: all three falsifiers are framed so that *the criterion explains away any apparent success* — e.g., scheme-articulation "transforms what it inspects," so a contemplative who *did* report the scheme could be re-described as having produced "content-about-relevance," not operative inspection. The article flags this self-sealing risk ("a worked-out counter-example would put the stipulation under pressure") but does not specify what would distinguish genuine articulation from the criterion's mode-shift escape hatch. The falsifiers are operational at the level of *behaviour* (a bypass either happens or not) but the *interpretation* retains a degree of freedom the article should bound more tightly. Medium issue at most — the lesion-dissociation falsifier is genuinely clean and the article correctly weights it highest.

### The Buddhist Philosopher (Nagarjuna)
The article's "attenuation without elimination" thesis (§"The Eye That Cannot See Itself Seeing": "every reported case is reported from somewhere") is in direct tension with the Buddhist claim that *nirodha-samapatti* / cessation is precisely the case where the medium is removed and nothing is reported from anywhere. The article cites "contemplative cessation" nowhere by name despite invoking "contemplative bracketing" as an edge case. Nagarjuna would say the cluster's central empirical generalisation — the medium can be deformed but consciousness does not continue with it fully removed — is exactly what the cessation literature contests, and the article owes that case an engagement. This is the most substantive content gap: the "attenuation without elimination" claim is load-bearing (it is "what the constitutive reading predicts and what a capacity-status reading does not," per §"The Eye That Cannot See Itself Seeing") yet the one tradition that reports elimination is not addressed.

## Counterarguments to Address

### "Attenuation without elimination" vs. contemplative cessation
- **Current content says**: §"The Eye That Cannot See Itself Seeing" — "consciousness does not continue with the medium fully removed… That asymmetry is what the constitutive reading predicts and what a capacity-status reading does not." §"What Would Falsify This Cluster" lists "verifiable scheme-articulation while operating" but not a cessation case.
- **A critic would argue**: The contemplative / Buddhist literature on *nirodha-samapatti* and contentless cessation reports states in which the field is alleged to be fully removed, not merely attenuated — a direct counter-example to the load-bearing generalisation. The article elsewhere in the corpus engages cessation (the corpus has cessation-relevant void articles); this apex inherits a claim the broader Map treats more carefully.
- **Suggested response**: Add one or two sentences acknowledging the cessation case and either (a) folding it into "outputs-by-deformation" (the cessation report is itself a *post-hoc* output, not inspection-while-operating, preserving the asymmetry) or (b) flagging it as the open empirical question that bears on the constitutive reading. Either move is cheap and closes the most exploitable gap. Cross-link the relevant corpus cessation article.

### Why is *transparent first-person* inspection the privileged test?
- **Current content says**: The circularity is "not a defect better methods could overcome"; medium-status features remain "tractable to outputs analysis… while resisting transparent inspection of its own operation."
- **A critic would argue** (Dennett): Third-person science inspects all four operations routinely; privileging first-person transparent inspection as the test smuggles in the very introspective primacy dualism wants to defend.
- **Suggested response**: The article need not concede the point but should state explicitly *why* first-person operative-transparency is the relevant test for *this* category (because the void is defined relative to the field's self-presentation, not relative to third-person tractability). One clarifying sentence in §"Content-Status…" or §"The Inflation Risk" would inoculate the charge of question-begging.
