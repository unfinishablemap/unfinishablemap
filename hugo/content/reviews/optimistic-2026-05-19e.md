### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)

Nagel would feel directly addressed by **clinical-dissociation-as-systematic-evidence**'s §"Depersonalization-Derealization" subsection (lines 58–66). The article's distinction between *intact processing* and *altered felt-reality* is Nagel's bat-question reformulated as a clinical syndrome: there is something it is like to inhabit oneself, and that something-it-is-like-ness can selectively vanish while cognitive capacity persists. "The patient can reason, speak, and act. What vanishes is the felt sense that any of this is happening to *them*." This is the Nagelian point routed through psychiatric phenomenology rather than animal phenomenology, and the precision is striking — DPDR is a controlled experiment on the felt-reality stamp.

**Neurological-dissociations** routes Nagel's question through the affective channel at lines 82–88 (pain asymbolia). The clean separation between sensory-discriminative dimension (intact) and affective-motivational dimension (abolished) is the same move Nagel makes — there is a *what-it-is-likeness* to pain's awfulness that is not exhausted by the sensory signal. The article's commentary at line 88 — "If pain's awfulness were just more processing in the same nociceptive stream, you could not abolish it while leaving the processing intact" — is Nagel-style reasoning rendered as a clinical-grade discriminator rather than as a thought experiment.

**Anaesthesia**'s §"Content Without Access" treatment of ketamine (lines 70–75) gives Nagel the inverse exhibit. Behavioural unresponsiveness matches propofol, yet patients report vivid experiences. The xenon-ketamine contrast (same primary receptor, opposite phenomenological outcomes) is exactly the kind of *modular structure of what-it-is-like-ness* that Nagel's framework asks for but standard neuroscience-only frameworks struggle to deliver. The phenomenology dissociates from the pharmacology in a way that names interface components as distinct *qualitative registers*, not just distinct information streams.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)

**Active-reboot**'s §"What active reboot does not establish" closing paragraph (line 89) is McGinnian humility *applied to a single finding*: the closing-reopening asymmetry is real, the mechanism-shared reopening pathway is real, the temporal precedence of molecular preparation over behavioural return is real, *and these facts together constrain — without determining — which consciousness-substrate relationship is correct*. This is McGinn's "intellectual humility about what we can know" deployed without sliding into epistemic defeatism.
