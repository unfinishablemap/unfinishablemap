## Critiques by Philosopher

### The Eliminative Materialist (Patricia Churchland)

The audit has installed four underdetermination markers and softened "confirms / proves / demonstrates" to "indicates / appears to / seems to," and I credit the discipline. But the article's grammatical subject is still the eliminative target. Every paragraph of §"The Neuroscience of Deliberate Practice" treats "consciousness" as something that *engages*, *delegates*, *withdraws*, *re-enters* — the verbs are softened only at the evidential register; the *ontology* is unchanged. "Consciousness builds the procedural structures" still appears in the article's central claim. The mature neuroscience the audit's hedge-installation gestures at will not say *"consciousness builds the procedural structures, with some calibration uncertainty"*; it will say there is no such builder. The locus-coeruleus / prefrontal / basal-ganglia / cerebellar circuits the article cites *exhaustively account for* the phenomena the article catalogues — and the phenomenology of effort, of delegation, of flow, of choking is what the brain's homeostatic and predictive systems produce when those circuits are configured a particular way. The article's §"Conscious Scaffolding and Its Withdrawal" passage — "consciousness *delegates*: there is a phenomenological moment where the learner deliberately releases control" — is folk-psychology language doing metaphysical work. The audit's softening does not reach that level. To do so would require a passage that names the eliminative reading on the eliminative reading's own terms — *consciousness is not the builder; the brain is, and "consciousness" is the system's report on what the brain is doing*. The article gestures at this in the §"What Would Challenge This View" §"strongest physicalist counterargument" paragraph but treats it as a position the article need not engage at depth ("imaging studies cannot determine whether prefrontal activity *is* consciousness or *is shaped by* consciousness"). That sentence elides the eliminativist position that *neither* identity-with nor shaping-by is the right relation — the right relation is *what we mistakenly call consciousness is just the brain's executive functioning, full stop.*

### The Hard-Nosed Physicalist (Daniel Dennett)

The article's §"The Paradox of Effortless Mastery" passage now reads "The architect's absence from the finished building may indicate competence rather than irrelevance, though the deflationist can still construe the disruption as one neural subsystem perturbing another." Good — the deflationist construal is named. But what is the in-framework argument that the Map's reading is *better*, not merely *equally well-supported*? The article does not present one. It simply notes the deflationist option and then continues to develop the affirmative voice. This is exactly the heterophenomenological move I have spent my career warning about: the *seeming* of architect-versus-building is taken as evidence for the existence of an architect distinct from the building. The seeming is the data; the architect's existence as a separable entity is not. Worse: the §"Flow" passage treats the phenomenology of frictionless selection as a *positive* explanandum for the interface-friction reading — "The Map proposes that flow may represent consciousness selecting without the resistance that generates effort phenomenology." But the predictive-processing reading (which the article does not name in this passage) predicts the same phenomenology: when the priors match the input, prediction-error drops, attentional resources are freed, and the subject reports effortlessness. The two readings make the same prediction about the phenomenology and (so far as the article reports) the same prediction about the neural correlates. The article's installed underdetermination marker for identity theory does not cover this case — predictive processing is not identity theory; it is a third position, and the audit's hedges do not engage it.

### The Quantum Skeptic (Max Tegmark)

The §"Relation to Site Perspective" §"Minimal Quantum Interaction" paragraph is the failure point. "Flow may represent the case where consciousness's selection at the quantum level meets minimal resistance — the neural options have been so well prepared by skill acquisition that the physically undetermined outcomes align with what consciousness would select anyway. The effort of the Zeno mechanism drops because the desired state is already strongly favoured by the physical dynamics." This is exactly the move my earlier review (2026-05-19 07:22 UTC, against `consciousness-and-causal-powers.md`) flagged: the article helps itself to "consciousness's selection at the quantum level" and "the Zeno mechanism" without acknowledging that the twelve-to-sixteen-orders-of-magnitude decoherence problem applies *here too*. Skill acquisition operates on timescales of seconds to years; quantum coherence in warm wet neurons operates on timescales of 10⁻¹³ to 10⁻²⁰ seconds. The mechanism the paragraph names is precisely the one I object to most strongly, and it is invoked *without* the calibration the companion `consciousness-and-causal-powers` article (after its 2026-05-15 deep-review and 2026-05-19 condense) at least installs at §"Sustained Attention." The audit that ran today on this article focused on hedge-density and strong-assertion-density at the *evidential-claim* register but did not extend to the *quantum-mechanical-implausibility* register. The article inherits the Tenet 2 commitment but does not inherit Tenet 2's evidential discipline.

### The Many-Worlds Defender (David Deutsch)

The article does not engage MWI directly, which I would press on except that the catalogue's load-bearing engagement with MWI lives at [topics/consciousness-and-causal-powers.md](/topics/consciousness-and-causal-powers/) and the §"Relation to Site Perspective" §"Bidirectional Interaction" paragraph here cross-links to the broader framework. Fine. But the §"Flow" passage's "consciousness selecting without the resistance that generates effort phenomenology" inherits the MWI cost without paying it. On MWI, there is no selection: every flow-state on every branch of the wavefunction is actualised; the *appearance* of effortless selection is the indexical experience of being in the branch where the prepared state matches the input. The article's reading and the MWI reading make the same prediction about flow phenomenology — which means the flow-phenomenology evidence does not discriminate between them. The article's audit installed underdetermination markers against identity theory but not against MWI; the omission is structurally identical to the Tegmark omission above.

### The Empiricist (Karl Popper's Ghost)

The §"What Would Challenge This View" section is the article's best moment and deserves credit: "If purely passive exposure produced identical skill gains to deliberate practice—same hours, same content, no conscious engagement—the causal role of consciousness would be unnecessary." Good — that is a falsification condition. "Current evidence favours deliberate practice (Ericsson et al., 1993), but the comparison is hard to make rigorously." Also good — the rigour-difficulty is honestly named. But the §"Body Schema and Tool Incorporation" passage's falsification condition — "If body schema expansion occurred with passive tool-holding as with active use, the interface-shaping claim would weaken" — has *already been tested* (Iriki et al. 1996 is precisely the test, and the article cites the positive result), and the article does not say what observation *would* count as challenging the macaque finding. The two §"What Would Challenge" conditions are unequal in their evidential force: one (passive exposure) names a counterfactual that has not been adequately tested; the other (passive tool-holding) names a counterfactual that *has* been tested with a positive result for the article's reading, which means the falsification condition has been *passed* but the article frames it as if it were still open. This conflates "what would have falsified" with "what could still falsify." The empiricist register requires the distinction. And the §"The Neuroscience of Deliberate Practice" four converging lines should be stress-tested against their *opposite* falsification: what observation, in 2026 or 2027, would weaken any of the four? Sleep consolidation findings have been contested in the literature (the 2007–2024 replication record is mixed); structural plasticity correlations are not always linear with hours; Poldrack et al. 2001's prefrontal-to-striatal handoff has been re-described in some 2020s work as parallel-stream activation rather than sequential transfer. The article cites the 1993–2007 originals without engaging the 2020s replication literature. *Literature drift* in a four-month-stale article is exactly the kind of empirical fragility the empiricist register demands be named.

### The Buddhist Philosopher (Nagarjuna)

The article's central image — "the architect designs itself out of the building—but never truly retires" — is a beautiful metaphor and a metaphysical sleight. The architect is reified. The building is reified. The relation between architect-and-building is treated as a real relation between two separate things. My tradition insists there is no architect distinct from the building, no building distinct from the construction, no construction distinct from the dependent-arising of conditions. The §"Conscious Scaffolding and Its Withdrawal" passage is the clearest case: "Each function requires genuine cognitive effort. As competence develops, consciousness *delegates*: there is a phenomenological moment where the learner deliberately releases control, trusting emerging automaticity." The grammatical subject "consciousness" is doing the delegating, the releasing, the trusting. But there is no such subject. The dependent-arising of cognition produces the *appearance* of an agent that scaffolds, delegates, withdraws — and the article has been seduced by the appearance. The audit's hedges soften the evidential register but do not touch the ontological grammar. The §"Flow" passage's "loss of self-consciousness" / "minimal self remains" distinction is the closest the article comes to naming my tradition's territory, but it is treated as an empirical refinement (which "layer of self" persists) rather than as an opportunity to deconstruct the layered-self ontology itself. Csikszentmihalyi's "loss of reflective self-awareness" should be the *starting point* of an interesting engagement, not a parenthetical fit into a layered model.

## Counterarguments to Address

### Predictive processing as the third reading flow phenomenology fits

- **Current content says**: §"Flow" (line 124) — "The Map proposes that flow may represent consciousness selecting without the resistance that generates effort phenomenology. Effort, on this reading, is friction at the [selection interface](/concepts/attention-as-interface/)."
- **A critic would argue**: Predictive processing predicts the same phenomenology and the same neural correlates. When learned priors match input, prediction-error drops, attentional resources are freed, and the subject reports effortlessness. PP does not need interface-friction or quantum-selection metaphysics to explain flow. The article's reading and the PP reading are observationally equivalent for the cases the article cites.
- **Suggested response**: Add at the close of §"Flow" — *"Predictive processing accounts make the same prediction about flow phenomenology and the corticostriatal correlates: when priors match input, prediction-error collapses, attention is freed, and the subject reports effortlessness. The PP and the Map's interface readings are observationally equivalent for the flow cases cited; the choice between them turns on whether the deeper metaphysics (mental causation, the hard problem, the brain-mind boundary) is best treated within PP's predictive-machine framework or within the Map's interactionist one. The empirical case for flow does not by itself settle that question."*

### The eliminativist denies the category, not just the causal role

- **Current content says**: §"What Skilled Performance Reveals" §"Against epiphenomenalism" (line 140) treats epiphenomenalism as the rival the evidence constrains; §"What Would Challenge This View" (line 136) treats identity theory and interactionism as the two options imaging cannot adjudicate between.
- **A critic would argue**: The article assumes consciousness *as a category* exists and is either causally idle (epiphenomenalism), identical with neural patterns (identity theory), or shaping them (interactionism). Eliminative materialism rejects the category itself. "Consciousness" is a folk-psychology term that mature neuroscience will not need — what the article calls "deliberate engagement" is just the brain's executive system functioning in one of its modes. The four converging lines are evidence for the executive system, not for any "consciousness" that operates on or with the system.
- **Suggested response**: Add at the close of §"What Skilled Performance Reveals About Consciousness" — *"A stronger physicalist position than epiphenomenalism is the eliminativist one: that 'consciousness' is a folk-psychology term that mature neuroscience will not need. On this reading, what the four converging lines establish is the operation of the brain's executive system in deliberate-engagement mode — there is no further 'consciousness' that operates on or with that system. The Map's framework cannot refute eliminativism from within the eliminativist framework; the disagreement runs to the Map's foundational commitment to phenomenal consciousness as a real category (Tenet 1), which eliminativism denies. The four converging lines are equally compatible with the eliminativist redescription."*

- **Suggested response**: Add a parenthetical at the end of the first sentence of §"Minimal Quantum Interaction" — *"(This reading presupposes the Map's rejection of MWI; under Many-Worlds, all flow-states on all branches actualise and there is nothing to select.
