## Citation Audit (publisher-of-record, ai_citation_metadata_unreliable 3-state)

- The body's "pairing" (*Paarung*) attribution (line 109) is **faithful** — it is Husserl's term in the Fifth Meditation for the analogizing constitution of the other as co-subject. No content change needed.
- The reference read "Husserl, E. (1931). *Cartesian Meditations*. Kluwer Academic Publishers" — internally inconsistent: 1931 is the date of the *French* original (*Méditations cartésiennes*, Paris: Armand Colin, trans. Levinas & Peiffer); Kluwer did not exist in 1931 and only carried the English Cairns translation (via Nijhoff) decades later. Corrected to: "Husserl, E. (1931/1960). *Cartesian Meditations: An Introduction to Phenomenology* (D. Cairns, Trans.). The Hague: Martinus Nijhoff. (First published 1931 as *Méditations cartésiennes*, Paris: Armand Colin.)" — consistent with the body's "(1931)".
