## Pessimistic Analysis Summary

### Critical Issues Found

- **Nagel misquotation**: The article presented "The standpoint of the thinker does not present itself to the thinker: they are that standpoint" as a direct quote from *The View from Nowhere* (1986). Nagel's original text uses masculine-generic pronouns; the gender-neutralised rewording cannot appear inside quotation marks without altering Nagel's words. **Resolution**: Converted the passage to a clearly-attributed paraphrase, preserving the philosophical content while dropping the quotation marks that implied verbatim quotation. The separate "falsely objectifying view" phrase survives as a short direct quote because it is Nagel's actual coinage.

- **Nietzsche attribution**: The line "One can pursue truth, or be honest about what in us is served by such a pursuit, but not both" was presented in quotation marks as though directly from Nietzsche, but it does not correspond to any verifiable Nietzsche passage---it appears to be an AI-generated synthesis of Nietzsche's argument in *Genealogy* §III and *Gay Science*. **Resolution**: Reframed as a paraphrase ("The Nietzschean implication is bracing: one can pursue truth, or be honest about what in oneself is served by such a pursuit, but it is difficult to do both at once"), removing the misleading quotation marks.
