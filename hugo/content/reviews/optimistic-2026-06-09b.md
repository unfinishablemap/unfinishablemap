## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
`agent-causation.md` keeps the hard problem load-bearing without letting it do illegitimate work: the verification-limit section concedes that consciousness "cannot verify its own causal efficacy from inside, because every check is performed by the faculty under investigation," then builds a five-anchor convergent case that explicitly survives the failure of any single anchor. Chalmers would recognize the structural honesty — the explanatory gap is treated as a feature of the territory, not a debt the article pretends to have paid.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
The interface localization is exactly the move Stapp's framework wants: agent causation "interfaces with physics at quantum indeterminacies, where physics specifies probabilities but not outcomes," with the decoherence/timing-gap objection met by the quantum-biology coherence results rather than waved away. The article cites the open challenge (warm-tissue decoherence) at the same place it cites the mitigation — no concealment.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
`libet-experiments.md` and the agent-causation phenomenology section center the first-person in the right register: the felt distinction between being *pulled* by a loud noise versus *directing* attention to difficult text is mapped to ventral vs dorsal networks without pretending the third-person signature settles the first-person question. The phenomenology of effort is treated as evidence-bearing precisely because its correlation with cognitive load "would be coincidental" if epiphenomenal.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
`prehension.md` is a model of disciplined selective borrowing. It states the tension openly — "Whitehead's system treats prehension as universal ... which entails panpsychism. The Unfinishable Map takes prehension's *relational structure* as illuminating without accepting that all actualities are experiential" — and flags that "Whitehead would reject the restriction." This is the correct use of the persona: process-philosophical resonance (active incorporation of the past, subjective aim, inheritance-plus-novelty) is harvested for structural insight and explicitly *not* cashed out as evidence for panpsychism. `agent-causation.md`'s creative-generation section likewise invokes Whitehead's continuity-of-causation as *complementary support*, never as the engine of a commitment.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
The cluster supplies precisely the sourcehood-plus-alternative-possibilities pairing Kane requires, and tracks his own 2024 convergence toward agent-causal elements (`agent-causation.md` cites *The Complex Tapestry of Free Will* and notes the cross-tradition convergence). The luck-objection response is the event-causal-at-the-moment-of-effort route Kane (1996) develops, integrated rather than merely cited.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
The verification-limit section is the cluster's quiet McGinn exhibit: both interactionism and epiphenomenalism "hit first-person verification failure for structural reasons, which suggests the failure inheres in the question's architecture rather than in particular arguments." That symmetry — neither side can certify itself from inside — is exactly the cognitive-closure thesis given as an empirical exhibit rather than asserted.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
**This persona's verdict is load-bearing here, and it is strongly positive — but on the *moral*-tier analogue of the discipline rather than the evidential-status scale.** `compatibilist-symmetry-challenge.md` is the worked example of restraint Birch should want: it concedes that "sophisticated reasons-responsive determinism explains the same deliberative phenomenology, the same praise/blame practices, the same character-formation arc" as libertarian agent causation, then locates the differentiator *outside* moral theory (the hard problem, the explanatory gap, the conditions for downward causation). The article explicitly forbids the four slippage moves, including "claiming unique moral leverage" without naming a discriminator and "treating compatibilism as pragmatic-only." Most impressively, it applies the discipline *reflexively*: the third residue (regret over genuine alternatives) is flagged as leaning on the Map's own tenets, "so it is a discriminator internal to the Map's framework rather than a tenet-neutral moral one." That is tenet-as-leverage being praise-worthily *declined* in the very sentence that introduces it. The Process-Philosopher and Hardline-Empiricist verdicts converge here — `prehension.md` resolves the panpsychism-temptation honestly, and `compatibilist-symmetry-challenge.md` resolves the uniqueness-temptation honestly — which is the signature of a cluster that has metabolized both disciplines.
