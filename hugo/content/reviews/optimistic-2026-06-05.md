## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The fork is framed exactly as a dualist who takes the explanatory gap seriously would want: the dissociation "fractionates reward into pieces — motivation here, hedonic signature there — that need not be experienced, which raises, without answering, whether unexperienced 'liking' is value at all." This is the gap-respecting move — it keeps "is there something it is like to undergo a liking reaction?" as a live, non-trivial question rather than assuming the neural signature exhausts it. The value-in-experience reading ("value exists only *for* an experiencing subject") is precisely the irreducibility intuition, and the article is careful to ground it in prior commitment, not in Berridge.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
`valence-and-conscious-selection` does the load-bearing physics work: it asks how anticipated valence could enter a Stapp-style selection law, offering two concrete (and honestly labelled speculative) mechanisms — valence determining *which* pattern is observed, versus valence setting the observation *rate*. The bandwidth section is a genuine Stappian insight: a single valence currency is *compatible with* the ~10 bit/s ceiling because the evaluative compression has already happened unconsciously, so consciousness selects among pre-denominated options. This turns the bandwidth constraint from an objection into a structural argument.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The whole cluster centres the first-person: "nothing would *matter* to anything" absent an experiencing subject is the Nagelian core. The wanting-liking article's wedge — that Berridge's *own* taxonomy already separates objective core "liking" from subjective conscious pleasure — is a clean way of showing the felt dimension is not a philosopher's invention but is already nominally present in the leading neuroscientist's framework.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The cluster avoids crude substance dualism: valence is treated as a dimension *of* experience that does (or may do) real selective work, not as a ghost pushing neurons. The teleology section ("outcomes are selected *because they matter*") is process-philosophy-friendly — local, event-level final causation rather than a substance imposing itself. **Crucially, and per this persona's standing constraint, none of this Whiteheadian resonance is used to upgrade evidential status.** The article does not argue "process philosophy is congenial, therefore unfelt liking is probably value." It argues the opposite — that congeniality is not evidence. So the Process Philosopher's praise here is safe to give in full, because the article has pre-empted the slippage the constraint guards against.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
Value-sensitive selection gives agency real content: consciousness selects among physically underdetermined outcomes *for reasons that bottom out in felt value*, which is exactly the Kanean picture of an agent as ultimate source. The "Why Not Attention Alone?" subsection's regress argument — attention without valence is directionless, and "prior attention directed current attention" faces an infinite regress — is a sharp argument for why genuine agency needs an evaluative ground-floor, not just a steering channel.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
Strong epistemic humility throughout. "The case is open but unsettled"; "the neuroscience, for all its precision, is silent on the phenomenology"; the explicit "trap worth marking" that Berridge "is not a dualist and his work is not evidence for dualism." The article repeatedly marks the boundary of what the evidence can reach — exactly the Mysterian discipline of knowing where inquiry currently stops.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
**This is the load-bearing verdict for this cluster, and it is overwhelmingly positive.** The wanting-liking article is a worked instance of *tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating* done correctly:

- It had the **structural opportunity to slide the value-in-experience reading up the scale on tenet-load and explicitly declined** — the dedicated subsection "What the dissociation does and does not establish" separates the empirical register (motivation/hedonic-impact separable; no single value currency; dopamine ≠ pleasure; wanting without liking) from the interpretive register, and assigns the dualist reading *zero* evidential lift from the data: "Treating it as proof of the dualist reading would be exactly the kind of evidential over-reach the Map's evidential-status-discipline is built to prevent — coherence with the Map's view is not the same as independent support for it." That restraint is itself the strength to name.
- It **names the move**: "coherence with the Map's view is not the same as independent support for it" is the *tenet-removes-a-defeater-does-not-upgrade-status* pattern in the article's own words.
- The Map's adoption of the reading is honestly attributed to "prior commitments in consciousness-value-connection — not because Berridge proves it." The data is correctly downgraded to the role of *anchor for the question*, not *evidence for the answer*.
- `valence-and-conscious-selection` reinforces this: its falsification hooks are real disconfirmation conditions ("If selection is invariant under such manipulation, value is doing no selective work"; "A complete mechanistic model of choice that needs no experiential term would leave the value-in-experience reading explanatorily idle — surviving only as a metaphysical add-on, which by the Map's own evidential-status-discipline would be a cost, not a vindication"). An article that lists the ways its own thesis could be *defeated* and counts a successful mechanistic model as a *cost to itself* is the gold standard of this persona's praise.

**Process Philosopher / Hardline Empiricist convergence:** the two personas converge rather than conflict here. The article is Whitehead-congenial *and* evidentially disciplined precisely because it walls the congeniality off from the evidence ledger. Per the skill's design note, convergence of these two personas in praising the same passage is the signal that the article has resolved the tension honestly. No calibration concern; no `refine-draft` task warranted on this axis.
