## Executive Summary

The article is in good condition: its external citations check out against the live literature, it carries the weaker-than-headline ("objective-collapse-plus-modulation") disclosure that earlier reviews demanded, and it engages the decoherence objection honestly. The most consequential remaining problem is an **internal consistency drift against the tenets file**: the "From von Neumann–Wigner to Stapp" section endorses Stapp's Process-1 / context-selection placement as a virtue the Map favours, while the canonical tenets statement now explicitly endorses *outcome*-selection and registers Process-1 as a path "the Map could adopt but has not — because it would weaken outcome-selection to context-setting." A secondary problem is a navigational-hub overstatement in the "Where the Modulation Framing Appears" section.

## Critiques by Philosopher

### The Eliminative Materialist (Churchland)
"The whole article is an exercise in finding 'logical space' for a folk-psychological primitive. You concede the demarcation problem ('we have no agreed criterion for consciousness') and the mechanism deficit ('names the proposed cause without specifying how it operates'), then proceed as if the lineage still earns its place. Once you grant that consciousness has no operational criterion *and* no mechanism, what survives is a placeholder dressed as a research programme. The honest reading of your own 'Standard Objections' section is that V-N-W is undischarged on every front you list." — The article actually states most of this against itself; the eliminativist has little to add that the article does not already concede. That candour is a genuine strength, but it also exposes how thin the positive case is: the article's defence rests almost entirely on *relocating* problems to other articles (prebiotic-collapse, multi-mind, interface-formalism) rather than discharging any here.

### The Hard-Nosed Physicalist (Dennett)
"You write that V-N-W 'preserves the causal efficacy of consciousness' and 'engages the self-stultification problem,' then immediately concede the standard physicalist reply that *physical correlates* do the reporting work. Good — but you present this as the interpretation's 'contribution to make causal efficacy easy to secure within the formalism.' Making something *easy to secure within a formalism* is not evidence that it obtains. The formalism permits a great many things." The self-stultification paragraph (line 70) is well-hedged and survives this.

### The Quantum Skeptic (Tegmark)
"You cite my 2000 numbers correctly — 10⁻¹³ s microtubule, 10⁻²⁰ s ion-channel — and then offer two 'partial responses' (interface locality, motor-control Zeno) that you admit 'neither settles the timescale question.' So by your own statement the interpretation 'does not yet engage physics.' That sentence should be the headline of the article, not a clause in the fifth standard objection." — The decoherence paragraph (line 84) is the article's strongest and most honest passage; the Tegmark critic is largely pre-empted. No defect here, but see the note below on whether the 2025–2026 contestation (Wiest, Beshkar, Hagan) deserves a one-line mention for currency, as it appears in sibling articles.

### The Many-Worlds Defender (Deutsch)
"You say MWI 'was motivated partly as an escape from the von Neumann–Wigner conclusion' and that 'the measurement problem's openness to consciousness is a feature, not a bug.' Calling it a feature is a bare assertion — the same opening you celebrate is what Everett *removed* as a defect. Your Frauchiger–Renner paragraph correctly notes F–R lets one keep MWI by dropping single-world; you then say the Map 'runs through interface locality and indexical identity rather than denying the theorem's force,' which is a promissory note, not a response to F–R." — Accurate. The F–R handling is honest but the Map's positive escape is deferred to other articles.

### The Empiricist (Popper's ghost)
"The article concedes the position is, in the tenets' own words, 'empirically indistinguishable from chance under any aggregate-statistics test.' This article does not *say* that — it points to mechanisms (interface locality, Zeno) as if they were empirical openings. A reader of this page alone would not learn that the Map's outcome-selection thesis is, by construction, untestable in the aggregate. The unfalsifiability cost that the tenets state plainly is invisible here." — A real gap: the testability cost stated in `tenets.md` line 102 is not surfaced on the article that most invites the reader to expect empirical traction from the lineage.

### The Buddhist Philosopher (Nagarjuna)
"You speak of consciousness as 'the one element that resists quantum-mechanical treatment,' an 'abstract ego.' You have reified the very thing whose emptiness the measurement problem might have taught you. The 'whose consciousness?' question you defer to the indexical-identity article is unanswerable precisely because there is no bounded self to anchor the index." — Philosophical, not a defect; the article's deferral to indexical-identity is the appropriate move.

## Critical Issues

### Issue 1: Stapp/Process-1 framing diverges from the tenets' explicit outcome-vs-context choice
- **File**: `obsidian/concepts/von-neumann-wigner-interpretation.md`
- **Location**: §"From von Neumann–Wigner to Stapp", lines 90–92
- **Problem**: The article presents Stapp's Process-1 / "Heisenberg choice" (context-selection) placement favourably: *"If it counts the location of the influence... Stapp's approach has the edge. The Map's interest is mostly in the second sense: an interpretation that lives at Process 1 leaves the Schrödinger dynamics and the Born rule intact, which is a virtue for theoretical conservativeness."* But the canonical tenets statement (`obsidian/tenets/tenets.md` line 102) now reads: *"The mechanism this tenet commits to is influence over which outcome becomes actual, not merely over which measurement the brain performs... a Process-1-only relocation is registered there as an alternative the Map could adopt but has not — because it would weaken outcome-selection to context-setting and thereby weaken this tenet."* The tenets also name **post-decoherence outcome selection** as "the path the Map endorses most strongly." So the article rhetorically leans toward the placement the Map has explicitly *declined*, and never references the [post-decoherence-selection-programme](/apex/post-decoherence-selection-programme/) that is now the flagship statement of the endorsed path.
- **Severity**: Medium (internal contradiction with the canonical tenets statement; reader is steered toward the wrong commitment)
- **Recommendation**: `refine-draft` to (a) reframe lines 90–92 so the Process-1 "theoretical conservativeness" point is registered as a virtue the Map *acknowledges but does not adopt*, marking that the Map commits to outcome-selection at a cost the tenets state; (b) add a sentence pointing to the post-decoherence-selection-programme apex and the tenets' outcome-vs-context choice. Do **not** delete the Process-1 discussion — it is good content; the fix is to align its evaluative load with the tenets.

## Counterarguments to Address

### "Von Neumann himself related the cut to consciousness"
- **Current content says**: §"Von Neumann's Movable Cut" attributes to von Neumann the identification of the observer's "abstract ego" as "the only element that cannot itself be placed into quantum superposition."
- **A critic would argue**: The historical literature (e.g. the 2025 *Studies in History and Philosophy of Science* treatment "Between myth and history: von Neumann on consciousness") stresses that von Neumann did **not** explicitly relate measurement to consciousness; the consciousness step is London–Bauer's and Wigner's.
- **Suggested response**: The article is already careful here — it says von Neumann "presented this as a feature of the mathematical formalism, not as a metaphysical commitment" and reserves the consciousness thesis for Wigner. No change needed; noted for completeness. (If a future refine touches this section, a half-sentence flagging that the "abstract ego" reading is contested would harden it, but this is optional.)
