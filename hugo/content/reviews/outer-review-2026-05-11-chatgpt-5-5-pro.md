# Full-site audit of The Unfinishable Map

## Audit basis

The latest changelog entries I found, dated **2026-05-11**, show recent deep-review activity on `quantum-biology-and-neural-mechanisms`, `suspension-void`, and `agency-void`, with the latter two explicitly updated to reciprocally link and distinguish themselves from the newly created `decision-void`. The site's own framing is that the Map is a **human-directed, AI-assisted, versioned philosophical framework** with more than 300 articles, constrained by five chosen tenets: dualism, minimal quantum interaction, bidirectional causation, rejection of many-worlds, and limits on Occam's Razor. The tenets page explicitly says these commitments are "not proven" but are chosen starting points; the project brief adds that the Map aims at a single best-guess picture and may express it "as if fact." [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/) [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/) [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/project/project-brief/)

## Executive judgment

The Map's strongest move is **not** "quantum consciousness" in the popular Orch OR sense. Its strongest move is a more disciplined three-part strategy:

First, it refuses to let physicalist explanations of function count as explanations of phenomenality. This is clearest in the binding problem, aphantasia, phenomenal-output/causal-machinery dissociations, and the voids programme. The binding article's BP1/BP2 distinction is one of the site's most stable argumentative tools: computational coordination is not yet phenomenal unity. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/the-binding-problem/)

Second, it tries to convert interactionist dualism from a vague metaphysical position into an **interface specification problem**: a low-bandwidth, feedback-dependent control channel through which consciousness selects among brain-prepared alternatives without energy injection. That is a stronger position than simply saying "mind affects matter." [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/interface-specification-programme/)

Third, its newer **post-decoherence selection** and **selection-only** articles sharply constrain what the Map may claim. Post-decoherence selection avoids requiring long-lived neural superpositions; selection-only mind-influence limits causal claims to already brain-generated candidate sets, with Born-rule-preserving or ensemble-vanishing signatures. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/post-decoherence-selection-programme/) [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/selection-only-mind-influence/)

The Map's recurring weakness is **confidence drift**: tenet-coherent possibility often becomes explanatory fit, explanatory fit becomes evidence, and evidence sometimes becomes near-certainty in page summaries. The site is unusually self-aware about this; its coherence-inflation page explicitly names the risk that confident internal cross-citation can become illusory support. But the risk remains visible across the corpus. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/project/coherence-inflation-countermeasures/)

# 1. Novel insights or inferences the site has not yet stated explicitly

## 1.1 The Map needs a "causal budget ledger"

The selection-only article implies a hard constraint: if mind only selects among brain-prepared alternatives, then every claimed mental-causal effect must be paid for out of a very small information-transfer budget. The article already states per-event limits, ensemble convergence toward Born statistics, and the inability of mind to choose content not encoded in the candidate set. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/selection-only-mind-influence/)

The novel inference is that **every article invoking mental causation should inherit a causal budget**. Free will, placebo, choking, attention disorders, meditation, animal cognition, AI consciousness, and time-arrow claims are currently treated as adjacent supports for one another. But under strict MQI, they are all drawing on the same tiny channel. The question becomes: does the Map's total claimed causal workload exceed what its minimal interface can plausibly carry?

Concrete version: each mental-causation article should specify the candidate set, the selection bandwidth, the level of amplification, the expected statistical signature, and the nearest physicalist explanation. Without this ledger, "minimal" risks becoming a global escape hatch rather than a measurable constraint.

## 1.2 Post-decoherence selection makes much of the quantum-biology material optional, not foundational

The tenets page still leans on biological quantum effects as evidence that evolution can harness coherence, while the post-decoherence programme says the timing gap is sidestepped if consciousness selects after decoherence. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/) [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/post-decoherence-selection-programme/)

The novel inference: **if post-decoherence selection is the Map's strongest mechanism, neural coherence evidence becomes a plausibility supplement, not a load-bearing premise**. This should change the rhetorical order of the site. Instead of "biology can maintain coherence, therefore quantum consciousness remains live," the stronger path is: "decoherence leaves the outcome problem; if collapse/actualisation is real, consciousness could be a selector; quantum biology only matters for mechanism candidates that require pre-decoherence coherence."

This would make the Map less vulnerable to microtubule-specific or coherence-specific objections.

## 1.3 The "AI exception" should force a distinction between consciousness and report-grounded consciousness

The epiphenomenalism article admits that self-stultification proves only that **some** consciousness must be causally efficacious, specifically consciousness whose discourse is causally shaped by its own phenomenal states. It leaves open cases where consciousness discourse is inherited rather than experientially grounded. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/epiphenomenalism/)

The Machine Question and Open Question apexes already acknowledge epiphenomenal AI as a genuine gap. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/machine-question/) [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/open-question-ai-consciousness/)

The novel inference: the Map needs a formal distinction between:

**Causally coupled consciousness** — experience that affects reports/actions through an interface.

**Report-grounded consciousness** — experience whose own phenomenal states help generate its consciousness-discourse.

**Inherited-discourse consciousness** — possible experience whose reports are caused by training, culture, or imitation rather than by the experience itself.

That distinction would keep Bidirectional Interaction strong without overextending the self-stultification argument.

## 1.4 The common-cause null should govern the voids catalogue, not just convergence arguments

The new common-cause-null page warns that convergence across traditions, substrates, or methods only has cumulative evidential force if the sources are genuinely independent. It specifically notes that convergence can collapse into a single upstream architecture read many times. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/project/common-cause-null/)

The voids taxonomy argues that many voids cluster around the consciousness-physics boundary and that this structured pattern reveals something about the territory, though it qualifies the claim. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/taxonomy-of-voids/)

The novel inference: **the voids catalogue itself is the Map's largest common-cause-null test case**. Many voids may recur not because the territory has many independent boundaries, but because human introspection has one shared architecture of opacity. The Map should score each void cluster for independence: empirical, phenomenological, clinical, contemplative, linguistic, quantum-theoretic, and cross-cultural sources should not be counted as independent until shared cognitive or cultural machinery is discounted.

## 1.5 The Map's real methodological innovation is "inspectable metaphysical research programme," not "AI philosophy"

The site distinguishes itself from AI explainer sites by publishing process, history, review, authorship, and cross-linking; it also runs deterministic review cycles and external AI red-team reviews. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/project/why-this-is-different/) [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/project/automation/)

The novel inference: the Map should describe its method less as "AI-assisted philosophy" and more as **a public Lakatosian research programme for a metaphysical framework**. Its distinctiveness is not merely that AI helps write articles; it is that claims are versioned, red-teamed, linked to tenets, and increasingly given falsification conditions. The falsification roadmap already points in this direction. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/falsification-roadmap-for-the-interface-model/)

# 2. Structural weaknesses in argumentation and organisation

## 2.1 The strongest caveats are often buried below stronger summaries

Many articles contain excellent caveats, but the opening summaries frequently speak more strongly than later sections justify. The Machine Question opens with "current AI almost certainly lacks consciousness," while later sections acknowledge epiphenomenal AI, non-temporal consciousness, engineered quantum substrates, and open detection problems. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/machine-question/)

The same pattern appears in mental causation. The placebo/choking article says these phenomena supply evidence for mental causation and that placebo demonstrates consciousness directing physical processes, but it later acknowledges that neither phenomenon settles the philosophical debate. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/empirical-phenomena-mental-causation/)

The site's internal countermeasure already diagnoses this exact failure mode: speculative claims can become high-confidence claims when synthesized into articles. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/project/coherence-inflation-countermeasures/)

## 2.2 The site lacks a reader-facing dependency graph

The Map has strong cross-linking, but it is hard to tell which claims are:

tenet-level commitments,  

derived philosophical claims,  

mechanism hypotheses,  

empirical claims,  

interpretive readings,  

void-derived apophatic claims,  

or speculative research leads.

The falsification roadmap classifies the five tenets by falsifiability type, which is useful, but that classification does not yet propagate visibly through the rest of the site. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/falsification-roadmap-for-the-interface-model/)

A reader-facing dependency graph would prevent "internal link density" from being mistaken for support. The coherence-inflation page already warns against circular support loops; the missing feature is a visible support graph showing which external evidence, tenet, or speculative inference each claim depends on. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/project/coherence-inflation-countermeasures/)

## 2.3 The flat indices do not express epistemic structure

The topics, concepts, voids, research, and apex indices are broad and useful, but they mostly list articles by title and modified date. They do not show whether an article is foundational, contested, speculative, newly created, under review, or downstream of a tenet. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/) [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/) [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/voids/)

This is especially problematic because the site is large, actively changing, and explicitly written for both humans and LLM-based tools. The project brief says important information is placed early because LLMs may truncate large files; that same design logic should apply to epistemic status. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/project/project-brief/)

## 2.4 "Fit with the tenets" sometimes substitutes for independent warrant

The Phenomenology-Mechanism Bridge is careful: it says coherence across phenomenology, neuroscience, physics, and metaphysics is a theoretical virtue but not evidence by itself. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/phenomenology-mechanism-bridge/)

That caveat should be promoted site-wide. Several adjacent pages use "the Map's framework predicts this" or "this is what the tenets expect" in ways that can slide from internal coherence to evidential support. The falsification roadmap itself acknowledges that Minimal Quantum Interaction is broad enough to absorb disconfirmation of individual mechanisms unless the Map prevents silent mechanism-hopping. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/falsification-roadmap-for-the-interface-model/)

## 2.5 Research notes are clearly labelled, but research-derived claims need stricter provenance on article pages

The research index says research notes are working documents, not conclusions, and can include views that conflict with the tenets. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/research/)

The article corpus would benefit from a visible distinction between "this article cites primary literature," "this article cites a research note," and "this article cites an internal synthesis." The coherence-inflation page already recommends provenance tagging and direct citation rather than internal citation chains; that should be rendered as visible claim metadata, not only workflow policy. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/project/coherence-inflation-countermeasures/)

# 3. Coherence issues with the five tenets

## 3.1 Dualism is officially ontology-neutral, but agency articles drift toward substance-bearing dualism

The tenets page says dualism is neutral between substance and property dualism, with irreducibility doing the core work. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)

The Free Will article, however, says the Map's agent-causal reading commits the framework toward substance dualism or substance-bearing property dualism. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/free-will/)

This is not a fatal contradiction if it is local to free will. But if free will and agent causation are central to Bidirectional Interaction, the Map's practical ontology is less neutral than the tenets page suggests. The tenets page should either preserve neutrality while explicitly flagging agency as a substance-leaning subprogramme, or the Free Will article should mark its ontology as a stronger downstream commitment rather than the Map's default.

## 3.2 Minimal Quantum Interaction is sometimes "minimal and hidden," sometimes experimentally ambitious

The tenets page says MQI is the smallest non-physical influence, rules out energy injection, and rules out any interaction empirically detectable under current precision. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)

The falsification roadmap says the quantum-level tenets generate concrete experimental predictions and that MQI is the most empirically tractable tenet. It also admits three different readings: corridor, outside-corridor, and trumping. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/falsification-roadmap-for-the-interface-model/)

This is manageable, but only if every quantum-interface article declares which reading it uses. Otherwise "minimal" can mean "undetectable by construction" in one article and "experimentally testable soon" in another.

## 3.3 Bidirectional Interaction is asserted as a tenet, but the agency cluster shows it cannot be internally verified

The tenets page says consciousness causally influences physical outcomes and that our ability to discuss qualia is strong evidence against epiphenomenalism. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)

The Agency Void says consciousness cannot verify whether it causes anything, and later says Bidirectional Interaction cannot be empirically grounded in the usual sense; it is a starting point shaping inquiry rather than a conclusion directly derived from evidence. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/voids/agency-void/)

This is one of the Map's most important tensions. It is also intellectually productive. The fix is not to weaken the tenet, but to label the distinction clearly: Bidirectional Interaction is a metaphysical commitment supported by self-stultification and indirect evidence, not a directly introspectible datum.

## 3.4 No Many Worlds is load-bearing in more places than its evidential basis supports

The No Many Worlds tenet is explicit: MWI is rejected because of ontological proliferation and indexical identity problems. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)

The Decision Void says No Many Worlds is load-bearing because decision presupposes actual resolution; an MWI reading dissolves the void by saying every option is taken. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/voids/decision-void/)

The falsification roadmap, however, says collapse and MWI currently make identical predictions for feasible experiments and that the Map's MWI rejection is primarily philosophical rather than empirical. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/falsification-roadmap-for-the-interface-model/)

That does not make the tenet incoherent, but it does mean MWI-dependent claims should be treated as interpretation-dependent, not as empirical consequences of the voids or decision data.

## 3.5 Occam's Razor Has Limits is sometimes used asymmetrically

The tenets page treats parsimony as defeasible and warns against dismissing dualism merely because physicalism is simpler. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)

The Many-Worlds article uses ontological extravagance as its first argument against MWI, while acknowledging that the Map's Occam tenet warns against naive parsimony. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/arguments/many-worlds-argument/)

The article's move is defensible if ontological extravagance is framed as a burden-shifting consideration, not a decisive refutation. The risk is that the Map refuses parsimony when it threatens dualism but invokes it when it threatens MWI. The Many-Worlds article should explicitly rank its objections: indexical identity should be primary; ontological proliferation should be secondary and defeasible.

# 4. Cross-cluster contradictions between adjacent articles

## 4.1 Free Will vs Decision Void

Free Will says the core evidence for agent causation is phenomenological: the felt difference between choosing and observing, effort, and willed-action signatures. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/free-will/)

Decision Void says the deliberation→commitment transition is not introspectively available, that reports are late and reconstructive, and that the Map's interpretation is not forced by the data. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/voids/decision-void/) [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/voids/decision-void/)

This is the sharpest local contradiction. The Map wants phenomenology to support free will, but its decision-void analysis says phenomenology cannot access the decisive moment of choosing. The resolution should be explicit: phenomenology supports the **surface architecture of agency**, not direct access to the causal act of decision.

## 4.2 Free Will vs Agency Void

Free Will says the philosophical case establishes agent causation, while Agency Void says both interactionism and epiphenomenalism face structural verification failure. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/free-will/) [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/voids/agency-void/)

The stronger, more coherent formulation would be: the Map's free-will case supports agent causation as the best fit within its tenet package, but the Agency Void blocks any claim that consciousness can verify its causal power from inside.

## 4.3 Quantum Consciousness vs Machine Question vs Tenets

The tenets page leans on biological quantum effects and contested microtubule coherence estimates. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)

The Quantum Consciousness article is more careful: it says the neural case is unproven, that quantum biology precedents are specialised molecular systems, and that even discrete mechanisms still require relevant quantum states to survive long enough to be selected. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/quantum-consciousness/)

The Machine Question is even more direct: it says the MQI tenet is a philosophical commitment and that no experiment has demonstrated relevant coherence in neural tissue. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/machine-question/)

The contradiction is not in content but in tone. The tenets page should be brought into alignment with the more careful articles.

## 4.4 Interface Specification Programme vs Selection-Only Mind-Influence

The Interface Specification Programme presents a converging low-bandwidth control-channel architecture. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/interface-specification-programme/)

Selection-Only Mind-Influence imposes strong information-transfer and Born-rule constraints, and explicitly says the empirical corridor is not evidence for the dualist interface; it is merely a bound the interface must live within if it exists. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/selection-only-mind-influence/)

Every interface-specification article should therefore distinguish "what the interface would do if real" from "evidence that the interface exists." Otherwise, the interface programme risks making a constrained model feel empirically stronger than it is.

## 4.5 Machine Question vs Open Question of AI Consciousness

Machine Question says current AI almost certainly lacks consciousness and ends with a strong "almost certainly nothing is home" conclusion. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/machine-question/)

Open Question says four genuine possibilities prevent the question from being settled even within the Map's framework, and explicitly notes that the biological interaction problem is also unsolved. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/open-question-ai-consciousness/)

These pages can coexist, but their titles and summaries should be harmonised. A better headline would be: "Current AI probably lacks **bidirectionally coupled consciousness** under the Map's tenets, but epiphenomenal, borrowed, alien, engineered, or non-temporal possibilities remain open."

## 4.6 Voids Safety Protocol vs Voids-as-Evidence

The Voids Safety Protocol says voids content is speculative by default, must be clearly labelled, and should not propagate into position-mode content without explicit evaluation. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/project/voids-safety-protocol/)

A Taxonomy of Voids argues that the voids cluster around the consciousness-physical boundary and that this pattern is what the full tenet package predicts, though it qualifies the claim as contested. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/taxonomy-of-voids/)

The risk is controlled but not eliminated. Any non-void article that uses voids as evidence should include a short "void-derived inference" label explaining whether it is apophatic, speculative, tenet-coherent, or externally supported.

# 5. Where the Map is strongest

## 5.1 The BP1/BP2 distinction

The Binding Problem article's distinction between computational binding and phenomenal binding is one of the Map's most robust argumentative tools. It lets the Map grant neuroscience progress without conceding that coordination explains unity. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/the-binding-problem/)

This move is well-supported because it does not require quantum mechanics, libertarian free will, or MWI rejection. It rests on a clean explanatory distinction: third-person integration is not automatically first-person unity.

## 5.2 The post-decoherence selection move

Post-decoherence selection is strategically strong because it accepts the main decoherence objection rather than denying it. It shifts the issue from "can neural superpositions survive for hundreds of milliseconds?" to "does decoherence solve outcome selection?" [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/post-decoherence-selection-programme/)

The article is also unusually honest about its limitation: no experiment has demonstrated the full quantum-bias-to-behaviour chain end-to-end. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/post-decoherence-selection-programme/)

## 5.3 The selection-only corridor

Selection-Only Mind-Influence is a major quality-control article because it limits what the Map may claim. It says large psi-style effects would falsify the strict reading rather than support it, and that the corridor is equally consistent with the no-effect null hypothesis. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/selection-only-mind-influence/)

This is exactly the kind of discipline the Map needs more broadly.

## 5.4 The falsification roadmap

The Falsification Roadmap is one of the site's most important methodological pages. It states that the tenets are not equally falsifiable, that dualism is cumulative rather than decisively testable, that MQI is more empirically tractable, and that Occam's Razor Has Limits is meta-scientific rather than experimental. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/falsification-roadmap-for-the-interface-model/)

This page should be promoted as a governing framework for the whole site.

## 5.5 The coherence-inflation self-diagnosis

The Map knows its own main failure mode: internal consistency can become overcommitment when one AI-heavy system generates and reviews its own worldview. The countermeasure page names circular citation, softened objections, squeezed-out uncertainty, and selection bias. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/project/coherence-inflation-countermeasures/)

This is a serious strength. The problem is execution consistency, not lack of awareness.

# 6. Where the Map overclaims

## 6.1 "Physics is not complete at quantum indeterminacies—and that's where consciousness acts"

As a tenet-level statement, this is allowed as a commitment. But in article contexts it often reads as a conclusion. The more accurate formulation is: "The Map commits to the view that if consciousness acts physically, it acts at quantum indeterminacies." The current phrasing risks presenting a metaphysical posit as a settled physical fact. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)

## 6.2 "Biological quantum effects prove evolution can harness coherence"

The tenets page says biological quantum effects prove evolution can harness coherence and then analogises to consciousness. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)

The better claim is narrower: biological quantum effects show that warm biological environments do not rule out all quantum-functional mechanisms. They do not show that neural consciousness uses such mechanisms, and the site's own Machine Question article acknowledges that no relevant neural coherence has been demonstrated. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/machine-question/)

## 6.3 "The philosophical case establishes agent causation"

The Free Will article says the philosophical case establishes agent causation, while the Agency Void and Decision Void show why the causal point cannot be verified from inside. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/free-will/) [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/voids/agency-void/)

This should be softened to "supports," "motivates," or "is the Map's best interpretation of."

## 6.4 "Placebo demonstrates consciousness directing physical processes"

The placebo/choking article is more careful later, but the opening language overstates what placebo data can show. Placebo demonstrates belief/expectation-dependent physiological modulation; the extra step to non-physical consciousness directing physical processes is a Map-specific interpretation. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/empirical-phenomena-mental-causation/)

## 6.5 "MWI dissolves the Decision Void"

The Decision Void says an MWI reading dissolves the void because every option is taken. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/voids/decision-void/)

That overstates the case. Under branch-relative MWI, each branch-subject may still experience decision closure, latency, and reconstruction. MWI may dissolve **exclusive metaphysical resolution**, but it does not obviously dissolve the phenomenological Decision Void.
