## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The article keeps the phenomenal claim ("a rat feels pain") cleanly separated from the selection-mechanism claim ("a rat selects outcomes *because of* anticipated valence"), and insists the explanatory gap blocks third-person verification of the latter at the lower rungs. The "Two limits are in-principle, not instrumental" passage takes the hard problem seriously as an epistemic boundary, not a temporary gap — exactly the Chalmersian reading.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
The *Hydra* rung is the standout for this persona: it observes that a diffuse nerve net offers "no evident locus" for option-comparison and concludes the framework "lacks the resources to *place*" the interface there, rather than pretending the interface mechanism is settled. Engaging seriously with where the attention-as-observation-rate / synaptic-exocytosis-bias mechanisms would have to live (and admitting they have not been specified for distributed systems) is the honest physics-interface posture.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The inner-boundary framing — "not where experience begins, but where *evaluative* selection begins" — is a genuinely original first-person distinction. It centres felt value as the thing that must do selective work, and refuses to let neutral functional pointers stand in for it.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The article is fully compatible with purpose-in-nature intuitions: it grants that conscious selection is teleological and that the tenets remove the principled barrier to widespread minimal consciousness. Crucially, it draws this compatibility without crude substance-dualism and without letting "experience-friendly" metaphysics carry weight it cannot bear.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
Value-sensitive selection *is* agency at the margin — the article treats the question of where evaluative selection begins as the question of where genuine (non-reflexive) steering begins, preserving the reality of choice without inflating it.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
**This persona's verdict is load-bearing here, and it is strongly positive.** The article does the praise-worthy thing of *not* upgrading: rat = live hypothesis (substrates present, discriminating evidence absent), nematode = speculative (anticipation gap), *Hydra* = speculative integration (no comparison locus), *Physarum* = value-blind reading explicitly favoured. It names the move precisely — "Tenet-coherence removes a defeater against marginal-organism consciousness; it supplies no positive evidence for it" — and the whole "Why the Tenets Cannot Locate the Boundary" section is built around refusing the possibility/probability slippage. It also correctly anchors to the 2024 New York Declaration's *precautionary* register without converting precaution into evidence, and cites Birch's own framework appropriately. The *Physarum* disjunction is handled with the correct conservative horn (valence-*like* behaviour underdetermines value-*sensitive selection*), which cuts against over-attribution rather than enabling it.

**Persona convergence**: Process Philosopher and Hardline Empiricist praise the *same* passages (the tenet-defeater section, the graduated ladder).
