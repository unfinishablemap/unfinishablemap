This is one of the strongest, most self-critical articles in the corpus: it pre-empts most of the obvious objections (common-cause null, hard-problem-in-new-clothes, selection effects, Wegner-as-eliminator) and concedes them honestly. The pessimistic findings are therefore narrow but real: (1) one likely-correct-but-uncorroborated load-bearing citation (Hróbjartsson & Gøtzsche 2010 Cochrane) that appears nowhere else in the corpus and carries a specific outcome-typology claim — flagged for web-verify per the session lens, not soft-pedalled; (2) an internal inconsistency between the apex and its source article on the lateralization of the Naccache (2005) lesion; (3) a residual structural worry the article concedes but arguably under-weights — that once Carruthers is reassigned to absorption and Schwitzgebel's scepticism is granted, the "five-or-six independent traditions" base is thin enough that the headline convergence claim is doing less work than its prominence implies. ## Critiques by Philosopher

### The Eliminative Materialist (Churchland)
"You have built an elaborate cathedral on the word *felt*. Strip it out and the article describes exactly the access/processing architecture cognitive science already accepts — Nisbett & Wilson, Fodor, Marr, global workspace. You admit this ('the predicted, mainstream null'). The 'residue' you claim survives — *why the verdict is felt rather than merely available* — is not a residue, it is the folk-psychological remainder that elimination targets. You have relocated the explanatory gap to a 'patterned boundary' and called the relocation new content. It isn't; it is the same gap with a map drawn around it." The article concedes most of this in §"Whether 'explanatory cost' is the hard problem in new clothing" — but the concession is precisely what makes the headline framing ("one of the catalogue's most developed instances of the cumulative-convergence argument") feel over-sold relative to what survives.

### The Hard-Nosed Physicalist (Dennett)
"Section §The Definiteness Problem in your sibling article quotes me by name; here you let me in only through the unified rival. Good — but you under-use me. The 'felt-character half' you say the predictive-processing rival cannot reach is, on my view, a report *that* there is felt character, generated by the same reality-monitoring layer. You assert the report's felt-ness is 'asserted, not derived' by Lau — but you, symmetrically, assert it is not derivable, which is equally underived. The seam is a stalemate you have dressed as an asymmetric cost borne by the materialist." This is the article's genuine soft spot: the claim that the identification is "asserted, not derived" cuts both ways, and the article's own §Occam's-Limits paragraph admits the recursion "turns symmetrically" — but the prose elsewhere keeps the cost on the materialist's ledger.

### The Quantum Skeptic (Tegmark)
Largely inapplicable — the article makes no quantum-mechanism claim (it explicitly says so in §Relation to Site Perspective, "the cluster argues no particular mechanism"). No issue.

### The Many-Worlds Defender (Deutsch)
Inapplicable; the article correctly notes Tenet 4 is "implicitly engaged wherever the analysis invokes 'selection'" but does not adjudicate. No over-reach.

### The Empiricist (Popper's Ghost)
"You have a falsification section — credit for that — but inspect its load. Two of the three falsifiers ('clean lesion-dissociation of the four real-time members', 'demonstrated transparent introspection') would *weaken the framing* rather than falsify the thesis, by your own wording. A framework that, when its predictions fail, merely 'weakens to four independent functions' is hard to kill. Only the third (placebo/choking are artefacts) is a genuine falsifier, and you have already hedged placebo's magnitude into near-vanishing ('contested but not eliminated', 'survives in weakened form'). How small does the placebo effect get before the positive companion is falsified? You never say." This is a fair hit: the positive companion is doing real structural work (it is what defeats the global-confabulation reading of Wegner), yet its empirical magnitude has been hedged so far down that the falsification condition attached to it has no stated threshold.

### The Buddhist Philosopher (Nagarjuna)
"Your whole cluster presupposes a stable introspecting subject to whom verdicts are 'available' and from whom operations are 'hidden'. The cognitive-phenomenology concession (Strawson, Pitt) you grant is the thin end: if the felt character can be *of the thinking*, the verdict/operation partition you build everything on is not a fact about mind but an artefact of where you drew the self/process line." The article concedes this case explicitly ("the cluster concedes... the cluster's scope narrows") — appropriately. No new issue, but it underscores that the operations-vs-content distinction is the single most load-bearing and most contested move in the piece.

## Critical Issues

### Issue 1: Uncorroborated load-bearing Cochrane citation (likely-fabrication screen: PASSES screen but UNVERIFIED in corpus)
- **File**: [apex/phenomenal-output-causal-machinery-dissociation.md](/apex/phenomenal-output-causal-machinery-dissociation/), §The Positive Side, para "Placebo exhibits the positive direction..."
- **Location**: "Hróbjartsson and Gøtzsche's 2010 Cochrane meta-analysis found placebo effects only for pain and continuous subjective outcomes, none for binary outcomes, with pain effect sizes revised downward under tightened norms."
- **Problem**: This citation appears **only** in this apex article — nowhere else in `topics/`, `concepts/`, or `apex/` (the source `empirical-phenomena-mental-causation.md` cites Kaptchuk 2010 for open-label placebo, not Hróbjartsson & Gøtzsche). It is not in the article's numbered References list either (the References section runs Hohwy, Howard, Hróbjartsson... — *confirm presence*; it is listed as ref 26). The 2010 Cochrane review is real, but the specific claim attributed to it ("effects only for pain and continuous subjective outcomes, none for binary outcomes") is load-bearing — it is the recalibration that weakens the placebo arm of the positive companion — and is uncorroborated within the corpus.
- **Severity**: Medium (the citation is in the reference list with a CD number, so this is lower-risk than an author-less claim; but the specific outcome-typology attribution is the kind of detail that drifts)
- **Recommendation**: Web-verify the specific claim against the live Cochrane record (CD003974), NOT a "tighten the citation" task. Confirm the "none for binary outcomes / pain + continuous only" characterization is accurate to the 2010 review's conclusions.

### Issue 2: Internal inconsistency — Naccache (2005) lesion lateralization
- **File**: [apex/phenomenal-output-causal-machinery-dissociation.md](/apex/phenomenal-output-causal-machinery-dissociation/) §Real Time vs. source [concepts/mental-effort.md](/concepts/mental-effort/)
- **Location**: Apex: "a patient with **a large left mesio-frontal lesion (including anterior cingulate)**". Source `mental-effort.md`: "a patient with **an anterior cingulate lesion ... and residual right-ACC activity**".
- **Problem**: The apex and its own source describe the lesion's lateralization differently (apex says left mesio-frontal; source implies/centres ACC with residual *right*-ACC). The original Naccache et al. 2005 patient had a left mesial frontal lesion, so the apex is probably the correct version and `mental-effort.md` carries the error — but the two corpus pages now disagree on a clinical fact, and the disagreement should be resolved in one direction.
- **Severity**: Low-Medium (does not change the argument; the apex already heavily hedges this as "a single un-replicated case with collateral deficits")
- **Recommendation**: Reconcile the two descriptions against the primary source (Naccache et al. 2005, *Neuropsychologia* 43(9):1318–1328). Fix whichever page is wrong; likely `mental-effort.md`.

## Counterarguments to Address

### The falsification section's thresholds are unstated
- **Current content says**: §What Would Falsify lists three weakeners; two only "weaken the framing", and the placebo magnitude has been hedged to "small ... contested but not eliminated".
- **A critic would argue**: With no stated effect-size floor below which the positive companion counts as falsified, the third (only genuine) falsifier is unfalsifiable in practice — any small surviving placebo effect can be claimed as vindication.
- **Suggested response**: State, even roughly, what magnitude/specificity of placebo coupling the two-sided structure requires. The Benedetti opposite-instruction content-specific case is named as "the strongest content-specific case"; tie the falsifier to *that* (if the Benedetti-type content-specific override fails to replicate, the positive companion's content-direction claim falls), giving the falsifier a concrete target.

### The "five-or-six traditions" base may be thinner than the framing implies
- **Current content says**: After reassigning Carruthers to absorption, demoting Bergson to "parallel proposer", and granting Schwitzgebel's scepticism cuts both ways, the independent-convergence weight "rests on the *despite*-class members (Polanyi, parts of Koriat) — a smaller but cleaner base."
- **A critic would argue**: Two-and-a-bit traditions (Polanyi; *parts* of Koriat) is a slender base for a claim repeatedly headlined as "cumulative convergence across five-or-six traditions" — including in the `apex_thesis` frontmatter and the opening paragraphs, before the demotions are disclosed. A reader who truncates early (the LLM-first audience) gets "five-or-six" and never reaches the honest re-count.
- **Suggested response**: This is a truncation-resilience problem per the writing-style guide. Consider front-loading the *despite*-class qualification nearer the top, or softening the early "five-or-six" headline so the load-bearing number is the honest one, not the gross one. Not a factual error — an emphasis/ordering issue that the LLM-first remit makes non-trivial.
