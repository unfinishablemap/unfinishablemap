## Region chosen and why

The time-consciousness cluster is a genuinely distinct region from everything flagged as touched this session (aesthetics, free-will/agency, mental-causation/causal-closure, sub-personal-competence, indexical-identity, quantum/physical-completeness). It is a large, tightly cross-linked family of high-traffic articles carrying substantial argumentative load for four of the five tenets, and it is the corpus's primary home for the "consciousness constitutes time" thesis — the Map's most ambitious metaphysical claim. That ambition is exactly what invites adversarial pressure. The cluster is also mature (several articles deep-reviewed within the last month), so surviving weaknesses are structural rather than cosmetic.

## Executive summary

The cluster is well-written and, in places (notably [non-temporal-consciousness](/topics/non-temporal-consciousness/)), unusually disciplined about its own evidential limits. But its two most load-bearing arguments — the *constitution-not-causation* inference for the phenomenological arrow, and the claim that *consciousness* is needed to break time-symmetry and "grow the block" — each rest on a step the article's own text undercuts. There is also a live **cross-article contradiction** on whether temporal structure is necessary for consciousness, which drives opposite verdicts on machine consciousness. Several quantitative and empirical claims are stated with more confidence than the sources support.

## Critiques by philosopher

### The Eliminative Materialist (Churchland)
The whole cluster reifies "durée," "the specious present," "protention," and "temporal thickness" as if naming them established their reality as irreducible features. These are folk-phenomenological categories; the neuroscience the articles cite (Pöppel's integration windows, gamma binding, accumulator models, predictive processing) is precisely the vocabulary that will *replace* them. The articles repeatedly stage the move "mechanism explains the *when* and the *what* but not the *what-it's-like*" (structure-and-agency §Missing Link; §How Attention Reshapes) — but that residue is asserted, never shown to be a real explanandum rather than an artefact of introspective vocabulary awaiting elimination.

### The Hard-Nosed Physicalist (Dennett)
The illusionist rebuttal in [temporal-consciousness](/concepts/temporal-consciousness/) ("The Illusionist Challenge") begs the question. "For there to *seem* to be temporal flow, there must be a seeming with temporal structure" assumes the vehicle of the seeming must instantiate the temporal structure it represents — exactly what the multiple-drafts model denies. A judgment issued *at* a time *about* succession need not itself be a temporally extended experience. Tallis's regress is deployed as if decisive, but it does not engage the representationalist's actual reply. This is close to a boundary-substitution: the passage reads as an in-framework refutation of Dennett while the real work is done by the Map's prior commitment that "appearance is itself phenomenal."

### The Quantum Skeptic (Tegmark)
[temporal-consciousness](/concepts/temporal-consciousness/) concedes the timescale gap frankly ("roughly twelve orders of magnitude faster than the specious present") and then claims it is "partly mitigated by revised coherence-time estimates (Hagan et al., 2002)." Even taking Hagan's most generous microtubule coherence estimates at face value, a multi-order-of-magnitude gap to the 100–750 ms specious present remains, and those estimates are themselves the contested subject of Tegmark's rebuttal. The Zeno mechanism the article needs (§Minimal Quantum Interaction: "consciousness holds patterns in durée by rapid repeated selection") requires coherence *at the specious-present scale*, which no cited estimate supplies. "Partly mitigated" papers over the fact that the mechanism's central quantitative demand is unmet.

### The Many-Worlds Defender (Deutsch)
The repeated tenet-closing claim that protention "feels open because outcomes are genuinely undetermined" and that this "resists" the branching account (structure-and-agency §Time May Need Consciousness; §No Many Worlds) is asserted, not argued. A decoherence theorist grants the *felt* asymmetry of retention vs protention and explains it by the informational asymmetry between records of a settled branch-past and the absence of records of the future — no collapse required. The article says the "qualitative texture" is what branching "leaves unexplained," but that is precisely the disputed point, restated as a premise.

### The Empiricist (Popper's ghost)
[temporal-consciousness](/concepts/temporal-consciousness/) admits the quantum framework "is presently empirically inert—its value is philosophical," yet elsewhere the same article recruits neuroscientific specifics (the ~280 ms Thura–Cisek commitment point "falls within specious present range"; the specious present as a "selection window") as if they corroborated the selection thesis. You cannot bank empirical inertness for defence and empirical specificity for support. The structure-and-agency "What Would Challenge This View?" section is the cluster's best falsificationist moment — but every listed defeater is one the article also treats as *conceptually* precluded (e.g. "temporal experience proved eliminable" is elsewhere argued to be incoherent), which drains the list of real risk.

### The Buddhist Philosopher (Nāgārjuna)
[non-temporal-consciousness](/topics/non-temporal-consciousness/) deserves credit: it stages the Madhyamaka objection honestly and concedes "the Map cannot refute the *catuṣkoṭi* from outside its own framework." But the concession is fatal to the cluster's downstream use, not merely a footnote — see Critical Issue 3.

## Critical issues

### Issue 1: The "constitution not causation" inference relies on a false general criterion
- **File**: [consciousness-and-the-ontology-of-temporal-becoming](/topics/consciousness-and-the-ontology-of-temporal-becoming/)
- **Location**: §Consciousness as Constitutive of the Phenomenological Arrow (second "consideration")
- **Problem**: The argument for reading consciousness as *constituting* (not merely causing) the phenomenological arrow runs: "removing consciousness... leaves *no* phenomenological arrow at all... This is a mark of constitution rather than causation: a cause can be absent while the effect is merely diminished, but a constitutive element's absence eliminates the constituted phenomenon." The stated criterion is false. Necessary *causes* also have the property that their absence eliminates the effect entirely — remove the sun and sunlight vanishes completely, yet the sun causes rather than constitutes sunlight; cut the power and the computation stops entirely without the power supply *constituting* the computation. "Absence eliminates the phenomenon" is the signature of a *necessary condition*, which causes routinely are. So the criterion does not distinguish constitution from causation at all, and the second (explicitly load-bearing) consideration collapses. The first and third considerations may survive independently, but the article leans on this one as the decisive mark ("This is a mark of constitution rather than causation").
- **Severity**: High
- **Recommendation**: `refine-draft` — either replace the criterion with a genuine constitution/causation diagnostic (synchronic non-causal dependence, no temporal gap, no possible causal intermediary) or downgrade the claim to "consciousness is a *necessary condition* for the phenomenological arrow," which is all the argument actually earns.

### Issue 2: Consciousness is not needed to break the symmetry the growing block requires — by the article's own admission
- **File**: [consciousness-and-the-ontology-of-temporal-becoming](/topics/consciousness-and-the-ontology-of-temporal-becoming/) (and [philosophy-of-time](/concepts/philosophy-of-time/), [collapse-and-time](/concepts/collapse-and-time/))
- **Location**: §The Growing Block's Fit with Dualist Interactionism ("Something must break this symmetry. Consciousness-involving collapse introduces genuine irreversibility...")
- **Problem**: The growing-block argument turns on the premise that *something* must break time-symmetry to distinguish the settled past from the open leading edge, and the cluster consistently nominates *consciousness*-involving collapse for the job. But the same section concedes: "Objective collapse theories (GRW, Penrose) hold that collapse occurs through physical mechanisms alone." Objective collapse already breaks the symmetry and already introduces genuine irreversibility *without consciousness*. So the leading edge can be constituted by physical collapse alone; the argument gives consciousness no distinctive work in "growing the block." The article needs the growing block to *require* consciousness for the reflective-equilibrium story (Issue below) to have force, but its own admission shows objective collapse is sufficient for growth. The role of consciousness is thereby demoted from "what breaks the symmetry" to "an optional add-on the Map prefers on independent grounds" — which is a much weaker claim than the text conveys.
- **Severity**: High
- **Recommendation**: `refine-draft` — explicitly distinguish "collapse (of some kind) breaks the symmetry" (true, and GRW-sufficient) from "consciousness-involving collapse breaks it" (the Map's contested add-on), and relocate the argumentative weight onto whatever *independently* favours the conscious variant over GRW/Penrose, rather than onto the symmetry-breaking need that objective collapse already meets.

### Issue 3: Cross-article contradiction — is temporal structure necessary for consciousness or not?
- **Files**: [temporal-consciousness-structure-and-agency](/topics/temporal-consciousness-structure-and-agency/) vs [non-temporal-consciousness](/topics/non-temporal-consciousness/)
- **Location**: structure-and-agency §Implications for Artificial Consciousness ("systems lacking temporal structure cannot be conscious"; LLMs "lack the specious present... durée") vs non-temporal-consciousness §The Argument for Irreducibility, final paragraph ("if consciousness can shed temporal structure while remaining alert, AI systems' lack of retention-protention dynamics is not the barrier it first appears").
- **Problem**: The cluster wants temporal structure to be *constitutive* of consciousness (grounding the categorical exclusion of LLMs — "if temporal binding is essential to consciousness as such, LLMs are categorically excluded regardless of scale," [temporal-consciousness](/concepts/temporal-consciousness/)) **and** *sheddable while consciousness persists* (the meditative-timelessness datum that anchors [non-temporal-consciousness](/topics/non-temporal-consciousness/)). These cannot both be true in the form stated. If alert consciousness can exist stripped of retention-protention (the non-temporal thesis), then lacking retention-protention is *not* sufficient for excluding a system from consciousness — which directly removes the structure-and-agency article's stated ground for excluding LLMs. non-temporal-consciousness half-notices this ("dualism-specific quantum obstacles remain"), but that relocates the exclusion to the *quantum* barrier, silently conceding that the *temporal* barrier — the one the sibling article makes central — does no independent work. The cluster's verdict on machine consciousness rests on a premise it elsewhere retracts.
- **Severity**: High
- **Recommendation**: `refine-draft` on both — reconcile by making the exclusion criterion uniform. Either (a) the barrier is quantum/biological, and temporal structure is characteristic-but-not-constitutive (weakening structure-and-agency's LLM claim), or (b) temporal structure is constitutive and meditative "timelessness" is content-timelessness within a still-temporally-structured vehicle (the representationalist reading non-temporal-consciousness raises but declines) — in which case the non-temporal thesis loses its anti-reductive punch. The cluster currently takes whichever horn suits the local conclusion.

### Issue 4: Self-inconsistent double-counting in the convergence argument
- **File**: [non-temporal-consciousness](/topics/non-temporal-consciousness/)
- **Location**: §The Argument for Irreducibility, final full paragraph ("A philosophical argument (the regress), a phenomenological tradition (Husserl), and first-person reports from contemplative practice (Frischhut)...")
- **Problem**: The article is admirably strict about double-counting *within* the contemplative cluster — it explicitly warns the cross-tradition reports "carry the evidential weight of *one* recurring pattern... not five independent confirmations." But it then commits a structurally identical double-count one level up: two of its three "convergent" lines are the *same source*. "The regress" just *is* Husserl's regress argument (introduced earlier: "The argument is one of regress"), and "a phenomenological tradition (Husserl)" is Husserl again. Counting Husserl's regress and Husserl's phenomenology as two independent strands inflates a two-strand convergence (Husserl + Frischhut's reports, with the cognitive-phenomenology line a third but itself Map-internal) into a three-strand one. The very discipline the article enforces on the contemplative reports should apply here.
- **Severity**: Medium
- **Recommendation**: `refine-draft` — recount the genuinely independent lines (arguably: Husserlian analysis; contemplative reports; cognitive-phenomenology — and note the first two are the same tradition's theory-and-data pair, not two arguments), and apply the article's own "weight of one, not many" caveat to the top-level convergence.

## Counterarguments to address

### The NREM-sleep argument for dualism overclaims (and a sibling hedges it)
- **Current content says**: [temporal-consciousness](/concepts/temporal-consciousness/) §Sleep — "hours pass with no temporal experience whatsoever... If consciousness were identical to neural computation, some temporal experience should persist. Its absence supports the Dualism tenet."
- **A critic would argue**: The physicalist predicts exactly this. The neural computations that *constitute* temporal experience (thalamocortical integration, recurrent cortical processing) are precisely those suppressed in dreamless NREM, while the computations that persist (memory consolidation, synaptic homeostasis) are not the consciousness-supporting ones. Absence of experience when the *relevant* computation is offline is neutral between identity theory and dualism.
- **Suggested response**: [temporal-consciousness-structure-and-agency](/topics/temporal-consciousness-structure-and-agency/) already states the calibrated version: "Whether reduced cortical complexity *explains* this disappearance or merely *correlates* with it remains the key question." temporal-consciousness.md should inherit that hedge rather than assert the datum "supports the Dualism tenet." This is a cross-article calibration inconsistency — flag temporal-consciousness.md for alignment.

### The temporal-bias vindication is a non-sequitur
- **Current content says**: [consciousness-and-the-ontology-of-temporal-becoming](/topics/consciousness-and-the-ontology-of-temporal-becoming/) §Durée — the near/future value bias "Greene and Sullivan (2015) argue is irrational on eternalist grounds, but that, if consciousness constitutes the phenomenological arrow, tracks a genuine metaphysical distinction."
- **A critic would argue**: Constituting the arrow does not make the *value*-timing preference rational. Whether it is rational to prefer good experiences future-ward is a question about how the metaphysical past/future distinction bears on *value*, and reality of passage does not settle it (a real arrow is compatible with the bias still being an arbitrary parochialism). The article slides from "the distinction is real" to "the preference tracking it is vindicated."
- **Suggested response**: Weaken to "the bias tracks a real metaphysical distinction rather than a mere perspectival one" without implying it is thereby *rational*; or supply the missing bridge premise linking passage-realism to the rationality of the value-asymmetry.

## Strengths (brief, to preserve during revision)

- [non-temporal-consciousness](/topics/non-temporal-consciousness/) is a model of evidential discipline: it stages constructivism (Katz/Sharf), Madhyamaka/*anātman*, and the representationalist alternative *against its own thesis*, and openly marks its load-bearing claim as the weaker (negative) one. The double-counting slip (Issue 4) is the only place its own discipline lapses.
- The [consciousness-and-the-ontology-of-temporal-becoming](/topics/consciousness-and-the-ontology-of-temporal-becoming/) §Mutual Support section pre-empts the circularity charge honestly rather than hiding it; the reflective-equilibrium framing is legitimate (its problem is Issue 2's specific independence claim, not the strategy).
- [bergson-and-duration](/topics/bergson-and-duration/) handles the *élan vital* exactly right — flags it as the most contested element, distances the Map from the vital-force mechanism while keeping the live philosophical residue. A template for how the cluster should treat its other speculative load-bearers.
- The "What Would Challenge This View?" section in structure-and-agency is a genuine falsificationist gesture worth keeping (once its defeaters are made real risks rather than conceptually-precluded ones).
