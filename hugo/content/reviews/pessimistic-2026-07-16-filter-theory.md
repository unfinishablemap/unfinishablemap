# Pessimistic Review

**Date**: 2026-07-16
**Content reviewed**: `obsidian/concepts/filter-theory.md` (last deep review 2026-06-20; ~24 days converged)

## Executive Summary

Filter Theory is a mature, unusually well-hedged concept page. Its central honesty move — the closing "cluster carries the evidential weight of one pattern, not seven" caveat — passes the Altered-State Symmetry audit cleanly, and the illusionist section engages Frankish on functional terms rather than by tenet fiat. The remaining weaknesses are calibration-level rather than structural: one section over-labels the bandwidth data as "a stronger discriminator" when a production model (GWT) predicts the same access bottleneck; the strong enhancement-asymmetry claim in "The Core Claim" is in tension with the article's own later concession that production accommodates coherent enhancement; and "quantum sensitivity" is asserted as a load-bearing interface criterion without defense against the standard decoherence objection.

## Critiques by Philosopher

### The Eliminative Materialist (Churchland)
The whole page is folk-psychological framing dressed as physics. "Interface," "reducing valve," "bandwidth" — these are metaphors standing in for a mechanism nobody can specify. The engram concession (Josselyn & Tonegawa 2020) is the tell: when the neuroscience matures, the "filter" story gets *harder* to tell, not easier, and the article admits it. Where the page is strong, it is strong only because it retreats to "empirically equivalent to production." Empirical equivalence to the theory that will actually be vindicated is not a virtue.

### The Hard-Nosed Physicalist (Dennett)
The illusionist section is the honest core of the dispute, and the article's rebuttal ("compounds the explanatory burden") is a real argument, not a dodge — credit where due. But it overstates. On a functional-seeming account, DMN disruption *reconfigures* the introspective model; there is nothing mysterious about a reconfigured self-model reporting "more vivid," because vividness-reports are exactly the kind of quasi-phenomenal state Frankish predicts introspection will generate. The article treats "systematically enhanced misrepresentation" as a special burden, but a global reduction in self-modeling constraint predicts precisely richer (less bounded) self-report. The burden is symmetric.

### The Quantum Skeptic (Tegmark)
"Quantum sensitivity" is listed as the first of five criteria that make brains suitable interfaces (Objections section; Simple Organisms section) and it is doing real work — it is the property that distinguishes a brain from *Physarum*. Yet the page offers no defense of it against warm-brain decoherence, deferring entirely to `brain-interface-boundary`. On a concept page that leans on the criterion twice, a bare assertion is not enough. The math I would demand (decoherence timescales vs. neural integration timescales) is exactly the calculation the framework never shows.

### The Many-Worlds Defender (Deutsch)
Less exposed here — MWI is not directly engaged. But note the MQI objection-response concedes consciousness-selection is "empirically indistinguishable from random collapse." If your interaction is indistinguishable from the noise, the parsimonious reading is that it *is* the noise, and no branch-selecting agent is needed.

### The Empiricist (Popper's ghost)
The article twice concedes its central claim is empirically equivalent to production and that the MQI mechanism is "indistinguishable from random collapse." By its own admission the core thesis is unfalsifiable. It then offers bandwidth (~10 bits/s) as a discriminator — but a global-workspace production model *predicts* a conscious-access bottleneck. A "discriminator" both theories predict discriminates nothing. This is the sharpest live issue on the page (see Issue 1).

### The Buddhist Philosopher (Nagarjuna)
The Buddhism section already flags the crucial disanalogy (*anātman* vs. independent consciousness) honestly. My only push: the "reducing valve" picture still reifies a consciousness-substance upstream of the filter. The cessation evidence you cite cuts against you as easily as for you — if selfhood itself can "drop away," the thing doing the filtering was never a unified independent subject to begin with.

## Critical Issues

### Issue 1: Bandwidth over-labeled as "a stronger discriminator"
- **File**: [concepts/filter-theory.md](/concepts/filter-theory/)
- **Location**: "How could we ever distinguish filter from production?" — "A stronger discriminator emerges from bandwidth constraints..." (line 151)
- **Problem**: The passage frames the ~10 bits/s conscious-throughput ceiling (Zheng & Meister 2025; Sauerbrei & Pruszynski 2025) as a *stronger* discriminator between filter and production, then walks it back to "not decisive." But a production model — specifically Global Workspace Theory, which the article cites elsewhere — predicts exactly a bottleneck localized at conscious *access* rather than at the substrate. The datum is fully consistent with production; it does not discriminate. Calling it "a stronger discriminator" (even hedged) mis-weights it.
- **Severity**: Medium
- **Recommendation**: Downgrade to "a suggestive re-description" and add one clause noting that GWT-style production models predict the same access-localized bottleneck, so the pattern is shared, not discriminating. Keep the "different research questions" point, which is fair.

### Issue 2: Enhancement-asymmetry claim in "The Core Claim" is in tension with the closing concession
- **File**: [concepts/filter-theory.md](/concepts/filter-theory/)
- **Location**: "The Core Claim" — "production models predict only degradation or alteration" (line 62) vs. "What Filter Theory Does and Doesn't Claim" — "different receptor systems and disrupted circuits straightforwardly predict different—even coherent—phenomenal outcomes" (line 185)
- **Problem**: Early in the article the enhancement-from-disruption asymmetry is presented as something "production models predict only degradation or alteration" cannot easily accommodate. The closing section flatly concedes the opposite: sophisticated production *straightforwardly* predicts coherent, even enhanced, phenomenal outcomes via redistributed processing. The two passages are not contradictory once "sophisticated" vs "naive" is read in, but as written the Core Claim overstates the asymmetry that the article itself later dissolves.
- **Severity**: Medium
- **Recommendation**: In "The Core Claim," qualify to "*naive* unified-switch production models predict only degradation" and forward-reference the closing caveat, so the strong asymmetry is scoped to the naive target from the outset rather than being retracted 130 lines later.

### Issue 3: "Quantum sensitivity" asserted as a load-bearing interface criterion without on-page defense
- **File**: [concepts/filter-theory.md](/concepts/filter-theory/)
- **Location**: Simple Organisms section (line 118); "Why brains specifically?" (line 143)
- **Problem**: Quantum sensitivity is the criterion that does the discriminating work between a brain and a neuron-free problem-solver like *Physarum*, and it is one of five criteria repeated in the objection-response. Yet it is asserted, not argued, and the standard decoherence objection is never acknowledged on the page. The reader is sent to `brain-interface-boundary`, but a criterion invoked twice as load-bearing warrants at least a one-clause acknowledgment that its physical basis is contested.
- **Severity**: Medium
- **Recommendation**: Add a single calibrating clause where the criterion is first invoked, flagging that quantum sensitivity in warm neural tissue is contested (decoherence) and pointing to `brain-interface-boundary` for the defense, rather than presenting it as settled.

### Issue 4: Covert-consciousness prevalence figure may overstate the source
- **File**: [concepts/filter-theory.md](/concepts/filter-theory/)
- **Location**: Covert Consciousness — "Up to 25% of patients classified as behaviorally unresponsive show clear signs of consciousness" (line 110), cited to Claassen et al. 2019 and Owen et al. 2006
- **Problem**: Claassen et al. 2019 reported cognitive-motor dissociation in ~15% of unresponsive patients; the widely-cited Kondziella meta-analytic figure is also ~15%. "Up to 25%" is higher than either cited source appears to support. This is a factual-currency concern, not a framing one.
- **Severity**: Low (verify at source)
- **Recommendation**: Reconcile the figure with Claassen 2019 (report ~15%, or cite the specific source for the 25% ceiling if one exists). Verify at the publisher before editing.

## Counterarguments to Address

### The bandwidth bottleneck
- **Current content says**: The ~10 bits/s ceiling localized at conscious access is a "stronger discriminator" that "filter theory predicts."
- **A critic would argue**: Global Workspace Theory — a production model — predicts an access-stage bottleneck as its defining feature. Both frameworks predict the datum.
- **Suggested response**: Present it as a shared prediction that reframes the research question, not as evidence favoring filter over production.

### Enhancement from disruption
- **Current content says (early)**: Production predicts "only degradation or alteration."
- **A critic would argue**: Different circuits produce different (including richer) experience is the null production hypothesis; the article concedes exactly this at the end.
- **Suggested response**: Scope the asymmetry to *naive unified-switch* production from the first mention.
