### Critical Issues Found

1. **Conflated / wrong-author citation: reference 12 (RPT-vs-GNWT).** The article carried:
   `Pitts, M. A., Metzler, S., & Hillyard, S. A. (2019). Recurrent processing theory versus global neuronal workspace theory. Neuroscience of Consciousness, 2019(1), niz005.`
   This entry conflated **three distinct sources**:
   - The **title** "Recurrent processing theory versus global neuronal workspace theory" belongs to a **comment by Montemayor & Haladjian**, not to Pitts.
   - The **authors** "Pitts, Metzler, Hillyard" — Michael A. Pitts's real relevant paper is *The relationship between attention and consciousness: an expanded taxonomy...* (Pitts, **Lutsyshyna**, Hillyard, **2018**, Phil Trans R Soc B 373(1755), 20170348). "Metzler" is a fabricated middle author (no such co-author; unrelated to the Shepard & Metzler mental-rotation "Metzler" elsewhere in the corpus).
   - The **venue/id** "Neuroscience of Consciousness 2019(1) niz005" is a wholly unrelated article (Windt, dream-imagery commentary).
   Publisher-of-record verification (PMC6460080 → Oxford/Royal Society) shows the PMC link the source research note actually consulted resolves to **Montemayor, C., & Haladjian, H. H. (2019). "Recurrent processing theory versus global neuronal workspace theory: a comment on ... by Pitts et al." Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society B, 374(1770), 20180517.** This is the paper whose title the article already used, and it is a legitimate reference for the RPT-vs-GNWT framing the article discusses. Real-paper / wrong-metadata defect → **fixed, not deleted**: corrected authors (Montemayor & Haladjian), venue (Phil Trans R Soc B), volume/issue (374(1770)), article number (20180517), and expanded the title to its true "a comment on Pitts et al." form.
