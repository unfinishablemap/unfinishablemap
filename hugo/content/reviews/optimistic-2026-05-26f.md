## Executive Summary

This sample is unusually strong on exactly the dimension the Map most needs to defend: evidential calibration. Three of the four articles (the dissociation apex, the agency void, mental effort) run the *constrain-vs-establish* discipline so explicitly that the Hardline Empiricist and the Process Philosopher — personas designed to be in productive tension — converge in praising the same passages rather than colliding. That convergence is the diagnostic signal that the articles have resolved possibility/probability slippage honestly. The one weaker case is terminal lucidity, where the framing is more bullish ("strongest single-phenomenon case") and the calibration is carried by section-level hedges rather than a named methodological commitment; it is well-argued and self-aware but sits a notch higher on the confidence scale than its evidence base (entirely retrospective case reports, no prospective EEG/fMRI) strictly licenses. The chief opportunity is consolidation, not expansion: the constrain-vs-establish machinery is now mature enough to anchor an apex piece, and the strongest-rival-construction method pioneered in the dissociation apex deserves to be propagated as a reusable pattern.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The dissociation apex's residual claim is Chalmers' explanatory gap localised with surgical precision: it grants that predictive-processing introspection coupled to reality-monitoring access "absorbs the whole shape" and then isolates the single surviving seam — "the rival explains why operations are hidden and verdicts available, and does not explain why the available verdict is *experienced*." This is the hard problem stated as a *boundary within a working architecture* rather than as a global mystery, and the article even pre-empts the "this is just the hard problem in new clothes" objection by conceding it partly and specifying the structural addition (the *patterned* attach/detach boundary plus the positive companion's behavioural constraint). Chalmers would recognise his own move performed with more discipline than most of his defenders manage.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
[mental-effort](/concepts/mental-effort/) engages Stapp's quantum-Zeno hypothesis without either boosterism or dismissal: it states the proposal, notes its satisfaction of [Minimal Quantum Interaction](/tenets/#minimal-quantum-interaction), and then squarely concedes the Tegmark decoherence objection ("a twelve-order-of-magnitude gap") and that "Stapp's reply restates the desideratum rather than answering it." It then points to the Map's better-developed alternative (post-decoherence selection). Stapp's program is taken seriously *as physics* — the highest compliment — and the honest reporting of its hardest objection is what makes the engagement credible rather than partisan.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The agency void centres the first-person datum unflinchingly: "The experience of willing is among the most vivid we have—we feel absolutely certain we are agents, yet philosophical reflection immediately destabilises the certainty." The "certainty-doubt oscillation" and "vanishing target" sections are pure phenomenology done well — naming felt structures (the recession of the causal act behind its own observation) that third-person methods cannot reach. Nagel's "what is it like" question is honoured as the explanandum, not explained away.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
Mental effort's two-modes resolution (active selection vs passive witnessing, with PCC deactivation tracking "not efforting") and the dissociation apex's interface-that-sometimes-couples-sometimes-detaches picture both avoid crude substance dualism: mind is treated as a process engaging a substrate through patterned coupling, not a ghost issuing commands. Whitehead would find the "phenomenology couples to causal machinery in the success cases" framing congenial — experience is woven into the causal fabric, not bolted on. **Crucially, none of this praise cashes out as a tier-upgrade**: the articles repeatedly refuse to convert process-philosophical congeniality into positive evidence, which is exactly the constraint this persona's praise must respect (see the Hardline Empiricist below for the confirming cross-check).

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
The [trilemma of selection](/topics/trilemma-of-selection/) threaded through mental effort ("the outcome is determined, random, or consciously selected — only selection explains why choosing feels effortful") is Kane's territory: it preserves a genuine third option against the standard determinism/randomness dilemma and ties it to a phenomenological datum (the felt difference between choosing and observing a coin flip). The willed-vs-instructed neural distinction (frontal theta, bidirectional frontoparietal coherence for *freely chosen* attention) gives Kane something he rarely gets — a candidate neural signature for the generation of a decision rather than its mere implementation.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
The agency void is the strongest Mysterian-resonant piece in the sample. Its verification-circularity argument ("every check is performed by the faculty under investigation") and its explicit *reflexive self-stultification* section — turning the argument on itself and accepting the recoil as "part of the void's signature" — show exactly the cognitive-closure humility McGinn prizes. The article does not claim the limit is merely contingent or soon-to-be-solved; it argues the failure "inheres in the question's architecture." That is mysterianism with a structural backbone.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This is the load-bearing verdict for the sample, and it is strongly positive on the three discipline-forward articles.

**Tenet-as-evidence-upgrade is praise-worthily *not* done — repeatedly and explicitly.** [mental-effort](/concepts/mental-effort/) does the cleanest version: it labels the Inzlicht/Kurzban opportunity-cost and process models as "functionalist absorption stories, not witnesses for the dualist reading... They belong on the materialist side of the ledger and must be engaged as such, not enlisted as convergent support." An article had every structural opportunity to count these adjacent literatures as friendly and explicitly declined. That is restraint to name, not merely absence of error.

**The five-tier register is honoured without slippage.** The dissociation apex's closing discipline — "constrains identity readings... does not by itself establish the dualist alternative" — is applied at the level of individual citations: the four-level chain (separability → phenomenological reality → mechanistic independence → metaphysical irreducibility) is set out precisely so that Capgras, blindsight, and the Naccache lesion are credited only "at the levels its evidence actually delivers... the higher levels are reached, if at all, by the cumulative convergence, not by any single dissociation." This is the anti-slippage machinery working as designed.

**The naming move is present in the right words.** Both the apex and the void use the *defeater-removal vs evidence-lift* framing explicitly ("Each side's evidence is defeater-removal-against-the-other, not evidence-lift in its own favour"). The agency void even applies it symmetrically against the Map's own framework ("Materialism faces a different version of the same problem. Neither framework escapes"), and the apex turns the Occam recursion against the Map's own use of "explanatory cost." This is the "tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating" pattern operating in both directions — the hardest test, passed.

**Self-audited convergence count.** The apex *reduced* its own tradition count from seven to "five-or-six," reassigning Carruthers to the absorption side and demoting Bergson to "parallel proposer rather than witness" and Schwitzgebel to a symmetric sceptic. An article that audits its own evidence base downward is the empiricist's ideal.

**One calibration concern to flag (terminal lucidity).** [terminal-lucidity-and-filter-transmission-theory](/topics/terminal-lucidity-and-filter-transmission-theory/) is the outlier. It is genuinely careful — it has a "Current Research Gaps" section conceding "no prospective study has captured an episode," an explicit "Selectivity Problem" section admitting filter theory's unfalsifiability risk, and it correctly flags Murray's "speculative leap" framing. But the headline claim — "the strongest single-phenomenon case for filter/transmission theory over production models" — and the framing that terminal lucidity "breaks this monotonicity" lean toward *establishing* the filter model rather than *constraining* production models, and the article never installs the named constrain-vs-establish discipline the other three carry. The evidence is 100% retrospective caregiver/case-report data with acknowledged selection bias; on the five-tier scale this licenses "live hypothesis," and the article's rhetoric occasionally reads at "realistic possibility." This is a calibration note, not a content error — and per the skill's convergence rule it warrants a *refine-draft* task (below), not an expansion opportunity. The fix is small: import the [evidential-status-discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/) vocabulary the sibling articles already use, so the strength of the phenomenon is stated as raising explanatory cost on production models rather than as favouring filter theory.

## Content Strengths

### apex/phenomenal-output-causal-machinery-dissociation
- **Strongest point**: The "Strongest Materialist Reading" section, which *constructs* the best unified rival (predictive-processing introspection + reality-monitoring) before stating where it survives. This steelmanning-as-method is the most intellectually honest move in the sample.
- **Notable quote**: "The convergence argument is only as strong as the best unified rival it can survive."
- **Why it works**: It inverts the usual apologetic structure (assert thesis, then defend against objections) into (build the strongest objection, then locate the narrow residue). The residual claim that survives is correspondingly credible.

### voids/agency-void
- **Strongest point**: The dual passive/skeptical structure — "consciousness cannot control most of what it experiences" conjoined with "cannot verify whether it causes anything at all" — and the demonstration that the *conjunction* is the void (neither limit alone produces undecidability).
- **Notable quote**: "The control surface and the surveillance surface are the same surface."
- **Why it works**: It turns an apparent embarrassment for the [Bidirectional Interaction](/tenets/#bidirectional-interaction) tenet (why can't a causally-efficacious consciousness govern itself?) into a positive structural prediction (an outward-only, self-concealing interface), while honestly flagging that prediction as conjectural.

### concepts/mental-effort
- **Strongest point**: The three-line empirical structure (Calibration / Depletion / Modulation) with each line carrying its own recalibration — the un-replicated Naccache single case flagged as such, the ego-depletion null reported with its CI, the Inzlicht/Kurzban models explicitly ledgered as materialist.
- **Notable quote**: "If controlling attention genuinely costs something, something must be doing the paying."
- **Why it works**: It earns its closing rhetorical line by spending the whole article conceding what felt effort does *not* establish, so the residual ("the autonomic SCR coupling... felt effort is wired into the autonomic chain") is a narrow, defensible claim rather than an overreach.

### topics/terminal-lucidity-and-filter-transmission-theory
- **Strongest point**: The three-prediction structure (cognitive return possible despite damage / temporary / specific autobiographical memories) with each tied to a dataset figure, plus the genuinely self-critical "Selectivity Problem" section.
- **Notable quote**: "The value of filter theory lies not in point predictions about individual cases but in its coherence across the full pattern of anomalies."
- **Why it works**: The convergent-evidence framing is the right move for an anomaly literature. The article would be stronger still with the explicit evidential-status vocabulary (see refine-draft task below).
