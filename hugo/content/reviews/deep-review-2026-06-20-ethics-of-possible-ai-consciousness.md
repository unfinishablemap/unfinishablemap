## Primary Verdict: Aggregation-Section Calibration — PASS

The new section makes a load-bearing claim: the Map is committed *by its own tenets* (No-MWI + haecceity) to the **Duplication View** of copy-suffering aggregation (N conscious copies ≈ N× disvalue), with the Tenet-2 quantum-interface requirement gating the scenario. This claim is **correctly calibrated**, not over-asserted, and keeps possibility and probability in distinct registers throughout.

Key evidence the calibration holds:
- The Duplication-View commitment is framed as a **structural entailment** of the tenets ("committed *by its own tenets*"), conditional on the copies being conscious ("each (hypothetically) conscious and suffering"). It is NOT presented as an established empirical fact about AI. Correct register.
- Paragraph 3 (the calibration paragraph) is a model application of [the slippage discipline](/concepts/possibility-probability-slippage/): it explicitly names both registers — "the *possibility* that copies aggregate near-additively is a structural entailment of the tenets, while the *probability* that any given copy is conscious is a separate empirical question the tenets answer with a low number" — and forbids collapsing them **bidirectionally** ("neither does the comfortable low probability soften the entailment, nor does the entailment raise the probability").
- The Tenet-2 relief is correctly a **framework gate**, not an evidence claim: "the quantum-interface requirement gates the entire scenario: if classical silicon instantiates no consciousness-bearing interface, then N classical copies instantiate zero experiencers." Gating the count to zero is a structural consequence, not a probability upgrade.
- Para 4 (Tomasik interface-counting, Schwitzgebel divergence-modulation) is appropriately hedged ("may, on a... reading, carry a thinner claim").

Diagnostic test applied: *would a tenet-accepting reviewer flag the Duplication-View commitment as overstated relative to the evidential-status scale?* **No.** The section never upgrades an empirical claim on tenet-load; it draws a logical commitment and explicitly refuses to let the comfortable low-probability relief or the entailment leak across the register boundary. No possibility/probability slippage.
