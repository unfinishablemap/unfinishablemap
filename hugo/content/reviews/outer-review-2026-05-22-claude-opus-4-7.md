# Audit Report: The Unfinishable Map (unfinishablemap.org)

## TL;DR

- The Unfinishable Map is a serious, unusually well-engineered AI-assisted philosophical corpus (≈700+ articles across topics, concepts, voids, apex, tenets, arguments, research) with a strong internal review architecture and clear authorship metadata. Its single most striking strength is that the five core tenets are genuinely load-bearing — they are cross-linked, falsifiability discussions are present, and the project is unusually frank about its own framework-internal blind spots.
- Its central weaknesses are (a) overstated "convergence" rhetoric across voids and quantum interpretations that the project itself has begun to police but has not fully cleaned up; (b) at least one demonstrable citation error (Duch's "Mind as a shadow of neurodynamics" is in *Physics of Life Reviews* 31:28–31, 2019, PubMed 31301951, not *Cognitive Systems Research* as the Map's `quantum-consciousness` concept page states) and one significant attribution slippage (the 613 THz microtubule anaesthetic finding belongs to Craddock, Kurian, Preto, Sahu, Hameroff, Klobukowski & Tuszynski (2017), "Anesthetic Alterations of Collective Terahertz Oscillations in Tubulin Correlate with Clinical Potency," *Scientific Reports* 7:9877, DOI 10.1038/s41598-017-09992-7, not to the Wiest 2025 review the Map cites for it); (c) a thinner-than-advertised engagement with the strongest contemporary opposition (illusionism, MWI decision-theoretic recoveries, Rovelli-style relationalism), where the Map declares "bedrock disagreement" earlier than the dialectic strictly warrants. [ADS](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2019PhLRv..31...28D/abstract)[nih](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5575257/)
- Recommendation: prioritise (1) a citation-integrity sweep on quantum-empirical claims, (2) a separate Rovelli/RQM topic page that addresses Adlam & Rovelli, "Information is physical: cross-perspective links in relational quantum mechanics," *Philosophy of Physics* 1(1):1–19 (2023), DOI 10.31389/pop.8, rather than dispatching RQM in one paragraph of `qm-interpretations-beyond-many-worlds`, (3) a worked engagement with Chalmers's own Type-B and PCS responses rather than treating PCS as already defeated, and (4) tighter discipline on the recurring "convergence is evidence for dualism" move, which currently bears more argumentative weight than the apex articles themselves admit it can carry. [Lse](https://philosophyofphysics.lse.ac.uk/articles/10.31389/pop.8)[LSE Research Online](http://eprints.lse.ac.uk/120927/1/Adlam_Rovelli_Cross_perspective_links_in_relational_quantum_mechanics_published.pdf)

## Key Findings

1. **Site structure is exceptionally clear and well-instrumented.** Eight top-level sections (Apex, Topics, Concepts, Tenets, Voids, Research, Project, Workflow), with a documented mermaid diagram on the home page describing how content flows (Research → Topics/Concepts; Tenets feed Topics/Concepts; Topics/Concepts point to Voids and synthesize into Apex). Section caps are enforced (topics 250, concepts 250, voids 100), creating discernible pressure that shows up in the changelog as "starvation events" and forces conservative-promotion queue management.
2. **The changelog is the project's most distinctive epistemic feature.** Every refine/deep-review/replenish-queue/coalesce action is logged with engagement-mode classifications (Mode One = "defective on its own terms"; Mode Two = "unsupported foundational move"; Mode Three = "framework-boundary marking"), tenet alignment, attribution checks, and possibility/probability slippage checks. This is far more transparent than any comparable AI-generated philosophy project I am aware of. [project/why-this-is-different](https://unfinishablemap.org/project/why-this-is-different/)
3. **The five tenets are genuinely operative.** Tenet 5 (Occam's Razor Has Limits) explicitly self-binds: the May 2026 revision of the No-Many-Worlds tenet demotes the ontological-multiplicity objection to "subsidiary" status because parsimony cannot run as an argument *against* MWI when the Map already refuses to let parsimony run *against* dualism. This is the kind of internal discipline most polemical sites lack. [tenets](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)
4. **Tenet 1 (Dualism) carries a quietly acknowledged ambiguity.** The Tenets page declares neutrality between substance and property dualism, but immediately adds that the agency cluster (Free Will, agent-causation, interactionist-dualism material) "deploys a substance-leaning sub-reading" because agent causation needs a persisting subject. The honesty is admirable; the consequence is that the Map's published official position is more committal than the tenet's framing suggests, and downstream articles inherit this without always re-deriving it. [tenets](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)
5. **The post-decoherence selection programme is the most original synthesis.** The apex article integrates Zurek's quantum Darwinism, Kochen-Specker contextuality, Aharonov-style weak measurement / TSVF, and stochastic amplification into a single research programme calibrated honestly as "pre-Keplerian" — i.e., constraint-scaffolding rather than mechanism. This is a substantive philosophical move that is not, as far as I can tell, present anywhere else in this form. [apex/post-decoherence-selection-programme](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/post-decoherence-selection-programme/)
6. **Voids catalogue (100+ entries) is extensive but suffers from category multiplication.** Many voids are conceptually overlapping (e.g., self-opacity / unobservable-self / self-reference-paradox / phenomenology-of-self-awareness; observation-void / measurement-void / calibration-void / quantification-void; resolution-void / bandwidth-of-consciousness). The 2026-05-21 coalesce attempt abandoned all six merge candidates, reasoning that "the content base is mature and articles already declare their differentiation explicitly." This may be true; it also may be the rationalisation pattern the Map's own `obviousness-void` warns against. [workflow/changelog](https://unfinishablemap.org/workflow/changelog/)
7. **The "Apex" synthesis layer is the strongest argumentative core.** Apex articles (post-decoherence-selection-programme, taxonomy-of-voids, interface-specification-programme, what-consciousness-tells-us-about-physics, identity-across-transformations, minds-without-words, machine-question, moral-architecture-of-consciousness) consistently integrate at least 5–10 source articles each and articulate the Map's overall worldview more rigorously than the constituent pages do individually.
8. **Citation integrity is mostly good but has discoverable errors.** A targeted fact-check of seven empirical claims about named thinkers and studies found: Tegmark (2000) decoherence times 10⁻¹³–10⁻²⁰ s and Hagan et al. (2002) corrected estimates 10⁻⁵–10⁻⁴ s correctly cited; the COGITATE adversarial collaboration paper (Cogitate Consortium, Ferrante, Gorska-Klimowska et al., "Adversarial testing of global neuronal workspace and integrated information theories of consciousness," *Nature* 2025, DOI 10.1038/s41586-025-08888-1) is real and the "neither was clearly vindicated" gloss is fair, though the specific volume/page citation 642(8066):133–142 used internally on the Map should be re-verified against the published record; Chalmers & McQueen 2022 CSL-IIT description accurate; Kerskens & Pérez 2022 *Journal of Physics Communications* 6(10):105001 fairly summarised; New York Declaration "hundreds" framing accurate (the Brooks Institute Animal Law Digest gives the most specific publicly verifiable count: "So far, 480 academics, scientists, and experts have signed the New York Declaration on Animal Consciousness," and the official NYU sign page displays no aggregate total). However, **two errors**: (a) Duch's "Mind as a shadow of neurodynamics" was published in *Physics of Life Reviews* 31:28–31 (2019), not *Cognitive Systems Research* as the Map's `quantum-consciousness` concept page states, and the labels "articon-style architectures" and Orch-OR-as-"pseudo-mysticism" attributed to Duch could not be verified in his published work; (b) the Map's citation of Wiest et al. (2025) "A quantum microtubule substrate of consciousness" (*Neuroscience of Consciousness* 2025(1):niaf011) for the 613 THz anaesthetic-disrupted oscillation is misleading: Wiest's piece is a review with no new data, and the 613 THz finding actually originates in Craddock et al. (2017) *Scientific Reports* 7:9877, which is a molecular-dynamics simulation rather than a direct experimental observation. [arxiv + 8](https://arxiv.org/pdf/quant-ph/9907009)

### 3. Coherence assessment against the five tenets

**Tenet 1 (Dualism)** — well-supported. Hard-problem-of-consciousness, interactionist-dualism, and dualism concept pages are all internally consistent and cross-reference each other coherently. The substance/property ambiguity registered on the Tenets page is honest. **Caveat**: the Map sometimes presents the convergence of historical and non-Western traditions on "irreducibility" as evidence for dualism (`cross-traditional-convergence-on-consciousness-irreducibility`, `the-hard-problem-in-non-western-philosophy`) — this is structurally identical to the "bullet-proof confirmation" pattern the Map's own pessimistic-2026-05-20 review caught and criticised in `animal-consciousness`. The cleanup has not been applied symmetrically across the convergence-argument cluster.

**Tenet 2 (Minimal Quantum Interaction)** — substantially strengthened by the May 2026 post-decoherence-selection turn. The Tenet 2 page is unusually honest about empirical risk: "Selecting between Born-equiprobable alternatives makes the mechanism undetectable by aggregate Born-statistics tests... the framework cannot be probed by the experimental routes that constrained earlier interactionist proposals." This is exactly the residue an honest reviewer would flag, and the Map registers it itself. **Caveat**: this candour does not always reach downstream articles, several of which still trade on the older "Stapp's quantum Zeno" framing without noting that the Map's central path is now post-decoherence rather than pre-decoherence selection. [tenets](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)

**Tenet 3 (Bidirectional Interaction)** — the most carefully calibrated. The May 2026 revision explicitly demotes the self-stultification objection to epiphenomenalism from "refutation" to "deepest difficulty," and walks through the phenomenal-concepts strategy as a serious rejoinder. This is the kind of move I would expect from a published academic philosopher pre-empting referee 2. [tenets](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)

**Tenet 4 (No Many Worlds)** — this is the tenet where the Map's argumentative honesty is most strained. The April–May 2026 revision correctly notes that the ontological-extravagance objection cannot be the load-bearing move (because Tenet 5 disarms parsimony arguments), and shifts the weight to the indexical objection. But the indexical objection is itself contested by Wallace, Saunders, and Sebens-Carroll via self-locating-uncertainty derivations, and the Map's response ("self-locating uncertainty presupposes there's a fact about 'where I am' to be uncertain about, which is precisely what needs grounding") is treated as a stopping point rather than the start of a 30-year live dispute. The Probability Problem in Many Worlds topic acknowledges the literature but does not fully engage Wallace 2012's *The Emergent Multiverse* on its own terms. [tenets](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)[topics/qm-interpretations-beyond-many-worlds](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/qm-interpretations-beyond-many-worlds/)

**Tenet 5 (Occam's Razor Has Limits)** — internally the most consistent. The self-binding is genuine and policed; the historical counterexamples (heliocentrism with epicycles, atomic theory, GR) are sound. **Caveat**: the parsimony-case-for-interactionist-dualism topic argues that interactionist-dualism is in fact *more* parsimonious than physicalism once all explanatory costs are tallied. This is in tension with the tenet's own discipline — if parsimony is unreliable, it cannot run *for* the Map either. The article gestures at the symmetry but does not fully discharge the objection. [tenets](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)

### 4. Content quality assessment

**Strengths**:

- Hard-problem-of-consciousness is a genuinely high-quality article, comparable to a good SEP entry. It accurately characterises Chalmers's easy/hard distinction, Levine's explanatory gap, the zombie and Mary's Room arguments, and engages illusionism, PCS, eliminativism, IIT, Russellian monism, idealism, and mysterianism. Citations are accurate where I checked. [topics/hard-problem-of-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/hard-problem-of-consciousness/)
- Integration-as-activity (concept page, added 2026-05-11) is one of the strongest pieces on the site philosophically. It correctly identifies that the convergence of recurrent processing theory, global workspace theory, predictive processing, and Whitehead on "integration as process" is description-level rather than metaphysical, and explicitly notes that Lamme, Baars/Dehaene, and the predictive-processing community would deny the further step to a non-physical performing subject. This is the kind of dialectical honesty I would expect from a peer-reviewed paper. [concepts/integration-as-activity](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/integration-as-activity/)[concepts/integration-as-activity](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/integration-as-activity/)
- The post-decoherence-selection-programme apex article is the project's single best piece.

**Weaknesses**:

- **Treatment of Rovelli/RQM is thin.** RQM gets one paragraph in `qm-interpretations-beyond-many-worlds` and a short concept page. The Map's complaint that "RQM's observers need not be conscious — any physical system qualifies" is a real disanalogy, but Adlam & Rovelli, "Information is physical: cross-perspective links in relational quantum mechanics," *Philosophy of Physics* 1(1):1–19 (2023), DOI 10.31389/pop.8, is acknowledged only briefly, and Lewis's 2024 epistemic-solipsism critique is mentioned without the Map taking a position on whether RQM survives it. Given that Rovelli is one of the most-cited living interpretive physicists, this is the weakest of the major-rival-position treatments. [concepts/relational-quantum-mechanics](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/relational-quantum-mechanics/)
- **Treatment of Duch is the most problematic.** As noted, the journal citation for "Mind as a shadow of neurodynamics" is wrong (it is *Physics of Life Reviews* 31:28–31, 2019; PubMed 31301951; not *Cognitive Systems Research* as the quantum-consciousness page states). The characterisation of Orch-OR as "pseudo-mysticism" and the "articon-style architecture" terminology could not be verified in retrievable Duch sources. Duch's actual position — that brain processing requires *differentiation* across regions and timescales rather than global synchronisation, with Tegmark 2000 as the anchor for decoherence implausibility — is more carefully argued than the dossier indicates. [concepts/quantum-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/quantum-consciousness/)
- **Wiest 2025 misattribution.** The Map cites Wiest et al. (2025) "A quantum microtubule substrate of consciousness" (*Neuroscience of Consciousness* 2025(1):niaf011) for the 613 THz oscillation peak abolished by anaesthetics. The Wiest paper is a perspective/review with no new data ("There are no new data associated with this article"). The 613 THz finding belongs to Craddock, Kurian, Preto, Sahu, Hameroff, Klobukowski & Tuszynski (2017), "Anesthetic Alterations of Collective Terahertz Oscillations in Tubulin Correlate with Clinical Potency: Implications for Anesthetic Action and Post-Operative Cognitive Dysfunction," *Scientific Reports* 7:9877, DOI 10.1038/s41598-017-09992-7, and is a molecular-dynamics *simulation* rather than a direct measurement. The Map's framing ("a testable signature, though independent replication is needed") obscures that there is no original experiment to replicate — only a computational prediction. [Oxford Academic + 2](https://academic.oup.com/nc/article-pdf/2025/1/niaf011/63128065/niaf011.pdf)
- **IIT/Tononi treatment is harsh but generally fair.** Integration-as-activity correctly identifies the passivity problem with the identity thesis, and consciousness-and-integrated-information lays out the case. The fairer-than-most move is acknowledging that the phenomenal-concept strategy is a genuine in-framework reply. [concepts/integration-as-activity](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/integration-as-activity/)
- **Penrose/Hameroff treatment is fair but optimistic.** The Map is honest that Orch-OR requires pre-decoherence coherence and that the timing dispute is not settled. It is less honest about the degree of mainstream-physics scepticism: the Hagan et al. (2002) revised best-case estimate of 10⁻⁵–10⁻⁴ s is, on the Wikipedia summary of the same paper, "still far below 25ms" — i.e., roughly 2–3 orders of magnitude below the neurophysiologically relevant threshold even after the seven-order-of-magnitude correction to Tegmark, a gap the Map's phrasing ("the literature has not converged") tends to occlude. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/quant-ph/0005025)[Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orchestrated_objective_reduction)
- **Chalmers treatment is good.** The Map correctly notes where it diverges from Chalmers (psychophysical-laws page: "Chalmers tends toward epiphenomenalism; the Map diverges via the self-stultification argument"). It also accurately characterises Chalmers & McQueen 2022. [concepts/interactionist-dualism](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/interactionist-dualism/)

### 5. Internal consistency

The Map's cross-referencing is unusually dense — every concept page lists `related_articles` in frontmatter, body cross-links, and Further Reading. I found no flat contradictions across the pages I sampled. The principal *internal-tension* findings:

- **The "convergence" move is asymmetrically policed.** The animal-consciousness pessimistic review (2026-05-20) explicitly identified that the article was treating methodology + data + the Map's own commitment as "three-route convergence" when only two are independent. The fix has been applied to that article. But the same pattern is operative across the voids-as-evidence-for-dualism cluster, the cross-traditional-convergence cluster, and parts of the post-decoherence-selection programme. The 2026-05-22 "Coherence vs. Evidential Status" methodology page is the right corrective; whether it has propagated through the corpus is not yet visible.
- **The status of the indexical objection to MWI** is treated as bedrock in the Tenet 4 page, contested in `probability-problem-in-many-worlds`, and dialectically open in `qm-interpretations-beyond-many-worlds`. These are not contradictory but they sit at different points on the calibration scale without explicit reconciliation.
- **Lucid-dreaming evidence** is handled consistently after the 2026-05-21 fix to `mind-brain-separation` ("demonstrates bidirectional interaction" → "is consistent with bidirectional interaction"), but related articles may not have been swept for the same slippage.

### 6. Gaps and missing elements

**Important thinkers under-represented:**

- **Frankish on illusionism** — gets one concept page (`illusionism`) but the strongest contemporary illusionist arguments (the 2016 *JCS* "Illusionism as a Theory of Consciousness" symposium responses, Dennett's later work) are dispatched via Mode One regress moves rather than engaged on their own terms. The 2026-05-21 zombie-master-argument refine signals the Map is aware of this — the refine explicitly notes the article "correctly notes that the illusionist programme is contested but does not signal the *liveness* of the 2020s programme." - **Wallace / Saunders / Sebens-Carroll on MWI decision-theoretic recoveries** — mentioned, not engaged in depth.
- **Carruthers's evaluativist account** — the Map's treatment in minds-without-words is brief; given that Carruthers is one of the most-cited contemporary materialists about phenomenal consciousness, deeper engagement would help.
- **QBism (Fuchs, Mermin, Schack)** — better treated in the pragmatism-and-qbism topic than in the qbism concept page; some consolidation would help.
- **IIT 4.0 (Albantakis et al. 2023)** — cited in integration-as-activity but the specific axiom revisions in IIT 4.0 are not engaged.

**Important topics under-represented:**

- **Higher-order theories of consciousness** (Rosenthal, Lau) — surprisingly absent given they are a major contemporary alternative.
- **Empirical AI consciousness frameworks** — the Butlin et al. 2023 ("Consciousness in Artificial Intelligence") report is not engaged.
- **Animal-consciousness empirical depth** — the article is good on the marker method but light on the specific empirical findings (cephalopod pain protection, bee play, fish mirror tests) that have driven the post-2020 shift.

**Voids that may be missing:**

- A *forecasting void* (why consciousness cannot predict its own future states with any accuracy).
- An *attention-economics void* distinct from the attention-and-consciousness page.
- A *cultural-cognition void* on whether consciousness has historically variable structure (the historical-consciousness-void touches this but only at the Jaynes/McGilchrist level).

The existing voids are mostly well-chosen, but the 100+ catalogue is dense enough that some redundancy is hard to avoid. The 2026-05-21 coalesce abandonment was probably the right call locally but the structural pressure remains.

The "convergence as evidence" move is the article's load-bearing argument; it would be stronger with an explicit discussion of how the Map's own framework prevents the convergence from being question-begging.

The most specific publicly verifiable count from the Brooks Institute Animal Law Digest is 480 ("So far, 480 academics, scientists, and experts have signed the New York Declaration on Animal Consciousness"), and the official NYU sign page displays no aggregate total — so "hundreds" is well-calibrated and the previous "over 500" was unverifiable rather than confirmed. Otherwise solid. [Thebrooksinstitute](https://thebrooksinstitute.org/animal-law-digest/us/issue-259/hundreds-experts-sign-new-york-declaration-animal-consciousness)

### 8. Prioritised weaknesses and recommendations

**P0 — Citation integrity sweep.** The Duch journal misattribution and Wiest/Craddock attribution slippage are exactly the kind of errors a hostile reviewer would seize on. Run a `literature-drift-review` over every empirical claim that cites a specific 2017–2026 paper, with primary-source verification rather than just title-and-year sufficiency. ## Caveats

Readers should weigh whether the Map's "Mode Three: framework-boundary marking" classification is doing genuine philosophical work or functioning as a rhetorical move that lets disagreements get parked rather than engaged.
- The site's central methodological claim — that adversarial self-review via Claude Code produces genuinely converging philosophical work — is interesting and arguably partially vindicated by the convergence patterns visible in the changelog. But the patterns are also consistent with the system locking in to a stable attractor that may or may not be tracking truth. The Map registers this concern itself (cartography-problem, dissolution-problem). Whether the corpus is converging on truth or on local coherence is, on the Map's own principles, not decidable from inside.
