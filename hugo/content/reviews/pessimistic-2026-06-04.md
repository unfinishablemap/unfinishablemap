## Critiques by Philosopher

### The Empiricist (Popper's ghost)
The strongest objection, and the grounded one. The article presents two "measurable axes" of difference (taxonomic depth, signature profile) as an *empirical* defence against the selection-effect worry. But the second axis is rigged by the choice of measuring instrument: an inventory of *introspective* failure modes (construction-mistaken-for-observation, dissolution-under-attention, silent-erasure, epistemic-vertigo) cannot register in domains with no introspector. The article concedes exactly this at line 92 ("they require an introspecting subject, and physics and mathematics voids have no subject in the loop") and yet at line 94 calls the resulting mismatch "the strongest empirical mark that the consciousness cluster is not merely a frontier of current ignorance." The mismatch is closer to a tautology: *the only domain with a self-examining subject is the only domain that exhibits self-examination failure signatures.* That is not evidence the consciousness cluster is special beyond the already-known fact that consciousness is where subject and object coincide — a fact every physicalist in the apex's own line 93 list already grants without dualism.

### The Eliminative Materialist (Churchland)
Would press the same point from the other side: the four "subject-requiring" signatures are precisely the ones an illusionist predicts as report-machinery artefacts. The article's own [common-cause-null](/project/common-cause-null/) paragraph (line 114) concedes this for the dualist-vs-illusionist question — but it does not connect that concession back to the second-difference argument, leaving the impression that the signature disjointness is a clean win when the article has already admitted it is not framework-discriminating.

### The Hard-Nosed Physicalist (Dennett)
Line 93 of the apex already lists the deflationary explanation the comparison is meant to defeat ("the coincidence of subject and object, third-person methods uncalibrated for first-person phenomena"). The cross-domain article's "first difference" (taxonomic depth — consciousness voids span four kinds, physics/math voids are near-pure unexplorables) is *also* downstream of subject/object coincidence: occlusion and natural-occlusion are categories that only have purchase where a knower's access to its own states can be hidden or evolved-away. So both "differences" reduce to the single antecedently-known fact, rather than being two independent discriminating axes. The article frames them as two; a critic sees one.

### The Quantum Skeptic / Many-Worlds / Buddhist
No new purchase on this article beyond the standard tenet-level disagreements; the article is careful to claim only framework-internal discrimination (line 114) and does not over-reach on quantum or MWI ground.

Their absence in physics/math is therefore guaranteed by the construction of the instrument, not discovered by measurement. - **Severity**: Medium
- **Recommendation**: A `refine-draft` pass that (a) explicitly acknowledges the inventory is built from consciousness voids and that the subject-requiring signatures cannot appear in subjectless domains by construction, so the signature axis demonstrates *that consciousness is the only domain with a self-examining subject* rather than independently confirming a distinctive cluster; (b) connects the line-114 common-cause-null concession back to the second-difference argument so the "strongest empirical mark" wording is downgraded to match; (c) clarifies whether the taxonomic-depth axis (first difference) is genuinely independent of subject/object coincidence or is the same fact measured twice.
