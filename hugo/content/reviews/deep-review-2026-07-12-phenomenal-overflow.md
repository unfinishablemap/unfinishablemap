### Critical Issues Found

- **LOCUS 1 — framing over-read of Nartker et al. 2025 (calibration/framing accuracy).** The article listed Nartker et al. (2025) under "phenomena suggest phenomenal overflow" and, in the Map's own voice, read the finding as "phenomenology may persist even when workspace broadcast fails," relegating the degraded-access reading to "deflationary theorists." Web-verify of the paper's own conclusion (eLife 100337) shows this is an over-read: the authors (Firestone is a well-known skeptic of rich unconscious perception) interpret the residual feature-sensitivity as *residual conscious vision* — "inattention degrades but does not eliminate awareness" — not as inaccessible phenomenology. Because subjects *can* report location/colour/shape when probed, the result is arguably about degraded **access**, not phenomenology **outside** access, which is precisely what Block's thesis requires. The result is genuinely double-edged: it undercuts the "awareness requires attention" premise central to GWT (friendly to overflow's anti-functionalist thrust) but does not cleanly evidence phenomenology beyond access. **Resolution**: re-framed the passage to (a) attribute the graded/"residual conscious vision" reading to the authors themselves, (b) state the double-edge explicitly, (c) note the authors' skepticism of rich unconscious perception and that the study "constrains the overflow debate more than settles it." No overstatement introduced; evidential-status discipline preserved. Length-neutral (in-place re-frame, ~+25 words).
