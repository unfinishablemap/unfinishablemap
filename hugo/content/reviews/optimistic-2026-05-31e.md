## Executive Summary

This is a mature, calibration-disciplined trio doing the Map's hardest abstract work: showing that the *standard* objections to dualist causation (causal closure, the explanatory poverty of psychophysical laws, the apparent completeness of physics) each rest on metaphysical assumptions that are contestable or provably false. The standout strength is that all three articles explicitly mark the difference between *removing a defeater* and *supplying evidence* — the self-reference article does so by name ("Removing a defeater is not the same as supplying positive evidence") and routes through [evidential-status-discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/). The Hardline Empiricist and the Process Philosopher converge in praise here rather than conflicting, which is the signature of honest calibration. The main opportunity is structural, not corrective: these three articles share a single load-bearing concept — *outcome-level openness / which-outcome-obtains* — yet none of them links to the other two.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The laws/dispositions article takes the explanatory gap seriously at the level of *metaphysics of properties*, not just phenomenology: the Mary point is deployed exactly where it bites (against pandispositionalism — "no catalogue of causal roles entails qualitative character"). The self-reference article correctly cites Chalmers (1995) *against* the Lucas-Penrose route while preserving the destination — a model of how to use a sympathetic philosopher's own criticism without flinching. Chalmers' upward psychophysical laws are named as the scaffolding the Map extends downward. This is the hard problem treated as a constraint on the metaphysics of laws, which is genuinely underrepresented elsewhere.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
The probability-interpretation article gives Stapp's quantum-Zeno proposal a fair hearing as a *live alternative* (Born-bending) to the Map's working corridor-dualism reading, rather than collapsing all interface views into one. The self-reference article's treatment of Tonetto's "statistical closure with outcome-level openness" is precisely the structural opening Stapp's interactionism needs — the formalism fixes ensemble statistics but not which outcome actualises. Engaging seriously with the physics-consciousness interface at the level of *what the formalism does and does not determine* is exactly the Stappian register.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The probability article's central move — that randomness "has no phenomenal character," that "we cannot feel 30% versus 70%" — is Nagel's "what is it like" question turned into a diagnostic. The claim that consciousness engages the world through meaning rather than probability, and that the Born rule is "a third-person description of a first-person process," centres subjective experience exactly where physicalist treatments would erase it. The phenomenological/cognitive probability-blindness distinction is a genuinely Nagelian refinement: it isolates the irreducibly first-person residue from the merely contingent cognitive bias.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The laws/dispositions article's powerful-qualities identity theory (Martin, Heil) — quality and power as two aspects of one nature — is the most Whitehead-friendly machinery on the Map: it avoids crude substance dualism by refusing to peel the felt quality apart from its causal directedness. "We know what it is like to be a powerful quality because we *are* powerful qualities" is process-philosophy-resonant in the best way. Crucially, this resonance is kept on the *property-nature* axis and is **not** used to upgrade any organism or system up the evidential-status scale — the article's claims are about the metaphysics of properties-in-general, not about which systems are conscious. The Whitehead-friendliness here removes a defeater (crude-dualism implausibility) without smuggling in a tier-upgrade, which is exactly the discipline the persona constraint requires.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
The laws/dispositions article's "dispositional modality problem" section grounds the selection role of qualitative character: at a quantum indeterminacy compatible with several manifestations, "the felt quality present at the site selects its own manifestation." This is the property-metaphysics underpinning for Kane's quantum-indeterminacy-makes-room-for-freedom thesis — the felt quality does genuine selection work without adding force, which is precisely the "no force, but real causal relevance" structure libertarian agency needs to escape both determinism and randomness.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
Both the probability and self-reference articles wear appropriate epistemic humility. The probability article explicitly invokes McGinn's cognitive closure for *phenomenological probability blindness* — "consciousness cannot grasp the probabilistic structure of its own contribution to quantum measurement because that structure exists at a level of description that consciousness cannot occupy while also being consciousness." The self-reference article's "What This Does and Does Not Show" section is a textbook humility-marking move: Lawvere unification ≠ consciousness resolves the limitation; undecidability ≠ consciousness fills the gap; Frauchiger-Renner ≠ dualism dissolves it. McGinn would approve of a framework that knows the shape of its own ignorance.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This is the cluster's strongest persona verdict, and it is **load-bearing**: all three articles are defeater-removal articles, the exact place where possibility/probability slippage is most tempting, and all three explicitly decline the slippage.

- The self-reference article names the discipline outright: "Removing a defeater is not the same as supplying positive evidence... The incompleteness results lower the barrier to a consciousness-based reading of measurement; they do not raise the evidential standing of that reading. The claim earns no upgrade on the strength of the defeater's removal alone — it remains a live hypothesis." This is the *"tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating"* pattern stated almost verbatim, routed through [evidential-status-discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/). **Tenet-as-evidence-upgrade is here praise-worthily *not* done** — the article had every structural opportunity to read "physics is formally incomplete" as evidence *for* consciousness-collapse and explicitly refused.
- The self-reference article also models honest framework-stage calibration: it concedes the model-reality distinction (undecidability is a feature of the *model*, not the system; finite systems remain decidable) and argues the case is strengthened *anyway* — earning the conclusion rather than overclaiming it.
- The laws/dispositions article keeps the one genuinely empirical commitment visible and isolated: the "decoherence hostage" (Tegmark 2000; Schlosshauer 2007), explicitly flagged as "the framework's one genuinely empirical commitment" distinct from the metaphysical eliminations. It even concedes that the powerful-qualities vs inert-categorical readings are "empirically indistinguishable" and that the argument "turns on internal coherence... not on empirical adequacy." That is precisely the register-separation discipline the persona praises.
- The probability article holds corridor dualism as "the Map's working hypothesis rather than a settled commitment" and keeps three interface readings live pending brain-internal Born-rule testing. No premature convergence.

No calibration concern surfaced in this cluster. The Process-Philosopher praise (powerful-qualities resonance) and the Hardline-Empiricist praise (defeater-removal ≠ evidence) attach to *different passages on different axes* and do not conflict — the articles resolved the tension honestly. No refine-draft task is warranted on calibration grounds.
