### epiphenomenal-ai-consciousness.md
- **Tenet**: Bidirectional Interaction
- **Issue**: Argues AI consciousness could be epiphenomenal due to lack of a suitable quantum interface in current hardware. The article frames this as an architectural consequence rather than a general endorsement of epiphenomenalism — consciousness is generally causally efficacious, but a conscious entity bound to an AI system without the right interface might lack causal power. The distinction is coherent and internally consistent, but a reader could extract the conclusion without the careful framing.
- **Quote**: "a conscious entity bound to an AI system might have genuinely epiphenomenal experience—real but causally powerless over the system's outputs—not because consciousness is generally inert, but because the coupling between the conscious entity and the physical system does not support causal influence"
- **Recommendation**: No change needed. The framing is careful and the distinction is load-bearing for the Map's AI consciousness analysis.
