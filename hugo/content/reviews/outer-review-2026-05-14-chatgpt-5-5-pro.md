## Audit verdict

**Revise, not retract.** The article is useful and unusually candid for a dualist-leaning treatment: it names REBUS, admits non-knockdown status, and links to nearby consciousness topics. But its central rhetorical move still leans too hard on an older **“less brain activity → richer mind”** motif. The page metadata says the article was created on March 8, 2026 and last modified May 6, 2026; the article itself opens by claiming that psilocybin, LSD, and DMT suppress the default mode network while producing expanded perception, ego dissolution, and “more real than real” experiences, then presents this as a challenge to production models. ## 1. Empirical claims about psychedelics and brain activity

### Main correction: “decreased activity” is too crude

The article says BOLD-fMRI studies “consistently show” classic psychedelics decreasing activity within the DMN, then uses that as the basis for the “brain doing less, mind experiencing more” paradox. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/psychedelics-and-the-filter-model/) That should be rewritten. The strongest current cross-drug evidence does **not** support a simple global “less activity” claim. A 2026 international mega-analysis of 11 resting-state fMRI datasets across psilocybin, LSD, mescaline, DMT, and ayahuasca found a core signature of **increased functional connectivity between transmodal networks and unimodal networks**, while within-network reductions were **weak-to-moderate, selective, and variable** across drugs and networks. [Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41591-026-04287-9)

The better current formulation is: **classic psychedelics acutely disrupt or desynchronize large-scale network organization, often involving the DMN, while increasing between-network coupling and/or neural entropy under some measures.** A 2024 Nature precision-mapping study of psilocybin found broad functional-connectivity disruption, reduced distinction among networks, strongest effects in the DMN, and a persistent decrease in anterior hippocampus–DMN connectivity for weeks; that supports DMN involvement, but not the article’s simplified “less brain activity” gloss. [Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-024-07624-5) A 2023 systematic review likewise supports acute disruption of within-DMN connectivity and increased between-network connectivity, while cautioning that the causal and therapeutic centrality of DMN changes remains unclear. [OUP Academic](https://academic.oup.com/ijnp/article/26/3/155/6770039)

### Current citations are historically relevant but now aged

Carhart-Harris 2012 and Tagliazucchi 2016 remain important historical citations, but they should no longer carry the empirical weight of the article’s neuroimaging section. The article should add the 2026 mega-analysis, the 2024 precision functional mapping study, a 2024/2025 review-level synthesis, and the May 2026 first-psilocybin-use EEG/MRI study. The May 2026 study is especially relevant because it found acute EEG entropy increases and correlations with later well-being/insight, but also reported weak or largely non-significant BOLD fMRI primary outcomes and treated the work as exploratory/hypothesis-generating. [Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-026-71962-3)

The article also underplays non-DMN circuitry. A 2025 fMRI scoping review describes a broader picture of de-differentiation with the DMN “at its core,” but also emphasizes thalamic, amygdala, medial temporal, and other subcortical involvement. [Frontiers](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/neuroscience/articles/10.3389/fnins.2025.1606798/full) A 2026 Nature Medicine review summarizes the current mechanistic picture as acute neural desynchronization plus subacute neuroplasticity, not as a simple decrease in neural activity. [Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41591-025-04194-5)

### The article overstates the epistemic force of insight and therapeutic effects

The page’s epistemology section argues that if psychedelic insights were “pure confabulation,” their therapeutic value would be mysterious, and it treats therapeutic outcomes, selective access, cross-cultural convergence, and creative output as evidence for “genuine epistemic value.” [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/psychedelics-and-the-filter-model/) That is too strong. Therapeutic usefulness does not establish metaphysical or veridical truth. It may show salience, emotional reconsolidation, memory recontextualization, increased cognitive flexibility, expectancy effects, or therapist-supported meaning-making.

The article should update Griffiths-era outcome citations with newer review evidence. A 2025 meta-analysis found that intensity of psychedelic experience correlates with clinical improvement, especially in clinical settings, but that supports a psychological/clinical mechanism rather than filter-model access to independent reality. [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0149763425000867) A 2025 systematic review found that psychedelic-catalyzed insight is common, dose-related, and often associated with therapeutic improvement, while also noting heterogeneity and possible publication/selective-reporting issues. [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0149763425001174)

It should also add a trial-method caveat. A 2026 JAMA Psychiatry systematic review found that blinding integrity is often poor in psychedelic RCTs, with substantial functional unblinding in psilocybin, LSD, and ayahuasca trials. [JAMA Network](https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jamapsychiatry/fullarticle/2847667) A 2025 active-placebo review similarly argues that weak blinding, expectancy, concomitant psychotherapy, curated settings, and the role of subjective experience limit simple causal attribution. [OUP Academic](https://academic.oup.com/ijnp/article/28/4/pyaf023/8106348)

### “Selective access” is the weakest empirical claim

The article’s proposed discriminator—access to forgotten autobiographical material, content the subject could not infer, or knowledge beyond prior epistemic reach—is exactly the right kind of test in principle. But the current text treats such reports too generously before evidence has been supplied. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/psychedelics-and-the-filter-model/) It should sharply distinguish:

1. **Phenomenological access**: the person feels they have accessed something hidden, deep, or prior.
2. **Psychological usefulness**: the content helps reframe life, emotion, or self-understanding.
3. **Veridical access**: the content is independently confirmed and could not plausibly arise from memory, inference, suggestion, or confabulation.

Only the third would meaningfully support the filter-model claim. The current article mostly has evidence for the first two.

### Quantum section is labeled speculative, but still too integrated

The article’s “Quantum Dimension” section cites Hameroff, Kerskens & Pérez López, and Babcock et al. while saying the quantum mechanism is speculative and not relied upon. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/psychedelics-and-the-filter-model/) That caveat is good, but the section still risks importing speculative support into the main argumentative flow. The site’s own May 11 changelog records a recalibration of quantum-biology claims away from “settled refutation” language and toward contested, possibility-level framing. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/workflow/changelog/) This article should mirror that discipline: biological quantum effects may be relevant to long-term mechanism exploration, but they do not presently strengthen the psychedelic filter-model argument.

## 2. Filter-model framing versus REBUS, entropic brain, and predictive processing

The article says evidence supporting REBUS also supports the filter interpretation, with the difference being metaphysical. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/psychedelics-and-the-filter-model/) That is partly fair, but too generous to filter theory. The same evidence is better classified as **empirically underdetermined**: compatible with filter theory, but also predicted or explained by naturalistic alternatives.

REBUS proposes that psychedelics relax the precision of high-level priors, increasing bottom-up information flow and enabling revision of overweighted beliefs. [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0031699724012961) That is not merely a neutral redescribing of filter theory; it is a mechanistic rival that explains ego dissolution, perceptual novelty, emotional release, and belief revision without invoking a non-produced mind. Follow-up REBAS work connects acute entropy/subjective effects with post-acute belief change and well-being, while emphasizing supportive context and replication limits. [Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-023-28111-3)

The entropic brain framework is also under-treated. It offers a way to interpret “richer,” “higher,” or more flexible conscious states in terms of neural entropy and altered dimensions of consciousness, while warning against collapsing all forms of consciousness into a single scale of “more” or “less.” [OUP Academic](https://academic.oup.com/nc/article/2023/1/niad001/7103464) The article should stop using “richer experience” as if it automatically means more consciousness, more reality-contact, or less filtering. It may mean increased entropy, weakened self-world boundaries, altered salience, or intensified affective meaning.

The missing counterarguments are substantial:

- **Increased richness need not imply external access.** Predictive-processing accounts can explain novelty and vividness through altered precision-weighting, not through a wider aperture onto mind-independent reality.
- **Noetic certainty is not veridicality.** A feeling of revelation can result from altered confidence, salience, and self-model disruption.
- **Therapeutic durability is not filter-specific.** REBUS/REBAS, neuroplasticity, memory reconsolidation, contextual support, and integration practices already predict durable change.
- **DMN disruption is not unique to filter-loosening.** The site’s own DMN article says production and filter readings can both account for psychedelic self-experience, and that reports of wider access are not direct measurements of wider access. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/default-mode-network/)
- **Pluralistic mechanisms are now mainstream.** Reviews discuss 5-HT2A receptor effects, plasticity, thalamo-cortical filtering, claustro-cortical mechanisms, predictive loosening, affective changes, belief change, social context, and lasting behavior change rather than a single decisive mechanism. [ScienceDirect+1](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0149763422002822)

The article’s current “possible divergences” section is promising, but it should be reframed as a list of **future discriminators**, not as evidence the filter model is already pulling ahead. The site’s May 13 changelog added “Mode Four: Empirical Underdetermination” to its direct-refutation discipline; this article is a strong candidate for that label. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/workflow/changelog/)

## 3. Tenet alignment and hard-problem discipline

This is the main argumentative weakness. The article’s “Relation to Site Perspective” section says the psychedelic neuroimaging pattern is a “significant pharmacological challenge” to production models, that filter theory predicts the observed result, and that simple materialist “less brain means less mind” is contradicted by the data. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/psychedelics-and-the-filter-model/) That reads as tenet-protective. It turns a real but underdetermined empirical pattern into support for the site’s dualist architecture before rival explanations have been fully adjudicated.

The site already has better calibration elsewhere. Its main Filter Theory article says both production and filter theories can explain basic neuroscience, that their difference can look metaphysical rather than empirical, and that filter theory lacks independent criteria for specifying which disruptions should enhance versus degrade consciousness. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/filter-theory/) The DMN article similarly says neither production nor filter interpretations are forced by the data alone. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/default-mode-network/) The psychedelics page should inherit that restraint.

The hard problem is bracketed too quickly. The page is right that psychedelic evidence can be relevant to the broader production-versus-transmission debate, but the hard problem is a general metaphysical challenge to reductive physicalism, not a local inference from DMN disruption. The article currently risks this slide:

> altered DMN activity → richer experience → production model strained → filter theory favored → dualism reinforced

A more defensible version would be:

> psychedelic neuroimaging shows that ordinary self-world modeling can be profoundly reconfigured; this is compatible with filter theory and useful for the site’s dualist research programme, but REBUS, entropic brain, predictive processing, and pluralistic neuropsychology explain much of the same terrain without resolving the hard problem.

That keeps the hard problem in view without using it as a shortcut around empirical underdetermination.

## Concrete potential improvements

### To `psychedelics-and-the-filter-model`

1. **Rewrite the opening thesis.** Replace “brain doing less produces mind experiencing more” with: “classic psychedelics acutely desynchronize and reconfigure large-scale brain networks, especially self-related and transmodal networks, while producing intensified and altered experience.”
2. **Replace “decreased DMN activity” with modality-specific language.** Separate BOLD signal, cerebral blood flow, functional connectivity, EEG/MEG power, entropy, metabolism, and receptor-level effects. Do not treat all as “activity.”
3. **Update the empirical evidence block.** Add the 2026 international mega-analysis, 2024 psilocybin precision-mapping paper, 2026 first-use psilocybin EEG/MRI paper, 2026 Nature Medicine review, and 2024/2025 review-level syntheses.
4. **Downgrade the REBUS paragraph.** Change “evidence supporting REBUS simultaneously supports filter interpretation” to: “REBUS evidence is compatible with filter theory but does not discriminate in its favour unless filter theory supplies independent, risky predictions.”
5. **Reclassify the article as Mode Four / empirical underdetermination.** The page should explicitly say that psychedelic neuroscience currently underdetermines production, predictive-processing, entropic, and filter interpretations.
6. **Revise the “possible divergences” section.** Keep selective access as a possible discriminator, but require prospective, independently verifiable cases and controls against suggestion, false memory, inference, and post-hoc reconstruction.
7. **Remove therapeutic durability as a filter-specific discriminator.** REBUS/REBAS, neuroplasticity, memory reconsolidation, psychotherapy, expectancy, and integration practices already explain durable change.
8. **Revise the epistemology section.** Replace “therapeutic value would be mysterious if confabulated” with: “therapeutic value can arise from psychologically useful restructuring even when metaphysical or autobiographical truth remains uncertain.”
9. **Add a blinding/expectancy caveat.** Any appeal to clinical outcomes should note functional unblinding, active-placebo limitations, therapist/context effects, and expectancy.
10. **Quarantine the quantum section.** Move it into a clearly marked speculative appendix or boxed note. Link it to the site’s quantum-biology and MQI caution pages, and state that it does not presently adjudicate psychedelic consciousness models.
11. **Rewrite the “Relation to Site Perspective” section.** Replace tenet-confirming language with: “psychedelic evidence is congenial to the filter model and relevant to the Map’s dualist programme, but it remains compatible with leading physicalist and predictive-processing accounts.”

3. **Introduce a citation-aging rule for neuroscience.** Any neuroimaging generalization based mainly on pre-2020 single-site studies should be paired with the latest review, meta-analysis, or mega-analysis where available.
4. **Add a psychedelic-clinical-method gate.** Claims using therapeutic outcomes should check blinding, expectancy, active placebo, psychotherapy, integration, sample size, and preregistration status.
5. **Add a noetic-veridicality gate.** Articles should never move from “felt revelatory” to “truth-tracking” without independent validation.

7. **Use the new empirical-underdetermination discipline here.** This article is a clean test case for the site’s May 13 Mode Four framework: it is not a failure of the argument, but it should be marked as underdetermined rather than tenet-confirming.
