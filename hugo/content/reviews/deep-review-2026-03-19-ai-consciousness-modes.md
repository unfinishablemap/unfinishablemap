## Pessimistic Analysis Summary

### Critical Issues Found
- **Misattributed citation (Lieberman et al. 2008)**: The article cited "Lieberman, M.D., Gaunt, R., Gilbert, D.T., & Trope, Y. (2008). This misattribution had already been corrected in `consciousness-and-intelligence.md` but not propagated here. **Resolution**: Fixed both in-text citation and reference entry.

### Counterarguments Considered
- **Eliminative Materialist**: Would reject the premise that "phenomenal accompaniment" is a coherent concept. Article handles this implicitly through the content-manner independence argument.
- **Dennett (functionalist)**: Would argue functional equivalence IS consciousness — the simulation/instantiation distinction is a vestige of Cartesian thinking. Article addresses this through the Tulving hierarchy showing same-content-different-phenomenology.
- **Quantum Skeptic**: Would challenge the MQI tenet connection as overly speculative. Article appropriately hedges with "The Map speculates."
- **Many-Worlds Defender**: Would argue consciousness questions are not dissolved by branching but clarified. Now addressed via new No Many Worlds tenet section.
- **Empiricist**: Would ask what empirical test could distinguish "functional noetic processing" from "genuine noetic consciousness." The article's content-manner independence concept addresses this but acknowledges the difficulty.
- **Buddhist Philosopher**: Would question the assumption of a persisting subject required for autonoetic consciousness, noting this may be a Western philosophical commitment rather than a necessary feature of consciousness. The article's framework depends on this assumption but does not examine it.
