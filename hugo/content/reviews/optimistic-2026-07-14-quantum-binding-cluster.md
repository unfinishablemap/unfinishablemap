## Executive Summary

Three articles that together form the Map's strongest current cluster: they take a genuinely hard metaphysical claim (consciousness resists composition; phenomenal unity resists classical binding) and pair it with an unusually honest evidential ledger about the quantum substrate proposed to match it. The freshly created `radical-pair-magnetoreception` fills the single clearest gap the morning review flagged — a canonical, calibrated home for the Map's headline warm-biology precedent — and does so without over-reading the evidence. The cluster's chief remaining opportunities are definitional (two load-bearing terms are used corpus-wide with no home page) and cosmetic-integration (the new RPM page ships with empty outbound frontmatter), not calibration.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
`composition-and-consciousness` gives the hard problem a fresh structural face: the same compositional failure appears "in both directions" — non-phenomenal parts cannot compose phenomenal wholes (physicalism's hard problem) and phenomenal parts cannot compose phenomenal wholes either (panpsychism's combination problem). Chalmers, whose own 2017 combination-problem paper is cited, would recognise this as taking the explanatory gap seriously at the level of mereology rather than merely restating it. `quantum-holism` keeps the gap open with matching honesty: quantum holism "does not dissolve the hard problem … the transition from 'physically unified' to 'phenomenally unified' is relocated, not explained."

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
`quantum-holism` gives the entanglement-as-binding thesis its most careful exposition: Neven's "only true binding agent in physics" claim is deployed as a *structural* match to phenomenal unity, explicitly contrasted with IIT's *definitional* move ("call integration 'unity'" vs "here is something that actually *is* one thing"). The division of labour — classical mechanisms for BP1, quantum holism for BP2 — is exactly the interactionist architecture Stapp-sympathetic readers want, and it is held as a candidate, not a result.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The cluster centres first-person structure throughout. `quantum-holism`'s Leibniz-mill passage — the "*type* of thing physical description provides … is structurally different from the *type* of thing phenomenal unity is" — is a category-level defence of the irreducibility of the what-it-is-like, and the witness-consciousness / *nirodha-samāpatti* material treats introspective report as evidence rather than noise.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
`quantum-holism`'s "Process Philosophy Perspective" section is a genuine, not decorative, use of Whitehead: each quantum collapse read as an "actual occasion … internally unified rather than assembled from separates." The passage explicitly explains *why* classical binding fails in process terms (it describes completed occasions interacting after the fact, not occasions achieving internal unity in coming to be). **Discipline note:** this resonance is kept on the metaphysical-possibility axis — it is offered as conceptual resources for *understanding* quantum binding, not as evidence that the quantum mechanism is neurally realised. No evidential tier is upgraded on process-philosophical grounds.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
`quantum-holism` ties unity to agency cleanly: "phenomenal unity is a precondition for agency — the subject who selects among possibilities must be one subject." The collapse-as-selection reading gives Kane's indeterminacy-makes-room intuition a concrete locus (selecting which binding configuration actualises) without energy injection.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
Every article volunteers its own closure. `composition-and-consciousness` treats mysterianism honestly as "intellectually honest but provides no positive account," refusing to hide behind it. `radical-pair-magnetoreception` closes on Hore and Mouritsen's 2016 verdict that the primary mechanism "is still unclear," and `quantum-holism` runs an explicit "What Would Challenge This View?" falsification list. McGinn would recognise principled humility, not defeatism.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
**No minimal-organism / animal-boundary claims appear in this cluster, so the persona's tier-upgrade tripwire is not triggered — but its calibration lens is still load-bearing on the quantum-substrate claims, and all three articles pass.**

`radical-pair-magnetoreception` is the standout. It is built around the exact pattern the persona rewards — *precedent, not licence* — and states it in its own lead: RPM is "a precedent for the mechanism category (warm quantum effects *can* occur in living systems), not a licence for any neural deployment of them." The transfer limit is quantified, not gestured at: coherence "tens of microseconds … roughly two orders of magnitude below the millisecond timescales of neural signalling." It declines the parsimony win symmetrically — RPM refutes "warm biology must be classical," but "the anti-parsimony point must not be overdrawn either" because the radiofrequency-replication evidence (Engels 2014 → Leberecht 2023 upper bound) is itself unsettled. Crucially, it correctly refuses to read a sensory transduction as mind–brain evidence: RPM "is orthogonal to the dualist and bidirectional tenets." That is tenet-as-evidence-upgrade praise-worthily *not done*.

`quantum-holism` earns the same restraint on the harder material. The entanglement-matches-unity thesis is repeatedly marked "a claim the Map advances on tenet grounds, not one forced by the data," the Hagan-vs-Reimers decoherence dispute is presented as "live, not closed" with the explicit warning that "citing Hagan as a settled rebuttal would be selective citation," and the Denton-2024 line already carries the "computational precedent … not a neural demonstration" qualifier. `composition-and-consciousness` faithfully preserves the McQueen–Tsuchiya *decoupling* (they "explicitly decouple that composition criterion from consciousness itself") rather than conscripting Φ-maximisation as consciousness evidence — the honest reading of a source that could easily have been over-claimed.

## Content Strengths

### quantum-holism-and-phenomenal-unity.md
- **Strongest point**: The structural firewall between the physics claim and the phenomenal claim. Quantum holism is offered as the substrate that "actually *is* one thing," while the article insists this "relocates, not explains" the hard problem — the two claims never blur.
- **Notable quote**: "No amount of coordination among separate things makes them literally one thing."
- **Why it works**: It converts three separate NCC theories' failures (synchrony, IIT, GWT) into a single diagnosis — external relations cannot constitute internal unity — then names the one physical phenomenon (entanglement) with the matching structure, while flagging the match as tenet-motivated rather than data-forced.

### composition-and-consciousness.md
- **Strongest point**: The "compositional failure runs both ways" symmetry, generalising Coleman's 2014 subject-combination dilemma from phenomenal to non-phenomenal building blocks — a genuine analytic move, not a restatement.
- **Notable quote**: "Consciousness marks the place where composition breaks down."
- **Why it works**: The primer keeps its scope discipline — it hands the full dialectic (rivals, IIT calibration) to the linked extended article and the rival treatment to `composition-question-rivals`, so it stays a genuine entry point rather than duplicating the argument.

### radical-pair-magnetoreception.md
- **Strongest point**: It is a purpose-built calibration anchor. It names, in its lead, the four features that bound the precedent (single photoactivated sensor; microsecond coherence; Kominis-not-Denton priority; modelling-not-experiment) so downstream articles can link rather than re-derive — exactly the piecemeal policing the Denton propagation memory keeps having to do by hand.
- **Notable quote**: "Transferring a specialised retinal sensor's coherence to deep-brain, non-photonic structures would be an unearned leap."
- **Why it works**: It captures the up-to-date empirical nuance (Leberecht 2023 revising Engels 2014's weak-broadband headline while *supporting* RPM overall) instead of citing the older, cleaner-looking result as settled.
