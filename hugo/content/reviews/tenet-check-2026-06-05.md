## Summary

No tenet violations found. The corpus is in strong alignment with all five foundational
tenets. Every appearance of a ruled-out view (illusionism, eliminative materialism,
many-worlds, quantum mysticism/psychokinesis, epiphenomenalism, parsimony-against-dualism)
occurs in one of three legitimate framings:

1. **Opponent under rebuttal** — the view is stated to be argued against (e.g. illusionism's
   regress problem, the argument from reason against physicalism, the indexical objection
   to many-worlds).
2. **Conditional inside a Map argument** — "if physicalism is true, then..." constructions
   that are antecedents of anti-physicalist arguments, not assertions.
3. **Definitional / "see also" gloss** — link descriptions or citation lines that describe
   what an opposing view holds without endorsing it.

This is exactly the disciplined engagement the writing-style and tenet framework call for:
opposing positions are presented fairly and then placed against the Map's commitments.

## Method

Read `obsidian/tenets/tenets.md` for current tenet definitions and Rules-out clauses, then
ran targeted pattern searches across `topics/`, `concepts/`, and `positions/` for each
tenet's ruled-out language:

- **Dualism**: "consciousness is an illusion", "just/merely neurons/brain activity",
  "materialism/physicalism is true/correct".
- **Minimal Quantum Interaction**: psychokinesis/telekinesis endorsement, energy injection,
  quantum woo / New Age / "the universe is conscious" / cosmic consciousness.
- **Bidirectional Interaction**: "consciousness is epiphenomenal / causally inert / along
  for the ride" as bare endorsement.
- **No Many Worlds**: MWI endorsement ("many-worlds is correct/true", "favour many-worlds").
- **Occam's Razor Has Limits**: parsimony/simplicity used to decisively favour physicalism
  or rule out dualism as an endorsed conclusion.

Each pattern's hits were inspected in context. All hits resolved to legitimate engagement
(categories 1-3 above). Notable concentrations that were verified clean:

- ~30 illusionism references — all present the regress objection or treat illusionism as
  the challenge the Map answers (`functional-seeming`, `meta-problem-of-consciousness`,
  `substrate-independence`, etc.).
- ~25 many-worlds references — all reject MWI on the indexical-identity grounds Tenet 4
  specifies, with several models (e.g. `epistemic-advantages-of-dualism`,
  `death-and-consciousness`) explicitly marking the rejection as a framework-boundary
  commitment rather than an in-framework refutation, which matches the tenet's own honest
  framing.
- ~20 psychokinesis references — all distinguish the Map's minimal, brain-internal,
  conservation-respecting interaction from gross psychokinesis (`brain-specialness-boundary`,
  `bidirectional-interaction`, `causal-powers`), or cite the micro-PK null literature
  (Maier-Dechamps 2018, Bösch et al. 2006) as evidence the Map accommodates.
- cosmopsychism/idealism references — all treated as rival monist positions, fairly
  stated and then engaged (combination/decombination problem).
- "physicalism is true" — every instance is the antecedent of the argument from reason /
  self-stultification argument, i.e. used to derive physicalism's self-defeat.
- "parsimony favours physicalism" — every instance is the opponent's premise that
  `parsimony-epistemology` and `parsimony-case-for-interactionist-dualism` dismantle under
  Tenet 5; the corpus also correctly applies Tenet 5's *self-binding* (Map arguments do not
  lean on parsimony either).
