## Pessimistic Analysis Summary

### Critical Issues Found

- **Wrong-author quote misattribution (Lacan → Žižek). FIXED.** The Convergence
  §"Lacan: Desire as Structure" presented `"fantasy constitutes our desire,
  provides its coordinates"` as a direct Lacan quotation, with References entry 5
  pointing to Lacan, *The Four Fundamental Concepts of Psycho-Analysis* (1973). The
  verbatim string is **Žižek's**, from *The Sublime Object of Ideology* (1989) — his
  Kantian-transcendental-schematism gloss on Lacan ("a fantasy constitutes our
  desire, provides its coordinates; that is, it literally teaches us how to desire").
  It is not a line from Four Fundamental Concepts. This is a wrong-author
  misattribution (critical per the attribution-accuracy discipline), and it is the
  textbook "quote fidelity is orthogonal to reference-metadata" pattern: the
  2026-06-18 ledger correctly marked the Lacan 1973 *book reference* real-correct,
  but that check ratifies the book's existence, not the authorship of the quoted
  words.

  Resolution (length-neutral, ~+6 words): re-attributed the gloss to Žižek in text —
  "organised through fantasy—or, in Žižek's gloss on this Lacanian point, fantasy
  'constitutes our desire, provides its coordinates'" — and added References entry 6,
  Žižek, S. (1989), *The Sublime Object of Ideology*, Verso (subsequent entries
  renumbered 7–11). The surrounding Lacanian substance (objet petit a as
  object-cause of desire, desire misidentifying its target, desire mediated by the
  symbolic order) is genuinely Lacan and much of it is in Four Fundamental Concepts,
  so the Lacan reference stays valid for the paragraph's exposition; only the quoted
  string needed re-attribution.
