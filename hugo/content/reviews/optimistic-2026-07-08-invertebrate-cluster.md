## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The cluster takes the explanatory gap seriously at the *lower* limit, where it is easiest to wave away. `consciousness-in-simple-organisms.md` makes the scale-independence of the hard problem its Dualism anchor: "If we cannot explain how human neural activity produces experience, we cannot explain it for nematodes either. The explanatory gap exists at every complexity level." Chalmers would recognise his own point being pressed exactly where physicalists most want a smooth gradient to dissolve it.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
The Minimal-Quantum-Interaction constraint is worked hard and honestly. The invertebrate article derives *scale independence*, *architectural flexibility*, *evolutionary independence*, and *anaesthetic convergence* as constraints on the coupling mechanism, then concedes the live difficulty: "the thermal and electromagnetic environment of a bee's million-neuron brain differs substantially from a human cortex, raising the question of whether decoherence constrains coupling differently across architectures." Stapp would value that the quantum-interface commitment is stated as a constraint to be met, not a solved mechanism.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The octopus trilemma centres "what it is like" precisely. The article insists on the phenomenal-vs-access distinction rather than eliding it: the octopus "plainly has *access* unity (its arms coordinate to unified purposes) while the open question is whether it has *phenomenal* unity (one encompassing 'what it is like')." That is Nagel's question kept load-bearing inside a distributed nervous system.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
Well and *correctly* served. `consciousness-in-simple-organisms.md` gives Whitehead a full "Process Philosophy Perspective" section — actual occasions, prehension, "degrees of integration, not presence/absence" — and then does the disciplined thing: "This perspective doesn't commit the Map to full Whiteheadian metaphysics." Crucially, the process-friendly reading is never used to lift an organism up the evidential scale. Whitehead's intuition removes the mystery of emergence; it is not cashed as evidence that *Physarum* is a subject.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
`valence-and-conscious-selection.md` gives agency real teeth: the value-sensitive horn makes the Map's physics "locally teleological" — "outcomes are selected *because they matter*, not merely *by* something to which things matter" — while `agent-teleology` grounds selection in reasons that "bottom out in felt value." Kane would value that the article refuses to let selection be arbitrary (the anti-randomness argument at "Random choice would make conscious causation purposeless") without smuggling the criterion in unexamined.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
Directly honoured. The nematode is offered as an *empirical* exhibit of cognitive closure: "we possess complete structural knowledge of 302 neurons yet cannot determine whether the worm experiences anything... This is what cognitive closure looks like empirically." McGinn's thesis is usually argued *a priori*; the cluster gives it a worked instance.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
**This is the cluster's standout strength, and the persona's verdict here is load-bearing because every article touches minimal-organism consciousness.** The cluster does the tenet-as-evidence-upgrade that is *praiseworthy to not do*, over and over, as explicit machinery rather than incidental caution:

- `consciousness-in-simple-organisms.md` carries a dedicated **"Evidential Register: Tenet vs. Evidence"** section that names the exact slippage the persona guards against: "The risk worth defending against is treating 'not ruled out by our metaphysics' as if it were 'positively supported by the evidence' — a slide that lets metaphysical permission absorb negative empirical findings rather than be constrained by them." (Verified present, verbatim, at the article's evidential-register section.)
- The same article states the anti-absorption rule as a standing constraint: **"The interface reading must not absorb the negative empirical signal."** Failing Unlimited Associative Learning "should flow through to the evidential-status verdict for each organism... The interface reading lowers our confidence that UAL marks a *necessary* boundary; it does not raise our confidence that organisms below it are conscious." (Verified verbatim.) This is the persona's ideal move — a coupling-marker reframe that is explicitly barred from erasing a negative empirical signal.
- The five-tier scale is applied *downward and consistently*: *C. elegans* at "live hypothesis, sliding toward speculative integration"; Hydra and *Physarum* at "speculative integration"; *Physarum* read as "cognition without consciousness as the more parsimonious reading." No boundary case is ever placed at "realistic possibility" on tenet-load.
- `marginal-organism-scope-of-value-sensitive-selection.md` makes the restraint its thesis: **"the boundary cannot currently be located, and the Map's tenets do not help locate it... This temptation must be resisted, and resisting it is load-bearing."** (Verified verbatim.) The *Physarum* disjunction — that valenced-looking behaviour "underdetermines value-*sensitive selection*," demoting approach/avoidance as a marker "up the whole ladder" including for the rat — is calibration reasoning of the first order: it lets a limiting case discipline the confident cases rather than the reverse.
- `valence-and-conscious-selection.md` extends the same discipline to the Map's *own preferred* horn: it writes the value-blind horn "its strongest statement, not merely its weakest objection," concedes "value-blindness is the *more minimal* dualism" the Map "would be *forced* to hold if mechanism-availability and parsimony were the only arbiters," and states plainly "leaning value-sensitive without a mechanism is an aspiration, not a position." A fork the Map *wants* to win, argued against itself on parsimony grounds — exactly the restraint the persona rewards.

The Process Philosopher and the Hardline Empiricist converge on the *same* passages (the "Process Philosophy Perspective" and "Evidential Register" sections sit one heading apart and do not fight). By the skill's own criterion, that convergence indicates the possibility/probability tension is resolved honestly. **No calibration concern; no `refine-draft` warranted.**

## Content Strengths

### marginal-organism-scope-of-value-sensitive-selection.md
- **Strongest point**: The *descending ladder* device — rat → nematode → Hydra → slime mould, "each rung removing a structural prerequisite the value-sensitive horn appears to need (anticipatory evaluation, a central integration point, a nervous system at all)." It converts a vague "where's the boundary?" into a structured diagnosis of *which* prerequisite fails *where*, and then observes "the markers degrade independently."
- **Notable quote** (verified): "*anticipatory* valence — the property the selection law actually needs — is never positively demonstrated at any rung below the mammal, and is contested even there."
- **Why it works**: It separates scope-of-consciousness from scope-of-*value-sensitive-selection* ("an organism could be minimally conscious yet still select value-blindly"), a distinction most treatments collapse — and that separation is what keeps the whole ladder from sliding into over-attribution.

### consciousness-in-simple-organisms.md
- **Strongest point**: The evidential-register scaffolding (tenet-vs-evidence section + per-organism five-tier table + the UAL anti-absorption paragraph) is a reusable template for how the Map should treat *every* boundary case. It is the concrete embodiment of `evidential-status-discipline`.
- **Why it works**: It makes the discipline *structural* (a labelled section and a status column) rather than relying on the reader to infer restraint from tone.

### valence-and-conscious-selection.md
- **Strongest point**: Steelmanning the horn the Map disfavours. The "Positive Case for Value-Blindness" section is a genuine argument, not a strawman waiting to be knocked down — and it explicitly ties the obligation to steelman back to `evidential-status-discipline` ("a fork resolved by attrition rather than argument would violate the Map's discipline").

### High Priority

#### Plant cognition and the plant-neurobiology debate (as a dualist interface-test)
- **Discrete subject**: The controversy over learning-like behaviour in plants — Gagliano's *Mimosa pudica* habituation and associative-conditioning claims, electrical signalling and the Venus flytrap's counting (*Dionaea*), and the "plant neurobiology" dispute (Calvo/Trewavas vs Taiz et al.) — read through the Map's cognition-without-consciousness / interface lens.
- **Coverage-check result**: **Confirmed genuinely absent.** `grep -rli "gagliano\|trewavas\|calvo\|phytoneuro\|venus flytrap\|mimosa pudica"` over the entire repo returns nothing; there is no plant-cognition article or research note. It is *not* redundant with the slime-mould treatment: `Physarum` is a single-celled non-neural case, whereas plants are multicellular non-neural systems with genuine electrical action potentials and systemic signalling — a distinct rung the `marginal-organism` ladder currently *skips* (it jumps from Hydra's diffuse nerve net straight to the single-celled slime mould). Plants are the canonical "learning without neurons in a multicellular body" case.
- **One-line rationale**: The Map already treats *Physarum* as its clean "cognition dissociates from consciousness" exhibit; plants are the higher-profile, more-contested version of exactly that test and would let the simple-organism ladder discharge its most obvious omission.
- **Tenet alignment**: Tenets 1 & 5 — a boundary case where the dualist framing (defeater-removal, not evidence-elevation) and the five-tier discipline are directly exercised. Note for the writer: this is a *high* over-attribution-risk subject; the article must apply the same "tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating" discipline the rest of the cluster models.

#### Michael Levin's basal / bioelectric cognition (xenobots, "cognition all the way down")
- **Discrete subject**: Levin's bioelectric-signalling account of morphogenesis-as-cognition, xenobots/anthrobots as engineered minimal agents, and the TAME ("Technological Approach to Mind Everywhere") framework — assessed as the strongest current *materialist* "agency/cognition without brains" programme and as a candidate sub-neural coupling substrate.
- **Coverage-check result**: **Confirmed genuinely absent.** No article or research note mentions Levin (the corpus's "Levin/Levine" hits are all Joseph Levine of the explanatory gap), bioelectricity, xenobots, or morphogenesis-as-cognition. [concepts/baseline-cognition.md](/concepts/baseline-cognition/) — the nearest neighbour — contains none of these terms; it is Tomasello/"zone of latent solutions" material, a different research programme.
- **One-line rationale**: Levin's bioelectric code is precisely a proposed *sub-neuronal* organisational level, which bears directly on the cluster's own conclusion that the coupling mechanism must "operate at a fundamental level of neural organisation — perhaps at the level of individual neurons or synapses rather than large-scale circuits"; xenobots also give the interface model an *engineered* minimal-agent test case it currently lacks.
- **Tenet alignment**: Tenets 1 & 2 — bioelectricity as a candidate (materialist) rival account of where organisation-that-looks-like-mind lives; a natural cognition-without-consciousness case for the Map to engage as it engages IIT and GWT.

### Medium Priority

#### Fish sentience as a boundary case between the mammal rung and the invertebrate frontier
- **Discrete subject**: The fish-pain debate — Sneddon/Braithwaite's positive nociception-plus-behaviour case vs Key's (2016) "why fish do not feel pain," teleost neuroanatomy and the pallium-vs-cortex question — positioned between the "clearly conscious" mammal/bird cases and the contested invertebrate frontier.
- **Coverage-check result**: **Confirmed absent as a dedicated subject.** "Fish" appears in the corpus only obliquely (the Sneddon *criteria* are invoked for the *rat* rung in `marginal-organism`); there is no fish-sentience article and no `fish feel pain / teleost` treatment. The Map covers `cetacean-and-corvid-consciousness`, invertebrates, and simple organisms but skips the vertebrate case that has generated the sharpest published pain-scepticism.
- **One-line rationale**: Fish are the vertebrate boundary case where the "realistic possibility" default is publicly contested on neuroanatomical grounds (no six-layered cortex), making them a cleaner test of the Map's threshold-vs-gradient claim than the invertebrates, where the dispute is about presence rather than architecture.
- **Tenet alignment**: Tenets 1 & 5. Lower priority than the two above because the dualist interface angle is less distinctive (fish are less architecturally surprising than plants or bioelectric agents), but it fills a real vertebrate-ladder gap.
