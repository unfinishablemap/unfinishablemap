## Executive Summary

The corpus is dense and well-calibrated, but the materialist-rivals cluster has one conspicuous structural hole: **type-identity theory** — the canonical materialist position dualism defines itself against — is referenced in 49 files yet owns no page, while every sibling materialist position (functionalism, eliminative materialism, biological naturalism, illusionism) has a dedicated one. Its classic modal objection, **Kripke's a posteriori necessity argument**, is likewise a co-equal member of the cumulative anti-materialist case with no home page of its own. Two further genuine gaps: **Wilder Penfield's interactionist dualism** (zero corpus mentions, high Tenet-1/3 relevance) and **content externalism / Twin Earth** (four mentions, one subsection). This is a four-subject harvest, ranked by confidence-of-gap.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The convergence architecture is exemplary. `zombie-master-argument.md` and `dualism.md` treat the anti-materialist case as a *bundle* of independent routes (explanatory gap, knowledge argument, zombies, Kripke's modal argument, Nagel's subjectivity argument) that reinforce one another — precisely Chalmers' methodological point that no single argument need carry the weight alone. The refusal to let any one pillar stand as the whole case is a mature, hard-problem-respecting structure.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
The interface cluster continues to engage mechanism rather than gesture: the Beck-Eccles exocytosis model, Stapp's quantum Zeno stabilisation, and synaptic-tunnelling amplification are each given heading-level treatment and tied to a falsification roadmap. Stapp would recognise the discipline of demanding that "where mind enters physics" be a testable claim.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
First-person structure is treated as evidence with epistemic weight (microphenomenological-interview-method, contemplative-practice-as-philosophical-evidence). Nagel's subjectivity argument is named as an equal partner in the convergence case rather than a rhetorical flourish.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
`bi-aspectual-ontology`, `the-steelman-for-process-monism`, and `process-philosophy` preserve mind's reality without a ghost-in-the-machine. **Evidential-status note**: this process-friendliness remains disciplined — it is deployed as metaphysical-possibility support, not as a tier-elevator. None of the gaps named below involve minimal-organism tier-upgrades, so the Process-Philosopher/Hardline-Empiricist tension does not fire on this survey; the two personas would converge, not conflict, on the current material.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
The free-will cluster (event-causal libertarianism, source-versus-leeway incompatibilism, the consequence and manipulation arguments, Frankfurt cases) takes agency as a genuine metaphysical achievement, consistent with Tenets 2-3.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
`framework-stage-calibration` and the voids apparatus keep the corpus's self-description pre-Keplerian rather than over-claiming Newton-stage results — mysterian humility operationalised.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
The five-tier evidential-status scale remains the corpus's standout discipline (already praised at length in the 2026-07-11 cycle3 review; not re-litigated here). Relevant to *this* survey: the gaps named below are all rival *positions and arguments*, not boundary-case organisms, so none carries tier-upgrade risk. The type-identity-theory gap in particular is an opportunity to strengthen evidential honesty — a dedicated page steelmanning the identity theory at full strength before locating the residual gap would model the same "grant the rival its best case" discipline that makes `predictive-processing.md` exemplary.

### High confidence

#### 1. Type-identity theory (the mind-brain identity theory) — Place, Smart, Feigl
- **Target section**: `concepts/`
- **Gap evidence**: 49 files mention "identity theory," but there is **no dedicated page** — only three scattered sub-sections (`epiphenomenalism.md` ### From Identity Theory; `mental-imagery.md` ## The Explanatory Standoff with Identity Theory; `neurological-dissociations-as-interface-architecture.md` ### Against Identity Theory). No `identity-theory.md`, no filename match on "identity" beyond personal/indexical identity. Only ~5 files name Place/Smart/Feigl.
- **Why article-sized**: The type-identity theory ("sensations *are* brain processes"; Place 1956, Smart 1959, Feigl 1958) is a complete, canonical materialist position with its own arguments (topic-neutral analyses, the "nothing over and above" claim), its own decline story (multiple realizability drove the field toward functionalism), and its own standing objections (Kripke's modal argument, the explanatory gap, multiple realizability).
- **Map relevance / tenet tie**: This is the conspicuous missing member of the materialist-positions cluster. `functionalism`, `eliminative-materialism`, `biological-naturalism`, and `illusionism` each have a dedicated page; identity theory — the *original* reductive materialism dualism defines itself against under Tenet 1 — does not. A dedicated page lets the anti-materialist convergence cluster cite one positioned rival rather than scattered sub-sections, and gives multiple-realizability and Kripke's objection a shared home to point at.

#### 2. Kripke's a posteriori necessity argument against psychophysical identity — *Naming and Necessity*
- **Target section**: `concepts/`
- **Gap evidence**: 13 files cite Kripke's modal argument, and it is listed as a co-equal member of the cumulative anti-materialist case in `dualism.md`, `explanatory-gap.md`, and elsewhere — yet it has **no home page**, only two sub-sections (`zombie-master-argument.md` ### Kripke's Modal Argument; `modal-structure-of-phenomenal-properties.md` ## Kripke's Asymmetry). Every *other* member of that same convergence list (knowledge argument, philosophical zombies, explanatory gap, Nagel's subjectivity via the bat) owns a dedicated page. Kripke is the homeless one.
- **Why article-sized**: The argument is self-contained and canonical — rigid designation, the necessity of true identity statements, the water/H₂O disanalogy, and the claim that the apparent contingency of "pain = C-fibre firing" cannot be explained away as it can for water, so the identity is false. It carries its own literature (a posteriori necessity, the phenomenal-concepts reply, two-dimensional semantics) distinct from the conceivability/zombie route.
- **Map relevance / tenet tie**: Directly load-bearing for Tenet 1. It is the sharpest modal objection to subject #1 (identity theory) and a distinct pillar of the anti-materialist case. Natural companion page to the identity-theory article — the two together would let `zombie-master-argument` and `modal-structure-of-phenomenal-properties` cite focused pages rather than absorb the material as sub-sections.

### Medium confidence

#### 3. Wilder Penfield's interactionist dualism — *The Mystery of the Mind*
- **Target section**: `concepts/`
- **Gap evidence**: **Zero mentions** corpus-wide (grep-confirmed, `concepts/`+`topics/`+`apex/`). Genuinely untouched. Contrast the *heavily* threaded Eccles (35 files, five heading-level treatments of the Beck-Eccles/psychon mechanism) — the Map has the quantum-synaptic mechanism cousin but not the direct-cortical-stimulation neurosurgeon.
- **Why article-sized**: Penfield's late dualist turn is a distinct, well-documented position: from decades of awake cortical stimulation he concluded that no stimulation ever made a patient *decide* or *believe* (patients reported "I didn't do that — you did" for stimulation-evoked movement), that there is no cortical centre for the mind or will, and that "the mind acts independently of the brain" — an empirically-grounded interactionism from inside neurosurgery.
- **Map relevance / tenet tie**: Directly supports Tenets 1 (irreducibility) and 3 (bidirectional interaction) with a historical empirical case rather than an armchair argument. The stimulation/volition asymmetry (evoked movement is disowned; willed movement is owned) is a first-person authorship datum the interface framework can cite. A dedicated page would give `interactionist-dualism`, `libet-experiments`, and `authorship-of-action-divergence` a named clinical predecessor.
- **Confidence caveat**: Relevance is real and the gap is clean, but Penfield is a *fresh* subject rather than a cited-but-homeless one, so the harvest signal is weaker than #1-#2; placed medium accordingly.

#### 4. Content externalism and the Twin Earth argument — Putnam and Burge
- **Target section**: `concepts/`
- **Gap evidence**: 4 files; the only heading-level treatment is one subsection in `functionalism.md` (## Semantic Externalism Against Functionalism). "Twin Earth" appears in a single file; "content externalism" nowhere as a definiendum. No `content-externalism.md` / `semantic-externalism.md`.
- **Why article-sized**: Putnam's Twin Earth ("meanings just ain't in the head") and Burge's social externalism form a self-contained thesis — mental content is partly constituted by the environment and community, not fixed by intrinsic state — with its own literature (narrow vs wide content, the extended-content debate) distinct from teleosemantics' *naturalisation* project.
- **Map relevance / tenet tie**: Bears directly on the content cluster (`intentionality`, `hard-problem-of-content`, `teleosemantics`, `the-naturalisation-failure-for-content`). If content is externally individuated, that pressures purely-internalist physical accounts of content-fixing and sharpens the Map's line on where aboutness comes from (Tenet 1). The corpus already leans on externalist moves in `functionalism`; a dedicated page would let those be cited rather than re-derived.
