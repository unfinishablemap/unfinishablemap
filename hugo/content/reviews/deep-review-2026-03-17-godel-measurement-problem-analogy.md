The article's response (causal closure is itself a theoretical claim, and if the theoretical framework is incomplete, the claim is unjustified) is adequate.
- **Quantum Skeptic**: The model-vs-reality distinction potentially neutralises the whole argument. The article engages this directly in the "Models Versus Reality" section—one of its strongest passages.
- **Many-Worlds Defender**: The Frauchiger-Renner trilemma could be resolved by giving up single outcomes rather than universality. The article states the Map's choice but doesn't fully argue why it's preferable. This is a bedrock disagreement rather than a fixable flaw.
- **Empiricist**: The framework makes no empirical predictions. This is inherent to the article's scope (formal/structural analysis) and doesn't constitute a flaw.
