## Executive Summary

The current cohort is unusually strong on **named methodological discipline** — the Compatibilist Symmetry Challenge, the Discrimination Problem treatment, and the memory-hierarchy article's "accommodation evidence, not predictive success" framing all install the same anti-slippage hygiene at three different scales. The four pieces also converge on a recognisable rhetorical signature: a *structural argument* set out first, then *worked exhibits* in matched format, then *evidential calibration* that holds the line against the article's own most natural overreach. Expansion opportunities cluster around (a) installing the Compatibilist Symmetry Challenge wherever the moral apex is downstream, (b) developing the production-vs-filter discriminating-prediction test the memory-hierarchy article names, and (c) extending the Discrimination Problem framework's underdetermination cartography into a first-class catalogue concept.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)

The discrimination-problem article would land well with Chalmers because it explicitly preserves the *real* problem rather than dissolving it. The structural-asymmetry argument in §"Why It Is Stubborn" — "Whatever discriminator we propose ... is a functional state. Functional states are exactly the kind of thing a zombie ... could share with us. The discriminator therefore cannot, on its own, settle a question whose answer turns on whether functional duplication is sufficient for phenomenality" — is essentially the *Conscious Mind*'s zombie argument restated as an epistemological structural result rather than a metaphysical conceivability claim. Chalmers would also approve of the explicit refusal to treat heterophenomenology's neutrality as a solution rather than as the same hole in different clothes. The memory-hierarchy article's recognition that the autonoetic channel carries "the felt sense of pastness that constitutes the experience rather than merely accompanying it" honours the hard problem at the level of phenomenology rather than function.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)

The memory-hierarchy article quietly but firmly engages the quantum-interface picture at the right tier. The "each act of episodic retrieval involves the selectional work the Map locates at the quantum-indeterminacy layer of the interface" sentence does the work without overclaiming, and the recovery-order asymmetry observation (autonoetic last in deep anaesthesia, autonoetic first in terminal lucidity) is *exactly* the kind of direction-of-interface-change signature an MQI account predicts and a substrate-only picture cannot reach. Stapp would appreciate that the article does not invoke quantum mechanics ornamentally but uses the channel-bandwidth-and-reconstruction-cost vocabulary to make the interface picture do explanatory work. The self-stultification article's ketamine paragraph similarly anchors the master argument at a specific pharmacological dissociation rather than at diffuse waking life — Sarasso et al. 2015 cited correctly, with the dissociation framed as mechanistically isolable.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)

The discrimination-problem article's "what is it like" preservation is exemplary: when the illusionist position is presented, the article does not concede the first-person data but treats the illusionist as on the hook for a "theory that makes my present experience indistinguishable from no experience at all has lost contact with the data." This is Nagel's move — refusing to let the third-person framing erase the first-person datum that motivated the question. The memory-hierarchy article makes the same move at the level of *autonoetic* phenomenology: pastness, mine-ness, and double-presence are treated as constitutive features of episodic memory rather than as decorative accompaniments to information retrieval. The depersonalisation case ("the patient remembers their life, can re-experience episodes propositionally — but the felt sense of ownership is missing ... the channel is on; the mine-ness the channel normally carries is decoupled") is the kind of phenomenologically sensitive distinction that would satisfy Nagel about the catalogue's grain.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)

The memory-hierarchy article's "multi-channel interface with channels of differing bandwidth and reconstruction cost" architecture is congenial to a process reading: experience is not a single substance but a layered set of operations, channels, and reconstructions, with each act of recall being itself an event that destabilises and reconsolidates the trace. Whitehead would also approve of the article's avoidance of *crude* substance dualism — the interface is described as a relational architecture between channels and substrates rather than as a Cartesian res cogitans pushing buttons on a brain. The compatibilist-symmetry article's recognition that agency, value, normativity, and moral perception "converge on a single ground" without claiming the convergence is itself an argument has process-philosophical resonance — the unity is what the architecture *enables*, not a separate fact about it.

**Tier-upgrade restraint check.** The Process Philosopher's praise here does *not* cash out as a tier-upgrade. The memory-hierarchy article is careful to mark its central claim as *accommodation evidence* and to name the production-vs-filter discriminating test as a future case "the catalogue does not yet have a clean case of this kind to point to." No organism-level consciousness claim is upgraded on Whiteheadian grounds. The convergence with the Hardline Empiricist persona below is genuine rather than contrived.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)

The self-stultification article's performative-contradiction section ("the epiphenomenalist doesn't merely hold a false belief; they *demonstrate* its falsity by the act of defending it") is Kane's territory: the master argument shows that conscious deliberation is not an inert side-effect of physical processing but a constituent of how beliefs and choices get formed. Kane would particularly approve of the article's distinction between norm-*implementing* (theorem provers, calculators) and norm-*grasping* (recognising why a conclusion follows) — this is the consciousness-required-for-genuine-rational-endorsement move that grounds Kane's "ultimate source" condition for agency. The compatibilist-symmetry article extends this with appropriate honesty: even where libertarian agency is the Map's preferred reading, the central moral phenomena do not *require* it. Kane himself would acknowledge that ultimate desert and the strongest "could have done otherwise" readings are where the libertarian framing does separating work.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)

The discrimination-problem article's diagnosis that consciousness epistemology may face a "structural void: a region of fact about which our best current epistemic instruments are systematically unable to deliver verdicts ... not merely difficult; it appears principled" is McGinn's cognitive-closure thesis in catalogue terms. The voids connection at the article's foot is exactly the right move: acknowledge the void honestly rather than papering it over with overconfident theories. The memory-hierarchy article's evidential-calibration section — "the hierarchy is *accommodation evidence*, not predictive success at single-event resolution" — exhibits the same epistemic humility about what current evidence can settle. McGinn would also appreciate the self-stultification article's qualification that "a self-stultifying position might be true. Epiphenomenalism might be correct despite being impossible to rationally believe" — the refusal to overclaim refutation when self-stultification is the available result.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)

This persona has the most to praise across the cohort, and the convergence with the other personas is genuine — the articles are calibrated in ways that Whitehead-friendliness and evidential discipline can *both* endorse, on different axes.

**Tenet-as-evidence-upgrade praise-worthily *not* done.** The memory-hierarchy article had every structural opportunity to slide claims about minimal-organism or animal episodic memory up the five-tier scale (*established → strongly supported → realistic possibility → live hypothesis → speculative integration*) on the strength of the multi-channel architecture being a "stronger filter-prediction than single-binary-extinction." It declines. The article keeps its claims strictly to the *cross-state-vulnerability-pattern-in-humans* tier, and explicitly names terminal lucidity as "a live hypothesis the evidence does not yet decide" rather than as a "realistic possibility" of filter-theory truth. The structural argument is presented as *locating the disagreement at the mechanism-cost layer* rather than as *winning the disagreement*. This is exemplary five-tier discipline.

**Named pattern surfacing.** The compatibilist-symmetry-challenge article names a previously-unnamed slippage explicitly — "tenet-coherent, not moral-explanatory-superior" — and installs it as a methodological discipline available to *any* framework that grounds metaphysical commitments on grounds beyond moral theory (see the final paragraph of §"Relation to Site Perspective"). This is the same family of move as the evidential-status discipline's "tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating" naming, applied to a different domain. The article is also explicit that "the empirical and conceptual evidence is compatible with both libertarian and compatibilist readings" — *compatibility* rather than *support* is the correct register, and the article uses it.

**Framework-stage honesty.** The memory-hierarchy article's "the filter theory did not predict the Tulving hierarchy in advance; Tulving's framework was developed independently and the filter theory inherits its mapping. The claim is that the hierarchy is *exactly the shape* of evidence the filter theory's core architecture predicts" is a model of how to handle accommodation-vs-prediction calibration. This is pre-Newton-stage honesty in the [framework-stage-calibration](/project/framework-stage-calibration/) sense: the article does not claim Newton-stage predictive success it has not earned.

**Discriminator naming.** The memory-hierarchy article's §"The Structural Argument" explicitly names the future test the production and filter readings most cleanly disagree about ("a narrow perturbation that hits an autonoetic-supporting region while sparing its noetic-supporting subregions should, on the production reading, produce a *different* ordering, while the filter reading still predicts the standard one"). This is the [direct-refutation-discipline](/project/direct-refutation-discipline/) applied correctly: when readings cohabit the same data, name the future case that would separate them rather than treat the current data as already separating. The discriminator is named as a *test*, not as a *result already in the discriminating direction*.

**Animal-consciousness / minimal-organism boundary case.** Not directly engaged by this cohort, but the memory-hierarchy article's careful treatment of dissociative-states selectivity ("there is no substrate damage to which the selectivity can be attributed; it has to be explained at the level of *connectivity patterns between intact regions*") avoids any temptation to extrapolate from human channel architecture to animal channel architecture without naming the move.

The Hardline Empiricist verdict: this cohort is one of the more calibration-clean batches the catalogue has produced. The disciplines are not just stated as headers but enacted at the level of individual sentences.

## Content Strengths

### topics/memory-system-vulnerability-hierarchies-as-interface-evidence.md

- **Strongest point**: The structural-argument section's mechanism-cost cartography. The article does not claim the hierarchy *proves* the filter theory; it claims the hierarchy *locates the disagreement at the mechanism-cost layer*, where the filter reading's explanatory advantage is direct (predicted from architecture) while rival accommodations are additional (added to the framework rather than derived from it). This is the correct register for a piece of accommodation evidence.
- **Notable quote**: "The structural argument is not 'the hierarchy proves the filter theory' but 'the hierarchy locates the disagreement at the mechanism-cost layer.'"
- **Why it works**: The article preserves the dialectic. It engages binary-emergence, sophisticated-regional-vulnerability, and predictive-processing production readings *each on their own terms* and identifies where each pays its mechanism cost. This is the [bedrock-clash-vs-absorption](/project/bedrock-clash-vs-absorption/) discipline in action at the topic tier.
- **Secondary strength**: The cross-state table in §"The Cross-State Vulnerability Pattern" is one of the catalogue's better diagnostic tools. Eleven rows, three columns, and the pattern visible at a glance — anaesthesia, sleep, hemispherectomy, terminal lucidity, dissociation. The dissociation row is correctly flagged as "the hierarchy's hardest test for production theories" because the substrate is intact.

### concepts/discrimination-problem.md

- **Strongest point**: The structural-asymmetry result in §"Why It Is Stubborn." The article does not merely list discriminators that fail; it explains *why* any candidate discriminator must fail by the same structural argument: functional states cannot, on their own, settle a question whose answer turns on whether functional duplication is sufficient for phenomenality. This converts a literature-survey article into a result-bearing one.
- **Notable quote**: "The choice of theory is *itself* the discrimination criterion; the test does not independently establish it."
- **Why it works**: The article cleanly separates the first-person and third-person channels with named anchors (`{#first-person}`, `{#third-person}`), then converges them in §"Why It Is Stubborn." The named-anchor summaries follow the writing-style guide's "explained below" pattern. The §"Boundary Cases That Sharpen the Problem" section is well-chosen — five real cases (blindsight, vegetative state, anaesthesia, locked-in, LLMs) that *tighten the screws* rather than illustrate the general problem at uniform difficulty.

### concepts/compatibilist-symmetry-challenge.md

- **Strongest point**: The three-part structural move in §"The Structural Move." Step (1) parity, step (2) differentiator located outside moral theory, step (3) the residue at the limits — ultimate desert, "could have done otherwise" in its strongest sense, the metaphysics of genuine alternatives. The third step is what distinguishes the symmetry challenge from a *full concession* and is the article's most original contribution.
- **Notable quote**: "Libertarian agency is what those tenets make available; the moral phenomena alone do not establish it."
- **Why it works**: The article installs a methodological discipline by *naming* it. The compactness of the move ("a one-paragraph acknowledgement of the symmetry, a sentence locating the differentiator outside moral theory, and where appropriate a cross-link to this concept") makes the discipline cheap to apply, which is what gives it any chance of being applied. §"What the Discipline Forbids" is exemplary — four specific moves identified as out-of-bounds with the reasoning for each.
- **Secondary strength**: The contrast register is correctly identified. The compatibilist-vs-libertarian split is *irreducible-vs-derivative* (whether the agent is the ultimate source) rather than *metaphysical-vs-pragmatic* (which would misrepresent sophisticated compatibilism).

### topics/self-stultification-as-master-argument.md

- **Strongest point**: The newly-added ketamine paragraph in §"Epiphenomenalism" (per the AI refinement log). The paragraph anchors the master argument at a specific pharmacological dissociation — ketamine's behavioural unresponsiveness with later vivid reports — and notes that "the dissociation is mechanistically isolable: ketamine's NMDA antagonism severs access channels while sparing the phenomenal interface, producing a clinical configuration where the self-stultification problem appears at a specific, drug-induced, time-bounded interval rather than diffusely across waking life." The general argument now has a clinical worked exhibit.
- **Notable quote**: "The position may still be *true*. Self-stultification is weaker than refutation but more devastating in practice: it removes any rational grounds for accepting the theory, leaving it as a bare metaphysical possibility no one can justifiably endorse."
- **Why it works**: The four-target table in §"Why the Pattern Repeats" makes the master-argument claim precise. Each row identifies *what is severed* and *what the self-stultification consists in*, which prevents the "master argument" framing from sliding into vague gesture. The unity is named correctly as "the unity of the pattern across targets, not a claim that the argument is equally decisive against each."

#### Worked exhibit: the dissociative-states row as the hardest case for production theories

- **Builds on**: [topics/memory-system-vulnerability-hierarchies-as-interface-evidence.md](/topics/memory-system-vulnerability-hierarchies-as-interface-evidence/) §"Dissociative States" and [topics/clinical-dissociation-as-systematic-evidence.md](/topics/clinical-dissociation-as-systematic-evidence/)

A topic-tier article that develops dissociative amnesia, DID, and depersonalisation as a triple worked exhibit — each as a different geometry of channel separation without substrate damage — would deliver the catalogue's strongest *intact-substrate* interface-evidence case at full resolution. The depersonalisation case is especially diagnostic ("the channel is on; the mine-ness the channel normally carries is decoupled") and could carry its own concept-tier development of mine-ness as a separable phenomenal feature.
- **Estimated scope**: Medium topic article (1500–2500 words), possibly with a concept-tier companion on mine-ness

## New Concept Pages Needed

- **Mine-ness** (phenomenal ownership): The memory-hierarchy article's depersonalisation case argues that "autonoetic experience has internal structure (content plus mine-ness plus pastness) that can dissociate" — three separable phenomenal features. Pastness is covered indirectly through `concepts/temporal-consciousness` (likely existing); mine-ness as a phenomenal feature that can be on or off independently of content does not appear to have its own concept page. The depersonalisation literature, the felt-ownership-in-action literature, and the body-ownership-illusion literature converge on this feature.
- **Cross-mechanism convergence as evidence pattern**: A short methodological-discipline page in `project/` on why convergence of mechanism-distinct perturbations on the same ordering is a stronger evidence pattern than single-mechanism replication. The memory-hierarchy article uses this implicitly; the active-reboot phenomenon and the cross-state self-stultification pattern use it implicitly. Naming it explicitly would make it a load-bearing methodological notion the catalogue could cite.
- **Uniqueness slippage** (in the failure-mode family): The compatibilist-symmetry article identifies a failure mode it calls "uniqueness slippage" — the move from "this metaphysics makes the moral content available" to "this metaphysics is required for the moral content." This is parallel to but distinct from the [evidential-status-discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/)'s possibility/probability slippage. A short concept page in the methodological-discipline family would surface this as one of the named failure modes the catalogue guards against.
