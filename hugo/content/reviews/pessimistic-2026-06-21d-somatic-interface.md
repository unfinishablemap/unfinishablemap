## Executive Summary

The article is structurally clean — no forbidden editor-vocabulary labels, no `**Evidential status:**` callouts, no over-strong language, the altered-state symmetry audit does not apply (no supportive-cluster citations), and the two empirically load-bearing citations (Frontiers 2025 "There is no such thing as interoception," DOI 10.3389/fpsyg.2025.1488415; Garfinkel et al. 2015, *Biological Psychology* 104:65–74) were web-verified as real and faithfully glossed. ## Critiques by Philosopher

### The Eliminative Materialist (Churchland)
Every clinical phenomenon you cite — pain asymbolia, phantom limbs, somatic-marker deficits, vestibular vertigo — "was discovered and explained within materialist neuroscience" (your own caveat, line 40). You then say the interface reading "adds a further claim" that the channel "structures the *phenomenal character*" and that this "is not fully explained by the neural pathway that carries it" (line 54). But "not fully explained yet" is precisely the eliminativist's promissory territory, not evidence for a non-physical contributor. You have given me a vocabulary, not a discovery. The whole article is folk-psychological re-description ("the body proposes, consciousness disposes," line 76) of results that came with a complete mechanistic story attached.

### The Hard-Nosed Physicalist (Dennett)
You repeatedly state both readings "predict the same cleavage" and "account for the clinical data equally well" (lines 60, 78, 106). On your own telling the interface reading earns *zero* differential predictive purchase. So what work is it doing? You answer: it makes the explanatory gap "a structural *feature* of the model rather than an unsolved problem" (line 40). But declaring a problem solved by absorbing it into your axioms is not an explanation — it is the move you accuse materialism of ("explain away," line 40) wearing the opposite hat. A model that "accommodates" the hard problem by stipulating a non-physical contributor has not closed the gap; it has renamed it.

### The Quantum Skeptic (Tegmark)
The Minimal Quantum Interaction section (line 116) says the somatic interface "may be where this biasing is most phenomenologically visible," then immediately concedes the same gaps are "equally consistent with undiscovered neural mechanisms" and that this is "speculative... a framework prediction, not a clinical one." That is a claim that cannot be wrong and therefore says nothing. There is no decoherence calculation, no proposed locus, no order-of-magnitude — exactly the empty-physics-dressing I object to.

### The Many-Worlds Defender (Deutsch)
Minor exposure here, but the appeal to "indexical identity" is absent and the article leans entirely on the unargued premise that phenomenal character has a "non-physical contributor" (line 116). That premise is imported, not earned by the somatic data.

### The Empiricist (Popper's Ghost)
Three explicit self-immunising statements: "does not predict different clinical outcomes" (line 40), "Both frameworks predict the same cleavage" (line 60), "This is a framework prediction, not a clinical one" (line 116). By your own admission no experiment could distinguish your reading from materialism. This is not even wrong. To its credit the article is *honest* about this — but honesty about unfalsifiability does not convert it into a virtue.

### The Buddhist Philosopher (Nagarjuna)
The article treats "the body's side of the interaction" and "what consciousness contributes" (line 112) as two standing relata. Christina's case is read as consciousness losing its "anchorage" while persisting (line 66) — but a Madhyamaka reading would say the dissolution of the felt self in that case is evidence *against* a persisting inhabitant, not for one. The "inhabitant of the map rather than its product" framing (line 78, 106) reifies exactly the occupant whose emptiness the case might show.

## Critical Issues

### Issue 1: Dissociation-to-irreducibility non-sequitur (the article's own discipline violated)
- **File**: `obsidian/concepts/somatic-interface.md`
- **Location**: line 52 (interoception / vmPFC) and line 66–68 (Christina / proprioception)
- **Problem**: The article is mostly scrupulous about marking the materialist account as complete and the interface reading as a *metaphysical re-description*. But two passages quietly upgrade a *dissociation* into evidence for *irreducibility* without the bridging argument. Line 52: vmPFC patients "make catastrophically poor decisions despite intact logical reasoning—suggesting the body's felt signals carry information that abstract cognition cannot replicate." The dissociation shows the *somatic-marker pathway* carries decision-relevant information; it does not show that information "cannot be replicated" by other (non-felt, fully physical) computation — Damasio's own framework is materialist and would deny that gloss. Line 68: Christina's case is read as proprioception contributing "the background sense of physical selfhood... Without it, consciousness remains but loses its anchorage." Fine as phenomenology, but the surrounding "reveals what proprioception contributes to the somatic interface" framing slides toward treating the dissociation as evidence for the interface ontology rather than for modular neural architecture — which the article *elsewhere* (line 98) correctly attributes to "distinct neural pathways."
- **Severity**: Medium
- **Recommendation**: A light refine. At line 52 soften "information that abstract cognition cannot replicate" to "information that *deliberative* cognition does not, in these patients, recover" (claim about the patients, not an in-principle irreducibility). At line 68 add the half-sentence parity already used elsewhere ("materialism reads this as modular neural architecture; the interface reading redescribes the same modularity as channel structure"). This brings the two early channel subsections up to the citation-grade parity discipline the rest of the article maintains.

### Issue 2: "Adds a further claim" is the contested premise, not an inference from the data
- **File**: `obsidian/concepts/somatic-interface.md`
- **Location**: lines 54, 76, 92, 100, 112
- **Problem**: The recurring rhetorical shape is: state the complete materialist account → "The interface reading adds a further claim" that the channel *structures phenomenal character* and that character "is not fully explained by the neural pathway." The reader can mistake "adds" for "the data warrant adding." They do not — by the article's own parity admission. The added claim is just the hard-problem premise (phenomenal character has a non-physical contributor), reasserted at the somatic level. This is the *interpretive-equivocation* risk from `evidential-status-discipline`: the clinical evidence (epistemic/empirical) is recruited to underwrite a metaphysical conclusion that is logically independent of it.
- **Severity**: Medium (it is consistently *labelled* as metaphysical, which mitigates; the issue is the cumulative "adds" framing inviting the reader to treat the addition as evidence-driven).
- **Recommendation**: Once, near the top (e.g. after line 42), state plainly that the somatic data are *neutral* between the two readings and that the interface reading's appeal is theoretical economy *given* the hard problem assumed elsewhere — not independent confirmation drawn from the body. This is a single-sentence honesty upgrade, not a restructure. It would also pre-empt the Dennett/Popper critiques above.

### Issue 3: Self-cancelling Minimal-Quantum-Interaction paragraph
- **File**: `obsidian/concepts/somatic-interface.md`
- **Location**: line 116
- **Problem**: "the somatic interface may be where this biasing is most phenomenologically visible" is asserted and then fully retracted in the same breath ("equally consistent with undiscovered neural mechanisms... speculative... framework prediction, not a clinical one"). The net informational content is nil, and "most phenomenologically visible" is an unearned superlative — no comparison to other candidate loci (e.g. attention, perceptual binding) is offered to justify "most."
- **Severity**: Low
- **Recommendation**: Either drop the superlative ("a candidate locus where..." rather than "most... visible"), or cut the speculative first clause and keep only the honest framework-prediction statement. Do not let an empty over-claim sit under the MQI tenet, the Map's most fragile commitment.

## Counterarguments to Address

### "Interface reading is explanatorily idle"
- **Current content says**: both readings explain the data equally well; the interface reading "adds" a metaphysical claim and provides a "framework where the explanatory gap... is a structural feature."
- **A critic would argue**: a re-description that makes no novel prediction and absorbs the explanandum into its axioms is idle — it neither explains nor predicts; it relabels.
- **Suggested response**: the article already has the materials for the honest reply (the value is *theoretical*, not predictive: it dissolves the hard problem's status as an anomaly *given* dualism). State that the payoff is coherence-with-tenets, not empirical adjudication — and own that this is a tie the somatic data cannot break. Do not let "adds a claim" masquerade as "the body shows."

### "Christina cuts against the inhabitant"
- **Current content says**: proprioceptive loss leaves consciousness intact but unanchored — consciousness is the inhabitant, the body the medium.
- **A critic would argue (Nagarjuna/deflationist)**: the felt self degraded as the channel failed, which is what you'd expect if selfhood is *constituted by* the channels, not an inhabitant that survives their loss.
- **Suggested response**: acknowledge the case is dialectically two-edged; the interface reading's claim is that *some* phenomenal subject persisted (Christina reported the loss), not that selfhood is channel-independent.
