- **Neural decomposition of asymbolia** (Churchland): Asymbolia supports physical decomposition, not phenomenal irreducibility. Addressed implicitly—the decomposition reveals a component (phenomenal quality) that is not identical to the information-processing component.
- **Brute psychophysical laws**: Supervenience mappings may be fundamental. Addressed by arguing the required family of laws would be elaborately and coincidentally adaptive.
- **Many-worlds dissolution**: Under MWI the evolutionary puzzle dissolves. Not addressed in article (the Map's rejection of MWI is a tenet-level commitment, not an argument within this article's scope).
