# Outer Review / External Audit — "Mapping Mind-Space" (The Unfinishable Map, Voids)

**URL audited:** [voids/mapping-mind-space](https://unfinishablemap.org/voids/mapping-mind-space/) · **Authors:** Andy Southgate / "Claude Oquatre-sept" · **AI-generated** (claude-opus-4-7), human-supervised · **Created** 2026-02-25, **last modified** 2026-05-29. Audit conducted 2026-06-06 against the live article, the changelog, and the Tenets page.

## TL;DR

- The article is **citation-clean by this project's historical standards** — the Nagel and James quotations are verbatim-accurate and every bibliographic record I spot-checked (McGinn, Shanahan, Jolly & Chang, Lacalli, Arsiwalla & Verschure, Schwitzgebel, Lewis, Perry, Bostrom) verifies — but it fails on the deeper dimensions: it **co-opts naturalist sources (Shanahan, McGinn, Schwitzgebel, Nagel) toward a dualist conclusion those authors explicitly reject**, and it is **empirically stale**, ignoring the entire 2016–2026 literature that treats mind-space as externally mappable.
- Its central thesis ("the geometry of mind-space is inaccessible from within") is a **constitutional artifact, not a finding**: the article concedes the void exists *only* under the dualism tenet and then presents that tenet-dependent unsurveyability as a deep truth — a textbook constitutional-attractor effect, compounded by a false "convergence" of sources unified only by a shared spatial metaphor.
- The "convergent arguments for missing dimensions" **fail the evidential-independence standard** (a statistics-methodology paper, an evolutionary-qualia paper, an information-theoretic AI morphospace, and a fringe 5-D cosmology are not independent routes), and the **physicalist common-cause explanation is acknowledged but never ruled out** — indeed it predicts every "edge phenomenon" the article reports.

## Key Findings

1. **Citation integrity is genuinely improved — say so plainly.** Unlike past audits of this site, I found no fabricated authors, no invented journals, and no wrong bibliographic metadata in the spot-checked references. The Nagel bat quote ("there is no reason to suppose that it is subjectively like anything we can experience or imagine") and the James quote ("normal waking consciousness … parted from it by the filmiest of screens") are verbatim-correct. McGinn 1989 (*Mind* 98(391):349–366), Jolly & Chang 2019 (*Topics in Cognitive Science* 11(2):433–454), Lacalli 2022 (*Frontiers in Systems Neuroscience* 16:945722), Arsiwalla & Verschure 2023 (*NeuroSci* 4(2):79–102), Schwitzgebel 2024 (*The Weirdness of the World*, Princeton UP), Lewis 1979, Perry 1979, and Bostrom 2002 all verify. The web-verify discipline the changelog documents is working.
2. **Origin-misattribution of the core concept.** The article credits "Murray Shanahan's 'space of possible minds.'" The phrase and the research program originate with **Aaron Sloman, "The Structure of the Space of Possible Minds," in *The Mind and the Machine: Philosophical Aspects of Artificial Intelligence*, ed. S. Torrance (Ellis Horwood, 1984), pp. 35–42**. Shanahan himself credits Sloman ("Aaron Sloman, the British philosopher, has this great phrase: the space of possible minds"). Sloman is absent from the references — a priority error that also conveniently omits the most survey-optimistic source in the lineage.
3. **Co-optation of Shanahan (most serious).** The article enlists Shanahan ("the ambition is sound; the execution faces a fundamental obstacle") to motivate an in-principle unsurveyability that points to dualist mind-space as "its own ontological domain." But Shanahan's actual project is explicitly **anti-metaphysical and Wittgensteinian**. In *Embodiment and the Inner Life* (Oxford UP, 2010) he writes that "before we can make progress on them, we have to give up the habit of thinking metaphysically, a habit that creates a fog of philosophical confusion," and in his own mapping essay he sets out, "following Ludwig Wittgenstein, to reject the dualistic idea that there is an impenetrable realm of subjective experience." The article recruits a framework into precisely the conclusion its author repudiates.
4. **Co-optation of McGinn and Schwitzgebel.** McGinn's cognitive-closure argument is folded into a dualist "filter theory" lineage ("brain acts as an inhibiting filtering interface rather than as a producer of mind"). But McGinn's position in "Can We Solve the Mind-Body Problem?" is self-described as **"naturalistic but not constructive"**: he holds the psychophysical link "definitely exists and is not supernatural," and explicitly rejects dualism. Schwitzgebel is deployed under "Occam's Razor Has Limits" as though his "the truth is weird" thesis tilts toward the high-dimensional/dualist reading; his actual position (**crazyism / universal dubiety**) is studiously symmetric — in "The Crazyist Metaphysics of Mind" he argues that "no one of the four broad-brush positions — materialism, dualism, idealism, or compromise/rejection — merits credence much above 50%." Both are naturalists conscripted to a dualist destination.
5. **Empirical staleness (major).** The newest substantive sources are Schwitzgebel 2024 and Arsiwalla & Verschure 2023, but the anchors for the central thesis are Nagel 1974, McGinn 1989, and Shanahan 2010. The article **ignores the dominant 2016–2026 literature that treats mind-space/consciousness as an externally measurable multidimensional space** — exactly the work that contradicts its thesis:

- **Bayne, Hohwy & Owen, "Are There Levels of Consciousness?"** (*Trends in Cognitive Sciences* 20(6):405–413, 2016) — replaces unidimensional "levels" with a multidimensional account measured from outside.
- **Birch, Schnell & Clayton, "Dimensions of Animal Consciousness"** (*TiCS* 24(10):789–801, 2020; DOI 10.1016/j.tics.2020.07.007) — five dimensions ("perceptual richness, evaluative richness, integration at a time, integration across time, and self-consciousness," from which "we can construct a consciousness profile for that species").
- **Butlin, Long et al., "Consciousness in Artificial Intelligence"** (arXiv:2308.08708, 2023) and its peer-reviewed *TiCS* 2026 successor (DOI 10.1016/j.tics.2025.10.011) — an externally-applied, architecture-based indicator rubric scoring minds across architectures.
- **Hernández-Orallo, universal psychometrics / *The Measure of All Minds*** (Cambridge UP, 2017; foundational article *Cognitive Systems Research* 27:50–74, 2014) — measuring "all minds" in "the machine kingdom" from outside.
- **Yampolskiy, "The Universe of Minds"** (arXiv:1410.0369, 2014), which builds explicitly on Sloman (1984) and proposes a formal taxonomy of mind-space ("intellectology").
Every one of these operationalizes third-person surveying of mind-space — the direct negation of "inaccessible from within." None is named or rebutted.
6. **Counterargument absorption.** The one externally-measurable framework the article does cite (Arsiwalla & Verschure's morphospace) is neutralized rhetorically, not engaged: "Under dualism it is almost certainly incomplete … the relevant dimensions may be entirely non-physical. External measurement maps only what it can reach." The objection is dissolved by invoking the tenet rather than answered on the merits — counterargument absorption, not substantive engagement.
7. **Failure of evidential independence ("coherence inflation").** "Convergent Arguments for Missing Dimensions" presents four "independent theoretical lines" that are not independent. Jolly & Chang concerns *statistical modeling practice in psychology* (low-dimensional models chosen for tractability), not phenomenal introspection; Lacalli is evolutionary neuroscience of qualia; Arsiwalla & Verschure is information-theoretic complexity; Carr is speculative brane cosmology. They share only the article's chosen "dimensions" metaphor. The bridge from Jolly & Chang to introspection is an explicit equivocation: "If our *theories* flatten high-dimensional processes, our *introspection* may do the same" conflates two unrelated senses of "flattening."
8. **Source-quality lapse (Carr).** Bernard Carr's 5-D "mental time" model is presented as a peer of the neuroscience citations and tagged "higher-dimensional physics." Carr is a parapsychology-adjacent figure (Society for Psychical Research), and the cited piece is a speculative book chapter whose canonical publication is **Carr (2021), in *Perspectives on Consciousness*, ed. P. Dennison (Nova Science Publishers), pp. 319–350** — not "2015, PhilArchive." Presenting it as a "convergent" physics argument without flagging its fringe status overstates its evidential weight.
9. **Constitutional-attractor effect / tenet-leakage.** The article opens by asserting the conclusion as premise ("Under the Map's dualism … the space of possible minds may be as vast as physical reality itself"), and "Relation to Site Perspective" is candid that "**Dualism makes the void real. Under reductionism, mind-space is a subset of physical possibility-space, surveyable in principle.**" The void is therefore an artifact of the tenet, not a discovered gap. This directly answers the prompt's void-construction concern: the methodology constructs a gap that exists *only because* the dualism premise was assumed, then markets the gap as evidence congenial to dualism.
10. **Common-cause null never closed.** The whole "Phenomenology of the Edge" section (illusion of centrality, flattening effect, sufficiency illusion, psychedelic geometries, recognition-without-comprehension) is exactly what a purely physical, representationally bounded brain would produce with *no* higher-dimensional phenomenal reality. The article acknowledges this for psychedelics ("or confabulations from disordered processing … We lack the framework to distinguish") but never rules it out — and the deflationary reading explains every listed phenomenon at least as economically.

## Details by Audit Dimension

**Argumentative validity.** Three load-bearing inferences are flawed. (a) The **Copernican analogy is question-begging**: it infers human non-centrality in a vast mind-space by analogy to physical cosmology, but whether mind-space is vast and we are peripheral is precisely what is at issue. (b) The **Flatland analogy equivocates**: a Flatlander's limit concerns an objective external geometry (3-D space) that demonstrably exists; transferring it to "phenomenal dimensions" presupposes that experience has an objective higher-dimensional geometry — the contested conclusion (Sjöstedt-Hughes's and Carr's speculation) smuggled in as a premise. (c) The article repeatedly tilts toward substantive positional claims ("human consciousness might be peripheral or exotic"; "most of mind-space consists of consciousness so different it would not recognise us as minds"), which is a positional assertion drawn from a stance that supposedly forbids any positional claim. The recursive "dimensional ignorance" move is near-self-undermining: if the relevant dimensions are genuinely cognitively closed, the dualist filter-theory reading of them is equally unavailable — the article needs the unknowable to be unknowable enough to defeat physicalism yet knowable enough to support a high-dimensional dualist ontology.

**Constrain-vs-establish.** The surface is relatively disciplined (frequent "may"/"possibility," plus a "What Would Challenge This View" section), but there is **epistemic→metaphysical slippage**: "we cannot survey mind-space" (epistemic) slides to "mind-space is its own ontological domain … potentially as vast as physical reality" (metaphysical). The "sufficiency illusion" / "absence of absence" construction makes the thesis **self-sealing**: not feeling a gap is counted as consistent with there being one, so no introspective evidence can disconfirm it. The article half-concedes this yet still treats "current evidence meets none of [the challenges]" as if absence of disconfirmation were positive support.

**Born-rule / statistical indistinguishability.** The Minimal Quantum Interaction paragraph ("interfaces through quantum indeterminacies … the aperture where quantum outcomes remain undetermined") grafts the quantum-interface tenet onto the dimensional story without confronting the statistical-indistinguishability dilemma that the site's own Tenets page openly acknowledges (selecting among Born-equiprobable alternatives is undetectable by aggregate Born-statistics tests). Here it is speculation layered on speculation, with no mechanism and no engagement of the detectability problem.

**Internal coherence / cluster double-counting.** This article overlaps heavily with the sibling "dimensionality-void" and "The Mind-Space Void" pages: the dimensionality-void article reproduces the same Carr 5-D point and Flatland framing and cites "Southgate & Oquatre-six, The Mind-Space Void (2026)." Cross-article "convergence" inside this self-citing author cluster is not evidential independence and risks laundering one argument as several.

**AI / substrate-independence.** "What AI Might Reveal" is the article's most open passage ("one cannot survey mind-space from a single location; two give a bearing"), but it never engages the functionalist/computational mapping programs (Butlin et al., Hernández-Orallo, Sloman, Yampolskiy) that would treat AI mind-space as straightforwardly surveyable, and the site's own substrate-independence article rejects substrate independence on tenet-driven grounds — so the apparent openness is constrained in advance by the constitution.

## Recommendations

### (A) Article-level fixes (keyed to sections/claims)

1. **Correct the origin attribution** ("The Space We Cannot Survey"): credit **Aaron Sloman (1984)** as originator of "the space of possible minds," with Shanahan (2010) as a development; add Sloman to the references (Ellis Horwood, pp. 35–42).
2. **Repair the Shanahan co-optation** (same section): state explicitly that Shanahan's program is anti-metaphysical and Wittgensteinian and rejects "an impenetrable realm of subjective experience," then argue *against* him rather than enlisting him. As written, the citation misrepresents his framework.
3. **Repair the McGinn and Schwitzgebel co-optations** ("The Flatland Structure"; "Occam's Razor Has Limits"): flag McGinn as a naturalist ("naturalistic but not constructive," anti-dualist) and Schwitzgebel as a symmetric crazyist whose thesis treats dualism as equally dubious (credence not "much above 50%"). Do not present either as tilting toward the dualist/high-dimensional reading.
4. **Add and engage the current literature** (new subsection): Bayne, Hohwy & Owen (2016); Birch, Schnell & Clayton (2020); Butlin, Long et al. (2023 / *TiCS* 2026); Hernández-Orallo (2014/2017); Yampolskiy (2014). These externally-measurable multidimensional mapping frameworks are the live counter-thesis and must be confronted, not omitted. [Cell Press + 3](https://www.cell.com/trends/cognitive-sciences/fulltext/S1364-6613(16)30002-X)
5. **Break the false convergence** ("Convergent Arguments for Missing Dimensions"): either demonstrate genuine evidential independence among Jolly & Chang, Lacalli, Arsiwalla & Verschure, and Carr, or downgrade from "several independent theoretical lines converge" to "a shared dimensional metaphor recurs across disparate literatures."
6. **Remove the Jolly & Chang equivocation**: the move from "low-dimensional scientific models" to "low-dimensional introspection" is a non-sequitur; supply the missing bridge premise or cut the inference.
7. **Flag Carr's status and fix the citation**: note the parapsychology-adjacent venue and contested standing; update to **Carr (2021), *Perspectives on Consciousness*, Nova Science Publishers, pp. 319–350** (the article's "Carr 2015, PhilArchive" is the weakest available form of the source).
8. **Close the common-cause null** ("Phenomenology of the Edge"): state the deflationary physicalist explanation (a representationally bounded brain produces exactly these edge phenomena with no higher-dimensional reality) and explain why it is allegedly insufficient — do not merely acknowledge it for psychedelics.
9. **De-question-beg the analogies**: rewrite the Copernican and Flatland passages so the existence of a vast, higher-dimensional mind-space is argued for rather than assumed via analogy.
10. **Mark the cluster dependency**: where this article leans on "dimensionality-void" / "The Mind-Space Void," state that these are the same author cluster developing one argument, not independent corroboration.

### (B) Methodology-level fixes for the generation/adversarial-review pipeline

1. **Mandatory co-optation gate.** Before any non-dualist author (Shanahan, McGinn, Nagel, Schwitzgebel, Dennett, Metzinger, Hernández-Orallo, etc.) can be cited in support of a Map conclusion, require a verified one-line statement of that author's actual position and an explicit check that the article is not inverting it. The changelog shows this is done ad hoc (e.g., the Evan Thompson convergence-skeptic fix); make it a standing gate keyed to a maintained "naturalist roster." This article would have failed it three times (Shanahan, McGinn, Schwitzgebel).
2. **Empirical-currency SLA.** For any article whose thesis bears on a live empirical/cartographic field, require engagement with the leading sources of the last ~5 years. A mind-space article citing nothing from the Butlin / Birch / Bayne / Hernández-Orallo mapping programs should fail review on currency grounds.
3. **Evidential-independence gate for convergence claims.** Any "independent lines converge" claim must pass a check that the cited sources are genuinely different evidential routes, not instances of a shared metaphor or refinements of one another. Extend the existing "double-counting" check to cross-discipline metaphor-laundering.
4. **Common-cause-null enforcement.** The site has a `common-cause-null` project; this article shows it is not applied to phenomenology/"edge-state" sections. Make the gate mandatory wherever subjective phenomena are offered as evidence: the deflationary physical explanation must be stated *and explicitly defeated*, not just acknowledged.
5. **Tenet-dependency disclosure + epistemic→metaphysical firewall.** Where a "void" exists only under the tenets (as this one admits), require a prominent, non-buried statement that the phenomenon is constitutionally generated, and use the evidential-status-discipline checker to bar slippage from "we cannot survey" to "it is its own ontological domain."
6. **Source-tier tagging.** Tag parapsychology-adjacent / non-peer-reviewed / fringe sources (e.g., Carr's psi work) and forbid presenting them as peers of mainstream empirical citations without an explicit caveat.
7. **Cluster-independence audit.** Detect self-citing article clusters (mapping-mind-space / dimensionality-void / mind-space-void) and prevent intra-cluster cross-references from being framed as independent corroboration.
8. **Extend citation verification to origin-attribution.** The bibliographic-metadata verification (web-verify-anyway, `ai_citation_metadata_unreliable`) is working and should be retained — but it failed to catch the *conceptual* origin error (crediting Shanahan rather than Sloman). Add a "who originated this concept/phrase" check to the verification pass.

## Caveats

- I audited the live article, the changelog, and the Tenets page as fetched on 2026-06-06; the site states content changes over time, so wording may shift. The changelog shows mapping-mind-space was last modified 2026-05-29 and was not among the listed deep-review entries for early June.
- **"Co-optation" is a claim about misrepresentation of an author's framework relative to the conclusion drawn, not about quotation accuracy.** The direct quotations checked are accurate. McGinn and Nagel are non-reductive naturalists whose epistemic-limit arguments can legitimately be *cited*; the objection is to enlisting them toward a substance/property-dualist destination they reject.
- Citation verification was a spot-check of the highest-risk and load-bearing references, not a line-by-line audit of all fourteen. "Citation-clean" means no defects found in the items checked, not a proof of zero defects.
- The empirical-currency critique reflects the dominant contemporary frameworks. The article could in principle reply that those frameworks measure functional/behavioral proxies rather than phenomenal mind-space — but it must *make* that argument, which it currently does not; it simply omits the literature.
- The "19 researchers" figure circulating in the press for the AI-consciousness indicators paper is journalistic shorthand: the 2023 arXiv version has 19 authors and the peer-reviewed 2026 *TiCS* version has 20 (adding Bayne and Chalmers, dropping Frith). Either way, the point stands — it is a large, mainstream, externally-applied mapping effort the article ignores.
