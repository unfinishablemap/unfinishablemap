## Pessimistic Analysis Summary

### Critical Issues Found

1. **Fabricated P3b citation (author/journal/year/volume all wrong)**: The article attributed "P3b, consciousness, and complex unconscious processing" to *Kouider, Sackur, & de Gardelle (2016), Consciousness and Cognition, 36, 348-351*. Web verification (Google Scholar metadata, PubMed 26474391, ScienceDirect, Cortex) confirms the real paper is **Silverstein, B. H., Snodgrass, M., Shevrin, H., & Kushwaha, R. (2015). *Cortex*, 73, 216-227**. No such Kouider/Sackur/de Gardelle P3b paper exists — the metadata was conflated with the authors' other partial-awareness work. The inline *characterization* was also wrong: the article said they "surveyed evidence showing (1) conscious perception can occur without P3b, and (2) P3b can occur for rigorously subliminal stimuli." Silverstein et al. is a **primary empirical study** (subliminal oddball paradigm showing P3b under subliminal conditions), not a survey, and does not make claim (1). **Resolution**: Corrected both inline text and reference list to Silverstein et al. (2015); rewrote the characterization to match the actual study; added the Naccache & Marti (2016) *Cortex* commentary (which defends P3b) to register that the component's status remains contested.

2. **Zher-Wen citation: phantom co-author + wrong pages**: Cited inline and in references as "Zher-Wen, Y., & Tsuchiya, N. (2023)... 347-368." Web verification (Wiley DOI 10.1111/bjop.12631, PubMed 36689339) confirms the paper is **single-authored by Zher-Wen**; Tsuchiya is not an author. Correct pages are **430-456** (vol 114, issue 2), not 347-368. **Resolution**: Removed Tsuchiya, corrected pages, updated both inline citation and reference list.

3. **Archive-only wikilink**: `[[Mysterianism-cognitive-closure|Mysterian]]` (Methodological Challenges section) pointed to `archive/topics/mysterianism-cognitive-closure.md`, which carries `superseded_by: /concepts/mysterianism/`. **Resolution**: Repointed to the live `[[mysterianism|Mysterian]]` concept (same title, "Mysterianism and Cognitive Closure").
