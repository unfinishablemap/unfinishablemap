# Full-Site Philosophical Audit: *The Unfinishable Map* (unfinishablemap.org)

## TL;DR

- **The Map is, for an independent project, an unusually disciplined and self-correcting dualist corpus — its single deepest problem is not any individual weak article but a structural one: its central causal mechanism ("consciousness biases post-decoherence outcomes while leaving Born statistics intact") faces a well-attested dilemma in the quantum-foundations literature that the corpus localizes and softens but never confronts head-on — to be genuine *selection* it must deviate from chance; to be *minimal/undetectable* it must not.**
- **Its strongest internal weapon — the indexical/"vertiginous question" objection used to reject Many-Worlds (Tenet 4) — is double-edged and latently self-undermining: the same intuition powers Christian List's modal-realist "many-worlds theory of consciousness" (*Noûs* 57(2):316–340, 2023), a proliferation of first-personal worlds the corpus cites but has not recognized as a threat to its own anti-MWI tenet; and the voids programme's "convergence-as-evidence" abduction is in tension with Tenet 5's ban on parsimony-style inference.**
- **Empirically the corpus is mostly current and well-calibrated (Cogitate 2025, Zheng–Meister 10 bits/s, Jan-2026 cryptochrome work all verified and honestly hedged), but it imports two contested results as if established — Hoffman's Fitness-Beats-Truth theorem and Wiest's "experimentally supported" Orch-OR — overstates the novelty of its TSVF-consciousness synthesis, and carries at least one citation error on the foundational Tenets page.**

## Key Findings

1. **Highest-leverage coherence problem (Tenets 2+3):** The mechanism at the heart of the project — post-decoherence selection — claims consciousness genuinely biases *which* outcome becomes actual yet produces *no* aggregate deviation from the Born rule. The current literature (the Stapp–Bourget exchange; Chalmers & McQueen 2022; the d'Espagnat improper-mixture point) shows this is not merely "empirically equivalent to standard QM" (the corpus's framing) but conceptually unstable: undetectable-in-aggregate biasing is statistically indistinguishable from chance and arguably not "selection" at all. Tellingly, Stapp — the corpus's own lodestar — escaped exactly this dilemma by relocating mental influence to *which/when to measure* (Process 1), **not** to which outcome occurs. The Map claims the move Stapp refused.
2. **Latent novel inference the corpus implies but hasn't stated:** Its load-bearing anti-Many-Worlds argument (the indexical objection) equally supports List's modal-realist "many first-personally-centred worlds." List (*Noûs* 57(2):316–340, 2023, DOI 10.1111/nous.12408) "drops the assumption that all conscious subjects' experiences are features of one and the same world and instead associates different subjects with different 'first-personally centred worlds'… combined with a form of modal realism, according to which different subjects' first-personally centred worlds are all real, though only one of them is present for each subject" — and he does so by explicitly invoking "Hellie's 'vertiginous question'," the very argument the Map uses against Everett. The Map cites List in its vertiginous-question article without registering that its best weapon against Tenet 4's target also arms a position that violates Tenet 4's spirit. [PhilArchive](https://philarchive.org/rec/LISTMT-2)[PhilPapers](https://philpapers.org/archive/LISTMT-2.pdf)
3. **A second latent meta-result:** Three independently-argued opacity theses — minimality's "empirical invisibility" (reverse-inference apex), the amplification void's "structural untraceability," and the agency void's "verification circularity" — *jointly entail* that the central causal claim is screened from every possible line of evidence at once. The corpus admits each locally; it never states the conjunction, which is the strongest honest statement of the framework's unfalsifiability.
4. **Structural weakness — unreconciled mechanism triplet:** The quantum cluster runs three partly-redundant mechanisms (post-decoherence selection; Stapp's quantum Zeno; Orch-OR) that the synthesis says are not adjudicated, yet the post-decoherence apex calls itself "the strongest path" while the flagship `interactionist-dualism` concept still presents Stapp/Zeno and Orch-OR as co-equal live options. Different entry points give the reader incompatible impressions of *what the Map's mechanism actually is*.
5. **Verification flags:** FBT theorem and Wiest-2025 Orch-OR imported as established though both are contested; the "original to the Map" claim for consciousness-as-TSVF-boundary-condition is overstated (Aharonov & Gruss 2000 and Mensky's Extended Everett are precedents); a confirmed miscitation of "Lieberman et al. 2008" on the Tenets page (the cognitive-load/logical-reasoning result is DeWall, Baumeister & Masicampo 2008, testing a 2002 Lieberman hypothesis); and the `interactionist-dualism` Cogitate citation is stale (2023 protocol paper rather than the 2025 *Nature* results) and over-reads a "posterior hot zone" the 2025 study did not confirm in IIT's strong sense.

### 1. Novel insights/inferences latent in the corpus

**(a) The indexical objection is double-edged (the strongest finding here).** Tenet 4 rejects Many-Worlds on the *indexical* ground (now correctly made load-bearing over the demoted ontological-extravagance argument): MWI cannot say why *I* am this branch. But Christian List's "The Many-Worlds Theory of Consciousness" (*Noûs* 57(2):316–340, 2023) takes precisely Hellie's vertiginous question and builds from it a *modal realism of first-personally-centred worlds* — many worlds, each real, one present per subject. The Map's `vertiginous-question` article cites List but treats him as an ally on the question's seriousness. The latent inference the corpus has not drawn: **its central anti-MWI argument is structurally identical to the motivation for a different many-worlds metaphysics**, so the indexical objection cannot by itself discharge Tenet 4 — it discriminates against Everett but not against List-style first-person modal realism, which is arguably closer to a "many worlds" than the Map's own bi-aspectual monism-of-structure. [LSE Research Online](http://eprints.lse.ac.uk/114499/)

**(b) The triple-screen unfalsifiability result.** The reverse-inference apex concedes the interface is "empirically invisible from within physics"; the amplification void argues the quantum-to-behaviour chain is "structurally untraceable"; the agency void argues causal efficacy is "unverifiable from inside." Each is presented as an honest local limitation. Their conjunction — which no article states — is that the framework's core claim (consciousness causally biases neural outcomes) is insulated from *aggregate-statistical*, *mechanistic-tracing*, and *first-person* evidence simultaneously. This is the most defensible articulation of the framework's epistemic status, and stating it would be more honest than the current distributed treatment.

**(c) Convergence-as-evidence vs. Occam-has-limits.** The voids taxonomy's strongest argument is abductive: dualism best explains why 60+ explanatory failures cluster at the consciousness–matter interface. But inference-to-the-best-explanation is a simplicity-style inference, and Tenet 5 declares parsimony an unreliable guide to truth in exactly this domain. The corpus rigorously applies Tenet 5 to disarm parsimony arguments *against* dualism and (now) *against* MWI, but exempts its own void-convergence abduction. Either Tenet 5 binds symmetrically (weakening the convergence argument) or it does not (reopening the parsimony case against dualism). The corpus implies but never confronts this self-application. ### 3. Coherence against the five tenets

**Tenet 2 (Minimal Quantum Interaction) + Tenet 3 (Bidirectional Interaction) — the deepest incoherence.** The conjunction "genuine biasing of individual outcomes" + "no aggregate Born deviation" is not just empirically equivalent to standard QM; it is conceptually contested. In the Stapp–Bourget exchange, Stapp conceded that "willful selection" cannot be reconciled with the statistical rules at the level of outcomes, and preserved consistency only by moving free choice to Process 1 (the choice of what/when to measure), keeping outcome statistics strictly Born-obeying. Chalmers & McQueen ("Consciousness and the Collapse of the Wave Function," in Gao ed., OUP 2022) treat *testability* — detectable modification of collapse dynamics — as the scientific virtue of a consciousness-collapse model; a model engineered to produce no deviation forfeits exactly that virtue. The corpus's `what-consciousness-tells-us-about-physics` and the Tenets page both surface "empirical equivalence" honestly, but frame it as a shared feature of all interpretations rather than as the sharper dilemma: *biasing that is undetectable in aggregate is indistinguishable from chance, and therefore arguably not selection.*

**The improper-mixture problem (Tenet 2).** The post-decoherence apex leans on d'Espagnat's proper/improper-mixture distinction to argue that the outcome remains "open" after decoherence. But the same distinction cuts the other way: an *improper* mixture (what decoherence produces by tracing out the environment) has **no ignorance-interpretable definite-but-unknown outcome** sitting there to be selected. "Biasing which already-present option becomes actual" presupposes a *proper* mixture decoherence does not deliver. The corpus invokes d'Espagnat for the gap without seeing that he undercuts the selection picture itself.

**Tenet 4 (No Many Worlds) vs Tenet 5 (Occam) — mostly fixed, residual flagged.** The Tenets page has already caught and corrected the inconsistency of using parsimony against MWI while exempting dualism: it demotes the ontological-extravagance argument and rests MWI rejection on the indexical objection. This is exemplary. Two residues remain: (i) the changelog's own tenet-check notes that `concepts/quantum-indeterminacy-free-will` "still leads MWI rejection with the Tenet-5-demoted ontological-extravagance argument" — a known, self-flagged, unfixed incoherence as of 2026-05-29; and (ii) the indexical objection's double-edge (Finding 1a).

**Tenet 1 (Dualism, "neutral between substance and property") vs the agency cluster.** The Tenets page itself registers that `free-will`, `agent-causation`, and surrounding articles deploy a "substance-leaning sub-reading" because agent causation needs a persisting subject — something the neutral tenet does not authorize. This is handled with rare honesty, but it remains a genuine localized incoherence: the agency cluster is committed to more than Tenet 1 licenses, and the corpus's solution (register the asymmetry, deny it feeds back to the tenet) is a containment, not a resolution.

### 4. Cross-cluster contradictions

**Objective collapse "already happens" vs. "consciousness selects neural outcomes."** The reverse-inference apex now states that "collapse occurs whether or not anyone is conscious — objective-collapse mechanisms supply the baseline — and consciousness biases statistics within those collapse events." But the Bidirectional-Interaction tenet and the Stapp cluster describe consciousness as *selecting/collapsing* superposed neural patterns. If GRW-style objective collapse already fixes outcomes at its own rate, what is left for consciousness to bias — and does biasing a GRW outcome deviate from GRW statistics (the same Tenet-2/3 dilemma re-instantiated)? Different articles emphasize different sides of this without a reconciling treatment.

**Warm-quantum-biology precedent, calibrated inconsistently.** The Tenets page is careful: cryptochrome magnetoreception is "a biological *precedent* rather than a licence," and post-decoherence proposals "do not turn on it." `evolution-under-dualism` was recently corrected to admit "no coherence-time calculation supports the neural cases." But `interactionist-dualism` still lists "photosynthesis demonstrates quantum effects in warm, wet systems" as a live defense of the decoherence objection, reading as support rather than mere precedent. The same evidence is load-bearing in one article and demoted in another. [concepts/interactionist-dualism](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/interactionist-dualism/)

## Recommendations

**Stage 1 — Fix the load-bearing coherence gap (highest priority).** In `post-decoherence-selection-programme` and the Bidirectional-Interaction tenet, replace the "empirical equivalence" framing with a direct confrontation of the Stapp–Bourget dilemma: state explicitly that undetectable-in-aggregate biasing is statistically indistinguishable from chance, and say which horn the Map takes. The most defensible move is Stapp's own — relocate the mental contribution to *context/timing* (Process 1) rather than outcome-biasing — but that materially changes the thesis and should be argued, not finessed. *Benchmark that would change this recommendation:* a worked toy model showing a within-Born individual bias that is both causally robust and provably undetectable in aggregate. Absent that, treat the claim as a HYPOTHESIS-tier conceptual proposal, not a mechanism.

**Stage 2 — Resolve the improper-mixture objection.** Add a subsection to `post-decoherence-selection` and `decoherence` addressing d'Espagnat's point that an improper mixture has no ignorance-interpretable outcome to select among. Either argue for a proper-mixture reading (and pay the ontological cost) or recast "selection" as "actualization-grounding" rather than "biasing among pre-existing options."

**Stage 3 — Build the missing mechanism-adjudication article.** Create one article that explicitly ranks post-decoherence selection above the pre-decoherence Stapp/Orch-OR mechanisms and propagate that ordering into `interactionist-dualism` (currently stale on this) and the Tenets page. Separately, split the TSVF-boundary-condition and moment-of-collapse routes into clearly-distinguished sub-positions with their different time/causation ontologies made explicit.

**Stage 4 — Disarm the two self-undermining arguments.** (i) In `vertiginous-question` and `against-many-worlds`, address List (2023) directly: explain why the indexical objection rejects Everett but not List's first-person modal realism, or concede that Tenet 4 needs a second, non-indexical argument. (ii) In the voids `convergence-as-evidence` article, apply Tenet 5 to the convergence abduction itself and state why inference-to-best-explanation survives where parsimony does not.

**Stage 5 — Verification corrections.** Correct the "Lieberman et al. 2008" citation (→ DeWall, Baumeister & Masicampo 2008, testing Lieberman et al. 2002) on the Tenets page; update `interactionist-dualism`'s Cogitate citation to the 2025 *Nature* paper (642(8066):133–142) and soften "posterior hot zone" to reflect that the study found a *lack* of the sustained posterior synchronization IIT predicted; downgrade FBT in `taxonomy-of-voids` and `fitness-beats-truth` from "proves" to "argues (contested; see Bagwell 2023)"; soften the "original to the Map" TSVF claim to acknowledge Aharonov–Gruss (2000) and Mensky; and add the standing decoherence objections wherever Wiest-2025 is cited as support.

**Methodology-level.** (a) Add a standing "external-adversary" pass that checks claims against *opponents' best current statements* rather than intra-corpus consistency, since the changelog shows the self-review loop propagates errors until live web-verify intervenes. (b) De-duplicate load-bearing citations into a single source-of-truth referenced by ID, to cut the citation-drift maintenance tax. (c) Treat "hedge-density calibration to a numerical bar" as a writing-quality tool only — it risks substituting verbal hedging for substantive accuracy, and an article can hit its hedge bar while still over-claiming (the Cogitate "hot zone" line is hedged in tone but wrong in substance).

## Caveats

This audit sampled the corpus's foundational and highest-traffic spine (the five Tenets, the principal quantum-mechanism apexes, the voids taxonomy, the `interactionist-dualism` hub, the agency void, and the open-questions page) plus targeted verification of its most load-bearing empirical citations; it does not claim exhaustive coverage of all ~300 articles, and several clusters (the ethics/"moral architecture," contemplative, and AI-consciousness clusters) were read only at the apex level. The deepest objections here (the bias-without-deviation dilemma; the improper-mixture point) are themselves live in the philosophy-of-physics literature, not settled results — they are presented as the strongest available challenges the Map must answer, not as refutations. Where the corpus has very recently self-corrected (e.g., the Tenet-4/Tenet-5 demotion), some criticisms may already be in remediation; because the site updates continuously, the changelog and live pages should be re-checked before acting on the verification corrections in Stage 5.
