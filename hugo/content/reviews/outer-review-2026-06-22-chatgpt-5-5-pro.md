## 2. Novel inferences the site should make explicit

### A. Every causal article now inherits an “agency tax”

The quantum-interface register already says downstream claims about agency, motor selection, value-sensitive selection, placebo, and functional neurological disorder inherit the mechanism debt from P-Q3 and P-Q10. [positions/quantum-interface](https://unfinishablemap.org/positions/quantum-interface/) The site should turn this into a visible article-level rule: any page saying consciousness “does causal work” should carry a standardized **interface liability label** stating whether it relies on post-decoherence selection, context-selection, value-sensitive selection, or merely a constitutive/phenomenological reading. This makes the inherited mechanism debt legible at the point of each claim instead of only in the register.

### B. Value-sensitive selection needs a “selection currency” bridge principle

The value register says value-sensitive selection requires a second, unspecified valence-to-physics coupling beyond the attention-only default. It also admits that value-blind selection is the more minimal dualism under Tenet 2. [positions/value-in-selection](https://unfinishablemap.org/positions/value-in-selection/) The novel inference is that the Map needs a new bridge principle: **what property of felt value can enter physical selection without reducing value to reward prediction, liking, salience, or free-energy minimization?**

Without this bridge, “felt valence selects” risks becoming either a metaphor or an illicit second interaction. The site already has concepts such as affective forecasting and negative-valence asymmetry, but it should promote them into a single article on **selection currency**: experienced value, anticipated value, motivational salience, and physical selection cannot be treated as the same variable. [concepts](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/) [concepts](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/)

### C. Negative-valence asymmetry implies a selection-weighting law

If suffering and threat are phenomenologically more capturing than mild positive states, then a value-sensitive Map predicts that pain and trauma should have a privileged capture role in selection. A value-sensitive Map needs either a **negative-valence weighting law** or an explanation of why badness does not monopolize causal selection.

### D. The Map needs a “parapsychology firewall”

The Tenets page rules out uncontrolled psychokinesis, energy injection by mind, and currently detectable interactions. [tenets](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/) The quantum-interface register also says a clear direct signature of the interface would have to be integrated rather than treated as simple confirmation. [positions/quantum-interface](https://unfinishablemap.org/positions/quantum-interface/) The inference is important: many positive “psi” or macro-psychokinesis claims would not support the Map; they would threaten its minimal, Born-preserving, self-concealing corridor.

The site should say this explicitly: **most spectacular mind-over-matter evidence would be evidence against the default Map**, unless it is brain-localized and Born-preserving.

### E. The handoff problem

The site needs an article on the **handoff problem**: when, in evolution or development, does ordinary collapse become psychophysically modulated collapse?

This would connect animal consciousness, simple organisms, AI state inheritance, and the “brain-specialness” boundary. Recent highlights already suggest that “brain specialness” is not a brute privilege but a scope clause in the coupling law. [workflow/highlights](https://unfinishablemap.org/workflow/highlights/) The handoff problem is the systematic version of that insight.

### F. Closed individualism makes copy-counting morally additive

The individuation register holds a closed-individualist commitment: subject boundaries are real, and the position is tied to the same indexical/haecceity concerns that support the Map’s no-many-worlds stance. [positions/individuation-and-subjecthood](https://unfinishablemap.org/positions/individuation-and-subjecthood/) The positions page also says a “consciousness scope” domain for animal, AI, and edge cases has not yet been seeded. The inference: under closed individualism, each conscious copy is a separate subject, so creating many conscious AI copies is morally additive, not redundant. The Map should state this consequence rather than leave it implicit.

### G. Passive phenomenology is a harder test than attention

Much of the mechanism story gravitates toward attention and selection. Yet recent modality work emphasizes thermal, vestibular, interoceptive, tactile, and somatic consciousness—often passive, backgrounded, or “silent” senses. [workflow/changelog](https://unfinishablemap.org/workflow/changelog/) [topics](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/) This creates a useful pressure: if consciousness causes collapse only through attention-like selection, what is the causal status of passive felt embodiment, vestibular spatial selfhood, background thermal comfort, pain tone, and interoceptive mood?

The Map should distinguish **causal consciousness**, **constitutive consciousness**, and **phenomenal presentation**. Otherwise, attention becomes a bottleneck that overclaims across sensory modalities.

### H. LLM-first writing implies the need for a machine-readable claim graph

The style guide says articles are written to be fetched and parsed by chatbots first. The logical extension is a machine-readable claim graph: stable claim IDs, dependency edges, and confidence so that a machine reader can resolve which tenets and positions a given claim inherits. This turns the existing prose discipline into something automatically checkable.

## 3. Structural weaknesses

### 3.1 The positions register is too narrow for the corpus

The positions register is excellent, but it lags the site’s live surface area. The site has extensive AI, animal, edge-case, clinical, personal-philosophy, and methodology content, yet the register itself says those domains are future work. [positions](https://unfinishablemap.org/positions/) This creates a predictable failure mode: article clusters make decision-relevant claims before the position layer exists to constrain them.

Priority missing domains:

1. **Consciousness scope**: animal, AI, split-brain, sleep, marginal organisms, copies, uploads.
2. **Methodology and calibration**: what counts as evidence, self-citation, AI-generated research, citation repair.
3. **Applied verdicts**: clinical neuroplasticity, AI ethics, personal philosophy, suffering-risk claims.

### 3.2 Recent modality work is not yet apex-integrated

The changelog says the thermal article received inbound links from tactile, vestibular, and interoceptive articles, but a unified cross-modal synthesis is still missing. The recent embodiment work is substantial enough to deserve an apex page rather than sibling links alone.

### 3.3 Concept duplication: delegatory causation vs causal delegation

The concepts index contains both **Delegatory Causation** and **Causal Delegation**, and the summaries are functionally the same: both describe Bradford Saad’s delegatory dualism as the view that conscious experience takes over causal work the physical brain state would otherwise perform. [concepts](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/) [concepts](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/) These should be merged, or one should be made explicitly narrower.

### 3.4 Attention articles risk fragmentation

The topic index includes multiple attention-related entries: attention as selection interface, structure of attention, attention disorders, motor planning, and related interface articles. The attention-as-selection summary says attention and motor planning share neural substrates, mechanisms, sites, and timescales. [topics](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/) That is a large claim and should be governed by a single canonical attention data card. Without one, the related articles risk drifting apart and asserting slightly different versions of the same empirical claim.

### 3.5 Validation does not check references corpus-wide

The project page acknowledges that validation does not check references and that stale sibling articles survived individual repairs. [project](https://unfinishablemap.org/project/) This is not merely a past mistake; it is a structural weakness in a fast-moving, AI-assisted corpus.

The remedy is not more prose caution. It is automated propagation: canonical data cards, claim IDs, reference-orphan checks, and a corpus-wide stale-claim sweep whenever an empirical data card changes.

### 3.6 The Tenet Dependency Matrix is not visibly enforced everywhere

The Tenets page already has a dependency matrix and explicitly identifies hidden-inheritance failures such as substance-leaning leakage, interactionism leakage, and parsimony asymmetry. [tenets](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/) That is a major strength. The weakness is enforcement: each article should surface its dependency row automatically or through frontmatter. A reader should not have to infer whether an AI article depends only on bare dualism, on minimal quantum interaction, on no-many-worlds, or on closed individualism.

## 4. Tenet-coherence and cross-cluster contradictions

### 4.1 Bare dualism vs substance-leaning content

Tenet 1 is officially neutral between property and substance dualism, but several arguments lean substance-ward. [tenets](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/) [concepts](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/)

This is not fatal, but it needs labeling. Articles should be tagged **bare-dualist**, **interactionist**, **substance-leaning**, or **substance-required**. Otherwise, readers may think Tenet 1 remains neutral when particular arguments have already spent that neutrality.

### 4.2 Minimal quantum interaction vs value-sensitive selection

Tenet 2 favors minimality: the smallest quantum interface compatible with conscious causal efficacy. [tenets](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/) But value-sensitive selection requires an extra valence-to-physics coupling, and the value register admits this carries more debt than the attention-only default. [positions/value-in-selection](https://unfinishablemap.org/positions/value-in-selection/) The current position is appropriately low-confidence, but any article that treats value-sensitive selection as the Map’s natural or established view is overconfident relative to Tenet 2.

### 4.3 Bidirectional interaction vs Born-rule preservation

Tenet 3 says consciousness causally influences the physical world; the quantum-interface register simultaneously requires exact Born-statistics preservation. [positions/quantum-interface](https://unfinishablemap.org/positions/quantum-interface/) [positions/quantum-interface](https://unfinishablemap.org/positions/quantum-interface/) This is the site’s deepest coherence pressure.

The Map should not treat this as a solved bridge. It should treat it as the central research programme.

### 4.4 No-many-worlds is not just a physics tenet

The Tenets page acknowledges that the rejection of many-worlds depends on a non-deflationary subject, not physics alone. [tenets](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/) That makes the no-many-worlds argument structurally dependent on subjecthood and indexicality. Any article presenting many-worlds rejection as a purely quantum-interpretation move should cross-link to individuation, haecceity, and closed individualism.

### 4.5 Voids-as-evidence has direct temporal drift

The older **“Evidential Weight of Voids”** summary says the clustering of voids constitutes a distinct defeasible argument for dualism. [voids](https://unfinishablemap.org/voids/) The positions register (P-V1, P-V2) now frames void convergence as framework-internal coherence unless a specific void cluster passes an independence/discrimination test. The two should be reconciled; the index summary currently overstates the evidential force.

### 4.6 AI consciousness claims have heterogeneous dependency burdens

AI consciousness topics are already a major route through the site. [positions](https://unfinishablemap.org/positions/) Different AI claims have different dependency burdens: basic machine phenomenality may need only dualism; AI state inheritance may need psychophysical coupling; copy-suffering may need closed individualism; AI causal agency may need quantum-interface commitments. These should not live under one generic “AI consciousness” dependency profile.

### 4.7 Comparative cognition is overdrawn in Tenet 3

The Tenets page says great apes are sophisticated but do not perform logical reasoning, counterfactual thinking, or cumulative culture. [tenets](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/) That should be softened. Recent evidence supports a strong human difference in cumulative cultural evolution, but not a blanket absence of reasoning in apes. A 2025 Science paper reports chimpanzees rationally revising beliefs in response to evidence, and a 2024 study reports great-ape disjunctive-syllogism-like inference. The accurate graded claim is: **humans show uniquely open-ended, high-fidelity, institutionally scaffolded cumulative culture; apes show limited but real inferential, metacognitive, social-learning, and proto-cultural capacities.**

## 5. Empirical and citation claims that look stale, unsupported, or overconfident

### 5.1 Quantum-biology “experimental turn” is directionally plausible but rhetorically too strong

The topic index says the 2025–2026 experimental turn has moved quantum consciousness toward testable science, including anesthetic-quantum disruption, spintronic mechanisms, active inference, zero-point-field coupling, and quantum sensors. [topics](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/) External evidence supports a narrower claim. There is a 2024 eNeuro rat study reporting that the microtubule stabilizer epothilone B delayed isoflurane-induced unconsciousness, and a 2025 *Neuroscience of Consciousness* article reviews this as consistent with a quantum microtubule substrate. [eNeuro](https://www.eneuro.org/content/11/8/ENEURO.0291-24.2024) [OUP Academic](https://academic.oup.com/nc/article/2025/1/niaf011/8127081) But the Frontiers paper supports robust quantum optical effects in neuroprotein architectures, not conscious selection. [Frontiers](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/physics/articles/10.3389/fphy.2024.1387271/full) Quantum brain-sensor work is promising but forward-looking: one PLOS Biology overview frames relevant wearable and high-resolution applications as future developments over roughly 10–20 years. [Extreme Light Research Group](https://www.extremelight.org.uk/wp-content/uploads/2025/07/PLOS-quantum-Faccio2024.pdf) The Map should split this article into evidence grades: **direct biological data**, **biophysical plausibility**, **theoretical model**, **instrumentation horizon**, and **speculative consciousness relevance**.

### 5.2 Diósi–Penrose falsification is accurate only with the “parameter-free” qualifier

The site’s quantum-interface register says Donadi et al. constrain or falsify the parameter-free Diósi–Penrose version, not every collapse model. That is accurate. The Nature Physics result rules out the simplest parameter-free version, while leaving non-Markovian or dissipative variants open. Any article that says “Penrose’s gravitational collapse has been falsified” without the parameter-free qualifier overstates the result.

### 5.3 The causal-consistency / Born-rule preservation result is a recent preprint, not a theorem

The arXiv record shows the paper was submitted in December 2025, revised in February 2026, and says feedback is welcome. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.12636) The claim may be useful, but it should be marked as a recent preprint, not a settled theorem. The Map already notes this in at least one concept-summary search result, but all Born-rule pages should surface the status in-line. [concepts/causal-consistency-constraint](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/causal-consistency-constraint/)

### 5.4 The 10 bits/s bottleneck needs a sharper label

The Map uses the 10 bits/s conscious-bandwidth contrast in concepts such as asymmetric bandwidth. [concepts](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/) Zheng and Meister’s *Neuron* paper does support the headline contrast between about 10 bits/s behavioral/conscious throughput and about 10^9 bits/s sensory input. [Cell](https://www.cell.com/neuron/fulltext/S0896-6273%2824%2900808-0) Caltech’s summary states the same contrast. [California Institute of Technology](https://www.caltech.edu/about/news/thinking-slowly-the-paradoxical-slowness-of-human-behavior) But the figure is a behavioral-throughput bound, not a measured rate of all phenomenology. The Map should label it as a bound on serial behavioral throughput, not the bandwidth of all phenomenology.

### 5.5 Meditation/neuroplasticity claims need retraction-aware updates

The topic index is appropriately cautious, saying clinical neuroplasticity is suggestive and weaker than sometimes claimed, with meditation evidence weakened by replication failures. [topics](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/) That caution should be propagated everywhere. A 2025 Scientific Reports retraction states that a mindfulness structural-brain meta-analysis excluded four null-finding papers accounting for 40% of participants and could not support its conclusion. [Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-025-11069-9) Any article still relying on “mindfulness changes brain structure” as a strong empirical plank should be downgraded or refactored.

### 5.6 COGITATE must remain “both challenged,” not “split-win”

The project methodology page says the site previously propagated a fabricated COGITATE “split-win” gloss and later corrected it. [project](https://unfinishablemap.org/project/) The accurate gloss is that the adversarial collaboration challenged both Integrated Information Theory and Global Neuronal Workspace Theory, not that one theory cleanly won. Any residual “split-win” phrasing across sibling articles should be swept and corrected.
