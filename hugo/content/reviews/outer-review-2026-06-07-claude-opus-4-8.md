# Outer Review: "Duhem-Quine Underdetermination in Consciousness Science" (The Unfinishable Map)

## TL;DR

- The article is empirically current and gets the headline philosophy roughly right, but it commits the exact class of error the site is prone to: it uses a metaphysically-neutral epistemic thesis (underdetermination) to manufacture evidential *parity* for dualism, and it inflates a non-independent cluster of anti-physicalist arguments into a "convergence" that allegedly lifts dualism above post-hoc status.
- It contains at least one outright empirical misstatement of its load-bearing example: the claim that "IIT better predicted face orientation decoding, while GWT better predicted object category decoding" misrepresents the 2025 COGITATE *Nature* paper, which found category (face–object) decoding in **both** posterior and prefrontal cortex (consistent with both theories) and orientation decoding in posterior cortex **only** (a challenge to GNWT, not a GNWT win).
- Its self-aware "underdetermination cuts both ways" section is the best part, but the Lakatosian rescue is performative inoculation: it relabels the self-undermining tension rather than resolving it, and never confronts that the site's own minimal-quantum-interaction mechanism is built to be empirically silent — which makes the claim that the dualist programme is "progressive" incoherent on the article's own terms.

## Key Findings

1. **Philosophy of science is mostly accurate but selectively presented and overstated.** The article correctly distinguishes holist from contrastive underdetermination and is faithful to Chalmers' actual first/third-person data claim. But it never engages Laudan & Leplin's "Empirical Equivalence and Underdetermination" (1991) — the single most important mainstream critique — nor Laudan's "Demystifying Underdetermination," and it never notes that Duhem himself explicitly restricted holism to physics and denied it applied to physiology and certain branches of chemistry. It presents contrastive underdetermination as far more settled than the literature treats it.
2. **The "deeper in consciousness science" thesis has a defensible kernel but is used to smuggle a metaphysical conclusion.** The legitimate point (first-person data, theory-ladenness of introspection, explanatory gap) is real. The illegitimate move is treating the explanatory gap as a revisable "auxiliary assumption" whose persistence marks underdetermination — which begs the central question of whether the gap is ontological or merely epistemic.
3. **A factual error in the central empirical exhibit** (the COGITATE decoding claim — see Details).
4. **The convergence argument fails the evidential-independence standard.** The article cites conceivability, knowledge, Nagel, and Kripke arguments as "independent lines converging," while elsewhere describing the phenomenal-concepts strategy — which is precisely the thesis that one common factor explains all of them. You cannot have it both ways.
5. **Tenet leakage and the constitutional-attractor effect are pervasive.** Every philosophy-of-science strand is routed to the same terminus: dualism is legitimate/live/equally-supported. The "Occam's Razor Has Limits" tenet is deployed to neutralize the chief cost of dualism.
6. **Citations are real, not fabricated** — the defects are mischaracterization and selective omission, not invention.

## Details

### Dimension 1 — Philosophical accuracy of the Duhem-Quine / underdetermination claims

**What it gets right.** The opening statement of the Duhem-Quine thesis (no hypothesis tested in isolation; logic alone cannot determine which auxiliary to revise) is accurate. The article's explicit distinction between **holist underdetermination** (which belief to revise when a prediction fails) and **contrastive underdetermination** (whether evidence can confirm one theory over empirically equivalent rivals) is correct and well-drawn, matching the standard taxonomy in the Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy entry on underdetermination. The attribution to Quine of radicalizing Duhem to "all knowledge" in "Two Dogmas of Empiricism" (1951) is accurate.

**Where it overstates and cherry-picks.** Three problems, in increasing severity:

(a) **It misses Duhem's own scope restriction.** Duhem explicitly confined confirmation holism to physics and stated that experimental theory in physics "is not the same as in fields like physiology and certain branches of chemistry"; he also kept logic and mathematics outside the "theoretical group." This matters enormously for an article whose entire thesis is that underdetermination is *more severe* in a non-physics science. Duhem's restriction is prima facie evidence *against* the article's central claim, or at least a complication it is obligated to address. It does not.

(b) **It ignores the principal mainstream critique.** The literature does not treat contrastive (empirical-equivalence) underdetermination as a secure result. Laudan & Leplin (1991, *Journal of Philosophy* 88(9):449–472) argue that empirical equivalence neither follows easily nor entails underdetermination, because "theories with exactly the same empirical consequences may admit of differing degrees of evidential support." Quine himself, in his later work, downgraded the underdetermination thesis to "not much more than a conjecture." The article asserts "both forms afflict consciousness science, but the second is especially severe" without acknowledging that the second form is the contested one. This is one-sided presentation of a live debate as settled doctrine — coherence inflation applied to philosophy of science itself. [Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy](https://plato.stanford.edu/archives/fall2015/entries/scientific-underdetermination/)[Semantic Scholar](https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/e5be/bc940c76d35d8449a5fffaeac92cdbba1007.pdf)

(c) **It misuses Stanford's unconceived alternatives.** Stanford's "problem of unconceived alternatives" is explicitly about empirically *inequivalent*, well-confirmed rivals, and is an *anti-realist* "New Induction" concluding we should doubt the truth of current theories. It is not a metaphysically neutral leveller and does not selectively favor dualism — by its own logic it equally implies that *dualism itself* is probably false or destined to be superseded by some unconceived alternative. The article conscripts it to suggest the "current menu" might be missing dualist-friendly options, inverting the argument's actual import.

**Verdict:** Competent at the textbook level, but it conflates the secure thesis (holist underdetermination) with the contested one (contrastive/empirical-equivalence) and the distinct anti-realist PUA, presenting a contested and partly anti-realist literature as a unified, dualism-permitting result.

### Dimension 2 — Is the "underdetermination runs deeper" argument sound or overstated?

**The defensible kernel.** Consciousness science does face distinctive epistemic obstacles: the first-person/third-person data split (faithfully drawn from Chalmers 2013), the theory-ladenness of introspective report, the historical collapse of introspectionism, and theory proliferation. On scale, the article's "several hundred distinct theories" is fair: Kuhn's 2024 paper (*Progress in Biophysics and Molecular Biology* 190:28–169) surveys over 200 recognised theories in ten categories, and his 2025 follow-up (*Current Neurology and Neuroscience Reports* 2025;26(1):5) states the Landscape "organizes more than 350 explanations of phenomenal consciousness." To this extent the article is not fantasizing.

**The smuggle.** The pivotal move is Section 4, "The explanatory gap as auxiliary assumption," which reframes the physicalist's commitment that the gap can be closed as a *revisable auxiliary*, and the gap's persistence as a *marker of underdetermination*. This begs the question. Whether the explanatory gap reflects an ontological gap or merely the structure of our concepts (the phenomenal-concepts strategy) is the very thing in dispute. By treating the gap as an auxiliary that "no experiment can adjudicate," the article presupposes it is permanent and ontologically significant — the dualist conclusion dressed as a premise. Epistemic underdetermination is metaphysically neutral; the article repeatedly converts it into metaphysical support for non-physicalism.

**Empirical currency — a genuine strength, with two notable omissions.** Unlike the site's known staleness problem, this article is current: it engages COGITATE (*Nature* 2025), Negro's Lakatosian paper (*Neuroscience of Consciousness* 2024, niae012), and Kuhn's landscape (2024). However:

- It never mentions the **15 September 2023 open letter** "The Integrated Information Theory of Consciousness as Pseudoscience" (Fleming, Frith, Goodale, Lau, LeDoux et al.), posted to PsyArXiv and signed by 124 scholars including Bernard Baars, Daniel Dennett, and Joseph LeDoux, nor the **10 March 2025 *Nature Neuroscience* commentary** "What Makes a Theory of Consciousness Unscientific?" (signed by many of the same writers). This is a serious omission *given the article's own Lakatosian framing*: it presents IIT as a co-equal "progressive research programme" while suppressing that a vocal segment of the field regards it as unfalsifiable — the precise "degenerating" charge the article's framework is built to assess. (For calibration: an anonymized survey of consciousness-paper authors found that of 60 respondents only 8% "fully" agreed and 20% "not at all" agreed with the pseudoscience letter — a vocal minority, not a majority. The article should engage the dispute, not omit it; but a reviewer should not overstate the dispute either.)
- The framing leans on IIT and GNWT as the field's two poles; the COGITATE paper itself flags recurrent-processing and higher-order theories. Minor, but it slightly inflates the "incompatible conclusions from the same data" picture.

### Dimension 3 — Citation accuracy

**No fabrications detected.** Every reference checked resolves to a real work with largely correct metadata:

- Duhem 1906/1954, *The Aim and Structure of Physical Theory* — correct.
- Quine 1951, "Two Dogmas," *Philosophical Review* 60(1):20–43 — correct.
- Chalmers 2013, "How Can We Construct a Science of Consciousness?", *Annals of the NYAS* 1303(1):25–35 — correct, and the gloss ("first-person data are irreducible to third-person data and vice versa; both are needed") is faithful to Chalmers' text.
- Stanford 2006, *Exceeding Our Grasp*, OUP — correct.
- Kuhn R.L. 2024, *Progress in Biophysics and Molecular Biology* 190:28–169 — correct.
- Negro 2024, *Neuroscience of Consciousness* 2024(1):niae012 — correct.
- COGITATE Consortium 2025, *Nature* 642(8066):133–142 — correct (DOI 10.1038/s41586-025-08888-1).
- Loar 1990/1997; Chalmers 2007 (in Alter & Walter, *Phenomenal Concepts and Phenomenal Knowledge*); Balog 2012; Davidson 1974 (*Proceedings and Addresses of the APA* 47:5–20); Lakatos 1970; Kuhn 1977 — all real and correctly placed. (Minor: Balog's most-cited 2012 piece on acquaintance is "Acquaintance and the Mind-Body Problem," in Gozzano & Hill eds.; her "In Defense of the Phenomenal Concept Strategy" is the master-argument reply. The article's bare "Balog 2012" is adequate but could disambiguate.)

**The real citation problem is mischaracterization and omission, not invention.** "Could not verify" applies to nothing here; "appears fabricated" applies to nothing here. The flag is: faithful bibliography, distorted *use*.

**The COGITATE distortion (load-bearing).** The article states: *"The results were mixed: IIT better predicted face orientation decoding, while GWT better predicted object category decoding."* Verified against the primary *Nature* paper, this is a distortion bordering on fabrication of the result-structure:

- **Category (face–object) decoding succeeded in BOTH posterior cortex AND prefrontal cortex** across iEEG, MEG, and fMRI; the paper states this was "consistent with IIT and GNWT predictions." It was not a GNWT win over IIT. (Preregistered prediction #1 was supported in the sense that "decoding conscious contents is maximal from posterior regions but often unsuccessful from PFC.")
- **Orientation (face orientation) decoding succeeded in posterior cortex but was essentially absent in PFC** (Bayesian analyses supported the null in PFC). This was the *divergent* result, and it *challenged GNWT* (which requires conscious content decodable from PFC). It is not a separate "IIT predicted it better" category set against a GNWT category win.
- Orientation could **not** be decoded for objects in either region, so "object category vs orientation" is not even the contrast the article implies.

The article does correctly reproduce the abstract-level summary ("IIT was challenged by the absence of sustained synchronisation in posterior cortex; GWT was challenged by the lack of ignition at stimulus offset and limited prefrontal representation"), the sample (256 subjects across 12 laboratories; the iEEG subset came from 29 epilepsy patients), the suprathreshold stimuli, and the three modalities. So the error is specifically in the prediction-1 decoding gloss, where it appears to have invented a symmetric "each theory won one dimension" structure the data do not support. The Seth quote ("the theories are just too different in their assumptions and explanatory goals") is authentic, but it is a press statement by one investigator, not a collective acknowledgment by "the investigators." ### Dimension 4 — Internal consistency with the five tenets / self-undermining

**The honest part.** The "Relation to Site Perspective" section is unusually candid: it concedes that "underdetermination cuts both ways," that "'Consciousness is not reducible to physical processes' is a confident commitment, not a tentative hypothesis," and that "evidence alone cannot settle whether that commitment is correct." This is exactly the self-application the audit brief demands, and credit is due. [topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness/)

**Why the rescue fails (performative inoculation).** The article defuses the self-undermining charge by invoking Lakatos: the tenets are the "hard core" of a research programme, "chosen because they define a distinctive research direction rather than because evidence compels them," and what matters is whether the programme is "progressive." This *relabels* the tension rather than dissolving it, in three ways: [topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness/)

(a) **The progressivity claim is empty on the site's own terms.** A Lakatosian programme is progressive only if it generates *novel, confirmed* predictions. The article asserts the Map's programme will ("neural processes at quantum-sensitive decision points may show statistical signatures inconsistent with purely physical noise"). But the site's own Tenets page concedes that the preferred mechanism — post-decoherence selection — is *constructed to leave aggregate Born statistics undisturbed* and is therefore "undetectable by aggregate Born-statistics tests," a "consistency claim" rather than a "novel-prediction claim," with the empirical falsifier "well beyond current experimental reach." A programme whose load-bearing mechanism is empirically silent cannot coherently be claimed to be Lakatos-progressive. The article wants the Lakatosian honour ("progressive") while the cited mechanism forfeits the Lakatosian currency (novel confirmed predictions). This internal incoherence is never confronted. [topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness/)[tenets](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)

(b) **The "proves too much" problem.** If underdetermination is as *structural and permanent* as the article argues (not dissolvable by better experiments), it equally undercuts any confidence that the dualist programme is the progressive one — the very judgement the article reserves for the future ("The Map holds that they will"). Deep structural underdetermination is corrosive of the article's own optimism, not just of physicalist optimism. [topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness/)

(c) **It leans on a parity claim that does not follow.** "Neither programme can claim evidential supremacy" is asserted as though it followed from underdetermination. It does not: Laudan & Leplin's exact point is that empirically equivalent theories can still differ in evidential support. Underdetermination shows physicalism is not *forced*; it does not establish *parity*. This is the constrain-vs-establish gate failing in the dualist's favor. [topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness/)

**Tenet leakage.** Present throughout the "neutral" exposition. The theoretical-virtues passage concludes that Kuhnian values "systematically malfunction" for consciousness and therefore "metaphysical commitments — including dualism — function as recognised components of rational theory selection rather than as illegitimate intrusions into science." That is a tenet-driven conclusion presented as an output of the analysis. The "framework dependence / false fluency / lossy translation" passage imports the Map's proprietary vocabulary as neutral description and uses it to recast a physicalist's *disagreement* as a *cognitive deficit* ("stripping away what made it compelling") — a co-optation of Davidson, who argued the opposite (that the very idea of incommensurable schemes is unintelligible). [topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness/)[topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness/)

**Constitutional-attractor effect.** Strong. Duhem, Quine, Lakatos, Stanford, Kuhn, and the phenomenal-concepts debate are each routed to the identical conclusion: dualism is legitimate and not dismissable. When every independent input yields the same output regardless of its native direction (Stanford and Davidson in particular point *away* from the Map's use), the channelling is done by the constitution, not the evidence.

### Dimension 5 — Logical gaps and unsupported leaps

(1) **The master fallacy: "not ruled out" → "positively supported."** The article repeatedly slides from "physicalism is underdetermined" to "dualism is a live/equally-supported option." Underdetermination is symmetric and licenses neither side; converting it into support for one rival is the precise illegitimate move the audit brief names.

(2) **Convergence without independence (coherence inflation).** The "Framework Dependence and Convergence" section claims conceivability, knowledge, Nagel's subjectivity, and Kripke's modal arguments are "multiple independent lines" that "converge," so that "the probability that they are all mistaken decreases." But the article *itself* presents the phenomenal-concepts strategy, whose entire content is that a *single* common factor — the special first-person mode of presentation of phenomenal concepts — generates all of these intuitions at once. Papineau and others argue explicitly that the "explanatory gap" is just the expression of one underlying dualist intuition. If that is even possibly right, the four arguments are not independent, the convergence inference collapses, and the article's own "epistemology of convergence arguments" (which lists independence as a required condition) refutes the very inference it makes. A self-inflicted evidential-independence failure. [topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness/)[Consc](https://fragments.consc.net/djc/2005/10/papineau_on_phe.html)

(3) **Equivocation on the conclusion.** Even granting convergence, the four arguments converge (at most) on *non-entailment* — that physical truths do not a priori entail phenomenal truths. That is an epistemic conclusion compatible with type-B physicalism, Russellian monism, and other non-dualist positions. The article slides from "physical description does not entail phenomenal experience" to "dualism." This is an equivocation between an epistemic gap and a metaphysical (ontological) gap — the very distinction the phenomenal-concepts strategy is built on, and which the article elsewhere acknowledges. [topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness/)

(4) **Counterargument absorption of the phenomenal-concepts strategy.** The article calls PCS "the most sophisticated physicalist response," then neutralizes it by (i) invoking Chalmers' 2007 "master argument," (ii) recasting PCS as "an auxiliary hypothesis move of exactly the kind Duhem-Quine predicts," and (iii) charging it has "primarily accommodated existing intuitions rather than predicting new phenomena." Each is rhetorical rather than substantive: it omits that Balog (2012) and Carruthers & Veillet have replied to Chalmers' master argument, with the mainstream verdict that the exchange is a *stalemate*, not a dualist win. And the "no new predictions" charge is suicidal: by the site's own admission, the dualist quantum mechanism also predicts no detectable new phenomena. The symmetry the article demands when it suits dualism is dropped when it does not. [topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness/)

(5) **Common-cause null not discharged.** The physicalist's best debunking explanation of the "convergence" — that all anti-physicalist arguments share a *common cognitive cause* (a built-in dualist intuition / the structure of phenomenal concepts), which would explain the convergence *without* any non-physical fact — is the live null hypothesis. The article gestures at it (via PCS) but never genuinely rules it out; it merely acknowledges and dismisses it. Under the site's own common-cause-null discipline, a convergence argument that does not defeat the shared-cause explanation cannot be used to lift dualism above post-hoc status — which is exactly what the article uses it to do ("dualism is not in the weak epistemic position of a theory that merely fits the data post hoc"). [topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness/)

## Recommendations

### LIST A — Article-level improvements (keyed to specific claims/sections)

1. **Correct the COGITATE decoding claim (Section "The COGITATE Adversarial Collaboration").** Delete "IIT better predicted face orientation decoding, while GWT better predicted object category decoding." Replace with the verified finding: category (face–object) decoding succeeded in *both* posterior and prefrontal cortex (consistent with both theories); orientation decoding succeeded in posterior cortex but was absent in PFC (a challenge to GNWT). This is a factual error in the load-bearing exhibit and must be fixed first.
2. **Add Laudan & Leplin (1991) and Laudan's "Demystifying Underdetermination" to Section "The Duhem-Quine Thesis."** State plainly that contrastive/empirical-equivalence underdetermination is contested, that empirical equivalence does not entail equal evidential support, and that Quine himself downgraded the thesis to a "conjecture." Scope every downstream use of "underdetermination" to the form actually licensed.
3. **Note Duhem's scope restriction.** Add a sentence acknowledging Duhem confined holism to physics and excluded physiology/chemistry/logic — and address why that does not defeat the "deeper in consciousness science" thesis. As written, the omission looks evasive. [New World Encyclopedia](https://www.newworldencyclopedia.org/entry/Confirmation_holism)
4. **Repair or retract the convergence argument (Section "Framework Dependence and Convergence").** Either (a) drop the independence claim and present the four arguments as expressions of one underlying intuition (consistent with the phenomenal-concepts strategy the article itself describes), or (b) supply a positive argument that they are evidentially independent that survives the PCS common-cause objection. You cannot assert independence here while endorsing the single-common-factor PCS framing two sections earlier.
5. **Close the epistemic→metaphysical equivocation.** Wherever the text moves from "physical description does not entail phenomenal experience" to "dualism," insert and exclude the intervening alternatives (type-B physicalism, Russellian monism). Non-entailment does not equal non-physicality.
6. **Engage the 2023 IIT "pseudoscience" letter (124 signatories, PsyArXiv, 15 Sept 2023) and the 10 March 2025 *Nature Neuroscience* commentary** — while reporting the survey calibration (only ~8% of polled consciousness researchers "fully" endorsed the label). The Lakatosian framing obliges the article to address the degeneration/unfalsifiability charge head-on rather than presenting IIT as an unproblematic progressive programme.
7. **Steelman the phenomenal-concepts strategy properly.** Add the Balog/Carruthers-Veillet replies to Chalmers' master argument and report the mainstream "stalemate" verdict, rather than presenting Chalmers 2007 as decisive. Drop the "no new predictions" charge or apply it symmetrically to the Map's own quantum mechanism.
8. **Confront the progressivity incoherence (Section "Relation to Site Perspective").** Reconcile the claim that the dualist programme is Lakatos-progressive with the Tenets page's admission that post-decoherence selection is empirically undetectable in aggregate. Either specify a genuinely novel, in-principle-confirmable prediction, or concede the programme cannot currently claim progressivity and is held on non-evidential grounds.
9. **Replace the asserted parity ("Neither programme can claim evidential supremacy") with the weaker, defensible claim** that underdetermination shows physicalism is not *compelled*. Parity is a stronger conclusion that underdetermination does not deliver.
10. **De-leak the "neutral" sections.** The theoretical-virtues and framework-dependence passages should mark their dualism-favorable conclusions as tenet-driven interpretations and should not recruit Davidson (who argued against incommensurable schemes) as if he supported the "lossy translation" picture.

### LIST B — Methodology-level improvements to the AI generation / adversarial-review pipeline

1. **Mandatory primary-source verification of every empirical *result claim*, not just citation metadata.** The COGITATE error passed because the bibliographic entry was correct while the *description of the findings* was wrong. Add a gate: for any cited experiment, the specific result sentences must be checked verbatim against the primary paper's results/abstract, and prediction-by-prediction claims must match the paper's own prediction structure.
2. **An equivocation/level-shift checker keyed to the epistemic→metaphysical slide.** Flag any inference of the form "X is underdetermined / not entailed / not ruled out" → "therefore [dualism / non-physicalism] is supported / live / equally probable." Require an explicit intervening step naming the non-dualist alternatives that survive the same epistemic premise.
3. **A convergence-independence gate.** Whenever the corpus runs a "multiple independent arguments converge" move, automatically check whether the same (or a linked) article endorses a single-common-factor explanation of those arguments (e.g., the phenomenal-concepts strategy). If so, block the independence claim until independence is separately argued. This directly targets coherence inflation.
4. **A self-undermining-consistency check for global epistemic levellers.** Any article deploying a universal sceptical thesis (underdetermination, theory-ladenness, pessimistic induction, unconceived alternatives) must pass a test that the thesis, applied at full strength, does not also dissolve the site's own positive tenets — and must not "rescue" the tenets with a Lakatosian relabel unless it can name a novel, confirmable prediction. Treat "chosen hard core" as a flag, not a resolution.
5. **A constrain-vs-establish ledger entry, enforced.** For each article, require an explicit line stating whether the cited evidence *constrains* physicalism (shows it not compelled) or is being used to *establish* dualism (positive parity/support), and reject the latter unless a positive evidential argument independent of underdetermination is supplied.
6. **A co-optation roster covering philosophers of science, not just philosophers of mind.** Include Duhem, Quine, Laudan, Stanford, and Davidson, flagging when their anti-realist or anti-dualist theses are inverted toward a dualist conclusion they would reject (Stanford's PUA and Davidson's anti-scheme argument are both misused here).
7. **A currency SLA tied to controversy, not just date.** For any theory presented as a legitimate research programme (IIT, GNWT), require a check for field-level legitimacy disputes (e.g., the 2023 IIT pseudoscience letter) within a fixed recency window, so Lakatosian "progressive/degenerating" framings cannot omit the field's own degeneration charges — and require the dispute be reported with calibration (signatory count *and* survey-level support), not cherry-picked in either direction.
8. **A "performative inoculation" detector.** Flag the pattern: opponent's strongest response named → neutralized in the same breath by relabeling it (e.g., "an auxiliary-hypothesis move") or by a single cited authority, without engaging the published replies to that authority. Require at least one substantive reply-to-the-reply before an objection may be marked as handled.
