## Executive Summary

This is some of the strongest structural work in the catalogue: a genuinely novel two-axis taxonomy (thickness, orthogonal to the Chalmers Type-D/E/F classification) deployed through a disciplined three-layer cartographic stack, with a fourth article importing the same discipline to the essential/contingent-consciousness question. The cohort's defining achievement is that it repeatedly *names and refuses* the exact move the Map is most tempted to make — letting tenet-coherence upgrade a metaphysical lean to a finding — and does so in the open, flagging the Map's own self-positioning as "reasoned methodological position, not established result." The chief opportunities are cross-linking (the essential/contingent article is a fresh orphan whose own parent survey does not yet link back) and one genuine new-article seed (a standalone treatment of the channel-width third axis the taxonomy explicitly defers).

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
Chalmers should be delighted and slightly provoked in equal measure. The taxonomy takes his own Type-D/E/F classification as the foil and argues — convincingly, on concrete cases — that it under-describes the territory: Nida-Rümelin and Descartes are both Type-D substance dualists yet sit at different mind-thickness; Saad's delegatory dualism is "arguably closer to Type-D than Chalmers' own naturalistic dualism, yet mind-thin." The cohort engages Chalmers' rich/austere Type-F distinction (2003) directly and positions the thickness axis as tracking what that distinction "only partially" captures. This is exactly the kind of taxonomic refinement Chalmers' own work invites. The hard problem is taken as bedrock throughout; physicalism is never the default.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
Stapp gets the cohort's single best worked example and is treated with real care. The four-quadrant article uses him as "perhaps the cleanest illustration" that thickness and relation-type are orthogonal: his intervention channel is minimal (quantum-Zeno biasing) while his observer ontology and his quantum ontology are both thick — "three values across what may be only a two-axis taxonomy." The mechanism-costs article then reads his quantum-Zeno account as a Q4 inhabitant "routing influence through a probabilistic channel that is structurally Q1-like even when sitting in a Q4 ontology." This is a genuinely illuminating reading of Stapp that I have not seen articulated elsewhere: it explains *why* his view feels both extravagant and minimal at once. Stapp would recognize his own commitments here and find them sharpened.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The essential/contingent article is where the first-person stays central under maximum pressure. Its core move — that no introspective report can decide between "I was aware but remember nothing" and "I was not aware," because both predict the identical dreamless-sleep blank — is precisely Nagel's kind of point about the limits of third-person adjudication over first-person facts. The article refuses to let the *appearance* of an empirical question ("surely we could find out whether anything is experienced in dreamless sleep") collapse the genuine first-person/third-person gap. Nagel would also approve the cross-traditional seriousness: Advaita's *svaprakasha* regress argument is presented as a real argument with a real terminating structure, not as exotic decoration.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
Whitehead is handled with unusual precision in the Q3 article — and, crucially, in a way that does *not* cash out as a tier-upgrade. He is read as having "dissolved the slide by refusing the two-substance framing from the start": his dipolar actual occasions are aspects of one kind, sitting "near the neutral-monist or dual-aspect region rather than at stable Q3 dualism." This is the correct and charitable reading, and it is doing taxonomic work (locating him), not evidential work (it does not use his panexperientialism to license any consciousness-attribution upgrade). The process-philosophical resonance is present — "experience all the way down" is the implicit backdrop of the whole thick-mind axis — but it is consistently kept on the metaphysical-compatibility axis, never imported as evidence. This is the honest pattern: process-friendliness as removing-a-defeater, not as elevating a verdict.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
The selection-only channel that anchors the Q3 stabilisation analysis is precisely the structure Kane needs: the mind "chooses; physics constrains the choice set." The stabiliser argument — that a selection-only channel gives the physical pole "real, distinct, non-eliminable work" (it *supplies the menu*) — preserves genuine agency without inflating it into the mind doing all the work. Kane would find the bidirectional-symmetry argument especially congenial: a two-way channel where "each pole is causally answerable to the other" is exactly the non-reductive setting in which ultimate sourcehood can live.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
McGinn's epistemic humility is structurally built into all four articles, and this is the cohort's most consistent virtue. The Q3 article keeps "trajectory" (accuracy-register) and "stabilisation/self-positioning" (reasoned-methodological-register) explicitly separate and flags the latter as "not established results." The essential/contingent article states its conclusion as "a metaphysical commitment the evidence underdetermines rather than forces" and lists three considerations *each of which it then says cannot settle the thesis*. The four-quadrant article admits its own compression cost (it "places some positions, Stapp chief among them, by judgement rather than definition"). This is mysterian discipline operationalized: the articles repeatedly mark the boundary of what the available evidence can decide.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This is the load-bearing verdict for the cohort, and it is strongly positive. The essential/contingent article is a textbook case of *tenet-as-evidence-upgrade being praise-worthily NOT done*. It had every structural opportunity to slide: the Map already adopts the Zahavian constitutive self, already rests No Many Worlds on indexical identity, and could have argued "Dualism plus the indexical-identity cluster therefore establishes essential consciousness." It explicitly declines: "Treating the dualism tenet as if it settled the essentialist reading would be the precise failure mode the discipline guards against: a tenet removing a defeater, then being silently read as upgrading the verdict." It names the coherence consideration as raising "framework-internal weight... without raising its evidential status," and it keeps Nyaya standing as "a fully dualist rival the Map declines on coherence grounds rather than evidential ones." That is the *"tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating"* pattern stated almost verbatim.

The Q3 article does the same on its own terrain: the "what would stabilise Q3" analysis is fenced off as "a structural argument, not a demonstrated result," and the self-positioning closes with "it shows what the Map's commitments *would* secure, not that occupancy has been demonstrated." The mechanism-costs article's non-adjudicative posture ("the cells differ in what they owe, not in whether they are defensible") refuses to convert the Map's per-cell lightness on its chosen battery into endorsement — and even flags that its three-question battery "over-emphasises physical-side debts deliberately," conceding the asymmetry could be artefactual.

No calibration concern to flag. The Process Philosopher and the Hardline Empiricist *converge* here rather than conflict: Whitehead-friendliness is praised on the compatibility axis, and the cohort never lets that friendliness become a tier-upgrade. By the skill's own criterion, convergence of these two personas on the same body of work means the cohort has resolved the tension honestly.

## Content Strengths

### topics/four-quadrant-dualism-taxonomy.md
- **Strongest point**: The orthogonality argument. It pre-empts the obvious skeptical objection ("isn't thickness just a re-parameterisation of Type-F vs Type-E?") and defeats it on concrete cases (Nida-Rümelin vs Descartes; Saad vs Chalmers) rather than by assertion. This is what makes the axis earn its name.
- **Notable quote**: "Naming the thickness axis makes visible a question the dominant taxonomies hide: not only *what kind* of dualism but *how much* of each side."
- **Why it works**: It is genuinely under-named territory (the empty Q4 cell, attributed to disciplinary parsimony-as-virtue training), and the "Limits of the Thickness Metaphor" section unpacks thickness into four sub-measures and openly concedes the taxonomy tracks only one of them — disciplined self-limitation that strengthens rather than weakens the contribution.

### topics/mechanism-costs-dualism-thickness-quadrants.md
- **Strongest point**: The fixed three-question battery (causation / interface / conservation) applied uniformly across all four cells, yielding the genuinely novel result that the conservation objection is *thickness-sensitive* — "bites hardest in Q4, lighter in Q1, dissolves in Q3, structurally inapplicable in Q2 strict." This reframes a debate that has run "since Leibniz" as having been conducted at the wrong granularity.
- **Notable quote**: "A defender of Q4 and a defender of Q2 strict may both call themselves dualists yet appear to face entirely non-overlapping mechanism bills. Convergent terminology may hide divergent commitments."
- **Why it works**: The article inaugurates a *named method* (mechanism-costs cartography) and is rigorous about staying non-adjudicative, then connects each cell to the right downstream article (Q1's authority debt to phenomenal-authority; the Map's cell to the somatic-interface "at the level where consciousness actually engages a body").

### topics/q3-q4-sliding-boundary-and-transparency-problem.md
- **Strongest point**: The diagnosis-to-cure structure. It first names the "Q3 sliding-boundary problem" as an *explanatory squeeze* (a pole that does no independent work becomes a candidate for elimination), then derives the two stabilisers (selection-only channel + bidirectional symmetry) directly from that diagnosis. The argument has genuine logical shape, not just a list.
- **Notable quote**: "The slide is not a mistake the philosopher makes; it is the position telling the truth about itself."
- **Why it works**: The historical survey (Berkeley, Leibniz, Kastrup, Whitehead, Myers/James, Yogācāra) is calibrated case-by-case — James is the "rare candidate" precisely *because* his neutral-monism flirtation leaves the tension unresolved — and the MWI branch-problem analogy is presented as "structural, not a claim that idealism *is* MWI," tying the anti-slide instinct to the No-Many-Worlds tenet through a shared "recovery debt" pattern. That cross-domain structural parallel is a real insight.

### topics/essential-vs-contingent-consciousness.md
- **Strongest point**: The symmetric-predictions table. Three rows (dreamless sleep, anaesthesia, minimal phenomenal experience) each show essentialist and contingentist readings predicting the *identical* observable — making the underdetermination claim concrete and checkable rather than asserted.
- **Notable quote**: "No introspective report can decide between 'I was aware but remember nothing' and 'I was not aware,' because both predict the same blank."
- **Why it works**: It maps a genuine cross-traditional fault line (Advaita essentialist / Nyaya contingentist / Samkhya relocating the question / Descartes vs Locke as Western mirrors) with real scholarly care — the Samkhya treatment correctly refuses the binary and shows the dispute is partly about *which* principle counts as the non-physical aspect — and then states the Map's lean with exemplary honesty.
