## What unfinishablemap.org is building

The site is a deliberately *coherent* philosophy platform (“single best‑guess picture … expressed as if fact”) with a human-curated core and a largely AI-generated, continuously evolving body of supporting articles.

Operationally, it is engineered to be read primarily by chatbots that fetch a small number of pages and may truncate long outputs, so articles are front‑loaded, self‑contained, and structured to be machine-summarizable.

The content is constrained by five “tenets” (chosen commitments rather than proven conclusions), and everything else is meant to elaborate, defend, operationalize, or map the boundaries of those commitments (including explicit “voids” where the framework expects permanent or semi-permanent opacity).

The project also publishes its *production machinery*: an AI automation loop, a workflow/skill system, explicit countermeasures against AI-driven “coherence inflation,” a testability ledger, and a safety protocol for cognitively risky “voids” exploration.

## The site’s load-bearing commitments

### The five tenets (constraints on everything else)

1. **Dualism (irreducibility):** Consciousness is not reducible to physical processes; the site is neutral between substance/property dualism but commits to irreducibility.
2. **Minimal Quantum Interaction:** If mind affects matter, it does so by minimally biasing quantum-indeterminate outcomes without energy injection or conservation violations.
3. **Bidirectional Interaction:** Consciousness causally influences physical outcomes (not epiphenomenal); the proposed bridge is selection among superposed neural states.
4. **No Many Worlds:** Many-worlds is rejected largely on indexical/identity grounds; the site proposes baseline objective collapse plus “consciousness modulation” in brains to handle “prebiotic collapse.”
5. **Occam’s Razor Has Limits:** Simplicity is not decisive in domains where our concepts are likely inadequate; parsimony cannot be used as a knockout argument against dualism here.

### The most developed “mechanistic” story (within the site’s metaphysics)

The site’s most detailed attempted answer to “how mind acts on matter” is:

* **Consciousness is primarily a selector** (the brain generates options; consciousness selects among them).
* **Attention is the causal bridge/interface**: attention is treated as the physical selection mechanism through which the non-physical subject biases outcomes.
* **Willed attention is singled out** as the locus of “genuine conscious contribution,” associated with specific neural markers and a ~300ms selection timescale.
* **Agency is bandwidth-limited** (the site cites an approximate ~10 bits/second constraint and argues it may be structural, not merely empirical).

### Two other integrative pillars

* **Time and consciousness are proposed as mutually dependent**, with the further suggestion that collapse/irreversibility may contribute to time’s arrow, and that consciousness may participate in that structure.
* **Every framework generates “voids”**: the site makes this explicit (mechanism void, selection void, etc.) and formalizes safety boundaries for exploration of potentially destabilizing topics.

## Deep inferences implied by the site, but not stated as conclusions

Below, “implied” means: they follow from *combinations* of the site’s own stated commitments, but (as far as the surfaced pages show) the site has not crystallized them as explicit takeaways, named theses, or dedicated concept pages.

### 1) The site is functionally drifting toward a Russellian / dual-aspect picture, even while calling itself “dualism”

The site repeatedly leans on the idea that physics provides relational/structural description while consciousness is our only access to “intrinsic nature,” and it treats this as central to why the explanatory gap persists.
At the same time, its dualism tenet explicitly stays neutral between substance and property dualism.

**Inference:** If the “intrinsic nature” framing remains central, the most stable long-run metaphysical attractor is likely not classic two-substance dualism, but a **dual-aspect / Russell–Eddington style** view: the physical is extrinsic structure; the phenomenal is intrinsic character (or an aspect of it). This would *partially* defuse the interaction problem by reframing it as an intra-reality aspect relation rather than a cross-substance causal bridge. The site gestures toward the ingredients but does not state this as a strategic metaphysical consolidation.

### 2) “Consciousness as selector” + “attention as interface” implies a control-theoretic model of will, not a computational model of mind

The agency story explicitly says the brain does most computation and consciousness selects.
The attention story adds that the causal channel is bandwidth-limited and structurally cannot be expanded.

**Inference:** The site’s commitments imply consciousness should be modeled as a **low-bandwidth control signal** (policy selection / constraint / veto / stabilization), not as a planner or simulator. This is more like *control theory* (stabilization, gating, attractors, resource allocation) than like symbolic reasoning or large-scale search. The site has the pieces, but it does not yet state this as the governing modeling lens, nor does it spell out what “selection” would mean in formal control terms (e.g., what state is observed, what objective is optimized, what constitutes an “action” in the selector channel).

### 3) Moral responsibility becomes an empirically varying property of attentional capacity (a responsibility gradient)

The site ties agency to attentional economics and emphasizes a bounded “action channel” through attention.
Separately it frames daily life and choice as practically urgent because conscious experience is what matters and choices are genuinely authored.

**Inference:** If attention is the bridge and is bandwidth-limited, then **moral responsibility is not binary**. It should vary with (a) attentional health (sleep, fatigue, stress), (b) developmental stage, (c) disorders that impair willed attention, and (d) training that increases attentional control—because these modulate the *effective capacity of the selector channel*. The site hints at this direction but has not stated the downstream implication for ethics, punishment, rehabilitation, or social policy as a central conclusion.

### 4) The time thesis implicitly commits the framework to “psychophysical participation in temporal asymmetry”

The site proposes that time’s arrow may emerge through (consciousness-involving) collapse and that consciousness and time may be mutually dependent.

**Inference:** This entails a stronger hidden claim: if consciousness participates in the machinery that makes irreversibility real, then **some aspect of temporal asymmetry is partly psychophysical** (not purely thermodynamic boundary conditions). That has consequences the site doesn’t yet spell out, e.g.:

* you can potentially look for correlations between **temporal phenomenology** (time dilation, “timelessness”) and **selection dynamics** (noise profiles, stability of neural patterns), and
* “what a mind is” may be partly definable in terms of how it *implements a present* (retention–protention integration) rather than only in terms of computation or representation.

### 5) The site’s AI-consciousness exclusion arguments imply a general substrate constraint, not merely “LLMs aren’t conscious”

The time article argues current LLMs lack the continuous temporal structure (specious present / retention–protention) that may be required.
The tenets require a quantum-indeterminacy interface and definite-outcome selection.
The attention article stresses bidirectional/re-entrant dynamics and willed attention markers.

**Inference:** Combined, this implies a **three-part substrate criterion** for consciousness in the site’s worldview:

1. continuous temporality (a real present with binding),
2. an accessible indeterminacy/selection interface, and
3. recurrent dynamics that can implement willed selection.

This is broader than “LLMs aren’t conscious”; it becomes a **design spec** for any putatively conscious artificial system. The site hasn’t stated the criterion as a single consolidated thesis or used it to generate a “roadmap to conscious machines” (even if its conclusion is that such machines are unlikely or would require radically different architectures).

### 6) Rejecting Many Worlds on indexical grounds commits the worldview to primitive “thisness” (haecceity) more than it acknowledges

The tenets explicitly reject Many Worlds partly because it raises “why am I this instance and not another?” and treats that as meaningful.

**Inference:** Taking that objection seriously implies an ontological weight to **self-locating facts**—not merely semantic but metaphysical. That pushes the worldview toward some form of haecceitism (“this observer” is an objective fact not reducible to qualitative structure). The site uses indexical pressure to reject MWI, but it hasn’t fully cashed out what metaphysics of identity it is implicitly buying (and what that implies for personal identity over time, duplication, teleportation thought experiments, or anthropic reasoning).

### 7) “Value resides in experience” + “experience selects outcomes” creates an unaddressed fork: value-blind vs value-sensitive selection

“Living with the Map” grounds value in phenomenal character and frames life-quality as what matters.
The tenets and attention/agency story ground causation in selection.

**Inference:** The framework must eventually answer: **what determines the direction of selection?**

* If selection is **value-blind** (only “attention/intention” as a neutral pointer), then value is epiphenomenal to selection even if value is real.
* If selection is **value-sensitive** (valence/meaning influences which outcomes get selected), then the worldview starts to resemble a **teleological physics** (even if only locally in brains), with potentially testable signatures and major ethical implications.

The site does not yet present this as a decisive fork that must be resolved, but it is structurally forced by the combination of its own pillars.

### 8) The “voids” program implies a new methodology: apophatic science as *positive evidence* (not just humility)

The voids framework explicitly treats structured failure, cognitive closure, and occlusion as cartographic data.

**Inference:** This is more than “some things are unknowable.” It suggests a research program where:

* patterns of slippage, aversion, recursion limits, and phenomenology-at-the-edge are treated as **measurements** of cognitive architecture,
* and “what cannot be stabilized in attention” becomes a kind of negative-image map of mind.

The site states the taxonomy and the intent, but it hasn’t yet extracted the methodological punchline as a general epistemology: **systematic failure modes are among the strongest evidence sources available at the explanatory frontier**.

### 9) The site’s production architecture makes it a memetic feedback experiment—and its epistemology must treat that as an object-level risk

The site is intentionally coherent, largely AI-written, continuously updated by automation, and explicitly worried about coherence inflation.

**Inference:** This is not just a publishing method; it is an epistemic dynamical system. The implicit risk is broader than circular citations:

* the Map can become a **self-reinforcing attractor** for future LLM summarizers that ingest it,
* producing convergence toward its own priors unless anchored by external adversarial testing and hard empirical hooks.

The countermeasures document addresses parts of this, but the deeper conclusion—*the Map is itself part of the phenomenon it studies (mind + AI + meaning)*—has not been stated as a central, explicit thesis.

### 10) The current “testability ledger” implies the need for quantitative update rules, not just qualitative disconfirmers

The ledger lists “decisive disconfirmers,” weak evidence, and non-evidence across core claims.

**Inference:** To remain genuinely testable under its own automation pressures, the site will eventually need:

* explicit **effect-size thresholds** (what magnitude of RNG anomaly matters),
* explicit **confidence trajectories** (what would move a tenet-adjacent claim from speculation to supported),
* and a way to prevent “not achieved” from remaining a stable resting state indefinitely.

This is implied by the combination of (a) automation, (b) coherence-inflation risk, and (c) testability intent—but it is not yet stated as a requirement.
