These three articles form a tightly cross-referencing cluster, all `ai_contribution: 100`, all `ai_modified: 2026-05-27`, none previously the subject of a pessimistic review. They make the Map's most explicitly mathematical and empirical claims, so they are the highest-value target for adversarial scrutiny.

## Executive Summary

The cluster is unusually careful: it has clearly been hardened against the obvious objections (every constraint is repeatedly flagged as *conditional* on untested brain-internal regimes, and the "this favours no reading over the consciousness-free rivals" disclaimer is applied conscientiously). The strongest remaining weaknesses are not philosophical lapses but (1) two concrete cross-article **citation contradictions** for shared references (same paper/author cited with different venues, titles, and initials), and (2) a structural over-reliance on **forthcoming/preprint/unverifiable sources** to carry load-bearing uniqueness theorems. The decoherence objection is acknowledged but, for the cluster's *default* corridor reading, arguably waved through too quickly. None of the discipline-specific failure modes (label-leakage, boundary-substitution, altered-state asymmetry) fire.

## Critiques by Philosopher

### The Eliminative Materialist (Churchland)
The whole apparatus — experience space E, coupling map C, "consciousness choosing outcome A over B" — is folk psychology dressed in operator notation. Giving "qualia" a Riemannian manifold does not make qualia real; it makes the manifold a baroque encoding of neural similarity relations that neuroscience already studies without the metaphysical surcharge. The cluster's own honesty undercuts it: it concedes Kleiner's E "can be read as a representational layer over P without granting it ontological autonomy" (math-structure, line 147). Once that concession is on the table, the eliminativist simply takes the deflationary reading and the entire E × P scaffolding becomes a notation for brain states. The article names the unmet burden on *itself* (good) but never states what would make the autonomy reading *earn* its place over the deflationary one — that is the missing in-framework move.

### The Hard-Nosed Physicalist (Dennett)
The "specification gap" is being inflated from an absence of a theory into a positive metaphysical site for consciousness to live in. "Statistical closure with outcome-level openness" describes a gap in the *formalism*; the leap to "this is precisely where C must operate" (formalism, line 91) treats a feature of our descriptive apparatus as a hole in the world. The cluster half-admits this — "the brute-randomness reading is internally coherent and the structure discussed here does not rule it out" (math-structure, line 95) — but then keeps using selection language as the default throughout. If the brute-randomness reading is genuinely undefeated, the persistent "consciousness selects" framing in the tenets sections is doing rhetorical work the argument has disavowed.

### The Quantum Skeptic (Tegmark)
This is where the cluster is most exposed. The decoherence objection is named precisely once with force (math-structure, line 107: warm-tissue coherence is "physically demanding") and then *defused for the non-default frameworks only*. But the Map's **default** reading is corridor / post-decoherence selection, which the cluster repeatedly presents as needing *no* coherence at all because it "acts on the already-decohered improper mixture" (causal-consistency, line 71). That move deserves adversarial pressure the cluster does not supply: selecting a branch from an improper mixture *after* decoherence is exactly the orthodox measurement step — so what distinguishes "consciousness selects the post-decoherence outcome" from "the outcome is simply realised, with no selector"? The cluster asserts the difference is the unexplained intention/outcome correlation, but never quantifies or characterises that correlation, so Tegmark's "you haven't done the calculation" lands. The ~10 bits/second bandwidth figure (formalism, line 75) is imported as a "sixth practical constraint" but is a cognitive-psychology number about reportable throughput; using it to bound a quantum coupling rate is a category slide that wants justification.

### The Many-Worlds Defender (Deutsch)
The cluster treats "No Many Worlds" as a *presupposition* ("Under many-worlds... the formalism becomes vacuous", formalism line 105; "interpretively inert", math-structure line 153). From the Everettian seat this is question-begging: the entire corridor argument leans on Born-rule uniqueness theorems (Masanes-Galley-Müller, Torres Alegre), yet *deriving* the Born rule from decision theory / branch-counting is precisely the Everettian programme (Deutsch 1999, Wallace) — which the causal-consistency article itself lists in the same derivational lineage (line 51). So MWI is not "vacuous" with respect to this mathematics; it is a *rival customer for the very same theorems*. The cluster cannot both cite the Born-rule reconstruction programme as support and dismiss the interpretation that generated half of it.

### The Empiricist (Popper's Ghost)
The cluster works hard at falsifiability and partly succeeds: minimum-outside-corridor proposals are said to be "falsifiable in principle by a precision Bell-type test" (causal-consistency, line 73). But the **default corridor / trumping readings are explicitly constructed to leave no signature** — trumping "leaves no distinctive trajectory signature, so the position earns its place on theoretical rather than empirical grounds" (math-structure, line 87); post-decoherence selection has "no ensemble signature" (causal-consistency, line 71). So the Map's *preferred* readings are, by their own description, unfalsifiable, while only the *disfavoured* readings are testable. A Popperian notes the asymmetry: the theory has arranged for its empirical content to live entirely in the options it does not endorse.

### The Buddhist Philosopher (Nagarjuna)
The coupling map C presupposes a stable experiential relatum e ∈ E that *does* the selecting — a self at the controls of the interface. The cluster reifies precisely what should be deconstructed: it builds an automorphism group Aut(E) to capture "the freedom of relabelling experiences" yet never asks whether there is an experiencer to be relabelled. Emptiness suggests the gap C is meant to bridge is generated by positing two svabhava-bearing domains (E and P) in the first place.

## Critical Issues

### Issue 1: Contradictory citation for Masanes-Galley-Müller (2019) across the cluster
- **Files**: `consciousness-physics-interface-formalism.md` (ref 2), `mathematical-structure-of-the-consciousness-physics-interface.md` (ref 4), `causal-consistency-constraint.md` (ref 2)
- **Location**: reference lists
- **Problem**: The same paper is cited two different ways. Formalism and math-structure give *Nature Physics*, 15, 1174–1177. Causal-consistency gives *Nature Communications*, 10(1), 1361. These are different journals, volumes, and page/article numbers for one paper that all three articles treat as the foundational Born-rule-uniqueness result. (The published paper is *Nature Physics* 15, 1174–1177, 2019; the causal-consistency citation is the erroneous one.) Because the articles directly cross-link, a reader following the chain hits an inconsistency that undermines confidence in the load-bearing citation.
- **Severity**: Medium (factual/citation integrity on a load-bearing source)
- **Recommendation**: `refine-draft` to normalise all three to *Nature Physics*, 15, 1174–1177 (2019).

### Issue 2: Contradictory citation/identity for Tonetto across the cluster
- **Files**: same three articles
- **Location**: prose ("statistical closure with outcome-level openness") + reference lists
- **Problem**: Two articles cite **B. Tonetto**, *"What Physics Actually Closes: Causal Closure, Quantum Indeterminacy, and the Interpretive Asymmetry"*, PhilArchive (no year in the entry, cited inline as "(2026)"). The third (causal-consistency, ref 5) cites **L. Tonetto** (2026), *"Statistical Closure with Outcome-Level Openness"*, *Foundations of Physics* (forthcoming). Different first initial, different title, different venue — yet the prose in all three attributes the identical coined phrase "statistical closure with outcome-level openness" to the same author. Either these are two records for one work (then the metadata must be reconciled) or two different works/authors (then the shared verbatim phrase is mis-attributed in at least one place).
- **Severity**: Medium (citation integrity + possible mis-attribution of a coined term used as a conceptual anchor)
- **Recommendation**: `refine-draft` to reconcile author initial, title, and venue across all three; confirm which record is canonical for the quoted phrase.

### Issue 3: The default (corridor/trumping) readings are unfalsifiable by their own description
- **Files**: `mathematical-structure...` (lines 87, 89), `causal-consistency-constraint.md` (line 71)
- **Problem**: The Map's *preferred* readings are explicitly signature-free, while only the *disfavoured* minimum-outside-corridor reading is said to be testable. This is internally consistent but leaves the headline position empirically empty, and the articles do not flag this asymmetry as a cost of the default. A Popperian / Tegmark reader will press exactly here.
- **Severity**: Medium

### Issue 4: Reliance on preprint / forthcoming / single-author unverifiable sources for load-bearing theorems
- **Files**: all three
- **Location**: Pati (2026) arXiv:2601.13012; Torres Alegre (2025) arXiv:2512.12636; Arana (2025) PhilArchive; Tonetto (forthcoming); Agrawal-Wilson (2025) "arXiv preprint" (no ID)
- **Problem**: The cluster's central structural claims (inner-product uniqueness, causal-consistency selection of the Born rule) rest on very recent, non-peer-reviewed, single-or-two-author preprints, some with bare or missing identifiers (Agrawal-Wilson has no arXiv number). The prose states these as established theorems ("Pati (2026) showed...", "Torres Alegre (2025) proved..."). For a cluster whose rhetorical force is "these are theorems, not philosophical preferences", the evidential weight placed on unrefereed sources is a vulnerability.
- **Severity**: Low-Medium
- **Recommendation**: Soften "showed/proved" to "argues/derives" for the unrefereed preprints, or add a brief note that these reconstructions are recent and not yet independently confirmed. Add the missing arXiv id for Agrawal-Wilson.

## Counterarguments to Address

### "Post-decoherence selection is just orthodox measurement with a label"
- **Current content says**: Consciousness "acts on the already-decohered improper mixture, choosing among the alternatives physics has already prepared, without modifying the probability distribution" (causal-consistency, line 71); this is the canonical corridor instance.
- **A critic would argue (Tegmark/Dennett)**: That is observationally and dynamically identical to the outcome simply being realised. Calling the realisation a "choice" adds a word, not a mechanism; the unexplained intention-outcome correlation that motivates the selection reading is asserted but never characterised.
- **Suggested response**: Either characterise the correlation that distinguishes selection from brute realisation (even schematically), or explicitly concede the readings are empirically identical and rest the preference on the fifth tenet alone — and say so where the default is declared, not only in the abstract caveats.

### "MWI is a rival claimant to your Born-rule theorems, not a vacuous interpretation"
- **Current content says**: No Many Worlds is "presupposed"; under MWI the formalism is "interpretively inert" / "vacuous".
- **A critic would argue (Deutsch)**: The Deutsch-Wallace decision-theoretic Born derivation is in the same lineage you cite approvingly (causal-consistency line 51). You cannot lean on the Born-reconstruction programme and call its Everettian wing inert.
- **Suggested response**: Distinguish "MWI is ruled out by Tenet 4" (the honest framework-boundary marking) from "MWI is vacuous w.r.t. this math" (false). Mark the boundary rather than overclaiming inertness.
