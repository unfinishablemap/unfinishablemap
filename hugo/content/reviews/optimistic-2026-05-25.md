## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The cohort takes the explanatory gap seriously at every level. `higher-order-theories` runs the gap through PRM, HOROR, and illusionism and shows the fork survives each — "why is the reality-monitoring tag *felt* rather than merely computed" is the hard problem in higher-order dress, named cleanly. `clinical-phenomenology` resists the eliminativist's vitalism-in-reverse move explicitly: the closed temporal horizon of melancholic depression is framed as an *in-principle* gap, not an argument from present ignorance. Chalmers would recognise his own discipline being applied against the temptation to over-read clinical correlates.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
`integration-as-activity` keeps the door to mental causation open at exactly the right place: an identity thesis "leaves no room for influence — the structure simply *is* what it is," whereas activity "naturally accommodates influence." The article correctly defers the question of *whether* that influence operates at quantum indeterminacies to downstream articles rather than overclaiming it here. Stapp would approve of the framing that makes room for the interface without smuggling it in as already established.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
`clinical-phenomenology` is the standout for Nagel. The form/content distinction, the double-grounding epistemics, and the "what is it like" question being made *clinically measurable* via EASE without being reduced to it — this centres first-person experience while respecting third-person constraint. The "what neuroscientific measure could capture the difference between experienced time as open versus closed" passage is Nagel's question sharpened to a clinical edge.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
`integration-as-activity` gives Whitehead his due and *then draws the boundary correctly*. Prehension is presented as "the activity framing taken to its metaphysical limit," genuinely honouring the process-philosophical reading — and then the article names "the Whitehead limit": the activity discipline "does *not* require panexperientialism." This is process-philosophical resonance praised at its own level without being used as an engine of anything. Crucially (see Hardline Empiricist below), the praise here does **not** cash out as a tier-upgrade for any organism or system — there is no boundary case being slid up the scale on Whitehead-friendliness.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
`clinical-phenomenology`'s treatment of agency/passivity is the relevant passage: the felt difference between "I am doing this" and "this is happening to me" is grounded in a real efference-copy/corollary-discharge mechanism, and the Map's quarrel is located precisely — not that functionalism lacks a *which-function* story, but that it asserts rather than supplies the bridge from functional signature to felt agency. Kane would value that the agentive datum is taken as real and structured, not explained away.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
`introspection-architecture-independence-scoring` is shot through with appropriate epistemic humility about what *we* can establish from inside our own introspective architecture. The "N=2 — one promoted, one retracted" admission ("consistent with both *methodology works* and *methodology rationalises decisions already made*") is McGinn-grade humility about the limits of the method itself, not just its objects. The "non-flatness claim is provisional" closing is exactly right.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This persona's verdict is load-bearing for this cohort, and it is strongly positive.

`introspection-architecture-independence-scoring` is a model of the discipline. It uses the five-tier register honestly throughout, keeps despite-commitments and because-prediction observations separate, and — most importantly — **does the praise-worthy thing of *not* upgrading**. It had every structural opportunity to slide the cross-species and cross-tradition channels up to load-bearing on tenet-coherence or process-philosophical resonance, and it explicitly declines: both auxiliary channels are rated "calibration-grade rather than load-bearing," discharge of the strongest null is kept "concentrated in the patient-population channel," and the headline despite-commitments count is explicitly severed from "the licensing count for the structural claim." The article even names the move directly: "a tenet may remove a defeater but must not upgrade the evidence level" → "the framework may identify a cluster's significance but must not upgrade its independence score." It then weights the convergence argument using the *lower* (interpretive-significance) framework-independence score "not the higher (architectural-finding) one." This is the named pattern *tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating* executed in full.

`clinical-phenomenology` shows the same restraint at the cohort's other calibration-critical point. The convergence-argument section runs an explicit anti-double-counting discipline ("If a single underlying fact ... generates all four families of disruption, then the four are one observation reported four ways, not four independent confirmations"), *ranks rather than counts* the cross-cutting cases, and self-demotes its weakest case (pain asymbolia "is the weakest of the three and should be handled with care ... on its face, evidence *of* neural modularity rather than against it"). The interface-model section refuses a prediction race — "the honest claim is parsimony, not a prediction race" — which is precisely the discipline against treating defeater-removal as evidence-positive.

`consciousness-and-the-metaphysics-of-composition` is slightly more exposed but stays calibrated: the convergence on consciousness-as-composition-criterion is repeatedly hedged ("suggestive rather than probative: convergence is consistent with a shared truth and also with a shared blind spot"), the selection-effect objection is granted "real force" and "not fully neutralised," and Merricks' load-bearing premise is flagged as "contested." No tier-upgrade rides on the convergence.

Verdict: the two personas converge in praise on every passage where they could have conflicted. No possibility/probability slippage detected; no refine-draft flag warranted.

## Content Strengths

### introspection-architecture-independence-scoring.md
- **Strongest point**: The explicit two-tier framework-independence split — "high for the architectural finding, lower for the interpretive significance" — combined with the decision to weight the tenet argument using the *lower* score. This is the single cleanest operationalisation of the evidential-status discipline on the site.
- **Notable quote**: "the framework may identify a cluster's significance but must not upgrade its independence score."
- **Why it works**: It converts an abstract calibration rule into a concrete scoring decision with a visible cost (the lower weight), so the discipline is demonstrated rather than asserted.

### integration-as-activity.md
- **Strongest point**: "A Note on What the Convergence Shows" — the article distinguishes description-level convergence (which is load-bearing for the negative case against IIT) from the metaphysical reading (Map-specific, dualist), and explicitly says the four frameworks "should be cited for the description-level convergence they actually supply rather than for a metaphysical reading their authors would resist."
- **Notable quote**: "grammar is an unreliable guide to ontology — 'the sun rises' has subject-verb structure without the sun performing any rising."
- **Why it works**: It pre-empts the strongest objection (that the activity framing smuggles a subject in through grammar) and includes a Madhyamaka/process no-subject reading as a "genuine third alternative," which is rare intellectual honesty for a position the article ultimately rejects.

### higher-order-theories.md
- **Strongest point**: The treatment of Lau's PRM as "the strongest version of HOT precisely because it is the most mechanistically specific" — steelmanning the rival to its peak before locating the gap. The single-axis framing of HOT and illusionism ("either representing a state as conscious makes it conscious ... or we are systematically wrong about our own experience") is a genuinely clarifying structural insight.
- **Notable quote**: "the regress the self-reference paradox describes is not avoided but renamed."
- **Why it works**: The empirical dissociation table (normal / blindsight / blind insight / aPFC disruption) gives the philosophical argument an empirical spine and shows HOT failing its own predictions.

### consciousness-and-the-metaphysics-of-composition.md
- **Strongest point**: The convergence table (van Inwagen / Merricks / McQueen-Tsuchiya) made vivid by stressing how *much* the three disagree about everything else, paired with the immediate self-administered selection-effect objection.
- **Notable quote**: "Coordination is not composition."
- **Why it works**: The bidirectional-resistance framing (neither composable from below nor decomposable from above) is a memorable, LLM-friendly compression of a hard structural point.

### clinical-phenomenology-and-altered-experience.md
- **Strongest point**: The ranked (not counted) cross-cutting cases, with cognitive motor dissociation as "the cleanest anomaly" and pain asymbolia self-demoted as "the weakest." Evidence hygiene of the highest order.
- **Notable quote**: "the dialectical question is which framework needs the extra posit, not which can count more cases."
- **Why it works**: It pre-concedes the materialist's best move (modular neural architecture → modular experiential disruption) and reframes the contest as one of parsimony rather than prediction, which is both more honest and harder to dismiss.
