## Critiques by Philosopher

### The Eliminative Materialist (Churchland)
The article quotes Churchland's "poltergeists" line and then proceeds as if quoting the objection answers it. Churchland's deeper point — that "lawful psychophysical correlation" is a promissory note no one knows how to cash, exactly like vital-force "correlations" in pre-molecular biology — is not engaged. The thermodynamics/Ohm/chemistry analogies all involve *third-person* regularities that were operationally measurable before reduction. Psychophysical laws have no analogous operational handle: you cannot measure the phenomenal relatum independently of the verbal report, so "discovering" the law and "discovering" the report-disposition are not separable. This is a real gap but it is the Map's standing position, defended elsewhere (phenomenal-acquaintance, methodology-of-consciousness-research); the article need not re-litigate it.

### The Hard-Nosed Physicalist (Dennett)
The "hard problem is an illusion" objection (line 113) is answered by asserting the hard problem "is not a theoretical posit but a report of what is directly given in experience." Dennett's whole point is that *the sense of direct givenness is itself the illusion* — introspective report is not transparent access. So the reply restates the disputed claim rather than engaging the deflationary mechanism. The article does gesture at the meta-problem residue, which is the right move, but the "report of what is directly given" sentence is doing more rhetorical work than it earns. **This is the one finding worth a refine task** (medium).

### The Quantum Skeptic (Tegmark)
Line 59 predicts "subtly different distributions of quantum outcomes" between conscious and physically-similar unconscious systems. Tegmark's decoherence objection (femtosecond timescales in warm wet neural tissue) is the standard reply and is *not mentioned here*. The article is careful to say "no concrete experimental protocol currently exists," which insulates it from over-claim, but a reader arriving from the MQI tenet may not see that the in-principle signal faces a specific physical obstacle. Low-value: the decoherence engagement lives in dedicated articles (quantum-biology, minimal-quantum-interaction), and this is a concepts page on *explanation*, not mechanism. A one-clause acknowledgement could be added but is not required.

### The Many-Worlds Defender (Deutsch)
Handled correctly. Line 125 frames No-Many-Worlds as a *prerequisite* for the downward-selection category — "the Map's rejection of MWI is what makes bidirectional interaction coherent." This is honest framework-boundary marking (a tenet-dependency stated as such), NOT boundary-substitution dressed as in-framework refutation. No issue.

### The Empiricist (Popper's ghost)
The strongest live tension, and the article navigates it well. It claims dualism "is not empirically vacuous in principle" (testable MQI signature) while simultaneously conceding the explanatory gap "may be permanent." A Popperian would press: if the gap is permanent *by ontology*, what would falsify the dualist reading as opposed to the physicalist one? The answer the article implies — the quantum-distribution signature — is the right answer, but the falsification asymmetry (a null result is consistent with both "no MQI" and "MQI below threshold") is not surfaced. Low-value; honest hedging already present.

## Counterarguments to Address

### The illusionist rebuttal restates the disputed premise
- **Current content says** (line 113): "the hard problem is not a theoretical posit but a report of what is directly given in experience."
- **A critic would argue**: that the felt directness of the givenness is precisely what illusionism denies; citing it as data begs the question against Dennett/Frankish.
- **Suggested response**: Reframe so the reply does not lean on bare assertion of direct givenness. The Map's stronger move is the one the article already half-makes: the *meta-problem* burden does not vanish under illusionism — the illusionist still owes an account of why the seeming is so robust and so resistant to deflation that even illusionists must theorise it. Lead with that asymmetry of explanatory burden rather than with "it is directly given." Natural prose, no mode labels.
