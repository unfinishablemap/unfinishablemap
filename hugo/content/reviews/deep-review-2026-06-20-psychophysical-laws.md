## Critical Issues Found and Fixed

1. **Misquote presented as verbatim (Chalmers 1995).** The article quoted
   Chalmers as "Where we have new fundamental properties, we also have new
   fundamental laws." The verbatim text in "Facing Up to the Problem of
   Consciousness" (consc.net/papers/facing.html, §6) is **"Where there is a
   fundamental property, there are fundamental laws."** A paraphrase inside
   quote marks is a critical defect. **Fixed** to the verbatim wording.
   (The 2026-05-31 review listed this quote as "correct" — intra-corpus
   ratification of a misquote.)

2. **Orphan inline citation (Beck & Eccles 1992).** "Beck and Eccles (1992)"
   was cited inline with a specific year and mechanism (vesicle-tunneling
   exocytosis) but had **no References entry** — only Eccles (1994) *How the
   Self Controls Its Brain* was listed. Web-verified the real paper:
   Beck, F. & Eccles, J.C. (1992), "Quantum aspects of brain activity and the
   role of consciousness," *PNAS* 89(23), 11357-11361,
   doi:10.1073/pnas.89.23.11357. **Fixed** by adding the verified References
   entry (the inline cite and the attributed mechanism are both faithful — fix
   is to add the reference, not remove the cite). References renumbered 1-16.

3. **Near-orphan reference + imprecise attribution ("t-shirt problem").**
   Reference 11 (Schaffer, "Naturalistic Dualism and the Problem of the Physical
   Correlate") had no clean inline anchor, and the body said "What Schaffer
   calls the 't-shirt problem'." Web-verify shows the term is older (Schaffer
   attributes it to Adams 1987 / Latham 2000; the vivid t-shirt formulation is
   McLaughlin's) — Schaffer *presses* it against naturalistic dualism in the
   Physical-Correlate paper rather than coining it. **Fixed** to "The 't-shirt
   problem' Schaffer presses against naturalistic dualism … so named because the
   fundamental laws could not be written legibly on a t-shirt," which is
   accurate to the source and ties the body to Reference 11.
