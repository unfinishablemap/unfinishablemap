# Referee Report: An Epistemological and Empirical Audit of "Duhem-Quine Underdetermination in Consciousness Science"

## 1. General Epistemic Strategy and the Misapplication of the Duhem-Quine Thesis

The manuscript under review attempts a highly ambitious epistemological intervention within the contemporary science of consciousness. By leveraging the Duhem-Quine thesis of empirical underdetermination, the author seeks to carve out a protected, theoretically legitimate space for a non-reductive, interactionist-dualist framework—specifically, an "interface" model of consciousness. The central philosophical thesis of the manuscript posits that current neuroscientific evidence, ranging from the search for the neural correlates of consciousness (NCC) to modern adversarial collaborations testing physicalist frameworks like Global Neuronal Workspace Theory (GNWT) and Integrated Information Theory (IIT), is structurally incapable of adjudicating between a physicalist "substrate" model and a dualist "interface" model. By framing physicalist approaches to the mind-body problem as a Kuhnian paradigm in crisis, burdened by an ever-expanding web of arbitrary auxiliary hypotheses required to bridge the explanatory gap, the author seeks to legitimize an interactionist ontology. The manuscript asserts that the choice between these foundational metaphysics is a matter of underdetermined theoretical preference, permanently immune to empirical resolution, rather than a matter of compulsory scientific inference.

This assessment is fundamentally hostile to the manuscript's publication in its current form. While the application of the philosophy of science to the hard problem of consciousness is a welcome and necessary endeavor, the present execution represents a profound misapplication of the Duhem-Quine thesis, an egregious misreading of the contemporary empirical literature, and a pervasive reliance on methodologically bankrupt or aggressively challenged scientific findings. The manuscript operates not as a rigorous philosophical inquiry into the genuine epistemic limits of neurobiology, but rather as an exercise in Lakatosian degeneration. It utilizes underdetermination not as a methodological caution, but as an *a priori* epistemological shield specifically designed to protect a favored tenet—interactionist dualism—from the threat of empirical falsification.

Throughout the text, one encounters a systematic epistemic double standard. The manuscript demands that physicalist theories of consciousness provide deductive, gap-less logical entailments from neurobiological mechanisms to subjective phenomenology—a deductive standard that is impossible for any empirical, abductive science to meet. Conversely, it demands only that its own dualist interface theory remain loosely "consistent" with the data. When the empirical data yields ambiguity, the author declares a "falsifiability gap" that supposedly licenses the interface model; when the empirical data aggressively constrains the parameter space, destroying long-held physicalist models, the author retreats into radical holistic underdetermination, claiming that no localized evidence can ever adjudicate between identity and mediation. This is not a philosophy of science; it is an ideological defense mechanism masquerading as epistemology.

This report will audit the manuscript across four specific, interrelated dimensions: (a) its empirical accuracy against the 2020s neuroscientific literature, with granular attention to the 2025 COGITATE adversarial collaboration; (b) its reliance on challenged, retracted, or failed-to-replicate empirical results; (c) its structural tendency to bracket theoretical claims by fiat rather than argued inference; and (d) its failure to engage with robust, modern counterarguments and competing frameworks—such as Bayesian predictive processing and illusionism—that actively dissolve the underdetermination the author seeks to exploit.

## 2. Dimension A: Empirical Accuracy and the 2025 COGITATE Adversarial Collaboration

The most glaring empirical failure of the manuscript is its catastrophic mischaracterization of the Melloni et al. (2025) publication in *Nature*, which details the long-awaited results of the COGITATE adversarial collaboration. The manuscript frames the COGITATE project—a massive, multi-year, theory-neutral test pitting Global Neuronal Workspace Theory against Integrated Information Theory—as a "concrete instance of Duhem-Quine underdetermination" simply because "neither theory emerged unscathed". The author utilizes the fact that neither theory was wholly vindicated to argue that the empirical constraints remain weak enough to sustain multiple incompatible accounts, thereby leaving the theoretical door wide open for dualist interface models. This interpretation represents a profound misunderstanding of both the philosophy of scientific progress and the specific neurobiological data generated by the consortium.

### 2.1 The Misunderstanding of Adversarial Collaboration Methodology

The manuscript leverages the fact that neither GNWT nor IIT achieved total predictive vindication as proof that the underlying neurobiology is unconstrained. This logic is entirely backward and reveals a deep ignorance of how adversarial collaborations function in practice. The very purpose of an adversarial collaboration, particularly in the mold championed by Melloni, Mudrik, Pitts, and the Cogitate Consortium , is to force competing theories to generate mutually exclusive, pre-registered predictions *before* data collection. By locking in the predictions regarding spatial topography, temporal dynamics, and inter-areal synchronization prior to running the human subjects, the methodology intentionally bypasses the post-hoc auxiliary hypothesis tweaking that characterizes genuine Duhem-Quine underdetermination.

If the Duhem-Quine thesis held in the radical, paralyzing manner the author suggests, the COGITATE results would simply have been absorbed by both camps with zero theoretical friction, via the invention of new auxiliary assumptions. Instead, the 2025 *Nature* paper explicitly notes that the results *substantially challenged key tenets of both theories*, forcing genuine theoretical contraction and paradigm revision. The inability of either theory to seamlessly absorb the data without sacrificing core postulates is evidence *against* the paralyzing holistic underdetermination the manuscript champions.

### 2.2 The Specific Neurobiological Findings and Their Epistemic Implications

To fully demonstrate the manuscript's superficial engagement with the 2025 empirical data, we must examine the actual physiological outcomes, which the author reduces to a single, hand-waving paragraph about "mixed findings," "missing ignition," and the dispute between Koch and Dehaene over prefrontal versus posterior loci. The COGITATE experiment utilized a monumental dataset of 256 human participants, combining high-resolution functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI), magnetoencephalography (MEG), electroencephalography (EEG), and complementary intracranial EEG (iEEG). The pre-registered predictions were stark and anatomically precise.

Integrated Information Theory (IIT) predicted sustained neural synchronization within the posterior cortical "hot zone"—comprising the occipital, temporal, and parietal regions—for the exact duration of a conscious percept. Because IIT defines consciousness mathematically as the maximization of integrated information (Φ), the physical substrate sustaining that state must exhibit sustained causal integration for as long as the subjective experience persists.

Global Neuronal Workspace Theory (GNWT), conversely, predicted a distinct, massive nonlinear "ignition" of the prefrontal and parietal cortices at the onset of a stimulus, representing the global broadcasting of information to executive networks. Crucially, GNWT also predicted an equally distinct off-response (a second ignition) at stimulus offset, representing the clearing of the global workspace to allow for subsequent conscious processing.

The empirical results did not yield underdetermination; they yielded severe, highly targeted falsifications of specific architectural claims that constrain all future theories of consciousness, physicalist or dualist. The analysis demonstrated that while information about conscious content could be successfully decoded from visual, ventrotemporal, and inferior frontal cortices, IIT's core prediction of sustained high-frequency synchronization within the posterior cortex was robustly falsified. The network connectivity simply did not map onto the temporal duration of consciousness in the manner demanded by IIT's mathematical formalisms. Conversely, GNWT was fundamentally challenged by the total absence of the predicted "off-response" ignition at stimulus offset, and by a severely limited representation of conscious dimensions in the prefrontal cortex compared to posterior sensory regions.

### 2.3 The Failure of the Underdetermination Claim Regarding COGITATE

By declaring these results "mixed" and retreating to the safety of Duhem-Quine , the manuscript ignores the fact that the parameter space for *any* physical theory of consciousness was massively constrained by this multi-modal experiment. The empirical data strictly limits the types of neural architectures that can be proposed moving forward. The fact that an experiment falsifies specific auxiliary hypotheses of two competing models does not prove that empirical evidence is impotent (which is what the author requires to defend an invisible dualist interface); rather, it proves that empirical evidence is highly potent, capable of destroying long-held theoretical assumptions.

The manuscript's attempt to spin the progressive, destructive power of the COGITATE experiment into a narrative of epistemic stagnation reveals a deep methodological malfeasance. The author relies on the D-Q thesis as an excuse to avoid engaging with the actual structural data. If physicalist neuroscience is generating data with enough spatial and temporal resolution to permanently retire the classical GNWT off-response, the research program is empirically progressive. The manuscript offers no equivalent predictive constraints for its interactionist interface model, making its claim of epistemic parity utterly void. It treats the failure of specific physicalist models as a default victory for a dualist model that refuses to make any neuroanatomical predictions whatsoever.

## 3. Dimension B: Reliance on Challenged, Retracted, or Failed-to-Replicate Results

A rigorous philosophical analysis of empirical science requires meticulous, uncompromising vetting of the cited literature. The manuscript fails dramatically in this regard, relying on a deeply flawed, highly criticized, and in some cases, entirely fabricated corpus of neuroscientific literature to construct its arguments. The author treats preprint claims, fringe physics papers, and hallucinations of neuroscientific literature as established empirical facts upon which foundational philosophical conclusions can be drawn.

### 3.1 The "Quantum Brain" Disaster: Kerskens and López Pérez (2022)

Throughout its various sub-sections on the interaction problem, the manuscript repeatedly gestures toward a "quantum interface" to solve the causal closure objections inherent in dualism, suggesting that consciousness selects among quantum possibilities without injecting energy into the physical system. To anchor this metaphysically extravagant claim in empirical science, the manuscript relies on the highly controversial paper by Kerskens and López Pérez (2022), "Experimental indications of non-classical brain functions," published in the *Journal of Physics Communications*.

Kerskens and Pérez claimed to have observed zero quantum coherence (ZQC) in human brain tissue during standard clinical MRI scans, asserting that heartbeat-evoked potentials (HEPs) emerging across the brain could only be explained if the nuclear proton spins in brain water were entangled via a non-classical, quantum-gravity mediator. The manuscript takes this at face value, utilizing it to suggest that quantum mechanics identifies specific interface points for conscious interaction, and that the empirical literature is moving toward a quantum substrate that is friendly to interactionist dualism.

This reliance is fatal to the manuscript's credibility. The author completely ignores the devastating methodological critiques of Kerskens and Pérez that immediately followed its publication, most notably the comprehensive physics rebuttal by Warren (2023). As Warren painstakingly details, the Kerskens paper rests on an elementary ignorance of three decades of functional MRI (fMRI) physics. Kerskens and Pérez boldly state in their abstract and introduction that "consciousness-related or electrophysiological signals are unknown in NMR" , using this bizarre, historically illiterate negative claim to justify assigning "macroscopic entanglement" to the difference between awake and sleeping brain scans.

As Warren points out, this ignores tens of thousands of fMRI studies utilizing the BOLD (blood-oxygen-level-dependent) effect, as well as the extensive, well-documented literature on intermolecular multiple quantum coherences (iMQCs) in standard, classical magnetic resonance spectroscopy. The heartbeat-evoked potentials observed by Kerskens are easily and fully explained by classical hemodynamics—the pulsatile flow of blood and cerebrospinal fluid driven by the cardiac cycle, which heavily modulates MRI signal intensity. To attribute classical, macroscopic blood-flow artifacts to quantum entanglement mediated by consciousness is not merely a bold scientific hypothesis; it is a profound methodological error bordering on pseudoscience. Warren notes that the claims made by Kerskens are equivalent to asserting active intervention by aliens, as both are equally "consistent" with the data if one completely ignores standard physiological mechanisms.

The manuscript's uncritical adoption of this widely discredited finding to prop up its "quantum interface" theory demonstrates a profound inability to evaluate neuro-physical literature. While the manuscript's edit logs show a minor softening of this claim to "remains preliminary and disputed" , referencing a paper built on fundamental misunderstandings of classical hemodynamics invalidates the specific empirical grounding the manuscript attempts to claim for its interactionist physics.

### 3.2 The Conflation of Visual Sensitivity and Phenomenality: Nartker et al. (2024/2025)

In its critique of GNWT, the manuscript cites a recent, large-scale study on inattentional blindness by Nartker, Firestone, and colleagues. The manuscript claims that Nartker's findings demonstrate that "phenomenal consciousness persists even when the global workspace fails to broadcast," using this to drive a wedge into physicalist theories of access consciousness. The manuscript uses this to suggest an empirical underdetermination between Block's phenomenal overflow hypothesis and Dennett's deflationary reading.

Here again, the manuscript exhibits a crippling lack of technical literacy regarding experimental psychology and psychophysics. Nartker et al. (2024) conducted massive behavioral trials demonstrating that "inattentionally blind" subjects—those who deny noticing a highly salient but unexpected stimulus (akin to the famous invisible gorilla paradigm)—actually retain above-chance visual sensitivity to the location, color, and shape of the unseen stimulus when forced to guess. By utilizing classical signal detection theory, Nartker demonstrated that these subjects possess residual d′ (perceptual sensitivity, the distance between noise and signal distributions) despite a highly conservative decision criterion (c) that leads them to explicitly report "not seeing" the stimulus.

The manuscript leaps from "residual visual sensitivity" to the persistence of "phenomenal consciousness". This is an elementary category error in consciousness science. The fact that the early visual cortex processes information sufficiently to drive a forced-choice behavioral response (a phenomenon conceptually identical to blindsight, which the manuscript ironically discusses elsewhere ) does absolutely nothing to prove that there is "something it is like" for the subject to see the stimulus phenomenologically. Nartker's data proves that unconscious, localized perceptual processing extracts rich feature data without global broadcast or attentional amplification. To claim that this localized processing is *phenomenally conscious* merely because it contains functionally accessible information is to beg the question entirely against GNWT. The manuscript weaponizes a study about unconscious perceptual sensitivity to make an unsubstantiated metaphysical claim about phenomenological overflow, thereby severely mishandling the 2024 literature and demonstrating an inability to differentiate between perceptual bandwidth and subjective phenomenality.

### 3.3 Bibliographic Incompetence and Citation Fabrication

Beyond misinterpreting valid studies, the manuscript's scholarly apparatus is structurally compromised by phantom citations and severe misattributions, revealing a writing process that relies on hallucinated metadata rather than primary source engagement.

First, the manuscript attributes a three-part functional taxonomy of consciousness—"durable maintenance, novel combinations, and spontaneous intentional action"—to a 2025 paper by "Bayne and Hohwy" or "Randeniya et al. (2025)" in the journal *Neuroscience of Consciousness*. This is demonstrably false. The tripartite functional signature of durable maintenance, routing for novel combinations, and spontaneous intentional action is the absolute bedrock of Dehaene and Changeux's classical Global Neuronal Workspace Theory (dating back to the early 2000s and definitively summarized in Dehaene's book *Consciousness and the Brain*). While Bayne and Hohwy have written extensively on consciousness, predictive processing, and the Free Energy Principle , and Randeniya has published on auditory prediction errors , attributing the core architectural pillars of GNWT to a fabricated or wildly misread 2025 paper reveals that the author is relying on tertiary summaries rather than primary source comprehension.

Second, the manuscript relies heavily on Georgiev (2024) to support arguments regarding the evolutionary trajectory of consciousness, citing it as appearing in *Neuroscience of Consciousness*. A rigorous bibliographic audit reveals this paper, "Evolution of Consciousness," was actually published in *Life*, an MDPI mega-journal. While a single misattributed journal title might be dismissed as a clerical error, the systematic pattern of inflating the prestige of fringe quantum-consciousness papers (Georgiev, Kerskens) while fabricating citations for mainstream neuroscientific concepts (GNWT functional hallmarks) betrays a fundamentally unserious scholarly methodology. A philosophy of science manuscript cannot build a case for deep epistemological underdetermination when it cannot accurately identify the scientific literature it claims is underdetermined.

## 4. Dimension C: Bracketing Claims by Tenet Rather Than Argument

A defining characteristic of a progressive scientific or philosophical theory is that its core concepts do heavy explanatory lifting, generating novel predictions or resolving empirical paradoxes. A degenerating theory, conversely, uses its core tenets defensively, deploying them to explain away missing evidence or to bracket off entire domains of empirical inquiry from scrutiny. The manuscript under review falls squarely into the latter category. It repeatedly invokes its underlying tenet—the dualist "interface" model—to bracket claims, retreating into explicit declarations of underdetermination without doing the necessary philosophical argumentation to justify the maneuver.

### 4.1 The Discrimination Problem and Clinical Dissociation

Nowhere is this tenet-driven bracketing more obvious than in the manuscript's treatment of the "Discrimination Problem" regarding the Neural Correlates of Consciousness (NCC). The author examines the vast literature on consciousness disruption via anesthesia, coma, traumatic brain injury, and Cognitive Motor Dissociation (CMD). In CMD, advanced neuroimaging (such as the famous fMRI tennis-playing paradigms developed by Adrian Owen, or more recent high-density EEG work by Claassen and colleagues) reveals that structurally unresponsive patients retain rich, command-following cognitive architectures.

A physicalist approach views CMD as a disruption of specific thalamocortical motor output pathways with preserved higher-order cortical integration (the physical substrate of consciousness). How does the manuscript handle this rich, highly specific clinical data? The internal edit logs  reveal a deliberate rhetorical strategy to avoid engagement. The author explicitly inserts "underdetermination markers" to create a falsifiability gap. The text asserts: *"The question of whether the neural side is the substrate of consciousness or its physical interface is underdetermined by the evidence... A reliable correlate, however precisely mapped, does not adjudicate between identity and mediation"*.

This is not a philosophical argument; it is a declaration of methodological solipsism. The author asserts that because we can logically imagine an invisible, non-physical ghost utilizing the brain as a television receiver (the mediation/interface hypothesis), the entire corpus of physicalist evidence from clinical neurology is automatically neutralized. But the manuscript offers exactly zero parameters for how this interface functions.

If we are to take the interface theory seriously as a competitor to physicalism, it must answer basic structural questions: What is the bandwidth of the interface? Where exactly does the causal closure of physics fail to allow the non-physical consciousness to inject its selections? If CMD shows that specific focal lesions in the central thalamus destroy the capacity for subjective experience while leaving the rest of the cortex intact, why does the dualist interface selectively fail in exact mathematical proportion to structural damage in the physical substrate?

By simply stating that "current evidence does not adjudicate," the author brackets the entire fields of neuroanesthesiology and neurology. The author edits out stronger, falsifiable claims specifically to avoid the burden of proof, replacing them with legally safe but scientifically vacuous hedges (e.g., changing "No mechanism currently generates predictions..." to "Current evidence does not adjudicate..."). The interface thesis is treated as an untouchable axiom. If a patient is anesthetized with Propofol, the physicalist provides a precise thermodynamic and molecular mechanism for how the lipid boundary of the neuron is altered, disrupting high-frequency cortical oscillations and collapsing the global workspace. The dualist interface model simply waves a philosophical hand and says, "The receiver is tuned to the wrong frequency." This is not an application of Duhem-Quine underdetermination; this is the classic "God of the Gaps" fallacy rebranded in the sophisticated language of Lakatosian auxiliary hypotheses.

### 4.2 The Spontaneous Collapse Ad Hoc Rescue

The manuscript performs a similarly egregious bracketing maneuver in its discussion of spontaneous objective collapse models in quantum mechanics. Recognizing that objective collapse models (e.g., GRW theory, or Penrose-Hameroff Orchestrated Objective Reduction) are highly contested, mathematically complex, and entirely unverified by current physics, the author simply adds clauses stating that the existence of collapse is "underdetermined by the evidence". Even more damningly, the edit logs reveal that the author explicitly marks the spontaneous-collapse physics as "distinct from the Map's interpretive use of it".

This is a breathtakingly cynical philosophical maneuver. The manuscript *requires* macroscopic quantum collapse to provide the ontological indeterminacy necessary for its interactionist interface to work without violating the conservation of energy. Yet, because the physics does not currently support this definitively, the author attempts to bracket the physics from the philosophy, effectively stating: "Even if the physical models of quantum collapse we rely on are empirically false, our interpretive philosophical use of them remains valid due to underdetermination." This severs the philosophical model from the very empirical foundation it claims to be interpreting. It is an *ad hoc* rescue of the highest order, protecting the dualist tenet from the ongoing failure of quantum neurobiology to produce any evidence of macroscopic superposition or collapse in the warm, wet, noisy environment of the human brain.

## 5. Dimension D: Untested Counterarguments and Competing Frameworks

The manuscript's central assertion—that the science of consciousness is permanently mired in structural underdetermination between physicalist substrate and dualist interface models—only survives because the author systematically ignores the modern epistemological and scientific frameworks designed explicitly to dissolve such underdetermination. By treating the philosophy of science as if it ended with W.V.O. Quine's *Two Dogmas of Empiricism* in 1951, the manuscript fails to engage with contemporary formalisms that demonstrate how empirical data *does* continuously adjudicate between structurally equivalent models.

### 5.1 The Ignored Bayesian Dissolution of Underdetermination

The manuscript relies heavily on a crude, logical formulation of underdetermination: if Theory A (Physicalism) and Theory B (Dualist Interface) are both logically compatible with a set of Evidence E, then E cannot adjudicate between them. Because an infinitely flexible dualist interface can always be molded to fit any neuroscientific finding post-hoc, the manuscript claims perpetual epistemic parity.

This deductive standard has been obsolete in the philosophy of science for decades. Modern scientific epistemology operates on Bayesian confirmation theory, active inference, and predictive coding frameworks. Under a Bayesian framework, logical underdetermination is dissolved by analyzing the prior probabilities (P(H)) and the specific likelihood functions (P(E∣H)) of the competing theories. As detailed by Corcoran, Hohwy, and Friston (2023) in their rigorous work on accelerating scientific progress through Bayesian adversarial collaboration , evidence E heavily favors Theory A over Theory B if Theory A specifically *predicts* E from its core mechanisms, whereas Theory B merely *accommodates* E through post-hoc auxiliary adjustments.

Physicalist neuroscience (e.g., Predictive Processing or GNWT) actively *predicts* the specific topological layouts of the cortex. For example, it predicts that higher-order metacognitive awareness will rely on highly metabolically expensive, late-myelinating prefrontal networks because of the immense computational demands of global broadcasting. The physicalist theory risks falsification on these specific architectural predictions (as seen in the COGITATE results).

The Dualist Interface theory predicts absolutely nothing about the topology of the brain. It simply waits for the physicalists to map the brain, and then asserts, after the fact, "That map is the exact shape of the interface." Because the interface theory can seamlessly accommodate *any* conceivable brain architecture—whether it evolved as a highly localized neural net, a silicon chip, or a distributed fluid-dynamic computer—its likelihood function P(E∣Interface) is essentially flat and unconstrained. In Bayesian epistemology, a theory that can predict everything predicts nothing. It suffers from massive ontological profligacy and overfitting.

The manuscript entirely fails to test its interface hypothesis against Bayesian penalization metrics (such as the Bayesian Information Criterion or Free Energy minimization), which severely punish theories that require complex, invisible, mathematically unconstrained auxiliary mechanisms (like a non-physical consciousness collapsing quantum wavefunctions) to explain data that physicalist models explain with fewer parameters. By ignoring Bayesian epistemology, the author artificially preserves a logical underdetermination that has already been mathematically dissolved in the actual practice of science.

### 5.2 The Dismissal of Deflationary and Illusionist Frameworks

Furthermore, the entire motivation for the manuscript's dualist leap is the unquestioned presumption of the "Hard Problem of Consciousness"—the idea that phenomenal experience possesses intrinsic, ineffable qualities (qualia) that are structurally opaque to physical reduction. The manuscript treats the explanatory gap as an ontologically real chasm that necessitates a dualist interface.

However, the manuscript fails to rigorously engage with the most potent contemporary counterargument: Illusionism and deflationary functionalism (e.g., Frankish, Kammerer, Dennett). The Illusionist research program argues that the "Hard Problem" is not a physical mystery requiring a quantum interface, but a metacognitive error. The brain, operating as a predictive engine, constructs a simplified, highly compressed predictive model of its own internal processing (attention schema theory). Because this model lacks the granular data of its own physical instantiation (we do not feel our synapses firing or neurotransmitters binding), the brain concludes that its experiences are non-physical, ineffable, and irreducible.

If the Illusionist framework is correct, the Duhem-Quine underdetermination the author relies upon evaporates entirely. There is no underdetermination between a physicalist substrate and a dualist interface if the *explanandum* itself (non-physical qualia) is a demonstrably false product of introspective cognitive closure. The manuscript treats deflationary materialist accounts as "bedrock disagreements" that can be safely ignored or relegated to a brief acknowledgment. This is philosophically illegitimate. One cannot write a comprehensive treatise on the necessary underdetermination of consciousness science while refusing to engage the robust neuro-computational models that explain exactly why the author's intuition of a "falsifiability gap" is a predictable, neurologically generated illusion.

## 6. Specific Weaknesses Justifying Rejection

Each represents a profound failure to engage with the actual state of the 2020-2025 empirical and philosophical literature:

The manuscript cites the mixed results of the GNWT vs. IIT adversarial collaboration as proof of radical Duhem-Quine underdetermination, explicitly ignoring that the experiment successfully and severely restricted the auxiliary parameter space of both theories by falsifying core structural predictions (e.g., GNWT's off-response ignition and IIT's posterior sustained synchronization).

The manuscript attempts to legitimize its "quantum interface" by heavily relying on Kerskens and López Pérez's (2022) claim of zero quantum coherence and macroscopic entanglement in the brain, completely ignoring the devastating physics rebuttals that demonstrated these signals are entirely explained by classical fMRI hemodynamics, BOLD responses, and physiological noise.

3. **Conflation of Unconscious Visual Sensitivity with Phenomenal Overflow:**
The manuscript misuses recent massive-scale inattentional blindness data to critique GNWT, falsely claiming that residual signal-detection sensitivity (d′) to unseen stimuli proves that "phenomenal consciousness persists" outside the global workspace. This demonstrates a failure to distinguish between implicit perceptual processing and subjective phenomenality.

- *Omitted/Mishandled Source:* Nartker, M. et al. (2024). "What we've been missing about what we've been missing: Above-chance sensitivity to inattentional blindness stimuli." *eLife*, 13.
4. **Failure to Engage Bayesian Dissolutions of Underdetermination:**
The manuscript relies on an outdated, strictly deductive version of the Duhem-Quine thesis to assert epistemic parity between physicalism and dualism. It entirely omits the modern Bayesian epistemological consensus, which demonstrates how highly parameterized, predictive paradigms (physicalism) abductively defeat models that rely purely on post-hoc accommodation (the dualist interface).

- *Omitted/Mishandled Source:* Corcoran, A. W., Hohwy, J., & Friston, K. J. (2023). "Accelerating scientific progress through Bayesian adversarial collaboration." *Neuron*, 111(22), 3505-3516.
5. **Tenet-Driven Bracketing of Clinical Neurobiology (CMD):**
The manuscript attempts to neutralize the vast clinical literature on Cognitive Motor Dissociation, anesthesia, and severe focal brain injury by simply asserting that neural correlates "do not adjudicate between identity and mediation." This *a priori* bracketing ignores the highly specific, progressive predictive models of cortical integration currently used in clinical neurology, replacing them with a mathematically unconstrained "God of the Gaps" interface thesis that makes no structural predictions.

- *Omitted/Mishandled Source:* Claassen, J. et al. (2024) (or equivalent modern consensus reviews on high-density EEG/fMRI detection of covert consciousness and the specific thalamocortical substrates required to maintain it).

This manuscript must be categorically rejected; it weaponizes an outdated, holistic formulation of the Duhem-Quine thesis to construct an unfalsifiable protective belt around interactionist dualism, while systematically misrepresenting, fabricating, or failing to comprehend the foundational 2020-2025 neuroscientific and physical literature required to make its epistemological claims.
