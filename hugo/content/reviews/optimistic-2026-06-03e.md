## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The article takes the explanatory gap as the *enabling condition* for its value theory rather than re-arguing it: "if fundamental it needs no external grounding." Chalmers would applaud that the hard-problem irreducibility is doing real work (consciousness as an independent foundation for value) rather than being recited as decoration. The falsification clause "if a completed neuroscience explained *why* there is something it is like to be conscious in purely physical terms" correctly names the one result that would strip the foundation — exactly the Chalmersian bet.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
Stapp would welcome that the weight-of-choice argument routes through Bidirectional Interaction and a quantum-biasing candidate mechanism — *and* would not be embarrassed by it, because the article flags the proposal as "a candidate mechanism rather than a fact the argument leans on," explicitly subordinated to the open decoherence question. The decoupling "The weight-of-choice claim does not *need* the quantum apparatus" is precisely the move that lets a quantum-mind reading remain live without over-claiming.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
Strong showing. The Nagel handling is the article's most careful piece of intellectual history: it explicitly *rescues* "The Absurd" (1971) from the cosmic-smallness misreading ("being tiny or short-lived does not generate absurdity") and relocates the absurd in the structural feature of reflective consciousness — then notes the diagnosis "sits closer to the Map than to nihilism." Centering the only-actual-perspectives-are-conscious point ("the universe *as such* has no point of view") is the view-from-nowhere critique done right.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The Whitehead section is a model of restraint: "Whitehead's process philosophy offers a congenial model, not an argument the Map relies on… Both views assume rather than prove this, so the resonance is illustrative." This is exactly the calibration the persona's constraint demands — process-philosophical resonance is named as *illustrative compatibility*, never as the load-bearing support for any evidential upgrade. The "actual occasions… each a unique achievement of value" framing is praiseworthy *as* resonance and is explicitly walled off from doing argumentative work.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
Kane would be satisfied that the luck objection is met head-on rather than dodged: "if a choice is not fixed by prior causes, what makes it *yours* rather than mere noise?" answered by the agent-causal reply ("the persisting subject directly authors the choice rather than chance squeezing it out"). Crucially, the article does not *need* him to be right — it grants the compatibilist "can accept most of this article," positioning indeterminism as deepening rather than as a load-bearing premise. That is agency taken seriously without hostage-taking.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
The "Relation to Site Perspective" close invokes mysterianism directly — "we may be cognitively closed as to *how* consciousness grounds meaning" — and the article models that humility throughout via its framework-boundary vocabulary. McGinn would note approvingly that the view is held as falsifiable (six explicit defeat conditions) rather than as a closed system.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This is the article's strongest calibration achievement and the load-bearing persona for this review, because the whole piece advances a *contested* value-realist position. Three things the article does that this persona exists to praise:

1. **Tenet-as-evidence-upgrade is praise-worthily NOT done.** The article had every structural opportunity to present "consciousness is fundamental, therefore its value is established" as a result. It declines: the nihilist "denies exactly this identity, so the Map carries it openly as a premise rather than establishing it against the nihilist's own commitments." Rawlette's identity thesis is explicitly flagged as "a live, contested position the Map builds on, not settled territory" and "lightly engaged in mainstream metaethics." That is *"tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating"* practiced, not merely preached.

2. **The five-register separation is structural, not decorative.** The "What Each Claim Rests On" section is a textbook unbundling: core thesis (argued, load-bearing) vs. Dualism (assumed) vs. weight-of-choice (rests on Bidirectional Interaction + speculative quantum candidate) vs. No-MWI (framework-boundary commitment the Map "cannot establish on MWI's own resources"). Each opponent (Metz, Benatar, Nagel, Olson/Mackie, Street, Frankish) is met with an explicit "where this stops short of refutation the residue is terminological / a framework-boundary disagreement" — registers kept separate every time.

3. **The honest concessions are the spine, not the footnotes.** "This answers the tracking horn but not the adaptive-link horn, and honesty requires saying so" (Street); "the hardest is *motivation*… discharges irreducible normativity only by accepting the identity thesis the error theorist rejects" (Olson); "the Map may be grounding *lived significance* rather than Metz's 'meaning' — a boundary marked, not argued away" (Metz). Naming where the contested identity thesis is the only thing carrying the reply — rather than smuggling it as established — is the restraint this persona rewards.

**Convergence verdict:** the Process Philosopher and Hardline Empiricist praise *different aspects without conflict* here (Whitehead's resonance is sealed off as illustrative; the value-realism is carried as a contested premise). That convergence is the diagnostic that the possibility/probability tension is honestly resolved — no calibration concern to flag, no refine-draft warranted on evidential grounds.
