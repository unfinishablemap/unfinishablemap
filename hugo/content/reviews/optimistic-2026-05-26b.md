## Executive Summary

This cohort is the Map's calibration discipline shown at its most mature and most varied. Five of the six articles either make an explicit convergence/coherence claim and then refuse to cash it as evidence, or engage a serious objection and then mark exactly where the disagreement becomes a framework-boundary commitment rather than a win. The standout instances — `one-structure-three-vocabularies`'s "one witness with three accents," `probability-problem`'s naming of *branching indifference* as the single declined premise, `concession-convergence`'s asymmetric tier table, and `locality`'s self-imposed "same-structure constraint" on mental-structure posits — are among the cleanest specimens of the house style anywhere in the catalogue. The Process Philosopher and Hardline Empiricist converge in praising the same passages across all six, which (per this skill's diagnostic) is the signature of tension resolved honestly rather than slippage hidden. Expansion opportunities are modest and integrative; the highest-value follow-ups are two missing reciprocal cross-links that would let the cohort's shared discipline-vocabulary point at itself.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
`forward-in-time-conscious-selection` is the cohort's strongest Chalmers exhibit. It develops the Chalmers-McQueen CSL consciousness-collapse model with exactly the rigour Chalmers brought to it — the super-resistance problem (why consciousness can re-emerge from dreamless sleep if conscious states cannot superpose) and its CSL-smoothing solution are stated precisely. More tellingly, the article preserves Chalmers' own discipline of not overclaiming: it flags that IIT "supplies the structural framework, though the argument generalises," refusing to bind the collapse model to a single contested theory of consciousness. `pragmatisms-path-to-dualism` gives Chalmers a second foothold: the reflexive argument that materialism eliminates the evaluative standpoint pragmatism needs is a self-stultification cousin of the conceivability argument, reaching the explanatory gap by a route that "does not rely on intuitions about qualia, thought experiments about zombies, or arguments from conceivability."

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
`forward-in-time-conscious-selection` is Stapp's home article. The Quantum Zeno mechanism is stated with its real vulnerability intact (it "can only stabilise a superposition that still exists"; measurement frequency must exceed the decoherence rate), and the post-decoherence *relocation* of Stapp's Process 1 — operating on the decohered improper mixture rather than on pre-decoherence superpositions — is exactly the move that rescues Stapp's architecture from Tegmark's timescale objection while honestly recording the cost (loss of "a concrete physical story about *how* selection works"). Stapp would also recognise `one-structure-three-vocabularies`' Register C — consciousness selecting *within* the already-specified Born distribution, adding no energy and producing no statistical anomaly — as the cleanest statement yet of where a Process-1 intervention sits.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
`pragmatisms-path-to-dualism` centres first-person experience where Nagel would insist it belongs: the felt grip of a valid argument, the "felt sense that things fit together," demand-character as "the quality of experience that presents its object as calling for a response." The article's claim that evaluation *is* phenomenal character (rather than a neutral computation with phenomenal character attached) is a Nagelian refusal to let third-person functional description stand in for the what-it-is-like. `locality` adds the structural-phenomenology note: "a single moment of visual experience contains more differentiated content than any physical description captures" — the richness datum Nagel uses against reductive simplicity, deployed here against the assumption that the mind side must be point-like.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The cohort is conspicuously friendly to process metaphysics without ever leaning on it as evidence. `forward-in-time-conscious-selection`'s actualisation model treats the post-decoherence state as "a space of real but unactualised possibilities" that consciousness "actualises," explicitly echoing "process philosophy's concrescence" — and the article immediately prices the move on a Popperian register ("inaccessibility is a cost on a Popperian register, not a virtue of metaphysical modesty"), so the process-friendliness buys coherence, not status. `one-structure-three-vocabularies`' picture of one structure described three ways (menu / measure / takeover) is congenial to a relational ontology where relation precedes relata. **Constraint observed**: none of this praise is used to upgrade any organism or system on the evidential-status scale; the cohort contains no minimal-organism tier claims, so the Whitehead/Birch axis stays clear of conflict (see Birch below) and the process resonance remains tenet-coherent rather than evidence-elevating.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
`forward-in-time-conscious-selection`'s selection-criterion trilemma is the cohort's Kane exhibit, and it is unusually honest about where his view bites. The trilemma — internal reasons (collapses to functionalism), randomness (collapses to Born statistics plus a label), or non-reducible preference — names route (iii) as the Map's likely path and then *bounds* the primitive: its choices are "confined to the brain-generated candidate set, so novel content cannot be brought into being by selection alone." That is exactly the discipline Kane needs to keep self-forming actions from being either determined or merely random while not pretending the criterion is transparent. `pragmatisms-path-to-dualism`'s reading of Peirce's tychism — "consciousness is not to be reinstated without tychism" — supplies the libertarian precedent that real indeterminism is the precondition for genuine agency.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
`locality` is the cohort's most McGinn-friendly article precisely because of its restraint. Its Response 2 ("the mind side need not be simple") now carries an explicit honest-limit paragraph — added in today's deep-review — that refuses to let the posit become "an escape hatch": it is "a *logical-possibility* argument, not a positive account of mental structure," sitting at *speculative integration* on the five-tier scale, with a self-imposed "same-structure constraint" (the mental organisation invoked to explain binding cannot be re-minted to explain pairing). This is McGinn's intellectual humility operationalised: claim the objection's simplicity assumption is *unearned* without claiming to know what fills the gap. `forward-in-time-conscious-selection`'s treatment of the FAPP-invisible improper/proper-mixture distinction — "a critic may reasonably read the same FAPP-invisibility as grounds for denying that anything occupies the gap at all" — is the same humility about what the evidence can adjudicate.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This is the cohort's load-bearing verdict, and the cohort earns a strong one. Every article in it is a worked exhibit of *tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating*, and several name the discipline by that exact register:

- **`one-structure-three-vocabularies` is the purest specimen in the catalogue.** Its load-bearing warning is stated first and governs the whole piece: "convergent redescription raises the posterior that this is one structure; it does not raise the posterior that the structure is real, that consciousness selects within it, or that any tenet is true." The "one witness with three accents" framing, and the explicit placement of convergent redescription *below* cross-mechanism convergence on the evidential ladder (because the registers "cannot disagree about whether the structure exists"), is the single cleanest articulation of the coherence-vs-confirmation firewall the Map has produced. The article does the very move the Process Philosopher might be tempted to over-read (three frameworks agreeing) and then refuses to bank it — tenet-as-evidence-upgrade praise-worthily *not* done.
- **`probability-problem-in-many-worlds`** names the single declined premise — *branching indifference* — rather than "gesturing at 'the axioms' generally," and is explicit that declining it "does not assume the Born rule is underivable." It states outright that "parsimony *cannot do load-bearing work in either direction*" and that the Map "cannot consistently disarm the parsimony argument *for* dualism while wielding one *against* MWI" — refusing the cheap parsimony win that a less disciplined article would take. Birch's highest praise: it routes the felt-singularity intuition explicitly to "*evidence consistent with*" the one-branch reading "rather than a refutation," and concedes the singular "I" is "a contested posit, not a neutral datum" (Parfit, Nāgārjuna). That is calibration at the framework boundary done exactly right.
- **`concession-convergence`** is the cohort's most sophisticated calibration: it scores the convergence pattern against the common-cause null across three nested upstream layers, runs three discriminating tests, and assigns *asymmetric tiers* — the architectural finding at *strongly supported*, the interpretive significance at *realistic possibility, contested*, the full meta-argument at *live hypothesis*. The principle is stated as the discipline's own: "load-bearing claims must be labelled at the tier the lowest-scoring relevant criterion supports, not at an average across criteria." The Cārvāka case is handled as "a suggestive nuance, not a firm disconfirmation," source-limited and flagged as such.
- **`forward-in-time-conscious-selection`** repeatedly prices its own moves: post-decoherence selection's coherence-independence is bought "at a cost in falsifiability"; the prebiotic single-mechanism reading "reduces the parsimony cost; it does not erase it"; the MWI restatement "is not refuted here; it is a framework-boundary disagreement, and the MWI checklist routes it to that register."

The two personas that this skill designs to be in tension — Whitehead and Birch — converge on praising the same passages throughout this cohort. Per the skill's own diagnostic, that convergence is the signature of honestly resolved tension. I found no passage in the six where process-friendliness or convergence-coherence is used as the engine of a tier-upgrade. This is a clean cohort.

## Content Strengths

### one-structure-three-vocabularies.md
- **Strongest point**: The three-register table (delegation / bandwidth / quantum selection, each with "supplies" and "leaves open" columns) makes the single-object hypothesis legible at a glance while the surrounding prose forbids reading the fit as confirmation.
- **Notable quote**: "Stating the same thing three ways is not three witnesses agreeing. It is one witness with three accents."
- **Why it works**: It converts a tempting inflation (three frameworks agree → better confirmed) into its correct, deflated form (three vocabularies for one posit → tidier, not truer) using the catalogue's own evidential-status and cross-mechanism-convergence machinery. The "three independently motivated coarsenesses landing at the same grain" observation is a genuine insight that earns the single-object hypothesis without overclaiming it.

### probability-problem-in-many-worlds.md
- **Strongest point**: The "What Would Success Even Look Like?" section — granting the entire Deutsch-Wallace programme counterfactually and showing that even total success "would be a claim about rational agency and self-location, not about physics."
- **Notable quote**: "The theorem is valid, one premise is optional, and the Map's reason for treating it so is independently motivated by its account of the conscious subject."
- **Why it works**: It is the rare anti-MWI piece that concedes the opponent's strongest possible result and locates the disagreement precisely at a tenet-level commitment, refusing both the parsimony shortcut and the pretence of an in-framework knockdown.

### concession-convergence.md
- **Strongest point**: The asymmetric tier table separating the architectural finding (*strongly supported*) from the interpretive significance (*realistic possibility, contested*) from the meta-argument (*live hypothesis* / *strongly supported* in its weaker constraint-form).
- **Notable quote**: "Unifying three contestable claims yields one contestable claim, not a confirmed one." (the sibling formulation in `one-structure`, mirrored here as the proliferation-explanation)
- **Why it works**: It applies the common-cause-null discipline to a *meta-argument* (the methodology the catalogue developed on first-order clusters), and is candid that the strongest cross-observer channel — non-Western narrowing physicalisms — remains "unbuilt," a gap rather than a passed test.

### locality.md
- **Strongest point**: The "same-structure constraint" on mental-structure posits — mental organisation invoked to answer one interface puzzle must do the cross-puzzle work, not be re-minted per objection.
- **Notable quote**: "A response that needs a differently-shaped mind for each objection has explained nothing."
- **Why it works**: It closes a long-standing unfalsifiability gap (flagged in pessimistic-2026-03-28-c) without retreating from the response itself, and assigns the residual posit its honest tier (*speculative integration*).

### pragmatisms-path-to-dualism.md
- **Strongest point**: The "functionalist escape route closes" section — showing that the reflexive argument and the phenomenal-normativity analysis each have a separate functionalist reply, but the *bridge* between them forecloses both at once.
- **Notable quote**: "The dualist's conclusion preserves the method's authority; the functionalist's drains it."
- **Why it works**: It pre-empts the obvious "every position is reflexive" objection with a clean asymmetry argument, and grounds the reflexive case in pragmatism's *own* criterion rather than imported dualist intuitions.
