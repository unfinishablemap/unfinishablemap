## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The article takes the explanatory gap seriously without overclaiming: it locates the agent at "the exact point where its constitutive work happens" across three frameworks and insists the gap is real, while explicitly refusing to convert "rivals presuppose an agent" into "therefore dualism." This is the hard problem deployed as a diagnostic, not a cudgel.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
The quantum instantiation ("the observer with no nature") engages QBism, Healey, Brukner-Zeilinger, and Stapp himself as a family that places an agent at the centre of the formalism. The article respects that this is a genuine structural feature of agent-centred quantum foundations, not a local quirk.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The "property with no performer" and "evaluation with no evaluator" framings center the first-person standpoint precisely: a property with no one whose property it is, an experience with no subject for whom there is something it is like. The "something it is like to be the agent" phrasing is doing real load-bearing work.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The activity-first instantiation ("activities require agents performing them — dancing requires a dancer") avoids crude substance dualism by routing through the doing/doer relation. The diagnostic preserves mind's reality as a structural requirement without reifying it as a Cartesian object.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
The "Bidirectional Interaction" section correctly marks the quantum agent as "not an inert spectator" while declining to assert the causal role — "the agent-shaped hole marks the site of possible interaction without proving interaction occurs." Agency is taken seriously as a *site*, not smuggled in as a result.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
Strong epistemic humility throughout. The "What the Diagnostic Does Not Establish" section is a model: the hole is "a *cost* — an explanatory bill left unpaid — not a contradiction," and a QBist "can grant the whole diagnosis" and answer that bracketing is a feature. The article refuses to pretend it closes the case.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This is the load-bearing verdict and it is strongly positive. The article performs the praise-worthy *non*-move at the center of evidential-status discipline: it had every structural opportunity to slide "rivals presuppose an agent" up the scale toward positive evidence for dualism, and explicitly declined — "Removing a competitor's escape route is not the same as supplying positive evidence for one's own position. A tenet may remove a defeater; it may not upgrade the evidence level." It names its own filling as "a *posited* filling, not a proven one" and quarantines the positive case to the hard problem "not by the hole." The Process Philosopher's panpsychic-resonance praise and the Hardline Empiricist's restraint praise **converge** here rather than conflict — the mark of an article that has resolved the tension honestly. No calibration concern; no slippage; no tier-upgrade. The "Bracketing Versus Structural Dependence" section is exactly the right discipline: it concedes the thin-reading escape route ("agent as bet-taker or prescription-follower") is live and that the contested claim "has to be earned framework by framework."
