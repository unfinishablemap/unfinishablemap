## 2. Five-Dimension Analysis

### Dimension 1 — Attribution and citation fidelity

This is where the article most decisively beats prior expectations for the site. Verified against primary sources:

Crucially, the substantive claim is *also* correct: reading Bealer's own text confirms he introduces "Bas van Fraassen's major/sergeant case" on p. 26, applies his screening-off test to it across pp. 26–27, and states in footnote 2 that "mental-to-mental and mental-to-physical causation will be seen to belong to a special, hitherto unnoticed, species of 'trumping preemption'… The notion of trumping preemption is Jonathan Schaffer's." The site's sentence — "Bealer (2007) develops a non-reductive account that construes mental-to-mental and mental-to-physical causation as a form of preemption, appealing directly to the major–sergeant command case" — is accurate, and if anything understates Bealer (his own term is specifically *trumping* preemption, and he credits Schaffer by name at fns 2, 13, and 18). This is the attribution most likely to have been fabricated or misdated by an AI system; it is neither. Bealer has a genuine, sustained publication on the preemption route, contrary to the prior that his corpus is confined to modal epistemology, intuition-as-evidence, and the self-consciousness argument against materialism. [philarchive](https://philarchive.org/archive/BEAMC)[philarchive](https://philarchive.org/archive/BEAMC)

### Dimension 2 — Fidelity of the causation exposition

Mostly strong, one real error and one spin:

- **Factual error in the Merlin/Morgana case.** The article says Merlin and Morgana "each cast the same hex on the prince **at the same time**… yet Merlin's is the cause because his spell **was first**." This is internally contradictory and misdescribes Schaffer's original: in Schaffer 2000 (p. 165) Merlin casts *at noon* and Morgana *at six that evening* — the temporal gap is the whole point, because the selecting law is "the first spell cast on a given day." Simultaneity is a feature of the *military* case, not the magic case. The error sits in the flagship exposition and should be corrected; it also muddies the very sufficiency/authority distinction the article is built on.
- **Spin on Hitchcock (2011).** The article says Hitchcock "partially rehabilitates trumping within a contrastive framework." Hitchcock's actual stated conclusion (verbatim from the *Synthese* abstract, DOI 10.1007/s11229-010-9799-y) is deflationary: "there is little reason to regard trumping as a species of causal preemption." Analysing trumping contrastively while concluding *against* its status as a distinct species is not naturally described as "rehabilitation." This overstates how friendly the cited source is to the category the article needs. [Springer](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11229-010-9799-y)
- Bernstein (2015, *Acta Analytica* 30(1): 41–57) is correctly reported (trumping re-describable as overdetermination-with-insensitive-effects or early preemption with a hidden cutting point). Lewis's influence response (alter Merlin's spell → effect changes; alter Morgana's → no change) is correctly rendered. Schaffer 2000, Kim 1998, Crane 1995, Papineau 2001, Mills 1996, Lowe 2003 are all correctly attributed and characterised.

### Dimension 3 — Co-optation firewall and evidential independence

The firewall **holds** for the hard cases, which is a genuine strength and unusual:

- **Schaffer** (a naturalist about causation who would reject the dualist payload) is *not* enlisted for the dualist conclusion. The article explicitly flags the disanalogy: "Schaffer's cases are fictional (laws of magic) or conventional (military rank)… Nature is not in the business of stipulating such rules, and Schaffer's structure on its own gives no reason to expect a non-stipulative analogue." This is exactly the concession a co-optation-aware referee wants.
- **Vaassen** is presented as a *rival*, not a fellow-traveller — no breach.
- **Bealer and Saad** are actual interactionist dualists defending the preemption route, so citing them toward a dualist conclusion is legitimate, not co-optation.

Evidential-independence: Bealer (2007) and Saad (2025) are **not** independent converging witnesses — Saad explicitly builds on and cites Bealer, and both descend from Schaffer. The article handles this correctly by framing them as a *lineage/pedigree* ("a longer pedigree than is usually noticed") rather than as independent convergence, so there is no evidential-independence inflation here. [Springer](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11098-025-02290-3)[concepts/delegatory-causation](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/delegatory-causation/)

One performative-inoculation-adjacent moment: Crane (1995) and Papineau (2001) are named as critics who "have pressed" the observable/unobservable-closure worry, but their arguments are not actually engaged — a one-line gesture standing in for engagement.

### Dimension 4 — Constrain-vs-establish gate and the missing physicalist rival

This is the article's real epistemic ceiling, and to its credit it largely says so itself. It concedes trumping "offers no new empirical signature," is "deliberately designed to be invisible at the trajectory level," and that "the template shows *how* a dualism… could be articulated; it does not show that such a dualism is *warranted*." That is a textbook statement that the material **establishes consistency rather than constraining the space against physicalism**. Saad's own paper concedes the predictive equivalence of delegatory dualism with subset dualism, epiphenomenalist dualism, overdeterminist dualism, and physicalism ("the same predictive boat"). So the trumping template does zero discriminating work against physicalism; it is a coherence resource. The article is close to admitting exactly this — the constrain-vs-establish gate is, unusually, *not* failed by concealment, only skirted by leaving the demotion implicit. [springer](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11098-025-02290-3)

Where the article underperforms is **rival coverage**. The brief's target rival — interventionist/counterfactual dissolution of exclusion — is only half-present:

- **Present and well-framed:** Vaassen's interventionism.
- **Absent:** Thomas Kroedel's counterfactual dualist mental causation ("Dualist Mental Causation and the Exclusion Problem," *Noûs* 49(2): 357–375, 2015, DOI 10.1111/nous.12028 — on which "some physical events counterfactually depend on, and are therefore caused by, mental events") — the most direct dualist competitor to the preemption route, and one **Saad himself cites**. Its omission from an article surveying dualist mental-causation strategies is the single most conspicuous gap.
- **Absent:** Karen Bennett's compatibilist dissolution of exclusion ("Why the Exclusion Problem Seems Intractable, and How, Just Maybe, to Tract It," *Noûs* 37(3): 471–497, 2003), List & Stoljar ("Does the Exclusion Argument Put Any Pressure on Dualism?," *AJP* 95(1): 96–108, 2017, arguing a similar response to exclusion is available to dualists), and the Woodward/Baumgartner interventionist-exclusion debate (Baumgartner arguing interventionism does *not* rescue non-reductive causation; Woodward's "independent fixability" reply). These are the strongest physicalist-side reasons to think the exclusion problem the article treats as the motivating pressure may already dissolve without any dualist apparatus. Their absence lets the article present trumping as answering a live problem whose liveness the omitted literature contests. [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/261442395_Interventionism_and_Causal_Exclusion)

### Dimension 5 — Tenet leakage, constitutional-attractor, internal coherence

- **Containment of tenet material is good.** The neutral exposition (Schaffer's Cases; the Lewis Response; What Trumping Isolates) is essentially tenet-free; the tenet read-back is quarantined in a clearly labelled "Relation to Site Perspective" section. This is better hygiene than the constitutional-attractor worry would predict, and there is no detectable tenet leakage into the ostensibly neutral exposition.
- **Agent-causation overreach (unsupported).** The claim that trumping's authority/sufficiency decoupling gives agent-causal libertarianism "the structure it needs but has historically struggled to articulate" is asserted without citation and is probably wrong: agent-causal libertarians (O'Connor, Clarke) want the agent to *make a difference* among genuinely open alternatives, not merely to be the "authoritative author" of an already-physically-sufficient movement. Authority-without-difference is closer to epiphenomenalism than to libertarian agency. This sentence should be cut or heavily qualified.
- **Internal-coherence tension with the Minimal Quantum Interaction tenet.** The "Minimal Quantum Interaction" bullet markets trumping as "no push, no energy contribution, only the selection of one already-sufficient process as authoritative," preserving observational closure. But the Map's Tenet 2 (per the `tenets` page) has consciousness *biasing otherwise indeterminate quantum outcomes* and explicitly *rejecting strong causal closure* — a difference-making model that changes the physical trajectory within the indeterminacy. Trumping changes nothing about the trajectory and preserves closure; quantum selection changes which outcome occurs. These are competing mechanisms, not obviously combinable, and Saad's own paper treats "quantum interactionist dualism" (observationally testable, closure-violating) as a *different* theory from his closure-preserving delegatory dualism. The article papers over this as "remains open." This is a site-level coherence-inflation risk: two flagship mechanisms the Map wants simultaneously are in tension, and the trumping article's difference-making-free model is actually closer to the epiphenomenalism it aims to escape than to the Map's own quantum-selection channel. This is an epistemic-to-metaphysical slide in miniature: a coherence virtue (invisibility) is quietly converted into an ontological asset without confronting that the same invisibility is what the epiphenomenalist enjoys. [springer](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11098-025-02290-3)

### (a) Article-specific fixes

1. **`concepts/trumping-preemption`** — Correct the Merlin/Morgana exposition: Merlin casts at noon, Morgana at six that evening; the spells are *not* simultaneous. Remove the internal contradiction between "at the same time" and "was first." Reserve simultaneity for the military case only.
2. **`concepts/trumping-preemption`** — Fix reference #16: Schaffer, "Contrastive Causation," *Philosophical Review* 114(3): **327–358** (not 297–328).
3. **`concepts/trumping-preemption`** — Cut or heavily qualify the agent-causal-libertarianism claim; as written it conflates "authoritative authorship of a sufficient movement" with the difference-making among open alternatives that libertarians actually require. If retained, cite O'Connor or Clarke and flag the disanalogy.
4. **`concepts/trumping-preemption`** — Recharacterise Hitchcock (2011): report his stated conclusion that "there is little reason to regard trumping as a species of causal preemption," rather than describing him as partially rehabilitating trumping.
5. **`concepts/trumping-preemption`** — Add engagement (not just naming) of the omitted dualist rival **Kroedel 2015** (counterfactual dualist mental causation) and the physicalist exclusion-dissolution literature (**Karen Bennett 2003**, **List & Stoljar 2017**, **Woodward/Baumgartner** interventionism). At minimum, concede that if these dissolutions succeed, the exclusion pressure the template answers may not be live.
6. **`concepts/trumping-preemption`** — Either cite Paul & Hall (2013) inline (e.g., on the overdetermination-reduction of trumping, which is their view) or move it to Further Reading; resolve the reverse orphan. Fix the "Subset Law**" → "Subset Law*" rendering.
7. **`concepts/trumping-preemption`** and **`concepts/delegatory-causation`** — Add an explicit note that the preemption/trumping route (closure-preserving, non-difference-making) is a *distinct and potentially competing* mechanism from the Map's quantum-selection channel (`tenets`, `bidirectional-interaction`), rather than leaving their relation as "remains open."
8. **`concepts/delegatory-causation`** — Mirror any shared-exposition fixes; its current military-only framing of the Schaffer case is fine, but confirm it does not inherit the simultaneity error, and align its "Subset Law*" quote with the corrected parent page.

### (b) Site-wide methodology fixes

1. **Add a page-range verification pass to `deep-review`.** The Schaffer 2005 error (297–328 vs 327–358) is exactly the class of defect — correct author/venue/year, wrong pages — that intra-corpus cross-checking cannot catch and that the changelog already shows recurring elsewhere (e.g., the Mashour/TINS volume-page fixes logged 2026-07-14). Page ranges should be verified at publisher of record, not just DOIs and titles.
2. **Institute a mandatory "strongest-rival-present?" checklist keyed to the article's motivating problem.** Here the motivating problem is exclusion, yet the canonical dissolutions (Bennett compatibilism, List & Stoljar, Woodward/Baumgartner interventionism) and the nearest dualist competitor (Kroedel) are absent while friendlier sources are present — an asymmetry the review process should flag automatically.
3. **Add a cross-article coherence audit for tenet-level mechanism conflicts.** The trumping-vs-quantum-selection tension is invisible at the single-article level but is a live contradiction across the corpus. Reviews should test whether a newly imported mechanism is consistent with, or a rival to, the mechanisms other tenet-bearing articles rely on.
4. **Convert one-line "critics have pressed X (Crane; Papineau)" gestures into either genuine engagement or explicit "not engaged here" pointers**, to prevent performative-inoculation citations from accumulating.
5. **Formalise the establish-vs-constrain register in frontmatter.** This article nearly self-demotes to coherence-only; a machine-readable "evidential status: consistency-only / constraining / establishing" tag would make that calibration auditable and prevent later revisions from silently upgrading it.

*Referee's closing note: this is a case where the adversarial prior — that an AI-generated dualist site would fabricate a role for Bealer, misframe Vaassen, or misdate Saad — was tested hard and did not hold. The primary-source record vindicates the article's three most contestable attributions. The remaining defects are ordinary scholarly ones (a factual slip, a page range, an over-reach, a coverage gap), not integrity failures. The correct posture is a demanding REVISE-HARD that fixes the errors and forces the exclusion-dissolution rivals onto the page, while permanently pinning the dualist payload at coherence-only — not the harsher registers, which the evidence does not support.*
