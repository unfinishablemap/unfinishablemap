The article is mature and well-cited; citation metadata for the core instability cluster (Howell 2014, Pautz 2017/2019, Cutter 2019, Kind 2015) matches the sibling corpus exactly. Three substantive issues remain. (1) A **source-resists-claim / metaphysical-vs-epistemic** problem in the "What Would Challenge This View?" section, where the Kerskens & Pérez (2022) and Khan et al. (2) The Williams (2021) → "requires rejecting many-worlds" passage slides from a *compatibility* claim (epistemic/modal) to a *physics-disagreement* claim (metaphysical) without the bridging clause its sibling article was explicitly patched to carry. ## Critiques by Philosopher

### The Eliminative Materialist (Churchland)
The whole article is intramural dualist-vs-monist bookkeeping over a folk-psychological posit ("quiddity") that no maturing neuroscience will need. But this is a fair complaint against the *topic*, not a flaw in the article: the piece honestly flags Russellian monism's lack of distinctive predictions (line 72) and turns the same falsifiability blade on the Map ("a more falsifiable theory is more testable, not thereby more likely"). No action.

### The Hard-Nosed Physicalist (Dennett)
Line 104's deployment of Strawson's "reality of experience" argument — "experience is the one thing we know exists non-inferentially, so any theory denying it undermines its own epistemic basis" — is asserted as a resource against illusionism without acknowledging that the illusionist denies precisely the "non-inferential" premise. The article states the move but does not register that illusionists reject its starting point. Medium-low; the article elsewhere ([illusionism](/concepts/illusionism/)) carries the fuller debate, and this section explicitly defers there.

### The Quantum Skeptic (Tegmark)
See Critical Issue 1. The decoherence-rebuttal at line 144 leans on the Kerskens & Pérez and Khan results harder than the corpus norm permits.

### The Many-Worlds Defender (Deutsch)
See Critical Issue 2. The pessimistic-2026-04-15 review already recorded Deutsch's standing objection: the article "assumes MWI is wrong (per the Map's tenets) and then uses this assumption to criticise Russellian monism." Line 130 of the current text states the cost-framing ("the Map counts as a cost") less explicitly than the sibling article, which was patched on 2026-04-17 precisely to prevent a skim-reader misreading.

### The Empiricist (Popper)
The article's self-aware passage at line 72 ("This is a methodological contrast, not evidence that the Map is true") is exactly the falsifiability hygiene a Popperian wants, and pre-empts the "not even wrong" charge against the Map's own MQI. Strength to preserve.

### The Buddhist Philosopher (Nagarjuna)
"Avoids combination" (line 98) posits "unified mental subjects … the unified subject is fundamental." Nagarjuna would press that positing a fundamental unified subject simply *stipulates away* the combination problem by reifying the very self that is empty. The article treats fundamental unified subjecthood as a free advantage rather than a contested posit carrying its own burden. Medium — note as a counterargument to address, not a defect requiring rewrite.

## Critical Issues

### Issue 1: Suggestive evidence recruited as decoherence rebuttal (source-resists-claim + epistemic/metaphysical)
- **File**: [concepts/russellian-monism.md](/concepts/russellian-monism/)
- **Location**: "What Would Challenge This View?" — line 140 (challenge condition 2) and the "Why these conditions seem unmet" paragraph, line 144: *"Decoherence objections are complicated by recent evidence (Kerskens & Pérez 2022; Khan et al. 2024)."*
- **Problem**: The corpus deliberately frames Kerskens & Pérez (2022) as *"suggestive context, not a load-bearing response"* (see `workflow/changelog.md:1786`) and routinely attaches skeptical framing (Warren critique, preprint status). Khan et al. (2024) is, in the corpus's own words, *"indirect evidence"* and *"circumstantial support"* that establishes microtubule *relevance*, not quantum robustness at consciousness-relevant scales. Here both are recruited — without their standard hedges — to support the stronger claim that the decoherence challenge condition "seems unmet," i.e. that interactionism retains "the empirical advantage." This is the *weak-reading-evidence-recruited-for-strong-conclusion* pattern: observational/indirect findings (epistemic) doing the work of "decoherence does not defeat quantum interaction" (a claim about how the brain is constituted). The sources would resist this load.
- **Severity**: Medium
- **Recommendation**: `refine-draft` — soften to the corpus-standard framing ("recent results are *suggestive but indirect*, and are contested (Warren); they do not settle the decoherence question"). Keep the dialectical point that the objection is "not decisive" (cf. `qm-interpretations-beyond-many-worlds.md:113`) without implying the challenge condition is met or unmet.

### Issue 2: Compatibility → physics-disagreement slide on Williams (2021)
- **File**: [concepts/russellian-monism.md](/concepts/russellian-monism/)
- **Location**: lines 42 and 130. Line 42: "Russellian monism's natural compatibility with many-worlds (Williams 2021) reveals a deep divergence: the Map requires consciousness to do causal work at quantum measurement, which Russellian monism does not." Line 130: "If Russellian monism's natural quantum-mechanical home is many-worlds, this creates a deep incompatibility … The two frameworks disagree not just about consciousness but about physics itself."
- **Problem**: Williams (2021) supports the *modal/epistemic* claim that constitutive Russellian panpsychism is *most naturally compatible* with Everett — not the *metaphysical* claim that Russellian monism's "natural home **is** many-worlds" or that the two frameworks "disagree … about physics itself." Compatibility-with is being upgraded to commitment-to. The sibling `russellian-monism-versus-bi-aspectual-dualism.md:99` carries an explicit cost-framing clause (added 2026-04-17 to fix exactly this skim-risk per `tenet-check-2026-04-17`); line 130 here is more guarded than line 42, but line 42's "Russellian monism's natural compatibility … reveals a deep divergence" does the upgrade in the article's *second paragraph*, where front-loaded LLM readers will take it as settled. Note this is the *recurring* Deutsch objection (pessimistic-2026-04-15): the divergence is real only on the Map's own anti-MWI commitment.
- **Severity**: Medium
- **Recommendation**: `refine-draft` — insert the sibling's cost-framing clause at line 42 ("a compatibility the Map counts as a *cost* of the Russellian view, given the No Many Worlds tenet") and qualify "natural home is" to "is most naturally compatible with." Preserve the substance; mark the disagreement as turning on the Map's tenet rather than presenting it as a neutral physics result.

## Counterarguments to Address

### Fundamental unified subjects "avoid" the combination problem
- **Current content says**: "By positing unified mental subjects rather than combinations of micro-subjects, substance dualism sidesteps the combination problem entirely … the unified subject is fundamental" (line 98).
- **A critic would argue** (Nagarjuna / a panpsychist): positing fundamental unified subjects does not *solve* combination, it *declines to play* — paying the cost of an unexplained primitive (where do discrete subjects come from? the [pairing-problem](/concepts/pairing-problem/)) instead of the combination cost. Calling this "avoids" rather than "relocates" the burden is question-begging.
- **Suggested response**: Reframe as "relocates the burden from combination to subject-individuation, a trade the Map accepts" and cross-link the pairing-problem treatment. Low-medium; honest-trade framing already used elsewhere in the corpus.
