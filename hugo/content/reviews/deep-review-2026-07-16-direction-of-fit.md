### Medium / Low Issues Found (corrected)
1. **Term-coinage precision (corrected)**: The article stated "Anscombe
   introduced direction of fit through an analogy in *Intention* (1957)."
   Anscombe introduced the *distinction* via the shopping-list example but
   never used the phrase "direction of fit"; the term entered the literature
   later (usually credited to Searle's work on illocutionary acts, which recast
   Anscombe's example as the metaphor's clearest illustration) and is, per the
   scholarly record, "often wrongly attributed to Anscombe herself." Corrected
   to name the distinction as Anscombe's while explicitly noting she did not
   coin the phrase and flagging the common misattribution. Coinage credit left
   lightly hedged ("usually credited to Searle") because the phrase's precise
   origin is contested in the literature (Austin, Searle, Platts all cited in
   various histories); the article avoids asserting a single contested coiner as
   fact. Web-verified via publisher/reference sources (Wikipedia, BJHP
   Vol 26 No 3, Humberstone 1992) — not self-verified against unfinishablemap.org.
