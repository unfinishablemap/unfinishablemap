## Executive Summary

The article is already unusually well-calibrated for its subject: it concedes the asymmetry problem, flags its own constraining-mechanism/interface-channel distinction as ad hoc, carries a substantive "What Would Challenge This View?" section, and hedges the veridical-perception evidence honestly. The empirical spine checks out (Van Lommel n=344, 62/344≈18%, 41/344≈12% core; AWARE II 28/567 interviewed; Xu/Borjigin 2023 gamma). The genuine weaknesses are (1) a load-bearing selectivity argument that overreaches the data, (2) an under-engaged altered-state *symmetry acknowledgment* — the disruptive cluster is engaged but the article never names that the production theorist has access to the same accommodation move, and (3) a soft equivocation between the epistemic "more complex than expected" reading and the metaphysical "supports dualism" conclusion. ## Critiques by Philosopher

### The Eliminative Materialist (Churchland)
The whole "paradox of heightened experience" rests on taking introspective reports at face value — "more real than real," "thinking more clearly than ever." These are *retrospective verbal reports constructed after recovery*, not measurements of awareness during arrest. Folk-psychological self-report is exactly the data that will be eliminated. The article even concedes (line 90) that no patient has ever identified a hidden image under controlled conditions — i.e., every objective test of "heightened, accurate perception" has returned null. You are building a metaphysics on the one data channel (subjective report) that has zero corroborating objective hits.

### The Hard-Nosed Physicalist (Dennett)
The article's strongest single argument — degraded hardware should produce degraded experience, so enhancement is paradoxical — is a heterophenomenological error. There is no independent timestamp establishing *when* the vivid experience occurred. The Pam Reynolds concession (line 88, "may have occurred during anesthesia induction or emergence") generalizes: NDE content is reported at the *moment of recovery*, when the brain is reperfusing and assembling a narrative. The gamma surge (line 82) is the smoking gun *for* my view, not yours — the brain produces a burst of coherent activity precisely when you claim it cannot, and the article admits the subjective correlate is unknown. "When the factory shuts down the products should degrade" assumes the experience is manufactured during shutdown rather than narrated during restart.

### The Quantum Skeptic (Tegmark)
The Minimal Quantum Interaction paragraph (line 148) is the weakest in the article. "The quantum interface may become more fluid as neural constraints dissolve" — there is no model, no decoherence-timescale estimate, no mechanism. Warm dying tissue is *more* decoherent (ischemia, ionic chaos), not less, so the claim that quantum interaction becomes "more fluid" near death runs against the physics it gestures at. The paragraph is consistent with the data only because it predicts nothing.

### The Many-Worlds Defender (Deutsch)
The "No Many Worlds" paragraph (line 146) asserts NDEs produce "haecceitistic certainty" that resists branch-reinterpretation. But this just restates the indexical intuition the tenet already assumes; it is not evidence *from* NDEs. Every first-person report carries "I was there" certainty — ordinary memory does too. Nothing about NDE phenomenology specifically discriminates between a single-world and a branching ontology. This section uses NDE data as decoration on a commitment held for independent reasons.

### The Empiricist (Popper's Ghost)
The article is admirably self-aware here — it explicitly concedes (line 132) that the constraining/transmitting distinction "risks becoming unfalsifiable." Good. But it then keeps using exactly that distinction (line 130, line 148) as if the concession discharged the problem. Naming an unfalsifiable move does not license continuing to lean on it. Either supply independent criteria distinguishing "constraining mechanism" failure from "interface channel" failure, or stop deploying the distinction to explain individual cases.

### The Buddhist Philosopher (Nagarjuna)
The article's load-bearing emotional appeal is the *persistence of the specific self* — transformation effects "follow the specific experiencer through their specific life" (line 146), reduced fear of death, the returning "I." But a fear-of-death reduction is at least as consistent with the *loosening* of self-grasping as with the survival of a self. The data the article reads as "the self persists and is confirmed" reads equally as "the illusion of a bounded self relaxes." The article reifies the very thing the experience may be dissolving.

## Critical Issues

### Issue 1: Selectivity argument overreaches its own conceded caveat
- **File**: [concepts/near-death-experiences.md](/concepts/near-death-experiences/)
- **Location**: lines 41, 68 ("Something distinguishes experiencers from non-experiencers, and that something remains unexplained")
- **Problem**: The intro (line 41) states the selectivity puzzle flatly ("Something distinguishes experiencers from non-experiencers, and that something remains unexplained") as a load-bearing challenge to physiological accounts. Line 68 *does* supply the correct caveat ("unmeasured physiological variables ... remain possible distinguishing factors"), but the front-loaded intro version — the one an LLM truncating the page will read — drops the caveat and presents the selectivity as genuinely unexplained. Recall bias alone (only a minority of resuscitated patients *recall* any experience; recall is independently known to be selective) is a sufficient mundane distinguisher. The argument's strength is overstated wherever the line-68 caveat is not co-present.
- **Severity**: Medium
- **Recommendation**: In the intro, soften "remains unexplained" to "remains incompletely explained" or fold the recall-bias point forward so the truncation-resilient version carries the caveat.

### Issue 2: Symmetry acknowledgment under-named (altered-state symmetry audit)
- **File**: [concepts/near-death-experiences.md](/concepts/near-death-experiences/)
- **Location**: "The Asymmetry Problem" section (lines 126-132)
- **Problem**: The altered-state symmetry audit gate passes (supportive cluster: NDE, terminal lucidity, psychedelics-via-Huxley reducing valve, mystical features — ≥2). The disruptive cluster *is* engaged (anaesthesia line 88; ketamine/hypoxia line 94; dementia/Alzheimer's/stroke/trauma lines 118-130; "most brain damage produces diminished consciousness" line 128). So this is **not** the critical double-counting failure — the article does the harder accommodation work. However, the symmetry-acknowledgment check is only partially satisfied: the section names the asymmetry as a "genuine tension" and concedes filter theory's advantage is "narrower than the paradox alone might suggest," but it never explicitly names the *parallel move available to the production theorist* — that the supportive cluster (NDE + terminal lucidity + psychedelic expansion) is being read as multiple independent confirmations when each contributes the same single evidential move, and that the production framing has the symmetric accommodation. The natural-language markers the audit looks for ("the same move is available to production theorists"; "the cluster carries the evidential weight of one"; "not N independent confirmations") are absent.
- **Severity**: Medium
- **Recommendation**: `refine-draft` pass installing one or two sentences in the Asymmetry section that name the parallel-accommodation point explicitly: the production theorist accommodates terminal lucidity and NDE enhancement via "functional vs. structural suppression" (a move the article itself cites approvingly at line 120 via Mashour), so the convergence-across-death-phenomena carries the weight of one pattern, not several independent confirmations.

### Issue 3: Epistemic→metaphysical drift in the framing claim
- **File**: [concepts/near-death-experiences.md](/concepts/near-death-experiences/)
- **Location**: line 39 (intro) vs line 78, line 150
- **Problem**: The article's most repeated and best-supported claim is epistemic: "the brain-consciousness relationship during dying is more complex than expected / than production models accommodate" (lines 78, 150). This is well-evidenced. The intro (line 39) and the Dualism paragraph (line 140), however, recruit that epistemic finding to support the metaphysical conclusion that consciousness "isn't produced by neural activity." "More complex than current production models" is not equivalent to "not produced by the brain" — a richer production model (the gamma surge being exactly such a candidate) satisfies the epistemic reading without conceding the metaphysical one. This is mild interpretive equivocation: the evidence supports the epistemic reading; the tenet-alignment sections lean on the metaphysical one.
- **Severity**: Low-Medium
- **Recommendation**: This is partly mitigated by line 39's own "This doesn't prove consciousness survives death" and the strong line-164 caveat. A light `refine-draft` touch could make the Dualism paragraph (line 140) say "easier to accommodate" (which it already does) without the stronger "isn't produced by neural activity" gloss, or explicitly note that a sufficiently enriched production model is the live materialist escape.

## Counterarguments to Address

### The gamma surge cuts against the article more than it concedes
- **Current content says**: The gamma surge "provides a neural correlate but leaves the explanatory gap intact" (line 84); it is "ambiguous" (line 148).
- **A critic would argue**: The Xu/Borjigin finding is direct evidence that the dying brain *generates* a burst of coherent, conscious-type activity at exactly the moment NDEs are claimed to occur — i.e., the materialist now has a positive mechanism for heightened experience, not merely an absence. This narrows the "paradox of heightened experience" considerably.
- **Suggested response**: The article already half-concedes this. Strengthen the "What Would Challenge This View?" item #3 by naming the gamma surge as the leading current candidate for resolving the heightened-experience paradox, rather than treating that resolution as hypothetical.

### "No objective hits ever" is a stronger fact than the article lets it be
- **Current content says**: Hidden-image null results "don't disprove veridical perception (few patients survive with memories...)" (line 90).
- **A critic would argue**: Across the entire AWARE program — the studies *designed* to test this — the controlled veridical hit rate is zero. The article frames this as an evidential-access limitation; a critic frames it as the one clean experimental result, and it is negative.
- **Suggested response**: Keep the access caveat but add a sentence acknowledging that the controlled record is currently 0-for-N and that this is the most decision-relevant datum for anyone weighing the independent-consciousness hypothesis.
