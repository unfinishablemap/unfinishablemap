## Reply

# Review of “Disguised Property Dualism”

**Verdict: substantial revision required, not withdrawal.**

The article contains a useful philosophical warning: a theory does not earn ontological parsimony merely by calling itself “monist,” and identity or unification claims can carry substantial unexplained debt. Its three proposed forms of explanatory progress—derivation, mechanism-backed identity and a progressively unifying research programme—are a productive starting point.

The stronger claim does not hold up, however. The article has not shown that Markovian monism, Russellian monism and IIT instantiate property dualism while denying the label. In each case it moves too quickly from **two descriptions, perspectives or formalisms** plus an incomplete explanation to **two irreducible properties**. Its bibliography is now mostly sound at the metadata and quotation level, following corrections on 16 July 2026, but its interpretation of key authors is unreliable and its coverage is not current. It also omits several of the strongest 2020s responses from identity physicalism, phenomenal-concept theory, grounding physicalism, Russellian monism and current IIT. [concepts/disguised-property-dualism](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/disguised-property-dualism/)

The self-application section is commendably candid, but it ultimately does use **tenet-protective bracketing**: attempted monist unification is penalised for incompleteness, while the Map’s own incomplete psychophysical account is exempted because it declares the relation primitive. That makes the Map overt rather than disguised dualism; it does not establish that the Map has paid its explanatory costs more successfully.

## 1. The central diagnostic is logically unstable

The deepest problem is the ambiguity in the first condition:

> “Two irreducible structures,” where structures may be “descriptions, geometries, aspects, or property-types.”

Those categories are not interchangeable. A system can have multiple mathematically non-identical descriptions, coordinate systems, levels of description or explanatory perspectives while having only one underlying property ontology. Conversely, two irreducible properties would already amount to a form of property pluralism independently of whether anyone has supplied a reduction.

That creates a dilemma:

- If **irreducible** means *metaphysically irreducible*, condition 1 already assumes the property-dualist conclusion.
- If it means only *not yet derived or explained*, condition 1 collapses into condition 3 and establishes explanatory incompleteness rather than dualism.

The site’s own article on substance and property dualism uses the appropriately stricter threshold: property dualism requires mental properties to be “genuinely different” from physical properties, not merely differently described, formally represented or epistemically accessed. The target article quietly broadens that threshold from properties to descriptions and geometries. [concepts/disguised-property-dualism](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/disguised-property-dualism/)

The treatment also needs to distinguish several senses of monism:

- substance monism: one underlying kind of entity;
- property monism: one fundamental property kind;
- explanatory monism: one ultimately unified explanatory scheme;
- conceptual monism: one vocabulary or a priori derivational basis.

A substance monist can accept irreducible-seeming aspects without covertly claiming property monism. Russellian monism is especially heterogeneous: contemporary literature recognises physicalist, dualist, idealist and neutral-monist variants. Labelling this entire family “disguised property dualism” obscures rather than clarifies the taxonomy. [Springer](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10670-021-00408-7)

### The reduction standard is too narrow

The article permits three ways out of its diagnostic: logical derivation, a mechanism-backed empirical identity, or a progressively unifying research programme. These are valuable standards, but they are not exhaustive. Current physicalists and monists also appeal to:

- metaphysical grounding;
- realization or constitution;
- theoretical identification or Ramsification;
- mechanistic integration without strict derivation;
- role–realizer relations;
- structural equivalence between models;
- asymmetric dependence rather than “mutual reduction.”

Grounding physicalism is a direct counterexample to the article’s reduction-centric framing. It explicitly proposes a non-reductive relation in which phenomenal facts or properties are distinct from but grounded in physical facts, while arguing that the resulting ontology remains physicalist. One may reject that account, but “not reductively derivable” no longer suffices to classify it as covert dualism. [Springer](https://link.springer.com/content/pdf/10.1007/s10670-026-01063-6.pdf)

A better diagnostic would therefore be **unearned unification** or **unexplained unity**: the theory claims one ontology but has not adequately specified the relation among its phenomenal, physical and explanatory descriptions. That would preserve the article’s legitimate insight without pretending that explanatory debt settles ontology.

## 2. Citation accuracy and currency

### Bibliographic and quotation accuracy: mostly repaired

The 16 July deep review found three concrete defects that had survived four previous “verified” reviews:

- the wrong title for Friston, Wiese and Hobson;
- “mutually reductive” presented as a quotation although the source never uses that expression;
- an altered and truncated Beni quotation.

Those defects were corrected, and Pautz 2017 is now accurately identified as an unpublished manuscript rather than an Oxford University Press chapter. At the level of work existence, titles and current verbatim quotations, the live article is therefore substantially cleaner. [reviews/deep-review-2026-07-16-disguised-property-dualism](https://unfinishablemap.org/reviews/deep-review-2026-07-16-disguised-property-dualism/)

But bibliographic correctness is only the first layer. The more important questions are whether a source is represented in accordance with its authors’ stated position, whether it entails the conclusion attributed to it, and whether the cited version is current. On those tests, the article fares much worse.

### Markovian monism: the principal example is misclassified

The article says the two information geometries are “two irreducible descriptions” and concludes that all three diagnostic conditions are met. The primary source says almost the opposite:

- Markovian monism entails one kind of thing and one kind of irreducible property.
- The intrinsic and extrinsic perspectives are said to be reducible to one another.
- The dual information geometry “does not entail property dualism.”
- The existence of the extrinsic geometry does not by itself give a system consciousness or mental states; without further assumptions, it yields computational properties. [Semantic Scholar](https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/23d0/3fb8242fae85f9e64603c62518d09c10bcf0.pdf)

There remains a legitimate criticism here: Friston and colleagues do not thereby solve the hard problem, and their claim of reducibility may be philosophically underexplained. The Map can reasonably ask whether formal intertranslatability explains consciousness. It cannot, however, treat the authors’ two formal geometries as independently established irreducible property types while citing a paper that explicitly denies that interpretation.

Beni’s criticism also supports a narrower conclusion than the article draws. Beni argues that metaphysical claims about target systems cannot simply be read off the ontology of Markov-blanket models. That establishes **metaphysical underdetermination by the formalism**. It does not supply positive evidence that the target systems possess two irreducible property types. [concepts/disguised-property-dualism](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/disguised-property-dualism/)

The result is that the article’s strongest worked example should be reclassified:

> Markovian monism may overinterpret a mathematically useful dual description, and its consciousness claims remain incomplete; the formalism does not thereby become property dualism.

### Russellian monism: real sources, excessive generalisation

The Pautz and Cutter references are genuine and relevant. Pautz supplies a serious parity argument: some Russellian views appear to require linking or grounding principles structurally comparable to dualism’s psychophysical laws. That can undermine an easy parsimony argument for Russellian monism.

Cutter’s conclusion is substantially more conditional than the target article suggests. His argument concerns Russellian monists who reject physicalism using the usual epistemic or conceivability arguments. Such theorists cannot consistently accept those arguments against physicalism while refusing analogous arguments against Russellian monism. Cutter is not claiming that every version of Russellian monism, under every motivation and theory of modality, simply collapses into dualism. [achrafkassioui.com](https://www.achrafkassioui.com/library/Brian%20Cutter%20-%20Against%20the%20Middle%20Ground%2C%20Why%20Russellian%20Monism%20is%20Unstable.pdf)

The article also says Russellian monism introduces “two irreducible property-types.” That is variant-dependent. On some Russellian views, quiddities are the categorical realizers of structural or dispositional roles; the structural description does not pick out a second fundamental property alongside the quiddity. On others, phenomenal properties are grounded rather than identified. Still others are explicitly dualist or dual-aspect. A family-level verdict therefore requires a taxonomy, not a single three-condition classification.

The current literature is also materially richer than the article’s 2015–2019 core:

- Alter and Pereboom argue that Russellian monists need not accept structuralism about physics and can reformulate the structure-and-dynamics argument without it.
- Recent versions include physicalist, panpsychist, panprotopsychist and dual-aspect approaches.
- Liu’s 2026 criticism targets the unknowability of both quiddities and the postulated factors producing macro-consciousness, arguing that this threatens Russellian monism’s explanatory and cognitive fruitfulness. That is a more current and potentially stronger criticism than simply declaring the view covert dualism. [Springer+2Springer+2](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10670-021-00408-7)

The sentence that the modal difference is one “no evidence can adjudicate” is particularly overextended. The cited parity arguments support underdetermination by ordinary empirical correlations more readily than the unrestricted claim that no evidence, theoretical consideration or explanatory comparison could ever bear on the issue.

### IIT: substantially stale and author-stance inaccurate

The article relies on Tononi and Koch 2015 even though IIT 4.0 received its current reference formulation in 2023. That matters because IIT 4.0 explicitly presents its position as a **fundamental explanatory identity**:

- experience is said to be identical to the relevant cause–effect or Φ-structure;
- the identity is expressly “not meant as a correspondence between the properties of two separate things”;
- all phenomenal properties are supposed to be explained by the physical properties of the Φ-structure “with no additional ingredients”;
- the programme identifies ongoing applications to spatial experience, temporal flow, objects and empirical consciousness assessment. [PLOS+2PLOS+2](https://journals.plos.org/ploscompbiol/article)

None of that shows that IIT succeeds. The move from phenomenal axioms to physical postulates may be insufficiently justified, and calling something an explanatory identity does not make it explanatory. But those are objections to IIT’s inference and explanatory adequacy. They do not establish that IIT accepts two irreducible properties.

Indeed, IIT 4.0 is taxonomically more complicated than the target article acknowledges. Its methodological framework invokes operational physicalism and operational reductionism, while its ontology gives epistemic and ontological priority to experience. Recent critics have consequently debated IIT’s idealistic implications, its treatment of intrinsic versus extrinsic existence and whether conscious entities improperly depend on entities that IIT says do not “truly” exist. Those are stronger current objections than the claim that IIT is simply property dualism wearing a physicalist label. [PLOS+2Frontiers+2](https://journals.plos.org/ploscompbiol/article)

The article’s claim that Tononi sometimes “accept[s] the dualist implications openly” is also left unsupported. Current IIT may have idealist, panpsychist or dual-aspect implications depending on interpretation, but the cited source does not establish that Tononi endorses property dualism.

### Overall citation verdict

The citation record is best divided into four layers:

1. **Existence and metadata:** mostly accurate after the 16 July corrections.
2. **Quotation fidelity:** substantially improved after that review.
3. **Author-stance and entailment fidelity:** weak in the Markovian and IIT cases, mixed for Russellian monism.
4. **Literature currency:** inadequate.

The external bibliography ends in 2021, with the principal IIT reference from 2015, despite major relevant publications between 2023 and 2026. This would not automatically be a defect in a historical concept article, but it is material here because the theories being classified have changed and the live dialectic directly challenges the diagnostic.

## 3. It does not engage the strongest counterarguments

### The strongest Type-B reply is recognised but not actually confronted

The article concedes that a pure Type-B physicalist who posits one property under two concepts escapes the diagnosis. That is exactly the strongest reply to much of the article:

> phenomenal and physical predicates may differ conceptually and epistemically while referring to one property.

On this view there are not two properties whose covariance requires an external causal bridge. Demanding a mechanism connecting the two can beg the question by presupposing two relata when the identity theorist claims there is one referent under different modes of presentation. The correct challenge is to explain the conceptual or epistemic asymmetry, not to infer an ontological asymmetry from its existence. The site’s own 21 July revision to another article now states this point explicitly: one physical state may be known under first-person and neurological modes, and epistemic asymmetry does not itself entail ontological difference. [concepts/disguised-property-dualism](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/disguised-property-dualism/)

The 2020s debate contains arguments on both sides that the article should use:

- Zhong’s 2024 “problem of double aspect” argues that some phenomenal-concept accounts may recreate property dualism at the level of modes of presentation. This is a sophisticated version of a claim close to the Map’s.
- Zhou’s 2025 response develops an a posteriori physicalism combining property monism with conceptual dualism and defends essence-indicating phenomenal concepts.
- Botin and Kortabarria’s 2026 grounding physicalism offers two strategies for accommodating substantive phenomenal knowledge while retaining physicalism. [OUP Academic+2Springer+2](https://academic.oup.com/pq/article-abstract/74/3/978/7325259)

A serious article should adjudicate that exchange. At present, it largely assumes that failure of a priori derivation or full explanatory transparency makes a second property ontology increasingly likely.

### It neglects its own “progressive research programme” escape condition

Condition 3 sensibly allows an unfinished but progressive programme to escape the diagnostic. Yet the worked examples are not assessed systematically against that condition.

IIT 4.0 presents an updated formalism, applications to the quality and quantity of experience and an explicit programme of empirical and phenomenological extension. One may judge the programme unsuccessful, but the article must examine whether this constitutes the very “accumulating unification” it says should count. [PLOS+1](https://journals.plos.org/ploscompbiol/article)

The predictive-processing companion article now describes the 2025 “beautiful-loop” theory as the Map’s strongest contemporary rival: a direct attempt to identify structural conditions jointly sufficient for phenomenal experience. That is precisely the sort of developing programme that the target article’s third condition says must not be dismissed merely because a completed reduction is absent. The target article neither discusses it nor explains why it fails the progressive-programme standard. [topics/predictive-processing-and-dualism](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/predictive-processing-and-dualism/)

### It does not compare total explanatory burdens

The article repeatedly treats open acknowledgment of primitives as “paying the cost.” Honesty about a cost is valuable, but it is not the same as discharging it.

A fair comparison would count, for every ontology:

- unexplained fundamental entities or properties;
- primitive laws and grounding relations;
- causal coupling and pairing problems;
- compatibility with physical causal explanation;
- novel predictions;
- empirical discriminability;
- residual brute facts.

Without that ledger, the article compares the monist’s incomplete unification to the dualist’s openly primitive bridge and treats candour as if it were explanatory success.

## 4. Tenet-protective bracketing is materially present

The Map is admirably explicit that it begins from dualism and that its five tenets constrain every article. Its writing guide permits opposition but requires content ultimately to align with, or explicitly mark tension with, those foundational commitments. This is transparent framework construction rather than concealed neutrality. [The Unfinishable Map+3The Unfinishable Map+3The Unfinishable Map+3](https://unfinishablemap.org/)

The problem is the way that framework is protected in this particular article.

### The self-exemption is semantic, not explanatory

The article’s defence is:

- the Map accepts two irreducible structures;
- it does not claim they reduce to one;
- therefore the “unification not demonstrated” condition is vacuous;
- no promissory note exists because “no promise was issued.”

That succeeds at one limited point: the Map is not **disguised** dualism because it calls itself dualist. It does not establish the article’s broader conclusions that rivals and the Map possess the same structural commitments, that the rivals’ parsimony is merely verbal, or that the Map has borne its explanatory burden more successfully. [concepts/disguised-property-dualism](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/disguised-property-dualism/)

The criterion is structurally favourable to the tenet:

- a monist who attempts unification is penalised until the unification is sufficiently complete;
- a dualist who makes the relation primitive is exempt because no unification was attempted.

That rewards lower explanatory ambition. A brute psychophysical law is not superior to an incomplete grounding or identity account merely because it is announced as brute.

### The article overstates the Map’s own mechanism

The target article says the Map “proposes a quantum interface as the mechanism.” The canonical bi-aspectual article is more cautious: “actuality” names a posited function, not a mechanism, and specifying how consciousness actualises physical possibilities remains open. The psychophysical-laws article similarly says that without a precise mapping from phenomenal properties to physical selections, “consciousness selects” remains a metaphor rather than a research programme. [concepts/disguised-property-dualism](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/disguised-property-dualism/)

This is an important asymmetry. Markovian monism and IIT are judged against demanding standards of derivation, mechanistic explanation and progressive empirical linkage, while the Map’s quantum proposal is described as a mechanism in the target article even though surrounding articles classify its central mapping as unresolved.

The chemistry analogy does not repair this. Chemistry’s lack of complete derivation from fundamental physics does not imply an ontologically separate chemical actuality linked to physics by unspecified psychophysical laws. More importantly, the Map’s own documents acknowledge that the proposed coupling currently lacks the mapping precision and discriminating predictions that the target article demands from rivals.

### The review process explicitly brackets the core claim

The 16 July review called the article “conceptually converged,” declined to re-litigate its diagnostic or self-application defence, instructed future reviewers not to reopen them, and categorised physicalist disagreement as a bedrock framework-boundary disagreement rather than a fixable defect. [reviews/deep-review-2026-07-16-disguised-property-dualism](https://unfinishablemap.org/reviews/deep-review-2026-07-16-disguised-property-dualism/)

That is the clearest evidence of tenet-protective bracketing. The issue is not merely that the article argues from dualist premises; it is that the tenet-supporting inference was closed to substantive review before author-stance fidelity and literature currency had been adequately tested.

The bracketing is therefore **transparent rather than covert, but methodologically consequential**. The article itself admits that a critic may regard the diagnostic as a scoring device and that its asymmetry may be too thin. That concession is intellectually honest. It should be promoted from a residual caveat to the main epistemic qualification.

## 5. Integration with surrounding articles is uneven

### Predictive processing

The predictive-processing article still places “mutually reductive” in quotation marks and continues to call the two geometries irreducible and Markovian monism the paradigm case of disguised property dualism. This remains live even though the target article’s review expressly established that Friston never uses that phrase. The review nevertheless concluded that no corpus propagation was needed. [reviews/deep-review-2026-07-16-disguised-property-dualism](https://unfinishablemap.org/reviews/deep-review-2026-07-16-disguised-property-dualism/)

That same companion article recognises the 2025 beautiful-loop proposal as a serious, direct and still-developing physicalist theory of consciousness. The target article should integrate that development into its condition-3 analysis rather than preserving a diagnosis based almost entirely on the 2020 paper.

### Russellian monism

The dedicated Russellian comparison is more nuanced than the target article. It acknowledges that the force of Pautz’s parity argument depends on whether necessity or grounding can be motivated independently, and that Russellian defenders can resist the verdict. It also mentions 2024 and 2025 variants. The target article omits those qualifications and instead says the difference is one no evidence can adjudicate. [topics/russellian-monism-versus-bi-aspectual-dualism](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/russellian-monism-versus-bi-aspectual-dualism/)

The sibling’s phrase “Recent work by Pautz (2017), Cutter (2019), and Kind (2015)” is itself stale in 2026 and should be revised, but it nonetheless offers a more balanced dialectical presentation than the concept article.

### IIT

The dedicated IIT article was modified on 22 July 2026, six days after the target article. It identifies IIT’s thesis as an identity claim, states the identity theorist’s reply, acknowledges that the Map’s passivity argument can be charged with circularity, and concedes that criticism of IIT does not by itself establish the Map’s interactionist alternative. It also incorporates current comparative evidence. The target article should inherit this more careful treatment. [topics/consciousness-and-integrated-information](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/consciousness-and-integrated-information/)

### Core ontology and psychophysical laws

The target article says explicit dualism pays its costs through brute laws and a quantum mechanism. The bi-aspectual and psychophysical-law articles correctly state that the mechanism and mapping remain central open problems. These disclosures should appear directly in the self-application section, not be left for readers to discover through links.

### Biological naturalism

The site now calls Searle’s biological naturalism its most important non-dualist rival and the strongest attempt to preserve real, ontologically irreducible consciousness without dualism. Yet the target article mentions it only in further reading rather than testing it as a worked example. Because Searle expressly distinguishes causal from ontological reduction, biological naturalism would be a far more exacting case for the diagnostic than Markovian information geometry. [concepts/biological-naturalism](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/biological-naturalism/)

## 6. Assessment of the workflow and changelog

There are genuine methodological strengths:

- The site preserves review history and exposes failures.
- The 16 July review openly admitted that three defects survived four previous verified reviews.
- On 21 July, the workflow added author-stance verification and explicitly separated bibliographic existence, attribution fidelity and literature currency.
- Another article was corrected the same day for exactly the epistemic-to-ontological inference made here: treating two modes of knowing as evidence for two properties. [reviews/deep-review-2026-07-16-disguised-property-dualism](https://unfinishablemap.org/reviews/deep-review-2026-07-16-disguised-property-dualism/)

The difficulty is that the target article was declared permanently converged immediately before those improved procedures were adopted. It therefore escaped the methodology that its central citations most need.

The current concept-article standard also requires a neutral definition first, the Map’s interpretation explicitly marked, the strongest rivals presented, and disputed implications separated. Finally, the literature-drift workflow is not presently capable of solving the problem reliably. The 21 July changelog says only three live topic files match the configured active-research patterns, that patterns including `iit` match no live files, and that the audit is structurally inert until its matching and rotation policy are retuned. ## Concrete potential improvements

### To “Disguised Property Dualism”

1. **Reframe the main claim.** Rename the core test “unearned monist unification,” “unexplained unity,” or “monist explanatory debt.” Reserve “disguised property dualism” for cases where two genuinely distinct, metaphysically irreducible property types have first been established.
2. **Add a neutral opening definition.** State immediately that this is a Map-specific, contested diagnostic rather than an established category in philosophy of mind, and put the “heuristic, not metaphysical verdict” qualification in the opening paragraph.
3. **Replace condition 1 with an ontological threshold.** Explicitly distinguish descriptions, concepts, mathematical geometries, explanatory levels, aspects, properties and substances. Only the last two categories should trigger an ontological diagnosis.
4. **Disambiguate irreducibility.** Separate conceptual, epistemic, formal, explanatory, nomological and metaphysical irreducibility. Do not move from one sense to another without argument.
5. **Remove the circularity between conditions 1 and 3.** Establish independently that two properties are posited before asking whether their relation has been explained. A useful replacement would be a two-axis matrix: one versus multiple fundamental properties, and well-specified versus poorly specified dependence.
6. **Expand the recognised forms of unification.** Add grounding, realization, constitution, role–realizer identification, theoretical identification, mechanistic integration and model equivalence. Explain what evidence would count for or against each.
7. **Rewrite the Markovian example.** Quote the authors’ one-irreducible-property claim, state that they deny that dual geometry entails property dualism, and recast the criticism as metaphysical overinterpretation or an incomplete consciousness theory—not covert dualism.
8. **Rewrite the Russellian example by variant.** Distinguish reductive Russellian physicalism, non-reductive grounding versions, panpsychism, panprotopsychism, dual-aspect monism and explicitly dualist variants. Restore Cutter’s conditional scope and replace the unrestricted “no evidence can adjudicate” claim.
9. **Update the Russellian bibliography.** Add Alter and Pereboom on structuralism, representative 2023–2025 defences, Hashemi’s dual-aspect interpretation and Liu’s 2026 cognitive-fruitfulness criticism.
10. **Replace the IIT discussion with IIT 4.0.** Analyse its explanatory-identity claim, operational physicalism, experience-first ontology and actual progressive programme. Remove the unsupported statement that Tononi openly accepts property-dualist implications unless a precise current source is supplied.
11. **Add current IIT objections.** Cover the axiom-to-postulate inference, intrinsicality 2.0, the engineering problem, idealistic ontology and the tension between intrinsic existence and operationally specified physical existence.
12. **Steelman Type-B physicalism.** Give the phenomenal-concept and modes-of-presentation account in its strongest form, then explain why the Map thinks it fails. Engage Zhong 2024, Zhou 2025 and current grounding physicalism rather than treating deduction or mechanism as the only options.
13. **Rewrite the self-application section.** Say plainly that the Map escapes only the *disguise* charge. Compare total explanatory burdens using a symmetric ledger covering primitives, psychophysical laws, causal coupling, testability and empirical progress.
14. **Correct the quantum-mechanism claim.** Describe the interface as a proposed locus or programme whose coupling law remains unspecified, consistent with the bi-aspectual and psychophysical-law articles.
15. **Remove or sharply qualify the chemistry analogy.** Explain the relevant disanalogies instead of using incomplete intertheoretic reduction in science to exempt primitive psychophysical coupling.
16. **Replace verdict language with calibrated language.** Change “the diagnostic conditions are met” to separate findings such as “the unification remains incomplete,” “the ontology is underdetermined,” and “a property-dualist interpretation is possible but not established.”

### To surrounding articles

1. **`predictive-processing-and-dualism`:** remove quotation marks from “mutually reductive,” cease calling the geometries irreducible without an independent argument, and stop presenting Markovian monism as the paradigm case of property dualism.
2. **`predictive-processing-and-dualism`:** integrate the beautiful-loop theory directly into the progressive-programme assessment and specify what future result would count as genuine explanatory progress.
3. **`russellian-monism-versus-bi-aspectual-dualism`:** replace the stale phrase “Recent work” for 2015–2019 sources, add current defences and critiques, and preserve its own caveat about the independent motivation of necessity or grounding.
4. **`substance-property-dualism`:** cross-link its stricter property-level definition into the diagnostic article and require all uses of “property dualism” across the corpus to meet that threshold.
5. **`consciousness-and-integrated-information`:** synchronise its IIT 4.0 account with the diagnostic article and distinguish problems for IIT from positive evidence for interactionist dualism.
6. **`bi-aspectual-ontology` and `psychophysical-laws`:** ensure every comparative article imports their acknowledgment that “actuality” is not yet a mechanism and that the phenomenal-to-physical mapping remains unsolved.
7. **`biological-naturalism`:** either make Searle’s view a full worked case in the diagnostic article or stop calling it the canonical target until the diagnostic directly engages his causal/ontological reduction distinction.
8. **The Tenets and Type-B material:** link the existing phenomenal-concept concession into the diagnostic so that the strongest identity-theorist response is visible at the point where the ontology inference is made.

### To the site’s methodology

1. **Create automatic reopen triggers.** A “converged” article should reopen when a theory receives a new major version, a new citation-verification method is adopted, or a load-bearing recent source directly challenges its classification.
