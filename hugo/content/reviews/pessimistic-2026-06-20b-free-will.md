## Critiques by Philosopher

### The Eliminative Materialist (Churchland)
The article's central evidential engine — "structured phenomenology + neural correlates is *better explained* by genuine agent involvement than by systematic misleading" (lines 101, 105, 189) — assumes the phenomenological taxonomy (initiation / sustained control / effort / veto) carves a real joint. Churchland would say this is folk-psychological pre-theory: the "four components" are not natural kinds but introspective artifacts, and the "four independent tricks" rebuttal (line 105) begs the question by treating them as four things needing four explanations rather than one confabulatory disposition with four labels. The article *does* anticipate her ("though whether the phenomenology is ultimately veridical is precisely what the eliminativist disputes, and the argument here aims to shift the burden rather than to close the question") — an honest concession that defangs much of the attack, but see Issue 2: the "four tricks" framing is doing more argumentative work than the concession admits.

### The Hard-Nosed Physicalist (Dennett)
"The physicalist can accommodate the neural data—different neural modes explain different signatures. But the physicalist must then explain why the phenomenological distinction *exists*" (line 101). Dennett's reply: the physicalist owes no such explanation beyond the functional one already given — willed and automatic processing differ functionally, the system represents that difference, and "feeling effortful" *is* that representation. The article frames the explanandum as "why the distinction exists" when the physicalist has already answered it; the residual demand ("why does it feel like something") is the hard problem smuggled in, not a free-will-specific debt. This is fair-but-by-design (the Map is openly dualist), so it is an in-framework disagreement, not a flaggable gap.

### The Quantum Skeptic (Tegmark)
The article is commendably non-committal on mechanism (line 117: "The Map doesn't commit to any single mechanism") and explicitly concedes (line 117) "whether a quantum interface, in particular, would deliver *genuine origination* rather than mere indeterminism is itself underdetermined." Tegmark's decoherence objection is routed to `[[decoherence]]` rather than answered here. No overreach — the article does not assert a quantum mechanism works; it asserts the philosophical case does not depend on one. Defensible.

### The Many-Worlds Defender (Deutsch)
The No-MWI paragraph (line 203) is the article's strongest single passage: it cleanly distinguishes "branch-local agent who deliberates and reports choosing" (which MWI supplies) from "counterfactual exclusion" (which it cannot), and correctly downgrades the rejection to a framework-boundary disagreement on personhood/indexical identity rather than overclaiming "selection requires real collapse." Deutsch would still say the demand for "counterfactual exclusion" is itself the question-begging anthropocentric premise — but the article *names* this as a tenet-register move and routes it honestly to the MWI checklist. This is the discipline working correctly; no flag.

### The Empiricist (Popper's ghost)
The "What Would Challenge This Framework?" section (lines 170-179) lists four falsifiers, but three of the four are arguably unwinnable-by-construction for the critic: "prior neural states *sufficient* for choice outcomes, leaving no residual variance" and "proof that causal closure holds without exception at all scales" are demands the article's own [agency-void](/voids/agency-void/)/`causal-interface` apparatus elsewhere argues are *structurally* unverifiable from the first person. So the falsification conditions, while real in principle, are positioned such that the framework's other commitments predict they cannot be met — a soft unfalsifiability the article does not flag. This is a genuine tension worth noting (Issue 3 in the older catalogue sense), though the article's honest "This isn't a proof" framing (line 189) blunts it.

### The Buddhist Philosopher (Nagarjuna)
The article leans hard on a persisting *substance* self (lines 81, 93, 146: "the agent *as persisting substance* directly causes choices"). Nagarjuna: this reifies exactly what is empty. The article routes the substance commitment to `[[where-the-substance-commitment-enters]]` and is admirably explicit that the substance-lean is "downstream of the agent-causal commitment, not inherited from the Dualism tenet" — so a reader can locate and reject the move. Fair handling; in-framework disagreement, not a gap.

## Critical Issues

### Issue 1: Dream-incorporation evidence imported without its source's calibration caveats
- **File**: `obsidian/topics/free-will.md`, line 150
- **Location**: §"Agent Causation and Supporting Evidence" — "Dream problem-solving provides independent evidence: dream incorporation more than doubled solving rates for unsolved puzzles, suggesting consciousness generates novel approaches in a phenomenally different mode. If consciousness were epiphenomenal, the phenomenal character of dreaming should be irrelevant to problem-solving success."
- **Problem**: This is a genuine **cross-article calibration asymmetry** (sibling of the empirical-record-currency-drift / context-loss family). The source article [topics/dream-consciousness.md](/topics/dream-consciousness/) carries explicit, substantial caveats on exactly this result (Konkoly et al. 2026): *"This is a small study (N=20) using participants selected for lucid dreaming experience—not a representative sample. The incorporation effect is significant in the paper's main regression model (p = .037) but only a non-significant trend (p = .057) in a simpler chi-square framing... It has not been independently replicated."* The free-will article strips all of this and presents the finding as bald "independent evidence" with a strong "more than doubled" headline and an epiphenomenalism-defeating conditional. Calibration that the corpus *has already done* is silently lost on import. **VERIFIED**: the underlying figures (42% vs 17%, N=20, p=.037/.057) were grep-confirmed in `dream-consciousness.md` lines 126-128, 150, 170; the free-will article's line 150 carries none of them.
- **Severity**: Medium (calibration regression on an internally-sourced empirical claim; not a fabrication — the result is real and faithfully cited at source).
- **Recommendation**: `refine-draft` pass: soften "provides independent evidence" → "provides suggestive (and contested) evidence," and add a single clause flagging the small lucid-dreamer sample / non-replication, matching the source article's calibration. Length-neutral or near-so.

## Counterarguments to Address

### The "four independent tricks" independence assumption (Issue 2)
- **Current content says** (line 105): "Dismissing volitional phenomenology as illusion would, on this reading, require not one trick but four independent tricks — fabricating initiation, sustained control, effort gradients, and active veto, each calibrated to real cognitive demands with distinct neural substrates."
- **A critic would argue**: The rhetorical force depends on the four being *independent* — four separate improbabilities multiply into one large improbability. But the eliminativist posits a *single* disposition (the brain confabulates an agency-narrative tracking processing demands); on that hypothesis the "four" are one phenomenon with four facets, and there is exactly *one* trick, not four. The article asserts independence ("four independent tricks") without arguing it. The very fact that all four "track real cognitive demands with distinct neural substrates" is *equally* what a single demand-tracking confabulation predicts — the clause meant to establish independence is consistent with the unified-confabulation alternative.
- **Suggested response**: Either (a) argue the independence (why would a unified confabulation produce *dissociable* clinical breakdowns — schizophrenia initiation-failure vs anarchic-hand ownership-failure — if it were one mechanism? the dissociation evidence on line 105 actually *does* gesture at this and is the stronger move), or (b) drop "independent" and rest the argument on the dissociation data alone, which carries the weight without the unearned multiplication. **Medium** — the substance is recoverable; the word "independent" overstates.

### Soft unfalsifiability of two of the four stated falsifiers (Issue 3 — note, not task)
- **Current content says** (lines 174, 177): falsifiers include neural states shown "*sufficient*... leaving no residual variance" and "proof that causal closure holds without exception at all scales."
- **A critic would argue**: The article's own `[[agency-void]]` and `[[causal-interface]]` machinery argues these are structurally unverifiable from the first person / systematically invisible — so the framework predicts its own falsifiers cannot be reached. This is the standard Popperian charge against interactionism.
- **Suggested response**: Lower priority — the article's "This isn't a proof... a coherent picture" framing (line 189) is an honest pre-emption, and the falsifiers are real *in principle* (a *third-person* demonstration of sufficiency is not barred by the first-person verification-failure argument). Worth a one-clause acknowledgment that these are third-person-empirical falsifiers not blocked by the first-person opacity results, but not severe enough to mint its own task. Fold into the Issue-1 refine if convenient.
