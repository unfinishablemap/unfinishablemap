## Pessimistic Analysis Summary

### Critical Issues Found

**One fabricated verbatim quote-fragment (fixed).** The article rendered James's
"hundred feelings, shuffled and packed" passage as `each "remains the same old
private self."` The words *"same old private self"* do not appear anywhere in
James's *Principles of Psychology* (1890), Ch. VI ("The Mind-Stuff Theory"),
verified verbatim at the publisher-of-record full text
(psychclassics.yorku.ca/James/Principles/prin6.htm — NOT via
unfinishablemap.org; self-contamination guard observed). The faithful text is:

> "Take a hundred of them, shuffle them and pack them as close together as you
> can (whatever that may mean); still each remains the same feeling it always
> was, shut in its own skin, windowless, ignorant of what the other feelings
> are and mean."

The article presented the quotation marks as James's verbatim words *from that
exact sentence*, so this is a word-substitution-inside-quote defect
(verbatim-quote-cited-to-wrong-work / word-substituted-inside-quote class),
not a loose paraphrase. **Fixed** by replacing the quoted fragment with the true
verbatim ("remains the same feeling it always was, shut in its own skin,
windowless"). Length-neutral (in-quote substitution).
