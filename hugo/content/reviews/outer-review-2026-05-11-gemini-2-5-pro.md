Interestingly, while strictly anti-physicalist, the platform remains philosophically nuanced regarding the traditional division between substance dualism (which posits distinct physical and mental substances) and property dualism (which posits one substance with distinct physical and mental properties). To support the assertion of irreducibility, the platform does not rely on a single line of reasoning. It deploys seven independent anti-physicalist vectors, including the knowledge argument, conceivability (zombie) arguments, intentionality, the unity of conscious experience, and the fundamental nature of subjectivity. Furthermore, the platform frames this commitment meta-epistemologically: keeping dualism as a live, viable option is scientifically and philosophically responsible given the cost-asymmetry of the debate. If materialism is false but universally adopted, science blinds itself to vast aspects of reality; if dualism is false but explored, science merely loses research efficiency but remains ultimately correctable.   

### Tenet 2: Minimal Quantum Interaction

To possess genuine causal efficacy without violating the fundamental laws of physics, a dualist model must identify a precise, physically permissible interface. The Minimal Quantum Interaction tenet specifies that if consciousness influences the physical world, it must do so exclusively at the microscopic quantum level. Specifically, consciousness is hypothesized to bias otherwise indeterminate quantum outcomes without injecting new energy into the system or violating the conservation laws of thermodynamics.   

The underlying logic of this tenet ruthlessly exploits the well-documented ontological incompleteness of standard quantum mechanics. While the deterministic Schrödinger equation perfectly maps the temporal evolution of probability amplitudes, standard physical theory provides no sufficient physical realizer to dictate *which* specific outcome transitions from a superposition of possibilities into a definite actuality upon measurement. The Map posits that irreducible consciousness operates precisely within this specific causal vacuum. It is explicitly termed a "minimal" interaction because it represents the absolute smallest necessary conceptual deviation from standard physical models required to permit mind-matter causation, firmly anchoring the mind's agency in the principle of "selection, not injection".   

### Tenet 3: Bidirectional Interaction and Epistemic Survival

Interactionist dualism intrinsically requires a bidirectional flow of causation. The Bidirectional Interaction tenet asserts that physical brain states structure, limit, and constrain conscious experience (the upward causation universally accepted by neuroscience), just as conscious states causally dictate physical brain states and subsequent motor behaviors (the downward causation rejected by epiphenomenalism).   

The platform defends downward causation by arguing that denying it leads to inescapable epistemic self-stultification. The argument posits that if consciousness is truly epiphenomenal—a useless byproduct of neural activity—then human utterances, writings, and philosophical debates regarding consciousness are not actually caused by the subjective experience of consciousness itself. If our theories of consciousness are not causally linked to our experience of it, we have zero epistemic grounds to trust those theories, completely undermining the physicalist denial of mental causation.   

Furthermore, evolutionary biology provides a powerful, indirect form of support. The platform highlights that phenomenal valence—the acute, subjective sensation of pleasure or pain—tracks evolutionary fitness with extraordinary precision. If the phenomenal sensation of pain did not genuinely, causally induce an organism to withdraw from physical danger, natural selection would have had no mechanism by which to optimize it. Thus, bidirectional causation is defended not merely as a metaphysical luxury, but as a strict biological necessity.   

### Tenet 4: Rejection of Many-Worlds (No MWI)

The platform explicitly and aggressively rejects the Many-Worlds Interpretation (MWI) of quantum mechanics. MWI mathematically eliminates the quantum measurement problem by proposing that wave function collapse never actually occurs; instead, the universe continuously and seamlessly branches, realizing every possible quantum outcome in parallel, orthogonal, and entirely real universes.   

The Map's rejection of this paradigm is grounded in three profound philosophical critiques :   

1. **Ontological Proliferation:** MWI exacts a devastating and infinite ontological cost. The platform rejects the postulation of countless parallel realities and infinite copies of every conscious observer merely to preserve the mathematical elegance of deterministic equations.
2. **The Indexical Identity Problem:** MWI completely fails to provide theoretical resources to answer the indexical question of subjective identity. In a branching universe, the question "Why am I experiencing *this* specific branch and not another equally real branch?" becomes meaningless. The Map asserts that indexical identity is a genuine feature of reality, and an interpretation of physics that dissolves the continuity of the experiencing subject trades one mystery (measurement) for a far deeper one (identity).
3. **The Prebiotic Collapse Problem:** If the Map argues that consciousness collapses the wave function, it must address cosmology: how did the physical universe evolve, form stars, and undergo chemical reactions for billions of years before conscious biological observers existed?. To resolve this, the Map innovates the hypothesis of *Objective Reduction with Consciousness Modulation*. It theorizes that entirely physical mechanisms—such as spontaneous localization or gravitational objective reduction—provide a constant "baseline collapse" across the prebiotic cosmos. Consciousness, however, explicitly interfaces with this baseline collapse within highly structured biological neural systems, modulating quantum outcomes locally where the brain provides the appropriate, evolved physical interface.

### The Energy Conservation Problem and Quantum Selection

The most devastating historical critique of interactionism, sharpened extensively by thinkers like Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz, is the conservation of energy objection. The argument posits that if a non-physical mind "pushes" physical matter—for instance, by triggering a specific neuron to fire to initiate a motor action—it must necessarily inject energy into a closed physical system, thereby blatantly violating the first law of thermodynamics.   

The Map circumvents this formidable obstacle via the precise mechanics of the Minimal Quantum Interaction tenet. The framework theorizes that consciousness does not interact with classical, deterministic systems; rather, it operates exclusively at points of genuine quantum indeterminacy within the brain. In quantum mechanics, physical laws establish the exact probability distribution of potential outcomes, but they remain strictly and ontologically silent regarding which specific outcome will actually manifest upon the event of measurement. The platform posits that consciousness acts as the selective agent, choosing among these physically permissible, equal-energy alternatives.   

To operationalize this theoretical stance, the framework explicitly invokes the Quantum Zeno Effect, referencing models developed by quantum physicists such as Henry Stapp. Under this specific model, intense, directed mental attention functions equivalently to a series of highly rapid quantum observations. By continuously "observing" a specific, desired quantum state within the neural interface, consciousness can effectively "freeze" that neural pattern in place, preventing it from evolving into alternative, unwanted states. Because this cognitive process merely biases probabilities already inherent in the physical system's superposition, it requires exactly zero energy injection, rendering the thermodynamic conservation objection entirely moot. Furthermore, the platform notes that demanding strict, local conservation of energy inside the highly complex environment of the brain essentially begs the question, as it assumes the very causal closure of the physical that dualism explicitly sets out to challenge.   

### The Pairing Problem and the Metaphysics of Spatial Dualism

Another historically significant objection is the "pairing problem," famously articulated by philosopher Jaegwon Kim. The objection asks: If two identical, entirely non-physical minds exist outside of spatial dimensions, what structural mechanism could possibly pair Mind A exclusively with Body A, and Mind B exclusively with Body B, if they lack the physical, spatial coordinates necessary to ground that causal connection?.   

The Map resolves this complex issue by adopting and defending a framework of *spatial dualism*. In doing so, it explicitly and forcefully rejects the classical Cartesian assumption that the mind lacks spatial extension. Instead, the Map posits that the mind is, in fact, spatially located—specifically situated within the brain, exactly at the neurobiological sites where it interfaces with physical matter. The distinctiveness of consciousness, the framework clarifies, is entirely *metaphysical*, not spatial. A mental property can possess wholly irreducible, non-physical phenomenal qualities—such as the subjective sensation of pain, the experience of redness, or the unified nature of subjectivity—while simultaneously maintaining a strict spatial geometry and location that solves Kim's pairing problem natively and effortlessly.
