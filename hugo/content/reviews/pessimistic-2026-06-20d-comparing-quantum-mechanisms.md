## Critiques by Philosopher

### The Eliminative Materialist
"You catalogue four mechanisms whose entire empirical record — by your own admission — 'is also consistent with classical explanations' and which 'none generates predictions cleanly distinguishing it from classical neuroscience.' Strip the dualist premise you stipulate in the opening paragraph and nothing in this comparison survives as evidence *for* a non-physical mind; it survives only as taxonomy of where you would *like* one to act. The honest title is 'where dualists could insert an interface,' not 'mechanisms.'" — This is a fair characterisation but the article largely pre-empts it: it explicitly flags the classical-sufficiency parity throughout and frames the exercise as fit-ranking-within-a-stipulated-premise, not as evidence. Not a defect; the premise-stipulation is disclosed in sentence one. (See Finding A, though — the taxonomy is not as clean as the article claims.)

### The Hard-Nosed Physicalist

That is curve-fitting promoted to a virtue. The criterion rewards the theory most explicitly reverse-engineered from introspection." — The article hedges this honestly (lines 84, 147) but the *preference ordering* still inherits the credit: Stapp ranks co-first partly on a criterion the article itself rates as low-weight and post-hoc. Worth a flag (Finding C, low/medium).

## Critical Issues

### Issue 1: Taxonomic self-contradiction — "all four are consciousness-mediated-collapse variants"
- **File**: [topics/comparing-quantum-consciousness-mechanisms.md](/topics/comparing-quantum-consciousness-mechanisms/)
- **Location**: §The Post-Decoherence Gap, line 60 (vs lines 64, 70, 100)
- **Problem**: Line 60 lists five selection families and explicitly places **Penrose-Diósi under family (2) "objective collapse"** alongside GRW and CSL. The same paragraph then concludes: *"Only consciousness-mediated collapse [family 1] locates a non-physical agent where physics leaves outcomes undetermined. The four mechanisms compared here are all variants within this family."* But Orch OR (one of the four) is the Penrose-Diósi mechanism just assigned to family (2) — and the article's own Orch OR section reinforces this, calling its collapse "gravitational… automatic once threshold is reached, leaving no role for felt effort" (line 70), i.e. objective, not consciousness-mediated. Fisher is also a strained member: line 100 states "nothing in the proposal requires a non-physical observer." So the sweeping "all four are variants within [the consciousness-mediated-collapse] family" is contradicted by (a) the article's own family assignment of Penrose-Diósi and (b) its own caveats on Orch OR's automaticity and Fisher's observer-independence.
- **Severity**: Medium
- **Recommendation**: Soften line 60 to reflect the article's actual position: the four are the candidate *interfaces the Map reads as compatible with consciousness-mediated selection*, with Orch OR and Fisher being the strained members (Orch OR's bare collapse is objective and must be supplemented by a selection role; Fisher's proposal as stated requires no observer and earns dualist relevance only when coupled to a selection story). This is a one-or-two-sentence calibration, not a restructure — and it actually strengthens the later §preference-ordering point that Orch OR "pays the highest tenet-fit cost."
- **Grep status**: VERIFIED present in live file (lines 60, 70, 100 quoted verbatim above; corroborated by [positions/quantum-interface.md](/positions/quantum-interface/) which treats Donadi as constraining the Diósi-Penrose *objective*-collapse model).
