## Pessimistic Analysis Summary

### Critical Issues Found

- **Over-scoped categorical claim (currency)** — Lede opened "MMI is the *one* mainstream reading of quantum mechanics that writes minds directly into the formalism." False as a categorical: consciousness-*collapse* interpretations also write mind into the formalism — the von Neumann–Wigner tradition (which the article itself cross-links) and, more pointedly, the formalized Chalmers–McQueen consciousness-collapse models, a live 2020s program. RESOLVED — re-scoped to "the *no-collapse* reading... that writes minds directly into the formalism," with a parenthetical distinguishing consciousness-collapse approaches (mind in the collapse dynamics) from MMI (unitary physics, multiplicity in minds). This is the accurate distinctive claim and preserves the MWI contrast in the next sentence. This is the currency vector the review discipline targets — a categorical claim the live literature refutes, invisible to the lexical superlative-scanner.

- **Paraphrase presented as verbatim quote (Albert & Loewer)** — The motivating-constraint sentence was in quotation marks attributed to Albert and Loewer (1988) ending "...observed with complete certainty." Independent verify: the string "complete certainty" does not appear in the paper; the paper says "with certainty" (p.198) and speaks of an observer who "correctly reports that she has a definite belief" (p.206). The prior review's ledger claimed this passage "verified" — it was not verbatim. Same defect class as the prior review's own IEP paraphrase-as-quote fix. RESOLVED — de-quoted to reported speech preserving the paper's actual "with certainty" phrasing and the substance.

- **Citation metadata: Sebens & Carroll year/venue** — cited as "(2016)" inline and References with no volume/pages. The citable published record is *BJPS* 69(1): 25–74 (2018); 2016 is the online-first year. RESOLVED — References updated to 2018, 69(1): 25–74 (online 2016); inline cite reconciled 2016→2018.
