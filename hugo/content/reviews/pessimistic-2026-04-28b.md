## Critiques by Philosopher

### The Eliminative Materialist (Patricia Churchland)
"Cross-activation between V4 and grapheme-form area in fusiform gyrus is a neuroscience finding, not a metaphysical mystery. The article's claim that 'no amount of neurological description conveys what it is like to experience a synesthetic colour' is the conclusion the article wants, presented as a premise. Synesthetes' reports are exactly what an eliminative theory predicts will be unreliable when subjects describe their own neural patterns through inherited folk-psychological vocabulary. Calling this an 'extra qualia problem' adds an explanatory unit ('qualia') to a phenomenon that already has a complete neural description; that is the opposite of the parsimony the Map elsewhere claims to honour. The article hides this move by routing it through Wager's term-of-art rather than defending the qualia-realism it presupposes."

### The Hard-Nosed Physicalist (Daniel Dennett)
"Heterophenomenology, please. The 'extra qualia challenge representationalism' argument requires that we accept synesthetes' reports as direct evidence about phenomenal states, not just as data about what synesthetes are inclined to say. The article's own line — 'most synesthetic experience is mediated through semantics' — is a confession that the content is functional/representational at a deeper grain than the article credits. If grapheme-meaning processing is part of what generates the colour, the 'identical representations' claim has already been falsified: the representations are not identical, they're identical-at-a-shallow-level. The 'extraordinary feature' (dual occupancy without blending) is also under-defended; rivalrous interpretations exist (e.g. inattentional rather than co-located) and the article cites only MacPherson's reading."

### The Quantum Skeptic (Max Tegmark)
"The article does not invoke quantum mechanics directly — but it does invoke E-space (Rosseinsky & Bhatt 2022) as if it were established cartography, and a 'filter theory' as if mechanism were specified. Both are placeholders. 'High-dimensional space mapping all possible qualia' has no operational definition, no metric, no orthogonality conditions, and no measurement procedure. Citing it gives the section the surface texture of physics ('several times as many dimensions as there are categories of qualia') without doing physics."

### The Many-Worlds Defender (David Deutsch)
"The article's 'No Many Worlds' paragraph is the weakest of the four tenet sections. 'My experiential palette is *this one*, not some other' is exactly the indexical fact MWI accommodates via Saunders/Wallace-style indexical observer-relative branching — there is nothing in synesthetic asymmetry that pressures the universal wavefunction. Reporting indexicality as anti-MWI evidence misrepresents the live debate. The article quietly imports a particular reading of indexicality (one that requires a unique branch) without arguing for it."

### The Empiricist (Karl Popper's Ghost)
"What observation could distinguish 'consciousness restricted by neural architecture' from 'consciousness identical with neural architecture'? The filter theory reads the developmental pruning data as evidence for an extra-cerebral consciousness whose access is narrowed. But every datum cited — synesthetic reports, training fade-out, sensory-deprivation cross-modal effects, neonatal connectivity — is fully consistent with brain-only processing producing the relevant phenomenal-access patterns. The article admits the neonatal hypothesis evidence is 'individually weak, collectively suggestive' but then deploys it as load-bearing for filter-theory dualism. That is unfalsifiability with a politeness layer. The teleological 'designed for narrowness' (Sec. 'Developmental Occlusion') is also unbecoming — design language for a non-design process needs justification or rephrasing."

### The Buddhist Philosopher (Nagarjuna)
"The 'synesthetic void' presupposes the very phenomenal continents whose boundary it claims to map. 'Architectural incompatibility' between minds depends on those minds being substantial enough to be incompatible. The Map elsewhere allows that void-discourse can dissolve its objects under analysis (cf. apophatic-cartography); this article does not. 'Qualia,' 'E-space,' and 'phenomenal architecture' are all conventional designations being treated as ultimately real. The void you describe is itself empty of the kind of fixed boundary your prose attributes to it."

E.g. line 43: *"The recipient lacks the phenomenal reference points that would make understanding possible — a structural incommensurability rather than ordinary expressive difficulty."* Line 83: *"The barrier is architectural incompatibility, not privacy or secrecy — a concrete instance of the [explanatory-gap](/concepts/explanatory-gap/) between two human minds rather than between physical description and phenomenal experience."*

### Issue 2: "Active architectural maintenance" inference from training fade-out is over-strong
- **File**: `obsidian/voids/synesthetic-void.md`
- **Location**: Lines 63, 69 ("the brain reasserts its narrower experiential configuration … the void is maintained by active architectural constraints, not passive absence of stimulation")
- **Problem**: The Bor et al. (2014) finding that trained synesthesia faded within ~3 months is fully consistent with passive skill atrophy without rehearsal. The article never considers this null-hypothesis explanation before concluding "active maintenance." This is a non-trivial leap because it is what licenses the filter-theory reading later in the section.
- **Severity**: Medium
- **Recommendation**: Insert one sentence acknowledging the alternative: *"The fade is also consistent with ordinary skill atrophy without rehearsal; the architectural-maintenance reading is the stronger of two compatible interpretations."* Then either defend the stronger reading or qualify the conclusion.

### Issue 3: Filter-theory reading does not flag observational equivalence with brain-only account
- **File**: `obsidian/voids/synesthetic-void.md`
- **Location**: §"The Developmental Occlusion," lines 73–77, especially *"the relationship between neural pruning and experiential narrowing is not identity but interface"* and the [filter](/concepts/filter-theory/) cross-link
- **Problem**: The section reads pruning data as evidence for a consciousness that has its access narrowed by a brain. Every cited finding is observationally identical under brain-only physicalism (pruning produces narrower phenomenal patterns because phenomenal patterns are narrower neural patterns). The article does not flag this equivalence; it presents the dualist reading as the natural inference.
- **Severity**: Medium-High (this is the section that most directly carries Dualism-tenet weight; weakness here weakens the tenet support)
- **Recommendation**: Add one explicit "Source/Map separation" sentence: *"Brain-only readings of these data are equally available; the Map's filter-theory reading is preferred for its consilience with [the relevant other commitments], not because the data force it."* This honours the deep-review's stated discipline of source/Map separation, which is currently maintained in tone but not made explicit at the point where the inference is strongest.

### Issue 5: "Asymmetry of access" claim is asserted, not defended
- **File**: `obsidian/voids/synesthetic-void.md`
- **Location**: Line 81: *"Synesthetes can understand non-synesthetic experience—they have it too, minus the extra qualia."*
- **Problem**: This assumes a synesthete can access a counterfactual-non-synesthetic version of their own experience by subtraction. But the symmetric difficulty is plausible: a lifelong synesthete may be no better at imagining what it is like to lack the cross-modal qualia than a non-synesthete is at imagining what it is like to have them. Subtractive imagination of one's own mental life is not obviously easier than additive imagination of someone else's. The article takes the asymmetric reading as default without argument.
- **Severity**: Low–Medium
- **Recommendation**: Either defend the asymmetry (cite empirical work on synesthetes' reports about counterfactual-non-synesthetic experience) or weaken the claim ("appears asymmetric, though synesthetes' subtractive access to their own non-synesthetic baseline is itself uncertain").

## Counterarguments to Address

### Heterophenomenology (Dennett)
- **Current content says**: "Identical representations produce different experiences" challenges representationalism via the empirical asymmetry between synesthetes and non-synesthetes.
- **A critic would argue**: Reports of phenomenal difference are reports, not phenomenal data. The synesthete and non-synesthete have non-identical functional states even when the surface stimulus is identical, because grapheme→colour processing is part of the synesthete's representational pipeline. The "identical representations" claim equivocates on the level of representation.
- **Suggested response**: Add a one-sentence acknowledgement that the argument requires accepting reports as direct evidence of phenomenal states, then point to the Map's stance on heterophenomenology (the [functional-seeming](/concepts/functional-seeming/) / [introspection](/concepts/introspection/) articles) for further discussion.

### Brain-only naturalism (Churchland)
- **Current content says**: Dualism is the natural reading of synesthetic extra qualia.
- **A critic would argue**: Synesthesia is among the most successfully naturalised consciousness phenomena. Cross-activation between V4 and the fusiform grapheme area is documented; nothing in the data demands a non-physical extra. The article's "no amount of neurological description conveys what it is like" is question-begging.
- **Suggested response**: One sentence in §Relation to Site Perspective acknowledging that the Map's reading is interpretive: the same data are compatible with brain-only physicalism, and the Map's preference rests on broader commitments rather than on synesthesia alone.

### MWI accommodates indexicality (Deutsch / Wallace)
- **Current content says**: "The void is indexical … not abstract observers sampling all possible experiential positions."
- **A critic would argue**: This is not anti-MWI evidence; it is exactly what indexical observers under MWI report.
- **Suggested response**: Either drop this paragraph (the No-Many-Worlds tenet is not load-bearing here) or weaken: "The void is indexical, which the Map's No-Many-Worlds tenet treats as substantively non-trivial (cf. no-many-worlds-discussion for why we read indexicality stronger than MWI does)."

### Filter theory unfalsifiability (Popper)
- **Current content says**: Neonatal synesthesia pruning suggests consciousness is "filtered" by the brain.
- **A critic would argue**: No experiment could distinguish filtration from identity. The reading is metaphysics dressed as empirical inference.
- **Suggested response**: Add the observational-equivalence flag (Issue 3 above).
