## Stability Notes

- Enactivists will always reject the mind-body distinction that makes dualism's question intelligible. This is a bedrock disagreement, not a flaw to fix.
- The article takes the strong position that enactivism's irreducibility commitment is unstable without dualism. Some enactivists (especially Thompsonian neo-Kantians) would contest this by arguing that transcendental irreducibility is neither physical nor non-physical. Future reviews should not re-flag this as critical — it is the Map's philosophical position, clearly argued.
