## Pessimistic Analysis Summary

### Critical Issues Found

1. **Sextus Empiricus quote attribution** — The article presented "When appearances and arguments balance, the inquirer naturally reaches *epochē*" as a direct quote from Sextus. This phrasing is a paraphrase, not verbatim from *Outlines of Pyrrhonism*. **Resolution**: Replaced quoted passage with paraphrase grounded in PH I.10 ("a state of mental rest in which one neither denies nor affirms"), with explicit citation.

2. **Heidegger "disponibilité" attribution** — The article quoted "the spirit of *disponibilité* before What-Is which permits us simply to let things be in whatever may be their uncertainty and their mystery" as Heidegger's words. This phrasing actually traces to J.M. Anderson's translator's introduction (borrowing Marcel's *disponibilité*), not to Heidegger himself. Presenting it as Heidegger's voice is misattribution. **Resolution**: Replaced with paraphrase ("a comportment of openness toward what is, allowing things to remain in their uncertainty and mystery rather than seizing them through the will") that captures the same content without falsely attributing.

3. **Husserl "pure phenomenal field" quote** — "experience itself: the pure phenomenal field" was presented as a Husserl quote. The phrase "pure phenomenal field" is not standard Husserl; he speaks of "pure consciousness" or "the field of phenomenologically reduced consciousness." **Resolution**: Replaced with paraphrase ("the field of pure consciousness for analysis") preserving the canonical *Ideas* I §32 quote which is verifiable, and softening "neither affirmation nor denial of the world" from a quoted to an attributive phrasing.
