## Executive Summary

The agent-shaped-hole cluster is unusually well-disciplined on evidential status: both articles explicitly mark the diagnostic as defeater-removal rather than positive evidence for dualism, and neither overclaims a tenet as proven. The one genuine, web-verified defect is a **citation author-list error** in the pragmatist-quantum article: the DeBrota et al. (2021) Born-rule paper is attributed to "DeBrota, Fuchs & Schack," but Schack is not an author — the real fourth/third authors are Pienaar and Stacey. This is a famous-name substitution (Schack is the canonical "S" of QBism's founding Caves-Fuchs-Schack trio, so the wrong name is highly plausible). It appears once in the reference list and twice in-text. Beyond that, one direct-refutation-discipline soft spot on the Healey engagement is worth a light touch.

## Critical Issues

### Issue 1: DeBrota et al. (2021) author-list error (famous-name substitution)
- **File**: [topics/pragmatist-quantum-foundations-and-the-agent.md](/topics/pragmatist-quantum-foundations-and-the-agent/)
- **Location**: Reference #2 (line ~164); in-text at line ~54 ("DeBrota, Fuchs & Schack 2021") and line ~96 ("DeBrota, Fuchs, and Schack (2021) formalised this through the 'urgleichung'")
- **Problem**: Schack is not an author of this paper. Correct authors: DeBrota, J. B., Fuchs, C. A., Pienaar, J. L., & Stacey, B. C. Web-verified at APS (Phys. Rev. A 104, 022207) and arXiv:2012.14397.
- **Severity**: Medium (factual citation defect; load-bearing because the urgleichung/Born-coherence result is the article's technical centrepiece, but the result itself and the title/venue are correctly stated).
- **Recommendation**: Replace "Schack" with "Pienaar & Stacey" in reference #2; update both in-text attributions to "DeBrota, Fuchs, Pienaar & Stacey (2021)" or the conventional "DeBrota et al. (2021)". Do NOT touch the Caves-Fuchs-Schack 2002 or Fuchs-Mermin-Schack 2014 references — Schack is correctly present on those.

## Counterarguments to Address (minor, non-blocking)

### Healey engagement — direct-refutation soft spot
- **File**: [topics/pragmatist-quantum-foundations-and-the-agent.md](/topics/pragmatist-quantum-foundations-and-the-agent/), "Healey: The Cost of De-Psychologising the Agent"
- **Current content says**: a purely physical agent-criterion "will face difficulties structurally similar to those besetting functionalist accounts," and under dualism a pragmatic criterion "will either track that fact (reintroducing consciousness) or diverge from it."
- **A critic (Healey) would argue**: this disjunction's force depends on there being "a real fact about which systems are conscious" — i.e., it is powered by Tenet 1, not by anything internal to Healey's framework. The article is fairly honest about this (it says "Under dualism this move loses its attraction"), so this is at the boundary, not over it.
- **Severity**: Low. The reply is mostly honest framework-boundary marking already. A light refine could make explicit that the boundary-drawing objection (functionalist-style indeterminacy of "agent") is the *in-framework* leg that does NOT depend on Tenet 1, while the "real fact about consciousness" leg is the tenet-register move — keeping the two clearly separated per [direct-refutation-discipline](/project/direct-refutation-discipline/) (natural prose, no mode labels).
