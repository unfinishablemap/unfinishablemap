### Rule-Internal Coherence

The four levels are cleanly distinguished: separability is a data claim; phenomenological reality is an experience claim; mechanistic independence is a cognitive-architecture claim; metaphysical irreducibility is a philosophical claim. The rule's *"Evidence for any one level does not automatically count as evidence for the next"* maps onto the constrain-vs-establish move: Levels 1–2 *constrain* simpler readings; reaching Level 4 requires additional argument that *establishes* the deeper claim. The mapping is implicit but coherent and was already noted in the prior review's stability notes.
