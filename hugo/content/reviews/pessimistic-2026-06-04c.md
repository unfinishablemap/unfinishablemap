## Critical Issues

### Issue 1: Falsifiability list is scoped to a mechanism the article explicitly demotes
- **File**: [concepts/interactionist-dualism.md](/concepts/interactionist-dualism/)
- **Location**: "Selection, Biasing, and Falsifiability" section, lines 140–142 (the four-falsifier list), read against the mechanism-non-commitment at lines 134, 150 and the post-decoherence preference.
- **Problem**: The section answers the unfalsifiability worry with: *"What would falsify quantum interactionism? (1) Proving quantum indeterminacy is illusory; (2) proving neural computation is purely classical; (3) proving the Zeno effect cannot be triggered by mental effort neurally; (4) proving anesthesia works purely through classical mechanisms. The theory is speculative, not unfalsifiable."* But falsifiers **#3 (Zeno) and #4 (anaesthesia-classical)** are tied to the **Stapp/Zeno mechanism**, which the article elsewhere explicitly declines to commit to ("The Map does not commit to which proposal is correct"; "The Map takes no position on which quantum mechanism is *correct*, only that some interface exists") and in fact **demotes**: it *prefers* post-decoherence selection "precisely because it does not require coherence to survive the decoherence the objection invokes." Refuting the Zeno mechanism (#3) — or showing anaesthesia is classical (#4) — would falsify a mechanism the article has already ranked *behind* its preferred reading; it would leave post-decoherence selection, the position the article actually holds, untouched. So the list answers "is *Stapp's model* falsifiable?", not "is *the position the Map holds* falsifiable?". The genuinely position-level falsifiers are #1 and #2 (indeterminacy illusory; neural computation purely classical) — and those are the much harder, arguably in-principle-only ones. A Popperian critic gets to say: *the two "easy" falsifiers on your list test a model you don't endorse; strip them and your position rests on two in-principle falsifiers, which is exactly the unfalsifiability worry the section opened with.*
- **Internal-inconsistency reinforcement**: The companion [topics/comparing-quantum-consciousness-mechanisms.md](/topics/comparing-quantum-consciousness-mechanisms/) grades Stapp/Zeno **"Falsifiability: Moderate"** and states plainly: *"The model lacks predictions distinguishing Zeno-mediated from classical attention. A discriminating test would require detecting the quantum signature of repeated rapid observations — beyond current technology."* So the very test falsifier #3 invokes is, by the Map's own sibling page, beyond current technology — in tension with this article's confident closing "The theory is speculative, **not** unfalsifiable." The two pages disagree on how falsifiable the Zeno leg is, and the flagship is the more optimistic of the two.
- **Severity**: Medium (argument-defensibility; corpus-propagating because this is a 433-inbound hub whose falsifiability framing is cited elsewhere).
- **Recommendation**: `refine-draft` pass that re-scopes the list to the *position* not the mechanism. Either (a) relabel: present #1/#2 as position-level falsifiers and #3/#4 explicitly as *mechanism-specific* falsifiers that bear on Stapp's model (a sub-mechanism the Map prefers *not* to lean on), so the reader sees the position survives their refutation; or (b) add the honest concession the sibling page already makes — that the discriminating Zeno test is "beyond current technology" — and soften the closing to match the sibling's "Moderate" grade (e.g. "falsifiable in principle, with current discriminating tests beyond reach"). Preserve the genuine point that explanatory-coherence claims stand independently of mechanism falsifiability. This is a calibration/scope fix, not a deletion.
