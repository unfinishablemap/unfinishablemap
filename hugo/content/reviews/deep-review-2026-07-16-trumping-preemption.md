## Critical Issues Found (and fixed)

- **Baumgartner attribution inverted (CRITICAL).** The new exclusion-dissolution paragraph read "interventionist treatments in the Woodward tradition **(pressed by Baumgartner)** locate mental causes as difference-making control variables with no exclusion competition to resolve." This inverts Baumgartner's role. Baumgartner is the author of the interventionist *exclusion* argument — that interventionism, strictly applied, does **not** rescue non-reductive mental causation (Baumgartner 2009/2010; *Interventionism and Causal Exclusion*). The dissolution the sentence describes (mental causes as difference-making control variables, no exclusion competition) is **Woodward's**, defended via "independent fixability" *against* Baumgartner. The 2026-07-15 Claude Opus outer review itself framed this correctly ("Baumgartner arguing interventionism does *not* rescue non-reductive causation; Woodward's independent fixability reply"); the inversion was introduced when that finding was compressed during integration. **Fix**: reassigned the dissolution to the Woodward tradition and recast Baumgartner as the counter-pressure: "…no exclusion competition to resolve (Baumgartner presses the contrary — that interventionism, strictly applied, does not rescue non-reductive causation, keeping the exclusion pressure live)." This corrects the direction, honors the reviewer's "represent the debate" remit, and makes the article's honesty about its own motivating problem sharper (the dissolution is itself contested).

- **Paul & Hall (2013) reverse orphan (CRITICAL, cross-reference).** Reference #14 (*Causation: A User's Guide*, OUP) was never cited inline — a References entry with no inline anchor (§2.4 step 5). The 2026-07-15 todo flagged it as item (5) but the consolidated pass left it unresolved. **Fix**: added an inline cite at the natural taxonomy point in "Schaffer's Cases" — "what they break, measured against the standard taxonomy of redundant causation (Paul and Hall 2013)." Paul & Hall's *User's Guide* is precisely the standard catalogue of the early-preemption / late-preemption / overdetermination species the paragraph contrasts trumping against, so the cite is substantive, not decorative.
