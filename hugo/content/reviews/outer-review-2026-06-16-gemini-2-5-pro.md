## Audit Dimension A: Empirical Accuracy Against the 2020s Literature

To mount a credible defense of interactionist-dualism, any contemporary project must anchor its proposed non-physical interventions in rigorous, empirically verified physical systems. *The Unfinishable Map* attempts this by co-opting recent literature in network neuroscience and quantum biology. However, a close examination of the cited 2020s literature demonstrates that the site routinely subjects empirical data to severe interpretative distortion, extracting metaphysical conclusions that the underlying science explicitly resists.

### The Misappropriation of Corpus Callosotomy and Network Resilience Data

In its extensive treatment of split-brain patients, the site attempts to dismantle physicalist accounts of the unity of consciousness. The framework argues that classic mid-twentieth-century split-brain experiments by Sperry and Gazzaniga, which posited two separate spheres of conscious awareness, are drastically underdetermined. To advance its own dualist "Interface Argument," the site leans heavily on recent neuroimaging literature, specifically highlighting a 2025 study by Santander et al. published in the *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences* (PNAS). The site argues that because Santander et al. demonstrated that a minuscule fraction of intact posterior callosal fibers can sustain full interhemispheric integration and temporal synchrony, this serves as evidence for a "layered unity" model. The project concludes that phenomenal unity belongs to a non-physical consciousness itself, rather than its physical substrate, characterizing the severed corpus callosum merely as a damaged "receiver" that garbles the broadcast of an already unified, immaterial mind.

This interpretation is an egregious non-sequitur that profoundly misrepresents the network neuroscience paradigm utilized by Santander et al. The 2025 PNAS study is explicitly predicated on the assumption that structural brain connectivity enables long-range communication between specialized information processing systems. The finding that partial callosotomy patients retain widespread patterns of interhemispheric functional connectivity—despite the severance of the vast majority of callosal axons—is presented by the authors as a testament to the immense structural plasticity and rerouting capabilities of the brain's remaining physical architecture. The remaining 1 cm of the splenium acts as a highly efficient, localized functional bridge that dynamically compensates for the lost physical bandwidth.

The site illicitly leaps from a purely biological demonstration of graph-theoretic network resilience to a sweeping metaphysical claim of non-physical unity. If phenomenal unity were truly the intrinsic property of a non-physical consciousness that merely "interfaces" with the brain via a quantum or biophysical antenna, then complete callosotomies should not produce the sweeping disruptions of interhemispheric network architectures and profound behavioral disconnection syndromes that Santander et al. explicitly document in patients lacking those spared posterior fibers. The data aligns flawlessly with a classical, physicalist model of neural network routing and compensation. Appending a dualist "interface" layer to this data lacks any explanatory power and directly contradicts the very neurobiological plasticity mechanisms the paper seeks to illustrate.

### False Equivalence in Quantum Biology Extrapolations

A cornerstone of the site's physical mechanism for dualist interaction is its "Minimal Quantum Interaction" tenet. This axiom posits that consciousness exerts causal influence over the physical body by biasing otherwise indeterminate quantum outcomes at specific neural loci, supposedly doing so without violating thermodynamic conservation laws. To defend the biological plausibility of quantum coherence surviving in the "warm, wet, and noisy" environment of the human brain, the site points to the established phenomenon of avian magnetoreception via cryptochrome proteins. The site heavily cites a 2026 study from Princeton University (Luo et al., published in the *Journal of the American Chemical Society*) which utilized computational simulations to demonstrate that well-separated radical pairs (spanning over 18 angstroms) within European robin cryptochrome-4a maintain coherence for microseconds.

The project wields this specific finding as a broad biological precedent to shield macro-scale quantum consciousness theories—such as Orchestrated Objective Reduction (Orch OR) or the site's own endorsed "Post-Decoherence Selection" mechanism—from the severe, longstanding decoherence timescale critiques leveled by physicists. However, this constitutes a severe false equivalence. The avian cryptochrome radical pair mechanism relies on a highly specific, photoactivated intermolecular electron transfer chain driven exclusively by blue-light excitation within the retina. The precisely evolved protein scaffolding and specific solvent reorganization around the charge provide essential thermodynamic stability, structurally isolating the sensor to prevent the immediate recombination of the radical pair.

Extrapolating from a highly specialized, photon-triggered retinal protein to the deep-brain, non-photonic cytoskeletal structures (such as neuronal microtubules) required for global neural quantum effects represents an unearned, structurally invalid empirical leap. The thermodynamic environments, molecular geometries, and functional trigger mechanisms are entirely disparate. The 2020s literature on quantum biology meticulously restricts its claims to specialized enzymatic catalysis, photosynthetic energy transfer, and highly localized sensory mechanisms; the site’s attempt to commandeer this highly constrained, specific biophysics literature to validate unconstrained global quantum neural selection demonstrates a profound lack of biophysical and methodological rigor. The fact that evolution engineered a microsecond quantum sensor in the eye does not provide empirical cover for sustained quantum coherence in the deep cortex.

## Audit Dimension B: Results That Have Been Challenged, Retracted, or Failed to Replicate

### The Misrepresentation of Adversarial Falsification in the Cogitate Consortium

The site dedicates considerable analytical space to the Cogitate Consortium, a massive, internationally funded adversarial collaboration initiated to test the contrasting neural predictions of Global Neuronal Workspace Theory (GNWT) and Integrated Information Theory (IIT). The findings of this collaboration, published across the *Nature* and *PLoS* portfolios (e.g., Melloni et al., 2023), provided deeply mixed results that challenged both dominant physicalist models. Specifically, GNWT failed to reliably demonstrate its predicted late prefrontal "ignition" upon stimulus offset, while IIT failed to show the predicted sustained inter-areal synchronization in the posterior cortex throughout the duration of a conscious percept.

*The Unfinishable Map* deliberately weaponizes this mixed outcome. Framing the discussion under the heading "Why No Experiment Can Settle the Consciousness Debate," the site asserts that the underdetermination witnessed in the Cogitate findings is a permanent, structural feature of consciousness science. The site argues that because these two leading, mathematically formalized physicalist theories encountered empirical anomalies, the entire physicalist enterprise is subject to inescapable Duhem-Quine underdetermination, thereby granting permanent epistemic parity to interactionist-dualism.

This interpretation fundamentally misunderstands and misrepresents the epistemology of adversarial collaboration. The explicit goal of the Cogitate project, as forcefully articulated by lead researcher Lucia Melloni, was to make theories highly vulnerable to empirical falsification, raising the methodological bar for how neuroscientific ideas are tested, not to dissolve the enterprise of physicalist neuroscience entirely. The failure to observe specific spatial or temporal neuroimaging biomarkers predicted by GNWT or IIT does not logically entail that consciousness operates outside the physical domain; it simply forces the refinement, revision, or eventual abandonment of those highly specific neurocomputational models.

By contrast, the site utilizes the vulnerability of these physicalist models to shield its own highly speculative "Post-Decoherence Selection" mechanism—a mechanism that the site explicitly admits is mathematically designed to be "empirically indistinguishable from chance". This represents an egregious asymmetric evidentiary standard. The framework demands definitive, flawless predictive success from neurobiological models while granting its own dualist posits a permanent exemption from empirical falsification under the guise of unresolvable "underdetermination."

This argument is thoroughly bracketed by its own definitional commitments. When subsequently presented with the philosophical question of whether a functionally equivalent silicon system could be conscious, the site concludes it cannot, solely because the system lacks the arbitrarily mandated biological quantum selection gap.

The site relies heavily on the quantum measurement problem to secure a theoretical space for the causal role of consciousness. Under the "Minimal Quantum Interaction" tenet, the framework argues that environmental decoherence selects preferred bases but completely fails to solve the outcome problem—that is, why one specific, definite outcome actualizes from the remaining classical mixture. The site purposefully injects consciousness into this precise explanatory gap, positing a "Post-Decoherence Selection" mechanism wherein the immaterial mind biases the final outcome.

Because the site formally rejects the Many-Worlds Interpretation (MWI) in its fourth tenet—citing the "indexical identity problem" wherein personal identity becomes incoherent across endlessly splitting branches, as well as criticizing MWI's ontological extravagance—it strictly forces a collapse interpretation of quantum mechanics. This renders the entire interactionist project philosophically vacuous. The causal efficacy of consciousness is thus reduced to an unfalsifiable postulate derived directly from the site’s prior metaphysical commitments against epiphenomenalism, rather than functioning as a genuine, testable biophysical hypothesis.

## Audit Dimension D: Untested Counterarguments and Competing Frameworks

However, the site entirely omits recent, profound advancements in predictive processing, active inference, and specifically the Integrated World Modeling Theory (IWMT) proposed by theorists such as Safron and aligned with the spatial-intelligence architectures of Assran et al. in 2023–2025. IWMT provides a rigorous mathematical and computational account of consciousness that views phenomenal experience not as an irreducible, non-physical substance interacting via quantum gaps, but as an iteratively computed maximum a posteriori estimate. Under IWMT, consciousness is a highly compressed, multi-modal representation of likely system-world relations, utilized by embodied organisms to navigate complex environments.

In this framework, phenomenal consciousness is conceptually understood as a kind of biological computational object—akin to a highly advanced "deep fake" created by a generative architecture to optimize organismic survival through spatial intelligence and temporal prediction. By framing consciousness as an active, generative computational construct rather than a passive functional state that mysteriously "accompanies" processing, IWMT directly bridges the explanatory gap. It explains *why* the system generates a unified, qualitative field: because a unified, qualitative generative model is the most computationally efficient way to parameterize complex sensorimotor interactions. The site briefly notes in its changelog that it "borrows the math" of predictive processing because equations are "metaphysically neutral" and describe a "branching brain" suitable for a selecting consciousness. This deeply superficial engagement completely fails to address the ontological implications of generative models, which render the dualist intervention conceptually redundant.

4. **Epistemic Mischaracterization of Adversarial Falsification:** The site fallaciously equates the failure of specific spatial/temporal predictions in the GNWT vs. IIT adversarial collaboration to an inescapable, structural underdetermination of all physicalist theories, thereby misusing empirical falsification within a paradigm as evidence against the validity of the paradigm itself. 5. **Omission of Generative Computational Frameworks (IWMT):** In its dismissal of functionalist and computationalist approaches to machine consciousness, the site relies on outdated qualitative thought experiments and completely ignores recent, formalized advancements mapping phenomenality to iteratively computed maximum a posteriori estimates within generative spatial-intelligence architectures.
