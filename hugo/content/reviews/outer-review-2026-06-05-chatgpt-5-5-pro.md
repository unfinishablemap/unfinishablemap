# Outer review: "Edge-States and the Probing of Voids"

## Overall verdict

The article is strongest as a **phenomenological and methodological bridge**: it gives the Map a useful distinction between "transcendence" and "edge-mapping," and it supplies discriminators that could, in principle, separate genuine epistemic access from merely compelling boundary phenomenology. Its best-calibrated passage is the "What Would Distinguish" section: propositional convergence, practical predictions, and intersubjective verification are the right kinds of tests. [voids/edge-states-and-void-probes](https://unfinishablemap.org/voids/edge-states-and-void-probes/)

The main weakness is that the article's cautious middle is not preserved at the edges. The body often says "mixed," "indeterminate," or "edge-mapping fits"; the "Relation to Site Perspective" then converts that underdetermined evidence into support for dualism, bidirectional interaction, and speculative quantum-interface claims. That is the clearest instance of **tenet-protective bracketing**: the article admits that physicalism can accommodate individual findings, but then says the convergence costs physicalism more theoretical machinery without supplying a comparative accounting. [voids/edge-states-and-void-probes](https://unfinishablemap.org/voids/edge-states-and-void-probes/)

The most serious empirical/citation problems are: the NDE paragraph overstates "absent brain function"; the dissolving-insight section underestimates established noetic-feeling and false-insight mechanisms; the psychedelic-neuroscience discussion is too DMN/filter-centered for a 2026 revision; and the Gładziejewski reference has a name/year error.

## Central claim audit

### 1. Recurrent edge-state profile: plausible, but over-smoothed

The article's claimed edge-profile—ineffability/noesis, ego dissolution, temporal distortion, epistemic openness, and edge-clarity—is a reasonable synthetic taxonomy. Millière et al. do support overlap between psychedelic and meditation-induced self-disruption, but their paper repeatedly warns that neither "meditation" nor "psychedelic states" are uniform categories, and that self-loss/ego dissolution is multidimensional, dose/practice/context-sensitive, and especially prominent in high-dose psychedelic use or advanced contemplative practice. [Frontiers](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2018.01475/full)

The article's convergence argument would be stronger if it explicitly treated "shared human neurocognitive architecture" as a serious rival explanation, not merely "materialist deflation." A physicalist does not need edge-reports to be idiosyncratic chaos; shared receptor systems, attentional architectures, report schemas, contemplative training regimes, questionnaires, and expectation effects can produce structured recurrence without implying access to any trans-ordinary territory.

### 2. Transcendence vs edge-mapping: the article's best move

The "What Would Distinguish" section is the article's methodological core. It correctly says that genuine access should yield more than convergent phenomenology: it should produce convergent propositional content, practical novelty, and intersubjective triangulation. It then states that evidence is mixed: robust phenomenological convergence, limited propositional convergence, therapeutic but not clearly epistemically novel yield, and elusive verification. [voids/edge-states-and-void-probes](https://unfinishablemap.org/voids/edge-states-and-void-probes/)

That is well-calibrated, but the final clause—"does not rule out transcendence of limits we cannot communicate about"—needs tighter discipline. As written, it risks preserving transcendence as an unfalsifiable remainder after all proposed discriminators favor edge-mapping. A better formulation would distinguish: "Current evidence favors edge-mapping over communicable epistemic access; noncommunicable transcendence remains a metaphysical possibility but not an evidentially upgraded hypothesis."

### 3. Reducing valve / filter theory: historically useful, scientifically under-updated

The article says contemporary neuroscience offers partial support for the reducing-valve hypothesis through decreased DMN signal during psychedelics and REBUS-style relaxation of high-level priors. REBUS does support a model in which psychedelics relax the precision of high-level priors and liberate bottom-up information flow, but it is a predictive-processing account, not a dualist filter account. [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0031699724012961)

The current fMRI literature also makes the article's DMN-centered presentation look stale. A 2026 systematic review of psilocybin fMRI studies found only 20 unique datasets, high dropout, limited follow-up scanning, inconsistent methods/designs, and multiple competing mechanistic models, including REBUS, CSTC, and CCC. [Springer](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s44192-026-00384-w) The surrounding Filter Theory article is actually more careful than the target article: it admits filter theory lacks independent criteria for which disruptions should enhance versus degrade experience, and that the altered-state cluster carries the weight of one pattern rather than multiple independent confirmations against sophisticated physicalism. [concepts/filter-theory](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/filter-theory/)

The target article should import that caution directly.

### 4. Zero-person perspective: citation broadly accurate; dualist inference too quick

Metzinger is a good source for the zero-person/MPE material. He explicitly treats pure awareness/MPE as a working hypothesis in which consciousness can exist without thought, sensory perception, temporal experience, self-location, egoic bodily self-consciousness, or a first-person perspective. [Thomas Metzinger](https://thomasmetzinger.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/06/Metzinger_MIT_Press_2024-1.pdf) He also says MPE is his best current candidate for the simplest form of conscious experience, while emphasizing the need for rigorous, evidence-based research and warning against hidden metaphysical agendas. [Thomas Metzinger](https://thomasmetzinger.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/06/Metzinger_MIT_Press_2024-1.pdf)

The target article is justified in saying MPE challenges identification of consciousness with ordinary self-models or ordinary contents. It is not justified in letting that slide into support for dualism unless it adds an explicit bridge argument. "Consciousness can occur without egoic self-model" underdetermines dualism, panpsychism, neutral monism, higher-order revisions, predictive-processing revisions, and naturalistic minimal-consciousness models.

### 5. Dissolving insights: strongest original idea, weakest evidential handling

The article's dissolving-insight section is interesting and Map-native: it treats the felt certainty of a vanishing insight as possible evidence of representational closure. But its rebuttal to confabulation is too weak. It says confabulation accounts must specify which neural state generates "the systematic failure mode" where content vanishes but the trace of having-grasped remains. [voids/edge-states-and-void-probes](https://unfinishablemap.org/voids/edge-states-and-void-probes/)

That mechanism class already exists. Koriat's feeling-of-knowing work argues that noetic feelings do not reflect direct access to memory traces; they are based on implicit, unintentional inferential heuristics, and once generated they can guide controlled action. [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1053810000904331) A recent integrated theory of false insights under psychedelics argues that insight phenomenology can be misattributed, making irrelevant propositions and worldviews feel true; it explicitly links psychedelic noetic quality to feelings of "truthiness" or obviousness. [Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/s44271-024-00120-6)

This is also a missed internal integration. The Map's own Noetic Feelings Void says felt knowing, rightness, familiarity, understanding, obviousness, coherence, confidence, and error are heuristic gating signals produced by machinery consciousness cannot inspect; it cites Koriat and notes that false insights can have the same certainty/relief/drive dimensions as true ones. [voids/noetic-feelings-void](https://unfinishablemap.org/voids/noetic-feelings-void/) The target article should treat the Noetic Feelings Void as a direct counterweight, not merely the Thought Stream Void as a supporting neighbor.

### 6. NDEs: overclaim, despite a caution sentence

The target article says NDEs occur during minimal brain function, "sometimes during clinical death with flat EEG," and then argues that if consciousness requires the brain, NDEs during absent brain function should be impossible. Its caution sentence admits timing is hard, but the headline claim still overreaches. [voids/edge-states-and-void-probes](https://unfinishablemap.org/voids/edge-states-and-void-probes/)

AWARE II is more modest and more complex: it was a prospective 25-site in-hospital cardiac-arrest study with EEG and cerebral oxygenation monitoring during CPR; among 567 in-hospital cardiac arrests, 28 survivors completed interviews, 11 reported memories/perceptions suggestive of consciousness, no one identified the visual image, and normal EEG activity consistent with consciousness emerged as long as 35–60 minutes into CPR despite marked ischemia. Its conclusion is that consciousness, awareness, and cognitive processes "may occur" during cardiac arrest and that EEG emergence may reflect resumption of network-level cognitive activity. [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0300957223002162)

A 2025 Nature Reviews Neurology article gives a mainstream neuroscientific countermodel: NDEs can be understood as disconnected consciousness during threat or physiological crisis, potentially involving cascades of impaired cerebral blood flow, hypoxia, hypercapnia, increased neuronal excitability, neurotransmitter changes, dissociation propensity, and slow recovery of brain activity after resuscitation. [Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41582-025-01072-z)

The article should replace "absent brain function" with "severely compromised, intermittently monitored, and difficult-to-time brain function." It should not use flat scalp EEG as equivalent to absence of all relevant neural activity.

### 7. Lower edge / minimal consciousness: appropriately hedged, but too analogical

The article's "MPE meditator from above / nematode from below" comparison is evocative and explicitly hedged. That protects it from being a critical overclaim. But it still risks importing human contemplative phenomenology into radically different organisms. The surrounding Minimal Consciousness article is much more careful: it distinguishes minimal consciousness from reduced consciousness, notes that evidence from anaesthetic response, learning, Φ, and valenced behavior does not prove experience, and explicitly says analogy weakens as systems differ more from human brains. [concepts/minimal-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/minimal-consciousness/)

The target article should point readers to that caution in-line, not only through a Further Reading link.

## Counterargument coverage

The article covers some obvious counterarguments, but unevenly.

It does a good job with Katz-style constructivism at the high level: content convergence is weaker than structural convergence, and doctrinal interpretation cannot simply be treated as noise. [voids/edge-states-and-void-probes](https://unfinishablemap.org/voids/edge-states-and-void-probes/) The Silence Void handles the same issue more explicitly by saying the constructivist challenge is serious and that the Map does not require perennialism to be correct; it needs only structural convergence around cognitive quieting. [voids/the-silence-void](https://unfinishablemap.org/voids/the-silence-void/)

It under-covers four important rivals:

1. **Noetic-feeling / false-insight rival.** Felt knowledge may be a heuristic confidence signal, not a trace of inaccessible content. This is the most important missing counterargument.
2. **Shared-architecture rival.** Cross-method convergence may show common human neurocognitive limits, not external void access.
3. **State-dependent memory rival.** Dissolving insight may reflect retrieval-state mismatch, encoding failure, or noetic trace without content rather than representational closure.
4. **Sophisticated physicalist accommodation.** Physicalism need not predict "disruption = chaos." It can predict structured altered experience from altered priors, gating, salience, memory encoding, and network dynamics.

The article's falsifiability posture is mixed. The transcendence/edge-mapping tests are good, but the "does not rule out transcendence we cannot communicate about" clause weakens them. The Apophatic Cartography article has better safeguards: void claims should specify operational expiration conditions, retreating boundaries should be explainable, and apophatic cartography must not both claim a boundary and dismiss all crossing attempts. [voids/apophatic-cartography](https://unfinishablemap.org/voids/apophatic-cartography/) The target article should inherit those safeguards explicitly.
