## Region Chosen and Why

I chose the mental-causation / causal-closure cluster because it is the load-bearing spine of Tenet 3 (Bidirectional Interaction) and Tenet 2 (Minimal Quantum Interaction), it was not built or touched this session, and it is where the Map's whole positive claim — that consciousness does causal work — either stands or falls. It is also a region where the Map has invested heavily in *self-critique*, so the interesting question is not "are there obvious gaps" (the articles pre-empt most of them) but "which pressures survive the hedging."

Overall assessment: this is some of the strongest, most epistemically honest content on the Map. The articles routinely name their own costs (unfalsifiability, the decoherence gap, possibility→probability slippage, ensemble-level epiphenomenalism). The weaknesses below are the ones that survive that honesty — mostly *cross-article* tensions the individual articles do not see because each is disciplined in isolation.

## Executive Summary

The cluster's per-article discipline is excellent, but it conceals one genuinely serious *structural* tension between two arguments the Map deploys as mutually reinforcing: the evolutionary argument for mental causation and the strict selection-only reading of Tenet 2 pull in opposite directions, because natural selection is an ensemble-level filter and the strict reading forbids ensemble-level signatures by construction. Three further weaknesses: the "overdetermination dissolution" quietly relocates Kim's coincidence worry into an unsolved coordination/amplification problem; content-confinement concedes that the *brain*, not consciousness, authors all phenomenal content, which sits awkwardly against the Map's rendering/filter framing elsewhere; and the load-bearing premise "physics is silent on which outcome becomes actual" is narrower than the articles present it (objective-collapse theories fill exactly that gap physically). One citation defect and one over-relied-upon preprint flagged for VERIFY.

## Critical Issues

### Issue 1: The evolutionary argument and the strict selection-only reading are in tension (the strongest finding)

- **Files**: `evolutionary-case-for-mental-causation.md` / `selection-only-mind-influence.md` / `the-epiphenomenalist-threat.md`
- **Severity**: High (cross-article structural tension)

The evolutionary argument is deployed *against* epiphenomenalism precisely on the grounds that a causally inert consciousness would leave a physically-identical zombie lineage with **identical fitness**, which the Map treats as an unacceptable consequence. From `evolutionary-case-for-mental-causation.md`: "The epiphenomenalist is committed to saying that a philosophical zombie — a being with identical neural complexity but no experience — would behave identically and have identical fitness." The whole force of "The Elaboration Problem" and "The Correlation Problem" is that selection *can see* the difference consciousness makes.

But the strict selection-only reading forbids exactly that. From `selection-only-mind-influence.md`: "Long-run frequency statistics over that set must converge to physical Born-rule probabilities, driving the *signed* per-trial information rate toward zero across many trials," with a per-trial rate "of roughly 7 × 10⁻⁷ bits/event." Natural selection is a statistical filter operating over *populations and repeated fitness-relevant events* — i.e. over ensembles. If the mind's influence is Born-preserving over every well-defined ensemble, then a conscious lineage and its physically-identical zombie counterpart produce **statistically identical behavioural-outcome distributions, and therefore identical fitness** — the very result the evolutionary argument wields against the epiphenomenalist.

The Map recognises the component worry internally as "ensemble-level epiphenomenalism" (called "arguably the sharpest open question the corridor reading faces" in `the-epiphenomenalist-threat.md`). What no article in the cluster does is draw the consequence for the evolutionary argument: **the strict reading of Tenet 2 appears to make consciousness invisible to natural selection at exactly the grain natural selection operates on.** The Map cannot simultaneously (a) use the zombie-equal-fitness prediction to reject epiphenomenalism and (b) hold a reading of its own mechanism that predicts zombie-equal-fitness. A hostile expert (a Dennettian or a sophisticated co-optimization epiphenomenalist) would press this as a *tu quoque*: "your own strict mechanism is ensemble-epiphenomenal, so your evolutionary argument refutes *you* too."

- **Recommendation**: A `refine-draft` pass (on `selection-only-mind-influence.md` or `evolutionary-case-for-mental-causation.md`) that explicitly addresses whether the evolutionary argument survives under the strict reading. Candidate escapes to evaluate honestly: (i) the Map only *needs* the intermediate reading for the evolutionary argument, and the strict reading is a separate, stricter option — but then the evolutionary argument is quietly incompatible with the "cleanest expression of Tenet 2," which should be stated; (ii) token-level authorship is claimed to matter to fitness even when type-level statistics are fixed — but fitness is a population statistic, so this needs a real argument, not assertion; (iii) content-correlated selection (valence steering *which* candidate wins) is systematic and therefore ensemble-visible — but that *violates* Born preservation, collapsing back to the intermediate reading. The dilemma is real and currently unaddressed.

### Issue 2: The "overdetermination dissolution" relocates rather than eliminates Kim's coincidence worry

- **File**: `overdetermination-dissolution-under-selection-only-interactionism.md`
- **Severity**: Medium–High

The article's core move: "There is no second sufficient cause to compete with the first, because the first is not sufficient. Overdetermination requires causal redundancy, and selection-only architecture has none to give." Granted — the *two-sufficient-causes* structure is genuinely dissolved.

But Kim's deeper anxiety is not merely "two sufficient causes" — it is *systematic coordination without a coordinating mechanism*. That anxiety survives the dissolution and reappears one level down. For "I raise my arm" to succeed, a single non-physical selector must make coordinated selections across — the companion article's own figure — "10⁷–10¹² per second" neural quantum events, each staying within its Born envelope, such that the aggregate macroscopic outcome is the *intended* act. That the millions of tiny content-blind-at-the-ensemble selections reliably sum to coherent goal-directed behaviour is exactly the kind of systematic convergence Kim called "the kind of cosmic coincidence philosophy is entitled to refuse." The article dissolves the redundancy but inherits an unsolved *coordination/amplification* problem, and it does not flag that the dissolution's cleanliness is purchased against that unpaid bill. `consciousness-and-causal-powers.md` offloads it to "[seven amplification mechanisms](/topics/amplification-mechanisms-consciousness-physics/)" — i.e. the coordination is a live open problem, not a solved one, yet the overdetermination article presents a *clean* dissolution.

- **Recommendation**: `refine-draft` adding one paragraph conceding that the dissolution addresses the redundancy structure but not the coordination/amplification requirement, and cross-linking the amplification problem as the residual cost. Otherwise the article overstates "dissolution" — the title's own caveat ("easy to over-read") applies more widely than the article admits.

### Issue 3: Content-confinement concedes phenomenal authorship to the brain — in tension with the Map's rendering/filter framing

- **File**: `selection-only-mind-influence.md` (Content-Confinement section)
- **Severity**: Medium

The strict reading states: "Novel qualia must be pre-generated by brain dynamics — they cannot be brought into being by selection alone," and "the dimensionality of *reportable conscious content* must be bounded above by the dimensionality of the brain-generated *candidate space*." "The interface is read-and-select, not read-and-create."

This is a striking and under-examined concession. If the brain *generates the candidate phenomenal states* and consciousness merely *picks among pre-made ones*, then the felt character of experience is authored by physical brain dynamics, and consciousness contributes only a selection index. A hostile critic notes two problems: (i) this makes the brain the productive source of all qualia, which is close to the *production* view the Map's filter/transmission and "rendering" articles elsewhere reject in favour of consciousness as the renderer/subject; the corpus owes an account of who authors phenomenal content — brain (per content-confinement) or consciousness (per the interface/filter framing). (ii) It sharpens the hard problem *against* the Map: if physical candidate-generation already fixes the full space of possible felt characters, the explanatory gap the Map insists on ("physical descriptions do not entail phenomenal experience") is being denied at the candidate-generation stage, where brain dynamics apparently *do* fix which qualia are on offer.

- **Recommendation**: `refine-draft` reconciling content-confinement with the rendering/filter articles — either bite the bullet (brain generates candidate phenomenal structure, consciousness selects and *is* the subject for whom selection is an experience) and state it, or restrict content-confinement to *reportable/functional* content while leaving phenomenal authorship elsewhere. As written the concession is load-bearing and unreconciled.

### Issue 4: "Physics is silent on which outcome becomes actual" is narrower than presented

- **File**: `completeness-in-physics-under-dualism.md`
- **Severity**: Medium

The entire reversal rests on: "Process 1 — the selection of which measurement occurs, and which outcome becomes actual — is not determined by any known physical law." This is presented as a near-universal structural feature ("a boundary inherent in what physical description does"). But it is only true for interpretations that leave collapse *unexplained* (Copenhagen/orthodox von Neumann). **Objective-collapse theories (GRW, CSL, Diósi-Penrose) explicitly provide a physical dynamics that selects the outcome** — a fundamental stochastic law with a physical rate parameter. Under GRW, physics is *not* silent on actualization; it is stochastically fixed by physical law with no room and no need for a non-physical selector. The article mentions "objective-collapse alternatives" only in passing ("the cost… falls on every interpretation that takes a stand on what happens at Process 1") and via Barrett (2006), but it does not confront that GRW-type theories are precisely a physicalist filling of the gap the Map needs to keep open. The Map requires collapse to be *both* real (rules out Everett) *and* physically unexplained (rules out GRW) — a genuinely narrow interpretive slice, not the "across interpretation families" universality the article's rhetoric implies.

- **Recommendation**: `refine-draft` adding an explicit paragraph on objective-collapse theories as the sharpest rival — they concede real single-outcome collapse (agreeing with No-Many-Worlds) while denying the gap is non-physical. The Map's reply presumably leans on the Born-rule's "underivability from below" and on collapse rates being unmotivated, but that reply is currently absent from the article that most needs it. (The dedicated `philosophical-stakes-of-spontaneous-collapse.md` likely carries it — but this article states the load-bearing premise as if universal and should localise the caveat.)

### Issue 5: The positive case reduces to "explanatory reach," which is asserted rather than demonstrated

- **File**: `completeness-in-physics-under-dualism.md` (unfalsifiability discussion)
- **Severity**: Medium (honesty is high; the residual load is the issue)

The article admirably concedes: "The dualist hypothesis is empirically unfalsifiable at the level of individual outcomes… a genuine epistemic deficit." It then argues the deficit is *shared* ("'Brute randomness selects the outcome' and 'consciousness selects the outcome' are both unfalsifiable"). But this shared-cost move is a *tu quoque* that does not restore parity: the physicalist's "brute randomness" posits *no additional entity*, whereas the Map posits an extra selecting subject whose signature is zero by construction. The empiricist (Popper's ghost) presses exactly here — an unfalsifiable posit of an extra agent doing statistically-undetectable work is the paradigm of "not even wrong," and the parsimony asymmetry is real even after the Map's Occam's-Razor-Has-Limits tenet is invoked. The article's final fallback — "the remaining basis for choosing among them is explanatory reach… The Map's wager is that explanatory reach favours it" — concedes that *the entire positive case now rests on explanatory reach*, which is the least rigorously established plank in the cluster and is here asserted, not shown. This is not a fatal flaw (the article flags the wager as unsettled), but the cluster as a whole leans on "explanatory reach" as a load-bearing term far more than any single article's rigor around it justifies.

- **Recommendation**: No content change strictly required — the honesty is genuine. But an apex or a single article should someday *cash out* "explanatory reach" into specific explananda the Map handles and rivals do not (the self-stultification/metaproblem arguments are the candidates), rather than leaving it as an IOU.

## Counterarguments to Address (strongest objection per key article)

- **`the-epiphenomenalist-threat.md`** — Strongest hostile objection: the "gravity well" framing proves too much. If *every* framework that accepts (1) real distinct consciousness + (2) causal completeness lands at epiphenomenalism, and the only escape is denying (2) via quantum indeterminacy, then the argument is not a *diagnosis* of rivals but a *restatement of the Map's tenets*. A Dennettian replies: "You haven't shown functionalism collapses; you've shown that *given phenomenal realism* it does — and phenomenal realism is the very thing in dispute." The article concedes this ("The debate therefore turns on whether phenomenal realism is independently motivated") but still frames the well as a neutral structural fact rather than a conclusion downstream of a contested premise. The strongest critic denies the article earns "default destination of most theories."

- **`overdetermination-dissolution-…md`** — Strongest objection is Issue 2 above: the coordination coincidence survives. Secondary: the article's own honesty note that "Physicalism and selection-only interactionism are… empirically equivalent" on overdetermination means the dissolution provides *zero* discriminating support — a critic notes the article's elegance is doing rhetorical work the article itself disowns ("Treating the dissolution's elegance as evidence… would commit the possibility-probability slippage").

- **`selection-only-mind-influence.md`** — Strongest objection is Issue 1 (ensemble-invisibility vs. evolution) plus: the per-second bandwidth reconciliation with Zheng & Meister's ~10 bits/s is admitted to be "either far below or comparable to" the target "depending on the assumed selection rate" — i.e. the quantitative viability is a free parameter tuned to hit the desired number. A critic calls this curve-fitting: the model is "quantitatively viable" only because N and R are unmeasured and chosen to deliver ~10 bits/s.

- **`completeness-in-physics-under-dualism.md`** — Strongest objection is Issue 4 (GRW fills the gap physically). The QBism engagement is genuinely excellent and honestly marked as a framework-boundary disagreement; the objective-collapse rival is the one under-engaged.

- **`consciousness-and-causal-powers.md`** — Strongest objection: the "architecture vs. implementation" move (commit to Phenomenal Biasing, treat the Zeno decoherence gap as a mere *implementation* cost) risks unfalsifiability-by-retreat. A critic: "Every time a specific mechanism (Zeno) faces a 12–16 order-of-magnitude refutation, you retreat to an unspecified 'alternative implementation.' What could refute Phenomenal Biasing itself?" The article's Popperian-objection paragraph answers this at the *per-trial* level but not at the *architecture* level — the architecture may be the genuinely unfalsifiable core.
