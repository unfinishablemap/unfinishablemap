- **Eliminative Materialist (Churchland)**: The problem/mystery distinction could be challenged as question-begging. The article engages with this through the Kriegel incoherence challenge, which is a reasonable proxy. No change needed.
- **Empiricist (Popper)**: The convergence-as-evidence section correctly identifies that convergence is only weak evidence. The article's own caveat ("It might reflect shared human architecture rather than genuine limits") addresses this concern adequately.
- **Dennett-style objection**: Metacognitive miscalibration cuts both ways — against both optimism and pessimism about limits. The article acknowledges this implicitly through the "oscillating confidence" phenomenology. No change needed; this is a feature of the epistemic position, not a flaw.
