## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)

The mine-ness article would land particularly well with Chalmers because it identifies a phenomenal feature that is *separable* from content, agency, and reflective self-awareness — exactly the kind of architectural decomposition the hard problem makes available once one stops treating consciousness as monolithic. The §"Defining the Feature" section's three clarifications ("mine-ness is not the content of experience," "mine-ness is not the sense of agency," "mine-ness is not reflective self-awareness") install a Chalmers-friendly carving of the phenomenal territory: a structural feature with internal dimensions that pathology can dissociate, none reducible to the others. The depersonalisation evidence in §"Evidence for Separability" is presented at Chalmers' register — phenomenal content preserved, the for-me character degraded, the dissociation itself the explanandum. The anti-correlated metacognitive signal article's regime-conditional qualifier in §"Regime-Conditional, Not Universal" is the Chalmers move applied to introspection: the claim is sharp where it earns its sharpness and is *narrower* than a global anti-introspection scepticism would invite. Chalmers would approve of the discipline.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)

The targeted-lesion design-space article engages the quantum-interface picture at the right register. §"Honouring the Evidential-Status Discipline" explicitly notes that "a clean result favouring the filter reading would tighten the architectural case but not settle the metaphysical question" — the article does not claim the design space, if delivered, would *establish* the MQI picture, only that it would *constrain* the architectural commitments an MQI interface would have to satisfy. This is the discipline Stapp's better critics ask for and Stapp's better defenders accept. The §"The Animal-Model Variant" section is similarly disciplined: the structural cost of trading the spatial-selectivity problem for the autonoetic-measurement problem is named, and neither variant is claimed as discriminator-ready. The mine-ness article's §"Bidirectional Interaction" section uses Stapp's territory without overclaiming — *"agency is the phenomenal correlate of [the causal role]"* is the right register, distinguishing the phenomenal feature from the causal claim it might one day support.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)

The mine-ness article is exemplary Nagel territory: the first-person datum is preserved even where the article concedes structural challenges to the phenomenology. Zahavi's irrelational characterisation, Heidegger's *Jemeinigkeit*, Gallagher's agency/ownership distinction, and Billon's "lack of a phenomenal feature that marks experiences as mine" are all used to articulate the for-me character in a way that refuses to let third-person framing erase the first-person datum. The depersonalisation patient's "behind glass" report is treated as data to be *explained* rather than dismissed. Nagel would also appreciate the article's resistance to the elegant-physicalist-explanation move: §"Occam's Razor Has Limits" notes that "predictive processing and self-model accounts give elegant, parsimonious explanations of mine-ness as the output of a transparent self-modelling process," but immediately follows with "the elegance of these explanations may purchase simplicity at the cost of leaving the phenomenal character unexplained." This is the Nagel point exactly.

The anti-correlated metacognitive signal article also makes a Nagelian move at §"Architecture-vs-significance distinction" — the architectural finding (a metacognitive signal can invert in identifiable regimes) is separated from the framework-laden significance the Map attaches to it. The first-person datum is preserved as data; the metaphysical interpretation is bracketed at the appropriate register.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)

The targeted-lesion design-space article's framework — "the bandwidth-and-reconstruction-cost picture the filter reading uses to derive the channel ordering" — is congenial to a process reading: channels, routings, reconstructions, and substrate-vs-channel dissociations are all *event-architecture* rather than substance-architecture. The article's avoidance of crude substance dualism is exemplary: §"Relation to Site Perspective" describes the interface as a relational architecture between channels and substrates, not as a Cartesian res cogitans pushing buttons on a brain. The mine-ness article's "mine-ness is *prior* to the subject-object distinction. It is not a relation between two pre-existing things (the experience and the subject who owns it); it is part of how experience is structured from the start" is direct process-philosophical territory: ownership is constitutive of the experiential event rather than a separate fact about a separately-existing subject.

**Tier-upgrade restraint check.** Both articles have abundant structural opportunity to use process-philosophical compatibility as a tier-upgrade engine for organism-consciousness claims. The targeted-lesion article does *not* extrapolate from the human design space to animal-model consciousness claims (§"The Animal-Model Variant" is explicit that "the structural cost is that *autonoetic consciousness as such* cannot be directly measured in non-human animals"). The mine-ness article does *not* upgrade the separability claim to a metaphysical-tier "mine-ness requires a non-physical subject" conclusion; §"Bidirectional Interaction" frames mine-ness as *the more fundamental feature* the Map's interactionist reading would make available, rather than as evidence for the interactionist reading. The Process Philosopher's praise here is genuine and does not cash out as a tier-upgrade. The convergence with the Hardline Empiricist persona below is honest.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)

The mine-ness article preserves the structural distinction Kane needs: agency and ownership are *independent* phenomenal features with independent grounds. Schizophrenic thought insertion (agency lost, ownership preserved) and depersonalisation (ownership fading, agency intact) deliver the double dissociation Kane's framework can use to argue that ultimate-source agency is the more demanding requirement — a being can have full ownership without full agency, but not vice versa for the ultimate-source reading. The article does not directly engage Kane's framework but installs the phenomenal architecture Kane's framework requires.

The targeted-lesion article is mostly outside Kane's territory but its §"Honouring the Evidential-Status Discipline" applies the kind of in-principle-discriminable empirical discipline Kane respects: the question "is the production-vs-filter dispute discriminable?" is answered as "yes, by a specific class of experiments not yet conducted," and the article specifies *which* future case would resolve it. This is the discipline Kane's empirical-friendly libertarian writing applies to the agency question — name the discriminator, do not claim it is delivered, hold the dispute open at the right tier.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)

The inventory-blindness article is a McGinn-grade treatment of cognitive closure at the catalogue's apex of mysterian discipline. §"The Structure of the Blindness" is exemplary: the four-level recursive structure ("you lack cognitive capability C; you lack the concept of C; you lack awareness that you lack the concept of C; you have no framework for asking whether capabilities exist at levels you cannot conceive") is the cognitive-closure thesis articulated as an *epistemological* result rather than a metaphysical claim. The Stanford 2006 unconceived-alternatives evidence and the non-Euclidean-geometry case are correctly framed as *inductive* support for the structural pattern rather than as proof. McGinn would also approve of §"Why Absence Produces No Signal" — the distinction between inventory blindness and anosognosia / inattentional blindness / unknown unknowns sharpens the concept into something more rigorous than McGinn's original cognitive-closure thesis, while preserving the structural insight.

The targeted-lesion article also makes a Mysterian move at §"What the Existing Data Cannot Deliver" — three structural limitations (lesion breadth, cross-channel measurement asymmetry, within-subject replication scarcity) bound what the catalogue can presently claim. The article does not promise that future work *will* deliver the discriminator; it specifies what would have to change for it to be deliverable. McGinn would appreciate the honest demarcation of present limits without collapsing into either premature pessimism or unwarranted optimism.

## Content Strengths

### topics/targeted-lesion-discriminating-tests-between-production-and-filter-readings-of-the-memory-hierarchy.md

- **Strongest point**: The four-ingredient specification in §"The Discriminator Stated Cleanly." The article does not gesture at a discriminating test; it specifies what would have to be true for one to be delivered: (1) focal lesion or focal perturbation, (2) concurrent multi-channel memory probing, (3) expected ordering under each prediction stated in advance, (4) replication channel across mechanism-distinct perturbations. Each ingredient is then evaluated against what the existing literature can supply. This is the [direct-refutation-discipline](/project/direct-refutation-discipline/) enacted as experimental-design specification.
- **Notable quote**: "The four ingredients are independently challenging; their combination has not yet been delivered."
- **Why it works**: The article preserves the dialectic at the experimental-design tier rather than collapsing into either "the test is impossible" or "the test is just a matter of running the experiment." Each existing approximation (K.C., H.M., semantic dementia, REM-disrupting brainstem lesions) is treated *on its own terms* and the specific reason it falls short is identified. This is the catalogue's strongest worked exhibit of how to move a structural disagreement from Mode Four (empirical underdetermination) toward a future Mode One (in-framework refutation) without overclaiming present resolution.
- **Secondary strength**: §"Candidate Target-Region Pairs" is unusually specific — three named region-pairs with the existing functional-neuroimaging convergence that makes each tractable, and with the technological-maturity considerations (TMS, transcranial focused ultrasound) that determine which would deliver the test soonest. The Verhagen et al. 2024 ultrasound-of-thalamus citation in particular grounds the anterior-thalamic-nuclei target in current technological feasibility rather than speculative future capability.

### concepts/mine-ness.md

- **Strongest point**: The double-dissociation argument in §"Evidence for Separability." Schizophrenic thought insertion (agency lost, ownership preserved) and depersonalisation (ownership fading, agency intact) deliver the cleanest possible separability argument: the two features can come apart in both directions. The article does not merely list dissociations; it explains *why* the dissociations are the right evidence for the separability claim.
- **Notable quote**: "If mine-ness were not separable, DPD would be impossible to describe. The patient could not say 'my experiences feel as if they are not mine' because the experiences would either be theirs (in which case mine-ness is intact) or not (in which case they could not be reporting them as their own). The fact that DPD descriptions are coherent—that patients can both *have* the experiences and *report them as alienated*—is itself evidence that mine-ness is a feature distinct from the bare having of experience."
- **Why it works**: The argument is structural rather than evidential-listing. It turns DPD reportability into an argument *for* separability. The Knappik 2022 challenge is correctly treated in §"Empirical Caveat" — Knappik's pluralism about mine-ness is acknowledged without conceding that it defeats the concept ("Even if mine-ness is plural rather than singular, *each* form is still a separable phenomenal feature"). The defeasibility condition is stated honestly: "The separability thesis is in principle defeasible. It would be undermined by a body of cases in which the for-me character co-varies *perfectly* with phenomenal content or with the sense of agency."
- **Secondary strength**: §"What Mine-ness is Not" — four explicit disambiguations (not personal identity over time, not bodily ownership, not haecceity, not having a self-concept) prevent the concept from sliding into adjacent notions. The disambiguation from haecceity in particular is well-handled: "Haecceity is the *metaphysical* 'thisness' of a particular; mine-ness is the *phenomenal* feature by which experience presents as a subject's. The relation between them is the [vertiginous-question](/topics/vertiginous-question/): why does this particular consciousness exist as me rather than as someone else?" The relation is identified rather than conflated.

### concepts/anti-correlated-metacognitive-signal.md

- **Strongest point**: The three-failure-mode taxonomy in §"Anti-Correlation vs Mere Unreliability." Noise (yields to averaging), miscalibration (yields to scale), anti-correlation-in-regime (yields only to independent identification of regime *R*). The third case is the structural one — averaging *amplifies* error in *R*, and calibration-from-inside requires the very signal that is inverted. This is one of the cleanest methodological-discipline distinctions the catalogue has produced.
- **Notable quote**: "Calibration-from-outside is possible only if regime *R* can be identified independently of *S* — and in self-report contexts, the identification of *R* often *is* via *S*."
- **Why it works**: The taxonomy converts a literature-survey article into a result-bearing one. The Coutinho 2021 finding is the empirical anchor, but the article's contribution is the structural analysis of *what kind of signal-failure* anti-correlation in regime *R* is, and *why* it is the structurally hardest case. The §"Regime-Conditional, Not Universal" qualifier is load-bearing: the claim is sharp where it earns its sharpness and is *narrower* than a global anti-introspection scepticism would invite.
- **Secondary strength**: §"Architecture-vs-significance distinction" is one of the catalogue's better articulations of the two-tier framework-dependence-discount structure: an architectural finding survives translation across multiple metaphysical vocabularies; the significance attached to it does not. The article applies the appropriate framework-dependence-discount at each tier, making the discipline's structure explicit rather than implicit.

### concepts/inventory-blindness.md

- **Strongest point**: §"The Structure of the Blindness" — the four-level recursive structure (lack C; lack concept of C; lack awareness that you lack concept of C; lack framework for asking whether capabilities exist at levels you cannot conceive) is the cognitive-closure thesis articulated as an *epistemological* result. The recursion-level structure makes the article's central claim precise in a way McGinn's original treatment was not.
- **Notable quote**: "Cognitive processes generate signals when they *operate* — perception produces percepts, memory produces recollections, recognition produces identifications. A missing process generates nothing because there is nothing to generate from. The signalling architecture is absent along with the capability."
- **Why it works**: The article distinguishes inventory blindness from three near-neighbours (anosognosia, inattentional blindness, unknown unknowns) and explains *why* it is structurally different from each. The Dunning-Kruger parallel is correctly limited to *measurable* bounded domains rather than extrapolated into a general principle. The Stanford 2006 unconceived-alternatives evidence and the non-Euclidean-geometry case are framed as inductive support for the structural pattern.
- **Secondary strength**: §"Relation to Site Perspective" cleanly identifies the strongest tenet connection (Tenet 5, Occam's-Razor-has-limits) and treats the Dualism connection as the weaker indirect-support case rather than as the load-bearing claim. The article's evidential discipline is preserved at the tenet-routing tier.

### concepts/cross-cultural-convergence-on-mental-causation.md

- **Strongest point**: The §"Objections and Limitations" treatment of the Barrett et al. 2021 cross-cultural intuitive-materialism finding. The article does not paper over the inconvenient finding (most participants across six populations defaulted to intuitive materialism, not intuitive dualism); it absorbs it as *strengthening* the convergence argument: "Philosophical traditions that investigated consciousness carefully arrived at mental causation *despite* starting from materialist intuitions shaped by evolutionary pressures—making the convergence an achievement against the cognitive grain, not an expression of it."
- **Notable quote**: "These philosophical traditions go far beyond naive intuition—they develop sophisticated arguments that engage counterarguments and refine positions over centuries."
- **Why it works**: The article turns a candidate counter-evidence (Barrett et al. on default intuitive materialism) into an enhancer of the convergence argument by distinguishing *naive intuition* from *sustained philosophical investigation*. The selection bias objection (Cārvāka, certain Buddhist schools) is acknowledged honestly: "The convergence is not universal but predominant: most traditions that seriously investigate the mind-body relationship conclude that consciousness is causally efficacious." This is the discipline the catalogue's better convergence articles install.
