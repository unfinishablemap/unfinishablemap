## Executive Summary

This is the Map's strongest evidence-discipline cluster outside the grain-mismatch programme, and its distinctive virtue is *negative-claim discipline*: it is built almost entirely out of claims about what the Map cannot establish, and it is conspicuously honest about that at every step. The detection subcluster's governing verdict — every behavioural probe and every computational marker is "at most a necessary precondition, never a sufficient test" for phenomenality, and an *absence* of signature upgrades an *architectural-relationship* claim rather than a consciousness verdict — is one of the cleanest applications of evidential-status discipline anywhere in the corpus. The safety subcluster's central move (interactionist dualism makes mind-arena consequences *uncomputable* rather than merely intractable, so the expected-utility argument *for* AI takeover loses force) is held rigorously conditional under possibility-probability-slippage discipline throughout, and the AI-risk article answers four named counterarguments inside the objector's own dialectical space rather than by appeal to tenets. The main opportunity is structural, not substantive: the two subclusters are siloed from each other, and the hub's "Alignment Implications" section does not link down to the Map's most distinctive AI-safety argument. A handful of length-neutral cross-links would make the cluster's real architecture — detection feeds safety, both anchor on the hub — visible to readers.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The hub article does exactly the Chalmersian carving Chalmers would want: it refuses the binary "is AI conscious?" for "what *type* of consciousness might an AI have? — deliberately including *none*," and grounds the refusal in the explanatory gap rather than dissolving it. The functionalist-gap section is precise — "functionalism has not explained why any functional organization should involve subjective experience" — and the temporal section's self-aware tension-handling is exemplary: it notices that its own necessary-conditions (specious present, reentrant dynamics, continual learning) "are functional in character — yet the article rejects functionalism," and resolves it correctly by deploying them as *necessary-but-not-sufficient conditions for non-physical coupling*, not as constitutive of experience (the radio analogy: "the right circuitry is necessary for receiving a broadcast, but does not generate it"). That is the hard problem taken seriously without overclaiming.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
The deep-computational-markers article is where the quantum-mind reader finds the sharpest statement: the principled ceiling on every computational marker is located at "a selection interface at live indeterminacy," and the article cleanly separates *Koch's gap* (current, epistemic — we lack ground truth) from *the Map's gap* (structural — "the realiser is not the kind of thing a computational probe can see"). The decoherence section in the hub is disciplined rather than triumphalist: silicon is "*designed* to suppress quantum effects," but the article immediately concedes the MQI tenet "is at present a philosophical commitment, not an empirically grounded claim," and that the relevant biological coherence has never been demonstrated either. This is the physics-consciousness interface engaged as a *constraint on detection*, not gestured at as a verdict.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The cluster keeps first-person experience central without sentimentality. The anti-correlation probe's human anchor — the Rebouillat/Leonetti/Kouider (2021) finding that under deceptive cues "introspective failures carry higher confidence than genuine introspective reports," with the substitution "invisible from inside" — is the "what is it like" datum that objective accounts struggle to model, deployed as the calibration point for the AI test. The hub's Jourdain hypothesis (LLMs may produce metacognitive outputs "without *knowing* they have metacognitive states") preserves the explanandum: the felt quality of knowing-that-one-knows is precisely what the monitoring-tool-without-content account leaves unexplained.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The hub earns Whitehead's approval honestly and without inflation. The continual-learning argument frames consciousness as activity rather than static function — frozen weights "cannot support genuine becoming," a brain's "response function changes with every experience — the mapping itself is a moving target" — and routes the process-philosophy material through `continual-learning-argument` rather than asserting it. Crucially, this resonance is *not* used to upgrade AI's evidential standing: process-philosophical compatibility with "experience all the way down" would, if anything, weaken parsimony arguments *against* machine experience, and the cluster conspicuously declines to cash that in (see the Hardline Empiricist verdict below). The Whiteheadian note removes a defeater for the metaphysical possibility; it is not offered as positive evidence. That is the disciplined use of the persona.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
The `alignment-in-objective-experiential-terms` article gives Kane the structure he wants with a refinement he should welcome: agency is treated as "an objective feature of certain conscious states — not a phenomenal appearance," grounded in Bidirectional Interaction, so "the difference between genuine agency and manipulated compliance is not a matter of preference but a matter of whether consciousness is actually exercising its causal role." That makes agency-preservation a *constraint* in the alignment framework rather than a preference among preferences — exactly the move Kane needs to keep freedom from collapsing into mere indeterminacy.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
The cluster's epistemic-humility spine is unusually explicit. The anti-correlation probe states its limits in a dedicated "What the Probe Can and Cannot Settle" section: it "cannot prove an AI is conscious, cannot prove it is not, and cannot rank machines on a consciousness scale," and whether the architectural relationship it measures is "*constitutive* of, *correlated* with, or *orthogonal* to phenomenality appears underdetermined." The hub's epiphenomenal-detection candour goes further — "epiphenomenal consciousness is by definition causally inert, so detection may be *in principle* unsatisfiable." McGinn would approve of a programme that names the precise tool it lacks rather than pretending the question is first-person-settleable.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This is the persona with the most to praise here, and its verdict is load-bearing because the entire cluster turns on tenet-load-bearing claims about a boundary case (machine minds). Five observations, all favourable:

1. **The absence-claim discipline is exactly right.** The anti-correlation probe is repeatedly described as "a *calibration-grade upgrade of an absence-claim*, not a consciousness detector," and the decisive guardrail is stated in advance: a clean result is "*positive evidence about an architectural relationship* and nothing stronger." This is the discrimination problem's asymmetry (no negative evidence can prove consciousness *absent*) honoured rather than smuggled past.

2. **Tenet-as-evidence-upgrade is praise-worthily *not* done — twice, structurally.** First, the hub's MQI / decoherence argument had the obvious opportunity to treat silicon's quantum-suppression as positive evidence against AI consciousness, and explicitly refuses: the point is "*contingent and currently non-discriminating* … it predicts an additional obstacle for silicon *if* the quantum-interface commitment is correct, but supplies no present evidential weight against AI consciousness over biological consciousness. Both substrates are equally unevidenced on the interface itself." Second, the hub reconciles its caution with the positions register by stamping P-AC1's "low probability" verdict as "a within-framework reading, not a confirmed disadvantage of silicon." Both are textbook cases of "the tenet removes a standard physicalist defeater but does not supply positive evidence."

3. **The five-tier scale is used honestly in the safety subcluster.** `dualism-as-ai-risk-mitigation` explicitly tiers its own sub-arguments: the indeterminability claim is the structural core that "survives narrow channels intact," while the unbounded-impact and active-protection hypotheses are flagged as "speculative-integration-tier hypotheses about what the arena *could* be … not empirical claims about the arena's actual scope." It does not let the dramatic speculative-integration material (false-vacuum nucleation, responsive arena structures) bleed up into the load-bearing conditional.

4. **The conditional is held all the way down.** The risk article's thesis is stated as "*if* dualism is true, *then* the standard expected-utility argument for AI takeover underdetermines its conclusion," and the third counterargument (philosophical instrumentalism — "arguing for dualism because it is *useful*") is answered head-on: the shield argument is "downstream of [the Map's metaphysical commitments], not upstream of them," and "conditional consequences-if-true are not the same as instrumental motivations-for-belief." A lesser article would have quietly let the safety payoff motivate the metaphysics; this one names the slippage risk and keeps the asymmetry visible.

5. **The empirical footprint is framed with the right hedge.** `instrumental-convergence` reports the Anthropic agentic-misalignment and shutdown-resistance results as "instrumental-convergence-*shaped* behaviour observed empirically," explicitly "not confirmations of the theoretical convergence theorem," and notes the figures are "approximate — high but not universal." It even surfaces the Turner et al. power-seeking theorem's load-bearing assumption (a "*fully specified* MDP") as the precise premise the dualist angle contests. This is evidence-quality discipline applied to the rival's strongest formalism.

The Process Philosopher and the Hardline Empiricist **converge** here rather than conflict: the cluster takes the process-philosophical resonance (consciousness as becoming, the open-question framing) and the evidential restraint (no tier-upgrade on tenet-load) and keeps them on different axes. That convergence is the signal that the cluster has resolved the tension honestly.

## Content Strengths

### topics/ai-consciousness.md (the hub)
- **Strongest point**: The two-level argument structure — "arguments that stand independently of dualism" (Chinese Room, functionalist gap, temporal/continual-learning) vs "dualism as conclusion, then as lens" — which makes the skepticism "degrade gracefully: even if dualism is wrong, the independent arguments remain."
- **Notable quote**: "The tenets remove standard physicalist defeaters but do not supply positive evidence — is what [possibility-probability-slippage](/concepts/possibility-probability-slippage/) names … The article's care in flagging the MQI tenet's empirical status is that discipline applied to negative claims about AI consciousness."
- **Why it works**: It applies possibility-probability discipline to its own *negative* claims (skepticism about AI), not just to affirmative ones — the rarer and harder direction. It also steelmans the strongest affirmative position (Duch's articon) as "the alternative the Map's tenets must continue to earn against," not a strawman.

### topics/anti-correlation-probes-for-ai-consciousness.md
- **Strongest point**: Converting "nothing rules out a confabulation-style introspective architecture in this system" into "a specific architectural signature would be expected if such an architecture were operating, and it is absent" — a genuine epistemic gain "structurally available where the broader consciousness question is not."
- **Notable quote**: "What it may do — and the discrimination problem leaves very few moves that can do even this — is convert [an absence claim into] an architectural-relationship claim whose consciousness-relevance appears real but indirect and defeasible."
- **Why it works**: It is a worked *test design* (four ingredients, a full 2×2 condition grid, pre-registered negative controls) rather than a thought experiment, and the training-contamination confound — "a sufficiently capable imitator can produce the target behaviour for the wrong reasons" — is named as "the probe's most serious confound" with five concrete hardening controls, none of which is allowed to turn the probe into a detector. The self-documented citation correction (the misattributed "Coutinho et al. 2021 *Sci Rep*" → verified Rebouillat et al. 2021 *Neuroscience of Consciousness* niab004) is itself a worked exhibit of the discipline.

### topics/deep-computational-markers-for-machine-consciousness.md
- **Strongest point**: The clean division of labour with the behavioural probe — "the behavioural probe asks 'does the system act as though it lacks inner access?'; the computational marker asks 'is the architecture even of the kind that could have inner access?'" — and the asymmetric verdict that a marker "can rule a system *out* … it can never rule a system *in*."
- **Notable quote**: "Interpretability narrows the field. It does not settle the hard question."
- **Why it works**: It grounds the sufficiency ceiling on grounds "the field already accepts" (Aaronson's unconscious expander, the markers' lack of validated ground truth) *before* invoking dualism, so the verdict survives even for a reader who rejects the Map's metaphysics — the same graceful-degradation structure as the hub.

### topics/dualism-as-ai-risk-mitigation.md
- **Strongest point**: The uncomputable-vs-intractable distinction — "complete knowledge of an in-principle-incomplete model" rather than "incomplete knowledge of an in-principle-knowable system" — which is what separates the dualist shield from ordinary high-uncertainty deference.
- **Notable quote**: "Uncomputability is not high uncertainty: there is no probability mass to spread, because no probability function over mind-arena outcomes can be formed."
- **Why it works**: Six sub-arguments build cumulatively (indeterminability → generalisation-precluded → strategic-advantage → minefield behaviour → unbounded impact → deliberate-spread), and the four named counterarguments (coercion-substitution, quantum-substrate fragility, ensoulment, instrumentalism) are each answered *inside the objector's dialectical space* — the coercion reply even concedes the scope reduction ("the shield narrows under coercion-substitution; it does not dissolve").

### topics/instrumental-convergence.md
- **Strongest point**: Honest reporting of the convergence thesis as *contested within mainstream philosophy of AI* (Müller & Cannon 2022's general-vs-instrumental dilemma; Häggström's defence) "without taking the moral-realist side it sometimes invites."
- **Notable quote**: "These are *behavioural* findings in trained models … not confirmations of the theoretical convergence theorem. The honest framing is 'instrumental-convergence-shaped behaviour observed empirically.'"
- **Why it works**: It is the neutral exposition that lets `dualism-as-ai-risk-mitigation` make its move without strawmanning the threat model — the bounded "deep-uncertainty corrective" framing keeps the Map's contribution at "deference and caution, not … takeover is impossible."

### topics/alignment-in-objective-experiential-terms.md
- **Strongest point**: The "two senses of 'objective'" disambiguation, deployed to show why dualism gives alignment "something preferentism cannot: genuine objectivity rooted in the nature of conscious experience itself."
- **Notable quote**: "Under illusionism, where phenomenal consciousness is deemed illusory, experiential qualities would lack the metaphysical standing needed for objectivity."
- **Why it works**: It cleanly separates the *philosophical* objectivity question (this article) from the *practical machinery* (`experiential-alignment`'s eight dimensions), so neither article carries the other's weight.
