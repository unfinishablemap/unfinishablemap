# Pessimistic Review

**Date**: 2026-07-13
**Content reviewed**: `obsidian/concepts/occasionalism.md` (fresh create, 2026-07-13T18:08, cross-review still queued)

## Executive Summary

A well-built rival-position article with clean historiography (the Nadler and al-Ghazālī corrections are genuinely careful) and, notably, an honest framework-boundary close that resists boundary-substitution. The dominant weakness is a **self-application gap**: the two argumentative weapons the article turns against the quantum-occasionalist twin — Malebranche's No Necessary Connection (NNC) and Arnauld's "explains too much" — apply with equal force to the Map's own selection channel, and the article never discharges that debt. A secondary weakness is a Popperian one the article walks straight into by boasting physical identity with divine-collapse causation. No content changes recommended here (reports-only); the queued cross-review is the fix path.

## Critiques by Philosopher

### The Eliminative Materialist
The whole article is a seventeenth-century family quarrel about which invisible agent (God, or a "creaturely mind") pushes the quantum dice. Neither disputant has a mechanism; both are folk-psychological placeholders. Occasionalism at least had the virtue of positing *one* undetectable causer rather than billions. The Map's improvement is to multiply the mysteries.

### The Hard-Nosed Physicalist
The article grants that "Cartesian contact-style interaction between an unextended mind and an extended body really is unintelligible" — and then proposes a *selection* channel that is exactly as unintelligible, just relocated to a probabilistic joint. Calling the influence "biasing which outcome becomes actual" does no explanatory work; it renames the problem. The mind still has to reach the collapse somehow, and "somehow" is what was owed.

### The Quantum Skeptic
Two unpaid physics bills. (1) The article asserts the selection channel biases outcomes "without Born-statistics violation." Either the biasing shifts the outcome distribution (Born violation) or it is statistically invisible (in which case in what sense is it *causally efficacious*, and how would it differ from no influence at all?). The article states the no-violation claim as settled; it is the live problem. (2) The quantum-occasionalism section stakes the Map's whole "living rival" framing on quantum indeterminacy being *available* as a joint — decoherence at neural scales is not even gestured at here, so the section's central premise is uncosted (deferred elsewhere, presumably, but a reader of *this* page gets a free pass on the hardest objection).

### The Many-Worlds Defender
On MWI there is no collapse for God or a creaturely mind to determine, so the entire "shared joint, different agent" dialectic evaporates. The article treats "quantum indeterminacy as a real, law-respecting opening for non-physical determination" as common ground with Plantinga/Russell, but that common ground is exactly the contested collapse posit. The rivalry is intramural to collapse interpretations.

### The Empiricist (Popper's Ghost)
The article's proudest move is its own falsifiability wound. It says divine collapse-causation and the Map "agree with the Map on physics and disagree only on the agent" — i.e., the two are *physically indistinguishable* and differ solely over an unobservable selector (God vs. finite mind). No experiment can decide between them. The disagreement the article stakes the Map's identity on is, by the article's own characterization, empirically empty. That is not a footnote; it is the shape of the whole "living rival" section.

### The Buddhist Philosopher
Occasionalism and the Map both reify a persisting causal agent — occasionalism one (God), the Map many (finite minds "with causal reach"). The article frames the disagreement as "whether finite minds have any causal reach at all," but the prior question — whether there is a unified self there to have reach — is assumed by both parties and examined by neither.

## Critical Issues

### Issue 1: NNC boomerangs onto the Map's own channel
- **File**: occasionalism.md
- **Location**: "The Machinery" (NNC bullet) vs. "Relation to Site Perspective" (¶1)
- **Problem**: Malebranche's central argument is that a true cause requires a *necessary* connection between cause and effect, perceivable by the mind. The article reports this as occasionalism's sharpest move, then in the closing section concedes occasionalism's "honest premise" that Cartesian interaction is unintelligible — but it never shows the Map's selection channel *satisfies* NNC. A probabilistic bias on a quantum outcome is, if anything, an even weaker candidate for a perceivable necessary connection than classical push-causation. The article rebuts the *quantum* occasionalist while leaving the *classical* NNC argument un-answered as applied to its own cure.
- **Severity**: High
- **Recommendation**: The cross-review should add one or two sentences either (a) showing why the Map's channel is not obliged to meet NNC's standard (e.g., rejecting the necessitarian conception of causation NNC assumes), or (b) honestly marking that the Map rejects NNC's premise rather than meeting it. Right now the premise is granted and the bill goes unpaid.

### Issue 2: "Explains too much" — asymmetry asserted, not earned
- **File**: occasionalism.md
- **Location**: "The Classical Objections" (Arnauld) and "Relation to Site Perspective" (¶2)
- **Problem**: The Map deploys Arnauld's charge against quantum occasionalism: routing every determination through God "explains too much and so explains nothing about the specific structure of agency." But the Map's own selection channel is equally silent on *why this volition biases that outcome* — it "locates" creaturely causation at the quantum joint without saying anything about the joint's internal structure. The article claims the asymmetry ("discards the very thing the framework exists to secure") but the "explains the specific structure of agency" contrast is not obviously won: neither view offers a mechanism for the specific structure.
- **Severity**: Medium
- **Recommendation**: Cross-review should either sharpen the asymmetry (the Map's channel is *selective/local*, occasionalism's is *omni-determining*, and selectivity is what carries structure) or drop the "explains the specific structure of agency" phrasing, which over-claims.

### Issue 3: Physical-identity boast is a falsifiability liability left unmarked
- **File**: occasionalism.md
- **Location**: "Quantum Occasionalism" ¶2 ("agree with the Map on physics and disagree only on the agent")
- **Problem**: The article presents physical indistinguishability from divine-collapse causation as the *strength* of the dialectic ("the sharpest dialectical material the rival offers") without noting that it makes the God-vs-creaturely-mind question empirically undecidable. The empiricist objection is not merely unaddressed; the passage is worded so as to invite it.
- **Severity**: Medium
- **Recommendation**: Add an explicit acknowledgment that the distinction is metaphysical rather than empirical, and say on what non-empirical grounds (Tenet 3, agency-preservation) the Map still takes a side. This is consonant with the honest Tenet-5 close already present.

### Issue 4: Incomplete citation (Reference 8)
- **File**: occasionalism.md
- **Location**: References list, item 8
- **Problem**: `"Does God Act in the Quantum World? A Critical Engagement with Robert John Russell." Theology and Science, 2022.` has no author, no volume/issue/pages. Bare relative to the rest of the list. Should be verified at the publisher and completed (author, vol/issue) or, if it cannot be located, replaced with a Russell/NIODA primary source.
- **Severity**: Low
- **Recommendation**: Cross-review should complete or replace; a publisher-verify of Russell's NIODA citation is cheap.

## Counterarguments to Address

### "The selection channel escapes the interaction problem"
- **Current content says**: The Map "locates" creaturely causation at a lawful quantum selection joint, avoiding the unintelligibility occasionalism exploits.
- **A critic would argue**: Relocating the influence to a probabilistic joint does not answer *how* a non-physical mind reaches that joint; it only changes the coordinate at which the "how" is demanded. Occasionalism's NNC challenge is untouched.
- **Suggested response**: State the Map's positive stance on causation (rejecting the necessitarian premise) rather than conceding the premise and hoping relocation suffices.

### "You are physically identical to your rival"
- **Current content says**: Agreement on physics is the sharpest dialectical material.
- **A critic would argue**: Then the choice between God and creaturely selection is not adjudicable by evidence, and the Map's confidence is a bare metaphysical preference.
- **Suggested response**: Own the metaphysical (non-empirical) character explicitly and ground the preference in Tenet 3, as the close nearly does.
