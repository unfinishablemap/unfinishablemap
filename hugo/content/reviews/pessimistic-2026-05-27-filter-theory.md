# Pessimistic Review

**Date**: 2026-05-27
**Content reviewed**: `obsidian/concepts/filter-theory.md` (3281 words, ai_contribution 100, last_deep_review 2026-04-17 — over a month stale; conceptual hub for Tenet 1 and the altered-state-evidence cluster)

## Executive Summary

Filter theory is one of the Map's load-bearing concept hubs and is, on the whole, carefully hedged — its honesty about the metaphysical-vs-empirical underdetermination and the post-hoc enhancement asymmetry is genuine and should be preserved. Three concrete, fixable defects nonetheless surfaced: (1) a citation misattribution in the bandwidth paragraph that conflates Zheng & Meister with Sauerbrei & Pruszynski and omits the actual source; (2) an overstated NDE statistic that drops the denominator qualification its own child article (`near-death-experiences.md`) carefully installs; (3) a non-sequitur in the meditative-cessation subsection. Plus an altered-state convergence-double-counting gap: the hub presents seven supportive subsections as a cumulative case while delegating the symmetry caveat to child articles rather than stating the parallel-accommodation point in its own prose.

## Critiques by Philosopher

### The Eliminative Materialist (Churchland)
The article's central move — "correlation doesn't prove production" — is true but empty as stated, because it equally undercuts filter theory: correlation doesn't prove *transmission* either. The piece concedes this ("the difference appears metaphysical rather than empirical," line 61) yet keeps assembling a seven-item "Evidence Supporting Filter Theory" section as though the cases accumulate. They don't accumulate toward filter theory specifically; they accumulate against *naive* production only, which no contemporary neuroscientist holds. The folk-psychological "broadcast" the radio receives is exactly the reified mental object I expect to be eliminated; naming Huxley's "Mind at Large" (line 134) is poetry, not a referent.

### The Hard-Nosed Physicalist (Dennett)
Line 87 already does my work for me — it admits the anaesthetic divergence "refutes naive unified-switch physicalism but not sophisticated functionalism." Good. But the rest of the article does not carry that concession through. The "psychedelic paradox," covert consciousness, and dreams are each presented as if they favour filter theory, when each is equally a redistribution-of-processing story. The illusionist section (lines 152-160) is the most honest part: it concedes filter theory "doesn't decisively refute functional seeming." Make the rest of the article live up to that paragraph.

### The Quantum Skeptic (Tegmark)
The transmission-mechanism objection (line 138) waves at Minimal Quantum Interaction and then immediately concedes it is "empirically indistinguishable from random collapse." That concession is honest but it guts the "mechanism" answer: an undetectable mechanism is not a mechanism, it is a placeholder. No decoherence-timescale engagement appears here (it lives elsewhere on the Map), so a reader arriving at the hub gets a mechanism claim with the teeth removed and no pointer to where the femtosecond problem is actually faced.

### The Many-Worlds Defender (Deutsch)
Not directly engaged in this article; the omission is defensible since the hub delegates interpretation-of-QM disputes downstream. No issue raised.

### The Empiricist (Popper's Ghost)
The article's strongest self-aware moment is conceding the enhancement asymmetry is "partly post hoc" (line 61) and "a pattern-level asymmetry rather than a precise testable prediction" (line 150). But it then offers the ~10 bits/s bandwidth result as "a stronger discriminator" — and *misattributes the citation* (see Critical Issue 1). A discriminator resting on a garbled citation is not yet a discriminator. The bandwidth signature is also not obviously filter-discriminating: a production theorist predicts a serial access bottleneck for global-workspace reasons too.

### The Buddhist Philosopher (Nagarjuna)
The article honestly flags its own disanalogy (line 176): Buddhism denies a permanent self, filter theory posits independent consciousness. Good. But the cessation subsection (line 97) leans on "consciousness *releasing* its grip on the filter from inside" — reifying exactly the independent experiencer that *anātman* dissolves. The Map is entitled to that reification under Tenet 1, but the sentence smuggles it in as observation rather than commitment.

## Critical Issues

### Issue 1: Bandwidth citation misattribution
- **File**: [concepts/filter-theory.md](/concepts/filter-theory/)
- **Location**: Line 150 (objection "How could we ever distinguish filter from production?") and References (line 220)
- **Problem**: The ~10 bits/s conscious throughput figure, the 10⁹ neural-processing figure, AND the "bottleneck appears specifically at conscious access" claim are all attributed to "**Sauerbrei & Pruszynski 2025**." But the dedicated, well-cited [topics/bandwidth-of-consciousness.md](/topics/bandwidth-of-consciousness/) attributes the ~10 bits/s and ~10⁹ figures to **Zheng & Meister 2025**, and cites Sauerbrei & Pruszynski *specifically and only* for the complementary point that *unconscious motor control* vastly exceeds the conscious ceiling. Filter-theory's reference list contains Sauerbrei & Pruszynski but **does not list Zheng & Meister at all** — so the actual source of the headline figure is missing from the references. This is the same class of intra-corpus citation drift the changelog has been chasing repeatedly (cf. the Wiest/Khan and Zheng-Meister/Nørretranders corrections logged 2026-W17).
- **Severity**: Medium (factual/attribution; the figures themselves are real and correctly used in the sibling article)
- **Recommendation**: Re-attribute the ~10 bits/s and 10⁹ figures to Zheng & Meister 2025; keep Sauerbrei & Pruszynski for the "bottleneck is at conscious access, not the substrate" point; add Zheng & Meister 2025 to the reference list. Mirror the phrasing in `bandwidth-of-consciousness.md` lines 61/89/91.

### Issue 2: Overstated AWARE II statistic (denominator dropped)
- **File**: [concepts/filter-theory.md](/concepts/filter-theory/)
- **Location**: Line 101 (Near-Death Experiences subsection)
- **Problem**: States "Parnia's AWARE II study (2023) found that **39% of cardiac arrest survivors** reported conscious awareness during arrest." The Map's own authoritative [concepts/near-death-experiences.md](/concepts/near-death-experiences/) (deep-reviewed 2026-05-26) gives the correct framing: "among the small subset who completed interviews (**28 of 567** cardiac arrest patients), 39% reported some form of conscious awareness ... this figure reflects a self-selected sample and **cannot be generalized to all survivors**." The hub article inflates a 39%-of-28-interviewed figure into 39%-of-survivors, exactly the over-generalisation the child article was edited to prevent. The "more real than real" quote is also attributed to "(Van Lommel et al. 2001)" here, whereas the child article treats it as a general experiencer report (line 110), not a Van Lommel 2001 quotation.
- **Severity**: Medium-High (a load-bearing empirical claim, overstated against the Map's own corrected source; a reader/LLM fetching the hub gets the un-hedged number)
- **Recommendation**: Restate as the child article does — qualify the denominator (28 of 567 interviewed; self-selected) and decouple "more real than real" from the Van Lommel 2001 cite (or source it correctly).

### Issue 3: Non-sequitur in the cessation argument
- **File**: [concepts/filter-theory.md](/concepts/filter-theory/)
- **Location**: Line 97 (Meditative Cessation)
- **Problem**: "If consciousness merely emerged from brain activity, voluntary cessation should be impossible—one cannot voluntarily eliminate the activity producing oneself." This does not follow. A self-modifying physical system can routinely down-regulate the very activity that produces a given state — voluntarily falling asleep is the everyday counterexample; a process suspending itself via a syscall is the computational one. A production theorist faces no contradiction: the volition and the cessation are sequential brain states, and the system that issues the "stop" command need not persist through the resulting quiescence. The claim is stated flatly with no hedge, unlike the surrounding text.
- **Severity**: Medium (a clean logical gap in an otherwise well-hedged article; weakens rather than strengthens the case)
- **Recommendation**: Either drop the "should be impossible" overreach or downgrade to the defensible version — that voluntary, content-free, *recoverable* cessation is *harder for production accounts to motivate* than for filter accounts, without claiming impossibility.
