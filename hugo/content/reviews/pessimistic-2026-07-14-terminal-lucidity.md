## Critiques by Philosopher

### The Eliminative Materialist (Churchland)
The whole edifice rests on "cognitive clarity returned," a folk-psychological description of caregiver reports across 250 years, most of them retrospective and none accompanied by neural recording during the episode. The article grants this ("caregivers overinterpreted" is on the table) and rests its case on autopsy-confirmed structural damage rather than the reports alone — a fair defense. But note the residual folk-psychology dependence: "recognised family members," "recalled specific events" are the exact intentional-idiom claims eliminativism predicts will dissolve under a network-level neuroscience. The article's own citation of the authors' "network-level return of cognitive function" framing concedes the eliminativist's preferred description is already available.

### The Hard-Nosed Physicalist (Dennett)
The article's monotonicity argument ("more damage means less capacity, always") is the strongest move and is honestly hedged: it targets "the simplest version" of production models, not production as such. Dennett's rejoinder is exactly the one the article already installs — transient disinhibition / neurochemical surge over spared circuitry — and the article concedes the prospective profile (brief, trigger-linked, 4.1% terminal) favors it. So the article does not overclaim here.

### The Quantum Skeptic (Tegmark)
Not applicable — the article makes no quantum-mechanism claim. Clean.

### The Many-Worlds Defender (Deutsch)
Not engaged and not relevant to this content.

### The Empiricist (Popper's Ghost)
The sharpest live tension. The article states the interface/filter distinction "introduces flexibility that risks unfalsifiability — any pattern of damage could be retrospectively classified as 'interface degradation' or 'filter loosening.'" It then relocates filter theory's value to "coherence across the full pattern of anomalies." A Popperian presses: coherence with a pattern is not confirmation, and an unfalsifiable interpretive frame that accommodates *both* enhancement (terminal lucidity) and diminishment (anaesthesia, dementia) by post-hoc reclassification has surrendered predictive content. The article names this cost but does not fully discharge it — though naming it explicitly is itself good discipline, and the "discriminating measurement is in-episode neural recording shown irreducible to a neurochemical event" passage supplies a genuine falsification target. Net: honestly handled, no task warranted.

### The Buddhist Philosopher (Nagarjuna)
Untouched — the article does not reify a persisting self beyond what the phenomenon requires (it speaks of "capacities" and "access," not a soul-substance). The Relation-to-Site-Perspective section's dualist reading is flagged as presupposing the framework it defends. No emptiness-based finding.
