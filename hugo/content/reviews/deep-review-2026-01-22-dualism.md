## Pessimistic Analysis Summary

### Critical Issues Found

1. **Eliminativist challenge unaddressed** - Article assumed consciousness is a coherent natural kind without engaging with the eliminativist position that "subjective experience" may be confused folk psychology destined for elimination
   - **Resolution**: Added new "Eliminativist Challenge" subsection with Searle's self-undermining response—we cannot coherently deny experiences because the denial would itself be an experience

2. **Zombie inconceivability response missing** - Dennett and others argue zombies are actually inconceivable once fully specified
   - **Resolution**: Added paragraph addressing this objection—the response assumes what it needs to prove (that functional specification exhausts what needs specifying)

3. **Falsifiability unclear** - Position seemed empirically insulated
   - **Resolution**: Added paragraph specifying what would count as evidence against dualism (structural account of subjective character, zombie incoherence under analysis, no quantum indeterminacy in voluntary action)

4. **Decoherence objection to quantum interaction** - Claim that consciousness operates at quantum indeterminacies not defended against thermal decoherence challenge
   - **Resolution**: Added explanation that Stapp's proposal doesn't require long-lived superposition, only sub-millisecond quantum influence on neural firing

### Medium Issues Found

1. **Conceptual vs epistemic gap underspecified** - Why the gap is conceptual (not just current ignorance) needed stronger explanation
   - **Resolution**: Expanded explanatory gap section to clarify that physical concepts (structural, functional, relational) cannot *in principle* capture subjective character—it's a type difference, not information deficit

2. **Missing concrete mechanism for interaction** - Article referenced quantum indeterminacies but didn't name specific proposals
   - **Resolution**: Added Stapp's quantum Zeno mechanism as leading candidate in both the Interaction Problem section and Relation to Map's Perspective

3. **No connection to agency/free will** - Missing motivation for why interactionism matters beyond metaphysics
   - **Resolution**: Added sentence connecting to agency and moral responsibility—"our choices genuinely influence outcomes, not just in appearance but in reality"
