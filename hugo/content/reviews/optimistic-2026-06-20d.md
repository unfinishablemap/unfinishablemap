# Optimistic Review

**Date**: 2026-06-20
**Cluster reviewed**: The cross-modal / sensory-interface cluster — the new apex [apex/cross-modal-capability-division.md](/apex/cross-modal-capability-division/) and its five source treatments (`concepts/capability-division-in-vision`, `topics/auditory-consciousness-and-the-interface`, `topics/chemosensory-consciousness-and-the-interface`, `topics/tactile-consciousness-and-the-interface`, `topics/dual-domain-capabilities-in-proprioception-and-spatial-imagination`), plus the cluster-adjacent `topics/sensorimotor-contingencies-and-the-interface`.

## Executive Summary

This is one of the strongest, most disciplined clusters on the Map: five per-modality treatments of the brain-side/mind-side capability division, now crowned by an apex (`cross-modal-capability-division`) that does the genuinely apex-grain work — converting an inductive generalisation over the Map's own articles into a precise, externally-grounded *asymmetry* claim (modality-specific brain machinery, supramodal mind-side access boundary) and then naming the rival that predicts the same datum. The opportunities are almost entirely cross-link hygiene, not prose expansion: the cluster's internal link graph is asymmetric, and one thematically-central article (`sensorimotor-contingencies`) is integrated *into* the cluster but does not link *out* to it.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The apex keeps the explanatory gap exactly where it belongs and refuses to let neural machinery dissolve it. The chemosensory article's framing is the sharpest instance: it concedes combinatorial coding "*explains the discrimination*" and adopts that explanation, then isolates the residue — "why the pattern of activation is *like anything at all*, why the spearmint pattern feels minty rather than merely registering as distinct." That is the hard problem stated with no inflation. Chalmers would also approve of the apex's "ownership" term — the move from information-available-*to-the-system* to information-available-*to-someone* — as the place a broadcast theory genuinely under-describes.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
Less central to this cluster (perception, not measurement), but the Minimal-Quantum-Interaction relation note is handled honestly: the apex states the mind-side access boundary "sits below the resolution at which current neuroscience can localise a mechanism" and explicitly disclaims that "no cross-modal datum establishes the quantum interface." Stapp's program is not over-recruited here, which is to its credit.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The tactile and chemosensory treatments are the most Nagelian on the Map. Touch "stages the owner explicitly: the touching hand is also a touched object"; smell has "a privileged, thalamus-bypassing route to memory and affect (the Proust phenomenon)." Each removes one of vision's conveniences — spatial layout, a smooth quality space — precisely to show that the first-person character survives the removal. "What is it like" is centered without being asserted as a knock-down argument.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The cluster avoids crude substance dualism: the division is drawn as a *labour split* (extraction/organisation vs. unity/ownership/flexible deployment), not as two stuffs. The apex's insistence that "only the *form* of the brain-side labour recurs, never its substrate" is a process-flavoured reading — what recurs is an activity-pattern across unrelated machinery, not a shared thing. This resonance is praiseworthy on its own axis (see Hardline-Empiricist note for the boundary it must not cross).

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
Tangential to this cluster; the Bidirectional-Interaction note carries the agency weight — "active haptics requires the perceiver's movement to deliver content," spatial imagination "shapes physical action" — framing the mind side as participant rather than passive screen. No over-claim of libertarian freedom from perceptual data.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
Well-served. The apex repeatedly marks the limits of what the cross-modal evidence can show: the supramodal signature is "consonant... without being probative," the ownership claim is "strong on argument and thin on direct cross-modal evidence," and the sensorimotor article closes its Further Reading on `[[mysterianism]]` — "cognitive closure and why the interface's grammar may be all we can map." The cluster knows where its own map runs out.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This is the load-bearing verdict for this cluster, and the apex passes cleanly. It is a model of evidential-status discipline:

- It explicitly invokes `[[evidential-status-discipline]]` to forbid "treating a shared explanandum as proof of the interface," and names the Global Neuronal Workspace as the rival that predicts *the same* supramodal signature — so the datum "does not discriminate between them."
- It practices **tenet-as-evidence-upgrade-that-is-praise-worthily-NOT-done**: it had the structural opportunity to read the supramodal signature as confirmation of the interface and explicitly declined — "The Map must out-accommodate the workspace, not enlist Sanchez against it."
- It is candid about its thinnest point: "there are supramodal-access experiments but... no direct cross-modal *ownership* experiments. The ownership claim is a place the workspace looks incomplete, not a place the Map has independent evidence the workspace lacks."
- It calibrates rather than buries the disanalogies (ownership uneven across senses; binding/awareness doubly dissociable per Deroy/Chen/Spence 2014; the conscious boundary internally graded per Fahrenfort 2017), and applies the `[[common-cause-null]]` to cap how much independent confirmation a single shared-upstream pattern can supply.

The Process Philosopher's "form recurs, not substrate" resonance and the Hardline Empiricist's "consonant-not-probative" discipline **converge** on the same passages here rather than conflicting — which, per this skill's design, is the signal that the article has resolved the possibility/probability tension honestly. No calibration concern; no `refine-draft` triggered on evidential grounds.

## Content Strengths

### apex/cross-modal-capability-division.md
- **Strongest point**: The negative-then-positive structure — "The Brain Side Does Not Recur" followed by "The Mind-Side Boundary Carries a Common Signature" — turns a vague "the division generalises" into a falsifiable asymmetry, then hands the asymmetry straight to the rival theory that predicts it.
- **Notable quote**: "The brain side is a family of unrelated solutions to unrelated transduction problems."
- **Why it works**: It earns its apex status by saying something *no source article could say from inside its own modality*, while importing exactly one new external citation (Sanchez 2020) as the load-bearing positive evidence — disciplined apex grain, not a re-survey.

### topics/chemosensory-consciousness-and-the-interface.md
- **Strongest point**: Uses the most vision-unlike modality as a controlled removal of physicalist footholds (no spatial location, no smooth quality space, mirror-image enantiomers smelling different).
- **Notable quote**: "There is no place where the smell is, no axis along which it sits."
- **Why it works**: Every reductive convenience is named, conceded as a real explanation of *discrimination*, and then shown not to touch the *felt-quality* residue. Accommodation-not-proof done repeatedly and explicitly.

### topics/tactile-consciousness-and-the-interface.md
- **Strongest point**: The touching-touched perceiver and the movable rubber-hand body boundary make ownership *vivid* — the apex correctly singles touch out as where its mind-side ownership term is strongest.
