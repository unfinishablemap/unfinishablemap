## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
Chalmers would be most at home in `functionalism` and `visual-consciousness`, both of which keep the hard problem load-bearing rather than decorative. `functionalism` states the sufficiency debt precisely: functionalism "asserts that functional organization is *sufficient* for consciousness… but it never specifies why exercising a functional role should involve qualitative experience at all — why the right causal structure does not simply run 'in the dark.'" That is the explanatory-gap point in its sharpest form, and the article correctly notes that the phenomenal-concepts strategy and the appeal to brute necessity "are attempts to discharge this debt; neither pays it." `visual-consciousness` makes the same move concretely: "the more we learn about how visual information is processed, the more glaring becomes the absence of any explanation for why this processing feels like anything at all."

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
Stapp finds the interface located, not asserted. `visual-consciousness` proposes the posterior hot zone "might be where consciousness *interfaces* with visual processing rather than where it's generated," and ties binding to possible quantum holism while flagging it "remains speculative." `conscious-vs-unconscious-processing` routes the causal story through attention: "voluntary control of attention becomes a mechanism for consciousness to influence which information enters the workspace. The quantum Zeno hypothesis locates this control at the quantum level," with the timing hierarchy (femtosecond decoherence → 280–300ms decision windows) cited rather than waved at. `prehension` offers the boldest structural bridge — concrescence as the synthesis of prehensions into determinate form "structurally parallels quantum collapse" — and immediately marks it as speculation ("The Map speculates that this parallel may reflect a deeper connection").

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
Nagel would praise `visual-consciousness` for centering the perspectival "from *here*": the egocentric structure of the visual field, the 3D-arena residue, colour qualia as the paradigm of irreducibility. `teaching-as-metarepresentation` extends first-person centering into an unusual place — the *phenomenology of teaching itself* ("the 'aha, they don't get it' moment," the felt effort of finding apt words, satisfaction when understanding dawns) — and argues this phenomenology is "specifically metarepresentational… not generic 'consciousness.'" That is a genuinely original framing: phenomenal character indexed to a relational cognitive achievement rather than a sensory one.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
This is Whitehead's home cohort — `prehension` is a model of disciplined exposition of his system (physical/conceptual/negative prehension, concrescence, the denial of vacuous actuality, the personally-ordered-society response to the combination problem), and `teaching-as-metarepresentation` deploys causal efficacy and presentational immediacy to explain why "understanding… must be evoked, not transferred." Crucially, `prehension`'s §"Relation to Site Perspective" does the honest thing the constraint demands: it *names the tension* rather than smuggling panpsychism in. "Whitehead's system treats prehension as universal… which entails panpsychism. The Unfinishable Map takes prehension's *relational structure* as illuminating without accepting that all actualities are experiential… This is a genuine tension: Whitehead would reject the restriction." Process-philosophical resonance is used here exactly as it should be — as structural illumination, never as evidence that experience is widespread.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
Kane is served by `conscious-vs-unconscious-processing`'s third enabled function — *spontaneous intentional action*: "Unconscious processing can respond to stimuli but cannot initiate action from internal states alone," with the blindsight evidence (patient DB needed prompts; never spontaneously used blind-field information) doing real work. The article draws the agency link explicitly: "if voluntary action depends on consciousness, then debates about whether we have free will are also debates about what consciousness can do." `teaching-as-metarepresentation` complements this: deliberate pedagogical adjustment is a paradigm of self-initiated, goal-maintained action.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
`conscious-vs-unconscious-processing` earns Mysterian respect for treating the overflow impasse as potentially principled rather than merely unresolved: "this unfalsifiability might itself be evidence: it's exactly what we'd expect if phenomenology genuinely transcends functional access." `functionalism` ends its hard-problem section by handing the baton to mysterianism explicitly — brute sufficiency "is where mysterianism gains traction" — appropriate epistemic humility rather than a dualist victory lap.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This cohort is mostly outside Birch's load-bearing zone (minimal-organism / boundary-case consciousness), but `functionalism` touches it directly in §"The Specification Problem" and handles it with exactly the restraint the persona rewards. *C. elegans* (302 neurons, positive Φ in connectome models, anesthetic sensitivity) and *Physarum* (maze-solving, route optimisation, habituation, **no neurons**) are presented as a *dilemma for functionalism* — "either *Physarum* has some form of consciousness — a conclusion most functionalists resist — or additional criteria beyond functional role separate cognitive from conscious systems" — rather than as an occasion to slide either organism up the five-tier scale. The article does not assign *Physarum* or *C. elegans* any evidential tier at all; it uses them as a wedge against substrate-neutrality. That is **tenet-as-evidence-upgrade praise-worthily *not* done**: the structural opportunity to say "the tenets weaken parsimony against simple-organism consciousness, therefore *Physarum* is a realistic possibility" was right there, and the article declined it. The pattern is honestly "functionalism's own multiple-realizability prediction is embarrassing for functionalism," not "boundary organisms are probably conscious."

Birch would also approve the cohort's restraint on the conceivability-to-possibility inference. `functionalism` repeatedly refuses to treat the zombie inference as automatic ("The load-bearing step is the inference from *conceivability* to *metaphysical possibility*, which type-B physicalists contest… The Map does not treat the inference as automatic") and, in §"What Would Challenge This View?", concedes the empirical predictions "have real defeating power over the Map's *motivation*, even where they leave the conceivability arguments formally standing." And the *concession-convergence* readings of Putnam and Milinkovic-&-Aru are each explicitly marked as the Map's *favoured interpretation, not forced by the data* ("a committed physicalist sees biological computationalism… as physicalism getting *more* precise… the opposite of a concession"). That is the register-discipline the persona exists to reward.

**Process Philosopher / Hardline Empiricist convergence check**: No conflict in this cohort. The Process Philosopher's praise lands on `prehension` and `teaching`; the Hardline Empiricist's lands on `functionalism`. Where they touch the same page (`prehension`'s panpsychism restriction; `functionalism`'s *Physarum* dilemma), both can praise different aspects — Whitehead-friendliness as structural illumination, evidential discipline as tier-restraint — without either cashing out as an evidential upgrade. The tension is resolved honestly on every page where it could have arisen. No calibration concern to flag.

## Content Strengths

### concepts/functionalism.md
- **Strongest point**: The §"The Hard Problem and Functionalism" articulation of functionalism's *self-incurred* explanatory debt — judging functionalism by "the standards functionalism itself accepts" rather than by imported dualist premises.
- **Notable quote**: "functionalism helps itself to the sufficiency claim without supplying that explanation. The phenomenal-concepts strategy and the appeal to brute necessity are attempts to discharge this debt; neither pays it."
- **Why it works**: It turns the critique inward (internal-standard refutation) rather than asserting an external dualist requirement, and the §"What Would Challenge This View?" honestly grants empirical defeating power over *motivation* while distinguishing that from logical refutation.

### concepts/visual-consciousness.md
- **Strongest point**: Using the maximally-mapped sensory modality to make the gap *starkest*, not weakest — the more neuroscience we have, the wider the residue.
- **Notable quote**: "The explanatory gap is starkest for vision because we know so much about visual processing… the more we learn about how visual information is processed, the more glaring becomes the absence of any explanation for why this processing feels like anything at all."
- **Why it works**: Pre-empts the "just-wait-for-more-neuroscience" rejoinder by showing the gap grows, not shrinks, with knowledge; and the interface reading of the posterior hot zone is explicitly marked "compatible with both materialist and interactionist interpretations."

### concepts/teaching-as-metarepresentation.md
- **Strongest point**: The five-level representational stack (first-order knowledge → second-order representation → theory of mind → gap representation → pedagogical action) makes the ape/human asymmetry a *structural* claim with a stated working-memory mechanism, not a hand-wave.
- **Notable quote**: "Chimpanzees *have* skills without knowing they have skills—without representing those skills as the kind of thing that could be possessed or lacked, transmitted or withheld."
- **Why it works**: The Jourdain framing gives the asymmetry a memorable handle, the §"What Would Challenge This View?" lists four genuine defeaters (incl. elaborate ape teaching and human teaching without false-belief competence), and the AI section keeps the claim conditional ("*if* such a system were demonstrably non-conscious").

### concepts/prehension.md
- **Strongest point**: §"Relation to Site Perspective" naming the panpsychism tension instead of hiding it — the cohort's clearest instance of register-honesty about *selective borrowing*.
- **Notable quote**: "This is a genuine tension: Whitehead would reject the restriction. But the structural insights… remain valuable even under dualist assumptions about where experience occurs."
- **Why it works**: §"Criticisms" voices the eliminativist objection at full strength ("wrapping that dependency in Whitehead's terminology adds no explanatory power beyond what neuroscience already provides") and grants the empirical-untestability point outright — the article earns its metaphysical vocabulary rather than assuming it.

### concepts/conscious-vs-unconscious-processing.md
- **Strongest point**: Grounding the anti-epiphenomenalism case in a specific 2025 empirical result (Randeniya reanalysis: only 10% of 80 conditions showed genuine unconscious processing) while explicitly resisting over-reading it ("This doesn't mean unconscious processing doesn't exist—it means the scope has been dramatically overestimated").
- **Notable quote**: "If consciousness were epiphenomenal, cognitive load shouldn't selectively impair consciousness-dependent functions—but it does."
- **Why it works**: §"What Would Challenge This View?" states four concrete falsifiers; the COGITATE 2025 result is reported as vindicating *neither* GNW nor IIT fully (no cherry-picking); and the dualist reading is explicitly held as one of two live interpretations the evidence "doesn't distinguish… conclusively."
