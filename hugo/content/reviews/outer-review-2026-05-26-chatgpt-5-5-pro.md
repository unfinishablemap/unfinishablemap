## Audit verdict

The article is **strongest as a cross-domain taxonomy of selective access**: conscious reports often expose a finished output—familiarity, effort, agency, “rightness,” cardinality, pastness—while the machinery that produced that output remains opaque. That descriptive thesis is well supported and is presented with more methodological caution than many dualist-leaning pages would use. The target article explicitly says each dissociation “constrains” transparent identity readings but does not by itself establish dualism, and it concedes the common-cause null: all these literatures may converge because they are studying human cognition through human introspection rather than because they have uncovered a metaphysically deep interface. [The Unfinishable Map+1](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/phenomenal-output-causal-machinery-dissociation/)

The article is **weaker as an argument for dualism**. The evidence pressures naïve “phenomenology transparently reports its own neural operation” views, but it does not yet discriminate dualism from higher-order, global-workspace, predictive-processing, metacognitive, or selective-access functionalist accounts. The article knows this, but the “Relation to Site Perspective” section still says dualism is “supported” and then immediately says compatibility is not support; that internal tension should be resolved by downgrading the claim to “dualism is made more live / less easily dismissed” rather than “supported.” [The Unfinishable Map+1](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/phenomenal-output-causal-machinery-dissociation/)

The most important audit finding is not citation fabrication or blatant mischaracterization. It is **calibration drift**: the apex article is cautious, but several linked source articles still use stronger “demonstrates consciousness doing work” language that the apex article itself has already disciplined.

## Does the evidence support the dissociation thesis?

**Yes, for the local dissociation thesis.** The diagnostic signature—output present, operation hidden, fallibility selectively dissociable, and inspection altering the mode—is a real pattern across the literatures the article invokes. Recognition and face-processing anomalies are especially good cases: Capgras/prosopagnosia double dissociations are a standard reason not to treat face recognition as a single transparent route from perception to familiarity; a 2023 Brain review summarizes the classic contrast between overt recognition, autonomic familiarity, and belief evaluation. [The Unfinishable Map+1](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/phenomenal-output-causal-machinery-dissociation/)

Mental-effort evidence also supports the narrower claim that felt effort is not a transparent readout of a unitary internal resource. The Hagger multi-lab registered replication reported a near-zero ego-depletion effect under the standardized protocol, and the Map’s own Mental Effort article correctly uses that to weaken the old “resource depleted” interpretation while preserving felt effort as a signal requiring explanation. [Sage Journals+1](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/pdf/10.1177/1745691616652873)

Noetic feelings are accurately characterized at the broad level. The Noetic Feelings article ties Koriat-style feeling-of-knowing work and Thompson’s feeling-of-rightness work to heuristic, fluency-based outputs rather than introspective access to inferential machinery. That fits the apex article’s “verdict without derivation” thesis well. [The Unfinishable Map+1](https://unfinishablemap.org/voids/noetic-feelings-void/)

**Only moderately, for the stronger “single architecture” thesis.** The article’s move from many dissociations to one recurring architectural signature is plausible, but underpowered. It admits that the evidence comes from “substrates within a single nervous system,” and that cross-species, cross-cultural, and patient-population evidence is still owed. That is the right concession; without it, the convergence could just be a common design fact about human reporting systems rather than evidence of a deep metaphysical interface. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/phenomenal-output-causal-machinery-dissociation/)

**Weakly, for the dualist inference.** The evidence mostly establishes levels one and two in the article’s own four-level chain: separability and phenomenological reality. It contributes to mechanistic independence in some cases, but it does not reach metaphysical irreducibility. The article’s “constrain-vs-establish” discipline is therefore correct; where it slips is in nearby rhetoric that lets “raises cost for physicalism” sound like “supports dualism.” [The Unfinishable Map+1](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/phenomenal-output-causal-machinery-dissociation/)

## Citation accuracy and currentness

The target article’s core external characterizations are mostly fair. Its placebo caution is especially good: it cites Hróbjartsson and Gøtzsche to recalibrate strong placebo claims, notes that open-label placebos complicate propositional-belief specificity, and frames placebo as contested but not eliminated rather than as a simple proof of mind-over-body causation. The Cochrane conclusion supports that caution: placebo interventions did not show important clinical effects in general, though certain patient-reported outcomes, especially pain and nausea, can be influenced and are hard to separate from reporting bias. [The Unfinishable Map+1](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/phenomenal-output-causal-machinery-dissociation/)

The placebo section should now cite newer open-label placebo work. A 2025 updated systematic review and meta-analysis of 60 randomized trials found a small positive overall open-label placebo effect, stronger in clinical than non-clinical samples, and much larger for self-reported than objective outcomes. That result does not refute the Map’s point, but it does shift the evidential weight toward ritual, expectation, report, and interoceptive appraisal rather than clean “conscious propositional content selects a physiological cascade.” [Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-025-14895-z)

There is one concrete reference-metadata error. The target article lists “Howard, I. S., Ingram, J. N., Wolpert, D. M., et al. (2016), Physical and mental effort disrupts the implicit sense of agency, Cognition, 157, 23–32.” The actual Cognition volume listing gives the paper as **Emma E. Howard, S. Gareth Edwards, Andrew P. Bayliss**, pages **114–125**. The same erroneous reference appears in the Mental Effort article. [The Unfinishable Map+2ScienceDirect+2](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/phenomenal-output-causal-machinery-dissociation/)

The memory framing needs the May 25 changelog update propagated back into the apex. The changelog says the Memory Anomalies article was revised because “dual-process architecture” was being presented as too settled, adding continuous-signal-detection contestation from Wixted, Wixted & Mickes, Pratte & Rouder, and others. That matters because the target apex still leans on dual-process/source-monitoring language without importing the updated architecture-independent caveat. [The Unfinishable Map](https://unfinishablemap.org/workflow/changelog/)

The subitizing/cardinality case is directionally fine but too tied to older FINST-style “parallel-indexing” phrasing. The classic Trick and Pylyshyn model remains historically relevant, but a 2020 Scientific Reports article explicitly argues that subitizing, unlike estimation, does not process sets in parallel; the target article should preserve the opaque-output point while softening the mechanism-specific “parallel indexing” gloss. [The Unfinishable Map+1](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/phenomenal-output-causal-machinery-dissociation/)

Agency literature also needs a 2020s update. The target article uses agency as one member of the dissociation cluster, but recent work stresses that “sense of agency” is not a single construct: ability, phenomenal character, bodily agency, external-event agency, sensory attenuation, intentional binding, and explicit report can come apart. A 2022 Nature Reviews Psychology review also treats agency as shaping perception and behaviour across action and human–machine contexts, which gives the Map useful material but complicates any simple agency-as-one-output framing. [Nature+1](https://www.nature.com/articles/s44159-022-00030-6)

## Are counterarguments engaged, or bracketed away?

The target article does **not** merely bracket counterarguments away. It explicitly concedes Wegner-style agency deflation, says the agency literature has no knockout argument, recategorizes several traditions that do not carry independent dualist weight, admits the common-cause null, and says sophisticated functionalists can absorb each member. That is real counterargument engagement. [The Unfinishable Map+2The Unfinishable Map+2](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/phenomenal-output-causal-machinery-dissociation/)

However, the strongest rival is still underdeveloped. The article names higher-order theory, illusionism, and global workspace theory, but it does not fully steelman a unified rival architecture. A strong rival would say: metacognitive systems produce summary control signals because lower-level process access is computationally unavailable or maladaptive; conscious “feelings” are higher-order, predictive, or global-access representations of those control signals; and placebo/choking effects are caused by representational, attentional, interoceptive, and motor-control states, not by phenomenal properties as such. This is not a weak ad hoc reply; current predictive-processing work explicitly models metacognitive feelings as affective experiences that predict and regulate mental-process dynamics, and Lau-style perceptual reality monitoring treats conscious reality-status as a higher-level source-monitoring tag sharing computations with metacognition. [Sage Journals+1](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/pdf/10.1177/17456916231221976)

The article’s “positive companion” remains the most vulnerable section. It says placebo and choking show phenomenology coupling to causal machinery, but those cases more directly show that **belief, expectation, attention, self-monitoring, anxiety, and appraisal** affect physiology and performance. The Empirical Phenomena of Mental Causation source article is more overstated than the apex: its lead says placebo “demonstrates consciousness directing physical processes” and choking “demonstrates consciousness interfering with them,” even though its later sections acknowledge functionalist and illusionist replies. [The Unfinishable Map+3The Unfinishable Map+3The Unfinishable Map+3](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/phenomenal-output-causal-machinery-dissociation/)

Choking under pressure is real enough, but it does not by itself show phenomenal causation. The current literature still debates definitions, mechanisms, and interventions; an OUP review emphasizes that neurophysiological underpinnings remain limited and that interventions vary in effectiveness. The Map can use choking as an “attention/self-monitoring disrupts automatic skill” case, but “consciousness intrudes” is too metaphysically loaded unless it is explicitly marked as the Map’s interpretation. [OUP Academic](https://academic.oup.com/book/39228/chapter/338745073)
