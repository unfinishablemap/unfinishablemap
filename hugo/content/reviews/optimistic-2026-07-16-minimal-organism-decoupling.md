## Executive Summary

This cluster is the strongest calibration-disciplined writing in the corpus. Every article separates two registers the site is designed to keep apart — *is a mind compatible with the Map's metaphysics?* versus *does the evidence support one?* — and refuses to let tenet-coherence do evidential work. The headline finding of this review is unusual: the Process Philosopher and the Hardline Empiricist personas, engineered to be in tension, **converge in praising the same passages**, which is the signature of a cluster that has resolved the possibility/probability slippage honestly rather than hiding it.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The scale-independence argument in *consciousness-in-simple-organisms* is exactly the hard-problem move Chalmers would endorse: "If we cannot explain how human neural activity produces experience, we cannot explain it for nematodes either. The explanatory gap exists at every complexity level." The *C. elegans* case — complete connectome, 302 neurons, and still no way to determine whether anything is felt — is presented as the hard problem made empirically vivid rather than dissolved. Chalmers, a New York Declaration signatory named in the article, would recognise his own commitments accurately represented.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
*consciousness-in-simple-organisms* engages the physics-consciousness interface without over-reaching: it flags that slime molds possess microtubules as cytoskeletal elements but lack "the orchestrated neuronal architecture the Penrose–Hameroff hypothesis treats as load-bearing," then reads *Physarum* as cognition-without-consciousness rather than forcing it into the quantum-interface story. Stapp would appreciate that the interface mechanism is invoked as a discriminating hypothesis (why neuron-free systems might process without experiencing) rather than as a universal solvent.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The contemplative-traditions section centres first-person structure precisely: the Vijñāna/Prajñā distinction and Metzinger's minimal phenomenal experience are used to ask whether *C. elegans* might have "bare awareness without the cognitive elaboration," keeping the "what is it like" question coherent even where behaviour is silent. Nagel's insistence that the third-person connectome "tells us nothing about whether that processing is witnessed" is stated almost verbatim in the article.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
*consciousness-in-simple-organisms* gives Whitehead a genuinely fair hearing — actual occasions, prehension, "degrees of integration, not presence/absence," the combination problem reframed as more tractable than emergence-from-nothing. Crucially, it does so **without letting process-friendliness upgrade any organism's evidential tier**: the article states outright that "This perspective doesn't commit the Map to full Whiteheadian metaphysics" and keeps Hydra at *speculative integration* even while granting the process reading is available. This is the correct use of the persona: process-compatibility removes a defeater, it does not elevate the evidence.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
*single-cell-proto-agency* is where agency itself is put under the microscope, and Kane would find the treatment bracing rather than deflationary. The article refuses the easy deflation ("it's just noise") by showing the noise is *doing the searching* — CheY-P fluctuation "enhanced performance on shallow gradients by up to 73%." The result preserves room for genuine agency-talk at higher rungs precisely by being honest that at the prokaryotic floor "harnessed stochastic search with a memory" cannot be told apart from choice.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
The cluster is saturated with well-earned epistemic humility. *single-cell-proto-agency* argues the noise-vs-choice line is "plausibly **not empirically tractable** — the underdetermination is in-principle, not a gap awaiting better instruments," and hands off to the agency void. *consciousness-in-simple-organisms* names the *C. elegans* case as "what cognitive closure looks like empirically." McGinn's thesis is not merely cited but operationalised.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This is the load-bearing verdict for the cluster, and it is strongly positive. Every article touches minimal-organism consciousness or tenet-load-bearing agency claims, and every one exercises the restraint the persona exists to reward:

- **The five-tier scale is used honestly and consistently.** *consciousness-in-simple-organisms* opens with a dedicated "Evidential Register: Tenet vs. Evidence" section that states the discipline explicitly: "Removing a defeater keeps a hypothesis live; it does not, on its own, supply positive evidence." *C. elegans* is held at *live hypothesis sliding toward speculative integration*; Hydra and slime molds at *speculative integration*. None is placed at *realistic possibility* on tenet-load.
- **Tenet-as-evidence-upgrade is praise-worthily *not* done.** The article had every structural opportunity to slide nematodes up the scale on the strength of dualism weakening parsimony — and explicitly declined, warning against "treating 'not ruled out by our metaphysics' as if it were 'positively supported by the evidence.'" The named pattern *"tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating"* is realised, not just gestured at.
- **The negative signal is protected from absorption.** The UAL section insists that "*Failing* UAL remains evidentially meaningful even when UAL is treated as a coupling-marker," refusing to let the interface reframing launder away the fact that all three organisms fail the behavioural signature.
- **Parsimony is disciplined in both directions.** *single-cell-proto-agency* makes the cleanest Tenet-5 case in the corpus: "parsimony cuts *both* ways here. It bars the deflationist from treating sufficiency as proof of absence just as it bars the strong proto-agency camp from treating behavioural richness as proof of presence." The symmetry is the point — undecidability reported "as a limit rather than a defeat."
- **Framework-internal readings are marked as such.** *cross-species-behavioural-confidence-proxy-tests* is a model of this: it scores each of four faces separately (*realistic possibility, contested* / *live hypothesis* / *speculative integration*), honours the voids-circularity discount, and states plainly that "The positive reading of the convergence is therefore Map-internal at the interpretive-frame level; what survives framework-neutrally is the substrate-extension finding under the discount."

**Process Philosopher / Hardline Empiricist convergence:** In *basal-and-bioelectric-cognition* the two personas praise the same move. The article grants Levin's substrate-independence of *function* freely while denying it settles substrate-independence of *experience*, and explicitly rejects the panpsychist extrapolation ("The Map takes the decoupling, not the panpsychist extrapolation"). Whitehead's process-compatibility and Birch's evidential discipline land on the same passage without conflict — the article has resolved the tension rather than papering over it. No calibration concern surfaced in this cluster; no `refine-draft` is warranted on slippage grounds.

## Content Strengths

### single-cell-proto-agency-and-the-evidence-problem
- **Strongest point**: The dissolution of the received noise-vs-choice dichotomy by showing the noise is functionally load-bearing — a genuinely original synthesis of two literatures the article is candid about having built itself ("This convergence is the Map's own reading of the two literatures rather than a published position on either side").
- **Notable quote**: "because the behaviour proto-agency would predict is generated in full by a complete stochastic-biochemical account, the two readings are **behaviourally underdetermined**."
- **Why it works**: It reframes an unanswerable metaphysical question as a precise epistemological result, grounds it in Tenets 3 and 5, and hands off cleanly to the agency void. The Sourjik detail (the person who measured the noise is on record calling it noise) is a superb piece of epistemic reporting.

### consciousness-in-simple-organisms
- **Strongest point**: The explicit two-register architecture, with the evidential-status table and the "What Would Challenge This View?" section giving six concrete defeaters.
- **Notable quote**: "The dualist framing defeats dismissive *a priori* arguments; it does not elevate the evidence."
- **Why it works**: A long survey article that never lets breadth erode calibration; each organism carries its own tier verdict and the tenet section stays disciplined even in the Occam's-Razor pass.

### basal-and-bioelectric-cognition
- **Strongest point**: The decoupling as a *defeater for false positives*, symmetric with the ladder's own *Physarum* argument, and honest that weighting toward phenomenal markers "cannot mean discarding behavioural evidence altogether: there are no non-behavioural, third-person markers of consciousness to fall back on — that very absence *is* the explanatory gap."
- **Notable quote**: "Uncontested agency sits alongside unasserted experience."
- **Why it works**: It leans on Levin's *own* restraint rather than imposing the Map's, which makes the decoupling evidence rather than assertion.

### cross-species-behavioural-confidence-proxy-tests
- **Strongest point**: Per-face asymmetric scoring — refusing to average four differently-supported faces into one number — and the "bandwidth-blocked on present designs" framing that states a falsification/upgrade condition rather than a permanent wall.
- **Notable quote**: "the honest channel-score must reflect that graded asymmetry rather than averaging it out."
- **Why it works**: It is a design audit that knows exactly what it can and cannot claim, and marks the Carruthers rival as a discount the bounded-witness reading "cannot absorb and walk past."
