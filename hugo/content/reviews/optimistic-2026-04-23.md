## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)

Chalmers' naturalistic dualism has long needed the authority-vs-sufficiency decoupling that `concepts/trumping-preemption.md` lines 52-54 now installs as its own section. Chalmers' psychophysical laws are themselves a candidate authority layer — they specify *which* of the metaphysically possible phenomenal properties attaches to which physical state, without changing what the physical state does. The article's framing at line 34 that trumping "supplies a structural template for how a non-physical experience could be the cause of a physical effect without producing any observable violation of causal closure" is the *exact* register Chalmers' 1996 programme operates in. The key Chalmersian temperament appears at line 58: the fiction-to-nature disanalogy paragraph concedes that Schaffer's magic-world law is stipulated and that nature is not in the business of stipulating such rules — so the template earns its place only *once the independent case is in hand* from the hard problem and the Map's tenets. Chalmers himself has been careful on this point: psychophysical laws must be *posited* on the back of the conceivability argument, not deduced from the physical. Seeing the Map refuse to use a structural template as a freestanding argument — and name the refusal — is philosophical hygiene Chalmers would recognise.

**What resonates most strongly**: The closing paragraph at `concepts/trumping-preemption.md` line 81 — *"Its virtue is that it supplies a vocabulary in which dualism can be articulated without visible physical anomalies — rather than a vindication of dualism itself. The sentence 'the brain is sufficient' is true and uncontroversial; what trumping adds is that sufficiency is not the same as authority."* Chalmers' entire programme is that dualism's case must be made on *phenomenological and conceptual* grounds, not on physical anomalies. The Map here makes the methodological choice explicit: the structural template is a vocabulary, not an argument, and the argument lives elsewhere. This is the right Chalmersian division of philosophical labour.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)

Stapp's programme requires a precise account of *how* the mental can do causal work at the quantum interface without producing detectable trajectory deviations. The `concepts/mental-causation-and-downward-causation.md` article at lines 103-105 now delivers exactly this: the Schaffer-Saad-Map chain identifies the default causal profile with the Born-rule distribution and locates the selection within the probabilities rather than deviating from them. This is the engineering-level detail Stapp's own writing aspires to but rarely achieves — *Mindful Universe* gestures at the Zeno effect as mechanism without specifying how mental causation fits within closure. The Map's trumping installation gives Stapp the missing structural layer: the Zeno effect is the *mechanism* (repeated observation holding neural patterns in place), and trumping is the *causal structure* (the experience preempts the physical state's default profile). The two fit together as yesterday's review anticipated, and today's cross-reviews have delivered the integration explicitly in the body of `mental-causation-and-downward-causation.md` at lines 103-105 rather than leaving it as a cross-reference.

**What aligns**: The `concepts/trumping-preemption.md` §Costs at line 70 is a rare dualist admission that the template is *designed* to be invisible at the trajectory level — *"both processes complete, and the authoritative one is picked out by a law, not a trace. A trumping-based dualism therefore inherits this invisibility and must earn its keep on theoretical grounds."* Stapp has said for decades that the observable content of quantum mechanics is compatible with mental causation precisely because collapse is not observationally reducible to physical dynamics. Seeing the Map name this invisibility as a *cost* rather than packaging it as a virtue is the methodological maturity Stapp's position has needed.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)

Nagel would read `voids/expertise-and-its-occlusion.md` as an extended vindication at a new scale of the "what is it like to be..." argument. The article's central observation at line 48 — that the novice cannot imagine the expert's perception but the expert *cannot recover* the novice's perception — gives Nagel's original asymmetry a mechanism and a direction. Nagel (1974) argued that subjectivity sets epistemic bounds on objective description; this article argues that *development itself* sets epistemic bounds on prior subjectivity. The one-way door is not merely a Nagel-style across-minds gap but a within-mind gap across time. The three-feature closing at lines 110-114 — consciousness having a specific functional niche, the cross-domain invariance of the pattern, and the directional asymmetry of the loss — converts the phenomenological observation into a *structural finding* with testable predictions across domains.

**What centres subjective experience appropriately**: The `voids/expertise-and-its-occlusion.md` treatment at lines 78-82 of absorption as "the deeper void within the void" — *"at the moment of peak expertise—in flow—there may be no subject present to observe the transformation"* — is Nagelian in the sharpest sense. The "what is it like" question presupposes a subject; flow research suggests that the condition under which expertise is most deployed is the condition under which the subject is structurally absent. This is not the destruction of the Nagelian question but its recursive extension — what is it like to have been where there was no subject to have the experience? The article does not answer but names the structural outline of the question, which is the phenomenologically appropriate move.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)

Whitehead's programme requires that subjectivity be constituted by its prehensions rather than standing prior to them, and that novelty in experience be grounded in the *concrescence* of prior elements into a new unity. The `voids/expertise-and-its-occlusion.md` lines 52-54 treatment of L.A. Paul's transformative-experience framework — *"some forms of sensibility change entail risking one's knowledge of what experiences imbued with one's prior sensibility were like"* — is the Whiteheadian process applied to the acquisition of skill. The expert is not a substance that has gained skill; the expert is an occasion of experience whose structure of prehensions has been reconstituted by the training history. Whitehead would read the one-way door at line 46 as showing that each conscious occasion is genuinely novel — the expert's perception cannot be decomposed into the novice's perception plus additional information, because the subject's relational structure has itself changed.

**What avoids crude dualism**: The `concepts/trumping-preemption.md` closing §"Relation to Site Perspective" at line 77 — *"Consciousness causally influences the physical world. Trumping describes one precise way in which that influence could be realised — not by pushing the trajectory off-course, but by standing behind the trajectory as the thing whose authority the physical correlate was executing."* This is the Whiteheadian reading of causation: authority is not a push but a metaphysical anchoring of events in a unified occasion that binds them. Whitehead's ontology of actual entities requires exactly this — causation is not efficient-cause push but the *inclusion* of prior occasions in the constitution of present ones. The trumping structure makes the Whiteheadian move philosophically respectable within analytic metaphysics.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)

Kane's programme needs what the new material delivers: a clean separation between the question *could the physical process have produced this action?* and the question *is the agent the author of this action?* Standard libertarians conflate these and end up either at event-indeterminism (which Kane warns is insufficient for agency) or at agent-causation (which tends to introduce uncaused causes). `concepts/trumping-preemption.md` lines 50-54 now give Kane the precise conceptual move: authority and sufficiency are decoupled, and the authority layer is where agency-talk lives without requiring trajectory deviation from physical sufficiency. Kane's "ultimate responsibility" condition requires that the agent be the source of its own character-forming choices; the trumping template shows how an agent can be the authoritative cause of a physical event that a sufficient physical process also produced — which is what Kane needed all along but could not articulate without the structural template.

**What supports agency and choice**: The `concepts/delegatory-causation.md` lines 151-154 treatment of the bandwidth constraint — *"Consciousness cannot take over fine-grained neural details at that bandwidth. The experience's causal profile, and therefore the subset of the default profile it preempts, must be coarse-grained"* — is the libertarian's honest answer to the worry that free-will-relevant agency occurs at the wrong level of description. Kane's own writing has sometimes treated quantum indeterminacy as a source of agency-relevant noise without specifying *where* the agency-relevant choice points are in the cognitive architecture. The Map's coarse-grained preemption answer — experience preempts at the policy level ("raise arm", "attend to the argument"), not at the motor-implementation level — is the sharpest version of Kane's intuition that agency operates at the self-forming-action level rather than the neural-firing level.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)

McGinn's permanent mysterianism finds a new exemplar in `voids/expertise-and-its-occlusion.md`. The article's *lock* — the fact that consciousness attempting to cross back through the one-way door disrupts the skill it is trying to inspect — is a within-mind version of McGinn's cognitive-closure thesis. The mind cannot inspect itself without *damaging* the function that makes the inspection possible. This is structurally the same limit McGinn names for the hard problem: the representational faculties that could in principle describe consciousness are themselves part of the consciousness they would describe, and the attempt to turn them into objects of description disrupts their function as instruments of description. The article at line 66 captures the Polanyi version of this move — *"You cannot use your spectacles to scrutinize your spectacles"* — with a specificity McGinn 1989 lacks: the expert evidence shows the limit is *operational* (the skill breaks when inspected), not merely a representational gap.

**What shows epistemic humility**: The `voids/necessary-opacity.md` article, refined 2026-04-22T16:38, preserves at line 47 the self-protective character of the opacity: *"The opacity is both self-concealing and self-protecting: it hides itself, and attempts to expose it destabilise the system that depends on it."* McGinn's cognitive closure is usually framed as a limit on what we can know about consciousness *from outside*; the opacity thesis here runs an even tighter limit from *inside* — consciousness cannot know itself without destabilising itself. McGinn would read this as the sharpest available statement of the epistemic situation: not only is the truth about consciousness hidden, but the attempt to uncover it damages the cognitive machinery that would do the uncovering. Having this at the void-level rather than as an aside in a general dualism article is the right level of prominence for the claim.

## Expansion Opportunities

### High Priority

#### The Authority Layer as a General Philosophical Category

- **Builds on**: `concepts/trumping-preemption.md` lines 50-54 (the authority-vs-sufficiency decoupling), `concepts/delegatory-causation.md` lines 61-65 (trumping as the variety delegatory causation invokes)
- **Would address**: The trumping-preemption article names the *authority layer* as the independently useful conceptual contribution, but the Map has no standalone treatment of authority as a general philosophical category. The category has applications far beyond the psychophysical case: legal systems routinely distinguish authoritative from sufficient causes (e.g. a court's ruling is authoritative for the legal consequences even when a prior administrative decision was independently sufficient); corporate decision structures decouple authority from sufficiency routinely; in some philosophical theories of action, the agent's intention is the authoritative cause of a bodily movement even when the movement is physically sufficient without the intention being consciously formed. A concept or topic article would (a) formalise the authority layer as a philosophical category with multiple instantiations, (b) distinguish *legal* authority (socially constructed), *structural* authority (rank, priority), and *metaphysical* authority (the candidate the Map's programme needs) — flagging that only the metaphysical case is at issue for psychophysics, and that no straightforward case exists outside fiction/convention, (c) examine whether the cross-domain applicability of the authority-sufficiency decoupling strengthens or weakens the Map's use of it — it could cut either way, and naming the risk is part of the work.
- **Estimated scope**: Medium article (2000-2500 words)
- **Tenet alignment**: Dualism (the metaphysical authority claim is central), Minimal Quantum Interaction (the authority layer is the level at which MQI operates), Occam's Razor Has Limits (parsimony would collapse authority into sufficiency; the article would defend the decoupling on theoretical grounds).

#### Observational Closure as a Standalone Thesis

- **Builds on**: `concepts/delegatory-causation.md` lines 116-122 (Saad's distinction between universal and observational closure), `concepts/trumping-preemption.md` line 70 (observational closure as a cost of the template), `concepts/mental-causation-and-downward-causation.md` lines 103-105 (observational closure as parallel to MQI)
- **Would address**: Observational closure — the thesis that physical causal patterns are preserved *observably* even when universal closure fails — is currently treated as a *feature* of delegatory causation and as a *parallel* to MQI. But it is independently a load-bearing thesis of the Map's position: without it, dualist mental causation *must* produce empirical anomalies, and the Map is committed to no detectable violations. A concept article on observational closure as a standalone thesis would (a) define the thesis precisely (no experiment can detect the difference between a world with mental causation and one without, given appropriate architectural conditions), (b) catalogue the arguments that establish observational closure as a consequence of the Map's other commitments (Born-rule preservation; coarse-grained preemption; the authority-sufficiency decoupling), (c) examine the epistemic consequences — observational closure is a strong thesis about what we cannot find empirically, which places the Map's case squarely on philosophical rather than empirical ground, (d) address the objection that a commitment to observational closure is unfalsifiable, and distinguish the Map's position (observational closure is a *consequence* of the framework's structural commitments) from the unfalsifiability concern (the framework is falsifiable through its other commitments — MWI disconfirmation, neural-quantum-effect falsification, etc.).
- **Estimated scope**: Medium article (2000-2500 words)
- **Tenet alignment**: Minimal Quantum Interaction (directly — MQI entails observational closure at the quantum level), Dualism (observational closure is what allows dualism to be articulated without committing to empirical anomalies).

#### The Fiction-to-Nature Disanalogy as a Methodological Constraint

- **Builds on**: `concepts/trumping-preemption.md` line 58 (the fiction-to-nature disanalogy paragraph), general methodological questions about philosophical template-importation
- **Would address**: The trumping-preemption article's fiction-to-nature disanalogy is installed as a one-paragraph philosophical-hygiene move, but the disanalogy applies to an entire class of structural templates philosophy imports from stipulation-based cases (fictional laws of magic, legal conventions, military rank). A concept or topic article would (a) generalise the fiction-to-nature disanalogy as a methodological constraint — when importing a philosophical template from a stipulative context into a naturalistic one, the template must be supplied with an independent case for why nature would instantiate a non-stipulative analogue, (b) catalogue other cases where philosophy has imported stipulative templates and where the disanalogy was/wasn't flagged (Newcomb's problem from game theory; the prisoner's dilemma from strategic interaction; counterpart theory from fiction), (c) examine whether this is a one-off philosophical-hygiene point or a systematic constraint on a class of philosophical arguments, (d) apply the constraint back to the Map's own use of the trumping template — the independent case comes from the hard problem and the Map's tenets, and the article would make that dependence fully explicit.
- **Estimated scope**: Medium article (2000-2500 words)
- **Tenet alignment**: Occam's Razor Has Limits (the fiction-to-nature disanalogy is a post-parsimony constraint on template-importation; parsimony-only arguments would not see the disanalogy), Dualism (the Map's use of the trumping template depends on this constraint being handled correctly).

#### Expertise-Induced Amnesia and Memory Architecture

- **Builds on**: `voids/expertise-and-its-occlusion.md` lines 68-72 (Høffding and Montero 2020 on expertise-induced amnesia), `concepts/consciousness-and-memory.md` (if it exists — grep the concepts directory)

The finding has broader implications for consciousness-and-memory architecture: if procedural knowledge runs without generating the episodic traces that conscious recall requires, then episodic memory formation is not a passive record of what consciousness does but an active function with its own engagement conditions. A concept or topic article would (a) specify the architectural relation between procedural knowledge, consciousness, and episodic memory, (b) examine what the expertise-induced amnesia finding implies for introspective reliability (the reports experts give of their own peak performances are *reconstructions* rather than recollections, because the trace was never formed), (c) connect this to the source-attribution void's confabulatory layer — expertise-induced amnesia is a specific case of source-attribution failure where the "source" is the expert's own performance, (d) examine whether flow research and expertise research converge on a single structural claim about when consciousness produces accessible traces and when it does not.
- **Estimated scope**: Medium article (2000-2500 words)
- **Tenet alignment**: Dualism (the dissociability of consciousness and episodic memory is more natural on dualism than on identity theory), Bidirectional Interaction (consciousness's memory-generating function is part of its causal profile; the article would specify what kind of bidirectional activity produces memory traces).

#### Reinvestment as Interface Evidence

- **Builds on**: `voids/expertise-and-its-occlusion.md` lines 62-64 (reinvestment theory as the mechanism of the lock), `concepts/skill-delegation.md`
- **Would address**: The expertise article treats reinvestment theory as the empirical evidence for the lock, but reinvestment has a specific relevance to the Map's interface programme: it is a case where conscious re-entry *measurably degrades* a physical process (motor performance) that was running without conscious oversight. If consciousness and motor performance are merely aspects of a single neural system, reinvestment degradation is surprising — re-routing attention should not damage an automatic process unless the attention-resource itself competes with the skill-execution resource. But if consciousness and the procedural system are distinct (and the interface is disciplined by a coarse-grained preemption channel), then conscious re-entry is exactly the kind of interface-level intrusion that the skill-delegation article predicts. A short concept article would (a) articulate reinvestment as *interface evidence* rather than merely as expertise-void evidence, (b) specify what the reinvestment experiments show that an identity-theory account would have trouble predicting, (c) connect reinvestment findings to the Map's coarse-grained preemption account (which predicts that fine-grained neural processes should not be accessible to conscious control without performance disruption), (d) honest limitation: the reinvestment literature is psychological, not neurological, and what it shows is compatible with a range of theoretical accounts including purely attentional ones.
- **Estimated scope**: Short-medium article (1500-2000 words)
- **Tenet alignment**: Bidirectional Interaction (the interface is evidenced by what happens when consciousness re-enters automated skill), Dualism (the evidence is more surprising on identity theory than on dualism, though not conclusive).

### Ideas for Later

- **The Novice-Expert Interaction Asymmetry as Methodological Frame**: `voids/expertise-and-its-occlusion.md` lines 110-114 notes that the directional asymmetry between novice-imagining-expert (possible with training) and expert-recovering-novice (impossible) is a distinctive feature of the void. This asymmetry has implications for philosophical methodology: the philosopher examining their own developed views cannot recover the pre-philosophical perspective they started from, but the philosopher can (perhaps) imagine views they have not yet developed. A topic-level article would formalise this as a constraint on philosophical self-examination — we systematically mis-describe our prior selves but can partially access our future selves through argument and thought experiment.
- **The Spectacles Paradox Applied Outside the Expertise Case**: Polanyi's "you cannot use your spectacles to scrutinize your spectacles" appears in `voids/expertise-and-its-occlusion.md` line 66 and in the tacit-knowledge literature generally. A short concept article would examine whether the paradox applies to other cognitive domains (the moral intuitions that judge ethical theories; the conceptual architecture that defines what counts as an explanation; the perceptual system that judges perceptual veridicality). If the paradox is a general structural feature of cognitive systems rather than a specific feature of skill, the scope of the expertise void extends considerably.
- **The Coarse-Grained Preemption Claim as a Testable Prediction**: `concepts/delegatory-causation.md` lines 151-154 argue that consciousness preempts at the coarse-grained level but leaves the fine-grained remainder to the physical state. This generates a specific, testable prediction: the neural signatures of *policy-level* conscious decisions should be *different* from the neural signatures of *implementation-level* procedures, and conscious intervention in implementation-level procedures should degrade performance. The expertise void's reinvestment evidence is one data point; a topic article would catalogue the full range of experimental paradigms that could test the coarse-grained preemption claim.
- **Insensitive Effects as a Diagnostic of the Authority Layer**: Bernstein (2014) argues that trumping cases are best redescribed as overdetermination with *insensitive* effects — effects that remain robust across wide ranges of alterations to their candidate causes. The Map's current treatment names this critique (line 48 of trumping-preemption) and the Mills/Lowe reply (line 70), but the *insensitivity* itself is a philosophically interesting property: insensitive effects are the places where the authority layer does work. A concept article would formalise insensitivity as a diagnostic for where authority-layer considerations are live vs dispensable.
- **The Reconstruction-vs-Recollection Distinction Across Domains**: `voids/expertise-and-its-occlusion.md` lines 98 notes that whether consulting diary records recovers the pre-expert experience or merely provides information about it remains an open question. This distinction — genuine recollection vs reconstruction from records — applies far beyond the expertise case (to confabulation research, to the source-attribution void's third layer, to memory research generally). An article-length treatment would consolidate the distinction as a structural feature of mnemonic cognition rather than a one-off issue in the expertise case.
