## Pessimistic Analysis Summary

### Critical Issues Found (quote-fidelity, all fixed)

- **Bergson — inserted word in verbatim quote.** Article read: *"Time is an invention or it is nothing at all."* The authorized Arthur Mitchell translation of *Creative Evolution* (Ch. IV) reads **"Time is invention or it is nothing at all."** — NO "an" — verified verbatim at the publisher-of-record full text (Project Gutenberg ebook #26163, italicized sentence immediately followed by "But of time-invention physics can take no account"). The "an" was **added on 2026-06-25** by a prior review that trusted libquotes (an aggregator citing a Modern Library "p. 361") over the actual Mitchell text; that "correction" corrupted three files. This is the quote-verify-self-contamination-via-own-page / aggregator-ratification failure mode. **Corrected back to "Time is invention…" and family-resolved** across [process-and-consciousness](/apex/process-and-consciousness/) and [bergson-and-duration](/topics/bergson-and-duration/). (A fourth file, [creative-consciousness](/concepts/creative-consciousness/), already carried the correct no-"an" form and was left untouched.)
- **Whitehead — misquote (inversion/compression).** Article read: creativity is *"the ultimate category"*. Whitehead's actual term is the **"Category of the Ultimate"** (the categoreal-scheme heading whose formula is "the many become one, and are increased by one"); creativity itself is "the ultimate" / "the universal of universals" *principle*, not "the ultimate category". Verified against *Process and Reality* exposition (Category of the Ultimate = the many-become-one-and-are-increased-by-one formula). **Reworded to the source form** in [process-philosophy](/concepts/process-philosophy/) ("Whitehead names this creative advance his 'Category of the Ultimate'…") and, in the two siblings, to `creativity is "the ultimate," his Category of the Ultimate`. The unquoted paraphrase in [epistemic-advantages-of-dualism](/topics/epistemic-advantages-of-dualism/) ("creativity … is the ultimate category", not in quotes) was left as an acceptable gloss, not a verbatim-quote claim.
- **James — pronoun altered inside verbatim quote.** Article read: conscious states seem *"shut in their own skin"*. James's verbatim (*Principles of Psychology* 1890, "The Mind-Stuff Theory") is singular: **"shut in its own skin, windowless, ignorant of what the other feelings are and mean"** (each *feeling* shut in *its* own skin). Verified at psychclassics-class sources. **Corrected in [process-philosophy](/concepts/process-philosophy/)** to "each conscious state seems 'shut in its own skin'" (verbatim singular).
