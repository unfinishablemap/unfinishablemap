## Warnings

### concepts/coupling-modes.md
- **Tenet violated**: Tenet 2 (Minimal Quantum Interaction)
- **Issue**: The "Probability Control" coupling mode section presents RNG experiment results with language that lends implicit credibility to empirically detectable mind-matter interactions. While the article's conclusion disfavors probability control as "least minimal," the framing that "some meta-analyses find small effects" is not paired with the theoretical explanation for why Born-rule-violating probability control is ruled out by the interface formalism.
- **Quote**: "Random number generator (RNG) experiments have attempted to detect such deviations. Results are mixed—some meta-analyses find small effects, others attribute these to publication bias. No consensus exists that consciousness reliably biases external quantum systems."
- **Recommendation**: Add a sentence clarifying that any genuine probability-control effect would constitute an empirically detectable deviation from Born-rule statistics, which the interface formalism's preserved Born rule and no-signalling constraints prohibit. The passage should explain why probability control is ruled out on theoretical grounds, not merely note the absence of empirical consensus.

### topics/consciousness-and-the-philosophy-of-mathematics.md
- **Tenet violated**: Tenet 2 (Minimal Quantum Interaction)
- **Issue**: The article posits a second mode of consciousness-reality interaction—a "non-causal mode of access" to abstract Platonic mathematical objects—that extends the interaction model beyond what MQI defines. MQI specifies consciousness biasing quantum indeterminacies in neural systems without injecting energy. Extending this to a distinct interaction with abstract entities expands the framework's scope without flagging it as a speculative departure.
- **Quote**: "If consciousness can interact with the physical world at points of quantum indeterminacy without violating physical conservation laws, its interaction with abstract mathematical structure may operate through an analogous non-causal mode of access."
- **Recommendation**: Add a qualifier acknowledging this is a speculative extension beyond the Map's core MQI commitment, which concerns physical quantum systems specifically. The analogy is interesting but should be flagged as going beyond what the tenets establish.
