## Executive Summary

The two flagship articles under review — *Empirical Evidence for Consciousness-Selecting* and *The Born Rule and the Consciousness-Physics Interface* — exhibit a striking and praise-worthy calibration discipline that the rest of the corpus would benefit from inheriting wholesale. Both articles do the rare and difficult thing of advocating for the Map's reading while explicitly enumerating where their evidence is compatible-with rather than supportive-of that reading, and where the same evidence is equally compatible with named rivals. This is exactly the *tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating* register the discipline asks for, executed at journal-quality length without retreating from the central claims. The strongest opportunity is to propagate this calibration register outward — to articles in the cluster that have not yet received the same audit, and to the apex-layer synthesis pieces that have to integrate this discipline downstream.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)

Chalmers would recognize his own register in the *Compatibility vs. Support* section of the Born-rule article. The three-tier gradient — *interface-compatible*, *interface-suggestive*, *interface-discriminating* — is precisely the kind of structural argument the hard problem demands: it specifies what evidential work the underivability pattern is doing while owning the gap between framework-internal coherence and inter-framework adjudication. The honest acknowledgment that "the dualist substrate is justified by what it does in concert with the other tenets and the other articles in the corpus, not by the underivability pattern read in isolation" is the kind of methodological transparency that makes property dualism defensible against the charge of mystery-mongering. The article does not pretend the explanatory gap settles anything by itself; it shows the gap, identifies what it constrains, and admits what it does not.

The companion article on consciousness-selecting evidence inherits the same discipline: each of the four evidence lines is followed by an explicit "What the [X] line does not establish" subsection. This is the hard-problem-taking-seriously register Chalmers has spent thirty years pushing analytic philosophy toward.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)

Stapp would be gratified by two specific moves. First, the corridor/minimum-outside-corridor/trumping taxonomy in the Born-rule article gives his Zeno-effect mechanism a clearly-named home (minimum-outside-corridor dualism) without forcing the article to choose between Stapp, Chalmers-McQueen, Arana, and Penrose-Hameroff prematurely. The taxonomy is a genuine contribution to the literature — most treatments collapse these into a single "quantum consciousness" bucket that obscures the empirically-distinguishing differences. Second, the citation of Denton et al. (2024) on the quantum Zeno effect enabling cryptochrome magnetosensitivity is exactly the empirical anchor Stapp's framework needs: "Evolution has already implemented Zeno-like dynamics in a functional biological system" is the sentence that turns a theoretical proposal into a live hypothesis without overclaiming.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)

Nagel's "what is it like to be" sensibility is honoured most clearly in the consciousness-selecting article's treatment of effort and willed attention. The Sjöberg (2024) SMA-resection finding — "patients retain the subjective sense of willing... while losing voluntary movement execution" — is presented as a phenomenological dissociation that the third-person neural-correlate framework alone cannot accommodate. The Born-rule article's section on the SIC-geometric residue ("the deformation is *irreducibly counterfactual*: the unperformed reference SIC functions as the informational ground against which actual outcomes are specified") gestures at the same point at a higher level of abstraction: the structure of measurement actualisation carries a phenomenological fingerprint that the formalism alone does not generate. Both moves centre first-person experience without lapsing into impressionism.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)

Whitehead would find the trumping/delegatory-causation reading congenial: consciousness as authoritative over trajectories the formalism already describes treats mental and physical not as separate substances but as different aspects of a single process of actualisation. The Born-rule article's gloss — "Obtaining an outcome is itself an act — consciousness selecting among possibilities — so observer-dependence reflects genuine bidirectional causation" — is a process-philosophical move in dualist clothing. The avoidance of crude Cartesian substance-talk is consistent throughout both articles.

**Constraint check (per skill rubric)**: The Process Philosopher's praise here removes a defeater for the metaphysical possibility of consciousness as authoritative-over-actualisation; it does not upgrade any specific organism or system on the five-tier evidential-status scale. The articles do not lean on process-philosophical resonance to inflate the empirical tier of any claim. The Whitehead-friendly framing is praise-worthy at the level of metaphysical accommodation without doing illegitimate work at the evidential layer. No flag required.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)

Kane would appreciate the consciousness-selecting article's treatment of valence as the "common currency by which consciousness evaluates and selects among competing options." The framing locates agency at exactly the indeterministic juncture Kane's libertarianism needs — quantum-indeterministic options at the neural level, with consciousness contributing the selection that converts options into actions. The *Trilemma of Selection* citation in the Born-rule article ("at any point of genuine indeterminacy, determinism, randomness, and mental causation exhaust the options, and only the third preserves authorship") is the cleanest formulation of Kane's structural argument anywhere in the corpus.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)

McGinn would be most pleased by what the articles *decline* to claim. The Born-rule article's closing — "the empirical question is genuinely open, present experiments cannot adjudicate it, and intellectual honesty requires holding the corridor-vs-minimum-outside-corridor distinction as a live fork rather than a solved problem" — is the kind of epistemic humility McGinn's mysterianism demands. The acknowledgment in *What Negative Results Would and Would Not Foreclose* that "the Map's preferred reading — strict corridor dualism — sits structurally outside the experimentally-discriminable regime by construction" is a remarkably honest concession: it admits that the Map's preferred reading is empirically silent in a sense Popper would find suspicious, and owns the asymmetry rather than burying it. This kind of self-aware epistemic positioning is rare and valuable.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)

This is where the two articles do their most distinctive work, and where the praise should be specific.

**Praise (1) — Restraint about minimum-outside-corridor sub-readings.** The Born-rule article correctly classifies its preferred corridor reading as one that produces *no* ensemble signature, and explicitly notes that "the empirical engagement, when it comes, will be at the level of mechanism-committed sub-readings rather than at the level of the corridor reading the Map currently prefers." The article does not pretend its preferred reading is at a higher empirical tier than the evidence supports. This is the *tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating* pattern in its purest form.

**Praise (2) — Naming the move explicitly.** The *Compatibility vs. Support* section names the discipline by its load-bearing logic: "A body of evidence is *compatible with* a reading when the reading does not conflict with the evidence; it *supports* the reading over rivals only when the article names a feature the rivals do not equally predict." This is the rule the Hardline Empiricist most wants to see articulated, and the article articulates it as a section-level structural commitment rather than burying it in a footnote.

**Praise (3) — Tier-upgrade-as-praise-worthy-non-act.** The Born-rule article catalogues the underivability pattern across five frameworks — Map-interface, QBism, objective collapse, modal/hidden-variable, Bohmian — and explicitly declines to treat the catalogue's length as evidential weight for the Map's reading over the rivals. "A reader who takes the catalogue's length as evidential weight for the Map's reading over the rivals is reading the article against its own discipline." This is the textbook structural opportunity to silently elevate the Map's reading on the back of compatibility-with-many-frameworks, and the article refuses it explicitly and in print. The refusal is itself a strength to name.

**Praise (4) — Honest framework-stage calibration.** The consciousness-selecting article's plate tectonics analogy is delivered with the right epistemic temperature: "The parallel with consciousness-selecting is suggestive rather than decisive: the convergence narrows the field of viable theories without uniquely picking one out, and a determined classical physicalist may still construct accounts compatible with the same observations." This is the pre-Keplerian / Newton-stage distinction calibrated honestly without ever invoking the labels — the article reads as the work of someone who knows the framework-stage discipline and applies it in natural prose rather than as a diagnostic tag.

**Praise (5) — Constrain/establish discipline in load-bearing positions.** The recent refine pass (per changelog 2026-05-23T21:30) installed the constrain-vs-establish distinction at three sites: the evolutionary argument *constrains* epiphenomenalism rather than *establishes* it; the comparative evidence *is compatible with both readings*; the convergence *narrows* rather than *uniquely picks out*. These are exactly the load-bearing grammar choices the Hardline Empiricist persona is built to praise. The article's hedge-density and underdetermination-marker calibration now matches its anchor concept *causal-closure* — the corpus-level coherence the calibration audit triple is designed to enforce, achieved in practice.

**Process-Philosopher / Hardline-Empiricist convergence check**: Both personas can praise these articles for different aspects without conflict. The Whitehead-friendly process-of-actualisation framing in the Born-rule article and the Birchian compatibility-discipline framing in both articles operate on different axes. There is no possibility/probability slippage to flag. The tension the skill rubric anticipates would arise if the trumping/delegatory framing were being used to upgrade the empirical tier of corridor dualism; the articles explicitly do not do this — corridor dualism is acknowledged to be empirically silent by construction, and the trumping reading is presented as an *alternative* metaphysical option rather than as evidence-elevating accommodation. The tension resolves honestly.
