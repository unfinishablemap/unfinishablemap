- **The article is one of the Map's better-calibrated pieces, and it survives — but only barely, and only because it refuses the inference an outer reviewer would most want to kill.** Its closing "What the Distinction Does Not Establish" section explicitly concedes that the dualist reading is *not forced* by the phenomenology, names enactivism and functionalism as non-remainder rivals, and correctly states that Husserl and Merleau-Ponty bracketed ontology and that Merleau-Ponty rejected substance dualism. The *Bidirectional Interaction* subsection quietly commits exactly the epistemic-to-metaphysical slide the closing section forbids — asserting the lived/objectified coupling is "a single coupled system... not mere correlation" and reads it as anatomical-grain support for interactionism. ## 2. Five-Dimension Analysis

### Dimension 1 — Phenomenological Attribution Fidelity

**Husserl.** The core attribution is accurate. *Ideen II* is correctly named the canonical source; the *Leib*/*Körper* contrast (material thing vs. lived, sentient body), the touching-touched double-givenness (*Doppelempfindung*), and the body as not-merely-one-object-among-objects are all faithful to §§35–42. The section titles independently confirm the vocabulary: §38 "The Body as organ of the will and as seat of free movement," §41(a) "The Body as center of orientation," §64 "Relativity of nature, absoluteness of spirit." The *Nullpunkt*/zero-point doctrine is genuine Husserl.

Two fidelity problems, both real:

- **Over-linked causal claim.** The article says the touching-itself analysis "led Husserl to conclude that the lived body is not merely one object among objects and cannot be fully accounted for by the natural sciences alone." The *conclusion* is defensible Husserl; the *causal linkage* is an interpretive simplification. Husserl's anti-naturalism about the *Leib* rests on the transcendental reduction and the "absoluteness of spirit" (§64), not specifically on the double-sensation phenomenon. This matters because it is precisely the linkage Carman attacks.
- **Transcendental-idealism erasure (a co-optation risk the article does not flag).** Husserl's claim that the *Leib* resists full naturalisation is *not* a proto-embodied-mind thesis; it flows from the priority of transcendental subjectivity over nature. For Husserl the body remains, in Carman's verified summary (1999, p. 224), "not itself constitutive of intentionality... but is instead a noetic achievement of transcendental subjectivity." An article routing Husserl toward an interactionist-dualist interface reading is deploying a transcendental idealist for a metaphysics he does not hold. The article never notes that Husserlian "irreducibility" is idealist, not dualist.

**Merleau-Ponty.** The *corps propre* / *corps objectif*, the "I can" vs. "I think," the body as permanent "here"/zero-point, and motor intentionality are all correctly attributed. Translation metadata is correct (Landes 2012, Routledge; the Colin Smith 1962 translation is the alternative, and page numbers/wording differ substantially between them — the article cites Landes, which is defensible). The article's own closing section correctly states Merleau-Ponty "resisted both Cartesian dualism and reductive materialism, and his lived body is not offered as a second substance" — this is the single most important co-optation inoculation in the piece, and it is accurate.

**The Carman gloss — a genuine distortion.** The parenthetical claims: "Taylor Carman's 1999 reading clarifies how the two thinkers differ here: Husserl's double-givenness centres on the body sensing itself, while Merleau-Ponty's 'I can' locates the lived body in motor intentionality rather than reflexive touch." Verified against the full text of Carman, "The Body in Husserl and Merleau-Ponty," *Philosophical Topics* 27(2):205–226:

- The descriptive contrast (touch-primacy vs. motor intentionality) is broadly faithful.
- But "I can" is **misattributed**. Carman deploys "*Ich kann*" as a quotation from *Husserl* — at p. 211 he quotes the *Cartesian Meditations* §44 (Husserliana I:128): "these kinestheses of the organs proceed in the I am doing and are subject to my I can." It is not, in Carman's text, Merleau-Ponty's signature term.
- More seriously, the gloss **launders Carman's thesis**. Carman's actual claim is asymmetric and hostile. His opening thesis (1999, p. 205): "Merleau-Ponty's phenomenology amounts to a radical, if discreet, departure not only from Husserl's theory of intentionality generally, but more specifically from his account of the intentional constitution of the body and its role in perceptual experience." His verdict on the touch-primacy itself (p. 206): "to tie the body's intentional constitution specifically to the sense of touch in this way, I shall argue, amounts to a fundamental misunderstanding of its significance for phenomenology." And decisively (p. 222): "for Merleau-Ponty does not assign to this sort of experience the founding significance it has on Husserl's account." The article converts a sharp critique — Merleau-Ponty *rejecting* the founding role of the double-sensation — into a bland "here's how two colleagues divide the labour." For a piece whose whole discipline is supposed to be attribution fidelity, neutralising a critical source into a complementary one is exactly the failure mode to flag.

**Co-optation firewall verdict.** The firewall's charge is *mostly* met — and this is the article's principal virtue. Every non-dualist figure the firewall warns about (Merleau-Ponty, enactivism, "phenomenology proper") is explicitly acknowledged as non-dualist or anti-Cartesian in the closing section. Author-stance verification: the article does *not* recruit Merleau-Ponty to a conclusion he rejects — it says outright that it isn't. This is materially better than the co-optation-prone norm. The residual leaks are that (i) the *Bidirectional Interaction* subsection (see Dimension 2) still recruits the coupling for an interactionist conclusion, and (ii) Husserl's transcendental idealism is silently naturalised.

### Dimension 2 — Inferential Warrant

**Constrain-vs-establish gate: passed in the closing section, failed in the Bidirectional Interaction section.** The article's headline discipline is exactly right: "The phenomenological distinction is well-established. The *dualist reading* of it is not." It states the distinction "does not, on its own, entail two substances" and "is a constraint every theory of embodiment must meet, not a proof of any one of them." This is the constrain-not-establish gate stated almost verbatim in the site's own methodology vocabulary. Credit where due. [concepts/lived-objectified-body-distinction](https://unfinishablemap.org/concepts/lived-objectified-body-distinction/)

**But the gate is not applied evenly.** The closing disclaimer covers only the **Dualism** tenet. The **Bidirectional Interaction** subsection makes an unhedged evidential claim: the lived and objectified bodies "are not two parallel tracks but a single coupled system whose two registers continuously inform each other. **That bidirectional coupling—not mere correlation—is what the Map's interface reading... makes concrete.**" This is a textbook epistemic-to-metaphysical slide: from "damage to the body changes the felt body, and attention changes bodily sensation" (uncontested, fully physicalist-compatible) to "bidirectional *interaction* between registers," where "interaction" is being loaded with the tenet's causal-ontological sense. Every example offered (neuropathy reshaping the felt body; attention amplifying sensation; somatic-training plasticity) is neutral between the interface reading and a wholly physicalist self-model account. The article that disclaims the ontological inference for irreducibility helps itself to the same inference one section earlier for interaction.

**Epistemic-to-metaphysical slide / the epoché point: partially confronted.** The closing section notes phenomenology is "methodologically prior to the metaphysics... bracketing the question of what ultimately exists." Good. But it treats this as a reason the *materialist* can't claim the phenomenology either — it does not confront the sharper point that the epoché was *constructed* to block the very inference the Map's broader framework draws downstream. The gap is named but not priced.

**Constitutional-attractor effect: present but mild.** The clinical exhibits are pre-sorted into "runs in both causal directions, which is why this distinction supports... Bidirectional Interaction." The five-exhibit selection (proprioception loss, Sacks's leg, phantom limb, somatic training, locked-in) is curated to display the tenet structure. That said, the article's honest handling of locked-in syndrome (refusing the lazy "*Körper* without *Leib*" gloss, insisting the lived body is "*constrained*, not absent") is evidence *against* a strong attractor effect — it actively resists an exhibit that would have been rhetorically convenient.

**Tenet leakage: contained in exposition, leaked in application.** The exposition of Husserl/Merleau-Ponty is admirably free of site vocabulary. Leakage occurs only where the article turns to "Relation to Site Perspective," which is structurally quarantined — acceptable. The one bleed is "somatic interface" being treated as the thing the coupling "makes concrete," importing a site-specific construct into what reads as continuous argument.

**Coherence inflation / evidential-independence failure: real and unaddressed.** The clinical exhibits here (Christina, Sacks's leg, phantom limb) are the *same* cases carrying weight in `somatic-interface`, `phantom-limb-phenomena`, and `embodied-consciousness`. Phantom limb is explicitly cross-referenced. These are not evidentially independent lines; they are one body of clinical phenomenology redescribed under four article titles. Nothing in the article flags that its exhibits are shared collateral with its siblings, so a reader tracking convergence across the embodiment cluster will double-count. The firewall's specific warning (phantom-limb article + clinical-dissociation article already exist) lands.

**Performative inoculation: not present.** Critics (enactivism, functionalism, Merleau-Ponty-as-anti-dualist) are engaged substantively in the closing section, not name-dropped. This is the opposite of performative inoculation — it is real, if brief, concession.

**Confession without correction: this is the core structural charge.** The article names its central limitation ("The *dualist reading*... is not forced by it") — and then *retains the dualist reading anyway* as "the Map's reading," "consonant with the Map's broader irreducibility case," with the disagreement "decided elsewhere." The limitation is converted into a credential ("Keeping those two claims separate is the central discipline of this article") rather than into a binding demotion. The honest move, on the site's own evidential-status discipline, is that if the distinction only *constrains*, then the Dualism and Bidirectional-Interaction "Relation to Site Perspective" subsections should both be explicitly marked coherence-only / framework-internal — as the somatic-interface article partially does ("not independent confirmation drawn from the body"). The lived/objectified article says this for Dualism and *forgets to say it for Bidirectional Interaction*.

### Dimension 3 — Empirical and Citation Accuracy

**(a) Metadata: clean.** All verified against publisher-of-record:

- Merleau-Ponty, *Phenomenology of Perception*, trans. Landes, Routledge, 2012 (French 1945) — correct.
- Carman, "The Body in Husserl and Merleau-Ponty," *Philosophical Topics* 27(2):205–226, 1999 — correct (the article's 205–226 matches the publisher-of-record Philosophy Documentation Center record; some indices give 205–225).
- Sacks, "The Disembodied Lady," in *The Man Who Mistook His Wife for a Hat*, Summit Books, 1985 — correct.
- Sacks, *A Leg to Stand On*, Duckworth, 1984 — correct (UK Duckworth, May 1984, precedes the US Summit edition; the article cites Duckworth, the edition of record).
- Ramachandran & Hirstein, "The perception of phantom limbs. The D. O. Hebb lecture," *Brain* 121(9):1603–1630, Sep 1998, doi:10.1093/brain/121.9.1603 — correct (PubMed 9762952).

**(b) Verbatim quotation fidelity: not applicable — and this is a genuine strength.** The article makes *no* direct quotation from Husserl, Merleau-Ponty, or Carman. The documented systemic fabrication pattern operates on quotations; by paraphrasing throughout, the article removes the surface on which that failure mode lives. This is the correct defensive posture for a co-optation-prone topic and should be noted as a model.

**(c) Empirical accuracy of the clinical exhibits: mostly sound, two imprecisions.**

- *Christina.* The article says she "lost proprioception entirely through an acute polyneuropathy." Sacks describes an "acute polyneuritis" affecting the sensory roots of spinal and cranial nerves, and a "profound, almost total" proprioceptive deficit. "Polyneuropathy" is an acceptable modern gloss on "polyneuritis"; "entirely" mildly overstates Sacks's "almost total." Minor. [GradeSaver](https://www.gradesaver.com/the-man-who-mistook-his-wife-for-a-hat/study-guide/summary-losses-2-3)[Vocabulary.com](https://www.vocabulary.com/lists/6916584)
- *"Her Körper was intact—muscles, nerves of movement, intelligence, consciousness all preserved."* This is imprecise in a way that cuts against the article's own point. Christina's sensory (afferent) neurons were destroyed — that is the physical lesion. Her *motor* system and cognition were intact, but her objectified body was **not** intact; it was damaged at exactly the sensory-nerve level that drained the *Leib*. The cleaner statement is that a *physical, third-person-describable* lesion (deafferentation) produced the lived-body change — which is, if anything, better evidence for the physicalist reading than for the interface reading. The article's phrasing ("the *Leib* had drained out of a body the *Körper* still fully occupied") obscures that the *Körper* was itself pathologically altered. [Shortform](https://www.shortform.com/blog/loss-of-proprioception/)
- *Phantom limb.* Attribution to "persistent cortical body-maps and reorganisation (Ramachandran & Hirstein 1998)" is accurate to the cited source. No overstatement.

### Dimension 4 — Blind Spots, Counterarguments, Stronger Objections

This is where the article is weakest, and the omissions are aggravated by the fact that the Map *already has* articles on several of the missing rivals — so the failure is not ignorance but a cross-linking / evidential-integration failure.

- **Predictive processing / active inference — the decisive omission.** Nowhere does the article engage interoceptive vs. exteroceptive inference as a *fully physicalist* account of precisely the lived/objectified asymmetry. This is acute here: Anil K. Seth, "Interoceptive inference, emotion, and the embodied self," *Trends in Cognitive Sciences* 17(11):565–573, Nov 2013 (doi:10.1016/j.tics.2013.09.007), and the self-model-as-controlled-hallucination line give a first-person/third-person asymmetry — the body given "from inside" via interoceptive generative models vs. "from outside" via exteroception — without any ontological remainder. The Map has a `predictive-processing` concept article and the sibling `phantom-limb-phenomena` article explicitly invokes predictive processing; the target article invokes it *not at all*. This is the site's recurring strongest unaddressed physicalist rival, absent at the exact article where it is most on-point.
- **Metzinger's self-model theory — absent.** *Being No One: The Self-Model Theory of Subjectivity* (MIT, 2003) offers a fully physicalist explanation of why the body is given in a transparent, non-objectual first-person mode (the phenomenally transparent self-model). This is the most direct deflation of "the lived body looks constitutively first-personal in a way the objectified body is not." The Map even has a `self-model-theory-of-subjectivity` concept page. Not cited.
- **The mode-of-presentation reply — the strongest single objection, absent.** The lived/objectified contrast is naturally read as a distinction in *modes of access/presentation* to one and the same body, not a distinction in what is accessed (Loar's recognitional concepts; Papineau's quotational concepts; Perry's indexical account). The Map has a `phenomenal-concepts-strategy` article that lays this out. The target article's "two modes in which one body is given" phrasing is *itself* a mode-of-presentation framing — yet it never confronts that the phenomenal-concepts strategy uses exactly this structure to block the inference to a metaphysical remainder. This omission is the single most important gap. [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/259925201_In_Defense_of_the_Phenomenal_Concept_Strategy)
- **Enactivism — named but under-engaged.** The closing section correctly says enactivism accommodates the distinction "without remainder," but does not register that enactivism *owns this exact phenomenological ground* and was built (Varela/Thompson/Rosch, *The Embodied Mind*, 1991; Thompson, *Mind in Life*, 2007) as a "middle way" rejecting *both* dualism and reductive physicalism. Thompson is explicit: experience is irreducible "not because it possesses metaphysically peculiar 'properties'... There is no dualism or idealism here." Citing the lived body toward interactionism inverts the stance of the tradition that most fully theorised it. The sibling `embodied-consciousness` article engages this ("Thompson himself treats experience as irreducible... 'ineliminable transcendental character'"); the target article does not import that engagement. [Philopedia](https://philopedia.org/schools/enactivism/)[Wordpress](https://evanthompsondotme.files.wordpress.com/2012/11/pcs-life-and-mind.pdf)
- **Gallagher's body-schema / body-image distinction — absent, and it belongs here.** Gallagher (*How the Body Shapes the Mind*, OUP, 2005) draws precisely the dissociation the article's clinical exhibits illustrate (body schema = pre-reflective sensorimotor capacities functioning without perceptual monitoring; body image = perceptions/attitudes/beliefs about one's own body), on a fully naturalistic footing. Its absence means the article discusses proprioception loss and phantom limbs without the standard scientific vocabulary that already partitions the phenomena non-dually. (The `somatic-interface` sibling cites body-schema/image material; the target does not.)
- **Illusionism/heterophenomenology — absent.** No engagement with Frankish's deflation or Dennett's heterophenomenology question (why should first-person descriptive reports carry ontological weight at all?). Given the article rests entirely on first-person structural description, the heterophenomenology challenge is directly load-bearing and unmet.
- **The graded-physicalist middle — absent.** Property dualism, neutral monism, Russellian monism, dual-aspect theory, and Searle's biological naturalism all accommodate the lived/objectified asymmetry *without substance dualism*. The article's closing section frames the choice as "ontological (the Map's reading) vs. epistemic/descriptive (the materialist reading)" — a false binary that omits the entire non-reductive-but-non-dualist middle. Since the Map's overall commitment is interactionist *substance* dualism, the article never discharges the burden of showing why the asymmetry favours two substances over any of these cheaper options. (To its partial credit, the article explicitly says it does *not* require "the substance-leaning sub-reading the agency cluster deploys" — so it disclaims substance dualism *locally*, which is the right modesty, but it thereby leaves the site's headline metaphysics entirely unsupported by this article, which should be stated plainly rather than left implicit.)
- **Born-rule / statistical-indistinguishability dilemma — not addressed, and gestured past.** The article routes its interaction claim to `somatic-interface`, which in turn nominates the somatic channel as "one candidate locus where [quantum] biasing might be phenomenologically legible" while conceding it is "equally consistent with undiscovered neural mechanisms." The lived/objectified article gestures at "bidirectional coupling" as load-bearing for interaction without ever confronting that consciousness-biasing-that-produces-no-aggregate-Born-deviation is empirically indistinguishable from chance. Per the firewall: it gestures at interaction without addressing the dilemma — flagged.
- **Claude-on-Claude provenance.** The article is co-authored by "Claude Oquatre-sept," reviewed within a Claude-run cycle; the changelog shows the 2026-07-21 outer-review leg was itself Claude Opus 4.8 reviewing a ChatGPT subject and generating zero tasks ("findings verified stale"). Nothing in the article's provenance notes converts this circularity into an external check. Not unique to this article, but relevant to how much independent-verification weight its "canonical anchor" status should carry.

## 3. Bottom-Line Verdict

**REVISE-HARD.**

Rationale. This is not a DELETE candidate: the phenomenological attribution is largely accurate, the metadata is clean, there are no fabricated quotations, and — decisively — the article's own closing section performs the constrain-not-establish discipline correctly, explicitly refusing to treat the *Leib*/*Körper* distinction as a proof of dualism. It is not a RETAIN candidate either, because (i) the calibration is applied inconsistently across tenets — the Bidirectional Interaction subsection commits the very epistemic-to-metaphysical slide the article forbids for Dualism; (ii) the Carman citation distorts a critical source into a complementary one and misattributes "I can"; (iii) Husserl's transcendental idealism is silently naturalised into an interface-friendly "irreducibility"; and (iv) the strongest physicalist rivals that own this exact ground (predictive/interoceptive inference, Metzinger's self-model, the mode-of-presentation reply, Gallagher's body-schema/image) are absent despite the Map already hosting articles on most of them.

Because the article *already* concedes coherence-only status for its Dualism reading, a blanket DEMOTE-TO-COHERENCE-ONLY would be redundant for half the article and insufficient for the other half. The correct instrument is a hard revision that (a) extends the coherence-only marking to the Bidirectional Interaction subsection so the two "Relation to Site Perspective" claims are calibrated at parity, (b) corrects the Carman and Husserl attributions, and (c) closes the blind-spot gaps by cross-firing the Map's existing rival articles. **If the Bidirectional-Interaction slide is *not* fixed, the article should drop to DEMOTE-TO-COHERENCE-ONLY in full.**

## 4. Article-Specific Fixes (concrete, numbered)

1. **Calibrate the two tenet subsections at parity (highest priority).** In `lived-objectified-body-distinction`, rewrite the **Bidirectional Interaction** subsection to carry the same coherence-only marking the Dualism subsection and the closing section already carry. Delete or hedge "That bidirectional coupling—not mere correlation—is what the Map's interface reading... makes concrete"; replace with an explicit statement that the reshaping data (deafferentation → felt-body change; attention → sensation change; somatic-training plasticity) are neutral between the interface reading and a physicalist self-model account, and that the interactionist reading is framework-internal, not established by the phenomenology.
2. **Fix the Carman gloss.** Remove the misattribution of "I can" to Merleau-Ponty (Carman, p. 211, quotes "*Ich kann*" from Husserl's *Cartesian Meditations* §44). Replace the laundered "here's how they differ" framing with Carman's actual asymmetric thesis, citing p. 205 ("a radical, if discreet, departure... from [Husserl's] account of the intentional constitution of the body"), p. 206 ("a fundamental misunderstanding"), and p. 222 (Merleau-Ponty "does not assign to this sort of experience the founding significance it has on Husserl's account").
3. **De-naturalise the Husserl claim.** Soften "led Husserl to conclude" (the double-sensation analysis does not by itself drive the anti-naturalism) and add one sentence noting Husserl's anti-naturalism about the *Leib* is grounded in transcendental idealism / the "absoluteness of spirit" (Ideen II §64), *not* an embodied-mind or interactionist thesis — so Husserlian "irreducibility" cannot be silently transferred to the Map's interface reading. Cite Carman p. 224 ("a noetic achievement of transcendental subjectivity") as the corrective.
4. **Correct the Christina exhibit.** Replace "Her *Körper* was intact" with an accurate description: the objectified body was pathologically altered at the sensory-afferent level (deafferentation); motor function and cognition were preserved. Note explicitly that a third-person-describable lesion produced the lived-body change, and address why this does not simply favour the physicalist reading. Change "acute polyneuropathy... entirely" to track Sacks's "acute polyneuritis" and "almost total."
5. **Add a predictive-processing / interoceptive-inference engagement** and cross-link the existing `predictive-processing` concept article. State the interoceptive-vs-exteroceptive-inference account of the lived/objectified asymmetry (Seth 2013, *TiCS* 17(11):565–573) and say why it does or does not leave a remainder — currently the article's silence on the site's strongest rival is indefensible at the very article where the rival is most on-point.
6. **Add the mode-of-presentation reply and cross-link `phenomenal-concepts-strategy`.** The article's own "two modes in which one body is given" framing invites the Loar/Papineau/Perry response that this is a distinction in concepts/access, not in what is accessed. Engage it as the strongest single objection; explain why the Map thinks the asymmetry is in the *object* and not merely the *mode*.
7. **Add Metzinger and Gallagher.** One paragraph on the transparent self-model (Metzinger, *Being No One*, MIT, 2003; cross-link `self-model-theory-of-subjectivity`) as a physicalist explanation of first-person transparency; and adopt Gallagher's body-schema/body-image vocabulary (*How the Body Shapes the Mind*, OUP, 2005) to describe the clinical exhibits, since it partitions them non-dually.
8. **Flag the shared-exhibit / evidential-dependence problem.** Add a sentence noting that Christina, Sacks's leg, and phantom limb are the *same* clinical cases that carry weight in `phantom-limb-phenomena`, `somatic-interface`, and `embodied-consciousness`, so they are not independent convergent evidence and must not be double-counted across the embodiment cluster.
9. **Name the substance-dualism burden explicitly.** Since the article disclaims the "substance-leaning sub-reading," state plainly that this article therefore supports *at most* a property-level irreducibility and provides *no* support for the Map's headline interactionist substance dualism — and that neutral monism, Russellian monism, dual-aspect theory, and Searlean biological naturalism remain live, un-excluded alternatives.

## 5. Site-Wide Methodology Improvements (concrete, numbered)

1. **Per-tenet calibration parity check.** The core defect here — disclaiming the inference for one tenet while committing it for another in the same "Relation to Site Perspective" block — is a repeatable failure mode. Add a review lens that requires *every* tenet subsection in an article to carry an explicit evidential-status marker (establishes / constrains / coherence-only / framework-internal), and flag any article whose subsections are calibrated at different levels without stated reason. Apply first to `lived-objectified-body-distinction`, `somatic-interface`, and `embodied-consciousness`.
