## Executive Summary

This is a strong, coherent cluster whose defining virtue is a single repeated move executed with discipline across six very different sub-domains: each article concedes that the rival's *mechanism* is correct and well-described, then locates the disagreement precisely at the metaphysical residue the rival's own proponents leave open. PCS adopts Carruthers–Veillet's isolation account and re-locates the dispute at first-person content; STM adopts Schacter–Addis–Michaelian's constructive system wholesale and re-locates it at autonoesis; the chemosensory article adopts combinatorial coding and the limbic-bypass anatomy and re-locates it at felt character; NDE adopts the dying-brain gamma surge as a live enriched-production escape; MMI grants that minds-in-the-formalism is a coherent program and defeats it on indexical, not parsimony, grounds; phenomenal value realism holds the identity claim while granting illusionism its strongest form. The Process Philosopher and the Hardline Empiricist — the two personas designed to be in tension — converge in praise here rather than conflict, because every article visibly declines to convert tenet-coherence or process-friendliness into an evidential upgrade. The single most valuable opportunity is not a new article but an `apex-evolve` refresh: this cluster supplies six fresh worked exhibits for `steelmanning-as-method`, whose current exhibit set is thin.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)

Chalmers is centred literally and structurally — his master argument is the spine of `phenomenal-concepts-strategy`, and the article handles it with rare honesty: it explicitly declines to present the argument as decisive ("The argument has not closed the debate, and the Map does not present it as having done so"), reads it as a *burden-shift* rather than a refutation, and walks the strongest live rejoinder (Carruthers–Veillet's first-person/third-person equivocation charge) at full strength before locating its cost. The dualist payoff is stated as a relocation, not a victory: the reply "does not so much dissolve the gap as relocate the disagreement to the question of whether first-person content is exhausted by physical description." `chemosensory-consciousness` extends the gap into the modality vision never shaped theory to expect — "why the spearmint pattern feels minty rather than merely registering as distinct" is the explanatory gap pressed where no stimulus geometry can absorb it. This is the gap used as leverage, never as a club.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)

The physics-consciousness interface is engaged at exactly the right altitude in `many-minds-interpretation`, and the article's discipline is the sort Stapp's own program benefits from. It refuses the cheap parsimony win ("The Map must *not* lean on 'an infinity of minds is profligate' as a parsimony refutation") and instead isolates the load-bearing objection as *indexical*: MMI's mind is "a passive lottery winner," "distributed *over* unitary outcomes, or rides *on* decoherence-fixed ones, never selecting *among* them," whereas the Map's interface claim is "real local collapse, biased by a determinate, persisting self." That is von Neumann/Stapp interactionism stated as a precise contrast with the closest mainstream rival, not as hand-waving. The article's most Stapp-friendly observation is that MMI's own architects — analytic philosophers with no dualist agenda — were forced to concede non-supervenience under pressure from the measurement problem; the interface reading reads that as evidence for the *plausibility* of dualism while sharply distinguishing its own active mind from MMI's epiphenomenal one.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)

"What is it like" is load-bearing throughout, and the cluster repeatedly makes it the residue that survives the rival's grant. `simulation-theory-of-memory` is the sharpest instance: it adopts the entire constructive-simulation mechanism and then isolates the autonoetic "*this-happened-to-me* marker" as exactly what construction cannot supply, citing the simulationist literature's *own* internal admission (Rivadulla-Duró's discrimination problem) that "the theory cannot simultaneously defend the simulational character of episodic memory and ground our ability to discriminate between memories and imaginings." Using the rival's self-generated residue rather than an external objection is Nagel sharpened. `chemosensory-consciousness` centres the first-person where it is least deniable — odour quality "is not read off the stimulus and has no spatial scaffolding at all" — and notes the Proust phenomenon as affect arriving *with* the smell "often before it can be named," a phenomenological datum that fits no dispassionate-snapshot model.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)

The cluster invites the process framing without leaning on it, and does so with the discipline the persona's constraint requires. `near-death-experiences` gives Whitehead a named, explicitly *complementary* role — "if experience pervades reality at every level rather than emerging from non-experiential matter, NDEs don't require explaining how consciousness 'survives' brain failure" — and crucially does not let this framing upgrade the evidential standing of survival; the article's own verdict elsewhere is that NDEs "don't *prove* dualism; they raise the cost of the materialist account and leave the enriched-production escape open." `phenomenal-concepts-strategy` runs the same move: its Whitehead section reads the directness of phenomenal concepts as possibly "tracking something irreducible" while the article's overall verdict remains the modest "something resists physical explanation." In no article is process-philosophical resonance used as the engine of a tier-upgrade — exactly the honest pattern, and the Hardline Empiricist (below) converges in praising it rather than flagging slippage.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)

`phenomenal-value-realism` takes agency seriously as a distinct, irreducible value dimension — "the experience of authoring one's actions carries significance independent of hedonic payoff" — and ties it to agent causation rather than treating choice as decorative. The article's indexical-value move is Kane-friendly in a way most value theories are not: "*this* experience matters — not just experiences-of-this-type," which preserves the moral weight of the particular agent's particular choice, and connects it to the No-Many-Worlds tenet ("Reducing *this* suffering matters only if *this* outcome is the outcome"). `simulation-theory-of-memory` gives agency a constructive role too: remembering is "goal-directed selection among candidate reconstructions, and the *setting* of that goal is where consciousness contributes directional input," which is the agent-as-source claim in the memory register.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)

Epistemic humility is structural, not ornamental. Every article in the cluster carries an explicit "What Would Challenge This View?" / "What Would Change This Verdict" section listing concrete, falsifiable defeaters — `near-death-experiences` lists four, `phenomenal-value-realism` four, `many-minds-interpretation` three, each tied to current evidence. `near-death-experiences` invokes mysterianism precisely where it belongs — "the NDE puzzle may exceed human conceptual resources entirely … explaining why both materialists and dualists find each other's positions baffling" — and demarcates it carefully rather than collapsing categories. `phenomenal-concepts-strategy` marks Ball's eliminativism as "collateral damage" to the knowledge-argument route and then explicitly notes the Map's dualism "does not stand or fall on the knowledge argument," refusing to overstate what any single argument secures.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)

This is the cluster's signature strength and the persona's verdict is strongly positive. The repeated, disciplined move across all six articles *is* the thing this persona most wants done: grant the rival its mechanism, then refuse to convert tenet-coherence into an evidential upgrade. The articles name the move explicitly and repeatedly.

- `near-death-experiences` does the hardest version: it dismantles its *own* supportive evidence. It flags that NDE enhancement, terminal lucidity, and psychedelic expansion "instantiate the *same* pattern … so the cluster carries the evidential weight of one pattern, not three independent confirmations," concedes the deflationary "feeling of clarity" reading must be granted first, and keeps the enriched-production (gamma-surge) escape open as a live competitor. This is evidential restraint applied against the home team.
- `many-minds-interpretation` states the discipline as a tenet note: the load-bearing objection "is *indexical*, not ontological: not 'too many minds' but 'no fact about which mind is mine'" — explicitly refusing the parsimony shortcut the tenet warns against, in both directions.
- `chemosensory-consciousness` repeats "accommodation, not proof" as a structural refrain, conceding after each line of evidence that "the physicalist has a credible account of each" and that the combinatorial coding, the hyperbolic odour geometry, and the limbic-bypass anatomy are all "*physicalist mechanism[s]*" the Map adopts. The framing "an illustration, not a demonstration" is calibration done in the open.
- `phenomenal-value-realism` holds its strong identity claim while granting illusionism "the strongest challenge" its full force, and carefully scopes the identity to the *affective* component after the pain-asymbolia dissociation rather than overclaiming.
- `phenomenal-concepts-strategy` is double-edged about Ball rather than treating a physicalist's self-inflicted wound as a gift: "the Map records it as such rather than treating Ball as a gift to dualism."

The Process Philosopher and Hardline Empiricist converge in praise on every article in this cluster — the diagnostic signal that the possibility/probability slippage has been resolved honestly rather than papered over. No calibration concern rises to the level of a refine-draft trigger.
