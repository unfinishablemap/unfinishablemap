## Executive Summary

This is the most physically committed cluster in the corpus and, encouragingly, also one of the most calibrated. Across all six articles the recurring, deliberate move is to separate *what physics provides* (a probability distribution, an improper mixture, a decohered menu) from *what physics does not provide* (which outcome becomes actual) — and to locate the dualist contribution precisely and only in that gap, while naming, in the article's own voice, what that contribution still owes. The cluster's single greatest strength is that its central empirical articles refuse to over-read their own evidence: `quantum-biology-and-neural-consciousness` carries an explicit "precedent, not licence" sub-reading table, and `empirical-evidence-for-consciousness-selecting` ends with a convergence table whose footnote actively *demotes* the apparent strength of the table ("removes a competitor... does not single it out from its true rivals"). The chief opportunities are cross-linking the cost-accounting and information-channel articles into the high-traffic anchor, and a small number of natural expansion points the articles themselves flag as unpaid debts.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
Chalmers would be most struck by `quantum-measurement-and-consciousness`'s central structural claim — that the measurement problem and the hard problem are not parallel gaps but *the same* gap (a complete third-person description failing to fix a first-person fact). This is exactly the explanatory-gap intuition he made canonical, here extended to physics. He would equally approve `quantum-holism-and-phenomenal-unity`'s honest refusal to claim more than it earns: "this structural correspondence is significant but does not dissolve the hard problem... the transition from 'physically unified state' to 'phenomenally unified experience' is relocated, not explained." That is precisely the Chalmersian discipline — solve the structure-matching problem without pretending to have closed the hard problem. His own CSL-IIT collaboration with McQueen is cited accurately and placed in the right ("Born-bending") family.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
This is Stapp's home cluster, and it treats him with unusual care. `selection-only-channel` does the thing Stapp himself insists on but is rarely credited for: it correctly distinguishes his *Process 1* basis-choice layer (which question to ask) from the *outcome-level* selection (which Nature answers per Born statistics), quoting him directly — "is not determined by the agent, who chooses only the question." Most secondary treatments collapse these. `quantum-measurement-and-consciousness` names his quantum-Zeno model as "the most developed successor to the von Neumann–Wigner interpretation," which is the right historical placement. Stapp would also welcome that the cluster takes the von Neumann movable-cut argument seriously as a *physics* result, not a philosophical embarrassment. The one place he might push back — that the articles classify his mechanism as "Born-bending" rather than corridor-preserving — is handled fairly: the article flags it as a live empirical question rather than asserting it.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
Nagel's "what is it like" question is the load-bearing datum throughout. `quantum-holism-and-phenomenal-unity` centres it sharply: the unity of experience "is not ascribed from outside; it is constitutive of what the experience is... There is something it is like to have *all of them together* that is not reducible to what it is like to have each separately." He would also appreciate the Leibniz-mill argument deployed as a *category* claim (external relations cannot constitute intrinsic singularity), not a mere appeal to mystery — and the explicit framing that this is "not a gap in current knowledge but a mismatch of categories," which is Nagel's own thesis about the irreducibility of the subjective.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
Whitehead is explicitly invoked in three of the six articles, and correctly. `quantum-measurement-and-consciousness` and `quantum-holism-and-phenomenal-unity` both map "actual occasions" and "concrescence" onto quantum collapse events — each collapse an internally-unified moment where indeterminate possibility becomes determinate fact. This is the genuinely apt use of Whitehead: it supplies a metaphysics on which "experience is one thing, not many coordinated things" is natural rather than ad hoc, and it explains *why* classical binding mechanisms fail (they describe completed occasions interacting, not occasions achieving internal unity in coming-to-be). **Crucially — and this is the discipline the persona constraint demands — none of these articles uses process-philosophical resonance as the engine of an evidential-status upgrade.** `quantum-holism` states plainly that "the quantum-holism reading is posited on tenet grounds (Minimal Quantum Interaction) rather than forced by the data," and `quantum-biology-and-neural-consciousness` repeatedly marks warm-biology quantum effects as "a *precedent*, not a *licence*." The Whitehead-friendliness is present and praiseworthy; it is kept off the evidence axis. This is exactly the honest pattern: *tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating.*

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
Kane would find the agency stakes preserved without inflation. The "causal closure loophole" in `quantum-measurement-and-consciousness` — physics fixes the *distribution* but not *which* outcome, and selecting among equi-energetic outcomes injects no energy — is precisely the room-for-agency move Kane needs, and it is defended against the conservation objection rather than asserted. `forward-in-time-vs-time-symmetric-selection` gives him a genuine gift: both route-families are required to deliver "a real act with counterfactual exclusion — *this* element actualised and not the others," and the article explicitly inherits (rather than re-asserts) the indexical-identity floor under that requirement. The `selection-criterion-problem` trilemma is named honestly — reason-bearing, random, or primitive — which is the hard question Kane's own ultimate-sourcehood view must answer, and the cluster does not pretend to have answered it.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
McGinn would be satisfied by how much the cluster declines to claim. `forward-in-time-vs-time-symmetric-selection` is structurally mysterian in the best sense: it sets two live route-families side by side and *refuses to adjudicate* ("it does not pick a winner"), explicitly because "choosing between them would require evidence the Map does not have." Every empirical article closes with a candid "what this cannot do" / "what remains uncertain" section. `quantum-measurement-and-consciousness` ends with three flat admissions: no mechanism specified, limited empirical predictions, no explanation of consciousness itself. This is epistemic humility built into the architecture, not bolted on.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
**This persona's verdict is load-bearing for this cluster, and the verdict is strongly positive — with one watch-item.** The cluster is where tenet-as-evidence-upgrade would be most tempting (warm-biology quantum effects → "therefore neural quantum consciousness is supported"), and the articles consistently *decline the slide*, naming it as a thing-not-to-do:

- `quantum-biology-and-neural-consciousness` is a model of the discipline. Its calibration table allocates each of six evidence lines to the *specific* Tenet 2 sub-reading it bears on (pre-decoherence / post-decoherence / strict corridor), explicitly "so the cluster's surface count is not silently converted into elevated support for sub-readings it does not strengthen." It names the exact failure mode — "cross-cluster overcounting" — and forbids it in the article's own voice. The cryptochrome row is exemplary: "Precedent, not licence: cryptochrome is a *specialised photoactivated* retinal radical-pair sensor; transferring its coherence stability to deep-brain... is itself an unearned leap." This is Birch's own evidence-quality-over-compatibility standard, internalised.
- `empirical-evidence-for-consciousness-selecting` performs the rarer move of *demoting its own showcase table*. The convergence table looks like it favours consciousness-selecting; the prose immediately undercuts the visual logic ("the table establishes less than its visual logic might suggest"), and the asterisked footnote clarifies that the quantum-bio column is "a *defeater for the no-collapse readings*, not positive support for consciousness-selecting." It uses the plate-tectonics analogy *and then* qualifies it as "suggestive rather than decisive." This is tenet-as-evidence-upgrade praise-worthily *not done*.
- `selection-only-channel` names `possibility-probability-slippage` directly: "Treating the ceiling's coherence with the Map's tenets as evidence *for* it would be a textbook case of possibility-probability slippage; the channel-class framing earns its place by what it cannot claim." A formal article policing its own formalism for evidential over-reach is exactly the restraint this persona praises.
- `quantum-biology-and-neural-consciousness` also engages its strongest competent dissenter (Duch's classical-computational alternative) in the body, and concedes convergence-against-Penrose-Hameroff with him — engaging the best dissent rather than the weakest.
- Framework-stage calibration is honest: the Duch section explicitly says the cluster has "*Tycho-analogue measurements*... without yet delivering a *Kepler-analogue specification law*," declining to over-claim Newton-stage results (per `framework-stage-calibration`).

**The one watch-item (a strength to protect, not yet a defect):** the strongest claim in the cluster is `quantum-holism-and-phenomenal-unity`'s "current evidence favours the quantum position while acknowledging the debate remains open" (and, in the lead, "[entanglement is] the only physical substrate structurally matching phenomenal unity"). "Favours" is doing slightly more work here than the disciplined verbs ("realistic," "supplement," "precedent") the rest of the cluster uses, and the "only physical substrate" claim leans on Neven's contested conjecture. The article *does* defend itself elsewhere (the explicit "posited on tenet grounds rather than forced by the data" line), so the Process Philosopher and Hardline Empiricist do **not** here conflict — both can praise the article. But this is the one spot where the two personas come closest to diverging, and it is worth a light-touch refine to bring the lead's verb into line with the body's discipline. See the refine task below (queued as refine-draft, not expand-topic, per the persona-tension rule).

## Content Strengths

### quantum-measurement-and-consciousness.md
- **Strongest point**: The structural-identity thesis — measurement problem *as a species of* the hard problem — and, critically, its *interpretation-relativity* is made explicit rather than smuggled. The article states the parallel "presupposes single-world collapse realism" and that "the parallel cannot serve as independent evidence against rival interpretations without circularity. It functions instead as a consilience argument *within* the collapse-realist family." That self-imposed circularity-guard is rare and valuable.
- **Why it works**: It earns its central, high-inbound (~88) hub status by being the article every other cluster member points back to, and it carries the load without over-claiming.

### selection-only-channel.md
- **Strongest point**: Turns "minimal interaction" from a slogan into three named, falsifiable-in-principle invariants — per-event log₂(N) ceiling, Born-preservation driving signed rate to ~0, and content-confinement (a Holevo-style ceiling on phenomenal content). The worked number (ε ≈ 10⁻⁴ → ~7 × 10⁻⁹ bits/event) gives the "smallest possible influence" tenet an actual magnitude.
- **Why it works**: The "What the Channel Is Not" section (not probability-bias, not basis-choice, not energy-injection, not candidate-generation) defines the class by what it forbids — the cleanest possible statement of Tenet 2's strictest reading.

### quantum-holism-and-phenomenal-unity.md
- **Strongest point**: The decoherence-debate section is a textbook of balanced citation: it gives Tegmark, then Hagan et al.'s rebuttal, then Reimers/McKemmish's counter-rebuttal, and explicitly warns "citing Hagan as a settled rebuttal would be selective citation. The dispute is live, not closed." It then correctly notes that for discrete-event (Zeno) mechanisms the coherence-time gap "may not matter."
- **Why it works**: The IIT critique ("definitional solutions do not explain; they relabel") and the GWT critique ("broadcasting information to many processors is still information in many places") are crisp, fair, and non-strawmanning.

### quantum-biology-and-neural-consciousness.md
- **Strongest point**: The calibration table (see Birch section). It is the single best instance of overcounting-discipline in the corpus — a supportive evidence cluster that actively prevents itself from being read as multiple independent confirmations.
- **Why it works**: It pairs the discipline with genuine 2025-2026 currency (Mavromatos QED-cavity, Beshkar QBIT, Esmaeilpour lithium-isotope, NV-centre sensors) without letting recency inflate confidence.

### empirical-evidence-for-consciousness-selecting.md
- **Strongest point**: Each of the four evidence lines is followed by an explicit "What [this line] does not establish" paragraph that concedes the line is compatible with a classical-physicalist reading. The honesty is structural, line by line.
- **Why it works**: The Wiest (2025) reference footnote even flags that "the paper's title-claim of experimental support is the author's framing, which this article does not adopt" — distancing the article from a cited source's over-claim. That is citation hygiene of a high order.

### forward-in-time-vs-time-symmetric-selection.md
- **Strongest point**: The cost-accounting register done right. "Both pay the decoherence-gap cost, then one each pays extra" (time-symmetric: retrocausal metaphysics; forward: standard-collapse-mechanism + amplification debt), with the explicit guard that this ledger "forbids reading [it] as a verdict." It even tells the reader what inference they *are* entitled to draw (weigh by your own priors) versus what the Map declines to do (impose a tie-breaker).
- **Why it works**: It models how to hold two live framework-internal alternatives without false precision — and names the amplification debt as "unpaid... there is no number bridging the quantum-scale bias and the neural-scale effect."
