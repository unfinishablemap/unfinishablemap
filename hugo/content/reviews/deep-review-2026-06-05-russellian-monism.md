## Citation Audit (exhaustive, publisher-of-record)

Every citation web-verified against the publisher of record. Findings:

### CRITICAL — Williams (2021) wrong-title chimera + fabricated source-conclusion

The article cited *Williams, R. (2021). Russellian monism and quantum mechanics. Journal of Consciousness Studies, 28(5-6), 145-172* and attributed to it the load-bearing claim that "constitutive Russellian panpsychism is **most naturally compatible** with Everett's many-worlds interpretation."

Web-verification (IngentaConnect, PhilArchive open-access PDF, PhilPapers) establishes:

- **The real paper at JCS 28(5-6), 2021, pp. 145-172** is by **George R. Williams** and is titled **"Can the Psi Data Help Us Make Progress on the Problem of Consciousness?"** — NOT "Russellian monism and quantum mechanics." Correct venue/volume/issue/pages; **wrong title** (invented).
- **The paper's actual thesis** is the opposite-leaning one: RM + quantum holism → cosmopsychism aligned with **Bohm's implicate order** (a collapse-free hidden-variable framework that, in Williams's words, "obviates the need for wave function collapse"), fitting the **psi data**. The full text contains **zero** occurrences of "Everett" or "many-worlds."
- The SEP entry on Russellian Monism (the research note's other cited source for the Everett claim) does **not** discuss Everett, many-worlds, or any quantum interpretation at all.

So the No-Many-Worlds divergence argument — present in the lead, the falsifiability framing, and the "Relation to Map's Perspective → No Many Worlds" section — was resting on a fabricated source-conclusion attached to a wrong-titled real paper. This is the dominant chimera class (real venue/year/pages, wrong title, fabricated stance) and was only catchable by live web-verify, not intra-corpus cross-check (the defect had already propagated; see below).

**Resolution applied**: Removed the Williams attribution and the citation entirely. Recast the No-Many-Worlds argument to rest on the accurate, source-free reasoning the Map can make on its own — RM assigns consciousness no causal role at measurement, so it is *interpretation-neutral* (many-worlds included); the Map's No-Many-Worlds commitment turns that neutrality into a divergence because the Map *requires* consciousness at collapse. Calibrated as a cost on the Map's own commitments, not as positive evidence. Edited in the lead and in the No-Many-Worlds subsection; removed reference #14.
