## Critiques by Philosopher

### The Eliminative Materialist
"Meditative timelessness" is a report from a brain whose temporal-tracking machinery has been deliberately quieted, not evidence of a non-temporal *ground*. The article concedes the representationalist reading (line 63) and then privileges the stronger one because "Husserl's regress argument supports this." That is the regress doing all the work — and the regress is a transcendental argument, not a finding. Churchland would say the whole construct is folk-phenomenology promoted to metaphysics.

### The Hard-Nosed Physicalist
The article is honest that the phenomenology under-determines the metaphysics (lines 67, 110, 126), which blunts the Dennettian attack. The residual target is the "convergence" rhetoric: three heterogeneous lines (regress, Husserl, Frischhut) "harder to dismiss in the company of the others" (line 110). Dennett would note that a transcendental argument and a meditation report are not independent evidence streams — if introspective reports are unreliable, the regress doesn't rescue them, and if the regress is sound it needs no corroboration.

### The Quantum Skeptic
The MQI section (line 124) is the most careful version of the post-decoherence move I have seen — it explicitly refuses to claim it supplies dynamics or relaxes the no-energy/no-Born-violation constraints, and it names the amplification chasm. Tegmark's standard "femtoseconds" objection is pre-empted by relocating the role to post-decoherence selection. Fair. The honest residue: "a non-temporal selector acting at this interface" is still a posit with no model, which the article admits.

### The Many-Worlds Defender
Handled adequately (line 128): the article does not over-claim, restricts the load-bearing anti-MWI case to the indexical objection, and treats ontological multiplicity as subsidiary. No new fault.

### The Empiricist (Popper)
Falsifiability is explicitly conceded (line 114): "No clear empirical test currently distinguishes this constitutive identity from a deflationary alternative." The article downgrades the strong constitutive claim to abductive integration rather than asserting it. This is the disciplined move, not a dodge.

### The Buddhist Philosopher (Nagarjuna)
The Madhyamaka section (lines 79–83) is unusually self-aware: it concedes the *catuṣkoṭi* cannot be refuted from outside its framework and adopts a partial-credit response, locating the substantial-subject inference in the dualism tenet rather than in the phenomenology. This is correct framework-boundary marking, not boundary-substitution. No critical issue.

## Counterarguments to Address

### Convergence as evidential double-counting (the central finding)
- **Current content says** (lines 67, 69, 110): cross-tradition reports (Theravāda Jhāna, Tibetan rigpa, Zen kenshō, Advaita witness, Christian apophasis) exhibiting "structural recurrence" are "more than coincidence"; and the regress + Husserl + Frischhut are "mutually supporting lines" whose convergence is "harder to dismiss in the company of the others."
- **A critic would argue**: The cross-tradition list is one evidential move (alert awareness reportable without temporal markers) recurring, not five independent confirmations — the same double-counting pattern the altered-state-symmetry discipline targets. Separately, the article reads the *disruptive* cluster (propofol, split-brain) as confirming the interface picture (lines 89–91), but never states that a production/identity theorist has the *same* accommodation move available for those cases. The convergence is presented as asymmetrically favourable to the non-temporal reading.
- **Suggested response**: Add one or two sentences (most naturally at the close of "Meditative Evidence", line 67, and/or in the fragmentation section, line 91) making the parallel-accommodation point explicit: the cross-tradition cluster carries the evidential weight of *one* pattern, not five; and the anaesthesia/split-brain data are equally accommodatable by a production theory, so they constrain rather than confirm. This is a `refine-draft`, not a rewrite.
