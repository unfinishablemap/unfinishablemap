## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)

The medium-status apex's reframed Bidirectional Interaction paragraph is exemplary Chalmersian conditional-claim discipline: *"The bridge from medium-status to interface-candidacy is consistency-and-suggestion rather than evidence: the structural profile of medium-status (operative-while-invisible, mode-fragile, breakdown-revealable) is *compatible with* the profile a non-physical interface would be expected to show, but compatibility is not evidence — many physical features (DNS resolution, the speaker's articulatory motor commands during fluent speech, predictive-coding precision-weighting) have the same profile without being non-physical interfaces."* This is precisely the dialectical posture *The Conscious Mind* practises in its treatment of psychophysical bridge laws — naming the structural similarity between phenomenal-property profiles and candidate physical realisers without claiming the similarity establishes identity, and explicitly flagging the *affirming-the-consequent* failure mode as a logical hazard that the argument is *not* committing. The cited counter-examples (DNS resolution, articulatory motor commands, predictive-coding precision-weighting) are particularly strong because each is genuinely operative-while-invisible and mode-fragile — exactly the profile the medium-status framing relies on — and yet none invites a non-physical reading. The article's commitment to making the conditional structure explicit ("*if* the dualist tenets are correct on independent grounds, medium-status names where the interface would be expected to sit") earns the bridge-claim its keep.

The new §"Stipulation, not Discovery" framing is also Chalmers-grade. *"Medium-status as offered here names a *kind of cognitive role*, not a substrate; the category is neutral between functionalist and dualist readings of how the role is realised. The dualist commitments invoked later are an additional, independent move; they do not derive from the typology."* Property dualism specifies that phenomenal properties are type-distinct from physical properties; it does not specify that any particular cognitive *role* must be type-distinct. The apex's clarification that the typology is neutral between functionalist and dualist realisers preserves the right level of substantive disagreement — the dualist reading is a separate commitment that survives or fails on its own grounds, not a consequence smuggled into the categorical framework.

The new §"What Would Falsify This Cluster" section is also Nagel-grade. *"A category whose evidence comes mostly from breakdowns risks being structurally indistinguishable from a category whose alleged contents do not exist — the Popperian worry already faced by phlogiston."* The section's three operational candidates (controlled bypass of the relevance filter, lesion-dissociation of the four operations, verifiable scheme-articulation while operating) are precisely the Nagelian discipline of refusing to let phenomenological categories become self-sealing: each candidate names a *third-person* observation that would dissolve the category by demonstrating that the operation is not in fact operative-while-invisible, mode-fragile, or breakdown-revealable in the constitutive sense.
