## Reply

# Adversarial Audit: "Empirical Phenomena of Mental Causation: Placebo and Choking" (The Unfinishable Map)

## TL;DR — Orientation and Headline Verdict

**Verdict: REVISE-HARD, trending toward DEMOTE-TO-COHERENCE-ONLY.** The article is unusually well-behaved on citation hygiene — every empirical reference I checked against publisher-of-record sources (Levine et al. 1978 *Lancet*; De la Fuente-Fernández et al. 2001 *Science*; Benedetti et al. 2003 *J Neurosci*; Goebel et al. 2002 *FASEB J*; Beilock & Carr 2001 *JEP:General*; Masters 1992 *Br J Psychol*; Fendel et al. 2025 *Sci Rep*) is real, correctly attributed, and in several cases reported with commendable honesty about its limits. The article is **not** guilty of fabrication or gross miscitation. Its fatal problem is conceptual, not bibliographic: it equivocates between *mental causation in the weak sense* (folk-psychological/representational states having physical effects — which physicalism, functionalism, and even most epiphenomenalists already accept under causal closure) and *mental causation in the strong sense the dualist needs* (the non-physical, phenomenal/qualitative aspect of a mental state doing causal work over and above its physical realiser). Every datum it adduces — opioid/dopamine release tracking expectancy, immune conditioning, choking under self-focus — is fully and standardly explained by physical mechanisms (descending prefrontal-brainstem modulation; Bayesian predictive coding; reinvestment/working-memory load; attentional reallocation). The choking argument in particular does **not** cut against epiphenomenalism: the disruptive variable is *attention/monitoring as a functional process*, which is exactly what an epiphenomenalist predicts physical sub-processes do.

Three-bullet bottom line:

- **The phenomena are non-discriminating.** Both placebo and choking are predicted equally well by the leading physicalist accounts (predictive coding for placebo; reinvestment/functional-attention for choking), so by the Map's own "constrain-vs-establish" gate they constrain neither dualism nor physicalism — the article's evidential payload rounds to near zero.
- **The central philosophical claim is invalid.** Choking does not cut against epiphenomenalism: it shows one *physical/functional* sub-process (explicit monitoring) interfering with another (proceduralised execution), which physicalism and epiphenomenalism both predict; it does not isolate phenomenal character as the cause.
- **The strongest rivals are cited only by gesture.** The field-defining physicalist sources (Büchel et al. 2014 *Neuron*; Wager & Atlas 2015 *Nat Rev Neurosci*; reinvestment theory; Montero's empirical-deflation critique) are absent or relegated to a wikilink — "performative inoculation" rather than engagement.

The article's own hedges ("constrains rather than establishes," "cost-of-explanation rather than unique-prediction") are doing real defensive work, to its credit — but those hedges also concede the phenomena are non-discriminating, which means the title oversells. Retain it only as a coherence-level illustration of the Bidirectional-Interaction tenet, explicitly flagged as non-evidential.

## (i) Empirical Accuracy

The empirical substrate is largely accurate, and the article deserves credit for not overstating the headline placebo literature — a rare virtue.

- **Placebo neurochemistry is correctly reported but tells against the article's thesis.** Levine, Gordon & Fields (1978, *Lancet* 312(8091):654–657) showing naloxone-reversible placebo analgesia, and De la Fuente-Fernández et al. (2001, *Science* 293(5532):1164–1166) showing striatal dopamine release on inert injection, are both real and accurately characterised. But both are textbook demonstrations of **physical brain states** (endogenous opioid release via the descending PAG–RVM system; nigrostriatal dopamine) doing the causal work. Nothing in either result isolates "belief content" as a non-physical causal factor; the content is realised in prefrontal expectancy circuitry, and the downstream cascade is neurochemical. Wager & Atlas (2015, *Nat Rev Neurosci* 16(7):403–418) — the field's review of record, which the article does **not** cite — frames the entire phenomenon in explicitly physicalist terms: "Placebo effects are beneficial effects that are attributable to the brain–mind responses to the context in which a treatment is delivered rather than to the specific actions of the drug... mediated by diverse processes — including learning, expectations and social cognition." [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/11498597/)
- **The Benedetti et al. (2003) citation is handled with real sophistication.** The article correctly reports the study's genuinely subtle finding: verbal expectancy *overrode* conditioning for analgesic and motor responses, but had *no effect* on hormonal (GH/cortisol) secretion, which remained conditioning-driven. This is accurate (*J Neurosci* 23(10):4315–4323) and the article honestly restricts its content-specificity claim to the analgesic/motor channels. Good. But note the implication the article underplays: that conscious expectancy is just one input among several (conditioning, context) into a common physical modulatory system, and is dispensable in the hormonal case — which is what a mechanistic, physicalist account predicts. [Journal of Neuroscience](https://www.jneurosci.org/content/23/10/4315)
- **The open-label/objective-outcome concession is the article's most honest moment — and quietly devastating to its own case.** It correctly cites Fendel et al. (2025, *Sci Rep* 15:29940), which reports: "OLPs yielded a small positive effect across various health-related outcomes (k = 63, n = 4554, SMD = 0.35, CI 95% = 0.26; 0.44, p < 0.0001, I² = 53%)... between self-reported (k = 55, n = 3919, SMD = 0.39) and objective outcomes (k = 17, n = 1250, SMD = 0.09; Q = 7.24, p < 0.01)." The objective-outcome effect is effectively null. This replicates the earlier Spille, Fendel et al. (2023, *Sci Rep* 13:3640) finding of a significant self-report effect (k = 13; SMD = 0.43; 95% CI 0.28–0.58) "but not for objective outcomes (k = 8; SMD = −0.02; 95% CI −0.25, 0.21)." It also matches Hróbjartsson & Gøtzsche (2010, Cochrane CD003974), which confined placebo's effect largely to patient-reported continuous outcomes vulnerable to reporting bias. Once one concedes the objective effect is ~null, the "measurable physiological changes directed by belief content" framing is substantially undercut: the robust placebo effect is largely on *felt report*, exactly where reporting bias and demand characteristics live.
- **Choking citations are accurate but the phenomenon is more contested than stated.** Beilock & Carr (2001, *JEP:General* 130(4):701–725), Beilock et al. (2002, *JEP:Applied* 8(1):6–16), Masters (1992, *Br J Psychol* 83(3):343–358) and Baumeister (1984, *JPSP* 46(3):610–620) are all real and correctly attributed. The article concedes "some findings await replication," which is appropriate. But it understates a live theoretical schism: explicit-monitoring/self-focus theory and **distraction theory** make opposing predictions, and the mechanism remains unsettled — Harris, Wilkinson & Ellmers (2023, *Neurosci Biobehav Rev* 148:105115) propose a predictive-processing account that "may also unite the apparently opposing self-focus and distraction approaches to choking." Critically, every operative theory — explicit monitoring, reinvestment, distraction, predictive processing — explains choking in **functional/computational terms** (declarative vs procedural processing, working-memory load, precision-weighting of motor predictions). None invokes phenomenal/qualitative consciousness. [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/11757876/)[British Psychological Society](https://bpspsychub.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/j.2044-8295.1992.tb02446.x)

## (ii) Inference Validity

This is where the article fails, and the failures are structural.

- **The weak/strong mental-causation equivocation.** The article's load-bearing move is to treat "belief and attention reliably produce content- and mode-tracked physical effects" as evidence for the dualist's strong thesis. But the weak thesis (mental states, qua functional/representational states, have physical effects) is common ground — physicalists, functionalists, and token-identity theorists all affirm it. The data establish only the weak thesis. The article needs the strong thesis (the *phenomenal* character does causal work irreducible to its physical realiser), and nothing in placebo or choking isolates that. The article half-sees this — its repeated retreat to "the felt quality tracks content precisely, and why is *that* accompanied by experience?" is really just a restatement of the hard problem, not new causal evidence.
- **Choking does not cut against epiphenomenalism — the article's central philosophical claim is invalid.** The article argues: "If attention were epiphenomenal, why would those states produce *worse* performance precisely when accompanied by the phenomenology of self-monitoring? You cannot confabulate interference." This is a non-sequitur. The epiphenomenalist holds that *physical* brain processes cause behaviour and that phenomenal feel is a causally inert by-product. Choking is standardly explained by a *physical/functional* sub-process — the engagement of explicit, working-memory-mediated, declarative control circuitry interfering with proceduralised motor circuitry (reinvestment; constrained-action hypothesis; Wu's "attention as selection for action," a deliberately functional notion that he keeps separable from consciousness). That one physical sub-process (monitoring) degrades another (automatised execution) is *exactly* what physicalism and epiphenomenalism predict. The disruption tracks the *functional mode* of attention, not demonstrably its phenomenal character. The "you cannot confabulate interference" point shows only that the interference is real, not that its cause is non-physical or phenomenal. So choking is fully consistent with epiphenomenalism and does not discriminate. [e-space](https://e-space.mmu.ac.uk/630489/3/Conscious%20motor%20processing%20and%20the%20pressure-performance%20relationship-%20A%20systematic%20review%20(minor%20revisions)%20final%20version%20(with%20tables).pdf)[PhilPapers](https://philpapers.org/rec/WUAAS)
- **The "two-sided / matched-grain" symmetry is rhetorical, not evidential.** Placebo (mind→body, expectancy improves outcome) and choking (mind→body, monitoring degrades outcome) are presented as mutually reinforcing because they operate "across substrates that share no common machinery." But both reduce to the same schema: *physical brain process → physical bodily outcome.* The "different substrates" point increases the *breadth* of physical psychoneural coupling, not its *depth* against physicalism; a physicalist expects expectancy circuits to modulate many downstream systems. The conjunction raises the "cost" only if one already assumes brute psychophysical law is the only physicalist option — which ignores that the standard physicalist account (functional realisation + predictive processing) is not brute at all.
- **Epistemic-to-metaphysical slide.** The article slides from "the mental description is predictive/useful and tracks outcomes finely" (epistemic) to "consciousness *does* something" non-physical (metaphysical). It partly inoculates against this with the evidential-status-discipline hedge, but the hedge then coexists with section headings ("The Interface Revealed," "consciousness *withdraws* from execution") that assert the metaphysical reading as established architecture.
- **The exclusion argument is acknowledged but not answered *by the phenomena*.** The article correctly identifies Kim's exclusion argument and pivots to Lowe's agent causation as the escape. But this concedes the key point: placebo and choking do *nothing* against exclusion, because they do not exhibit overdetermination-free non-physical causation. The metaphysical work is done entirely by the separate agent-causation and Minimal-Quantum-Interaction machinery, not by the empirical phenomena. The phenomena are therefore evidentially inert with respect to the actual obstacle (exclusion); they establish at most an *explanandum* the article already concedes is "compatible with various mechanisms." [Philopedia](https://philopedia.org/arguments/causal-exclusion-argument/)

## (iii) Tenet Coherence

- **Constitutional-attractor effect is visible.** The article reads its five-tenet framework (interactionist dualism, Minimal Quantum Interaction, Bidirectional Interaction, No Many Worlds, bounded Occam) back into phenomena that are antecedently neutral. The "two-sided" placebo/choking framing maps suspiciously cleanly onto the Bidirectional-Interaction tenet ("positive direction" / "same channel in reverse"), and the "interface friction" gloss on choking is a tenet-derived interpretation presented as if read off the data. The data do not contain a "channel" or an "interface"; those are imported. The changelog confirms this is a designed feature: a 2026-05-28 cross-review inserted a "choking phenomenon" paragraph into the *interface-friction* concept article to reciprocally link the two — i.e., the phenomenon is being recruited to illustrate the architecture, not to test it.
- **To its credit, the article's calibration discipline is real.** It repeatedly states the phenomena "constrain rather than establish," names the framework-boundary disagreement, and concedes "future neuroscience could rewrite the picture." This is better epistemic hygiene than most of the corpus. But calibration honesty does not rescue an argument whose evidential contribution, once the hedges are honoured, rounds to zero. If each phenomenon merely "raises the cost" of an absorbing account without discriminating, and the absorbing account (predictive processing) is independently elegant and non-brute, then the cost raised is negligible.
- **Internal tension with the project's own predictive-processing article.** The Map's *predictive-processing-and-dualism* article concedes that PP "is not itself a theory of consciousness," adopts the PP architecture, and explicitly treats placebo as "expectations — strong priors in PP's framework — produc[ing] measurable physiological outcomes." That is a fully physicalist mechanism for placebo. The mental-causation article leans on that same predictive-processing framing ("expectations function as strong priors") while simultaneously claiming placebo is evidence *against* the epiphenomenalist/physicalist reading. The project cannot both adopt predictive processing as the physical mechanism of placebo and cite placebo as evidence that a physical mechanism is insufficient.

## (v) Strongest Unaddressed Rival

The single strongest rival, unaddressed by name, is the **Bayesian predictive-processing / active-inference account of placebo** (Büchel et al. 2014; Ongaro & Kaptchuk 2019; Wager & Atlas 2015). On this account, expectation is a high-precision prior; the descending pain-modulatory system implements predictive coding; "belief content" is just the content of a prior probability distribution physically encoded in cortical–brainstem circuitry; and the physiological outcome is the brain's inference minimising precision-weighted prediction error. This account predicts the entire pattern the article cites — content-specificity, nocebo, conditioning/expectancy interaction, the self-report/objective gradient — with **no remainder** requiring non-physical causation. If placebo is fully predicted by a physicalist predictive-processing model, it is by definition non-discriminating between dualism and physicalism, and the "constrain-vs-establish gate" closes against the article: a phenomenon equally predicted by both theories constrains neither. [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0896627314001925)

For choking, the parallel unaddressed rival is the **reinvestment / working-memory / functional-attention cluster** (Masters & Maxwell 2008; Wulf et al. 2001 constrained-action hypothesis; Wu's "attention as selection for action," a functional notion explicitly separable from phenomenal consciousness; and the 2023 predictive-processing reframing). Reinvestment is defined as "the manipulation of conscious, explicit, rule-based knowledge, by working memory, to control the mechanics of one's movements" — a purely functional construct. Each account explains choking as one physical/functional control mode displacing another, with no appeal to qualia. The article cites Beilock & Carr in passing as if the explicit-monitoring finding *supports* a phenomenal reading, but the very theory it cites is functional. Montero's empirical-deflation critique compounds this: the lab "experts" are often not genuine experts, the effects are small and ecologically fragile, and the causal direction is disputed.

## Bottom-Line Verdict

**REVISE-HARD now; if the revision cannot produce a genuinely discriminating argument — which I doubt it can, because the phenomena are non-discriminating in principle — then DEMOTE-TO-COHERENCE-ONLY.** The article should be explicitly reclassified from "empirical evidence/case for mental causation" to "an illustration of how the Bidirectional-Interaction tenet would interpret two well-known phenomena, with the explicit caveat that the phenomena are equally well explained by physicalist predictive-processing and functional-attention accounts and therefore provide no evidential discrimination." The title overclaims and should change. The placebo/choking conjunction is best understood as **PERPETUALLY CONTESTED** in the precise sense that it cannot, even in principle, adjudicate the dispute it is enlisted to settle — and the article should say so in its own voice rather than burying it in hedges. I do not recommend DELETE/MERGE: the topic is legitimate and the writing is careful; but a merge of the choking material into *interface-friction* (its natural home, per the changelog cross-link) and the placebo material into *predictive-processing-and-dualism* or *clinical-neuroplasticity-evidence-for-bidirectional-causation* is a defensible alternative to a standalone "empirical case" article.

That paragraph inherits the same invalid "phenomenal efficacy" inference and should carry the same functional-deflation caveat.

## (B) Site-Wide Methodology Improvements

1. **Add a "discrimination test" gate to the review workflow.** The corpus already enforces an "evidential-status-discipline" and a "constrain-vs-establish" distinction, but it does not enforce the prior question: *is this phenomenon predicted equally well by the leading physicalist rival?* If yes, the phenomenon discriminates nothing and must be labelled non-evidential, not merely "constraining." Placebo under predictive coding is the paradigm case. Make "names and engages the strongest physicalist mechanism by name" a hard pass/fail criterion — the current process catches citation-metadata errors with great rigour but lets through "performative inoculation" (gesturing at rivals via wikilink without engaging their argument).
2. **Audit for the weak/strong mental-causation equivocation corpus-wide.** Multiple articles (this one, *epiphenomenalism*, *clinical-neuroplasticity-evidence-for-bidirectional-causation*, *consciousness-as-amplifier*, *contemplative-practice-as-philosophical-evidence*) appear to recruit phenomena where folk-psychological/representational causation (weak, physicalist-compatible) is silently upgraded to non-physical phenomenal causation (strong). A targeted review pass keyed to this slide would likely find it is systematic.
3. **Distinguish "functional/access consciousness" from "phenomenal consciousness" as a standing reviewer check.** The choking error generalises: wherever the corpus infers phenomenal efficacy from a phenomenon driven by *attention/monitoring/effort as a functional process*, it commits the same fallacy. A reviewer prompt should ask, for any "consciousness does X" claim, whether X is carried by access/functional consciousness (physicalist-safe) or genuinely by phenomenal character.
4. **Apply a recency gate to empirical fields that have moved.** The citation apparatus verifies that cited works are real, but does not check that the *field's strongest current rival* is cited. Placebo (predictive coding, 2014+; circuit-level, 2024) and choking (reinvestment 2008; PP reframing 2023) both show foundational-but-stale citation profiles that omit the threatening recent work. Add "is the strongest post-2015 rival account cited?" to the deep-review checklist.
5. **Guard against the constitutional-attractor effect explicitly.** Because the five tenets are read back into neutral data (the "channel," "interface," "bidirectional" framings), reviewers should be required to ask whether a phenomenon's interpretation is *imported from the tenets* or *forced by the data*, and to label tenet-derived structure as interpretation. The reciprocal cross-linking workflow (e.g., choking ↔ interface-friction) amplifies this attractor and should itself be flagged as a coherence-manufacturing risk, not just a navigation aid.
6. **Treat "raises the cost without discriminating" as a coherence claim, not an evidential one.** The corpus's recurring "raises the cost of the absorbing account" move is not evidence for dualism unless the cost is shown to be asymmetric against a fully-specified rival. Reviewers should require that any "cost" claim specify the rival's actual commitment and show the cost is not equally borne by the dualist (who owes brute psychophysical laws of their own).
