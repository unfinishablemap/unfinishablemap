## Executive Summary

The four articles share one template — expound a reductive/non-dualist rival, then in "Relation to Site Perspective" deploy the Map's signature **relocation objection** (the explanatory gap re-appears; the rival re-describes rather than explains) plus a framework-relative resistance. Citation metadata is clean and the framework-boundary marking is mostly honest (self-model-theory Move 1 is a model of the discipline). The recurring weakness is an **exclusion-symmetry / tu-quoque class** — the same class the prior 2026-07-11 core-tenet review found in `argument-from-reason.md` (Finding 3, now fixed): each article wields a criterion (relocation of the gap; "adds nothing causally = epiphenomenal"; conceivable-absence = distinct property) that, applied evenhandedly, presses on the Map's own account, but presents it as a rival-specific defeat. Two per-article internal tensions (biological-naturalism's dilemma horn 1; first-order-representationalism's transparency/mental-paint clash) are also flagged.

## Critical Issues

### Issue 1: The relocation objection is deployed as a rival-specific defeater, but its criterion does not discriminate in the Map's favor (cross-article)
- **File**: all four; sharpest at `first-order-representationalism.md` (line 84, 88), `recurrent-processing-theory.md` (line 64), `self-model-theory-of-subjectivity.md` (line 56), `biological-naturalism.md` (line 87)
- **Location**: each "Relation to Site Perspective" section
- **Problem**: The load-bearing resistance move in all four is: identifying feel with X "does not close the explanatory gap; it relocates it" — read as a *mark against* the rival ("a re-description of the mystery rather than an explanation of it," first-order line 84). But the Map's own answer to "why does X feel like anything?" is to relocate the felt character to the non-physical side and postulate its irreducibility (self-model line 56: "this is where a non-physical contribution does work the functional story leaves untouched"; first-order line 88: "the irreducibility Dualism takes as its starting point"). By the objection's own criterion — *does it close, or merely relocate, the gap?* — dualist coupling also relocates rather than closes. The Map has a genuine reply available (a *postulate* is dialectically different from a *failed reduction*; the Map's explanatory debt is "why posit the non-physical / why does coupling feel like anything," a different debt, not no debt). But none of the four articles states this asymmetry; each lets the relocation objection read as though it selectively damages the rival while leaving the Map's account untouched. A Dennett/Churchland critic answers: "postulating irreducibility is not superior to failing to reduce it — you have relocated the mystery too, and simply declined to be embarrassed by it."
- **Severity**: Medium (High as a *pattern* — it is the corpus's most-repeated move)
- **VERIFIED / SPECULATIVE**: VERIFIED (the symmetry is textual; what is under-flagged is real). The severity is tempered because the Map's postulate-vs-failed-reduction defense is *available* — it is just never made explicit as the reason the objection does not boomerang.
- **Recommendation** (routing to orchestrator): one honest sentence per article, or a single shared clause, naming what the Map actually relies on — that it treats irreducibility as a *starting commitment* (Tenet 1) rather than a claimed explanation, so the relocation objection marks a *promise the rival makes and breaks*, not an explanatory advantage the Map has earned. Do not delete the objection; earn the asymmetry it silently assumes.

### Issue 2: `biological-naturalism.md` horn 1 commits the epistemic→metaphysical slide the article itself concedes is the contested point
- **File**: [concepts/biological-naturalism.md](/concepts/biological-naturalism/)
- **Location**: line 85 (Horn one) against line 81 (steelman)
- **Problem**: Horn 1's trigger is stated as: "If the first-person feature is not fixed by, and not deducible from, the complete third-person neural description — if it is conceivable that the neural facts hold while the phenomenal feature varies or is absent — then it is a further fact, a distinct property, whatever it is called." That is a slide from an *epistemic* premise (non-deducibility / conceivable absence — a limit on what the third-person description delivers) to a *metaphysical* conclusion (a distinct property). This is exactly the conceivability-to-metaphysics inference Searle denies, and it is the epistemic/metaphysical equivocation the pessimistic-review skill's own Audit flags. The article *concedes* at line 81 that "whether 'a physical feature with an irreducible first-person ontology' is really distinct from 'a non-physical property' is precisely the contested point, and the Map does not claim Searle is simply confused" — then eight lines later runs horn 1 *as if that inference were settled* ("then it is a further fact, a distinct property, whatever it is called"). The dilemma's force depends on a premise the same article admits is contested. Against Searle specifically this begs the question: his entire position is that ontological (definitional/epistemic) irreducibility does not entail a distinct property.
- **Severity**: Medium
- **VERIFIED / SPECULATIVE**: VERIFIED — internal inconsistency between line 81 and line 85.
- **Recommendation**: horn 1 should be conditionalized on the disputed inference rather than asserting it — e.g. "*if* conceivable independent variation is granted to carry ontological weight (the move Searle resists), then..." — so the dilemma is honestly a *conditional* pressure, matching the line-81 concession, not a covert assertion that the conceivability argument succeeds.

### Issue 3: `biological-naturalism.md` wields causal-exclusion against Searle while asserting — not showing — that the Map's own mechanism escapes it (fresh instance of the exclusion-symmetry class)
- **File**: [concepts/biological-naturalism.md](/concepts/biological-naturalism/)
- **Location**: line 87 (horn two: "a feature that adds nothing causally to the neurobiology is doing no work") and line 89 ("its own interactionist dualism ... takes the causal-exclusion seam seriously rather than defining it away")
- **Problem**: Horn 2 convicts Searle of epiphenomenalism via "adds nothing causally = does no work." The Map then claims its interactionist dualism "takes the causal-exclusion seam seriously rather than defining it away." But the Map's *preferred* minimal-interaction / corridor reading faces the structurally identical ensemble-level-epiphenomenalism worry the prior 2026-07-11 core-tenet review identified for `argument-from-reason.md` (Finding 3): consciousness's token-selection averages to the Born measure and "adds nothing at the aggregate level." So the very charge that sinks Searle's horn 2 recurs against the Map's own mechanism. "Takes the seam seriously rather than defining it away" is *asserted* here, not shown — and the corpus elsewhere (born-rule / ensemble-level-epiphenomenalism) holds that exact question *open*. This is a new occurrence in a different file from the resolved Finding 3, so it is not a re-raise; it is the same class recurring where the fix has not propagated.
- **Severity**: Medium
- **VERIFIED / SPECULATIVE**: VERIFIED that the claim is asserted-not-shown and that the symmetric worry exists; whether the corridor reading ultimately escapes it is the genuinely-open question, which is the point — the article should not present it as settled.
- **Recommendation**: soften line 89 from "takes the seam seriously rather than defining it away" (which implies the Map has *solved* what it charges Searle with dodging) to language conceding the Map faces its *own* live exclusion worry (ensemble-invisible selection vs. a Born distribution that already fixes the statistics), cross-linking `ensemble-level-epiphenomenalism`. Mirror of the fix already applied to `argument-from-reason.md`.

### Issue 4: `first-order-representationalism.md` both leans on Block's "mental paint" and "accepts transparency in full" — opposed commitments left unresolved
- **File**: [concepts/first-order-representationalism.md](/concepts/first-order-representationalism/)
- **Location**: line 76/78 (mental paint = introspectable *intrinsic, non-representational* features) vs. line 86 ("The Map can accept the transparency datum in full")
- **Problem**: The transparency datum (Harman/Tye) just *is* the claim that introspection finds *only* worldly represented properties and *no* intrinsic non-representational features. Block's mental paint is the direct denial: experience *has* introspectable intrinsic non-representational features. The article presents mental paint as pressure on representationalism (dialectical sympathy) yet then has the Map "accept the transparency datum in full" — i.e. side with Tye's phenomenology against Block's. It cannot have both: welcoming mental paint (intrinsic features are there) while accepting transparency in full (they are not findable). The article distances from Block on *authorship* grounds ("Block is himself a physicalist ... dialectical pressure rather than ... dualist allies," line 78) but never resolves the *phenomenological* conflict between the two data it invokes. A reader is left thinking the Map has it both ways.
- **Severity**: Medium
- **VERIFIED / SPECULATIVE**: VERIFIED — textual opposition between the cited datum and the endorsed datum.
- **Recommendation**: pick a side explicitly. The dualist "acquaintance directed at worldly properties" reading (line 86) is compatible with accepting transparency *and* denying reduction, but then the Map should not also lean on mental-paint as congenial pressure — or, if it keeps mental paint, it should say the transparency it accepts is Tye's *introspective-availability* claim, not the stronger claim that there is no intrinsic phenomenal character at all. One clarifying clause fixes it.

### Recurrent loops as "coupling site" is empirically idle relative to RPT (Occam turned against the Map)
- **File**: `recurrent-processing-theory.md`, line 66, 70
- **Current content says**: re-entrant loops are "a natural candidate for the physical locus of that coupling" (flagged as Map speculation); and RPT's economy is "premature" under Tenet 5 (line 70).
- **A critic (Popper/Tegmark) would argue**: the Map's reinterpretation predicts *exactly* the same neural correlate as RPT — recurrent loops — while adding an undetectable non-physical coupling. It is empirically equivalent to RPT and so the dualist add-on carries no empirical weight; invoking Tenet 5 ("premature economy") against RPT while adding a strictly *more* ontologically costly, empirically idle coupling is Occam pressure pointing the other way.
- **Suggested response**: the speculation *is* honestly flagged as speculation in-article (line 66), which blunts the charge — but the Tenet-5 paragraph (line 70) should acknowledge that the Map's own coupling-site move buys no *empirical* discriminability over RPT and rests on the prior tenets, not on a distinguishing prediction. Severity: Low–Medium; SPECULATIVE (dialectical, not a factual error).
