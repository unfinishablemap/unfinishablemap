The rival-metaphysics cluster is some of the Map's most disciplined writing: each article steelmans a rival that could, if it succeeded, dissolve the Map's whole project, then declines to declare victory. All three explicitly separate "the Map judges this the better bet" from "the rival has been refuted," which is exactly the calibration posture Tenet 5 demands. ## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The cluster keeps the hard problem load-bearing without over-claiming. `russellian-monism.md` reproduces Chalmers' own panpsychism/panprotopsychism distinction faithfully, including the "protophenomenal zombies" revenge argument he raises against his own preferred variant — a sign the article is arguing with the literature rather than around it. The "What Russellian Monism Gets Right" section grants the conceivability argument its exact scope ("zombies are conceivable with respect to *structural* physics… deny zombies are conceivable with respect to *complete* physics"), which is the honest way to hold the anti-physicalist line.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
`four-category-ontology.md`'s "deliberate fork" is the cluster's sharpest Stapp-friendly moment: it stages Lowe's no-physics-gap fact-causation against the Map's minimal-quantum bias and makes the crux question explicit — *does the mental make any difference to the physical event-chain?* Lowe says no; the Map says yes, but only "a minimal, statistically undetectable one at indeterministic points." That is the interactionist wager stated without inflation, located precisely where physics is already indeterministic.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
`hylomorphic-dualism-and-the-interaction-problem.md` centres the first-person stakes correctly by grounding Thomistic immateriality in the *operation* of intellection ("grasping universal concepts is not the act of any bodily organ") rather than in a bare metaphysical posit. The article resists the temptation to flatten three very different theories into one, which preserves the phenomenological texture that separates near-physicalist structure hylomorphism from subsistent-soul Thomism.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The cluster avoids crude substance dualism precisely by *naming* where it leans substance and why. `four-category-ontology.md`'s framework-boundary qualification — "NCSD is a congenial option, not the Map's mandatory commitment… the Dualism tenet is officially neutral between substance and property dualism" — is the anti-crude-dualism move done explicitly. `hylomorphic-dualism.md` even lists process philosophy among the Map's other engagements with non-Cartesian metaphysics, keeping the door open to experience-inclusive readings without collapsing into them.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
`four-category-ontology.md` renders Lowe's two-way power cleanly: volition as "a 'spontaneous' power" exercisable rationally, with "unmoved movers" glossed honestly as *uncaused by prior events* while still agent-caused. The article then refuses to let the framework overreach — it flags that the four-category machinery "tells us *what a volition is metaphysically*, not whether the [Libet] timing evidence undercuts its spontaneity," and hands the empirical question off to the agency-and-will positions. Agency taken seriously; the timing objection not swept under the rug.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
Epistemic humility is structural here, not decorative. Every article states the dialectical situation plainly: hylomorphic dissolution "has not been refuted"; the Map "does not get to declare it settled"; "by the Map's own fifth tenet the Map cannot claim its framework is simply *simpler* and therefore true." `russellian-monism.md` frames its own greater falsifiability as "more testable, not thereby more likely." This is the mysterian virtue — knowing what the arguments do and don't establish — applied consistently.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This is the cluster's strongest suit, and the reason the two-persona tension resolves rather than fires. **The tenet-as-evidence-upgrade move is praise-worthily *not* done anywhere in the cluster.** Each article had the structural opportunity to let tenet-coherence do load-bearing work and explicitly declined:

- `four-category-ontology.md` states outright that the four-category ontology is "a **metaphysical framework the Map leans on**, not empirical evidence for dualism," and that it is "scaffolding for locating consciousness, not an argument that consciousness exists." Framework-coherence is claimed at the framework level and nowhere cashed as evidence.
- `hylomorphic-dualism.md` classifies its own preference for location over dissolution as "a **bet** about which unanswered question is the load-bearing one," and concedes "the dispute is not framework-neutral" — the honest *"tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating"* register.
- `russellian-monism.md` is the model case: it takes its *own* greater falsifiability and refuses to read it as a truth-claim ("a more falsifiable theory is more testable, not thereby more likely"), and its decoherence-evidence paragraph is exemplary calibration — Kerskens & López Pérez (2022) explicitly flagged as challenged (Warren 2023) and unconfirmed, Khan et al. (2024) tagged "circumstantial support… rather than a measurement of quantum robustness." These "soften the objection without settling it" — register-discipline of exactly the kind this persona exists to reward.

No minimal-organism / boundary-case tier-slippage appears anywhere in this cluster (it is not that kind of content), so the Process-Philosopher vs Hardline-Empiricist personas do not conflict here — they converge in praising the same framework-vs-evidence firewall from opposite ends. That convergence is the tell that the cluster has resolved the tension honestly.

## Content Strengths

### concepts/four-category-ontology.md
- **Strongest point**: The "deliberate fork" section — it uses a rival (Lowe's no-gap fact-causation) as the sharpest available foil for stating *what* the Map's minimal-interaction commitment adds and *why* it pays an empirical cost Lowe avoids. Rival-as-instrument, not rival-as-strawman.
- **Notable quote**: "The Map takes its branch because it wants consciousness to be a token-level *difference-maker* to the physical world, not only a determiner of facts about kinds—and because a purely structuring cause can look uncomfortably close to the epiphenomenalism the Map rejects."
- **Why it works**: It converts a metaphysics-literature distinction (fact- vs event-causation) into a precise statement of the Map's own load-bearing choice, and openly books the cost (a "genuine testability commitment") rather than presenting the choice as free.

### topics/hylomorphic-dualism-and-the-interaction-problem.md
- **Strongest point**: The three-way disaggregation of "hylomorphism" (structure / Thomistic / emergent) with an explicit ranking of fit to the Map, including the concession that structure hylomorphism "concedes closure" and so cannot deliver what the tenets require. It refuses the easy rhetorical win of treating one label as one rival.
- **Notable quote**: "Dissolution is the *rival's* move, and if it succeeds it is a cost to the Map, not a win."
- **Why it works**: The article inverts the usual apologetic reflex — it makes the rival's success a cost to itself and states the Map's reason for preferring location as a *judgment*, not a proof. The "substance connection" subsection then finds a genuine ally-relation (Thomistic subsistence supplies the persisting subject) while scoping the borrowing carefully.

### concepts/russellian-monism.md
- **Strongest point**: The "family of incompatible proposals" section, which turns fragmentation into an epistemic argument (a framework compatible with everything predicts nothing) and immediately disclaims the win as "a methodological contrast, not evidence that the Map is true."
- **Notable quote**: "By taking definite positions where Russellian monism stays neutral, it exposes itself to disconfirmation that an interpretation-neutral framework avoids. This is a methodological contrast, not evidence that the Map is true—a more falsifiable theory is more testable, not thereby more likely."
- **Why it works**: It runs three independent recent-literature attacks (Pautz parity, Cutter instability, Howell/Robinson epiphenomenalism-return) and lets the No-Many-Worlds tenet do the divergence work, all while keeping the falsifiability-vs-truth firewall intact.
