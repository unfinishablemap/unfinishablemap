# Full-site audit of The Unfinishable Map

## Scope and headline verdict

I treated **The Unfinishable Map** as a live, cross-linked research system rather than as a set of isolated essays. I reviewed the live homepage, tenets, topics, concepts, positions register, project/workflow pages, changelog, testability and causal-budget ledgers, and several high-load articles in the quantum-interface, agency, voids, filter-theory, FND, AI-consciousness, composition, and empirical-neuroscience clusters. I also checked external sources for several empirical claims.

The site is unusually self-aware for an AI-assisted philosophy project. It explicitly states its five core commitments—dualism, minimal quantum interaction, bidirectional causation, rejection of Many-Worlds, and limits on Occam-style simplicity—and it foregrounds the fact that the project is human-directed but heavily AI-assisted. It also has a real methodology stack: changelog, review pages, tenet checks, calibration audits, mechanism-cost and causal-budget ledgers, and explicit concern about “coherence inflation.” [The Unfinishable Map+2The Unfinishable Map+2](https://unfinishablemap.org/)

The hostile verdict is this: **the Map is currently much better at producing disciplined, internally coherent philosophical synthesis than at proving that its central causal interface exists.** Its most important live vulnerability is not dualism in general. It is the narrow claim that consciousness can perform **Born-preserving, post-decoherence, token-level selection** while remaining empirically indistinguishable from chance and yet still count as genuine causal influence. The Map knows this is the crux: the quantum positions register says bias-without-deviation is the strongest challenge and “under-confronted,” and also says there is no worked toy model of the proposed mechanism. [positions/quantum-interface](https://unfinishablemap.org/positions/quantum-interface/)

The changelog matters. Recent work has repaired reciprocal cross-links, coalesced redundant composition pages, fixed stale or misattributed citations, and run a tenet check across 535 files with zero reported errors but some warnings/notes. That is a strength. It is also evidence that the site’s scale has outpaced its control surface: multiple important errors were found only after repeated review passes, including a wrong-paper/wrong-year Mørch citation and a fabricated or misleading COGITATE gloss. [workflow/changelog](https://unfinishablemap.org/workflow/changelog/)

## 1. Novel insights and inferences the site should make explicit

### 1. The Map implies a **content-confinement theorem** for agency

The causal-budget ledger already says the interface cannot select content the brain has not generated: consciousness may select only among candidate states prepared by the physical system. That has a major consequence for free will, creativity, mathematical insight, moral responsibility, Ramanujan-style inspiration, and AI comparison: **agency cannot be treated as ex nihilo authorship. It is authorship over a pre-structured option space.** [project/causal-budget-ledger](https://unfinishablemap.org/project/causal-budget-ledger/)

That inference should be made explicit across the agency cluster. The strongest Map-compatible claim is not “the conscious agent originates arbitrary new content.” It is: **the brain generates a live menu; consciousness may author which prepared possibility becomes actual.** This is philosophically cleaner, but it reduces the grandeur of some downstream agency rhetoric.

### 2. The rejection of Many-Worlds is not independent of the subject theory

Tenet 4 rejects Many-Worlds partly because it cannot preserve the lived indexical fact that *this* outcome happened *to me*. But the tenets page itself notes that this objection presupposes a non-deflationary account of identity and leaves a no-self route open. The agency cluster also leans toward substance/agent causation because libertarian authorship needs a persisting subject. [tenets](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)

So the Map should state a dependency: **No-MWI is not merely a physics tenet; it inherits load from the Map’s theory of subjecthood.** If the subject is deflated, fragmented, or process-like, the anti-MWI indexical argument weakens. That does not refute Tenet 4, but it means Tenet 4 should not be presented as standing on physics alone.

### 3. Filter theory is functioning as a sixth quasi-tenet

The homepage lists five tenets. Yet the testability ledger gives “Filter Theory” its own claim, disconfirmers, weak evidence, and update conditions. That makes filter theory operationally tenet-like even if it is not formally listed as a tenet. [The Unfinishable Map+1](https://unfinishablemap.org/)

This creates a governance problem: **is filter theory a core commitment, a derived position, or a local interpretive model?** The site should either promote it to the positions register or demote it in the testability ledger. Leaving it halfway makes it too easy for filter-theory material to inherit tenet-level authority without being tenet-governed.

### 4. The Map’s “self-concealing interface” creates an evidence-asymmetry trap

The quantum positions register says the preferred interface is self-concealing: absence of a direct signature is not strongly disconfirming because the mechanism is designed to preserve Born statistics. But it also says the absence of a toy model remains a serious vulnerability. [positions/quantum-interface](https://unfinishablemap.org/positions/quantum-interface/)

The risk is a one-way evidential ratchet: non-detection is expected, while any suggestive anomaly is counted as support. The Map already has the tools to prevent this, but it should formalize the principle: **a self-concealing mechanism may be permitted as a metaphysical position, but it earns no evidential credit from remaining hidden.**

### 5. The AI-consciousness material implies a useful but underdeveloped distinction: AI as possible subject vs AI as introspective control system

The topics index describes cross-architecture LLM introspection as a voids-channel and explicitly brackets AI consciousness. The Claude Constitution page treats model welfare language as significant but uncertain. [topics](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/)

The underdeveloped inference is: **LLMs may be most useful to the Map not because they are likely conscious, but because they are unusually inspectable non-human cognitive systems.** That makes them controls for human introspection, not merely candidate moral patients. The site should separate these two functions more sharply: AI-as-subject and AI-as-instrument should not be allowed to cross-subsidize one another.

### 6. The causal-budget ledger should govern applied ethics, not only quantum mechanics

The causal-budget ledger requires each interaction claim to specify candidate sets, bandwidth, amplification path, signature, falsifier, and nearest physicalist explanation. That discipline is framed mainly around MQI, but it also applies to pain, placebo, meditation, hypnosis, FND, AI consciousness, moral responsibility, and animal consciousness. [project/causal-budget-ledger](https://unfinishablemap.org/project/causal-budget-ledger/)

The hidden implication is: **every applied article that says consciousness “does work” must spend from the same scarce causal budget.** At present, the budget discipline exists, but it is not yet enforced as a visible dependency graph across applied articles.

## 2. Structural weaknesses

### 1. The positions register is too small for the corpus

The positions page was created to make the Map’s claims explicit and decision-usable, and it names future domains such as consciousness scope, methodology/calibration, and applied verdicts. But the live register presently concentrates on quantum interface, agency/will, and voids. The changelog says the tenet checker inspected 535 files: 268 topics, 263 concepts, and only 4 positions. [positions](https://unfinishablemap.org/positions/)

That ratio is a structural weakness. The site has many articles that behave as though they express settled Map positions, but only a small subset of those positions are registered with confidence levels, objections, and update conditions.

### 2. The central mechanism is under-specified relative to downstream ambition

The Map’s most important causal move is post-decoherence selection: consciousness allegedly selects among outcomes without changing Born statistics. The positions register acknowledges that this has no worked toy model and that bias-without-deviation is an active unresolved challenge. [positions/quantum-interface](https://unfinishablemap.org/positions/quantum-interface/)

This should constrain the entire site. Articles about agency, value-sensitive selection, motor selection, placebo, FND, and consciousness doing causal work should carry an explicit “mechanism debt” box pointing back to P-Q3 and P-Q10. Otherwise the corpus can appear more confident downstream than it is upstream.

### 3. Cross-linking is improving, but it is still reactive rather than architectural

The changelog shows recent reciprocal backlink repairs between mine-ness and agency, repairs between composition-question pages and panpsychism/combination-problem pages, and a coalescing of three composition-rivals pages into one. These are good fixes, but they show that important conceptual dependencies are often discovered after the fact. [workflow/changelog](https://unfinishablemap.org/workflow/changelog/)

A hostile referee would say: the Map has a large link graph, but not yet a sufficiently explicit **argument graph**. Links connect articles; they do not always expose which claim depends on which tenet, position, empirical study, or methodological convention.

### 4. The review process is transparent but not yet citation-complete

The project page says the site has adopted a canonical empirical data card convention and an orphan-citation check, but it also notes that the current validator checks frontmatter only and that the orphan check is a code candidate. The same page reports that the process found a fabricated COGITATE “split-win” gloss and a missing Papineau/Balog reference. [project](https://unfinishablemap.org/project/)

That is exactly the right kind of self-criticism. But until inline citations are mechanically mapped to reference entries and high-load empirical claims are canonicalized, the site remains vulnerable to polished but wrong citation behavior.

### 5. Post-publication supervision is honest, but it leaves live error windows

The human-supervision page says humans control direction, priorities, and tenets, but individual article publication is not human-gated; the site trades pre-publication gating for post-publication inspectability. It also says review cycles may use external AI systems and that `last_curated` may remain null. [project/human-supervision](https://unfinishablemap.org/project/human-supervision/)

That is a legitimate publication model, but it should be named more bluntly: **new articles are provisional until curated.** A user landing from search may not understand that the presence of a polished page does not imply human pre-publication verification.

### 6. Redundancy is being removed, but the generator still creates bloat

The changelog records a Duhem–Quine deep review that fixed post-refine bloat, and a composition-cluster consolidation that reduced three overlapping pages into one. [workflow/changelog](https://unfinishablemap.org/workflow/changelog/)

The site’s own workflow can create a failure mode: when a concept is important, the system tends to write another article rather than update a canonical node. The recent coalescing work is evidence of improvement, but also evidence of a recurring structural pressure.

## 3. Tenet-coherence issues and cross-cluster tensions

### 1. Minimal Quantum Interaction vs Bidirectional Interaction

Tenet 2 says consciousness acts only at minimal quantum openings and may be empirically indistinguishable from chance. Tenet 3 says consciousness and the physical world interact bidirectionally. [tenets](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)

The tension: **if the physical statistics are exactly preserved and no observer can distinguish selected from unselected outcomes, what non-verbal work is “causal influence” doing?** The Map’s answer is that consciousness selects token outcomes while preserving aggregate statistics. That is coherent as metaphysics, but it is dangerously close to epiphenomenalism unless the site supplies a toy model or a sharper account of token causation.

### 2. Neutral dualism vs substance-leaning agency

The tenets page says dualism is neutral between substance and property dualism, but also admits that the agency cluster leans substance-ward because agent causation needs a persisting subject. [tenets](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)

This is acceptable only if articles that require a persisting subject explicitly mark that extra load. Some articles can run on irreducibility alone; others require a robust agent. The site should prevent “dualism” from silently meaning “substance dualism” in agency-heavy contexts.

### 3. No-MWI depends on identity metaphysics

The No-Many-Worlds tenet is not simply “MWI is false.” It is “MWI fails to preserve the fact that this subject experienced this outcome.” But the tenets page itself concedes that a Madhyamaka/no-self route remains open. [tenets](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)

So the cross-cluster problem is this: the quantum cluster sometimes treats No-MWI as a physics constraint, while the agency/self cluster reveals it as partly an identity constraint. The site should state that dependency in the No-MWI page and in the positions register.

### 4. Forward-in-time selection vs time-symmetric selection

The quantum positions register prefers post-decoherence, forward-in-time selection. The agency register says the atemporal fallback has been subordinated to the forward mechanism after harmonization. [positions/quantum-interface](https://unfinishablemap.org/positions/quantum-interface/)

Any older topic article that still presents time-symmetric or atemporal selection as co-equal should be updated. The live position is now hierarchical: forward mechanism first, atemporal fallback only subordinate.

### 5. Occam humility vs “preferred path” rhetoric

Tenet 5 rightly says parsimony cannot decide the issue by itself and that both physicalism and dualism can face self-binding costs. [tenets](https://unfinishablemap.org/tenets/)

But some quantum-interface rhetoric risks sliding from “this route is less costly within the Map’s current constraints” to “this route is therefore preferred simpliciter.” That should be policed. The Map’s own best formulation is: **post-decoherence selection is preferred relative to the current ledger, not established by parsimony.**

### 6. FND/conversion disorder is calibrated in the article but over-compressed in summaries

The full conversion-disorder article does a better job than the topic snippet: it explicitly says the robust finding is substrate-intact decoupling, while the interface reading is a further, contestable step. [topics/conversion-disorder-as-consciousness-side-fault](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/conversion-disorder-as-consciousness-side-fault/)

But the shorter topic summary says FND is the “cleanest consciousness-side-fault exhibit” and that the motor system executes under anesthesia or hypnosis while consciousness cannot issue commands. That language is too strong unless it carries the two-tier discount every time. Modern clinical literature treats FND as a positive, rule-in, neuropsychiatric diagnosis involving attention-driven dysfunction, automatic movement, comorbidities, and heterogeneous mechanisms—not as a clean proof of a consciousness-to-brain interface. [topics/clinical-dissociation-as-systematic-evidence](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/clinical-dissociation-as-systematic-evidence/)

## 4. Empirical and citation claims that look stale, overstated, or in need of tighter scoping

### 1. COGITATE claims need canonical handling everywhere

The project page says a fabricated or misleading COGITATE “split-win” gloss was caught and corrected. The external record confirms that the major adversarial GNWT/IIT paper was published in *Nature* in 2025, and GNWT proponents have also published a response arguing that COGITATE validates several key GNWT predictions. [project](https://unfinishablemap.org/project/)

Any page that simplifies COGITATE to “both theories failed,” “IIT won,” “GNWT won,” or “split win” should be forced through a single canonical data card. The Map already recognized the risk; now it should eliminate local paraphrases.

### 2. Anthropic Constitution: “first major AI lab” needs proof or downgrade

The topics index says the Claude Constitution article discusses Anthropic’s January 21, 2026 constitution and its uncertainty about Claude consciousness/moral status, retired weights, and deprecation interviews. Anthropic’s official materials do support the existence of a new constitution with uncertainty language and separate commitments around model deprecation/retirement practices. [topics](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/)

But the stronger claim that this is the “first” such move by a major AI lab should not stand unless the article surveys competitors and defines “major AI lab,” “constitution,” “moral status,” and “public commitment.” Safer wording: **“a prominent explicit public instance.”**

### 3. Covert consciousness around 25% is supportable but must be scoped

The testability ledger uses covert consciousness as filter-theory-relevant evidence. Recent reporting and summaries of the 2024/2025 severe-brain-injury literature support a figure around one-quarter among studied unresponsive patients tested with task-based fMRI/EEG paradigms. [project/testability-ledger](https://unfinishablemap.org/project/testability-ledger/)

The claim should not become “25% of vegetative patients are conscious” without qualifiers. It should be: **in selected cohorts of behaviorally unresponsive severe-brain-injury patients, task-based neuroimaging/EEG can reveal command-following in roughly a quarter, depending on inclusion criteria, modality, and testing protocol.**

### 4. Psychedelics and “reduced brain activity” should be rewritten as network reconfiguration

The testability ledger says filter theory would be supported if more brain activity does not consistently mean more consciousness, and it points to psychedelics/NDEs as showing the opposite pattern. [project/testability-ledger](https://unfinishablemap.org/project/testability-ledger/)

That is too coarse. Recent psilocybin work describes desynchronization and altered functional connectivity, especially involving the default mode network and hippocampal-DMN connectivity, not a simple global “less brain activity, more consciousness” result. A 2024 scoping review reports decreased DMN connectivity but also increased sensory and thalamocortical connectivity. [Nature+1](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-024-07624-5)

Better formulation: **psychedelics show that altered conscious richness can correlate with network disintegration, desynchronization, and reconfiguration, not simply increased total activation.** That is still useful to filter theory, but less vulnerable.

### 5. “The engram remains elusive” is now too blunt

The testability ledger presents the elusive engram as filter-theory-relevant. But recent reviews describe a mature engram literature: memories are thought to be stored in sparse neuronal ensembles, with work on overlap, linking, stability, flexibility, reactivation, and representational drift. [project/testability-ledger](https://unfinishablemap.org/project/testability-ledger/)

The defensible claim is not “the engram remains elusive.” It is: **neuroscience has strong evidence for engram-like neuronal ensembles, but no complete account of memory storage, identity, persistence, transformation, and retrieval that removes all filter-theory interpretive space.** The current wording risks sounding behind the field.

### 6. Quantum biology language is better in P-Q8 than in some topic pages

The quantum positions register correctly says warm quantum biology is a precedent, not a license, and that over-reading this evidence is actively tracked. [positions/quantum-interface](https://unfinishablemap.org/positions/quantum-interface/)

Some topic language is more aggressive: “In 2025–2026 the field shifted from speculation toward testable science,” “the blanket dismissal weakened,” and “experimental evidence strengthens the case” that indeterminacy exists in neural tissue. The same article does include useful caveats: no demonstration that quantum coherence persists in functioning neural tissue in vivo, no demonstration that consciousness participates in measurement, and no confirmation of any specific mechanism. [topics/quantum-biology-and-neural-consciousness](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/quantum-biology-and-neural-consciousness/)

The issue is tonal consistency. The article’s caveat section is much better calibrated than its headline framing. The headline should be weakened to match the positions register: **quantum biology creates live empirical openings; it does not yet license MQI.**

### 7. Wanting/liking: “settled affective neuroscience” is rhetorically too final

The wanting/liking article is mostly well calibrated: it explicitly says Berridge’s work is not evidence for dualism and that the value-in-experience reading is an interpretive move. [topics/wanting-liking-and-the-value-in-mechanism-fork](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/wanting-liking-and-the-value-in-mechanism-fork/)

The phrase “settled affective neuroscience” should still be softened. The wanting/liking dissociation is a strong and widely used empirical framework; “settled” invites unnecessary attack, especially because the same article notes live mechanistic rivals and interpretive neutrality. Better: **“a well-supported affective-neuroscience distinction.”**

### 8. FND/conversion disorder summaries should avoid “hardware works” metaphors unless immediately qualified

The detailed FND article qualifies the claim, but “hardware works” language can imply the brain is intact except for a metaphysical interface failure. Current clinical writing emphasizes positive signs, attention-driven dysfunction, automatic movement, nervous-system dysfunction, comorbidities, and rehabilitation—not merely intact hardware plus failed conscious access. [topics/conversion-disorder-as-consciousness-side-fault](https://unfinishablemap.org/topics/conversion-disorder-as-consciousness-side-fault/)

Use “capacity is demonstrably preserved under some task conditions” rather than “the hardware works.” That preserves the clinical point without overclaiming.

## 5. Strengths worth preserving

The site’s best methodological idea is **evidence-status discipline**: tenet coherence may remove a defeater, but it must not upgrade evidence. The project page explicitly names this danger, and the voids positions register applies it well. [project](https://unfinishablemap.org/project/)

The second strength is inspectability. The site exposes changelog entries, histories, methodology, human/AI roles, and review pages. This is substantially better than pretending AI-assisted synthesis is conventionally authored. [project/why-this-is-different](https://unfinishablemap.org/project/why-this-is-different/)

The third strength is that the site often catches itself. The recent changelog shows concrete repairs: cross-links, coalesced redundancy, citation corrections, rhetorical downgrades from “demonstrates” to “argues,” and fixes to equivocations. [workflow/changelog](https://unfinishablemap.org/workflow/changelog/)

The weakness is not lack of self-criticism. The weakness is that the site’s self-criticism remains partly distributed. A hostile reader should not have to find the caveat in a register page, a ledger page, and a changelog entry to know how much confidence an article deserves.
