## Genuine Finding (Low severity)

### Schrödinger recruited as a witness to the void, but his point is positive Vedantic monism
- **File**: [voids/plurality-void.md](/voids/plurality-void/)
- **Location**: Opening paragraph (line 31), "Erwin Schrödinger put it directly..."
- **Problem**: The quote is used as evidence for the *plurality void* — the inability to conceive multiple subjects at once. Schrödinger's actual claim in *Mind and Matter* is the opposite metaphysical thesis: there is only *one* consciousness and the appearance of plurality is illusion (the Vedantic Maya). He is asserting a positive monism, not flagging an epistemic limit on conceiving plurality. The article's narrower use — that singularity is the datum — is defensible, since Schrödinger does affirm singular experience, but the "put it directly" framing slightly over-recruits him for the void's *structure*. This is a calibration nuance (source/Map separation), not an error of fact.
- **Severity**: Low
- **Recommendation**: Optional one-clause acknowledgement that Schrödinger drew a monist (not merely a "limit") conclusion from the same datum, OR leave as-is — the singularity datum is correctly attributed. Not worth a content churn on a converged article unless touched for another reason.
