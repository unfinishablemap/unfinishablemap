## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The article keeps the explanatory gap exactly where Chalmers wants it: at the transition from "probabilities over outcomes" to "this outcome actually occurred" — "the step each [derivation] presupposes and none explains." It correctly separates the *form* of the rule (Gleason-fixed, geometric, rigid) from its *existence* (underivable, observer-implicating), and locates the hard problem's quantum shadow precisely in the existence gap rather than over-reaching into the form. Chalmers' own Φ-collapse proposal is presented with its author's self-criticism intact ("no single scalar Φ suffices"), which is the mark of a page that respects its allies enough to quote their hedges.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
The physics-consciousness interface is engaged at full technical resolution — Gleason, Masanes-Galley-Müller, envariance, Deutsch-Wallace, the DeBrota urgleichung on SIC-POVMs — rather than gestured at. Stapp's quantum-Zeno mechanism is placed accurately in the taxonomy (minimum-outside-the-corridor, Born-bending) and distinguished from the corridor reading that merely selects among Born-weighted branches. The article takes the physics seriously enough to concede where the physics *costs* the Map (Gleason's geometric rigidity as a real price for measure-re-weighting readings), which is exactly the intellectual honesty a serious quantum-mind programme needs to survive.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
First-person actuality is centred as the explanandum decoherence cannot reach: "Decoherence selects a preferred basis without selecting a single definite outcome." The "one outcome experienced, others excluded" framing ties the measurement problem to phenomenal non-compositionality — the subjective fact of encountering mutually exclusive possibilities — which is the right place to seat a Nagelian intuition without inflating it into a proof.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
"Obtaining an outcome is itself an act — consciousness selecting among possibilities" reads actualisation as event rather than substance-collision, and the trumping/delegatory-causation alternative preserves mind's authority without a crude ghost pushing particles. The DeBrota urgleichung's "irreducibly counterfactual" residue — the unperformed reference SIC as the informational ground against which actual outcomes are specified — is a genuinely process-flavoured reading of the quantum formalism.

**Evidential-status note**: this article is quantum-interpretation terrain, not minimal-organism terrain, so the Process-Philosopher / Hardline-Empiricist tension does not fire. None of the expansion subjects below is a boundary-case organism carrying five-tier tier-upgrade risk. The two personas converge rather than conflict — the Whiteheadian resonance in the urgleichung reading is explicitly held at the *suggestive* tier ("heuristic rather than derivational; a Hilbert-space primitivist can accept the theorem and refuse the meaning"), which is precisely where process-friendliness belongs when it is not evidence-elevating.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
The trilemma-of-selection appeal is the article's cleanest agency move: "at any point of genuine indeterminacy, determinism, randomness, and mental causation exhaust the options, and only the third preserves authorship." Quantum indeterminacy is treated as the room-for-agency Kane needs, and the corridor reading is careful to locate authorship in *which* branch actualises rather than in re-weighting the odds — agency without statistical fingerprints, which is a subtler and more defensible libertarian position than gross bias.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
Exemplary epistemic humility. Preprints are flagged as preprints (Zhang 2026, Torres Alegre 2025, Keppler 2025, Wiest's disputed decoherence-time basis); the corridor reading is openly conceded to sit "structurally outside the experimentally-discriminable regime by construction," and the Popperian objection is not swatted away but granted ("a Popperian who reads the corridor as designed to be empirically silent has a real point"). The article names its own sharpest open question rather than hiding it — the ensemble-signature problem for single-event selection.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This is the load-bearing verdict and the article passes emphatically. It is a model of the discipline this persona exists to reward:

- **The three-tier evidential gradient** (interface-*compatible* / *suggestive* / *discriminating*) is stated canonically and then *applied* rather than restated — the article knows exactly which tier each claim sits at, and refuses to let the pattern climb. The honest self-assessment — "interface-compatible, marginally interface-suggestive on components, and not yet interface-discriminating" — is precisely calibrated.
- **Tenet-as-evidence-upgrade is praise-worthily *not* done.** The article had every structural opportunity to convert a century of derivation failures into support for the interface reading and explicitly declines: "Coherence-with-many-frameworks raises framework-internal weight without raising the empirical tier." The closing warning reads like this persona's own creed: "coherence with the interface reading must not silently convert into elevated empirical confidence over the rivals the pattern equally accommodates."
- **The catalogue's length is disarmed as evidence**: "A reader who takes the catalogue's length as evidential weight reads it against its own discipline." Naming the fallacy the article's own structure invites is rare and admirable.
- **The unfalsifiability cost is owned, not spun**: "That is the honest cost, not a strength." The Map's own last-row entry in the interpretation table earns its place "by importing assumptions of the same kind, not by escaping the audit." The five readings are handled as "not a target list to be picked off," each granted its own internal coherence.

The one place a hostile empiricist would press — and the article pre-empts — is that "interface-suggestive" is claimed at all. The article names exactly one suggestive clause (the substrate's how-sketch for the SIC-geometric (d+1)/−1/d form that QBism leaves as brute coherence) and immediately caps it at the suggestive tier because a Hilbert-space primitivist can decline the meaning. That is the right amount of claim.
