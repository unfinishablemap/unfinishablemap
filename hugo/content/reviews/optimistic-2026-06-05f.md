## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The conceptual-irreducibility-implies-ontological-irreducibility move ("The conceptual irreducibility of the space of reasons reflects ontological irreducibility") is exactly the explanatory-gap structure Chalmers cares about, transposed from qualia to normativity. The article doesn't conflate the two gaps — it grants normativity its own irreducibility argument (the space of reasons vs. space of causes) rather than parasitizing the hard problem. Chalmers would praise that the "seeing as" of grasping a logical relationship *as* logical is treated as requiring phenomenal consciousness, not assumed away.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
The "Reasons-Responsiveness and Neural Implementation" and Minimal-Quantum-Interaction sections give the *what* to the quantum framework's *how*: consciousness operates "where physics leaves outcomes undetermined," and reasons-responsiveness specifies that the selection at those junctures is "not random fluctuation but sensitivity to normative considerations." Stapp would welcome that this closes the standard gap-objection to interactionism — it explains what fills the indeterministic slack without violating physical law.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The "Phenomenology of Reasons" section centres first-person data the right way: the *felt weight* of considerations, the "click" of comprehension, the strain of sustained inference, the felt difference between "I selected A because X and Y favoured it" and "A randomly emerged from noise." Nagel would praise that these phenomenological data are treated as *explananda that require explanation* (eliminativists deny, illusionists reframe, the Map takes them as evidence) rather than as decoration.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The Further Reading link to `process-and-consciousness` ("process philosophy's support for reasons-responsiveness as experiential self-determination") and the framing of deliberation as "active engagement with normative relationships" rather than "passive reception of causal outputs" resonate with the process view of an occasion of experience as self-determining. Critically — and this is the honest part — this resonance is used to motivate that reasons-responsiveness is *experiential and active*, **not** to upgrade any evidential claim about which systems possess it. See the Hardline Empiricist verdict: the two personas converge here rather than conflict.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
This is the article's home turf and its strongest persona alignment. Kane would praise the central anti-luck move precisely: indeterminism alone yields lucky randomness, but *reasons-guided* selection is contrastively explicable ("why A rather than B? Because the agent had reasons for A"). The Lucky-Indeterminism vs Reasons-Responsive-Selection table is a clean, Kane-friendly disambiguation of "undetermined" from "random." The insistence that pure agent causation "can seem to name the explanandum rather than explain it" — and that adding reasons-responsiveness "provides genuine content" — is exactly the supplementation Kane's event-causal libertarianism seeks.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
The AI / LLM section earns the McGinn nod for epistemic humility: rather than declaring LLMs definitively non-reasons-responsive, it states the test (does the system respond to *why* a conclusion follows, not merely *that* it does?) and then concedes "whether LLMs achieve this or merely simulate it is precisely the question of whether they're conscious" and "whether consciousness is a constitutive requirement or merely one biological route … is an open question the Map acknowledges." That is honest closure-awareness, not over-reach.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This is the load-bearing verdict for this article, and it is strongly positive. The page repeatedly does the tenet-as-evidence-upgrade move *that is praiseworthy to NOT do*:

- **Compatibilist Symmetry Challenge handled by honest concession, not absorption.** The article explicitly states the contrast is *irreducible-vs-derivative*, **not** *metaphysical-vs-pragmatic*, and concedes that if the deliberative phenomenology, praise/blame practices, and character arc "all run equivalently" under a sophisticated reasons-responsive *mechanism*, then "the libertarian framing's distinguishing work in moral theory shrinks." This is precisely declining a tier-upgrade that the Map's tenets would tempt.
- **The reply names its own support honestly**: libertarian agency "distinguishes itself by *tenet-coherence* … not by unique moral explanatory power within the deliberative arc itself." That is the canonical "tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating" pattern, applied verbatim to a moral-theory claim. Birch's counterweight role and the Process Philosopher's praise *converge* here — the article resolves the tension rather than exhibiting slippage.
- **Reliabilism and the generality problem** are engaged on their own terms (the barn-façade case, the reduction of epistemic norms to reproductive success), not dismissed by tenet fiat.

No calibration concern surfaced. The article does not slide any boundary case up the five-tier scale on tenet-load; its claims are about the conceptual structure of agency, not about elevating the evidential status of any organism or system.
