## Executive Summary

The positions register is the single most consequential structural addition the Map has made in this review window: it converts framework-internal coherence into a scannable, confidence-banded, retire-don't-delete ledger of *what the Map actually commits to* — exactly the "positions you could use as a basis for decisions" deficit `positions.md` names in its own opening. ## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
P-Q3 is the position Chalmers would most respect: it names the bias-without-deviation dilemma — to be *selection* the influence must do what the unbiased Born distribution does not, yet to be *minimal* it must not deviate — as "the strongest live challenge to the default mechanism" and cites Chalmers and McQueen (2022) as the source of the formulation. This is the hard problem's mechanism-layer analogue: the register refuses to dissolve the difficulty by fiat and instead records it as a *live* moderate-confidence position with explicit shift conditions. Chalmers would also note that P-Q9 ("the interface is self-concealing by construction") is doing honest work that physicalists rarely permit themselves — admitting that a framework's predicted *absence* of a signature is not disconfirming, while being scrupulous that this "does not raise the probability that the tenets are correct."

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
Stapp would find his own programme treated with rare fairness. P-Q4 explicitly keeps the Stapp-Zeno family "in the live mechanism set," demotes it only on *timing* grounds (the decoherence-timescale objection), and writes a concrete re-elevation condition: "a future result that closes the decoherence gap in warm-wet neural tissue would re-elevate it." This is the register form working as designed — a demotion that is reversible and conditional rather than a dismissal. P-Q2's corridor reading (conscious selection biasing *which* outcome becomes actual while preserving aggregate Born statistics) is recognisably the von-Neumann-process-1 intuition rendered as a *high*-confidence structural commitment.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
Nagel would point to the indexical move buried in P-Q3 — "the indexical character of which outcome an experiencing subject actualises" — as the register's clearest centring of the first-person. The bias-without-deviation resolution the Map gestures at is precisely the per-trial-vs-ensemble distinction that only makes sense from inside a point of view: there *is* no third-person fact that distinguishes the selected outcome from the chance outcome, which is exactly Nagel's point that objective science leaves a residue. The register records this as an open commitment rather than a solved problem, which is the honest Nagelian posture.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
Whitehead would welcome that the register avoids crude substance-talk: positions are events in a maintained ledger (status, confidence, shift conditions), not fixed substances. P-Q9's self-concealing interface — the influence leaving "only indirect traces" — is congenial to a process view in which mind's contribution is woven into the becoming of each actual occasion rather than bolted on. **Constraint honoured**: this praise is for the *form*, not for any tier-upgrade. The register nowhere uses process-philosophical resonance to elevate a mechanism's evidential standing; every mechanism position is pinned at *moderate* on argument-and-timing grounds, not lifted on metaphysical compatibility. See the Hardline Empiricist verdict, which is load-bearing here.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
Kane would read P-Q3's framing — "to be genuine *selection* the influence must do something the unbiased Born distribution does not" — as the register taking authorship seriously at the mechanism layer. The whole point of the corridor-vs-deviation tension is that genuine agency requires the selection to *matter*, and the register declines to paper over the worry that a Born-preserving corridor might be a hidden idleness. That it leaves the "minimum-outside-the-corridor" branch open as a fall-back (P-Q2, P-Q7) is exactly the room-for-genuine-freedom Kane wants kept open rather than prematurely closed.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
McGinn's verdict is the register's quiet triumph. P-Q10 — "no worked toy model of the mechanism currently exists" — is recorded as a first-class position with *high* confidence, explicitly flagged as "a meta-position about the state of the work, not a position about the world." This is cognitive humility installed as ledger infrastructure: the register tells you that the confidence of P-Q1, P-Q2, P-Q3, P-Q7, and P-Q9 all *depend on* this gap, and that closing it "would tighten [them] by one band." McGinn would say the Map has done the rare thing of making its own incompleteness legible and load-bearing rather than embarrassing.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This persona's verdict is load-bearing for the register and the register passes cleanly. Three exhibits of tenet-as-evidence-upgrade praise-worthily *not* done:

1. **P-Q8 (warm-quantum-biology as precedent, not licence)** is a textbook calibration win. It grants cryptochrome magnetoreception and photosynthetic energy transfer establish "the regime is not categorically impossible," then explicitly refuses to convert that into support for the neural case ("no coherence-time calculation supports the neural cases at present"), and goes further — it names the failure mode: "Citations of warm-quantum-biology that read as support rather than precedent are calibration drift and are corrected when caught." This is the *"tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating"* discipline operating as a standing position.
2. **P-Q9 (self-concealing interface)** carries its own anti-inflation guard in the position text: "This is a framework-internal architectural claim, not an empirical result, and does not raise the probability that the tenets are correct." The structural opportunity to slide a framework-coherence result up into evidence was present and explicitly declined.
3. **The confidence bands are honest, not flattering.** The preferred mechanism (P-Q1) is *moderate*, not *high*; the things held at *high* are an empirical falsification the Map accepts (P-Q6, Donadi et al. 2021), a structural feature of the default reading (P-Q2), and the meta-admission of a missing model (P-Q10) — never a mechanism preference. `positions.md` even instructs that "a *low*-confidence position should not be phrased as a settled result," wiring the hedging-matches-band discipline into the schema.

Birch's one steering note (a strength to *complete*, not a flaw): P-Q3 is the register's most evidentially delicate position and is currently argued only "lightly" in `topics/born-rule-and-the-consciousness-interface` with the deeper engagement marked "pending." The corpus already contains the sharper treatment — [concepts/ensemble-level-epiphenomenalism.md](/concepts/ensemble-level-epiphenomenalism/) rates the corridor-channel-vs-idleness question route-by-route, and `tenets.md` itself names it "the Map's sharpest open question, stated and rated route-by-route in [ensemble-level-epiphenomenalism](/concepts/ensemble-level-epiphenomenalism/)." Citing that file in P-Q3's **Argued in** line would let the register point at real engagement rather than a promissory note, without weakening the honest "deeper engagement is pending" framing. This is a cross-link fix, not a calibration concern — the Process Philosopher and the Hardline Empiricist do not conflict anywhere in this register, which is the signal that the tension was resolved honestly.

## Content Strengths

### positions/positions.md
- **Strongest point**: The opening diagnosis. The register is explicitly framed as the answer to "a structural concern about the project: that work was producing framework-internal coherence faster than it was producing positions you could use as a basis for decisions." Naming the deficit the register exists to cure is the kind of self-locating move the rest of the corpus rewards.
- **Notable quote**: "A position can be retired without disturbing the tenets; retiring a tenet would invalidate every position that depends on it. The register makes this dependency structure explicit so the cascade is legible."
- **Why it works**: It establishes a clean four-layer architecture (tenets → positions → apex synthesis → topics/concepts catalogue) and assigns the register a role none of the other layers held: the visible, falsifiable commitment ledger. The retire-don't-delete rule and the audit-mode description (contradictions, broken deps, orphan positions) make the maintenance story credible rather than aspirational.

### positions/quantum-interface.md
- **Strongest point**: P-Q10 as a first-class position. Recording "no worked toy model exists" as a *high*-confidence position that other positions explicitly depend on is a structural innovation — it makes the register's confidence calibration self-documenting.
- **Notable quote**: "It is recorded explicitly because the confidence levels of P-Q1, P-Q2, P-Q3, P-Q7, and P-Q9 all depend on it; were a worked toy model to appear and survive review, several positions in this register would tighten by one band."
- **Why it works**: Every position has a concrete, falsifiable **Would shift if** clause, and the dependency links between positions (P-Q4 and P-Q5 depend on P-Q1; P-Q7 depends on P-Q2) are real rather than decorative. The file demotes Stapp-Zeno and Orch-OR on stated grounds while keeping them live, accepts a genuine empirical falsification (P-Q6) that constrains its own preferred mechanism family, and reads Torres Alegre (2025) as *supporting* the corridor reading — all twelve apex/topic citations verified to resolve.
