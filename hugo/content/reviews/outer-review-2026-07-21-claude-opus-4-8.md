# Adversarial Peer-Review Audit — "Phenomenal Conservatism" (The Unfinishable Map)

## 1. TL;DR

- **Verdict: REVISE-HARD.** The article states Huemer's principle correctly (the 2007 quotation is verbatim-accurate against publisher-of-record) and largely honours the epistemic/metaphysical boundary, but it carries one outright citation misattribution, one fabricated title-gloss contradicted by Huemer's own words, an unverified load-bearing citation banked as a disclosure, and material omissions in a section that advertises currency.
- The single worst defect is an **author-stance / citation-fabrication failure**: the "isolation objection" is attributed to "Moretti and Tucker" when it is Kevin McCain's (*Erkenntnis*, 2017); Tucker is a *defender* of PC, and Moretti's signature objection is the "problem of reflective awareness." A competent referee catches this on first read, and it discredits the objections section.
- The brief's own framing must be corrected: **Huemer is an avowed interactionist substance dualist** who himself uses appearance-based reasoning to argue for dualism. The co-optation-firewall worry therefore inverts — the page is scrupulous *not* to claim him — but the deeper special-pleading risk (asymmetric deployment of PC against physicalist "seemings") survives and lives in the companion application article.

## 2. Five-Dimension Analysis

### (1) Citation fidelity

**CRITICAL FAILURE (author-stance + metadata).** The paragraph "*Isolation and Bayesian incompatibility. A more recent line, associated with Moretti and Tucker, presses an isolation objection…*" is wrong on every count. The isolation objection is **Kevin McCain (sole author), "An Isolation Objection to Phenomenal Conservatism," *Erkenntnis* 82(6):1381–1390 (2017), DOI 10.1007/s10670-017-9881-7** — Springer abstract: "phenomenal conservatism faces a problem analogous to the isolation objection to coherentism … can only avoid its version … by sacrificing its internalist character." **Chris Tucker is a defender of PC/dogmatism** (editor of the field volume; "Why Open-Minded People Should Endorse Dogmatism," *Philosophical Perspectives* 24:529–545, 2010). **Luca Moretti's signature objection is a different one — the "problem of reflective awareness"** (*American Philosophical Quarterly* 55(3):267–280, 2018; thesis: "if S becomes reflectively aware of an appearance, the appearance loses its inherent justifying power"). This is exactly the co-optation/author-stance failure the protocol treats as systemic: a defender is conscripted as an objector, and an objection is assigned to the wrong people under the wrong name — a citation-fabrication pattern that passes metadata review but fails role verification. [Internet Encyclopedia of Philosophy](https://iep.utm.edu/phen-con/)[PhilPapers](https://philpapers.org/rec/MAREAP)

**Second fidelity failure (fabricated stance-gloss).** The article asserts the label "compassionate" "marks the paper's central move … PC extends the same epistemic charity to all appearances." Huemer says the opposite in his own retrospective: "The title of this paper alluded to the phrase 'compassionate conservatism' that President Bush had made popular; the title hasn't aged well. **Compassion has nothing to do with the paper.** 'Phenomenal' comes from the Greek word 'phainomenon', meaning 'appearance'." The article has invented philosophical content for a topical pun — an author-stance error about the meaning of Huemer's own title.

**Third fidelity failure (confession without correction).** On BonJour: "precise pagination is left unstated here pending verification." The citation is trivially verifiable — BonJour, "In Search of Direct Realism," *Philosophy and Phenomenological Research* 69 (2004): 349–367 (metajustification demand at pp. 357–60), a criticism of Huemer 2001. Note also the article dates BonJour's critique to "mid-2000s" but the load-bearing work is 2004. A disclosed gap has been banked as a credential rather than closed.

### (2) Characterization fairness & currency

The principle is stated correctly and fairly; the 2001→2007 evolution is accurate (the 2007 wording was designed to encompass inferential as well as foundational justification). The self-defeat argument, the no-privileging generality thesis, and the contrast with Pryor's perception-restricted dogmatism are all faithfully rendered. Markie's over-liberality objection and Siegel's cognitive-penetration "bad basis" objection are fairly represented, as is Huemer's "phenomenal forcefulness/pushiness" reply.

**Currency is where it slips.** The section titled "Objections at the General Level" claims to catalogue the recurring families but omits: (a) the **bootstrapping / easy-knowledge** family (Cohen 2002, 2005; Vogel 2000) — conspicuous because the article itself relies on the SEP passage that foregrounds "too easy"; and (b) **Declan Smithies, "On the Global Ambitions of Phenomenal Conservatism," *Analytic Philosophy* 60(3):206–244 (2019), DOI 10.1111/phib.12167** — the leading recent systematic critique, which argues "the simplicity of phenomenal conservatism is not a theoretical virtue, but a theoretical vice, since it distorts the epistemological phenomena it is supposed to explain … including introspection, inference, and a priori justification." Additionally, the self-defeat "published rebuttal" is described but **never named** (a performative-inoculation citation; the real candidates are DePoe, *Philosophical Studies* 152(3):347–359, 2011; Hasan, *Philosophical Studies* 162(2):119–141, 2013; Mizrahi 2014). [PhilPapers + 2](https://philpapers.org/rec/DEPDTS)

### (3) Logical gaps & omitted counterarguments

The Bayesian paragraph presents the incompatibility worry as a live, unresolved probe of "formal coherence" but omits that the leading recent verdict is that PC survives it — **McCain & Moretti, *Appearance and Explanation* (OUP, 2021)**, ch. "Phenomenal Conservatism and Its Promises" (pp. 3–29): the common objections "that it is too liberal, it conflicts with Bayesianism, and it runs afoul of cognitive penetration … fail to pose a genuine threat to PC." This is an evidential-independence failure compounded by irony: the two names the article miscasts as objectors (McCain, Moretti) are in fact co-authors who *defend* PC against the very objection the article leaves standing.

The strongest dualism-relevant objection the article under-answers is the **tainted-source / cognitive-penetration objection applied to the specific seeming the site actually needs** — the seeming that consciousness is *irreducible*, which (unlike "pain hurts") is plausibly theory-laden. The page half-acknowledges this by routing the metaphysical work elsewhere, but its claim to rely on "the paradigm case least vulnerable to … cognitive-penetration objections" quietly trades on the invulnerability of "pain hurts" to license a payoff that in fact rests on the vulnerable seeming.

### (4) Coherence with dualist commitments / special pleading

The **constitutional-attractor effect** is on open display: "PC matters to the Map primarily through Occam's Razor Has Limits (Tenet 5) … PC supplies the positive epistemology to match." PC is explicitly recruited because a tenet needs an anti-parsimony epistemology. This is not fatal — PC is a mainstream, independently-motivated Huemerian view, not a fabricated convenience — but it puts the **constrain-vs-establish gate** on watch. On this concept page the gate mostly holds: it states plainly that PC "secures the *evidential base* … does not, by itself, deliver the *metaphysical conclusion*." That is correct discipline and is the main reason the verdict is REVISE-HARD, not DEMOTE.

The **special-pleading risk is asymmetric deployment**, and it lives in the companion article (`phenomenal-authority-and-first-person-evidence`) that this page designates as the application site. There, physicalist commitments (causal closure) are recharacterized as "theoretical commitments" with "no apodictic warrant," while phenomenal seemings are elevated — yet on PC's *own generality thesis*, the naturalist's seemings also earn defeasible justification. PC is being run in the discounting direction against physicalism and the crediting direction for dualism. The layered-authority apparatus supplies principled-sounding cover (the layer-1 apodictic tier), but the dualist *payoff* draws on layer-2-and-beyond seemings where the companion article itself concedes the case is "most exposed."

**Co-optation firewall — inverted.** The brief's premise that "Huemer is NOT a substance dualist in the site's sense" is factually wrong. Huemer is an avowed **interactionist substance dualist** (Wikipedia; his 2023 "Substance Dualism" podcast; his essay "Disembodied Souls Are People Too": "These make it seem that the mind must be something over and above the physical phenomena of the body and the brain … I favor the former view ('substance dualism') because I think it best accommodates very strong and widespread intuitions about personal identity"). So the page is not conscripting an unwilling naturalist — and, to its credit, it never claims Huemer as a dualist ally. The residual firewall concern is narrower and real: Huemer's dualism rests on appearance-based reasoning and personal-identity intuitions, **not** on the site's distinctive minimal-quantum-bidirectional-interaction apparatus or its rejection of Many-Worlds. Routing his general epistemology toward *that* specific metaphysics is a leap Huemer does not make; the concept page does not commit it, but the companion article gestures toward it (the "authority-law" / interface-formalization move). [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michael_Huemer)[Blubrry](http://counterapologetics.blubrry.net/2023/08/28/ca122-substance-dualism-w-michael-huemer/)

### (5) Epistemic-to-metaphysical slide (through-line)

On the concept page the slide is well-controlled — an entire paragraph exists to prevent it ("Conflating the two would overstate PC's reach: the principle earns phenomenal reports a seat at the evidential table without settling what they ultimately show"). This is the article's genuine strength. The slide re-emerges downstream in the companion article's "Rorty's Dilemma" section, where an *epistemic* property (incorrigibility of layer-1 reports) is converted into a *metaphysical* distinctness claim ("phenomenal states possess an epistemic property no physical state has"). That conversion — from "reports are incorrigible" to "the states are ontologically distinctive" — is precisely the slide the site's own vocabulary is built to catch, and it should be flagged at the application layer, not just the definitional one.

## 3. Bottom-Line Verdict

**REVISE-HARD.**

Not DELETE: the core exposition is accurate, the 2007 quotation is verbatim-clean, PC is a genuine and independently-motivated part of the site's evidential base, and the epistemic/metaphysical boundary is explicitly disciplined on this page. Not DEMOTE-TO-COHERENCE-ONLY: the defects are load-bearing but local and fixable, and the page does not itself commit the epistemic-to-metaphysical slide. Not FLAG AS PERPETUALLY CONTESTED: the disputed matters (self-defeat, cognitive penetration) are correctly presented *as* contested. But the misattribution, the fabricated title-gloss, the banked unverified citation, and the currency gap are serious enough that the article cannot stand as written.

## 4. Article-Specific Fixes (concrete, prioritized)

1. **Correct the misattribution immediately.** Replace "associated with Moretti and Tucker" with **Kevin McCain, "An Isolation Objection to Phenomenal Conservatism," *Erkenntnis* 82(6):1381–1390 (2017)**. Remove Tucker from the objector list; where mentioned, identify him as the volume's editor and a PC *defender*. Rename Moretti's contribution the **"problem of reflective awareness"** (*American Philosophical Quarterly* 55(3):267–280, 2018).
2. **Add the Bayesian rebuttal.** State that **McCain & Moretti, *Appearance and Explanation* (OUP, 2021)** and Moretti (2020) argue PC is reconcilable with Bayesian updating; do not leave the incompatibility worry standing unanswered. [Springer](https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-3-030-43392-5_4)
3. **Delete or rewrite the "compassionate" gloss.** Replace the invented "epistemic charity" rationale with Huemer's actual explanation (topical pun on Bush's "compassionate conservatism"; "compassion has nothing to do with the paper"). Make the generality/no-privileging point on its own terms.
4. **Close the BonJour citation.** Supply the pagination — *PPR* 69:349–367 (2004), metajustification demand at 357–60 — correct the "mid-2000s" to 2004, and delete the "pending verification" clause. Unverified load-bearing citations must not ship as disclosures.
5. **Meet the currency claim.** Add the bootstrapping / easy-knowledge family (Cohen 2002, 2005; Vogel 2000) and **Smithies (2019)**; name the self-defeat rebuttal author(s) (DePoe 2011; Hasan 2013; Mizrahi 2014).
6. **Tighten the "least vulnerable" framing** so the invulnerability of "pain hurts" does not silently license the theory-laden "irreducibility" seeming; cross-reference the companion article's own concession explicitly on this page.

**Benchmarks that change the verdict.** Implement and verify fixes 1–4 → article rises to RETAIN-with-watch. If re-examination of the objections cluster surfaces a *second* misattribution (i.e., the McCain/Tucker error is not isolated), escalate to DEMOTE-TO-COHERENCE-ONLY pending a full citation re-audit.

## 5. Site-Wide Methodology Improvements (concrete)

1. **Mandatory author-stance ledger for every cited critic/defender.** The McCain/Tucker/Moretti error is verbatim-clean but role-wrong — exactly the class the changelog's metadata-focused "deep-review" passes miss. Require a field per citation: "role in debate (defender / critic / neutral) + one-sentence actual thesis," verified at source. This is the highest-yield fix; it directly closes the co-optation-firewall gap.
2. **Ban "pending verification" as a publishable state for load-bearing citations.** Convert the site's confession-without-correction habit into a binding gate: an unverified citation is either verified before publish or the claim it supports is demoted/deleted — not shipped with a disclosure.
3. **Symmetry audit for every PC deployment.** Because PC is a generality thesis, any page that uses it to discount physicalist seemings must state what PC grants the opposing seemings and why those are defeated — closing the asymmetric-deployment loophole at the point of use.
4. **Decouple a literature-currency check from the convergence / no-op discipline.** The changelog marks many articles "converged / no-op" on byte-stability, but a byte-stable objections section can still fall out of currency (Smithies 2019 omitted). Trigger a currency pass on a calendar cadence independent of edit-churn.
5. **Run epistemic→metaphysical slide-checks at the application layer.** The concept page disciplines the slide; the companion topic re-introduces it. Slide-checks must run on downstream application articles where the metaphysical payoff is actually cashed, not only on the definitional page that hosts the disclaimer.
