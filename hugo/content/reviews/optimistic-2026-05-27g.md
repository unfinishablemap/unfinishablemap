## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The article locates the explanatory pressure exactly where Chalmers would want it: "at the gap between what consciousness *is like* and what cognition *does*." It does not overreach into claiming the hard problem is solved; it shows a within-species, empirically anchored case where phenomenology and function come apart at the very grain functionalism trades on. The refusal to treat the wedge as knockdown ("cumulative empirical pressure at the cluster level, not knockdown evidence in itself") is the kind of restraint that makes the hard-problem framing credible rather than rhetorical.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
Modest and appropriate. The interface-activity speculation is firewalled to "the level of explicit speculation, not tenet-level commitment" and the article explicitly says "source-attribution divergence does not by itself support this speculation." Stapp would appreciate that a candidate interface site is named without the data being conscripted to prove it.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The "What People Report" section is a small phenomenological gem: the observation that "the phenomenology of confident-but-wrong source memory is indistinguishable from inside from confident-and-correct source memory," and that discovery of one's spectrum position "typically arrives from outside," centers first-person experience precisely while showing its limits. The recalibration line — "not 'I should have known' so much as 'I had no idea I could not tell'" — is the article's best single sentence.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
Source attribution presented as "irreducibly inferential, probabilistic" — origin reconstructed at retrieval rather than read off a native tag — fits the process intuition that there is no substance-like stored "thought-with-its-origin," only an event of reconstruction. No crude substance-dualism is smuggled in. (Per skill constraint: this resonance is logged on the metaphysical-compatibility axis only; it is not used here to upgrade any evidential tier, and the article does not attempt such an upgrade — see Hardline Empiricist below.)

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
Indirect but present: the authorship-of-action / choice-blindness bullet engages the agency question (can the agent detect when a choice attributed to them was not theirs?). Kane would want the cause-attribution layer kept distinct from outcome-recognition — and the sibling article does exactly that, which is one more reason to surface the link.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
This article is a near-ideal Mysterian exhibit. Option 3 — "introspective reports about source-tagging are themselves systematically inaccurate" — is preserved as a live, load-bearing reading rather than dissolved. The article openly states "the only tool for studying source attribution is the tool whose unreliability is part of the question," yet shows the inquiry is not thereby paralyzed because the evidence lives at the third-person measurement layer. Humility about the first-person access without nihilism about the result.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This is the load-bearing verdict and it is strongly positive. The article does the tenet-as-evidence-upgrade thing praise-worthily *not*:

- The five-tier scale is used explicitly and correctly. The cluster-level discharge is pinned at *strongly-supported* **only conditional** on the common-cause null surviving the discrimination tests at the apex; "absent that survival, the cluster-level move sits at *live hypothesis*." That is exactly the honest register.
- The interface reading is held at "speculative-integration tier rather than as empirical force," with the explicit concession that "the data here are also fully compatible with reconstructive-cognition physicalism, and the interface reading does not draw additional empirical force from the source-attribution case alone."
- The article names its own circularity (it both *uses* and *contributes to* the cluster) and defers the common-cause-null relitigation to the apex rather than quietly discharging it locally. This is the named pattern "tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating" executed cleanly.
- The option-2 functionalist response is preserved with its cost marked, and the article warns against reading itself as "a false trilemma converging on the conclusion." That self-correction is precisely the discipline this persona exists to praise.

Process Philosopher and Hardline Empiricist **converge** here rather than conflict: the process-resonance is genuine and the evidential tiers are honestly calibrated, so both can praise different aspects without the praise cashing out as slippage. No calibration concern to flag; no refine-draft warranted on evidential grounds.
