### CRITICAL — Fabricated author/title/article-ID (Rosseinsky & Bhatt 2022)
- **Cited as**: Rosseinsky, M.J., & Bhatt, P.M. (2022). "Dimensions of Experience Space." *Frontiers in Systems Neuroscience*, 16, 828608.
- **Verdict**: FABRICATED metadata wrapping a REAL paper. The journal (Frontiers in Systems Neuroscience), year (2022), and BOTH verbatim quotes used in the article ("at least several times as many dimensions as there are categories of qualia"; "one or more multidimensional ur-experiences that combined multiple forms of experience together") match exactly one real paper:
  - **Lacalli, T.C. (2022). "On the Origins and Evolution of Qualia: An Experience-Space Perspective." *Frontiers in Systems Neuroscience*, 16, 945722.** (DOI 10.3389/fnsys.2022.945722; sole author Thurston Lacalli, Dept. of Biology, University of Victoria; verified via PMC9399462).
- Author names "Rosseinsky, M.J., & Bhatt, P.M." (a materials chemist + the recurring fabricated-this-session "Bhatt"), the title "Dimensions of Experience Space", and the article ID 828608 are all wrong/invented. The quotes themselves are genuine.
- **Fix**: in-text attribution "Rosseinsky and Bhatt (2022)" → "Lacalli (2022)"; reference entry rewritten to the real Lacalli 2022 paper. Quotes preserved verbatim (they are accurate to the real source).

### CRITICAL — Wrong journal/volume/pages + fabricated second entry (Wager)
- **Cited as**: Wager, A. (1999). "The Extra Qualia Problem." *Philosophia*, 27, 571-584; AND Wager, A. (2001). "Synaesthesia and the Problem of Qualia." *Southwest Philosophy Review*, 17, 163-170.
- **Verdict**: The real paper is **Adam Wager (1999), "The extra qualia problem: Synaesthesia and representationism," *Philosophical Psychology*, 12(3), 263-281** (verified via Taylor & Francis / ProQuest / PhilPapers). The cited 1999 metadata (*Philosophia* 27:571-584) has wrong journal, volume, pages, and a truncated subtitle. The 2001 *Southwest Philosophy Review* entry is unverifiable — no record found in Wager's documented synaesthesia corpus.
- **Fix**: collapsed to the single verified 1999 *Philosophical Psychology* reference; corrected in-text "(1999, 2001)" → "(1999)" and "Alan Wager" → "Adam Wager" (first name was also wrong).

### CRITICAL — Wrong book title (MacPherson 2007)

"Bentornata Realtà" is a different de Caro book.
- **Fix**: reference entry corrected to the Cartographies of the Mind volume. The "extraordinary feature" claim (two colours occupying one spatial location without blending) is correctly characterized.

### CRITICAL — Claim/citation mismatch (Merabet 2004)
- **Cited as**: brief visual deprivation of "approximately ninety minutes" inducing "tactile-evoked visual experiences."
- **Verdict**: The cited paper (Merabet et al. 2004, *J. Neuro-Ophthalmol.* 24(2):109-113) is about **FIVE-DAY (96h)** complete blindfolding producing **spontaneous visual hallucinations** (simple flashes → complex faces/landscapes) in 77% of sighted subjects — NOT 90-minute tactile-evoked percepts. The "~90 min tactile-evoked visual cortex recruitment" finding belongs to a DIFFERENT Merabet/Pascual-Leone study (the rapid-recruitment TMS/fMRI work, e.g. Merabet et al. 2008 PLoS ONE 3(8):e3046). The prose described one paper while citing another.
- **Fix**: aligned prose to what the CITED 2004 paper actually found ("several days of complete visual deprivation induced vivid visual hallucinations... in the majority of blindfolded sighted subjects"). The "latent cross-modal capacity normally suppressed" point survives intact. Added issue number to the reference.
