### High Priority

#### 1. The Unfolding Argument Against Causal-Structure Theories of Consciousness
- **Target section**: `concepts/`
- **Rationale**: Kleiner & Hoel's (2021) formal result — that a system's causal structure can be
  re-described (unfolded) into behaviourally equivalent networks with arbitrarily different
  structure — is a serious threat to *any* theory (IIT foremost) that locates consciousness in
  physical/causal organization. For the Map it cuts favourably: it is independent support that
  consciousness is not fixed by physical structure, and it sharply constrains what form a
  psychophysical law may take. It deserves a dedicated steelman-and-respond, not a passing
  mention.
- **Dedup verdict**: Grepped `unfolding argument`, `kleiner`, `behaviourally equivalent ... phi`.
  The *specific argument* returns **zero** hits in the IIT article
  (`consciousness-and-integrated-information.md` has 0 mentions of "unfolding argument"). The
  generic word "unfolding" appears ~27× but in unrelated senses. CLEAN.
- **NEAR-MISS**: [topics/hoel-llm-consciousness-continual-learning.md](/topics/hoel-llm-consciousness-continual-learning/) shares the author (Hoel)
  but is titled "Hoel's Disproof of LLM Consciousness" and covers the proximity/continual-learning
  argument (0 mentions of the unfolding argument). Content-distinct; the new concept exceeds it by
  treating the formal structure-underdetermination result the LLM article never touches.

#### 2. Anomalous Monism and the Denial of Strict Psychophysical Laws
- **Target section**: `concepts/`
- **Rationale**: Davidson's anomalous monism holds that mental events are token-identical to
  physical events yet there are **no strict psychophysical laws**. This is the sharpest available
  denial of the Map's entire psychophysical-law program (tenets 2 and 3 lean on the existence of
  lawful mind→matter coupling). A dedicated steelman-and-respond — why the Map thinks lawlike
  coupling survives Davidson's anomalism — is squarely tenet-relevant and currently absent.
- **Dedup verdict**: Grepped `anomalous monism`, `davidson`. Four scattered mentions corpus-wide;
  **zero** in either `psychophysical-laws-bridging-mind-and-matter.md` or
  `the-psychophysical-control-law.md`. No dedicated page. CLEAN.
- **NEAR-MISS**: none. The five existing "law" articles (`psychophysical-laws.md`,
  `conservation-laws-and-mental-causation.md`, etc.) *assert* lawful coupling; none engages the
  named rival that denies it.

#### 3. Phenomenal Vagueness and the Consciousness Sorites
- **Target section**: `concepts/`
- **Rationale**: Is consciousness determinate (a sharp on/off "light") or can there be genuinely
  indeterminate cases? The sorites/vagueness question is where minimal-organism and animal
  boundary cases actually live, and it bears directly on IIT's sharp-boundary claims and on the
  dualist picture of the interface. Minting it as evidential-discipline infrastructure (a place to
  ask whether "borderline conscious" is even coherent) supports the Hardline-Empiricist calibration
  program — provided it is written as an open metaphysical question, not a tier-upgrade lever.
- **Dedup verdict**: Grepped `phenomenal vagueness`, `sorites`, `determinacy of consciousness`,
  `borderline`. Only `quantum-indeterminacy-free-will.md` matches, in the unrelated
  quantum-indeterminism sense. No dedicated determinacy-of-consciousness page. CLEAN.
- **NEAR-MISS**: none clean. `panpsychisms-combination-problem.md` touches graded-experience
  intuitions but does not address vagueness/determinacy as such.
