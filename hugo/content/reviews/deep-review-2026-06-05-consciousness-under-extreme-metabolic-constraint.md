## Pessimistic Analysis Summary

### Critical Issues Found (all FIXED this pass)

1. **Stender 2014 — wrong metabolic percentages.** Body stated "Vegetative state patients showed 38% of normal" and "Minimally conscious patients showed 56%." The paper (PMC4294395) reports VS/UWS = **42%** and MCS = **55%** of normal cortical CMRglc. Fixed.
2. **Stender 2014 — "42% threshold" conflation.** The body presented "approximately 42%" as the threshold *separating* MCS from VS. 42% is the VS/UWS group *mean*; the diagnostic crossover is at **~44%** (emergence above 45%). Fixed; section retitled from "The 42% Metabolic Threshold" to "The Metabolic Threshold," body and filter-model bullet reworded.
3. **Stender 2014 — FABRICATED recovery statistic.** Body claimed the threshold "correctly predicted recovery of consciousness within twelve months in 94% of cases." **The paper contains no 12-month recovery-prediction statistic** — it reports diagnostic classification accuracy of up to ~83%. The "94% / twelve months" figure was invented. Removed and replaced with the real classification statistic.
4. **"Hoel 2024" — fabricated author attribution + wrong venue.** Reference 4 and the in-body NDE cite credited "Hoel, E. (2024), *Neuroscience of Consciousness*." The actual paper ("Consciousness and the Dying Brain," PMC11096058, the source of the "realer than real" framing) is **Mashour, G. A., Lee, U., Pal, D., & Li, D. (2024), *Anesthesiology* 140(6):1221–1231.** Erik Hoel is a real consciousness researcher (cited elsewhere on the Map for LLM-consciousness work), which made the false attribution plausible — the classic "plausible-but-wrong first-author" fabrication slot. Fixed in body and references.
5. **Cerri 2017 — wrong venue.** Reference 7 said "*Neuroscience of Consciousness*, 3(1)." Correct: **Cerri, M. (2017), *Journal of Integrative Neuroscience*, 16(s1), S19–S26** (PMID 29125496, verified exact volume/issue/pages). Fixed. (The "2% of basal metabolic rate" claim itself is sound — corroborated by the broader hibernation literature, e.g. Drew 2007.)
6. **Batthyány & Greyson 2020 — wrong / cross-attributed death-timing numbers.** Body claimed "84% of patients died within one week, 43% within 24 hours." Those figures actually belong to **Nahm et al. 2012's 19th-century case review** (and are correctly attributed to Nahm in the sibling [terminal-lucidity-and-filter-transmission-theory](/topics/terminal-lucidity-and-filter-transmission-theory/) article). Batthyány & Greyson report different figures (secondary sources diverge: ">90% within a week" / "66% within two days"). Rather than pin a contested precise number, replaced with the verified-safe phrasing the sibling article uses for Batthyány: "the majority of patients dying within hours to days." Fixed.
7. **Xu 2023 — clinical-setting mischaracterization.** Body repeatedly framed the study as "during cardiac arrest" / "after the heart stopped" / "thirty seconds into cardiac arrest." The actual cohort: **four comatose dying patients monitored before and after withdrawal of ventilatory support** (cardiac arrest follows withdrawal). The "thirty seconds" duration is not a clean figure in the paper (surge spans multiple dying-process stages). Reworded to "before and after ventilatory support was withdrawn—the prelude to cardiac arrest" and softened the duration claim. The cite itself (PNAS 120(19)) is correct.

### Medium Issues Found (fixed)

Replaced the spurious-precision range with "on the order of tens of percent, deepening toward burst-suppression."
