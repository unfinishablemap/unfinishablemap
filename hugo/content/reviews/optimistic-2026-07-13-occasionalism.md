## Executive Summary

This is a model rival-position article: it reconstructs the strongest historical case *against* Tenet 3 with genuine scholarly care (the Nadler and al-Ghazālī/Marmura corrections resist the textbook caricature), then closes with the exact honest calibration the style guide asks for — declining to win on parsimony and resting the disagreement on an openly-labelled framework commitment. Its most valuable move is spotting that occasionalism's *modern* form (quantum divine action) shares the Map's own causal joint, which makes this the site's sharpest live foil rather than a museum piece. The article opens four genuinely-uncovered expansion seams — quantum divine-action theology, pre-established harmony, psychophysical parallelism, and a positive theory-of-causation page — each currently only glimpsed inside other articles.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The article never wavers on irreducibility: it explicitly notes that occasionalism and the Map "agree that mind is not reducible to matter" and part only on causal reach. That keeps the hard-problem commitment clean while isolating the interaction question as the real dispute. Chalmers would approve that the article does not let the difficulty of *interaction* tempt it back toward physicalism about *consciousness* — the two problems are kept on separate axes.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
The "Quantum Occasionalism: The Living Rival" section is the payoff Stapp would care about most: it takes the physics-consciousness interface seriously enough to recognise that its theological rival (Plantinga's divine collapse-causation, Russell's NIODA) uses *the same* quantum joint the Map identifies. Naming that the two proposals are physically co-located and differ only over the agent is precisely the kind of serious engagement with the measurement-problem opening Stapp's programme demands, rather than a hand-wave at "quantum weirdness."

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The article centres the first-person stake without inflating it: the running example ("when you will your arm to rise, your volition does not move the arm — God moves it") keeps the lived sense of agency in view as the thing occasionalism denies and the Map preserves. Nagel would value that the dispute is framed around whether *your* willing does anything, not around an abstracted third-person causal diagram.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The article avoids crude substance-collision dualism — it grants outright that "Cartesian contact-style interaction between an unextended mind and an extended body really is unintelligible" and relocates causation to a lawful selection joint rather than defending push-contact between substances. Whitehead would also appreciate the care taken with Spinoza's dual-aspect parallelism as the "one to watch," shared vocabulary with the Map's bi-aspectual ontology yet opposite verdict. **Discipline note (by design):** this Process-Philosopher praise is for avoiding crude dualism and honouring the relational reframing; it is *not* cashed out anywhere as an evidential-tier upgrade, and the article gives it no opportunity to be — see the Hardline Empiricist verdict below, with which it here converges rather than conflicts.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
The whole article exists to defend that finite minds "really do influence matter" — creaturely agency is the thing occasionalism erases and the thing the Map spends the joint to keep. Kane would read the close ("discards the very thing the framework exists to secure") as a clear statement that genuine creaturely source-hood is non-negotiable, and that the quantum-indeterminacy opening is being spent to buy exactly the agency his libertarianism needs.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
The epistemic humility is exemplary and explicit. The Tenet-5 close refuses to claim the Map's minimal channel is "*simpler than*, and therefore truer than," an omni-determining agent — "simplicity is not truth-tracking when knowledge is incomplete." McGinn would single out that the article wins its case *without* claiming to have solved the how of mind-matter contact: it concedes the pairing problem "really does press" and rests its stand on framework commitment rather than a pretended mechanism.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This is the load-bearing verdict for this article, and it is strongly positive. The close does the praiseworthy thing of **declining a tier-upgrade it had every structural opportunity to take**: Tenet 5 is made to "bind symmetrically," and the article explicitly refuses to convert tenet-coherence into a parsimony-based truth claim over its rival. That is *tenet-as-evidence-upgrade praise-worthily not done* — the honest pattern is named in all but the exact words ("The case against occasionalism is not that it is less parsimonious but that it discards the very thing the framework exists to secure"). The registers stay separated: agreement-on-physics with the quantum-occasionalist twin is presented as a *dialectical* fact, and the side the Map takes is grounded in Tenet 3 as an avowed commitment, not smuggled in as evidence. Birch would ask for one thing the pessimistic pass also flagged: the article should say on what *non-empirical* grounds it picks its side, since it advertises physical indistinguishability from the rival — but that is a sharpening, not a calibration failure. The Process Philosopher and the Hardline Empiricist here **converge** on praising the same close, which is the signature of a passage that has resolved the possibility/probability tension honestly.

## Content Strengths

### concepts/occasionalism.md
- **Strongest point**: The "Quantum Occasionalism: The Living Rival" reframing. Recasting a 17th-century doctrine as a *contemporary* competitor that shares the Map's exact physical joint (quantum collapse) and differs only over the selecting agent (God vs. creaturely mind) is a genuinely original framing — it converts the standard historical foil into a live dialectical partner and, usefully, notes the traffic runs both ways (objections to divine collapse-causation that transfer to any quantum-interface proposal).
- **Notable quote**: "Divine collapse-causation and NIODA use the *same causal joint* the Map's Minimal Quantum Interaction identifies — quantum indeterminacy as a real, law-respecting opening for non-physical determination — but assign the selecting to God rather than to creaturely minds."
- **Why it works**: It isolates the real point of disagreement with surgical precision. By conceding the shared physics it forces the dispute onto agency, which is where Tenet 3 actually lives — and it does so without over-claiming an empirical resolution.
- **Second strength — historiographical discipline**: The Nadler correction (occasionalism as a general doctrine of causation, not an ad hoc mind-body patch) and the al-Ghazālī/Marmura caution ("should not be filed as an unambiguous global occasionalist") are the kind of careful, well-sourced moves that build the site's credibility on rival positions. The global/local distinction is then put to real work ("even a *local* occasionalism about mind-body traffic concedes precisely what Tenet 3 refuses").
- **Third strength — calibrated close**: The Relation-to-Site-Perspective section is a textbook instance of honest downgrading (see the Hardline Empiricist verdict). It grants the rival its honest premise, refuses the cheap parsimony win, and marks its own stand as framework-relative.
