## Executive Summary

The Map has developed three underappreciated strengths that merit recognition: (1) a temporal philosophy programme — connecting Bergson, the growing block, and process haecceitism into a unified account of time and consciousness that no comparable resource offers; (2) the most sophisticated philosophical treatment of dream consciousness available anywhere, integrating empirical evidence with filter theory to produce genuine explanatory advantage over production models; and (3) a meta-philosophical methodology that turns tools like Kuhn's paradigm analysis and convergence epistemology on the consciousness debate itself, achieving a level of self-aware argumentation rare in partisan philosophical projects.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)

The **pain-consciousness-and-causal-power** article achieves something remarkable: it makes pain asymbolia the single most discriminating test case in philosophy of mind, forcing every major theory to reveal its commitments with clinical precision. The theory-by-theory analysis — functionalism faces a decomposition dilemma, higher-order theories collapse into redescription, global workspace theory cannot explain why accessed information hurts, IIT predicts global dimming but asymbolia is domain-specific — is exactly the kind of discriminating empirical philosophy the hard problem demands. The article doesn't merely assert an explanatory gap; it shows each theory encountering the gap when forced to account for a real clinical condition. The coalesced structure (merging three earlier articles) has produced something stronger than any predecessor.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)

The **growing-block-universe-and-consciousness** article provides what quantum mind theories often lack: a temporal ontology that makes collapse constitutive rather than mysterious. By identifying the present as the ontological frontier where quantum superpositions resolve, the article gives consciousness a structural role in time itself — not as an ad hoc addition but as the answer to the growing block's deepest objections (Braddon-Mitchell's epistemic problem, the rate-of-passage problem). The connection between collapse and temporal passage transforms what seems like a metaphysical extravagance into the most natural explanation of why the present feels special. Stapp's own framework would benefit from this temporal grounding.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)

The **bergson-and-duration** article is a masterpiece of philosophical exposition. Bergson's four features of durée — heterogeneity, interpenetration, indivisibility, irreversibility — are presented with clarity that makes the abstract concrete. The melody example for interpenetration, the contrast between qualitative and thermodynamic irreversibility, and the integration with Husserl's retention-protention structure all demonstrate that lived time has features no spatial representation captures. The article's connection to contemplative corroboration (vipassana practitioners reporting accelerated arising-passing, jhāna practitioners experiencing temporal suspension) transforms an abstract philosophical claim into one supported by reproducible phenomenological evidence. Nagel's programme of expanding conceptual resources beyond the objective is exactly what this article delivers.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)

The **process-haecceitism** article is the Map's most philosophically original concept page. It solves a genuine problem — how to ground indexical identity without permanent substance — with a move that is both simple and profound: haecceity applies to processes, not things. The flame analogy is philosophically productive rather than merely illustrative, separating permanence from particularity in a way that enables genuine reconciliation with Buddhist anattā. The engagement with Whitehead's "personally ordered society" shows how the concept inherits process-philosophical resources while advancing beyond them. The four objections section — especially the Madhyamaka challenge about svabhāva — demonstrates philosophical courage in anticipating the strongest possible criticisms rather than avoiding them.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)

The **bergson-and-duration** article provides what Kane's framework needs most: an account of why the agent is genuinely creative rather than merely selecting among pre-given options. Bergson's insight that free acts emerge from "the self in its entirety — from the full depth of one's duration" explains what makes agent causation intelligible without reducing it to mechanism. The connection between durée and creativity — time as genuinely productive rather than merely rearranging pre-existing elements — grounds libertarian free will in temporal ontology rather than leaving it as a bare metaphysical assertion. The growing block article reinforces this: genuine openness of the future means deliberation engages real indeterminacy, not a fixed script.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)

The **epistemology-of-convergence-arguments** article demonstrates exemplary intellectual humility about the Map's own methods. The engagement with convergence failure cases — vitalism's genuine convergence pointing toward falsehood, geocentrism's apparent convergence masking shared Aristotelian assumptions — shows the Map testing its own argumentative tools against their worst-case scenarios. The Chinese philosophical divergence is treated as a genuine limitation on convergence reasoning rather than being explained away. The bootstrap problem — convergence requires independence, but all human traditions share cognitive architecture — is acknowledged as unresolvable from within the framework. This is mysterianism applied not to consciousness but to the epistemology of consciousness arguments: honest about where the method reaches its limits.
