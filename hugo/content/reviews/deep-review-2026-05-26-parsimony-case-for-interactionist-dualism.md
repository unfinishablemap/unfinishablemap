## Pessimistic Analysis Summary

### Critical Issues Found

- **Tenet 5 self-undermining / possibility-probability slippage (calibration error).** The article's load-bearing conclusions used parsimony as a *truth-tracking* heuristic to argue FOR dualism: "interactionist dualism emerges as the more parsimonious position" (opening), "those who value simplicity should *prefer* interactionist dualism" (Relation to Site Perspective), "the parsimony objection ... is not merely neutralised. It is reversed" (conclusion). The article *nowhere* invoked Tenet 5's self-binding — a grep for "truth-track / reliable guide / unreliable / heuristic" returned nothing in the body. Yet tenets.md line 112 states the Map "cannot consistently invoke simplicity to disarm parsimony arguments *against* dualism while invoking parsimony *against* many-worlds," and line 66 distinguishes truth-tracking parsimony (bound by Tenet 5) from constraint-respecting minimality (not bound). **Diagnostic test applied:** would a reviewer who fully accepts the Map's tenets still flag "should prefer dualism on simplicity grounds" / "the objection is reversed" as overstated? YES — Tenet 5 forbids treating the simpler theory as therefore truer in this domain, symmetrically for and against dualism. This is a calibration error correctable inside the framework, not bedrock disagreement. **Resolution:** reframed the entire thesis as *dialectical* — an internal critique of the objector who trusts parsimony ("granting that trust for the sake of argument... the objector's own weapon, wielded honestly, does not deliver the verdict it was raised to deliver"). Added an explicit Tenet-5 self-binding paragraph to the opening ("The Map does not endorse the inference *simpler-therefore-truer* here"). Rewrote the Dimensional Reversal close, the Relation-to-Site paragraphs, and the conclusion to the "clears space for, does not install dualism; the Map's positive case rests on its other arguments" pattern, mirroring the parsimony-void recalibration. The substantive arguments (Zanotti, Hubbard, Koksvik, Anderson, Chalmers, the three debts, idealism symmetry) are all preserved — only their epistemic register changed from "establishes dualism" to "disarms the objection on its own terms."

- **Anderson citation error (bibliographic factual error).** Reference 1 read "Anderson, C. J. (2002). ... *Journal of Theoretical and Philosophical Psychology*, 22(2), 149–168." Web verification (PhilPapers CHRCOR, APA PsycNet record 2002-10963-003, SSRN abstract 895730, multiple search summaries) converges on the correct locator: **21(1), 46–60, 2001**. The "22(2), 149–168 / 2002" string was wrong in year, volume, issue, and pages — it survived five prior reviews because the 2026-03-24 reviewer verified the *content* attribution (correct) and recorded "journal publication 2002" without checking volume/pages. The 2002 figure is the APA database *indexing* year, not the publication year. **Resolution:** corrected to "Anderson, C. J. (2001). ... A Critical Analysis of the Raze Dualism Argument for Materialism. ... 21(1), 46–60." Content attribution (idealism symmetry, Churchland "Raze Dualism" critique) was and remains accurate.
