## Critiques by Philosopher

### The Eliminative Materialist (Churchland)
Would press that the article's central engine — the "either biological organisation produces experience (= reductive physicalism) or it does not (= points to dualism)" dilemma at line 94 — quietly assumes the explanatory gap is real rather than a placeholder for incomplete neuroscience. **Assessment: this is a by-design Map commitment (the gap is argued for elsewhere, not asserted here), and the article explicitly flags the dilemma as non-forced at line 96. Not a defect.** The article already grants the eliminativist nothing it has not earned and names the underdetermination.

### The Hard-Nosed Physicalist (Dennett)
Would attack line 116's "Occam's Razor Has Limits" closing — "Claiming to have dissolved the mind-body problem while acknowledging that experience is irreducible does not reduce ontological commitments — it conceals them." Dennett would say this begs the question against the enactivist, who denies that "experience is irreducible" entails any hidden *ontological* commitment (it may be a methodological/transcendental claim). **Assessment: the article actually concedes exactly this at line 96 (the sui-generis "third option"). The tension is that the line-116 tenet-section phrasing is more confident ("conceals them") than the line-96 body concession ("the confidence of the better-supported reading"). Minor register-consistency point, not a critical issue** — the body has done the calibration work; the tenet section's punchier closing is acceptable rhetoric given the prior hedging.

### The Quantum Skeptic (Tegmark)
Little purchase here — the article correctly notes (line 112) that enactivists do not engage quantum-interaction models and that the Map's MQI hypothesis "operates at a level of description enactivism does not address." Honest framework-boundary marking, not substitution. No defect.

### The Many-Worlds Defender (Deutsch)
Line 114 claims enactivism's indexical phenomenology "is compatible with the Map's rejection of many-worlds." Deutsch would say compatibility is cheap — enactivism takes no stance on QM, so this is a non-result dressed as convergence. **Assessment: the article says exactly "Enactivism does not directly engage quantum interpretation" first, so it is not overclaiming; the compatibility note is appropriately weak. Not a defect.**

### The Empiricist (Popper's ghost)
Would ask what would falsify the claim that enactivism's irreducibility "points toward dualism." **Assessment: the article's line-96 underdetermination paragraph IS the Popperian honesty — it states the standpoint is underdetermined by evidence both sides accept. By-design evidential-status discipline, not an unfalsifiability defect.**

### The Buddhist Philosopher (Nagarjuna)
Strongest live counterargument the article does NOT address: Thompson's enactivism has explicit Madhyamaka roots (the closing chapters of *The Embodied Mind* deploy Nagarjuna's emptiness/groundlessness precisely to dissolve the self the Map's dualism wants to preserve). The article cites *The Embodied Mind* but never engages its Buddhist anti-foundationalism — the move that would say the "irreducible experiencer" the Map reads as evidence for dualism is itself the reified self enactivism deconstructs. **Assessment: a genuine engagement gap (see Counterarguments below), but expansion-scope, not a defect — and the article is near a sensible length; flag as optional, not actionable now.**
