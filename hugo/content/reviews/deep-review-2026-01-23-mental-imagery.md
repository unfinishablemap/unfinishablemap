## Pessimistic Analysis Summary

### Six Philosopher Perspectives

**Eliminative Materialist (Churchland)**: The article assumes phenomenal consciousness as given. The claim that LPFC engagement constitutes "conscious control" could be questioned—it might simply be more complex neural processing.

**Hard-Nosed Physicalist (Dennett)**: The voluntary/involuntary distinction is presented as tracking conscious/unconscious, but both could be neural processes with different access properties. The article doesn't fully engage with this challenge.

**Quantum Skeptic (Tegmark)**: The article lacks explicit discussion of decoherence timescales. The quantum mechanism connection in "Relation to Site Perspective" was underdeveloped.

**Many-Worlds Defender (Deutsch)**: No significant issues—the article doesn't claim quantum selection directly.

**Empiricist (Popper)**: The motor imagery citation originally said "significantly improves motor performance" without quantification. Needed more precision on effect sizes.

**Buddhist Philosopher (Nagarjuna)**: The article assumes a controller doing the visualizing without examining the nature of this controller.
