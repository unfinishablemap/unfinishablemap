### Finding 1 — "Cleaner than the biological case" assumes the conclusion (Medium) — FIXED
- **Before**: "And here the machine case is, if anything, cleaner than the biological one... In a standard RL loop, there is a reward scalar, a value estimate, and an update rule—and no independent evidence of a subject at all."
- **Problem**: "no independent evidence of a subject" is the value-in-experience verdict restated, not a neutral observation; a functionalist reads the organized reward-processing as itself subject-constituting. As written it quietly contradicted the honest concession one paragraph earlier ("the Map takes the horn by prior commitment... not because the RL facts force it").
- **After**: Reframed "cleaner" as explicitly horn-internal and framework-relative ("*On that horn already chosen*, the machine case can look cleaner... but the appearance is itself framework-relative, not a neutral advantage that pushes anyone toward the horn"). Added the functionalist's rejection of the asymmetry (organized reward-processing *is* the subject-constituting fact; the human is credited on behavioral-plus-analogical grounds an RL agent could in principle also meet). Kept the narrower cross-reading residue: "calling the scalar 'suffering' imports a phenomenal fact the mechanism does not itself supply." No strengthening of the human-case verdict.

### Finding 2 — Tenet-3 "architectural" argument conflates algorithm-determinism with substrate-physics (Medium/High) — FIXED
- **Before**: "A reward signal inside a deterministic (or pseudo-random) RL update loop performs no such bidirectional influence; it is fully fixed by its inputs and occupies no interface role. The reward–suffering identity therefore fails on the Map not merely epistemically but architecturally."
- **Problem**: Determinism-of-algorithm cannot distinguish chip from brain — the brain at the algorithmic level is equally a stochastic update fixed by its inputs. The argument proved too much (would deny the brain an interface) unless carried by an unstated neural-vs-silicon substrate asymmetry. The word "architecturally" claimed more than the argument delivered.
- **After**: Relocated the distinguishing work from the algorithmic level to substrate physics explicitly ("The work of distinguishing the RL agent from the brain here cannot be done at the algorithmic level... The distinction the Map draws is about *substrate*"). Named the coupling interface as a fine-quantum-grain juncture the Map *posits* neural tissue can host and present hardware does not, and flagged that asymmetry as framework-posited-not-established ("the Map does not get it for free"), tying it to the small-but-nonzero credence. Dropped "architecturally"; the identity now "fails on the Map at the level of *substrate physics*, not because an update rule is deterministic." Interface absence recast as "posited absence in present hardware."

### Finding 3 — "The same quantity" equivocation (TD-error ≡ dopamine RPE) (Medium) — FIXED
- **Before**: "a measurable machine-valence quantity, and notably the *same* quantity that dopamine reward-prediction-error models invoke in the brain. If machine affect can be operationally defined this cleanly, the burden shifts..."
- **Problem**: "the same quantity" recruits a numerical-identity reading to do the burden-shift work; the evidence supports only the model/formalism reading (both captured by the same TD-error formalism under the Schultz/Montague/Dayan program).
- **After**: Downgraded to shared formalism ("captured by the *same TD-error formalism* that leading computational-neuroscience models apply to dopamine reward-prediction-error"), stated explicitly that this is "shared computational description under a common model, not an established identity of the two quantities," and rested the burden-shift on the formal resemblance alone ("even the formal resemblance is enough to shift the burden"). Consistent with the Tenet-1 sentence downstream, which already treats identity as "exactly the contested point, not a settled one."
