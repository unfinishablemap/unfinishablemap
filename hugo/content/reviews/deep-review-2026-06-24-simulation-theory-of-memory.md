### Critical Issues Found

**1. Wrong-author + wrong-venue misattribution (Sant'Anna → Michaelian).** The body (former line 53) and References entry attributed *"Radicalizing simulationism: Remembering as imagining the (nonpersonal) past"* to **André Sant'Anna (2022)**. The cited venue "Philosophical Psychology 35(8), 1235–1255" was also wrong. **Resolution**: re-attributed the body claim to Michaelian (2022); corrected the reference to *Philosophical Psychology* 37(5), 1170–1196 (print 2024; online-first 2022), DOI 10.1080/09515089.2022.2082934. This is the classic wrong-author defect class — author+year intra-corpus matching ratifies it; only live publisher search catches it.

**2. Unverifiable verbatim quotations attributed to Schacter, Addis & Buckner (2008).** Three quoted strings were not found verbatim in the cited 2008 paper: "the constituent elements of episodic memories are stored independently"; "facilitates mental simulation, as details are easily extractable…and can be recombined…"; and "constructive and prospective rather than archival" (the word *archival* does not appear in either the 2008 or 2007 paper). The underlying *claims* are faithful to the constructive-episodic-simulation literature (the 2007 paper does say the system "flexibly extracts and recombines elements of previous experiences"), but presenting unfound strings as verbatim quotes is a fabricated-quote defect. **Resolution**: reworded the passage as paraphrase, attributing the recombination point to Schacter & Addis (2007) and the constituent-elements framing to the 2008 account, dropping all quote marks.

**3. **4. Orphan reference (Addis et al., 2009).** Listed in References but never cited inline (inline↔References integrity, §2.4 step 5). **Resolution**: added a brief inline cite at the shared-core-network claim — the 2009 paper specifically establishes distinct subsystems for remembering vs imagining within the shared network, which fits the spot.
