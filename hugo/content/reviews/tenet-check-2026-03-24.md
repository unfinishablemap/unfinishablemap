## Warnings

### concepts/objectivity-and-consciousness.md (Tenet 1: Dualism)
- **Tenet violated**: Dualism
- **Issue**: Implicit physicalist assumption about consciousness origins in the "Why Not Idealism?" section
- **Quote**: "Consciousness evolved in organisms because those organisms needed to navigate a physical world. Biological evolution presupposes physical processes operating before consciousness emerged."
- **Context**: This is presented as the Map's own position, but it implicitly assumes consciousness was *produced* by biological evolution — a physicalist framing. The site's dualist account (articulated in evolution-of-consciousness.md, interactionist-dualism.md) holds that evolution built neural *interfaces*, not consciousness itself: "Brains don't generate consciousness; they channel it... What evolved was not consciousness itself but increasingly sophisticated consciousness-brain coupling."
- **Recommendation**: Reframe to note that under dualism, physical processes precede consciousness-brain *coupling*, not that consciousness is a product of evolution.

### apex/attention-as-causal-bridge.md (Tenet 3: Bidirectional Interaction)
- **Tenet violated**: Bidirectional Interaction
- **Issue**: Unqualified assertion that consciousness is causally inert in exogenous attention
- **Quote**: "Exogenous attention (~100ms): a loud noise, a flash of light. The brain captures attention reflexively through the ventral attention network. Consciousness is along for the ride."
- **Context**: "Along for the ride" is the exact phrasing the tenet rules out. No framing indicates this is an opponent's view, a contested claim, or only a partial description. The contrast with willed attention exists later, but this sentence lands as a bald positive claim.
- **Recommendation**: Add qualification — e.g., "In these cases, consciousness initially tracks rather than drives the shift, though it may subsequently modulate the response" — or note that this is a simplification for expository purposes.

### topics/machine-consciousness.md (Tenet 3: Bidirectional Interaction)
- **Tenet violated**: Bidirectional Interaction
- **Issue**: "One-Way Consciousness" section gives extended positive treatment to causally impotent consciousness as a live possibility
- **Quote**: "If one-way consciousness is possible—experience occurring without the ability to influence physical processes—then an upload might preserve subjective experience even in a deterministic classical substrate. The upload would be conscious but causally impotent."
- **Context**: Previously flagged by tenet-check reviews (2026-03-17, 2026-03-22). The article labels it speculative and notes it "would undermine" the tenet, but the extended serious treatment creates stronger tension than the hedging resolves.
- **Recommendation**: Strengthen the framing to make clear this is a hypothetical that the Map's tenets resist, or shorten the treatment to reduce the impression of endorsement.
