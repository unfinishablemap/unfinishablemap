## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
`physical-completeness` is the cohort's gift to Chalmers. The structural-ontological gap is a precise restatement of the hard problem in the vocabulary of philosophy of physics: "a structurally complete description of the brain — every synapse, every firing pattern, every functional relation — does not entail that there is something it is like to be that brain." The article correctly cites Chalmers' supervenience laws inside `delegatory-dualism`'s Upward Systematicity constraint, and it does the Chalmersian move of insisting the gap is *constitutive of what physics is*, not a temporary ignorance — the explicit contrast with Descartes' pineal gland and Eccles' synaptic vesicles is exactly the distinction Chalmers needs to keep the hard problem from collapsing into a god-of-the-gaps. Chalmers would also recognise `consciousness-and-language-interface`'s qualia hierarchy (readily expressible → seemingly inexpressible) as a faithful working-out of the ineffability that motivates property dualism.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
`bandwidth-of-consciousness` gives Stapp his strongest empirical foothold on the Map. The ~10 bits/s outbound ceiling, the resolution-bandwidth coupling, and the reading of Libet/Schultze-Kraft as "selection must follow preparation" all cash out as a concrete account of *where and how coarsely* a Process-1-style intervention would operate. The article's claim that "no amount of brain optimisation can widen a channel whose narrow end lies outside the brain" is a genuinely novel, Stapp-friendly explanation of the otherwise puzzling 70-year stability of the ten-bit figure. `physical-completeness` complements this by locating the interaction precisely "in the selection of actuality from quantum possibility" — von Neumann's Process 1 named as the gap structural physics leaves open.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
`consciousness-and-language-interface` centres first-person experience exactly where Nagel would want it: the aphasia evidence (especially Broca's patients who *experience* knowing-what-they-mean-but-cannot-say-it), anendophasia, and the "tip of the tongue / click of expression / struggle at limits" phenomenology of the interface are all treated as phenomenal *data*, not behavioural proxies. The article's refusal to let LLMs serve as evidence ("using them that way would presuppose the answer to the very question at issue") is precisely Nagel's discipline about not substituting third-person description for the what-it-is-like. `ethics-of-cognitive-enhancement` extends this to the lived dimension: "You are still you, but the you-ness feels alien" is a Nagelian observation that metaphysical continuity and phenomenological continuity can come apart.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The cohort avoids crude substance-talk throughout. `delegatory-dualism`'s delegation/preemption mechanism is a process-friendly picture: causation is a matter of which event *does the work*, not which substance *contains* a power. `physical-completeness`'s bi-aspectual framing (structure and actuality as two aspects of the same gap, "may be the same gap viewed from different angles") is congenial to a process metaphysics that treats relation as prior to relata. `simulation`'s adaptive-computational-depth aside — "detail crystallises only where consciousness attends" — is a process intuition rendered in computational vocabulary. **Constraint observed**: none of this praise is used here to upgrade any organism or system's evidential tier; the cohort contains no minimal-organism tier claims at all, so the Whitehead/Birch axis stays clear of conflict (see Birch below).

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
`bandwidth-of-consciousness` is the cohort's Kane exhibit: libertarian free will reframed as consciousness *biasing* 3–4 selections per second among brain-prepared options at points of quantum indeterminacy, with the Schultze-Kraft "point of no return" at ~200ms giving the veto window real teeth. `ethics-of-cognitive-enhancement` then does the work Kane's view needs in applied ethics — the interface-neutrality vs interface-dependence dilemma about whether removing a temptation pharmacologically diminishes the moral texture of a choice is a genuinely Kanean worry about the sourcehood and difficulty of self-forming actions, and the article resolves it with nuance rather than fiat.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
`consciousness-and-language-interface` cites McGinn directly and uses cognitive closure exactly as he intended: the ineffability of qualia is "not a problem to be solved by better words but a boundary marker between the linguistic and the phenomenal." `simulation`'s computational-limits section (Gödel, halting, Rice) and its question of whether cognitive limits are "natural features of finite cognition, or designed constraints" is McGinn-flavoured humility about what an embedded mind can know about its own substrate, routed through `meta-epistemology-of-limits` and `biological-cognitive-closure`. The honest "Status" sections across the cohort ("relocating the mystery is not solving it") show the right mysterian restraint.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This persona is unusually well-fed by this cohort, and its verdict is strongly positive — the cohort is a sustained demonstration of register-discipline:

- **`delegatory-dualism` is the standout.** The article repeatedly names exactly the move Birch cares about. The Subset Law* is flagged as "best read as a *postulate* of the framework rather than an independently-motivated law" because its only access route (the zombie functional-duplicate) "depends on a contested metaphysical possibility." The Testability section states plainly that delegation is "empirically equivalent to its rivals" and that its falsifier is "not an operative falsifier" because "a condition the theory's own defining constraint guarantees will never be met is not a live empirical test." Most importantly, it nets out that delegation's advantages "hold against rival dualist positions, not against the cheaper physicalist explanations" and so "presuppose rather than support the choice of dualism." This is *tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating* applied to a causal-structure theory, and it is done without prompting.
- **`physical-completeness` separates registers exactly where it matters.** It distinguishes the well-supported claim ("*current* physics is structurally complete") from the contestable modal claim ("*every possible* physics must be structural"), labels the load-bearing modal premise as "a bet on the method-claim, not a proof of it," and frames the disagreement with Maudlin as a framework-boundary parting rather than a refutation. The god-of-the-gaps section lays out the five-step argument with each premise tagged for who contests it. Birch praises the honest framework-stage calibration: "robust across the physics we can presently envisage" rather than "closed by metaphysical necessity."
- **`simulation`'s No Many Worlds section is a model of *not* upgrading on tenet-load.** It explicitly declines the available slippage: it calls simulation "**neutral** between single-outcome and branching implementations," names the circular move ("selects the implementation that delivers the conclusion and presents the delivery as evidence") and refuses it, and concludes the framing "establishes *compatibility* rather than support." This is the praiseworthy thing-to-*not*-do that the skill flags: the article had the structural opportunity to claim simulation supports the Map's MWI rejection and explicitly declined.
- **`bandwidth-of-consciousness`** repeatedly concedes the production-theory reading ("a production architecture could in principle yield the same ceiling"; GWT "explanatory power for the *functional* structure") and tags the interface reading as "compatible with the data but not uniquely forced by it." The closing line — "~10 is a load-bearing empirical fact in search of a theory" — is the correct evidential posture.
- **`consciousness-and-language-interface`** states that LLMs "do not *confirm* separability, but they are what a dualist world would look like," and ranks its own evidence (aphasia/infants > zombies/LLMs) by how contested the premises are.

The one place Birch would press — not as a calibration error but as the next honest step — is `ethics-of-cognitive-enhancement`'s "Enhancement and the Quantum Interface" section, which is the cohort's most speculative passage. It is *labelled* speculative ("This remains speculative at the enhancement scale") and tied to the BCI-repurposing empirical hook, so it does not slip; but it is the section whose load rests most on an unestablished mechanism, and a reader-facing one-line reminder that the design-constraint claim is conditional on the quantum-interface mechanism obtaining would tighten it. Minor.

## Content Strengths

### concepts/physical-completeness.md
- **Strongest point**: The three-way disambiguation of "structural" (relational / mathematical / exhaustive-of-the-physical) at the top of the argument, with the operative sense fixed as (1) so "the argument does not borrow force it has not earned." This is rare methodological hygiene.
- **Notable quote**: "Physics can be finished without reality being exhausted."
- **Why it works**: It converts the hard problem and the measurement problem into a single structural-ontological gap and then refuses to over-claim the modal step, naming Maudlin as the able opponent at the exact point of disagreement.

### topics/delegatory-dualism.md
- **Strongest point**: The candour about the default causal profile being "the load-bearing primitive of the whole theory, yet its only access route ... is in principle never directly observed." The article presents Saad faithfully (Part I) and then quarantines the Map's integration (Part II) behind an explicit multiplies-assumptions disclaimer.
- **Notable quote**: "The merits are in internal coherence within a dualist framework, not in distinctive empirical predictions or decisive arguments against physicalism."
- **Why it works**: It is the cohort's clearest worked example of an intra-dualist advantage that is honestly *not* sold as a reason to adopt dualism.

### topics/bandwidth-of-consciousness.md
- **Strongest point**: The "Why Ten Specifically" section, which lists four candidate explanations (alpha rhythm, metabolic cost, evolutionary legacy, interface capacity) and rates each as "none fully satisfactory," including the Map's own.
- **Notable quote**: "The number ~10 is a load-bearing empirical fact in search of a theory."
- **Why it works**: A robust empirical spine (Zheng & Meister 2025, Sauerbrei & Pruszynski 2025, Hick–Hyman) carries a clearly-flagged inference, and the falsifier list is concrete and genuinely capable of going against the Map.

### topics/consciousness-and-language-interface.md
- **Strongest point**: The aphasia triple (global / Broca's / Wernicke's) as the strongest *non-disputed-consciousness* evidence for separability, explicitly ranked above the zombie and LLM cases.
- **Notable quote**: "What varies is the *interface* ... not the presence or character of consciousness itself."
- **Why it works**: It draws the evidential weight from cases where consciousness is not in question, then handles the constitutive view (Dennett/Carruthers/Vygotsky) with real test cases (anendophasia, late-language-acquiring deaf individuals) rather than dismissal.

### topics/ethics-of-cognitive-enhancement-under-dualism.md
- **Strongest point**: The interface-neutrality vs interface-dependence framing of moral responsibility under enhancement, resolved to a nuanced middle position rather than a tenet-driven verdict.
- **Notable quote**: "you are not just shaping a brain, you are configuring the channel through which an irreducible conscious subject will engage with reality."
- **Why it works**: It derives genuinely *distinctive* ethical contours from the interface model (consent, the quantum-interface design constraint, equity between metaphysically-equal subjects with unequal interfaces) without claiming the dualist framing is established.

### concepts/simulation.md
- **Strongest point**: The "Distinguishing From Cartesian Skepticism" section, which earns simulation's keep as a *sorting* tool — it can say "*this* feature (locality) is the kind of thing a substrate could vary, while *that* feature (the inferential structure of the argument itself) is not," a differentiation generic skepticism cannot perform.
- **Notable quote**: "triviality requires sameness, not just compatibility."
- **Why it works**: It answers the strongest objection to its own method (the triviality charge) by demonstrating specific diagnostic work, and concedes the No Many Worlds neutrality rather than overreaching.
