### Counterarguments Considered

- **Eliminative Materialist**: The dissociations could reflect independent neural subsystems rather than a consciousness-interface architecture. The article handles this by noting the implicit processing gap, but the eliminativist could claim implicit processing *is* the relevant processing and "conscious access" is an illusion. This is a bedrock philosophical disagreement.

- **Dennett-style functionalist**: Patient reports of "preserved consciousness" are heterophenomenological data about beliefs, not direct evidence of a separate consciousness. The article's reliance on patient self-report is acknowledged in the epistemological challenge section.

- **Quantum Skeptic**: The MQI tenet connection via dopamine thresholds is speculative. The article appropriately uses indirect/conditional language ("gains indirect support," "consistent with").

- **Empiricist**: The falsifiability section is honest but could be sharper about what specific experimental setup would distinguish interface-degradation from consciousness-alteration. Deferred—the epistemological challenge section acknowledges this as genuinely unresolvable from inside.
