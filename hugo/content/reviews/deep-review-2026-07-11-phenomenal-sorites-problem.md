## Critical Issue Addressed — Interface-Threshold / Existence-Fact Conflation

The pessimistic cluster review's High-severity finding was a genuine cross-article contradiction. The sorites article (L71/L77) recruited the specific [interface-threshold](/concepts/interface-threshold/) concept — "a simple, non-graded interface-threshold relation" — to secure the on/off SUBJECT-EXISTENCE fact. But interface-threshold.md's own body says its threshold is an EFFICACY/selection-grade boundary with "phenomenal consciousness may well be present" on BOTH sides ("not that consciousness arises but that consciousness becomes effective"). Recruiting it for the existence fact was a category error. Two compounding problems: (i) sorites L27 first set interface-threshold aside as merely mechanistic, then L71 re-imported it for the metaphysical job — internal slippage; (ii) the reciprocal back-link on interface-threshold.md L128 framed interface-threshold AS "the on/off consciousness-fact coupling," contradicting its own body.

**Resolution — option (b), explicit disambiguation across both files:**

1. **Sorites L71 (core rewrite)**: The sharp fact is now located in "the simple, non-graded engagement of the non-physical mind-brain coupling itself — whether that coupling engages at all." interface-threshold is explicitly demoted to "a distinct and prior boundary from" this — "which marks where an already-engaged coupling becomes selection-effective: that threshold has phenomenal consciousness present on both sides and settles what a subject's experience can do, not whether a subject exists, so it is not the construct that secures the on/off fact." The existence job is now the general coupling relation; interface-threshold is retained as a labelled contrast, not as the securing construct.
2. **Sorites L27**: reworded so interface-threshold is characterised up front as "where an already-engaged coupling becomes selection-effective — an efficacy boundary with phenomenal consciousness present on both sides" vs. the prior existence question. This discharges the L27/L71 self-slippage: both passages now treat interface-threshold as the efficacy boundary, never the existence boundary.
3. **Sorites L77 (Further Reading gloss)**: changed from "the simple, non-graded coupling the Map uses to secure a sharp subject-fact" to "a distinct, later efficacy boundary with consciousness present on both sides; contrasted here with the coupling's mere engagement, which is what secures the on/off subject-fact."
4. **interface-threshold.md L128 (reciprocal back-link)**: changed from "Why the on/off consciousness-fact needs a simple, non-graded coupling" to "The prior question of whether a subject exists at all, secured by the coupling's mere engagement rather than by this efficacy threshold, which has consciousness present on both sides." Neither file now claims interface-threshold secures the existence fact.

## Secondary Issue Addressed — Composition Antony-Conditional Debt (pessimistic finding 2)

The article invokes Antony's conditional (sharpness-plus-correctness sinks complex-property theories) but did not discharge the tension that composition-and-consciousness grounds its convergence in GRADED proxies (integrated information Φ, organic "life"). Added a bridging clause to the L71 paragraph: since by Antony's conditional those graded proxies cannot themselves carry the on/off fact, the sharpness composition-and-consciousness borrows must trace back to the non-graded coupling, not to the graded criteria. This converts a latent contradiction into an explicit, resolved dependency (the Map is motivated to resist ontic vagueness precisely because composition-and-consciousness leans on phenomenal sharpness).
