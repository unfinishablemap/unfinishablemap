## Executive Summary

This cluster is the strongest calibration exhibit in the corpus. Each article rigorously separates *what an argument delivers* from *what the Map wants it to deliver*, and — remarkably — the Map's own arguments get the same discipline as its opponents': the Consequence Argument is stated as clearing the ground for incompatibilism *only*, the manipulation argument is named the Map's "most dangerous opponent" and given at full strength, and the interface reply is filed as "a contested bet rather than a demonstration." The rare condition holds where the Process Philosopher and the Hardline Empiricist praise the *same* passages, which is the signature of possibility/probability slippage having been designed out rather than papered over. The main opportunity is consolidation (an apex or hub that displays the now-complete leeway → source → structural map), not new argument-articles.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The cluster keeps the explanatory work honest about where dualism does and does not enter. The consequence article explicitly refuses to let the argument "vindicate the Map's dualist picture" — dualism is not smuggled in as the payoff of a neutral modal argument. Chalmers would approve that the hard problem's cousin here (the sourcehood gap) is treated as a real gap without being oversold as proof of the non-physical.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
The manipulation and consequence articles give the quantum interface exactly the role Stapp's program needs: consciousness *settling* which of the physically-open post-decoherence outcomes becomes actual, framed as a genuine selection mechanism rather than a randomiser. The Lewis "local miracle" reframing — reading the divergence miracle as "a minimally owned one" authored by consciousness — is an original and physics-literate touch that turns a canonical compatibilist device into a mirror of interactionism.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
Frankfurt's willing/unwilling/wanton addict trichotomy is presented with its first-person texture intact — the unwilling addict "acts under a force he experiences as external to himself." The identification/alienation distinction centres what it is *like* to own or disown one's own will, which is precisely the register Nagel insists third-person taxonomy cannot replace.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
The mesh article resists crude substance-talk: freedom is located in a *synchronic relation among mental states*, a matter of fit between levels rather than a thing possessed. Crucially, the Whiteheadian sympathy is kept off the evidential axis — the source-versus-leeway article's "wide source incompatibilism" placement is argued on the Map's independent commitments, never on process-philosophical resonance. Nothing here is upgraded on metaphysical-compatibility grounds.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
Kane's event-causal libertarianism is named and steelmanned as the target of the disappearing-agent objection, and the cluster takes agency seriously enough to concede where Kane is vulnerable (the luck/rollback worry) rather than declaring victory. The Map's agent-causal upgrade over event-causal libertarianism is presented as answering the disappearing-agent objection "by supplying a genuine originator" — Kane's project honoured and then extended.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
Epistemic humility is structural throughout: "the dialectic remains open into the 2020s"; "no formulation has won consensus, and none has been shown to be beyond repair"; the interface bias-vs-settling question is registered as "a live hypothesis pending a toy model, not an established result." The cluster repeatedly stops at the edge of what anyone can currently establish and says so.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
**This is the load-bearing verdict for this cluster, and it is emphatic praise.** These five articles are a working demonstration of the discipline the persona exists to reward:

- **Tenet-as-evidence-upgrade is praise-worthily *not* done, repeatedly and by name.** The mesh article: "not a sourcehood-shaped hole that only dualism can fill, and the Map does not read it as evidence for dualism. It borrows the *structure* of the manipulation argument, not its metaphysical conclusion." The source/leeway article: "nothing about carving incompatibilism into leeway and source families favors dualism, and the Map should not present it as though it did." Each article had the structural opportunity to slide its conclusion toward dualism on tenet-load and explicitly declined.
- **The Tenet-5 move is stated in its honest, symmetric form every time.** Where the Map invokes "Occam's Razor Has Limits" to decline Pereboom's parsimony verdict, it immediately concedes "this move buys no positive support for agent causation either" and "It blocks the objection from being a refutation; it does not convert the Map's bet into a demonstration." This is the *"tenet removes a defeater but does not upgrade evidential status"* pattern executed precisely.
- **The opponent's hand is called strong.** "Pereboom's objection to libertarian control remains forceful and unresolved"; "the Map places its bet there knowingly, and grants that the opponent's hand is strong." The wild-coincidences objection is booked as "a real cost the Map's framework pays, not one it has discharged."
- **Framework-stage honesty.** The interface's statistical-invisibility is named as "the near neighbour of Pereboom's complaint that indeterministic control is indistinguishable from luck" — the article identifies its own most damaging structural resemblance rather than hoping the reader misses it.

No calibration flag arises anywhere in this cluster. The Process/Empiricist tension is resolved, not evaded.

## Content Strengths

### the-consequence-argument-for-incompatibilism.md
- **Strongest point**: The "What the Argument Establishes — and What It Does Not" section, which insists the hard determinist and the libertarian *share* the argument and diverge only on a premise it is silent about. This is the clearest single statement of leeway-scope discipline in the corpus.
- **Notable quote**: "The Consequence Argument clears the ground; the indeterminist premise and the agent-causal account do the positive building. Presenting the argument as though it *proved* the Map's dualism would be exactly the over-reading to avoid."
- **Why it works**: It front-loads the scope limit in the second paragraph (truncation-resilient), then earns it with the McKay–Johnson agglomeration counterexample and the Lewis local-miracle objection given at full technical strength.

### the-manipulation-argument-and-hard-incompatibilism.md
- **Strongest point**: Framing hard incompatibilism as a *partial ally* — "the Map's most dangerous free-will opponent precisely because it is a partial ally" — which correctly diagnoses that the threat is entirely on the second (anti-libertarian) front, not the shared incompatibilist premise.
- **Notable quote**: "determinism rules out basic-desert responsibility (the four-case argument); event-causal indeterminism cannot restore it (luck); and agent-causal indeterminism, which could restore it, is empirically unmotivated (wild coincidences)."
- **Why it works**: It gives both the soft-line and hard-line compatibilist replies real weight, and it does not pretend the interface reply defeats Pereboom — it offers it "as one contested answer, not a refutation."

### source-versus-leeway-incompatibilism.md
- **Strongest point**: The "Source Incompatibilism Is Not Libertarianism" section, using Pereboom as the decisive counterexample (a source incompatibilist who denies we have free will). This pre-empts the single most common conflation in the area.
- **Notable quote**: "Libertarians and hard incompatibilists therefore *share* the source premise and diverge only on whether it is satisfiable."
- **Why it works**: The article functions as a genuine hub — it explicitly maps the Map's three argument-articles onto the leeway/source/pivot structure, so a reader arriving at any one can locate the whole.

### frankfurt-hierarchical-mesh-theory-of-the-will.md
- **Strongest point**: The disambiguation of Frankfurt's *two* 1969-vs-1971 contributions up front, refusing to let the cases and the hierarchical theory be conflated — then locating the mesh theory as "a third thing that brackets both" leeway and source.
- **Notable quote**: "the mesh cannot by itself certify that the will is *authored* rather than merely *arranged*."
- **Why it works**: It presents a rival compatibilism "at full strength as a paradigm," names Watson's regress objection and Frankfurt's wholeheartedness reply as "live, not settled," and draws the source-incompatibilist wedge without claiming the wedge is dualism's property.

### luck-objection.md
- **Strongest point**: Naming both conditions libertarianism must satisfy (alternative possibilities *and* sourcehood) and treating the objection as the genuinely harder of the Map's two problems — the consequence article even flags that "clearing the compatibilist field does nothing to answer it."
- **Why it works**: It sets up the selection-not-randomness reply while conceding the reply is contested, keeping the register honest.
