### Medium Issues Found
- **Dissociation-language precision on the fresh J-space paragraph (fixed).** The new paragraph called the J-space result "the strongest empirical case to date that A-consciousness and P-consciousness *come apart* in a real system." The J-space case shows access fully instantiated with the phenomenal question left *open* — phenomenal consciousness is not shown absent — so it is a case of *separability*, not a demonstrated Block-style dissociation. The article itself reserves "come apart" for genuine dissociation (§dissociations, "If they sometimes come apart, they cannot be identical"), so the phrasing was internally inconsistent as well as slightly overstated. Reworded to "the strongest empirical case to date for the *separability* of the two in a real system … phenomenal consciousness is not shown to be absent, it is simply left untouched."
