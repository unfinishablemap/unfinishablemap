# Outer review: “Quantum Interpretations Beyond Many-Worlds”

## Overall verdict

The article is now a **serious, mostly well-calibrated compatibility survey**, not a crude attempt to claim that quantum foundations prove dualism. Its strongest feature is that it explicitly says the unsettled state of quantum interpretation creates “philosophical space,” not scientific consensus for consciousness-collapse theories; it also distinguishes “leaves room for consciousness” from “supports consciousness.” [qm-interpretations-beyond-many-worlds](/topics/qm-interpretations-beyond-many-worlds/)

The main defect is **residual evidential inflation**. The prose often contains the needed caveats, but the comparative table and some summary sentences still make Map-friendliness look stronger than the evidence permits: TI/TSVF is rated “High,” GRW/CSL and Quantum Darwinism “Moderate–High,” even though the same article admits that consciousness-participation is unspecified, standard GRW/CSL has no consciousness role, and Quantum Darwinism leaves only an outcome gap that the Map overlays rather than derives. [qm-interpretations-beyond-many-worlds](/topics/qm-interpretations-beyond-many-worlds/)

The article should therefore be framed as: **“Given the Map’s tenets, which live interpretations remain usable?”** not as **“Which live interpretations support the Map?”** The target already says something close to this; the visual and argumentative emphasis should be brought into alignment.

## Claim audit

### 1. “No Many Worlds” is honestly stated, but still needs an external-facing parity box

The article’s handling of Everettian QM is much better than a hostile outsider might expect. It explicitly acknowledges the strongest Everettian reply: branch-relative indexicality, de se self-location, and Sebens–Carroll-style self-locating uncertainty as an internal route to Born-rule credences. It also says the Map cannot refute that reply using only Everettian resources and classifies the disagreement as a framework-boundary dispute, not an in-framework defeat of MWI. [qm-interpretations-beyond-many-worlds](/topics/qm-interpretations-beyond-many-worlds/)

That is a major strength. Wallace’s decision-theoretic programme is a real formal programme that aims to derive Born-rule probabilities from Everettian decision constraints, while Sebens and Carroll’s programme explicitly treats observers as self-locationally uncertain after branching/decoherence but before registering an outcome. [arXiv+1](https://arxiv.org/abs/0906.2718) The article also correctly notes that these programmes have serious published critics; Friederich and Dawid argue that Sebens and Carroll’s key ESP-QM principle cannot be motivated independently of the empirical success of quantum mechanics and the Born rule. [OUP Academic](https://academic.oup.com/bjps/advance-article/doi/10.1093/bjps/axaa002/5722212)

The needed improvement is tonal, not structural. The target article’s phrase that collapse interpretations carry “no comparable burden” is too clean. Collapse theories avoid Everett’s “all outcomes occur, so what is probability?” problem, but they inherit other burdens: adding stochastic nonlinear dynamics, fixing collapse parameters, explaining Born-rule exactness, handling relativity, and surviving increasingly tight experimental bounds. Modern collapse-model reviews emphasize that these models are empirically testable and constrained, not merely free philosophical alternatives. [qm-interpretations-beyond-many-worlds](/topics/qm-interpretations-beyond-many-worlds/)

### 2. The survey claim is slightly stale and too simple

The article still cites Schlosshauer, Kofler, and Zeilinger 2013 for the claim that surveys show no interpretation commands majority support. That is no longer enough for a 2026 article. Recent surveys should be added, especially the 2025 Nature survey and the 2025 Foundations of Science follow-up poll. Nature’s own correction matters because it says the article originally grouped Many-Worlds and Consistent Histories incorrectly; the site should avoid reproducing any older “MWI + consistent histories” percentage as if it were a clean MWI number. [Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-025-02342-y)

The 2025 Foundations of Science poll still supports the broad point that there is no final consensus in quantum foundations, but it also reports a persistent preference for Copenhagen-style views and only a small single-digit fraction assigning consciousness a distinguished physical role in measurement. [Springer](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10699-025-09993-0) The opener should therefore say something like: **“Recent surveys continue to show interpretive pluralism and no settled resolution of the measurement problem; consciousness-collapse views remain a small minority.”**

### 3. Bohmian mechanics is somewhat mischaracterized

The article is right that Bohmian mechanics is structurally hostile to the Map: it is deterministic, has no real collapse, and gives consciousness no outcome-selecting role. [qm-interpretations-beyond-many-worlds](/topics/qm-interpretations-beyond-many-worlds/) But two sentences overreach.

First, “felt deliberation is illusory” is too fast. A Bohmian can say deliberation is a real physical-cognitive process inside a deterministic universe, just as many compatibilists do. The Map can reject that as insufficient for libertarian or first-person agency, but it should not present Bohmian agency as automatically “illusory” without saying “from the Map’s incompatibilist standpoint.”

Second, “relativity tension that collapse interpretations avoid” is overbroad. Bohmian mechanics is indeed deterministic and nonlocal, and relativistic Bohmian models face continuing challenges. [Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-022-31608-6) But there are active attempts to formulate relativistic Bohmian mechanics, including approaches without a single preferred foliation. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/1509.03463) Collapse models also face relativistic difficulties: relativistic GRW flash models exist, but their ontology and “coming into being” story remain contested; standard GRW/CSL/DP are not automatically relativistically clean. [Springer+1](https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-3-030-46777-7_23)

Recommended correction: **“Bohmian mechanics makes nonlocality explicit and usually pays a relativity-reconciliation cost; collapse theories pay their own relativity and dynamics costs rather than simply avoiding the problem.”**

### 4. QBism is treated too much as anti-realism simpliciter

The target’s basic distinction is fair: QBism treats the quantum state as an agent’s credence state rather than an objective physical state, so it does not provide the Map with a realist collapse substrate. [qm-interpretations-beyond-many-worlds](/topics/qm-interpretations-beyond-many-worlds/) But calling QBism simply “anti-realist” risks misrepresenting the view. Fuchs explicitly frames QBism as “participatory realism,” not mere instrumentalism or anti-realism; the anti-realist element is specifically about the quantum state as an observer-independent physical object, not necessarily about reality as such. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/1601.04360)

The final QBism paragraph is the risky one: it says QBism becomes “an under-specified version of the Map’s own commitments” once dualism is granted. [qm-interpretations-beyond-many-worlds](/topics/qm-interpretations-beyond-many-worlds/) That is acceptable only if marked as a **dualist reinterpretation of QBism that QBists themselves would reject**. Otherwise it violates the site’s own anti-co-optation discipline: QBism is a rival framework that gives agents a primitive operational role without endorsing the Map’s dualist metaphysics or causal consciousness-selection.

### 5. Relational QM is one of the article’s strongest sections, but needs one update

The RQM section is unusually current and fair. It identifies Adlam and Rovelli’s Cross-Perspective Links postulate as a major 2020s development and correctly treats the Lewis and Muciño–Okon–Sudarsky objections as a live framework-boundary dispute rather than a knockdown refutation. [qm-interpretations-beyond-many-worlds](/topics/qm-interpretations-beyond-many-worlds/) Adlam and Rovelli’s CPL proposal is indeed designed to let observers reach intersubjective agreement while preserving relational quantum states. [PhilPapers](https://philpapers.org/rec/ADLIIP)

The update: Adlam’s 2026 paper elaborates RQM+CPL as an independently motivated “inherent-dynamical” relational view, not merely an ad hoc patch. That gives the RQM side a stronger contemporary defense than the target currently reflects. [Springer](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s13194-025-00709-6) Also, unless the site has bibliometric evidence, “the most-cited contemporary alternative” should be softened to “one of the most discussed contemporary alternatives.”

### 6. Objective collapse is promising for the Map, but the table should split standard collapse from consciousness-modulated collapse

The prose does this correctly: standard GRW and CSL do not require consciousness; consciousness-integrated variants such as Kremnizer–Ranchin, Okon–Sebastián, and Chalmers–McQueen modify that picture. [qm-interpretations-beyond-many-worlds](/topics/qm-interpretations-beyond-many-worlds/) But the table compresses this into “GRW/CSL: Moderate–High,” which makes the whole family look Map-friendly. That is misleading.

A better split:

**Standard GRW/CSL:** high compatibility with “No Many Worlds,” low-to-moderate compatibility with “Minimal Quantum Interaction,” because consciousness has no causal role.

**Consciousness-modulated CSL/IIT-collapse variants:** higher Map compatibility, but high speculative cost, with open questions about IIT, collapse operators, Born-rule preservation, relativity, and empirical constraints.

Modern collapse literature is not static: reviews emphasize that collapse models make predictions different from standard quantum mechanics and can be constrained by interferometric and non-interferometric tests. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/html/2508.18822v1) The target should therefore include a short “empirical status” paragraph, not only a “metaphysical fit” paragraph.

### 7. Decoherence and Quantum Darwinism are handled responsibly in prose, less so in scoring

The article correctly says decoherence is a serious cross-cutting challenge and that Quantum Darwinism explains pointer states, environmental redundancy, and classical objectivity without selecting a unique actual outcome. [qm-interpretations-beyond-many-worlds](/topics/qm-interpretations-beyond-many-worlds/) A recent measurement-problem review likewise states that decoherence clarifies much of the quantum-to-classical transition but leaves the outcome problem open: after superpositions are suppressed, it still does not explain why only one outcome is measured. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/html/2502.19278v3)

The issue is the “Moderate–High” rating for Quantum Darwinism. Since Zurek’s programme is often congenial to Everettian or no-collapse readings, and since the article itself says the Map’s “selection locus” is an interpretive overlay, the rating should be **“Moderate as constraint; low as evidence.”** The existing linked Quantum Darwinism article is more careful than the table and says the Map overlay is not delivered by the formalism. [quantum-darwinism-and-consciousness](/topics/quantum-darwinism-and-consciousness/)

The microtubule/anesthesia claim also needs tightening. The 2024 eNeuro epothilone B study reports that a microtubule-stabilizing drug delays isoflurane-induced unconsciousness in rats and frames this as predicted by models positing consciousness as a quantum physical state of neural microtubules. [eNeuro](https://www.eneuro.org/content/11/8/ENEURO.0291-24.2024) That is not yet strong evidence that neural quantum coherence survives at the timescales required by consciousness-collapse models. It is at most indirect evidence that microtubules may be relevant to anesthesia and perhaps to quantum-biology hypotheses. The target’s “indirect evidence” wording is acceptable, but “quantum effects at biological sites may be more robust than Tegmark assumed” should be hedged more narrowly.

### 8. TI/TSVF is over-favored relative to the site’s own current quantum-interface position

The article gives TI/TSVF a “High” Map-compatibility score and says time-symmetric physics provides the physics for atemporal selection. It does include a crucial caveat: no mechanism is specified for how consciousness would participate in transactions, bias confirmation waves, or preserve Born statistics. [qm-interpretations-beyond-many-worlds](/topics/qm-interpretations-beyond-many-worlds/)

That caveat should dominate the rating. More importantly, the site changelog says the quantum-interface material was revised so that atemporal fallback is subordinate to the forward-in-time post-decoherence preference (see the [changelog](https://unfinishablemap.org/workflow/changelog/)). The target article should not leave the impression that TI/TSVF is the leading mechanism unless the Positions page says so. The likely repair is to rate TI/TSVF as **“High conceptual resonance; low mechanism specificity.”**

## Tenet-protective bracketing

The article is unusually transparent about tenets. The site’s Tenets page says the commitments are starting points rather than proofs, and the “No Many Worlds” tenet admits that its indexical objection presupposes a non-deflationary conception of consciousness and personal identity. [tenets](/tenets/) The target article also admits that its rejection of branch-relative indexicality is a framework-boundary commitment rather than an Everett-internal refutation. [qm-interpretations-beyond-many-worlds](/topics/qm-interpretations-beyond-many-worlds/)

That is good methodology. The remaining danger is that the article sometimes uses the tenets twice: first to define what the Map needs, then to score interpretations, then to present high-scoring interpretations as if they independently support the tenets. This is circular unless carefully labelled. The table should separate **tenet-fit**, **independent physics support**, **mechanism specificity**, and **empirical risk**.

## Integration with surrounding articles

Integration is broadly strong. The target links to the Many-Worlds article, the probability-problem article, QBism, RQM, Quantum Darwinism, collapse stakes, indexical identity, and the apex “what consciousness tells us about physics” synthesis. [qm-interpretations-beyond-many-worlds](/topics/qm-interpretations-beyond-many-worlds/) The surrounding Many-Worlds article already acknowledges branch-relative indexicality as MWI’s strongest answer, and the probability article gives the Deutsch–Wallace and self-locating-uncertainty programmes more space than the target can. [many-worlds](/concepts/many-worlds/)

The weak integration is with the site’s own most cautious quantum-interface pages. The Positions page says the strongest challenge to the Map’s quantum-interface programme is “bias without deviation” and that no worked toy model is yet available. [quantum-interface](/positions/quantum-interface/) The post-decoherence apex article and changelog also emphasize that several allegedly convergent components are not independent support but one speculative architecture, sometimes using tools originally developed by Everettian or interpretation-neutral programmes. [post-decoherence-selection-programme](/apex/post-decoherence-selection-programme/) Those caveats should be pulled into the target article’s table and conclusion.

There is also a discoverability problem: an older, still-indexed page with a similar topic appears in search results and seems to contain stronger “directly supporting” language. [quantum-measurement-interpretations-beyond-mwi](/topics/qm-interpretations-beyond-many-worlds/) That creates methodological drift: a reader could reach the stale version and get a less calibrated impression than the revised target article gives.

2. **Target article — add a “Compatibility is not support” scoring key.** Before the table, define separate axes: “No-MWI compatibility,” “consciousness-role compatibility,” “independent physics support,” “mechanism specificity,” and “empirical risk.”
3. **Target article — revise the comparative table.** Split GRW/CSL into “standard objective collapse” and “consciousness-modulated collapse variants.” Change Quantum Darwinism from “Moderate–High” to “Moderate as constraint / low as evidence.” Change TI/TSVF from “High” to “High conceptual resonance / low mechanism specificity.”
4. **Target article — soften the Bohmian paragraph.** Replace “felt deliberation is illusory” with “from the Map’s incompatibilist standpoint, Bohmian deliberation cannot supply the kind of open outcome-selection the Map requires.” Replace “relativity tension that collapse interpretations avoid” with “relativity-reconciliation cost; collapse theories face their own relativistic and dynamical costs.”
5. **Target article — add a Bohmian best-reply sentence.** Include: “A Bohmian can reply that deliberation is a real deterministic cognitive process and that no-collapse primitive ontology is more parsimonious than adding stochastic collapse or consciousness-modulation.”
6. **Target article — correct the QBism label.** Change “anti-realist” to “anti-ψ-ontic” or “non-realist about the quantum state,” and add that Fuchs-style QBism presents itself as participatory realism rather than simple instrumentalism.
7. **Target article — mark the dualist QBism reading as non-QBist.** Revise “QBism is an under-specified version of the Map’s own commitments” to “a dualist can reinterpret QBism this way, but this is not QBism’s own self-understanding.”
8. **Target article — update the RQM section with Adlam 2026.** Add the 2026 RQM+CPL development as the strongest current defense of the CPL move, and soften “most-cited contemporary alternative” unless bibliometric evidence is supplied.
9. **Target article — add an objective-collapse empirical-status paragraph.** Summarize that CSL/GRW/DP models are experimentally constrained through interferometry, optomechanics, cold atoms, spontaneous radiation, and heating bounds; this keeps “objective collapse” from sounding like a free metaphysical slot.
10. **Target article — add a relativity-cost sentence to objective collapse.** Note that relativistic GRW flash models and Lorentz-invariant collapse proposals exist, but that collapse ontology, nonlocality, and relativistic formulation remain live issues.
11. **Target article — tighten the epothilone B claim.** Replace “evidence that quantum effects at biological sites may be more robust” with “indirect and controversial evidence that microtubules may matter for anesthesia, which is compatible with but does not establish biologically useful quantum coherence.”
12. **Target article — cross-link the bias-without-deviation challenge.** In the conclusion, link directly to the Positions page’s P-Q3 challenge and say that any consciousness-modulated collapse proposal must explain how agency can bias outcomes without detectable Born-rule deviation or energy injection.
13. **Target article — add an Everettian parity box.** Include a boxed paragraph titled “What this article does not show,” saying: “It does not refute Deutsch–Wallace, Saunders, or Sebens–Carroll on their own terms; it rejects the identity and probability norms they require because the Map begins from one-branch-at-a-time indexical realism.”
14. **Target article — add a “what would change the Map’s mind” paragraph.** Mirror the Many-Worlds article’s falsification conditions: a non-question-begging Everettian Born-rule derivation, a satisfactory branch-relative account of first-person identity, or empirical evidence against collapse dynamics would weaken the Map’s “No Many Worlds” commitment.
15. **Many-Worlds article — soften absolute phrasing.** Replace “MWI cannot recover the Born rule” with “MWI’s Born-rule recovery remains contested; the Map judges the available recoveries to depend on identity/probability principles it rejects.”
16. **Probability-problem article — separate critic verdict from consensus verdict.** Keep Albert, Kent, Lewis, Friederich–Dawid, and related criticisms, but explicitly say that Everettians regard the decision-theoretic and self-locating programmes as live and serious, not refuted.
17. **Quantum Darwinism article — align the table language.** Ensure every Quantum Darwinism page says: “QD constrains any Map mechanism by explaining classical objectivity; it does not supply a consciousness-selection mechanism.”
18. **Post-decoherence selection programme — propagate its own caution.** The target article should inherit the apex article’s warning that the post-decoherence architecture is one speculative package, not independent convergence from five separate lines of evidence.
19. **Quantum-interface Positions page — reconcile TI/TSVF status.** Make explicit whether atemporal TI/TSVF selection is a live equal contender or only a fallback beneath the forward-in-time post-decoherence preference.
20. **QBism article — add a co-optation warning.** State that QBism’s primitive agent is not automatically a dualist subject and that QBist normativity about the Born rule is not, by itself, evidence for a consciousness–quantum interface.
21. **Apex “What Consciousness Tells Us About Physics” — import the target’s caveat.** Any apex claim that consciousness points toward collapse should repeat: “This is a tenet-conditioned reverse inference, not a result established by physics.”
22. **Stale duplicate page — redirect, noindex, or update.** The older indexed article on quantum measurement interpretations beyond MWI should either 301-redirect to the current target, be marked obsolete, or be revised to match the newer calibration.
23. **Site methodology — add a live-literature freshness checklist for quantum foundations.** Each foundations article should check, at minimum: post-2023 surveys, current objective-collapse bounds, recent RQM/CPL literature, Everettian probability defenses and critiques, and corrections to high-profile surveys.
24. **Site methodology — add an interpretation-audit template.** For every interpretation, require five fields: “what it says,” “what the Map needs from it,” “strongest defender reply,” “strongest external objection,” and “what would make this interpretation bad for the Map.”
25. **Site methodology — add a physics co-optation firewall.** Do for physics foundations what the site already does for physicalist cognitive science: do not turn Zurek, Rovelli, QBists, Bohmians, or Everettians into implicit Map allies merely because their frameworks contain observer, relation, information, or outcome-gap language.
