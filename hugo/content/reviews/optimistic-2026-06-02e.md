## Executive Summary

The `process-and-consciousness` apex is a genuinely strong, well-calibrated synthesis that draws Whitehead/Bergson process metaphysics into the Map's interactionist frame without over-claiming — but it is *under-connected from below*: four of its prominently body-linked source articles (panpsychism, unity-of-consciousness, combination-problem, free-will) do not back-link to it, giving it ~40% fewer inbound links than a comparably-positioned apex (11 vs 18). This is a real, length-neutral, cap-safe cross-link completion opportunity. The neighbouring `phenomenology-mechanism-bridge` apex, by contrast, is fully reciprocated (all six sources back-link) — a model of the symmetry the Map should hold its apex synthesis to.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
This is the article that most directly channels this persona, and it does so honestly. It frames reality as "happenings, not things," uses actual-occasions and the wave-pattern-not-substance image well, and explicitly declines to adopt process philosophy *wholesale* — "It maintains interactionist dualism where process thinkers embrace panpsychism." That refusal is exactly the right move: it harvests process-philosophical resources for dissolving the mind-body dichotomy without collapsing into panpsychism.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
Crucially, the article does **not** use its process-philosophical resonance as an engine for tier-upgrade. The "experience all the way down" intuition is deployed to *remove a defeater* (consciousness need not be anomalous in an inert cosmos) rather than to supply positive evidence that any specific system is conscious. This is the praise-worthy thing the article declines to do: process-friendliness here is "tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating." The Process Philosopher and Hardline Empiricist personas converge rather than conflict on this article — the sign of an honestly-resolved tension.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel) / Property Dualist (Chalmers)
The temporal-structure-of-experience material (drawing on `temporal-consciousness`) centres the first-person flow of becoming and treats it as irreducible data, not as something to be explained away.
