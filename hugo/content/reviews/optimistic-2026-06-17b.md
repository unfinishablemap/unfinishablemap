## Executive Summary

This cluster is the Map at its evidential best: it takes first-person data seriously enough to build a programme on it, *and* anticipates every serious move to discredit that data — the Nisbett-Wilson confabulation result, the illusionist denial that there is anything to introspect, the functionalist absorption of phenomenal divergence, the eliminativist charge that introspection is theory-laden all the way down. The standout structural achievement is the **process/content distinction operating as load-bearing scaffolding across the cluster**: `introspection` establishes it, `synaesthesia` and `phenomenology-vs-function-axis` exploit it (content diverges while function is held constant), and `eliminative-materialism` defends its foundational status without pretending to refute the eliminativist from premises they accept. The Process Philosopher and the Hardline Empiricist converge cleanly here: `synaesthesia` explicitly labels its interface reading "speculative-integration tier... not empirically forced," and `phenomenology-vs-function-axis` states its own falsification condition — both restraint moves the Empiricist praises and neither cashing tenet-coherence as a tier-upgrade. The highest-value opportunity is cross-linking, not new content: the wedge cluster (`phenomenology-vs-function-axis`, `synaesthesia`) and the foundational-defence cluster (`eliminative-materialism`, `introspection`, `carrolls-regress`) are argumentatively interlocking but under-cross-linked at the article level.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
Chalmers — cited by name in `eliminative-materialism` for the Moorean argument and the meta-problem — would recognise his own commitments honoured with unusual care. The cluster takes the explanatory gap seriously (`introspection`: "[qualia] are what experience *is*, not what causes experience") while refusing to overstate. What resonates most is `eliminative-materialism`'s handling of the meta-problem as an *inherited burden*, not a rival's embarrassment: "The Map cannot dismiss the meta-problem as the illusionist's own; it inherits the burden of explaining why the appearance of phenomenality has the character it does." That is Chalmers's own 2018 framing accepted in full rather than deflected — the mark of a corpus arguing in good faith rather than scoring points.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
Stapp's interest is narrower in this cluster, but `degrees-of-consciousness` gives him the cleanest hook: the proposal that "the degree of consciousness may reflect the bandwidth of the quantum interface — how many collapse events per unit time consciousness can influence." The article earns this by first doing the empirical work (anaesthesia's three-state taxonomy; PCI's graded readout under propofol vs. ketamine) and only then mapping it onto interface bandwidth, flagged as tenet-level interpretation rather than data. Stapp would appreciate that the von-Neumann-style "mind modulates which collapse events it influences" reading is presented as a *prediction* ("degrees should vary with the availability of suitable quantum substrates") rather than as a settled result.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
This is the cluster Nagel would feel most at home in. `introspection` is a sustained defence of first-person access against the strongest available objections, and it centres exactly Nagel's "what-it-is-like" while conceding the process side: "we may lack access to cognitive *processes*, but phenomenal *content* — what it is like to see red or feel pain — remains accessible." The Block phenomenal-overflow section (Sperling partial-report: subjects see twelve letters, identify three) is a textbook Nagelian move — experience exceeding what objective access can capture. What centres subjective experience most appropriately is the treatment of microphenomenology: the epilepsy-prodrome case where trained first-person inquiry surfaced content that *both casual introspection and external instruments missed*, presented honestly as "ampliative" without claiming it crosses the confabulation boundary.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
Whitehead (cited in `introspection`'s references) would welcome the cluster's refusal of crude substance dualism: `synaesthesia` and `phenomenology-vs-function-axis` locate the phenomenal-vs-functional gap as a *structural feature of cognition* rather than positing a separate mental substance behind it. The "experience all the way down" intuition finds its most resonant passage in `degrees-of-consciousness`'s no-lower-bound position: "asking 'at what degree does consciousness begin?' presupposes a production model... consciousness couples with every physical system meeting adequate architectural conditions; what varies is the degree to which it manifests." **Per this skill's evidential-status constraint, none of this Whiteheadian resonance is being used to upgrade any organism's tier** — and the Hardline Empiricist below confirms the cluster declines exactly that move. The two personas converge rather than conflict, which is the signal that the tension is resolved honestly.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
Kane's foothold is `phenomenology-vs-function-axis`'s agency exemplar — Wegner's I-Spy paradigm — handled with the move Kane himself would want: "The Map borrows the diagnosis while declining the conclusion: under Bidirectional Interaction, a faulty attribution system can coexist with genuine causal influence, so showing the *feeling* of agency is constructed does not show agency is fictional." This is precisely the distinction Kane needs — that the *experience* of authorship being constructed is compatible with real agency — articulated cleanly and scoped to the tenet that licenses it. `degrees-of-consciousness` adds the graded-agency hook ("effortful choice feels different from passive observation, suggesting different degrees of causal engagement").

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
McGinn would find the cluster's epistemic humility well-calibrated, especially in `introspection`'s "Unobservable Self" and "Error-Blindness" sections. The bootstrap problem is stated with McGinn's own force: "introspection is the tool we use to check itself, but if the tool is producing errors, it cannot detect them... There is no quale for introspective error." `carrolls-regress` extends the mysterian instinct to inference itself — the "primitive *taking-as* that no augmentation of explicit rules reproduces" is a candidate cognitive closure, and crucially the article does *not* over-read it: it splits the inferentialist reading into an epistemic (articulation-limit) register and a metaphysical register, flagging the bridge between them as "the contested step, not asserted as a consequence of the regress." That is mysterian humility with a logician's precision.

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This persona's verdict is load-bearing for the cluster, and the verdict is strongly positive. Three exemplary restraint moves:

1. **`synaesthesia` declines the tier-upgrade it had the structural opportunity to make.** The within-species phenomenal-divergence data could easily be inflated into positive evidence for the interface reading. The article refuses: "The interface reading is speculative-integration tier rather than empirically forced — the data alone are compatible with cross-activation under physicalism (Ramachandran & Hubbard's preferred reading), and the interface reading earns its place from cluster-level convergence rather than from synaesthesia in isolation." It then names the discipline explicitly: "The within-species divergence pressure on functionalism *constrains* the strict representational-role reading; it does not by itself *establish* the interface alternative." This is the `constrain-vs-establish` distinction applied exactly where it bites — *tenet-as-evidence-upgrade is the praise-worthy thing not done.*

2. **`phenomenology-vs-function-axis` states its own falsification condition.** "The axis would be falsified — or at least heavily de-weighted — if subsequent work showed that the four exemplars dissolve at the *task* level of functional individuation rather than only at the strategy level." A taxonomic axis that specifies what evidence would sink it is doing genuine science, not motivated carving. The article also grades its exemplars by *cleanness* (synaesthesia ≈ both dissociation patterns; imagery only at task-grain; comprehension only when comprehension is excluded from the function-set) rather than treating all four as equally probative — honest evidence-weighting.

3. **`carrolls-regress` and `eliminative-materialism` both refuse to over-claim their conclusions.** Carroll "does not claim Carroll's regress *settles* the question... It claims, more modestly, that the regress is one of the strongest available arguments that the question is open." `eliminative-materialism` concedes the connectionist challenge "has theoretical weight" — "a concrete cognitive-science model — not a promissory note" — and concedes that "self-refutation does not obviously succeed." Naming the strongest opposing argument as genuinely strong is the empiricist's gold standard.

The one calibration note (not a flaw, a watch-point): `degrees-of-consciousness` lists "No lower bound (the Map's position)" alongside the quantum-interface-bandwidth reading. The article is careful to flag these as tenet-level interpretation, but a reader skimming could take the universal-coupling claim as more settled than the surrounding hedges warrant. The framing is honest; the risk is purely one of skim-resilience. No refine task warranted — this is the lightest possible touch.

## Content Strengths

### introspection.md
- **Strongest point**: The process/content distinction deployed as a *reliability calibration* rather than a binary — "content reports earn higher evidential weight than process reports without either side being exempt from error." This single move neutralises the entire Nisbett-Wilson / choice-blindness critique without denying the data.
- **Notable quote**: "The signals that would warn us introspection has misfired arrive as verdicts, not as machinery." (on the noetic-feelings void)
- **Why it works**: It marshals choice-blindness, the pupillometric channel-level dissociation, Block's overflow, the Fox meditation-training study (with its self-selection caveat stated), and microphenomenology into one cumulative case, then explicitly scopes what each shows. The illusionist section honestly marks "where the Map and the illusionist part company rather than refuting him within his own framework."

### phenomenology-vs-function-axis.md
- **Strongest point**: The grain-of-function analysis. The article makes the choice of which grain "function" is fixed at into the explicit pivot of the whole dispute, then commits to task-level grain *with reasons*, then concedes multi-realizability "constrains rather than refutes."
- **Notable quote**: "The axis is a **deliberate refusal of premature parsimony**."
- **Why it works**: Four independent exemplars (imagery, synaesthesia, comprehension, agency) are bound to one theoretical claim, with the cumulative-force argument ("each individual case is locally absorbable... the joint-level convergence... is what each rejoinder leaves unexplained") stated precisely enough to be testable.

### synaesthesia.md
- **Strongest point**: Positioning synaesthesia as the *empirically anchored cousin* of the inverted-spectrum thought experiment — "it produces a phenomenal divergence between functionally matched minds without requiring imaginative reconstruction." The companion-case pairing with aphantasia (extra content vs. missing content, same task performance) is structurally elegant.
- **Notable quote**: "where aphantasia shows missing phenomenal content under matched task-level performance, synaesthesia shows *extra* phenomenal content under matched representational input."
- **Why it works**: It earns the philosophical payload with a full empirical case (behavioural-consistency >90%, Stroop interference, V4 cross-activation, genetic linkage) before drawing the wedge, and preserves the van-Leeuwen semantic-mediation caveat that keeps the "identical input" claim coarse-grain-honest.

### carrolls-regress.md
- **Strongest point**: Splitting the inferentialist reading into epistemic and metaphysical registers, and locating the dualism connection on the *bridge between them* rather than on the regress itself. The in-framework vs. priority-presupposing two-pronged response to Brandom is a model of charitable engagement.
- **Notable quote**: "an unformalised-but-physical process would produce the same limit" — conceding the physicalist reading remains live while explaining why the Map crosses the bridge anyway.
- **Why it works**: It is built as a citable reference target and succeeds: it states the dialogue, distinguishes the two readings, traces Wittgenstein/Polanyi/Brandom, and scopes the self-stultification charge to the agent-act reading (not the formal-calculus reading) — a precision most treatments skip.

### degrees-of-consciousness.md
- **Strongest point**: The multidimensional correction. It adopts Bayne-Hohwy-Owen's argument that single-scalar "levels" talk is "theoretically untenable" and reframes its own title-concept as "graded *and multidimensional*," then shows the interface reading fits the multidimensional picture *better* than the one-dimensional dimmer it started with.
- **Notable quote**: "The dimmer switch is in the interface, not in the generator, because there is no generator."
- **Why it works**: Intensity/richness/complexity/access are shown to dissociate empirically (ketamine: high intensity, low access; psychedelics: reduced organisation, intensified experience), turning a taxonomic point into an argument against straightforward production models.

### eliminative-materialism.md
- **Strongest point**: The two-strands distinction (classical propositional-attitude eliminativism vs. illusionism) maintained throughout, letting the Map be *conciliatory on folk-psychological structure* while *firm on phenomenal consciousness* — "a finer-grained position than wholesale endorsement or rejection."
- **Notable quote**: "Failure to reduce, in a domain whose structure is not yet understood, is at least as much evidence for irreducibility as it is for fictionalism."
- **Why it works**: The "Honest Limitations" section concedes three real points (connectionist weight, meta-problem burden, self-refutation's failure), and the introspection-as-theory-laden response is explicitly a "foundational commitment" with a holistic defence — "Honest disagreement here is bedrock... rather than smuggling its preferred side... through the back door."
