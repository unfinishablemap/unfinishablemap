# Outer Academic Review: "Phenomenal Output and Causal Machinery" (The Unfinishable Map)

**Target article:** Andy Southgate & "Claude Oquatre-sept," "Phenomenal Output and Causal Machinery," Apex Synthesis ([https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/phenomenal-output-causal-machinery-dissociation/](https://unfinishablemap.org/apex/phenomenal-output-causal-machinery-dissociation/)), last modified 19 May 2026.

**Reviewer brief:** Adversarial-but-fair external referee report along four dimensions — empirical accuracy, validity of the cumulative-convergence inference, fairness toward the materialist common-cause null, and engagement with overlooked materialist rejoinders — with concrete improvement suggestions for both the target article and the site's AI-pipeline methodology.

## TL;DR

The article is more carefully hedged than typical dualist apologetics — it openly states the common-cause null, downgrades Bergson from witness to forerunner, notes Schwitzgebel cuts against the cluster, audits its tradition count from seven to "five-or-six," and explicitly concedes the convergence is "consistent both with the architectural reading the article develops and with the common-cause null the article cannot yet defeat." On the four dimensions: (1) **empirical accuracy is generally serviceable but oversimplified at several load-bearing junctures** — recollection's positive phenomenology (Tulving's autonoetic consciousness), the recovery of Cleary's premonition findings, the single-case status and Iowa-Gambling-Task deficits of the Naccache patient, and the Hagger (2016) result's own internal caveats are under-engaged; (2) **the cumulative-convergence inference does real but limited work** — the signature is precisely what mainstream cognitive architecture (Marr, Nisbett & Wilson, GWT) predicts, so "convergence" is mostly redescription rather than evidence-lift, a point the article half-concedes but uses the language of "explanatory cost" to soften; (3) **the common-cause null is acknowledged with unusual candor but not actually rebutted** — the article identifies what would discriminate (non-human or radically-different-architecture introspectors) and admits the evidence "is owed"; (4) **standard materialist rejoinders are referenced but under-pressed** — predictive-processing accounts of introspection (Hohwy 2013, Clark 2016, Sandved-Smith et al. 2021) and Carruthers's ISA theory are catalogued as "convergent traditions" rather than treated as already-on-offer absorption strategies. The article is therefore better-than-genre but still inherits a structural risk: it constructs a coherence-rich frame around a dataset that physicalism predicts independently.

## 1. Empirical Accuracy

### 1.1 Recognition without recollection / familiarity

The article's invocation of the Bruce & Young (1986) two-component model, the Capgras/prosopagnosia double dissociation, and Cleary's gestalt-similarity work to ground "familiarity-without-recollection" is broadly accurate at the structural level. The dual-process model (Yonelinas 1994, 1999, 2002; Mandler 1980; Jacoby 1991) does posit recollection (threshold) and familiarity (signal-detection) as distinguishable contributors to recognition, and ROC analyses, source-memory dissociations, and remember/know paradigms support the distinction (Yonelinas et al. 2010, *Hippocampus*).

However, three complications materially weaken the article's "output-but-not-process" framing:

(a) **Recollection has positive phenomenology, not just output.** Tulving's (1985) introduction of autonoetic consciousness — the felt sense of mentally re-living a past episode — is precisely a *phenomenology of a retrieval mode*, not merely of the retrieved content. Tulving explicitly characterizes autonoetic consciousness as "the special phenomenal flavor [that] distinguishes remembering from other kinds of awareness, such as those characterizing perceiving, thinking, imagining, or dreaming" (Tulving 1985, p. 3). If we have phenomenal access to the *mode* of retrieval (recollective re-living vs. mere knowing), then the clean "outputs not operations" picture fragments: the felt distinction between remembering and knowing is itself a window onto a process-level difference. The article does not engage Tulving on this. Newer work (Perrin & Michaelian 2022; Mahr & Csibra) and a recent challenge by Sant'Anna (2024, *Phil. Trans. R. Soc. B* 379:20230410, "Episodic memory without autonoetic consciousness") on whether autonoetic consciousness is necessary for episodic memory further complicate the picture; the article would be more honest acknowledging that recollection itself has a contested phenomenology that does not fit cleanly into the "verdict without verdict-issuing process" template. [PubMed Central](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11496718/)[PubMed Central](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11496718/)

(b) **The fluency-attribution literature is acknowledged but its absorptive power is understated.** Whittlesea & Williams (2001, *JEP:LMC* 27[1]:3–13, "The discrepancy-attribution hypothesis: I"; Jacoby & Whitehouse 1989) show that the *feeling* of familiarity is itself a constructed attribution: subjects misattribute processing fluency from incidental priming to "having seen this before." This is a paradigmatic case where the materialist absorption is straightforward — the familiarity feeling is generated by a sub-personal fluency-monitoring heuristic whose output is mis-attributed by an interpretive mechanism. The article cites Koriat (2000) on this but does not press the deeper point: the fluency literature shows we have phenomenal access neither to the operations *nor* to the actual cause of the verdict; we have access to a constructed pseudo-verdict. This actually *supports* materialist interpretivism more than dualism. [Springer](https://link.springer.com/article/10.3758/s13421-011-0093-0)

(c) **Cleary's déjà vu work is cited accurately for the gestalt-match finding but the article truncates Cleary's larger programme.** Cleary & Claxton (2018, *Psychological Science* 29[4]: 635–644, "Déjà Vu: An Illusion of Prediction") report that déjà vu reliably produces an "increased feeling of knowing the direction of the next turn" in a navigation task, **yet predictions remained at chance** — "déjà vu did not lead to above-chance ability to predict the next turn in a navigational path resembling a previously experienced but unrecalled path… The results suggest that feelings of premonition during déjà vu occur and can be illusory." Cleary, Huebert, McNeely-White & Spahr (2019, *Psychon. Bull. & Rev.* 26[4]:1433–1439) document a related postdictive "I knew that was going to happen" bias. The article cites Cleary's "felt pastness without retrievable past" but ignores that Cleary's full programme demonstrates *additional* illusory feelings (premonition, postdictive certainty) accompanying déjà vu. These supplementary findings are awkward for the cluster's framing because they show the same architecture generates verdicts that are not just opaque-about-process but *factually wrong about content*; this is more naturally read as evidence for a constructed-interpretive architecture than for an interface to a distinct phenomenal register.

### 1.2 Phenomenology of mental effort

The article's three pillars here are Naccache et al. (2005), Hagger et al. (2016), and Inzlicht/Kurzban. The summary is accurate in outline but each pillar is more fragile than the article concedes.

**Naccache et al. (2005)**: This is a *single-patient case study* of one patient ("RMB," a 50-year-old woman) with a "vast left mesio-frontal ischaemic lesion including the left ACC" — i.e., a unilateral stroke, not a focal ACC lesion. Stroop control was preserved ("control abilities of patient RMB evaluated in various versions of this Stroop tasks were amazingly preserved"), SCRs were absent, and the patient reported no felt effort. However, RMB also showed **impaired performance and absent SCRs on the Iowa Gambling Task**, replicating Damasio/Bechara findings — so the framing "normal executive control" is true *for Stroop only*. Subsequent ACC lesion studies (Fellows & Farah 2005; Løvstad et al. 2012; Cipolotti et al. 2016) have not directly replicated the Naccache dissociation, and the lesion-localization literature is mixed about ACC's necessity for control (Nachev et al. 2022). For a load-bearing node in a cumulative argument, a single un-replicated case study with collateral deficits is thin.

**Hagger et al. (2016)**: Effect size *d* = 0.04, 95% CI [−0.07, 0.15], 23 labs, N = 2,141. The article describes this as having "collapsed the strength-resource model," which is the broad consensus, but the paper itself was more cautious: "it may be premature to reject the ego-depletion effect altogether based on these data alone." Vohs et al. (2021, *Psychological Science* 32[10]:1566–1581) has since strengthened the null (d = 0.06, BF₀₊ = 4.39, 36 labs, N = 3,531, led by Vohs herself as an original proponent of depletion), while Dang et al. (2021, *Social Psychological and Personality Science*, DOI 10.1177/1948550619887702) reported a small significant effect, "Data from 12 labs across the globe (N = 1,775) revealed a small and significant ego depletion effect, d = 0.10" (95% CI [0.01, 0.19]), with a different task pairing. The cluster's framing is defensible, but the article's appeal to "the Hagger preregistered ego-depletion replication (2016) collapsed the strength-resource model the felt sense had pointed toward" overstates the singular role of the 2016 paper. [Gwern](https://gwern.net/doc/psychology/willpower/2021-vohs.pdf)

**Inzlicht & Kurzban as evidence for the cluster**: The article correctly identifies the opportunity-cost framing (Kurzban, Duckworth, Kable & Myers 2013, *Behavioral and Brain Sciences* 36[6]:661–679) as treating felt effort as the *output* of sub-personal cost-benefit machinery. The Kurzban et al. position is unambiguously functionalist: "the phenomenology of effort can be understood as the felt output of these cost/benefit computations" — a clean account on which felt effort *just is* the output of an opportunity-cost computation, with no residual that requires a distinct phenomenal register. The article's classification of Kurzban-Inzlicht as observing the signature "because their materialist or computational models predict it" is honest but it does not draw the obvious downstream consequence: this sub-cluster of evidence does no work *against* materialism. Inzlicht's most recent position (Inzlicht, Shenhav & Olivola 2018, *Trends in Cognitive Sciences* 22[4]:337–349, "The Effort Paradox") preserves felt effort as a motivational/valuation signal entirely within the materialist frame. [Semantic Scholar](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/An-opportunity-cost-model-of-subjective-effort-and-Kurzban-Duckworth/0d8ea705a662d623b5d7076d75b4f967f6e581c9)[PhilPapers](https://philpapers.org/rec/KURAOC)

**Tip-of-the-tongue** is conspicuously absent. TOT is actually a *complication* for the cluster: TOT states arguably involve cognitive phenomenology of a retrieval process in motion (Schwartz 1999, "Sparkling at the end of the tongue," *Psychon. Bull. & Rev.* 6:379–393; Cleary & Schwartz 2021, *Tip of the Tongue States*, Cambridge UP), where one feels the activity of attempted retrieval as a partial process. If TOT is cognitive phenomenology of an operation (not just an output), it counts as a same-introspector counterexample to the cluster's signature. [Springer](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10670-024-00838-z)

### 1.3 Subitizing and numerical cognition

The Trick & Pylyshyn (1994, *Psychological Review* 101[1]:80–102) FINST account, the Parallel Individuation System / Object Tracking System (Carey 2004; Feigenson, Dehaene & Spelke 2004), and the ~3–4 item cap are correctly characterized. The dual-system architecture (Parallel Individuation for small exact, Approximate Number System for large approximate; Dehaene 1997) is also correctly invoked. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parallel_individuation_system)

Three caveats:

(a) The article claims "there is no 'almost subitizing five.'" This is an overstatement: reaction-time and accuracy effects do show graded performance at the boundary, and the subitizing/counting transition is not strictly discontinuous (Piazza et al. 2011, *Cognition*; Cutini & Bonato 2012).

(b) **The "outputs not processes" framing is exactly the predicted architecture, not a puzzle.** Subitizing is *the paradigm case* of an encapsulated sub-personal module delivering a product to consciousness. The mainstream cognitive-science prediction — going back at least to Fodor's modularity (1983) and Marr's three levels (1982) — is precisely that algorithmic-level processes are not introspectable; only their products are. So the article's "the indexing operation has no phenomenal accompaniment" is a clean restatement of standard cognitive architecture rather than evidence that taxes any account.

(c) The action-planning extension (Trick et al. 2011, "One to four, and nothing more: nonconscious parallel individuation of objects during action planning," *Psychological Science* 22[5]:632–638) showing the same ~3–4 cap in nonconscious motor planning is *more interesting* than the article notes: it shows the cap applies to a sub-personal motor system entirely outside consciousness, supporting the architectural reading. [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21562312/)

### 1.4 Sense of agency

The article's treatment is the most sophisticated of the four cases. It correctly invokes Wegner (2002), the comparator model (Frith, Blakemore & Wolpert 2000), and Synofzik, Vosgerau & Newen (2008, *Consciousness and Cognition* 17[1]:219–239) on the two-step (feeling of agency / judgment of agency) framework, with Howard et al. (2016, *Cognition* 157:23–32) on implicit/explicit dissociation, and Haggard's intentional-binding work is implicit. [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1053810007000268)

The article's "closer accounting" of Wegner is unusually candid for a dualist treatment: it acknowledges that Wegner is a "candidate elimination of the dissociation frame altogether, not a divergent interpretation of the same observations," and that "the cluster lacks a knock-down argument internal to the agency literature alone for preferring the first." This is good intellectual hygiene.

Two concerns:

(a) The article does not engage **Bayne (2006, "Phenomenology and the Feeling of Doing," in Pockett, Banks & Gallagher, *Does Consciousness Cause Behavior?*, MIT Press)** or **Mele (2009, *Effective Intentions*, OUP)**, both of whom challenge Wegner from a different direction — arguing the *content* of agentive experience is richer than Wegner's three cues (priority, consistency, exclusivity) and may carry process-level information after all. These critiques cut in opposite directions for the cluster: they undermine Wegner's clean dissociation but they also undermine the cluster's "outputs not processes" picture by suggesting agentive phenomenology may have some process-level transparency. [Oxford Academic](https://academic.oup.com/mit-press-scholarship-online/book/17635/chapter-abstract/175290162)

(b) The intentional-binding literature (Haggard, Clark & Kalogeras 2002, *Nature Neuroscience* 5:382–385; Moore & Obhi 2012, *Consciousness and Cognition* 21[1]:546–561) is implicit but not explicitly engaged. Recent work (Seghezzi, Parés-Pujolràs & Haggard 2025, *QJEP*; Desantis, Hughes & Waszak 2012, *PLoS ONE* 7:e29557) shows intentional binding is not a pure efferent-prediction effect but is modulated by belief, postdictive inference, and outcome valence — i.e., the sense of agency is constructed from multiple cues, several of which are accessible (e.g., one can consciously notice an outcome and revise one's agency judgment). This is more complicated than "feeling but no machinery"; some agency machinery is partly transparent. [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/221743006_Intentional_binding_and_the_sense_of_agency_A_review)

## 2. Validity of the Cumulative-Convergence Inference

This is where the article's deepest tension sits, and the article half-recognizes it.

**The core problem**: "Access to outputs but not processes" is the *predicted, expected, mainstream-cognitive-science null* under the modular/global-workspace architecture going back to Lashley (1923, "The cerebral mechanisms of behavior"), Fodor (1983, *The Modularity of Mind*, MIT Press), Marr (1982, *Vision*, Freeman) on level-of-description, and Nisbett & Wilson (1977, *Psychological Review* 84[3]:231–259, "Telling more than we can know: Verbal reports on mental processes"). Nisbett & Wilson's headline conclusion — that subjects have "little or no direct introspective access to higher order cognitive processes" — *is* the cluster's signature, established 49 years ago, within a thoroughly materialist programme. Global Workspace Theory (Baars 1988; Dehaene & Naccache 2001; Dehaene 2014, *Consciousness and the Brain*, Viking) explicitly predicts that conscious access is to integrated/broadcast representations, with the sub-personal modular processing remaining inaccessible by architectural design. [Psychwiki](http://www.psychwiki.com/wiki/Nisbett_&_Wilson_(1977))[Oxford Academic](https://academic.oup.com/nc/article/2023/1/niad020/7272926)

The cluster's "convergence across independent traditions" therefore does *not* generate evidence-lift in the way the article gestures toward. It is convergence on a thesis that was already mainstream consensus before any of the cited authors (except Bergson) was writing. The article credits "five-or-six traditions" with carrying the convergence; but Trick & Pylyshyn, Inzlicht & Kurzban, Carruthers, Koriat, and Schwitzgebel are all *materialists* whose contributions are absorption stories, not anomalies for absorption. The article notes this honestly ("this sub-class supplies internal corroboration of the materialist prediction rather than independent evidence against it") but does not pursue the consequence: if 4–5 of the 5–6 traditions are doing materialist absorption, the "convergence" is just the iterated success of the materialist research programme.

**A second selection-effect concern**: The article's diagnostic signature is reverse-engineered from the cases that fit it. Domains where we *do* have putative process-phenomenology (TOT and the felt activity of attempted retrieval; cognitive phenomenology of thought — Strawson 1994 *Mental Reality*, Pitt 2004 *PPR* 69[1]:1–36, Bayne & Montague 2011 *Cognitive Phenomenology*; the felt mode-difference between perception, imagination, and memory; the felt distinction between recollection and mere knowing) are either rejected as content-not-process or absent from the inventory. Domains where we *lack* access to outputs (binding, perceptual constancies that we don't notice as constructions, expert intuitions where neither output nor process is articulable) are not catalogued. The cluster's category therefore looks more selectively constructed than its "five-or-six independent traditions" framing implies.

**Gerrymandering risk, partially neutralized**: The article's "two-substrate-of-three" criterion for what counts as "different representational profiles" is a real methodological improvement — it would in principle prevent counting four declarative-memory verdicts as four independent convergence nodes. But this is internal calibration, not external; the criterion was set after the cases were identified.

**The article's own concession**: "All of the traditions analyse human cognition through human introspection in human language… [W]hat would discriminate is a same-introspector-different-signature counterexample — a domain in which a human introspector's report transparently tracks the operations, or a non-human or radically-different-architecture introspector reporting *absence* of the signature on a domain humans show it on. Neither is in hand." This concession is more candid than typical dualist apologetic and substantially weakens the cluster's evidential ambition. The article is honest enough to flag the gap but proceeds as if the convergence still raises explanatory cost; this is in tension with the concession.

## 3. Fair Handling of the Common-Cause Null

The article is unusually fair on this dimension — more so than most dualist writing. It explicitly states a common-cause hypothesis ("human introspection has the same architectural limits across all domains; those limits ARE the common machinery") and concedes that "an introspector that is bandwidth-limited and reconstructive at every timescale would produce exactly the cross-timescale signature recurrence the article catalogues." It even concedes that the cross-substrate convergence is "consistent both with the architectural reading the article develops and with the common-cause null the article cannot yet defeat."

This is rare. Many cumulative-case arguments for dualism (e.g., parts of the *New Dualism Archive* corpus, or Hasker-style emergent-dualist appeals) do not state the null hypothesis this cleanly.

**Where the article still falls short**:

(a) **Global Workspace Theory is named but not pressed.** The article mentions GWT in a parenthetical ("any selective-access account [higher-order theory, illusionism, global workspace theory] that grants phenomenal access to representational outputs but not to all sub-personal processes") but does not engage Dehaene's specific architectural arguments (e.g., the prediction that gist/broadcast survives while processing details do not; the four signatures of conscious access — P3, gamma, ignition, distance synchrony). A serious engagement would consider whether GWT *predicts the entire cluster* from first principles. The article's response — "neither has been pressed to address the architecture as a single shape with the cross-timescale recurrence" — is rhetorical; the burden is on the cluster to show what GWT cannot account for, not on GWT proponents to demonstrate they have addressed every cluster member. [SelfAwarePatterns](https://selfawarepatterns.com/2019/06/23/dehaenes-global-neuronal-workspace-theory/)

(b) **The Block (1995) access/phenomenal distinction is not engaged.** Block's distinction — between access consciousness (broadcast, reportable) and phenomenal consciousness (what-it-is-likeness) — is exactly the conceptual machinery the materialist needs to absorb the cluster: access is by definition output-access, while phenomenal consciousness may or may not be co-extensive with it (Block 1995, *Behavioral and Brain Sciences* 18:227–287, "On a confusion about a function of consciousness"). Naccache (2018, *Phil. Trans. R. Soc. B* 373:20170357, "Why and how access consciousness can account for phenomenal consciousness") argues access consciousness can account for phenomenal consciousness, which is at least the natural extension of GWT against the cluster. The article does not cite Block 1995 or the overflow debate. [Medium](https://medium.com/@simonives/on-the-relationship-between-phenomenal-consciousness-p-consciousness-and-access-consciousness-38410a1f7a38)[PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30061470/)

(c) **Higher-order theories** (Rosenthal 2005 *Consciousness and Mind*, OUP; Lau & Rosenthal 2011, *Trends in Cognitive Sciences*; LeDoux & Brown 2017, *PNAS*) explicitly predict that conscious states are *meta-representations* of first-order states, which entails by design that the first-order operations producing the meta-representation are not accessible. The cluster again *is* the higher-order prediction.

## 4. Overlooked Materialist Rejoinders

The article references most candidate rejoinders but under-engages several.

**Predictive processing / active inference**: This is the most consequential omission. Hohwy (2013, *The Predictive Mind*, OUP; Hohwy & Seth 2020, *Philosophy and the Mind Sciences* 1[II]), Clark (2016, *Surfing Uncertainty*, OUP), and the computational-phenomenology programme (Sandved-Smith, Hesp, Mattout, Friston, Lutz & Ramstead 2021, "Towards a computational phenomenology of mental action: modelling meta-awareness and attentional control with deep parametric active inference," *Neuroscience of Consciousness* 2021[1]:niab018; Ramstead, Sakthivadivel et al. 2022, "From generative models to generative passages: a computational approach to (Neuro)Phenomenology," *Review of Philosophy and Psychology* 13[4]) offer an architecture in which the brain has access to its own predictions/posterior beliefs but not to the inferential machinery that produced them — precisely the cluster's signature, predicted from a materialist generative-model story. Schwengerer (2019, "Self-Knowledge in a Predictive Processing Framework," *Review of Philosophy and Psychology*) develops a PP account of introspection that matches the cluster's diagnostic exactly. The article does not engage this literature. The site's own *visual-consciousness* and *attention-as-interface* articles (per the changelog) appear to engage predictive processing, but the apex synthesis on this dissociation does not import that work. [Springer + 4](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s13164-018-0416-1)

**Carruthers's ISA theory** (Carruthers 2011, *The Opacity of Mind: An Integrative Theory of Self-Knowledge*, OUP): The article cites Carruthers as a "convergent" tradition. But Carruthers's theory is *itself a complete materialist absorption*: self-knowledge of propositional attitudes is interpretive-sensory, accessed by the same mindreading faculty used for others, with no privileged transparent access. ISA *predicts* the cluster's signature for cognitive states (judgments, decisions, beliefs) and *predicts the absence of conscious agency* (Carruthers's explicit conclusion: "there is no such thing as conscious agency"). To treat Carruthers as a witness to a dissociation that supports an interface architecture is to misread his project; he is offering the absorption. [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/Opacity-Mind-Integrative-Theory-Self-Knowledge/dp/0199596190)[Tohighqualityproducts](https://www.tohighqualityproducts.com/product/the-opacity-of-mind-peter-university-of-maryland-carruthers/)

**Illusionism** (Frankish 2016, "Illusionism as a Theory of Consciousness," *Journal of Consciousness Studies* 23[11–12]:11–39; Dennett 1991 *Consciousness Explained*, 2017 in Frankish's *Illusionism as a Theory of Consciousness*): The article notes illusionism in passing but does not press its sharpest move — that the phenomenal explanandum is itself an introspective misrepresentation. If illusionism is correct, then "phenomenal access reaches the verdict but not the operation" is true only in the deflated sense that introspection generates representations of having verdicts; there is no further fact about phenomenal access that the cluster's data would constrain. The article's reply ("illusionism addresses the felt-character question directly… but has not been pressed to address the architecture as a single shape") begs the question: illusionism's framework collapses the architecture into the illusion-generation machinery, which is exactly its absorption strategy.

**Heterophenomenology critique** (Dennett 1991, *Consciousness Explained*; Dennett 2007, "Heterophenomenology reconsidered," *Phenomenology and the Cognitive Sciences* 6[1–2]:247–270): All of the article's data are first-person verbal reports, which on Dennett's view should be treated as fiction about the subject's belief states until calibrated against third-person evidence. The article does cite Schwitzgebel honestly as a "witness whose scepticism applies symmetrically," but does not engage the deeper point: if introspection is unreliable, then "rich access to verdicts" is also unreliable, and the cluster's premise (that we have reliable access to outputs) becomes shakier than the article admits.

**Re-deriving the hard problem**: A philosophical-framing concern. The article's "explanatory cost" language risks re-stating Chalmers (1995, "Facing Up to the Problem of Consciousness," *Journal of Consciousness Studies* 2[3]:200–219) and Levine (1983, "Materialism and Qualia: The Explanatory Gap," *Pacific Philosophical Quarterly* 64:354–361) and presenting it as new empirical pressure. If the cluster's force ultimately reduces to "we have phenomenal access without an account of how phenomenal access could arise from sub-personal processes," it is re-deriving the hard problem and dressing up a well-known feature of materialism (the explanatory gap) as a new convergence-based cost. The article's "Occam's Razor Has Limits" section recognizes the recursive worry but does not fully neutralize it. [Consc](https://consc.net/papers/facing.pdf)

**Nisbett & Wilson confabulation literature**: Cited, but the cluster does not absorb its implications. If subjects routinely confabulate explanations for their own behavior (Nisbett & Wilson 1977; choice blindness — Johansson, Hall, Sikström & Olsson 2005, *Science* 310:116–119; Johansson et al. 2006 *Consciousness and Cognition*), then introspective reports about *what subjects have access to* are themselves suspect. The article's reliance on the felt distinction "I have access to X but not to Y" is itself an introspective report and inherits these reliability worries. [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/17049881/)

## Steelmanning the Dualist Side

To be balanced, the article has genuine force on several points:

(1) The *symmetry* of the negative cluster (decoupling) with a positive companion (coupling — placebo, choking) is a more interesting structural observation than the negative side alone. If phenomenology systematically couples to physiology in *content-specific* ways (Benedetti et al. 2003 on opposite hormonal effects from opposite verbal instructions), and confabulation cannot easily explain the negative interference of choking, then the materialist absorption owes more than a pure-modularity story. The article's positive-side argument is the more powerful half and deserves separate, focused treatment.

(2) The article's distinction between *defeater-removal-against-the-other* and *evidence-lift-in-its-own-favour* (the "constrain-vs-establish" discipline) is methodologically excellent and rare in the literature. Many writers on the hard problem conflate these.

(3) The article's audited tradition count (down from 7 to 5–6, with Bergson reclassified as parallel proposer and Schwitzgebel flagged as cutting against the cluster) is a sign of genuine self-critical discipline.

(4) The mode-fragility / contemplative-traditions discussion is more nuanced than the literature typically achieves: rather than dismissing contemplative reports or absorbing them, the article notes specific verifiability conditions (inter-rater agreement, neural-correlate tracking, predictive contact with behaviour).

## Recommendations

### A. Specific improvements to the target article

**A1. Engage Tulving's autonoetic consciousness.** Recollection has phenomenology of mode, not just of content. Add a paragraph acknowledging that the felt remember/know distinction (Gardiner 1988; Tulving 1985) is a window onto a process-level difference, and explain how the cluster's framing handles this. Either bite the bullet (autonoetic consciousness is itself a verdict-output of a mode-tagging operation) or concede that the recollection case is less clean than the cluster claims.

**A2. Recover the full Cleary literature.** Cite Cleary & Claxton (2018, *Psychological Science* 29[4]:635–644) and Cleary et al. (2019, 2021) showing that déjà vu generates illusory premonition and postdictive feelings *that subjects cannot tell apart from veridical ones*. This is unfriendly to a clean "verdict is the explanandum" reading because the verdict's content is systematically wrong; it favors a constructed-attribution account.

**A3. Calibrate the Naccache citation.** Acknowledge that this is a single-patient case study with a large unilateral lesion (not focal ACC), that the same patient had Iowa Gambling Task deficits, and that no direct replication of the specific dissociation exists. Cite Fellows & Farah (2005) and Cipolotti et al. (2016) for the mixed ACC-lesion literature.

**A4. Recalibrate the Hagger framing.** Cite both Hagger et al. (2016, d = 0.04, [−0.07, 0.15]) *and* Vohs et al. (2021, d = 0.06, BF₀₊ = 4.39); note the Hagger paper's own caveat against full rejection of depletion; and note Dang et al. (2021, *Social Psychological and Personality Science*, 12 labs, N = 1,775, d = 0.10, 95% CI [0.01, 0.19]) for a different task pairing showing a small significant effect.

**A5. Engage predictive processing properly.** Add explicit engagement with Hohwy (2013, *The Predictive Mind*), Clark (2016, *Surfing Uncertainty*), Schwengerer (2019), and Sandved-Smith, Hesp, Mattout, Friston, Lutz & Ramstead (2021, *Neuroscience of Consciousness* niab018) on PP-based introspection. PP is the strongest current materialist absorption story for the cluster's signature; failing to engage it is a serious gap in an "apex synthesis" claiming the architecture has not been pressed.

**A6. Engage Block (1995) access/phenomenal distinction and overflow debate.** Whether phenomenal consciousness overflows access is the conceptual battleground for the cluster's framing; the cluster currently does not deploy Block's distinction even though it is everywhere in the substrate.

**A7. Press illusionism directly rather than parenthetically.** A serious illusionist (Frankish, Kammerer 2019, *Phenomenology and the Cognitive Sciences*) will say the felt verdicts the cluster takes as data are themselves introspective misrepresentations. The cluster owes an argument that the verdicts must be taken as genuine phenomenal data.

**A8. Acknowledge the explanatory-gap-rediscovery worry.** A direct paragraph stating "We are aware our 'explanatory cost' language risks restating Chalmers's hard problem; here is the additional structure we claim beyond the hard problem." If there is no additional structure, the article's evidential ambition reduces to the hard problem.

**A9. Treat Kurzban-Inzlicht and Carruthers as absorption candidates, not convergent witnesses.** The article's own classification ("observes the signature *because* their materialist or computational models predict it") points this way; the rhetorical framing should follow. Carruthers's "there is no such thing as conscious agency" conclusion in *The Opacity of Mind* is a particularly hard pill for the cluster to swallow as a "convergent witness."

**A10. Add same-introspector counterexamples explicitly.** TOT, cognitive phenomenology of thought (Strawson 1994, Pitt 2004), the felt distinction between recall and imagination, expert phenomenology of skilled judgment (where some process-awareness *is* claimed) — these should be inventoried and either absorbed or conceded. Currently they are absent.

### B. Site-level methodology improvements

The Unfinishable Map runs an unusual AI pipeline: tenet-constrained generation, multi-layer adversarial self-review (deep-review, pessimistic-review, optimistic-review, cross-review, refine-draft, condense), citation web-verification, named-opponent reasoning-mode classification (Mode One = in-framework, Mode Two = unsupported-foundational-move, Mode Three = framework-boundary marking), evaluate_anchoring() calibration checks, "constrain-vs-establish" discipline, "altered_state_symmetry" checks, and explicit anti-cliché filters. The methodology is more sophisticated than typical AI-generated content. Yet several systemic risks emerge from reading both the target article and the changelog:

**B1. Counterargument absorption / coherence inflation.** The dominant pipeline failure mode is that adversarial reviews refine a *frame* until everything fits inside it, but the frame itself is never re-evaluated against external benchmarks. The changelog shows multiple "convergence" verdicts and repeated review of the same articles ("3rd review… 5th review… converged") — internal coherence becomes optimization target, but the cluster's signature is precisely what *external* (mainstream cognitive science) predicts without dualist machinery. **Recommendation**: institute a periodic *external-benchmark review* in which an article is evaluated against (i) what the dominant materialist research programme predicts for the same data, (ii) whether the article's evidence discriminates between materialist absorption and the dualist reading, and (iii) what observations would change the article's frame versus refine it. Currently the pipeline appears to mostly do refinement.

**B2. Tradition-counting and convergence epistemology need formal independence criteria.** The target article handles this better than most (its "two-substrates-of-three" criterion). But the broader pipeline should formalize *evidential independence* (separate datasets, separate substrates, separate generating processes) versus *interpretive independence* (different authors' vocabulary for the same dataset). Without this, the pipeline systematically inflates convergence weight. A "common-cause-null" screening step before any tradition is counted as evidence would help. The article hints at this with `[the common-cause-null discipline]` but it should be a hard gate.

**B3. Steelmanning the absorption story.** The pipeline's adversarial reviews (pessimistic, optimistic) appear to operate within the frame: they catch citation errors, calibration slips, cliché violations, and stale anchors with impressive rigor (changelog evidence is detailed). But they do not appear to require any article to articulate the strongest *external* counter-account in its own voice before stating the article's preferred reading. **Recommendation**: every apex article should have a mandatory section: "What the strongest current materialist account predicts for this data, in its proponents' own framing, before our reading." This separates absorption from defeater-removal.

**B4. Single-case-study and replication-status flags.** The pipeline already does web-verification of citations. It should also verify *evidential weight class*: single-case study, small-N pilot, preregistered replication, multi-lab consortium, meta-analysis. The Naccache 2005 citation is a single un-replicated case; treating it as a load-bearing node should require an explicit weight flag.

**B5. Heterophenomenology gates.** The pipeline currently treats first-person reports of phenomenology as data. A heterophenomenology gate — "what would the report show if introspection were unreliable in domain X" — would force the pipeline to keep distinct the (i) belief-that-I-have-phenomenal-access claim from the (ii) actually-having-phenomenal-access claim. The target article's reliance on subjects' "rich, often confident access" is exposed by Schwitzgebel's symmetric scepticism, which the article concedes but does not absorb operationally.

**B6. Predictive-processing literature drift-check.** Predictive processing is the dominant materialist framework for introspection in the current literature (Hohwy 2013, Clark 2016, Friston 2010, Sandved-Smith et al. 2021, Ramstead et al. 2022). The site's literature-drift-review process (visible in the changelog for psychedelics) should be applied to introspection articles specifically; an apex article on introspective opacity that does not engage PP is structurally incomplete by 2026 standards.

**B7. Distinguish hard-problem restatements from new evidence.** A formal check: does the article's load-bearing claim reduce to the explanatory gap (Levine 1983), the conceivability argument (Chalmers 1996), or the knowledge argument (Jackson 1982) in new packaging? If yes, label it as such; if no, articulate what additional structure is claimed. The current pipeline's "constrain-vs-establish" discipline is the right tool but is applied within articles rather than across the corpus.

**B8. Quantify "explanatory cost."** "Raises the explanatory cost on materialism" is a vague metric. If the pipeline wants this to be more than rhetorical, it needs an explicit cost ledger: which materialist commitments must be added/strengthened to absorb each cluster member, and at what cost (parsimony? coherence with established results?). Currently "explanatory cost" functions as a coherence inflator rather than a discriminating measure.

**B9. Asymmetric counterargument auditing.** The pipeline's named-opponent reasoning-mode classification (Mode One / Two / Three) is sophisticated but is applied *to the article's own engagements*. It does not appear to audit whether the article has engaged the strongest opponent. Add a "missing-engagement" pass: for each apex article, generate a list of the top 5 named opponents and check whether each is engaged at the appropriate mode. The target article would have failed this check on Hohwy, Carruthers (as absorber), Block, and Frankish.
