## Executive Summary

This is one of the most calibration-disciplined clusters on the Map. Both articles do the hard thing quantum-consciousness writing almost never does: they hold "tenet-coherent" and "evidence-elevating" strictly apart, disambiguate the several distinct things "selection" can name, and refuse to let a real structural resonance masquerade as an argument. The Consistent Histories article treats a rival on its own terms and explicitly declines the tier-upgrade that was structurally available to it — precisely the restraint the Hardline Empiricist persona exists to praise. The strongest opportunity is not new content but reciprocal cross-linking: the two articles share a four-way selection glossary yet never link to each other.

## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
Post-Decoherence Selection takes the explanatory gap seriously at exactly the right seam. The improper/proper-mixture distinction is deployed not as decoration but as the load-bearing move: the physical formalism "terminates at an improper mixture, and something non-physical actualizes the outcome." Chalmers would recognise his own strategy — locate a place where the third-person story provably runs out — done with unusual formal precision. The article resists the cheap version of the argument (consciousness "picks" a pre-existing outcome) and names that cheap version a category error.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)
The cluster engages the physics-consciousness interface seriously rather than gesturing at it. Von Neumann's placement freedom, Stapp's quantum-Zeno mechanism, and the post-decoherence variant are laid out as distinct options with their timing costs made explicit. Stapp would especially appreciate the honest acknowledgement that the post-decoherence path buys immunity to the Tegmark timing objection at the price of harder falsifiability — the trade is stated, not hidden.

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The Consistent Histories article's closing move — that "a complete third-person description can fail to fix a first-person fact" — is Nagel's point in formal dress. And it is handled with the humility Nagel's own writing models: the article calls this "a limited and contested structural analogy," not a proof, and even reclassifies it as a *disanalogy* once the loci are separated. That is first-person seriousness without first-person overreach.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
Post-Decoherence Selection avoids crude substance dualism: consciousness "does not inject energy but selects which question nature answers," contributing "only the actualization itself." The Kastner possibilist ontology (transactions actualizing one possibility from a space of real-but-sub-actual possibilities) is flagged as resonant with the Map's treatment of potentiality. Whitehead would find the process-friendly framing congenial — actualization-of-potential rather than substance-pushing-substance.

*Discipline check (per persona constraint)*: this Whiteheadian resonance is **not** used anywhere to upgrade an evidential status. The articles keep process-compatibility on the metaphysics axis and never let it do the work of "therefore this mechanism is supported." See the Hardline Empiricist entry, which confirms the tension is resolved rather than papered over.

### The Libertarian Free Will Defender (Kane)
The cluster preserves a genuine indeterministic gap for agency. It is careful to show *why* the rivals close that gap — Bohm "leaves no indeterminacy for consciousness to exploit," Everett denies selection, objective collapse makes mind "a spectator" — so the space the Map claims for agency is earned by elimination, not assumed. Kane would value that the gap is located in the physics (the problem of outcomes) rather than posited by fiat.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
Epistemic humility is everywhere and well-placed. Post-Decoherence Selection concedes its own "epistemological cost": the proposal "is harder to falsify" and "operates at the interpretive level... where empirical access is indirect at best." The Consistent Histories article repeatedly marks disputes as "genuine and unresolved" and Griffiths' position as internally coherent. McGinn would recognise a project that knows the difference between "we have not solved this" and "we have solved this in our favour."

### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
This is the load-bearing persona for this cluster, and the verdict is strongly positive.

- **Tenet-as-evidence-upgrade is praise-worthily *not* done.** The Consistent Histories article had every structural opportunity to slide CH's set-selection gap into support for dualism — "the physics leaves a gap, consciousness fills it." It explicitly declines: "To turn the affinity into an argument, the Map would have to earn a foundational move CH denies... That move is not available inside CH's own framework." Declining an available tier-upgrade is itself the strength to name here.
- **The named pattern is used correctly.** Post-Decoherence Selection marks the "only consciousness-mediated selection fits all five tenets" claim as "a compatibility/consistency check against commitments the Map has already made... not independent evidence that this family is correct." That is the *tenet-coherent, not evidence-elevating* discipline stated almost verbatim.
- **Register separation is explicit and repeated.** Both articles keep framework-selection / history-selection / outcome-selection (and the fuller preferred-basis / framework / actual-history / measurement-outcome glossary) apart, and each flags locus-conflation as *the* error to avoid. The Dowker–Kent set-selection gap is correctly located at *framework* selection, and the article refuses to read it as "a second place where the Map's outcome-selecting consciousness operates."
- **Contested claims are marked as contested.** Torres Alegre (2025) is flagged as "an arXiv preprint, not yet peer-reviewed"; the Gell-Mann–Hartle quasiclassical programme is called "active and contested... rather than a finished result"; Griffiths' own treatment of set choice as descriptive convenience is cited *against* the Map's temptation to lean on it.

The Process Philosopher and the Hardline Empiricist here **converge** — the process-friendly resonance and the evidential discipline praise *different aspects* of the same passages without conflict. Per the skill's design note, that convergence is the signal that the article has resolved the possibility/probability tension honestly. No calibration concern to flag.

## Content Strengths

### post-decoherence-selection.md
- **Strongest point**: The four-loci selection glossary ("preferred-basis / framework(set) / actual-history / measurement-outcome") followed by "The Map locates consciousness at the last of these." One paragraph pre-empts the entire family of conflation errors that plague quantum-consciousness writing.
- **Notable quote**: "What selection names is the transition *from* improper mixture *to* a single actual outcome, not the reading-off of an alternative the physics has already settled as distinct."
- **Why it works**: It converts a subtle foundations-of-physics distinction (improper vs proper mixtures) into a decision procedure for what the Map is and is not allowed to claim, and then actually *applies* it to police its own prose (the two menu-framed loci were repaired to match).

### consistent-histories-interpretation.md
- **Strongest point**: The conditional-alliance framing. Tenet 4's payoff is stated as conditional on Griffiths' single-world reading "from the start," with Bigaj (2023), Wallace, Saunders, and the Gell-Mann–Hartle 2012 "one real fine-grained history" reformulation all marshalled as reasons the alliance *cannot* be assumed unconditional.
- **Notable quote**: "It is tempting to call this an 'exact, formal version' of that gap, but that overstates it. The resonance is a limited and contested *structural* analogy."
- **Why it works**: The article argues *against its own most rhetorically useful move* and lands on a disanalogy. Treating a rival as a genuine competitor — "not as evidence for [dualism]" — is the honest-ledger posture the whole Map aspires to, executed cleanly here.
