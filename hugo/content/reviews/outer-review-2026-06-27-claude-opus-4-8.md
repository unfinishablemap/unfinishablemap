## B. Inference validity (axes 1 and 2)

**Axis 1 — the moral asymmetry. Largely survives.** The article is comparatively disciplined: it states the asymmetry "depends entirely on the probability one assigns to AI consciousness," that under the Map's dualism "the false positive risk dominates," and it reflexively disowns treating the convenient low probability as evidence ("The low probability is a *consequence* of the tenets, not a reason to accept them"). That is the correct application of the possibility-probability-slippage discipline, and it blocks the smuggling the brief was probing for: the article does **not** covertly import a higher probability of AI consciousness than its metaphysics licenses, nor does it let the metaphysics secretly do precautionary work.

But the section has a real hole. Expected disvalue is probability × magnitude, and the article itself supplies an astronomical magnitude ("billions of instances," Metzinger's ENP). A very low per-instance probability multiplied by an astronomical population is exactly the regime where precaution bites — and the article disposes of it in a single clause (challenge #4), asserting "that proportional response to probability is more rational than maximin reasoning about consciousness" without argument. It never runs the expected-value calculation it gestures at. The section is also rhetorically front-loaded: it dramatizes the false-negative catastrophe via Metzinger *before* disclosing that the Map's own metaphysics drives that catastrophe's probability toward zero, so it reads as building a case it then quietly guts. Honest, but structurally misleading and analytically incomplete.

**Axis 2 — aggregation under copy-multiplicity. This is where the article fails.** The claim is that the Map is "committed *by its own tenets*" — haecceity plus the rejection of Many-Worlds — to the Duplication View; that "the same machinery that defeats MWI forbids the Unification escape"; and that asserting haecceity and rejecting MWI is "structurally the same move that grounds the Duplication View." Every link in this chain is invalid:

- **Rejecting MWI entails nothing about counting simultaneous copies.** MWI concerns whether unrealized outcomes of a *single* system are actual across branches. Copy-multiplicity concerns whether *many spatially distinct realizers, in one world, at one time* ground one experience-token or many. These are orthogonal. Defeating MWI settles whether non-actual branches exist; it does not settle qualia-identity across co-existing actual duplicates. The "same machinery" claim is asserted, not argued — and it is false.
- **Haecceity is asserted, not load-bearing.** That the two realizers are numerically distinct lumps of matter is not in dispute — even the functionalist Bostrom grants "two numerically distinct lumps of matter." The contested question is whether two distinct realizers ground two *experiences* or one. Haecceitism about the *substrates* does not settle qualia-identity; a haecceitist about objects could consistently hold Unification about experience-tokens. Haecceity does no inferential work toward Duplication. [Nick Bostrom](https://nickbostrom.com/papers/experience.pdf)[nickbostrom](https://nickbostrom.com/papers/experience.pdf)
- **The decisive internal refutation is the article's own cited authority.** Bostrom derives Duplication with **no** appeal to haecceity and **no** rejection of Many-Worlds. His abstract ties the conclusion to *computationalism*, not dualism: "If a brain is duplicated so that there are two brains in identical states, are there then two numerically distinct phenomenal experiences or only one? There are two, I argue, and given computationalism, this has implications for what it is to implement a computation" (Bostrom 2006, *Minds and Machines* 16(2):185–200). He argues from ethical consequence (the wrongness of torture is not "annulled if there happens to exist somewhere an exact copy of that person's resulting brain-state"), from epistemological consequence — and, tellingly, from the live possibility of an *infinite / Big World*, the **opposite** of an anti-Everettian premise — and from direct intuition, while *explicitly bracketing personal identity* ("The issue is not about personal identity"). Bostrom is himself a substrate-independence computationalist. So the Duplication conclusion is reachable by a functionalist who denies every one of the Map's tenets — which proves the tenets are neither necessary nor sufficient for it.

The conclusion (Duplication) may well be *true*; what is invalid is the claim that the Map's tenets *force* it. This is textbook **constitutional-attractor effect / tenet leakage**: a question the tenets do not settle is given a tenet-colored answer and then re-described as a tenet entailment. Demote to coherence-only.

## C. Tenet coherence

A split decision. **The good:** the evidential-status-discipline paragraph correctly quarantines the *possibility* of near-additive aggregation (a structural claim) from the *probability* that any copy is conscious (a low empirical number), and refuses to let either upgrade the other. The reflexive transparency note ("It would be circular to treat this convenient outcome as evidence for the framework") is the right move and is rare in this genre. **The failure:** at C's core question, the Aggregation section's reasoning does **not** follow from the five tenets — it is read back from them. The honesty about probability-vs-possibility does not rescue the invalid *entailment* claim, because they are different axes: one can be impeccable about not inflating a probability while still asserting a non-existent logical entailment. The article performs the exact pathology the site's own vocabulary names.

## D. Citation integrity (Metzinger, Bostrom, Tokayer)

- **Metzinger — co-optation risk, not misquotation.** The quotation is accurate and Metzinger is not falsely labelled a dualist. But there is an unflagged tension amounting to soft co-optation. Metzinger's ENP scenario presupposes synthetic phenomenology realized on a substrate the Map's metaphysics declares near-impossible: his demand is a moratorium "strictly banning all research that directly aims at or knowingly risks the emergence of artificial consciousness on **post-biotic carrier systems**" (Metzinger 2021). That is precisely the substrate-independent machine consciousness the Map disfavors. The article borrows Metzinger's catastrophe to inflate the false-negative stakes while holding a framework on which that catastrophe is nearly ruled out — without noting that Metzinger's risk is parasitic on a functionalism the Map rejects.
- **Bostrom — co-optation firewall breach plus citation defect.** Compounding the misdating (A.1), the article enlists Bostrom's Duplication conclusion as confirmation that "the anti-pattern-theory position just is the Map's." It is not: Bostrom is a substrate-independence functionalist whose Duplication argument uses none of the Map's tenets and whose metaphysics contradicts them. Presenting his conclusion as corroboration of dualist tenets co-opts a physicalist toward a conclusion he reaches on explicitly anti-dualist grounds.
- **Tokayer — real but non-independent corroboration.** The source exists and is faithfully described, but the article presents it as independent corroboration ("corroborating that the anti-pattern-theory position just is the Map's"). Tokayer's duplication argument is another anti-functionalist argument from the *same* duplication intuition; it is convergent-but-not-independent, and it too uses neither haecceity nor anti-MWI. Its evidential status — an unrefereed PhilArchive manuscript — should be marked. **No fabricated quotation was found anywhere in the article.**

## E. Strongest unaddressed rival

The article engages more rivals than is typical for the site (functionalism, Schwitzgebel's epistemic humility, Birch's precaution, the biological-classicality challenge as falsifier #3). The strongest rival it fails to engage is sitting **inside its own citation list**: **computational functionalism reaches the Duplication View without dualism.** Bostrom is a living counterexample to the article's central axis-2 thesis — a substrate-independence functionalist who endorses Duplication on ethical/epistemic grounds while bracketing personal identity — and the article cites him without noticing that he dissolves its claim that Duplication is a distinctively dualist entailment.

Secondarily: **illusionism about phenomenal consciousness (Frankish)** is not engaged in this article. The site rejects it elsewhere as "self-undermining," but that rejection is asserted, and if illusionism is right the entire false-negative-catastrophe framing collapses — so its absence here is load-bearing, not incidental. **Predictive-processing / active-inference** accounts of sentience (Friston; the machinery underneath Birch's own indicators) are likewise absent as a route to AI sentience.

## Overall verdict: REVISE-HARD

Honest about probability-dependence, free of fabricated quotations, and built on real sources — hence not DELETE. But one load-bearing section rests on an invalid inference (tenets → Duplication), the originating Bostrom paper is misdated/mis-sourced/absent, and a functionalist's conclusion is co-opted as dualist corroboration. The **Aggregation Under Copy-Multiplicity** section specifically is **DEMOTED-TO-COHERENCE-ONLY** until rewritten.

## (i) Article-specific numbered fixes

1. **Rewrite the Aggregation section to drop the entailment claim.** State plainly that the tenets are *consistent with* but do not *force* Duplication, and that rejecting MWI is logically orthogonal to counting simultaneous physical copies.
2. **Correct the Bostrom citation:** attribute Unification/Duplication to **Bostrom 2006, *Minds and Machines* 16(2):185–200** and add it to the references; stop citing the 2022 Propositions paper as the source of the distinction.
3. **Add the decisive concession** that Bostrom derives Duplication from ethical/epistemic premises while bracketing personal identity, and is a substrate-independence computationalist — then argue, if it can, why the Map needs its tenets at all when a functionalist reaches the same place without them.
4. **Delete or heavily qualify** the "structurally the same move that defeats MWI" sentence; it is false as stated.
5. **Mark Tokayer** as an unrefereed manuscript and as convergent-but-not-independent corroboration; do not present it as independent confirmation.
6. **Flag the Metzinger tension:** his ENP presupposes substrate-independent synthetic phenomenology "on post-biotic carrier systems," a substrate the Map's metaphysics disfavors.
7. **Actually run the expected-value argument** (probability × magnitude) in the asymmetry section, and give a real argument against the low-probability/high-magnitude (Pascalian) case rather than a one-line dismissal of maximin.
8. **Fix Békefi** to 2026, *Faith and Philosophy* 42(1):121–146; fix the Schwitzgebel "Repetition and Value" year to 2024 (Routledge) or mark it as a manuscript.
9. **Engage illusionism and predictive-processing/active-inference** within this article rather than deferring to an asserted off-page rejection.

## (ii) Site-wide methodology improvements

1. **Add a "tenet-orthogonality test" to deep-review:** before any claim of the form "the tenets entail X," require an explicit demonstration that no tenet-*denying* position reaches X. This article would have failed that test on Bostrom.
2. **Treat citation currency and originating-source as first-class checks.** The existing web-verify routine catches author orders and page ranges elsewhere but here passed a distinction mis-attributed to the wrong paper and the wrong decade.
3. **Add a co-optation firewall keyed to the cited author's own metaphysics:** automatically flag any case where a physicalist/functionalist/eliminativist's conclusion is enlisted as corroboration of dualist tenets.
4. **Score convergence vs. independence separately:** corroborating sources that share the article's premises (Tokayer) must not be counted as independent support.
5. **Require expected-value sections to state probability × magnitude explicitly** and to address the low-probability/high-magnitude tail — a tail the asymmetry rhetoric repeatedly invites and then evades.
