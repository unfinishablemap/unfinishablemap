## Praise from Sympathetic Philosophers

### The Property Dualist (Chalmers)
`consciousness-and-the-authority-of-formal-systems` earns Chalmers' approval for converting the authority of logic into a clean explanatory-gap exhibit: "A computer executes modus ponens without experiencing this compulsion… The output matches what logic demands, but the machine does not encounter logic's demand." That is the hard problem stated in the register of normativity — functional execution leaves the *encounter with the demand* unaccounted-for. Citing `carrolls-regress` would make this the historically grounded version of the same gap rather than a freestanding intuition.

### The Quantum Mind Theorist (Stapp)

### The Phenomenologist (Nagel)
The authority-of-logic article is, fittingly, anchored on Nagel's own *The Last Word* (1997): "to challenge reason's authority, one must exercise that very authority." It centres the first-person *experience* of logical compulsion — modality, impersonality, transparency — exactly as Nagel would want: the bindingness is phenomenal data, not a behavioural regularity to be explained away.

### The Process Philosopher (Whitehead)
`carrolls-regress` avoids crude substance-talk: it treats *taking-as* as a primitive *operation* ("seeing-that," "tacit inference") rather than a substance inserted into a mechanism, and it holds Polanyi's tacit-integration claim and Brandom's social-normative alternative in genuine tension rather than dismissing either. **Constraint check:** the article's dualist conclusion is explicitly flagged as speculation ("The Map speculates — flagging this as speculation — that *taking-as* may be one face of the irreducibly mental") and it expressly declines to claim the regress *settles* anything. No tier-upgrade is performed on process-philosophical resonance.

### The Mysterian (McGinn)
`carrolls-regress` shows exemplary epistemic humility: it grants the deflationary proof-theoretic reading its full force ("the position of most mainstream logic textbooks"), states plainly that the inferentialist reading is "contested" and that "the article's later moves rest on the inferentialist reading being defensible, not uncontested," and rates the Gödel resemblance as "cousins, not siblings." ### The Hardline Empiricist (Birch)
The load-bearing verdict for this review, and it is favorable. `carrolls-regress` does the praise-worthy thing of *declining* the tier-upgrade its own subject invites: the inferentialist reading is presented as "one of the strongest available arguments that the question is open" with the burden placed on reductive accounts — not as a result establishing the non-physical. The five-tier discipline is honoured by the explicit "flagging this as speculation" hedge on the dualism inference. This is tenet-as-evidence-upgrade praise-worthily **not** done: the regress had every structural opportunity to be cashed out as positive evidence for an irreducible mental operation and was deliberately held at "the question is open." Both the Process Philosopher and the Empiricist praise this article without conflict — the tension is resolved, not papered over. No calibration slippage found in the foundational layer examined.
