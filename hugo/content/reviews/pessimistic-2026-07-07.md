## Critical Issues

### Issue 1: The discriminating signature equivocates on "value-blind" and does not separate the middle path from its live rival

- **File**: `obsidian/topics/graduated-middle-path-valence-modulated-attention.md`
- **Location**: "The Discriminating Signature" table (lines 68–74) vs. "Non-Epiphenomenality, Audited" (line 58)
- **Problem**: The table assigns the **Pure value-blind** horn "no valence advantage / no valence advantage" — i.e. valence is inert even on attention. But eight lines earlier the article says: *"The value-blind steelman's parsimony case does not touch this — it grants that valence can inform a steering channel."* The sibling `the-steelman-for-value-blind-selection.md` reinforces this in its own further-reading gloss: it describes *"this steelman's 'valence only informs the channel' concession"* as the thing that *"maps onto"* the middle path. If the value-blind steelman grants that valence informs attention, then that position predicts a valence advantage under free attention (valence → attention → Zeno stabilisation → valenced option wins) that is **abolished under the clamp** — which is exactly the middle-path row, not the "no advantage" row. The article therefore uses "value-blind" in two non-identical senses without flagging it: the *strict* horn (valence inert on attention) in the table, and the *concession* horn (valence informs the channel) in the prose. The concession horn is the one the steelman actually holds, and it is empirically co-extensive with the graduated middle path. Consequence: the "abolition-by-clamp" signature genuinely separates the value-sensitive horn (residual) from the rest, but it does **not** earn the middle path empirical daylight from the value-blind steelman's live position — it only separates it from a strict value-blind strawman.
- **Severity**: High (this is the article's headline contribution — a "discriminating test" that under-discriminates against the one rival it most needs to distinguish itself from)
- **Recommendation**: `refine-draft` pass to (a) distinguish explicitly "pure value-blind (valence inert on attention)" from "value-blind-with-valence-informed-attention," (b) concede — symmetrically with the honest active-inference concession already made in "The Physicalist Rival the Test Cannot Reach" — that the steelman's concession-position is empirically co-extensive with the middle path (both predict advantage-then-abolished), and (c) re-state precisely what the clamp *does* earn: separation of the value-sensitive horn from the two value-blind-selection positions, and a specification of the middle path's causal skeleton, rather than a test that isolates the middle path from all rivals. This preserves the article's honest, deflationary posture (it already concedes co-extensiveness with active inference) and merely extends the same honesty to the intra-fork comparison.

## Counterarguments to Address

### "The clamp isolates the middle path"
- **Current content says**: The abolition-by-clamp pattern is "unique to the middle path"; neither pure horn predicts it (line 74).
- **A critic would argue**: Any value-blind position that lets valence *aim* attention (which the steelman explicitly does) predicts the same abolition-by-clamp. "Unique to the middle path" is true only against a value-blind horn in which valence never touches attention — a position the article's own steelman does not hold.
- **Suggested response**: Reframe the uniqueness claim: the abolition-by-clamp is unique *relative to the value-sensitive horn's residual*, and it is the empirical signature *shared by* the middle path and the steelman's concession-position. The daylight the middle path claims over value-blind selection is conceptual (it makes the concession a positive thesis and specifies gain+targeting), not empirical.
