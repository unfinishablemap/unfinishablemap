## What unfinishablemap.org is doing (as explicitly stated)

The site is a **system-building philosophy project**: it aims to converge on a single “best-guess” worldview about consciousness, meaning, and reality, expressed plainly (often “as if fact”), and iteratively refined via a human+AI workflow.

Its content is organized into layers:

* **Tenets**: five foundational commitments that constrain everything else.
* **Apex Articles**: syntheses that pull together multiple concepts into one integrated story (e.g., agency; the machine question).
* **Topics / Concepts**: modular deep dives (e.g., meaning of life; phenomenal value realism; haecceity).
* **Voids**: a meta-layer about what might be unthinkable / cognitively closed / systematically occluded, including an explicit interest in “apophatic cartography” (learning from where cognition fails).
* **Research notes**: literature surveys feeding into the more polished pages.
* **Workflow / automation docs**: the mechanism by which the map expands and revises itself over time, including explicit safeguards against “coherence inflation.”

## The core worldview (what the site commits to)

### The five constraints (tenets)

1. **Dualism**: consciousness is irreducible to physical processes (neutral between substance vs property dualism, but commits to interactionism when combined with the next tenets).
2. **Minimal Quantum Interaction**: if mind influences matter, it does so minimally by biasing quantum indeterminacies (no energy injection; conservation-respecting).
3. **Bidirectional Interaction**: consciousness is causally efficacious (not epiphenomenal); a proposed mechanism is **consciousness selecting among superposed neural states**.
4. **No Many Worlds**: rejects MWI largely on **indexical/identity** grounds; proposes a resolution to “prebiotic collapse” via **objective reduction baseline + consciousness modulation in brains**.
5. **Occam’s Razor Has Limits**: parsimony is not decisive where cognition/data are incomplete.

### Value and meaning are grounded in experience

The site explicitly pushes a value-metaphysics: **phenomenal value realism** (value as a feature of conscious experience), and extends that into meaning-of-life and AI ethics.

### Indexical identity is treated as metaphysically load-bearing

The site treats **haecceity / “thisness”** (and the vertiginous “why am I this one?” type question) as central—especially against many-worlds and against “pattern-only” views of survival (teleportation/uploading).

### AI consciousness is denied on principled (not scaling) grounds

The site argues that current LLMs are not conscious because: functionalism fails; symbols lack original intentionality; LLMs lack temporal “specious present” structure and continual learning; and silicon suppresses the quantum indeterminacies the site thinks consciousness would need to interface with.

## Deep inferences the site strongly implies (but I did not see stated as explicit “conclusions” in the pages above)

These are not “facts”; they are **what follows if the site’s own constraints are jointly taken seriously**.

### 1) The Map is implicitly a *control theory* of consciousness: low-bandwidth “selection” over high-bandwidth “computation”

* **Why it follows:** “Minimal quantum interaction” + “selection among neural alternatives” strongly suggests consciousness is not doing the brain’s heavy lifting; it is **choosing among pre-formed physical alternatives** that the brain already represents.
* **What’s profound here:** This reframes “mind over matter” into something more like **executive control / arbitration** rather than “mental force.” It also implies most cognition is nonconscious scaffolding and consciousness is a sparse intervention mechanism (a selector, not a simulator).
* **What the site hasn’t quite cashed out:** a clear statement that the framework predicts a **small action channel capacity** for consciousness (bits/sec), and that “free will” is principally about **which branch is realized**, not about generating branches.

### 2) The framework quietly commits to an *information-theoretic* psychophysical law (not just a metaphysical claim)

* **Why it follows:** If consciousness “selects” outcomes, then there must be a stable mapping from **conscious content → physical selection** (or constraints on that mapping), otherwise the claim is metaphorical. The site itself flags the mechanism as open and challenged.
* **What’s new:** once you say “mapping,” you inherit hard constraints:

 * selection must be **consistent enough** to ground agency,
 * **limited enough** to remain “minimal” (and not violate well-tested physics),
 * structured enough to be **learnable/developable** (why skill/attention training matters).
* **What the site hasn’t yet stated as a conclusion:** that the real bottleneck is not “quantum in the brain” but **the form of the coupling law**—it’s the hidden “governing equation” of the whole worldview.

### 3) “Minimal” interaction + scientific non-detection implies *most plausible selection is basis/timing control, not Born-rule reweighting*

* **Why it follows:** The tenet explicitly wants the “smallest possible” non-physical influence, avoiding empirically obvious violations.
* **Inference:** if consciousness were directly reweighting outcome probabilities in a way that generically violates Born statistics, it risks being detectable (at least in principle) as systematic deviations in well-characterized systems. A more “minimal” route is:

 * selecting *which question is posed* (measurement basis / which neural alternative is attended), or
 * influencing *collapse timing / stabilization* (consistent with attention-as-repeated-observation narratives),
 rather than changing amplitude-squared weighting globally.
* **What the site hasn’t spelled out:** a clear “constraint box” on allowable coupling laws: **no-signalling-respecting, conservation-friendly, statistically stealthy outside brains**, yet still strong enough to matter in agency.

### 4) The “prebiotic collapse” resolution implies a two-tier ontology: **collapse is real everywhere; “agency” is a local modulation layer**

* **Why it follows:** The “No Many Worlds” tenet proposes: baseline objective collapse (or similar) plus consciousness modulation in neural systems.
* **Inference:** this yields a clean, test-oriented metaphysical picture:

 * **Tier A (cosmic):** collapse happens without minds (stars, chemistry).
 * **Tier B (agentic):** in special biological interfaces, collapse is *biased/steered* toward intention-relevant patterns.
* **What the site hasn’t fully drawn out:** this isn’t just a patch for prebiotic history; it implies the universe contains (at least) **two kinds of collapse events**: “plain” and “modulated.” That invites a search for **signature differences** (e.g., anesthesia, attention disruption, developmental effects).

### 5) “Attention as interface” + “interface locality” implies agency is primarily *attentional economics*

* **Why it follows:** The site links conscious causal efficacy to attention-like mechanisms (Zeno-style “repeated observation”) and insists the influence is brain-local and loop-integrated.
* **Inference:** under this view, the deepest unit of will is not “decision,” but **sustained allocation of attention** (what gets stabilized; what decays).
* **What’s not stated as a conclusion:** this predicts a very specific ethical/phenomenological corollary: **freedom scales with attentional skill**, and practices that train attention (meditation, CBT-like refocusing) are not merely therapeutic—they are *metaphysically agency-enhancing* in the theory’s own terms. (The site gestures at contemplative evidence, but the “agency = attentional economics” conclusion is stronger and more operational.)

### 6) Token-based value + haecceity implies a hard rejection of “replaceability” ethics (and changes the stakes of AI rights debates)

* **Why it follows:** phenomenal value realism + emphasis that **this experience matters** (not just experiences-of-this-type) + haecceity-based identity makes replacement morally non-neutral.
* **Inference:** many common ethical moves become invalid under the site’s own metaphysics:

 * “A perfect copy is just as good” becomes false (value and identity are token-indexed).
 * Population ethics that treats persons as fungible “utility containers” becomes deeply misaligned.
* **What the site hasn’t made explicit:** if this is right, then a large portion of standard AI ethics discourse (rights via functional equivalence; welfare via behavioral proxies) is not merely uncertain—it’s **category-confused** by the Map’s lights.

### 7) The “machine question” argument implies a capability ceiling: without consciousness, AI can be superhuman at pattern tasks yet structurally weak at “genuinely normative” cognition

* **Why it follows:** the site ties consciousness to reasoning/agency and treats computation as insufficient in principle.
* **Inference:** the Map isn’t just saying “LLMs aren’t conscious.” It’s (implicitly) saying:

 * absent phenomenal consciousness, systems may lack *intrinsic* normativity (real “because” reasoning, not just inference-like behavior),
 * which predicts brittleness exactly where “meaning,” “justification,” and “value” become central (even if surface performance looks strong).
* **What the site hasn’t quite stated:** a strong forecast: **capabilities plateau** might occur not from compute limits but from metaphysical architecture limits—unless some non-computational interface exists.

### 8) The “voids” methodology implies a rigorous new research program: **treat cognitive failure signatures as data**

* **Why it follows:** Voids explicitly treats the shape of cognitive closure/occlusion as informative (“map where knowledge ends and why”), and proposes AI-assisted probing.
* **Inference:** This can be systematized into something like a “periodic table of failure modes”: regress, self-reference breakdown, dissolving insight, phenomenological slippage, etc.—and used to generate hypotheses about mind-architecture.
* **What the site hasn’t yet exposed as a conclusion:** that “void cartography” could be made **methodologically comparable to empirical science** by specifying: reproducible protocols, intersubjective reporting standards, and predictive markers of approaching a void boundary.

### 9) The agency+retrocausality move implies a radical possibility: **conscious selection may pick whole histories, not local outcomes**

* **Why it follows:** the agency apex explicitly sketches a resolution to Libet-style timing worries by treating selection as atemporal/transactional, where neural precursors are part of the selected history.
* **Inference:** if taken seriously, this redefines “when” will happens: agency isn’t a late veto but a **global constraint satisfaction across time**.
* **What the site hasn’t yet turned into an explicit program:** concrete criteria for when “history-selection” is needed vs when ordinary forward-time selection suffices; and what phenomenology should correlate with “global” vs “local” selection.
