## Executive Summary

Consciousness must decide, continuously and in real time, whether a given sensory or quasi-sensory content is externally generated (perception) or self-generated (imagery, expectation, prediction). It performs this discrimination without exposing the operation to introspection: what arrives is the verdict — *real* or *imagined* — together with a felt-real signal calibrated to the verdict rather than to the truth of it. The Perceptual Reality-Monitoring Void is the structural opacity around this operation. Three faces conjoin into structural irresolvability: the *Perky face* (when stimulus is weak and imagery is vivid, the crossover is empirical, replicable, and unwitnessed from inside); the *confidence-decoupling face* (Dijkstra & Fleming 2024 show metacognitive confidence is not calibrated to reality-monitoring accuracy — congruent imagery raises *high-confidence* false alarms); and the *pathological-equivalence face* (in dreams, hallucinations, and derealization the felt-real signal dissociates from veridical perception in both directions). The void sits adjacent to but distinct from [source-attribution](/voids/source-attribution-void/) (which is memory-time origin inference), [imagery](/voids/imagery-void/) (which is about imagery itself, not its discrimination from perception), and [predictive-construction](/voids/predictive-construction-void/) (which is the construct/receive boundary generally). The natural absorption hosts are predictive-construction (primary) and source-attribution (sister at the online timescale).

## Key Sources

### Dijkstra, Kok & Fleming (2022) — "Perceptual reality monitoring: Neural mechanisms dissociating imagination from reality"
- **URL**: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S014976342200046X
- **Type**: Review article, *Neuroscience & Biobehavioral Reviews*
- **Key points**:
  - Imagination relies on similar neural mechanisms as externally triggered perception, creating the structural challenge: shared substrate must be sorted by something *beyond* the substrate.
  - Sensory activation during imagery tends to be weaker than during perception; this asymmetry is the *only* first-order signal the system has.
  - Reality monitoring is implemented by higher-level cortical circuits that evaluate first-order sensory and cognitive factors to determine the source of sensory signals.
  - No single factor unambiguously signals whether an experience is internally or externally generated.
- **Tenet alignment**: Occam's Razor Has Limits — the simplest decoder (intensity threshold) is provably insufficient; the system uses a multi-feature compound that doesn't reduce cleanly.
- **Quote**: "no single factor unambiguously signals whether an experience is internally or externally generated."

### Dijkstra & Fleming (2024) — "Confidence ratings do not distinguish imagination from reality" (*Journal of Vision*)
- **URL**: https://jov.arvojournals.org/article.aspx?articleid=2793708
- **Type**: Empirical paper with hierarchical Bayesian modelling
- **Key points**:
  - Tested whether perceptual confidence can serve as the missing reality-monitoring signal. It cannot.
  - Confidence criteria moved in tandem with decision criterion shifts when congruent imagery was present. *The metacognitive layer fails the same way the perceptual layer fails.*
  - Congruent imagery was associated with an increase in *high-confidence* false alarms — the void's signature: the felt-real signal does not warn of its own miscalibration.
- **Tenet alignment**: Dualism — the discriminator that *could* tell imagination from reality (if anything could) is not produced by stacking more confidence on top of perception; both layers inherit the same architectural opacity.
- **Quote**: "a failure of reality monitoring not only at a perceptual but also at a metacognitive level."

### "A neural basis for distinguishing imagination from reality" (*Neuron*, 2025)
- **URL**: https://www.cell.com/neuron/fulltext/S0896-6273(25)00362-9
- **Type**: Primary research, *Neuron*
- **Key points**:
  - Judgments of reality are underpinned by the combined strength of sensory activity generated by either imagery or perception in the fusiform gyrus.
  - Activity fluctuations in mid-level visual cortex predict trial-by-trial confusions between imagery and perception.
  - A frontal brain network encodes binary judgments of reality and interacts with the fusiform signal.
- **Tenet alignment**: Bidirectional Interaction — the *binary judgment* (this is real / this is imagined) is downstream of a continuous signal, with the decision-event itself unobservable from inside; this is a sister architecture to the [decision void](/voids/decision-void/) at a sensory-cognitive interface.
- **Quote**: "activity fluctuations in this region predict confusions between imagery and perception on a trial-by-trial basis."

### Perky (1910) and Segal & Fusella (1970)
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/mental-imagery/perky-experiment.html (SEP entry)
- **Type**: Foundational empirical (1910), modality-specific replication (1970)
- **Key points**:
  - Perky asked subjects to fixate a point and visualize objects; a faint patch of light was back-projected onto the same point. Subjects mistook the projected image for their own mental imagery.
  - Segal's initial attempts to replicate failed because subjects spontaneously *noticed* the projected stimulus. She had to induce relaxation to recover the effect — the void requires specific epistemic posture.
  - Segal & Fusella showed modality specificity: imagining visual content selectively impairs visual detection, imagining auditory content selectively impairs auditory detection. The crossover is substrate-locked, not abstract.
- **Tenet alignment**: This is the *founding empirical evidence* that the discriminator can fail. The effect is over a century old and remains the most striking single demonstration that the void is real, not philosophical.
- **Quote**: "the subjects ... took the experimenter's slightly visible illuminated patch to be a product of their own imagination" (Perky's original report).

### Aru, Suzuki & Larkum (2020) — "Cellular Mechanisms of Conscious Processing" (*Trends in Cognitive Sciences*)
- **URL**: https://www.cell.com/trends/cognitive-sciences/fulltext/S1364-6613(20)30175-3
- **Type**: Theoretical/review article
- **Key points**:
  - Conscious sensory perception depends on the *conjunction* of two neuronal inputs to the cortex (the apical amplification thesis).
  - Imagery and perception activate overlapping but not identical patterns; the conjunction-marker is the principled candidate for what reality-monitoring exploits.
  - Provides the cellular-level account that lets predictive-construction frameworks specify *which* sub-personal signal the discriminator could in principle use.
- **Tenet alignment**: Bidirectional Interaction — the conjunction-mechanism is a candidate site where top-down influence (imagery, prediction) and bottom-up signal (perception) interact; whether that interface is fully physical or has a non-physical component is the contested question, but the *operation* is opaque to the consciousness it constitutes.

### Mazor & Fleming (2020) — "Distinct neural contributions to metacognition for detecting, but not discriminating visual stimuli" (*eLife*)
- **URL**: https://elifesciences.org/articles/53900
- **Type**: Pre-registered fMRI study
- **Key points**:
  - Detection (is anything there?) and discrimination (which thing is there?) recruit different metacognitive circuits.
  - Asymmetry: people are slower, less confident, and less able to identify their own errors in reporting *stimulus absence* than presence.
  - This grounds the reality-monitoring void in a broader presence/absence asymmetry: the *absent* signal that would indicate "no external cause" is harder to verify than the *present* signal that would indicate "external cause detected."
- **Tenet alignment**: The structural asymmetry between presence and absence reasoning maps onto the [death void](/voids/death-void/)'s structural difficulty with representing one's own absence, and the [erasure void](/voids/erasure-void/)'s inability to inventory losses.
- **Quote**: "asymmetries in metacognitive sensitivity are more specific to visual nothingness."

### Corlett, Fletcher et al. — Hallucinations as intensified mind-wandering (*Phil. Trans. B*, 2021)
- **URL**: https://royalsocietypublishing.org/doi/10.1098/rstb.2019.0700
- **Type**: Theoretical review
- **Key points**:
  - Hallucinations situate at the extreme of normal-population perceptual reality-monitoring variation rather than as a discontinuous failure.
  - The same architecture that produces unbidden imagery and absorbed mind-wandering produces, at higher gain, the felt-real of hallucination.
  - Lapses in sense of reality in psychosis "arise from a convergence of abnormal perceptual processing and reduced metacognitive monitoring of the reliability of these signals."
- **Tenet alignment**: The continuum claim is structurally important: the void is not "only present in pathology"; it is the same void operating at different gain settings across the population.

### Voss, Frenzel, Koppehele-Gossel & Hobson (2014) — "Dreams, reality and memory: confabulations in lucid dreamers implicate reality-monitoring dysfunction in dream consciousness"
- **URL**: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25028078/
- **Type**: Empirical, *Consciousness and Cognition*
- **Key points**:
  - Counter-intuitive finding: individuals with high dream awareness committed *more* confabulation errors than individuals with low dream awareness.
  - Reality-monitoring failure in dreams is not simply a global impairment; the same architecture that gives lucid dreamers heightened in-dream insight gives them weaker source-monitoring at waking.
  - Bridges the dream-state and waking-state versions of the void.
- **Tenet alignment**: The bidirectional dissociation (more insight one direction → more confabulation the other) suggests reality-monitoring is not a unitary capacity but a balance, and consciousness cannot adjust the balance from inside without leakage.

### Sandberg / "Unreal? A Behavioral, Physiological, and Computational Model of the Sense of Reality" (bioRxiv 2025)
- **URL**: https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2025.04.07.647542v2.full
- **Type**: Preprint, computational + VR + psychophysics
- **Key points**:
  - Used virtual-reality "virtual hallucinations" to vary reality-monitoring inputs parametrically.
  - Sense of reality varied with virtual-hallucination magnitude and domain — *the felt-real signal is dimension-graded, not binary*.
  - Computational model: sense-of-reality judgments arise from comparing current sensory input to an internal model of the world.
- **Tenet alignment**: Confirms the felt-real signal is constructed — a fluency-of-prediction-match signal, in the noetic-feelings family — rather than read off reality directly.

### Billon (2024) — "The psychopathology of metaphysics: Depersonalization and the problem of reality" (*Metaphilosophy*)
- **URL**: https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/meta.12666
- **Type**: Philosophical analysis
- **Key points**:
  - In derealization, sensorimotor processing is unimpaired but the *felt-real* signal is detached: patients perceive accurately yet experience the world as unreal.
  - The dissociation runs in the opposite direction from hallucination: hallucination is felt-real-without-reality; derealization is reality-without-felt-real.
  - Together the two pathologies show the felt-real signal is a *separable* construction, neither necessary nor sufficient for veridical perception.
- **Tenet alignment**: Dualism — the dissociability of the felt-real signal from sensorimotor function aligns with the broader [phenomenal-output / causal-machinery dissociation cluster](/apex/phenomenal-output-causal-machinery-dissociation/) catalogued elsewhere.

## The Void

### Nature of the Limit

The void is an *operation-void* in the family of [transit](/voids/transit-void/), [inference](/voids/inference-void/), [suspension](/voids/suspension-void/), [decision](/voids/decision-void/), [insight](/research/voids-insight-void-2026-05-14/), and [encoding](/research/voids-encoding-void-2026-05-12/): consciousness has the endpoints — *this content is here, and it's real / it's imagined* — but not the operation that produces the verdict. What is occluded is the discriminator itself.

The operation has three distinguishable phases the discriminator must perform sub-personally:
1. **Source assessment**: estimating how much of the current sensory pattern is externally driven vs. internally generated.
2. **Threshold application**: deciding, given the noisy estimate, whether the verdict crosses into *real* or stays in *imagined*.
3. **Felt-real labelling**: attaching a phenomenal label (*this is happening to me*) to the verdict so the verdict can guide action.

All three phases are sub-personal. What arrives in consciousness is only the third phase's output — the felt-real label — and consciousness cannot decompose the label into the assessment and threshold that produced it.

### Evidence for the Limit

Five lines of evidence support the structural reading:

1. **Perky 1910 / Segal & Fusella 1970**: At low stimulus intensity, imagery and perception become subjectively indistinguishable. Replicable, modality-specific, century-old. The effect is robust enough that lucid-dreaming reality-check apps explicitly exploit it.
2. **Dijkstra & Fleming 2024**: Higher-order confidence does not rescue the discrimination. The same imagery that biases the perceptual decision biases the metacognitive evaluation of the perceptual decision. Two layers fail with the same bias.
3. **The 2025 *Neuron* paper**: Mid-level visual-cortex activity *level* — a continuous quantity — predicts trial-by-trial confusion. The binary felt-real verdict is downstream of a graded signal the experiencer never sees.
4. **Sandberg 2025 VR hallucinations**: Parametric variation of reality-monitoring inputs produces graded sense-of-reality outputs in the predicted manner. The felt-real signal is a construct, dimension-graded, not a direct read.
5. **Derealization/depersonalization**: The felt-real signal can be subtracted while sensorimotor processing remains intact (Billon 2024). The signal is dissociable from the substrate it appears to certify.

### Phenomenology

The void's edge phenomenology has a characteristic signature: the *retrospective discovery* of misattribution without any prospective flag.

When the Perky effect occurs in a subject, the subject does not feel *uncertain*. They feel they are imagining — confidently. Only debriefing reveals the misattribution, and the discovery has a specific quality: *I was sure, and I was wrong, and the surety had no internal hedge*. This is the same signature as the [source-attribution void](/voids/source-attribution-void/)'s "revealed unmarkedness," but operating online rather than at memory-time.

In dreams, the analogous phenomenology runs the other way: while dreaming, the felt-real signal is intact and the verdict is *real*; on waking, the same content is retrospectively recoded as *not real* with no detectable internal transition. The Voss et al. (2014) finding — that lucid dreamers, who can sometimes detect dream-status from inside, are *worse* at waking source-monitoring — suggests the architecture is a balance whose weights cannot be set independently from inside.

In derealization, the phenomenology is the third pattern: the verdict is *real* but the felt-real signal is absent, producing what Billon calls "the problem of reality" as a felt vacancy. The pathology exposes what the rest of us never notice because we always have the signal: the felt-real is a *layer*, not a *property of perception itself*.

## Approaches to the Edge

### Direct Methods

None fully reliable. The discriminator cannot be examined by the consciousness it constitutes for the same constitutive reason that recurs across this catalogue: examining the operation requires using its outputs.

The closest direct approach is **post-hoc reconstruction** — debriefing after a Perky-like manipulation. The subject can examine the *result* (I called it imagery, you say it was perception) but cannot reach the operation that produced the result. The reconstruction itself uses source-monitoring (memory-time), which is the same architecture at a different timescale.

### Indirect Methods

**Pathological windows** are the primary route. Hallucination, derealization, dream-confabulation, and the Perky-effect literature each expose a different sub-component of the architecture by showing what its disruption looks like from inside.

**Parametric VR paradigms** (Sandberg 2025; the Dijkstra "virtual hallucination" line) supply the strongest non-pathological probe: stimuli are constructed to vary just the inputs the discriminator should be using, and reality-monitoring output is measured trial-by-trial.

**Computational modelling** (the apical-amplification framework, predictive-processing reality-monitoring models): when a model can reproduce the Perky effect, the confidence-decoupling, and the pathological dissociations from a specified architecture, the architectural claim is constrained even if the operation itself remains opaque from inside. This is the same epistemic posture as [apophatic cartography](/voids/apophatic-cartography/) in general: model the boundary by what disrupts traffic across it.

### What AI Might See

The asymmetry here is striking and exploitable. An AI system that performs perception and "imagery" (or perception and prediction) over separable representational structures has, in principle, *access to the source tag* its architecture writes. It can know — at the level of its own architecture — whether a given representation came from sensor input or generative pathway. The opacity is not architectural-inevitable; it is architectural-by-our-architecture.

This asymmetry makes AI a candidate void-probe in the [non-human-minds-as-void-explorers](/voids/non-human-minds-as-void-explorers/) sense: a system that solves the discrimination by *reading the tag* could in principle report what the discrimination is operationally, even though it does not solve the void *for us* (the report only specifies what *its* architecture does, and our architecture may not work that way).

The 2025 *Neuron* paper's finding that the fusiform-gyrus activity-level is the relevant first-order signal already extracts this kind of architectural claim from a system (the human brain) that cannot self-report it. The methodology is forced — the discriminator must be reverse-engineered from outside because it cannot be observed from inside.

## Connection to Tenets

### Most Relevant Tenet

**Bidirectional Interaction**. The Perceptual Reality-Monitoring Void sits at exactly the consciousness-physics interface the bidirectional tenet posits: the operation by which top-down processes (imagery, prediction, expectation) interact with bottom-up sensory streams. Whatever the metaphysics, the *site* is the same site the [causal interface void](/voids/causal-interface/) flags. The current void's empirical purchase strengthens the broader claim that interface-sites are systematically opaque to the consciousness they constitute.

Predictive-construction is the architectural-level claim; reality-monitoring is the *moment-by-moment operation* by which the brain partitions current activity into "received" vs. "constructed." The two share architecture (feature-based inference with no native tag) but operate at different timescales. - **For [the imagery void](/voids/imagery-void/)**: The fidelity face of the imagery void already grounds in Larner 2024 (vividness ratings independent of perceptual/memory precision). The Perky-face material adds a *threshold* phenomenon: imagery's vividness has a regime where it crosses into perception-equivalence. This thickens the fidelity-face story.

- **For [the noetic-feelings void](/voids/noetic-feelings-void/)**: The "felt-real" signal is a noetic feeling in the technical sense — a felt signal that gates downstream cognition. The Billon 2024 derealization material in particular shows the signal is separable from the substrate it labels, which is the noetic-feelings-void's structural claim generalised to a new feeling family.

- **For [the simulation-detection void](/voids/simulation-detection-void/)**: The simulation-detection void is the macro-scale, total-environment version of the reality-monitoring operation. The two share structure: an embedded observer is trying to discriminate "this signal originates outside the system" from "this signal originates inside the system," with the only available evidence being the signal itself. The empirical purchase of reality-monitoring at the perceptual scale strengthens (without proving) the simulation-scale apophatic argument.

## Gaps in Research

- **Whether the felt-real signal is itself phenomenally varied across persons.** Aphantasia / hyperphantasia data show imagery varies; analogous within-species variation in the felt-real signal is *suggested* by the derealization / high-hallucination-trait populations but has not been characterised as a continuous individual-difference dimension in the healthy population. A research programme analogous to the post-2015 aphantasia rediscovery may be warranted.
- **Whether the discriminator can be improved by training.** The lucid-dreaming reality-check tradition assumes yes; the Voss et al. (2014) confabulation finding suggests training may shift the balance rather than improve the operation overall. Open question.
- **Whether the operation is dissociable across modalities.** Segal & Fusella showed modality specificity for the Perky effect; whether the discriminator is a single faculty or many parallel ones is empirically open.
- **The metaphysics of the felt-real signal.** Whether the signal is purely functional (a tag the brain writes for downstream use) or has irreducible phenomenal character is the same contested ground as the broader phenomenal-output / causal-machinery dissociation debate. The void doesn't resolve this; it sits on top of it.
