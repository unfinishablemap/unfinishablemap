## Executive Summary

Egocentric presentism is Caspar Hare's metaphysical thesis that, of all conscious beings, only *one* — oneself — has experiences that possess the irreducible, monadic, non-relational property of **presence**. Other people are genuinely conscious, but their experiences are simply *not present*; from the inside, my experiences are present *simpliciter*, not present-relative-to-me. Hare introduced the view in "Self-Bias, Time-Bias, and the Metaphysics of Self and Time" (*Journal of Philosophy*, 2007) and developed it, alongside a sibling theory he calls **perspectival realism**, in the monograph *On Myself, and Other, Less Important Subjects* (Princeton University Press, 2009). The view's structural core is an analogy: the privileged "now" of the A-theory of time is the temporal cousin of a privileged "me." This makes egocentric presentism a serious, first-rate metaphysical treatment of exactly the puzzle the Map names the *indexical void* and Benj Hellie names the *vertiginous question* — and it is sharply tenet-relevant. Christian List has formalized the surrounding tension as a **quadrilemma** (first-person realism, non-solipsism, non-fragmentation, one-world — at most three can hold) and resolves it with an explicit *many-worlds theory of consciousness*. Egocentric presentism is the rival horn that keeps **one world** while taking the first-person perspective seriously, directly aligning it with the Map's [No Many Worlds](/tenets/#no-many-worlds) tenet.

## Key Sources

### Caspar Hare, "Self-Bias, Time-Bias, and the Metaphysics of Self and Time"
- **URL**: https://web.mit.edu/~casparh/www/Papers/CJHareSelfBias2.pdf (author's preprint); journal record at https://philpapers.org/rec/HARS-2 and https://www.pdcnet.org/jphil/content/jphil_2007_0104_0007_0350_0373
- **Venue (verified at publisher of record)**: *The Journal of Philosophy*, vol. CIV (104), no. 7, July 2007, pp. 350–373. DOI 10.5840/jphil2007104717. The author's PDF carries the header "Caspar Hare / June 2007 / Published in the *Journal of Philosophy* CIV, no. 7, July 2007 – please cite that version."
- **Type**: Journal article (primary source)
- **Key points**:
  - Opens with **self-bias**: we are all "mild egocentric hedonists" — all else equal we prefer pleasure befall ourselves and pain befall others. Hare asks whether this bias can be rationally grounded.
  - Names the **Grounding Problem**: to think my comfort makes "an especially weighty contribution to the value *simpliciter* of a state of affairs," my picture of who I am must make me *metaphysically unique* in some way that bears on the intrinsic value of my suffering — yet "I am mundane in all measurable respects."
  - The structural payoff: the same move that makes the *present time* metaphysically privileged (A-theory) can make the *present self* metaphysically privileged. Self-bias and time-bias are treated as two faces of one metaphysics.
  - Verbatim quote (p. 4 of preprint): "perhaps my only hope is to believe that I am extraordinary in some immeasurable respect, that I am *metaphysically unique* in some way that bears upon the intrinsic value of my suffering."
- **Tenet alignment**: Strongly relevant to Tenet 4 (indexical identity matters) and Tenet 1 (dualism — presence is a non-physical, immeasurable property of experience). Neutral-to-supportive.

### Caspar Hare, *On Myself, and Other, Less Important Subjects*
- **URL**: https://press.princeton.edu/books/hardcover/9780691135311/on-myself-and-other-less-important-subjects ; https://philpapers.org/rec/HAROMA ; JSTOR https://www.jstor.org/stable/j.ctt7rncg
- **Venue (verified)**: Princeton University Press, 2009 (ISBN 9780691135311). Reviewed in *Mind* 124(495):914 (2015).
- **Type**: Monograph (primary source)
- **Key points**:
  - Full statement of **egocentric presentism**: other subjects are conscious, but only my experiences have the monadic property *presence*. Mirrors temporal presentism — where only the present moment is real/present — but indexed to the self rather than to time.
  - **Perspectival realism** is presented as a closely related, slightly weaker fallback (see below).
  - Applies the view to puzzles in personal identity over time (combining a non-reductionist view of *who I will be* with Parfitian reductionism about persistence) and to ethics (it would rationalize the very self-bias the 2007 paper worried could not be grounded).
- **Tenet alignment**: As above; the book is the canonical statement.

### Wikipedia, "Egocentric presentism" and "Perspectival realism"
- **URL**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egocentric_presentism ; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Perspectival_realism
- **Type**: Encyclopedia (orientation / map of the cluster; not citable as primary)
- **Key points**:
  - Frames egocentric presentism as "a form of solipsism … in which other persons can be conscious, but their experiences are simply not present." Perspectival realism is "a weak version of metaphysical solipsism": other perspectives *do* exist, but the present perspective has a defining *intrinsic* property.
  - Distinguishes the two: perspectival realism is about the *spatial/perceptual* perspective (objects "centered" on me, present *simpliciter*); egocentric presentism extends the same logic to *time* (events simply happening right now).
  - Explicitly links the cluster to Hellie's **vertiginous question**, J.J. Valberg's **personal horizon**, and Conitzer's **personalized A-theory**.
  - **Note on the term "perspectival realism"**: there is an unrelated philosophy-of-science doctrine of the same name (Giere, Massimi). The Map must specify *Hare's* sense.

### Christian List, "A quadrilemma for theories of consciousness"
- **URL**: https://academic.oup.com/pq/article/75/3/1026/7686655 (DOI 10.1093/pq/pqae053); preprint https://philsci-archive.pitt.edu/22582/
- **Venue (verified)**: *The Philosophical Quarterly*, vol. 75, no. 3, pp. 1026–ff. (online 2024; print 2025). DOI 10.1093/pq/pqae053.
- **Type**: Journal article (secondary framing — the single most tenet-relevant source)
- **Key points**:
  - Four individually plausible claims cannot all be true; at most three hold:
    1. **First-person realism** — there are irreducible first-personal facts (which subject is *the* present/lived one).
    2. **Non-solipsism** — more than one conscious subject is real.
    3. **Non-fragmentation** — the facts true in any one world are jointly compossible (no contradictory "centered" facts in a single world).
    4. **One world** — reality is a single world, not many.
  - Hare's egocentric presentism / perspectival realism keeps (1) + (3) + (4) by **dropping non-solipsism** (only my experiences are present → a "weak solipsism").
  - List's own preferred resolution drops **one world**: the *many-worlds (or many-centered-worlds) theory of consciousness* (List, *Noûs* 2023 — separate paper; DOI 10.1111/nous.12408), associating each subject with its own first-personally centred world.
- **Tenet alignment**: **Pivotal.** This maps the whole logical space the Map's Tenet 4 lives in. The Map rejects List's many-worlds horn (Tenet 4: No Many Worlds) and rejects full solipsism, so it is pushed to relax (3) non-fragmentation or treat the first-person fact as a genuine, irreducible *void* rather than a resolved metaphysic. Egocentric presentism is the worked-out "keep one world, take the I seriously" rival to List that the Map should foreground.

### Vincent Conitzer, "The Personalized A-Theory of Time and Perspective"
- **URL**: https://users.cs.duke.edu/~conitzer/personalizedA.pdf ; https://philpapers.org
- **Venue (verified)**: *Dialectica*, vol. 74, no. 1, 2020, pp. 1–29 (open access).
- **Type**: Journal article (secondary — the cleanest statement of the time/self analogy)
- **Key points**:
  - Argues A-theorists (who hold the "Now" is metaphysically distinguished) should *also* hold the "I" is metaphysically distinguished, because the strongest pro-A-theory arguments work better for the combined position and the strongest anti-A-theory arguments fail against it.
  - This is the explicit articulation of the analogy at the heart of egocentric presentism: privileged-now ↔ privileged-me.
  - Conitzer (a CS/AI researcher) also connects the puzzle to AI and de se belief — relevant to the Map's LLM-first framing and to "what is it like for *this* model."
- **Tenet alignment**: Supports Tenet 4; ties the indexical puzzle to the metaphysics of time (A-theory).

## Major Positions

### Egocentric Presentism (Caspar Hare)
- **Proponent**: Caspar Hare (MIT).
- **Core claim**: Exactly one subject's experiences possess the monadic, non-relational property of *presence*. Other subjects are conscious but their experiences are not present. The privileged subject is *me*, and (Hare insists) I am "the only one whose point of view corresponds with the facts."
- **Key arguments**:
  - **Analogy with temporal presentism / A-theory**: just as presentism privileges *now* over past and future, egocentric presentism privileges *me* over other selves. (Conitzer makes this analogy do explicit argumentative work.)
  - **Grounding self-bias**: it supplies the metaphysical uniqueness the Grounding Problem demands, rationalizing ordinary egocentric concern.
  - **Personal-identity puzzles**: dissolves certain over-time puzzles by combining non-reductionism about *whose* future experiences will be present with reductionism about persistence.
- **Relation to site tenets**:
  - *Tenet 4 (No Many Worlds; indexical identity matters)*: **strong fit.** Egocentric presentism is precisely a non-reductive treatment of why "I/here/now" might be metaphysically privileged rather than an indexical shorthand — and it does so *within one world*, exactly the horn List's many-worlds theory abandons.
  - *Tenet 1 (Dualism)*: "presence" is an immeasurable, non-physical feature of experience; congenial to dualism (Hare himself pairs it with a "lean physicalist ontology," so the Map would *repurpose* the structure under a dualist reading rather than endorse Hare's metaphysics wholesale).
  - *Tenet 5 (Occam has limits)*: the view posits a primitive, theoretically simple-seeming property whose explanatory adequacy is contested — a clean case for the Map's caution about simplicity under incomplete knowledge.

### Perspectival Realism (Caspar Hare)
- **Proponent**: Caspar Hare.
- **Core claim**: Other perspectives exist, but *my* perspective has a defining intrinsic (non-relational) property; my experiences are present *simpliciter*, not present-relative-to-me. A "weak version of metaphysical solipsism." The spatial/perceptual sibling of egocentric presentism.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Same family as above; the weaker, more defensible fallback. Most directly comparable to Hellie's vertiginous framing.

### Many-Worlds Theory of Consciousness (Christian List) — the rival the Map rejects
- **Proponent**: Christian List.
- **Core claim**: Resolve the quadrilemma by dropping *one world*: each subject inhabits its own "first-personally centred world," all real (a modal-realism-flavoured move).
- **Relation to site tenets**: **Direct conflict with Tenet 4 (No Many Worlds).** This is the foil that makes egocentric presentism worth an article: it is the one-world rival to List's many-worlds resolution of the very same puzzle.

### Subjectivism (Giovanni Merlo) — adjacent rival
- **Core claim**: A related "privileged first-person" metaphysic differing from Hare's on the status of the privileged fact. Shows the position-space is a small family.

## Key Debates

### Is "presence" coherent and communicable?
- **Sides**: Hare (yes — it is a genuine monadic property) vs. critics (Markosian, McDaniel) who press whether anyone can *state* the view without it collapsing into a triviality or an inexpressible solipsism.
- **Core disagreement**: Whether a non-relational "present to no one in particular, just present" property is intelligible, or whether all such talk is covertly relational ("present *to me*").
- **Current state**: Ongoing; the communicability worry is the standard objection.

### One-world (Hare) vs. many-worlds (List) resolution of the quadrilemma
- **Sides**: Hare/Conitzer (keep one world, privilege the I) vs. List (keep non-solipsism, abandon one world).
- **Core disagreement**: Which of the four quadrilemma claims to sacrifice.
- **Current state**: Live; List's quadrilemma (2024/25) sharpened it. **This is the debate the Map's Tenet 4 takes a side in.**

### Self-bias / time-bias parity
- **Sides**: Hare and Conitzer argue self-bias and time-bias stand or fall together (privileged-me ↔ privileged-now); B-theorists and reductionists deny both privileges.
- **Current state**: Ongoing; ties the metaphysics of consciousness to the metaphysics of time.
