## Executive Summary

The Argument from Mechanism is a named inferential pattern (a *non-sequitur* when used uncritically) that runs: *phenomenon X CAN be produced by mechanism M; therefore all instances of X ARE produced by M*. Logically this is the conflation of **sufficiency** with **necessity** — showing M is *sufficient* to produce X (M → X, in some cases) does not show M is *necessary* for X (X → M, in all cases). Yoshihara & Yoshihara (2018, *J. Neurogenetics*) document this exact "misapplied necessary-and-sufficient" error as endemic in neuroscience (optogenetics, command-neuron debates), independently confirming that the Map's operational rejection of the move tracks a real, named methodological problem in the source literature. The Map already deploys the rejection across out-of-body experiences, psychedelics, lucid dreaming, near-death/terminal lucidity, and dissociation — but has no concept anchor naming the move itself. The decisive design requirement is **symmetry**: the discipline must cut against materialist over-reach ("we induced X with M, therefore X is nothing but M") *and* dualist over-reach (the explanatory-gap mirror: "no mechanism explains X, therefore mind" — which is argument from ignorance / affirming the consequent). The concept sits *beside but distinct from* two existing Map anchors: it is NOT [possibility-probability-slippage](/concepts/possibility-probability-slippage/) (a tenet→evidence slippage internal to the framework) and it is the *fallacy-named* companion to the *editor-discipline* constrain-vs-establish move that [evidential-status-discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/) already owns. Acceptable for a methodology/discipline concept to lean on argument rather than heavy citation; the citations below are web-verified and should not be padded.

## The Precise Logical Structure

### The core non-sequitur

The Argument from Mechanism is the inference:

> (P1) Mechanism M is sufficient to produce phenomenon X. (Established empirically — e.g. by stimulation, lesion, drug, induction.)
> (C) Therefore every instance of X is produced by M / X is *nothing but* M.

The conclusion does not follow. Establishing **sufficiency** (in some cases M produces X) is silent on **necessity** (X requires M; no other route to X exists). The two are logically independent:

- A condition can be **sufficient but not necessary**: oxygen plus a spark is sufficient for fire, but fire has other ignition routes. (Standard textbook framing — Wikipedia, *Necessity and sufficiency*; *List of fallacies*.)
- A condition can be **necessary but not sufficient**: oxygen is necessary for fire but oxygen alone does not produce fire. ("Everywhere there is oxygen there is fire" is the canonical illustration of the error.)

The Map's own canonical statement of the move (in [out-of-body-experiences](/topics/out-of-body-experiences/), the materialist-side instance) is exact and reusable:

> "That a phenomenon can be produced by mechanism M does not show that all instances of the phenomenon are produced by M. Visual experience can be induced by direct cortical stimulation; that does not make ordinary vision hallucinatory. Pain can be induced by pinching a nerve; that does not make all pain a nerve-pinch artefact. The correct inference … is narrower: there exists a neural mechanism *sufficient* to produce OBE-like phenomenology. Whether it is *necessary* … is a separate question that stimulation studies cannot answer."

### Adjacent formal-logic descriptions (each names part of the structure, none names the whole)

The move can be glossed through several established fallacies, and naming which it is keeps the concept precise:

1. **Modal / necessity-sufficiency confusion** — treating a sufficient condition as if it were necessary (the GMAT-CR "confusing sufficiency with necessity"; Yoshihara & Yoshihara's "misapplied-N&S"). This is the *cleanest* match for the materialist direction.
2. **Affirming the consequent** — "If M, then X; X; therefore M." From "the mechanism would produce this phenomenology" + "we observe this phenomenology" inferring "therefore the mechanism is operative / is the whole story." Invalid because X may have other antecedents.
3. **Affirming the converse / fallacy of the converse** — from "M causes X" inferring "X implies M."
4. **Hasty generalisation from existence to universality** — from *one* demonstrated production route to *all* instances.
5. **Genetic fallacy (in the debunking register)** — taking the causal origin of an experience (it was *induced* by M) to settle its veridicality or its nature. Inducing a hallucination-like state by stimulation does not, by itself, show that all tokens of the experience are non-veridical.

These are facets, not the concept. The Map concept should name the *substantive content-domain pattern* — the inference from a producible mechanism to a universal/exhaustive explanation in mind/consciousness debates — and note that it instantiates (1)–(5) depending on phrasing.

### Distinguish it from correlation-is-not-causation

The Argument from Mechanism is a *stronger-looking* move than mere correlation. Mechanism arguments usually have genuine **interventional** evidence (stimulation, ablation, pharmacology) — not just correlation — so "correlation ≠ causation" understates the problem. The interventional evidence *does* establish causal sufficiency; what it cannot establish is necessity, exhaustiveness, or that the induced token is representative of all tokens. Naming the concept as a sufficiency-necessity gap (not a correlation gap) is the precise framing.

## The Symmetric Application (load-bearing design requirement)

The discipline must cut **both ways**. This is the single most important feature distinguishing it from a partisan debunking tool.

### Materialist over-reach (M → "X is nothing but M")

The dominant direction in the literature. Stimulation or induction of a phenomenon is read as showing the phenomenon is *exhausted by* the mechanism:

- **OBE**: TPJ stimulation produces OBE-like phenomenology (Blanke & Arzy 2005) → "all OBEs are TPJ artefacts." The Map rejects this; empirically, Braithwaite et al. (2016) found *no* role of the right TPJ in abnormal body perception, and reviews note stimulation reproduces *the sensation of leaving the body but not all the phenomenology*, and that other regions (medial prefrontal, vestibular) are implicated — so TPJ is neither clearly sufficient for the full phenomenon nor necessary.
- **Mystical / religious experience**: Persinger's "God Helmet" temporal-lobe stimulation → "religious experience is just temporal-lobe activity." Critiqued heavily: failed independent replication (the strongest predictor of effect was prior paranormal belief, even with a *sham/inert* helmet — Granqvist et al.), and stimulation produces TLE-like perceptual disturbance, not the full content.
- **NDE**: dying-brain neuro-functional models reproduce overlapping themes → "NDEs are nothing but anoxic/REM-intrusion hallucination."
- **Psychedelics**: REBUS/entropic-brain mechanism predicts the neuroimaging → "the filter/dualist reading is otiose." The Map's [psychedelics-and-the-filter-model](/topics/psychedelics-and-the-filter-model/) already handles this correctly at the *compatibility-vs-support* level: the REBUS evidence is *compatible with* filter theory but does not *discriminate* — a sibling discipline, see below.

Yoshihara & Yoshihara (2018) is the in-literature confirmation that this is a *recognised* neuroscience error, not a Map invention: they document "sufficient" in optogenetics being read as "necessary," distorting command-neuron and circuit-function conclusions, and recommend replacing "necessary and sufficient" with "indispensable and inducing" precisely to block the slide.

### Dualist over-reach (the mirror: ¬M → "therefore mind")

The discipline's symmetry forces it to reject the dualist version with equal force:

- **The explanatory-gap mirror**: "No physical mechanism explains X (e.g. phenomenal consciousness); therefore X is non-physical." This is **argument from ignorance** ("no evidence X is physically explicable, therefore it is not") and/or **affirming the consequent** ("if dualism, we'd expect an explanatory gap; there is a gap; therefore dualism"). Physicalists rightly reply the gap may be *epistemic* (a limit on present knowledge) rather than *ontological*. The Map must NOT run this move.
- **Terminal lucidity / NDE on the dualist side**: the temptation to read "current mechanisms cannot accommodate lucidity in a degraded brain" as "therefore consciousness is transmitted, not produced." The Map's [terminal-lucidity-and-filter-transmission-theory](/topics/terminal-lucidity-and-filter-transmission-theory/) handles this by exploiting an *asymmetry the standard pattern cannot accommodate* (Christopher 2022's "loss-of-function lesion premise") rather than by a bare ignorance move — the disciplined form.
- **OBE veridicality**: "the self-model-dislocation mechanism does not exhaust OBEs; therefore consciousness leaves the body." The Map explicitly declines this: the strong dualist reading "depends on rare and contested veridical-perception cases, and the controlled evidence from the AWARE studies has so far returned zero hits on hidden targets."

The symmetric statement: *demonstrating a mechanism is sufficient does not establish it is the whole story (materialist error); demonstrating no mechanism is yet sufficient does not establish a non-physical story (dualist error).* Both convert a gap in one direction into a positive conclusion in the other.

## Place in the Map's Named-Discipline Catalogue

The Map runs a family of named methodological disciplines. The Argument from Mechanism concept must be positioned precisely against the two the task names, because superficially they look similar.

### vs. [possibility-probability-slippage](/concepts/possibility-probability-slippage/) — DIFFERENT discipline

| | Argument from Mechanism | Possibility/Probability Slippage |
|---|---|---|
| Domain | A move in the *external* mind/consciousness *evidence* debate (sufficiency→necessity over empirical mechanisms) | A move *internal* to the Map's reasoning (a *tenet* that secures metaphysical *possibility* silently read as raising *empirical probability*) |
| What is conflated | Sufficiency vs. necessity of a *mechanism* | Modal register (tenet-permits) vs. evidential register (evidence-supports) |
| Who commits it | Materialists *and* dualists, in published debate | The tenet-protected Map reasoner, in its own prose |
| Trigger object | An interventional result (stimulation/lesion/drug) | A tenet doing defeater-removal work |

They are genuinely distinct vectors and the concept page must say so. Slippage is about the framework upgrading its own claims; the Argument from Mechanism is about how *anyone* misreads mechanism evidence. They can co-occur (a Map article could both over-read its tenets *and* misjudge a mechanism claim), but the diagnoses and fixes differ.

### vs. [evidential-status-discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/) — the fallacy-named companion to "constrain vs. establish"

This is the closest relative and the relationship must be stated carefully to avoid redundancy. The evidential-status-discipline already contains the **"Constrain vs. Establish"** section and explicitly designates the OBE/TPJ passage as "the catalogue's canonical materialist-side instance," and already articulates **"The symmetric form"** (dualist must not treat phenomenology as having *established* dualism; materialist must not treat stimulation as having *established* reduction).

So the Argument from Mechanism is NOT a new discipline that duplicates this. The honest framing for the concept page:

- **evidential-status-discipline** is the *editor-internal labelling discipline* — how the catalogue *grades and labels* claims (the five-tier scale, the constrain-vs-establish register, the diagnostic test). It lives in `project/` as editor vocabulary not exposed in article prose.
- **argument-from-mechanism** (proposed `concepts/`) is the *publicly-facing named fallacy* — a content concept readers and articles can *point at*, the logical anatomy of the specific non-sequitur, portable across the induction debates. It is the *object* the constrain-vs-establish discipline polices; the discipline is the *editorial response* to encountering it.

Put differently: constrain-vs-establish is the *rule the editor applies*; the Argument from Mechanism is the *named mistake out in the world* that the rule is calibrated against. A concept page gives the Map a citeable anchor so the OBE, psychedelics, NDE, lucid-dreaming, and dissociation articles can each link to *one* named concept instead of re-deriving the sufficiency-necessity point inline. The page should explicitly cross-reference evidential-status-discipline (constrain-vs-establish) as its editor-side operationalisation and possibility-probability-slippage as its sibling-but-distinct discipline.

### Sibling: compatibility-vs-support (a related-but-different failure)

The psychedelics case is *not* primarily an Argument-from-Mechanism case — it is a *compatibility-vs-support* case (the same evidence fits filter theory and REBUS, so it discriminates neither). Worth distinguishing on the page: Argument from Mechanism = "M is sufficient, therefore M is the whole story"; compatibility-vs-support = "the evidence fits my reading (and also the rival's), therefore it supports mine." The page should note psychedelics belongs more to the latter, to avoid mis-cataloguing.

## Relation to Site Tenets

- **Tenet 5 (Occam's Razor Has Limits)** — central. The materialist Argument from Mechanism is usually *powered by* an implicit parsimony move: "we have a sufficient mechanism, so positing anything further is unnecessary." Tenet 5 denies that simplicity is a reliable guide under incomplete knowledge; a *sufficient* mechanism is not a *complete* one, and the parsimony shortcut from "M suffices" to "nothing but M" is exactly the over-application of Occam the tenet rejects. The page's tenet section should lead here.
- **Tenet 1 (Dualism)** — the concept *protects* the dualist option (sufficiency of M does not foreclose a filtered/interface reading) *without* licensing the dualist over-reach. The symmetry requirement is what keeps the concept honest under Tenet 1: it must reject "no mechanism, therefore mind" with the same rigour it rejects "mechanism, therefore nothing but mechanism." This is the tenet-discipline link the page must make explicit — Tenet 1 is the *beneficiary* of the materialist-side correction but is *bound* by the dualist-side correction.
- **Tenet 3 (Bidirectional Interaction)** — secondary: the filter/interface readings the concept keeps live are the ones on which consciousness causally couples to (is not produced by) the substrate.

The honest tenet framing: the Argument from Mechanism, named and applied symmetrically, is what lets the Map hold the filter/interface reading *open* (modal register) without claiming the mechanism evidence *establishes* it (evidential register) — directly continuous with the constrain-vs-establish discipline.
