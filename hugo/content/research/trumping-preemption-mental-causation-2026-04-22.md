## Executive Summary

Trumping preemption is a fourth species of redundant causation introduced by Jonathan Schaffer (2000), alongside early preemption, late preemption, and symmetric overdetermination. In a trumping case, two causally complete processes both run to their effect, yet only one counts as the cause because a meta-level rule (law, hierarchy, priority) selects it as authoritative. Schaffer's magician case — where only the first spell of the day is efficacious — exposed a gap in Lewis's counterfactual theory and prompted Lewis's 2000 "influence" revision. Because trumping separates *sufficiency* from *authority*, it offers a structural template for dualist mental causation: a non-physical experience can be the cause of a physical effect while its physical correlate remains sufficient in the counterfactual sense, avoiding both brute overdetermination and visible violations of physical closure. Bradford Saad's *preemptive dualism* and *delegatory dualism* (Saad 2025) are the most developed applications of this template to date; Saad's account is already covered in a dedicated research note, so this document focuses on the trumping mechanism itself, its standing in the causation literature, and what it buys (and costs) when recruited for mental causation.

## Key Sources

### Schaffer (2000), "Trumping Preemption"
- **URL**: http://www.jonathanschaffer.org/trumping.pdf
- **Type**: Journal article (*Journal of Philosophy* 97: 165–181)
- **Key points**:
  - Introduces trumping as a distinct form of redundant causation where both causal chains run to completion
  - Originating case: Merlin and Morgana both cast the same spell; laws of magic dictate that only the first spell of the day is efficacious
  - Paraphrased "major and sergeant both shout 'Advance!'" variant makes the hierarchy explicit
  - Counterfactual dependence fails in both directions (had Merlin not cast, Morgana's would have sufficed; had Morgana not cast, Merlin's would have sufficed), yet Merlin intuitively causes
  - Used as a counterexample against Lewis-style counterfactual theories
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral toward [tenets](/tenets/); supplies a general causal mechanism whose *application* to dualism aligns with Dualism and Bidirectional Interaction
- **Notable framing**: Trumping shows that sufficiency is not the same as authority — a key move for dualists who want mental causes without denying physical sufficiency

### Lewis (2000), "Causation as Influence"
- **URL**: (discussed via https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/causation-counterfactual/)
- **Type**: Journal article (*Journal of Philosophy*, reprinted in Lewis 2004)
- **Key points**:
  - Responds to Schaffer by replacing dependence with *influence*: C influences E iff altering C would alter E
  - Claim: altering Merlin's spell (e.g., making it a turn-into-newt spell) changes the outcome; altering Morgana's does not
  - Critics (including Schaffer in later work) argue Lewis-style alteration analyses still founder on *insensitive effects* — effects that are robust against wide ranges of alteration
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral

### Schaffer (2005), "Contrastive Causation" and Hitchcock (2011), "Trumping and Contrastive Causation"
- **URLs**:
  - https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11229-010-9799-y
  - https://authors.library.caltech.edu/records/b7brd-vvs47
- **Type**: Journal articles (*Synthese*)
- **Key points**:
  - Schaffer argues causation is contrastive ("c rather than c\* caused e rather than e\*"); trumping drops out naturally because the relevant contrast for Merlin's spell is "Merlin's spell rather than no spell," not "Merlin's rather than Morgana's"
  - Hitchcock pushes back: the contrast needed to validate the trumping intuition is the very thing that trumping is supposed to explain — risk of circularity
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral

### Bernstein (2014–2016), "A Closer Look at Trumping" / "What Causally Insensitive Events Tell us About Overdetermination"
- **URL**: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s12136-014-0231-y ; https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11406-014-9554-6
- **Type**: Journal articles (*Acta Analytica*, *Philosophia*)
- **Key points**:
  - Argues Schaffer's trumping cases reduce to either (i) overdetermination with *insensitive* effects or (ii) early preemption with a hidden cutting point
  - "Reading late-decision-structured trumping as asymmetric overdetermination balances intuitions about causation while respecting the metaphysical facts"
  - Important implication for mental causation: if trumping collapses into overdetermination, then a dualist who appeals to trumping may only have relocated the overdetermination worry
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral; flags a metaphysical cost for any mental-causation account that leans on trumping

### Saad (2025), "A Dualist Theory of Experience" (*Philosophical Studies* 182: 939–967)
- **URL**: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12062107/
- **Type**: Journal article
- **Key points**:
  - Preemptive dualism: experiences *preempt* their accompanying physical states from causing downstream effects
  - Delegatory dualism: two laws — Subset Law\* (experience instantiates when its causal profile matches the physical state's *default* profile) and Delegatory Law (matched-profile experiences cause instead of physical bases)
  - Only *unobservable* violations of physical closure occur; observable closure is preserved
  - Trumping provides the structural model: both sufficient, but only one authoritative
- **Tenet alignment**: Aligns with Dualism, Bidirectional Interaction, and Minimal Quantum Interaction (closure violations are restricted to the interaction interface)
- **Note**: Covered in depth at [bradford-saad-delegatory-dualism-2026-01-28](/research/bradford-saad-delegatory-dualism-2026-01-28/); this note treats Saad as one application of a broader mechanism

### Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy — *Counterfactual Theories of Causation* and *Mental Causation*
- **URLs**:
  - https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/causation-counterfactual/
  - https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/mental-causation/
- **Type**: Reference
- **Key points**:
  - SEP Counterfactual entry: Lewis's 2000 theory was developed *specifically* in response to trumping
  - SEP Mental Causation entry: does not mention trumping or Schaffer — the connection to mental causation is largely a post-2020 development, driven by Saad and a handful of related authors

### Mills (1996), Lowe (2003) — dualist-overdetermination and systematic-dependence responses
- **Type**: Journal articles (cited via SEP)
- **Key points**:
  - Mills: dualists should embrace mental–physical overdetermination as innocuous
  - Lowe: systematic mind–body dependencies make overdetermination non-coincidental
  - Contrast: trumping-based approaches try to *avoid* overdetermination by replacing it with authority-based preemption

## Major Positions

### Trumping as Genuine Fourth Category (Schaffer)
- **Proponents**: Jonathan Schaffer; Bradford Saad (as application)
- **Core claim**: Trumping is a *sui generis* causal structure distinct from overdetermination and early/late preemption; a meta-level law or hierarchy selects one of multiple sufficient processes as "the" cause
- **Key arguments**:
  - Merlin/Morgana intuition: clear causal asymmetry without physical cutting
  - Major/sergeant intuition: hierarchy makes the authoritative command count
  - Counterfactual dependence fails both ways; only a higher-order rule resolves the tie
- **Relation to site tenets**: If correct, trumping provides exactly the kind of "quiet" causal structure that [tenets](/tenets/) require — consciousness influences the physical without producing observable closure violations or energy anomalies

### Trumping Reduces to Overdetermination with Insensitive Effects (Bernstein, Hitchcock, critics of Schaffer)
- **Proponents**: Sara Bernstein; Christopher Hitchcock (partial); various counterfactual theorists
- **Core claim**: Schaffer's cases are best analyzed as symmetric overdetermination where the effect is insensitive to variations in one of the causes; the "trumping" verdict is a contrast-sensitivity artifact, not a new causal kind
- **Key arguments**:
  - Lewis's influence account handles the standard magician case
  - Asymmetric overdetermination captures the intuition without positing a new category
  - Contrastive accounts beg the question about which contrast is relevant
- **Relation to site tenets**: Mildly threatens trumping-based mental causation: if trumping is really overdetermination, then dualist accounts built on it inherit the standard overdetermination objection (Kim). The Map's response would be Mills/Lowe-style — overdetermination between mental and physical is non-coincidental because of systematic psychophysical laws

### Trumping as Mechanism for Mental Causation (Saad and related)
- **Proponents**: Bradford Saad (explicitly); implicit precursors in Lowe, Hasker, Swinburne
- **Core claim**: Dualists can analyze mental causation as experiences *trumping* their physical correlates; a psychophysical law plays the role Schaffer's "first spell of the day" law plays in the magic case
- **Key arguments**:
  - Avoids overdetermination (only the experience is authoritative)
  - Avoids epiphenomenalism (the experience, not the physical base, is the cause)
  - Avoids visible closure violations (the physical base remains causally sufficient; only the "attribution" changes)
  - Systematicity is supplied by psychophysical laws, which the dualist needs anyway
- **Relation to site tenets**: Strong alignment with Dualism, Bidirectional Interaction, and Minimal Quantum Interaction. The Map's existing [delegatory-causation](/concepts/delegatory-causation/) concept is one developed form of this position

### Pre-Trumping Dualist Approaches (for contrast)
- **Overdetermination acceptance** (Mills 1996, Lowe 2003): Dualists should simply accept mental–physical overdetermination as non-problematic
- **Epiphenomenalism** (Jackson's earlier view): Accept that mental states do not cause physical effects
- **Identity theory** (Smart, Place): Mental properties *are* physical properties, so no competition
- **Non-reductive physicalism with realization** (Bennett, Yablo, Thomasson): Mental and physical causes are not genuinely independent; no rampant overdetermination
- **Relation to trumping-based accounts**: Trumping offers a structurally distinct option — *not* identity, *not* mere overdetermination, *not* realization-subsumption. It is a fourth way

## Key Debates

### Is Trumping a Real Causal Kind?
- **Sides**:
  - *Yes*: Schaffer, Saad, contrastive causation theorists
  - *No*: Bernstein, Hitchcock (partially), Lewis (via the influence account)
- **Core disagreement**: Whether the intuition that Merlin (not Morgana) is the cause reflects a distinct metaphysical structure or is an artifact of contrast, insensitivity, or hidden early preemption
- **Current state**: Active. Consensus has not formed. The trumping verdict is widely reported as a *datum* that any theory of causation should accommodate, but its correct *analysis* remains disputed

### Does Trumping Solve Mental Causation?
- **Sides**:
  - *Yes, when paired with psychophysical laws* (Saad): Trumping plus Subset Law\* plus Delegatory Law yields a coherent dualist account
  - *No, it merely relocates the overdetermination worry* (implicit in Bernstein-style critiques): If trumping is really insensitive overdetermination, then Kim's exclusion argument reapplies
  - *Partially, but simpler routes are available* (Lowe, Mills, non-reductive physicalists): Overdetermination is non-problematic if mind–body laws are systematic; trumping may be over-engineering
- **Core disagreement**: Whether the trumping structure can do genuine metaphysical work beyond what other dualist or non-reductive accounts already offer
- **Current state**: Very active, driven largely by Saad's 2024–2025 papers; the Map should treat this as a live research program, not a settled position

### Compatibility with Physical Closure
- **Sides**:
  - *Fully compatible* (Saad): Trumping-based dualism only violates closure at the *attribution* level; observable physics is unchanged
  - *Not compatible* (standard closure advocates): Any causal authority over physical effects by non-physical states violates closure, whether or not the base state would be sufficient
- **Core disagreement**: Whether "causation" that does not alter the trajectory still counts as a closure violation
- **Current state**: Depends on one's theory of causation. On a productive/oomph theory, trumping without trajectory change is *not* real causation. On a counterfactual-or-influence theory with authority layers, it can be

## Gaps in Research

- **Empirical tests**: No searches turned up empirical or neuroscientific proposals for distinguishing trumping-based dualism from ordinary physicalism. The trumping view is intentionally designed to be empirically indistinguishable at the trajectory level, so this is by design — but the Map should be explicit about it.
- **Pre-Saad dualist uses of trumping**: Unclear whether anyone before Saad explicitly invoked Schaffer's trumping for mental causation. Possible candidates: Lowe's "invisible causation" framing, Hasker's emergent dualism, Swinburne. Worth a deeper literature search if writing an article.
- **Connection to quantum collapse models**: The Map's existing content on Stapp and on [attention-motor-planning-quantum-interface-2026-01-23](/research/attention-motor-planning-quantum-interface-2026-01-23/) treats the interaction interface as quantum-level. Whether trumping-style authority can be *realized* at a quantum collapse interface (vs. being purely an attribution-level story) is an open question this research did not resolve.
- **Access to paywalled sources**: Saad's *Australasian Journal of Philosophy* paper (2024) and Bernstein's papers were not directly accessible; summaries rely on abstracts and secondary discussion.
- **Formal semantics of "authority"**: The key move — a law selecting one of several sufficient causes as authoritative — is not yet given a standard formal semantics. Potential gap the Map could flag.
