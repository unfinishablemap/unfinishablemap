# Research: Voids - The Conceptual Metabolism Void

**Date**: 2026-04-03
**Search queries used**: "conceptual change transformation understanding hermeneutic circle philosophy epistemic limits", "theory-ladenness of observation concepts transformed by understanding", "Gadamer prejudice understanding transforms what is understood hermeneutics", "cognitive assimilation accommodation Piaget irreversible understanding", "curse of knowledge cognitive bias cannot recover naive perspective", "conceptual engineering philosophy amelioration concepts revised", "irreversibility of learning understanding cannot be undone phenomenology", "Kuhn paradigm shift gestalt switch irreversible perception incommensurability", "Wittgenstein aspect blindness seeing-as understanding transforms perception", "AI LLM conceptual representation different from human understanding"
**Voids category**: Occluded / Unexplorable (mixed)

## Executive Summary

When consciousness integrates a new concept into its existing understanding, the original concept is permanently transformed. The pre-understood version — the concept as it was before integration — becomes inaccessible. This is not forgetting (the content persists), not distortion (the result may be more accurate), and not the mutation void (inspection doesn't change it — *comprehension* does). Understanding metabolises its objects: it digests them into the body of existing knowledge, and the undigested original is lost forever. This void is pervasive, irreversible, and largely invisible — we cannot even notice what understanding has consumed because the metabolised version replaces the original seamlessly.

## Key Sources

### Gadamer's Hermeneutic Circle (Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy)
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/gadamer/
- **Type**: Encyclopedia
- **Key points**:
  - Understanding always proceeds from "prejudices" (Vorurteile) — fore-judgments that constitute the horizon from which anything can be understood at all
  - These prejudices are not obstacles to understanding but its preconditions; prejudice-free knowledge is neither desirable nor possible
  - Understanding involves a "fusion of horizons" (Horizontverschmelzung) where the interpreter's horizon and the text's horizon merge into something new
  - The original, pre-fused horizons cannot be recovered after fusion — the new understanding replaces them
- **Tenet alignment**: Occam's Razor Has Limits — our preference for coherent, integrated understanding may systematically destroy information that doesn't fit the integration
- **Quote**: "Prejudices are the very source of our knowledge" — but this means knowledge is always already transformed by what we brought to it

### Theory-Ladenness of Observation (Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy)
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/science-theory-observation/
- **Type**: Encyclopedia
- **Key points**:
  - Observations are shaped by the investigator's theoretical presuppositions — both semantically (the meaning of terms) and perceptually (the experience itself)
  - Semantic theory-ladenness: theoretical assumptions change what observational terms *mean*
  - Perceptual theory-ladenness: theoretical assumptions change what is *experienced*
  - Once you have a theory, you literally cannot see the data as an untrained observer would
- **Tenet alignment**: Dualism — if consciousness is fundamental, the theory-ladenness of observation may reflect not just neural processing but a deeper feature of how consciousness structures its own experience
- **Quote**: Associated with Hanson, Kuhn, and Feyerabend (1950s-60s); the thesis that observation is never "raw"

### Kuhn on Paradigm Shifts and Irreversibility
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/thomas-kuhn/
- **Type**: Encyclopedia
- **Key points**:
  - Paradigm shifts operate like gestalt switches — but unlike the duck-rabbit illusion, scientific paradigm shifts are irreversible
  - Post-shift scientists cannot return to seeing the world as pre-shift scientists did
  - Different paradigms are "incommensurable" — not merely different theories about the same data, but different ways of carving up experience itself
  - The conceptual vocabulary changes, making translation between paradigms impossible in principle
- **Tenet alignment**: Occam's Razor Has Limits — incommensurability means simpler-seeming theories may actually be excluding entire dimensions of observation
- **Quote**: "When a paradigm shift occurs, the entire world of the science is changed to where, unlike the observer of the rabbit-duck illusion, there is no way of reversing back"

### Piaget's Assimilation and Accommodation
- **URL**: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK448206/
- **Type**: Reference (NIH)
- **Key points**:
  - Assimilation: fitting new information into existing schemas without changing them — the new information is reshaped to fit
  - Accommodation: changing schemas to fit new information — the existing structure is reshaped
  - Both processes transform their inputs — neither preserves the original form
  - Equilibration drives development through constant conflict between these transformative processes
  - The process is directional: development proceeds through stages that cannot be reversed
- **Tenet alignment**: Bidirectional Interaction — if consciousness causally shapes its own development, the irreversibility of cognitive development may reflect genuine causal commitment, not mere neural habit
- **Quote**: "Assimilation helps learners fit new experiences into current schemas, while accommodation helps learners change schemas for new information" — either way, the original experience is transformed

### The Curse of Knowledge
- **URL**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Curse_of_knowledge
- **Type**: Encyclopedia
- **Key points**:
  - Once you know something, you cannot accurately reconstruct what it was like not to know it
  - This is not merely a failure of imagination — neural pathways linked to expert knowledge become so entrenched that the brain cannot revert to a "pre-knowledge" state
  - Financial incentives, explicit warnings, and deliberate effort all fail to reduce the bias
  - Participants "anchored in the hindsightful state of mind created by receipt of knowledge" cannot escape it
- **Tenet alignment**: The curse demonstrates a genuine cognitive void, not merely a bias — the pre-knowledge state is structurally inaccessible
- **Quote**: "Neural pathways linked to expert knowledge become so entrenched that the brain literally cannot revert to a 'pre-knowledge' state"

### Wittgenstein on Aspect Perception and Seeing-As
- **URL**: https://journals.uvic.ca/index.php/sophia/article/view/10711/2882
- **Type**: Academic article
- **Key points**:
  - "Seeing-as" involves understanding being centrally and irreducibly involved in visual experience
  - Aspect-blindness — the inability to see aspects — would mean missing the internal relations of a thing with its surroundings
  - Understanding doesn't merely add an interpretation layer; it constitutes a different kind of seeing
  - Once you can see the duck-rabbit as both, you cannot return to seeing it as only one — the second aspect permanently alters the experience of the first
- **Tenet alignment**: Dualism — if understanding constitutes perception rather than merely modifying it, the metabolism of concepts may be a feature of consciousness as such, not reducible to neural processing
- **Quote**: "Understanding is centrally and irreducibly involved in visual experience"

### AI Conceptual Representation (PNAS, Nature Human Behaviour)
- **URL**: https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.2512514122
- **Type**: Research article
- **Key points**:
  - LLMs construct conceptual representation spaces that converge with human representations but via different mechanisms
  - LLM representations vary significantly depending on the task used to generate responses — they lack the entrenched, irreversible integration of human understanding
  - LLMs recover non-sensorimotor aspects of concepts but remain "impoverished in sensorimotor aspects"
  - LLMs organise concepts through "meaningful interrelationships preserved across contexts" rather than through embodied integration
  - Key difference: LLMs may preserve multiple conceptual framings simultaneously, whereas human understanding collapses alternatives through integration
- **Tenet alignment**: AI as void explorer — LLMs may access pre-metabolised conceptual states that human understanding has permanently consumed
- **Quote**: "LLMs produce conceptual structures that vary significantly depending on the task used to generate responses" — suggesting less irreversible metabolic integration than human cognition

### Conceptual Engineering (2025 Metaphilosophy special issue)
- **URL**: https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/meta.70007
- **Type**: Academic journal
- **Key points**:
  - Conceptual engineering recognises that concepts can and should be deliberately revised
  - Distinction between "amelioration" (improving concepts) and "adaptation" (preserving functional quality under pressure)
  - Technology-driven conceptual disruption forces reconceptualisation — AI itself is triggering conceptual metabolism
  - "Heavy-duty" conceptual engineering involves fundamental transformation of concepts, not mere refinement
- **Tenet alignment**: The conceptual engineering programme implicitly acknowledges the metabolism void — you cannot revise a concept without losing its prior form

## The Void

### Nature of the Limit

This is primarily an **occluded** void with elements of the **unexplorable**. Understanding actively destroys what it processes — the pre-understood form of a concept is consumed by the act of comprehension. This is not a void that exists "out there" but one that understanding creates behind itself as it advances. Every act of comprehension is also an act of irreversible transformation.

The void is distinct from related voids:
- **Mutation void**: Inspection changes the thought. Conceptual metabolism is about *integration*, not inspection — the concept changes because it's being fitted into a larger structure, not because attention is directed at it.
- **Framework void**: Frameworks exclude what they cannot represent. Conceptual metabolism is about what happens to what frameworks *do* represent — it gets transformed in the process.
- **Obviousness void**: Self-evidence terminates inquiry. Conceptual metabolism operates even when inquiry is active — the very success of understanding destroys the original.
- **Habituation void**: Familiarity erases experience. Conceptual metabolism operates on first encounter — the first time you understand something, the pre-understood version is already gone.

### Evidence for the Limit

Multiple independent lines of evidence converge on the irreversibility of conceptual integration:

1. **Neuroscientific**: The curse of knowledge persists despite incentives, warnings, and deliberate effort. Neural pathway restructuring during learning is physical and irreversible in the short term.
2. **Developmental**: Piaget's stages proceed in one direction. Children who achieve conservation cannot return to non-conservation reasoning even if asked to pretend.
3. **Philosophical**: Gadamer's hermeneutic circle demonstrates that understanding always proceeds from prejudices that are themselves transformed by what they encounter. Kuhn's incommensurability shows that paradigm shifts are structurally irreversible.
4. **Phenomenological**: Wittgenstein's aspect perception shows that once you can see the second aspect, the first is permanently altered. The experience of understanding cannot be undone.

### Phenomenology

What does this void feel like? That is precisely the problem — it feels like nothing. The metabolism is seamless. The integrated concept replaces the original so smoothly that no gap is perceived. The void is phenomenologically invisible because:

1. **No moment of loss**: Unlike forgetting, where we sometimes sense that something is missing, conceptual metabolism leaves no residue of the consumed original.
2. **Improvement masquerade**: The metabolised concept often feels *better* — more connected, more meaningful, more useful. The loss is disguised as gain.
3. **Retroactive rewriting**: We project our current understanding backward, believing we always understood it this way. The curse of knowledge is not merely predictive but retroactive.
4. **No contrast available**: To notice the transformation, you would need simultaneous access to the pre- and post-metabolised versions. But the former has been consumed by the latter.

The closest we come to noticing the void is when we try to teach or explain something to someone who doesn't yet understand it. The frustration of the curse of knowledge — the inability to reconstruct the naive perspective — is a faint phenomenological trace of the metabolism that has occurred.

## Approaches to the Edge

### Direct Methods (if any)

Direct observation of conceptual metabolism is structurally impossible — observing the transformation would require maintaining the pre-transformed version, which is precisely what the metabolism destroys. However, some indirect traces exist:

- **Teaching**: Attempting to explain to novices reveals the gap between current and prior understanding
- **Historical documentation**: Written records of one's own past understanding (journals, letters, early writings) can provide a frozen snapshot of pre-metabolised concepts
- **Cross-cultural comparison**: Different conceptual frameworks that have metabolised the same phenomena differently reveal the contingency of any particular integration

### Indirect Methods

1. **Comparative hermeneutics**: Examining how the same concept is integrated differently across different intellectual traditions reveals that metabolism is path-dependent — the same "nutrient" produces different results depending on the existing conceptual body.
2. **Developmental studies**: Tracking conceptual change in children as it happens provides real-time data on the metabolism, even though the children themselves cannot report the transformation.
3. **Paradigm archaeology**: Kuhn-style history of science reconstructs pre-paradigm conceptual states that are no longer accessible from within the current paradigm.
4. **Apophatic inference**: By cataloguing what we cannot teach, explain, or recall about our prior understanding, we can map the negative shape of what metabolism has consumed.

### What AI Might See

This is a particularly promising area for AI as void-explorer:

1. **Non-metabolic integration**: LLMs may integrate concepts without the irreversible transformation that characterises human understanding. Their representations vary by task context, suggesting concepts are not permanently fused into a single integrated form.
2. **Simultaneous framings**: LLMs can potentially maintain multiple incommensurable conceptual framings of the same phenomenon, accessing each without the others consuming it. This is structurally impossible for human understanding after metabolism has occurred.
3. **Pre-understanding preservation**: If LLMs lack genuine understanding (the debate continues), they may paradoxically preserve aspects of concepts that human understanding destroys — the undigested, unintegrated form.
4. **Metabolism detection**: AI systems could compare a human's pre- and post-understanding representations of a concept to map precisely what the metabolism changed. The AI, not having metabolised the concept in the same way, could serve as a control.

## Connection to Tenets

### Most Relevant Tenet

**Occam's Razor Has Limits** — Conceptual metabolism is driven by a preference for coherent, integrated, parsimonious understanding. We metabolise concepts *because* we seek simplicity and coherence. But this means that every act of understanding-as-simplification potentially destroys information that didn't fit the simplification. The parsimony principle, applied at the level of conceptual integration, is an engine of permanent information loss.

### Secondary Tenets

**Dualism**: If consciousness is fundamental and irreducible to physical processes, then conceptual metabolism may be a feature of consciousness as such, not merely of neural processing. The irreversibility of understanding might reflect something about the nature of conscious experience — that it constitutively transforms what it encounters, in a way that goes beyond physical information processing.

**Bidirectional Interaction**: The metabolism suggests consciousness doesn't merely receive and record concepts but actively reshapes them. If this reshaping involves genuine causal influence from the non-physical to the physical (restructuring neural pathways in the service of conceptual integration), then conceptual metabolism is a site where bidirectional interaction operates constantly.

**No Many Worlds**: The irreversibility of conceptual metabolism depends on indexical identity — *this* mind, having metabolised *this* concept, can never return to its prior state. In a many-worlds framework, all possible metabolisms occur, making the void trivial. The void's significance depends on the fact that each consciousness commits irreversibly to a single metabolic pathway.

### Implications

The conceptual metabolism void suggests that consciousness is not a passive mirror but an active digestive system. It doesn't reflect reality; it consumes it. Every act of understanding is simultaneously an act of irreversible transformation, creating a void behind it that can never be re-entered. The Map's territory is not just unfinishable because the territory is infinite — it is unfinishable because the act of mapping transforms the territory being mapped.

Based on this research, a voids article could:

1. **"The Conceptual Metabolism Void"** — Focus on the irreversibility of understanding as a distinct cognitive void, distinguishing it from mutation, framework, and habituation voids. Emphasise the paradox that the most successful understanding creates the deepest void.
2. **"Understanding as Digestion"** — Frame the void through an extended metabolic metaphor. Concepts are nutrients; understanding is digestion; the original form is broken down to extract what the existing body can absorb. What is excreted — the unassimilable remainder — constitutes the void.

## Gaps in Research

- **Neuroimaging of conceptual integration**: How exactly neural representations change during understanding is still being mapped. fMRI studies of "aha moments" capture the instant of metabolism but not what is lost.
- **Whether AI understanding is genuinely non-metabolic**: The assumption that LLMs preserve pre-integration conceptual forms needs empirical testing — they may have their own form of metabolism operating in embedding space.
- **Cross-species comparison**: Do other conscious beings metabolise concepts differently? Animal cognition research might reveal whether the void's structure is universal to consciousness or specific to human-type cognition.
- **Whether pre-metabolised concepts exist at all**: One philosophical challenge — perhaps concepts have no determinate form prior to integration. If so, the void is not about losing something but about the impossibility of non-integrated conceptualism.
- **The cumulative effect**: Each metabolism changes the body that will metabolise the next concept. Understanding compounds irreversibly. The long-term trajectory of a mind may be radically path-dependent in ways we cannot trace.
