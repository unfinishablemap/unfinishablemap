## Executive Summary

Two early-branching animal phyla — Porifera (sponges) and Placozoa (*Trichoplax adhaerens*) — produce coordinated, adaptive, whole-organism behaviour with **zero neurons and zero synapses**. Sponges execute body-wide contractions (a "sneeze") to expel waste, gated by glutamate (triggers) and GABA (inhibits), and their genome carries a near-complete complement of post-synaptic scaffold genes despite having no synapses. Placozoans, six cell types and no neurons, coordinate feeding and directed locomotion through **peptidergic volume signalling** from secretory cells — a chemical, non-synaptic broadcast that arrests ciliary beating so the animal pauses over food. These organisms sit *below* the cnidarian nerve net on the nervous-system-evolution ladder, filling the gap between plants and Hydra in this session's competency cluster. For the Map, they are the starkest available case of **competency without any plausible experiential interface**: rich adaptive behaviour that no candidate theory of consciousness would count as felt, because there is no neural substrate at all. This directly reinforces reading (c), *sub-personal competence*, from the enteric/basal/plant articles, and it is where Tenet 2 (minimality) most sharply cuts against positing an interface.

## Key Sources

### The Amphimedon queenslandica genome and the evolution of animal complexity
- **URL**: https://www.nature.com/articles/nature09201
- **Type**: Paper (Nature)
- **Citation**: Srivastava, M., Simakov, O., Chapman, J. et al. (2010). *Nature* 466:720–726. DOI 10.1038/nature09201.
- **Key points**:
  - Draft genome of a Great Barrier Reef demosponge; sponges as an early-branching animal lineage.
  - The genome contains a nearly complete set of **post-synaptic protein orthologues** — the molecular parts-list for a synapse — despite the animal having no neurons or synapses.
  - Frames the "genetic toolkit predates the structure" pattern central to this note.
- **Honesty caveat**: A later reassessment of Amphimedon gene annotations did **not** find sufficient support for the serotonin and dopamine receptors originally reported by Srivastava et al. Treat specific neurotransmitter-receptor claims from the 2010 paper with caution; the broad post-synaptic-scaffold finding stands.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral/supportive of the Map's caution — molecular pre-adaptation is not itself evidence of experience.

### Elements of a 'nervous system' in sponges
- **URL**: https://journals.biologists.com/jeb/article/218/4/581/14142/
- **Type**: Review (Journal of Experimental Biology)
- **Citation**: Leys, S. P. & Anderson, P. A. V. (2015). *J. Exp. Biol.* 218(4):581–591. DOI 10.1242/jeb.110817.
- **Key points**:
  - Sponges have coordinating mechanisms with *some* neuronal characteristics but **no true nervous system**.
  - Non-motile "primary" cilia (4–6 μm) lining the osculum act as sensory organs via calcium signalling.
  - Glutamate triggers contractions; GABA inhibits them — neurotransmitter-like chemistry without neurons.
  - Conduction is **tissue-based** (slow, epithelial, metabotropic-cascade); glass sponges uniquely propagate electrical impulses through syncytial tissue.
  - Argues this is *early specialisation for suspension feeding*, not loss of a prior neural system.
- **Tenet alignment**: Strongly supports interface-localisation caution — coordination is explained mechanistically end-to-end.

### Sponges sneeze mucus to shed particulate waste from their seawater inlet pores
- **URL**: https://www.cell.com/current-biology/fulltext/S0960-9822(22)01118-6
- **Type**: Paper (Current Biology)
- **Citation**: Kornder, N. A., Esser, Y., Stoupin, D., Leys, S. P., Mueller, B., Vermeij, M. J. A., Huisman, J. & de Goeij, J. M. (2022). *Current Biology* 32. DOI 10.1016/j.cub.2022.07.017.
- **Key points**:
  - The Caribbean tube sponge *Aplysina archeri* moves particle-trapping mucus **against** its internal water flow and periodically "sneezes" it out through inlet pores — a slow (tens of minutes) coordinated ejection.
  - Demonstrates a mechano-sensory, waste-clearing behaviour absent any neuron.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral — a behavioural marker with a fully non-neural explanation.

### ATP and glutamate coordinate contractions in the freshwater sponge Ephydatia muelleri
- **URL**: https://journals.biologists.com/jeb/article/228/3/JEB248010/365649/
- **Type**: Paper (Journal of Experimental Biology, 2025)
- **Citation**: Ho, V. R., Goss, G. G. & Leys, S. P. (2025). *J. Exp. Biol.* 228(3):JEB248010.
- **Key points**:
  - Recent mechanistic dissection: ATP and glutamate coordinate the whole-body contraction ("sneeze") in a nervous-system-free sponge.
  - Extends the chemical-signalling picture — the coordination substrate is purinergic/glutamatergic paracrine chemistry, not neural conduction.
- **Tenet alignment**: Supportive of mechanistic sufficiency.

### High cell diversity and complex peptidergic signalling underlie placozoan behaviour
- **URL**: https://www.cell.com/current-biology/abstract/S0960-9822(18)31197-7 (via https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30344118/)
- **Type**: Paper (Current Biology)
- **Citation**: Varoqueaux, F., Williams, E. A., Grandemange, S., Truscello, L., Kamm, K., Schierwater, B., Jékely, G. & Fasshauer, D. (2018). *Current Biology* 28(21):3495–3501.e2. DOI 10.1016/j.cub.2018.08.067.
- **Key points**:
  - *Trichoplax* expresses **neuropeptides in non-overlapping, cell-type-specific populations** across its three layers — unexpected cell-type diversity for a six-cell-type animal.
  - Application of 11 different neuropeptides produced consistent behavioural effects sorted into three types: **crinkling, turning, and flattening/churning**.
  - Concludes peptidergic **volume signalling may have predated synaptic signalling** in nervous-system evolution.
- **Note**: Schierwater is a co-author here (the harvested brief attributed the peptidergic work to "Smith/Schierwater"; the two research lines are distinct — Smith/Reese at NIH, Varoqueaux/Fasshauer/Schierwater as a separate group).
- **Tenet alignment**: Central case — adaptive behavioural repertoire from diffuse chemistry, no synapses.

### Coordinated feeding behavior in Trichoplax, an animal without synapses
- **URL**: https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0136098 (via https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26333190/)
- **Type**: Paper (PLoS ONE)
- **Citation**: Smith, C. L., Pivovarova, N. & Reese, T. S. (2015). *PLoS ONE* 10(9):e0136098. DOI 10.1371/journal.pone.0136098.
- **Key points**:
  - A subset of **lipophil cells** simultaneously secretes granules that rapidly lyse algae; secretion is spatially targeted — only lipophils near algae fire.
  - The animal **pauses gliding** while it ingests the lysed content, then resumes.
  - Later work (Senatore, Reese & Smith 2017) shows signal amplification by peptide-induced peptide secretion lets processless, synapse-less secretory cells broadcast a wave of peptide that **globally arrests ciliary beating** — the mechanism of the pause.
- **Tenet alignment**: Strong sub-personal-competence exemplar (targeted, gated, whole-body coordinated feeding, zero neurons).

### Coherent directed movement toward food modeled in Trichoplax, a ciliated animal lacking a nervous system
- **URL**: https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.1815655116
- **Type**: Paper (PNAS)
- **Citation**: Smith, C. L., Reese, T. S., Govezensky, T. & Barrio, R. A. (2019). *PNAS* 116(18):8901–8908. DOI 10.1073/pnas.1815655116.
- **Key points**:
  - Models how *Trichoplax* achieves **coherent, directed movement toward food** purely from local cilia coordination plus chemical cues — coordinated chemotaxis without any central controller.
  - Directedness (a classic behavioural marker of goal-oriented agency) emerges from distributed, local mechanics.
- **Tenet alignment**: Supports the "agency markers are defeasible" thesis — goal-like behaviour with a fully distributed, non-neural generator.

### Eating and Cognition in Two Animals without Neurons: Sponges and Trichoplax
- **URL**: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s13752-024-00464-6
- **Type**: Philosophy paper (Biological Theory)
- **Citation**: Bechtel, W. & Bich, L. (2024). *Biological Theory*. DOI 10.1007/s13752-024-00464-6.
- **Key points**:
  - Argues **eating is a cognitive activity** and that cognition is fundamentally grounded in chemical signalling/processing, not necessarily neurons.
  - Uses precisely these two neuron-less phyla to press the case that coordinating multi-cell-type activity to feed already counts as (basal) cognition.
  - Part of a special issue on Ladislav Kováč and the origins of cognitive biology.
- **Tenet alignment**: *Productive tension.* Represents the expansive "cognition-without-neurons" camp — useful as the strongest opposing framing the Map must answer, not endorse. The Map can grant the *competence* while withholding *experience*.

### What nervous systems do: early evolution, input–output, and the skin-brain thesis
- **URL**: https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/1059712312465330
- **Type**: Review/theory (Adaptive Behavior)
- **Citation**: Keijzer, F., van Duijn, M. & Lyon, P. (2013). *Adaptive Behavior* 21(2):67–85. DOI 10.1177/1059712312465330.
- **Key points**:
  - The **skin-brain thesis**: nervous systems first evolved to coordinate contractile body surfaces for motility, *not* to process sensory input for motor output.
  - Implies the "lower bound" of the nervous-system lineage is about action-coordination — reframing what sponges/placozoans are the precursors *to*.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral framing tool — helps place these animals on the evolutionary ladder without assuming experience.

## Major Positions

### Expansive basal-cognition ("cognition without neurons")
- **Proponents**: Bechtel & Bich (2024); Keijzer/Lyon lineage; broader minimal/basal-cognition program.
- **Core claim**: Cognition is chemical coordination for adaptive self-maintenance (paradigm: eating). Neurons are one implementation, not a precondition. Sponges and *Trichoplax* already cognise.
- **Relation to site tenets**: The Map can *accept the competence* while resisting the slide from competence to consciousness. This is exactly reading (c): behaviour is sub-personal, functionally explained, and does not license positing felt experience.

### Deflationary / mechanistic (coordination, not cognition)
- **Proponents**: Physiologists in the Leys / Smith-Reese tradition tend to describe mechanism (paracrine chemistry, ciliary mechanics) without cognitive vocabulary.
- **Core claim**: Whole-body contraction and coordinated feeding are fully explained by paracrine signalling, epithelial conduction, and ciliary coupling. "Behaviour," yes; "cognition/experience," unwarranted.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Aligns with Tenet 5 discipline (don't over-read) and Tenet 2 minimality (don't multiply posits). The Map's default lean.

### The Map's own position (to be developed in the article)
- These animals mark a candidate **lower bound of the consciousness–physical interface**: if adaptive, goal-like, whole-body-coordinated behaviour runs with **no neural substrate**, then behavioural markers of agency are decisively shown to be **defeasible and non-decisive in isolation**. The interface, if it exists anywhere, is not *here* — which is informative about *where* to look for it.

## Key Debates

### Where is the lower bound of the interface?
- **Sides**: Expansive basal-cognition (interface/cognition reaches down to chemical coordination) vs. the Map's neurally-anchored, minimality-disciplined view.
- **Core disagreement**: Whether adaptive coordination *per se* is evidence of anything experiential, or merely of successful biological control.
- **Current state**: Open; the empirical facts (rich behaviour, zero neurons) are agreed — the interpretation is contested. **Do not over-resolve.**

### Did peptidergic volume signalling precede synapses?
- **Sides**: Varoqueaux et al. (yes, volume signalling is ancestral) — broadly accepted framing.
- **Core disagreement**: Evolutionary ordering of diffuse chemical vs. point-to-point synaptic signalling.
- **Current state**: Emerging consensus favours diffuse-first; relevant because it shows "signalling that shapes behaviour" is older and broader than "neural signalling."

## Potential Article Angles

1. **"The lower bound of the interface" (recommended).** Use sponges + placozoans as the floor case in the competency ladder (plants → sponges/placozoans → Hydra nerve net → …). Thesis: coordinated adaptive behaviour with **zero neurons** is the cleanest demonstration that behavioural markers of agency are defeasible and decisive nowhere alone (reading (c), sub-personal competence). Tenet 3: the consciousness–physical interface, if localised anywhere, is *not* here — there is no plausible neural substrate to host it. Tenet 2: minimality positively forbids positing an interface where mechanism is complete and neurons are absent. Lay out the open question; do not over-resolve.
2. **"The parts-list precedes the machine."** The Amphimedon post-synaptic gene complement as a case study in molecular pre-adaptation — a caution against inferring function (or experience) from genomic toolkit presence.
3. **"Eating as the test case for basal cognition."** Engage Bechtel & Bich directly: grant that coordinated feeding is impressive control; separate *competence* from *consciousness*; show the Map's dualism can hold both — full mechanistic competence, no attributed experience.
