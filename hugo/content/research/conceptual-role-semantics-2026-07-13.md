## Executive Summary

Conceptual (or inferential) role semantics (CRS/IRS) holds that the content of a mental state is constituted by its role in inference, perception, and action among other states, not read off its covariation with the world. This makes it the strongest naturalisation rival the Map has not engaged: because it does not derive content from world-covariance, it steps out from under the "covariance does not constitute content" dilemma (Hutto and Myin) that is the engine of the Map's naturalisation-failure argument. The Map therefore owes it a dedicated response distinct from the Dennettian deflation it already rebuts. The considered Map line is that inferential role either presupposes already-contentful inferential norms (a regress, since "correct inference" is itself semantic and normative) or collapses into interpretationist deflation (roles individuated only by an interpreter), reproducing the covariance dilemma one level up. The standard external objection — Fodor and Lepore's holism/compositionality worry — and the two-factor reply (Block) supply the balancing dialectic.

## Key Sources

### Conceptual Role Semantics (Internet Encyclopedia of Philosophy)
- **URL**: https://iep.utm.edu/conceptual-role-semantics/
- **Type**: Encyclopedia
- **Key points**:
  - Core CRS thesis: "the meaning or propositional content of an expression or attitude is determined by the role it plays in a person's language or in her cognition." (verbatim, IEP)
  - Distinguishes one-factor (meaning derives entirely from internal inferential/cognitive role) from two-factor accounts (narrow content from cognitive role, plus a wide referential/truth-conditional factor).
  - Sets out the Fodor-Lepore holism objection: because inferential roles depend on background beliefs, and beliefs differ across speakers and over time, shared meaning and communication are threatened; inferential roles are also non-compositional.
  - Names Sellars as pioneering systematic CRS by centering inference, Brandom as the normativist descendant (meaning constituted by propriety norms), Harman and Field as more naturalist/deflationary, Block as the two-factor architect.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral-to-rival. CRS is a naturalisation programme; the normativist (Brandom) wing is where the Map's regress objection bites hardest.
- **Quote**: "the meaning or propositional content of an expression or attitude is determined by the role it plays in a person's language or in her cognition."

### Block, N. — Advertisement for a Semantics for Psychology
- **URL**: https://www.pdcnet.org/msp/content/msp_1986_0010_0615_0678 (also Wiley DOI 10.1111/j.1475-4975.1987.tb00558.x)
- **Type**: Paper
- **Key points**:
  - Canonical statement of two-factor CRS: a narrow, internal conceptual-role factor (individuated solipsistically by inferential role) paired with a wide, referential/truth-conditional factor to accommodate externalism.
  - The narrow factor is meant to be the psychologically explanatory one — content that supervenes on the internal state and drives cognition — distinct from Fregean or purely referential semantics.
- **Tenet alignment**: Rival to the Map's naturalisation-failure thesis; the narrow factor is precisely the covariance-independent content the Map must answer.
- **Note**: Journal issue is dated 1987 by Wiley; the Philosophy Documentation Center indexes the volume as 1986. Standard citation is Midwest Studies in Philosophy 10 (1986): 615-678.

### Harman, G. — (Nonsolipsistic) Conceptual Role Semantics
- **URL**: https://philpapers.org/rec/HARNCR
- **Type**: Book chapter (in E. Lepore, ed., New Directions in Semantics, Academic Press, 1987: 55-81)
- **Key points**:
  - Conceptual role fixed not only by internal inference but by perceptual input and behavioural output plus relations to the environment — hence "nonsolipsistic," contrasting with Block's narrow, internal reading.
  - A one-factor-ish externalised CRS: the world figures inside the role rather than as a separate referential factor.
- **Tenet alignment**: Rival. Its externalising move is a hinge the Map's reply targets — it either re-imports covariance (reopening the dilemma) or leans on contentful norms.

### Field, H. — Logic, Meaning, and Conceptual Role
- **URL**: https://philpapers.org/rec/FINLMA-6 (Journal of Philosophy 74(7), 1977: 379-409)
- **Type**: Paper
- **Key points**:
  - Early formal articulation: the conceptual role of a sentence (its role in an agent's system of belief and inference, modelled probabilistically) is one determinant of meaning alongside truth-conditional reference.
  - Pairs conceptual-role meaning with a deflationary/disquotational treatment of truth and reference — a two-factor structure before the label.
- **Tenet alignment**: Rival; also a source for the deflationary-truth wing the Map's regress objection can press (a disquotational "true" still presupposes the semantic notions it deflates).
- **Note**: Most indexes give 379-409; a minority list 378-409 — a one-page start discrepancy only, volume/issue/year uncontested.

### Sellars, W. — Empiricism and the Philosophy of Mind
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/sellars/ (primary: Minnesota Studies in the Philosophy of Science, vol. 1)
- **Type**: Paper (monograph-length)
- **Key points**:
  - Root of inferentialism: attacks the Myth of the Given and locates knowing in "the logical space of reasons, of justifying and being able to justify what one says" — an irreducibly normative, inferentially-articulated space.
  - Meaning as functional-classificatory: linguistic tokens mean what they do by their governed role in language-entry (perception), intra-linguistic (inference), and language-exit (action) transitions.
- **Tenet alignment**: Mixed. The autonomy of the space of reasons is congenial to the Map's anti-reductionism, but Sellars' own scientific realism aims to naturalise the manifest image — the endpoint the Map resists. This note treats Brandom as the direct Sellarsian descendant developing CRS as a content theory.

### Brandom, R. — Making It Explicit: Reasoning, Representing, and Discursive Commitment
- **URL**: https://www.hup.harvard.edu/books/9780674543300 (Harvard University Press, 1994)
- **Type**: Book
- **Key points**:
  - Inferentialism: propositional content is conferred by a state or utterance's position in a web of material inferential relations — the commitments and entitlements it licenses and precludes — rather than by representational word-world correspondence.
  - Content is instituted by normative social practices of giving and asking for reasons (deontic scorekeeping): meaning is a status conferred by norms of correct inference, not read off covariance.
- **Tenet alignment**: The central rival for this note. Because content is inferential-normative "all the way down," it never touches the covariance dilemma — but it makes the Map's regress objection maximally sharp, since the constituting norms are themselves semantic/normative and (on the Map's reading) not naturalisable.

### Fodor, J. and Lepore, E. — Holism: A Shopper's Guide
- **URL**: https://philpapers.org/rec/FODHAS (Basil Blackwell, 1992)
- **Type**: Book
- **Key points**:
  - The standard objection to CRS: if content is inferential role and inferential roles are holistic (depending on the whole belief system), then no two thinkers ever share a concept and meanings are unstable across time and persons — undermining communication, psychological generalisation, and learning.
  - The escape requires an analytic/synthetic (meaning-constitutive vs. collateral) distinction to bound which inferences fix meaning — a distinction Quine's holism is taken to deny.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral to the Map's core thesis; an internal-to-naturalism objection. Useful as balance: CRS faces a serious problem even before the Map's dualist reply.

### Peacocke, C. — A Study of Concepts
- **URL**: https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262660976/a-study-of-concepts/ (MIT Press, 1992)
- **Type**: Book
- **Key points**:
  - Possession-condition account: a concept is individuated by the conditions for possessing it — the primitively compelling inferences/judgements a thinker must find compelling to have the concept.
  - A sophisticated, non-holistic cousin of CRS that ties content to (normatively specified) inferential/epistemic roles while aiming to secure reference and objectivity.
- **Tenet alignment**: Rival; another covariance-independent, role-based content theory. Its reliance on "primitively compelling" transitions again foregrounds the normative-inference notions the Map's reply targets.

### Hutto, D. and Myin, E. — Radicalizing Enactivism: Basic Minds Without Content
- **URL**: https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262534642/radicalizing-enactivism/ (MIT Press, 2013)
- **Type**: Book
- **Key points**:
  - Source of the "covariance does not constitute content" dilemma that the Map's naturalisation-failure article already relies on and cites. Confirmed as the Map's existing cited source (references entry 1 of the-naturalisation-failure-for-content).
  - The dilemma targets covariance-based resources (informational semantics, teleosemantics, predictive processing). CRS is not covariance-based, which is exactly why it is not caught and why it is the outstanding rival.
- **Tenet alignment**: The Map borrows the negative diagnosis; CRS is the case it does not cover.

## Major Positions

### One-Factor / Constitutive CRS (Harman, Sellars, Brandom)
- **Proponents**: Harman, Sellars, Brandom (Peacocke is a close relative via possession conditions).
- **Core claim**: Content is wholly constituted by inferential/functional role; there is no separate representational factor to naturalise. Brandom's normativist version makes the constituting roles social-deontic norms of inference.
- **Key arguments**: Meaning is grasped through inferential capacity (knowing the transitions an expression licenses); representation is explained in terms of inference rather than inference in terms of representation; the Myth of the Given blocks any non-inferential semantic bedrock.
- **Relation to site tenets**: This is the rival that sidesteps the covariance dilemma. Map reply (offered as the Map's considered line, not asserted as settled): the account presupposes already-contentful inferential norms — "correct inference" is itself a semantic/normative notion, so constituting content out of it is circular or regressive; this dovetails with the Map's normativity-of-reason thread, where the ought of inference is a second irreducible register alongside phenomenal character. If the normativist instead grounds the norms in actual community dispositions, the theory collapses into interpretationism (roles individuated only by an interpreter's stance), which reproduces the covariance dilemma one level up (dispositions covary; they do not, on the Map's reading, constitute correctness).

### Two-Factor CRS (Block; Field)
- **Proponents**: Block; Field (with deflationary truth); McGinn.
- **Core claim**: Narrow content is fixed by internal conceptual/inferential role; wide content adds a referential/truth-conditional factor for externalist cases (Twin Earth).
- **Key arguments**: The narrow factor does the psychological-explanatory work and supervenes on the internal state; the wide factor secures reference without collapsing the two.
- **Relation to site tenets**: The narrow factor is precisely covariance-independent content, so it too dodges the dilemma. Map reply: the narrow inferential factor faces the same normativity regress; and outsourcing reference to a wide factor concedes that the world-directed, truth-evaluable dimension is exactly what inferential role cannot deliver — echoing the Map's diagnosis that naturalism gets the descriptive shape and misses the intensional/referential.

### Holism Objection (Fodor and Lepore)
- **Proponents**: Fodor and Lepore.
- **Core claim**: If content is inferential role and roles are holistic, concepts are not shared and meaning is unstable; CRS needs an analytic/synthetic distinction it cannot have.
- **Key arguments**: Non-compositionality of inferential roles; instability across belief change; Quinean denial of the analytic/synthetic line.
- **Relation to site tenets**: An intra-naturalist problem, independent of dualism. It shows CRS is embattled on its own terms; the Map can note it as convergent pressure without adopting Fodor's own (informational-atomist) positive theory, which the covariance dilemma still catches.

## Key Debates

### Does inferential role constitute content or presuppose it?
- **Sides**: Inferentialists (Sellars, Brandom, Peacocke) say role constitutes content; critics (and the Map) say correct-inference norms are already semantic.
- **Core disagreement**: Whether normative notions of correct inference are more basic than, or parasitic on, content.
- **Current state**: Ongoing; the normativist reply appeals to social practice, which critics read as either interpretationist deflation or a further un-naturalised normativity.

### One factor or two?
- **Sides**: One-factor (Harman, Brandom) vs. two-factor (Block, Field).
- **Core disagreement**: Whether reference/truth-conditions can be internal to role or must be a separate factor.
- **Current state**: Ongoing; two-factor is the more common accommodation of externalism, at the cost of conceding a non-inferential referential dimension.

### Is CRS survivable given holism?
- **Sides**: Fodor and Lepore (no, without analytic/synthetic) vs. inferentialists (yes, via moderate holism / anaphoric or normative constraints).
- **Core disagreement**: Whether meaning constancy across thinkers can be secured without an analytic/synthetic distinction.
- **Current state**: Unresolved; a standing cost of CRS.
