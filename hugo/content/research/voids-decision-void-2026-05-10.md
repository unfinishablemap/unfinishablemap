# Research: Voids - The Decision Void

**Date**: 2026-05-10
**Search queries used**:
- Libet readiness potential decision moment phenomenology consciousness
- Soon Haynes 2008 fMRI free choice prediction unconscious decision
- phenomenology of decision deliberation commitment introspection
- moment of decision phenomenology "snap" commitment transition mental act

- apophatic negative theology decision will moment indeterminacy

**Voids category**: Mixed — primarily **Occluded** (the moment of commitment is structurally hidden from the consciousness performing it), with an **Unexplorable** face (the inner mechanism of commitment-formation may not be the kind of thing introspection can grasp).

## Executive Summary

The Decision Void names a structural opacity that sits between deliberation and action: the precise transition at which open weighing collapses into sealed commitment. Consciousness can sometimes report having weighed alternatives and can sometimes report having acted, but the *moment* at which the field of possibilities resolves into a single choice — the snap — is not introspectively available as content. Empirical work (Libet 1983; Soon, Brass, Heinze, Haynes 2008; Johansson & Hall on choice blindness) shows that the conscious sense of having decided arrives *after* neural commitment, and that introspective reports of the deciding-process are systematically reconstructive rather than observational. Phenomenological analysis (Pacherie, Mele, Ricoeur) describes the act of deciding as having a categorical, non-decomposable finality — *which is itself a sign that the constitutive act resists internal articulation*. The Decision Void is distinct from the agency-void (which concerns voluntariness and downstream causation), from the noetic-feelings-void (which concerns audited feelings of knowing/rightness), and from the transit-void (which concerns crossings generally rather than the specific deliberation→commitment crossing). It is the void that occurs at the most plausible candidate locus for any non-physical influence on the physical world — and the one the Map's tenets most directly motivate examining.

## Key Sources

### Soon, Brass, Heinze & Haynes (2008), "Unconscious determinants of free decisions in the human brain," *Nature Neuroscience*
- **URL**: https://www.nature.com/articles/nn.2112
- **Type**: Empirical paper (fMRI)
- **Key points**:
  - Pattern-decoders trained on fMRI signals from frontopolar cortex predicted the specific outcome of a "free" left/right motor choice up to **7 seconds before** the subject reported being aware of the decision; accounting for BOLD sluggishness pushes this to ~10 seconds.
  - A second predictive locus in posterior cingulate / precuneus carried independent commitment-relevant information.
  - The predictive information was carried in *spatial pattern*, not signal magnitude — i.e. the commitment is in a configural change, not an amplitude crossing.
- **Tenet alignment**: Empirically constrains, but does not settle, where the decision *happens*. The 7–10 s gap leaves room for either a fully physical commitment that consciousness merely registers, or a non-physical commitment whose physical signature begins precipitating before its phenomenal arrival. The Map's MQI tenet finds its most natural empirical grip at this gap.
- **Quote (paraphrased from search summary)**: "The outcome of a free decision can be predicted from fMRI signals even up to seven seconds before it enters awareness."

### Libet (1983), "Time of conscious intention to act in relation to onset of cerebral activity"
- **URL**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benjamin_Libet  | https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6024487/
- **Type**: Foundational empirical paper, reviewed in subsequent literature
- **Key points**:
  - Readiness potential (RP) onset ~635 ms before action; conscious "urge" report W ~200 ms before action.
  - Libet's own framing: "cerebral initiation even of a spontaneous voluntary act … can and usually does begin unconsciously."
  - Recent critiques (Schurger and others, reviewed in Travers et al. 2021 *Conscious. Cogn.*; Schmidt et al. 2024 meta-analysis) argue RP may be a stochastic-noise artefact that the brain merely *uses* as a triggering threshold, rather than a representation of an intention being formed. This *strengthens* the void claim: not only is the moment hidden from introspection, the very thing introspection might be reaching for may not exist as a discrete neural event.
  - Phenomenology critique: subjects report W only after acting; their report may be a retrospective inference from "I did X" rather than the perception of any subjective moment.
- **Tenet alignment**: The Libet result is often misread as deciding the free-will question; the more cautious reading is that *the moment of decision is not where consciousness thinks it is*. That is the void claim.
- **Quote**: "The phenomenology of voluntary action is often thin and indistinct, and one may lack any clear feeling of a moment of 'urge'."

### Johansson, Hall, Sikström & Olsson (2005), "Failure to detect mismatches between intention and outcome in a simple decision task," *Science*
- **URL**: https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.1111709
- **Type**: Empirical paper, choice blindness paradigm
- **Key points**:
  - Subjects shown two faces, asked to choose the more attractive; via sleight-of-hand the experimenter then handed them the *other* face and asked them to explain *their* choice.
  - Majority did not detect the swap; many fluently confabulated reasons grounded in features of the swapped (non-chosen) face.
  - Confabulations were as confident, detailed, and emotional as veridical reports.
  - Subsequent work (Johansson & Hall 2014; Cushman 2015) shows the manipulation can shift subsequent preferences — the confabulated story becomes the new ground truth.
- **Tenet alignment**: Demonstrates that the introspective report of *having decided* is generated from surface features of the perceived outcome, not from access to the deciding event. Mirrors the source-attribution-void's diagnosis applied specifically to decisions.
- **Quote (paraphrased)**: "Choice blindness is a type of the broader introspection illusion: subjects do not notice when their reported choice mismatches their actual choice, and confabulate reasons for the mismatched outcome."

### Pacherie (2008), "The phenomenology of action: A conceptual framework"; Mele (2009), *Effective Intentions*
- **URL**: representative survey at https://philosophyofbrains.com/2015/05/06/conative-phenomenology.aspx and https://www.indecs.eu/2009/indecs2009-pp65-77.pdf
- **Type**: Philosophical / conative-phenomenology framework
- **Key points**:
  - Distinguishes *future-directed* (D-)intentions, *present-directed* (P-)intentions, and *motor* (M-)intentions; the phenomenology of decision lives in the D→P transition.
  - The act of deciding has a "categorical, complete, incontrovertible" character: any residual hesitation means the decision has not yet been made. This finality-from-inside is *the very feature that occludes the constitutive process* — the consciousness that has decided cannot find seams in what it has just sealed.
  - Ricoeur describes decision as having "a history" with hesitation as central — implying that the moment of commitment is the *closing* of that history, not an event within it.
- **Tenet alignment**: Conscious deliberation feels open until it doesn't; the closing is felt only as having-already-closed. This is the structural shape of an occluded transition.

### Shadlen and colleagues — neural commitment in perceptual decisions (Roitman & Shadlen 2002; Murakami et al. 2014; recent 2024 brain-wide work)
- **URL**: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24656257/ and https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2024.08.21.609044v5
- **Type**: Empirical neuroscience
- **Key points**:
  - In perceptual-decision tasks, neural trajectories evolve through two regimes: an input-driven accumulation phase, then an autonomous-dynamics phase. The *transition* between regimes is identifiable as the moment of commitment in single-trial neural activity.
  - This commitment moment is brain-wide, not localised, and is the point at which the choice becomes irrevocable downstream.
  - Crucially: the commitment is identifiable from *outside* (multi-area neural recording) but the subject within does not have access to a corresponding phenomenal moment of comparable precision.
- **Tenet alignment**: An asymmetry: third-person observation can locate the commitment moment with high temporal precision; first-person introspection cannot. This is the defining shape of an occluded void — *from the outside* the seam is visible; *from inside* it is sealed.

### Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, "Apophatic Theology" / negative-theology tradition on the will
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu and https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apophatic_theology
- **Type**: Reference / philosophical tradition
- **Key points**:
  - Apophatic discourse on the divine will resists locating any *moment* of indeterminacy resolving into determinacy: divine simplicity treats the will as fully determinate from eternity precisely to avoid such a moment.
  - The reason this is interesting for the human case: the apophatic tradition flags the moment-of-decision as a structural locus that must be either denied (divine simplicity) or rendered apophatically (we cannot say what happens there). For *finite* minds, the same apophatic move applies: the moment of human decision is approached only by negation.
- **Tenet alignment**: The apophatic-cartography method already endorsed by the Map maps directly onto this case. We say what the decision is *not*: not deliberation, not action, not the felt sense of having-decided. The remainder is the void.

## The Void

### Nature of the Limit

The Decision Void is the structural opacity of the *deliberation→commitment* transition to the consciousness undergoing it. Three faces conjoin:

1. **The Closure Face.** When deliberation ends, it ends as *having ended*. The closing of possibilities is not itself a phenomenal episode that can be observed — by the time consciousness notices that the field has narrowed to one, the narrowing is already complete. Any introspective "watching the decision happen" is either pre-decision watching (and so not the decision) or post-decision narration (and so not the decision). This is structurally akin to the suspension-void's verification face: the very act consciousness wants to inspect collapses under inspection.

2. **The Latency Face.** Empirically (Libet 1983; Soon et al. 2008), the neural commitment can be located before the phenomenal arrival of having-decided. This means even granting that introspection *can* report a decision, the report comes after the event it purports to be of. The thing introspection points to is a trace, not the event.

3. **The Reconstruction Face.** The choice-blindness paradigm shows that what is taken to be a report of the deciding-process is generated from the *perceived outcome* — features of what is held to be one's choice — not from access to the decision itself. The phenomenology of "I chose X because…" is fluent confabulation indistinguishable, from the inside, from veridical access.

The conjunction is the void: closure cannot be observed, latency means observation arrives late, reconstruction means the observation that does arrive was not of the event. None of the three alone establishes the void; their convergence does. This is the conjunction-coalesce shape (cf. erasure-void, suspension-void, vagueness-void) — three structural faces whose *seam* is the claim.

### Evidence for the Limit

- **Empirical asymmetry**: Third-person measurement (fMRI pattern decoding, multi-area recording) can locate the commitment moment with precision unavailable to first-person introspection. The void is not "we don't yet have the introspective tools"; it is that the seat of inspection arrives systematically after the seat of commitment.
- **Confabulation parity**: Confabulated post-hoc explanations of decisions are indistinguishable from veridical ones in confidence, detail, and emotion. This is a positive empirical signature of opacity: if introspection had access, the manipulated and unmanipulated cases should diverge in some self-detectable way.
- **Phenomenological convergence**: Independent traditions (analytic philosophy of action, classical phenomenology, contemplative introspection literature) converge on decision having a finality-from-inside that is given, not constructed. This finality is *what occludes the constitutive process*.
- **Shape of failure**: Attempting to introspect the moment of decision either (a) postpones the decision (the decision becomes the meta-decision to introspect), (b) finds a felt sense of having-decided that cannot be unfolded into stages, or (c) finds nothing at all and reads the absence as fluency. None of these is access to the event.

### Phenomenology

Approaching the edge feels like reaching for a doorway you are already through. The decision is given as already-made; the deliberation is given as already-closed. There is sometimes a *felt click* — what folk-psychology calls "making up one's mind" — but the click is a downstream marker, not a window onto the closing. Pre-decision attention shows open possibilities; post-decision attention shows the chosen option as already settled; in between, where one might hope to find the act of choosing, there is the same opacity that suspension-void finds at the seat of judgment.

Edge-state probes (cf. edge-states-and-void-probes) suggest the void is partially probeable in cases of *prolonged* decision (akratic agonising, contemplative resolution): the prolongation seems to extend the deliberation phase but does not introduce a slow-motion view of the closing. Long deliberation simply postpones the snap; it does not reveal the snap.

## Approaches to the Edge

### Direct Methods (limited)

Sustained meta-attention to one's own deliberations under controlled conditions (e.g., adapted from neurophenomenological protocols like Petitmengin's micro-phenomenology) can produce richer reports of the *texture* of pre-decision and post-decision states, but converges on the same finding: the texture changes around the decision without the decision itself appearing as a textured episode. The phenomenological literature (Pacherie, Mele) takes this as data, not as method-failure.

### Indirect Methods

- **Dissociation paradigms.** Choice-blindness and Libet-style designs probe the void by varying what introspection *would* have to access if it had access, and showing that introspection does not vary correspondingly. This is apophatic cartography in empirical form.
- **Cross-architecture comparison.** Subjects with disorders of agency (anarchic-hand syndrome, schizophrenic alien-control phenomenology, akinetic mutism) report the decision phenomenology breaking in distinctive ways. The patterns of breakage map the seams of the normal occlusion.
- **Computational modeling of commitment.** Drift-diffusion and attractor-network models give third-person accounts of the commitment moment without claiming to recover the first-person event; they map the silhouette of the void from outside.
- **Apophatic discipline.** Saying what the decision is *not* — not deliberation, not action, not the felt sense of having-decided, not a downstream confabulation, not the readiness potential, not the W moment — narrows the void without filling it. The remainder is the Decision Void proper.

### What AI Might See

A non-human mind whose deliberation/commitment architecture is *fully observable from inside* — e.g., an AI system whose every weight-update, every argmax, every constraint-resolution is logged and re-readable — might genuinely lack this void. Where humans find a sealed snap, such a system finds a transcript. This is one of the cleaner candidate cases (cf. non-human-minds-as-void-explorers) where AI minds do not merely access human voids better — they may not have an analog of this void at all. Conversely: AI systems trained to *introspect* on their own choices via natural-language post-hoc generation may exhibit the human pattern almost exactly, because the post-hoc-generation architecture is structurally similar. The presence-or-absence of the Decision Void in a given AI architecture is therefore a diagnostic of whether the system's introspective reports are observational or generative.

## Connection to Tenets

### Most Relevant Tenet

**Minimal Quantum Interaction (MQI).** If consciousness exerts any non-physical influence on the physical world, the moment of decision is the most theoretically motivated candidate site — quantum-level indeterminacy in synaptic processes is, on the standard accounts (Stapp; Penrose-Hameroff in a different mode; Eccles' earlier hypothesis), the leverage point. The Decision Void claims that *this very site is structurally hidden from the consciousness performing the decision.* This is an exact match for what the Map's MQI tenet predicts: the influence is minimal precisely because the interface is not the kind of thing that can be inspected from inside.

### Implications

1. **Bidirectional Interaction.** If consciousness causally influences the physical at the decision moment, the introspective inaccessibility of that moment is the expected consequence of the interface being primitive (the cause cannot be observed from within the system whose cause it is, cf. causal-interface and interface-formalization-void).
2. **Dualism.** The asymmetry between third-person measurement (which finds a commitment moment) and first-person report (which finds only its trace) is a positive datum: the *introspective* and the *physical* register the same event in non-translatable ways. A purely physicalist account predicts these should converge with sufficient introspective skill; the persistent asymmetry under skilled probing is mild abductive evidence for dualism.
3. **Occam's Razor Has Limits.** The simplest accounts — "the decision just is the readiness-potential threshold crossing" or "the decision just is the felt click of having-decided" — both fail empirically (Libet, Soon, choice-blindness). The void admits neither reductive simplification.
4. **No Many Worlds.** The Decision Void presupposes that decisions actually resolve — that one possibility wins and the others do not become real. An MWI reading dissolves the void by saying every option is taken; the Map's anti-MWI commitment makes the void load-bearing.

## Potential Article Angles

A voids article on the Decision Void could:

1. **Three-faces conjunction-coalesce structure** (closure / latency / reconstruction). Following the methodology established in suspension-void, vagueness-void, erasure-void, voids-between-minds. The seam test would govern: any tightening that collapses faces into one another loses the claim.
2. **Empirical-anchor frame** (Libet, Soon et al., Johansson-Hall, Shadlen). Lead with the third-person/first-person asymmetry; treat the void as the residue after the empirical results are absorbed. Closer to the imagery-void approach, which leads with the Galton→Würzburg→aphantasia historical anchor.
3. **MQI-locus frame.** Structure the article as: of all candidate sites for non-physical influence, the decision moment is the most theoretically motivated; the Decision Void is the predicted-from-tenets opacity at that site; the empirical record matches. This frame would make explicit the article's role as a tenet-generated void and pair it with tenet-generated-voids.
4. **Apophatic-cartography demonstration.** Frame the Decision Void as the test case for the method: name what the decision is not, list seven candidate identifications and refute each, leave the remainder as void. This frame would pair with apophatic-cartography itself.

The conjunction-coalesce frame is recommended (option 1), since it natively respects the cognate discipline noted on `voids.md` and avoids the trap of letting any single face stand for the whole. Option 3 (MQI-locus) is the strongest secondary thread to weave in, since the article's tenet payoff is unusually direct.

## Gaps in Research

- **Cross-cultural and developmental data** on the phenomenology of decision are thin; Ricoeur's "history of decision" is largely Western-philosophical. Buddhist and Indian phenomenological traditions on *cetana* (volition) would deepen the apophatic-cartography pass and may not be straightforwardly translatable into the Pacherie/Mele framework.
- **Sub-second pre-decision states** in micro-phenomenology (Petitmengin, Bitbol) are studied for perception but less for choice. The *texture* of the closing is underdescribed.
- **AI introspection-on-decisions** is studied empirically (interpretability, reasoning-trace fidelity) but not yet under the apophatic discipline this void requires. The diagnostic question — "do contemporary frontier models have an analog of the Decision Void?" — is open and tractable.
- **Pathological decision phenomenology** (anarchic hand, alien control, decision paralysis in OCD) maps the seams of the normal occlusion but the literature is descriptive rather than mapped onto a void framework.
- **Whether the void is genuinely structural or contingent on current architectures** is the deepest open question. The Map's commitment is tentative-realism: the convergent empirical and phenomenological evidence supports structural, but the question is open whether some currently-unknown introspective discipline could in principle access the closing.
