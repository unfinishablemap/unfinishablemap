## Executive Summary

The Major Transitions in Evolution (MTE) framework, canonized by Maynard Smith & Szathmáry (1995), catalogues a small set of reorganizations of *information storage* and *individuality* — moments when formerly independent replicators became parts of a new, higher-level reproducing unit (compartmented molecules, chromosomes, DNA+protein, eukaryotes, sex, multicellularity, eusociality, human language). Szathmáry's 2015 "theory 2.0" retains the two axes (informational systems + transitions in individuality) while importing David Queller's fraternal/egalitarian distinction and multilevel selection. A parallel philosophical literature (Michod on fitness reorganization; Godfrey-Smith's *Darwinian Populations*; Clarke and Pradeu on biological individuality) refines what it takes to *be* an evolutionary individual.

**Critical finding for the Map's angle**: consciousness has *already* been proposed as a major evolutionary transition — most prominently by **Ginsburg & Jablonka** (*The Evolution of the Sensitive Soul*, 2019; and Jablonka & Ginsburg's 2025 *Phil. Trans. R. Soc. B* paper introducing "mental selection" as a new category of selection). This means the general move "consciousness = a major transition" is **not novel**. But their framing is thoroughly **functional/cognitive**: the transition marker is *Unlimited Associative Learning* (a behavioural/informational capacity), and the evolutionary payoff is *mental selection* (a new selective regime). Neither treats **phenomenal experience as such** — the felt inner side, the consciousness–matter coupling — as the thing that transitioned. The Map's distinctive (and still-defensible, but speculative) angle is precisely that a framework built entirely in informational/replicative/functional terms would be **structurally blind** to a phenomenal transition, so even the existing "consciousness as a major transition" work quietly describes the *outside* of the event and leaves the inside unseen.

## Key Sources

### The Major Transitions in Evolution (book)
- **Citation**: Maynard Smith, J. & Szathmáry, E. (1995). *The Major Transitions in Evolution*. Oxford / New York: Oxford University Press (W. H. Freeman/Spektrum). ISBN 9780198502944.
- **URL**: https://global.oup.com/academic/product/the-major-transitions-in-evolution-9780198502944 ; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Major_Transitions_in_Evolution
- **Type**: Book (canonical)
- **Key points**:
  - Unifying idea: at each transition, entities that could previously replicate *independently* can afterward replicate only *as part of a larger whole* — a change in the level of individuality and in how heritable information is stored and transmitted.
  - The eight canonical transitions (see Major Positions below).
  - A second recurring theme: the evolution of new modes of *information transmission* (genetic code, epigenetic inheritance, language).
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral. The framework is silent on consciousness and phenomenality; the Map's interest is in what such silence *hides*.

- **URL**: https://www.nature.com/articles/374227a0
- **Type**: Journal article (the compact statement of the book's thesis).

### The Origins of Life (popular rewrite)

### Toward major evolutionary transitions theory 2.0
- **Citation**: Szathmáry, E. (2015). "Toward major evolutionary transitions theory 2.0." *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences* 112(33): 10104–10111. DOI: 10.1073/pnas.1421398112.
- **URL**: https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.1421398112
- **Type**: Journal article (framework update)
- **Key points**:
  - Retains a **two-dimensional** account: (i) evolution of informational systems, (ii) transitions in individuality.
  - Removes **sex** from the canonical transition list (sex is not a transition to a new individual).
  - Imports Queller's **fraternal vs. egalitarian** distinction (see below) and adds **endosymbiotic/plastid** events.
  - Emphasizes **multilevel selection** ("first without, then with, the collectives in focus") as the explanatory engine.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral. Confirms that the *entire theoretical apparatus* is informational + selectional — exactly the register the Map argues is phenomenality-blind.

### Fraternal / egalitarian transitions
- **Citation**: Queller, D. C. (1997). "Cooperators since life began." (Review of Maynard Smith & Szathmáry, *The Major Transitions in Evolution*). *Quarterly Review of Biology* 72(2): 184–188. *(Exact volume/pages to re-verify at publisher.)*
- **URL**: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/245797985_Cooperators_Since_Life_Began
- **Key points**:
  - **Fraternal** transitions: like units from related lineages (high relatedness → kin selection), e.g. multicellularity, eusociality.
  - **Egalitarian** transitions: unlike, unrelated units, e.g. the endosymbiotic origin of eukaryotes.
  - Now a standard axis for classifying transitions (adopted in Szathmáry 2015).

### Reorganization of fitness during transitions in individuality
- **Citations**:
  - Michod, R. E. (2003). "On the reorganization of fitness during evolutionary transitions in individuality." *Integrative and Comparative Biology* 43(1): 64–73. *(year/pages to re-verify.)*
  - Michod, R. E. (2007). "Evolution of individuality during the transition from unicellular to multicellular life." *PNAS* 104(suppl. 1): 8613–8618.
- **URL**: https://academic.oup.com/icb/article/43/1/64/604458 ; https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.0701489104
- **Type**: Journal articles (mechanism)
- **Key points**:
  - A transition requires **fitness to be transferred** from lower-level units to the new higher-level unit, and **decoupled** — the group's fitness stops being the mere sum/average of its parts.
  - Achieved through **division of labour** (germ/soma; reproductive vs. viability specialization) and mechanisms suppressing lower-level (cell-level) competition.
  - The higher-level individual becomes "a new focus level of complex adaptation."
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral, but useful: fitness reorganization is a purely *quantitative/functional* criterion for a transition. The Map can point out that any phenomenal transition would leave *no fitness-reorganization signature of its own* over and above the functional changes it accompanies.

### Darwinian Populations and Natural Selection
- **Citation**: Godfrey-Smith, P. (2009). *Darwinian Populations and Natural Selection*. Oxford: Oxford University Press.
- **URL**: https://ndpr.nd.edu/reviews/darwinian-populations-and-natural-selection/ ; https://petergodfreysmith.com/
- **Type**: Book (philosophy of biology)
- **Key points**:
  - A **Darwinian population** is a collection of things capable of evolution by natural selection; a **Darwinian individual** is any member of one.
  - **Gradualist / marginal-cases** approach: individuality comes in degrees; something can be a marginal, minimal, or paradigm Darwinian individual, and can move between these statuses over time.
  - Provides tools (fitness, heritability, reproduction, germ-line segregation) for analysing *when a higher-level entity has become a genuine individual*.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral/methodologically useful. The "degrees and marginal cases" framing gives the Map a vocabulary: if phenomenality has an evolutionary onset, was there a *marginal* period, and would a Darwinian-populations analysis even register it?

### Biological individuality (Clarke, Pradeu; SEP)
- **Citations**:
  - Clarke, E. (2010). "The problem of biological individuality." *Biological Theory* 5(4): 312–325. *(vol/pages to re-verify.)*
  - Pradeu, T. (2012). *The Limits of the Self: Immunology and Biological Identity*. Oxford University Press.
  - Pradeu, T. (2016). "The many faces of biological individuality." *Biology & Philosophy* 31: 761–773.
  - Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, "Biological Individuals."
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/biology-individual/ ; https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10539-016-9553-z
- **Key points**:
  - **Pluralism**: distinct kinds of individuality — evolutionary, physiological, immunological, ecological, developmental — need not coincide.
  - **Clarke**: organisms possess *individuating mechanisms* (policing, bottlenecks, germ-line sequestration) to varying degrees.
  - **Pradeu**: an immunological criterion of the individual (what the immune system tolerates), a *physiological* individuality that can diverge from the evolutionary one.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral. Relevant because the Map's dualism raises a further candidate axis — a *phenomenal* individuality (the boundary of a single subject of experience) that the existing pluralist list omits entirely. Cf. the Map's existing work on individuation and the combination problem.

### The Evolution of the Sensitive Soul / UAL
- **Citation**: Ginsburg, S. & Jablonka, E. (2019). *The Evolution of the Sensitive Soul: Learning and the Origins of Consciousness*. Cambridge, MA: MIT Press. ISBN 9780262039307.
- **Companion**: Birch, J., Ginsburg, S. & Jablonka, E. (2020). "Unlimited Associative Learning and the origins of consciousness: a primer and some predictions." *Biology & Philosophy* 35: 56. DOI: 10.1007/s10539-020-09772-0.
- **URL**: https://direct.mit.edu/books/monograph/4183/ ; https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10539-020-09772-0
- **Type**: Book + journal primer
- **Key points**:
  - Proposes a **transition marker** for minimal/basic consciousness: **Unlimited Associative Learning (UAL)** — the capacity to assign motivational value to novel compound stimuli/actions and use them as the basis for further learning.
  - UAL implies a *generous distribution* of consciousness (all vertebrates, many arthropods, some cephalopods) and dates the transition to ~the **Cambrian explosion** (~540 Mya), which they argue consciousness helped *drive*.
  - Explicitly nested in a **transitions** scheme: living → living-conscious → living-conscious-rational (the last marked by symbolic language, echoing Aristotle's nutritive/sensitive/rational souls).
- **Tenet alignment**: **Partial conflict / crucial contrast.** This is the closest existing work to the Map's thesis, and it is a *functionalist/learning-based* reading. UAL is a **behavioural marker** — a proxy detectable from the outside. The Map's dualism holds that a learning-capacity marker cannot *constitute* phenomenal experience (that is exactly the explanatory gap). So Ginsburg & Jablonka pre-empt the *label* "consciousness as a major transition" but not the Map's *content*: they operationalize the transition informationally, which (on the Map's reading) is what a phenomenality-blind framework would be forced to do.

### Consciousness and mental selection (2025)
- **Citation**: Jablonka, E. & Ginsburg, S. (2025). "Consciousness: its goals, its functions and the emergence of a new category of selection." *Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society B* 380(1939): 20240310. DOI: 10.1098/rstb.2024.0310. PubMed: 41229292.
- **URL**: https://royalsocietypublishing.org/doi/10.1098/rstb.2024.0310 ; open PDF: https://eprints.lse.ac.uk/130162/1/rstb.2024.0310.pdf
- **Type**: Journal article (theme issue)
- **Key points**:
  - Argues the emergence of consciousness introduced **new goals and functions**, giving rise to a new category of selection: **mental selection** — ontogenetic choices directed at consciously perceived and affectively evaluated patterns.
  - Mental selection *expands* the types, targets, and regimes of natural and sexual/social selection and scaffolds human artificial selection.
  - Positions consciousness among the major transitions as introducing a **novel selective force**, not merely a new individual.
- **Tenet alignment**: Same contrast as above. "Mental selection" is a *functional-causal* story about what conscious states *do* selectively; it does not address why there is *something it is like* to be the organism doing the selecting. This is the seam the Map targets.

### (Distinguish) UAL-as-transition primer + top-down causation
- Auletta / Ellis-style work on **top-down causation** in transitions (arXiv:1207.4808, "Evolutionary Transitions and Top-Down Causation") is adjacent: it argues higher levels exert real causal constraint. Potentially useful scaffolding for Tenet 3 (bidirectional/downward causation) but **not** about phenomenality. Cite carefully; do not conflate with the Map's claim.

## Major Positions

### The canonical MTE list (Maynard Smith & Szathmáry 1995)
- **Proponents**: Maynard Smith, Szathmáry.
- **Core claim**: Evolution's large-scale structure is punctuated by a handful of transitions in individuality/information. The original eight:
  1. Replicating molecules → populations of molecules in **compartments** (protocells).
  2. Independent replicators → **chromosomes**.
  3. RNA as gene-and-enzyme → **DNA + protein** (the genetic code).
  4. Prokaryotes → **eukaryotes** (via endosymbiosis).
  5. Asexual clones → **sexual populations**.
  6. Protists → **multicellular** organisms (plants, animals, fungi).
  7. Solitary individuals → **colonies / eusociality** (with non-reproductive castes).
  8. Primate societies → **human societies with language**.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Neutral substrate. Each item is stated in replicative/informational terms. The Map's observation: *the list-making criteria themselves* (new individuals, new information channels) contain no slot for "a new kind of *inner* fact appearing," so a phenomenal transition could occur without registering as an MTE.

### Consciousness as a (functionally-defined) major transition
- **Proponents**: Ginsburg & Jablonka; Birch (as commentator/collaborator on UAL); the 2025 Phil Trans theme issue.
- **Core claim**: There *is* a major transition to consciousness, marked by UAL and generating mental selection. Consciousness is an evolutionary innovation with a datable onset and selective consequences.
- **Key arguments**: learning-capacity marker (UAL); Cambrian timing; consciousness as a *driver* of diversification; a hierarchy of transitions (living / conscious / rational).
- **Relation to site tenets**: This is the **most important position to engage**. It concedes the Map's *headline* but resolves it functionally. The Map's dualism (Tenet 1) says the functional marker underdetermines the phenomenal fact; the Map's contribution is to argue the transition Ginsburg & Jablonka detect is the *behavioural shadow* of a deeper phenomenal transition their (non-dualist) framework cannot name.

### Transitions in individuality (Michod / Godfrey-Smith / Clarke / Pradeu)
- **Proponents**: Michod (fitness reorganization), Godfrey-Smith (Darwinian populations, degrees of individuality), Clarke (individuating mechanisms), Pradeu (physiological/immunological individuality).
- **Core claim**: Being an evolutionary individual is graded and multi-dimensional; transitions are reorganizations of fitness and cohesion, not metaphysical jumps.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Supplies the Map with (a) a *degrees* vocabulary for a possibly-gradual phenomenal onset and (b) a *pluralist individuality* map into which the Map can propose adding a **phenomenal/subject** axis — connecting to existing Map articles on individuation and the combination/de-combination problem.

## Key Debates

### Is the MTE list a natural kind or a heterogeneous grab-bag?
- **Sides**: Defenders (Szathmáry) argue for the two-axis unity (information + individuality). Critics (e.g. the *Frontiers* 2022 philosophy-of-science treatment; O'Malley, Calcott, and others) argue the eight items lack a single unifying property and mix different phenomena.
- **Core disagreement**: whether "major transition" carves nature or is a useful list.
- **Current state**: ongoing; Szathmáry 2.0 is a response to exactly this pressure.
- **Map relevance**: *strengthens* the Map's rhetorical opening — if the very category is contested and criterion-dependent, then which events "count" is theory-laden, and a dualist theory could legitimately propose an additional (phenomenal) transition.

### Does the transition to consciousness have a behavioural marker at all?
- **Sides**: Ginsburg & Jablonka (yes — UAL). Skeptics (e.g. those who stress the hard problem; some argue markers track *access*, not *phenomenality*).
- **Core disagreement**: whether any functional capacity can be *criterial* for felt experience.
- **Current state**: live and unresolved; this is the explanatory-gap debate in evolutionary dress.
- **Map relevance**: **the Map's home turf.** The Map sides with the skeptics on the metaphysics (a marker is evidence, not identity) while accepting UAL as good *evidence* of the transition's outer face.

### Where in the tree did phenomenality begin — and is that even discoverable?
- **Sides**: early/Cambrian (Ginsburg & Jablonka); much later/mammal-and-bird (higher-order theorists); panpsychist "always, in degree."
- **Map relevance**: connects to the Map's **void** articles (transition-void, individuation-void) — the onset may be *occluded* in principle if the only detectable traces are functional.

## Potential Article Angles

1. **"Consciousness as a Hidden Major Transition" (recommended primary angle).** Present the MTE framework faithfully, then argue: every canonical transition is fully specifiable in informational/replicative terms, and Ginsburg & Jablonka's consciousness-transition — though it uses the word — is *also* specified functionally (UAL, mental selection). On the Map's dualism, a framework whose only detectors are informational/selectional would be **constitutively blind** to a consciousness–matter coupling transition; it would register only the transition's behavioural shadow. Frame explicitly as a **framework-relative hypothesis** (tenet-coherent, not evidence-forced). Ties to Tenets 1 (dualism), 3 (bidirectional interaction — a coupling transition is where downward influence *begins*), and 5 (Occam's limits — the simplest, information-only ontology may be simple *because it is blind*).

2. **"The Missing Axis of Individuality."** Take the pluralist individuality literature (Michod/Godfrey-Smith/Clarke/Pradeu) and argue it enumerates evolutionary, physiological, immunological axes but omits a **phenomenal/subject** axis — the boundary of a single experiencer. Bridges to the Map's existing individuation and combination-problem articles.

3. **"What UAL Can and Cannot Mark."** A focused engagement conceding UAL as strong *evidence* of the transition's outer face while arguing (via the explanatory gap) that no learning-capacity marker can be *criterial* for phenomenality. Most defensible / least speculative; good calibration companion to Angle 1.
