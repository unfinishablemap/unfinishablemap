## Executive Summary

The question of whether consciousness survives bodily death sits at the intersection of metaphysics, philosophy of mind, and personal identity theory. Dualism opens the logical possibility of survival since immaterial minds could persist independently, but does not guarantee it—functional dependence on bodies might be essential. the site's existing personal-identity article already rejects Parfit's reductionism on grounds that indexical identity matters; this research extends that framework to the survival question. Key finding: the site's commitment to non-reducible consciousness and indexical uniqueness provides stronger resources for survival than materialist alternatives, though survival remains an open question rather than a commitment.

## Key Sources

### Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy - Afterlife
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/afterlife/
- **Type**: Encyclopedia
- **Key points**:
  - Dualism permits but doesn't guarantee survival; functional dependence might be essential
  - H.H. Price developed detailed "dream-image world" conception of disembodied existence
  - Van Inwagen's resurrection worry: recreating a body from scratch yields only a duplicate, not identity
  - Zimmerman's "closest continuer" response: particles "bud" at death, maintaining causal continuity
  - Theism and afterlife belief are mutually supporting
- **Tenet alignment**: Aligns with Dualism (permits survival) and No Many Worlds (identity questions matter)
- **Quote**: "If dualism is true, it does not necessarily follow that persons will survive the death of their bodies."

### Internet Encyclopedia of Philosophy - Immortality
- **URL**: https://iep.utm.edu/immortal/
- **Type**: Encyclopedia
- **Key points**:
  - Plato's arguments: cycles/opposites, reminiscence, affinity with forms
  - Descartes: indivisibility of mind implies survivability
  - Interaction problem remains central objection to dualism
  - Phineas Gage case used to argue brain dependence
  - Three identity criteria (soul, body, psychological) each have problems
  - Parfit's solution: abandon numerical identity, focus on psychological continuity
- **Tenet alignment**: Aligns with site's rejection of Parfit; conflicts with materialist objections
- **Quote**: "If mental activity reduces to cerebral activity, then death brings the total end of a person's existence."

### Ducasse - A Critical Examination of the Belief in a Life After Death (1961)
- **URL**: https://psi-encyclopedia.spr.ac.uk/articles/cj-ducasse
- **Type**: Academic monograph
- **Key points**:
  - Radio analogy: destroying the receiver ends the program locally, but program continues on the air
  - Early position: we have a "right, but not an obligation" to believe in survival
  - Later position: "balance of the evidence so far obtained is on the side of the reality of survival"
  - Coined term "paranormal" to distinguish from "supernatural"
  - Examined mediumistic evidence, apparitions, reincarnation memories
- **Tenet alignment**: Aligns with Dualism and Bidirectional Interaction—consciousness as non-reducible transmitter
- **Quote**: "Smashing the receiver of a radio brings to an end a program on the air, but this does not in the least warrant concluding that the program was a product of the radio."

### Van Inwagen on Resurrection and Materialism
- **URL**: https://www3.nd.edu/~jspeaks/courses/2006-7/20208/van-inwagen-resurrection.html
- **Type**: Academic analysis
- **Key points**:
  - Materialist resurrection requires physical continuity
  - "Simulacrum model": God secretly preserves corpse, replaces with duplicate
  - Van Inwagen later called this a "just-so story"—establishes possibility, probably not true
  - Problem: makes God seem devious
  - Zimmerman's alternative: "budding" particles at death maintain causal continuity
- **Tenet alignment**: Van Inwagen's materialism conflicts with site's dualism; but his identity concerns align
- **Quote**: "Even God cannot reassemble the molecules of a cremated individual in a manner that will make the miraculously assembled person one and the same as the individual cremated."

### Near-Death Experiences - 2024/2025 Research
- **URL**: https://med.virginia.edu/perceptual-studies/
- **Type**: Empirical research
- **Key points**:
  - 2024 University of Michigan study: gamma wave surge in dying brains (2 of 4 patients)
  - First evidence in humans of "end-of-life surge" previously only seen in rats
  - NDEs occur when brain is severely compromised—paradox for materialist explanation
  - Veridical perceptions: 120+ cases documented by Rivas et al. (2023)
  - 5-10% of general population report NDE memories
  - Charlotte Martial (Liège): "We don't question anymore the reality of NDEs"
  - Critique: no patient in surge study survived to confirm subjective experience
- **Tenet alignment**: NDE evidence is suggestive but not conclusive; aligns with possibility of consciousness beyond brain
- **Quote**: "Given that NDEs appear to occur at the very moment in cardiac arrest when the brain is severely compromised, some researchers interpret this as evidence for the continuity of consciousness beyond the brain."

## Major Positions

### Dualist Survival (Traditional View)
- **Proponents**: Plato, Descartes, C.J. Ducasse, contemporary substance dualists
- **Core claim**: Consciousness/soul is immaterial and therefore not subject to physical decomposition; survival is logically possible and may be actual
- **Key arguments**:
  - Immaterial substances cannot decompose like material things
  - Consciousness has properties (indivisibility, privacy) incompatible with physicality
  - Radio analogy: brain may be receiver, not generator, of consciousness
  - Empirical evidence from NDEs, mediumship, reincarnation memories
- **Relation to site tenets**: Strongly aligns with Dualism tenet; site's framework makes survival coherent. However, site doesn't commit to survival—only to its logical possibility given dualism.

### Materialist Extinction
- **Proponents**: Epicurus, Lucretius, modern physicalists
- **Core claim**: Consciousness is identical with or produced by brain processes; when the brain dies, consciousness ends
- **Key arguments**:
  - Neural correlates show consciousness depends on brain
  - Brain damage affects consciousness (Phineas Gage)
  - No mechanism for disembodied consciousness
  - Correlation is best explained by identity
- **Relation to site tenets**: Conflicts with Dualism and Bidirectional Interaction tenets. Site rejects this view.

### Materialist Resurrection (Van Inwagen, Zimmerman)
- **Proponents**: Peter van Inwagen, Dean Zimmerman, Christian materialists
- **Core claim**: Survival requires bodily resurrection; physical continuity must be maintained somehow
- **Key arguments**:
  - Van Inwagen's simulacrum: God preserves corpse secretly
  - Zimmerman's budding: particles "bud" at death, maintaining causal connection
  - Identity requires physical continuity for materialists
- **Relation to site tenets**: Conflicts with Dualism tenet. The identity problems van Inwagen raises are relevant, but site's dualism provides different resources.

### Parfit's "What Matters" View
- **Proponents**: Derek Parfit
- **Core claim**: Personal identity may have no determinate answer in survival cases, but this doesn't matter; what matters is psychological continuity, which can be preserved by replicas
- **Key arguments**:
  - Identity is not a "further fact" beyond psychological/physical continuity
  - Teletransportation preserves what matters even if it doesn't preserve identity
  - Survival questions may be indeterminate without being problematic
- **Relation to site tenets**: Conflicts with No Many Worlds and site's personal-identity article. Site holds indexical identity matters; replicas aren't you.

### Quantum Soul Hypothesis (Hameroff)
- **Proponents**: Stuart Hameroff, (cautiously) Roger Penrose
- **Core claim**: Consciousness arises from quantum processes in microtubules; at death, quantum information may be released and persist
- **Key arguments**:
  - Orch OR links consciousness to quantum coherence
  - Quantum information is preserved, not destroyed
  - NDEs explained by temporary release and return of quantum information
  - If not revived, information may persist as "soul"
- **Relation to site tenets**: Aligns with Minimal Quantum Interaction. Speculative but provides scientific framework for survival possibility.

### Agnostic Position (Ducasse later period)
- **Proponents**: Later Ducasse, many contemporary philosophers
- **Core claim**: Evidence for survival exists and is substantial, but not conclusive; we have reason to believe but not proof
- **Key arguments**:
  - Empirical evidence from parapsychology is suggestive
  - Philosophical arguments open the possibility
  - Conclusive proof may be impossible
  - Reasonable to hold belief but not certainty
- **Relation to site tenets**: Most compatible with site's framework—survival is possible, evidence exists, certainty is unavailable.

## Key Debates

### Identity Problem for Disembodied Survival
- **Sides**:
  - Critics: We cannot re-identify disembodied souls; they lack spatiotemporal location
  - Defenders (Hasker): This conflates epistemology with metaphysics; identity doesn't require our ability to detect it
- **Core disagreement**: Whether personal identity requires spatiotemporal criteria
- **Current state**: Ongoing. Site's emphasis on indexical identity sidesteps some problems—identity is what it is regardless of our criteria.

### Van Inwagen's Duplication Problem
- **Sides**:
  - Van Inwagen: Reassembled matter yields duplicate, not original
  - Zimmerman: "Budding" particles maintain causal continuity
  - Dualists: Problem doesn't arise if identity is in non-physical consciousness
- **Core disagreement**: Whether physical continuity is necessary for survival
- **Current state**: Site's dualism provides different answer: identity is in consciousness, not matter. But question of what maintains consciousness-identity remains open.

### NDEs as Evidence
- **Sides**:
  - Proponents (Greyson, Rivas): Veridical perceptions, dying-brain-yet-heightened-consciousness paradox
  - Skeptics: Physiological explanations (anoxia, endorphins, temporal lobe activity)
- **Core disagreement**: Whether NDEs provide genuine evidence for consciousness beyond brain
- **Current state**: Ongoing. 2024 brain surge studies complicate picture—heightened activity exists, but none of studied patients survived to confirm experience.

### Parfit vs. Non-Reductionism
- **Sides**:
  - Parfit: Identity is reducible; what matters is psychological continuity
  - Non-reductionists: There's a further fact about identity; replicas aren't you
- **Core disagreement**: Whether indexical identity is real and significant
- **Current state**: Site's existing personal-identity article commits to non-reductionism based on No Many Worlds tenet.
