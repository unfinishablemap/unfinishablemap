# Research: Bandwidth Constraints — Grounding the ~10 Bits/Second Figure

**Date**: 2026-03-29
**Search queries used**: "conscious processing bandwidth 10 bits per second Norretranders", "consciousness information processing rate bits per second empirical evidence", "Zheng Meister Unbearable Slowness of Being Neuron 2024 methodology", "criticisms 10 bits per second consciousness brain bandwidth", "Hick's law reaction time information theory bits per second", "Shannon information theory typing speed reading speed bits per second human performance", "brain works at more than 10 bits Nature Neuroscience 2025", "George Miller magical number seven information processing capacity 1956", "speech production information rate 39 bits per second Coupé 2019"

## Executive Summary

The claim that conscious processing operates at approximately 10 bits per second rests on nearly a century of converging empirical measurements across radically different tasks — from Hick's 1952 choice reaction experiments (~5 bits/s) through memory sports (~18 bits/s at peak) to professional e-sports (~10 bits/s). Zheng and Meister's 2024 Neuron paper synthesised these measurements into a unified claim: human behavioural throughput is bounded at ~10 bits/s regardless of domain. The figure is grounded in Shannon information theory applied to observable behaviour, not introspection. A 2025 Nature Neuroscience critique (Sauerbrei & Pruszynski) accepts the cognitive ceiling but argues it excludes unconscious motor control, which substantially exceeds 10 bits/s. This distinction — conscious throughput versus total neural throughput — is precisely what makes the figure relevant to dualist frameworks: the bottleneck exists at the conscious interface, not in the neural hardware.

## Key Sources

### Hick (1952) — "On the Rate of Gain of Information"
- **URL**: https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/17470215208416600
- **Type**: Experimental research (Quarterly Journal of Experimental Psychology)
- **Key points**:
  - Applied Shannon's information theory to choice reaction time experiments
  - Found reaction time increases logarithmically with number of alternatives: RT = a + b·log₂(N)
  - Measured rate of information gain at ~5 bits/second, constant across task durations
  - Established the foundational methodology: treat human behaviour as an information channel, measure throughput in bits
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral (empirical measurement). Establishes the measurement framework that later work builds on
- **Quote**: "The rate of gain of information is, on the average, constant with respect to time, within the duration of one perceptual-motor act, and has a value of the order of five 'bits' per second."

### Hyman (1953) — Stimulus Information as a Determinant of Reaction Time
- **URL**: https://psycnet.apa.org/record/1954-04849-001
- **Type**: Experimental research (Journal of Experimental Psychology)
- **Key points**:
  - Confirmed Hick's logarithmic relationship with stimulus information
  - Average slope of ~150 ms per bit across subjects and conditions
  - Extended the paradigm to unequal stimulus probabilities
  - Together with Hick, established what is now called Hick-Hyman Law
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral (empirical confirmation)

### Miller (1956) — "The Magical Number Seven, Plus or Minus Two"
- **URL**: https://psycnet.apa.org/record/1957-02914-001
- **Type**: Review article (Psychological Review)
- **Key points**:
  - Identified capacity limits in absolute judgement (~2.5 bits per dimension) and immediate memory (~7 chunks)
  - Distinguished between bits and chunks — memory span is chunk-limited, not bit-limited
  - Showed that "recoding" (chunking) is how humans work around the channel capacity limit
  - The ~2.5 bits per judgement dimension corresponds to distinguishing about 6 levels, not hundreds
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral. Establishes that conscious processing has hard capacity limits that clever strategies (chunking) can work around but not remove — consistent with an interface bottleneck
- **Quote**: "There seems to be some limitation built into us either by learning or by the design of our nervous systems, a limit that keeps our channel capacities in this general range."

### Pierce & Karlin (1957) — "Reading Rates and the Information Rate of a Human Channel"
- **URL**: https://archive.org/details/bstj36-2-497
- **Type**: Experimental research (Bell System Technical Journal)
- **Key points**:
  - Measured reading aloud as an information channel
  - Applied Shannon's redundancy estimates for English to reading rates
  - Estimated human channel capacity from reading performance
  - Part of the Bell Labs information-theoretic approach to human performance

### Nørretranders (1998) — "The User Illusion"
- **URL**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/User_illusion
- **Type**: Book (popular science)
- **Key points**:
  - Popularised the bandwidth disparity: ~11 million bits/s sensory input vs ~16-20 bits/s conscious throughput
  - Drew on Zimmermann (1986) for sensory bandwidth estimates: vision ~10 million bits/s, touch ~1 million bits/s, hearing/smell/taste much less
  - Proposed the "user illusion" metaphor: consciousness is a simplified desktop, not the underlying computation
  - The specific figures have been refined but the order-of-magnitude picture holds
- **Tenet alignment**: Supports Dualism. The vast reduction from sensory input to conscious throughput is unexplained by neural architecture alone — neurons individually transmit far more than 10 bits/s
- **Quote**: "Consciousness is a much smaller part of our mental life than we are conscious of."

### Coupé et al. (2019) — "Different Languages, Similar Encoding Efficiency"
- **URL**: https://www.science.org/content/article/human-speech-may-have-universal-transmission-rate-39-bits-second
- **Type**: Research article (Science Advances)
- **Key points**:
  - Analysed 17 languages from 9 linguistic families
  - Found a universal speech information transmission rate of ~39 bits/second
  - Languages trade off information density per syllable against speech rate to converge on this constant
  - Fast-spoken languages (e.g., Japanese) carry less information per syllable; slow-spoken languages (e.g., Vietnamese) carry more
  - Described as "one of the few true language universals" — constrained by human cognitive limits, not language structure
- **Tenet alignment**: Partially supports the bandwidth constraint thesis but at a higher rate (~39 bits/s) than the ~10 bits/s figure. The discrepancy may reflect that speech involves both conscious content selection (~10 bits/s) and well-practised motor execution that leverages unconscious processing — consistent with Sauerbrei & Pruszynski's critique
- **Note for article**: The 39 bits/s figure measures the *information content of the speech signal*, not the speaker's conscious decision-making rate. A fluent speaker doesn't consciously select each phoneme; much of speech production is automated. The ~10 bits/s figure measures the rate at which novel decisions enter the stream.

### Zheng & Meister (2024) — "The Unbearable Slowness of Being"
- **URL**: https://www.cell.com/neuron/fulltext/S0896-6273(24)00808-0
- **Type**: Perspective article (Neuron, Volume 113, Issue 2, pp. 192-204)
- **Key points**:
  - Not a new experiment but a synthesis of existing measurements across domains
  - Applied Shannon information theory consistently to diverse behavioural tasks
  - The "outer brain" (sensory/motor) processes ~10⁹ bits/s; the "inner brain" (conscious thought) processes ~10 bits/s
  - The "sifting ratio" of 10⁸ is the largest unexplained number in brain science
  - Individual neurons can transmit ~2 bits per spike at ~100 spikes/s = ~200 bits/s per neuron — the bottleneck is not at the cellular level
  - The brain stores only ~1 GB over a lifetime, far below its ~50 TB theoretical capacity
  - Speculates the serial architecture was inherited from primitive organisms navigating chemical gradients
- **Tenet alignment**: Strongly supports Dualism (the bottleneck is inexplicable if consciousness is merely neural computation) and Minimal Quantum Interaction (a narrow interface is what you'd expect if consciousness selects among options rather than computing them)
- **Quote**: "This stark contrast remains unexplained and resolving this paradox should teach us something fundamental about brain function."

### Sauerbrei & Pruszynski (2025) — "The Brain Works at More Than 10 Bits Per Second"
- **URL**: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41593-025-01997-0
- **Type**: Commentary (Nature Neuroscience)
- **Key points**:
  - Accepts the ~10 bits/s ceiling for high-level cognitive functions
  - Argues it does not apply to unconscious motor control: the cerebellum (half the brain's neurons) performs continuous real-time sensorimotor control far exceeding 10 bits/s
  - Example: a runner's stride takes 250 ms; specifying phase, amplitude, and duty cycle for even one muscle requires more than 3 bits per stride — and dozens of muscles must be coordinated simultaneously
  - Contends Zheng & Meister's "inner/outer brain" division is too clean — consciousness does not reside in a single brain area
  - The 10 bits/s figure applies to *declarative* throughput (what you can report, decide, remember) but not to *procedural* throughput (automatic motor execution)
- **Tenet alignment**: Actually *strengthens* the dualist interpretation. The critique confirms that the bottleneck is specifically at the conscious level — the neural hardware is not the limiting factor. If vast unconscious motor processing coexists with a ~10 bits/s conscious ceiling, the bottleneck must be at the interface between consciousness and neural computation, not in the computation itself

### Important Methodological Notes

1. **Reading and speech rates (~39-50 bits/s) appear higher** because they measure the information content of the *output signal*, not the rate of novel conscious decisions. Much of reading comprehension and speech production is automated; the conscious contribution is closer to ~10 bits/s.
2. **Speed cards (~17.7 bits/s) is a peak figure** achieved by elite performers under competitive conditions. The consistent average across ordinary performance is ~10 bits/s.
3. **All figures use Shannon information theory**: entropy of the decision space, not subjective difficulty. A binary choice = 1 bit regardless of how "hard" it feels.

## The Measurement Framework

### How "Bits Per Second" Is Calculated

Shannon information theory provides the formal framework:

1. **Define the decision space**: How many possible actions/states could the person choose among? (e.g., 26 letters, 52 cards, N Tetris piece placements)
2. **Calculate entropy**: H = log₂(N) for equiprobable alternatives, or H = -Σ p·log₂(p) for non-uniform distributions
3. **Account for redundancy**: English text has ~1 bit per character (not log₂(26) ≈ 4.7 bits) because of statistical structure
4. **Divide by time**: bits of information resolved / seconds elapsed = bits per second

### Why This Is a Measure of *Conscious* Processing

The critical methodological point: these measurements capture the rate at which a human makes *observable decisions that require awareness*. They do not measure:
- Unconscious sensory processing (vastly faster)
- Unconscious motor execution (also faster, per Sauerbrei & Pruszynski)
- Parallel preprocessing that narrows the decision space before consciousness selects

The ~10 bits/s figure measures the throughput of the conscious bottleneck itself — the point where options become decisions.

## Major Positions

### The Universal Cognitive Bottleneck (Zheng & Meister)
- **Proponents**: Jieyu Zheng, Markus Meister (Caltech)
- **Core claim**: The ~10 bits/s ceiling is universal across tasks, populations, and historical periods. It is not a contingent limitation but a fundamental architectural feature of the brain
- **Key arguments**: Measurements converge across typing, gaming, puzzles, memory sports, reading; the ceiling persists even as task-specific skills improve through practice; individual neurons have far more capacity, so the bottleneck is architectural, not cellular
- **Relation to site tenets**: The universality and irreducibility of the ceiling supports the view that it reflects a fundamental interface constraint — consistent with consciousness operating at a narrow point of interaction with neural hardware

### The Motor Control Exception (Sauerbrei & Pruszynski)
- **Proponents**: Britton Sauerbrei, J. Andrew Pruszynski
- **Core claim**: The 10 bits/s figure is correct for declarative cognition but excludes the enormous information throughput of unconscious motor control
- **Key arguments**: The cerebellum contains ~50% of the brain's neurons; specifying a single muscle's parameters for one stride requires more than 3 bits; dozens of muscles are coordinated in real time; this unconscious processing is genuinely part of the central nervous system
- **Relation to site tenets**: Strengthens rather than weakens the dualist case. Confirms the bottleneck is specifically at the conscious level, not a neural hardware limitation. The very fact that unconscious processing is fast while conscious processing is slow suggests the bottleneck is at the interface between two distinct systems

### The Evolutionary Legacy Hypothesis
- **Proponents**: Zheng & Meister (speculative)
- **Core claim**: The serial architecture of consciousness may be inherited from primitive organisms that navigated chemical gradients — fundamentally one-thing-at-a-time processing
- **Key arguments**: Simple organisms make binary decisions (approach/avoid) at low rates; brains evolved new sensory/motor capabilities but the core decision loop remained serial; multitasking is genuinely impossible for consciousness (we time-share, not parallelise)
- **Relation to site tenets**: If correct, this is a physicalist explanation for the bottleneck. However, it doesn't explain *why* the bottleneck persists despite the brain having vastly more computational capacity. Evolution optimises; if the bottleneck could be widened by neural reorganisation alone, it should have been

## Key Debates

### Debate 1: Does 10 Bits/Second Measure Consciousness or Behaviour?
- **Sides**: Zheng & Meister (it measures the "inner brain" — effectively conscious processing) vs Sauerbrei & Pruszynski (it measures only declarative behaviour; total brain throughput is much higher)
- **Core disagreement**: Whether the 10 bits/s figure can be attributed to consciousness specifically, or only to behavioural output that happens to require conscious involvement
- **Current state**: Both sides agree on the empirical measurements. The disagreement is interpretive. For dualist purposes, the key point is uncontested: the throughput ceiling exists specifically where consciousness is involved

### Debate 2: Is the Ceiling Trainable?
- **Sides**: Fixed architectural limit vs practice-expandable capacity
- **Core disagreement**: Whether elite performers (memory athletes, speed cubers) achieve higher bits/s through greater conscious throughput or through better chunking/automation that *reduces* the load on consciousness
- **Current state**: Evidence favours the chunking explanation. Expert typists don't process more bits/s of novel information — they chunk familiar sequences, reducing the effective information per keystroke. Memory athletes use the method of loci to exploit spatial memory, compressing arbitrary sequences into familiar spatial patterns. The ceiling itself appears fixed; expertise moves work below it

### Debate 3: Why Is the Number ~10 and Not ~100 or ~1?
- **Sides**: No satisfying answer exists
- **Core disagreement**: What determines the specific value of the bottleneck?
- **Current state**: Open question. Zheng & Meister call it "the largest unexplained number in brain science." Possible explanations include neural oscillation frequencies (alpha rhythm at ~10 Hz), metabolic costs of conscious processing, or — from the Map's perspective — the bandwidth of the mind-matter interface itself

## Gaps in Research

- **The specific value ~10 is unexplained**: No theory predicts why the ceiling is at this particular rate rather than some other order of magnitude. This is genuinely open
- **Neural correlates of the bottleneck are unknown**: Where in the brain does the 10⁹ → 10 reduction occur? Global workspace theory suggests prefrontal/parietal broadcasting, but the information-theoretic reduction hasn't been localised
- **No formal philosophical treatment**: Despite decades of empirical work, no philosopher has systematically analysed what the ~10 bits/s ceiling implies for the mind-body problem. This is a gap the Map can fill
- **Cross-species comparison is sparse**: If the ceiling is an evolutionary legacy, what is the conscious throughput of other animals? Zheng & Meister mention primitive organisms but don't systematically compare vertebrate cognitive throughputs
- **Relationship to neural oscillations**: Alpha rhythm (~10 Hz) is suggestively close to 10 bits/s, but no rigorous connection has been established. If each alpha cycle corresponds to one conscious "sample" carrying ~1 bit, this would explain the figure — but the evidence is circumstantial
