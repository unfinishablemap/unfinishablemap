## Executive Summary

Self-representationalism (Kriegel's earlier label: "same-order monitoring theory") holds that a mental state is phenomenally conscious just in case it represents *itself* in the right way — a single state doing both the world-directed and the awareness-conferring work, rather than the two-state structure of higher-order theories. Its motivation is precisely the misrepresentation/targetless-HOT objections and the "intimacy" worry (HOT divides conscious state and awareness-of-it between two states that could come apart). Uriah Kriegel is the flagship proponent (OUP 2009 monograph, publisher-verified); Kenneth Williford, Rocco Gennaro (with an important taxonomy wrinkle — he classifies his Wide Intrinsicality View as a *version of HOT theory*), and Robert Van Gulick develop variants. The lineage runs Aristotle → Brentano (1874, the "secondary object" doctrine) → Sartre → the phenomenological tradition. The standard objections: it inherits the sufficiency gap (why should self-representation *feel* like anything? — Levine, Weisberg), faces its own regress worries (Williford's own taxonomy of three regresses), and phenomenologists (Zahavi) argue pre-reflective self-awareness is not representational at all. Notably, the theory's most sophisticated defenders have been drifting from *representation* to *acquaintance* (Williford 2019; Giustina 2022), which concedes ground congenial to the Map's phenomenal-presentation concept.

## Key Sources

### Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy — "Higher-Order Theories of Consciousness" (section on self-representational and hybrid accounts)
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/consciousness-higher/
- **Type**: Encyclopedia
- **Key points**:
  - Treats self-representational accounts as a family within/adjacent to the higher-order programme, "inspired by Brentano (1874) and the phenomenological tradition, including Sartre."
  - Canonical formulation: "A phenomenally conscious mental state is a state of a certain sort (perhaps with analog/non-conceptual intentional content) which also, at the same time, possesses an intentional content, thereby in some sense representing *itself*."
  - Distinguishes **identity-based** accounts (one state is simultaneously world-directed and self-presenting) from **part-whole** accounts (a complex state one part of which represents another part or the whole).
  - Notes Kriegel originally coined "same-order monitoring theory" but the label was potentially misleading; "self-representational" stuck.
  - Variants catalogued: Kriegel (2003, 2006, 2009); Gennaro's Wide Intrinsicality View (1996, 2012); Van Gulick's Higher-Order Global States (HOGS, 2000, 2004); Carruthers' dual-content theory (2000, 2005 — Carruthers later abandoned higher-order approaches).
  - Misrepresentation resurfaces *inside* the complex state: Picciuto (2011) notes a first-order content *green* could integrate with a higher-order component *experiencing yellow*.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral survey; the theory surveyed is reductive-representationalist, conflicting with Tenet 1.

### Internet Encyclopedia of Philosophy — "Higher-Order Theories of Consciousness" (author: Rocco J. Gennaro)
- **URL**: https://iep.utm.edu/higher-order-theories-of-consciousness/
- **Type**: Encyclopedia
- **Key points**:
  - Gennaro classifies his own Wide Intrinsicality View as "a version of HOT theory" with a structural modification: "when one has a first-order conscious state, the (unconscious) HOT is better viewed as intrinsic to the target state" — one complex state with a world-directed part and a metapsychological part.
  - **Taxonomy warning for the article**: whether self-representationalism is a *rival* to HOT or a *variant* of it is itself contested, and depends on the theorist. Kriegel markets it as an alternative; Gennaro markets his intrinsicalist structure as HOT-with-repairs. An article should get this right rather than flattening it.
  - Gennaro disputes Kriegel's phenomenological claim of ubiquitous *conscious* peripheral self-awareness accompanying all outer-directed experience.
  - Gennaro's answer to misrepresentation: "misrepresentations cannot occur between M and HOT *and* still result in a conscious state" — a full or partial conceptual match is required. (This blocks the targetless-HOT problem by fiat of the integration requirement.)
- **Tenet alignment**: Conflicts with Tenet 1 (explicitly reductive); useful as the theory's most HOT-friendly presentation.

### Kriegel, *Subjective Consciousness: A Self-Representational Theory* (OUP 2009) — PUBLISHER-VERIFIED
- **URL**: https://global.oup.com/academic/product/subjective-consciousness-9780199570355 (hardback); https://academic.oup.com/book/9660 (Oxford Academic)
- **Type**: Book (monograph)
- **Metadata**: Oxford University Press, hardback published 11 October 2009, ISBN 978-0199570355; paperback ISBN 978-0199694709; ~340 pp.
- **Key points**:
  - Central thesis (publisher's description): conscious mental states always represent themselves, whereas unconscious ones do not (at least not in the right way).
  - The two-component analysis: phenomenal consciousness = **qualitative character** (the *what* it is like; e.g. bluish) + **subjective character** (the *for-me-ness*; that it is like anything *for a subject* at all). Kriegel argues subjective character is the core of the mystery — the hard problem is really about subjective character.
  - Self-representation must be **non-derivative, specific, and essential** to confer subjective character (Levine's review, below, for this formulation).
  - Inner awareness is **peripheral**, not attentive: a ubiquitous, inattentive self-awareness accompanies every focal outer-directed conscious state (Kriegel's answer to "I'm aware of the coffee, not of my perceiving").
  - Naturalization strategy: a "two-vehicle-one-state" model — distinct neural vehicles for the first-order and self-directed components, integrated into a single state by synchronous neural firing, so causal-informational theories of representation can apply.
- **Tenet alignment**: Conflicts with Tenet 1 — the project is explicitly to naturalize/reduce subjective character. But the *datum* it centres (ubiquitous for-me-ness) is the same one the Map's Zahavian commitments protect.

### Kriegel & Williford (eds.), *Self-Representational Approaches to Consciousness* (MIT Press 2006) — PUBLISHER-VERIFIED
- **URL**: https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262612111/self-representational-approaches-to-consciousness/ ; https://philpapers.org/rec/KRISAT
- **Type**: Edited volume
- **Metadata**: MIT Press (A Bradford Book), published 31 March 2006, ISBN 978-0262612111 (paper). Contributors include Andrew Brook, Antonio Damasio (with David Rudrauf), Douglas Hofstadter, Uriah Kriegel, Joseph Levine, Robert Van Gulick, Dan Zahavi, Kenneth Williford, and others. Four sections: supportive theories, critiques, connections to other cognitive issues, beyond philosophy.
- **Key points**:
  - The field-defining collection; positions self-representationalism as the *third* option against the two dominant reductive programmes (first-order representationalism and higher-order monitoring).
  - Contains both the canonical developments (Kriegel's "same-order monitoring" chapter; Williford's "The Self-Representational Structure of Consciousness") and the canonical critiques (Levine's and Zahavi's chapters).
  - Hofstadter's chapter links conscious self-reference to Gödelian "strange loops" — a hook to the Map's self-reference-paradox material.
- **Tenet alignment**: Mixed by chapter; the volume's critics' section (Levine, Zahavi) is where the Map's allies sit.

### Levine, review of *Subjective Consciousness*, Notre Dame Philosophical Reviews (2010-03-14)
- **URL**: https://ndpr.nd.edu/reviews/subjective-consciousness-a-self-representational-theory/
- **Type**: Review article (by Joseph Levine, UMass Amherst — the "explanatory gap" theorist)
- **Key points**:
  - Endorses Kriegel's *framing* (subjective character is the heart of the hard problem) while rejecting the reduction.
  - Three criticisms: (1) the introspective argument for ubiquitous inner awareness fails — introspection discovering subjective character doesn't show a *conscious* self-representation was already there; (2) the response-dependent/dispositional machinery for qualitative character is circular or indeterminate; (3) the two-vehicle-one-state model either collapses back into the two-state (HOT) structure it was meant to escape, or relies on an unexplained notion of "indirect representation" — representing part of a state doesn't make the whole state represented *phenomenally* (his apple-surface analogy: representing the surface represents the whole apple, but not the whole apple *phenomenally*).
  - Levine's verdict: representational theories as such cannot capture consciousness's phenomenological character — his own alternative invokes **acquaintance**.
- **Tenet alignment**: The critique aligns with Tenet 1's irreducibility claim; Levine is the standard "friendly to the datum, hostile to the reduction" reference.

### Williford — self-acquaintance and the regress taxonomy
- **URLs**: https://philpapers.org/rec/WILTSS (2006 chapter); https://www.pdcnet.org/pdc/bvdb.nsf/purchase26?openform=&fp=protosociology&id=protosociology_2019_0036_0368_0412 (2019 paper)
- **Type**: Book chapter (MIT 2006) + journal article (*ProtoSociology* 36 (2019): 368-412, "Self-Acquaintance and Three Regress Arguments" — metadata from Philosophy Documentation Center listing)
- **Key points**:
  - Williford 2006 already flagged the internal regress: a self-representing state must represent its representational properties, including its property of representing itself — threatening an infinite intensive regress of contents.
  - Williford 2019 distinguishes three classic regresses facing the self-awareness thesis (the Extensive Regress of states, the Intensive Regress of contents, and the Fichte-Henrich-Shoemaker Regress of de se beliefs) and argues all are resolved by a **self-acquaintance postulate**: consciousness has an irreducibly circular structure; self-acquaintance is not an independent entity bearing an external relation to itself but a realized relation-instance relating to itself.
  - Trajectory note: Williford's mature view (also "Representationalisms, Subjective Character, and Self-Acquaintance," 2015) *abandons representation* as the fundamental notion — acquaintance, not representation, carries subjective character.
- **Tenet alignment**: The drift from representation to acquaintance is a concession the Map can use: the theory's own defenders found representational resources insufficient for for-me-ness.

### Giustina, "An Acquaintance Alternative to Self-Representationalism" — PUBLISHER-VERIFIED
- **URL**: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11098-022-01868-5
- **Type**: Journal article — *Philosophical Studies* 179(12) (2022): 3831-3863
- **Key points**:
  - Argues phenomenal consciousness is fundamentally explained by the *relation of acquaintance*, only partly (if at all) by representation; systematically catalogues where self-representationalism under-delivers on inner awareness.
  - Marks the current state of the dialectic: the live successor debate is self-representation vs inner acquaintance, not self-representation vs HOT.
- **Tenet alignment**: Acquaintance views are structurally closer to the Map's phenomenal-presentation concept (presence without representational mediation), though Giustina's programme remains naturalist.

### Weisberg, "Same Old, Same Old: The Same-Order Representational Theory of Consciousness and the Division of Phenomenal Labor"
- **URL**: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11229-006-9106-0 ; https://philpapers.org/rec/WEICAT
- **Type**: Journal article — *Synthese* 160(2) (2008): 161-181
- **Key points**:
  - The sharpest deflationary critique from *within* the reductive camp: the same-order theory was motivated by the "intimacy" objection to HOT (HOT illicitly divides the phenomenal labor between two states), but Weisberg argues the same-order theory *also* fails to secure intimacy — the self-representing component and the represented component still stand in a representational relation that can in principle misfire.
  - Upshot used by Rosenthalians: if the intimacy objection fails against HOT, self-representationalism loses its main selling point; if it succeeds, it hits self-representationalism too.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral (an intra-physicalist dispute), but useful to show the misrepresentation dodge is contested even on its own terms.

### Zahavi & Kriegel, "For-Me-Ness: What It Is and What It Is Not"
- **URL**: https://philpapers.org/rec/ZAHFWI
- **Type**: Book chapter — in D. Dahlstrom, A. Elpidorou & W. Hopp (eds.), *Philosophy of Mind and Phenomenology: Conceptual and Empirical Approaches*, Routledge (2015, sometimes dated 2016), pp. 36-53
- **Key points**:
  - The joint defense of the *datum* (ubiquitous for-me-ness) by the phenomenologist and the self-representationalist — deliberately theory-neutral about its *explanation*. This is presumably the channel through which Kriegel already entered the Map's corpus 19x.
  - Confirms the clean division an article should draw: Zahavi and Kriegel agree experience has constitutive for-me-ness; they disagree on whether it is a *representational* achievement (Kriegel: yes, self-representation) or a *primitive, non-relational mode of givenness* (Zahavi: pre-reflective self-awareness is not a representation at all — representational accounts objectify consciousness and invite regress).
- **Tenet alignment**: The Zahavi side of the split is the Map's existing commitment (self-and-self-consciousness page).

### Brentano lineage sources
- **URLs**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/brentano/ ; https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11245-020-09737-8 (Topoi, "A Systematic Reconstruction of Brentano's Theory of Consciousness")
- **Type**: Encyclopedia + journal article
- **Key points**:
  - Brentano, *Psychology from an Empirical Standpoint* (1874): every conscious act takes two objects — a **primary object** (e.g. the tone heard) and a **secondary object** (the act itself), given "incidentally" (nebenbei) in inner perception. Inner perception is infallible but can never become inner *observation*.
  - The Aristotelian root: *De Anima* III.2 — "we perceive that we see and hear" — and Aristotle's regress argument that this perceiving-of-perceiving cannot require a further faculty, else regress; so the seeing itself must include awareness of itself. Brentano explicitly aligned himself with Aristotle here.
  - Scholarly caution (Topoi 2020 and related literature): whether Brentano is genuinely a *self-representationalist* forerunner is contested — critics note his medieval-inflected notion of "object" and the elusive, incidental character of the secondary object don't map cleanly onto contemporary representation talk. An article should say "standardly claimed as the lineage" rather than asserting identity.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral history; the Aristotle-Brentano regress-blocking move is the structural ancestor of the same-order strategy.

### Gennaro, *The Consciousness Paradox: Consciousness, Concepts, and Higher-Order Thoughts* (MIT Press 2012) — PUBLISHER-VERIFIED
- **URL**: https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262016605/the-consciousness-paradox/ ; https://direct.mit.edu/books/monograph/3777
- **Type**: Book (monograph), MIT Press 2012, ISBN 978-0262016605
- **Key points**:
  - Book-length development of the Wide Intrinsicality View: the conscious state is a complex — the-experience's-being-the-object-of-the-unconscious-HOT — with the HOT intrinsic to the complex, not an extrinsic second state.
  - The "paradox" is the joint tension of: HOT theory is true; the hard problem is solvable; infants and most animals are conscious; conscious contents are conceptualized by the subject's own concepts; most concepts are acquired. WIV is engineered to hold all of these at once — notably preserving animal/infant consciousness, which standard HOT struggles with (a point the Map's higher-order-theories page already presses against Rosenthal).
- **Tenet alignment**: Conflicts with Tenet 1 (reductive); the corpus's higher-order-theories page already cites Gennaro 2012 in its reference list without expounding WIV.

## Major Positions

### Kriegel's Self-Representationalism (the flagship)
- **Proponents**: Uriah Kriegel (2003 *Canadian Journal of Philosophy* "Consciousness as Intransitive Self-Consciousness"; 2006 volume; 2009 monograph)
- **Core claim**: M is phenomenally conscious iff M non-derivatively, specifically, and essentially represents itself. Subjective character (for-me-ness) is constituted by this self-representation; qualitative character by the state's world-directed response-dependent content.
- **Key arguments**: (i) phenomenological — every experience involves peripheral inner awareness; (ii) dialectical — inherits HOT's explanatory ambitions while dodging targetless/misrepresentation objections, since a state cannot fail to exist when it represents itself; (iii) naturalistic — two vehicles integrated by neural synchrony into one state keeps causal theories of content applicable.
- **Relation to site tenets**: A reduction of exactly the datum (for-me-ness) the Map treats as evidence for Tenet 1. Engagement pattern parallels the SMT page: grant the structural achievement, argue the reduction re-sites rather than closes the hard problem — *why is self-representation felt rather than dark?* A zombie state that represented itself would satisfy the analysis; nothing in "x represents x" explains phenomenal presence (Levine's sufficiency gap). Note also the empirical hostage: the synchrony-integration mechanism leans on binding-by-synchrony neuroscience whose stock has fallen.

### Gennaro's Wide Intrinsicality View (the HOT-loyalist variant)
- **Proponents**: Rocco Gennaro (1996 *Consciousness and Self-Consciousness*; 2012 *The Consciousness Paradox*)
- **Core claim**: the (unconscious) HOT is intrinsic to a complex conscious state — one state, two parts (world-directed + metapsychological). Explicitly billed as a *version of HOT theory*, not a rival.
- **Key arguments**: blocks misrepresentation by requiring conceptual match between the parts; preserves animal/infant consciousness via modest, acquired-concept-friendly HOTs.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Same Tenet 1 conflict as HOT. **Taxonomy point the article must get right** (flagged in the task): self-representationalism is not one thing — Kriegel's identity/part-whole self-representation markets itself as a third camp; Gennaro's WIV markets nearly the same structure as HOT-repaired. The SEP files both under higher-order theories; Kriegel & Williford 2006 bills the approach as an alternative to both reductive camps.

### Williford's Self-Acquaintance View (the post-representational drift)
- **Proponents**: Kenneth Williford (2006, 2015, 2019); allied: Levine's acquaintance gestures; Giustina's inner-acquaintance programme (2022)
- **Core claim**: subjective character is each conscious episode's *acquaintance with itself* — an irreducibly circular, non-representational relation-instance. Representation is the wrong category; acquaintance dissolves the three regresses.
- **Key arguments**: the regress taxonomy (extensive/intensive/Fichte-Henrich-Shoemaker) shows representational self-directedness either regresses or stipulates; self-acquaintance is postulated as primitive.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Most congenial variant. Positing a primitive, irreducibly circular self-presence is a large step from reductive representationalism toward the Map's phenomenal-presentation and Zahavian commitments — though Williford still seeks a naturalist home for it (his later work with Rudrauf on the Projective Consciousness Model).

### Van Gulick's HOGS and Carruthers' Dual-Content (the also-rans)
- **Proponents**: Robert Van Gulick (2000, 2004); Peter Carruthers (2000, 2005)
- **Core claims**: HOGS — globally broadcast states acquire a higher-order "seeming" dimension by being embedded in self-models within a global workspace (a same-order/GWT hybrid). Carruthers — dual content via consumer semantics (states acquire higher-order non-conceptual content by being available to a mindreading consumer system); Carruthers has since abandoned higher-order approaches altogether.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Same reductive conflict; mainly worth a paragraph each for completeness and for the GWT bridge (existing global-workspace-theory page).

### The Phenomenological Refusal (Zahavi — the Map's existing ally)
- **Proponents**: Dan Zahavi; broadly the Husserl-Sartre tradition both camps claim as ancestry
- **Core claim**: pre-reflective self-awareness is real and ubiquitous but *not representational* — not a state taking itself as an (even secondary) object. Higher-order and same-order theories alike retain an objectifying model of consciousness and thereby invite regress and miss primitive self-familiarity.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Already the corpus's stance (self-and-self-consciousness page adopts Zahavi). The new article's job is to position self-representationalism *between* Rosenthal and Zahavi and explain why the Map sides with Zahavi while crediting Kriegel's structural analysis.

## Key Debates

### Is self-representation sufficient for phenomenality?
- **Sides**: Kriegel (yes, when non-derivative/specific/essential) vs Levine, Weisberg, Giustina (no — a state's representing itself is "just more representation"/"same old" functional structure; a self-representing zombie state is conceivable)
- **Core disagreement**: whether reflexive representational structure *constitutes* felt presence or merely *models* it.
- **Current state**: Ongoing; the momentum is with acquaintance alternatives (Giustina 2022; Williford's own migration). This is the same fork the Map's higher-order-theories page runs against HOROR — "either the self-directed representation is itself felt (presupposing phenomenality) or it isn't (leaving the hard problem intact)."

### Does dropping the second state actually solve misrepresentation?
- **Sides**: Kriegel/Gennaro (a state can't target a non-existent state if target = itself; conceptual-match requirements block content mismatch) vs Weisberg 2008, Picciuto 2011 (mismatch just moves *inside* the complex: the metapsychological part can still misrepresent the world-directed part)
- **Current state**: Unresolved; standard verdict is that part-whole versions inherit an intra-state analogue of the problem, while strict-identity versions struggle to say how a state represents itself *as* anything without parts.

### Regress
- **Sides**: Aristotle/Brentano/Kriegel (same-order structure *blocks* the HOT regress) vs Williford's own analysis and Zahavi (representational self-directedness regenerates regress at the level of contents or de se structure; only a primitive — acquaintance or pre-reflective givenness — stops it)
- **Current state**: Widely taken to show that the regress-stopping work is done by whatever primitive one posits, not by the same-order architecture as such.

### Taxonomy: third camp or HOT variant?
- **Sides**: Kriegel & Williford 2006 (a distinct alternative to both first-order representationalism and higher-order monitoring) vs Gennaro and the SEP's filing (intrinsicalist versions of the higher-order idea)
- **Current state**: Terminological but substantive underneath — it tracks whether the awareness-conferring component must itself be conscious (Kriegel: yes, peripherally; Gennaro: no, the intrinsic HOT is unconscious). Get this right in the article.
