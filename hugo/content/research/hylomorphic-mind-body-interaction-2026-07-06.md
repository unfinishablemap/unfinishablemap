## Executive Summary

Hylomorphism (Greek *hylē* "matter" + *morphē* "form") is the Aristotelian doctrine that a natural substance is a compound of matter and substantial form, where form is the organizing structure that makes the matter the kind of thing it is. Applied to mind, the soul is treated as the form of the living body rather than as a separate substance, which purports to dissolve the mind-body *interaction problem* by denying that mind and body are two things that need to causally reach across a gap in the first place. The framework has undergone a strong contemporary revival: William Jaworski, Anna Marmodoro, Robert Koons, David Oderberg, Kit Fine, Kathrin Koslicki, and Edward Feser have all developed structure- or form-based accounts, several explicitly aimed at the problem of mental causation. Two distinct families exist: a **non-dualist "structure" hylomorphism** (Jaworski) that treats structure as a real, causally relevant but broadly naturalistic principle, and a **hylomorphic (Thomistic) dualism** (Feser, Oderberg) on which the rational soul is a subsistent form capable of surviving the body. Both reject the Cartesian picture of two complete substances, so the Map's *interactionist substance-leaning* commitments align only partially and require careful positioning.

## Key Sources

### Form vs. Matter (Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy)
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/form-matter/
- **Type**: Encyclopedia
- **Key points**:
  - Aristotle introduces matter and form in the *Physics* to account for change, then extends the hylomorphic framework to substances generally.
  - Soul and body are treated as a special case of form and matter; the essence/form of a human being is the soul.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral/mixed. The framework is anti-reductive (aligns loosely with Dualism) but the standard reading makes the soul inseparable from body, which sits in tension with a substance-leaning interactionism.

### Aristotle's Psychology > A Fundamental Problem about Hylomorphism (SEP)
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/aristotle-psychology/suppl1.html
- **Type**: Encyclopedia (supplement)
- **Key points**:
  - A tension internal to Aristotle: matter is normally *contingently* enformed (bronze can lose its statue-shape and persist), but a human body is *essentially* enformed by its soul—a corpse is only "homonymously" a body.
  - This "fundamental problem" bears directly on whether the soul-body relation can model genuine two-way interaction or collapses into identity.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral. Names a structural difficulty any hylomorphic-interaction account must address.

### William Jaworski, *Structure and the Metaphysics of Mind* (OUP, 2016)
- **URL**: https://philpapers.org/rec/JAWHAT-3 ; review: https://ndpr.nd.edu/reviews/structure-and-the-metaphysics-of-mind-how-hylomorphism-solves-the-mind-body-problem/
- **Type**: Book / academic review
- **Key points**:
  - "Structure" (hylomorphic form) is a basic ontological and explanatory principle: living things are materials *organized* in a way that confers developmental, metabolic, perceptive, and cognitive powers.
  - Solves mental causation via a "dual explanandum" strategy: mental and physical descriptions pick out structured manifestations of powers, so there is no genuine overdetermination.
  - Hylomorphists can accept or reject various definitions of "mental"/"physical"; the framework is deliberately ecumenical.
- **Tenet alignment**: Partially conflicts with Dualism-as-irreducibility-of-the-phenomenal. Critics note Jaworski's emergentism about structure is *not* meant to license robust downward causation, and the dual-explanandum strategy is also available to physicalists—so it may not deliver the strong mind-over-matter causation the Map wants.
- **Quote**: "Hylomorphism is a metaphysical framework that takes structure to be a basic ontological and explanatory principle" and "enables a workable metaphysics of mental causation."

### William Jaworski, "Hylomorphism and Mental Causation" / Brains Blog series
- **URL**: https://philpapers.org/rec/JAWHAM-2 ; https://philosophyofbrains.com/2016/05/12/hylomorphism-and-the-problem-of-mental-causation-part-1.aspx
- **Type**: Paper / blog exposition
- **Key points**:
  - Frames mental causation as the overdetermination worry: actions have mental causes, actions have physical causes, these are distinct, so actions look overdetermined.
  - Hylomorphic reply: mental and physical causes are not two competing sufficient causes but aspects of one structured power-manifestation.
- **Tenet alignment**: Relevant to Bidirectional Interaction; but the "no real overdetermination" move dissolves rather than vindicates a separate mental cause.

### Edward Feser, "Vallicella on hylemorphic dualism" (and related defenses)
- **URL**: http://edwardfeser.blogspot.com/2011/08/vallicella-on-hylemorphic-dualism.html
- **Type**: Philosopher's blog / defense
- **Key points**:
  - Hylemorphic dualism holds the immaterial (form) and material (matter) are *not* two distinct substances sharing only efficient causality; they are co-principles of one substance.
  - Claims an advantage over Cartesian substance dualism precisely on the interaction problem: there is no gap to bridge because mind and body were never two independent things.
  - Aristotelians recognize formal, material, and final causation, not just efficient/mechanistic causation—so the "how does the immaterial push the material?" question is said to rest on a mistaken mechanistic premise.
- **Tenet alignment**: Aligns with Dualism (irreducibility) and offers a *rival* solution to interaction; conflicts with the Map's quantum-interface framing (Tenets 2-3), which presupposes a locatable causal interface rather than dissolving it.

### Thomistic Hylomorphism / hylemorphic dualism (Aquinas, Feser, Oderberg)
- **URL**: https://aquinasonline.com/dualism/ ; https://thomistica.net/contributions/https/thomisticanet/quaestiones-url/hylomorphic-dualism ; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mind%E2%80%93body_dualism
- **Type**: Encyclopedia / tradition exposition
- **Key points**:
  - The soul is the substantial form of the living body—co-extensive with it, not a separate thing.
  - What makes it *dualist*: the rational soul is uniquely **subsistent**—capable of surviving bodily death—because intellectual operations are immaterial.
  - Distinguished sharply from Cartesian dualism: the person is one substance, not a mind piloting a body.
- **Tenet alignment**: Aligns with Dualism and, via subsistence, with the Map's persisting-subject requirement for agent causation. Diverges from the Map on mechanism: no quantum interface, and the immateriality claim is grounded in intellection, not in the explanatory gap or quantum indeterminacy.

### Robert Koons, "Against Emergent Individualism" / emergent hylomorphism
- **URL**: https://www.newdualism.org/papers-Jul2020/Koons-AgainstEmergentIndividualism.pdf ; https://www.researchgate.net/publication/342957364_HYLOMORPHISM_EXPLANATORY_PRACTICE_AND_THE_PROBLEM_OF_MENTAL_CAUSATION
- **Type**: Paper (PDF)
- **Key points**:
  - The collective manifestation of the powers of matter sustains an emergent individual, but that emergent individual exerts **downward causal control** over its matter.
  - Structured wholes essentially and continuously configure the materials that compose them; if constituents lost their powers they could no longer compose the whole's activities.
- **Tenet alignment**: This is the *closest* fit to the Map's Bidirectional Interaction tenet—explicit downward causation from the whole (form) to matter. Still lacks the quantum-minimality specification of Tenet 2.

### Nussbaum & Putnam, "Changing Aristotle's Mind" (in *Essays on Aristotle's De Anima*)
- **URL**: https://philpapers.org/rec/PUTCAM ; https://academic.oup.com/book/32491/chapter/269649237
- **Type**: Book chapter (foundational modern revival text)
- **Key points**:
  - Reads Aristotle as anti-reductive but not anti-materialist: form/organization is key, and perception "cannot be explained from the bottom up" yet *is* realized in material transitions.
  - Historically fused hylomorphism with functionalism ("Aristotle as proto-functionalist")—a reading Putnam himself later retracted.
- **Tenet alignment**: The functionalist reading *conflicts* with Dualism (functionalism is a materialist theory of mind). Useful as the pivot showing why "hylomorphism" ranges from near-physicalist to robustly dualist.
- **Quote**: Aristotle "rejected Democritean reductive materialism and Platonic idealism in favor of a position in which the organization, or form, of things is key to understanding their nature, even when that 'thing' is the soul."

### The History of Hylomorphism: From Aristotle to Descartes (ed., reviewed BMCR & NDPR)
- **URL**: https://bmcr.brynmawr.edu/2025/2025.01.26/ ; https://ndpr.nd.edu/reviews/the-history-of-hylomorphism/
- **Type**: Edited volume / reviews
- **Key points**:
  - Traces the doctrine's development and transformations across antiquity, the medieval Arabic and Latin traditions, and into the early modern period where Descartes' substance dualism displaced it.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral (historical scaffolding).

## Major Positions

### 1. Aristotelian Hylomorphism (classical)
- **Proponents**: Aristotle; interpreters Burnyeat, Nussbaum, Putnam, M. Frede.
- **Core claim**: A living being is one substance = matter organized by substantial form (soul). The soul is the actuality/form of a body having life potentially; it is not a separable thing (except, controversially, the *nous*/active intellect).
- **Key arguments**: The unity of the organism; the homonymy principle (a dead eye is only homonymously an eye); form as the principle of a thing's characteristic powers.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Anti-reductive, so friendly to Dualism's *spirit*, but the strong inseparability reading undercuts the two-relata picture the Map's interactionism assumes. No quantum interface; interaction is "dissolved," not located.

### 2. Structure Hylomorphism (naturalistic, non-Cartesian)
- **Proponents**: William Jaworski; adjacent structural-metaphysics work by Kit Fine, Kathrin Koslicki.
- **Core claim**: Structure/organization is a real, sui generis ontological and explanatory principle that is causally relevant and that individuates living things and their powers.
- **Key arguments**: Dual-explanandum solution to mental causation; multiple-realizability handled by structure; avoids both reductive physicalism and substance dualism.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Weakest fit. Explicitly ecumenical about "mental"/"physical"; critics (e.g., the "Modern Hylomorphism and the Reality and Causal Power of Structure" skeptical line) argue its structure-emergentism does not secure robust downward mental causation—so it does not obviously deliver the irreducible, causally efficacious mind the Map's Dualism + Bidirectional Interaction tenets require.

### 3. Hylomorphic / Thomistic Dualism
- **Proponents**: Aquinas; contemporary: Edward Feser, David Oderberg, J.P. Moreland (critically engaged), Eleonore Stump.
- **Core claim**: The person is one hylomorphic substance, but the rational soul is a *subsistent* form—immaterial in its intellectual operations and able to survive death. Neither Cartesian dualism nor materialism.
- **Key arguments**: Immateriality of concepts/universal thought (intellect has no bodily organ); the interaction problem is a pseudo-problem born of mechanism; the four-cause ontology gives non-mechanistic modes of causation.
- **Relation to site tenets**: **Strongest alignment on irreducibility and on a persisting subject** (supports agent-causation's need for a substance-bearing subject; cf. `where-the-substance-commitment-enters`). **Diverges on mechanism**: grounds immateriality in intellection rather than the explanatory gap, and offers a *rival* dissolution of interaction that competes with the Map's quantum-interface (Tenets 2-3) rather than complementing it.

### 4. Emergent Hylomorphism (powers + downward causation)
- **Proponents**: Robert Koons, Anna Marmodoro (powers/structure), Michele Paolini Paoletti.
- **Core claim**: Substantial forms are emergent individuals sustained by the joint manifestation of the powers of their matter, and they exert downward causal control over that matter.
- **Key arguments**: Powers ontology; continuous configuration of constituents; genuine whole-to-part causation.
- **Relation to site tenets**: **Best structural fit for Bidirectional Interaction** (explicit downward causation). Would still need to be married to Tenet 2's minimal-quantum specification, which hylomorphists do not supply.

## Key Debates

### Is hylomorphism a form of dualism or a third way?
- **Sides**: Feser/Oderberg — it *is* a (non-Cartesian) dualism (subsistent form). Jaworski — it is a distinct naturalistic framework, neither dualism nor physicalism. Nussbaum/Putnam (early) — near-functionalist, effectively materialist.
- **Core disagreement**: Whether "form" is robust enough to be immaterial/subsistent, or is just organization realized in matter.
- **Current state**: Ongoing; the term "hylomorphism" covers a wide spectrum, which is itself a source of confusion in the literature.

### Does hylomorphism actually solve the interaction problem or dissolve the question?
- **Sides**: Defenders — there is no gap because mind and body are co-principles of one substance, and mechanistic causation is not the only mode. Critics — for the *rational/subsistent* soul that survives the body, the interaction problem re-enters through the back door (how does a separable immaterial intellect engage bodily processes?).
- **Core disagreement**: Whether rejecting mechanistic causal closure is legitimate or question-begging.
- **Current state**: Active; central to the Feser-vs-Cartesian-dualist exchanges.

### Does structure hylomorphism secure downward mental causation?
- **Sides**: Jaworski — the dual-explanandum move handles causation without overdetermination. Skeptics — that move is equally available to physicalists and does not establish robust *downward* causation.
- **Core disagreement**: Whether structural emergence is causally load-bearing or explanatorily redundant.
- **Current state**: Unresolved; the live edge of the contemporary debate (Marmodoro/Paolini Paoletti *Synthese* 2021 special issue).

## Potential Article Angles

Based on this research, an article could:

1. **"Hylomorphism as a rival dissolution of the interaction problem"** — Position hylomorphic dualism against the Map's quantum-interface approach. Both reject Cartesian two-substance interaction; the Map *locates* the interface (post-decoherence selection), hylomorphism *dissolves* the demand for one by rejecting mechanistic causal closure. A tenet-aligned article would present hylomorphism fairly, then argue why the Map still needs a locatable interface (Tenets 2-3 require a where/how that hylomorphism declines to give). This is the strongest angle: honest engagement with a serious competitor.

2. **"Substantial form and the persisting subject"** — Connect Thomistic *subsistent form* to the Map's substance-leaning agent-causation commitment (`where-the-substance-commitment-enters`). Hylomorphic dualism independently motivates a persisting, causally-efficacious subject without Cartesian baggage—useful support, with the caveat that its grounding (immateriality of intellection) differs from the Map's (explanatory gap + quantum indeterminacy).

3. **"Downward causation: Koons's emergent hylomorphism and the Bidirectional Interaction tenet"** — Focus narrowly on whether whole-to-part (form-to-matter) causation gives a non-quantum model of Tenet 3, and whether it can be *combined* with minimal-quantum-interaction rather than treated as an alternative.

4. **"The spectrum problem: why 'hylomorphism' is not one theory"** — Map the range from Jaworski's near-physicalist structure to Feser's subsistent-soul dualism, using the Putnam functionalism episode as the cautionary tale. Clarifies which hylomorphisms the Map can borrow from and which it must reject.
