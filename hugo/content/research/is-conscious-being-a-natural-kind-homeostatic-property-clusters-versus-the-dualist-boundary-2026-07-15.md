## Executive Summary

Richard Boyd's homeostatic property cluster (HPC) theory redefines natural kinds without essences: a kind is a family of co-occurring properties held together by an underlying "homeostatic mechanism," with **vague, fuzzy boundaries** and **no individually necessary shared essence**. HPC has become the dominant anti-essentialist account of biological and special-science kinds and has been extended to psychiatric kinds (Kendler, Zachar & Craver 2011, who rename it "mechanistic property clusters"), and refined by Slater (2015, "Stable Property Cluster") and Khalidi (2013).

The Map's tenet-diagnostic question — *could "conscious being" be an HPC kind, or does dualism force a sharp, essence-like joint?* — maps precisely onto an **existing and unresolved debate about whether "phenomenally conscious" is vague or sharp**. Michael Antony (2006), Jonathan Simon (2017), and others defend the "intuition of sharpness" (consciousness is determinately on or off, no borderline cases); Papineau and defenders of "borderline consciousness" (e.g. the 2023 *Philosophical Studies* paper) hold that it is vague and admits degrees. Vagueness/degrees is the natural home of HPC-style and gradualist materialism (cf. Birch, Schnell & Clayton 2020 "dimensions of animal consciousness"); sharpness is what dualism predicts.

**Novelty finding**: Nobody in the retrieved literature has explicitly asked *whether "conscious being" is a Boyd-style HPC kind* and used the answer as a **dualism-vs-materialism diagnostic**. The closest work — Shea (2012) and Bayne & Shea (2020) — argues consciousness *can* be studied as a natural kind, but via "nomological clusters" / natural-kind *methodology*, NOT via Boyd's HPC apparatus, and without taking the dualist sharp-boundary line. So the Map's specific synthesis (Boyd HPC → entails boundary vagueness → collides with the sharpness intuition → dualism supplies a *ground* for sharpness) appears genuinely novel as a framing, even though every component (HPC theory; the sharpness/vagueness debate) is well-established. **This is a synthesis/connection article, not a discovery.**

## Key Sources

### Natural Kinds (Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy)
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/natural-kinds/
- **Type**: Encyclopedia (Bird & Tobin)
- **Key points**:
  - Three families of view: (1) essentialist/microstructural (Kripke–Putnam) — membership fixed by discoverable essences (water = H₂O), demands **sharp** boundaries; (2) Boyd's HPC — clusters sustained by homeostatic mechanisms, **vague** boundaries, no property individually necessary; (3) conventionalist/pluralist (promiscuous realism) — kinds relative to interests.
  - Paraphrased from the entry: HPCs occur when mechanisms cause properties to cluster by making deviations from the cluster unlikely to persist; in such cases the kind's boundaries are vague and none of the properties need be individually necessary for membership.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral framework; the essentialism/HPC contrast is the article's load-bearing axis.

### Boyd, "Realism, Anti-Foundationalism and the Enthusiasm for Natural Kinds" (1991)
- **Type**: Journal article — *Philosophical Studies* 61(1–2):127–148. DOI 10.1007/BF00385837. (Twenty-Ninth Oberlin Colloquium.)
- **Key points**: Introduces HPC. Natural-kind status hinges NOT on mind-independence or demonstrable essence but on whether the kind **supports inductive/explanatory generalization**. This is the founding statement.
- **Tenet alignment**: Conflicts with Tenet-style essentialism about the conscious/non-conscious joint IF applied to consciousness; the "inductive success, not essence" criterion is explicitly anti-essentialist.

### Boyd, "Homeostasis, Species, and Higher Taxa" (1999)
- **Type**: Book chapter — in R. A. Wilson (ed.), *Species: New Interdisciplinary Essays*, Cambridge, MA: MIT Press, pp. 141–185.
- **Key points**: Species are the paradigm HPC kind — a homeostatically sustained clustering of properties/relations, vague at the edges, no essence. Extends HPC as the anti-essentialist account of biological taxa.

### Kendler, Zachar & Craver, "What kinds of things are psychiatric disorders?" (2011)
- **Type**: Journal article — *Psychological Medicine* 41(6):1143–1150.
- **Key points**: Adapt Boyd's HPC to psychiatry but **rename it "mechanistic property cluster" (MPC)** — dropping "homeostatic" because it wrongly implied physiological equilibrium; MPCs are sustained by heterogeneous, variable causal mechanisms. Conclude that MPC "offers the most promising answer to the question of what kinds of things psychiatric disorders are," while cautioning it may be "too early" to force classification into HPC kinds.
- **Tenet alignment**: Illustrates the HPC template applied to a *mental* domain — the direct precedent for asking the same of "conscious being." Mental kinds treated as cluster-y, vague, mechanism-sustained.

### Slater, "Natural Kindness" (2015)
- **Type**: Journal article — *British Journal for the Philosophy of Science* 66(2). Won the 2015 BSPS Karl Popper Prize.
- **Key points**: Argues HPC should drop its commitment to *causal homeostatic mechanisms* and keep only the **cohesiveness/stability** they provide → the "Stable Property Cluster" (SPC) account. Liberalizes cluster theory (properties may be held by multiple mechanisms or merely reliably correlated).
- **Relevance**: Shows the cluster-kind program is a live, evolving research area, not a fixed target.

### Khalidi, *Natural Categories and Human Kinds* (2013)
- **Type**: Book — Cambridge University Press.
- **Key points**: Realist but liberal/pluralist account; kinds as nodes in causal networks; permits **crosscutting** categories (a thing can belong to overlapping natural kinds). Earlier "Natural Kinds and Crosscutting Categories," *Journal of Philosophy* 1998.
- **Relevance**: The main non-essentialist alternative to strict HPC; relevant if the Map wants to distinguish "cluster" from "crosscutting causal-node" readings.

### Shea, "Methodological Encounters with the Phenomenal Kind" (2012)
- **Type**: Journal article — *Philosophy and Phenomenological Research* 84(2):307–344.
- **Key points**: Argues phenomenal consciousness **can and should be studied as a natural kind**, using **"nomological clusters"** (properties clustering under natural laws/mechanisms) — a natural-kind methodology closely related to but NOT labelled HPC. Natural kinds need no intrinsic essence, only reliable inductive support.
- **Novelty bearing**: This is the closest existing work to "consciousness as a cluster kind." It reaches a materialist-friendly, essence-free verdict and does NOT engage the dualist sharp-boundary alternative.

### Bayne & Shea, "Consciousness, Concepts, and Natural Kinds" (2020)
- **Type**: Journal article — *Philosophical Topics* 48(1):65–84.
- **Key points**: The "natural-kind strategy" is among the most promising routes to *validating measures of consciousness* in hard cases (infants, brain damage, animals, AI). Distinguishes the **phenomenal concept** of consciousness from a **natural-kind concept** of it; the science needs the latter.
- **Novelty bearing**: Confirms consciousness-as-natural-kind is an active project, but again framed methodologically (measure validation), not as an HPC-vs-dualist-joint metaphysical fork.

### Antony, "Vagueness and the Metaphysics of Consciousness" (2006)
- **Type**: Journal article — *Philosophical Studies* 128(3):515–538 (DOI 10.1007/s11098-004-7488-8).
- **Key points**: Names the **"intuition of sharpness"**: many find CONSCIOUS STATE / CONSCIOUS CREATURE to be **sharp, admitting no borderline cases**. Argues that IF the concept is sharp and correct, then common versions of **identity theory, functionalism, AND dualism** are false, because each would make consciousness supervene on a vague physical base. (See also Antony, "Are Our Concepts CONSCIOUS STATE and CONSCIOUS CREATURE Vague?")
- **CALIBRATION FLAG**: Antony lists *dualism* among the theories threatened by sharpness. The Map cannot glibly say "sharpness → dualism." The Map's line must be narrower: *substance/interactionist dualism with a discrete (quantum-interface) locus of interaction* can ground a sharp joint in a way property-supervenience theories cannot. Cite Antony as the source of the sharpness argument, and treat his "dualism too is threatened" claim as an objection the Map must answer, not evidence for the Map.

### Simon, "Vagueness and Zombies: Why 'Phenomenally Conscious' Has No Borderline Cases" (2017)
- **Type**: Journal article — *Philosophical Studies* 174 (DOI 10.1007/s11098-016-0790-4). (Dissertation: "The Sharp Contour of Consciousness.")
- **Key points**: Defends sharpness via the **Positive Characterization Thesis** — every genuine borderline case admits a positive characterization; but the conceivability of zombies shows there can be **no positive characterization of a borderline case of "phenomenally conscious"** → no borderline cases → the phenomenal sorites fails.
- **Relevance**: The strongest recent *pro-sharpness* argument; independent of dualism but congenial to it (leans on zombie conceivability, which the Map already engages).

### Papineau — vagueness of phenomenal concepts
- **Type**: Discussed via Antony's replies ("Papineau on the Vagueness of Phenomenal Concepts," *dialectica*) and Papineau, *Thinking about Consciousness* (2002).
- **Key points**: Phenomenal concepts (incl. consciousness-as-such) ARE vague — insufficiently specified to pick out exactly one property; indeterminacy is about *which* property is referred to, not where to cut a continuum. The materialist-friendly *pro-vagueness* pole.

### Birch, Schnell & Clayton, "Dimensions of Animal Consciousness" (2020)
- **Type**: Journal article — *Trends in Cognitive Sciences* 24(10):789–801.
- **Key points**: Rejects a single more/less-conscious scale for a **multidimensional profile** (perceptual richness, evaluative richness, integration at a time, integration across time, self-consciousness). Consciousness varies **continuously along many axes**.
- **Relevance**: The empirical/theoretical face of *gradualism* — the practising-science version of "consciousness is cluster-y and admits degrees." A natural ally of an HPC reading and a live challenge to strict sharpness (though note: gradualism about *contents/richness* is distinct from vagueness about the *on/off* fact of any experience at all — a distinction the article must preserve).

### Additional / peripheral

## Major Positions

### HPC / cluster-kind reading of "conscious being" (materialist-congenial)
- **Proponents (of the components)**: Boyd (HPC); Shea, Bayne & Shea (consciousness as natural kind, methodologically); Kendler et al. (mental kinds as MPC); Birch et al. (dimensions/gradualism).
- **Core claim**: "Conscious being" is a cluster of co-occurring, mechanism-sustained capacities (report, global broadcast, valence, integration, self-modeling) with **vague edges and marginal cases**; no single essence; kind-hood earned by inductive/explanatory yield.
- **Fit with metaphysics**: Sits naturally with materialist **gradualism** — degrees and borderline cases are expected, even welcome.
- **Relation to Map tenets**: Conflicts with the dualist prediction of a sharp joint. Not refuted by anything the Map holds; it is the live rival.

### Sharp-boundary / essence-like reading (dualist-congenial)
- **Proponents (of the components)**: Antony, Simon (sharpness); Descartes-style substance dualism generally.
- **Core claim**: For any creature at any time it is **determinately** true or false that there is something it is like to be it — the "light" is on or off. This is an essence-like monadic joint, not a fuzzy cluster.
- **Fit with metaphysics**: Sits naturally with **interactionist dualism**: if consciousness makes a discrete non-physical difference (minimal quantum interaction), there is a fact of the matter about whether that difference is being made — a sharp joint located at the interaction interface.
- **Relation to Map tenets**: Coheres with Tenets 1–3 (dualism, minimal quantum interaction, bidirectional interaction). The sharp boundary **follows FROM** the Map's dualism; the Map does not claim HPC-failure *proves* dualism.

### Conventionalist / pluralist reading
- **Proponents**: strong conventionalists; Khalidi (liberal realism, crosscutting kinds); promiscuous realists.
- **Core claim**: "Conscious being" is a category carved to interests (moral, scientific, legal); asking for its mind-independent joint is a category error.
- **Relation to Map tenets**: Conflicts with the Map's realism about the conscious/non-conscious distinction; worth engaging as the deflationary option.

## Key Debates

### Is "phenomenally conscious" vague or sharp?
- **Sides**: Antony (2006), Simon (2017) — sharp, no borderline cases; the phenomenal sorites fails. vs. Papineau, "borderline consciousness" (2023) — vague, admits indeterminate cases.
- **Core disagreement**: Whether the *fact of any experience at all* (not richness of contents) can be indeterminate.
- **Current state**: **Unresolved and active.** This debate IS the Map's tenet-diagnostic in disguise. The Map's contribution is to route it through Boyd HPC: HPC kinds are constitutively boundary-vague, so classifying "conscious being" as HPC *presupposes* the vagueness side; the sharpness side implies "conscious being" is NOT an HPC kind but something essence-like.

### Can consciousness be a natural kind at all?
- **Sides**: Shea, Bayne & Shea — yes, methodologically (nomological cluster). vs. eliminativists (Machery on psychological kinds; "no science of consciousness" arguments) — no.
- **Current state**: Ongoing; note nobody in this camp frames it as HPC-vs-dualist-joint.

## Potential Article Angles

The research→expand chain may later create a `concepts/` article. Recommended framing:

1. **Primary angle (tenet-diagnostic, novel synthesis)**: "Is conscious being a natural kind?" Present Boyd HPC honestly as the leading essence-free account of special-science kinds; show that HPC entails constitutive **boundary vagueness**; observe that this collides head-on with the well-established **sharpness intuition** (Antony, Simon); then argue that the Map's **dualism supplies a metaphysical ground** for the sharp joint (interaction interface) that property-supervenience materialism cannot. Keep the materialist HPC/gradualist reading fully live as the rival.
2. **Alternative angle**: Treat it as a decision-relevant `apex applied` node — moral-status and AI-consciousness stakes turn on whether consciousness is a fuzzy cluster (degrees → graded moral status) or a sharp joint (all-or-nothing → discrete moral threshold).
3. **Positions-register candidate**: A position entry — "The conscious/non-conscious distinction is a sharp, mind-independent joint (essence-like), not a homeostatic property cluster" — confidence-banded, dependent on the dualism + minimal-interaction tenets.

### CALIBRATION GUARDRAILS (carry into expand-topic)
- **Do NOT** let "dualism predicts a sharp boundary" slide into "HPC-failure proves dualism." The valid claim is *conditional and coherentist*: IF the Map's dualism, THEN a sharp joint follows (tenet-coherent). HPC's failure to fit consciousness would be *consistent with* dualism, not a proof of it. (cf. memory: expand-topic-lead-overclaims-phenomenal-absence.)
- **Answer Antony's objection**: he argues sharpness threatens *dualism too*. The Map must show why *interactionist/substance* dualism with a discrete interaction locus escapes the supervenience-on-a-vague-base problem that sinks property dualism and functionalism. This is the article's real burden.
- **Preserve the distinction** between (a) vagueness about *whether there is any experience at all* (the on/off fact — where sharpness is defended) and (b) gradation in the *richness/contents* of experience (Birch et al.'s dimensions — compatible with a sharp on/off). Conflating them would misrepresent both camps.
- Materialists **can** consistently hold consciousness is an HPC kind. Present that as a respectable live option, not a straw man.
