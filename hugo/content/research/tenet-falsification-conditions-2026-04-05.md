# Research: Falsification Conditions and Testable Predictions for the Five Tenets

**Date**: 2026-04-05
**Search queries used**: "falsifiability of dualism consciousness testable predictions", "quantum consciousness testable predictions Penrose Hameroff Orch OR falsification experiments", "interactionist dualism empirical test causal closure falsification mental causation", "many worlds interpretation falsification test experiments", "Occam's razor limits falsifiability simplicity epistemology", "Hameroff Penrose Orch OR 20 testable predictions confirmed falsified 2024 2025", "consciousness energy conservation violation neuroscience experiment", "quantum biology falsification decoherence warm biological systems microtubule coherence 2024 2025", "collapse vs no-collapse quantum mechanics experimental test 2024 2025", "Marco Masi obsession with Occam's razor parsimony limits"

## Executive Summary

Each of the Map's five tenets occupies a different position on the falsifiability spectrum. The quantum interaction and collapse tenets (Tenets 2, 3, 4) generate the most concrete experimental predictions, while the core dualism claim (Tenet 1) and the epistemological claim about parsimony (Tenet 5) function more as framework commitments testable through cumulative evidence rather than single decisive experiments. Importantly, the major rival theories of consciousness (IIT, Global Workspace) face similar falsifiability challenges, and a 2021 PMC paper argues that standard falsification criteria apply poorly to consciousness theories generally. The Map should engage honestly with these asymmetries rather than overclaiming testability.

## Key Sources

### Falsification and Consciousness (Kleiner 2021)
- **URL**: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8052953/
- **Type**: Journal article (PMC)
- **Key points**:
  - Falsification of consciousness theories requires comparing predicted experience with inferred experience via report or behavior
  - If inference and prediction are independent, any minimally informative theory is automatically falsified
  - This structural problem affects all theories of consciousness, not just dualism
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral — applies equally to materialist and dualist theories
- **Quote**: "Testing requires comparing a theory's predicted experience with an inferred experience using report or behavior."

### Orch OR as Falsifiable Theory (Hameroff 2020)
- **URL**: https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/17588928.2020.1839037
- **Type**: Journal article (Cognitive Neuroscience)
- **Key points**:
  - Hameroff argues Orch OR is "the most complete, and most easily falsifiable theory of consciousness"
  - 20 testable predictions published in 1998; as of 2014 review, 6 confirmed, 0 refuted
  - Key falsification condition: if quantum interference in microtubules cannot be demonstrated, or if demonstrated proves insensitive to anesthesia, Orch OR is falsified
- **Tenet alignment**: Directly supports Tenets 2 and 3 (quantum interaction, bidirectional causation)

### Gran Sasso Experiment on Gravity-Related Collapse (Donadi et al. 2021)
- **URL**: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41567-020-1008-4
- **Type**: Experimental paper (Nature Physics)
- **Key points**:
  - Tested the Diosi-Penrose model of gravity-related wavefunction collapse underground to reduce cosmic ray interference
  - Found no spontaneous radiation predicted by Diosi's version of the model
  - Rules out the natural parameter-free version of the Diosi-Penrose model
  - Penrose's original model (without specific dynamical predictions about radiation) is not directly ruled out
- **Tenet alignment**: Challenges one specific mechanism for Tenet 2, but does not falsify the broader commitment
- **Quote**: "The simplest version of the gravity-related collapse model is highly implausible."

### Dualism: An Empirical Test? (King)
- **URL**: https://www.newdualism.org/papers/P.King/dualism.html
- **Type**: Philosophy paper
- **Key points**:
  - Proposes that interactionist dualism could in principle be empirically tested
  - If energy conservation violations or causal closure gaps were detected in neural systems, this would constitute empirical evidence for dualism
  - No such violations have been detected, but absence of evidence is not evidence of absence at current precision
- **Tenet alignment**: Supports Tenet 3 by showing empirical testability is at least conceivable

### Conservation Laws and Philosophy of Mind (Gamez 2022)
- **URL**: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9038821/
- **Type**: Journal article (PMC)
- **Key points**:
  - Conservation laws trivially nearly entail causal closure of the physical
  - Soul-to-body causation should violate conservation laws per Noether's theorem
  - Quantum interactionist dualism sidesteps this by acting at indeterminacies (no energy injection required)
  - A semiclassical framework using quantum sum-over-histories has been proposed as a mechanism
- **Tenet alignment**: Directly engages Tenets 2 and 3; the Map's "no energy injection" commitment is precisely the strategy identified here

### Adversarial Collaboration Testing IIT vs GNWT (2025)
- **URL**: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-025-08888-1
- **Type**: Experimental paper (Nature, April 2025)
- **Key points**:
  - First major adversarial collaboration testing competing consciousness theories
  - IIT passed 2 of 3 pre-registered predictions; GNWT passed 0
  - Neither theory fully confirmed or falsified
  - Demonstrates that consciousness theories can be subjected to empirical testing
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral — shows that even materialist consciousness theories face falsification challenges comparable to dualism

### Experimental Blueprint: Decoherence vs Objective Collapse (2025)
- **URL**: https://arxiv.org/html/2512.02838
- **Type**: Preprint (arXiv)
- **Key points**:
  - Proposes levitated-optomechanical platform to distinguish decoherence from objective collapse
  - Uses Bayesian inference to discriminate collapse-induced excess decoherence from environmental noise
  - If successful, could directly test whether objective collapse is real
- **Tenet alignment**: Directly relevant to Tenet 4 (No Many Worlds) — if objective collapse is confirmed, MWI is disfavored

## Falsification Analysis by Tenet

### Tenet 1: Dualism (Consciousness is irreducible)

**Type of claim**: Metaphysical framework commitment
**Falsifiability status**: Not directly falsifiable by a single experiment, but subject to cumulative evidential pressure

**What would count as falsification**:
- A complete, verified physical explanation of subjective experience that closes the explanatory gap — not merely neural correlates of consciousness (which are compatible with dualism), but a demonstration that physical description *entails* phenomenal experience
- Successful construction of an artificial system that demonstrably has subjective experience, with a physical explanation of *why* it does — though verifying this faces the other-minds problem
- A principled resolution of the knowledge argument (Mary's Room) showing that phenomenal knowledge *is* physical knowledge in a rigorous sense

**What would NOT count**:
- Finding more neural correlates of consciousness (compatible with both dualism and physicalism)
- Demonstrating that brain damage affects consciousness (dualism already expects this via the interaction interface)
- Showing that anesthesia eliminates consciousness (expected under interactionist dualism)

**Current status**: No path to decisive falsification exists. The IIT vs GNWT adversarial collaboration (Nature, 2025) shows that even physicalist consciousness theories face similar falsification difficulties. Kleiner (2021) argues the problem is structural: falsifying any consciousness theory requires comparing predicted experience with behaviorally inferred experience, which introduces unavoidable circularity.

**Honest assessment**: Dualism is a framework commitment, not a scientific hypothesis in the Popperian sense. Its strength lies in the persistent failure of physicalist alternatives to close the explanatory gap, rather than in positive predictions it makes. The Map should acknowledge this asymmetry directly.

### Tenet 2: Minimal Quantum Interaction

**Type of claim**: Mechanism hypothesis with empirical content
**Falsifiability status**: The most empirically tractable tenet — generates specific, testable predictions

**What would count as falsification**:
- **Decisive**: Demonstration that quantum coherence is physically impossible in any biological neural system, under any conditions, at any timescale relevant to neural processing
- **Strong**: Falsification of all proposed mechanism candidates (Orch OR, Stapp's Zeno model, and any future proposals) leaving no viable mechanism for quantum-level mind-matter interaction
- **Moderate**: Proof that quantum indeterminacy plays no functional role in neural computation — that classical noise fully accounts for all neural stochasticity

**Specific experimental predictions (from Orch OR)**:
1. Quantum coherence in microtubules should be demonstrable at biologically relevant temperatures
2. Such coherence should be sensitive to anesthetic gases (which are known to bind in hydrophobic pockets of tubulin)
3. Microtubule dynamics should show signatures distinguishable from classical thermal noise

**Current experimental status**:
- Gran Sasso experiment (2021): Rules out the Diosi-Penrose model's simplest form but not Penrose's original collapse proposal
- Microtubule coherence: 2024-2025 theoretical work suggests collective quantum states (superradiant excitonic states) may persist at room temperature. Energy migration of ~6.6 nm demonstrated in microtubules, reduced by anesthetics
- Epothilone B experiment (2024): Rats given microtubule-binding drug took longer to lose consciousness under anesthesia — consistent with but not proof of Orch OR
- 2025 blueprint for distinguishing decoherence from objective collapse using levitated nanoparticles (arXiv:2512.02838)

**Honest assessment**: This tenet has the most empirical traction. The 20 predictions framework from Orch OR, with 6 confirmed and 0 refuted as of 2014, provides a concrete falsification program. However, the tenet is broader than Orch OR — it commits only to *some* quantum-level interface, not to a specific mechanism. This breadth makes the tenet harder to falsify than any single mechanism.

### Tenet 3: Bidirectional Interaction

**Type of claim**: Causal-structural claim with indirect empirical implications
**Falsifiability status**: Moderate — some predictions testable, but core claim is difficult to isolate

**What would count as falsification**:
- **Decisive**: Proof that the physical world is strictly causally closed at all levels, including quantum — that there is literally no room for non-physical influence on any physical outcome
- **Strong**: Demonstration that all human cognitive capacities attributed to consciousness (logical reasoning, counterfactual thinking, cumulative culture) can be fully replicated by unconscious processing, refuting the functional role argument
- **Moderate**: Comprehensive Libet-style experiments showing that conscious awareness *never* precedes neural commitment in any decision type

**Specific predictions**:
1. Cognitive tasks requiring conscious processing (logical reasoning, counterfactual thinking) should be specifically impaired by conscious-process interference — confirmed by Lieberman et al. (2008)
2. There should exist cognitive capacities in humans that have no unconscious analogue and no parallel in non-conscious animals — baseline cognition hypothesis provides evidence
3. If consciousness biases quantum outcomes, extremely precise measurements of neural quantum states might reveal non-random patterns inconsistent with standard quantum predictions — beyond current experimental precision

**Conservation of energy challenge**:
- Noether's theorem implies conservation laws entail causal closure (Gamez 2022)
- The Map's "no energy injection" response is the standard move: consciousness biases outcome selection at quantum indeterminacies without adding or removing energy
- This is consistent with quantum mechanics, where measurement outcome selection does not violate conservation laws

**Honest assessment**: The self-undermining argument against epiphenomenalism (if consciousness is inert, our reports about it are accidentally right at best) remains the strongest philosophical support. Empirical support is indirect. The tenet predicts a functional role for consciousness, but establishing that the functional role requires *non-physical* causation rather than complex physical processing is extremely difficult.

### Tenet 4: No Many Worlds

**Type of claim**: Interpretation choice with potential empirical content
**Falsifiability status**: Historically considered unfalsifiable, but emerging experimental proposals

**What would count as falsification**:
- **Decisive**: Experimental demonstration of quantum interference between macroscopic branches (Deutsch's 1985 Wigner's friend variant) — would confirm that all branches persist
- **Strong**: Detection of inter-branch communication or effects (currently beyond experimental capability)
- **Moderate**: Demonstration that objective collapse models are all ruled out, leaving MWI as the only viable interpretation

**What would SUPPORT No Many Worlds**:
- Experimental confirmation of objective collapse (any version) — directly supports collapse-based interpretations over MWI
- The levitated-optomechanical experiments proposed in 2025 could distinguish decoherence (compatible with MWI) from genuine collapse (incompatible with MWI)
- Detection of collapse-induced excess decoherence beyond environmental noise baselines

**Current experimental status**:
- Deutsch's 1985 proposal requires placing macroscopic objects in coherent superposition — beyond current technology
- 2023 arXiv paper (2303.08820) proposes quantum optics experiments testing how conscious experience traverses the multiverse — novel but untested
- Gran Sasso experiment (2021) constrains specific collapse models but does not settle the collapse vs. no-collapse question generally
- 2025 experimental blueprints (arXiv:2512.02838) offer the most promising near-term path to testing collapse reality

**Honest assessment**: The deepest challenge here is that MWI and collapse interpretations make identical predictions for all currently feasible experiments. The Map's rejection of MWI is motivated primarily by philosophical concerns (indexical identity, ontological profligacy) rather than empirical evidence. This is a legitimate philosophical stance but should not be presented as empirically testable in the near term. The collapse vs. no-collapse question may become experimentally accessible within decades as quantum technology advances.

### Tenet 5: Occam's Razor Has Limits

**Type of claim**: Epistemological/methodological thesis
**Falsifiability status**: Not a scientific claim — an epistemological position about scientific methodology itself

**What would count as falsification**:
- **In principle**: A rigorous proof that parsimony reliably tracks truth across domains — that simpler theories are more likely to be correct. This would require establishing a deep metaphysical connection between simplicity and reality
- **Historical evidence against the tenet**: If the history of science showed that simpler theories consistently won over more complex rivals, with no significant exceptions. (The record shows the opposite — see historical counterexamples in the tenets page)

**What would NOT count**:
- Individual cases where parsimony succeeded (the tenet does not deny that parsimony sometimes works)
- Mathematical demonstrations that simpler models generalize better (Bayesian model selection, MDL) — these are about prediction, not truth

**Supporting evidence**:
- Popper himself connected simplicity to falsifiability, not truth — simpler theories are easier to test, not more likely correct
- Masi (PhilArchive) documents cases where dogmatic parsimony obstructed progress in evolutionary biology
- PNAS (2024) paper on model parsimony acknowledges that parsimony's edge is context-dependent
- Historical counterexamples (heliocentrism, atomic theory, QM, GR) constitute positive evidence for the tenet

**Honest assessment**: This tenet is meta-scientific rather than scientific. It cannot be falsified by experiment because it makes claims about the reliability of a methodological principle. Its strength is cumulative historical evidence. The Map should present it as what it is: a well-evidenced epistemological position that removes a common objection to the other four tenets, rather than a scientific hypothesis.

## Key Debates

### Can Consciousness Theories Be Falsified at All?
- **Sides**: Kleiner (2021) argues structural problems make all consciousness theories difficult to falsify; Hameroff (2020) argues Orch OR is specifically designed to be falsifiable; 124 scholars (2023) signed a letter calling IIT unfalsifiable pseudoscience
- **Core disagreement**: Whether behavioral/neural inference can ever reliably access subjective experience for comparison with theory predictions
- **Current state**: The IIT vs GNWT adversarial collaboration (Nature, 2025) demonstrates that some empirical traction is possible, but neither theory was decisively confirmed or falsified

### Quantum Biology: Real or Impossible?
- **Sides**: Tegmark (decoherence too fast), Hameroff/Hagan (corrected timescales are viable), Fisher (quantum cognition via Posner molecules)
- **Core disagreement**: Whether quantum coherence can survive in warm, wet biological conditions
- **Current state**: Ongoing. Avian magnetoreception provides proof-of-concept for biological quantum effects. Microtubule experiments show suggestive but not definitive results

### Causal Closure: Empirical Fact or Philosophical Assumption?
- **Sides**: Kim, Papineau (causal closure is well-established); Lowe, Meixner (closure is assumed, not proven, especially at quantum level)
- **Core disagreement**: Whether physics guarantees that every physical event has a sufficient physical cause, particularly at quantum indeterminacies
- **Current state**: Quantum mechanics itself does not enforce causal closure at the level of individual measurement outcomes — this is the gap the Map's framework exploits

## Gaps in Research

- No comprehensive philosophical treatment of falsification conditions specifically for interactionist dualism as a system (most work addresses specific mechanisms like Orch OR rather than the framework commitment)
- The connection between quantum biology results (avian magnetoreception, photosynthesis coherence) and neural quantum effects remains speculative — no one has demonstrated quantum coherence in actual mammalian neurons
- Stapp's quantum Zeno approach has fewer concrete testable predictions than Orch OR and has received less experimental attention
- No adversarial collaboration has tested dualism vs physicalism directly (the IIT/GNWT test was between two physicalist-compatible theories)
- The epistemological status of Tenet 5 (parsimony limits) has not been subjected to formal Bayesian analysis — is there a way to quantify when parsimony is unreliable?
