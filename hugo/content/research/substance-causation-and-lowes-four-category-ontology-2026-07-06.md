## Executive Summary

E. J. Lowe (1950–2014) built a systematic Aristotelian-inflected metaphysics — the four-category ontology — on which the primary causal relata are **substances** (individual objects), not events. His **substance causation** account holds that an agent qua persisting substance is the cause of its actions, exercising **two-way powers** (notably volition, a "spontaneous" power exercisable in light of reason). This is paired with **Non-Cartesian Substance Dualism (NCSD)**: the self/person is a distinct substance from its organic body but is not separable/disembodiable, and bears both mental and physical properties. Crucially, Lowe offers a model of mental causation on which the mental cause does *not* compete with neural event-causation for the same effect and adds *no energy* to the brain — mental **fact-causation** determines *what kind* of movement occurs while event-causation handles the *specific* movement — so a dualist can claim genuine mental causation **without positing a causal gap in physics**. This makes Lowe a near-ideal metaphysical anchor for the Map's Tenet 1 (Dualism) and Tenet 3 (Bidirectional Interaction), while its "no-gap" move sits in productive tension with the Map's Tenet 2 (Minimal Quantum Interaction), which *does* locate the interaction at indeterministic quantum junctures.

## Key Sources

### The Four-Category Ontology: A Metaphysical Foundation for Natural Science (E. J. Lowe)
- **URL**: https://global.oup.com/academic/product/the-four-category-ontology-9780199254392 ; https://ndpr.nd.edu/reviews/the-four-category-ontology-a-metaphysical-foundation-for-natural-science/
- **Type**: Book (Oxford University Press, hardcover 2006, ISBN 9780199254392; paperback 9780199229819)
- **Key points**:
  - Two cross-cutting distinctions — particular/universal and substantial/non-substantial — generate the four categories.
  - The four categories: **objects** (substantial particulars), **kinds** (substantial universals), **attributes** (non-substantial universals), **modes / tropes** (non-substantial particulars).
  - Formal ontological relations tie them: **instantiation** (universal→particular) and **characterisation** (substantial↔non-substantial). The grid is sometimes called the "ontological square," with Aristotle's *Categories* as ancestor.
  - Claims metaphysics is a foundation for natural science: laws are relations between kinds and attributes; dispositions/powers fall out of the grid.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral-to-supportive scaffolding; the ontology itself is not dualist, but it makes room for substances with irreducible causal powers.
- **Citation status**: TITLE + venue (OUP 2006) verified at publisher of record and corroborated by NDPR review.

### Personal Agency: The Metaphysics of Mind and Action (E. J. Lowe)
- **URL**: https://global.oup.com/academic/product/personal-agency-9780199217144 ; https://ndpr.nd.edu/reviews/personal-agency-the-metaphysics-of-mind-and-action/
- **Type**: Book (Oxford University Press, hardcover 2008, ISBN 9780199217144; paperback 2010, ISBN 9780199592500)
- **Key points**:
  - The most systematic contemporary defence of **substance causation**; "strictly speaking there is no such thing as event causation" — only agents (animate and inanimate) cause.
  - **Agent causation is a species of substance causation**, not a sui generis add-on; human agency is distinctive only in its rational features.
  - **Volitions** are basic mental actions — "spontaneous exercises of the will," a **two-way power** rational agents freely exercise in light of reason. Free agents are "unmoved movers, initiators of new causal chains."
  - Contains the **fact-causation vs event-causation** model of mental causation (a decision is causally responsible for the *fact that a movement of a certain kind* occurs; neural events cause the specific movement).
  - Includes the **Unity Argument** for NCSD: mental states require a unified subject the body (a mereological sum of replaceable parts) cannot provide.
- **Tenet alignment**: Strongly supportive of Tenets 1 & 3; the two-way power is a direct metaphysical model of libertarian agency.
- **Citation status**: TITLE + venue (OUP 2008) verified at publisher of record; NDPR review confirms.

### "Substance Causation, Powers, and Human Agency" (E. J. Lowe)
- **URL**: https://academic.oup.com/book/3104/chapter-abstract/143918088 ; https://global.oup.com/academic/product/mental-causation-and-ontology-9780199603770
- **Type**: Book chapter — Chapter 6 (pp. 153–172) in *Mental Causation and Ontology*, eds. S. C. Gibb, E. J. Lowe & R. D. Ingthorsson (Oxford University Press, 2013)
- **Key points**:
  - A theory of substance causation grounded in **powers and liabilities**: a match has the *power* to ignite, wood has the *liability* to burn; causation is the manifestation of a power meeting a reciprocal liability.
  - The human **will is a "spontaneous" power** that can be exercised rationally — a compact restatement of the *Personal Agency* thesis.
- **Tenet alignment**: Supportive; powers/liabilities give a mechanism-language for "consciousness selecting among physical possibilities."
- **Citation status**: TITLE + venue + chapter/pages verified via Oxford Academic and OUP volume page.

### "Non-Cartesian Substance Dualism and the Problem of Mental Causation" (E. J. Lowe)
- **URL**: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10670-006-9012-3 (DOI 10.1007/s10670-006-9012-3)
- **Type**: Journal article — *Erkenntnis* 65(1): 5–23 (2006)
- **Key points**:
  - NCSD is **better placed than Cartesian dualism or standard physicalism** to explain the *possibility* of mental causation.
  - Proposes a model of mental causation **consistent with what is known about the CNS/brain** — no rewiring of neuroscience required.
  - The **entanglement argument**: neural causes of several parallel bodily movements can be causally "entangled" with one another, but their mental causes are not so entangled — therefore mental causes cannot be identical to neural causes (a non-identity / anti-reduction argument).
- **Tenet alignment**: Strongly supportive of Tenets 1 & 3.
- **Citation status**: TITLE + venue + volume/pages (Erkenntnis 65, 5–23, 2006) verified at Springer (publisher of record).

## Major Positions

### Substance Causation (Lowe's positive view)
- **Proponents**: E. J. Lowe; sympathetic development by Sophie Gibb, Michele Paolini Paoletti.
- **Core claim**: The fundamental causal relata are substances, not events. "X caused E" is grounded in *a substance exercising a power*. Event-causation is at best derivative or (Lowe's stronger claim) a façon de parler.
- **Key arguments**: Powers/liabilities ontology; the regress and individuation problems for pure event-causation; agent causation needs no special metaphysics if *all* causation is substance causation.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Direct grounding for **Tenet 3 (Bidirectional Interaction)** — the conscious subject is a genuine cause, not an epiphenomenal rider. Grounds the Map's recurring "agent qua substance" phrasing (`where-the-substance-commitment-enters`, `agent-causation`).

### Non-Cartesian Substance Dualism (NCSD)
- **Proponents**: Lowe; related: Hoffman, some hylomorphists.
- **Core claim**: The person/self is a distinct substance from the organic body (not identical to it or any part), yet is **necessarily embodied** — no Cartesian disembodied res cogitans. The self bears both mental and physical properties.
- **Key arguments**: Unity Argument (a subject of experience must be simple/unified in a way the body's replaceable-part mereology is not); the entanglement argument for mental/neural non-identity.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Supports **Tenet 1 (Dualism)** with a *substance* reading while dodging Cartesian interaction embarrassments. Note: the Map's Dualism tenet is officially neutral between substance and property dualism (`substance-property-dualism`, `where-the-substance-commitment-enters`), so a Lowe article must present NCSD as *a* congenial option, not the Map's mandatory commitment.

### The Fact-Causation Model of Mental Causation
- **Proponents**: Lowe.
- **Core claim**: A decision/volition causes the **fact that a movement of a certain kind occurs**; the **specific** neural-muscular event is caused by neural events. The two do not compete for the same effect, so there is no overdetermination and **no causal gap in the physical chain**.
- **Key arguments**: Distinct causal *relata* (facts vs events) → distinct causal *roles* → exclusion premise ("same effect, two sufficient causes") is not triggered.
- **Relation to site tenets**: This is the crux for the Map. It shows a dualist can have mental causation *without* an energetic intervention — which **contrasts** with the Map's **Tenet 2 (Minimal Quantum Interaction)**, on which consciousness *does* make a (minimal) physical difference at quantum-indeterministic junctures. The article should stage this as a genuine fork: Lowe buys closure-compatibility by making the mental cause structuring rather than energetic; the Map instead accepts a minimal physical difference located where physics is already indeterministic. Both aim to avoid a crude "gap"; they differ on whether the mental makes *any* difference to the physical event-chain.

## Key Debates

### The Exclusion Problem
- **Sides**: Kim (physicalist, closure + exclusion → no irreducible mental cause) vs Lowe (dualist).
- **Core disagreement**: Whether a distinct mental cause is "excluded" by the sufficient physical cause. Lowe denies the shared premise that mental and physical causes target *the same* effect under *the same* causal relation.
- **Current state**: Ongoing. Lowe's non-competition reply is influential but contested (Kistler; Gibb's own refinements). Critics ask whether "causing the fact that a kind of movement occurs" is doing real work or relabeling.

### Does Substance Causation Reduce to or Replace Event Causation?
- **Sides**: Lowe (substance causation primary; event-causation eliminable/derivative) vs mainstream event-causal orthodoxy (Davidson-descended).
- **Core disagreement**: Whether treating substances as relata solves or merely relocates the problems of causal individuation and the causal relata.
- **Current state**: Live in powers-metaphysics literature (Mumford, Anjum, Paoletti).

### Timing / Libet-style Worries for Spontaneous Volition
- **Sides**: Those who read Libet-type readiness-potential data as pre-empting conscious will vs Lowe-style two-way-power theorists.
- **Core disagreement**: Whether a "spontaneous, uncaused" volition is empirically credible given neural precursors.
- **Current state**: Contested; connects to the Map's existing agency/free-will cluster. (The task brief flags "timing" as a main objection to research — this is where it lands.)
