## Executive Summary

The Born rule and its two closest cousins — third-order interference vanishing (Sorkin) and the inner-product/no-signalling link (Pati, Masanes-Galley-Müller steering results) — have been tested with high precision, but **only in photon, atom, superconducting-qubit, and triple-slit systems**. No published experiment directly probes the Born rule inside living neural tissue during a conscious act of selection, which is precisely the regime that consciousness-interface programmes target. The 2025-2026 experimental landscape contains three active strands that are *relevant* but not *direct* tests: (i) witness-style searches for quantum coherence/entanglement in living brains (Kerskens & Pérez 2022 and follow-ups; anesthetic-target studies), (ii) brain-to-quantum-system coupling devices (Nirvanic "Spark of Life", 2025-2027 roadmap), and (iii) legacy micro-PK/RNG meta-analyses (Bösch-Steinkamp-Boller 2006, with Maier-Dechamps 2018 Bayesian evidence *against* the effect). None of these isolates Born-rule violation as such. The question "is Born-violating brain-internal physics empirically live?" therefore has a precise answer: **no direct test exists, the relevant regime is untested, and the field is still building the first-generation instruments that could probe it indirectly**. The corridor-vs-outside-the-corridor decision for the Map's Minimum Quantum Interaction tenet remains empirically open.

## Key Sources

### The measurement postulates of quantum mechanics are operationally redundant (Masanes, Galley, Müller 2019)
- **URL**: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-019-09348-x
- **Type**: Paper (Nature Communications)
- **Key points**:
  - Born rule and the projection postulate follow from the other QM postulates (Hilbert-space states, unitary evolution, tensor-product composition) *plus* a "finite-parameter / state-estimation" postulate.
  - The derivation is operational and conditional on these premises; it does not address what happens where a premise fails.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral. Clarifies that the Born rule is *not* an independent empirical fact — it rides on the other postulates, any of which may fail in untested regimes (including inside brains during conscious selection).
- **Quote**: "the mathematical structure of quantum measurements […] can be deduced from the other quantum postulates […] and the assumption that ensembles on finite-dimensional Hilbert spaces are characterized by finitely many parameters."

### Testing Born's Rule in Quantum Mechanics with a Triple Slit Experiment (Sinha et al. 2009-2010)
- **URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/0811.2068 and https://perimeterinstitute.ca/news/pi-and-iqc-researchers-perform-triple-slit-test-quantum-mechanics
- **Type**: Paper + institutional report
- **Key points**:
  - Sorkin's third-order interference parameter measured with single photons through a three-slit aperture.
  - Third-order contribution bounded at < 10⁻² of the expected second-order contribution (i.e., Born rule confirmed to roughly 1%).
  - Follow-up and higher-precision runs since have tightened this bound further but remained in optical systems.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral-to-supportive of Tenet 2 (Minimal Quantum Interaction). The tested regime is photons in vacuum apparatus; brain-internal regimes are untested.
- **Quote**: "the magnitude of a third-order interference contribution was shown to be less than 10⁻² of the magnitude of the expected second-order interference contributions."

### Causal Consistency Selects the Born Rule: A Derivation from Steering in Generalized Probabilistic Theories (2025, arXiv:2512.12636)
- **URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.12636
- **Type**: Paper (2025)
- **Key points**:
  - In any GPT satisfying purification (and therefore admitting steering), the only no-signalling-compatible probability rule is linear inner-product form.
  - Yields |⟨φ|ψ⟩|² as the unique causally consistent assignment.
  - *Conditional* on: purification, steering as formalised, exact no-signalling.
- **Tenet alignment**: Tightens the conditional case for the corridor, but is itself an "if postulates then Born" result, not an empirical universality claim. Alignment same as MGM: Born rule is locked in *where the premises hold*.

### Which-way Double Slit Experiments and Born Rule Violation (Sawant et al., ca. 2015)
- **URL**: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/309317624_Which-way_double_slit_experiments_and_Born_rule_violation
- **Type**: Paper
- **Key points**:
  - Proposes a new measure of Born rule violation, correct to all orders, based on which-way detector effects on interference patterns.
  - Opens experimental avenues beyond triple-slit Sorkin parameter tests.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral. Remains an optical proposal — not deployed in neural tissue.

### Toward a test of the Born rule in high-energy collisions (Phys. Rev. D, 2025)
- **URL**: https://journals.aps.org/prd/abstract/10.1103/4llk-pt1j
- **Type**: Paper
- **Key points**:
  - Argues high-energy collision data can place novel constraints on Born rule deviations.
  - Extends testing regime into extreme-energy physics.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral. Further confirms the absence of deviation in *non-neural* settings, leaving the brain-internal regime still untested.

### Quantum computation in brain microtubules: Decoherence and biological feasibility (Hagan, Hameroff, Tuszyński 2002)
- **URL**: https://link.aps.org/doi/10.1103/PhysRevE.65.061901
- **Type**: Paper
- **Key points**:
  - Response to Tegmark (2000) who put microtubule decoherence at femtoseconds.
  - Argues, with Debye-layer screening and ordered water, decoherence times up to ~10⁻⁵ s — seven orders of magnitude longer than Tegmark's estimate.
- **Tenet alignment**: Supports the possibility of biologically relevant quantum coherence in the brain, which is necessary but not sufficient for Born-rule-violating consciousness physics.

### A quantum microtubule substrate of consciousness is experimentally supported and solves the binding and epiphenomenalism problems (Babcock, Hameroff et al. 2025)
- **URL**: https://academic.oup.com/nc/article/2025/1/niaf011/8127081
- **Type**: Paper (Neuroscience of Consciousness, 2025)
- **Key points**:
  - Reviews experimental evidence: room-temperature quantum effects in microtubules; microtubule resonances controlling membrane spiking in neurons; volatile anaesthetics targeting microtubules; "direct biophysical evidence of a macroscopic entangled state in the living human brain."
  - Frames this as support for Orch-OR.
- **Tenet alignment**: Supports the possibility of a physical substrate where the standard QM postulates (unitary evolution without additional collapse dynamics) might break down — aligned with Tenet 1 (Dualism) and Tenet 3 (Bidirectional Interaction).
- **Caveats**: Interpretation contested; no direct test of Born-rule violation *per se*.

### Quantum-entangled higher states of consciousness (Kerskens & Pérez 2022, follow-ups Lopez Perez et al. 2023; review at Frontiers 2025)
- **URL**: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/human-neuroscience/articles/10.3389/fnhum.2025.1676585/full
- **Type**: Paper and review
- **Key points**:
  - MRI protocol applied to proton spins in "brain water"; uses entanglement-witness logic adapted from quantum-gravity experiments (if two known quantum systems entangle via an unknown mediator, the mediator must itself be quantum).
  - Detected MRI signals resembling heartbeat evoked potentials that MRI should not normally resolve.
  - Signal correlated with short-term memory performance and with sleep-wake transitions.
- **Tenet alignment**: Aligns with Tenet 1 (dualism needs a physical interface where non-physical influence can land); suggests macroscopic quantum coherence is present where a Born-rule-violating interface could plausibly operate.
- **Caveats**: Alternative classical explanations have been proposed but not fully developed; not a test of the Born rule itself.

### Macroscopic quantum effects in the brain (Keppler 2025, Frontiers in Human Neuroscience)
- **URL**: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/human-neuroscience/articles/10.3389/fnhum.2025.1676585/full
- **Type**: Paper (December 2025)
- **Key points**:
  - Cortical microcolumns (~100 neurons) posited to couple to the electromagnetic zero-point field via glutamate-mediated resonance.
  - Proposes a coherence-domain mechanism at the field level.
- **Tenet alignment**: Panpsychic/neutral monist in motivation, but supplies a *candidate interface* compatible with a modified-Born dualism if one wishes.

### Nirvanic "Spark of Life" experiment (Gildert, 2025-2027)
- **URL**: https://www.nirvanic.ai/ and https://thequantuminsider.com/2025/01/11/is-consciousness-research-the-next-big-quantum-use-case/
- **Type**: Industry experimental programme
- **Key points**:
  - Robot sensory-action feedback loop coupled to a quantum computer's qubits; wave-function collapse is used to "choose" actions.
  - Test whether a quantum-controlled agent behaves differently (statistically) from a classical one.
  - CEO forecasts 1-2 years to interesting signal.
- **Tenet alignment**: Framing is panpsychist ("QCAT"), not dualist, but the *experimental design* is the closest operational analogue to a "does consciousness bias quantum outcomes?" test currently on the roadmap.

### Micropsychokinesis: Bösch, Steinkamp, Boller meta-analysis (2006)
- **URL**: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16822162/
- **Type**: Meta-analysis (Psychological Bulletin)
- **Key points**:
  - 380 studies of intention→RNG effects.
  - Overall effect size small but statistically significant.
  - Strong inverse correlation between sample size and effect size.
  - Extreme heterogeneity.
  - Monte Carlo: results compatible with publication bias.
- **Tenet alignment**: If real, would be a direct indication of Born-rule deviation (consciousness biases quantum random outputs). Evidence status: weakly positive but confounded.

### Intentional Observer Effects on Quantum Randomness: A Bayesian Analysis (Maier & Dechamps 2018)
- **URL**: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5872141/
- **Type**: Paper (pre-registered, Bayesian)
- **Key points**:
  - Strong Bayesian evidence for the *null* hypothesis — no detectable micro-PK effect on quantum RNG.
  - Directly contradicts the PEAR / Bösch positive-effect signal when analysed with modern preregistered methodology.
- **Tenet alignment**: Counter-evidence to gross Born-rule violation by unaided intention in RNG-style setups. Does not rule out smaller, targeted, brain-internal deviations.

### Princeton Engineering Anomalies Research (PEAR) programme summary
- **URL**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Princeton_Engineering_Anomalies_Research_Lab and https://psi-encyclopedia.spr.ac.uk/articles/princeton-engineering-anomalies-research-pear/
- **Type**: Institutional summary
- **Key points**:
  - 12 years, >1000 experimental series, composite 7σ signal, effect sizes 0.1–1%.
  - A 227-participant multi-lab replication (~2M trials) failed to reproduce the effect.
  - Methodology criticised (statistics, stopping rules, non-preregistered analysis).

### Global Consciousness Project (Nelson, 1998-present)
- **URL**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Global_Consciousness_Project and https://noosphere.princeton.edu/
- **Type**: Ongoing distributed REG network
- **Key points**:
  - Claimed composite 7σ deviation linked to "global events."
  - Strong critiques: post-hoc event selection, XOR bit-stream issues, absence of a mechanism.
  - Treated by mainstream physics as not providing robust Born-rule violation evidence.

### Consciousness and the Collapse of the Wave Function (Chalmers & McQueen 2022)
- **URL**: https://philarchive.org/rec/CHACAT-24
- **Type**: Paper/chapter
- **Key points**:
  - Combines IIT (Φ measure) with CSL-style collapse dynamics.
  - "Φ resists superposition, and superpositions of distinct Φ values trigger collapse."
  - Later work (Collapse and Measures of Consciousness, 2021) argues no single scalar measure suffices — *any* such model leaves certain same-measure superpositions of distinct conscious states persistent.
- **Tenet alignment**: Important for Point 4 of the research task — this is a *consciousness-mediated collapse* model. At the ensemble level, with Φ-dependent stochasticity added, the Born rule is **generally not preserved**: the collapse probabilities depend on a consciousness-related variable that standard QM does not include. So Chalmers-McQueen is, strictly, Born-rule-modifying at the neural interface (even while it inherits the CSL mathematical structure that is empirically constrained elsewhere).

### Collapse Theories (SEP) and bounds on GRW/CSL parameters (2024-2025)
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/qm-collapse/
- **Type**: Encyclopedia + empirical updates
- **Key points**:
  - GRW/CSL add nonlinear, stochastic collapse terms to Schrödinger evolution; mass-density coupled.
  - LISA Pathfinder, X-ray/germanium detector experiments, and intergalactic-medium heating constraints have squeezed the parameter space.
  - Diósi-Penrose (simplest form) ruled out by germanium detector data.
  - GRW/CSL itself has "survived by a whisker" at its originally proposed parameter values.
- **Tenet alignment**: A baseline GRW/CSL collapse — universal, consciousness-independent — is empirically on thin ice but not dead. If it were modified with Φ-coupling (Chalmers-McQueen) or a brain-specific enhancement, the parameter space would shift into less-tested regions.

### Stapp / Kauffman Ontologically Real Possibles and Actuals
- **URL**: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0303264722002015
- **Type**: Paper (Kauffman, 2022) and Stapp's collected writings
- **Key points**:
  - Quantum measurement treated as an *acausal* conversion of possibles into actuals by mind.
  - Stapp's quantum Zeno selection picture: rapid conscious attention can hold a particular eigenstate, shifting probabilities away from those a non-conscious measurement would produce.
  - Quantum Zeno at the brain scale *does* modify the Born distribution in a frequency-of-observation-dependent way.
- **Tenet alignment**: Candidates for "minimum outside the corridor" dualism. Stapp's Zeno picture is explicitly Born-rule-bending in how selection frequencies bias outcomes.

### Time-Extended Consciousness-Weighted Quantum Probabilities (Arana 2025)
- **URL**: https://philarchive.org/rec/ARATCQ-2
- **Type**: Paper (PhilArchive)
- **Key points**:
  - Introduces a time-integrated functional that *perturbs* Born probabilities by an exponentially suppressed factor λ.
  - Recovers standard Born in the λ → 0 limit.
  - Presented as a falsifiable phenomenological weighting model.
- **Tenet alignment**: A worked example of a "Born-violating-but-arbitrarily-close" proposal — matches the scenario Point 4 of the task wants made explicit.

## Major Positions

### Position A: Corridor Dualism (Consciousness Selects Within the Born Distribution)

- **Proponents**: The Unfinishable Map's current working hypothesis; sympathetic readings of Stapp (without Zeno); various "interface" authors who insist on preserving ensemble Born statistics.
- **Core claim**: Consciousness influences which branch is actualised on a single measurement event, but the influence averages out to exactly |⟨φ|ψ⟩|² over the ensemble. No statistical signature.
- **Key arguments**:
  - Consistent with every tested Born-rule regime.
  - Maximally conservative reading of MQI — minimal detectable footprint.
  - Compatible with Masanes-Galley-Müller, Pati, Sorkin bounds simultaneously (if the four MGM postulates still hold in the brain, which they would under corridor dualism).
- **Relation to site tenets**: Most conservative realisation of Tenets 1-4. But see problem below.
- **Problem**: If consciousness literally cannot bias ensemble statistics, what is the mechanism of *bidirectional* causal interaction (Tenet 3)? Single-event selection with no statistical footprint risks collapsing into epiphenomenalism-at-the-ensemble level — a subtle form of the very problem dualism is meant to solve. This tension is where "minimum-outside-the-corridor" becomes a live option, not an indulgence.

### Position B: Minimum-Outside-the-Corridor Dualism (Born Rule Bends at the Brain Interface)

- **Proponents**: Stapp (Zeno variant), Chalmers-McQueen (Φ-dependent collapse, taken as Born-modifying at the neural ensemble level), Arana (explicit phenomenological modification), Penrose-Hameroff (OR is a modification of QM, not selection within standard QM), Kauffman-Radin-Stapp "acausal actualisation."
- **Core claim**: Corridor selection is *insufficient* to produce the observed correlation between conscious intention and physical outcome, so MQI requires an interaction that modifies Born probabilities — but only in the brain-internal regime.
- **Key arguments**:
  - Exponentially-suppressed modifications (Arana's λ) could be empirically consistent with all existing tests while still producing macroscopic effects on selection.
  - Penrose-Hameroff OR is explicitly non-unitary and non-Born; the theory lives or dies on deviations from standard QM.
  - Micro-PK signals, if real at any level, require Position B.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Tightens Tenets 1 and 3 by giving the interaction a real physical signature. Sits uneasily with the strong reading of Tenet 2 (Minimal Quantum Interaction) *only if* one reads "minimal" as "corridor-respecting"; sits naturally with the correct reading ("smallest interaction actually sufficient").

### Position C: No Consciousness-Physics Interaction (Orthodox / Many-Worlds / Decoherent Histories / Bohmian)

- **Proponents**: Mainstream physics orthodoxy, Everettians, Bohmians.
- **Core claim**: There is no consciousness-physics interaction. Apparent selection is either branch-indexical (MWI), pilot-wave deterministic (Bohm), or a matter of which history one is in (Consistent Histories).
- **Key arguments**: Every tested Born-rule regime confirms standard QM; no anomaly has survived preregistered replication.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Rejects Tenets 1 and 3. Map is committed against this position by tenet.

## Key Debates

### Debate 1: Does "Born rule survives all tests" entail "Born rule holds everywhere"?

- **Sides**: Orthodoxy (yes, absence of evidence in tested regimes + theoretical derivations from postulates = evidence of absence) vs. interface-programme authors (no, all tests are in photon/atom/qubit regimes and all derivations are conditional on postulates that may fail where conscious selection operates).
- **Core disagreement**: Whether the MGM / Pati / Sorkin results license universal conclusions or only within-premise conclusions.
- **Current state**: Ongoing. The 2025 GPT-steering result strengthens the conditional case; it does not close the empirical gap.

### Debate 2: Are Kerskens-style "quantum brain" signals really evidence of brain-internal quantumness?

- **Sides**: Hameroff / Babcock / Keppler (yes; the MRI entanglement-witness logic is valid; anesthetic data support the same conclusion) vs. skeptics (challenges to the entanglement interpretation, though no alternative classical account yet published).
- **Core disagreement**: Whether entanglement witnesses designed for other contexts license a "therefore nuclear spins in the brain are entangled" inference.
- **Current state**: Active. A replication programme and a full alternative-explanation paper would settle it.

### Debate 3: Micro-PK — is there any robust signal at all?

- **Sides**: Bösch-Steinkamp-Boller (small positive effect) vs. Maier-Dechamps 2018 pre-registered Bayesian analysis (evidence for the null) vs. Radin (insists on robust presentiment/mind-matter effects in other protocols).
- **Core disagreement**: Whether the small positive effects in meta-analyses are real or artefacts of publication bias and flexible analysis.
- **Current state**: Post-2018 preregistered work trends toward the null. The "empirically live" status of the original PEAR-style signal is very weak.

## Potential Article Angles

Based on this research, an article could:

1. **"The Brain-Internal Gap in Born-Rule Testing"** — Present the empirical state honestly: the Born rule is tested to exquisite precision in photon/atom/qubit/triple-slit regimes and nowhere else. Frame the brain-internal conscious-selection regime as an *untested* extrapolation. Catalogue which of Masanes-Galley-Müller's four postulates a consciousness-interface theory might need to break (most naturally: measurement-uniqueness, because a conscious observer *is* the measuring apparatus, and compositional neutrality, if E × P coupling violates subsystem-independence). This directly services the P1 refine-draft task on `topics/mathematical-structure-of-the-consciousness-physics-interface.md`.

2. **"Corridor Dualism vs Minimum-Outside-the-Corridor Dualism"** — A dedicated concept article distinguishing the two Map-compatible readings of Tenet 2 that emerge from this research. Argues that MQI, read strictly, does not privilege the corridor — it privileges whatever minimum produces the observed effect. Presents the corridor as the Map's current working hypothesis, not a commitment.

3. **"What Would a Brain-Internal Born Violation Look Like?"** — Operational article mapping Arana's λ, Stapp's Zeno-rate, Chalmers-McQueen's Φ-coupling, and Penrose's OR timescale onto a common observable: statistics of decision outcomes under tightly-controlled stimulus protocols, anomalies in brain-connected quantum systems (Nirvanic-style), or entanglement-witness departures from standard QM predictions (Kerskens-style). Non-committal on whether any of these signals has been observed; committal on what *would* count as one.

4. **"Interpretive Asymmetry Revisited: The Born Corridor as Contested Empirical Terrain"** — Extends Tonetto's point to the Map's own domain: we do not hold hidden-variable or Bohmian or MWI proposals to a higher standard than orthodox QM, even though none has been directly confirmed in untested regimes either. The same courtesy should extend to Born-violating consciousness-interface proposals: they are not "ruled out by mathematics," they are *conditional* on postulates that have been empirically validated in photon/atom settings and not in brains.

## Gaps in Research

- **No direct brain-internal Born-rule test appears to exist in the published literature as of 2026-04-23.** The closest are (i) witness-level evidence of macroscopic quantum coherence in brain-adjacent substrates (Kerskens, microtubule studies) and (ii) coupled-device proposals (Nirvanic). Neither isolates the Born rule itself as a variable.
- **Chalmers-McQueen ensemble-level statistics.** The literature is ambiguous on whether their Φ-dependent collapse preserves Born probabilities at the ensemble level or (as argued here) silently modifies them. Their 2021 paper arguing against single-measure models makes this question sharper; a focused re-reading is warranted before taking a definitive stance.
- **The computational-thresholds argument (Aaronson) for why Born rule is "natural".** Aaronson's PostBQP = PP result shows computational consequences of changing the measurement postulate; the question of whether brains (which are not obvious polynomial-time quantum computers) would register a difference is not settled in the literature reviewed here.
- **Penrose OR empirical timescale at neural mass scales.** Worth a dedicated follow-up: what does Penrose's E_G ~ ℏ / τ predict for the mass/decay-time involved in microtubule-scale coherent states, and how does that interact with the decoherence-time estimates in Hagan-Hameroff-Tuszyński?
- **Whether anyone has attempted a direct "probe the Born distribution inside a brain-coupled quantum system" experiment.** Radin's double-slit-observation studies (2013-2016) are the closest historical attempt; their methodology and preregistration status need a dedicated review that this task did not conduct.
