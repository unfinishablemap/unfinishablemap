## STRUCTURAL-CLAIM ASSESSMENT: does the asymmetry recur? — NO, interoception BREAKS the pattern (twice)

This is the central finding and the article's reason to exist. The apex's structural claim is an **asymmetry**: modality-specific BRAIN side (no shared substrate across V1 / cochlea / olfactory receptor code) vs. supramodal MIND side (a common conscious/unconscious access signature, Sanchez et al. 2020). Interoception **inverts both terms**:

1. **The brain side CONVERGES rather than diverging.** Across the five exteroceptive/proprioceptive modalities the apex's negative finding is that "nothing is shared" on the brain side — unrelated transduction solutions. Interoception is the opposite: a *distinctive, convergent* cortical substrate. Craig (2002, 2009) and Critchley et al. (2004) identify the insula (posterior → anterior re-representation) plus anterior cingulate as the integrative hub for visceral afferents across cardiac, respiratory, gastric, thermal, and nociceptive channels. Where exteroception's brain side is a *family of unrelated solutions*, interoception's brain side is a *convergence onto a shared visceral-integration cortex*. So the apex's "the brain side does not recur" is a claim about *exteroception specifically*, not a general law — interoception is the counter-instance that shows the modality-specificity was contingent on the distance senses' separate transducers.

2. **The mind side is dominated by AFFECT and OWNERSHIP rather than detached representation.** The apex already flags that its mind-side "ownership" term is "uneven across the senses — vivid in touch and proprioception, faint in smell." Interoception is the *limiting case at the vivid end*: visceral states are barely represented as detached perceptual *objects* at all. They present as valenced, owned bodily feeling. Craig's framing ("the material me"), Seth & Critchley's interoceptive-inference account of *emotion* as the felt-body posterior, and Park & Tallon-Baudry's "neural subjective frame" all converge on interoception as the substrate of the *subject pole* itself — not content delivered *to* a subject but the felt ground *of* subjecthood. So the apex's mind-side term doesn't merely recur weakly; it changes *type*: from "phenomenal unity of a perceived object" (exteroception) to "affective ground of selfhood" (interoception).

**Net structural finding for the article**: the cross-modal asymmetry is an exteroceptive regularity, not a universal one. Interoception is the modality where the brain side converges and the mind side becomes the subject rather than an object — which is exactly why it had to be surveyed separately, and why it sharpens (rather than merely extends) the apex. This is a non-trivial, apex-grade structural claim and the strongest argument for writing the article.

**Evidential-status caution (carried into the article)**: every one of these findings is *equally* a physicalist story. The convergent insular substrate is what a physicalist predicts if the body needs one homeostatic integrator. Interoceptive-inference / active inference (Seth, Barrett & Simmons, Friston) is a *physicalist RIVAL framing to out-accommodate*, NOT evidence for the interface. The "interoception grounds the subjective frame" literature (Park & Tallon-Baudry, Damasio) is explicitly used by its authors to argue consciousness *arises from* the felt body — the opposite of the Map's "body is had, not constitutive" reading (the same Damasio tension the void already records). The article must hold the structural finding as *consonant-not-probative*, exactly as the apex holds the Sanchez signature.

## Executive Summary

Interoception (cardiac, respiratory, gastric, thermal, nociceptive, plus the constitutively unfelt visceral chemistry) is the inward completion of the Map's modality survey. The capability division applies: the brain extracts and integrates visceral state largely without awareness (homeostatic and allostatic regulation runs continuously below threshold), while the narrow band that reaches consciousness arrives as owned, valenced bodily feeling. But interoception **breaks** the apex's headline asymmetry on both sides: the brain-side substrate *converges* onto insula/ACC rather than being modality-specific, and the mind-side contribution is *affect and selfhood* rather than detached representation. The honest physicalist rival — interoceptive inference / active inference — predicts all of this, so the structural finding is consonant with the interface reading without being probative of it. The article's value is the precise structural claim (the cross-modal asymmetry is exteroceptive, not universal) plus the empirical tractability interoception uniquely affords (Garfinkel's three dissociable dimensions; the cardiac/tactile competition datum).

## Major Positions

### Convergent-substrate interoception (Craig / Critchley)
- **Proponents**: A.D. (Bud) Craig; Hugo Critchley.
- **Core claim**: A dedicated afferent system re-represented in insula/ACC integrates visceral state into the felt "material me."
- **Relation to site tenets**: Supplies the *brain-side-converges* disanalogy with the apex. Neutral on metaphysics; Craig's "material me" leans constitutive (rival to the Map's had-body reading).

### Interoceptive inference / active inference (Seth, Barrett & Simmons, Friston)
- **Proponents**: Anil Seth; Lisa Feldman Barrett & W. Kyle Simmons; Karl Friston.
- **Core claim**: Felt body and emotion are top-down predictions tested against ascending visceral error; allostatic regulation and felt emotion are one inferential system.
- **Relation to site tenets**: The PRIMARY physicalist rival. Closes the perception-and-emotion loop with no non-physical remainder. The Map out-accommodates (the inference architecture is exactly what an interface operating below experience would *also* produce) — it does NOT enlist this as evidence.

### Interoception as the substrate of selfhood (Damasio; Park & Tallon-Baudry)
- **Proponents**: Antonio Damasio; Park & Tallon-Baudry.
- **Core claim**: Consciousness *arises from* the felt body / visceral subjective frame; feelings are the source of consciousness.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Direct CONFLICT with the Map's reading (consciousness is wider than the felt body; the body is *had*, not constitutive). This is the same tension the void already records under Damasio. The article should reproduce the void's handling: both readings fit the data; the interoceptive datum *forces* the choice, it does not settle it.

## Key Debates

### Does the cross-modal capability-division asymmetry generalise inward?
- **Sides**: The apex's implied generalisation (asymmetry recurs) vs. the finding here (it inverts on both terms).
- **Core disagreement**: Whether "modality-specific brain / supramodal mind" is a universal signature of perceptual consciousness or an artefact of the distance senses' separate transducers.
- **Current state**: The article's contribution — argues it is exteroceptive, not universal. Interoception is the disconfirming inward case.

### Is the felt body constitutive of consciousness or had by it?
- **Sides**: Damasio / Park–Tallon-Baudry (constitutive) vs. the Map (had).
- **Core disagreement**: Whether the "material me" is what consciousness *is* or one of consciousness's *objects*.
- **Current state**: Unresolved by evidence; the interoceptive case is where the choice is forced.
