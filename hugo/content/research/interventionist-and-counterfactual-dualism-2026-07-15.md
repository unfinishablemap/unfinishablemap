## Executive Summary

This note maps the **dualist** version of interventionist/counterfactual mental causation — the family in which the non-physical experience is *itself* a difference-making cause of behaviour, standing *alongside* its physical base rather than being selected by an authority law over it. This is the Map's single most important live rival to its own preferred causal structure (the closure-preserving **trumping/delegation** route of `trumping-preemption` and `delegatory-causation`). At present the rival appears in the corpus only inside `trumping-preemption.md`, where it is named but treated as a competitor rather than developed on its own terms.

The rival has two branches that a downstream article should keep distinct: (1) **counterfactual dualism** (Kroedel 2015), on which mental events cause physical effects because those effects counterfactually depend on them via psychophysical laws — no preemption, no exclusion; and (2) **interventionist co-causation** (Vaassen 2024, building on the Woodward difference-making tradition), on which mental states are genuine causes because they are *control variables* for behaviour, whatever energy the physical base transfers. Both descend from the older **lawful co-causation / invisible-causation lineage** (Mills 1996; Lowe 2003), on which the systematic mind–body law strips the resulting overdetermination of its "bad", *coincidental* character. All eight target citations were publisher-verified (see **Gaps to Verify** for two corrections to the supplied metadata).

The tenet-diagnostic that should be the eventual article's spine: **the five tenets do not entail either route** (tenet-coherent, not tenet-entailed). But Tenet 2 (Minimal Quantum Interaction) is the *discriminating* tenet, and on a literal reading it leans *toward* co-causation, not trumping — because MQI is itself a genuine (if minimal) difference-making channel, whereas trumping is difference-making-*free*. The Map's stated preference for trumping therefore rests on a *methodological* commitment to observable-closure preservation that is not itself one of the five tenets. Naming this sharpens, rather than weakens, the Map's own position.

## Key Sources

### Kroedel, T. (2015). "Dualist Mental Causation and the Exclusion Problem"
- **URL**: https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/nous.12028
- **Venue**: *Noûs* 49(2): 357–375. DOI 10.1111/nous.12028. **PUBLISHER-VERIFIED.**
- **Type**: Journal paper
- **Key points**:
  - Explicitly a **dualist** account. If dualism is combined with the assumption that psychophysical laws have a *special status*, it follows that some physical events counterfactually depend on — and are therefore caused by — mental events.
  - Mental causation is analysed as **difference-making**: "if our minds had been different, the physical world would have been different; therefore the mind causes events in the physical world."
  - Solves the exclusion problem *two* ways, and lets the dualist choose: (a) *deny* the physical effect is overdetermined by mental + physical causes; or (b) *accept* overdetermination but argue it is benign because the case is sufficiently *dissimilar* to prototypical (coincidental) overdetermination.
- **Tenet alignment**: Aligns with Dualism (Tenet 1) and Bidirectional Interaction (Tenet 3 — genuine difference-making). Neutral on Tenet 2. This is the **nearest dualist competitor** to the trumping route, and Saad cites it in situating his own view (already noted in `trumping-preemption.md`). Where trumping secures *authority without counterfactual dependence*, Kroedel secures *counterfactual dependence without authority-selection* — the mirror-image strategy.

### Vaassen, B. (2024). "Mental Causation for Standard Dualists"
- **URL**: https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/00048402.2024.2335325
- **Venue**: *Australasian Journal of Philosophy* 102(4): 978–998. DOI 10.1080/00048402.2024.2335325. **PUBLISHER-VERIFIED.** (Note: "AJP" = *Australasian*, not *American* — this is the paper the Map already cites as "Vaassen 2024".)
- **Type**: Journal paper
- **Key points**:
  - Targets the "standard objection" to dualism: dualism seemingly cannot account for mental phenomena causing behaviour, given that behaviour is physically necessitated by entirely physical phenomena.
  - Argues an **interventionist** account provides mental causation *within* a standard dualist ontology: mental states qualify as causes because they are **points of control** (difference-making variables) for behaviour, whatever energy the physical base transfers.
  - **Both** the experience and its physical base come out as genuine causes — this is *co-causation*, not preemption. As `trumping-preemption.md` correctly notes (author-corrected framing, per the Vaassen email), his is a *both-are-causes interventionist* view, NOT a preemption view.
- **Tenet alignment**: Strong fit with Tenet 3 (difference-making causal influence) and Tenet 1 (works within *standard* dualism). The core rival to the trumping template.

### Vaassen, B. (2021). "Causal Exclusion without Causal Sufficiency"
- **URL**: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11229-020-02723-y
- **Venue**: *Synthese* 198: 10341–10353. DOI 10.1007/s11229-020-02723-y. **PUBLISHER-VERIFIED** (198:10341–10353 confirmed).
- **Type**: Journal paper
- **Key points**:
  - **Caution for the downstream article**: this is *not* a pro-dualist / pro-co-causation paper. It is an **analysis of the exclusion argument itself**. Vaassen argues that non-reductionist objections claiming exclusion arguments rely on a problematic notion of *causal sufficiency* are superficial, because exclusionists can reformulate the argument in terms of *physical sufficiency* instead — and the reformulated argument survives.
  - Useful as background on *why* the exclusion pressure is robust (it does not depend on a contestable "causal sufficiency" premise), which is precisely the pressure both the trumping and co-causation routes must answer.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral/methodological. Sharpens the problem the Map's route is built to solve; does not itself take the dualist side. Shows Vaassen's own trajectory: he *tightens* the exclusion argument in 2021, then argues (2024) that standard dualists can nonetheless escape it via interventionism.

### Vaassen, B. (2022). "Halfway Proportionality"
- **URL**: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11098-022-01803-8
- **Venue**: *Philosophical Studies* 179: 2823–2843. DOI 10.1007/s11098-022-01803-8. **PUBLISHER-VERIFIED** (volume + pages confirmed; supplied metadata gave no page range).
- **Type**: Journal paper
- **Key points**:
  - On the *proportionality principle* (causes should be "enough and not too much" for their effects), invoked by many to address causal exclusion. Vaassen argues one half of the principle is overly demanding and the other half unobjectionable.
  - Tangential to the dualist co-causation thesis but part of Vaassen's exclusion/difference-making toolkit; relevant only if the downstream article engages proportionality objections. Lower priority.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral/methodological.

### Zhong, L. (2023). "A causal argument for physicalism"
- **URL**: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s44204-023-00110-1
- **Venue**: *Asian Journal of Philosophy* 2, **article number 71** (2023). DOI 10.1007/s44204-023-00110-1. **PUBLISHER-VERIFIED — with corrections** (see Gaps to Verify: the supplied title "A *new* causal argument for physicalism" and locator "2(54)" are both wrong; actual title is "A causal argument for physicalism", article 71).
- **Type**: Journal paper
- **Key points**:
  - Included as a **contrast/rival to the dualist route**: a competing *non-reductive-physicalist* way around exclusion. Zhong develops a causal argument for physicalism *in general* (not reductive physicalism), concluding all mental stuff is metaphysically necessitated by *some* physical stuff — a milder, non-reductive physicalism.
  - Generated a substantial 2024–25 exchange in *Asian Journal of Philosophy* (responses from Jessica Wilson, Thomas Kroedel, Bram Vaassen, Wenjun Zhang, and others), including "Closure, counterfactualist causation, and Zhong's *new* causal argument for physicalism" (2025) — this response paper is where the "new causal argument" phrasing originates.
- **Tenet alignment**: **Conflicts** with Tenet 1 (Dualism) — it is a physicalist route. Its value to the Map is diagnostic: it shows the co-causation/counterfactual machinery can be run *for* physicalism as well as for dualism, so the Map must show why the dualist reading is not thereby undercut.

### Mills, E. O. (1996). "Interactionism and Overdetermination"
- **URL**: https://philpapers.org/rec/MILIAO
- **Venue**: *American Philosophical Quarterly* 33(1): 105–117. **PUBLISHER-VERIFIED** (title, venue, volume, pages confirmed).
- **Type**: Journal paper
- **Key points**:
  - The founding "lawful co-causation" move already cited in `trumping-preemption.md`. Argues the causal-closure argument against dualism fails because overdetermination is not ruled out, and defends **overdeterministic interactionism**: physical events such as an arm's rising are overdetermined by both a non-physical mental cause and a physical cause.
  - The key structural claim the Map borrows: overdetermination is objectionable only when *coincidental*; a systematic mind–body connection makes the convergence non-coincidental, so the "bad" flavour of overdetermination is absent.
- **Tenet alignment**: Aligns with Tenets 1 and 3. This is the *ancestor* of Vaassen's co-causation and the Map's Mills/Lowe reply.

### Lowe, E. J. (2003). "Physical Causal Closure and the Invisibility of Mental Causation"
- **URL**: https://philpapers.org/rec/LOWPCC (reprinted in *Personal Agency*, OUP, pp. 58–78: https://academic.oup.com/book/6201/chapter/149805864)
- **Venue**: In S. Walter & H.-D. Heckmann (eds.), *Physicalism and Mental Causation: The Metaphysics of Mind and Action*, Imprint Academic, pp. 137–154. **PUBLISHER-VERIFIED** (chapter, editors, publisher, pages confirmed).
- **Type**: Edited-volume chapter
- **Key points**:
  - The "invisibility" half of the Map's reply. Develops models of mental causation on which, *if correct*, it would nonetheless appear to physical scientists that all the causation in human behaviour is purely physical.
  - Distinguishes what the physicalist is entitled to — **observational** closure (no *detectable* trajectory violation) — from closure *full-stop*. If lawful co-causation preserves observational closure, the dualist has not taken on the bad kind of overdetermination.
- **Tenet alignment**: Aligns with Tenets 1 and 3, and supplies the *observational-closure* concept the Map's own `observational-closure` article leans on. The pivotal source for the diagnostic below: Lowe's route, like the Map's trumping route, prizes *observable* invisibility — but reaches it by *co-causation*, not by authority-selection.

## Major Positions

### Position A — Counterfactual Dualism (Kroedel 2015)
- **Proponents**: Kroedel; sympathetic machinery in Bennett (2003, the "compatibilist" counterfactual test the Map already cites).
- **Core claim**: Given psychophysical laws with special status, physical effects counterfactually depend on mental events, so mental events *cause* them. Exclusion dissolves: either no overdetermination, or benign overdetermination.
- **Key arguments**: Difference-making semantics of causation; dissimilarity from coincidental-overdetermination prototypes.
- **Relation to site tenets**: The nearest dualist rival. Diverges from trumping on *what secures efficacy*: counterfactual dependence (Kroedel) vs authority-selection under sufficiency (trumping). A dualist could accept Kroedel and drop trumping entirely.

### Position B — Interventionist Co-Causation (Vaassen 2024)
- **Proponents**: Vaassen; grounded in Woodward-style interventionism (and, on the exclusion side, Baumgartner's interventionist pressure, already flagged in `trumping-preemption.md`).
- **Core claim**: Mental states are difference-making control variables for behaviour; both mental and physical are genuine causes; no preemption, no exclusion competition to resolve.
- **Key arguments**: Difference-making ≠ energy transfer, so closure (no extra energy source) is *compatible* with mental causation; the "standard objection" conflates the two.
- **Relation to site tenets**: The core rival. Note the crucial structural difference from trumping — **two causes, not one authoritative cause**.

### Position C — Lawful (Overdeterministic) Co-Causation (Mills 1996; Lowe 2003)
- **Proponents**: Mills; Lowe; this is the lineage the Map's own Mills/Lowe reply invokes.
- **Core claim**: Behaviour is overdetermined by mental + physical causes; the *systematic* psychophysical law makes the overdetermination non-coincidental (Mills) and *observationally invisible* (Lowe), so the standard overdetermination objection loses its sting.
- **Relation to site tenets**: The historical root of both A and B, and already partly inside the Map. The article's job is to show that this lineage's natural modern form is *co-causation*, which competes with — rather than instances — trumping.

### Position D (contrast/rival) — Non-Reductive Physicalist Route (Zhong 2023)
- **Proponents**: Zhong; the surrounding *Asian Journal of Philosophy* symposium.
- **Core claim**: A causal argument supports *non-reductive physicalism* — mental stuff is metaphysically necessitated by physical stuff — using causal/counterfactual machinery structurally similar to the dualist routes.
- **Relation to site tenets**: **Conflicts** with Tenet 1. Included as the physicalist mirror: the same difference-making toolkit can be turned *against* dualism, so the article must not treat co-causation machinery as intrinsically dualist-friendly.

## Key Debates

### Debate 1 — One authoritative cause (trumping) vs two genuine causes (co-causation)
- **Sides**: Saad/Bealer (delegation/preemption: the experience *trumps* the base, single authoritative cause) vs Vaassen/Kroedel/Mills/Lowe (co-causation: both are causes; the systematic law makes the overdetermination benign).
- **Core disagreement**: Whether *authority-selection under sufficiency* is needed at all, or whether the exclusion worry dissolves once causation is read as difference-making + the mind–body law is systematic.
- **Current state**: Live and unsettled. `trumping-preemption.md` already concedes that if any exclusion-dissolution (Kroedel, Bennett, List–Stoljar, Baumgartner-interventionism) succeeds on its own terms, the trumping template "may be answering a question already closed."

### Debate 2 — Does closure-preservation favour either route?
- **Sides**: Both routes claim to preserve *observable* closure. Trumping preserves it by being difference-making-free (no trajectory change at all). Vaassen/Lowe preserve it because difference-making need not be energy transfer (closure as a claim about energy/forces is untouched).
- **Core disagreement**: Whether "the physical is closed" is even the right battleground — Vaassen says closure and mental causation are simply compatible; the Map's trumping route treats closure-preservation as the *prize* it is engineered to win.
- **Current state**: This is where the tenet-diagnostic bites (below): both preserve *observational* closure, so closure alone does not decide between them.

## Tenet-Diagnostic Framing (the eventual article's spine)

**Question posed by the harvest task**: does Minimal Quantum Interaction (Tenet 2) + Bidirectional Interaction (Tenet 3) favour the closure-preserving **trumping** route (the Map's current preference) or the difference-making **co-causation** route?

**Landing (tenet-coherent, NOT tenet-entailed):** the tenets are jointly *satisfiable* by both routes, but they do not pull symmetrically — and, non-obviously, the discriminating tenet leans *toward* the rival.

1. **Closure alone does not decide it.** Both routes preserve *observable* closure. Trumping does so by being difference-making-free (both processes complete; a law picks the authoritative one; no trajectory change). Vaassen/Lowe co-causation does so because difference-making is not energy transfer — closure as an energy/force claim is simply untouched. So the Map cannot appeal to observational-closure-preservation to break the tie; both rivals deliver it.

2. **Tenet 3 (Bidirectional Interaction), read literally, favours co-causation.** Tenet 3 says consciousness *causally influences* the physical world. Co-causation makes the experience a genuine *difference-maker* — the most natural reading of "influence." Trumping's authority-without-any-difference is exactly the structure the Map itself flags as uncomfortably close to `epiphenomenalism` / `ensemble-level-epiphenomenalism`: efficacy relocated onto an *authority axis* with no trajectory signature. A difference-making reading of Tenet 3 is more securely satisfied by co-causation.

3. **Tenet 2 (Minimal Quantum Interaction) is the discriminating tenet — and it also leans co-causation.** This is the sharp, non-obvious result. MQI posits an *actual, minimal* non-physical influence on quantum outcomes: a genuine (if statistically invisible) *biasing* of indeterminate collapse. That is a **difference-making channel** — it gives up *strong* causal closure while preserving *observational* closure. Trumping is the opposite: **difference-making-free**, strong-closure-*preserving*. So the Map's own Tenet-2 mechanism is structurally continuous with the *co-causation* family (a real, minimal difference-maker) and structurally *discontinuous* with trumping. `trumping-preemption.md` already concedes this internal seam: "the quantum-selection channel is … best read as a distinct and potentially competing mechanism to the trumping route rather than an instance of it." A Map that takes MQI as a real biasing influence has therefore *already* chosen a difference-making channel at the quantum level; adopting trumping at the psychophysical level introduces a *second, structurally different* (difference-free) channel, and the two sit in mild tension.

4. **What actually drives the Map's trumping preference is a methodological commitment, not a tenet.** The trumping route's distinctive prize is *strong*-closure preservation plus zero trajectory footprint — maximal empirical conservatism. That is a *posture* (minimise the detectable metaphysical footprint), not one of the five tenets. Naming this is the article's payoff: the Map's preference for trumping over co-causation is **tenet-coherent but tenet-underdetermined**, and it trades on closure-conservatism rather than on anything the tenets strictly require. The co-causation rival is worth articulating precisely because it is the route a *literal* reading of Tenets 2+3 would favour.

**Fair statement of the rival (for the article's frame):** the interventionist/counterfactual-dualist co-causation family is not a weaker cousin of trumping to be dispatched; it is the route most continuous with the Map's own difference-making commitments (Tenet 3 literally, Tenet 2's biasing channel structurally). The Map prefers trumping for its stronger closure-preservation and cleaner invisibility, and it may reasonably do so — but the preference is a *choice under underdetermination*, and stating the co-causation rival fairly is what makes that choice visible and defensible rather than assumed.
