## Executive Summary

Daniel Dennett's **Multiple Drafts Model (MDM)**, introduced in *Consciousness Explained* (1991) and developed with Marcel Kinsbourne in "Time and the observer" (1992), is the most direct materialist attack on the architecture that interactionist dualism appears to require. Its core move is negative: reject the **Cartesian Theater** — any single place and time in the brain where "it all comes together" for a central observer/audience. In its place MDM posits parallel, multitrack "editorial" processes that continuously revise content, with no canonical stream and no privileged moment at which unconscious processing "becomes" conscious. From this Dennett argues the **Orwellian vs. Stalinesque** question (was an experience misremembered, or was the memory-forming experience itself doctored pre-consciously?) is **ill-posed**: content-fixation is *probe-dependent*, so there is "no fact of the matter" distinguishing the two. **Heterophenomenology** is the paired third-person method; the later "**fame in the brain**"/"**cerebral celebrity**" gloss recasts consciousness as competitive influence over time rather than arrival at a place. The standing objections split MDM's anti-homuncular neuroscience (widely accepted) from its further eliminativist/verificationist steps (widely contested). **For the Map this is Tenet 1 + Tenet 3's primary rival: Dennett's critique targets exactly the "consciousness watching a screen" picture the Map must disavow, and the Map can adopt most of the anti-homuncular science while rejecting the eliminativist conclusion.**

## Major Positions

### Multiple Drafts Model (Dennett; Dennett & Kinsbourne)
- **Proponents**: Daniel Dennett, Marcel Kinsbourne.
- **Core claim**: Perceptual and cognitive content is fixed by parallel, distributed "editorial" processes running in multiple neural tracks. At any moment several "drafts" of content coexist; none is the canonical, consciously-witnessed version. There is no finish line, no "Cartesian Theater," and no central Meaner/observer.
- **Key arguments**: (1) The color-phi and cutaneous-rabbit illusions show the brain "postdicts" content, so subjective timing ≠ neural timing. (2) Any theory placing a threshold "where and when content becomes conscious" smuggles in a homunculus. (3) Because content-fixation is probe-dependent, Orwellian and Stalinesque reconstructions are empirically and conceptually indistinguishable — the difference is not a real difference.
- **Relation to site tenets**: The anti-homuncular architecture is COMPATIBLE with the Map and even useful to it. The inference from "no Cartesian Theater" to "no phenomenal consciousness / no qualia / no fact of the matter" is the CONTESTED step the Map rejects.

### Cartesian Theater / "Cartesian materialism" (Dennett's target, not a real proponent's view)
- **Proponents**: Nobody explicitly — Dennett presents it as an unacknowledged default that materialists slide into after rejecting the Cartesian soul.
- **Core claim (as caricatured)**: There is a privileged place/time in the brain where "it all comes together" and is presented to consciousness.
- **Key arguments against (Dennett)**: There is no such convergence zone; distributed processing needs no audience.
- **Relation to site tenets**: The Map must NOT be read as endorsing this. The Map's consciousness–brain interface (Tenet 3) is NOT a spatial theater and posits NO homunculus watching a screen.

### Heterophenomenology (Dennett's method — cross-ref, has its own page)
- **Core claim**: Take subjects' sincere reports as data about *what they believe* they experience, bracketing whether the beliefs are true; build the theory third-personally.
- **Relation**: Already covered at `concepts/heterophenomenology.md`. Reference, do not duplicate. Note only the MDM-specific point: MDM is the *positive model* heterophenomenology's data are meant to constrain.

### Global Workspace Theory (friendlier successor — cross-ref, has its own page)
- **Core claim**: Consciousness = information "broadcast" in a workspace, globally available to consumer systems.
- **Relation to MDM**: Often read as a more neurally grounded, less metaphorical MDM; Dennett's "cerebral celebrity" explicitly welcomes the GWT picture. Covered at `concepts/global-workspace-theory.md` — reference the lineage, do not duplicate.

## Key Debates

### Does MDM *eliminate* or *explain* phenomenal consciousness?
- **Sides**: Dennett (explains — there was never intrinsic qualia to eliminate) vs. Chalmers/Block/Strawson (MDM changes the subject, describing verbal-report dispositions, not experience).
- **Core disagreement**: Whether "phenomenal consciousness" beyond functional/representational role is real. This is the hinge between MDM and the Map's Tenet 1.
- **Current state**: Ongoing and central. This is exactly where MDM belongs to the eliminative-materialism/illusionism family (cross-ref) — but MDM's *distinctive* contribution is the architectural (Cartesian-Theater) argument, which this page owns.

### The "Dennett denies qualia" charge
- **Sides**: Dennett ("Quining Qualia," 1988) argues qualia as intrinsic, private, ineffable properties do not exist; critics say this denies the datum.
- **Note / CITATION DISCIPLINE**: The often-quoted "there are no qualia"-type lines circulate in many near-verbatim forms. **Flag, do not assert as a verbatim quote** unless checked against "Quining Qualia" or *Consciousness Explained* primary text.

### Is probe-dependence coherent? (the verificationism objection)
- **Sides**: Critics (many BBS commentators; Block) say "there is no fact of the matter which reconstruction is true" is illicit verificationism — an undetectable difference can still be a real difference. Defenders (and some later reconstructions) argue the "no fact of the matter" verdict can be reached without global verificationism.
- **Core disagreement**: Whether an in-principle-undetectable difference in the timing of content-fixation is a genuine difference. **This is the Map's opening**: the Map can accept the anti-Theater neuroscience while treating the verificationist inference as an over-reach — an instance of Tenet 5 (Occam/simplicity has limits) plus a refusal to let epistemic access exhaust ontology.
- **Current state**: Unresolved; the verificationism charge is the most durable objection.
