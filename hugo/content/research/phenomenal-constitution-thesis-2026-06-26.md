## Executive Summary

The Phenomenal Constitution Thesis, as the Map uses it, is the claim that the phenomenal character of a conscious state **constitutes** (rather than merely accompanies, causes, or covaries with) the meaning/content of that state. The term-of-art "constitution" is doing real metaphysical work, and the literature supplies a precise sense of it on two fronts. (1) In philosophy of mind, Elijah Chudnoff (2015) draws the canonical **constituting-vs-accompanying** distinction within the cognitive-phenomenology debate: liberals who say cognitive states *constitute* a proprietary phenomenal character are distinguished from weaker liberals who say phenomenal character merely *accompanies* cognition, and from skeptics who say sensory states accompany it. (2) In general metaphysics, **constitution** is a well-developed relation (the statue/clay case) that is explicitly *not identity* and *not supervenience* — the constituted thing "consists in / is nothing over and above" its constituter yet remains numerically distinct (Wiggins; Johnston 1992; Baker; SEP *Material Constitution*, Wasserman, rev. 2026). A third, convergent thread: acquaintance theorists explain the immediacy of phenomenal self-knowledge *via constitution* — the introspective state is partly **constituted by** the introspected state (Horgan & Kriegel 2007; Gertler; Chalmers 2003), which is the bridge between PCT and the Map's existing `phenomenal-acquaintance` concept. The headline distinction the anchor page must make: PCT is **stronger than phenomenal realism** (which only asserts qualia are real), **stronger than supervenience** (mere covariation), and **not identity** (it can keep content and quale numerically distinct while making one metaphysically depend on the other).

- **Verification**: VERIFIED at publisher of record. Google Books confirms title/author/publisher/year/series. IEP entry independently confirms the conceptual content.
- **Key points**:
  - Draws the **constituting vs accompanying** distinction: whether cognitive states *constitute* their own phenomenal character or sensory states merely *accompany* them. IEP renders the constitutive sense as "a non-causal explanatory relation that can alternatively be picked out by 'in virtue of' or 'constitutively dependent on'."

So the Map's frequent "Chudnoff (2015) distinguishes accompanying from constituting" is faithful to his *distinction*, but the Map sides *against* Chudnoff on the content-determinacy verdict. The anchor page should state this precisely (he supplies the distinction; he is a *critic* of the strong content-PCT the Map adopts).

## Major Positions

### PCT — strong reading (the Map's view)
- **Proponents (of strong phenomenal-intentionality / content-PCT)**: Horgan & Tienson, Pitt, Strawson, Siewert, Kriegel (phenomenal-intentionality program). The Map adopts the content-constitution horn *abductively*.
- **Core claim**: The content/meaning of (at least some) mental states is *constituted by* their phenomenal character — to grasp a meaning *is* to undergo a certain experience; the content cannot be separated from the experiential character.
- **Key arguments**: content-determinacy (phenomenal character fixes "rabbit" vs "undetached rabbit parts" where physical description underdetermines); understanding-vs-processing contrast; the Chinese Room verdict.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Aligns with **Tenet 1 (Dualism)** — makes phenomenal character explanatorily indispensable, resisting functional reduction.

### Accompaniment view (weak liberalism)
- **Proponents**: weaker cognitive-phenomenology liberals; Chudnoff (2015) on *content* specifically.
- **Core claim**: Cognitive states have (or are accompanied by) phenomenal character, but content-determinacy is handled by non-phenomenal machinery; phenomenology *accompanies* rather than *constitutes* meaning.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Neutral-to-tension; concedes phenomenal reality but denies it the constitutive role the Map needs.

### Skeptic / deflationary view
- **Proponents**: Tye, Dretske, Prinz.
- **Core claim**: Apparent cognitive phenomenology reduces to accompanying sensory imagery / inner speech / affect; no proprietary cognitive phenomenal character, a fortiori none that constitutes content.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Conflicts with the Map; the deflationary alternative is the one PCT must beat abductively.

### Grounding/constitution physicalism (the *inverse* deployment)
- **Proponents**: grounding physicalists (and constitution-physicalists); Goff argues *against* it; Mendelovici favors an *identity* view of phenomenal intentionality (a useful contrast: identity, not constitution).
- **Core claim**: Phenomenal properties are *grounded in / constituted by* physical properties (non-reductive but physicalist).
- **Relation to site tenets**: Conflicts with **Tenet 1**. Important for the page because it shows "constitution" is direction-neutral vocabulary; the Map must specify *which way* the constitution runs and why the physical→phenomenal direction faces the revelation/knowledge-argument objections.

## Key Debates

### Constitution vs accompaniment (within cognitive phenomenology)
- **Sides**: strong liberals (constitution) vs weak liberals (accompaniment) vs skeptics (no proprietary CP).
- **Core disagreement**: whether phenomenal character does *explanatory/metaphysical work* on content or is an epiphenomenal companion to it.
- **Current state**: ongoing; phenomenal-contrast arguments are the contested evidence; introspective reliability is the methodological battleground.

### Constitution vs identity vs supervenience (the relation itself)
- **Sides**: constitution-without-identity (Baker, Thomson, Wiggins) vs identity theorists vs those who collapse it into supervenience.
- **Core disagreement**: whether two numerically distinct things can fully coincide; whether supervenience (mere covariation) is too weak to capture the asymmetric "in virtue of" dependence.
- **Current state**: mature in metaphysics; the lesson PCT should import — constitution is *asymmetric, non-causal, hyperintensional dependence*, stronger than supervenience and weaker than identity.

### Direction of constitution (mind→content vs physical→mind)
- **Sides**: phenomenal-intentionality program (phenomenal constitutes content) vs grounding/constitution physicalism (physical constitutes phenomenal).
- **Core disagreement**: which relata are fundamental.
- **Current state**: live in 2020s metaphysics of mind; the Map sits with the former and rejects the latter.
