## Executive Summary

Wheeler's "it from bit" (1989/1990) is a stronger claim than the participatory universe taken alone. While the participatory universe says observers constitute reality through measurement, "it from bit" makes an ontological assertion: information is the fundamental substrate of reality, and physical things ("its") derive their existence from binary informational acts ("bits"). This thesis spawned three distinct research programmes: (1) quantum information foundations (Brukner-Zeilinger), which takes the finite information content of quantum systems as axiomatic; (2) digital physics (Fredkin, Wolfram, Zuse), which treats the universe as a computation; and (3) informational realism (Dembski and others), which accepts information's fundamentality but rejects observer-creation. Wheeler's thesis also provoked a significant counter-movement — Barbour's "bit from it" — arguing that things remain primary and information derivative. For the Map's framework, "it from bit" is a double-edged sword: it opens space for non-material foundations (supporting Dualism and Occam's Razor Has Limits) but risks collapsing into idealism or computational physicalism, neither of which fits the Map's interactionist dualism.

## Key Sources

### Wheeler, "Information, Physics, Quantum: The Search for Links" (1990)
- **URL**: https://philpapers.org/archive/WHEIPQ.pdf
- **Type**: Academic paper (foundational)
- **Key points**:
  - Coined "it from bit" — presented Tokyo 1989, published 1990
  - Four conclusions: (1) the world cannot be a giant machine ruled by preestablished continuum law; (2) no spacetime continuum at the microscopic level; (3) QM's probability functions conceal their information-theoretic source; (4) the elementary quantum phenomenon — the device-intermediated yes-no question — is closer to primordial than any other element of physics
  - Key passage: "Every it — every particle, every field of force, even the spacetime continuum itself — derives its function, its meaning, its very existence entirely — even if in some contexts indirectly — from the apparatus-elicited answers to yes or no questions, binary choices, bits."
- **Tenet alignment**: Strong alignment with Occam's Razor Has Limits and Dualism (immaterial foundations). Supports No Many Worlds (measurement produces definite outcomes — single bits, not superpositions)

### Zeilinger, "A Foundational Principle for Quantum Mechanics" (1999)
- **URL**: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1023/A:1018820410908
- **Type**: Academic paper (peer-reviewed)
- **Key points**:
  - Directly inspired by Wheeler — takes information as QM's most fundamental notion
  - Foundational principle: "An elementary system represents the truth value of one proposition" — exactly one bit
  - Randomness in non-measured observables is a necessary consequence of finite information; entanglement arises when all information specifies joint rather than individual properties
  - Developed further with Brukner: QM derived from information-theoretic axioms
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral to supportive. Randomness from finite information connects to the Map's claim that quantum indeterminacy is where consciousness acts

### Barbour, "Bit from It" (FQXi 2013 / Springer 2015)
- **URL**: https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-3-319-12946-4_17
- **Type**: Academic paper / book chapter
- **Key points**:
  - The most rigorous counter-thesis to Wheeler's "it from bit"
  - Identifies three distinct kinds of information; argues Wheeler conflated them
  - Wheeler's "bits" (detector clicks) are features of physical things, not free-floating information
  - Concludes: "bit" derives from "it" — but concedes this does not necessarily destroy the argument for nature's digital character
- **Tenet alignment**: Does not threaten the Map's core commitments. The Map requires quantum indeterminacy as an interface for consciousness, not that information is ontologically primary. Even if Barbour is right, the measurement problem remains

### Dembski, "It From Bit: What Did Wheeler Get Right — and Wrong?" (2021)
- **URL**: https://mindmatters.ai/2021/05/it-from-bit-what-did-john-archibald-wheeler-get-right-and-wrong/
- **Type**: Critical philosophical analysis
- **Key points**:
  - Wheeler correctly identified information as fundamental but wrongly inferred observer-creation from the fact that measurement requires information
  - All scientific measurements reduce to bits — but this reflects methodology, not ontology
  - Proposes "informational realism": nature is genuinely informational without requiring observer-creation
- **Tenet alignment**: Compatible. Informational realism preserves the information-consciousness interface without observer-created-reality circularity

### Horgan, "Physicist John Wheeler and the 'It from Bit'" (2021)
- **URL**: https://johnhorgan.org/cross-check/physicist-john-wheeler-and-the-it-from-bit
- **Type**: Science journalism / philosophical analysis
- **Key points**:
  - Wheeler's three intellectual phases: (1) everything is particles; (2) everything is fields; (3) everything is information
  - "I do take 100 percent seriously the idea that the world is a figment of the imagination"
  - Ironic tension: Wheeler's framework implies truth is perpetually elusive, yet Wheeler sought ultimate understanding
- **Tenet alignment**: This tension — between "the world is a figment" and "we can understand it" — is precisely what the Map navigates

### Stanford Encyclopedia: "Quantum Approaches to Consciousness"
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/qt-consciousness/
- **Type**: Encyclopedia (peer-reviewed)
- **Key points**:
  - Wheeler's "it from bit" is frequently misinterpreted as mere yes-no alternatives; properly, "only the selection of a question entails a particular partition that allows the definition of bits"
  - Wheeler's "meaning circuit" massively differs from syntactic Shannon information
  - SEP reads Wheeler as dual-aspect: connecting subjective selection with objective registration, neither purely physical nor purely mental
- **Tenet alignment**: The dual-aspect reading aligns more closely with the Map's interactionist dualism than commonly assumed. The "meaning circuit" concept connects to the experiential character the Map associates with consciousness
- **Quote**: "Meaning (not causation) is the appropriate concept to interpret correlations between the mental and the physical."

### FQXi Essay Contest: "It from Bit, or Bit from It?" (2013)
- **URL**: https://forums.fqxi.org/d/1589
- **Type**: Essay contest / community debate
- **Key points**:
  - FQXi's 2013 essay contest dedicated to Wheeler's thesis; no consensus emerged
  - Generated the Springer volume *It From Bit or Bit From It?* (2015), now a standard reference
- **Tenet alignment**: The unresolved debate supports Occam's Razor Has Limits

## Major Positions (Summary)

Five positions emerge from the sources above. See Key Sources for detailed arguments and tenet alignment.

| Position | Proponents | Core Claim | Map Compatibility |
|----------|-----------|------------|-------------------|
| **It from Bit** | Wheeler | Physical reality derives from information-theoretic acts; the universe is a "self-excited circuit" | Supports Dualism/Bidirectional Interaction but risks idealism |
| **Quantum Information Foundations** | Brukner, Zeilinger | QM should be derived from information axioms; randomness follows from finite information | Neutral — compatible but does not require consciousness |
| **Digital Physics** | Fredkin, Zuse, Wolfram | The universe is fundamentally computational (cellular automata) | **Conflicts** with Dualism — computational physicalism reduces consciousness to computation |
| **Informational Realism** | Dembski | Information is fundamental but observers don't create reality | Compatible — preserves information-consciousness interface without circularity |
| **Bit from It** | Barbour | Information is a feature of things, not the reverse | Does not threaten core commitments — the measurement problem remains regardless |

**Critical distinction**: Wheeler's participatory information (observer-involving) vs. digital physics' computational information (observer-independent) use the same word "information" for fundamentally different concepts. The Map aligns with Wheeler's version, not digital physics.

## Key Debates

### Is "It from Bit" Idealism?
- **Core disagreement**: Whether "it from bit" is ontological (information *is* the fundamental substance) or methodological (information is the best framework for physics)
- **Current state**: Unresolved. The SEP's dual-aspect reading is most careful. The Map should align with this: "it from bit" opens conceptual space for non-material foundations without committing to idealism.

### Information vs. Computation
- Wheeler's "it from bit" treats information as extracted through participatory measurement acts. Digital physics treats information as computation — mechanical, observer-independent. Despite similar vocabulary, these are fundamentally different.
- The Map must clearly distinguish these: Wheeler's version supports the framework; digital physics undermines it.

### Does "It from Bit" Require Consciousness?
- Wheeler was ambiguous about whether "observer-participancy" requires conscious observers or merely physical interactions producing records. Stapp and the Map require consciousness; Brukner-Zeilinger and digital physics do not; QBism requires an "agent" but won't specify what counts.
- This is the crux for the Map. The strongest version — the one with genuine philosophical import — requires something more than physical registration. If any physical interaction constitutes "observation," then "it from bit" reduces to the trivially true claim that physics requires measurement apparatus.
