## Executive Summary

Motor control provides a second domain—beyond attention—where consciousness might exercise selection through the quantum Zeno mechanism. Research reveals a rich neural architecture for voluntary movement that parallels attention: frontal theta oscillations, a ~300ms decision timeline, and neurally distinguishable "willed" versus "instructed" action. Critically, Libet's challenge has been substantially weakened by Schurger's stochastic accumulator model (the readiness potential may be noise rather than decision) and by the "free won't" framework (consciousness vetoes rather than initiates). Desmurget's parietal stimulation studies show that conscious motor intention arises in parietal cortex while premotor regions execute movement without awareness—supporting a selection model where consciousness operates at the "what to do" level, not "how to do it." The basal ganglia's action selection mechanism (brake/release model) offers another candidate interface site. This research strengthens The Unfinishable Map's framework by extending the quantum Zeno mechanism from attention to motor action.

## Key Sources

### Libet's Original Experiments (1983)
- **URL**: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/6640273/
- **Type**: Foundational experimental study
- **Key points**:
  - Readiness potential (RP) precedes conscious awareness of intention by ~350ms
  - RP begins ~550ms before movement; conscious "W" time is ~200ms before
  - Libet proposed "free won't"—consciousness can veto prepared actions
- **Tenet alignment**: Initially seemed to challenge Bidirectional Interaction, but Libet's own interpretation preserves a role for conscious selection (vetoing)
- **Quote**: "The conscious will emerging at ~−200 ms could either allow or block the volitional process to go to completion"

### Schurger et al. - Stochastic Accumulator Model (2012)
- **URL**: https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.1210467109
- **Type**: Computational neuroscience paper
- **Key points**:
  - RP may be artifact of averaging stochastic neural noise
  - Leaky stochastic accumulator model explains RP shape and wait-time distribution
  - Movement occurs when random fluctuations cross threshold
  - "Takes the sting out of the Libet argument"
- **Tenet alignment**: Strongly aligns—removes apparent evidence against conscious causation
- **Quote**: "The RP may reflect ongoing stochastic fluctuations in neural activity that favor the spontaneous emission of a movement at certain times more so than at others"

### Desmurget et al. - Parietal Stimulation and Motor Intention (2009)
- **URL**: https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.1169896
- **Type**: Human neurosurgical study
- **Key points**:
  - Parietal stimulation creates conscious intention to move without movement
  - Higher intensity creates false belief of having moved (with no EMG activity)
  - Premotor stimulation causes movement WITHOUT awareness
  - Dissociation: intention (parietal) vs execution (premotor)
- **Tenet alignment**: Strongly supports site framework—shows intention is where consciousness contributes, separate from motor execution machinery
- **Quote**: "Stimulating the right inferior parietal regions triggered a strong intention and desire to move... participants believed they had really performed these movements, although no electromyographic activity was detected"

### Fried et al. - Single Neuron Recording of Volition (2011)
- **URL**: https://www.cell.com/neuron/fulltext/S0896-6273(10)01082-2
- **Type**: Human single-neuron recording study
- **Key points**:
  - Progressive neuronal recruitment over ~1500ms before decision awareness
  - 256 SMA neurons predict impending decision with >80% accuracy 700ms before awareness
  - "Volition emerges once a change in internally generated firing rate crosses a threshold"
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral—shows neural precursors but doesn't determine whether consciousness selects from these patterns
- **Quote**: "A population of 256 SMA neurons is sufficient to predict in single trials the impending decision to move with accuracy greater than 80% already 700 ms prior to subjects' awareness"

### Stapp - Quantum Zeno Mechanism for Motor Control
- **URL**: https://www-physics.lbl.gov/~stapp/QID.pdf
- **Type**: Theoretical physics paper
- **Key points**:
  - "Template for action" held in place by quantum Zeno effect
  - Consciousness operates through ion channel probing actions
  - Effort proportional to observation rate (bits/sec of central processing)
  - Addresses Libet timing through Heisenberg-style collapse framework
- **Tenet alignment**: Central to site's Minimal Quantum Interaction and Bidirectional Interaction tenets
- **Quote**: "By virtue of the quantum Zeno effect, a 'template for action' can be held in place despite the normal dissipative physical tendencies, allowing the consciously intended bodily action to occur"

### Bengson et al. - Frontal Theta in Willed Attention (2019)
- **URL**: https://academic.oup.com/cercor/article/29/7/2832/5041999
- **Type**: EEG study
- **Key points**:
  - Frontal theta oscillations increase for willed vs instructed attention
  - Bidirectional frontoparietal Granger causality during willed attention
  - Theta indexes frontal decision-making
  - ~500ms post-decision theta increase
- **Tenet alignment**: Aligns—shows neural signature distinguishing willed from automatic
- **Quote**: "Theta oscillations are the neuronal signals indexing decision-making in the frontal cortex, and mediating reciprocal communications between the frontal executive and parietal attentional control regions during willed attention"

### Basal Ganglia Action Selection
- **URL**: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5815934/
- **Type**: Review article
- **Key points**:
  - BG output is tonically inhibitory—a "braking system"
  - Action selection = releasing the brake on desired motor programs
  - Direct pathway (D1, dopamine-excited) enables action; indirect pathway (D2) inhibits
  - Selection mechanism must choose among competing motor programs
- **Tenet alignment**: Aligns—the "brake release" model fits selection framework where consciousness determines which patterns are disinhibited
- **Quote**: "The output of the basal ganglia is analogous to a braking system. When a desired action is initiated... basal ganglia output neurons projecting to that generator decrease their discharge, thereby removing tonic inhibition"

### Efference Copy and Sense of Agency
- **URL**: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8662721/
- **Type**: Theoretical neuroscience paper
- **Key points**:
  - Efference copy predicts sensory consequences of motor command
  - Sense of agency arises when predicted matches actual sensory feedback
  - Forward model in cerebellum integrates prediction and feedback
  - Disruption → loss of agency, passivity experiences
- **Tenet alignment**: Provides mechanistic substrate—efference copy may be where consciousness "reads" whether selection succeeded
- **Quote**: "Sense of agency arises from the contingency of intentions, motor predictions and the sensory feedback or action outcome"

### SMA/Pre-SMA and Action Initiation
- **URL**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supplementary_motor_area
- **Type**: Encyclopedia review
- **Key points**:
  - SMA: internally generated (vs stimulus-driven) movement initiation
  - Pre-SMA: action selection, sequence preparation, conflict resolution
  - Readiness potential generated in SMA/pre-SMA
  - SMA stimulation can produce movement, urge to move, or speech arrest
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral—identifies neural substrate but doesn't determine causal direction
- **Quote**: "The supplementary motor area is involved in the initiation of internally generated as opposed to stimulus driven movement"

### Sjöberg - SMA Surgery and Free Will (2024)
- **URL**: https://academic.oup.com/brain/article/147/7/2267/7685995
- **Type**: Clinical neuroscience review
- **Key points**:
  - SMA resection shows RP is "completely irrelevant" to free will debate
  - Patients with SMA resection retain sense of voluntary action
  - Schurger's stochastic model is cited as alternative
  - Findings challenge Libet's interpretation
- **Tenet alignment**: Strongly aligns—removes apparent neuroscience challenge to conscious causation
- **Quote**: "Experiences of SMA surgery clearly demonstrate that the findings made by Libet et al. are in fact completely irrelevant to the neuroscientific discussion about free will"

## Major Positions

### Position 1: Neural Determinism
- **Proponents**: Sam Harris, Daniel Wegner
- **Core claim**: Free will is an illusion; RP shows decisions are made unconsciously before awareness
- **Key arguments**: Libet timing, Soon et al. 10-second prediction, causal closure
- **Relation to site tenets**: Conflicts with Bidirectional Interaction; site response via Schurger critique and retrocausality

### Position 2: Compatibilist Free Will
- **Proponents**: Daniel Dennett, Frankfurt
- **Core claim**: Free will = acting from endorsed desires without coercion; determinism irrelevant
- **Key arguments**: We care about identification with reasons, not ultimate origination
- **Relation to site tenets**: Site rejects as insufficient—doesn't address whether consciousness contributes causally

### Position 3: Libertarian Agent Causation
- **Proponents**: Chisholm, Lowe, (site's framework)
- **Core claim**: Agent as substance directly causes actions; not reducible to event chains
- **Key arguments**: Phenomenology of effort, self-stultification problem for epiphenomenalism
- **Relation to site tenets**: Aligns with Dualism, Bidirectional Interaction

### Position 4: Quantum Free Will (Stapp)
- **Proponents**: Henry Stapp, Jeffrey Schwartz
- **Core claim**: Consciousness operates through quantum Zeno effect; effort = observation rate
- **Key arguments**: QZE mechanism, neuroplasticity evidence, phenomenology match
- **Relation to site tenets**: Central to Minimal Quantum Interaction

### Position 5: Stochastic Model (Schurger)
- **Proponents**: Aaron Schurger
- **Core claim**: RP is artifact of threshold-crossing on accumulated noise; neutral on free will
- **Key arguments**: Accumulator model fits RP shape and wait-time distribution
- **Relation to site tenets**: Removes apparent evidence against conscious causation; opens space for site's framework

## Key Debates

### Debate 1: Does the Readiness Potential Reflect Decision or Noise?
- **Sides**: Libet interpretation (RP = unconscious decision) vs Schurger interpretation (RP = stochastic artifact)
- **Core disagreement**: Whether RP has cognitive significance for volition
- **Current state**: Schurger model increasingly accepted; 2024 reviews cite it favorably

### Debate 2: Free Will vs Free Won't
- **Sides**: Initiation model (consciousness starts action) vs Veto model (consciousness stops prepared action)
- **Core disagreement**: Where in action sequence consciousness contributes
- **Current state**: Veto model preserves a role for consciousness; site could accept either

### Debate 3: Motor Intention—Parietal or Frontal?
- **Sides**: Parietal origin (Desmurget) vs SMA/pre-SMA origin (Libet tradition)
- **Core disagreement**: Where conscious motor intention emerges
- **Current state**: Evidence for dissociation—parietal = intention, frontal = execution

### Debate 4: Decoherence Objection to Quantum Free Will
- **Sides**: Tegmark (decoherence kills quantum effects in brain) vs Hameroff/Stapp (biology preserves coherence)
- **Core disagreement**: Whether quantum coherence survives long enough to matter
- **Current state**: 2024-2025 evidence of biological quantum effects weakens Tegmark position
