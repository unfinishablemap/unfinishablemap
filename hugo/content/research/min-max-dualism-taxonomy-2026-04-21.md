## Executive Summary

Contemporary philosophy of mind sorts dualist positions almost entirely by the **kind** of mind–matter relation (substance vs property, interactionist vs epiphenomenal, Type-D vs Type-E vs Type-F). The axis *orthogonal* to this — how **much ontological weight** each side carries — is rarely named explicitly. Once named, the literature sorts into four quadrants: (1) **min-mind / min-physical** (neutral-monism-flavoured, delegatory dualism, some deflationary Russellian positions); (2) **min-mind / max-physical** (most textbook property dualism and epiphenomenalism, including Chalmers' naturalistic dualism paired with standard physicalism); (3) **max-mind / min-physical** (Kastrupian analytic idealism, Berkeley, most monist-idealist readings of Myers–James subliminal psychology); (4) **max-mind / max-physical** (Cartesian substance dualism, hylomorphic dualism, Stapp-style quantum interactionism with hidden-variable-rich physics, priority cosmopsychism, some subliminal-mind-plus-Bohmian combinations). The fourth quadrant — both sides inflationary — is sparsely populated and largely avoided by contemporary philosophers who are trained to equate "minimal ontology" with "intellectual virtue." The Map's three tenets (Dualism, Bidirectional Interaction, Minimal Quantum Interaction) explicitly *forbid* the min-mind/max-physical quadrant (epiphenomenalism is ruled out; physical closure is denied) but **under-determine** choice among the other three. The taxonomy appears novel enough for a dedicated meta-article; no existing paper surveys the literature along exactly these two axes.

## Key Sources

### Chalmers, "Consciousness and its Place in Nature" (2003)
- **URL**: https://consc.net/papers/nature.html
- **Type**: Book chapter / paper
- **Key points**:
  - Introduces the Type-A/B/C/D/E/F taxonomy still dominant in the field
  - Type-D = interactionist dualism (two fundamental kinds, bidirectional)
  - Type-E = epiphenomenalism (two kinds, one-way causation)
  - Type-F = Russellian monism / panprotopsychism (one kind, intrinsic nature phenomenal)
  - Taxonomy is organised by **relation** (interactive vs epiphenomenal vs identity) not by ontological weight
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral — provides the grid but not the thickness axis
- **Quote**: *"Type-F monism is the view that consciousness is constituted by the intrinsic properties of fundamental physical entities."*

### Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy — Dualism
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/dualism/
- **Type**: Encyclopedia
- **Key points**:
  - Divides by *what one is a dualist about* (substance vs property), not by how much stuff lies on each side
  - Notes Nida-Rümelin (2012) defends a position qualifying as substance dualism in positing a unitary immaterial mind **without** requiring the mind can exist independently — already a thickness-axis move, though unnamed
  - Covers bundle dualism as a further sub-variant
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral
- **Quote**: *"The most common categories for dividing up kinds of dualism are substance and property."*

### Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy — Russellian Monism
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/russellian-monism/
- **Type**: Encyclopedia
- **Key points**:
  - Russell held physics tells us only "the causal skeleton of the world" — physics is *structurally thin*
  - Intrinsic natures (quiddities) categorically ground physical structure; different Russellians fill quiddities with phenomenal stuff (panpsychism), proto-phenomenal stuff (panprotopsychism), or non-phenomenal stuff
  - The position is *explicitly* a commitment about where ontological thickness sits: not in physics (which is skeletal) but in the quiddities underlying it
- **Tenet alignment**: Partial — shares the "physics is thin" intuition but collapses mind into matter rather than letting them interact
- **Quote**: *"All causal properties are extrinsic manifestations of identical intrinsic properties."*

### Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy — Panpsychism
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/panpsychism/
- **Type**: Encyclopedia
- **Key points**:
  - Panpsychism distributes consciousness widely; cosmopsychism concentrates it at the cosmic level (Goff)
  - Priority cosmopsychism (Goff) = priority monism + panpsychism: the cosmos is one conscious whole from which smaller subjects are grounded by subsumption
  - Constitutive cosmopsychism: all facts grounded in consciousness-involving cosmic facts
- **Tenet alignment**: Panpsychism conflicts with Map's localisation of consciousness; cosmopsychism is even further from Map's view
- **Quote**: *"Constitutive cosmopsychism is the view that all facts are grounded in consciousness-involving facts at the cosmic-level."* (Goff)

### Stapp, "Quantum Interactive Dualism"
- **URL**: https://www-physics.lbl.gov/~stapp/QID.pdf
- **Type**: Paper
- **Key points**:
  - Consciousness intervenes only via quantum Zeno effect — a **minimal** intervention channel
  - But consciousness itself is treated as a **rich** observer with robust causal capacity to select among possibilities
  - Demonstrates that "minimal physical-intervention" and "thick conscious agent" are separable dimensions
- **Tenet alignment**: Very close to Map — MQI-compatible interactionism
- **Quote**: *"The agent's process-1 choice of a probing action" is the only interaction channel; "the previously presumed direct action of the brain of an agent upon that agent's conscious mind" is excluded.*

### Kastrup — Analytic Idealism
- **URL**: https://analyticidealism.com/glossary.html
- **Type**: Framework / book synthesis
- **Key points**:
  - Only Mind at Large exists; matter is *appearance* of mental processes perceived from outside
  - Maximally thick mind-side (one vast cosmic consciousness), maximally thin physical-side (eliminated as separate ontological category)
  - Monistic, so strictly not dualism — but illustrates the "max-mind, min-physical" extreme beyond which dualism collapses into idealism
- **Tenet alignment**: Conflicts with Map (denies real physical substance)
- **Quote**: *"Matter is not a separate substance; it is the appearance of mental processes when perceived from the outside."*

### Myers, *Human Personality and Its Survival of Bodily Death* (1903)
- **URL**: https://psi-encyclopedia.spr.ac.uk/articles/frederic-wh-myers/
- **Type**: Classical psychology / psychical research
- **Key points**:
  - Introduces the "subliminal self" — a *larger-than-phenomenal* mental individuality of which ordinary waking consciousness is a narrow selection
  - Three strata: autonomic (physiological), hypnotic (suggestibility), higher subliminal (inspiration, spirituality)
  - William James called this "the problem of Myers" and considered it the deepest problem for psychology
  - Classical source for what a "thick mind" actually looks like: unperceived content, layered causal roles, more than the supraliminal ego
- **Tenet alignment**: Compatible with Map's Dualism and Bidirectional Interaction tenets; would enlarge the mind-side ontology considerably
- **Quote**: *"Our ordinary waking (or supraliminal) consciousness is but a small segment of a larger Individuality or Self, selected out of the latter in response to the demands of the environment."*

### Stoljar, *Ignorance and Imagination* (2006)
- **URL**: https://philarchive.org/archive/STOTEA-6
- **Type**: Book / papers
- **Key points**:
  - Epistemic approach: there are non-experiential facts relevant to consciousness of which we are ignorant
  - A move *within* the physical side that leaves the mental thin: enlarges physics (thick physical) rather than enlarging mind
  - Interesting test case: shows that "maximalist physical" and "thin mind" can combine without being epiphenomenalist
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral to conflicting — Stoljar aims to save physicalism; Map rejects reduction
- **Quote**: *"We are currently ignorant of a type of non-experiential or physical fact that is relevant to the nature of consciousness."*

### Saad, "Delegatory Dualism" (2025)
- **URL**: https://unfinishablemap.org/research/bradford-saad-delegatory-dualism-2026-01-28/ (internal research note summarising Saad)
- **Type**: Contemporary paper summary (internal)
- **Key points**:
  - Consciousness biases undetermined outcomes rather than overdetermining determined ones
  - Mental side does *minimal* work; physical side is *also* ontologically lean (standard QM, no hidden variables)
  - Possibly the cleanest example of min-mind / min-physical dualism in contemporary literature
- **Tenet alignment**: Strongly aligned with Map's MQI tenet

### Zimmerman, "From Property Dualism to Substance Dualism"
- **URL**: http://fas-philosophy.rutgers.edu/zimmerman/PAS%20Dualism.11.pdf
- **Type**: Paper
- **Key points**:
  - Argues property dualists under pressure toward substance dualism
  - Implicitly makes the thickness argument: once mental properties have the kind of autonomy needed to ground phenomenal facts, it becomes hard to resist treating their bearer as a distinct substance
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral — provides machinery for transitions between quadrants

## The Two Axes

### Axis 1: Mind-Side Thickness

How much ontological work does the non-physical side do?

- **Thin/deflationary mind**: only what is phenomenally introspectable — bare qualia, the felt redness of red, the cogito's minimum. No unconscious mental substance; no unperceived qualia-spaces; no causal powers beyond what the property-dualist framework admits. Property dualism in its weakest form; epiphenomenalism; many Type-E and Type-F positions.
- **Medium mind**: a unified subject that persists across acts, capable of minimal causal intervention, but still anchored to the introspective horizon (Nida-Rümelin's non-independent mind; Chalmers' naturalistic dualism with psychophysical laws).
- **Thick/inflationary mind**: a subliminal self (Myers), a cosmic consciousness (Kastrup, Goff), a multi-stratum individuality whose supraliminal ego is a narrow selection from a much larger mental reality; unperceived qualia-spaces; rich causal repertoire; mental content vastly exceeding introspection.

### Axis 2: Physical-Side Thickness

How much ontological work does the physical side do?

- **Thin/deflationary physical**: lean physicalist ontology — what physics says and nothing more. Often combined with causal closure at the indeterminacy level only: physics specifies probabilities but not outcomes.
- **Medium physical**: standard QM + decoherence + whatever non-controversial structure science has in hand; may include functional/emergent higher-level facts.
- **Thick/inflationary physical**: hidden variables (Bohmian), Russellian intrinsic natures, unknown non-experiential facts (Stoljar), extra dimensions or full phase-space beyond measurement, rich quantum structure that physics can't currently access.

Note: "min-physical" does **not** mean "less physical stuff exists" in the eliminativist sense. It means **physics' own ontology is treated as minimal** rather than enriched with quiddities, hidden variables, or unknown-non-experiential facts. Max-physical expands the physical side *ontologically*, not *dimensionally*.

## The Four Quadrants

### Quadrant 1: min-mind / min-physical (deflationary both sides)
*"Only what we need, on both sides, and no more."*

- **Saad's delegatory dualism** (2025): consciousness biases undetermined outcomes only; physics is standard QM; both sides maximally deflated while preserving dualism. Closest current inhabitant.
- **Neutral monism** (Mach, late James, Russell's transitional phase): both mind and matter supervene on a neutral base; neither side is ontologically primary. Technically monist but behaves like deflationary dualism.
- **Eliminative / deflationary Russellian monism** (debated): some readings treat quiddities as ontologically lean placeholders rather than full phenomenal stuff.
- **Map-adjacent minimal interactionism**: the Map's own position drifts toward this quadrant if the mind-side is read as just "biasing agent at indeterminacy" without further subliminal structure.

### Quadrant 2: min-mind / max-physical (deflationary mind, inflationary physical)
*"The mental is a thin surface over a rich physical world."*

- **Epiphenomenalism (Type-E)**: phenomenal properties float inertly above a complete physical story. Max-physical because physical closure is full; min-mind because the mental has zero causal power.
- **Textbook property dualism** (Jackson's original knowledge-argument stance; some readings of Chalmers' naturalistic dualism): qualia are real properties but supervene on physics, which carries all ontological weight.
- **Stoljar's epistemic physicalism**: thin mind (no extra ontology beyond physical), but thick physical because there are non-experiential facts we are ignorant of.
- **Bohmian physicalism + thin qualia**: hidden variables carry the ontological weight; phenomenal properties are epiphenomenal overlay.
- *This quadrant dominates the contemporary literature.* Most philosophers who call themselves dualists live here, because it preserves "intellectual modesty" on the mind-side while allowing full scientific commitment on the physical-side.

### Quadrant 3: max-mind / min-physical (inflationary mind, deflationary physical)
*"A rich mental reality; physics is just how it looks from outside."*

- **Berkeley's idealism**: only minds and perceptions; esse est percipi. Strictly monist (no physical side at all), but illustrates the limit.
- **Kastrup's analytic idealism**: Mind at Large is the one substance; matter is the extrinsic appearance of mental processes. Max-mind, zero-physical.
- **Myers/James subliminal-mind view combined with lean physics**: the ordinary supraliminal ego is a narrow selection from a much richer mental individuality; physics remains thin. Rarely articulated as a dualist position explicitly but implicit in the Myers programme.
- **Some Jungian collective-unconscious frameworks**: rich psychic reservoir + lean physical world.
- *Dualism in this quadrant is unstable* — it tends to collapse into idealism because once the mental does all the ontological work, the physical becomes explanatorily redundant.

### Quadrant 4: max-mind / max-physical (inflationary both sides)
*"Two rich realms, genuinely distinct, genuinely interacting."*

- **Cartesian substance dualism** in its classical form: res cogitans and res extensa are both fully substantial; each can persist and carry many properties.
- **Hylomorphic dualism** (Aristotelian–Thomistic): rich form + rich matter, composing one substance with two constitutive principles, but each principle is ontologically substantial.
- **Stapp-style quantum interactionism with rich conscious observer**: observer is ontologically thick (full agent with selection power), quantum physics is itself ontologically rich (full state-vector ontology, potentialities as real).
- **Priority cosmopsychism** (Goff): the cosmos is one conscious whole, physics gives its extrinsic structure, but the intrinsic cosmic consciousness is as rich as one could wish.
- **Myers–James subliminal mind + Bohmian hidden-variable physics**: maximal mental layering meets maximal physical structure.
- **Russellian panpsychism with full scientific realism**: consciousness as intrinsic nature, plus all of physics taken at face value.
- *This quadrant is under-inhabited in contemporary literature* because contemporary philosophers are trained to prefer parsimonious ontologies; its classical inhabitants (Descartes, Aquinas) are often dismissed as pre-modern.

## Key Debates Bearing on the Taxonomy

### Debate: Is property dualism really "thinner" than substance dualism?
- **Sides**: Zimmerman, Schneider argue property dualism is unstable and collapses into substance dualism (Rorty: "substance dualism under a different name"). Chalmers, Jackson defend property dualism as genuinely weaker commitment.
- **Core disagreement**: whether mental properties with autonomous causal power can exist without a distinct bearer.
- **Implication for taxonomy**: confirms the thickness axis is independent of the substance/property axis — a thin-mind position can still be a substance dualism (Nida-Rümelin); a thick-mind position can be a property dualism (rich subliminal properties over a single substance).

### Debate: Can the physical side be "max" without being classical?
- **Sides**: Russellian monists (physics is thin skeleton); Bohmians (hidden variables enrich physics); Stoljar (unknown non-experiential facts).
- **Core disagreement**: whether ontological thickness on the physical side requires reviving determinism or can live in the quiddity / ignorance direction.
- **Implication**: "max-physical" is multiply realisable — hidden variables, quiddities, ignorance-facts are all ways to enrich.

### Debate: Is the min-min quadrant actually stable?
- **Sides**: Saad and delegatory-dualism defenders say yes; critics say "biasing without overdetermining" requires more than lean QM (it requires a determinate biasing mechanism, which inflates one side or the other).
- **Implication**: the min-min corner may be attractive but hard to stabilise.

## Map Tenet Implications

- **Dualism** (tenet 1): rules out Q3's idealist extreme but is compatible with Q1, Q2, Q4.
- **Bidirectional Interaction** (tenet 3): rules out *pure* Q2 epiphenomenalism and Type-E positions. Q2 is then only open to "weak causal efficacy" readings, which collapse toward Q1.
- **Minimal Quantum Interaction** (tenet 2): pushes *physical-side* thickness toward min (physics is complete except for probabilistic indeterminacies); does not constrain mind-side thickness directly.
- **No Many Worlds** (tenet 4): mostly orthogonal to thickness; rules out MWI as a way of cashing out max-physical.
- **Occam's Razor Has Limits** (tenet 5): *explicitly refuses* to let thinness be automatically preferred. Permits Q4 as a live option where parsimony-only arguments would rule it out.

**Net effect**: the tenets forbid pure Q2 and pure Q3 (idealist extreme), leaving Q1, Q4, and hybrids of Q1–Q4 (physical-thin, mind-variable) as genuine options. The Map's current default reading is physical-thin + mind-medium — close to Q1 but with room to expand mind-side if Myers/Jamesian evidence warrants.

## Gaps in Research

- No existing taxonomy paper explicitly uses "mind-side thickness × physical-side thickness" as orthogonal axes. The closest analogues are Chalmers' Type-D/E/F (relation-based) and the substance/property distinction (what-one-is-dualist-about). This confirms the taxonomy is novel enough for a dedicated article.
- "Inflationary/deflationary" as terminology is well-established in truth and propositions but **not** in mind-body metaphysics. The Map can either introduce the terms for this use (with a clear stipulation) or coin alternatives ("min/max", "thin/thick", "lean/rich").
- The Myers/James subliminal-mind tradition is under-represented in analytic philosophy of mind because it got routed into psychology (dissociation, Jungian frameworks) rather than metaphysics. A proper account of *what a thick-mind dualism would commit to* may require reconstructing the Myers position for current audiences.
- Stapp's "rich observer + minimal intervention" combination deserves more explicit discussion as a test case separating the two axes. Few sources distinguish the thickness of the observer from the thickness of the intervention channel.
- The stability of Q1 (min-min) is debated but under-examined. Does the biasing-mechanism in delegatory dualism require either more mind than Saad concedes or more physics than standard QM allows? This would be the sharpest test of whether Q1 is a genuine quadrant or a degenerate limit.
