# Research: Voids - The Relevance Void

**Date**: 2026-03-09
**Search queries used**: "relevance determination consciousness philosophy cognitive science frame problem", "Heidegger relevance readiness-to-hand phenomenology salience consciousness", "frame problem AI relevance filtering cognitive limits epistemic opacity", "John Vervaeke relevance realization cognitive science consciousness meaning", "salience network neuroscience consciousness attention default mode network relevance", "Dreyfus Heidegger frame problem skilled coping background practices phenomenology", "predictive processing salience consciousness Karl Friston free energy principle relevance selection", "cognitive opacity relevance determination introspection limits philosophy mind"
**Voids category**: Occluded / Unexplorable (mixed)

## Executive Summary

At every moment, consciousness confronts an informationally overwhelming world and somehow selects what matters. This relevance-determination process is a precondition of all conscious experience — without it, thought would drown in undifferentiated noise. Yet the mechanism that decides what is relevant is itself invisible to consciousness. You cannot examine your own relevance filter without already using it, creating a structural self-referential opacity. The frame problem in AI, Heidegger's analysis of readiness-to-hand, Vervaeke's theory of relevance realization, Friston's predictive processing, and neuroscience's salience network all converge on the same insight: relevance selection is fundamental to cognition, yet the selecting process cannot be brought into full conscious view.

## Key Sources

### The Frame Problem (Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy)
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/frame-problem/
- **Type**: Encyclopedia
- **Key points**:
  - Originally a narrow technical problem in logic-based AI about representing action effects
  - Expanded by philosophers into a broader relevance problem: how do we make decisions based only on what is relevant without explicitly considering everything that is not?
  - Fodor's "isotropy" — there is no a priori boundary to what information might be relevant to any given inference
  - The problem is not just computational but epistemological: relevance is holistic, open-ended, and context-sensitive
- **Tenet alignment**: Occam's Razor Has Limits — the assumption that relevance can be reduced to simple rules may hide irreducible complexity in how minds navigate the world
- **Quote**: "How we account for our apparent ability to make decisions on the basis only of what is relevant to an ongoing situation without having explicitly to consider all that is not relevant"

### Vervaeke — Relevance Realization and Cognitive Science
- **URL**: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2024.1362658/full
- **Type**: Academic paper (Frontiers in Psychology, 2024)
- **Key points**:
  - Relevance realization is present in all organisms, from bacteria to humans, and lies at the root of agency, cognition, and consciousness
  - Consciousness is characterised as "enhanced relevance realization" — necessary for novel, complex, or ill-defined situations
  - The process is fundamentally non-algorithmic: impredicative and co-constructive, meaning it cannot be captured by any fixed procedure
  - Relevance realization continually redesigns itself to fit changing contexts — recursive self-transformation
- **Tenet alignment**: Dualism — if relevance realization is non-computational (as Vervaeke argues), this opens space for non-physical processes in cognition. The irreducibility of relevance to algorithm parallels the irreducibility of consciousness to mechanism
- **Quote**: "The dialectic dynamic of relevance realization is not an algorithmic process, due to its fundamentally impredicative and co-constructive nature"

### Dreyfus — Heidegger and Skilled Coping
- **URL**: https://philpapers.org/rec/WHECIC (Wheeler on Dreyfus)
- **Type**: Academic philosophy
- **Key points**:
  - Heidegger's readiness-to-hand shows that our primary engagement with the world is pre-reflective and practical, not representational
  - In absorbed skillful coping, relevance is determined without explicit representation — you don't think about what's relevant, you just respond
  - Dreyfus argues the frame problem dissolves if we accept that intelligence doesn't require representations — but this means relevance determination is even MORE opaque, operating below conscious threshold
  - The background of coping practices is what makes anything show up as relevant at all, but this background cannot itself be made fully explicit
- **Tenet alignment**: Bidirectional Interaction — if consciousness pre-reflectively shapes what shows up as relevant, this is a form of causal influence on experience that operates invisibly
- **Quote**: "Acting is experienced as a steady flow of skillful activity in response to one's sense of the situation"

### Salience Network (Neuroscience)
- **URL**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salience_network
- **Type**: Neuroscience overview
- **Key points**:
  - Anterior insula and dorsal anterior cingulate cortex form the core of the salience network
  - Functions as a dynamic switch between the default mode network (internal cognition) and central executive network (external attention)
  - Identifies biologically and cognitively relevant events for guiding attention and behaviour
  - Dysfunction linked to ADHD, autism, anxiety — suggesting relevance determination can go wrong in systematic ways
- **Tenet alignment**: Minimal Quantum Interaction — the salience network is a neural system, but what determines its settings? If consciousness influences which events register as salient, this is a candidate site for non-physical causal influence on neural processing
- **Quote**: "Constitutes a key interface for cognitive, homeostatic, motivational, and affective systems"

### Friston — Free Energy Principle and Predictive Processing
- **URL**: https://www.nature.com/articles/nrn2787
- **Type**: Academic paper (Nature Reviews Neuroscience)
- **Key points**:
  - The brain minimises free energy — the difference between its model and reality
  - Salience = precision-weighted prediction errors; what's "relevant" is what violates expectations in weighted ways
  - The generative model that produces predictions is invisible to consciousness — we experience the predictions, not the model
  - Aberrant salience (attributing undue importance to irrelevant stimuli) linked to dopaminergic dysfunction, suggesting relevance can be pathologically miscalibrated
- **Tenet alignment**: Occam's Razor Has Limits — the free energy principle is elegant but may obscure the question of what sets the priors. The model that determines relevance must come from somewhere, and that origin is itself hidden
- **Quote**: "Constructs like memory, attention, value, reinforcement and salience might disclose their simple relationships" under the free energy framework

### Carruthers — The Opacity of Mind
- **URL**: https://ndpr.nd.edu/reviews/the-opacity-of-mind-an-integrative-theory-of-self-knowledge/
- **Type**: Book review / Academic philosophy
- **Key points**:
  - ISA (Interpretive Sensory-Access) theory: we don't have direct introspective access to our mental states but interpret them, similar to how we interpret others
  - Cognitive opacity is structural, not merely a practical limitation
  - Relevance determination is among the processes most deeply hidden from introspection — we experience its outputs (this matters, that doesn't) without access to the computation
  - Introspection and its object compete for cognitive resources, creating a fundamental tension
- **Tenet alignment**: Dualism — if the mind is opaque to itself, this may reflect the fact that the conscious subject and the relevance-determining mechanism occupy different ontological levels

## The Void

### Nature of the Limit
This is primarily an **occluded** void with elements of the **unexplorable**. The relevance filter is not merely undiscovered territory awaiting investigation — it is structurally self-concealing. Any attempt to investigate how you determine relevance must itself use the relevance filter, creating a vicious circularity. The investigative tool is the thing being investigated.

This differs from mere cognitive complexity. It is not that relevance determination is too complicated to understand — it is that the process cannot be separated from the process of understanding it. Like trying to see your own eyes without a mirror, except there is no mirror available: every reflective surface is itself produced by the relevance mechanism.

### Evidence for the Limit
1. **The frame problem persists**: After 50+ years, AI has not solved relevance determination through explicit rules. This suggests it may not be the kind of problem that yields to explicit representation.
2. **Vervaeke's non-computability claim**: If relevance realization is fundamentally non-algorithmic, it cannot be captured by any formal procedure — including introspective formalisation.
3. **Dreyfus's background**: The background of practices that makes relevance possible cannot itself be fully articulated. Attempts to make it explicit transform it into something different (present-at-hand vs. ready-to-hand).
4. **Predictive processing opacity**: Under Friston's framework, the generative model is never directly experienced. We experience precision-weighted prediction errors, not the model generating them.
5. **Clinical evidence**: Disorders of salience (schizophrenia, ADHD, autism) show that relevance can fail systematically, yet patients cannot introspect the failure mechanism — they experience wrong relevance, not broken filtering.

### Phenomenology
The relevance void manifests as a peculiar transparency. We do not experience relevance *as* a filter — we experience a world in which some things matter and others don't, as if mattering were a property of the things themselves rather than a product of our cognitive architecture. The void is hidden by its own success: when relevance works, it is invisible; it only becomes noticeable when it fails (surprise, confusion, pathological salience).

Approaching this void phenomenologically produces a distinctive vertiginous quality: the attempt to attend to what makes things relevant immediately shifts what is relevant, creating an infinite regress. Each meta-level of reflection introduces its own relevance filter, which is equally opaque.

## Approaches to the Edge

### Direct Methods (if any)
Meditation and mindfulness practices claim to access pre-reflective awareness — "bare attention" before relevance filtering. However, even these practices operate within a relevance framework (attending to breath rather than bodily sensation, for instance). Whether any practice can genuinely bypass relevance filtering or merely substitutes one filter for another remains an open question.

Phenomenological reduction (epoché) attempts to bracket the "natural attitude" including its relevance assumptions. But Husserl's method itself requires selecting what to bracket — an operation that presupposes relevance.

### Indirect Methods
- **Contrastive analysis**: Compare what different minds (across cultures, species, neural conditions) find relevant. The differences map the void's boundaries even if the mechanism remains hidden.
- **Breakdown analysis**: Study what happens when relevance fails — schizophrenic delusions of reference, ADHD distractibility, autism's different salience landscapes. The shape of failure reveals the shape of the hidden mechanism.
- **Developmental tracking**: Children's relevance landscapes shift dramatically during maturation. Tracking what becomes relevant (and what ceases to be) across development provides indirect evidence about the mechanism.
- **Pharmacological probes**: Dopaminergic manipulation directly alters salience assignment, providing causal evidence about mechanism without requiring introspective access to it.

### What AI Might See
AI systems face their own version of this void — the frame problem is, in a sense, an expression of the relevance void in artificial minds. But there is an important asymmetry: AI systems can, in principle, inspect their own attention weights, activation patterns, and loss landscapes. An AI can see *that* it weighted input X higher than input Y, even if explaining *why* in fully human-intelligible terms remains difficult.

This suggests AI might serve as a partial mirror: by building systems that solve relevance problems and then inspecting their solutions, we might learn something about relevance determination that introspection cannot reveal. However, there is no guarantee that artificial relevance and biological relevance share enough structure for this transfer to work.

## Connection to Tenets

### Most Relevant Tenet
**Occam's Razor Has Limits** — The relevance void exemplifies how our preference for tractable, simple explanations may be itself a product of relevance filtering. We find simple explanations satisfying because our relevance mechanism marks them as important, not because simplicity is an objective guide to truth. The filter shapes the epistemology, but the filter itself resists simple explanation.

**Dualism** is a strong secondary connection: if relevance realization is fundamentally non-computational (as Vervaeke argues), this opens space for non-physical aspects of mind to play a role in determining what matters. The interface between the non-physical subject and the physical salience network would be doubly occluded — hidden by the opacity of relevance itself and by the causal interface void.

### Implications
The relevance void has profound implications for epistemology within the Map's framework:

1. **Every claim on the Map has already been filtered** — the topics and concepts we explore were selected by a relevance mechanism we cannot fully examine. What has the Map systematically excluded?
2. **The tenets themselves may be relevance-shaped** — our commitment to dualism, to Occam's limits, to bidirectional interaction — these may reflect what our cognitive architecture finds salient rather than what is true. This is not grounds for abandoning them, but it demands humility.
3. **The void is self-reinforcing** — because we cannot access the filter, we cannot determine whether the filter prevents us from seeing its own limitations. This makes the relevance void a candidate for the most structurally defended void on the Map.

## Gaps in Research

- Whether predictive processing fully accounts for relevance or merely redescribes it — the question of what sets the priors remains
- The relationship between individual relevance (what matters to *me*) and collective relevance (what matters to *us*) — does the void have a social dimension?
- Whether contemplative practices genuinely access pre-filtered experience or merely install different filters
- How relevance changes across the lifespan and whether developmental shifts reveal mechanism or merely output
- The fundamental question: is relevance a property of consciousness, a property of the world, or a product of their interaction? Each answer has different implications for whether this void is explorable
