## Executive Summary

Michael Levin's research programme argues that goal-directed, memory-bearing, problem-solving competency is present in non-neural biological tissue, and that the substrate for much of this competency is *bioelectric* — standing patterns of resting membrane voltage (Vmem) across cell collectives that store and impose anatomical target states. Empirically, Levin's lab has (a) rewritten planarian body plans by editing bioelectric pattern memory rather than the genome, producing stably two-headed worms; (b) induced complete ectopic eyes in Xenopus by imposing a hyperpolarization signature on non-eye tissue; and (c) with Bongard, Kriegman and Blackiston, built "xenobots" — computer-designed proto-organisms assembled from frog skin and heart cells that move, navigate, and (2021) undergo *kinematic self-replication*. Levin frames all of this as "basal cognition" or "cognition all the way down" (with Dennett), formalized in the "cognitive light cone" and the TAME framework (2022).

The Map-critical point: **Levin explicitly brackets phenomenal sentience.** His "cognition" is a functional/engineering stance — a claim about competencies (goals, memory, anticipation, error-correction) that is deliberately agnostic about, and does not assert, felt experience. This makes the programme a source of *agency-without-experience* evidence that pressures the Map's valence-and-conscious-selection commitments from the opposite side to the plant/Physarum cases: not "does simple life feel?" but "does sophisticated goal-pursuit require feeling at all?" If robust agency-language applies to voltage gradients in a flatworm stump, then agency- and teleology-talk cannot by themselves license attributions of consciousness. That decoupling is usable *for* the Map: it sharpens the boundary the Map already tries to draw between functional competency and phenomenal presence.

### Technological Approach to Mind Everywhere (TAME)
- **URL**: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/systems-neuroscience/articles/10.3389/fnsys.2022.768201/full (also arXiv:2201.10346; PMC8988303)
- **Citation**: Levin, M. (2022). "Technological Approach to Mind Everywhere: An Experimentally-Grounded Framework for Understanding Diverse Bodies and Minds." *Frontiers in Systems Neuroscience*, 16, 768201. DOI 10.3389/fnsys.2022.768201.
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed framework paper
- **Key points**:
  - Proposes a scale-free framework for recognizing and comparing "diverse intelligences" across evolved, engineered, and hybrid substrates.
  - Cognition is treated as a matter of degree defined by competencies, not a binary reserved for brains.
  - Explicitly an engineering/pragmatic stance aimed at prediction and control, not a metaphysical claim about phenomenal consciousness.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral-to-tension. Substrate-independent *competency* is compatible with the Map, but only because Levin decouples it from experience; it becomes a *conflict* only if read (against Levin's own framing) as substrate-independent *consciousness*.

### Cognition all the way down (Levin & Dennett)
- **URL**: https://aeon.co/essays/how-to-understand-cells-tissues-and-organisms-as-agents-with-agendas
- **Citation**: Levin, M., & Dennett, D. C. (2020). "Cognition all the way down." *Aeon Essays*, 13 October 2020.
- **Type**: Popular/essayistic (co-authored by a major philosopher of mind)

- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral. The instrumentalist framing is precisely the "agency without experience" wedge the Map can use.

### The Computational Boundary of a "Self" (cognitive light cone)
- **URL**: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2019.02688/full (PMC6923654)
- **Citation**: Levin, M. (2019). "The Computational Boundary of a 'Self': Developmental Bioelectricity Drives Multicellularity and Scale-Free Cognition." *Frontiers in Psychology*, 10, 2688. DOI 10.3389/fpsyg.2019.02688.
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed framework paper
- **Key points**:
  - Introduces the **cognitive light cone**: the spatiotemporal scale of goals an agent can represent and pursue (a schematic of *goal* space, not sensing/effector range).
  - Bioelectric signalling is proposed as the mechanism that binds many cells into a single larger "self" with larger goals — scaling the light cone.
  - Examples ladder from ticks (tiny cone) to dogs to humans.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral-to-tension (same as TAME). The light cone is a functional boundary of goal-representation, not of felt selfhood.
- **Verify status**: VERIFIED.

### A scalable pipeline for designing reconfigurable organisms (xenobots, 2020)
- **URL**: https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.1910837117
- **Citation**: Kriegman, S., Blackiston, D., Levin, M., & Bongard, J. (2020). "A scalable pipeline for designing reconfigurable organisms." *PNAS*, 117(4), 1853–1859. DOI 10.1073/pnas.1910837117.
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed empirical paper
- **Key points**:
  - Evolutionary algorithm designs body plans in simulation; best designs are physically built from *Xenopus laevis* skin and cardiac cells.
  - Resulting "xenobots" locomote (driven by heart-cell contraction), navigate, push payloads, and self-repair after damage — behaviour not exhibited by the cells' native frog anatomy.
  - Demonstrates that frog cells, freed from the usual embryonic context, spontaneously build a novel functional organism — evidence for plastic, context-independent morphogenetic competency.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral. Bears on agency/competency, not directly on consciousness. Relevant as a limit case for "novel goal-directed behaviour with no nervous system to speak of."
- **Verify status**: VERIFIED (authors, title, journal, vol 117 issue 4, pages 1853–1859, DOI).

### Kinematic self-replication in reconfigurable organisms (xenobots, 2021)
- **URL**: https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.2112672118
- **Citation**: Kriegman, S., Blackiston, D., Levin, M., & Bongard, J. (2021). "Kinematic self-replication in reconfigurable organisms." *PNAS*, 118(49), e2112672118. DOI 10.1073/pnas.2112672118.
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed empirical paper
- **Key points**:
  - Xenobots gather loose stem cells in the dish and pile them into new xenobots — *kinematic* (assembly-based) self-replication, distinct from any known animal/plant reproduction.
  - AI-optimized "C"/Pac-Man shapes dramatically increase replication yield.
  - Reframes "reproduction" as an emergent behaviour of a designed cell collective, not a genetically-scripted life cycle.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral. Strengthens the agency-without-nervous-system case; no phenomenality claimed or implied.
- **Verify status**: VERIFIED via search indexers (authors, title, PNAS 118(49), DOI). Direct PNAS fetch returned HTTP 403 (paywall/bot-block), so metadata is cross-checked against Wikipedia, KROs project site, and PNAS listing rather than the article page itself.

### Long-Term, Stochastic Editing of Regenerative Anatomy (planarian bioelectric memory)
- **URL**: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0006349517304277 (PMC5443973)
- **Citation**: Durant, F., Morokuma, J., Fields, C., Williams, K., Adams, D. S., & Levin, M. (2017). "Long-Term, Stochastic Editing of Regenerative Anatomy via Targeting Endogenous Bioelectric Gradients." *Biophysical Journal*, 112(10), 2231–2243. DOI 10.1016/j.bpj.2017.04.011.
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed empirical paper
- **Key points**:
  - Briefly interrupting gap-junctional communication (octanol) resets the planarian bioelectric circuit; some worms then regenerate as *two-headed* and continue producing two-headed offspring in later rounds of cutting with no further treatment.
  - The altered body plan is stored in the *bioelectric* network, not the genome — a rewritable, reversible anatomical "pattern memory."
  - Reversible with ion-channel/pump modulation — the memory is a stable attractor state of the voltage circuit.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral-to-supportive. A concrete non-genetic, non-neural *memory* substrate — a clean case of information storage decoupled from anything one would call experience.
- **Verify status**: VERIFIED (authors incl. Christopher Fields & Dany Spencer Adams, title, Biophysical Journal 112(10), page 2231, DOI).

### Transmembrane voltage controls embryonic eye patterning (ectopic eye induction)
- **URL**: https://journals.biologists.com/dev/article/139/2/313/45402 (DOI 10.1242/dev.077917; PMC3243095)
- **Citation**: Pai, V. P., Aw, S., Shomrat, T., Lemire, J. M., & Levin, M. (2012). "Transmembrane voltage potential controls embryonic eye patterning in *Xenopus laevis*." *Development*, 139(2), 313–323. DOI 10.1242/dev.077917.
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed empirical paper
- **Key points**:
  - A specific hyperpolarization signature marks the endogenous eye field; imposing that Vmem state on non-eye cells induces well-formed *ectopic* eyes, even far outside the anterior neural field.
  - Vmem acts as an *instructive* master signal (upstream of eye-field transcription factors), transduced via a Ca²⁺-channel pathway.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral. Bioelectric *instruction* of complex organ morphology; a mechanism story, not a mind story.
- **Verify status**: VERIFIED (authors, title, Development 139(2):313, DOI).

## Major Positions

### Levin's functional basal cognition (engineering/instrumentalist stance)
- **Proponents**: Michael Levin; Daniel Dennett (on the philosophical framing); collaborators Bongard, Kriegman, Blackiston, Fields.
- **Core claim**: Goal-directedness, memory, anticipation, and error-correction are real, measurable competencies present across scales and substrates — including single cells and non-neural tissue — and are best studied via an intentional/cybernetic stance. Bioelectric networks are a principal mechanism that scales small cellular goals into large anatomical ones.
- **Key arguments**:
  - Empirical: planarian pattern memory, ectopic eyes, xenobot behaviour and self-replication show competencies without the standard neural apparatus.
  - Methodological: agency-talk pays off in predictive/control power (find the "pressure points").
  - Conceptual (cognitive light cone / TAME): "mind" is a continuum of goal-scale, not a brain-gated kind.
- **Relation to site tenets**: The competency claims are largely orthogonal to the Map's dualism because **Levin brackets sentience**. He does not assert that a xenobot or a flatworm stump *feels*. This is the crucial reading to preserve. The programme is a *conflict* only under a misreading that equates competency with consciousness — a misreading the Map should explicitly reject and then exploit.

### The deflationary/critical reading
- **Proponents**: Various critics (e.g. the "Nope, it isn't cognition all the way down" line of pushback); more cautious cognitive scientists.
- **Core claim**: "Cognition" here is metaphor/redescription; calling homeostasis a "goal" and a voltage attractor a "memory" inflates cybernetic feedback into mentalistic language without earning it.
- **Key arguments**: Predictive payoff can be had with plain dynamical-systems vocabulary; agency-talk risks equivocation.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Convergent with the Map on one point — that agency-language does *not* entail experience. The Map need not adjudicate whether Levin's usage is "really" cognition; it only needs the weaker, widely-shared conclusion that *functional* goal-talk is silent on phenomenality.

## Key Debates

### Does substrate-independent competency imply substrate-independent consciousness?
- **Sides**: Naive extrapolation (competency scales → mind scales → maybe experience scales) vs. Levin's own careful bracketing (competency is measured; experience is left open).
- **Core disagreement**: Whether the continuum Levin draws in *goal-space* is also a continuum in *phenomenal* space.
- **Current state**: Levin repeatedly declines to assert phenomenal consciousness for his systems. The Map's position aligns with the bracketing: [substrate-independence](/concepts/substrate-independence/) of *function* is granted; substrate-independence of *experience* is exactly what the Map denies is settled by such evidence.

### Is "agency" in xenobots a discovery or a redescription?
- **Sides**: Emergent-novelty reading (genuinely new goal-directed organisms) vs. deflationary (impressive but ordinary cell mechanics under a mentalistic gloss).
- **Core disagreement**: How much theoretical weight "agency" can bear absent a nervous system.
- **Current state**: Ongoing; empirically the *behaviours* (locomotion, kinematic replication, self-repair) are not disputed, only their description.

## Connection to the Marginal-Organism Ladder / Valence-and-Conscious-Selection

This is the reason the subject was harvested, so it deserves explicit treatment in any downstream article:

- The Map's [marginal-organism-scope-of-value-sensitive-selection](/topics/marginal-organism-scope-of-value-sensitive-selection/) asks *where valence-denominated conscious selection bottoms out* (rat → nematode → Hydra → slime mould). That ladder is pressured **from the plant/Physarum side** by cases of apparent behaviour in the absence of nervous systems — tempting one to push the consciousness floor *lower*.
- Levin's programme pressures the *same* ladder from the **opposite side**: it supplies richly goal-directed, memory-bearing, self-replicating systems (flatworm bioelectric circuits, xenobots) for which sophisticated *agency* is uncontested but for which even the principal investigator declines to claim *experience*. This severs the inference "impressive goal-directed behaviour ⇒ probably conscious."
- Net effect for [valence-and-conscious-selection](/topics/valence-and-conscious-selection/): Levin's decoupling is *evidence for* the Map's structural commitment that value-sensitive *conscious* selection is a distinct question from functional value-tracking. A thermostat-like Vmem attractor "pursues" an anatomical target; nothing about that pursuit implies a felt valence. So the marginal-organism boundary should be drawn on phenomenal criteria, not competency criteria — otherwise the boundary marches all the way down to single cells and voltage gradients, which even sympathetic sources treat as functional, not felt.
- Practical upshot: the ladder in [graduated-middle-path-valence-modulated-attention](/topics/graduated-middle-path-valence-modulated-attention/) and the boundary-work in [marginal-organism-scope-of-value-sensitive-selection](/topics/marginal-organism-scope-of-value-sensitive-selection/) gain a *defeater for false positives* — a principled reason to discount agency/behaviour as evidence of valence, keeping the search focused on markers of experience rather than markers of competency.
