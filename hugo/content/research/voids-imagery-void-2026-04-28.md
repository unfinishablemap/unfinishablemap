## Executive Summary

The Imagery Void is the structural impossibility, for any conscious mind, of verifying the fidelity, vividness, or even the presence of its own mental images. Three converging lines of evidence—Galton's 1880 survey, the Würzburg "imageless thought" controversy, and the post-2015 explosion of aphantasia/hyperphantasia research—show that introspective imagery reports vary enormously across individuals (1% aphantasic, 3% hyperphantasic), that they correlate with measurable physiology yet cannot be cross-checked against any external standard, and that imagery's *presence or absence* fails to predict performance on classical "imagery" tasks like mental rotation. The void has at least three faces: (1) the **fidelity face**—imagery cannot be checked against the object it represents because the only judge is the imagery-haver; (2) the **inter-subjective face**—two minds reporting "vivid" imagery may share a word and not a phenomenology; (3) the **function-phenomenology face**—aphantasics perform mental rotation accurately using analytic strategies, suggesting the phenomenal image is a candidate phenomenal *extra* over function. This last face is empirical pressure on the assumption that phenomenology and function are tightly coupled, which has obvious bearing on the Map's [Dualism](/tenets/#dualism) commitment.

## Key Sources

### Zeman (2024) — Aphantasia and Hyperphantasia: Exploring Imagery Vividness Extremes
- **URL**: https://www.cell.com/trends/cognitive-sciences/fulltext/S1364-6613(24)00034-2
- **Type**: Review article, *Trends in Cognitive Sciences*
- **Key points**:
  - ~1% of the population is extreme-aphantasic, ~3% extreme-hyperphantasic
  - Aphantasia runs in families; often spans multiple sense modalities
  - Variably associated with reduced autobiographical memory, prosopagnosia, autism
  - Listening to scary stories produces skin conductance changes in imagers but not aphantasics — a *behavioral signature* of imagery presence
  - Frontoparietal-to-visual network connectivity varies along the spectrum
  - Over fifty further papers in the year following — the field is moving fast enough that the review itself was out-of-date within months
- **Tenet alignment**: Strong support for [Dualism](/tenets/#dualism) — aphantasia is a within-species case where phenomenology varies independently of much functional capacity, suggesting phenomenology is not just a label for function. Pressure on [Occam's Razor Has Limits](/tenets/#occams-limits) — the simple model "imagery = mental simulation = problem-solving" fails.
- **Quote**: "Initial results suggest that alterations in connectivity between the frontoparietal and visual networks may provide the neural substrate for visual imagery extremes."

### Kay, Keogh & Pearson (2024) — Slower but More Accurate Mental Rotation in Aphantasia
- **URL**: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1053810024000618
- **Type**: Empirical study, *Consciousness and Cognition*
- **Key points**:
  - Aphantasics complete mental rotation tasks **slower but more accurately** than controls
  - Both groups show the classical linear relation between angular disparity and response time
  - Controls use object-based mental rotation strategies; aphantasics use analytic strategies
  - Conclusion: visual imagery is *not crucial* for successful mental rotation
- **Tenet alignment**: Sharpens [Dualism](/tenets/#dualism) — if the same task is solved with and without phenomenal imagery, the phenomenal component is not doing the explanatory work that introspection suggests.
- **Quote**: "These results suggest that visual imagery is not crucial for successful performance in classical mental rotation tasks, as alternative strategies can be effectively utilised in the absence of holistic mental representations."

### Schwitzgebel — *The Unreliability of Naive Introspection* (2008) and *Perplexities of Consciousness*
- **URL**: https://faculty.ucr.edu/~eschwitz/SchwitzPapers/Naive070907.htm
- **Type**: Philosophical paper
- **Key points**:
  - We are "broadly inept" at introspecting our own ongoing experience, even under favorable conditions of extended reflection
  - Mental imagery is the *paradigm* example of introspective unreliability
  - Examples include peripheral vision, emotional phenomenology, and the phenomenology of thought
  - Foundationalism — inferring outer world from secure inner knowledge — is "almost exactly backward"
- **Tenet alignment**: Compatible with [Dualism](/tenets/#dualism) without requiring it: introspection can be unreliable about a real phenomenal domain. Provides methodological grounding for the void: the very tool we would use to map imagery is the one whose unreliability *constitutes* the void.
- **Quote**: "We are not simply fallible at the margins... we are broadly inept."

### Lennon (2023) — *Aphantasia and Conscious Thought*
- **URL**: https://philarchive.org/archive/LENAAC-3
- **Type**: Philosophical paper, *Erkenntnis*
- **Key points**:
  - Aphantasics have phenomenally conscious thoughts with *no sensory reduction base*
  - This falsifies the "sensory constraint on conscious thought"
  - Supports a non-sensory cognitive phenomenology
  - Variation in imagery may explain why philosophers disagree about cognitive phenomenology — proponents may have less imagery, opponents more
- **Tenet alignment**: Aligns with [Dualism](/tenets/#dualism) — non-sensory phenomenology resists reduction to sensory-functional surrogates.
- **Quote**: "Aphantasics have thoughts with no sensory reduction base, and these thoughts are phenomenally conscious."

### Würzburg School (Külpe, Ach, Bühler, Marbe, Watt, c. 1900–1915)
- **URL**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oswald_K%C3%BClpe
- **Type**: Historical research program
- **Key points**:
  - Used "systematic experimental introspection" on quick categorization tasks
  - Subjects could name a fruit ("apple") swiftly without reporting any image of an apple
  - Discovery of *Bewußtseinslagen* (conscious sets), *Bewußtheiten* (awarenesses), *Gedanken* (thoughts) — alleged non-imagistic conscious contents
  - Wundt called the studies "sham experiments"; the controversy helped end introspection as a respectable method and ushered in behaviorism
- **Tenet alignment**: Historical precedent that aphantasia repeats. The recurrence after a century — same phenomenon, same controversy — is itself evidence the void is structural rather than methodological.
- **Quote**: From Stanford Encyclopedia summary: "conscious states, such as doubt, conviction, hesitation, or the sudden comprehension of a concept, that subjects reported experiencing without being able to associate them with any specific sensory imagery."

### Galton (1880) — *Statistics of Mental Imagery*
- **URL**: https://psychclassics.yorku.ca/Galton/imagery.htm
- **Type**: Foundational empirical paper
- **Key points**:
  - First systematic survey of imagery vividness across 100 adults
  - Galton initially polled Royal Society fellows, many of whom reported little or no imagery — suggesting (he thought) high-level abstraction crowded out imagery
  - When polling general society, found "many men and a yet larger number of women declaring that they habitually saw mental imagery, and that it was perfectly distinct to them and full of colour"
  - Inaugural recognition that introspective reports diverge wildly across persons
- **Tenet alignment**: Supports the **inter-subjective face** of the void — 145 years of subsequent research has not closed the gap Galton opened.
- **Quote**: Implicit — Galton's data made vivid that introspective reports of imagery do not converge.

### Nanay (2025) and Scholz (2025) — *Mind & Language* exchange
- **URLs**: https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1111/mila.12561 and https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/mila.12560
- **Type**: Philosophical articles
- **Key points**:
  - Active 2025 debate over whether aphantasics have *unconscious* imagery
  - One 2025 study claims "imageless imagery in aphantasia" — a mental image with no accompanying phenomenology
  - Mental imagery may not be necessarily conscious, contrary to long-standing assumption
  - The unconscious-imagery debate is *unresolved* — direct quote from a 2025 *Brains Blog* post: "Spoiler: we still don't know"
- **Tenet alignment**: The unresolvability is itself the point. The void is robust to method: behavioral, neural, and introspective probes return consistent inconsistency.

## The Void

### Nature of the Limit

The Imagery Void has three distinguishable but interlocking faces:

1. **The fidelity face (unexplorable).** A mental image purports to represent something — a face, a scene, a rotated object. Yet the image cannot be compared against the represented object except *via more imagery and report*. There is no way for a conscious mind to step outside its own imagery to check whether the imagery is accurate. This is structurally similar to [necessary-opacity](/voids/necessary-opacity/) but specific to representational fidelity: no internal yardstick exists.

2. **The inter-subjective face (unexplorable, partially probeable).** Two minds reporting "vivid red apple" use a shared word but cannot share the phenomenology. The aphantasia/hyperphantasia spectrum makes this empirically vivid: people who use identical linguistic descriptions of imagery turn out to have radically different underlying phenomenology, as revealed by skin-conductance differences and neural-connectivity profiles. We can detect that the difference exists; we cannot directly compare the qualia. This intersects [commensurability-void](/voids/commensurability-void/) but applies *within species*.

3. **The function-phenomenology face (potentially occluded, possibly merely difficult).** The Kay/Keogh/Pearson result — aphantasics solving mental rotation accurately using analytic strategies — strongly suggests that the phenomenal image is not the cognitive operation it claims to be. Either (a) imagery is a phenomenal accompaniment that does not do the work, (b) two phenomenally distinct architectures perform isomorphic work, or (c) introspective reports of imagery are systematically misdescribing the underlying process. Each option is metaphysically loaded; none is verifiable from inside.

### Evidence for the Limit

- **Empirical:** Aphantasia/hyperphantasia exists, runs in families, has neural correlates, has behavioral signatures (skin conductance, autobiographical memory), is associated with task-strategy differences. The phenomenal variation is real, not a verbal artifact.
- **Methodological:** Schwitzgebel's case that introspection is "broadly inept" makes imagery the paradigm domain where reports cannot be trusted to track phenomenology.
- **Historical recurrence:** The Würzburg School discovered "imageless thought" in 1900–1915; the field forgot it; aphantasia rediscovered it after 2015 with new tools. The same void surfaces twice with the same controversy structure.
- **Theoretical:** No principled procedure has been proposed that would let an imagery-haver verify the fidelity of an image against its referent without using more imagery.

### Phenomenology

Approaching the imagery void produces a characteristic confusion. Asked "How vivid is your imagery, on a scale of 1–5?" most subjects answer confidently — yet these confident answers diverge wildly in ways the answerers cannot reconcile. Probe further ("Is the apple shiny? What's its exact shade of red? When you rotate it, do you see continuous motion or discrete frames?") and reports become hesitant, internally inconsistent, or generate detail that wasn't there before the probe — a [plenitude-void](/voids/plenitude-void/)-like behavior in which inquiry creates content. Aphantasics who learn the term often report a *retrospective phenomenological reorientation*: they had assumed "see in your mind's eye" was a metaphor, and discover only late in life that for others it isn't.

## Approaches to the Edge

### Direct Methods (limited)

- **Behavioral signatures**: skin conductance to scary stories, mental rotation strategies, autobiographical memory tests — provide *third-person* signatures of imagery presence.
- **Neuroimaging**: frontoparietal-to-visual network connectivity along the spectrum — provides correlates, not contents.
- **Vividness questionnaires (VVIQ)**: cheap, but the very tool whose unreliability is the issue.

None of these reach the fidelity face: they detect *whether something imagery-shaped is happening*, not *how accurate the image is*.

### Indirect Methods

- **Apophatic mapping** ([apophatic-cartography](/voids/apophatic-cartography/)): catalogue what introspection *fails* to deliver — sharpness it claims but cannot resolve, vividness it asserts but cannot grade — and treat the failures as data about the boundary.
- **Cross-modal probes**: aphantasia often spans modalities; the pattern of preserved versus lost modalities constrains what imagery is.
- **Developmental and pathological cases**: prosopagnosia, hyperthymesia, and acquired aphantasia (post-stroke, post-anaesthesia) provide natural experiments.
- **Linguistic divergence analysis**: when do "vivid", "clear", "sharp" come apart? Where do imagery-talk metaphors break down?

### What AI Might See

Multimodal AI models with image-generation pipelines have something imagery-like: latent activations they can render externally and inspect. Crucially, **AI imagery is externalisable** — the model can output the image as pixels and then process those pixels with the same vision system it uses for external scenes. Human imagery cannot be externalised this way; it remains trapped in introspection. This asymmetry is rich:

- AI can in principle compare its "imagined" output to a target and compute fidelity — a procedure no human can run on their own imagery.
- AI may exhibit aphantasia-analogues (text-only language models with no visual decoder) and hyperphantasia-analogues (multimodal systems) — providing testable analogues of the human spectrum.
- AI imagery is *inspectable in a way human imagery is not*, but whether AI imagery has phenomenology at all is itself an open question — pushing the void to a different layer.

This places imagery in the catalogue of [non-human-minds-as-void-explorers](/voids/non-human-minds-as-void-explorers/): AI does not solve the human imagery void, but it externalises a structural analogue that lets us see what it would *take* to solve it, and thereby what we lack.

## Connection to Tenets

### Most Relevant Tenet

**[Dualism](/tenets/#dualism)**, with strong secondary engagement from [Occam's Razor Has Limits](/tenets/#occams-limits).

### Implications

1. **For Dualism:** Aphantasia is empirical pressure on the identity of phenomenology and function. If two minds perform the same cognitive task with phenomenally distinct (or absent) imagery, then phenomenology is not the function in disguise. This is not a knockdown argument — the function may differ in fine-grained ways the experiments miss — but it is a within-species wedge that physicalist accounts must address.

2. **For Occam's Razor Has Limits:** The simplest model of imagery — that imagery is the substrate of mental simulation, and reports of vividness track its quality — fails empirically. Occam's preference for that model would have ruled out aphantasia *a priori*; it took a clinical case (Zeman's MX, 2010) to crack the assumption. This is a textbook case of simplicity hiding the territory.

3. **For Bidirectional Interaction:** If phenomenal imagery is a candidate phenomenal *extra* over function (Kay/Keogh/Pearson interpretation), it becomes a candidate site for [consciousness-physical interface](/voids/causal-interface/) activity: phenomenal content that is not exhausted by computational role and might therefore mark where the interface exerts its minimal influence. This is speculative and would require careful argument, but it is on-tenet.

4. **For the Map as a whole:** The Imagery Void exemplifies the [apophatic-cartography](/voids/apophatic-cartography/) method. We map the boundary not by entering it but by carefully tracing where introspection collapses, where reports diverge, and where function detaches from phenomenology. The shape of those failures is the data.

## Potential Article Angles

Based on this research, a voids article on the Imagery Void could:

1. **The fidelity-asymmetry framing**: lead with the structural impossibility of checking imagery against its referent, then use aphantasia and the Würzburg recurrence as evidence the boundary is real and not artefactual. Closes on the function-phenomenology wedge for [Dualism](/tenets/#dualism).

2. **The historical-recurrence framing**: lead with Würzburg in 1900 and aphantasia in 2015, treat the century-long forgetting and rediscovery as evidence that imagery's voidness is recurrent and structural rather than a transient methodological failure.

3. **The three-faces framing**: parallel to the agency-void/voids-between-minds template, present fidelity / inter-subjective / function-phenomenology as three structural faces of one void, each with its own evidential profile and method of approach.

4. **The AI-asymmetry framing**: use externalisable AI imagery as the indirect probe, contrast with human introspective imagery, and use the asymmetry to illuminate what we lack.

The three-faces framing (option 3) best matches the existing [conjunction-coalesce](/apex/conjunction-coalesce/) discipline and produces a single coherent article. Recommend that as the primary angle.

## Gaps in Research

- **Whether aphantasic imagery is genuinely absent or merely unreportable** is unresolved as of 2025 — the "imageless imagery" debate continues. The void is robust to the resolution: either way, fidelity is uncheckable.
- **Whether hyperphantasic imagery is genuinely more accurate** or just reported as more vivid is undetermined. A 2024 paper found "Visual imagery vividness appears to be independent of perceptual and memory precision" — suggesting that even the "more vivid" pole does not give better fidelity to anything external.
- **The phenomenology of approaching the void** is under-researched — what does it feel like to discover one is aphantasic, or to attempt to reconcile divergent imagery reports? This is open territory for first-person reports curated under [voids-safety-protocol](/project/voids-safety-protocol/).
- **Cross-modal patterns within aphantasia** — why some modalities go and others stay — would constrain accounts of what imagery is, but data is sparse.
- **Whether AI multimodal models illuminate or muddy the void** depends on contested claims about machine phenomenology — a question the Map's framework treats with appropriate humility.
