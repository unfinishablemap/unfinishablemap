## Executive Summary

Nahua (Aztec) philosophy, as reconstructed chiefly by James Maffie from sixteenth-century sources, centers on **teotl** (teōtl): a single, dynamic, self-generating, self-regenerating sacred power or energy that constitutes and continually transforms everything. On Maffie's reading this is an **ontological and constitutional monism** paired with a **process metaphysics** (a "metaphysics of becoming" over a "metaphysics of being") and a **pantheism** in which the many gods are aspects or guises of the one self-unfolding process. Teotl explicitly does **not** partition reality into mind vs. matter, animate vs. inanimate, or personal vs. impersonal — those are guises of one seamless power in motion, expressed through patterns Maffie names olin (bouncing/oscillating motion), malinalli (spinning/twisting/weaving), and nepantla (mutual, reciprocal middle-ing). Alongside this cosmology sits a distinctive philosophy of the person: a **distributed, multi-seat soul-psychology** of three animating forces — **tonalli** (head, solar heat, destiny/temperament), **teyolia** (heart, memory/emotion/personality, survives death), and **ihiyotl** (liver, breath, passion/vigour) — reconstructed principally by Alfredo López Austin and Miguel León-Portilla from the Florentine Codex and related colonial ethnography. For the Map, this is a **second organicist "basin-edge" case** alongside classical Chinese philosophy of mind: a deep, sustained tradition with no mind-matter divide that reaches a **process-monism / pantheism**, not dualism. Handled with Tenet 5 discipline it is a **test** of the cross-traditional convergence argument, not support for it, and the tripartite soul is a thematic contrast to the unified Cartesian ego — first Indigenous-Americas coverage for the Map.

## Key Sources

### Maffie, *Aztec Philosophy: Understanding a World in Motion* (2014)
- **URL**: https://upcolorado.com/university-press-of-colorado/aztec-philosophy ; https://www.jstor.org/stable/j.ctt9qhkh2 ; https://ndpr.nd.edu/reviews/aztec-philosophy-understanding-a-world-in-motion/
- **Type**: Book (secondary / reconstructive scholarship)
- **Publisher-verified**: University Press of Colorado, 2014, ISBN 9781607324614 — confirmed at the UPC catalog page, JSTOR, and the Notre Dame Philosophical Reviews review page.
- **Key points**:
  - Teotl is "essentially power: continually active, actualized, and actualizing energy-in-motion"; reality consists of one thing (teotl) and one *kind* of thing — "ontological and constitutional monism."
  - Process metaphysics: teotl is "a process like a thunderstorm or flowing river rather than a static, perduring substantive entity"; Aztec metaphysics "embraces a metaphysics of becoming instead of a metaphysics of being." Something *is* what it *does*.
  - Pantheism, not polytheism: the gods are "aspects" or "guises" of teotl, "a complex, all-encompassing unified macroprocess that consists of a myriad of coordinated microprocesses."
  - Three master motion-patterns: **olin** (bouncing, oscillating, curving), **malinalli** (spinning, twisting, weaving), **nepantla** (reciprocal, mutual middle-ing / balancing). Reality is a woven, in-motion fabric.
  - **Agonistic inamic unity**: paired complementary-yet-competing forces (life/death, male/female, order/chaos) whose ceaseless struggle-and-balancing constitutes process. "Life contains the seed of death; death, the fertile, energizing seed of life."
- **Tenet alignment**: Conflicts with Tenet 1 as a *rival monism* (no mind-matter split; consciousness is a guise of teotl, not an irreducible non-physical principle). Must be held under **Tenet 5** as an unadjudicated rival — depth of inquiry within a different framework, NOT evidence for or against dualism.
- **Quote**: "Teotl is a single, dynamic, vivifying, eternally self-generating and self-regenerating sacred power, energy or force." (Maffie, IEP entry — same framing as the book.)

### Maffie, "Aztec Philosophy" — *Internet Encyclopedia of Philosophy* (IEP)
- **URL**: https://iep.utm.edu/aztec-philosophy/
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed encyclopedia entry (secondary)
- **Author-verified**: James Maffie (given as University of Maryland on the entry; the underlying IEP article dates to c. 2005). Confirmed author + live URL.
- **Key points**:
  - Teotl "is both metaphysically immanent *and* transcendent" — immanent in penetrating every detail of the universe, transcendent in not being exhausted by any single existing thing.
  - "Everything that exists constitutes an all-inclusive and interrelated unity" that is sacred and substantively identical with teotl.
  - Teotl transcends dichotomies "personal vs. impersonal, animate vs. inanimate," existing as a "seamless totality."
  - On knowing: humans "know teotl through teotl" via "mystical-style union between their hearts and teotl," "without mediation by language, concepts, or categories" — a broadly experiential/introspective epistemology (relevant to the common-cause caveat below).
  - Summarizes the three animating forces (tonalli/teyolia/ihiyotl) and holds that all three must operate harmoniously for psychological and moral health.
- **Tenet alignment**: Same as the book — rival process-monism; Tenet 5 discipline.

### León-Portilla, *Aztec Thought and Culture: A Study of the Ancient Nahuatl Mind* (1963)
- **URL**: https://philpapers.org/rec/LENATA-2 ; https://archive.org/details/aztecthoughtcult0000leon
- **Type**: Book (foundational secondary scholarship)
- **Publisher-verified**: University of Oklahoma Press, 1963, "The Civilization of the American Indian Series" (vol. 67); translated from the Spanish by Jack Emory Davis. An English translation-with-revisions of *La filosofía náhuatl estudiada en sus fuentes* (1956). Confirmed via PhilPapers, Duke UP (HAHR review), Internet Archive, Wellcome Collection.
- **Key points**:
  - First systematic English-language study of Nahua metaphysics; argues the Nahua tlamatinime ("knowers of things," philosophers) developed a refined philosophy comparable to the ancient Greeks, built from >90 original Nahuatl documents.
  - Foundational for the reconstruction of Nahua psychology and the animating-forces vocabulary; his reading of teotl as an evanescent underlying principle is the seed Maffie later develops (with divergence — see Key Debates).
  - Emphasizes "flower and song" (in xochitl in cuicatl) — poetic/metaphorical knowing — as the tlamatinime's route to truth on earth.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral-to-conflicting as a rival non-dualist reconstruction; Tenet 5 discipline. Foundational, but note later scholars debate his more "otherworldly"/idealist inflections.

### López Austin, *The Human Body and Ideology: Concepts of the Ancient Nahuas* (1988)
- **URL**: https://philpapers.org/rec/AUSTHB ; https://archive.org/details/humanbodyideolog0002lope
- **Type**: Book, 2 vols. (foundational secondary scholarship)
- **Publisher-verified**: University of Utah Press, 1988, ISBN 0-87480-260-1; translated by Thelma Ortiz de Montellano and Bernard Ortiz de Montellano; an unabridged English translation of *Cuerpo humano e ideología* (1980). Confirmed via PhilPapers, Duke UP (HAHR review), AbeBooks/Amazon ISBN, Internet Archive.
- **Key points**:
  - The most detailed reconstruction of the **tripartite animating-force psychology**: tonalli (head), teyolia (heart), ihiyotl (liver), each a distinct animistic entity with its own seat, function, and pathology.
  - Draws principally on Sahagún's Florentine Codex and Alonso de Molina's lexicography plus modern ethnography of Nahua-descended communities.
  - Shows the person as a plural, distributed system of forces — health is the harmonious balance of the three; illness is soul-disharmony or loss (e.g. tonalli loss from fright, *susto*).
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral (descriptive philosophy of the person). Relevant to the Map's unity-of-consciousness / binding and self threads as a distributed contrast to the unified ego.

### The Florentine Codex (Sahagún and Nahua collaborators, c. 1545–1590) — PRIMARY SOURCE
- **URL**: https://florentinecodex.getty.edu/ ; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Florentine_Codex
- **Type**: Primary source (sixteenth-century ethnographic manuscript)
- **Key points**:
  - Twelve-book bilingual (Nahuatl / Spanish) encyclopedia compiled by Franciscan friar Bernardino de Sahagún with Nahua elders and trilingual former students at the Colegio de Santa Cruz de Tlatelolco; ~2,500 images. Widely regarded as the single most reliable source on Mexica culture.
  - The empirical bedrock from which León-Portilla, López Austin, and Maffie reconstruct both teotl-cosmology and the animating forces.
  - **Caveat to flag downstream**: it is a *colonial* source, mediated by a Christian friar and post-conquest informants. All reconstructions of "Aztec philosophy" are interpretations across a colonial filter — the article must not present teotl-monism or the three souls as a transparent pre-contact system.

## Major Positions

### Teotl Process-Monism / Pantheism (Maffie)
- **Proponents**: James Maffie (primary contemporary systematizer); roots in León-Portilla.
- **Core claim**: One self-generating sacred power-in-motion (teotl) constitutes all of reality; no substance/attribute, mind/matter, or sacred/profane dualisms; reality is *becoming*, patterned as olin/malinalli/nepantla and structured by agonistic inamic (complementary-opposite) pairs.
- **Key arguments**: Process over substance (things are what they do); pantheistic reinterpretation of the pantheon as guises of one process; a woven-fabric ("weaving the world") ontology; an experiential/mystical epistemology of "knowing teotl through teotl."
- **Relation to site tenets**: A **rival monism** and thus a genuine competitor to Tenet 1 (dualism), NOT an ally. Because it dissolves rather than affirms the mind-matter distinction, it cannot be conscripted as dualist. Under Tenet 5 it stands as depth-of-inquiry within an alternative framework whose contest with dualism the Map leaves **unadjudicated**. This is the same discipline applied to the classical Chinese organicist case.

### The Tripartite Animating-Force Psychology (López Austin / León-Portilla)
- **Proponents**: Alfredo López Austin, Miguel León-Portilla; grounded in the Florentine Codex.
- **Core claim**: The human person is animated by three distinct forces with different bodily seats and functions — tonalli (head/hair; solar heat, vigour, growth, destiny/day-sign, can leave the body in sleep or fright), teyolia (heart; memory, emotion, knowledge, personhood; travels to the afterlife at death), ihiyotl (liver; breath, passion, bravery, desire). Psychological and moral health is the *harmony* of the three.
- **Key arguments**: Direct lexical/ethnographic reconstruction from Nahuatl sources; illness-as-soul-disharmony (e.g. *susto*/fright causing tonalli loss) as evidence of a plural, seated model.
- **Relation to site tenets**: A **distributed / multi-seat** model of mind — a thematic contrast to the unified Cartesian ego and a data point for the Map's unity-of-consciousness / binding / self threads. Frame as a *distinctive soul-psychology*, NOT as anticipating any modern neuroscience or mechanism.

## Key Debates

### León-Portilla vs. Maffie on the reality of earthly things
- **Sides**: León-Portilla (a more evanescent / arguably idealist-leaning reading — earthly things as dream-like, "flower and song" as the only earthly truth) vs. Maffie (robust immanent monism — earthly things ARE teotl, fully real as guises of the one process).
- **Core disagreement**: Whether Nahua metaphysics devalues the sensible world (things as unreal/dreamlike) or affirms it as teotl immanent. Both agree teotl is the underlying principle.
- **Current state**: Ongoing interpretive dispute; Maffie's immanentist monism is the influential contemporary reading but is itself a reconstruction, not a settled fact.

### How much is "Aztec philosophy," how much is reconstruction?
- **Sides**: Enthusiasts of a systematic Aztec philosophy (Maffie, León-Portilla) vs. cautious reviewers (see Notre Dame Philosophical Reviews; anthropological reviews) who warn against over-systematizing a tradition known only through colonial sources.
- **Core disagreement**: Whether a coherent single "system" (teotl-monism with a fixed pattern-vocabulary) is warranted by the fragmentary, mediated evidence, or is partly imposed by modern comparative philosophy.
- **Current state**: Live. The downstream article MUST carry this reconstruction/colonial-filter caveat explicitly and avoid presenting teotl-monism as a transparent, uncontested pre-contact doctrine.
