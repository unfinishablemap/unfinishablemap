## Executive Summary

The transactional interpretation (TI), introduced by physicist John G. Cramer in 1986, treats
each quantum event as a completed "handshake" between an emitter and an absorber: a forward-in-time
(retarded) "offer wave" and a backward-in-time (advanced) "confirmation wave" that lock into a
standing-wave "transaction." Its lineage is the Wheeler-Feynman absorber theory of classical
electrodynamics (1945/1949), extended to the quantum wavefunction. TI is explicitly time-symmetric
and nonlocal, claims to derive the Born rule from the offer-times-confirmation product, and treats
wavefunction collapse as the completion of a transaction. Ruth E. Kastner's Possibilist / Relativistic
Transactional Interpretation (PTI / RTI) is the notable modern refinement: it relocates offer and
confirmation waves to a pre-spacetime realm of physical possibility (so no literal signal travels
backward through spacetime) and argues spacetime itself emerges from actualized transactions. The
standard objection is Maudlin's contingent-absorber thought experiment. TI is a physicalist
interpretation; the Map's interest is instrumental — it is the natural ontological home for the
time-symmetric / retrocausal selection machinery the corpus already invokes, borrowed WITHOUT
endorsing its physicalism.

## Key Sources

### Cramer, "The transactional interpretation of quantum mechanics" (1986)
- **Citation (web-verified)**: J. G. Cramer, *Reviews of Modern Physics*, vol. 58, no. 3,
  pp. 647-688, July 1986.
- **Verification note**: Volume 58, issue 3, page range 647-688, year 1986 confirmed across
  Wikipedia's transactional-interpretation entry, SciRP and SciEPub reference records, and matches
  the figure already used in the corpus (quantum-indeterminacy-free-will cites 647-688). NOTE:
  the task brief's hint of "647-687" is off by one; the verified end page is 688. (collapse-and-time
  currently cites only the start page "647" — the downstream expand should use the full 647-688.)
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed journal paper (canonical source of record for TI).
- **Key points**:
  - A quantum event is an "atemporal" transaction: the emitter sends a retarded offer wave; a
    responding absorber returns an advanced confirmation wave; reinforcement between them (described
    as occurring "in pseudotime," a heuristic sequencing device, not literal time) forms the completed
    transaction / standing wave.
  - Born rule: the confirmation wave arriving back at the emitter has amplitude proportional to the
    product of the offer wave and its complex conjugate, i.e. psi times psi-star = |psi|^2, so the
    strength of the "echo" the emitter receives from a given absorber reproduces the Born probability.
    (Paraphrase; the "echo has strength P = psi psi-star" wording is UNVERIFIED.)
  - "Collapse" is reinterpreted as the completion of the transaction — no separate collapse postulate.
  - TI is explicitly nonlocal (consistent with Bell-inequality violation) yet claims to be
    relativistically invariant and fully causal because the "backward" wave is part of an atemporal
    completed handshake rather than a usable signal.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral-to-supportive machinery, physicalist framing. The time-symmetric,
  bidirectional handshake structure is congenial to Tenet 2 (Minimal Quantum Interaction) and Tenet 3
  (Bidirectional Interaction) if repurposed; but as Cramer states it, TI is a fully physical, observer-
  independent interpretation with no consciousness role — so it conflicts with the Map's dualism
  (Tenet 1) as a complete metaphysics. Borrow the mechanism, not the physicalism.

### Wheeler and Feynman, absorber theory of radiation (1945, 1949)
- **Citations (web-verified as to journal/year)**: J. A. Wheeler and R. P. Feynman, "Interaction
  with the Absorber as the Mechanism of Radiation," *Reviews of Modern Physics* 17 (1945);
  and "Classical Electrodynamics in Terms of Direct Interparticle Action," *Reviews of Modern
  Physics* 21 (1949). (Volume/page specifics not re-verified this run — the downstream expand
  should confirm 17:157 and 21:425 before citing page numbers.)
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed journal papers (the classical-electrodynamics ancestor of TI).
- **Key points**:
  - Time-symmetric reformulation of classical electrodynamics: radiation is a direct interaction
    between source and absorber, with the field taken as half the sum of retarded (forward-in-time)
    and advanced (backward-in-time) solutions of Maxwell's equations.
  - The absorber's summed advanced response supplies the "radiation reaction" and picks out the
    ordinary retarded arrow of radiation. Cramer's move is to carry this offer/confirmation structure
    over from the classical field to the quantum wavefunction.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral (physics substrate). Establishes that time-symmetric advanced+retarded
  solutions are a respectable, non-fringe feature of foundational physics — useful support for the
  Map's claim that time-symmetric selection is not ad hoc.

### Kastner, *The Transactional Interpretation of Quantum Mechanics: The Reality of Possibility* (2012)
- **Citation (web-verified)**: R. E. Kastner, Cambridge University Press. ISBN 9780521764155.
  Publication date most commonly given as 2012 (hardcover, ~251 pp.); some records cite 2013.
  The corpus (collapse-and-time) currently cites 2012 — recommend keeping 2012 with a "(2013 in
  some listings)" note for the downstream expand.
- **Type**: Academic monograph (source of record for PTI).
- **Key points (paraphrase)**:
  - Possibilist Transactional Interpretation (PTI): offer and confirmation waves are real but
    "sub-empirical" possibilities that do NOT live in spacetime; they inhabit a pre-spacetime realm
    of physical possibility. Only actualized transactions are spacetime events.
  - This dissolves the usual "signal travels backward in time" worry: advanced waves are
    non-actualized offers/confirmations in the possibility realm, so no literal retrocausal signaling
    occurs and ordinary forward causation among actualized events is preserved.
  - Spacetime is emergent: the network of actualized transactions is what constitutes spacetime,
    rather than transactions happening within a pre-given spacetime.
- **Relation to site**: This is the single most Map-relevant refinement. A "pre-spacetime realm in
  which selection occurs" is a natural interface for a dualist selection story (see Map Angle below);
  collapse-and-time already gestures at "consciousness operates in the pre-spacetime realm, selecting
  which possibilities become actual." Still physicalist as Kastner presents it — the Map adds the
  consciousness reading; PTI does not require it.

### Maudlin objection (contingent-absorber thought experiment)
- **Primary source**: T. Maudlin, *Quantum Non-Locality and Relativity: Metaphysical Intimations of
  Modern Physics* (Blackwell; 1st ed. 1994, later eds. 2002, 2011). ISBN of a later ed. 9781444331271.
  (Secondary sources sometimes date the specific thought experiment to 1996; the book of record is
  1994 — verify which edition contains the argument before citing a year.)
- **Type**: Monograph / standard objection in the literature.
- **Key points (paraphrase)**:
  - A "contingent absorber" setup: the position of a second absorber depends on whether the particle
    is detected at a first absorber, so which absorbers exist to send confirmation waves is itself
    contingent on the outcome. Maudlin argues TI cannot consistently assign a determinate transaction —
    the pseudotime story appears to need the answer before it can compute the answer.
  - Widely read as a serious (some say fatal) challenge to Cramer's original TI. Responses divide
    between (a) patching original TI (Cramer added a hierarchy/ordering to pseudotime transaction
    formation) and (b) modifying TI — most notably Kastner's possibilist/relativistic move, which
    argues the challenge dissolves because offers are possibilities, not spacetime trajectories.
- **Relation to site**: Important honesty item. Any TI-based selection story the Map tells inherits
  this objection; the Map should present Maudlin's challenge and the PTI/RTI reply rather than assume
  TI is unproblematic.

## Major Positions

### Cramer's original Transactional Interpretation (1986)
- **Proponent**: John G. Cramer.
- **Core claim**: Quantum events are atemporal transactions completed by an offer wave (retarded
  solution) and a confirmation wave (advanced solution); the |psi|^2 Born rule and "collapse" both
  fall out of the completed handshake. Physicalist, observer-independent, no consciousness required.
- **Key arguments**: Wheeler-Feynman lineage; time-symmetric field equations already contain advanced
  solutions; nonlocality without signaling; a visualizable mechanism for EPR/Bell correlations.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Machinery aligns with Tenets 2 and 3 (minimal, bidirectional/temporal
  interaction) but the interpretation as a whole is physicalist and thus not endorsed under Tenet 1.

### Kastner's Possibilist / Relativistic TI (PTI / RTI)
- **Proponent**: Ruth E. Kastner.
- **Core claim**: Offers and confirmations are real physical possibilities in a pre-spacetime realm;
  only actualized transactions are spacetime events; spacetime emerges from the transaction network;
  no literal backward-in-time signaling; Maudlin's challenge dissolves in the relativistic direct-action
  formulation.
- **Relation to site tenets**: The most useful refinement for the Map. A pre-spacetime possibility
  realm supplies a candidate locus for non-physical selection (Tenets 1-3) that is Born-rule-preserving
  and does not require literal retrocausal signals. Still physicalist as stated by Kastner.

### Standard critique
- **Proponent**: Tim Maudlin (and follow-on critics).
- **Core claim**: The contingent-absorber case shows original TI cannot consistently fix transactions;
  the pseudotime formation story is circular for outcome-dependent absorber configurations.
- **Current state**: Live debate; original TI patched with pseudotime ordering, PTI/RTI claims full
  dissolution, some critics (e.g. Lewis) argue the challenge can be reformulated.

## Key Debates

### Are advanced waves ontic or a calculational device?
- **Sides**: Cramer/Kastner treat advanced (confirmation) waves as physically real (Kastner: real but
  pre-spacetime possibilities) vs. instrumentalists who read advanced solutions as mere bookkeeping.
- **Core disagreement**: Whether the backward-propagating solution names something in the world or is
  a mathematical convenience that yields the right forward-time statistics.
- **Map bearing**: The Map's retrocausal-selection articles lean on an ontic-but-non-signaling reading;
  PTI's "real possibility, not spacetime signal" is the cleanest fit.

### Does TI survive the Maudlin contingent-absorber challenge?
- **Sides**: PTI/RTI (dissolved) vs. residual critics (reformulable). Original-TI-with-pseudotime-
  hierarchy sits between.
- **Current state**: Unresolved consensus; PTI/RTI is the strongest defense.

### Is TI genuinely distinct from, or complementary to, other time-symmetric approaches?
- **Sides**: TI vs. the Two-State-Vector Formalism (Aharonov et al.) vs. general retrocausal /
  time-symmetric hidden-variable programs.
- **Map bearing**: The corpus already distinguishes forward-in-time vs. time-symmetric selection; a TI
  article should locate TI precisely within that map rather than conflating "retrocausal" approaches.
