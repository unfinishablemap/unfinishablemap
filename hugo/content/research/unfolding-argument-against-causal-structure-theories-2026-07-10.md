## Executive Summary

The **unfolding argument** (Doerig, Schurger, Hess & Herzog 2019) is a formal challenge to "causal-structure theories" of consciousness — theories that make phenomenal experience depend on a system's internal causal organization rather than its input-output behaviour. Integrated Information Theory (IIT) is the paradigm target, alongside Recurrent Processing Theory (RPT). The argument runs: for any recurrent (feedback) network, one can construct a purely **feedforward** network that is input-output identical yet, by IIT's own measure, has integrated information Φ = 0 and is therefore unconscious. Since the two systems are behaviourally indistinguishable, no experiment could ever decide between them. So a causal-structure theory is caught in a dilemma: either it is **false** (it assigns different consciousness to behaviourally identical systems, at least one verdict being empirically ungroundable) or it is **unfalsifiable** (outside science). The unfolding construction was later formalised via automata theory (Hanson & Walker 2021, using the Krohn-Rhodes decomposition). IIT defenders reply from two directions — that the "equivalent" feedforward system is not truly equivalent under perturbation/plasticity (Usher 2021; O'Reilly-Shah, Selvitella & Schurger 2026), and that IIT is an **intrinsic** theory that was never committed to input-output measurement in the first place (Tsuchiya, Andrillon & Haun 2020; the intrinsicality postulate). The debate remains live and unresolved.

**Relevance to the Map**: This is a formal, internal-to-cognitive-science pressure on the leading *physicalist* structural theory of consciousness. It is broadly favourable to the Map's dualism — if the most mathematically ambitious physicalist theory is either false or unfalsifiable on its own terms, physicalist confidence should drop (Tenet 5: Occam's razor has limits; simplicity/elegance of a structural measure does not guarantee it tracks consciousness). But it must be handled honestly: (a) the argument's "behavioural equivalence ⇒ same consciousness" premise is itself a *functionalist* premise that a dualist need not endorse, and which cuts against some interface framings too; (b) IIT has substantive replies. See "Relation to Site Tenets" and "Honest caveats" below.

## Key Sources

### The unfolding argument (original paper)
- **Citation**: Doerig, A., Schurger, A., Hess, K., & Herzog, M. H. (2019). "The unfolding argument: Why IIT and other causal structure theories cannot explain consciousness." *Consciousness and Cognition*, 72, 49-59.
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.concog.2019.04.002 · **PMID**: 31078047
- **Verification**: VERIFIED at PubMed (vol 72, pp 49-59, DOI confirmed; Epub 2019-05-09).
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed paper
- **Key points**:
  - Defines "causal-structure theories": consciousness is fixed by internal causal organisation. IIT (feedback ⇒ conscious; feedforward ⇒ not) and RPT are the exemplars.
  - Uses results from the theory of computation: any input-output function realised by a recurrent network can be realised by a feedforward ("unfolded") network. For IIT the unfolded twin has Φ = 0.
  - Concludes the theories are "either false or outside the realm of science."
  - Contrasts with function/access theories (global workspace, higher-order, predictive coding) whose predictions attach to functions that many connectivity patterns could realise — these escape the argument.

### Formalisation via automata theory
- **Citation**: Hanson, J. R., & Walker, S. I. (2021). "Formalizing falsification for theories of consciousness across computational hierarchies." *Neuroscience of Consciousness*, 2021(2), niab014.
- **DOI**: 10.1093/nc/niab014 · **arXiv**: 2006.07390
- **Verification**: VERIFIED (Oxford Academic 2021 vol 2021 issue 2, article niab014; PMC8339439).
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed paper
- **Key points**:
  - Gives the unfolding construction a rigorous basis using the **Krohn-Rhodes theorem** from automata theory: any deterministic finite-state automaton can be realised by a strictly feedforward architecture (a nested sequence of preserved partitions), giving Φ = 0 for all states.
  - Argues IIT "may already be falsified even in the absence of experimental refutation" — the falsification is *formal/epistemological*, not empirical.
  - Note: Hanson & Walker also authored "No Substitute for Functionalism — A Reply to 'Falsification & Consciousness'" (arXiv:2006.13664), part of the same dispute with Kleiner & Hoel.

### IIT-side reply (the canonical rebuttal)
- **Citation**: Tsuchiya, N., Andrillon, T., & Haun, A. (2020). "A reply to 'the unfolding argument': Beyond functionalism/behaviorism and towards a science of causal structure theories of consciousness." *Consciousness and Cognition*, 79, 102877.
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.concog.2020.102877
- **Verification**: VERIFIED venue/vol/article (Monash / ResearchGate / PhilPapers). CAUTION: the title appears in two variants across databases — "towards a **science**" (PhilPapers/Monash) vs "towards a **truer science**" (some ResearchGate/DigitalCommons records). Use "towards a science of causal structure theories" as primary; flag the variant when citing.
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed reply
- **Key points**:
  - The unfolding argument smuggles in **functionalism/behaviourism** as a premise (that behavioural equivalence entails experiential equivalence). IIT rejects this premise outright.
  - The feedforward "unfolding" is astronomically less efficient/compact; natural selection would not build brains that way — so the twin is biologically irrelevant even if mathematically constructible.
  - Points toward IIT's **intrinsicality** commitment: IIT is a theory of a system's intrinsic cause-effect power, not of its extrinsic input-output map, so demanding input-output falsifiability misframes the theory.

### First-person rescue attempt refuted (UA authors respond to the responders)
- **Citation**: Herzog, M. H., Schurger, A., & Doerig, A. (2022). "First-person experience cannot rescue causal structure theories from the unfolding argument." *Consciousness and Cognition*, 98, 103261.
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.concog.2021.103261
- **Verification**: VERIFIED (Consciousness and Cognition vol 98, article 103261; ScienceDirect S1053810021001872; open access).
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed reply-to-replies
- **Key points**:
  - Answers the intrinsicality/first-person defence: pure first-person experience cannot ground a *science* of consciousness because science requires measurement, and experience is only accessible via reports, brain activity, and their relations — all of which are behavioural/third-person quantities to which the unfolding construction applies.
  - Keeps the dilemma alive: appealing to intrinsic experience either re-imports measurable proxies (and the argument bites) or exits science.

### Phenomenology-first framing of the dispute
- **Citation**: Negro, N. (2022). "Can the Integrated Information Theory Explain Consciousness from Consciousness Itself?" *Review of Philosophy and Psychology*.
- **DOI**: 10.1007/s13164-022-00653-x
- **Verification**: VERIFIED venue/DOI (Springer). Page/volume not captured — flag as "advance/online; verify vol/pp before use."
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed philosophy paper
- **Key points**:
  - Frames IIT as a "phenomenology-first" theory whose explanation is *self-evidencing* (consciousness explained starting from consciousness). Argues this circularity can be virtuous but leaves a "data-fitting" problem that the unfolding argument sharpens.
  - Useful for the article's "what kind of theory is IIT" framing — connects the unfolding dispute to the deeper methodological question of whether a structural theory can be tested from the third person at all.

### Latest development — the plasticity caveat (2026)
- **Citation**: O'Reilly-Shah, V. N., Selvitella, A. M., & Schurger, A. (2026). "A caveat regarding the unfolding argument: implications of plasticity." *Neuroscience of Consciousness*, 2026(1), niag027.
- **DOI**: 10.1093/nc/niag027
- **Verification**: VERIFIED (Oxford Academic 2026 issue 1, article niag027).
- **Type**: Peer-reviewed paper
- **Notable**: **Aaron Schurger, a co-author of the original 2019 unfolding argument, co-authors this caveat** — a partial walk-back from one of the argument's own architects.
- **Key points**:
  - When a recurrent network has **synaptic plasticity** (weights change during processing), the unfolding premise breaks: "no static feedforward network can track the evolving function of a plastic recurrent network over arbitrary time intervals."
  - Distinguishes **state-space** trajectories (different internal states within a fixed function) from **function-space** trajectories (the plastic system moves through function space itself). A static unfolded twin cannot follow.
  - Implication: theories that incorporate fast plasticity on perception-relevant timescales **restore empirical testability** — a concrete boundary condition limiting the unfolding argument's reach.

## Major Positions

### The unfolding argument (Doerig, Schurger, Hess, Herzog; formalised by Hanson & Walker)
- **Core claim**: A theory that fixes consciousness by internal causal structure, while assigning different verdicts to input-output-identical systems, is either false or unfalsifiable.
- **Key premises**:
  1. *Functional/behavioural equivalence*: any recurrent net's input-output function is realisable by a feedforward net (Krohn-Rhodes / unfolding).
  2. IIT scores the feedforward twin Φ = 0 (unconscious) and the recurrent original Φ > 0 (conscious).
  3. Science can only access the input-output map; the two twins are indistinguishable to it.
  4. Therefore the differing consciousness verdict is either wrong or untestable.
- **Relation to site tenets**: A physicalist-internal result that *weakens* the strongest physicalist structural theory. Consonant with Tenet 5 (simplicity/mathematical elegance of Φ does not certify truth). NOTE the honest tension: premises 1 and 3 are functionalist; a dualist who holds that consciousness is not fixed by physical structure at all (Tenet 1) can agree the *behavioural* twins are conscious-indistinguishable without conceding the physicalist framing — but should not lean on the argument as if its functionalist premise were the Map's own.

### IIT defence 1 — intrinsicality / anti-functionalism (Tsuchiya, Andrillon, Haun; Tononi/Albantakis school)
- **Core claim**: IIT is a theory of *intrinsic* cause-effect power, not of extrinsic behaviour. Demanding input-output falsifiability presupposes the functionalism IIT denies. The feedforward twin, though behaviourally matched, has a different intrinsic causal structure and so genuinely differs in consciousness — that is a feature, not a bug.
- **Supporting point**: biological implausibility/inefficiency of the unfolded architecture (selection would never build it).
- **Relation to site tenets**: Interesting for the Map because IIT here abandons the functionalist bridge — which is *also* something the Map's dualism does (consciousness not reducible to function). But IIT keeps consciousness physically grounded (in intrinsic causal structure), so it remains a rival physicalist/emergentist stance, not an ally.

### UA authors' counter — measurement forecloses the first-person rescue (Herzog, Schurger, Doerig 2022)
- **Core claim**: You cannot ground a *science* of consciousness on pure first-person experience; every scientific handle on experience is a third-person proxy the unfolding construction reaches. So intrinsicality either re-admits measurable proxies (argument bites) or leaves science.

### The plasticity limit (O'Reilly-Shah, Selvitella, Schurger 2026)
- **Core claim**: Static-network unfolding fails for plastic networks; consciousness theories built on fast plasticity are testable after all. A principled *boundary* on the argument rather than a refutation of it.

## Key Debates

### Is behavioural/functional equivalence a legitimate premise?
- **Sides**: UA camp (Doerig, Schurger, Herzog, Hanson, Walker) says science has no choice but to work from the input-output map. IIT camp (Tsuchiya, Tononi, Albantakis) says that premise *is* functionalism and begs the question against a causal-structure theory.
- **Core disagreement**: Whether "two systems with the same behaviour must be scientifically assigned the same consciousness" is a neutral methodological principle or a contested substantive thesis.
- **Current state**: Ongoing; genuinely unresolved. The 2022 Herzog reply and the 2026 plasticity caveat show the exchange is still evolving on both sides.

### Is the unfolded twin actually equivalent?
- **Sides**: Usher (2021) and the 2026 plasticity paper argue the "equivalent" feedforward net diverges under perturbation or synaptic change, so premise 1 is too strong. UA camp maintains equivalence holds for the finite input-output function that science can test.
- **Current state**: Partially conceded — the original architects now accept a plasticity carve-out.

### Does the argument generalise beyond IIT?
- Targets any theory where consciousness supervenes on causal structure independent of outward function (IIT, RPT). Function/access theories (global workspace, higher-order, predictive coding) are explicitly *not* targets. This matters for the Map's positioning: the argument is narrow-gauge, aimed at structural physicalist theories.

## Relation to Site Tenets (summary)

- **Tenet 1 (Dualism)**: The argument targets *physicalist* structural theories; a dualist who denies consciousness supervenes on physical structure sidesteps it. The Map is not itself a "causal-structure theory."
- **Tenet 2/3 (Minimal quantum interaction / Bidirectional interaction)**: DOUBLE-EDGED. The UA's functionalist premise, applied naively, presses on any consciousness-difference without behavioural consequence. The Map's escape is that its interaction *is* bidirectional (produces minimal physical effects), and it does not tie consciousness to internal recurrence vs feedforward structure. Article must make this explicit rather than cheerlead.
- **Tenet 4 (No many worlds)**: Not directly engaged.
- **Tenet 5 (Occam's razor has limits)**: Strongest fit. Φ is a maximally simple, elegant structural posit; the argument shows elegance does not secure truth or testability. A clean case study for the tenet.

## Honest caveats (do NOT cheerlead)

- The unfolding argument is *not* a proof that IIT is false; its own conclusion is a **disjunction** (false OR unfalsifiable), and serious replies (intrinsicality, non-equivalence, plasticity) keep the first disjunct contestable.
- The argument rests on a **functionalist premise** the Map does not itself hold; presenting it as vindicating dualism without flagging this would be a category error the pessimistic-review lens will catch.
- IIT defenders have a coherent position (intrinsicality) that is *structurally similar* to the Map's own anti-functionalism — the Map cannot use "IIT is anti-functionalist and therefore unfalsifiable" as a criticism without the same charge rebounding on non-behavioural aspects of dualism. Handle with care.
- The 2026 plasticity caveat shows the field is actively narrowing the argument's scope; any article should present the argument as *live and contested*, not settled.
