## Executive Summary

The Entropic Brain Hypothesis (EBH), introduced by Robin Carhart-Harris, David Nutt and colleagues (2014), proposes that the entropy of spontaneous brain activity indexes the richness/informational content of conscious states, within upper and lower bounds beyond which consciousness is lost. Normal waking consciousness sits just *below* criticality (a "sub-critical" regime that constrains experience and enables reality-testing/metacognition); psychedelics push the brain *toward* (or past) the critical point, raising entropy and producing richer, less constrained experience ("primary states"). The 2018 "revisited" update reframed the theory around brain criticality and proposed concrete entropy measures. In 2019 Carhart-Harris and Karl Friston folded EBH into the predictive-processing-based REBUS ("Relaxed Beliefs Under Psychedelics") / "anarchic brain" model. The hypothesis enjoys real but contested empirical support; key concerns are about the reliability of entropy measures (esp. Lempel–Ziv complexity), conceptual ambiguity in "entropy," and falsifiability of the predictive-processing extension.

**Relevance to the Map**: This topic overlaps heavily with existing articles ([psychedelics-and-the-filter-model](/topics/psychedelics-and-the-filter-model/), [predictive-processing-and-dualism](/topics/predictive-processing-and-dualism/)). EBH is a physicalist theory but is *metaphysically modular*: it predicts a *quantity* (entropy/criticality) correlating with conscious *richness*, while remaining silent on what makes the activity phenomenal. That correlational-but-not-explanatory structure is the exact wedge the Map's filter/dualist reading exploits. An article should foreground the entropy/criticality dimension (not yet covered in depth) rather than re-treading the REBUS/filter ground.

## Key Sources

### The entropic brain: a theory of conscious states informed by neuroimaging research with psychedelic drugs (2014)
- **URL**: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/human-neuroscience/articles/10.3389/fnhum.2014.00020/full (also PMC3909994)
- **Type**: Paper (founding statement of EBH), *Frontiers in Human Neuroscience*
- **Authors**: Carhart-Harris RL, Leech R, Hellyer PJ, Shanahan M, Feilding A, Tagliazucchi E, Chialvo DR, Nutt D
- **Key points**:
  - Entropy of spontaneous brain activity indexes the informational richness of a conscious state.
  - Introduces "primary" (entropic, less constrained, e.g. psychedelic, early psychosis, REM/dreaming, infant cognition) vs "secondary" (normal adult waking, constrained, reality-testing) consciousness — a partial revival of Freudian primary/secondary process.
  - Normal waking sits just below criticality; "entropy suppression" is what gives ordinary consciousness its narrow, reality-tested, metacognitive character.
  - Markers of approach-to-criticality cited: increased repertoire/variance of brain states, reduced within-network integrity (esp. DMN), increased between-network connectivity, broader metastability.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral-to-useful. Physicalist in framing but the *richness-indexing* claim is metaphysically detachable.
- **Quote**: "the entropy of spontaneous brain activity indexes the informational richness of conscious states" (paraphrase of the abstract's core claim).

### The entropic brain – revisited (2018)
- **URL**: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0028390818301175 ; https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29548884/
- **Type**: Review/update, *Neuropharmacology* (vol. 142)
- **Author**: Carhart-Harris RL
- **Key points**:
  - Claims the "entropy elevated in the psychedelic state" principle is now well supported by independent studies.
  - Foregrounds **brain criticality** — evidence that psychedelics move the brain closer to a critical point (self-organized criticality / edge-of-chaos).
  - Offers more concrete candidate entropy measures and proposes new studies; extends EBH to psychiatric/neurological applications (depression, disorders of consciousness).
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral. The criticality framing is a physical-dynamics story; says nothing about phenomenality.

### REBUS and the Anarchic Brain: Toward a Unified Model of the Brain Action of Psychedelics (2019)
- **URL**: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6588209/ ; https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31221820/
- **Type**: Review (theoretical synthesis), *Pharmacological Reviews*
- **Authors**: Carhart-Harris RL, Friston KJ
- **Key points**:
  - Unifies EBH with Friston's **free-energy principle** / predictive processing.
  - Psychedelics relax the **precision of high-level priors/beliefs**, liberating bottom-up information flow (esp. from limbic sources).
  - The "narrative self/ego" is itself a high-level prior; relaxing it underlies ego dissolution and therapeutic "belief revision."
  - "Anarchic brain" = the loosened hierarchical control as priors lose their grip.
- **Tenet alignment**: This is the physicalist extension already engaged in [psychedelics-and-the-filter-model](/topics/psychedelics-and-the-filter-model/) and [predictive-processing-and-dualism](/topics/predictive-processing-and-dualism/). The Map treats REBUS as compatible with — but not discriminating in favour of — filter theory.

### Spotlight commentary: REBUS and the anarchic brain (2020)
- **URL**: https://academic.oup.com/nc/article/2020/1/niaa007/5856367 ; PMC7290155
- **Type**: Commentary, *Neuroscience of Consciousness*
- **Authors**: Noorani T, Alderson-Day B (and others in the spotlight collection)
- **Key points**:
  - Urges more attention to **set and setting / contextual factors** shaping extreme experiences and their aftermath.
  - Suggests REBUS's comparisons to certain non-psychedelic altered states may miss more informative parallels.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral; a methodological/scope critique, not metaphysical.

### Psychedelics, entropic brain theory, and the taxonomy of conscious states: a summary of debates and perspectives (2023)
- **URL**: https://academic.oup.com/nc/article/2023/1/niad001/7103464 ; PMC10072236 ; PubMed 37025356
- **Type**: Review, *Neuroscience of Consciousness*
- **Authors**: Rankaduwa S, Owen AM
- **Key points**:
  - Synthesises debates over EBT; notes the theory claims "level of consciousness / richness can be measured by amount of disordered brain activity."
  - Frames EBT within the broader project of using psychedelics as a *tool* to map the neural mediation of conscious dimensions.
  - Surveys what remains contested in the entropy-richness link.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral; useful balanced survey.

### Self-organized criticality as a framework for consciousness: A review study (2022)
- **URL**: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2022.911620/full ; PMC9336647
- **Type**: Review, *Frontiers in Psychology*
- **Key points**:
  - Critical systems show maximal metastable states, sensitivity to perturbation, and avalanche dynamics.
  - In EBH's framing psychedelics tune the brain *closer* to the critical point relative to normal waking (which is slightly sub-critical), while loss of consciousness moves it away.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral physical-dynamics framework.

### Edge-of-chaos / near-critical electrodynamics during conscious states (2021–2025 criticality literature)
- **URLs**:
  - "Consciousness is supported by near-critical cortical electrodynamics" — https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2021.06.10.447959 (Toker et al.; later PNAS 2022)
  - "Criticality supports cross-frequency cortical-thalamic information transfer during conscious states" — https://elifesciences.org/articles/86547
  - "Differential effects of propofol and ketamine on critical brain dynamics" — https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7785236/
  - "DMT-induced shifts in criticality correlate with self-dissolution" (2025) — https://www.jneurosci.org/content/jneuro/early/2025/10/24/JNEUROSCI.0344-25.2025.full.pdf
- **Type**: Empirical papers
- **Key points**:
  - Conscious states operate near an **edge-of-chaos** critical point (transition between periodic/stable and chaotic/unstable dynamics).
  - Psychedelics push the brain *toward* criticality; anaesthesia pushes it *away*. Propofol dramatically restricts avalanche size/duration and collapses dynamical complexity; **ketamine preserves more awake-like dynamics** (an important asymmetry).
  - 2025 DMT work: shifts in criticality correlate with degree of self-dissolution — directly links the criticality measure to a specific phenomenological dimension.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral but *interesting for the Map*: the anaesthesia asymmetry (ketamine vs propofol) connects to the Map's [anaesthesia-and-the-consciousness-interface](/topics/anaesthesia-and-the-consciousness-interface/) treatment.

### Critiques of entropy measures (Papo 2016 commentary; Lempel–Ziv robustness)
- **URLs**:
  - Papo D, "Commentary: The entropic brain..." — https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/human-neuroscience/articles/10.3389/fnhum.2016.00423/full ; PMC5004455
- **Type**: Commentary
- **Key points**:
  - Entropy (a measure of randomness) may be **ill-qualified per se** to characterise brain activity, which is neither fully regular nor fully random.
  - "Metastability" as defined (variance in network synchrony) may just count metastable states rather than measure metastability/criticality; entropy, metastability and variance can collapse into the same information.
  - A *true* configurational entropy requires extensive phase-space exploration; otherwise the measure reflects only "the random side of complexity."
  - Later work flagged **Lempel–Ziv complexity** — a flagship EBH measure — as yielding the *weakest* associations in some studies, raising replication/robustness questions.
- **Tenet alignment**: Supports the Map's "measure ≠ explanation" wedge — the *operationalisation* of the central quantity is itself contested.

## Major Positions

### Entropic Brain Hypothesis / Entropic Brain Theory (EBH/EBT)
- **Proponents**: Carhart-Harris, Nutt, Leech, Tagliazucchi, Chialvo (criticality), Friston (via REBUS).
- **Core claim**: Brain-activity entropy indexes the richness of conscious content; consciousness sits between an upper and lower entropy bound; waking is slightly sub-critical; psychedelics raise entropy toward/past criticality.
- **Key arguments**:
  - Cross-study evidence of elevated entropy/complexity in psychedelic states (EEG/MEG Lempel–Ziv, fMRI repertoire/variance, between-network connectivity).
  - Criticality signatures (avalanche statistics, long-range temporal correlations, critical slowing) track conscious level.
  - Explains the "less activity / more experience" paradox and ego dissolution within one framework.
- **Relation to site tenets**: The theory is a *correlational/structural* claim about a physical quantity. It is compatible with the Map's filter reading: "entropy indexes richness" can be re-described as "the filter's bandwidth widens." It does **not** explain *why* high-entropy activity is *felt* — the hard problem is untouched, which the Map can press.

### Brain Criticality / Self-Organized Criticality view
- **Proponents**: Chialvo, Toker, Tagliazucchi, Atasoy; broader complex-systems neuroscience.
- **Core claim**: Consciousness is supported by near-critical (edge-of-chaos) dynamics; richness/flexibility is maximal near criticality; LOC moves the system sub-critical (ordered) and psychedelics move it super-critical/closer-to-critical.
- **Key arguments**: avalanche power laws, perturbational complexity (PCI), ketamine-vs-propofol dissociation, cortico-thalamic information transfer peaks at criticality.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Provides a precise physical *signature* of conscious states without explaining phenomenality — again, a mechanism-but-not-explanation pattern the Map foregrounds. The criticality/interface analogy is fertile: criticality could be re-read as the dynamical *condition under which the interface operates*, not the *source* of consciousness.

### REBUS / Predictive-processing extension
- **Proponents**: Carhart-Harris & Friston.
- **Core claim**: High-level priors lose precision under psychedelics; bottom-up flow is liberated; entropy rise = relaxation of hierarchical Bayesian control.
- **Relation to site tenets**: Already treated by the Map. Compatible with filter theory; the formalism is metaphysically neutral (per [predictive-processing-and-dualism](/topics/predictive-processing-and-dualism/)). REBUS supplies *mechanism*, not *metaphysics*.

### Naturalistic-deflationary opposition (relevant rival)
- **Proponents**: Chris Letheby (predictive self-binding); Adam Safron (ALBUS — "Altered Beliefs Under Psychedelics," 2025, refines REBUS with bidirectional precision changes); Rankaduwa & Owen (cautious taxonomy).
- **Core claim**: Entropy/criticality and prior-relaxation fully explain psychedelic phenomenology *within physicalism*; "more real than real" = opaque self-models, not access to anything beyond the brain.
- **Relation to site tenets**: The strongest opponent of the Map's filter/dualist reading; the EBH article must engage it (as the filter-model article already does with Letheby).

## Key Debates

### Does entropy *index*, *constitute*, or merely *correlate with* conscious richness?
- **Sides**: EBH proponents treat entropy as a robust *index* of richness; critics (Papo) argue entropy is conceptually ill-suited and the chosen operationalisations conflate distinct quantities.
- **Core disagreement**: Whether a single scalar (entropy/criticality) can capture the multidimensionality of conscious states, and whether it explains anything or just labels them.
- **Current state**: Ongoing. The 2023 Rankaduwa & Owen survey and the push toward *dimensional* taxonomies of consciousness reflect dissatisfaction with one-number accounts.

### Are the entropy measures reliable? (Lempel–Ziv robustness)
- **Sides**: Early enthusiasm for LZ complexity as a conscious-level correlate vs later studies finding it among the *weakest* predictors.
- **Core disagreement**: Whether headline results replicate across measures, modalities (EEG/MEG/fMRI), and labs.
- **Current state**: Unresolved; an active methodological concern.

### Is REBUS/EBH falsifiable?
- **Sides**: Critics note predictive-processing frameworks have many free parameters (priors, precision weights, approximations) that can post-hoc fit almost any result; defenders point to specific predictions (entropy ↑ under psychedelics; criticality shifts).
- **Core disagreement**: Whether the framework makes risky predictions or merely re-describes.
- **Current state**: Live; ALBUS (Safron) and REBAS refinements partly respond by specifying dose-dependent, bidirectional precision changes.

### Is waking really sub-critical, or is criticality the conscious baseline?
- **Sides**: EBH (waking slightly sub-critical, psychedelics → critical/super-critical) vs criticality theorists who hold that *conscious waking itself* is near-critical and LOC is the departure.
- **Core disagreement**: Where on the order–chaos axis ordinary consciousness sits, and whether psychedelics increase or merely reorganise criticality.
- **Current state**: Partially convergent — most agree LOC is sub-critical/ordered; the waking-vs-psychedelic placement is debated.

## Potential Article Angles

Based on this research, an article could:

1. **"The Entropic Brain Hypothesis" as a topic article centred on the entropy/criticality dimension** (the gap not yet covered). Thesis: EBH supplies a *quantitative dynamical signature* of conscious richness but is metaphysically modular — it indexes *that* richness varies with entropy/criticality without explaining *why* the activity is felt. The Map reads the entropy–richness correlation as a description of the **filter's bandwidth**: widening the filter (raising entropy toward criticality) admits richer content; this re-description is empirically equivalent to the physicalist reading on the altered-state data alone (mirroring the discipline already used in [psychedelics-and-the-filter-model](/topics/psychedelics-and-the-filter-model/)). Connects to Tenet 1 (dualism: entropy correlates with but does not constitute phenomenality) and the [evidential-status-discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/).

2. **Criticality-as-interface-condition angle**: re-read near-critical dynamics as the *dynamical regime under which the consciousness interface operates at maximal bandwidth*, with the ketamine-vs-propofol anaesthesia asymmetry as a bridge to [anaesthesia-and-the-consciousness-interface](/topics/anaesthesia-and-the-consciousness-interface/). Honest caveat: this is interpretive overlay, not a discriminating prediction.

3. **The measurement-honesty angle**: foreground the Papo critique and Lempel–Ziv replication concerns to show that even the *physical quantity* at the heart of EBH is contested — reinforcing the Map's [meta-epistemology-of-limits](/voids/meta-epistemology-of-limits/) point that "we have a number" is not "we have an explanation."
