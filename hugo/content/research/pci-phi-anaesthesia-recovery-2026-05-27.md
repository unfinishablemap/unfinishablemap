## Executive Summary (HONEST ASSESSMENT — READ FIRST)

**The claim as currently worded overreaches the literature and should be rewritten before any citation is installed.** Two distinct sub-claims are being conflated:

1. **"Integration measures dissociate from behavioural responsiveness"** — *strongly supported* in the 2024–2025 literature. PCI and related complexity/Φ measures stay high in states where behaviour is absent (ketamine unresponsiveness, REM, disconnected dreaming). This is the well-established dissociation, and it is the genuine, citable finding.

2. **"Integration measures recover *before* behavioural responsiveness *during emergence*, consistent with the workspace being prepared in advance"** — this specific *temporal-ordering* claim about emergence is **NOT what the 2024–2025 PCI/Φ papers establish.** The papers I verified treat consciousness-capacity (PCI, Φ) as a *level/state* measure that is low under anaesthesia and recovers — they compare states (awake vs. sedated vs. recovery), not the fine-grained *temporal sequence* of integration-recovery vs. behaviour-return during a single emergence episode. None of the headline 2024–2025 PCI/Φ papers I found reports "integration recovers and *then* behaviour follows" as a timed sequence.

The closest real paper for the *temporal-precedence* idea is **Lewis et al. (2018)** — a transient sleep-like cortical state that *precedes* return of behavioural responsiveness — but (a) it is 2018, not 2024–2025, and (b) it is about a K-complex-like *sensory-gating* state, not about integrated-information/PCI recovery. So even the precedence intuition is anchored by an older, mechanistically different finding than the sentence implies.

**Recommendation for the downstream refine pass:** Either (A) narrow the sentence to the dissociation that the literature actually supports — *"PCI and Φ measures track consciousness independently of behavioural responsiveness, staying high in unresponsive states such as ketamine and REM"* (cite Onoda 2025, Breyton 2025, Van Maldegem 2025) — or (B) keep a precedence framing but explicitly source it to Lewis et al. (2018) and mark it as a distinct, older, sensory-gating finding rather than "2024–2025 PCI and Φ literature." Do **not** cite the 2024–2025 PCI/Φ papers *as* support for the temporal-ordering claim; they don't make it.

**Evidential tier (per [evidential-status-discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/)):**
- The *dissociation* (PCI/Φ ≠ behavioural responsiveness): **Strongly supported.**
- *Integration-recovers-before-behaviour as a timed emergence sequence*: **Live hypothesis** at best on the 2024–2025 PCI/Φ corpus; the corpus is largely *silent* on the timing rather than confirming it. Calling it a finding of "recent IIT work" is a calibration error of the constrain-vs-establish type the discipline is built to catch.

## Key Sources (ALL WEB-VERIFIED)

### Onoda, Miyauchi, Kan & Akama (2025) — Φ decrease/recovery, fMRI
- **Full citation**: Onoda, K., Miyauchi, S., Kan, S., & Akama, H. (2025). Decrease and recovery of integrated information Φ during anesthesia and sleep on human functional magnetic resonance imaging. *Neuroscience of Consciousness*, 2025(1), niaf024. DOI: 10.1093/nc/niaf024. PMID: 40901488.
- **Type**: Empirical paper (human fMRI).
- **Key points**:
  - Φ (integrated information) decreased during anaesthesia-induced loss of consciousness at both global and local levels.
  - Φ was lower during mild and deep sedation than during wake and recovery stages.
  - Treats "recovery" as a *state* (wake / mild / deep / recovery), **not** a temporal-ordering experiment.
- **Does it support the claim?** Supports "Φ tracks consciousness level and recovers"; **does NOT** support "Φ recovers *before* behavioural responsiveness." Silent on the timing.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral on tenets; compatible with both filter and production readings (Φ-tracking is interpretation-agnostic).

### Breyton, Fousek, Rabuffo, Sorrentino, Kusch, Massimini, Petkoski & Jirsa (2025) — spontaneous complexity tracks consciousness
- **Full citation**: Breyton, M., Fousek, J., Rabuffo, G., Sorrentino, P., Kusch, L., Massimini, M., Petkoski, S., & Jirsa, V. (2025). Spatiotemporal brain complexity quantifies consciousness outside of perturbation paradigms. *eLife*, 13, RP98920. DOI: 10.7554/eLife.98920. PMID: 41128753.
- **Type**: Empirical/modelling (resting-state complexity).
- **Key points**:
  - "Fluidity"/complexity metrics from *spontaneous* activity discriminate conscious vs. unconscious states across anaesthetics — i.e. you can get PCI-like discrimination without the TMS perturbation.
  - Massimini (Milan PCI group) is a co-author — this is the closest "Massimini-group 2025" paper to the article's framing.
  - Acknowledges "behavioral response might break down, but consciousness doesn't necessarily vanish" (the dissociation).
- **Does it support the claim?** Supports the *dissociation* and the *level-tracking*; **static state comparisons, not recovery-timing trajectories.** Does NOT establish integration-before-behaviour during emergence.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral; the dissociation between complexity and behaviour is the kind of fact filter theory finds congenial but does not require.

### Van Maldegem, Vohryzek, Atasoy, et al. (2025) — connectome harmonics track disconnected consciousness under ketamine
- **Full citation**: Van Maldegem, M., Vohryzek, J., Atasoy, S., et al. (2025). Connectome harmonic decomposition tracks the presence of disconnected consciousness during ketamine-induced unresponsiveness. *British Journal of Anaesthesia*, 134(4), 1088–1104. DOI: 10.1016/j.bja.2024.12.036. PMID: 39933965.
- **Type**: Empirical (human, ketamine).
- **Key points**:
  - Connectome harmonic decomposition (CHD) tracks alterations in conscious awareness *independently of behavioural responsiveness*.
  - Ketamine "decouples these two facets" — awareness vs. behavioural responsiveness; ketamine signature aligns with psychedelic states, not with propofol/brain-injury unconsciousness.
- **Does it support the claim?** Best verified support for the **dissociation** sub-claim (integration-type signal preserved while behaviour is absent). Does NOT speak to emergence *timing*.
- **Tenet alignment**: Directly relevant to the article's existing ketamine point (PCI/complexity preserved while access is blocked) — coheres with the filter reading the article already uses elsewhere. Note: this is *compatibility*, not discrimination (see [evidential-status-discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/) §"coherence vs. evidential status").

### Lewis, Piantoni, Peterfreund, et al. (2018) — transient cortical state PRECEDES emergence (the real "precedence" anchor — but 2018, not 2024–2025)
- **Full citation**: Lewis, L. D., Piantoni, G., Peterfreund, R. A., Eskandar, E. N., Harrell, P. G., Akeju, O., Aglio, L. S., Cash, S. S., Brown, E. N., Mukamel, E. A., & Purdon, P. L. (2018). A transient cortical state with sleep-like sensory responses precedes emergence from general anesthesia in humans. *eLife*, 7, e33250. DOI: 10.7554/eLife.33250. PMID: 30095069.
- **Type**: Empirical (human iEEG + scalp EEG, propofol).
- **Key points**:
  - A distinct transient brain state with large, distributed, K-complex-like slow sensory-evoked potentials occurs *after propofol is off but before signs of recovery*.
  - "LPs mostly occur in the minutes preceding ROC [return of consciousness]."
- **Does it support the claim?** This is the genuine *"a neural state precedes behavioural recovery"* finding — but it is about a **sensory-gating / sleep-like state**, NOT integrated-information / PCI recovery, and it is 2018. Using it to anchor a "2024–2025 PCI and Φ" sentence would be a date-and-mechanism mismatch. If a precedence framing is kept, this is the paper to cite, with the framing corrected.
- **Tenet alignment**: Congenial to the "workspace prepared before consciousness re-enters" reading; the article's §"The Active Reboot" already gestures at exactly this ("dynamics normalising just prior to connected consciousness"). The Mashour 2021 + Lewis 2018 pairing already in/near the section is the honest anchor for the precedence intuition.

### Tasserie, Uhrig, Sitt, Manasova, Dupont, Dehaene & Jarraya (2022) — DBS restores consciousness signatures (Paris group)
- **Full citation**: Tasserie, J., Uhrig, L., Sitt, J. D., Manasova, D., Dupont, M., Dehaene, S., & Jarraya, B. (2022). Deep brain stimulation of the thalamus restores signatures of consciousness in a nonhuman primate model. *Science Advances*, 8(11), eabl5547. DOI: 10.1126/sciadv.abl5547. PMID: 35302854.
- **Type**: Empirical (macaque, central-thalamus DBS under anaesthesia).
- **Key points**:
  - Central-thalamic DBS during anaesthesia restored arousal *and* neural signatures of consciousness (broad dynamic repertoire of spontaneous activity; hierarchical response to auditory violations); control-site (ventrolateral) stimulation did not.
  - This is the Sitt/Jarraya-group result that grounds the "central thalamus as an active switch" idea — relevant to the article's "active reboot / dedicated reopening pathway" thesis but at the *circuit* level, not the PCI-timing level.
- **Does it support the claim?** Supports the broader "active reboot / dedicated reopening machinery" thesis; it is 2022, not 2024–2025, and is about *causally restoring* signatures via stimulation, not the natural emergence timing of integration vs. behaviour.
- **Tenet alignment**: Strongly congenial to [Bidirectional Interaction](/tenets/#bidirectional-interaction) — a dedicated circuit reopens the channel. Caveat per discipline: equally readable as production-side "switch turned back on." Compatibility, not discrimination.

### Luppi, Golkowski, Ranft, Ilg, Jordan, Bzdok, et al. (2025) — anaesthesia reduces brain-fingerprint uniqueness
- **Full citation**: Luppi, A. I., Golkowski, D., Ranft, A., Ilg, R., Jordan, D., Bzdok, D., et al. (2025). General anaesthesia decreases the uniqueness of brain functional connectivity across individuals and species. *Nature Human Behaviour*, 9(5), 987–1004. DOI: 10.1038/s41562-025-02121-9. PMID: 40128306.
- **Type**: Empirical (human + cross-species fMRI).
- **Key points**:
  - Under anaesthesia, individual brains become less self-similar and less distinguishable from each other and from other primates; depth-dependent; reproducible across sevoflurane and propofol; **reversed on recovery**.
- **Does it support the claim?** Tangential. Documents an integration-related effect that reverses on recovery, but says nothing about *integration-vs-behaviour timing*. Useful background, not a load-bearing anchor.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral.

### Luppi, et al. (2026) — convergent transcriptomic/connectomic controllers of integration breakdown (DISTINCT paper; note the date)
- **Full citation**: Luppi, A. I., et al. (2026). Convergent transcriptomic and connectomic controllers of information integration and its anaesthetic breakdown across mammalian brains. *Nature Human Behaviour*. DOI: 10.1038/s41562-025-02381-5. Published online 2026-01-28. (PMID not yet indexed at time of search.)
- **Type**: Empirical/modelling (humans, macaques, marmosets, mice).
- **Key points**:
  - Breakdown of information integration is a convergent effect of diverse anaesthetics across mammals; regional breakdown coincides with PVALB/Pvalb gene-expression topography.
  - **About WHERE/HOW integration breaks down (spatial topography, molecular controllers), NOT recovery timing.**
- **Does it support the claim?** No — wrong axis (spatial/molecular, not temporal). Also published Jan 2026, so it is not "2024–2025" literature.
- **CAUTION FOR THE REFINE PASS**: The GitHub repo `netneurolab/luppi-cognitive-matching` mislabels this DOI with the *other* Luppi paper's title ("General anaesthesia reduces the uniqueness..."). The two are distinct papers with distinct DOIs (02121-9 vs 02381-5). Do not merge them. This is exactly the citation-metadata-unreliability pattern flagged in project memory — verify against the Nature page / PubMed, not against the repo README.
- **Tenet alignment**: Neutral.

## Major Positions / What the Field Actually Holds

### Position A: PCI/Φ are *capacity-for-consciousness* measures that dissociate from behaviour
- **Proponents**: Massimini/Sarasso/Casali (Milan); Sitt/Naccache/Dehaene/Jarraya (Paris); Jirsa group; Onoda et al.
- **Core claim**: PCI (and spontaneous-complexity / Φ surrogates) index the brain's *capacity* for integrated, differentiated information, which tracks consciousness *level* and is robustly *dissociable from behavioural responsiveness* — high in ketamine unresponsiveness and REM where behaviour is absent but experience is present.
- **Relation to the article's claim**: This is the solid, citable core. It justifies "integration measures and behavioural responsiveness come apart." It does **not** by itself justify a temporal-precedence-during-emergence claim.

### Position B: Emergence is preceded by an identifiable transient neural state
- **Proponents**: Lewis/Purdon/Brown (2018); Mashour group (already cited in the article).
- **Core claim**: A specific transient cortical state (sleep-like K-complex sensory responses) appears in the minutes *before* return of behavioural responsiveness.
- **Relation to the article's claim**: This is the real "workspace prepared in advance" anchor — but it is a *sensory-gating* state, not "integration/Φ recovery," and it is 2018. The article's neighbouring sentences (Mashour et al. 2021; "dynamics normalising just prior to connected consciousness") already carry this load correctly; the disputed sentence tries to add a PCI/Φ-flavoured restatement that the PCI/Φ papers don't actually supply.

### Position C (the trap): cluster-coherence inflation
- The temptation is to read Positions A + B + the Hu et al. KCC2 "active reboot" + Tasserie DBS as a *cluster* that jointly establishes "integration recovers before behaviour as part of a prepared-workspace mechanism." Per [evidential-status-discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/) §"Coherence vs. Evidential Status", cluster-coherence earns *framework-internal weight*, not a tier-upgrade for the specific empirical timing claim. The honest verdict: the *dissociation* is strongly supported; the *prepared-in-advance temporal ordering as an IIT/PCI finding* is a live hypothesis the cited corpus does not discriminate.

## Potential Refine Angles (for the downstream chain)

1. **Minimal fix (recommended)** — Replace the disputed sentence with the dissociation it can actually support:
   > "Complexity and integrated-information measures (PCI, Φ, connectome-harmonic signatures) track consciousness independently of behavioural responsiveness — they stay high in unresponsive states such as ketamine sedation and REM (Onoda et al. 2025; Breyton et al. 2025; Van Maldegem et al. 2025)."
   This is honest, citable, and reinforces the article's existing ketamine point.

2. **Precedence fix** — If the "prepared in advance" framing is to be kept, anchor it on Lewis et al. (2018) and label it correctly as a sensory-gating transient state preceding behavioural recovery, *not* as a 2024–2025 PCI/Φ finding. Pair with the Mashour 2021 sentence already present.

3. **Do not** install any 2024–2025 PCI/Φ citation *as* support for "recovers before behavioural responsiveness" as a timed emergence sequence — that mapping is unsupported and would be a calibration error.
