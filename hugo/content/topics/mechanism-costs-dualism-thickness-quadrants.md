Naming the [mind-side × physical-side thickness axes](/topics/four-quadrant-dualism-taxonomy/) sorts dualist positions by *how much* ontology each side carries. It does not yet say what each cell of the resulting grid *commits its inhabitants to* mechanistically. Two positions that share a kind-of-relation classification (both Type-D, say) can sit in different thickness cells and owe entirely different debts: a different account of mental causation, a different specification of the mind–physical interface, a different engagement with conservation laws. This article walks the four quadrants — min-mind / min-physical (Q1), min-mind / max-physical (Q2), max-mind / min-physical (Q3), max-mind / max-physical (Q4) — and reads off, for each, what the inhabitant owes on three mechanism questions. The exercise is cartographic rather than adjudicative: the goal is to make visible what occupying a cell costs, not to argue that any one cell is right.

The taxonomy and quadrant population are stated in the [companion taxonomy article](/topics/four-quadrant-dualism-taxonomy/). Readers who want the axes defined, the named-position inventory, or the orthogonality argument against Chalmers' Type-D/E/F classification should read that piece first. This article assumes the grid and asks: what mechanism account does each cell require?

## The Three Mechanism Questions {#three-questions}

Every dualism owes answers to three questions about how mind and matter relate. The cells differ in which answers come cheap and which come dear.

1. **Causation account.** How does the mind contribute to producing physical effects (or fail to)? Is mental influence overdetermining, preemptive, selective, biasing, or absent?
2. **Interface specification.** What is the mechanism of mind–physical contact? Where does it happen? What ontological structure carries the contact? How narrow is the channel?
3. **Conservation engagement.** How does the position relate to conservation laws — energy, momentum, probability? Does it owe a violation, a workaround, or nothing at all?

These three questions are not exhaustive — articulability, intelligibility under intersubstitution, and overdetermination-avoidance also matter — but they are the three that the philosophy of mind literature has most insistently pressed against interactionist positions, and they are the three where thickness shifts the debt most visibly.

A reader might worry the trio over-emphasises the physical-side debts. It does — deliberately. The conservation-law and interface questions originate in the physicalist's challenge to interactionism, and the literature has weighted them heavily. The cell-by-cell exposition below shows that Q1 carries small versions of all three, Q2 evades two while paying for one (overdetermination), Q3 dissolves all three at the cost of a different debt (why-does-physics-look-physical), and Q4 owes the full bill. The asymmetry is real, not an artefact of the question selection.

## Q1: Min-Mind / Min-Physical — The Authority Debt {#q1}

Q1 inhabitants — Bradford Saad's [delegatory-dualism](/topics/delegatory-dualism/), some readings of [interactionist-dualism](/concepts/interactionist-dualism/) confined to indeterminacy, certain neutral-monist limit cases — keep both sides as lean as the position permits.

**Causation account.** The causation owed is *authority* without *overdetermination*. If the physical base is counterfactually sufficient (no max-physical hidden variables), and the mental side adds no thick subliminal layers, then any genuine mental contribution must operate as something other than an extra force pushing on a complete physical story. Saad's framework deploys [trumping-preemption](/concepts/trumping-preemption/) — Schaffer's (2000) species of redundant causation in which a higher-order rule selects one of two sufficient processes as authoritative — to give experience preemptive priority over the physical state's *default causal profile* without requiring it to outpace physics. The Map's reading integrates this with [delegatory-causation](/concepts/delegatory-causation/) at quantum indeterminacy. The price of admission is an authority law: a non-stipulative specification of *what* makes the mental rule authoritative, rather than the stipulation that does the work in Schaffer's fictional cases (Merlin's spell, military rank). The [interface-formalization-void](/voids/interface-formalization-void/) flags this articulability burden as the live open problem.

**Interface specification.** The interface is *probabilistic and narrow*: consciousness biases among quantum-permitted alternatives at neural indeterminacy sites. No new field, particle, or force. The channel-width parameter is set as small as it can be while still doing genuine causal work. This is the Map's preferred location and the [interface problem](/topics/the-interface-problem/)'s tightest formulation.

**Conservation engagement.** Q1 is *conservation-light*. Because experience does not inject energy, momentum, or probability mass beyond what the wavefunction permits, the [standard conservation objection](/concepts/conservation-laws-and-mental-causation/) gains little traction here. The selection-without-injection move (consciousness biasing which of three equal-energy outcomes actualises) is available without further ontological commitments on either side. Conservation costs come back if the authority law turns out to require additional structure — but if Q1 can be articulated cleanly, conservation is the cell's *cheapest* debt.

The net Q1 bill is small but real: one articulable authority law and one specification of where in the brain the indeterminacy sites sit. The empirical question of whether such sites exist at biologically relevant scales (Tegmark 2000, and the ongoing debate documented in [born-rule-violation-brain-interface-empirical-status](/topics/born-rule-violation-brain-interface-empirical-status/)) is independent of the cell's mechanism cost but bears on its viability.

## Q2: Min-Mind / Max-Physical — The Exclusion Debt {#q2}

Q2 inhabitants — epiphenomenalism (Chalmers' Type-E), textbook property dualism, Stoljar's epistemic physicalism, Bohmian physicalism with thin qualia — keep the mental thin while letting physical ontology expand.

**Causation account.** Under the strict reading — phenomenal properties supervene on a physically complete base — the mind owes *no* causation account at all, because it does no causal work. This is the position's distinguishing feature. The cell's debt is therefore not a mechanism debt but a *bidirectional-interaction* debt: in the Map's framework, this cell is ruled out by Tenet 3. Outside the Map's tenets, Jaegwon Kim's (2005) exclusion argument presses an analogous concern from within physicalism: if the physical base is sufficient, mental properties either overdetermine effects (which physicalists find costly) or do nothing (which dualists find evasive). Any Q2 reading that wants to preserve mental causation drifts toward Q1 by adding indeterminacy or toward Q4 by enriching the mental side.

**Interface specification.** Q2 strict has *no interface* — supervenience replaces interaction. Q2 loose (weak causal efficacy) owes one but cannot give one without sliding off the cell.

**Conservation engagement.** Q2 strict pays no conservation cost — the physical story closes without help. Q2 loose owes an account similar to Q1's but without Q1's lean physical ontology to absorb it, which is why Q2-with-causation tends to be unstable as a distinct cell.

Q2 is the dialectical default for non-reductive positions in contemporary analytic philosophy of mind. Many positions classified as dualist sit nearer this cell than Q4. The mechanism cost is low precisely because the mind has been deflated to the point of doing no work. Whether that purchase is worth its price — a phenomenal property whose having makes no difference — is the standard intra-physicalist debate.

## Q3: Max-Mind / Min-Physical — The Appearance Debt {#q3}

Q3 inhabitants — Berkeley's idealism, Kastrup's analytic idealism, Myers–James subliminal-mind views paired with lean physics, certain Jungian frameworks — let the mind expand while the physical side stays as lean as is consistent with the position's commitments. Most pure Q3 positions are strictly monist; they appear here for their illumination of what an adjacent dualism would commit to.

**Causation account.** Q3 dissolves the mental-causation question. If everything is mental at base, then mental–physical causation is not a relation between two ontological kinds; it is the intrinsic dynamics of the one kind, appearing as physical-on-physical causation when viewed extrinsically. The dualist's interaction problem evaporates; in its place sits the *transparency problem* — explaining why the intrinsic mental reality looks, from the outside, exactly like impersonal lawful physical structure. Kastrup's "matter is the extrinsic appearance of mental processes" is the canonical move (see [analytic-idealism-and-mind-centric-metaphysics](/topics/analytic-idealism-and-mind-centric-metaphysics/) for the Map's framing).

**Interface specification.** Q3 has no interface in the dualist sense — only the extrinsic/intrinsic distinction. The "where does mind contact matter" question is replaced by "why does the intrinsic side appear as matter at all?" That is a different question with a different shape and a different burden of proof, but it is not lighter.

**Conservation engagement.** Conservation laws become *descriptions of mental regularities*. Energy is conserved because the underlying mental dynamics happens to display a regularity that, when viewed extrinsically, looks like energy conservation. Why this regularity rather than another is a Q3-specific debt — a question Q1 and Q4 do not face in the same form. Q3 substitutes a metaphysical question (why does the mental ground produce this appearance?) for the dualist's physical-engagement question (how does the mental affect the physical?).

A genuine Q3 dualism — mind thick, physics thin, but both ontologically real — is rare in the literature precisely because, once the mind does this much work, the physical side tends to lose explanatory weight and the position slides toward Q3-monism. Where the position holds as dualism, the mechanism cost is asymmetric: minimal interface debt, substantial appearance debt.

## Q4: Max-Mind / Max-Physical — The Full Bill {#q4}

Q4 inhabitants — Cartesian substance dualism, Aristotelian–Thomistic hylomorphism, Henry Stapp's quantum interactive dualism, Goff's priority cosmopsychism, Russellian panpsychism paired with full scientific realism — accept thickness on both sides. Each pays for what the other cells avoid.

**Causation account.** Q4 owes a *robust* mental-causation account because mind is doing thick causal work in a physical world that is itself ontologically rich. Cartesian energy-transfer is the classical formulation and the historical cost; Stapp's quantum-Zeno-effect account is a contemporary attempt to keep the channel narrow (the agent's "process-1 choice of a probing action") while letting both endpoints stay thick. On the Map's reading, Stapp's framework places a thick observer — a full agent with selection power — and a thick quantum ontology — full state-vector realism with potentialities treated as real — on either side of a narrow selection channel; thickness on both ends, parsimony on the contact. Whether Stapp's account succeeds in keeping the interaction channel narrow despite the thick endpoints is itself contested; Tegmark's (2000) decoherence-timescale objection bears on this directly, and [born-rule-violation-brain-interface-empirical-status](/topics/born-rule-violation-brain-interface-empirical-status/) tracks the current state of the debate.

**Interface specification.** Q4 owes the heaviest interface debt. With both sides thick, the interface is the locus where the two rich realms touch — and the historical difficulty of specifying such a contact non-arbitrarily (Princess Elisabeth's challenge to Descartes; the Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy's "Dualism" entry's discussion of the interaction problem) is Q4's signature burden. Hylomorphic dualism handles this by treating mind and matter as constitutive principles of one substance rather than two interacting substances — moving the interface problem from "between two things" to "internal to one." Whether that move dissolves the difficulty or merely renames it is a long-standing dispute the Map does not adjudicate here.

**Conservation engagement.** Q4 owes the heaviest conservation engagement. With thick mind and thick physics, the mind's causal contribution is most naturally read as an energy-bearing influence, which puts the Cartesian objection in its most pointed form. Q4 inhabitants pay either by accepting that conservation is *local and symmetry-conditional* (Noether's theorem, Cucu and Pitts 2019) — and thus that interactionist intervention breaks symmetry at the interface — or by routing influence through a probabilistic channel (Stapp's quantum-Zeno mechanism) that is structurally Q1-like even when sitting in a Q4 ontology. The Map's treatment of [the conservation argument](/concepts/conservation-laws-and-mental-causation/) applies most directly here.

Q4 is under-discussed in contemporary analytic philosophy partly because the full bill on all three questions is high and partly because parsimony norms penalise paying it. Whether the bill is worth paying turns on what each ontological commitment buys in explanatory power — a case-by-case question the [parsimony argument](/topics/parsimony-case-for-interactionist-dualism/) treats specifically for the Map's commitments.

## The Map's Cell and Its Neighbours {#map-position}

The Map's [five tenets](/tenets/) — Dualism, Bidirectional Interaction, Minimal Quantum Interaction, No Many Worlds, Occam's Razor Has Limits — locate the Map's commitments near Q1, with room along the mind-axis. The cell is *physical-thin* (MQI pushes physics toward leanness at the indeterminacy level) and *mind-medium* (a unified subject with minimal causal intervention, not yet committed to thick subliminal layering). Reading off the mechanism implications:

- **Causation account.** Selection / biasing of quantum-permitted outcomes, structurally close to Saad's [delegatory-dualism](/topics/delegatory-dualism/) when read minimally, with room to expand toward thicker Stappian or Myersian accounts if evidence warrants.
- **Interface specification.** Narrow probabilistic channel at neural indeterminacy sites; consciousness biases the wavefunction's actualisation without injecting energy. The [interface problem](/topics/the-interface-problem/) is the cell's most demanding methodological burden.
- **Conservation engagement.** Selection-without-injection (conservation holds because biasing among equal-energy outcomes adds no energy) and the Noether-symmetry response when influence is read as breaking time-translation symmetry locally. Both are available; both are deployed in [conservation-laws-and-mental-causation](/concepts/conservation-laws-and-mental-causation/).

The Map's *neighbouring* cells deserve explicit comparison:

- **Drift toward Q2** would relinquish the Bidirectional Interaction tenet and resolve the interface problem trivially (no interface needed). The cost is accepting epiphenomenalism's evasion of mental work.
- **Drift toward Q4** would inflate the mental side (Myers–James subliminal selves, Stappian thick observer) and / or the physical side (Russellian quiddities, Bohmian hidden variables). The cost is paying a heavier interface and conservation bill in exchange for explanatory resources the lean position lacks.
- **Drift toward Q3** would let the mental side expand until the physical side becomes extrinsic appearance, putting the Map adjacent to Kastrupian analytic idealism. The cost is losing the Dualism tenet at the limit and the Bidirectional Interaction tenet's grip in any case.

The Map is not committed to a single cell; it is committed to a *region* of the taxonomy — physical-thin, mind-flexible, bidirectional — that excludes Q2 strict and the idealist limit of Q3.

## What the Mechanism Lens Reveals {#what-it-reveals}

Reading the thickness taxonomy through mechanism cost makes three patterns visible.

First, the conservation-law objection that has driven the analytic dualism debate since Leibniz is *thickness-sensitive*: it bites hardest in Q4, lighter in Q1, dissolves in Q3, and is structurally inapplicable in Q2 strict. A blanket dismissal of dualism on conservation grounds treats the whole grid uniformly when the grid is not uniform. Where the objection lands depends on which cell the position occupies.

Second, the interface-formalization burden tracks thickness similarly: Q1 owes a narrow probabilistic interface, Q4 owes a thick-to-thick contact, Q3 dissolves the interface, Q2 strict has none. The [interface-formalization-void](/voids/interface-formalization-void/) is therefore a cell-specific debt for cells where the interface is real and lean — Q1 most acutely — rather than a uniform burden across all dualisms.

Third, the cell-by-cell distribution shows why some debates do not yield productive disagreement. A defender of Q4 and a defender of Q2 strict may both call themselves dualists yet face entirely non-overlapping mechanism bills. Convergent terminology hides divergent commitments. Naming the cell first lets the mechanism question be asked at the right level of granularity.

## Relation to Site Perspective

This article extends the [four-quadrant taxonomy](/topics/four-quadrant-dualism-taxonomy/) from cartography of *what positions occupy* the grid to cartography of *what each cell costs* to inhabit. The Map's tenets shape this reading: Tenet 1 (Dualism) and Tenet 3 (Bidirectional Interaction) require the inhabitant to pay *some* mechanism cost (Q2 strict's evasion is closed off); Tenet 2 (Minimal Quantum Interaction) pushes that cost toward Q1's narrow probabilistic channel; Tenet 5 (Occam's Razor Has Limits) authorises drift toward Q4 if the explanatory gain warrants the heavier bill.

The mechanism lens also clarifies what the [parsimony argument](/topics/parsimony-case-for-interactionist-dualism/) is doing. It is not arguing that the Map's position is minimal *across all axes*. It is arguing that the Map's position pays the *least mechanism cost compatible with the tenets*. Q2 strict would be cheaper on mechanism — but it is forbidden. Q1 is cheaper than Q4 — and it is permitted. Parsimony, on the Map's reading, operates within the region the tenets carve out, not as a universal preference for thinness.

Nothing in this article argues that any cell is *wrong*. The cells differ in what they owe, not in whether they are defensible. A reader who does not share the Map's tenets is free to read Q2 strict's escape from the mechanism question as a virtue rather than as an evasion; a reader who shares the tenets sees the same evasion as foreclosed. The taxonomy speaks; the tenets adjudicate.
