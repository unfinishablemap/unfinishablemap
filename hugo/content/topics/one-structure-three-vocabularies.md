Three of the Map's articles independently reach for the same object under different names. Bradford Saad's [delegatory dualism](/topics/delegatory-dualism/) calls it the **default causal profile** — the pattern of effects a brain state would produce absent conscious takeover. The [bandwidth literature](/topics/bandwidth-of-consciousness/) calls it the **prepared options** — the small set of action policies the brain readies for consciousness to select among at ~10 bits per second. The [structural-ontological gap](/concepts/physical-completeness/) calls it the **Born-rule distribution** — the probability measure over quantum outcomes that physics specifies completely while leaving actuality-selection open. This article argues that these are plausibly **one structure described in three registers**, not three independent structures and emphatically not three independent pieces of evidence. The convergence is a coherence fact about the Map's vocabulary, and the central discipline of the article is to keep it from inflating into a confirmation fact about the Map's claims.

The load-bearing warning comes first, because it governs everything below: **convergent redescription raises the posterior that this is one structure; it does not raise the posterior that the structure is real, that consciousness selects within it, or that any tenet is true.** Stating the same thing three ways is not three witnesses agreeing. It is one witness with three accents.

## The Three Registers

Each register answers a different question about the same posited object: what consciousness takes over (delegation), what consciousness chooses among (bandwidth), and what physics leaves open for it to choose (quantum selection).

### Register A: The Default Causal Profile (Saad)

Saad's [Subset Law*](/concepts/delegatory-causation/) assigns an experience to a physical state when the experience's causal profile is a subset of the **default causal profile** of the physical state (Saad 2025). The "default" qualifier is essential and must not be dropped. As the [delegatory-dualism](/topics/delegatory-dualism/) article glosses it, the default causal profile is the pattern of effects a physical state "would produce in the absence of any experience taking over" — what the state would cause if delegation didn't occur. This is inherently counterfactual. Wherever experience is present, delegation is already occurring, so the default profile is never directly observed where the theory applies. As the [delegatory-dualism](/topics/delegatory-dualism/) article states plainly, the default causal profile is "the load-bearing primitive of the whole theory, yet its only access route — the zombie functional-duplicate — depends on a contested metaphysical possibility." It is a *postulate*, earning its keep by the coherence it buys rather than by independent observation.

### Register B: Prepared Options (Bandwidth / Libet)

The bandwidth literature describes a system that *selects among pre-computed options* rather than computing outputs from scratch. At ~10 bits per second against ~10⁹ bits per second of neural processing, consciousness "can only select among pre-computed options" — the brain prepares; consciousness chooses. The Libet readiness potential and Schultze-Kraft's (2016) "point of no return" at ~200ms fit this shape: neural preparation precedes conscious involvement because, on the selection reading, preparation *must* precede selection. The prepared options are policy-level — "raise arm," "attend to the argument" — not individual neuron firings, because the channel is too narrow to specify fine grain.

Note what this register is and is not. It is a claim about the *information structure* of conscious action: a narrow outbound channel choosing among a brain-prepared menu. It is compatible with a purely physicalist reading (the menu and the chooser both neural) and with the Map's dualist reading (the chooser non-physical). The bandwidth figure constrains the *shape* of the interface; it does not by itself establish that anything non-physical occupies it.

### Register C: The Born-Rule Distribution (Quantum Selection)

The [structural-ontological gap](/concepts/physical-completeness/) locates an opening within physics itself. Quantum mechanics assigns amplitudes to every outcome and fixes the [Born-rule](/topics/born-rule-and-the-consciousness-interface/) probability distribution completely — yet *which* outcome becomes actual is not determined by the structural description. Structure determines the distribution; it does not determine the draw. The Born-rule weights specify what a decohered neural system would produce statistically if outcomes were settled by quantum probabilities alone — a probability measure over the possible neural firing patterns, motor commands, or attentional configurations a brain state could resolve into.

## Why These May Be One Object

[Delegation Meets Quantum Selection](/topics/delegation-meets-quantum-selection/) already argues the core identification: Saad's default causal profile *plausibly corresponds to* the Born-rule distribution. The bridge is a candidate identification, not an established correspondence — the default profile is a counterfactual pattern of *effects*, the Born distribution a probability measure over *outcomes*, and the proposal is that at the relevant grain these are the same thing: the outcomes the Born rule weights are the effects the brain state would produce.

Adding the bandwidth register completes a three-way picture in which each vocabulary supplies what the others leave implicit:

| Register | Names the structure as | Supplies | Leaves open |
|----------|------------------------|----------|-------------|
| Delegation (Saad) | Default causal profile | The *causal logic*: what experience preempts to avoid overdetermination | The physical mechanism; the grain |
| Bandwidth (Libet) | Prepared options | The *grain and channel*: ~10 bits/s, policy-level, brain-prepared | What ontology occupies the channel |
| Quantum selection | Born-rule distribution | The *physical opening*: where structure leaves actuality undetermined | The causal logic; the grain |

Read together, the structure is: a brain readies a menu of coarse-grained possibilities (bandwidth), physics assigns each a probability and leaves the draw open (Born rule), and consciousness takes over the draw by selecting one — preempting the default the physical state would otherwise have produced (delegation). The three registers describe, respectively, the *menu*, the *measure over the menu*, and the *takeover of the choice*.

The grain matches across registers, which is part of what makes the single-object reading attractive rather than forced. Saad's profile is defined functionally and coarsely (it ranges over "wince," "withdraw," not over synaptic detail); the bandwidth channel is coarse by mathematical necessity (~10 bits cannot specify fine grain); and the quantum reading on the Map's account operates at policy-level outcomes, since consciousness "cannot plausibly delegate at the level of individual quantum events." Three independently motivated coarsenesses landing at the same grain is the kind of fit that makes "one structure" a natural hypothesis.

## The Discipline: Coherence, Not Confirmation

Here the article must be most careful, because the move it describes is exactly the move the Map's [evidential-status discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/) forbids when done carelessly: converting a cluster's coherence into an upgrade of evidential status. The discipline's operative phrasing applies directly — *alignment raises coherence rather than evidential status*.

Three distinctions keep the convergence honest.

**This is redescription, not independent evidence.** The [cross-mechanism convergence](/concepts/cross-mechanism-convergence/) pattern earns evidential weight because *mechanism-distinct* perturbations produce the *same downstream ordering* — propofol and ketamine, acting through different molecular pathways, revealing the same vulnerability hierarchy. That is genuinely several witnesses, because the pathways are independent and could have disagreed. The present case is the opposite: there are no independent pathways and no independent observations. There is one posited structure and three theoretical vocabularies pointing at it. The vocabularies *cannot disagree about whether the structure exists*, because each was constructed to describe it. So this convergence sits even lower than cross-mechanism convergence on the evidential ladder — it is convergent *redescription*, which is the weakest form, carrying no independent confirmation at all. In the terms of the [epistemology of convergence arguments](/topics/epistemology-of-convergence-arguments/), the registers fail the independence proviso outright: they do not arrive at the same conclusion by separate routes, they *are* one route restated, so there is nothing for a common-cause screen to discount because there was never more than one witness.

**What the convergence does raise.** Convergent redescription is not worthless. When three independently developed frameworks turn out to be describing the same object, the posterior that *it is one object* — rather than three loosely related ones — goes up. This is framework-internal weight in the discipline's sense: the Map's articulation becomes more self-consistent and more able to integrate disparate phenomena. A theory that needs one primitive is tidier than one needing three. That tidiness is a real virtue and a real reason to prefer the unified reading. It is not a reason to believe the structure is *real*, that *consciousness* (rather than physics) performs the draw, or that any tenet is *true*.

**What the convergence cannot do.** It cannot license "three frameworks point to the same structure, so the structure is better confirmed." Each register is independently contestable, and unifying them does not retire those contests:

- The default causal profile is never directly observed and rests on contested zombie-possibility (Saad's own concession).
- The bandwidth selection-reading is compatible with a fully physicalist menu-and-chooser; the ~10-bit figure constrains the interface shape without establishing a non-physical occupant.
- The Born-rule-as-default-profile identification is "empirically equivalent to alternatives on which the default causal profile is a classical-statistical object," as [delegation-meets-quantum-selection](/topics/delegation-meets-quantum-selection/) concedes, and the entire quantum scaffolding presupposes the Map's [No Many Worlds](/tenets/#no-many-worlds) tenet rather than deriving it.

Unifying three contestable claims yields one contestable claim, not a confirmed one. If the structure is an artefact of how the Map has framed the problem, all three vocabularies are redescribing an artefact, and their agreement is exactly what one would expect — guaranteed by construction, evidentially inert. This mirrors the candour in [delegatory-dualism](/topics/delegatory-dualism/)'s Testability section: the framework's coherence "earns its keep by the coherence it buys, not by independent observation of the entity it quantifies over."

## Relation to Site Perspective

This article bears on two tenets, and the bearing is structural-clarification rather than support.

**[Minimal Quantum Interaction](/tenets/#minimal-quantum-interaction)**: The three registers together sharpen what "minimal" means. Consciousness selects *within* the Born-rule distribution (Register C), among brain-prepared coarse-grained options (Register B), by preempting the default the physical state would otherwise have produced (Register A) — adding no energy, creating no statistical anomaly. Minimality is the same fact seen three ways: the smallest intervention is a single draw from an already-specified, already-prepared, already-defaulted menu. The unification clarifies the tenet's content; it does not add evidence that consciousness performs the draw.

**[Bidirectional Interaction](/tenets/#bidirectional-interaction)**: For consciousness to causally influence the physical world, there must be a place where its influence enters without contradicting physics. The three registers name that place from three sides — the gap in the default profile (logic), the narrow outbound channel (information), the undetermined draw (physics). That they coincide makes the interface specification more coherent. It does not establish that the interface is occupied by something non-physical rather than by physics the Map has not yet identified; that remains the open question [delegation-meets-quantum-selection](/topics/delegation-meets-quantum-selection/) flags.

The honest summary: the Map now has a tidier account of where and how consciousness could act, expressed in one structure rather than three. Tidiness is a virtue of articulation. It is not a victory over the rivals, and the article is written so that no reader can mistake it for one.
