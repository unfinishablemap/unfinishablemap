The [two-axis thickness taxonomy](/topics/four-quadrant-dualism-taxonomy/) sorts dualist positions by how much ontological weight the mind-side and the physical-side each carry. In its "Limits of the Thickness Metaphor" section it concedes a deferral: thickness is really a family of measures — *structural richness*, *causal work*, *counterfactual role*, and *channel width* (how narrow the interaction interface is) — and the grid tracks only the first. It flags one position, Stapp, as placed "by judgement rather than definition" because his thick endpoints and narrow channel collapse into a single max/max cell. This article develops the deferred axis: it treats channel width as a third locating coordinate, orders the canonical positions along it, tests whether it separates cases the two-axis grid conflates, and then runs the coherence-test on the one region of the axis that turns out conspicuously empty — the wide-channel/thin-pole cell.

The posture throughout is the parent's posture: cartographic, not adjudicative. Channel width is a locating instrument. It tells you where a position sits, not whether the position is correct. Where the analysis touches the Map's own commitments — the [Minimal Quantum Interaction](/tenets/#minimal-quantum-interaction) tenet is, read structurally, a claim about *minimum channel width* — that connection is flagged as tenet-coherence, not as evidential support, per [evidential-status-discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/).

## What Channel Width Measures {#what-it-measures}

Channel width is a measure of the *interface*, not of either pole. The thickness axes ask how much ontology each side carries; channel width asks how much passes *between* the sides, and through how narrow an aperture. The two are logically independent: a thick mind can touch a thick world through a hair-thin channel, and a thin mind could in principle act through a wide one.

To keep channel width distinct from its three sibling sub-measures:

- **Structural richness** counts layers a side posits above the minimum (subliminal strata, hidden variables). It is a property of a *pole*.
- **Causal work** measures how much a side contributes to producing effects. Also a property of a pole.
- **Counterfactual role** asks which features an effect depends on. A property of the *dependency relation*.
- **Channel width** asks how constrained the interface itself is: what kinds of operation cross it, and how much information or work the crossing carries. A property of the *interface*.

The [selection-only-channel](/concepts/selection-only-channel/) supplies the sharpest formalisation of "narrow." A selection-only channel lets the mind act solely as a selector among physically-prepared alternatives, "contributing nothing to the alternative set itself." Its per-event capacity is bounded above by log₂(N) bits for N brain-generated candidates; Born-rule preservation drives the long-run signed information rate toward zero; and the reportable phenomenal content is bounded by the brain-side candidate space (content-confinement). These three constraints are what *minimum* channel width looks like, made precise. Channel width at its lower limit just *is* the selection-only channel.

Wider channels relax these constraints in order. A *probability-bias* channel still acts within a fixed candidate set but shifts the distribution {p_i} away from its physical-side values — wider than selection-only because it no longer preserves Born statistics. A *basis-choice* channel (Stapp's *Process 1*) lets the mind choose which observable the brain measures, modifying the candidate set itself — wider still. A *candidate-generation* channel lets the mind contribute novelty to the alternative set. And an *energy-injection* channel transfers a quantity physics tracks as work across the interface — the widest aperture, and the classical Cartesian one. Channel width is thus an ordering, from selection-only at the floor to energy-transfer at the ceiling, by what operations the interface admits.

## A Channel-Width Ordering Across Canonical Positions {#ordering}

The same canonical positions the two-axis grid populates can be ordered along channel width. The characterisations below match how the existing corpus already describes each position; this article relocates them onto a new coordinate rather than re-characterising them.

**Saad's delegatory dualism — narrow (selection-only).** [Delegatory causation](/concepts/delegatory-causation/) has experience preempt the *default causal profile* of a physical brain state by [trumping](/concepts/trumping-preemption/) rather than competing — and, crucially, "the experience causes exactly what the physical state would have caused by default." The Map's integration identifies that default profile with the Born-rule distribution over neural quantum outcomes, so consciousness "selects *within* Born-rule probabilities rather than deviating from them." Selection within a fixed, Born-preserving candidate set is precisely the selection-only channel. Saad's bare mechanism sits at the channel-width floor. (The "default" qualifier is load-bearing: drop it and the Subset Law becomes circular. The narrowness is a property of *what crosses* the interface, not of either pole.)

**Stapp's quantum-Zeno dualism — narrow channel, thick endpoints.** This is the position that forces the third axis. The parent taxonomy reads Stapp as max-mind / max-physical — a thick observer with full selection power on one side, a thick quantum ontology of full state-vector realism on the other, and a narrow selection channel between them. His intervention is "quantum-Zeno biasing only," holding neural patterns against decay by observation rate; consciousness "*selects* among patterns the brain presents" rather than generating them — what the mechanism-costs reading calls "a probabilistic channel that is structurally Q1-like even when sitting in a Q4 ontology." So Stapp's *channel* is near the floor — narrow, selection-flavoured, energy-injecting nothing — while both *poles* are thick. (A qualifier the corpus preserves: Stapp's basis-choice layer, the *Process 1* choice of which observable to probe, is wider than strict outcome-level selection-only, so his channel is narrow but not at the absolute floor.)

**Cartesian substance dualism — wide (energy-transfer).** The classical formulation routes mental influence through what the mechanism-costs article calls "Cartesian energy-transfer," the move that puts the conservation objection in its most pointed form. The interface carries a quantity physics tracks as work. The channel is wide: it admits energy injection, the widest operation in the ordering. Descartes sits at the channel-width ceiling regardless of where his poles sit.

**Kastrup's analytic idealism — no channel.** Kastrup is, by the parent's scope note, a monist included for contrast. Analytic idealism replaces interaction with an *intrinsic/extrinsic* distinction: matter is the extrinsic appearance of mental processes, perceived from the outside. There are not two things in contact, so there is no interface for anything to cross. The relevant question becomes the [transparency problem](/topics/q3-q4-sliding-boundary-and-transparency-problem/) — why the intrinsic mental reality looks, from outside, like impersonal physical structure. On the channel-width axis Kastrup does not score *zero width*; he scores *not applicable*. A channel-free position is off the axis, not at its bottom. This matters for the separation test below.

This yields a rough ordering — Saad (floor) → Stapp (near floor, plus a basis-choice layer above) → probability-bias interactionism → Cartesian energy-transfer (ceiling) — with Kastrup standing outside it entirely.

## Does the Third Axis Separate What the Grid Conflates? {#separation-test}

The parent claims the two-axis grid conflates Stapp's case and that a channel-width axis would resolve it. The claim is testable, position by position.

**The Stapp case: yes, cleanly.** On the two-axis grid Stapp is one point — max-mind / max-physical — and the grid cannot show that his interface is minimal; the parent admits placing him "by judgement rather than definition." Adding channel width gives Stapp three coordinates that no longer collide: thick mind, thick physical, narrow channel. The "three values across what may be only a two-axis taxonomy" the parent noticed now have three axes to live on. This is the axis earning its name: it converts a judgement call into a coordinate.

**The Saad/Stapp distinction: yes.** On the two-axis grid Saad (min/min) and Stapp (max/max) already sit far apart, so the grid does not conflate them. But channel width reveals something the thickness axes hide — that the two share a *channel class*. Both route influence through a narrow, selection-flavoured, Born-respecting aperture; they differ in pole thickness, not interface type. The thickness grid makes them look maximally different; the channel-width axis shows they are kin at the interface — positions far apart in thickness can be neighbours in channel width.

**The Kastrup case: the axis correctly refuses to place him.** Here the third axis earns its keep by *declining* to order. A naive reading might put Kastrup at "zero channel width," next to Saad. But selection-only is the *narrowest interaction*; no-channel is the *absence of interaction*. The transparency-problem analysis shows these are categorically different burdens — Saad owes an account of how selection crosses the interface; Kastrup owes an account of why the intrinsic looks extrinsic. Channel width discriminates "narrowest crossing" from "nothing crosses," which the thickness grid (where Kastrup sits at the max-mind edge as a monist limit case) does not surface at all.

So the axis passes the parent's test for Stapp, and does additional separating work: it groups Saad and Stapp by channel class despite their thickness distance, and it cleanly partitions interaction-with-a-narrow-channel from no-interaction-at-all. The two-axis grid plus channel width locates every canonical position by definition where the grid alone needed judgement for at least one.

A limit to record: channel width is itself a family, not a scalar. "Narrow" can mean *few bits per event*, *Born-statistics-preserving*, *energy-inert*, or *candidate-set-fixed*, and these need not coincide — Stapp is energy-inert and selection-flavoured at the outcome level but candidate-set-modifying at the basis layer. Reading a position onto the axis can still require a judgement about *which* sense of narrowness is doing the locating work. The third axis reduces the judgement the parent flagged; it does not abolish judgement.

## The Vacant Cell: Does a Wide Channel Force Thick Poles? {#vacant-cell}

Plotting the canonical positions against pole thickness leaves one region of the plane conspicuously empty: no named position pairs a *wide* channel with *thin* poles. The Saad/Stapp pairing shows the two coordinates are genuinely independent at the *narrow* end — same channel class, different thickness. But Cartesian energy-transfer pairs a wide channel with thick poles, and nothing pairs a wide channel with thin poles. Is that empty region a contingent gap in the literature, or a structural impossibility — does a wide channel *force* at least one pole to thicken? This section runs the coherence-test, applying the existing mechanism-cost machinery to reach a cartographic verdict.

### What the Vacant Cell Would Have to Claim {#what-it-claims}

A wide-channel/thin-pole position must conjoin two commitments that, on inspection, pull against each other.

**The wide-channel commitment.** "Wide" means the interface admits operations richer than selection among physically-prepared alternatives. The selection-only channel's taxonomy of what-it-is-not gives the ordering precisely: a wide channel *shifts the candidate distribution away from Born values* (probability-bias), *modifies the candidate set itself* (basis-choice or candidate-generation), or *transfers a quantity physics tracks as work* (energy-injection). The two widest operations — candidate-generation and energy-injection — are the ones that make a channel unambiguously wide rather than merely intermediate, so they are the test cases.

**The thin-pole commitment.** "Thin mind" means, per the [mind-side thickness scale](/topics/four-quadrant-dualism-taxonomy/#mind-side), only what is phenomenally introspectable — bare qualia, no unconscious mental substance, no unperceived qualia-spaces, no causal repertoire beyond what property-dualism admits. "Thin physical" means physics' own ontology with nothing added — no hidden variables, no quiddities, no ignorance-facts, with closure holding except at probabilistic indeterminacy.

So the inhabitant must claim an interface that injects energy or generates novel candidates, driven by a mind that is no more than bare introspectable qualia, acting on a physics no richer than the bare quantum formalism. The coherence-test asks whether anything can satisfy both halves at once.

### The Forcing Argument: Width Pulls at Both Poles {#forcing-argument}

Run each wide operation through the mechanism-cost battery — the three questions ([causation, interface, conservation](/topics/mechanism-costs-dualism-thickness-quadrants/#three-questions)) the existing machinery already applies cell by cell. Each wide operation levies a debt that one pole or the other must thicken to pay.

**Energy-injection forces the physical pole (and probably the mind pole) to thicken.** An energy-injecting channel transfers work across the interface — the [heaviest conservation engagement](/topics/mechanism-costs-dualism-thickness-quadrants/#q4). A thin physical pole — bare quantum formalism with closure at the indeterminacy level — has no room to absorb a non-conservative injection. Either conservation fails (a substantive *addition* to the physics — the physical pole has thickened into a modified-dynamics ontology), or the injection is illusory; the debt cannot be paid from a thin physical pole's resources. And the injecting side must be the kind of entity that *carries* energy to deliver, which a bare-qualia mind, with no causal repertoire beyond the introspectable, is not. Energy-injection is itself a thick causal repertoire. Both poles are pulled upward.

**Candidate-generation forces the mind pole to thicken.** A candidate-generation channel lets the mind "supply novelty beyond the brain-encoded set" — exactly the operation the [selection-only channel forbids](/concepts/selection-only-channel/#what-the-channel-is-not). A mind that *generates* candidates the brain did not encode is, by definition, a mind whose content exceeds the brain-side candidate space — carrying content beyond the introspective-and-brain-encoded horizon. That is the [thick-mind commitment](/topics/four-quadrant-dualism-taxonomy/#mind-side) verbatim: "mental content vastly exceeding introspection." A thin mind has nothing extra to contribute; the moment it contributes novelty, it has stopped being thin. Candidate-generation forces the mind pole up.

**Probability-bias is the marginal case — and it is not wide.** Shifting the Born distribution within a fixed candidate set is wider than selection-only but does not, on its own, force either pole to thicken: a mind can bias {p_i} without carrying extra subliminal content, and a physics can have its statistics nudged without acquiring hidden variables. This is why probability-bias sits in the *intermediate* reading of the [Minimal Quantum Interaction](/tenets/#minimal-quantum-interaction) tenet rather than at the wide end. The forcing argument does not reach it — but neither does the "wide" label. Probability-bias occupies the middle of the axis, not the vacant wide cell. It is evidence that the *middle* of the channel-width axis is compatible with thin poles, not that the *wide* end is.

The pattern is consistent: the two operations that make a channel unambiguously wide each levy a debt payable only by thickening a pole. Width and thinness are not independent at the extremes the way the Saad/Stapp pairing showed at the narrow end.

### Contingent Gap or Structural Impossibility? {#verdict}

The forcing argument does not deliver a clean universal impossibility; it delivers a structured verdict with one exception.

**For candidate-generation: structural.** The forcing here is analytic, not contingent. "Mind contributes novelty beyond the brain-encoded set" and "mind carries no content beyond the introspective-and-brain horizon" are contradictories. No articulation could satisfy both, because the second is the negation of the first's enabling condition. The wide-channel/thin-mind combination *for candidate-generation channels* is structurally impossible — empty by geometry, not by historical accident. The literature did not fail to find this position; there is no position to find.

**For energy-injection: structural on the standard reading, with a contingent-gap escape.** The conservation debt analytically excludes a thin *physical* pole if conservation is inviolable: a thin physics has no resources to absorb non-conservative injection, so the physical pole must thicken into a modified-dynamics ontology. That much is structural. But there is a thin escape the literature has genuinely not articulated: a position that takes the physical pole as thin *and accepts a localised conservation violation as a brute primitive* — a "thin-substance Cartesianism" that injects energy through a bare interface and simply denies conservation is exceptionless, without enriching either pole. Whether such a position keeps the physical pole genuinely thin (a brute exception to a law is arguably itself a thickening) or merely relabels the debt is the kind of question the [Q4 conservation analysis](/topics/mechanism-costs-dualism-thickness-quadrants/#q4) flags as a dispute the Map does not adjudicate. This is the one cell that is a *contingent* gap: not analytically forbidden, but unoccupied because no one has found it worth defending. The historical Cartesian energy-injector came bundled with a *res cogitans* thick on the mind side; nobody has built the thin-mind, thin-physics, conservation-denying variant.

So the vacant cell partitions. Most of it is structurally forbidden — candidate-generation under any reading, energy-injection under conservation-respecting readings. A sliver is a contingent gap — energy-injection that pays its conservation debt by denying conservation rather than enriching physics. The either/or resolves as *both, in different sub-regions*: the forcing is real wherever conservation and content-confinement are held fixed, and relaxes only where one of those background commitments is given up — itself a substantive move, not a free thin-pole position.

### Symmetry With the Q4 Vacancy {#q4-symmetry}

The two-axis thickness taxonomy already noted a [systematically under-populated quadrant](/topics/four-quadrant-dualism-taxonomy/#q4) — max-mind/max-physical, avoided because the discipline reads parsimony as a virtue. The channel-width axis has its own vacant region, but the two vacancies have *opposite* explanatory structures, and the contrast is itself a cartographic finding.

Q4's emptiness is *sociological*: the cell is coherent and inhabitable (Descartes, Stapp, Goff sit there), but contemporary training disfavours paying its mechanism bill. Nothing forbids Q4; the discipline avoids it. The wide-channel/thin-pole cell's emptiness is *mostly structural*: the cell is largely incoherent, so the literature's avoidance tracks genuine impossibility rather than preference. Distinguishing the two kinds of emptiness is part of what a locating axis is *for*. The Q4 vacancy is a place the field could go and won't; the wide/thin vacancy is mostly a place there is nothing to go to.

A naive cartographer, seeing two empty cells, might treat them as the same kind of gap and predict both will fill as the literature matures. The mechanism-cost analysis shows they will not. Q4 may fill if parsimony norms loosen (the Map's [Occam's Razor Has Limits](/tenets/#occams-razor-has-limits) tenet is one such loosening). The structurally-forbidden part of the wide/thin region cannot fill, because no coherent position projects there. Only the contingent sliver — conservation-denying thin Cartesianism — is a genuine prediction-of-possible-occupancy, and even that is a position the field has reasons to find unattractive.

## Relation to Site Perspective {#site-perspective}

The Map's [Minimal Quantum Interaction](/tenets/#minimal-quantum-interaction) tenet is, read through this axis, a commitment about *channel width* specifically — the narrowness of the interface is what MQI is about. The [selection-only-channel](/concepts/selection-only-channel/) is "the strictest reading" of that tenet: the smallest deviation from physical dynamics, leaving candidate set, Born distribution, and energetic balance all untouched. In channel-width terms, MQI says: locate the Map at or near the floor of this axis, independent of where the thickness axes place it.

This is what the third axis clarifies that the two-axis grid blurred. The grid put the Map "near Q1, with room along the mind-axis" — leaving open whether thickening the mind-side toward Stappian or Myersian richness would force the interface to widen. Channel width shows it need not, and the forcing argument explains *why*: at the narrow end of the axis, channel width and pole thickness are genuinely independent — Saad (thin poles) and Stapp (thick poles) share the narrow-channel class — whereas *width* forces thickness. The asymmetry — width constrains the poles, narrowness does not — is exactly what gives the Map room to commit firmly on the channel while staying loose on the poles. "Thin substance, indispensable role" was always a slogan about two different things — pole thickness and channel width — and the third axis lets them be stated separately: a narrow channel firmly (MQI), a thin mind-pole only loosely (mind-medium, with room to expand).

Three disciplinary cautions, in keeping with [evidential-status-discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/). First, that MQI maps onto "minimum channel width" is a structural restatement of a tenet, not an argument that the channel *is* narrow in nature; naming its axis does not upgrade it to a finding, and treating the elegance of the framing as evidence *for* MQI would be exactly the [slippage](/concepts/possibility-probability-slippage/) the discipline guards against. Second, that the wide-channel/thin-pole cell is mostly forbidden is *not* evidence for the Map's narrow channel: ruling out one region of logical space does not raise the probability of the Map's region; it only sharpens what the alternatives are. Third, the channel-width ordering inherits the open empirical question its narrowest member already carries — whether brain-scale quantum indeterminacies exist for a narrow channel to exploit at all (Tegmark's decoherence objection). Channel width locates positions in logical space; it does not certify that the floor of the axis is physically occupiable.

The Map's tenets also leave the contingent-gap sliver untouched: the conservation-denying thin Cartesian variant is not a position the Map occupies or needs. The Map's conservation engagement runs through selection-without-injection ([conservation laws and mental causation](/concepts/conservation-laws-and-mental-causation/)), keeping the channel narrow so that no conservation debt is incurred. The Map sits at the opposite end of the axis from the vacant cell, paying its conservation bill at the floor rather than denying the bill at the ceiling.

## Where This Axis Points Next {#points-next}

Several open questions follow from holding channel width separate.

**Does the basis-choice layer deserve its own treatment?** Stapp's narrowness is split: outcome-level selection-only, basis-level candidate-modifying. The [selection-only-channel](/concepts/selection-only-channel/) already flags that "the basis-choice layer above sits outside the selection-only class strictly construed." A position can be narrow at one layer and wider at another, which suggests channel width may itself be layered rather than single-valued. Whether a *thin*-mind basis-choice channel is coherent — whether choosing which observable the brain measures requires subliminal content — is a finer test the binary candidate-generation analysis does not settle.

**Is the contingent sliver worth articulating?** The conservation-denying thin-Cartesian variant is logically available but undefended. Whether a brute conservation exception counts as keeping physics thin is a question the [mechanism-cost cartography](/topics/mechanism-costs-dualism-thickness-quadrants/) could take up by running its three-question battery on the variant explicitly. This analysis locates the sliver; it does not fully cost it.

**Where do the off-axis positions go?** Kastrup is off the axis because idealism has no interface. Hylomorphic dualism — interface "internal to one substance" rather than "between two things" — may be off it for a different reason, or may sit at an exotic point. Mapping the channel-free and quasi-channel positions is the natural extension once the axis exists.

**Do other locating axes have structurally-forbidden cells?** If the channel-width axis has a region empty by geometry rather than by sociology, the two-axis thickness grid and the mechanism-cost battery may have analogous forbidden regions. A systematic survey of which cells across the Map's locating taxonomies are *structurally* versus *contingently* empty is a cartographic project the distinction drawn here makes newly askable.
