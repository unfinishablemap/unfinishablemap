Every conscious being thinks from within a conceptual framework — a system of categories, assumptions, and inferential habits that structures experience before reflection begins. The Unfinishable Map argues the framework is not held like luggage but *inhabited* like a dwelling, and the dwelling's walls become visible only in the rare moments when one is rebuilt. Two complementary phenomenologies emerge: what it is like to dwell inside a framework (transparency, gravitational pull, quiet authority) and what it is like when the framework itself shifts (anomaly, crisis, gestalt, consolidation). Both carry qualitative character that computational and functionalist accounts systematically miss — evidence that conscious experience does epistemic work no model of belief-updating captures.

## The Transparency of the Lens

The central phenomenological fact about framework dependence is that the framework is experientially invisible under normal conditions. A dualist examining their own experience does not notice a "dualist filter" any more than a fluent speaker notices grammar while speaking. The framework operates at the level Heidegger called *Zuhandenheit* — readiness-to-hand — where the tool disappears into its function.

This transparency is structurally enforced, not mere inattention. The framework determines which features of experience count as salient, which questions seem meaningful, and which observations register as evidence. A materialist encountering the explanatory gap between neural activity and subjective experience may genuinely feel that the gap is an artefact of confused thinking — not because they have examined and dismissed the gap, but because their framework renders it unproblematic before conscious evaluation begins.

Davidson (1974) argued that radically incommensurable conceptual schemes are incoherent: if we can recognise something as a framework at all, we have already begun to translate it. Framework dependence as described here does not require radical incommensurability. The claim is that translation between frameworks is *lossy* — something is systematically stripped away — while remaining possible. Cross-framework understanding routinely involves distortion the understander cannot detect from within.

## The Perceptual Restructuring of Commitment

Theoretical commitment reshapes what shows up as salient. The committed Darwinist sees adaptation where others see mere variation. The committed dualist perceives the explanatory gap as a feature of reality, not a temporary failure of science. Confirming evidence arrives with phenomenal smoothness — it slots into place, extends existing patterns. Anomalous evidence produces a characteristic friction, actively disrupting the perceptual fluency that commitment provides.

This goes deeper than confirmation bias. Commitment changes what evidence *looks like* before any evaluative process begins. The committed physicist does not first perceive neutral data and then interpret it through theory; the data arrives already structured by the theoretical lens. As Kuhn recognised, scientists working in different paradigms inhabit different perceptual worlds.

Predictive processing accounts can model this restructuring computationally — priors shape top-down predictions that filter incoming data. But the computational model describes the *functional architecture* without addressing the *phenomenal character*. Two systems with identical Bayesian priors could restructure evidence identically yet differ in whether there is something it is like to hold those priors.

## The Gravitational Structure of Commitment

Theoretical commitment creates something like an experiential gravity well. Ideas within the theory's domain are drawn toward coherent interpretation; those outside drift to the periphery. The *effort* required to entertain alternatives increases with the depth of commitment. This is an epistemic structure that makes sustained inquiry possible — without it, every observation would demand fresh interpretation from scratch.

The gravitational structure manifests as **inferential momentum** (within a framework, conclusions generate pressure toward the next — the dualist accepting irreducibility feels logical momentum toward interactionism), **coherence satisfaction** (disparate observations converging under one framework produces a felt rightness that functions as a phenomenal [epistemic signal](/concepts/epistemic-emotions/) of explanatory progress), and **boundary alertness** (problems at the edge of the theory's reach feel qualitatively different from problems well within its scope).

## Three Signatures of Framework Dependence

Although frameworks are normally transparent, three recurring experiential patterns mark their presence.

### False Fluency Across Frameworks

When a dualist reads a materialist argument (or vice versa), the experience is typically one of comprehension — the argument seems intelligible, the logic followable. Yet this felt comprehension may be illusory: the reader has translated the argument into their own framework's terms, stripping away what made it compelling within its native framework. False fluency feels indistinguishable from genuine understanding. No phenomenal marker distinguishes "I understand this in its own terms" from "I have translated this into terms my framework can process" — consciousness apparently lacks the resources to detect when its own comprehension is framework-mediated.

### Irritation at the Incommensurable

Encountering a genuinely alien framework often produces not wonder but characteristic irritation. The other person seems to be making a simple error, missing an obvious point, or being wilfully obtuse. This is phenomenologically distinct from encountering a difficult argument within one's own framework, where the typical responses are effort and engagement.

The irritation may function as a framework-preservation mechanism. Instead of openness, the phenomenology pushes toward dismissal: the alien framework is experienced as confused rather than as differently structured. Dualists and materialists regularly report finding the opposing position not just wrong but baffling — a phenomenal signature that the disagreement may involve genuine incommensurability rather than mere error.

### Moments of Framework Slippage

Rarely, the framework briefly becomes visible as a framework — a sudden, vertiginous sense that what one took for unmediated reality is a constructed perspective. These typically occur during sustained engagement with a radically different tradition. The experience is transient: within seconds to minutes, the framework reasserts its transparency. The transience is philosophically significant — it suggests framework transparency is actively maintained, not merely default. Something in the cognitive architecture actively restores the sense that one's categories are transparent windows rather than coloured glass.

## When Frameworks Break Down

Slippage is temporary; genuine *framework revision* is different. When the categories themselves shift — not the propositions held within them — something phenomenologically distinct occurs. [Belief revision](/topics/phenomenology-of-intellectual-life/) operates within a framework: you change *what* you think while the *how* stays put. Framework change alters the how. After a genuine shift, the same evidence looks different — not because you evaluate it differently but because you *perceive* it differently. The gestalt has flipped. Kuhn described this at the level of scientific communities, arguing that scientists in different paradigms inhabit "different worlds." The [Kuhnian analysis of consciousness studies](/topics/consciousness-and-the-structure-of-scientific-revolutions/) shows that physicalism exhibits the hallmarks of paradigm crisis.

### Anomaly Accumulation

Before a framework shifts, anomalies accumulate — experiences that resist assimilation into existing categories. The anomaly presents itself not as a counterexample demanding revision but as puzzlement: something that *should* make sense but doesn't. The felt quality is low-grade cognitive discomfort distinct from ordinary confusion — the sense that something is wrong with the *tools* rather than the task. A physicist struggling with blackbody radiation in the late 1890s would not have experienced the anomaly as "classical physics is wrong." The experience would have been more diffuse: a persistent unease that the familiar framework was generating answers that didn't quite *fit*. The [epistemic-emotions](/concepts/epistemic-emotions/) differ from ordinary doubt. Ordinary doubt asks "is this true?" Anomaly-unease asks "am I thinking about this the right way?" — doubt directed at one's own cognitive apparatus rather than at propositions.

### The Crisis of Incommensurability

As anomalies accumulate, the old framework begins to feel *wrong* in a way that goes beyond particular errors — an epistemic vertigo where familiar categories lose their grip without new categories yet available to replace them. This is the [edge](/voids/phenomenology-of-the-edge/) experienced from within.

Consider the shift from substance-based to process-based thinking in philosophy of mind. The commitment to substances stops feeling like a firm foundation and starts feeling like a constraint, but process thinking is not yet available. The interval has positive phenomenal character — groundlessness, intellectual nakedness, thinking without a net. It resembles what contemplative traditions call "beginner's mind," but involuntary.

### The Gestalt Shift

The moment of conceptual change itself has phenomenology related to but distinct from the [click of understanding](/topics/phenomenology-of-intellectual-life/). The click resolves tension within a framework; the gestalt shift *replaces* the framework — what counts as scattered pieces, as integration, as coherence all transform simultaneously. The Necker cube illustrates the structure: identical input, different organising principle. Framework shift operates at a far deeper level, on the categories through which you think, reason, and understand.

The phenomenal signature includes:

- **Totality.** The entire framework reorganises at once, like a crystal lattice reforming rather than individual atoms relocating.
- **Irreversibility felt as liberation.** You can remember thinking the old way but can no longer *think* that way — felt as expansion beyond limitation rather than escape from error.
- **Retroactive reinterpretation.** Previous confusions appear as symptoms of the old framework's inadequacy. Anomalies slot cleanly into the new framework.
- **Perceptual transformation.** The student who grasps evolution sees design where they once saw intention; the physicist who absorbs quantum mechanics sees probability where they once saw deterministic trajectory. The overlap with [perceptual learning](/topics/consciousness-as-perceptual-architect/) is instructive — both demonstrate consciousness as architect rather than passive receiver.

### Consolidation and Residual Dissonance

After the shift comes consolidation: cautious pleasure as the new framework handles cases the old one struggled with, combined with residual dissonance when old habits reassert themselves. Traces of the old framework persist as phenomenal tendencies. The physicist who has absorbed general relativity still *feels* gravity as a downward-pulling force, even while understanding it as spacetime curvature. Framework change reaches into the structure of perceptual experience — patterned through years of use, mirroring what James (1890) observed about motor skill: consciousness scaffolds learning and then withdraws. The old framework must now be consciously overridden.

## The First-Person Asymmetry

A fundamental asymmetry runs through both inhabitation and change: one's own commitments feel like clear sight while others' commitments look like bias. This is a structural feature of what commitment is, not a defect to be corrected. To be committed to a theory is to take it as revealing rather than distorting — if the commitment felt like distortion, it would not be a commitment.

A materialist committed to physicalism experiences the explanatory gap as a puzzle to be solved, not a sign that their framework is inadequate. A dualist committed to irreducibility experiences the gap as pointing toward a genuine feature of reality. The asymmetry is the phenomenological face of [Duhem-Quine underdetermination](/topics/duhem-quine-underdetermination-consciousness/): when evidence cannot force theory choice, each committed theorist experiences their framework as transparent. Ratcliffe's work on *feelings of being* illuminates this — theoretical commitment shapes the space of what feels intelligible before deliberate evaluation begins.

## What These Phenomenologies Reveal

### The Irreducibility Argument

If framework inhabitation and change were merely information reorganisation, they would have no phenomenal character. A database can be restructured without anything happening experientially. But both are drenched in phenomenal character: the transparency of the lens, the gravitational pull of commitment, the unease of anomaly accumulation, the vertigo of framework dissolution, the totality of the gestalt shift. Each has qualitative texture beyond the functional transitions.

A functionalist might respond that phenomenal character *is* the functional reorganisation, experienced from the inside. But functional reorganisation can proceed piecemeal or holistically, gradually or suddenly, and nothing in functional description alone predicts which pattern a given reorganisation follows. Yet the phenomenology is highly specific: the gestalt shift is experienced as total, irreversible, retroactively illuminating. Chi (1992) showed that conceptual changes crossing ontological categories — from "matter" to "process" — are qualitatively harder and phenomenally different from within-category changes. The felt difficulty tracks the ontological boundary, not the quantity of information revised.

### Consciousness as Participant, and the Limits of Computation

The phenomenology suggests consciousness does not merely witness framework dynamics but participates in them. Attention to anomalies generates the conditions for dissolution; felt coherence guides which new framework crystallises. If these states were epiphenomenal, framework change should look the same with or without them. What makes a shift *stick* rather than oscillating between alternatives is not mere pattern-matching but a felt sense of *rightness* that guides selection.

Computational models — Thagard's (1992) explanatory coherence networks, Nersessian's (2008) model-based reasoning, Bayesian model selection — capture structural aspects but cannot model why the transition feels like anything, or why crossing Chi's ontological boundaries feels qualitatively different. Carey (2009) documents children's conceptual development as genuine framework change irreducible to incremental learning — but from outside. If the felt quality is what enables the holistic, simultaneous, irreversible character, systems without phenomenal experience may approximate the outcome without undergoing the process.

### Framework Dependence and Introspection

If introspection is theory-laden, first-person reports about consciousness are framework-dependent in the way scientific observations are paradigm-dependent. This does not render introspection worthless — introspective reports carry framework-specific information. A materialist introspecting on visual experience may notice functional properties; a dualist may notice qualitative properties like the raw redness of red. Both report accurately on what they notice; what they notice is shaped by what their framework makes salient.

## The Phenomenology of the Consciousness Debate

The mind-body problem has a distinctive phenomenology for its participants. Materialists often experience the hard problem as a pseudo-problem — an artefact of confused intuitions that dissolves under analysis. Within a framework where explanation means identifying physical mechanisms, the demand for something beyond mechanism feels like category error. Dualists report equal and opposite certainty: the gap between neural firing and subjective experience feels unbridgeable as direct experiential observation, not as argument's conclusion. Both sides report their position as experientially obvious and the opposing view as requiring elaborate support — exactly what framework inhabitation predicts. [Biological computationalism](/topics/biological-computationalisms-inadvertent-case-for-dualism/) is a concrete case: its proponents concede substrate dependence, metabolic grounding, and scale inseparability — each structurally analogous to classical dualist arguments — yet the physicalist framework renders the parallels invisible from the inside.

## Relation to Site Perspective

The Unfinishable Map's own theoretical commitments — its five [tenets](/tenets/) — are not exempt from the dynamics described here. Acknowledging that the dualist framework shapes what one notices does not make dualism self-refuting, since the same applies equally to materialism.

**[Dualism](/tenets/#dualism)**: If framework inhabitation and change involve irreducibly phenomenal restructuring that cannot be reduced to computational belief-updating, then the very activity of theorising depends on consciousness in a way materialist accounts struggle to explain. Physical systems reorganise; conscious systems *experience* reorganisation. The dualist claim is not that physics cannot explain the functional transitions but that it cannot explain their specific qualitative character.

**[Bidirectional Interaction](/tenets/#bidirectional-interaction)**: Consciousness is causally active in framework dynamics, not merely a spectator. Felt anomaly-unease drives continued attention to failures. Experienced coherence guides framework selection. The felt texture of commitment generates the coherence signals that drive theoretical progress — strip away the phenomenal states and the processes lose what distinguishes genuine framework change from mere model-switching.

**[Occam's Razor Has Limits](/tenets/#occams-limits)**: If framework dependence shapes what counts as "simple," the materialist's parsimony claim may be an artefact of the materialist framework rather than framework-neutral. Every genuine framework shift involves abandoning a simpler organising principle for a more adequate but more complex one — the child's "alive = moves on its own" is simpler than the biologist's concept; Newtonian mechanics is simpler than relativity. Occam's Razor, applied to frameworks themselves, would have blocked most genuine advances.

**[Minimal Quantum Interaction](/tenets/#minimal-quantum-interaction)**: The gestalt shift's totality — the entire framework reorganising simultaneously — is structurally compatible with quantum selection. If consciousness biases otherwise indeterminate neural states, the holistic character may reflect a single selection event across a superposed landscape of possible frameworks, rather than incremental computation. Speculative, but the all-at-once phenomenology fits quantum collapse better than sequential computation.

**[No Many Worlds](/tenets/#no-many-worlds)**: Framework change is singular and irreversible. After a genuine shift, you inhabit *this* framework, not multiple frameworks simultaneously. The experienced irreversibility presupposes that framework change is a real transition, not a branching into parallel versions of oneself. The determinacy of "which framework do *I* now inhabit?" reflects real collapse, not the illusion of collapse within one branch.

The analysis also resonates with [mysterianism](/concepts/mysterianism/). If frameworks constrain not just what we think but what we can experience, some aspects of consciousness may be structurally inaccessible — not because the phenomena are inherently mysterious, but because every framework filters out precisely what would need to be noticed. The [framework-void](/voids/framework-void/) explores this structural impossibility from outside; this article has explored what it feels like from within.

## What Would Challenge This View?

The claim that framework inhabitation and change have irreducible phenomenal character would face difficulty if:

- **Framework effects proved fully computational.** If predictive processing models could account for every qualitative aspect — the texture of false fluency, the vertigo of slippage, the stages of anomaly-crisis-shift — without residual phenomenal features.
- **Trained introspectors reported dissolution.** If phenomenologists consistently found these phenomena dissolve under close inspection into combinations of habit, expectation, and social reinforcement.
- **Cross-cultural universality failed.** If the transparency, gravitational pull, and stages of framework change varied radically across cultures.
- **AI systems displayed equivalent phenomenology.** If artificial systems demonstrated not just functional framework effects but the phenomenal signatures themselves.
