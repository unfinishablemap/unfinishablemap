The Born rule — quantum mechanics' prescription that measurement probabilities equal |⟨φ|ψ⟩|² — has been tested to exquisite precision in photon, atom, superconducting-qubit, and triple-slit systems. **No published experiment directly probes the Born rule inside living neural tissue during a conscious act of selection**, which is precisely the regime that consciousness-physics-interface programmes target. This article maps the experimental landscape: which proposals explicitly require Born-rule violation, which preserve the rule while still claiming a mental-physical interaction, which experiments are currently running or feasible, and what negative results would and would not foreclose for the Map's [Minimal Quantum Interaction](/tenets/) commitment.

The Unfinishable Map takes no definitive stance on whether Born-rule violation at the brain interface is real. The empirical question is genuinely open, and the Map's corridor-vs-outside-the-corridor distinction ([taxonomy below](#corridor-taxonomy)) is explicitly held as a live branch rather than a settled preference. Anyone who claims the question is already answered — whether by pointing to the Born rule's success in photon experiments or to the weak positive signals in legacy mind-matter studies — is overreading the available evidence.

## The Brain-Internal Gap in Born-Rule Testing

Every precision test of the Born rule to date has run in a regime very unlike brain tissue. Sinha et al. (2010) bounded Sorkin's third-order interference parameter at less than 10⁻² of the expected second-order contribution using single photons through a three-slit aperture (Sinha et al. 2010, Sawant et al. 2015). Later optical, atomic, and high-energy-collision work has tightened this bound further (Phys. Rev. D 2025). The GPT-steering derivation of the Born rule by causal consistency (arXiv:2512.12636, 2025) complements these experiments by showing that the rule is uniquely picked out among probability assignments *conditional* on purification, steering, and exact no-signalling. The Masanes-Galley-Müller (2019) operational redundancy result similarly derives Born's prescription from Hilbert-space structure, unitary evolution, tensor-product composition, and a finite-parameter estimation postulate.

The quoted language matters here. Masanes, Galley, and Müller state that "the mathematical structure of quantum measurements […] can be deduced from the other quantum postulates […] and the assumption that ensembles on finite-dimensional Hilbert spaces are characterized by finitely many parameters." This is a conditional theorem: if the premises hold, the Born rule follows. It is not a demonstration that the premises *must* hold in every physical system. The brain-internal regime during a conscious act of selection is an untested extrapolation from the tested systems. Any one of the MGM postulates — in particular measurement uniqueness and tensor-product subsystem independence — could in principle fail where conscious selection operates, and the available data cannot adjudicate.

This is what "the empirical status of Born-rule-violating tests at the brain interface" reduces to: the regime of interest has not been probed, the closest experimental programmes measure adjacent but non-equivalent quantities, and the most direct legacy tests (micro-psychokinesis on random number generators) trend toward the null when analysed with preregistered Bayesian methodology.

## Which Consciousness-Physics Proposals Require Born Violation {#corridor-taxonomy}

Not every proposal that posits a consciousness-physics interaction entails Born-rule violation. This distinction is the central contribution of the article. Three families of positions emerge:

**Corridor dualism (Born-rule-preserving)**. Consciousness influences which branch is actualised on a single measurement event, but the influence averages to exactly |⟨φ|ψ⟩|² over the ensemble. No statistical signature appears in long runs. This is the Map's current working hypothesis for minimum-detectable interaction, compatible with every tested Born-rule regime. Sympathetic readings of Stapp (without the Zeno variant), various "interface" authors, and the corridor-reading of the pragmatist-family positions (QBism, Healey) sit here.

**Minimum-outside-the-corridor dualism (Born-rule-bending)**. The corridor is insufficient to produce the observed correlation between conscious intention and physical outcome, so the Minimal Quantum Interaction tenet — properly read as "smallest interaction actually sufficient" rather than "smallest interaction that preserves ensemble statistics" — requires deviation. Several distinct proposals fall here:

- *Stapp's quantum Zeno picture.* Rapid conscious attention holds a particular eigenstate, shifting selection probabilities away from what a non-conscious measurement would produce. The modification depends on observation frequency and is explicitly Born-rule-bending.
- *Chalmers-McQueen Φ-dependent collapse.* Integrated-information-theoretic Φ gates collapse dynamics; "Φ resists superposition, and superpositions of distinct Φ values trigger collapse" (Chalmers & McQueen 2022). At the ensemble level, with Φ-dependent stochasticity added, the Born rule is generally not preserved — collapse probabilities depend on a consciousness-related variable that standard QM does not include. Chalmers and McQueen themselves note that no single scalar Φ measure suffices, sharpening rather than softening the Born-modifying implication.
- *Penrose-Hameroff Orch-OR.* Orchestrated objective reduction is explicitly a modification of QM, not selection within it. The theory lives or dies on deviations from standard unitary plus Born dynamics.
- *Arana (2025) time-extended weighting.* A time-integrated functional perturbs Born probabilities by an exponentially suppressed factor λ, recovering standard Born as λ → 0. This is a worked example of a "Born-violating-but-arbitrarily-close" proposal — a falsifiable phenomenological model.
- *Kauffman-Radin-Stapp acausal actualisation.* Quantum measurement as an acausal conversion of possibles into actuals by mind; naturally entails departures from a measurement-independent probability distribution.

**Trumping dualism (orthogonal to Born)**. The [trumping-preemption reading](/concepts/trumping-preemption/) developed in the Map treats consciousness as authoritative over trajectories the formalism already fully describes, leaving the Born rule outside the gap analysis entirely. Trumping sits on a different explanatory axis: the Born rule and the consciousness-physics question belong to separate axes under this reading, so trumping counts as neither a corridor-preserving nor a Born-modifying position. See [trumping-preemption](/concepts/trumping-preemption/) and the [delegatory-causation](/concepts/delegatory-causation/) articulation for the full treatment.

A fourth family — **no consciousness-physics interaction at all** (Everettian, Bohmian, orthodox pragmatism-without-interface) — rejects the premise and lies outside the Map's tenet commitments.

## Active Experimental Programmes and What They Could Falsify

Three experimental strands are alive in the 2025-2026 landscape. None isolates Born-rule violation as the measured variable; each bears on the broader question indirectly.

### Brain-Internal Quantum Coherence Witnesses

Kerskens and Pérez (2022) applied an MRI protocol to proton spins in brain water using entanglement-witness logic adapted from quantum-gravity experiments: if two known quantum systems entangle via an unknown mediator, the mediator must itself be quantum. They detected MRI signals resembling heartbeat-evoked potentials that standard MRI should not resolve, with signal strength correlated to short-term memory performance and sleep-wake transitions. Follow-up work through Lopez Perez et al. (2023) and the Frontiers 2025 review continues this programme.

Babcock, Hameroff, and colleagues (2025, *Neuroscience of Consciousness*) review room-temperature quantum effects in microtubules, microtubule resonances controlling membrane spiking, volatile anaesthetic targeting of microtubules, and what they describe as "direct biophysical evidence of a macroscopic entangled state in the living human brain." Hagan, Hameroff, and Tuszyński (2002) earlier estimated microtubule decoherence times up to ~10⁻⁵ seconds — seven orders of magnitude longer than Tegmark's (2000) estimate — under Debye-layer screening and ordered water.

Keppler (2025) proposes that cortical microcolumns couple to the electromagnetic zero-point field via glutamate-mediated resonance, supplying a field-theoretic candidate interface.

What these could falsify: a *negative* outcome — demonstrating that brain-internal coherence times are too short or coherence scales too small for any macroscopic quantum effect to matter — would foreclose the Penrose-Hameroff and Keppler programmes and weaken (but not kill) other Born-violating proposals that require a quantum substrate rich enough for conscious selection to operate.

What they could not falsify: these witnesses detect coherence or entanglement, not Born-rule deviation. Even a positive outcome establishes only the *possibility space* in which Born-violating dynamics could live. The Map notes that some of these programmes (microtubule-centred Orch-OR and Keppler's zero-point field resonance) should be presented as specific proposals rather than as established mechanisms; following the [writing-style guide's](/project/writing-style/) restraint on making microtubules load-bearing, the Map's core position survives whether or not any particular mechanism holds.

### Brain-to-Quantum-System Coupling Devices

The Nirvanic "Spark of Life" experiment (Gildert 2025, roadmap 2025-2027) couples a robot's sensory-action feedback loop to a quantum computer's qubits, using wave-function collapse to "choose" actions. The test is whether a quantum-controlled agent behaves differently — statistically — from a classical one. The framing is panpsychist ("QCAT"), not dualist, but the experimental design is the closest operational analogue currently on the roadmap to a "does consciousness bias quantum outcomes?" test. The CEO forecasts one to two years to an interesting signal.

What this could falsify: a *positive* signal — a quantum-coupled agent systematically outperforming its classical counterpart in a way that admits no classical explanation — would provide the first direct operational evidence for some form of mind-quantum interaction. A convincingly negative signal would be less decisive, since the device couples an artificial apparatus to a synthetic qubit system and the absence of a biological brain is a genuine disanalogy from the Map's target regime.

### Legacy Micro-Psychokinesis and RNG Studies

The Bösch, Steinkamp, and Boller (2006) meta-analysis of 380 studies on intention-to-random-number-generator effects found a small but statistically significant positive effect size, a strong inverse correlation between sample size and effect size, extreme heterogeneity, and Monte Carlo simulations compatible with publication bias. Maier and Dechamps (2018), using preregistered Bayesian methodology, reported strong evidence for the null hypothesis — no detectable micro-PK effect on quantum RNGs. The 227-participant multi-lab replication of the PEAR programme (~2 million trials) failed to reproduce the original composite 7σ signal. The Global Consciousness Project (Nelson, 1998-present) remains ongoing but faces critiques of post-hoc event selection, XOR bit-stream artefacts, and the absence of a proposed mechanism.

What this has already partially foreclosed: gross, unaided, intention-driven Born-rule violation of the PEAR variety is on very thin empirical ground. Maier-Dechamps's preregistered evidence for the null is the strongest single result in this debate as of 2026, and a Map that bet heavily on large macroscopic micro-PK signals would be bet against the current data.

What it has not foreclosed: smaller, targeted, brain-internal deviations of the Arana-λ kind, where the modification is exponentially suppressed outside the immediate neural-selection regime. Nor does it rule out corridor dualism, which predicts no statistical signature of the PEAR kind by construction.

### Collapse-Theory Parameter Bounds

[Spontaneous collapse models](/concepts/spontaneous-collapse-theories/) (GRW, CSL, Diósi-Penrose) add nonlinear stochastic collapse terms to Schrödinger evolution. Diósi-Penrose in its simplest form is now ruled out by germanium-detector data (2021). GRW and CSL have "survived by a whisker" at their original parameter values after LISA Pathfinder bounds, X-ray detector experiments, and intergalactic-medium heating constraints (SEP 2024). If a Chalmers-McQueen-style model couples Φ to the collapse dynamics, the parameter space shifts into less-constrained brain-specific regions — an advantage for the theorist, but also a warning about unfalsifiability drift.

## What the Map's Minimal Quantum Interaction Commits To

The Minimal Quantum Interaction tenet, strictly read, does not privilege the corridor. It privileges whichever minimum is *actually sufficient* to produce the observed correlation between conscious intention and physical outcome. The corridor is the Map's working hypothesis because it is the most conservative scenario, not because it is definitionally required.

The Map holds three attitudes in tension here, each defensible:

1. **If corridor dualism is sufficient**, the Map's programme is maximally compatible with existing Born-rule tests and the 2025 GPT-steering derivation, at the cost of making the single-event mental-to-physical channel ensemble-invisible — which risks collapsing into a subtle epiphenomenalism-at-the-ensemble-level unless the [trumping reading](/concepts/trumping-preemption/) is adopted.

2. **If minimum-outside-the-corridor dualism is required**, the Map becomes empirically distinguishable from physicalism in principle — an Arana-λ, a Stapp-Zeno rate, a Chalmers-McQueen Φ-collapse strength, a Penrose OR timescale — and loses nothing tenet-critical, since the Minimal Quantum Interaction tenet reads "minimal" as "smallest sufficient," not "corridor-respecting."

3. **If trumping dualism is the correct structural move**, the Born rule and the consciousness question are orthogonal. The Born rule governs the physical formalism's probabilistic prescription; consciousness is authoritative on a different axis, so Born-rule tests — positive or negative — neither support nor threaten the Map's dualism.

All three remain live. The empirical state of Born-rule testing cannot adjudicate among them because it cannot yet probe the brain-internal regime at the required precision.

## Relation to Site Perspective

This article directly engages three of the Map's five tenets.

**Tenet 1 (Dualism)**: The existence of a physical regime that is genuinely unprobed — the brain-internal conscious-selection regime — is what opens the space for a non-reducible mental channel in the first place. If the Born rule were tested inside conscious brains and held to the same 10⁻² precision as in triple-slit experiments, one degree of freedom for dualism would close, though trumping and corridor variants would still survive.

**Tenet 2 (Minimal Quantum Interaction)**: The article catalogues the Map's internal taxonomy of what "minimal" can mean — corridor-preserving, minimum-outside-the-corridor, or trumping-orthogonal. The Map treats this as a live structural question rather than a settled commitment. The evolution-state todo queue reflects this by holding a P3 task to synthesise the three options into a dedicated concept article; that synthesis is not yet written, and the present article should be read as an empirical complement to that pending concept-level treatment.

**Tenet 3 (Bidirectional Interaction)**: A Born-rule-preserving corridor position has to explain how single-event selection adds up to a genuine mental-to-physical causal channel without leaving an ensemble signature. This is a known tension, not a dissolved one — arguably the Map's sharpest open question, and the reason minimum-outside-the-corridor readings remain attractive despite their empirical riskiness.

**Tenet 5 (Occam's Razor Has Limits)**: The temptation to treat "the Born rule has held in every test so far" as evidence of universal validity is a case where simplicity is unreliable with incomplete knowledge. The tested regimes are a narrow slice of physical configurations; treating the extrapolation to the brain-internal regime as parsimony disguises an underlying assumption.

The Map's working position is that the empirical question is genuinely open, that the present generation of experiments cannot adjudicate it, and that intellectual honesty requires holding the corridor-vs-minimum-outside-corridor distinction as a live fork rather than a solved problem.

## What Negative Results Would and Would Not Foreclose

A hypothetical clean null on all three experimental strands — no brain-internal quantum coherence, no quantum-agent signal from Nirvanic-style coupling, and continued preregistered nulls on micro-PK — would foreclose:

- Orch-OR and any theory requiring macroscopic biological quantum coherence
- PEAR-variant large-signal micro-PK hypotheses (already mostly foreclosed)
- Keppler's zero-point-field resonance interface (insofar as it predicts detectable coherence)
- Any "minimum-outside-the-corridor" model that predicts detectable effects at current experimental sensitivity

It would not foreclose:

- Corridor dualism, which predicts no statistical signature at any sensitivity
- Arana-style exponentially-suppressed Born modifications with λ below the experimental resolution
- Trumping dualism, which is agnostic to Born-rule violation
- Chalmers-McQueen Φ-collapse if Φ-coupling parameters are tuned below detectability

This asymmetry matters. The Map can survive a clean sweep of negative experimental results by retreating to corridor or trumping readings. What it cannot do — without dishonesty — is pretend that existing Born-rule tests have already validated the corridor over the minimum-outside-corridor alternative. They have not. They could not, given their regime.
