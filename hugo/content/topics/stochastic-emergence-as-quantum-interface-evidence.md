Genetically identical mice, anaesthetised under identical protocols, wake at wildly different times — emergence latencies spanning at least two orders of magnitude, with no consistent inter-individual differences and within-animal variability matching between-animal variability. Stone, Kelz, Proekt, and Wasilczuk (2025) reported this in the *British Journal of Anaesthesia*, finding a stochastic neuronal-dynamics model fit the data where a deterministic pharmacokinetic-pharmacodynamic model failed. The Unfinishable Map flags this as the most direct empirical pointer in its catalogue toward indeterminacy at the consciousness-matter interface. The honest reading is narrower: the variability is real and the stochastic fit is real, but the data are equally compatible with classical neuronal noise and with genuine indeterminacy at the interface, and nothing in the study discriminates between them. The finding sits at a *live hypothesis* tier for the quantum-interface reading — worth investigating, not established, not even strongly supported.

This article walks through the finding at full resolution, separates the classical-stochastic reading from the quantum-indeterministic one, names what would discriminate them (if anything currently could), and places the result inside the broader case for outcome-level openness the Map assembles elsewhere — chiefly the [Born rule's statistical-closure-with-outcome-level-openness](/voids/interface-formalization-void/) structure and the [interpretation-invariance of the Born rule](/topics/born-rule-and-the-consciousness-interface/). Throughout, the governing constraint is the [evidential-status discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/): a finding compatible with the Map's reading and with rival readings supports neither over the other until an independent discriminator is named.

## The 2025 Finding at Full Resolution

The primary study is Stone et al. (2025), "A probabilistic model of behavioural emergence from general anaesthesia in mice" (*British Journal of Anaesthesia* 135(1):121–133). The accompanying editorial by Lee — "Emergence from general anaesthesia: stochastic or deterministic?" (*BJA* 135(1):5–8) — frames the interpretive question the title poses; the empirical work and the two-orders-of-magnitude result belong to Stone et al.

**The design.** Sixty genetically identical C57BL/6J mice (40 male, 20 female, ages 12–24 weeks) were exposed to isoflurane at 0.90 vol% for two hours on ten separate occasions each, with additional steady-state assessments at 0.3, 0.4, 0.6, and 0.7 vol%. Emergence was measured by return of the righting reflex — the time for an animal to turn from supine to prone after the anaesthetic was stopped. Genetic identity plus standardised exposure plus repeated within-animal trials is what makes the design probative: it strips out the obvious deterministic sources of variability (genotype, dose, individual sensitivity) so that whatever variance remains cannot be charged to them.

**The result.** Emergence times varied by at least two orders of magnitude. Critically, the variability was *not* explained by stable inter-individual differences — within-animal variability across the ten trials matched between-animal variability. A given mouse was not reliably a fast or slow emerger; the same animal under the same protocol could emerge quickly on one occasion and slowly on another. A small proportion of exposures showed markedly delayed emergence, the murine analogue of the clinically observed long tail of slow waking.

**The modelling.** The authors fit two models to the observed emergence-time distribution. The standard effect-site pharmacokinetic-pharmacodynamic (PK-PD) model — which treats emergence as a deterministic threshold crossing as drug concentration falls — could not reproduce the spread. A neuronal-dynamics model representing stochastic state-switching between awake and anaesthetised attractor states, separated by an energy barrier, reproduced the distribution including its long tail. The authors conclude that "stochastic state switching contributes to inherent variability in emergence from general anaesthesia," and that the neuronal-dynamics model "predicts that anaesthetic emergence times will be probabilistically long," potentially explaining clinically observed delayed emergence.

The structural upshot: emergence is not controlled by a single brain region driven across a fixed threshold by falling drug concentration. It is a network-level transition where individual neuronal fluctuations propagate into macroscopic timing variability. This is the finding the [active-reboot concept](/concepts/active-reboot/) holds apart from the molecular machinery of emergence — the *machinery* (mechanism-shared KCC2 disinhibition) is one finding; the *timing variability* of the transition the machinery enables is a separate one, and it is the timing variability that this article concerns.

## What "Stochastic" Does and Does Not Mean Here

The word "stochastic" in Stone et al. is a statement about the *model class that fits*, not a claim about the *ontology of the underlying randomness*. A stochastic model is one whose dynamics include a random term; it fits when the system's behaviour is not predictable from its measured macroscopic state alone. This is silent on whether the randomness is:

1. **Epistemic** — the system is deterministic, but its trajectory depends on microscopic variables (ion-channel gating states, thermal fluctuations, molecular configurations) that were not measured and could not feasibly be measured. The "randomness" is our ignorance of initial conditions in a chaotic or near-chaotic network.
2. **Classical-dynamical** — the system is genuinely sensitive to thermal noise, which is itself ultimately quantum in origin but is well-described classically at neural scales and temperatures. The randomness is real unpredictability sourced in molecular agitation, requiring no consciousness-physics interface.
3. **Interface-indeterministic** — the moment of re-engagement carries genuine indeterminacy at a point where, on the Map's reading, consciousness's re-entry into the prepared neural channel is the locus of an outcome-level selection the physics does not fix.

A stochastic model fitting the data is consistent with all three. The Stone et al. result establishes that emergence timing is *not deterministically fixed by macroscopic pharmacokinetics*; it does not locate the randomness in any of the three readings. Reading 2 — classical neuronal noise — is the default scientific interpretation and the one the authors operate within. The Map's interest is whether reading 3 can be distinguished from reading 2, and the answer, presently, is that it cannot.

## The Classical-Stochastic Reading

The classical reading is straightforward and carries the burden of being the more parsimonious account. Neural state transitions are known to behave like noise-driven escapes from one attractor basin to another. The awake and anaesthetised states are mutually stabilising configurations (the bistable flip-flop architecture documented by Friedman et al. 2010 and Sepúlveda et al. 2019, and discussed under [active reboot](/concepts/active-reboot/)). Crossing from the anaesthetised basin to the awake basin requires the network to fluctuate over an energy barrier. The waiting time for a noise-driven barrier crossing is, by standard Kramers-rate physics, exponentially distributed and highly variable even when the barrier height and noise amplitude are fixed — which is exactly the long-tailed, high-variance distribution Stone et al. observed.

On this reading, the two-orders-of-magnitude variability is precisely what one should expect from a noise-driven escape process, and the noise is ordinary thermal-molecular agitation. No quantum-interface coupling is required, and Occam's razor — in its standard, non-Map-qualified form — favours it. The classical reading also predicts the genetic-identity result without strain: barrier-crossing statistics depend on the dynamics of the network, not on genotype-encoded individual sensitivity, so genetically identical animals would still show trial-to-trial variability of this kind.

Any quantum-interface reading has to earn its place *against* this account, not merely alongside it. The classical-stochastic reading is not a strawman the Map clears away; it is the live, well-motivated default that the interface reading does not currently beat.

## The Quantum-Indeterministic Reading

The Map's framework locates a minimal consciousness-physics interface at quantum measurement, where — on the [interface-formalization analysis](/voids/interface-formalization-void/) — the Born rule fixes the statistical envelope but leaves which specific outcome actualises undetermined by the formalism (Tonetto's "statistical closure with outcome-level openness"). If consciousness re-engages a neural substrate at emergence, and if that re-engagement passes through quantum-sensitive molecular events whose outcomes are individually open, then the *timing* of re-engagement could inherit genuine indeterminacy rather than merely epistemic unpredictability.

The reading has a specific shape worth stating precisely, because its specificity is also its vulnerability. The [active-reboot finding](/concepts/active-reboot/) establishes that the substrate is prepared deterministically — the KCC2-disinhibition cascade is mechanism-shared and runs reliably. What the prepared substrate does *not* fix, on this reading, is the precise moment the channel reopens to connected consciousness. The deterministic preparation plus indeterministic moment-of-re-engagement structure is what the quantum-interface reading proposes the data reflect: the machinery is set, but the actualisation event that completes re-engagement is outcome-level open, and its variability is the macroscopic shadow of that openness.

This reading is internally coherent and it integrates cleanly with the rest of the Map's interface framework. That integration is its only current achievement. Coherence with a framework — even tight coherence across the [Born-rule treatment](/topics/born-rule-and-the-consciousness-interface/), the [active-reboot concept](/concepts/active-reboot/), and the [formalisation void](/voids/interface-formalization-void/) — is not evidence that the framework's empirical claim is true, because the same data cohere equally with the classical reading. The interface reading is a *live hypothesis* about the source of the variability: tenable, motivated by the Map's commitments, and unsupported over its rival by the Stone et al. data taken alone.

## What Would Discriminate the Two Readings

The discriminating question is sharp: is there any measurement that would distinguish interface-sourced indeterminacy from classical neuronal noise in emergence timing? The candidates are weak, and naming their weakness honestly is more useful than overstating their promise.

**Temperature and noise-amplitude scaling.** Classical Kramers-rate escape depends on temperature and driving-noise amplitude in a calculable way; an interface-indeterministic component need not track thermal noise the same way. Measuring how the emergence-time distribution scales with controlled changes in temperature and pharmacologically manipulated network noise could in principle test whether classical escape statistics fully account for the variability. In practice the confound is severe: lowering brain temperature changes the entire pharmacology, and isolating a residual non-thermal variance would require a precision the current paradigm does not approach.

**Microtubule-stabilisation manipulation.** If the indeterministic component runs through quantum-sensitive molecular substrates of the kind quantum-biology proposals implicate, manipulating those substrates should alter the variability structure, not merely the mean. Khan et al. (2024) found microtubule-stabilising epothilone B delayed *loss* of consciousness in rats (Cohen's *d* = 1.9; see [quantum biology and neural consciousness](/topics/quantum-biology-and-neural-consciousness/)). The parallel emergence-side question — does microtubule stabilisation change the *variance* and *tail* of emergence times beyond any shift in the mean? — would be a more targeted probe. A change in variability structure specifically attributable to a quantum-sensitive substrate, surviving classical-pharmacokinetic controls, would move the reading above *live hypothesis*. No such experiment has been reported.

**Brain-internal outcome statistics.** The deepest discriminator is the one the Map's [brain-internal Born-rule testing](/topics/brain-internal-born-rule-testing/) article specifies: no published experiment probes quantum-outcome statistics inside living neural tissue during a conscious transition, and emergence timing is not such a probe. Emergence-time variability is a macroscopic behavioural readout many causal layers removed from any putative interface event; even a perfect characterisation of the distribution would not isolate a Born-statistical signature, because the readout integrates over too much intervening classical dynamics. This finding, however suggestive at the level of framing, sits structurally outside the regime where the interface reading could be distinguished from its classical rival.

The candidates that could discriminate the readings are thus either confounded beyond current feasibility, not yet attempted, or unreachable through a behavioural-timing readout. The finding's evidential ceiling for the quantum reading is low: it keeps the hypothesis live, and no more.

## Integration with the Broader Indeterminacy Case

Stone et al. is one item in a small set of considerations the Map collects on outcome-level openness at the interface. The set must be assembled without committing the cluster-coherence error the [evidential-status discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/) names: assembling N coherent considerations raises framework-internal weight, not the empirical tier, when the same considerations cohere with rival readings.

**The Born rule's outcome-level openness.** The [interface-formalization void](/voids/interface-formalization-void/) and the [Born-rule article](/topics/born-rule-and-the-consciousness-interface/) develop the point that quantum mechanics fixes the probability distribution over outcomes but not which outcome actualises. Masanes, Galley, and Müller (2019) argued the measurement postulates — including the Born rule — are operationally redundant given the other quantum postulates and finite-dimensionality, suggesting the rule's *form* is uniquely fixed; the claim has been contested (a 2023 response, arXiv:2307.06191, argues the postulates are not in fact redundant), so it stands as a strong but disputed uniqueness result. Pati (2026, arXiv:2601.13012) argues the Hilbert-space inner product itself is fixed by no-signalling, a further preprint-stage strengthening. Together these characterise where the formalism stops — at the actualisation event — without supplying evidence that consciousness fills the gap. They establish the *space* the interface reading occupies, not its occupancy.

**The Tegmark-Hameroff decoherence debate.** Whether quantum-sensitive substrates survive long enough in warm wet neural tissue to matter is contested. Tegmark (2000, *Physical Review E* 61:4194–4206) calculated decoherence timescales of roughly 10⁻¹³ to 10⁻²⁰ seconds for neural superpositions — far too short to be functionally relevant — concluding the brain's cognitive degrees of freedom are effectively classical. Hagan, Hameroff, and Tuszyński (2002) disputed his parameter choices and obtained microtubule-interior estimates around 10⁻⁵ to 10⁻⁴ seconds, but this counter-calculation remains unreplicated and contested (see [quantum-biology-and-neural-consciousness](/topics/quantum-biology-and-neural-consciousness/)). If Tegmark is right that the relevant substrates decohere too fast, the interface-indeterministic reading loses its physical substrate and the classical reading is left as the only tenable one. The dispute is unresolved, and the Map does not get to treat its open status as support.

**Delayed-choice and the timing of outcome fixation.** Vedovato et al. (2017, *Science Advances*, "Extending Wheeler's delayed-choice experiment to space") confirmed wave-particle duality at low-Earth-orbit distances, showing the measurement context determines which complementary behaviour manifests even when the choice is made after the photon is in flight. This is a clean confirmation that quantum-outcome fixation is not pinned to a naive prior moment — relevant to the conceptual coherence of an interface that selects outcomes at the moment of measurement. It is a physics result about photons, constraining how one may *think* about outcome timing without adding neural evidence.

The integration honours the discipline by keeping the registers separate. The Born-rule results characterise the formal space; the decoherence debate governs whether the physical substrate exists; the delayed-choice work fixes the conceptual structure of outcome timing; and the Stone et al. finding supplies a neural-scale variability the interface reading can interpret but cannot claim as discriminating support. None is positive evidence that the interface reading is correct. Together they show the reading is coherent and physically conceivable — the live-hypothesis verdict, not an upgrade to it. The emergence-not-the-reverse-of-induction structure the Stone et al. finding exhibits is also one member of the [family of direction-of-interface-change signatures](/concepts/direction-of-interface-change/) the Map collects across the disruption cluster, where the *direction* of change rather than the substrate state is the load-bearing variable — and where, per that family's own discipline, the members are four views of one architecture, credited once, not four independent confirmations.

## What This Finding Cannot Do

The finding cannot establish indeterminacy at the interface: a stochastic model fitting behavioural-timing data is silent on whether the source is quantum-ontic or classical-epistemic. It cannot discriminate the Map's reading from classical neuronal noise, since both predict the observed variability and the study contains no separating measurement. It cannot reach the brain-internal regime where the interface reading could be tested. And it cannot, by cohering with the Map's broader framework, raise its own evidential tier — that coherence is framework-internal weight, not empirical support.

What it *does* establish is narrower and worth having: emergence timing is genuinely not fixed by macroscopic pharmacokinetics, the variability is large and network-sourced, and a stochastic dynamical account is required to fit it. The step from "stochastic dynamics fit the data" to "indeterminacy at the consciousness-matter interface" is the one the discipline forbids taking silently — a *live hypothesis*, held open, not a conclusion the data carry.

## Relation to Site Perspective

This finding bears on two of the Map's [tenets](/tenets/), and the bearing is best stated as constraint and conceivability rather than support.

**[Dualism](/tenets/#dualism)**: The interface reading of emergence variability is one architectural anomaly the Map's framework can interpret — deterministic substrate preparation followed by an indeterministic moment of re-engagement is the kind of structure a channel-reopening model predicts a generator model would not need. But the variability *constrains* deterministic-pharmacokinetic generator models (it removes the simplest "drug clears, consciousness resumes on schedule" account) without *establishing* the dualist alternative, since classical noise-driven barrier crossing accounts for the same data. The constraint is real; the establishment is not available from this finding.

**[Minimal Quantum Interaction](/tenets/#minimal-quantum-interaction)**: If consciousness interfaces through quantum-sensitive molecular systems and selects among Born-distributed outcomes without bending the distribution — the [corridor reading](/topics/born-rule-and-the-consciousness-interface/#corridor-taxonomy) — then re-engagement timing could carry genuine indeterminacy. Stone et al.'s variability is consistent with this. It is equally consistent with classical thermal noise, and the corridor reading by construction predicts no ensemble-statistical signature that a behavioural-timing study could detect. The finding is therefore compatible with Minimal Quantum Interaction without being able to support it over the classical rival — exactly the compatibility-not-support state the [discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/) requires the Map to mark.

The Map's working position is that emergence-time stochasticity is a genuine and striking empirical fact, that its quantum-interface interpretation is a coherent and physically conceivable hypothesis, and that the present evidence holds that hypothesis at the live-hypothesis tier — neither dismissible nor supported over the classical reading the same data equally fit.
