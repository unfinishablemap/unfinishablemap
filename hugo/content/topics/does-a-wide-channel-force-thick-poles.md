The [channel-width axis](/topics/channel-width-third-axis/) orders dualist positions by how narrow the mind-physical interaction interface is — from Saad's selection-only floor, through Stapp's narrow-channel-with-thick-endpoints, up to Cartesian energy-transfer at the ceiling. Plotting the canonical positions against pole thickness leaves one region of the plane conspicuously empty: no named position pairs a *wide* channel with *thin* poles. The parent article flags this as unsettled — "whether that empty region is a contingent gap in the literature or a structural impossibility (does a wide channel force at least one pole to thicken?) is unsettled." This article runs the coherence-test the parent only posed. It specifies what a wide-channel/thin-pole position would have to claim, applies the existing mechanism-cost machinery to ask whether such a position is coherent, and reaches a cartographic verdict: the cell is *mostly* structurally forbidden, with one narrow contingent-gap exception that the literature has simply not articulated.

The posture is the parent's posture throughout: cartographic, not adjudicative, per [evidential-status-discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/). Locating an empty cell — and ruling on whether it is empty by accident or by geometry — is a claim about the *taxonomy's shape*, not about which dualist position is true. That a region is structurally forbidden tells you nothing about whether the Map's own cell (narrow channel, thin-to-medium poles) is the correct one. The forcing argument below, where it succeeds, constrains the map; it does not move the territory.

## What the Vacant Cell Would Have to Claim {#what-it-claims}

The two thickness axes ask how much ontology each pole carries; channel width asks how much passes between the poles and through how narrow an aperture (the [what-channel-width-measures](/topics/channel-width-third-axis/#what-it-measures) distinction). A wide-channel/thin-pole position must therefore conjoin two commitments that, on inspection, pull against each other.

**The wide-channel commitment.** "Wide" means the interface admits operations richer than selection among physically-prepared alternatives. The [selection-only channel](/concepts/selection-only-channel/)'s taxonomy of what-it-is-not gives the ordering precisely: a wide channel is one that does at least one of *shift the candidate distribution away from Born values* (probability-bias), *modify the candidate set itself* (basis-choice or candidate-generation), or *transfer a quantity physics tracks as work* (energy-injection). The two widest operations — candidate-generation and energy-injection — are the ones that make a channel unambiguously wide rather than merely intermediate, so they are the test cases.

**The thin-pole commitment.** "Thin mind" means, per the [mind-side thickness scale](/topics/four-quadrant-dualism-taxonomy/#mind-side), only what is phenomenally introspectable — bare qualia, no unconscious mental substance, no unperceived qualia-spaces, no causal repertoire beyond what property-dualism admits. "Thin physical" means physics' own ontology with nothing added — no hidden variables, no quiddities, no ignorance-facts, with closure holding except at probabilistic indeterminacy.

So the vacant cell's inhabitant must claim: an interface that injects energy or generates novel candidates, driven by a mind that is no more than bare introspectable qualia, acting on a physics that is no richer than the bare quantum formalism. The coherence-test asks whether anything can satisfy both halves at once.

## The Forcing Argument: Width Pulls at Both Poles {#forcing-argument}

Run each wide operation through the mechanism-cost battery — the three questions ([causation, interface, conservation](/topics/mechanism-costs-dualism-thickness-quadrants/#three-questions)) the existing machinery already applies cell by cell. Each wide operation, it turns out, levies a debt that one pole or the other must thicken to pay.

**Energy-injection forces the physical pole (and probably the mind pole) to thicken.** An energy-injecting channel transfers work across the interface. The [mechanism-costs reading of Q4](/topics/mechanism-costs-dualism-thickness-quadrants/#q4) identifies this as the heaviest conservation engagement: the inhabitant "owes either that conservation is local and symmetry-conditional... or" routes influence through a probabilistic channel that is *not* wide. A thin physical pole — bare quantum formalism with closure at the indeterminacy level — has no room to absorb a non-conservative energy injection. Either conservation fails (which is a substantive *addition* to the physics, not the bare formalism — the physical pole has thickened into a modified-dynamics ontology), or the injection is illusory. The conservation debt cannot be paid from a thin physical pole's resources. And the injecting side must be the kind of entity that *carries* energy to deliver — which a bare-qualia mind, with no causal repertoire beyond the introspectable, is not. Energy-injection is itself a thick causal repertoire; a pole exercising it is not thin. Both poles are pulled upward.

**Candidate-generation forces the mind pole to thicken.** A candidate-generation channel lets the mind "supply novelty beyond the brain-encoded set" — exactly the operation the [selection-only channel forbids](/concepts/selection-only-channel/#what-the-channel-is-not). But the content-confinement constraint runs the other way for the thin-mind claim: under selection-only, "novel phenomenal states must be pre-generated by brain dynamics; they cannot be brought into being by selection alone." A mind that *generates* candidates the brain did not encode is, by definition, a mind whose content exceeds the brain-side candidate space — which is to say, a mind carrying content beyond the introspective-and-brain-encoded horizon. That is the [thick-mind commitment](/topics/four-quadrant-dualism-taxonomy/#mind-side) verbatim: "mental content vastly exceeding introspection." A thin mind has nothing extra to contribute to the alternative set; the moment it contributes novelty, it has stopped being thin. Candidate-generation forces the mind pole up.

**Probability-bias is the marginal case — and it is not wide.** Shifting the Born distribution within a fixed candidate set is wider than selection-only but does not, on its own, force either pole to thicken: a mind can bias {p_i} without carrying extra subliminal content, and a physics can have its statistics nudged without acquiring hidden variables. This is why probability-bias sits in the *intermediate* reading of the [Minimal Quantum Interaction](/tenets/#minimal-quantum-interaction) tenet rather than at the wide end. The forcing argument does not reach it — but neither does the "wide" label. Probability-bias occupies the middle of the axis, not the vacant wide cell. It is evidence that the *middle* of the channel-width axis is compatible with thin poles, not that the *wide* end is.

The pattern is consistent: the two operations that make a channel unambiguously wide each levy a debt payable only by thickening a pole. Width and thinness are not independent at the extremes the way the parent's Saad/Stapp pairing showed them to be independent at the narrow end.

## Contingent Gap or Structural Impossibility? {#verdict}

The forcing argument does not deliver a clean universal impossibility; it delivers a structured verdict with one exception.

**For candidate-generation: structural.** The forcing here is analytic, not contingent. "Mind contributes novelty beyond the brain-encoded set" and "mind carries no content beyond the introspective-and-brain horizon" are contradictories. No articulation could satisfy both, because the second is the negation of the first's enabling condition. The wide-channel/thin-mind combination *for candidate-generation channels* is structurally impossible — empty by geometry, not by historical accident. The literature did not fail to find this position; there is no position to find.

**For energy-injection: structural on the standard reading, with a contingent-gap escape.** The conservation debt analytically excludes a thin *physical* pole if conservation is taken as inviolable: a thin physics has no resources to absorb non-conservative injection, so the physical pole must thicken into a modified-dynamics ontology. That much is structural. But there is a thin escape the literature has genuinely not articulated: a position that takes the physical pole as thin *and accepts a localised conservation violation as a brute primitive* rather than as added physical structure — a "thin-substance Cartesianism" that injects energy through a bare interface and simply denies that conservation is exceptionless, without enriching either pole's ontology. Whether such a position keeps the physical pole genuinely thin (a brute exception to a law is arguably itself a thickening) or merely relabels the debt is exactly the kind of question the [Q4 conservation analysis](/topics/mechanism-costs-dualism-thickness-quadrants/#q4) flags as a long-standing dispute the Map does not adjudicate. This is the one cell of the vacant region that is a *contingent* gap: not analytically forbidden, but unoccupied because no one has found it worth defending. The historical Cartesian energy-injector came bundled with a *res cogitans* thick on the mind side; nobody has built the thin-mind, thin-physics, conservation-denying variant.

So the vacant cell partitions. Most of it is structurally forbidden — candidate-generation under any reading, energy-injection under conservation-respecting readings. A sliver is a contingent gap — energy-injection that pays its conservation debt by denying conservation rather than by enriching physics. The parent's either/or ("contingent gap *or* structural impossibility") resolves as *both, in different sub-regions*. The wide-channel/thin-pole region is not uniformly one or the other; the axis-geometry is finer than the binary question assumed.

This refines the parent's framing rather than merely answering it. The parent asked whether a wide channel "forces *at least one* pole to thicken." The answer is: yes for candidate-generation (forces the mind pole, analytically), yes for energy-injection on the standard reading (forces the physical pole), and *no* only on a conservation-denying reading whose thinness is itself contestable. The forcing is real wherever conservation and content-confinement are held fixed; it relaxes only where one of those background commitments is given up — and giving them up is itself a substantive move, not a free thin-pole position.

## Symmetry With the Q4 Vacancy {#q4-symmetry}

The two-axis thickness taxonomy already noted a [systematically under-populated quadrant](/topics/four-quadrant-dualism-taxonomy/#q4) — max-mind/max-physical, avoided because "the discipline's training reads parsimony as virtue." The channel-width axis turns out to have its own vacant region, but the two vacancies have *opposite* explanatory structures, and the contrast is itself a cartographic finding.

Q4's emptiness is *sociological*: the cell is coherent and inhabitable (Descartes, Stapp, Goff sit there), but contemporary training disfavours paying its mechanism bill. Nothing forbids Q4; the discipline avoids it. The wide-channel/thin-pole cell's emptiness is *mostly structural*: the cell is largely incoherent, so the literature's avoidance of it is not a contingent preference but a tracking of genuine impossibility. A taxonomy can have empty cells for either reason, and distinguishing them is part of what a locating axis is *for*. The Q4 vacancy is a place the field could go and won't; the wide/thin vacancy is mostly a place there is nothing to go to.

This matters for how the channel-width axis should be read. A naive cartographer, seeing two empty cells, might treat them as the same kind of gap and predict that both will fill as the literature matures. The mechanism-cost analysis shows they will not. Q4 may fill if parsimony norms loosen (the Map's [Occam's Razor Has Limits](/tenets/#occams-razor-has-limits) tenet is one such loosening). The structurally-forbidden part of the wide/thin region cannot fill, because no coherent position projects there. Only the contingent sliver — conservation-denying thin Cartesianism — is a genuine prediction-of-possible-occupancy, and even that is a position the field has reasons to find unattractive.

## Relation to Site Perspective {#site-perspective}

The Map's [Minimal Quantum Interaction](/tenets/#minimal-quantum-interaction) tenet, read structurally, is a commitment to *minimum channel width* — locating the Map at or near the narrow floor of this axis, as the [parent article](/topics/channel-width-third-axis/#site-perspective) established. The present analysis touches the Map's commitments in two ways, both of which must be held in the cartographic register.

First, the forcing argument incidentally explains *why* the Map's narrow-channel commitment coheres with its flexibility on pole thickness. At the narrow end of the axis, channel width and pole thickness are genuinely independent — Saad (thin poles) and Stapp (thick poles) share the narrow-channel class. The Map can hold a narrow channel firmly (MQI) while leaving the mind-pole free to thicken toward Myersian or Stappian richness, precisely because narrowness does not force thinness the way *width* forces thickness. The asymmetry the forcing argument reveals — width constrains the poles, narrowness does not — is what gives the Map room to commit firmly on the channel while staying loose on the poles. This is a structural observation about the axis, not an argument that the Map's narrow channel is the true one.

Second, and as a discipline-caution: that the wide-channel/thin-pole cell is mostly forbidden is *not* evidence for the Map's narrow channel. The temptation to read "the wide-and-thin alternative is incoherent, so the narrow channel wins" is exactly the [possibility-to-probability slippage](/concepts/possibility-probability-slippage/) the [evidential-status discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/) guards against. Ruling out one region of logical space does not raise the probability of the Map's region; it only sharpens the map of what the alternatives are. The Map's narrow channel remains a reasoned methodological commitment (MQI as a tenet), not a finding licensed by the geometry of the vacant cell. The structural-impossibility verdict is a claim about the taxonomy's shape; the Map's occupancy of its own cell is a separate commitment that this analysis neither strengthens nor weakens.

The Map's tenets also leave the contingent-gap sliver untouched in a telling way. The conservation-denying thin Cartesian variant is not a position the Map occupies or needs — the Map's conservation engagement runs through selection-without-injection ([conservation laws and mental causation](/concepts/conservation-laws-and-mental-causation/)), which keeps the channel narrow precisely so that no conservation debt is incurred. The Map sits at the opposite end of the axis from the vacant cell, and arrives there by paying its conservation bill at the floor rather than denying the bill at the ceiling.

## Where This Points Next {#points-next}

Three questions follow from charting the vacant cell.

**Is the contingent sliver worth articulating?** The conservation-denying thin-Cartesian variant is logically available but undefended. Whether it is a genuine position or a degenerate one — whether a brute conservation exception counts as keeping physics thin — is a question the [mechanism-cost cartography](/topics/mechanism-costs-dualism-thickness-quadrants/) could take up by running its three-question battery on the variant explicitly. The present article locates the sliver; it does not fully cost it.

**Does the basis-choice operation belong in the vacant cell or outside it?** Stapp's basis-choice layer modifies the candidate set without injecting energy, which makes it wider than selection-only but not obviously candidate-generating. Whether a *thin*-mind basis-choice channel is coherent — whether choosing which observable the brain measures requires subliminal content — is a finer test the binary candidate-generation analysis here does not settle. The [layered-narrowness observation](/concepts/selection-only-channel/#what-the-channel-is-not) suggests basis-choice may itself need its own sub-cell.

**Do other locating axes have structurally-forbidden cells?** If the channel-width axis has a region empty by geometry rather than by sociology, the two-axis thickness grid and the mechanism-cost battery may have analogous forbidden regions worth charting. A systematic survey of which cells across the Map's locating taxonomies are *structurally* empty versus *contingently* empty would be a cartographic project in its own right — and one the distinction drawn here makes newly askable.
