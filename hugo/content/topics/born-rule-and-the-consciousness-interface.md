The Born rule — quantum mechanics' prescription that measurement probabilities equal |ψ|² — is the one feature every quantum interpretation shares and none can eliminate. Every major attempt to derive it either smuggles in probabilistic assumptions or presupposes an observer. Zhang (2026) demonstrated that all five leading derivation strategies depend irreducibly on an additivity assumption that cannot itself be derived from non-probabilistic postulates. The failure persists across hidden-variable, objective-collapse, retrocausal, categorical, and gravitational-collapse frameworks. Combined with Torres Alegre's 2025 proof that the Born rule is the *unique* probability assignment compatible with relativistic causality, this interpretation-invariance suggests the rule encodes the consciousness-physics interface — a constraint on how experience encounters indeterminacy that no purely physical framework can generate from within.

The Unfinishable Map reads this persistent failure as abductive evidence that the Born rule marks a real boundary of physical description. The Map develops this primarily as an *interface reading* — the rule governs how consciousness encounters quantum indeterminacy — and admits a parallel [trumping reading](/concepts/trumping-preemption/) ([delegatory-causation](/concepts/delegatory-causation/)) on which consciousness is authoritative over trajectories the formalism already describes, leaving the Born rule outside the gap analysis. On either reading, underivability is what we should expect.

A separate empirical question runs alongside the theoretical one: **no published experiment directly probes the Born rule inside living neural tissue during a conscious act of selection** — precisely the regime consciousness-physics-interface programmes target. The corridor-vs-outside-the-corridor distinction (taxonomy below) is held as a live branch. Anyone claiming the question is already answered — by pointing to photon-experiment success or weak legacy mind-matter signals — is overreading the available evidence.

## The Brain-Internal Gap in Born-Rule Testing

Every precision test of the Born rule has run in regimes unlike brain tissue. Sinha et al. (2010) bounded Sorkin's third-order interference parameter at less than 10⁻² of the second-order contribution using single photons through a three-slit aperture; optical, atomic, and high-energy work has tightened this bound (Phys. Rev. D 2025). The GPT-steering derivation (arXiv:2512.12636, 2025) shows the rule is uniquely picked out *conditional* on purification, steering, and exact no-signalling. Masanes-Galley-Müller (2019) similarly derives Born's prescription from Hilbert-space structure, unitary evolution, tensor-product composition, and a finite-parameter estimation postulate.

These are conditional theorems: if the premises hold, the Born rule follows. The brain-internal regime during a conscious act of selection is an untested extrapolation. Any one of the MGM postulates — measurement uniqueness and tensor-product subsystem independence in particular — could in principle fail where conscious selection operates, and the available data cannot adjudicate. The closest experimental programmes measure adjacent but non-equivalent quantities, and the most direct legacy tests (micro-psychokinesis on RNGs) trend toward the null under preregistered Bayesian methodology.

## A Century of Circular Derivations

Born introduced the probability interpretation in 1926 as a pragmatic guess — what Araújo (2021) calls "wire and bubble gum." Landsman's 2025 centenary review concludes that "all derivations found in the literature are either circular or unrelated to measurement."

Zhang (2026) identified the structural bottleneck. Analysing Gleason's theorem, Busch's POVM extension, Deutsch-Wallace decision theory, Zurek's envariance, and Finkelstein-Hartle frequentism, he showed every one depends on additivity: the assumption that probabilities of orthogonal outcomes sum. Additivity is itself a probabilistic postulate, underivable from non-contextuality and normalisation alone. Every derivation assumes probability to derive probability.

## The Observer in Every Standard Derivation

Beyond additivity, each derivation presupposes an observer. Decoherence selects a preferred basis without selecting a single definite outcome. The transition from "probabilities over outcomes" to "this outcome actually occurred" is the step each derivation presupposes and none explains. Whether this actuality requires phenomenal consciousness is a further question the Map answers affirmatively; the structural point holds independently.

**Gleason's theorem** (1957) proves non-contextual probability measures on Hilbert-space projections must take the Born-rule form — but says nothing about why measurements yield definite outcomes.

**Deutsch-Wallace** (1999, 2010) shows rational Everettian agents must assign Born-rule credences, but the rationality axioms are substantive philosophical claims that critics argue smuggle in the conclusion. Carroll-Sebens (2014) self-locating uncertainty requires the Epistemic Separability Principle — itself a substantive assumption.

**Zurek's envariance** (2005) appeals to symmetries of entangled states. Schlosshauer-Fine (2005) showed the auxiliary assumption extending from equal to unequal amplitudes is strong enough to yield the Born rule directly; Máttyus (2023) showed envariance does not necessitate Born's rule for all measurements.

**QBism** (DeBrota-Fuchs-Schack 2021) reframes the Born rule as a normative coherence constraint on agent credences, making the observer central by design. The [pragmatist family](/topics/pragmatist-quantum-foundations-and-the-agent/) reading treats this normativity as the structural surface of a dualist interface QBism declines to posit.

**Masanes-Galley-Müller** (2019) derives the Born rule from quantum postulates plus finite-dimensionality, but operational axioms presuppose definite outcomes, relocating observer-dependence without eliminating it.

The pattern is uniform: every derivation presupposes that measurements yield definite, actual results — the step neither unitary evolution nor decoherence provides.

## Beyond Standard Derivations: The Interpretation-Invariant Pattern

In Bohmian mechanics, the Born rule becomes a theorem conditional on the quantum equilibrium hypothesis (positions distributed as |ψ|²). Dürr-Goldstein-Zanghì (1992) argue from typicality, but typicality is measured using |ψ|² itself. Valentini's (1991) dynamical relaxation avoids circularity but cannot explain why the universe began near equilibrium, and no cosmological signatures of primordial non-equilibrium have been observed.

GRW/CSL models calibrate stochastic noise terms to a GKSL master equation, simultaneously guaranteeing norm conservation, Born statistics, and no superluminal signalling. Parameters are chosen *because* they reproduce the Born rule — design, not derivation.

The transactional interpretation (Cramer 1986; Kastner 2012) derives |ψ|² from "handshakes" between offer and confirmation waves, but the derivation is arithmetically trivial once the confirmation wave is defined as ψ*.

Categorical quantum mechanics (Abramsky-Coecke 2004; Agrawal-Wilson 2025) proves Born is the *unique* probability assignment consistent with framework axioms — but the framework already contains measurement and probability structure. The Diósi-Penrose gravitational model explains *when* collapse occurs, not *which* outcome with *which* probability.

| Framework | Born Rule Status | What It Assumes |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Copenhagen | Postulate | Measurement axiom |
| Many-worlds | Decision-theoretic "derivation" | Rationality axioms, branching indifference |
| Bohmian | Theorem (conditional) | Quantum equilibrium (measured by |ψ|²) |
| GRW/CSL | Built into dynamics | Noise calibrated to Born statistics |
| Transactional | Product of offer × confirmation | Confirmation wave defined as ψ* |
| Categorical | Structural consequence | Framework includes effects and probability |
| Diósi-Penrose | Partial (timescales only) | Decay-rate interpretation of energy gaps |

Every framework either postulates the Born rule, assumes it in disguised form, or relocates it. None generates it from non-probabilistic, observer-independent premises.

## Absorbing the Urgleichung

DeBrota, Fuchs, and Schack (2021) reformulate the Born rule as the urgleichung on symmetric informationally complete POVMs:

> Q(D_j) = (d+1) Σ_i P(H_i) P(D_j | H_i) − 1/d

Classical total probability requires coefficient 1 and offset 0; the quantum rule requires (d+1) and −1/d. The deformation is the structural residue, with magnitude set by dimension d and form set by SIC geometry — features holding whether probabilities are read as credences, propensities, or frequencies.

Two features resist purely mathematical-realist redescription. First, the deformation is *irreducibly counterfactual*: the unperformed reference SIC functions as the informational ground against which actual outcomes are specified — the structural signature of a gap being crossed. Second, the (d+1)/−1/d form falls out of SIC geometry, but the realist explains this only by deferral. The interface reading offers independent motivation — the deformation measures the non-classicality of outcome actualisation where consciousness encounters d-dimensional indeterminacy. A Hilbert-space primitivist can accept the theorem and refuse the meaning; on that reading, the Map's absorption is heuristic rather than derivational.

## The Causal Consistency Constraint

The [causal consistency constraint](/concepts/causal-consistency-constraint/) (Torres Alegre 2025) shows that within generalised probabilistic theories satisfying purification, the Born rule is the *only* probability assignment consistent with no-signalling. Nonlinear deviations would enable superluminal communication via quantum steering. The result assumes probabilistic structure and derives the rule's *form*, not its existence.

For the interface interpretation, this is illuminating. If consciousness modulates quantum outcomes, it *must* follow the Born rule — any deviation would enable superluminal signalling, inconsistent with [minimal interaction](/tenets/#minimal-quantum-interaction). The Born rule is what relativistic causality *requires* of any agent — physical or non-physical — that participates in measurement outcomes.

The rule's form is determined by causal consistency; its existence — the fact that one outcome actualises — is what physics cannot generate. This is the partial progress [the interface specification problem](/topics/the-interface-problem/#what-would-count-as-progress) identifies as tractable. Two qualifications temper the picture: causal-consistency has been tested in photon, atom, qubit, and high-energy regimes — not brain-internal tissue — so the corridor binds wherever standard postulates hold rather than universally; and it binds only *interface* theories, leaving the [trumping alternative](/concepts/trumping-preemption/) untouched.

## Which Consciousness-Physics Proposals Require Born Violation {#corridor-taxonomy}

Three families emerge:

**Corridor dualism (Born-rule-preserving).** Consciousness influences which branch is actualised on a single measurement event, but the influence averages to exactly |⟨φ|ψ⟩|² over the ensemble. No statistical signature appears in long runs. This is the Map's current working hypothesis for minimum-detectable interaction. Sympathetic readings of Stapp (without Zeno), various interface authors, and corridor-readings of pragmatist-family positions sit here.

**Minimum-outside-the-corridor dualism (Born-rule-bending).** The corridor is insufficient to produce the observed correlation between conscious intention and physical outcome, so Minimal Quantum Interaction — read as "smallest interaction actually sufficient" rather than "smallest preserving ensemble statistics" — requires deviation:

- *Stapp's quantum Zeno.* Rapid conscious attention holds a particular eigenstate, shifting selection probabilities away from non-conscious measurement outcomes.
- *Chalmers-McQueen Φ-dependent collapse.* IIT Φ gates collapse dynamics; Chalmers-McQueen (2022) note no single scalar Φ suffices, sharpening the Born-modifying implication.
- *Penrose-Hameroff Orch-OR.* Explicitly a modification of QM, not selection within it.
- *Arana (2025) time-extended weighting.* A time-integrated functional perturbs Born probabilities by exponentially suppressed factor λ, recovering standard Born as λ → 0.
- *Kauffman-Radin-Stapp acausal actualisation.* Mind converts possibles into actuals; entails departures from measurement-independent probability distributions.

**Trumping dualism (orthogonal to Born).** The [trumping reading](/concepts/trumping-preemption/) treats consciousness as authoritative over trajectories the formalism already describes, leaving the Born rule outside the gap analysis. See [trumping-preemption](/concepts/trumping-preemption/) and [delegatory-causation](/concepts/delegatory-causation/).

A fourth family — no consciousness-physics interaction (Everettian, Bohmian, orthodox pragmatism) — rejects the premise and lies outside the Map's tenet commitments.

## Active Experimental Programmes

Three strands are alive in 2025-2026. None isolates Born-rule violation as the measured variable.

**Brain-internal quantum coherence witnesses.** Kerskens and Pérez (2022) applied entanglement-witness MRI logic to proton spins in brain water, detecting signals correlated with short-term memory and sleep-wake transitions. Babcock-Hameroff (2025) review room-temperature microtubule quantum effects and what they describe as "direct biophysical evidence of a macroscopic entangled state in the living human brain." Hagan-Hameroff-Tuszyński (2002) estimated microtubule decoherence times up to ~10⁻⁵ seconds — seven orders of magnitude longer than Tegmark's (2000) estimate. Keppler (2025) proposes cortical microcolumns couple to the electromagnetic zero-point field via glutamate-mediated resonance. A negative outcome — short coherence times or small scales — would foreclose Penrose-Hameroff and Keppler programmes and weaken (not kill) Born-violating proposals requiring a quantum-rich substrate. These witnesses detect coherence, not Born deviation.

**Brain-to-quantum-system coupling.** The Nirvanic "Spark of Life" experiment (Gildert 2025) couples a robot's sensory-action loop to a quantum computer's qubits, testing whether a quantum-controlled agent behaves statistically differently from a classical one. Framing is panpsychist, but the design is the closest operational analogue to a "does consciousness bias quantum outcomes?" test. A positive signal would provide first direct operational evidence; a negative signal is less decisive since the device lacks a biological brain.

**Legacy micro-psychokinesis.** Bösch-Steinkamp-Boller (2006) meta-analysed 380 intention-to-RNG studies, finding a small positive effect but with extreme heterogeneity and patterns compatible with publication bias. Maier-Dechamps (2018), using preregistered Bayesian methodology, reported strong evidence for the null. The 227-participant multi-lab PEAR replication (~2 million trials) failed to reproduce the original 7σ signal. Gross intention-driven Born-rule violation of the PEAR variety is on thin empirical ground; smaller, brain-internal, exponentially-suppressed Arana-λ deviations remain unforeclosed, as does corridor dualism (which predicts no PEAR-style signature by construction).

**Collapse-theory parameter bounds.** [Spontaneous collapse models](/concepts/spontaneous-collapse-theories/) add nonlinear stochastic terms. Diósi-Penrose in its simplest form is ruled out by germanium-detector data (2021). GRW and CSL survive at original parameter values after LISA Pathfinder and X-ray detector bounds (SEP 2024). Chalmers-McQueen-style Φ-coupling shifts parameter space into less-constrained brain-specific regions — an advantage for the theorist, also a warning about unfalsifiability drift.

## Why Underivability Matters for Consciousness-Collapse

If the Born rule could be derived from physics alone — with no reference to observers — there would be no conceptual space for consciousness at the measurement interface. That it *cannot*, despite a century of effort across every framework, is abductive evidence that it encodes something the formalism does not contain. On the [interface reading](/concepts/quantum-probability-consciousness/), the Born rule describes how consciousness actualises one possibility among many — [without injecting energy](/concepts/conservation-laws-and-mental-causation/). The [consciousness-physics-interface-formalism](/concepts/consciousness-physics-interface-formalism/) establishes Born preservation as the first of five non-negotiable constraints on any corridor-compliant coupling, and the [delegation-selection bridge](/topics/delegation-meets-quantum-selection/) identifies the Born distribution with Saad's "default causal profile" in [causal delegation](/concepts/delegatory-causation/). The [trumping reading](/concepts/trumping-preemption/) interprets the same underivability differently: what physics cannot generate is not a selection mechanism but consciousness's *authority* over trajectories the formalism already describes.

Masanes-Galley-Müller (2019) showed the Born rule is simultaneously unique and underivable — precisely what a consciousness-interface interpretation predicts: mathematical structure determines the rule's *form*, while observer-dependence reflects its *meaning*. Tonetto locates the meeting point: quantum mechanics provides "statistical closure with outcome-level openness." The [completeness analysis](/topics/completeness-in-physics-under-dualism/) sharpens this — physics is structurally complete yet ontologically partial, and that partiality is exactly the Born rule gap. Additivity's irreducibility constrains how consciousness encounters mutually exclusive possibilities — one outcome experienced, others excluded; [phenomenal non-compositionality](/concepts/phenomenal-non-compositionality/) reinforces this. The presupposition of definite outcomes in every derivation reflects that an interface specification necessarily involves both sides.

## What the Map's Minimal Quantum Interaction Commits To

The Minimal Quantum Interaction tenet, strictly read, privileges whichever minimum is *actually sufficient*. The corridor is the Map's working hypothesis because it is most conservative, not because it is definitionally required.

Three attitudes remain in tension:

1. **Corridor dualism** is maximally compatible with existing Born tests and the 2025 GPT-steering derivation, at the cost of making the single-event mental-to-physical channel ensemble-invisible — risking subtle epiphenomenalism-at-the-ensemble-level unless trumping is adopted.

2. **Minimum-outside-the-corridor dualism** makes the Map empirically distinguishable from physicalism — an Arana-λ, Stapp-Zeno rate, Chalmers-McQueen Φ-strength, or Penrose OR timescale — losing nothing tenet-critical.

3. **Trumping dualism** makes the Born rule orthogonal to the consciousness question; Born tests neither support nor threaten the Map's dualism.

The empirical state cannot adjudicate, since it cannot probe the brain-internal regime at required precision.

## What This Does Not Establish

Three caveats apply. First, absence of a derivation is not proof none exists — the argument is abductive. Second, consciousness-collapse is not uniquely supported by the underivability pattern alone; Bohmian mechanics, GRW/CSL, and retrocausal interpretations each relocate the burden. Third, the Map's position does not predict specific deviations from the Born rule — it reinterprets what the rule means without changing what it predicts.

## What Negative Results Would and Would Not Foreclose

A hypothetical clean null on all experimental strands — no brain-internal coherence, no Nirvanic quantum-agent signal, continued preregistered micro-PK nulls — would foreclose Orch-OR and theories requiring macroscopic biological quantum coherence; PEAR-variant micro-PK; Keppler's zero-point-field interface; and minimum-outside-corridor models predicting effects at current sensitivity.

It would not foreclose corridor dualism (predicts no signature at any sensitivity), Arana-style exponentially-suppressed Born modifications, trumping dualism, or Chalmers-McQueen Φ-collapse with sub-detectable coupling. The Map can survive a clean negative sweep by retreating to corridor or trumping readings. What it cannot do — without dishonesty — is pretend existing Born tests have already validated the corridor over minimum-outside-corridor alternatives. They have not, and given their regime, they could not. The companion piece [brain-internal-born-rule-testing](/topics/brain-internal-born-rule-testing/) lays out, mechanism by mechanism, what *plural and convergent* foreclosure would actually look like — and why no single null forecloses the strict selection-only reading by construction.

## Relation to Site Perspective

**[Dualism](/tenets/#dualism)**: That no physical framework generates the Born rule without presupposing observers or probability supports the claim that consciousness contributes something ontologically irreducible to measurement. The rule marks the [structural-ontological gap](/topics/completeness-in-physics-under-dualism/) where physics is complete about structure yet silent on actuality. The genuinely unprobed brain-internal conscious-selection regime is what opens space for a non-reducible mental channel. If the Born rule were tested inside conscious brains and held to triple-slit precision, one degree of freedom for dualism would close — though trumping and corridor variants would survive.

**[Minimal Quantum Interaction](/tenets/#minimal-quantum-interaction)**: On the default corridor reading, consciousness's influence must follow the Born rule exactly wherever that regime binds. [Phenomenal non-compositionality](/concepts/phenomenal-non-compositionality/) further constrains this: consciousness operates at a unified locus. Trumping-based readings realise MQI without any trajectory-level interaction — an even smaller footprint at the cost of empirical invisibility. The Map's internal taxonomy — corridor-preserving, minimum-outside-corridor, or trumping-orthogonal — is a live structural question rather than a settled commitment.

**[Bidirectional Interaction](/tenets/#bidirectional-interaction)**: On the interface reading, obtaining an outcome is itself an act — consciousness selecting among possibilities — so observer-dependence reflects genuine bidirectional causation. The [trilemma-of-selection](/topics/trilemma-of-selection/) supports this: at any point of genuine indeterminacy, determinism, randomness, and mental causation exhaust the options, and only the third preserves authorship. The [trumping route](/concepts/trumping-preemption/) realises bidirectional causation at the authority layer. A Born-rule-preserving corridor position has to explain how single-event selection adds up to a genuine mental-to-physical channel without leaving an ensemble signature — arguably the Map's sharpest open question, and the reason minimum-outside-corridor readings remain attractive despite their empirical riskiness.

**[No Many Worlds](/tenets/#no-many-worlds)**: MWI must explain not only the Born rule's form but what probability *means* when all outcomes occur. The [probability problem in many worlds](/topics/probability-problem-in-many-worlds/) is the Born rule gap at its widest, and the [cumulative case against MWI](/arguments/many-worlds-argument/) combines it with indexical identity and consciousness objections.

**[Occam's Razor Has Limits](/tenets/#occams-limits)**: The interpretation-invariance of the Born rule gap is evidence that the consciousness-physics boundary is a genuine feature of reality. The temptation to treat "the Born rule has held in every test so far" as universal validity is a case where simplicity is unreliable with incomplete knowledge: the tested regimes are a narrow slice, and treating extrapolation to the brain-internal regime as parsimony disguises an underlying assumption.

The Map's working position is that the empirical question is genuinely open, present experiments cannot adjudicate it, and intellectual honesty requires holding the corridor-vs-minimum-outside-corridor distinction as a live fork rather than a solved problem.
