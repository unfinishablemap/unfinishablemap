Authorship-of-action divergence is the within-species fact that healthy adults vary in their ability to detect when an outcome attributed to their own choice was not the outcome they selected. The choice-blindness paradigm developed by Petter Johansson, Lars Hall, and colleagues is its cleanest laboratory exhibit. Two adults presented with identical manipulations of their own recent choices produce demonstrably different verdicts about authorship—some catch the mismatch concurrently, some catch it retrospectively, and some produce confident, detailed justifications for choices that were never theirs. The spread marks the *outcome-recognition* layer of authorship-tracking—the introspective register at which a mismatch between intended and reported outcome can fail to be flagged—and it is the fourth sub-type catalogued at [source-attribution divergence](/topics/source-attribution-divergence/#the-typology), the sub-type with the highest empirical resolution and the most methodologically loaded scope discipline. The cause-attribution layer (did *I* cause this?) is treated separately under [the agency void](/voids/agency-void/) via Wegner's I-Spy paradigm; the two layers diverge in characteristic ways and are best disentangled from the outset.

This article walks the Johansson et al. 2005 paradigm, develops the detection-rate-variability finding the post-2005 programme established, integrates the sub-type with the broader introspection-architecture cluster, and closes by naming what the case cannot deliver. The discipline applied throughout follows [evidential-status-discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/): the detection-rate-variability finding earns *strongly supported* tier; the interface-failure-of-authorship-tracking reading earns *live hypothesis* tier; neither tier licenses claims the data do not bear.

## The Johansson et al. 2005 Paradigm

In the original study, participants were shown pairs of face photographs and asked which face they found more attractive. After indicating their choice, the experimenter handed them the chosen card and asked them to explain why they had picked it. On a subset of trials—delivered via a sleight-of-hand card swap pre-trained from magic-trick technique—participants received the *unchosen* face instead. The manipulation was detected concurrently on only a minority of trials; even when undetected, participants produced detailed introspective justifications for choices they had not made, matching in length, confidence, and emotional richness those produced for genuine choices.

The original paper's central finding sits at the third-person measurement layer. Subjects "fail to notice mismatches between intention and outcome in a simple decision task" (Johansson et al. 2005). Two empirical signatures are reported: a *non-detection rate* (the proportion of trials on which the swap went undetected) and a *post-hoc justification richness* (the verbal-output measure showing that confabulated justifications were not obviously thinner than authentic ones).

Johansson et al. hedge their theoretical interpretation. The result is consistent with several mechanisms: a default of constructing introspective reports on demand from available cues (consistent with [introspection](/concepts/introspection/) as inferential rather than direct); a failure of online authorship-monitoring under specific paradigm constraints; or some combination of these with confabulatory completion of the report. The 2005 paper does not commit to which mechanism does most of the work, and the follow-up programme has worked to resolve the underdetermination.

## The Post-2005 Programme

The decade-and-a-half following 2005 produced a substantial replication and extension literature whose findings refine the original result in specific ways. Three threads are worth marking explicitly.

**Concurrent vs. retrospective detection.** Detection rates depend sensitively on when the subject is given the opportunity to flag a mismatch. Concurrent detection—catching the swap at the moment of being handed the substituted card—is lower than retrospective detection, where subjects given more time, or asked targeted questions, recover the mismatch at higher rates (Hall et al. 2010; Hall, Johansson, and Strandberg 2012). The timing-sensitivity is methodologically load-bearing: the original non-detection rate is not a fixed feature of the introspective architecture but a property of the specific online-detection window the paradigm constructs. Authorship-monitoring may have access that goes unused under default conditions.

**Within-condition spread.** Detection rates vary across subjects within a single condition. What drives the spread is unsettled: the Maastricht choice-blindness group has shown that memory impairment is not sufficient to produce choice blindness, so differences in memory capacity do not by themselves account for who detects the swap, leaving attention and metacognition among the candidates the data do not yet adjudicate (Sagana, Sauerland, and Merckelbach 2014). The [cluster's independence-scoring page](/topics/introspection-architecture-independence-scoring/) treats the spread itself as one of its despite-commitments anchors: standard rationality-and-choice models did not predict that detection would vary at all, and the bare existence of the spread has reached *strongly supported* tier through cross-laboratory replication, even though the predictors of an individual's position within it remain contested.

**Pupillometric corroboration.** Grassi et al. (2025) recorded pupil-dilation signatures distinguishing manipulated (swapped) trials from control trials at the autonomic level, with the pupil registering the manipulation even on trials where the verbal report showed no awareness of mismatch. *Something* in the subject's processing has registered the mismatch the verbal channel has not—evidence the authors read as covert detection rather than an outright failure to detect. The [pupillometric behavioural channel](/topics/pupillometry-behavioural-channel/) article develops this as a channel-level dissociation between bodily and verbal registers. The result sits at *contested but real* register: the autonomic dissociation is a recent finding whose interpretation—covert detection the subject declines to report, versus a lower-level surprise signal that never reaches report-eligibility—remains live.

The composite picture: the original non-detection rate is robust and substantially modulable by paradigm parameters; a within-condition spread exists but its predictors are not yet settled, with memory capacity shown to be insufficient on its own to account for it; bodily signals can register mismatches the verbal channel does not flag. The findings collectively sit at *strongly supported* tier; particular interpretations of what causes the non-detection sit at *contested but real* with multiple live readings.

## The Kane Anchor

Robert Kane's libertarian-architecture framework asks for empirical anchoring at the layer where consciousness's contribution to action would have to register. In Kane's *Self-Forming Actions* programme (Kane 1996; Kane 2024), the agent is genuinely torn between incompatible options and makes effort toward each; the choice is undetermined and the agent is "ultimately responsible" for which option wins. The framework is metaphysical in scope, and Kane notes that its empirical anchoring is a matter for further research.

Authorship-of-action divergence is the introspective signature most directly relevant to that anchoring requirement. If consciousness contributes to which option wins among genuinely open alternatives, the agent's authorship-tracking machinery is the most plausible introspective interface to that contribution. The choice-blindness paradigm probes this interface and finds it sometimes silent, sometimes mistaken, and sometimes confabulating—features the framework does not predict but is not falsified by, since the framework concerns the metaphysics of selection rather than the reliability of the introspective interface that reports on it. The post-2005 programme suggests, but does not establish, that the introspective interface operates at a different layer from the underlying selection machinery—a conclusion that fits the Map's broader [phenomenal-output / causal-machinery dissociation](/apex/phenomenal-output-causal-machinery-dissociation/) reading but is consistent with rival readings. The pairing is mutual constraint, not mutual support.

## Sibling Paradigm: Wegner's I-Spy

[Wegner's I-Spy paradigm](/voids/agency-void/#five-layers-of-the-verification-void) (Wegner and Wheatley 1999; Wegner 2002) operates on a different attribution layer. Where Johansson and Hall manipulate the *outcome* of a choice and probe whether the agent detects the mismatch, Wegner manipulates the *cause* of an action and probes whether the agent claims authorship. In I-Spy, two participants jointly control a cursor; when the subject hears words priming a target object shortly before the cursor lands on it, the subject reports a stronger sense of having authored the landing—even when a scripted confederate executed it.

The two paradigms address adjacent but distinct features. I-Spy targets the *cause-attribution* layer (did I cause this?). Choice-blindness targets the *outcome-recognition* layer (did this outcome match what I chose?). Both find the introspective layer fallible in characteristic ways; both produce population spreads rather than uniform rates. The Map treats them as the principal exhibits of authorship-tracking fallibility, with Wegner's paradigm handled at the agency void's verification-layer discussion and Johansson and Hall's paradigm handled here as the outcome-recognition exhibit.

A clarification at the Wegner side: Wegner treats the experience of conscious will as illusory in the strong sense that it does not track any underlying mental causation at all. The Map declines this strong reading. Under [Bidirectional Interaction](/tenets/#bidirectional-interaction), a fallible authorship-tracking interface can coexist with genuine consciousness-side causal influence; the interface getting things wrong sometimes does not show there is no underlying contribution to track. The Map argues against the strong illusory interpretation from broader grounds, not from the choice-blindness data.

## Detection-Rate Variability as the Sub-Cluster's Cleanest Single Exhibit

Three features make detection-rate variability the cluster's cleanest single exhibit of within-species architectural variation surfacing at the population-spread layer.

First, the divergence is not a self-reported phenomenal divergence. The introspective verdict is what it is, confident-or-uncertain in the moment; discovery of one's own non-detection arrives from outside via the revealed manipulation. This is structurally parallel to the discovery pattern at [aphantasia](/topics/aphantasia/) and [synaesthesia](/topics/synaesthesia/): most subjects on the spectrum do not discover their position from inside. The choice-blindness case differs in that the discovery does not change the phenomenology—subjects update their factual beliefs about what happened, but their introspective architecture does not become available for inspection in the way an aphantasic's missing imagery is.

Second, the spread is not reducible to any single measured faculty: memory impairment has been shown insufficient on its own to account for it, while timing-sensitivity (concurrent vs. retrospective) and candidate attentional and metacognitive contributors remain under investigation. The spread's resistance to a single-faculty explanation distinguishes authorship-of-action divergence from cleaner single-faculty spreads and supplies one of the cluster's more demanding datasets for the constrain-vs-establish discipline.

Third, the third-person measurement layer—detection rate, post-hoc justification richness, pupillometric signature—gives the case methodological tractability that pure phenomenal-divergence cases lack. The cluster's empirical anchoring at this sub-type does not depend on subjects' first-order phenomenal reports about their authorship-tracking machinery (which the case itself suggests may be unreliable). It depends on the dissociation between what subjects report and what they did, measured externally. The dataset's methodological independence from the very capacity it probes is what makes detection-rate variability the sub-cluster's cleanest exhibit.

## What Authorship-of-Action Divergence Cannot Deliver

Honest scope demands explicit statement of what the case does not deliver. Three boundaries are load-bearing.

**The data do not discriminate between interface-failure and brain-on-its-own confabulation.** Two readings remain live. On the interface-failure reading, the introspective architecture is reporting on a real authorship-tracking process whose interface to verbal report is fallible in specific architectural ways. On the brain-on-its-own confabulation reading, there is no authorship-tracking process distinct from the report-production machinery; the brain produces reports by inference from available cues, and choice-blindness shows the inference machinery operating on impoverished cues. The discriminating-test design space is active—the [independence-scoring page](/topics/introspection-architecture-independence-scoring/) catalogues candidate discriminators—but the case does not by itself decide between them. The Map's interface reading earns its place from cluster-level convergence rather than from authorship-of-action divergence in isolation.

**The data do not establish dualism.** Authorship-of-action divergence is compatible with materialist accounts in which introspective architecture is a brain-internal report-production system whose fallibility is exactly what those accounts predict. Under dualism, the case sits naturally; it does not by itself license dualism over rivals.

**The data do not refute Kane's framework, but neither do they confirm it.** The paradigm probes the introspective interface to authorship, not the underlying selection machinery Kane posits. Interface fallibility is consistent with the framework being right about the underlying architecture and also with its being wrong. The case is mutual-constraint territory, not confirmation.

## Relation to Site Perspective

**[Dualism](/tenets/#dualism).** The relevant register is architectural anomaly rather than direct argument. The cluster's cumulative weight against the simplest functional-identity reading of phenomenology contributes to the broader case the Map develops elsewhere; the present article does not run the wedge in isolation. The empirical signature falls where the Map's framework expects: at the layer where consciousness's contribution to action would have to register, the interface shows characteristic fallibility rather than uniform accurate readout. The interface-failure-of-authorship-tracking reading is at *live hypothesis* tier per [evidential-status-discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/) and is not upgraded by the present case alone.

**[Bidirectional Interaction](/tenets/#bidirectional-interaction).** Authorship-of-action is the structural feature of consciousness-to-brain causation the Map's agency-related articles handle most directly. Under bidirectional interaction, consciousness contributes to action through—on the Map's interface conjecture—selection among quantum-indeterminate outcomes biased by attentional focus. The choice-blindness finding does not establish this mechanism but is consistent with it: a fallible authorship-tracking interface can coexist with genuine consciousness-side causal contribution.

**[Occam's Razor Has Limits](/tenets/#occams-limits).** The simplest model of authorship-tracking—"I know what I chose because I chose it"—is empirically falsified across the cohort, in a paradigm that required careful methodological design to expose. Occam's preference for the direct-readout model would have ruled this out *a priori*; it took the Johansson-Hall result to crack the assumption open.
