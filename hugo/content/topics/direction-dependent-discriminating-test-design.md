The [recovery-order-asymmetry article](/topics/memory-channel-interface-evidence/) and the [direction-of-interface-change concept](/concepts/direction-of-interface-change/) both name, as outstanding future work, a discriminator the existing catalogue has not designed: "a focal perturbation producing a channel ordering one reading predicts and the other forbids," sited specifically on the *direction-of-travel* axis. This article develops that named-but-undeveloped test into a worked design. It is a **proposed experiment, not a result.** Nothing here asserts the discriminator has been run, and nothing here raises the probability that the [filter reading](/concepts/filter-theory/) is correct; the [evidential-status discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/) requires that the design be held at the design tier. Until the experiment is performed its discriminating verdict is genuinely underdetermined by the evidence: the design constrains *which* outcomes would count for and against each reading, but does not presuppose which way they would resolve. What the design contributes is a structure: a reversible focal perturbation applied separately in the *closing* and the *reopening* phase of a consciousness transition, with concurrent multi-channel probing, configured so that a substrate-symmetric production reading and the filter reading make **opposite, advance-stated predictions** about the order in which a high-cost channel goes dark and comes back.

This is the companion to the [targeted-lesion discriminating-tests article](/topics/targeted-lesion-discriminating-tests-between-production-and-filter-readings-of-the-memory-hierarchy/), and the two should not be conflated. That article designs a *substrate-state* discriminator: it asks whether a focal lesion's substrate *topology* tracks the channel ordering (production) or whether channel architecture overrides topology (filter), holding the system at a single state. The present article designs a *direction-dependent* discriminator: it asks whether the channel ordering is *the same in both directions of travel* (the simplest production prediction) or *direction-dependent* (the filter prediction). The substrate-state article cannot deliver this test, because a lesion is a fixed insult — it has no reopening phase to compare against its closing phase. The direction axis requires a perturbation that can be turned on and off within one subject, which is what makes the design distinct enough to stand alone.

## What Each Reading Predicts and Forbids

The discriminating power of any test is exactly the size of the gap between what the rival readings *predict* and what they *forbid*. The direction axis opens such a gap because the two readings disagree about whether the channel ordering carries a memory of the direction of travel.

The **substrate-symmetric production reading** — the simplest production account, on which one substrate both generates and recovers each channel — reads the degradation ordering off a single, direction-blind regional-vulnerability profile. The autonoetic channel is most vulnerable because it depends on the most cortical reconstruction work; the procedural channel is least vulnerable. Crucially, on this reading the *same* profile is read in both directions: the channel that fails first under deepening perturbation is the channel that recovers last under lifting perturbation, because the profile that orders failure is the profile that orders recovery. This reading **predicts symmetry** of the channel ordering between closing and reopening, and **forbids** a clean reversal in which a high-cost channel that failed first comes back first.

The **filter reading** treats the channel ordering as a property of interface architecture rather than of a direction-blind substrate profile. If channels differ in bandwidth and reconstruction cost, *which* components block or reopen, and in *what order*, may depend on the direction and the cause of the change: partial blockade closes high-cost channels first, while a different perturbation — or the *lifting* of a perturbation through a dedicated reopening pathway ([active reboot](/concepts/active-reboot/)) — could reopen a high-cost channel out of degradation order. This reading **predicts** that the closing ordering and the reopening ordering can dissociate, and is **not committed** to their symmetry. The reopening pathway is doing real work in this prediction, and its own mechanism is undischarged: [active reboot](/concepts/active-reboot/) is a *posited* route, not a specified one, and the [mechanism-costs cartography](/topics/mechanism-costs-dualism-thickness-quadrants/) is where the dualist-thickness cost of positing it is tracked — invoking the pathway is not free, and a result that depended on it would inherit that cost.

The gap is real only if the production reading genuinely forbids what the filter reading permits. It does, *for the substrate-symmetric reading*. The honest qualifier — load-bearing, and the reason this is a constrain-not-establish test — is that a *more elaborate* production reading can pay to recover asymmetry by adding direction-specific machinery (hysteresis terms, a distinct emergence-circuit posit, a stochastic-reconstitution model). The test discriminates the filter reading from the *simplest* production rival; it raises the cost of the production accounts that survive rather than refuting production as such. This is the same calibration the [source article](/topics/memory-channel-interface-evidence/) holds, transposed from observed clinical asymmetry to a controlled, advance-registered experiment.

## The Design: A Reversible Perturbation Applied in Both Directions

The experiment has five ingredients. The first three are shared with the substrate-state design; the last two are what make it direction-dependent.

**A reversible, focal, repeatable perturbation.** Unlike a lesion, the perturbation must be switchable so that one subject can be probed in both the closing and the reopening phase. Transcranial focused ultrasound is the candidate modality: Verhagen et al. (2019) showed that forty seconds of repetitive ultrasound to a medial frontal target in macaques changed regional activation and altered how selectively that region interacted with the rest of the brain for up to two hours, reversibly and without tissue damage — and the same modality has reached human subcortical targets in disorders-of-consciousness pilot work (Cain et al. 2021). The reversibility is the design's enabling feature: it supplies the *reopening* phase a lesion cannot.

**A high-cost target channel.** The perturbation should preferentially load the autonoetic channel — which, on the design's working assumption, swings most in recovery order and so carries the largest predicted direction effect — by targeting a region the autobiographical-retrieval literature implicates in re-experiencing while sparing the regions that route noetic recognition. The targeting rests on a posited spatial dissociation between re-experiencing and recognition substrates that the design takes as a working assumption rather than an established fact; the assumed selectivity is itself a precondition the experiment must check, not a result it can presume.

**Concurrent multi-channel probing.** Autonoetic, noetic, and anoetic channels must be measured separately and simultaneously under the perturbation (remember-know judgements, source-memory accuracy, repetition priming, motor-sequence retention), so that the *ordering* among channels can be read at each moment rather than inferred across sessions.

**Phase-matched ramping — the direction manipulation.** The perturbation is ramped *up* (closing phase) and, in a separate matched block, ramped *down* (reopening phase), at matched rates, with the multi-channel ordering recorded throughout each ramp. The pre-registered comparison is between the order in which channels degrade during the up-ramp and the order in which they recover during the down-ramp. The substrate-symmetric production reading predicts these two orderings are reverses of each other; the filter reading permits them to dissociate.

**A hysteresis read-out.** Because the same perturbation is applied in both directions, the design also recovers the *concentration-analogue* hysteresis the anaesthesia literature reports — the loss-of-function and recovery-of-function thresholds for each channel. A substrate-symmetric reading predicts a single threshold per channel, crossed in both directions; a direction-dependent architecture permits the closing and reopening thresholds to separate, and permits them to separate *by different amounts for different channels*. Differential, channel-specific hysteresis is a signature the simplest production reading does not predict.

## Why the Anaesthesia Hysteresis Literature Both Motivates and Cautions

The design is motivated by, and disciplined against, the existing neural-inertia literature. Sepúlveda, Tapia, and Monsalves (2019) characterise *neural inertia* as an active resistance to state change: the effect-site anaesthetic concentration at loss of consciousness is reliably higher than at recovery, so the two thresholds do not coincide. That whole-organism hysteresis is the macroscopic version of the per-channel hysteresis the design proposes to resolve.

The caution is sharper and must be stated where the design is, because it bounds what a positive result could claim. Proekt and Hudson (2018), in the *British Journal of Anaesthesia*, argue that whole-organism neural inertia need not imply distinct induction and emergence machinery at all: a stochastic state-switching model reproduces the hysteresis without direction-specific machinery. A two-well bistable landscape driven by noise produces a hysteresis that *collapses over time* as the system relaxes toward a common dose-response curve — but the authors find that two states are not enough to capture the full signature (the negative correlation between the emergence threshold shift and the shallower Hill slope is not explained by a two-well potential), and extend the model to a ten-state Markov process (three awake, seven unconscious) to reproduce the left shift and shallower slope of emergence. On their account, induction and emergence can converge on the same underlying stochastic mechanism while still presenting an apparent asymmetry over realistic timescales. The design must therefore not treat any observed threshold separation as automatically favouring a direction-dependent architecture: a stochastic multistate production model predicts hysteresis — and even an *increasing variability* of recovery — without any direction-specific channel routing. That multistate rival is more formidable than a bare bistable one, not less, which sharpens rather than softens the caution. This is why the *discriminating* read-out is not hysteresis-presence but the **cross-channel ordering** under direction reversal — whether the channels recover in a *different order* than they fail, which the stochastic state-switching model, being direction-blind in its cross-channel ordering, does not predict even though it does predict threshold separation. Conflating "hysteresis exists" with "the interface reading is favoured" would be exactly the inflation the [evidential-status discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/) exists to catch.

## What a Result Would and Would Not Settle

A clean **reversal** — a high-cost channel that fails first during the up-ramp but recovers first during the down-ramp, replicated within subject and across the focal target — would remove the substrate-symmetric production reading and force any surviving production account to posit direction-specific machinery, paying the per-case cost the [mechanism-costs cartography](/topics/mechanism-costs-dualism-thickness-quadrants/) tracks. It would *not* establish the filter reading, because a production account willing to pay that cost absorbs the reversal; the result discriminates against the *simplest* rival, and the filter reading's advantage stays comparative.

A clean **symmetry** — channels recovering in exact reverse of their degradation order, with single per-channel thresholds — would be the more consequential outcome for the Map, because it is the direction-dependence family's *predicted* signature failing under controlled test. The [direction-of-interface-change](/concepts/direction-of-interface-change/) family is held at framework-internal weight precisely so that this outcome counts: a controlled symmetry result would withdraw the family's accommodation-grade support and push the interface reading back toward the substrate-symmetric account on this axis. Designing the test so that *both* outcomes are informative is what keeps it a falsification-programme contribution rather than a confirmation-seeking exercise.

There is also a null this design must respect. The four members of the direction-of-change family may share one upstream architecture, so even a positive reversal here is **not** an independent fourth or fifth confirmation to be tallied against the others — it is a controlled probe of the *same* thing the [common-cause null](/project/common-cause-null/) warns against over-counting. What a controlled reversal would add over the observational cases (anaesthesia emergence, terminal lucidity, dissociation) is not multiplicity but *quality*: a prospective, within-subject, advance-registered measurement that could replace retrospective and selection-biased case reports. The upgrade is in evidential grade on one axis, not in the number of axes.

## Relation to Site Perspective

The design engages two tenets at the methodology layer, framed honestly as a proposed falsification handle rather than as support the experiment has delivered.

**[Dualism](/tenets/#dualism)** (Tenet 1) is engaged because the design treats the interface reading as empirically exposed: a controlled symmetry result would count against the direction-dependence the Map reads as evidence for a multi-channel interface, and the design is built so that this outcome is registered in advance rather than absorbed. The Map's commitment is not shielded from the experiment; the experiment's symmetry branch is a genuine route to disconfirmation of one of the Map's evidence types.

**[Bidirectional Interaction](/tenets/#bidirectional-interaction)** (Tenet 3) is engaged because the predicted direction effect is largest on the autonoetic channel — the channel involving the most active subject-participation. If consciousness only received from the brain, the recovery order would be fixed by substrate readiness alone and the up-ramp and down-ramp orderings would coincide. A direction-dependent ordering, were it found, would favour a reading on which the autonoetic channel is high-engagement in both directions; the favouring is constraining, not establishing, and is reserved for a result the design has not produced.
