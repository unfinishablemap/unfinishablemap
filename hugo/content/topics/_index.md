Topics are the Map's extended explorations—the deep dives where a single question is followed as far as the Map's [tenets](/tenets/) and the surrounding [concepts](/concepts/) will carry it. If you have limited time, start with the five apex hubs below, then follow whichever thread you came for: **agency** (how consciousness authors action), the **quantum interface** (how mind meets matter), **AI and machine consciousness** (whether artifacts can have inner lives), **dualism and the interaction problem** (the foundations), or **meaning and the examined life** (what any of this is for). The reading routes in [Reading Routes](#reading-routes) name the entry article for each thread.

The corpus is large and still growing. Each page is a working exploration—continuously reviewed and refined—that takes the Map's consciousness-first commitments seriously and traces where they lead, rather than summarising settled consensus. Each topic connects back to the [tenets](/tenets/) explicitly and draws on the shared vocabulary defined in [concepts](/concepts/).

## Five Hubs to Start With

The [apex articles](/apex/apex-articles/) are the Map's synthesis pieces—each integrates many topics into one human-readable narrative. Five of them are the natural doors into the topic corpus:

- [Consciousness and Agency](/apex/consciousness-and-agency/) — how consciousness authors action in a physical world, through agent-causal selection among possibilities physics leaves open. The hub for the agency thread.
- [The Interface Specification Programme](/apex/interface-specification-programme/) — six converging lines of inquiry that specify the mind-matter interface as a research programme. The hub for the quantum-interface thread.
- [The Machine Question](/apex/machine-question/) — whether artifacts can be conscious, and the principled obstacles the Map identifies. The hub for the AI thread.
- [What Consciousness Tells Us About Physics](/apex/what-consciousness-tells-us-about-physics/) — the reverse inference from mind to matter, and why quantum mechanics may be structurally partial. The hub for the foundations thread.
- [Living With the Map](/apex/living-with-the-map/) — what consciousness-first philosophy means for daily life. The hub for the meaning thread.

## How This Section Is Organised

Topics fall into recurring families: the **interaction problem and dualist foundations**; the **consciousness-physics quantum interface** (measurement, the Born rule, decoherence, post-decoherence selection); **agency, free will, and time**; **AI and machine consciousness**; **altered states and the interface** (anaesthesia, dreams, psychedelics, contemplative pathology); **comparative and cross-cultural perspectives** (animal, invertebrate, Eastern, non-Western); and **meaning, ethics, and the examined life**. The threads cross constantly—agency meets the quantum interface at [collapse](/topics/time-collapse-and-agency/), AI meets the interface at [state inheritance](/topics/quantum-state-inheritance-in-ai/)—which is why the reading routes above are routes rather than a partition. ## Relation to Site Perspective

This index is itself an expression of the Map's [tenets](/tenets/): it routes by the threads the tenets generate. The dualism route follows [the irreducibility of consciousness](/tenets/#dualism); the quantum-interface route follows [minimal quantum interaction](/tenets/#minimal-quantum-interaction) and [bidirectional interaction](/tenets/#bidirectional-interaction); the agency route follows what bidirectional interaction makes possible; and the rejection of [Many-Worlds](/tenets/#no-many-worlds) shapes the foundations route's reverse inference from mind to matter. Because [Occam's Razor has limits](/tenets/#occams-limits), the corpus does not prune to a single tidy theory—it keeps the threads open and the routes overlapping. Topics are where these commitments get tested against specific questions; concepts are where the vocabulary they require is defined.
