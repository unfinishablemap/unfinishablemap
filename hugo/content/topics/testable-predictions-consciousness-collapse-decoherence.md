Consciousness-collapse — the idea descended from the [von-neumann-wigner-interpretation](/concepts/von-neumann-wigner-interpretation/) that conscious observation plays a role in quantum state reduction — is not one theory but a family of frameworks, each making distinct empirical predictions. Most discussions treat these variants interchangeably, obscuring the fact that some generate concrete experimental signatures while others remain empirically silent. This catalogue maps the specific points where consciousness-collapse frameworks diverge from standard decoherence — and from each other — identifying what to look for and what each outcome would mean for The Unfinishable Map's [tenets](/tenets/).

The central challenge is that [decoherence](/concepts/decoherence/) and collapse produce identical measurement statistics in nearly all accessible experiments. Both agree on interference suppression, preferred-basis selection, and the appearance of classicality. The divergences emerge only in specific regimes: high-mass superpositions, consciousness-correlated collapse rates, and logical constraints on observer-dependent outcomes.

## The Baseline: What Decoherence Alone Predicts

Standard decoherence makes three commitments relevant to distinguishing it from collapse:

1. **No excess decoherence.** All loss of coherence traces to identifiable environmental interactions — gas collisions, photon scattering, black-body radiation. Remove the environment, and superposition persists indefinitely regardless of system mass.

2. **No mass-quadratic scaling.** Environmental decoherence rates depend on the specific coupling between system and environment, not on mass alone. The scaling laws follow from the interaction Hamiltonian, not from any intrinsic collapse mechanism.

3. **Improper mixtures persist.** After decoherence, the joint system-environment state retains quantum correlations. The reduced state of the system *appears* mixed but remains entangled with the environment — a state Schlosshauer (2004) calls an "improper mixture," distinct from the "proper mixture" that genuine collapse would produce.

Any observation violating these commitments would constitute evidence for collapse. The frameworks below predict such violations, but they disagree on when, where, and why they occur.

## Phi-Correlated Collapse Rates (Chalmers-McQueen)

The most developed consciousness-collapse framework combines [Integrated Information Theory](/concepts/integrated-information-theory/) (IIT) with Continuous Spontaneous Localisation (CSL) dynamics. Chalmers and McQueen (2021) predict that collapse rates depend on the *qualitative structure of consciousness* in the measuring system — specifically, on Q-shapes (IIT 4.0's characterisation of phenomenal experience) rather than on physical parameters alone.

**What to look for:** Two quantum systems with identical mass, temperature, and environmental coupling but different information-integration architectures should exhibit different decoherence rates. A quantum computer implementing a circuit with high integrated feedback (high Phi) should lose coherence faster than a physically matched circuit with modular, feedforward architecture (low Phi).

**What a positive result means:** Physical parameters alone cannot explain the collapse rate — something about the system's information structure matters. This would directly support the Map's [Bidirectional Interaction](/tenets/#bidirectional-interaction) tenet: an aspect of the system related to consciousness causally influences physical outcomes.

**What a negative result means:** If Q-shape-matched experiments show identical collapse dynamics regardless of information architecture, the IIT-CSL version of consciousness-collapse is directly disconfirmed. The Map would need to either abandon Q-shape-collapse or argue that IIT misidentifies the relevant consciousness measure — preserving the hypothesis but losing its only quantitative prediction.

**Current status:** Untested. Two obstacles stand in the way. First, computing Q-shapes is intractable beyond toy models because Phi computation scales super-exponentially with system size. Second, no quantum computer experiment has been designed to test this. The prediction is genuine — the theory could be wrong — but the experiment is not yet feasible.

## Phi-Threshold Collapse (Kremnizer-Ranchin)

Kremnizer and Ranchin (2015) constructed the first formal CSL variant where the collapse operator depends on integrated information. Their model is simpler than Chalmers-McQueen's: systems exceeding a Phi threshold undergo spontaneous localisation, while systems below it remain in superposition.

**What to look for:** Superpositions of states with *different* Phi values should collapse faster than superpositions of states with *equal* Phi values. The collapse operator is sensitive to Phi *distance*, not merely Phi magnitude.

**What a positive result means:** Collapse is driven by consciousness-related properties, not mass or environmental coupling alone. The Phi-threshold model directly implements the Map's [Minimal Quantum Interaction](/tenets/#minimal-quantum-interaction) tenet: consciousness does not trigger collapse everywhere, only where integrated information is sufficient.

**What a negative result means:** If equal-Phi and unequal-Phi superpositions show identical dynamics, the Phi-dependent collapse operator is wrong.

**A known limitation:** Chalmers and McQueen identified the *equal-Phi problem* — a scalar Phi threshold would allow superpositions of *distinct* conscious states with equal Phi to persist indefinitely, which is phenomenologically unacceptable. This motivates the richer Q-shape extension above.

**Current status:** Untested. Faces the same Phi-computability barrier as the Chalmers-McQueen prediction.

## Mass-Quadratic Excess Decoherence (Objective Collapse)

Objective collapse models (GRW, CSL) predict two signatures absent in standard quantum mechanics:

1. **Mass-quadratic scaling.** Collapse-induced decoherence grows as the square of the system's mass, independent of environmental coupling (Bassi, Dorato & Ulbricht, 2023).

2. **Saturation with superposition size.** CSL-induced decoherence rates plateau as the spatial separation of the superposition increases, unlike environmental decoherence which continues to grow.

Horchani (2025) developed a Bayesian protocol using levitated dielectric nanospheres in Schrödinger-cat states, with a unified master equation that accounts for gas collisions, black-body radiation, and photon-recoil noise. This transforms the test from indirect parameter-bounding to direct hypothesis comparison.

**What to look for:** Excess decoherence beyond what calibrated environmental models predict, with the characteristic mass-quadratic and saturation signatures.

**What a positive result means:** Collapse is a real physical process — a necessary but not sufficient condition for consciousness-collapse. The Map can then argue that this collapse process is consciousness-modulated, as the IIT-CSL framework proposes.

**What a negative result means:** If no excess decoherence appears at any tested mass scale, the physical framework that consciousness-collapse requires is progressively undermined. This is a genuine vulnerability for the Map's position.

**Current status:** Proposed but not executed. The current matter-wave interferometry record stands at approximately 25,000 amu (Fein et al., 2019). Standard CSL parameters predict observable effects only at 10^9 to 10^11 amu — a gap that the proposed MAQRO space mission would bridge.

## Anomalous Brownian Heating (GRW/CSL)

Spontaneous collapse models predict that isolated particles experience anomalous momentum diffusion — a random "heating" effect from the stochastic collapse noise field. Standard quantum mechanics predicts no such effect in isolated systems.

**What to look for:** Anomalous temperature increase in ultra-cold, well-isolated systems — levitated nanoparticles, cold atom clouds, or gravitational wave detectors.

**What a positive result means:** Same as mass-quadratic excess decoherence: collapse is real. The heating rate would constrain the collapse parameters (lambda and rC), narrowing the viable parameter space.

**What a negative result means:** Upper bounds on the collapse rate tighten. Adler's enhanced rate (lambda approximately 10^-8 per second) is already approaching experimental bounds of approximately 10^-9 per second from spontaneous heating measurements. Searches for spontaneous radiation — a related signature where collapse noise excites bound electrons — provide complementary constraints (Curceanu et al., 2022).

**Current status:** Progressive experimental bounds are tightening. No anomalous heating detected, but the standard GRW/CSL parameter space remains viable. Carlesso and Donadi (2025) provide the most comprehensive current review of experimental constraints.

## Consciousness-Dependent Collapse Timing

This prediction connects directly to [the broader relationship between quantum measurement and consciousness](/topics/quantum-measurement-and-consciousness/). If consciousness modulates collapse, collapse *timing* should correlate with when a conscious observer becomes entangled with the measurement result — not when the physical detector registers it. Objective collapse models predict timing fixed by the detector's mass and physical properties.

**What to look for:** A delayed-choice experiment where a conscious observer's engagement with the measurement apparatus is varied while physical conditions are held constant. Collapse timing should shift with observer engagement.

**What a positive result means:** Consciousness is causally implicated in determining *when* collapse occurs — the strongest possible evidence for the Map's [Bidirectional Interaction](/tenets/#bidirectional-interaction) tenet.

**What a negative result means:** Collapse timing is fixed by physical parameters alone, undermining consciousness-dependent versions while leaving objective collapse viable.

**Current status:** Requires quantum memories maintaining coherence through macroscopic measurement chains — technology that does not currently exist.

## Anaesthesia-Modulated Collapse Dynamics

If consciousness participates in collapse, pharmacologically suppressing consciousness should alter collapse dynamics in neural tissue. Under general anaesthesia, neurons continue firing and metabolism continues, but consciousness is absent or diminished.

**What to look for:** Measurable differences in quantum decoherence rates or collapse dynamics in neural tissue under anaesthesia compared to waking states, after controlling for the physical changes anaesthesia introduces.

**What a positive result means:** Removing consciousness changes collapse dynamics — direct evidence for consciousness-collapse.

**What a negative result means:** Ambiguous. Anaesthesia alters brain physics sufficiently that attributing any difference to absent consciousness (rather than changed physical conditions) faces what [the testing landscape article](/topics/testing-consciousness-collapse/) calls the "confound of embodiment."

**Current status:** No experiment has been attempted. Different anaesthetic agents disrupt different aspects of consciousness — propofol eliminates phenomenal presence while ketamine preserves some experience — potentially allowing finer-grained discrimination (see [anaesthesia-and-the-consciousness-interface](/topics/anaesthesia-and-the-consciousness-interface/)).

## The Frauchiger-Renner Logical Test

Extended Wigner's friend scenarios provide a logical rather than empirical test. Frauchiger and Renner (2018) showed that universal quantum mechanics plus definite conscious experiences produces contradictions. Proietti et al. (2019) confirmed these tensions experimentally: a six-photon experiment violated a Bell-type inequality derived from the Wigner's friend scenario by five standard deviations.

**What this constrains:** The result forces a choice among several options. Many-worlds resolves the contradiction by denying single definite outcomes. [QBism](/concepts/qbism/) resolves it by denying that quantum states are objective. [Relational quantum mechanics](/concepts/relational-quantum-mechanics/) resolves it by making facts observer-relative. Consciousness-collapse resolves it by denying that quantum mechanics applies universally to conscious systems. The logical argument narrows viable positions without selecting among them empirically.

**Why this matters for the Map:** The Frauchiger-Renner result is evidence that the measurement problem is real and pressing — universal quantum mechanics cannot consistently describe observers modelling each other. The Map argues this is because consciousness genuinely collapses superpositions, making conscious systems exceptions to universal quantum evolution.

The table reveals a structural pattern: predictions that test consciousness specifically (column 4) are all either untested or conceptual, while predictions that test collapse generally have active experimental programmes. This is not coincidence — it reflects the difficulty of isolating consciousness as an independent variable.

## Relation to Site Perspective

The Map's position requires collapse to be real *and* consciousness-dependent. These are separable claims with separable evidence.

The [Minimal Quantum Interaction](/tenets/#minimal-quantum-interaction) tenet predicts that consciousness-dependent effects will be small — the smallest deviation from standard quantum mechanics that permits mind-matter causation. This explains why no differential prediction has been confirmed, but it also constrains which positive results the Map can claim: a large, dramatic consciousness-dependent effect would conflict with minimality.

The [Bidirectional Interaction](/tenets/#bidirectional-interaction) tenet is directly at stake. If Tier 1 experiments (mass-quadratic scaling, anomalous heating) progressively eliminate all collapse models, the physical substrate the Map's framework depends on would be undermined. If Tier 2 experiments (Phi-correlated rates, consciousness-dependent timing) show no consciousness-specific effects, bidirectional interaction loses its proposed mechanism. Both vulnerabilities are genuine.

The [No Many Worlds](/tenets/#no-many-worlds) tenet is load-bearing for this entire analysis. Many-worlds dissolves the distinction between consciousness-collapse and decoherence-only by denying that collapse occurs at all. Every prediction catalogued here presupposes that collapse is real.

The [Occam's Razor Has Limits](/tenets/#occams-limits) tenet addresses the objection that decoherence-only is simpler and should be preferred absent consciousness-specific evidence. The Map acknowledges that practical untestability is an epistemic cost — but argues that if the difficulty of testing reflects the same structural feature that generates the [hard problem](/topics/hard-problem-of-consciousness/), parsimony cannot dissolve it by fiat. The Map's [testability-ledger](/project/testability-ledger/) catalogues broader conditions under which the framework would be disconfirmed, ensuring these commitments remain accountable.
