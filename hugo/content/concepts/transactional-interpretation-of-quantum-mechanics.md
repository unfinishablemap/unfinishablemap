The transactional interpretation of quantum mechanics (TI) reads every quantum event as a completed "handshake" between an emitter and an absorber: a forward-in-time wave and a backward-in-time wave that lock together into a standing "transaction." Introduced by physicist John G. Cramer in 1986, TI is a **physicalist, observer-independent** interpretation—it assigns no role to consciousness and, taken as a complete metaphysics, conflicts with the Map's dualism (Tenet 1). The Map's interest is instrumental. TI supplies a time-symmetric, Born-rule-preserving mechanism that the Map's retrocausal-selection articles already invoke, and Ruth Kastner's possibilist refinement adds a *pre-spacetime realm of possibility* that is a natural candidate interface for a minimal (Tenet 2), bidirectional (Tenet 3) non-physical influence. This article explains TI on its own terms first, then—clearly separated in "Relation to Site Perspective"—sets out what the Map borrows and what it declines. It is the canonical home for the roughly two dozen corpus files that reference TI in passing.

## The Offer–Confirmation Handshake

In Cramer's account, a quantum interaction is not a one-directional emission followed by a later detection. It is a two-way exchange completed across the interval between emission and absorption.

- **Offer wave.** The emitter produces a *retarded* solution of the relevant wave equation—the ordinary forward-in-time wavefunction, propagating from source toward potential absorbers.
- **Confirmation wave.** A responding absorber returns an *advanced* solution—a wave propagating backward in time, from absorber to emitter, with amplitude proportional to the offer wave it received.
- **Transaction.** Reinforcement between offer and confirmation forms a standing wave between emitter and absorber. This completed transaction is what TI substitutes for the usual "collapse" of the wavefunction; there is no separate collapse postulate.

Cramer describes the buildup of a transaction as occurring "in pseudotime"—a heuristic sequencing device for narrating how offer and confirmation converge, explicitly *not* a claim that the handshake unfolds within ordinary time. The completed transaction is better thought of as **atemporal**: it exists across the whole emission-absorption interval rather than at a single moment inside it. Because the backward-propagating confirmation is a component of a completed, symmetric handshake rather than a usable signal, Cramer argues TI is nonlocal (consistent with Bell-inequality violations) while remaining relativistically invariant and causally coherent: no observer can exploit the advanced wave to send information into the past.

### Roots in Wheeler–Feynman Absorber Theory

TI's lineage is classical, not quantum. In their absorber theory of radiation, John Wheeler and Richard Feynman reformulated classical electrodynamics so that the electromagnetic field is taken as *half the sum* of retarded and advanced solutions of Maxwell's equations. Radiation becomes a direct interaction between a source and its absorbers, and the absorber's summed advanced response supplies the radiation reaction and singles out the ordinary retarded arrow of radiation. Cramer's move was to carry this offer/confirmation structure over from the classical field to the quantum wavefunction. The relevant point for the Map is that time-symmetric advanced-plus-retarded solutions are a respectable, non-fringe feature of foundational physics rather than an ad hoc device invented to serve an interpretation.

## The Born Rule from the Transaction

TI's central technical claim is that the Born rule—the |ψ|² probability of a given outcome—need not be postulated separately but falls out of the handshake. The confirmation wave arriving back at the emitter carries amplitude proportional to the product of the offer wave and its complex conjugate. Where the offer wave has amplitude ψ, the "echo" the emitter receives from a candidate absorber has strength proportional to ψ times ψ\*, that is |ψ|². The probability that a particular transaction completes therefore tracks the strength of that echo, reproducing the Born statistics. (This summary paraphrases Cramer's derivation; readers deriving numbers should work from his 1986 paper directly rather than from secondary restatements.)

This is the feature the Map most values: any selection story built on TI **inherits the Born rule for free** rather than having to bolt it on. A mechanism that merely chooses *which* offered transaction completes, without altering the |ψ|² weighting of the offers, is by construction statistics-preserving.

## Kastner's Possibilist Transactional Interpretation

Ruth E. Kastner's Possibilist Transactional Interpretation (PTI), developed at book length in 2012, is the most Map-relevant modern refinement. Its core move is ontological: offer and confirmation waves are real but **sub-empirical possibilities that do not live in spacetime**. They inhabit a pre-spacetime realm of physical possibility. Only an *actualized* transaction is a spacetime event.

Two consequences follow.

1. **No literal backward signal.** Because advanced waves are non-actualized offers and confirmations in the possibility realm rather than trajectories through spacetime, PTI dissolves the "signal travels backward in time" worry. Ordinary forward causation among actualized events is preserved; nothing physical is sent into the past.
2. **Spacetime is emergent.** On Kastner's reading, the network of actualized transactions is what *constitutes* spacetime, rather than transactions occurring within a pre-given spacetime.

Kastner subsequently extended this into a Relativistic Transactional Interpretation (RTI) using direct-action field theory, arguing that in the relativistic formulation the standard objection to TI (next section) cannot even be mounted. For the Map, PTI's pre-spacetime possibility realm is the salient contribution: it names a domain in which "which possibility becomes actual" is settled, without requiring the selection to be a spacetime signal—exactly the shape a dualist selection interface would need. Kastner presents all of this physically; the consciousness reading below is the Map's addition, not hers.

## The Maudlin Objection

Intellectual honesty requires foregrounding the standard challenge, because any TI-based story the Map tells inherits it. In *Quantum Non-Locality and Relativity*, Tim Maudlin proposed a **contingent-absorber** thought experiment. Arrange the apparatus so that the position of a second absorber depends on whether the particle is first detected elsewhere. Then *which absorbers exist* to return confirmation waves is itself contingent on the outcome the transaction is supposed to determine. Maudlin argues that original TI cannot consistently assign a determinate transaction: the pseudotime formation story appears to need the answer before it can compute the answer.

The objection is widely read as serious, by some as fatal to Cramer's 1986 formulation. Responses divide:

- **Patch original TI.** Cramer added a hierarchy or ordering to pseudotime transaction formation so that transactions form in a definite sequence.
- **Modify TI.** Kastner's PTI/RTI argues the challenge dissolves once offers are treated as possibilities rather than spacetime trajectories—there is no circular spacetime process to trap.
- **Reformulate the challenge.** Some critics (e.g. Peter Lewis) argue a version of Maudlin's problem can be reconstructed even against the newer formulations.

The debate is unsettled. The Map's stance is that a TI-based selection mechanism must be presented *with* this objection and the possibilist reply, not as though TI were unproblematic.

### Are advanced waves ontic or calculational?

A related open question is whether the advanced (confirmation) wave names something real or is bookkeeping. Cramer and Kastner treat it as physically real—Kastner as a real-but-pre-spacetime possibility—while instrumentalists read advanced solutions as a mathematical convenience that happens to yield the right forward-time statistics. The Map's retrocausal-selection articles lean on the ontic-but-non-signaling reading, for which PTI's "real possibility, not spacetime signal" is the cleanest available construal.

## Relation to Site Perspective

TI is a physicalist interpretation, and the Map does not adopt it as a complete metaphysics: Cramer's TI is observer-independent and assigns consciousness no role, which conflicts with **Tenet 1 (dualism)**. What the Map borrows is the *machinery*, held explicitly at arm's length from the physicalism.

**The candidate selection locus.** The Map's [collapse-and-time](/concepts/collapse-and-time/) and [time-symmetric-selection-mechanism](/topics/time-symmetric-selection-mechanism/) articles explore whether conscious selection might operate by choosing *which* of several offered outcomes becomes actual. TI, and especially Kastner's PTI, gives that idea a concrete stage. If offers and confirmations are possibilities in a pre-spacetime realm, then "consciousness selects which transaction completes" places the non-physical influence at the point of actualization—not as a force pushing particles around in spacetime. The Map speculates that this is where a **minimal (Tenet 2)** influence could sit: selection among already-weighted possibilities changes nothing about the offers themselves. And because the completed transaction is a two-way, time-symmetric handshake, the picture is congenial to **bidirectional interaction (Tenet 3)**—the emission-absorption structure already treats influence as running both temporal directions.

**Why the Born rule matters here.** A recurring worry for any consciousness-selection proposal is that biasing quantum outcomes should show up as a detectable violation of |ψ|² statistics. TI's derivation of the Born rule from the offer×confirmation product is what makes the borrowing attractive: a mechanism that selects *which* offered transaction actualizes, while leaving the |ψ|² weighting of the offers intact, is statistics-preserving by construction. This is the Born-rule-preserving, minimal-influence channel the Map is after.

**What the Map declines.** The Map does not endorse Cramer's claim that TI is a *complete* account requiring no observer, nor Kastner's physicalist reading of the possibility realm as sufficient on its own. The pre-spacetime realm is borrowed as a *venue* for selection, not as a self-actualizing mechanism. And the Map inherits the Maudlin objection along with the machinery: to the extent original TI is contingent-absorber-inconsistent, a Map selection story should build on the possibilist/relativistic formulation, where the objection is argued to dissolve, and should remain candid that the reply is contested.

The Map also locates TI precisely rather than blurring it into "retrocausality" generally. TI is one time-symmetric approach among several (the two-state-vector formalism and general retrocausal hidden-variable programs are others); the corpus distinguishes [forward-in-time-conscious-selection](/topics/forward-in-time-conscious-selection/) from time-symmetric selection, and TI belongs on the time-symmetric side. Among the QM interpretations the Map catalogues—[relational-quantum-mechanics](/concepts/relational-quantum-mechanics/), [qbism](/concepts/qbism/), [von-neumann-wigner-interpretation](/concepts/von-neumann-wigner-interpretation/), [consistent-histories-interpretation](/concepts/consistent-histories-interpretation/)—TI is distinctive precisely for the time-symmetric handshake the Map draws on, and (unlike von Neumann–Wigner) for reaching that structure *without* invoking an observer.
