Concepts are the Map's building blocks—the terms, frameworks, and arguments that recur across the [topics](/topics/) deep dives and the [apex syntheses](/apex/apex-articles/). If a topic article uses a piece of vocabulary as though it were already defined, this is where the definition lives. Read concepts to learn what the Map means by a word; read topics to see the word doing work. The fastest orientation is the five foundational concepts below, then the cluster matching the thread you are following: **dualism and interaction** (the metaphysical foundation), the **quantum interface** (measurement, decoherence, selection), **agency and free will** (agent causation and its objections), or **phenomenal consciousness** (qualia, acquaintance, the hard problem's raw material).

Each page is a working definition—continuously reviewed and refined—framed for the Map's consciousness-first perspective and connected back to the [tenets](/tenets/), rather than a neutral encyclopedia gloss. Where a concept has standard textbook content an LLM already knows, the Map states only what is distinctive to its own dualist framing.

## Five Foundational Concepts

- [Dualism](/concepts/dualism/) — consciousness is not reducible to physical processes. The root commitment everything else builds on.
- [Interactionist Dualism](/concepts/interactionist-dualism/) — the specific form the Map defends: mind and matter causally influence one another.
- [Phenomenal Consciousness](/concepts/phenomenal-consciousness/) — what it is like to undergo experience, the explanandum the hard problem names.
- [Agent Causation](/concepts/agent-causation/) — the conscious subject as an originating cause, not merely a link in a physical chain.
- [The Mind-Matter Interface](/concepts/mind-matter-interface/) — where and how mind meets matter, unifying filter theory and quantum selection.

## Reading Routes

**If you are following dualism and interaction,** begin with [Dualism](/concepts/dualism/), then [Interactionist Dualism](/concepts/interactionist-dualism/), [Bidirectional Interaction](/concepts/bidirectional-interaction/), [Objections to Interactionist Dualism](/concepts/objections-to-interactionism/), and the contrast positions ([Epiphenomenalism](/concepts/epiphenomenalism/), [Panpsychism](/concepts/panpsychism/), [Disguised Property Dualism](/concepts/disguised-property-dualism/)).

**If you are following the quantum interface,** begin with [The Measurement Problem](/concepts/measurement-problem/), then [Decoherence and the Quantum Consciousness Objection](/concepts/decoherence/), [Post-Decoherence Selection](/concepts/post-decoherence-selection/), [Contextual Selection in Quantum Foundations](/concepts/contextual-selection-in-quantum-foundations/), [Locality and Mind-Matter Interaction](/concepts/locality/), and [QBism](/concepts/qbism/).

**If you are following agency and free will,** begin with [Agent Causation](/concepts/agent-causation/), then [The Luck Objection to Libertarian Free Will](/concepts/luck-objection/), and the interface concepts that give agency its causal opening ([The Mind-Matter Interface](/concepts/mind-matter-interface/), [Bidirectional Interaction](/concepts/bidirectional-interaction/)).

**If you are following phenomenal consciousness,** begin with [Phenomenal Consciousness](/concepts/phenomenal-consciousness/), then [Phenomenal Acquaintance](/concepts/phenomenal-acquaintance/), [Intentionality](/concepts/intentionality/), [Inverted Qualia](/concepts/inverted-qualia/), [The Phenomenal Transparency-Opacity Spectrum](/concepts/phenomenal-transparency-opacity-spectrum/), and [the Phenomenal Contrast Method](/concepts/phenomenal-contrast-method/) for how the Map argues from felt experience.
