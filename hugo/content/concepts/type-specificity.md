**Type-specificity** is the structural feature an explanandum has when its character is determinate type-by-type rather than schematic. A reductive account of a type-specific explanandum must deliver *that* character — the particular shape of *this* type — rather than substitute a generic mechanism. Where the explanandum is type-specific and the proposed reduction is not, the appeal to a generic mechanism does not discharge the explanatory demand; it changes the subject.

The concept is methodological, not metaphysical. It does not by itself entail that any particular reduction must fail. It supplies a discipline for evaluating reductive proposals: ask whether the explanans matches the *form* of the explanandum, not merely its magnitude or its functional signature. Where the forms diverge, the proposal owes either a derivation of the form from the explanans or an honest acknowledgement that the generic move does not address the type-specific demand. The Map deploys type-specificity at three grains — metaphysical, phenomenal, and functional — each as an independent exhibit of the same structural pattern.

## The Structural Feature {#structural-feature}

A type-specific explanandum has *internal structure that individuates it from other members of its family*. Redness is one phenomenal type, painfulness is another, the taste of coffee is a third. Each is determinate in its character: the redness of red is not the painfulness of pain merely shifted along some intensity dimension. A reductive account that locates redness in some neural firing pattern but is silent on why *that* pattern realises *that* phenomenal type — rather than another type, or no type at all — has not explained redness. It has identified a correlate.

The contrast is with explananda whose specification is *schematic* — that is, where the explanandum names a role or a placeholder without committing to particular determinate features that the explanation must match. "Vital force" was such a placeholder. It did not pick out any particular feature a reduction had to deliver in any particular form. Any mechanism for any part of the target counted as success: Wöhler's urea synthesis (1828) reduced one part, Buchner's cell-free fermentation (1897) reduced another, the DNA structure (1953) reduced a third. Each reduction shrank the placeholder, because the placeholder had no determinate form a reduction was required to preserve.

Type-specificity is what the placeholder *lacks*. Where the explanandum is "this particular phenomenal type, in its determinate character," not "whatever it is that makes the organism alive," a reduction by generic substitution is structurally unavailable.

## The Vitalism Disanalogy {#vitalism}

The original deployment is in [the convergence argument for dualism](/topics/the-convergence-argument-for-dualism/): type-specificity is what distinguishes the consciousness case from past reductive successes, including the case of vitalism.

A vitalist in 1820 could have observed accurately that the chemistry-life gap had not yet narrowed. The temporal observation — *no reduction has yet been found* — is symmetric across consciousness and vitalism. The disanalogy is not temporal. It is structural, and it lies in the shape of the explanandum:

> "'Vital force' was a placeholder for unspecified mechanism, picking out no particular feature a reduction had to deliver in any particular form... Phenomenal qualities have a different shape: redness, painfulness, and the taste of coffee are each determinate phenomenal types. A reductive account must deliver *that* character type-by-type, and a complete reduction of one type would not extend automatically to others." ([the-convergence-argument-for-dualism](/topics/the-convergence-argument-for-dualism/), §What Convergence Does Not Prove)

This is the metaphysical-grain deployment. The disanalogy does not claim that phenomenal qualities are *unreducible* — that would be the convergence argument's conclusion, and using it here would be circular. The disanalogy claims something weaker and earlier: that the *form of the demand* placed on any reduction is type-specific, in a way the vitalist's demand was not. The historical pattern of reductive successes — fermentation, heredity, metabolism — does not extend automatically across this structural divide.

The disanalogy is not unlimited. Type-specific reductive demands could in principle be met. A future neuroscience that delivered "this is what redness *is* in physical terms" analogous to "this is what fermentation *is*" would discharge the demand at one type, and the discipline of [reductionism-and-consciousness](/topics/reductionism-and-consciousness/) would have to consider whether the discharge extends. Type-specificity names a structural feature of the explanandum, not a verdict on whether the explanandum can be addressed.

## Type-Specificity at the Unity Grain {#unity-grain}

The phenomenal-grain deployment is in [the binding problem](/topics/the-binding-problem/). The article catalogues five varieties of binding — intra-modal, cross-modal, temporal, cognitive, subject — and observes that each presents the same shared structure: physical processes are distributed, neural mechanisms coordinate them, experience is unified, and the gap between coordination and unity resists explanation.

The structural argument is type-specific. Each variety presents *its own* unity-type: the unity of a perceived scene is not the unity of a remembered episode shifted in modality, and neither is the unity of a self that owns both. Phenomenal unity at the perceptual grain, temporal unity at the diachronic grain, and subject unity at the ownership grain are determinate types whose character a reduction must match.

The article makes the type-specific demand explicit:

> "Any successful explanation must work across all five varieties: a theory that explains perceptual binding but cannot address temporal or subject binding has explained BP1, not BP2." ([the-binding-problem](/topics/the-binding-problem/), §The Shared Structure)

The persistence of the gap across all five varieties is the load-bearing observation. The persistence is what would not be expected if a generic mechanism were available, because a generic mechanism — synchrony, integration, broadcast — would discharge all five demands at once by addressing whatever they had in common. The varieties do not have a common type-shape; they have a common *structural pattern* (coordination-to-unity) at five distinct grains. A generic mechanism for the pattern does not address any of the five types as such.

This is the same discipline as the vitalism disanalogy, applied at the phenomenal grain. The shape of the demand — type-by-type across five varieties — is what places the constraint on what would count as a solution, derivable from the form of the explanandum rather than from any verdict about whether the explanandum is irreducible.

## Type-Specificity at the Capacity Grain {#capacity-grain}

The functional-grain deployment is in [baseline cognition](/concepts/baseline-cognition/). The article catalogues consciousness-dependent functional capacities — durable working-memory maintenance, novel combinations of operations, declarative metacognition, counterfactual thinking, recursive mindreading, cumulative culture — and argues that the human-ape gap systematically tracks these specific capacities rather than tracking cognition generically.

The argument is type-specific in the same structural sense. Durable maintenance is a different capacity-type from novel combinations, which is a different capacity-type from declarative metacognition. Each is individuated by its determinate character — what it *is* to hold an item across an interval, what it *is* to chain procedures novelly, what it *is* to represent a knowledge state as a knowledge state. A theory that explained the human-ape gap as a generic increase in *cognitive resource* — more memory, more computation, more associations — would be a generic-mechanism appeal at the capacity grain. It would not address why the gap tracks *these* capacities and not others equally resource-intensive.

The baseline-cognition framework's central inference is structurally type-specific: the systematic correspondence between consciousness-dependent capacities and the human-ape gap is itself the explanandum. A generic resource-expansion account cannot deliver the correspondence because it does not predict the type-by-type pattern; it predicts a generalised performance shift. The pattern is what would be expected if consciousness amplifies cognition through *specific* operations (durable maintenance, novel combinations, spontaneous intentional action) rather than through a generic boost.

The discipline operates here in the same way as at the other two grains: the form of the explanandum (capacity-by-capacity correspondence) constrains what would count as an explanans (a capacity-by-capacity mechanism), and a generic-mechanism appeal at the wrong grain does not discharge the demand.

## What Type-Specificity Does and Does Not Do {#scope}

Type-specificity is a methodological discipline, not a metaphysical conclusion. It does the following work:

- **Identifies the form-of-demand a reductive account must satisfy.** Where the explanandum is type-specific, a reduction must address the determinate character of each type rather than substituting a generic mechanism. This is a constraint derivable from the explanandum's structure, independently of any verdict on whether the constraint can be met.

- **Distinguishes consciousness from past reductive successes structurally.** Vitalism's "vital force" was schematic; phenomenal qualities are type-specific. The temporal observation that no reduction has yet been found is symmetric across the two cases; the structural observation about the shape of the demand is not.

- **Sets a non-circular constraint on what would count as a solution.** The constraint is "the explanans must match the form of the explanandum." This is derivable from the explanandum alone and does not presuppose the irreducibility conclusion the constraint might be invoked to support.

It does *not* do the following work:

- **Prove irreducibility.** Type-specific reductive demands could in principle be met. A neuroscience that delivered the determinate character of redness in physical terms would discharge the demand at redness; the discipline would then have to consider whether the discharge extends type-by-type or whether the first reduction reveals a generic strategy applicable to the family.

- **Defeat physicalism on its own.** Type-specificity is the structural feature of the explanandum, not an argument that physicalism cannot address the explanandum. It identifies what a successful physicalist account would have to deliver. Whether such an account is forthcoming is a separate question that [the-convergence-argument-for-dualism](/topics/the-convergence-argument-for-dualism/) addresses through the pattern of evidence rather than through type-specificity alone.

- **Stand on irreducibility.** The discipline must not be defended by appeal to "phenomenal qualities are irreducible, therefore the reductive demand is unmeetable." That would be circular: type-specificity is invoked in support of the irreducibility verdict, so it cannot draw its anti-generic-mechanism force from the verdict it helps support. The force comes from the structural correspondence between explanandum-shape and explanans-shape, recognised independently of any reduction's success or failure.

## Relation to Site Perspective {#relation-to-site-perspective}

Type-specificity supports the Map's [Dualism tenet](/tenets/#dualism) in a structurally constrained way. The discipline does not by itself establish that consciousness is irreducible. It does establish that the historical pattern of reductive successes — chemistry of life, atomic theory, molecular genetics — does not extend automatically to consciousness, because the explananda in those cases were schematic in ways phenomenal qualities are not. The convergence argument earns its irreducibility conclusion from the broader pattern of evidence (the hard problem, the explanatory gap, philosophical-zombie conceivability, contemplative convergence, and others); type-specificity is one structural feature that places the convergence's load-bearing observations in a coherent frame.

The discipline aligns with the [Occam's Razor Has Limits tenet](/tenets/#occams-limits). A parsimony-based preference for physicalism rests partly on the assumption that one kind of stuff is simpler than two. Type-specificity reframes the parsimony comparison: where the explanandum is type-specific and the proposed unified explanans does not deliver the type-specific demand, the apparent parsimony is purchased at the cost of an unaddressed explanatory gap. Parsimony as a tiebreaker requires equal explanatory power; type-specificity is one way the explanatory power may not in fact be equal.

The discipline observes the Map's standing constraint against [concession convergence](/concepts/concession-convergence/): when physicalist theories retreat toward positions that respect type-specificity (selectively reducing some phenomenal types while remaining silent on others, or treating type-specific phenomenal facts as primitive within a physicalist framework), the retreat is itself evidence that the type-specificity demand is being recognised, even where the verdict is not granted. The structural feature is doing work whether or not the metaphysical conclusion is drawn.
