Neutral monism holds that reality is built from entities of one fundamental kind that are intrinsically *neither mental nor physical*; minds and material objects are alike constructions out of — or groupings of — these neutral entities. Its classical statements come from Ernst Mach (1886), William James (1904), and Bertrand Russell (1921). From the Map's dualist standpoint it is the third monist rival: [physicalism](/concepts/materialism/) makes matter fundamental, [idealism](/concepts/idealism/) and [cosmopsychism](/concepts/cosmopsychism/) make mind fundamental, and neutral monism makes *both* derivative. It is the rival the Map's [Dualism](/tenets/#dualism) tenet most owes a demarcation against: it does not deny either relatum of the mind–matter relation but claims the relation itself is an artefact of sorting, so the interaction problem the Map answers with a quantum interface never arises. The Map's grounds for rejection ([given below](#relation-to-site-perspective)) centre on the *dualism suspicion* ([explained below](#the-dualism-suspicion)): pressed to say what the neutral base is, the view tends to resolve into covert mentalism, covert physicalism, or a two-aspect position that is dualism under another name.

## The Classical Programme: Mach, James, Russell

The founding trio arrived from different directions — physics, psychology, logic — which is part of the view's historical force; Banks (2014) reads them as one movement, "realistic empiricism."

**Mach.** In *The Analysis of Sensations* (1886), Ernst Mach proposed that the world consists of "elements" — colours, sounds, pressures, and the like. According to Mach, the same element counted in one functional context is physical and in another psychological: a colour considered in its dependence on the light source is a physical object, while considered in its dependence on the retina it is a sensation (Mach 1886, as presented in Stubenberg & Wishon 2023). The ego and the material object are alike provisional groupings of elements; Mach's motive was explicitly theoretical economy — a point the Map returns to under Tenet 5 below.

**James.** In "Does 'Consciousness' Exist?" (1904), William James answered his title question in the negative — consciousness as an entity "is the name of a nonentity, and has no right to a place among first principles" (James 1904: 477). What exists is a function, knowing, performed within a more primitive medium: "My thesis is that if we start with the supposition that there is only one primal stuff or material in the world, a stuff of which everything is composed, and if we call that stuff pure experience, then knowing can easily be explained" (James 1904: 478). On James's account, the same undivided bit of experience counts as a knower in one context of associates and as a thing known in another. The Map engages the earlier, pluralist James in [william-james-consciousness](/topics/william-james-consciousness/); his late drift toward neutral monism is a noted tension for that engagement ([pragmatist-quantum-foundations-and-the-agent](/topics/pragmatist-quantum-foundations-and-the-agent/)).

**Russell.** Russell criticised neutral monism in 1914 before converting around 1918; on the SEP's account he held some version of the view thereafter (Stubenberg & Wishon 2023). *The Analysis of Mind* (1921) is the canonical construction: "The stuff of which the world of our experience is composed is, in my belief, neither mind nor matter, but something more primitive than either" (Russell 1921: 10–11). Russell abandons the act–object analysis of experience — there is no mental *act* of sensing standing over against a sensed *object* — and relocates the mind–matter difference in *causal laws* rather than in stuff: the same sensation belongs to physics as a member of one causal series and to psychology as a member of another. By *The Analysis of Matter* (1927), events replace sensations and physics is said to reveal only the causal skeleton of the world — the structuralist strand that later feeds [Russellian monism](/concepts/russellian-monism/).

## What Does "Neutral" Mean?

The programme's central term is also its softest point. Stubenberg & Wishon (2023) distinguish five competing readings of neutrality: the *Neither View* (the basic entities are intrinsically neither mental nor physical), the *Actual Constituent View* and *Possible Constituent View* (the basic entities do, or could, figure as constituents of both minds and material objects), the *Law View* (the basic entities fall under both mental and physical laws — Russell's 1921 position approximates this), and the *Both View* (the basic entities are both mental and physical). The classical neutral monists favoured the Neither View, and it is the reading on which the position is a genuinely distinct third rival: on the other readings, nothing prevents the basic entities from being intrinsically mental or physical, and the view threatens to collapse into a position it was meant to displace.

Even the classical texts strain against pure neutrality. Russell's 1921 ontology admits images alongside sensations, and images are classed on the mental side only — a standard criticism holds that his neutrality was never complete. James's own candidate base is *pure experience*, a term that wears its apparent mentality on its face. Whether any positive characterisation of the base can avoid tilting toward one side is the question that structures the objections below.

## Descendant, Not Synonym: Russellian Monism

Neutral monism is frequently run together with [Russellian monism](/concepts/russellian-monism/); the SEP treats the latter as a *development from* the former, and the difference matters. Russellian monism posits quiddities — intrinsic natures realising the structure physics describes — and in most of its variants makes those quiddities phenomenal (panpsychism) or protophenomenal. A phenomenal base is precisely *not* neutral. Only its panprotopsychist and panqualityist wings inherit the Neither View and remain neutral monisms in the strict sense. The Map's detailed case against the quiddity programme — the [combination problem](/concepts/combination-problem/), Pautz's parity problem, Cutter's instability argument — lives on the [russellian-monism](/concepts/russellian-monism/) page and in [russellian-monism-versus-bi-aspectual-dualism](/topics/russellian-monism-versus-bi-aspectual-dualism/); this page addresses the older and more radical claim that the mental/physical sorting itself goes all the way down to nothing.

## The Contemporary Revival

After mid-century neglect, neutral monism has returned as an active research programme. Kenneth Sayre (1976) proposed *information* as the neutral base — the proposal the Map's own [information-as-interface view](/concepts/metaphysics-of-information-under-dualism/) treats as its nearest collapse risk. Thomas Nagel (2012) sketched a view on which the fundamental constituents are neither physical nor mental but ground both. Erik Banks (2014) reconstructed the classical trio as a single movement and defended an event-based descendant on which the same natural power manifests as both a neuron firing and a sensation. And David Chalmers's discussions of panprotopsychism and Sam Coleman's *panqualityism* — unexperienced qualities as the base, with awareness constructed from them — carry the neutral option inside the Russellian-monism debate (Chalmers 2013; Mihálik 2022). These respecifications are motivated largely by the oldest objection to the view.

## Where the Programme Strains

**The mentalism suspicion.** Historically the most frequent objection: sensations, pure experience, and percepts look paradigmatically mental, so the "neutral" base is mentalistic and the view is phenomenalism or panpsychism rebranded. The contemporary respecifications — information, events, powers, unexperienced qualities — are all attempts to escape this charge, at the price of making the base more remote from the empirical given that motivated the classical programme.

**The materialism suspicion.** From the other flank: late Russell arguably treats all events as physical, with "neutrality" downgraded to an epistemic thesis about how little physics tells us of intrinsic character. A base that is physical-but-unknown is physicalism with a modest epistemology, and the third rival disappears again.

**The quality/awareness gap.** Chalmers argues that no arrangement of qualities necessitates *awareness of* those qualities: a neutral or qualitative base may supply the materials of experience while leaving the experiencing itself unexplained. Coleman's higher-order-quotational panqualityism is the principal reply; Mihálik (2022) argues the reply still faces an explanatory gap for strong awareness. This is the [hard problem](/topics/hard-problem-of-consciousness/) re-arising inside the programme that promised to dissolve it, and it is structurally parallel to the combination problem the Map presses against panpsychism: constructing minds out of a base that is not already minded is exactly the step that resists articulation; the Strawson-style variant asks whether such construction avoids brute [emergence](/concepts/emergence/).

### The Dualism Suspicion

The objection the Map finds most instructive comes from within the literature itself (Stubenberg & Wishon 2023). Physics, on the neutral monist's own structuralist concession, delivers only extrinsic relations; intrinsic qualities show up only in experience. A view that posits a domain of relational structure *and* a domain of intrinsic quality, neither reducible to the other, has two fundamental aspects however firmly it insists on one stuff — property dualism in monist dress. This is the direct analogue of Cutter's instability argument against Russellian monism, which the corpus already develops: the middle position is not a resting place, and the pressure that pushed the theorist off physicalism pushes on through neutrality toward dualism. The five-readings problem feeds the same conclusion: the readings on which the base is genuinely neutral say least about it, and the readings with positive content collapse into a neighbouring position.

## Relation to Site Perspective

The Map interprets neutral monism as the most serious *structural* rival to its first tenet, and rejects it on three grounds — while conceding its genuine insights.

**Tenet 1 (Dualism).** Neutral monism denies the Map's [first tenet](/tenets/#dualism) head-on: there is no irreducible mental term, only neutral entities grouped two ways. The Map's response runs through the dualism suspicion above. Either the neutral base is given positive content — in which case, the Map holds, that content tips mental (James's pure experience), physical (late Russell's events), or double-aspect (structure plus quality) — or it is kept genuinely neutral by keeping it contentless, in which case the view has purchased its monism by declining to describe its one kind of stuff. The Map's [bi-aspectual dualism](/concepts/bi-aspectual-ontology/) takes the position the instability points toward and states it explicitly: structure and actuality as two irreducible aspects, with the honesty cost of the interaction problem paid openly rather than deferred. The demarcation against Sayre's information-monism is the working example: the Map treats information as a *relation between* two irreducible domains, not a neutral *substance beneath* them ([metaphysics-of-information-under-dualism](/concepts/metaphysics-of-information-under-dualism/)).

**Tenets 2 and 3 (Minimal Quantum Interaction, Bidirectional Interaction).** On the neutral monist construction, causation runs entirely along neutral lines; "mind" names one grouping of those lines, not a causal agent. Russell's own version is explicit that psychology and physics differ only in which causal laws they collect. That leaves consciousness nothing to *do* — the same [causal idleness](/concepts/epiphenomenalism/) the Map presses against other non-interactionist views, here built into the ontology from the start. The Map's [Minimal Quantum Interaction](/tenets/#minimal-quantum-interaction) and [Bidirectional Interaction](/tenets/#bidirectional-interaction) tenets require a mental term with genuine selective work at quantum indeterminacies; neutral monism has no such term to give.

**Tenet 4 (No Many Worlds).** Like Russellian monism, neutral monism is quantum-interpretation-neutral: nothing in the view requires anything to happen at measurement, so collapse-free interpretations remain fully available to it. The Map's [rejection of many-worlds](/tenets/#no-many-worlds) therefore marks a real divergence in physics, not a notational one — the Map's ontology requires selection among outcomes, and neutral monism's does not.

**Tenet 5 (Occam's Razor Has Limits).** Neutral monism's standing appeal is parsimony: one kind of stuff, no interaction problem, no bridging laws. The Map reads the view's history as a case study in its [fifth tenet](/tenets/#occams-limits): the single-stuff economy generated a century of unresolved successor problems — five incompatible readings of neutrality, the construction problem, the quality/awareness gap — so the simplicity was purchased on credit rather than banked. Parsimony under incomplete knowledge is an unreliable guide, and the Map declines to treat "one stuff is simpler than two aspects" as evidence against dualism, for the same reason it declines to wield parsimony *for* dualism.

**What the view gets right.** The Map's rejection is not a dismissal. Neutral monism saw early that physics is structural and silent on intrinsic character — the insight the Map shares with the whole Russellian lineage ([intrinsic-nature-void](/voids/intrinsic-nature-void/)). It refused to eliminate experience, taking the given as a datum rather than an embarrassment. And in the Map's [four-quadrant taxonomy](/topics/four-quadrant-dualism-taxonomy/) neutral monism sits in the min-mind/min-physical corner as a monist limit case that "behaves like deflationary dualism along both axes" — the position a dualist reaches by shaving both sides as thin as they will go. That the most economical monism ends up shaped like a dualism is, the Map suggests, evidence about where the joints of the problem really are.

## What Would Challenge This View?

The Map's assessment would need revision if the neutrality-specification problem were solved — a positive, stable characterisation of a base that provably tips neither mental nor physical — or if the quality/awareness gap were closed, showing how awareness is constructed from an unminded base without brute emergence. Coleman's panqualityism and Banks's power-events are the live candidates; Mihálik (2022) states the current obstacle for the first, and the second sits, by design, close to enhanced physicalism. Either success would also weaken the Map's parallel case against Russellian monism, since the two share the construction step.
