Concession convergence is the argumentative pattern in which multiple physicalist theories of consciousness, developed independently and from different starting assumptions, each arrive at defensive modifications that inadvertently concede the central dualist claim: functional organisation alone cannot account for consciousness. The pattern is significant because isolated concessions might reflect local theoretical difficulties, but when structurally identical retreats appear across competing frameworks, they constitute independent evidence that the difficulty is fundamental rather than theory-specific.

## The Pattern

The structure is consistent across cases. A physicalist theory begins with a broad claim—consciousness is computation, or information integration, or global broadcast, or higher-order representation. The [hard problem](/topics/hard-problem-of-consciousness/) then forces a narrowing move: the theory adds constraints specifying that not just *any* system with the right functional profile is conscious, but only systems with particular physical, architectural, or representational properties. Each narrowing move concedes ground to dualism by admitting that abstract functional description is insufficient. The concessions are not coordinated—the theories are rivals—which makes their convergence evidentially powerful.

## Four Case Studies

### Integrated Information Theory

[IIT](/concepts/integrated-information-theory/) begins by identifying consciousness with integrated information (Φ). But IIT's exclusion postulate restricts consciousness to systems with *maximal* integrated information, ruling out functional duplicates that integrate information in the wrong way. IIT further ties consciousness to *intrinsic causal power*—not all systems with the right functional profile are conscious, only those whose causal structure is irreducible. The theory's identity claim (consciousness *is* Φ-structure) then faces the charge that identity assertions are unexplanatory: asserting that a physical arrangement *is* experience does not explain why that arrangement is accompanied by experience. The [explanatory-gap](/concepts/explanatory-gap/) persists in the form of asking why this particular identity holds.

**The concession**: IIT rejects [substrate-independence](/concepts/substrate-independence/) by tying consciousness to intrinsic causal structure rather than abstract computation. Systems that are functionally identical but causally different can differ in consciousness. This is the [absent qualia](/concepts/functionalism/) intuition—the possibility that a system could be functionally identical to a conscious being yet lack experience—wearing a mathematical hat.

### Global Workspace Theory

[GWT](/concepts/global-workspace-theory/) proposes that consciousness arises when information enters a "global workspace" and is broadcast to multiple cognitive modules. But GWT explicitly addresses only *access consciousness*—the availability of information for reasoning, reporting, and action. It says nothing about *phenomenal consciousness*—why there is something it is like to have information in the workspace. GWT explains when and how information becomes accessible. It remains silent on why accessibility feels like anything at all.

**The concession**: GWT ties consciousness to specific neural architectures (the prefrontal-parietal network) rather than abstract computational properties. The COGITATE results (2025) further suggest that conscious content tracks posterior cortex activity rather than the broadcast network itself—consciousness may access whatever the posterior cortex provides, with the workspace enabling but not constituting that access. GWT is compatible with dualism if read as describing neural correlates rather than constituting consciousness.

### Higher-Order Theories

[Higher-order theories](/concepts/higher-order-theories/) propose that a mental state becomes conscious when represented by a higher-order thought or perception. But the theory faces dissociation evidence: patients and experimental subjects can have phenomenal experience without the higher-order representations the theory requires. The theory explains access to mental states without explaining why access feels like anything. Contemplative traditions report states of awareness without the subject-object structure HOT requires—"pure consciousness" where the observer-observed distinction dissolves.

Brown's (2025) HOROR theory attempts to close this gap by holding that the higher-order state represents the first-order state *as* having qualitative character. But HOROR shifts the explanatory burden rather than discharging it: why does representing a state "as qualitative" produce experience rather than merely representing qualitative content? The phenomenal-access gap reappears one level up.

**The concession**: HOT requires specific representational structures that may not transfer across substrates. The theory's own proponents have retreated from strong versions—Lycan moved from higher-order perception toward attention-based accounts (Lycan 1996, and subsequent revisions). The phenomenal-access separation HOT must draw (between the higher-order representation and the phenomenal character it supposedly creates) replicates the very distinction between functional organisation and experience that dualists identify.

### Biological Computationalism

[Biological computationalism](/concepts/biological-computationalism/) (Milinkovic & Aru 2025) holds that brains compute in a substrate-dependent, metabolically grounded manner fundamentally different from digital computation. Each distinguishing claim—substrate dependence, metabolic grounding, scale inseparability—maps onto a classical argument for dualism. The framework's central thesis, that "the algorithm is the substrate," dissolves the algorithm-substrate distinction in ways the authors may not intend: if knowing the algorithm does not tell you what is being computed in any consciousness-relevant sense, then computation as traditionally understood is not what produces consciousness.

**The concession**: Biological computationalism effectively endorses the absent qualia intuition for digital systems while trying to block it for biological ones. The gap between "any computation" and "only this specific physical process" is precisely the gap dualists have identified.

## Why Convergence Matters

Isolated theoretical difficulties prove little—every theory has problems. Concession convergence gains its force from independence and structural identity. Four features make this pattern evidentially significant:

**Independence.** The theories were developed by different researchers, from different starting assumptions, to solve different aspects of the consciousness problem. IIT begins from phenomenological axioms, GWT from cognitive neuroscience, HOT from philosophy of mind, biological computationalism from computational neuroscience. Their concessions are not borrowed from each other. The independence claim is bounded — all four operate within Western analytic philosophy with overlapping methodological resources, and the [common-cause-null discipline](/project/common-cause-null/) sharpens the question of how much triangulation that boundedness leaves. The audit below scores the pattern against three discriminating tests and the four criteria of apophatic cartography to identify which evidential weight the convergence earns.

**Structural identity.** Despite surface differences, each concession has the same logical form: the class of conscious systems must be narrowed beyond what abstract functional description permits. Whether the narrowing invokes intrinsic causal power (IIT), specific neural architecture (GWT), particular representational structures (HOT), or substrate-specific dynamics (biological computationalism), the move is the same—and it is the move dualists have always made. Each narrowing implicitly respects [type-specificity](/concepts/type-specificity/): the retreat replaces a generic-mechanism account (any system with the right functional profile) with a specification keyed to particular physical or architectural features, recognising the type-specific demand even where the metaphysical conclusion is not granted.

**Involuntariness.** The concessions are not strategic retreats designed to accommodate dualism. They are forced by the theories' own internal logic when confronted with the hard problem. Each theory's proponents would reject the dualist interpretation of their modifications. This makes the convergence more significant, not less: it is not a negotiated settlement but an independent arrival at the same structural limitation.

**Directionality.** The narrowing moves go in one direction only—toward greater specificity about what consciousness requires. No physicalist theory has successfully broadened its account (claiming consciousness is *more* widely distributed than initially proposed) without running into [panpsychism](/concepts/panpsychism/)—which, by attributing experience to fundamental physical entities, concedes that consciousness cannot be derived from structural or functional properties and must be posited as a basic feature of reality. The ratchet only turns toward the dualist position.

**Not normal refinement.** An objection arises: all theories add constraints as they mature. Newtonian mechanics added perturbation terms; evolutionary biology added genetic drift. Why treat physicalist narrowing as concession rather than ordinary scientific progress? The difference is that normal refinement preserves the theory's core explanatory claim while improving its precision. When GWT adds architectural specificity, it does not improve its explanation of *why* broadcast produces experience—it retreats from explaining experience at all, restricting itself to access. When IIT adds the exclusion postulate, it does not refine what integrated information explains—it restricts which systems count, conceding that integration alone is insufficient. The refinements do not make the theories better at their central task; they narrow the domain to avoid the cases where the theory visibly fails. This is defensive contraction, not progressive refinement.

## Independence Scoring Against the Common-Cause Null

The four features above name *why* convergence would carry inferential weight if the independence claim is sound. They do not by themselves establish that the claim *is* sound. The four theories — IIT, GWT, HOT, and biological computationalism — were all developed within Western analytic philosophy of mind during a period dominated by Chalmers's articulation of the hard problem. The [common-cause-null discipline](/project/common-cause-null/) therefore asks: what if the structural identity of the four narrowing moves reflects this shared methodological context rather than independent convergence on the explanandum? The N-fold count would then overstate the evidence — four physicalist theories shaped by a shared post-1995 dialectic, reading the *same* upstream feature four times rather than triangulating across four independent measurements.

This section applies the discipline. It identifies the discriminating tests that would distinguish independent triangulation from shared-context-rediscovered, scores the concession pattern against [the four criteria of apophatic cartography](/concepts/apophatic-cartography-four-criteria/) that [the taxonomy of voids](/apex/taxonomy-of-voids/) uses for sub-cluster scoring, and reports the tier the pattern earns under [the evidential-status discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/). The audit extends the methodology the catalogue worked out on first-order convergence clusters ([the surplus void](/voids/the-surplus-void/) retracted; [the introspection-architecture sub-cluster](/topics/introspection-architecture-independence-scoring/) partially retained) to a *meta-argument* for the first time — a third worked exhibit demonstrating that the calibration architecture applies to claims about the structure of the literature, not only to claims about the structure of cognitive failure.

### The Shared Upstream Architecture

The common machinery the null can plausibly invoke against the concession pattern has three nested layers, each more demanding than the last to discharge.

**The Chalmers-framing layer.** All four theories were articulated or substantially refined after the hard problem was sharpened in 1995. Their narrowing moves are responses to a dialectical situation in which "but does it explain phenomenal experience?" had become the standard challenge any physicalist theory must field. The narrowings could reflect the shared *framing* of the question rather than the structure of the explanandum.

**The analytic-tradition layer.** All four operate within a methodological tradition that treats absent-qualia, zombie, and inverted-spectrum intuitions as load-bearing dialectical instruments. A theory developed within this tradition is selected to be responsive to those instruments. The narrowing moves could reflect the tradition's *filtering function* rather than the explanandum's *type-specificity*.

**The shared introspective channel layer.** All four are developed by humans reasoning about consciousness through human introspection. The hard problem itself is articulated through introspective vocabulary — *what it is like*, *phenomenal character*, *qualitative feel*. If the introspective channel produces the appearance of an unbridgeable explanatory gap that physicalist theories must accommodate, the four theories' convergence on accommodating it is reading one upstream feature four times. The [common-cause-null discipline](/project/common-cause-null/) names this as the layer no current convergence claim can fully discharge.

Discharging the Chalmers-framing layer requires evidence from before 1995 or from outside the post-1995 dialectical situation. Discharging the analytic-tradition layer requires evidence from non-analytic traditions that engage consciousness theoretically. Discharging the shared-introspective-channel layer requires evidence from architectures that do not share the human introspective vocabulary.

### Three Discriminating Tests

Three classes of evidence would distinguish independent triangulation from shared-context-rediscovered, each ruling out a different layer of common machinery.

**Pre-1990s physicalist theories that narrowed in the same direction.** If the pattern is forced by the explanandum rather than by Chalmers's framing, physicalist theories developed *before* the hard problem was sharpened should exhibit the same retreat. The evidence here is partial but real. Smart (1959) handled "topic-neutral" qualifying language for after-images, routing phenomenal report through neutral functional description and conceding that phenomenal character is not delivered directly by the brain-state identity. Feigl's *The "Mental" and the "Physical"* (1958/1967) is the cleanest pre-hard-problem instance: Feigl frames physicalism as requiring identification of phenomenal qualia with *specific* neural-state types, anticipating the narrowing the four contemporary theories perform. Armstrong (1968) introduces functional individuation that is *already* substrate-non-neutral in ways the later debate would sharpen. The pre-1990s record shows incipient narrowing under a less-articulated version of the same dialectical pressure. The discharge is partial: the temporal correlation with Chalmers's articulation is real, and the pre-1990s narrowing is less systematic. But the *direction* of narrowing predates the hard problem's contemporary articulation, which is some evidence against the Chalmers-framing reading.

**Non-Western physicalist theories of consciousness that narrow the same way.** Genuinely independent triangulation would require physicalist theories developed outside the Western analytic tradition that arrive at structurally identical narrowing moves. The evidence here is thin. Contemporary Chinese philosophy of mind engages the hard problem in dialogue with the Western literature, so post-2000 Chinese work cannot serve as an independent test — the transmission channel is active. Pre-modern Buddhist Abhidharma's *citta-caitta* analysis identifies phenomenal characters by type but the framework is not physicalist in the contemporary sense. Japanese phenomenological-naturalist traditions (Nishida, Yuasa) operate at the interface with phenomenology rather than producing competitor theories to IIT or GWT. The honest report is that no non-Western tradition has produced a physicalist theory of consciousness structurally comparable to the four reviewed, so the cross-tradition discriminator is not yet available. This is a *gap in the discriminating tests*, not a failed discriminator.

**Physicalist theories developed in deliberate isolation from Chalmers's framing.** A different discriminator runs through theories whose authors explicitly disavow the hard-problem framing yet still narrow in the same direction. Dennett's heterophenomenology rejects the hard problem as a pseudo-problem yet his account is *forced* to add an increasingly specific story about why some functional patterns generate phenomenal *reports* while others do not — "fame in the brain" specifies architectural conditions in moves structurally parallel to GWT's architectural narrowing. Frankish's illusionism (2016) denies that there is phenomenal character to be explained, yet must narrow to specify *why the illusion of phenomenal character* arises in some architectures and not others. The narrowing-without-the-framing pattern is the strongest available evidence that the structure is forced by the explanandum (or by what its explanation must accommodate) rather than by Chalmers's framing alone. Two cases is not full triangulation, but the cases are precisely the cases the discriminator selects for: theorists who reject the framing yet exhibit the structure.

A convergence claim that withstood all three tests in clean form would have the strongest currently-available defence. The concession pattern withstands the first test partially (pre-1990s narrowing is incipient but real), fails the second test by absence of available evidence (no non-Western contemporary physicalist theories of consciousness comparable in articulation), and withstands the third test in substantive form (Dennett and Frankish narrow despite rejecting the framing). The aggregate verdict is *partial discharge with a salient gap*.

### Scoring on the Four Criteria

The same four criteria [taxonomy-of-voids](/apex/taxonomy-of-voids/) uses for its sub-cluster scoring apply to the concession-convergence pattern at the meta-argument layer. The scoring is asymmetric across criteria, in the way the introspection-architecture exhibit established asymmetric scoring is principled rather than evasive.

**Signature specificity: strong.** The narrowing signature has a distinct shape — *replace generic functional description with a specification keyed to particular physical, architectural, or representational features*, with the specification doing the work of excluding absent-qualia counter-examples. The four theories' specific narrowings are visibly different in content (IIT to intrinsic causal power; GWT to architectural specificity plus posterior-cortex anchoring under COGITATE 2025; HOT to specific representational structures; biological computationalism to substrate-dependent metabolic dynamics) but identical in form. The form is recognisable from a description of the move alone. Signature specificity passes in strong form because the narrowing is not generic theory-improvement — it is specifically the move that grants type-specificity while denying the metaphysical conclusion.

**Structured persistence: strong.** The narrowing pattern has held across competing programmes for sixty-plus years if Feigl/Place/Smart count as early instances, and across thirty years of explicit post-Chalmers articulation. Each theory's narrowing has been refined under sustained critical pressure without dissolving the structural feature: IIT's exclusion postulate has survived from Tononi 2008 through the COGITATE 2025 adversarial test; GWT's architectural specificity has been refined through Dehaene's neuronal global workspace iterations; HOT has produced HOROR and attention-based variants without abandoning the higher-order requirement; biological computationalism is recent (Milinkovic & Aru 2025) but builds on substrate-dependence claims that have persisted across the artificial-life and biocomputation literatures since the 1990s. The pattern is not a localised difficulty masquerading as a structural feature; it has survived translation across multiple programmes and across decades.

**Cross-observer convergence: mixed and asymmetric.** This is where the common-cause null bites hardest. Within Western analytic philosophy of mind, the convergence is strong but not independent in the sense the inference requires — the four theorists work in a tradition with shared methodological resources. Across traditions, the convergence is currently weak because the discriminator (non-Western contemporary physicalist theories of consciousness) is absent rather than negative. Across pre-vs-post-Chalmers framing, the convergence is moderate (the pre-1990s record shows incipient narrowing; the post-1995 record sharpens it). Across rejecting-the-framing cases (Dennett, Frankish), the convergence is moderate-to-strong on the narrowing structure even where the framing is rejected. The score is therefore *mixed across sub-channels and asymmetric in load-bearing*: the dominant evidence is within-tradition; the strongest *despite-commitments* anchors are Dennett and Frankish; the historically-isolated-tradition channel is unbuilt.

**Framework independence: moderate, with an architecture-vs-significance split.** Following the asymmetric application principle the introspection-architecture exhibit established, the score splits into two components. *Architectural finding* (the narrowing pattern itself — physicalist theories under sustained engagement with the hard problem narrow toward specifications that respect type-specificity): this survives translation into functionalist, identity-theoretic, illusionist, and global-workspace vocabulary. The pattern is observable from outside the Map's framework, by reviewers committed to physicalism. Score: high. *Interpretive significance* (the move from "physicalist theories narrow in this direction" to "the narrowing constitutes inadvertent concession of the dualist claim"): this is framework-shaped. A committed physicalist reads the narrowing as ordinary scientific progress under refined criteria of phenomenal-explanation; the Map reads it as concession. The interpretive move is the load-bearing one for the article's conclusion, and it is the move that lower framework independence will not support without further argument. Score: lower. The split is the same shape as the introspection-architecture exhibit: high on the architectural finding, lower on the interpretive significance.

### What Tier the Concession Pattern Earns

[The evidential-status discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/) supplies the five-tier scale (*established → strongly supported → realistic possibility → live hypothesis → speculative integration*). The aggregate scoring above translates into asymmetric tier assignments, conditional on which discriminating tests pass.

The *architectural finding* — that physicalist theories of consciousness, when sustained engagement with the hard problem is required of them, narrow in a structurally identical direction toward type-specificity-respecting specifications — earns the *strongly supported* tier. The score is supported by strong signature specificity, strong structured persistence, the substantive partial-discharge of the Chalmers-framing common-cause-null (Dennett and Frankish narrow despite rejecting the framing), and high framework-independence on the architectural claim itself. Reviewers committed to physicalism would recognise the narrowing pattern as real and ongoing — the disagreement is at the next step, not at this one.

The *interpretive significance* — that the narrowing constitutes inadvertent concession of the dualist claim that consciousness is not derivable from abstract functional organisation — earns the *realistic possibility, contested* tier. The same evidence that supports the architectural finding does not by itself support the interpretive move; the move requires the further argument that defensive contraction is structurally distinct from ordinary refinement (the "not normal refinement" subsection above carries this argument, but it is contested) and that the narrowing direction is informative rather than incidental. The interpretive move is also where framework-shape enters: the dualist reads the narrowing as concession, the physicalist as progress, and the dialectical situation is not adjudicated by the narrowing alone.

The *cumulative meta-argument weight* — the claim that the concession pattern, taken together, constitutes positive evidence for dualism — earns the *live hypothesis* tier in its full form and *strongly supported* in the weaker form that the pattern *constrains* physicalist explanation in ways the standard dialectical narrative obscures. The full form requires the interpretive significance claim to upgrade further, which the cross-observer convergence's mixed scoring will not currently bear without the cross-tradition discriminator landing. The weaker form (constraint rather than positive support) is what the architectural finding already supplies, and this is the form the article should be read as claiming in its current state.

The asymmetric tier assignments are the discipline's point: an article whose cluster scores high on three criteria and mixed on a fourth must label its load-bearing claims at tiers the lowest-scoring criterion supports, not at tiers averaging across the criteria. The architectural finding can earn *strongly supported*; the interpretive significance cannot earn higher than *realistic possibility, contested* without the cross-observer convergence gap closing; the meta-argument weight earns *live hypothesis* in its full positive form and *strongly supported* in its constraint form.

### The Pattern's Position in the Methodology Programme

[The taxonomy of voids](/apex/taxonomy-of-voids/)'s "Two Worked Exhibits in Independence Scoring" subsection (lines 180–188) names the calibration architecture's open burden: an instrument that scored every cluster the same would be doing no work, but two exhibits is not yet a vindication — a clean unfavourable test case remains the methodology's open burden. The concession-convergence audit above is a candidate third worked exhibit, and its scoring profile differs from both predecessors in informative ways.

[The surplus void](/voids/the-surplus-void/) was retracted from the evidential register. Its four-criterion scoring was moderate / strong / weak / weak — the weak cross-observer convergence and weak framework independence overwhelmed the moderate-and-strong scores on the other two. The verdict was framework-internal coherence rather than independent evidence.

[The introspection-architecture sub-cluster](/topics/introspection-architecture-independence-scoring/) was partially retained. Its scoring was strong / strong / mixed / strong-with-architecture-vs-significance-split. The cluster retained its evidential weight for the architectural finding through patient-population despite-commitments anchors (Schnider, Hirstein), with the interpretive significance scored lower per the split.

The concession-convergence pattern's scoring is strong / strong / mixed-asymmetric / moderate-with-architecture-vs-significance-split. The profile is closest to the introspection-architecture exhibit in its asymmetric framework-independence application and in its retention of substantial evidential weight at the architectural-finding tier. It differs from the introspection-architecture exhibit in that its strongest despite-commitments anchors are theoretical (Dennett, Frankish — theorists rejecting the framing yet exhibiting the structure) rather than empirical (Schnider, Hirstein — patient populations whose substrate physiology generates the signature). The substitution of theoretical-rejection anchors for empirical-substrate anchors is the discriminating feature of meta-argument independence scoring: the load-bearing despite-commitments evidence cannot come from patient populations because the convergence pattern is at the literature level rather than at the cognitive-architecture level. Theoretical rejecters who exhibit the structure are the meta-argument analogue.

The exhibit therefore demonstrates that the calibration architecture extends to meta-arguments, not just to first-order arguments about cognitive failure. The four criteria function as scoring categories at this layer too; the despite-commitments / because-prediction split has a meta-argument-level analogue (theorists who reject the framing yet narrow versus theorists who accept the framing and narrow); the architecture-vs-significance asymmetric scoring rule applies in the same form. With three exhibits — surplus void retracted, introspection-architecture sub-cluster partially retained, concession-convergence partially retained at the architectural tier — the methodology has scored across three distinct evidential profiles (full retraction, empirical-substrate partial retention, theoretical-rejecter partial retention), demonstrating non-flatness across the calibration instrument's intended range. A clean unfavourable test case at the meta-argument layer remains the open burden, parallel to the open burden at the first-order layer.

### What Changes If the Discharging Evidence Lands

The audit identifies one substantial open programme: the cross-tradition discriminator. If non-Western contemporary philosophy of mind develops physicalist theories of consciousness that exhibit the same narrowing structure, the cross-observer convergence score would strengthen, the architectural finding would be eligible to upgrade from *strongly supported* to *strongly supported with cross-tradition independence*, and the cumulative meta-argument weight in its full positive form would upgrade from *live hypothesis* to *realistic possibility*. Conversely, if pre-1990s narrowing evidence dissolves on closer examination — the Feigl, Smart, and Armstrong narrowings turning out to be functionally distinct from contemporary narrowing rather than incipient cases of the same pattern — the Chalmers-framing null would gain force and the architectural finding's tier would downgrade. The current scoring is a snapshot conditional on the evidence available; the discipline's calibration is live in both directions.

## Relation to Concession and Convergence Arguments

Concession convergence should be distinguished from, but connects to, two related argumentative strategies in the Map.

The [convergence argument for dualism](/topics/the-convergence-argument-for-dualism/) draws on multiple *positive* arguments (knowledge argument, conceivability, intentionality, binding) that independently support dualism from different starting points. Concession convergence is different: it tracks *negative* evidence from physicalism's own defensive modifications rather than positive arguments for dualism. The two patterns reinforce each other—positive arguments converge on dualism while physicalist theories independently retreat toward it.

The [parsimony case](/topics/parsimony-case-for-interactionist-dualism/) identifies the "proliferation problem": physicalists have generated functionalism, higher-order theories, global workspace theory, illusionism, eliminativism, and multiple identity theories—structurally incompatible responses to the same problem. Concession convergence explains *why* the proliferation occurs: each framework must add specificity to accommodate the hard problem, and the additions are structurally identical to the dualist position they deny.

The [epistemology of convergence arguments](/topics/epistemology-of-convergence-arguments/) analyses when such patterns constitute evidence. Concession convergence satisfies the key conditions: genuine independence across disciplines, robustness under theoretical variation, and specific rather than vague agreement—each theory narrows toward the same structural limitation.

## Relation to Site Perspective

The Unfinishable Map's [tenets](/tenets/) predict the concession convergence pattern.

The **Dualism** tenet holds that consciousness is not reducible to physical processes. Concession convergence shows physicalist theories independently arriving at this conclusion through their own internal development—not because dualists imposed it, but because the phenomenon demands it. The Map interprets this as indirect confirmation: if every serious attempt to explain consciousness in purely physical terms is forced to add something beyond abstract functional description, the additional element may be what dualists call the non-physical.

The **Occam's Razor Has Limits** tenet predicts that apparent simplicity will prove deceptive in the consciousness domain. Concession convergence is a concrete mechanism by which this deception unfolds: each physicalist theory starts with a simple claim, accumulates qualifications, and ends with a framework whose complexity rivals or exceeds the dualism it was meant to replace. The [parsimony reversal](/topics/parsimony-case-for-interactionist-dualism/)—the positive argument that dualism is more parsimonious once all costs are tallied—gains empirical grounding from observing this pattern play out across multiple programmes.

The pattern extends beyond consciousness studies. In [philosophy of mathematics](/topics/concession-convergence-philosophy-of-mathematics/), formalism, logicism, intuitionism, and structuralism each begin from incompatible starting points yet are independently forced to concede that mathematical understanding outruns formal characterisation—the same structural gap, appearing in a domain where formal systems are maximally precise.

The Map speculates that concession convergence will continue. Future physicalist theories addressing the hard problem will face the same structural pressure: either remain broad enough to be falsified by absent qualia cases, or narrow sufficiently to replicate the dualist distinction between functional organisation and consciousness. The pattern is not an accident of current theories but a consequence of the problem's structure.
