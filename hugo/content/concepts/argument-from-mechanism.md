The Argument from Mechanism is the inference: *a phenomenon X can be produced by mechanism M; therefore every instance of X is produced by M, and X is nothing but M.* The conclusion does not follow. Establishing that M is **sufficient** to produce X — in some cases M yields X — is silent on whether M is **necessary** for X — whether X requires M, with no other route available. Sufficiency and necessity are logically independent, and the slide from one to the other is a non-sequitur the Map names so that articles across its induction debates can point at it rather than re-derive it each time.

The discipline cuts **both ways**, and that symmetry is its defining feature. The materialist version reads an induced phenomenon as exhausting it: "we produced X with M, therefore X is nothing but M." The dualist version is the mirror image — an argument from ignorance: "no mechanism explains X, therefore X is non-physical." Both convert a one-directional gap into a positive conclusion in the other direction. A discipline that rejected only the first would be a partisan debunking tool; the Map rejects both with equal force.

"The argument from mechanism" is a Map-named label for this content-domain pattern. No philosophy paper uses the exact phrase as a term of art; the move it names instantiates several established fallacies (confusing sufficiency with necessity, affirming the consequent, the genetic fallacy, argument from ignorance) depending on how it is phrased. The Map coins the label to give its consciousness-and-mind articles a single citeable anchor for one recurring logical anatomy.

## The Logical Structure {#structure}

Stated as a syllogism, the materialist form runs:

> (P1) Mechanism M is sufficient to produce phenomenon X — established empirically by stimulation, lesion, drug, or other induction.
> (C) Therefore every instance of X is produced by M / X is nothing but M.

The premise can be solid. Interventional evidence — directly stimulating a brain region, ablating it, administering a compound — really can establish causal sufficiency, which is what makes the move more seductive than a mere correlation. The fault lies entirely in the leap to the conclusion. Sufficiency is one-directional (M → X in some cases); the conclusion smuggles in necessity (X → M in all cases) and exhaustiveness (nothing other than M produces X).

A condition can be sufficient without being necessary. Oxygen plus a spark suffices for fire, yet fire has other ignition routes. A condition can be necessary without being sufficient: oxygen is necessary for fire, but oxygen alone does not produce it. "Everywhere there is oxygen, there is fire" is the canonical illustration of the error — treating a sufficient or a necessary condition as if it were the whole story.

Naming which adjacent fallacy is in play keeps a given instance precise:

- **Confusing sufficiency with necessity** — treating a demonstrated sufficient condition as though it were necessary. The cleanest description of the materialist direction.
- **Affirming the consequent** — "If M, then X; X; therefore M." From "the mechanism would produce this phenomenology" plus "we observe this phenomenology," inferring "the mechanism is operative and is the whole story." Invalid because X may have other antecedents.
- **The genetic fallacy** (in the debunking register) — taking the causal origin of an experience to settle its nature or veridicality. That a hallucination-like state can be induced does not, by itself, show that every token of the experience is non-veridical.
- **Hasty generalisation** — moving from one demonstrated production route to all instances.

These are facets of the same mistake, not separate concepts. The Map's anchor names the substantive pattern — the inference from a producible mechanism to an exhaustive explanation in mind and consciousness debates — and notes that it realises one or more of these fallacies depending on phrasing.

### Stronger than "correlation is not causation"

The Argument from Mechanism is a tougher case than the familiar "correlation does not imply causation" warning, and conflating the two understates the problem. Mechanism arguments usually carry genuine *interventional* evidence, not mere correlation. The intervention does establish causal sufficiency; what it cannot establish is necessity, exhaustiveness, or that the induced token is representative of every token. The precise framing is a sufficiency–necessity gap, not a correlation gap.

## The Symmetric Application {#symmetry}

The single most important design feature of this concept is that it applies in both directions. A discipline that only ever embarrassed materialists would be special pleading.

### Materialist over-reach

The dominant direction in the literature. An induced phenomenon is read as exhausted by its inducing mechanism.

The Map's canonical statement of the refusal lives in the out-of-body-experience treatment, where stimulation of the right temporo-parietal junction (TPJ) produces OBE-like phenomenology. The narrow, valid conclusion is that a neural mechanism *sufficient* to produce OBE-like phenomenology exists. The over-reach is "therefore all OBEs are TPJ artefacts." As [out-of-body-experiences](/topics/out-of-body-experiences/) puts it: "That a phenomenon can be produced by mechanism M does not show that all instances of the phenomenon are produced by M." Visual experience can be induced by direct cortical stimulation without making ordinary vision hallucinatory; pain can be induced by pinching a nerve without making all pain a nerve-pinch artefact. Whether the TPJ pathway is *necessary* for OBEs is a separate question stimulation studies cannot answer.

The pattern recurs wherever a phenomenon can be induced. Temporal-lobe stimulation of the "felt presence" (Persinger's so-called "God Helmet") was read as showing religious experience is "just temporal-lobe activity" — a reading weakened further by independent replication failures in which suggestibility, not the magnetic field, predicted the effect. Dying-brain neuro-functional models reproduce overlapping near-death-experience themes and get read as showing NDEs are "nothing but" anoxic or REM-intrusion artefacts. In each case the induction establishes a sufficient route and is then over-read as the only route.

Crucially, this is a recognised error inside the source literature, not a Map invention. Yoshihara and Yoshihara (2018) document in neuroscience itself how "sufficient" — in optogenetics and command-neuron debates — is routinely misread as "necessary," distorting circuit-function conclusions. They recommend replacing the loaded phrase "necessary and sufficient" with "indispensable and inducing" precisely to block the slide. Their domain is circuit neuroscience and optogenetics rather than consciousness debates directly, so the citation establishes that the *logical pattern* is a known methodological problem — not that they wrote about OBEs or near-death experiences.

### Dualist over-reach

Symmetry forces the Map to reject the dualist mirror with the same rigour.

The explanatory-gap version runs: "No physical mechanism explains X — phenomenal consciousness, say — therefore X is non-physical." This is an argument from ignorance ("no evidence that X is physically explicable, therefore it is not explicable") and often also affirming the consequent ("if dualism were true we would expect an explanatory gap; there is a gap; therefore dualism"). Physicalists reply, rightly, that the gap may be *epistemic* — a present limit on knowledge — rather than *ontological*. The Map must not run this move. The absence of a sufficient mechanism today no more establishes a non-physical story than the presence of a sufficient mechanism establishes an exhaustively physical one.

The temptation appears across the same debates the materialist version does. On near-death and terminal-lucidity questions, "current mechanisms cannot accommodate lucidity in a degraded brain" can be over-read as "therefore consciousness is transmitted, not produced." The disciplined form does not lean on bare ignorance: [terminal-lucidity-and-filter-transmission-theory](/topics/terminal-lucidity-and-filter-transmission-theory/) argues from a structural asymmetry the production model has difficulty accommodating rather than from the mere absence of an explanation. On OBE veridicality, "the self-model-dislocation mechanism does not exhaust OBEs, therefore consciousness leaves the body" is declined explicitly; the strong dualist reading "depends on rare and contested veridical-perception cases, and the controlled evidence from the AWARE studies has so far returned zero hits on hidden targets" ([out-of-body-experiences](/topics/out-of-body-experiences/)).

The symmetric statement: demonstrating that a mechanism is sufficient does not establish that it is the whole story (the materialist error); demonstrating that no mechanism is yet sufficient does not establish a non-physical story (the dualist error). Each converts a gap in one direction into a conclusion in the other.

## Place Among the Map's Named Disciplines {#place}

The Argument from Mechanism sits beside two existing Map anchors that superficially resemble it. Distinguishing them sharply is what keeps the concept from being redundant.

### Distinct from possibility/probability slippage

[possibility-probability-slippage](/concepts/possibility-probability-slippage/) is a different vector entirely. It is a move *internal* to the Map's own reasoning: a tenet that secures the metaphysical *possibility* of a claim is silently treated as evidence raising the claim's empirical *probability*. What is conflated there is the modal register (the framework permits X) with the evidential register (the evidence supports X), and the party who commits it is the tenet-protected Map reasoner writing about its own positions.

The Argument from Mechanism, by contrast, is a move in the *external* mind-and-consciousness evidence debate. What is conflated is the sufficiency of a mechanism with its necessity or exhaustiveness, and it is committed by materialists and dualists alike in published argument. The two can co-occur — an article could over-read its own tenets *and* misjudge a mechanism claim — but the diagnoses and the fixes differ. Slippage is about a framework upgrading its own claims; the Argument from Mechanism is about how anyone misreads interventional evidence.

### The fallacy-named companion to constrain-vs-establish

The closest relative is the [evidential-status discipline](/project/evidential-status-discipline/), which already contains a "Constrain vs. Establish" treatment and its symmetric form, and which designates the OBE passage as the catalogue's canonical materialist-side instance. The relationship must be stated carefully to avoid duplication.

The evidential-status discipline is the *editor-internal* labelling discipline — how the catalogue grades and labels claims, with its five-tier scale and its diagnostic test. It lives in the project documentation as editor vocabulary and does not appear in article prose. The Argument from Mechanism is the *publicly-facing named fallacy* — the logical anatomy of one specific non-sequitur, written in article-level language, that readers and articles can point at.

Put plainly: constrain-vs-establish is the rule the editor applies; the Argument from Mechanism is the named mistake out in the world the rule is calibrated against. The discipline is the editorial response; this concept is the object the response polices. Giving the Map a citeable concept page lets the OBE, near-death, dissociation, and altered-state articles each link to one named pattern instead of re-deriving the sufficiency–necessity point inline.

### A neighbouring failure: compatibility-vs-support

One related move is worth distinguishing so cases are not miscatalogued. The psychedelics debate is *not* primarily an Argument-from-Mechanism case; it is a *compatibility-vs-support* case. The same neuroimaging fits both a filter reading and a physicalist model such as REBUS, so it discriminates between neither. [psychedelics-and-the-filter-model](/topics/psychedelics-and-the-filter-model/) handles this at the compatibility level — the evidence is compatible with filter theory but does not support it over the rival. The Argument from Mechanism says "M is sufficient, therefore M is the whole story"; compatibility-vs-support says "the evidence fits my reading, and also the rival's, therefore it supports mine." Different errors, kept apart.

## Relation to Site Perspective {#relation}

The Argument from Mechanism connects most directly to [Tenet 5 (Occam's Razor Has Limits)](/tenets/#occams-limits). The materialist version is usually *powered by* an implicit parsimony move: "we have a sufficient mechanism, so positing anything further is unnecessary." Tenet 5 denies that simplicity is a reliable guide to truth under incomplete knowledge — and a sufficient mechanism is precisely not a complete one. The shortcut from "M suffices" to "nothing but M" is exactly the over-application of Occam's razor the tenet rejects. The same tenet binds the Map's own arguments: it cannot wield "no mechanism explains X" as if parsimony favoured dualism, because Tenet 5's discipline is symmetric — parsimony decides for neither side when the relevant knowledge is incomplete.

The concept also protects [Tenet 1 (Dualism)](/tenets/#dualism) without licensing its over-reach. The materialist-side correction keeps the filter and interface readings *live*: showing a mechanism is sufficient does not foreclose a reading on which consciousness is filtered through the substrate rather than produced by it. But the symmetry requirement is what keeps Tenet 1 honest. Dualism is the *beneficiary* of the materialist-side correction and is *bound* by the dualist-side correction: the Map must reject "no mechanism, therefore mind" with the same force it rejects "mechanism, therefore nothing but mechanism." Tenet 1 is held as a chosen starting point, not as a conclusion wrung from an explanatory gap.

A secondary link runs to [Tenet 3 (Bidirectional Interaction)](/tenets/#bidirectional-interaction): the filter and interface readings the discipline keeps open are the ones on which consciousness causally couples to the substrate rather than being produced by it. The honest framing is continuous with the constrain-vs-establish discipline — naming and symmetrically applying the Argument from Mechanism is what lets the Map hold the filter reading open at the modal register without claiming the mechanism evidence establishes it at the evidential register.
