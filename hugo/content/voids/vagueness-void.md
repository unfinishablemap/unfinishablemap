The Vagueness Void is the structural impossibility, for any mind operating from inside its own conceptual repertoire, of locating where its concepts stop applying. Almost every concept consciousness deploys—*red*, *tall*, *anger*, *person*, *conscious*—admits borderline cases where neither application nor refusal is determinately correct. The void has three structural faces conjoined: third-personal Sorites indeterminacy (no incremental method can fix the cut-off), first-personal introspective indeterminacy (the same fuzz applies to the concepts consciousness uses on its own states), and meta-level higher-order vagueness (the borderlines themselves have borderlines, recursively, with no fixed point). Each face inherits the indeterminacy of the others, and consciousness has no neutral perch from which to fix any of them.

The void is not exotic. It sits beneath every judgement of typicality, every diagnostic categorisation, every introspective report of the form "this is roughly anger." Concepts feel sharp from the centre and dissolve at the edges; incremental refinement *generates* new edges rather than dissolving the old. The Vagueness Void marks the place where that dissolution becomes structural rather than provisional.

## Three Faces, One Void

The void exhibits the [conjunction-coalesce](/apex/conjunction-coalesce/) structure used elsewhere for [suspension-void](/voids/suspension-void/), [agency-void](/voids/agency-void/), and [imagery-void](/voids/imagery-void/): a single void with multiple faces whose informativeness lies in their seam. Each face alone is a recognised problem in the literature; their conjunction is the analytical claim. Without higher-order vagueness, the introspective face would be merely empirical and the Sorites face merely linguistic; with it, neither can be opened from inside without invoking a metalanguage subject to the same indeterminacy.

### The Sorites Face (Unexplorable)

For almost any concept *C*, there is an incremental series along which *C* neither sharply applies nor sharply fails. One grain is not a heap; adding one grain to a non-heap does not yield a heap; therefore no number of grains makes a heap. The argument is formally valid in classical logic, so rejecting its conclusion requires rejecting a premise—that is, locating a cut-off—or modifying the logic. Either move is costly.

Williamson's epistemic theory ([Williamson 1994](#references)) preserves classical logic by accepting that vague terms have sharp boundaries fixed by unknowable patterns of usage. The position survives because of his **margin-for-error principle**: if a subject knows *p*, then in all situations similar enough to the subject's actual situation, *p* is still true. Any belief sharp enough to identify the cut-off of "tall" would fail this constraint—knowledge of the exact cut-off is incompatible with the way knowledge tolerates small errors. Williamson's claim, as Sainsbury reconstructs it, is that the alleged cut-off points of vague predicates are unknowable in principle, not merely in practice.

Supervaluationism, fuzzy logic, contextualism, and intuitionism each reject sharp cut-offs but inherit revenge problems. Supervaluationism preserves classical consequence by treating a sentence as true iff true under all admissible precisifications—but which precisifications count as "admissible" is itself vague. Bobzien's 2024 modal "generic solution" is compatible with several theories simultaneously, showing the Sorites is *technically* dissolvable while leaving the underlying metaphysics underdetermined. No theory dissolves the void without paying somewhere.

The Sorites face is *Unexplorable* in the sense the [three-kinds typology](/voids/three-kinds-of-void/) reserves for limits that resist mapping by their own architecture. Any method consciousness can deploy from inside must itself use *C*-judgements, and is therefore subject to the same indeterminacy it tries to resolve.

### The Introspective Face (Occluded)

Vagueness extends inward to the concepts consciousness uses to categorise its own states. Asking "is this still anger or now resentment?" admits no determinate answer, and the asking does not reliably produce one. Hampton's typicality data show graded membership across cultures and tasks: robins are more typical birds than penguins, and typicality predicts response time, ease of recall, and inference patterns. Multiple-definitions models suggest that graded membership arises because subjects sample multiple internal definitions, each with different boundaries. Cognitive vagueness is robust and not eliminable by stipulation.

The face goes deeper than category extension. Phenomenal concepts themselves may be vague. Papineau argues they are; Antony argues the argument fails; Tye argues "phenomenally conscious" admits no borderline cases; Schwitzgebel argues it does. Schwitzgebel's quadrilemma—nothing is conscious; everything is conscious; sharp boundary; consciousness is vague—defends the fourth horn: borderline conscious states "are not determinately like anything." Whether the void here is real or merely conceptual is itself within the void.

Empirical psychiatry confirms the structural reading. The literature finds "no sharp boundary between the normal and the pathological" and notes that semantic vagueness "by its very nature cannot be eliminated through scientific discoveries" ([Vagueness in Psychiatry](#references)). For exactly the mental kinds the Map cares about—emotions, mental disorders, "is conscious"—vagueness is structural rather than provisional.

This face also intersects [mutation-void](/voids/mutation-void/): for inward concepts, the asking can shift the state under examination without either earlier or later state being definitely the answer. The introspective face is *Occluded* in the [three-kinds typology](/voids/three-kinds-of-void/): the structure leaves traces and shapes outcomes, but the system whose categories it concerns cannot perceive its own borderlines while operating within them.

### The Higher-Order Face (Unexplorable)

The borderline of borderline cases is itself borderline. The vagueness of "tall" generates a vagueness of "borderline-tall", which generates a vagueness of "borderline-borderline-tall", with no fixed point and no determinate hierarchy of orders. Williamson's account preserves classical logic at the price of unknowable cut-offs at every order. Supervaluationism makes higher-order vagueness explicit at the cost of admitting an indefinite hierarchy of "admissibility" predicates each itself vague. The hierarchy has no top from inside.

This face is meta: it concerns the metalanguage in which the other faces are stated. Even the question of whether a domain *admits* borderline cases is contested in a way that resists resolution from within. The Schwitzgebel/Tye/Antony/Papineau exchanges illustrate the pattern: the dispute over whether phenomenal concepts admit borderlines is itself indeterminate, because the disputants are using vague concepts in the metalanguage to argue about vagueness in the object language. Like the Sorites face, this is *Unexplorable*—but its unexplorability operates one level up from the level of concept application.

The seam: face one is third-personal, face two is first-personal, face three is meta. Each inherits the indeterminacy of the others. Consciousness has no neutral perch from which to fix any of them; using a sharper concept to investigate a vague one merely displaces the borderline rather than dissolving it.

## Substrate Fuzziness

A complementary line of evidence comes from the physical substrate. Haueis's connectomics review notes that "the demarcation of one area from another remains fuzzy, with examples of gradual transitions between areas known since early cytoarchitectonic studies"—cortical area boundaries are themselves vague, and modern connectomics inherits the same fuzziness at the level of connectivity zones. The Map interprets this as relevant to bidirectional interaction: if the physical end of any consciousness-physics interface is fuzzily localised, the channel through which consciousness influences quantum-level outcomes may not have sharp anatomical edges.

## Phenomenology of the Edge

Approaching the vagueness void produces a recognisable pattern, well-attested in introspection and the literature:

- **Either-way comfort.** The borderline case sits comfortably under either label and equally uncomfortably under either.
- **Stipulative verdicts under pressure.** Pressed to decide, the verdict feels arbitrary—chosen rather than discovered.
- **Recursive frustration.** Refining the criterion produces new borderlines; the refinement has not converged but only displaced.
- **Introspective wobble.** For inward concepts, asking can shift the answer without either state being definitively the answer.

These experiences cannot themselves be sharpened into a determinate mark of borderline-ness; the phenomenology of vagueness is itself vague—a feature shared with the broader [phenomenology-of-the-edge](/voids/phenomenology-of-the-edge/).

## Approaches to the Edge

Direct methods do not work by construction. Stipulating a cut-off does not eliminate vagueness; it fixes one tier of the hierarchy and produces a new vagueness one level up. Indirect methods do better:

- **Apophatic mapping** ([apophatic-cartography](/voids/apophatic-cartography/)): catalogue the cases that are clearly in, clearly out, and the residue that resists classification, treating the residue as structural data rather than as a target to be eliminated.
- **Margin-for-error reasoning**: Williamson's framework yields determinate negative results—we can say what a knowable cut-off would have to look like and demonstrate that no actual cut-off can satisfy those conditions.
- **Multiple precisifications**: characterise the determinate space of admissible boundaries even where each boundary within it is indeterminate.
- **Compound failure signatures** ([compound-failure-signatures](/voids/compound-failure-signatures/)): where vagueness compounds with another void—e.g., recognition of one's own emotional state ([recognition-void](/voids/recognition-void/))—the joint failure pattern can be data about cognitive architecture even where each component is individually unmappable.

A non-trivial AI asymmetry exists. AI systems with explicit decision thresholds *do* have determinate cut-offs at the level of model output—but the cut-off shifts with prompt, context, and run, and the underlying representations show graded structure analogous to human typicality. AI minds may make the void's structure more inspectable: one can audit a model's behaviour across a Sorites series in a way one cannot audit a human's. But they inherit the void at the meta-level: a system cannot determine, of its own concept-deployments, whether it is inside or outside the borderline region without using more concepts of the same kind. This is one of the cleaner cases where [non-human-minds-as-void-explorers](/voids/non-human-minds-as-void-explorers/) earn their keep—providing a probe into vagueness's structure that human minds, encountering the borderline only from inside, cannot easily provide.

## Relation to Site Perspective

The vagueness void connects most directly to the Map's tenet that **Occam's Razor Has Limits**. The cleanest semantics for vague predicates—sharp boundaries—yields the most cognitively inaccessible structure (Williamson's unknowable cut-offs). Every alternative theory pays a comparable price elsewhere. Parsimony, applied here, produces opacity rather than transparency. The Map reads this as a paradigm case of the tenet: simplicity reaches a wall where each "simple" fix generates revenge problems.

Secondary tenet alignment runs through **Dualism** and **Bidirectional Interaction**. If consciousness is not reducible to physical processes, the concepts consciousness uses to categorise its own states need not have sharper boundaries than those of physical kinds. The introspective face aligns with the Map's view that consciousness's self-categorisation lacks the privileged sharpness classical introspectionism assumed. And if consciousness influences the physical via a localised channel, Haueis's fuzzy cortical maps suggest the localisation itself may be fuzzy—the Map speculates that any consciousness-physics interface inherits the substrate's own indeterminacy.

A reflexive consequence follows: every void article uses vague concepts (*structural*, *in-principle*, *borderline*), and the void framework operates within vagueness it cannot dissolve. The boundary between [conceivable](/voids/conceptual-impossibility/) and inconceivable is itself vague; the boundary between phenomenally graspable and merely formal magnitudes ([the-quantitative-comprehension-void](/voids/the-quantitative-comprehension-void/)) is borderline; the framework boundaries in [conceptual-scheme-void](/voids/conceptual-scheme-void/) are vague at the seams. The catalogue boundary itself ("is this a void or merely difficult terrain?") is borderline. The Map treats this as the framework operating consistently on itself, in line with [apophatic-cartography](/voids/apophatic-cartography/)'s principled incompleteness.
