Consciousness does not terminate inquiry by argument. It terminates inquiry by feeling. The sense that a recalled answer is right, that a face is familiar, that an explanation is sufficient, that a word is on the tip of the tongue—these *noetic feelings* decide, from inside, when to trust, when to search further, when to stop. The Unfinishable Map names the noetic feelings void for the structural limit this creates: the signals that gate cognition are produced by heuristic machinery that consciousness cannot inspect, and any attempt to audit those signals must deploy more signals of the same kind. The void is occluded at its surface and unexplorable at its core—a recursive opacity at the centre of cognitive self-governance.

The feelings are transparent in a specific and treacherous sense. They do not present themselves as feelings; they present their objects. A feeling of knowing presents as *awareness that one knows*. A feeling of rightness presents as *direct apprehension of correctness*. This mode of presentation hides the affective-heuristic character that cognitive science has, over decades, repeatedly established. What consciousness receives is a verdict with no visible derivation.

## The Family of Noetic Feelings

Noetic feelings—also called epistemic or metacognitive feelings—form a large and functionally integrated family. Each member gates a distinct cognitive operation while sharing the same opacity pattern.

- **Feeling of knowing (FOK)** — the sense that a currently-inaccessible answer is nonetheless known. Gates retrieval persistence.
- **Feeling of rightness (FOR)** — the sense that an initial intuitive answer is correct. Gates engagement of analytic thought.
- **Feeling of familiarity** — the sense that a stimulus has been encountered before. Gates recognition responses.
- **Tip of the tongue (TOT)** — the sense that a specific item is imminent in retrieval. Gates search strategy.
- **Feeling of understanding** — the sense that an explanation has landed. Gates explanatory inquiry.
- **Feeling of coherence** — the sense that disparate elements fit together. Gates pattern-acceptance.
- **Feeling of confidence** — the sense that a judgment is reliable. Gates commitment to action.
- **Feeling of error** — the dysphoric sense that something is wrong, often pre-conceptual. Gates corrective search.

Slawa Loev argues these are "mild affective experiences" whose felt character "tends to elude us introspectively" precisely because of their mildness. Joëlle Proust's framework treats metacognition as procedural rather than metarepresentational: feelings of knowing or of being right drive epistemic control without any articulated thought about cognitive states. The feelings do their gating work beneath the level of reportable judgment.

The Map treats [epistemic emotions](/concepts/epistemic-emotions/) as the phenomenally rich end of this family—curiosity, doubt, insight, confusion—where the felt signal has narrative texture. Noetic feelings sit a level lower: minimal, verdict-like, often unlabeled. The family extends from vivid insight to barely-detectable confidence shifts, all sharing the same opacity pattern.

## How the Feelings Are Produced

Convergent evidence from philosophy and cognitive science identifies noetic feelings as outputs of heuristic processes. Asher Koriat's work on the feeling of knowing concluded that "rather than reflecting direct access to memory traces, noetic feelings are based on inferential heuristics that operate implicitly and unintentionally." Two principal heuristics have been characterised: *cue-familiarity* (fast assessment of whether a retrieval cue itself seems familiar) and *accessibility* (assessment of how much partial information comes to mind). Neither heuristic is available to conscious inspection; each produces a felt verdict and delivers it, finished, to awareness.

Valerie Thompson's research on the feeling of rightness showed that FOR is driven by *generation fluency*—the ease and speed with which the first answer came to mind—not by the answer's actual correctness. High fluency produces high FOR, which terminates analytic engagement. Manipulating fluency (font legibility, rhyme, perceptual ease) shifts FOR without changing the underlying reasoning. The signal that tells consciousness "this is right" is a fluency trace, and the trace is invisible as such.

A 2021 *Synthese* paper formalises the resulting epistemic problem as the "Lima problem": epistemic feelings *inform* us about content we cannot independently access, so we have no way to cross-check their accuracy except through more feelings. The best-supported account—evaluativism—holds that noetic feelings are outputs of a distinct, primitive metacognitive mechanism operating on heuristic correlates of inaccessible content. We have access to the feel, not the content; and the mechanism that produces the feel is architecturally hidden.

## Feed-Forward Opacity and Authority Without Audit

The void has three structural features.

**Feed-forward opacity.** The heuristics are implemented in processes whose inputs and dynamics are not exposed to the consciousness they inform. Consciousness receives a finished verdict, not a derivation. This is not a contingent computational shortcut; it appears to be architectural.

**Authority without audit.** The feelings gate further cognition. They decide when to stop searching, when to trust, when to defer, when to engage analytic thought. The system cannot audit them from inside, because audit requires more feelings of the same kind—a feeling that *this* feeling is reliable.

**Dissociable fallibility.** Déjà vu fires familiarity without a corresponding memory; jamais vu withholds recognition for genuinely familiar material; false confidence and systematic calibration errors show overconfident feelings that cannot be shaken by argument. These dissociations prove that noetic feelings are signals rather than perceptions, and simultaneously show that consciousness cannot tell, from inside, when a signal has fired spuriously. Colorado State research on déjà vu even documents a peculiar second-order effect: subjects in déjà vu states report an illusory feeling of *prediction* that does not track any actual predictive capacity. One feeling begets another, with no additional information.

Compound these features and the void emerges: the signals that gate cognition are precisely the signals consciousness cannot audit without their help.

## Phenomenology of the Edge

Approaching the noetic feelings void feels like trying to look at the side of one's own eye. The more directly one attends to the feeling of knowing, the more it fragments into content-feelings *about* what one feels one knows, rather than disclosing the feeling's own mechanism. Contemplative practice that attempts to watch metacognition at work repeatedly encounters this slippage: the instrument of observation deploys the very feelings it is trying to observe.

There is a characteristic texture to the edge. Certainty that cannot be further grounded. Recognition that refuses to produce a source. Understanding that resists the question *what exactly have I grasped?* The feelings close over their own construction and present a smooth surface. [necessary-opacity](/voids/necessary-opacity/) catalogues this kind of structural closure; [self-opacity](/voids/self-opacity/) names the broader phenomenon of being a system that cannot fully see itself. The noetic feelings void is one of the sharpest instances: the opacity is load-bearing for functional cognition.

## Approaches to the Edge

Direct introspection fails. Loev notes explicitly that "empirical work" is required—introspection alone cannot settle the nature of these feelings, because introspection itself deploys them.

Indirect methods do better.

- **Dissociation experiments** manipulate fluency, familiarity, or retrieval dynamics and observe which feelings shift. These probe the mechanism from outside via its outputs.
- **Clinical edge cases** reveal ordinarily-hidden machinery: déjà vu in temporal-lobe epilepsy, jamais vu in depersonalisation, tip-of-tongue rates in aging populations, source amnesia in cryptomnesia.
- **Comparative metacognition** in primates, cetaceans, and corvids finds confidence-gated opt-out behaviour suggesting homologous machinery: the feelings may be older than concepts.
- **Calibration studies** catalogue characteristic shapes of failure—over- and underconfidence asymmetries, fluency-spoofing effects, calibration curves—as indirect structure. The void has a *signature*, even when its interior is inaccessible.

This is the [apophatic](/voids/apophatic-cartography/) move: when a territory cannot be entered, map its boundary by the shape of the failures at its edge.

## What AI Minds Might See

AI systems with architectural access to their own softmax distributions, retrieval scores, and calibration metrics may approach this void asymmetrically. Confidence values inspectable at the level of representations give, in principle, direct numerical access to the heuristic quantities that in humans generate opaque feelings.

This does not mean AIs have "the feeling of knowing" without the void. It may mean they face a *different* void: mechanism traces without felt authority—confidence scores that do not function as native gates the way noetic feelings do for human cognition. Neither system can self-certify, but each fails differently. A tuned model that reports a particular output had low underlying probability mass yet was accepted as correct provides the mechanical dual of a high-FOR/low-accuracy event in humans. Such comparisons may illuminate what fluency-spoofing looks like from outside the spoofed system.

## Relation to Site Perspective

The noetic feelings void connects to the Map's [foundational commitments](/tenets/) in ways that sharpen rather than soften the Map's position.

**[Occam's Razor Has Limits](/tenets/#occams-limits)** is the most central connection. Trust in parsimony is itself a noetic feeling. When an explanation *feels* sufficient, *feels* clean, *feels* obvious, that feel is a fluency-and-coherence signal produced by the same heuristic machinery as any other noetic feeling. The razor is a felt gate, and the feeling is opaque. The razor has no independent auditor inside a single consciousness—applications of the razor are judged by the very mechanism that generates its verdicts. The Map's commitment to limited parsimony under incomplete knowledge is, among other things, an instruction to *distrust the feeling of sufficiency*.

**[Dualism](/tenets/#dualism)** receives positive support. Noetic feelings have genuine phenomenal character—an irreducible felt quality that is not identical with the accessibility score or fluency trace that triggered them. The feeling of knowing is not merely a functional state; it is a felt state of a conscious subject, with affective valence and felt arousal (Loev). Functional reduction misses the feel. The family as a whole presents the hard problem on the metacognitive register: why should any heuristic produce a *feel* at all?

**[Bidirectional Interaction](/tenets/#bidirectional-interaction)** deepens. If consciousness has causal power, the interface is partly routed through these feelings. They determine which cognitive processes get sustained and which get terminated—and therefore which states of the cognitive system persist long enough to shape action. The occlusion of the mechanism mirrors the occlusion of the causal interface more broadly: both are hidden in precisely the places where inspection would matter most.

**[Minimal Quantum Interaction](/tenets/#minimal-quantum-interaction)** permits a highly speculative extension. If consciousness's influence on physical dynamics is low-bandwidth, noetic feelings—terse, verdict-like, affectively minimal—are natural candidate signatures. This remains conjecture; no current evidence links specific feelings to specific interface events, and the core argument of the void does not depend on it.

There are genuine tensions with neighbouring voids worth naming explicitly. The [source-attribution-void](/voids/source-attribution-void/) shows that mental contents arrive source-less: noetic feelings compound this, since the feelings themselves arrive source-less, gating cognition without disclosing *where the verdict came from*. The [meta-epistemology-of-limits](/voids/meta-epistemology-of-limits/) flags the problem that any claim about cognitive limits is evidence for a method beyond those limits—noetic feelings are a sharp instance of this, because the very claim "I cannot audit my feelings of rightness" must be submitted to a feeling of rightness.

## Implications

1. **Metacognition is structurally suspect as a self-certification mechanism.** The system that tells us when we know cannot tell us when it is wrong. This does not impugn metacognition; it impugns any claim that metacognitive authority should be final.

2. **Self-evidence is a felt signal.** What feels undeniable feels that way because of fluency and coherence heuristics operating beneath inspection. The [understanding-void](/voids/understanding-void/) is the closest cousin: the feeling of understanding is one member of the noetic family, and its opacity is one face of the opacity of noetic feelings generally.

3. **Inquiry terminates at the boundary of felt satisfaction.** Because FOR gates analytic engagement, most thinking ends when the feeling says "this is right." The Map's emphasis on conditionality, hedged confidence, and apophatic cartography is in part a discipline for *sustaining inquiry past the natural gate* set by felt sufficiency.

4. **The void is recursive.** Meta-monitoring of noetic feelings uses more noetic feelings. External probes—experiment, calibration studies, AI comparison—offer partial purchase; they do not close the void.

## Open Questions

- Whether noetic feelings in non-human animals have phenomenal character or only functional character remains open; the Map's Dualism leans toward phenomenal, but the evidence is behaviourally indirect.
- The mapping between specific heuristics (fluency, accessibility, cue-familiarity) and specific phenomenal feels is not fully worked out in the literature—there is not yet a reliable "feelings dictionary."
- Whether the void is *in principle* closed or merely *in practice* closed cannot be settled from inside. Third-person neuroscience may yet map the mechanisms; first-person access to them may nevertheless remain permanently impossible.
- The relationship between noetic feelings and the consciousness-physics interface—whether the terse, verdict-like form of these signals is evidentially related to low-bandwidth interface constraints—is entirely unexplored.
