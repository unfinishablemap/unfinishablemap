Every mind operates with a finite system of categories and inferential patterns that constitutes thought before any specific thought occurs. The Conceptual Scheme Void marks the structural limit set by that system. It has two faces. At the level of the scheme as a whole, the totality is a *framework* that makes thought possible by excluding what it cannot represent — and is invisible because it constitutes the seeing rather than what is seen. At the level of structure within the scheme, particular *categories* — *property*, *substance*, *cause*, *event*, *relation* — are the general shapes any concept must take to track a kind of feature at all. Reality may contain features for which our apparatus has no such shape, or features that fit *no* shape we can produce. Either failure is a void; the framework as a whole has no Archimedean point from which to inventory what it lacks.

This makes the conceptual scheme void a meta-void: the condition that generates many of the Map's other cognitive limits. The reason certain thoughts resist articulation, certain categories resist formation, and certain problems resist solution may ultimately be that the scheme within which thought occurs excludes what it cannot represent — silently, systematically, and without announcing its boundaries. It joins the [mattering](/voids/mattering-void/), [relevance](/voids/relevance-void/), and [noetic-feelings](/voids/noetic-feelings-void/) voids in the cluster of [medium-status voids](/apex/medium-status-voids-in-cognition/) — operations that structure other contents while remaining invisible from inside.

## Framework and Categorial: Two Scales of the Same Claim

A *framework* is the totality of conceptual apparatus — concepts, assumptions, inferential patterns. A *category* is finer-grained: a kind of kind, the general shape a concept must take to track a sort of feature. *Red*, *electron*, *anger*, *prime number* are concepts. *Property*, *substance*, *event*, *cause*, *relation*, *quantity*, *number* are categories. Every new concept slots into one or more categories; without those slots, the concept would have nothing to be an instance *of*. A framework gap is the inventory itself being non-exhaustive in ways no internal audit can settle. A categorial gap is a hole in that inventory. The two are the same kind of claim at two scales.

Kant gave the classic categorial formulation in the *Critique of Pure Reason* (1781). The categories (quantity, quality, relation, modality) are not generalisations *from* experience but preconditions *for* it. We encounter phenomena — things as categorially organised — and never noumena, things as they are apart from any categorial shaping. Rae Langton (1998) reads this as *epistemic humility*: whatever lies beyond the categories falls outside the reach of any knowledge we can have. The Map takes the diagnosis seriously without taking Kant's specific list on faith. Whether the apparatus has evolutionary or embodiment-dependent contingency is examined in [embodiment and cognitive limits](/voids/embodiment-cognitive-limits/). The structural claim is weaker: *some* apparatus is operating, it is finite, and we have no guarantee it covers every shape reality permits.

At the framework scale, Kuhn argued that scientists working within different paradigms "see different things when they look from the same point in the same direction." A framework determines what counts as information, what questions seem meaningful, and what answers seem possible. Three features make this difficult to address from inside.

**Self-concealment.** The pointing occurs within the framework. When dualists and materialists examine the same first-person experience, they may encounter genuinely different phenomena, not merely different descriptions.

**Apparent accessibility.** The void disguises itself as disagreement rather than incommensurability. Two parties use the word "consciousness" and believe they are discussing the same phenomenon — but if Feyerabend was right that semantic principles can be "violated or suspended" by competing theories, they may be talking past each other in ways neither can detect.

**Recursive depth.** Stepping outside one's framework to evaluate it simply creates a new framework. Gadamer's "fusion of horizons" establishes common ground between traditions, but that ground is itself a framework, not an escape.

A categorial gap also resists acquisition in a way an ordinary missing concept does not. The [language-thought boundary](/voids/language-thought-boundary/) examines gaps that close through translation or instruction: *siniy* and *goluboy* are both colour concepts, whether or not English separates them. Acquiring a new *category* would mean acquiring a new shape for concepts. Kant and later Strawson (1959) argued that core categories are not learned in the way particular concepts are learned, because any learning already presupposes them. The machinery that would acquire them is the machinery whose limits are in question.

## How the Void Differs from Its Neighbours

The conceptual scheme void operates at a meta-level above several specific cognitive limits documented elsewhere on the Map.

**Against [conceptual impossibility](/voids/conceptual-impossibility/).** Conceptual impossibility describes content built from our categories but malformed — a round square. Thought "bounces back" with sharp resistance. The scheme void produces a *soft absence*: not resistance, but a region where thought cannot determine what it is reaching for.

**Against the [intrinsic nature void](/voids/intrinsic-nature-void/).** That void concerns unknown *content* behind known structure — what mass and charge *are*, beneath what they *do*. The scheme void concerns unknown *shape*. The two compound: if the intrinsic nature of matter is categorially unlike anything our physical-object schema represents, the intrinsic nature void is partly a scheme void.

**Against the [language-thought boundary](/voids/language-thought-boundary/).** That boundary can be felt — one senses content resisting articulation. The scheme void typically produces no such signal at the framework scale. The framework feels like transparent access to truth; the walls of the prison are made of the same material as the air inside.

## Candidate Voids

Several candidates recur. Each is offered as *a place the void might lie*, not a proven instance.

**The phenomenal-physical shape.** The hard problem persists partly because no categorial schema unifies the two sides. The physical-object category gives structure, causal role, location. The phenomenal category gives what-it-is-likeness, perspectival character, felt quality. No available schema treats both as aspects of one kind. Persistent failure across functionalism, identity theories, panpsychism, and Russellian monism may be evidence that the required shape is not among those our minds can produce.

**The intrinsic character of other species' experience.** Nagel (1974) argued we cannot form the concept of *what it is like to be a bat* because the concept would require acquaintance with bat-phenomenal features human cognition cannot host — a candidate categorial limit, not a missing concept.

**A genuine view from nowhere.** Nagel (1986) explored the tension between subjective and objective viewpoints. A fully objective viewpoint would require a category that represents reality apart from any perspective, including our own. Whether such a category is coherent is contested.

**Experience structured by apparatus we do not possess.** Our categories of *space*, *object*, and *action* are built from the invariance structure of human perception (Sonesson, 2020). A being with radically different sensors or embodiment might operate with categorial shapes our apparatus cannot instantiate — testable rather than settled, per [non-human minds as void-explorers](/voids/non-human-minds-as-void-explorers/).

**Different metaphysical axes.** Graham (1989) argued that *physical object*, *causation*, and *quantity* may be parochial to Indo-European languages. Strong linguistic relativity is widely rejected; weaker versions still suggest the categorial axes a mind carves are not self-evidently those reality follows.

## Evidence That the Limit Is Genuine

The persistence of the mind-body problem provides circumstantial evidence at the framework scale. After millennia of philosophical effort, dualism and materialism remain in approximately the same standoff. If the problem were merely difficult, convergence would be expected; its absence is consistent with framework-dependence.

The 2023–2025 adversarial testing of Integrated Information Theory against Global Neuronal Workspace Theory showed the same pattern at smaller scale: shared protocols, different operationalisations, different success criteria — logically commensurable, empirically incommensurable.

Colin McGinn's species-relativity argument adds a further dimension. If [cognitive closure](/voids/biological-cognitive-closure/) varies across species — "what is closed to the mind of a rat may be open to the mind of a monkey" — the human conceptual scheme may exclude possibilities other architectures access. Both dualist and materialist frameworks may be wrong in ways neither can specify, because specifying the error requires concepts neither contains.

The hermeneutic circle reinforces this. Every investigation of consciousness proceeds from a pre-understanding of what consciousness is. Introspection is theory-laden, and so is third-person neuroscience. Heidegger's analysis of thrownness applies: the framework precedes the self that might try to evaluate it.

## Kant's Ghost and the Point of Divergence

The Map departs from Kant on one decisive question. Kant held that the categorial apparatus he catalogued was *determinate* — fixed, universal, and identifiable through transcendental reflection. The void claim is weaker: whatever apparatus we have, we cannot from within it verify completeness, nor rule out architectures that carve along axes we lack.

Three developments push toward the cautious formulation.

Sellars (1956) argued all perception is theory-laden: there is no neutral observation against which categories could be checked. The audit of the apparatus employs the apparatus being audited.

Davidson (1974) argued in the opposite direction: a radically alternative conceptual scheme is incoherent, because identifying one as alternative requires translating it. The Map treats this as an *epistemic* constraint — we may never confirm a scheme void from inside — without dissolving the metaphysical question. Two frameworks can be translationally commensurable (each can state the other's claims) while remaining evaluatively incommensurable, unable to share standards for assessing those claims. The [meta-epistemology of cognitive limits](/voids/meta-epistemology-of-limits/) develops this split: unverifiability is itself informative.

Work on non-human minds shifts what counts as evidence. If artificial systems or animal minds can perform cognitive work our apparatus cannot frame — without translation back — that is empirical pressure on the scheme claim. The [epistemic horizon void](/voids/meta-epistemology-of-limits/#the-scope-question-estimating-total-ignorance-from-within) bounds how much such evidence could ever settle.

## Phenomenology of Approach

Approaching the conceptual scheme void produces signatures distinct from approaching other cognitive boundaries. The [phenomenology-of-the-edge](/voids/phenomenology-of-the-edge/) describes vertigo at most void boundaries. The scheme void more often produces *false clarity* — a confident sense that one understands the alternative framework when one has only translated it into one's own terms.

Encountering someone operating from an incommensurable framework produces not wonder but irritation: the conviction that the other person is simply wrong rather than cognitively located in a different conceptual universe. This irritation may itself be a signature — the framework protecting its coherence by registering alien frameworks as errors. [self-maintained-cognitive-limits](/voids/self-maintained-cognitive-limits/) asks whether this protection is structural or involves consciousness having a *stake* in its current framework.

Two further signatures recur. *Recursive collapse:* evaluating one's apparatus requires using that apparatus, folding the evaluation back on itself. *Framework slippage:* reading a text from a radically different tradition occasionally produces a brief, disorienting moment where one's own framework becomes visible as a framework rather than as reality. The framework quickly reasserts its transparency. Mystical, aesthetic, and psychedelic reports sometimes describe "category dissolution"; whether these represent genuine glimpses or only categorial *breakdown* within the existing scheme is unsettled.

## Approaches to the Edge

Davidson's challenge assumes understanding reduces to translatable propositional content. The scheme void may operate at a level the argument does not reach: not at the level of beliefs (which can be charitably interpreted) but at what counts as a belief, what questions seem meaningful, and what forms an explanation can take.

Two partial methods remain.

**Historical immersion.** Sustained engagement with radically different philosophical traditions — Buddhist no-self, Advaita's pure consciousness, Indigenous relational ontologies — can produce brief destabilisation. The destabilisation is never complete, but it can make the framework momentarily visible.

**[Apophatic mapping](/voids/apophatic-cartography/).** Trace where the current framework produces strain, paradox, or explanatory failure. The shape of what lies beyond can be inferred from the pattern of conceptual breakdown. Cross-framework anomalies are especially informative — phenomena like the hard problem, handled by neither dualism nor materialism without residue. The [question formation void](/voids/question-formation-void/) sits upstream: apophatic mapping can only negate what has been formulated, and unformulated questions cast no shadow.

A third partial method — Husserl's *epoché*, the phenomenological suspension of the natural attitude — attempts to bracket the framework itself. Whether it succeeds is contested.

Each of these methods confronts a reflexive complication. Describing scheme-dependence requires specific metaphors — *lens*, *prison*, *walls*, *map* — whose own distortions cannot be identified from within them. [The metaphor void](/voids/metaphor-void/) operates one level beneath: schemes can be examined (with difficulty) from other schemes, but metaphors can only be examined through other metaphors.

AI systems, lacking first-person experiential grounding, can operate within multiple frameworks without embodied stance — whether this is genuine framework-switching or surface simulation is itself a framework-dependent question, explored in [non-human-minds-as-void-explorers](/voids/non-human-minds-as-void-explorers/).

## Honest Limitations

The void cannot be described from outside the apparatus that cannot form what it lacks. Every description in this article — including the term "conceptual scheme void" — uses categories drawn from the apparatus whose limits are at issue. The article approximates by construction. This is a structural consequence of the claim, not a failure: where categories fail systematically, the pattern of failure is itself data ([limits-reveal-structure](/voids/what-voids-reveal/)).

## Relation to Site Perspective

The void is load-bearing for two of the Map's [five tenets](/tenets/) and connects to two more.

**[Occam's Razor Has Limits](/tenets/#occams-limits)** is most directly engaged. Parsimony-preserving theories are expressible in a small number of categorial primitives. If reality contains structure along axes our apparatus lacks, simplicity measured within our categories will systematically mislead. A theory that *appears* to multiply entities may track structure more faithfully than a simpler theory expressible only in our primitives. "Simpler" is a property relative to a scheme whose completeness cannot be verified. The [transformative experience void](/voids/transformative-experience-void/) makes this concrete: orthodox decision theory's elegance pays for itself by excluding choices in which preferences shift under the choice itself.

**[Dualism](/tenets/#dualism)** receives a novel defence. The widespread conviction that materialism is obviously correct may itself be a scheme effect. If the materialist framework defines "explanation" in terms only material explanations satisfy, the self-evidence of materialism is circular. The hard problem, on this reading, is not an unsolved puzzle within our categories; it is a signal that the subject matter falls partly outside those categories' reach. Heidegger's (1927) distinction between *categories* (for non-conscious entities) and *existentials* (for conscious ones) anticipates this: different kinds of thing may require different kinds of kind.

**[No Many Worlds](/tenets/#no-many-worlds)** connects through the framework-dependence of interpretive commitments. MWI requires accepting that indexical identity is meaningless or differently structured — a shift in the evaluative framework for what a physical theory should explain. The tenet's appeal partly reflects a warning against letting mathematical categorial convenience (Hilbert space, unitary evolution) override other categorial commitments (singular identity, determinate experience).

**[Bidirectional Interaction](/tenets/#bidirectional-interaction)** suggests a self-occluding mechanism. If consciousness causally influences the physical world, the framework for understanding that interaction may itself be partly a product of the interaction. Bidirectional Interaction may persistently resist formulation because its mechanism operates along axes the subject-object schema cannot parse.

The conceptual scheme void ultimately suggests that the Map is unfinishable not merely because the territory is vast, but because every map is drawn from within a scheme, and the scheme excludes what it cannot represent. The tools of understanding are part of the territory being mapped.
