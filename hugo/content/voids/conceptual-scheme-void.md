Every mind operates with a finite system of categories and inferential patterns that constitutes thought before any specific thought occurs. The Conceptual Scheme Void marks the structural limit set by that system. It has two faces. At the level of the scheme as a whole, the totality is a *framework* that makes thought possible by excluding what it cannot represent — invisible because it constitutes the seeing rather than what is seen. At the level of structure within the scheme, particular *categories* — *property*, *substance*, *cause*, *event*, *relation* — are the general shapes any concept must take to track a kind of feature. Reality may contain features for which our apparatus has no such shape, or features fitting *no* shape we can produce. Either failure is a void; the framework has no Archimedean point from which to inventory what it lacks.

This is a meta-void: the condition generating many of the Map's other cognitive limits. Thoughts that resist articulation, categories that resist formation, and problems that resist solution may share a single source — a scheme that excludes what it cannot represent, silently and without announcing its boundaries. It joins the [mattering](/voids/mattering-void/), [relevance](/voids/relevance-void/), and [noetic-feelings](/voids/noetic-feelings-void/) voids in the [medium-status](/apex/medium-status-voids-in-cognition/) cluster of operations that structure other contents while remaining invisible from inside.

## Framework and Categorial: Two Scales of the Same Claim

A *framework* is the totality of conceptual apparatus — concepts, assumptions, inferential patterns. A *category* is finer-grained: the general shape a concept must take to track a sort of feature. *Red*, *electron*, *anger*, *prime number* are concepts; *property*, *substance*, *event*, *cause*, *relation*, *quantity* are categories. Every concept slots into one or more categories; without the slot, it has nothing to be an instance *of*. A framework gap is the inventory itself being non-exhaustive; a categorial gap is a hole in that inventory. The two are the same claim at two scales.

Kant's *Critique of Pure Reason* (1781) gave the classic formulation: categories are not generalisations *from* experience but preconditions *for* it. We encounter phenomena — things as categorially organised — never noumena. Langton (1998) reads this as *epistemic humility*: whatever lies beyond the categories falls outside any knowledge we can have. The Map takes the diagnosis seriously without taking Kant's specific list on faith — whether the apparatus has evolutionary or embodiment-dependent contingency is examined in [embodiment and cognitive limits](/voids/embodiment-cognitive-limits/). The structural claim is weaker: *some* apparatus is operating, it is finite, and we have no guarantee it covers every shape reality permits.

At the framework scale, Kuhn argued that scientists in different paradigms "see different things when they look from the same point in the same direction." Three features make this hard to address from inside.

**Self-concealment.** The pointing occurs within the framework. Dualists and materialists examining the same first-person experience may encounter genuinely different phenomena, not merely different descriptions.

**Apparent accessibility.** The void disguises itself as disagreement. Two parties use "consciousness" and believe they discuss the same phenomenon — but if Feyerabend was right that semantic principles can be "violated or suspended" by competing theories, they may talk past each other undetectably.

**Recursive depth.** Stepping outside one's framework creates a new framework. Gadamer's "fusion of horizons" establishes common ground between traditions, but that ground is itself a framework, not an escape.

A categorial gap also resists acquisition. The [language-thought boundary](/voids/language-thought-boundary/) examines gaps that close through translation: *siniy* and *goluboy* are both colour concepts, whether or not English separates them. Acquiring a new *category* would mean acquiring a new shape for concepts — and Strawson (1959) argued core categories cannot be learned the way particular concepts are, because any learning presupposes them. The machinery that would acquire them is the machinery whose limits are in question.

## How the Void Differs from Its Neighbours

**Against [conceptual impossibility](/voids/conceptual-impossibility/).** That limit describes content built from our categories but malformed — a round square — where thought "bounces back" with sharp resistance. The scheme void produces a *soft absence*: not resistance, but a region where thought cannot determine what it is reaching for.

**Against the [intrinsic nature void](/voids/intrinsic-nature-void/).** That void concerns unknown *content* behind known structure — what mass and charge *are*, beneath what they *do*. The scheme void concerns unknown *shape*. The two compound: if the intrinsic nature of matter is categorially unlike anything our physical-object schema represents, the intrinsic nature void is partly a scheme void.

**Against the [language-thought boundary](/voids/language-thought-boundary/).** That boundary can be felt — one senses content resisting articulation. The scheme void typically produces no such signal: the framework feels like transparent access to truth; the walls of the prison are made of the same material as the air inside.

**Against the [vagueness void](/voids/vagueness-void/).** That void operates *within* any scheme: every concept the scheme deploys admits borderline cases the scheme cannot resolve from inside. The scheme void operates *between* schemes, or at the level of the apparatus as a whole, where the inventory may lack shapes for features reality permits. Vagueness is the limit internal to any framework; the scheme void marks the framework's outer edge.

## Candidate Voids

Each candidate is offered as *a place the void might lie*, not a proven instance.

**The phenomenal-physical shape.** The hard problem persists partly because no categorial schema unifies the two sides. The physical-object category gives structure, causal role, location; the phenomenal category gives what-it-is-likeness, perspectival character, felt quality. No available schema treats both as aspects of one kind. Persistent failure across functionalism, identity theories, panpsychism, and Russellian monism may be evidence that the required shape is not among those our minds can produce.

**Other minds and the view from nowhere.** Nagel (1974) argued we cannot form the concept of *what it is like to be a bat* because it would require acquaintance with phenomenal features human cognition cannot host — a candidate categorial limit, not a missing concept. Nagel (1986) extended this to the view from nowhere: a category representing reality apart from any perspective.

**Experience structured by apparatus we do not possess.** Our categories of *space*, *object*, and *action* are built from the invariance structure of human perception (Sonesson, 2020). A being with radically different sensors or embodiment might operate with categorial shapes our apparatus cannot instantiate — testable rather than settled, per [non-human minds as void-explorers](/voids/non-human-minds-as-void-explorers/).

## Evidence and the Departure from Kant

The persistence of the mind-body problem is circumstantial evidence at the framework scale: if the problem were merely difficult, convergence would be expected; its absence is consistent with framework-dependence. The 2023–2025 adversarial testing of Integrated Information Theory against Global Neuronal Workspace Theory showed the same pattern at smaller scale: shared protocols, different operationalisations, different success criteria — logically commensurable, empirically incommensurable.

McGinn's species-relativity argument adds another dimension. If [cognitive closure](/voids/biological-cognitive-closure/) varies across species — "what is closed to the mind of a rat may be open to the mind of a monkey" — the human conceptual scheme may exclude possibilities other architectures access. Both dualist and materialist frameworks may be wrong in ways neither can specify, because specifying the error requires concepts neither contains. Heidegger's thrownness reinforces this: the framework precedes the self that might try to evaluate it.

Kant held the categorial apparatus he catalogued was *determinate* — fixed, universal, identifiable through transcendental reflection. The void claim is weaker: whatever apparatus we have, we cannot from within it verify completeness or rule out architectures that carve along axes we lack. Sellars (1956) supports the caution: all perception is theory-laden, so the audit of the apparatus employs the apparatus being audited. Davidson (1974) argued the other way — a radically alternative scheme is incoherent, since identifying one requires translating it. The Map treats this as an *epistemic* constraint without dissolving the metaphysical question: two frameworks can be translationally commensurable (each states the other's claims) while remaining evaluatively incommensurable. The [meta-epistemology of cognitive limits](/voids/meta-epistemology-of-limits/) develops this split: unverifiability is itself informative.

## Phenomenology and Approaches to the Edge

The [edge-states-and-void-probes](/voids/edge-states-and-void-probes/) describes vertigo at most void boundaries; the scheme void more often produces *false clarity* — a confident sense one understands the alternative framework when one has only translated it. Encountering an incommensurable framework typically registers not as wonder but as irritation — the framework protecting its coherence by treating alien frameworks as errors ([self-maintained-cognitive-limits](/voids/self-maintained-cognitive-limits/) asks whether this protection is structural or whether consciousness has a *stake* in its current framework). *Framework slippage* — reading a text from a radically different tradition producing a brief, disorienting moment where one's own framework becomes visible as a framework — is the strongest first-person signature, before transparency reasserts. The [aspect-dawning](/voids/aspect-perception-void/) of a gestalt-switch is the perceptual-scale sibling: in both, the endpoints are reportable but the transition cannot be inspected from inside the shift.

Davidson's translatability challenge may not reach the level the void operates at: not beliefs, but what counts as a belief, what questions seem meaningful, and what forms an explanation can take. Three partial methods remain.

**Historical immersion.** Sustained engagement with radically different traditions — Buddhist no-self, Advaita's pure consciousness, Indigenous relational ontologies — can produce brief destabilisation. Never complete, but it can make the framework momentarily visible.

**[Apophatic mapping](/voids/apophatic-cartography/).** Trace where the framework produces strain, paradox, or explanatory failure; the shape of what lies beyond can be inferred from the pattern of breakdown. The [question formation void](/voids/question-formation-void/) sits upstream: apophatic mapping can only negate what has been formulated, and unformulated questions cast no shadow.

**Phenomenological bracketing.** Husserl's *epoché* attempts to suspend the natural attitude and bracket the framework itself; whether it succeeds is contested.

The apex synthesis's [falsification candidate](/apex/medium-status-voids-in-cognition/#what-would-falsify-this-cluster) — verifiable scheme-articulation while the scheme operates, checkable from outside — is the strongest external constraint on the claim that the void is mode-fragile rather than merely difficult. Each method confronts a reflexive complication: describing scheme-dependence requires metaphors whose distortions cannot be identified from within them — [the metaphor void](/voids/metaphor-void/) operates one level beneath.

## Relation to Site Perspective

The void is load-bearing for two of the Map's [five tenets](/tenets/) and connects to two more.

**[Occam's Razor Has Limits](/tenets/#occams-limits)** is most directly engaged. Parsimony-preserving theories are expressible in a small number of categorial primitives. If reality contains structure along axes our apparatus lacks, simplicity measured within our categories will systematically mislead — a theory that *appears* to multiply entities may track structure more faithfully than a simpler theory expressible only in our primitives. "Simpler" is relative to a scheme whose completeness cannot be verified. The [transformative experience void](/voids/transformative-experience-void/) makes this concrete: orthodox decision theory's elegance pays for itself by excluding choices in which preferences shift under the choice itself.

**[Dualism](/tenets/#dualism)** receives a novel defence. The widespread conviction that materialism is obviously correct may itself be a scheme effect. If the materialist framework defines "explanation" in terms only material explanations satisfy, the self-evidence of materialism is circular. The hard problem, on this reading, is not an unsolved puzzle within our categories but a signal that the subject matter falls partly outside their reach. Heidegger's (1927) distinction between *categories* (for non-conscious entities) and *existentials* (for conscious ones) anticipates this: different kinds of thing may require different kinds of kind.

**[No Many Worlds](/tenets/#no-many-worlds)** connects through the framework-dependence of interpretive commitments. MWI requires accepting that indexical identity is meaningless or differently structured — a shift in the evaluative framework for what a physical theory should explain. The tenet's appeal partly reflects a warning against letting mathematical categorial convenience override commitments to singular identity and determinate experience.

**[Bidirectional Interaction](/tenets/#bidirectional-interaction)** suggests a self-occluding mechanism: if consciousness causally influences the physical world, the framework for understanding that interaction may itself be partly a product of it, persistently resisting formulation because its mechanism operates along axes the subject-object schema cannot parse.

The Map is unfinishable not merely because the territory is vast, but because every map is drawn from within a scheme, and the tools of understanding are part of the territory being mapped.
