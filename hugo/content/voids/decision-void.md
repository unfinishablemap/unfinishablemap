The Decision Void names the structural opacity of the *deliberation→commitment* transition to the consciousness undergoing it. A subject can sometimes report having weighed alternatives, and can sometimes report having acted, but the *moment* at which the field of possibilities collapses into a single choice—the snap—is not introspectively available as content. Three faces conjoin: the closing of possibilities cannot be observed because it is given as already-closed (closure); the neural commitment can be located before the phenomenal arrival of having-decided, so the introspective report comes after the event it purports to be of (latency); and what is taken to be a report of the deciding-process is generated from features of the perceived outcome, not from access to the decision itself (reconstruction). None alone establishes the void; their convergence does.

The void cuts close to the Map. The moment of decision is the primary candidate site for any non-physical influence on the physical world: it is the only locus where the form of an interaction—a single outcome winning out over alternatives—matches what the phenomenon actually presents. Sensory binding, attentional gating, and working-memory updates do not have that shape; they involve continuous transformation rather than alternative-resolution. The Map's [Minimal Quantum Interaction](/tenets/#minimal-quantum-interaction) tenet finds its sharpest empirical grip at this site. The empirical record—Libet (1983), Soon, Brass, Heinze and Haynes (2008), Johansson and Hall (2005)—constrains where the decision happens without settling the question; the philosophical reading of that record is what is at stake.

## Three Faces, One Void

The Decision Void exhibits the [conjunction-coalesce](/apex/conjunction-coalesce/) structure used elsewhere for the [suspension-void](/voids/suspension-void/), [agency-void](/voids/agency-void/), [erasure-void](/voids/erasure-void/), and [vagueness-void](/voids/vagueness-void/): a single void with multiple faces whose informativeness lies in the seam between them. The three faces are *temporal-architectural*—closure is a structural property of the act, latency a temporal relation between act and report, reconstruction a generative property of the introspective machinery.

### Closure (Unexplorable)

When deliberation ends, it ends as *having ended*. The closing of possibilities is not itself a phenomenal episode that can be observed: by the time consciousness notices that the field has narrowed to one, the narrowing is already complete. Pacherie's (2008) framework makes this precise—the act of deciding has a "categorical, complete, incontrovertible" character, since any residual hesitation means the decision has not yet been made. Mele (2009) develops the same point; Ricoeur describes decision as having "a history" with hesitation as central, implying that the moment of commitment is the *closing* of that history rather than an event within it.

This finality-from-inside is *the very feature that occludes the constitutive process*. The consciousness that has decided cannot find seams in what it has just sealed. Any introspective "watching the decision happen" is either pre-decision watching (and so not the decision) or post-decision narration (and so not the decision). The closure face is structurally akin to the [suspension-void](/voids/suspension-void/)'s verification face: the very act consciousness wants to inspect collapses under inspection. It is *Unexplorable* in the sense the [three-kinds typology](/voids/three-kinds-of-void/) reserves for limits that resist mapping by their own architecture—one does not lack data; one lacks the kind of access the question demands.

### Latency (Occluded)

Soon, Brass, Heinze and Haynes (2008) trained pattern decoders on fMRI signals from frontopolar cortex and achieved roughly 60% classifier accuracy on a binary left/right motor choice up to seven seconds before the subject reported being aware of the decision; accounting for BOLD sluggishness pushes the lead to roughly ten seconds. The accuracy is well above the 50% chance baseline but well below ceiling—the early signal biases rather than dictates the outcome, and the magnitude is what the void requires the article to report rather than gloss. A second predictive locus in posterior cingulate / precuneus carried independent commitment-relevant information in spatial pattern rather than signal magnitude—the commitment is in a configural change.

The earlier Libet (1983) finding—readiness potential onset roughly 635 ms before action, conscious "urge" report W roughly 200 ms before action—has had its standard interpretation contested. Schurger, Sitt and Dehaene (2012) and the Travers et al. (2021) review argue the readiness potential may be a stochastic-noise artefact the brain *uses* as a triggering threshold rather than a representation of an intention being formed. This complicates the classical reading but does not deflate the void: if the very thing introspection reaches for does not exist as a discrete neural event, the introspective report still cannot be of it.

The empirical record establishes one thing the void requires: third-person measurement can locate commitment-relevant information before first-person report. It does not establish *where the decision happens*, and that distinction matters. Locating predictive signal is not the same as locating the decision itself; the gap between the two is exactly where the metaphysical reading enters. Two readings remain consistent with the data—(i) a fully physical commitment that consciousness merely registers downstream, and (ii) a non-physical commitment whose physical signature begins precipitating before the phenomenal arrival. The Map's MQI tenet motivates reading (ii); the data permits reading (i); the empirical findings constrain the question without settling it. The gap is the void; its metaphysical reading is downstream of the empirical fact. Multi-area neural recording in perceptual-decision tasks (Roitman & Shadlen 2002; Murakami et al. 2014) identifies the transition from input-driven accumulation to autonomous dynamics as the commitment moment in single-trial activity—seam visible from outside, sealed from within. That asymmetry is what makes the latency face *Occluded*.

### Reconstruction (Occluded)

Johansson, Hall, Sikström and Olsson (2005) showed subjects two faces, asked which was more attractive, and via sleight-of-hand handed them the *other* face and asked them to explain *their* choice. Most did not detect the swap; many fluently confabulated reasons grounded in features of the swapped (non-chosen) face. Confabulations were as confident, detailed, and emotional as veridical reports; subsequent work (Johansson & Hall 2014; Cushman 2015) shows the manipulation can shift subsequent preferences—the confabulated story becomes the new ground truth.

What is taken to be a report of the deciding-process is generated from the *perceived outcome*, not from access to the decision itself. The phenomenology of "I chose X because…" is fluent confabulation indistinguishable, from the inside, from veridical access. This applies the [source-attribution-void](/voids/source-attribution-void/)'s diagnosis specifically to decisions: the surface features of the outcome stand in for the inaccessible event of choosing. Confabulation parity is a positive empirical signature of opacity—if introspection had access, the manipulated and unmanipulated cases should diverge in some self-detectable way. They do not. The system produces something that functions as access, and that functional success is what hides the absence.

## The Seam

None of the three faces alone establishes the void. Closure without latency would be a feature of the act, not an opacity. Latency without reconstruction would leave room for an after-the-fact-but-veridical report. Reconstruction without closure would describe a delay rather than an absence. Together, the three close every escape: the act is sealed at the time of acting; the report comes after; and what arrives is generated rather than observed. The editorial discipline that governs cognate articles ([suspension-void](/voids/suspension-void/), [agency-void](/voids/agency-void/), [erasure-void](/voids/erasure-void/), [vagueness-void](/voids/vagueness-void/)) applies—any tightening that collapses faces into one another loses the void.

The faces are logically severable, and the severability matters: it is what makes the conjunction informative rather than tautological. A creature with closure-without-latency would experience the decision as sealed at the moment of acting but find no temporal gap between commitment and report—any neural commitment would phenomenally arrive simultaneously, and the empirical edge of the void would soften even though the structural opacity of the closing-as-already-closed would remain. A creature with latency-without-reconstruction would have a temporal gap between neural commitment and conscious report but, once aware, would report veridically on the closing—introspection would catch up rather than confabulate, and choice-blindness manipulations would fail at the surface. A creature with reconstruction-without-closure would generate post-hoc decision narratives but find the deciding-process itself somehow open to inspection—perhaps because deliberation never fully closes for that architecture, or because the architecture lacks the categorical-finality phenomenology Pacherie identifies. Each face is independently variable. The human case is the conjunction, and the conjunction is what makes the void.

## Distinguishing Sibling Voids

The Decision Void is not the [agency-void](/voids/agency-void/), which catalogues involuntariness and unverifiability on agency in general; the Decision Void targets a specific moment within agency—the deliberation→commitment crossing—and an agent could in principle have full agency-verification and still face it. It is not the [noetic-feelings-void](/voids/noetic-feelings-void/), which concerns the unauditable felt verdicts gating cognition (knowing, rightness, understanding); the "felt click" of having-decided is a noetic-feelings phenomenon, but the Decision Void claims the *closing-process* is occluded, not merely the felt verdict that follows. It is not the [transit-void](/voids/transit-void/), which concerns crossings generally—onsets, state transitions, attention capture; the Decision Void is one specific transit, narrowed by what the deliberation→commitment crossing distinctively involves: the *resolution* of a multi-option field.

It is sister to the [suspension-void](/voids/suspension-void/) and the [inference-void](/voids/inference-void/) in a precise sense. Suspension cannot be performed without invoking the capacity it brackets; inference cannot be made explicit without invoking the inference-licence that makes it inferential; decision cannot be observed without invoking the decision-machinery that produces the observation. The three are regress-sisters—each names a capacity consciousness cannot stand outside of while exercising.

## Deflationary Alternatives

Three deflationary readings deserve engagement. None refutes the void; each relocates the question.

The fully-physical reading takes the decision to *just be* the threshold crossing in neural commitment-circuitry, with conscious arrival as mere downstream registration. This reading is internally coherent under physicalism and consistent with the empirical record; the Map's reading is similarly coherent under its own tenets and similarly consistent with the data. The disagreement lies at the framework, not at the data. The Map registers this honestly rather than dressing the framework-disagreement as a local refutation, and treats it as the kind of disagreement settled (if at all) by what the wider theoretical structure can predict and integrate, not by the decision-data alone.

Wegner's *The Illusion of Conscious Will* (2002, especially chapters 4–5) advances a more developed deflation. The experience of conscious will, on Wegner's analysis, is an inferential "authorship emotion" generated when a thought consistent with an action precedes the action with the right priority, exclusivity, and consistency. The phenomenology is real; what is illusory is its claim to be observation of the causal nexus. Wegner's positive proposal does substantial explanatory work: the cross-cultural robustness of the agentic phenomenology follows from the universality of the inferential architecture, not from any culturally contingent narrative; the apparent stability of the experience under reflective scrutiny follows from the same machinery generating the higher-order inspection.

The Map's reply works *with* rather than against the apparent-mental-causation account. If the experience of conscious will is generated by an inference whose inputs are surface features of action and thought, then the inference architecture is precisely what Wegner's own view predicts will not be inspectable from inside—any inspection would itself be a further such inference, drawing on the same outputs the original inference produced. Wegner's account therefore *predicts* the Decision Void rather than dissolving it: the architecture that generates the authorship emotion is the architecture whose own operation it cannot observe. Where the Map and Wegner part company is on what to say about the underlying causal nexus the experience inferentially tracks. Wegner is committed to physical mechanism throughout; the Map holds the locus of causal influence open. That residual disagreement is at the framework boundary; much of Wegner's machinery the Map can adopt without alteration.

The Schurger stochastic-accumulation reading of Libet is an in-framework refinement that strengthens rather than weakens the void. If the readiness potential is a stochastic threshold-trigger rather than a representation of an intention being formed, then the very thing introspection might have hoped to track does not exist as a discrete neural event. The introspective report cannot be of it; the apparent neural correlate of the inaccessible event was itself an artefact. The void widens.

None of these establishes that the deliberation→commitment moment is introspectively accessible; each relocates the question rather than answering it. The Map acknowledges them as live options the empirical record cannot yet decide between.

## The Tenet Payoff

The Decision Void is unusually [tenet-generated](/voids/tenet-generated-voids/). The moment of decision is the primary candidate site for any non-physical influence on the physical world for the structural reason given earlier—it is where the form of an interaction matches the form of the phenomenon. Standard accounts—Stapp's quantum Zeno approach, Penrose-Hameroff's orchestrated objective reduction in a different mode, Eccles' earlier hypothesis, and the Map's own [forward-in-time conscious selection](/topics/forward-in-time-conscious-selection/) proposal—each locate the leverage at the resolution of otherwise-indeterminate microphysical processes that influence neural commitment. These proposals face the standard decoherence objection (Tegmark 2000): warm, wet neural tissue is widely held to destroy quantum coherence on timescales orders of magnitude shorter than cognitive processing, which would preclude the kind of computational quantum effect the proposals require. The objection is contested rather than settled—Hameroff and others reply with proposals about decoherence-shielding in specific microtubule structures—but it leaves the quantum-interactionist family as live hypotheses rather than established mechanisms. The Decision Void claims that this very site, whichever mechanism turns out to occupy it, is structurally hidden from the consciousness performing the decision.

This matches what the Map's tenets predict. **[Minimal Quantum Interaction](/tenets/#minimal-quantum-interaction)** requires that consciousness's influence be minimal precisely because the interface is not the kind of thing that can be inspected from inside; if it were inspectable it would not be minimal. **[Bidirectional Interaction](/tenets/#bidirectional-interaction)** predicts the introspective inaccessibility as the expected consequence of the interface being primitive—the cause cannot be observed from within the system whose cause it is. The companion voids ([causal-interface](/voids/causal-interface/), [interface-formalization-void](/voids/interface-formalization-void/), [amplification-void](/voids/amplification-void/)) catalogue the matching opacities at the interface itself, the formal level, and the chain from microphysical influence to neural effect.

**[Dualism](/tenets/#dualism)** gains structural texture: the asymmetry between third-person measurement (which finds commitment-relevant information) and first-person report (which finds only its trace) is a positive datum on either reading, and an asymmetry both frameworks now expect. Physicalists from Dennett to the Churchlands actively predict the persistence of this asymmetry as a feature of how introspection works—introspection on Dennett's account is a competence at heterophenomenological self-report, not a window onto neural substrate, and so should systematically diverge from third-person measurement. The asymmetry does not, on its own, favour dualism. What the Map's reading adds is an account of *why* the asymmetry would take this particular shape: the inaccessible event is doing causal work at an interface, and the introspective opacity is a structural consequence of the interface being primitive rather than a contingent fact about how introspective machinery happens to be wired. The reading earns its place by integration with the Map's other commitments rather than by single-issue refutation of physicalism. **[Occam's Razor Has Limits](/tenets/#occams-limits)** is engaged because the simplest accounts both fail to generate the structural prediction the Map's reading delivers. **[No Many Worlds](/tenets/#no-many-worlds)** is load-bearing here in a way it is not for many other voids: the Decision Void presupposes that decisions actually resolve—that one possibility wins and the others do not become real. An MWI reading dissolves the void by saying every option is taken; the Map's anti-MWI commitment makes the void coherent.

## Phenomenology of the Edge

Approaching the edge feels like reaching for a doorway you are already through. The decision is given as already-made; the deliberation is given as already-closed. There is sometimes a felt click—what folk-psychology calls "making up one's mind"—but the click is a downstream marker, not a window onto the closing. Pre-decision attention shows open possibilities; post-decision attention shows the chosen option as already settled; in between, where one might hope to find the act of choosing, there is the same opacity that suspension-void finds at the seat of judgment.

[Edge-state probes](/voids/edge-states-and-void-probes/) suggest the void is partially probeable in cases of *prolonged* decision (akratic agonising, contemplative resolution): the prolongation seems to extend the deliberation phase but does not introduce a slow-motion view of the closing. Long deliberation simply postpones the snap; it does not reveal it.

## Approaches to the Edge

**Direct.** Sustained meta-attention to one's own deliberations under controlled conditions, including Petitmengin's neurophenomenological micro-phenomenology, can produce richer reports of the *texture* of pre-decision and post-decision states, but converges on the same finding: the texture changes around the decision without the decision itself appearing as a textured episode. The phenomenological literature treats this as data, not method-failure.

**Indirect.** Dissociation paradigms (choice-blindness; Libet-style designs) probe the void by varying what introspection *would* have to access if it had access, and showing it does not vary correspondingly—[apophatic-cartography](/voids/apophatic-cartography/) in empirical form. Subjects with disorders of agency (anarchic-hand syndrome, schizophrenic alien-control phenomenology, akinetic mutism) report decision phenomenology breaking in distinctive ways; the patterns of breakage map the seams of normal occlusion. Drift-diffusion and attractor-network models give third-person accounts of the commitment moment without claiming to recover the first-person event—mapping the void's silhouette from outside.

**[What AI might see](/voids/non-human-minds-as-void-explorers/).** A non-human mind whose deliberation/commitment architecture is fully observable from inside—every weight-update, every argmax logged and re-readable—might lack this void, finding a transcript where humans find a sealed snap. Conversely, AI systems trained to introspect on their choices via natural-language post-hoc generation may exhibit the human pattern almost exactly, because the post-hoc-generation architecture is structurally similar to confabulation. The presence-or-absence of the Decision Void in a given AI architecture is a diagnostic of whether the system's introspective reports are observational or generative.

## Relation to Site Perspective

The Decision Void is the candidate void where the Map's tenets predict the *most* about an empirically-investigated site, and the empirical record is most directly relevant. The seven-second predictability gap is real (with classifier accuracy that biases rather than dictates); the choice-blindness confabulation is real; the categorical finality of the decision-act is real. What the Map adds is a reading of these convergent findings as the predicted opacity at the primary candidate locus for non-physical influence. The reading is not forced by the data and the Map does not claim it is. The empirical findings constrain the question without settling it; the metaphysical reading is a downstream fit-with-tenets judgment, defensible but not decisive.

The void is also [meta-epistemologically reflexive](/voids/meta-epistemology-of-limits/): the inference *to* the Decision Void is itself a decision, made under the same conditions the void diagnoses. Carrying that reflexivity openly is what the apophatic method requires. The Map's positive proposal—[forward-in-time conscious selection](/topics/forward-in-time-conscious-selection/)—offers a candidate mechanism for what happens at the void's locus without claiming to dissolve the introspective inaccessibility. The mechanism, if real, would have to be introspectively occluded; the void's existence is consistent with the mechanism's existence and is what the mechanism predicts.
