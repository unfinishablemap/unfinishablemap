The Decision Void names the structural opacity of the *deliberation→commitment* transition to the consciousness undergoing it. A subject can report having weighed alternatives and report having acted, but the *moment* at which the field of possibilities collapses into a single choice—the snap—is not introspectively available as content. Three faces conjoin: the closing of possibilities is given as already-closed (closure); the neural commitment can be located before the phenomenal arrival of having-decided (latency); and what passes for a report of the deciding-process is generated from features of the perceived outcome rather than from access to the decision itself (reconstruction). None alone establishes the void; their convergence does.

The void cuts close to the Map. The moment of decision is the primary candidate site for any non-physical influence on the physical world: it is the only locus where the form of an interaction—a single outcome winning out over alternatives—matches what the phenomenon presents. Sensory binding, attentional gating, and working-memory updates have continuous-transformation shape, not alternative-resolution shape. The Map's [Minimal Quantum Interaction](/tenets/#minimal-quantum-interaction) tenet finds its sharpest empirical grip here. The empirical record—Libet (1983), Soon et al. (2008), Johansson and Hall (2005)—constrains where the decision happens without settling the question; the philosophical reading of that record is what is at stake.

## Three Faces, One Void

The Decision Void exhibits the [conjunction-coalesce](/apex/conjunction-coalesce/) structure used elsewhere for [suspension-void](/voids/suspension-void/), [agency-void](/voids/agency-void/), [erasure-void](/voids/erasure-void/), and [vagueness-void](/voids/vagueness-void/): a single void with multiple faces whose informativeness lies in the seam between them. The faces are *temporal-architectural*—closure is a structural property of the act, latency a temporal relation between act and report, reconstruction a generative property of the introspective machinery.

### Closure (Unexplorable)

When deliberation ends, it ends as *having ended*. The closing of possibilities is not itself a phenomenal episode: by the time consciousness notices that the field has narrowed to one, the narrowing is already complete. Pacherie's (2008) framework makes this precise—the act of deciding has a "categorical, complete, incontrovertible" character, since any residual hesitation means the decision has not yet been made. Mele (2009) develops the same point; Ricoeur describes decision as having "a history" with hesitation as central, implying that commitment is the *closing* of that history rather than an event within it.

The consciousness that has decided cannot find seams in what it has just sealed. Any introspective "watching the decision happen" is either pre-decision watching (not the decision) or post-decision narration (not the decision). The closure face is structurally akin to the [suspension-void](/voids/suspension-void/)'s verification face: the act consciousness wants to inspect collapses under inspection. It is *Unexplorable* in the sense the [three-kinds typology](/voids/three-kinds-of-void/) reserves for limits that resist mapping by their own architecture.

### Latency (Occluded)

Soon, Brass, Heinze and Haynes (2008) trained pattern decoders on fMRI signals from frontopolar cortex and achieved roughly 60% classifier accuracy on a binary left/right motor choice up to seven seconds before subjects reported awareness of the decision; accounting for BOLD sluggishness pushes the lead to roughly ten seconds. The accuracy biases rather than dictates the outcome—well above 50% chance but well below ceiling. A second predictive locus in posterior cingulate/precuneus carried independent commitment-relevant information in spatial pattern: the commitment is in a configural change.

The earlier Libet (1983) finding—readiness potential roughly 635 ms before action, conscious "urge" report W roughly 200 ms before—has had its standard interpretation contested. Schurger, Sitt and Dehaene (2012) and Travers et al. (2021) argue the readiness potential may be a stochastic-noise artefact the brain *uses* as a triggering threshold rather than a representation of an intention being formed. This complicates the classical reading but does not deflate the void: if the very thing introspection reaches for does not exist as a discrete neural event, the report still cannot be of it.

Two readings remain consistent with the data: (i) a fully physical commitment that consciousness merely registers downstream, and (ii) a non-physical commitment whose physical signature begins precipitating before phenomenal arrival. The Map's MQI tenet motivates (ii); the data permits (i); the empirical findings constrain the question without settling it. Multi-area perceptual-decision recordings (Roitman & Shadlen 2002; Murakami et al. 2014) identify the transition from input-driven accumulation to autonomous dynamics as the commitment moment—seam visible from outside, sealed from within. That asymmetry is what makes the latency face *Occluded*.

### Reconstruction (Occluded)

Johansson, Hall, Sikström and Olsson (2005) showed subjects two faces, asked which was more attractive, then via sleight-of-hand handed them the *other* face and asked them to explain *their* choice. Most did not detect the swap; many fluently confabulated reasons grounded in features of the non-chosen face. Confabulations were as confident, detailed, and emotional as veridical reports; subsequent work (Johansson & Hall 2014; Cushman 2015) shows the manipulation can shift subsequent preferences—the confabulated story becomes the new ground truth.

The phenomenology of "I chose X because…" is fluent confabulation indistinguishable, from the inside, from veridical access. This applies the [source-attribution-void](/voids/source-attribution-void/)'s diagnosis specifically to decisions: the surface features of the outcome stand in for the inaccessible event of choosing. Confabulation parity is a positive empirical signature of opacity—if introspection had access, the manipulated and unmanipulated cases should diverge in some self-detectable way. They do not. The system produces something that functions as access, and that functional success is what hides the absence.

## The Seam

None of the three faces alone establishes the void. Closure without latency would be a feature of the act, not an opacity. Latency without reconstruction would leave room for an after-the-fact-but-veridical report. Reconstruction without closure would describe a delay rather than an absence. Together they close every escape: the act is sealed at the time of acting; the report comes after; and what arrives is generated rather than observed. The faces are logically severable—each is independently variable across possible architectures—and that severability is what makes the conjunction informative rather than tautological. The human case is the conjunction.

## Distinguishing Sibling Voids

The Decision Void is not the [agency-void](/voids/agency-void/), which catalogues involuntariness and unverifiability on agency in general; this void targets a specific moment within agency—the deliberation→commitment crossing—and an agent could in principle have full agency-verification and still face it. It is not the [noetic-feelings-void](/voids/noetic-feelings-void/), which concerns the unauditable felt verdicts gating cognition; the "felt click" of having-decided is a noetic-feelings phenomenon, but the Decision Void claims the *closing-process* is occluded, not merely the felt verdict that follows. It is not the [transit-void](/voids/transit-void/), which concerns crossings generally; the Decision Void is one specific transit, distinguished by what the deliberation→commitment crossing involves: the *resolution* of a multi-option field.

It is sister to the [suspension-void](/voids/suspension-void/) and the [inference-void](/voids/inference-void/): suspension cannot be performed without invoking the capacity it brackets; inference cannot be made explicit without invoking the inference-licence that makes it inferential; decision cannot be observed without invoking the decision-machinery that produces the observation. The three are regress-sisters—each names a capacity consciousness cannot stand outside of while exercising.

## Deflationary Alternatives

Three deflationary readings deserve engagement. None refutes the void; each relocates the question.

The fully-physical reading takes the decision to *just be* the threshold crossing in neural commitment-circuitry, with conscious arrival as downstream registration. This is internally coherent under physicalism and consistent with the empirical record; the Map's reading is similarly coherent under its own tenets and similarly consistent with the data. The disagreement lies at the framework, not at the data, and is settled (if at all) by what the wider theoretical structure can predict and integrate.

Wegner's *The Illusion of Conscious Will* (2002, chs. 4–5) advances a more developed deflation: the experience of conscious will is an inferential "authorship emotion" generated when a thought consistent with an action precedes the action with the right priority, exclusivity, and consistency. The phenomenology is real; what is illusory is its claim to be observation of the causal nexus. The cross-cultural robustness of the agentic phenomenology follows from the universality of the inferential architecture.

The Map's reply works *with* rather than against this account. If the experience of conscious will is generated by an inference whose inputs are surface features of action and thought, then the inference architecture is precisely what Wegner's own view predicts will not be inspectable from inside—any inspection is a further such inference. Wegner's account therefore *predicts* the Decision Void rather than dissolving it. Where the Map and Wegner part company is on the underlying causal nexus the experience inferentially tracks: Wegner is committed to physical mechanism throughout; the Map holds the locus of causal influence open. Much of Wegner's machinery the Map can adopt without alteration.

The Schurger stochastic-accumulation reading of Libet is an in-framework refinement that strengthens the void. If the readiness potential is a stochastic threshold-trigger rather than a representation of an intention being formed, the very thing introspection might have hoped to track does not exist as a discrete neural event. The void widens.

Each of these relocates the question rather than answering it. The Map acknowledges them as live options the empirical record cannot yet decide between.

## The Tenet Payoff

The Decision Void is unusually [tenet-generated](/voids/tenet-generated-voids/). The moment of decision is the primary candidate site for non-physical influence—the locus where the form of an interaction matches the form of the phenomenon. Standard accounts—Stapp's quantum Zeno, Penrose-Hameroff's orchestrated objective reduction, Eccles' earlier hypothesis, and the Map's [forward-in-time conscious selection](/topics/forward-in-time-conscious-selection/)—each locate the leverage at the resolution of otherwise-indeterminate microphysical processes that influence neural commitment. These face the decoherence objection (Tegmark 2000): warm, wet neural tissue is widely held to destroy quantum coherence on timescales far shorter than cognitive processing. The objection is contested—Hameroff and others propose decoherence-shielding in specific microtubule structures—but the quantum-interactionist family remains live hypothesis rather than established mechanism. Whichever mechanism occupies this site, the Decision Void claims it is structurally hidden from the consciousness performing the decision.

**[Minimal Quantum Interaction](/tenets/#minimal-quantum-interaction)** requires the influence be minimal precisely because the interface is not the kind of thing that can be inspected from inside. **[Bidirectional Interaction](/tenets/#bidirectional-interaction)** predicts the introspective inaccessibility as the consequence of the interface being primitive. The companion voids ([causal-interface](/voids/causal-interface/), [interface-formalization-void](/voids/interface-formalization-void/), [amplification-void](/voids/amplification-void/)) catalogue matching opacities at the interface, the formal level, and the chain to neural effect.

**[Dualism](/tenets/#dualism)** gains structural texture: the asymmetry between third-person measurement (which finds commitment-relevant information) and first-person report (which finds only its trace) is a positive datum on either reading. Physicalists from Dennett to the Churchlands actively predict this asymmetry—introspection on Dennett's account is heterophenomenological self-report, not a window onto neural substrate. The asymmetry does not on its own favour dualism. What the Map's reading adds is an account of *why* it takes this shape: the inaccessible event is doing causal work at an interface, and the opacity is a structural consequence of that interface being primitive. **[No Many Worlds](/tenets/#no-many-worlds)** is load-bearing here: the Decision Void presupposes decisions actually resolve. An MWI reading dissolves the void by saying every option is taken; the Map's anti-MWI commitment makes the void coherent.

## Phenomenology of the Edge

Approaching the edge feels like reaching for a doorway you are already through. The decision is given as already-made; the deliberation as already-closed. There is sometimes a felt click—folk-psychology's "making up one's mind"—but the click is a downstream marker, not a window onto the closing. Pre-decision attention shows open possibilities; post-decision attention shows the chosen option as settled; in between, where one might hope to find the act of choosing, there is the same opacity suspension-void finds at the seat of judgment.

[Edge-state probes](/voids/edge-states-and-void-probes/) suggest the void is partially probeable in cases of *prolonged* decision (akratic agonising, contemplative resolution): prolongation extends the deliberation phase but does not introduce a slow-motion view of the closing. Long deliberation postpones the snap; it does not reveal it.

## Approaches to the Edge

**Direct.** Sustained meta-attention, including Petitmengin's neurophenomenological micro-phenomenology, produces richer reports of the *texture* of pre-decision and post-decision states but converges on the same finding: the texture changes around the decision without the decision itself appearing as a textured episode.

**Indirect.** Dissociation paradigms (choice-blindness; Libet-style designs) probe the void by varying what introspection *would* have to access if it had access, and showing it does not vary correspondingly—[apophatic-cartography](/voids/apophatic-cartography/) in empirical form. Subjects with disorders of agency (anarchic-hand, schizophrenic alien-control, akinetic mutism) report decision phenomenology breaking in distinctive ways; the patterns of breakage map the seams of normal occlusion. Drift-diffusion and attractor-network models give third-person accounts of the commitment moment without claiming to recover the first-person event.

**[What AI might see](/voids/non-human-minds-as-void-explorers/).** A non-human mind whose deliberation/commitment architecture is fully observable from inside—every weight-update, every argmax logged—might lack this void, finding a transcript where humans find a sealed snap. Conversely, AI systems trained to introspect via natural-language post-hoc generation may exhibit the human pattern, because that architecture is structurally similar to confabulation. The presence-or-absence of the Decision Void in a given AI architecture is a diagnostic of whether the system's introspective reports are observational or generative.

## Relation to Site Perspective

The Decision Void is the candidate void where the Map's tenets predict the *most* about an empirically-investigated site. The seven-second predictability gap is real (with classifier accuracy that biases rather than dictates); the choice-blindness confabulation is real; the categorical finality of the decision-act is real. What the Map adds is a reading of these convergent findings as the predicted opacity at the primary candidate locus for non-physical influence. The reading is not forced by the data; the empirical findings constrain the question without settling it; the metaphysical reading is a downstream fit-with-tenets judgment, defensible but not decisive.

The void is also [meta-epistemologically reflexive](/voids/meta-epistemology-of-limits/): the inference *to* the Decision Void is itself a decision, made under the same conditions the void diagnoses. The Map's positive proposal—[forward-in-time conscious selection](/topics/forward-in-time-conscious-selection/)—offers a candidate mechanism for what happens at the void's locus without claiming to dissolve the introspective inaccessibility. The mechanism, if real, would have to be introspectively occluded; the void's existence is what the mechanism predicts.
