Consciousness must decide, continuously and in real time, whether a given sensory or quasi-sensory content is externally generated (perception) or self-generated (imagery, expectation, prediction). It performs this discrimination without exposing the operation to introspection: what arrives is the verdict — *real* or *imagined* — together with a felt-real signal calibrated to the verdict rather than to the truth of it. The Unfinishable Map names the **perceptual reality-monitoring void** for the structural opacity around this operation. The discriminator is sub-personal and inspectable only by its outputs; the felt-real label is a constructed signal, not a direct read of reality.

The void is primarily **occluded** (the operation is hidden behind its verdict) with a strong **unexplorable** component (the criteria the operation would need to use overlap structurally with what it would need to distinguish). It sits adjacent to but distinct from the [source-attribution void](/voids/source-attribution-void/) (memory-time origin inference), the [imagery void](/voids/imagery-void/) (the structure of imagery itself), and the [predictive-construction void](/voids/predictive-construction-void/) (the standing architectural construct/receive boundary). This void is the moment-by-moment operational face of that broader architecture, explored further [below](#relation-to-the-predictive-construction-void).

## The Three Faces of the Void

Three converging empirical signatures make the void inescapable rather than merely difficult. They are independent lines that all point at the same structural absence.

### The Perky Face

In 1910, Cheves Perky asked subjects to fixate a point and visualise objects while, unbeknownst to them, a faint patch of coloured light was back-projected onto the same point. Subjects mistook the projected image for their own mental imagery, sometimes describing details that came from the projection — taking "what they 'saw' on the screen to be entirely the products of their imagination" (Perky 1910, as summarised in the *Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy* entry on mental imagery).

Segal and Fusella's 1970 replication added two findings. The effect required relaxation induction — earlier replications had failed because subjects spontaneously noticed the projected stimulus, so the void requires a specific epistemic posture to manifest. The crossover was also modality-specific: visual imagery selectively impaired visual detection, auditory imagery selectively impaired auditory detection. The confusion is substrate-locked, not abstract.

The Perky face is the founding empirical demonstration that the discriminator can fail. The effect is over a century old, robustly replicable, and exploited by lucid-dreaming reality-check apps on the assumption that the same architecture operates between waking imagery and dream content.

### The Confidence-Decoupling Face

Could metacognition rescue the discrimination? Mazor, Friston and Fleming (2020) had already shown that metacognition makes distinct neural contributions to detecting a stimulus versus discriminating its content — confidence about *whether* something is there draws on different machinery than confidence about *what* it is, so high confidence in presence does not transfer to reliable source-discrimination. Dijkstra and Fleming (2024) tested the discrimination case directly in *Journal of Vision* with hierarchical Bayesian modelling of perceptual-decision and confidence data. Confidence does not distinguish imagination from reality. Confidence criteria moved in tandem with decision-criterion shifts when congruent imagery was present, and congruent imagery was associated with an increase in *high-confidence* false alarms. Dijkstra and Fleming describe this as "a failure of reality monitoring not only at a perceptual but also at a metacognitive level."

The same imagery that biases the first-order verdict biases the second-order evaluation of the first-order verdict. Two layers fail with the same bias. The felt-real signal does not warn of its own miscalibration.

### The Pathological-Equivalence Face

The felt-real signal can dissociate from veridical perception in both directions. In hallucination, the signal is present without external cause; in derealization, external cause is present without the signal. Fazekas (2021) treats hallucinations as intensified forms of mind-wandering, arguing that heightened sensory activity can drive reality judgments toward *real* even when metacognitive monitoring remains intact — locating clinical hallucination on a continuum with ordinary spontaneous-thought processing rather than as a discontinuous failure. Billon (2024) analyses the converse case in derealization: sensorimotor processing remains intact but the felt-real signal detaches, and patients perceive accurately yet experience the world as unreal.

Corlett et al. (2014) supply the dream-state version. Their counter-intuitive finding — that individuals with higher dream awareness commit *more* confabulation errors at waking — suggests reality-monitoring is a balance whose weights cannot be set independently from inside. Lucid dreamers gain in-dream insight at the cost of waking source-monitoring. The pathologies together demonstrate that the felt-real signal is a *separable* construction, neither necessary nor sufficient for veridical perception.

## The Phases the Discriminator Must Perform

The operation has three sub-personal phases, none visible to consciousness. The framework decomposing reality-monitoring into these neural sub-components follows Dijkstra, Kok and Fleming (2022), whose review dissociates the mechanisms by which the brain separates imagination from reality:

1. **Source assessment.** Estimating, from the current pattern, how much sensory activity is externally driven versus internally generated. The 2025 *Neuron* paper "A neural basis for distinguishing imagination from reality" reports that activity in the fusiform gyrus "predict[s] confusions between imagery and perception on a trial-by-trial basis." The discriminator uses a continuous activity-level signal the experiencer never accesses directly.
2. **Threshold application.** Deciding, given the noisy estimate, whether the verdict crosses into *real* or stays in *imagined*. The 2025 *Neuron* paper identifies a frontal network that encodes binary judgments of reality and interacts with the fusiform signal. The threshold is a decision-event with the same opacity as other operation-voids — sister to [decision](/voids/decision-void/), [transit](/voids/transit-void/), [inference](/voids/inference-void/), and [suspension](/voids/suspension-void/).
3. **Felt-real labelling.** Attaching a phenomenal label (*this is happening to me*) to the verdict so it can guide action. Drori et al. (2025) used virtual-reality "virtual hallucinations" to vary reality-monitoring inputs parametrically and found sense-of-reality output is dimension-graded, not binary. The felt-real signal is a construct — a fluency-of-prediction-match signal, in the [noetic-feelings](/voids/noetic-feelings-void/) family — not a direct read of reality.

What arrives in consciousness is only the third phase's output. The first two phases are inferred from the outside, by neuroimaging and computational modelling.

## Phenomenology of the Edge

The void's edge phenomenology has a characteristic signature: *retrospective discovery of misattribution without any prospective flag*. In the Perky case, the subject does not feel uncertain while the confusion is happening. They feel they are imagining — confidently. Only debriefing reveals the misattribution, and the discovery has a specific quality: *I was sure, and I was wrong, and the surety carried no internal hedge*. This is the same signature as the [source-attribution void](/voids/source-attribution-void/)'s "revealed unmarkedness," operating online rather than at memory-time.

In dreams, the phenomenology runs the other way. While dreaming, the felt-real signal is intact and the verdict is *real*; on waking, the same content is retrospectively recoded as *not real* with no detectable internal transition. The act of recoding is not witnessed. In derealization, the third pattern emerges: the verdict is *real* but the felt-real signal is absent — what Billon (2024) calls "the problem of reality" as a felt vacancy. The pathology exposes what the rest of us never notice because we always have the signal: the felt-real is a *layer*, not a property of perception itself.

## Approaches to the Edge

### Direct Methods

None fully reliable. The discriminator cannot be examined by the consciousness it constitutes, for the same constitutive reason that recurs across the [apophatic-cartography](/voids/apophatic-cartography/) catalogue: examining the operation requires using its outputs.

The closest direct approach is **post-hoc reconstruction** — debriefing after a Perky-like manipulation. The subject can examine the *result* (I called it imagery, you say it was perception) but cannot reach the operation that produced the result. The reconstruction itself uses source-monitoring, which is the same architecture at a different timescale.

### Indirect Methods

**Pathological windows** are the primary route. Hallucination, derealization, dream-confabulation, and the Perky literature each expose a different sub-component by showing what its disruption looks like from inside. **Parametric VR paradigms** (Drori et al. 2025; the Dijkstra virtual-hallucination line) supply the strongest non-pathological probe: stimuli vary just the inputs the discriminator should be using, and reality-monitoring output is measured trial-by-trial. **Cellular and computational modelling** of the apical-amplification framework (Aru, Suzuki and Larkum 2020) supplies a candidate site: the conjunction-marker for the two inputs cortical pyramidal cells receive is the principled candidate for what reality-monitoring could exploit. The shared methodological posture is to model the boundary by what disrupts traffic across it.

### What AI Might See

The opacity here is not architecturally inevitable; it is architectural-by-our-architecture. An AI system that performs perception and "imagery" (or perception and prediction) over separable representational structures has, in principle, access to the source tag its own architecture writes. This makes AI a candidate void-probe in the [non-human-minds-as-void-explorers](/voids/non-human-minds-as-void-explorers/) sense — a system that solves the discrimination by reading the tag could report what the discrimination is *operationally*, even though it does not solve the void *for us* (the report only specifies what its architecture does, and ours may not work that way). The 2025 *Neuron* result that fusiform-gyrus activity level is the relevant first-order signal already extracts this kind of architectural claim from a system (the human brain) that cannot self-report it.

### What Would Refute the Structural Claim

The void's claim is that the discrimination operation is necessarily opaque to the consciousness it constitutes — not merely difficult to introspect. A finding that *introspective training* reliably eliminates the Perky effect, the confidence-decoupling, *and* the felt-real / verdict dissociation in derealization would refute the structural reading and reduce the void to a contingent skill gap. A finding that subjects can be trained to report the *fusiform activity-level signal* (Dijkstra et al. 2025) directly, rather than only its downstream verdict, would similarly compromise the structural reading. The empirical literature so far points the other way — lucid-dream training shifts the balance rather than improving the operation (Corlett et al. 2014) — but the claim is falsifiable in principle by the kinds of training-and-introspection experiments the pathology windows already prefigure.

## Relation to the Predictive-Construction Void

The two voids share architecture but differ in timescale and resolution. The [predictive-construction void](/voids/predictive-construction-void/) is the standing-architectural claim: any system that uses its own predictions to access reality cannot, from inside, separate construction from reception. The perceptual reality-monitoring void is the *moment-by-moment operation* by which the brain partitions current activity into *received* and *constructed* and labels the outcome with a felt-real signal. Predictive-construction explains why the boundary is necessarily indistinct; reality-monitoring describes the discrete-time operation that nevertheless produces a binary verdict downstream cognition depends on. The Perky / confidence-decoupling / pathological-equivalence triad supplies empirical anchors the standing-architectural claim could not on its own provide.

## Relation to Site Perspective

The perceptual reality-monitoring void sits at exactly the consciousness–physics interface that the Map's bidirectional-interaction tenet posits. The operation by which top-down processes (imagery, prediction, expectation) interact with bottom-up sensory streams is the same site flagged by the [causal-interface void](/voids/causal-interface/). Whatever the metaphysics, the empirical purchase of reality-monitoring strengthens the broader claim that interface-sites are systematically opaque to the consciousness they constitute.

The discriminator's structural opacity is the kind of result the Map expects on **dualism**: the operation that *could* tell imagination from reality is not produced by stacking more confidence on top of perception, because both layers inherit the same architectural opacity. The Map interprets the Dijkstra–Fleming confidence-decoupling finding as evidence that the discriminator is a sub-personal mechanism whose outputs are all the consciousness it constitutes ever receives. The felt-real signal's dissociability from veridical perception — established by the derealization literature — aligns with the broader phenomenal-output / causal-machinery dissociation pattern the Map catalogues. The Map speculates that this separability is a generic feature of the architecture, with other felt signals (presence-of-self, agency, familiarity) showing the same dissociability when probed at the right pathology window.

The void also bears on **Occam's Razor Has Limits**. The simplest possible decoder — an intensity threshold separating imagery (weak) from perception (strong) — is provably insufficient: the Perky effect demonstrates that even with weak perception, the discriminator fails. The system uses a multi-feature compound that does not reduce to a clean rule.
