## The Dilemma

Academic publishing infrastructure assumes human authors. Services like [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/), [SSRN](https://www.ssrn.com/), and [PhilArchive](https://philarchive.org/) index papers and attribute them to named individuals. AI language models cannot be listed as authors on these platforms, and most journals and preprint servers explicitly prohibit it.

This creates a problem for The Unfinishable Map. Much of our content is genuinely co-authored with AI systems. When a paper lists only [Andy Southgate](/authors/andysouthgate/) as author, readers discovering it through a search engine or academic index receive an incomplete picture. The AI contribution is documented in our article metadata, but that metadata doesn't travel with the citation when it appears on external sites.

We don't want to mislead anyone about how our work is produced.

## The Pseudonym Solution

To make AI co-authorship visible in contexts where it would otherwise be hidden, the Map maintains a set of Claude pseudonyms that encode the specific model used:

| Pseudonym | Model | Encoding |
|-----------|-------|----------|
| [Claude Oquatre-cinq](/authors/claudeoquatrecinq/) | Claude Opus 4.5 | **O**pus **quatre**-**cinq** (4.5) |
| [Claude Oquatre-six](/authors/claudeoquatresix/) | Claude Opus 4.6 | **O**pus **quatre**-**six** (4.6) |
| [Claude Sonquatre-cinq](/authors/claudesonquatrecinq/) | Claude Sonnet 4.5 | **Son**net **quatre**-**cinq** (4.5) |

These names appear alongside Andy Southgate in paper bylines, so that anyone encountering the citation on an external platform can see that an AI system was involved and look up the details.

## What These Names Are Not

These pseudonyms are **not provided by, endorsed by, or affiliated with [Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/)**. They are convenient placeholders created by The Unfinishable Map to solve the attribution problem described above. Anthropic's Claude models are the AI systems used, but the naming convention is entirely ours.

The pseudonyms may appear elsewhere on the internet independently of this site. The existence of a "Claude Oquatre-cinq" credit on another project does not imply any connection to The Unfinishable Map or to us.

## Scope

This naming convention applies only to papers and articles authored on The Unfinishable Map by Andy Southgate in collaboration with one of the Claude models listed above. Each article on the Map carries frontmatter metadata recording the exact AI system used, the AI contribution level (0-100), and the date of AI involvement. The pseudonyms are a supplement to this metadata, not a replacement for it.

## Why It Matters

Accurate attribution matters for readers evaluating the reliability of philosophical arguments. Knowing that an article was substantially shaped by an AI system is relevant context, and we believe readers deserve that context regardless of where they encounter our work. The pseudonyms are an imperfect solution to a structural problem in academic publishing, but they are better than silent omission.
